France Announce Squad For Euro Qualifiers, 5 Stars Snubbed

07 Nov 2019, 18:31 by David Kappel

France have announced their squad for their upcoming 2020 European Championships qualifiers, but once again snubbed a host of high-profile names, including stars from Manchester United, FC Barcelona and Real Madrid.

Click through the gallery above to see some Les Bleus stars who were not called up for the latest squad.

The reigning world champions face Moldova and Albania in the next international break, and so far, France have picked up 19 points in Euro 2020 qualification, but sit behind Turkey in second place in their group.

Coach Didier Deschamps has once again rung the changes, and recalled Kylian Mbappe and Benjamin Mendy, who missed out on the previous squad.

Manchester United's Anthony Martial and Los Blancos forward Karim Benzema, though, remained out of the coach's plans.

Here is France's squad in full, as confirmed by the GFF:

Goalkeepers: Steve Mandanda (Marseille), Alphonse Areola (Real Madrid), Mike Maignan (Lille)

Defenders: Benjamin Pavard (Bayern Munich), Raphael Varane (Real Madrid), Clement Lenglet (Barcelona), Leo Dubois (Lyon), Presnel Kimpembe (PSG), Kurt Zouma (Chelsea), Benjamin Mendy (Manchester City), Lucas Digne (Everton)

Midfielders: Corentin Tolisso (Bayern Munich), Blaise Matuidi (Juventus), Moussa Sissoko (Tottenham Hotspur), Tanguy Ndombele (Tottenham Hotspur), N'Golo Kante (Chelsea)

Attackers: Olivier Giroud (Chelsea), Antoine Griezmann (Barcelona), Kylian Mbappe (PSG), Thomas Lemar (Atletico Madrid), Nabil Fekir (Real Betis), Kingsley Coman (Bayern Munich)

Injured duo Hugo Lloris and Aymeric Laporte were not available for selection.
