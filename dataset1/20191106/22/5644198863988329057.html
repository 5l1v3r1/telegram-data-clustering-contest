Bank of America, Raymond James to pay US$12 million restitution for fund overcharges

06 Nov 2019, 22:50

A U.S. regulator said units of Bank of America Corp and Raymond James Financial Inc will pay roughly 12 million in restitution to customers who incurred excess fees on their investments in so-called 529 plans, which are used to encourage saving for educational expenses.

REUTERS: A U.S. regulator said units of Bank of America Corp and Raymond James Financial Inc will pay roughly 12 million in restitution to customers who incurred excess fees on their investments in so-called 529 plans, which are used to encourage saving for educational expenses.

The Financial Industrial Regulatory Authority said Bank of America's Merrill Lynch unit will pay at least US$4 million and the Raymond James units will pay about US$8 million. None of the units admitted or denied wrongdoing.
