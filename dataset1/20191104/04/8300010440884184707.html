SoCal forecast: Strong winds subside as SoCal sees slightly cooler temps

04 Nov 2019, 04:37

LOS ANGELES (KABC) -- After a few weeks of intense red-flag wind conditions, Southern California will see slightly cooler temperatures Monday as strong winds subside.

Los Angeles and Orange counties will experience a cool cover of morning clouds with a high of 77. Temps will begin to climb back up by Friday with highs in the mid-80s.

The valleys and Inland Empire can expect a daytime high of 82 with even cooler temps through Wednesday.
