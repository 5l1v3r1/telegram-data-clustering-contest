Emma Caldwell murder probe lawyers interview witnesses as new court case looms

The interviews have taken place as Crown Office prepares groundwork for a potential prosecution.

17 Nov 2019, 04:30 by Brendan McGinty

Witnesses in the Emma Caldwell murder case have been interviewed by Crown Office lawyers as the reopened investigation reaches a critical new phase.

Solicitors under the instruction of the Lord Advocate, Scotland’s leading prosecutor, have taken statements from people identified as being vital to the second police probe into 27-year-old Emma’s death.

The interviews have taken place as the Crown Office and Procurator Fiscal Service lay the groundwork for a court case.

A police report on the murder, following a renewed four-year investigation, was filed to the Crown last year.

A source said: “The new police investigation was painstaking as it re-examined all the avenues of investigation from the original case, plus some significant new lines of inquiry.

 

“The report remains officially under consideration but the Crown has been working hard on it ever since it was submitted.

“The time it has taken will not have been easy for the family but there is a determination that this investigation will be watertight.”

Emma, from Erskine, Renfrewshire, went missing in March 2005 from the hostel where she lived in the south side of Glasgow.

She had become depressed following the loss of her sister and turned to drugs before working as a prostitute to fund the habit.

Her body was found in a remote location in woods near Roberton, Lanarkshire, several weeks later.

 

A murder inquiry resulted in four Turkish nationals being accused of Emma’s murder but charges were dropped as a crucial translation of recorded audio evidence was botched.

In 2015, the Sunday Mail revealed the identity of a forgotten suspect who was interviewed six times by police but never charged.

Later that year, then Lord Advocate Frank Mulholland ordered the investigation to be reopened.

The suspect, who cannot now be named for legal reasons and who has since been accused of other criminal offences , has denied any involvement but admitted he knew Emma.

In August 2018, a major new inquiry into unsolved historic crimes on the back of the reopened murder probe was revealed by the Sunday Mail.

That investigation, which remains ongoing, is looking at sexual offences which emerged following a re-examination of more than 1000 statements given to the original inquiry into Emma’s death.

Her mum Margaret has frequently spoken of the family’s frustration at the long wait for justice.

 

In an interview in August last year, she said: “The new investigation into my daughter’s death started more than three years ago and it is more than 13 years since she died.

“Above all, I would like to see justice for Emma but I would be happy if the inquiry into her death might also give justice to other women and victims.”

A spokesman for the Crown Office and Procurator Fiscal Service said: “This is a live investigation and it would not be appropriate to comment at this time.”

Chief Superintendent David McLaren said: “The investigation into Emma Caldwell’s murder remains open and a team of detectives continue to examine the circumstances of her death.

 

“Officers have undertaken a significant amount of work re-investigating the whole case and surrounding events in conjunction with the Crown Office and Procurator Fiscal Service, following instruction from the Lord Advocate in 2015.

“An advice and guidance report was submitted to the COPFS in June last year, which presented the findings, so far, of the murder re-investigation.”
