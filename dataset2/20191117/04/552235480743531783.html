Price cuts for Tartan Army veterans at Scotland games should be celebrated

The Sunday Mail says that after the win over Cyprus the good news continued as SFA see sense over tickets.

17 Nov 2019, 04:30 by Mail Opinion, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/mail-opinion/

Justice is not something Scotland fans are particularly familiar with.

Supporting the national side is more often than not a pastime to be endured rather than enjoyed.

The most deserved and hard-worked-for victories are routinely snatched away in ever more heartbreaking circumstances.

But just maybe last night’s 2-1 win over Cyprus marked a turning of the tide that will set us up for some of our biggest play-off games in 20 years.

And another major cause for celebration today is that after an 18-month Sunday Mail campaign, the SFA has finally agreed to see sense and offer older supporters cut-price tickets for those crucial matches.

 

More than anything, this is a victory for common sense, as the case for doing this couldn’t have been more clear cut.

Many of the fans who’ll benefit are people, often on very low incomes, who have loyally supported the team for generations.

When you consider the results over the years, there is a strong argument that such dedication should be rewarded with free entry to Hampden for life and some sort of medal.

But this isn’t just about altruism.

The truth is that the SFA has been shooting itself in the foot with its rigid ticketing policy.

We revealed in October that empty Hampden seats for five home Euro 2020 qualifiers has cost Scotland’s game more than £4million.

Just 127,969 fans had turned up for those matches out of a potential 259,330 – the equivalent of just 49 per cent capacity.

That is money that could be paying for the youth academies and training grounds the next generation of stars so badly need.

Cutting prices for people who will otherwise struggle to afford to go to matches is a great way to fill the stadium.

It also helps create the sort of atmosphere that every fan wants to see Scotland play in – and the sort of atmosphere that inspire a team to win.

So if there is one thing the SFA should be focused on, it is making sure that every single Scotland game is played in front of a full house.

Today’s decision will mean fans over 65 should be able to pick up affordable tickets for Nations League play-off matches in March 2020 that will give Steve Clarke’s squad the chance to get us to a major tournament for the first time since 1998.

The move has been backed by everyone from Age Scotland to former First Minister Henry McLeish.

The Sunday Mail has done its bit – let’s hope the team can make it worth our while.
