17 Nov 2019, 04:30 by Jamie Kemble

EX-RAMS ASSISTANT WARNED

Former Derby County assistant manager Jody Morris has reportedly been warned by Chelsea about his social media activity after a post in reference to ex-Blues boss Jose Mourinho.

Morris was Frank Lampard's assistant at Pride Park last season, before moving back to Stamford Bridge with his former teammate when Lampard became the new Chelsea head coach in July.

Morris is active on Instagram and Twitter, as he was during his Rams spell.

According to the Daily Mail, he has been told by Chelsea "to tone down his social media activity" following the Mourinho post.

NICHOLSON DRAWS INSPIRATION

Former Derby County full-back Shane Nicholson believes his old club are 'making progress'.

The Rams have made a slow start to the season and indeed to life under Phillip Cocu , sitting 16th in the Championship table.

But they are still within seven points of the play-offs, as well as heading in the right direction, according to Nicholson.

Speaking on RamsTV , the former defender who made 74 league appearances said: "Looking at the last three games we have managed something like 19 shots on target.

“For me, that shows that we are making progress. Against Forest last weekend we had 11 shots and seven of them were on target as well, which tells you the team is creating chances.

ACOSTA LATEST

There has been an update on the reported interest from Derby in D.C. United midfielder Luciano Acosta .

In an interview in Argentina , Acosta claimed his former D.C. United team-mate, Derby-bound Wayne Rooney , had told him to join him at Pride Park.

Rooney joins the Rams as a player-coach in January after spending nearly two years in the United States with the MLS outfit.

Acosta has also called time on his D.C. United career, and is looking for a new club.

Fresh reports in The Athletic claim that Acosta is “waiting for the call” from Derby over a potential move, but there has been no sign from the Rams that deal could take place.
