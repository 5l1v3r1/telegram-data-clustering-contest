BRILLIANT NEWS

Birmingham City fan Tommy Plant has made a remarkable recovery being critically ill - and Blues celebrated his return home with him by inviting him to St Andrew's.

The youngster suffered a cardiac arrest back in January and was in a coma for three days.

He initially recovered but returned to hospital four weeks ago and was in intensive care .

Boss Pep Clotet sent the seven-year-old his and the club's best wishes and the club followed that up by bringing him down to St Andrew's to welcome him back once he returned home.

His story captured the hearts of Blues players, who personally reached out to the youngster.

And they posed for pictures with Tommy in the dressing room and around the ground.

Blues don't have a match today due to the international break, their next Championship clash a trip to Huddersfield Town on Saturday week.

FORMER BLUES STAR MAKES SECOND DEBUT

League Two side Swindon Town have confirmed they have re-signed former Birmingham City defender Paul Caddis.

Caddis rejoins the Robins on a short-term deal, six years after leaving the club to sign for Blues in 2013.

The experienced right-back made more than 150 appearances in two spells for Blues, with his first stint coming as a loan spell from Swindon in the 2012-13 season.

His move to St Andrew’s was then made permanent that summer, and he went on to spend three-and-a-half more seasons with Blues before signing for Bury in 2017.

Since leaving Birmingham, Caddis has had spells at Bury, Blackburn Rovers, Bradford City, before rejoining the club he initially signed for in 2010.

His first stint with Swindon saw Caddis make 92 appearances and has now re-signed for the club on a short-term deal.

Prior to joining Swindon, the 31-year-old has been training with the club in recent weeks to keep fit and sharp and manager.

“He is a very good trainer, very fit and a good footballer as well – and, above all, his attitude is spot on,” said manager Richie Wellens.

Caddis featured in Swindon’s 302 win over Salford City on Saturday, coming on as a substitute for the final minutes of the clash at Moor Lane.

PLAYERS LINKED WITH BLUES

Birmingham City are already a third of the way through the 2019-20 Championship campaign - and the January transfer window is fast approaching.

The first month of 2020 will provide Pep Clotet and his rival managers in the second tier with the chance to bolster their squads for the rest of the season.

Clotet has already spoken about his plans for the window and the club strategy that has been drawn up.

"The club is preparing for any position in case something happens. We have a wide squad at the minute so we need to be selective with what we want to bring,” he said.

“I know the club is working very hard and they have already our targets selected. That for me is a pleasure to see in a club.

“The club is as committed as well as much as possible to be able to recruit as early as possible in January."
