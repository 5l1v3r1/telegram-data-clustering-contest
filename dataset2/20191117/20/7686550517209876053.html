Thousands turn out for incredible, weird and wonderful Swansea Christmas Parade

The event has been hailed a huge success

17 Nov 2019, 20:13 by Lucy John

Thousands of people who flocked to Swansea's annual Christmas parade were teated to a carnival of colour, music and dancing.

The parade began at the foot of Wind Street and wound through a number of streets before ending on The Kingsway.

Last year's event left many disappointed and was named one of the 'worst ever' Christmas parades, for its lack of floats and festive spirit.

Leader of Swansea Council, Rob Stewart assured everyone that the council "will get it right" this year - and they did.

The parade was particularly special this year, forming part of the 50th anniversary city status celebrations.

There were marching bands, colourful floats, festive film characters, Christmas inflatables, Santa in his sleigh and some special effects.

Young performers from Swansea drama and dance groups also took part.

Local choirs and vocalists entertained city centre visitors at performance points as spectators gathered around for the parade.
