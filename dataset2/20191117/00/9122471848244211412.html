LSU Player Sends Classy Message To Alabama QB Tua Tagovailoa

17 Nov 2019, 00:06 by Dan Lyons

Last weekend, LSU beat Alabama for the first time in eight years, to take control of the SEC West and the College Football Playoff race. Alabama beat Mississippi State this weekend, but suffered what may ultimately be the biggest loss of its season, with quarterback Tua Tagovailoa leaving the game with what has been reported as a season-ending hip injury.

Tagovailoa has received well wishes from all across the college football world. If the injury is as bad as it sounds, some worry that his career, and not just the rest of this season, could be in jeopardy. Hopefully that is not the case.

Everyone from Kansas City Chiefs star and NFL MVP Patrick Mahomes, to former Ohio State and Florida coach Urban Meyer has sent well wishes to the Alabama quarterback. Now, one of his SEC rivals has done the same.

LSU’s Breiden Fehoko, a native of Hawaii like Tua, tweeted his support this evening. “Alofa atu” is Samoan for “love you.”
