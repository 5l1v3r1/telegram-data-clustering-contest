‘Path Of Exile 2’ Is Revealed In A Surprise Announcement And It Looks Terrific

17 Nov 2019 by Kevin Murnane

Describing Path of Exile as a Diablo clone doesn’t begin to do the game justice. The developers at Grinding Gear Games took the loot-driven action RPG framework pioneered by Diablo and built a creative and compelling alternative that’s unlike any other game on the market. PoE’s creators have never been afraid to think outside the box and they’re at it again. Path of Exile 2 was revealed at this weekend’s ExileCon event and it looks terrific.PoE2 is a creative solution to a knotty problem. The original PoE is a very good game that’s built on an interlocking set of complex game mechanics. It works and it works well. But the developers came up with a lot of ideas about how things in PoE could be done differently as they refined and evolved their game over the years. The problem was many of these ideas would break the excellent game they were already running.

Rather than try to introduce substantially revised systems into Path of Exile 1, they combined them into PoE2. The standard move at this point would be to replace the old game with the new one but Grinding Gear didn’t want to do that. PoE1 is a great game with a devoted and loyal fan base. It didn’t make sense to drop it just because they had a variant of the game they thought was just as much fun to play.

Grinding Gear’s solution was to treat the two games as different campaigns and combine them into one game. Path of Exile 2 presents a seven-act storyline that takes place 20 years after the events of the original game. The two campaigns run on parallel tracks leading to the same atlas end-game content. All the expansion content from the past six years of development along with any cosmetics players may have purchased will be available in both.

Path of Exile 2 has the same set of seven character classes as the original but there are 19 new Ascendancy classes and the skill system has been redesigned. Characters in PoE2 will also be able to shapeshift at any time if they have the relevant skill. Bosses at all levels have been improved.

The complex gem system has been thoroughly revised. In PoE1, gems are slotted in individual weapons and pieces of armor. Replacing your gear means reconfiguring your gems which can be fiddly and annoying. Sometimes I find myself running with less than optimal gear because redoing the gems is just too much hassle. In PoE2, each type of weapon or armor has its own unique gem configuration and gems are slotted in a separate gem interface. If you want to change the gems slotted in the bow you’re using, you do it in the gem interface. If you find a better bow, all you have to do is equip it and the gem setup in the interface is automatically applied. It’s a huge improvement.

Path of Exile 2 also features physically-based rendering and a new lighting model. Characters have new models and animations. PoE1 didn’t look bad but PoE2 looks better. You can check it out in the new announcement and gameplay trailers.

The developers expect a beta for Path of Exile 2 to launch near the end of 2020. That’s a long time to wait for a game that looks so promising I want to play it right now but there will be things to look forward to in the meantime. More information about POE2 will be revealed in the coming months. Path of Exile 1 will continue to be updated on the regular schedule throughout the coming year and changes designed for PoE2 that can be introduced without breaking the original will be added during the build up to the beta.
