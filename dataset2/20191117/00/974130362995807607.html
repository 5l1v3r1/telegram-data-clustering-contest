Rianna Jarrett Named National League Player of the Year

17 Nov 2019 by Balls Team

At the Castle Oaks Hotels in Limerick on Saturday evening, Jarrett was honoured as the outstanding player of 2019 in a season that saw her help Wexford Youths win the S贸 Hotels FAI Women's Cup at Aviva Stadium, score her first senior goal for the Republic of Ireland, and finish as top scorer in the League.

The Wexford native was first up to the stage to collect her trophy for being top scorer and she returned as one of the 11 players to make the team of the season. But a third trip was required to get her hands on the night's big prize.

Galway WFC's Kayla Brady was named Under-17 Player of the Year, while Shelbourne's Emily Whelan got the Young Player of the Year Award.

Eamon Naughton was also recognised on the night as he was given a special services to the Women's National League award for his hard work and dedication since the League's inception.
