Bengaluru's Harlur residents hit the streets in protest

17 Nov 2019, 00:30 by Bangalore Mirror Bureau

By Poojitha Girish


No infrastructure, no votes, was the message they had for politicians

Residents of Haralur and neighbouring areas took to the street to protest against civic apathy on Saturday. The protest march began from Parappana Agrahara Main and culminated at Sarjapur Road.

During the protest, the residents held placards and raised slogans like ”No Roads, No Votes” and “Yedheli Yedheli” (wake up), sending out a clear message to politicians. “The roads are damaged with potholes and footpaths are crowded with parked vehicles. There have been numerous accidents and yet no traffic management,” said a protester. “The Harlur lake is not well maintained. There is an unbearable odour and so many mosquitoes, that children cannot risk stepping out to play or cycle,” said Latha Mahadevan, resident of Harlur.
