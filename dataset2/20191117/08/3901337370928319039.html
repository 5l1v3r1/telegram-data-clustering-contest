17 Nov 2019, 08:53 by SL International

Italian publication Corriere della Sera reported on Saturday that Conte received police proection after receiving the letter at his home.

However, the club have since come out confirm that the letter was sent to the club, not Conte.

Inter's statement read:

In relation to the news published today, Inter Milan states that Antonio Conte did not personally receive any threatening letters and, consequently, he did not go in person to file a complaint.

It was the club that received a letter and, as is the practice in this kind of situation, it contacted the competent authorities.

Conte's wife, Elisabetta Muscarello, also rubbished the report claiming a bullet had been sent their housedhold.

"For the record: the story of the bullet is a hoax!," she tweeted.

Conte's Inter currently sit one point behind Serie A leaders Juventus in second spot after 12 rounds of matches.
