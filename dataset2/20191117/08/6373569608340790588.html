Credit where credit is due, Plymouth Argyle's defence is finally starting to look the part

THE VERDICT: The Pilgrims had to work hard for their 1-0 win away to table-topping Forest Green

17 Nov 2019, 08:53 by Chris Errington

Plymouth Argyle's defending has left a lot to be desired for much of this season, culminating in the 4-0 Devon Derby drubbing by Exeter City at the end of October.

But there are now encouraging signs that the Pilgrims can produce the defensive resilience they are going to need, and on a consistent basis, to challenge for League Two promotion.

Whatever has been said and done by manager Ryan Lowe behind the scenes and on the training ground since the defeat by Exeter has had a positive effect when it has come to his side's defending.

They have kept clean sheets in both of their matches since then (excluding the EFL Trophy non-event against Chelsea Under-21s, which ended in a 1-0 loss).

Both have been on the road, and the Pilgrims have had to dig deep to keep out the opposition.

First, they beat Bolton Wanderers 1-0 in the FA Cup and then they went to League Two leaders Forest Green Rovers and came away with a win by the same scoreline.

Both are matches that Argyle would not have won in the opening couple of months of the campaign.

Back then, they were allowing far too many goals from crosses into their penalty area and often they were conceded late in goals too, which cost them a number of points.

Naturally, the defenders got most of the blame for this, although the saying 'you defend as a team' is very valid.

At times, Scott Wootton, Niall Canavan and skipper Gary Sawyer all came in for criticism, and deservedly so, and lost their places in the team.

So, credit where credit is due, that trio have been excellent in the victories over Bolton and Forest Green.

Wootton, in particular, seems to have rediscovered his confidence which was shot to pieces last term as he and Argyle struggled in League One.

It is also worth noting that the last two wins have come with Chelsea loan signing Josh Grant in the midfield holding role.

That has seen the Pilgrims playing with effectively four central defenders in their line-up, giving them an extra edge of steel.

There was a lot of talk at the start of the season about Argyle passing the ball out from the back and through the thirds, not least from Lowe himself.

Now, it feels as though the Pilgrims are concentrating more on some of the football fundamentals, such as getting their bodies in the way of crosses and shots.

Yes, you can play attacking football when you have the ball but, out of possession, you have to be prepared to do the 'dirty side'.

That includes standing up to the opposition and showing that you are not prepared to be intimidated by them.

The victory over Forest Green was a case in point. Rovers, under Mark Cooper, have been lauded as one of the most attractive sides to watch in League Two.

However, in the closing minutes against Argyle, Cooper sent on powerful centre-back Matt Mills to play as an extra striker.

There was no subtlety to their play then as the ball was repeatedly hit long and high towards the Argyle penalty area.

The Pilgrims had to defend a flurry of late corners, goalmouth scrambles and appeals for a penalty for a challenge on Mills by 'keeper Alex Palmer, but they survived them all.

It had not been the good, open game of football we had been promised by both managers but, come the final whistle, there were no complaints from Lowe about that.

It was job done for the Pilgrims and there will have been a great sense of satisfaction among the players, several of them battered and bruised, at the outcome.

The win was secured by Antoni Sarcevic's fourth goal of the season, which came from a short corner move with Danny Mayor in the 24th minute.

Sarcevic played the ball to Mayor, received it back and then cut inside into the Forest Green penalty area.

His eventual shot took a deflection off Rovers' defender Farrend Rawson and the ball flew high into the net past 'keeper Joe Wollacott.

Argyle needed defensive determination and organisation after that to hold onto their slender lead but they did so and three valuable points were picked up.
