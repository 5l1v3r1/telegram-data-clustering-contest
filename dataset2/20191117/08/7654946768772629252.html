Buttigieg dominates Iowa field in new CNN poll

17 Nov 2019, 08:11 by Selim Algar

The mayor is suddenly major.

Amplifying a recent surge, Pete Buttigieg dominated his rivals in a new CNN/Des Moines Register/Mediacom poll of likely Iowa caucus voters released Saturday.

The mayor of South Bend, Indiana, hit the 25 percent mark – a 16-point climb since September that put him nearly 10 percentage points clear of his main rivals.

Sen. Elizabeth Warren polled at 16 percent with Bernie Sanders right behind at 15 percent, according to the tally.

While Buttigieg’s numbers have fattened since September, Warren’s polling dropped by six percentage points and Biden dipped by five.

Sanders has picked up four percentage points since that stretch.

The latest poll comes on the heels of several other surveys that show Buttigieg abruptly vaulting to the top of the Democratic pack with Iowa voters.

No other candidates managed to crack double digits, according to the survey.

Minnesota Sen. Amy Klobuchar had 6 percent, and a cluster of five candidates limped along with 3 percent.

Former New York City mayor Michael Bloomberg, who has not yet declared his candidacy, had 2 percent.
