Woman fighting for life in hospital after serious M5 crash

This is what we know so far

17 Nov 2019, 09:04 by Joel Cooper

A woman is believed to be in a critical condition in hospital this morning after a serious crash on the M5 yesterday.

The incident involved a car and a lorry, with firefighters having to cut the female car driver out of the vehicle after it became wedged underneath the lorry.

Police, fire and ambulance crews were called to the southbound carriageway between junctions 22 and 23 following a report of a two-vehicle crash just before 6am on Saturday morning (November 16).

All traffic had to be stopped, resulting in hue tailbacks up to six miles long - with some drivers reporting being stranded for up to five hours.

This is what we know so far.

What happened?

The motorway was shut southbound between junction 22 for  Burnham-on-Sea  to junction 23 for  Bridgwater  shortly before 6am.

A spokesman for Devon and Somerset Fire and Rescue said a car had  crashed into the back of a lorry  and had "become stuck under the rear of the lorry".

The female driver of the car was cut free from her vehicle and was taken to hospital with serious head injuries.

Avon and Somerset Police  has since described her condition as "critical". The lorry driver was treated for shock.

The carriageway was shut for several hours as  police officers investigated the collision and utilised a drone.

The vehicles involved were cleared from the motorway at around 2.30pm and spilled fuel was cleaned off the carriageway.

The southbound carriageway reopened after 3pm and the nearby slip road was reopened at around 3.30pm.

What have police said?

A spokesman has described a female driver involved in the crash as being in a "critical condition"  and are appealing for witnesses to come forward.

He said: "We are pleased to confirm that our colleagues in Highways England have re-opened the M5 southbound from junction 22 following the serious collision this morning.

"We'd like to thank everyone for their patience and understanding while we carried out an investigation at the scene.

"The motorway was closed from J22 southbound following the collision between a car and lorry shortly before 6am today.

"A woman car driver who suffered life threatening head injuries remains in hospital in a critical condition. Enquiries into the incident are continuing.

"We're keen to hear from anyone who was in the area at the time of the incident who may have dashcam footage to help our collision investigators."

The fire service statement in full

A spokesman for  Devon and Somerset Fire and Rescue  said they were called to the scene at 5.56am following a call from a member of the public.

He said: "Devon and Somerset Fire and Rescue Service received a call from a member of the public reporting a road traffic collision at the above location.

"Two fire appliances from Burnham-on-Sea and a heavy rescue vehicle from Taunton were immediately mobilised by Fire Control.

"On arrival the Incident Commander confirmed that a car had run in to the back of a lorry and become stuck under the rear of the lorry with one female casualty still inside the car.

"Crews quickly got to work to stabilise the vehicle and then set about extricating the casualty from the car using Hydraulic Rescue Equipment.

"The female was taken by ambulance to hospital and the driver of the lorry was treated for shock."

Anyone with information or dash cam footage of the incident is asked to contact Avon and Somerset police on 101 quoting reference number 5219265362.
