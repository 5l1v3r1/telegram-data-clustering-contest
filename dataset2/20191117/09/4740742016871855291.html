Glastonbury 2020: The surprising acts odds-on favourite to headline festival

The latest acts rumoured to be on the lineup

17 Nov 2019, 09:00 by Tristan Cork

Bookies appear to be pretty certain on who are going to be the three headliners for next year's Glastonbury Festival, judging by the latest odds released by bookmakers William Hill.

Three acts have been named odds-on favourites to be the three Pyramid Stage headliners, as bookies scour the pop world looking for clues into the inner-workings of the festival.

William Hill have named Taylor Swift as the outright favourite, with odds of 7/2 on - meaning someone betting a fiver would only get 70p back.

The American singer is apparently in love with all things English - she recently released a song all about hanging out in London with her English boyfriend - so a performance on the Pyramid Stage would appear to fit into her new image. She would be the first female headliner in four years, since Adele in 2016, to top the bill.

Second favourite is Paul McCartney, who William Hill are giving odds of 9/4 on. The Beatles legend will have just celebrated his 78th birthday a couple of weeks before the festival in 2020, and would be the oldest Glastonbury headliner in the festival's history were he to belt out Hey Jude on the main stage.

The third favourite - still odds-on - is Kendrick Lamar, with William Hill giving odds of 4/6 for. The Californian artist is one of the most successful hip-hop acts of all time, and, like Taylor Swift has never even performed at Glastonbury before.

Paul McCartney is the only one of the three to have headlined Glastonbury before - he topped the bill in 2004 along with Muse and Oasis.

With Emily Eavis ruling out the likes of Fleetwood Mac and other early favourites recently, the odds on some other, more traditional guitar-based rock bands are following closely behind, with Coldplay fourth favourites, along with Foals and The 1975.

Interestingly, odds on Oasis staging one of the most anticipated reunion comebacks ever appear to be lengthening by the day. William Hill's odds appear to make it clear they view the chances of Noel and Liam kissing and making up to headline Glastonbury more remote than either of their much-less successful individual bands getting the Eavis nod.

And Queen are given long odds by William Hill - and perhaps should be even longer, given the comments of Queen guitarist Brian May about his falling out with Michael Eavis over the badger cull.
