Poor horses left traumatised by firework display next to stables

The horse can be seen running around in circles in a distressing video

17 Nov 2019, 09:00 by Olivia Tobin

A heart wrenching video has shown a horse trembling with fear as a firework display goes off at a nearby pub.

The horse at Prospect Farm, in Wirral, can be seen running around in a circle shaking as the fireworks are heard from a display being held at nearby pub, The Saughall.

Farmer Philip Broster said the distressing video was filmed on November 9 as he claimed embers from a bonfire floated into and around a hay barn where the horses were being kept.

The 43-year-old said he and wife, Amy Broster, 26, "dread" Bonfire Night and live in fear of a fire starting or the animals being in distress.

After being contacted by the ECHO, the pub's owners apologised to the horse and its owners and promised it wouldn't happen again.

Mr Broster, who lives at the farm with his wife, parents and son, Harry, three, said: "It's so distressing for myself because I don't want there to be a fire.

"It's not good for us and it's not good for the animals' welfare. The distress and the disturbance of it is horrible."

The Saughall said: "We are now looking into the issues with the relevant advisers. We will release a further detailed statement once this has been completed."

When approaching Bonfire Night, Mr Broster said the family usually take preventative steps to stop the horses from being affected by the loud bangs.

This can include sedating the animals or making sure they are in a safe space.

Before the pub's display, Mr Broster claims he approached the owner and was told the fireworks "wouldn't include rockets" and would begin at 7.30pm.

However, Mr Broster said the display started half an hour earlier which he claims did not allow enough time to sedate the horses.

Heartbreaking footage from the night, Mr Broster claimed, showed fireworks streaming into the sky above the hay barn as well as one of the horses in distress.

A voice can be heard telling the horse: "It's ok, it's ok, good boy."

Mr Broster said the farm had suffered two fires before, not because of fireworks, but the cost to repair the damage was up to £20,000.

He said: "We're just trying to get our view out there how dangerous it could be."

Videos of the farm on Bonfire Night are understood to have been widely shared on social media.

On November 10, The Saughall Pub posted: "Thank you for all of those who attended our annual organised bonfire night event 9/11/19.

"We are aware however of issues which have been raised through social media. We are now looking into the issues with the relevant advisers. We will release a further detailed statement once this has been completed.

"A full risk assessment and relevant paperwork is available for anyone to view please email enquiries@saughallpubandkitchen.co.uk

"In the meantime we will continue to serve our loyal customers in the usual way.

"Merseyside Police have been informed of the situation and are working with us.

"Many thanks."

Marc Edmunds, tenant at Saughall Pub said: “The pub has traditionally held a bonfire and fireworks event in the past and we wanted to continue bringing the community together in this way.

"We went through all the appropriate channels in advance to ensure we organised it in a safe and legal way, including seeking permission from the council, police and fire service.

"Like others, we were shocked to see the video of the horse.

"We are relieved that it is now OK and we won’t put on a similar event in the future without ensuring this kind of thing won’t happen again.

"It was never our intention to cause any distress and can only apologise to the horse and the wider public.”
