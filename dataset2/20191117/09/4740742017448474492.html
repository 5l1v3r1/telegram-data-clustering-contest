Scouting report: Dream European debut for Bristol Bears' Welsh fly-half rising star Ioan Lloyd

We shine a spotlight on the performance against Zebre of the 18-year-old everyone is talking about

17 Nov 2019, 09:18 by Daniel Evans

A mere 18 years old, barely out of school, wearing the number-10 jersey for Bristol Bears at Ashton Gate.

No problem at all for Ioan Lloyd.

Not only did the rising star score another try, not only did he knock over eight kicks out of eight, he was man of the match to boot in the 59-21 European Challenge Cup victory over Zebre Rugby.

Lloyd didn't have a massive impact in the early stages of the contest as the Italian Pro14 outfit started pretty well in an end-to-end opening.

Lloyd's first conversion was a simple one but his second was a tricky touchline effort after Toby Fricker had scored in the left corner.

In the mean-time he was passing crisply and looking for gaps in the visitors' defence, not afraid to thrown in a jink and a step.

In the 25th minute, the former Wales U18s player burst into life. After a lot of crabbing and scrappy play from both sides he picked up the ball on the Bristol 10m line, accelerated in field then headed for a hole between two Zebre players.

Neither touched him as he streaked through the gap, got up to top speed and streaked away to secure his side's bonus-point try.

After adding the simple two points a 40m penalty followed. In fact, none of his eight shots at goal went anywhere other than between the sticks.

Lloyd's influence on the game lessened once Callum Sheedy replaced Charles Piutau in the 46th minute and Lloyd went to full-back.

It also coincided with Zebre's resurgence and Bristol's indiscipline; meaning Lloyd had little ball in the opposition half for a good 20 minutes.

He did execute a try-saving tackle on wing Pierre Bruno, though, by dragging his right leg into touch as Zebre looked to make use of an overlap.

After converting Charlie Powell's 77th try from wide on the right, Lloyd made way for Andy Uren to a rapturous applause from the Ashton Gate crowd.

A couple of minute's later, he was announced as man of the match. Not a bad maiden start, all told.

"I've always said if you're good enough, you're old enough," said DoR Pat Lam after the match. "If you perform, you'll get the games."

On this evidence, the former Clifton College pupil will get plenty more opportunities this season and for years to come.

"I'm really impressed with him," said number eight Luke Hamilton, who was also making his first senior start for the club.

"He's good enough to step up and, as he proved today, he's a great talent. When people say he's 18 you have to stop and think: Is he actually 18?

"He pulled the strings for us today. He really led our attack and, off the tee, he was outstanding as well.

"It's always good to have someone young coming through."
