Picnic spots in city witness heavy footfalls

Several roads see traffic congestions

17 Nov 2019, 19:48 by Staff Reporter

Picnic sports in the city saw heavy footfalls as picnickers thronged the places on the third Sunday of ‘Karthika Masam’.

A good number of families, members of residential associations, colleagues, friends gathered at the hotspots since morning.

Kambalakonda Wildlife Sanctuary, Indira Gandhi Zoological Park, Thotlakonda, Kailasagiri and the beaches at Rushikonda, Bhimili, Yarada and others saw huge rush. Many picnickers played games and organised cultural programmes, fun events. They also had lunch with their families. Youth were seen taking selfies.

“We planned a trip to Yarada beach. But our parents wanted to go Kailasagiri. We are enjoying here too,” said A. Vishvesh, a private employee and among a group of 18 picnickers. The Beach Road stretch from Park Hotel Junction to Naval Coastal Battery, Tenneti Park was jam packed in the evening.

Visitors throng Agency

Tourist spots in the Visakhapatnam Agency have also been witnessing a heavy rush since November 1. Being Sunday and picnic season, Borra Caves, Katika waterfalls, Araku Valley, Ananthagiri coffee plantations, Chaparai waterfalls, Lambasingi saw heavy footfalls. A number of hotels including that belonging to APTDC are getting good bookings.
