Official visits Kazhuveli, water source for city

It was developed at a cost of ₹22 lakh

17 Nov 2019, 19:42 by Special Correspondent

Managing Director of Tamil Nadu Water Resources Conservation and Rivers Restoration Corporation K. Satyagopal on Sunday inspected a waterbody near Marakkanam, as the State government is planning to source water from there for drinking water needs in Chennai.

According to an official release, the plan is to treat the water from Kazhuveli waterbody in Villupuram district before supplying it to Chennai.

The waterbody has been developed under the Kudimaramathu Scheme, at a cost of ₹22 lakh.

Villupuram Collector L. Subramanian and senior officails from the Public Works Department were present.
