Advanced surgery programme for children from February 3

17 Nov 2019, 19:27 by Special Correspondent

Kasturba Medical College and Hospital, Attavar, in association with Peedh Parai Internatinal, Houston, USA, and Bantwal Anath Mallya Charitable Trust, will conduct the 4th annual free advanced paediatric surgical programme ‘Bal Shalyakriya Mission’ between February 3 and 7.

In a press release, John Ramapuram, Medical Superintendent, KMC Hosptals, Mangaluru, said during the five-day programme at the KMC Attavar, untreated complicated surgical conditions of children upto 16 years would be screened and treated for free. Expert paediatric surgeons from the U.S. and KMC Hospital Attavar would be involved.

Associate Professor of Paediatric Surgery KMC Mangaluru Sadashiva Rao said patients of Anorectal malformation, Hirschsprungs disease, Vesicoureteric reflux, PUJ obstruction, Thoracic congenital anomalies and other types of unattended / correction paediatric surgery cases can be referred for programme. Head of Department of Orthopaedics Surendra Kamath said children from Shivamogga, Kodagu, Udupi, Chikkamagaluru, Uttara Kannada, Davangere, Kasaragod and Kannur districts have been screened for the the programme.

Manipal Academy of Higher Education will bear expenses of diagnostics and facilities, while Bantwal Ananth Mallya Charitable Trust will bear the cost of the medicine, consumables and food for the children.
