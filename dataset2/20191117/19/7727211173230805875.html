Tranmere Rovers fan arrested over homophobic abuse hurled during Wycombe Wanderers match

A 24-year-old man from Tranmere was arrested by Merseyside Police during the match this afternoon

17 Nov 2019, 19:06 by Charlotte Hadfield

A Tranmere Rovers fan was arrested on suspicion of spouting homophobic abuse at the referee during a match with the Wycombe Wanderers.

Police are also investigating allegations by the Wycombe Wanderers goalkeeper Ryan Allsop that he was subject to homophobic abuse.

Tranmere Rovers Football Club have said they are taking the matter "very seriously".

The 24-year-old man from Tranmere was arrested by Merseyside Police, following allegations that he made homophobic comments which were directed at the referee, during the Tranmere Rovers versus Wycombe Wanderers this afternoon, Sunday 17 November.

Meanwhile officers have also launched an investigation into a further allegation of homophobic abuse which was directed towards the Wycombe Wanderers goalkeeper Ryan Allsop, after he made an official complaint during the game, which Wycombe Wanderers won 2-0.

Police are working alongside both clubs and the FA to investigate the allegations and anyone who witnessed the incidents or recorded anything are being urged to come forward.

In a statement this evening, Tranmere Rovers Football Club said they are "very disappointed" to hear what had happened and that the club takes these matters "very seriously" with a zero tolerance policy.

A spokesperson, from Tranmere Rovers FC, said: "Tranmere Rovers Football Club are very disappointed to learn of a homophobic comment made during today’s match against Wycombe Wanderers.

"The Club takes matters like this very seriously with a zero tolerance policy.

"As a consequence of today's actions, one supporter has been arrested and the matter is being dealt with by the police.

"The Club will make no further comment whilst the police are dealing with this investigation."

According to the Mirror Online, the FA have also confirmed they are investigating the incident and awaiting a report from the referee before deciding what course to take.

Chief Inspector, Jason Crellin, of Merseyside Police, said: "We're grateful to the player, both clubs and the stewarding staff for intervening and reporting these abhorrent incidents immediately.

"I also want to reassure them and everyone present and watching on television that we will be working alongside both clubs and the authorities to fully investigate the allegations.

"If you were at the game and saw, heard or recorded anything, let us know and we will take positive action.

"Hate crime has no place in our communities, and not least at a showcase sporting event attended and watched by many people.

"We're determined to do all in our powers to identify anyone involved in such offences, and ensure they are put before the courts. We stand alongside the club in our resolve to ensure that any such incident is reported promptly and investigated thoroughly."

Anyone with information can contact @MerPolCC or 101 with reference 297 of 17 November. You can also contact Crimestoppers anonymously on 0800 555 111.
