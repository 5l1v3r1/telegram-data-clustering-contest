Luis Enrique's 2017 comments hint becoming next Arsenal manager is a big possibility

The Barcelona legend has been out of a job since leaving his post as Spain manager to be with his daughter before she tragically passed away in August

17 Nov 2019, 22:00 by Alex Milne

Former Barcelona manager Luis Enrique is reportedly one of the names being lined up to replace Unai Emery should Arsenal decide to sack the struggling Spaniard.

And judging by his comments in 2017, a job in England could appeal to Luis Enrique, who admitted he would like to manage in the country 'at some point.'

Emery is under huge pressure following a dreadful run of results for the Gunners which has seen them fail to record a victory in their last four Premier League games.

They now lie eight points outside the Champions League qualification places, and some reports have claimed time may be running out for Emery unless he turns it around soon.

Along with the likes of Jose Mourinho and Brendan Rodgers, Luis Enrique has been one of the names touted as a possible replacement for Emery, and in an interview with FourFourTwo two years ago the 49-year-old said a role with an English side has always appealed to him.

He was being linked with the Reading job at the time, but said: "I was cycling in South Africa when I got a call about Reading. It was gossip, I never spoke to them.

"I'd like to manage in England at some point. My wife wouldn't like the weather, she's from Barcelona and likes the sun. It would depend on the team and the football they played."

Luis Enrique won the treble with Barcelona in the 2014-2015 season, and in total won nine trophies in his three seasons as manager at the club.

He later went on to become Spain manager, but stepped down when his daughter was diagnosed with bone cancer and tragically died in August.

Emery's Arsenal have a big opportunity to get back to winning ways when they host struggling Southampton on Saturday.
