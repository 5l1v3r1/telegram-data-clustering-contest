NOT INTERESTED

Scotland boss Steve Clarke says he’s not looking for revenge against Kazakhstan

17 Nov 2019, 22:28 by Robert Grieve

STEVE CLARKE isn’t gunning for revenge over Kazakhstan this week — just three points.

Scotland were humiliated 3-0 in Astana back in March, a result that led to Alex McLeish’s sacking.

But Clarke — who has called up Kilmarnock’s Eamonn Brophy to replace injured Lawrence Shankland — is only interested in a third consecutive victory before the Euro 2020 play-off next March.

He said: “I’m not one for revenge. It’s an important game because we want to finish third.

"We also want to go into the play-off game on the back of three wins in a row and it’s a chance to do that.

“We want that feel-good factor and slowly it’s starting to come back.

“The fans will have gone home from Cyprus pleased after a 2-1 win.

“It will be nice to see a few of them back at Hampden on Tuesday.

“In the last few games I’ve made four or five changes. Right now I’m not envisaging I’ll make that many this time.”

Kenny McLean was injured for the Cyprus match but could come back into contention.

Clarke added: “He picked up a knock with his club but we didn’t pull him out the squad as we had enough withdrawals.

“Kenny has an outside chance of being available. We’ll assess what we have injury-wise and if we feel we need to pull Kenny back in we can do that.”
