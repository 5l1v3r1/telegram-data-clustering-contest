BOMB THREAT

Scots mum-of-six ‘petrified’ after hate mail yob threatened to petrol bomb home with cancer-stricken lad, 2, inside

17 Nov 2019, 22:54 by Stuart Patterson

A MUM of six yesterday told how she was sent a threat to petrol bomb her family home as her lad of two fought cancer.

Sarahjane Galbraith, 30, was left “petrified” after receiving chilling hate mail warning her to abandon a cash drive to help seriously-ill son Lachlan Stewart.

The culprit told her they would torch her house with all of her kids inside — including Lachlan who needed an emergency op to remove a football-sized tumour on his kidney.

Sarahjane, of Lossiemouth, Moray, said: “What kind of person targets a little boy who has cancer?

“The letters said our movements were being watched and petrol bombs were coming to the house. They made me feel scared to be in my home.

“They accused us of using Lachlan’s illness as a way of making money, which is not true.”

Sarahjane had just given birth to youngest child Billie-Jo when pals rallied round and started a campaign which raised £5,000 for stricken Lachlan and his loved ones.

But a mystery critic was so outraged by the community effort they started sending the family vile letters.

The first arrived weeks after Lachlan underwent a nine-hour op at the Queen Elizabeth Hospital in Glasgow on the stage four neuroblastoma.

Another labelled “final warning” read: “Making money from a seek bairn. Hold head in shame. This stops or boys cum burn vans and house.”

Alarmed Sarahjane and partner Jimmy Stewart, 43, called in cops who launched a probe.

Officers put their home under watch in a bid to catch the sender.

Police arrested a woman, 37, but are still investigating the notes.

Sarahjane said: “A friend started the fundraising so we could try and put a smile on Lachlan’s face when his treatment finished.

"Were they jealous? Is that why they tried to scare us when my boy had cancer and my baby girl was days old? It’s horrible.”

Lachlan’s treatment finished earlier this month and he got to ring a special bell at the Royal Aberdeen Children’s Hospital to mark the occasion.

He’d been stuck in wards since May last year so his parents are planning a bumper Christmas for him and siblings, Alistair, 11, Alisha, 10, Archie, 6, Sarahjane, 5, and little Billie-Jo.

Sarahjane is also planning to throw Lachlan a third birthday party and hopes to use the fundraising cash to take him on a trip to Blackpool.

She added: “The money is all for Lachlan and we haven’t touched a penny of it. When he rang that bell a weight lifted off my shoulders.

“This is going to be his first chance to properly celebrate his birthday.

“We’ve got superheroes coming and a bouncy castle. It will be a big day.”

A Police Scotland spokesman confirmed tonight: “A 37-year-old woman has been arrested and further enquiries are ongoing.”
