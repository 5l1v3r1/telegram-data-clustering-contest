Boris Johnson admits businesses didn't want Brexit as he begs for their backing

The shameless Tory leader will claim only his party - which has had two referendums, three general elections and three leaders in the last five years - can deliver 'certainty' to British firms

17 Nov 2019, 22:30 by Mikey Smith, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/mikey-smith/

Boris Johnson will admit that British businesses didn’t want Brexit as he pleads for their support in the general election .

The shameless Tory leader will claim only his party can deliver ‘certainty’ - despite the Conservatives having rammed through two referendums, three General Elections and three Prime Ministers in less than five years.

He’s set to give a speech to the Confederation of British Industry (CBI) - an industry body representing thousands of firms, which campaigned heavily against Brexit .

But Johnson is set to announce a raft of business tax cuts as a sweetener to help them ‘make the most’ of the Brexit they didn’t want.

The cuts will include reductions to business rates and National Insurance contributions.

He’ll make the announcement at the CBI’s annual conference in London on Monday.

 

“Let’s not beat around the bush, big business didn’t want Brexit,” Mr Johnson is expected to say.

“You made that clear in 2016 and this body said it louder than any other.“

 “But what is also clear is that what you want now - and have wanted for some time - is certainty.

 “So that you can plan and invest, so you can grow and expand, so that you can create jobs and drive prosperity.

 “Whilst you didn’t want it, the people did vote for it. And so it was for politicians to deliver it.”

 

He will go on to blame Parliament for his party’s inability to secure a Brexit deal - accusing MPs of “actively tried to sabotage the democratic will of the people”.

The Tory leader will also promise to increase tax relief for firms who buy, lease or construct buildings.

And he’ll promise to increase the rate of tax credits for research and development by 1%.

Dame Carolyn Fairbairn, the CBI's director general commented on Mr Johnson's refusal to extend the transition period - despite having failed to deliver on his October 31 deadline for Brexit.

She said: "One of the things I think is really important to understand is that if you are in business, living through the last three years, these rolling cliff-edges of climbing up a mountain with stockpiling and then climbing down again, could not be more damaging."

She added: "Let's make sure that we don't end up with yet another cliff-edge, possibly in June, or possibly at the end of December.

"Because every time we do that we take investment away from our country."
