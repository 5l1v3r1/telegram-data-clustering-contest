HOUSE DRAMA

Ayrshire man rushed to hospital after ‘confronting intruder in his home’ as cops make arrest

17 Nov 2019, 18:29 by Ewan Mowat

A MAN in his 60s has been rushed to hospital after allegedly confronting an intruder in his Ayrshire home this morning.

He was seriously hurt in the reported incident in Irvine around 11.15am.

A 26-year-old man has been arrested.

Cops swooped on Stronsay Court this morning, finding the resident badly hurt.

An ambulance rushed him to Crosshouse Hospital in nearby Kilmarnock.

His condition is described as serious but stable.

His injuries are not thought to be life threatening.

The 26-year-old was also taken to Crosshouse after being nicked.

A spokesperson for Police Scotland said: "Around 11.15am on Sunday 17 Nov 2019, police were called to Stronsay Court, Irvine, following a report that a man (in his 60s) had been injured.

"The injured man has been taken ambulance to Crosshouse Hospital in Kilmarnock for treatment.

"Medical staff describe serious but stable - not thought threat to life.

"A 26 year old man has been arrested in connection with the incident and has been taken to Crosshouse Hospital for assessment. Enquiries are continuing."
