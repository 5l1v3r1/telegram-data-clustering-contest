University of Regina Cougars' Michaela Kleisinger enjoys landmark weekend

17 Nov 2019, 18:16 by Regina Leader-Post

Michaela Kleisinger posted a double triple-double for the University of Regina Cougars women’s basketball team on the weekend.

She hit double digits in points, rebounds and assists to help the Cougars defeat the University of Winnipeg Wesmen on Friday and Saturday at the Centre for Kinesiology, Health and Sport.

On Friday, Kleisinger had 12 points, 11 rebounds and 11 assists in the Cougars’ 92-46 victory, becoming the first U of R player to register a triple-double in a Canada West game.

She followed up with 17 points, 12 rebounds and 11 assists Saturday as the Cougars won 84-51.

Entering the weekend, Madi Docherty, Jana Schweitzer and Joanna Zalesiak were the only Cougars female players to register an 11-assist game. Kleisinger then tied that record in back-to-back games.

Alexi Rowden added 15 points for the Cougars on Saturday. Emily Dewey added 14 points for the U of R, which got eight points and 11 rebounds from Macaela Crone.

The Cougars (3-3) are to oppose the University of Saskatchewan Huskies on Friday and Saturday in Saskatoon.

• • •

In men’s basketball, the Cougars also improved to 3-3 by sweeping the visiting Wesmen.

On Friday, Matt Barnard’s 20-foot jumper at the buzzer gave the Cougars a 72-71 win. Winnipeg had assumed a one-point lead with 2.5 seconds remaining in the fourth quarter.

Regina followed up with an 86-78 win on Saturday. The Cougars’ Carter Millar had his fourth double-double in six conference games this season, leading the team with 23 points and 11 rebounds.

The Cougars will visit the Huskies next weekend for a two-game set.

• • •

In women’s hockey, the Cougars and University of Alberta Pandas split a weekend set at the Co-operators Centre.

On Friday, Jane Kish made 21 saves for her third consecutive shutout as the Cougars won 2-0. Jaycee Magwood and Jordan Kulbida were the goal scorers.

Autumn MacDougall’s hat trick powered the Pandas to a 3-0 victory on Saturday.

The Cougars (3-7-2-0) will visit the Huskies on Friday before playing host to the U of S on Saturday (7 p.m., Co-operators Centre).

• • •

The Cougars’ women’s volleyball team is 1-7 after being swept by the Thompson Rivers WolfPack in Kamloops, B.C.

Game scores were 25-23, 25-22, 25-22 (Friday) and 25-14, 25-19, 25-11 (Saturday).
