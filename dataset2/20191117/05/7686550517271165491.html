These are the richest people in Wales you've probably never heard of

They have all made it to Wales Rich List 2019

17 Nov 2019, 05:00 by Marcus Hughes

Wales is home to plenty of monumentally wealthy people - many of whom have become household names.

Familiar people top the rich lists every year, including Celtic Manor Resort owner and telecoms magnate Sir Terry Matthew, Admiral insurance founder Henry Engelhardt, and Iceland co-founder Sir Malcolm Walker.

This year's Wales Rich List, published in the latest edition of Wales Business Insider, contains plenty of those recognisable faces, many of whom have had a huge impact on the economy and life in Wales.

But the list also includes a number of people you might not have heard of, in spite of their wealth.

A fortune of £21m was required to make it onto the top 100.

Sir Terry Matthews remains Wales' only billionaire, with his wealth rising to £1.31bn from £1.24bn thanks to success at the Celtic Manor Resort and his other companies.

But many of Wales' richest people are feeling the pinch, with 23 of the individuals and families who featured in this year's list experiencing a fall in the size of their fortunes.

Here are some of Wales Rich List 2019 names you might not have heard of:

Graham Anwyl (76) and family

Worth £77m

Anwyl Group was established in 1930 in Rhyl as a joinery and woodworking contractor and now operates as two divisions: Anwyl Homes and Anwyl Construction.

The company specialises in building large residential projects like care homes and housing developments.

The Ewloe based company enjoyed a record year with turnover growing 49%in 2017-18.

The fourth generation family run business is chaired by Graham Anwyl, and paid out a £2.5m dividend over the past 12 months.

Gerald Leeke (78) and family

Worth £66m

Leekes department store was founded at the end of the nineteenth century by James Henry Leeke.

Since then it has grown to include four department stores, a building centre in Tonypandy and a chain of conservatory concessions.

In 1994 Leekes Retail and Leisure Group bought The Vale Resort in Hensol, in the Vale of Glamorgan.

The family's department store chain dipped into the red in 2017-18, with turnover falling to £85m.

Michael McCarthy (77) and family

Worth £57m

Michael McCarthy set up property developer Rightacres in 1970 and chairs the business to this day, while his son Paul is chief executive.

The company is continuing to work as the exclusive developer of Cardiff's Central Square with more office space and apartments completed in recent years.

BBC Cymru Wales is currently in the process of relocating its headquarters to the site directly outside Cardiff Central train station.

Nicholas Griffith (55) and family

Worth £43m

Nicholas Griffith is the little-known owner of Cardiff's Philharmonic pub and Wyndham Arcade through his property firm G Capital.

His company has reputation for taking disused industrial sites and transforming them into brand new business parks.

William Watkins (53)

Worth £41m

William Watkins made his fortune as the managing director of Radnor Hills, the mineral water and fruit juice company.

The company started in 1989 after a young William returned back to his family farm from university and decided to diversify the business by bottling water on the family land - originally for the airline industry.

Radnor Hills installed their eighth production line last year and saw turnover top £42.1m in 2017/18.

Drew Nelson (64)

Worth £25m

Dr Drew Nelson co-found and CEO of IQE, a supplier of semiconductor components used to make smartphones.

The business was launched in 1988, and more than 30 years on the Cardiff-based technology firm has a stock market value of £443m.

The company have manufacturing plants in the UK, Taiwan, Singapore and America but their headquarters still remain firmly rooted in Cardiff.

Terry Edgell (53), Dilwyn Howells (54), and Nigel Williams (53)

Worth £24m each

Dilwyn Howells, Nigel Williams and Terry Edgell co-founded timber company Premier Forest Products in 1993.

After spending the first month working from Terry's house in Cwmbran, they moved to a number of new sites before settling at Alexandra Dock, Newport, where the headquarters still stand today.

The company supplies wood products for shopfitters, furniture makers, carpenters, caravan makers and a number of other customers.

The business reported profits of £9.3m and a turnover of £126.1m in 2017-18.

Robin Jones (51) and family

Worth £22m

Wrexham-based Village Bakery was established in 1934 and produces a range of bread and pies.

A fire devastated the main production facility earlier this year, but managing director Robin Jones told the media the company would "bounce back".

The fire came after a strong performance in 2017-18, with £4m profit.

Raj Aggarwal (70)

Worth £26m

Raj Aggarwal performs many roles.

Mr Aggarwal studied at Cardiff University before eventually buying his own small pharmacy on City Road and growing a chain.

The public health expert is also India’s Honorary Consul in Wales and last year became the first Indian-born recipient of the position of Honorary Lieutenant Commander in the Royal Navy.

He holds investments in cosmetic retailer Escentual, run by his son Rakesh, and operates Cardiff's Clarins salon.

Mario Kreft (63) and Gill Kreft (62)

Worth £28m

The Kefts own Pendine Park Care, which has eight care homes in Wrexham and Caernarfon with 440 residents.

The company made a steady £4.2m profit on £19.6m sales last year.

Jayne Lewis (54) and family

Worth £59m

Jayne Lewis owns the mining and development company Merthyr (South Wales) which runs the enormous Ffos-y-fran opencast mine just outside Merthyr Tydfil.

The family's company Gwent Holdings made £13.4m profit on £56.1m sales in 2017.

John Burns (70)

Worth £30m

John Burns developed his pet food company while working as a vet in 1993.

His food has been specifically designed to manage common health problem in cats, dogs and rabbits.

According to Inside Business Wales, the Kidwelly-based company's finances took a hit by an £850,000 donation to Burns' foundation in 2019-18 and a £9.2m payment to buy his ex-wife out from the company.

Steve Nicholls (58) and Jacqueline Nicholls (59)

Worth £23m

Steve Nicholls is managing director of Complete Core Business Solutions.

The Abercynon-based business packs and fills tobacco products and e-cigarettes.

Profits fell 20% to £1.9m last year with a turnover of £11.7m.

Alex Loven (32)

Worth £32m

Alex Loven is the youngest person on the 2019 Wales Rich List.

The 32-year-old has built one of the UK's largest online retailers of sports clothing and equipment.

In 2018 they had grown to 100 employees and supplied internationally renowned football clubs including Manchester United and Rangers FC.

Tim Batstone (60) and family

Worth £42m

Tim Batstone's company HB Leisure designs makes and sells theme skill park games - the type that you can win cuddly toys by winning, by, for example throwing balls at a hoop.

The firm's products have become fixtures at Legoland, Alton Towers, Drayton Manor, Blackpool Tower and Madame Tussauds.

Alexander Smart (46) and Gareth Smart (40)

Worth £60m

Brothers Alexander and Gareth Smart run JR Smart (Builders), a Cardiff-based construction company.

The firm was founded by their father John and has developed 2.5 million sq ft of commercial property.

Their recent projects include the development of Cardiff's Capital Quarter.

Company profits rose to £8.2m in 2017.
