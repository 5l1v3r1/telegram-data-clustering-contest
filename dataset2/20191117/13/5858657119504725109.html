Striking workers at Rosewood Hotel Georgia reach tentative deal

17 Nov 2019, 13:23 by Lisa Steacy

Summary

According to a spokesperson for the hotel, the tentative deal was reached Saturday, after a 29-hour bargaining session

A spokesperson for the hotel says the deal addresses safety concerns with panic buttons, program to prevent harassment

VANCOUVER (NEWS 1130) — After 58 days behind picket lines, workers at the Rosewood Hotel Georgia have reached a tentative agreement to end the strike.

According to a spokesperson for the hotel, the tentative deal was reached Saturday, after a 29-hour bargaining session.

“The Hotel has implemented a workplace safety program that the Union has called ‘industry-leading’ and includes programs specifically designed to prevent harassment and discrimination and empower our employees around these issues. Rosewood Hotel Georgia has now become the first Union-represented hotel in Vancouver to broadly install panic buttons covering the majority of employees across multiple departments,” says a news release issued Sunday.
