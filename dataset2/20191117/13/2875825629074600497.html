Man Utd and Liverpool interested in Wigan teenager Joe Gelhardt ahead of January transfer window

Highly-rated teenager Joe Gelhardt is wanted by Man Utd, Liverpool, Man City, Tottenham, Everton and Chelsea after impressing for Wigan in the Championship

17 Nov 2019, 13:31 by Irishmirror.ie

Manchester United and Liverpool lead a six team queue for Wigan's highly rated forward Joe Gelhardt.

Teenage starlet Gelhardt has the caught the attention of Ole Gunnar Solskjaer and Jurgen Klopp, alongside Manchester City , Tottenham , Everton and Chelsea , according to the Daily Star.

The 17-year-old, who was born in Liverpool , has netted once in six Championship appearances for Wigan to send Premier League scouts purring.

Gelhardt is currently rated art around £5million.

He made his debut as a 78th minute substitute in the Latics' Carabao Cup tie with Rotherham last August, before making his league bow in April.

Gelhardt has been a regular in the England youth set-up having turned out for the Under 16s, 17s and 18s - who he currently captains.

Gelhardt signed a fresh 'long term' contract at the DW Stadium on Saturday and Wigan's executive chairman Darren Royle praised him for his progression through the club.

“Creating a pathway from academy to first team is a priority of our manager and our owners and Joe is certainly a talent that has benefited from that philosophy,” said Royle.

“He is an exciting talent who has already shown this season what he can bring to this football club.

“Joe, his family and his representatives believe Wigan Athletic is the best place for Joe to continue his development and play football.

“I must once again place on record my gratitude to the club’s CEO Jonathan Jackson for his long-term belief in the development of our own young players, which is continuing to bear fruits and to our academy manager Gregor Rioch, Kenny Williams [Head of Academy Recruitment] and the staff at the academy for their hard work with all of our young players on a daily basis.

"I know this is news our academy staff will celebrate as another achievement and recognition of their passion for their work.”
