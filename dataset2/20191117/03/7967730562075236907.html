Perth's most iconic buildings open their doors for Open House

17 Nov 2019, 03:32

The District Court of WA is giving the chance for visitors to see where some of the state's most notorious prisoners sit awaiting court appearances.

The District Court is once again participating in Open House Perth this Sunday until 4pm and one of the most popular features is a tour of the custody centre.

The court gives visitors a behind-the-scenes look at the cells where accused people are held when they attend court.

The public will also see the state’s first purpose-built, high security courtroom and judicial staff will be there to answer questions about the court, which next year celebrates its 50th anniversary.

District Court Judge Christopher Stevenson organises the open day and said visitors gain insights and valuable information about how the court works.

“It is very important that the community feel that they can have access to their courthouses
and this is a particularly significant courthouse because it’s purpose-built and it’s where all
jury trials, except a few in the Supreme Court, are conducted,” he said.

Last year, 477 trials were listed at the District Court and about 15,000 members of
the public were required to attend for jury service.

Open House Perth happens every November and allows the public to go inside some of Perth's most iconic buildings.

Office blocks, stunning houses to modern civic spaces are all on the list. This year the Barrack Street Arch is also open for a stickybeak.

Now in its eighth consecutive year, 400,000 people have taken part in Open House Perth events since it began in 2012.

The District Court will be open until 4pm Sunday.
