Dabo Swinney Comments On Injury To Alabama QB Tua Tagovailoa

17 Nov 2019, 03:19 by Andrew McCarty

On Saturday afternoon, Alabama’s star quarterback suffered a season-ending injury.

Tua Tagovailoa, who entered the game with a questionable designation, was carted off the field after dislocating his hip. He was flown to a hospital in Birmingham where he was diagnosed with a dislocated hip and partial hip fracture.

It’s a devastating blow for Tua, his family and the Alabama football program.

But one college football coach knows it’s much more than that. It’s a blow to football as a whole.

Clemson head coach Dabo Swinney raved about Tua when asked about the quarterback’s season-ending injury.

Here’s what he had to say, via The State:

“Obviously that’s a big loss for them. It’s a big loss for college football, because Tua, he’s a special player. You hate to play against him, but you have great respect for guys that you know do things the right way. He’s just the epitome of a winner. Just a great winner. I hate that for him, but I’m sure they’ve got great doctors and he’ll bounce back. It won’t be the last time you see Tua,” Swinney said.

Tua’s college career could be over, but he’s expected to fully recover from the devastating injury, which is great news for his pro prospects.
