Mum's warning after she saw her toddler 'turn lifeless' after taking nicotine gum

"He couldn't lift his arms and his eyes were rolling"

17 Nov 2019, 14:48 by Anna Lewis, Geoffrey Bennett

A mum is warning of the dangers of nicotine gum after her toddler chewed some and turned "lifeless".

Sophie Balint's son Tommy, two, put some of the gum in his mouth and suffered nicotine poisoning.

A neighbour trained in first-aid gave initial help before Tommy was taken to hospital and recovered.

Sophie, 23, said the drama unfolded after Tommy and big brother Ozzy, four, found nicotine gum in her Port Talbot home.

She told Wales Online: "We were redecorating my mother's house and the boys went in the bedroom and must have just thought it was chewing gum.

"It was in their mouths for a couple of minutes and the next thing Tommy starting crying and saying he had a bad belly.

"Within five minutes he started being sick and within about 15 or 20 minutes he was lifeless lying on the floor.

"He couldn't lift his arms and his eyes were rolling.

"Luckily the next door neighbour was a first aider so he came to help.

"He was on the phone to the ambulance and we had to lie him on the sofa flat and keep him awake."

Large amounts of nicotine can prove "life threatening" to children.

Symptoms of nicotine toxicity overdose or poisoning including vomiting, sweating, drowsiness, shaking, confusion and seizures.

By the time an ambulance arrived Tommy had come round.

Sophie said: "The doctors said 80% of it had gone by that time.

"It was only 4mg nicotine so that was quite low.

"They said if he had chewed the whole packed it could have shut his organs down.

"They did some tests to check his heart and check his blood but he was back to normal.

"The doctor who saw Tommy was shocked, he had never seen anything like this. He had no idea how toxic it is."

'It was so scary'

She described what happened as terrifying.

She added: "In the house you wouldn't keep paracetamol in a drawer but there might be chewing gum.

"People don't know about this. It was so scary."
