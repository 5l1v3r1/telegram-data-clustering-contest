Liverpool face Virgil van Dijk wait ahead of Palace after defender pulls out of Holland squad

The Liverpool defender will miss the upcoming Euro 2020 qualifier

17 Nov 2019, 14:03 by Andy Ha

Liverpool defender Virgil van Dijk has pulled out of the Netherlands squad ahead of their upcoming European Championship qualifier against Estonia.

Van Dijk captained Holland to a 0-0 draw against Northern Ireland on Saturday night as they sealed qualification for next summer's tournament.

But on Sunday, the Dutch FA confirmed that the 28-year-old has left the squad due to "personal circumstances", and would miss the game.

It remains to be seen if the withdrawal will affect his availability for Liverpool's clash against Crystal Palace after the international break.

Jurgen Klopp's side opened up an eight point lead at the top of the Premier League table with a 3-1 win over Manchester City last week, and they will bid to continue their unbeaten start at Selhurst Park.

Despite the points advantage, Van Dijk has outlined how Klopp keeps his players grounded by telling them to focus on each game as it comes.

He said: “Of course I can sense how much winning the title is ­spoken about.

“For our fans, winning the championship has been way too long, so they place the Premier League title now above everything.

“No competition is more ­important than this one, but we need to keep the fever outside.

“Klopp tells us every week, ‘Focus on the next match’.

“We are doing alright, but it is only November. The hardest months of football are still waiting.”

The Reds are waiting to see if Mohamed Salah, Andrew Robertson, Joe Gomez and Jordan Henderson will recover in time for the trip to London, after they all left their respective international camps this week.

Salah, in particular, is a big concern after receiving an extended period of time off to recover from an ongoing ankle problem.

Klopp recently discussed Salah's injury, stating: "I think it is more really annoying - for him, not me - the fact that it always has a bit of fluid in, and he has to deal with it, we get it out and it comes back, and stuff like that."
