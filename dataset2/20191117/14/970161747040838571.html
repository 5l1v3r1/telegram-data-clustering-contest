Liverpool star Virgil van Dijk leaves Netherlands squad 'for personal reasons'

17 Nov 2019, 14:01

Liverpool defender Virgil van Dijk has left the Netherlands squad due to ‘personal reasons’ and will not play in their final Euro 2020 qualifier against Estonia on Tuesday.

The centre-back skippered his nation at Windsor Park against Northern Ireland on Saturday night, helping to secure the one point his side needed to qualify for next summer’s tournament courtesy of a 0-0 draw.

Although the Netherlands can still qualify as group winners if they beat Estonia and Germany lose, their final qualifying match has effectively become a dead rubber.

And they will have to play the match without their captain, with a statement from the Dutch national team confirming he had been given permission to leave the squad early.

The announcement explained: ‘National team coach Ronald Koeman cannot rely on VirgiI van Dijk on Tuesday against Estonia.

‘The captain of the Dutch national team has to let go of the game due to personal circumstances and has since left the Orange training camp.’

Van Dijk is not believed to be injured despite managing several niggles this season, and played the full 90 minutes against Northern Ireland.

The former Southampton defender recently spoke about his relationship with Liverpool manager Jurgen Klopp and credited the German with getting the best out of him.

‘I have a very similar connection with Jurgen Klopp at Liverpool as I had earlier with Ronald Koeman at Southampton and I still have with Koeman with the national team,’ said Van Dijk last week.

‘Like Koeman, Klopp knows exactly how to get the best out of me – by being critical. When the media are hyping me and being very positive, he will downplay the praise all that – often with a wink.’
