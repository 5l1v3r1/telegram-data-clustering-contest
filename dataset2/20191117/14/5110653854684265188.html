What channel is NASCAR on today? Time, TV schedule for championship race at Homestead

17 Nov 2019, 14:18 by Tadd Haislop

What channel is the NASCAR race on today? What time does the NASCAR race start? These are the questions that have plagued race fans every Sunday,when each Monster Energy NASCAR Cup Series races were scheduled to be shown oneither NBC or NBCSNfor the secondhalf of the 2019 seasonwith differing start times.

As for Sunday's Ford EcoBoost 400at Homestead-Miami Speedway, the Cup Series' onlyvisit to the track allyear and the season finale that will decide the 2019 champion,the channel that will broadcast the raceis NBC. The race at Homestead will start at 3p.m. ET.

The 3p.m. ET start time for therace at Homesteadon Sunday is the third suchstart time over the last three weeksof theseason. As for the TV channel, the NASCAR Cup Series season finaleis the seventh race on NBC this year.As part of NBC'sTV deal with NASCAR for the 2019 season, NBC wasslated to show seven of the final 20 races of the season, with the other 13 scheduled to be shown on NBCSN.

Sunday's race at Homestead,which will consist of threestages (80laps, 80laps and 107laps),is the 36th and finalrace on the NASCAR Cup Series schedule. The Championship 4, which was set last week in Phoenix, consists of Kyle Busch, Martin Truex Jr., Denny Hamlin and Kevin Harvick.

Homestead-Miami Speedway is a 1.5-mile oval that features 18-20 degree banking in the turns and four-degree banking on the identical, 1,760-foot stretches. Joey Logano won this race last year to take home the 2018 NASCAR Cup Series championship.
