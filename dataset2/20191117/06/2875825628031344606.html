Family of ISIS bride Lisa Smith want her to tell her story on RTE's Late Late Show when she returns to Ireland

EXCLUSIVE: Sources have indicated that the former Irish Army soldier turned ISIS bride, 38, will be arrested as soon as she touches down

17 Nov 2019, 06:00 by Sylvia Pownall

Lisa Smith’s family want her to tell her story in a closed set interview on the Late Late Show when she returns to Ireland.

Sources have indicated that the former Irish Army soldier turned ISIS bride, 38, will be arrested as soon as she touches down at Baldonnel Aerodrome on Tuesday.

Family spokesman Peter Fitzpatrick said: “They’ve been approached by the Late Late Show and other media all looking for interviews, but Lisa told them she wants to give her own version.”

The family have said they“don’t know what to expect” when she returns home next week.

Ms Smith, from Dundalk in Co Louth, is due to be repatriated from Turkey and will face a security assessment and possible prosecution.

Ms Smith was detained in northern Syria in March along with her two-year-old daughter, after ISIS’ downfall, on suspicion of supporting the terror group.

Family spokesperson Peter Fitzpatrick TD told the Irish Sunday Mirror: “They [Lisa’s family] literally don’t know what is going to happen.

“They are afraid the child will be taken off her. She has said she will answer any questions whatsoever from gardai or security.

“The family don’t know what to expect – basically what kind of reception she’s going to get or whether she’ll be arrested.

“There’s been a lot of speculation but she just wants to come home to her country with her child, she’s very concerned for her child.”

Lisa is believed to have escaped a detention camp with IS recruiter Tooba Gondal in mid-October and was held in a rebel
safehouse on the Turkey-Syria border where she begged to come home.

The women reportedly walked for miles with their children after fleeing Ain Issa camp when they were picked up by rebel fighters with the Syrian National Army.

Earlier this year photographs emerged of Ms Smith posing with guns but she claims she has never been a member of the terror group.

Mr Fitzpatrick said: “There are a lot of rumours about her.

“She has been in touch with her family, mainly by text. She says she misses home and wants to come home.

“Her mother has never seen her grandchild and she can’t wait to see the child.

“There have been threats, yes. The reason the family don’t go public and
talk is they’re afraid they might say something that makes it worse.

“Lisa has told them when she comes home she will meet the public and give her version of events.

“They have been approached by RTE, The Late Late and other media all looking for interviews.

“They want to wait for Lisa to come home and give her version herself.

“She says she’s innocent, she has an opportunity when she comes home to prove that.”

Some residents in Dundalk are said to be uneasy about her return, with reports some have written to their local council to express their outrage.

One source said: “She can’t just rock up as if she did nothing. People are genuinely upset about this.”

RTE has been contacted for comment.
