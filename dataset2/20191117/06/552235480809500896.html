John McGinn on Aston Villa myths he's debunked and the Jack Grealish hobby he doesn't rate

The midfielder is loving proving everyone wrong in the Premier League.

17 Nov 2019, 06:00 by Scott McDermott, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/scott-mcdermott/

A rat and a runner – that’s how John McGinn viewed himself when he played in Scotland.

As humble a footballer as you’ll come across, self-deprecation comes naturally to him.

But that’s how he was pigeonholed as a kid at St Mirren and later on at Hibs.

A grafter, an ankle biter, a midfield buzz-bomb who got around the pitch at 100mph.

But watch him now for Aston Villa, playing further forward in Dean Smith’s side and supporting his front players.

Sure, he’s still got the energy and work ethic he’s always had.

But it’s more controlled now, more measured.

If you run about like a headless chicken in a league with the likes of Kevin de Bruyne, Fabinho, Youri Tielemans and David Silva, they’ll play around you.

Instead, McGinn has learned to pick his moments.

With the likes of Jack Grealish and Douglas Costa beside him in Villa’s engine room, he can afford to.

And he’s reaping the benefits. Not only is he building a reputation as one of the Premier League’s top midfielders, he’s also cemented his place in Steve Clarke’s Scotland line-up.

 

Now the national boss is also deploying him in an advanced role – which saw him net three against San Marino in October and one against Cyprus yesterday.

McGinn would have laughed if you’d called him a creative, attacking midfielder a few years ago.

But not now. Thanks to Smith’s coaching, quality team-mates and the intensity of England’s top-flight, he has changed his game.

And as McGinn prepares to take on Kazakhstan at Hampden on Tuesday night, the 25-year-old said: “If you thought I could play further forward a few years ago, I’d have laughed.

“I probably got sucked into the idea in Scotland that I was just a rat and a runner.

“But Dean has given me the confidence to get involved higher up the park and I’m enjoying it.

“I’m still learning and I have so much to improve on if I want to play in that position at Premier League level.

“But it’s good that I’m getting games there at my club to hopefully benefit me with Scotland.

“When I played for Hibs, it was probably the most erratic I’ve ever been. But at Villa, you’re not allowed to do that. You have a certain role in the team.

“At Hibs, there were times when I tried to do a lot of things myself.

“I wasn’t as well drilled or coached as I am now. I’ve learned more about the game down here.

“I pick my times not to go and chase opponents. I used to do it too much.

“Now, I just listen to the coaches. The manager has been brilliant for me. He’s told me to trust in myself and show a bit of character.

“I feel I’ve added that to my game now. It’s hard to get a
balance because I don’t want to lose what I’m known for, the energy and bustle.

“I need to be the same type of player – but fine tune it. Down south, you see players getting older with so much coaching and they’re scared to do things.

“I’m not keen to be overly coached and I still want to separate myself from other players in the league because I definitely feel I can offer something different.

“Don’t ask about my shooting, though. I shoot a lot more now and get pelters off the lads for it.

“But I need to get my fantasy football points up.”

McGinn’s humour is just one of the reasons why he’s now one of the most popular figures in the Aston Villa dressing room.

A blossoming friendship with Villa skipper and England hopeful Grealish is testament to that.

His impact should be proof that Scotland can boast talented players but McGinn admits that it’s still a battle convincing some people.

He said: “Their criticism of Scottish football doesn’t hurt me. It gives me motivation
to prove them wrong every weekend. I’m craving success with the national team so I can finally go back down there with a big smile on my face.

“Too many times I’ve gone down the road off the back of a poor result. But we know we
have the ability in the squad to change that.

“English people’s view of the Scottish game is something I don’t think we’ll ever change.

“But Celtic and Rangers’ results against the likes of Lazio and Porto in Europe helps me an awful lot when I go to training on a Friday morning.

“Jack is someone I’ve hit it off with. We have similar interests. Not sunbeds, though – he’s the good looking one.

“But we both love our football so we stay at the training ground to do a bit extra or watch games when they’re on.

“He’s just a good guy. I get on with most of the boys but he’s of a similar age so I cut about with Jack a lot.”

One of those poor Scotland results McGinn talks about was the 3-0 humiliation in Kazakhstan that kicked off our dismal Euro 2020 campaign.

That defeat all but ended our hopes of qualifying via the conventional route – and signalled the end of Alex McLeish’s second reign as gaffer.

For McGinn, it was his worst night in a dark blue shirt – and it’s why he’s determined to put it right in midweek.

He said: “That night was probably the lowest point in my Scotland career.

“It was such an important game but the Scotland team went down without a whimper.

“It still hurts and is a massive reason why we can’t qualify from the group. It couldn’t have gone any worse.

“This game gives us an opportunity to put it right.

“All the Scottish fans want to see are players giving everything.

“We’ve not given them enough to smile about and that’s what we want to change.”
