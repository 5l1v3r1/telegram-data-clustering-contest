Neil Lennon admits Rangers' return to challenge Celtic is the fire that fuels his relentless trophy hunt

The Parkhead boss is enjoying his second stint in the job and reveals the circumstances that drove his departure the first time around.

17 Nov 2019, 06:00 by Gordon Waddell, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/gordon-waddell/

When Neil Lennon first sent shockwaves around Europe as Celtic boss – he had ambitions further afield.

After losing the core of his team that beat Barcelona in the Champions League in 2012, the Parkhead gaffer also wanted out.

After a dire stint at Bolton that forced him to rebuild his reputation at Hibs, most people would expect Lennon to regret that decision.

But he doesn’t. Not even after two sensational victories over Lazio in the Europa League, including that historic 2-1 win in Rome.

Lennon refuses to look back. Instead, those results have made him realise that he never wants to leave the Hoops of his own accord ever again.

He appreciates what he’s got at Celtic – and that’s why he’s savouring every moment of their current run.

As well as their European exploits, Lennon is embroiled in a title fight with Rangers again – something he didn’t have back in 2014 when the Ibrox club were in Scotland’s lower leagues.

As he reflected on his first stint as manager and what transpired, he said: “Have the Lazio nights made me ask myself why I left here in the first place?

“Listen, I had my reasons for leaving at that time.

“I don’t regret it because it was the right thing for me to do at that moment in my career.

“I was ambitious then and wanted to try other things. But I feel differently now.

“After Barcelona we changed the squad, sold a lot of good players – and there was no Rangers.

“In my last year, we won the league by 29 points so you’re thinking: ‘Is it time for me to change and time for the club to make a change as well.’

“Maybe it needed a new voice because four years is a long time in modern football at a club like this.

“So it was a good time to go.

“And I left on the best of terms so the door would maybe be open again in the future. You can’t predict that but thankfully that’s how it’s turned out.

“And having this opportunity again is fantastic, I’m really enjoying it.

“I wanted to bring those type of big nights back to Celtic Park. And the Lazio win was the perfect storm for a European game at Parkhead.

“The game matched the atmosphere, occasion, performance and result.

“We got a taste against Cluj at home but Lazio was even better. And I want more of it.

“Once you get a taste, you want the players and fans to get more nights like that.

“It makes me never want to leave again. I’m older and wiser now.

“Now, I’ve got a second crack at it, I’m really grateful for the opportunity.”

Lennon is now embracing everything that comes from being Celtic manager.

Brendan Rodgers was a ridiculously hard act to follow.

But the adulation he received in the Olympic Stadium at full-time was confirmation that he’d achieved it.

He’s now got a Europa League last-32 clash, a Betfred Cup Final – and a mouthwatering Premiership title race to look forward to.

Lennon said: “I inherited a good squad but we’ve brought in quality players. So I’m loving what I’m doing.

“It’s a high-pressure job but I’m embracing the challenge.

“We’re top of the league, top of our Europa League group and in a cup final so I can’t ask more of the players.

“Also, the fact that Rangers are back challenging, that drives me on too. It heightens the senses and pushes me on. The way we’re playing right now is fantastic.

 

“With these big games we’ve got coming up, I can’t ask for much more as a manager.

“I can’t imagine a feeling like we had against Lazio anywhere else in football. That’s just what Celtic brings.

“I talk to the players a lot about that feeling.

“It’s not about money or medals, it’s about the feeling you get after a performance like that.

“Only those players as individuals can experience that glow of complete satisfaction.

“It doesn’t last long in this game – but that’s what you play for. When you hear Lazio boss Simone Inzaghi talking about us and the atmosphere at Celtic Park, it’s a great compliment. He spoke really well after our home game.

“It made me proud of the club and it meant a hell of a lot to me.”

One of those quality players Lennon talks about is £7million centre-back Christopher Jullien.

The former Toulouse star is a symbol of the new Lennon era at Celtic – and was pivotal in both victories over Lazio.

The manager always believed the Frenchman would be a standout for his new-look side but still wants more. The Hoops gaffer said: “I always liked Christopher’s physicality, aerial threat and defensive capabilities.

“And we’re now seeing that manifest itself in his performances.

“He should actually have scored a few more goals by now but he’s adapting to the British game well and has performed in Europe too.

“Christopher’s in a good place, and enjoying the intensity of being at Celtic.

“At first, it’s a bit of a shock but players grow into it then it becomes an exciting environment to be in.

“That’s what he’s beginning to experience now.”
