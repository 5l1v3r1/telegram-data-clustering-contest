Carol Vorderman pours scorn on Prince Andrew's denials in extraordinary interview

Prince Andrew has been on the receiving end of social media backlash following his BBC Newsnight interview - and Carol Vorderman is unconvinced

17 Nov 2019, 15:01 by Irishmirror.ie

Carol Vorderman has added her own scathing comments as she waded into the backlash against Prince Andrew .

During his BBC Newsnight interview with Emily Maitlis centred around his friendship with Jeffrey Epstein - which has since been branded "disastrous" - the former Countdown star poured scorn on the royal.

In a series of tweets a "shocked" Carol wrote that the 45-minute interview was "car crash" viewing and she was left floored at the series of statements the Queen's third child made.

"I'm truly so shocked that my brain is saying 'you didn't just see or hear what you think you just saw and heard'... #PrinceAndrew So where can I watch that car crash again just to check?" she remarked.

The Duke of York defended his reputation and made the decision to speak on the record about his friendship with Epstein , who was found hanged in a New York jail cell in August while awaiting a sex trafficking trial.

Epstein, 66, previously admitted prostituting a minor in 2008 and spent 13 months in prison after agreeing a plea bargain.

In 2015, Andrew was dragged into the scandal when one of Epstein's victims alleged she had been forced to have sex with him when she was just 17 - which the prince categorically denies.

Pilot and racehorse owner Carol wrote of Prince Andrew's comments about Epstein as the Saturday night interview aired.

"So I didn't speak to him for 4 years because it wasn't appropriate, but when he was released from prison for sex offences I didn't ring him..no... I flew to America to tell him in person that I STILL wasn't going to talk to him...RUN THAT BY ME AGAIN."

She continued while also responding to other people watching the interview: "Then after he was released for sex offences, & even tho I hadn't spoken to him for 4 yrs, I wanted to 'show leadership' so I went for a walk with him in Central Park to tell him I STILL couldn't speak to him but somehow had a 4 night sleepover in his house....?!?!?! #PrinceAndrew."

Seeming baffled at what she as watching, Carol added alongside questioning face emojis: "I stayed at his home on a 4 night sleepover to tell him that I didn't want to be his friend anymore. even tho I hadn't spoken to him for 4 years as he'd been in jail for sex offences BUT I only saw him for a walk..Oh and a dinner party..Oh and in the passage #PrinceAndrew."

During the programme Prince Andrew denied dancing with Virginia at upmarket nightclub Tramp because he was at a Pizza Express in Woking at the time, as well as several other baffling claims.

One PR guru branded the TV stint "disastrous" and "like watching a man in quicksand" as millions tuned in to watch host Emily Maitlis grill the royal.

PR and crisis consultant Mark Borkowski said it was a lesson on "how not to do" public relations.

"I have never seen anything so disastrous. For any students of PR, that is how not to do it," he said.

A body language expert also said Prince Andrew was being 'evasive and potentially deceptive' during the sit down.

Experienced body language expert and psychologist Darren Stanton watched Andrew with a laser-like scrutiny as the 59-year-old broke his silence on the topic of the allegations and Epstein.

"The behaviour I observed during the documentary is consistent with someone being evasive and potentially deceptive," Mr Stanton told MirrorOnline.

"I am not convinced the account was consistent or congruent with the true emotion of the Prince."
