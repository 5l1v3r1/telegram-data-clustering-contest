Birmingham set for bone-chilling Polar Vortex as temperatures plunge to -1C

The mercury is going to plummet to single digits for much of the UK this week with Birmingham expected to see lows of -1°C Monday night into Tuesday morning

17 Nov 2019, 15:28 by Katie Brooks, https://www.birminghammail.co.uk/authors/katie-brooks/

Birmingham is in for freezing temperatures as a "collapsed polar vortex" is predicted to bring "severe weather" to Europe.

Snowstorms have ravaged southeast France and meteorologists are warning that it will have a knock-on effect in Britain.

The mercury is going to plummet to single digits for much of the UK this week with the West Midlands expected to see lows of -1°C Monday night into Tuesday morning.

The Met Office then expects temperatures to start warming up again into the higher single figures as the week progresses.

Meteorologist Andrej Flis told the   Express : “The weakening of the stratospheric polar vortex is well underway.

“In the past days, the polar vortex reached 40-year record strength for this time of year. It would likely strengthen even further, but its rise to power was stopped and reversed, due to two pressure/temperature waves that press onto the vortex.

That reduces the speed of the stratospheric polar jet stream.

“That is a strong sign of a collapsed polar vortex, and high probability of a sudden stratospheric warming event by the end of the year.

“What all this means for our weather? Well, a collapsed vortex always greatly increases the chances for winter weather towards Europe.”
