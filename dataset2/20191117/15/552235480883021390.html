Dario Del Fabro's debt to ex-Rangers star Rino Gattuso as he hails Scottish football

The 24-year-old joined Kilmarnock on a seaosn long loan deal and revealed why he's a fan of Scottish football.

17 Nov 2019, 15:47 by Mark McDougall, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/mark-mcdougall/

Dario Del Fabro has admitted his loan move to Kilmarnock came about after learning about Scottish football from Rino Gattuso.

Gattuso spent a year at Rangers in 1997/98 and Del Fabro reckons that he owes his career to that time in Glasgow.

Del Fabro played under Gattuso during a loan spell from Cagliari at Pisa in 2016/17 before moving to Juventus.

And chats with the 41-year-old AC Milan legend is what prompted his interest in Scottish football.

Speaking about the former Rangers midfielder, he told the Times: "It was here that he became a man, became a player."

Having had loan spells in the second tier of Italian football, Del Fabro wanted to try something different and the 24-year-old moved to Scotland.

He's impressed at the heart of defence for the Rugby Park club and admits the style of football suits him.

He said: "In Serie B, everyone knows me and I know everyone. I wanted to try something different and find a new challenge.

"The Scottish league I know very well thanks to Gattuso, but I'd also followed it myself and knew it had been a great launch pad for many great players. I knew Kilmarnock was one of the oldest and most important clubs and I've found out that the fans are very warm but also quite demanding.

"These things have been frustrating and in the matches where we've had half a defence we've struggled a bit but my time in Scotland has already been a big step forward for me.

"Perhaps in Italy there is a little bit more quality, but the game here is a lot more dynamic. There's a lot more energy, a lot more aggression. Italian football is very slow and tactical, whereas here you can't breathe for even a second and I'm loving it."

Killie started the season terribly under Angelo Alessio but have turned it around and being a tougher team to face.

And the centre back has an interesting comparison for Kilmarnock's players as he said: "We're very compact and the whole team knows how to defend. We can make quick attacks but our strength is our compactness. We're like bricklayers. We do a dirty, hard job, but it's getting results.

"I'm loving it here, so maybe the boss will speak about it with the club and with Juventus and we'll see what happens. The career of a footballer is very strange because in a year anything can happen and many things can change."
