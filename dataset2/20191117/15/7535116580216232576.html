Many Borneo pygmy elephants had been killed because of poaching and hunting for their tusks. In order to stop this cruel act from occurring, the Sabah authorities had been working hard in protecting these endangered elephants.

As of yesterday (16 November), a female pygmy elephant was found dead at the Kinabatangan plantation, Kota Kinabalu. Its carcass was found by plantation workers, and several lizards were feeding on the body at 5:50 am. After the discovery, a report was made to the Sabah Wildlife Department (SWD).
