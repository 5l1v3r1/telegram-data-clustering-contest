Body found in search missing Whitburn chef Jonathon Donaldson

17 Nov 2019, 16:32 by Ewan Mowat

A BODY has been found in the search for missing West Lothian man Jonathon Donaldson.

The 26-year-old vanished last Monday after leaving his work at The Oatbridge Hotel in Uphall.

It is thought the chef then got a bus towards Whitburn, but it isn't known where he get off and loved ones hadn't seen him since.

His family launched a search party to try and trace him.

But today police confirmed they had found a body.

The body has not been formally identified but Jonathon's family have been made aware.

We previously told how the sister of Jonathon had shared a heartfelt plea for her brother to return home.

His sister Cassandra wrote on Facebook: "If anyone sees my brother Johny Donaldson can you please contact the family or police.

"He is missing and not been in touch with anyone since Monday and nobody has seen him."

She added: "This is so heartbreaking for myself and my family and we are extremely concerned for his welfare."

It is understood that an eyewitness reported seeing Jonathon leaving the hotel before boarding a bus.

Casandra previously said: "We have received information from eyewitnesses to say Johny was seen leaving the Oatridge Hotel in Uphall to go to the Scotmid situated across the road to buy a scratch card.

"He was then boarding a number 600 bus around 4:15pm on November 11th that goes towards Whitburn in the West Lothian area.

"We do not know at present what stop he got off at."
