Waxing salon owner reflects on her success and growth as 10 year anniversary approaches

There are now two branches of nkd, in Nottingham and Leicester

17 Nov 2019, 16:52 by Matthew Bunn

A waxing salon owner has paid tribute to her customers as she prepares to celebrate 10 years since opening her first shop .

The last decade has been a whirlwind for entrepreneur Rebecca Dowdeswell, who got married and fell pregnant in between incorporating nkd in June 2009 and opening the first salon in Bridlesmith Walk that December.

The 43-year-old admits the last 10 years have been a tough time to start and build a company, but today she is employing about 15 people and has two branches in Nottingham and Leicester.

“I feel so proud of what we have created,” she said.

“Approaching the 10 year anniversary has made me reflect on what things were like in 2009 and so many businesses that are not around now.

“It has been a really hard decade to build a retail business and survive and grow and I feel really proud we have been able to do that while, unfortunately, an awful lot of businesses haven’t been able to make it through those tough times.

“I feel really proud.”

Mrs Dowdeswell didn’t have a natural journey towards starting nkd.

After leaving university she spent some time working in Brazil, where she was first introduced to waxing.

When she returned to the UK in 2000, she says “salons were doing (waxing) very badly and it was hard to get a good wax here”.

She then later travelled around Asia where she found a host of specialist waxing salons, where the idea for nkd first formed.

But it was not until after the credit crunch in 2007/08 that she decided to take the plunge and set up her own specialist waxing salon.

She had been working as a journalist and business analyst in the automotive industry, but jobs dried up after the crash.

She recalled: “I just decided to bite the bullet at that point.

“I had no background in the beauty industry and had a moment of madness where I thought ‘I am going to do this’.

“I had no idea about the challenges that would be ahead but I was very confident in the vision and I still am.

“I have made it work but not without a huge amount of toil and graft and blood, sweat and tears.”

The salon moved to its current home in Pelham Street in 2015, having been previously based in Bridlesmith Walk. The Leicester branch opened in 2017.

The company now offers both female and male waxing services and a lash and brow bar.

Asked what has enabled the firm to grow in the last decade, Mrs Dowdeswell pointed to her focus on customers.

She said: “I have always looked at it from the customer’s point of view.

“For us, the customer has always been king and we have such a loyal customer base now.

“The feedback we get on a daily basis is phenomenal, we are just very good at keeping clients happy.

“I didn’t want this to be another general salon, so I have put a lot into the brand. I think that has helped us because we are recognised – we are experts.”

On what the future holds for nkd, she added: “I am excited because I think we have achieved a lot in the last 10 years, but there is a lot more to achieve.”
