Get in the mood for Christmas in West Lothian

There's plenty on in the county on the run-up to the festive season.

17 Nov 2019, 16:03 by Debbie Hall, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/debbie-hall/

Get in the mood for Christmas and grab a bargain at the same time with West Lothian’s Christmas fayres.

First up is the West Lothian Cats Protection who would love to see you at their event.

It takes place on Saturday, November 23, at Newyearfield Farm Community Centre, Hawk Brae, Livingston.

Come along, join in the fun and support your other favourite charities; Petsearch, Dogs Trust and Forht Hedgehog Hositpal along with Cats Protection.

One sale will be Paluma Beads, Chipstix Ltd, along with tombola, home baking, Christmas gifts and much more.

Grab hold of the chance to start your Christmas shopping early in a relaxed style at Torphichen Village Christmas Market.

This will be held at Torphichen Community Education Centre.

Come along to the cafe and enjoy some delicious homebaking plus activities to keep the little ones busy while you shop.

Stallholders will be advertised shortly at www.visitwestlothian.co.uk/ as they are confirmed.

It will be a licensed event so why not enjoy some mulled wine and mince pies and Santa himself may even make an appearance.

All stallholder queries should be directed to torphichenchristmas@gmail.com.

Chistmas comes to Bathgate on Saturday, December 7, from 12.30pm.

Enjoy the annual day of festive fun, celebrations and activities in Bathgate town centre.

And on the same day Razzmatazz Events are thrilled to announce their third annual Christmas Craft Fair and Fun Day at Inveralmond Community High School.

On the day there will be over 40 local craft suppliers providing all manner of gifts and crafts just in time for Christmas.

And there will be something for everyone.

As well as exhibitors there will a Christmas photobooth, kids drawing table, raffle, Santa’s grotto and music from the Christmas Brass Band.

Full details of suppliers will be advertised on social media so stay tuned to this event.

Why not get the kids’ list to Santa early this year with a visit to his grotto at Almond Valley Heritage Centre.

This festive season, jolly Santa Claus is back for a visit to Livingston, where he’ll set up home in his magical winter cottage from December 7 until December 24.

Time slots for Santa visits are from 10.15am to 4pm.

For a real treat join Santa for a boat trip on the Union Canal with Bridge 19-40 Union Canal Society on Saturday December 7 or Sunday 8 . There are four trips each day at 11am, noon, 1pm or 2pm.
