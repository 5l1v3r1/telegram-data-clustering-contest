James Arthur quits Twitter after trolls 'hurt' him with online abuse

Arthur rose to fame in 2012 when he won the ninth series of The X Factor

17 Nov 2019, 16:34 by James Rodger, https://www.facebook.com/jamesrodgerjournalist

James Arthur has been forced to quit Twitter - after being left "hurt" by trolls online.

The singer said some of the abuse directed at him had gone too far.

The 31-year-old, who shot to fame through ITV1 talent show The X factor, told trolls that if that was their aim, they had won.

Arthur - who has previously spoken about his experience of depression and anxiety - tweeted on Sunday: "Have to be honest some of the things I read on here go a bit too far and quite frankly f***** hurt, if that was your goal you win."

The singer, from Middlesbrough, then continued: "Maybe I should have thicker skin at this point I don't know but either way this is my last tweet, I'm loggin off."

He concluded: "Peace out Twitter friends."

He added: "All posts will be done by digital team moving forward.

"Thanks for all the love and support shown by fans on this platform over the years it's been emotional but I've gotta delete this app."
