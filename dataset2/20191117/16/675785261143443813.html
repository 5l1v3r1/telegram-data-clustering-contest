Worcester 'murder' victim was 'kind' 25-year-old woman who was 'a great laugh'

West Mercia Police have arrested a 44-year-old man after Sara Hopkins, 25, was found dead in the Worcestershire town of Upton-upon-Severn

17 Nov 2019, 16:38 by Talia Shadwell, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/talia-shadwell/

A young woman found dead near a fire station in Worcester has been named as grieving friends paid tribute to the 'positive, kind' 25-year-old.

The body of Sara Hopkins was found in the streets of Upton-upon-Severn yesterday morning.

Police were called to the scene at roughly 8am Saturday.

A 44-year-old man was arrested on suspicion of murder and is currently in custody, West Mercia Police said.

Sara had previously worked at the Plough Inn Pub in the small Worcestershire town.

Sara's social media profiles said she worked in planning for Permadoor.

Pal Holly Barrett wrote a tribute to Sara on Facebook, whom she described as her best friend in high school who had bought joy into her life.

"Sara, I'll never forget you and the beautiful friendship that we had.

"I'll love you forever and I'll always remember the many amazing years that we spent together. Rest easy, my girl."

She added: "You were my best friend during high school and carried on making me laugh and smile into our twenties.

"You were always so positive, kind and a great laugh to be around. You had my back when not many others did and I'll always be grateful for the time that we spent together."

Detective Inspector Jim Bayliss said yesterday: “Our enquiries are very much in their early stages and at this stage the woman’s death is being treated as suspicious.

"A man has been arrested and is currently in police custody.

“Extra officers will be in the area, both to carry out enquiries and to offer reassurance."

West Mercia police said yesterday the victim's next of kin had been informed.

Investigators did not clarify whether the victim and the 44-year-old man arrested were known to one another.
