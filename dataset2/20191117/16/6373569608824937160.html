Man charged over Ford stabbing is named

Rory Wallace faces three charges after incident on Friday evening

17 Nov 2019, 16:37 by Carl Eve

A 23-year-old man from Stoke will appear at Plymouth Magistrates' Court on Monday where he faces three charges relating to an alleged stabbing incident on Friday evening.

Rory Wallace, formerly of Victoria Place, Stoke, but now of no fixed abode, was arrested on Friday evening following an incident in a block of flats in Alexandra Road, Ford .

His alleged victim, said to be in his 20s, remains in Derriford Hospital where he was last described as conscious and stable. He is understood to have suffered multiple wounds to his neck and back, although his injuries were described by police as "not life threatening".

Police later confirmed that Wallace and the injured man were known to each other.

Wallace, aged 23, was charged lated Saturday night with three offences - wounding with intent to cause grievous bodily harm, assault by beating of an emergency worker and criminal damage.

Detectives have confirmed that a weapon has been seized and has been sent for forensic examination.

Wallace has been remanded into custody and will appear on Monday morning before magistrates.
