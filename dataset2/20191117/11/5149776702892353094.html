NHS worker kicked off the bus in the dark and told 'you can't pay extra fare'

She had missed her stop and her Robin Hood card was no longer valid

17 Nov 2019, 11:36 by Matt Jarram

An NHS worker said she was booted off a bus and had to walk 15 minutes in the dark to get back to the stop she had missed because the driver refused to accept her money.

Mariama Amoah, 38, from Hucknall, had just worked overtime at Nottingham's City Hospital and wanted to get home.

The Hucknall mum said she usually takes the Trent Barton Threes from the hospital grounds to Moor Bridge where she takes a tram back to Hucknall.

The hospital administrator finished work at around 7.30pm on Sunday, November 10.

She caught the bus, but due to exhaustion, had completely missed her stop at Moor Bridge.

Mrs Amoah said she has a Robin Hood card that allows her to travel within the city boundary but because she had missed her stop it was no longer valid.

She said: "I was very tired and missed my usual stop at Moor Bridge. I use this bus twice a day six days a week.

"The bus stopped and I looked up to see the driver standing in front of me.

"He asked if I had a Robin Hood card, I answered 'yes.'

"He then told me to 'get off the bus. You can't go any further.'

"I said 'it was dark so could I pay to Hucknall please?' He informed me it was company policy to throw people off the bus and not to let them pay.

"I pointed out how illogical this was as I would have to get on the bus and pay the fee to the next driver. He said he didn't make the rules."

Mrs Amoah said she felt embarrassed and didn't want to argue in front of other customers.

"I got off the bus and had no idea where I was," she said.

"I called my husband and followed the road back until I got my bearings.

"It was cold and dark. There was no reason to kick me off. I was prepared to pay.

"He kicked a woman off the bus in the dark. I am a 5ft 2 woman on my own. I did not want to do that.

"If something had come of this then questions would have been asked. What happens if it had been a teenage girl?"

Trent Barton said an investigation has been launched to determine what happened.

A spokesman for the company said: "We were sorry to hear about this customer's complaint and have been in touch with her so we can carry out a full investigation over the coming days.

"We will be back in contact with her to let her know the outcome of the investigation."
