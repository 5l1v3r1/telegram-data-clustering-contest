'Oh the look'

Numan on Rangers transfer and making Advocaat his personal flight attendant

17 Nov 2019, 11:06 by Gary Taylor

ARTHUR NUMAN has lifted the lid on his transfer to Rangers and how he made Dick Advocaat his personal flight attendant.

The new Feyenoord boss, who will come up against his old employers in the Europa League last this month, left PSV for Ibrox in 1998.

Numan was made his first priority signing - despite interest from the likes of Atletico Madrid.

Advocaat made it clear to the defender he would be following him to Glasgow and Numan has told how he had a bit of fun with his boss on David Murray's private jet.

He told the Sunday Post: "I remember the day Dick pulled me aside, quietly, and told me he was going to Glasgow Rangers and that I was going with him.

"He made it clear he wanted to sign me.

"I told him I wanted to consider other options because I knew Atletico Madrid also wanted me.

"But he made it clear I was going to Rangers and that was the end of the conversation.

"Before I signed we flew over to Scotland from Holland in David Murray's private jet.

"I think there was only three people on the plane.

"I decided to have a bit of fun with Dick, so I told him that if he wanted me to sign then he should make me a coffee and bring me sandwiches. Oh the look he gave me!

"I had a good laugh seeing dick being my personal flight attendant!."

Steven Gerrard's current Rangers crop travel to Rotterdam on November 28 knowing a victory will see them join Celtic in the Europa League last 32.

Numan, who will be in attendance at the match, loved his time with the Light Blues and has hailed Rangers' 'unique' support.

He added: "Speaking from my own point of view, Rangers was the most fantastic football club to play for. It's special, very special.

"I still go back to Glasgow six or seven times a year. The supporters treat me brilliantly, like a king, to be honest.

"When I have friends or family over from Holland with me they cannot believe the adulation and warmness because it doesn't happen in Holland.

"I can walk down the street in Holland and nobody will look at me twice. It's different in Glasgow.

"Mikey Mols, Bert Konterman and all the lads will tell you the same thing. Glasgow and the Rangers supporters are so unique.

"The Dutch guys all love the club. That's why all come back time after time.

"I'm sure Dick feels the same. I had conversations with him and I know he felt something very special for Rangers.

"He loved the Treble success of 1999 and the Champions League nights. He just wanted to win more games and do even better. We all did."
