How Toon teen Elliot Anderson went from Level 7 and idolising Yohan Cabaye to a deal with NUFC

Newcastle United teenager Elliot Anderson is living the dream after scoring his first goal for the Magpies in the EFL Trophy

17 Nov 2019, 11:00 by Lee Ryder

Geordie teenager Elliot Anderson once gazed down from Level 7 of the Leazes End in admiration of Yohan Cabaye and Hatem Ben Arfa as a kid and dreamt that one day he would get the chance to run out on the hallowed turf at St James' Park.

At just 16-years-old earlier this season, Anderson sprinted on to the field against Swansea reserves to achieve such a feat but, after penning a new contract on his 17th birthday this month, the next step for the North Shields youngster will be the biggest.

As it stands he has squad No 67 at Newcastle and he is some way from Cabaye's old number four, but he is on the right track.

Anderson, an attacking midfielder who has been at the club since the age of nine after being spotted at Wallsend Boys Club, has had some exciting moments in recent times after signing his first professional contract and then netting his first competitive goal for the club in the EFL Trophy at Port Vale.

The teenager is trying to take things in his stride but knows he is now living the dream of thousands of other kids on Tyneside.

He told the Sunday Sun: "I was a supporter of the team as a kid.

"I watched players like Yohan Cabaye, Hatem Ben Arfa and Peter Lovenkrands.

"I used to watch from right at the top of Level 7 so it is quite surreal to be around the place now.

"I just continue to work hard though."

His first goal may have come in a dead rubber group match in this year's EFL Trophy and in the end, it counted for nothing on the night.

But from a United point of view, it was the highlight and the 23 fans who still opted to back the Under-21 team in the competition on a freezing November night in Burslem may go on to say they were there when Anderson scored his first big goal for Newcastle.

He reflected: "I'm buzzing to score my first competitive goal for Newcastle.

"But the lads did well overall, we came out against a League Two side and put in a good performance.

"It was nice to score.
