Eamonn Brophy earns Scotland call as Lawrence Shankland is ruled out of Kazakhstan clash

The Kilmarnock frontman could win his second cap after replacing Shankland in the squad.

17 Nov 2019, 17:10 by Mark McDougall, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/mark-mcdougall/

Eamonn Brophy has been called up to the Scotland squad as a replacement for Lawrence Shankland.

The Dundee United striker has pulled out of the game on Tuesday having been an unused sub for Saturday's 2-1 win over Cyprus.

Kilmarnock star Brophy has one cap for his country, which came in the home win over Cyprus back in June.

The 23-year-old hasn't been at his best for the Rubgy Park side this season, with just three goals in 16 games in all competitions - but was hugely impressive under Steve Clarke last season.

The last of his three strikers did come in Killie's final game before the international break when his goal helped earn a 2-2 draw with Hamilton.

Shankland missed Dundee United's win over Queen of the South because of international duty with fellow striker Nicky Clark grabbing one of the three goals.

The 24-year-old scored in his only start for the national side during a 6-0 win over San Marino.

It was his second cap, having made his debut in the heavy defeat to Russia.
