Watch: Ashton Agar takes blow on nose while trying to dismiss brother in Marsh One-Day Cup

Ashton Agar suffered a laceration to the bridge of his nose in Western Australia's Marsh One-Day Cup clash against South Australia after dropping a catch hit by his younger brother, Wes Agar.

17 Nov 2019, 17:17 by Sports Desk

Ashton Agar, Australia’s left-handed spinner, was left with a bloody nose while attempting a catch to dismiss his own brother in Western Australia’s Marsh One-Day Cup clash against South Australia on Sunday.

Agar suffered a laceration to the bridge of his nose in the domestic one-day match after he dropped a catch off Wes Agar at Karen Rolton Oval.

Rated as one of the better fielders down under, the 26-year-old ran around to attempt a straightforward catch at mid-on but slipped and copped the ball right between his eyes. With blood on Agar’s face, teammate Jhye Richardson called for medical assistance quickly.
