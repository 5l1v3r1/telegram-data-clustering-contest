MAGGIE LAY

Rod Stewart reveals his first romp lasted just four seconds – and inspired smash hit Maggie May

17 Nov 2019, 21:26 by George Mair

SIR Rod Stewart has revealed the romp that inspired No1 hit Maggie May lasted just four seconds.

The legendary former ladies’ man, 74, admitted he was 16 when he lost his virginity in a tent to an older woman — and it was a flop.

He later penned the 1971 smash about his deflating experience at the Beaulieu jazz festival in Hampshire.

Rod said: “I don’t know what her name was, I just called her Maggie May so she’ll never know.

“It all happened in a tent in about four seconds. That was my first encounter with a female, sexually-wise — four seconds.”

Maggie May became Rod’s first big solo hit on both sides of the Atlantic, topping the UK charts for five weeks.

But Rod revealed record label bosses didn’t rate it because of its lack of chorus.

He told Radio Scotland: “I played it to them and they said ‘where’s the sing-along bit in the middle?’

“I said ‘there’s no chorus, it just rambles on’. But they put it on the album.”

Despite the tent disaster, the singer is famed for his popularity with the ladies.

He has eight kids with five women but is happily wed to third wife Penny, 48.

The Celtic fan also said twice-weekly footie matches tamed his party lifestyle.

He added: “I couldn’t do drugs and drink too much, because it would have ruined my game.

“Football saved me. It’s helped me so much and I miss not playing.”
