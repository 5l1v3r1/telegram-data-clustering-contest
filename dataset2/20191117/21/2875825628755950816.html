I'm A Celebrity's Ant and Dec mock Prince Andrew's 'sweat' comment after Newsnight disaster

As they introduced the show, Declan Donnelly cracked a joke about Prince Andrew's disastrous Newsnight interview

17 Nov 2019, 21:21 by Irishmirror.ie

I'm A Celebrity... Get Me Out Of Here! 2019 kicked off tonight and Ant and Dec were on top form with the topical jokes just minutes in.

As they introduced the show, Declan Donnelly cracked a joke about Prince Andrew's disastrous Newsnight interview.

Ant McPartlin said the stars in this year's line-up were "sweating" with nerves, but Dec said that "not everyone can sweat".

This was a reference to Prince Andrew claim that he wasn't "profusely sweating" when he danced with then 17-year-old Virginia Roberts because a medical condition meant he couldn't sweat.

Ant said: "And what a line up we've got in store for you this year.

"Ten celebrities all of them very nervous, all of them sweating."

"Presuming they can sweat, not everyone can sweat apparently," Dec interuppted.

There were laughs from the crew but Ant kept a poker face and carried on with the introductions.

The Duke of York last night answered questions for the first time about his links to paedophile billionaire Jeffrey Epstein.

He stuttered, stumbled and licked his lips as Newsnight host Emily Maitlis grilled him about allegations he'd had sex with Roberts, who claims she was trafficked by Epstein to be a sex slave

He said he didn't meet her at the club becuse he was at a Pizza Express in Woking at the time.

I'm A Celebrity kicks off tonight with a host of famous faces heading to the jungle for three weeks of gruelling challenges.

On the line-up is Olympian and reality star and transgender icon Caitlyn Jenner, former Girls Aloud star Nadine Coyle, former EastEnders star Jacqueline Jossa, Good Morning Britain presenter Kate Garraway, Radio presenter Roman Kemp, former Big Brother star Adele Roberts, comedian Andrew Maxwell, Rak-Su singer Myles Stephenson, former rugby player James Haskell, and former footballer Ian Wright.

There are also a few rumoured late additions.
