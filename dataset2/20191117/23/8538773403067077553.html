Euro 2020 qualifying: Who will reach the finals?

17 Nov 2019, 23:01 by Dale Johnson

Euro 2020 kicks off in June next year and 20 of the 24 places at the tournament will be secured this week. Here is a look at the latest qualifying permutations and scenarios.

The top two teams (20 in total) in each group qualify automatically. Four more nations will qualify via playoffs, qualification for which is based on UEFA Nations League performance.

Seventeen nations have sealed their place so far.

What is still to be decided this week?

There are three automatic places that will be decided over the next few days. Those places will go to:

- Two of Denmark, Republic of Ireland, Switzerland (Monday)
- Hungary, Slovakia or Wales (Tuesday)

The 16 teams in the playoffs will also be confirmed, with the draw for paths and home advantage in the finals taking place on Friday, Nov. 22.

GROUP A

England have won the group and will be seeded in the finals draw. Czech Republic have also made it to finals, while Kosovo will be away to North Macedonia in the League D playoffs. Bulgaria are guaranteed a playoff, path to be confirmed.

GROUP B

Ukraine and Portugal have qualified. Serbia will be away to Norway in the League C playoff semifinals.

GROUP C

Nov. 19: Germany vs. Northern Ireland, Netherlands vs. Estonia

Germany have qualified and will be seeded in the finals draw if they win at home to Northern Ireland.

Netherlands are also through and could still top the group and be seeded should they win and Germany drop points.

Northern Ireland are in the playoffs, path to be determined, while Belarus will be away to Georgia in the League D playoff.

GROUP D

Nov. 18: Republic of Ireland vs. Denmark, Gibraltar vs. Switzerland

Denmark top the group and need a point in Dublin to guarantee qualification, though they would also qualify with a defeat if Switzerland were to lose to Gibraltar.

Any win for Republic of Ireland will almost certainly be enough, while Switzerland need a point to be sure of being at the finals.

In the unlikely event of Switzerland drawing with Gibraltar, Ireland would have to beat Denmark by at least two goals to qualify at their expense.

Whichever of the three teams misses out is sure of a playoff.

Georgia will be at home to Belarus in the League D playoff semifinals.

GROUP E

Nov. 19: Slovakia vs. Azerbaijan, Wales vs. Hungary

Croatia have qualified, and will be in Pot 2 for the draw, leaving a three-way battle for second.

In pole position are Hungary, who would be guaranteed to qualify with a win in Wales. If Hungary draw, they can qualify only if Slovakia fail to beat Azerbaijan.

Wales are third and have to beat Hungary, a result that would guarantee qualification.

Slovakia must beat Azerbaijan and hope Wales draw at home to Hungary.

The two teams who do not qualify automatically are guaranteed a playoff.

GROUP F

Nov. 18: Spain vs. Romania

Spain have secured their place, and will be sure of being seeded if they beat Romania.

Sweden will also be at the finals after finishing second. Norway will be at home to Serbia in the League C playoff semifinal, while Romania will be in the playoffs though the path is uncertain.

GROUP G

Nov. 19: Poland vs. Slovenia

Poland are through and still have a slim chance of being seeded in the finals draw should they win at home to Slovenia.

Austria have qualified in second, while North Macedonia will play Kosovo at home in the League D playoff path. Israel will also be in the playoffs, path to be determined.

GROUP H

France and Turkey have both qualified for the finals, while Iceland are guaranteed a home semifinal in the League A path.

France have a chance of being seeded in the finals draw, if Spain drop points against Romania.

GROUP I

Belgium have won the group and will be seeded in the finals draw, while Russia have qualified and Scotland will have a home semifinal in the League C playoff path.

GROUP J

Italy are through and are guaranteed to be seeded in the finals draw. Finland are also through to their first tournament while Bosnia and Herzegovina will have a home semifinal in the League B playoff path.

PLAYOFFS

The best-performing nations from the UEFA Nations League who do not qualify automatically for Euro 2020 will get a playoff place, and the paths are now starting to take shape.

There will be 16 teams in the playoffs, with four in each from a UEFA Nations League path.

The winners of the two one-legged semifinals (March 26) will meet in the final (March 31) for a place at Euro 2020.

The highest-ranked nation will be at home in the semifinal. While on Nov. 22 (11 a.m. UK, 6 a.m. ET), UEFA will draw the home team for the final ties.

The playoff system is explained in greater detail here.

The only way this scenario can now happen is if both Wales and Slovakia fail to qualify automatically. The teams involved may yet change through the final few days of qualifying.

There is a special condition in the playoff draw principles that states all hosts should be given the best chance to qualify. As such, it is expected that, wherever possible, there will only be one host in each playoff path.

That means Romania would likely move from the League C path to League A because, as hosts, they require a playoff route to the finals that does not include another host and Scotland, as Nations League group winners, by rights should be in the League C path. Bulgaria and Israel would then draw for who takes the remaining slot in the League C path and who also moves to League A.

There would be a draw to decide which of Slovakia, Wales and Northern Ireland move to League A. Republic of Ireland would be expected to remain in path B so it has a host, along with Nations League winners Bosnia.

If the League B path has four teams, one semifinal would be Bosnia vs. Northern Ireland while the other would be between two of Denmark, Wales, Slovakia and Republic of Ireland.

FINALS DRAW

The draw will be held on Saturday, Nov. 30, in Bucharest, Romania. The draw ceremony will begin at midday ET (6 p.m. CET) and is expected to last 50 minutes in total (with 20 minutes for the draw itself).
