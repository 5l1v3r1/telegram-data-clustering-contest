DAMAGING

Boris Johnson and Jeremy Corbyn’s plans ‘could harm economy’ as SNP candidate says freedom of movement ‘crucial’

17 Nov 2019, 23:30 by Christine Lavelle

BUSINESS leaders yesterday warned election pledges by Labour and the Tories could harm the economy.

Confederation of British Industry chief Carolyn Fairbairn claimed Jeremy Corbyn’s nationalisation plans would “freeze investment”.

Ms Fairbairn also insisted PM Boris Johnson’s post-Brexit proposals to drive down the number of immigrants by charging them £625 to cover NHS care costs risks creating a skills shortage.

She told Sky News’s Sophy Ridge: “We need people to come and help us renew our economy.

"It’s not just brightest and best, it’s people at all skill levels across our economy that we need.”

And on Mr Corbyn’s plans to take BT into public ownership, she added: “That will freeze investment.

“I have talked to businesses who are already sitting there thinking ‘maybe we’re next’.

“This programme that is appearing to value none of the contribution that business makes will simply shut investment out of our country.”

Mr Corbyn yesterday hinted free movement of people could continue after Brexit if Labour wins power on December 12.

The Labour leader told the BBC’s Andrew Marr: “There will be a great deal of movement.”

It comes amid a split among party figures after a conference motion to “maintain and extend” the freedom of movement of people was backed.

But Nats Westminster candidate Stewart McDonald said: “Freedom of movement is crucial for Scotland and any attempts to restrict it would inflict lasting harm.”
