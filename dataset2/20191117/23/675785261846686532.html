The Queen will support Prince Andrew - but will not be happy, royal expert says

Prince Andrew and the royal household are reeling from the fallout of his car-crash Newsnight interview, in which he failed to apologise for the Jeffrey Epstein scandal

17 Nov 2019, 23:06 by Ingrid Seward, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/ingrid-seward/

Agreeing to this interview is quite different to anything the Duke of York has ever agreed to before.

He has made mistakes in the past but never felt it necessary to justify them. Sadly, there seems to be nothing positive to come out of it.

He persisted in doing it because he hoped it would help him to move on, but it’s only made matters worse.

He is the Queen’s son and she will support him – although she cannot be happy about it.

I was surprised he did not apologise at the end, when he was given every chance.

He should have shown humility and reiterated how sorry he was. Unfortunately, he did not use that opportunity.

I don’t see that this interview has gained anything and presume the reason the Duke agreed to do it was to try to clear his name.

But it doesn’t seem to have done anything other than heap more criticism on him.

This has been hanging over him for years and has just got more and more difficult.

Buckingham Palace and the BBC began negotiating at the start of this year, and any doubts Andrew might have had about doing such an interview were well-founded.

In my opinion, he should not have gone ahead. The Royal Family always move on – they have to.

People’s memories are short, but no one will be forgetting this.
