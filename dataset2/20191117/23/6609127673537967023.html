SCOTTISH SUN SAYS

Tory and Labour pledges on immigration and broadband have gone down like a lead balloon with business chiefs

17 Nov 2019, 23:27 by The Scottish Sun

FOR once the Conservatives and Labour have triggered some agreement with their electioneering — though it’s most definitely not the ringing endorsement the parties’ leaders might have wanted.

No, key election pledges by Boris Johnson and Jeremy Corbyn are potentially dangerous to the economy.

And don’t take our word for it.

This is the view of Carolyn Fairbairn, boss of the CBI.

She has issues with both the PM’s post-Brexit immigration crackdown — and Mr Corbyn’s off-the-wall proposal of free broadband for everyone.

Firstly, the Tory scheme to drive down the number of people coming here risks creating a skills shortage, it seems.

As Ms Fairbairn told Sky News: “We need people to come and help us renew our economy.”

And just as troubling is the Labour leader’s plot to take BT Openreach into public ownership.

The industry boss added: “That will freeze investment. I have talked to businesses who are already sitting there thinking ‘maybe we’re next’.”

So, in summary, major pledges by both main parties have gone down like a lead balloon with business chiefs.

But there is a long road ahead in the race for No10.

Mr Johnson and Mr Corbyn have the time and opportunity to try to win over reluctant industry leaders.

Whether the public will be persuaded too remains to be seen.
