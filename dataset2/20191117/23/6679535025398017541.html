Puducherry govt. to raise ₹200 crore from market borrowing

Auctioning of dated securities on Tuesday

17 Nov 2019, 23:58 by Special Correspondent

The government is auctioning dated securities with a seven-year tenure in an open market borrowing exercise to raise ₹200 crore.

A note from the Finance Department said the securities will be issued for a minimum nominal amount of ₹10,000 and in multiples of ₹10,000 thereafter.

The auction will be conducted by the Reserve Bank of India, Mumbai Office (Fort), on Tuesday.

Interested persons, firms, companies, corporate bodies, institutions, PF, Trusts, Regional Rural Banks, Cooperative banks etc may submit a single consolidated non-competitive bid in electronic format on the Reserve Bank of India core banking solution (e-Kuber) available on the site www.rbi.org.in on November 19, between 10.30 a.m. and 11.30 a.m.

The competitive bids need be submitted electronically on e-Kuber between 10.30 a.m. and 12 p.m. on the same day.

The result of the auction will be displayed by the RBI on its website the same day.

Successful bidders should deposit the price amount of the stock covered by their bids by means of a banker's cheque or demand draft payable at Reserve Bank of India, Mumbai (Fort), or Chennai on November 20 before the close of banking hours.

Interest rate

The government stock will bear interest at the rate determined by the Reserve Bank of India at the auction. Interest will be paid on May 20 and November 20 of every financial year until repayment.

The stocks will qualify for ready forward facility, the Finance Department said.
