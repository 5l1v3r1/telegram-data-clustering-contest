Amy Winehouse's tragic final days as every man in her life let her down

After years of battling addiction, Amy Winehouse died from alcohol poisoning on July 23, 2011 at the age of just 27

17 Nov 2019, 07:30 by Molly Pike, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/molly-pike/

It's been almost a decade since the tragic death of Amy Winehouse, who was just 27 when she passed away.

After years of battling addiction, she died from alcohol poisoning on July 23, 2011.

Just months before her death, Amy had checked into rehab to get ready for her European tour that summer.

It was claimed that Amy's manager and her dad Mitch Winehouse didn't want her to stop performing, despite her fragile state - something Mitch denies.

In 2009 she was recovering in Saint Lucia and her dad turned up with a camera crew, something they argued over.

She told him: "Why have you done this to me? You have to come out with a camera crew! Are you only interested in what you can get out of me?"

She managed a few months of sobriety in 2010, but reportedly downed several bottles of wine before getting on stage at what was meant to her comeback gig in Serbia.

She was booed off stage by the 20,000-strong crowd, who also pelted her with paper cups after she stumbled through songs and forgot lyrics.

The tour was promptly cancelled.

Around this time Amy had also broken up with her boyfriend Reg Traviss, who had reportedly become fed up of her drinking.

He had been there at her bedside when doctors had told her to stop boozing or die.

Despite moving on with Travis, Amy seemed to still have feelings for her ex, Blake Fielder-Civil.

In 2010, a year after they divorced and one year before her death, she'd tweeted: "I love my husband Blake an it ain't wrong! Marry for life."

Blake has admitted that he was the one who introduced her to heroin.

While she'd managed to give up drugs before she died, years of abuse on her body had taken its toll.

Two weeks before she died it was claimed that she suffered a seizure and was found collapsed in a London street.

Her last public performance came just two days before her death when she joined her goddaughter and protege Dionne Bromfield on stage at The Roundhouse in London.

Amy had one, final conversation with her bodyguard, Andrew Morris, before she went to bed on the evening of July 22

He said: "She was showing me some clips on her laptop and she was singing and she said 'boy, I can sing' and I said 'damn right, you can sing.

"She said 'if I could I would give it back just to walk down the street with no hassle, I would'."

The next morning Andrew found Amy motionless on her bed.
