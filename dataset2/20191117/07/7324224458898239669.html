Dudley school pupils to perform Miss Saigon

Students at a Dudley school will take to the stage later this month to perform award-winning musical Miss Saigon.

17 Nov 2019, 07:59 by Dayna Farrington

Nearly 100 students from Ellowes Hall Sports College have been involved with the production of Miss Saigon, which will be performed between November 20 and 29.

Auditions for the show took place in April and since the new school term started in September, students have been working every lunchtime on the production.

Students who will be acting, singing, dancing and performing in the orchestra have been rehearsing for hours, while behind the scenes others have been working on stage management, lighting, sound engineering, make-up and wardrobe.

Miss Saigon is based on Giacomo Puccini's opera Madame Butterfly and tells the tragic tale of a doomed romance involving an Asian woman abandoned by her American lover.

Sarah Waterfield, from Ellowes Hall, said: "The students and staff have worked extremely hard and put in so many hours of rehearsals and always given it their all, to ensure we give the audience the fantastic show they will no doubt be expecting.
