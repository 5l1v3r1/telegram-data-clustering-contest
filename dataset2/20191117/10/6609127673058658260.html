SAM'S THE MAN

Stoke City eyeing January transfer swoop for Aberdeen star Sam Cosgrove

17 Nov 2019, 10:05 by Alan Nixon

STOKE CITY are eyeing a January move for Aberdeen striker Sam Cosgrove.

The Potters have been watching Cosgrove, all season and new boss Michael O’Neill is a fan.

O’Neill was checking out the Yorkshire-born 22-year-old’s family background in case he qualified for Northern Ireland.

Cosgrove, who cost £25,000 from Carlisle, has bagged 16 goals this season.

The striker had previously been linked with a move to Lazio.

There was reported interest from the Serie A side in the target man but Cosgrove rubbished the rumours.

He said: "I heard about it but I think it was a load of rubbish to be honest.

"Two years ago you could have asked me [what I want out of my career] and I’d have said playing for any professional club and I’d be happy.

"I always believed in myself as a footballer, but I couldn’t have wished it to go any better so far."

Aberdeen boss Derek McInnes is desperate to keep his top-scorer who is in lethal form this term.

He insisted at the start of the season, Cosgrove would be going nowhere.

He said: "We’ve had enquiries about Sam already from other clubs but he will be staying with us this season.

“He’s improved so much in a short period of time and hopefully there’s more to come from Sam."
