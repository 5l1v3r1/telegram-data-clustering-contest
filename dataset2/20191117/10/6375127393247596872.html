PH’s Science City shines

17 Nov 2019, 10:20 by Steve A. Gosuico

SCIENCE CITY OF MUñOZ – It is now this city’s turn to shine exactly 40 days before Christmas.

This after City Mayor Nestor L. Alvarez led city and barangay officials along with Rep. Micaela “Mikki” Salvador-Violago, of the province’s second district, in the switching-on activity for the Science City of Muñoz Grand Pailaw 2019 at 7:35 p.m. here on Nov. 15.

Thousands of local residents gathered on Friday in front of the city hall compound as they witnessed this year’s Christmas lights design featuring giant replicas of rice grains and other designs which lighted up the whole façade of the city hall building and its entire compound, including Tobias street.

Alvarez, who has been nursing a fever for three days already, said the Pailaw event serves as a kick-off ceremony for the city’s 19th Charter anniversary celebrations on December 9 next moThis city is regarded as the country’s first and only chartered science city and the second in Asia next to Japan’s Tsukuba Science City.

It is being showcased as the region’s center of scientific research on agriculture as it plays host to the Central Luzon State University and other research institutions like the Philippine Rice Research Institute, the Philippine Carabao Center, the Bureau of Postharvest Research and Extension, the Phil-Sino Cooperation Center for Agricultural Technology, and the National Freshwater Fishery Technology Center.
