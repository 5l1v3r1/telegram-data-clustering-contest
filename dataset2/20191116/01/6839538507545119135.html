Teenage girls, 16 and 17, arrested for alleged knifepoint robbery at Oaklands Park

16 Nov 2019, 01:33

Two teenage girls armed with a knife have been arrested after they allegedly used the blade to steal a mobile phone from a woman at Oaklands Park.

Police officers tracked the pair – just 16 and 17 years old – near a unit complex Oaklands Park on Friday night.

The girls used the knife to confront a woman on Crozier Tce before taking the phone and running away, police say.

The victim was not injured.

Officers searched the area and allegedly found the pair with the phone.

The oldest girl, from Morphettville, and other suspect, from Noarlunga Downs, were arrested and charged with aggravated robbery.

Both were refused bail will appear in Adelaide Youth Court on Monday.
