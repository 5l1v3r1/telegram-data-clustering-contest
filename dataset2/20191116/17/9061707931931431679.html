Young Derbyshire woman 'struggling to cope' after suicides of her brother and sister

They were aged 21 and 24

16 Nov 2019, 17:59 by Noah Martin

A heartbroken Derbyshire woman who lost her sister and brother to suicide has revealed how she has been "existing, not living".

Bethany Palmer, 26, lost the two siblings in the space of just two years.

Now, the mum-of-three has talked candidly about her grief in the hope it will encourage others to never give up on life.

Recalling the double-tragedy, Bethany said her brother Joseph was aged 24 when he hanged himself in an outhouse at her property, where he had been staying in 2017.

Only two years before that, her 21-year-old sister, Sophia, had also taken her own life.

Bethany, who lives in Ilkeston, said: "It is always there, for years I've been existing, not living. It's like someone just took my life, threw it up in the air, and it's scattered everywhere.

"But now is the time to turn it into something positive instead of letting it have a hold over me."

On the evening that Joe died, Bethany said there was a "sense of calmness" in her house.

"The house had been stressful leading up to it, but on the night everything was peaceful. The children were peaceful.

"I was clearing away pots, we'd had homemade lasagne and Joe was out in the shed because that was his chill-out space."

Bethany said "something kept her inside" and it wasn't until she got suspicious an hour later she went to find Joe - but it was too late.

"From that night I felt empty,” she said. “I felt like I was just stuck in this moment and the whole world was just carrying on.

"I kept reliving the experience over and over again. I kept seeing his face as I was opening the shed door. It was dark and I had a torch.

"I can still see it now, my torch light slowly going through the shed and shining onto him."

After Joe's death, Bethany said she became "depressed", struggled to get out of the house and couldn't hold down a job.

Bethany said: "With my sister Sophia, I suppose you could say we saw it coming, she was really unwell and had been sectioned because she was suicidal.

"But with Joe, we thought because he knew how it felt and the effect it had the first time round, he wouldn't have gone through with it."

Haunted by flashbacks to the night that Joe died, Bethany said "even now" if she is struggling to sleep, she can "still see his face".

"Even letting the dogs out to the back garden, I just take a deep breath and it's like I'm there again, in the crisp cold air."

She added: "After Sophia we all just drifted, we did it without realising.

"We were close as siblings and I think we were scared to lose another, so if we drew away we wouldn't have that close bond."

As well as her siblings, Bethany said the suicides have had a "massive impact" on her three children - AJ, 10, Zachary, eight, and three-year-old Phoebe.

"They have seen a lot, you try to protect them from it but police have been at the door, they were still awake when my brother happened.

"We told them he was in a motorbike accident but they listened to conversations, they aren't stupid.”

Bethany said little Phoebe was "really close" to her uncle and they would often spend an evening curled up on the sofa, chatting.

However, she only realised the true extent of the impact when her oldest child started saying he "would kill himself" when he was angry or upset.

"It made me realise the full extent of what it’s teaching the younger generations, that suicide is the answer and it’s not the answer.”

There were a total of seven siblings in her family before the tragic losses but Bethany said the suicides have drawn them apart.

Despite the consequences for her family, Bethany said she doesn't feel angry about what happened, only a sense of guilt. A month before he died, Joe had made an attempt on his life in the outhouse but Bethany had managed to stop him.

"We didn’t speak about it again and I think that was me being too scared to talk about it, bringing too much back from my sister, but I should've done more," she said.

Bethany said she is only just starting the process of putting her life back together and is "slowly starting to make steps forward".

"I'd like people to, whenever they are feeling like that, even if they are completely done, to just hold on.

"If you think you're in debt or anything like that, and you think that's you're only escape, it isn't, I don't think anyone truly wants to end their life, they just want an escape."

Bethany said she wants people to "reach out more" but cited a lack of support once people are discharged from crisis teams as a problem.

"I've got a few ideas about what I want to do around mental health and helping others, it's just about putting those into action.”
