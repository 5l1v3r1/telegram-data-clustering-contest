#KidsTakeOver Waterloo Region for National Child Day

On Nov. 20th, CYPT is challenging the Region to 'engage with children and youth in meaningful ways'

16 Nov 2019, 17:00 by Ariel Deutschmann

The Children and Youth Planning Table of Waterloo Region is asking residents to let the 'kids take over' on National Child Day.

According to CYPT, National Child Day is and opportunity for communities across Canada to 'engage with children and youth in meaningful ways.'

Some suggestions the CYPT has on how to engage with kids on National Child Day, include letting them to plan an assembly at school or allowing them to take over a radio show.

Between Nov. 18th to Nov. 20th, the CYPT says they will help match kids with opportunities in Waterloo Region with requests made on their website.

For residents not choosing an activity, they can still support this event by wearing blue and using the hashtag '#KTOWR' on social media.

Besides being National Child Day, CYPT says Nov. 20th is also the 30th anniversary of the Convention of the Rights of the Child, which was organized by UNICEF.

CYPT is a community-wide partnership that includes service providers, researchers and planning bodies and funders serving children, youth and families in Waterloo Region.

For more information about CYPT or National Child Day, go to childrenandyouthplanningtable.ca.
