Thug tried to hurl pregnant girlfriend out window after man liked Facebook photo

16 Nov 2019, 17:56

A violent bully subjected his pregnant girlfriend to two vicious attacks where she was stripped naked, punched in the gut, and nearly thrown out of a window.

Ashley Brazier, 31, of Whitstable in Kent, picked up his 7st 2lbs partner and tried to chuck her out the window after he noticed a man had ‘liked’ her Facebook photo.

Brazier, who has a history of harassment, battery, and criminal damage, only failed because she managed to extend her body across the frame and resist.

But her boyfriend’s anger only intensified and he pinned her to a wall by the throat with such force, he lifted her from the floor.

He then chased her in a ‘petrifying’ game of ‘cat and mouse’, prosecutor Amy Nicholson told Canterbury Crown Court this week.

Brazier then struck the pregnant victim in the face with her phone after accusing her of having an affair.

The court also heard how a ‘spiteful and cruel’ Brazier had previously thrown ‘away her father’s ashes’ Ms Nicholson said.

She added: ‘What happened has ruined her life.’

Brazier was jailed for three years and nine months for grievous bodily harm and actual bodily harm.

Brazier has 41 previous convictions, including two for harassment, six for battery, 10 for criminal damage, as well as multiple breaches of court orders.

During the attack Brazier repeatedly spat in his victim’s face as she fought to escape. He slapped her so hard her ear rang.

Brazier punched his girlfriend in the gut, dislodging her rib and puncturing her lung  – which doctors later had to inflate twice.

He then stripped her and locked her out, which Judge Rupert Lowe described as his ‘signature attack’.

Brazier only stopped when he realised the extent of her injuries.

She was sent to the Queen Elizabeth The Queen Mother Hospital in Margate, Kent, where doctors installed a drainage tap.

The victim did not know she was in the early stages of pregnancy at the time of the assault.

In an earlier attack, Brazier was suspicious of one of his partner’s texts while he was out drinking with friends in Ramsgate, Kent.

He called her ‘a slag’, slapped her, pulled out clumps of her hair and spat in her face in front of a seven-month-old baby in June.

Judge Rupert Lowe scalded Brazier for his campaign of violence and humiliation, adding that he posed a ‘significant risk to the public’ but could not impose an extended sentence because his powers were restricted.
