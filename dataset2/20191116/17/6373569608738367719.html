Plymouth stabbing suspect still held by police as alleged victim recovers in hospital

Detectives confirm the men are known to each other as their inquiries continue

16 Nov 2019, 17:31 by Carl Eve

Police are continuing to hold a man suspected of stabbing his victim several times in a Ford block of flats late last night.

Investigators have told Plymouth Live the 23-year-old local man was arrested on suspicion of wounding with intent to cause grievous bodily harm following the incident the block in Alexandra Road at around 10.15pm on Friday evening.

Police have confirmed the men are known to each other and no-one else was involved.

The alleged victim, described as being aged in his 20s, remains at Derriford Hospital where he is conscious and stable. He is understood to have suffered multiple wounds to his neck and back, although his injuries were described by police as "not life threatening".

The scene has been guarded since last night at residents told Plymouth Live that the National Police Air Service helicopter was seen and heard over the city at around 10.30pm last night.

Detectives have carried out door to door inquiries, in an attempt to glean any information they can from residents in the block.

Anyone with information regarding the incident can contact police via phone on 101, or via email on 101@dcpolice.uk, quoting log 919 of November 15. Alternatively, you can call Crimestoppers on 0800 555 111 and report information anonymously.
