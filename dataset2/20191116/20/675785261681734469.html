MI5 to recruit 'working class spies' in a black and ethnic minority diversity drive

Called the Summer Diversity Intelligence Internship it is aimed at black and ethnic minority recruits who are 'full of enthusiasm, with a sense of team spirit and a passion for learning'

16 Nov 2019, 20:29 by Liz Perkins

James Bond prefers an Aston Martin – but today’s spy chiefs are after ­candidates with Ford Fiestas.

MI5 has launched a recruitment programme to lure interns from a wider social background.

The posts are aimed at ethnic minority applicants from working class families.

They must be problem solvers full of enthusiasm, with a sense of team spirit and a passion for learning, who are determined to make a positive impact, says the advert.

Candidates are warned not to discuss their application for the £24,717-a-year post with anyone other than a close family member.

Director general Andrew Parker said: “Keeping the country safe is a challenge that requires the best efforts of the richest mix of the most talented people.

 

"So it is essential that ours is an inclusive workplace offering rewarding careers for all.”

Earlier this year, the Social Mobility Foundation charity named MI6 one of the top 75 UK employers for promoting social mobility.

Charity boss David Johnston said: “They are very aware of the stereotype – you go to Eton, Oxford and then you become James Bond.

“They also realise that there’s an awful lot of talent in the country that would not even consider working for them or think that they would be suitable candidates.”

MI6 – which was bombarded with interest from working class applicants after last year’s Salisbury poison attack – is looking to appoint 800 new staff over two years.
