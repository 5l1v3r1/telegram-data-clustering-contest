Emmerdale star Michelle Hardwick says 'I'm so glad I can help fans come out'

Actress, 43, says she gets trolled over role as Charity Dingle’s fiancée Vanessa Woodfield - but she gets far more messages of support as soaps normalise same-sex relationships

16 Nov 2019, 20:20 by Phil Cardy, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/phil-cardy/

Defiant Emmerdale star Michelle Hardwick has told of her pride at helping fans of the soap come out as gay.

The lesbian actress revealed she gets trolled over her role as Charity Dingle’s fiancée Vanessa Woodfield – but the homophobic taunts are far outnumbered by messages of support.

Michelle, 43, said: “I get a few, ‘You just need a real man’, or ‘Vanessa just needs a real man’ comments. You think, ‘Oh right, really?’

“And then you look at their profile picture and you think, ‘Yep, you’d be it’.

“But soaps are fabulous at normalising same-sex relationships. If you’re showing two males or two females, you are normalising it in people’s houses every night and that’s what we need to be doing.”

 

Michelle, who married Emmerdale producer Kate Brooks in September, said fans are largely “very supportive” – and even thankful for her role as the show’s feisty vet.

She said: “I had a tweet only the other day from a girl saying she was so scared to come out to her dad – but he said to her, ‘Look, as long as you’re happy, I’m happy’.

"So I tweeted her and said, ‘I’m proud of you. I’m proud of your dad’. If we’re helping even just that one girl, then it’s absolutely brilliant.”

She thinks Vanessa’s relationship with Charity, played by Emma Atkins, could also be a hit with fans “because we’re older women, in our 40s”.

She added: “It might even be the way they got together. They didn’t necessarily like each other. It was an attraction that happened naturally and they were fighting it.

 

Speaking about her first same-sex relationship at 18 she said: “I tried to fight it. I had a few boyfriends, but it never felt right. You just battle with it, don’t you? It was hard.”

Michelle didn’t come out to her family until her late 20s. “I was scared about upsetting them,” she said. “But they’ve been so supportive.”

Speaking about her recent marriage in Memphis to Kate, she added: “We’re still on cloud nine. It was the best day of my life.”

But she’s not expecting Vanessa and ­Charity’s big day to go as well.

She said: “If Emmerdale weddings are anything to go by, it can’t just go smoothly, can it? I’d be very shocked if it did.”
