Falkirk sack Ray McKinnon as League One strugglers call time on ill-fated reign

The 49-year-old has paid the price for an up and down start to the season

16 Nov 2019, 20:56 by Graeme Young, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/graeme-young/

Ray McKinnon has been sacked as Falkirk boss after the League One side's latest stuttering result.

The 49-year-old has paid the price for an up and down start to the season with the pre-season title contenders sitting in fourth place.

And the Bairns have moved quickly to remove McKinnon after the 1-1 draw Dumbarton leaves them three points behind league leaders Airdrie.

The former Dundee United boss caused controversy when he left Morton in August 2018 to join Falkirk just weeks into his reign at Cappielow.

And McKinnon couldn't revive slumping Falkirk as they were relegated from the Championship last season.

He was backed in the summer transfer window but results haven't improved with just six wins from 14 league games.

It comes just after Falkirk called time on American-based businessman Mark Campbell's pursuit of the club.

The club released a brief statement on Saturday evening announcing his exit.

It read: "Falkirk FC can confirm that Ray McKinnon and Darren Taylor have been relieved of their duties as first team manager and assistant manager with immediate effect.

"Interim chairman, Lex Miller and the board of directors wish to place on record their best wishes to Ray and Darren for the future."

McKinnon appeared in a sombre mood just hours ago as he discussed his side's dreary display against Dumbarton.

He said: "The late goal is scant consolation. We come here wanting the three points, we've walked away with one. So I'm really disappointed with the performance and result today.

"I just think today we were below average right through the team. There were a lot of stray passes and never had a flow to our game.

"There's maybe a wee lack of confidence as well. If you pull all that together you get a below average performance and that's what we got."
