Romelu Lukaku contradicts Ole Gunnar Solskjaer's comments on Man Utd exit

Romelu Lukaku admits he told Ole Gunnar Solskjaer he wanted to leave Manchester United in March, five months before completing his move to Inter Milan

16 Nov 2019, 12:59 by Liam Prenderville, http://www.facebook.com/mirrorfootball

Romelu Lukaku has claimed Ole Gunnar Solskjaer wanted him to stay at Manchester United .

Lukaku, 26, left United for Inter Milan in a £73m deal in the summer after falling down the pecking order under Solskjaer.

The Belgian striker's absence has been felt with Marcus Rashford carrying the burden of being United's main attacking threat this season.

After Lukaku's exit, Solskjaer suggested he 'happily' let him go, insisting: "Romelu's head was not here for us working forward as a group.

"I have got lots of respects for Romelu but his time was up here."

Lukaku has claimed Solskjaer wanted him to stick around but he was determined to leave, initially outlining his intentions to the United boss back in March.

“Solskjaer has my eternal respect. He understood me when I said in March that it was time for me to leave," he told Het Belang van Limburg.

“I have been in the Premier League as an 18-year-old boy and now I am 26. Solskjaer wanted to keep me, but I was ready for something new.”

Lukaku has enjoyed a decent start to life in Milan, scoring nine times in 12 appearances.

He has faced problems off the pitch though after being subjected to racial abuse from opposition supporters.

Speaking ahead of Belgium's clash with Russia, he said: "I knew it would happen. I was prepared because I had spoken to other footballers about it.

"But I'm having a good time in Italy. The people on the street are very good to me and it's on that, that I want to focus."
