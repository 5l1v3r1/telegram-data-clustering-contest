Meet the Shiremoor takeaway queen who has shed six stone

Tina Hassan, 39, works at the Route 66 takaway shop at Shiremoor and has to resist the kebabs and pizzas she once loved after dropping from a dress size 22 to a size 12 after joining Slimming World

16 Nov 2019, 12:00 by Lisa Hutchinson

Takeaway shop manageress Tina Hassan gorged on the pizzas and kebabs she served and ballooned to a massive 22 dress size.

Weighing in at a huge 18 stone 8 lbs she realised she needed to change her eating habits to get healthy.

Suffering from daily migraines her doctor said it was her sheer weight that was the root to her symptoms.

And she joined Slimming World in a bid to shed the pounds.

Now the 39-year-old has reached her target weight of 11 stone 13 lbs and is now down to a slinky size 12.

“It is so difficult working in the takeaway shop and serving the pizzas I loved but I do resist them now and think of the healthy recipes I can cook the Slimming World way,” said Tina, of Shiremoor, North Tyneside.

For six years Tina has worked in the Route 66 takeaway in Shiremoor and grew partial to the foods she dished out. But it was her unhealthy lifestyle that caused her severe migraines.

She added: “I was suffering constant migraines on a daily basis and was hiding in the house because of them but also because of my low self confidence and my weight. I didn’t like how I looked and realised I had to do something about it.

“It was the doctor at the hospital that said my weight was causing strain on my body. I had bad knees, a bad back and hips and that was the cause of the migraines. But now my aches and pains have all gone and I only get the occasional migraine, which I can cope with. I feel brilliant.”

Tina now works out in the gym and has swapped the fatty foods for healthy options.

“I’ve even figured out that the chicken kebabs with salad and chilli sauce we sell aren’t that fattening so I can have one of these now and again.

“Before I never ate a vegetable and now my favourite is broccoli. I have dry fried broccoli with Cajun spice and I love it. I’ll eat dry fried vegetables with a low fat yogurt dip, you feel really good about yourself when you are doing it.”

Mum-of-one Tina now is addicted to clothes shopping and visits Manchester regularly for retail trips with 19-year-old son Arron.

“I like shopping more than him now, I’ve got three wardrobes full of clothes. Aaron is very proud of me as I am of him. He’s training to be a chef and he’s seen how healthy food has helped me.”

Tina is winning compliments from her takeaway customers as they comment on her weight loss.

“I love my job and have some fantastic customers, lots of which have noticed and mentioned the weight loss, which is lovely. I was definitely addicted to pizzas, kebabs and crisps but now I know how to cook things a healthy way,” added Tina.

“I’m nearly 40 years of age and never knew how to cook things this way and I now love what I eat. I’m still amazed and proud of myself, I did it.”

Tina is so chuffed with her weight loss she is now becoming a Slimming World consultant to help others on their weight loss journeys.

She is super excited about re-launching her group and can’t wait to meet everyone and start helping more people reach their goals.

Tina’s Slimming World group will be held at Foxhunters Sports Pavilion, Preston Gate, North Shields, every Wednesday at 5.30pm and 7.30pm from November 20.
