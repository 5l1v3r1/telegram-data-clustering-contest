Parish Notes Nov. 16-22

16 Nov 2019, 12:00 by Jocelyn Bennett, Saskatoon StarPhoenix

Parish events for the coming week.

Please send items for Parish Notes to weekend@thestarphoenix.com with the words “Parish Notes” in the subject line. Submission deadline is the Friday one week before at 10 a.m.

Psychology, Spirituality, Sexuality and Intimacy: Saturday, 9 a.m. to 4 p.m., at Queen’s House Retreat and Renewal Centre, 601 Taylor St. West. An interactive workshop exploring the relationship between psychology and spirituality, sexuality, and intimacy in our lives. With Rev. Wilburn Nelson. Cost is $40, with $10 for lunch. Register at 306-242-1916, receptionist@queenshouse.org. Information at queenshouse.org.

Poinsettia Tea, Craft & Bake Sale: Saturday, 10 a.m. to 3 p.m., at St. Stephen’s Anglican Church, 10 Grosvenor Cres. Christmas crafts, baking, and lunch. Admission is free.

Clavet Christmas Craft Sale: Saturday, 10 a.m. to 3 p.m., at Clavet Hall. Presented by Clavet United Church. Handcrafted items and homemade lunch. Admission is free.

Grace-Westminster UCW Christmas Tea and Bake Sale: Saturday, 2 to 4 p.m., at Grace-Westminster United Church, 505 10th St. East. Variety table, jewelry and scarves, bake table, silent auction, door prizes, and children’s table. Admission is by donation.

Affirming Sunday: Sunday, 11:15 a.m.,  at St. Thomas Wesley United Church, 808 20th St. West. Guest speaker Roslyn M. Compton on interdependence benefits us all. Coffee and refreshments will be available after the service. Everyone is welcome.

The Gift — Enjoy God’s Gifts Together: Sunday, 6 p.m., at the Western Development Museum. A fundraising dinner presented by LuMinHoS (Lutheran Ministry in Hospitals of Saskatoon). Christmas dinner, bake sale, cake auction, speakers and entertainment. With guest speakers Brynn Boback-Lane and Dr. Elke Mau, and music by the Saskatoon Men’s Chorus and Marley Hauk. Cost is $40. Tickets at 306-244-4743, 306-229-3515, office@luminhos.org, luminhos.org.

Silent Guided Retreat: Nov. 25-28, at Queen’s House Retreat and Renewal Centre, 601 Taylor St. West. Meet daily with a spiritual director and spend time praying with scripture. With Dianne Mantyka. Live-in cost is $525. Register at 306-242-1916, receptionist@queenshouse.org by Monday. Information at queenshouse.org.

Exploring the Soul Energies of the Enneagram Centres: Nov. 23, 9:30 a.m. to 4 p.m., at Queen’s House Retreat and Renewal Centre, 601 Taylor St. West. Explore how the divine energies of the enneagram connect us to our soul, how to listen to the soul and how to live from that deeper place. With Rick McCorrister. Cost is $95 for one session or $260 for all three, and includes lunch. Register at 306-242-1916, receptionist@queenshouse.org by Wednesday. Information at queenshouse.org.

Gala Fundraising Dinner: Thursday, 5:30 p.m., at Queen’s House Retreat and Renewal Centre, 601 Taylor St. West. Dinner, silent auction, entertainment by Greystone Singers, and raffle. Cost is $125, and includes a $75 charitable receipt and a raffle ticket. Tickets at 306-242-1916, receptionist@queenshouse.org.

Pasta Dinner: Thursday, 6 p.m., at St. George’s Cathedral Auditorium, 214 Ave. M South. Adults cost $15, children ages six to 12 cost $10, and children ages five and under eat free. Tickets at 306-664-3459, 306-382-1647, 306-370-1024.

One Light Chant and Dance: Second Saturday of the month, 7:30 p.m., at Stensrud Lodge, 2202 Mceown Ave. A Dances of Universal Peace event combining singing of sacred phrases from all the world’s spiritual traditions with simple circle dances and live music. No experience necessary. Admission is free. Information at chantndance@sasktel.net.

Sunday Meditation: Sundays, 10 a.m., at Windsor Chapel, 704 Windsor St. Presented by the Centre for Spiritual Living Saskatoon. Learn how to create the life you long for. Sunday celebration follows at 10:30 a.m. Information at 306-975-2022, cslsaskatoon.com.

Buddhist Meditation: Sundays, 4-5:30 p.m. Guided meditation taught by the monks from the Buddha Meditation Centre of Saskatoon. Everyone is welcome. Class is free. Information at meditationsaskatoon.org, 306-374-2840.

Service of Prayer for Post-Trauma Survivors: First Monday of the month, September to June, 5:30 to 8 p.m., at St. Matthew’s Anglican Church, 135 109th St. West. Open to anyone who is post trauma in need of prayerful support. With Rev. Karen Walter.

Celtic Evening Service: Tuesdays, 7 p.m., at St. George’s Anglican Church, 624 Ave. I South. Celtic music, tea and coffee. Each mid-month service will begin with a potluck at 6 p.m.

Ladies’ Bible Study: Wednesdays, 9:30 to 11:30 a.m., at Louise Avenue Congregational Church, 1602 Louise Ave. Study will be “The Holy Spirit.”  With coffee and fellowship. Information at sbwest@sasktel.net.

L’Arche Prayer Nights: Wednesdays, 7 p.m. Prayer and fellowship with L’Arche Saskatoon, an ecumenical Christian community. Everyone is welcome from all faiths. Information at wthiessen@larchesaskatoon.org.

Taizé Service: Second Wednesday of each month, 7 to 8 p.m., A time of music, prayer, silence and contemplation. Everyone is welcome. Information at 306-242-1916, receptionist@queenshouse.org.

GriefShare Recovery Group: Thursdays to Dec. 5, 7-9 p.m., at Louise Avenue Congregational Church, 1602 Louise Ave. Anyone who has lost a loved one is welcome to attend. Information at 306-262-4119, 306-931-1297.

Women in Ministry Friday Luncheon: First Friday of the month, 11:45 a.m. to 1:30 p.m., Queen’s House Retreat and Renewal Centre, 601 Taylor St. West. For women employed in ecclesial ministry; retired from a ministry position; or studying for ecclesial ministry. Cost is $13. Register at 306-242-1916, receptionist@queenshouse.org.

Craft, Bake & Book Sale: Nov. 23, 8 a.m. to 2 p.m., at McClure United Church, 4025 Taylor St. East. With a pancake breakfast from 7:30 to 10:30 a.m., and a hotdog lunch from 11 a.m. to 1 p.m. Everyone is welcome.

Pre-Advent Market and Tea: Nov. 23, 1 to 4 p.m., at St. John’s Anglican Cathedral Parish Hall, 816 Spadina Cres. Christmas baking, homemade turkey, pies, jams, jellies, preserves, crafts, plants, books, and a children’s make-and-take area. Tea tickets are $5.
