Dan Osborne puts on united front with kids as Jacqueline Jossa enters jungle

Dan Osborne posed with son Teddy and daughters Ella and Mia as his wife Jacqueline Jossa made her way into the I'm A Celebrity camp

16 Nov 2019, 12:27 by Emmeline Saunders

Dan Osborne has got his hands full as his wife Jacqueline Jossa has jetted Down Under ahead of her I'm A Celebrity... Get Me Out Of Here! stint.

The former TOWIE star, who was fired from the ITV reality show in 2015 over threats to stab his ex-girlfriend , put on a united front as he posed with his three children.

In the topless snap, Dan snuggled up with his eldest son Teddy and his daughters Ella and Mia with Jac as they got used to life without their mum.

"Morninggg not the best picture but this is us first thing in the morning, cuddles with daddy always first, then breakfast time," Dan captioned the shot.

His former TOWIE co-star Mark Wright commented underneath: "V cute mate."

New dad Dom Lever of Love Island fame also gushed: "Love this bro."

And another follower joked, "Now mamas away, we need to see dad doing hair tutorials", to which Dan replied, "OK I'll give it a go", along with a crying-laughing emoji.

It comes as ex-EastEnders star Jacqueline was spotted carrying out her first task for I'm A Celebrity: making her way to the snake and bug-infested jungle camp.

She and the other stars - including Caitlyn Jenner, Ian Wright and Nadine Coyle - were seen arriving in mud-splattered 4x4s to a clearing in Australia's New South Wales before clambering into helicopters or canoes.

Caitlyn and her Team Red buddy James Haskell were given a rickety canoe to get across to the other side of a river, while Team Yellow - Nadine Coyle and Adele Roberts - were tasked with jumping out of a helicopter.

Ahead of her jungle stint, mum of two Jacqueline insisted it was time for people to stop thinking of her as a pushover , after several high-profile wobbles in her and Dan's marriage.

The pair temporarily split last year while Jac was heavily pregnant with Mia, and Dan moved out amid constant arguments before they later reconciled.

"I think people think I am a pushover or a doormat," Jac commented this week.

"I also think some people pity me. I want to show I am fine, I am up for a laugh and that I will go for it and try my best."

And Jacqueline admitted she is going to struggle away from her two daughters during her jungle stint.

The actress found it so difficult she didn't even explain to her children she was going to be on I'm A Celebrity.

"I didn't know how to face even telling them. In the end, I thought the best idea was to say, 'Mummy is going away for work' and I would see them soon. My family is so close-knit and I will miss them all terribly," she added.
