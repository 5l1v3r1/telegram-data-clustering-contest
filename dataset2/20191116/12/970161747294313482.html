Gunmen open fire on buses carrying Muslims to vote in Sri Lankan election

16 Nov 2019, 12:32

Gunmen have opened fire on buses carrying Muslims to cast their ballots in the Sri Lankan presidential election.

Attackers also threw stones and blocked more than a hundred vehicles with burning tires, according to a group monitoring election violence.

Police officials confirmed the convoy of buses – carrying hundreds of Muslim residents from the north-west town of Puttalam – were hit on Saturday morning.

There are no reported injuries and officers are investigating, said Manjula Gajanayake, spokesman for the Colombo-based Centre for Monitoring Election Violence.

It is unclear whether any of the attackers have been arrested.

The presidential election comes after 269 people were killed by Islamic extremists in a suicide bomb attack on Easter Sunday this year.

Fears remain among minority Tamils and Muslims about a return to power of Gotabaya Rajapaksa, a hard-line former defence official under his brother, ex-President Mahinda Rajapaksa.

In his campaign, Mr Rajapaksa cast himself as the only candidate capable of protecting Sri Lankans from attacks.

The Elections Commission had encouraged Muslims – who fled their homes in the northern district of Mannar in 1982 when the separatist insurgency of Tamil rebels began to grow – to register to vote.

However, they had not arranged enough transportation to bring them from their homes in the northwestern district of Puttalam, Mr Gajanayake claimed.

Mr Rajapaksa had been expected to win over ruling party candidate Housing Minister Sajith Premadasa.

However, the race appeared very close before voting day.

President Maithripala Sirisena, who was elected in 2015, is not seeking re-election.

Nearly 16 million of Sri Lanka’s 22 million population are eligible to vote to select a new president from a record 35 candidates.

Results are expected as early as Sunday.
