Police hunt masked man after attempted knifepoint robbery in Torquay

The incident took place at around 8.30am at the McColl's shop

16 Nov 2019, 11:23 by Shannon Hards

Police are hunting for a masked man after an attempted knifepoint robbery in Torquay early this morning.

An individual entered the McColl's shop on St Marychurch Road at Plainmoor at around 8.30am.

The Devon and Cornwall Police force incident manager, who oversees all live incidents in the region, said: "An individual entered the store, dressed pretty much all in black with a mask on.

"They were in possession of a small knife and asked for contents of the till, which hasn't been handed over. They promptly left on foot.

"Officers were dispatched and enquiries are ongoing. Officers are still at the scene making enquiries and conducting searches.

"If anyone has any information or saw anyone around the McColl's shop on 155 St Marychurch Road, particularly a male in black at around 8.25am to 8.30am, we would ask them to get in touch quoting log number 257 of November 16."

It is believed the shop remains closed at this time. Nobody answered the phone there.

Anyone with information regarding the incident can contact police via phone on 101, or via email on 101@dcpolice.uk, quoting log 257 of November 16.

Alternatively, you can call Crimestoppers on 0800 555 111 and report information anonymously.
