Gateshead man found with sick images of kids on his phone and tablet

Stephen Earl pleaded guilty to possessing indecent images, which he collected over a two-year period

16 Nov 2019, 08:00 by Sara Nichol

A man was found to have vile images of children on his mobile phone and tablet.

Stephen Earl had collected the pictures over a two-year period and some were category A, the most serious kind, a court heard.

The sick images were discovered after police raided the 56-year-old's home address in Crowly Avenue, Gateshead, and seized electronic devices.

In total, 10 pictures were found, with three being in category A.

Now, Earl is facing a possible prison sentence after he pleaded guilty to three counts of making an indecent image of a child.

Oriana Frame, prosecuting at South Tyneside Magistrates' Court, said the photos were collected between March 2016 and July 2018.

She said: "The defendant's home address was searched as a result of a warrant where he lives with his wife.

"Multiple devices were seized as part of that search and they were analysed by police digital forensic examiners. Images were found on two devices - a mobile phone and a tablet.

"Both belonged to the defendant and he admitted they were his."

The court heard that three category A images, five category B and two category C were discovered on the two devices.

Miss Frame submitted that the case was too serious to be sentenced at the magistrates' court.

District Judge Davies agreed and Earl was released on conditional bail to next appear at Newcastle Crown Court on December 4.

Full mitigation will be heard on that occasion.
