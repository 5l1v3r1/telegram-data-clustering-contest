As cruel as Disney's evil villains may be, they sure are fascinating, and their twisted schemes make for some thrilling plot twists. Ariel wouldn't have met Prince Eric without Ursula, and without Jafar, Aladdin wouldn't have known the Genie, let alone ridden his iconic magic carpet. There's no doubt that these villains hold a special place in Disney fans' hearts, which is why interior design startup Modsy reimagined their evil fortresses into modern-day homes. Focusing on five wicked characters — Ursula, the Evil Queen, Cruella de Vil, Captain Hook, and Jafar — each room holds a sinister tone complementary to its villainous inspiration. If you can't hold back your sheer love for Disney enemies or are simply fascinated by what their dark residencies would look like in 2019, take a step inside their modern-day escapes and see for yourself with the imagery ahead!

Ursula From The Little Mermaid's Living Room

Ursula's home looks straight from under the sea, covered in ocean-like accents. From the bedroom's shell-shaped bed to the living room's sea-urchin-inspired coffee table, there's no doubt Ursula's modern-day home is inspired by the city of Atlantica. In addition to its nautical interior, the home also exhibits small pieces of Ursula's personality, such as her crystal ball and King Triton's trident, which she uses to steal his crown. If you enjoy the ocean look of this space, the items ahead will suit your taste.

Evil Queen From Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs' Living Room

"Mirror, mirror, on the wall, who's the fairest of them all?" The Evil Queen's modern-day house is filled with accents to flatter her fancy, such as a mirror in every room and a robust armchair that acts as her throne. In addition, the dark blues and purples that cover the home's walls and furniture are an ode to the queen's royal attire. Complete with recognizable treasures like red apples and self-portraits, this home is exactly how the Evil Queen would have dreamt it to be: all about her. Captain Hook From Peter Pan's Sitting Area

Inspired by Captain Hook's adventures at sea, the modern-day home shows off a classic colonial style. With a map of Neverland in the sitting area and telescope in the study, Captain Hook can always be prepared for his next attack. In fact, the sitting area is designed for his victories, in which he'd celebrate over a drink with his crew. Modsy specifically designed his sitting area with no couch, as they'd expect Captain Hook to be fairly private around others. If you love the style of Captain Hook's home, create the look for yourself with the products ahead.
