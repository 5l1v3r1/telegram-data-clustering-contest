Classy Germany Qualify For EURO 2020

16 Nov 2019, 23:44 by SL International

UEFA Euro 2020 Qualifier

Result: Germany 4-0 Belarus

Date: 16 November 2019

Venue: Borussia-Park

Germany qualified for the 2020 European Championship after an emphatic 4-0 victory over Belarus at the Borussia-Park Stadium on Saturday evening.

The home side started with an all-out attacking trio led by Serge Gnabry, Timo Werner and Leon Goretzka, with Matthias Ginter in for the suspended Emre Can.

Germany looked to prize open the visitors in the earlier moments, as a stinging shot from Ginter had Alyaksandr Hutar at full stretch with the parry eventually going behind for a corner.  Die Mannschaft threatened once again before the 20th minute mark though Kimmich long-range shot rattled against the top of the side-netting.

Belarus were soon on the charge and closed in to the opener eight minutes to the interval following a rasping curler from Ihar Stasevich, but Manuel Neuer went full stretch to parry the forward.

The pressure finally paid off for the Germans when Ginter flicked home from point-blank range following a low cross from Gnabry three minutes before halftime.

Germany pushed to second gear four minutes from the restart as Goretzka picked his spot to cushion home a well-timed delivery from Toni Kroos to double the lead.

Ginter, who landed the opener, carved out the third goal on 55 minutes when an exchange of passes on the right ended with Kroos calmly slotting beyond Hutar.

Neuer denied the visitors a consolation when he got down to push Stasevich’s spot-kick behind for a corner with 15 minutes to go following a foul in the danger area.

Germany drew the contest to a close in the last seven minutes when Kroos twisted and turned in the area for a delightful near post finish past Hutar as the hosts ran out 4-0 winners to secure qualification.
