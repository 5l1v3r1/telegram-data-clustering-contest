Knowledge Drop: How Suge Knight Forced Dr. Dre To Change His Album Title To ‘2001’

16 Nov 2019, 15:30 by Grant Rindner

Today marks the 20th anniversary of Dr. Dre’s 2001, which redefined the West Coast icon’s legacy and career. But according to an MTV article from 1999, it was originally supposed to be released under the name Chronic 2000, a reference to his seminal 1992 debut The Chronic and the then-impending new millennium. The change was forced upon Dre by Suge Knight, his former friend turned foe and Death Row Records co-founder, who released a compilation titled Suge Knight Represents: Chronic 2000.

According to Andrew Barber on Twitter, Suge’s Chronic 2000 was advertised as featuring unreleased music from Snoop and even Dre himself, but neither artist appeared on the project.

In May 1999, Chronic 2000 was released to critical derision but a solid commercial performance, peaking at No. 11 on the Billboard 200. The record featured music from former Dre collaborators like 2Pac, Daz Dillinger, and Outlawz. It also included diss songs against Dre collaborators Eminem (“Presenting Miilkbone”) and Snoop Dogg (“Don’t Forget Where You Came From”).

The latter even features a jab at Dre that alludes to Suge taking his album title, where Swoop G raps:

Dr. Dre thanks a lot for that Chronic you dropped
Now I’m droppin mine in ‘99
The present is mine
In the year two-thous, please believe we holdin it down

The rift between Dre and Suge was cemented in 1996, when Dre left Death Row to found Aftermath. Dre even released a maligned compilation of his own Dr. Dre Presents… The Aftermath, which featured artists like King T, Scarface, and RBX.

Ultimately, 2001 was still a massive success, going six times platinum and winning Dre and Eminem a Best Rap Performance by a Duo or Group Grammy for “Forgot About Dre.” With the help of producer Mel-Man, Dre reinvented his sound, moving away from G-funk to something more gothic and string-heavy.
