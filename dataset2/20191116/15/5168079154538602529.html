Pakistan air traffic controller saves Jaipur-Muscat flight after pilot’s Mayday alert: Report

16 Nov 2019, 15:28 by Times News Service

Muscat: An airline travelling to Muscat carrying 150 passengers from Jaipur in India was saved by an air traffic controller from Pakistan after it encountered abnormal weather conditions, according to media reports.

Pakistan-based The News International reported that the airline flying over Karachi on Thursday was caught in the middle of lightning and dropped down from an altitude of 36,000 feet to 34,000 feet almost immediately.

According to the report, the pilot immediately initiated emergency protocol and broadcast "Mayday" to nearby stations. The air traffic controller from Pakistan responded to the call of pilot and directed it through the dense air traffic in the vicinity for the remaining journey in the Pakistani airspace. It is worth noting that Pakistan had closed its airspace on February following airstrikes with India. — Agencies
