Gardai have 'foiled a Kinahan/Hutch feud hit' as two men were arrested and a loaded gun was seized in Dublin

A premises in the Clondalkin area was searched by gardai at around 11.30pm and gardai found a a loaded semi-automatic pistol.

Two men aged in their 20’s and 30’s were arrested.

Both men are detained at Clondalkin Garda Station.

Speaking today Assistant Commissioner John O’Driscoll of Special Crime Operations said "This is yet another successful intervention by the Garda Síochána in a potential threat to life incident, involving the seizure of a loaded firearm and the arrest of suspects”.

The older man is believed to have links to the highest levels of the Kinahan crime gang and is a suspect in at least two of the feud murders.

He was arrested in a garden room at the back of a house with a loaded gun beside him, RTE reports.

He is thought to have been living abroad but had returned to Ireland.

Gardai understand that Friday night's intervention saved a life and it is thought to be highly significant.
