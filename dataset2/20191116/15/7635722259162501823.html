Investigation after ATM robbery in Co Louth

16 Nov 2019, 15:25 by Jack Quann

Gardaí are appealing for witnesses after an ATM theft in Co Louth.

It happened on Main Street in Dunleer at around 3.30am on Saturday morning.

The ATM had been damaged in the Bank of Ireland branch - a digger and lorry were discovered at the scene.

Gardaí say "a significant amount" of damage was caused to the premises.

They are appealing to any person in Dunleer between 3.00am and 4.00am on Saturday and who saw anything unusual, or to any road users with camera footage, to make contact.

Gardaí are also appealing to anyone who saw any suspicious vehicles in the Dundalk area at this time.

They believe those responsible left the scene in a silver Toyota Land Cruiser, with a partial registration '04-MH', heading north of Dunleer.

They are appealing for any person who saw this vehicle - or knows the whereabouts of the vehicle - to contact Drogheda Garda station on 041-987-4200, the Gardaí Confidential Line at 1800-666-111 or any Garda station.
