Sabres game day: Rasmus Asplund makes his NHL debut

16 Nov 2019, 15:40

Matchup: Buffalo Sabres (9-6-3) vs. Ottawa Senators (8-10-1)
Where: KeyBank Center
When: 7:25 p.m faceoff due to Hockey Night in Canada broadcast and pregame Hockey Fights Cancer Night ceremonies
TV: MSG
Radio: WGR 550

Long-awaited debut: The local analytics community howled on June 25, 2016, when then-Sabres GM Tim Murray made a deal just before the start of the second day of the NHL Draft in KeyBank Center. Defenseman Mark Pysyk, the longtime darling of the numbers crowd, was sent to Florida along with a couple draft picks for Dmitry Kulikov and a second-round pick.

Murray thus moved up from No. 38 to No. 33 to get the man he wanted – Swedish center Rasmus Asplund. Kulikov had a disastrous one-season run in Buffalo while Pysyk remains in Florida but has been in and out of the lineup as a healthy scratch.

For his part, right until he was fired after the 2016-17 season, Murray insisted the deal was about moving up to get Asplund and always felt the Swede would develop into a strong two-way player in the Buffalo lineup.

We get our first look at Asplund tonight as the 21-year-old will make his NHL debut for the Sabres against the visiting Ottawa Senators. He will become the seventh player this season to make his Buffalo debut – but the first of that group to play their initial game in the NHL. Johan Larsson remains day to day with an upper body injury.

"You're just looking back at all the workouts you did in all the summers past," said Asplund, who noted his family will be watching on TV back home at Sweden with the opening faceoff well past 1 a.m. "There's a lot of emotions going. I don't even know if I'm happy, nervous all at the same time."

"We're looking forward to seeing him step in here today. He's ready," said coach Ralph Krueger. "It's a big hole to fill but we think Rasmus is more than ready for this opportunity. ... We haven't given him a big to-do list. The positive thing is training camp, all the principles and concepts have been laid out for him. They're working with the same language in Rochester."

A big part of Asplund's emotion is rooted in tonight's celebration of Hockey Fights Cancer Night. He lost both of his paternal grandparents to the disease, with his grandfather passing away shortly after he returned home following the 2016 draft.

"I visited him all of May every day, went to the draft, got home and he passed away the day after I got home," Asplund said. "He basically waited for me to get home from the draft, show him the draft jersey and all that. So this is pretty big, emotional for me and my family."

The lineup: Linus Ullmark (3-3-1, 3.12/.915) will be in goal as the Sabres try to snap their 0-4-2 slide. They will use the same six defensemen they did Thursday against Carolina, with Colin Miller and John Gilmour being healthy scratches. The most recent defense pairs had Jake McCabe with Rasmus Ristolainen, Rasmus Dahlin with Brandon Montour and Marco Scandella with Henri Jokiharju.
