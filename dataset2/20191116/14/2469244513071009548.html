Officials shocked as lorry heads wrong way on M5 to dodge traffic

Police also say some drivers are using the hard shoulder

16 Nov 2019, 14:24 by Joel Cooper

Police and Highways officials say they are "shocked" by some of the tactics being used by drivers to avoid traffic on the M5.

There is severe congestion on the motorway after a serious crash this morning and a second crash hours later - both of which required traffic to be stopped.

But the authorities dealing with the incidents cannot believe the way some motorists are choosing to handle the situation.

Highways England Tweeted this shocking picture of a large lorry completely blocking both roads while trying to U-turn down a closed road.

In the Tweet they said: "There is alot of congestion on the #M5 today due to multiple serious incidents.

"This does not however provide anyone with the ability or excuse to go through roadwork closures, or go down the road the wrong way."

Police have also shared their frustration with the behaviour of drivers.

An off-duty officer for the Alliance Roads Policing Team tweeted this picture below showing drivers on the hard shoulder.

The officer said: "Currently sat in stationary traffic on the M5 South due to road closure at J22 off duty.

"Shocked how many people think it's acceptable to use the hard shoulder."

Earlier Highways England also tweeted a picture of motorists trying to negotiate around cones to escape traffic by driving down a closed road.

They shared the picture below, saying: "This action is ILLEGAL!

"You are putting yourselves, our road workers and other road users at great risk!

"The traffic has now been released, we ask for your patience during these stoppages."A serious crash around 6am this morning has left a woman in hospital with serious head injuries.

The collision, which involved a car and a lorry, happened on the M5 Southbound carriageway between junctions 22 and 23.

It is understood the A38 is also affected as traffic diverts onto the main road to avoid the motorway closure.

A second crash around an hour ago left a car on its roof on the M5 Northdbound between Junctions 18 and 17.

The road was blocked and traffic was stropped while police, fire and ambulance crews made their way to the scene.
