Wrexham fans troll Rangers with Celtic scarves and banner at Ibrox in Challenge Cup clash

16 Nov 2019, 18:03 by David Fowler

WREXHAM fans trolled Rangers during their Tunnock's Caramel Wafer Challenge Cup clash by unveiling a Celtic banner and scarves in the away end at Ibrox.

The National League side were backed by 1000 travelling supporters at Ibrox.

And while they were disappointed to see their side eliminated after the Light Blues strolled to a 2-0 victory, supporters took their chance to take a swipe at Gers fans.

A banner which had he badges of Celtic, Bohemians and Wrexham was held aloft in the rammed away section.

Green and white Hoops scarves were also waved as fans taunted the Ibrox faithful during the match.

But the Light Blues youngster made easy work of the clash on the park as Jamie Barjonas and Nathan Young-Coombes scored either side of the break.

Jon Flanagan and George Edmundson were handed run-outs in the youth side defence as they secured a win in front of just over 5000 fans.

Rangers Colts are now into the semi-final of the competition after wins over Berwick, Stranraer, Ballymena, Solihull Moors and Wrexham.

For a spot in the final they could now face Raith Rovers, Partick Thistle or Inverness.
