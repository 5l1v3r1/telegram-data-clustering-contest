Tua Tagovailoa Was Reportedly “Screaming In Pain” After Hip Injury vs. Mississippi State

16 Nov 2019, 18:46 by Matt Lombardi

Alabama quarterback Tua Tagovailoa suffered what appeared to be a significant hip injury just before halftime of his team’s game against Mississippi State on Saturday afternoon. Apparently, Tagovailoa was in a great deal of pain.

Tagovailoa was dragged to the ground by two Bulldogs defenders and appeared to land awkwardly. ESPN’s Molly McGrath reports that the Alabama quarterback was “screaming in pain” on the ground after the injury.

Tagovailoa was carted off the field. The only word we have is that it’s some kind of hip injury.

Tagovailoa came into the game nursing an ankle injury he suffered against Tennessee back in October. He played against LSU after a “tightrope” surgery just two weeks prior.
