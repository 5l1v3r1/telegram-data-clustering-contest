Video: Mississippi State Fans Show Great Class After Tua Tagovailoa Injury

16 Nov 2019, 18:55 by Andrew McCarty

In the final moments of the first half of Alabama’s game against Mississippi State, star quarterback Tua Tagovailoa left the game on a cart.

He rolled out to his left, but was hit by two Mississippi State defenders. Head coach Nick Saban revealed Tua suffered a hip injury on the play.

Tua, who played last weekend in a limited capacity against LSU, is also recovering from an ankle injury. While he played last weekend, it was clear he wasn’t at 100-percent – he even said after the game that he played on one foot.

Several analysts, including Tim Tebow, suggested Tua shouldn’t play in today’s game.

Regardless, as Tagovailoa left the game on a cart before the end of the first half, Bulldogs fans showed an incredible amount of sportsmanship for the injured quarterback.

Mississippi State fans rang their cowbells as Tua left the field.
