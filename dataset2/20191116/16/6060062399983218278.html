The two students killed by a classmate in a shooting at a high school in Santa Clarita, California, have been identified.

Fourteen-year-old Dominic Blackwell and 15-year-old Gracie Anne Muehlberger were identified by the Los Angeles County coroner's office as the two students to die in Thursday's shooting at Saugus High School. Three other students, a boy and two girls, were hospitalized with injuries but expected to recover, according to ABC 7 News.

The gunman, identified as 16-year-old Nathaniel Berhow, shot himself in the head after opening fire and died the following day.

Tributes for Gracie and Dominic poured out on social media following the shooting.

In a statement posted on a GoFundMe for Gracie's family, her parents called her the "light of our lives."

"It is with the most unexplainable brokenness that we share our Gracie went to be with Jesus on Thursday morning," said a statement on the GoFundMe. "Our vivacious, funny, loyal, light of our lives, Cinderella, the daughter we always dreamed to have, fiercely strong and lover of all things fashionable â€” was our best friend. She is going to be missed more than words will ever be able to express."

A GoFundMe for Dominic described the 14-year-old as a "shining light personified by a goofy laugh, cheesy smile, and a huge, caring heart."

Neighbors told CBS Los Angeles that Dominic came from an "incredible family" that was "very loving."

"This has been a very traumatic situation for the community for my neighborhood in particular," Dave Lyznick, a neighbor of the Blackwell family, told CBS Los Angeles.

Los Angeles County Sheriff's Department Sheriff Alex Villanueva said on Friday that the gunman's attack was planned, though the targets appeared to be random.

"It was deliberate. He knew how many rounds he had, for example," Villanueva said, according to the New York Post. "He seemed very familiar with firing the weapon."

Tyler Nilson, a 17-year-old senior who was on the cross country team with Nathaniel, told Insider that he never would have expected this from his classmate.

"To an extent I feel ashamed that I didn't know him better, since we weren't super close friends," he told Insider. "We're all bewildered, since he never stood out as a loner or as the aggressive type. He was somebody I'd least expect to commit such an atrocity."
