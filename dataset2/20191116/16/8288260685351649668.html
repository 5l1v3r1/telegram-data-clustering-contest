Big night as BirminghamLive scoops string of honours at Midlands Media Awards

Zoe Chamberlain named reporter of the year and Jane Haynes picked up story of the year on night of success at Villa Park

16 Nov 2019, 16:19 by James Cartledge, https://www.birminghammail.co.uk/authors/james-cartledge/

Journalists from BirminghamLive were among the big winners at this year’s Midlands Media Awards.

It was a night on which writers, photographers and online teams from our Reach plc parent company notched up five wins and 14 commendations.

BirminghamLive’s Zoe Chamberlain, whose work also appears in the Birmingham Mail and Sunday Mercury, was named news reporter of the year.

Her winning portfolio included exclusives on disturbing social media suicide sites, violence at a city school, and breadline children facing holiday hunger.

Jane Haynes won the Story of the Year award for her reporting of LGBT protests at schools on BirminghamLive. She was also highly commended in the feature writer of the year category.

 

The Birmingham Mail and BirminghamLive were named Team of the Year for their work together on ‘Back Where We Belong’, an Aston Villa promotion special.

BirminghamLive’s Sanjeeta Bains was highly commended in the feature writer of the year and story of the year stakes, and also for story of the year, an interview with a grieving mum whose son fell victim to knife crime.

And Christina Savvas, of BirminghamLive, was highly commended for the best use of social media.

Across our sister titles there were wins, too, for Bobby Bridge of the Coventry Telegraph, named Sports Journalist of the year, and The Staffordshire Newsletter, voted weekly newspaper of the year.

And the Sunday Mercury’s Mike Lockley was highly commended in both the columnist of the year and news reporter of the year categories.

Others from Reach plc to be highly commended included Fred Hughes, The Sentinel/Stoke on Trent Live (Columnist); The Sentinel/Stoke-on-Tent Live (Supplement); Steve Bould, The Sentinel/Stoke on Trent Live (Photographer); r  
Matthew Panter, Tamworth Herald (Sports Journalist); Chris Philpotts, Tamworth Herald (Weekly news reporter); Nicholas Reid, Derby Telegraph & Derbyshire Live (Daily news reporter); Lincolnshire Echo (Weekly newspaper of the year) and Lincolnshire Live (Website of the year).

The awards, organised by Birmingham Press Club, were staged at Villa Park’s Holte Suite, and presented by Bob Warman and Llewela Bailey.

More than 200 entries were submitted, and club chairman Llewela said: “The awards recognise the achievements of the region’s talented journalists, photographers, broadcasters and bloggers.

"Our experienced panel of judges really had their work cut out scrutinising a wealth of outstanding work.”
