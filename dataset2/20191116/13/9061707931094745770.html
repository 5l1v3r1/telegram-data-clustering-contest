'Unclear' - Three Derby County players who would benefit from a January transfer

Latest Derby County transfer news | Could any of these players benefit from a loan stint away from Pride Park?

16 Nov 2019, 13:05 by Mark Wakefield

There are a number of players in the Derby County squad that have not been seeing that much first-team action.

Rams boss Phillip Cocu is in his first season at Pride Park, and has been spending the first few months in the job figuring out what his best starting line-up is.

Some players have been featured regularly, while others have had to settle for a place on the bench, or perhaps not even a part of the matchday squad at all.

Others have also been working their way back from respective injuries, and are yet to get fully match fit.

We take a look at which Rams players could do with a spell on loan in the January transfer window for one reason or another.

Jonathan Mitchell

Jonathan Mitchell is currently third-choice goalkeeper at Pride Park, and has really struggled for first-team action this season.

Kelle Roos has been preferred as Cocu’s main stopper, while on-loan keeper Ben Hamer has been utilised in the two Carabao Cup fixtures.

Mitchell was on the substitute’s bench for the opening-day win over Huddersfield Town, but has not been part of the matchday squad since the arrival of Hamer.

Last season, Mitchell had loan spells at Oxford United and Shrewsbury Town, and it may been in the 24-year-old’s best interest to have another temporary stint away from Pride Park.

Max Bird

Max Bird broke onto the scene in September 2017 as a 16-year-old, when he featured in a Carabao Cup defeat to Barnsley at Oakwell.

His league debut would come over a year later when former manager Frank Lampard introduced the midfielder in place of Mason mount for the closing moments of a 2-1 win over Swansea City last December.

Since Cocu’s arrival, Bird has made two first-team appearances which have both come in the Carabao Cup against Scunthorpe and Nottingham Forest, respectively.

He was also an unused substitute in the 2-0 defeat to Hull City last month in the Sky Bet Championship – the only time he has featured in the matchday squad for a league game this season.

At 19 years old, there is still plenty for Bird to learn as a player, but a brief stint on loan to play regular first-team football may help his development in the long run.

Craig Forsyth

Another player who has been plagued with injuries in recent months is Craig Forsyth.

The left-back suffered anterior cruciate ligament (ACL) damage to his left knee in the home defeat by Aston Villa in November. His two other ACL injuries during his time at Derby were to his right knee.

Since then, the 30-year-old has been building up his fitness and has recently been featuring regularly for the Under-23s, most recently on Wednesday night in the 7-1 win over Leeds United in the Premier League Cup.

Derby currently have Max Lowe and Scott Malone as first-team left-backs, while youngster Lee Buchanan can also perform in that role.

It is unclear what the future holds for Forsyth, and maybe a loan spell away from Derby in order to get first-team minutes may be the best option to him.
