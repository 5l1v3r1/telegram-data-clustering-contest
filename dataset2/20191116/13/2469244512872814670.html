Serious M5 crash sees warning issued ahead of Exeter City v Cheltenham Town

The clash will see hundreds of fans descend on the ground in Exeter but there are two hour delays facing those travelling from Gloucester shire

16 Nov 2019, 13:02 by Philippa Jenkins

Football fans are being advised to leave earlier for the scheduled clash between Exeter City and Cheltenham Town this afternoon after a serious M5 crash.

The southbound carriageway of the motorway has been closed since early this morning after a crash at 6am near between junctions 22 and 23, near Bridgwater.

It has been confirmed by Avon and Somerset Police that a woman has been injured.

Police, fire and ambulance crews were called to the scene after a car crashed into the back of a lorry, leaving it wedged underneath the lorry.

A woman was still inside the car when firefighters arrived at the scene.

There are currently two hours of delays on the motorway and any fans heading the match at St James Park this afternoon for a 3pm kick off are being advised to leave early to avoid being late or missing the game entirely.

Cheltenham Town have tweeted the following advice:

"TRAVEL: An incident on the M5 around junction 22 of the motorway means traffic is delayed for the journey down to Exeter.

"We advise fans to consider leaving a little earlier."

Exeter City have also issued a warning.
