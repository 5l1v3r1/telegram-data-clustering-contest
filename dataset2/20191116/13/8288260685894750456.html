'It was best' - Newcastle United injury verdict might surprise Aston Villa fans

Aston Villa latest news | Dean Smith's side welcome Steve Bruce and the Magpies to Villa Park on November 25 when the Premier League returns from the international break

16 Nov 2019, 13:23 by Richard Cusack

Newcastle United will cope just fine without captain Jamaal Lascelles, according to former player Malcolm Macdonald.

Lascelles suffered a serious knee injury in a tussle with AFC Bournemouth striker Callum Wilson during the 2-1 defeat of Eddie Howe's side at St James' Park on Saturday.

He was replaced by Paul Dummett after 20 minutes and as a result Newcastle will be without him for at least six weeks - obviously ruling him out of the Villa Park trip on November 25 when the Premier League returns after the international break.

Lascelles hadn't missed a single game for Steve Bruce 's side, but they are well stocked at centre-back with Florian Lejeune set to return from injury and former Villan Ciaran Clark hitting form again.

Macdonald wrote in his column for ChronicleLive : "When a football club loses its skipper for the rest of the year it's normally a huge blow."

"However with respect to Jamaal Lascelles if Newcastle United had to be robbed of a senior player for a long while it was best that it was a central defender.

"Because United are so well off at the back.

"Think of it, Ciaran Clark, Federico Fernandez and Paul Dummett - none automatic selections - have come in recently and all have been absolutely outstanding.

"And two quality performers in Fabian Schar and Florian Lejeune are just about to return from injury so United can stand the absence of Lascelles.
