Why most of Tara Moss' wardrobe is filled with vintage finds

16 Nov 2019, 13:00 by Georgie Gordon

How would you describe your style?
Inspired by the 1940s.

Why do you love 1940s fashion?
I have a great connection to the period because I grew up on film noir, reading hard-boiled Raymond Chandler novels and hearing World War II stories from my Oma and Opa. The clothing then was strong: shoulder pads, nipped waists and that classic red lip I love. Also, it was practical enough that you could run to the bomb shelter in your sturdy heels, but also go out for dinner.

What’s the oldest thing in your wardrobe?
Most of my clothes are from the 1930s to the ’60s but I do have some antique pieces. The oldest thing is a Victorian mourning shawl: silk, with beading and fur trim. It’s exquisitely beautiful.

What is the most recent fashion item you bought?
A victory pin that would have been worn by a patriotic woman during World War II. Victory pieces are one of my obsessions.

How would you dress for ...
... a first date? Exactly as I always do. So, a 1940s dress, Mary Jane shoes and my walking stick, Wolfie.
... a plane? When I flew out to Australia from Vancouver, I wore a black jersey Vivienne Westwood dress, Fluevog boots and had a corset in my bag to put on when I got off the plane. It’s not how everyone dresses, but it’s more comfortable for me than pants.
... the Oscars? I’d wear vintage, or an Alex Perry number – he made my wedding dress and knows how to dress my hourglass figure.

Do you have a favourite fashion designer?
Lilli Ann – they were very big in the middle of the last century. I’ve only got one of their suits but I am always on the lookout for more.

What are three essential items in your wardrobe?
Red lipstick is number one – even though it’s not technically part of my wardrobe, I don’t feel dressed without it. Number two would be a sturdy but feminine heel, and number three would be a classic 1940s day dress.

Can you remember a favourite outfit you had as a child?
A devil costume with a cape and little horns that my mother made me for Halloween when I was about six years old.

Do you have a favourite party look?
Surprise, surprise, I’d wear a 1940s dress, in red – one of my favourite colours – and costume jewellery.

Your worst fashion mistake?
I would say I dressed terribly up until seven years ago, when I started wearing vintage. I didn’t have the courage before then.

What would you buy if money were no object?
Vintage hats and early Chanel.

What would you never wear?
A sweatsuit.

Who is your style muse?
Ava Gardner, when she burst onto the screen in The Killers.

What was your first fashion moment?
It was when I got my first big modelling job at the age of 16 and I bought a Versace suit. It was
around 1990, I was living in Milan and there were those wonderful Versace ads of all the supermodels of the day in miniskirt suits with big gold buttons and shoulder
pads. That was the suit I bought and I’ll never part with it.
