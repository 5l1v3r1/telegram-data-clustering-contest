ESPN’s Computer Is Predicting A Major Top 10 Upset Today

16 Nov 2019, 13:11 by Andrew Holleran

Week 12 of the 2019 college football regular season has arrived, as several key games are set to kick off in a couple of hours. Will we be getting any upsets today?

ESPN’s Football Power Index seems to think so.

The Worldwide Leader’s computer is predicting a big-time top 10 upset that would affect the College Football Playoff race today.

Minnesota jumped up to No. 8 in the College Football Playoff rankings following last week’s big win at home over Penn State. The Golden Gophers are 9-0 on the season. The Big Ten West contenders are right in the thick of the playoff race.

ESPN’s Football Power Index has P.J. Fleck’s team losing today, though.

ESPN’s computer model is predicting No. 20 Iowa to upset No. 8 Minnesota on Saturday. The FPI is giving the Hawkeyes a 60.8 percent chance of winning, giving the Golden Gophers just a 39.2 percent chance of winning.

A loss would certainly hurt Minnesota’s College Football Playoff chances, though the Golden Gophers wouldn’t be eliminated from contention with a loss.

Minnesota and Iowa are set to kick off at 4 p.m. E.T. on FOX.
