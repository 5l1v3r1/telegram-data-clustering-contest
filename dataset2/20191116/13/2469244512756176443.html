Anger as frustrated M5 motorists drive through cones to escape traffic chaos

"This action is ILLEGAL! You are putting yourselves, our roadworkers and other road users at great risk!"

16 Nov 2019, 13:07 by Joel Cooper

Highways England has issued a warning to motorists on the M5 after cars were spotted driving through cones to escape heavy traffic.

Two crashes on the M5 are causing long delays this morning, with some drivers having been held up for several hours.

But Highways England took to Twitter to warn motorists that driving through the cones was illegal and puts people at risk.

In the Tweet they shared the picture below and said: "This action is ILLEGAL!

"You are putting yourselves, our road workers and other road users at great risk!

"The traffic has now been released, we ask for your patience during these stoppages."

A serious crash around 6am this morning has left a woman in hospital with serious head injuries.

The collision, which involved a car and a lorry, happened on the M5 Southbound carriageway between junctions 22 and 23.

It is understood the A38 is also affected as traffic diverts onto the main road to avoid the motorway closure.

A second crash around an hour ago left a car on its roof on the M5 Northdbound between Junctions 18 and 17.

The road was blocked and traffic was stropped while police, fire and ambulance crews made their way to the scene.
