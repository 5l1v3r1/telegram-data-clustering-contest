Anthony Joshua's five sparring partners ahead of Andy Ruiz Jr rematch

Anthony Joshua has drafted in some new names to prepare for Andy Ruiz Jr as he looks to avenge his shock defeat in the rematch in Saudi Arabia next month

16 Nov 2019, 13:17 by Irishmirror.ie

Anthony Joshua has been leaving no stone unturned is his preparations to face Andy Ruiz Jr in a rematch next month.

The British heavyweight star will look to recapture his world title belts in Saudi Arabia after being stunned in New York in June.

Joshua was undone by Ruiz, 6ft, at Madison Square Garden with his dislike for fighting those shorter than him on full display.

He has been forced to go back to the drawing board with coach Rob McCracken - and it has led to a major change in training camp approach.

With a full camp to prepare for Ruiz and his style - rather than being expected to face Jarrell Miller, 6ft4, until a few weeks before his June 1 fight - Joshua has rung the changes.

Previous favourite sparring partners such as Lawrence Okolie and Martin Bakole have not been called up, instead turning to some unknown prospects and one particularly big name to put him through his paces.

Here's a run-down of the five men we know have been in Joshua's camp ahead of his huge night.

Dereck Chisora

Dereck Chisora needs no introduction.

The British heavyweight veteran is still fighting for one more world title shot and after devastating stoppage wins over Artur Szpilka and David Price he is back in the hunt.

Chisora and Joshua go back to Joshua's days as an amateur at Finchley ABC and his relentless, come-forward style will have arguably been Joshua's biggest test of camp.

Chisora's manager, David Haye, gave an insight into Joshua's camp and says he is in great shape, although he kept details to a minimum.
