What Wales now need to qualify for Euro 2020

This is exactly where Wales stand after the latest round of results

16 Nov 2019, 21:40 by Ian Mitchelmore

Wales are just one win away from qualifying for Euro 2020 after a perfect set of results on Saturday.

Goals from Kieffer Moore and Harry Wilson saw Ryan Giggs' men earn a deserved 2-0 win over Azerbaijan in Baku, before attention turned to Croatia's clash at home to Slovakia, with Wales needing the visitors to drop points in order to keep their hopes of automatic qualification firmly alive.

Despite going in 1-0 down at the interval, goals from Nikola Vlasic, Bruno Petkovic and Borna Barisic sealed victory for the Croatians, with Robert Mak also being sent off for two bookable offences.

It means Wales are now just one triumph away from qualifying for next summer's championships in a winner-take-all showdown at home to Hungary on Tuesday night.

What Wales now need to qualify automatically

Wales will qualify for Euro 2020 if they beat Hungary on Tuesday night, regardless of what happens in the other game between Slovakia and Azerbaijan.

But anything other than victory at Cardiff City Stadium will see Wales miss out as they currently trail the Hungarians by a point.

What about the Nations League?

Wales are already guaranteed a play-off spot in the Nations League if they fail to qualify automatically.

This is because Ukraine, Russia and Sweden have already qualified automatically.

The play-offs take place in March next year.
