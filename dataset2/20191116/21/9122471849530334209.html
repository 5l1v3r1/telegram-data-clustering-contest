Shea Patterson Reacts To Michigan’s Blowout vs. Michigan State

16 Nov 2019, 21:42 by Chris Rosvoglou

If the Michigan Wolverines faced the Michigan State Spartans every week, Shea Patterson would be considered a Heisman Trophy candidate.

Patterson entered this season with high expectations. Another year in Jim Harbaugh’s system was supposed to do wonders for the senior quarterback.

The addition of Josh Gattis forced Michigan to make a few changes to its offense. After a few months of growing pains, it appears the Wolverines have found their identity on offense.

Michigan is usually a team that values running more than passing. On Saturday, the script was completely flipped.

Patterson completed 24-of-33 pass attempts for 384 yards, four touchdowns and no interceptions.

Following the game, Patterson said “I wish I could have two more shots with them. Wish I could have started here and had four shots (at Michigan State).”
