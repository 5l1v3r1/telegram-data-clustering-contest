Chinese soldiers help clean up debris after Hong Kong protests

16 Nov 2019, 21:06 by Eileen AJ Connelly

China’s People’s Liberation Army soldiers made a surprise appearance in some Hong Kong streets on Saturday, helping to clean up debris and barricades left by anti-government protesters.

Small groups of demonstrators who remained nearby harassed the soldiers, who, wearing shorts and T-shirts, labored alongside city workers and residents to pick up bricks and other debris, The Associated Press reported.

The city, rocked by increasingly violent protests for five months, had a relatively quiet Saturday, though demonstrators kept a major cross-harbor tunnel blocked. The calm followed some of the worst violence of the year, including a cop shooting a student on Monday.

The presence of the soldiers could enrage protesters even more, Reuters reported. The movement was sparked by a proposed law that would have made extradition to the mainland easier, and has since morphed into anger over perceived Chinese meddling in the semi-autonomous city.
