15 Delicious Dishes to Bring to Friendsgiving That Are Made For Sharing

16 Nov 2019, 21:40 by Lauren Manaker

Rounding up your besties to celebrate Friendsgiving is one of the season's best (and most loved) traditions. The festive get-together allows you to celebrate in your own creative style without the added stress of awkward family talk at the table. There's also no need to stick with traditional Thanksgiving dishes like mashed potatoes or green beans if you don't want to. While it's best to always coordinate with the host on what you can bring, you can offer to have a little more fun with a side dish or dessert. After all, this is your group of close friends, so if the dish isn't great, it'll still make for a great story later. Keep scrolling for 15 dishes you can bring to Friendsgiving dinner this year to shake things up.

Appetizers are a must at any Friendsgiving gathering. Instead of opening up a premade hummus container and calling it a day, spice it up with some fresh produce and herbs by following this recipe. Pair it with some homemade pita chips and watch the compliments come pouring in!

This savory Fall appetizer looks fancy but is actually super easy to make. The crostini includes some classic Thanksgiving ingredients like pumpkin seeds and cranberries, and introduces some unexpected additions like creamy goat cheese. The flavor combo is unreal.

4 Pumpkin Thanksgiving Sangria

Skip the plain white wine this year and bring your host or hostess a festive and yummy sangria! The combo of fruit, wine, pumpkin butter, spices, and brandy will be sure to please any party guest.

Braising the green beans allows for them to cook slowly and not end up as a chewy or stringy mess. The end result is a deliciously tender veggie with some amazing spices.
