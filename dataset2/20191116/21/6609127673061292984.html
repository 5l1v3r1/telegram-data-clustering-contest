POO DIRTY RAT

A Boris Johnson General Election win would see our food infiltrated by rat poo, warns SNP MSP

16 Nov 2019, 21:00 by Chris Musson

A NATS MSP last night claimed a General Election win for Boris Johnson could see the food chain infiltrated by rat poo.

Emma Harper said a post-Brexit trade deal with US President Donald Trump would lead to rodent faeces and hair being “acceptable” in our grub if EU standards are dumped.

Her alert came after MSPs heard different regulations in the States meant Britain might have to bow to American rules.

Ms Harper said the US Food and Drug Administration allow “certain levels” of rodent poo, hair and insect parts in produce.

The South Scotland MSP added: “It’s bad enough that Tories want to open the floodgates to US imports of chlorinated chicken.

“But who knows what else we’ll be eating if Boris Johnson’s mob sink to this level. He must rule out any attempt to scrap standards protected by the EU.”

Ms Harper told a Holyrood committee coco beans can legally contain “up to 10mg of poop a pound” in the US.

She asked John Kerr, the Scottish Government’s head of agricultural policy, if a UK-US trade deal could lead to similar rules.

Mr Kerr said: “If you accept the minimum standards another country is trading under, then your lowest quality produce may be at that standard.”

But Tory MSP Adm Tomkins last night called the SNP attack “hysterical nonsense”.

He said: “Continuing to trade with the EU means standards will stay in place, protecting food regulation.”

Meanwhile Boris is to face Lord Buckethead in Uxbridge in the December General Election.

The PM will also go up against a candidate called Count Binface in his West London constituency.

Lord Buckethead has famously appeared alongside three former Prime Ministers in the past - most recently Theresa May in 2017.

The fictional character also stood against Margaret Thatcher in 1989, and John Major in 1992.
