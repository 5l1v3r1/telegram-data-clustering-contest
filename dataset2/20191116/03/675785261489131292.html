Michael Schumacher's wife Corinna hits back at claims she is hiding F1 legend away

Corinna Schumacher responded to manager Willi Weber's remarks by saying seven-times Formula One champ is 'in the best of hands' and she is following his wishes

16 Nov 2019, 03:52 by Gareth Bicknell

Michael Schumacher's wife Corinna has hit back at his former manager's claims that she is hiding the truth about the Formula One legend's condition.

Seven-times F1 champion Schumacher suffered a serious brain injury in December 2013 in a skiing accident in the French Alps, which left him in a coma.

Little has since been revealed about Schumacher's condition of the seven-time world champion since he moved to being treated at his family's Lake Geneva home in September 2014.

Earlier this month, Willi Weber, his long-term manager, said he believed Corinna Schumacher feared the truth about Schumacher’s condition entering the public domain . He claimed she had knocked back several requests by him to see the former Ferrari driver.

But now Corinna has hit back at Weber in a rare interview, saying 50-year-old Schumacher was "in the very best of hands" and she was following his wishes by keeping details of his condition under wraps.

 

"He is in the best of hands right now and we are doing everything we can to help him," she said to She's Mercedes magazine.

"Try to understand that we follow Michael's desire to keep his health a secret.

"You can be sure that he is in the very best of hands and that we are doing everything humanly possible to help him.

"Please understand if we are following Michael's wishes and keeping such a sensitive subject as health, as it has always been, in privacy."

 

Weber spoke out earlier this month in German TV documentary The Michael Schumacher Tale.

He said: "I know that Michael has been hit hard, but unfortunately I do not know what progress he makes .

"I’d like to know how he’s doing and shake hands or stroke his face.

"But unfortunately, this is rejected by Corinna. She’s probably afraid that I’ll see right away what’s going on and make the truth public.

"I firmly believe in Michael’s recovery, because I know he is a fighter. If there is a chance, he will use it. That cannot be the end.

"I pray for him and am convinced that we will see him again."

Schumacher's seven F1 crowns included five in a row between 2000 and 2004, after he also won back-to-back titles in 194 and 1995.

Lewis Hamilton is one behind Schumacher's record after clinching the 2019 title earlier this month.
