Collision, fuel leak, Sabine Pass, Texas

16 Nov 2019, 03:22 by Erofey Schkvarkin

Offshore supply ship CHERAMIE BOTRUC 22 and tug MARIYA MORAN collided near the entrance to Sabine Pass, Port Arthur, Texas, in the morning Nov 14, reported US Coast Guard. Fuel tank of one of the vessels was breached, some 10 tons of diesel fuel leaked, according to USCG press-release. The source of the release has been secured and the spill has been contained. Boom and sorbent material have been placed around the vessel while it remains anchored outside of the channel.
Damaged vessel which leaked fuel wasn’t identified, but according to data and tracks, it’s supply ship CHERAMIE BOTRUC 22. She moved to Sabine and was berthed, on Nov 15.
Offshore supply ship CHERAMIE BO TRUC 22, IMO 7621865, dwt 500, built 1975, flag USA, manager L&M BOTRUC RENTAL LLC.
Tug MARIYA MORAN, IMO 9764128, GT 708, built 2015, flag USA, manager MORAN TOWING CORP.
