HOOPS BLOW

Celtic blow as Edouard could miss FIVE Euro qualifiers next summer if France reach Olympic final

16 Nov 2019, 09:15 by David Friel

CELTIC star Odsonne Edouard could miss over half of his side’s Euro qualifiers next year after revealing his Olympics dream.

The Tokyo 2020 Under-23 tournament runs from July 23 to August 8, with France among the favourites.

Edouard is desperate to make the squad for Japan — but that would rule him out of key qualifiers.

Celts will begin their Champions League or Europa League bid between July 7 and 9 and face eight games in eight weeks.

If France go all the way to the final, Edouard would miss at least five of the Hoops’ qualifiers.

And Celtic chiefs would have to bring him back without any break if he was to take part in the final three.

But the in-form striker insists he’s determined to play a leading role in Sylvain Ripoll’s squad in the Far East next summer.

He said: “It’s a very important competition, one that every sportsman dreams of participating in.

“I have waited a long time to be called up by France at this level and I am keen to do well so I make an impression.”

Edouard has impressed French Under-21s chief Ripoll with his prolific form for club and country.

He said: “Odsonne is very efficient for Celtic and he has shown the same sharpness with us.”
