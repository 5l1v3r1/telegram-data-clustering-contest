'WORTH SHARING'

Lynsey Sharp announces break from athletics following cancer scare as she urges women to ‘go and get smear test’

16 Nov 2019, 09:41 by Rachel Macpherson

SCOTS athlete Lynsey Sharp has revealed she'll be "taking it easy" for a few weeks after a cancer scare.

The 29-year-old told fans on social media that she was recovering from surgery - after a smear test revealed she had pre-cancerous cells.

And now Lynsey is urging other women to get their own smear tests done.

Taking to Instagram, the athlete said: "The last few weeks have been a bit rough.

"Last week I had surgery to remove cells which came back today as pre cancerous. A smear test, lead to a biopsy which lead to surgery.

"I have to take it easy for a few weeks but I’m getting there and...health takes precedence.

"If I hadn’t gone for a smear test I wouldn’t have been able to catch this before it developed any further. Please please please go and get a smear test...it takes 5 minutes.

"If sharing this encourages even one of you ladies to go then it will be worth sharing."

Fellow athletes flocked to the post to offer support and hail the "important" message.

Canadian runner Melissa Bishop wrote: "Worth sharing girl!! Rest up."

While athlete Seren Bundy-Davies added: "This is so important 💗 hope you’re feeling 100% soon!"

And Australian athlete Genevieve Gregson said: "Get well and thanks for sharing. Thinking of you and a speedy recovery."

It comes after we reported how one Glasgow student was diagnosed with endometriosis by chance after her first smear test despite years of chronic pain.
