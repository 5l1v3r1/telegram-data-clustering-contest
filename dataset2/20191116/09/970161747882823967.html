Paramedic finds 'disgusting' handwritten note on ambulance blocking driveway

16 Nov 2019, 09:24

A paramedic returned to find an abusive note left on her ambulance after responding to an emergency call.

Lauren Wheeler from Harlow, Essex, was helping someone in need in Southend-on-Sea when an unidentified neighbour wrote a ‘disgusting’ letter that slammed the ambulance worker for blocking their driveway.

The note, written all in block capitals, read: ‘Don’t care if you’re an ambulance you can’t block people’s driveways!

‘Either ask or f***ing come out when we beep the horn for 10 minutes’.

Lauren posted the note on Facebook under the caption: ‘Love getting cute notes in my job’ with a laughing crying emoji.

Outrage was sparked by the note, with multiple people naming the situation ‘unbelievable’.

John Kear wrote: ‘Don’t care if you are a human, you CANT be that much of a d**khead.’

Kate Wright added ‘Wtf !!! Some people’s utter stupidity and selfishness astounds me’ while Camillé Wyre said: ‘Oh my god that’s disgusting !!!’

Responding to angry comments, Lauren joked: ‘I will make sure I knock on everyone’s door for the rest of the night’, along with an eye roll emoji.

In a statement, a spokeswoman for East of England Ambulance Service urged residents to think about whether their driveways being blocked was more important than saving a neighbour’s life.

She said: ‘We are asking members of the public who abuse ambulance crews over parking issues to remember they are there to save lives.

‘This plea follows an abusive note being left on an ambulance parked in Southend while crews were attending a medical emergency last night.

‘Our crews are instructed to be considerate where they park.

‘However in areas where parking is limited, an ambulance may need to park in the first available space to attend a critically ill or injured patient.

‘No-one deserves to be abused for doing their job, so if a marked ambulance is parked across your drive for a few minutes, please think – is your parking space more important than a neighbour’s life?’
