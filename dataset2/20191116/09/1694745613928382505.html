The 7 Best Indian Foods You Can Get From Trader Joe's

16 Nov 2019, 09:35 by Erin Cullum

Trader Joe's frozen food section is truly a wonderful place. One frozen cuisine in particular that you shouldn't overlook is Trader Joe's Indian food — some of it is legitimately as delicious as takeout. We tapped our editors, who are just as enthusiastic about Trader Joe's as they are about Indian food, to find out which items from TJ's are worthy enough for a space in your shopping cart. The next time you have an insatiable craving for Indian food but would rather avoid the restaurant prices, opt for these frozen Indian foods you can get at Trader Joe's, plus a couple you won't find on ice.

Prices and products may vary by Trader Joe's location.

1 Trader Joe's Channa Masala ($2)

"My favorite Indian food from Trader Joe's is the frozen Channa Masala — chickpeas cooked in a spiced tomato sauce. With no weird preservatives or unnecessary ingredients (plus no cream, a bonus for me), this vegetarian dish is seriously authentic. Serve it over basmati rice or with naan, and you've got yourself a restaurant-worthy meal." — Erin Cullum, assistant editor, Food

2 Trader Joe's Chicken Tikka Masala ($3)

"Combined, my sister and I have probably eaten over 500 boxes of Trader Joe's Chicken Tikka Masala. It's delicious and perfect to pack for the office or heat up after a long day at work (pro tip: it's also helped cure a hangover or two). It feels like comfort food but is portioned so perfectly that I'm satisfied without doing damage to my diet (which I cannot say for Indian delivery)." — Lizzy Eisenberg, director, Business Development

"Rivals the best Indian food restaurants — and it's frozen!" — Shannon Vestal, director, Celebrity and Entertainment

3 Trader Joe's Palak Paneer ($3)

"This is my favorite Trader Joe's Indian frozen meal and it pairs perfectly with their garlic naan! The spinach and cheese combo is so delicious." — Christen Nino De Guzman, associate, Collective Partnerships

4 Trader Joe's Garlic Naan ($2)

"Supereasy bread to have on hand that heats up quickly and is great with Indian food or just a salad." — Lauren Hendrickson, director, Editorial Product Management

5 Trader Joe's Vegetable Masala Burger ($2)

"I was somewhat of a skeptic when it came to Indian food until I visited Babu Ji in NYC. Since then I've embraced everything about the cuisine. Trader Joe's has an awesome vegetarian masala burger for when I'm in need of a flavor-packed yet healthy meal." — Samantha Netkin, assistant editor, Native

6 Trader Joe's Masala Simmer Sauce ($2)

"While not as good as the real thing, the simmer sauces make dinner such an easy process. Add chicken and veggies to the sauce, heat it up, and you're done. Super easy, quick, and flavorful! We like to serve it alongside TJ's premade naan for a complete meal." — Michele Foley, senior editor, Fitness

7 Trader Joe's Curry Simmer Sauce ($2)

"Just as flavorful as the Masala Simmer Sauce but without any cream, the authentic Curry Simmer Sauce is full of tomato puree, garlic, coriander, cumin, turmeric, cloves, and lemon juice." — EC
