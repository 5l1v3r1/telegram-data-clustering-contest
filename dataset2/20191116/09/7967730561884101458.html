Trimble fires up for monster second half as United overtake Phoenix

16 Nov 2019, 09:08 by Roy Ward

Melbourne United rode a red-hot Melo Trimble to another comeback win over South East Melbourne Phoenix on Saturday night.

Trimble went scoreless in the first half as he turned his focus to shutting down Phoenix star John Roberson but after half-time, the 24-year-old put his head down and drove to the basket, scoring 22 points including the go-ahead free throw with just 2.1 seconds to play in a 96-95 win.

Trimble, who also had three steals, wasn't the only star as David Barlow and Mitch McCarron put on an impressive defensive display on Phoenix star Mitch Creek in the second half, restricting Creek to 27 points after he had 19 at half-time.

The win sees United up 2-1 in the season series with the Phoenix, with one game to play in the final round. United's season record is now 6-4 while the Phoenix are 5-3.

Phoenix import centre Keith Benson went from bench-warmer last week to star this week with a season-high 20 points and five blocks but it wasn't enough as United found a way to win despite trailing for much of the game and most of the last term.

Benson's dunk tied scores at 95-95 with just seconds remaining but Trimble managed to drive and draw a foul so he could shoot the match-winning free throw.
