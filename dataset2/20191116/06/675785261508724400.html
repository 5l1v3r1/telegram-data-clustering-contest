Real Madrid eye Chelsea winger Callum Hudson-Odoi ahead of potential transfer move

Madrid boss Zinedine Zidane and president Florentino Perez are admirers of Hudson-Odoi as well as Man Utd transfer target Erling Braut Haaland

16 Nov 2019, 06:06 by Gareth Bicknell

Real Madrid are weighing up a move for Chelsea youngster Callum Hudson-Odoi, according to latest reports from Spain.

Hudson-Odoi was heavily linked with a move to Bundesliga giants Bayern Munich earlier this year before agreeing a new five-year contract at Stamford Bridge.

But the 19-year-old's new deal has not stopped speculation surrounding the Chelsea winger, and he is reportedly on a list of four young targets being monitored by Madrid manager Zinedine Zidane and president Florentino Perez.

Spanish publication El Desmarque says both Zidane and Perez are admirers of England international Hudson-Odoi and are keeping tabs on the teenager ahead of a potential summer swoop.

The La Liga giants are also tracking Manchester United target Erling Braut Haaland of Red Bull Salzburg, Everton striker Moise Kean and Villarreal forward Samu Chikwueze.

Hudson-Odoi revealed it only took one conversation with boss Frank Lampard to convince him to sign a new Chelsea deal, and insists he is pleased with his decision to snub Bayern Munich.

"I can't say exactly what he said but he just said he believes in me," said Hudson-Odoi. "He wants me to work hard and if I believe in myself then he believes in me and things will go well for me at this club.

"And he wants me to play for him. They were encouraging signs for me that you want to hear from a manager, that he believes in you and he has faith in you, that you can become a good player and achieve a lot of stuff at the club.

"I was excited. It was exciting times that a new manager was coming in and also you know that he will play at least some of the youth boys as well.

"So it's good to know that you are going to play with some of the players from before.

"I said from the start that I made the right decision to stay at Chelsea. I've been there all my life. I've loved the club from day one."
