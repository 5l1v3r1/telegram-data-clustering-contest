State committee on hands-free driving bill reaches agreement

16 Nov 2019, 04:08 by Manoella Macedo

BOSTON - Massachusetts is one step closer to a hands-free driving law.

On Friday, the 6-member committee appointed to work out differing House and Senate distracted driving bills, has announced that an agreement has been reached on a conference report.

The new legislation will restrict the use of hand-held cellphones while driving a motor vehicle and will update an existing law regarding racial profiling by law enforcement.

Representative Bill Straus and Senator Joe Boncore, co-chairs of the committee, have both signed the conference report, which is being circulated for signatures of the other Senate and House conference committee members.

On Monday, the conference report is expected to be filed with the House clerk on Beacon Hill.

It is still unclear when the bill will be introduced in the House.

Currently, Massachusetts is the only New England state without a hands-free driving law.
