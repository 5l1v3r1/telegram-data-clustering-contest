PRICE TAG REVEALED

Peterborough United have reportedly put a £13m price tag on striker Ivan Toney - a player repeatedly linked with a move to Derby County.

Toney has netted 12 goals in 20 appearances for the League One Posh, having scored 23 during the 2018-19 campaign.

He and Peterborough teammate Marcus Maddison were linked with Derby earlier in the campaign, while Posh owner Darragh MacAnthony took to Twitter earlier this week to pour cold water on speculation that the pair had been spotted at the Rams' Moor Farm training ground.

Premier League duo Bournemouth and Burnley are among the other clubs to have reportedly been targeting a move for Toney in the past, alongside Middlesbrough and Bristol City.

And amid speculation, The Sun are now suggesting that Peterborough would ask for £13m to sell the League One hotshot.

SHINNIE MAKES TRANSFER ADMISSION

Derby County midfielder Graeme Shinnie has opened up on the struggles he has faced with the Rams – and even admitted he considered leaving Pride Park.

Shinnie was signed by Derby last season on a pre-contract agreement ahead of his deal with former club Aberdeen that expired in the summer.

When that deal was finalised, Frank Lampard was manager of the Rams, who then went on to replace Maurizio Sarri as Chelsea boss.

Phillip Cocu stepped into the former England international’s shoes, but took time to include Shinnie in his starting line-ups.

Shinnie did not feature in the Championship until the 2-0 win over Luton Town at the start of October, when he played 76 minutes of the clash at Pride Park.

The Scotland international had not been included in the matchday squads of five of Derby’s previous 10 league matches, and was regularly with Darren Wassall’s under-23s outfit.

But the 28-year-old has played a part in each of Derby’s last six league fixtures, and netted a stoppage time winner against Wigan Athletic last month to bag his first goal for the Rams.

Fresh from earning a call-up to the Scotland national team, Shinnie admits he considered leaving Derby after being forced to watch some matches from the stands during the early stages of his Rams career.

However, he insists he did not believe he had made a mistake in moving to Pride Park.

ACOSTA UPDATE

There has been an update on the reported interest from Derby County in DC United midfielder Luciano Acosta .

In an interview in Argentina , Acosta claimed his former DC United team-mate, Derby-bound Wayne Rooney , had told him to join him at Pride Park.

Rooney joins the Rams as a player-coach in January after spending nearly two years in the United States with the MLS outfit.

Acosta has also called time on his DC United career, and is looking for a new club.

Fresh reports in The Athletic claim that Acosta is “waiting for the call” from Derby over a potential move, but there has been no sign from the Rams that deal could take place.

The report also adds that Acosta could be “leveraging his situation in order to get a better deal”, with Boca Juniors also reportedly in the running for his signature.
