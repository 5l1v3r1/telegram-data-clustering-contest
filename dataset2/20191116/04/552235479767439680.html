Government must act after Glasgow hospital water infection scandal

Record View slams NHSGGC's 'wall of secrecy' amid the Milly Main scandal.

16 Nov 2019, 04:30 by Record View, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/record-view/

The £842million Queen Elizabeth University Hospital was an exciting project and long overdue for patients in the west of Scotland.

It was hoped the state-of-the-art facility would serve patients and staff well. But the QEUH has been a disaster for patient safety and should never have opened.

Water at the facility was contaminated and patients died after contracting infections linked to bird droppings.

This week we exposed a new low. A whisteblower came forward to say that 26 child cancer patients had acquired infections believed to have orginated from the hospital water supply.

One of the kids, the probe found, died after contracting a water-based bacteria.

 

Kimberly Darroch has today come forward to say she believes her daughter Milly was the child who died.

The brave mum said Milly was in remission and set to leave hospital but contracted the infection and died.

She believes there has been a cover up and NHS Greater Glasgow and Clyde are responsible for this wall of secrecy. She added: “I think they knew prior to Milly’s death that the water was contaminated and they knew this was the infection that killed my daughter.”

Her harrowing story shames this appalling health board and the Government must act.

 

Nicola Sturgeon knows her pitch at this election is a major gamble.

She is pushing for independence in a country that voted no in 2014 and to stay in Europe when the UK is heading out.

In the north-east today, Sturgeon knows a considerable number of voters are more than happy to get shot of Brussels. They also returned a swathe of Tory MPs in 2017, in part, as a reaction to independence.

But her central pitch is clear – Boris Johnson must go.

Tories in seats across Scotland used to have a leader who wanted to stay in Europe. Suddenly they can’t get enough of Johnson’s lack of principles, political maturity or common sense.

The Tory claim that fishing communities will magically transform with Brexit is far-fetched.

Theresa May was given a chance in Scots constituencies two years ago, and she blew it – don’t let Johnson make it even worse.

 

Gerry Cinnamon has achieved levels of fame few acts can hope for.

By selling out Hampden Park in four hours, he has made Scottish history.

But it’s the way he’s done it, by word of mouth, his fans and on his own terms, that makes him truly special.
