Valade nets SO winner; Rangers victorious over Kingston

The win snaps a three-game losing skid for Kitchener, who visits London Sunday

16 Nov 2019, 04:41 by Mark Pare

With a 15-year old exceptional status player in the building, it was the veterans that proved to be exceptional when the Kitchener Rangers needed it most.

Reid Valade scored in the fourth round of the shootout, and Lucas Pfeil stopped Zayde Wisdom to clinch a 4-3 win over the Kingston Frontenacs Friday night at The Aud.

Jonathan Yantsis became the first Ranger to reach 10 goals this season, opening the scoring on a tap in with just over two minutes left in the first period.

He would score his second of the night with 15 seconds left in regulation to force overtime.

Greg Meireles made quite the return, scoring the other Ranger goal in the second period to give his team a two-goal lead, as well as adding an assist to Yantsis' late-game heroics.

Axel Bergkvist put up a two-assist effort, and in his 200th career OHL game, Riley Damiani extended his point streak to four games with an assist.

Dawson Baker and Wisdom scored 35 seconds apart late in the second to tie the game at two, before Dustin Hutton gave the lowly Frontenacs a 3-2 lead halfway through the third.

Shane Wright was held off the scoresheet for Kingston, but scored in the shootout.

Pfeil made 20 saves in the win, while Christian Propp stopped 31 shots in his Kingston debut and took the loss.

Kitchener is in London on Sunday afternoon for their first meeting against the Knights.

Pregame starts at 1:30 p.m. on 570 NEWS.
