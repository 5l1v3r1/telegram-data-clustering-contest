Rapist jailed for murdering student whose shrine was visited by Meghan Markle

The merciless killing of Uyinene Mrwetyana in South Africa sparked nationwide outrage as citizens protested over the violence against women - and Meghan Markle also paid tribute during her royal tour of Africa

16 Nov 2019, 04:37 by Tiffany Lo

A rapist has been handed three life sentences for killing a student whose shrine was visited by Meghan Markle .

The brutal of killing of Uyinene Mrwetyana in August sparked large protests in South Africa as people demanded an end to violence against women.

Uyinene was raped twice before she was bludgeoned to death by post office worker Luyanda Botha in August.

Her body was hidden in the post office's safe before Botha removed it and set it on fire, according to prosecutors.

It was found 15 miles away a week later.

Botha, 47, was sentenced on Friday after admitting rape and murder charges.

He received a life sentence for murder and two life sentences for rape and an additional five years for attempting to dispose of her body.

He will not be eligible for parole for 25 years.

Judge Gayaat Salie-Hlophe said at Western Cape High Court: "She fought bravely and courageously. This was a heinous and cruel act and crime.

"Though it may be cold comfort to you today, it is not without significance that she was a fighter and clearly had a fighting spirit for her life."

Uyinene's uncle said: "This judgement won't in any manner replace Uyinene but we do appreciate."

In September, the Duchess of Sussex, tied a ribbon to a shrine in honour of Uyinene during her visit to Cape Town.

Meghan left a message on a yellow ribbon which read: "We stand together in this situation. Harry & Meghan 26th September 2019."

The tribute was written in the local language Xhosa.

Uyinene's family has announced a scholarship in her name at the University of Cape Town where she studied film and media.
