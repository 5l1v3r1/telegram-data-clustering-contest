Gareth Southgate insists Raheem Sterling hasn't been damaged by Joe Gomez bust-up

Raheem Sterling was left out of England's Euro 2020 qualifier with Montenegro after an altercation with Liverpool defender Joe Gomez in the Three Lions cafeteria

16 Nov 2019, 22:30 by Simon Mullock, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/simon-mullock/

England manager Gareth Southgate insists Raheem ­Sterling hasn’t been damaged by his ­bust-up with Joe Gomez – and backed the Three Lions star to prove he is one of the best in Europe.

Sterling will return to Southgate’s side for the final Euro qualifier against Kosovo in Pristina tonight after being left out of the squad that beat ­Montenegro 7-0 to clinch their place in the finals on Thursday night following a scrap with Liverpool defender Gomez.

Southgate believes Sterling will not let the issue affect him.

Southgate said: “Raheem will play against Kosovo – and he’s looking forward to it.

“What has been suggested [about the fallout of the Sterling-Gomez row] is so far removed from seeing the team socialise and train together, that it’s incredible.

“They are so tight. We’ve seen Raheem’s Twitter posts regarding Joe, and I have also had a long chat with Raheem.

“He’s looking forward to playing and, if anything, I think what has happened will only help to strengthen the bond between the players.

“Raheem is 100 per cent an important player for us. He’s one of the best players in Europe.

“I’m delighted for him that we have qualified because he now knows that he’s going to get the chance to play in a European Championship, where he can be one of the star players.”

Sterling issued a full and frank apology to Gomez for grappling with the 22-year-old Liverpool star less than 24 hours after Manchester City were beaten 3-1 at Anfield.

Sterling also took to social media to defend Gomez – who has been ruled out of today’s clash with a knee injury – from a fans’ backlash after he was booed when coming on as a substitute at Wembley.
