CBS Analyst Makes Bold Cardale Jones Comparison For Alabama

16 Nov 2019, 22:12 by Dan Lyons

Alabama already needed some help to make the College Football Playoff, after last week’s loss to LSU. Today, with the injury to Tua Tagovailoa, that became a lot more difficult.

Nick Saban kept Tagovailoa, who has been nursing an ankle injury, in the game against Mississippi State up 35-7 with about three minutes left in the first half. The legendary head coach wanted to get his starting quarterback some reps in the two-minute drill during a real game, and was going to pull him after that drive.

On a throwing play, he went down hard, and had to be airlifted to a hospital in Birmingham. It is now being reported that Tagovailoa suffered a dislocated hip and posterior wall fracture, and is out for the season.

That puts the onus on Mac Jones, who has looked solid when he’s played for Tua Tagovailoa this year. Winning out, which would include an Iron Bowl game against Auburn, will be very difficult.

During today’s big Georgia at Auburn game, CBS analyst Gary Danielson stumped for Alabama’s College Football Playoff candidacy, even without Tua. He argued that if Mac Jones steps in and wins out, it should be treated like the 2014 Ohio State situation with Cardale Jones.
