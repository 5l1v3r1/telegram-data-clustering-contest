Bolton fire: Terrifying footage shows just how fast blaze spread in student block

EXCLUSIVE A video shows just quick the fire spread to the top floor but Greater Manchester Fire and Rescue said the cladding is not the same as Grenfell

16 Nov 2019, 22:50 by Phil Cardy, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/phil-cardy/

Terrifying footage shows devastating flames rip through the fourth floor right up to the roof of a student accommodation block in a matter of minutes.

The video taken by Ryan Pardon, exclusively obtained by the Sunday People, shows flames engulf the building which is home to 211 students.

Ryan, who filmed the blaze from a neighbouring flat, started recording when the fire had taken hold on the fourth and fifth floors of six-storey Cube in Bolton.

But within 90 seconds it had spread to top level - and a mere 40 seconds later they were through the roof.

Alarms can be heard going off throughout the video, as Ryan and his friend speculate about the cladding used on the block of flats.

 

Fire crews were examining the cladding - which was a central factor in the Grenfell disaster which claimed 72 lives in 2017.

Eyewitness Ryan told the Sunday People: “The speed it spread was terrifying.

"The cladding has to be looked at. My building has the same cladding, they’re only four metres apart, and we were worried the fire would spread.

“It’s a miracle that no-one was seriously hurt or killed. I couldn’t sleep last night, my mind was racing about what could’ve happened.”

 

He added: “I’ve been told I won’t be able to go home for two or three days. Every building needs to be tested to make sure the cladding is safe. It doesn’t matter about the cost.”

More than 200 firefighters fought through the night to bring the blaze under control while students made their way to safety.

Shocked witness spoke of flames “crawling up the cladding” with every floor of the six-storey building alight.

Yesterday bosses at Greater Manchester Fire and Rescue said the cladding on The Cube was NOT the same as Grenfell - but is under investigation because the fire spread rapidly on the outside.

 

Assistant chief Fire Officer Dave Keelan said lessons learnt from Grenfell helped with the evacuation.

He said: “The procedures and subsequent training we have done since then has paid absolute dividends. The fire spread extremely quickly, that’s why I can’t praise the firefighters enough for initiating a full evacuation as soon as they got here.”

The Fire Brigades Union condemned the “complete failure” of the UK’s fire safety system and said not enough had been done to make cladded buildings safer.

General secretary Matt Wrack said: “It’s deeply troubling to see fire spread rapidly up a building’s exterior again - a shocking indictment of the government’s shameful inaction after Grenfell.”

The fire broke out at 8.30pm on Friday in the privately-owned building which houses students at the University of Bolton. Witnesses spoke of flames “tearing up and across” the building.

Student Melissa McGarrigle, 18, said: “Everyone thought it was just the fire alarms acting up as usual until we heard people screaming.”

Onlooker Dominic Walsh, 36, said: “We could see the embers in the sky. We could see the building glowing at the top.”

Paramedics treated two people at the scene for minor injuries, including one person rescued by fire crews from an aerial platform.

Rescuers believe there are no casualties but are appealing for students to confirm they are safe and contact their parents to re-assure them.

Action group Grenfell United said the fire “brings back memories”.
