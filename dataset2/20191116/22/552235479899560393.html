Rod Petrie promises to address the Hampden bugbear that annoys every supporter

The SFA president is adamant that taking control of the ground from Queen's Park will make a big impact as he outlines 2030 World Cup dream.

16 Nov 2019, 22:29 by Scott McDermott, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/scott-mcdermott/

Rod Petrie wants to have a revamped Hampden Park in place to host games at the 2030 World Cup.

The SFA president admits the fan experience at the national stadium is nowhere near good enough.

But he hopes when they take full ownership of the ground in August, they can begin to make gradual improvements.

And if the UK are successful in their proposed joint bid for the World Cup in a decade – he’d love to have a fully renovated Hampden as one of the key venues.

Petrie accepts that a lot of hard work – on both the bid and the stadium - will need to be done to make it happen.

But as president, he’s determined to make Hampden a better place to go for supporters in the future.

 

Petrie said: “Hampden will be European class next summer when it hosts Euro 2020 games.

“But we are in discussions with other home associations about a 2030 World Cup bid which would give us a 10-year timeline to look at Hampden as a host stadium for that.

“We’d all be delighted with that because we want a stadium which is entirely appropriate for the best competition in the world.

“It’s early stages and a lot of work has to be done. We take over the stadium in August after Euro 2020 and that’s been a game-changer.

“With access to Hampden 365 days a year, we can find ways to enhance the revenue for the benefit of Scottish football – and that will mean some changes to the stadium.

“We have to have a plan, a target, an objective to achieve. And the 2030 bid could give us that.

“I’m not going to sit here and say Hampden is perfect. But decisions made in the past have delivered what it is today.

“In terms of being European class there have been enhancements, like big screens and digital turnstile access.

“These things will make it comparable to the 11 other venues at Euro 2020.

“But the supporter experience, as we all know, doesn’t score very highly.

“The reason for that is the 24 degree factor rate of the stands compared to a 34 degree rate at the likes of Easter Road or Tynecastle.

“So that’s the challenge for us. If we are getting a new stadium, why are we getting it?

“Because we want to make things better for Scottish football. Then it’s about the vision, in terms of the enhancements we can make.

 

“For domestic football, we have to make it atmospheric when it’s not full – but ready to rock when it’s full.

“And that’s also relevant when it might be a neutral venue for other big games that we might attract.”

The venue of the 2030 World Cup will be decided by FIFA in 2024 and if the UK are successful, the SFA would then look at how to pay for a new Hampden.

Income from qualifying for major tournaments would help – but Petrie says his board are capable of finding funds from elsewhere.

He said: “We have to look at the creativity of the people on the SFA board.

“We have a big change now, by taking over the ownership of Hampden.

“Generating money from qualifying and doing great things on the pitch would be a strong contributor.

“But we have to be more creative and see if there are other ways to bring funding to the party for the things we want to see done.”
