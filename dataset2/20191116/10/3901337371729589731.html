Contract Talks Initiated For In-Demand Striker

16 Nov 2019, 10:29 by Soccer Laduma

PSL clubs that are still interested in signing Joseph Mhlongo might have to look elsewhere, should the negotiations between Black Leopards and the player’s handlers yield positive results. 

In fearing of losing the forward on a free to Absa Premiership rivals, Lidoda Duvha have opened up negotiations with the player’s representatives with a view of keeping him at the club beyond his current spell, as reported in edition 1149 of Soccer Laduma.

Mhlongo has entered into the last year of his contract with Lidoda Duvha, with his existing deal set to expire in June next year.

In recent weeks, the 29-year-old has been linked with a number of clubs in Mzansi’s topflight, including AmaZulu, Kaizer Chiefs and Orlando Pirates.

However, indications are that Leopards view the player as an integral part of their team and want to keep him.

Mhlongo’s representative, Ratshibvumo Mulovhedzi, confirmed to the Siya crew that negotiations between the player’s camp and Leopards have been initiated.

“As I speak to you, I am talking to Black Leopards about Joseph Mhlongo and the negotiations are progressing well. We are negotiating about a new contract and we are getting there,” confirmed the representative.

“Yes, we have not yet finalised, but we are getting there. The club gave us their offer and we have made them our counter-offer. They are in the process of making a decision and we are waiting for them,” concluded Mulovhedzi.
