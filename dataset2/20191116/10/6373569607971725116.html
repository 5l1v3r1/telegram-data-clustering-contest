Calls for Royal Navy fleet of huge £250m warships to be based in Plymouth

Babcock International revealed it has been awarded a contract by the UK Ministry of Defence to deliver its newest fleet Type 31 general-purpose frigates

16 Nov 2019, 10:31 by Joel Cooper

There are calls for a new fleet of £250 million British warships to be based at Plymouth.

Yesterday Babcock International revealed it has been awarded a contract by the UK Ministry of Defence to deliver its newest fleet Type 31 general-purpose frigates.

The programme will provide the UK with a fleet of five ships, at an average production cost of £250 million per ship.

The formal contract award follows the announcement earlier this year that Babcock's Team 31 had been selected as preferred bidder following a competitive process.

Babcock's says its Arrowhead 140 is a "capable, adaptable and technology-enabled global frigate with a proven design and build strategy".

Although the ships themselves will be assembled at Babcock's Rosyth facility, they will involve supply chains throughout the UK, in line with the UK's shipbuilding strategy.

And Labour General Election candidate Luke Pollard has called for the ships to be based at Plymouth once they have been built.

In a tweet he said: "Good news! I argued that Babcock’s bid was the best value and now the contract has been signed let’s push on to get these ships built.

"And when they’re built let’s have them based in Devonport. Another argument I’ve been making consistently as Devonport’s MP and now too."
