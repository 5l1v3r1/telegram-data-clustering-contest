What time and channel is Artem Lobov v Jason Knight II on in Ireland? Key information for Bare Knuckle FC 9

Lobov emerged victorious in the first fight between the pair earlier in the year and is confident of doing so again

16 Nov 2019, 10:52 by Robert Hynes

Artem Lobov and Jason Knight clash in a bare knuckle boxing rematch this weekend.

Lobov emerged victorious in the first fight between the pair earlier in the year and is confident of doing so again.

He was last seen in action when beating Paulie Malignaggi and wants to fight the former professional boxer again.

Here's everything you need to know about Artem Lobov v Jason Knight II:

Where and when is the fight?

It takes place at the Mississippi Coast Convention Centre in the early hours of Sunday, November 17 Irish time. 

What time does it start at?

The main card starts at 2am Irish time, with the main event between Lobov and Knight expected to get underway around 4am.

What channel is it on?

The fight is not being shown on Irish TV, but you can watch it online.

Quotes corner

Artem Lobov told Sporting News: "Number one, preparing for the first fight, I was doing it blindfolded.

"I didn’t know what to expect because I had never tried it before. I couldn’t train properly because I didn’t know anyone who did bare-knuckle. So my preparation was a little wrong in terms of my cardio work in getting ready for the two-minute rounds.

"What I was doing was incorrect. I was preparing for a marathon, and the fight was a sprint. I had it all wrong. This time around, I completely changed my preparation.

"Being able to train nothing but boxing for a full year made me a much better boxer as I brought in professional boxers helped me make a lot of gains. I look forward to showing the world what I’ve been working on."

Jason Knight speaking to MMA Fighting about the first fight: “My game plan went out the window. I trained a lot better than I fought. I didn’t plan on throwing looping ass punches and trying to knock him out with every shot, but that’s what happened.

“We went to war. We both got cut up bad. We both got dropped a few times. More or less, we underestimated each other.”
