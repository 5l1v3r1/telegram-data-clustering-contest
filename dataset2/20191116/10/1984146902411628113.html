Mist, fog and rain hit the North East and there could be snow on the horizon

The Met Office say Newcastle and the North East will see relatively mild, damp weather this week but the long term forecast predicts some snow and ice

16 Nov 2019, 10:29 by Hannah Graham

It's set to be a week of fog, rain and relatively mild temperatures - but things might become more wintry soon.

According to the Met Office, this weekend is set to be fairly warm for the time of year, but beset by rain.

On Saturday, those hoping to catch a first glimpse at Newcastle's Christmas Market will be met with cloud and outbreaks of rain, which will be heavy at times. A cool northeasterly breeze will keep things feeling fairly chilly, but the maximum temperature is a relatively balmy 9 °C.

A "cloudy and damp night" will bring mist and fog patches.

Sunday will see more cloud, with the risk of further light rain or drizzle, but the possibility of sunnier spells and less breeze. The maximum temperature will be 10 °C.

But looking further into the future, Met Office forecasters say things could become somewhat wintrier as November develops.

Next week, weather systems pushing into the UK will make conditions rainy, with strong winds making warmer temperatures feel cold.

And, a spokesperson said: "There is a greater than normal chance of overnight fog forming in any clearer spells overnight, which could be slow to clear in places.

"The showers may continue to be wintry over the highest ground bringing some sleet or snow at times."

Temperatures will be average or slightly above for the time of year. And between November 30 and December 14, rain will become less likely - but frost, as well as the unpleasant-sounding "freezing fog" may start to appear.

Temperatures are expected to be around average, or just above, for the time of year.
