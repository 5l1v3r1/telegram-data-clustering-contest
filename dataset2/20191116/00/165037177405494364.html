Bale starts for Wales against Azerbaijan, hasn't played for Real Madrid in 42 days

16 Nov 2019 by BeSoccer

The Welshman, Gareth Bale, who hasn't played for Real Madrid since 5th October, after getting injured 8 days after returning from his national team, started for Wales on their trip to Azerbaijan this evening in the Euro 2020 qualifiers.

Bale got injured on 13th October, in the last few minutes of the 1-1 draw with Croatia in matchday 6. The Welshman, who scored his side's goal in the draw, was kept on the park limping as Wales had made their 3 subs.

On his return to Madrid, without any official medical report, Bale began a recovery phase exercising outside the group and missing his side's matches. He hasn't played since 5th October, when Real Madrid faced Granada (4-2) in matchday 8 of La Liga Santander, where he was replaced by the Colombian James Rodriguez in the 83rd minute.

Training that Bale instead took part in days later for Wales as they prepared for their match against Azerbaijan.

Now, 42 days after his last Madrid match, he returned in Baku, where he started and was named captain as Wales aim to qualify for Euro 2020.
