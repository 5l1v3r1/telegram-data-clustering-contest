The shortlisted films are from India, Australia, Argentina, Brazil, Bangladesh, Chile, Croatia, Egypt, France, Germany, Greece, Iran, Japan, Mexico, Myanmar, Nepal, Holland, Poland, Turkey, USA and other countries. Vittal Mallya Road, from 5pm to 9.30pm, from November 16 to 17, call 9886099736

Reserve some laughs
Ours is a cosmopolitan city, where we speak a myriad languages. We talk to an auto driver in Kannada, talk to a paanwala in Hindi and speak English at work. What would happen if you streamline all these stories? You get a two-day ‘Multilingual Comedy Festival’. Comedy Stars of Bengaluru is back to make your Saturday night fun in four different languages. Groups such as Gandhi Class (a Kannada comedy collective), Desi Stars of Comedy (Hindi) and Rock Stars of Comedy (English) will take the challenge. At Wanderers, HRBR Layout, Kalyan Nagar, at 3pm, 5pm and 7pm, from November 16 to 17, (entry) `299,bookmyshow.com

At KTPO, No 5, Whitefiled Industrial Area, from 11am to 8pm, on November 16, (entry) `599 onwards, comicconindia.com

At the end of the class, you can take home the creative artwork that you just made, with the help of your instructor, Nithya.

WHEN: 10am-1pm, November 16

Life of a legend
Indian Music Experience is presenting ‘Credo of a Dancer: An in-depth exhibition on the life and art of legendary Bharatanatyam dancer,
T Balasaraswati’. It provides insights into the socio-economic context of her time, all of which shaped her artistic expression. The inauguration
will be followed by a dance performance by Aniruddha Knight, grandson of Balasaraswati. It is an immersive experience that deconstructs the technique and mystique behind one of India’s greatest dancers. Don’t miss it.

WHERE: Indian Music Experience, Brigade Millenium, JP Nagar
WHEN: 5pm onwards, November 16 ENTRY: Free
CALL: 9686602366

Book lovers, unite
The book under review in this edition of ‘The Urban Solace Lovers’ Meet’ is The Adventures of Tom Sawyer by Mark Twain. It is an 1876 novel, about a young boy growing up along the Mississippi River. It is set in the 1840s in the fictional town of St Petersburg, inspired by Hannibal, Missouri, where Twain lived as a boy. In the novel, Tom Sawyer has several adventures, often with his friend Huckleberry Finn. Originally a commercial failure, the book ended up being the bestselling of any of Twain’s works during his lifetime. Though overshadowed by its sequel, Adventures of Huckleberry Finn, the book is considered a masterpiece of American literature, and was one of the first novels to be written on a typewriter. As for the author, he needs no introduction. But if you are just getting started into the world of literature, know this. Samuel Langhorne Clemens, known by his pen name Mark Twain, was an American writer, humorist, entrepreneur, publisher, and lecturer, and was lauded as ‘the father of American literature’. His wit and satire, in prose and in speech, earned praise from critics and peers, and he was a friend to presidents, artists, industrialists, and European royalty.

The event has been curated by Neeraj, who loves reading but can’t claim to be an avid reader. He works in an IT company as a capital market domain expert.

WHERE: Urban Solace, 32 Annaswamy Mudaliar Road, opposite Ulsoor Lake
WHEN: 11.30am-1pm, November 17 ENTRY: Free CALL: 9845013055
