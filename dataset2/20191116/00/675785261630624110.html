Ryan Giggs in Euro 2020 qualification warning to Wales stars ahead of Azerbaijan clash

Wales boss Giggs demands discipline from his players as Dragons target maximum points from final two games in bid to reach Euro 2020 finals

16 Nov 2019, 00:38 by James Nursey, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/james-nursey/

Ryan Giggs has urged his stars to avoid picking up costly suspensions in Baku as Wales’ Euro 2020 bid enters “squeaky bum time”.

Wales are without the suspended Joe Allen this evening for their penultimate qualifier away to Azerbaijan.

And Giggs is wary key duo Daniel James and Gareth Bale are both one booking away from missing Wales’ potentially crucial final qualifier at home to Hungary on Tuesday.

Dragons boss Giggs said: “We’ve seen Joe Allen miss out on this game because of suspension.

“Discipline is a big part of winning games so we will need to be disciplined.

“You still you want players to be competitive and committed but with an eye of not giving away stupid fouls to get easy yellow cards.”

Giggs has been boosted by the return of Juventus midfielder Aaron Ramsey, who is pushing to start his first qualifier in this campaign.

Giggs has now urged Wales to build on two crucial draws last month against Slovakia and Croatia as he eyes automatic qualification.

And he confirmed it was “squeaky bum time” when the legendary phrase used by his ex-Manchester United boss Sir Alex Ferguson was put to him.

Giggs replied: “It has been for the last few games. They’re two big games and obviously you’re running out of games.

“We will probably need to win both games. We’re just focused on Saturday and winning the game. It’s as simple as that.

“The players are ready, they’re looking forward to it. But Azerbaijan had a good result 1-1 at home last time here against Croatia, so it’s not easy, but the lads are ready for it.”

If Wales win their final two games and Slovakia drop points against either Croatia or Azerbaijan, Giggs' side will claim a spot at next summer's Euro 2020 finals.
