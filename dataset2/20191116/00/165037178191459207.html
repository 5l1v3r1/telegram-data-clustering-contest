Episode nine of the group of death

16 Nov 2019 by BeSoccer

It is the penultimate matchday of group C, the group of death. Holland, Germany and Northern Ireland are still fighting for two places at Euro 2020.

The draw will be in just two weeks because the group phase will end in a few days. Two matchdays remain in this group and the first of them will be this Saturday.

Germany host Belarus while Holland travel to Northern Ireland. Estonia, the other team in the group, will sit out. The first three teams are fightng for two berths and that could well end up being decided on this matchday. 

The Germans will be at the Euros with a draw as long as Holland win in Belfast. That will increase the gap between the Germans and the Northern Irish to four points, something the Northern Irish team would not be able to make up on the last day.

Joachim Low's men must win to have it in their own hands on the last day. They could still qualify with two losses depending on what Holland do. The Dutch team are in the best position as they have Estonia at home in their final match. 

Northern Ireland are in the toughest position as they have to face two direct rivals in three days. O'Neill's men have shown in the past they can do well though. 

What looked like a straight forward group for Germany and Holland has not turned out that way. 

The good news for all three sides is that whoever finishes outside the top two is guaranteed to have a second chance in the Nations League play-offs. Germany, Holland and Northern Ireland are all guaranteed at least play-off spot and Belarus will also be in the play-offs.
