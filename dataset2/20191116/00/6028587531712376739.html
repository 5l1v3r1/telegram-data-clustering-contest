‘Joker’ Tops ‘Dark Knight’ To Score Another Huge Box Office Benchmark

16 Nov 2019 by Scott Mendelson

Joker tops The Dark Knight ($1.004 billion in 2008) to become the biggest movie never to play in China, as Doctor Sleep, Last Christmas and Midway take discomforting drops on Friday. In holdover news, Lionsgate’s Midway earned $2.5 million (-61%) on Friday, setting the stage for a likely $8.6 million (-53%) weekend and $34.99 million ten-day cume. That’s not a good hold, which is at least partially due to Ford v Ferrari stealing away the older male demographics, but Lionsgate was only on the hook for domestic and UK distribution and related marketing costs. A $50 million domestic finish will be squarely okay for the studio. Paramount’s Playing With Fire, which I may try to see with the kids later today, earned another $1.9 million (-47%) for a likely $7.67 million (-40%) second weekend and $24.618 million ten-day cume. The Jon Cena kid-targeted flick cost $30 million, so this one will need decent overseas numbers to justify itself.

Last Christmas earned another $2 million (-51%) on its second Friday, as the disappointing holiday romcom will likely earn just $6.6 million (-42%) in its second weekend for a $22.5 million ten-day cume. That sets up Paul Feig and Emma Thompson’s Emilia Clarke/Henry Golding flick for a final domestic total of just-under $40 million. Unless it pulls Love Actually or In Time number overseas, it’s a whiff. Warner Bros.’ Doctor Sleep crashed in weekend two, earning $1.685 million (-68%) on Friday for a likely $5.77 million (-59%) second weekend. That’ll give Mike Flanagan’s $45 million horror flick a $24.628 million ten-day total and a likely domestic finish of over/under $37 million. Audiences didn’t care about a sequel to The Shining. Once again, IP proved to be a repellent.

Warner Bros. Joker, on the other hand, earned another $1.475 million (-42%) to bring its domestic cume up to $318.44 million. That puts it above the raw domestic grosses of The Fellowship of the Ring ($313 million in 2001), Thor: Ragnarok ($315 million in 2017) Harry Potter and the Sorcerer’s Stone ($317 million in 2001) and Iron Man ($318 million in 2008). It positions the film for a $5.2 million (44%) weekend and $322 million domestic cume. It crossed $1 billion worldwide on Friday and, with a new global cume just over the $1.004 billion gross of The Dark Knight (back in 2008), Joker is now the biggest global grosser, sans inflation, to never play in China. It should be at over/under $1.015 billion worldwide by tomorrow night.

Walt Disney’s Maleficent: Mistress of Evil earned $1.187 million (-40%) to pass $100 million domestic, setting the stage for a $4.86 million (-42%) fifth weekend and $105.653 million 31-day cume. Presuming it has still earned 78% of its money overseas, the $185 million Angelina Jolie flick will end tomorrow night with around $465 million worldwide. That’s past the arbitrary 2.5x “break even” point. It may not be a super smash, but it’ll eventually break even (and possibly turn a profit) over the long haul as it makes its way to $500 million global. Moreover, to the extent that the ten million (or more) Disney+ subscribers missed this one in theaters, it’ll be an A-level premiere when it pops up on the streaming service next year.

Focus Features’ Harriet earned $1.25 million (-37%) on its third Friday, setting the stage for a $4.56 million (-38%) weekend and solid $31.663 million 17-day cume. This one has shown strong legs, as befitting its A+ Cinemascore grade, and I wouldn’t count Cynthia Erivos out of the Oscar race just yet. In other Oscar news, Fox Searchlight’s Jojo Rabbit earned $754,000 (-33%) on Friday in 995 theaters for a likely $2.66 million (-33%) fifth weekend and $13.44 million domestic cume. I still think this crowdpleasing Taiki Waititi dramedy ends up as an Oscar front runner, because A) it’s playing well with consumers and would-be voters and B) Disney is going to push it hard to show that Fox movies can still win Oscars under the Disney umbrella.

Skydance’s Terminator: Dark Fate earned another $1.1 million (-62%) on Friday for a likely $3.98 million (-63%) third weekend and miserable $56.5 million 17-day cume. Presuming it kept is domestic (Paramount)/overseas (Fox/Disney, save for China) split intact, it’ll end tomorrow with around $232 million worldwide. Zombieland: Double Tap will have $70.3 million domestic as of tomorrow, while Parasite will earn $1.86 million (-28%) for a robust $14.4 million domestic cume. Black & Blue is no big hit, but the terrific Deon Taylor-directed action drama, starring Naomi Harris and Tyrese Gibson, will cross $20 million domestic tomorrow. Motherless Brooklyn had a decent hold in weekend two, but it lost 731 theaters in weekend three and tumbled 79% for a $135,000 weekend and $8.45 million ten-day cume.
