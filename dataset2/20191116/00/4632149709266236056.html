Things to do today in Bengaluru

16 Nov 2019, 00:30 by Bangalore Mirror Bureau

Turning one
Art@L1 is celebrating its first anniversary with, of course, an art show. Called Bon Voyage, this newly-curated art and photography exhibition will feature a stunning collection by Reuben Kataria and Porus Kharghat and some artworks from Shaun Heffernan and Vijit Pillai. On view are people and places of all kinds, done in myriad colours.

WHERE: Floor L1, JW Marriott Hotel, Vittal Mallya Road
WHEN: November 16-17
CALL: 67189999

Dance to EDM
DJ and producer Anastasia Kristensen is back in the city to make your Saturday night happening with her explosive set of techno music. A whirlwind in the DJ booth, she promises to make you move and groove to her edgy yet sophisticated beats. The Copenhagen-based Kristensen is a poster-child of techno world, mixing music, colours textures and light into a great mix.

WHERE: Sanchez, UB City, Vittal Mallya Road
WHEN: 10pm-1am, November 16
ENTRY: `2,000 (cover)
CALL: 9886580087

Listen to an epic tale
Stories from the Mahabharata is an endeavour by Aparna Jaishankar to recreate and reignite an interest in this epic. Few stories in the world are as intriguing as the Mahabharata, with its numerous plots and sub-plots and its characters, who have strengths and flaws in equal measure. So bookmark the event and be here with your family.

WHERE: Lahe Lahe, HAL 2nd Stage, Kodihalli
WHEN: 5pm-6pm, November 16
FEE: `200, eventshigh.com
CALL: 9886294444

Take an art break
The exhibition by Magali Couffon de Trevros is called Intimate êtr. Etre means ‘to be’ or ‘being’, which is about embracing the idea of humans as individuals, transcending labels, typecasts and prejudices. The exhibition showcases a 12-part portrait series, each one through the play of colour and light, creating striking contrasts and capturing moments of an uninhibited day.

WHERE: DDIR Architecture Studio, Apartment, 9, Shanti Nagar
WHEN: 7pm onwards, November 16
CALL: 8041237872

The mood is indie
Chennai-based band F16s will be in town tonight to give you a taste of their alternative indie music. In their time spent as a band, they have traversed the length and breadth of the Indian indie music landscape, releasing two EPs and a full-length debut album, and performing at prime festivals and venues across the country. The band recently ventured into Southeast Asia, with a six-city tour through Singapore, Cambodia, Vietnam and Thailand.

WHERE: Liquid Bar, Hyatt Centric MG Road Bangalore, 1/1 Swami Vivekananda Road
WHEN: 9pm, November 16
FEE: `1,000 (cover)
CALL: 40187200

Make a mask
The art of Cherial mask-making has its origins in the Cherial Scroll Painting traditions of Telangana. Wandering storytellers would use these scrolls and masks in traditional storytelling when they travelled from village to village. Drawn from daily life and mythology, the smaller masks and other toys would be sold in the villages once the stories were done with. You can learn the art too, as Dhanaloka Saikiran Verma, the son of master Cherial artists

D Nageshwar and D Padma, is conducting a workshop in the city today.

WHERE: UB City, 24, Vittal Mallya Road, Ashok Nagar
WHEN: 10am and 4pm, November 16

ENTRY: `2,250 , bookmyshow.com
