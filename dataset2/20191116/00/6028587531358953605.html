The Draw

The rematch of what is arguably the most intense and entertaining bare-knuckle fight in recent memory is slotted for the main event. Knight gets another crack at Lobov in hopes of evening the score after dropping a unanimous decision back in April.

Both men had mildly successful runs with the UFC and Lobov’s fame grew because of his association with Conor McGregor. He has positioned himself as arguably the top-rated bare-knuckle fighter in the world. His victories over Knight and then former boxing world champion Paulie Malignaggi has Lobov sitting pretty high on most lists.

Can he make it two in a row over Knight? That’s what fans will be watching for on Saturday.

The co-main event figures to be a slugfest that could end abruptly. Heavyweight powerhouse Chase Sherman defends his BKFC Heavyweight title against the rugged Joey Beltran. Sherman has seemingly found his niche with bare-knuckle fighting. He’s won a title and established himself as one of the toughest outs in the promotion. Beltran has been around the combat sports landscape for a long time. He wants to prove he’s ready to reach new heights with his latest fight promotion.

PPV Outlook

BKFC President Dave Feldman said the first meeting between the two men did nearly 200,000 pay-per-view buys, though some industry experts doubted the validity of that estimate.

This weekend’s event won’t be going head-to-head with a huge UFC card, though there is an event set for Sao Paulo, Brazil featuring Jacare Souza’s light heavyweight debut against Jan Blachowicz as well as a fight between Mauricio “Shogun” Rua and Paul Craig. We’ll have to wait to see what numbers are reported this time around.
