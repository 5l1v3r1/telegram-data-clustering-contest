West Ham v Arsenal at risk due to WW2 bomb

16 Nov 2019 by BeSoccer

The West Ham v Arsenal match scheduled for the 9th December could be canceled. The reason is none other than the discovery of a WW2 bomb close to the London Olympic Stadium.

Incredible but true. On Saturday an explosive was found on the outskirts of the London Olympic Stadium. The Police even cordoned off a 150m perimeter.

"We think it is an unexploded bomb. It's crazy to think of all the people walking around here during the Olympics and we had no idea it was here", a source told 'The Sun'.

In this way, according to the aforementioned source, a West Ham match could thus be canceled. The match will be against Arsenal on 9th December.

They will play against Tottenham on 23rd November before that match, but it is not in the Police's plans to cancel that match, as they will not get things underway until the 25th.

"The bomb is in a tricky position, so could take up to two weeks to sort it out, which takes us into the second week of December",Â the aforementioned source stated.

A representative of the Olympic Park also spoke. They confirmed that there was "an unexplained metal object that was found when working near the Queen Elizabeth Olympic Park".
