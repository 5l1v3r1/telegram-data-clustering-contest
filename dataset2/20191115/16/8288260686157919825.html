The Tamworth Constituency candidates have been announced for the upcoming General Election.

On December 12, voters will pile into their local polling station to mark their ballots in favour of who they want to see running the country for the next five years.

In total there will be six candidates for people to choose from with the return of Tamworth's latest incumbent Christopher Pincher who won the majority in 2017, over Labour candidate Andrew Hammond and Liberal Democrat candidate Jennifer Pinkett.

In 2017, Christopher Pincher had an overriding majority with 61% of the Tamworth vote and he will be hoping to hold his seat in Parliament this election.

The conservatives gained the seat from Labour back in the 2010 election.

Who are the Tamworth candidates?

  • Labour and Co-operative Party- Christopher Bain
  • UK Independence Party (UKIP)- Robert Bilcliff
  • The Conservative Party Candidate- Christopher Pincher
  • The Green Party Candidate- Andrew Tilley
  • Liberal Democrat- Rob Wheway
  • Independent- John Wright

The Tamworth constituency includes: The Borough of Tamworth, and the District of Lichfield wards of Bourne Vale, Fazeley, Little Aston, Mease and Tame, Shenstone, and Stonnall.

Why are we having an early election?

A general election - which determines who runs the country - normally happens every five years.

There are 650 constituencies, where voters are invited to select an MP to represent their area.

Nearly three-and-a-half years ago voters were called to the ballot box to decide whether the UK should remain in or leave the European Union.

The majority chose to leave and we were supposed to have left on March, 29 this year, however, it has since been delayed after MPs failed to back plans put forward by former Prime Minister Theresa May and now Boris Johnson.
