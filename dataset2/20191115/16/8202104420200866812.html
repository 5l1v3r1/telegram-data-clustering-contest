Letter of the Day: Just like our kids today

15 Nov 2019, 16:00 by Reader Letters

Letter to the editor from Garry Johnston

I found the article “Rewarded with sugar, rats taught to drive” (NP, Oct. 28) very interesting. It stated that rats held in a more stimulating environment with ‘various interesting objects’ keeping them active, actually had a greater ability to learn to drive a small vehicle around a rectangular track to a sugary cereal treat at the destination, then those kept in a cage. It did not mention anything about physical health after the sugary cereal treats.

I see a correlation to today’s children and young people. If the only stimulation is from a screen and using their thumbs and the bulk of the rewards are sugary or fast foods … well then they have basically chosen their cage and a lower ability to learn and poorer health will surely follow.

Garry Johnston

Saskatoon
