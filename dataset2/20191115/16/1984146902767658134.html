North East restaurants, takeaways & shops offering cheap food on anti-food waste app Too Good To Go

The likes of Yo Sushi and Pizza Punks offer discount 'magic bags' of surplus food for around £3 through the Too Good To Go app

15 Nov 2019, 16:31 by Simon Meechan

Diners can get huge discounts on takeaways and cut food waste by using the Too Good To Go app.

Too Good To Go partners with food businesses to sell food which they would otherwise throw out. Customers who use the app get discounts on meals from the likes of Yo Sushi, Pizza Punks and Leila Lily's, but they do not get any say in what they order. Instead, they are given a 'magic bag' of food which would otherwise be thrown out. The food is safe to eat, and usually surplus leftover from that day.

Too Good To Go Customers will pay around £3 for a meal. Often, the meals are worth around £10, if they were ordered directly off the restaurant.

Morrisons supermarkets around the UK are Too Good To Go partners, as are many Greene King pubs.  In Newcastle, popular and highly rated restaurants like Pizza Punks , Leila Lily's, Gingerinos , Gills Fish and Chips and Flint Pizza are among those who cut food waste by selling surplus produce through Too Good To Go.

To try it, download Too Good To Go's app, register and browse through the restaurants offering surplus food that day. Once you order, you are given an e-receipt, which you take to to the restaurant and redeem for your magic box.

To Good To Go says it has saved 24,658,169 meals, and counting, while 1.8m are using the app in the UK.
