TAXMAN HITS BACK

Rangers tax case: HMRC hit back at ‘£50m blunder’ claims insisting they ‘did not miscalculate anything’

15 Nov 2019, 14:23 by Ewan Mowat

HMRC have hit back at claims they were responsible for Rangers going bust - after a bombshell report stated up to £50million could be wiped from the club's tax bill.

In a tweet last night they insisted they "didn't miscalculate anything" and won the case in the Supreme Court.

The shock report in The Times claimed the final EBT bill could be as low as £20m.

If this figure was known back in 2011, the Ibrox club may not have been plunged into financial turmoil leading to liquidation.

But Thomas Wallace, Head of Tax at London-based firm WTT, claimed in a Q&A that HMRC did NOT make any serious mistakes in the case.

And the tax authority tonight took to social media to clarify their position on the matter.

The HMRC press office wrote: "As widely reported today and to clarify: HMRC won against Rangers' tax avoidance in the Supreme Court, and did not miscalculate anything."

Meanwhile, a Gers fan group demanded a public inquiry into the incident.

Craig Whyte, 48, paid Sir David Murray, 68, the token sum of £1 in 2011 to take over Rangers. But they plunged into administration and liquidation the following year.
