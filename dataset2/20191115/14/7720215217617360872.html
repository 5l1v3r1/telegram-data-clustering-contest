PayPal’s PornHub Blacklisting Offers Crypto Adoption Opportunity

15 Nov 2019, 14:17 by Osato Avan-Nomayo

Twitter Facebook WhatsApp Google+ Buffer LinkedIn

PayPal has suddenly ceased offering support for popular adult entertainment website PornHub. Performers on the platform now have to use other payout methods which include cryptocurrencies with some industry leaders saying the development is a positive for mainstream crypto adoption.

In recent times, crypto adoption has been growing in the adult entertainment scene with several platforms enabling virtual currency support. Some commentators say crypto is increasingly showing the world the benefits inherent in a censorship-resistant payment gateway.

PayPal No Longer Supports PornHub Payouts

PornHub issued a statement on its website announcing the decision by PayPal to discontinue offering support to the adult entertainment service. An excerpt from PornHub’s statement reads:

“We are all devastated by PayPal’s decision to stop payouts to over a hundred thousand performers who rely on them for their livelihoods. If you have PayPal as your payout option please select a new method and update your information in your Model Settings tab.”

Given the popularity of PayPal as a payment means, close to 100,000 performers on the platform were affected. PornHub suggested that affected performers utilize other payment means including cryptocurrency.

Several responders to the announcement by PornHub expressed displeasure at the development calling out PayPal for their censorship antics.

Crypto Adoption to the Rescue?

Some crypto heavyweights have come out in the last few hours to suggest that PayPal’s pulling out presents an opportunity for greater crypto adoption by PornHub and its team of performers. Tweeting in response to the issue, Binance CEO Changpeng Zhao remarked that the news sounded “like an opportunity for crypto.”
