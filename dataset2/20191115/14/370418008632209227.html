Listen To Fall Out Boy’s New Song, Bob Dylan

Fall Out Boy have unveiled a previously-unreleased song from their American Beauty/American Psycho era.

15 Nov 2019, 14:24

With today marking the release of their new best of album, Greatest Hits: Believers Never Die – Volume Two, there’s a previously-unheard Fall Out Boy song now available for fans to listen to.

The track goes by the name Bob Dylan, and was actually written for the Chicago kings’ 2015 American Beauty/American Psycho album. Only now, it’s finally hit the light of day.

“I remember recording it,” guitarist Joe Trohman tells Kerrang!. “I was like, ‘You guys are fucking idiots for not using this song.’ I’m happy now. Less that I got my way, because I don’t really push hard, but because it’s coming out. I always hated the culture in this band that we would have cool B-sides and leftover songs that we would do nothing with. So I’m glad I spoke up. Maybe I should speak up more often…”

As well as Bob Dylan, Greatest Hits: Believers Never Die – Volume Two also features a ton of brilliant material from the past six years of the band’s career – from comeback single My Songs Know What You Did In The Dark (Light ’Em Up) through to this year’s surprise collaboration with Wyclef Jean, Dear Future Self (Hands Up).

“Sometimes I wake up and I just laugh, because it feels so improbable,” vocalist and guitarist Patrick Stump admits of the band’s post-hiatus life. “It feels so ridiculous and unlikely to me. The odds of us having this second act seemed so infinitely small, that it’s still crazy. It’s beyond surreal.”
