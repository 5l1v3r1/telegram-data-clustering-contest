Disney+ adds warning for racist content in classic films like Peter Pan, Dumbo

Disney+ is taking a responsible approach to potentially offensive content by giving clear disclaimers for its oldest films with racist depictions

15 Nov 2019, 14:34 by Lewis Knight, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/lewis-knight/

Disney+ is warning its viewers with disclaimers for offensive content in some of its older titles.

According to The Hill, some disclaimers have been shown on classic titles such as Dumbo and Peter Pan.

Dumbo contains a scene with the character of Jim Crow, who was seen as a stereotype of African-Americans, while the portrayal of Native Americans in Peter Pan also received a disclaimer.

Meanwhile, The Aristocats and The Lady and The Tramp were criticised for offensive Asian acents.

The disclaimer notes on the films that: "It may contain outdated cultural depictions."

According to Buzzfeed News, a scene from Fantasia has been edited out featuring a dark-skinned centaur serving its lighter-skinned counterparts.

One film that is not part of the viewing library on Disney+ is the 1946 film Song of the South, which grossed $65 million dollars on its release and won Best Original Song at the Academy Awards for "Zip-a-Dee-Do-Dah".

The film, which followed a young white boy going to live on his grandfather's plantation following the American Civil War and befriending an African-American man named Uncle Remus, has been widely recognised for its offensive racists stereotypes.

According to The Guardian, Disney CEO Bob Iger previously said in 2011 that a release of the film on home media "wouldn't necessarily sit right or feel right to a number of people today".
