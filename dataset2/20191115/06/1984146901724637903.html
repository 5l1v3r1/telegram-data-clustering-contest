New bar to offer wine tasting and local beers to 'affluent' clientele in Wallsend

Businessman Steven Ratcliff decided to open a watering hole on the high street after he realised there was nowhere in the town he and his wife would like to have a drink

15 Nov 2019, 06:00 by Herbert Soden

A luxurious new bar featuring wine tasting and local beers will be opening in Wallsend, if the council give the go-ahead to the plans.

Businessman Steven Ratcliff decided to open a watering hole on the high street after he realised there was nowhere in the town him and his wife Lauren wanted to have a drink.

"When me and my wife socialise its with friends who live in Whitley Bay and Tynemouth," he said.

"Every-time we said 'do you want to go for a quick drink, or bite to eat' we realised there wasn't much in Wallsend.

"In mine and Lauren's opinion there is a lot of professional, more affluent people in the town who want somewhere like this.

"My wife is my muse and she is a great sounding board for my ideas, I wanted to open the kind of place we would like to socialise in.

"I own a salon in the town and I get a lot of feedback from customers and they are all really excited."

Mr Ratcliff has applied to North Tyneside Council for permission to sell alcohol, hot food and drink, and provide entertainment at his premises between midday and 11pm seven days a week.

And if he is successful he expects the bar to open its doors for the very first time in March next year.

He said: "I want people to be able to come for a sociable couple of drinks before they go home and get up for work the next day.

"I'm working with the police and I'll be responsible."

He has also asked for permission to offer live music between, midday and 10pm.

"I want to put on local comedians and acoustic artists," he explained.

Mr Ratcliff said he will be working with local businesses to offer a tantalising range of drinks and anti-pasti platters.

"I am working with Tyne Bank Brewery and a Gosforth wine merchant. I'll be offering wine tasting nights to get people's palette's going.

"There is a lot of younger professional people buying their first homes in Wallsend I want to encourage them to use the town, and this will be good for the local economy."

North Tyneside Council's licensing department have yet to make a decision on the application.
