Plymouth Argyle goalkeeper Alex Palmer named in League Two Team of the Season

Football statistics website WhoScored.com have come up with the best players of 2019/20 so far

15 Nov 2019, 06:00 by Chris Errington

Plymouth Argyle goalkeeper Alex Palmer has been included in the League Two Team of the Season so far by influential football statistics website, WhoScored.com.

The 23-year-old loan signing from West Bromwich Albion is the only Pilgrims’ player to get that recognition.

Palmer was Plymouth Live’s Argyle player-of-the-month for August and he has continued to impress between the posts since then.

WhoScored.com launched full statistical coverage of League One and League Two this season.

That means player performance ratings across all of England’s top four divisions are now available.

Their data analysts have come up with the League Two Team of the Season using their unique statistical ratings.

Palmer, who has been an ever present for Argyle in their 16 league games this term, has a rating of 6.95.

The WhoScored.com verdict on Palmer was as follows:

“The sole Plymouth representative is also the only goalkeeper with an assist to his name.

“Palmer earns his spot between the sticks in this side with his 45 saves ranking highly in League Two.

“Only Tom King of Newport (eight) has kept more clean sheets than the 23-year-old (six) in the division this season.”

The WhoScored.com League Two Team of the Season in full is:

Goalkeeper: Alex Palmer (Argyle)

Right-back: Perry Ng (Crewe Alexandra)

Centre-back: Charlie Goode (Northampton Town)

Centre-back: Ben Richards-Everton (Bradford City)

Left-back: Ibou Touray (Salford City)

Right midfield: Courtney Senior (Colchester United)

Centre midfield: Paul Lewis (Cambridge United)

Centre midfield: Ebou Adams (Forest Green Rovers)

Left midfield: Joseph Mills (Forest Green Rovers)

Striker: James Hanson (Grimsby Town)

Striker: Danny Rose (Mansfield Town)
