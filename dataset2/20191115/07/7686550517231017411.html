McDonald's to change breakfast serving hours across the UK

It also means changes to the times you can order your breakfast delivered

15 Nov 2019, 07:46 by Neil Shaw

McDonald's has announced it is to change its breakfast hours across the UK after a recent trial - and it's great news for hash brown and McMuffin fans.

Every branch of McDonald's is to add an extra 30 minutes to its breakfast serving times - meaning you will be able to get your hands on Breakfast Wraps and pancakes until 11am.

The new rules come into force across the country next week after a successful trial in a handful of restaurants earlier this year.

The changes will take place on Wednesday, November 20.

The test hours were tried out in 122 restaurants in the south of England.

The changes also mean if you're a fan of Big Macs - or anything else on the main menu - you will have to wait another 30 minutes before they go on sale every day.

Michelle Graham-Clare, Vice President Food & Marketing at McDonald’s UK and Ireland said: "We all understand the pain of missing out on a McMuffin by a matter of minutes.

"We have listened to our customers’ pleas and are delighted to extend breakfast serving time until 11am, particularly as we head into the festive season when we see a rush of latecomers searching for those all-important hash browns.

If you like ordering your breakfast through Uber Eats, the changes mean the cut-off time will now be 10.45am instead of 10.15am.
