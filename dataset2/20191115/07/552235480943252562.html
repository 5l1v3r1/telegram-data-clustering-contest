Alfredo Morelos transfer warning issued by Rangers star's agent as he tells clubs to act soon

The Colombian striker has been on fire this season and has been heavily linked with a move away from Glasgow.

15 Nov 2019, 07:44 by Mark McDougall, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/mark-mcdougall/

Premier League clubs who are interested in signing Alfredo Morelos have been told to act now or spend even more money to get him.

The Rangers star has been heavily linked with moves to the likes of Aston Villa and Crystal Palace and one of his representatives has fired a warning to suitors ahead of the January window.

The 23-year-old has scored 22 goals already this season and continues to impress for Steven Gerrard's men as they battle with Celtic at the top of the Premiership.

Theofilos Karasavvidis works for World in Motion - who represent Morelos - and claims Rangers don't have a huge fee placed on his head.

Gerrard and Rangers sporting director Ross Wilson both insist Morelos isn't going anywhere in the January window but Karasavvidis believes clubs need to make a move for him soon.

He told Tuttomercatoweb: "He is a player who has always scored a lot since he arrived in Europe.

"This is a goal machine. He is a player worth following, (the clubs) who want a striker must come and follow him.

“He does not have a very high value. My advice is to take him before his name is on everyone's lips."

Morelos has 70 goals in 116 appearances for Rangers since moving to the club in 2017 from HJK.

Previous bids of around £10million from Chinese Super League clubs had been rejected and Morelos has often spoken of his wish to play in the English Premier League.

There's yet to be any concrete offers for him but his current form mixed with his improved disciplinary record has attracted admiring glances from around Europe.

He's currently in the USA with Colombia as he prepares for two friendlies against Peru and Ecuador.
