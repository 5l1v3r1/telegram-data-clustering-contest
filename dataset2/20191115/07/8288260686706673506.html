‘Not true’ - Wolves issue clear response to £13m transfer claim

Wolverhampton Wanderers transfer news | WWFC have been linked with a move for the 19-year-old midfielder

15 Nov 2019, 07:00 by Alex Dicken, https://www.facebook.com/alexdickenjournalist/?fref=ts

Wolverhampton Wanderers have categorically denied any interest in Swedish midfielder Dejan Kulusevski.

The 19-year-old, who plays his club football for Serie A side Atalanta on loan from Parma, has been linked with a host of Premier League clubs including Manchester City and Arsenal.

And, according to reports earlier this month, Wolves had joined the race for Kulusevski with boss Nuno Espirito Santo ready to sanction a £13million offer.

Head of recruitment John Marshall has refuted those claims, however, and insists the Swede isn’t someone who is currently on Wolves’ radar.

“I also read this indiscretion, but it is not true,” Marshall told TMW at a scouting convention in Amsterdam. “He is not a player that interests us."

 

Wolves dipped into Serie A during the summer to sign Patrick Cutrone, Bruno Jordao and Pedro Neto, with the former signed from AC Milan for a fee that will reach £20million.

On Cutrone, Marshall said: "He has everything to become more and more a protagonist of our reality. And besides he is a player much loved by the fans, in the field he always gives everything. We are very satisfied with him.”
