Boss blasts Gartcairn drop-outs stating he would never have missed a game

Raging Jamie McKenzie was spoke out after just 13 players were available for last weekends cup exit against Kilsyth.

15 Nov 2019, 07:00 by Mark Pirie, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/mark-pirie/

Boss Jamie McKenzie has called for his players to show they are committed to the Gartcairn cause this weekend against Cambuslang Rangers.

The gaffer reckons there is a generational gap between the current crop of players and when he was on the park following the West of Scotland Cup exit against Greenock.

“The players have been told in no uncertain terms that we have got to be better.” he told the Airdrie and Coatbridge Advertiser.

“Just now it looks like it hurts me more than it hurts them – that isn’t the way this should be.

“Last weekend there was a lot of reasons players dropped out of the game, but I would say that when I was playing I would never have missed a game.

 

“In this day and age it’s different but when I was playing you would build your week around the game.

“I’ve spoken to a few managers and players from my generation and they are having the same problem.

“They don’t seem to have the same desire that my generation had.”

He added: “It’s a massive game now against Cambuslang. If we lose a gap starts to form at the bottom of the table.

“My players need to cut out the mistakes.”
