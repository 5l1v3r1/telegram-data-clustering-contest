Woman who was found dead on M5 named

The M5 was closed for several hours

15 Nov 2019, 07:26 by Melissa Jones, Jamie Hawkins

A woman who was found dead on the M5 last month has been named.

The motorway was closed for hours on October 30 after a missing woman's body was found.

On Thursday, the woman's name was confirmed by a coroner at a first hearing of an inquest into how she died.

Judith Clinton, 66, from Abbeydale, Gloucester, was reported missing on the morning of her death on October 30.

Her body was found on the M5 motorway between junctions 12 for Quedgeley and 11A for Brockworth.

She was last seen on the evening of October 29.

Gloucester Coroner's Court heard that, at 12:49am the following day, a motorist called police to say they had seen a body on the southbound carriageway,  Gloucestershire Live reports .

Motorists who  moaned about the delays  caused by the grim discovery have  since been criticised.

Police arrived on the scene, where they confirmed these reports.

While investigations took place, the motorway surrounding these areas was shut and later re-opened the following afternoon.

A post-mortem found the precise cause of death was unascertainable.

Following the tragedy, many took to social media to pass on their condolences to the family.

The inquest, which opened on Wednesday afternoon, is due to resume on February 6 next year.
