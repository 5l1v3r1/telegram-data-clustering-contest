Angry note left on ambulance parked outside someone's house

Someone wasn't happy their drive was blocked while medics did their job

15 Nov 2019, 13:40 by Neil Shaw

A paramedic responding to an emergency returned to her ambulance to find a rude not stuck to the window - ranting that she had blocked someone's driveway.

Lauren Wheeler posted a picture of the abusive message to Facebook.

It reads: "Don't care if you're an ambulance you can't block people's driveways! Either ask or f***ing come out when we beep the horn for 10 minutes."

Lauren, from Harlow, Essex, wrote on her post: “Love getting cute notes in my job" with a laughing crying emoji, writes MirrorOnline .

One Facebook user responded: "Oh my god that’s disgusting!!!" and another wrote: "Wow, really got time to knock and ask or just pop out while possibly trying to save someone’s life!"

A third added: "He may need you one day!!!"

The incident is believed to have happened in Southend-on-Sea.

In August, an abusive note was left on the windscreen of an ambulance parked in Luton calling the paramedics “blind idiots”.

In December last year, an ambulance in Manchester was left with an abusive note filled with expletives.

A North West Ambulance Service spokeswoman said "our patients are our number one priority".
