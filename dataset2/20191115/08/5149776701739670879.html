'Shop window' - Former Nottingham Forest youngster hoping to end West Brom nightmare

Oliver Burke has struggled to live up to the billing since leaving Forest a little over three years ago

15 Nov 2019, 08:00 by Barry Cooper

Oliver Burke hopes his move to Spain will see him find the form which will put him firmly back in the shop window.

Since leaving Nottingham Forest for £13m in 2016, Burke has endured something of a nomadic existence, moving to Germany then Scotland back to England and finally to Iberia for the remainder of the campaign.

The 22-year-old made three appearances for West Brom at the start of the season, including in the 2-1 win at the City Ground before moving to La Liga and has since played nine times for the Basque-based club, the most recent coming in last weekend's 3-0 win over Valladolid.

“I’m really enjoying it. We had a good result at the weekend, so it’s going really well,” Burke said while on Scotland duty.

“I’m learning a lot each day – the coaching staff speak English very well.

“I have a lot of video meetings, they talk to me one-to-one and it’s really nice to know somebody wants to help you and get the best out of you.

“I feel really happy there and I’m developing as much as I can.”

Burke’s big money move to Leipzig came to an end in 2017 when he moved to West Brom for £15m, before being farmed out to Celtic on loan last season, a switch which didn't really work out.

The Scotland international has played just 24 times for the Baggies in total, with Burke admitting his journey since leaving the City Ground has been far from ideal.

“For me, it’s been really frustrating.

“Not staying at one club and cementing a spot.

“I think this is part and parcel of my career right now, it’s the way it’s going for me.

“I’d love to be at a club and be the first name on the teamsheet every week – I think it will come with time.”

Despite being largely restricted to coming off the bench in Spain, Burke says he’s doing everything within his power to improve.

“I’m still at a good age and I’m doing everything I can now to learn the game.

“When opportunities do come, I hope I'm able to cement a spot, stay in the team and get a good run of games because that’s what I need right now to get as much confidence as possible.

“Good times are coming.”

What will become of his future at West Brom remains to be seen for Burke, though as it stands, it would appear his stay at the Hawthorns is coming towards a conclusion.

“The situation at West Brom is very difficult right now because I’ve been there for so long, but haven’t played many games for them.

“You feel that you’re not so much part of that team because I’ve been to other clubs, it can be difficult.

“The lads are great with me when I go back, it’s a really good environment to be in.

“For me now, I have to focus on this loan, take it all in and hopefully good things come from me going on loan.

“Hopefully it puts me in the shop window.”
