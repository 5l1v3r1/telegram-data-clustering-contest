Erdogan Says Cannot Abandon the S-400s For 'Patriots'

15 Nov 2019, 08:30

Turkey’s president Recep Tayyip Erdogan said Washington was not right to propose that Ankara get rid of the Russian S-400 missile defenses it purchased, calling it an infringement of sovereign rights, according to Turkish media.

In a meeting at the White House on Wednesday, US President Donald Trump urged Erdogan to abandon the S-400 systems that began arriving in Turkey in July despite threats of sanctions from Washington.

Asked after his meeting whether Turkey would consider not activating the S-400s, Erdogan told reporters Ankara cannot harm its relations with Russia.

He also again held out the option of buying US Patriot defenses, Reuters reported.

“We said, ‘We see the proposal to remove the S-400s completely while buying the Patriots as an infringement of our sovereign right and certainly do not find it right,’” he was quoted as saying.

“I cannot abandon the S-400s because of Patriots now. If you are going to give us Patriots, give them,” he added.

Erdogan also said that he saw a much more positive approach to the F-35 issue from Trump.
