Newcastle United fans are all saying the same thing after Miguel Almiron scored for Paraguay

Almiron is yet to score for Newcastle but bagged his second goal in his last three games for his country

15 Nov 2019, 08:16 by Sean McCormick

Newcastle United fans are all saying the same thing after Miguel Almiron netted the winning goal for Paraguay last night.

Almiron scored the only goal of the game as La Albirroja ran out 1-0 winners over Bulgaria in a friendly win in Sofia.

The 25-year-old picked up the ball on the edge of the box, shifted the ball on to his left foot and whipped a fierce effort past Bulgaria goalkeeper Georgi Georgiev to give his side the lead.

It was his second goal in three international games for his country - in stark contrast to his goalscoring form at Newcastle.

Almiron arrived on Tyneside for a then club record fee in January and his form helped Newcastle transform their season.

Ex-Magpies boss Rafa Benitez and current Newcastle manager Steve Bruce are both big fans of the former Atlanta United star, particularly his work rate, pace, and ability to create a chance for himself or a team-mate in difficult situations.

The one thing that has been lacking is a goal for the Magpies. He has had a glut of huge opportunities this season to end his duck and for one reason or another it just hasn't happened.

Almiron boasted an impressive goalscoring record for Atlanta, finding the net 22 times in 70 games for the 2018 MLS Cup champions.

But after a goalbound effort struck team-mate Allan Saint-Maximin before ballooning over an empty net against Bournemouth last weekend, fans started to question whether he just wasn't meant to score for Newcastle.

But a well taken goal for his country on Thursday evening has offered hope and Newcastle fans are now desperate for him to transfer his form in front of goal for his country to club level.

'Squashy' took to Twitter to joke: "Put a Paraguay top on Almiron and he might start doing the business for us."

Adam Beckett added: "Miguel Almirón has scored a goal. It’s happened. Not for Newcastle of course, but still. He can do it!"

And Murray White tweeted: "Almiron got a goal for Paraguay. Hopefully gives him some confidence in front of goal and can get off the mark in black-and-white soon."
