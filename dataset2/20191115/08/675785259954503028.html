Timo Werner 'must accept Jurgen Klopp term' to agree Liverpool transfer

The Premier League club are reportedly interested in a move for the RB Leipzig striker

15 Nov 2019, 08:56 by Hassan Rashed

Timo Werner would have to accept a back-up role at Liverpool in order to complete a move to Anfield, according to reports.

Although the Reds have made a stunning start to the season and currently hold an eight-point lead at the top of the Premier League, Jurgen Klopp is understood to be looking to strengthen his side when the transfer window re-opens in January.

And the German has reportedly put RB Leipzig star Werner at the top his list as he looks to guide the Merseyside club to their first league title since 1990.

Liverpool have long been linked with a move for the striker, but Bleacher Report claims that the 23-year-old would have to agree to a place on the bench in to secure a switch to the European champions.

Despite already scoring an impressive 15 goals in all competitions for Leipzig this season, the Germany international would face a tough task to force his way into a dominant front three.

Roberto Firmino, Sadio Mane and Mohamed Salah have been on top form in the early stages of the season and played a crucial role in the Reds' unbeaten start to the league campaign.

Werner's record of 61 goals in just 104 Bundesliga games for Leipzig has seen him establish himself as one of the top talents in Europe, with Manchester United and Bayern Munich also believed to be showing interest in the former Stuttgart man.

He is currently under contract until 2023, but current boss Julian Nagelsmann has claimed that he would not stop his star forward from leaving if the right opportunity arises.

"I’ve already advised many players to take off their wedding ring with their current club to move somewhere else," Nagelsmann said in an interview with Sport Bild .

"I’d do the same with Timo Werner if he were developing faster than the team at RB Leipzig."
