BT Sport confirm they have beaten ITV to retain Champions League rights in £1.2bn deal

BT Sport have pipped ITV to retain the rights to show Champions League in the UK until 2024 in a huge boost to the broadcaster

15 Nov 2019, 10:16 by Liam Prenderville, http://www.facebook.com/mirrorfootball

BT Sport have confirmed they have retained Champions League TV rights in the UK in a staggering £1.2bn deal.

ITV had been hoping to win back the rights but BT have announced they have retained rights to the competition until 2024.

They have secured the rights to all 420 games in the Champions League, Europa League and the new UEFA Conference League.

Sky Sports, who had previously held the rights, had also mounted a challenge but missed out to BT.

The new deal will come into play in 2021 and will offer an increase of 77 matches from the current contract.

BT have confirmed they will pay £400m each year for the rights.

BT's CEO of Consumer Division, Marc Allera, said: "We’re delighted to remain the home of UEFA Champions League in the UK. BT Sport leads the way when it comes to UEFA Champions League coverage, and we’re very excited to continue to bring our world class coverage to one of the most exciting football competitions in the world - whether that’s broadcasting 12 games simultaneously or delivering industry leading images in 4K UHD.

“With increasingly more ways to watch our content, with more games on show than ever before, and alongside our excellent line up of other competitions and sports, BT Sport is going from strength to strength.”

The Champions League returns next week with the Premier League sides looking good for a place in the knockout stages.

Manchester City are all but through in Group C, as are Liverpool in Group E.

Tottenham are second in Group B while Chelsea's battle to qualify is set to go down to the wire.
