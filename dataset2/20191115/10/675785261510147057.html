Roy Keane offers brutal advice to Ian Wright ahead of I'm a Celebrity

ITV pundits Roy Keane and Lee Dixon were discussing Ian Wright's stint on I'm a Celebrity...Get Me Out of Here

15 Nov 2019, 10:10 by Liam Prenderville, http://www.facebook.com/mirrorfootball

Roy Keane has offered some advice to fellow pundit Ian Wright ahead of his stint on I'm A Celebrity...Get Me Out Of Here.

Arsenal legend Wright is in the jungle this year with the hit ITV show launching on Sunday.

Wright will follow in the footsteps of former footballers Neil Ruddock, Jimmy Bullard and Harry Redknapp, who won the show in 2018.

Wright missed England's victory over Montenegro on Thursday night with the 56-year-old a regular pundit for ITV, alongside Keane.

Discussing his stint in the jungle, Manchester United legend Keane said: I'm looking forward to watching it but I've asked him 'please don't cry whilst you're in there'."

Wright's former teammate Lee Dixon also had his say on his involvement in the show.

"He's either going to win it or he will be out after an hour because it's Wrighty," Dixon said.

"He loves his food, his sleep and he doesn't like to be buried. There's every chance of that all going wrong, it'll be good watching.

"He loves his food, he loves his Nancy's cooking. He can cook himself but there will be nothing to eat!"

Former Arsenal defender Ashley Cole was coming through the ranks during Wright's time in north London.

Cole added: "He was a bit of the joker and as a young kid if we used to see him he'd kick you up the bum, meg you, 'go get me a cup of tea', 'get my boots' so we'd try to hide from him as much as possible."
