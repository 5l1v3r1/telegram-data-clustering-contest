Legionella bacteria found at Armadale Hospital

15 Nov 2019, 10:03

Legionella bacteria has been found during routine testing of the water supply at Armadale Hospital.

The bacteria can cause Legionnaires’ disease, a potentially fatal form of pneumonia, if it is inhaled from water or soil.

No patients have contracted Legionnaires’ disease because of the bacteria, which was believed to have been found in rooms of the hospital which are not regularly used.

An Armadale Hospital spokesperson said there was negligible risk to staff and patients and no cause for alarm.

“We will continue to monitor this situation, and we have comprehensive processes in place to manage situations such as these,” she said.

“We will continue to monitor the hospital’s water supply and have undertaken a number of precautions including the flushing of water lines.”

Routine water supply testing at the hospital is designed to detect bacteria, or any other issues, before it can affect patients or staff.

The hospital remains fully operational.

Around 72 cases of Legionnaires’ disease are diagnosed in WA every year. It is treated with antibiotics, and can be fatal if not treated in time.
