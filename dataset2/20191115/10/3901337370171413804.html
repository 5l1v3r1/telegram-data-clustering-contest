'I Was Ready To Kill Luis Suarez'

15 Nov 2019, 10:27 by SL International

Following Raheem Sterling's bust-up with England teammate Joe Gomez earlier in the week, a Liverpool star has revealed why he was once "ready to kill" former colleague Luis Suarez during a training session.

Read: Ronaldo Facing Two-Year Ban?

On Monday, Sterling was dropped from the England squad after reportedly grabbing Gomez by the throat in an incident that left the Liverpool defender with a scratch beneath his eye.

The severity of the incident has since been played down by both players, with Sterling accepting his punishment and issuing a public apology, but it has opened discussion into the dynamic of football dressing rooms.

Reds captain Jordan Henderson has since revealed how he, too, nearly came to blows with former teammate Suarez during their shared time at Liverpool.

"I was trying to do the best I can do," Henderson said, according to the Daily Mail.

"Being a footballer, there is criticism and people doubting you all the time.

"At that time, I was a young player and there were one or two things Luis did in training that I didn't like.

"It made me feel I wasn't good enough to be in the same team in training. His arms were up, like 'what the f***, what is he doing', like I shouldn't be there.

"That really hurt me. He did it three times, and then I exploded and I was ready to kill him. From that point, I had a good relationship with Luis. The next game I set him up for his goal.

"He was brilliant with me after that and I was really close to him after that, and he was another big player I learned from."
