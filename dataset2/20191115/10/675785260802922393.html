MotoGP rider Michele Pirro's bike goes up in flames at start of practice

Italian rider Michele Pirro had to deal with a fiery end to his free practice session in Valencia ahead of the final MotoGP race of the season

15 Nov 2019, 10:18 by Rich Jones, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/rich-jones/

MotoGP rider Michele Pirro had a fiery moment in practice ahead of the final race of the season as his bike burst into flames.

Pirro was out on track going around a left handed corner when he slowed up noticing something was not right with his bike.

But he initially seemed unaware of the violent flames beneath him as an inferno started near the back wheel.

He eventually took a look down and appeared to do a double take before ditching the bike quickly in the gravel trap.

Marshals swooped in with fire extinguishers to put out the blaze as Pirro walked back to the paddock unharmed by the alarming incident.

The experienced Italian, 33, is a test rider for Ducati and unlikely to appear in Sunday's final race of the season.

He takes part in practice sessions as the team work on the bike which is ridden by main team riders Andrea Dovizioso and Danilo Petrucci.

Pirro did race in Italy, finishing seventh in his home Grand Prix, and retired in his second race of the year in San Marino.

Fabio Quartararo clocked the fastest time in free practice one, ahead of Jack Miller and world champion Marc Marquez.

Pirro clocked the 13th quickest time despite his fiery exit, just ahead of British ace Cal Crutchlow.

Former Ducati rider Jorge Lorenzo announced his retirement yesterday, meaning the race in Spain will be his last in the sport.

"I love this sport but above all I love winning," Lorenzo said at a special news conference also attended by series CEO Carmelo Ezpeleta.

"I have let myself down and I have let down Honda but I think this is the best decision for me and the team. We are both winners and we always have to fight to win."
