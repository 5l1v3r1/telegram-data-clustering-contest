Gadget Book: Planetarium scoops 2019 Royal Society’s Book Prize

15 Nov 2019, 10:01 by Alun Williams

Here’s a possible stocking filler for a budding young Gadget Master – Planetarium: Welcome to the Museum is the winner of the 2019 Royal Society’s Young People’s Book Prize.

It’s the work of astrophysicist Raman Prinja and artist Chris Wormell and seeks to portray “galleries of galactic matter”.

The book was cited for addressing all aspects of space, from the Sun and Solar System to the lives of stars, the Milky Way and beyond.

“The illustrations in this book are fantastic and they really capture the vast scale of space – you get to see inside Neptune, the life cycle of stars, and learn about exoplanets,” said Chair of the judges, Professor Sheila Rowan FRS.

“The writing is clear, informative, and a pleasure to read, with descriptions that draw you in. I can really imagine Planetarium inspiring young people about the wonders of the universe.”

Authors

Raman Prinja is Professor of Astrophysics at University College London and he specialises in massive stars, star formation and the evolution of galaxies.

He is a multiple winner of UCL faculty and department teaching awards and is keen to bring the subject of astronomy to a wider audience. He has written several successful books on the subject, including Night Sky Watcher which was shortlisted for the Royal Society Young People’s Book Prize in 2015.

Note that a Junior version of the book is also available for £10.99 (aimed at a younger age group of around age 8 upwards). It is an abridged version of the original and smaller in size, the publishers tell us.

Shortlist

The Royal Society reports that more than 10,000 young people from 471 UK schools and youth groups selected Planetarium as their favourite science book. It was chosen from a shortlist of six titles, which had been put forward by a panel of adult judges, chaired by Royal Society Fellow Professor Sheila Rowan. The famous author Michael Rosen was part of the panel.

You can read more about the Young People’s Book Prize and also see the other books on the shortlist.

The winning authors receive an award of £10,000.
