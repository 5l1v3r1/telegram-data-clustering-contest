Irvine Meadow boss Brian McGinty keen to cling onto Kilwinning's coat-tails ahead of end of month derby

Second-placed Meadow travel to Largs on league duty this Saturday (Nov 16) before hosting Glenafton in a Scottish Junior Cup fourth round tie the following weekend.

15 Nov 2019, 22:09 by Eric McGowan, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/eric-mcgowan/

Brian McGinty is praying Irvine Meadow can cling on to the coat-tails of Premiership leaders Kilwinning ahead of a derby showdown at the end of the month.

Second-placed Meadow travel to Largs on league duty this Saturday before hosting Glenafton in a Scottish Junior Cup fourth round tie the following weekend.

With Kilwinning already out of the Scottish, Chris Strain’s side have an opportunity to steal a march on Meadow before the sides meet at Buffs Park on November 30.

 

McGinty is desperate not to lose too much ground and is challenging his players do the double over Largs by taking all three points at Barrfields.

He said: “We know if we win we are guaranteed to still be sitting second going into the Kilwinning game, regardless of what other teams do.

“That’s the carrot for the players. We know Kilwinning can pull away from us but it’s about keeping that to a minimum by doing our bit.

“It’s going to be a really tough game against Largs. They are a very good team and Stuart Davidson always has them very well organised.

“We have a lot of big games coming up this month but at this moment in time, Largs is the biggest and that’s where our full focus is.”

Meadow go into the clash on the back of a 3-1 win away to Thorniewood United in the first round of the West of  Scotland Cup.

 

McGinty reshuffled his pack for the clash and reckons making full use of his squad will be crucial for such an important run of matches. He said: “Its more about getting boys game time than resting players. We want everyone to be ready for the schedule ahead.”

Striker Adam Forsyth made his long awaited return from injury by coming off the bench for the last 10 minutes and comes into consideration for Saturday.
