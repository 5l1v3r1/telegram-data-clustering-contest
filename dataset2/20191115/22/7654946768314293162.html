James Holzhauer wins ‘Jeopardy!’ Tournament of Champions in nail-biting game

15 Nov 2019, 22:32 by Hana R. Alberts

James Holzhauer got the last laugh.

The “Jeopardy” whiz, who won 32 regular games in a row over the summer, took home the $250,00 grand price in the storied game show’s Tournament of Champions Friday night — besting the contestant who ended his streak in the process.

Over the two-night final, Holzhauer — a 35-year-old professional gambler from Las Vegas — faced off against Emma Boettcher, a 27-year-old librarian from Chicago who beat him on June 3, and Francois Barcomb, a 50-year-old physics teacher from New Paltz, NY, who won the Teachers’ Tournament.

On Thursday night, at the end of the first half of the Tournament of Champions final, Holzhauer led with $49,326, while Boettcher trailed with $26,400 and Barcomb tallied $1,800.

But in the tense and exciting faceoff Friday night, Boettcher lead the field going into final Jeopardy, with $21,600 to Holzhauer’s $17,785 and Barcomb’s $1,600. All three contestants got the Final Jeopardy clue correct, and Boettcher was the victor of night 2 with $38,600. Holzahuer ended night 2 with $27,597 and Barcomb with $3,200. But when Thursday night’s totals were added in, Holzhauer emerged victorious: His podium read $76,923, while Boettcher’s flashed $65,000 and Barcomb’s $5,000.

Holzhauer winnings now total $2,712,216. He has earned the third-highest total in “Jeopardy!” history, trailing only Ken Jennings’ $3,370,700 (over a record 74 straight regular-season victories plus tournaments) and Brad Rutter’s $4,688,436.

Holzhauer, who has talked jokingly about “payback” in promotional videos posted on Twitter, complimented his foe on her performance in the finals.
