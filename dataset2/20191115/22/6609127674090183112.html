MY TYPE ON PAPER

Love Island hunk Anton Danyluk reveals reading calms him as he backs Scottish Sun’s Free Books for Nurseries campaign

15 Nov 2019, 22:36 by Colan Lamont

BEEFY bookworm Anton Danyluk turns heads wherever he goes but the Love Island star is just as happy turning pages.

The reality TV fave shot to fame following his stint on the ITV dating show during the summer — and admits it’s been hard to adjust to his new-found celebrity status.

But as well as pumping iron, the 25-year-old gym owner likes to lift the weight of stardom off his shoulders by burying his head in a good book.

Anton says: “I wasn’t a big reader until a few years ago when I read The Secret by Rhonda Byrne and that completely changed who I am as a person.

“Reading is something that helps to quiet your mind. I love doing it and escaping whatever is going on.

“Sometimes you can have too much going on in your head and reading can be calming.

“It’s easy to forget that you’re also learning while you read. Knowledge is power and that’s why I read books when I’m on the go.”

Anton’s recent passion for reading is why he’s right behind The Scottish Sun’s Free Books For Nurseries campaign in association with HarperCollins Publishers.

Although mum Sherie Ann read to him religiously as a youngster, he admits he lost his love for books when he started school.

Now he wants caregivers to really encourage little ones to let their imaginations run wild and have fun reading before it seems like a chore when they’re older.

And he is urging nurseries to sign up to our £950,000 giveaway before registration closes at 11.59PM ON MONDAY.

He says: “The younger you start reading the better. You want it to be viewed as something fun because it is.

“Kids have such great imaginations and books are an amazing outlet for them to really enjoy themselves.

"The sooner that kids get passionate about reading, the less of a chore it will seem when they are given books at school.

“They will also be so much better at it as they feel more confident too.”

And Anton — who recently split with his Love Island beau Belle Hassan, 21 — thinks the first step to improving literacy rates is putting down the latest technology.

Although he reckons iPads and iPhones have their place, he wants to see pre-schoolers picking up books instead.

"The sooner that kids get passionate about reading, the less of a chore it will seem when they are given books at school."Anton Danyluk

He adds: “I still wish that I read more in school. And it was hard to reach kids then.

“But it’s as hard as it has ever been now because they are all on tablets, phones and watching YouTube from a young age.

“Getting back to basics is so important. They can get so engrossed in technology — which is great since they’re so technically aware — but it takes away from what is important too.

“Learning to read — and to enjoy it — is so important for the rest of your life.”

And no one knows that more than Anton’s mum.

After she stopped going to school regularly at the age of eight, the 55-year-old credits books with boosting her education.

Ever since Sherie Ann launched Scotland’s first popcorn-making business MacCorns in 1991, the life coach wanted to release a kids’ book with a positive message for the whole family.

She felt like she was banging her head against a brick wall after trying to get published following Anton’s birth in 1994.

But thanks to her newfound fame following Anton’s Love Island stint, Sherie Ann has decided to revive her book Meet The MacCorns of Scotland: The Funkie Fitness Family.

Sherie Ann says: “I developed characters when Anton was born and started to create stories around them about being positively powerful popcorn people who explode to 42-times their size.

“The characters teach the kids about how to be the best you can be in every way and how routines and healthy habits reap rewards.

“It will help kids learn, in a fun way, how to do everyday routines like getting up and making the bed.

“It’s a book that’s perfect for pre-school kids and their adults can read it to them.

“But it’s also something that the whole family can enjoy together.”

When Anton was young, Sherie Ann used to read to him every day about the popcorn people she was dreaming up.

She thinks more adults should be trying to bring out the creative side of their kids.

Sherie Ann says: “I’m more of a storyteller and I’d tell Anton that his mammy has these powerful popcorn people.

“He loved it. It was so much fun for him.

“I think with me leaving school so early, I realised I wanted to learn things and that the best way to do that was by reading things that were fun but also interesting and educational.

“The problem nowadays is getting away from putting an iPad in their face.

“Now they are all watching YouTube as toddlers.

“I see it on holiday and I hate it. You are sitting having breakfast and the family don’t talk to the kids — they sit them in a high chair with a tablet watching cartoons.

“Reading is so much fun. It’s important kids know that.”

And now Anton can’t wait to see the book he helped inspire come to life when Sherie Ann’s debut goes to print.

He hopes the popcorn characters he was fascinated by capture the imagination of other kids and helps them to find their passion for reading.

Anton says: “I told mum she had to write this book because she’s so passionate about it.

“She told me all about the characters as a kid and now we can use our platform to use them to get other kids reading too.

“I’m so proud of her for trying to make a difference.”
