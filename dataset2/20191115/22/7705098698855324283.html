Jon Favreau is Serious About Making a New Star Wars Holiday Special

15 Nov 2019, 22:37 by Robert Workman

Back in September, we posted a report about director Jon Favreau looking into creating a new Star Wars Holiday Special. During a recent fan event for The Mandalorian series, Favreau reiterated that he’s still working on it.

“Oh, I would definitely be interested in doing a Holiday Special,” reiterated Favreau in an interview with Variety. “And I’m not going to say who I would be interested in. But one of the people is a member of the cast in an upcoming episode of the show. So we’ll leave it at that for now.”

As for why he feels a new Star Wars Holiday Special would make sense, Favreau provided further explanation. 

“I’ve been thinking about it. It’s ready, the ideas are ready. I think it could be really fun. Not as part of this, but there’s an excitement around it because it was so fun and weird, and off and not connected to what Star Wars was in the theater. ‘The Mandalorian’ cartoon, the Boba Fett cartoon, from the holiday special was definitely a point of inspiration for what we did in the show.”

The original Star Wars Holiday Special aired in 1978 and immediately became a cult classic. While some see it as a disaster, others enjoyed its humorous tone and characters. Even Rifftrax got into the action with a custom riff for the special, complete with commentary for its commercials.

There’s no further word on when Favreau would begin production on the show, or if Disney would even greenlight it. But considering the success of The Mandalorian on Disney+, it’s not impossible. Favreau has already shown he can weave holiday magic with Elf.
