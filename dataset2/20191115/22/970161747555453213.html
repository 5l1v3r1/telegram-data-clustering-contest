I'm A Celebrity's Ian Wright reveals sleeping habit could cause havoc in camp

15 Nov 2019, 22:52

Ian Wright’s very unfortunate sleeping habit could cause havoc for hisI’m A Celebrity…Get Me Out Of Here! campmates.

The ex-footballer has admitted that he has a rather bad habit of sleepwalking…and will probably need another contestant to help back into bed.

We can see that going down very well with a group of hungry and sleep-deprived celebrities, can’t you?

‘If I do sleepwalk and they direct me back to the bed, that’s what my mum used to do as a kid,’ he said.

‘Dennis Bergkamp used to turn me back around towards my bed, all I need is someone to turn me back around towards the bed.

‘Let’s hope that somebody is looking out for me. I haven’t sleptwalk in a while but this is a different environment isn’t it. Hopefully it’ll happen and we can all have a laugh.’

Luckily, Ian will have a whole host of campmates to call upon should a sleepwalking emergency arise.

He’s joining a veritable star-studded line-up, including reality TV icon Caitlyn Jenner, EastEnders actress Jacqueline Jossa and Capital FM DJ Roman Kemp.

It also feels like the former footballer might be a bit of a problem in the camp, as his mother has previously revealed she’s worried that the bushtucker trails could trigger a deadly asthma attack for her son.

Time to wrap him up in cotton wool we think?

Nesta Wright, 90, said: ‘When he was a kid he suffered really badly with asthma, he doesn’t have it now as an adult but going in those small places with water dripping – it’s scary.’

All we’re saying is – make sure you have medic Bob on standby at all times!

There’s also been some obligatory drama before the new series has even started – with Capital FM DJ Roman Kemp embroiled in a social media scandal.

The son of Spandau Ballet’s Martin Kemp had to apologise for previous ‘offensive’ material across his old accounts.

Taking to his Instagram story recently, he shared a video with his 204,000 followers, explaining: ‘I wanted to talk about something I’ve seen a lot of celebrities talking about, and people around the world talking about, and that is the use of language online.’

‘Being totally honest, I’m shocked and appalled and ashamed to say I did find things,’ he continued. ‘I did find things I’m not at all proud of. I don’t associate myself with that. I know the person who wrote those things is not the man I am now, but they were there.’

I’m A Celebrity returns 17 November, at 9pm, on ITV.
