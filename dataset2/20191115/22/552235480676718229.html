Tragic Liam Smith's friends holding charity football match to mark one year since teen's disappearance

Liam was reported missing in November 2018, his body was found in March this year.

15 Nov 2019, 22:00 by Kathleen Speirs

Grieving friends of Liam Smith are organising a charity football match to mark one year since the tragic teen was last seen alive.

Liam, 16, was reported missing from is home on November 18, 2018, sparking a massive police search.

The Aberdeenshire schoolboy's body was found in Craig of Affrusk at about 3pm on March 22.

It’s on the opposite side of the River Dee near Banchory from where he was thought to have vanished.

Police say there are no suspicious circumstances surrounding his death.

 

To mark one year since his disappearance, Liam's friends are hosting a charity football match on Sunday.

An avid football fan, Liam's heartbroken pals say all money raised from the game will go to local charity, Mental Health Aberdeen.

Kyle Leiper launched the Just Giving page.

In a statement on the site Kyle wrote: "In memory of our friend Liam we thought it would be a good idea for us all to get together and play in a charity football match. 

"This will take place on Sunday,  November 17 at 1 pm at Westdyke Leisure Centre grass pitch in Westhill. 

 

"The two teams playing will be Liam’s friends he went to Elrick Primary School with other friends he made at Westhill Academy. 

"We have chosen to donate any funds raised to Mental Health Aberdeen which is a local charity.

"It provides counselling and information services to adults and young people experiencing difficulties related to their mental health and wellbeing.  

"Football played a huge part in Liam's life and we all shared many football related memories with him over the years.

"So we thought this would be a very fitting way to remember him whilst raising awareness."

 

The page has raised over £4,500 so far with loved ones, locals and strangers donating in their droves.

One commented: "Well done boys, a great way to remember Liam."

Another posted: "Such a great cause and a heartbreaking story that remains ingrained in the minds of us all up here."

A third wrote: "Well done boys, such a great cause.

"Have a great day on Sunday, as that’s what Liam would want."
