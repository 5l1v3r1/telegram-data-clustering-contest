Glen Kamara dismisses Rangers exit rumours as he breaks silence on Leeds link

The midfielder has impressed for Rangers and has been heavily linked with a move down south.

15 Nov 2019, 12:07 by Mark McDougall, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/mark-mcdougall/

Rangers midfielder Glen Kamara insists he is ignoring rumours linking him with a move away from Ibrox.

The Finland international only moved to Rangers last January but his form has earned him admiring glances from England.

Leeds United are the latest team to be linked with a move while Brighton are also reportedly interested.

A fee worth up to £10million is being suggested for Kamara - who cost Rangers just £50k when he moved from Dundee.

But the 23-year-old isn't interested in listening to the speculation and told YLE in his homeland: "You know what, I’m a Rangers player until told otherwise, I have no relevance to anything else so I’m focused on Rangers and Finland at the moment.

"Going up to Scotland’s been good for me, for my career. You have everything there, nutrition, the coaches, you’ve got your team-mates, better players who make you better yourself, so it’s been great."

Finland could qualify for Euro 2020 with a win over Liechenstein and Kamara admitted the nerves he used to get have disappeared.

He added: "When I was younger yeah but not any more. I wouldn’t say I’m an old veteran but I kind of got used to it. I feel fine, I’m just excited for the game as usual. I feel like we can just go out there and do what we can do, and do our best, and hopefully we can go and get a result.

"I’ve got a few childhood friends coming to see the game tomorrow who I haven’t seen in years upon years, so that’s going to be exciting.

"Everyone’s been really good, supportive, messaging me and that."
