4 men convicted for ad exec’s killing

15 Nov 2019, 12:40 by Hector Lawas

The Las Piñas City regional trial court yesterday convicted four men for the 2013 killing of advertising executive Kristelle “Kae” Davantes

In a 13-page decision, Judge Salvador Timbang Jr. of Branch 253 sentenced Lloyd Benedict Enriquez, Samuel Decimo, Kelvin Jorek Evangelista and Jomar Pepito to reclusion perpetua for robbery with homicide.

Likewise, they were ordered to pay the victim’s family P50,000 as indemnity, P50,000 in moral damages,  and P25,000 in temperate damages.

“Based on what was proved by evidence their intent were to rob Kristelle. Their primary action was to take what they can,” the decision read in part.

The court noted that the accused sold the victim’s personal property  confirming the intent to gain.

Davantes was found dead—gagged with a handkerchief, tied using her car’s seatbelt and with five stab wounds on her neck—under a bridge in Silang, Cavite on Sept. 7, 2013.

She was at the gate of their house at Moonwalk Village in Las Piñas City from a night out with friends when she was held at gunpoint by two of the accused.

She was then taken, pinned down and placed at the back seat of her Toyota Altis.

When police found Davantes’ body, her belongings and her car were already missing.
