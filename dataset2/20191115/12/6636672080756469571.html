British Broadband

15 Nov 2019, 12:41 by David Manners

Controversy over this morning’s Labour Party initiative to connect every house in the country to fibre and provide broadband services to everyone free of charge, is growing.

The head of BT has said Labour’s £20 billion costing is half what the real cost will be.

Owners of private networks like Virgin have asked where does a competing, free service leave them.

The plan to raise the £230 million annual running costs of British Broadband from taxing the American digital giants looks problematic – given the difficulties in the past of taxing these companies.

The plan to connect the most remote houses to fibre first looks particularly uneconomic because the remote areas produce the least revenue.

There seems to be no plan to integrate 5G into British Broadband where cellular would be a cheaper connection option than fibre.
