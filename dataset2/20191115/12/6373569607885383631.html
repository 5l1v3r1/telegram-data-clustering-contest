What Plymouth Argyle manager Ryan Lowe expects from Mark Cooper's table-topping Forest Green

The Pilgrims will travel to Gloucestershire for their first-ever league meeting with Forest Green

15 Nov 2019, 12:26 by Chris Errington

Plymouth Argyle manager Ryan Lowe is not surprised that Mark Cooper’s Forest Green Rovers are sitting at the top of League Two.

Cooper shares the same football principles as Lowe, who has spoken very highly about him as their respective sides prepare to face each other at The New Lawn on Saturday.

Lowe said: “I’m looking forward to it. It’s going to be a good game of football - for once.”

In a division where many teams get the ball from back to front as soon as possible, Lowe and Cooper take a very different approach.

Cooper almost led Forest Green to promotion in only their second season as an EFL club last term.

They finished fifth in the table before losing to Tranmere Rovers in the play-off semi-final.

Lowe, who was a Sky Sports analyst for their coverage of both legs of the semi-final, believes there is much to like about Cooper and Rovers.

They are currently one point clear at the top of the table with a record of nine wins, four draws and only three defeats.

Lowe said: “Coops has done a fantastic job there. He has just missed out the last couple of years.

“He’s a terrific bloke – one of the good guys. He plays the right way, hence he has been up there the last few seasons.

“I have got nothing but the utmost respect for Coops. We have good conversations before and after games when we are down there (at Forest Green).

“He has got a good set of players and it’s no coincidence they are top of the league, let’s have it right.

“They have got some good players and they show consistent performance week in week out.

“It’s a test for us going there. We are certainly going there full of confidence and to look for a win, but so are Mark Cooper’s team also.”

Forest Green have lost only once at home in the league this season, when they were beaten 2-0 by Newport County on August 31.

A lot of Rovers’ success has been based on an excellent defensive record.

Lowe said: “They haven’t scored a lot of goals but they have only conceded 10, which is good in any league.

“If you are only conceding that with 16 games gone, then it is terrific.

“But we know what Coops is about, we know the way he sets up, we know his style of play. He’s very honest in his work.

“So it’s no coincidence that they are up there and have only conceded 10 goals.”

Lowe added: “We are going to the league leaders at the moment. Whether they are going to be there come the end of the season is to be seen.

“But, for me, I think Coops has got it right this year. I’m sure they are certainly going to be in the top seven fighting for promotion again.

“We are going there looking to get points on the board too.”

After playing Forest Green, Argyle’s next league fixture will be against Bradford City, who are currently in second spot, at Home Park on Saturday, November 23.

So these two matches will be a good indication of how the Pilgrims are shaping up under Lowe.

He said: “We are eight points off number one, and I would have took that, but we have got two big games coming up.”

Cooper, meanwhile, has also talked positively about Lowe in the build-up to Argyle’s visit.

He said: “I am sure (Argyle) will finish at the top end of the league. If yoou are going to get promoted you have to finish above Plymouth.

“When you are trying to drastically change the style of play, like what Ryan is trying to implement, it does take time.

“They will get it right and have got good players. Ryan knows how to build a really entertaining team.”
