Festive fun begins in Perth city centre

Christmas lights switch-on this weekend

15 Nov 2019, 12:41 by Rachel Clark, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/rachel-clark/

The Christmas festivities officially begin this weekend with the turning on of the Perth Christmas lights.

A whole weekend of entertainment and events is planned throughout the city centre on Saturday, November 16 and Sunday, November 17.

Although the main event on Saturday evening is the turning on of the Christmas lights, there is also live entertainment, live ice sculpting, markets and food and drink to enjoy as well.

On Saturday, November 16 the main stage on Tay Street will be entertaining from 2pm.

The McAndrews Sisters and Last Orders will kick off proceedings, before Boomin’ and The Giants of British Rock - a band made up of original members of Uriah Heep, Bad Company, Whitesnake, FM and Thunder - take to the stage.

A Christmas parade will leave from Thimblerow car park at 5.30pm and will make its way through the city centre to the main stage on Tay Street.

Provost Dennis Melloy will then be joined by local cancer fighter Lily Douglas on stage to officially turn on the lights at 6.15pm.

The big moment will trigger a massive fireworks display over the River Tay.

The best views of the fireworks will be from Perth Bridge, Queen’s Bridge and Tay Street.

Celebrities and household names will then take to the main stage, including TV presenter Stephen Mulhern, Basil Brush and music acts Atomic Kitten, Clubland Classics featuring Dario G, N Trance and Alice Deejay, Scottish singer-songwriter Nina Nesbitt.

The music will finish with Lionel Vinyl, a top tribute act to Lionel Richie.

There will be two other stages packed with entertainment as well on Horsecross Plaza and King Edward Street.

The children’s stage will be on Horsecross Plaza outside the concert hall, with acts including Cobblers the Clown, Basil Brush, Chloe Ashe, Mother Goose, Chico and the Julie Young Dancers, finishing with a Christmas pop party.

After the lights have been turned on, the stage turns into a live music stage with Woodrow Wilson and Lee Memphis King, a top tribute act to Elvis.

Local performers will be on the King Edward Street stage from noon until 5pm, including Inspire Dance Studio, Alan Sweeney, the Fair Baton Twirlers, Ad Lib’s youth performers and adult performers, students from Perth College UHI, CC Dance, Letham Majorettes, Perth Amateur Operatics Society, Phoenix All Stars, Will from Sweeney Todd and Dynamic Dance.

While all of the entertainment is going on, there will be two markets on throughout the weekend for revellers to explore.

Perth Festive Market set up in the city centre yesterday (Thursday, November 14) and will run in King Edward Street and High Street until Sunday, November 17 from 10am until 6pm each day.

The market features a continental street market with traders from all over the world selling food and crafts, and other stalls selling Christmas treats.

And on George Street there will be the ‘Chocolate, Gin and Things Market’.

This market will run on Saturday and Sunday from 10am until 5pm, selling artisan chocolates and local craft gins.

The fun will continue on Sunday, November 17 with food and live music on Horsecross Plaza and King Edward Street.

The City of Perth Feast will take place from 10am until 7pm.

There will be a celebration of music, dance, food and drink on King Edward Street, as well as a chance to visit Santa’s ice grotto.

Perth Cake Fest returns once again from noon until 4pm.

Amateur bakers from across the area have been tasked with recreating Perthshire’s iconic buildings and landmarks in cake form, and Simon Preston will be piecing them all together to create the area’s stunning edible landscape.

Those who prefer something a bit more savoury can enjoy the Zuppa Soup Festival, again from noon until 4pm.

And the Handmade Show will be in Perth Concert Hall on Sunday from 10.30am until 4.30pm.
