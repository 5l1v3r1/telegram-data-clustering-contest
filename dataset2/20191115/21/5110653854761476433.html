'AFC North football' needs a culture shift after Myles Garrett suspension

15 Nov 2019, 21:49 by Bill Bender

In the aftermath of the ugly incident involving Cleveland defensive end Myles Garrett swinging a helmet at Pittsburgh quarterback Mason Rudolph on Thursday, it is impossible not to look at the calendar and see the Steelers and Browns have a rematch at Heinz Field on Dec. 1.

How will those teams respond to what happened then?The NFL took the first shot Friday, issuing expedited suspensions. Garrett is suspended indefinitely and at a minimum for the remainder of this regular season and playoffs, and he should be. Pittsburgh's Maurkice Pouncey got three games, and Cleveland's Larry Ogunjobi received one game. Rudolph did not receive punishment, but he should have been suspended, too.

That doesn't answer the question about the rematch. Will these teams try to retaliate? What will the NFL do to ensure that the notion of "AFC North football" does not spill overand lead to a serious injury, which was thankfully avoided in Cleveland's 21-7 victory at FirstEnergy Stadium?Hopefully this is the last one on that scaleamong these division rivals.

Let's get this part out of the way first:Garrett,who is 6-4 and weighs 271 pounds,could have seriously injured Rudolph (or worse) had the crown of the helmet hit the quarterback instead of the cushion on the back. Garrett's response to Rudolph trying to yank his helmet off first is not justified.

Those who have watched AFC North football knew this moment was coming. Physical football and dirty hits are part of the division's lore, but it crossed the line for good Thursday and it's on the NFL to draw the line before something more serious happens.

To be clear, there is nothing wrong with physical, hard-hitting football and rivalries that are just as intense and mean-spirited as the ones as the college campuses in those states. The Steelers and Ravens have done that for years. But even when Pittsburgh's Ryan Clark knocked out Baltimore's WillisMcGaheein the 2008 AFC championship game, it was not on this level.
