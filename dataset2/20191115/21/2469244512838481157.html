Firefighters rescue woman after car flips over hedge and plunges 15ft in A396 crash

A woman has been taken to hospital with head injuries after the crash near Silverton

15 Nov 2019, 21:54 by Lewis Clarke, Katie Timms

A woman was rescued by firefighters after the car she was driving was involved in a crash which resulted in the vehicle falling 15ft.

At around 6.10pm this evening three cars collided on the A396 Tiverton Road between to the Chitterley Business Park and the The Ruffwell Inn in Silverton.

Devon and Cornwall Police, South Western Ambulance Service and Devon and Somerset Fire and Rescue were all called to the scene after it was reported that one car "flipped over a hedge into a field" and then fell 15ft, leaving a woman trapped.

The 81-year-old man who was in the passenger seat of the car that flipped managed to leave the vehicle on his own.

A police spokesperson confirmed that there were no other reports that anyone else was injured in the incident at this time.

The Devon Air Ambulance was requested, although it was not clear whether the woman was taken to the Royal Devon and Exeter Hospital via land or air ambulance.

She is said to have suffered injuries to her head, although the severity of her injuries is not known at this time.

Melissa Phillips, from Silverton, said: "Air ambulance was on the football pitch. It circled three times before it could land and was shining its spotlight around the school/Roach Lane/ Livingshayes.

"It seemed like it was struggling to land and had to really scope out the footy field."

Drivers were urged by the police to avoid the area and find alternative routes as officers closed the road at around 6.30pm to carry out accident investigation work.

Traffic between Exeter and Tiverton was affected as a result of the closure.

The road remained closed at 9.45pm and a police spokesperson said it was likely that the road would remain closed for a large part of the night.

Anyone who has any information on the collision, or witnessed it and may be able to help police with their enquiries, can call 101 or email 101@dcpolice.uk.

Alternatively, you can report information anonymously via Crimestoppers on 0800 555 111.
