IYC urges security agencies to probe Nembe pre-election violence

15 Nov 2019, 21:20 by https://www.facebook.com/Cheerfulpraise

Mike Odiegwu, Yenagoa

 

The Ijaw Youths Council (IYC) Central Zone, has called on security agencies to investigate the pre-election violence that rocked Nembe Local Government Area in Bayelsa State and left some persons dead and others injured.

The Zonal Chairman, Kennedy Olorogun, spoke on Friday during a one-day nonviolent summit, praise and prayer he organised to harp on the need for peace before, during and after the election.

Olorogun described the shooting and killing in Nembe as barbaric unacceptable and called on security agencies to up their game to forestall recurrence.

He said persons involved in the incident should be fished out and prosecuted to serve as deterrent to others.

Olorogun said there was no alternative to peace saying election should not be viewed as war but a democratic contest involving brothers.

He asked youths to eschew violence and stop allowing themselves to be used by politicians to disrupt the peace in the state.

Olorogun said: “We condemn in the strongest of terms the devilish act in Nembe that led to the death of so some youths with many people wounded.

“The IYC leadership also call on INEC and Security agencies to exhibit the characters of civility and neutrality to ensure the election is peaceful, transparent, credible, free and fair”.

In their various remarks, the Commissioner for Youths, Ebipatei Apaingolo and IYC National Secretary, Alfred Kemepado called on youths to stay away from violence during the poll.

Kemepado particularly told the youths that IYC had not and would not endorse any candidate for the election.

He, however, said members were free to support and vote for any candidate of their choice.

“Any purported endorsement is a crime to Ijaw nation. I urge the clan chairmen and IYC members to go down to their communities to exercise their civic responsibility of voting and I appeal to security agencies to be professional in discharging their duties and not to compromise the process”.
