Myles Garrett Responds To Indefinite NFL Suspension

15 Nov 2019, 18:54 by Tzvi Machlin

Anyone who saw Browns DE Myles Garrett swing a helmet at Steelers QB Mason Rudolph yesterday knew a suspension was looming. And that’s exactly what he got.

The NFL didn’t even need an entire day to figure out what to do after seeing the footage. Earlier today they handed down an indefinite suspension to Garrett.

Fines were also issued to both teams and lesser suspensions to other players who got involved.

Now Garrett himself has responded to the suspension.

In a statement, Garrett apologized to Rudolph, the Browns, and the NFL as a whole. He called what he did “a terrible mistake” and admitted to losing his cool before acting selfishly. Garrett pledged to be accountable and learn from his mistake.

“Last night, I made a terrible mistake. I lost my cool and what I did was selfish and unacceptable. I know that we are all responsible for our actions and I can only prove my true character through my actions moving forward. I want to apologize to Mason Rudolph, my teammates, our entire organization, our fans and to the NFL. I know I have to be accountable for what happened, learn from my mistake and I fully intend to do so.”
