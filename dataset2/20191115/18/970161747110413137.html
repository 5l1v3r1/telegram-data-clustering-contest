Perrie Edwards all smiles as boyfriend Alex smashes England appearance

15 Nov 2019, 18:05

Perrie Edwards was all smiles after smashing the latest performance with her Little Mix bandmates.

The Shout Out To My Ex singer is currently in Manchester as part of the band’s LM5 tour, which is coming to an end in London on 22 November.

And the 26-year-old was spotted clutching adorable dog Hatchi as they made their way into the seriously swanky Hilton Hotel moments after getting off stage.

She nailed the off-duty chic look in a black Little Mix hoodie and grey NASA tracksuit bottoms, completing her outfit with scuffed white trainers.

We’re not entirely sure what put that grin on her face but we’re betting it had something to do with seeing boyfriend Alex Oxlade-Chamberlain scoring a goal for England in their game against Montenegro on Thursday.

While Perrie wasn’t able to be in the crowd at Wembley – because of the aforementioned gig at Manchester Arena – she definitely would’ve kept an eye on that score throughout the night.

The couple – dubbed Alerrie – started dating way back in 2016, and have been practically inseparable ever since.

However, the Touch singer found herself on the wrong side of football fans recently after she accidentally managed to spell her beau’s name wrong on Instagram.

Yes, seriously.

The X Factor winner took a second to give the 26-year-old a shout-out following his winning goal for Liverpool in the Champions League.

But she was clearly too excited about his big moment, as she added an extra ‘e’ onto her Instagram story.

Quoting a chant about the footballer, she captioned it: ‘You to me are everything, my Alex Oxlade-Chamberlaine OH BABY OH BABEHHH!’

Side note: Pezza decided to skip out the part of the song where supporters praise him for ‘s**gging Little Mix,’ which is understandable.

While it is quite a catchy chant, unimpressed Liverpool fans called her out for the spelling mistake – with Alex even getting on the action.

Noticing the slip-up, he screenshot it and shared it to his own story, joking: ‘Spell my name right at least will ya (sic).’

She styled it out well, replying: ‘It’ll be way easier to spell when it’s my second name and I have to write it all the time.’
