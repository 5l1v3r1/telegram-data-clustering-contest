Ottumwa woman raises over $1K for mom who lost belongings in camper fire

15 Nov 2019, 18:53 by Beth Waldon

OTTUMWA, Iowa â€” An Ottumwa woman is helping her mother get back on her feet after a camper she had been staying in was lost to a fire.

Tracy Dixson was visiting family in Florida last month when a suspected electrical failure started the fire.

"I was immediately like, 'Oh my God is she OK, is she hurt, how is the dog and the cat, how much does she have left, will I have to go down there?'," said Dixson's daughter, Haven Kriegshauser.

Dixson and her pets made it out safely. But...

"She lost pretty much all of her possessions," Kriegshauser said.

Dixson had just paid nearly $4,000 to remodel the used camper. Now, Kriegshauser tells KTVO the RV is destroyed.

"So she basically had to start from the ground up, so we were really just trying to get her situated," she said.

Kriegshauser started a GoFundMe account for her mom. Kriegshauser has already raised more than half of her $2,500 goal.

"Because of this, she's actually been able to take care of herself during this time," Kriegshauser said. "That's really important to me, I worry about her a lot."
