Santa Clarita shooting: Gracie Anne Muehlberger identified as a victim

15 Nov 2019, 18:26 by Gabrielle Fonrouge and Natalie Musumeci

One of the two teen students slain in the Southern California school shooting has been identified.

The victim was identified as Gracie Anne Muehlberger who had just turned 15-years-old on Oct. 10, the Los Angeles County Coroner confirmed to The Post Friday.

Muehlberger, along with a 14-year-old boy, was gunned down by alleged shooter Nathaniel Berhow Thursday morning at Santa Clarita’s Saugus High School.

Police previously said that the female victim was 16-years-old. The coroner is still working to confirm the identity of the 14-year-old.

Surveillance footage captured the suspect, who also attended the school, walk onto the campus’ quad, pull a .45-caliber semiautomatic pistol from his backpack and shoot five fellow students before turning the gun on himself.

The shooter survived a self-inflicted gunshot wound to the head and remained in critical condition Friday.

A 15-year-old girl who was shot in the hip and a 14-year-old girl shot in her shoulder and lower abdomen remained hospitalized, but were both expected to make a full recovery.

A 15-year-old boy who was shot and injured had been treated and released from a local hospital.
