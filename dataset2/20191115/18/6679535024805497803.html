Third train service to Bengaluru likely

15 Nov 2019, 18:22 by Special Correspondent

Bowing to demands from different quarters, the Southern Railway is likely to introduce a third train service from north Kerala to Bengaluru.

After a meeting of Railway officials attached to the Palakkad Division held in Kozhikode last week, another round of discussion was held in Chennai. Priority has been accorded for starting a new train service to Bengaluru, said M.K.Raghavan, MP. At present, weekly trains Yesvantpur Express (16528) and Yesvantpur Express (16566) run from Kozhikode to Bengaluru. A proposal to convert the weekly train (16565/16566) into a daily service was under consideration. But, more services were required, especially from north Kerala, to connect to the IT city, said Mr. Raghavan.

Southern Railway Chief Operations Manager (COM) S. Anantharaman, with whom he had a meeting, also assured the MP that steps would be taken to improve rail connectivity for the passengers of Malabar. The steps include starting of MEMU trains in the Mangaluru-Kozhikode-Shoranur sector.

The COM said he would also look into the possibility of starting a Mangaluru-Madurai Intercity Express train, besides extending the Goa-Mangaluru Intercity Express to Shoranur.

Mr. Raghavan said another proposal was regarding increasing the frequency of trains such as Rajadhani Express, Mangalore Kacheguda Express, Kannur-Thiruvananthapuram Jan Shatabdi Express and Howrah-Mangaluru Vivek Express. Proposals such as establishing a pit-lane and upgrading the West Hill Station as second terminal for Kozhikode station and additional coaches for Parasuram Express also came up during the discussion, he said.
