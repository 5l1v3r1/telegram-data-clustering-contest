Watch countdown to Nottingham's Christmas light switch-on

10, 9, 8, 7...

15 Nov 2019, 18:01 by Rachel Gorman

No fireworks? No problem! Was the verdict given to this year's Nottingham Christmas light switch-on.

Taking place at 4.30pm this afternoon (Friday, November 15), the traditional Winter Wonderland opening ceremony was down-sized from last year amid plans to invest more funds into the city's New Year's Eve celebrations.

Starting earlier than usual (it was advertised as "just after dusk"), more than a hundred people gathered in Old Market Square to watch the festive fun which included four ice skating performances and carols.

The more low-key nature of the event didn't seem to bother spectators, who praised the switch-on.

"I know some cities have celebrities or fireworks but that doesn't matter," said Adam Greaves, 30, of Radford, "It's all about the atmosphere and the atmosphere was perfect."

Chole Dixon, 20, of Basford, agreed. She said: "It was absolutely beautiful. It really helps marks the start of Christmas."

Kyaan Johnson, 22, of Basford, added: "I really liked it. I didn't know it was happening but saw people gathering. I'm glad I caught it."

The lights, tree, ice rink and bars will remain outside the council house until New Year's Day.

Councillor Dave Trimble, portfolio holder for leisure and culture, said: “Winter Wonderland is a fantastic attraction with something for visitors of all ages and is regularly ranked amongst the top Christmas markets in the UK, most recently in Vogue magazine.

"We are pleased to welcome so many people to the city centre and to show off the Old Market Square in its best light, giving Nottingham’s retail, leisure and hotel sectors a real boost in the run up to Christmas, right up to New Year’s Eve.”
