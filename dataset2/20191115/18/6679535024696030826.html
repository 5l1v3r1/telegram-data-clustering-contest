‘Sufficient Tamil railway reservation forms supplied to post offices’

15 Nov 2019, 18:20 by Staff Reporter

As allegations of railway reservation forms being issued only in Malayalam and English at a post office in Coimbatore went viral on social media, railway officials clarified on Friday that they have supplied sufficient forms in Tamil and English.

“Post offices in areas with significant Malayalam-speaking populations requested for the forms in Malayalam,” an official from the Salem Division of Southern Railway told The Hindu. Therefore, bilingual forms were one side contains particulars printed in Malayalam and other side in English were supplied to the ticket reservation counters in the post offices concerned.

Isolated incident

The official said that they have not received any complaint on this issue yet and the social media post might have been due to an isolated incident, according to the official.

An official of the Department of Posts said the post office mentioned in the social media had received the forms in Tamil on Friday. The Department gives only space for the reservation counters, the official said.
