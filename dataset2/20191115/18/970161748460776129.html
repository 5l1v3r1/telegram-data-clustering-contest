Former hostages Terry Waite and John McCarthy reunited

15 Nov 2019, 18:01

Former hostages Terry Waite and John McCarthy have been reunited at an event honouring humanitarian work.

The British pair were present when Ingrid Betancourt received the St Martin’s Lifetime Achievement Award in London.

The award commended Ingrid’s humanitarian work after she was released from six-and-a-half years in captivity by the FARC guerrilla army in Colombia.

Terry and John are rarely seen in public together but are both active members and supporters of the charity Hostage International.

The last time they were together was in May when John supported Terry as he received the same achievement award.

Hostage International was co-founded in 2004 by Terry, who saw a gap in provision for families and former hostages.

He said: ‘I often feel that the difficulties that families face are almost equally as difficult as what the hostage himself or herself faces.

‘Hostage International gives much needed emotional and practical support to the families affected and returning hostages.’

In January 1987, Terry travelled to Beirut in Lebanon on behalf of the Archbishop of Canterbury to try to help with the release of Western hostages, including John.

However, he was kidnapped himself by a cell linked to the militant group Hezbollah.

Terry was held for 1,763 days, the majority of which he was deprived of contact with the outside world.

He was released 28 years ago and will mark the anniversary with an event in London’s Trafalgar Square on Monday November 18.

The bells of St-Martin-in-the-Fields will ring out before Terry shares his story to raise funds for Hostage International.

Ingrid was captured in 2002 and held in the Colombian jungle, often chained to trees, until she was rescued more than six years later.

She has praised the work of Hostage International and added: ‘Coming back after captivity is as hard as the captivity.’
