Ford Mustang Mach-E: Everything We Know

It's been leaked early, and you can find all the details here.

15 Nov 2019, 09:25 by Anthony Karr

In the new era of electrified vehicles, it comes as no surprise that almost every manufacturer sells or will very soon sell an all-electric model. Ford is no stranger to this trend and wants to capitalize on the booming crossover and SUV segment, as well as the iconic Mustang moniker, with an all-new, pure electric, high-riding machine. Here’s everything we know about the upcoming Ford Mustang Mach-E electric crossover.

Gallery: Ford Mustang Mach-E Revealed

What is it?

In January 2018, Ford announced it would launch a new electric crossover that will be inspired by the Mustang. It was then revealed it could be called Mach1, but the marque’s fans reacted negatively and forced Ford to rethink its decision. The car's official name, Mustang Mach-E, was recently made official.

Spy photos and renderings have provided a good preview of what to expect, but then a massive leak from November 2019 basically revealed the exterior design, and it's pretty close to the renderings. See the gallery above for reference.

It was also confirmed Lincoln will get its own version of the EV, but it will probably feature a vastly different shape.

Gallery: Mustang-Inspired EV Crossover Spy Shots

What does it look like?

Our exclusive rendering suggested the Mach-E will have a sleek and sporty profile that's heavily inspired by the sports car. However, spy photos of a prototype wearing a production body (above) and the aforementioned leaked images have shown the overall shape will be a bit boxier with a long front overhang and short rear overhang.

As far as we can tell, the Mach-E is going to be roughly the same size as today’s Ford Edge. The single interior image available so far hints at a Tesla-style dashboard with a massive screen on the center console.

What’s under the hood?

An all-electric powertrain! The leaked info conf irms the Mach-E will boast a maximum range of about 300 miles if equipped with the optional extended-range battery and rear-wheel drive. The all-wheel-drive version will take a bit of a hit in the range department, offering around 270 miles of plug-free driving. Other models will be available with even less range, though also lower prices.

In terms of power, outputs of 255 horsepower, 282 hp, and 333 hp are expected depending on the trim level and the powertrain. The most powerful and fastest of all models, the Mach-e GT, will have a 0-60 mph (0-96 kph) time of about four seconds.

When we will see it?

Soon! Ford has announced the Mustang Mach-E will make its official debut on the evening of Sunday, November, 17th, before being put on display at the L.A. Auto Show later that week. 

The entry-level Mach-E will go on sale next year with a starting price of $43,895, while the "First Edition" – a limited- run launch trim – will start at $59,900. The GT top trim level will set you back $60,500, but won't be available initiallye.
