B.C. Transit cancels one trip in Greater Victoria on Friday morning

15 Nov 2019, 09:28 by Cindy E. Harnett

B.C. Transit cancelled just one bus trip due to bus availability on Friday morning.

The following trips will not operate due to bus availability:

  • 16 Uptown/UVic leaving Carey at Ravine at 8:03 a.m.

Another trip on the 15 Esquimalt/UVic route was reinstated after being cancelled.

The transit system was plagued with cancellations in September and October as mechanics struggled to keep Victoria’s aging fleet on the road.

Last week, B.C. Transit borrowed seven buses from Abbotsford and Kelowna in an attempt to prevent trip cancellations on Greater Victoria routes. Four the two-year-old buses came from Abbotsford, three from Kelowna.

B.C. Transit is still delivering more than 99 per cent of its scheduled services every day, which includes 3,200 trips on weekdays, said spokesman Jonathon Dyck.

Last week, B.C. Transit said it was operating with the number of buses it needs. There are 302 buses in the fleet, based in Victoria and Langford.

B.C. Transit’s Victoria operation saw an almost 10 per cent increase in ridership in September, year-over-year. Ridership on the No. 50 bus from the West Shore to downtown Victoria has jumped 44 per cent in five years.

In 2020 there will be 80 new buses, which will reduce the average fleet age from 12 years to six years. A significant percentage of the new buses will operate on compressed natural gas. Ten electric buses are scheduled to arrive in 2021.

The borrowed buses from Abbotsford and Kelowna will be returned when Victoria’s new buses arrive.
