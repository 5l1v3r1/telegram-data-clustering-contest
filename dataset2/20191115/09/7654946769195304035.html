What the Nets are looking for from Iman Shumpert

15 Nov 2019, 09:21 by Brian Lewis

DENVER — The Nets needed an energetic wing defender even before Caris LeVert got hurt. Once LeVert damaged ligaments in his right thumb, they couldn’t afford not to add one.

Enter Iman Shumpert, who was signed Wednesday, suited up Thursday against the Nuggets — but didn’t get into the Nets’ 101-93 loss.

“He fits a need right now. Perimeter defense, that’s always been his calling card,” said coach Kenny Atkinson, who was an assistant with the Knicks during Shumpert’s four-year run at the Garden. “I … know him well, know the personality. He gets after it defensively. That will be his role for us.

“[Garrett Temple] is a heck of a defender, and we can add another guy like Shump who can really get stops on the perimeter. And he’s versatile. He can play multiple positions. … It shores us up a little on the perimeter.”

Shumpert spent his first four NBA seasons with the Knicks, then headed to Cleveland, where he not only won a championship ring, but also played alongside new Nets teammates Kyrie Irving and Joe Harris. That kind of familiarity can only help Shumpert as he tries to settle in.

“Oh, hell yeah. It’s a comfort for me, but it’s also a mutual respect and understanding,” Shumpert said. “I don’t have to reintroduce myself to those guys. I can be myself, do what I normally do and be free out there, especially with KA being the head coach.

“He was my guard coach. When I first got drafted, I worked out with him 30, 45 minutes before practice every day. Being back under his wing, it just feels full circle being back in New York.”

Atkinson expressed hesitancy about throwing Shumpert right into the fire, predictable since he hasn’t played this season.

Shumpert started 40 games for the Kings last season, averaging 8.9 points and 36.6 percent shooting from 3-point range before being dealt to Houston. He didn’t make much of an impact with the Rockets, but he’s perfectly suited for what the Nets need — wing defense off the bench.

“We sat down and talked. Life comes full circle sometimes,” Atkinson said. “He was just a young developing player when I was with him. I enjoyed working with him. I love his personality, and I love his aggressiveness. He’s an aggressive person by nature. “That fits a little bit of a need for us, that perimeter, aggressiveness. He forces turnovers, which is something we’re not great at.… I’m looking forward to working with him.”

LeVert got hurt Sunday, and Shumpert learned of the Nets’ interest the next day. They first spoke Tuesday and he signed Wednesday.

“No matter where I go, I bring an energy and a passion,” Shumpert said. “[They have] a great group of guys, talented guys. Not that I don’t fit in, but I bring something totally different, where it’s not in anybody’s way, it’s not anything but a jell to bring things together.

“It’s an energy, making broken plays happen, create more opportunities for other guys, creating extra possessions and just being able to play ball, man.”
