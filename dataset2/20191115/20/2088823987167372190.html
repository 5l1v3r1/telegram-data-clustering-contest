Caputo, booted from Stone trial, alleges threat from U.S. marshal

15 Nov 2019, 20:24 by https://www.facebook.com/JerryZremskIBuffaloNews/

WASHINGTON – Michael R. Caputo of East Aurora sat for two weeks as his best friend, Roger Stone, went on trial on felony charges of perjury and witness tampering.

But when the federal jury convicted Stone on all counts Friday, Caputo quickly showed his displeasure – and got into a scrape with a U.S. marshal as a result.

After the verdict, Caputo refused to stand in respect for the jury, as is customary. Told by a U.S. marshal that he had to do so, Caputo stood – and turned his back on the jury.

"I decided to do something to show my displeasure without disrupting the proceedings," Caputo said afterward.

A U.S. marshal then ordered Caputo out of the courtroom, handing the Republican political consultant off to another federal agent.

That's when, according to Caputo, things got uncomfortable.
