Birmingham's German Christmas Market named second best in world

This year's top Christmas markets were ranked on a number of factors, including variety, entertainment, local traditions and reader suggestions

15 Nov 2019, 05:30 by James Rodger, https://www.facebook.com/jamesrodgerjournalist

Birmingham's iconic German Christmas Market has been named the second best festive event in the world.

The Second City's legendary Frankfurt-inspired festive extravaganza has been named the second best Christmas market on Earth for 2019.

Big 7 Travel has released its official list of the top 50 finalists today.

With the festive season approaching, travellers across the world are finalising plans for winter breaks.

This year's top Christmas markets were ranked on a number of factors, including but not limited to vendor variety, entertainment, local traditions, reader suggestions.

1.5million social media users had their say, with Birmingham beaten only by Christkindlmarkt, which takes place in Leavenworth, Washington, USA.

The rest of the top ten saw Toronto finish tenth, with Manchester the only other British representative in eighth.

Aachener Weihnachtsmarkt, situated in Aachen, Germany, came ninth, with Christkindlmarket in Chicago seventh, and Santurantikuy Market in Cusco, Peru, sixth.

Fifth place saw Stellenbosch Slow Market in South Africa named, while the Christmas Village at Belvedere Palace in Vienna, Austria, came fourth.

Zagreb's Advent Market in Croatia came third.

 

Widely referred to as the German market, Birmingham's annual extravaganza was first brought here by the organiser of Frankfurt's own festive market in 1997. It had just 10 stalls in its first year.

It was such a success that it became an annual event, returning in 2001 and coming back every year since then. There are now dozens of stalls sprawling through the city centre.

This is now the the largest outdoor Christmas market in the UK, and the biggest German market outside Germany and Austria.

Weekends tend to be the busiest times especially Friday and Saturday evenings and Sunday afternoons - great if you enjoy a bustling atmosphere but it can get very crowded.

Weekdays are less hectic though it can get very busy in the middle of the day (from noon to 2pm when shop and office workers go there in their lunch break).

It experiences a second busy spell after 5pm on weekday evenings, up until about 7.30pm, as people stop for a drink and a bite to eat on their way home from work in the city centre.

Visitors will find a large range of traditional goods and gifts, such as handcrafted decorations, toys, jewellery and more. There's also a selection of tempting food and drink. Pretzels, schnitzels, bratwursts, and roasted almonds can be washed down with glühwein, weissbeer or hot chocolate.

The huge Christmas trees also make an impessive festive backdrop.

The market will run until  December 23, 2019.
