The Buzz: Plenty to do from the Bay to the Bijou this November

15 Nov 2019, 05:43 by Jess Hovingh

Lovers of fast cars, drifting and chasing that adrenalin rush, head to Esperance Speedway Saturday, November 16, for the Esp v Kal Challenge. Check out www.esperancespeedway.com for more info.

The Growers Market takes place once a fortnight on Saturday mornings from 9am to 11am at Nulsen Primary School on November 16 and 30. The Museum Village Markets will be held Sunday, November 24, from 8.30am to 12.30pm. Peruse the crafts and local bits 'n bobs and grab a bite to eat on a beautiful Esperance Sunday morning.

For your artistic children, the Esperance Cannery Arts Centre has some fantastic classes. Coily christmas trees, surf paints, portraits in charcoal & ornaments in clay. Jump on the Cannery website for all workshop information, www.cannerartscentre.com.au/artlab

Balmy Nights on Saturday, November 16, is a treat for the senses and an evening to welcome the coming Summer. A celebration of the creativity and brilliant local businesses on offer, together with the live sounds of five brilliant WA female artists. Featuring Jamilla, Fieldsy, Rachel Vibart, MissGenius and Dr Tasty. Head to the Cannery Arts Centre website for tickets.

Local thespians are putting their talents on display in 'a hilarious show for all the family'. Robin Hood and the Babes in the Woods is a performance of comedic proportions, featuring dance, slapstick comedy, audience participation and over-the-top villains and heroes. For tickets email admin@thebijoutheatre.org.au.

For anyone chasing a bargain or two, come along to the Collectors Fair Sunday, November 24, 9am to 2pm Esperance Mechanical Restoration Group, 138 McLean Road. Featuring rope making, spinning and soap making demonstrations, oil lamps, cross stitch, jewellery and more, there will be something for the collector in all of us! Entry is a gold coin donation, for more visit www.emrgesperance.com/ .

Coming soon is the Shire of Esperance's Edge of the Bay Festival, kicking off with the huge BMX, Scooter and Skate competition. Registrations from 10am with heats and finals throughout the day. Come on down and show off your dardy skills! Saturday, November 30, from 10am to 6pm.
