Burton man was caught with crack cocaine and heroin in the street

He also obstructed a police officer

15 Nov 2019, 19:00 by Beth Pridding

A Burton man has admitted possessing crack cocaine and heroin with intent to supply after being caught by police in the town.

David Bowes, 23, of Blakeholme Court, was arrested in Dallow Street, in the town, on Tuesday, November 12.

He was later charged with possession of heroin and crack cocaine with intent to supply, possession of cannabis and wilfully obstructing a police constable.

When he appeared at North Staffordshire Justice Centre, in Newcastle-under-Lyme, yesterday, Thursday, November 14, Bowes admitted all the charges against him.

A second man, aged 42, from Burton, was also arrested at the time on suspicion of possession of heroin and crack cocaine. He was released under investigation while police carry out further enquiries.

Bowes will next appear at Stafford Crown Court for sentencing. The date of this hearing has not yet been set.

He has been given unconditional bail until then.

Another man Bilal Shafiq, 18, of Uxbridge Street, Burton, also appeared before magistrates on Thursday.

He has been charged with possession of crack cocaine with intent to supply after he was arrested after a vehicle stop in Blakeholme Court in Burton, on November 12. He has not entered a plea to the charge.

He will appear at Stafford Crown Court on December 16 and has been granted conditional bail.
