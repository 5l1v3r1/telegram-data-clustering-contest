Congress demands FIR in Rafale case

SC clearly states that the case be probed by CBI: Prithviraj Chavan

15 Nov 2019, 19:57 by Alok Deshpande

Former Maharashtra Chief Minister and senior Congress leader Prithviraj Chavan on Friday demanded that the Central Bureau of Investigation (CBI) file an FIR in the Rafale case and claimed that the Supreme Court had not given a clean chit to the Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP)-led central government.

Addressing a press conference at the party office, Mr. Chavan said the Congress had demanded a probe by a Joint Parliamentary Committee into the alleged scam and sticks to it even today.

Mr. Chavan said, “The BJP rejoiced over the Supreme Court’s decision to dismiss review petitions without reading the full judgement. In fact, instead of giving a clean chit to the Modi government, the court has asked for filing of FIR and probe through the CBI, and said that petitioners Arun Shourie, Prashant Bhushan and Yashwant Sinha be apprised of the investigation.”

The judgement clearly states that the Rafale case be probed by the CBI, the Congress leader said.

If there is no irregularity in the deal, the Narendra Modi government should grant permission under Section 17-A of the Prevention of Corruption Act to conduct a probe against the Prime Minister, Mr. Chavan said. As per section 17-A, Centre’s permission is required to file an FIR against prime minister.
