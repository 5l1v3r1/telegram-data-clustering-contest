At IIIT-D exhibition, one-touch solutions for everyday concerns

15 Nov 2019, 19:26 by Express News Service

One-touch solutions to get rid of household scrap, updates on Metro train delays and checking indoor air quality — these are among the apps developed by students of Indraprastha Institute of Information Technology Delhi (IIIT-D) and on display at ‘Building Better Interfaces’ exhibition on the campus Friday.

Among the apps featured at the one-day exhibition was ‘Swapeco’, which promises to send a scrap collector to the doorstep with just a click. “There are issues with kabadiwallas. You may not find one according to your time, or sometime they use faulty weighing scales…Once a person downloads the app and schedules a pick-up time, either we go personally, or identify kabadiwallas and get them to work with us,” said Mukul Chhabra, a student of Electronics & Communication Engineering, one of the developers of the app.

The app has been functional since February, and the service is currently functional only in South Delhi.

Another app, DMRC Connect, provides information of delays in Metro services and acts as a platform for commuters to submit their grievances. However, the app has not been rolled out.

Abhijeet Mishra of Computer Science and Designing has made PAQsys, a device which monitors indoor air pollution: “The device shows the amount of volatile organic compounds…users can get all details on the app.”
