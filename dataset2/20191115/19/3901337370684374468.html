Messi Closes In On Incredible Pele Record

15 Nov 2019, 19:57 by David Kappel

Lionel Messi is steadily closing in one of the most impressive milestones in the game set by Brazil legend Pele.

The FC Barcelona captain once again stole the headlines last weekend, when he netted a hat-trick against Celta Vigo.

He scored from the penalty spot before adding two more from direct free-kicks to hand his side an important three points in the LaLiga title race.

The treble took his tally to nine strikes in the 2019/20 campaign, and also saw the Argentine also close the gap on Selecao legend Pele.

The former Santos striker still holds the record for the most official goals for a single club, having netted 643 times for the Brazilian club.

Messi, though, is closing in on Pele after netting goals number 610, 611 and 612 for Barcelona against Celta. As a consequence, he is now just 31 strikes shy of the all-time record.

The 32-year-old has scored 40 or more goals in his past 11 successive seasons, and if he manages to reach that figure in 2019/20 again, he will at least match Pele's incredible record.
