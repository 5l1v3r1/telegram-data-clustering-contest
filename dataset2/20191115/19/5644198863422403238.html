University tells non-students to go now as Hong Kong campus showdowns loom

15 Nov 2019, 19:41

HONG KONG: The president of Hong Kong's Chinese University, which anti-government protesters have turned into a fortress stockpiled with petrol bombs and bows and arrows, threatened on Friday (Nov 15) to call in "assistance" unless all non-students leave.

The hilly campus was the scene of violent clashes this week, with protesters hurling petrol bombs at police and on to a highway linking the largely rural New Territories with the Kowloon peninsula to the south and Hong Kong island beyond.
