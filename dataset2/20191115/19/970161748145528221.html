Cristiano Ronaldo approves Juventus' transfer move for Paul Pogba

15 Nov 2019, 19:11

Cristiano Ronaldo has given his stamp of approval to Juventus’ move for Paul Pogba, according to reports.

Pogba has been heavily linked with a move back to Juventus and is reportedly keen to leave Manchester United next summer.

The midfielder failed in a bid to force his way out of United during the summer amid interest from Juventus and Spanish giants Real Madrid.

El Desmarque claim Juventus are exploring the possibility of re-signing Pogba, who left the club for United in an £89million move in 2016.

Pogba has struggled to live up to that huge price tag at United and said during the summer he wanted a new challenge.

Juventus have checked in with their star man Ronaldo about moving for Pogba and the Portugal international is happy for the club to sign the Frenchman.

Pogba is currently sidelined through injury and has played just five times for United this season due to ongoing ankle problems.

Juventus will surely face competition from Real if they firm up their interest in Pogba.

Zinedine Zidane is a big fan of Pogba and is the driving force behind Real’s interest in him.

United were demanding £179million for Pogba during the summer and intend to stick to that asking price if clubs come calling again in January.
