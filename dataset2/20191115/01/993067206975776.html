Anti-Claire Hanna banner put up in south Belfast

15 Nov 2019, 01:00 by CLAIRE SIMPSON

A BANNER asking if Shankill bomber Sean Kelly will be campaigning for SDLP Westminster candidate Claire Hanna has been put up in south Belfast.

The SDLP has already said that Kelly will not form part of any of its canvassing teams.

The banner was put up at the junction of Oak Street and Donegall Pass - a traditionally loyalist area in the south of the city.

Ms Hanna is the only nationalist candidate in the South Belfast constituency.

Sinn Féin said it would not stand in the area to maximise the chances of remain candidate Ms Hanna.

Kelly, who planted an IRA bomb on the Shankill in 1993 that killed nine people, is an election worker for Sinn Féin in north Belfast.

The DUP's Sir Jeffrey Donaldson has previously claimed that a vote for the SDLP was akin to a vote for Sinn Féin in the December 12 general election.

"Sean Kelly has been canvassing for Sinn Fein in North Belfast, but will he now be canvassing on behalf of the SDLP too?" he said.

"I accept everyone can have a past and can change, but Sean Kelly has shown no remorse for murdering nine innocent people, including two children."

An SDLP spokesman branded Sir Jeffrey's comments as "fake news".
