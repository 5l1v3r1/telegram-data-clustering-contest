More needs to be done to sustain GP service

15 Nov 2019, 01:00

The latest survey in relation to family doctors in Northern Ireland will add to the sense that the under-pressure GP service is facing an even more difficult future.

A report by the Royal College of General Practitioners in Northern Ireland (RCGPNI) has found that a quarter of GPs surveyed believe they will not be working in general practice in the north in five years' time.

The most common reasons given were retirement and stress. A third of GPs felt so stressed at least once a week that they could not cope.

There is no doubt that surgeries, the first point of contact for most patients in the health service, are under enormous strain.

The average number of registered patients per practice has increased by 11 per cent since 2014 while the demographic is of an increasingly elderly population with a rising need for medical services.

Where once it was possible to get a GP appointment within a couple of days, now patients are resigned to waiting weeks to see their doctor.

This is having a knock-on effect with people going to emergency departments with ailments that could be dealt with at the primary care stage, intensifying the problems being felt at hospitals.

Clearly, there are major issues with regard to workload but the medical profession is also facing acute problems in attracting and retaining GPs.

It is not unusual to hear of long established, mainly rural, practices shutting their doors for good when a local GP retires but unfortunately there is a shortage of young doctors wanting to work in this area of medicine.

The Department of Health has increased the number of GP training places but this year not all of those places have been filled, which is hugely disappointing.

The authorities need to understand why doctors are reluctant to train as a GP or run a practice.

According to this survey, stress and workload are key concerns but the rising cost of securing medical indemnity in Northern Ireland is seen as a significant financial burden.

Quite simply, the health service in Northern Ireland cannot afford to lose more doctors from general practice than are being trained.

If anything we need to be doing more to hold on to experienced medics and ensuring sufficient numbers are coming through to sustain this vital element of the healthcare system.
