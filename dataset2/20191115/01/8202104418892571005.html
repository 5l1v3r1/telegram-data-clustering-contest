A man who allegedly defrauded 24 victims of more than $2 million has been arrested following a joint investigation by the Saskatoon police and Saskatoon RCMP.

Sixty-nine-year-old Vincent John Mullee was arrested Wednesday and faces charges of criminal breach of trust, fraud over $5,000 and laundering proceeds of crime.

The investigation began when the Financial and Consumer Affairs Authority began investigating Mullee and his business as a independent financial advisor in June 2018. A subsequent investigation by detectives in the SPS economic crime section and Saskatoon RCMP’s provincial general investigation section.

The investigation uncovered 24 victims of the alleged fraud — 13 of whom live within Saskatoon, with the remaining 11 living in areas within Saskatchewan and British Columbia.

Mullee made his first court appearance on Thursday morning and is scheduled to make a second appearance on Nov. 28.
