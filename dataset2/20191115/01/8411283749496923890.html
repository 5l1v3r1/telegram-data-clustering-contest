China sales of new energy cars down in October

15 Nov 2019, 01:18 by Adam Hwang, DIGITIMES, Taipei

China-based makers produced 95,000 new energy vehicles and sold 75,000 units in October 2019, decreasing 35.4% and 45.6% respectively on year, according to China Association of Automobile Manufacturers (CAAM).

The output consisted of 78,000 electric vehicles (EVs) and 16,000 PHEVs (plug-in hybrid electric vehicles), slipping 33.3% and 44.3% respectively on year, CAAM said. The sales consisted of 59,000 EVs and 16,000 PHEVs, respectively dropping 47.3% and 38.7% on year.

During January-October, 983,000 new energy vehicles were produced and 947,000 units were sold, respectively increasing 11.7% and 10.1% on year, CAAM indicated. The former consisted of 795,000 EVs and 186,000 PHEVs, respectively growing 18.4% and falling 10.6% on year, and 1,391 fuel cell vehicles,while the latter consisted of 750,000 EVs and 196,000 PHEVs, respectively rising 15.0% and dipping 5.7% on year and 1,327 fuel cell vehicles.
