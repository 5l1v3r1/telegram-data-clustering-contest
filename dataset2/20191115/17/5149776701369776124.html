Bingham residents pay tribute to 39-year-old mum after double death at house

Police still at the scene where an incident occurred on Wednesday afternoon

15 Nov 2019, 17:35 by Matt Jarram

Heartbroken staff from a Bingham care home have paid tribute to a 39-year-old woman who died following an incident at her home address.

Police are still guarding the property in Porchester Road as they establish what happened after a 57-year-old man and 39-year-old woman were found dead on Wednesday, November 13.

Dozens of flowers have been passed to police officers to place outside the door of her home, with friends and employees in tears as they approach the driveway.

The identity of the male and female have not yet been disclosed by the police nor has the circumstances surrounding their deaths.

But colleagues of the 39-year-old mum said she worked as a senior carer at Beauvale Care Home in Moor Lane in Bingham.

Close friend and carer at Beauvale, Cheryl Terry, said she had worked there for 12 years and was loved by both patients and staff.

She said: "We had lots of laughs. She was a very inspiring lady.

"It is a horrible nightmare that she has gone. My memory of her was moving her into this house. She had lived in Bingham before, but in a little flat."

Tanya Foster, 24, who works in the kitchens at Beauvale, was also horrified by the news of her death.

She said: "She was easy to get along with. She had goals. She had not long passed her driving test in October. She just wanted to achieve.

"She was lovely. I have only been at the care home a month or two and she used to say 'hello' to me every day and ask me how I was.

"Everyone enjoyed her, she was a hard worker, and always busy.

"She will be greatly missed. It is just a shock to everyone. We all wish it was not true."

Kim Smith, 29, a carer at the home, added: "She was loving and caring. We were like a family."

Officers were called to the address just before 3.40pm on Wednesday, November 13.

Nottinghamshire Police said the man and woman are believed to be known to one another, and were both pronounced dead at the scene.

Post-mortem examinations are taking place to establish the exact cause of death.

Detective Chief Inspector Rob Routledge, who is leading the investigation, said: "Following our initial investigations we now believe this is an isolated incident and at this time we are not looking for anyone else in connection with the deaths.

"Our investigation will continue to establish the full circumstances surrounding the deaths.

"Our thoughts at this time are very much with the loved ones of those who have died and we are liaising with family to offer them support.

"Formal identification has not yet taken place. A cordon remains in place at the address while we continue with our enquiries and we will continue to have a police presence in the local area.

"We are aware that Bingham is a small community and this incident will have an impact on its residents. We would advise anyone who wishes to raise any issues to please speak to one of our officers in the area."
