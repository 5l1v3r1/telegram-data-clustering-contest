AI computing solutions firm expands city facility

15 Nov 2019, 17:08 by Special Correspondent

Artificial Intelligence (AI) computing solutions firm, Blaize, formerly known as Thinci, has expanded the capacity of its Hyderabad facility and proposes to fill up all the 450 seats there within 18 months.

The US firm has a headcount of over 325 across locations globally, CEO Dinakar C.Munagala said, adding of that 250 are in Hyderabad, a number expected to touch 450 over the next 12-18 months.

He was speaking to the media here on Friday to announce that Blaize has emerged from the stealth mode and unveiled a next-generation computing architecture to meet the demands and complexity of new computational workloads found in AI applications.

To queries, he said the Hyderabad facility, set up about six years ago, is important and the work undertaken include core processer design and software driver development. He said chip design and architecture, front end design as well as board level design capabilities were some of the focus areas.

On the product line-up, he said a second product is being built and would be announced in the first quarter of 2020. The company is laying a strong roadmap for the chips it is building and targets at the three focus segments of automotive, smart vision and data centres.
