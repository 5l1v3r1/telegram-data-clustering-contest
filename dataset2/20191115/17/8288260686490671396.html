Call for more safety measures at deadly Pelsall junction

Lee Marlow was killed at the junction of Hall Lane and Vicarage Road in 2016. Lee's nephew has kicked off a petition for better safety measures to be installed

15 Nov 2019, 17:07 by Charlotte Regen, https://www.birminghammail.co.uk/authors/charlotte-regen/

A family left heartbroken when a dad was killed by a delivery driver have called for road safety measures at the deadly junction.

Father-of-three, Lee Marlow, was travelling on his motorbike when Miles Byrne ploughed into him at the junction of Hall Lane and Vicarage Road in the Pelsall area of Walsall.

Lee suffered catastrophic head and chest injuries and was pronounced dead at the scene on September, 17, 2016.

Byrne, 49, of Tamworth, received a three-and-a-half year jail sentence and was banned from driving for three years following the crash.

Now, Lee's devastated nephew, Jack Marlow, has kicked off a petition, calling for better safety measures to be installed at the junction - you can sign it here.

Speaking to Black Country Live, he said: "I just want the time on the traffic lights to be delayed as they change too quickly and don't allow enough time for cars to get over.

"There have been too many accidents. I have spoken to the council and I'm not happy about the outcome. If the traffic lights had a longer delay there would be enough time for cars to get over. It would decrease the amount of accidents."

Black Country Live has approached Walsall Council press office for a comment.
