PRISON DEATH

Steven Donaldson killer found dead in Perth prison as co-accused have appeals to reduce their sentences rejected

15 Nov 2019, 17:27 by Aidan Smith

THE killer of Steven Donaldson has been found dead in HMP Perth - as his co-accused saw appeals to reduce their sentences rejected.

Steven Dickie is believed to have taken his own life and was discovered by prison officers overnight.

A SPS spokesperson said: "Steven Alexander Dickie, 24, a prisoner at HMP Perth has died.

"He was convicted at Edinburgh Court on 30 May 2019.

"Police Scotland have been advised and the matter reported to the Procurator Fiscal.

"A Fatal Accident Inquiry will be held in due course."

Dickie's death came as Tamsin Glass and Callum Davidson appealed their sentences for culpable homicide and murder.

Both had their appeals rejected.

Donaldson's sister Lori said: "We're glad the appeal was rejected. We were in agreement with the sentence from the beginning.

"It was horrible seeing them again, it was horrible having to go through it again.

"We are just glad it is done and we got the outcome that we wanted."

Earlier this year Dickie was given a life sentence for murdering oil worker Mr Donaldson.

Donaldson's body was discovered in the car park at Kinnordy Nature Reserve near Kirriemuir, Angus, in the early hours of June 7 last year.

Detectives said the 27-year-old from Arbroath had been the victim of a "sustained and brutal attack".

Dickie and Davidson were convicted in May of murdering the offshore worker following a trial at the High Court in Edinburgh.

The pair, both 24, were jailed for life when they returned to the court.

Glass, 20, who was an ex-girlfriend of Donaldson was told she would be caged for 10 years after being convicted of culpable homicide.

The trio planned to launch an attack at the quiet park before driving Steven to the Kinnordy Nature Reserve where they killed him.

Graphic evidence from a forensic pathologist detailed the extent of the horror injuries inflicted on Donaldson - which included severing his spinal cord in two places.

Prosecutors believe the pair used an axe or machete type weapon to inflict the killer blows but these weapons were never recovered.

After killing him the pair then set fire to his car in an effort to hide any evidence.

The jury spent more than nine hours in their deliberations.

Eventually they found both Davidson and Dickie guilty of murder.

Meanwhile, Glass was found guilty of culpable homicide.
