Man, 22, arrested over stabbing outside chip shop in Castle Bromwich

A man in his 30s was left with an arm injury while the knife suspect has been bailed pending further enquiries

15 Nov 2019, 17:57 by Stephanie Balloo, https://www.facebook.com/MissBalloo/

A young man was arrested three days on from a stabbing outside a busy row of shops, police have now revealed.

A 22-year-old man has been arrested on suspicion of assault after a knife attack outside Gregory's Fish Bar, Castle Bromwich on Monday, November 11.

The victim, a man in his 30s, was stabbed on Green Lane near to the junction of Windleaves Road at around 7.40pm.

He suffered an arm injury and was taken to hospital by ambulance. Fortunately his injury was not thought to be life-threatening.

The arrest came on Thursday, November 14 and has since seen a man bailed pending investigation.

A spokesman for West Midlands Police said: "A 22-year-old man was yesterday (14 Nov) arrested on suspicion of assault and has been bailed pending further enquiries."

A previous statement in the wake of the attack, read: "Police were called to reports of a stabbing in Green Lane, Castle Bromwich, at around 7.40pm last night (November 11).

"A man in his 30s has been taken to hospital with an arm injury which is not thought to be life-threatening.

"Investigations are at the early stages and anyone with information can contact us quoting crime reference number 20SH/267117D/19, via Live Chat at west-midlands.police.uk between 8am and midnight, call 101 anytime or contact Crimestoppers anonymously on 0800 555 111."
