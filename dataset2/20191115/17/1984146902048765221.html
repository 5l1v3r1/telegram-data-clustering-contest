DeAndre Yedlin: Why Newcastle United dressing room have 'bought into' Steve Bruce and his tactics

The American full-back is enjoying life at the moment after scoring his first ever goal at St James' Park on the back of tip from Bruce

15 Nov 2019, 17:00 by Lee Ryder

DeAndre Yedlin has spoken about life under Steve Bruce at Newcastle United.

The USA international has had to fight his way back into the team following a summer operation on his groin and the arrival of Sweden international Emil Krafth.

Bruce has challenged Yedlin to offer more going forward from right wing-back and the former Spurs defender responded with a diving header to haul his team back into the game in the 2-1 win over AFC Bournemouth last weekend.

When asked about how he is enjoying life under a different boss, Yedlin told the Chronicle: "We all bought into his game plan and all bought into his tactics.

"I think it shows.

"When we go a goal down everybody is fighting on the pitch.

"That is the main thing with his teams, it's to fight.

"I think it has been a recurring thing over the years too, the guys on this team are fighters.

"It's great for the club."

Bruce has altered the United tactics in recent weeks and wants his wing-backs to be more of a threat as he looks to move away from the ultra defensive unit that kept Newcastle in the Premier League in recent years.

He said: "In recent weeks Steve told me and Jetro Willems to get in the box a bit more to add another guy in the box.

"Looking at it from a wing-back point of view when an extra body is coming forward it is tough to defend.

"We have been trying to do that.

"It has worked out a bit.

"We're coming into the front and back post and hoping for something.
