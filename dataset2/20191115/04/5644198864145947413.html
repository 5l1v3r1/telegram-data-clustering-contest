Italy declares state of emergency in Venice after flood

15 Nov 2019, 04:59

VENICE: Italy on Thursday (Nov 14) declared a state of emergency for Venice after an exceptional tide surged through churches, shops and homes, causing millions of euros worth of damage to the UNESCO city.

Prime Minister Giuseppe Conte said the Cabinet had approved the state of emergency and ordered the immediate release of €20 million (US$22 million) in funds for "the most urgent interventions" in the devastated city after Tuesday's flooding.
