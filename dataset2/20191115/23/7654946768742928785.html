Darren Sproles done for season — and possibly career — with quad injury

15 Nov 2019, 23:25 by Jake Nisse

This might be it for for Darren Sproles.

After struggling with injuries throughout the last two seasons, the 36-year-old running back will again be sidelined after suffering a torn right hip flexor muscle in practice this week.

He will now miss the rest of the season, after he previously suffered a partial tear of the same muscle against the Jets in Week 5, according to a team statement.

Sproles’ latest injury means he will finish this season with just 17 games (15 regular season, two postseason) played since 2017, spelling a potential end to an underrated career.

A three-time Pro Bowl selection on special teams, Sproles currently ranks fifth all-time in all-purpose yards, largely in part to his prowess as a kick and punt returner. His tally of 19,696 yards ranks behind only Emmitt Smith, Walter Payton, Brian Mitchell and Jerry Rice.

Despite his diminutive 5’6″ frame, Sproles has proven to be a durable player for much of his career, outlasting return specialist contemporaries such as Josh Cribbs and Devin Hester.

Now, he may choose to hang it up.

The running back’s injury means that the Eagles are turning to another familiar face for relief, as they re-signed Jay Ajayi to a deal on Friday.

Ajayi hasn’t played an NFL game since tearing his ACL last October while playing for the Eagles.

Both Ajayi and Sproles won the Super Bowl with Philadelphia in 2018, but Sproles played just three games that season after suffering an early season injury.

The Eagles host the Patriots on Sunday.
