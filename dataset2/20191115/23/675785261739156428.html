Dad stunned after opening Twirl chocolate bar to find three fingers inside

A 27-year-old man from Swindon in Wiltshire was shocked when he opened up a Orange Twirl only to discover that instead of two chocolate fingers inside, there were three

15 Nov 2019, 23:43 by Elly Roberts, Jonathan Coles

A dad was stunned after he bought a 'Twirl' chocolate bar which had three fingers.

Samuel May bought the Orange Twirl for 49p expecting there to be two bars inside.

When he unwrapped the bar - which did appear slightly larger than usual from the outside - he found it had a staggering three fingers inside.

The miraculous '50% more chocolate than expected' moment came at the end of a lengthy hunt for a particular bar by Sam.

The man, from Swindon, Wiltshire, said: "I’d been looking for these Orange Twirls for ages. Me and my partner absolutely love chocolate but we couldn’t find them anywhere.

 

"They were by the tills when I got there. So I was quite excited to have found them and I picked up a couple for me and my partner.

"I was looking at them and thought ‘that one is a bit bigger than the other’.

"I was just holding it and could feel there were in fact three fingers in it. One on top of the other two. It’s mad isn’t it?

"I feel like I just can’t believe it. I can’t find these bars for ages and then pick first one up and it’s got three fingers in.''

 

The 27-year-old is not sure what he’ll do with the third stick of chocolate, but is currently considering sharing it with his youngest daughter, Nalah.

He said: "I’ve got an eight-month-old who likes sucking on chocolate at the moment so I may give it to her to enjoy. It’s just one of those funny things.

"It would be nice if it was the only one in the world. Hopefully there’s more good luck to come, now is a good time to buy a lottery ticket.''

A spokeswoman for Cadbury said: “The reaction to the launch of Cadbury Twirl Orange has been incredible and the product is in very high demand across the country.

"This guy has hit the jackpot by finding three fingers in a rare limited-edition Orange Twirl.

"We can’t promise there will be any more to come.”
