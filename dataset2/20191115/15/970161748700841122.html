15 Nov 2019, 15:41

Billy Connolly has stopped dipping his willy into G&Ts, so his next round at the pub is safe.

The comedian revealed in his book, Tall Tales and Wee Stories: The Best of Billy Connolly, that he was once thrown out of a theatre in Scotland for putting his penis into his drink.

As you do.

‘I was banned from the Traverse Theatre in Edinburgh for having my willy in a gin and tonic,’ he wrote.

‘That’s taking it, I confess, that’s taking it a bit far.

‘Actually I was trying not to be thrown out: “That’s right – throw me out! You didn’t know I was disabled – that’s how I drink!”’

But the 76-year-old is adamant that he has put a stop to his antics.

In a chat with The Scotsman, he said: ‘That’s true. I was banned. Then they had an exhibition of photographs and invited me in to see it and I said “but I’m banned”, so they lifted the ban for the night.

‘Then they lifted it completely. So I’ve been back.’

And he has absolutely not repeated the offence, explaining: ‘No I don’t stick my willy in a gin and tonic anymore.

‘I gave up drinking. I still have the willy.’

Good to know.

In fact, Billy gave up drinking ‘years ago’, crediting wife Pam for ‘saving’ him.

‘I loved the whole pub thing, getting p**sed with the boys, telling stories into the night…,’ he added to the publication. ‘And it was getting control, changing my personality.

‘I became this other man. Pamela would say “bogey man has arrived”. And when it’s changing your personality you have to have a close look at it and do something about it. There’s nothing like the truth to put you right.’
