How to check if your home insurance will cover damage from flooding

You may be wondering whether your home has the correct insurance in place

15 Nov 2019, 15:41 by Hayley Watson

Derbyshire has been battered by flooding this week following heavy rainfall.

While the rain has just about stopped for now, residents have been urged to stay vigilant as flood warnings and alerts remain in place across the region.

Several roads across the county are closed and Cross Country trains have been hit by cancellations, causing disruption to travel .

A number of schools have also been closed due to the extent of the flooding.

The Environment Agency says it is working with emergency responders and local authorities to protect people and properties in Derbyshire and other parts of the country.

If your home has been affected by flooding, you may be wondering whether your home has the correct insurance in place to make a claim.

Here is what the government's Money Advice Service says you should do in order to check if your home is covered.

Check all the details

To check whether you are covered in the event of a flood, the service says: "Check all the details in your buildings and contents insurance policies that relate to flooding and make sure you have enough cover to protect you in the event of a flood.

"Even if your insurance isn’t up for renewal, it’s worth checking."

Call the insurance company

Next, homeowners will want to call their insurance company to inform them.

It continues: "Call your insurance company to query anything you’re not sure about.

"New laws mean that it’s up to insurance companies to ask the right questions about what they want to know.

"But if your property has a history of flooding, you should inform your insurance company – even if they don’t ask.

"The last thing you need is a hitch if you ever need to make a claim."
