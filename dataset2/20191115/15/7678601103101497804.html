Joyland Festival to return in December

15 Nov 2019, 15:11 by Gisela Swaragita

After it was last held in 2013, Joyland Festival is set to return in December, bringing with it a long-awaited international line-up such as United States musician Frankie Cosmos, US chill-wave pioneer Washed Out, and Canadian indie rock unit TOPS.

The festival will be held on Dec. 7-8 at Panahan Field, Gelora Bung Karno sports complex, Central Jakarta.

Aside from international headliners, Indonesian talent will also join the event as performers and curators.

“This year we’ve asked Efek Rumah Kaca, Soleh Solihun and Anggun Priambodo to curate the music, stand-up comedy and film,” Joyland Festival program director Ferry Dermawan said in a statement made available for The Jakarta Post.

Efek Rumah Kaca has chosen several musical acts to play at one of the festival's areas named Lilypad, Ferry said. Among the acts are Jirapah, Puti Chitara, Mad Mad Men and Sir Dandy -- all have performed at Kios Ojo Keos, a mini establishment managed by Efek Rumah Kaca. 

Meanwhile, Soleh Solihun has invited comedians such as Yusril Ihza Fahriza, Oki Rengga, Ali Akbar, Awwe, Aci Resti, Kiky Saputri, Eka Kuota and Nury Zhafira to put on a comic show at a corner named Shrooms Garden.

Meanwhile, artist and filmmaker Anggun Priambodo has collected fiction, documentary, experimental and animation films produced by Indonesian filmmakers and artists for Cinerillaz, an area that aims to show a number of short but intimate films.

Festival goers can expect to visit and take a break at the venue's corner in between music shows.

A complete line up of Joyland Festival 2019 is available on its social media accounts.

Besides Frankie Cosmos, Washed Out and TOPS, the festival will also feature Yves Tumor from the US, Hatchie (Australia) and Anna of the North (Norway) as performers. Meanwhile among the local line up are Hindia, The Adams, Barasuara, Frau and The Trees and The Wild.

Music enthusiasts can purchase tickets at Kiostix.com and Kios Ojo Keos with prices starting from Rp 200,000 (US$14.20) for an early entry daily pass, to Rp 2.5 million for a group-of-five ticket. (kes)
