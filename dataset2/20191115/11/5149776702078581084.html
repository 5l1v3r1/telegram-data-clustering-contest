New video of City Ground redevelopment - and Nottingham Forest fans are going to love it

Club provides an exciting glimpse of what to expect when the project is completed

15 Nov 2019, 11:44 by Sarah Clapson

Plans for Nottingham Forest’s redevelopment of their City Ground home continue to gather pace.

And an exciting new video from the Reds provides supporters with a glimpse of what to expect once the project is completed.

The Peter Taylor Stand is set to be rebuilt to cater for an increased capacity of about 10,000 fans, and will also include new state of the art dressing rooms, a museum, club shop, executive boxes and media suites.

Improvements will also be made to the other three sides of the ground, with the Bridgford Stand to be extended too.

The overhaul will see enhanced seating positions, upgraded concourse areas and catering options, improved accessible facilities and new conference and events spaces, with the club also keen to promote greater community usage.

A planning application is expected to be submitted before the end of the year and work is due to get underway in 2020, with a 15-month completion target.
