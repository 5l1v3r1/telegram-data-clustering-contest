Hucknall dad's anger as garden floods for second time in two years

The dad said his two sons were left upset by the garden flooding, where they go and play football

15 Nov 2019, 11:59 by Ben Reid

A Hucknall dad has spoken of his anger after his garden was completely flooded for the second time in two years.

Heavy rainfall battered Nottinghamshire on Thursday, November 14, where a yellow weather warning was in place for 13 hours.

Floods wreaked havoc across the county during the day and dad of two David Bull was left furious that his garden in Johnson Avenue was covered with water.

The 41-year-old said his two sons were left upset by the garden flooding, where they go and play football.

"It's happened to me twice now. It's major flooding," he told Nottinghamshire Live.

"The council dug a big hole in Tichfield Park for extra water to run off into. But it hasn't helped my garden at all. Nowhere else floods that I know of around here.

"It is worrying to see. My two lads have been left really upset and it keeps happening. Every time it rains I worry.

"My garden is just a floodplain.

"It just came in so fast. I just feel a bit forgotten about by the council."

Councillor for Hucknall North, John Wilmott, of the Ashfield Independents, told Nottinghamshire Live Ashfield District Council was preparing for a specialist to go out and help Mr Bull.

He added: "Flooding is never a good thing, wherever it happens, and I can assure Mr Bull we will be helping him where we can.

"My thoughts go out to any families affected by flooding."

An Ashfield District Council spokesman said: "Our Environment Team is aware of this isolated issue involving a property adjoining Titchfield Park in Hucknall.

"We are in contact with the affected resident and we are working with specialists to better understand why this single property is affected by excess water."
