Tyson Fury outlines three-fight plan ahead of Deontay Wilder rematch

Fury is due to take on Wilder on February 22 but the world champion must first beat Luis Ortiz next weekend

15 Nov 2019, 11:41 by Martin Domin, https://www.facebook.com/mirrorfighting

Tyson Fury will target the winner of Anthony Joshua's rematch with Andy Ruiz Jr if Deontay Wilder loses to Luis Ortiz.

Fury has agreed a deal to face heavyweight world champion Wilder in a rematch in February 22.

But the American must first overcome Ortiz next weekend in a repeat of their thrilling clash last year.

And should Ortiz upset the odds, Fury will look for an alternative opponent.

"When one door closes another one opens. So if he loses, he loses. He’ll have a rematch with Ortiz no doubt. And I’ll look for the winner of Andy Ruiz Jr vs Anthony Joshua on the same date," he told talkSPORT.

If Fury does fight Wilder - and wins - he may have to agree to a trilogy clash before targeting a unification bout.

"I believe 100 per cent yes. It has got to happen. I have three fights left on my contract," he added. "Deontay Wilder is next and if Wilder wants it again, he can. Then I will fight the winner of Joshua and Ruiz Jr for sure."

Fury released his autobiography this week to much acclaim and detailed his struggles with depression.

Fury explained how he had gone to the pub dressed in a skin-tight skeleton costume at the height of his drinking and mental health issues and decided to return home early.

"At that point I knew I couldn't do it on my own," he said. "I got on my knees and, for the first time in my life, I knew I couldn't stop going to the pub (and stop) eating on my own.

"I was crying. When I arose, I was bouncing with excitement. I had faith that everything was going to be alright."

Fury spoke of the importance of his religious beliefs and admitted to a possible psychosis diagnosis by his psychiatrist, who claimed in the book that "without faith, he would have killed himself".

Fury added: "At one point I believed I was being tortured by demons. When you're really mentally unwell, you do see and hear things."

Fury also opened up about some of the abuse and mistreatment he has faced as a member of the travelling community.

He said: "I used to get called disgusting names. I was bullied quite a bit as a kid until I got big and started fighting back.

"I have been refused entry to pubs and restaurants because I'm a traveller."
