A temporary shelter housing the victims of Cyclone Idai was blown away, According to reports by ZBC News Online. Heavy rains hit Chimanimani last night, blowing and washing away holding tents which housed Cyclone Idai survivors. Classroom blocks were also destroyed while roofs were blown away as shown in the below pictures.

Earlier this year, Chimanimani was hard hit by Cyclone Idai sadly resulting in hundreds losing their lives and a huge number displaced from their homes. The victims have been living in make-shift shelters ever since.

It never rains but pours (literally) for these people as mother nature seems to be coming back for them, barely a year after Cyclone Idai left them homeless and impoverished.

An armed gang of thieves was caught red-handed on camera looting electronics in a GTel shop in Mutare. The Zimbabwean mobile phone company which is close to Mutare Central Police Station lost high-end cellphones valued at more than $70 000 following the robbery.

In the video footage captured on the CCTV system, which was availed to the Manicapost, the gang was caught in action as they went about their dastardly business. The break-in is reported to have been done in a flash, just under a minute and 28 seconds. The suspects, who were driving a silver Toyota Runx, pounced at the shop at exactly 12.55 am and threatened the two security guards who were manning the premises with death. Consequently, the guards fled the scene. The robbers gained entry after they smashed the entrance door with an iron bar.
