India vs Bangladesh: Mayank Agarwal notches up second double Test ton in style

15 Nov 2019, 11:04 by IANS

Highlights

  • Mayank Agarwal hit Mehidy Hasan for a massive six over wide long-on to reach the milestone
  • Mayank is the second highest run-getter in Tests this year and is behind Steve Smith who has 774 runs
  • Mayank had scored 215 on October 2 in Visakhapatnam against South Africa

INDORE: Mayank Agarwal notched up his second Test double hundred in a little more than a months' time to underline his credential as an opener in the five-day format, on Day 2 of the first Test against Bangladesh on Friday.

Agarwal, who was dropped on 32 by Imrul Kayes off Abu Jyaed late on the first day, hit Mehidy Hasan for a massive six over wide long-on to reach the milestone and raised his bat in celebration towards the dressing room where skipper Virat Kohli rose to his feet in adulation.

The 28-year-old Karnataka batsman is the second highest run-getter in Tests this year and is behind Australia's Steve Smith who has 774 runs from seven innings.

Mayank took 303 balls to cross the double hundred mark and his innings was laced with 25 fours and five sixes. It was fitting that Mayank got to his double ton off Mehidy whom he took a liking to and scored most runs off. However, Mehidy ended Mayank's 243-run knock when he was caught by Abu Jayed at deep midwicket.
