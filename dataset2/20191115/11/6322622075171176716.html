Sarawak's petroleum tax payment: 'We are still negotiating,' says Petronas

15 Nov 2019, 11:02 by Bernama

KUCHING (Nov 15): Petroliam Nasional Bhd (Petronas) is still negotiating with the Sarawak Government with regard to payment of the five percent petroleum products sales tax that it owes to the State, said its chairman Datuk Ahmad Nizam Salleh.

He said the negotiations also involved the Federal Government.

“No (legal action has been taken against Petronas), we are still negotiating,” he told reporters after the launch of Petrosains Playsmart Kuching, here today.

Ahmad Nizam was asked to comment on Sarawak's intention to take legal action against Petronas after the national oil company failed to settle the tax despite being given until Oct 31 to do so.

“We are working closely with the State Government to resolve the issue,” he added.
