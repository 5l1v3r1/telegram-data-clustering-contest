Bayern Munich chiefs confirms imminent Arsene Wenger talks over managerial vacancy

Former Arsenal boss Wenger, 70, had been open to moving to the Allianz Arena - thanks in part to his close friendship with chief Rummenigge

09 Nov 2019, 21:14 by Matt Maltby, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/matt-maltby/

Bayern Munich chief Karl-Heinz Rummenigge has revealed that Hans-Dieter Flick will remain in charge "until further notice" - and he will hold talks with Arsene Wenger to explain the situation.

Flick took over from Niko Kovac, who was sacked following the 5-1 thrashing by Eintracht Frankfurt last weekend, and has guided Bayern to impressive wins over Olympiacos and Borussia Dortmund.

Former Arsenal gaffer Wenger, 70, had been open to moving to the Allianz Arena - thanks in part to his close friendship with chief Rummenigge.

The Frenchman, however, seemingly pulled out of the race to become the next Bayern boss over the weekend.

And now Rummenigge has appeared to suggest that Flick will be given an opportunity to stake a claim for the permanent job.

 

He told Sport1: “We will work with Hansi Flick until further notice. I have respect for Arsene. We will call him and clear things up. I don't want to stress him."

This revelation comes after Wenger insisted that his interest in the job has ended.

The Frenchman hasn't had a job in football management since he departed the Gunners in 2018 after 22 years at the helm in north London - where he won three Premier League titles and seven FA Cups.

"I wish good luck to Bayern, and for me the situation is settled," beIN Sports report him saying.

Meanwhile, two goals from the seemingly unstoppable Robert Lewandowski helped Bayern Munich banish the nightmare of last week's thrashing by Frankfurt that led to the sacking of Kovac.

Six days after Bayern's heaviest Bundesliga defeat for over 10 years in losing 5-1 to Eintracht, Bayern hammered old rivals Borussia Dortmund 4-0 at the Allianz Arena to move up to third and a point behind leaders Borussia Moenchengladbach.

Poland striker Lewandowski scored the first and third goals in the 17th and 76th minutes to take his tally to a remarkable 26 in 22 games for club and country this season.

Serge Gnabry added a VAR-confirmed second just after the break, with a Mats Hummels own goal 10 minutes from time compounding Dortmund's misery.
