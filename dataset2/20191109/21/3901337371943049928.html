'That's Been The Difference From The Past Few Seasons'

09 Nov 2019, 21:27 by Soccer Laduma

Man of the Match Daniel Cardoso has explained what has been the difference between the current Kaizer Chiefs side compare the ones in recent season.

Read: Chiefs Win Soweto Derby Thriller To Move 10 Points Clear

Cardoso struck the winner from the penalty spot as the AmaKhosi defeated Orlando Pirates 3-2 to move 10 points at the top of the Absa Premiership on Saturday.

The defender feels there is a lot more fight in the current Chiefs side compared to seasons gone by.

"You can see we're actually fighting as a team, I mean look at the past few games, we've been fighting with all our heart and might and I think that's the difference from the past few seasons, you know, we're sticking together and it all starts at training," Cardoso told SuperSport after the game.

Chiefs allowed Pirates to get back in the game after taking a two-goal lead and Cardoso says they are making a habit of getting relaxed with a comfortable lead.

"First of all thanks to God for giving us this great game we produced today, from both sides, very entertaining game so credit to the fans, what a great spectacle today, five goal thriller, when last... they [fans] paid for this and we delivered today.

"Thanks also to Chiefs supporters, if you look at the stadium, it's unbelievable, all black and yellow and it's helping us in the field of play," he went on.

"We started very well today, when we were 2-0 up, I think we got a bit relaxed, it seems like it's becoming a habit, when we got two, three goals up we start to relax a bit and that shouldn't be the way to take the game, because if you become too relaxed, we gonna start conceding silly goals like we did earlier.

"It was a very good free-kick from [Vincent] Pule, from a tight angle, I've got to give him the heads up for that and also the second goal was also a great goal from [Gabadinho] Mhango but we needed to deal with it better you know. Going 2-0 up in the first half we needed a better control of the game."
