Logan Paul net worth as YouTube star rakes in millions from KSI rematch

Logan Paul made his fortune through social media but has now turned to the world of professional boxing

09 Nov 2019, 20:55 by Martin Domin, https://www.facebook.com/mirrorfighting

Logan Paul will cash in tonight whether he wins or loses his rematch with fellow social media star KSI.

But his rise to become one of the most influential social media users is no rags to riches story.

Paul was born in Ohio to parents Pamela Ann Stepnick and Gregory Allan Paul with the family later completed by younger brother Jake.

Paul dabbled in several sports at school but it was YouTube which grabbed his attention from as young as 10 years old when he began posting videos to a channel called Zoosh.

He remained education-focused however and went to college to study industrial Engineering but by this point he had a sizeable YouTube following and had a made a splash on the now defunct Vine platform.

Paul took the decision to drop out of college in 2014 and moved to Los Angeles where he rented a house with other up-and-coming social media stars.

Within a year his following across the major platforms had exploded with his YouTube videos watched by millions.

At this stage he began earning serious money; while initially his six-second clips on Vine would earn him $2,000, he quickly increased that to six figures once in LA.

Slowly but surely Paul began to break into the mainstream with a part in Law & Order and roles in adverts for Pepsi and HBO.

By 2016, Vine had been bought by Twitter and uploads were disabled, but Paul had grown his YouTube following to such an extent that he was reportedly earning upwards of $200,000 a month from the video sharing platform.

But his earnings and reputation took a hit in 2017 when he and a group of friends visited the infamous 'suicide forest' in Japan and filmed a dead body.

He was punished by YouTube and lost sponsorship details which he believes cost him $5million.

“YouTube had to take a stance,” he told the Hollywood Reporter. “They’re not going to let some kid fuck up their ad platform.”

But Paul was not down for long, in part thanks to his 'Maverick' merchandise which earns up $10m a year, which meant he is understood to have earned more than $14m last year.

And of course, this was all before he branched out into the world of boxing, starting with his first fight with KSI in Manchester last year.

Estimates have varied widely as to how much the social media stars earned from the bout which drew a crowd of 15,000 and was watched by millions on YouTube pay-per-view.

Claims that they shared $150m were rubbished with the final figure likely to have been in the seven figures.

That figure could double after the rematch with KSI this weekend and that could take Paul's net worth into the $20m range.
