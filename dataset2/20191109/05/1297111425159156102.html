University of Regina Rams' Greg Nesbitt nominated for U Sports Award

University of Regina Rams assistant coach Greg Nesbitt has been recognized by Canada West for his tireless contributions to football.

09 Nov 2019, 05:45 by Regina Leader-Post

University of Regina Rams assistant coach Greg Nesbitt is the Canada West nominee for the Gino Fracas Award.

The award, introduced by Football Canada, honours a U Sports assistant coach who works tirelessly for the benefit of his players and fellow coaches.

Nesbitt was a defensive lineman with the junior Rams from 1976 to 1980. He spent four seasons as the LeBoldus Golden Suns’ head coach before joining the U of R Rams’ coaching staff in 2004.

Scott Flory, who played high school football for LeBoldus, is Canada West’s coach of the year after guiding the University of Saskatchewan Huskies to a berth in the conference championship game, the Hardy Cup.

The Huskies are to meet the host University of Calgary Dinos on Saturday with the Canada West title at stake.

The U of S won five of the conference’s seven major awards. Along with Flory, the Huskies’ award winners were running back Adam Machart (player of the year), linebacker Ramsey Derbas (rookie of the year), defensive lineman Evan Machibroda (top lineman) and defensive back Nelson Lokombo (outstanding defensive player).

U Sports awards winners are to be announced Nov. 21 in Quebec City, during Vanier Cup week.

The U of R Cougars women’s basketball team split a pair of Canada West games against the Mount Royal University Cougars in Calgary.

Mount Royal won 71-68 on Friday, one day after the Cougars prevailed 67-52. The split left the U of R with a 1-3 record.

Emily Dewey led Regina with 19 points and nine rebounds on Friday. Michaela Kleisinger added 12 points and six rebounds.

On Thursday, Kleisinger and Faith Reid led the Cougars with 14 points. Reid scored 11 of her points during a third-quarter flurry, but left the game early in the fourth frame with an injury that did not allow her to play on Friday.

Dewey and Macaela Crone each had 12 points and nine rebounds during the Cougars’ first victory of the season.

In men’s basketball, the U of R posted its first victory of the season by defeating Mount Royal 101-59 on Friday. Carter Millar led the team with 17 points and 11 rebounds.

On Thursday, Mount Royal defeated the visiting U of R side 96-87 in men’s basketball. Millar led the U of R with 26 points and 17 rebounds.

Both U of R Cougars teams are to return home for games Nov. 15 and 16 against the University of Winnipeg Wesmen. Games are set for 6 p.m. (women) and 8 p.m. (men), both nights.

The Saskatchewan High Schools Athletic Association’s 6A football final is slated for Saturday, 1 p.m., at Mosaic Stadium.

The Golden Suns, champions of the Regina Intercollegiate Football League’s Schwann Conference, will meet the Saskatoon Holy Cross Crusaders for the provincial title.

LeBoldus last won a provincial title in 2016, when it defeated Holy Cross 47-29 in Saskatoon. That was the Golden Suns’ fifth consecutive SHSAA crown.

Bradley Sentes made 57 saves for the Regina Silver Foxes during Thursday’s 5-3 Prairie Junior Hockey League loss to the Triple Seven Chrysler Storm at the Doug Wickenheiser Arena.

The game was tied 3-3 before the Storm got third-period goals from Tristyn Bryks and Dimitri Kourles. Reece Hodson made 32 saves for the Storm.

The Regina Pat Canadians are to play host to the Battlefords Stars in Saskatchewan Midget AAA Hockey League action on Saturday. Game time is 7:45 p.m., at the Co-opeators Centre.

The Pat Canadians are coming off Thursday’s 4-2 loss to the visiting Notre Dame Hounds.

Blake Rasmuson had two goals for Notre Dame, which got singles from Jhett Larson and Josselin Dufey.

Logen Hammett and Cal Caragata replied for Regina, which got two assists from Noah Kuntz.

Caragata leads the league in goals (14) and points (29) after 14 games.
