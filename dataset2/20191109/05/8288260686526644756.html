Hunt to find two women suspected of duping OAP, 91, in cruel Sutton Coldfield con

Victim was targeted by two women who knocked her door in Eachelhurst Road in Walmley, Sutton Coldfield

09 Nov 2019, 05:30 by Nick Horner

A ‘heartbroken’ granddaughter of a 91-year-old OAP targeted in a distraction burglary has urged the public to help police catch those responsible.

Police say two women made repeated attempts to get the elderly Eachelhurst Road resident in Walmley, Sutton Coldfield, to answer her door at around 5.30pm on Wednesday (November 6).

On the third attempt the pensioner let the women in her home – only for money to be stolen from her purse before the women left.

The granddaughter of the 91-year-old victim, who asked not to be named, said: “I am devastated and heartbroken. It’s disgusting. These people must be inhuman to do this to target a woman of this age.

“These two ladies approached my nan’s house and came up the drive and tried knocking on the door. Then they left. They came back again and went again.

“The third time they came back they were hammering on the door. They were laughing and acting cool and collected. They were making jokes and adjusting their scarves.

“You can see it all on my nan’s Nest camera footage.

“Then my nan answered the and you can hear her say ‘not today’.

“But they carry on and she lets them in the house.”

 

The upset 32-year-old added: “Family members visit my nan every day. We found out that the women have taken money from my nan’s purse.

“I don’t know what happened when they went in the house and I don’t want anyone else to go through it.

“My nan has lived in Sutton all her life and in that house for years and years

“It just goes to show what kind of low lives they are to do this to her.”

West Midlands Police confirmed they are investigating the incident.

A spokeswoman for the force said: “Police are investigating a distraction burglary in Eachelhurst Road in the Walmley area of Sutton Coldfield at around 5.30pm on Wednesday (November 6).

“Two women knocked on a 91-year-old woman’s house and entered before leaving with cash from her purse.

“Enquiries continue and anyone with information is asked to contact us via Live Chat at www.west-midlands.police.uk between 8am and midnight, or call 101 anytime.

“To remain anonymous please call Crimestoppers on 0800 555 111. Quote log number 2099 of 6 November.”
