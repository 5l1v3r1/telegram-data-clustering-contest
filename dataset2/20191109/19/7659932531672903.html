Iran Says Injuries Jump to 520 in Friday Quake

09 Nov 2019, 19:00

Iran's state TV reported that the number of injured people from a magnitude 5.9 earthquake on Friday has jumped to 520 from more than 300.

The updated figure followed the end of rescue operations in more than 80 remote villages Tark county in Iran's Eastern Azerbaijan province, some 400 kilometers (250 miles) northwest of the capital, Tehran.

It said 28 were hospitalized and the rest released with minor injuries, adding that the death toll has remained at five people.

Iran experiences an earthquake per day on average. In 2003, a magnitude 6.6 earthquake flattened the historic city of Bam, killing 26,000 people.

In 2017, a magnitude 7 earthquake struck western Iran and killed more than 600 people and injured more than 9,000.
