Taxi driver's fury as he's given £70 parking fine 'while helping elderly woman'

Nigel Cooke claims the parking warden told him "I don't give a toss" when he tried to explain that he was helping the woman, who is in her 70s and uses a cane, in Nailsworth, Gloucestershire

09 Nov 2019, 11:55 by Samuel Port

A taxi driver claims he was given a £70 parking ticket while he was helping an elderly woman who has difficulty walking on her own.

Nigel Cooke, 51, said he was helping the customer, aged in her 70s, down a flight of steps to his car when a parking warden handed him a fine.

The driver claims the warden told him "I don't give a toss" when he tried to explain, and the "embarrassed" customer even offered to pay the fine after the incident in Nailsworth, Gloucestershire.

Mr Cooke admits his car was partially obstructing a pavement, but says the warden should have been "more compassionate", GloucestershireLive reports.

The Freebs Taxis driver, from Stroud, said he parked his car by a stairwell so he could help the woman down to his car from a beauty salon.

 

He claims his car was "three quarters" on a private road and a "quarter" on a pavement while he picked up the fare on Wednesday morning.

But about three minutes later, as he was helping the woman, who uses a cane, a traffic warden approached the car and gave him a ticket, he said.

Mr Cooke added: "She can’t walk very well and she can grab the handrail to go on the steps.

"So basically I pulled up to pick her up.

"I parked there and ran up the stairs and waited three minutes for her to come out.

"I’ve opened the door and then the traffic warden’s just stood there and, honestly, he couldn’t get the ticket on the screen quick enough.

"So I’ll hold my hands up say that I am obstructing a little bit but it didn’t stop people walking past from the pavement to the opposite side.

"I told him that I am picking a lady up and he told me, ‘I don’t give a toss’.

"I said, 'Please don’t swear', and he goes, ‘It’s not swearing’, and all that. It’s just his attitude, really."

Mr Cooke said the woman was "embarrassed" and even offered to pay the fine.

He added: "I totally agree with having traffic wardens.

"I think in a little town like Nailsworth, it sort of needs it - just really for people who park stupidly, so I'm not totally against them.

"But I think (the warden) could have been more compassionate really."

A Gloucestershire County Council spokesperson said: “We’re sorry to hear this taxi driver was unhappy with his experience with a civil enforcement officer.

"We take these concerns very seriously and will be fully investigating the matter.

“The PCN can be challenged by following the instructions on the reverse of the ticket and if an appeal is submitted we will consider all points raised and respond appropriately.”
