EXCLUSIVE: Blades runner Brown recalls when Sheffield United were last sixth in the top flight

Jim Brown was United's goalkeeper the last time the Blades were sixth back in May 1975

09 Nov 2019, 11:31 by David Anderson, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/david-anderson/

Sheffield United sitting sixth may be a novelty for any Blades fan under 50, but Jim Brown has seen it all before.

Brown was United’s goalkeeper when they were last this high in the top flight in May 1975.

Ken Furphy’s side, which boasted Tony Currie, the man acclaimed as their best-ever player, finished four points behind champions Derby and just one outside Europe.

Brown, who signed for £65-a-week when he joined from Chesterfield the previous year, claims they had many of the same qualities as Chris Wilder’s team.

“Realistically, like Sheffield United fans now, we thought if we stay up, we’ll have done well,” said the former Scotland international.

“Early on we had a few good results, which bred confidence, and that’s the same for the players this season.

“We had goalscorers, Alan Woodward scored 12 and Tony Field got 11, and other good players like Eddie Colquhoun, Len Badger and Ted Hemsley.

“From January 4, we played 18 games and only lost two of them. 

“We drew our last game at Birmingham 0-0 and we finished just a point outside a UEFA Cup place.

“We felt disappointed to get so close, even though we didn’t set out to make Europe. To fall at the last was hard to take.”

Brown, 67, kept 11 clean sheets and was named United’s Player of the Year that season, but says the undoubted star was the mercurial Currie.

“He was unbelievable, a great player,” he said. “I played in America in the same team as Johan Cruyff and played against Carlos Alberto and not many of them matched TC.

“I remember playing against Arsenal when he nutmegged Alan Ball, when he sat on the ball and blew kisses to the crowd. He was so confident and he gave us confidence. I also played in the game when him and Alan Birchenhall clashed and then kissed each other.

“Football then was more romantic. We all wanted to win, but you could have a laugh.”

That season is a cautionary tale for the Blades and Furphy was sacked five months later in October 1975 because of their poor start.

Within 12 months, Currie was sold to Leeds and the Blades were relegated, beginning a downwards spiral to the Fourth Division by 1981.

Brown, who has been Chesterfield’s commercial manager since 1985, is confident only good times lie ahead for United under the streetwise Wilder.

“I’m chuffed for them,” he said. “They’re not a team of internationals, like we weren’t, but the players are playing for Chris Wilder, like we played for Ken Furphy.

“They have a good defence, a good keeper and are well organised. I hope they can sustain it.”

Brown combined his role as Chesterfield’s commercial manager with emergency reserve goalkeeper for 12 years and was on the bench, aged 43, for their 1995 Division Three play-off win over Bury.

“The team went away to Wembley on the Friday to stay in a hotel,” said Brown, who made his last appearance on the bench at 45. “I had to stay until the Saturday morning because I had 30 buses to see off. 

“I jumped on the last bus, got to Wembley, got changed, sat on the bench and then went up and got my winner’s medal.”
