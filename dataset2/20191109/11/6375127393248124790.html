Worker nearly kills stepdaughter

09 Nov 2019, 11:15 by Edd Reyes

POLICE arrested a construction worker who almost killed his stepdaughter when the latter asked her mother to drive him out of their house her stepfather Friday in Caloocan City.

Caloocan police chief P/Col. Noel Flores said Elmer Mahas, 57, was arrested in a follow-up operation Friday afternoon by elements of Caloocan Police Community Precinct (PCP) 5 in Camarin Road, Barangay 178 almost 10 hours after he nearly clubbed to death Jemeline Lozano, 25, canteen staff.

The inciden,t according to Col. Flores happened inside the victim’s abode located along San Isidro Kiko, Bgy. 178 at around 7:20 a.m.

Lozano was about to leave for work when she saw her stepfather inside their house, prompting her to ask her mother to drive him out.

The suspect, who got irked, grabbed a piece of wooden club and hit his stepdaughter repeatedly in different parts of the body while uttering “Papatayin na lang kita”, until the victim lost consciousness.

Mahas immediately fled towards an unknown direction while the victim was rushed by her mother to Dr. Jose N. Rodriguez Memorial Hospital where she underwent treatment and presently under observation.
