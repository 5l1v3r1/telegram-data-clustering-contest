Maruti Suzuki India cuts production for ninth straight month in October

09 Nov 2019, 10:39 by TIMESOFINDIA.COM

Highlights

  • Maruti produced a total of 1,19,337 units in October as against 1,50,497 units in the same period last year
  • Passenger vehicles or PV sales in the month of October stood at 1,17,383 units, down 20.85 per cent, as against 1,48,318 units posted in the year-ago period

NEW DELHI: Maruti Suzuki India (MSI) on Saturday reported a 20.70 per cent decline in its production numbers during the month of October. This is the carmaker's ninth straight fall in the production figures. Maruti produced a total of 1,19,337 units in October as against 1,50,497 units in the same period last year, the automaker said in a statement to exchanges.

Passenger vehicles or PV sales in the month of October stood at 1,17,383 units, down 20.85 per cent, as against 1,48,318 units posted in the year-ago period, it added.

Production of mini and compact segment cars including Alto, New WagonR, Celerio, Ignis, Swift, Baleno and Dzire stood at 85,064 units as against 1,08,462 units in August last year, down 21.57 per cent.

Mid-sized sedan Ciaz saw its production reduced to 1,922 units in October from 3,513 units in the same month last year.

Light commercial vehicle Super Carry's production was also trimmed to 1,954 units last month from 2,179 units in October 2018, the filing said.

Although, production of utility vehicles such as Vitara Brezza, Ertiga, XL-6 and S-Cross surged 0.93 per cent to 22,736 as compared with 22,526 units in the year-ago month.

In October, the automaker had cut its production by 17.84 per cent at 1,32,199 units.

Maruti made the most out of the festive season, posting its first growth in domestic passenger vehicles sales after seven months in October.

The country's largest automaker had posted a growth of 4.5 per cent in its domestic wholesales at 1,44,277 units in October as against 1,38,100 units in the same period last year.
