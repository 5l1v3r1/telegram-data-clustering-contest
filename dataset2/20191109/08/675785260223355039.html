Inside KSI and Logan Paul's pro training camps ahead of rematch

The YouTubers will headline a sold-out Staples Center this weekend, with Billy Joe Saunders on the undercard

09 Nov 2019, 08:00 by Ned Keating

YouTube sensations KSI and Logan Paul face off in the boxing ring for a second time in the early hours of Sunday morning - this time as professionals.

Some 14 months on from their first meeting in the ring under amateur rules, the social media stars are topping the bill at a sold-out Staples Center in Los Angeles this weekend.

Despite world champions Billy Joe Saunders and Devin Haney defending their straps on the undercard, the main event will see two rookes make their pro debuts.

The bout is being promoted by Matchroom's Eddie Hearn, who also has the likes of Anthony Joshua in his stable of boxers.

With the headguards removed and the duo trading blows with 10oz gloves, their training regimes for the rematch have been far more thorough this time around.

It seems both KSI and Paul have been taking the training seriously, with both spending time working under some of boxing's biggest names.

And the pair have not been shy in offering fans a glimpse into their preparation for one of the year's most talked about fights.

Paul goes into this weekend's bout as the odds-on favourite to triumph and has been spending time with a former heavyweight world champion in his bid to beat his fierce foe.

Ex-WBO champ Shannon Briggs has been working with the 24-year-old's team in recent months and was even part of the online star's training camp.

And even though Paul is gearing up for his first bout as a pro, it seems Briggs did not go easy on his fellow American.

‘Things are completely different this time around – the camp…it’s like a death camp right now," revealed the 24-year-old's best friend Mike Maljak.

"There’s absolutely no distractions, the entire team is ready to die for this victory.

"Shannon's got one goal and that’s to go in the ring and kill his opponent. There’s no distractions, no bull***, no “I’ve got to make up for Tokyo, or I’ve got to make up for YouTube.”.’

"Logan doesn’t plan on there being one punch he feels. He is going into that ring to eat – to literally eat – KSI is a meal for him.

KSI, meanwhile, has been working closely with Viddal Riley, himself a veteran of three pro bouts at cruiserweight.

Riley is relatively young for a trainer at just 22, but he clearly has an ability to coach after signing a promotional deal with Floyd Mayweather's promotional company.

The 26-year-old, whose real name is Olajide Olatunji, has spent time at training camps in Las Vegas and Los Angeles ahead of his pro debut.

And he was even able to knock one about out in sparring, demonstrating the power KSI boasts in his gloves ahead of Saturday's rematch.

Judging by their training, it seems Saturday's fight may justify it's main billing - even if it is between two men making their pro debuts.
