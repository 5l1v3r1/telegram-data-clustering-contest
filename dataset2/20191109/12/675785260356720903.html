Robbie Williams slams Coleen Rooney for 'dangerous' plan to publicly shame Rebekah Vardy

Pop star Robbie Williams says Coleen Rooney was blinded by a lust for revenge and didn't consider the devastating consequences for fellow WAG Rebekah Vardy

09 Nov 2019, 12:07 by Louise Randell, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/louise-randell/

Robbie Williams has said Coleen Rooney was blinded by lust for "revenge" in her plan to publicly shame Rebekah Vardy.

Coleen, 33, outed Jamie Vardy's wife as part of an elaborate scheme to expose a mole who had been leaking stories from her private Instagram account to the press.

After the WAGatha Christie row went public, pregnant Rebekah, 37, was bombarded with sickening abuse from trolls even though she denied she was behind the leaks.

Take That star Robbie has now waded into the drama and insisted Coleen was wrong to subject Rebekah to such public torment.

In an interview with The Telegraph Magazine : Robbie explained: "Let's say Rebekah was guilty - which we don't know.

"How she will feel inside right now is 10 times worse than how Coleen would have felt when those things were happening to her...

 

"It's a dangerous game to play... I understand Coleen's need for revenge and I probably would have done the same thing but we have to be careful...

"It's suicide-causing - provoking those levels of shame."

Rebekah and her footballer husband Jamie were forced to cut their recent holiday to Dubai short when she was accused of selling stories on fellow WAG Coleen.

Coleen claimed to have planted false stories on her private Instagram account and revealed she'd blocked every account but Rebekah's to test whether or not the information would leak.

 

When the storied found their way into a national newspaper, Coleen told the world what had happened in a bombshell statement that went viral.

Rebekah vehemently denied leaking stories and said she was "disgusted" that she was facing such claims when heavily pregnant.

She has since claimed to have hired a forensic IT expert to prove her innocence and is considering legal action.
