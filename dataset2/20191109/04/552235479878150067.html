Man threatened to jump from motorway bridge after ex-partner took away his Xbox

Remi Alston, 26, pleaded guilty to causing a public nuisance after closing a stretch of the M56 for four hours.

09 Nov 2019, 04:30 by John Kelly

A man threatened to jump from a motorway bridge after his former partner took away his Xbox and internet router.

Remi Alston, 26, pleaded guilty to causing a public nuisance after he caused the shutdown of the M56 for four hours as police tried to talk him down.

Police in Greater Manchester said the incident ‘had a severe effect on already drained police resources on a busy Saturday night’.

Alston, who was said to have ‘mental health issues’, eventually came down voluntarily in the early hours of April 7 this year.

Manchester Crown Court heard that 12 police officers, two negotiators, and other emergency service professionals were called out to the bridge in the Wythenshawe area of the city.

The court heard he previously received a suspended sentence for a similar incident on Barton Swing Bridge last year.

Prosecutor Gavin Howie said that Alston had returned to his home to find his Xbox and other electrical items were missing, and he blamed his ex-partner.

He went to the bridge because he “wanted to hurt himself” but said he didn’t intend to commit suicide.

Police were called at about 1.30am and said he would ‘stay there even if it took three days’.

At about 4.50am, his ex arrived at the scene with a wireless router and Alston came down shortly after.

When he was arrested, Alston said he was ‘sick of the mickey being taken out of him’.

Defending, Robert Lancaster said that Alston has suffered from long standing mental health problems, and said he his client suffered from a ‘borderline personality disorder’ which can cause him to have ‘extreme reactions’.

Judge Suzanne Goddard QC said that Alston’s culpability was ‘significantly affected’ by his mental health issues, and passed a 15 month prison sentence, suspended for two years.

The judge told Alston: “It is self evident that it must have caused disruption to a large number of people, who were prevented from going about their lawful and usual business.”

Alston, from Eccles, was also ordered to complete 15 rehabilitation activity requirement days, and a 12 month alcohol treatment program.
