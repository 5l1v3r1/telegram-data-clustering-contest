St Johnstone 1 Hibernian 4: Saints comfortably beaten at McDiarmid Park

Stevie May netted a late consolation for the Perth side but it was too little too late

09 Nov 2019, 17:15 by Perthshire Advertiser, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/perthshire-advertiser/

St Johnstone’s hopes of piecing together a prolonged unbeaten run came to a crashing halt at McDiarmid Park on Saturday.

Hibernian striker Christian Doidge did the majority of the damage by netting a hat-trick while Scott Allan also got in on the act.

Stevie May grabbed a late consolation - his second since returning to the club - but Saints were comfortably beaten and dropped to second bottom of the standings.

Perth supporters would be hoping defensive frailties displayed on numerous occasions this season had been banished after a recent clean sheet against Hearts.

But one minute and 26 seconds into this Premiership fixture the home backline made life far too easy for capital neighbours Hibs to seize an early advantage.

It was ignited by a simple long ball up the centre of the pitch. Skipper Jason Kerr slipped on the wet turf and Florian Kamberi took control to drive the attack forward.

He cut-back across the face of goal for the unmarked Doidge who needed two bites of the cherry to eventually sweep low beyond Zander Clark from close range.

That same combination linked-up in the 17th minute to extend Hibs’ lead to two as disgruntled home fans expressed their disappointment.

The Swiss forward this time drilled a delivery into the box from the right flank, which Doidge neatly flicked into the back of the net.

Saints had offered little but Scott Tanser did call Chris Maxwell into action as the half hour mark approached with a tame, right-footed attempt.

Moments later Doidge, who hadn’t netted in the league until his trip to Perth, was inches away from grabbing an early third but couldn’t connect with Allan’s cross.

Tommy Wright’s side needed to raise the tempo but further pain was inflicted when Allan neatly finished three minutes into the second half.

Matty Kennedy gifted possession to the crafty midfielder and he proceeded to drive deep into Saints territory before getting the better of an outstretched Clark.

Worse was to come when Doidge climbed above Perth defenders to nod in Jason Naismith’s cross with 58 minutes played.

Substitute Callum Hendry was presented with a chance to pull back a late consolation but his touch evaded him when he needed it most.

Strike partner May did grab a 90th minute goal, turning home Anthony Ralston’s cross after a front post run. But by this point the game was gone.
