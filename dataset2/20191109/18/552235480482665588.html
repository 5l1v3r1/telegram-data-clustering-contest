Harry Kewell admits Hibs interest as he reveals he's researching the club

The former Liverpool star was at McDiarmid Park to watch Hibs take on St Johnstone and admits he would like to get back into management with the Easter Road club.

09 Nov 2019, 18:37 by Record Sport Online

Harry Kewell admits he is interested in taking over at Hibs after watching them batter St Johnstone at McDiarmid Park.

The former Liverpool and Leeds star had previously suggested he was only at the game as a 'friend of a friend' but has now confirmed he would take the job.

Kewell has been out of work since leaving Notts County after 14 games and just three wins in that time. He also had a spell in charge of Crawley Town, winning 18 of his 57 games in charge.

And now he fancies a crack at the Scottish Premiership if he Hibs give him the opportunity to return to management.

He said: “I’m sure I’m not the only one interested. It is a fantastic club with a fantastic set-up.

“You do your research and you hear the players are full-on and hard working.

“Of course it would be a cracking job but I’m sure there are hundreds of managers who would like this opportunity.

“I go to games every week as I like to keep my eye in. I don’t mind watching games on television but I prefer to see it through my own eyes so I can get the full picture.

“But I’m 100 per cent looking to get back into management. I love it, I enjoy it and it is something I feel very passionate about.

“I enjoy coaching and I think that is where my strengths lie. It is something I want to be involved in again.”

Kewell also admitted he regularly hears good things about Scottish football and the success of his former Anfield team-mate Steven Gerrard has piqued his interest.

But he insists he hasn't spoken to the Rangers boss about a move to Scotland.

He added: "I hear great things about the Scottish game. Steven [Gerrard] is here and he's doing really well.

"I think Steven's got enough on his plate at the moment. He's doing a fantastic job at Rangers and they had a fantastic win on Thursday. As players and coaches you maybe speak a bit to get a bit of info here or there but you kind of let people do their own business."
