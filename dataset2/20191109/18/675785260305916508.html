Accrington vs Crewe FA Cup clash left with one linesman after referee chaos

There was confusion at half-time when it emerged that the replacement referee picked up an injury and, despite receiving medical treatment, was also unable to return to action

09 Nov 2019, 18:07 by Matt Maltby, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/matt-maltby/

The FA Cup first-round clash between Accrington Stanley and Crewe Alexandra was sent into chaos after injuries to two referees left the encounter with just one linesman.

The match at Accrington's Crown Ground on Saturday was held up in the 23th minute when referee Neil Hair was unable to continue officiating the match after picking up a knock.

After a three-minute delay, Hair was replaced by one of his assistants and Crewe went into the break with a slender 1-0 lead thanks to Charlie Kirk's opener.

But there was confusion at half-time when it emerged that the replacement referee picked up an injury and, despite receiving medical treatment, he was also unable to return to action.

There was a delay to kick-off for the second half for over an hour, with fans waiting anxiously to discover whether the game would be called off.

After a look at the FA Cup rulebook, it was then decided that the second half would go ahead with just one assistant and, in another bizarre turn of events, a coin toss would decide which line he would run.

Crew tweeted: "We are going with 1 assistant and we will toss a coin to decide which line he runs! In the FA rules so we play. Kick off is 5.10pm... Crewe still lead 1-0 thanks to Kirkyâ€™s goal about 2 days ago..."

After a delay of over an hour, the game eventually got back underway and Crewe doubled their lead through a Chris Porter penalty.

Despite the unusual delay, the visitors' social media team seemed to see the funny side, posting a series of hilarious tweets.

One read: "Maybe FA Cup sponsors Emirates should fly out all the supporters who have remained at the game to Dubai to warm up after the game finishes at midnight?"

Crewe ultimately claimed a 2-0 win to book their place in the second round of the FA Cup.
