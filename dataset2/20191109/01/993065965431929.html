Belfast pizzeria fined for food safety breaches

09 Nov 2019, 01:00

A BELFAST pizzeria has been fined and ordered to pay compensation for food safety breaches.

The fine imposed at Belfast Magistrates Court yesterday came after Belfast Woodfired Pizza Company on the city's Lisburn Road failed to tell a customer with a food allergy that their order contained nuts, when in fact one of the ingredients were cashew nuts.

However, the customer had a nut allergy and suffered an anaphylactic reaction after eating a slice of vegan cheese pizza and was subsequently hospitalised.

Belfast Woodfired Pizza Company pleaded guilty to a complaint of placing food that was deemed unsafe and considered injurious to health to a specific category of consumers on the market.

The restaurant was fined £1,000 and ordered to pay compensation of £2,500 to the injured customer.
