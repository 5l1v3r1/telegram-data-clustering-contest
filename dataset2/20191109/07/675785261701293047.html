John Hartson tells Granit Xhaka what he must do to win back Arsenal supporters

EXCLUSIVE: Hartson believes the Swiss midfielder still has a future at the Gunners despite swearing at supporters during the 2-2 draw with Crystal Palace

09 Nov 2019, 07:30 by Alex Milne

Former Arsenal striker John Hartson says Granit Xhaka still has a future at the club if he can improve his performances on the pitch.

Xhaka was stripped of the captaincy by Unai Emery after he gestured and swore at supporters while being substituted during the Gunners' 2-2 draw with Crystal Palace last month.

Emery also refused to rule out Xhaka moving in the January transfer window, saying: "We’re speaking a lot about that. It’s clear. There’s no more words about that."

But Hartson, speaking ahead of a KP Nuts comedy gig for men's health charity Movember, thinks Xhaka still has the opportunity to win over Arsenal supporters.

"You win the fans back by being humble, being professional and putting in wonderful performances," Hartson said. "He's a good player, he's been playing for Arsenal for a number of years, and now he has got to take on a strong mentality to rise to the occasion.

"Sometimes you have to find an inner strength, deal with what you've done, don't sulk and move on rather than throwing your toys out of the pram.

"Yes, he will get a tough time from supporters initially, but this is where he's got to go and show the character that got him the captaincy in the first place.

"Arsenal is never about one person or one manager. The club will be there in a hundred years, it is bigger than one player."

Hartson does, however, believe Emery made the right decision in stripping Xhaka of the captaincy.

"I do think there's a future for Xhaka at Arsenal but I think Emery had to be seen doing something and I believe he made the right decision," he added.

"You simply can't react the way he did, and I think it was disrespectful towards the club and the supporters."
