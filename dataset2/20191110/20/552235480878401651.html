Queen of the South 3 Ayr United 1: Former Kilmarnock boss sinks Honest Men

There was Championship success for Allan Johnston over 10-man Ayr at Palmerston yesterday.

10 Nov 2019, 20:01 by Colin Paterson, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/colin-paterson/

Allan Johnston last night saluted his deadly Doonhamers as their second-half super show sank Ayr.

Goals from Connor Murray and Gary Oliver clinched victory for Queens after Michael Moffat had cancelled out Kevin Holt’s opener.

Ayr had Luke McCowan sent-off as their hopes of reducing the six-point gap to Championship leaders Dundee United crumbled at Palmerston.

And Queen of the South boss Johnston believes his top team got what they deserved for taking the game to the Somerset Park high-fliers after the interval.

He said: “It was a massive game for the fans in terms of bragging rights so I’m really happy to get the result and it was a great performance as well.

 

“Some of our passing and moving was really good in the second half when we totally dominated and thoroughly deserved to win.

“A few of the guys who haven’t played much lately showed a great attitude and with a wee bit more luck we could have scored a couple more.

“It’s been a great reaction from the players after the previous three games and they’ve set the standard now. We have a hard run of games coming up but this is a nice way to start them.”

The Honest Men could have taken the lead after just five minutes.

Moffat received the ball in the six-yard box and despite being surrounded by four defenders, dragged a shot inches wide of the far post.

 

Ayr dominated the early stages but it was Queens who struck the opener against the run of play in the 18th minute.

Some neat interchange play on the edge of the box involving Stephen Dobbie created space on the left-hand side for Holt to exploit and the defender emphatically blasted home when the ball was rolled into his path.

It was tough on the visitors but they continued to threaten and, in the 38th minute, grabbed an equaliser.

McCowan’s deep cross to the back post found Craig Moore who nodded down perfectly for Moffat to slam home his sixth goal of the season.

Ayr could have gone in at the break ahead when Moffat turned wide from another dangerous McCowan cross but it was the home side who restored their lead with a terrific goal in the 67th minute.

 

Midfielder Murray started then finished the move when Dobbie threaded the ball through for the 22-year-old to bend a shot into the top corner.

And Queens made it 3-1 in the 75th minute when Dobbie pinged the ball out to Scott Mercer who pulled back for Oliver to slot home.

The red mist descended on Ayr three minutes later when McCowan was sent-off for a nasty lunge on Oliver which led to some handbags.

Ayr gaffer Mark Kerr said: “Going into a break is never good when you have a result like that. We were happy coming in at 1-1 but we then lost a goal from nothing.

“I’ve not seen Luke’s red card but I think we’ve lost a bit of discipline and started chasing around and not kept our shape.”
