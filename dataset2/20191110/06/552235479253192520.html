Scott Arfield explains where Rangers Euro confidence comes from after Porto win

The 31-year-old admits the whole club expected to beat Porto and admits the strength in depth is a huge factor.

10 Nov 2019, 06:00 by Scott McDermott, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/scott-mcdermott/

Scott Arfield reckons Rangers can now take ANY team in Europe out of their comfort zone when Ibrox springs to life.

The midfielder played a key role in their 2-0 Europa League win over Porto in midweek that puts Rangers level on points with Group G leaders Young Boys.

Steven Gerrard’s men remain unbeaten at home in Europe and Arfield insists the players don’t fear any big gun that might roll into Ibrox.

The 31-year-old said: “Everyone at the club fancied us against Porto.

“When the big boys roll into town there’s always a great atmosphere at Ibrox. The crowd were waiting for something on Thursday night.

“Sometimes it just needs a tackle – and Ryan Jack put one in early. Then you can feel the place come to life.

“You have to give your all and make it as awkward as possible for the opposition’s technical players.

“And you saw how many times Porto gave the ball away. Even good players don’t like it when you get after them.

“It was a massive result. Do teams want to come here now?

“We can make this an uncomfortable place for anybody to come. The proof is the gaffer’s record in Europe.

“We’ve made this place our own again and so difficult for visiting teams.

“The result against Porto, who were in the quarter-finals of the Champions League a year ago, just adds to that.

“When you consider the calibre of player that turned up for them, we did ourselves proud.

“We’ve certainly come a long way in the space of 18 months. We reckon we can beat anyone – particularly at home with 50,000 fans waiting to go.

“Now we have to keep pushing and qualify from the group.”

Arfield was left out of the starting line up for both Porto games in favour of winger Brandon Barker.

But when he came on as a sub at Ibrox, Rangers’ performance went up a notch as Alfredo Morelos and Steven Davis grabbed decisive goals.

The former Burnley man admits he was frustrated to be benched by Gerrard but knows it is a sign of the quality at his manager’s disposal.

He said: “The gaffer has been around the block a lot longer than me so he knows how I feel.

“Even in my short career, I’m always going to be disappointed when I don’t play. But we’ve got no problems whatsoever.

“You can see the competition for places here now. Just look at our bench on Thursday night. There’s a group of players who train in the morning and don’t even make it on to the bench.

“That’s because we’ve strengthened and it’s why we feel we’re in a really good place at the moment.

“A lot of people spoke about it last year, didn’t they?

“As the season went on it caught up with us because we didn’t have that strength in depth.

“But we’ve certainly got it this season.

“When you’re left out of the team you have to somehow make an impact.

“And I think myself, Joe Aribo and Jermain Defoe did that on Thursday night. Everyone wants to play so of course I was disappointed.

“But it’s about channelling that in the right way so it helps the team.

“There’s no point moping about the place because we have a big squad.

“It’s difficult to play in every game so you have to make sure that when you come on you make an impact.”

Now Arfield is relishing the chance to grab a starting slot in today’s Premiership clash at Livingston.

 

He said: “In terms of fitness, I’m buzzing. Football is a wonderful thing and the time when I’m tired, I’ll have problems.

“I don’t feel tired at all. At this club you’re always on a wave of excitement and I can feel it getting even better.”

As Arfield gets older he doesn’t have to look far for inspiration with the likes of Davis and Defoe around him.

The Northern Irishman, now 34, has been impressive this term for Gerrard’s side but Arfield’s not surprised.

He said: “Davo’s not better than I expected. I’ve played against him so many times and know how good he is.

“As soon as he walked through the door here I knew he’d take us to a different level.

“Not just on the pitch but off it as well, the way he conducts himself.

“The young boys in the dressing-room look up to him and Jermain, the way they live their life.

“Their body shape is something that men their age don’t normally have!

“But they’re a credit to themselves and it’s fantastic to have them here.”
