The Scottish Sports Awards nominees in full as Andy Robertson is in contention for gong

The winners of all the 2019 awards will be announced in Glasgow on December 5.

10 Nov 2019, 06:00 by Gordon Waddell, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/gordon-waddell/

The challenge for the judging panel every year is picking winners when there are no losers.

And in 2019 it has been tougher than ever to whittle down the superstars and success stories you’ve been nominating for the past three months for the 13th annual Sunday Mail and sportscotland Scottish Sports Awards.

Our expert panel of judges, featuring internationals from five different disciplines and some of Scottish sport’s most influential figures, spent hours last week coming up with a set of winners who paint the nation’s achievements in the past 12 months in a glorious, golden light.

And we’re certain that the most glittering night on our sporting calendar, the only official awards night of the national agency for sport, will shine like the medals around our winners’ necks.

Once again we recognise world champions, European champions, British champions.

But it’s also a night when we will recognise our community champions, celebrating ALL that’s good in Scottish sport, from our schools and clubs all the way up to the best on the planet.

Who will take the big prizes, though? You’ll find out at Glasgow’s Doubletree by Hilton Hotel on December 5 – but for now take your pick from these.

Scottish Sports personality of the year

 

Duncan Scott (Swimming)

The Alloa 22-year-old continued to make waves in the pool with World Championship gold and bronze in China.

Katie Archibald MBE (Cycling)

Another top year for the track icon with World silver in Poland, followed by a full set of gold, silver and bronze at the European Championships.

Andy Robertson (Football)

 

The Liverpool full-back and Scotland skipper elevated himself to greatness with his club by becoming the first Scot to win the Champions League in 22 years.

Josh Taylor (Boxing)

The Tartan Tornado hit 2019 hard, staying unbeaten while taking both IBF and WBA world titles to make him Scotland’s king of the ring.

Laura Muir (Athletics)

Brilliant European indoor golden double in Glasgow and fifth in the Worlds in her second best ever time, despite losing six weeks to injury.

Seonaid McIntosh (Shooting)

An incredible 12 months for the 23-year-old, reaching world No.1 and becoming the first British woman ever to take gold in the 3P World Cup.

Jamie Murray (Tennis)

 

So often overshadowed by brother Andy, Jamie added a seventh Grand Slam title of his own to his CV at the US Open in the mixed doubles.

Robert MacIntyre (Golf)

Superb debut season on the European Tour for the Oban 23-year-old, including a top-six finish at The Open, makes him Rookie of the Year.

Coach/manager of the year

 

Catriona Matthew (Golf)

The 50-year-old Scot’s diligence over two years led to three days of Gleneagles magic as Europe lifted the Solheim Cup with the final putt.

Andy Young (Athletics)

The Glasgow coach’s reputation flourished during 2019 thanks to his work with Laura Muir, Jemma Reekie and Canadian Gabriela Debues-Stafford.

Scott Booth (Football)

Another superb season for the Glasgow City coach, landing the club’s 13th straight league title and reaching the quarters, at least, of the Champions League.

Local club of the year

 

Northwest Boxing Club (Kilmarnock)

Celebrating 50 years and a beacon of hope in a notoriously deprived area, they produce medal winners but do so much more.

Westquarter and Redding Cricket Club

Brilliantly inclusive and growing the game in the community, as well as award-winning work with disability sport and dementia sufferers.

Langholm Rugby Club

The perfect community club at the heart of the Muckle Toon, their driven, well-qualified coaches and helpers are about more than just their sport.

Local hero award

 

Fraser Johnston (Cycling)

The 22-year-old is the force of nature behind the Falkirk Junior Bike Club and is the epitome of community commitment for his 150-strong kids’ group.

Gary McLaughlin (Walking Football)

The man behind an incredible success story across Scotland. With a rocketing number of participants, it offers mental health benefits under the Football For Life banner.

David Scott (Athletics)

Founder of the brilliantly-named Fit Like Joggers Club, David has been an inspiration to thousands of runners in and around Aberdeen.

School sport award

 

St Brigid’s Primary, Glasgow

A new approach has doubled their kids’ participation in sport in five years –now 92 per cent take part. Inspiring in areas of multiple deprivation.

St Paul’s HS, Glasgow

More than 20 sports clubs, participation up 94 per cent in three years, more than a third of all pupils active in sport – a school that’s getting it right for their kids.

Glenboig Primary

The semi-rural Coatbridge school runs an amazing 25 different sports clubs with the help of staff, parents and Primary 7 sports leaders.

Young sports personality

 

Charlie Aldridge (Cycling)

The Crieff teenager took the mountain biking world by storm by becoming the first GB male to wear the rainbow stripes for his World Junior XC win in Canada.

Toni Shaw (Swimming)

Last year’s winner, the teenager was inspired again, adding six World medals to her Euro haul of 2018.

Katie Shanahan (Swimming)

A breakthrough year for the 15-year-old, hauling home seven medals, including three gold, from the European Juniors and Euro Olympic Festival.

Disability sport 

Scottish Wheelchair Curling

An inspired silver at the World Championships on home ice in Stirling, beating defending champs Norway along the way.

Neil Fachie (Para Cycling)

Won his 13th world title, a sprint gold in the Netherlands with pilot Matt Rotherham, as well as silver in the time trial.

Toni Shaw (Swimming)

The 16-year-old added to last year’s breakthrough season spectacularly with six medals – two of each colour – at the Worlds in London.

Team of the year

Scotland Women’s Hockey

Superb performance on home turf to win Euro Hockey II championships and rejoin the continent’s professional elite.

Scotland Men’s Squash

So often the nearly men, they earned a brilliant bronze in the European Championships with a win over reigning champs France.

Celtic FC (Football)

An unprecedented treble Treble, Neil Lennon stepping in to finish what Brendan Rodgers started. Back on top again this season.

Inspirational performance

 

England 38 Scotland 38 (Rugby)

One of the most incredible comebacks in rugby history, from 31 points down before half-time to a draw that retained the Calcutta Cup.

Andy Murray (Tennis)

The career obituaries were written until a post-hip-op comeback was sealed in a tear-stained European Open victory over Stan Wawrinka.

Duncan Scott (Swimming)

Has done plenty in the pool to inspire a generation – but his refusal to share a podium with Chinese drug cheat Sun Yang was hailed around the world.
