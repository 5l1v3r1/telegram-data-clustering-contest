Fifth Draw For Ea Lla Koto, Cosmos Back In Drop Zone

10 Nov 2019, 18:08 by Soccer Laduma

GladAfrica Championship log leaders, Uthongathi FC and second placed, JDR Stars, secured victories over Cape Umoya United and Swallows FC respectively, while lowly TS Sporting stunned high-flying Tshakhuma Tsha Madzivhandila..

In addition, three matches were drawn, including Jomo Cosmos very important clash with fellow-strugglers, Mbombela United.

Thanks to two late goals by Njabulo Ngcobo and Cleopas Dube, the Cane Cutters grabbed their 25th point, which left the Spirited Ones mid-table after yet another defeat.

Brooklyn Poggenpoel scored a penalty for Dominic Isaacs’ outfit.

The Hammer Boys bounced back after three games without a win to pour more misery on the Birds, who’re thirteen points off the pace.

Nigerian, Biodun Alabi, got his name on the scoresheet in the 6th minute via a spot kick and just before half time, Moeketsi Makhanya increased the lead.

Although Brandon Truter’s team reduced the deficit through Kagiso Malinga, their efforts were ultimately insufficient.

Abantu Bemthetho recorded only their second three-point haul after Kamohelo Sithole’s winner at the Kwabokweni Stadium.

Sizwe Mdlinzo, TS Galaxy inflicted more misery on last-placed.

The match between Free State Stars and Steenberg United ended all square at 2-2 with Ruzaigh Gamildien netting two penalties for the Cape Town team to bring his overall total to eight.

The Rockets and Royal Eagles produced the same result in Kameelrivier, where Austin Dube and Sizwe Mdlinzo were on target for Dan Malesela’s side.

At Olen Park, there were no goals when Cosmos entertained Mbombela but lots of drama as both sides were reduced to ten men following infringements.

Ezenkosi returned to the relegation zone and together with Mbombela have netted a mere five times in ten games.
