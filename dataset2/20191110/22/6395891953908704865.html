2 teens arrested after allegedly crashing stolen car into Worcester Police cruisers

10 Nov 2019, 22:18 by Kirsten Glavin

WORCESTER, Mass. - Two teenagers are under arrest in Worcester and are accused of leading police on a chase in a stolen vehicle before crashing into two cruisers.

Police say the incident began when an officer saw a vehicle on Russell Street without its headlights on around 6 p.m. on Saturday night. The officer checked the license plate and noticed it was stolen.

When trying to pull over the vehicle, the driver took off. A short time later, another police officer saw the vehicle and attempted to pull it over when the suspect put the vehicle in reverse and slammed into the cruiser before taking off.

The vehicle ran several stop signs before crashing into another cruiser.

The suspect's vehicle eventually crashed and police were able to place the 17-year-old driver under arrest. The teen is accused of spitting on one of the officers as he was taken into custody. He faces the following charges. 

  • Assault and Battery with a Dangerous Weapon
  • Receiving a Stolen Motor Vehicle
  • Resisting Arrest
  • Failure to Stop for a Stop Sign of Flashing Red Light 
  • Unlicensed Operation of a Motor Vehicle 
  • Illegally Attaching Plate
  • Leaving the Scene of Property Damage 
  • Operating Negligently so as to Endanger
  • Use of a Motor Vehicle without Authority
  • Failure to Stop for Police

A 17-year-old passenger is facing charges as well, including Receiving a Stolen Motor Vehicle, Resisting Arrest, Disorderly Conduct, and Use of a Motor Vehicle without Authority.
