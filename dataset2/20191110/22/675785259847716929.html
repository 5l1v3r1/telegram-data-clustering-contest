Alexandre Lacazette lifts lid on mood in Arsenal dressing room amid Unai Emery pressure

Arsenal were beaten 2-0 by Leicester on Saturday as the pressure increases on boss Unai Emery

10 Nov 2019, 22:30 by James Nursey, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/james-nursey/

Alexandre Lacazette admits Arsenal are low on confidence as the pressure grows on boss Unai Emery.

The Gunners’ record of 17 points and a minus-one goal difference is their worst start to a top-flight season after 12 games since 1982-83.

They had just one shot on target in this match from Lacazette in the 20th minute as skipper Pierre-Emerick Aubameyang later had a goal ruled offside.

Emery’s men are now winless in their last five Premier League away games and have just one win in seven games in all competitions.

Striker Lacazette said: “We miss confidence and we need the international break. I hope we are going to come back well. We need to work more than we do now to find confidence.

“We are very disappointed, we had chances in the first half. We couldn’t score and you need to take your chances in this type of game.

“We have to work again and keep working everyday. But I think we didn’t play so bad, we just missed our chances in the first half.”

Defeat leaves Arsenal below promoted Sheffield United well off the pace in the race for Champions League qualification.

The French striker added: “We want to be top four of course.

“Now we are nine points behind Chelsea and Leicester and it is going to be hard but everything is possible in football.

“We still have time until the end of the season. We are going to work to come back.”
