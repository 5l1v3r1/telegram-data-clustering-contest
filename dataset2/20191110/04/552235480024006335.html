Lawyer gets £10,000 fine after 10-year ban for third drink driving rap in six years

Martha Rafferty was hit with the fine by the Scottish Solicitors Discipline Tribunal after being found guilty of professional misconduct.

10 Nov 2019, 04:30 by Norman Silvester, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/norman-silvester/

A criminal lawyer with a series of drink-driving convictions has been found guilty of professional misconduct by a watchdog and fined £10,000.

Martha Rafferty, 59, was hit with the punishment after a hearing of the Scottish Solicitors Discipline Tribunal (SSDT) in Edinburgh.

The misconduct finding follows her conviction for drink-driving for the third time in six years.

It was also the second time in four years that Rafferty has been found guilty of professional misconduct.

The SSDT last week confirmed the decision against Rafferty, who has been a lawyer for 36 years.

It said: “She was found guilty of professional misconduct, censured and fined £10,000.”

 

The SSDT declined to give details of the “complaint” against Rafferty but said a full decision will be made public on their website within two months.

Rafferty has three identical drink-driving convictions from 2012, 2014 and 2018. On each occasion, she was convicted for refusing to take a breath test. The hearing last month follows her conviction in August last year.

Rafferty, of Dowanhill, Glasgow, had pled guilty at Hamilton Sheriff Court to a charge of twice failing to provide specimens of breath to the police.

The court was told she had appeared unsteady on her feet when she was spotted by officers in the car park at the town’s police station.

In October 2018, she was handed a 10-year driving ban and ordered to do 200 hours’ unpaid work.

Rafferty declined to comment when contacted by the Sunday Mail last week.
