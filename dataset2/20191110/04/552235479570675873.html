Exasperated cop on Barra in plea to stop drink-driving becoming the 'norm'

PC Colin Scott took to the Hebridean island's community website to warn that drink driving is not acceptable.

10 Nov 2019, 04:30 by Derek Alexander, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/derek-alexander/

It’s enough to drive road safety campaigners to despair.

But police on one of Scotland’s most remote islands have gone to the lengths of issuing an online appeal to locals to stop getting behind the wheel drunk.

Local bobby Colin Scott made the call amid claims it has become “the norm” for some residents to drink-drive.

The astonishing plea was posted on the island’s community website Guth Bharraidh – Scots Gaelic for Voice of Barra.

It read: “It has become apparent that certain individuals see mixing alcohol consumption with driving a motor vehicle as the ‘norm’ or acceptable.

“PC Scott couldn’t disagree more. Your licence, job, liberty, livelihood, community respect, personal safety and the safety of others – these are a few things that could be lost through driving under the influence.

“The legal limit in Scotland is 22mcg of alcohol in 100ml of breath and there is no sure-fire way to ensure how much one drink will impact on each individual’s body until it’s too late. The only way to ensure complete safety is not to drink drive.”

 

It adds: “If you know someone drink-driving, please inform local police. This can be done anonymously and should not be seen as ‘grassing’ but, in fact, protecting one another.”

Campaigners hit out at the soft approach to drink-driving, which was linked to 30 fatalities on Scottish roads in 2016, according to Transport Scotland figures.

IAM RoadSmart said: “On a small Scottish island, the relationship between the residents and local police is often very different from on the mainland. However, drink-driving is a crime no matter where it takes place. Police resources may be limited on Barra but the whole community should be working together to ensure its roads are safe for everyone.”

Scottish Tory shadow justice secretary Liam Kerr said: “I understand police have to occasionally try something different but a simple social media plea is not going to cut this level of crime.”

The island has a population of just over 1000 people.

Barra’s SNP councillor Donald Manford said: “Messages similar to this are not uncommon in remote communities. I’m not aware of any change in circumstances but there’s always a level of concern that varies.”

Police declined to comment.
