Man Utd 'weighing up £70m transfer return' of Arsenal summer target Wilfried Zaha

United are reportedly leading the race to sign their former player Zaha, who was linked with Arsenal move all summer

10 Nov 2019, 04:00 by Gareth Bicknell

Manchester United are reportedly planning a summer swoop for Wilfried Zaha - and will hope to succeed where Arsenal failed.

The Gunners were linked with Zaha all summer, but Crystal Palace refused to sell their star man.

Now United - who sold Zaha to Palace for £3.4million in 2015, are hoping they can bring the Ivory Coast international back to Old Trafford.

That's according to The Sun , who report that United are leading the race to sign the 27-year-old.

They will have to shell out for the winger though, with Everton having had a £70m bid for Zaha turned down in the summer.

Zaha subsequently handed in a transfer request, furious at seeing what he believed was a "reasonable offer" turned down.

Palace were unwilling to sell just 48 hours before the close of the window, however.

The winger had been Arsenal's top target throughout the summer, but they eventually signed Nicolas Pepe from Lille instead for £72m.

Zaha was Sir Alex Ferguson's final signing as United boss in 2013, joining from Palace for £10.8m.

But he endured a frustrating spell with the Red Devils and was sold back to Palace two years later.

However, the Sun reports that United believe an older and wiser Zaha can succeed in a second spell at Old Trafford.

Ole Gunnar Solskjaer's side signed right-back Aaron Wan-Bissaka from Palace for £50m during the summer.

Zaha would add pace and creativity to an attack that badly needs it, with United struggling in 14th place in the Premier League ahead of their clash at home to Brighton on Sunday.

United have won just three league games this season and are five points above the relegation zone.

They lost their last Premier League outing 1-0 away to Bournemouth last weekend, although since then they have beaten Partizan Belgrade 3-0 at Old Trafford to secure a place in the last 32 of the Europa League.
