Home and Away actor who suffered 'excruciating' rape aged 10 confronts abuser in court

WARNING: UPSETTING CONTENT - Bryan Wiseman suffers from chronic depression and PTSD 40 years after the repeated sexual abuse. The former actor said the incident left him in 'excruciating pain'.

10 Nov 2019, 00:56 by Tiffany Lo

A former Home and Away actor has told of the 'mental hell' he endured being raped as a child 40 years ago.

Trevor Allan Dale, 60, was sentenced on Friday to a minimum of five years and eight months in prison for raping former soap star Bryan Wiseman.

The brutal attack left the then-10-year-old bleeding and in 'excruciating' pain.

Bryan was repeatedly abused by Dale at his family home in Sylvania, southern Sydney in Australia in 1980.

The abuse started when he was cornered behind a granny flat when they played in the family's yard.

He said he got in trouble after the assault because his father thought damage to the granny flat's fibro wall was caused by his soccer ball.

In a victim impact statement, Bryan told the New South Wales District Court: "I have been a prisoner to what Trevor Dale has done to me for 40 years and there is no sentence that can make up for the mental hell that I have endured."

Bryan also said he was in year seven when he tried to warn a child living on Dale's street about the paedophile.

But it led to an onslaught of 'horrendous teasing and bullying' at school.

He revealed: "I was called names such as 'c***sucker...this teasing would happen on a daily basis.

The 50-year-old actor has been diagnosed with chronic depression and post-traumatic stress disorder.

"During my really bad times over the years I have even planned my own suicide - and planned how I would kill myself so as not to be found," he explained.

"I didn't want anyone at my funeral as I did not feel worthy enough."

It was only in 2017 Bryan went to New South Wales Police, which sparked a two-year investigation.

Dale was caught after police taped a phone call between Bryan and Dale, in which Dale told him the abuse 'was a bit of fun'.

Judge Siobhan Herbert said in court there'd been 'zero evidence of remorse'. Dale pleaded guilty to the rape and indecent assault and two other counts of indecent assault.

Bryan rose to fame as Dr John Wilson in Australian TV series 'Home and Away' - starring between 1998 and 2003.
