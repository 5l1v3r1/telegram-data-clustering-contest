Holiday Gift Guide 2019: The Best Travel Gifts For Badass Feminists

10 Nov 2019 by Lois Alter Mark

Women have had enough.They have found their voices and, as they travel the globe, they are proudly standing up for their rights.

These gifts will empower them as they make their way and, because women tend to take care of the world, many of them also give back to important causes with each purchase.

As Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie said, “We should all be feminists.” Sure, feminists experience delays and lost luggage and reclining passengers who end up in their lap, too; nevertheless, they persist.

It’s a spotted shirt, it’s a leopard print, it’s … RBG?! As subtle as the Notorious one herself, this great-looking shirt quietly makes a powerful statement. It’s soft and stretchy and super-comfy – would you expect anything else? – and it’s so empowering to surround yourself with little Ruth Bader Ginsbergs. Don’t like the snacks they’re offering in coach? Dissent! Price: $78

Joanna Taylor rescues fabric remnants and transforms them into meaningful personal talismans that make great traveling companions. Each one-of-a-kind cuff bracelet is stitched by hand, and provides strength and comfort every time you look at it. Because these conversation pieces are so soft and comfortable, you’ll want them for your every mood. To encourage you to use your voice, Taylor will customize the text so you can express yourself fully. Price: $48  

Show her you appreciate her with this luxurious cashmere travel set from feminist favorite, Lingua Franca.

Each set comes with a cozy blanket, socks and an eye mask hand-embroidered with “feminist.” It all comes in a hand-embroidered travel bag that lets her seatmates know not to mess around because she will ultimately win the armrest battle. Price: $425

Save them the trouble by carrying this stand-out tote that definitely sends a message. Big enough to fit a 13 inch laptop, it’s sturdy and machine washable and has enough pockets to fit everything you need. In fact, it’s so practical (and charitable, with your purchase helping to support Planned Parenthood), it just may make you smile. Price: $50

Owned by a self-proclaimed “nasty woman,” ModSock is the place to buy amazing socks whose hell-yeah messages are inspiring.

ModSock is all about girl power, and just knowing you are wearing some pretty inspiring women – Frida, Rosie, Michelle – on your feet will help you hold your head a little higher and get you through the toughest challenges. The ones with the curses are especially confidence-building and just may make the man-spreader next to you slink back into his own space. Price: From $8

Ask yourself what 86 year old Ruth Bader Ginsburg would do, and stay hydrated. This stainless steel vacuum insulated water bottle from the people who coordinated hundreds to plank like RBG on the steps of the Supreme Court holds 17 ounces and helps support Time’s Up Legal Defense Fund. You just may set your own precedent by carrying around this environmentally-friendly vessel. Price: $26

Feminists get stuff done, and they’re always multi-tasking, just like this great-looking toothbrush.

Made from bamboo, it’s lightweight and compostable, as is the cotton linen bag that keeps it protected during travel. The handcrafted handle has red bristles and a painted red bottom to prevent water from pooling and to signify that 10% of sales go towards protecting women’s rights and reproductive health. This should provide enough incentive to keep brushing. Price: $12

Given the right tools, women can fix anything. And the Swiss Champ is the right tool; in fact, it’s 33 of them in one.

Sure, it’s got tweezers and a nail file but it also has a wood saw, a screwdriver and a wire cutter. This beauty belong in every woman’s purse, checked bag or glove compartment. Price: $80

Made by artisans in Venezuela, this statement pouch holds your TSA-sized toiletries while helping to fight against hunger and child malnutrition. The vibrant color makes it easy to find in your carry-on after a red-eye flight so you can arrive fresh and ready to fight the patriarchy. Price: $30
