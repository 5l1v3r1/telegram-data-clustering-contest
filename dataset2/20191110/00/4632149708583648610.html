Bengaluru keeps its calm

10 Nov 2019, 00:30 by Bangalore Mirror Bureau

Security reinforcements, route marches by various forces help keep city peaceful

Tight police security arrangements in view of the Supreme Court’s judgement on the Ayodhya dispute on Saturday ensured that there were no untoward incidents in the city. Since Friday night, the city police were on regular route march for area domination to instil confidence in the public.

Section 144 was imposed across the city along with the deployment of 50 KSRP platoons, 30 City Armed Reserves, one CRPF platoon, 8,000 policemen, 1,200 home guards. The judges residential complex located at National Games Village in Koramangala also had elaborate security arrangements.

As many as 18 DCPs were on regular rounds to ensure that the city was peaceful. Along with them, 34 ACPs were with the forces to ensure that the city remained peaceful.

Along with the route march, the police also made vehicle route march for area domination. Areas like KG Halli, DJ Halli, Hennur, Shivajinagar, Frazer Town, Bharathinagar had more police deployment as they were sensitive areas.

Liquor ban was also imposed across the city.

Verdict welcomed

Leaders cutting across the partylines have welcomed the Supreme Court verdict.

Chief Minister BS Yediyurappa tweeted soon after the verdict and termed it as historic judgement. “Let us not see this verdict as victory of some or a defeat of others. Ensure that the social harmony is not disturbed,’’ he tweeted.

Former Chief Ministers HD Kumaraswamy and Sidda-ramaiaha too appealed people to respect the court’s verdict on Ayodhya. “Let us all respect the verdict of Rama’s birth dispute. Let us follow the ideology of this country which advocated peace and non-violence. Let us all live in harmony as usual and focus on development,” Kumarasawamy said, while stressing that there was no bigger God than the farmer who feeds people.

“It is the duty of all of us to respect the highest court of law. Each & everyone should abide by the Constitutional values of secularism & sense of brotherhood to ensure Peace & Harmony,” tweeted Siddaramaiah.

KPCC president Dinesh Gundurao said that the Supreme Court’s decision has further sterngthened the coutry’s secular values. “We have a constitution that protects all religions, all religions are united,” he expressing satisfaction over the SC resolving the more than a century-old issue.

Meanwhile, Karnataka Home Minister Basavaraj Bommai has said that the high alert would continue for the next two days across the State. The decision to keep a close vigil has been taken to avoid any untoward incidents in the next 48 hours.
