Boxer has lie down mid-fight and throws punches after bell in KSI vs Logan Paul undercard farce

Nikita Ababiy's fight with Jonathan Batista was stopped at the end of the first round after several minutes of chaos

10 Nov 2019, 05:08 by Rich Jones, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/rich-jones/

The final fight on the undercard between KSI and Logan Paul descended into farce as Jonathan Batista was disqualified at the end of the first round.

Dominican journeyman Batista was clipped on the back of the head by a shot by blue chip prospect Nikita Ababiy, who had scored a knockdown early in the fight.

Batista made the most of the blow by collapsing to the canvas and refusing to stand for several moments, prompting the doctor to enter the ring.

He eventually got up and continued, with Ababiy deducted a point for the illegal shot.

And with a new lease of life, Batista went for the jugular in the closing stages of the round only to continue his assault after the bell as the referee stepped in.

He was rightly disqualified by the referee after a bizarre series of events in the final clash before KSI and Logan Paul's long-awaited rematch.

Favourite Ababiy emerged with his eighth win from his eighth professional fight to continue the perfect start to his professional career.

But it was not the evening "White Chocolate" envisioned as Batista created a bizarre fight with a strange ending.

Sky Sports pundit and former world champion Johnny Nelson declared Batista "just didn't want to know" as he discussed a frustrating turn of events for Ababiy.

"He didn't want to know, that's it, Batista just didn't want to know," Nelson said.

"I think after the referee had split them, he wanted a way out, it's as simple as that.

"It will be frustrating because he wants to impress, he wants to show off, he wants to get the big knockout and it just didn't happen.

"He's fortunate not to get hit clean and get hurt."
