Who won KSI vs Logan Paul? Fight result as YouTube stars clash in LA

The two YouTube superstars met for a second time at the Staples Center after their draw in the first fight last year

10 Nov 2019, 05:47 by Rich Jones, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/rich-jones/

KSI edged out Logan Paul by split decision in their huge rematch in Los Angeles.

The YouTube superstars fought a dramatic second bout - but it was the Brit who was handed the decision by the narrowest of margins.

After a majority draw in Manchester last year, KSI - real name Olajide William Olatunji - was given the verdict.

Two scorecards scored it in his favour with scores of 57-54 and 56-55, whilst Logan Paul was handed a 56-55 verdict by the third judge.

KSI dominated the first three rounds and recovered from a frantic fourth round which saw him knocked down by an uppercut but Paul deducted two points for a shot as he was heading to the ground.

The highly-anticipated fight got off to a scrappy start with both fighters getting tangled up and the referee stepping in to clean things up several times.

Few shots were landing cleanly with KSI swinging wildly with the overhand right, although he did strike a decent shot in the second round as he fought on the front foot.

Logan Paul was twice on the canvas after losing his footing in round three, although neither were scored as knockdowns, as KSI continued to dominate the early exchanges.

The American responded with a good right hand early in the fourth round as drama ensued later in the round.

A vicious upper cut knocked KSI down, but Paul struck him with a blow to the back of the head as he was heading down leading to him being deducted two points by referee Jack Reese.

The deduction left Paul needing a stoppage but also had KSI struggling for balance and appearing tired entering the last two rounds.

Paul probably edged the closing stages, but KSI had done enough early on, with the two deducted points in the bizarre fourth round proving vital.

On the undercard, Billy Joe Saunders knocked out Marcelo Esteban Coceres in round 11 to defend his WBO super-middleweight world title whilst Devin Haney outclassed Alfredo Santiago to defend his WBC world lightweight title.
