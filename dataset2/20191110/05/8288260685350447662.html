The blind Jack Russell and other dogs in desperate need of a new home - can you help?

Raven's Rescue is looking for new homes for Bruno and his four-legged pals Bean, Logan, Rai and Imani

10 Nov 2019, 05:30 by Kelly Ashmore, https://www.facebook.com/dudleybclive/

Bruno is a blind Jack Russell in urgent need of a loving home.

The 12-year-old pooch is just one of the many unwanted hounds saved by Raven’s Rescue, a Dudley-based charity set up in 2012 to provide sanctuary to abandoned and abused animals.

Unlike some rescues, it doesn’t use kennels, preferring to place dogs into foster homes until they land on their paws and find a forever home.

And the charity is desperate to find the 'pawfect' place for Bruno to spend his later years.

Bruno doesn't let his poor eyesight get him down and he is described as “a happy chap” who loves to be around people.

He can see slightly, so is able to negotiate his way around the house without bumping into things.

And although he gets on well with other dogs, he does require a quiet home - could you help? 

The charity is also looking for new family's for Bruno's four-legged friends Bean, Logan, Rai and Imani.

All of the animals in the charity's care receive appropriate medical attention and are vaccinated, micro-chipped, neutered, behaviourally assessed and treated for parasites before re-homing. 

Home-checks and adoption fees apply.

If you think you could give one of these pooches a home, call Sarah on 07922 204 531, Ryan on 07766 466 761 or visit ravensrescueuk.weebly.com.

Bean

Bean is a two-year-old Mastiff who absolutely loves people and cuddles.

Due to her size, she'd need to be the only dog in a household with no young children.

She's described as a "super-friendly girl" who loves getting lots of exercise - as can be seen in the video above.

Logan

Logan is a very nervous boy who needs someone he can learn to trust and love.

The 10-year-old is in desperate need of an extremely patient owner who will let him come to them in his own time.

He is describe as “very sweet” and is good with dogs and cats, however because of his nervous nature, he cannot be rehomed with children.

Rai

Rai is a one-year-old Staffordshire bull terrier cross.

The youngster loves nothing more than long walks followed by plenty of cuddles.

She’s extremely friendly and loves everyone she meets.

However Rai needs to be in a home where she is the only pet and there are no young children.

Imani

Stunning Imani is now ready to put her paws up in her forever home. 

The German Shepherd needs to be the only pet in an adult-only household, with someone who has experience with the breed.

She’s initially shy and nervous when she meets new people but after a few treats she’s your best friend.

Imani will make the perfect companion for an active person who enjoys walks.

She’s approximately two-years-old.

Animal lovers living in and around Dudley are also urged to get in touch with Raven's Rescue if they would consider fostering a dog or cat while they wait for their forever homes.

The charity will supply everything you need to make the four-legged friend at home, cover all vet bills and provide support 24/7 for the duration of their stay.
