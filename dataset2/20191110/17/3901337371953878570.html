Leopards Humble CT City At Thohoyandou

10 Nov 2019, 17:21 by Soccer Laduma

Absa Premiership

Result: Black Leopards 2-1 Cape Town City

Date: 10 November 2019

Venue: Thohoyandou Stadium

Black Leopards got back on the winning trail in the Absa Premiership after defeating Cape Town City 2-1 at Thohoyandou Stadium on Sunday afternoon.

View The PSL Table

The Citizens made one change from the side that defeated Polokwane City on Wednesday as Thato Mokeke was brought back into the side, with Roland Putsche dropping to the bench.

The visitors got off to a blistering start midweek, but it was Leopards who got the opening goal in the 18th minute, with Robert N'Gambi nodding past Sage Stephens.

City looked to get themselves level and they were nearly rewarded after 27 minutes as Edmilson Dove's deflected effort forced Leopards goalkeeper Jonas Mendes into a superb save.

Stephens was called upon at the other end 10 minutes before the halftime break, with the City shot-stopper producing a fine save to deny the home side a second goal as the encounter remained 1-0 at the interval.

However, the Citizens were back on level terms five minutes after the halftime break as Surprise Ralani found the back of the net with a clinical finish from distance for his first league goal of the season.

Leopards coach Luc Eymael responded with a change on the hour mark, with Joseph Mlhongo making way for Themba Ndlovu just moments after Mwape Musonda squandered an opening for the hosts.

The home side had to wait until the 83rd minute to restore their advantage as Musonda turned provider to play in Lesedi Kapinga, who calmly slotted past Stephens.

That proved to be the winning goal as Leopards move up to 11th spot, while City head into the international break in 13th position.
