Man Utd player ratings from Brighton win as Marcus Rashford shines at Old Trafford

Manchester United made light work of Brighton as Andreas Pereira's opener, a Davy Propper own goal and Marcus Rashford's strike sealed a 3-1 win over Brighton

10 Nov 2019, 15:55 by David McDonnell, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/david-mcdonnell/

Manchester United bounced back from their Bournemouth humiliation last weekend with a comfortable 3-1 Premier League win over Brighton on Sunday. 

Andreas Pereira opened the scoring after good work from Anthony Martial, with the Brazil international's clipped effort taking a huge deflection off Dale Stephens to leave the helpless Mat Ryan with no chance.

Two minutes later and United's lead was doubled when another fortunate goal saw Davy Propper bundle into his own net after pressure from Scott McTominay, with VAR causing a huge delay after Harry Maguire was accused of handling the ball in the build up.

The visitors pulled a goal back in the second half when Lewis Dunk powered home a header but, within two minutes again, United had restored their two-goal lead when Marcus Rashford scored a cracker.

Here are how United's players rated...

 

David de Gea 7

Little to do between the posts but produced fine reaction save at close-range from Maupay in the second-half.

Aaron Wan-Bissaka 7

Defensively solid and a constant threat down the right going forward. Linked well with James.

Victor Lindelof 6

Shaky at the start, losing possession cheaply, but improved thereafter and rarely troubled.

 

Harry Maguire 7

United's skipper led by example. Strong in the air, anticipated danger and assured bringing the ball out.

Brandon Williams 8

Strong and committed throughout on first Premier League start. Denied a goal by Ryan.

Scott McTominay 7

Looked to have bundled ball over the line to make it 2-0, although given as an own goal.

 

Fred 7

Snapped into tackles early on and made some key interceptions. Growing in influence.

Andreas Pereira 7

His deflected shot opened the scoring for United after 17 minutes after key role in build-up.

Daniel James 8

Work-rate as impressive as his threat going forward. Denied goal on his birthday by Ryan.

 

Marcus Rashford 9 MOTM

Scored United's third off underside of bar, then missed open goal moments later. Booked.

Anthony Martial 8

Showed great persistence and awareness to set up Rashford for United's third goal.
