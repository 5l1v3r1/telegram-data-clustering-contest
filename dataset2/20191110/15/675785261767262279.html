Outrage after vandals spray paint cars with swastikas on Remembrance Sunday

The disturbing graffiti was discovered in the Ferrymead area of Canvey Island, Essex - an area known for its Jewish links

10 Nov 2019, 15:09 by Brendan McFadden, Joe Davies

Shocked residents in a community known for its Jewish links woke today on Remembrance Day to find sick vandals had spray painted cars and garages with swastikas and antisemitic messages.

The disturbing graffiti, which included phallic symbols, was discovered in Canvey Island, Essex, an area that is though to be home to around 60 orthodox Jewish families.

It is believed to have been painted late last night and in the early hours of the morning, Essex Live reports.

Holly Roache, who lives on Ferrymead, took a photo of her neighbour's car which was subjected to vandalism.

She said: "There’s a car at the entrance of our road that’s been targeted and also in other areas."

 

The swastika is a symbol of white supremacy and was the icon of Hitler's Nazi Party in Germany during WW2, to symbolise German nationalistic pride.

An Essex Police spokesperson said: "We are investigating after several reports of criminal damage to cars in Ferrymead, Canvey Island overnight.

"We were called this morning, Sunday 10 November, to reports that vehicles had been sprayed with racist and anti-Semitic graffiti.

"If you have not yet spoken to us about damage to your vehicle please call 101 and quote incident number 307 of today's date."

Many Jewish people moved to Canvey Island in search of a new life and cheaper housing, according to The Guardian.

The Queen joined senior royals and politicians to honour the war dead this morning at a service at the Cenotaph memorial on Whitehall in London.

It was among hundreds of ceremonies held across the country to remember the war dead.
