Neil Lennon issues Celtic, Rangers and Scotland fixture plea to SPFL

The Hoops would welcome a meeting with league bosses over fixture congestion.

10 Nov 2019, 11:41 by Liam Bryce, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/liam-bryce/

Neil Lennon wants the SPFL to aid Celtic, Rangers and the national team in their quest to improve the reputation of Scottish football.

The Hoops boss would welcome talks with the league's governing body over easing fixture congestion as he and counterpart Steven Gerrard strive for Europa League success.

But it's Scotland's plight Lennon feels is most urgent, with boss Steve Clarke preparing for Euro 2020 play-offs in March.

Lennon, whose side's European adventure will carry on past Christmas, believes the SPFL should bow to Clarke's every wish.

Asked if he would be interested in a meeting aimed at giving all three teams optimum opportunity, Lennon replied: "Yes. Predominantly for the national team more than anything else.

“Steve should get what he wants because the national team is a priority and these playoffs coming up are probably the most important games Scotland have had for a long time, so whatever he needs the authorities should give him what he wants, if it's the best for the national team for those games.”

Celtic and Rangers' involvement in continental competition ensures regular fixtures on Thursdays and Sundays, with TV companies having a major say.

Despite handling the schedule impressively so far, Lennon fears the heavy workload will catch up with his players eventually.

And, with Scotland's improving UEFA coefficient potentially yielded a second Champions League place, he believes more can be done to help.

Lennon added: "You are battling with the TV companies as well because they want the early kick-offs.

“You are playing on a Thursday night and then you are in Aberdeen on Sunday for a 12.15 kick-off. Yeah we played well, got a great result, but it's still the players who have to go out and do the work and the fatigue or injury can cost you going forward.

“Sometimes, we need a little bit of help on that, there's no question of that.”
