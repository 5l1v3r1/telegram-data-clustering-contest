Fernando Ricksen thanks 'Simply the Best' Rangers fans in tear-jerking final message

The Ibrox hero thanked the football world for their 'amazing support' and revealed his proudest moment for the Light Blues in a foreward to a new book just before his death.

10 Nov 2019, 11:33 by Nicholas Keyden, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/nicholas-keyden/

Fernando Ricksen paid tribute to Rangers supports he called "Simply the Best" in a tear-jerking final message before his tragic death.

Writing in a new book about famous moments in the club's history, the 44-year-old thanked the football world for their "amazing" support through his battle with Motor Neurone Disease.

The Dutch legend bravely fought the condition for six years before passing away at an Airdrie hospice on September 18.

He penned the poignant foreword for '10 matches 10 players' book in August, just weeks before his death using a special keypad which tracks eye movements.

Fernando said: "The support from the football world to me has been amazing.

“And I am continuously reminded how lucky I was to play for Rangers.

“Rangers are a very special club and the fans are Simply the Best.”

 

The book lists 10 of the Ibrox side's greatest achievements described by some of its most recognised players including Fernando's pal Nacho Novo, Berry Ferguson and Andy Goram.

It includes Rangers' thrilling final day of the season league win in 2005 dubbed Helicopter Sunday.

A chopper ferrying the trophy to be presented to Celtic at Motherwell had a last minute change of direction to Edinburgh after the Light Blues last-gasp winner beat Hibs 1-0 and clinch the title.

A beaming Fernando was the man to lift the trophy aloft in celebration in what he called his proudest moment as a player.

He added: "I had many highlights in my time at Ibrox and I’m not surprised to see Helicopter Sunday in the 10 chosen. It was a special day for all of us.

"I’m now spending my time in peaceful surroundings.

“Days can be long and predictable but I gain great satisfaction from reflecting on my football career."

 

Fernando's inclusion in the book came after he met author Stuart Thomson at a charity do in London back when he was "still able to talk".
