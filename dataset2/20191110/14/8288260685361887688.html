Police still hunting Amos Price over sickening Pelsall dog attack which left cat dead

Detectives want to question Amos Price after a lurcher-type was let loose to savage a pet cat in Woodlands Crescent, Pelsall, on October 23

10 Nov 2019, 14:40 by Kelly Ashmore, https://www.facebook.com/dudleybclive/

Police are still hunting the sick thug who allowed his dog to maul a helpless cat to death.

Detectives want to question Amos Price after a lurcher-type was let loose to savage a pet cat in Woodlands Crescent, Pelsall, on October 23. 

The horrifying attack - which has sparked national outcry - was captured on CCTV and reported to police and the RSPCA.

However despite releasing a mug shot of Price and appealing for the public's help to trace him, West Midlands Police officers are yet find the 30-year-old.

The distressing footage - which can be viewed above - shows a man provoking the dog to attack the cat, called Cleo.

He can be seen grabbing the dog by the collar and lifting it up to ensure it is aware of the cat, which was sat on a wall.

Cleo was then dragged from the wall and onto the ground, where the dog sunk its jaws into her, shaking her from side to side before disappearing from shot.

Heartbroken Gary Truefitt only learned of his beloved pet's death after he reviewed CCTV footage when Cleo - who he had owned for eight years - didn't return home.

He said: “I noticed my gate was left open, which was suspicious, so I decided to check the CCTV and was completely shocked by what I found. I was very upset, I went to my neighbour's house as I didn’t know what to do.

 

“Cleo will be very much missed, she was a daft cat in lots of ways and would lie along the driveway, even when there were cars about but she was a companion to me. It’s just awful to think of her being attacked in this way.”

The killing has been branded a "horrifying act of cruelty" by Chief Superintendent Andy Parsons, from Walsall Police as he urged anyone with information about Price's whereabouts to come forward.

He said: “This is a horrifying act of cruelty which has left us utterly disgusted.

 

“I’m aware of the appalling CCTV that captured this shocking behaviour which, like the rest of the community, I wholly condemn.

“I realise this is highly emotive but I’d urge people to remain calm and help us by coming forward with information on his whereabouts." 

Anyone with any information about the whereabouts of Price, who has not already spoken to police, is urged to get in touch via Live Chat at  www.west-midlands.police.uk  between 8am and midnight.

Alternatively people can call 101 anytime or contact Crimestoppers, anonymously, on 0800 555 111.
