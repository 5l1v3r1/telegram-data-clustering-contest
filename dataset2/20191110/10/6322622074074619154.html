MH360 turned back in mid-air because of landing gear issue -- Malaysia Airlines

10 Nov 2019, 10:30 by Bernama

PUTRAJAYA (Nov 10): The Malaysia Airlines flight MH360, which departed from Kuala Lumpur to Beijing yesterday evening, turned back in mid-air due to a landing gear issue.

In a statement today, Malaysia Airlines said the flight's pilot made a decision to return to Kuala Lumpur International Airport (KLIA) in the interest of passenger safety.

“The aircraft landed safely at 9.03pm on the same day and [the issue] is currently being rectified by engineers.

"The [new] flight MH360,serviced by another aircraft, departed Kuala Lumpur at 12.30 am on Nov 10 and arrived at Beijing at 6.35am on the same day,” the airline said.

Malaysia Airlines added refreshments were provided to all passengers and all onward connections were arranged.

“Safety is Malaysia Airlines’ number one priority at all times and it regrets the inconvenience caused to its passengers,” it added.

It was reported that flight MH360 departed KLIA at 6pm yesterday and was scheduled to arrive in Beijing, China at 12.20 am today.
