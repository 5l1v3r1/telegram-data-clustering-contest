Danny Mayor returns for Plymouth Argyle in FA Cup win but where does that leave George Cooper?

THE VERDICT: Mayor started the victory over Bolton Wanderers but Cooper was on the bench

10 Nov 2019, 10:01 by Chris Errington

Midfield playmaker Danny Mayor was back in the fold for Plymouth Argyle as they beat Bolton Wanderers in the FA Cup.

But his return meant that George Cooper was left out for the first round tie at the University of Bolton Stadium.

Cooper, who was Plymouth Live’s Argyle player-of-the-month for October, was an unused substitute for the 1-0 win.

It was good to see after the game that Cooper took to social media to praise his team-mates for their ‘great performance’ and add that he was ‘buzzing for the fans’.

However, the loan signing from Peterborough United must have been disappointed not to have featured in the tie.

A valid question to ask now is whether Pilgrims’ boss Ryan Lowe can find a way to accommodate two such talented players as Mayor and Cooper in the same side?

On their day, both are capable of being matchwinners, as they have already proven this season.

If Argyle are to start pushing up the League Two table towards the play-off positions in the coming weeks they will need the quality that Mayor and Cooper can bring.

It is a case of how Lowe fits them into the same team and there is no obvious solution.

Mayor had two games as a substitute after serving a one-match suspension when the Pilgrims beat Carlisle United 2-0 at Home Park on October 19.

Now refreshed and restored to the starting line-up, he will surely be one of the first names on the teamsheet every week.

While he has perhaps not hit the heights that some expected of him, the 28-year-old has still been a key part of their attacking play.
