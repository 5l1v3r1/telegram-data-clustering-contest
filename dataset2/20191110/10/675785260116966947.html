David Walliams 'will not go to Simon Cowell’s birthday party if Piers Morgan is there'

The Little Britain star is reportedly doing everything he can to avoid Piers Morgan after the newsman called him a 'snivelling toady'

10 Nov 2019, 10:03 by Tiffany Wallis

Simon Cowell has found himself in a tricky situation after David Walliams reportedly refused to go his birthday party if Piers Morgan is going to be there.

The music mogul abruptly cancelled his lavish 60th celebrations just days before the bash was due to take place last month and is yet to finalise a new date.

According to a new report, Simon is now facing a guest list dilemma as well as logistical issues.

Comedian David, 48, is said to have refused to attend the rearranged party unless Good Morning Britain host Piers' invitation is withdrawn.

A source told The Sun : "David refuses to be in the same room as his nemesis, and has told Simon it’s him or Piers.

"Poor Simon is stuck between a rock and a hard place. Piers and David may be ITV colleagues, but once the cameras stop rolling they cannot abide one another."

The source compared their feud to "open warfare" and hinted one of the British telly legends might have a meltdown if they both attended Simon's party.

The insider went on to reveal why the two stars appear to hate one another.

They said: "David hates Piers’ tabloid newspaper background, and is fairly snobby about it, whilst Piers thinks David is a bit of a duplicitous d**k - nice to his face, and snide behind it."

Last week claims suggested Simon was forced to postpone the milestone bash due to scheduling conflicts.

According to Mail Online , the TV star had celebrity guests flying in from all over the world specifically for the party.

However, Simon and his partner Lauren Silverman sent out cancellations just days before the birthday extravaganza was due to take place.

Piers and David have been embroiled in a long-running feud for years following a number of public spats.

Just last month, Piers slammed David online by calling him a "snivelling toady" after the BGT judge liked tweets slating the GMB presenter.

He wrote: "Amusing that @davidwalliams regularly ‘likes’ the nastiest tweets about me, yet is such a snivelling toady to my face."

Last year the controversial telly host called for the comedian to lose his spot on the BGT panel as he plotted his return after leaving the show in 2011.

Back in 2017, David refused an interview with Piers for his series Life Stories, opting instead to feature in a sit down chat with John Bishop’s series, A Conversation With.
