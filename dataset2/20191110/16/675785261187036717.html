Boy, 6, thrown 98ft from Tate viewing platform 'able to go outside again'

The six-year-old French boy who plunged 30m from the London gallery's viewing platform is making good progress as Jonty Bravery awaits trial for attempted murder

10 Nov 2019, 16:28 by Tom Pilgrim

The six-year old seriously injured after allegedly being thrown from the viewing platform of the Tate Modern art gallery in London is now able to venture outside, his family have confirmed.

The French youngster, who cannot be named because of his age, has also regained some movement in his legs as he continues to recover from the fall three months on.

He plunged 98-feet from the art gallery’s 10th floor on to a fifth-floor roof in August.

In a post on a GoFundMe page, which has raised more than £119,000 to support the boy's rehabilitation, his family said their "little knight" was making progress.

They said: "We would like to share with you some good news of our little knight.

"He is making lots of progresses. We can now bring him outside in a wheelchair to breath fresh air.

"It's tiring for him but he really likes it, of course.

"He's very smiling and he laughs a lot again. He begins to move his legs since a few days, just a tiny little bit but it is really wonderful to see...!"

"And just since today, he manages to make real kisses.

"It warms our hearts so much... Thank you all of you. We hope we will give you other good news as soon as possible."

Last month, the family revealed the six-year-old had been moved out of intensive care into a rehabilitation centre with an "armour of splints" keeping many of his limbs in place as he heals.

At the time the family announced: "He is now in a rehabilitation centre.

"He still has some metal in his body but instead of plasters he has got a full armour of splints: legs, feet, hands, arms, neck and torso.

"It is impressive - he looks like a little knight, and we can take these splints off sometimes.

"He also makes some little progresses - he moves his right hand more and more and arm on command (he catches us most of the time when we are nearby!) even if he feels sometimes a little bit sad when he doesn't manage to do what he tries."

Jonty Bravery, 18, is accused of attempted murder after allegedly throwing the boy from the London tourist attraction on August 4.

Bravery, from West London, is due to appear in court for a plea hearing next month.
