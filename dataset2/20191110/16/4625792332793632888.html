CNN's Stelter continues network on-air blackout on ABC News not running Epstein story

10 Nov 2019, 16:45 by Mike Brest

CNN's Brian Stelter failed to mention the bombshell news that ABC News declined to run a story on Jeffrey Epstein during the first episode of his Sunday show Reliable Sources since the story broke.

On Tuesday, Project Veritas released a video of ABC anchor Amy Robach discussing on a hot mic back in August the information she received years earlier from an interview with Virginia Roberts Giuffre. She can be heard expressing her frustration surrounding ABC News's refusal to air the interview and claimed the network made the decision after receiving threats from Buckingham Palace after it had found out Prince Andrew was implicated in the story.

Stelter, CNN's chief media correspondent, has referenced the scandal in his nightly newsletter multiple times this week. But, his failure to cover the story during his show continues the network's decision not to mention it on-air.

Instead, Stelter spent multiple segments on his show discussing the Democrats’ impeachment inquiry into President Trump and the role the media will play in the House’s proceedings. He also had former White House former communications director Anthony Scaramucci on his show.

CNN has yet to cover the video on-air since it was released, according to a review of their footage using Grabien, a television clipping service. After failing to cover the story in the first 24 hours after it broke, a CNN spokesperson pointed the Washington Examiner to Stelter's newsletter.

After the video of Robach was released, both she and ABC News issued similar statements disregarding the video and explaining that her interview with Giuffre "didn't air because we could not obtain sufficient corroborating evidence to meet ABC's editorial standards about her allegations."

The woman who recorded the video, Ashley Bianco, was an employee at ABC News but had started a job at CBS News in the days before Project Veritas released the video. CBS News ultimately fired her after ABC News was able to confirm that she was the person who recorded the video. However, she denied being the person to leak it in a Friday interview with Megyn Kelly.

Last month, Project Veritas released another uncover video, but CNN was the target. Cary Poarch, who claimed he was a satellite uplink technician at CNN’s Washington Bureau, recorded videos meant to show network President Jeff Zucker with an anti-Trump bias.
