Watch brave Perry Barr shopkeeper fight off robber as he leaps over off-licence counter

Hero Rakesh Bhardwaj had popped out the back of his shop Jazz’s Off-Licence in Perry Barr, when a man suddenly burst in and leaps over the counter

10 Nov 2019, 16:50 by birminghammail.co.uk

This is the moment a brave shop keeper tackles a would-be thief who tried to steal vodka from his off-licence.

Hero Rakesh Bhardwaj had popped out the back of his shop Jazz’s Off-Licence on Wellington Road, Perry Barr , when a man burst in, leapt over the counter and grabbed a bottle of Absolut.

Mr Bhardwaj, 57, ran to the aid of his colleague and managed to wrestle the bottle off the man and after some coaxing, marched him of the shop without injury.

Meanwhile one of Mr Bhardwaj’s friends managed to keep the thief’s accomplice from entering the shop and potentially escalating the situation.

Mr Bhardwaj said he was no hero but instinctively protecting his co-worker 59-year-old Jaswinder Kaur and his livelihood.

The father- of-two said: “I feel terrible because you come to work not to be abused and not to have violence. You do not expect to handle something like this.

“There’s a lack of policing. Hurry up Boris Johnson, 20,000 police officers, where are they? There’s hardly any police anywhere .

The drama unfolded just as he headed into the backroom after 9pm on Monday, November 4.

The proud shopkeeper, who keeps his business spotless, said: “I saw somebody in dark clothing dive over the counter and thought ‘what the hell’.

“The guy must’ve thought Jaswinder was on her own and suddenly I appear. He was holding a bottle of Absolute and she was holding that as well.

“I grabbed him too and said ‘let go of the bottle and I will let you go’ because I didn’t want a confrontation. ”

Mr Bhardwaj’s two friends emerged from the backroom and came to his aid. Meanwhile, the wanna-be thief’s friend saw what was happening to him inside the shop and tried to enter but was kept out.

“During the scuffle, his stocky mate with a red eye tried to enter and put his hood up. Luckily my friend kept the other guy outside because if he had come inside, we would have had a problem.

“Jaswinder was on the phone to the police.”

A spokeswoman from West Midlands Police said: “Officers were called to reports of an attempted theft at Jazz’s Off Licence, Wellington Road, Handsworth at just after 9pm on Monday 4 November.

“Police enquiries are ongoing.”
