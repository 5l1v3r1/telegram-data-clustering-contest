What channel is Liverpool vs Man City on? TV and live stream information

The biggest game of the Premier League season so far sees Liverpool and Man City face off in a clash that could be pivotal in the title race

10 Nov 2019, 07:20 by Sam Fletcher

Table-topping Liverpool take on Premier League champions Manchester City in what could be a deciding fixture in the race for the title.

The Reds have picked up 31 points from a possible 33, having won every match at Anfield this season.

But, one worrying statistic for Jurgen Klopp is that Liverpool have not kept a clean sheet in all competitions since the end of September, eight matches ago.

As for the visitors, they come into the match on the back of a struggling performance at home to Southampton .

Yet, only one team has been able to shut out City this season with the Sky Blues netting at least two goals in every other fixture they’ve played.

With 11 matches of the season already gone, you’d imagine it’d be one of these two lifting the Premier League trophy in May.

Here’s everything you need to know ahead of this crucial fixture…

When is the game?

The game is on Sunday 10 November.

Kick-off is set for 4:30pm.

Where is the game being played?

The game is being played at Anfield.

Liverpool have not lost a league match at home since May 2017.

What TV channel is the game on?

UK viewers will need a Sky Sports subscription to watch the match with the game being shown on both their Main Event and Premier League channels.

Coverage starts at 4pm.

Can you live stream the game?

UK subscribers to Sky Sports can stream the game through their website and the Sky Sports app.

Alternatively, you can follow all of the action on the Mirror Football live blog.

Liverpool team news

The Reds will have to do without Xherdan Shaqiri, Joel Matip and Nathaniel Clyne who all remain sidelined through injury.

Skipper Jordan Henderson missed Tuesday night’s Champions League win over Genk due to illness but is expected to be available for Sunday’s match.

Manchester City team news

Pep Guardiola’s injury woes continued on Wednesday night as Ederson was substituted at half-time of their Champions League clash with Atalanta. It’s expected he’ll miss out on Sunday.

Also out are Leroy Sane, Aymeric Laporte, Rodri, Oleksandr Zinchenko and David Silva with City needing a win to close the gap on the league leaders.
