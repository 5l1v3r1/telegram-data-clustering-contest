'I'm back,' ex-president Lula tells supporters after prison release

10 Nov 2019, 07:49 by Euronews with ReutersÂ 

"I want to tell them, I'm back," Brazil's former leader Luiz Inacio Lula told hundreds of supporters on Saturday, just one day after being freed from jail.

Lula's wide-ranging, 45-minute speech focused broadly on defeating Bolsonaro and improving the economic conditions of the working class, although he diverged frequently to talk about everything from his fiancee to solidarity with leftist governments in Bolivia and Venezuela.

Lula, who was president from 2003 to 2010, also took aim at a long list of political enemies, including Bolsonaro, Economy Minister Paulo Guedes and Justice Minister Sergio Moro, a former judge who initially ruled to convict Lula.

He said Guedes seeks to remake Brazil economically in the image of Chile, long seen as a model of financially conservative governance, but that those policies are the reason for the widespread street protests paralyzing its Latin American neighbour.

A judge ordered that Lula be freed on Friday, a day after Brazil's Supreme Court issued a broader ruling ending the mandatory imprisonment of convicted criminals after they lose their first appeal.

Corruption conviction

Lula had been imprisoned on a corruption conviction carrying a nearly nine-year sentence.

Bolsonaro told reporters in Brasilia, "Let's not give space to compromise with a convict."

Earlier on Twitter, the president called for supporters to rally around his government's agenda, which has included a severe tightening of public spending, saying that they must not allow Brazil's next phase of recovery to be derailed.

"Do not give ammunition to the scoundrel, who is momentarily free but full of guilt," Bolsonaro said.
