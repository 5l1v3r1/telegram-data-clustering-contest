Jake Paul claims KSI vs Logan Paul rematch was "rigged" in x-rated rant

Logan Paul lost to rival KSI in their grudge match - and younger brother Jake Paul claimed the sport is "rigged"

10 Nov 2019, 08:08 by Rich Jones, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/rich-jones/

Jake Paul claims the rematch between brother Logan Paul and KSI was "rigged" as he slammed boxing in an x-rated rant.

Paul was beaten by the narrowest of margins in a split decision after being deducted two points without prior warning for hitting KSI on the ground after a knockdown in round four.

That decision made all the difference when it went to the scorecards, with one of the three judges at ringside still scoring the money-spinning bout in favour of the American.

And younger brother Jake, who was sat at ringside sporting a replica WBC belt and claiming to be "WBC amateur champion" after beating KSI's younger brother on the undercard of their first clash last year, was furious with the outcome.

He accused KSI of dining with DAZN chief Joe Markowski the day before the point and claimed the sport is corrupt.

Addressing reporters in footage captured by iFL TV , Jake Paul said: "With that decision, that's just b*******.

"At the end of the day, I've been in this sport for a year now and, I probably shouldn't be saying this, but all this s*** is f****** rigged.

"You see people wining and dining each other, people at the Staples Center. Who was at the Staples Center last night with KSI? Joe Markowski.

"This sport is rigged, that's for sure.

"The short amount of time I've been here, I've heard multiple stories about people paying stuff off, choosing fights, what time people sign contracts.

"It's not an excuse, I'm just saying."

Immediately after the fight, Logan Paul declared his intention to "contest the commission" after his defeat.

"I thought it was a legitimate knockdown," Paul said. "I didn't get a warning.

"I got two points taken away from me and that's the reason I lost tonight. I would love to contest the commission because I think I should have got a warning. I don't think it was fair at all."
