Logan Paul blames KSI loss on illness and says he "thought he was going to die"

Logan Paul was beaten by KSI in their long-awaited rematch at the Staples Center in Los Angeles

10 Nov 2019, 08:47 by Rich Jones, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/rich-jones/

Beaten boxer Logan Paul blamed his defeat to KSI on illness - and claimed he "thought he was going to die".

Paul suffered a points defeat via split decision in the lucrative rematch between the YouTube stars at the Staples Center in Los Angeles.

The American was the pre-fight favourite but could not come out on top in their second encounter having drawn in Manchester as amateurs last year.

And Paul revealed he "woke up sick" and was far from at his best as a result.

Paul told BBC Sounds: "My gameplan kind of went out the window. That was the worst I've performed out of every sparring session I've had, ever.

"You can hear in my voice right now I'm sick, I'm nasally. I woke up sick, I've been sneezing all day and I tried to ignore that but it took probably 30-50% out of me.

"I don't know if I was trying to conserve energy, because I thought I was going to die because of my lungs, I don't know."

Paul was also unhappy with a crucial points deduction without prior warning which cost him victory.

He was deemed to have hit KSI on the deck after knocking him down with an uppercut in round four.

"I thought it was a legitimate knockdown," Paul said. "I didn't get a warning.

"I got two points taken away from me and that's the reason I lost tonight. I would love to contest the commission because I think I should have got a warning. I don't think it was fair at all."

His younger brother, Jake Paul, also accused boxing of being rigged in an x-rated rant.

"With that decision, that's just b*******," he said.

"At the end of the day, I've been in this sport for a year now and, I probably shouldn't be saying this, but all this s*** is f****** rigged.

"You see people wining and dining each other, people at the Staples Center. Who was at the Staples Center last night with KSI? Joe Markowski.

"This sport is rigged, that's for sure."
