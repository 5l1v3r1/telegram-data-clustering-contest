'Coming to Birmingham has been one of my best life choices' - meet the Romanian apprentice making a new life in the city

Raluca Munteanu moved to Birmingham four years ago and is now progressing in her new career with historic city manufacturer RH Nuttall

14 Nov 2019, 17:50 by Tamlyn Jones, https://www.facebook.com/tamlynjonesbpm/?fref=ts

Raluca Munteanu is a 23-year-old apprentice with historic Birmingham manufacturer RH Nuttall.

The company, which was established in 1860, is still run by two generations of the founding family and specialises in the production of non-metallic washers, gaskets and sealing strips.

It even supplied all of the washers for the Tower of London poppy installation in 2014.

Raluca left her native Romanian in 2015 to study psychology with criminology at Birmingham City University (BCU), graduating last year with a 2:1.

She has been working for RH Nuttall since April and is part-way through an apprenticeship programme which has already seen her promoted and handed greater responsibilities in the accounts side of the company.

Raluca was the 500th apprentice recruited through the Ladder for Greater Birmingham apprenticeship campaign which is aiming to create 1,000 new apprenticeships in Birmingham and Solihull (see factbox below for more information).

What has been your work experience so far?

I am an apprentice working in the accounts department and studying a business administration level 3 course through West Bromwich-based employment agency The Apprenticeship Works.

Most of my work experience was gained during my time at BCU where I worked for the international and European department and admin office.

I was a guidance adviser helping new students adapt to their university life and I also collaborated with the SU and international and EU department to create activities and workshops for the new students.

I also worked with the recruitment and enrolment department, helping them understand students' needs and international students on what to expect from their courses.

Why did you decide to leave your homeland to come to Birmingham?

During my third year of high school, I decided I wanted a career that would relate to psychology and I thought coming to the UK would give me a better chance of achieving this goal.

Coming here and studying at BCU has been one of my best life choices.

The experience here has exceeded my initial expectations and the knowledge I have accumulated so far has built a path towards my goals.

What have been your duties during your apprenticeship?

I started off doing simple admin duties like filing, answering the telephone and keeping our systems up to date with information from suppliers and customers.

I asked if there were other duties I could be taught so progressed onto the customer ledger - chasing debtors, sending statements, taking payments and investigating queries.

It gave me an understanding of how to maintain relationships with customers from which I learned more about purchase ledger.

I have now moved from the customer side to supplier side and have become one of the main points of contact for them.

I was taught how to process and post purchase invoices and how to keep a clean ledger, making everything easier for my manager, and trying to solve any queries.

How does being an apprentice help you?

The things I learn and the situations I am faced with are showing me how an actual business environment presents itself.

I now understand what it is like to work for a company and how important communication is between departments.

Also, I cannot emphasize how important the relationship is that you build with a company's clients and suppliers, without which your job would be much harder.

It is easier to sort out queries when everyone understands where each person is coming from and aims to solve issues on good terms.

I feel I have progressed well while being an apprentice and have picked things up quickly and been able to take on more responsibilities.

What are your goals now?

I would like to continue building up even more good relations with our suppliers and continue progressing on my course.

I aim to finish this before July and be hired in a proper account assistant position.

I would like to steadily advance and built up my work experience and continue to learn new things that enhance my knowledge.
