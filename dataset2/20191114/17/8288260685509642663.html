Woman knocked down by van in Walsall

The casualty was taken to the Queen Elizabeth Hospital after the accident on the afternoon of Thursday, November 14

14 Nov 2019, 17:07 by Ashley Preece, Charlotte Regen, https://www.facebook.com/PreeceBirminghamLive/, https://www.birminghammail.co.uk/authors/charlotte-regen/

A woman was seriously injured after she was hit by a van in Walsall.

The emergency services rushed to the scene on Shaw Street, near to the Wine and Dine restaurant, this afternoon, November 14.

Medics treated the woman, who was pedestrian, for serious injuries when they arrived on the scene at around 3.10pm.

She was being taken to Birmingham's Queen Elizabeth Hospital.

A paramedic officer, two ambulances and the Midlands air ambulance from Cosford all attended the scene.

A spokesman for West Midlands Ambulance Service said: "We were called at 3.10pm to Shaw Street, to reports of a collision between a van and a pedestrian.

"We sent two ambulances, a paramedic officer and the Midlands air ambulance from Cosford to the scene. We treated one patient, a woman, who was the pedestrian for serious injuries. She is going to be taken to the Queen Elizabeth Hospital by land ambulance."
