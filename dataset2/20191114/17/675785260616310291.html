Paul Routledge: Flood insult from the wally in wellies will not be forgotten

Boris Johnson nearly gets run out of town after turning up with promises of £500 to pay for damage caused by overflowing rivers and backed-up sewers

14 Nov 2019, 17:54 by Paul Routledge, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/paul-routledge/

“So, what are you going to put right today?” teased reader Richard as I left the Co-op with my Mirror.

Good question, old lad. So let’s get on with it, before we all drown – in Boris Johnson’s lies.

Somebody showed him Yorkshire on the map, and he came up ’ere to talk his way out of trouble over the floods. He only waded in deeper.

The Prime Minister was virtually chased out of town by angry voters furious over his lazy, weak, patronising incompetence.

They had enough on their plate, without bumbling Boris “an annoying little man who didn’t even get his wellies wet” posing for the TV cameras in floods of crocodile tears.

Congratulations to the victims of the near-biblical inundation of the lower Don Valley for calling power to account. They needed action earlier, and they didn’t get it. It took a week for ministers in Whitehall to shift gear from electioneering to the job they’re paid to do.

And then it was too little, too late – £500 per household for families swamped by three feet of filthy river water.

What do you want to replace: the fridge, or the living room carpet?

That’s as far as rich Bozza’s generosity will go, and it isn’t just pathetic. It’s an insult from Tory politicians spraying billions of pounds on eye-catching, deceitful promises in a sordid bid to remain in power. The money they’ve spent on botched Brexit alone would have made the whole country flood-proof.

They must think there are no votes in flood-hit Yorkshire, because these are all traditional Labour seats.

A miscalculation. The eyes of the country have been on this tragedy for many days, and it isn’t over by a long chalk.

Voters will remember how the Government let the people down. I can’t put this crisis right, Richard, but you can rely on me to bang on about it.
