Rich Kids Go Skint daughter from Exeter who spends £100 a week on takeaways gets shock living with struggling family of five

Devon, a trainee solicitor, has a monthly allowance from her parents which is almost as much as the Gibbins family spend on rent each month on 5Star's Rich Kids Go Skint tonight

14 Nov 2019, 17:33 by Howard Lloyd

A loaded trainee solicitor from Exeter swaps her pampered lifestyle and parents' mega-mansion for life with a debt-ridden family of five in Lincoln on tonight's Rich Kids Go Skint on 5Star (9pm).

For Devon, from Devon, money is not a concern. She receives a £500 monthly allowance from her parents, who also pay her bills - and they bail her out if she is ever short of cash.

Not only does she spend a lot on designer items, from Gucci and Louis Vuitton bags to £800 on a designer coat, but she also pays out £100 a week on takeaways.

She lives in a mansion the size of three small houses which has an incredible 12 bathrooms - three of which are just for her.

So she receives a shock when she goes to stay with the Gibbins family.

Paul and Sammie have three young kids and survive on a combined salary and benefits of £1,730 a month.

After bills, childcare and food, they have a little over £50 for the rest of the month.

They are also in £3,000 worth of debt.

Unsurprisingly, their first impressions of each are quite different.

After commenting on the fact the area does not have a Waitrose on her way to the Gibbins household, Devon said: "It looks a bit rough, quite run down, there's a lot of rubbish on the floor, the houses look quite small.

"I guess I am a little bit out of my comfort house."

Sammie is stunned when she learns how much Devon spends on herself.

Paul earns £1.100, while Sammie receives £630 of benefits. After paying £535 on rent, their utility bills and food shops, the couple is left with roughly £50.

All the while, they are trying to clear their substantial debt pile.

"If we had Devon's allowance we could be out of debt in four months," said Sammie, who was training to be a nurse but had to drop out to look after her kids.

"It upsets me a little bit to think she doesn't have to think about what she spends on clothes or her hair or her beauty.

"We could save for a new house, our life could be dramatically changed, which is scary to think."

Still, they manage to forge a strong understanding over the course of the programme.

Devon helps fix a bathroom door and goes hunting for clothes and gifts in a charity shop - impressing Sammie after picking out three presents for £7.46.

"When I saw how much the family had to budget it was quite eye-opening," Devon said.

"I don't have to think about how much I have left for the week. It's very hard what they have to do."

To help out, Devon offers to babysit for a night - although this pushes her to her limits as the kids argue for much of the evening.

But by the end of the programme, she was sad to say goodbye to the Gibbins family, who she said were all 'so nice'.

"I loved the time I spent with the family," she said.

"I think I'll take away the fact that I am very lucky, and I need to appreciate that a bit more and realise that not everyone is lucky enough to live the way I live.

"It's kinda sad to leave the family, they're all so nice. It was really opening to see how other people live."

Rich Kids Go Skint airs tomorrow at 9pm on 5STAR.
