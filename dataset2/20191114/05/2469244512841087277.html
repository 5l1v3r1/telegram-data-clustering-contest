The best and worst beaches in Devon for water quality

How did your local beach fare?

14 Nov 2019, 05:00 by Joel Cooper

Devon has performed well in the annual bathing water quality ratings for England with 37 beaches in the county rated as Excellent.

The figures, released today by Defra, show the results of monitoring at designated bathing waters throughout England.

Several beaches in Devon have improved their ratings since last year with Hope Cove, Thurlestone North, Bantham, Bigbury-on-Sea South, and Wembury all going from "Good" last year to "Excellent" this year.

Ladram Bay also improved from "Sufficient" to "Good".

The only beach to drop a rating was Croyde Bay in North Devon which was ranked "Excellent" last year and "Good" this year.

Combe Martin and Ilfracombe Wildersmouth beaches in North Devon continue to be ranked as poor, with Instow having a "permanent advice against bathing" warning in place.

Across the UK 98.3% of the 420 beaches measured met at least the minimum standard of water quality required.

300 beaches (71.4%) were rated as Excellent - including 37 in Devon.

Just seven bathing waters in England (1.7 per cent) - two of which are in Devon - did not meet the minimum standard, and were classified as Poor.
