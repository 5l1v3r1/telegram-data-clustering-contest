Police manhunt continues following armed police incident in Tiverton

Armed officers were called to an address on Saturday night

14 Nov 2019, 14:03 by Jamie Hawkins

Police say they are still locating a suspect involved in a firearms operation in Tiverton.

A manhunt was launched on Saturday night and a  cordon was put in place in the town centre , between Angel Hill and Bridge Street, while a number of resources, including armed and response officers, a police dog and the force helicopter, were called.

Police said that following a reported domestic related incident, officers attended an address in Angel Hill, Tiverton, at around 6pm on Saturday November 9.

Officers carried out enquiries to locate a male who was wanted in connection with domestic-related threats.

A representative of Devon and Cornwall Police said: "Due to the nature of the threats, armed officers, a police dog and response officers were deployed.

"The victim has been safeguarded by officers whilst enquiries to locate the offender continue.

"Anyone with information is urged to call police on 101, or email 101@dc.police.uk, quoting log 498 of 09/11/19.

"Information can also be passed anonymously to independent charity Crimestoppers by calling 0800 555111, or via www.crimestoppers-uk.org  "

A witness who was on the scene said that a man, who appeared to be in fear for his life, had knocked on his door shortly before police ushered the man from his flat and outside the cordons where he had been for an hour.

Devon Live requested an update on the incident on Monday and were told in a statement that "enquiries are ongoing."

A police spokesperson also confirmed that   "no firearms [had] been discharged" , "contrary to social media reports" and there was no wider threat to members of the public.
