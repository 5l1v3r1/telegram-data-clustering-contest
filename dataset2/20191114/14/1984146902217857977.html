When is England v Montenegro, is it on TV, who’s in the teams?

Gareth Southgate's side will play their 1000th international fixture tonight

14 Nov 2019, 14:20 by Jerry Chen

Tonight England will play their 1,000th game when they take on Montenegro at Wembley.

Each players wearing the “legacy numbers” will be honouring the monumental record while contributions from former footballers and managers will be recognised.

As the host the Three Lions aim will be trying to kept their 100 per cent track record in Group A.

The fourth-placed Montenegro are 12 points behind England in Group A. A win for England will secure a place for the nation for next summer’s tournament.

As for Montenegro, they so far are without a win in the seven games they played in the Euro 2020 qualifiers with four defeats and three draws under their belt. Their most recent game to Kosovo suffered resulted in a 2-0 loss.

After facing off Montenegro England will fly to the Czech Republic to face the home team on the Friday evening.

When is England v Montenegro?

The match takes place on tonight (October 14) with kick-off taking place at 7.45pm. The game is part of a packed Euro 2020 schedule, with a total seven games taking place across the groups.

Is England v Montenegro on TV? How can I follow it live?

The match will be broadcast live on ITV1, coverage starts at 7pm. England fans can also stream the game live via the ITV Hub available on a range of devices such as smartphones, laptops and tablets.

Team News

England will be without Raheem Sterling this evening. Gareth Southgate confirmed the Manchester City star will miss the game as punishment for an altercation with Liverpool defender Joe Gomez at St George's Park.

Meanwhile, Ross Barkley and Fabian Delph have pulled out of the squad through injury, while Aston Villa goalkeeper Tom Heaton has also withdrawn from the squad.

Heaton has since been replaced by Sheffield United's Dean Henderson. Henderson, alongside Leicester star James Maddison and Chelsea defender Fikayo Tomori, could win their first caps.

England likely XI:  Pickford; Trippier, Stones, Maguire, Chilwell; J Henderson, Rice, Mount; Sancho, Rashford; Kane
