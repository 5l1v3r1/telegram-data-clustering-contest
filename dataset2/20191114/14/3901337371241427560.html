Life After Pirates: Munetsi Opens Up

14 Nov 2019, 14:56 by Soccer Laduma

Former Orlando Pirates star, Marshall Munetsi, has opened about his move from the Buccaneers to Europe.  

Munetsi left Pirates at the end of last season to join Stade de Reims – a French Ligue 1 outfit.

The versatile 23-year-old is able to play in defence or midfield, had one of his best games for the French side against Paris Saint-Germain in September. 

In what was his first Ligue 1 start, the Zimbabwean had a memorable game with his superb performance against the likes of Brazil superstar Neymar and his teammates, leading Reims to a 2-0 win over PSG.

Munetsi is currently with the Zimbabwe senior national team for their upcoming Africa Cup of Nations qualifiers against Botswana and Zambia respectively. 

“The move has helped me a lot. You realise you have to work hard to meet the requirements in Europe. You have to be strong, mentally and physically. Generally, it helps you improve your game and we just hope to get a good result against Botswana,” the former Buccaneer has been quoted by Zimbabwe publication, NewsDay.
