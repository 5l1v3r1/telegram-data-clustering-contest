Thousands of child sexual exploitation victims seek help as grooming reports rise

A disturbing rise in calls to the NSPCC helpline Childline has included children reporting being forced to perform or watch sexual acts

14 Nov 2019, 22:26 by Mark Ellis, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/mark-ellis/

Rising numbers of children –some as young as nine – are seeking help after being sexually exploited, Childline has revealed.

The NSPCC’s helpline dealt with 4,500 youngsters who were groomed or forced in to sexual activity last year, up 16% on 2017.

Exploitation accounted for more than half of Childline’s 8,841 sexual abuse cases.

More than a third of victims were targeted online, usually through social media or video games.

Most said that they were forced to perform or watch sexual acts, or were persuaded into sending naked images or videos of themselves.

The NSPCC wants the Government to provide proper training for teachers delivering sex education.

Dame Esther Rantzen, Childline founder and president, said: “Older people need to recognise that victims feel trapped because of a sense of shame and humiliation.

“They think they will be blamed, but often they are tricked by people they believe to be genuinely in love with them.”
