Raheem Sterling defends Joe Gomez after some England fans boo Liverpool star

There was a smattering of boos around Wembley Stadium when Gomez came on to replace Mason Mount in the second half of England's 7-0 win over Montenegro

14 Nov 2019, 22:20 by Alex Milne

Raheem Sterling has defended Joe Gomez after the Liverpool defender was booed by a section of England fans during the 7-0 win over Montenegro.

The Three Lions made light work of their opponents as a hat-trick by Harry Kane and goals from Alex Oxlade-Chamberlain, Marcus Rashford, Tammy Abraham and an own goal from Aleksandr Sofranac of Montenegro gave England the victory.

There was one sour point, however, as some supporters booed Gomez as he was introduced as a second-half substitute - seemingly due to the part he played in Sterling being axed for the game.

Sterling and Gomez had an altercation in the canteen at St George's Park on Monday, with tempers still high from the Liverpool v Manchester City game on Sunday.

Sterling has since taken full responsibility for that incident and condemned the reaction of England fans to Gomez after the match.

 

Shortly after full-time Sterling took to social media to say: "To all the England fans, I wanted to leave things at it was but tonight I have to speak again: it was hard for me to see my team mate get booed for something that was my fault.

"Joe hasn’t done anything wrong & for me to see someone who keeps his head down and work hard..especially after a difficult week for him to be booed when he came on tonight was wrong.

"I’ve taken full responsibility and accepted the consequence. I felt as though I had to say this get home safe every one."

It is understood Gomez was the first person in the England changing room and the first to leave after the game.

 

England manager Gareth Southgate also spoke out on the incident in his post-match press conference, saying: "Joe had done nothing wrong. No England player should be booed when they wear the shirt. I don't get it. All the players were disappointed with that."

Sterling could be back in contention when England take on Kosovo in their last qualification game on Sunday, although Southgate's side have already confirmed their participation in next summer's tournament.
