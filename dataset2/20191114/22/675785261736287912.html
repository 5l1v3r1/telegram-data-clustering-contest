I'm A Celeb's Adele Roberts feels guilty about roaring rows with Jade Goody on BB

Jade almost quit the show in tears after the argument but was talked into staying and now looking back Adele feels guilty

14 Nov 2019, 22:16 by Lily Waddell, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/lily-waddell/

Adele Roberts has admitted she feels guilt for the way she acted towards others on Big Brother including Jade Goody – and so is returning to reality TV to show the real her.

Adele, 40, appeared on Big Brother 3 in 2002 and had a number of row in the house, most memorably one where she criticised Jade for having a verucca on her foot and not knowing what it was.

Jade almost quit the show in tears after the argument but was talked into staying.

Asked about her time on BB3 Adele said: “Looking back, I carried a lot of guilt about the way I acted in Big Brother and I feel like I let myself down and I could have been a much nicer person.

"I’d like to show a better side to myself this time. To both the campmates in there by treating them better but also to come out and have a positive experience not a negative one.

“I just think I judged people when I shouldn’t have, I should have tried to get to know them a bit more and I think I probably hurt people when I didn’t realise when I was in there.

"It was only because I’ve come out that I realised that words are really powerful and you should try and build people up. I just let the game get to me and got too negative and I just won’t do that again this time.”

The DJ says that her biggest fear going into the show is a lack of food when contestants do badly in trials and fail to win meals for camp.

She said: “I’m just worried a bit about the amount of food. Nobody will give me an honest answer about the food and I feel like there’s not going to be much so when I get hungry I get emotional and I just want to have my little safety toolkit so I can just calm my mind down and not lose it.”

Adele also says she will quit Radio 1 if they dont move her off the early breakfast slot when she returns.

She has to leave the house at 2am to get to the studios and she said: “I think it’s probably my time (to go), I’ve had four and a half years on it but it is a good show for Radio One to get new presenters and try them out so it’s a great training ground for people.

"I feel like hopefully after I’ve had this experience in the Jungle, Radio One can find another slot for me. I hope I can stay part of the family but I think it’s probably time for me to move on now.”
