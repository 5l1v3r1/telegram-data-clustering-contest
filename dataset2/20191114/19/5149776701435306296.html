'It was always on the edge of an explosion' - ex-Forest boss on England spat

Steve McClaren endured difficult spells in charge of England and Nottingham Forest

14 Nov 2019, 19:42 by Barry Cooper

Former Nottingham Forest manager Steve McClaren believes the England team has always been on the verge of an "explosion" and admits he wasn’t surprised to see fireworks go off this week.

Raheem Sterling and Joe Gomez were involved in a showdown at St George’s Park on Monday, just hours after Manchester City were beaten by Liverpool at Anfield.

The pair were involved in an angry confrontation on Merseyside, before picking up where they left off after joining up with Gareth Southgate’s Three Lions for their upcoming games.

Sterling was stood down from Thursday night’s Euro 2020 qualifier with Montenegro at Wembley, but not removed from the squad entirely, and McClaren , who took charge of the national team between 2006 and 2007 believes it was anything but a shock.

“International management is very difficult, especially with England. It was always a difficult atmosphere.

“It was always on the edge of an explosion and they’ve had one,” he told talkSPORT.

“There’s been many more and we all have spats.”

Sterling and Gomez are both chasing the Premier League title at their respective clubs, and the former Reds chief admits that club rivalry is often bubbling away in the background.

"Conflicts within the international squad can occur because of the rivalry between the clubs on the field which you can’t get away from,” he said.

“Sometimes they can be underlying and never come out.

“I think it’s good that it’s come out.

“Conflict can be a great opportunity to stamp your authority to sort things out, to come closer together.

“As long as Gareth handles that well, which he has done so far – the media are trying to explode that and make it bigger than what it is – they’ve handled it well.

“We’ll see how they go forward from that.

“It’s a little crack that needs to be sealed up.”
