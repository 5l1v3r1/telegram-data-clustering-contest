Man left covered in blood after being slashed by gang who tried to steal his dog in Glasgow

David Smith was attacked while taking his Rottweiler Bear out for a morning walk in Castlemilk on Thursday morning.

14 Nov 2019, 19:19 by Dailyrecord.co.uk

A dog walker was slashed across the head by hooded thugs who attempted to steal his Rottweiler in an early morning attack.

David Smith was walking his dog Bear in a wooded area between Tormusk Road and Fernhill Road in Castlemilk , Glasgow on Thursday morning when three men pounced.

The 37-year-old was left covered in blood after he was slashed and stabbed in the head by the gang.

He was rushed to hospital after the attack.

Wife Debbie Smith told how David and Bear tried to fight off the thugs as she appealed for witnesses to come forward.

She wrote: "A heads up to anyone walking their dog around Castlemilk.

"David was out with Bear this morning before work at the entrance to the wooded path on Tormusk Road and got jumped by three guys.

"Both my boys fought for their lives, Bear got stuck into one and David got stuck into another.

"Unfortunately the third one managed slash and stab David's head six times.

"His hand is pretty smashed up from punching them too.

"The area was sealed off by the police this morning investigating.

"If anyone knows or heard anything, please get in touch.

"Two of the guys must be injured from where David and Bear fought back."

Police are hunting the thugs who are all described as white European.

Constable Andy Reid said: “This was a particularly violent and sustained attack and has taken place in a wooded area which is popular with dog walkers and is used regularly by pedestrians.

"We would ask anyone who happened to be walking in the area, or close to it, and who may have seen or heard anything suspicious or noticed anyone suspicious loitering in the area to contact us on the 101 number, quoting the reference number 0437 of 14 November 2019.

"The three men can only be described as all being white European,.

"They all were approximately five feet, ten inches tall and were wearing black jackets with hoods up, partially covering their faces.

"Anyone wishing to pass on information and remain anonymous can do so by calling Crimestoppers on 0800 555 111.”
