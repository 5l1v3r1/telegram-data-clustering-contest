Notts County loan star Kyle Wootton targets glorious promotion back to the Football League

On-loan Scunthorpe forward has hit six goals so far this term

14 Nov 2019, 19:00 by Leigh Curtis

Kyle Wootton feels he is yet to reach the peak of his powers at Notts County as he targeted a glorious return to the Football League this season.

The striker took his goals tally to six for the season with a brace in the 3-2 FA Cup win at Ebbsfleet last weekend and is hoping to maintain that form when league duties resume against Barrow this weekend.

Wootton has done exceptionally well since signing on a season-long loan from Scunthorpe earlier this season and has started nearly every game since his arrival.

The young forward says he is relishing his time with the Magpies at Meadow Lane, but feels there is still room for improvement despite his impressive return so far.

But he is adamant that the ultimate aim for him and the rest of the squad is to be challenging for promotion back to the EFL.

“You could say it’s one of my favourite loan spells,” he said.

“I’ve got six goals, and I still feel I’ve got more to give and help the team out.

“The main position for us is that we want to be up there getting promoted.

“Hopefully that’s the outcome at the end of the season.”
