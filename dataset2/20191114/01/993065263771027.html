Man hit by bat in sectarian attack in Ballymena

14 Nov 2019, 01:00 by CLAIRE SIMPSON

A MAN was hit by a bat in a sectarian attack in Ballymena.

Police said the man was walking along Sentry Hill at around 11.30am on Tuesday November 5 when he was attacked by several masked men.

One of the gang was waving a knife.

The victim was punched and hit with a bat before he managed to escape.

Police said they are treating the attack as sectarian.

"Hate crimes have no place in today's society and will simply not be tolerated.

"While this man managed to escape with minor physical injuries, such as bruising and cuts, we could have been looking at a very different picture.

"He has, understandably, been left badly shaken by a dreadful ordeal."

Police appealed to anyone with information to contact them on 101.

In May 2006, 15-year-old Catholic schoolboy Michael McIlveen died after he was kicked and beaten with a baseball bat in an alleyway in Ballymena.
