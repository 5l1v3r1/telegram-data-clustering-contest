Carnival Australia cruise ship Sun Princess evacuated in Singapore after gastro outbreak

14 Nov 2019, 10:31

Sun Princess has again grappled with an outbreak of norovirus aboard one of their vessels that left Perth for Singapore earlier this month.

The highly contagious virus, which causes vomiting and diarrhoea, reportedly spread across the Carnival Australia owned-ship causing crews to evacuate all passengers for fumigation in Singapore yesterday.

The ship departed Fremantle on November 1, on what was supposed to be a 12-night voyage.

While the ship has recommenced its journey, it’s understood some passengers have decided to fly back to Australia.

Norovirus symptoms general start in the first or second day of contamination and usually lasts between one and three days.

The virus can be spread through touching objects, survive on surfaces and is easily spread from person-to-person.

In 2016 and 2017, the Carnival Australia experienced a string of outbreaks on eight consecutive voyages.

The outbreaks lead to a large scale Class Action Investigation into Carnival Australia on behalf of the affected passengers.

A Princess Cruises spokeswoman creating and maintaining a healthy onboard environment was “always a priority”.

“Onboard sanitation was swiftly stepped up following some guests reporting gastrointestinal symptoms confirmed as being due to Norovirus,” she said.

“The vast majority of the 2000 guests on Sun Princess were unaffected but it takes relatively few cases of illness to be reported for onboard sanitation measures to be increased.

“Out of an abundance of care and in line with best practice, extra cleaning of the terminal was also carried out yesterday.

“Guests remain isolated while they have symptoms.

“There are also constant reminders for guests to wash their hands regularly to stay well and help maintain a healthy environment.”

She wouldn’t comment when asked if passengers would be compensated or reveal how many people had been affected.
