Khabib Nurmagomedov doesn't enjoy his victories - except over Conor McGregor

Nurmagomedov has won each of his 28 mixed martial arts fights - but insists he does not enjoy his victories

14 Nov 2019, 09:39 by Martin Domin, https://www.facebook.com/mirrorfighting

Khabib Nurmagomedov has revealed the only fight he enjoyed winning was his victory over Conor McGregor .

The UFC lightweight champion has yet to taste defeat after 28 fights in mixed martial arts.

But he claims he has not enjoyed his most recent victories despite establishing himself as one of the best in the world.

Except, that is, his win over McGregor last year when he choked out the Irishman in the fourth round.

"At the beginning, when you're just starting out and winning fights, it brings you some sort of pleasure. You enjoy it," Nurmagomedov said.

"Recently I haven’t had that. In the last couple of years I’ve gone out there and won and it doesn't bring me any real joy to be honest.

"Well, with the exception of one fight, let’s say. In that fight I beat that guy [Conor McGregor] with pleasure, I'll tell you.

"I had fights in my career where I understood that I could hurt my opponent more, but I’d talk to him, tell him to give up so that I don’t beat him too hard.

"For example, if I had a painful hold where I could break something, I wouldn’t tighten it all the way, it’d just fix it so that the opponent would tap out.

"Because I have no aim to hurt anyone in this sport. Yes, we compete in a very brutal combat sport, but it’s never my goal to inflict a lot of pain on my opponent. If I understand that I’m winning, I don’t aim to hurt someone bad. Well, except for one fight."

Nurmagomedov defeated Dustin Poirier in September to retain his 155lb title and will defend it against Tony Ferguson in April or May next year.

But he has ruled out a boxing fight with Floyd Mayweather which both men have proposed since Mayweather beat McGregor in 2017.

"I think it would be better for me to fight Tony Ferguson rather than Floyd at this stage of my career," Nurmagomedov said. “I’m more focused on MMA.

"If he moved to MMA we would fight but I don’t think it’s possible. I don’t have plans to have a boxing fight next year."
