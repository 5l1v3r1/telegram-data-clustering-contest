Benefits cheat dodges jail term

Ayr Sheriff Court was told that Hans McLarty, 55, of Merrick Drive, Dalmellington, paid back the outstanding amount.

14 Nov 2019, 09:36 by Paul Behan, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/paul-behan/

McLarty was heavily fined, however, and will be made subject to a tag, prohibiting him from leaving his house between certain hours for the next nine weeks.

McLarty appeared at the sheriff court last week and admitted failing to give prompt notification to East Ayrshire Council over a change in circumstances which he knew would affect his housing benefit.

The offence happened between December 2013 and August 2018 and he obtained £6,000 which he was not entitled to.

The charge also stated that McLarty had too much savings to qualify for the benefits.

The original charge alleged that he obtained as much as £14,000 but this was reduced to £6000.

McLarty’s solicitor said: “All the housing benefit has been paid.

“He’s someone who is not keeping very well and he found himself in hospital for a week. He’s going to have an endoscopy.

“He’s somebody who has significant health issues.

“He’s worried about a custodial sentence and it’s affected his health.

“Prior to falling ill he had a very strong work history and felt aggrieved he was on benefits and put out of work, down to his own ill-health.

“He accepts full responsibility. It’s a failure to notify a change of circumstances but the money was not spent on luxuries.

“It was simply sitting in the bank and he has repaid that housing benefit and did it off his own back.”

Sheriff David Young QC told McLarty: “This is a case where I have to consider custody.

“However, I take into account you have repaid the money, your lack of convictions and your state of health. I can avoid imposing custody.”

McLarty was fined £900, payable at £40 per fortnight, and placed on a curfew order, meaning he cannot leave his home between 7pm and 7am for the next nine weeks.
