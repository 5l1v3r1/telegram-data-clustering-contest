'Toxic' levels of lead found in drinking water at Scots family home

Test results found the amount of lead in Suneeta Rathore’s mains supply to be eight times above recommended limits.

14 Nov 2019, 09:36 by Stephen Norris

A Kirkcudbright mum has has turned off the taps after “toxic” levels of lead were found in the water at her home.

Test results found the amount of lead in Suneeta Rathore’s mains supply to be eight times above recommended limits.

The contamination means Suneeta and her son must use bottled water for drinking and cooking.

Scottish Water detected the problem at the former police house on High Street after a plumber advised Suneeta to get it checked. “My supply of water is very toxic,” said the 53-year-old, who is awaiting blood test results.

“The levels are over eight times the regulatory safe standard. I have got written confirmation and the results.

“I have a 10-year-old who has been raised on that water since he was seven months old.

“In my financial state, I need help to get my water changed. I am just trying to make my water safe.”

 

Suneeta, who runs Pleat clothes shop on St Mary’s Street, believes nearby properties could also be affected.

She said: “It could be that all these communal pipes are in properties belonging to the police station.

“There’s a detached house, a semi-detached house and the police station itself.

“Scottish Water have assured me the pipes are not lead on their side. But beyond that the pipes are lead.”

Councillor Dougie Campbell helped Suneeta source bottled water from Scottish Water then the council.

He said: “I recognise the wider implications that there may be other properties in the local area that may have lead pipes.

“I have contacted senior council officers and Scottish Water managers so that they can take the appropriate action.”

A Scottish Water spokesperson said: “The cause of the raised levels of lead at the property can most likely be attributed to lead pipes or fittings, either in the property or in the street outside.

“We have advised a small number of our customers in the area to check if their pipework is made of lead and arrange to have it replaced.

“Scottish Water have inspected the public water mains and can confirm we found no lead pipes at this location.”

A council spokesperson said: “As soon as environmental health became aware of the result of the water sample, taken by Scottish Water, we notified public health medicine at Dumfries and Galloway NHS.

“NHS D&G provided relevant health information and will monitor and screen residents.

“Strategic housing will continue to offer support to residents to ensure that the issue can be resolved.

“There is a possibility that residents that have been identified and contacted by Scottish Water may come forward to request assistance.

“We will continue to liaise with partner agencies to address any further issues that may arise.”
