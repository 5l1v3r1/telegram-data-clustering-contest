Plymouth Argyle playmaker Danny Mayor makes promise to Green Army over his form

The 29-year-old signed for the Pilgrims in the summer with very high expectations for him

14 Nov 2019, 15:42 by Chris Errington

Plymouth Argyle midfield playmaker Danny Mayor has promised there is ‘definitely more to come’ from him performance-wise.

By his own admission, the 29-year-old has had an up and down start to his first season at Home Park.

Promotion from League Two remains the aim for Mayor, who has been a marked man for the opposition.

That has led to him being kicked time after time to try and stop him but he has vowed to ‘just keep getting up’.

There was much excitement among the Green Army when Mayor followed manager Ryan Lowe from Bury to Argyle this summer.

Widely regarded as one of the best players in League Two there were very high expectations for him.

Mayor has shown some good form for Argyle at times but consistency has been an issue for him.

He said: “There were a couple of things off the pitch that I wouldn’t say distracted me but made it a bit more difficult.

“I think it has been up and down really, not the form that I wanted. There is definitely more to come.”

Mayor does not mind being picked out as the Pilgrims’ main man by opposing sides, who believe if they can stop him they can stop Argyle.

“If that’s what they are saying then, of course, I’m happy with it because it means I’m doing something right, but we have got a lot of good players at Argyle,” he said.

Often that has led to Mayor being repeatedly fouled, without much in the way of protection from referees.

“I just try not to react,” he said. “I did it last season and the Gaffer (Lowe) lynched me so I can’t really do it again.

“I figure if you are getting kicked then you must be playing alright. Take it on the chin and just keep getting up.”

Mayor has spent most of his football in his native north-west of England.

He has no regrets about deciding to move to the opposite end of the country to play for Argyle.

He said: “I’m loving it down here. I have to admit I did think I would maybe struggle.

“I have not left home for a couple of years. I have never moved this far south. I feel like I’m in France.

“I actually love it. It is different and I can do stuff with my little boy that you can’t do up north so I’m very relaxed down here.”

Mayor was one of five Bury players who were brought to Home Park by Lowe following their promotion-winning campaign in League Two last season.

He believes they have settled in well at their new club and that another promotion is their collective target.

Mayor said: “There is absolutely no cliques. I have come down here with all the Bury lot, but some of the lads that I get on with were here last year.

“I get on really well with Joe (Riley), he’s my room-mate.

“The Gaffer always says it doesn’t matter about ability, he wants to sign good lads and that’s what he has got here.

“Everyone wants the same thing. Everyone wants to push forward and get promoted.”

Mayor returned to Argyle’s starting line-up for the 1-0 FA Cup first round win at Bolton Wanderers last Saturday.

Initially, he had to serve a one-match suspension for the 2-0 home league win against Carlisle United on October 19 after being booked five times this season.

He then played as a second half substitute in the next two games, including the 4-0 Devon Derby drubbing at Exeter City, due to the form of George Cooper.

Mayor understood why Lowe went for Cooper instead of him for those matches, but was glad to get recalled against Bolton.

He said: “I love playing football. That’s what I have come down here to do.

“I knew the Gaffer couldn’t change it. Coops was playing unbelievable. I accepted that and I bided my time, but I’m glad to be back on the pitch.”

Mayor added: “I just want us to win games and if George is the one who is setting up goals then he deserves his place.

“The aim is to get promotion, whatever team we field. It’s definitely good to have George pushing me and me pushing him.”

Argyle return to League Two action on Saturday with a difficult task awaiting them at table-topping Forest Green Rovers.

“As well as us (Bury), they were one of the better teams at playing football. They did it really well and they have carried on.”
