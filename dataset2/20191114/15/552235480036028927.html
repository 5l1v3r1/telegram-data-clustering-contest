Truck debt ends in violence as woman 'thought she was going to die'

Alexander Burke was jailed for two years following the horrific attack.

14 Nov 2019, 15:58 by Wishaw Press, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/wishaw-press/

A woman wept as she described her terror when a pick-up truck deliberately rammed her vehicle at a busy motorway junction near Strathclyde Park.

Theresa Townsley said a man leapt out of the Ford Transit truck and used a scythe to smash her windows.

She claimed the attack happened after she and her sister had fled from a car park where her husband was brutally assaulted over an alleged £13,000 debt.

The 38-year-old mother told a jury at Hamilton Sheriff Court she thought she was going to die when the violence erupted.

Alexander Burke, 28, was jailed for three years after being found guilty of assaulting James Wilson to his severe injury and permanent disfigurement.

He was also convicted of assaulting Ms Townsley and her sister Mary Jean Townsley as they cowered in their vehicle.

 

His dad, also Alexander, 48, was found guilty of the attack on the sisters, but cleared of assaulting Mr Wilson. He was jailed for two years.

A third man, James Johnstone, 23, walked free when he was acquitted of assaulting Mr Wilson and battering a car with a hammer.

All those involved in the case were said to be members of the travelling community. The accused live in the Aberdeen area.

James Wilson was allegedly owed £13,000 for one of the pick-ups he had sold to Alexander Burke senior.

The court heard a meeting to sort out the payment had been arranged for a car park near M&D’s Theme Park on May 26.

But Mr Wilson claimed that eight or nine men arrived in two pick-ups and Burke senior got out of one of the vehicles and threatened to kill him.

Then, he alleged, Burke junior battered him over the head with a spade.

 

Medical evidence agreed by all sides stated that Mr Wilson suffered a serious wound which would have required 40-50 conventional stitches but was instead treated with continuous stitching.

Describing the background to the meeting, Ms Townsley alleged Burke senior had stolen the pick-up from her husband, Mr Wilson.

This had been reported to the police who were actively looking for the truck.

The witness agreed with Neil Thomson, prosecuting, that the park meeting had been arranged to “finalise payment”.

Ms Townsley said what happened instead was a running battle.

She claimed Burke senior emerged from a pick-up, pointed at James Wilson and said ‘You’re dead, you’re dead’.

Then, she alleged, Burke junior punched her husband several times.

She and her sister Mary Jean panicked and fled in Mary Jean’s minibus, but they found themselves sandwiched between the pick-ups as they made their way to the Raith interchange.

Ms Townsley sobbed as she said: “Sandy Burke senior was driving one of them. I didn’t see who was driving the other.

“Both pick-ups just kept ramming us. I couldn’t count how many times it happened.

 

“We were stopped on the Raith interchange roundabout when Burke junior jumped out of one of the pick-ups.

“It was rush hour and there was a lot of traffic.

“He put a scythe through the passenger side window and tried to cut me.

“He was saying ‘You’re dead, you’re dead’. We thought we were going to die.

“Burke junior smashed the rest of the windows then ran back to the pick-up.

“We managed to get back to Strathclyde Park where I phoned the police.”

The accused did not give evidence, but defence lawyers urged the jury to reject the Crown version of events.

They suggested James Wilson was a man of violence, pointing to three weapons – a knuckleduster, a sharpened metal pole and a wrench – found in his Cherokee Jeep as proof.

 

Sandy Morrison, representing Burke senior, said Theresa Townsley’s evidence about her sister’s vehicle being rammed repeatedly was “incredible”.

He asked the jury: “Where are the other drivers to give independent evidence? Where is the dash cam footage? There is none.”

Andy Thomson, Burke junior’s lawyer, also suggested the story about the rush hour ramming had been made up.

He said: “It is astonishing that an incident like this happens on that busy interchange at 6.30pm and no one stops or phones the police. That makes no sense.”
