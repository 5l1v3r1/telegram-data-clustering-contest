Bayern Munich told Pep Guardiola is 'best solution' amid return rumours

The Spaniard has been linked with a return to the Bundesliga with Bayern after their recent sacking of Niko Kovac has left Hansi Flick in temporary charge

14 Nov 2019, 15:31 by Darren Wells, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/darren-wells/

Bayern Munich have been urged to re-appoint Pep Guardiola as manager, by club legend Lothar Matthuas.

The Man City boss enjoyed three successful years with the German club from 2013-16, winning back-to-back Bundesliga titles in each season.

He is also said to still be in close contact with Bayern's owners, while his family is reportedly unsettled in Manchester.

And following the sacking of Niko Kovac, Matthaus feels a return for Guardiola to Bavaria would be an ideal fit.

Matthaus told Bild: "If Bayern want to play possession football, there is no better solution than Guardiola.

"It would be a win for Bayern Munich and for the Bundesliga."

Guardiola is under contract with City until the end of the 2020-21 campaign, and there is currently no suggestion he is considering departing the Etihad.

However should he stay at the Sky Blues beyond this season, it will be his longest reign at any of the clubs he has managed.

Bild also claim that Guardiola still owns a flat in the centre of Munich, where his wife Cristina and their three children felt at home.

Cristina has recently returned to Spain to live, after failing to settle in Manchester.

Guardiola has already broke his own three-year rule by staying for a fourth season with Man City, after previously admitting his regret at prolonging his Barcelona tenure, before ending his time at Bayern after three seasons.

Bayern are currently under the caretaker stewardship of Hans-Dieter Flick, who was operating as Kovac's assistant before his sacking.

Flick has led Bayern to two wins in two matches so far, after a heavy 5-1 defeat to Eintracht Frankfurt was enough to convince the club's board to part ways with Kovac.

Bayern Munich chief Karl-Heinz Rummenigge has revealed that Flick will remain in charge "until further notice, giving him a chance to stake a claim for the post on a permanent basis.
