The first thing you need to know is that at the end of 2018, Apple gave the MacBook Air a power boost, and, for first time since it launched 10 years previously, gave the much-loved Mac laptop a redesign. Then in July 2019 Apple made a slight change to the screen - adding True Tone, a technology that means that the colours and brightness can adjust depending on your surrounding light. Other than this change the 2018 and 2019 models are identical, the specs did not change when Apple updated the machines in July 2019. The price did change, however. 

The other change in July 2019 was that Apple stopped selling the older MacBook Air - which used to cost £949 in the UK, or $999 in the US. We think that the older model was overpriced and didn't recommend buying one at Apple's price. However, there may still be a few good deals on that older model - we really wouldn't recommend spending more than £700 on it, though.

How much does the new MacBook Air cost?

When it launched in 2018 the MacBook Air was more expensive than the previous model was, starting at £1,199/$1,199. At the time Apple continued to sell the old 1.8GHz MacBook Air for £949. However, in 2019 Apple discontinued the £949 model and reduced the price of the new-style MacBook Air by £100.

This is the current line up:

  • 1.6GHz, Dual-Core, 8th gen (3.6GHz Turbo Boost), 128GB: £1,099/$1,099
  • 1.6GHz, Dual-Core, 8th gen (3.6GHz Turbo Boost), 256GB: £1,299/$1,299

These specs are exactly the same as the 2018 model, apart from the addition of True Tone in the display. Prices are down £100 per machine compared to the 2018 model.

It's not only the price of the standard model that has decreased. Apple also cut the price of some build-to-order options. For example, there is now the option of a 1TB SSD for £400/$400, rather than a 1.5TB SSD for £2,399/$2,399.

  • 16GB memory: +£180/$180 (£1,579/$1,579)
  • 512GB SSD storage: +£200/$200 (£1,599/$1,599)
  • 1.5TB SSD storage: WAS +£1,000/$1,000 (£2,399/$2,399) NOW 1TB SSD +£400/$400

If you were to choose all the build-to-order options the top-spec model would cost £1,879, rather than £2,579 as it did previously.
