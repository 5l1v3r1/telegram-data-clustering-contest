Livingston could look to add to squad as injury list piles up

Boss Gary Holt joked that he may have to put his boots on as the Lions deal with a lengthy injury list.

14 Nov 2019, 15:06 by Callum Carson, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/callum-carson/

Gary Holt joked that he might have to come out of retirement to help ease his side’s injury woes.

Skipper Alan Lithgow is set to miss several weeks following surgery to remove his appendix, and joins Nicky Devlin and Cece Pepe on the injury list.

Lithgow (31) missed the defeat to Rangers and could struggle to feature again before the winter break, while Frenchman Pepe picked up a calf injury in a recent reserves game.

Devlin’s season could be in doubt after he damaged knee ligaments in last month’s draw with Hearts.

Chris Erskine and Lee Miller have also missed time recently, while Lyndon Dykes and Scott Pittman have only just returned to first team action.

 

Holt said: “Bringing someone in is something we’re looking at, but are they going to be up to speed with us having so many games in quick succession before the winter break?

“We have to have a look at what’s out there because we’re right down to the bare bones just now. I might even have to get my boots back on.

“Alan is going to be a big miss and I’m not sure how long he’ll be out for, but that’s part of football and it’s something we have to deal with.”

He added: “Our injuries are affecting us. Even with Pitts and Lyndon, they’re still playing catch up on the fitness side of things so the break is ideal.”
