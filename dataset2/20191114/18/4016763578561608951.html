Migos Win Copyright Battle Over “Walk It Talk It”

14 Nov 2019, 18:28 by Caitlin Kelley

Migos just won a copyright infringement lawsuit over their Drake-assisted “Walk It Talk It.”

Last October, a North Carolina rapper filed a complaint against the Atlanta trio, claiming that the 2018 hit infringed on the copyright of a 2007 record called “Walk It Like I Talk It.” U.S. District Court Judge Analisa Torres dismissed the complaint this week.

In legal documents obtained by Complex, Torres ruled that the plaintiff Leander Pickett (aka M.O.S.) had no case because the phrase “walk it like I talk it” is too common for copyright protection. The judge pointed to rap songs with similar lyrics: Jeezy’s “3 A.M.,” Paul Wall’s “March N’ Step,” and Wiz Khalifa’s “Be Easy.”

“Because songwriters must be free to borrow sayings and expressions from popular culture, the Second Circuit and courts in this district have found that short and commonplace phrases are not protectable, even when used as the title or repeated lyrics of a song, as is the case here,” Torres said. “The only similarity between the two works at issue, the lyrics ‘walk it like I talk it,’ is not original to the author and is, therefore, not protected by the copyright laws.”

Walk it like I talk it

Walk it like I talk it (yeah)
