Connor Brown murder trial: Teenager relives the moment his friend fell fatally injured to the ground

Leighton Barrass and Ally Gordon deny murdering Connor Brown in an alleyway in Sunderland

14 Nov 2019, 18:16 by Rob Kennedy

A teenager told a court of the moment his fatally wounded friend fell to the ground after allegedly being stabbed following a row over £5.

Prosecutors say Connor Brown was stabbed five times by Leighton Barrass after an altercation in an alleyway.

Newcastle Crown Court has heard Connor was "brave" in punching Barrass after he threatened to stab people as he looked for revenge for being punched by Stephen Nunn.

It is alleged that after Barrass stabbed him, Ally Gordon kicked and stamped on Connor.

Both men deny murdering 18-year-old Connor in Sunderland in February and are standing trial.

Nr Nunn, who was then 18, told police during a video recorded interview, which was played in court, how he bumped into Connor in Gatsby's bar during the Saturday night out and said he was "happy, having a laugh, enjoying himself".

Mr Nunn said he and Connor were in a lane behind the bar when they saw Gordon, who he vaguely knew, with Barrass, who was a stranger to him and he described as having a "babyish" face. He said both men looked "high or drunk".

He told police that he was offered "pills" by the men and refused but when asked if he would swap £10 for two fives, he ended up £5 out of pocket.

He said Barrass, who "looked off his head or wired" then started to get "rowdy, swearing and stuff".

Mr Nunn added: "He started coming into my personal space and I punched him because I felt threatened by way he was going on towards me.

"He wasn't threatening at first but swearing, getting rowdy. The condition he was in made me feel like I was in some sort of threat so I defended myself and I hit him."

Mr Nunn said he planned to go back towards the bar when he heard someone shouting "he's got a knife".

He added: "I didn't really think he had a knife with him. Obviously he did.

"I turned around and there was a massive commotion, like rumble sort of thing, a bunch of people together.

"I remember seeing Connor's white top and an arm come around and hit him.

"I don't know if it was a punch or with the blade but at the time I didn't believe he had a knife with him."

Mr Nunn said his view of exactly what happened was obscured by the movement of people.

He said he felt like he "froze" during the incident, which lasted "a minute at most, might have been 30 seconds".

He added: "All I remember, after the commotion separated, was Connor coming towards me and as he got closer I started to see a bit blood on him.

"It was on his top, he had a white top on, it was easy to notice. As he started to get closer he fell."

Mr Nunn said he ran to get help from nearby doorstaff when he realised what had happened.

He added: "I wanted to stay and help him because he's my mate."

Barrass, 20, of Hartside Square, Sunderland and Gordon, 20, of Polmuir Road, Plains Farm,  Sunderland, who were allegedly acting as a "team", deny murder.

Barrass has admitted having a knife in the back alley that night while Gordon denies possessing a different knife on his way to Scotland.

The trial continues.
