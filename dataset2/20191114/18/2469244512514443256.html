Tragedy of 'supportive friend' found dead in Torquay woods

David Brian Gerry was found in the woodlands near Watcombe Beach

14 Nov 2019, 18:31 by Molly Dowrick

The body of a man described by friends as a 'good and supportive friend' who 'spoke his mind with an eccentric flair' was found in woods in Torquay earlier this year, an inquest heard.

The family of David Brian Gerry, 43, reported that he'd gone missing and had been experiencing suicidal thoughts in May this year.

Devon and Cornwall Police launched a major search operation following concerns for Mr Gerry's welfare.

After more than 20 police, ambulance and Coastguard personnel searched for Mr Gerry, his body was found in the woods near Watcombe Beach.

Assistant Coroner, Deborah Archer, told the inquest at Plymouth Coroners' Court that David Brian Gerry had experienced depression since his early 20s, and had a 'long-standing low mood' and reduced motivation.

He was married, had two children and a great relationship with his family, the inquest was told, and enjoyed playing football, being a key member of a five-a-side team in Torquay.

Mr Gerry had been taking medication and receiving counselling on-and-off for the two years before he died and had 'life-long struggles with low mood,' the coroner said.

He told a therapist he had 'good days and bad days' in June 2018, but his sleep had improved since receiving counselling.

By July 2018, Mr Gerry 'described feeling better,' Ms Archer said and his mood had started to lift.

He soon referred himself to the depression and anxiety team, the inquest heard.

Ms Archer told the inquest that Mr Gerry's parents and close family were a "protector factor" in his life, and it was his assessment by the team that first mentioned suicidal ideas.

But in July 2018 he was thought to have no feelings of self-harm, nor any further 'dark thoughts'.

In October 2018, Mr Gerry separated from his wife, Lucy, and moved back to his parents' house, Ms Archer said.

Mr Gerry's later review, in January 2019, reported that he expressed a strong desire to reduce his medication, but by the May he reported experiencing a low mood and anxiety, with a loss of motivation and tearfulness most days.

However, he had no suicidal ideation, the inquest heard.

The inquest also heard that Mr Gerry had obtained a sick note from his GP and had been signed off work for two weeks.

Ms Archer said this provided 'evidence of forward planning'.

On the last day Mr Gerry was seen, Monday May 20, the inquest heard that Mr Gerry had gone for a meal at his parents' house, and said goodbye to his father with a big 'bear-hug', while he held his mother's hand for a significant amount of time, which the pair found unusual.

This was the last time his parents saw him.

He then went to the Acorn Centre for his weekly football game, and acted 'different that evening', his friend said, he "didn't want to engage in conversation," the inquest heard.

He was found by police and coastguard in the woods near Watcombe beach. He had died by hanging.

Mr Gerry was described by his friend as a "good and supportive friend, a handy-man who'd help me out a lot."

"He spoke his mind with an eccentric flair" and played in a band, the inquest heard, and had run and played for the five-a-side football team at the Acorn Centre in Torquay for over nine years.

He was always calm, never lost his temper and "would welcome anyone to join in," the inquest heard.
