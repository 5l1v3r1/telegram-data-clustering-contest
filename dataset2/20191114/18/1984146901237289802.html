Three young men arrested after shop worker is threatened with a knife in broad daylight robbery

The suspects were arrested after thieves attempted to steal a bike from outside a Northumberland furniture shop

14 Nov 2019, 18:39 by Katie Collings

Three suspected thieves have been arrested after a shop worker was threatened with a knife in broad daylight.

At about 11.10am on Wednesday police received a call that two men had attempted to steal a bike from outside a Northumberland furniture shop.

The thieves had allegedly been attempting to steal a silver Suzuki SV1000 from outside Delcor Furniture on Double Row in Seaton Deleval.

They were confronted by a brave staff member but the culprits then brandished a knife and threatened to stab him.

The men then attempted to make off with the bike but abandoned it a short distance away after again being challenged by the staff member, and a second man who owned the bike.

Police were called and were able to identify three suspects who were arrested and remain in police custody.

Detective Inspector Tam Fowler, who oversees the Operation Benelli team, praised the bravery of the members of the public.

He said: “This would have been a terrifying incident for the two men who confronted the men who were trying to steal the bike.

“There actions have allowed us to respond quickly to make these arrests while we were also able to seize a suspected stolen bike from a nearby address.

“I want to praise the men for the courage they have shown and I hope our quick response shows them how serious we take this offending.

“It is completely unacceptable to steal someone else’s property but it is also very concerning that these offenders were carrying a knife.

“We need the public to work with us and if they see people acting suspiciously in their community then please give us a call.

“It was a fantastic piece of work by both our Op Benelli team and response officers in the area and our CID detectives will now take on this investigation.”

The three men arrested – aged 21, 19, and 17 – remain in police custody at this time.
