UK weather forecast: Heavy rain to bring further flood misery with a week's worth tonight

The Met Office is warning "saturated ground in vulnerable locations" could face more rain this evening and overnight, while snow will continue to fall for some

14 Nov 2019, 18:02 by Abigail O'Leary

Heavy rain and snow will continue to hammer parts of the country throughout tonight as "vulnerable" towns in northern England are being warned to brace for further misery.

The Met Office is warning this evening could bring heavy rain for central and northern England - leading to further disruption and flooding.

Two weather warning remains in place until later tonight.

One covers the midlands areas including, Birmingham and Leicester, stretching into parts of the south west and mid and south Wales.

This warning is in place until 23:45 on Thursday evening.

 

A second yellow weather warning is also in place throughout this evening, covering areas including Sheffield, Lincolnshire and Nottingham.

Forecasters are warning of the potential for further flooding and possible damage to buildings and homes.

Aidan McGivern from the Met Office also warned of more snow on some higher ground.

He said: "Throughout this evening, rain and hill snow will continue in parts and we will see wet weather for much of the rest of Thursday.

"More rain and hill snow could be seen in Wales, Western England and Scotland.

"A swathe of cloud and rain continue in places with this heavy rain falling on to saturated ground and in vulnerable locations.

"We could see 20-30mm of rain in South Yorkshire, Lincolnshire, north midlands, the east and South east Wales."

"There's risk of localised flooding and tricky driving conditions - with yellow warnings there."

Sleet and snow will move in for parts of west England and Wales, with these wintery showers mainly falling on ground above 400m in the Welsh mountains.

Rain is expected to turn to showers by the end of the night.

Temperatures will remain cold overnight, with a widespread frost by Friday morning.

By tomorrow, temperatures could plunge to as low as -6 on higher ground in Scotand.

Friday promises to be brighter with more winter sunshine in parts.

Aidan added: "Sunshine will come through for eastern England in complete contrast to Thursday's weather, but we will still see one or two showers for northern England, north east Scotland and parts of East Anglia and the south east, west Wales and Cornwall."

On Friday, Northern Ireland and most of Scotland will enjoy some good November sunshine, feeling brighter, dry but chilly.

 

Five day weather forecast

This Evening and Tonight:

Rain and hill snow over England and Wales becoming confined to northern England and far south of Scotland by dawn.

Northern Ireland and the rest of Scotland clearer but also wintry showers and isolated icy patches in some coastal areas.

Friday:

England and Wales drier than Thursday, but windy with some rain developing over East Anglia and southeast England later.

Northern Ireland and most of Scotland brighter and drier, but cold.

Outlook for Saturday to Monday:

Remaining rather cold with further rain and showers, along with patchy overnight frost and fog.

Fog/frost becoming more widespread by Monday as drier, colder weather spreads in from the north-west.
