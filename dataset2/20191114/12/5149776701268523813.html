This is what Nottingham Forest boss Sabri Lamouchi has said about January transfer window

Will the Reds add to their ranks when the window opens?

14 Nov 2019, 12:49 by Sarah Clapson

The January transfer window is fast approaching, and work is sure to already be going on behind the scenes at Nottingham Forest on new player comings and goings.

Twelve new signings were added in the summer, with that business paying dividends as the Reds currently sit fifth in the Championship table.

Head coach Sabri Lamouchi has been very guarded about what will happen when the winter window opens, with his focus at the moment very much on what is happening on the pitch.

Here is what the Frenchman has already said about his January plans...

Lamouchi on strikers

Forest have already been linked with a number of players, including Newcastle United striker Dwight Gayle.

When asked if up front is an area which may need strengthening, Lamouchi has said: “January, for me, is so far away. There’s no point talking about players who are not in my squad.

“I want to just focus on my players. I am happy with them.”

Lamouchi had previously admitted he would like to have seen his attacking ranks bolstered further in the summer.

Although the club did look at free agents available, the Frenchman considered there to be a lack of suitable options.

Lamouchi on Tyler Walker

On the possibility of whether he could recall Tyler Walker, who has been in good form on loan at Lincoln City, he said back in September: “We have reflected on this, but unfortunately he can come back just in January, not before.

“It is good to see him doing well, of course.

“Tyler in Lincoln is doing well, so that is one example of the benefit of going on loan.”

Lamouchi on defenders

Defender Alexander Milosevic last month left the club “by mutual consent” after failing to make a single appearance this season.

The Reds still have centre-backs Michael Dawson, Joe Worrall, Chema, Tobias Figueiredo, Yohan Benalouane and Michael Hefele on their books.

Asked if Milosevic’s departure could free up space in the squad or funds ahead of January, Lamouchi gave nothing away.

“You are talking about the next window,” he said at the time. “We have 11 or 12 games to play until then, so I just want to focus on Luton (who were the club’s next opponents).”

Lamouchi on recruitment duo

In October, Forest unveiled Francois Modesto as technical director and Jose Anigo as head of international recruitment, although both are understood to have already been working at the club for some time.

Speaking about their appointments, Lamouchi said they would have an eye on the “future”.

“It is good for the club. They are working for the future,” he said when discussing the duo last month.

“I know them from before, so it is good to have them with us.

“They help because the manager cannot do everything alone, so they anticipate some (transfer) windows and the (transfer) market, and to work for the long-term with the club.”

Both Modesto and Anigo are believed to have played a role in the Reds’ summer recruitment.

Asked if the announcement of the pair comes with a view to January, Lamouchi said at the time: “Yes, but this is not my job for now.

“Before January we have 14 games to play, so I just want to focus on my job on the pitch now.

“But the club starts to work for the future, of course.”
