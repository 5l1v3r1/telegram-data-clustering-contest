Met Office on where and when it will snow in Devon today

More wintry showers are expected to fall on parts of Dartmoor, Exmoor and Okehampton

14 Nov 2019, 11:34 by Jamie Hawkins

More snow is expected to fall in Devon today, according to the Met Office.

Heavy wintry showers fell on parts of Dartmoor, Exmoor and Okehampton on Wednesday afternoon, which caused travel chaos for many.

Snow settled across Dartmoor overnight, with temperatures at Princetown struggling to get about freezing on Thursday.

Police said around two inches fell in some areas, with further snow expected at some point today.

The latest weather forecast from the Met Office suggests there will be flurries in Okehampton and Dartmoor throughout the day.

A yellow warning for snow was in place on the Somerset border on Thursday morning, although that has since been lifted.

Where and when it is expected to snow in Devon:

Met Office forecast for south west England:

Today:

Northern parts of the region will experience a wet, cold and windy morning, but some temporary brightness for southern coastal regions is likely. A wet afternoon rain will turn lighter. Maximum temperature 7 °C.

Tonight:

The rain will gradually disperse throughout the night, but most will stay overcast with the odd drop of rain. The cloud and strong winds should prevent a widespread frost forming. Minimum temperature 1 °C.

Friday:

Another overcast and cold day for most, but there could be some brighter periods in the morning for coastal parts of Cornwall. Breezy elsewhere, with some light showers at times. Maximum temperature 8 °C.

Outlook for Saturday to Monday:

Saturday will be another cold and breezy day, with scattered showers, but sunny spells in the west. A chance of brighter, but still cold weather on Sunday and Monday.
