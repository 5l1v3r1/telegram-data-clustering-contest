Seven new routes announced on Flybe's 2020 summer schedule

Tickets go on sale today

14 Nov 2019, 11:19 by Howard Lloyd

Tickets for Flybe's new 2020 summer schedule go on sale today, including seven brand new routes.

The Exeter-based airline will feature 104 routes in total with a choice of up to 2,500 flights per week across the UK and Europe.

These include seven new routes – six from London Southend and one between Manchester and Stuttgart.

Exeter Airport to Chambery is one of eight winter ski routes that has been extended through easter.

Tickets go on sale on the Flybe website for travel between March 29 and October 24 next year.

The airline, which together with Stobart Air, is owned by Connect Airways, will next year launch its operations under the new name of Virgin Connect following the completion of a full re-brand as part of the extended Virgin family. 

Highlights of Flybe’s 2020 Summer schedule

For travel Sunday , March 29 – Saturday, October 24 2020 inclusive

  • Six new routes from London Southend Airport – Belfast City (18 flights per week): Edinburgh (18 p/w): Glasgow (18 p/w): Newcastle (13 p/w): Isle of Man (7 p/w): Jersey (up to 2p/w)
  • Four continuing routes from London Southend - Caen, Groningen, Newquay and Rennes
  • 8 winter ski routes extended through Easter - to Chambery from Birmingham, Exeter and Southampton; and to Geneva from Cardiff, Exeter, Isle of Man, Jersey and Southampton

Connect Airways’ CEO Mark Anderson said: “I am very excited with our new Summer 2020 schedule and its focus on our business customers and regional communities.

"The schedule includes many of our most popular high frequency services with better timings to accommodate our frequent fliers as well as new flights from London Southend airport.

"By increasing our presence at London Southend our customers will also, for the first-time next summer, benefit from Flybe’s ability to create a truly customer-centric regional network.

“This schedule strengthens Flybe’s commitment to providing unparalleled connectivity from regional airports to destinations across the UK, Europe and beyond."
