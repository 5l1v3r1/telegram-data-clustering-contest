Huge drugs bust in Efford as six men arrested

The arrests come following community concerns around drug dealing in Efford

14 Nov 2019, 11:36 by Carl Eve

Six men have been arrested by specialist police tasked with tackling organised crime groups in Plymouth.

Officers from Plymouth’s Proactive Converter Team carried out a series of searches late last night at a number of addresses in the Efford area.

The searches came after a car was stop checked and searched in Plymouth last night. Two men - the driver and a passenger -  were arrested which in turn led to search warrants being executed at properties in Dartmeet Avenue, Embankment Road, Higher Efford Road, Stour Close and Efford Walk. 

By the end of the night’s work the team had seized what they have described as “several thousand pounds worth" of suspected class A drugs - cocaine and MDMA - as well as a quantity of class B cannabis. Police also seized a large amount of cash suspected of being the proceeds of criminal activities.

Officer say they have also seized what they believe to be an industrial press and it is suspected this may have been used to ‘press’ the cocaine into tightly-sealed packs for transportation.

Six men – aged 53, 50, 29, 28 and two aged 27 – were arrested on suspicion of conspiracy to supply class A and class B drugs and money laundering. They were taken to Charles Cross custody suite where they await interview. Police say all six men are from the Efford area.

A spokesman for the Plymouth Proactive Converter team told Plymouth Live: “These actions come following community concerns around drug dealing in Efford. We have arrested a number of men we suspect of being involved in the supply of class A and class B drugs.

“We are keen to get further information from the community regarding the illegal supply of drugs, particularly regarding other individuals or groups who may not yet be known to police. The public’s help is vital in ensuring our communities are safe from drugs supply and the violence which inevitably goes with it.

“We would urge people to contact us directly via the 101 number, or via email on  101@dc.police.uk  or call people can call the independent charity Crimestoppers anonymously on 0800 555111.”
