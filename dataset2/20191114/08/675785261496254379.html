Arsenal engulfed in bitter spat as Raul Sanllehi 'blocks appointment' of club legend to Gunners board

Arsenal board members including Sir Chips Keswick are reportedly keen on the appointment of David O'Leary - but head of football Sanllehi is at odds with them

14 Nov 2019, 08:10 by James Whaling, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/james-whaling/

Arsenal have reportedly been engulfed in a bitter row with chairman Sir Chips Keswick and head of football Raul Sanllehi at odds over the potential appointment of club legend David O'Leary to the Gunners board.

Keswick is keen to enlist the help of O'Leary as a football consultant in an official capacity to advise the board on football matters.

But the move has been met by resistance from Sanllehi, report the Daily Mail.

Sanllehi appointed former Arsenal midfielder Eduo as the club's first technical director over the summer and the Spaniard - who was previously at Barcelona - seemingly doesn't feel the need for O'Leary in a newly-created football position.

O'Leary is currently a club ambassador at the Emirates and is seen at the majority of Arsenal home games.

He spent 19 years in north London as a player and has close links to some senior figures at the club - including Keswick who has been chairman since 2013.

Arsenal have made a disappointing start to the season with defeat at Leicester on Saturday leaving them eight points off the top-four pace after just 12 fixtures.

They are already 17 points adrift of pace-setting Liverpool at the top of the league and face an uphill battle to restore their position at Europe's top table.

Boss Unai Emery is under huge pressure to keep his job with the Gunners having not tasted victory in the Premier League since early October.

There have also been reports of some players mocking Emery behind his back for his perceived poor grasp of English.
