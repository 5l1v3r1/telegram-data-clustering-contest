'If I Give Messi 10 Balls, He Will Score 11 Goals!'

14 Nov 2019, 08:28 by Kurt Buckerfield

A LaLiga manager has discussed new ways of trying to stop FC Barcelona superstar Lionel Messi from scoring free-kicks, but appears to have accepted there is no way to thwart the five-time Ballon d'Or winner.

Ronaldo Facing Two-Year Ban?

The Argentine has struck on four occasions from direct set-pieces for the Blaugrana already this season, including two in his latest match against Celta Vigo.

Messi and Barca will take on Leganes next after the international break, and their manager Javier Aguirre does not believe there is a way to properly stop the 32-year-old.

Aguirre was even asked if taking away a wall when Messi stands over free-kicks might help, but he was not confident this would be an effective strategy.

"Well, I have no idea (how to stop him), because every team that I have faced him with, I could never stop him, that I remember. A small draw here and there, perhaps," he told Cadena SER.

"One of the coaching staff proposed it today, and I told him, 'Look, if I leave Messi outside the box against (Leganes goalkeeper) Pichu Cuellar, I give him 10 balls and he'll score 11 goals'. There is no way.

"In the end, we have discussed it a lot (not deploying a wall for free-kicks). If we put one guy under the post, another laying on the ground... we try 20 000 ways.

"The most logical thing to do is to avoid unnecessary fouls. But it's also not possible, because he escapes from you, the scoundrel!"

13 Players Who've Scored 50 Free-Kicks Or More

Messi has scored 52 free-kicks in his career so far, and is among 13 players who have scored 50 or more times from set-pieces in their career.
