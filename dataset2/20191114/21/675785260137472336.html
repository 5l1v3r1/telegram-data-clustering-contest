Gabriel Jesus insists Man City can still land third successive Premier League title

Liverpool's 3-1 win at Anfield on Sunday put Jurgen Klopp's side nine points clear of the reigning Premier League champions

14 Nov 2019, 21:07 by David Anderson, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/david-anderson/

Gabriel Jesus claims Manchester City can chase Liverpool down again in the title race.

Jesus insists the battle for the Premier League is far from over, despite Jurgen Klopp’s side’s commanding nine-point advantage over the champions.

City overturned Liverpool ’s seven-point lead at the turn of the year to claim the title by a single point.

They also pipped Liverpool for the Premier League crown in 2014 when Brendan Rodgers’s side were in control with just three games remaining.

Defiant Jesus claims City still have plenty of matches to claw back Liverpool’s lead with over two thirds of the campaign to go.

“There are 26 matches left and that’s a long way out even to think who will win the Premier League,” said the Brazil striker.

“Liverpool are playing really well. They were beaten only once last season, but we will take them on and keep fighting for the title again.

“We will try to do our maximum and we will recover. We know we have the capacity to do this. Obviously, we want to win the championship.”

Jesus has put the title race to one side to focus on Brazil’s friendly against Argentina tonight in Riyadh before they face South Korea in Abu Dhabi on Tuesday.

Neymar is missing with a hamstring injury and Jesus claims working with Sergio Aguero can help him realise his dream of emulating the Paris St Germain star’s 61 goals for Brazil.

“The dream is to follow what he has achieved,” said Jesus, who has 18 goals from his 37 caps.

“But I know I have to work hard in every game, every training session for City and for Brazil, and I know I have to score goals.

“I am also learning at City to do this, working with Sergio. He’s a top player, the record scorer for the club, and I can only learn from him. He scores almost every game.”

Rodri is also upbeat about City and praised Pep Guardiola, claiming the Blues boss has already made him a better player.

City’s record £62.8million signing, who is with Spain for Friday’s Euro 2020 qualifier against Malta in Cadiz, says he is learning so much from him.

“Since the beginning of the season I have added a lot of new knowledge,” said the midfielder.

“It has helped me a lot that he played in the same position as me. He gives me lots of advice on how he sees football.

“I’m growing a lot, not only tactically, but individually in many aspects I wasn’t aware a central midfielder should have, like driving with the ball, which is something that is demanded in the Premier League.

“Obviously other factors than the coach can influence things, but we have the best coach in that sense.”
