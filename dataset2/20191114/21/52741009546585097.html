Regional council approves the development of over 600 affordable housing units over the next 10 years

It's part of a master plan which was approved at Wednesday's council meeting

14 Nov 2019, 21:00 by Casey MacDonald

Regional council has given the green light to create 638 affordable homes on five Waterloo Region Housing properties over the next 10 years. 

It's part of a master plan which was approved at Wednesday's council meeting. 

The master plan was developed over the last two years with the help of the community. 

Over the past five years, over 200 new affordable housing units have been built through regional investments, with 92 units currently under construction and 61 in development. 

The region will also form an innovation table early in the new year, which will include stakeholders from outside the housing sector in order to identify new ways to generate affordable housing. 

Currently, Waterloo Region Housing owns and operates over 2700 community housing units in Waterloo Region.
