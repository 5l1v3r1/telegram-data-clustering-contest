Plymouth Argyle's Jose Baxter gamble did not work out but it was worth trying

The 27-year-old midfielder has left the Pilgrims by mutual consent because of injury

14 Nov 2019, 21:28 by Chris Errington

In the end it did not work out for either Jose Baxter or Plymouth Argyle but it was worth a try.

The 27-year-old midfielder’s stay at Home Park was only a brief one, but even in that short time his technical ability was clear for all to see.

However, a troublesome calf injury which he could not shake off has led to him leaving the Pilgrims by mutual consent.

The short-term contract which Baxter signed just before the start of the season would have ended in January.

With no return from his injury in sight, as manager Ryan Lowe revealed during his media call at Home Park on Thursday, parting company now was sadly the sensible option.

It is a shame because Baxter showed in most of his five starts and seven substitute appearances for Argyle that when fully fit he would be a valuable asset.

He could never get fully fit, though, after arriving at Home Park so late in pre-season as his calf problem stopped him in his tracks.

Baxter said: “Because of a recurring calf injury that I have suffered this season, it seemed sensible to come to an agreement with the club so that I and they can both plan for the future.

“I am very grateful for everyone associated with Argyle for welcoming me with open arms.

“My stay has been short but memorable and it is a fantastic club with an amazing manager and coaching staff, great people who work behind the scenes and a fantastic Green Army following.

“I wish everyone at the club all the success in the world.”

Baxter, who burst onto the football scene as a teenage talent with Everton in the Premier League, has had well-documented off-the-pitch issues over recent years.

They have included two failed drugs tests and also having to deal with his mental health.

Nonetheless, Lowe was a huge admirer of the ability of Baxter and ready to give him a chance at Argyle.

It was a low-risk gamble on Lowe's part with a short-term contract until January agreed so that both parties could assess how it went before committing to any long-term deal.

As it turned out, Baxter’s stay at Home Park did not even last that long due to his persistent calf problem.

Lowe said: “We wish Jose all the best in his recovery from his injury and in his future career following that.”

The announcement that Baxter had left Argyle came on Thursday evening, and only hours after Lowe had spoken about the midfielder’s injury situation with the media.

At the time he talked to reporters there was no indication from the Pilgrims’ boss that Baxter would be moving on.

However, the pair had a discussion later in the day and they agreed to end his contract by mutual consent.

Had Baxter been able to get fully fit, Argyle would have had one of the best players in League Two on their hands.

Unfortunately, despite glimpses of his talent, Baxter’s time at Argyle will be seen as a case of what might have been.
