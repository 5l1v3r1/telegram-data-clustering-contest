England player ratings as Three Lions run riot with Montenegro thrashing at Wembley

ENGLAND 7-0 MONTENEGRO: Harry Kane scored three, with Alex Oxlade-Chamberlain, Marcus Rashford and Tammy Abraham all on the scoresheet also

14 Nov 2019, 21:37 by Darren Lewis, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/darren-lewis/

England's Three Lions ripped Montenegro apart in their 1000th game to book their tickets for next summer’s European Championships.

Gareth Southgate’s stars - the youngest side in 60 years - shrugged off the controversy surrounding Raheem Sterling’s bust up with Joe Gomez on Monday to demolish Montenegro.

Liverpool defender Gomez, however, was booed by sections of the Wembley crowd when he made his belated appearance as a 70th-minute substitute. The disappointing reception came in stark contrast to Sterling’s applause for him from the stands.

Skipper Harry Kane had earlier grabbed a hat-trick while Leicester left-back Ben Chilwell provided a hat-trick of assists with Alex Oxlade- Chamberlain and Marcus Rashford joining in the fun.

Montenegro defender Aleksander Sofranac put the ball through his own net with 24 minutes left for 6-0.

Kane’s treble left England top of Group A and means he has now scored 31 times for his country. The Spurs hitman now needs just nine more to go level with Michael Owen on 40 international goals.

Kane is the first player to score a hat-trick in consecutive appearances for England at Wembley, having netted three against Bulgaria last time out.

He is also now England's all-time highest scoring starting captain with 24 goals.

England had raced into a 4-0 lead after just half an hour - the earliest they had done so for 73 years - before Kane added their fifth shortly before half time.

A host of Three Lions legends, including Paul Gascoigne, Trevor Francis, David Seaman and Wayne Rooney were then presented to the Wembley crowd at half time.
