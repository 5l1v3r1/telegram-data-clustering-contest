Birmingham and Solihull flood map as River Blythe and River Cole threaten to burst

The River Blythe is affected and there are five warnings in place for the River Cole which threads through Small Heath, Sparkhill, Hay Mills, Greet and Solihull

14 Nov 2019, 16:45 by birminghammail.co.uk

Thousands of homes could be at risk as flood warnings were issued for Birmingham and Solihull tonight.

The Government is warning that flooding is expected in some West Midlands areas ‘with immediate action required.’

The River Blythe at Earlswood near Solihull is under threat and five warnings are in place for the River Cole.

The River Cole threads through Bordesley, Small Heath, Sparkhill, Tyseley, Hay Mills, Greet, through to Solihull. There are already multiple road closures in Solihull and a section of the A38 Bristol Road South is submerged.

Alerts have been issued for the following:

  • River Blythe at Earlswood from Valley Road
  • River Cole in Sparkhill stretching from Warwick Road all the way down to Formans Road
  • River Cole in Greet from Warwick Road to Seeleys Road
  • River Cole at Hay Mills around the junction of the Small Heath Highway, Kings Road and Coventry Road
  • River Cole at Sarehole in Hall Green from Cole Bank Road, Sarehole Road, Bankside up to the Stratford Road
  • River Cole at Solihull Lodge around the High Street area

A video showed a section of the A38 Bristol Road in Longbridge currently flooded as heavy rain causes chaos across Birmingham.

The dramatic scenes came after the city was lashed by torrential rain and heavy winds.

The recording was taken at around 1pm on Thursday on the A38 near a Premier Inn and McDonald’s.

The River Rea is also raging past the Custard Factory in Digbeth.

West Midlands Fire Service said: “When roads are wet, or covered in ice or snow, take extra care when driving. Stopping distances are much longer and your car might be more difficult to control.”
