I always think about beating Magnus Carlsen: Anish Giri

14 Nov 2019, 16:14 by Prasad RS

CHENNAI: The upcoming Tata Steel chess tournament in Kolkata - to be held from November 22-26 - is headlined by Magnus Carlsen who returns to the country six years after he was crowned a world champion.

Carlsen taking on five-time former world champion Viswanathan Anand is being given the top billing. However, the tournament will be special for one more player - Anish Giri - for whom the trip to India will be a homecoming of sorts.

"Despite having an Indian grandmother and a Nepalese father, I have never been to India before. I have been to Nepal a couple of times, which isn't that far. I have a lot of Indian friends, and so I have some idea of what I am about to experience," the world no. 5 told TOI. Anish's father Sanjay hails from Nepal while his mother Olga is a Russian.

A packed schedule may deny the Dutch Grandmaster a chance to visit his grandmother's birthplace -Varanasi - but he doesn't mind seeing a few places in Kolkata or even try the local cuisine.

"I was hoping to make a bigger trip out of it, but the schedule is way too full. But I wouldn't mind trying the Indian food as long as it's not too spicy. I already have a few recommendations on the restaurants I should visit," said the 25-year-old.

The event in Kolkata will give Anish another chance to take on Carlsen. The two never miss an opportunity for a friendly banter on the social media, and Anish is looking forward to the contest.

"I always think about beating Carlsen. In that sense, I don't give much importance to where the battle takes place. This tournament will give a chance to meet the Indian chess fans as well."

Anish, who won the Shenzhen Masters this year, is keen on putting together consistent performances before he gears up for the 2020 Candidates which will be held in Yekaterinburg, Russia from March 15 to April 5.

"I am about to qualify for the Candidates by the average rating of this year, and with that in mind I can't be unsatisfied (about how the year has gone). Winning the Shenzhen Masters was one of my highlights this year. I did well in Zagreb and Saint Louis tournaments despite having a bad start. I want to approach Candidates next year at the peak of my performance," said Anish.
