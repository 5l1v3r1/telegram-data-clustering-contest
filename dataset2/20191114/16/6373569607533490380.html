Leyton Orient sack former Plymouth Argyle manager Carl Fletcher after just 29 days

Fletcher was in charge of the East London club for only six games

14 Nov 2019, 16:08 by Chris Errington

Former Plymouth Argyle manager Carl Fletcher has been sacked by Leyton Orient only 29 days after being appointed as their head coach.

Fletcher's first match in charge saw the O's triumph 4-0 away to Grimsby Town on October 19.

They then lost 4-0 at Argyle the following Tuesday, which was the start of a run of five games without a win.

Last time out they lost 2-1 at home to non-League Maldon and Tiptree in the FA Cup first round.

With Orient sitting in 16th position in League Two, ahead of a home game against Scunthorpe United on Saturday, the club issued a statement about Fletcher on Thursday afternoon.

It read: Leyton Orient has today parted company with head coach, Carl Fletcher, with immediate effect.

Chairman, Nigel Travis, said, “It’s unfortunate for the Club that we have parted company with Carl after such a short time, particularly as we are a club that tries to preserve stability.

“However, we felt this was in the best interests of all parties and we thank him for his hard work and wish him every success in the future.”

Ross Embleton, Danny Webb and Jobi McAnuff will take control of first-team affairs in the meantime, as they did for an interim period at the beginning of the season.
