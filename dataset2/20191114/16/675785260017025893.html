Pep Guardiola writes to 114 Man City fans and offers them VIP tickets to Shakhtar tie

The fans made the long trip to watch City in Ukraine and have been rewarded for their support with a night of entertainment in the club's hospitality suites

14 Nov 2019, 16:58 by Darren Wells, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/darren-wells/

Pep Guardiola has personally invited 114 Man City fans for a VIP hospitality experience at their Champions League clash with Shakhtar Donetsk.

The City boss signed letters to each fan, whom had all travelled to the Ukraine for the away Group C fixture in mid-September.

Guardiola's note thanked them for their 'dedicated support', as well as details of the reward for the match on November 26.

Those that made the 4,000-mile round trip will now be the beneficiaries of one of City's high end hospitality packages, which are priced at anything from £359.

City fan Darren Page shared a picture of his letter on Twitter , along with the message: "Thanks Pep and @ManCity."

The letter read: "Dear Darren, on Wednesday 18th September you and 113 other dedicated supporters made the long trip to watch us play against Shakhtar Donetsk in Ukraine, and I would like to take this opportunity to say thank you.

"We were so pleased to start our Champions League campaign with a victory. We faced a tough Shakhtar team but I felt that we played well, controlled the game and deserved to win.

"To have your support in these games means so much, and as I have always said, we are nothing without our fans."

It was penned by Guardiola himself, who added: "'As a gesture of our thanks, we would like to invite you to a City hospitality experience at the return fixture vs Shakhtar Donetsk on 26th November 2019.

"On behalf of the players and the staff, I would like to thank you again for your incredible support. Pep Guardiola."

All recipients of the letter are now set for a nights entertainment on behalf of the club.

City's top Tunnel Club VVIP package costs £12,000 for 10 people, and includes heated and padded seats behind the team dugouts, five-course fine dining; and unlimited drinks in a private booth.

It also boasts a "world-first in football" view at players when they line up in the tunnel through a glass window.

The Tunnel Club Premier package offers the same perks, minus the private booth, for £600 per person.

Or the fans could be treated to a night in the Chairman's Club, which sees them join directors and celebrities in a box on the half-way line, complete with the five-course meal, all for £359 each.

And the game itself is still of large importance for City, whom can seal their progression to the knockout stage with a win over Shakhtar.

They are currently top on 10 points - five clear of the Ukrainian outfit - with Dinamo Zagreb joint second in the table, and Atalanta bottom on just one point.
