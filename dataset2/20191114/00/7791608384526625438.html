Jameela Jamil Recreates John Lennon & Yoko Ono’s Rolling Stone Magazine Cover

14 Nov 2019 by Izza Sofia

Body activist Jameela Jamil and comedian Celeste Barber will make your jaw drop with their steamy kiss on InStyle magazine’s December 2019 cover.
Jamil, posed in an elegant black satin dress with a sweetheart neckline and high slit, smirks as Barber kisses her on the cheek. The two are reprising John Lennon and Yoko Ono’s famous 1981 Rolling Stone cover.
In this edition, the actress wears her long black locks down and keeps her face natural with pink lip color and eyeliner. Barber, on the other hand, dons a nude bodysuit with her hair styled in curls and smoky eyeshadow on her lids.
Barber and Jamil’s playful editorial sees the pair going on a girl’s night out, filled with tears, alcohol and pizza.
In contrast, the original cover features a naked Lennon in a fetal position and curling up to a fully-clothed Ono.
The accompanying InStyle magazine shoot envisions Barber in a glamorous green gown while Jamil dazzles in sequins. Another photo shows them sharing a carb fest, with Jamil in a ruffled dress with a train whereas Barber puts on a pink and white sequin gown.

The pair explain that they are hoping to break barriers and are leading the best life, all in their own terms, of course.
Jamil details that she has been having health problems due to the measures she had taken when she was younger to get thinner. She elaborates that she recently turned down US$3 million from detox brands, because she strongly believes in encouraging people to live their best lives no matter their body shape or size.
