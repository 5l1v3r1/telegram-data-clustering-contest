Famous ‘Star Wars’ Scene With Hans And Greedo Gets A Baffling Edit On Disney+

14 Nov 2019 by Yimin Huang

Disney+ has a very wide selection of Star Wars content, including the original 1977 release of Episode IV-A New Hope.
In one particularly controversial scene, Harrison Ford’s Han Solo was seen firing at the bounty hunter Greedo after they met at the Mos Eisley cantina on Tatooine, per Huffpost.
The scene was later famously edited to make it look like Greedo fired first, as the series creator George Lucas did not want Han Solo to be perceived as a “cold-blooded killer,” per Washington Post. The edited scene infuriated many fans, who donned “Han Shot First” T-shirts in protest.
Now, there is a newer version of the scene, where Greedo shouts something bizarre at Han Solo that sounds like “Maclunkey.” Apparently, the term was said by none other than Lucas himself, as stated in Huffpost. This version is the one shown on Disney+.
As there are no English subtitles for the scene, resourceful fans who know the meaning of the term have posted the explanation on Twitter. The definition is supported by the website Slate, where the phrase is revealed to be “ma klounkee.”
The term was also used in the 1999 prequel The Phantom Menace and can be translated to “I’ll end you.”
The odd edit of the scene also prompted hilarious responses from Star Wars fans, with many making the word “Maclunkey” the butt of jokes.
