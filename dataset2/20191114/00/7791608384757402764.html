Incredible Algorithm Wipes Out Water From Underwater Photos To Stunning Effect

14 Nov 2019 by Mikelle Leow

If you’ve always wanted to make your underwater shots look as vivid as those aquarium screensavers, a new algorithm has made it possible to attain that vibrancy without Photoshop. In fact, Photoshop might pale in comparison in this aspect, as the AI promises to render “physically accurate” images without artificial color enhancements.
Created by MIT oceanographer and engineer Derya Akkaynak and Tali Treibitz from the University of Haifa, the algorithm is aptly called Sea-thru, and is devised to “remove the water” from underwater photos.
This takes away the greenish-blue tint of the pictures, revealing the true color of ocean organisms like fish and coral reefs.
To train the program, Akkaynak captured over 1,100 photos from “two optically different water bodies,” with each scene containing a color palette to help the algorithm gauge their actual hues.
Instead of artificially colorizing sea objects, Sea-thru analyzes how light travels in the water of a particular photo. As such, one drawback of the algorithm is that multiple photos entailing various angles and distances of the same scene are required to visualize its true color.

From there, the tool reverses color distortion from the water, pixel by pixel, to retrieve the image’s colors.
