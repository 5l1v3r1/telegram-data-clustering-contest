Israel and Gaza start truce after two days of deadly violence

14 Nov 2019 by BNN

In the Middle East, after two days of military attacks between Israel and Palestinian militants that has left 32 people dead, a ceasefire has come into force, according to Egyptian, UN and militant representatives.

British public broadcaster BBC reports that Gaza’s Hamas-run health ministry says 32 Palestinians were killed by Israeli fire. On the side of Israel, 63 people have been treated for injuries and stress symptoms in Israel.

The violence began after an Israeli air strike killed a Palestinian Islamic Jihad commander.

Israel has not confirmed the ceasefire.

UN Middle East peace envoy Nickolay Mladenov stated both the UN and neighbouring Egypt had «worked hard to prevent the most dangerous escalation in and around Gaza from leading to war.»
