How To Watch UFC Fight Night 164 Prelims

14 Nov 2019 by Trent Reinsmith

The UFC heads to Brazil on Saturday for UFC Fight Night 164. The ESPN+ streaming event, which is headlined by a light heavyweight matchup between Jan Blachowicz and Ronaldo “Jacare” Souza, marks the promotion’s final 2019 visit to Brazil. The bout is Souza’s debut at 205 pounds. Blachowicz is coming off a knockout win over former UFC middleweight champion Luke Rockhold, who was competing in his first light heavyweight contest.In the co-main event, ex-UFC light heavyweight champion Mauricio “Shogun” Rua, who has been competing since 2002 and a member of the UFC roster since September 2007, faces Paul Craig in a light heavyweight matchup. Craig has been fighting since 2013. Both fighters are coming off “Performance of the Night” bonus-inning stoppage victories.

Before the five-fight main card, the UFC presents seven preliminary bouts on ESPN+. 

At the top of the preliminary card, Sergio Moraes, who is looking to avoid the first three-fight losing skid of his professional career, faces James Krause in a welterweight contest. Krause is on a five-fight winning streak. Krause, who has had success as a coach and trainer, is returning to the octagon for the first time since a knockout win over Warlley Alves in August 2018.

Also on the prelims is ex-bantamweight champion Renan Barao. Barao has struggled mightily since he lost the title in 2014. He had a 32-fight unbeaten streak headed into his title defense against T.J. Dillashaw. Dillashaw won that contest by TKO in the fifth round. Barao is 2-7 in his past nine outings. He hasn’t secured a fighter since he defeated Phillipe Nover in September 2016. Barao faces Douglas Silva de Andrade in a featherweight matchup. Andrade began his career on a 23-fight unbeaten run. He is 3-3 since he joined the UFC and coming off a TKO loss to Petr Yan.

HOW TO WATCH OR STREAM UFC FIGHT NIGHT 164

UFC Fight Night 164 takes place at Ginasio do Ibirapuera in Sao Paulo, Brazil. The entire card streams on ESPN+. Prelims begin at 5 p.m. ET, followed by the main card at 8 p.m. ET.
