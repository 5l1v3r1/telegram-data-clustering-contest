Things to do today in Bengaluru

14 Nov 2019, 00:30 by Bangalore Mirror Bureau

Watch a film
The movie I Dream In Another Language portrays the journey of Martin, a young linguist. He arrives in a small jungle settlement hoping to record a conversation between two elderly men, the last remaining speakers of the Zikril language. Unfortunately for him, the men haven’t spoken to each
other in 50 years. So will they talk to each other, will they not? Find out.

WHERE: The Courtyard, 105 KH Road, Shanthi Nagar WHEN: 6.30pm-8.30pm, November 14
ENTRY: Free CALL: 8040124484

It’s showtime
Have you heard of Binodini Dasi? A play tonight will give you a glimpse of the rise and fall of this famous South Asian actress. Born to prostitution, she started her career as a courtesan and eventually joined Calcutta’s National Theatre. As Nati Binodini, the play moves forward, it introduces several acquaintances of Binodini. Then it slowly takes a steep turn and brings to light the sacrifice, pain, betrayal, and misfortune of the famous actress, and how the mentally devastated actress left the stage at the age of 25.

WHERE: Ranga Shankara, JP Nagar
WHEN: 7.30pm, November 14-15
ENTRY: `200, bookmyshow.com

Learn about arts
Dr Rekha Raju will present a thematic performance called Jeeva – Gratitude to Life. She aims to introduce classical dance to the underprivileged children of the school where she will perform, where more than 500 students get free education.

WHERE: Jeevodya, No. 70, behind Petra Nilaya Convent, Kannuru WHEN: 11am
onwards, November 14 ENTRY: Free

Rewind and play
Crystal Codes is a five-piece band from Bengaluru, which covers progressive rock, blues, jazz, country and instrumental music. Their journey started from a basement where the members would jam and cover Pink Floyd songs. The band comprises Shashwat on vocals, Rexy on the rhythm guitar, Selva on the lead guitar, Sunny on the keyboard, Eavin on the bass guitar and Barry on drums. Don’t miss their gig tonight, as they play the best of rock ‘n’ roll.

WHERE: Hard Rock Café, St Marks Road, Shanthala Nagar, Ashok Nagar
WHEN: 8pm-11.30pm, November 14
ENTRY: `750, bookmyshow.com

Children’s Day special


Cook up a storm
The Deli Classroom is back for the second time, continuing its efforts to bring children of all abilities together in one kitchen. At the ‘Little Chef Station’, chef Saurabh Arora will take them on a culinary adventure and help them discover ingredients, flavours and cooking techniques. The workshop will start with an ice-breaking session, after which, Arora will teach these mini masterchefs how to rustle up gnocchi and cake popsicles. At Smoke House Deli,
53, Lavelle Road, Ashok Nagar, from 3pm to 5pm, on November 14, entry fee is `800, call 9986102111

Make your own toys
Children love their toys, so how about getting them to make them one? Tiny Tails Workshop is inviting kids just for that. Conceptualised with the aim to take them back in time, when toys were sewn from discarded textiles lying unused at home, it will help them discover the joy of creating their own imaginary playmates with textiles. At the end of the three-hour workshop, they will get to take the creative toy home. At The Purple Turtles, 25 Lavelle Road, Shanthala Nagar, from 3pm to 6pm, on November 14, fee is `1,500, call 9741408132


Get ’em popcorn
Adding to the enthusiasm of Children’s Day, this cinema chain is hosting a series of screenings only for kids. Called ‘Kids’ Day Out Film Festival’, they have curated some of the most popular children’s films such as Frozen – Part 1, The Lion King–2, Aladdin, and Incredibles–2. Not just that, tiny tots can participate in a lot of activities, expect surprise gifts and visits by mascots, and more. So parents, book them a show now. At PVR Phoenix Marketcity, PVR Superplex, Vega, and PVR Forum across the city, at 4pm & 7pm, from November 14 to 17, call 9845706155

Hope is a tree
In order to increase the green cover in Karnataka, students from 48,000 government schools will plant about 2.5 lakh trees across the state. This is part of an eco-initiative called Prerana MyTree. At Government Higher Primary School, K Gollahalli, Hemmigepura, from 11am, on November 14
