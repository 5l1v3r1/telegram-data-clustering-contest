Frankie Edgar Is Headed For A Humbling Loss Or An Unlikely Victory At UFC Fight Night 166

14 Nov 2019 by Brian Mazique

Former UFC Lightweight champion, UFC Featherweight title challenger and future Hall-of-Famer Frankie Edgar is moving down to bantamweight. While on the surface that sounds like a cool idea, there are several reasons for Edgar fans to be worried.Cory Sandhagen, the man who will welcome Edgar to the 135-pound division, might be the worst possible matchup for him.

Also, while the move to 135 is sensible, it might be coming four years too late.

Edgar just turned 38 and he’s coming off a loss to UFC Featherweight champion Max Holloway at UFC 240 in July. There wasn’t much of a future for Edgar at featherweight.

That seemed clear after he was knocked out by Brian Ortega in March 2018, but a victory over the spiraling Cub Swanson earned Edgar a lifetime-achievement title shot against Holloway.

Now that he’s embracing a move to bantamweight, the UFC is serving the 5’6” Edgar up to a 5’11” Sandhagen who is in the midst of a seven-fight win streak. Sandhagen is a gifted striker who also boasts a wide range of grappling talents. He’s beaten the hard-hitting John Lineker and multi-talented Raphael Assuncao in his last two bouts, and could be just one or two wins away from his own title shot.

If we’re being honest, most can see Sandhagen’s movement and length creating an insurmountable challenge for Edgar. To win, Edgar has to close the distance and make it a grappling match, but there is no guaranteed he’ll have a marked advantage over Sandhagen on the ground.

In every way possible, this seems like a bad matchup for Edgar. However, it is a fight and strange, unexpected things can happen. At the time of publication, there were no odds on this bout. However, I’d be shocked if Sandhagen wasn’t a sizable favorite.

If Edgar can pull the upset, it will be arguably the biggest win of his career. Almost no one will expect Edgar to beat a man 11 years his junior, five inches taller, and who also employs a style that should give him problems. If Edgar loses, and is stopped or wiped out over the course of three rounds, talk of the legend’s retirement will be augmented.

At that point, you have to wonder why Edgar was given this kind of a matchup in his first bantamweight bout. Edgar is never one to run from challenges, but this one isn’t likely to end well.
