Infosys founder NR Narayana Murthy's son Rohan finds work-life balance

14 Nov 2019, 00:30 by Anupama Bijur

Aparna Krishnan, daughter of Navy Commander, will tie the knot with Rohan at a five-star hotel in the city on Dec 2

#BENGALURU This has been a good year for NR Narayana Murthy and Sudha Murty’s family. First it was their son-in-law, Rishi Sunak, married to their daughter, Akshata, who was appointed Chief Secretary to the Treasury in July 2019 in British Prime Minister Boris Johnson’s cabinet.

On December 2, Rohan Murty, the Murthys’ second born, will marry Aparna Krishnan, who is the General Manager operations at Soroco, a company founded by Rohan.

Sources close to the family say that they knew that the young couple were planning to get married but there was no confirmation on the date.

According to sources, the wedding invites are in the mail and the one-day wedding is likely to be held at ITC Gardenia, although it is yet to be confirmed.

The families want to keep the wedding ceremony simple and according to Hindu traditions while the reception will be gala event.

Aparna Krishnan has a Bachelor of Arts (Economics) degree from Dartmouth College, a private Ivy League University in the US. She also has a degree in Political Science from the London School of Economics and Political Science. Aparna’s parents are from Kochi. Savitri Krishnan, the bride-to-be’s mother is a retired State Bank of India employee, while her father is Commander KR Krishnan, a former Indian Navy officer. According to an ex-Merchant Navy officer in Bengaluru, Krishnan is an executive officer and the bride’s family and friends have already received their wedding invitations and are expected to be in Bengaluru for the same.

The couple will tie the knot on December 2

Before joining Soroco, Aparna had worked as an analyst at McKinsey and Sequoia Capital, and then moved to Craftsvilla, an Indian e-commerce, fashion and accessories portal. Her profile on the Soroco page says that she has exemplary yoga skills.

While Rohan’s sister Akshata along with her husband Rishi and their two daughters, Krishna and Anoushka will be here for the wedding, Sudha and Narayana Murthy continue to be busy with their engagements as usual.


On November 12, Narayana Murthy was in a talk with Lord John Browne, former Group Chief Executive, BP and former President of the Royal academy of Engineering and author of Make, Think, Imagine.

On November 14, children’s day, Sudha Murty will launch her latest book, The Daughter from a Wishing Tree. She will be in conversation with Kiran Mazumdar-Shaw, Chairperson and Managing Director, Biocon Limited. This is Sudha’s fourth book in the popular mythology series preceded by The Serpent’s Revenge: Unusual Tales from the Mahabharata and The Man from the Egg: Unusual Tales about the Trinity; and The Upside-Down King:


Unusual Tales about Rama and Krishna. This book tells numerous stories about women in mythology and the power of the female divinity, including tales of common women from ancient India.
