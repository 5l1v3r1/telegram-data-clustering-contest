Facebook, Instagram Challenge Paypal With New Donation Tools

14 Nov 2019 by Michael Nuñez

Facebook announced three new products on Thursday that will make it easier for people to donate to charities and causes they want to support. The products include a new fundraiser sticker for Facebook Stories, a donate button for Instagram business accounts and a donate button for video game live-streamer accounts.The updates push Facebook deeper into online charitable donations industry, which is currently dominated by incumbents GoFundMe and Paypal—both of which charge a 2.9% payment-processing fee on each donation, in addition to 30 cents for every donation. Facebook does not charge a fee on any donations.

“Before Facebook, we were nickel-and-diming fundraisers through Paypal and getting a percentage taken,” said Military Mama Network founder Geriann Wiesbrook at the Facebook product unveiling on Thursday. “I don’t know who came up with the [automated monthly donations] thing, but that was also a game-changer because our monthly donors are going up.”

The nonprofit donations industry is a fringe business that quietly generates billions of dollars each year. In 2018, more than 55 million people used Paypal to give $9.6 billion to nonprofits. Since 2010, GoFundMe has been used to raised more than $5 billion. Facebook, by comparison, has helped individuals and organizations raise more than $2 billion since 2015.

The new donation tools play an important role in Facebook’s growing reach in digital payments and its increased emphasis on groups and private messaging. Although the company may not derive a direct monetary value from donations, the tools encourage people to continue messaging, organizing, and engaging with like-minded people on Facebook.

In a speech at Facebook’s annual developers’ conference in May, CEO Mark Zuckerberg said that he wants users to have more conversations in groups and showcased a new look that put groups at the center of the Facebook app. Adding the ability for groups to promote and collect donations on Facebook furthers this goal.
