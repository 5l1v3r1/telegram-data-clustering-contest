More waterside apartments and homes earmarked for banks of the River Trent

The proposed development marks the second phase of the vast project

14 Nov 2019, 06:00 by Jamie Barlow

The multi-million-pound transformation of the city's waterside could be boosted further with plans in the pipeline to build more apartments and homes on the banks of the River Trent.

Plans have been submitted to revamp the former Merrill Brown sign-making site to the east of Trent Lane, between Nottingham and Colwick.

A five-storey block that will include 27 one and two-bedroom apartments is planned.

Planning documents, submitted to Nottingham City Council, state: "The proposed apartments are all of a generous size relative to many contemporary precedent developments, and feature spacious, well-lit living kitchen-dining spaces with modern kitchen facilities, and good-sized bedrooms with en suite bathrooms to all master bedrooms in two-bed units."

Meanwhile, a range of 12 two, three and four-bedroom homes are also in the pipeline.

All of the houses are designed to have a back garden area and parking space.

The proposed development marks the second phase of the vast project.

On land to the south work has started to build 23 apartments and 50 homes as part of a scheme called Pelham Waterside.

Jules Hunt, associate director of FHP, the company marketing the first phase of properties, said 10 of the new homes were already reserved.

"The first one on Trent Lane is due to move in in February," she said.

Luxury riverside apartment blocks have regenerated the city's riverside in recent years surrounding where the latest phase of Pelham Waterside is eamarked.

One project is the River Crescent development, in Waterside Way, which was built in 2008-09 and includes more than 100 two and five-bedroom apartments. It was home to the former England football manager, Sven-Göran Eriksson, during his brief spell at Notts County in 2009.

Developers also want to build 81 luxury apartments to the south of Trent Lane at the site of the former Park Yacht Club.

Chris Hobson, director of policy at East Midlands Chamber, said: “The increase in waterside apartment developments, especially in previously derelict areas, offers a number of benefits.

"First and foremost, providing a greater number of homes is a positive and we have seen in recent years that these types of projects typically have sustainability and regeneration at their heart.

“There are also positive knock on benefits including the employment created and contracts won in the building phase, while business is aided by new residents who spend their money locally.”
