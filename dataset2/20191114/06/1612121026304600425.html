Montblanc Summit 2+ is poised to be a rare LTE Wear OS smartwatch

14 Nov 2019, 06:46 by Jared Peters

The Montblanc Summit 2 was one of the first Wear OS smartwatches to utilize Qualcomm’s Snapdragon Wear 3100 platform, and since we haven’t officially had a replacement yet, that still makes it a compelling option for someone looking for a smartwatch that can still blend in with nicer jewelry.

But a year later, Montblanc wants to up their game with their already-successful watch. The Summit 2+ has stopped by the FCC and looks to offer a key upgrade, and something that’s pretty rare for Wear OS watches.

Unfortunately, most of the hardware is unknown, so we’re not sure if this is going to be using the upcoming Wear 3300 platform. But we do know that the FCC filing has confirmed that the Summit 2+ will be a connected LTE watch, and it’ll have bands to ensure that it works on Verizon, too. It still packs in Bluetooth, GPS, and NFC, but the battery is taking a small hit and going down to just 300mAh from 340mAh on the original Summit 2.

Fingers crossed that it does have an upgraded processor so that LTE connectivity doesn’t create the shortest battery life to ever exist on a smartwatch.
