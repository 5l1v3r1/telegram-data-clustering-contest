First trailer for Elisabeth Moss film The Invisible Man is utterly terrifying

The Invisible Man hits cinemas in 2020 and stars Elisabeth Moss and The Haunting of Hill House star Oliver Jackson-Cohen

08 Nov 2019, 10:43 by Lewis Knight, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/lewis-knight/

What you can’t see can hurt you.

Elisabeth Moss , award-winning star of The Handmaid's Tale and Mad Men, stars in an all-new terrifying take on The Invisible Man.

The new film is a re-visioning of the original science fiction novel by H.G. Wells and reboots Universal Pictures' 1933 film of the same name with Gloria Stuart and Claude Rains.

Trapped in an abusive relationship with a wealthy scientist, Cecilia Kass (Moss) escapes in the dead of night and goes into hiding with her sister (Harriet Dyer), alongside their childhood friend ( Aldis Hodge ) and his teenage daughter (Euphoria star Storm Reid).

But when Cecilia’s abusive ex (played by The Haunting of Hill House star Oliver Jackson-Cohen) commits suicide and leaves a huge amount of money to her, Cecilia begins to fear that he isn't really dead at all.

 

As increasingly dangerous circumstances begin to occur, Cecilia begins to question her own sanity as she believes an unseen tormentor is making her life a living nightmare.

Horror maestro Jason Blum has delivered The Invisible Man with his Blumhouse Productions and he's got in some killer minds to execute this new take on the character.

The Invisible Man is written, directed and executive produced by Leigh Whannel l, one of the minds behind of the Saw franchise.

This trailer was certainly creepy enough on its own.

The Invisible Man is released in UK cinemas on February 28, 2020.
