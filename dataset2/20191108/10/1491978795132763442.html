Max Porter: ‘Books don’t tend to make me cry. Sentimental TV adverts, on the other hand...'

08 Nov 2019, 10:00 by Max Porter

The author of Lanny and Grief is a Thing With Feathers on the influence of John Berger and his admiration for translators

The book I am currently reading
By my bed, Afropean by Johny Pitts. In my bag, In the Cut by Susanna Moore. In the bathroom, Air Histories by Christopher Meredith and I Remain in Darkness by Annie Ernaux.

The book that changed my life
In Parenthesis by David Jones.

The book I wish I’d written
I’d like to have written or illustrated a book as perfect as Angry Arthur by Hiawyn Oram and Satoshi Kitamura. I also wish I’d made a start on my new book.

The book that had the greatest influence on me
A cluster of books I read at around the same time: Briggflatts by Basil Bunting; Glass, Irony, and God by Anne Carson; Riddley Walker by Russell Hoban; Dart by Alice Oswald. These were the books that lit my fuse.

The book that is most overrated
I think the whole idea of rating, under or over, better or best, is a nonsense, an unkindness to art and each other. It’s not a good way to think about literature. We need sharper critical tools. If we spent more time reading and less time rating we’d all feel better.

The book that changed my mind
Every book is changing it, I hope, but I’ll say John Berger. I’ll never forget the way his books quenched a thirst I didn’t know I had. His work is a radical force for good in the world.

The last book that made me cry
Oddly, given how much I like to weep, books don’t tend to do it for me. Sentimental TV adverts, on the other hand …

The last book that made me laugh
Oi Frog! by Kes Gray and Jim Field.

The book I couldn’t finish
Loads! But most recently Barbara Vine’s A Dark Adapted Eye because my wife nicked it off me.

The book I’m most ashamed not to have read
I am ashamed that I can only read books in English. I hope to rectify that, but in the meantime I worship translators.

The book I give as a gift
The books I’ve given away most in recent years are Wendell Berry’s Why I Am Not Going to Buy a Computer, Denise Riley’s Say Something Back, Sarah Moss’s Ghost Wall, Ágota Kristóf’s The Notebook, Shigeru Mizuki’s Nonnonba, Tarjei Vesaas’s The Ice Palace and Bunny vs Monkey by Jamie Smart.

The book I’d most like to be remembered for
Ah, we can’t be worrying about that.

My earliest reading memory
Roger Red-hat and Jennifer Yellow-hat by Sheila McCullagh, with a giant on the hill throwing rocks. I was reading with the teacher, following her finger under the words. I pronounced island “izland” and as I did, it sounded correctly in my mind so I tried again, in unison with my teacher, we hit it perfectly together: “ISLAND” and it was very exciting.

My comfort read
Avocado Baby, or Would you Rather …, or Simp, or Mr Gumpy’s Outing. Anything by John Burningham.

• Max Porter’s Lanny is shortlisted for the Waterstones Book of the Year 2019.
