Scots thief jailed after dog attacks police officer during 20 minute rampage

Stephen McCafferty, 22, yelled at pet dog Mia to attack two terrified members of the public before turning its attention to PC Ross Meldrum.

08 Nov 2019, 14:48 by David Meilkle

A thief who ordered his Staffordshire bull terrier to maul three people including a police officer during a robbery has been jailed for 10 months.

Stephen McCafferty, 22, yelled at pet dog Mia to attack two terrified members of the public before turning its attention to PC Ross Meldrum during a 20 minute rampage.

McCafferty robbed a branch of Costcutters in Hamilton , Lanarkshire, and assaulted staff member Fiona Sutherland as she tried to throw him out after he stole scratchcards and wine.

The yob's dog sank its jaws into the arm of her colleague Joseph Springett before McCafferty assaulted another woman in an opticians.

   

He then set the dog on Dariuz Gorczynski outside a primary school after stealing a mobile phone from a car.

Police were called and a chase saw McCafferty cornered but he screamed at the dog to attack PC Meldrum and it mauled his hand.

Officers used batons to contain McCafferty and arrest him.

McCafferty, of Blantyre, Lanarkshire, appeared at the Hamilton Sheriff Court and was jailed after admitting robbery and assault in June this year.

Describing the attack on PC Meldrum, depute fiscal Scott King said: "Police were advised of what was going on and began searching the area to find the accused and saw him outside the primary school.

"He began to run followed by his dog and was eventually traced in a dead end where he began repeatedly to instruct his dog to get them shouting ' get them Mia'.

"Police drew their batons because of the dog but it ran towards PC Meldrum and bit him on the left hand.

 

"The accused's aggressive behaviour required them to strike him with a baton and apply straps before carrying him to a nearby police van."

Those who had been bitten were taken to hospital and given tetanus injections.
Andy Iles, defending, said: "He is realistic about the outcome of this process and how serious this matter is.

"This incident was a combination of two factors, two decades in the making with his upbringing and an addiction to street valium.

"He has shown adequate and genuine remorse for his behaviour and was shocked at how he had acted."

Sheriff Thomas Millar said: "You heard the narration and accept it was quite a horrendous course of conduct which has to be met with a period of custody."

Mia is now being cared for by a family member.
