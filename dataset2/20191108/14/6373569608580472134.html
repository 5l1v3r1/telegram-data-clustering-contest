Ryan Lowe has 'no hard feelings' towards goalkeeper Remi Matthews over red card incident

Lowe was sent-off playing for Bury against Argyle at Home Park in January 2018

08 Nov 2019, 14:06 by Chris Errington

Plymouth Argyle manager Ryan Lowe has insisted he has ‘no hard feelings’ towards Bolton Wanderers goalkeeper Remi Matthews over a red card incident at Home Park in 2018.

Lowe was playing for Bury at the time, near the end of his playing career, when he was sent-off for a challenge on Matthews, who was on loan to the Pilgrims from Norwich City.

Now, nearly two years on, Lowe is the manager of Argyle, who are away to Matthews and his Bolton team-mates in the FA Cup first round tomorrow.

Lowe was reminded of the incident at his pre-match media call ahead of the trip to the University of Bolton Stadium and said with a laugh: “I will give him a slight dig when I walk out the tunnel!”

He added: “It was the worst journey of my life. Six hours on the way home and no-one spoke to me.”

Lowe was a first half substitute for bottom-of-the-table Bury when they played Argyle in a League One game on January 6, 2018, which they lost 3-0.

He was sent-off in the 60 minute after he chased after a through ball into the Pilgrims’ penalty area.

Matthews dived forward to get the ball but referee Trevor Kettle decided that Lowe’s challenge on the ‘keeper warranted his dismissal.

Derek Adams, who was Argyle manager, described it as ‘a clear red card’.

To this day, Lowe insists ‘I didn’t touch him’ but he said: “It’s football. I don’t carry things on. I don’t dwell on things.

“He (Matthews) played the game and I wish some of my players would play the game sometimes when they are being whacked.

“Look, it is what it is. I have got no hard feelings against him at all.”

Matthews has made a total of 15 starts for Bolton this season and his recent performances have earned praise from manager Keith Hill.
