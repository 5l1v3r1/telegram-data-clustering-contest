Italian senator and Holocaust survivor under police protection after online threats

08 Nov 2019, 01:41 by Tim Pearce

Police in Italy are protecting an 89-year-old Holocaust survivor after she received hundreds of death threats on social media.

Liliana Segre is an Italian senator-for-life, a title bestowed by the Italian president to former heads of state or accomplished artists or scientists. Senators-for-life are given the full power and authority of an elected lawmaker, as well as some legal immunity.

Hundreds of threats began targeting Segre online after she called on the Italian Parliament to create a committee to combat hate, according to the BBC. Segre's motion passed.

"I appealed to the conscience of everyone and thought that a commission against hatred as a principle would be accepted by all," Segre said after the vote, noting that several abstentions by right-wing lawmakers made her feel "like a Martian."
