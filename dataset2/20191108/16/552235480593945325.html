Brute who glassed man in Johnstone pub caged for 19 months

Stephen Torrance, 39, plunged a glass tumbler into Brogan Moore's forehead, leaving him bleeding heavily.

08 Nov 2019, 16:35 by Ron Moore, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/ron-moore/

A burly yob who glassed a man’s face in a savage pub attack has been jailed for 19 months.

Stephen Torrance, 39, charged at victim Brogan Moore, and plunged a glass tumbler into his forehead, leaving him bleeding heavily from a wound above his eye.

He also grabbed him around the shoulders and shoved him over a table, sparking mayhem in the pub.

Paisley Sheriff Court heard Torrance had been drinking at the Thorn Inn, in Johnstone, when he took umbrage at Mr Moore, who was with a group of friends.

Procurator Fiscal Depute Keri Marshall told the court the victim had been spending the evening with pals on February 2 this year when he encountered Torrance at the bar and he started abusing him.

She said: “The complainer had gone to The Thorn Inn, Johnstone, and was drinking with friends. The evening was relatively uneventful.

The group had some drinks. At 9.30pm witness Moore made his way to the bar area to buy some drinks. At that point he come into contact with a male unknown to him. The male is the accused.

“While at the bar various comments were made by the accused to Mr Moore, which he felt were abusive, so he challenged back with comments.

“A minor altercation took place. The witness Moore walked away from the accused to return back to where his friends were.”

The court heard at that point Torrance flipped out and went after Mr Moore.

The prosecutor added: “While the complainer was walking away the accused pursued him and grabbed him by the throat.

“Mr Moore became free and showed his hands to deter the accused from further assault, and walked towards his friends.

“The accused then charged at him holding a pint glass in his right hand.

“The accused struck witness Moore on the face with the pint glass and pushed him over a table seated behind him.”

The court was shown CCTV footage of the entire attack where several drinkers jumped in immediately afterward and grabbed Torrance and pulled him away from the victim.

Ms Marshall told how the victim was left bleeding heavily from the wound above his left eye.

He was taken to the Royal Alexandria Hospital in Paisley for treatment and he received 12 stitches for his injuries.

The doctor who treated him confirmed the injury will result in a permanent facial scarring.

Yesterday electrical supervisor and father-of-two Torrance, from Glasgow Road in Paisley, appeared at a pre-trial hearing where he pleaded guilty to a charge assault to severe injury and permanent disfigurement against Mr Moore, where he seized hold of him by the throat, pursued him, and struck him on the head with a glass causing it to smash, and pushed him over a table.

Defence agent Campbell Porter said Torrance had been “horrified” at what he had done and realised he would being going to jail because he has previous convictions for violence.

He added: “There is not a lot can be said in mitigation for the incident. He consumed too much alcohol, became involved in an altercation, and his reaction is what your lordship has seen.

“He must face the consequences for that behaviour.”

Sheriff McCartney agreed and locked up Torrance for 19 months, reduced from two years, because of his early plea of guilty.
