Birmingham City scouting report - How young defender is faring during League Two loan stint

Latest Birmingham City news | Blues sent the young defender on loan to League Two side Macclesfield Town in a bid for him to gain first-team experience

08 Nov 2019, 17:00 by Mark Wakefield

Birmingham City sent a number of players out on loan at the start of the season in a bid for them to gain first-team experience.

One of those said players is defender Corey O’Keefe, who is spending the campaign on loan with Macclesfield Town.

O’Keefe has just one first-team appearance under his belt for Blues, and has gone to Moss Rose for his second loan spell following a brief stint with non-league outfit Solihull Moors last season.

So how has the Blues defender been getting on so far during his loan spell?

 

The 21-year-old made his debut for Macclesfield in their Carabao Cup first round tie against Blackpool in August.

The Silkmen won the tie on penalties as O’Keefe played the first half but was substituted at the interval by then-manager Sol Campbell.

He would get his first chance in a league game just a few days later after he came off the bench for the final 15 minutes of a 2-1 win away at Northampton Town.

O’Keefe’s first league start would come just a week later as he played 83 minutes of Macclesfield’s 1-0 win over Scunthorpe, where he played on the right-hand side of a five-man midfield.

The on-loan Blues defender has been utilised in a number of other positions while with the Silkmen, including left-midfield, right-back and defensive midfield.

So far, O’Keefe has made 17 appearances during his loan spell at Macclesfield, and has proved to be capable of operating in a number of roles in the starting 11.
