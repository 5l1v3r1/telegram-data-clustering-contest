Nikki Haley: 'I don't feel comfortable' with Trump asking Ukraine for 'favor'

08 Nov 2019, 22:54 by John Gage

Nikki Haley admitted that she wasn't "comfortable" with President Trump asking Ukraine to investigate Joe Biden but said the House does not have any basis for impeaching him because there was no quid pro quo.

In a preview clip for the CBS Sunday interview, the former ambassador to the United Nations said, "I don't feel comfortable" with Trump asking the president of Ukraine for a "favor" during their infamous July 25 phone call.

"I don't think it's good for us to ever want a foreign country to investigate an American," she said. "That's just not something we want to get into the practice of doing."

However, she still dismissed the impeachment proceedings, saying, "I also don't feel comfortable with the fact that we're having an impeachment process during an election year."
