Boris was north of the border but under the radar

Boris Johnson’s latest day trip was to a rural distillery miles from Elgin. As with the other outings, the public were excluded.

08 Nov 2019, 04:30 by Record View, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/record-view/

It was often said Tony Blair never felt comfortable in Scotland and this is undoubtedly true for charlatan Boris Johnson.

Although Tory strategists insist the Prime Minister is popular north of the border, Johnson is curiously reluctant to put it to the test.

His rare visits to Scotland have included fleeting trips to Faslane, a farm in Aberdeenshire and a call on Nicola Sturgeon at Bute House, where he left by the back door.

Johnson’s latest day trip was to a rural distillery miles from Elgin. As with the other outings, the public were excluded.

Remarkably for a man who says he cares about every part of the UK, Johnson as PM has never gone walkabout in Scotland to meet the voters he believes like him.

 

Johnson and his minders probably fear the “Iain Gray treatment”, a reference to when the former Scottish Labour leader was chased into a branch of Greggs during the Holyrood election in 2011.

The Tory leader is wise to keep his excursions to Scotland under the radar.

Slippery and untrustworthy, he also has a habit of making insulting comments about Scotland and the country’s public spending. His right-wing politics are also a major turn-off.

Johnson is expected to come to Scotland three times during the General Election campaign. He should walk the streets of Glasgow and accept the verdict of the city’s voters.

Road to justice

Scotland’s justice secretary Humza Yousaf has listened to the families of those who have needlessly died in our prisons – now the learning must begin.

The review he ordered yesterday must deliver in the name of those who should never have lost their lives in custody.

Allan Marshall died following brutal restraint, Katie Allan and William Lindsay, took their own lives in a system which betrayed them.

Nothing will bring them back but if this review prevents another death in our prison system, at least some good will come from tragedy.

Their deaths are a story of cover-ups, destruction of evidence, collusion, lies and neglect. This move from Humza Yousaf is laudable but it is a first step on the road to justice for victims of a broken system.

The justice secretary is trying. It is time the Lord Advocate did the same.
