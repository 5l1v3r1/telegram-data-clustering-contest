Best Singles Day Deals: When, What & Why You Should Care

Singles Day is a big event in China, but increasingly becoming better known to the rest of the world as a good opportunity for consumers to snap up some great deals. We explain when and what is Singles Day, and outline where to find some the best deals.

08 Nov 2019, 13:39 by Marie Black

'Poppy Day' or 'Remembrance Day' is significant in the UK for remembering those who have fallen in the line of duty, but elsewhere in the world 11 November also has significance for being 'Singles Day'.

Originating in China in 1993, Singles Day is now celebrated around the globe, and especially online. If you've never heard of it, consider that the number 1 represents an individual, or a single person. The number 11 could then be said to represent two singles coming together, and 11.11 two separate pairs of singles coming together. Thus 11 November is all about giving single people the opportunity to meet other singles.

How that translates to some great tech deals being available for Singles Day we're not entirely sure, but hey, we won't look a gift horse in the mouth. Maybe you can impress your new friend with your shiny new phone or tablet.

As it's a Chinese event, most of the best Singles Day deals will be available at Chinese retailers such as GearBest. These retailers are interesting to UK consumers for their ability to significantly undercut UK prices. 

Increasingly we're seeing big brands such as Acer get in on the Singles Day act (with 11% off everything when you use the code ELEVEN11 between noon on Friday 8 November and midnight on 11 November) - give it another year or two and Singles Day could be the next Black Friday. These offers are of particular interest because they carry none of the same caveats as does buying from China.

Though we've never encountered any problems, there is inevitably some element of risk associated in buying goods outside the European Union, as well as longer delivery times and, potentially, more complicated returns procedures. The one thing you really must factor into your purchasing decision is import duty, which if requested by Customs is payable at 20% of the value on the shipping paperwork.
