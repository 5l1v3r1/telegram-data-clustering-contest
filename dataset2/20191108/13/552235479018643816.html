Tributes paid to 'happy and caring' Blantyre man after tragic death

Heartbroken pals have planned a balloon release in memory of the popular young man.

08 Nov 2019, 13:19 by Record Reporter

Tributes have been paid to a 'happy and caring' young man who died in Blantyre .

Kyle Monteith, 21, tragically died on Thursday morning.

Heartbroken pals have flooded social media with tributes to 'one of the nicest guys ever'.

Connor Gilchrist said: "A pleasure to have grown up with, never without a laugh. Only wan Kyle Monteith."

Kai Robert said: "Heartbroken for the family and loved ones of Kyle Monteith, hope they can keep strong and carry on for him. Imperative we all look out for one another, that everyone knows that they are never alone and things can and will get better. RIP Kyle."

Cameron Kennedy said: "One of the nicest and funniest guys I ever had the pleasure of calling a pal. Fly high and rest easy KMT."

Aaron Black said: "Even if they are always laughing and smiling always check up on your pals you never know whats going on in their head. Will love you forever KMT rest easy my brother."

 

Harrison Reilly said: "Sad day for us all,thinking of Kyle's mum and rest of family. Rest easy KMT."

Steven Robertson said: "Today a lost my little brother, another great life taken, brings nothing but happiness and joy to the people around him. Fly high little brother love you so f****** much kmt."

Devon Longmuir: "Always putting a smile on everyone's face, my brother forever and ever KMT."

Callum Clarkson said: "Heartbroken KMT."

Shea Summers: "Rest peace brother you were loved so much mate."

Ryan Murphy said: "Canny believe it man, RIP Kyle mate."

Heartbroken girlfriend Elle Jean shared an emotional statement urging people to check in on their pals.

She wrote: "The most kind, caring boy who put everybody else before himself no matter what.

 

"He always treated me like an absolute princess and anybody who knows me knows I worshipped the ground he walked on.

"He had his whole future ahead of him, if only he could see now the devastation he has left behind.

"You were so so loved kyle, the past year and a bit I have known you has been the happiest time of my life and I will cherish those memories forever.

"You're at peace now my king. We will meet again someday and I will be in your arms once more.

"Never knew heartache like this, if only he could see how loved he really was. Life will never be the same again, we will be together again one day my boy."

"If anybody is ever feeling low or down my dm's are open to absolutely anybody. No matter where you are in life or what's going on someone always loves you and you will leave so many people heartbroken.

A balloon tribute is set to take place on Saturday night at 6pm at High Blantyre Park.

Those who knew Kyle have been asked to come down and say goodbye.
