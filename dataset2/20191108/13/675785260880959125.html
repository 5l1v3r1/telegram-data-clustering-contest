Ederson out of Liverpool vs Man City due to injury confirms Pep Guardiola

The Brazilian was injured in the Champions League draw with Atalanta in midweek and will now miss the crunch Premier League showdown at Anfield

08 Nov 2019, 13:38 by David McDonnell, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/david-mcdonnell/

Ederson is out of Manchester CIty's title showdown at Liverpool on Sunday.

City's first-choice goalkeeper suffered a thigh muscle strain in the 1-1 Champions League draw away to Atalanta in midweek.

Pep Guardiola had hoped Ederson would recover in time to play at Anfield, but the City boss confirmed today that he is out.

It means Claudio Bravo, who was sent off in Italy after coming on as a substitute, will play in goal for City in a game they cannot afford to lose if they are to stay in touch with Liverpool in the title race.

Guardiola said: “He [Ederson] is not able to play. [But] We have another top keeper - Claudio Bravo can do it too.

"We won the Carabao Cup thanks to Claudio. He's an exceptional keeper, he played a lot of games for Chile."

 

Bravo's last Premier League start for CIty was 18 months ago at Southampton on the final day of the 2017-18 season, when City had already won the title.

Asked whether he was confident in the Chilean, Guardiola declared: "Why shouldn’t I be confident in my players? I would not like to be a player if my manager doubts me.

"I see him every day in training and how good a goalkeeper he is, we’re not going to lose because of Claudio.

"The red card at Atalanta is because we lost the ball in a dangerous position. He won the Copa America, respect that guy, he’s an incredible goalkeeper.”

Guardiola also said he hopes there is no repeat of the attack on the City team coach, which saw Liverpool fans pelt the vehicle with missiles before a Champions League quarter-final tie in April 2018.

The City boss said: “Hopefully it will be an incredible game and hopefully not the same is going to happen like two seasons ago, when it was more than one bottle [thrown]."
