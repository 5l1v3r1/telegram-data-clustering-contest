Scot shark attack victim named after hand found inside creature

The 44-year-old went missing while snorkeling off the coast of the French island of Réunion, in the Indian Ocean, reports say.

08 Nov 2019, 08:14 by Lynn Love

These are the first pictures of the Scottish man whose arm was bitten off by a shark in the Indian Ocean

The man has been named locally as Richard Martyn Turner from Edinburgh.

The 44-year-old went missing while snorkeling off the coast of the French island of Réunion, in the Indian Ocean, reports say.

An arm found in a captured shark's stomach has been identified as his after his wife recognised the wedding ring on one of the fingers.

Mr Turner's wife, Verity, reported his disappearance on Saturday and was asked by police to check the remains extracted from the shark, it is claimed.

She told cops she recognised the ring on one of the hand's fingers, The Sun reports

A police source told the newspaper: ''The hand and forearm of a man was found inside the stomach of one of the captured tiger sharks.

"The sharks were caught because they were swimming way out of their natural habitat. They were in the lagoon area which is shark-free because of the coral.

"But they somehow managed to find a way in."

DNA tests are underway to confirm whether the limb is Mr Turner's.

The Sun reported that the man who vanished in Reunion on Saturday was named locally as Mr Turner.

The arm found in the shark belonged to him, according to the newspaper.

Police are not sure if the man who the arm belongs to was killed by a shark or whether he was eaten after drowning.

A spokesman for the local search and rescue crew – the Centre Regional Operationnel de Surveillance et de Sauvetage told the Daily Mail: “The victim was certainly swimming but it is not clear whether he was attacked or he was already dead when he was eaten.

“One possibility is that he became unwell while in the lagoon and was taken by the currents into deeper water.”

Mr Turner and his wife were staying at the island's prestigious Lux hotel, celebrating her 40th birthday.

An investigating source previously said: "The tourist and his wife were spending a week on the island and he had gone out swimming in the Hermitage Lagoon, off Saint-Gilles, alone on Saturday.

"When he did not get back, his wife sounded the alarm, and a full search was launched, involving boats and a helicopter.

"Divers searched around a coral reef in the area, and there were also patrols on land involving sniffer dogs. Sadly, the man could not be found."

A police source told the Scottish Sun that the sharks were three to four metres long. 

It is understood that the shark was killed because it was considered a threat to holidaymakers, the Scotsman reported.

There are frequent shark attacks around Reunion, which is 500 miles from Madagascar.

The island, which sits on the so-called 'Shark Highway' between South Africa and Australia, has had 10 fatal shark attacks since the year 2000.

Tiger sharks have a record second only to Great White sharks for attacks on humans.
