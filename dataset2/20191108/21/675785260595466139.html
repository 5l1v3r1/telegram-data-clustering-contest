Jurgen Klopp admits Sean Cox return to Anfield will be highlight of Liverpool career

The Liverpool fan makes his first trip back to the stadium after suffering serious injuries in an assault by Roma fans ahead of the Champions League semi-final first leg last year

08 Nov 2019, 21:05 by David Maddock, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/david-maddock/

Jurgen Klopp will welcome Sean Cox to Anfield on Sunday, admitting it will be one of the highlights of his Liverpool career.

And he has told the fan from Ireland that Reds fans will demonstrate the club anthem is far from just an anthem sung on the club terraces.

Cox was attacked by Roma fans before the Champions League semi-final in 2018, and left in coma after suffering brain injuries from the assault.

But he has shown remarkable resilience to recover slowly from the attack, and now he will return to Anfield for the first time since that night.

Klopp revealed that the news of the attack was one of his worst moments in football. Yet he says Sunday will be an emotional and joyful occasion.

“When it happened to Sean it was probably really the lowest point for me in my whole time at Liverpool because that is something that should not happen,” he said.

“It shouldn’t happen in life and it should not happen around a football game. It happened around a football game.”

Sean has recovered some speech and has left hospital, but his wife Martina explained there is still a long way to go in his recovery.

"He finds it very difficult to express himself verbally and, while it seems he can hear and understand more, his speech remains very challenged," Mrs Cox said.

"It is unlikely that he ever will walk again which is difficult to accept for someone as active as Sean used to be."

Klopp though, expects Liverpool fans to extend their club anthem to show that Sean will always be welcomed as part of the Anfield family.

“Coming from a low point, this is one of my highlights since I've been here. How the Liverpool community dealt with it, I hope he feels that,” the manager added.

“We don't only sing You'll Never Walk Alone - we really live that and in this specific situation it is very important to show that.

“I really hope they feel that the whole time and they will feel it in the future and we can help him in this recovery. It is so nice and I am really looking forward to it.

“I hope we can organise for me to see him before the game for a couple of minutes – I really want to.

“The love for Sean and his family have for this club and football I hope it gave him strength and power in his recovery time – we know it is not finished yet and there is a long way to go – but having that we can give him the opportunity to come back to watch a really big game, and he wants to do that, for me.”
