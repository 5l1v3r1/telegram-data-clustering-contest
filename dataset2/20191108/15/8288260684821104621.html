Wolverhampton Council appoints new director of governance

Former South Staffs employee David Pattison is announced by leader as successful candidate for council's new director of governance role vacant since early 2019

08 Nov 2019, 15:21 by Joe Sweeney, https://www.birminghammail.co.uk/authors/joe-sweeney/

A new Director of Governance has been appointed by Wolverhampton City Council, its leader announced last night (Wednesday) - occupying a Â£140,000 post that has been vacant since early this year.

Councilor Ian Brookfield (Lab. Fallings Park), told a meeting of the full council that successful candidate David Pattison will also serve as Solicitor to the Council and Monitoring Office when he takes up his new role.

"This is something that we've been looking for, as this position needs to be filled. We were fortunate enough only just on Tuesday to have had a final set of interviews, so I'd like to thank everyone who took part in those," he said.

"We are immensely pleased that we think we have found the best candidate for the job. Mr Pattison comes to us from South Staffordshire, where he held a similar post.

"He's energetic, with plenty of ideas and he'll be a great collaborator who will work with everyone across all our corporate priorities.

"We did have two very strong candidates in the end, but Mr Pattison was just another step above," he added.

Leader of the opposition, Cllr Wendy Thompson (Con. Tettenhall Wightwick) said: "I'm very happy to second the resolution for the appointment of the Director of Governance, as all the candidates went through a very rigorous process."
