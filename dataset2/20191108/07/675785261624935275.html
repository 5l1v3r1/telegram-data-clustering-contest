What time is KSI vs Logan Paul weigh-in? Start time and live stream details

YouTube stars Logan Paul and KSI clash in Los Angeles tomorrow night - but first they must weigh in

08 Nov 2019, 07:00 by Martin Domin, https://www.facebook.com/mirrorfighting

KSI and Logan Paul will come face to face for the final time today when they weigh in for their eagerly-awaited rematch.

The next time these two YouTube stars see each other will be tomorrow night at the Staples Center when they stand in opposite corners of the ring.

The rivals have traded verbal jabs all week and it is almost time for them to exchange the real thing.

But first they must weigh in at or under the cruiserweight limit of 200lb in downtown Los Angeles this afternoon.

After yesterday's tetchy press conference, there could well be fireworks on the scales.

Here is everything you need to know about the weigh-in...

What time is is?

KSI and Logan Paul are due on the scales at 1pm local time (9pm GMT).

Where is it?

The weigh-in will take place at the X box Plaza in LA Live, 800 W Olympic Blvd., Los Angeles

Do I need a ticket?

No, entry is free to the public

Can I stream it?

Yes, Matchroom will be streaming the weigh-in on their YouTube channel.

When is the fight?

The fight is on Saturday night at the Staples Center in Los Angeles. KSI and Logan Paul are expected to walk to the ring at 8pm local time (4am GMT).

What TV channel is it on?

The fight will be shown on DAZN in the US and on Sky Sports Box Office in the UK.

Is there a live stream?

The fight can be streamed on DAZN or via Sky Sports' website

Who is on the undercard?

Devin Haney vs Alfredo Santiago

Billy Joe Saunders vs Marcelo Esteban Coceres

Ronny Rios vs Hugo Berrio

Diego Pacheco vs Aaron Casper

Alexis Espino vs Gregory Clark

Reshat Mati vs Cody Peterson

Joshua Brueckner vs Tyler Smith

Latest news

KSI and Logan Paul took a back seat as their trainers almost came to blows in Hollywood.

The YouTube stars came together for the penultimate time before the first bell of their rematch on Saturday.

But it was former heavyweight world champion Shannon Briggs who stole the limelight.

Briggs will be in Paul's corner for the sequel at the Staples Center while KSI has been trained by professional fighter Viddal Riley.

And it was the coaches who traded insults across the stage.

Briggs said: "This is great for boxing, it's going to introduce new boxing fans to the sport.

"I had to be part of it, I've been connected to Logan for 25 years through a guy I met on the street, Paulo, who is now his manager.

"This is a great opportunity but my man has the ability and I know he can be the champ because I did it twice

"This is the pro ranks and you came to this fight with a butter knife of a trainer.

"This is war, you're going to get beat up real bad. I said to Logan you better put this guy in hospital."
