Middendorp Admits Chiefs’ Derby Decision…

08 Nov 2019, 07:00 by Soccer Laduma

Kaizer Chiefs’ Ernst Middendorp has admitted that he made decisions this week that were influenced by the upcoming Soweto Derby on Saturday afternoon…

Read: 17 Shots To Make Amakhosi Smile As Chiefs Sprint Towards Title

The Glamour Boys made eight changes last night when they went into battle with bottom-of-the-table Chippa United at Nelson Mandela Bay Stadium, with only three players retaining their places from the side that started the Telkom Knockout win over the Buccaneers.

Middendorp confirmed that the club’ medical team suggested that Khama Billiat, Lebogang Manyama and Erick Mathoho all needed a bit more rest, and he has also explained that his decision to leave out Daniel Akpeyi was based on the fact that a booking would have seen him miss this weekend’s bumper clash through suspension.

As quoted by the club’s official website, the German said, “Daniel Akpeyi is clearly on three yellow cards and we had to look at that and make a decision in that area. I’m glad Bruce Bvuma played well and kept a clean sheet…

“The games before and after the Soweto Derby are tricky. This one is more complicated and needed some juggling. Playing a Derby two weekends in a row and having a midweek game was always a challenge. A huge compliment goes to the players. They are really coming to the party and adapting well to the situation.”
