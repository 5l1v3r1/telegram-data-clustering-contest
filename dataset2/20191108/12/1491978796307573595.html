Nature's disco: Elsa Leydier's pop-art paradise – in pictures

08 Nov 2019, 12:00

From glitchy parrots to pop-art champagne, the photographer’s work explores our perceptions of nature, influenced by strong colour and the climate crisis

  • Leydier’s work is in the Curiosa section of the Paris Photo fair until 10 November

Untitled #3, from Braços verdes Olhos Cheios de Asas, 2016

Elsa Leydier reproduces a vision of nature that borrows colours from pop art and adds a luxury aesthetic. Each image, for her, preserves a paradise found. Leydier was the winner of the Maison Ruinart prize at Paris Photo and finalist of the Dior prize for photography for young talent in 2019. All photographs: Elsa Leydier/courtesy Intervalle

Petralhada, from Brazil System Error, 2018

Brazil System Error are a series of images gleaned from social networks and manipulated by the artist. These images illuminate the multiple realities of human experience while drawing attention to the non-visible in innovative ways

Heatwave, 2019

In her 2019 project Heatwave, Leydier joined the harvest in Champagne, France, in September 2019. It inspired an artistic vision of the connection between nature and the people who work with the vines

Heatwave, 2019

Using light, Leydier wanted to raise the issue of climate change that is transforming the connection between people and nature

Heatwave, 2019

Leydier is interested in how photography can represent specific places. She also highlights the role of images in how our perception of the natural environment is constructed

Heatwave, 2019

In Heatwave, Leydier blends conventional photography with experimental methods. These include rayograms, made by filtering light through materials used to protect champagne from its adverse affects

Heatwave, 2019

Champagne is affected by light, which has an impact at each stage of its production process. The vineyards are planted on hillsides to capture the sun’s rays as effectively as possible. The cellars are carefully protected from UV rays, which could leave the champagne with un goût de lumière (a taste of light), spoiling its subtle palette of aromas

Heatwave, 2019

In the making of this series, Leydier decided to deviate from her usual photography practices and experiment with equipment itself
