Suspect, 14, in custody in death of well-known animal rescuer found tied to bed: police

08 Nov 2019, 12:13 by David Aaro

A 14-year-old suspect was in police custody on Thursday night in relation to the death of an animal rescue organization head who was found in his Northeast Philadelphia home on Monday, police said.

The suspect, a girl, surrendered to police with her attorney. Police spokesman Sgt. Eric Gripp said no charges have been filed at this time, according to the Philadelphia Inquirer.

Albert Chernoff, 59, the president of Alley Cat Animal Rescue, was found by authorities naked and tied up to his bed with a large wound on his head and gashes on his chest just before 3:00 a.m. last Tuesday.

He died at the scene.

"If you have rescued animals, cats in particular, in Philadelphia you've interacted with Al," Blake Martin of ACCT Philly, a local Animal Shelter, told Fox 29. "He was a really sweet guy."

On Wednesday, police released surveillance video obtained from the home of Chernoff, which shows an unnamed person walking through his living room, washing their hands in the kitchen sink and looking in the freezer and refrigerator before leaving his home.
