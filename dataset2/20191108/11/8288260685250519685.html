'Right direction' - Former Birmingham City starlet signs new contract

Latest Birmingham City news | The former Blues defender left St Andrew's in the summer after being released by the club

08 Nov 2019, 11:42 by Mark Wakefield

Former Birmingham City youngster Dom Bernard has been rewarded for his recent form with a new contract at Forest Green Rovers.

The 22-year-old was released by Blues at the end of last season , leaving the club without a single first-team appearance to his name.

The versatile defender was then taken on trial by Forest Green in the summer, and was handed an initial one-year deal with the League Two outfit.

Bernard’s performances for Forest Green have impressed enough to see him awarded an extension on his previous deal, with the new contract running up to the end of the 2020/21 campaign.

 

The former Blues defender has gone on to make 18 appearances already this season in all competitions, showing his flexibility by playing at right-back, right wing-back and right of a defensive back three in that time.

Bernard went on to express his delight at signing his new deal, and is determined to improve on his good start at his new club.

“I’m happy to be at this great club which is going in the right direction,” Bernard told the club’s website.

“Obviously I am happy to be involved in the majority of the games, I have adapted well and everything is going well at the moment and long may it continue.

“With the formation we play, we’ve changed it around and I have played right-back and right centre-back as well, it’s ideal and the versatility of having to adapt to those positions, is quite comfortable for me.

“We’ve been talking for a week now and I signed my contract in the summer and this is a good extension, something that I wanted to build on but that is off the pitch and I am glad that it is done.”
