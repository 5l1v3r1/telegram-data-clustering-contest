LIVE ISL 2019-20 | Kerala Blasters FC vs Odisha FC - Match 17

08 Nov 2019, 09:54 by SportsCafe Desk

Hosts Kerala Blasters, who are eighth on the table, will face newcomers Odisha FC, sitting sixth on the table, at Jawaharlal Nehru Stadium in Kochi on Friday. Both teams are desperate for the win as both have only one win in the last three matches, although pressure will be more on Kerala Blasters.

Preview

Kerala Blasters will be under heavy pressure to face Odisha FC in front of the passionate Kochi fans on Friday at the Jawarharlal Nehru Stadium in Kochi.  Blasters are performing poorly in every department and are struggling with injuries to foreign players as well. Their only win came against ATK in the season opener as they reel at the eighth position on the table.

Meanwhile, Odisha FC are riding on high confidence after their goal scoring night in Mumbai to win their first game of the season with a 4-2 scoreline. Even though they have two losses, they have fought well in those games and sitting sixth on the table with the same points as Kerala but with superior goal difference.

Key Players

Eelco Schattorie's key player is Nigerian Bartholomew Ogbeche, who has two goals in his account this season, while Blasters have scored only three goals. Schattorie brought Sahal Abdul Samad in the starting eleven against Hyderabad, who assisted Rahul KP for a goal and the Dutchman might again hand a chance to the Kerala Duo in the starting eleven.

In the Odisha camp, the Spanish duo of Xisco Hernandez and Aridane Sandane are in excellent form having five goals between them in three games. Young Jerry Mawihmingthanga, however, is the key Indian player under Josep Gombau's disposal alongside Spain’s Aridane Santana.

Team News

Kerala Blasters faced a huge blow in the previous match against Hyderabad FC by injuring Dutch defender Gianni Zuiverloon. The yellow brigade has previously lost star defender Sandesh Jhingan during the pre-season and Schattorie is more worried about the absence of another foreigner Mario Arques.

Odisha boss Josep Gombau may bring back Carlos Delgado to the defence who was earlier suspended for a red card. Spanish goalkeeper Francisco Dorronsoro should be making way for Indian goalkeeper Arshdeep Singh. The Spaniard will be pleased to field a strong fit side.

Head to Head

Both teams faced each other 12 times ( Odisha FC formerly Delhi Dynamos ). Both of them won four times and the four went on to draw.

Manager History

Josep Gombau

The Spaniard’s coaching career starts at the age of 16 and has managed La Liga giants Barcelona’s youth team. He also has experience in Asia and Australia and this is his second year in India. Last year he was struggling with Delhi Dynamos managing only four games with Delhi Dynamos last season. But the management has shown faith in him to continue.

Eelco Scahttorie

The Dutch coach has previously managed several clubs in Asia and has experience with I-League clubs and did have a wonderful season with NorthEast United FC last season. He took NorthEast to their first-ever playoffs. Schattorie will have to fulfil the huge hopes of the passionate Kerala fans. He even receives advice from fans for the team selections on social media.
