Toni Kroos slams Gareth Bale over Real Madrid absence after latest Wales call-up

Bale has not played for Real Madrid since picking up an injury on international duty in October, but is set to play for Wales again in their next matches

08 Nov 2019, 09:43 by Alex Richards, https://www.facebook.com/AARichards/

Real Madrid midfielder Toni Kroos has slammed Gareth Bale for playing for Wales.

Germany international Kroos, 29, has taken aim at his teammate as he addressed Bale's recent injury, when asked about his future at the Santiago Bernabeu.

Bale, 30, has not played for the Spanish giants since suffering an injury while on international duty in October and is not expected to play against Eibar this weekend.

But the Wales captain has been included in Ryan Giggs' squad for the upcoming Euro 2020 qualifiers against Azerbaijan and Hungary, and is expected to play.

During an interview with German blog Real Total after Los Blancos' 6-0 win over Galatasaray on Wednesday night, Kroos, who has long been one of the Madrid stars closest to Bale, had his say.

"It is a little inconvenient when you go to your national team, then you do not play for your club," said an unhappy Kroos.

"After that, you are fit to play for your national team again. Maybe this is not the best time.

"I hope everything is OK when he comes back."

The comments came after Kroos was asked about the Bale's future at the Spanish club and admitted that it is an awkward situation for all concerned.

After initially stating "I do not know", Kroos added: "He was already about to leave the club in summer.

"After these types of things, where they both [club and player] seem to have moved on, it is difficult to return to normality.

"Taking that into account, I have got the impression that he is motivated and has also had a few notable performances."

Wales are currently fourth in Group E, with eight points from six games so far.

Victory in Baku, against an Azerbaijan side with just one point from their six matches, is imperative for Giggs' side as they aim to reach next summer's finals.
