CR7 In Trouble? Sarri On Claims Superstar Left Stadium Early

11 Nov 2019, 21:02 by David Kappel

Juventus coach Maurizio Sarri has responded to claims superstar Cristiano Ronaldo left the stadium before their game against AC Milan on Sunday was finished. Could the five-time Ballon d'Or winner be in trouble?

Click through the mini-gallery above to see some images of the Ronaldo's early substitution on Sunday night.

The 34-year-old was substituted after just 55 minutes in his side's 1-0 win over Milan on the weekend, with his replacement Paulo Dybala scoring the eventual winner.

Ronaldo looked upset as he was taken off and walked straight down the tunnel, with Calciomercato suggesting that he left the stadium before the final whistle, and his manager Sarri has since responded to the reports.

"Disrespectful to his teammates? Like I said, I don't know," Sarri said, as quoted by the Daily Express, when asked about the rumours.

"If it's true that he left before the end, it is a problem to be resolved with his teammates."

Amid claims that Ronaldo himself had asked for his substitution, Sarri once again addressed the player's reaction after he walked straight down the tunnel.

"The important thing is that he made himself available. If he gets angry when he comes out, this is part of the game," the Italian tactician added.

“It's only natural a player is going to be irritated about leaving the pitch, especially when he worked so hard to be there.

"All players who are trying to give their best will have at least five minutes of being annoyed when they are substituted, but a coach would, in general, be much more worried if he didn't seem upset."
