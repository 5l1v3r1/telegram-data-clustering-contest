Stray cat lucky to be alive after suffering chemical burns

Cats Protection Lanarkshire are appealing for donations to help with Taz's vet bills

11 Nov 2019, 12:31 by Paul Thomson, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/paul-thomson/

A stray cat is lucky to be alive after suffering chemical burns.

Cats Protection’s Lanarkshire Branch found three-year-old Taz close to death, after he was believed to have come into contact with chemicals, leaving him with severely burnt skin and fur.

The CPL are taking good care of him as he begins his long road to recovery, but they are now are appealing for donations towards his vet bills.

Taz had been hanging around a local smallholding, where a kind lady had been feeding him.

He disappeared for a several weeks and when he turned up in a neighbour’s garden he was in a terrible state.

He was rushed to the vets, where he was diagnosed with chemical burns.

CPL branch co-ordinator, Alex Thomson, said: “We believe it is likely that he put his head into some kind of agricultural chemicals as he had been spotted on a local farm.

 

“Taz has been taken in by one of our fosterers, who has been offering him plenty of TLC, as well as painstakingly applying antibiotic cream to affected areas every day.

"This has been painful and difficult for him but he has learnt to trust her, and we are starting to see slight improvements.”

Because of the severity of Taz’s injuries he requires ongoing vet treatment.

Volunteers at the Lanarkshire Branch of Cats Protection are hoping that fellow cat-lovers will dig deep to donate after seeing Taz’s plight.

Alex added: “We don’t often do public appeals but this is a long term and expensive case, but it is worth it as he is the most friendly and affectionate little guy you can imagine, despite his ordeal.”  

Anyone wishing to donate to Taz’s care should visit  https://www.cats.org.uk/lanarkshire  and click the donate button.

Cats Protection’s Lanarkshire Branch is part of a national network of around 250 volunteer-run branches and 36 centres that help around 200,000 cats and kittens each year.
