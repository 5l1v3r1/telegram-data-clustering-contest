Here Is The Reason Why “Ok Boomer” Is Activating Your Google Assistant

11 Nov 2019 by Johan Moreno

The hottest viral catchphrase used by Generation Z today will make fun of baby boomers - and may also trigger your Google Home. Various Redditors and YouTubers have noticed that saying “Ok boomer” or “Hey Boomer” will trigger Google Assistant to activate on their Assistant-enabled speakers.

While Google Assistant will wake up after it hears the phrase, the device will not actually respond to requests after hearing “Ok boomer.”

It is likely that the device is being triggered with the phrase because of a “false accept,” said a Google spokesperson. The spokesperson said that there may be noise or words in the background that Google’s software has interpreted to be a hot word that would activate the device, like “Ok Google.” The company added that the company has protections in place from preventing false accepts from happening while using Assistant.

Both Google and boomer have the same “oo” sound contained within both words, which may be triggering a false accept. Other Redditors have noticed that words like “egg noodle” and “poodle” have successfully activated their device.

“Ok boomer” is a popular catchphrase with Generation Z (the age group of 7-year-olds to 22-years-old), used when expressing their frustration with something said by an older person.
