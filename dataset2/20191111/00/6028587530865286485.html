Winter has its perennials: wool scarves, snug sweaters and boots of any kind. And no other fashion item encapsulates the season—and brings together all the aforementioned layers—more than a chic camel coat. Its sandy hue acts as a neutral base, allowing for what’s underneath to pop while also highlighting the rich colors of autumn leaves.

Whether you dress it up with a navy suit and loafers for a day at the office, or down with jeans and a pair of high-tops for a weekend outing with the boys, a great camel coat is versatile enough to work in myriad situations. Here are some of the best of the season that are well worth the investment

Givenchy Slim-Fit Wool and Cashmere-Blend Coat

From the streamlined profile to the pronounced gold buttons with the “4G” logo, this camel coat from the French label Givenchy is elegance personified. Factor in the structured shoulders and jacquard lining and this cashmere-blend topper by designer Clare Waight Keller is a must-have for anyone looking for a high dose of, as the French say, éclat.

Loro Piana Livingstone Cashmere Topcoat

Loro Piana is the quintessential cold-weather brand. So much so that many other labels boast of how they source materials from this storied Italian wool purveyor, creating quality pieces made of the finest cashmere and other plush knits. Just take a look at the Livingstone coat—with its parka-style silhouette oversized pockets and striking suede collar—and understand why Loro Piana has such a great reputation.

Schott NYC Single-Breasted Officer Wool-Blend Topcoat

French and Italian brands are considered to be the pinnacle in the fashion industry. And though there is some truth to that statement, don’t discount the output of stateside labels. Schott NYC, in particular, has shown how the U.S. contingent is worthy of the same praise as its foreign brethren—evidenced by this slick wool camel coat with a distinct collar and brown buttons.

Ted Baker London Danez Sim-Fit Wool Overcoat

No matter your body size, slim fits, when cut with precision, do as the name suggests. And who doesn’t want to look a few pounds lighter, especially in a season when pies and pumpkin-spice brews are seemingly everywhere? So, if you want to hide the fact that you’ve had a few too many slices for dinner, check out this overcoat by Ted Baker.

Theory Melton Topcoat

When it comes to brands that offer elegant pieces at relatively affordable prices, Theory is at the top of most lists. Season after season, its stable of suits, separates, and coats stick to sleek mindset. With a three-button closure, notch lapel and length that sits right above the knee, this camel coat, in particular, is a great example of the Theory’s resolutely timeless quality.

Topman Camel Double Breasted Coat

For those with smaller frames, a double-breasted coat will do wonders. The way it adds structure to your build in all the right places, making it seem that you’ve been pumping iron more than you probably have, makes it a virtual gym membership—without the hassle. And if you’re looking for a camel option that won’t break the bank, check out this one by Topman.

Saint Laurent Tailored Car Coat

Want to take your wardrobe to the next level? Look no further than Saint Laurent, one of the menswear industry’s buzziest brands. Designer Anthony Vaccarello imbues his collection with a rock ’n’ roll, making it a go-to for those that prescribe to that vibe. With this Tailored Car Coat, however, he stuck to a more en masse sensibility with a fitted silhouette that appeals to anyone who values quality. Make sure to take it for a spin.
