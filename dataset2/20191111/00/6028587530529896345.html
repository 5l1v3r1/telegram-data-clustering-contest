Travel Professionals Recommend These 40 Top-Rated Products To Pack In Your Suitcase

11 Nov 2019 by Fiona Tapp

As airlines impose more and more baggage restrictions on travelers, our suitcase real estate becomes pretty valuable. To earn a spot in your carry on or inside your luggage, travel gear, clothes and beauty products need to be more than just useful or pretty. They have to really enhance your travel experience.Travel professionals including airline staff, travel writers, bloggers, influencers and travel agents log a lot of miles traveling all around the world. They know which travel products live up to the hype and which you can pass on.

Separated into different categories including the best clothes and shoes to wear to the airport, the self care products that you can squeeze in your bag and the best case to choose to put it all inside.

These are the 40 top-rated products for your next trip as recommended by travel professionals.

The Best Luggage And Bags

Travel experts and frequent flyers agreed that when checking luggage, the most important elements were that the case was sturdy, strong and easy to spot on the carousel upon arrival.

The iFLY Fibertech Luggage collection comes in adorable candy colors like mint, grape and lemon. The hard shell keeps everything safe and protected inside yet is still light enough to lift easily.

Run through the airport at speed with the four 360-degree wheels when connections are tight or just look good on the curb waiting for your taxi! The brand also offers a 10-year warranty for total peace of mind.

Airlines allow for a personal item on board, experts suggest you maximize your space by taking a bag that can be used for a variety of purposes once you arrive at your destination.

Susan Nevin is a 25-year veteran flight attendant and always takes a small leather backpack on her jaunts. “You can dress it up or down, and pair it with jeans or a little black dress. They are so versatile,” she says.

The Holly & Tanager Protege leather mini backpack is a bestselling chic, lightweight option with front and back pockets and enough space for a 13 inch laptop!

Nevin also likes to take at least one bag that can be used more casually for the beach, like this one from Sea Bags of Maine. These sustainable bags are made from recycled sail cloth and come in nautical themed designs like stripes, anchors and marine life.

For an elegant choice for the evening The Lo & Sons Waverley 2 bag fits the bill. It can be worn four ways, across the body, on the waist as a belt bag, over the shoulder or as a wristlet.

Overwhelmingly, regular travelers, like Cepee Tabibian, the founder of SheHitRefresh, favor luggage pods or packing cubes to keep their belongings organized in transit.

Try the pack pods from Six Moon Designs which come in bright colors and are water resistant.

Inside the compact bag that weighs less than two pounds, you’ll find 11 pieces of fitness equipment including resistance bands, a door anchor, and an inflatable Pilates ball to use in conjunction with the 20 downloadable videos. You get all the benefits of a full make up bag with just a couple of products, plus they are really cute!

Hair Essentials

Mary Sander is a travel agent who regularly visits hotels to check them out prior to recommending them to her clients. For travel across North America, she always makes space in her luggage for her Dyson Airwrap. “It’s my absolute favorite product and I can’t travel without it!” She says.

The Dyson Airwrap features the coanda effect which seems to magically attract hair to the wand where it then wraps and creates perfect curls. The set also comes with a hairdryer attachment and flat and round brushes.

Don’t forget to pack a smart little kit like the Cricket ‘Eat, Sleep, Messy Bun Hair Tin’ available from Ulta, which comes stocked with hair ties and bobby pins to keep you looking good on the go.

If space is really at a premium, pack a folding travel hairdryer like the T3 Featherweight Compact Folding Dryer which comes with dual voltage making it perfect for traveling.

A trip to your local CVS pharmacy before you leave for the airport is essential. Stock up on vitamins and minerals, any supplements or over the counter remedies for the usual travel maladies like motion sickness, and also grab a travel sized first aid kit, just in case.

Travel experts know how to arrive feeling refreshed rather than fried even after a long flight and little sleep. Shop Saje for mini travel kits like their Pocket Farmacy Remedy Convenience Kit with uplifting scents to beat jet lag and solve common travel gripes like digestive issues or the effects of stress.

Hats Worth Packing

Susan Nevin wears Skypro, the brand specially designed for airline professionals, but anyone can benefit from their advanced comfort and quality. Their Florence Barnes ankle boots are chic and can easily be worn all day and into the evening.

For really snowy, cold destinations choose a cozy pair of NeverWet treated boots from Bearpaw which resist water and stains and keep your feet super toasty and warm.

If you’re flying to a beach or warm destination you’ll need some flip flops, but why not save space without sacrificing style by packing a pair of Cambiami customizable sandals. Fitted with smart removable straps you can have three (or more) sandals in one!

Shoes that do double duty really are the stars of your traveling wardrobe. The innovative Pashion Footwear brand shoes switch from stylish high heels to comfortable flats in seconds and offer two completely different looks in one pair of shoes.

The heel attachment when the shoes are flat is so small it can fit into a pocket!

Travel experts also loved the versatility and comfort of casual, light shoes. These can be worn around the city, to and from the airport and if you do need to pack them, they won’t make your case too heavy.

Top picks included shoes from Merrell including their Gridway collection of super light and comfortable shoes made with 100% recycled yarn knit.

OTBT Shoes also scored high points for their incredibly light and cozy wool sneakers which are so on trend for the season and the perfect choice for those hitting their baggage weight allowance.
