Why Was Wright Medical Group Stock Up 30% Last Monday?

11 Nov 2019 by Trefis Team

Wright Medical Group (NASDAQ: WMGI), a medical device maker that caters to the orthopedic market, saw its stock rally by over 30% on Monday, after news emerged that it would be acquired by larger rival Stryker Corporation. Stryker will pay $30.75 in cash for each of Wright’s outstanding shares, valuing the company’s outstanding equity at ~$4 billion. For perspective, the stock closed at $29.04 on Monday. The deal is expected to close over the second half of 2020. Stryker indicated that Wright’s expertise in devices for the upper extremities (shoulder, elbow, wrist, and hand) would complement its existing product lines.
