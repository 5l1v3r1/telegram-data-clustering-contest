Two pedestrians killed in separate road collisions

11 Nov 2019, 01:00

TWO people have died and a man has been injured following three separate weekend road collisions.

An 88-year-old man and a pedestrian in her 40s both died in hospital following separate road collisions on Friday.

Daniel Louden, from the Ballymoney area, died on Saturday night after being injured in a one-vehicle crash on the Frosses Road, Ballymoney at around 8am.

The woman, whose name has not been released, was struck by a car on the Shore Road, Newtownabbey on Friday morning. It is understood she was on her way to work when she was struck close to the Northern Regional College.

The police have appealed for information about both incidents.

Ballymoney UUP councillor, Alderman Tom McKeown, said: "This is a terrible tragedy and our thoughts are with Mr Louden's loved ones at this incredibly difficult time."

Speaking of the Shore Road incident, DUP councillor Paul Hamill said: "This has been a very difficult weekend for the woman's family, and sadly they are now dealing with bereavement. It is a tragedy for all concerned, and I'm certain the community will rally and support each other in the coming period."

Police in Co Down meanwhile are appealing for information on a one-vehicle collision in which a car came off a road and crashed in a field.

Officers were travelling along the Downpatrick Road outside Ballynahinch at around 10.50pm on Saturday when they came across a tree that appeared to have been knocked down.

On further inspection they found a black Renault Clio car in a nearby field with an injured man inside. He was taken to hospital for treatment but his condition is not known.

PSNI Inspector Brian Mills said: "I want to appeal to anyone who may have been travelling on the Downpatrick Road on Saturday between 10pm and 10:50pm and believes they may have witnessed what occurred, or captured what occurred on their dash cam to call us on 101."
