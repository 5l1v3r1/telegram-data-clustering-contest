Billie Eilish Is Giving Tickets Away To Fans ‘Fighting’ Climate Change

11 Nov 2019, 04:58 by Jared Richards

Billie Eilish is encouraging fans to take a stand on climate change by offering free tickets for those who ‘take action’, in addition to committing to making her concerts more sustainable.

Eilish, who was in Australia this April and May, has continually toured this year off the back of her massive debut album, When We All Fall Asleep, Where Do We Go?.

While announcing even-more new dates, the 17-year-old pop-star committed to making her concerts greener (via NME). To do so, she’ll be teaming up with Reverb, an environmental non-for-profit who have previously worked with Shawn Mendes, Fleetwood Mac and P!nk, among others.

Part of this involves a ban on plastic straws and increased recycling options at venues, as well as an ‘eco-village’ set-up at concerts allowing fans to learn more about what they can do to help. There’s also an opportunity to win tickets via Global Citizen, a website which offers redeemable points for each positive ‘action’ individuals do (largely signing petitions).

Earlier this month, Eilish attended a youth climate strike in LA with Greta Thunberg. When We All Fall Asleep… cut ‘all the good girls go to hell’ references our climate crisis, exploring anxiety stemming from California’s unprecedented fires in 2018, which all-in-all affected 750,000 acres of land, as well as claiming more than 17,000 homes and 100 lives.

Eilish will release a new song called ‘everything i wanted’ next week, her first new music since a pretty shoddy ‘bad guy’ remix featuring Justin Bieber. Listen to ‘all the good girls go to hell’ below.
