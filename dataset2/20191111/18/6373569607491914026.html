Former Royal Navy medic has a message for thief who stole poppy collection money

'I’d like to give a message to the person who did it. They’re not welcome at Knowle House Surgery anymore and I feel they should hold their head in shame'

11 Nov 2019, 18:01 by Peter Chapman

Craig Smith-Avery is an ex-serviceman and the practice manager of Knowle House Surgery - where it is believed money being raised for the Royal British Legion was taken on Friday.

The box disappeared and was found empty in the waiting room by Mr Smith-Avery who was 'disgusted' and placed a sign in the surgery to condemn the 'scumbag' thief.

Mr Smith-Avery has been at the surgery for 23 years. Before that he served as a medic in the Royal Navy for 12 years until rheumatoid arthritis caused him to leave.

He is passionate and proud to have spent time in the forces. Taking money from the nation's largest Armed Forces charity upset him enough to ban the thief and put up a sign.

On the incident, he said: "On Friday lunchtime, I believe, a member of the public wanted to purchase a poppy.

"My receptionist went to get the poppy jar, which we normally just keep behind reception, and couldn’t find it. She put out a message saying 'has anybody seen it?'

"From that nobody came back saying they’d seen it. Everybody said it was on reception.

"With that I wandered around the practice to have a look, and outside the practice. We’ve got a blind spot in our reception and I found the poppy jar behind the blind spot and it had obviously been forced open.

"All the the contents, the money, were taken away.

"I know my team are very saddened, upset and quite frankly disgusted that somebody would rob a charity, steal from a charity.

"For me personally as an ex-serviceman I think it’s outrageous that they chose the poppy appeal to target.

"I’d like to give a message to the person who did it. They’re not welcome at Knowle House Surgery anymore and I feel they should hold their head in shame at the fact that they feel they can steal from a charity."

Craig estimates there was between £50 and £80 taken.

The Royal British Legion is the country’s largest Armed Forces charity, with 235,000 members, 110,000 volunteers and a network of partners and charities.

The police have been notified but won't be taking further action.
