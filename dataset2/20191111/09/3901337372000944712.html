Pep: What I Told City Stars Amid Handball Controversy

11 Nov 2019, 09:28 by David Kappel

Manchester City boss Pep Guardiola has revealed what he told his players after his side's 3-1 defeat to Liverpool, during which he furiously ran up and down the sidelines over two controversial refereeing calls.

Click through the mini-gallery above to see images of a furious Guardiola on Sunday.

The Spaniard and his team conceded their third loss of the 2019/20 Premier League campaign when they went down 3-1 at Anfield on Sunday.

The Reds' opener came just a minute after City felt they should have received a penalty after the ball hit Trent Alexander-Arnold's arm inside the box.

Bernardo Silva, though, had handled the ball before it deflected on to Alexander-Arnold's arm and VAR did not intervene.

The visitors were further aggrieved when the ball hit Alexander-Arnold's arm inside the box in the second half, but again no penalty was given.

Guardiola was seen running towards the fourth official, holding up two fingers and shouting "two handballs, two!", but he insisted after the game that his players must not to react to the incidents.

"Heads up, guys. Do not to use the social media to say your thoughts on some departments. Talk about how good the football was," he said he told his stars, as quoted by ESPN.

He was seen shaking match official Michael Oliver's hand in an animated manner after the full-time whistle, but insisted this was not a sarcastic reaction.

"No, I was not sarcastic," he said.

"It was 'thank you very much'. All the time, when I speak to referees and my colleagues, I say 'thank you and good luck'."

City have dropped to fourth place, and are now nine points behind unbeaten leaders Liverpool, with Leicester City and Chelsea moving above the defending champions following their wins on Saturday.
