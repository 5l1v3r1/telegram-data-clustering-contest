Jack Aitchison hailed as Celtic loanee nets for fourth game running

The 19-year-old is impressing while on loan at Forest Green and his side sit top of the League Two table.

11 Nov 2019, 08:25 by Mark McDougall, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/mark-mcdougall/

Celtic youngster Jack Aitchison is continuing his fine form out on loan with Forest Green Rovers.

And the Hoops loanee netted his fourth goal in four games during his side's FA Cup win over Billericay.

He had gone six games without scoring since netting the winner on his debut against Cambridge but has hit a fine run of form in recent weeks.

Starting with a goal against Mansfield, he also netted against Morecambe and Cheltenham to keep his side at the top of the English League Two table.

And local website Gloucestershire Live have been full of praise for the 19-year-old, admitting that the club would love to sign him on a permanent basis but insist he's destined for a higher level.

They said: "The on-loan Celtic star is really shining since moving to Nailsworth in the summer and on Saturday, he scored his fourth goal in four games.

"He has scored important League Two goals against Mansfield Town, Morecambe and Cheltenham Town, and perhaps a less important one against Billericay, but it’s still crucial for his momentum to continue.

"The Scottish attacker is a clinical finisher; rarely missing any opportunities he gets and at the weekend demonstrated that once again.

"The 19-year-old has scored five goals for Rovers in all competitions now, including the four in as many games, and he will undoubtedly make it at a strong level in the future.

"Unfortunately for Forest Green, this means it is virtually impossible for the Nailsworth-based club to sign him on a permanent deal.

"However, Aitchison’s form, if continued, with his incredible talent, can definitely help Rovers in their bid for promotion to League One."

Aitchison is Celtic's youngest ever player and goalscorer after he netted on his debut in a 7-1 win over Motherwell.

He came off the bench in what was Ronny Deila's final match in charge and scored with his first touch.
