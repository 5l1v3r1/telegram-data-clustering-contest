Pierre-Emerick Aubameyang's relationship with YouTube channel AFTV 'causing dismay' at Arsenal

Arsenal striker Aubameyang has been appointed the club's most recent captain after Granit Xhaka's fall-out with fans - but it hasn't gone down well with all at the Emirates

11 Nov 2019, 08:21 by James Whaling, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/james-whaling/

Unai Emery seemingly has yet another problem on his hands at Arsenal after reports emerged that new skipper Pierre-Emerick Aubameyang's relationship with YouTube channel AFTV is causing dismay at the Emirates.

Emery turned to star striker Aubameyang to take the armband after Granit Xhaka's tantrum against Crystal Palace led to the Swiss midfielder being stripped of the captaincy.

Xhaka swore at Arsenal fans as he was jeered during his substitution and has since failed to play for the club with his future at the Emirates looking bleak.

Although Aubameyang is a generally poplar figure at Arsenal, his appointment as skipper has caused an element of concern, report The Athletic.

And that is down to his rapport with AFTV, which is said to have 'irritated' some figures at the club.

Some at Arsenal apparently believe the upsurge in popularity of AFTV, formerly known as ArsenalFanTV, has contributed to the negativity towards the team.

Aubameyang has appeared to 'like' a number of posts from AFTV on Instagram , including one from June which criticised Emery and another in September which encouraged the sale of Xhaka.

The 30-year-old has also reportedly been urged to stop engaging with 'Troopz' - one of AFTV's most notorious contributors - after Aubameyang seemingly invited him to games at the Emirates in his executive box.

It is a situation Emery scarcely needs and one he must try and swiftly resolve given Aubameyang's importance to him - and the club - on the pitch.

The Gabonese international has plundered 50 goals in 78 appearances since arriving from Borussia Dortmund and is their top scorer this campaign with eight strikes in the Premier League.

His contract runs out at the end of next season and - while Arsenal are keen to extend - Aubameyang is understood to prefer to see if the Gunners qualify for next season's Champions League.

Arsenal are already eight points off the top-four pace after they were well beaten by second-placed Leicester in a 2-0 defeat the King Power Stadium on Saturday.
