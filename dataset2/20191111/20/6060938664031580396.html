70,000 BSNL staff opts for VRS, company hopes to save Rs 7,000 crore a year

11 Nov 2019, 20:56 by Pankaj Doval

Highlights

  • The company hopes to save over Rs 7,000 crore annually after the completion of the process, and is eyeing to turn profitable in a period of five years
  • BSNL CMD Pravin Kumar Purwar said around 70,000 workers have already accepted the VRS, and he expects more to opt in before the closing date of December 3

NEW DELHI: Armed with a generous government bailout, state-run ailing telecom PSU BSNL expects more than half of its 1.55 lakh employees to opt for VRS, leading to a savings of nearly Rs 600 crore per month on salary bills, once the process gets completed. The company hopes to save over Rs 7,000 crore annually after the completion of the process, and is eyeing to turn profitable in a period of five years.

BSNL CMD Pravin Kumar Purwar said around 70,000 workers have already accepted the VRS, and he expects more to opt in before the closing date of December 3. “We have given a handsome VRS package and are confident that our target of 80,000 people opting for VRS will be met,” Purwar told TOI. VRS acceptance at BSNL has exceeded government’s initial estimate of 58,000 employees.

BSNL is the country’s biggest loss-making PSU and is estimated to have losses of nearly Rs 14,000 crore in FY18-19. After the government announced an over Rs 70,000 crore relief package for BSNL and the other (smaller) state-run telcocom company MTNL, the companies hope that reduced manpower bills and introduction of new technologies such as 4G will help them chart out a credible and healthy revival path.

“In March, we had written to the government for provision of 4G spectrum. We have been preparing and planning now, and hope to launch 4G services over the next 6 months. This is our target,” Purwar said.

Asked whether the reduced manpower will have any impact on the operational efficiencies of the company, he said that BSNL has been taking steps to add people “through outsourcing” model in certain areas. “Functions such as plant maintenance and running of rural exchanges may see some impact due to the absence of manpower after the VRS. We are taking care of this through an outsourcing model.”
