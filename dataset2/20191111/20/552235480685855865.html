Six-week old kittens found dumped in skip in Livingston

One of the kittens is believed to be fighting for its life following the discovery in Livingston.

11 Nov 2019, 20:42 by Kathleen Speirs

Two tiny kittens have been found dumped in a skip in Livingston.

Shocking images of the six-week-old kittens huddled together in freezing conditions were shared by the Cat Protection West Lothian on Saturday.

While one of the cats is doing okay, the other is believed to be fighting for its life.

The charity shared photos of the abandoned animals on their official Facebook page.

The post read: "These two six-week-old kittens were retrieved from inside a box which was put into a skip in Livingston. 

"They must have spent last night, remember is was minus two degrees, in this box in the skip.

"Wed got them to our vet this morning, they spent the day in an oxygen tent with a heat pad.

"The wee ginger kitten is okay but it's touch and go for the other one."

 

Volunteers are urging anyone with information about the mother to come forward.

The post continued: "Our real concern now is for the mum cat, she will not be neutered.

"If anyone knows anything we will pay for the mum cat to be neutered, no questions asked, nothing.

"Please, please, please just call.

"We won't ask your name.

"Just please let us pay to get the mum neutered and there will be no more unwanted kittens put in a box and into a skip."

The photos sparked outcry on social media.

Frank Love commented: "Why in God's name did they just dump the poor things to die?

"Surely they are aware of organisations like Cats Protection and SSPCA who would do their utmost to help re-home them.

"Even leaving them in a place where they would be easily found would have been preferable to a skip.

"Totally heartless."

 

Julie Reid added: "Why can people not just call you and admit they can't look after them?

"Instead they dump them in the most horrible situation.

"We all have struggles in life but doesn't mean we have to be cruel.

"That's why amazing organisations like the cat protection is here."

Lynda Bell wrote: "It takes a certain type of person to do this!

"Something needs to be done.

"Absolutely sickening!"
