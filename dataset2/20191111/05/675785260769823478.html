UK weather forecast: Five 'danger to life' flood warnings as fresh downpours hit

The Met Office has predicted flooding and up to 10cm of snow in parts of the UK today. The Environment Agency has issued more than 150 flood warnings and alerts for England

11 Nov 2019, 05:47 by Matthew Dresch, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/matthew-dresch/

The Environment Agency has five 'danger to life' flood warnings in force in England as further downpours ravage the UK this week.

An additional 48 flood warnings and 103 less serious flood alerts have also been issued across England.

The 'danger to life' alerts are in place for the River Don in Yorkshire, while the flood warnings are in north-east, central and south-west England.

Snow is expected to accompany the torrential rainfall this morning as flurries fall across the higher ground of Scotland and England.

The Met Office has a rain warning in force for parts of the north-west today, with the downpours due to end at noon tomorrow.

It has also issued an alert about heavy showers across the Midlands and north-east on Thursday.

 

The Don, which flows through Sheffield, Rotherham and Doncaster, hit its highest recorded level at just over 6.3m on Friday.

It will be a chilly start for most today with temperatures peaking at 10C this morning, before dropping to single figures across the whole country.

Meteorologists predict sub-zero temperatures on Wednesday, Thursday, Friday and Saturday morning.

Met Office forecaster Luke Miall said: "There will be some snow for higher ground in Scotland, say above 400 metres we could see accumulations of five to 10 centimetres in spots.

 

"A little bit of snow falling but not settling across the southern Uplands, Cumbria fells and perhaps the northern Pennines.

"There could be an early frost in the south-east. Rain continuing through the morning giving a fair lingering of heavy rain for a time across the far south-east.

"Then it's a day of sunshine and blustery showers, the showers will be heavy there will be hail and thunder mixed in. Always the west seeing the worst of those showers. We could see some getting into parts of central England and Wales through the afternoon."

 

He added that 'persistent rain and showers' will push into north-west England tomorrow, which could result in further flooding.

Tonight will be dull and damp across Scotland with cloudy skies and showers which will turn into longer spells of rain.

Cloud and rain will sink southwards during the night into parts of southern Scotland and northern England by dawn.

Tomorrow will be a mixed day of sunny spells and showers which will turn into longer spells of rain across Scotland, northern England and the Midlands.

 

Showers may be wintry over high ground across Scotland.

The best of the sunshine will be in the south and east to start but there will be showers later.

The weather on Wednesday will be mostly fine with lengthy sunny spells in the east.

Cloud will begin to build in from the west during the morning with rain across Northern Ireland, Wales and south-west England later.

On Thursday there will be rain across much of England and Wales.

Met Office five-day weather forecast

Today:

A band of rain, some heavy, will continue east across England, clearing the far south-east around midday, whilst becoming slow moving across the far north of Scotland. Sunny spells and blustery showers following. Windy with coastal gales in the west.

Tonight:

Windy with showers, but also some longer spells of rain across the NW, where gales will affect coastal districts. Best of the dry weather in the south-east.

Tuesday:

Rather cold, with sunny spells and showers, but also a band of more persistent rain moving south through the day. Very windy in the west.

Outlook for Wednesday to Friday:

Rain or showers at times in the south, perhaps with some snow on hills. Drier and colder in the north with some wintry showers. Windy at times.
