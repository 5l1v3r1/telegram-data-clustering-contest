'Scumbag' who stole poppy collection money publicly shamed

'To the scumbag who stole the poppy box you are no longer welcome at our surgery'

11 Nov 2019, 14:08 by Peter Chapman

A "scumbag" who stole money from the poppy collection box at a Plymouth GP surgery has been shamed in the most brutal way.

Knowle House Surgery practice manager Craig Smith-Avery has taken action after money was stolen from a charity collection box on site.

It is believed the money, which was being raised for the Royal British Legion, was taken sometime on Friday as the box disappeared and was found empty in the waiting room.

Now the GP practice has responded in the perfect way - by publicly shaming the thief and in no uncertain terms, telling them they are never allowed back.

About the theft Mr Smith-Avery said: "It was Friday just after lunch and one of the girls went to find the box, it wasn’t there.

"We couldn't find it and came to the conclusion someone swiped it.

"I went for a wander and in a blind corner of the office it was ripped open and emptied.

"I was disgusted. I'm ex-services, so that hurt. But stealing from charity in general is disgusting.

"I notified the police and apologised to the Royal British Legion."

Craig estimates there was between £50 and £80 taken. He said: "People have been good to put money in and support veterans and the fallen, and someone sees it as easy money.

"It makes me sad and angry."

He also placed a notice in the surgery informing the thief that they are no longer welcome at Knowle House. The message reads: "To the scumbag who stole the poppy box you are no longer welcome at our surgery.

"You should hang your head in shame."

The Royal British Legion is the country’s largest Armed Forces charity, with 235,000 members, 110,000 volunteers and a network of partners and charities.

The police have been notified but won't be taking further action, it has been reported on Radio Plymouth.
