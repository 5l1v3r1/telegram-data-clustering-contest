Man stabbed in the back outside busy row of shops on Kings Heath High Street

The knife victim was attacked in broad daylight in a popular shopping area just before midday on Monday afternoon, but the attacker is still on the run from police

11 Nov 2019, 14:02 by Stephanie Balloo, https://www.facebook.com/MissBalloo/

A man was stabbed in the back in broad daylight on a busy city high street.

The victim, in his 30s, was knifed on Kings Heath High Street just before noon on Monday afternoon, November 11.

He suffered knife wounds to his back, which were not believed to be life-theatening.

Police and ambulance crews were seen lining the street outside the Wilko and Burton stores at around 12pm, while an air ambulance landed at nearby Kings Heath Park.

 

West Midlands Police cars were seen rushing up and down the street on blue lights as the attacker remained on the run.

The stabbing victim was rushed to hospital for further checks.

Police urged anyone who witnessed the attack or who had information to come forward.

 

A statement from the force read: "Police are currently at the scene of a stabbing on Kings Heath High Street, Birmingham shortly before 12pm today (11 November).

"A man in his 30s has been taken to hospital to be treated for injuries to his back, which are not currently believed to be life threatening.

"Investigations are at the early stages and anyone with information can contact us quoting log number 960 of 11/11, via Live Chat at  west-midlands.police.uk  between 8am and midnight, call 101 anytime or contact Crimestoppers anonymously on 0800 555 111."

A spokeswoman for West Midlands Ambulance Service added: "We were called to reports of an assault on High Street, Kings Heath, Birmingham at 11.46am this morning.

"An ambulance, critical care car from the Midlands Air Ambulance and a BASICS doctor attended the scene. One man was given treatment on scene for injuries not believed to be serious before being taken to hospital for further checks.

BirminghamLive has contacted Wilko for a comment.
