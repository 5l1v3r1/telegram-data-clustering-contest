Best Christmas markets UK guide from the dates to top locations to bookmark

Best Christmas markets in the UK for 2019 including dates and locations across Manchester, Bath, Edinburgh and Birmingham

11 Nov 2019, 17:25 by Julie Delahaye, https://www.facebook.com/juliedjourno

There's nothing quite like Christmas markets to get into the festive spirit, and the food news for Brits that you won't need to go far.

The UK boasts some brilliant Christmas markets with popular hotspots including Manchester, Bath, Edinburgh and Birmingham.

We're talking everything from beautiful wooden stalls where you can buy everything from mulled wine and hot chocolate to handmade Christmas decorations and arts and crafts.

Some markets even offer extra fun and games with fairground-style rides and ice rinks.

We take a look at everything you need to know from the dates the markets are running, to the locations across the cities that need to be on your radar...

Thinking of going abroad instead? You may want to check out our round-ups of Europe's best Christmas markets .

1. Manchester

The main festivities will be taking place on Albert Square where you'll find heaps of stalls selling everything from mulled wine and gingerbread to arts and crafts or Christmas decorations.

However there are plenty more locations to discover too including markets on Exchange Square, Cathedral Gardens, Piccadilly Gardens, Market Street, New Cathedral Street, King's Street and St Ann's Square.

The Albert Square Christmas market will be running from Friday 8th November through to Sunday 22nd December.

You can find out more on the Visit Manchester website .

 

2. Bath

Bath's city centre will be getting a magical festive makeover this season with over 150 stalls decked in twinkling lights and offering up everything from beautiful gifts to seriously delicious food. (We're talking everything from hog roasts to mulled wine).

There are also plenty of fun workshops including a lesson on making a festive wreath to be discovered.

The Bath Christmas market will be on from Thursday 28th November through to Sunday 15th December.

You can find out more on the Bath Christmas market website .

3. Edinburgh

Edinburgh is pulling out all the stops with its Christmas market on Princes Street and Market Street.

As well as a plethora of traditional stalls and foodie hotspots, you'll also find highlights including a ferris wheel and fairground-style rides, and a Santa's Grotto.

There are also plenty of fun free events throughout the season from a Santa Fun Run to festive light shows.

Plus, as it's in the heart of the city centre it's ideal if you've never been to the city before - check out our Edinburgh city guide for top tips on what not to miss. (On a budget? You may also want to read our round-up of the best cheap Edinburgh hotels ).

The Edinburgh Christmas market will be running from Saturday 16th November through to the 4th January 2020.

You can find out more on the Edinburgh Christmas market website .

 

4. Birmingham

If you've always wanted to explore the best Christmas markets in Germany but you can't justify the annual leave/budget this festive season, then Birmingham's iconic market could be the next best thing.

The main bulk of the action takes place on and around Victoria Square where you'll find the picturesque wooden lodges with stalls selling clothes, trinkets, decorations and arts and crafts.

As for the food? Expect plenty of spots serving up Pretzels, schnitzels, bratwursts, and roasted almonds, as well as gluhwein, weissbeer (wheat beer), or even mouthwatering hot chocolate.

The market will run from the Thursday 7th November through to Monday 23rd December.

You can find out more on the Birmingham Christmas market website .

5. York

Looking to get your Christmas shopping done in a festive atmosphere, outside of the shopping centres? Then York's unique St Nicholas Fair could be just the place.

Located on Parliament Street, it offers everything from Christmas decorations to wizarding memorabilia (head to the Shambles to fulfil your magical needs), as well as plenty of brilliant food, clothing and general knick knacks sure to make for the perfect gift for a loved one.

The York market will run from Thursday 14th November until Sunday 22nd December.

You can find out more on the York Christmas Festival .

6. London

There are countless Christmas markets and festive offerings across London, but there are some hotspots that need to be on your radar.

One is the Hyde Park Winter Wonderland with heaps of stalls alongside fairground rides, a ferris wheel, vibrant bars and a plethora of dining options.

For something a little more laidback, the traditional stalls along the Southbank can be quite picturesque not to mention you can enjoy views of the river and London Eye.

 

7. Winchester

The Winchester Cathedral Christmas market has everything you could want for one of those postcard-worthy festive days out.

There are over 100 wooden stalls decorated in twinkling lights and selling beautiful decorations, the backdrop of the cathedral for the views, and there's even a dedicated Crafts Village.

The market will be running from Thursday 21st November through to Sunday 22nd December.

You can find out more on the Winchester Cathedral Christmas market website .

8. Bristol

Located in the city centre, the Bristol Christmas market offers over 50 stalls selling all sorts of gifts, as well as extras including visits from Santa and reindeer visits.

There is often live music on at the Jäger Barn Bar for those looking for a fun night out, while the more competitive of festive goers can try their hand at the sport of curling. 

The Bristol Christmas market will run from Friday 8th November through to Monday 23rd December.

You can find out more on the Visit Bristol website .

9. Nottingham

With over stalls, an ice bar, ice rink and live entertainment ranging from violinists to DJs, there's plenty of fun to be had at the Nottingham Winter Wonderland.

Then of course there are the countless food stalls with heaps of treats from gingerbread to mulled wine, as well as plenty of spots where you can pick up gifts for your loved ones.

The Nottingham market will run from Friday 15th November through to Tuesday 31st December.

You can find out more on the Nottingham Winter Wonderland website .

10. Liverpool

Those looking to get into the festive spirit will have plenty of choice across Liverpool.

There are markets taking place at Liverpool ONE (which will feature a 30m-high Christmas tree), St George's Hall and the Baltic Triangle, while the Liverpool Christmas Ice Festival is sure to be a hit with children and parents alike.

The Liverpool Christmas market outside St George's Hall will run from Saturday 16th November through to Monday 23rd December 2019.

11. Padstow

The Padstow Christmas Festival marks its 12th anniversary this year with another four-day celebration bringing a wealth of culinary delights and festive fun from some of Britain’s best loved chefs.

During the last twelve years, the festival, sponsored by Sharp's Brewery, has earned a reputation for showcasing a tempting selection of food ranging from chocolates, meats, and cheeses to artisan breads, cakes and preserves and pickles, not to mention award-winning craft beers, ciders, and locally-produced gins, rum, brandy, and vodka.

This year’s Chefs' Theatre line-up reads like a culinary Who’s Who, and includes Rick Stein, Nathan Outlaw, Paul Ainsworth, Nieves Barragán Mohacho, Phil Vickery,  Angela Hartnett, Tom Parker Bowles and Channel 4’s Million Pound Menu judge, Atul Kochhar, all ready to share their cooking secrets throughout the weekend.

New for 2019 is the ‘Silent Bistro’ - a unique 60 seater dining experience, pairing beer and food to music through headphones and the exclusive Secret Bar makes a welcome return.

Don’t forget to make a beeline into the town itself and enjoy its diverse collection of shops, boutiques, galleries, pubs and restaurants, while sampling Sharp’s festive ale, a limited edition Coconut Stout.

The market opens on December 5 and runs to December 8.

For more information visit the Padstow Christmas Festival website.
