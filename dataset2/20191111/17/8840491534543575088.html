Venue change as Mayo GAA stars turn their hand to volleyball

11 Nov 2019, 17:00

Members of the Mayo senior men's football team will play the Mayo women's volleyball team in a friendly volleyball match next weekend.

The one-off game will take place next Sunday, November 17, at 5.30 p.m. at Barnacarroll Community Centre, not Balla Secondary School at 4 p.m. as originally indicated.

The aim is to introduce volleyball to both boys and girls while having a bit of fun between two Mayo squads.

The Mayo women's volleyball team are encouraging locals to come out and learn about the sport, meet their team and have fun watching their ladies play against the men in a different field.

Said volleyball player, Mikal O'Boyle: “Volleyball is picking up quickly, so we wish to promote our sport--particularly to the younger crowd.

“We have a lot of respect for James Horan and the Mayo inter-county team who have made us so proud over the years, but of course we look forward to taking them on.

“We would encourage as many people as possible to come along to watch and maybe even consider getting involved in volleyball at some level.

"It'll be a fun day for all and a great way to see both Mayo sportswomen and sportsmen on one court”
