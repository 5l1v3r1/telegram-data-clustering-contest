'Handbags' Birmingham City fans won't be happy with Scott Parker's take on Jude Bellingham's booking

Bellingham had been left with a bloodied mouth after Anthony Knockaert fell on him - when Aleksandar Mitrovic needlessly became involved in the situation

11 Nov 2019, 15:07 by Brian Dick, https://www.facebook.com/blueswriterbriandick/?fref=ts

Birmingham City were not happy with Jude Bellingham’s booking in Saturday’s defeat to Fulham.

In first half injury time the 16-year-old was involved in a confrontation with match-winner in waiting Aleksandar Mitrovic, which led to referee Jarred Gillett showing a yellow card to both players.

Before that Bellingham had been left with a bloodied mouth after Anthony Knockaert fell on him - after which Mitrovic needlessly became involved in the debate which prompted the cautions.

The Blues youngster appeared to have done nothing to warrant a booking - and speaking after the game Pep Clotet admitted he didn’t know why he had been.

The Spaniard said: “I think Knockaert tried to tackle him - that’s what Jude told me, there was a  little bit of a misunderstanding with those two. Then Mitrovic appeared and I think it was normal emotion of the game.”

However, opposite number Scott Parker viewed the incident as a sign that Mitrovic is showing a different side to his undoubted goal-scoring prowess - even if it ended up with him being suspended for the match with QPR.

 

“I just see it as nothing really, in the heat of the moment a football match where you have got two teams that are fighting to get a result, just squaring up to each other, it’s a little bit of handbags really.

“I didn’t see nothing other than that, disappointing because obviously Mitro gets a booking and he will be out of the next game, bitterly disappointing but you wouldn’t want to take that out of the game - that’s part of it.

“Part of his game and part of the game I want him to have, that’s what I want us to be about - that side of it is exactly it.”
