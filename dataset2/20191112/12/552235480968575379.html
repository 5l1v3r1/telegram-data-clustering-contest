Monklands' Specsavers branches don pink outfits for Breast Cancer awareness day

Breast Cancer Now is the UK’s largest breast cancer charity and dedicated to funding research.

12 Nov 2019, 12:47 by Ian Bunting, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/ian-bunting/

Eyes were firmly fixed on fundraising fun as a Monklands opticians took part in Breast Cancer Now’s wear it pink day.

The teams at Specsavers in Airdrie and Coatbridge all donned the colour pink and decorated the stores in blush colours.

Funds were raised by donations to charity buckets and the collections raised £52.

Breast Cancer Now is the UK’s largest breast cancer charity and dedicated to funding research.

The charity believes that, by acting now, by 2050 everyone who develops breast cancer will live; but fundraising is vital to make it happen.

Andrew McGonagle, store director at Specsavers Airdrie and Coatbridge, said: ‘It was great fun to take part in wear it pink again this year with everyone getting on board to raise funds and awareness for Breast Cancer Now.

“A big thank you goes to everyone who donated; I’m delighted to be able to present these funds to this very worthy cause.”
