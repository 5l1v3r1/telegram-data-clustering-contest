Kerry Katona branded 'cokehead' by daughter's friend as she slams school mums for gossipping

Kerry Katona has hit back at gossipping parents at her daughter's school after one young child called her a 'cokehead'

12 Nov 2019, 09:08 by Emmeline Saunders

Kerry Katona was left reeling after her young daughter told her that friends at school had branded the Atomic Kittens singer a "cokehead".

The 39-year-old mum of five slammed gossipping mums and dads for talking about her previous addition issues, saying children of her daughter's age should not know about her lurid past.

"[She] came home a couple of weeks ago saying one of the kids in her class had called me a cokehead," Kerry wrote in her New magazine column.

"How do kids of [that age] know about my drugs past from over a decade ago? It goes to show the parents are talking about me to their children.

"I didn't know what a cokehead was at that age and I don't think the kids in [her] class should be aware of my problems," she went on.

"[She] is defensive of me, but I've always been open about it with my kids, so they know the truth."

Kerry became addicted to drugs at the age of 14, after her alcoholic mum gave them to her saying Kerry should try her "sherbet dip".

Years of cocaine abuse, toxic relationships and her bipolar diagnosis saw Kerry brought to the brink of suicide, unsure how she was still even alive.

Speaking of using cocaine as a crutch, Kerry said earlier this year: "I call it devils dandruff because it's toxic, it’s manipulating, it gives a false sense of security, it’s not really there for you, it's ruining you.

"It's an escapism. Taking coke is better than facing the scariest thing going on in your life. You're constantly chasing that buzz and you don't want the come down. The downers are awful."

She added: "After taking drugs I used to have fits. My eyes would roll back and I'd be frothing at the mouth.

"I could have died, I could have chocked on my own tongue, my own saliva."

Kerry's abusive ex-husband George Kay died in July this year from a drug overdose at the age of 39.

George, who shared daughter DJ with Kerry, died after eating cocaine off the desk of a Holiday Inn hotel in Cheshire, watched by horrified staff.
