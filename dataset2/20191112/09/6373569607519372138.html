Relocation of Plymouth Argyle season ticket holders to £8m-plus Mayflower Grandstand gets under way

It will be open for the first time when Derek Adams brings Morecambe to Home Park next month

12 Nov 2019, 09:44 by Chris Errington

Plymouth Argyle season ticket holders are being invited to relocate to the Mayflower Grandstand, which reopens next month.

After an £8 million-plus, 18-month renovation, it will be open to 50 per cent of its 5,403-seat capacity for the League Two game against Morecambe on Saturday, December 14.

That match will mark the first return to Home Park of former Pilgrims’ boss Derek Adams, who was appointed as Morecambe manager last week.

Letters from Argyle chief executive Andrew Parkinson were sent out on Tuesday to supporters who held a 2017/18 season ticket.

They are being offered the chance to relocate to the grandstand to a seat as close as possible to the one that they vacated.

Supporters are unlikely to be given an identical seat number to the one that they previously held.

That is because the seat numbering runs the opposite way to before, so seat one is at the Devonport End of the grandstand, for example.

Argyle have a team of staff members ready to assist supporters with their relocation inquiries.

The club has also set up a dedicated email address grandstand@pafc.co.uk to help make the process as smooth as possible.

Supporters can also call 01752 907700 or visit the Home Park Ticket Office to discuss their seat migration.

The grandstand will be open to 75 per cent of its capacity – 4,050 seats – for the league game against Stevenage on Sunday, December 29.

It will then be fully open, taking the Home Park capacity up to 18,200, when Swindon Town are Argyle’s opponents on New Year’s Day.
