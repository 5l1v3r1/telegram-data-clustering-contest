Hong Kong leader says protesters 'paralysing' the city are selfish

12 Nov 2019, 03:01

HONG KONG, Nov 12 — Hong Kong's embattled leader Carrie Lam today said protesters who are trying to “paralyse” the city were extremely selfish and hoped all universities and schools would urge students not to participate in violence.

Lam was speaking a day after police shot a protester and a man was set on fire in some of the most dramatic scenes to grip the city during the more than five months of civil unrest.

Yesterday, Lam said that the violence roiling the former British colony exceeded protesters' demands for democracy and demonstrators are now the people's enemy. — Reuters
