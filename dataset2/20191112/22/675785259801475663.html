Coronation Street 'highly likely' to begin airing seven episodes a week, writer says

Corrie writer Ellen Taylor has said that the cast and crew are already struggling with filming six episodes a week

12 Nov 2019, 22:48 by Rose Hill, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/rose-hill/

A writer on Coronation Street has suggested that it is highly likely that the ITV soap will follow in rival Emmerdale's footsteps by airing seven episodes a week.

Currently, the Corrie is scheduled to air two episodes on Mondays, Wednesdays and Fridays, upped from the original five a week.

And it looks like the number of episodes could increase even more, which the writer has said it is already struggling with.

Ellen Taylor, who wrote Sinead Tinker's harrowing cancer storyline, has said that the six episodes a week is already putting a strain on the writing team, directors and producers.

She is also worried that it will get even more difficult for the crew and has become convinced that the soap's episodes will soon be upped to seven a week.

The writer told The Sun : "Increased volume isn’t always a good thing. It’s a lot more work for everyone. It’s great for more Corrie, but is it best in the long run? I don’t know.

"We’re never going to go back down. Emmerdale are already at seven episodes so you know it’s going to happen at some point."

Pointing out that there are a lot of challenges in terms of production, including needing more cast and a bigger set, she said that it also threw up challenges for her.

She said: "When you have six episodes, particularly with our transmission pattern, it’s like you have to ramp up the story very quickly.

"When it’s six episodes a week you have to ramp everything up more for the next Monday and I think it makes the story telling more heightened - you’ve got to have more dramatic moments because you’ve got to keep hooking the viewers in and I’m not sure that’s always a good thing."

However she conceded that that was the nature of TV today and that everything has become "a bit more high octane".

She added: "I still think Corrie maintains a balance very well but I think because of those six episodes a week those high octane storylines and hooks to keep the viewers there have become a more dominant feature of the process."

*Coronation Street airs Mondays, Wednesdays and Fridays at 7.30pm and 8.30pm on ITV
