Raheem Sterling described as "big leader" by England team-mate Callum Hudson-Odoi

Sterling won't play any part in England's game against Montenegro on Thursday after being dropped following an altercation with Liverpool defender Joe Gomez

12 Nov 2019, 22:30 by Andy Dunn, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/andy-dunn/

Callum Hudson-Odoi has described Raheem Sterling as a ‘big leader’ in the England squad.

And the Chelsea star believes Sterling is a role model with both club and country.

Hudson-Odoi stuck to the party line, insisting the squad had talked about the Sterling-Gomez situation and were happy moving on to the game against Montenegro on Thursday night.

But he was keen to emphasise just how important Sterling is to the young players in this England squad.

Hudson-Odoi said: “Raheem is like an idol to me … what he’s doing for club and country is an amazing thing which I will try to replicate myself.

“It is an amazing feeling to have someone like him doing what he’s doing and being around you every time. I want to keep learning off what he does.

“He’s very encouraging, very positive about everything he does. He’s a big leader in the team.

“When someone like that has a big say on racism, it is a big feeling for all of us. He is sticking up for everyone who is involved in the game. It is a great feeling to know he’s going to do that.”

Hudson-Odoi was less forthcoming when asked about Gareth Southgate’s decision to drop Sterling for his altercation with Joe Gomez.

“What’s happened, happened,” said Hudson-Odoi. “It is a situation where we have dealt with it as a team. We have moved on.

“We have had a little talk with each other and made sure everything is OK.

“We just want to move on as a team and focus on the game.”

England require only a point from their two games against Montenegro and Kosovo to secure qualification for next summer's Euro 2020.
