Deepak Chahar on a roll, claims another hat-trick

12 Nov 2019, 11:45 by TIMESOFINDIA.COM

Highlights

  • Playing for Rajasthan, Chahar claimed a hat-trick against Vidarbha in the Syed Mushtaq Ali Trophy match
  • On Sunday, Chahar became the first Indian men cricketer to take a hat-trick in the T20Is
  • The 27-year-old Chahar also registered a world record haul of six for seven in the third and last T20I against Bangladesh

NEW DELHI: Having become the first Indian men cricketer to take a hat-trick in the T20Is, India pacer Deepak Chahar replicated his effort in just three days time. Playing for Rajasthan, Chahar claimed a hat-trick against Vidarbha in the Syed Mushtaq Ali Trophy match on Tuesday.

Bowling first after losing the toss, the 27-year-old Chahar returned impressive figures of 4/18 in 3 overs to help Rajasthan restrict Vidarbha to 99/9 in 13 overs.
