Sai Praneeth overtakes Kidambi Srikanth; Satwik-Chirag climb to 7th in BWF Rankings

12 Nov 2019, 11:05 by TIMESOFINDIA.COM

Highlights

  • Praneeth, who became the first Indian male shuttler in 36 years to win a medal at the World Championships, has jumped to 10th place
  • Praneeth's compatriot Kidambi, however, has slipped to 13th in the men's singles rankings
  • The pair of Satwiksairaj Rankireddy and Chirag Shetty has also jumped to No. 7 in the latest BWF rankings

NEW DELHI: Indian shuttler B Sai Praneeth on Tuesday leapfrogged badminton star Kidambi Srikanth to enter the top 10 in the latest BWF Rankings.

Becoming the first Indian male shuttler in 36 years to win a medal at the World Championships in Switzerland in August earlier this year, Praneeth has now reached a career-best ranking of 10th.

Praneeth's compatriot Kidambi, however, has slipped to 13th in the men's singles rankings.

Praneeth also won a silver medal at the Swiss Open in March earlier this year. The 26-year-old caused a major upset by beating Rio Olympics gold medallist and London Olympics bronze medallist Chen Long in the semis of the Swiss Open before losing 21-19, 18-21, 12-21 to China's Shi Yuqi in the summit clash.

The Andhra lad was honoured with the Arjuna Award in 2019.

Meanwhile, in the men's doubles rankings, the pair of Satwiksairaj Rankireddy and Chirag Shetty has also jumped to No. 7 in the latest BWF rankings.

Satwik and Chirag had won their maiden Super 500 title at the Thailand Open in August. The star Indian pair also reached the finals at the French Open Super 750.
