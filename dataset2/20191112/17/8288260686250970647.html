Confirmed: Manchester United dealt injury blow ahead of Aston Villa clash

Aston Villa latest news | Ole Gunnar Solskjaer's side welcome the Villa Park outfit at the beginning of December in the Premier League after a clash with Newcastle United

12 Nov 2019, 17:41 by Richard Cusack

Manchester United midfielder Scott McTominay has pulled out of the Scotland squad for European Championships qualifiers against Cyprus and Kazakhstan.

McTominay had to be carried off the Old Trafford pitch in Sunday's 3-1 defeat of Brighton and Hove Albion with an ankle injury, the international team-mate of Aston Villa star John McGinn withdrawing from his national team's camp as a result.

The Scots will play Cyprus in Nicosia on Saturday night before hosting Kazakhstan at Hampden Park the following Tuesday, with McGinn called up for those matches.

But McTominay's withdrawal hands Villa a major boost ahead of the Premier League meeting between the pair in Manchester at the beginning of December.

The 22-year-old has started every Premier League game this season.

Villa host Newcastle United the week before in a crucial relegation battle before travelling to Ole Gunnar Solskjaer's side, who are in form having won four of their last six matches in all competitions.

McTominay limped out of the Europa League defeat of Partizan Belgrade the match before Brighton, damaging his ankle both times.

Arsenal defender Kieran Tierney, AFC Bournemouth's Ryan Fraser and Liverpool full-back Andy Robertson joined McTominay in leaving the Scotland squad.

Scotland assistant head coach Alex Dyer refuted suggestions players had decided to leave the Scotland squad for any other reason.

"They are injured you can't do nothing about that," he said.
