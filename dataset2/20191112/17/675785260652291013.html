Sergio Romero let three Man Utd stars into an "Anthony Martial secret"

The Manchester United goalkeeper played alongside Anthony Martial at Monaco before signing for the Red Devils and informing the club of the youngster's quality

12 Nov 2019, 17:58 by Jake Polden, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/jake-polden/

Sergio Romero claims he let three Manchester United stars into the "secret" of Anthony Martial in 2015 - before the Red Devils signed him.

Back in 2014 Romero was on loan at Martial's former club Monaco from Sampdoria and after signing for United a year later, spoke to David De Gea, Juan Mata and Ander Herrera.

He told the Spanish trio that Monaco had a youngster in their academy named Martial and that one day "you'll all be talking about him."

Martial followed Romero to United in 2015, with Louis van Gaal splashing out £36million rising to £58m for the Frenchman - the highest fee paid for a teenager at that point.

Reminiscing on his first memories of Martial, Romero told Utd Unscripted: "Back in 2015, not long after I joined United, I was chatting with Ander Herrera, David De Gea and Juan Mata.

"I let them in on a secret. 'There's a kid at Monaco. Anthony Martial. For sure, in a short while, you'll all be talking about him'.

"I'd known about Anthony for two years. In the 2013/14 season, I went to AS Monaco on loan for a year from Sampdoria in Italy.

"Anthony was still very young, just a kid - I think he was 17 years old at that time - but you could already see that he was that he was a special player with lots of class and lots of talent. The kid was a natural.

"You'd just be amazed at the things he would do in the training sessions."

Martial is enjoying a positive run of form at Manchester United, but has fallen down the pecking order with the France national team.

France boss Didier Deschamps recently spoke out about future opportunities to play the forward. He said: "He was part of the French team on a regular basis, but recently he was often injured.

"He came back not so long ago and it's going well in his role as a central striker with Manchester United. For a long time he's been part of the players likely to be called up."
