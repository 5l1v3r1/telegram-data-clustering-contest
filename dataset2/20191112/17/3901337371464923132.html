Pep Linked With Shock Bayern Return

12 Nov 2019, 17:45 by David Kappel

With Manchester City dropping nine points behind Liverpool and reports suggesting that Pep Guardiola might leave the club at the end of this season, the Spaniard has now been linked with a shock return to Bayern Munich.

The German giants are currently without a full-time manager after Niko Kovac departed the Allianz Arena nine days ago. Hans-Joachim Flick has since taken over in the interim and guided the Bavarians to two comfortable wins, over Olympiacos and Borussia Dortmund.

Flick, a former assistant coach to Germany boss Joachim Low, will likely lead Bayern until the end of the season, when the club's bosses will present a new coach, unless the incumbent can convince all doubters otherwise with silverware.

According to The Athletic, though, the Bundesliga champions' dream candidate to lead the club into a new era of success, in particular in the UEFA Champions League, would be Tottenham Hotspur coach Mauricio Pochettino.

Bayern "would do anything to get him" if he and Spurs were to go their separate ways, but Manchester United and Real Madrid could also be after his services.

However, the same report also suggested that there is a chance that Bayern will lure Guardiola back to Germany.

It is the feeling in some circles that this season is his last with City, and he still has a strong relationship with Bavarian club's leadership and also retains a property in Munich.
