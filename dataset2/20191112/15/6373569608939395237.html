Dopey criminal jailed after falling asleep in Plymouth court

Drug addict Thomas Fagan started to doze off in the dock during his hearing

12 Nov 2019, 15:40 by Stuart Abel

A drug addict thief has been jailed after he started to doze off in the dock during a court hearing.

Prolific thief Thomas Fagan, aged 32, appeared to be falling asleep as he sat before a judge.

He was facing jail in any case for failing to tackle his heroin addiction despite help from counsellors and probation officers.

The probation service dragged him to Plymouth Crown Court because he has failed to comply with a community order for a string of offences – including threatening to stab a security guard.

Judge Paul Darlow remanded Fagan into custody until Monday so he is wide awake during sentencing.

He told him: “I won’t have you falling asleep through proceedings.”

Fagan, who started using heroin at the age of 14, sat in the dock with his head drooping.

A male supporter, thought to be his father, called out his name from the public gallery in an apparent attempt to revive him.

Fagan, of no fixed address, denied he had been asleep as he was taken away in handcuffs.

Judge Darlow had warned Fagan that he would adjourn the hearing if he could not pay attention.

Ali Rafati, Fagan’s barrister, had told the court that his client had been “passing in and out of consciousness” before the hearing.

Holly Rust, for the probation service, told the hearing that Fagan had shown a “complete disregard” of his community order.

She added: “He has no desire to address his substance misuse. It is probation’s recommendation that the order be revoked and he be resentenced.”

Fagan threatened to stab a security guard with scissors as he stole a Surprise Doll from Smyths in September last year. He was under the influence of Valium at the time of the offence in Friary Mill.
