Sporting and construction giant Bill Barr OBE dies, aged 80

The head of Barr Construction was famous for his sporting passions that included Ayr United and the Ayr Scottish Eagles.

12 Nov 2019, 15:22 by Mike Wilson, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/mike-wilson/

A giant of the sporting and business world has died, leaving an indelible mark on Ayrshire.

Bill Barr OBE, who was 80, passed away on Friday.

The former Ayr United chairman, who bankrolled the club’s only national cup final, was hailed for leading his beloved Honest Men on a “rollercoaster ride”.

His Ayr Scottish Eagles completed an ice hockey Grand Slam in only their second season in 1997-98.

Bill transformed his family’s small building firm into one of the UK’s largest privately-owned construction companies.

During his 34 years at the helm, the Barr organisation built everything from motorways to whisky distilleries, and schools to water treatment works.

He oversaw the construction of more than 100 retail supermarkets and 35 rugby and football stadiums throughout Britain, including Southampton’s 36,000-seater St Mary’s.

Current Ayr United director, Lewis Grant, said: “He took the town of Ayr – and the whole county for that matter – on the greatest rollercoaster ride.”
