Instagram copies TikTok with new 'Reels' feature - here's how it works

Instagram's new feature, called Reels, lets you create 15-second video clips set to audio or music, and share them with your followers via your Story

12 Nov 2019, 15:43 by Shivali Best, https://www.facebook.com/shivalibestjournalist/

They’re two of the biggest rivals in the social media industry, and now it seems that Instagram has taken a leaf out of TikTok’s book with the launch of its latest feature.

The feature, called Reels, lets users create 15-second video clips set to music, and share them on their Stories.

According to TechCrunch , who first reported the new feature, users can access a huge catalogue of music to soundtrack their Reels, or borrow audio from another video.

Surprisingly, Instagram has been fairly open about the fact that it copied the feature from TikTok.

Speaking to TechCrunch , Robby Stein, Instagram’s director of product management, said: “I think Musically before TikTok, and TikTok deserve a ton of credit for popularising this format.

 

“No two products are exactly the same, and at the end of the day sharing video with music is a pretty universal idea we think everyone might be interested in using. The focus has been on how to make this a unique format for us.”

Reels can be found in the Instagram Stories shutter modes tray, alongside the Boomerang and Super-Zoom options.

Users can choose whether to record a video with no audio, to borrow audio from another audio, or to add a popular song to their recording.

And unlike standard videos on Stories, users can record or upload multiple video clips to fill out their Reel.

Initially, the feature is only available for Instagram users in Brazil, where it’s called Cenas.

It remains unclear when, or if, it will launch for users around the world. Mirror Online has contacted Instagram for comment.
