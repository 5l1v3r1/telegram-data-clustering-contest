Police descend on Fremont High School in South LA after multiple fights, 'volatile' unrest on campus

12 Nov 2019, 23:30 by ABC7.com staff

SOUTH LOS ANGELES (KABC) -- A large police presence descended on a high school in South Los Angeles Tuesday morning as police tried to disperse crowding students following several reports of "volatile" unrest on campus.

Multiple fights broke out on the Fremont High School campus on San Pedro Street around 11 a.m., with rocks and bottles being thrown, according the Los Angeles Police Department 77th Division. Officers who responded to the school put out a call for help and formed a scrimmage line outside the campus.

According to Los Angeles Unified School District school police, the disturbance began after students walked off campus during a demonstration and reportedly started throwing rocks and bottles at officers. More than a dozen police units were seen parked outside the school.

It's unclear what prompted the unrest. No injuries or arrests have been reported.
