Seth Meyers: 'Voters don’t want a self-appointed billionaire savior'

12 Nov 2019, 16:59 by Adrian Horton

Late-night hosts responded to Michael Bloomberg’s potential presidential run and Trump’s Maga rap challenge

Seth Meyers: ‘No appetite for another billionaire president’

“Now if you’re a Democratic primary voter, I know what you’re thinking,” said Seth Meyers on Monday’s Late Night. “You’ve been watching the rampant corruption of the Trump presidency, the catastrophic failure of the Trump administration, and the destructive influence the wealthy have had on politics and you’re saying to yourself: you know what can fix this? A different rich guy.”

That self-appointed rich guy of the moment would be former New York City mayor Michael Bloomberg, who has filed paperwork to enter the Democratic primary field, “because if we’ve learned one thing from the last decade of politics – from the financial crisis to the Trump presidency – it’s that rich people, with zero exceptions, know what they’re doing,” joked Meyers.
