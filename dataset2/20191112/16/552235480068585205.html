Dumfries and Galloway Council officials consult on ideas to bridge £12 million funding gap

Community conversations are being held on the ideas, which include council tax increases and hiking marina fees

12 Nov 2019, 16:03 by Stephen Temlett, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/stephen-temlett/

Further council tax increases and cutting the number of secondary school subjects offered to pupils are two methods the local authority may use to bridge a £12million funding gap.

The council has already agreed savings of just over £3m for next year but needs to find much more.

And it has drawn up a list of options to cut costs which also includes reducing the roads maintenance budget and a 30 per cent reduction in events funding.

The council has emphasised that no decision has been made but staff will hold a series of “community conversations” over the next month to get the public’s feedback.

 

The report states: “We continue to face huge challenges with more budget cuts a reality over the next three years.

“Forecasts of our budget show the council can expect to have £49m less to spend on services and resources. This is nearly 15 per cent of our current budget over that period and is in addition to almost £106m that has been cut since 2010.

“The council’s agreed budget timetable required council officers to prepare a summary of options to close the council’s funding gap for 2020/21.

“No decisions on these options have been taken. All these options will be considered by councillors over the next few
months.

 

“Feedback from citizens, customers, staff and partners will be reported to councillors at a full council meeting on December 17 to inform their decisions when setting their budget for 2020/21 in February 2020.”

As well as cuts, which also include the reconfiguration of social work staffing, the budget options also look at ways the council could increase its income.

A three per cent council tax rise for the next two years would eventually generate an extra £6.8m for the council coffers.

Other methods suggested include increasing fees for Stranraer and Kirkcudbright marinas, removal of free access to leisure facilities for talented athletes and “move towards full cost recovery” for burial services.

For a list of the dates and times for community conversations visit the council’s website at www.dumgal.gov.uk/budget .
