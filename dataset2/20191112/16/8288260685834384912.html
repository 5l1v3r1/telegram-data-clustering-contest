'Dictated' - Aston Villa forgotten man sends Wolves message

Aston Villa latest news | Dean Smith's side lost 2-1 to their West Midlands rivals on Sunday thanks to goals from Ruben Neves and Raul Jimenez with Trezeguet netting for the visitors

12 Nov 2019, 16:47 by Richard Cusack

Orjan Nyland has been discussing his Aston Villa Premier League debut.

The goalkeeper was thrust into action in Sunday's 2-1 defeat to Wolverhampton Wanderers , replacing Jed Steer who picked up a calf injury just seven minutes into his first start of the season.

Nyland , who before Sunday had only played Under-23 football this season, took Steer's place on the substitute's bench with the former Norwich City man drafted in to replace injured first-choice 'keeper Tom Heaton.

The Norwegian international had only played 23 times before for Villa, signed by Steve Bruce in August last year.

He missed most of last season through a ruptured Achilles injury.

Although he wasn't able to help his side avoid a third-straight League defeat , Nyland will look to kick on and become a regular feature in Dean Smith's matchday squad going forward.

“It was zero to 100 from my point of view," he told the club's website.

"You never want to replace an injured teammate, but for me it was about doing the thing that I’ve been working for.

 

“I know both sides of the game.

"I know how it feels to be the number one and I know how it feels to prepare the other guy every day.

“You just have to work every day and be ready.

"I got my opportunity and I just had to try and grab it.”

Villa boss Smith lambasted his players for a poor first-half at Molineux in which his side found themselves 1-0 down.

The second 45 minutes wasn't much better, with Villa slipping to 17th place and just three points clear of the relegation zone after 12 League matches.

"We all agreed at half-time that we had to go out and be better, and we did play better in the second half," Nyland added.

“That’s something we can take away for the next couple of games.

 

"But for the rest of the season, we need to start brightly and not give the opposition an advantage.

“We dictated the second half, created chances and I felt like we could have had the equaliser, but unfortunately it was too late by the time we scored.”
