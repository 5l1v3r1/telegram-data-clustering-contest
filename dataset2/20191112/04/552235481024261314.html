Little Scots sisters face legal bid to stop violent dad who beat them from winning access case

The 11 and eight-year-old siblings have written to Scotland's justice minister pleading for help after the brute was granted legal aid to begin his fight.

12 Nov 2019, 04:30 by Vivienne Aitken, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/vivienne-aitken/

Two young girls are facing a court fight to prevent the dad who assaulted them being allowed to see them again.

One of the girls, Lucy (not her real name), 11, said: “They will have to physically drag me from my house. I never want to see him again.”

And her little sister, eight, who we refer to as Joanne, said: “I am frightened to be with my dad in case he hurts us again.”

The girls were shocked when, just a month after he was found guilty of assaulting them, their dad began legal proceedings to be allowed access again.

 

Last month, he was given legal aid to begin his fight.

The girls have sent a letter to Justice Minister Humza Yousaf, in which Lucy wrote: “People say that’s it's better to have two parents involved in your life, but our life was not better, it was awful.”

Their mum Susan (not her real name) and dad split more than seven years ago.

The girls refused to see him on one occasion because they were going to a cheerleader rehearsal.

The next time they saw him, Lucy said their dad “shook us by the neck and said, ‘Make sure that doesn’t happen again’”.

 

She described how, on a trip to a caravan, he punched her in the stomach and grabbed Joanne by the throat.

The girls told a teacher and their dad was given a community payback order in court.

Labour’s justice spokesman James Kelly said “there are questions” to answer in the case.

A Scottish Government spokesman said: “While we cannot comment on individual cases, we want to ensure that all victims and children are protected.”
