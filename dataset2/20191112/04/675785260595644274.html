Liverpool 'in advanced talks' with Bournemouth over transfer of Arsenal target Ryan Fraser

Fraser was the Premier League's second highest assist king last season and could be involved in a January deal involving Liverpool's on-loan ace Harry Wilson

12 Nov 2019, 04:40 by Gareth Bicknell

Liverpool are reportedly in advanced talks to sign Bournemouth winger Ryan Fraser in the January transfer window after he was linked with Arsenal over the summer.

Fraser was on Arsenal's radar in the summer following an impressive season with Bournemouth as he notched 14 Premier League assists.

The Scotland international's haul was the second highest tally in the league behind then Chelsea player Eden Hazard.

He has not repeated that form this season, amid speculation that relations have broken down between the 25-year-old and manager Eddie Howe.

Bournemouth are hoping to cash in on the £27m-rated flyer when the transfer window reopens rather than lose him for nothing when his contract expires next summer.

And Liverpool are leading the race to land him, with on-loan Reds winger Harry Wilson possibly set to sign permanently for Bournemouth as part of the deal.

That's according to TalkSport , who claim the club is in advanced talks with Bournemouth.

TalkSport correspondent Alex Crook, who said: "My understanding is Jurgen Klopp is lining up a surprise move for Bournemouth’s Ryan Fraser.

“You can understand why Bournemouth have tried to hold on to him but I think now a move in January would suit all parties.

“He would be a great signing for Liverpool. He is not going to get in that front three ahead of Mohamed Salah, Roberto Firmino and Sadio Mane, but what an option off the bench."

With his contract running down, Liverpool may be able to land Wilson for a cut price £10m.

It is anticipated the Cherries could ask Liverpool to sweeten the deal by offering Wales winger Wilson as part of the exchange.

The 22-year-old has scored four goals in 11 appearances on loan at Bournemouth this season, having been Derby County's top scorer during his loan spell with the Championship club last term.
