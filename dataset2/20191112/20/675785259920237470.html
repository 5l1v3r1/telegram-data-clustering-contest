General election: Coronation Street star eyes being an MP one day as she comes home - to meet Corbyn

Nicola Thorp plays Nicola Rubinstein in the ITV soap and is a passionate Labour supporter

12 Nov 2019, 20:39 by Dan Bloom, https://www.facebook.com/mirrordanbloom

Coronation Street's Nicola Thorp opened the door to becoming a Labour MP as she met Jeremy Corbyn in her hometown.

The 31-year-old campaigner returned to Blackpool - a stone's throw from the rock factory her grandfather Bill founded in 1969.

The TV star, who plays Nicola Rubinstein in the ITV soap, won't rule out standing herself one day - as long as it's in the town.

"It’s not a never, of course! I’m positive. Never say never," she said.

"I think it’s really important that younger people, particularly younger women, get involved with politics."

The actor shot to prominence in 2016 when she was sacked from a temp job for refusing to wear high heels - sparking outcry.

She joined Corrie in 2017 and yon Tuesday she joined Labour's leader on the campaign trail.

In a joint interview with the Mirror, Mr Corbyn declared he will stay as Prime Minister for five full years if he wins the election.

Mr Corbyn - who will be 75 by 2024 - said he will "absolutely" stay the full length of a Parliament if he wins adding: "I’m fit and healthy and very happy."

A YouGov poll put the Tories 14 points ahead of Labour.

But Mr Corbyn said: "I never comment on opinion polls, I only comment on the prospects of success, and they are very very good."

 

As Ms Thorp jumped on Labour's battlebus, Mr Corbyn backed her No1 plea to revive seaside towns - slapping new regulations on cramped, unfit 'houses of multiple occupancy'.

She said: "B&Bs are owned by private landlords, who often live offshore and aren’t aware of the conditions their tenants are living in.

"I visited one weeks ago and we weren’t even allowed to walk all the way inside it because it wasn’t deemed safe enough."

Mr Corbyn - whose party is carrying out a 'policy review' of seaside towns - also suggested Victorian resorts could be revived with new arts projects and funds from offshore wind farms.

But as the pair chatted, Mr Corbyn admitted he's more of an EastEnders fan.

"Sorry, I’ve got to be honest about it," he said. "I watch EastEnders and I find it so bizarre I actually enjoy it.

"There cannot be such a crime-ridden place in the country that hasn’t had a collective ASBO yet."

Mr Corbyn would not confirm if Labour will keep its 2017 vow to end free movement for EU citizens after Brexit .

 

He said "ask me after Saturday" - when Labour's factions agree the manifesto.

But signalling a far softer approach than the Tories, he said: "We depend on a lot of European workers in this country.

"And a lot of British people depend on being able to work and travel in Europe. I don’t want to take those opportunities away from either group."

 

Mr Corbyn also claimed Ian Byrne - who shared expletive-filled online posts - had "changed".

Labour's ruling NEC kept the West Derby candidate despite outcry from some members about the posts, which called bra tycoon Michelle Mone a "c***" and Tory Esther McVey a "b******".

Mr Corbyn said the remarks were "disgusting and disgraceful and totally unacceptable" - but the decision was for the NEC.

He told the Mirror: "My personal view is that he’s explained what went on, explained in the context and the attitude he had at that time in his life, and I hope and believe that he has very much changed his personality and his approach.

"And I want people to be educated to get away from that kind of disgusting misogynistic language that he’s put forward."

 

Ms Thorp became a Labour backer after failing to vote in 2010 because of "anger and confusion and misrepresentation".

Now she lives on a houseboat so doesn't have a legal postal address.

Instead she has to cast her vote in her hometown, where her dad Geoff, 61, mum Jan, 61, and brother Dave, 29, run a factory making Blackpool rock.

 

She spoke to the Mirror two weeks after opening up about life with Borderline Personality Disorder in a bid to help others.

She told us she was wrongly diagnosed with bipolar disorder and "heavily medicated" in 2012 before finally being diagnosed last year.

"I thought I was going crazy," she said. "It was through that diagnosis and being linked with other people with similar diagnoses that I felt normal again and felt like I was in control of it again."
