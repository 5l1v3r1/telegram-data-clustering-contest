Auction Preview: European Collections at Bonhams, London, November 20

12 Nov 2019 by BLOUIN ARTINFO

What: European Collections

Where: Bonhams, 101 New Bond Street, London, W1S 1SR

When: November 20, 10:00 am GMT

Public Viewing: November 17, 2019 | 11:00 - 15:00 GMT, November 18, 2019 | 09:00 - 16:30 GMT, November 19, 2019 | 09:00 - 16:30 GMT, November 20, 2019 | 09:00 - 10:00 GMT

Top Lots at the Sale:

— “A pair of large French 19th century gilt bronze figurative candelabra”, after a model by Francois-Thimothee Matilin. Each with a pair of 18 light two-tier candelabra with cast acanthus scrolled candle arms and a central urn-shaped sconce. Measurements: 54cm wide, 55cm deep, 134cm high (21in wide, 21 ½ in deep, 52 ½ in high) (2). Featured in lot 30. Estimate: £ 40,000 - 60,000 (51,000 - 77,000 USD)

— “An impressive complete walnut, carved giltwood and gilt bronze mounted library”, North Italian, 19th century. Comprising of three sides with alphabetized open bookshelves above paneled doors, together with three paneled doors surmounted by oval panels featuring medallions with portrait busts of High Renaissance masters: Leonardo da Vinci, Michelangelo, and Raphael. Measurements: 260cm high (102in) the two sides: 500 cm long (196 ½ in), and the central section: 600 cm long (236in). Featured in lot 40. Estimate: £ 40,000 - 60,000 (51,000 - 77,000 USD)

— “An Important German early 19th century mahogany and gilt bronze mounted console table”, Attributed to Johannes Klinkerfuss, (1770–1831) and Casimir Munch, Stuttgart. The dished white marble top above an ornately cast frieze above two bulbous front supports cast with flower heads on a flame mahogany plinth base. Measurements: 129cm wide x 51.5cm deep x 84.5cm high, (50.5in wide x 20in deep x 33in high). Featured in lot 61. Estimate: £ 40,000 - 60,000 (51,000 - 77,000 USD)

— “A large pair of French 19th century gilt and patinated bronze eight light candelabra”, in the manner of Claude Michallon (1751-1799). The figures are (likely to be) Zephyr and his wife Flora. Measurements: 56cm wide, 38cm deep, 139cm high (22in wide, 14 1/2in deep, 54 1/2in high); the pedestals: 82cm high, (33 in high) (4). Featured in lot 31. Estimate: £ 30,000 - 50,000 (35,000 - 58,000 USD)

— “A fine and possibly unique mid 18th century French quarter striking cartel clock with moonphase and perpetual calendar”, the movement and dial signed “Vander Cruse a Paris”. The case by Antoine Foullet. The waisted case is decorated with hand-painted flowers and floral groups on a red ground. Measurements: the clock 120cm (47.1 n) total height including bracket 160cm (63in) Featured in lot 44. Estimate: £ 25,000 - 35,000 (29,000 - 40,000 USD)

— “A unique first quarter of the 19th century Austrian ormolu, ebony and alabaster-mounted burr thuya wood automata organ clock”, J. Ad. Hoyer, Wien. The architectural case is set with ormolu mounts and balustrades, the niches supported on alabaster columns, Measurements: height 53cm x width 55cm x depth 32cm (height 20.9in x width 21.7in x depth 12.6in). Featured in lot 38. Estimate: £ 20,000 - 30,000 (23,000 - 35,000 USD)

— “An Important South German late 15th Century carved limewood figure group of The Deposition”, Circle of Erasmus Grasser (circa 1450-circa 1515). With five figures mourning and cradling Christ's body into the tomb, on a later velvet stand, 64cm wide x 16cm deep x 40cm high (25in wide x 6in deep x 15 ½in high). Featured in lot 77. Estimate: £ 20,000 - 30,000 (23,000 - 35,000 USD)
