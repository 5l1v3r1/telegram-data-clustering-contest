Gate-crashing can lead to a full-time job. Ask Suresh

12 Nov 2019, 00:30 by Pragna L Krupa

He tried to sneak in to watch his favourite hero Prabhas on screen but found a real hero in the police station

Suresh Tarapawar, a 23-year-old orphan was caught sneaking into a cinema hall without a ticket, to watch his favourite hero Prabhas’s movie Saaho. But while he was mooning over a cinema hero, a real-life hero was waiting to give him a new life and a second chance. And how the orphan’s story evolves is much like the story of Saaho, where the audience is kept guessing who the real hero is.

Police inspector, Deelip Kumar of Adugodi police station found Suresh at the station, frightened and expecting the worst.

On questioning Tarapawar, Kumar learnt that the boy was caught by the cinema hall management at Forum Mall and handed over to the police.

Inspector Kumar said, “The first thing I noticed in Tarapawar was his innocence and when I learnt about his past and his struggles, I felt like giving him a chance at a better life. Everyone has a turning point in their life and if he had managed to get inside without a ticket, that would have become a habit.

And each time he would have been emboldened to do a bigger crime He was genuinely regretful of his attempt to enter the cinema hall without a ticket and he did it only because he could not afford to buy one. He told me how difficult his life had been since he came to the city. I gave him food and asked him to work at the police station as housekeeping staff to which he immediately agreed.”

Tarapawar is from Gulbarga and was living in a government hostel until he turned 10. He discontinued his schooling and then started working in a restaurant there to earn a living. Despite working in the restaurant, Tarapawar said he was happy because the job assured him three meals a day and a salary, even if it was very low. He came to the city about six months ago with big dreams of getting a job and living a new life. Suresh said, “From the past six months, I have worked at eight to 10 places in the city but none of them paid me for the work I did.


Whenever I asked them for the money, they would threaten me and make me work some more before kicking me out without pay. I mostly worked at small restaurants and roadside food stalls so that I would get three meals a day. Once in a while I would get tips from customers. I used to sleep on footpaths and in empty grounds. On the day Prabhas’s movie, Saaho, was released, I went to the theatre to simply look around. When I saw the crowds that had gathered to watch the show, I couldn’t stop myself. I really wanted to watch his movie and tried to sneak in saying that I was accompanying someone who was inside. They caught me and handed me over to the manager who threatened to send me to jail and handed me over to the police. I was really scared when I came to the police station but the inspector was kind and gave me a second chance. He even got me a movie ticket to watch the movie that same evening and gave me temporary shelter.”

Tarapawar added that the Inspector gave him a new set of clothes, some food and even a room near the police station to stay. “I feel very safe working here at the police station. Everyone is very helpful and I learn new things every day. I got my first salary of Rs 7,000 recently,” he said. Inspector Dileep Kumar said that he was helping him find another job and would help him open a bank account too.


The incident came to light recently when the Deputy Commissioner of Police (South - East), Isha Pant came to the station for a visit.
