Apple Accidentally Publishes Photo Of Cut Apples On Its App

12 Nov 2019 by Mikelle Leow

Apple’s developers might have a sense of humor, but it’s likely the company doesn’t want this sort of creative juice to seep through its core to the masses.
Unfortunately, one such joke reached the eyes of the public before Apple had time to take it down. As spotted by MacRumors, the tech giant posted a stock photo of what looked like Granny Smith apples on the Apple Store app as the placeholder image for a “Mock Store.”
Hilariously, upon clicking in to learn more about this “Mock Store,” Apple users also discovered that the company had provided a map indicating it was in the middle of the Pacific Ocean.
The listed address, “Bubb Road in Cupertino, California,” exists, and is just 10 minutes away from Apple Park—but it’s certainly not an underwater concept let alone an Apple Store.
One witty user even tried ordering a 10.2-inch iPad from the “Mock Store,” and was given the option of picking up the tablet in-store.

As it turns out, there is an actual “mock store,” though it’s not at Bubb Road. 60 Minutes reported back in 2015 that Apple has a test retail location used to trial new products and marketing collaterals before they go public.
