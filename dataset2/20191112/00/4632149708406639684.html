Police forced to turn ghost-busters in Yeshwantpur

12 Nov 2019, 00:30 by Bangalore Mirror Bureau

7 youngsters arrested after spreading fear on the roads by chasing after motorists, passersby in spooky make-up and clothes

A group of seven youngsters were having a whale of a time, scaring the wits out of passersby and uploading the videos only. Their fun finally came to an end after the police cracked down on them in the wee hours of Monday. The men have been arrested for creating a nuisance.

Police feel the group was inspired by online scary prank videos and so were on scaring-the-people spree in and around Yeshwanthpur area.

The accused have been identified as Shawn Mallik, 20, Naveed, 20, Sajeel Mohammed, 21, Mohammed Aqyub, 20, Sakeeb, 20, Sayed Nabil, 20, and Usaf Ahamed. All are residents of RT Nagar and Nagawara. The group have their own YouTube channel where they upload such prank videos for entertainment. They used to pull pranks on the public and upload them on the Youtube and other websites. Based on a complaint filed by the public, the police swung into action and took the seven into custody.

Police said that the group had dressed themselves as ghosts and went around pulling pranks in Sharifnagar in Yeshwantpur.

According to the police, the youths wore white clothes, a wig and red paint as fake blood and used to target motorists and pedestrians and used to take videos of the pranks. The police who were on rounds during the night, received a complaint from the public about the gang who were scaring people and recording it. The group had earlier recorded such videos during the night in the same area.

A senior officer said that all of them are students of different colleges and are pursuing courses like Engineering, BBM and BSc. Yeshwantpur police have registered a case and are investigating.
