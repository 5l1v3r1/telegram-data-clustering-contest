The Elizabeth Warren Shorts: For-Profit Education And For-Profit Prisons

12 Nov 2019 by Alap Shah

Stock prices are already being affected by the movement of Senator Warren’s presidential candidacy in the betting markets and national polls. While the S&P 500 has been making new all-time highs recently, as President Trump likes to remind us on Twitter, investors are exiting the two sectors that would be very negatively affected by a President Warren: for-profit education and for-profit prisons. We can see this very clearly in the relative returns of for-profit education and for-profit prison stocks against the S&P 500 since mid-late August, when Senator Warren took the lead in the betting markets.Senator Warren broke out of the pack on betting site PredictIt in mid-August. 

Recent Iowa polling puts her at number one in that state, and a solid number two, behind former vice-president Joe Biden, across national polls, according to agglomerated poll data on RealClear Politics. 

Using Sentieo’s Alexa web traffic data integration, we see Senator Warren’s campaign site being consistently the top site in terms of page views versus the other two top candidates’ sites (based on the betting leaders). We are using a 30-day moving average for smoothing. 

We see a similar dynamic in Twitter mentions. Mentions of @ewarren, the Senator’s Twitter handle, have been leading for most of the last six months. Again, we are using a 30-day moving average for smoothing. 

Senator Warren’s platform is very comprehensive, with about 60 issues discussed in great detail. 

We reviewed two of these, “End Private Prisons” (originally published on June 21, 2019) and “Affordable Higher Education for All” (originally published on April 22, 2019)

Senator Warren’s plan for private prisons is clear across the board, from federal agencies down to the states.

“ Ban private prisons and detention facilities. There should be no place in America for profiting off putting more people behind bars or in detention. That’s why I will shut down the use of federal private detention facilities by ending all contracts that the Bureau of Prisons, ICE, and the U.S. Marshals Service have with private detention providers. And I will extend these bans to states and localities by conditioning their receipt of federal public safety funding on their use of public facilities.”

“After an appropriate transition period, ban for-profit colleges from receiving any federal dollars (including military benefits and federal student loans), so they can no longer use taxpayer dollars to enrich themselves while targeting lower-income students, service members, and students of color and leaving them saddled with debt.”
