The Trade Desk Names Former Amazon Exec Blake Grayson CFO

12 Nov 2019 by Howard Gensler

The Trade Desk (NASDAQ:TTD) today announced that Blake Grayson is joining the ad-tech company as its new CFO.Grayson will replace Paul Ross, who’s retiring after 5 years as CFO.

Grayson comes to The Trade Desk after serving as Vice President, Finance, for the International Consumer business of Amazon.

“Blake has spent the last decade scaling key Amazon businesses into multibillion dollar behemoths, including AWS, and brings the global financial experience required to help drive The Trade Desk forward at this stage in our growth journey,” said Trade Desk CEO Jeff Green in a statement. “I’m thrilled to welcome Blake to the team and I know that he’s inheriting an outstanding team.”

In his own statement, Grayson said, “Throughout the process of getting to know Jeff, members of the board and the leadership team, I have been impressed with the opportunities in front of The Trade Desk. I’m excited to apply my expertise to help scale existing businesses as well as invest in new ones that will continue to drive profitable long-term growth.”

Green also praised Ross for overseeing The Trade Desk’s hugely successful IPO in September 2016 and driving revenue growth and profitability.

The Trade Desk IPO raised $84 million, selling 4.667 million shares at $18 per share. The next day the stock climbed 67% to close at $30.10. After reaching a high in August over $289 per share, the stock has been in the mid-$220s this week.
