Find Out Just How Much CO2 Your Website Is Emitting

12 Nov 2019 by Mikelle Leow

You might be cutting back on plastic by using less plastic straws and carrying your own shopping bag, but there’s one factor you might not have taken into account. The internet requires energy, too, and given consumers’ reliance on their digital devices, many websites might not be so kind to the environment.
If you’re considering designing your website for the good of the earth, there’s a cool tool to let you know how much carbon dioxide your site is emitting. The Website Carbon Calculator breaks your site down into compelling visuals and bite-sized data about how sustainable your website is.
To use the calculator, simply drop your URL into the given field and you’re good to go. The tool will then help you humorously visualize your site’s CO2 production by measuring it in terms of a sumo wrestler’s weight, as well as the number of bubbles or cups of tea that amount of CO2 can produce.
Great news: it turns out that tech giants like Google and Facebook are pretty eco-efficient, and have been discovered to be running on sustainable energy.
Google, the world’s most-visited site, “is cleaner than 86-percent of websites tested,” and only produces 0.26g (0.09lb) of carbon dioxide each time a visitor enters its site. Each year, Google emits “the same weight as 0.21 sumo wrestlers and as much CO2 as boiling water for 4,272 cups of tea.”
Facebook, on the other hand, is apparently cleaner than 74-percent of websites culled between 2017 and 2018, and emits “the same weight as 0.51 sumo wrestlers and as much CO2 as boiling water for 10,395 cups of tea.”
