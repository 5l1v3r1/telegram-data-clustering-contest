Bolivia’s Morales resigns after losing security forces’ support

12 Nov 2019, 00:30 by Agencies

LA PAZBolivian President Evo Morales(pictured) has resigned after three weeks of sometimes violent protests over his disputed re-election, as his allies denounced an alleged coup.

“I resign my post as president,” the leftist Morales said Sunday in a televised address. The streets of La Paz immediately exploded in celebration, but violence and vandalism later erupted overnight.


In the confusion, a group of 20 lawmakers and government officials took refuge at the Mexican ambassador's residence, and Mexico announced it was offering asylum to Morales as well.

Armed Forces chief Williams Kaliman told reporters he was asking Morales “to resign his presidential mandate to allow for pacification and the maintaining of stability.”


Opposition senator, Jeanine Anez, said on Sunday she would assume the interim presidency of Bolivia.
