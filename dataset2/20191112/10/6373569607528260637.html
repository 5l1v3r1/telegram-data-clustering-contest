Ryan Lowe outlines his ambitions for Plymouth Argyle's youth academy

The Pilgrims' boss believes there are some potentially good players coming through the ranks

12 Nov 2019, 10:38 by Chris Errington

Plymouth Argyle manager Ryan Lowe will be having a large say in the shaping of the club’s youth academy.

There are going to be changes at Home Park after the departure of academy director Kevin Hodges, which was announced last week.

How the academy will be run going forward has not been confirmed, but what is clear is that Lowe will be providing a lot of input.

He has watched a number of the under-18s’ games this season, even taking half-time team talks on occasions.

Apprentices Jude Boyd and Rubin Wilson have also been given their first team debuts in EFL Trophy ties.

Lowe wants Argyle to become known not just for the success of their senior side but for their academy too.

The Pilgrims’ boss said: “First and foremost, Kev served the club well. I had a great relationship with him.

“For me, I want to wish him all the best in what he chooses to do next.

“In terms of the club going forward, it’s something I’m big on, I have given kids a chance already.

“I don’t want to just have the best team in the league, I want to have the best academy also.

“When that comes along, definitely I will be a part in hopefully shaping it.”

Argyle’s technical consultant – and Lowe’s mentor – Neil Dewsnip was the head of Everton’s academy for 17 years.

Lowe said: “Neil will be helping along the way as well where we need to go with it.

“Neil is the best in the business. He has run academies for so many years – I won’t tell you how many years, he will shout at me for showing his age.”

Lowe was asked how important it was for Argyle, a club out on a limb geographically, to develop its own players.

He replied: “I’m only early in my tenure here. The academy has been okay. We have got some good players.

“You look at (Adam) Randell, who has been an ever present in the squad this season.

(Klaidi) Lolos has made appearances, there is Jude Boyd and Rubin Wilson, so there are some players there.

“Some clubs haven’t even got that but our standards now, we want to have the best.”

Lowe added: “We have got some good kids in the academy, let’s have it right. We have got some fantastic talent in there.

“I’m up there most Tuesdays and Thursdays watching them, ranging from nine right the way up.”

Argyle’s apprentices spend a lot of time in and around the first team squad so Lowe has got a good understanding of their abilities and personalities.

Lowe said: “We know all the kids. They have got their own dressing room so they are here (at Home Park) and they have breakfast with us.

“We know what type of players they are, we know what type of characters they are. We have seen a lot of them.
