Bridget Jones' author says Jim'll Fix It had 'don't leave Jimmy with kids' rule

Helen Fielding, who worked for the BBC show in her early 20s, said that there was a rule on set that kids shouldn't be left alone with Jimmy Saville

12 Nov 2019, 21:18 by Rose Hill, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/rose-hill/

Bridget Jones Diary author Helen Fielding says the team of Jim’ll Fix It had a rule on set: “Don’t leave Jimmy alone with the children”.

The writer, who worked for the BBC show in her early 20s, says producers “must have known” to have suspicions about his behaviour.

Helen said her role behind the scenes after graduating from Oxford University, was during a conversation with Alain de Botton, was asked whether she had her suspicions about his behaviour.

“Well, we always said: ‘Don’t leave Jimmy alone with the children,’” she told the audience. “But we were sort of 23, and you know.... I am sure the producers must have known.

 

“We didn’t think he would do anything. But he was so creepy. We always stayed with the kids.”

Joking about her stint on the show after leaving Uni, she said at the School of Life event at London’s Emmanuel Centre: “I always had aspirations to be quite literary and intellectual - but I worked for Jim’ll Fix It, and things like that.”

The author, 61, admitted she now feels uncomfortable watching the first Bridget Jones Diary films in light of the #MeToo movement.

“Post #MeToo, I just couldn’t believe the amount of casual sexism in literally every single scene,” she said. “The hand on the bum, you know Mr Tits Pervert.

“The terrible thing was I just didn’t think about it. I was just writing stuff and that is what happened all the time. You know Daniel [Cleaver - one of Bridget’s suitors] would say ‘Mr Tits Pervert is disgusting. P.S I like your tits in that top.’”

Helen also fuelled speculation she based her Mark Darcy character on Labour’s Keir Starmer, admitting she declines to answer when asked specifically about it.

Helen will present a BBC documentary to mark 25 years since she created Bridget next year. She will explore the cultural impact of the franchise, which started in 1995.
