Teen urges 'Jekyl' boyfriend to murder his ex in texts sent during sick attack

The teenager lovers, who cannot be named for legal reasons, conspired to murder his 16-year-old ex-girlfriend at her home, a jury at Manchester Crown Court heard

12 Nov 2019, 21:27 by Chris Osuh

A teenage girl was savagely stabbed by her 17-year-old ex-boyfriend who was egged on by his current partner.

The 16-year-old victim, who cannot be named for legal reasons, was left with life-threatening injuries after she was brutalised in her Greater Manchester home.

The attack was launched after ex-boyfriend's current partner found out he was still seeing her behind her back.

His betrayal having been found out, the boy set about trying to "prove his love" for his new partner by attacking the ex-girlfriend.

The crime was detailed in a string of messages in which the victim describing the moments before she was stabbed.

 

On the night of May 4 the boy turned up at the victim's home armed with a large kitchen knife and carrying a change of clothes.

He told his ex-girlfriend he had been "ordered to kill her" before asking if she would kill herself for him.

The teenager even suggested she put make up on her neck to make it look like she had been strangled, so they could take a picture and pretend he had killed her.

He had been told to get photographic evidence by his current girlfriend, who was waiting outside.

In the two hours she spent outside the property while the victim was terrorised inside, the jealous teenager sent more than 50 texts encouraging him.

She had been "incensed" and "felt humiliated" when she learned of her partner seeing his former girlfriend.

And she had told him that he would "have to work hard to win back her affections".

The day before the attack he searched online for 'asphyxiation', 'how many years is someone in for murder' and 'how long do murderers serve in prison'.

On the day of the attack, he told his girlfriend: "I’m doing it now."

"Vid it then delete it when I see it please. I cannot stop crying," she replied.

"Vid it," she continued.

The victim would later describe her attacker as "like Jekyll and Hyde".

In the moments before he turned on the victim with the knife, she cried out for her sister and pleaded "let me out".

Instead he launched a savage attack.

In a garden at the side of her house he took what the victim described as "massive kitchen meat knife" and attacked her head, neck, armpit and back.

It was mere good fortune, jurors at Manchester Crown Court would be told in this month's trial, that she survived.

"Done it…you good that I did it", he texted his current girlfriend, and subsequent co-defendant, in the aftermath.

"Bare weight lifted," she replied.

Then, a flurry of further messages from her.

"Love you."

"Did you get rid of the phone?"

"Leave fast."

"Undies (slang for police) on the area."

"Show me vid."

"Leave now."

"We got sh*t to do."

"Why aren’t you replying?"

And then, as she became suspicious he hadn't gone through with it: "Hurry up, what’s taking so long?"

After the pair had left the area, while the victim was bleeding from ten wounds, the attacker told his lover: “I did it to prove I love you…there was no other way to keep you."

"I know there wasn’t…I’m gonna protect you at all costs", she replied.

"Just breathe, calm down, make sure you feel alright," she went on, adding, "on Tuesday I’ll link you and have an entire identity change n get rid of everything alright....I would have done the same for you."

Instead, the boy was arrested within minutes on suspicion of attempted murder, found standing at a bus stop with two Matalan bags - stuffed with bloodstained clothes and the weapon used in the attempted murder.

Earlier that day, his girlfriend and accomplice had bought Dettol wipes to clean the knife with.

The boy went on to deny attempted murder, although he didn't give evidence in his defence, while his girlfriend denied intentionally encouraging or assisting the boy to commit murder.

Both were found unanimously guilty of those offences, although the boy was cleared of rape.

At trial, his co-defendant said she thought her boyfriend was going to her love rival's house to split up with her for good.

She claimed that when she said 'vid it', she only wanted him to film evidence of the break-up.

But the texts damned her.

They proved she not only knew what he was doing, but goaded him on and advised him on how to try and get away with it.

It was, as jurors were told by the prosecutor, "unbelievable... but for the fact that it actually happened".

Judge Alan Conrad QC adjourned the case for sentencing next month
