Plymouth Argyle to find out their EFL Trophy fate when Bristol Rovers play on Wednesday night

Rovers can beat the Pilgrims to qualification for the last 32 of the competition

12 Nov 2019, 14:50 by Chris Errington

Plymouth Argyle will learn their fate in the Leasing.com Trophy when the final game in their group is played on Wednesday night.

The Pilgrims are currently second in Southern Group B, behind Chelsea Under-21s who have already secured a spot in the last 32 of the competition.

However, Argyle can be overtaken in the table by Graham Coughlan Bristol Rovers, which would see them miss out on qualification for the knockout stage.

Rovers will play the last game of the group against Swindon Town at the Memorial Stadium.

They know a win, or a penalty shoot-out success following a draw after 90 minutes, would see them advance in the competition, at the expense of Argyle.

However, a penalty shoot-out loss or a defeat to Swindon, who have been beaten in their previous two Trophy ties, would see Rovers finish third in the group.

Argyle would then progress to the last 32 as runners-up to Chelsea Under-21s and be drawn against one of the seven other group winners from the southern section.

The second round draw is set to take place on Saturday at 2pm, live on the Sky Sports Soccer Saturday show.

Argyle missed out on a chance to guarantee their qualification when they lost 1-0 to Chelsea Under-21s at Home Park on October 29.
