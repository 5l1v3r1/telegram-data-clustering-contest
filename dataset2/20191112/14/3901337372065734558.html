Sterling Reacts To Being Dropped From England Squad After Bust-Up

12 Nov 2019, 14:26 by Kurt Buckerfield

After being dropped for England's first match of the international break over a bust-up with one his teammates, Manchester City superstar Raheem Sterling has reacted to manager Gareth Southgate's decision.

Click through the mini-gallery above to see pictures of Sterling's altercation with Joe Gomez on Sunday.

The 24-year-old was involved in an altercation with Joe Gomez during City's 3-1 loss to Liverpool in this past Sunday, a tense situation that is believed to have continued when the England players met up in camp ahead of their upcoming 2020 European Championship qualifiers.

The Daily Mail reported that Sterling and Gomez had a heated exchange in a canteen on Monday, with Southgate responding by dropping the City forward for England's clash with Montenegro on Thursday night.

Sterling has since released a statement on social media, admitting that his emotions got the better of him, but added that he and Gomez have since made up.

"First and foremost, everyone knows what that game means to me!" he wrote in an Instagram Story, according to GiveMeSport.

"Everyone knows that I am not that way inclined and more to the point. Both Joe and I have had words and figured things out and moved on.

"We are in a sport where emotions run high and I am man enough to admit when emotions got the better of me.

"This is why we play this sport because of our love for it – me and @joegomez5 are good, we both understand it was a 5-10 second thing, it's done, we move forward and not make this bigger than it is.

"Let's get focus on our game on Thursday."
