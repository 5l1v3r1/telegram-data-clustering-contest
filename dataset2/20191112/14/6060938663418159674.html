SBI Research pegs FY20 growth at a low 5%; Q2 at a lower 4.2%

12 Nov 2019, 14:10 by PTI

Highlights

  • SBI Research attributed their lower projection to the plunging automobile sales, deceleration in air traffic movements, flattening core sector and declining investment in construction and infrastructure
  • The note comes a day after factory output contracted by a full 4.3 per cent in September -- worst in nine years

MUMBAI: House economists at the nation's largest bank SBI on Tuesday sharply slashed GDP forecast for the full year to a low 5 per cent, and warned of financial instability if the central continues with its easy money policy in its bid to spur growth.

The sharp downward revision to the tune of 110 basis points (bps) comes within a month of its previous projection of 6.1 per cent and within a week of international rating agency Moody's downgrading the sovereign outlook to negative from stable citing falling growth and weakening government finances on the back of massive tax giveaways and plunging tax collection.

The note comes a day after factory output contracted by a full 4.3 per cent in September -- worst in nine years -- the SBI economists said the second quarter GDP growth will print in at a lower 4.2 per cent.

SBI Research attributed their lower projection to the plunging automobile sales, deceleration in air traffic movements, flattening core sector and declining investment in construction and infrastructure.

GDP expansion hit a six-year low of 5 per cent in the June quarter on concerns surrounding consumption and private investment, and many analysts have cut their growth forecasts.

Japanese brokerage Nomura expects GDP to grow at 4.9 per cent for the year to March -- the lowest projection so far.

SBI, which had a forecast of 6.1 per cent in line with the number projected by the Reserve Bank, has cut its forecast to 5 per cent in a sharp cut in outlook and added that the same will go up to 6.2 per cent in FY21.

The slower growth should be looked at in context of a "synchronised global slowdown" and India is also significantly lower in the economic uncertainty index compared to its peers, the report said.

"We now expect larger rate cuts from RBI in the December policy. However, such rate cut is unlikely to lead to any immediate material revival, rather it might result in potential financial instability," they warned.

It can be noted that the RBI has cut interest rates by a cumulative 135 bps in 2019 in its bid to revive the sagging growth, best utilising the comfort afforded by stability in its core objective of price rise.

The government should now hope for not having "negative policy surprises" on sectors like telecom, shadow banks and power, they said.

"It is imperative that a lasting solution is worked out for NBFCs which has been much delayed now. We believe given the crisis of confidence in the financial markets, provision of central bank liquidity for NBFCs is necessary to ensure the stability of the financial system," the report said.

In telecom, the government need to encourage new investors to set up networks by addressing the woes of the incumbents, it said, adding in the power sector, despite a 15 per cent increase in installed capacity, average plant load factor of thermal plants dipped to an all time low of 48.9 per cent in October, while renewables generation declined by 6.4 per cent.
