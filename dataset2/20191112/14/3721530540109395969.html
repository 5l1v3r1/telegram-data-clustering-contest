Disney Plus isn’t technically down, but good luck watching anything

Follow Polygon online:

12 Nov 2019, 14:45 by Austen Goslin

Disney Plus, Disney’s new streaming service, is currently experiencing very slow loading speeds, to the point where the content is basically unavailable. I’ve waited 30 minutes (and counting) to begin Dumbo. Other Polygon staff members have waited similar amounts of time to load The Mandalorian. So, whether you want to check out the latest Star Wars show, or just watch Forky embrace his inner trash, you may have to wait a little — or a lot.

Disney hasn’t officially explained why its brand-new service is, for many users, chugging at a glacial pace. But the debut of The Mandalorian, combined with interest in the company’s sprawling back catalog, have seemingly clogged up the platform. While the delays are annoying, the excitement of launch day regularly slows — if not outright crashes — servers, most commonly in the world of multiplayer video games.

Related

People across Twitter are reporting frustrating loading times, ranging from the content itself lagging to the user being unable to connect at all. Others are reporting error 76, which points to “slow internet connection speeds” as the source of the problem.

When the platform launched in the wee hours of the morning on Tuesday, the site worked pretty well. But as more and more users logged on, it appears that the Disney back end has been swamped. We don’t see many new streaming sites pop up to as much fervor, so there’s little precedent in the world of video streaming. Oddly enough, the closest comparison may be the launch of the pro wrestling streaming service the WWE Network in 2014, which had its own set of problems.
