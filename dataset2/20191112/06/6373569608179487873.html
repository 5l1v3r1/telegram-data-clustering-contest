Jobs boost for Plymouth with industrial parks set to expand

Progress is being made on business projects across the city

12 Nov 2019, 06:00 by Edward Oldfield

Plans are progressing for a series of city council-funded industrial units to support the growth of the economy in Plymouth.

The local authority is expected to submit a planning application in December for Phase 3 of Hearder Court at Langage Science Park.

The proposal for around 27,000 square feet of industrial space costing an estimated £2.9m will support up to 68 jobs.

City council leader Tudor Evans revealed a planning application was due to go in before Christmas.

The development was named in honour of Plymouth scientist Jonathan Nash Hearder, an electrical engineer, inventor and educator who was an advisor on the first Atlantic cable.

Phase One in the industrial park on the edge of Plympton was completed in 2015 and is occupied by Dartmouth Foods Ltd. The £3.2m second phase was completed last year.

Cllr Evans highlighted progress on the next part of the scheme as one of a series of city council-funded developments coming forward to support the local economy.

The Labour leader told a meeting of the council’s ruling Cabinet: “We are confident as we have proved with a number of these projects, if we build it they will come. 

“Taxpayers can be reassured we are investing this money wisely. It produces jobs, it produces business rates, it produces confidence for the future.”

Cllr Evans also revealed work was due to start in January at Broadley Park, Roborough, a £2.6m scheme to create 22,500 square feet of work space, supporting an estimated 62 jobs.

And he said a planning application was expected to be submitted early next year for 20,000 square feet of health units at Plymouth International Business Park at Derriford.

The council is also set to fund the construction of a new 126-bed hotel on a redevelopment site at Millbay.
