Deepak Chahar is not the first Indian bowler to claim a T20I hat-trick, it was actually Ekta Bisht

12 Nov 2019, 06:34 by TIMESOFINDIA.COM

NEW DELHI: Indian medium pacer Deepak Chahar became the talk of the town when he claimed a hat-trick against Bangladesh in the third and final Twenty20 International in Nagpur and helped India clinch the three-match series 2-1.

With Bangladesh chasing a target of 175, the 27-year-old Rajasthan seamer bagged 6 wickets for just 7 in 3.2 overs to register the best-ever figures in the history of T20 internationals, surpassing the previous record held by Sri Lanka's Ajantha Mendis who scalped 6/8 against Zimbabwe in 2012.

Man-of-the-Match Chahar dismissed Shafiul Islam, Mustafizur Rahman and Aminul Islam to claim the incredible feat.

But Chahar is not the first Indian cricketer to take a T20 international hat-trick, as was widely reported and tweeted by the BCCI.
