Man Utd squad fly out to Dubai for warm weather training - without Ole Gunnar Solskjaer

Those players not on international duty travelled to the UAE for a three-day trip to prepare for United's next Premier League game against Sheffield United

12 Nov 2019, 19:08 by Alex Milne

A number of key Manchester United players were pictured in a Dubai restaurant as they take advantage of the international break by engaging in some warm-weather training.

The likes of Juan Mata, Ashley Young and Aaron Wan-Bissaka were all present in the Middle East country, but there was no sign of manager Ole Gunnar Solskjaer, who looks to have stayed in the UK.

The squad were seen with celebrity chef Nusret Gökçe- otherwise known as Salt Bae- and Mata, Young, Wan-Bissaka, Lee Grant, Andreas Pereira, Axel Tuanzebe, Marcos Rojo and Fred can all be seen in the photo.

The players are expected to stay in Dubai for two more days, training with United coaches, while teammates such as Marcus Rashford, Daniel James and Harry Maguire represent their countries.

United have a number of injury issues, with Scott McTominay the latest to suffer after hurting his ankle towards the end of the weekend win over Brighton , and the likes of he, Paul Pogba, Luke Shaw and Nemanja Matic were not present in Dubai, staying in Manchester instead to continue their rehabilitation.

United face an intriguing-looking game against high-flying Sheffield United in their next game on November 24, but they should be full of confidence following their impressive performance in the 3-1 win over Brighton on Sunday.

Following the victory Solskjaer said: "That was our best performance of the season. It was an excellent performance from the lads. When you see them going forward, it's a joy to watch.

“How it didn't end up five, six or seven, we don't know, but it was exhilarating and entertaining and great to see the boys enjoy themselves.

“It's been great these two games, with loads of attacking play. We should have had more goals, but the intentions are there and they're enjoying themselves

“They young players are performing and learning. They've had some tough lessons, but they've learned from them."
