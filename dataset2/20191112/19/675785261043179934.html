Sex text ex Tory MP Andrew Griffiths stands aside - to back bid by his wife

He did not get the support he needed from his local Conservative party in Burton

12 Nov 2019, 19:42 by Nicola Bartlett, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/nicola-bartlett/

Disgraced former Tory MP Andrew Griffiths has given up his bid to contest the seat again but will back a bid by his wife.

Mr Griffiths, who was exposed sending a barmaid and her pal £700 and offering to rent a flat to meet for sex, has failed to win the outright majority he needs to stand again in the general election .

The politician, who has represented the constituency for nine years, failed to gain the majority he needed from the local party last night.

And he decided to withdraw ahead of the meeting rather than face a second humiliating defeat.

It came after the Chair, President and all officers of East Staffordshire Conservative Association said they did not support his candidacy for the Burton parliamentary seat.

But instead Kate Griffiths has put herself forward for the Conservative nomination alongside former MP Stewart Jackson and Virginia Crosbie.

Mr Griffiths, who is married with a baby girl, said he contemplated suicide after he was  exposed by the Sunday Mirror  for sexting barmaid Imogen Treharne, 28, and her friend.

He resigned as a government minister and blamed the behavior on a series of personal tragedies.

Mr Griffiths said he was abused as an eight year old by a 15-year-old boy, suffered depression as he and his wife struggled to conceive a child, and learnt that his brother had been diagnosed with cancer.

In the “sexts” sent while he was minister for small business, Griffiths called himself “Daddy”, promised money for sexy photos, and described sex he claimed to have had with other women.

 

The Sunday Mirror revealed how the new dad sent Treharne and her pal £700 and offered to rent a flat to meet for sex.

He demanded explicit photos and videos and described degrading sex acts in crude detail.

Standards chiefs ruled they could “not be sure” Mr Griffiths, 48, sent or received explicit messages when he would have been “engaged in Parliamentary activities”.

But the probe ruled some were sent during office hours.

Evidence shows several were sent in close proximity to Commons votes he took part in.

One to Imogen Treharne was at 4.22pm as a vote on an EU matter was taking place.

Mr Griffiths sent 2,000 texts in all, referring to himself as “Daddy” and outlining sex acts.

After last night's vote he said: "I am pleased to have received so much support last night and frustrated to fall just one vote short.

"I am looking forward to tonight's meeting so that I can further explain my record of success in delivering for Burton and Uttoxeter.

"Having voted every time in parliament for Brexit , I want the chance to finish the job and deliver on the promise to respect the referendum and get us out of the EU."

East Staffordshire Conservative Association chairman Wileman said: "Andrew was not adopted [last night] as he needs a majority.

"Both sides [of support and opposition for Mr Griffiths] were heckling each other.

"Questions were allowed but only brief ones which had been sifted through beforehand, rather than members being allowed to put their hands up.

"A sitting MP needs to secure a majority so there is a second meeting tonight.

"Andrew will have the right to be there as will other candidates.

"I am not aware of who they are as the candidate selection process is coordinated nationally.

"There has been frustration for some considerable time. There is a feeling of anger within the association that the decision should be made locally as people know their members.

"I would imagine that given it is such a contentious issue that the party nationally needs to have some control over it.

"A significant amount of members were concerned about how the process was carried out and has caused anger and frustration about the process itself.

"We haven't yet received an application from Andrew to stand tonight, but I understand he may well stand and then there will be other candidates who will be able to apply.

"We clearly wanted a selection process so members could have their say."
