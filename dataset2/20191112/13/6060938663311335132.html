The year 2019 has been a learning year for me: Harikrishna

12 Nov 2019, 13:11 by Prasad RS

CHENNAI: Grandmaster P Harikrishna - along with Vidit Gujrathi - may have the tag of wildcards at the upcoming Grand Chess Tour leg of Kolkata but one will take them lightly at their own peril.

Harikrishna, who finished second in the rapid event of Tata Steel chess in Kolkata behind Hikaru Nakamura, is understandably excited at the prospect of featuring in the tournament once again. "I had a good tournament last year and it will be nice to be back in action against the top players in the world," Harikrishna told TOI.

With wildcards such as Anton Korobov - in Bucharest leg of GCT - giving a good account of themselves, Harikrishna seems to be deriving some confidence from their show. "It is a good sign.
