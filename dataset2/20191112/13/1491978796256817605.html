Tutu dreams: the Royal Academy of Dance at 100 – in pictures

12 Nov 2019, 13:44

From tabletop pointe work to getting friendly with a skeleton – the RAD’s century of ballet training saw teachers including Adeline Genée and Margot Fonteyn spread dance around the world

Adeline Genée, an acclaimed ballerina, was the first president of the Association of Operatic Dancing, created in 1920 to improve the standard of ballet training in the UK. She is pictured circa 1932, with young members of the Association, before a gala performance at Drury Lane. The Association changed its name to the Royal Academy of Dance in 1935 Royal Academy of Dance: Celebrating 100 Years is available on 2 Dec 2019 from Royal Academy of Dance Enterprises
