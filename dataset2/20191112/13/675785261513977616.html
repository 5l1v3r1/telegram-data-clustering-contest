Man finds heartwarming note left by firefighters who saved his home from flames

Paul Sekfy shared the message on Facebook with others saying it was 'heartwarming news in a heartbreaking story' as wildfires in Australia continue to rage

12 Nov 2019, 13:52 by Luke Matthews, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/luke-matthews/

A man who returned home to find a sweet message from firefighters who saved his house from flames shared it online to offer a bright moment in devastating circumstances.

Australia has declared a state of emergency as 'catastrophic' wild bushfires rage across the country, claiming three lives and destroying hundreds of homes.

In New South Wales alone there are 60 fires, 40 of which are are considered out of control.

Paul Sekfy returned to his home over the weekend to find a note from the Urunga Rural Fire Service left behind on his kitchen bench.

 

It read: "It was our pleasure to save your house. Sorry that we could not save your sheds. Urunga RFS. p.s. - we owe you some milk."

Paul posted a snap of the note to Facebook with the caption: 'The best note on my kitchen bench since the morning after my wedding'.

The image racked up 4,700 shares and even appeared to reach the very fireman who had penned it.

Kale Hardie-Porter replied to say: "Hi Paul Sekfy, you have made my day!

"I'm happy to know that my note got to you in one piece (knowing that the house survived once we had to leave).

 

"Our crew of 4 did the best we could with what we had but unfortunately your two sheds didn't last a second.

"We took refuge in your house for a moment and that's when we discovered the fridge."

He also apologised for his 'shocking handwriting' before saying it 'was late and I couldn't see a thing'.

Kale signs it off: "Again it was our pleasure to do a little good in such horrendous conditions. All the best in future."

Others were quick to share in the sweet moment, with one writing: "Heartwarming news Paul in a heartbreaking story for many. Those fire fighters are legends."

Another added: "If you've just saved my house, hell you can have my whole fridge and everything inside it too!"

Paul declined to reveal the contents of the note he discovered the day after his wedding.
