England fans demand Bobby Moore's first cap is taken off auction where it's listed for £10k

Relatives of World Cup winning captain Moore, who died in 1993, have decided to auction the historic blue velvet cap he was awarded in 1963

12 Nov 2019, 18:55 by Andy Lines, https://www.facebook.com/AndyLinesDailyMirror/

England fans have demanded that the cap Bobby Moore won when he first skippered his country should be saved for the nation.

They were speaking after it emerged that the 1966 World Cup hero’s cap was being auctioned later this month.

Some demanded that the FA step in to buy it and donate it to a museum.

It is expected to sell for £10,000.

The World Cup hero’s relatives have decided to auction the historic blue velvet cap as England prepare for their historic 1,000th game.

The symbolic item was presented to the late 1966 captain following England’s 4-2 victory over Czechoslovakia in 1963.

West Ham season ticket holder Terry Trundle, 56, said: “Bobby Moore’s first cap as England captain should be a national treasure.

“His cap should be put on display where football fans can see it.

“It would be nice if the FA would step in and buy it for all England fans.”

Birmingham City supporter Chris Collins, 53, said: “It would be a travesty if the cap went into private hands and left the country.

“£10,000 is nothing in football these days the FA should buy it.”

Moore died back in 1993 shunned by the game he loved and having to work for Capital Radio and the Sunday Sport.

He could only dream of earning the wages of the modern player with current England stars earning more in one week than Moore did in his whole career.

The National Football Museum told the Mirror they would love to put the cap on display if a wealthy benefactor wanted to buy it and loan it to them.

Philippa Duxbury said: “We would of course be interested in displaying it as part of the England Story at the museum, should the eventual buyer wish to discuss this with us.”

The other two caps to be sold are valued at £6,000 each were for Moore’s appearances against Portugal in 1964 and Yugoslavia in 1972.

The auctioneers are expecting a huge amount of interest in the sale on November 20th.

Ben Jones, a specialist at Mullock’s, said: “Bobby Moore is one of the most iconic names in English footballing history, even more so that it was his first international appearance as England captain.

“All the caps are in very good condition and appear to have been kept dry and out of the light.”

In the build up to the sale England will be playing their 1000th international match against Montenegro on Thursday night.

Mr Jones added: “We have had interest and the fact that England’s 1000th International match is approaching is beneficial.”
