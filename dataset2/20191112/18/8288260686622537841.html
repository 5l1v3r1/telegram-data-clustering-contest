Aston Villa handed major injury boost ahead of Newcastle United and Manchester United clashes

Aston Villa latest news | Dean Smith has had to deal with an injury headache but one man is ready to hand him a boost ahead of the Premier League's return in two weeks

12 Nov 2019, 18:06 by Richard Cusack

Aston Villa's Jota has issued an injury update as he battles back from a hernia operation.

The summer signing from Birmingham City underwent surgery at the beginning of the month.

Jota hasn't featured since the September 25 Carabao Cup win at Brighton and Hove Albion .

He was out of the starting XI picture before his operation, an unused substitute for three matches until he went under the knife.

The Spaniard had started three of five matches, substituted in for his first couple of Villa outings.

With Villa really struggling with injuries, Jota has provided a major boost for boss Dean Smith .

First-choice goalkeeper Tom Heaton missed the 2-1 defeat to Wolverhampton Wanderers last time out, with captain Jack Grealish and starting centre-back also sitting out the trip to Molineux.

To make matters worse, Heaton's replacement Jed Steer suffered a calf injury and Matt Targett limped out in first-half injury time.

But Jota could be back soon, posting a video on Instagram of him doing sprinting drills with strength and conditioning coach Paddy Moore.
