The best VPN for Disney Plus

Our pick of the top six VPN providers if you're looking to appear in the US, Canada or The Netherlands - currently the only countries where it's available

12 Nov 2019, 18:07 by Anyron Copeman

The highly anticipated streaming service Disney+ is out now in the US, Canada and The Netherlands, but not elsewhere. 

Unlike Apple TV+, Disney has a huge library of content from launch. Most of the recent Marvel films are here, alongside old classics such as Hannah Montana and even the full Simpsons collection. Add eagerly awaited original content such as The Mandalorian or Falcon and the Winter Soldier and it becomes a compelling package.

However, the UK release date has been confirmed for 31 March 2020, meaning we have months to wait to watch the new shows.

While it's disappointing that we'll have to wait more than four months for access to the same content, there are potential ways to get around this. A virtual private network (VPN) gives you an IP address from a country that you select. With some, you can even have more granular control over a specific region.

Unfortunately, a VPN isn't enough - signing up for Disney+ also requires a payment method from the appropriate country, though we've found (and tested) a sneaky workaround using a US Apple ID and some digital gift cards. For more info, read our detailed guide on how to watch Disney Plus in the UK.

If you're simply looking for a VPN at a great price, visit our round-up of the best VPN deals available right now or our pick of the best streaming VPNs if you want to watch a wider range of streaming services.

It's our favourite service from a usability standpoint, with great speed and a number of premium features at an affordable price. You can appear in 62 countries in total, including the US, Canada and The Netherlands. 

It's available for as little as £2.82 per month if you sign up for the three-year deal, but there's plenty of flexibility with two-year, one-year, six-month and one-month plans also available.
