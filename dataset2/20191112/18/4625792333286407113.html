‘Clearly angling for the job’: Former GOP strategist says Haley is trying to get Trump to 'dump' Pence

12 Nov 2019, 18:04 by Madison Dibble

Former Republican strategist Steve Schimdt claimed Nikki Haley is trying to nudge President Trump toward ditching Mike Pence as vice president and letting her take his place.

Since leaving her post as United Nations ambassador, Haley has defended Trump and touted her loyalty to the president. In her new book, With All Due Respect, Haley detailed an exchange in which she stood by the president when Rex Tillerson and John Kelly urged her to turn against him.

During a Tuesday appearance on Morning Joe, Schmidt doubted the credibility of her claim, arguing it is part of a larger attempt to land on Trump’s 2020 ticket.

"I find her statements on this not particularly credible,” Schimdt said. “I think they speak to her ambition, and I think Mike Pence is probably hanging out back there, thinking, 'Hey, I got a 1 in 365 chance in picking the day that Trump’s going to dump me from the ticket and put her on sometime in the next year.'"
