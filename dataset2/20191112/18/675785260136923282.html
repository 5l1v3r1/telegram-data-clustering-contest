Victor Lindelof admits he "misses" former Man Utd boss Jose Mourinho

Jose Mourinho signed Victor Lindelof for Manchester United from Benfica and helped him progress into a first team player before he was sacked in 2018

12 Nov 2019, 18:44 by Jake Polden, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/jake-polden/

Victor Lindelof says he misses former Manchester United boss Jose Mourinho being in football, adding: "He's one of the world's best coaches".

Mourinho signed Lindelof for Man United from Benfica and helped him progress into a first team player before he was sacked and replaced by Ole Gunnar Solskjaer in 2018.

Since then Lindelof has nailed down a place in the Red Devils first team alongside £80million summer signing Harry Maguire.

Mourinho, meanwhile, is yet to return to football management, despite confirming he has received a number of job offers.

It seems the Portuguese, who has won trophies in four countries, is waiting for the right job, but Lindelof thinks it's a shame he's not currently managing.

"He is, after all, one of the world's best coaches and he is supposed to have a job at one of the biggest clubs. I miss him in football," Lindelof told Aftonbladet.

"He's a great coach and a great person, whom I owe a great deal. It was he who gave me the chance here, who brought me into everything.

"I have all the respect in the world for him and appreciate everything he has said and done for me, from scolding to a hug.

"Even if I did not agree, at the time… he has very great charisma, you had great respect for him. He's won everything. When he says something, you listen."
