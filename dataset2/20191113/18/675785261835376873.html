Vicky Pattison blames social media for the mental health epidemic in deeply honest post

The reality TV starlet shared a candid post about the impact social media has on mental heath and insisted it has 'destroyed our ability to be happy'.

13 Nov 2019, 18:47 by Tiffany Wallis

Vicky Pattison has shared a deeply honest post about the impact social media has on mental health on Tuesday.

The reality TV scarlet, 31, blamed social media for "destroying our generations ability to be truly happy" and slammed unrealistic images of perfection posted to Instagram .

The former Queen of the jungle shared a duo of snaps with an iconic before and after comparison.

In the first, she dazzled on the red carpet at last month's Pride Of Britain Awards and another image of her the next morning as she went make-up free and pulled a funny face.

The former Geordie Shore star admitted that Instagram has made her feel "deflated, inferior, upset and depressed" in the past and promised to change the way she posts on the social media app.

She shared the lengthy post with her 4.4 millions followers.

Vicky wrote: "Unpopular Opinion: I think social media has absolutely destroyed our generations ability to be truly happy.

"We’re always seeing someone else who we think has it better than us and it makes us feel like what we have isn’t enough."

After admitting how scrolling on the app made her feel it, Vicky said it was a "shame" and a "real waste" as the service could be "an international support system of people motivating each other".

The telly star encouraged users to change their ways and show "their truth, their vulnerability and their authentic self" instead of posting filtered pictures to hide insecurities.

Vicky finished her heartfelt post by touching on mental health.

She penned:"You're telling people that it’s OK to not be perfect, to struggle sometimes, to feel pain, to not be OK.

"So this is me, showing you a little glimpse behind the glamour... my silly truth after the pride of Britain the other week.

"Sending lots of love and positivity to anyone reading this. You are beautiful, you are worthy, you are more than a Valencia filter, you are enough."

The emotional post was liked by nearly 25,000 followers, with many rushing to the comments section to praise Vicky on her truthful words.

The Newcastle-born babe has always been open about her struggles surrounding body image.

Fans have been loving the beauty's honesty, and thanked her for being so candid on the topic of health and body image.

She already has legions of adoring fans - not only did she win the hearts of Geordie Shore viewers, fans of I'm A Celebrity: Get Me Out Of Here voted her as Queen of the Jungle back in 2015.
