Pelosi to Democrats: 'We need to have a common narrative'

13 Nov 2019, 18:48 by Susan Ferrechio

House Speaker Nancy Pelosi told fellow Democrats ahead of Wednesday’s impeachment hearings to stick to a uniform narrative in response to media questions.

“This is a very serious event in our country,” the California lawmaker told Democrats Wednesday morning in a closed-door meeting. “We wish it could have been avoided. None of us came here to impeach a president. All of us take an oath to protect and defend the Constitution, but as I just said, we’re legislating at the same time.”

Pelosi coached Democrats on messaging about President Trump, who the party accuses of abusing the office of the presidency and obstructing Congress.

Pelosi told Democrats, Trump “must be held accountable, he is not above the law. No president is. And we have a responsibility to make sure that that is the message, not only for today but for the ages.”

Democrats plan two weeks of public hearings featuring many of the witnesses deposed in closed-door sessions by a special impeachment panel.

Pelosi said she had asked Intelligence Committee Chairman Adam Schiff of California, who is overseeing the impeachment proceedings, to provide Democrats with “a narrative at the end about a summation of what happened there.”

She warned Democrats to “not to get distracted” if Republicans disrupt the hearings. “The press will say, ‘What do you think of the Republicans doing this?’ They don’t think so I don’t think about what they think, but I do think that we need to have a common narrative.”
