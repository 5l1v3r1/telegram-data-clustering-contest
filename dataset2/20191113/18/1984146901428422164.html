Holly Willoughby breaks down in tears on This morning as co-star recalls domestic abuse past

Therapist Eva Speakman revealed that her abusive ex-boyfriend once put her in hospital after an attack

13 Nov 2019, 18:05 by Katie Collings

This Morning presenter Holly Willoughby broke down in tears on air as her co-star Eva Speakman revealed her history of domestic abuse.

Speaking to Holly and Phillip Schofield, therapist Eva admitted that she still found it hard to talk about the abuse.

Talking about an unnamed ex-boyfriend, Eva said that she felt "shame and guilt" for allowing the abuse to happen.

The life coach also revealed that only recently told her mother about the abuse, and that she had "lied all the way through" the relationship to her family.

She said: “It was most definitely grooming in that there is a gift and validation of being loved.

“Having been bullied at school and having low self esteem I think that made me more vulnerable and more needy.

“I’d probably become somewhat desensitised to verbal abuse.”

Host Phil asked: “What was being said? How did it progress?”

She replied: “It progressed to being made to feel that I was stupid. That I shouldn’t be speaking to certain people.

“There was a lot of control, which I didn’t realise at the time.”

Eva, who was joined by her now-husband Nik Speakman, then admitted that she knew he was going to violent by "a look on his face".

She said: “There was a look, and I knew that if that look came about then I’d have to be really careful.”

Recalling a harrowing moment that took place in public, she said: “I was talking to my friends, having a really good time and I got the look.

“I felt a little bit brave because I was with people I trusted.

“I asked the question ‘what was that look for?’ And then I got headbutted three times.

“I remember hitting the deck, I think I hit the bar, and then I remember excruciating pain that I have never felt before.

“And even though there was so much pain physically, and I was really frightened, I felt shame again because people had seen this.”

After being taken to hospital by her cousin, Eva revealed that she tried to beg her cousin not to tell her parents.

But after realising that something needed to be done, she decided that she needed to open up about the abuse.

The violent ex-broke her father’s nose for not allowing him to see Eva after she had left the hospital.

Eva said: “You can hurt me but you don’t hurt my family. That made me say ‘enough’."

Eva also revealed an incident where her ex smashed the windows of her car while she was inside.

The life coach managed to escape the situation after driving to her parents house with the smashed up car.

She admitted that her ex became furious purely because she had spoken to somebody, leaving him ‘embarrassed and ashamed’.

Holly praised Eva for opening up about her past, saying: “You are so brave. I’m full of admiration for you.”

Asked what advice she would give to people going through an abusive relationship, she said: “There is no shame in this, you are the victim not the perpetrator.

“One in four women face some sort of abuse or violence. You have to talk it through, you have to realise that it’s not your fault."
