Our guide to the best festive fairs and Christmas markets across Notts

Crafts, jewellery, art, clothing, accessories, food, drink and more

13 Nov 2019, 15:28 by Lynette Pinchess

Where do you find a unique gift for the hard-to-please relative who has everything? Nottinghamshire's wealth of Christmas markets are a good start.

Nottingham Winter Wonderland is sure to be busy, especially after the kudos of being named one of the best in the UK by Vogue.

So if you want to avoid the hustle and bustle in the Old Market Square - not to mention the hefty city centre parking fees - the alternative is to visit some of the other Christmas markets and festive fairs around the county where crafts, jewellery, art, clothing and accessories that you won't find on the high street are begging to be snapped up.

And for the foodies in your life there's an abundance of scrumptious presents to surprise them on Christmas Day.

November 22 to 24 is stacking up to be the main weekend with events at several venues - but there's plenty of other dates besides.

Holme Pierrepont Hall

Around 1,000 people flock to the annual event in the grandeur of the hall, taking place on Thursday, November 14.

There will be a variety of individual, design-led and artisan stalls, handpicked to offer a broad range of unique gifts.

Food gifts, women's fashion and accessories, beauty treats, men's present ideas, toys and stocking fillers will be on sale.

Stalls include small batch gin and rum from the award-winning A Little Luxury Distillery, cashmere from Bird of Paradise, and beautiful homewares from Chloe Jonason Interiors.

Taking place all day, from 9.30am to 4pm there's plenty of time to peruse at leisure. The event will once again raise funds for Rainbows Children's Hospice, which last year amassed £9,500.

Admission is £5 donation. Buy in advance online or on the door.

Location: Holme Pierrepont NG12 2LD

The Walled Garden

Another lovely location for a festive shopping trip is at Beeston Fields. Hosted by the Marshmallow Kitchen, the handpicked selection of stalls will offer plenty of special gifts and treats that can't be found on the high street, plus artisan food and festive drinks.

Beat the Christmas rush by heading here on Sunday November 17, from 10am to 4pm.

Entry is £1 and parking is free. Dogs are welcome.

Location: Old Drive, Wollaton Road, Beeston NG9 3DA.

The Carriage Hall

The wedding venue will turn into an exciting Aladdin's cave of gifts from some of the East Midlands' most imaginative craft and artisan suppliers.

Among them will be jewellery, Christmas decorations, candles, hand-made knitted clothing, jams and chutneys, cosmetics and beauty products.

Friday, November 22 is the date. The time, noon to 6pm.

Wood-fired pizzas, hot drinks, mulled wine and prosecco will give shoppers a break from their spending spree. Or since the venue is next to Perkins Bar & Bistro, it's too good an opportunity to miss out on lunch, afternoon tea or dinner at the restaurant.

Location: Station Road, Plumtree NG12 5NA

Welbeck Winter Weekend

The three-day shopping extravaganza returns to the Welbeck Estate, November 22 to 24, to celebrate all things hand-made, bringing together more than 100 artisan makers and food producers.

The food market will have 30 food stalls to whet your appetite, specially selected by the award-winning Welbeck Farm Shop, while the Art Marquee's 75 artists and craftspeople have been carefully curated by The Harley Gallery.

For the first time since since its launch 19 years ago, the event will also include a special marquee dedicated to showcasing the artisan makers and producers based in the historic estate, near Worksop. Take the opportunity to find out more about Welbeck Abbey Brewery, Welbeck Bakehouse, Welbeck Dairy, Stichelton Cheese and The School of Artisan Food.

Admission and parking are free. Open 10am to 5pm Friday and Saturday and until 4pm on the Sunday.

Location: Just off the A60, Welbeck, Worksop S80 3LL

Maggie's Nottingham

There will be plenty of Christmas cheer when the cancer support centre holds its annual festive fair. As well as lots of lovely stalls for presents and stocking fillers, there will be a raffle and the Rockabillies Choir will be singing.

The kitchen table will be groaning under the weight of home-made cakes and mince pies - perfect with a cup of mulled wine.

Make it a date on Saturday, November 23, from 11am to 2pm, and have fun spending money to help a good cause.

Entry is £1 per adult and free for children.

Location: Gate 3, Nottingham City Hospital Campus, Hucknall Road, NG5 1PH

Nottingham Contemporary

Explore hundreds of beautiful, bespoke products from the very best designers and makers from Nottingham and beyond at the city's art gallery.

Held over three days, November 22 to 24, there's plenty of time to pop into the free craft fair. There will also be family craft activities, live music and seasonal food and drink in the cafe bar.

Kicking off on Friday, from 6pm-8.30pm, the wine will be flowing and the mince pies plentiful. The festive fair will also be open 10am to 6pm on the Saturday and 11am to 5pm on the Sunday.

Location: Weekday Cross, Nottingham NG1 2GB

Winterfest

The two-day celebration of diverse local hand-made crafts has become an established event on the Thoresby Park calendar.

Notts artists have the opportunity to exhibit their talent and creativity and visitors can buy original artwork straight from the artist.

And of course, there will be lots of festive food and drink to buy, musical entertainment and the chance to see Father Christmas.

Soak up the atmosphere while discovering something not found on the high street at this year's event on Saturday, November 23, 10am to 6pm and Sunday, November 24, 10am to 4pm.

Entry and parking are both free.

Location: Netherfield Lane, Ollerton NG22 9EP.

Conscious Christmas Market

Banish the plastic and packaging this Christmas. If you're looking for a more ethical approach to festive giving, Shop Zero's Conscious Christmas Gift Market is the place to find sustainable gifts from local makers and businesses.

The market will be held on the Malt Cross's beautiful balcony on Sunday, November 24 from 11am to 5pm.

Location: St James's Street, Nottingham NG1 6FG.

Tom Browns Brasserie

Food stalls, handcrafted gifts and individual boutiques will be at the annual Christmas market, taking place on Sunday December 1.

Admission will be free to the event, taking place 10am to 4.30pm.

Feeling peckish while shopping? Refuel with BBQ food, coffee, cake and mulled wine. Or book a table in the restaurant for before or after.

Location: Trentside, Gunthorpe NG14 7FB
