Furious Man City decide against Bernardo Silva appeal over fear of increased ban

Bernardo Silva is facing a one-game ban - but furious Man city do not plan to appeal the punishment given for a controversial tweet

13 Nov 2019, 15:23 by David McDonnell, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/david-mcdonnell/

Manchester City are furious with Bernardo Silva's one-game ban for sending a controversial tweet - but will not appeal for fear of the FA increasing the suspension.

Silva was hit with a one-match ban, a £50,0000 fine and ordered to undertake an education programme after pleading guilty to an FA charge of misconduct.

The ban means Silva, one of City's most influential players, will miss their huge Premier League clash at home against Chelsea a week on Saturday.

The charge related to a tweet Silva sent on September 22 to team-mate Benjamin Mendy, comparing the City left-back to a character depicted on a packet of Conguitos, a chocolate brand available in Spain and Portugal.

Silva deleted the post 46 minutes after he published it, then tweeted: "Can't even joke with a friend these days."

The FA charged Silva after stating the post constituted an “aggravated breach” of its rules on social media behaviour, because it appeared to reference Mendy's race.

Although Silva accepted the charge, City are dismayed at the independent disciplinary commission's decision to ban him for one game, believing a fine and a warning would have been sufficient.

Leicester's Hamza Choudhury was fined £5,000 by the FA after accepting a charge of misconduct for historic racist and offensive tweets earlier this year, but escaped a ban, with City angry at what they see as a lack of consistency from the game's governing body.

City felt Silva's unblemished previous disciplinary record, combined with the fact that Mendy - his close friend - took no offence at the tweet, should have seen him escape a ban.

Those feelings were made clear by City in their submissions and mitigation to the FA, on behalf of Silva, but the governing body felt a one-match ban was an appropriate sanction.

The FA released a statement which read: "Bernardo Silva has been suspended for one first team competitive fixture, fined £50,000 and must complete face-to-face education after admitting a breach of FA Rule E3.

"The Manchester City midfielder’s social media activity on 22 September 2019 breached FA Rule E3(1), as it was insulting and/or improper and/or brought the game into disrepute, and constituted an “Aggravated Breach”, which is defined in FA Rule E3(2), as it included reference, whether expressed or implied, to race and/or colour and/or ethnic origin."
