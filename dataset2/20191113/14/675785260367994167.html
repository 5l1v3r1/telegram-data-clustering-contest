Deontay Wilder explains difference between himself and Anthony Joshua

Joshua lost his three heavyweight world titles to Andy Ruiz Jr in June - but rival Wilder remains champion

13 Nov 2019, 14:03 by Martin Domin, https://www.facebook.com/mirrorfighting

Deontay Wilder has claimed Anthony Joshua's mindset cost him his heavyweight world titles.

The Brit lost his three belts to Andy Ruiz Jr in one of the sport's biggest upsets in New York in June.

Wilder, meanwhile, retained his by knocking out Dominic Breazeale inside a round and returns to action on Saturday week in his rematch with Luis Ortiz.

And he explained why he believes he still reigns while Joshua must win back his crown.

"Me and Joshua have a different mindset. Mine is strong, way stronger than his," Wilder told Sky Sports .

"Joshua knew he would lose one day," Wilder said. "When it was time he handed his belts over to Ruiz Jr - he literally transferred the belts to this man.

"For me? My mindset is different. I'm a totally different king, a totally different beast.

"I am the best in the world and I prove it each time I go in the ring.

"I'm not worried about making mistakes and, if I do, rest assured I will correct it as the fight goes on.

"I'm fighting for one champion, one face, one name - the [undisputed] champion. Nobody will get in my way, nobody."

Wilder has fought 42 times as a professional - but plans to fight on for six years.

“Six years is my max that I’m planning to finish my career,” he said.

“In the beginning of my career, we had a plan of things to move forward with.

"I can’t say things went exactly as planned. In life, things don’t go as planned. It’s a great to be with a group of guys that have my best interest at heart.

"The people who were planning the beginning of my career are planning the ending as well. I think the ending is going to be a little bit sweeter than the beginning.

"In the beginning you have a long task ahead of you and you don’t know where the roads will lead. I did the best I can in the heavyweight division. I brought a lot of excitement. I bring a lot of drama."
