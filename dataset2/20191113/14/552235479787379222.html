Alexandria man Reuben flown back to Scotland after Bali horror crash

The Vale born IT worker has been supported by people from throughout the area, and his work colleagues.

13 Nov 2019, 14:46 by Lorraine Weir

An Alexandria man left fighting for his life after a horror motorbike crash in Bali was due to return to Scotland on Monday.

Reuben Armstrong boarded a flight on Sunday, headed for Glasgow, where he will continue treatment following the incident on the holiday island he calls his second home.

The Lennox Herald reported how the Vale-born IT worker had to undergo emergency surgery after suffering a severe head injury.

But he is now back on home soil with mum Diane, from Tullichewan.

 

His best friend Lincoln Savage, who set up a Justgiving page to help pay for treatment in Bali and his repatriation, shared the good news with us and said: “It just makes you realise how lucky we are in this country to have the healthcare we do.

“At this point in the UK, we would just be trying to give him our support.

“The added stress of having to raise extra funds has been really difficult.

“We have had to raise a lot of funds to get him back by a repatriation specialist which is a ridiculous amount of money.”

 

But Lincoln, who has known Reuben for the last decade, paid tribute to the company the pair work for, Equals, who have contributed to the funds.

He said: “We both work in London and the company we’ve worked for has never seen anything like it.

“On the day, they saved his life. If we didn’t get that money from our CEO, we don’t know what would have happened.

“Since then they have been helping me with the loan to pay the medical bills while raising money in the background.

“The whole company also donated a day’s wages which was about £13,000. They cancelled the Christmas party and all sorts.

“It’s a testament to who he is. He’s really well liked and everyone at work has been saying he’s such a lovely guy. He’s always chatting to people.”

 

The 27-year-old was out with his friend on his motorbike when he crashed, hitting his head on a wall.

He suffered frontal lobe damage in the smash and was initially in a coma before undergoing brain surgery.

Because of the effect of the trauma on Reuben’s brain, he has become very agitated and is expected to resume treatment at Gartnavel Hospital following the 7500-mile flight.

Lincoln, 29, said: “Surprisingly, he’s doing well.

“Physically he’s in good condition but he’s still struggling to come to terms with what’s happened.

“The shock for him of waking up in the hospital in Bali, with his mum and gran has been really confusing.”

 

Reuben, who attended Levenvale Primary moved to Grimsby before setting up home in London 10 years ago. He split his time between there and Bali where he had just put a deposit down on a flat before the crash.

He wasn’t wearing a helmet, which is fairly standard in Bali, when he came off his motorbike.

His insurance company refused to pay out as a result and so family and friends had to launch a mass fundraising campaign to pay medical bills there and to get him home.

This included a fundraiser earlier this month in Grimsby and various events at his workplace.

A Justgiving page set up by Lincoln has also managed to raise over £38,000.
