Rylan Clark-Neal collapses to the floor and sobs after Children In Need challenge

The teary scenes mirrored his infamous X Factor meltdown in front of Nicole Scherzinger

13 Nov 2019, 14:33 by Lucy Needham, https://www.facebook.com/dailymirror/

Rylan Clark Neal collapsed to the floor in tears after completing his exhausting 24 hours Karaoke challenge for Children In Need.

The It Takes Two host had been tasked with singing for a whole day and night while Radio 2 listeners spurred him on with their donations.

At 9:15am on Wednesday morning, Rylan's stint finally came to an end with a rousing rendition of Tina Turner's The Best.

For his finale hurrah, Rylan was joined by his Strictly co-presenter Zoe Ball and Pudsey Bear himself as they counted down the final seconds on the clock.

Radio 2 Breakfast host Zoe then confirmed that Rylan had helped raise £845,239, causing the shattered star to burst into tears and drop to his knees.

During his last hour, Rylan had been surprised in the studio by M People's Heather Smalls and Craig David and the Saturdays' Frankie Bridge also popped by.

Rylan was particularly ecstatic to meet Craig, who duetted with him on 90s anthem Fill me In.

"Oh my God. This is unbelievable, I can’t believe it. This is not actually happening," Rylan cried. "Craig knows how much of a fan I am and I’ve met him before but to do that, I’m actually on cloud nine right now."

It's not the first time Rylan has showed off his vocal skills in public.

The new Supermarket Sweep host became a household name following his controversial appearance on the X Factor .

Rylan was picked to be in Nicole Scherzinger's category back in 2012.

Despite his frequent clashes with Gary Barlow, Rylan became a firm fan favourite and finished the series in fifth place.

Since then Rylan's career has gone from strength to strength and he's even buried the hatchet with his former nemesis.

Last month the pair ran into each other at a showbiz party but there was no bad blood.

Rylan and Gary were pictured cuddling up together and shared the snap on social media the next morning.

Former X Factor hopeful Rylan tweeted the sweet snap, captioning it: "After 7 years..... we're good."

He added a red heart and tagged Gary in it, who responded with a thumbs up emoji and another heart.
