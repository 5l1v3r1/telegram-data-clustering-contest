War medals and priceless jewellery stolen from Plymouth home

Devon and Cornwall Police is appealing for information after the 'high value' burglary

13 Nov 2019, 14:37 by Molly Dowrick

War medals and priceless jewellery have been stolen from a Plymouth home in a heartless burglary, police have revealed.

The 'high value' burglary has seen a significant amount of jewellery, several watches, two parachute regiment cap badges and two war medals taken from a property on Larkham Lane in Plympton.

The incident occurred on Thursday, September 26, between 8.30 and 5.30pm.

Now, Devon and Cornwall Police has released further information about the burglary and is appealing for information from the public.

Devon and Cornwall Police says entry to the property was 'forced' before the 'high value' burglary took place.

Police say the victim, a man in his 50s, was informed by his family that a number of items, including two war medals and high value jewellery, had been taken from his home.

Investigating officer DC Jason Rowe said: "A significant amount of jewellery and valuable items were taken from this property during this incident back in September.

"The victim, a man in his 50s, was informed by his family that a number of items had been taken from the address.

"These items range from watches and earrings, to medals and gold sovereign coins and are of sentimental value to the victim.
