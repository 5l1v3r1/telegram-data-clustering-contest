Teen who arranged for boyfriend to rape girl, 12, moans 'people call me a nonce'

Chloe Bradley, who was 17 at the time, says she wishes "this whole thing would go away" after she arranged for her 27-year-old boyfriend, Anthony Roscoe, to rape the 12-year-old girl

13 Nov 2019, 08:01 by Luke Traynor

A teenager who arranged for her 27-year-old boyfriend to rape a 12-year-old girl has said "I did wrong" and revealed that "everyone is calling me a nonce".

Chloe Bradley, then 17, conspired with Anthony Roscoe, 27, to bring the child to his home, where he had sex with the girl and Bradley in quick succession.

Predator Roscoe, jailed for four years for rape, later told stunned detectives: “When somebody hands you the Holy Grail, you’re going to grab it, aren’t you?”

Now, after Bradley, who supposedly has the emotional age of a 10-year-old, avoided jail for her part in the rape in August 2017, she has spoken to the Liverpool Echo about her actions and says she has “learned her lesson.”

 

She said: “I accept I did something wrong. I’ve done my punishment.

“Everyone is calling me a nonce.

“I got a suspended sentence and I’ve learned my lesson. I was hanging around with the wrong people.”

Bolton Crown Court heard how Bradley messaged her on-off boyfriend Roscoe, from St Helens, Merseyside, about a rendezvous and then escorted the young girl to his home.

He alternated between having sex with Bradley and raping the 12-year-old victim, Bolton Crown Crown court heard.

Police became involved by chance when a passing patrol spotted the girls leaving Roscoe’s home at 5.30am the next day.

The 17-year-old’s phone was examined and was found to contain conversations between herself and Roscoe.

Bradley, who served six months on remand in Styal Prison in Cheshire, described that experience as “horrible.”

The Wigan teenager, who lives with her grandparents in Worsley Hall, said she met Roscoe online before he began to send her regular text messages.

Speaking to the Echo yesterday, Bradley added: “In my head I’m 19 [she was 17 at the time of the offence] but I’m not an adult, I’m still a kid.

“I just wish this whole thing would go away. I’d make different choices.

“Roscoe isn’t the best of people, but I thought he was an alright lad before this happened. I’ve changed my opinion now.

“It’s not good, everyone knows where I live now, and word has got round the estate.

“I’m close to my grandparents and I’m sorry for what I did.”

Bradley pleaded guilty to arranging the commission of a child sex offence and was given 22 months’ jail, suspended for a year.

She was also ordered to wear an electronic tag for three months and sign the Sex Offender Register for 10 years.

In mitigation for Roscoe, Michael Hagerty said: “He believed the complainant was 16 and the sexual activity was with ostensible consent.

“Both Bradley and the complainant went to the defendant’s house, rather than the other way round. It’s clear both were of the clear intention of having sex with the defendant.

In mitigation for Bradley, Paul Woods said: “She was 17 when the offences were committed.

“Her functional age and her emotional and cognitive behaviour are described as that of a 10-year-old.

“She is a very vulnerable individual.”

Sentencing, Judge Graeme Smith said: “You told officers in your interview: ‘When somebody hands you the Holy Grail, you’re going to grab it, aren’t you?’.

“I am quite satisfied the prospect of a threesome blinded you to everything else.”

Roscoe was also made subject of a Sexual Harm Prevention Order for five years and ordered to sign the Sex Offender Register indefinitely.

Bradley was also made subject of a Sexual Harm Prevention Order for five years.
