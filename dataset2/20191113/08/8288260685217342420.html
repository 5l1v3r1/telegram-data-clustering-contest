Smyths Toys customers convinced 2019 Christmas advert sees boy say 'fat b*****d'

It joins the likes of Sainsbury's, Asda and Aldi - as well as Debenhams, Very and Argos - in setting the tone ahead of December

13 Nov 2019, 08:27 by James Rodger, https://www.facebook.com/jamesrodgerjournalist

Stunned TV viewers have been left utterly convinced a Christmas advert for the 2019 festive season features a rude word.

Many shoppers, customers and consumers have been left commenting on Smyths Toys' advert.

The ad for the toy firm, which operates stores across Birmingham and the Black Country, was released this week.

It joins the likes of Sainsbury's, Asda and Aldi - as well as Debenhams, Very and Argos - in setting the tone ahead of December.

But eagle-eyed fans of the brand have been left speculating it contains an "unacceptable" word.

The 40-second trailer features a young boy called Oscar.

In the ad, Oscar sings about his dream of being a toy instead of human to the tune of Beyonce's 2008 hit 'If I Were a Boy'.

When little Oscar points a toy gun at Pokemon character Pikachu as he whizzes around his local branch of Smyths Toys Superstores, he says: "I'd master the fart blaster for sure."

But speculation on social media has asked whether he instead says: "I’d master the fat b*****d for sure".

 

"Your advert is totally unacceptable for a child's toy store with it calling someone a fat b*****d," one customer fumed at Smyths.

ASA have responded to confirm they did receive a complaint about the ad as the complainant believed it sounded like the boy who was singing uses a swear word.

"Please someone tell me what the lyrics are to the smyths advert because all I can hear is "I've mastered the fat b*****d," another wrote.

A third chipped in with: "Is it me or does the little boy on the smyths advert call pikachu a fat b*****d."
