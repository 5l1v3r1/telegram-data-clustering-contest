The World’s First Instagram Bee Calls on Followers to Save Its Species

13 Nov 2019, 05:30

Instagram gained a new fabulous influencer and her name is B. She draws attention with perfect beach photos, traveling shots, and details of her everyday life. One more thing you need to know — B is a bee! And through her Instagram account, she also raises funds to save her species and discusses why bees are so important!

So here at Bright Side we did a little investigation and found out why B is so worth following!

The little bee celebrity, whose name is B, already has over 160k followers and every attribute of a regular celebrity Instagram — beach photos, outfits of the day, yoga snaps, bathroom morning routines, and traveling selfies.

But the first insect Insta-blogger also has an important message behind all these pics - B was created by Fondation de France in the summer 2019 to raise awareness and create a fundraising platform in order to save this insect from extinction.

The bio says "Follow me guys: the more followers I have, the more bees we will save!" As the Fondation de France has stated, all money raised will go to the bee fund to finance the rebuilding of habitats and educate people on the importance of bees in the global ecosystem.

Bees are not just about honey, thanks to their pollination we have fruits, berries, nuts, and even roots and seeds. Here's what little B says "So, name a thing you love. Chocolate? I make it. Ice cream? I make it. Coffee? Always me. Broccoli? Me again. Clothes? Still me. We are behind most of the things you use every day!"

The Fondation de France, which is the largest French philanthropic organization, was founded in 1969 and now works not only within the country, but also throughout Europe, uniting specialists, volunteers, donors, and field operatives. According to the foundation, in France alone approximately 30% of bee colonies become extinct every year.

Among other tasks, the fund also raises awareness about the harmful influence of pesticides on the insects and helps farmers switch to safe fertilizers. According to the organization "Friends of the Earth" the UK alone has lost 13 species of bees since the 1900s and the remaining 35 are now also in danger of extinction.

This little bee shares the small achievements of the fund and details of their work, like the update on upcoming partnerships. So if you're interested in being a part of this, you can even send В a direct message and help the fund!
