Arsenal 'hand trial' to MLS wonderkid Cole Bassett over prospective transfer

Colorado Rapids teenager is to spend a week with Gunners' under-23s and hopes to show he can be a first-team player for Unai Emery's side

13 Nov 2019, 05:04 by Gareth Bicknell

Arsenal could be set to sign MLS rising star Cole Bassett after handing the Colorado Rapids midfielder a week-long trial.

Bassett, 18, is a Gunners fan and is expected to train with Arsenal's under-18s during the international break.

The teenager is hoping to impress in a bid to break into Unai Emery's first-team plans, according to the Denver Post .

“As most people already know, this is my favourite club," he told the publication.

"I don’t know which team it will be because it’s an international break for them.

 

"Hopefully, there will be a couple of guys missing with the first team and I can get with them.

“But I’m just excited to get over there and show up basically.

Bassett was impressed by Arsenal when they played a friendly against Rapids.

“When Arsenal came over here and played us, you could see there are little things that they do that I just don’t quite have yet,” Bassett said.

“I’m eager to learn from them. Picking up things from every player will help improve my game. It’s going to be a great learning experience.”

Colorado Rapids are owned by the Kroenkes, who also own Arsenal - and that would help grease the wheels of the transfer if Bassett impresses during his trial.

Bassett broke into the Rapids' first team for the 2019 season after working his way through the club's youth system.

He made 20 appearances in his breakthrough season, scoring twice.

Rapids have already rejected interest in the player, with the Denver Post claiming that Freiburg made an offer for him after Bassett trained with the German club.

According to football stats website Transfermarkt he is valued at just £600,000 - so could prove a bargain if Arsenal decide to swoop for him in the January transfer window and Rapids can be persuaded to let him go.
