Narayana Murthy’s son Rohan Murty to marry Aparna Krishnan

13 Nov 2019, 23:00 by Shilpa Phadnis

Highlights

  • The wedding on December 2 will be a private ceremony with close family members in Bengaluru
  • The Murthy family will host a reception at a luxury hotel the same evening

BENGALURU: It’s wedding bells for Rohan Murty. Infosys co-founder NR Narayana Murthy’s son is marrying Aparna Krishnan, daughter of Kochi-based Savithri Krishnan, a retired SBI employee, and Commander KR Krishnan. The two met through a common friend in Mumbai about three years ago.

The wedding on December 2 will be a private ceremony with close family members in Bengaluru. The Murthy family will host a reception at a luxury hotel the same evening. “It’s a meeting of minds and values,” a source close to the family said.

Aparna did much of her schooling in India. When she was 15, she got a full-scholarship to study at the United World College in Canada where she completed her high school. She later did a bachelor of arts in economics from Dartmouth College, a US private Ivy League research university. She worked briefly at McKinsey and Sequoia Capital in Bengaluru.

Rohan grew up in India and attended Cornell University as an undergraduate. He received a PhD in computer science from Harvard University and is a junior fellow at Society of Fellows at Harvard University. He has been a computing innovations fellow at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT). Rohan has written several academic papers and his research interests span networked systems, embedded computing, and distributed computing systems.

In 2010, he founded the Murty Classical Library of India with a generous gift to Harvard University. The library aims to reintroduce great Indian literary works over the past two millennia to a new generation. Rohan is currently setting up a computer science research lab, sources said.
