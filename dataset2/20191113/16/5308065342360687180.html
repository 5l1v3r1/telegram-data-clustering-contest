Prince Harry and Prince William meets for the first time since rift

13 Nov 2019, 16:20

- Advertisement -

It’s been a while since Prince Harry and Prince William met following rumours of their rift and now they have appeared for their first event together.

Prince Harry admitted last month that he and Prince William were “on different paths at the moment” in his royal tour documentary for ITV.

For the Festival of Remembrance at London’s Royal Albert Hall on Saturday, the brothers were accompanied by their wives Meghan Markle and Kate Middleton.

Ever since Prince Harry confirmed that there was a rift between him and his brother, they have just made their first appearance together.

- Advertisement -

At the annual Festival of Remembrance at the London’s Royal Albert Hall on Saturday evening, the “Fab Four” were reunited.

Prince William and Kate and Prince Harry and Meghan entered the venue separately and were not seated together.

Prince Harry finally confirmed that he and Prince William were not in good terms in an interview last month.

In the ITV documentary, “Harry and Meghan: An African Journey,” the prince revealed that the brothers are “on different paths at the moment.”

“Part of this role and part of this job, this family, being under the pressure that it’s under, inevitably stuff happens,” he said.

“But look, we’re brothers, we’ll always be brothers,” he added. “We’re certainly on different paths at the moment but I’ll always be there for him and as I know he’ll always be there for me. ”

Meghan was dressed in a beautiful black dress and her hair combed back into an updo while Kate donned a navy dress with a glitzy black headband.

The ladies wore poppy pins to mark Remembrance week.

The event is to commemorate all those who have lost their lives in conflict.

The Queen and other royal members of the royal family were present for the event.
