Hopes are high as two Dumbarton football clubs unite

On Saturday Dumbarton FC and Dumbarton United officially revealed a new agreement that will see United’s youngsters handed the chance to start on a pathway to senior football with West Dunbartonshire’s only SPFL side.

13 Nov 2019, 16:38 by Fraser Clarke, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/fraser-clarke/

Chiefs at two of Dumbarton’s oldest football clubs hope that a new partnership can be beneficial to both.

On Saturday Dumbarton FC and Dumbarton United officially revealed a new agreement that will see United’s youngsters handed the chance to start on a pathway to senior football with West Dunbartonshire’s only SPFL side.

The move came about via local business C&G Systems, who are major sponsors of both clubs.

And Sons chairman John Steele was quick to pay tribute to the company for putting the foundations for the partnership in place.

 

He said: “When C&G came in as stadium sponsors, they were keen that the Dumbarton community became more involved with us.

“They’ve organised the whole relationship and I’m delighted that it’s happening.

“Without being immodest, when I started being involved there was an us and them situation between the board and our supporters, and the same situation existed between the club and the community.

“I’ve been keen to change that dynamic and we’re taking small steps now in achieving that.”

United chairman Gerry Docherty echoed Steele’s thoughts and highlighted the benefits that the new agreement has for both sides.

He told the Lennox: “We’ve been working in the local community for 61 years and we’d been in touch with Dumbarton FC over that time.

“Over the period we’ve tried to do this a number of times but there’s never been anybody to link the clubs together.

“I think it’s just the right timing to link the clubs. Everything has a timing and I think just now is just the perfect timing for everybody.

“It’s a massive opportunity for us to interphase with the club and it’s also a good opportunity for the senior club to interphase with the local community.

“But it’s a partnership. It’s not a one-way street, there will be lots of benefits for our players looking to progress into senior football.”

 

C&G Systems’ commercial director, Andy Gordon, expanded on the positives for Dumbarton United.

He said: “Dumbarton United’s oldest age group at the moment is under-16, but adopting a player pathway they’ll be able to have an under-19s again because it’s very difficult to retain players when they will just go to other clubs who do.

“It’s a hugely positive step for both clubs.

“When we started sponsoring Dumbarton FC, David Lavery from Dumbarton United Blacks 2005s got in touch and asked if we’d like to sponsor them, and a big thing for my brother, Dave Gordon MD at C&G, was to get involved in grassroots sport in Dumbarton and a be a bit more front facing in the community.

“This partnership is very, very positive for Dumbarton to have an outward facing link to the community and for Dumbarton United.”

Sons’ chairman Steele concluded: “We have to engage with young
people because if we don’t engage with them then the future for clubs like us is not good.

 

“The elderly supporters that are our lifeblood just now will not be there and ultimately the club will not be there.

“So it’s essential that we work with the community and get them involved with us.”
