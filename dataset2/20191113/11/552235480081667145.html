This house looks onto the twin of Ailsa Craig

You can see the "fake Ailsa" from your window

13 Nov 2019, 11:19 by Stephen Houston

In days of yore, travellers on foot or horseback thought they were nearing their destination.

Ailsa Craig was a helpful compass of its era as people headed to the coast.

And they mistook Knockdolian hill for it, only to realise the volcanic plug of Paddy’s Milestane was about 13  miles distant.

Knockdolian, at 869 feet high, is the dominant feature of the Stinchar Valley landscape.

You get amazing views of the “fake Ailsa” from this rural bungalow, pictured below, called Auchenclery.

It has a splendid elevated position about a mile and a half from the conservation village of Colmonell, so stomping distance for a thirst quencher at the Boars Head.

Galloway & Ayrshire Properties (GAP) has just put the house to market.

Auchenclery is an all-on-the-level two-bedder with a wonderful panoramic view west to Knockdolian.

The house is 1960’s built and enjoys gravel parking to the front and a big lawn at the back.

The dominant room is the lounge, with a floor to ceiling window, varnished wooden floor and a fireplace with log burner.

Heating throughout is by oil as there are no gas mains in the countryside.

You get a spacious kitchen diner - with sliding doors to the raised patio - with a separate neuk for a dining table. This area has faux Tudor beams which are an acquired taste of another era.

A utiling room leads to the side garden via a handy porch.

Unusually in a house of this size you get a big bathroom ensuite to one of the bedrooms and a separate shower room.

The attached garage is 21 foot by 12 foot and also houses the oil boiler, and has light and power. It could easily become an extension to the living accomodation.

Auchenclery is bounded by a fine old drystane dyke.

GAP on 01465 713498 is handling the sale, offers over £143,750.
