'Talent is there' - Lowdown on Jack Clarke as Nottingham Forest are linked with Leeds United man

Latest Nottingham Forest transfer news | The Reds have recently been linked with a move for the Tottenham Hotspur forward who is currently on loan at Leeds United

13 Nov 2019, 17:54 by Mark Wakefield

Nottingham Forest have recently been recently been linked with a move for Jack Clarke when the January transfer window opens.

The 18-year-old is currently on loan at Leeds United from Premier League side Tottenham Hotspur, although he has seen little game time.

According to Sporting Life, he is “likely” to be recalled by his parent club in January as, it is claimed, a couple of other Championship clubs “have expressed an interest in taking him for the second half of the season”.

It is said the Reds could be one of those teams interested as they may look to add another winger when the window opens.

So what do we know about the latest player linked with a move to the City Ground?

The basic info

Jack Clarke is a wide forward/winger at Spurs, currently on loan at Championship outfit Leeds.

Clarke was born in York on March 23, 2000 and started his career with the Whites as he progressed through the club’s youth set up.

He stands at 5ft11” tall and has been capped four times at England Under-20 level in his relatively young career.

Career so far

Clarke worked his way through the youth ranks at Elland Road to eventually make his first-team debut for the Whites in October 2018 against Brentford in the Championship.

Under manager Marcelo Bielsa, Clarke was named on the substitute’s bench and replaced Stuart Dallas in the 70th minute – he was 17 years old when he made his debut.

His first goal would come just over two months later when he scored Leeds’ first goal in a 3-2 win over Aston Villa at Villa Park.

Leeds were trailing that game 2-0, but Clarke’s strike started the comeback which enabled the Whites to claim all three points in B6.

He would go on to to make 47 appearances for Leeds in his debut season in the first-team, with 27 of those coming in the starting line-up.

Clarke’s performances impressed in the Premier League as Tottenham saw fit to sign the 18-year-old on a deal until 2023, with Spurs paying around £10m for his services.

Mauricio Pochettino then loaned Clarke back to Leeds for the 2019/20 season - but he has rarely featured this term.

He is yet to make an appearance for Leeds in the Championship this season, with both of his two outings for the Whites coming in the Carabao Cup.

What could Clarke add to Forest?

There is no doubting Clarke’s ability to beat a defender on the wing and cause serious problems for the opposition.

Spurs saw that talent and decided to take a chance and sign him up, but they also know he is not the finished article.

Two goals in his debut season is still a pretty low return for a player in a team who have large amounts of possession.

At 18-years-old, you do not expect him to be the complete player – if he was, he would be in one of the top leagues in Europe.

The talent is there, and a loan spell at the City Ground could provide an opportunity to home his skills, as well as boost Forest’s attacking prowess.
