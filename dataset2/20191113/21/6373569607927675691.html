Plymouth Argyle miss out on a place in the last 32 of the EFL Trophy

Bristol Rovers denied the Pilgrims a spot in the knockout stage by winning their final group game

13 Nov 2019, 21:34 by Chris Errington

Plymouth Argyle are out of the Leasing.com Trophy after Bristol Rovers won the final game in Southern Group B.

Graham Coughlan’s Rovers beat Swindon Town 1-0 at the Memorial Stadium on Wednesday night.

It is the third consecutive season that Argyle have gone out of the competition before the knockout stage.

The Pilgrims finished third in the group, with Rovers ending up top of the table and Chelsea Under-21s also qualifying for the last 32 in second spot.

Swindon were bottom of the table without picking up a point from their three games.

The Robins were captained by former Argyle goalkeeper Luke McCormick against Rovers, while ex-Pilgrims’ midfielder Toumani Diagouraga was also in their starting line-up.

The only goal of the game came in the 72nd minute and was scored by Mark Little.

Argyle manager Ryan Lowe led Bury to the semi-finals of the EFL Trophy last season but could not guide the Pilgrims past the group stage this term.
