Another SAFA Official Accepts Role Elsewhere 

13 Nov 2019, 13:00 by Soccer Laduma

Another South African Football Association official has accepted a new role elsewhere, though on part-time basis. 

Following this month’s exit of Russell Paul, who has been the acting CEO at SAFA, to take up a role as tournament director of the 2022 Qatar World Cup, it has been confirmed that Sudesh Singh has accepted a role to help the Zambia senior national team in their Africa Cup of Nations qualifying campaign.

Zambia are scheduled to play against Algeria on Thursday, as well as against Zimbabwe next Tuesday.

Among South African-based players called up by coach Aggrey Chiyangi in Zambia’s squad are Kaizer Chiefs’ Lazarous Kambole and the Orlando Pirates duo of Augustine Mulenga and Justin Shonga.
