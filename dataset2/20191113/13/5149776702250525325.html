Anna Soubry given award for leaving Conservatives and standing up to abuse

She has been subjected to threats because of her anti-Brexit stance

13 Nov 2019, 13:59 by Matthew Bunn

Change UK leader and parliamentary candidate for Broxtowe Anna Soubry has won an award for "political courage".

The former Conservative MP, who had an 863-vote majority at the 2017 election, has actively campaigned for a 'People's Vote' on the Brexit referendum and was subjected to threats because of her stance.

Last month she warned "good people" would not want to stand at the 2019 election because of fear of similar attacks .

She was presented with the award by the Political Studies Association for leaving her party - in February - to uphold her values and for standing up to abuse and threats. She was presented with the award alongside Luciana Berger, who has suffered anti-semitic abuse during her career.

Ms Berger is now a member of the Liberal Democrats after leaving the Labour Party and Change UK this year.

Ms Soubry said: "I am hugely grateful, but it says a lot about the state of British political life at the moment that standing up to racist anti-semitism, as Luciana has done, and my refusing to give in to threats and abuse, merits a special award".
