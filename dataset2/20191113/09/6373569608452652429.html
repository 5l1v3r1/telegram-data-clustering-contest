Apprentice star Claude Littner sparks fury after suggesting nurses using foodbanks should 'work more'

His Twitter comments said nurses struggling on low pay should take second jobs

13 Nov 2019, 09:53 by Talia Shadwell, Matthew Dresch, Jamie Hawkins

Apprentice star Claude Littner has sparked anger after suggesting nurses struggling to get by should work more.

Claude, who is an advisor on Lord Alan Sugar's BBC One show, said that nurses who are low-paid should take second jobs.

His comments saying it would be "less demeaning than visiting a food bank" saw the British-American businessman come under fire, The Mirror reports.

The 70-year-old then doubled down on his views on struggling nurses on Twitter despite a flood of criticism.

He wrote: "If a nurse or anyone else need to make ends meet, nothing wrong with extending hours or finding part-time work."

Many critics were quick to point out that many NHS nurses already work long hours for low pay, and their shift work affected their ability to find second jobs.

But Mr Littner wrote there was: "Nothing wrong with extending hours or finding part time work."

His comments come as reports of nurses forced to resort to using Britain's foodbanks.
