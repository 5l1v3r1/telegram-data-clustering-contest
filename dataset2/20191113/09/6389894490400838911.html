UK inflation cools to slowest rate in almost three years

13 Nov 2019, 09:39 by Harry Robertson

UK price inflation has fallen to its slowest rate in almost three years in October, official figures showed today, in the latest sign that the economy is cooling.

The consumer price index (CPI) – a gauge of how much prices have risen over the year – 1.5 per cent in October, down from 1.7 per cent in September, the Office for National Statistics (ONS) said.

The figures mean inflation has slowed to well below the Bank of England’s target rate of two per cent, increasing the chances that Threadneedle Street could soon cut interest rates.

Slowing price growth for furniture, household equipment and maintenance, and recreation and culture acted as a drag. It was partially offset by rises in clothing and footwear prices, however.
