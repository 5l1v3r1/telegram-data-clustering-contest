Luke Pollard's office attacked again with more homophobic graffiti

'I am not going to be made a victim of by people with hate in their hearts'

13 Nov 2019, 09:33 by Sarah Waddington

MP candidate Luke Pollard has had his city centre office attacked again overnight with more homophobic graffiti.

At the end of October Mr Pollard's Frankfort Gate office was vandalised twice in one week.

On October 27 an anti-gay slur was daubed on the Labour politician's office windows, after previously being vandalised previously seven days before .

At the time he praised the "most incredible and positive reaction from people in Plymouth" in response to the incident.

But now his office has been vandalised yet again, with the MP candidate, who is up for re-election on December 12 in the forthcoming general election, saying he is "not going to be made a victim of by people with hate in their hearts".

Mr Pollard said: "My office was graffitied again last night with more homophobic slurs. I've offered to sit down with the individuals who have done this to listen to their concerns; that request has been ignored and they've clearly sent a message here again that they want to continue their homophobic attacks on me.

"I'm not going to be made a victim of by people who have hate in their hearts, nor am I going to let my staff that work so hard from this office, helping people with homelessness, with Universal Credit problems, with housing issues be threatened and feel unsafe going to work."
