Man jailed for 10 months for possessing 32,000 xanax tablets

13 Nov 2019, 01:00

A 40-year-old north Belfast man has been jailed for 10 months for possessing 32,000 Xanax tablets.

Several tubs filled with the Class C drug were found during a police search of a house in the Newington Street area of Belfast in November 2017.

Sean Rooney was linked to the find via fingerprints, and when his flat at Oceanic Avenue was subsequently searched, 28 grams of herbal cannabis was found in the property.

Despite initial denials, Rooney admitted to possessing the cannabis and to possessing the Xanax with intent to supply.

Belfast Crown Court heard Rooney turned to drugs and began to self-medicate following a family tragedy, and has since regretted his involvement with possessing the Xanax as he has seen the destructive affect these drugs have on people.

Crown prosecutor Philip Henry told Judge Stephen Fowler QC that the 32,000 Xanax tablets were located under the stairs of a house which was unoccupied and undergoing renovation and which was searched on November 17, 2017.

When officers searched Rooney's apartment five months later, on April 5 2018, small amounts of cannabis were found at various locations.

Rooney was arrested the day his flat was searched, and while he admitted possessing the Xanax tablets, he made the case he had been storing the tablets in exchange for Â£100 to clear his drugs debt.

Defence barrister Joel Lindsay said Rooney suffered a family tragedy in 2013 which resulted in him self-medicating.

Regarding the Xanax, Mr Lindsay said it was his client's case that he was handed a couple of bin bags which he stored but that he had "no idea" there were so many tablets and "sheer panic set in" when he realised the number involved.

Judge Fowler said: "It's clear the reason for him becoming involved in this is to fund his own drug habit - a significant addiction to cannabis."

The judge also spoke of "the scourge of these drugs in society and the misery it heaps on others" and ordered the tablets and cannabis seized be destroyed.
