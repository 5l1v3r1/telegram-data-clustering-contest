Space travelling teddy returns to Newmains school after month on space station

Pupils at St Brigid’s Primary welcomed back “Space Teddy” who had completed a full month on board the International Space Station as part of a NASA project.

13 Nov 2019, 06:00 by Gregor Farquharson, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/gregor-farquharson/

A mission to space proved no more than a teddy bear’s picnic for one little school mascot.

On Friday, pupils at St Brigid’s Primary in Newmains welcomed back “Space Teddy” who had completed a full month on board the international space station as part of a NASA project.

The cuddly toy, complete with school tie, left Newmains with the astronauts last year as part of a scheme organised by the Scottish Space School program at University of Strathclyde.

Marianne Ballantyne, department manager of Electronic and Electrical Engineering at the university, said: “As part of the space school programme, NASA visit schools within a one-hour drive of Glasgow.

“Joel Montalbano, who is the deputy ISS program manager, asked the school to provide a small memento to go to space and the school gave him Teddy.

“Nothing goes to space without him saying so and he stuck to his word and got it taken up.”

Space Teddy travelled 12.7 million miles between May 4 and June 3 before being returned to the school last week.

Pupil Erin McLaughin, 11, said: “I wasn’t here to see the teddy go but it is cool to see it come back to Earth.”

Emil Filipczak, 10, said: “It is good entertainment for people, it gives us a story to tell everyone.”

Maya Robertson, 12, was in primary seven when the teddy went away. She went back to visit when he returned.

She said: “It is good to see Teddy back after that journey.”
