Piers Morgan clashes with GMB guest who claims Rule Britannia should be banned

The Apprentice star Joanna Jarjue suggested the song Rule Britannia should be banned, after singer Lily Allen called for the move

13 Nov 2019, 12:23 by Charlotte Tutton

Good Morning Britain presenter Piers Morgan hit out at The Apprentice star Joanna Jarjue, after she backed claims the patriotic song Rule Britannia should be banned.

The debate took place on the show on Wednesday, after singer Lily Allen called for the banning of the song via her Instagram.

She received a backlash from followers over the claims, with Piers also blasting the singer on the show today.

But someone in support of the comments was Joanna, who commented that the lyrics were not appropriate.

Piers wasn't happy with her claims though, defending it as reminder "we’re a great country".

 

Joanne told him: "The song is written in the middle of the 1700s and that was when Britain was fully partaking in the slave trade.

"So to have lyrics like rule Britannia and Britons will never be slaves while then fully participating in slavery, in 2019 I find that completely outdated."

Piers asked: "So you would ban it too would you?"

She replied: "Yeah I would, well banning it completely... if people want to kind of sing it in their own private homes..."

A baffled Piers then asked her: "You’d ban it from the airwaves, we'd never play it in public again?

"One of the greatest patriotic anthems this nation has ever had."

Joanna said: "I don’t think we should be playing it and singing it on things like Remembrance," questioning why it was "patriotic".

Outraged, Piers told her: "Because it stirs the soul and it reminds us of the fact we’re a great country."

It's then that he was left calling her out again, when she admitted the song gave her a chill and said: "What I think is it reminds us of colonial rule.

"When I listen to it especially on days like Remembrance Day I think it's a little bit insensitive, I think for the fallen soldiers, especially the ones from the Commonwealth."

Piers hit back: "What, to celebrate them winning the World War? For risking and sacrificing their lives for us? It's insensitive?

"You realise the ones that survived the war all sing that song proudly don't you?"

Good Morning Britain airs weekdays at 6am on ITV.
