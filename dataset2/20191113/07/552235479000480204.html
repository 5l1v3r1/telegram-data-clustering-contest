Arsenal 'need Steven Gerrard' as club legend wants Rangers boss to replace under-fire Unai Emery

Emery has been publicly backed by the Arsenal board but they are eight points adrift of a top-four finish and winless in their last four league matches.

13 Nov 2019, 07:38 by Graeme Young, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/graeme-young/

Arsenal hero Emmanuel Petit insists the time is now for Arsenal to move on from beleaguered boss Unai Emery.

And the Frenchman reckons Steven Gerrard would be a perfect candidate to take the reins at the Emirates with the Spaniard feeling the heat.

Emery has been publicly backed by the Arsenal board but they are eight points adrift of a top-four finish and winless in their last four league matches.

The Rangers manager, who has made no secret of his desire to bring trophies to Ibrox, was named by Jurgen Klopp as his Liverpool heir apparent in September.

But Petit insists the Gunners should also be keen on the 39-year-old who has turned heads with the job he is doing north of the border.

Gerrard has Rangers competing on four fronts as they look to seal a place in the last 32 of the Europa League. They will also face title rivals Celtic in the Final of the Betfred Cup next month.

And the French World Cup winner is convinced the Anfield icon is just the man to reignite the Arsenal dressing room after a wretched start to the season.

Petit told Paddy Power: “Arsenal needs a great leader now,”

“Someone who can bring back the passion, someone respected by the players, someone who can fill the team with energy and the winning mentality.

“Steven Gerrard, for example, is like this.

“I want to see passion, emotion. Someone who, when he takes your hand, you can feel the electricity.

“I want someone who can bring that to the Arsenal dressing room, because they need to wake up.

"My opinion is that most of the players have given up on Emery, so it's time for him to go."
