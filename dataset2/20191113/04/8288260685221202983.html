Chelsea star makes Wesley admission

Chelsea star Willian has revealed he has been following Wesley's early Premier League performances ahead of his maiden Brazil call up.

The Aston Villa striker was a surprise name in Brazil's latest squad for the friendly fixtures against Argentina and North Korea.

Since arriving on B6 for a club-record £22m fee, Wesley has netted four times in 12 top flight performances, but still has his fair share of claret and blue doubters.

A number of fans continue to call for Dean Smith to look at other options to lead the line, including splashing the cash in the January window.

 

Newcastle handed major boost for Villa clash

Whilst Villa are in the midst of a major injury crisis, their next opponents Newcastle United may be about to receive a huge fitness boost.

Dean Smith ’s side have a number of doubts for the visit of the Magpies after a series of injuries derailed their latest Premier League game.

Villa were without three key men for their journey to Wolverhampton Wanderers, with Tom Heaton (calf) , Jack Grealish (calf) and Bjorn Engels (hip) all ruled out before kick off.

Those issues were compounded in the first 45 minutes of action at Molineux, when Jed Steer was stretchered off and Matt Targett hobbled off.

Villa target speaks out amid rumours

Villa transfer target Alfredo Morelos says he is eyeing a regular place in Colombia's first-team setup.

The Rangers hot-shot has been heavily linked with Smith's side, who could be in the market for a striker in January to help out struggling Wesley.

Morelos hasn't been shy about expressing his desire for a move away from the Ibrox side , for whom he netted his 22nd goal of the season at the weekend.

The Colombian international scored his ninth Scottish Premiership goal in 12 games in Sunday's 2-0 win at Livingston - he also has 11 goals in the Europa League for Steven Gerrard's side.

 

Villa flop's message to Newcastle fans

"Ease off Steve Bruce."

That's the message to Newcastle fans from former Villa defender Micah Richards .

Bruce makes his first return to B6 since his sacking in 2018, when the two teams meet at the end of the month.

It will be Bruce's first appearance back at Villa Park, since the infamous 'cabbage-gate' that marred his final game in the claret and blue dugout.
