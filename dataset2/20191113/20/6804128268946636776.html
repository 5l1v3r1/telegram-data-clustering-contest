Jose Mourinho to Arsenal: Contender for Spurs job, Man United star's hint, demand from next job

A round-up of all the latest Jose Mourinho speculation

13 Nov 2019, 20:25 by Andy Ha, Tashan Deniran-Alleyne

Jose Mourinho remains out of work, but it is no secret that the former Porto, Chelsea, Inter Milan and Real Madrid boss is keen on a return to the Premier League.

Mourinho has not found another job since being sacked by Manchester United last season and his prediction of being back on the touchline this season still hasn't yet come to fruition.

But could he be back in the dugout sooner rather than later? Reports claim the 56-year-old is keen to take charge of another Premier League club because he wants to become the first manager to win major honours with three English sides.

Is Arsenal his destination? Or will it be Totttenham?

With that said, here's all the latest stories.

Mourinho's one demand

In September, Mourinho revealed he was ready for a return to management - but on one condition.

And that's to manage a club where the aim is to win.

"The only thing I know is what I don't want," Mourinho told the Coaches' Voice. "What I don't want is crystal clear.

"What I want - the general profile of it of course - but I cannot say clubs and country. I am quite pathological in this sense that I have to play to win.

"Then if I win or don't win it's my problem, the players, the club and the structure. But I need a project where the feeling is that I play to win.

"If someone gives me a wonderful, incredible, amazing 10-year contract and the objective of the team is to stay in the first part of the table, If you finish seventh or eighth or ninth is perfect that's not for me. That's my nature."

Full story.

La Liga hint

Less than a week after that interview, Mourinho dropped a major hint that he was set for a return to La Liga six years after leaving Real Madrid.

"The most important thing for me is that my next role is with an elite club that has an ambition which challenges and excites me," he said. "I miss coaching a lot, but I won’t return to working just for the sake of coaching again.

"I love La Liga and the pride I have to have won this fantastic league is massive.

"I may have left six years ago but trust me, spiritually, and in the manner I speak of La Liga, I am an ambassador even without the official title of ambassador."

Zidane speaks out

Amid links with a return to the Santiago Bernabeu, current Los Blancos boss Zinedine Zidane appeared unbothered.

"It doesn't annoy me that his name is linked, that's how it is," the Frenchman said. "I don't have to say if it annoys me or not, that's the situation.

"Here, you lose a game and you have to change something. It's difficult, but that's the reality."

Wenger dig

It's little surprise that Arsenal fans are split on this Mourinho rumour. Those who are against the 'special one' replacing Emery are probably big supporters of Arsene Wenger.

And that stance isn't going to change if Mourinho continues to poke fun at his old rival.

Problem is, he can't resist a dig.
