'Make us title challengers' - Man United fans respond to Mauricio Pochettino reports

The Spurs boss has been linked with Manchester United in the past

13 Nov 2019, 20:32 by Andy Ha

Manchester United fans took to social media to respond to reports that Mauricio Pochettino is under increasing pressure to save his job at Tottenham Hotspur.

Pochettino has repeatedly been linked with a move to Old Trafford, but signed a new five-year deal at Tottenham in 2018 to seemingly quash any prospect of a move to Manchester.

However, Spurs have endured a poor start to the season, amid a backdrop of player unrest and frustration from Pochettino at a perceived lack of backing in the summer transfer window.

And the negativity has spilled onto the pitch where Tottenham sit 14th after a series of poor performances in the Premier League, the latest of which saw Spurs fortunate to draw against Sheffield United at the weekend.

After the 1-1 draw, Pochettino said: "We are disappointed because we knew we were going to find a team who would be aggressive and very energetic, well organised.

"This is the worst opponent you can have when you come to play from Wednesday in the Champions League, a very long way.

"I am disappointed because in the moment we broke them down to go and lead 1-0 in the second half, not keeping that advantage or scoring the second goal in that moment when we dominated the game.

"We feel so disappointed, I don't know the word to use. Upset or frustrated, but it is not a happy feeling."

According to The Telegraph, Pochettino is heading towards the point of 'no return' in north London, as fears grow that the Argentinian will not be able to turn Spurs' fortunes around.

Tottenham face fierce rivals West Ham after the international break, a game which could prove decisive for Pochettino's future.

The report claims Spurs could turn to Massimiliano Allegri or Jose Mourinho if they were to appoint a replacement mid-season, but making a change at the end of the campaign could see Daniel Levy target Bournemouth’s Eddie Howe, RB Leipzig’s Julian Nagelsmann, or England manager Gareth Southgate.

If Pochettino does leave Spurs, the 47-year-old will reportedly have no lack of suitors with Manchester United and Real Madrid still big admirers.

United fans took to social media to voice their opinion on whether Pochettino would be a good fit at Old Trafford.
