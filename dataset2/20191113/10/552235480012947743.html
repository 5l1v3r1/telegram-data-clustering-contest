Newmains knife man given ten month jail sentence

John Paul Quinn had recently moved into the area with his girlfriend.

13 Nov 2019, 10:24 by Wishaw Press, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/wishaw-press/

A domestic row proved costly for a man after police found a knife among the possessions he’d taken into the street.

John Paul Quinn, 31, was jailed for 10 months at Hamilton Sheriff Court on Friday.

He admitted unlawful possession of the blade in Banavie Road, Newmains, on August 27.

Quinn had been given bail in another case only 11 days previously.

The court heard a neighbour called police about a disturbance at the home of his girlfriend.

 

Scott King, prosecuting, said: “They were directed to the rear of a block of flats.

“The accused saw the officers and ran off, but was caught quickly. As he was being taken to a police car, he asked if he could retrieve three bags of possessions which he had dropped in a garden.

“The officers were advised that Quinn might have had a knife. The bags were searched and a knife with a blade measuring 17cm was found among his clothing.”

Defence agent John McLaughlin said Quinn is from Cambuslang and had moved into his girlfriend’s home two days earlier.

 

The solicitor explained: “There was an argument and he left to let matters calm down. When he returned he noticed his bags had been packed.

“The knife was with his possessions when he moved in and he had forgotten about it.”

Passing sentence, Sheriff Thomas Millar told Quinn: “Custody is inevitable due to your record, but I’ll reduce the sentence from 15 months to ten because of your early guilty plea.”
