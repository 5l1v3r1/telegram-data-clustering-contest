Ex-Barca Star Announces Retirement, Buys US Club

13 Nov 2019, 10:27 by SL International

A former FC Barcelona star, who excelled in Spain's triumph at the 2010 FIFA World Cup in South Africa, has announced his impending retirement from football.

Via a post on his official Twitter account, former La Furia Roja forward David Villa, who currently plays for Vissel Kobe in Japan, announced on Tuesday that he will retire from football at the end of the current J1 League campaign.

"After 19 years as a professional, I have decided to retire from playing football at the end of this season," Villa tweeted.

"Thank you to all the teams, coaches and teammates that have allowed me to enjoy this dreamed career. Thank you to my family, that has always been there to support me.

"It is my objective to put the cherry on top by winning the Emperor's Cup with Vissel Kobe on 1 January."
