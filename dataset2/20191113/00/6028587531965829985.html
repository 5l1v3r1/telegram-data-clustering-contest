Holiday Gift Guide 2019: The Best Gifts For Carb Lovers

13 Nov 2019 by Melissa Kravitz Hoeffner

Carbs are like friends, you can never have too many. And for the carb-lover who eagerly ingests all the holiday baked goods and still wants more, a carb-filled gift is just the thing they need to keep them happy this season.

Rao’s Pasta & Sauce Sampler

A pantry stocked with top notch weeknight pastas and sauces to slather on them is indeed the perfect gift for pasta lovers. Six of Rao’s most popular sauce’s, including marinara, bolognese and vodka are packaged with two long pastas and three short pastas, so pasta lovers can mix and match their noodle and sauce combos throughout the winter. $80, Raos.com

Imported Japanese Rice

Upgrade rice to a gift-worthy bundle with The Rice Factory’s imported grains from prefectures across Japan. Rare and high quality varieties of the rice include Niigata Koshiibuki, known for its savory taste and YamagataTsuyahime, which has an attractive gloss for paella or pilaf. Rices are packaged in attractive brown paper bags, so all they need is a bow on top! Starts at $13.99, trf-ny.com

Silpat Perforated Aluminum Tray

Avid bakers can save a little space with this brand new product that serves as both a baking sheet at cooling rack. The two-in-one product is perforated, which allows for air to circulate for a better bake on cookies and more. A silicone Silpat mat sits on top of the tray, and once its removed from the oven, the tray, coated with non-stick Teflon, can be flipped and freshly baked items transferred to cool. $39.95, Williams-Sonoma.com

Specialty Salt

Whether your carb lover leans towards sweet or savory, salts will amp up their carbs of choice in any meal. Saltverk’s sustainable sea salt is hand harvested from Iceland’s Westfjords and is produced with 100% geothermal energy, so even the tiny flakes come with a gift-worthy story. Flavors include seaweed salt, arctic thyme and black lava salt, and gift sets are available. Starts at $12.99, Saltverk.com

Soft Pretzel Kit

Making pretzels is nearly as easy as eating a them, and fun too! This kit has all the necessary ingredients to make a popular bar snack at home, plus the gift of teaching users to bake pretzels again and again once the initial ingredients run out. Bagel kits are also available for those opt to carbo-load sandwich-style. $30, FarmSteady.com

Sobaya Premium Soba

Great soba is hard to find stateside, and one Manhattan restaurant is trying to change that. Sobaya’s new packaged dry buckwheat soba noodles and bottled premium soba sauce are made in-house and can be shipped for free with orders over $35. It’s like gifting a trip to Japan, read in a bowl when a carb lover is craving some much needed warmth and comfort. Starts at $6.75, Sobaya-nyc.com

Zingerman's Bread Club

The famous Michigan bakery and deli ships homemade seasonal breads nationwide (additional charge for Alaska and Hawaii), so sandwich, toast and crust enthusiasts can indulge in a new loaf each month. Special varieties include chocolate sourdough, pumpernickel, French mountain bread, black olive farm bread and much more. Gift some nice butter (like Bennett’s) or condiments along with the certificate of bread club membership. Starts at $75 for three months, Zingermans.com

Carbs by Laura Goodman

Carbs can be vilified in our diet-centric culture, but there’s so much to love about the simple carbohydrate. Goodman’s book celebrates the joy of carbs with recipes for rice, pasta, pastries, breads and much more. $12.49, Amazon.com
