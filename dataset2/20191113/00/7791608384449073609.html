The Mandela Effect will make you question even the most mundane memories from the past.
According to researcher Fiona Broome, the term refers to when people have memories of pop culture or current events that don’t match historical records.
For example, the product that is used to make your house smell fresh is actually “Febreze,” instead of “Febreeze.’

Many people agree that certain events are real, and that the false memories could hail from the brain’s imperfect recall. These could be company brand names, movie details or even songs.
