Artists At A Brooklyn Art Fair Offered To Draw Naked Portraits Of Participants

13 Nov 2019 by Yimin Huang

The sixth edition of The Other Art Fair was held in Brooklyn, with a daring project known as Get Nude Get Drawn. The project sought willing participants to pose naked for artists, and in return they took home a free nude portrait of themselves.
Artists Mike Perry (animator for Broad City) and Josh Cochran were behind the project. Back in 2011, they held a similar event where models on Craigslist took part in a two-day nude portrait sketch that eventually produced hundreds of drawings in two 12-hour sessions, per Artnet.
Perry also told the publication that the artists had many opportunities to sketch a variety of people, from “exhibitionist” types to their artist friends, and even a “pregnant art director.”
He added that he began “figure drawing” at the age of 15, and found out more “about humanity” each time he sketched the human body. Cochran also saw his sketches as a way to find “new ways” of “working and seeing.”

The artists constructed a private studio to be used for their project at the fair, and have decided to keep the poses short so models do not find it too challenging. Perry explained that a prolonged pose might cause one’s quads to “shiver” and sweat to “drip down.”
Eight other artists also participated in the project. Every model received one nude portrait drawn by each artist. The remaining works were showcased at the fair, and cost US$20 each.
The Other Art Fair took place from 7 to 10 November this year. For more information, you can visit the official site here.
