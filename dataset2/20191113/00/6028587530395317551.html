The Draw

Jacare Souza might be the greatest middleweight in UFC history to never get a shot at the title at 185 pounds. Jacare’s inability to get the nod is a product of him losing at inopportune times, and being passed over by the UFC.

He made his UFC debut in May 2013 and scored an impressive submission victory over Chris Camozzi. That win was the first in a 5-0 stint he enjoyed when he joined the promotion, and from March 2012 to April 2015 when submitted Camozzi again, Jacare won eight in a row.

When you consider the reputation he brought to the UFC, it’s perplexing that he never got a title shot. His first loss was a controversial split-decision defeat to Yoel Romero, who has already fought for the title twice, and could be closing in on a third shot against Israel Adesanya in 2020.

With a 9-4 record in the UFC, and closing in on his 40th birthday in December, Jacare is moving up to light heavyweight.

His first challenge is Polish powerhouse Jan Blachowicz. In his last fight, Blachowicz destroyed and may have ended the career of former UFC Middleweight Champion Luke Rockhold.

Blachowicz has struggled to get a title shot at light heavyweight, but he has compiled a far less impressive 7-5 record with the promotion. Prior to his win over Rockhold, he was stopped by former title challenger Thiago Santos.

A win for Jacare or Blachowicz may not produce an immediate title shot, but it will push both veterans closer to that goal.
