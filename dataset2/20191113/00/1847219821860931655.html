Criminal process launched against Russian citizen for possible laundering of EUR 2.3 million

13 Nov 2019 by BNN

Latvia’s State Revenue Service (VID) has commenced a criminal process for possible laundering of EUR 2.3 million of illegally obtained funds by some Russian citizen, as confirmed by VID.

Combating money laundering, VID Tax and Customs Police have commenced a criminal process for possible laundering of large amounts of illegally obtained funds.

«Based on information acquired through risk analysis, a 1986-born Russian citizen detained at Riga International Airport at the beginning of November. The person had declared cash money on him worth USD 240 100. The origin of this money will soon be determined. Police then performed investigative activities in multiple Latvian banks, as well as searches of safe deposits registered with the detained person. USD 1.5 million in cash was confiscated,» said VID representatives.

VID Tax and Customs Police commenced a criminal process over possible laundering of illegally obtained funds between 2015 and 2019, legalizing illegally obtained funds worth EUR 2.3 million.

The person in question has been made a suspect. A security measure not associated with deprivation of freedom has been applied.

Limitation of cash money movement across the border is one of the priorities for VID in order to prevent money laundering activities, the service reminds.

In 2019 VID Tax and Customs Police launched a total of 38 criminal processes for non-declaration of cash or improper declaration of cash. Thanks to these efforts, police have confiscated EUR 4 926 488 in cash and EUR 1 378 249 from bank deposit boxes.
