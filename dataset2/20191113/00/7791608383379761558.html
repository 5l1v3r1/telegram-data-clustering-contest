Nike Airs Pretending To Be Exposed To More Air Are Retailing For US$500 & Up

13 Nov 2019 by Mikelle Leow

Sneaker collectors usually keep their prized possessions clean and in perfect condition, but they might covet these unique variations due to their grittiness.
A couple of Nike’s more iconic silhouettes have been given the “aged” treatment, somewhat, by Japanese luxury retailer PRINCIPE PRIVÉ, which has remixed the sneakers to appear as if they have gone rusty.
On Nike’s sacai Blazer Mid and Air Force 1 Low, the footwear customizer has hand-painted aesthetic elements mirroring oxidized metal and rust to simulate the seasoned look. The high-cut silhouette is priced at JP¥121,000 (US$1,109), while the Air Force 1 Low, available in black and white, goes for US$545.
Despite their aged appearances, the shoes are decorated with an acrylic paint blend and won’t wash out or transfer onto clothing. Find out more here.
