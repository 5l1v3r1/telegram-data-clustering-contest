2 held for theft at Devi Shetty’s house

13 Nov 2019, 00:30 by Bangalore Mirror Bureau

The Koramangala police have arrested two women for stealing gold jewellery and diamonds worth Rs. 22 lakh from the house of a well known cardiac surgeon in the city. The women had recently joined as domestic helps at the house of surgeon Devi Shetty and had stolen the jewellery when the residents of the house had gone out.


The accused Divya, a native of Soraba taluk in Shivamogga and Girija, of Kundapura, have been absconding since then. The police have recovered the stolen gold and diamond jewellery from them. Police said that the two had stolen gold and diamonds worth Rs. 22 lakh from the house of the founder of Narayan Health Care, The surgeon resides with his family in Koramangala 3rd block.

According to the police, the theft took place while Dr. Shetty was out along with his son, Aneesh, and came to light on October 26 when they checked their cupboard. His employee, Shajan Joseph had filed a complaint with the Koramangala police on October 31. In his complaint he had mentioned that they suspected three new helps, Anupama, Divya and Girija, who had joined the house recently. Divya and Girija had quit later.


The police came to know while questioning Anupama that she had no role in the theft. When the police interrogated Divya and Girija, the two confessed to them about the theft.
