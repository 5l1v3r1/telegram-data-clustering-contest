No legalisation. Estonia rejects calls for taking cannabis market in state’s hands

13 Nov 2019 by BNN

Over 1 000 activists in Estonia have signed a proposal for the state to tax and enforce its control over the illegal market of cannabis, by legalising it. Yet, legislators have not welcomed the idea citing health damage from the substance.

Estonian public broadcaster ERR reports on Wednesday, November 13, that the proposal with over 1,000 signatures asks the state to regulate the cannabis market, impose taxes and an age limit on sales of 21 years.

EKRE MP Oskar-Aleksander Lesment estimated in his own research of the Estonian black market that it is worth around EUR 170 million and said taxing it would benefit the state budget and regulate access to cannabis for minors.

Chairman of the Riigikogu Social Affairs Committee Tõnis Mölder (Centre Party) disagreed.

«The economic aspect or the benefits we get from it do not outweigh the health damage,» Mölder argued.

He added that in Estonia’s current political direction there are no plans to legalize or relax the rules concerning around the use of cannabis, ERR reports.
