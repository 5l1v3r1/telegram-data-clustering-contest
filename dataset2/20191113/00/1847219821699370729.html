Harmony wants Honour to Serve Riga to cease working with independent deputies

13 Nov 2019 by LETA

Harmony political party may agree to continue working in the coalition of Riga City Council under the condition Honour to Serve Riga terminates cooperation with the Independent Deputies Faction, said vice-chairperson of Riga City Council Anna Vladova after meeting with Harmony’s faction on Wednesday, 13 November.

Harmony has collected signatures to organize the next extraordinary meeting of the city council to dismiss Vadims Baraņiks from his posts as Vice-Mayor of Riga and board member of Riga Freeport.

This decision is justified with Baraņiks’ loss of trust, meaning he can no longer continue working in the coalition.

If Honour to Serve Riga no longer cooperates with Independent Deputies Faction, they are left with 28 votes together with Harmony. There is also For Riga bloc, which unites non-party deputies. Until now this party has cooperated with the Independent Deputies Faction.

The Independent Deputies Faction unites city council deputies Valērijs Petrovs, Vitālijs Dubovs, Rosļikovs and Baraņņiks. They were excluded from Harmony in June 2019 after refusing to vote for Sandris Bergmanis as the next vice-mayor.
