Imran Khan praises Turkey’s diplomacy model

29 Nov 2019, 10:01 by Anadolu Agency

KARACHI: Pakistan’s Prime Minister Imran Khan on Thursday praised Turkey’s international diplomacy and asked his country’s envoys to follow Ankara as a model in terms of diplomacy.

Addressing the concluding session of his country’s envoys to Africa, PM Imran underlined Turkey’s economic, diplomatic, and political achievements in the last 18 years.

“After Recep Tayyip Erdogan came to power, the mindset in Turkey changed,” he was quoted as saying by the state-run Pakistan Television.

“Turkey has got confidence in the past 17-18 years. It’s trade has pushed higher and the country itself got vibrancy,” he added.

PM Imran observed that Islamabad had failed to reap the benefits of its diplomacy in Africa in terms of trade and business.

Islamabad plans to join a host of countries that are investing in Africa.

Ankara is already pursuing its “Africa initiative” to boost its commercial and business relations with African countries, and the continent as a whole.
