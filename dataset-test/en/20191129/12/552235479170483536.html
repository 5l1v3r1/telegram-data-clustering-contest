Police appeal after thieves steal a number of Apple products from Currys PC World

A number of Apple electrical products were stolen.

29 Nov 2019, 12:16 by Kirsten McStay, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/kirsten-mcstay/

Police in Lanarkshire are appealing for information following a break-in to Currys PC World in the early hours of Thursday  (November 28, 2019)

The incident happened around 4.20am at the store in the Palace Grounds Retail Park, Hamilton, where a number of Apple electrical products were stolen.

The suspects are described as four males, all wearing dark coloured hooded tops, bottoms and trainers with scarves over their faces. They are also believed to have had access to a vehicle.

Detective Inspector Brian Lindie, of Lanarkshire CID, said: “The theft from the store was clearly targeted and co-ordinated with a high value of goods stolen.

“We are appealing to anyone who may have been in the area at the time that may have seen anyone matching these descriptions, or observed someone driving a vehicle in an unusual manner to get in contact with us.

“As it gets nearer to Christmas, we would urge all shop owners and customers to be extra vigilant to any suspicious behaviour and report this to police.”

Police Scotland can be contacted by calling 101 and quoting incident number 0398 of Thursday 28 November 2019
