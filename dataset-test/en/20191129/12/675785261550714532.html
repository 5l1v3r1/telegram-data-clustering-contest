Robbie Savage's Premier League predictions as Arsenal set to struggle again

The BT Sport pundit also tips Jose Mourinho's winning start to continue while there are difficult tests for Man City and Man Utd, and a bottom of the table clash

29 Nov 2019, 12:22 by Robbie Savage, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/robbie-savage/

Robbie Savage predicts another difficult week for Arsenal after their run of five Premier League matches without a win.

The Gunners head to Carrow Road for the first fixture in Freddie Ljungberg's interim reign following Unai Emery's sacking.

They face a Norwich side fresh off the back of a 2-0 win away at Everton and with hopes of sparking a resurgence to lift them out of the relegation zone.

Across north London, Arsenal's rivals Tottenham have also faced managerial upheaval, and Sav reckons Jose Mourinho's winning start will continue.

Spurs got off to a flyer under their new boss with their first win on the road since January last week.

And they can follow that up with another victory as they welcome the visit of Bournemouth on Saturday.

The battle for the title rolls on, and Sav feels all of the top four will get a favourable result this weekend.

Liverpool host Brighton at Anfield as they bid to maintain their eight-point lead over Leicester .

The Foxes are preparing to face Everton at home, in what could be the final curtain on Marco Silva's time with the Toffees should they fail to come away with any points.

Defending champions Man City are hot on the tail of the league leaders after a big victory against Chelsea last week, but must navigate a tricky trip to Newcastle , where they lost last season.

Speaking of Chelsea, they're in action at home to struggling West Ham , with Hammers boss Mauricio Pellegrini another manager under pressure.

At the foot of the table Southampton and Watford go head to head at St Marys with both sides in desperate need of a win.

Elsewhere Burnley and Palace meet at Turf Moor, while high fliers Wolves and Sheffield United clash at Molineux.

And rounding off the weekend's action, Man Utd prepare for the visit of Aston Villa in Sunday's late kick-off.

Here's how Sav sees the fixtures panning out...

Robbie Savage's Premier League predictions

Newcastle 0-2 Man City

Burnley 1-0 Crystal Palace

Chelsea 2-0 West Ham

Liverpool 3-0 Brighton

Tottenham 2-0 Bournemouth

Southampton 1-0 Watford

Norwich 1-1 Arsenal

Wolves 1-1 Sheffield Utd

Leicester 2-0 Everton

Man Utd 2-1 Aston Villa
