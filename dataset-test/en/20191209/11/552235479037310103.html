Man arrested after woman seriously injured in Newton Mearns house as cops probe 'disturbance'

The 41-year-old woman was raced to hospital.

09 Dec 2019, 11:39 by Kathleen Speirs

A man has been arrested after a woman was left seriously injured in East Renfrewshire.

Police swooped on a house in Castle Road at around 10pm on Sunday, following reports of a 'disturbance' inside a house.

A 41-year-old woman was rushed to hospital for serious injuries.

Medics say her injuries are not believed to be 'life-threatening'.

A 37-year-old man has been arrested in connection with the incident.

Police officers were still on the scene this morning.

A spokesman from the force told the Record: "We can confirm that a 37-year-old man has been arrested in connection with a disturbance within a house on Castle Road in Newton Mearns.

"The incident was reported shortly after 10.05pm on Sunday, December 8.

"The incident was contained to within the property and there was no wider risk to the public.

"Inquiries are ongoing."
