New Premier League plans to improve VAR emerge as football chiefs look to sort current chaos

The system has come in for criticism since it was introduced to the Premier League at the start of the season but it could soon be in for a revamp

09 Dec 2019, 11:57 by Alex Milne

Plans are being put in place to display further information on big screens at Premier League football grounds when there is a VAR decision taking place.

The system has been criticised this season for sometimes leaving match-going supporters in the dark, with fans unhappy they are not being told why a VAR check is in progress.

According to the Daily Mail , however, the Premier League have met with operators of the big screens at each club in London this week to discuss the logistics behind displaying extra information.

Currently when there is a check all that is put on the screen is 'VAR check in progress', followed by the final verdict when a decision has been made.

This means that supporters often have no idea exactly what the system is checking, which has led to frustration and booing inside stadiums.

The matches set to take place on December 21 and Boxing Day later this month have been earmarked as the ones where the additional information will be implemented.

There will also be two meetings this Friday to discuss other potential improvements to the system at the Premier League's base at Gloucester Place in London.

Fans at Manchester United and Liverpool will still be left in the dark even with the new additional information, however, as they do not have a big screen inside their grounds.

VAR has split opinion ever since its implementation, and as always there were a number of controversial calls in the weekend's action, such as in the Manchester derby.

Manchester City midfielder Rodri admitted after the game he and his teammates are fed up with the system , saying: "We are p***ed off by it but there is no point (talking) for us," he said.

"We have to keep fighting and don't focus on these things.

"It is other people's jobs - we can't scream. Some decisions can change matches and VAR is there for getting decisions (right). It is not an excuse for us."
