Congress Opposes Citizenship Amendment Bill in Lok Sabha, Terms it ‘Unconstitutional’

Congress leader Manish Tewari said the foundation for the two-nation theory was laid in 1935 in Ahmadabad by Savarkar in a Hindu Mahasabha session and not the by Congress.

09 Dec 2019, 12:56

New Delhi: After Union Home Minister Amit Shah introduced the Citizenship Amendment Bill 2019 in Lok Sabha for debate and its passage, Congress leader Manish Tewari on Monday strongly opposed the Bill and said it is against the Constitution and against the basic right of equality.

“The Citizenship Amendment Bill 2019 is against Article 14, 15, 21, 25 and 26 of the Indian Constitution. This bill is ‘unconstitutional’ and against the basic right of equality,” Tewari said during the debate hour.
