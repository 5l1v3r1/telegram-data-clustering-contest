The pictures that prove Rangers defender Filip Helander was not playing Celtic onside

Pictures prove the Celtic winner in the Betfred Cup Final WAS offside.

09 Dec 2019, 09:20 by Jonathan McFarlane

In a rivalry so contentious it's no surprise that the refereeing decision that settled the match has caused so much conjecture and debate.

TV coverage left little doubt on most people's minds that Christopher Jullien 's winner should have been ruled out for offside but in the aftermath of the game, stills have emerged claiming to show that Filip Helander had played the goalscorer onside.

Pictures show that the Ibrox defender is hidden from view in front of Jullien but his arm is clearly visible and in line with the furthest body part of the onrushing Celtic players.

You can see the big Swede's position more clearly in the above image but given the angle it's impossible to decipher position relating to offside.

In this image you can see when the ball is struck, Helander's arm is in line with the attacking Hoops stars. Some think this means Edouard is onside.

However, the IFAB laws of the game state a player is in an offside position if:

 - any part of the head, body or feet is in the opponents’ half (excluding the halfway line) and

 - any part of the head, body or feet is nearer to the opponents’ goal line than both the ball and the second-last opponent

The hands and arms of all players, including the goalkeepers, are not considered.
