OPPO will be launching the OPPO Reno 3 series of smartphones later this month. The Chinese manufacturer has already confirmed that the upcoming Reno 3 and Reno 3 Pro are dual-mode 5G-ready phones. The Reno 3 series is now for reservations on the official website in China. Recently, a mysterious OPPO PCRM00 / PCRT00 smartphone was spotted on the database of China’s 3C authority through which it was confirmed that it is an upcoming 5G phone from the company. Today, the phone has appeared on TENNA authority’s database. The listing lacks images of the phone, but it has revealed some of its key specs. The preliminary specs of the PCRM00 / PCRT00 5G phone suggests that it could be the Reno 3 Pro 5G phone.

According to the TENAA listing of PCRM00 / PCRT00 5G, it measures 159.4 x 72.4 x 7.7mm. The device is fitted with a 6.5-inch display and it houses a 3,935mAh minimum rated battery. The 3C listing of the phone reveals that it could be shipped with a charger that supports 30W rapid charger.

OPPO PCRM00 / PCRT00 Could be Reno 3 Pro 5G

OPPO has already confirmed that the Reno 3 Pro 5G has a slim profile of 7.7mm and it houses a battery of 4,025mAhv which seems to be its typical capacity. Rumors have it that it will be arriving with a 6.5-inch AMOLED display. Hence, it appears that the newly spotted PCRM00 / PCRT00 5G smartphone could be the OPPO Reno 3 Pro 5G.

The Chinese manufacturer has already confirmed that the Snapdragon 765G mobile platform will be powering the Reno 3 Pro 5G. It will be the second phone after the Redmi K30 Pro that debuts tomorrow to feature the SD765G chipset. OPPO has already revealed that the Reno 3 Pro 5G has an AMOLED display with punch-hole placed at its top-left corner. The display seems to be curved towards the edges. Rumors are rife that it carries support for 90Hz refresh rate and 180Hz touch sampling rate. It will arrive with an in-screen fingerprint reader.

It is speculated that the Reno 3 Pro 5G could be coming with 12 GB of RAM. Its rear side could be fitted with an OIS assisted 48-megapixel + 8-megapixel + 13-megapixel + 2-megapixel quad camera setup.
