Christmas events and activities happening across the south west to enjoy with loved ones

Fill up your festive calendar with a range of celebratory events

09 Dec 2019, 17:26 by Leena Sidat

From fun-filled family activities to short breaks away to escape daily stresses, there is lots happening in Bristol and the surrounding areas this Christmas.

So, if you’ve still got a few dates free and are looking for festive events to enjoy with your nearest and dearest, take a look at the list below.

1. The Crealy Christmas Spectacular

The Crealy Christmas Spectacular is back and set to be better than ever, with an all-new Christmas on Ice Show: The Snow Globe Adventure.

Featuring Santa’s Magical Showtime and Santa’s Toy Factory, every child will get to choose their own present to take home from thousands of wonderful hand-picked toys and gifts.

This all-day family experience also includes entry to other special magical attractions, including the new and improved Mary’s Christmas Bakery, Rudy’s Reindeer Feeding Station, Santa’s Post Office and the Decoration Station.

You can also enjoy rides across the theme park and over 75,000 square feet of indoor play areas.

Tickets are limited and are selling fast, so make sure you get yours for the biggest and best Christmas event in the south west! 

Where to go: Crealy Theme Park & Resort, Sidmouth Road, Exeter, Devon, EX5 1DR

Get in touch: Visit www.crealy.co.uk/christmas or call 01395 233 200

2. Clarkson’s Remembrance Carol Service

This year, Clarkson’s annual Remembrance Carol Service will be held at St Luke's Church, Hatfield Road, BA2 2BD on Sunday, December 8, 2019.

The service will be at 3pm and led by the lovely Reverend Matthew Frankum. Everyone is welcome to attend.

Invitations will be sent out over the next week, with tree tags enclosed. Anybody who does not receive a direct invitation but would like to do so in future, or cannot make it, contact any of the branches.

Refreshments will be served in the church after the service as usual.

Clarkson’s is also supporting the wonderful charity TIP, Time is Precious, which does amazing things for local children and families.

Where to go: St Luke's Church, Hatfield Road, BA2 2BD

Get in touch: Visit www.clarksonsfuneraldirectors.co.uk, call 01225 426 822 or email contact@clarksonsfuneraldirectors.com

3. St Mary Redcliffe Church

One of Bristol’s finest buildings, St Mary Redcliffe Church is a treasure of international importance and a masterpiece of Gothic architecture - recognised as being one of the finest churches in the country. What better place to celebrate Christmas!

If you fancy soaking up the special festive atmosphere, join the church for a traditional season of festive events, family friendly celebrations and candlelit services.

St Mary Redcliffe has events running all through December and is open each day from 8am to 5pm.

Click here for the church’s full Christmas programme.

Where to go: The Parish Office, 12 Colston Parade, Redcliffe, Bristol, BS1 6RA

Get in touch: Visit www.stmaryredcliffe.co.uk

4. West Somerset Railway

Bring the children to the West Somerset Railway where they can travel by train from the seaside town of Minehead to Blue Anchor to see Santa in his grotto. All children will be able to meet and greet the man himself, and receive a special gift.

Adults will be treated to a mince pie and alcoholic festive drink, with a non-alcoholic alternative also available, whilst you soak up the festive atmosphere. All trains have an on-train buffet car.

Santa Specials depart from Minehead only. Please note that no dogs are allowed on these services.

Where to go: The Railway Station, Minehead, Somerset, TA24 5BG

Get in touch: Visit www.west-somerset-railway.co.uk

5. Wells Cathedral

This Christmas, Wells Cathedral has multiple events taking place within its beautiful, historic walls.

Enjoy the Cathedral Candlelight Concerts on December 19, 20 and 21, at 7pm - booking is required in advance.

The cathedral’s Carol Services will take place at 6pm on December 22 and 23, with Family Crib Services on December 24, at 2pm and 3.15pm. The Eucharist of Christmas Night event will also take place on Christmas Eve at 11pm to the early hours of Christmas Day morning, 12.30am.

The cathedral will also have Christmas Day services on December 25, for all to attend.

Where to go: Cathedral Green, Wells, Somerset, BA5 2UE

Get in touch: Visit www.wellscathedral.org.uk

6. Harry Potter Studio Tour, Berrys Coaches

Experience magic first hand this Christmas and embark on the Harry Potter Studio Tour.

Explore behind the scenes and discover the magical world of Harry Potter at the Warner Bros Studio. Take a stroll down Diagon Alley, peer into the cabinets of Dumbledore’s office, ride a broomstick and have a spell-binding time.

The studio tour now includes entrance into the Forbidden Forest.

Berrys Coaches offers a wide range of day excursions throughout the year from Wellington, Taunton, North Petherton and Bridgwater. Customers can choose from a range of tours such as city shoppers, theatre excursions or perhaps a scenic tour.

Where to go: Wellington, Taunton, North Petherton and Bridgwater - click here

Get in touch: Visit www.berryscoaches.co.uk

7. Cricket St. Thomas Hotel

If you’re looking for a festive getaway before Christmas, but don’t want the hassle of travelling far, Cricket St. Thomas has the perfect solution.

Imagine taking all the highlights of the Christmas season and bringing them all together, right on your doorstep.

Your stay will include a welcome drink on arrival, pre-dinner cabaret, three-course dinner, live party band, and breakfast, as well as full access to leisure facilities and the stunning Cricket St. Thomas House and Gardens - all for £79pp per night.

The price is based on two people sharing, includes breakfast and dinner.

Where to go: Chard, Somerset, TA20 4DD

Get in touch: Visit the website, call 01460 30111 or email cricketstthomas.reception@bourne-leisure.co.uk
