PM Imran Khan inaugurates Pakistan’s first National Science & Technology Park

09 Dec 2019, 14:22 by Mati

RAWALPINDI, Pakistan: Prime Minister Imran Khan on Monday inaugurated the National Science & Technology Park (NSTP), a flagship project of the National University of Sciences & Technology (NUST).

The Chief of Army Staff (COAS) and the Chairman Board of Governors (BoG) NUST General Qamar Javed Bajwa received the prime minister on his on arrival at NUST.

NSTP being first of its kind in Pakistan is a platform to catalyse the knowledge economy, has been launched under the overarching theme of “INNOVATE PAKISTAN” – a platform designed to synergise researchers, creators, investors, implementers and end users of technologies to transform innovative ideas into successful products and services, while promulgating a culture of entrepreneurship in Pakistan.

The programme would augment the government’s recent launch of “Kamyab Pakistan” programme aimed at development of national youth.

The prime minister also visited the Research and Development Centres established by China and Turkey at NSTP NUST.

Speaking at the occasion, Prime Minister Imran Khan commended the vision of NUST leadership for having planned and accomplished this much needed initiative.

The prime minister said that all antiquated systems of economic progress have been upstaged by the new and enormously improved “Knowledge Economies” wherein any Country’s economic substance and outlook are the sum of its intellectual capital, research and innovation prowess and edge in technological advancements.

It’s worth mentioning that NUST is among top 400 universities of the world.

The Federal Ministers, Ambassadors of friendly Countries, the Rector NUST, senior government and renowned industry representatives from across the Country, and the Park’s local and international tenants comprising tech giants, high-tech Small and medium enterprises (SMEs) and startups were also present at the occasion.
