This stretched, bulletproof Rolls-Royce Phantom costs $3.3 million

If you’ve been watching this space, you probably heard about Hoovie’s adventures. He has been on a spending spree lately, buying all sorts of cars …

09 Dec 2019, 14:38 by Gabriel Nica

So you thought the Rolls-Royce Phantom is the best Roller you can get? You thought it’s also quite expensive, didn’t you? Well, I’m about to rock your world with an even better proposition that’s based on the Phantom and yet so much more. What we have here today might be a good proposition for services created to protect very important people around the world, such as the Secret Service, for example.

This is a stretched, bulletproof Rolls-Royce Phantom. It comes from the guys over at Klassen, who are otherwise known for other interesting, unique projects they have signed off before. Based on the long wheelbase Rolls-Royce Phantom, their stretched version has over a meter extra in between the axles, raising the car’s total length to 7,032 mm (277 in or 23.1 feet). That means this thing will carry you and several other people in comfort wherever you go.

It’s not just luxury, space and comfort this thing provides. According to the ad on a popular car-selling website in Europe, the car is also B7 certified which means it can withstand gun fire and small explosions thus making it a good choice in case you live in a dangerous part of the world. If that’s the case though, you might want to keep a low profile and not spend 3 million Euros ($3.3 million) on a car that will definitely attract a lot of attention to you and your loved ones.

Of course, there are several other interesting features inside this bulletproof Rolls-Royce Phantom, such as a new entertainment system, a new sound system and so on. What’s surprising though is that there’s no mention of an upgrade under the hood. Sure, the 6.75-liter twin turbo V12 of the standard Rolls-Royce Phantom is able to carry more than just the 2.6 tons of the standard car but then again, a bit more power never hurt, especially since this stretched version is definitely also heavier.
