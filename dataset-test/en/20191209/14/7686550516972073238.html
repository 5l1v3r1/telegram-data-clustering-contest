Boris Johnson took a reporter's phone to avoid looking at a picture of a four-year-old lying on a hospital floor

The Prime Minister had to be reminded to give the phone back

09 Dec 2019, 14:34 by Ruth Mosalski

Boris Johnson took an ITV reporter's phone and put it in his pocket after being asked to look at the photo of a sick little boy sleeping on a hospital floor.

The Prime Minister was being interviewed by ITV News political correspondent Joe Pike, who asked Mr Johnson to look at a photo of four-year-old Jack, whose mother Sarah Williment covered him with coats to keep warm as he waited for a bed at Leeds General Infirmary.

She had taken him there last Tuesday fearing he had pneumonia.

During the interview, Mr Johnson wouldn't look down at the photo on Mr Pike's phone, instead saying he would "study it later" as he attempted to steer the conversation on to Tory investment in the NHS.

Mr Pike then said to the Prime Minister: "You refuse to look at the photo. You've taken my phone and put it in your pocket Prime Minister."
