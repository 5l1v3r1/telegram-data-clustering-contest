Golden Globes Nominations 2020: ‘Joker,’ ‘Irishman’ for Best Picture, ‘Succession,’ and More

The Hollywood Foreign Press Association makes its picks for the best film and television series of 2019.

09 Dec 2019, 13:35 by Zack Sharf

The nominations for the 77th Golden Globe Awards were announced this morning and have reshaped the conversation around the 2019-2020 awards season. The nominations come from the Hollywood Foreign Press Association (HFPA), which is made up of 90 journalists from around the world. Golden Globe film nominees include top Oscar prospects “Marriage Story,” “The Irishman,” and “Once Upon a Time in Hollywood.” On the television side, Emmy winners “Game of Thrones” and “Fleabag” are once again in the mix, as are “Succession” and “Big Little Lies.”

The Golden Globes awarded “Bohemian Rhapsody” (Drama) and “Green Book” (Musical/Comedy) with its top two film prizes in 2019. The latter went on to win the Oscar for Best Picture. The four acting Oscar winners Rami Malek, Olivia Colman, Mahershala Ali, and Regina King all won Globes first. The Globe winner for Best Director was Alfonso Cuarón, who repeated at the Oscars. “Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse” also won the Oscar after picking up the Globe for Best Animated Feature. The top television prizes went to “The Americans” and “The Kominsky Method.”
