I'm A Celebrity winner Jacqueline Jossa puts her wedding ring back ON and quaffs champagne after insisting she's 'absolutely fine' amid Dan Osborne cheating claims

09 Dec 2019, 13:56 by Connie Rusk
  • Jacqueline Jossa put her wedding ring on to film I'm A Celebrity... Coming Out show on Monday 
  • The actress, 27, was crowned winner on Sunday but her victory was overshadowed by accusations about her husband Dan Osborne's fidelity
  • While she was in the jungle, it was claimed Myles Stephenson took Jacqueline to one side to claim his ex, Gabby Allen DID have a fling with her husband
  • Chloe Ayling also claimed to have had a threesome with Dan and fellow CBB star Natalie Nunn in 2018
  • Dan vehemently denied the claims and said the girls have 'no morals' 
  • Jacqueline told her fans she was 'absolutely fine' after turning down all  television and newspaper interviews 
  • The couple tied the knot on June 24, 2017, and they are parents to Ella, four, and Mia, 18 months 
  • Jacqueline 'has Dan's back' and believes he's 'an amazing person,' according to The Sun 

Jacqueline Jossa put her wedding ring back on in a defiant display on Monday after her husband Dan Osborne's alleged cheating scandal. 

The actress, 27, wore the diamond sparkler to celebrate her jungle win with her I'm A Celebrity co-stars after telling her Instagram followers she is 'absolutely fine'. 

Jacqueline proved she would not let the rumours overshadow her win as she arrived to the bash, held at the Palazzo Versace hotel, with a glass of champagne in hand. 

Ahead of the celebrations, the soap star took to Instagram to end speculation on her well-being after she dramatically cancelled all interviews- a first in the show's 17-year history. 

Fans had worried about how Jacqueline would react to Chloe Ayling's claims she and Dan had engaged in a threesome together last year with fellow Celebrity Big Brother star Natalie Nunn.  

But Jacqueline broke her silence on Instagram. She said: 'Hello everybody. Thank you so much for voting for me. If you voted, it means the absolute world.

'I cannot quite believe it's happening. I just want to let you all know, I am absolutely fine and it has been the best experience of my whole entire life and my babies are here.'

And Jacqueline certainly brushed off the drama as she filmed for the I'm A Celebrity... Coming Out show, set to air next Thursday, with her fellow campmates.

The former EastEnders actress wore her floral crown and beamed as finalist Andrew Whyment toasted her win and Kate Garraway offered a congratulatory hug.      

Jacqueline showed no signs of the pressure she might be feeling from the public over she will handle the latest batch of cheating allegations.

Jacqueline became the first winner in the show's history to cancel live TV and newspaper interviews after Chloe Ayling claimed she had a threesome with her husband in 2018- little more than 12-months after the couple exchanged vows.

There were also reports Myles Stephenson, 28, told Jacqueline his ex-girlfriend Gabby Allen, 25, did have a fling with her husband while they were in the jungle. 

Dan has denied both accusations and hit out at Chloe saying she has no morals. 

Traditionally a key feature on breakfast TV, Lorraine Kelly admitted she would not be interviewing this year's winner while giving viewers a run-down of her daily show on Monday morning.  

A spokesperson for I'm A Celebrity told MailOnline: 'Jacqueline wants to spend as much time as possible with her family in OZ before they have to travel back to the UK. Plus she's filming Coming Out.' 

Separate sources have since claimed Jacqueline is keen to avoid all publicity - some of which comes with a lucrative fee - opting instead to devote her remaining time in Australia to husband Dan and their two children, Ella, four, and 18-month old Mia.

'She has to film for the coming out show which airs later this week, so cancelled all other TV offers like GMB and Lorraine to spend more time with the family,' an insider told The Mirror.  

Jacqueline signed-up to I'm A Celebrity for viewers to see a different side of her, insisting she 'wasn't a doormat'. 

The actress' marriage two-year marriage has been chequered with allegations of Dan cheating on her.  

In March, Dan denied claims he had kissed Love Island's Alexandra Cane behind Jacqueline's back and a month after that, he also denied a fling with his PR pal Steph Ledigo. 

And in 2015, he spoke of his regret over staying at his ex Megan's house (the mother of his son, Teddy) as this led to cheating rumours. 

Despite a string of allegations against her partner, Jacqueline 'has Dan's back' and believes he's 'an amazing person,' according to a source who spoke to The Sun. 

'She genuinely believes Dan and will always have his back. She accepts who she's with and where she's at, her marriage vows meant something to her and she thinks he's an amazing person,' they told the publication. 

The same close friend told The Sun that Jacqueline doesn't entertain the rumours, saying that she's grown a 'thick skin' about it. 

They added: 'You have to remember that all of Jacqueline’s relationship with Dan has been surrounded by negativity since the very beginning – Dan's ex Megan was pregnant when they got together and there was constant attention and speculation.' 

This year's cast of I'm A Celebrity were in a celebratory mood as they all indulged in some champagne to acknowledge Jacqueline being crowned Queen of the Jungle.

Coronation Street star Andy, 38, finished as runner-up while radio presenter Roman Kemp, 26, came in third place.  

The stars indulged in a glass of bubbly before they all shared a series of snaps posing with Jacqueline's winning staff to mark the end of the show.

During the wrap party James Haskell's wife Chloe Madeley celebrated by jokingly stealing the crown for herself.

The rugby star, 34, shared a snap of his other half, 32, as she proudly donned the EastEnders actress' flower crown after being crowned Queen of the Jungle. 

James also shared a snap with his good friend Caitlyn Jenner who he called his 'jungle wife'.

Chloe looked stunning in the snap as she donned a silk white dress with James writing: 'We have a thief in the house' alongside the snap.

James and Caitlyn, 70, bonded during their time in the jungle, starting on day one when the rugby player chose the former Olympic athlete to partner up with for the first challenge.    

While there were more than a few squabbles in the jungle over the last few weeks, the celebrities had clearly put it all behind them as they enjoyed their remaining time in Australia together before returning home. 

James shared a snap of himself posing proudly alongside Jacqueline after her win, calling her 'inspirational' and a deserving winner.  

He wrote: 'I could not think of a more deserving winner!! @jacjossa smashed this on so many levels. She was inspirational and dealt with every challenge like a hero. She looked after me in camp like a sister. 

'I loved getting to know her and see her grow. Her story is inspiring and she has shown herself to be mega on every level. My wife @madeleychloe is a huge fan and wanted her to win from day one, as did I.' 

James' wife Chloe also posed with Jacqueline's staff while Dan jokingly squeezed the rugby star's muscles while they all hung out backstage.

Kate Garraway, 52, beamed as she celebrated the finale and posed for a snap with pal Andrew Maxwell, 45, where she donned a hat bearing his catchphrase 'My Love'.

Caitlyn Jenner also shared a snap of herself celebrating Jacqueline's win and praised the actress for how far she had come throughout her time in the jungle.

The former Olympic athlete, 70, wrote: 'Congratulations to my sweet friend @jacjossa witnessing your growth on this show was amazing! Congrats Jungle Jack!'

Caitlyn was joined by her partner Sophia Hutchins, 23, who said she was 'so proud' of the sports star for how she fared inside the jungle and posted a cosy snap of the pair to her Instagram.
