‘Ghostbusters’ is getting a sequel 36 years after the original movie — here’s the first trailer

09 Dec 2019, 17:19 by Olivia Singh
  • Sony Pictures released the first trailer for “Ghostbusters: Afterlife,” a sequel that follows the original film that was released in 1984.
  • The movie centers on a “completely broke” family comprised of a single mom named Callie (played by Carrie Coon) and her two kids, Trevor and Phoebe, (Finn Wolfhard and McKenna Grace, respectively) who move to a small town called Summerville.
  • Wolfhard’s character explains that a “creepy old farmhouse” was left behind in their name by his late grandfather.
  • They discover equipment that belonged to the ghosthunter, including the iconic suits, the ghost trap, and the Ecto-1 vehicle.
  • “There hasn’t been a ghost sighting in 30 years. New York in the ’80s, it was like the walking dead,” Paul Rudd‘s character, a teacher named Mr. Grooberson, says, referring to the events of the first “Ghostbusters” movie that starred Dan Aykroyd.
  • “Afterlife” also seemingly disregards the 2016 “Ghostbusters” reboot that was written and directed by Paul Feig and starred Melissa McCarthy, Kristen Wiig, and Leslie Jones.
  • Watch the trailer below. “Ghostbusters: Afterlife” hits theaters on July 10, 2020.
