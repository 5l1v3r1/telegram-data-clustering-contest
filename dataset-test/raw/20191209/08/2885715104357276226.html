No reason that employment has come down due to demonetisation, says Gangwar

09 Dec 2019, 08:34 by Express Web Desk

By: Express Web Desk | New Delhi | Updated: December 9, 2019 2:04:33 pm

Labour Minister Santosh Kumar Gangwar; Right. (Express photo by Renuka Puri)

In response to remarks that demonetisation has severely impacted jobs, Labour and Employment Minister Santosh Kumar Gangwar told Lok Sabha on Monday that there is no reason to show that employment has come down.

During the proceedings, TMC member Kalyan Banerjee said that thousands of labourers in his constituency have lost jobs due to demonetisation and asked whether the government would take steps in this regard.

The union minister responded saying that the government is implementing various schemes for generating more employment. “There is no reason that employment has come down (Aisa koi karan nahi hai ki rozgar kam hua hai),” the minister said during the Question Hour.

Gangwar further said that every citizen has a right to migrate to any part of the country in quest of better job opportunities and prospects. “The Constitution of India guarantees all citizens the fundamental right to move freely through the territory of India,” he said.

Gangwar said the government is also implementing the Inter-State Migrant Workmen (Regulation of Employment and Conditions of Service) Act, 1979 to mitigate hardships caused by migration.

In November 2016, the central government demonetised old Rs 500 and Rs 1,000 currency notes.
