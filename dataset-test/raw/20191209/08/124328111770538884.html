I'm A Celebrity's Jacqueline Jossa becomes the FIRST winner to cancel ALL live TV interviews amid reports husband Dan Osborne had a 'threesome' (so will this be the end of the road for the couple?)

09 Dec 2019, 08:26 by Rebecca Davison for MailOnline
  • A spokesperson for I'm A Celebrity told MailOnline: 'Jacqueline wants to spend as much time as possible with her family in OZ before they have to travel back to the UK. Plus she’s filming Coming Out' 
  • The 27-year-old beat Corrie's Andy Whyment to be crowned jungle queen but her win has been overshadowed by her husband Dan's alleged philandering 
  • While she was in the jungle, it was claimed that Myles Stephenson took Jacqueline to one side to claim his ex, Gabby Allen DID have a fling with her husband Dan 
  • Chloe Ayling also claimed to have had a threesome with Dan and fellow CBB star Natalie Nunn in 2018, but Dan denied the claims saying Chloe has 'no morals' 
  • Dan has previously been forced to deny flings with his PR pal Steph Ledigo, his ex Megan Tomlin and also denied kissing Love Island star Alexandra Cane 
  • It was previously reported that Jacqueline had contacted girls directly who had been linked to her husband, but the pair always managed to patch things up  
  • Jacqueline and Dan tied the knot on June 24, 2017, and they are parents to Ella, four, and Mia, 17 months

l’m A Celeb’s Jacqueline Jossa has become the first winner to EVER cancel live TV and newspaper interviews after Chloe Ayling's claim she had a threesome with her husband in 2018, little more than 12-months after the couple exchanged vows.

The latest news came after the couple looked incredibly loved up when they reunited after Jacqueline, 27, was crowned jungle queen on I'm A Celebrity on Sunday - but this was before she had chance to read any of the reports.   

Now people are wondering if the star will call time on her marriage which has been plagued by cheating rumours, all of which Dan, 28, has previously denied.   

Traditionally a key feature on breakfast TV, Lorraine Kelly admitted she would not be interviewing this year's winner while giving viewers a run-down of her daily show on Monday morning.. 

Former EastEnders actress Jacqueline beat Corrie's Andy Whyment to be given the winner's title after taking on gruelling final Bushtucker trials which saw Jacqueline hold a huntsman spider in her mouth and Andy, 38, eat a camel's hoof. 

But despite weeks of reports of cheating claims made against Dan amid rumours that Myles Stephenson, 28, had told Jacqueline that Gabby Allen, 25, did have a fling with her husband, all appeared well between them. 

But hours later, Lorraine Kelly said on ITV that she had decided to decline post-jungle interviews on Monday, despite all other campmates taking part after leaving the jungle. 

A spokesperson for I'm A Celebrity told MailOnline: 'Jacqueline wants to spend as much time as possible with her family in OZ before they have to travel back to the UK. Plus she’s filming Coming Out.'

Separate sources have since claimed Jacqueline is keen to avoid all publicity - some of which comes with a lucrative fee - opting instead to devote her remaining time in Australia to husband Dan and their two children, Ella, four, and 18-month old Mia. 

'She has to film for the coming out show which airs later this week, so cancelled all other TV offers like GMB and Lorraine to spend more time with the family,' an insider told The Mirror.  

When asked how ITV had handled telling Jacqueline about the claims from an aftercare perspective, they added: 'The duty of care of all of our contestants is of the utmost importance to us. We take the welfare of all of the celebrities very seriously and have measures in place to ensure they are supported at every stage of the process.'

In March, Dan denied claims he had kissed Love Island's Alexandra Cane behind Jacqueline's back and a month after that, he also denied a fling with his PR pal Steph Ledigo. 

And in 2015, he spoke of his regret over staying at his ex Megan's house (the mother of his son, Teddy) as this lead to cheating rumours. 

He apologised publically after a tape was leaked in which he told Megan that his relationship and unborn child with Jacqueline were a ‘mistake,’ and also threatened his ex with violence.   

Despite all the recent claims, Jacqueline and Dan looked very loved up as they arrived back at the Versace Palazzo hotel after the show's final, with Dan looking thrilled to have his wife back. 

She had spoken of her love for him during her final speech, saying 'I love Daniel, I love my kids, I love my family.'

Still dressed in her jungle clothing, she and Dan were greeted by their children and the rest of the campmates and no doubt Jacqueline's mother and father who had come to help out with the children. 

Dan, dressed in a smart blue striped shirt had his arm constantly around his wife after weeks of denying any sort of cheating claims, taking to Instagram stories to protest his innocence. 

Former CBB star Chloe Ayling had claimed that she and Natalie Nunn had taken part in a threesome with Dan last year and Natalie's husband, Jacob Payne, has dumped her and moved out of their house since the claims. 

Dan hit back on social media soon after and said the girls had 'no morals.' 

Meanwhile, Jacqueline made an incredible speech after winning the show about how taking part had given her confidence. 

She said she spends her life 'trying to change trolls' minds' about what they think of her but that she had completely just been herself on the show. 

Andy reassured her that 'people love her for her' and she said that having the constant encouragement of the other campmates on the show had made her realise she could confront her fears.

Jacqueline and Dan tied the knot on June 24, 2017, and they are parents to Ella, four, and Mia, 17 months.
