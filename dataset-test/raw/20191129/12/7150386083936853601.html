Maldives ex-President Yameen Sentenced to 5 Years Prison Over Money Laundering Charges

Yameen has been accused of receiving US $1 million of government money through a private company as part of a deal to lease a number of tropical islands for hotel development.

29 Nov 2019, 12:24

Male: Maldives former President Abdulla Yameen was found guilty of money laundering and sentenced to five years in prison with a fine of USD 5 million.

The Maldives Criminal Court Judge Ali Rasheed, who headed a panel of five judges trying the case on Thursday, told court that it had been established beyond any reasonable doubt that Yameen had taken money that he knew was embezzled from the state, Xinhua news agency reported.

Yameen has been accused of receiving US $1 million of government money through a private company as part of a deal to lease a number of tropical islands for hotel development.

The former President, however has denied all charges.
