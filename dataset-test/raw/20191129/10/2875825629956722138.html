Kim Kardashian and her kids trek across mountains in Kanye West's Closed On Sunday video

The Kardashian-West family have starring roles in the imaginative music video, with North screaming the 'Chick-fil-A' sign off and Kris Jenner riding a tank

29 Nov 2019, 10:47 by Irishmirror.ie

Kanye West 's music video Closed On Sunday has dropped on Thanksgiving and the star enlisted the help of his A-list family to create the imaginative masterpiece.

The new music video for the track from the singer’s recent gospel album, Jesus is King, took the world by storm today after Kanye, Kim Kardashian , 39, and their kids North, Saint, Chicago and Psalm were spotted in the video.

While the song has been made popular by its infamous Chick-fil-A reference, the music video is thought to be filmed on the sprawling Wyoming ranch that Kanye, 42, recently bought for $14 million.

North West, six, stars alongside her extended Kardashian clan in the music video and is seen screaming the words Chick-fil-A' in Kayne's place, while the family huddle together on some rocks before trekking across barren land.

The little girl takes after her father by blurting out the iconic phrase after the US chicken fast food chain agreed to stay shut one day a week following the founder's religious beliefs.

It has since became famous for closing on Sundays and Thanksgiving Day, with the opening line in Kanye's track saying: "closed on Sunday, you’re my Chick-fil-A."

The music video appears to be a total family affair with Kourtney Kardashian and Kris Jenner also making quirky appearances.

World-famous Momager Kris glamorously emerges from an army of jeeps, meanwhile power couple Kimye walk across an almost post-apocalyptic scene after appearing to have slept in the desert huddled to their loved ones.

The camera pans to show a dramatic backdrop, as the family stagger to their feet before slowly walking along a large boulder, as Kanye cradles baby Psalm.

Kris then re-appears in the other-wordly scene and joins a gathered crowd that includes Kourtney and her children Mason, Penelope and Reign.

Later, another group of people assemble and seem to worship the rapper in a cult-like fashion as he takes center-stage in the middle of a circle.

Inspired by his infamous Sunday Service, the video also sees gospel singers and the hitmaker standing on top of a rock, with the sun rising behind him as he belts out his controversial track.

The Stronger singer's ninth studio album boasts eleven Christian hip hop tracks that features a significant gospel influence.

Jesus Is King is Kanye's first album since the release of Ye in 2018.

He also announced that this Christmas he's releasing a new "Sunday Service album" called Jesus is Born.

The Jesus Walks rapper recently revealed he's giving himself the elaborate title of Christian Genius Billionaire Kanye West so his fans can understand him better.
