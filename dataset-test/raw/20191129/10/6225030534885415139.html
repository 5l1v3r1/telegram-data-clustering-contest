Got Stormy headlines Del Mar's Matriarch, an east vs. west battle

29 Nov 2019, 10:04 by Victor Ryan

It will be a battle of east versus west Sunday when the gates open for the Grade 1, $300,000 Matriarch Stakes on closing day at Del Mar.

Got Stormy has made the return trip to Southern California for trainer Mark Casse following her runner-up effort in the Breeders’ Cup Mile at Santa Anita Park, while Chad Brown invades from New York the one-mile feature with Grade 2 winner Significant Form. Not to be left out, trainer George Weaver has sent out Grade 3 winner Daddy is a Legend from his eastern base, and Brad Cox ships in Juliet Foxtrot. Among the local hopefuls are a pair for trainer Neil Drysdale: Toinette and Simply Breathless, who just ran 1-2 at this distance in the Nov. 3 Goldikova (G2) at Santa Anita. That was the fourth victory in the last five starts for Toinette, who has been patiently brought up the ranks by Drysdale. Got Stormy will be back against her own gender after three straight starts against males, which includes a win in the Fourstardave (G1) at Saratoga in August. The daughter of Get Stormy has amassed $1.25 million in career earnings and has hit the board in all seven starts this year with three wins. “She’s been solid all year,” said Shane Tripp, assistant to Casse. “What she did at Saratoga, winning two stakes a week apart, kind of speaks for itself. She shows up every time. Significant Form, a 4-year-old that cost $575,000 as a yearling, has taken a big step forward this year for owner Stephanie Seymour Brant. The daughter of Creative Cause won the Intercontinental (G3) at Belmont when making her belated seasonal debut in June and added wins in the Ballston Spa (G2) at Saratoga in August and the Noble Damsel (G3) at Belmont last time out on Sept. 21.
