Syed Mushtaq Ali Trophy: Abhimanyu Mithun picks five wickets in six balls

29 Nov 2019, 13:17 by Prasad RS

Highlights

  • Mithun came up with a fine performance -- taking 5/39 which included a hat-trick -- that helped Karnataka register a eight-wicket win over Haryana
  • Karnataka's target would have been in excess of 200 if not for the brilliant final over bowled by Mithun
  • Mithun became the first Indian to have taken a hat-trick in all domestic competitions

SURAT: On the eve of the Karnataka-Haryana Syed Mushtaq Ali T20 semis clash, the spotlight was on former India pacer Abhimanyu Mithun as it had emerged that he was summoned for questioning in the Karnataka Premier League's spot-fixing scandal. Despite being in the news for the wrong reasons, the 30-year-old came up with a fine performance -- taking 5 for 39 which included a hat-trick -- that helped Karnataka register a resounding eight-wicket win over their opponents at the Lalbhai Contractor Stadium on Friday.

Haryana's Himanshu Rana (61, 6x4, 2x6) and Chaitanya Bishnoi (55, 7x4, 1x6) powered their side to an imposing 194/8 in their 20 overs. But the total proved too little for the power-packed Karnataka line-up who chased it down in just 15 overs. Their reliable opening pair of KL Rahul (66, 4x4, 6x6) and Devdutt Padikkal (87, 11x4, 4x6) added 125 in just 9.3 overs to make a mockery of the contest. After Rahul's departure, Padikkal added 57 runs with Mayank Agarwal for the second wicket to help the side march into the summit clash on Sunday.
