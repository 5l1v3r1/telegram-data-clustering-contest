Bling it on with a visit to the Scottish jewellery shop that Rita Ora loves

Gold is at it's most popular in years, says John Stewart Jewellers.

29 Nov 2019, 15:55 by Lorraine Howard, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/lorraine-howard/

It’s a favourite with the fashion crowd but gold jewellery is also the Christmas gift that keeps giving.

From chunky statement rings to stacking bracelets and layered necklaces, gold jewellery has been the comeback trend of 2019 - and there’s no sign of it disappearing.

John Stewart Jewellers have the biggest stock of gold in Scotland and say customers have also gone mad for anything that’s personalised, from pendants to rings.

The store, which has branches in Irvine, Ayr, Glasgow and Clydebank, is a favourite with celebs', footballers and sports stars and includes Rita Ora amongst its high profile fans.

The singer popped into the Glasgow branch and bought a large selection of chains and necklaces, which she later showed off performing on stage and on her Instagram.

Take inspiration from Rita, who never leaves the house without a selection of impressive jewels, pendants and trinkets, by wearing out-sized earrings and over-sized chain links - the bigger and bolder the better.

One of John Stewart Jewellers  most popular ranges for Christmas is the exclusive Nomination collection of Italian jewellery for women, men and children, where you can personalise your bracelet with a charm or message.

With over 30 years of experience they are also well known for bespoke commissioned pieces, from one -off gold pieces to entire diamond sets.

Because gold retains its high value, it is always worth reusing and remodeling into another piece.

So if you have old gold jewelery you no longer wear, or that’s out of fashion, the experts at John Stewart can rework it into something you'll love again, at a fraction of the cost.

Each branch is happy to provide a free consultation to advise on all re-modelling and commission work.

They also buy gold for cash if you want to clear out any old pieces of jewellery languishing in a drawer at home.
