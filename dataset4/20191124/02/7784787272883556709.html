Coulthard ‘happier’ after ending four-month wait with Newcastle Race 31 podium

24 Nov 2019, 02:03 by James Pavey

Fabian Coulthard will head into the summer break “a little bit happier” after he ended a four-month podium drought at the Coates Hire Newcastle 500 on Saturday.

Coulthard had failed to reach the top three in six events following the Townsville round in July – a stark contrast to his run to second in the standings after claiming nine podiums (including wins at Phillip Island and Perth) in the opening 18 races.

The Kiwi has since dropped to fourth in standings, and is all but certain to remain there regardless of what happens in Sunday’s Race 32 finale.

Race 31 on Saturday proved a blessed day for Coulthard, who ended his wait for a trophy – even if it took a slice of luck to get there following Jamie Whincup’s late off.

Critically, Whincup’s error - coupled with a Scott McLaughlin-Coulthard podium - helped swing the teams’ championship battle Shell V-Power Racing’s way.

Heading into Sunday’s race, the margin between the top two teams is 143 points.

However, the smiles remained for Coulthard - who cast aside the Bathurst team orders scandal which threw his name into the spotlight - to return to the rostrum.

“It’s nice to get back on the podium,” he said.

“Obviously we have a long break after this one so even if we have a good run today like we did, you can go away over Christmas and be a little bit happier.

“I think if you leave here and you’ve had a shocker, you’ve got a couple of months to think about it. It’s not like since Townsville I’ve gone, ‘I’m not bothered about a podium’.

“I’ve been working just as hard at every event to do the best job you can, and we’ve had a couple of fourths and things like that where we’ve just been off the podium.

“So we just keep coming back, keep pushing, keep trying and I think we’re starting to get a good direction.”

Coulthard came close to the podium at Sandown, teaming with Tony D’Alberto to finish fourth.

It took just one event for the Kiwi to rise one place, and Coulthard – who started sixth on Saturday – heaped the praise on his team for aiding his return to the top three, and strengthening the team’s grip on the fight for pit lane supremacy.

“The strategy changed a fair bit on the run,” he explained.

“We were doing one thing and then we weren’t, and then we were going to do this again and then we’re not.

“I was a bit confused there at one point, but the boys did a good job, thinking on their feet.

“That’s what this sport is about, you have got to have the team behind you and today we had a great team.

“Any time you can have two cars on the podium, it’s a credit to everyone.”
