Girls Aloud trio respond to claims they have a WhatsApp group to slag off Nadine

Cheryl, Nicola Roberts and Kimberley Walsh say they only want her to do well in the Outback - and that none of them have been voting for her to do the grueling Bushtucker trials

24 Nov 2019, 02:56 by Irishmirror.ie

Nadine Coyle's former bandmates have dismissed suggestions they are part of a WhatsApp group slagging off the I'm A Celebrity star.

Cheryl, Nicola Roberts and Kimberley Walsh have all distanced themselves from claims they have been attacking the Irish camp-mate.

Accusations came that they had created a message group which poked fun at her - and vote for the 34-year-old to do the Bushtucker trials.

But hitting back, the former Girls Aloud stars issued a statement through MailOnline which said they only have "love for each other" - despite their differences.

Cheryl said: "We only want to see Nadine do well, and regardless of any differences we might have had, we will always all have love for each other.

"We did not enjoy watching her distressed when jumping out of the plane. And we don’t have time for group chats regarding the trials on the show!"

And Kimberley also slammed the rumours, and said she found it hard to watch when Nadine broke down in tears prior to her skydive.

"I felt really emotional watching Nadine jump out of the plane and texted Cheryl saying how hard it was to watch. I think she’s doing great in there," she said.

Nicola added: "We don’t have a group chat about I’m A Celebrity, this just isn’t true. We only wish Nadine the best in the jungle and hope she does well on the show."

A source alleged to The Sun the trio thought "it would be funny to vote for Nadine to do the Bushtucker Trials" as "it’s all a bit of a laugh".

While Nadine has been appearing on I'm A Celeb, the Irish beauty addressed the rumoured rift between her former bandmates.

An intrigued Kate Garraway asked the brunette beauty why she wasn’t friends with the other members of Girls Aloud or saw them.

Nadine replied: "Everyone gets on with their lives, I was friends with them all, it was all nice and lovely.

"But I was always in the gay bars with all the guys, I’d be out partying, me and ten guys, I was having a laugh, I was 18-19 years old."
