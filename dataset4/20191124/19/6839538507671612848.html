Home in Adelaide's inner-north totally destroyed by suspicious fire | Cannabis crop discovered after house fire in Adelaide's west

24 Nov 2019, 19:31

Police are investigating a suspicious fire that completely destroyed a home in Adelaide's inner-north. Meanwhile, a Cannabis crop was found after a house fire in Adelaide's west last night.

Police and fire crews were called to Florence Cres in Brompton about 9:10pm where 10 MFS appliances, with 45 firefighters and one command vehicle, found a home engulfed in flames.

Thankfully, the home was unoccupied at the time and two dogs in the backyard survived.

The blaze was extinguished in 40 minutes and was contained to the property, causing $350,000 worth of damages.

The fire is believed to be suspicious and police are investigating as the cause remains undetermined.

Meanwhile, a Cannabis crop was found after a house fire in Marleston last night.

Police and MFS were called to the Cudmore Tce property about 8:20pm with reports of smoke coming from the roof.

Once fire crews extinguished the blaze, a hydroponic crop was found inside with at least 20 plants being grown.

Anyone with information is urged to contact Crime Stoppers on 1800 333 000, or online at www.crimestopperssa.com.au.
