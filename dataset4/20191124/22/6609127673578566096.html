DEAL WITH THE DEVIL

Dad of murdered Arlene Fraser would accept killer hubby being freed from jail if he reveals where he dumped wife’s body

24 Nov 2019, 22:40 by Stuart Patterson

THE dad of slain Arlene Fraser revealed he would accept her killer hubby being freed — if he finally disclosed where his daughter’s body was dumped.

Hector McInnes, 79, made the claim as he urged closure from son-in-law Nat Fraser, 60, over the 1998 murder.

The grieving parent, of Bonnyrigg, Midlothian, said: “It would be a deal with the devil but it would be worth it.”

Hector also told how he fears that time is running out for him to find out his murdered girl’s last resting place.

He is hopeful a new law could force evil Fraser, 60, to admit to what he did with his wife’s body if he ever wants to be considered for release from jail.

But with the brute not eligible for parole for 11 years, Hector said: “I’ll be long gone by then.

“However, it’s really important to the family that we know where Arlene’s remains are.

“This new legislation sounds really important — like it could be a big change for us.

“I would even accept Fraser’s release from prison if he told us what happened to her.”

Explaining how he would feel if that ever happened, the great-grandad said: “I would be living my life again.

"It would be a bit better than it is now because I would know where Arlene is.”

His daughter was 33 when she was last seen in April 1998 at home in Elgin, Moray, as she waved kids Jamie and Natalie, then ten and five, off to school.

Fraser has since been convicted of her murder at two separate trials. It’s claimed he paid a hitman £15,000 to kill Arlene.

The mystery of where her body was dumped is one of the cases that led to Justice Secretary Humza Yousaf revealing plans last week for Suzanne’s Law.

The parole reform would keep killers locked up for as long as they keep their secrets.

It was named after Suzanne Pilley, 38, of Edinburgh, who was murdered in 2010 by ex David Gilroy, 56. He has refused to say where he left her remains.

But Hector believes Fraser may now be too used to life behind bars to jump at any chance of securing his freedom.

He said: “He’s probably resigned himself to a cushy life in prison. He’ll be institutionalised.

“He’ll be into a routine and probably accepts it and thinks he’s better off where he is.”

Hector said he and the family got some comfort from holding a church service for Arlene five years after she vanished.

But he admitted she is never far from their thoughts, saying: “We’ve had more than 20 years of this. It’s always with us.

“One good thing, I suppose, is that we can’t be hurt any more.”

Fraser was first caged for Arlene’s murder in 2003.

That conviction was quashed on appeal but he was convicted following a second trial in 2012 and jailed for at least 17 years.

He had six months added to his sentence last year for using a mobile phone in his cell at Addiewell nick, West Lothian.
