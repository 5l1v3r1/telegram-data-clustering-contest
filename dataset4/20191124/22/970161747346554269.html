I'm a Celeb's Kate Garraway, Ian Wright and James Haskell clash over chivalry

24 Nov 2019, 22:09

I’m a Celebrity… Get Me Out of Here! stars Kate Garraway, Ian Wright and James Haskell got into a spat on tonight’s episode of the show.

The TV presenter, ex football-player and ex-rugby player, who along with the rest of the camp are at this point very tired and hungry, argued over chivalry, of all things.

As the camp made a plan for tomorrow’s live challenge, the trio clashed over the order in which the celebrities would participate.

As well as stars for the group, winners of the challenge would win a roast dinner in the Jungle Dining Room for one camp mate, based on descending order that the team must decide on beforehand.

Therefore those in position one, two and so on had a higher chance of getting a roast dinner.

This promptly caused tensions to rise.

It was eventually decided that the women of the camp would go at the top (in order: Adele, Jacqueline, Nadine, Caitlyn and Kate) with the men then following (James, Myles, Roman, Andrew, Ian, Cliff and Andy).

James then snapped: ‘Can we just all agree moving forward that for anything else and I think every man in here agrees that woman go first and cut all that chivalrous nonsense.’

Kate snapped back: But that is chivalrous nonsense.’

To this James replied: ‘You fight every time, just got with it,’ with Kate responding: ‘I’m allowed to have a view too.’

Ian then chimed in and said to Kate: ‘You’re too nice, that’s how you ended up in the other camp.’

Then, James finally added: ‘We don’t need a debate, women go first, men after that.’

Drama.

Tensions didn’t simmer there though as Ian and Andy got into a spat over the way the Match of the Day star spoke to his campmate.

‘You keep shouting at me Ian,’ Andy said while standing up.

Ian looked at him and said: ‘Who is shouting, when did I shout?’ to which Andrew replied: ‘Just then.’

After a few moments, Andy said: ‘I’m sorry, I’m really tired, I don’t know, I’m really tired… You’re my pal and I’m just really sensitive.’

Ian then extended the olive branch and said: ‘We’re both hungry, we’re both tired,’ before hugging it out.
