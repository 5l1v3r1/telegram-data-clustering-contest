I'm A Celebrity's Dingo Dollar Challenge causes unexpected jungle spat

I'm A Celebrity's Dingo Dollar Challenge caused an unexpected jungle spat between Andrew Whyment and James Haskell

24 Nov 2019, 22:30 by Simon Duke

An unexpected jungle spat broke out between two of I'm A Celebrity's most mild-mannered contestants on Sunday night and it was all down to the latest Dingo Dollar Challenge.

After completing the challenge itself - which saw Kate Garraway get pelted with powder paint - she and Roman Kemp headed to the kiosk to discover they had a choice of jelly beans or salted nuts. They chose jelly beans.

Kiosk Kev called the celebrities back in I'm A Celebrity's Main Camp and Andrew Whyment answered the phone.  The question was ‘What % of adults do not trust politicians?’ A-59% or B-79%"

Adele thought it would be 79%, Andrew thought lower. Jacqueline said nobody trusts any politicians. 

Coronation Street actor Andrew felt it was 59%, James asked for a show of hands any most of the celebrities thought 79% so James told Andrew to say that, to which he retorted “Not everyone put their hands up," only for James to snap back straight away, saying“they did’.”

Andrew walked away in a huff, declaring “It’s just as shambolic as ever, we’re not getting chocolate.”

Later the camp still discussed the tension over the phone call, Cliff told Andrew to “Let it go” and sang it in the style of Disney’s Frozen.

Andrew wanted more time to debate the question.

Talking in the Bush Telegraph James said “Everyone’s tired, everyone’s a bit chippy, everyone’s had a long day, things happen.  I’m just mortified if he (Andrew) think I just closed him down.”

Heading to the Telegraph himself, Andrew said “I said my piece, everyone said their piece and big James gave me a hug, job done.”

Roman and Kate came back and revealed they’d all won jelly beans, everyone cheered and Andrew reacted “Thank God for Adele, thank God people didn’t listen to me.”
