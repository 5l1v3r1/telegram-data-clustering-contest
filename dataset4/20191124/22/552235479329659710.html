Lyndon Dykes issues Scotland come-and-get-me plea as Livingston striker eyes play-off call-up

The Australia-born Livi forward has insists he wants to play for Steve Clarke’s side after spending weeks pondering his options.

24 Nov 2019, 22:29 by Craig Swan, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/craig-swan/

Lyndon Dykes has nailed his colours to Scotland’s international mast.

The Australia-born Livingston striker insists he wants to play for Steve Clarke’s side after spending weeks pondering his options.

The 24-year-old had at first intimated that Australia would be a preferred option if he had the opportunity as it was the country of his birth.

However, Dykes has now changed his outlook and said: “I’ve said a couple of things in the last few weeks, but I’ve sat down at home and my son was born in Scotland. My parents are Scottish.

“I don’t really have any family born in Australia besides me and my sister, so I don’t see why I couldn’t play for Scotland, to be honest.

“I watched them in the international break and they have a brilliant team. If I ever got the opportunity I’d love to play for Scotland.

“When it all started it’s a lot of talk. You go with the flow. But then I see Shanks (Lawrence Shankland) pulled out injured and they called up (Eamonn) Brophy.

“I think: Why can’t I do this? And also I’ve lived here for a few years now. My parents were born here, I’ve got Scottish blood.

“I’m not someone who’s just rocked up in the country for the first time and wants to play, so I thought to myself I could maybe try and make it a goal for myself, to try to play for Scotland.

“I think they’ve got a fighting spirit watching them and I really hope they make the Euros now. I’d love to be a part of that, if I ever got an opportunity of course.

“But I’m just putting my head down and not getting carried away with that kind of stuff.

“I’ve still got to perform and show I’m good enough for a step-up.

“After watching them, they played well. John McGinn was the stand-out, but I think I can maybe give a little bit of a different option for Steve Clarke.

“I’m putting my head down and I’m going to try to work to maybe get into one of the international squads.”
