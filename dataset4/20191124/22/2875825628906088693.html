I'm A Celebrity screaming fight ends in tears as Ian Wright faces 'bullying' claims in Andrew Maxwell row

A heated argument over 'sexist' decisions descended into chaos when tearful Andrew Maxwell accused shouting Ian Wright of 'singling him out' on tonight's I'm A Celebrity

24 Nov 2019, 22:01 by Irishmirror.ie

The I'm A Celebrity camp was embroiled in another massive fight on Sunday night's show - with Ian Wright facing 'bullying' claims.

A heated argument over 'sexist' decisions descended into chaos when tearful Andrew Maxwell accused shouting Ian Wright of purposefully 'singling him out'.

It all started with a tense debate over who who should get roast dinners ahead of an upcoming trial - with Kate Garraway confronting James Haskell over "chivalrous nonsense".

The Good Morning Britain host snapped back at the camp leader over his insistence that women should always go first before the men.

As the camp began to clash, comedian Andrew said that he did not like the way former football Ian kept speaking to him.

Frustrated Andrew, 45, then stood up and said: “You keep shouting at me Ian.”

Ian, 56, looked at him and asked: “Who is shouting, when did I shout?”

When Andrew told him it was "just then", Ian claimed: “I wasn’t even talking to you, what did I say?”

In the Bush Telegraph, Roman Kemp admitted he was a little bit lost and did not understand how the situation had happened.

Back in camp, Ian told Andrew: "What are you talking about, I didn’t shout at you."

When Andrew asked why Ian was singling him out, Ian firmly denied that he was and asked the comedian to repeat what he had supposedly said.

As things got heated, Andrew claimed he could not remember and was tired so walked to his bed.

Ian was not happy with the situation, but Andrew then walked back to the football pundit and opened his arms to make up.

Andrew said: “I’m sorry, I’m really tired, I don’t know, I’m really tired."

When Andrew started to get emotional in front of Ian, the former Arsenal striker said: “Don’t cry.”

Close to tears, apologetic Andrew confessed: "I’m not going to cry, I’m sorry."

A tired and emotional Ian and Andrew both hugged each other as they settled their differences.

“You’re my pal and I’m just really sensitive," admitted Andrew.

When Ian asked what was up with him, Andrew confessed he was just tired.

Ian replied: “We both are dude. We’re both hungry, we’re both tired.”

Andrew said: “I know, I know, I’m sorry.”

They both emotionally hugged each other again and the tension was relieved in camp.
