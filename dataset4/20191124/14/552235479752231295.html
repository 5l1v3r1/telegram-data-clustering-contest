Steven Gerrard reveals his Rangers man of the match as boss appeals for 'ruthless' streak

The Rangers boss wants his side to be more composed in forward areas but there was one man who really impressed him.

24 Nov 2019, 14:49 by Jonathan McFarlane

Steven Gerrard praised Ryan Jack as his top performer but called for his team to be more decisive in front of goal after a 3-1 win against Hamilton.

The Rangers boss was also critical of his defenders as he watched his side huff and puff to victory, with individual brilliance rather than cohesive team play making the difference.

Rangers have played better but they are now grinding out results as a team challenging for the title must - something Gerrard was keen to stress despite his misgivings about the performance.

Speaking to BT Sport, he said: "We have certainly played better. I thought we played good in patches today but not consistently. I thought Hamilton competed really well and done well to stay in the game.

"We created enough but we weren't clinical or ruthless enough in front of goal.

"I think you have to give Hamilton credit. I think the easy one is to use the excuse of the international break but some of my better players today were my international players so we won't use that excuse. I think it's just us - at certain times we have to go and get the second goal quicker.

"We were poor on their goal. We were poor in three or four positions throughout the game where we haven't won the first header so there are a lot of bones to pick over from it.

"But we have won 3-1 away from home after the international break so we have to take the positives.

"We can't run the risk or chance it with a performance like that. We have to perform at a better level so the results are not at risk - because it could have been in those final stages.

Ryan Kent took the headlines with a double, with his first goal a barnstorming long-range finish into the top corner.

And Gerrard said: "It's a fantastic strike on his weak side, if he has one, and he puts it in the top corner. It's a fantastic goal and I really like the second "goal as well as it was more of a composed finish.

"Ryan (Jack) gets the first one and I thought Ryan was our best player today in terms of consistency through the 90 minutes."

Hamilton counterpart Brian Rice was happy with the performance, despite defeat and highlighted the display of his young players.

Rice said: "We set the team up to be as positive as possible. I've got two strikers on the pitch against Rangers and I've got a young kid at 17 playing centre-half.

"But they're in the team because they're good players. I like to think I'm a positive person, I try to look on the bright side of things.

"I think the team today reflected everything we want to do."
