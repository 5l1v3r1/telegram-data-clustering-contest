Axa SA (EPA:CS) Given Consensus Recommendation of “Buy” by Analysts

24 Nov 2019, 14:48 by Doug Wharley

Axa SA (EPA:CS) has received an average rating of “Buy” from the twelve brokerages that are covering the stock, MarketBeat reports. Three investment analysts have rated the stock with a hold rating and nine have assigned a buy rating to the company. The average 12-month target price among analysts that have covered the stock in the last year is €26.95 ($31.34).

Several analysts have recently commented on the stock. Goldman Sachs Group set a €29.00 ($33.72) price target on shares of AXA and gave the company a “buy” rating in a research report on Tuesday, November 19th. Morgan Stanley set a €26.70 ($31.05) price target on shares of AXA and gave the company a “buy” rating in a research report on Tuesday, August 6th. Barclays set a €24.30 ($28.26) price target on shares of AXA and gave the company a “neutral” rating in a research report on Thursday, October 24th. Royal Bank of Canada set a €29.50 ($34.30) price target on shares of AXA and gave the company a “buy” rating in a research report on Wednesday, July 31st. Finally, Nord/LB set a €27.00 ($31.40) price target on shares of AXA and gave the company a “buy” rating in a research report on Tuesday, August 27th.

Shares of CS stock traded up €0.02 ($0.02) during trading hours on Tuesday, hitting €25.02 ($29.09). The company’s stock had a trading volume of 4,341,950 shares, compared to its average volume of 9,520,000. The stock has a fifty day moving average of €24.12. AXA has a twelve month low of €22.13 ($25.73) and a twelve month high of €27.69 ($32.20).

AXA Company Profile

AXA SA, through its subsidiaries, provides insurance and asset management services worldwide. It operates in seven segments: France, Europe, Asia, AXA XL, the United States, International, and Transversal & Central Holdings. The company offers a range of insurance products, including life and savings, property and casualty, and health.
