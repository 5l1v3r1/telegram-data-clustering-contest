These two VAR calls ‘changed the game’. Moments like this are becoming all too common

24 Nov 2019, 21:11 by Max Sherry

A-League, Premier League or the World Cup, it doesn’t matter — the Video Assistant Referee (VAR) will always find a way to make a contentious decision.

Perth Glory became the controversial technology’s latest victim with two major calls going against them in their hotly-contested grand final replay with Sydney FC.

While VAR must be praised for correctly awarding Sydney an early penalty for a blatant shirt grab by Diego Castro, it later showed the same recurring inconsistency that has infuriated fans all season.

The first incident came when Sky Blues recruit Kosta Barbarouses’ goal was allowed to stand despite calls for an agonisingly close offside.

Barbarouses was put clean through on goal after a lofted ball from Brandon O’Neill and made no mistake with the finish as he fired the ball past Perth shot stopper Liam Reddy.

But VAR quickly intervened — or so we thought.

Referee Daniel Elder was made aware of a potential offside and play was subsequently delayed while VAR checked it.

Remarkably, those watching in the booth judged that Barbarouses’ shoulder blade was fractionally behind Ivan Franjic’s back foot — despite clear images that indicated otherwise.

Ultimately, the goal stood and Perth’s resulting cries fell on deaf ears.

But the VAR wasn’t done just yet.

Glory were robbed of their own breakthrough goal when Bruno Fornaroli was judged to be offside after pulling one back in stunning fashion.

The Perth forward, who had just become the fastest player to reach 50 A-League goals, rifled the ball into the back of the net with a venomous volley to reduce the deficit to just one goal.

But as the 32-year-old wheeled away in passionate celebration, VAR entered the frame to a chorus of boos around nib Stadium.

After a couple of minutes of deliberation, the officials chalked off the goal saying that Fornaroli received the ball in an offside position from Chris Harold’s deflected shot.

“I’m disappointed about this tonight,” Fornaroli said after the game.

“This changed the game.”

The score ultimately ended 3-1 to Sydney FC as Steve Corica’s side moved up to second on the A-League ladder.

As for Perth, they’re now in sixth place with just one win all season.
