PUT IT PLAQUE

Paisley widow blasts crematorium bosses who removed her late hubby’s pic from memorial plaque

24 Nov 2019, 21:22 by Chris Taylor

A WIDOW has hit out at crematorium bosses who removed her late hubby’s pic from a memorial plaque.

Grieving Mary Webb, 63, was stunned when told the memento of her beloved Graham was among scores ripped off to allow the stone to be cleaned.

And she says she was “disgusted” when officials revealed she’d have to rake through photos of dead strangers to find it again.

The ordeal came a year after the retired chef died of lung cancer aged 67 and was laid to rest at Woodside cremmy in Paisley.

Mary, of the town, had placed a pic from their engagement party 41 years ago on to a tribute message on a wall in the site’s memorial garden.

She explained: “Recently a friend who was at a funeral there told me Graham’s photograph was gone. I rushed over and it was away.

“I was told it was for cleaning but they’ve not been returned.

"I phoned them and they said I could trawl through all the pictures they’d taken.

“They are families’ personal memories, so nobody has any right to go through them.

“I was also told it would cost £50 to put up a pic on the wall. I was utterly disgusted.

“Now I have nowhere to grieve my husband. I just want to kiss his photo and say goodnight.”

Funeral directors Fosters, who took over the centre in June, charge £199 for a 4in x 10in plaque.

Signs warn “personal tributes” will be removed.

A spokesman said: “It was never our intention to cause any upset.

“We’re putting in a new records system to allow staff to locate any such items for families in future.”
