No handshake, no eye contact: A-League’s all-time scorer Besart Berisha fumes after bizarre substitution

24 Nov 2019, 21:23 by Max Sherry

Taking off the A-League’s all-time leading scorer when you’re team is in desperate need of a goal seems like a very bizarre decision to make.

Besart Berisha looked visibly upset and frustrated after being substituted by head coach Mark Rudan with his side trailing the Central Coast Mariners.

Chasing the game, Western United’s star striker was taken off just after the hour mark after missing multiple chances — opportunities a player of his calibre should be burying.

With his side needing a goal to level things up, it seemed only a matter of time before prolific sharp shooter Berisha would find the back of the net for the A-League newcomers.

But just as the 34-year-old was getting into his threatening stride, Western United boss Rudan had decided he’d seen enough.

Berisha looked bemused as the fourth official’s board went up confirming the substitution and slowly made his way towards the bench while unravelling the tape on his wrists.

Looking not best pleased with his manager’s questionable decision, the former Brisbane Roar and Melbourne Victory marksman stormed towards the dugout — avoiding an embrace or hand shake with Rudan.

Berisha is the A-League’s all-time leading goalscorer with 116 goals.
