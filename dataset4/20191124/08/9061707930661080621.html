Burton mum looks amazing after being 'shocked' into losing weight

She's been through an incredible change

24 Nov 2019, 08:00 by Rhea Turner

A Winshill mum was shocked into losing four stone following a stark warning from a colleague - and is now helping others do the same.

Angela Grist started piling on the pounds after the births of her children.

But after a recent health scare she decided enough was enough and swapped fish and chips for fresh fruit.

Angela, 55, said: "When the children were younger I just got used to looking after them first.

"I worked full time so I was cooking child-friendly meals that weren't always the healthiest, and eating out more.

"I have always eaten quite healthily but would have takeaways if I was tired.

"Sometimes it was just easier to go to the fish and chip shop.

"I was also drinking alcohol more regularly then I do now; I would have it three or four times a week.

"Before I knew it, my weight was creeping up.

"You try to lose weight and think you can manage it, but then, when you are happy and comfortable in a relationship and you get complacent, the weight piles on again."

Angela, who works in mental health services, said she felt her weight was holding her back on a trip to New York, but her wake-up call came from a colleague who warned her about her health.

She said: "I couldn’t enjoy the things I wanted to. I went to New York with my husband and I struggled walking round everywhere.

"Then a couple of years ago I had a brain scan as I was getting quite severe headaches. Thankfully the scan was all clear but I spoke to a colleague about it.

"I work in memory services so I asked one of my colleagues who is a doctor about it. He told me not to worry, but ten minutes later he came to find me and said I need to look after my cardiovascular hygiene, which basically told me to watch my weight and look after my cholesterol and blood pressure.

"Working with people with dementia I know that if those things are compromised there can be serious health risks.

"It dawned on me then that I was putting my own health at risk and it made me change my lifestyle."

The mother of two decided to join her local Slimming World group after previously having success with the programme.

She said: "I have done it two or three times over a span of 25 years and this is my third journey with them.

"It was the late 80s and early 90s when I first started and I hadn’t been in about 12 years when I decided to go back.

"After meeting my husband, Simon, I wanted to enjoy life more.

"I wanted to be able to go to normal shops and buy things off the rack."

Angela tipped the scales at 15 stone and five pounds when she joined the Burton group in 2015 and was wearing sizes 16 to 18.

But after changing her diet she says her life has been changed and she now fits in to sizes 10 to 12 - and sometimes an eight.

She said: "I have lost nearly four stone in total and I feel like a different person.

"My ultimate aim was to get healthier. I wanted to walk without getting out of breath and get my confidence back, but the change has been incredible.

"I have been at my target weight for a couple of years now and I have much more energy and I feel more confident. I can actually dress up and look nice.

"Sometimes it is really easy to think I have reached target and take your foot off the gas, so I joined a social team which keeps me motivated and I am always trying to help others.

"I have been called an inspiration and given the title of 'woman of the year' from the other slimmers.

"It has changed the way I think about food.

"I batch-cook now. I get the cookbook out and plan ahead and my husband is very supportive and gets involved too."

Her newfound confidence has given Angela a boost and she is now taking a leap into helping others by joining Slimming World as a consultant.

She said: "It is a bit daunting but I am very excited. I have been asked before but with working full time I couldn’t commit.

"However, I am retiring next year and I have made so many encouraging friends that, when the opportunity came about, they gave me the belief I could do it."
