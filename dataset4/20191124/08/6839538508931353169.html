NSW abseiler falls off cliff after wasp sting

24 Nov 2019, 08:08

An abseiler who fell off a cliff after he was stung by a wasp has been rescued in a joint operation involving police and paramedics.

The 37-year-old man - who had a collapsed lung, broken ribs, and a fractured leg - could not be rescued overnight due to poor weather.

The man fell about 10 metres after a wasp stung him while he was abseiling on Saturday, causing an anaphylactic allergic reaction, NSW Police said.

Police were called to the Kanangra-Boyd National Park in the central tablelands region on Saturday afternoon.
