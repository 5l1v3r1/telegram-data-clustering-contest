Why Ryan Lowe says Plymouth Argyle do not need to have a Plan B in games

The Pilgrims' boss believes sticking to his principles is starting to pay off for his side

24 Nov 2019, 08:11 by Chris Errington

Ryan Lowe believes his insistence on Plymouth Argyle sticking to his Plan A for playing football is starting to pay off.

The Pilgrims have lost only one of their last eight League Two games – the 4-0 Devon Derby drubbing by Exeter City last month.

Argyle’s 2-1 victory over high-flying Bradford City at Home Park on Saturday was their fourth win out of the last five.

Lowe, appointed as their manager in the summer, has seen his side begin to play with more consistency over recent weeks.

There is also a better understanding from the players about what he wants and from them and the win against Bradford highlighted that.

Lowe said: “It gradually takes time. We have had discussions with the lads and we have shown them video clips of what we have been good at and how we have done it.

“But the football takes care of itself. We put the sessions on for the lads to play that way, and as you have seen, every single one of them – man to man – was excellent in it.

“I think there was a move on the 17th minute when we nearly scored and I was like ‘Wow, that would have been some goal’.

“Look, that’s my style of play. That’s what I want from my boys and they are taking on board everything we ask for.

“It doesn’t always come off because you have got opposition that are trying to stop you but when we are in full flow like that we look a good team.”

Argyle led 2-0 at half-time against Bradford, whose boss Gary Bowyer made a double substitution and switched from a 4-3-3 system to 3-5-2.

Initially, that posed some problems for the Pilgrims, whose lead was cut by a Niall Canavan own goal in the 51st minute.

But Lowe was not tempted to try something different tactically and, in the end, Argyle came out on top.

Lowe said: “There is not many times we will change our formation. We will just keep plugging away at it.

“They say ‘Do you have a Plan B?’ Well, no, just do Plan A better in some circumstances.

“Gary has obviously changed and it was effective more for them, but they have played that again this season also so they can adapt to it.

“Come the end of it you think ‘Well, okay, we have nullified their 4-3-3 and we have certainly nullified the 3-5-2’.”

Argyle have won all three of their games since they lost heavily to Exeter at St James Park on October 26.

Lowe said: “We are on a good run of form and we have got to continue that. There will probably be another loss somewhere along the way because you can’t win every game.

“We have said the league is so inconsistent, but we are looking forward now.
