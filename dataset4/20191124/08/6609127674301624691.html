ROAD DRAMA

Woman rushed to hospital with serious injuries after being hit by car near Glenrothes shopping centre

24 Nov 2019, 08:49 by Ewan Mowat

A WOMAN has been rushed to hospital with serious injuries after being hit by a car in Fife last night.

The 49-year-old was struck by the motor near Kingdom Shopping Centre in Glenrothes around 6.45pm.

Emergency services raced to Church Street following the smash.

The road was locked down by police as they probed the crash.

The woman was taken by ambulance to Ninewells Hospital in Dundee, where she is in a serious but stable condition.

Cops are now calling on anyone who may have seen the crash to come forward.

Sgt Alastair Purvis, of Glenrothes Road Policing Unit, said: “The woman was taken to hospital with serious injuries but is now in stable condition.

"We are appealing for anyone who may have witnessed the crash to come forward.”

A spokesperson for the force said: "Around 6.45pm on Saturday November 23, police were called to a road traffic collision involving a car and a pedestrian on Church Street, Glenrothes.

"Emergency services attended and the road was closed.

"A 49-year-old woman was taken to Ninewells Hospital with serious injuries.

"Enquiries are ongoing."

It came just over an hour after cops swooped on the shopping centre after reports of a man with a "knife and a firearm".

Cops recovered the blade and the replica gun.

A 16-year-old was arrested.
