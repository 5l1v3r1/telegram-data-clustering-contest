Jose Mourinho's earnings from being hired and fired by Premier League clubs

The Portuguese has taken over from Mauricio Pochettino at Tottenham Hotspur, and will take charge of his first game at West Ham on Saturday lunchtime

24 Nov 2019, 08:30 by Mark Jones, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/mark-jones/

It is safe to say that the Premier League is a little bit obsessed with Jose Mourinho.

The former Chelsea and Manchester United boss strode into Tottenham Hotspur this week, taking over from the sacked Mauricio Pochettino and signing a three-and-a-half year deal in North London.

Mourinho will took charge of his first Tottenham game on Saturday when his new side went to West Ham in a London derby, with that match officially kicking off his fourth spell in the Premier League.

And those four spells have been very, very lucrative ones.

When Mourinho breezed into English football as 'The Special One' at Chelsea back in 2004, he signed a deal worth £5million a year as per a CNN report at the time.

Just over three years later he was out on his ear though, as a row with Roman Abramovich and deepening tensions at the club led to him walking out.

Ten years after that the Mirror's James Whaling revealed that Chelsea paid out £23.1million to Mourinho and his staff when they were axed from the club, eventually turning up at Inter Milan the following summer.

After successes at Inter and Real Madrid , Mourinho was back in England in the summer of 2013 as he started a second spell with Chelsea that was worth £8.5million a year according to a Guardian report at the time.

Mourinho won the Premier League title in 2014/15, but he was off again just seven months later with the Mirror reporting that the Portuguese and his staff were given an £8.3million payoff.

Manchester United were the next club to come calling for his services, and when he was appointed as the new Old Trafford boss in 2016 it was reported by USA Today that he signed a £12million a year deal, although he was also earning more from endorsements.

His United adventure ended in acrimony and a £19.6m payofffor him and his staff in December last year, while he's now popped up at Spurs on a £15million a year , three-and-a-half year deal.

That Premier League obsession certainly has been very good to him.
