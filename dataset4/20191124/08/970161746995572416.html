Man stabbed to death outside west London train station

24 Nov 2019, 08:43

A man has been stabbed to death outside a west London train station.

Emergency services were called to West Ealing station, on Drayton Green Road, at 1.18am this morning.

They found the victim, in his 20s, suffering from a stab wound and he later died at the scene, despite the best efforts of paramedics.

Enquiries are underway to trace and notify next of kin, while no arrests have been made.

Officers are still working to formally identify the man and a post mortem will take place in due course.

Images from the scene show a damaged black Mini Cooper and a white BMW, with clothing, medical equipment and a pair of trainers scattered across the road.

A taxi office manager said he hard a ‘loud bang’ near the rank, before seeing a man ‘get out and stab another driver’, the Mirror reports.

He said: ‘Lots of police turned up and paramedics. We have been told to stay in our office and not to leave.’

It is the second fatal stabbing in London over two days, after one man was killed in Whitechapel on Saturday morning.

Three others were injured in the violent incident and rushed to hospital by emergency services.

Police are appealing to anyone with information about the West Ealing stabbing to get in touch.

Officers can be contacted on 101, quoting 550/24NOV, or tweeted via @MetCC.

Alternatively, they can be contacted anonymously through Crimestoppers on 0800 555 111.
