Ellie Gould demanded tough laws for young killers weeks before boy, 17, murdered her

Ellie Gould, 17, wrote in an A-level assignment asking for proper punishment for kids who kill weeks before her teen ex-boyfriend Thomas Griffths stabbed her to death

24 Nov 2019, 05:13 by Tiffany Lo

A schoolgirl wrote an essay calling for tougher penalties for children who kill - just weeks before she was stabbed to death by her 17-year-old boyfriend.

Ellie Gould, 17, was murdered at her parent's home the day after she broke up with "obsessed" Thomas Griffiths in May.

In an A-level assignment just weeks earlier, Ellie mentioned the murder of two-year-old James Bulger in 1993.

She wrote about how the families of such victims deserved justice and demanded proper punishment for children who kill.

Just weeks later she was stabbed to death in a jealous rage by Griffiths, who placed the knife in her dead hand to try to make the murder look like a suicide.

Griffiths, 17, was sentenced handed a life sentence and must serve a minimum of 12 years and six months.

Ellie's mum Carole, of Calne, Wiltshire, said: "Ellie thought sentences for child killers should bring justice for victims' families so that's what we're fighting for.

"Ellie would be screaming at me to get her the justice she deserves."

She said she will 'never forgive Griffiths'.

"It was shocking that he was capable of such violence," she told The Sun .

Griffiths will be eligible to apply for a sentence reduction after half the time imposed.

Due to his age, the serving terms can be shortened if 'the offender's welfare may be seriously prejudiced by his continued imprisonment'.

Ellie's family say he should serve at least 17 years, equivalent to Ellie's age, but a court decision on any review will be made on December 6.

In an interview with the BBC, Mrs Gould said she wanted to see Griffiths spend the rest of his life in prison.

"He's a danger to society, particularly to women," she said.

"He became obsessed with Ellie within a matter of weeks. He could become obsessed with another woman and who knows what could happen?"
