10 of the most beautifully romantic hotels you just have to take your partner

They have the potential to leave even the most tense couples all loved up

24 Nov 2019, 11:53 by Julie Bayley

Some hotels are made for romance.

Whether it's parents desperate to escape mad family life for a night or a couple just wanting to change things up, weekends away can do wonders for any relationship.

Inspectors behind the Good Hotel Guide 2020 visited hundreds of hotels for this year's edition - and kept an eye out for the best places in the country for a sybaritic weekend.

Here we highlight their top 10, which range from a Victorian fantasy castle to a glamorous boutique hotel, and quote the description from the guide itself.

Langar Hall,

Langar, Nottingham

With poetry on the walls and a canopy four-poster, the Bohemia room is the honeymooners’ choice at this Georgian mansion in the Vale of Belvoir. Others prefer the Cartland room, beloved of the doyenne of romantic fiction, the late Dame Barbara. The hotel is described as charming, relaxed and caring.

Room rate: B&B from £125

Augill Castle

Kirkby Stephen, Cumbria

This Victorian fantasy castle in the Eden valley, with views of fells and dales, enchants all who stay. Most bedrooms have a four-poster, some a real fire. The Gatehouse suite occupies one of two towers, with private garden.

Room rate: B&B from £180

Lewtrenchard Manor

Lewdown, Devon

Classic rooms, some with four-poster beds, overlooking gardens and parkland. Rooms are filled with antiques, oil paintings, roaring fires and a friendly atmosphere. It's described as a wonderful hotel with a special feel.

Room rate: B&B from £180

La Sablonnerie

Little Sark, Channel Islands

Arrive by horse-drawn carriage at this 16th-century stone farmhouse on a car-free island ablaze with gorse in summer. Sleep in a rustic cottage bedroom. Take a dip in the pools of Venus and Adonis. Dine in a rose-filled garden. This hotel is unabashedly old-fashioned.

Room rate: B&B from £137

Hazlitt's

London

You can repose on a bed adorned with gilded cherubs and soak in a vintage roll-top bath at this sumptuous boutique hotel in raffish Soho, adorned with silks and velvets, portraits and porcelains, the last home of 18th-century essayist William Hazlitt. A sensuous escape.

Room rate: B&B from £232

Castle Leslie

Glaslough, Co. Monaghan

Rooms come with tales of wartime espionage, whirlwind romance and royal visits, at Samantha Leslie’s ancestral lakeside pile. Book flamboyant Aggie’s room with four-poster, a roll-top bath in a curtained alcove.

Room rate: B&B from 140 Euros.

The Pig at Bridge Place

Bridge, Kent

The latest in the Pig family occupies a fabulous Jacobean manor and former music venue that hosted the likes of Led Zeppelin. Climb a stairway to the heaven of a four-poster room, or sleep in a hop pickers’ hut, on stilts, by the river.

Room rate: From £110

Coes Faen

Barmouth, Gwynedd, Wales

Rooms come with luxury extras at this astonishing B&B overlooking the Mawddach estuary. Perhaps a Welsh slate steam room; an outdoor cedar hot tub; a rock terrace with views of Snowdonia. And Tuscan style dinners.

Room rate: From £185

Glenapp Castle

Ballantrae

A fountain sparkles on the forecourt of this turreted, Scottish baronial-style castle, reached by a wooded drive. Bedrooms have sea or garden views, The Penthouse offers a sauna and roof terrace. Dine as the sun sets over Ailsa Craig and the Mull of Kintyre.

Room rate: From £395

No.15 Great Pulteney

Bath

The Hideout Suite at this glamorous boutique hotel has everything for a romantic weekend, with statement art and lighting, a sitting room with a real-flame gas fire, a Sonos music system, hot tub and steam-room shower.

Room rate: From £115
