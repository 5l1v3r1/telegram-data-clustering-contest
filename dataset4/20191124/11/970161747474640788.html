Strictly Come Dancing judge Bruno Tonioli thanks fans for loving his singing

24 Nov 2019, 11:52

Strictly Come Dancing’s Bruno Tonioli has thanked viewers for showing the love after he sang on the show.

Bruno sang and danced the show’s opening number on Saturday night’s live show.

Taking to the stage in a white tuxedo and surrounded by pink feather fans, the Strictly judge performed Can’t Take My Eyes Off You.

On Twitter the 63-year-old sent a message of thanks to viewers for all their positive comments.

Admitting he was ‘nervous’ about performing, he tweeted: ‘Thank you all so much for your kind comments on my singing!! I was so nervous, I’ve been practising so much as well as having singing lessons… something different and certainly took me outside my comfort zone.’

He was congratulated by the show’s band, who tweeted him: ‘You did brilliantly Bruno’.

Viewers were loving the ‘camp’ performance and some called for him to do it every week.

Bruno recently defended the show’s same sex routine between dancers Johannes Radebe and Graziano di Prima.

The two men performed to Shine by Emeli Sande during a recent results show, which racked up nearly 200 complaints from viewers.

Describing the news as ‘very sad’, he tweeted: ‘It’s hard to believe after such progress in society and many other topics going on that over 200 people felt so upset they complained when 2 men danced with each other… I just don’t know what to say… Very sad. #bbcstrictly.’

Saturday was an emotional night for contestant Karim Zeroual, who broke down in tears after dedicating his performance to his mum.

The star took on the couples’ choice routine with a contemporary dance to Drops Of Jupiter by Train.

Strictly Come Dancing continues tonight at 7.15pm on BBC One.
