Unseen photos that show Wirral as you've never seen it before

Photos from the 1930s right up to the 1990s show Wirral in a different light

24 Nov 2019, 11:49 by Lorna Hughes

Fascinating images from our archives reveal Wirral as you've never seen it before - from the way we used to travel to sunny days beside the seaside for the Wirral Show.

We've delved into the ECHO's archives for these photos from the 1930s right up to the 1990s.

Over the years our photographers have snapped everything from the summer days that saw holidaymakers flocking to New Brighton to the changing fortunes of our town centres and incredible aerial views giving a glimpse of how our streets used to look.

We were also granted access to some of Wirral's most famous work places, including Squibbs, Cadbury's in Moreton and Birkenhead's Cammell Laird.

One photo from March 1949 shows Wirral’s Remploy site, part of a network of factories set up after World War II to provide sheltered employment for disabled people.

The Liverpool ECHO story accompanying it read: “A corner of the bright workroom where disabled men are taught to work with the leather.

"On the left is a cutting out punch, centre a woman instructor is seen giving guidance at the sewing machine, and right others are having instruction in hand cutting of the leather.”

Sadly the factory, which specialised in textile production, shut in 2012 as part of a raft of closures after the government announced it was cutting its funding.

Other archive images show radical changes for Birkenhead town centre in the 1960s with the construction of flyovers to reduce congestion caused by traffic heading to the Birkenhead tunnel.

Crowds took part in a walkover on July 14, 1969 - the day before the flyovers opened - while one striking photo shows how Shaftesbury Boys Club on Thomas Street was cut off from the town by a flyover passing only a few feet from the front door.

The ECHO reported that the club intended to build a new £70,000 HQ on a 6.5 acre site off Borough Road.

In the 1950s and 60s New Brighton was the place to be in the summer months, and our photos show packed beaches, the fun of donkey rides by Fort Perch Rock and the excitement of a model boat race in New Brighton in August 1954.

And they show the sad end for New Brighton’s famous miniature railway after it was sold at auction, together with the adjoining playground, for £567.

The locomotive Crompton and a 24-seat coach were bought for £400 by Bill Morris, 50, of Hereford - who’s pictured, and planned to run the railway at fetes and shows.  

Over in Hoylake , we took a photo of Hugh Williamson leading excitable youngsters gathered for the town's annual beach mission - a mix of evangelism and fun and games for children - in August 1975.

New Brighton Baths is Wirral's most famous open-air swimming pool but our photos also show what it was like at the nearby Derby Pool in the height of summer - and the rarely seen pool in New Ferry.

Meanwhile images of Wirral’s best known street scenes show how they've undergone a radical transformation over the years.

What is now a branch of Domino's and a charity shop in Liscard was once the ABC cinema - and nearby Liscard Road was a popular, bustling shopping street.

Our archive images also show a deserted Grange Road, Birkenhead on May 6, 1960, when shoppers stayed at home to watch the Royal wedding.

Princess Margaret married Antony Armstrong-Jones at Westminster Abbey - the first Royal wedding to be broadcast on television.
