SSE PLC/S (OTCMKTS:SSEZY) Given Average Rating of “Hold” by Analysts

24 Nov 2019, 13:12 by Amy Steele

Shares of SSE PLC/S (OTCMKTS:SSEZY) have been given a consensus rating of “Hold” by the eight brokerages that are covering the company, MarketBeat reports. Six research analysts have rated the stock with a hold recommendation and two have given a buy recommendation to the company.

A number of analysts recently issued reports on the company. Berenberg Bank lowered SSE PLC/S from a “buy” rating to a “hold” rating in a report on Thursday, November 7th. HSBC upgraded SSE PLC/S from a “hold” rating to a “buy” rating in a report on Tuesday, October 22nd. Zacks Investment Research lowered SSE PLC/S from a “buy” rating to a “hold” rating in a report on Thursday. Credit Suisse Group lowered SSE PLC/S from an “outperform” rating to a “neutral” rating in a report on Monday, October 14th. Finally, Barclays upgraded SSE PLC/S from an “underweight” rating to an “equal weight” rating in a report on Monday, September 30th.

Shares of OTCMKTS SSEZY remained flat at $$16.80 during trading hours on Tuesday. The company had a trading volume of 126,810 shares, compared to its average volume of 44,408. The company has a debt-to-equity ratio of 1.48, a quick ratio of 1.00 and a current ratio of 1.03. The company has a 50-day moving average price of $16.50 and a two-hundred day moving average price of $14.74. SSE PLC/S has a 12-month low of $12.75 and a 12-month high of $17.46.

About SSE PLC/S

SSE plc engages in the generation, transmission, distribution, and supply of electricity. The company operates through three segments: Wholesale, Networks, and Retail. It generates electricity from gas, oil, coal, water, and wind. The company transmits and distributes electricity to approximately 3.7 million homes and businesses across the north of the central belt of Scotland and also central southern England; owns and maintains 132 kilovolts (KV), 275kV, and 400kV electricity transmission network; and manages 130,000 kilometers of overhead lines and underground cables, 106,000 substations, and approximately 100 subsea cables.
