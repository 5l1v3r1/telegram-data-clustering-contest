Chris Martin pays his children to appear on latest Coldplay album

24 Nov 2019, 13:46

Chris Martin has revealed that he paid his children to appear on Coldplay’s latest album.

The rocker’s two children, 15-year-old Apple and 13-year-old Moses, who he shares with ex-wife Gwyneth Paltrow, have featured on a track on his band’s latest album, Everyday Life, and he revealed that he also asked them if they wanted to be a part of the new record.

‘I asked them. And I pay them, proper fees like everyone,’ he revealed. ‘And they are allowed to say no.’

Chris also revealed that being a father has had some influence on the fifth track on the album, Daddy.

‘I don’t like explaining songs. However, that came from three places,’ he added to The Sunday Times’ Culture magazine. ‘First, I have many people in my life whose dads f*** off, which is sad and confusing and, if you empathise as a dad, you wonder, what was he feeling that he thought that would be best? Second, I have kids, and I have to leave all the time.’

The 42-year-old explained that learning about the continued existence of racism and lack of children without fathers in the United States also played an important part in shaping the song.

‘[That song was] written about learning about the prison industrial complex in America; the outright racism woven into so many laws, and kids, who, as a result, are denied their fathers,’  he said.

‘The world is in such a way that you can no longer just sit back with your creature comforts and ignore the rest of it.’

It comes after Coldplay recently revealed that they will not tour for their new album unless their shows will be carbon neutral, as they want to do their bit for the environment.

‘We’re taking time to see how our tour can be actively beneficial,’ Chris told BBC News. ‘All of us have to work out the best way of doing our job.

‘We’re taking time over the next year or two to work out how can, not only our tour be sustainable, but how it can be actively beneficial.’

Coldplay’s eighth studio album Everyday Life is out now.
