Tyson Fury’s trainer responds to Deontay Wilder knocking out Luis Ortiz

Wilder had lost each of the first six rounds to the tricky Cuban in their heavyweight world title rematch on Saturday night

24 Nov 2019, 13:23 by Mark Jones, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/mark-jones/

Tyson Fury’s trainer dismissed concerns about Deontay Wilder’s freak power after his devastating KO of Luis Ortiz.

Wilder had lost each of the first six rounds to the tricky Cuban in their heavyweight world title rematch on Saturday night.

But he detonated a stunning right hand in the dying embers of the seventh session to set up a February rematch with Fury.

The Brit’s trainer Ben Davison was ringside in Las Vegas and believes his man will prove a far greater test than Ortiz.

“I’ve said before, Luis Ortiz is a fantastic fighter but he’s not Tyson Fury ,” he told Sky Sports.

“I also learned that he [Wilder] was a different man fight week to what he was to when he fought Tyson.

“Tyson 100 per cent got under his skin, I definitely saw that in there, which is pretty understandable.

“The pressure of a big fight around the corner affected him.

“People will say he looked poor, but he took his time and he did what he was supposed to do.”

Fury was dropped twice by Wilder last December but recovered on both occasions to hear the final bell.

But despite doing enough to win the fight, the challenger was denied victory when the judges scored the bout a draw.

And Davison is confident he will have the game plan to complete the job early next year.

“Obviously his power is something you have to negate,’ he said.

“Mentally, Tyson is the strongest fighter I’ve come across in history and he’s proved that on a number of occasions.

“How do we do it? I’m not going to say my ideas on how you would do it. But it’s definitely not what Luis Ortiz just did there.

“Fair play to him, he had a good go but he got complacent and walked straight into distance. That’s a recipe for disaster.”
