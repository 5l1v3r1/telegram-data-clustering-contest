Jadon Joseph joins Rockets

24 Nov 2019, 13:58 by Miriam Halpenny

The Kelowna Rockets announced today that they have acquired 20-year-old centre Jadon Joseph from the Moose Jaw Warriors.

Joseph was exchanged for forward Kyle Crosbie, overage forward Kobe Mohr and goaltender James Porter.

“We needed to get a right shot centre-man that can consistently win face-offs,” said Rockets president and GM Bruce Hamilton. 

“Kobe was a big part of this deal, they really wanted him back in the trade. James wanted to get back into the league and Crosbie wanted a chance to play more minutes.”

For the Moose Jaw Warriors, Joseph has appeared in 21 games this season, posting 13 points and 21 penalty minutes.

In the last four seasons, he has played in 209 WHL regular season games, collecting 113 points and 120 penalty minutes.

Joseph has also played for Lethbridge, Vancouver and Regina.

He will be making his debut with the Rockets on Tuesday at Prospera Place at 7:05 p.m., against the Edmonton Oil Kings.
