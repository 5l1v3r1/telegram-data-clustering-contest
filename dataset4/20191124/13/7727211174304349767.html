Blue Story film banned from all Merseyside Vue cinemas after mass brawl

Following violent clashes in a Birmingham cinema all Merseyside cinemas have banned the new film

24 Nov 2019, 13:07 by Olivia Tobin

Film Blue Story will not be shown in any Merseyside cinemas, after a mass brawl broke out involving more than 100 youths.

Violent clashes were seen in Star City entertainment complex, near Nechells, Birmingham, on Saturday evening as officers rushed to break up a fight.

Police stormed the cinema after receiving reports of "more than 100 people" fighting with machetes.

Videos shared on social media showed teenage girls and boys involved in the fight, as police arrested a number of youngsters, including a 13-year-old girl.

Following the violence, the British drama has now been pulled from all Vue cinemas across the country.

A recorded message on the cinema answer phone says that the movie will no longer be screened at any of its venues.

The message is heard when trying to call Vue's branch in Birkenhead.

It said: "We regret that we will no longer be screening the film Blue Story at any of our venues.

"Should you have a booking for this film, please say cancellation when prompted and a member of our team will be with you shortly to assist you with your booking."

It is understood that the film is banned from Southport Vue and Cheshire Oaks cinemas too.

The film was released on Friday and it stars Top Boy's actor Michael Ward.

A description of the film reads: "Timmy and Marco are best friends who live in different London boroughs.

"That’s until Marco is beaten up by one of Timmy’s friends, and the pair are drawn into a gang war where your postcode decides which side you’re on.

"Rapman, real name Andrew Onwubolu, directs this British drama about the effects of gang violence in London."

Saturday's fight erupted at about 5.30pm as many children had flocked to the entertainment complex dressed in Frozen costumes for the sequel of the Disney hit.
