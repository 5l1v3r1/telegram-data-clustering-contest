EastEnders pay tribute to Dame Barbara Windsor by naming Louise's baby Peggy

24 Nov 2019, 13:30

EastEnders is set to pay tribute to the soap legend Dame Barbara Windsor by naming a new Walford resident after her infamous character Peggy Mitchell. Peggy’s granddaughter Louise Mitchell (played by Tilly Keeper) is pregnant with Keanu Taylor’s (Danny Walters) baby and is due to give birth in upcoming weeks, with the unborn infant to be christened after the late Queen Vic landlord.

Barbara’s husband Scott Mitchell revealed the actress was thrilled by the soap’s decision and it made her ‘quite emotional’.

He admitted: ‘She thought what a lovely thing it is Peggy Mitchell will live on in a family member. What a lovely tribute.

‘It made her quite emotional and I have been reminding her each time we watch the show every night.’

Scott added to The Sun: ‘Each time her whole face lights up.’

Babs’ Peggy first arrived in Albert Square back in 1994, having taken over the role from Jo Warne.

She was a major character on the soap with a number of huge storylines, such as her failed relationship with Archie Mitchell (Larry Lamb) and her on/off friendship with Pat Evans (Pam St Clement), and of course had the iconic catchphrase ‘get out of my pub’.

Peggy bowed out of EastEnders in 2016 when it was revealed she had refused her cancer treatment. She later took her own life in a bid to not be beaten by the illness she was facing.
