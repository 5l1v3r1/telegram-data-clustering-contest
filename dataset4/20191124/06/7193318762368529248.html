OU women's basketball: Cougars strike early then hold off Sooners

24 Nov 2019, 06:07

Oklahoma dropped its first road contest of the season as Houston down the Sooners, 85-69, on Saturday.

Junior guard Ana Llanusa led the way with 21 points for her fifth straight game in double figures, but she missed all seven of her 3-point shots. Oklahoma was only 5 of 22 as a team from behind the 3-point arc.

Both teams shot poorly from the 3-point line and free throw line. The Sooners sank 10 of 18 free throw attempts while Houston was 14 of 22. The Cougars made 7 of 28 3-point shots.

Nydia Lampkin added 10 points and six rebounds for the Sooners. Madi Williams scored 10 points and pulled down nine rebounds and three assists.

The Cougars jumped on the Sooners early, leading 28-16 after the first period. Oklahoma cut the lead to 40-35 at halftime but Houston (3-2) took control by outscoring the Sooners 23-15 in the third quarter.

Houston had five players score in double figures. Oklahoma (3-2), hosts Abilene Christian on Tuesday. Game time is 7 p.m.
