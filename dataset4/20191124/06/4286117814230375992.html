NCP-Congress-Sena alliance has clear majority of 165 MLAs, says Sanjay Raut ahead of SC hearing

Shiv Sena MP Sanjay Raut has said the NCP-Sena-Congress alliance has the support of 165 MLAs and is in a position to form the government in Maharashtra.

24 Nov 2019, 06:12

Ahead of the Supreme Court hearing of the joint petition against Maharashtra governor's decision to swear-in Devendra Fadnavis as the chief minister, Shiv Sena has said the NCP-Congress-Sena alliance has 165 MLAs in its support.

Sanjay Raut said, "Sharad Pawar is a national leader. If BJP is trying to form the government, it will not happen. It is a wrong step taken by BJP and Ajit Pawar. 165 MLAs are with Shiv Sena, Congress and NCP."

Sanjay Raut also said the November 30 deadline given to prove majority in Maharashtra Assembly is nothing but eyewash only to enable defections.

Meanwhile, NCP leader Jayant Patil, who was appointed the NCP legislature party leader after the removal of Ajit Pawar, has said NCP has 51 MLAs with the party. Jayant Patil offered a letter to governor informing Ajit Pawar's replacement as legislature party leader.

NCP won 54 seats in the Maharashtra assembly elections, some of who went with Ajit Pawar to the early morning surprise swearing-in ceremony of Devendra Fadnavis and Pawar. NCP has said most of the MLAs who went to the swearing-in have come back to the party.

According to party sources, Jayant Patil has claimed the support of 51 MLAs. He reached the Raj Bhavan on Sunday morning to present a letter to the Governor with the signatures of 51 MLAs.

I have approached Raj Bhavan with the signatures of 51 MLAs. Governor is in Delhi, Patil said according to sources.

Shiv, NCP and Congress on Saturday moved the Supreme Court seeking quashing of Maharashtra governor's move to swear-in Devendra Fadnavis and Ajit Pawar as the chief minister and deputy chief minister of the state.

The newly forged alliance between arch rivals Shiv Sena and NCP-Congress has said they have enough support that will go past the required majority in the Maharashtra assembly and Fadnavis government has been appointed with unfair means.

The three parties have also sought a direction to Governor Bhagat Singh Koshyari to invite them to form the government under the leadership of Sena chief Uddhav Thackeray.

Seeking quashing of the governor's decision inviting Fadnavis to form government, the plea said it is "unconstitutional, arbitrary and illegal", and violates Article 14 of the Constitution.
