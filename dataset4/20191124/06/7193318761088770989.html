Oklahoma scene: OSU runners become All-Americans

24 Nov 2019, 06:07

OSU runners become All-Americans

Oklahoma State cross country runners Molly Born and Taylor Somers earned All-American status Saturday at the NCAA Championships in Terre Haute, Indiana.

Both Cowgirls finished inside the top-20, as Born took 16th and Somers took 18th. The pair became the two highest-finishing women that OSU's Dave Smith has coached in his 11 seasons at the helm.

Born, a sophomore from Lake Quivira, Kansas, ran a time of 20:28.2 in the 6K race. Somers, an Omaha, Nebraska, native, ran a time of 20:30.

Born and Somers became the first Cowgirl cross country All-Americans since Ingeborg Loevnes in 2015, and the 11th and 12th Cowgirls in program history.
