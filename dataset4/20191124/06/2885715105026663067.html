Explained: In Maharashtra drama, the key legal provision — anti-defection law

NCP supremo Sharad Pawar has said that his nephew's action is an act of indiscipline, and attracts the provisions of the anti-defection law. What is this law?

24 Nov 2019, 06:05 by Chakshu Roy

In the extraordinary political drama unfolding in Maharashtra since dawn on Saturday (November 23), it remains unclear precisely how many Nationalist Congress Party (NCP) lawmakers are with Ajit Pawar in his decision to join hands with the BJP.

NCP supremo Sharad Pawar has said that his nephew’s action is an act of indiscipline, and attracts the provisions of the anti-defection law.

“NCP MLAs who support the BJP should know this move attracts provisions of anti-defection law,” Pawar said in a joint press conference with Shiv Sena chief Uddhav Thackeray who until past midnight on Friday looked set to be the next Chief Minister of Maharashtra.
