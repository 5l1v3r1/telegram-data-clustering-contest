Luis Ortiz ahead on scorecards when Deontay Wilder produced stunning knockout

24 Nov 2019, 06:41

Deontay Wilder was behind on all three judges’ scorecards when he knocked out Luis Ortiz in the seventh round of their rematch in Las Vegas.

The heavyweight champion set up a second showdown with Tyson Fury in February after successfully defending his WBC title with another explosive knockout.

But Wilder took his time feeling his way into the fight and hadn’t troubled Ortiz before firing off a game changing right hand.

The referee called off the fight as Ortiz struggled to get back to his feet and the scorecards show just how close the Cuban came to securing revenge over Wilder.

All three judges had Ortiz ahead after seven rounds, with Dave Moretti and Steve Weisfeld scoring it 59-55 and Eric Cheek seeing it 58-56.

Ortiz appeared disappointed to be pulled out of the contest, but admitted he was surprised by how fast the count was.

‘This is boxing, I told everybody that it was going to be him or me and that it wasn’t going to go 12 rounds,’ he said.

‘I was clear-headed. When he said seven I was trying to get up but the count was maybe quicker than I thought.

‘I’m ready for whatever comes about.’

Wilder confirmed after his victory he would fight Fury next and explained why he couldn’t take any chances against Ortiz.
