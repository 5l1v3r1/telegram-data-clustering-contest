Exclusive: Former Yeovil Town boss is leading contender for Bristol City post

Lee Johnson's old midfield teammate has held talks about U23s position

24 Nov 2019, 06:00 by Gregor MacGregor

Former Yeovil Town manager Darren Way is the leading contender to join Bristol City and take the Robins' vacant U23s head coach role.

Bristol Live understands that Way is keen on the position and that preliminary talks have been held over the job.

City are conducting a thorough recruitment process for the post, however, and are believed to have widened the search for a successor to former U23s boss Luke Williams as wide as possible. A final decision has yet to be made.

Williams left the club 10 days ago, as we exclusively revealed recently, later being announced as joining up as an assistant head coach at MK Dons.

Assistant U23s coach Alex Ball has been standing in since then, with the Bristol City Development Squad beating Crystal Palace U23s 1-2 at the Eagles' training ground last week, and the Robins have considered promoting Ball permanently.

But, according to our sources, Way remains the leading contender for the post, given his good relationship with Johnson and Jamie McAllister (having played alongside the Bristol City head coach and been a coach at Yeovil Town when McAllister was there too), knowledge of the football scene in the South West and previous work with the Glovers.

Way took over at Hush Park in 2015 but was sacked in 2019 and is currently looking for the right job to return to football with.

While internal discussions over filling the post were held, one interesting name to note that was mentioned by our sources was that of Louis Carey - the club's record appearance holder - and the former central defender is working in a similar capacity at U16s level with Southampton and helping Rob Edwards to coach the Wales U19s.

Carey is not believed to be interested in returning to Ashton Gate at this time however.

Assistant head coach Dean Holden was unsure on how or even if the club would replace Williams when we asked the former Oldham head coach in the pre-Nottingham Forest press conference.

"I'm not sure, to be honest. That'll be a club decision," explained Holden last Tuesday.

"It's only happened in the last few days. We wish Luke well; obviously it's a great job for him, assistant manager at a good club in MK Dons. It's a chance for him to experience first team football again. In terms of what we're going to do in replacing him, I'm not sure yet."

Bristol City made no comment when contacted regarding this story.
