Steven Gerrard issues Rangers challenge as Ibrox side enter season defining block

The Rangers boss knows that the six weeks until the winter break could be the biggest of his managerial career.

24 Nov 2019, 06:00 by Scott McDermott, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/scott-mcdermott/

Rangers have already overcome a few psychological barriers with Steven Gerrard in charge.

Their first Old Firm league victory in six years. Back in a European group stage after nearly a decade in the wilderness.

And reaching a first League Cup Final since 2011. All achieved by a squad, moulded and gelled together by Gerrard in 17 months at the helm.

But in the space of 29 days in December, across eight games of football, his players will face the biggest test yet of their mental toughness.

And by the end of it, the manager will know for certain if they have the resilience – and quality – to lift silverware.

They have Premiership fixtures against Hearts, Aberdeen, Motherwell, Hibs and Kilmarnock. Not to mention an Old Firm clash at Celtic Park.

Crammed in amongst them, there’s the Betfred Final against the Hoops at Hampden as well as two Europa League ties against Feyenoord and Young Boys to qualify for the last 32.

To say it’s huge for Gerrard and his team is an understatement. It certainly won’t faze him, a player who has been through it all in his career.

But it’s the acid test for his players. If they emerge unscathed in the title battle, and with a trophy or Euro progress in the bag, it could define their season.

Gerrard is refusing to look too far down the line, beyond Hamilton Accies today and a trip to Holland in midweek.

But he’s convinced his players have the character to tackle any challenge.

He said: “There’s no getting away from it. There’s some tough, challenging fixtures in the next block of games.

“But we’ve earned that, by winning a semi-final and having something to play for in Europe. So it’s an exciting period and one we’re looking forward to.

“I watch the players closely every day. The way we finished the last block of games, there was a real hunger there.

“They’re looking forward to getting their teeth into them.

“But I know it’s a big challenge. We don’t have to go to Celtic Park until December 29 – and we’re still only in the middle of November. So we’ll concern ourselves with that when we have to.

“Right now, we have to beat Hamilton away on astroturf, live on TV with an early kick-off. Everyone is waiting for a bit of drama.

“Is it right for people to talk about the next block of fixtures and challenges within it? 100 percent, yes.

“But it would be naive to start talking about an Old Firm game, the tie with Young Boys in a couple of weeks or the League Cup Final. That would take my focus off Hamilton.

“Do I have any doubts about the players’ mentality? I never doubt this group.

“I put them together, I signed them for a reason – because I have belief and confidence in them.

“Does that mean we’re going to smash everyone and it’s going to be perfect? That’s not something I can say right now.

“But we’re a good, strong team. And we’ve proved so far that we’ve been okay when tough challenges come our way.

“Looking at each of those December fixtures in isolation, I have confidence that we’ll be good enough and in a really good position to compete in the challenges we face across the board.”

Gerrard has already used his entire squad to good effect this term.

Tinkering with his team has been risky at times. But overall, it’s paid off given where Rangers find themselves in three competitions.

That was his plan in the summer – to recruit enough bodies to compete on all fronts. And he’s well aware it’s a strategy that will be tested to the full before the winter break in January.

He said: “It’s the exact reason why we went for volume.

“You have to picture scenarios where you have a run in the cup and go to the wire in the Europa League.

“We knew we could be in a situation where we have 10 or 11 games in a six-week period – and that’s what we’ve got.

“So two things are important – volume and quality. That’s what we tried to do when we put the squad together.

“I didn’t want to say last summer that we were the finished article. People would jump all over you if you had a setback or bump in the road. I like to do the talking when the games start and so far we’re doing okay.

“I need to keep driving everything and make sure the players are focused.

“We set the scene for them, show them the schedule and what’s ahead.

“But it’s important the players put blinkers on and worry about the next three points or next competition – that’s all that matters.”

With Celtic playing yesterday, there’s added pressure on Rangers to get a result at Hamilton this afternoon.

It’s a scenario that will be repeated – for both sides – as the title race begins to gather pace. And that’s why Gerrard wants his players to fly out the traps in EVERY game they play.

He said: “It’s impossible to forget the Celtic score, we’ll always be aware of their result.

“But it’s vital you focus on your own job and what you can control.

“At the start of the last group of fixtures, we had a 15-minute period at Hearts when we were slow out of the blocks. And it cost us.

“That’s the reason we just have to concentrate on ourselves.”
