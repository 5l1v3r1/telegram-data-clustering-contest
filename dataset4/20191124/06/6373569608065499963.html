Joe Edwards apologises to wife after forgetting his Plymouth Argyle goal celebration for twin sons

The 29-year-old was on target in the Pilgrims' 2-1 defeat of Bradford City at Home Park

24 Nov 2019, 06:00 by Chris Errington

Joe Edwards owned up to forgetting about a promise he made to his wife after he scored one of the goals for Plymouth Argyle in their 2-1 win against Bradford City.

Edwards was on target for the Pilgrims as they made it four wins out of their last five League Two games.

His wife, Hannah, gave birth to twin baby boys – Archie and Louie – on October 28.

Edwards had promised he would celebrate scoring a goal for Argyle with a rocking motion with his arms, as seen done by many footballers over the years.

However, he admitted he had been too excited after putting the Pilgrims 2-0 up in the first half.

Edwards said: “It was a pleasing goal and something that I can dedicate to my two boys who have recently been born, which is nice to do.

“I was supposed to do a celebration, which I forgot. I was too excited at the time, and Danny (Mayor) grabbed me.

“I can only apologise to my Missus for that one. She asked me to do that one thing and I didn’t do it.”

Edwards was asked whether he thought he would be forgiven and replied: “I’m not sure.”

He added: “I’m hoping so. She has already text saying ‘Well done’ so she will be happy I have scored.”

Argyle had taken the lead as early as the fifth minute with a goal from striker Byron Moore.

Edwards then doubled their advantage in the 25th minute with a fine low left finish from the edge of the Bradford box.

The chance came after Edwards closed down City goalkeeper Richard O’Donnell and forced him into a poor, rushed clearance.

The ball only went as far as Argyle striker Joel Grant and his pass to Edwards set him up for his shot into the net.

Edwards said: “We had worked on pressing and making sure we put them (Bradford) on the back foot, and we did that.

“For some reason I ended up pressing the goalkeeper and it paid off. I was in the box and managed to score.

“I think just overall at the moment in training if you are the nearest man to the ball you have got to press whether it’s your man or not.

“You have got to make sure you are on the front foot and I ended up being there. It forced them into a mistake and it led to a goal for us, which was great.”

Argyle’s victory over Bradford, who arrived at Home Park in third position in the table, came seven days after their 1-0 win at leaders Forest Green Rovers.

Edwards said: “We have had two massive games now against teams at the top of the league and we have come away with six points, which is fantastic.

“It’s something we have really worked hard on the last couple of weeks, knowing these two games were coming up.

“It was massive to put in the performances and get the victories.”

He went on: “We know on our day we can beat these teams but at the start of the year we lacked that consistency a little bit, which was frustrating.

“I feel now we are working well as a team and everyone is on the same page now. It’s showing in the performances and definitely in the results.”

Prior to beating Forest Green and Bradford, Argyle also won 1-0 away to League One outfit Bolton Wanderers in the FA Cup first round.

Yet, before that tie, they had suffered a humiliating 4-0 Devon Derby defeat away to Exeter City.

Edwards admitted he and his team-mates knew that they owed the Green Army some good results after that debacle.

He said: “It was a tough one because we were in good form before the game. When you perform like we did, it was frustrating for everyone and especially the fans.

“We knew then we had to do something that would get them back on side and show that we really do care and it was just a one-off.

“We needed to show that we mean business and I think we have done the last three games.

“That (the Exeter defeat) will hopefully be put to the side now and we can concentrate on looking forward.”

After having much the better of the first half, Argyle came under pressure at the start of the second period.

Bradford changed tactics to match up to the Pilgrims after a double substitution and their deficit was reduced to 2-1 in the 51st minute by an own goal from Niall Canavan.

He redirected a low cross from former Argyle defender Anthony O’Connor into his own net.
