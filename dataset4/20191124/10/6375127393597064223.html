Problematic, impractical

24 Nov 2019, 10:07

IN the Philippines, an impoverished Southeast Asian nation of English-speaking and election-crazy people, the president and vice president (VP) may belong to different political parties.

Described as problematic, counter-productive and highly-impractical, the set-up prompted a veteran solon to propose that our next Chief Executive and VP should be elected in tandem.

“We should adopt the American system wherein the president and the vice president are elected together as one ticket belonging to the same political party,” said Rep. Lito Atienza.

Likewise, the American vice president presides over the 100-member U.S. Senate, according to Atienza.

Atienza, a former mayor of Manila and now a party-list member of the House of Representatives, made the statement shortly after President Duterte said he still distrusts VP Leni Robredo.

If and when given the opportunity, Atienza said he would push for the amendment of the Charter so that the president and the VP would be elected jointly, rather than separately.

The same rule should also apply to provincial governors and vice governors as well as city/municipal mayors and vice mayors, said Atienza, a former member of the Bayanihan Dance Troupe.

Let’s then revisit and amend the 1987 Constitution, which was drafted by a government-appointed commission during the incumbency of the late President Corazon “Cory” C. Aquino.

We want to believe that Atienza’s move hinges on the greater consideration that his proposed amendment to the fundamental law of the land should benefit the country and the Filipino people.

Baguhin na natin ang problemadong set-up para huwag ng maulit ang nangyayaring iringan sa pagitan nina Pangulong Rodrigo R. Duterte ng Davao City at VP Leni Robredo ng Naga City.
