'Any team' - Steve Bruce addresses Jack Grealish's Aston Villa future before Newcastle United game

Aston Villa latest | Jack Grealish signed a five-year deal with Villa in September 2018, while Steve Bruce was still manager at Villa Park - but their sides will face off on Monday night

24 Nov 2019, 10:05 by Josh Murray

Steve Bruce has labelled Jack Grealish as Aston Villa's "crown jewel" ahead of Monday night's clash with Newcastle United.

Grealish was one of Villa's key performers as they reached the Championship play-off final under Bruce in 2017-18.

They were beaten by Fulham in that showpiece, before Grealish helped lead Dean Smith's men to promotion 12 months on.

The current Villa captain signed a new five-year deal at the club in September 2018, when Bruce was still in charge, and he has since been linked with a host of Premier League rivals, including Tottenham Hotspur and Manchester United.

The midfielder has scored twice and registered three assists so far this League season.

When asked if Grealish may need to leave Villa Park to make the most of his eye-catching quality, Bruce told the Telegraph: "Do you want me killed? I have to say fair play to the owners, even though they sacked me, the first thing they did was to give Jack Grealish a new five-year contract and say we want to build the team around him.

"How refreshing to every Villa supporter that was.

"He is the crown jewel and he never wanted to leave.

"It would be difficult for anyone to buy him with the size of contract he has got.

"He could play any team - Manchester United, Tottenham, Chelsea, Arsenal - I rate him that highly."

Grealish could make his return from injury against Bruce's Newcastle at Villa Park tomorrow (Monday).
