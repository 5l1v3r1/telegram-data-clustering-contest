Fantastic Four Director Josh Trank Reviews His Film: 'I'm Not Zack Snyder'

24 Nov 2019, 10:11 by Tom Chapman

Josh Trank recently reviewed 2015's Fantastic Four, tearing apart his own controversial comic book outing. While Tim Story had mixed success with 2005's Fantastic Four and 2007's Fantastic Four: Rise of the Silver Surfer, Trank's film became almost universally hated among comic book fans and critics alike.

Starring Miles Teller, Michael B. Jordan, Kate Mara, Jamie Bell and Toby Kebbell, Fantastic Four had a famously troubled production. In the past, Trank has called out 20th Century Fox for not letting him make 'his' movie, and it seems that time hasn't changed his opinion when it comes to his thoughts on Fantastic Four.

Giving it two stars on Letterboxd, Trank wrote, "The movie is ALRIGHT. I was expecting it to be much worse than it was. I literally haven’t seen it since like two weeks before it came out, and I was in a heavily fucking traumatized state of mind. Why? Eh, save that for another time."

He goes on to praise the movie's cast but said he isn't sure whether he made the movie they deserved to headline. He then referenced the mythical Trank Cut, nodding to Justice League's equally convoluted production. "Is there a #releasethetrankcut? Doesn’t matter. I’m not Zack Snyder," he added.

20th Century Fox envisioned a Fantastic Four sequel, but with the movie bombing at the box office, the cast was released from their contracts. Despite Tim Miller's plans for the Fantastic Four to appear in Deadpool 2, the characters have been left in limbo since 2015. However, with Disney's recent acquisition of 20th Century Fox and Ant-Man's Peyton Reed saying he'd love to make a Fantastic Four movie, Trank is more hopeful for the future. He concluded, "And I just hope Peyton Reed makes the next Fantastic Four and crushes it. And that I get a cameo."

Only recently, Trank gave fans a glimpse of what his original script contained and confirmed there was supposed to be much more character development. Elsewhere, deleted scenes tease that the fan-favorite Fantasticar was part of the original vision.
