Pitney Bowes (NYSE:PBI) Rating Lowered to Sell at Zacks Investment Research

24 Nov 2019, 10:32 by Jessica Moore

Zacks Investment Research cut shares of Pitney Bowes (NYSE:PBI) from a hold rating to a sell rating in a research report sent to investors on Wednesday, Zacks.com reports. They currently have $5.25 price target on the technology company’s stock.

According to Zacks, “Pitney Bowes’s third-quarter earnings beat the Zacks Consensus Estimate. However, the bottom line declined year over year. Nonetheless, revenues improved year over year on growth in Global Ecommerce and Presort Services. Strong performance of domestic parcel platform, increased volumes across Marketing Mail, First Class Mail, and Bound and Packet Mail, drove growth. Higher equipment sales and improvement in business services are positives.  Further, Pitney Bowes’ bottom-line guidance for 2019 holds promise. However, impact from market exits, lower revenues from supplies, support services and financing weighed on the top line. Furthermore, adverse changes in postal regulations across key markets and intensifying competition are likely to negatively impact profitability. Changing business mix is also likely to limit margin expansion.”

About Pitney Bowes

Pitney Bowes Inc offers customer information management, location intelligence, and customer engagement products and solutions in the United States and internationally. The company operates in three segments: Commerce Services; Small & Medium Business Solutions; and Software Solutions. The Commerce Services segment provides cross-border e-commerce solutions, domestic retail and e-commerce shipping solutions, fulfillment, and delivery and return services; and mail sortation services that allow clients to qualify large volumes of first class mail, marketing mail, and bound and packet mail for postal work sharing discounts.
