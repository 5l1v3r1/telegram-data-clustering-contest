Oklahoma business people for Nov. 24, 2019

24 Nov 2019, 10:00

Business People recognizes Oklahoma firms' recent new hires and promotions. To include your announcement, send information and a photo to Business Editor Adam Wilmoth at awilmoth@oklahoman.com.

Joe T. Shockley, CPA, has joined the Stride Bank as a director. Shockley has worked in the banking industry in Oklahoma for more than 40 years and recently retired as the executive vice president and chief financial officer for Southwest Bancorp Inc. and its subsidiary bank, Bank SNB. Shockley is also founder and CEO of Shockley Consulting LLC, a bank consulting company. After graduating from the University of Oklahoma with a bachelor’s degree in business administration in 1973, Shockley worked in public accounting with Arthur Andersen & Co. and a regional firm in Oklahoma City as the manager of the firm’s banking practice. Shockley later became controller for First Oklahoma Bancorporation (FOBC) and its subsidiary bank, The First National Bank of Oklahoma City, eventually becoming chief financial officer. He served as an executive at First Interstate Bank of Oklahoma, CAMCO, the First Interstate Bank of Oklahoma, Boatmen's Bank and BanFirst Corp. before joining Southwest Bancorp.

Tatiana Bonnefoi has been named chairwoman of Purse Power Inc., an Edmond--based, woman-owned and women-led start-up technology company. Bonnefoi is the global supply manager for Apple, where she focuses on taking new products into mass production. Bonnefoi holds master’s degrees in mechanical engineering and business from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology. Before joining Apple, she was a senior associate with PwC Consulting and a supply chain management intern with General Electric.

Lauren Hammonds Johnson has joined the Oklahoma Workers’ Compensation Commission as general counsel. She spent the past six years working for the Oklahoma Office of the Attorney General as an assistant attorney general. She received her bachelor’s degree in accounting from Oklahoma State University and earned her Juris Doctorate from the University of Oklahoma College of Law.

Devika Madhavan, M.D., has joined the INTEGRIS Endocrinology North clinic. An endocrinologist, Madhavan earned a Bachelor of Medicine and Bachelor of Surgery from the Amrita Institute of Medical Sciences and Research Centre in India. She completed the internal medicine residency program at Central Michigan University College of Medicine. She received fellowship training in endocrinology, diabetes and metabolism from the University of Oklahoma Health Sciences Center. Her areas of interest include: Diabetes, thyroid disorders, polycystic ovarian syndrome, pregnancy and diabetes, metabolism disorders and hyperlipidemia.

John Salame has joined communications consulting firm Saxum as art director in its Oklahoma City office. Salame previously worked as an art director at Studio Flight and Staplegun and as a graphic design intern at Goadabode. He holds a bachelor’s degree in graphic design and music performance from the University of Central Oklahoma.

Gateway First Bank announced the appointment of Deirdre Cherry as its chief credit officer. Cherry has been in the banking industry for more than 30 years, primarily with JPMorgan and its predecessor institutions and Bank of America. Her experience has focused on providing credit solutions to clients ranging from large corporations through the ultra-high net worth private client segment. She earned a master’s degree in business administration from Pace University and a bachelor’s degree in economics from Santa Clara University.

Candor has named five new employees to its digital media, account services and administrative teams. Chris Hodge joins the company as a digital strategist. Hodge specializes in strategic development and implementation to help brands achieve goals, optimize their digital footprints and increase conversions. Through his past agency experience, he became skilled at helping clients increase their social followings, web traffic and e-commerce sales. Garrett Davis joins Candor as a social media specialist. His areas of expertise include social media strategy, content creation, brand development and digital targeting. With a background in broadcast journalism and filmmaking, Davis uses his creativity to produce clever, eye-catching content for social media. Other additions at Candor include account executive Alison Hooks and account coordinator Sarah Ederer. Hooks is a certified Project Management Professional and has agency experience tackling marketing and branding problems. Ederer, who has a background in event planning and management, juggles client, vendor and departmental needs to facilitate events of all sizes. Lindsey Rickards also joins the team as an administrative assistant. She is an experienced office manager and executive assistant.
