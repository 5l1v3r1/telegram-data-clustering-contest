Scotland's top plastic surgeons go to court over cocaine slur

Dr Ravichandran is alleged to have made a comment at an industry event indicating that Dr Darren McKeown had taken drugs.

24 Nov 2019, 04:30 by Gordon Blackstock

Two of Scotland’s leading cosmetic clinic owners are involved in an extraordinary spat over an alleged drug slur.

Plastic surgeon Dr Darren ­McKeown has launched a legal action against fellow surgeon Dr Simon Ravichandran.

The case centres on an alleged remark made at a cosmetic ­industry event in London last year.

Dr Ravichandran – a specialist in ear, nose and throat surgery who quit the NHS for private work in Glasgow – is claimed to have made a comment that Dr McKeown had taken cocaine.

Dr McKeown, who has appeared in TV documentaries about his work, was angered and is now suing Dr Ravichandran at the Court of Session in Edinburgh.

Paperwork lodged at the ­country’s top civil court will claim his thriving cosmetic business, also in Glasgow, suffered as a result of the accusation.

The case, due to begin on January 29, will hear claims that at least three people heard the allegation.

But Dr Ravichandran, the ­president and founder of the ­Association of Scottish Aesthetic Practitioners, denies saying it.

His legal team will argue that as well as being false, Dr McKeown has been unable to prove financial loss to his ­business and will claim the case shouldn’t be heard in ­Scotland because the incident is said to have happened in London.

Dr McKeown, who has instructed MacRoberts Solicitors to handle the action, strenuously denies any involvement with drugs.

The 38-year-old often gives ­interviews about cosmetic surgery and discusses work celebrities have had done.

It has been claimed he previously paid his personal assistant in expensive designer Louboutin shoes instead of money.

In 2016, former Glasgow ­University student Dr McKeown, from Hamilton, personally injected over 3200 dermal fillers in a single year, making him the biggest user of the procedure in the country.

A spokesman for Dr McKeown, who runs Aesthetic Medicine ­Institute, refused to comment.

Dr Ravichandran, 44, is also a prominent practitioner in ­Scotland’s cosmetic industry.

He set up private clinic Clinetix in 2006 and continued to work for NHS Lanarkshire until 2016.

When we contacted his clinic, a colleague said he was in Thailand and could not be reached.

His solicitors BKF & Co said: “The action is being defended.”
