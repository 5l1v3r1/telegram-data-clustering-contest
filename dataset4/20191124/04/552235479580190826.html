Raging neighbours can't move man living in van outside their home for two years

Andy Calvert lives outside Cameron Weir and his mum Sheila's house in Lanarkshire and local council say they can't budge him.

24 Nov 2019, 04:30 by Jennifer Hyland

A man who is living in a parked van outside a family home has refused to move despite their complaints.

Andy Calvert has been in the same spot outside a house occupied by Cameron Weir and his mum Sheila for two-and-a-half years.

Cameron and Sheila claim a wood burner used by Andy causes fumes to pour into their house and back garden.

But the local authority appears powerless to intervene and, while Andy says he sympathises, he is refusing to move.

Cameron, 45, of Stonehouse, Lanarkshire, said: “We live in a quiet residential area and this really is making our life hell.

“In summer, we can’t even enjoy sitting in our back garden because of the fumes from the vehicles and the stove.

“It’s really noisy because when he works on his two cars and motorbike, he revs the engines.

“He also has a number of people visiting and they are so loud that we can’t hear our TV in our living room. It’s a nightmare.”

Cameron has complained to police and local MSP Christina McKelvie, who contacted South Lanarkshire Council on his behalf.

But it is understood because the van sits on an unadopted road, the council has no authority to move the vehicle.

A letter sent by the local authority to McKelvie last month stated that its On Street Living team monitored the area for three weeks and “no nuisance behaviour” was detected.

When approached by the Sunday Mail, Andy blamed the council for his housing issues.

He said: “The council evicted me from my flat because I fell behind on rent. I had nowhere to go but my van.

“It was minus six that night and I had to kick the door open as it had frozen shut. Now they want me to declare myself homeless but I have somewhere to live.

“They made me homeless so, until they admit that, I’m not moving.”

The 58-year-old, who works as a long-distance lorry driver, has kitted out his van with a double bed, a TV, a fridge, and wood panelling for insulation. He uses a gas ring to cook, has a chemical toilet and showers at work.

Andy admits the wood burner he bought for £50 from eBay was “smokey at first” but claims it is better now.

The van also uses batteries for power and has two gas canisters and solar panels.

Despite sympathising with Cameron and Sheila, Andy is refusing to move.

He said: “I’ve nothing against them. They have a nice fancy house and don’t want a hobo at their back door. But I’m not breaking any laws. It’s my choice and a matter of principle.”

South Lanarkshire Council’s executive director of housing Daniel Lowe said: “The council is aware of the concerns raised by Mr Weir and his mother and has kept them fully updated on our position in relation to this matter.

“Unfortunately, at this juncture, the council does not have sufficient evidence to raise a court action for unlawful encampment but is continuing to monitor this situation closely on an ongoing basis and is keeping this matter under review.”
