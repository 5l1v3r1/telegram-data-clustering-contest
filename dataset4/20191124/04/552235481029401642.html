Foxes blamed for Scottish Junior Cup game call off but rival club cite stag-do plan

The tie between Bridge of Don Thistle and Fauldhouse United was cancelled after foxes allegedly dug holes all over the pitch.

24 Nov 2019, 04:30 by Record Reporter

Football can be a game full of excuses – but the one that caused a Scottish Junior Cup clash to be called off is surely a first.

The tie between Bridge of Don Thistle and Fauldhouse United was cancelled after foxes allegedly dug holes all over the pitch.

A local referee decided on Friday night that the park at Aberdeen Sports Village was too dangerous to play on.

But Fauldhouse officials last night claimed there was not a blade of truth in the excuse.

The West Lothian club insisted the real reason behind the call-off was that Bridge of Don players wanted to go on a stag do.

United chairman Steven Sharpe, who was in Aberdeen on business, checked the pitch yesterday morning and said it was in “pristine” condition.

Last night, Fauldhouse manager Jon Connolly described the call-off as “an absolute farce”.

He said: “Bridge of Don didn’t even bother to contact us, so we had to find out from the Scottish Junior Football Association.

“It looks as if the referee, whoever it is, has done them a favour because there are rumours of a stag do going around, which means that they’ll have players missing, so they’re trying to play the game when it suits them.

“My chairman went to check the pitch this morning and it’s absolutely fine.”

Bridge of Don Thistle, co-managed by Lewis Muirhead and Thomas Wilson, were unavailable for comment last night.
