Perrie Edwards squashes Little Mix feud rumours

24 Nov 2019, 23:12

Little Mix star Perrie Edwards has shut down talk of rifts between her and bandmates Jade Thirlwall, Leigh-Anne Pinnock and Jesy Nelson.

Rumours of tension have plagued the British group for years but Perrie has shut them down once and for all.

Speaking to The Mirror at Radio 1 Teen Awards in London today, Perrie said: ‘We’ve always had rumours [of arguments] the last few years.

‘But that comes with being in a girl band. People assume girls don’t get along with girls.’

Little Mix won the best group prize at the awards.

Jade, who joined Perrie at the event, chimed in: ‘We literally never fight. We’re really boring.’

Speaking about Little Mix’s sixth LP, Jade added: ‘We go back into the studio next week to start writing our next album so we can’t wait to do that.’

The girls’ last effort, LM5, reached number three in the UK album charts last year.

Perrie also chatted about the girls’ upcoming music talent search show Little Mix: The Search, which will air on BBC1 in the coveted Saturday night slot at some point next year.

She said: ‘Auditions are happening now. There have been thousands of people auditioning. It’s insane.’

According to the BBC, the winning group ‘could be all-female, all-male or mixed [and] get a career launch-pad like no other – supporting Little Mix on their UK Summer Tour 2020’.

The group will certainly have great mentors in the form of Little Mix, who famously got together on the 2011 series of X Factor.

Their number one singles are Black Magic, Wings, Shout Out to My Ex and their debut single Cannonball.

We can’t wait for 2020, and a new decade of Little Mix.
