Video: Gronk’s Rant About The Cowboys-Patriots Game Is Going Viral

24 Nov 2019, 23:33 by Tzvi Machlin

It’s pretty clear that the weather has been a factor in today’s Cowboys-Patriots game. The three missed field goals from both sides through the first few quarters is proof enough of that.

Yet the Patriots are holding firm with a 10-6 lead midway through the third quarter. Tom Brady has taken advantage of some opportunities, while Dak Prescott has struggled to get into a rhythm, and with little help from his offense.

FOX analyst and former Patriots tight end Rob Gronkowski thinks he knows why the Patriots look so much better.

In a halftime rant that quickly went viral, Gronk broke down the difference between Bill Belichick and Jerry Jones in getting their teams ready for inclement weather games.

Gronk said that while Belichick makes sure that his players prepare for every kind of weather, Jones lets the team play indoors when they’re uncomfortable.

“If there’s a little rain in Dallas, a little snow in Dallas, Jerry Jones comes out and says “Do you guys wanna practice in the bubble today?”
