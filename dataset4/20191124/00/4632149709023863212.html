Things to do today in Bengaluru

24 Nov 2019, 00:30 by Bangalore Mirror Bureau

Catch this concert

The wait is over. FiveSixZero, Bengaluru’s very own dance music festival, will open tonight with a great line-up of DJs, a chance to have brunch with artists, and a flea market to indulge in. Homegrown DJs will open the show with back-to-back sets, you know, just to warm you for the big night ahead. In the evening, The Main Stage will play commercial music, to be headlined by Krewella and Nucleya; De:Vision – The Underground Stage, will cover the underground side of techno music and will be headlined by Sal O Cin, and She Said So will feature four female DJ. The fest is held in association with Bangalore Mirror and is an initiative of Times Red Cell.

WHERE: The Lalit Ashok, Kumarakrupa Road
WHEN: 12pm onwards, November 24
TICKETS: bookmyshow.com DETAILS: fivesixzero.in

Japan calling

T he annual Japan Food Fest is back, and this time, The Consulate-General of Japan will serve some delicious Japanese sparkling sake and Banga Roll (vegetarian sushi roll) free of cost. That’s just for starters. They have lined up stage shows, including a performance by the Japanese choir group ‘Royal Echo.

WHERE: Shangri-La Hotel, Vasanth Nagar
WHEN: 1pm-6pm, November 24
CALL:45126100

What is identity?

As a concept, Identity is found complex to apprehend and worse, it is misunderstood. But over a cup of coffee, The Archive, a literature society group, will help you decipher, analyse and break it down in the way that they know best – through facts, literature and a tinge of comedy. At The Archive, the aim is to provide the necessary tools for the millennial youth to make informed decisions on their own.

WHERE: Social, Church Street WHEN: 9am, November 24 ENTRY: Free CALL:9152017971

An evening of classics

As part of its ongoing fund-raising efforts towards acquiring a concert-standard grand piano, The BSM Youth Orchestra will present an evening of gems of classical music by the great masters such as Handel, Vivaldi, Corelli, Holst and lesser-known composers such as Zimmer, Spata and Brubaker. It will be conducted by Grace Biswas, a faculty member of the strings department at the school.

WHERE: Aruna Sunderlal Auditorium, The Bangalore School of Music
WHEN: 5.30pm, November 24
ENTRY: `300, bookmyshow.com

It’s a DIY world

The Handmade Collective is celebrating its 10th year with
a bazaar that’s brought together over 100 Indian craft forms and more than 120 artisans, creators, makers, NGOs and designers on
one platform. So shop for exquisite craft pieces and artworks or pick up eco-friendly products to show you support to the green movement. Plus, you can participate in workshops and refuel your tired selves at food stalls.

WHERE: Jayamahal Palace, #1, Jayamahal Road
WHEN: 11am-8pm, November 24
