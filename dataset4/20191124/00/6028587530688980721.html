Nature Photographer Of The Year: Inspirational 2019 Winning Images

24 Nov 2019 by Cecilia Rodriguez

The 2019 winners of the Nature Photographer Of The Year photo competition are an inspirational reminder of the extraordinary wonder of the natural world. A black-and-white photo of two rabbits jumping high into the air taken in Kiskunsag, near the Hungarian village of Bócsa by the Hungarian nature photographer Csaba Daróczi, was chosen as the overall winner of the contest as well as the winner of the Black-and-White category. This photo was chosen, according to the judges, because of the “atmosphere, timing, beautiful light, perfect sharpness and composition.”

The Italian photographer Giacomo Redaelli, winner of the Youth category (11-17 years old) is the creator of the photo above, showing a nest of long-tailed tits getting fed by their mother.

“This photo proves that you don’t have to get extreme rare or extraordinary species in front of your camera to create a great nature photo,” says jury chairman Keith Wilson. “If you have a vision and a creative mind you can create a great shot close to home. The other remarkable thing is that it’s a black-and-white photo and because of that there’s a lot of energy emerging from it.”

The winners in the other nine categories, the special young NPOTY award and of the Fred Hazelhoff portfolio award, which was given to the beautiful photos of Romanian photographer Gheorghe Popa, were chosen from 14,000 entries from more than 73 countries, a new record for this competition.

The Nature Photography contest was created to celebrate the beauty of nature photography by the Nature Talks foundation, a non-profit organization that works with different world conservation projects.

The contest is open to professional and amateur photographers from all countries and ages starting from 10 years old and offers €25,000 in prizes, including €3,000 for the overall winner and assorted photo equipment.

The winning images are shown at the Nature Talks Photo Festival and will travel through the Netherlands, Belgium and France.

“We believe that we can show the beauty of nature worldwide by sharing the most amazing images you all make,” write the organizers. “If we learn to love nature, we’ll also protect her.”

The photo contest for the 2020 edition will open for entries on December 1, 2019, and end on May 1, 2020.
