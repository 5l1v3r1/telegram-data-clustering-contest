Thanksgiving storm on track with high impact results

24 Nov 2019

SOUTHLAND - (INT) - Forecasters are tweaking details of the anticipated Thanksgiving storm.

SoCal weather is tranquil for now, but sunshine gives way to a high impact, major winter storm Wednesday through Friday. Widespread rain is expected, and may be heavy at times. Isolated thunderstorms are possible Wednesday afternoon through Friday.

The mountains will bear the highest precipitation totals. Localized flash flooding will be possible, and there may be a threat of debris flows in/near recent burn scars.

Isolated thunderstorms are possible beginning Wednesday afternoon and lasting through Friday
afternoon.

The rain and snow is the result of a cold air mass settling over the area Wednesday evening into Friday resulting in lower snow levels and heavy snow in many mountain communities and mountain passes.

The storm will strike during the busy Thanksgiving holiday travel period, with widespread hazardous conditions on the roadways.
