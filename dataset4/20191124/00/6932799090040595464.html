Deal | Save as much as US$600 on Canon cameras and lenses

24 Nov 2019 by Deirdre O Donnell

Canon has unveiled its Black Friday 2019 week deals, which include a number of high-end cameras, on its own website. This store also offers discounts on some lenses, as does Amazon at present. These listings also include Tamron or Sigma attachments.

Black Friday 2019 is nearly upon us; however, many brands and stores have taken the initiative and brought their corresponding sales events forward this year. Canon US is one of them: the premium camera brand has already posted a page of deals for the week in question. They include a number of high-end DSLRs, the most discounted of which is the EOS R, which is being sold with an RF 24–105mm F4 L IS USM lens to match it.

This bundle normally goes for $3399, but is down to $2499 for Black Friday week. Alternatively, the 30.4MP EOS 5D Mark IV (camera body only) is going for $600 less than its normal price of $3099. Those looking for something else could look to the EOS 90D, which is currently $1449 rather than its normal 1599. However, pre-existing owners of this camera may be interested in $400 off an EF 100-400mm F4.5-5.6L IS II USM lens to add telephone capability to their unit.

Those interested in a more complete package of a gift for themselves or someone else could also consider the  EOS Rebel T6 Double Zoom Lens Kit. This bundle comes with the camera in question, as well as 2 extended lenses: the EF-S 18-55mm f/3.5-5.6 IS II and the EF 75-300mm f/4-5.6 III. It also includes a shoulder bag, battery pack, charger, cable and a strap for the EOS Rebel variant in question.

Alternatively, Amazon has listed some deals on third-party lenses for cameras such as these. They include $200 off a Sigma 35mm F1.4 Art DG HSM for this brand. In addition, the Tamron SP 70-200mm F/2.8 Di VC G2 for Nikon FX-mounted and Canon EF-mounted units are $100 off at present. These prices may not last long, however. In addition, Canon US is offering free shipping and returns at its store until December 31, 2019.
