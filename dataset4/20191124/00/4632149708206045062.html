Cheteshwar Pujara: Not easy to pick pink ball with kind of bowlers we have

24 Nov 2019, 00:30 by Bangalore Mirror Bureau

Cheteshwar Pujara said Bangladesh's pink-ball inexperience has led to their batsmen taking blows.

On batting under lights

The first session is easier to bat. When there is light, the ball starts to swing a little bit more. It's a new challenge for batsmen as we only batted in sunlight with red ball in Tests.

Difference between SG and Kookaburra pink ball

The SG ball is helping spinners a little more than the Kookaburra ball. When I played with the Kookaburra in the Duleep Trophy, I don't think there was so much assistance for spinners apart from wrist spinners. But with the SG ball, we saw Taijul Islam getting some purchase and R Ashwin too getting a little bit more spin. But still, it is not as much as you get with the red ball.

On India's declaration timing

That was the right time to bowl as the ball was swinging. We felt if we start bowling at the time we can pick early wickets. That is what happened.

On so many batsmen getting hit on the head

As a batsman, it is not easy to pick the ball (pink) especially the short ball with the kind of fast bowlers we have.

Paint the town pink

Keeping up with the hype, to mark the historic occasion of the first-ever day-night Test in India, all the iconic spots of Kolkata, where the match will be held will turn pink. The famous tourist points in the city are lit up in pink to attract more people to the country's first pink-ball Test. Landmarks like the Big Ben on Laketown, Howrah Bridge, Tata Centre, Eden Gardens, the Elliot Park, and Shahid Minar are all decked in pink to mark the occasion. If that's not enough, the names on the manual scoreboard will also be written in pink and the stands will be lit in pink once the sun goes down. Photo: PTI

'Feels a bit heavy'

Indian cricket will finally embrace the pink revolution as Kohli's formidable team lock horns with a deflated Bangladesh in their maiden Day/Night Test. At the practice sessions, one thing that surprised Virat Kohli was that the pink ball felt like a heavy hockey ball to him. "It's purely because of the extra glaze on the ball, it is definitely much harder. For some reason, it felt heavy and even the throws took a lot more effort than the red colour to reach the wicketkeeper," he said. Photo by Debajyoti Chakraborty/ BCCL
