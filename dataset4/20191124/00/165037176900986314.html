Emery is still in the spotlight

24 Nov 2019 by BeSoccer

Arsenal drew against Southampton, which takes them to five games without a win in the Premier League and not in the top four. Unai Emery's work is more questioned than ever and Tony Adams, an Arsenal legend, spoke about the Gunners coach to 'BT Sport'.

Unai Emery is having a hard time as Arsenal coach. The 'Gunners' are not in the tio 4 after their fifth game without winning, against Southampton. A result that brought a wave of critics to the Spanish coach.

Arsenal legend, Tony Adams, the star of a well-known stint at Granada, was especially critical of Unai Emery with 'BT Sport'. The Brit spoke about Arsenal's bad performance and questioned Emery's suitability for the role.

"It was a really bad game. I don't want any coach to lose their job, but the club needs to improve," Adams assured, who also stated that Arsenal desrved the boos that they received from the fans at the end of the game.

"I am able to get a team together and be more organised than this. They are all asleep," he added, whilst also showing his "tremendous disappointment" for Emery's team.
