Woeful Phil Jones hauled off at half-time as Graeme Souness slams Man Utd defender

Jones was easily outmuscled by Lys Mousset before John Fleck ultimately handed Sheffield United a deserved lead at Bramall Lane on Sunday afternoon

24 Nov 2019, 17:49 by Matt Maltby, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/matt-maltby/

Manchester United defender Phil Jones endured a miserable 45 minutes at Sheffield United, making a crucial mistake before being hooked off and slammed by pundit Graeme Souness.

Scott McTominay's injury saw Jones come in for his first Premier League start of the season for United but it was an appearance to forget for the former Blackburn ace.

Jones was easily outmuscled by Lys Mousset before John Fleck ultimately handed the hosts a deserved lead at Bramall Lane on Sunday afternoon.

And to compound Jones' misery, he was hauled off at half-time and replaced by Jesse Lingard as Ole Gunnar Solskjaer opted for a chance of tactics.

At half-time, Sky Sports pundit Souness laid into the defender and was baffled as to why he didn't clear the danger.

He said: “Jones has done everything wrong for the goal. Protect the ball. Try and get your team a goal kick or at worse put it out for a throw in.

"He just gets it all wrong. Jones isn’t a cute player – he hasn’t got a great touch, so he should just put it into row Z at worst.”

Gary Neville echoed Souness' views - although he reckons that any of the 11 players who started could have been hooked off at the break.

The United legend said: “I don’t think any of the Manchester United players could have complained about being taken off at half-time – they were awful. He had to change something.”

Solskjaer's decision to haul off Jones seemingly didn't pay off, as the Blades doubled their lead in the second half through a fine Mousset strike.

A lovely through-ball from Fleck found Mousset in the middle of Manchester United's half and he marched forward before sending a low drive into the bottom corner of De Gea's net from 25 yards.

Manchester United turned the game on its head as three quickfire goals from their academy graduates put them 3-2 up.

Brandon Williams scored his first goal for the club in the 72nd minute before substitute Mason Greenwood connected with a superb Marcus Rashford cross five minutes later to level things up.

And Rashford put the visitors in front with a goal of his own two minutes later.

But Sheffield United mounted a comeback of their own and Oli McBurnie volleyed home in stoppage time to secure a deserved point for the hosts.
