Whaley Bridge police chief reveals 'sexist and homophobic' abuse over hairstyle led her to quit social media

24 Nov 2019, 17:22 by Telegraph Reporters

Whaley Bridge police chief Rachel Swann has revealled that “sexist and homophobic” abuse over her hairstyle led her to quit social media. 

Deputy chief constable Rachel Swann appeared in front of the national media in August when a dam at Toddbrook Reservoir partially collapsed, putting the town of Whaley Bridge at risk of flooding. Roughly 1,500 residents were evacuated from the area. 

But the officer said attention was drawn away from the incident as people instead focused on criticising her appearance.

One comment said: "Is that what a senior police officer looks like these days??"

She said she was also called a “disgrace” and berated for not wearing a hat. 

Ms Swann said: "Yes, I am a woman. Yes, I might have a slightly different hairstyle. Yes, I am quite small. 

"The bit that astounded me was I could not believe that my mere existence could cause such a depth of feeling."

"I can take a bit of banter but then it became sexist and homophobic, and really, really insulting.

"The bit that really hurt was when people said I had no standards and I was letting policing down."

She added that she had been advised not to wear a hat so the public could see her eyes and “gain trust."

Councillor Barry Lewis, Lincolnshire PCC, jumped to her defence and tweeted that Ms Swann had “saved the valley” during the emergency at Whaley Bridge and “that’s what’s important.'' 

The Lincolnshire police and crime commissioner Marc Jones said Ms Swann was “outstanding” and that the criticism was from “largely retired, male ex-cops”.

He said: "A group of largely retired, male ex cops have an issue with her 'modern' hairstyle.

 “She's a respected, able officer, exactly the sort of person we should encourage to rise to the top of policing.” 

Derbyshire Chief Constable Peter Goodman branded the remarks "unnecessary and hurtful" and said that he was “proud” to have her as his deputy chief constable in Derbyshire. 

Ms Swann who began her career in Leicestershire Police and joined the Derbyshire force in 2019 said the abuse has been recorded as a hate incident.
