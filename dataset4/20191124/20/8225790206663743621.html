Surf declining for north and west shores, but advisory still up for east-facing beaches

24 Nov 2019, 20:35 by Ben Gutierrez

HONOLULU, Hawaii (HawaiiNewsNow) - Strong trade winds are still pushing in advisory-level surf for east-facing shores, but a high surf advisory for north and west shores has been canceled as a short-lived northwest swell declines slowly.

The advisory remains in effect until 6 a.m. Monday for the east-facing shores of Kauai, Oahu, Molokai, Maui and the Big Island.

East shore waves are forecast at 6 to 9 feet, with rough and choppy conditions.

Beachgoers should expect strong breaking waves, along with strong longshore and rip currents that can make swimming difficult and dangerous. Use caution and heed all advice from ocean safety lifeguards.
