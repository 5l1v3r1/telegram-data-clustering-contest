Meet the order of Irish nuns invited to appear on Britain's Got Talent

So far the Poor Clares of Nuns’ Island in Galway are resisting invitations to appear and sing in front of millions of viewers

24 Nov 2019, 20:01 by Louise Roseingrave

An order of Irish nuns has caught the eye of a top Britain’s Got Talent scout - who has invited them to perform on the show.

But so far the Poor Clares of Nuns’ Island in Galway are resisting invitations to appear and sing in front of millions of viewers.

The musical sisters released a song last month, Calm The Soul, which has clocked up more than 50,000 views online.

The nuns are tickled by the invitation to appear on TV, but feel an appearance might be counter-productive to their cause.

Sr Colette, Abbess of the Poor Clare Monastery, said: “A researcher from the show has been in touch a number of times asking us to perform ‘Calm the Soul.’
