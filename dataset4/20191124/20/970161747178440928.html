Jeremy Corbyn says people will continue to 'die on streets' under Tory manifesto

24 Nov 2019, 20:26

The Conservative manifesto is the ‘same old same old’ and will not stop people ‘dying on the streets’ because of austerity, Jeremy Corbyn has claimed.

The Labour leader said the ‘brutality’ of the Universal Credit system was placing ‘truly horrific’ stress on vulnerable people, as he launched a new scheme for a ‘cultural renaissance’ in London this evening.

In an exclusive interview with Metro.co.uk, on the day that the Tories unveiled their General Election manifesto, Mr Corbyn heavily criticised the governing party’s record on poverty.

He said: ‘Homelessness and rough sleeping is increasing – [it is] very hard to put an exact figure on it but Shelter and Crisis will both give you estimates which run into many thousands more than the so-called official count that there is and you come across all the time people dying on the streets of this country.

‘We are not a poor country, that should simply not happen.’

Mr Corbyn continued: ‘There is also, sadly, the number of people taking their own lives when they have been on Universal Credit, or have been told to undertake a capability for work assessment test, been told they are fit for work when they are clearly are not, appeal it and it takes a very long time to appeal it and during that period are taking their own lives.

‘It is truly horrific the stress that many people, particularly those with mental health conditions, are put under, by the brutality of the Universal Credit system.’

In September, Mr Corbyn tweeted a video in which the journalist Ash Sarkar said 120,000 people have died as a result of austerity, based on an academic paper. However, Full Fact have suggested the claim needs to be treated with a ‘bit of caution’.
