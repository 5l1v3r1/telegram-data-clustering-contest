Soon, survey to legalise hawkers in Delhi

As per the Street Vendors Act, 2014, eviction of vendors is illegal before a survey is conducted by the TVCs, and before vending zones are declared and licences are issued to surveyed vendors.

24 Nov 2019, 20:37 by Ananya Tiwari

To legalise street vending and hawking across the capital, town vending committees (TVCs) will conduct surveys of vendors as per the Street Vendors Act, 2014, in the coming weeks.

As per the Street Vendors Act, 2014, eviction of vendors is illegal before a survey is conducted by the TVCs, and before vending zones are declared and licences are issued to surveyed vendors.

The Delhi government’s urban development department has agreed to assist municipalities in the survey. Manisha Saxena, principal secretary of the department, said, “We are empanelling agencies to help the TVCs.”

The proposal inviting bids from surveying agencies was put online on October 31 and on November 6, the urban development department held a pre-bid meeting with five agencies.

Several evicted vendors met SDMC and NDMC officials on November 19 to demand their inclusion in the survey. Ramesh Verma, additional commissioner SDMC, said, “We will meet vendors and officials to discuss it.”

In the past few months, hawkers have been evicted from Jama Masjid, Darya Ganj Book Bazaar, Lajpat Nagar and Karol Bagh.
