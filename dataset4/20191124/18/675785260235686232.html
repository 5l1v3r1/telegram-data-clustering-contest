Aldi Kevin the Carrot: How to get your cuddly toy in time for Christmas

Kevin the Carrot, which is available at Aldi, is selling out quickly at the moment, leaving thousands of shoppers disappointed after they queued for several hours

24 Nov 2019, 18:14 by Bradley Jolly, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/bradley-jolly/

Aldi shoppers can purchase a furry toy called Kevin the Carrot at its supermarkets ahead of Christmas.

But the cute mascots are selling out so quickly that many shoppers have been left disappointed already.

Some woke up early to pre-order the toys but they were all gone after just a few hours.

Aldi has, though, limited purchases to two per customer.

Normal-sized toys are priced at £3.99. You can buy an absurdly giant one for £19.99, which stands at one metre tall, and one of Kevin’s arch-nemesis, the evil Pascal the Parsnip.

But frustrated punters expressed their anger today.

"They sold whilst I waited and within seconds of that happening they were being sold on eBay for stupid prices," one dad said.

And an irate gran said: "I was in the queue With 33,000 in front of me.

"There must be a better way of doing this. We don't stand a chance of getting the Aldi Kevin family now, disappointed grandchildren."

Another lady wrote: "I was on the night shift at work. I tried to get on the Aldi site from midnight.  It took me three hours to get to the page where I could see I was in a queue, by 4 am everything was sold out.  These toys are for sale on eBay already."

Shoppers can also snap up Kevin's wife Katie and three children.

The mascot debut in Aldi Christmas TV adverts in 2016 and this year's inspiration came from Peaky Blinders.

A spokesman for Aldi told Mirror Online: “We’re sorry that some shoppers were unable to get their hands on these toys online this morning, however, demand has been extremely high.

"As with all of our Specialbuy products, these will also only be available while stocks last."
