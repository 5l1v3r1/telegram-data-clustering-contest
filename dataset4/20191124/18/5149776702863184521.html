Revealed: The number of people who don't earn the real living wage in Nottinghamshire

Mansfield and Newark are some of the worst areas

24 Nov 2019, 18:08 by Oliver Pridmore

New figures claim thousands of people across Nottinghamshire are not earning a 'real living wage'.

Produced by the Annual Survey of Hours and Earnings (ASHE), the figures show more than one third of employees in some parts of the East Midlands don't earn the hourly rate.

The real living wage is a voluntary rate set by the Living Wage Foundation which hundreds of employers have signed up to - including Bassetlaw District Council and Robin Hood Energy in Nottinghamshire.

The real living wage was increased from £9 to £9.30 at the start of November, differing from the Government's national living wage which currently stands at £8.21 an hour.

Now, the new figures have revealed thousands of people in Nottinghamshire don't earn that amount.

The figures, broken down by parliamentary constituency, show that Mansfield and Newark have the highest number of people in the county who don't earn the wage.

Here are the numbers for Nottinghamshire in full:

Ashfield - 1,910 people not earning real living wage (19.1% of 10,000 employees)

Bassetlaw - 2,240 people not earning real living wage (22.4% of 10,000 employees)

Broxtowe - 2,034 people not earning real living wage (22.6% of 9,000 employees)

Gedling - 1,170 people not earning real living wage (23.4% of 5,000 employees)

Mansfield - 3,300 people not earning real living wage (30% of 11,000 employees)

Newark - 3,300 people not earning real living wage (30% of 11,000 employees)

Nottingham East - 1,773 people not earning real living wage (19.7% of 9,000 employees)

Nottingham North - 1,582 people not earning real living wage (22.6% of 7,000 employees)

Nottingham South - 4,664 people not earning real living wage (21.2% of 22,000 employees)

Rushcliffe - 775 people not earning real living wage (15.5% of 5,000 employees)

Sherwood - 3,177 people not earning real living wage (35.3% of 9,000 employees)

HMRC refused to comment when contacted by Nottinghamshire Live.
