Three-point barrage powers University of Regina Cougars to victory in Saskatoon

The University of Regina Cougars men's basketball team made 18 three-pointers during Saturday's win over the host U of S Huskies.

24 Nov 2019, 18:16 by Regina Leader-Post

Eighteen three-pointers powered the University of Regina Cougars men’s basketball team to a 103-74 Canada West victory over the University of Saskatchewan Huskies on Saturday in Saskatoon.

The victory was the Cougars’ first over a nationally ranked team since Jan. 22, 2016, when the U of R defeated the eighth-seeded UBC Thunderbirds 83-79 in Vancouver. The U of S was fifth in last week’s U Sports rankings.

Regina shot 51 per cent from three-point range. Carter Millar had four treys. Greishe Clerjuste, Samuel Hillis and Josiah Thomas each had three.

Clerjuste had a game-high 26 points, plus six rebounds and four assists. Millar and Hillis each had 20 points to help the Cougars improve their record to 4-4. The Huskies dropped to 5-1.

On Friday, the Huskies won 89-83 in overtime. Regina had held leads of 30-16 and 40-19.

Late in the fourth quarter, Saskatoon led 80-77 before a basket by Hillis and a free throw by Thomas forced overtime.

Millar led the Cougars with 18 points and 14 rebounds. Thomas added 17 points and teammate Brayden Kuski chipped in with 15.

Next up for the Cougars is a weekend set with the sixth-ranked University of Alberta Golden Bears. Games are set for Friday and Saturday at the Centre for Kinesiology, Health and Sport. Tip-off is scheduled for 8 p.m., both nights.

• • •

In women’s basketball, the No. 1-ranked Huskies swept the visiting Cougars in a two-game set.

The U of S won 73-59 on Friday and 74-45 on Saturday.

Macaela Crone led the Cougars with 16 points and eight rebounds on Friday. Michaela Kleisinger (15) and Emily Dewey (10) also scored in double figures for the U of R.

Faith Reid‘s 16 points paced the Cougars (3-5) on Saturday. The Huskies are 6-0.

The Cougars are to play host to Alberta on Friday and Saturday. Both games are set for 6 p.m.

• • •

The Cougars women’s hockey team split a weekend set with the Huskies.

On Friday, the Cougars won 3-2 in a shootout in Saskatoon. Jaycee Magwood and Jenna Merk scored for Regina in the shootout, during which Cougars goalie Jane Kish stopped two of three Huskies shots.

Jordan Kulbida and Tamara McVannel scored in regulation time for Regina. Kish made 31 saves, including five in the five-minute overtime period.

Bailee Bourassa had both goals for the Huskies.

The Huskies rebounded Saturday with a 4-3 victory at the Co-operators Centre. Bourassa snapped a 3-3 tie early in the third period to help the Huskies improve to 6-4-1-3.

Magwood, Paige Hubbard and Jadyn Kushniruk scored for Regina (3-8-3-0).

The University of Calgary Dinos are to visit the Co-operators Centre for games Friday and Saturday. Both contests are set for 7 p.m.

• • •

Saskatchewan swept the Cougars in men’s hockey, winning 3-1 in Regina on Friday and 2-0 in Saskatoon on Saturday.

Tristan Frei scored for the Cougars on Friday. Jared Dmytriw, who is from Craven, had both goals for the Huskies on Saturday. Travis Child made 28 saves for the shutout.

The Cougars (2-11-1) are in Calgary next weekend.

• • •

The Cougars’ Brian Palaschuk won three bronze medals Saturday at the Canada West swimming championships in Lethbridge.

He placed third in the men’s 100-metre butterfly, 200m freestyle and 400m individual medley.

Bree Crookshanks won a bronze medal in the women’s 100m breaststroke.

• • •

Calgary swept the host Cougars in women’s volleyball. Game scores were 19-25, 25-16, 25-18, 25-13 (Friday) and 25-19, 25-23, 25-10 (Saturday).

The Cougars (1-9) are to visit the University of Winnipeg Wesmen next weekend.
