Government owes community a 'big apology' over Lumsden Maternity Centre downgrade - MP Hamish Walker

24 Nov 2019, 18:08

Ministers were warned pregnant women would be at risk due to the distance they would have to travel for maternity services in Lumsden, Newshub can reveal.

Now the Government is being urged to apologise over the handling of the Lumsden Maternity Centre downgrade. 

The Southland town's birthing centre was downgraded in April, but since then many have voiced concerns over maternity services in the area.

In August, MPs voted not to reinstate the centre, despite acknowledging there were issues with the downgrade. A committee rejected a petition by Clutha-Southland MP Hamish Walker to reinstate full services at the centre.

But Newshub can reveal ministers were warned pregnant women would be at risk due to the distance they would have to travel.

"The fact is they haven't followed their own Ministry of Health advice - this is disgraceful," Walker told Newshub.

"Five mothers gave birth without making it to a primary birthing unit. That's because the Government has treated Southland people like second-class citizens."

He says it shows incompetence by the Government.

"This is an absolute train wreck, it has been all along. The Health Minister and the DHB need to give the community a pretty big apology."

Health Minister David Clark declined to comment. 

Earlier this month, a report compiled by Ernst and Young gave a damning assessment of Southland's maternity hubs. The report criticised the "informal" management processes and said that there was an "inconsistent delivery of service".
