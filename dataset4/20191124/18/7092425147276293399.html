Westlife fan opens up about how "I'll See You Again" gave her peace after her son's death

24 Nov 2019, 18:03 by Nick Fitzgerald

Conor passed away some four years ago

A long-time Westlife fan recently opened up about how the band's song "I'll See You Again" gave her peace, following the death of her son Conor.

Sinead Doyle attended Friday night's show of the Late Late where she gave a beautiful recollection on how the song helped her cope.

Her son was stillborn at 38 weeks, some four years ago, and Sinead remembers one day she was out walking and the song came on.

I'll See You Again was included in a playlist she was listening to at the time. And it just hit her. She felt at peace about the death during listening.

Addressing The Late Late Show crowd she said:

"I have a favourite song, I'll See You Again. When I heard it a few years ago I didn't really pay much attention, it was a nice song.

"I'm a huge fan for years and years. Our son died four years ago. He was born sleeping at 38 weeks.

"That was four years ago and about a year ago I was out walking, I have the playlist of all the albums and it (I'll See You Again) came on.

"People used to say to me 'Oh there's a feather', that must be Conor. And when you lose someone you kind of get angry and you go 'Yeah whatever'.

"I heard the song again and I hadn't heard it in a long time. It gave me peace."

Following her heartfelt recollection, Sinead was then invited up on stage by host Ryan Tubridy to meet Westlife who were guests of the show on the night.
