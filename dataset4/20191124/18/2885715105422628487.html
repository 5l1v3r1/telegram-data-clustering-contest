Watch: Manchester United concedes in 90th, held 3-3 at Sheffield United

Manchester United scored three goals in a seven-minute span from the 72nd to go ahead in dramatic fashion.

24 Nov 2019, 18:52 by AP

Manchester United conceded a 90th-minute equalizer after recovering from a two-goal deficit to draw 3-3 with Sheffield United in a wild Premier League match on Sunday.

Oli McBurnie’s goal required a review by VAR for a possible handball but it was awarded — to loud roars by the home fans at Bramall Lane — to stop Man United climbing to fifth place in the standings.

Having fallen 2-0 down after 52 minutes, Man United scored three goals in a seven-minute span from the 72nd to go ahead in dramatic fashion.

All of those goals were scored by academy graduates, with 19-year-old Brandon Williams and 18-year-old Mason Greenwood netting for the first time in the league before Marcus Rashford completed the remarkable comeback.
