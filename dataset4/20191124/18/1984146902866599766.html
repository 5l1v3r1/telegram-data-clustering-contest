Aston Villa boss Dean Smith wary of Newcastle's pace and power as he outlines respect for Steve Bruce

Bruce will lock horns with former club Aston Villa tomorrow and in Smith, he will face the man who replaced him at Villa Park

24 Nov 2019, 18:00 by Sean McCormick

Aston Villa boss Dean Smith has spoken of his respect for Newcastle boss Steve Bruce and has praised the job he has done at St James' Park so far.

Bruce returns to Villa Park tomorrow for the first time since being sacked as Villa boss last season. Bruce took the Villans to a Championship play-off final in 2018. His replacement, Smith, went one better and guided the West Midlands giants to a Premier League return last season.

After a spell with Sheffield Wednesday, Bruce replaced Rafa Benitez as Newcastle boss in the summer to make his own return to the Premier League.

The two sides meet on Monday evening as they both look for the win that will pull them further clear of the relegation zone.

Smith has been impressed with the job Bruce has done at Newcastle and he is particularly wary of the Magpies' pace and power up front.

"I have an awful lot of respect for what he has done in management but, more importantly, for how he is as a man," he told BBC Sport.

"There were a lot of questions about the club and where it was going but Steve has gone in and done what he does best - got them organised.

"They've got pace and power up front. They are winning games - they've won the last couple - and they beat Manchester United at home as well. They are in a good run of form at the moment."
