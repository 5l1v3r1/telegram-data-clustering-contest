Drayden Ramage backstops Regina Pat Canadians to victory

Drayden Ramage notched a shutout for the midget AAA Regina Pat Canadians on Saturday.

24 Nov 2019, 18:07 by Regina Leader-Post

Drayden Ramage made 15 saves for the shutout as the Regina Pat Canadians blanked the Prince Albert Mintos 4-0 in Saskatchewan Midget AAA Hockey League action at the Co-operators Centre on Saturday.

Blake Swetlikoff scored two goals for the Pat Canadians, who got singles from Hunter Degelman and Cal Caragata.

After 19 games, Caragata leads the league in goals (20) and points (37).

The Pat Canadians are to return to action Tuesday against the Notre Dame Hounds (8 p.m., Duncan McNeill Arena).

• • •

Allee Gerrard had two goals and three assists for the Regina Rebels in Saturday’s 9-0 Saskatchewan Female Midget AAA Hockey League victory over the Weyburn Richardson Pioneer Gold Wings at the Co-operators Centre.

Megan Hayhurst added three goals and one assist. Sydney Pedersen (two goals, one assist) and Emma DeCorby (four assists) also provided offensive support for goaltender Chantel Weller, who made 19 saves for the shutout.

Olivia Leggett and Molly Flynn completed the scoring.

• • •

Isaiah Thomas scored at 4:24 of overtime to give the Estevan Bruins a 5-4 Saskatchewan Junior Hockey League victory over the host Kindersley Klippers on Saturday.

Southey-born Tanner Manz added two goals for Estevan.

Also on Saturday, Luke Nkwama had three goals and one assist for the host Melville Millionaires in their 5-1 victory over the visiting La Ronge Ice Wolves.

Millionaires goalie Berk Berkeliev made 40 saves.

• • •

Two goals by Tristan Sulz helped the Extreme Hockey Regina Capitals defeat the host Saskatoon Westleys 4-1 in Prairie Junior Hockey League action Saturday.

Kyle Colwell and Joey Prebushewski also scored for the Capitals. Matthew Conlon replied for the Westleys, who were outshot 48-22.

• • •

The Moose Jaw Vanier Vikings and Rouleau Rebels won Saskatchewan High Schools Athletic Association volleyball championships Saturday.

Vanier won the 4A boys title. Rouleau was victorious in 1A boys.

Birch Hills (3A boys) and Thunderchild (2A boys) also won provincial titles.

• • •

Oroma Bongomin scored 35 points to lead the Regina Christian Grizzlies to a 70-50 victory over the Scott Blues in Regina Intercollegiate Basketball League junior boys action Friday.

Josh Panipekeesick led Scott with 32 points.

In a junior girls game, Morgyn Geisam scored 14 points for the Grizzlies in a 43-27 victory over Scott. Tori-Anne Desjarlais led the Blues with seven points.
