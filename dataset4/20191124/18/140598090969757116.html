Former New York Mayor Michael Bloomberg enters Democrat presidential race

24 Nov 2019, 18:02 by David Millward

After weeks of speculation, former New York City mayor Michael Bloomberg has entered the crowded race for the Democratic presidential nomination.

The 77-year-old billionaire, ranked by Forbes as the 11th richest man in the world, announced his candidature on his website pledging to defeat Donald Trump and rebuild the country.

"We cannot afford four more years of President Trump's reckless and unethical actions.

“He represents an existential threat to our country and our values. If he wins another term in office, we may never recover from the damage."

Mr Bloomberg’s decision to enter the race has raised questions over how his publishing company will cover the campaign.

In an email to staff, Bloomberg editor-in-chief John Micklethwait said they would not be investigating its founder.

"We will continue our tradition of not investigating Mike (and his family and foundation) and we will extend the same policy to his rivals in the Democratic primaries.

“We cannot treat Mike's Democratic competitors differently from him."

Mr Bloomberg is the second Democratic moderate to throw his hat into the ring in less than a fortnight, following Deval Patrick, the former governor of Massachusetts.

Both entered the race amid fears that former vice president Joe Biden is in danger of being eclipsed by Elizabeth Warren, the Left-wing Massachusetts senator who has vowed to impose steep tax increases on the wealthiest Americans to pay for her ambitious “Medicare for All” proposals.

Within minutes of Mr Bloomberg’s announcement, the Warren team sent out a fund-raising appeal to supporters, accusing him of using his wealth to buy the nomination.

“Since filing paperwork to join the presidential race two days ago, Michael Bloomberg has poured over $30 million into buying a week's worth of ads in media markets all over the country,” supporters were told in an email.
