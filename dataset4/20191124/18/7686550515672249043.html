The rugby evening headlines as South African 'does a Vahaamahina' and ex-Ireland lock brands Lyon 's**te'

It's been another busy day in the rugby universe

24 Nov 2019, 18:35 by Matthew Southcombe

Your evening headlines for Sunday, November 24.

Montpellier lock 'does a Vahaamahina'

Jacques du Plessis was sent off earlier today during Montpellier's match against Gloucester for an act that is being compared to that of Sebastien Vahaamahina.

The French lock was shown red in the Rugby World Cup quarter-final against for elbowing Aaron Wainwright in the jaw in the middle of a maul.

Around the hour mark at the GGL Stadium a similar incident played out as du Plessis wrestled with Gerbrandt Grobler at the centre of a maul before proceeding to elbow him square in the face.

When checking with his TMO, referee George Clancy asked: "Is this like the one with France in the World Cup?"

The lock was shown a red card for the offence but his side held on for a 30-27 victory.
