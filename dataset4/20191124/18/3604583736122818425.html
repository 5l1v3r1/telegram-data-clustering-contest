Daylesford burglaries: Shepherds Flat man pleads guilty

24 Nov 2019, 18:00 by Erin Williams

A Shepherds Flat man has pleaded guilty to burglaring three properties more than 10 years ago despite not remembering the offending.

Simon Frazer, 49, burgled the properties at Korweinguboora in September 2006 and Hepburn Springs in November 2008, leaving traces of his blood behind.

The Ballarat Magistrates Court was told on Thursday Frazer stole $800 worth of property during the Korweinguboora burglary and $7300 worth of items from two Hepburn Springs units.

The stolen items, which included two televisions, a DVD player and washing machine, have never been recovered.

Police prosecutor Senior Constable Simon Pearce said on July 11, 2019, Frazer was at the Royal Hotel playing pool when he stole a bank card from the victim's leather jacket pocket.

He said the card was used to buy a taxi fare and more than $100 worth of items from the Coles supermarket in two transactions.

Frazer could not recall the incident because he was drunk, while the burglaries had also been erased from his memory.

He sought a sentenced indication on three counts of burglary and one count of obtaining property by deception.

Defence lawyer McClennan said the sudden death of his client's wife lead to Frazer's anxiety and depression that were largely untreated.

He said Frazer had a long-standing cannabis addiction where he smoked half-a-gram a day, but he was now free of the drug.

Mr McClennan said Frazer was seeking a sentence indication because the burglaries occurred more than 10 years ago and he could not remember them but he wanted the matter finalised.

Magistrate Michelle Mykytowycz said Frazer would have received immediate imprisonment on the burglary charges 10 years ago but she was prepared to hand him a non-custodial sentence.

"These are serious burglaries, home burglaries with high value items stole. There really needs to be general deterrence," Ms Mykytowycz said.

He accepted the indication and pleaded guilty to the offences, including breaching a community correction order.

"Domestic burglaries are very serious. People should feel safe in their house," Ms Mykytowycz said.

"When someone breaks into a house and steals items, it breaches safety and people really should feel safe in their homes."

Frazer was sentenced to a community correction order with a work component only of 70 hours of community work.

His previous CCO was varied to restart on Thursday for 12 months.
