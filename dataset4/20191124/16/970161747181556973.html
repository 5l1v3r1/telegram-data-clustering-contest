Sonic film was always doomed in Hollywood, despite million-dollar edits

24 Nov 2019, 16:23 by Mel Evans

A blue hedgehog has all of a sudden wreaked havoc on the internet. Who knew such small creatures wielded such dominant power?

When Sonic the Hedgehog’s Hollywood endeavour was first glimpsed in the form of a trailer, every man and his dog was up in arms over how the little fella looked and how ‘not funny’ the film appeared on first glance.

Not even fictional animal characters originating in 1991 can escape heated critique, it appears. The heat got so bad, in fact, that a new trailer was made to appease the punters. Even though we should all be celebrating the fact he’s getting a big screen welcome at all.

Reacting to the viral backlash, editors put together a fresh trailer that took the armchair critiques into account, redesigning the whole thing and showing how seriously Hollywood takes the fandom’s love of this nostalgic character.

But punters still aren’t convinced they’ll be getting a gaming-accurate representation of Sonic, let alone a funny one – so even if the re-designed trailer had been revealed first, Sonic would still be causing mass outrage in the gaming community.

The film’s studio was forced to fork out millions – MILLIONS – and delayed release by three months to keep an army of screeching fans happy and make the character look more like the Sonic of their dreams.

Evidently gaming fans hold such pull over Hollywood that a bunch of execs signed off on expensive edits to appease the baying crowds. If that doesn’t speak to the sway of fandom, I don’t know what will.

It seems no matter how many edits are made, Sonic will never look close enough to his iconic gaming likeness and this exposes how Hollywood is doomed to fail on fan-favourite pop culture icons.

No one is taking away your right to game with Sonic if the character isn’t a perfect representation in a film, but think not about how outraged you are and about what can be gained from sharing the greatness with a new generation.

Sonic is the 14th biggest game in history so it’s clear there are millions of fans who don’t want to see his profile tarnished by the big wigs of Hollywood, or perhaps even projected into a film in the first place.

Yet I think the backlash has uncovered a certain toxicity within the fandom which sees hardcore gaming loyalists refuse to accept any sort of deviation from what they believe to be the ultimate Sonic. Which, I should add, is perfectly subjective.

I can never understand why we can’t just enjoy nice things as they arise without comparing them to others. In this case, Sonic has been compared to the Detective Pikachu film as if they were Kardashians.

What I can understand, though, is that long-time fans may be feeling jilted, and that’s where I hear you.
