Conor McGregor news: The Notorious' fight return in doubt as UFC release schedule

The former two-weight champion had claimed he would fight on January 18 in Las Vegas

24 Nov 2019, 16:27 by Martin Domin

Conor McGregor's return to the UFC looks to have been put on hold after the promotion released its early schedule for 2020.

The former two-weight champion had claimed he would fight on January 18 in Las Vegas with Donald Cerrone his likely opponent.

McGregor's manager last week insisted a deal was close that would see the Dubliner fight for the first time since last October.

But in the UFC's schedule of events, the first pay-per-view event of 2020, UFC 246, will take place on February 9 at the Honda Center in Houston.

Light-heavyweight champion Jon Jones is expected to headline the event when he defends his title against Dominic Reyes.

With McGregor only likely to fight in either Las Vegas or New York, the former champion looks set to be sidelined until March.

The Dubliner has been shrouded in controversy recently having appeared in court charged with assaulting a man in a pub.

He was fined just €1,000 and appeared ready to make his long-awaited return to the cage.

His manager Audie Attar, speaking on ESPN, recently said: “I would say that talks have been progressing.

"We’ve met them, we’re in close communication with them. I would hope that an announcement could happen in the coming weeks, but at the end of the day, it’s still a work in progress."

Of course, if a deal is struck, UFC 246 could be switched to Las Vegas' T-Mobile Arena in January with the Houston event becoming UFC 247.

Or the company could break from tradition and give McGregor's return a new moniker to mark the occasion.

The former featherweight and lightweight ruler has been out of action since he was choked out by 155lb king Khabib Nurmagomedov last year.

UFC chief White said last month that he eager to see ‘The Notorious’ take on ‘Cowboy’ Cerrone, he admits an agreement has yet to be reached.

“We have nothing done,” he told TMZ . “Nothing is even close to being done, so I hate to even talk about this stuff and say it because it might not happen.

“It might happen, but we’re looking at Cowboy.

“No3 vs No5, January 18 at the T-Mobile [Arena] in Las Vegas.
