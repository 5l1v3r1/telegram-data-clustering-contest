10 SeaBus sailings cancelled Sunday as transit strike continues

24 Nov 2019, 16:34 by Jonathan Szekeres and Nikitha Martins

Summary

Give yourself some extra time if you're planning to use transit Sunday

10 SeaBus sailings have been cancelled as the labour dispute continues to impact service

No negotiations have been held between the bus drivers' union and their employer this weekend

VANCOUVER (NEWS 1130) – No deal has been reached, and that means another day of delays and cancellations because of the Metro Vancouver transit strike.

10 SeaBus sailings have been axed for Sunday evening.

Lonsdale Quay sailings will be departing every 30 minutes compared to the regular 15 minutes as of 6:30 p.m. to around 9:00 p.m, and Waterfront sailings will be departing every 30 minutes as well from about 6:45 p.m to 9:00 p.m.

Translink is also warning it will likely take you longer to catch a bus, although those delays are nothing compared to what’s planned for later this week, when bus drivers have threatened to shut down bus service for three straight days beginning on Wednesday.

Bus drivers and their employer have not been bargaining this weekend, and while SkyTrain workers have, they haven’t been able to reach a deal.
