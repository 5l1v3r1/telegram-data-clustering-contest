View from connections ahead of Sunday's action at Auteuil

24 Nov 2019, 16:12 by Jason Kiely

Jason Kiely speaks to jockey Theo Chevillard ahead of his ride on today's big-race favourite Edgeoy. Also Olivier d’Andigné on progressive hurdler Cotée Sud

On the Edge of greatness

With the year almost finished at Auteuil racecourse, we have time for one final ‘Super Sunday’ card at the spectacular Parisian track where over €960,000 prize money has been put on offer for the eight races. The ground described as ‘very heavy’.

The highlight is the Grade Two Prix Georges Courtois, a race that has been won by some fantastic champions over the years including Bel La Vie and Remember Rose. Although today's eight-strong field may be lacking the presence of an established star, it may be a case of discovering a new one as Edgeoy lines-up as favourite for this prestigious chase.

A hugely impressive winner when making all the running and jumping his rivals into submission in the Prix Heros XII just over a month ago, the François Nicolle trained five-year-old has been in fantastic form since returning from his summer break and his jockey Theo Chevillard (who replaces the suspended Angelo Zuliani) suggests the best could be yet to come

“Edgeoy is going well at home, he has come out of his race well from the last day and we have freshened him up nicely, hopefully well will see him progress again this weekend he is a very nice horse for the upcoming years,” said the jockey.
