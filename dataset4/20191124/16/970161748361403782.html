The Grand Tour changes Jeremy Clarkson's mind about climate change

24 Nov 2019, 16:13

Jeremy Clarkson’s eyes have been opened to the climate crisis after filming a recent episode of The Grand Tour.

The presenter has made no secret of his issues with environmentalists in the past.

His epiphany came during filming of an episode in southeast Asia.

Along with Richard Hammond and James May, the trio attempted to navigate the Mekong river, but they found parts of it had dried up leaving the presenter ‘genuinely alarmed’.

Jeremy told The Times: ‘The irony is not lost on me. A man who hosted a car programme for 30 years, limited to 7mph by global warming.

‘It’s the first time that we’ve ever admitted to there being global warming. We were completely screwed on that [trip].’

He added that one lake, Tonle Sap, used to be ‘knee-deep’ but when he was there there was no water.

It meant that filming was regularly scuppered with their boat either running aground or having to be towed.

In a swipe at climate crisis campaigner Greta Thunberg, Jeremy said: ‘It was alarming, genuinely alarming. But we don’t blame mankind for it. We’ll let Greta [Thunberg] do that.’

The Grand Tour season four will kick off with the first of its ‘feature-length epic road trips’ next month.

The episode is called The Grand Tour Presents: Seamen and in their announcement of the new season’s release date Amazon Prime say: ‘For one time only, the presenters have taken a break from four wheels and instead undertaken an ambitious journey in boats. Their adventure-packed voyage takes them along one of the world’s most iconic waterways – the Mekong Delta.

‘Fans can expect elements of their previous classic road trips, with the trio choosing their boats in the usual way they choose their cars, with their own off-the-wall rationale, putting their own spin on their individual vessels… as well as each other’s.’

Amazon Prime also recently confirmed that filming has wrapped on episode two in Madagascar.

The Grand Tour: Season 4 launches on Friday 13 December exclusively to Amazon Prime Video.
