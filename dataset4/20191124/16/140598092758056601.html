Towns devastated by flood to be deluged again as Met Office issues weather warnings

24 Nov 2019, 16:55 by Jack Hardy

Flood-stricken towns in South Yorkshire will face a fresh deluge this week, as forecasters warned heavy rain meant households should take “precautions”.

A major evacuation effort was launched in the village of Fishlake earlier this month after the River Don burst its banks and flooded much of the surrounding area. 

The Army was drafted in to help rescue people from more than 1,000 homes, leaving many residents homeless for days.

Now there could be further trouble in store for the Doncaster region as a yellow rain warning was issued by the Met Office for Wednesday, covering most of Yorkshire.

Flood warnings have not yet been put in place for Fishlake and nearby towns and villages, with the forecaster saying it was too early to judge how damaging the weather could be. 

Sophie Yeomans, from the Met Office, said the heavy rain could “potentially” heap further misery on flood-hit communities.
