Nintendo Was Worried Donkey Kong Country Was "Too 3D"

24 Nov 2019, 09:15 by Zack Zwiezen

This week marks the 25th anniversary of the classic and beloved Donkey Kong Country. To celebrate this milestone, some of the developers behind the game took part in a series of interviews about the game, its development and its release. According to one developer who worked on the game, Nintendo was actually worried DKC was “too 3D”, that it would be overwhelming for players and they would be unable to actually play it.

Released on November 21, 1994 for the SNES and developed by Rare, Donkey Kong Country was a 2D platformer built using 3D-looking assets. In a new video interview and documentary from Shesez, best known for the popular Boundary Break videos, we learn a bunch of interesting information about the development of this classic game.
