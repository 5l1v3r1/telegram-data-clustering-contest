Ryan Lowe's Plymouth Argyle on the rise after superb trio of wins in November

THE VERDICT: The Pilgrims are up to eighth in League Two after beating Bradford City 2-1

24 Nov 2019, 09:12 by Chris Errington

Plymouth Argyle will end up having played only three times in November but it has still been a very important, profitable month for them.

By the time comes around for their next game, away to Bristol Rovers in the FA Cup second round, it will be December 1.

Ryan Lowe’s Pilgrims have won all three of their matches this month and against good opposition too.

That humiliating 4-0 Devon Derby defeat at Exeter City at the end of October now seems in the distant past.

It says a lot about the character of this group of Argyle players that they have been able to put that game behind them.

There was a furious reaction from the Green Army in the immediate aftermath, and rightly so.

To their credit, though, the Pilgrims’ players have gone a long way to making amends for it with their results in November.

They had to wait two weeks for their next competitive match after the defeat by Exeter.

That was caused by the postponement of their game against Grimsby Town on November 2 due to structural damage to the Barn Park End roof caused by overnight gales.

So it was not until November 9 that Argyle finally got the chance to get Exeter out of their systems.

They started that process by beating Bolton Wanderers 1-0 in the FA Cup first round.

Argyle followed that up with a victory by the same scoreline at then League Two leaders Forest Green Rovers.

The Pilgrims then completed a 100 per cent November with a 2-1 defeat of high-flying Bradford City on Saturday.

Bradford arrived at Home Park third in the table and on a good run of form themselves, but they ended the day in seventh position after losing to Argyle.

Lowe, wisely, made the point that no-one in the Pilgrims’ camp was getting too carried away by their results in November.

Nonetheless, they also deserve a short opportunity to savour how far they have come since that fateful day at Exeter.

They could have crumbled after that. Instead, they have come together as a team and there is hope for what is still to come this season.

One of the keys to their success has been the outstanding form of midfielder Antoni Sarcevic.

He scored the winning goal against Forest Green but, generally, his performances, both in an attacking sense and defensively, have been excellent.

The three wins on the bounce have also come with on-loan Chelsea defender Josh Grant playing in a new midfield holding role.

That has given Argyle a more solid defensive look about them and a base to build from.

The Pilgrims have conceded only one goal in the last three matches, and that was an unfortunate 51st minute own goal from Niall Canavan.

With Grant excelling in a different position it has also led to Joe Edwards moving to right wing-back.

He is well on the way to making that his permanent place in the team, not least because he scored one of the goals against Bradford.

Edwards is a player who brings a lot of determination and energy to the team, which are both excellent assets for a wing-back to have.

He scored both goals in the 2-2 draw at Crawley Town in September from that position and now has added another one.

That is exactly what Lowe wants to see from the players in the wing-back positions in his favoured 3-5-2 formation.

It has all started to click into place for Argyle and while they are far from the finished article they are a lot further along the way to that than they were at the start of November.
