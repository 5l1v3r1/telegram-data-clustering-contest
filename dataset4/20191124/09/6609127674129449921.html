'RELISH IT'

Celtic boss Neil Lennon insists he’s loving title fight against Rangers

24 Nov 2019, 09:15 by Derek McGregor

CELTIC boss Neil Lennon last night told Rangers he’s LOVING their title fight.

The Hoops moved three points clear in what’s been a neck and neck battle at the top of the Premiership – with Steven Gerrard’s men at Hamilton tomorrow.

A James Forrest double and strikes from Odsonne Edouard and Scott Brown saw the Celts demolish Livingston – with Rennes up next in the Europa League at Parkhead on Thursday night.

And asked if he felt it was a proper title contest with Rangers, Lennon replied: “At the minute, no question, yes.

“Psychologically that’s a good win for us.

“We can enjoy our Sunday, you get 24 hours to enjoy, see what the result brings then you move on to the next one.

“We put that to bed, that was a tough game under the circumstances.

“But we’ve dealt with it absolutely brilliantly so we look forward to Rennes on Thursday.

“I think you’ve got to relish the pressure - you either relish it or fear it.

“So RELISH it. It’s great.

“Maybe it is something new for some of the players but I feel they are taking on the challenge well and I think the fans are enjoying it as well.

“There will be anxious moments as we go along, dips and troughs - but we are in a strong position currently and playing brilliantly.

“I don’t think about goal difference at the moment it is way too early.”

Lennon purred with satisfaction as his juggernaut team made it eight straight wins in all competitions, including five successive in the league without a goal lost since the defeat at Livingston early October.

He said: “We were very powerful today.

“Not just because of our fitness levels and the way we battled for second balls – but our football was superb.

“It was a comprehensive win against a tough team to beat at times.

“We were outstanding and when you come back from two weeks off you always worry that momentum will be lost.

“But there was no sign of that at all.

“There wasn't one weakness out there and I'm thrilled with that.

“Their attitude is first class, in terms of how we approached it.”

Lennon lost Jonny Hayes through injury but also welcomed back Leigh Griffiths.

He added: “You can see how popular Leigh is with the fans.

“It's a timely comeback from him with the volume of games we've got.

“Jonny Hayes popped his shoulder so we will have to scan it and see how it is, hopefully not too bad.

“We made the change and Greg Taylor came in and did a great job.”
