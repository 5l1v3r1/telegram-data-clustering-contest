EYES ON THE PRIZE

Steven Gerrard wants Rangers stars to remain focused ahead of big run of games

24 Nov 2019, 09:15 by Alan Potts

RANGERS and Celtic have romped clear of the rest of the field in a two-horse title race.

But Steven Gerrard wants his players to keep the blinkers on so they don’t veer off course.

The Gers gaffer has ordered his side to ignore the hurdles in the distance and focus on what’s right in front of them.

And that means taking it one game at a time instead of racing ahead of themselves.

The temptation may be to look ahead to Thursday’s Europa League clash away to Feyenoord — where a positive result will book a spot alongside Celtic in the last 32.

But Gerrard wants all eyes to be on Hamilton for their Premiership clash.

The Light Blues face a block of ten games before the winter break — a cup final against Celtic, two European ties and seven crunch Premiership clashes.

But the Ibrox boss wants clear heads as his side gallops towards the finishing line on all fronts.

He said: “You have to picture scenarios where you have a run in the League Cup and go to the wire in the Europa League.

“We knew we could be in a situation where we have ten or 11 games in a six-week period.

“So two things are important — volume and quality. That’s what we tried to do when we put the squad together.

“I didn’t want to say we were the finished article last summer because people will then jump all over you if you have a setback or bump in the road.

“I like to do the talking when the games begin and so far we’re doing all right. I need to keep driving everything and make sure the players are focused on the next game.

“Of course, we set the scene. We show them the schedule and what’s ahead of them.

“But it’s important the players put blinkers on and worry about the next three points — that’s all that matters.

“We have to beat Hamilton away on astroturf, live on TV, with an early kick-off. That’s all we have to focus ourselves on.

“Is it right for other people to talk about the next block of fixtures and the challenges within it? One hundred per cent yes.

“But it would be very naive of me to start talking about what I have to do at Celtic Park or against Young Boys or in the final. That would take my focus right off Hamilton.

“Celtic play in the league before us and it’s impossible to ignore their result.

“The players will all be aware of the score, but it’s vital you focus on your job and what you can control.

“Come kick-off against Hamilton, we’ll know exactly what’s happened. We had a period at the beginning of the last block, in this situation, where we were slow out of the blocks.

“It cost us. That’s the reason why we have to switch the focus on ourselves.”

Gerrard knows the run of games ahead could define their entire season.

The Rangers boss sees getting to finals and reaching the Europa League group stages as a reward for his players’ efforts so far this season.

Some people struggle when the pressure is ramped up, but Gerrard insists his squad have the mental toughness to get the job done when it counts.

He added: “There’s no getting away from it — there’s some really tough, challenging games coming up within the next block of fixtures.

“But we’ve earned that by winning a cup semi-final and having something to play for in Europe.

“So it’s a very exciting period of games. It’s one I’m looking forward to and I know my team are as well.

“I watch them closely every day. We had a couple of injuries recently but the players concerned­ are back fit already and raring to go.

“There was a real hunger in the way we finished the last block of games.

“And that means people at this club are excited about these games. They are looking forward to getting their teeth into them. But it’s a big challenge.

“I never doubt this group of players. I’ve signed them for a reason — because I have belief in them and confidence.

“We’re a good team, we’re a strong team and we’ve proved we can deal with the tough challenges that come our way.

“If you take all the fixtures in isolation, I have confidence that we are going to be good enough.

“We’re going to be in a good position to compete during the challenges that face us — right across the board.

“But there’s no need to focus too much on the block because we had a successful block last time by taking each game in isolation.”

Gerrard saw several of his key men feature for their countries over the last week.

He admits he may freshen up his side this afternoon with Thursday in mind, but insists that he won’t be making wholesale changes.

He said: “Do I have an eye on Thursday? Yes, but not too much. I know Feyenoord is seen as the glamour tie but the important game is Hamilton.

“Obviously lads have been playing internationals, some are a little bit tight. They’re fit, they’re available, but there are little things we have been monitoring.

“There’s numerous things I have to juggle around in terms of being ahead of the game and what I’m thinking about further down the road.

“That doesn’t mean I’ll pick a weaker team. It’s just what works in my eyes.”
