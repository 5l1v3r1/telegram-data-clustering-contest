DOUBLE DUTCH

Rangers star Sheyi Ojo wants Europa League double over Feyenoord and place in knockout stages

24 Nov 2019, 09:15 by Alan Potts

SHEYI OJO fired Rangers on the road to the Europa League last 32 with the only goal against Feyenoord back in September.

Now the on-loan Liverpool ace wants to complete the journey by getting the better of the Dutch side on Thursday and booking a place in the next round.

Steven Gerrard’s side travel to the Netherlands knowing that if they avoid defeat, it would put them in pole position to qualify from Group G.

Since the Ibrox victory Jaap Stam has been replaced in the Feyenoord dugout by former Rangers boss Dick Advocaat as the Little General tries to revive the Dutch giants’ fortunes.

Ojo believes his stunner in the 1-0 win two months ago was the most vital moment of his Gers career so far and he insists the squad believe they can get the job done.

He said: “I think it was important for everyone because it was the first game in the group.

“That was one of the best team performances we have had and we are looking to emulate that on Thursday.

“We’ll look to be as aggressive as we can with and without the ball. We did that in the home game and that’s why we dominated so much.

“Feyenoord are a top team with top players, so we are going to have to match that and probably play even better to get a good result.

“So far we have done really well and if we win or get a good result against Feyenoord, we should be through.

“Everyone is looking forward to it. The European run has been really good — it’s been a good experience for me and everyone is enjoying it.

“The fact that there are so many games coming thick and fast keeps it exciting and everyone wants to progress to the next stage. Hopefully we can do that.

“I think overall this season we have only lost two games. We are really confident going into this game and we back ourselves against any of the top teams.

“If we play our football like we know we can, we should be able to get a good result.”

Before the European adventure continues, Gerrard’s men have to take care of Hamilton Accies first.

Ojo knows Brian Rice’s men will throw up a different challenge than the Eredivisie side will on Thursday.

The wide man admits it can be difficult when some teams play with so many men behind the ball to try and stop the Light Blues.

However, he insists he doesn’t have a problem with it and says the Rangers players just have to make sure they take their chances when they come along.

He added: “Playing in the Europa League is a lot more tactical. It’s back and forth and in the Scottish league it’s predominantly us who have the ball so we need to try to break teams down.

“I have to adapt my game depending on the style of game. You just try to affect the game as much as you can.

“We have been working on that in training, how to beat low blocks. The manager is always telling me to try and affect the space in behind.

“I try do that as much as I can but there isn’t always a lot of space as a lot of the Scottish teams sit back.

"But I genuinely like to get on the ball too so it doesn’t bother me too much.

“As an attacking player you need to be even more clinical with your chances, or your movement in behind and to feet.

“It is definitely a challenge but whichever league you play in you have got to get the ball and try to affect the game and make things happen.”
