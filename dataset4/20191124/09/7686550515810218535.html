It's been an action-packed weekend and here are your rugby morning headlines for Sunday November 24

Injury blow for Wales hopeful

Wales newcomer Willis Halaholo has become an injury doubt for Wayne Pivac's first game in charge.

The Cardiff Blues centre, who has qualified on residency grounds, had to be helped from Arms Park pitch during the agonising 14-11 loss to Leicester in the Challenge Cup.

Halaholo was called into the Wales squad on Tuesday and was likely to feature in Saturday's clash with Warren Gatland's Barbarians at Principality Stadium on Saturday.

But his participation has been thrown into doubt with medics monitoring the situation on Sunday.

Blues coach John Mulvihill remained upbeat, saying: "He is icing the knee. Hopefully he will be okay."

Tipuric opens up on his battle with Warburton

Welsh star Justin Tipuric has opened up for the first time on his battle for the number seven jersey with Sam Warburton.

In a wide-ranging interview with WalesOnline, world-class flanker Tipuric explained he simply had to adopt a pragmatic approach when Warren Gatland opted for Warburton as skipper and his number one.

“Selection going against you is just the way the game can go," he says. “Everyone wants to start and when I was younger I might have been a bit more frustrated with not beginning a match.

“But the older you get the more you are able to put things into perspective and think how lucky you are just to be playing for your country.

“Even today there’s a lot of competition, with so many people who could start at No. 7.

“Before, everyone used to say we had loads of outside halves. But, for me, the crazy strength in depth in Wales now is at seven.”

Biggar 'as good as anyone in the world'

Wales fly-half Dan Biggar was showered with more plaudits after his dramatic Heineken Cup matchwinning kick for Northampton against Benetton.

Biggar landed a difficult penalty in the 85th minute to snatch the game 35-32, leading Northampton director of rugby Chris Boyd to say: "He doesn't miss very often and in those key situations he's as good as anyone in the world.

"We knew if we could get some field position and force an error or find a hole, three points would be enough to win."

Those were the only soothing words from Boyd, though, who rapped of his team's performance: "We were just miles off our game, which was really disappointing. It was probably our worst effort of the year."

Crisis club Saracens make confidence hit Ospreys pay

The Ospreys were on the wrong end of a Heineken Cup hiding against crisis club Saracens, falling to a 44-3 thrashing with a solitary James Hook penalty their only points.

The European and English champions have been hit by a whopping points deduction and hefty fine this season, but director of rugby Mark McCall said after the win: "It felt much more like ourselves.

"It is the first time the group has been together for five months and there has been a really positive atmosphere around the club because these lads like each other, so it's good we were able to put something on the field."

Ospreys coach Allen Clarke said of his side: "There's undoubtedly a bit of self-belief missing among the group at the moment, based on the run we've been on."

And finally, how about this moment of Finn Russell genius

Scotland star Finn Russell scored a sensational try in his team Racing's 21-21 thriller in the other game in Pool 4.

Receiving a pass just inside the Munster 22, the fly-half shaped as if to pass out wide... but then nutmegged his onrushing opponent with a clever grubber kick before going over for the try.
