CHRISTMAS CRACKER

Celtic WAG Helen Flanagan opens new M&D’s magical Santa Christmas wonderland

24 Nov 2019, 09:47 by Aidan Smith

HELEN FLANAGAN was on hand to open M&D's magical Santa wonderland this year.

The Scots theme park has once again transformed into a winter wonderland packed with activities for the festive season.

Celtic WAG Flanagan arrived on a Christmas train as she welcomed guests to kick off the festive season.

Festive revellers can meet Santa in Scotland’s largest grotto, visit live reindeer and join storytelling sessions while watching enchanting animated Christmas scenes at the park.

Visitors can also skate under the stars on the M&D’s on Ice open air 25 meter ice rink from November 23 until January 5.

This year, Autism Friendly sessions are also being introduced for the first time allowing everyone a chance to enjoy a skate around the rink.

Anyone looking for seasonal treats can head to the Bavarian Bar for a hot mulled wine or spiced cider and grab a festive bite to eat in Santa’s Christmas Inn.

The Outdoor Festive Markets – also returning for 2019 – offer the perfect opportunity to purchase stocking fillers with an array of and craft gifts for sale.

Santa and his elves will also be making an appearance between Saturday December 21 and Tuesday December 24 at Guiseppe’s Coffee Shop, allowing families a chance to meet Santa for breakfast.

There’s also a chance to enjoy the park’s rides as part of the Festive Rides experience, where visitors can enjoy rides such as the Big Wheel and the Sleigh Ride for just £2 per ticket.

Matthew Taylor, managing director of M&D’s Theme Park, said: “Everyone at M&D’s is always very excited for Christmas and to celebrate, we’re expanding our Santa’s Magical Wonderland offering even more for 2019.

“With opportunities for the children to meet the Father Christmas himself at our grotto and a chance for adults to enjoy our Christmas markets and a skate session on our outdoor ice rink, there’s something for everyone at M&D’s this winter.

“We are also delighted to have added even more autism-friendly sessions this year so everyone can have an unforgettable festive experience.”
