Clubber floored man after being told off for taking drink outside

The incident was captured on CCTV

24 Nov 2019, 09:34 by Rod Malcolm

A clubber caused bother after "a comedy moment" when he stood outside a bar with his outstretched right arm holding a drink just inside.

Jordan Marshall, 31, came up with the trick when told it was against licensing rules to take booze out of the Atrium bar in Newark, a court heard.

When door staff pushed him out of the way, he "lashed out" and floored one man who ended up with tiny fractures around an eye socket.

Marshall admitted wounding and was ordered to pay £1,500 compensation to the doorman, who was left with numbness and "a strange feeling" when he smiles. The father-of-five said that his children were "scared to cuddle him" when the injury was at its worst.

Sitting at Nottingham Crown Court, Judge Sally Hancox told him: "People don't like being pushed but a door person is entitled to do that with your behaviour.

"You are standing there in a comedy moment with the glass inside the night club. Then you get arsey with the door staff," she added.

The incident was captured on CCTV which was played in court before sentence was passed on Marshall of Clumber Avenue, Newark. He was given a 20-month prison term, suspended for two years, and must do 100 hours community work.

Kevin Jones, prosecuting, said that the doorman is a single parent who had to take 11 weeks off work and needed surgery.

"That made it difficult for him to support his family," added Mr Jones.

Mark Nicholls, mitigating, said that Marshall had "access to £2,000" for compensation. He added: "He is working full-time as a funeral parlour sales person."
