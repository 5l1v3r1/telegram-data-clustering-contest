Man arrested after Jewish family racially abused on London Tube

24 Nov 2019, 09:03

Police have arrested a man in Birmingham after a Jewish family was racially abused on the London Underground.

The suspect, whose name and age have not been released by police, was detained on Saturday night after a video filmed on Friday showed a man reading anti-Jewish bible passages to a couple and their three children while they were travelling on the Northern Line.

The footage showed the dad trying to distract his son by talking to him while the man was ranting at them.

A witness also said the man had blamed Jews for enslaving blacks and said they were responsible for the 9/11 terror attacks.

The man, who was wearing a cap and hoody, is seen in the video threatening a commuter off-camera when he tried to intervene before mum-of-two Asma Shuweikh stepped in.

She said she saw what was happening and knew she had to confront him.

Mrs Shuweikh said: ‘I would have loved more people to come up and say something because if everyone did, I do not think it would have escalated in the way that it did.

‘Being a mother-of-two, I know what it’s like to be in that situation and I would want someone to help if I was in that situation.’

Fellow commuter Chris Atkins took the video after catching the Tube heading south on the Charing Cross branch of the line at midday on Friday.

He added: ‘The Muslim woman didn’t take any s**t from him and really, really took him to task, very firmly and persistently.’

‘In this day and age we are told how intolerant everyone is and all religions hate each other and there you had a Muslim woman sticking up for some Jewish children.’

Mr Atkins said he had later swapped seats with the boy who was being racially abused in order to protect him.

The suspect was held on suspicion of committing a racially aggravated public order offence and he remains in custody.
