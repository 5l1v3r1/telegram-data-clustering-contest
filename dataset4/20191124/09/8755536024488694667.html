I’m A Celebrity bosses ‘tighten security around the camp’ after man found dead

"It is unsettling to know that there is someone out there who has committed such a horrible crime."

24 Nov 2019, 09:59 by Sarah Magliocco

I’m A Celebrity bosses have reportedly “tightened security” around the celeb-filled camp after a man was found dead in the area.

On Friday is was reported that New South Wales police launched a manhunt after a man was found dead under suspicious circumstances.

Detective Chief Inspector Brendon Cullen of the area’s homicide squad are investigating the discovery.

He told ABC: “The movements and the history of the victim is something that we are in the early stages of establishing.”

“We are very keen to speak to any person that was in Jack Evans Boat Harbour yesterday evening and this morning who might have seen something or know anything about this.”

“We do want to establish where this man is from and how he came to be in the state he is in now.”

I’m A Celeb is filmed on a disused banana plantation in far-north eastern New South Wales.

A source claimed to The Sun: “The celebs are being watched 24 hours a day, seven days a week by a team of staff and our security measures are incredibly tight.”

“So there is zero chance of anyone sneaking into camp. But of course it is very unsettling to know that there is someone out there, running from the police, who has committed such a horrible crime.”

The area is extremely remote, and was threatened by forest fires earlier this year.
