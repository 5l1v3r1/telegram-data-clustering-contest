Psychoeducation a family therapy for addiction

24 Nov 2019, 09:30

A recent article in The Herald, “Addiction blindfolds, not only addicts but their families.” recommends 12-step programs.

Twelve-step programs are a wonderful community resource. However, they are not evidence-based practice. Families need psychoeducation. Psychoeducational programs and 12-step programs are not mutually exclusive.

From Wikipedia: “Psychoeducation offered to patients and family members teaches problem-solving and communication skills and provides education and resources in an empathetic and supportive environment. Results from more than 30 studies indicate psychoeducation improves family well-being, lowers rates of relapse and improves recovery.”

In the U.S. we have an informational website Partnership for Drug Free America. A program outside of the U.S., Family Drug Support Australia, offers local educational programs, support groups, and includes the “empathetic and supportive environment” of true psychosocial education.

I just received their manual. Our community could learn and build resources based on decades of pioneering work done in Australia. We could provide psychoeducational offerings.

These resources have the power to provide perspective, self-compassion and strength in families.

From 1966 when addiction became part of my life, until 2015, my resources included 12-step programs, the family disease model of addiction, and occasional counseling.

When I discovered the psychoeducational programs outside of the United States, my life sense of wellbeing improved dramatically.

I would never deny the reality of pain. I lost my son, Jeff, in 2009. I lost his brother, Ed, in 2017.
