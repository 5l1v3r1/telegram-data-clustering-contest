Ronnie O'Sullivan explains his iconic UK Championship celebration in 2018

24 Nov 2019, 15:20

Ronnie O’Sullivan is back to defend his UK Championship title this year after being ‘riled up by the crowd’ on his way to lifting the trophy in 2018.

The Rocket triumphed at the UK Championship for a seventh time last season and celebrated in an uncharacteristically flamboyant manner.

The 43-year-old has a very calm demeanour about him these days, but it was not the case at the Barbican in York last December after beating Mark Allen 10-6 in the final.

O’Sullivan poured a bottle of water over his own head and jumped on the barrier to hold his trophy aloft in front of the fans.

It was unusual to see the Rocket celebrate in such a way and he has explained that it was down to the crowd getting on his back, a rare occurrence for the ever-popular player.

‘I was being riled up by the crowd. Obviously, it was an important match and sometimes your emotions overspill,’ O’Sullivan told Betway.
