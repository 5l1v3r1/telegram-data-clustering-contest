How long is Frozen 2 and does it have a post-credits scene?

24 Nov 2019, 15:57

Fans of Elsa and Anna have been flocking to cinemas this weekend as Frozen 2 opens across the UK.

The sequel reunites the two sisters in the follow-up to the 2013 animated smash, with trouble brewing in the kingdom of Arendelle and the characters forced to venture into mysterious new lands to save their home.

You might well be heading out to see it with younger members of the family in the coming weeks – but just how long is the film, and what’s this we’re hearing about a post-credits scene?

Here’s what you need to know…

How long is Frozen 2?

Frozen 2 runs for one hour and 43 minutes.

That makes it six minutes shorter than its predecessor, which stopped just 11 minutes short of the two-hour mark.

So you’re getting a little less Anna and Elsa for your dosh, but with the return of favourite characters including Kristoff (Jonathan Groff) and Olaf the snowman (Josh Gad) as well as newcomers voiced by Evan Rachel Wood and Sterling K Brown, there’s still plenty to keep junior cinemagoers – and their grown-up companions – entertained.

Plus, you’re getting a whole load of new songs including Into The Unknown – which will more than make up for the lack of Let It Go this time around.

What happens in Frozen 2?

Although the plot details were kept pretty quiet ahead of release, the trailer previously revealed that Elsa and Anna are set to journey beyond Arendelle in a bid to discover the origin of Elsa’s powers, and save their kingdom.

Or, as the trailer tells us: ‘We have always feared that Elsa’s powers were too much for this world. Now, we must hope they are enough.’

Does Frozen have a post-credits scene?

Frozen 2 does indeed have a post-credits scene, so you’ll need to stick around for the entire closing credits to get your little extra bit of Frozen.

We’re not going to give away what happens, but let’s just say fans of Olaf – and in particular of one moment earlier on in the film – will be very pleased indeed.
