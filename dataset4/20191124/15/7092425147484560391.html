Westlife grant terminally ill woman's dying wish

24 Nov 2019, 15:42 by Nick Fitzgerald

The four-piece were guests on Friday night's Late Late Show

Pop band Westlife granted a terminally ill Cork woman's dying wish on Friday night's Late Late Show with a really thoughtful gesture.

Mairead Nolan, 34, was diagnosed with terminal ovarian cancer last October, as reported by the Irish Sunday Mirror.

It has been her dream to meet Shane Filan, Nicky Byrne, Mark Feehily and Kian Egan for a very, very long time.

The lads made this a reality for the woman, inviting her backstage on Friday for a meet and greet and to watch them rehearse before their live performance.

Speaking to the Irish Sunday Mirror, she said:

“I am in floods of tears after meeting them, I was just so excited. It was better than my wildest dreams and it has given me a lift and the push I need to fight the cancer.

“I found out recently that I have a spot on my liver also, so I said to the lads that I didn’t think I would make the concert in August in Pairc Ui Chaoimh.

“But Nicky said he will bring me up on stage and it gave me a great boost. I will be looking forward to that.”

Along with her Mum Cora, Mairead started a campaign online in the hopes Westlife would come across it and grant her dying wish.

Her prayers were answered when she was invited backstage and she ended spending over an hour with the lads chatting and singing.

“They came over and were hugging me, kissing me and taking selfies. It was the best day of my life,' Mairead added.

“I can die happy now, if something happens to me at least I know I have met them and they were so nice to me.”
