STALKER WARDER

Creepy Scots prison officer who stalked ex for 18 months and called her 70 times a day from work phone leaves job

24 Nov 2019, 12:27 by Ewan Mowat

AN AYRSHIRE prison officer who called his ex up to 70 times a day from work during an 18-month stalking campaign has left his job.

Creepy Joe Pattie, 23, hounded his former girlfriend Blayre Howie, also 23, after she ended their relationship.

The prison warder bombarded her with so many calls from HMP Kilmarnock that she blocked the number.

Pattie also sent barrages of texts to his ex every day - some abusing her and others begging her to get back together with him.

Blayre claims his harrassment "took over [her] life" and she called cops and prison operators Serco to put a stop to it.

According to the Sunday Mail, her official complaint sparked an internal probe and Pattie is no longer working at the Kilmarnock nick.

Blayre said: “It took over my life. I was afraid to go outside. He really thought he was above the law.

“Once I had 70 missed calls from HMP Kilmarnock – I had to block the number.

“In the end, I contacted the police because he wouldn’t leave me alone and his work didn’t seem to be able to do anything about it.”

The pair met in April 2015, but Blayre claims her ex soon became “obsessive, manipulative, controlling and a pathological liar”.

In one abusive voicemail message, Pattie said: “F***ing phone me. I’ve not f***ing messaged her. You’re f***ing leaving me here, it’s absolutely ridiculous. F***ing phone me.”

At Kilmarnock Sheriff Court in September, he admitted engaging in conduct that placed Blayre in a state of fear or alarm between July 2017 and January 2019.

He was later hit with a community payback order and ordered to complete 160 hours of unpaid work.

Pattie was also give a two-year non-harassment order.

A Serco spokesperson said: “This individual no longer works at HMP Kilmarnock.”
