Grey's Anatomy is getting 'sexier' as it moves back to post-watershed slot

24 Nov 2019, 12:54

Grey’s Anatomy showrunner Krista Vernoff is going to bring the show back to its ‘sexy’ roots.

The medical drama is returning for its 16th season in 2020, and according to Vernoff it will come back with a bang. So to speak.

Grey’s was previously shifted forward to 8pm to make space for other shows but in another schedule switch up it will go back to its original 9pm slot.

Vernoff said that the ‘different rules’ for a post-watershed programme will definitely apply to the new season, and fans are going to get the racier content of earlier seasons.

She told Deadline: ‘There are different rules for a 9 PM show than there are for an 8 PM show, and we hope to take advantage of those rules.

‘Grey’s was definitely allowed to be a sexier show when it was on at 9 o’clock. So we are excited by the change back to our original (Thursday) time slot.’

She also spoke about the potential for a crossover with new 8pm drama Station 19, about a group of ridiculously good looking firefighters.

‘With Station 19 airing at 8 o’clock and Grey’s Anatomy airing at 9 o’clock there are very organic opportunities for Grey’s Anatomy to, for example, inherit patients we see rescued on Station 19,’ she added.

She added that the two shows being next to each other is ‘really exciting and opens up the storytelling’.

Grey’s Anatomy has been running for an impressive 14 years now, and luckily for fans it’s not going any where.

ABC Entertainment Karey Burke recently said the network has no plans to cancel it.

Speaking at the Television Critics Association summer press tour, Burke explained: ‘We love them. They are as deep a part of the fabric of ABC as anyone and certainly it will be up to them whether or not they want to continue the show.

‘I hope I’m watching with my grandchildren.’

Grey’s Anatomy is available to watch on Amazon Prime Video.
