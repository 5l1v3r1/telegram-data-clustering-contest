Yashwant-led team barred from moving out of Srinagar

24 Nov 2019, 12:48

Srinagar: The five-member group led by former BJP leader Yashwant Sinha visiting Kashmir was on Saturday prevented by Jammu & Kashmir authorities from travelling outside Srinagar citing a terrorist threat in the areas they intended to visit. Former Foreign Minister Sinha had spoken to National Conference leader Farooq Abdullah over the phone on Friday.

Former Chief Ministers Farooq Abdullah, Omar Abdullah, and Mehbooba Mufti are among the 50-odd politicians who have been detained by the J&K administration after the Centre’s move to revoke Article 370 on August 5. Sinha told that the phone call to the NC leader was facilitated by a friend. He said they had planned to travel to Pulwama and Shopian in south Kashmir but authorities restricted their movement citing terrorist threat in south Kashmir. “We had sent a written communication to deputy commissioner asking for permission to visit Farooq Abdullah, Omar Abdullah, Mehbooba Mufti and Yousuf Tarigami,” Sinha told. “We have been told that permission has not been granted.” “This is to stop us from going to these places and seeing the situation for ourselves,” he said. (IANS)
