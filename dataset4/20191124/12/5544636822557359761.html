IGP Adamu Redeploys Imohimi To Akwa Ibom

24 Nov 2019, 12:07 by Olugbenga Ige

The Inspector-General of Police, Mohammed Adamu, has transferred the new Ogun State Commissioner of Police, Edgal Imohimi to Akwa Ibom state.

Also, the Akwa Ibom police commissioner, Kenneth Ebrimson, is to assume duty in Ogun to replace Imohimi.

This was contained in a police signal from the office of the Nigeria Police Force, on behalf of the Inspector General dated November 22.

According to the signal, the posting as ordered by the Inspector General of Police is with immediate effect.

Concise News reports that on November 15 that Imohimi and Ebrimson had been posted to Ogun and Akwa Ibom respectively.

But in a dramatic twist, the duo were swapped, less down a week after they resumed duty in Ogun and Akwa Ibom.

Recall that Imohimi, less than 24 hours after he took charge of Ogun police command, ordered the officers in the state to resume to their original duty posts for a headcount.

The CP also set up a complaint call centre with 10 hotlines through which the public can report any officer demanding money from them under any guise.
