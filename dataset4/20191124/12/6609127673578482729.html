COVER-UP

NHS whistleblower claims to have identified 10 new dirty water scandal cases following Milly Main death

24 Nov 2019, 12:10 by Aidan Smith

AN NHS whistleblower has reportedly revealed 10 new dirty water scandal cases after the death of Milly Main.

Heartbroken mum Kimberly Darroch claims the NHS covered up her cancer-stricken daughter’s death after she contracted a dirty water infection at a Glasgow hospital.

She says she is “100 per cent” sure the dirty water infection was what caused her daughter’s tragic death.

And now the Daily Record claim seriously ill youngsters at the Queen Elizabeth University Hospital have been falling victim to dirty water bugs since 2016.

They report that the insider has approached Glasgow MSP Anas Sarwar and told how a 2017 inquiry found another 26 cases.

Stenotrophomonas infection was considered as the cause of death for one of the children.

Now it is claimed that 10 more cases of the same kind have been found in 2016.

Sarwar said: “These latest revelations from an NHS whistleblower show that the problems at this hospital have been going on for years.

"That begs the question why it has taken so long for action to be taken.

“If cases were identified as early as 2016, why wasn’t that addressed then so infections in 2017 may have been avoided?

“The priority now must be telling the truth to all 36 families affected in 2016 and 2017. That’s the very least they deserve following this scandal.”

NHS Greater Glasgow and Clyde have been contacted for comment by The Scottish Sun.
