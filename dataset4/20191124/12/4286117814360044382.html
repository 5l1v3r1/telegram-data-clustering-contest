No question of alliance with BJP, Ajit's statement misleading: Sharad Pawar

Sharad Pawar also said Ajit Pawar's statement was "false and misleading" to create confusion among the people of Maharashtra. The NCP chief reiterated that NCP has unanimously decided to ally with Shiv Sena and the Congress to form the government in Maharashtra

24 Nov 2019, 12:42

Moments after Maharashtra Deputy Chief Minister Ajit Pawar justified his actions of joining hands with the Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP), his uncle and Nationalist Congress Party (NCP) chief Sharad Pawar said there was no question of forming an alliance with the saffron party.

The NCP chief also said Ajit Pawar's statement was "false and misleading" to create confusion among the people of Maharashtra. Sharad Pawar reiterated that NCP has unanimously decided to ally with Shiv Sena and the Congress to form the government in Maharashtra

Sharad Pawar's statement has come just after Ajit Pawar, in his first statement after joining hands with the BJP and taking deputy chief minister's oath, said NCP's alliance with the saffron party would provide a stable government in Maharashtra for next five years.

Reacting to Ajit Pawar's statement on Twitter, Sharad Pawar said, "There is no question of forming an alliance with @BJP4Maharashtra."

NCP has unanimously decided to ally with @ShivSena & @INCMaharashtra to form the government. Shri Ajit Pawar's statement is false and misleading in order to create confusion and false perception among the people.
