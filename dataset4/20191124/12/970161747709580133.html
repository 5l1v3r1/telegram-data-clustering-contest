Eric Dier 'very happy' that Jose Mourinho has replaced Mauricio Pochettino

24 Nov 2019, 12:33

Tottenham midfielder Eric Dier says he is delighted to be working under Jose Mourinho after the Portuguese coach replaced Mauricio Pochettino in charge of the north London club.

Spurs had made a worrying start to the season, sitting in 14th place – 11 points adrift of the top four – when Pochettino was dismissed on Tuesday evening, with Mourinho swiftly announced as his successor the following morning.

Mourinho had just three training sessions before making his bow as Tottenham manager on Saturday, securing a 3-2 victory away to West Ham – their first away win in the Premier League since January.

One of his most notable changes was moving Dier back into the midfield to sit and protect the defence and the side gave an assured display for the first hour before conceding twice late on.

While Dier was full of praise for Pochettino – who gave him his debut and moulded him into a midfielder – if someone had to replace the Argentine he’s glad it was three-time Premier League title winner Mourinho.

‘I’m very happy that it’s him [Mourinho]. Obviously with my connection to Portugal when I was younger,’ Dier, who came through the academy at Sporting Lisbon, told ESPN after beating West Ham.

‘I grew up watching him coach great teams and winning the biggest titles in football. For me, it’s very special to be able to play for him.

‘We’re very lucky to have played for Pochettino and now for Mourinho. I feel very lucky to be able to have played for these two managers.’
