Brescia shrug off 'joke' Mario Balotelli racial insult after Massimo Cellino comments

Massimo Cellino racially insulted Mario Balotelli but Brescia have now come out and defended their chairman, insisting he was making a joke

25 Nov 2019, 20:24 by Darren Lewis, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/darren-lewis/

Mario Balotelli has been racially insulted by his own chairman at Italian club Brescia.

Asked why Balotelli had been dropped, ex-Leeds United owner Massimo Cellino said: "What can I say? That he's black and he's working to whiten himself but he has great difficulties in this.”

The slur is the latest in a string of insults aimed at the former Manchester City and Liverpool striker during his time in Italy

Italian Balotelli was racially abused by Verona supporters earlier this month but was persuaded to stay on the pitch after threatening to walk off.

Brescia boss Fabio Grosso left Balotelli out of Sunday's squad for the 3-0 defeat at Roma and challenged him to show more "determination and enthusiasm" to regain his place.

Responding to Cellino's comments regarding Balotelli's situation on Monday, the Serie A club issued a statement defending their position.

It read: "In relation to what president Massimo Cellino declared this afternoon about our player Mario Balotelli, Brescia Calcio explains that it was clearly a joke said as a paradox.

"It was clearly misunderstood and it was said in the attempt to downplay an excessive media exposure and aimed at protecting the player."

Brescia are bottom of Serie A, four points from safety after two wins from their opening 12 matches.

Balotelli has scored two goals in seven appearances since joining his hometown club in August.

The Palermo-born marksman has suffered racist abuse in Italy playing for Inter Milan and AC Milan over the past ten years.

In 2012 the Italian daily newspaper Gazzetta dello Sport were forced to apologise after publishing a cartoon depicting Balotelli, then playing for Italy at the European Championships, as King Kong on top of Big Ben.

The image, on the day of Italy’s game against England, led to a number of complaints from readers.

Earlier this season Balotelli was dismissed as “arrogant” by Brescia's ultras group following his reaction to racist abuse from Verona supporters.

Verona were handed a one-match partial stadium closure and the club banned one of their ultras, Luca Castellini, for more than 10 years after he said Balotelli could "never be completely Italian”.
