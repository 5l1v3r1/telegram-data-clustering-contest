'Nobody helped her' - Dublin model Monica Walsh pledges to end taboo around domestic abuse after tragic death of her aunt

Today is the UN Day for the Elimination of Violence Against Women

25 Nov 2019, 20:47 by Kayla Walsh

A Dublin model has devoted herself to campaigning on behalf of victims of domestic abuse after the tragic death of her aunt.

Monica Walsh, who has represented Ireland in international beauty pageants Miss World and Miss Universe, said domestic violence is still a “taboo subject”. 

“I was just 13 or 14 when my aunt and godmother passed away,” she told RSVP Live. 

“I remember her mother knocking on the door and telling us she had died - I asked what happened and nobody wanted to talk about it. 

“I only found out years later that she had been a victim of domestic violence. She was only 36, she had two kids.”

She said that after she found out what happened to her aunt, she realised she hadn’t picked up on the signs she was being abused. 

“Everything came back to me,” she said. 

“I remember her wearing sunglasses to hide a bruise on her eye. 

“Nobody helped her. If she or someone else had acted sooner she could still be alive and safe.”

Monica, who is originally from Poland, learnt more about domestic violence when she was competing in Miss Universe.

“I was representing Ireland in Belarus and each of the contestants had to prepare a speech about domestic violence,” she said.

“I started researching the topic, I went to a conference and heard stories from all around the world. 

“I realised this is a huge problem globally.” 

The mum of one is now writing a book called Get Me Out Of This Hell, comprising stories from three different survivors of domestic violence.

“They don’t want to talk about it with their friends, they want to give out the impression that they have a perfect happy family,” she went on. 

“It’s especially hard for migrants who don’t have their family and friends around them for support.

“I met up with one woman in Dublin, she was so afraid of her husband that we had to meet in her car.

“If I can save even one life, then my work will be worth it.” 

She added: “If you’re a victim of abuse, remember that it’s not your fault. 

“You deserve to feel safe in your own home.

“Reach out for help - whether it’s Women’s Aid or another organisation. You are not alone."
