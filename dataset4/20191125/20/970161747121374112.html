Ice hockey player gets struck by puck, pulls 9 teeth out but keeps on playing

25 Nov 2019, 20:43

A veteran ice hockey player was whacked in the face by a puck and pulled nine of his own teeth out – but kept on playing.

Florida Panthers defenseman Keith Yandle, 33, was filmed yanking his ruined teeth from his bloodied mouth during the game against the Carolina Hurricanes at the PNC Arena in Raleigh, North Carolina, on Saturday.

He underwent some quick dental work afterwards, according to MailOnline, before heading back out onto the rink the next day to face the Buffalo Sabres.

It is unclear whether Yandle’s lost teeth were his own, or whether he’d had veneers installed after a previous encounter with a puck.

Sadly, Yandle’s injury was in vain, with the Panthers losing against both the Hurricanes and Sabres.
