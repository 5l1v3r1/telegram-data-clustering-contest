Lucy McHugh’s mum says ‘blood turned to ice’ after daughter was raped and killed

Stacey White, 35, has described how her heart "almost stopped" when she discovered Stephen Nicholson - the man who she had invited into the family home - had been arrested over daughter Lucy's death

25 Nov 2019, 20:29 by Amber Hicks, https://www.facebook.com/dailymirror/

The mother of Lucy McHugh has told how her ‘blood turned to ice’ when she discovered her daughter had been raped and killed by their lodger.

The 13-year-old's body was discovered in woodland with 27 stab wounds near Southampton Outdoor Sports Centre last July.

Lucy had been lured there by "evil, narcissistic monster" Stephen Nicholson, 25, who killed the teen to prevent her revealing he had been sexually abusing her.

The paedophile tattoo artist had been grooming Lucy since she was 12.

"Depraved" Nicholson, who was jailed for life with a minimum sentence of 33 years, had been lodging in the family's house and was the 'best friend' of Lucy's stepfather.

More than a year on from the horrific ordeal, Lucy's mum Stacey White, 31, has laid bare the everlasting impact it has had.

She has recalled how the last conversation she had with her daughter was saying goodbye as she left for work.

Lucy replied: "OK mum, see you when you get back".

Stacey told the Sun Online: "I never could have imagined that would be the very last time I heard Lucy's voice, or saw my beautiful daughter alive."

She added: "As the hours ticked by, I began to worry. I hadn't heard from Lucy, which was unlike her, and when I messaged her friend [who she was supposed to be spending the day with], she said she hadn't seen her."

Stacey frantically began sending messages asking if anyone knew of Lucy's whereabouts before contacting police.

"Every single hour that ticked by was torture. I prayed for Lucy to walk through the door. But there was nothing," the mum added.

Nicholson claimed he hadn't seen Lucy since the previous week and messaged Stacey: "I hope they find her safe. Police are good at their job and they will find her soon, keep your chin up and stay positive."

Shortly afterwards, Lucy's body was discovered.

"I felt my blood turn to ice inside my veins," said Stacey.

"The room began to spin, and I had the sudden urge to vomit. I dashed from the room, feeling like my whole world was about to implode. I couldn’t breathe."

Stacey's "world came tumbling down" and her heart "almost stopped" when she discovered Nicholson - the man who they had invited into the family home - had been arrested over Lucy's death.

Nicholson had been taken in by Stacey and her partner Richard Elmes in the spring of 2017.

Having moved out of Lucy's home days before the killing, he launched the 'controlled and repetitive' attack to silence the teen, who incorrectly told him she was pregnant.

Jurors heard excerpts from Lucy's diary entries and notes dating back to May 2017 in which she wrote that Nicholson took her virginity and described his penis piercing.

In one note titled 'abuse', Lucy wrote that he would 'make me... rape me anyway', before detailing how she was forced to lock herself in the bathroom to avoid sex.

Other entries described how Nicholson choked her during sex.

He denied killing Lucy - but a jury saw through his web of lies.
