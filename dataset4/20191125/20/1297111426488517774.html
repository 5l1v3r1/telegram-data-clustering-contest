University of Regina Cougars swimmers excel at Canada West championships

The University of Regina Cougars won seven medals at the Canada West swimming championships on the weekend.

25 Nov 2019, 20:15 by Regina Leader-Post

The University of Regina Cougars set a team record by winning seven individual medals at the Canada West swimming championships, which concluded Sunday in Lethbridge.

That is the highest total since the program’s inception 22 years ago.

Etienne Paquin-Foisy won a gold medal in the men’s 200-metre butterfly and added a bronze medal in the 100m butterfly. The third-year Cougars swimmer has now won six conference medals.

Brian Palaschuk added a silver medal (men’s 200m individual medley) and finished third in the 400m individual medley and 200m freestyle.

Bree Crookshanks won bronze medal in the women’s 100m breaststroke (with a time of one minute 9.28 seconds) and 200m breaststroke (2:28.56). She set U of R and provincial time standards in both events.

Paquin-Foisy, Palaschuk and Crookshanks will compete in the U Sports championships, Feb. 20-22 in Victoria.

Taylor Kreutzer had four points to help the Extreme Hockey Regina Capitals defeat the West Central Rage 8-2 in Prairie Junior Hockey League action Sunday in Kerrobert.

Kreutzer and Matt Kesslering each had two goals for the Capitals, who got singles from Fraiser Belanger, Cale Tuer-Treble, Dawsen Parker and Kyle Colwell.

Avery Cholin and Ethan Osterhold replied for West Central, which was outshot 51-21.

The Capitals lead the Bill Johnston Division with a 16-1-0-0 record.

Also on Sunday, Alex Hicks scored in overtime to give the Assiniboia Southern Rebels a 5-4 victory over the host Prince Albert Titans.

Riley Skarbon had a goal and two assists for Assiniboia.

Megan Hayhurst had two goals and three assists for the Regina Rebels in their 10-2 Saskatchewan Female Midget AAA Hockey League victory over the host Weyburn Richardson Pioneer Gold Wings on Sunday.

Tavia Terry and Neena Brick also scored two goals for the Rebels, who got singles from Kaylee Dyer, Alexis Petford, Molly Flynn and Allee Gerrard. Emma DeCorby added three assists.

Sydney Keddle and Lauren Demmans replied for Weyburn, which was outshot 47-25.
