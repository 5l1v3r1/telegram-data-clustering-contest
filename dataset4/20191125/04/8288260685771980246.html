COWLEY ‘RESTS EASY’

 

Danny Cowley could not hide his disappointment after his Huddersfield Town side let slip their lead against Birmingham City on Saturday.

Blues went 1-0 down at the John Smith’s stadium but hit back with 12 minutes left when Lukas Jutkiewicz deflected home to seal a 1-1 draw.

Pep Clotet’s men are still searching to add to their two away wins this season, but they did end their four-game losing streak on the road with a point at the John Smith’s Stadium.

Frazier Campbell had put Huddersfield ahead, but Cowley could “rest easy” after seeing how his players performed.

CONNAL TRUEMAN VERDICT

Fifteen months ago the door to Birmingham City’s goal opened for Connal Trueman as Garry Monk gave the talented shot stopper his senior debut.

Within a couple of games - and an ordeal by set-piece by Tony Pulis’ Middlesbrough, that door closed as Monk brought Lee Camp to the club, a brave move by Monk, one for a more experienced player who in some ways had as much to prove as his younger rival.

Trueman remained behind that door - and Camp - for the next 44 Championship games, a young goalkeeper sitting on the bench, watching, learning - but most of all remaining ready for even the slightest chink of light. None came.

He could have been forgiven for wondering if it would ever appear, particularly when in the summer Monk left and David Stockdale’s slate was wiped clean by Pep Clotet. As 2018/2019 clicked into 2019/2020 and a new name was pencilled on to the manager’s/head coach’s office, it looked as though Trueman had slipped down the pecking order, from second to third choice.

Camp started the current campaign, Stockdale backed him up - and played in the final pre-season warm-up match as well as the Carabao Cup tie at Portsmouth, leaving Trueman scraping around for any kind of game-time.

With a pair of veteran obstacles in his way it seemed obvious the 23-year-old should have gone out on loan instead of risking a wasted season, knocking on the door that would never budge and caught betwixt the first team and development squad.

Yet in the last month Trueman was gone from third in line to first and on Saturday stepped through that door and into Blues’ starting line-up for just the third appearance of his career.

MONK’S SELECTION HEADACHE

Sheffield Wednesday look set to be without one of their key players when they face Birmingham City this week.

Blues take on the Owls at Hillsborough on Wednesday night in the Sky Bet Championship, and do so on the back of a draw.

Pep Clotet’s side were held to a 1-1 stalemate with Huddersfield Town on Saturday, which leaves Blues in 15th place in the Championship table.

Wednesday have been dealt a blow for the clash against Blues, with defender Liam Palmer set to miss the match due to suspension.

Palmer received a straight red card in Wednesday’s 2-1 defeat to West Bromwich Albion at the weekend, and is set to serve a three-match ban.

The Scotland international was given his marching orders after a challenge on West Ham loanee Grady Dianagana in the closing stages of the match at The Hawthorns.
