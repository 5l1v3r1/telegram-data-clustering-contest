Cedric linked

Wolverhampton Wanderers are interested in signing Southampton defender Cedric Soares, according to reports.

The 28-year-old has made only five Premier League appearances for the Saints this season and is expected to be available when the transfer window re-opens in January.

Cedric is out of contract at the end of this campaign and has already informed the club of his desire to move on.

According to TeamTalk, Southampton are willing to cash in to avoid losing him for nothing and Wolves have made an enquiry over the possibility of doing a deal.

Moutinho praise

Former Liverpool midfielder Danny Murphy has sung the praises of Joao Moutinho after his stunning goal set Wolverhampton Wanderers on their way to victory over Bournemouth.

The Portuguese star - who followed up the Vitality Stadium triumph by signing a new contract with Wolves - opened the scoring on Saturday by curling home a superb free-kick.

National media view

Wolverhampton Wanderers climbed to fifth in the Premier League table after Saturday's 2-1 victory at Bournemouth.

Moutinho's gorgeous free-kick got Wolves on the front foot at the Vitality Stadium, before Raul Jimenez notched his 14th goal of an eye-catching campaign.

Simon Francis was also sent off before the half-time break, and a 57th-minute header from Steve Cook was not enough to salvage anything for the 10-man Cherries.

Nuno's men have now won four of their last seven Premier League games - and they have not lost since September 14.

That form, the performance against Bournemouth and the individual display of Moutinho have all come in for praise from the national media.

Merson’s silly comments

Months after labelling Adama Traore ‘cringeworthy’, Paul Merson has put his foot in his mouth once again, writes Ben Husband.

Speaking ahead of the game he suggested that Wolves were lucky to have Nuno and that he could leave for a ‘top six’ side.

“I worried about these at the start of the season,” he said, “Because last year, they beat the good teams and I thought, if they don’t start beating the bad ones, they aren’t going to keep beating the good ones.”

“I like the manager, I think they are fortunate to have this manager. I don’t mean that disrespectfully but I think he could take one of the top six jobs. I’d take him at Arsenal tomorrow morning.”

Well ‘Merse’ after more than a dozen games, guess what? Wolves are a top six side. They were arguably one of the best six teams in the division last season and this time around there is an even stronger argument.

They say teams reflect their manager. And like Nuno, Wolves are ruthlessly aggressive and focused. So in a way, Merson is right. He may be managing a top six club. But it may be the one he's already at.
