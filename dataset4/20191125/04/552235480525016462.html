Police Scotland rise to Modern day slavery challenge with new squad

Modern day slavery is a vile trade taking place across Scotland and police need all the support they can get to fight it.

25 Nov 2019, 04:30 by Record View, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/record-view/

Modern day slavery is right here, right now in Scotland.

In recent weeks a dozen Vietnamese youngsters have presented themselves to the authorities having believed to have been trafficked across continents to be set to be sent to work in cannabis farms, nail bars and car washes in our towns and cities.

Police are also investigating concerns some were destined for the sex industry and escaped before being farmed out to flats operating across Scotland.

This vile trade is taking place all across the country and in response a new squad of police officers has been set up to deal with what will be a complex, challenging crime.

The victims are young people who speak little to no English and who are understandably scared and frightened to talk.

The police need all the sensitivity and support that they can get in trying to fight this scourge that is hidden in plain sight in our daily lives.

Anyone with any information or concerns about the welfare of someone they suspect of being trafficked should contact the police and help stop slavery.

Back rape victims

Shockingly, more than 2,000 rapes and attempted rapes were reported to police in Scotland over the last year.

 In one sense the rise is welcome because it shows that more people are coming forward to make complaints about serious sexual assaults.

But that also creates its own crisis as the support network to help people, overwhelmingly women, is not there to match the increase in reported rapes.

The Rape Crisis crowdfunding campaign, £16 for 16 days, calls on the public to stand against rape and sexual violence by donating or raising a minimum of £16 pounds through challenges, sponsorship, or workplace and community events.

Hundreds of women are being supported by Rape Crisis centres across Scotland, but hundreds more need help.

There could hardly be a more worthwhile cause to consider this Christmas.

Marc's a hero too

Inspired by his own sporting heroes Marc Fairbairn has found the courage to speak out about the debilitating condition brought on by motor neurone disease.

The 29 year old has lived with the illness for a decade, and although now confined to a wheelchair, he finds his daughter gives him the strength to keep fighting.

The young man is a hero himself.
