Nightingale housing project goes to planning committee

25 Nov 2019, 18:00 by Jolyon Attwooll

One of Ballarat's most talked about building projects goes before council's planning committee this week, with 11 objections to the project.

The Nightingale apartments - proposed by a group known for an emphasis on environmental sustainability - are proposed for 11 Davey Street in Ballarat Central.

If the plans go ahead, it would involve the demolition of an old warehouse, and the construction of a four-storey apartment block with 27 separate residences, including 17 two-bedroom dwellings, six one-bedroom flats and four three-bedroom dwellings. There would also be 14 car parking spaces proposed (a reduction of 24 on standard planning scheme requirements), as well as 54 bicycle spaces.

The proposals would follow the same, non-profit model as developments in Melbourne, with two built in recent years in Brunswick in the inner north. Apartments are sold at cost, and measures worked in to help the building be environmentally sustainable. Designed by Breathe Architecture, the Nightingale building projects do not employ real estate agents or build display suites.

If the apartments are built, 20 per cent would be set aside as affordable housing, and another 20 per cent reserved for 'key community contributors' such as nurses, teachers or those in creative industries.

Under the proposals, there would be basement parking for a reduced level of cars (14 in total), a solar energy facility on the roof, and a licensed food and drink premises limited to around 45 patrons.

As the first of its kind in regional Victoria, the buzz around the project has been unusual - with one council director describing it as "manna from heaven".

However, it has not been universally welcomed. With 11 objections to the application, council officers have recommended its approval subject to amended plans being approved.
