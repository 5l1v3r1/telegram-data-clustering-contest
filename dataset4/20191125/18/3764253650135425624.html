Kogi election: Dino Melaye dares APC to sue him over alleged vote-buying

25 Nov 2019, 18:45

- A former lawmaker, Dino Melaye, is accused by Mohammed Onogwu, an aide to the Kogi state governor, of buying votes

- Melaye, in turn, accuses the APC and Governor Yahaya Bello of being responsible for the election violence in Kogi state

- However, Onogwu dismisses the allegation linking Governor Bello, who was the APC governorship candidate in Kogi to the violence during elections in the state

A former lawmaker, Dino Melaye, has challenged the All Progressives Congress (ACP) to sue him over alleged vote-buying that reportedly occurred in the Kogi west senatorial election.

Melaye, who is the candidate of the Peoples Democratic Party (PDP) in the Kogi west senatorial election issued the challenge when he was accused of vote-buying on Sunday, November 24, by Mohammed Onogwu, an aide to the Kogi state governor on Channels TV.

“We have clips evidence where he (Melaye) was distributing money (voters) on the line where they were voting,” Onogwu said.

But Melaye who interrupted in laughter asked the APC to sue him.

“What are you waiting for? Sue me,” he said.

Melaye, in turn, made a counter allegation that APC was responsible for the violence which he said claimed 16 lives.

The former lawmaker said the PDP has evidence to prove that Governor Yahaya Bello and the APC were behind election violence in Kogi state

However, Onogwu dismissed allegation linking Governor Bello, who was the APC governorship candidate, to the violence in Lokoja during the elections.

The governor’s aide said Bello was at Agasa in Okene local government area with his convoy where he cast his vote and ensured that the exercise was peaceful.

Meanwhile, the Kogi state government has accused the PDP of trying to rubbish the victory of the All Progressives Congress (APC) in the November 16 governorship election in the state.

Kingsly Fanwo, the director-general media and publicity to Kogi state governor who made the allegation in a press statement on Sunday, November 24, said the attempt by the PDP to rubbish their victory will definitely fail.

The aide alleged that the PDP, through its propaganda wing, was attempting rubbish the well-deserved, hard-earned victory of the APC in the Kogi election.
