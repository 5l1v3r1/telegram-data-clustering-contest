'The most successful accident': Adults-only Agro hits road for birthday

25 Nov 2019, 18:00 by Toby Crockford

From interviewing former prime minister Bob Hawke and 1980s heart-throb Jason Donovan, to advertising recycled toilet paper and cracking crude jokes at the expense of his co-hosts, Agro has enjoyed a colourful life during the past 30 years.

Even though he admits it might kill him, puppeteer Jamie Dunn is taking Agro on a Queensland tour to celebrate the television icon's birthday.

Dunn admits he never believed Agro - "the most successful accident around" - would become the national sensation he did.

"I think it’s his eyebrows – the heritage he got from Mal Meninga. It’s just one big eyebrow that comes down like a roller door," he said.

"In all seriousness, Agro had something different, he went through the kids, to adults and to grandparents.

"When we did shopping centres, we did Westfield Strathpine [about 45 minutes north of Brisbane] and the parents and grandparents stayed with the kids for the show.

"We only went there once because nobody was shopping, everyone was watching the show."

When it comes to naming some of his favourite moments, Dunn has no hesitation.

"You know you're massive when the prime minister wants to talk one-on-one," he said.

"Agro was in an armchair and Bob Hawke was there - some would say there were two puppets in that one scene.

"Then there was the statue of Wally Lewis ... and any guest on Agro's Cartoon Connection had to turn towards Wally and do the salute. Agro would not speak to them until that happened.

"My favourite commercial was for recycled toilet tissue. I had to put my arm up through a dunny because the door opens and there's Agro sitting on the old dunny reading the paper. I just loved it."

The character has sprung back into popular culture after an outtakes video attracted more than 4 million views online and ice-cream chain Wendy’s relaunched the official Agro Cone for his 30th birthday.

Big Brother Australia star Ben Zabel, better known as Ben from Brisbane, will join the tour during audience's adults-only journey back in time.

Dunn was confident Agro's loyal audience from the '90s would buy tickets, and if sales did not go well, he would simply blame his new sidekick.

The Agro puppet to be used on the tour is, in fact, Agro II after the original became very dirty and a replica was made with no custard on its cheeks.

"The old one is in the bottom drawer of my dresser. When I die, probably just after these shows - and I use the term 'show' very loosely - he will be on eBay or hopefully the TV Hall of Fame," Dunn said.

The show promises classic Agro outrageousness, with Zabel providing an "entirely new canvas".

"It will be classic Agro while working with Ben, he is a wonderful man and I’m looking forward to working with someone new," Dunn said.

"He’ll be tense ... but if for any reason I felt he didn’t have it, I wouldn’t be there – actually I would still be there for the money."

However, there is one thing missing from the show that Mr Dunn would have liked.

"I would love to have a guest spot on the night with Israel Folau and get stuck into that young man. According to Israel, I may have started the bushfires," Mr Dunn said.

Agro Up Late with Jamie Dunn kicks off the statewide tour at the Brisbane Comedy Festival from March 17-22, 2020, with tickets on sale at Brisbane Powerhouse this Thursday.
