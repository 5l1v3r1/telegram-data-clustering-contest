Hunt for champagne-coloured BMW possibly linked to targeted Hallam shooting

25 Nov 2019, 02:47

A victim who survived being shot in the stomach three times at home in Hallam now fears for his life, as police hunt for a champagne-coloured luxury car possibly linked to the violent attack.

Police believe the October 6 shooting was targeted but the victim has been unable to shed any light as to why he was hit.

Armed Crime detectives have been told a group of men attended a Nettle Drive home in Hallam about 6.10pm and demanded entry to the property.

An altercation took place between the group and the 54-year-old victim and his 34-year-old female partner.

The Dandenong man was seriously injured after he was shot in the stomach at least three times and was rushed to hospital by his partner.

Detective Superintendent Peter Brigham said the victim had sustained “quite serious injuries” and he had “narrowly avoided being killed”.

“A number of shots were fired at the address,” Det Supt Brigham said. “We believe this was a targeted attack. He has concerns for his safety.

“We are very concerned about this, it has happened on a Sunday evening at 6pm.

“We have a large team of detectives investigating this matter.”

Police said they were unsure what the altercation was about, but the victim was co-operating with police.

The victim is said to be unsure of the specifics as to why he was shot.

“He has had extensive surgery, he will probably have ongoing health issues as a consequence of the injuries,” Det Supt Brigham said.

The car is not believed to be stolen.

Police said they hadn’t identified any links to outlawed motorcycle gangs in their investigation but were unable to eliminate that possibility.

CCTV from the area has led investigators to believe a champagne-coloured BMW sedan may be linked to the shooting.

The car has distinctive Euro-style V number plates and is likely to be a 2004 to 2007 model.

Anyone with information about the BMW including its owner and current whereabouts is urged to contact Crime Stoppers on 1800 333 000.
