Steve Bruce shelves plans to release an autobiography in order to concentrate on Newcastle job

Bruce had planned to release his autobiography Steve Bruce: Theatre of Dreams - playing, managing and everything in between in October

25 Nov 2019, 11:34 by Sean McCormick

Newcastle United manager Steve Bruce has shelved plans to release his autobiography in order to concentrate on his role as Magpies boss.

Bruce had planned to release his autobiography Steve Bruce: Theatre of Dreams - playing, managing and everything in between in October, but the release date has been pushed back so he can concentrate on his role at St James' Park.

The book is still available to pre-order on several bookselling sites including Amazon, WHSmith and Waterstones but its publication has been postponed indefinitely.

The Athletic report that the former Sheffield Wednesday, Aston Villa, Hull, Sunderland, Wigan and Birmingham boss put the book on hold last year as he was unable to dedicate his full attention to it in the aftermath of the death of his parents.

“I’ve put it on hold,” Bruce said.

“I just don’t have time any more. I’ve done a few little things for it, but that’s on the back-burner now.

"It was obviously very difficult to find any time when I was coming up and down the motorway to see my mam and dad and it wouldn’t be right to do it now. What happens at Newcastle might be worth a chapter or two anyway!”
