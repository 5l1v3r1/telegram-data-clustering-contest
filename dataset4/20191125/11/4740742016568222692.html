Watch the heartwarming moment former Bristol City, Bristol Rovers and Nottingham Forest defender's son gives team talk following suspected heart attack

He was hospitalised on Thursday

25 Nov 2019, 11:48 by David Byrom

There were plenty of well-wishers for Christian Edwards last week following the former defender's suspected heart attack.

Edwards has close links to Bristol Rovers, having played 99 league games for the Gas between 2003 and 2006, but he also previously represented Bristol City on loan from Nottingham Forest.

It meant fans from both Rovers and the Robins came together last week to send their love to the 43-year-old, who was hospitalised following a suspected heart attack.

Edwards manages Cardiff Met University and was taking a training session when he suffered the attack.

He has been linked with the university side since joining their teaching staff in 2010 and has helped lead the side to win promotion through four different levels of the Welsh football system to their current spot in the Premier Division.

They became the first British men's university side to compete in Europe this season when they reached the preliminary round of the Europa League.

Despite Edwards' hospitalisation, the side were in action on Saturday, taking on Cefn Druids.

After their 2-1 win helped leapfrog their opponents into sixth place, they were given a very special team talk from Edwards' son Isaac.
