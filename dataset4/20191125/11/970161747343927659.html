Naomi Scott owns red carpet alongside Olivia Colman and Hayley Atwell

25 Nov 2019, 11:30

Aladdin and Charlies Angels actress Naomi Scott owned the red carpet alongside the likes of Olivia Colman and Hayley Atwell at the 65th Evening Standard Theatre Awards.

Held at The London Coliseum on Sunday evening, the Disney star was every inch Jasmine as she sported a bedazzling burgundy crop and matching skirt.

The 26-year-old, fresh from the promotional trail of her latest flick in which she’s taking the helm as a tough-as-rocks crime fighter alongside Kristen Stewart and Ella Ballinska (who was also at the event), was a ceremonial goddess last night.

Matching her dress to her makeup, she added a fierce smattering of red eyeshadow to amp up the drama.

It’s theatre after all, darling.

Her appearance on the red carpet comes after she recently spoke about her ethnicity and how she found it hard making a break in the industry.

The star, who is of British and Indian descent, said to Teen Vogue: ‘There’s a thing of someone [being] like… “she’s not white, she’s not Black, she’s not Latina, what is she?”

‘There were definitely a few leads that I went for where I think, ultimately, I was maybe the other choice, the “exotic” choice, or the “other.”‘

Still, she shook off the recent controversy that is the box office takings of Charlie’s Angels to get her glad rags on alongside husband, footballer Jordan Spence.

She was joined by Avengers star Hayley Atwell, who worked a sheer, tulle gown for photographers, as well as model Jourdan Dunn, who brought the razzamatazz in a sequinned Green dress.

Not to be left out of a stylish celebration, Anna Wintour fronted up to the bash alongside daughter Bee Shaffer, who braved the cold in a white, cut-out gown.

Vogue editor Anna wasn’t about to let the chilly London air give her influenza, as she wore a floor-length coat over her silver gown.

Fresh from her debut as Queen Elizabeth in The Crown’s second series, Fleabag’s Olivia was on hand in a swish black ensemble.

Looking poised for the camera, Olivia wore a black, lace gown with white flowers embroidered down the middle and along the décolletage. You could say the outfit was fit for a queen? You could…

Olivia, who took to the stage to present Lynn Nottage and her production Sweat the award for best play, in partnership with Chanel, looked ecstatic as she posed with winners backstage.

This sure is one stylish red carpet if we do say so ourselves.
