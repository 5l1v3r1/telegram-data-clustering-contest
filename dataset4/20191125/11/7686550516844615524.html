Met Office forecasts freezing weather for Wales as polar air hits the UK

Temperatures will end the week a far cry from where they started

25 Nov 2019, 11:54 by Jillian MacMath

After an unsettled start to the week, polar air will surge into Wales by Thursday sending temperatures plummeting.

Those heading out to Christmas markets and Black Friday shopping will be in for quite a shock with temperatures in the single digits late this week.

But before the arrival of cold air, an unsettled weather pattern will grip much of the country.

The Met Office has issued yellow weather warnings for rain across south Wales on Tuesday and Wednesday, as heavy showers threaten to drench commuters and snarl traffic.

Downpours may also lead to flooding where up to 40mm of rain fall through Wednesday afternoon.

But a shift in the weather pattern is forecast for the latter half of the week, which will leave Wales feeling much more like mid-winter than late autumn.

Met Office spokesman Oli Claydon said: "In terms of a broad outlook, certainly for the main part of this working week, it will remain unsettled and relatively mild.

"But there are indications that at the end of the week we'll see colder conditions moving into the whole of the UK."
