Police update after elderly woman hit by ambulance on zebra crossing

She was taken to hospital with serious injures

25 Nov 2019, 11:23 by Jamie Hawkins

Police say an 88-year-old woman remains in a stable condition after she was hit by an ambulance in Exeter.

Several police cars and paramedics were called to Cowley Bridge Road on Friday afternoon after an ambulance, which was responding to an emergency call, struck the elderly woman on the zebra crossing by the Esso petrol station.

The road was initially closed in both directions causing long tailbacks between  Exeter  and the Crediton road.

Police remained at the scene on the petrol station forecourt after the road reopened.

A police spokesperson today (Monday) said the pedestrian was seriously injured, and officers are appealing for witnesses.

They said: "Police are appealing for witnesses after an ambulance was in collision with an elderly woman on a zebra crossing in Exeter.

"The incident happened around 3pm on Friday [22 November] on the A377 Cowley Bridge Road.

"The ambulance was responding to an emergency call when it was in collision with the 88-year Exeter woman on the crossing.

"She sustained serious injuries and was taken to Royal Devon and Exeter Hospital before being transferred to Derriford Hospital. She remains in a stable condition.

"Officers from the Exeter Roads Policing Unit are investigating the circumstances of the collision and appealing for any witnesses to come forward. 

"Please contact 101@dc.police.uk or phone 101, quoting log number 430 of 22/11/19."
