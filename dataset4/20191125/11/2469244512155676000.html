Police update after man arrested in serious Exeter stabbing

Two teenage boys were seriously injured

25 Nov 2019, 11:03 by Jamie Hawkins

A 20-year-old man has been released on police bail following a stabbing in Exeter.

Two teenage boys were seriously injured, one with puncture wounds to his stomach, in assaults at Haccombe Close on Friday (November 22), off Higher Barley Mount.

Police, paramedics and an air ambulance helicopter were called in at around 3.30pm.

The area was cordoned off for several hours whilst police carried out enquiries.

A police spokesperson today (Monday) said the man has been released on bail until December.

They said: "Police were called at around 3.30pm to Haccombe Close in Exeter following reports of two serious assaults on Friday 22 November.

"The victim, a teenage boy, has received a puncture wound injury to his stomach.

"Another teenage boy was also reported to have received an injury to his head. Their injuries are described as serious but not life-threatening.

"A 20-year-old man from Exeter was arrested on suspicion of causing grievous bodily harm with intent, wounding with intent and possession of a controlled class B drug. He has been released on police bail Friday until 20 December.

"Anyone who has any information is asked to contact police quoting log number 439 22/11/19."

Locals residents have been shocked by the incident at the rain drenched residential road.

One described seeing "some people fighting" before the realisation that one of them had been stabbed.
