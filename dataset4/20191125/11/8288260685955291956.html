Watch gangs of youths brawl at Cineworld, Broad Street - just hours after Star City machete terror

The incident has sparked fears the gang film is stirring violence in the city, but police say it was an 'isolated incident' which spilled into the foyer

25 Nov 2019, 11:28 by Stephanie Balloo, https://www.facebook.com/MissBalloo/

This is the moment a vicious brawl erupted inside another city cinema just hours on from a terrifying machete riot at Star City.

Violent scenes have now emerged from Five Ways Cineworld - and they were filmed a mere five hours after armed youths stormed the Vue Cinema in Nechells on Saturday night (November 23).

The worrying disorder, which was caught on camera and shared to Snapchat and can be viewed above , broke out at the Broad Street cinema around 10.45pm.

In alarming new footage, enraged youths can be seen hurling punches at one another in the foyer.

Around eight of the males are filmed fist fighting on the cinema's signature red carpet before two clear groups seem to separate.

Some pull away from the fight - fists still raised- but begin backing up towards the door onto Broad Street.

Keeping their eyes on the other group, the men make a backwards run for the exit.Â 

They appear to be chased out of the cinema by the others, with violence then spilling into the street.

A large crowd of cinema-goers can be seen watching on and staying out of the disorder.

Police swooped to the scene and broke up the disorder, but no weapons were seen and no one was believed to be injured.

It came just hours after a major disorder involving machetes as Blue Story was screened at Vue Cinema in Star City.

Seven officers were injured as police battled to control the Nechells riots from around 5.30pm.

The violence sparked fears over the gang film, which has since been axed from all Vu e cinemas, as it was believed to have been showing in Cineworld at the time.

The video caption reads: "This is what man's on after Blue Story?!?!? Mad film."

BBC Films swooped to defend the film, which they were involved in, saying it is centred around "love not violence".

Writer and director Andrew Onwubolu, known as Rapman, described the incident as "truly unfortunate" and said he hoped it was not an "indictment" of the film.

West Midlands Police say the latest Cineworld fight was an 'isolated incident' which spilled from the street into the foyer.

It is not believed be connected to the film, however police said they couldn't rule it out.

"Police were alerted at 10.45pm on Saturday night (23 Nov) to a disorder at Cineworld cinema, Broad Street, involving a group of about eight youths.

"They were moved on by staff and quickly dispersed.

"No weapons were seen and there are no reports of any injuries.

"It's believed this was an isolated incident and that the disorder started in the street and spilled into the foyer."
