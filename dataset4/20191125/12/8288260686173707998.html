Dean Smith on cabbage-gate, Newcastle threat and tweaks made to Steve Bruce's Aston Villa squad

Aston Villa v Newcastle United | Dean Smith opens up about the man he replaced, Steve Bruce, while discussing what was needed in order to get Villa promoted to the Premier League

25 Nov 2019, 12:00 by Ashley Preece, https://www.facebook.com/PreeceBirminghamLive/

Dean Smith has labelled the Aston Villa supporter who hurled a giant cabbage at Steve Bruce 13 months ago "a disgrace".

Bruce makes his long-awaited return to his former office for tonight's Monday Night Football encounter between Villa and Newcastle at Villa Park.

Prior to Villa's 3-3 draw with bottom-club Preston last October, Bruce was subjected to what was known as cabbage-gate after a supporter managed to roll the large vegetable towards the Geordie boss in protest. Bruce was sacked the following day before being replaced by Smith.

"I thought it was a disgrace," fumed the Villa boss ahead of facing off against the man he replaced. "You don't want to see that in the ground. I said that at the time.

"I was just embarrassed for the person who was bringing a cabbage in there."

As for Smith, the Villa chief admitted the side he took over from Bruce "had good players" - but last January was key to getting his side promoted.

"But they were all good players we had. What we had to do was add to the squad in January because we were struggling defensively. Axel Tuanzebe and James Chester were the only fit centre-halves and James was struggling up until January.
