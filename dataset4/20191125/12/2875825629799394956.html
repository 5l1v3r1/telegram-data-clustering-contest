Jose Mourinho gets down to work at Tottenham ahead of first home game in charge of new club

Spurs take on Olympiacos in the Champions League on Tuesday night knowing a win would seal qualification to the knockout stages of the competition

25 Nov 2019, 12:06 by Irishmirror.ie

New Tottenham boss Jose Mourinho appeared before the cameras on the training ground ahead of the Champions League clash with Olympiacos.

Cameras were allowed onto the training ground at Hotspur Way for a 15-minute open session and they saw Mourinho, less than a week into his new job, spend time with all three groups of players - those who played at the weekend, those that were not involved and the goalkeepers.

He again seemed in a good mood, exchanging pleasantries with some foreign journalists and sharing jokes with the staff.

Spurs fans will be encouraged to see Tanguy Ndombele and Jan Vertonghen in training after the two were ruled out of the weekend's win against West Ham with injury, while Harry Kane was also present despite suffering a knock in the game.

Mourinho was delighted after his reign Spurs got off the perfect start on Saturday with the 3-2 victory over the Hammers at the London Stadium, their first away win in the Premier League since January.

Goals from Heung-Min Son, Lucas Moura and Harry Kane gave Spurs a convincing lead, before Michail Antonio and Angelo Ogbonna restored some pride for the Hammers.

Mourinho will be desperate to win his first home game at the club, with victory sealing Spurs' place in the last-16 of the Champions League.

After the victory against West Ham Mourinho said: "It was very very important. 11 months without music in the away dressing room, without a smile or happiness. They did it. I am very happy for them and with them. If someone only saw the result they'd have thought it was very difficult for us.

But it was probably closer to a 4-0 than 3-1. I think my players paid the price of a very difficult week. International break, change of manager, new guy, different training sessions.

"We have a game in two days. I think we can go and win that match at home and qualify in the Champions League and I think the mood becomes high for the boys."
