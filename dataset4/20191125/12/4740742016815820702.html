Exclusive: Bristol City midfielder set for early return from Derby County loan due to Wayne Rooney arrival

The playmaker has lost his place in the Rams starting XI

25 Nov 2019, 12:55 by James Piercy

Jamie Paterson is set to make a return to Bristol City in January with Derby County ready to cut short his season-long loan, Bristol Live understands.

Paterson made the surprise switch to Derby on August 8 but has been unable to make a consistent impact at Pride Park, starting five Championship games with a further five appearances off the bench.

He was an unused substitute in Saturday's 1-0 win over Preston with Tom Lawrence preferred in the No10 position and Duane Holmes playing there in the previous fixture against Nottingham Forest.

Admittedly with the Christmas fixture list, there is ample opportunity for Paterson to play his way back into Philip Cocu's long-term plans but Wayne Rooney's arrival has further complicated the situation.

Rooney has begun working as a coach at Derby following his release from DC United and will be registered to play for the Rams from January 1, with the 34-year-old expected to be used as a withdrawn forward or playmaker.

That creates a logjam in the position for Phillip Cocu and with the considerable hype around Rooney's arrival and need to play him when available, Paterson's first-team chances are likely to become increasingly limited.

Furthermore, Derby have been linked for a January move for out-of-favour Celtic winger Scott Sinclair who they failed to sign in the summer, which caused the Rams to turn to Paterson.

It has always look an odd fit for the 27-year-old given his well-publicised Nottingham Forest allegiances and the fact he was known to be happy and settled at Bristol City, with the move coming as a surprise for player and fans alike.

Derby's intention to send Paterson back to BS3 gives head coach Lee Johnson the option to either re-integrate him into his first-team squad or negotiate a sale.

City won't be able to find a new loan club for Paterson as he came on for 27 minutes against Leeds on August 4 and EFL regulations state a player cannot play for three EFL clubs in one season.

There has been previous tension between player and coach but Johnson still values Paterson's attributes as a footballer with the 27-year-old a supremely-gifted individual who does a bring a certain x-factor to the team.

However, with Kasey Palmer, Callum O'Dowda, Niclas Eliasson and Sammie Szmodics, City are well-stocked in terms of wingers and central playmakers.

The return to fitness of Korey Smith and Adam Nagy joining Josh Brownhill and Han-Noah Massengo in central midfield, also further limits Paterson's chances in a deeper role.

Plus there is the potential that Joe Morrell or Liam Walsh could be recalled from their loans at Lincoln City and Coventry City, respectively, to further boost City's options.

Paterson is under contract until summer 2021 which means he still carries considerable transfer value and would prove an enticing option for a Championship team willing to invest in an experienced No10 or winger who is approaching his peak.
