Gas prices start to skyrocket

25 Nov 2019, 12:57 by Kirk Penton

Gas prices are on the rise in the Central Okanagan – but not in the north and south parts of the valley.

Several stations in Kelowna and West Kelowna this morning hiked their price for unleaded gasoline from 117.9 cents per litre to 131.9, which represents a whopping 14-cent hike.

However, gasbuddy.com indicates most Kelowna stations were still at 117.9 around the noon hour.

The cheapest gas in the Okanagan can still be found in Lake Country, where most stations were selling for 116.9.

Vernon was still holding steady at 122.9 as well.

Penticton prices are still on the lower end of the scale as well, checking in between 119.9 and 121.9 cents per litre.
