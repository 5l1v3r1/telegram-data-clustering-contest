S Gurumurthy claims he was behind Panneerselvam's 'fight for justice' after Jayalalithaa's death

25 Nov 2019, 12:12 by Gladwin Emmanuel

Chennai: RSS ideologue and Editor of Tughlak magazine S Gurumurthy on Sunday claimed that he was behind O Panneerselvam's revolt or "fight for justice" against the official faction of the AIADMK soon after late leader J Jayalalithaa's death in 2017.

The video of his speech, while addressing the golden jubilee commemorative event of the magazine, at Tiruchy, had gone viral drawing flak from the ruling AIADMK.

Gurumurthy reportedly said that when all arrangements were on to make VK Sasikala as chief minister he asked Panneerselvam, "Are you a man?" When Panneerselvam asked him what else to do, he told him to go and sit at Jayalalithaa's samadhi and he will get some idea there.

Gurumurthy claimed that his call to Panneerselvam to sit on a dharna at Jayalalithaa's samadhi prompted a change in Tamil Nadu politics. He also claimed that he had a role in unifying the two rival factions of AIADMK led by Edappadi Palaniswamy and O Panneerselvam.


With his remarks against Panneerselvam for "not being man enough" to take on the Sasikala faction, he stoked a controversy. Fisheries minister D Jayakumar described the remarks as "height of arrogance". Gurumurthy later clarified that his comments were not made to disrespect Panneerselvam.

Gurumurthy took to twitter on Monday and wrote, "When I spoke to OPS, I was not talking to him in any disrespectful way. I only talked to him saying why the AIADMK people are prostrating to Sasikala with no courage. OPS knows what I meant and he is the one who retrieved the AIADMK from Sasikala and that is why I have so much respect for him," Gurumurthy noted.
