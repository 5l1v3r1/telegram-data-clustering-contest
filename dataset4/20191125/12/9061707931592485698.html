Police burst three tyres to stop uninsured car driving through village

The car was 'boxed in' by officers

25 Nov 2019, 12:24 by Ben Waldron

Police officers burst three tyres of an uninsured car as it drove through a South Derbyshire village.

The driver of a Citroen without insurance failed to stop for Derbyshire Police officers as it travelled along the A444 near Overseal .

According to a tweet from Derbyshire Roads Policing Unit's dedicated Twitter page, three cars were sat further along the road and "stung" the vehicle, deflating three tyres.

The vehicle was then boxed in by officers and the four occupants were detained.

The tweet reads: "A444 near Overseal. Citroen with no insurance fails to stop for one of us.

"Driver didn't expect a gaggle of three more roads policing unit cars further down the road.

"Vehicle stung, three tyres deflated, boxed in and all four occupants detained. #TPAC #Traffic #Professional"
