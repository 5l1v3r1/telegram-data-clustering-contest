HLIB Research raises target price for Bumi Armada to 60 sen

25 Nov 2019, 01:45 by Wong Swee May

KUALA LUMPUR (Nov 25): Hong Leong Investment Bank (HLIB) Research has maintained its “Buy” rating on Bumi Armada Bhd at 50.5 sen with a higher target price (TP) of 60 sen (from 44 sen) and said its third quarter of 2019 (3Q19) earnings was above expectations.

In a note today, HLIB Research said Bumi Armada registered 3Q19 earnings of RM90.9 million (+41.6% quarter-on-quarter [q-o-q], +20.6% year-on-year [y-o-y]), lifting the cumulative nine months of 2019 (9M19) net profit to RM228.5 million (+27.7% y-o-y).

“This came above expectations due to (i) improved contributions from its floating production and off-loading (FPO) segment and (ii) higher than-expected JV & associates contribution, making up 91.5%/84.5%% of ours/consensus full-year forecasts,” the research house said.

In addition, it said that as expected, no dividend was declared.

HLIB said Bumi Armada’s core profit improved by +33% year-to-date (YTD) from RM178.9 million in 9M19 largely attributable to stronger FPO segment (+33.1%; better Kraken contribution) and joint ventures (JVs) (+10.4%).

However, this was partially offset by weaker offshore marine services (OMS) segment due to completion of the Lukoil project despite stronger offshore support vessel (OSV) utilisation.

Furthermore, the research house said floating production, storage and offloading (FPSO) Kraken is achieving material improvement in production efficiency this year and is expected to continue to produce stable earnings.

Meanwhile, it also said 5 OSVs were sold, leaving its total fleet at 32 in 3Q19, adding that Bumi Armada will continue to monetise its non-core assets.

“We understand that they are looking for a buyer of FPSO Claire as well. As evident by the better OSV utilisation of 58% in 3Q19 (vs. 51% in 2Q19), we can expect the OSV fleet utilisation to increase amidst fleet disposal and marginal improvement in daily charter rate (DCRs),” it said.

Hence, HLIB Research increased its financial year 2019-2021 (FY19-21) earnings by 10%-12% after imputing higher earnings contribution from its JV FPSO assets.

At 9:13am, shares of Bumi Armada dropped 2 sen to 48.5 sen, valuing it at RM2.97 billion.
