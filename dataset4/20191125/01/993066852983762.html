Book on children of the Troubles wins literary award

25 Nov 2019, 01:00

A BOOK examining the deaths of children during the Troubles has won a prestigious literary award.

Children of the Troubles, written by broadcaster Joe Duffy and journalist Freya McClements, received the Best Irish-published Book of the Year prize at the Irish Book Awards.

The authors dedicated their award to the 186 children killed during north's conflict.

Children of the Troubles: The Untold Story of the Children Killed in the Northern Ireland Conflict is based on original interviews with almost 100 families and extensive archival research.

The book includes children who were not previously publicly acknowledged as Troubles victims.

More than 115,000 votes were cast by the public to select the winners in each category at the awards show, which was hosted by Miriam O'Callaghan and Evelyn O'Rourke.

Posting a picture of their award on Twitter, Ms McClements said: "Honoured and delighted that Children of the Troubles has been recognised as Best Irish-published Book of the Year at the Irish Book Awards."

She said it was dedicated to memory of the children in the book and expressed her "heartfelt thanks to their families".
