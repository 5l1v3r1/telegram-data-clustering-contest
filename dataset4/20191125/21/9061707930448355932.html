Derby County's fine run of form ended by Leicester City

The young Rams were well beaten as five goals were scored in a dramatic first half at Holmes Park

25 Nov 2019, 21:35 by Chris Watson

Derby County Under-23s' impressive run was ended by a 4-1 defeat to Leicester City on Monday night.

The Rams had gone unbeaten in Premier League Two Division One since their opening day loss to Chelsea.

That run of nine league games without defeat has seen them climb to second in the table.

They had also been making good progress in the cup competitions - but came up against a Leicester side also in good form.

All the goals came in a dramatic first half at Holmes Park as the fourth-placed Foxes made it six wins out of six in all competitions.

The hosts took charge as they raced into a three-goal lead inside the opening half-hour.

George Hirst set City on their way early on, putting them ahead in the ninth minute.

Josh Eppiah doubled their advantage in the 26th minute, before Admiral Muskwe added a third soon afterwards.

Jahmal Hector-Ingram pulled one back for Derby in the 37th minute - but Leicester soon restored their three-goal cushion.

It was captain George Thomas who made it 4-1 four minutes before the break - and that proved to be the end of the scoring.

LEICESTER CITY: Johansson, Clark (Daley-Campbell, 79), Johnson, Ughelumba, L Thomas, G Thomas, Dewsbury-Hall, Eppiah, Muskwe (Reghba, 71), Wright (Lesshabela, 62), Hirst. Other subs: Davies (gk), Tee.

DERBY COUNTY: Mitchell, Bateman, Buchanan, Brown (Thompson, 79), Wassall, Minkley (Ebosele, 68), Mitchell-Lawson, Babos, Hector-Ingram, Sibley, Shonibare (Cresswell, 56). Other subs: Barnes (gk), Stretton.
