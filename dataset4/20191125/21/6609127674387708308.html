Aberdeen thug who pushed stranger down stairs dodges jail and asks to send him ‘sorry’ letter

25 Nov 2019, 21:35 by John Jeffay

A THUG who shoved a stranger down concrete steps dodged jail - then asked if she could write him a letter saying sorry.

Gemma Low, 27, got an 18-month supervision order and six-month curfew for pushing a man who allegedly mistook her for a vice girl.

The victim, 58, needed life-saving surgery after the horror attack in central Aberdeen.

During sentencing at the city’s sheriff court, Low asked: “Am I allowed to write a letter of apology to that man, or can I go and say sorry to him at least?”

Law chief Andrew Miller suggested she send a note for the procurator fiscal to pass on.

Low, of the city’s Ferryhill, shouted “Thanks for being nice” as she left court.

She had admitted assault to severe injury, permanent impairment and danger of life.

 

The man had part of his skull removed and fluid drained from his brain after December’s shove.

Low claimed he had made a “proposition”.

She told the court: “He said I was a prostitute.”
