I'm A Celebrity's Ant and Dec take aim at fans over 'impossible' trial backlash

I'm A Celebrity viewers hit out at the live trial on Sunday night, claiming the last challenge was 'impossible'

25 Nov 2019, 21:29 by Charlotte Tutton

I'm A Celebrity campmates took part in the live trial on Sunday night, hoping to win a roast dinner for everyone.

Unfortunately for Ian Wright, Cliff Parisi and Andy Whyment, they would not get their dinners because James Haskell and Roman Kemp failed the final challenge.

Viewers fiercely defended the boys though, complaining that the last task was "impossible" to complete.

They were also furious that Ian would not get his roast given how much he did in the live trial hosted by Ant and Dec.

Ant and Dec have since denied the challenge wasn't possible, revealing stand-ins managed to complete it in time.

But they didn't stop there, and the hosts decided to have one more dig at viewers complaining about the show.

On Monday night, the pair introduced the next part of the task that would see Myles Stephenson, Nadine Coyle, Kate Garraway and Andy battle to win care packages.

As Ant and Dec revealed what was coming up, they then began to mock the complaints from fans since the earlier trial.

They mentioned the apparently "barely possible" task that was "difficult" to complete, before looking for the right word to describe it.

The Mission Impossible movie theme tune began playing in the background, with then joking they still couldn't think of the term to define the task "unlikely" to be completed.

It's then that Dec said: "It's impossible."

Dec even blamed James' "sausage fingers" for not completing the trial, while not all of the fans watching at home saw the funny side.

Responding to Ant and Dec's jibes, viewers continued to blast the trial and called for justice for the campmates.

One fan said: "So pleased @antanddec can see the funny side of the live bushtucker trail, that was virtually impossible to complete. Laughing at someone’s else expense. Don’t rise to it @IanWright0 it’s what @ITV want."

Another tweeted: "Dec just saying 'I give up. It's impossible' has to be a reference to that unfair, impossible trial last night!"

I'm A Celebrity continues nightly at 9pm on ITV.
