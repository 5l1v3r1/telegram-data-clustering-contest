Dad sacked for pulling a sickie so he could take his son to see The Wiggles

25 Nov 2019, 14:05

A Toyota worker who called in sick so he could take his son to see The Wiggles was sacked after a colleague grassed him up.

Sean Mamo called in saying he couldn’t make it into the dealership in Yarrawonga, northern Victoria, because his boy wasn’t well.

But he actually had a pair of tickets for them both to see The Wiggles in concert, MailOnline reported.

Mr Mamo was rumbled when a picture of them both enjoying the gig posted to social media was shown to general manager Steven Zandt.

When quizzed by the owner he initially denied claiming personal leave, but admitted he’d gone to the concert when confronted with the picture.

Mr Mamo said he didn’t ask for a day’s annual leave because he didn’t think he would get it and he failed to apologise after being caught out.

The Australian reported that the incident, combined with his past poor performance, led the company to lose trust in Mr Mamo and give him the boot.

Mr Mamo lodged a claim for unfair dismissal with the Fair Work Commission, claiming the sick day was not enough to cost him his job.

But the application was dismissed by president Alan Coleman, who said Mr Mamo had been ‘dishonest’ in seeking ‘to obtain financial advantage by deception’ and ‘asserting a legitimate right to be absent from work when none in fact exists’.

Mr Colman ruled the bogus sick day was ‘of sufficient gravity’ for him to be fired.

Given all evidence, he concluded that dismissal was neither harsh, unjust nor unreasonable and ultimately ‘not unfair’.
