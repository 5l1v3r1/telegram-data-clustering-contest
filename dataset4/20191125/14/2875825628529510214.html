Tributes paid to brave Dundalk boy Daragh McNally, 11, who died after battling cancer

"Daragh will be forever missed and forever remembered by his heartbroken parents Colleen and Paul"

25 Nov 2019, 14:01 by Edel Hughes

Tributes have been paid to a brave young Co Louth boy after his death at the age of 11.

Daragh McNally, from Dundalk, passed away at Crumlin Children's Hospital on Sunday after battling an aggressive lymphoma.

The youngster was diagnosed with the illness in October, shortly after returning from a family holiday.

Daragh, who had autism, was delighted when his service dog Lenny won the national Nose of Tralee award in 2017 for helping him cope with behavioural issues and get him outside on a daily basis.

Family friend Sheila Murphy set up a GoFundMe page last month to help fund Daragh's cancer treatment and related expenses. The page raised over €28,000.

Sheila shared a message of gratitude on the page yesterday which read: "Thank you all from the bottom of my heart to each and everyone of you for all your love and support over the last number of weeks.

"We will all be forever grateful for everything and our little angel Daragh will be eternally in our hearts."

A funeral notice on RIP.ie reads: "Peacefully, at Our Lady’s Children’s Hospital, Crumlin, in his eleventh year, nestled in the mantle of his family’s love and devoted care.

"Daragh will be forever missed and forever remembered by his heartbroken parents Colleen and Paul, big brother Nathan, adoring sisters Naoise and Layla, doting grandparents Kevin and Nora Moran, Joe and Kathleen McNally, aunts Sharron and Frances, uncles Kevin, Cal, Aaron, Joe and Brendan, amazing cousins, special friend Sheila, buddy Lenny, extended family, neighbours and friends."

A family message read: "Colleen and Paul, the Moran and McNally families would like to take this opportunity to thank most sincerely, the doctors, nurses and staff of Our Lady’s Children’s Hospital Crumlin for the wonderful care they gave to Daragh, also the staff of Scoil Réalt na Mara and The Maria Goretti Respite”.

Daragh will lie in repose at the family home on Tuesday, November 26,between 3pm and 8pm.

Mourners are asked to abide by the reposing hours, the house is strictly private at all other times.

Removal will take place on Wednesday, November 27, at 10.30am driving to St Nicholas' Church, Dundalk, arriving for Funeral Mass at 11am to celebrate Daragh's life.

The funeral will proceed to St Patrick’s Cemetery.

Daragh'sfamily have asked mourners attending the funeral to wear blue to raise autism awareness.
