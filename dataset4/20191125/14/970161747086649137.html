Sex Education season 2 release date: A chlamydia outbreak is coming to Netflix

25 Nov 2019, 14:21

Sex Education season two is now officially in sight and by in sight we mean it’s charging at us ready for an early 2020 release date.

The surprise hit of 2019 is by far the best teen drama since Skins, following awkward Otis Milburn (Asa Butterfield), who becomes Moordale High School’s resident sex therapist, reluctantly following his mother’s (Gillian Anderson) footsteps despite never actually having sex himself.

However, this time around Otis has a girlfriend and the question is: can he practise what he preaches? We’ll find out when series 2 lands on Netflix on January 17, 2020.

With a chlamydia outbreak storming through the school like some unfortunate STI riddled tidal wave, no doubt Otis and his ‘business partner’ Maeve ( Emma Margaret Tachard-Mackey) will have class mates queueing around the yard for much needed sexual, and medicinal, advice.

Netflix released first look photos from Sex Education season 2 earlier this month, which prove absolutely nothing has calmed down for Otis, Eric (Ncuti Gatwa), Maeve and the rest of crew.

Adam (Connor Swindells) is now miles away in a military camp, struggling to come to terms with his sexuality after pulling Eric during detention.

As for Eric, alongside Otis, he appears to be distracting himself by perusing more needless but problematic adventures, while Maeve’s ex-boyfriend Jackson (Kedar Williams-Stirling) is still tormented by his parents piling on the pressure to become the next Michael Phelps.

Season two will welcome super intelligent but unlucky in love pupil Viv (Chinenye Ezeudu), French head-turner Rahim (Sami Outalbali) and Maeve’s new next door neighbour, Isaac (George Robertson).

Sex Education returns to Netflix on January 17 2020.
