BBC Sports Personality of the Year shortlist 2019: Wales captain Alun Wyn Jones nominated for prestigious award

Jones led Wales to the Six Nations Grand Slam in the saw year that he became the nation's record cap-holder

25 Nov 2019, 14:57 by Matthew Southcombe

Wales captain Alun Wyn Jones has been nominated for BBC Sports Personality of the Year.

The indomitable 34-year-old led Wales to the Grand Slam in style earlier this year, helping them beat Ireland 25-7 on the final day in Cardiff.

Perhaps even more impressive is that he played the majority of the game with ligament damage in his knee and was one of Wales' top performers on the day.

On a personal note, the second row became Wales' record cap-holder at this year's Rugby World Cup, taking over Gethin Jenkins. When he led the side out against Australia, he earned his 130th cap, making him the most-capped Welshman of all time.

By the end of the World Cup, he'd moved into second on the all-time cap list and currently sits five behind leader Richie McCaw, a milestone he looks destined to surpass.

Following the conclusion of the World Cup, legends of the game lauded the Wales skipper , with 1995 World Cup-winner Francois Pienaar branding him 'the best captain in the world'.

"I’ve said that if you look at how he’s rallied his team, when they were down and out and they came back when they shouldn’t have. I put that down to him," Pienaar told WalesOnline after the World Cup.

“To make that call against South Africa in the scrum. I thought ‘wow’ and bang, it got them right back in the game.

“That’s the mark of a great captain. I’m a fan of the way he handles himself. When I saw him put his arm around that little mascot singing the anthem, I thought ‘that’s generosity of spirit’.

“He's a great, great servant of the game and has really done well. If you look at the Welsh performances over the last two years, they’ve been phenomenal.”

Announcing Jones as a nominee, last year's winner Geraint Thomas said: "You've had an incredible year and I'm going to tell everyone why they should vote for you.

"You led Wales to a Six Nations Grand Slam, were their star player at the Rugby World Cup and became Wales' most capped player.

"You're a legend mate and you've got my vote. Everyone should vote for Alun on the night."

Jones finds himself on a shortlist with the great and the good of British sport.

Also nominated so far is England cricket star Ben Stokes, whose heroics hauled England back into the cricket World Cup final before eventually helping his side over the line.

Dina Asher-Smith is the second nominee, having become the first British female to win a major global sprint title as the claimed the World 200m gold in Doha.

The 23-year-old also picked up silvers in the 100m and the 4x100m relay.

Also nominated is England footballer Raheem Sterling, who won a domestic treble - the Premier League, FA Cup and League Cup - with Manchester City last season.

He was also named the Football Writers' Association Footballer of the Year.

The BBC are revealing their full short list through the day and there is more to come...
