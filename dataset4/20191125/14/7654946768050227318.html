Charles Schwab to buy rival TD Ameritrade in $26B stock deal

25 Nov 2019, 14:16 by Noah Manskar

Charles Schwab announced plans Monday to buy its rival TD Ameritrade for about $26 billion in a deal that will create a colossal discount brokerage.

The combined company will boast 24 million brokerage accounts with more than $5 trillion in client assets after the all-stock acquisition closes in the second half of next year, the two firms said.

“We believe the combination of our two great companies positions us to be competing and winning in the investment services business for the long run — the very long run,” Schwab President and CEO Walt Bettinger said in a statement.

Reports emerged last week that the two largest publicly traded discount brokerages would unite after scrapping commission fees for online trades, a move that shook up the industry and put pressure on the companies’ stock prices.

Ameritrade shares were up 3.8 percent in premarket trading Monday morning, while Schwab’s were down 0.1 percent.

Both companies’ boards of directors have unanimously approved the purchase, which will give TD Ameritrade stockholders 1.0837 Schwab shares for every Ameritrade share, the companies said.

TD Bank — which currently owns more than 40 percent of Ameritrade — will end up with a roughly 13 percent stake in the merged company, according to a press release. Ameritrade’s other stockholders will own about 18 percent, while Schwab’s existing shareholders will have a 69 percent majority, the firms said.

The combined company will eventually be headquartered at Schwab’s new campus in Westlake, Texas, northwest of Dallas. TD Ameritrade has also suspended its search for a chief executive and named Stephen Boyle interim president and CEO.

The one-time executive vice president and chief financial officer will guide the Nebraska-based company through its integration with San Francisco-based Schwab, which is expected to take 18 to 36 months after the deal closes, the firms said.

“Partnering with Schwab on this transformative opportunity makes the right strategic and financial sense for TD Ameritrade,” Boyle said in a statement. “… We can continue to challenge the status quo, pooling our resources and expertise to transform lives — and investing — and deliver sustainable, long-term value to our many stakeholders.”
