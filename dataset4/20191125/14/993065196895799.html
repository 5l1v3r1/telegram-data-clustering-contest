Man (36) admits stealing wallet from Conall Kerrigan who was later found dead near Derry walls

25 Nov 2019, 14:45

A 36-year-old man has pleaded guilty to stealing the wallet from a man who was later found dead between Derry's walls and Millennium Forum four years ago.

Adrian Vasile Aitoneanu, a Romanian national whose address was given as Selway Street in Rotherham, England, was re-arraigned on the day of his trial at Derry Crown Court on a charge of robbing Conall Kerrigan of his wallet and contents on August 23 2015.

Mr Kerrigan (35) had just returned home from England to Claudy and had planned to set up his own plumbing business.

He was out celebrating with friends on the night his body was found. At the time police said they did not believe his death was the result of a crime.

The defendant also pleaded guilty on re-arraignment to stealing an iPhone 6 valued at Â£600 from a woman on the same night.

Defence barrister Sean Doherty said there was a potential second defendant in the case, also a Romanian man, whose extradition from the Republic was recently approved.

Mr Doherty said that man had 15 days to appeal the extradition order.

A barrister for the Public Prosecution Service told Judge Elizabeth McCaffrey that if the extradition went ahead "proceedings here are ready to go".

Aitoneanu was released on continuing bail until his plea and sentence hearing takes place on January 7.

Judge McCaffrey ordered a pre-sentence report for that date and also adjourned the case against the man awaiting extradition until December 17.
