Star Wars classic to be screened in Nottingham with live symphony orchestra

Top price tickets are £70.50

25 Nov 2019, 14:11 by Rachel Gorman

It's only a matter of weeks until the final Star Wars instalment hits UK cinemas bringing the epic 42-year-long saga to an end (allegedly).

But that's not the last you'll see of Luke Skywalker and co on the big screen with film classic, Star Wars: Return of the Jedi, going on a small UK tour accompanied by a live symphony orchestra.

Following on from the UK premiere at London's Royal Albert Hall in October next year, the concert will tour through November including a stop-off at Nottingham's Royal Concert Hall on November 8.

The film will be projected onto a huge ultra-HD screen, while the symphony orchestra, on stage, bring to life the music of Oscar winning composer John Williams.

Tickets priced £35.50 to £70.50 go on sale on Friday, November 29 at disneytickets.co.uk and trch.co.uk.

Released in 1983, Return of the Jedi follows Luke Skywalker, Han Solo and Princess Leia, Jabba the Hutt, Yoda and the Ewoks in an adventure across the galaxy as Darth Vader prepares to unleash the final blow to the Rebel Alliance.

Legendary composer Williams is well known for scoring all nine of the Star Wars saga films, beginning with 1977’s Star Wars: A New Hope for which he earned an Academy Award for Best Original Score.

His scores for Star Wars: The Empire Strikes Back, Star Wars: Return of the Jedi, and Star Wars: The Force Awakens were nominated for Oscars for Best Original Score.

Star Wars: The Rise of Skywalker is released in the UK on December 19.

Star Wars: Return of the Jedi tour dates

Nottingham Royal Concert Hall, November 8
Liverpool Philharmonic Hall, November 9
Cardiff St David’s Hall, November 10
Sheffield City Hall, November 25
Bath Forum, November 26
Manchester Bridgewater Hall, November 27
Birmingham Symphony Hall, November 28
Glasgow Royal Concert Hall, November 29

Ticket prices: £70.50, £52.50, £44.50, £35.50
