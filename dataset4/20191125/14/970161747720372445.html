Outpouring of love for boy, 15, found dead in woods

25 Nov 2019, 14:34

A village has been left grief-stricken following the news that a missing teenage boy was found dead in woodland.

The body of Scott Smith, 15, was found on Saturday at around 1.30pm following a search for the boy who had been missing for three days.

Police said his death is not being treated as suspicious and his heartbroken family are being looked after by specially trained officers.

Following the devastating news, Skirlaugh has united to pay tribute to the young boy who has received an ‘outpouring of love’ from the close-knit community.

Emotional tributes have since been pouring in on social media, with many expressing their shock and pain for Scott’s family.

On Sunday, villagers paid their respects to the teenager at St Augustine’s church and lit a candle in his honour.

Reverend Andy Simpson said: ‘We had a very full church service this morning for Scott, for visitors to pay their respects after a message was sent out for people to join us in a special service.

‘It is still too soon to say anything, as this community is hurting, but what I will say there was very much an outpouring of love for this young man.’

Scott had not been seen since Thursday, November 21, and police launched a public appeal to help find him.

On Saturday, November 23, police were called to a remote woodland location on the outskirts of the village following an ‘incident’, where they found the boy’s body.

A cordon was put in place for several hours while police and forensic teams investigated the area and residents were advised to stay away from the scene.

He had been reported missing to the police on Thursday evening, after being last seen at about 5.30pm.
