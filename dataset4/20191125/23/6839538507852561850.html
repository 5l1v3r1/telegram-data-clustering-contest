The Flemings share their story of their complicated twin birth

25 Nov 2019, 23:27

Born 10 weeks early and weighing just 480g, twin Eloise Fleming was given just a 5 per cent chance of survival. Here’s her amazing story.

She might have been tiny but Eloise’s huge heart surprised everyone as she conquered every challenge thrown at her.

Today, 15-month-old Eloise continues to astonish her parents with her progress.

“She’s our little miracle baby. She’s fought through so much,” said proud mother Nicole Fleming.

Eloise and twin sister Isla, who tipped the scales at 1560g, entered the world 10 weeks premature.

They had a rare condition called twin-to-twin transfusion – meaning they were in two separate sacs but shared the same placenta in the womb.

Doctors told Nicole and husband Shannon the condition made chances of both girls surviving after birth slim.

Because of her extra weight, doctors were slightly more confident of Isla’s chances.

“We made a lot of our decisions based on getting Isla out safely and hoping that Eloise would just hold on a little bit longer,” Ms Fleming said.

But a rare perforated bowel after a heart surgery resulted in Isla’s tragic death at just four weeks old.

The Forest Range couple and their other children – Amelia, 10, Eamon, 8, and Ciara, 7 – spent months in the Women’s and Children’s Hospital during the twins’ journey.

In appreciation, the Flemings have donated more than $60,000 to the WCH’s neonatal unit.

The WCH Foundation’s Christmas campaign launched on November 21.
