‘Serial’ subject Adnan Syed won’t get a new trial, Supreme Court rules

25 Nov 2019, 23:40 by Tamar Lapin

Adnan Syed, the convicted killer whose story was chronicled in the hit podcast “Serial,” won’t be getting a new trial, the US Supreme Court ruled on Monday.

The justices chose to leave in place a ruling by Maryland’s highest court in March, that denied Syed’s bid for a new trial based on information uncovered in the 2014 audio series.

Millions of people tuned in to hear the story of Syed, who is serving a life sentence after being found guilty in 2000 of strangling his 17-year-old high school ex-girlfriend Hae Min Lee, and burying her body in a Baltimore park.

Prosecutors said Syed killed her after she broke up with him.

His current lawyer had argued that his trial attorney, the late Cristina Gutierrez, failed to pursue a classmate’s claims that she saw Syed at the Woodlawn Public Library at 2:15 p.m. on Jan. 13, 1999 — right when investigators say Lee was being strangled to death.

Two Maryland courts found that Syed deserved a new trial.

But the Maryland Court of Appeals denied Syed a new trial, saying there was little chance the outcome would have been different even if Gutierrez had done the work she should have.

Syed’s attorney said he and his client were “deeply disappointed” by the Supreme Court’s decision to uphold the ruling.

“But by no means is this the end of Adnan Syed,” said C. Justin Brown. “There are other legal options and we are exploring each and every one of them.”

Maryland Attorney General Brian Frosh said the evidence against Syed “was overwhelming.”

“We remain confident in the verdict that was delivered by the jury and are pleased that justice for Hae Min Lee has been done,” he said in a statement.
