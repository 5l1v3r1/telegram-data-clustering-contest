Bruce acknowledges Hourihane’s class soured his Aston Villa return

25 Nov 2019, 23:22

Midfielder had not started a game for Villa since October 19.

Newcastle manager Steve Bruce was left to rue “two pieces of magic” from Conor Hourihane as his return to Aston Villa turned sour.

Using his “wand of a left foot”, as described by Villa boss Dean Smith, the Republic of Ireland international stroked home Villa’s opener from a set piece in the 32nd minute before teeing up Anwar El Ghazi with another piercing free-kick delivery four minutes later.

The 2-0 victory ended a run of three successive Premier League defeats for Villa, while Bruce lamented the fact his side barely turned up against a club from which he was sacked just under 14 months ago after two years in charge.
