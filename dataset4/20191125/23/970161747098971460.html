Rachel Riley all smiles at White Christmas party after Jeremy Corbyn t-shirt row

25 Nov 2019, 23:51

Rachel Riley, who sparked criticism last week after wearing a t-shirt showing an edited picture of Jeremy Corbyn, put any backlash to one side as she stepped out with husband Pasha Kovalev tonight.

The pregnant Countdown star attended the White Christmas press night in London, along with the likes of Nicola Roberts and some of ITV’s Loose Women.

Rachel caused a social media storm last week over her choice of attire in a political Twitter post.

Writing on the night of ITV’s political debate between Jeremy Corbyn and Boris Johnson, Rachel said: ‘I didn’t feel comfortable knowing my workplace was to be full of racists tonight.

‘I don’t endorse Boris, but I do endorse #NeverCorbyn. Please see my pinned thread if you still don’t understand why.’

In an accompanying photo of herself, she wore a t-shirt showing an image of Corbyn holding a placard reading ‘Jeremy Corbyn is a racist endeavor’.

In the original picture, which dates back to the 1980s, the placard reads: ‘Defend the Right to Demonstrate Against Apartheid’.

Rachel and Pasha, who met on Strictly Come Dancing in 2013, are expecting their first child in December.

They revealed Rachel was pregnant in May 2019 and married two months later in Las Vegas.

The White Christmas event was to celebrate a special performance of the festive production, based on the 1954 film of the same name.

It’s currently showing at the Dominion Theatre in the West End, and is booking until the 4 January.

Girls Aloud star Nicola Roberts was also at the event.

It comes after she, along with former band mates Kimberley Walsh and Cheryl Cole, were last week forced to deny they had a Whatsapp group against I’m a Celeb’s Nadine Coyle.

The trio said in a statement: ‘We don’t have a group chat about I’m A Celebrity.

‘This just isn’t true. We only wish Nadine the best in the jungle and hope she does well on the show.’

Meanwhile, representing Loose Women were Coleen Nolan, Andrea McLean, Gloria Hunniford and Linda Robson.

It seems party season is officially upon us.
