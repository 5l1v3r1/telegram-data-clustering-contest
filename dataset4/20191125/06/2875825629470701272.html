Ambulance 'turnaround' times see huge rise as only 15.9% meet guidelines

Turnaround times deemed to be safe are being regularly missed throughout the country

25 Nov 2019, 06:00 by Liz Farsaci

Hospitals have seen a huge rise in the time it takes ambulance crews to prepare for new emergencies – with only 15.9% of cases meeting guidelines.

Turnaround times deemed to be safe by the Health Information and Quality Authority (HIQA) are being regularly missed throughout the country.

They measure how long it takes from an ambulance arriving at a hospital to when the crew is ready to accept another call.

The figures were released to Fianna Fail health spokesman Stephen Donnelly who described them as “shocking”.

The HIQA reported 95% of patients should be handed over from an ambulance crew to emergency department staff in less than 20 minutes. This differs from HSE guidelines, which state 30 minutes as the target time.

The 20-minute target was only met in 15.9% of cases in September.

The HSE 30-minute guideline was achieved 37.1% of the time.

Both sets of figures have dropped dramatically since September 2017, when they stood at 25.2% and 53.2% respectively.

For Kerry University Hospital, there is a turnaround of less than 20 minutes in just 1.7% of cases.

Mayo General, University Hospital Galway and South Tipperary all came in below the 5% mark.

Deputy Donnelly said: “The decline in the transfer turnaround times is yet another reflection of the pressures on hospitals and the lack of capacity.

“The main reason for the delay is because emergency departments are too busy with too few staff to process a patient coming in by ambulance.”

The Coombe Maternity Hospital and the National Maternity Hospital met the 20-minute turnaround times in 57.7% and 42.1% of cases respectively.

The figure for Temple Street Children’s Hospital was 60.9%.
