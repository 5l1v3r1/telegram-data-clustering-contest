Paytm raises $1 billion in fresh funding

Paytm has announced that it raised fresh funds from a group of investors, including existing shareholders such as Ant Financial, Softbank Vision Fund, and new investors including funds and accounts advised by T. Rowe Price Associates.

25 Nov 2019, 06:27 by Express Web Desk

India’s largest e-payments platform Paytm on Monday announced that it has raised fresh funds from a group of investors which include existing shareholders such as Ant Financial, Softbank Vision Fund. The company, however, did not disclose the volume of funds it has raised from them.

According to a report by news agency Reuters, the company has raised $1 billion in the latest round of funding.

“We are today announcing our latest equity fundraise from a group of investors, including existing shareholders such as Ant Financial, Softbank Vision Fund, and new investors including funds and accounts advised by T. Rowe Price Associates, Inc. among others,” the company wrote in a statement.

It further announced that Discovery Capital, which is an existing shareholder of the company, also participated in the round of fundraising.

The funds will be utilised in further expanding the company’s payments and financial services business in the rural parts of India and also create jobs.

“We will invest and support millions of rural Indians towards self-sustainability through job creation,” Paytm said in its statement.

Paytm is currently serving merchants in over 2,000 towns and cities spanning across 650 districts. It aims to bring low-cost mobile-enabled financial services to rural India.

Going forward, the mobile payments giant said that it will be expanding its offerings in the field of new-age financial services such as lending, insurance, investments and stockbroking.

“In the next phase, we are expanding our offerings in consumer internet and accelerating the vision of bringing new-age financial services such as lending, insurance, investments, stockbroking to the masses.”

Paytm will be investing Rs 10,000 crore over the next three years in a bid to get in more people under financial inclusion in the country.

“At Paytm, we are committed to bringing half a billion Indians to the mainstream economy by onboarding them in the formal financial ecosystem. This new investment by our current and new investors is a reaffirmation of our commitment to serve Indians with new-age financial services,” Paytm’s Founder and CEO Vijay Shekhar Sharma said in a statement.

Paytm is also strengthening its user engagement through the content services provided under Paytm Inbox which offers games, news, videos and more.
