Ole Gunnar Solskjaer praises Man United’s attitude after drawing 3-3 vs Sheffield United

25 Nov 2019, 06:38 by Archie Blade

Ole Gunnar Solskjaer praises Man United’s attitude after drawing 3-3 vs Sheffield United during their Premier League encounter on Sunday.

It is no secret that the Manchester United is vastly inferior to the ones that played under Alex Ferguson. However, when the manager says that the team has taken big strides after drawing a match against a newly promoted side, you know that the club has sunk into new lows.

Manchester United started their match against Sheffield United on the backfoot. They first conceded in the 19th minute before letting another one in the 52nd minute. In that time Ole Gunnar Solskjaer’s side had only registered one shot on target in comparison to Sheffield United’s 6.

United’s Academy graduates however, rose to the occasion and scored 3 goals in the span of 7 minutes to put the Red Devils in the driver’s seat. Brandon Williams first scored in the 72nd minute before Mason Greenwood added to the score in the 77th minute. Marcus Rashford then struck a couple of minutes later to put United ahead by a goal.

It seemed that United would leave Bramall lane with 3 points in the bag. However, Oli McBurnie levelled the scores after firing one behind the net in stoppage time. Most fans were disappointed at the team for allowing themselves to reach the point where even 3 goals couldn’t result in a victory. Ole Gunnar Soslkjaer though, has a different point of view.

Ole Gunnar Solskjaer praises Man United’s attitude after drawing 3-3 vs Sheffield United

United’s manager seemed content with the performance of the side and used their match against Everton last year as an example of why this team is better than the one, he managed last season.

“The difference between this team and last year’s team is huge,” he told Sky Sports after the game. “At 2-0, my mind is going back to Everton [where United lost 4-0 last season].

“No doubt about the drive, the energy, the attitude of these boys – they never give in.

“Last year we would have been three, four, or five down. This is such a big stride forward for them.”
