Weekend Roundup: Protests against racism, Bale deals with boos, City dump Chelsea

The big Premier League clash between Chelsea and Manchester City did not disappoint, two Dutch teams were part of a unique protest against racism and Gareth Bale was subjected to boos by his team's supporters.

25 Nov 2019, 15:33 by Sports Desk

With players back from the international break, club football saw significant developments, including Jose Mourinho’s return to the Premier League as the manager of Tottenham Hotspur. The weekend also saw a big Premier League clash, an anti-racism protest, Bale being booed and on-pitch blunders.

Here are the talking points from the weekend:

Chelsea goes down to a big club, again

In the biggest Premier League clash of the weekend, Manchester City came from behind to beat a young Chelsea 2-1 and stay within nine points of English Premier League leader Liverpool. The win, however, came at a price, as Pep Guardiola lost Sergio Aguero to a groin injury.

All the goals in an end-to-end contest at Etihad Stadium came in the first half as City responded to N’Golo Kante’s 21st-minute strike with goals from Kevin De Bruyne and Riyad Mahrez. Raheem Sterling looked like he had added a third, but his injury-time strike was ruled out for offside in a very tight VAR call (yes, again with City).
