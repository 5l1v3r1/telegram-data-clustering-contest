Jeremy Clarkson Says Climate Change is Real Now

25 Nov 2019, 15:00 by Tom Pritchard

Anyone who has paid any small amount of attention to Jeremy Clarkson knows a few basic things about him: he likes cars, and he doesn't believe human beings cause climate change. Well, I should correct you on that last one. No doubt Clarkson still likes cars (still barring the electric variety, I assume), but it seems he's changed his mind about the whole climate change topic.

In the past Clarkson has been very against the idea of climate change being a thing, dismissing it as 'science fiction' and once suggesting that global warming was a term made up by Margaret Thatcher to help her fight the striking miners. He even made the news recently for using his Sun column to attack climate activist Greta Thunberg - calling her a spoiled brat and suggesting she go back to school.

But now, while speaking to the Sunday Times, Clarkson says he's all in on it, after seeing the effects climate change has had for himself. Specifically a "graphic demonstration of global warming" while boating through Cambodia for The Grand Tour.

“The irony is not lost on me. A man who hosted a car programme for 30 years, limited to 7mph by global warming.

It’s the first time that we’ve ever admitted to there being global warming. We were completely screwed on that [trip].

There was no water in the Tonle Sap lake. I mean, it was knee-deep. And you could paddle in it. So we had two days of absolute frustration — being towed and grounding.”

He also added what comes across as another dig at Thunberg in the process:

“It was alarming, genuinely alarming. But we don’t blame mankind for it. We’ll let Greta do that.”

Still that's a pretty big bombshell, the biggest Clarkson has ever done I'd imagine, and if he does start bringing it up on The Grand Tour without the usual snark then maybe some of his viewers will stop dismissing climate change because he told them to.

But Jeremy is still very much a defender of car owners, claiming that car owners are being hit with higher penalties than vehicles that cause more pollution (like diesel) and that the home produces as much climate-warming gas as the cars of the world.

Well he has a point about diesel, but that doesn't give petrol cars a free pass. Especially since it's not just about the greenhouse gases. [The Times]
