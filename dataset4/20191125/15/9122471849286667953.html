Jason Garrett Makes Troubling Admission Following Loss To Patriots

25 Nov 2019, 15:31 by Andrew Holleran

Jason Garrett’s most-criticized decision in Sunday evening’s loss to the New England Patriots came on a 4th and 7 inside the red zone. The Cowboys trailed the Patriots, 13-6, with about six minutes to play. Garrett opted to kick a field goal, cutting the lead to four points, instead of going for it on fourth down.

FOX analyst Troy Aikman criticized the decision immediately, saying he would have gone for it. Others in football seemed to agree.

The win probability stats sided with Aikman and others.

The win probability, had they gone for it was 18.8 percent, according to ESPN Stats & Info. By kicking the field goal, the win probability was 16.7%

Garrett was asked about the win probability stats following the game. It turns out that the Cowboys’ coaching staff doesn’t use them during the game.

“We don’t use those stats during the game,” he told reporters.

Other teams, like the 8-2 Baltimore Ravens, reportedly use them on every play…
