General Election 2019: What are the parties promising on the NHS?

25 Nov 2019, 15:23

The NHS is at the centre of the parties’ election campaigns, with polls suggesting that many voters consider it a higher priority than Brexit.

So what are the major parties planning for the National Health Service?

Conservative

The Conservative party has pledged an extra 50,000 nurses amid a shortage of nursing staff.

Boris Johnson has also said that the party will bring back the nursing bursary, which the Tories themselves had scrapped.

According to the manifesto, the party says it will build 40 new hospitals, bring in 6,000 new GPs, as well as free hospital parking for vulnerable groups.

The Conservatives are also promising to invest £1 billion per year in social care over the next five years in a bid to stabilise the system.

Labour

The Labour party says its ‘immediate task’ will be to ‘repair our health service’, with an ‘urgent priority to end NHS privatisation’.

The party says it will increase spending on the NHS by an average 4.3% a year.

It will also offer free dental check ups and spend an extra £1.6 billion on mental health services.

Jeremy Corbyn has also said that the party will invest £2bn into a mental health fund that would end unsafe dormitory wards and to roll out a fleet of crisis ambulances, along with £845m for child mental health.

Liberal Democrats

The Liberal Democrats say they will add one penny on income tax to fund an extra £7 billion a year for the NHS and social care.

Leader Jo Swinson has also pledged to treat mental health with the same urgency as physical health.

The party also says it will set aside £10 billion in capital funding for facilities.

Green Party

The Green party claims it will increase the NHS budget by ‘at least’ £6billion a year every year until 2030 in its manifesto.

It also pledged to reinstate the nursing bursaries and replace private sector investment with ‘community leadership’.

Brexit Party

Nigel Farage’s party says it is committed to a ‘publicly-owned comprehensive service that is free at the point of use’.

The party has pledged to return existing private initiatives which have failed back into public ownership, and to introduce ‘24-hour GP surgeries to relieve the strain in A&E departments’.
