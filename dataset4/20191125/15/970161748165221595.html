BBC Radio's festive lineup will include 'all-consuming silence'

25 Nov 2019, 15:20

BBC Radio just announced its lineup for the festive season, which includes a three-part series exploring, well, silence in the Arctic.

From Christmas Eve, listeners will be able to take a slow walk – with host Horatio Clare – from the west coast of Greenland along the Arctic Circle Trail.

According to the BBC, you’ll be able to hear everything that ‘the silence and brutal force of the elements’ has to offer.

This includes the ‘beating of ravens wings, the crackle of frozen streams, the screaming winds and the sudden all-consuming silence when everything stands still.’

Horatio is accompanied by three huskies, as well as ‘music that captures the light, the colours and the ever changing atmospheres of the journey.’

BBC’s lineup also includes Darcey Bussell in conversation with Michael Berkeley.

She’ll be discussing her long and illustrious career, as well as the toll dancing took on her body.

The 50-year-old dancer announced she was stepping down from Strictly Come Dancing’s judging panel in April after having been with the show since 2010.

She said at the time: ‘I know I will miss being part of this unique show and the fact that it celebrates dance is something I am so passionate about. I hope that I may be very lucky to be asked back again one day.’

There’ll also be a special featuring recently eliminated Strictly contestant Michelle Visage.

The BBC’s festive new lineup includes Peaky Blinders star Cillian Murphy return to 6 Music to play some ‘tender radio for tender heads’ on New Year’s Day.

Meanwhile, Gemma Collins presents a special festive edition of her Radio 1 podcast, as well as festive music mixes from Greg James, Rylan, Scott Mills, Chris Stark, Trevor Nelson and Nadia Jae.

There’ll also be five guest editors on Radio 4’s Today programme, including climate activist Greta Thunberg, Baroness Hale of Richmond, Grayson Perry, Charles Moore and George The Poet.

Sounds like it’ll be an interesting listen this year.
