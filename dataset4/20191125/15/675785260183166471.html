Pilot missing amid rescue operation after 'plane crash' off coast of Anglesey

North Wales Police confirmed specialist officers are assisting a pilot's family after an aircraft crashed this afternoon. A search and rescue operation is still underway off Puffin Island, Anglesey

25 Nov 2019, 15:11 by Joseph Wilkes, https://www.facebook.com/dailymirror/, Sarah Hodgson

A pilot is missing after a light aircraft crashed into the water off a UK island this afternoon.

Police, the RNLI and the Coastguard have been involved in a frantic search and rescue mission since just before 1pm after a plane went down off Puffin Island, Anglesey, North Wales.

As of 5.55pm police have confirmed the light aircraft had "one pilot on board" and no other passengers.

Lifeboats are also involved in the operation after the plane "disappeared from radar contact" shortly before 1pm today.

Chief inspector Essi Ahari told reporters that "family liaison officers are supporting the missing pilot’s family".

He added: "The light aircraft was flying from Caernarfon Airport to the Great Orme in Llandudno and back this afternoon."

Police were initially called at 12.59pm to reports of a "possible crash".

North Wales Live reports it the light aircraft was most likely privately owned.

A spokesperson for the Coastguard told Mirror Online: "HM Coastguard is currently co-ordinating a search and rescue in the Puffin Island area of North Wales following a report at 12.47pm today that an aircraft had disappeared from radar contact.

"The search and rescue helicopter from Caernarfon has been sent as well as Coastguard Rescue Teams from Penmon and Bangor and a Senior Coastguard Operations Officer.

"The RNLI lifeboats from Beaumaris, Moelfre and Llandudno and North Wales Police have been sent."

The search was continuing in the dark as of 6pm.

FlightRadar24, which tracks the path of aircraft in the UK, showed a Cessna 172S Skyhawk had gone down.

One twitter user tweeted: "I was just looking on playback and it literally fell out of the sky."

A picture from the scene shows a large-scale police presence at the waterfront.

Police said: "We received a call at 12.59pm today (Monday) reporting a possible crash involving a light aircraft in the Penmon area. Officers are currently assisting HM Coastguard and our enquiries are ongoing."

The plane is believed to have taken off from Caernarfon Airport before it vanished.

Flight data from FlightRadar24 appears to show the Cessna light aircraft taking off from the Gwynedd airport before heading north east towards Llandudno.

As it flies over Llandudno , it begins to turn back and heads towards Anglesey , before the radar loses track of the plane at around the time the alarm was raised.

It has not been confirmed whether this is the missing plane by officials.

The Air Accident Investigation Branch has said that they have sent a team to investigate “an accident involving a light aircraft” that occurred near Beaumaris.

North Wales Fire and Rescue Service said they were not involved in the efforts to locate the aircraft.

The Welsh Ambulance Service said they have not yet been called out the assist at the scene.

North Wales Police released a photo of the Coastguard helicopter hovering over the water off the coast of Anglesey.

An RNLI spokesman said the aircraft was last seen two miles north east of Penmon.

The charity launched two lifeboats from Anglesey.

An RNLI spokesman said: "The volunteer crews are searching an area around Penmon and are joined by Coastguard teams from Penmon and Bangor and a Coastguard helicopter."

As of 4.20pm, rescue crews remained off the coast of Anglesey near Puffin Island searching for the missing plane.

At 4.30pm, North Wales Police tweeted that their "enquiries are ongoing" and they were still "assisting the Coastguard".

At 4.50pm two lifeboats, from Llandudno and Moelfre, remained on the scene, continuing to criss-cross the water in their search.

As of 5.10pm a third lifeboat had joined them and the three crafts continue to carry out their search in pitch-black conditions.

As of 9.11pm, both the search and rescue helicopter and an RNLI crew had left the scene after focusing their search on specific spot just north of puffin Island. Just one RNLI crew remains at the scene now.

As of 9.43pm, all crews have left the scene and the search has ended for the evening, and is likely to resume tomorrow.
