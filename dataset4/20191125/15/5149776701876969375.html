Nursery where children care for farm animals and play in woods rated 'outstanding'

'They often tell their parents they don't want to come home'

25 Nov 2019, 15:58 by Phoebe Ram

A nursery where children regularly 'come home messy' has received outstanding feedback across the board.

Peapod Day Nursery in Hickling Pastures, south Nottinghamshire, was visited by education watchdog Ofsted on October 17.

Described by the manager as having a 'homely' atmosphere, the nursery has a team of six nursery staff who care for 39 children up to five years old.

During their visit, inspectors praised the "passion shown by the manager and staff" and commented on how children spent "as much time as possible learning outside".

Manager since 2013, Louise Slater has worked her way up from an apprentice when she joined Peapod Nursery in 2004.

The trained teacher said: "We are based on a smallholding and have beautiful outdoor facilities including a wooded area.

"We spend a lot of time outside which takes away the boundaries of walls and allows the children more freedom. It shows massive progress in their development."

The school received an 'outstanding' rating overall, as well as in each individual area. At its previous inspectin it had been rated 'good' overall.

Ms Slater explained how older children are able to enjoy outdoor cooking around the nursery's fire pit as well as enjoying story times.

The younger ones take part in mud clay making, collecting sticks for dens and going on book hunts.

The inspector said: "Children are really excited to visit the 'Forest School' area and get involved in finding insects or toasting marshmallows.

"Staff take great care to plan the environment so that children can play safely.Children's behaviour is exemplary."

Ms Slater added that children are also able to help care for some of the nearby farm animals, including their favourite Danny the Donkey, ponies, cows and calves.

She said: "I think it's important to provide that opportunity. It helps teach them to respect and how to care for animals.

"They also collect eggs from the hens and we have trips to the cattle market where they can see fresh produce and learn where their food comes from.

"Some parents have said they love how messy their children get and that they often tell their parents they don't want to come home."

Staff were also praised for their 'dedication' to the children and how they worked "exceptionally well together".

The report also highlighted how well staff knew the children adding "they are well-versed in what their children know and are interested in".

Ms Slater added: "We're a great team and everyone is highly qualified. I have to thank my deputy. It was good to hear that staff felt supported.

"I was really proud and excited to hear the result, it just doesn't feel real. We all work so hard and it's so nice to be recognised."
