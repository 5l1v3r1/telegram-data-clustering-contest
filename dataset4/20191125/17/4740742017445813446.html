Stream through park in St Werburgh's turns 'milky white'

There are concerns over impact on environment

25 Nov 2019, 17:02 by Alexander Brock

Residents have been left baffled and concerned after a stream in a park abruptly turned "milky white".

The water in Mina Road Park in St Werburgh's in Bristol suddenly changed colour on Monday afternoon (November 25), leaving people living nearby anxious about possible impacts on wildlife.

The Environment Agency is yet to respond to Bristol Live on the issue.

Molly Gilbert, who lives next to the stream, said the strange phenomenon has happened several times in the past.

The 42-year-old said: "It's horrible and makes me want to cry.

"We have got a a very fragile ecosystem in the park with the sticklebacks and Kingfishers.

"It could have a horrible impact and that's awful.

"Its about time someone looked into this as the problem as it's so intermittent that I think people forget about it."

She went on to say that the milky water smelt like "chemicals" and that the substance in the water often disappears within minutes.

Hugh Holden, from the Mina Road Parkgroup, added the mysterious occurrence happens "fairly regularly".

A spokesman for Bristol City Council has said it is not something the council had been made aware of previously.

The Environment Agency has been contacted for comment.
