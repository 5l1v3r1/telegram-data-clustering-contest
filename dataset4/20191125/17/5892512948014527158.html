Second suspect arrested, charged in Aniah Blanchard missing person case for ‘disposing of evidence’

25 Nov 2019, 17:37 by Jesse Holland

***UPDATE: Police believe to have found the remains of Aniah Blanchard (details here)

More than a month after Aniah Blanchard went missing in Auburn, Alabama, a second suspect has now been arrested and charged in connection with her disappearance, according to WRBL.com, for what the police affidavit describes as “providing transportation” and “disposing of evidence.”

Blanchard is the stepdaughter of UFC heavyweight Walt Harris.

Antwon “Squirmy” Fisher, 35, was apprehended by U.S. Marshals last Friday night (Nov. 22, 2019) and joins 30-year-old Ibraheem Yazeed behind bars. Judge Steve Speakman denied bond based on Fisher’s extensive criminal history, which includes three years served for capital murder back in 2005.

Forensic evidence recovered from Blanchard’s car suggests foul play.

Yazeed also has a rap sheet that will make your head spin — including murder — and yet both men were free to come and go as they please while a mother of four got 12 years in Oklahoma City for selling $30 worth of pot. The United States criminal justice system was, is, and may forever be an unfixable mess that often does more harm than good.

A preliminary hearing is set for December 18th.

Anyone with information as to Blanchard’s whereabouts is asked to contact the Auburn Police Division Detective Section at 334-501-3140, the anonymous tip line at 334-246-1391, or the 24-hour non-emergency number at 334-501-3100.
