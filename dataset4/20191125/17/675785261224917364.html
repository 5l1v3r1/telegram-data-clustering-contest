Gary Neville tells Man Utd they need "five or six signings" in January transfer window

Manchester United boss Ole Gunnar Solskjaer has been warned by club legend Gary Neville he needs several new additions in the transfer window to strengthen his struggling side

25 Nov 2019, 17:01 by Rich Jones, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/rich-jones/

Manchester United have been told they need "five or six signings" to turn things around in January by former player Gary Neville.

Neville watched on in frustration in his role as co-commentator on Sky Sports as United drew 3-3 with Sheffield United in a frantic Bramall Lane encounter on Sunday.

Ole Gunnar Solskjaer's side came from 2-0 down to take a 3-2 lead only to concede a late equaliser, leaving them ninth in the Premier League table.

And he outlined the need for a number of signings in order to strengthen their side to a sufficient level to climb up the table - pinpointing three specific positions which need urgent attention.

“They need a striker, they need two midfield players, they need a left-back – they need five or six signings," Neville said.

“They can’t do that until January and obviously the summer but the midfield will not go away as a problem for Manchester United just because of an injury.

“It’s a problem full stop – it needs strengthening.”

Whilst Neville sees improvement needed in plenty of areas, he highlighted central midfield as the biggest cause for concern.

“The midfield’s just a problem for United full stop, let’s be clear about it,” he said.

“Even with Scott McTominay in it’s a problem. They haven’t strengthened in that area, obviously Pogba’s injured.

“It’s a problem for them and it’s just not good enough, the midfield just doesn’t have good enough quality.”

Injuries have played a part, but Fred and Andreas Pereira have struggled to make an impact as stand-ins for McTominay and Pogba.

A host of players have been linked with the Red Devils in January, including midfielders such as Sean Longstaff, Declan Rice, James Maddison, Nicolo Zaniolo and Eduardo Camavinga amongst others.

Strikers including Erling Braut Haaland, Mario Mandzukic, Moussa Dembele and Timo Werner have also been linked ahead of their latest transfer window.
