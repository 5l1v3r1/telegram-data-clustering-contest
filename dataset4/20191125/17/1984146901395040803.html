Shields Road shop closed down after continuing to sell illegal tobacco despite 'frequent' raids

During Newcastle City Council's latest raid on the Byker premises, 15,700 cigarettes and 3.5kg of hand rolling tobacco was seized.

25 Nov 2019, 17:26 by Hannah Graham

A shop which refused to stop selling illegal tobacco has been closed by a court.

Potraviny, a European mini-market on Shields Road , in Byker, Newcastle, has been handed a three-month closure order after Newcastle Magistrates' Court heard it had become a hotbed of "repeated illegal activity".

Newcastle City Council officers say they had carried out "frequent" visits to the shop and seized unlicensed tobacco, but owners continued to peddle the illegal products.

The court heard on Thursday how Trading Standards officers had seized 15,700 cigarettes and 3.5kg of hand rolling tobacco in their latest raid on the premises.

Alongside the three-month closure order, magistrates ordered three men involved with the business - Frantisek Veris, of Shields Road, Byker; Ronaldo Luis Khoudary, of Stanton Road, Arthur's Hill and Mohammed Abdul Kadir, of HMP Northumberland - to pay a total of £2,750 in costs. 

David Ellerington, the council's trading standards lead, said: “Frequent visits to this shop had resulted in the seizure of illegal cigarettes and tobacco, but despite this enforcement action, this business has continued to sell them.

“We took the decision that in order to make it clear that this illegal activity will not be accepted in Newcastle we had to go further and I’m pleased the courts have agreed with us.

“While all smoking is dangerous, illegal tobacco often doesn’t come with health warnings and is sold much cheaper than the real product, leading to young people becoming addicted.

“We also know it has links to organised crime and breeds anti-social behaviour in our communities. I sincerely hope this delivers a clear message that we will continue to take action against those who refuse to comply with the law.”

Newcastle City Council has urged anyone with information about the sale of illegal tobacco to report it, anonymously, to the Keep It Out campaign at  https://keep-it-out.co.uk/ .
