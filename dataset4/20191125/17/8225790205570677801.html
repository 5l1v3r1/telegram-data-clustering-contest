Speed, inattention were factors in a deadly Big Island crash, police say

25 Nov 2019, 17:16 by HNN Staff

HONOKAA, Hawaii (HawaiiNewsNow) - One person is dead and another seriously injured after an apparent head-on collision in Honokaa on Monday morning, Hawaii County officials say.

The crash, around 6 a.m., happened near mile marker 46 on Highway 19, according to police.

The man killed was identified Monday as 24-year-old Lloyd W. Edwards of Honoka ªa.

Officials said they arrived on scene to find extensive damage to a Dodge pickup truck and Honda Civic.

Investigators say Edwards was behind the wheel of the Hilo-bound Civic when he crossed the center line and slammed into the oncoming truck.

Edwards was not responsive when crews arrived. He was taken to the Hilo Medical Center where he was pronounced dead.

The driver of the Civic was also hospitalized and has since been upgraded to stable condition.

Both lanes of the highway were shut down for the investigation, but have since reopened.

Police believe speed and inattention were factors in the crash.

A negligent homicide investigation has been launched. Witnesses are being asked to contact Officer Blayne Matsui at (808) 961-2339.

This is the 23rd traffic fatality this year compared to 28 at this time last year.
