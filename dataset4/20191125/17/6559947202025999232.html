When Does Black Friday 2019 Start?

Officially, Black Friday 2019 kicks off on November 29. The biggest offers for the shopping event will run throughout the weekend and end on the Monday. Some deals will likely run into Cyber Monday, and possibly beyond, as the two events are now basically the same thing.

Black Friday is another misnomer in the shopping event world, and the scale of the event has continued to grow, turning into what is essentially a whole month of deals.

We predict you'll be getting at least one week worth of Black Friday bargains. Amazon will begin its Black Friday bargains on November 22, while Cyber Monday sales will extend past December 2 as well, so there are plenty of deals to choose from.

What is Black Friday and Cyber Monday?

What used to be an exclusively US based event after Thanksgiving, Black Friday has transformed the UK shopping market into something else, and effectively created a shopping holiday for the whole country.

Unlike the US, the UK seems more focused on online shopping rather than the typical 'Doorbuster' sales that can occur on the other side of the Atlantic.

Cyber Monday is simply a continuation of Black Friday, although it used to be separated by a difference in products, you shouldn't expect this in 2019.

November has become the month to buy tech in the UK, especially when it comes to Christmas gifts. The BBC reported last year that December shopping figures fell as a direct result of Black Friday sales.

How Long Does Black Friday Last?

As we've previously mentioned, Black Friday has rapidly transformed from a single day into a much larger event.

To spread out the time of sales, most retailers begin cutting prices much earlier than the intended sale date. This is why it can be slightly confusing as to when Black Friday actually begins.

To a certain degree, ignore the singular date, and expect sales from November to at least the beginning of December. The biggest and best deals will still occur during or around the Black Friday weekend, but there will always be plenty of other bargains hanging around for the rest of the month.
