Former Nottingham Forest striker enjoying new lease of life following City Ground exit

Latest Nottingham Forest news | The 36-year-old left the Reds in the summer and went on to sign for League One side Bolton Wanderers

25 Nov 2019, 17:30 by Mark Wakefield

Former Nottingham Forest striker Daryl Murphy is enjoying a new lease of life since his departure from the City Ground.

The 36-year-old left the Reds by mutual consent on transfer deadline day in September, and went on to sign for League One club Bolton Wanderers.

Prior to his Forest exit, the Republic of Ireland international scored 13 goals in 60 appearances for the Reds after his move to Trentside in 2017.

Murphy did not make a single first-team appearance for the Reds under manager Sabri Lamouchi, with his last outing coming against current club Bolton on the final day of last season.

Now at Bolton, the experienced striker has been in a rich vein of form for the Trotters in recent matches.

Murphy opened his account for Bolton in their 2-0 win over Bristol Rovers in October, and followed that up with what proved to be a winning goal in a 2-1 victory over Fleetwood Town earlier this month.
