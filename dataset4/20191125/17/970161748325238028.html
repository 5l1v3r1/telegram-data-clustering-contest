Where is Bikram Choudhury now after Netflix documentary sparks outrage?

25 Nov 2019, 17:29

Controversial new Netflix documentary Bikram: Yogi, Guru, Predator, has gripped viewers as it details a string of racial, verbal and sexual abuse allegations against the yoga teacher.

Bikram Choudhury, who launched Bikram Yoga in the 70s, became a star thanks to his craft and exercise classes, which he famously taught in just a pair of black Speedos.

While the documentary is seemingly damning, the 75-year-old exercise teacher has continued to deny the accusations made against him, and still teaches yoga to this day to a dedicated fan base.

Here’s where he is now, and why followers devoted to his practices refuse to let him go…

Where is Bikram Choudhury now?

Bikram Choudhury fled the US after losing a discrimination and harassment lawsuit against his former lawyer, Micki Jaffa-Bodden.

She was awarded $7million (£5.4million) by a jury, which he did not pay.

In January 2020, he is launching a 15-day tour of India with his followers, leading participants through Delhi through to Kolkata via Mumbai and Jodhpur.

Those who take part will be put up in hotels with Bikram, and every morning will start with a two-hour yoga session with him, at a total cost of $3950 (£3000).

It’s titled ‘Bikran’s India Legacy Tour’.

Of the six women who have accused Bikram of sexual assault, four have been settled out of court.

What has Bikram Choudhury said about the documentary?

A representative for Bikram told LA Times: ‘Bikram Choudhury totally refutes all the allegations of sexual misconduct and harassment presented in the film and is deeply upset by the continued character assassination.

‘Bikram believes that the concerted effort by money-motivated lawyers to proactively send letters to a database of all his clients, offering people free legal representation and the promise of $1 million insurance policy pay-outs is the primary motivation for this reputational catastrophe.

‘Bikram believes the Netflix film is nothing more than a repetition of old material.’

Bikram: Yogi, Guru, Predator is available now on Netflix.
