Deluded law student puts lives at risk by starting fire at her Plymouth home

Chichi Ofoegbu detained for psychiatric help after using cooking oil to start blaze

25 Nov 2019, 17:28 by Stuart Abel

A deluded student put lives at risk by setting fire to her Plymouth flat in the night after pouring cooking oil over the room, a court heard.

Seven neighbouring flats in the block had to be evacuated but hardly any damage was caused.

Chichi Ofoegbu, studying law at the University of Plymouth, admitted starting the fire at her home in St Judes, the city's Crown Court heard.

She pleaded guilty to arson reckless as to whether life was endangered in Seymour Avenue on May 11.

Ofoegbu also admitted causing criminal damage to the taxi on May 6.

She has since been held for treatment at a psychiatric unit.

Judge James Townsend, after reading two reports from doctors, told her: “I have to sentence you for what is a serious offence of arson potentially putting the lives of others in danger.

"It is quite clear that when you committed this offence and, indeed the previous criminal damage, you were in the grip of a psychotic episode.

“It is sad to see you here. You are of good character and have never been in trouble before. You are someone with considerable promise. I hope you will be released in the near future.”

The judge passed a Hospital Order under the Mental Health Act which will see Ofoegbu detained for treatment under doctors consider it is safe for her to be released.
