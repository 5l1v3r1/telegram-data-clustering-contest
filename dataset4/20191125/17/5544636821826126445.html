NPFL: Kano Pillars’ ‘Pele’ Free To Face League Leaders

25 Nov 2019, 17:36 by Ridwan Yusuf

The League Management Company (LMC) has lifted the twelve matches ban imposed on Kano Pillars talisman and Captain, Rabiu Ali aka Pele during the 2018/2019 Nigeria Professional Football League (NPFL) Super Six with effect from Monday 25th November, 2019.

Concise News reports that this is according to the media officer of the national champions, Rilwanu Idris Malikawa.

In a statement signed by the Chief Operating Officer of the LMC, Malam Salihu Abubakar on Monday the League body said it has decided to lift the suspension based on the written apology it received from Ali and Kano Pillars FC and the community services Ali took part in during the Ahlan Cup which indicates his remorse regarding what happened during the 2019 Super Six.

The statement said that Ali will now be available to face league leaders Plateau United of Jos in the Match Day Six cracker to be played on Sunday 1st December, 2019.

Furthermore, the LMC cautioned the experienced player to henceforth be a good ambassador of Kano Pillars, the Nigerian league, as well as his community.

On their part, Kano Pillars, under the leadership of Alhaji Surajo Shuaibu Yahaya has expressed their gratitude to the LMC for considering their request to lift the ban. Sai Masu Gida assured the LMC that the club would remain law-abiding in all of its engagement.

While speaking to Kano Pillars’ official website, the player in question, Ali expressed his satisfaction on his availability.

He thanked the LMC and urged the Sai Masu Gida fans to remain calm, assuring them that the struggling club will find it’s rhythm.

It would be recalled that Ali was banned for twelve domestic games by the LMC during the 2018/2019 Super Six in Lagos.
