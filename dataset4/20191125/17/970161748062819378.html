Iain Duncan Smith's office vandalised with 'Tory cuts kill' graffiti

25 Nov 2019, 17:43

Iain Duncan Smith has slammed the ‘absolute morons’ who graffitied his constituency office with the words ‘Tory cuts kill’.

One of the office windows was also sprayed with the words ‘Tories out’.

The former secretary for the Department of Work and Pensions, says the incident is one of a number of ‘threats and death threats made to him and his family’ in recent months.

The former Tory leader told the Evening Standard: ‘I have had violent threats, death threats to myself, threats to my family in the last few months.

‘All these people are absolute morons – if they are a member of a political party they should be disowned.

‘It is appalling that some people think they can shut down democracy.’

Mr Duncan Smith took to Twitter to express his outrage after discovering the graffiti as he arrived at his office this morning.

He Tweeted: ‘All opposition candidates must condemn such intimidation & criminal behaviour.

‘I have news for these democracy-hating thugs: this will never stop us from campaigning & delivering a @Conservatives majority government.’

Mr Duncan Smith insisted that the ‘threats and intimidation’ were not going to deter him from his re-election campaign in Chingford and Woodford Green.

He also highlighted how other Tory colleagues had also received death threats.
