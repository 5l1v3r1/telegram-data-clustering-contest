Good Samaritan takes dog on cross-country trip to reunite him with dying owner

25 Nov 2019, 17:56

A good Samaritan took a dog on a cross-country road trip to reunite him with his dying owner.

Bailey’s owner was recently diagnosed with a terminal illness and traveled from Minnesota to Washington State to receive treatment and live out her remaining days among friends.

The owner, who was not named, tried to take Bailey with her, but the dog was not allowed on the plane because of health issues associated with his breed.

Bailey was not allowed on the plane because he is a pug mix known to have problems breathing the air on planes – which is when Ryan Chukuske stepped in to take Bailey approximately 1,500 miles to be with his owner.

Chukuske works with Spot’s Last Canine Rescue and his wife, Megan Giesen, began a GoFundMe to help pay for the car rental, fuel, hotels, and flight home, which the Chukuske offered to pay for himself.
