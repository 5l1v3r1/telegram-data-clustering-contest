I'm a Celeb's Andy Whyment relentlessly bullied for loving drama and dancing

I'm a Celebrity star Andy Whyment was bullied so badly for his love of dancing he was nearly removed from school

25 Nov 2019, 09:03 by Irishmirror.ie

I'm a Celebrity star Andy Whyment, 38, seems laid back and confident during his appearance on the show.

But the Coronation Street actor hasn't had an easy ride to fame, and was once so badly bullied his dad considered moving him to another school.

Andy, who plays Kirk Sutherland on the ITV soap, was passionate about acting and dancing from a young age.

He began ballet aged eight, but other kids at his Bolton school were jealous and would relentlessly tease him.

“It was ­verbal rather than physical, but once one started a few of them would join in", Andy said.

"It was jealousy more than anything else. I was ­doing something with my life and they were just hanging about the street corners getting hammered every week.

“At one stage my dad was thinking of pulling me out of the school.

“But I told him I didn’t want him to, because if I left then they’d have won. So I stayed and got on with it.”

Andy has been on TV since he was a teenager, starting with roles on shows including Where The Heart Is and The Royle Family.

He also sang in a rock band and busked in Manchester for three years so he didn't have to "sponge off" his parents.

Andy said his bullies were "hanging around on street corners and I was doing something with my life".

He added that his acting success means he would always encourage kids now to follow their dreams.

"At the end of the day, I’ve done quite well out of it", he said.

“Always stick to your dreams. I was bullied because I was different and wanted to be an actor.

“If you are being bullied don’t let it get you down because bullies quickly become bored and move on, and speak to an adult you trust.”

Andy only entered the jungle camp on Thursday, but he's already become the new favourite to win the show.

He won the hearts of the viewers with his rolling commentary during a Bushtucker Trial which saw Cliff Parisi try to avoid Buffallos and lucky dips his way through to find his five stars.

Andy’s commentary and comparing it to “just like watching it on the telly” had hosts Ant and Dec in stitches throughout before being sent to Jungle Jail by the sheriff.
