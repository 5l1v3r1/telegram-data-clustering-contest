"My dad has 15 years to live due to incurable brain condition - and I may have it too"

Dale Piggin, 42, from Leicestershire, was diagnosed with Huntington's disease in September - and he now fears he has passed the genetic condition to his family members

25 Nov 2019, 09:27 by Tom Mack

A teenage boy has revealed his fears that he will develop the same incurable brain condition that his father has been diagnosed with.

Lorry driver Dale Piggin of Thurmaston, near Leicester, was told weeks ago that he was suffering from Huntington’s disease.

The condition affects sufferer’s ability to control their own bodies and eventually kills them, reports LeicestershireLive .

As a result, the 42-year-old already struggles to keep a grip on objects and is suffering dementia-like symptoms.

His family fear that he has just 15 years until the degenerative condition eventually kills him.

His 16-year-old son, Callum, has been told he has a 50-50 chance of having the disease but has to wait until he turns 18 in May 2021 before he finds out.

Because Huntington’s cannot be treated, children at risk of the condition are better off psychologically if they do not know whether or not they have the illness.

He said: “Dad is stressed and really not great but he’s trying to stay happy. He can't drive but he's looking for a new job he can do.

“He has a lot of symptoms now. He twitches a lot and he drops things because he can't grip well.

“It’s really bad to see him go through it all but also bad because I could get it and I can’t get tested until I turn 18.”

Callum's older brother Reece has two children and another on the way and is concerned about the impact that the disease might have on his family.

The teen added: “It’s like having Alzheimer's, dementia, motor neurone disease and Parkinson's all at once.

“I’m heartbroken for my dad but my mum now has to watch my dad slowly die because of this cruel disease and then possibly me and my brother too.”

Dale said the thought of having passed the disease is horrifying and says he had not heard about the debilitating condition until his diagnosis.

He said: "It's just really depressing and stressful but what's really gutting is that I might have passed it to my sons or my grandchildren.

"I didn't know anything about the disease until a few weeks ago and it's been a huge shock.

"Now it's getting hard to control my body and people notice so I tend not to want to go out of the house any more."

The first signs arrived four years ago.

Dale had been carrying some drinks when his elbow started shooting up in the air.

His wife, Vicky, said: "We were on holiday and he was walking back from the bar and his elbow was shooting up.

"Music was playing and it was like he was dancing so we were laughing."

It took years for the true cause of the symptoms to be revealed.

In September, a new neurologist saw Dale and his first thought was Huntington's.

Usually people being diagnosed with the disease nowadays are partly prepared for it because a relative already has it.

But Dale does not know of anyone in his family who has been diagnosed with it.

Vicky said: “Now it's been diagnosed it's sad and horrible and we can only be grateful he may have about 15 years before it kills him - compared to other illnesses other people get diagnosed with, it's a lot of time still to spend with his family, even though he won't remember most of it."

Callum has shared his family's story to raise money so they can make changes to their home.

Making major improvements to the house - including installing a bathroom and a stairlift will have a huge affect on how long Dale can live at home.

GoFundMe page  has already raised more than £1,300 in just five days.

Callum said: “We only shared the page around family and friends to start with and it was a big surprise when we raised so much.

“I’m not sure how it’s been so good – people are being really generous and giving more than we expected.”
