Gardaí launch murder investigation after man is shot dead in Coolock

25 Nov 2019, 09:08 by James Cox

Gardaí have launched a murder investigation after a man was shot dead in Coolock, north Dublin on Sunday.

The victim was named locally last night as Eoin Boylan.

Boylan, 22, died at Beaumont Hospital after a "number of apparent gunshot wounds".

The shooting took place on Clonshaugh Avenue in Dublin 17 at approximately 5:15pm on Sunday.

Gardaí suspect that the murder is linked to the ongoing gang feud in Coolock.

Minister for Justice Charlie Flanagan has condemned the attack.

“I was deeply concerned to hear of the shooting this evening in the Clonshaugh area. I condemn this wanton violence,” he said.

“I urge anyone with information to please contact An Garda Síochána and assist them with their investigation.”

Gardaí in Coolock opened a murder inquiry on Sunday evening.

A Garda statement reads: “Members of the Garda Technical Bureau are currently examining the scene and an incident room has been established at Coolock Garda station.

“The coroner has been notified and the services of the State Pathologist have been requested. Arrangements are being made for a postmortem examination.”

Gardaí in Coolock have appealed for any witnesses to come forward.

Any dashcam footage recorded in the vicinity of Clonshaugh Avenue between 4.30pm and 5.30pm on Sunday should be handed over to gardaí.
