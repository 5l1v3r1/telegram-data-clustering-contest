IN THE MIX

Little Mix announce Summer 2020 stadium tour with three Scottish shows – including opening night in Falkirk

25 Nov 2019, 09:40 by Ewan Mowat

LITTLE Mix have announced the dates for their upcoming UK tour - which starts and ends in Scotland.

The pop sensations will kick off their Summer 2020 shows at The Falkirk Stadium on Friday June 26 next year.

Jade, Jesy, Leigh-Anne and Perrie will play 21 gigs throughout the UK during their five week tour.

The stunning group will wrap up with two more Scottish shows.

On Saturday July 25, the band will play Ayr Racecourse before bringing their tour to a close the following night at Aberdeen's Hazelhead Park.

Speaking about the Summer 2020 Tour, the group said: “Performing live is our favourite thing to do as a band, we love it.

“Our last Summer tour in 2018 was one of our favourites ever, so we can’t wait for some more brilliant outdoor shows next year.

"We want everyone to come party with us in the sunshine!”

Tickets are on sale at 9am on Thursday November 28 at www.gigsinscotland.com.

At least 75p from every brief will be donated to Nordoff Robbins - a charity that uses music therapy to help people affected by life-limiting illness, isolation or disability.

Fans will be treated to a show filled with the chart-toppers' greatest hits like Touch, Black Magic and Shout Out To My Ex.

The girlband have sold more than 50million records worldwide since their formation in 2011.

They have also racked up more than 17 million Spotify listeners, received more than 3 billion Youtube views, and collated 12 billion streams worldwide.

Little Mix have bagged four UK No.1s and four platinum-selling albums.

The girls played three nights at Glasgow's SSE Hydro last month and played the AECC in Aberdeen during their summer tour last year.

But the outdoor gig turned into a nightmare for some fans as they had to lift metal fences to get out the venue.

Young girls were left "traumatised" as the crowd began to crush as they tried to leave.

One mum said it was a "free for all" as punters tried to exit the venue.
