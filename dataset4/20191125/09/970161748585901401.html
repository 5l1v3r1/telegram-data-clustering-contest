Storm Sebastien on course to crash into Britain with life-threatening weather

25 Nov 2019, 09:02

The Met Office has issued a ‘danger to life’ flood warning as storm Sebastien heads to the UK.

Rain alerts are also in place across much of South-West England for the next three days, ahead of the storm’s arrival which is expected to bring two inches of rain and 50mph winds on Tuesday.

Storm Sebastien is expected to bring travel chaos to large parts of the country when it makes landfall, hitting already flooded areas with heavy rain.

Huge waves are expected to crash into the coast of South-West England today.

A Met office forecaster told Metro.co.uk: ‘Over the next three days there will be a lot of unsettled weather.

‘There is a band of rain pushing north eastwards with heavy showers today.

‘Storm Sebastian is expected to make landfall tomorrow, with a band of heavy rain pushing across the UK.’

Today will be mostly cloudy with heavy rain forecast for Southern England and Wales, followed by sunny spells.

However, heavy rain will hit the southwest by dawn.

As storm Sebastien makes landfall, tomorrow will see rain move North-East across many parts of the country.

Most of the heavy rain will fall in Southern England, with as much as 60mm of rain hitting the region.

Showers will also follow into the South-West, where strong winds are expected.

Further low pressure on Wednesday is expected to bring outbreaks of heavy rain, especially across southern England and northern Scotland.

The Met Office has issued three rain warnings over the next three days.

The first for south-west England and south Wales ends at 10am today.

A second alert will be in force across South-West, South and South-East England from midday tomorrow to 3pm on Wednesday.

Up to 60mm of rain is expected to fall in some spots, equating to roughly two weeks of rain.

A final ‘danger to life’ flood warning will be put in place in Yorkshire from the midnight to 6pm on Wednesday.
