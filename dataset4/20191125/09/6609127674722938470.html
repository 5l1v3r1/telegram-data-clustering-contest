SIX SHOTS

Coolock shooting – Young thug, 22, blasted to death in front garden of his home named as Eoin Boylan

25 Nov 2019, 09:04 by John Hand

A YOUNG yob was last night whacked in his front garden as part of a bloody gangland feud.

Thug Eoin Boylan, 22, was blasted six times in the upper body outside his home on ­Clonshaugh ­Avenue, in the capital, at around 5.15pm.

The murder victim is known to the ­Gardai and it is believed the ­brutal attack is linked to an ­ongoing feud in the Coolock area.

Paramedics and gardai rushed to the scene, where they worked to save the man’s life at the scene.

He was rushed to Beaumont Hospital, where he was last night pronounced dead by medics.

Armed cops last night closed off the shooting scene where forensic and technical examinations were set to be carried out.

Gardai believe that Boylan was attacked by at least two gunmen, who fled the scene in a car.

The Irish Sun understands he was aware that his life was under threat before yesterday’s attack.

It is believed cops recently thwarted a hit on Boylan after they stopped a vehicle and seized a gun.

They’re probing whether Boylan was targeted by associates of slain drug dealer Sean Little.

He was found riddled beside a burning car in Balbriggan in May.

COOLOCK FEUD

Little was whacked just 24 hours after another pal was shot dead in Darndale and months after close friend Zach Parker was gunned down outside a gym in Swords.

The Coolock feud has seen a major escalation in the last few months with tit-for-tat incidents, including drive by shootings and houses targeted by fire bombs.

Another theory last night being probed is that Boylan’s murder may be linked to the Drogheda feud.

It’s believed Boylan was also playing a role in the dispute which has been ongoing since July 2018.

Local councillor Alison Gilliland, who lives just up the road from last night’s shooting, told the Irish Sun residents are terrified.

'NERVOUSNESS IN AREA'

She said: “This is a quiet settled area but that road is busy and there’s always people going up and down it.

"One of them could have been injured or even worse.

“It’s always very concerning to hear gun shots outside your own window or your own door.

“There is a lot of nervousness in the area about where is the next incident is going to be.

People are saying, ‘Could I be walking along the footpath when it happens or my kids or anyone from my family and then they’re accidentally hit from a stray bullet or something’.”

Councillor Racheal Batten added: “The worst part of it is that it is not shocking and there will be children walking to school tomorrow morning past Garda tape and asking what happened.

'CONDEMN THIS WANTON VIOLENCE'

Justice Minister Charlie Flanagan last night condemned the killing.

He said: “I was deeply concerned to hear of the shooting in the Clonshaugh area. I condemn this wanton ­violence.

"I urge anyone with ­information to please contact An Garda Siochana and assist them with their investigation.

“Anyone with information should contact Coolock Garda Station at 01 666 4200 or the Garda Confidential Line on 1800 666 111.”
