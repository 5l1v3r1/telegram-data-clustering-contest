Man arrested over alleged murder of Scots millionaire’s son ‘found stuffed in the boot of a car’ in California

25 Nov 2019, 09:35 by Blair Meikle

A MAN has been arrested in connection with the murder of a Scots millionaire's son in an alleged California gang shooting.

Adrian Bonar, 34, a former soldier in the US army, was found dead in the boot of a car at the side of a road in the city of Anaheim on October 17.

He lived about 75 miles away in Escondido, San Diego.

Now, The Times reports Antonio Silva, 27, is being held on suspicion of his murder after US officers raided his home and found a person who claimed to be the victim of a kidnapping, and also guns and drugs.

Adrian's dad Brian, 72, from Greenock, said fighting in Iraq sent his son spiralling into drug addiction and crime.

He said post-traumatic stress made him "get crazy" and make poor judgements.

An Anaheim police spokesman said: “Silva was taken into custody during the service of a search warrant at his residence. Amazingly, we encountered an adult male who had been kidnapped.

“We were able to rescue that person. Two rifles, two handguns and over four pounds of fentanyl [an opioid painkiller] were also found.”
