Kirstie Allsop declines Piers Morgan's GMB job offer

25 Nov 2019, 09:36

Kirstie Allsop has rejected Piers Morgan’s offer for a presenting job on Good Morning Britain as he continued to raise doubts about his future on the programme.

Piers wasted no time in asking whether the Location, Location, Location host wanted a position on the ITV show, with Susanna Reid pointing out that her on-screen partner could be about to leave for good.

‘It’s a natural progression for me,’ Piers insisted. ‘Glamourous brunette at the top of her game, a big TV star.’

He continued: ‘The question really is, Kirstie, if there is a vacancy, God forbid we’d lose Susanna but we’d all move on…’

Kirstie politely refused the role though, and graciously replied: ‘I would no more seek this job that someone with the patience of a saint does every day.’

‘Very diplomatic of you,’ Piers remarked.

Last week, Piers confirmed that he could quit Good Morning Britain in January ahead of the show’s time extension.

Piers’s future on the show comes as petitions were formed to get him out of the show over his outspoken views on gender fluidity, often slamming the ideas of the series.

Susanna has already been courting potential replacements on-air, joking Bill Turnbull could take over her co-host’s spot after leaving BBC Breakfast.

The pair even auditioned mini Piers and Susanna on today’s show as they continued raising suspicions and searched for potential replacements.

Susanna has previously joked that ITV ‘can’t afford’ Piers and he then began educating viewers about the importance of knowing their worth.

‘You have to understand your own value and then you have to stick to it,’ Piers said. ‘I value myself at X You value me at Y therefore, I’m taking my toys out of the pram and going to Z.’
