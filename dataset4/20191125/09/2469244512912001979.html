Man trapped under lawnmower fighting for life in hospital

He had to be airlifted to Derriford hospital

25 Nov 2019, 09:46 by Joel Cooper

A man who became trapped under a ride on lawnmower is understood to be fighting for his life in hospital this morning.

Fire crews, paramedics and an air ambulance were all called to the scene on the A377 near Umberleigh just before 4pm yesterday afternoon.

Firefighters removed the man from under the lawnmower before he was airlifted to Derriford Hospital in Plymouth.

It is understood he remains there this morning.

A spokesman for Devon and Somerset Fire and Rescue Service said: "One fire appliance from Chulmleigh and one from South Molton were sent to a report of a male person stuck beneath a ride on lawn mower at the above location.

"Liaising with paramedics, Fire crews released the injured casualty from beneath the lawn mower.Â 

"The male was taken to hospital by ambulance."

A spokesman for South Molton Fire Station took to Facebook to explain the role their crew played in the incident.

They said: "Fire crews assisted Devon air ambulance paramedics in conveying the male casualty from woodland to a waiting air ambulance.

"Fire crews lit up the landing site to enable the helicopter to take off safely and fly the casualty to Derriford hospital in Plymouth."

A spokesman for South Western Ambulance Service Trust said: "We were called at 3.57pm and attended with an air ambulance, a doctor, an ambulance and an operations officer.

"One patient was taken to hospital via air for further treatment."
