'Mowgli' girls rescued from squalid home where they ate styrofoam and wallpaper

Diana, 5, and Anya, 1, ate 'styrofoam, wallpaper, hair and other objects' to stay alive in foul flat that 'always had a terrible stench'

25 Nov 2019, 09:50 by Roksana Panashchuk, Lila Randall

Two young sisters were rescued from a ‘dirty’ flat after reportedly being neglected by their mother who spent her cash on alcohol.

Diana and Anya, aged five and one, did not eat properly and lived in an infested flat with their mother and her boyfriend in Dnipro, central Ukraine.

The 25-year-old mother - who has not been named - is said to have lived off the child benefits and only fed the children properly once a month.

The neglected sisters were found when a concerned neighbour called the police on November 21.

A female neighbour told local media: ''The woman and her boyfriend often quarrelled and fought with each other.

''They constantly shouted at the children and did not take care of them. The girls were always dirty and neglected.”

''There was always a horrible stench coming from their apartment.''

Diana and Anya were discovered on November 21 after neighbours called the police.

Police officers were reportedly shocked by the foul condition of the flat. The toilet was not working properly and the family used the bath tub instead.

The tub was covered with a thick layer of dirt and infested with worms.

A police told local media: ''The girls got used to sleeping in filthy bedclothes, eat from unwashed dishes and play on the littered floor.

''The stench in the rooms was horrific and it was difficult to breathe.''

Diana and Anya were hospitalised and diagnosed with malnutrition and lice.

Paediatrician Liya Segeda said: ''The girls' stool contains styrofoam, wallpaper, hair and other objects.

''They ate everything they could find in the apartment.”

Ms Segeda added that, while in hospital, it was the first time the children had clean bedclothes and saw a potty.

Police reported the incident to social services who are going to sue the mother and remove her parental rights.

Ludmila Kopylenko, a police spokeswoman, said: ''The mother and her boyfriend are unemployed. They spent child benefits for living.

''The children ate properly once per month. Their mother spent the rest of the money on alcohol.''
