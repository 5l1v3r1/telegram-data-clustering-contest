Hearts make their move to convince Daniel Stendel to take vacant Tynecastle post

25 Nov 2019, 22:30 by Gareth Law

HEARTS have made their move to convince Daniel Stendel to join them instead of waiting for a job in England.

SunSport understands the Jambos held talks with the German at the weekend.

We told last week how the ex-Barnsley boss was the new favourite for the Tynecastle hot seat.

But it became clear Ann Budge would have to push the boat out to tempt Stendel because of his preference to remain down south.

It is thought Budge has now made her pitch to Stendel having already spoken to Stuart McCall, Alan Irvine, Neil McCann and Steve Cotterill.

Stendel, 45, was axed as Tykes boss last month.

He’s under contract with them until the end of the season and must agree a severance package or Hearts may have to pay compensation

Meanwhile, Michael Smith has told his team-mates to toughen up mentally if they want to escape a relegation dogfight.

The Tynecastle side’s crushing 3-0 loss at Rugby Park left them just one point above bottom place, however, with St Johnstone drawing with Aberdeen there's now four teams level on 11 points.

Yet it was not so much the defeat, but the pitiful manner of it that has alarm bells ringing for Smith.

The Northern Ireland international reckons too many players felt they just had to turn up to collect the points, but they were in for a rude awakening.

Three goals inside seven extraordinary minutes at the start of the match brought Hearts crashing back to earth after their 5-2 win against St Mirren.
