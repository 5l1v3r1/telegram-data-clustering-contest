13 Festive Horror Movies You Need to Watch This Holiday Season

25 Nov 2019, 22:55 by Maggie Panos

Excited about the impending holidays, but not exactly raring to dig into those sappy Christmas movies? We have just the ticket! We've pulled together 13 holiday-themed horror movies that will probably make you terrified of Santa Claus — but in a fun way. A couple aren't terribly scary in case you're a bit of a wimp, but all of them will give you a dose of holiday cheer. Take a look and check out all the best horror movies on Netflix! (OK, in case you do want a bit of cheese with your wine, here are the best romances on Netflix, as well.)

1 Krampus

Adam Scott, Toni Collette, and Allison Tolman (Fargo) star in Krampus, the tale of a young boy who accidentally summons a demon on Christmas. It's funny, but if you know about the real legend, it'll also make you jump.

2 Santa's Slay

Get it? Like Santa's sleigh! The 2005 black comedy starts off when a murderous Santa ruins Christmas dinner by killing an entire family, including James Caan as the ill-fated father. Also involved: "hell-deer," the Canadian art of curling, and death by Zamboni.

3 Silent Night

How did you miss this 2012 gem featuring Jaime King, Malcolm McDowell, and Donal Logue? A murderous Santa is on the loose, but since it's Christmas Eve, tons of Santas are roaming the streets! Who's the real killer? You better hope they find out before more bodies drop.

4 Jack Frost

There's no way you don't want to see a movie about a serial killer who gets in a freak accident that results in his body fusing with snow. He becomes a killer snowman! He also rapes a girl, which makes this 1997 flick the most twisted movie on the list. In case you're wondering, there's a sequel: Jack Frost 2: Revenge of the Mutant Killer Snowman.

5 Edward Scissorhands

Edward Scissorhands is great year-round, but it's particularly relevant around Halloween and Christmas. A chunk of the movie takes place during the holiday season, when Edward (Johnny Depp) creates a giant ice sculpture of Kim (Winona Ryder). And, yes, this movie is scary, OK?

6 The Nightmare Before Christmas

Tim Burton's holiday classic, The Nightmare Before Christmas, also isn't a traditional horror movie, but it does have lots of scary elements — like basically everything in Halloween Town.

7 Silent Night, Deadly Night

This 1984 slasher film has spawned four sequels, and it's the inspiration for 2012's Silent Night, mentioned above. The movie features a young boy whose parents are murdered by a man in a Santa suit. The trauma changes him forever, and when he grows up, he goes on a killing spree of his own.

8 A Christmas Horror Story

What do you mean you don't even remember seeing commercials for this 2015 horror movie? Well, it's on demand now, so settle in for a night of William Shatner as a DJ host whose broadcast strings together four different nightmarish stories. Krampus makes an appearance in this one, too!

9 Silent Night, Bloody Night

If you inherit a house that was once an insane asylum, get rid of it as fast as possible. That's the message from 1974's Silent Night, Bloody Night. The movie follows an ax murderer who goes on a spree on Christmas Eve.

10 Black Christmas (1974)

You're in for a good time with 1974's dark classic Black Christmas. It's the story of a group of sorority girls who are picked off one by one by a murderer in the days leading up to Christmas, and it's often cited as an inspiration for Halloween.

11 Black Christmas (2006)

Katie Cassidy, Michelle Trachtenberg, and Oliver Hudson star in the equally terrifying (if not slightly campier) remake.

12 Christmas Evil

This 1980 slasher movie has earned a cult following in the decades since its release. When young Harry (Brandon Maggart) sees Santa touching his mom on Christmas Eve, he's scarred for life. (Don't worry, it's just his dad dressed up as Santa . . . but Harry doesn't know that.) He grows up, has a mental breakdown, and convinces himself that he's the real Santa — and the people on his "naughty" list need to be punished.

13 Gremlins

Gremlins (1984) may not be your typical holiday horror story, but Billy (Zach Galligan) does receive his new pet as a Christmas present. It's only later, when Billy stops following the three rules that Gizmo came with, that things start to get scary.
