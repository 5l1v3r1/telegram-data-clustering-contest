Pitt Stop

Dave Cormack has warned Aberdeen may be priced OUT of leaving Pittodrie

25 Nov 2019, 22:30 by Scott Davie

NEW Aberdeen chairman Dave Cormack has warned that the Dons may be priced OUT of leaving Pittodrie.

They had planned to move to a new stadium by 2023 — but Dons chief Cormack has revealed the flit could be delayed or even scrapped.

Cormack, who replaces Stewart Milne as Dons supremo next month, raised the majority of the £12.5million needed to fund their new training ground, Cormack Park.

But now the task is finding the £45m needed for a new stadium at the Kingsford complex.

It’s a job made tougher by the value of Pittodrie slumping to £11m as the economic downturn continues to hit the Granite City.

US-based tycoon Cormack’s contacts have brought in an additional £5m, including a new partnership with MLS outfit Atlanta United.

That will see the American side’s president, and former Spurs director, Darren Eales join the board.

When asked if Aberdeen might end up staying at Pittodrie, Cormack said: “You can never say never. If we can’t raise the money then we can’t do it.

“But we are confident our best opportunity is still to move to Kingsford.

"The 2023 date is moveable. I’m not fixated on two years or six years.

“But I want to make sure we are investing in the football team, as there is no way we are going to invest in a stadium and have a team which is ninth in the league.

“The critical factor for us is the performance of the first team. It’s a hard balancing act and £45m is a lot of money to raise.

“If you look at our recent report we’ve had Pittodrie valued, and there is no point in us trying to sell.

“We wouldn’t maximise the value of Pittodrie in the current downturn.

“Nobody can commit to money that isn’t there, but a new stadium is our best option.

“Let’s say we got £15m for the sale of Pittodrie, well Hearts spent £18m on building their new stand.

“It would cost us £55m to redevelop the stands here, so you weigh that up against £45m for a custom-built stadium.”

He reassured fans that Dons won’t try to raise funds quickly by selling top assets like Scott McKenna, Lewis Ferguson and Sam Cosgrove.

He added: “We have resisted offers for players. We did it last summer, and not just for Scott McKenna.

“But at the right time when there are players coming through, of course we will look at accepting offers.

“McKenna will move at some stage, but we have always said it has to be right for the player and Aberdeen."
