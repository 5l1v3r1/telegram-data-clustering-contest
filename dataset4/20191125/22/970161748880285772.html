Amanda Holden and Myleene Klass enjoy mums night out with champagne

25 Nov 2019, 22:21

Amanda Holden and Myleene Klass are mothers to young children so it’s no surprise the pair looked thrilled to enjoy evening off letting their hair down on a school night.

The radio presenters wisely left their partners at home to tuck in the kids as they attended Global’s Make Some Noise event in London on Monday night.

Myleene, who gave birth to her first son Apollo this summer, shimmered into the venue in a black and gold strapless gown, while Amanda kept it clean in a white asymmetrical jumpsuit.

The pair had each other for company as they walked the red carpet and seemed to share each other’s joy as they smiled and laughed with each other.

And the fun didn’t stop on the red carpet as Myleene, 41, shared a video of her bubbling champagne glass while singing her heart out to Paul Walker who was performing along with Stephen Miron.

Global’s Make Some Noise is a national charity which funds and empowers smaller charities across the UK, helping disadvantaged children and young people.

Elsewhere on the red carpet, Amanda also stopped to take photos with her Heart Breakfast co-host Jamie Theakston and with them both out on a Monday night, we won’t envy their heads tomorrow morning.

Other stars in attendance were Marvin Humes, Kelly Brook, Kirsty Gallacher and Georgia Toffolo, while singer Freya Ridings also did the honours of performing.

Smooth radio host Myleene spent some quality time with her baby boy hours before hitting the red carpet, and shared a cute video of the three-month-old snoring while having a good nap.

Apollo was probably tired out after being serenaded by his older sister, Myleene’s 12-year-old daughter Ava, who played the piano as he wriggled away in his baby chair.

‘He gets a private concert every morning! Some blues for Apollo this morning,’ Myleene captioned the adorable Instagram story.

Detailing Apollo’s ‘complicated’ birth back in August, Myleene recently revealed how she ‘lost five hours of her life’.
