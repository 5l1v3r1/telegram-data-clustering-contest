Daniel O'Donnell opens up about the death of his mother Julia

"It would be nice to see her again in heaven"

25 Nov 2019, 22:07 by Kayla Walsh

Daniel O'Donnell has opened up about the death of his mother, admitting he'd love to see her again in heaven - but he doesn't think that will happen.

Speaking to Ryan Tubridy on RTÉ Radio 1 today, he said: "It would be nice to see people that have gone before us.

"But I think people who are dead, of course you don't see them physically.

"They're there in your memories."

Daniel's mother Julia passed away at the age of 94 and the singer said that while he was very sad to lose her, she lived to a "great age".

"She was very alert, she was very much involved in everything we were doing," he went on.

"If she had another 20 years it would be great too. But her body just wore out.

"In the latter part of her life, every day there was something small wrong with her that we could do nothing about."

Speaking about religion, the star of Daniel and Majella's B&B Road Trip said he still goes to mass every Sunday.

"I do try to go every weekend, Saturday night or Sunday morning," he said.

"I get something out of going to mass. I want to believe that God is there, it gives me a good feeling.

He added: "If you dissect it all down it's unbelievable, how did all this happen?

"Mary became pregnant by the holy spirit and Jesus assumed up...If you want to poke holes in it it's very easy to.

"But I get a comfort out of the idea that there's something there that helps me along the way."

Daniel also paid tribute to Gay Byrne, saying he was always "gracious" and helpful to him.

"He was such a huge part of all of our lives growing up. From my own point of view, he was very helpful to me.

"Getting on the Late Late Show is a huge thing. He was very gracious and took me on.

"He interviewed me, I hadn't a lot to say that time. It's a huge loss, it's the end of an era."
