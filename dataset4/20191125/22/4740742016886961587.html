What the Met Office says about 'coldest winter for 100 years'

There will be snow, but where?

25 Nov 2019, 22:00 by Emma Grimshaw

Temperatures are set to plummet as snow blizzards batter parts of the UK during the 'coldest winter for 100 years', according to experts.

Exacta Weather claims the UK could see "some type of 100-year cold or snow records being breached this winter, reports the Express.

But forecasters at the Met Office say their long-range predictions look very different.

Experts at the authority say next month's temperature will actually be above average for this time of year.

A Met Office spokesman said: "At the the moment, the longest range we are looking at is January, but this is not a forecast, it's a probability.

"We're probably looking at milder than average conditions - so, not the coldest for 100 years, or a collapsed polar vortex."

He said the start of December will see heavy rain in the UK, with snow on northern hills by December 5.

It could then turn colder with frost and fog in the North.

From December 6 to 20, more rain and strong winds are set to batter the UK, predominantly in the West.

The long range forecast reads: "Snow is likely at times in the north, mainly over high ground.

"Some quieter, drier, colder spells are also likely, giving night frosts and some fog.

"These may occur more frequently in the north in the early part of this period but confidence for this aspect is very low.

"However temperatures for the most part are expected to be near or slightly above average, for the time of year."

The Met Office said the amount of snowfall experienced during December could not yet be determined.
