Healthcare Minister refuses to drop idea of financing healthcare at budget deficit’s expense

25 Nov 2019 by LETA

Latvian Healthcare Minister Ilze Viņķele is committed to meeting with Prime Minister Krišjānis Kariņš to discuss the idea of increasing healthcare funding at the expense of state budget deficit.

This much the minister promised on Monday, 25 November, at the conference of patient organization. According to her, the promised wage increase for healthcare workers can be secured by increasing state budget deficit. The minister mentioned at the conference that she agrees with trade unions representing doctors.

Commenting on the unity of healthcare workers, the minister said this is a historic moment.

«The louder the voices of doctors ring, the greater the possibility of them being heard,» said Viņķele.

When asked to comment the recent signature collection process to initiate Saeima’s dismissal, the minister said re-election of the parliament would not bring residents desired changes.

At the same time, she admits the law adopted at the beginning of the year on healthcare funding was a political mistake, stressing that in such cases legislators should avoid adding any percentage increases of funding in legislative drafts. The minister also stresses the lack of experience of young deputies. She considers this one of the reasons for the mistake.

Viņķele also mentioned that together with the State Chancellery a new prototype for wages is being developed. This new model may take into account workload equivalent and the type of job and daily duties.

As previously reported, Attīstībai/Par! political parties association had previously reported allocating EUR 60 million needed to raise wages for doctors at the expense of budget deficit. However, Prime Minister Krišjānis Kariņš did not support this proposal and asked Viņķele to focus on her main objective – sorting the healthcare system.

Finance Ministry stressed that if the government decides to breach European Union’s permitted deficit level, it is necessary to keep in mind long-reaching and expensive consequences for the state budget. Finance Ministry invited policy-makers «to be patient and understand that state budget expenditures have to be measured together with state budget revenue».
