Liam Gallagher steps out with his mum in Dublin before gig

25 Nov 2019, 00:46

Liam Gallagher might not seem like your typical mummy’s boy – but his mum Peggy was right by his side ahead of his latest gig in Dublin.

Bless.

The pair were seen leaving the Irish capital’s swish Merrion Hotel ahead of the show, which is the first of his new tour.

Given his various family woes – including an ongoing feud with brother Liam – it’s good to he has Peggy there for him.

The former Oasis frontman will play 25 dates across Europe between now and late February, including dates in Vienna, Berlin and Amsterdam.

He’ll also play London’s O2 Arena on 28 and 29 November, but is skipping his native Manchester.

The tour follows Noel’s recent comments that Liam’s Twitter account is the reason we don’t have an Oasis reunion. Gutted.

‘It’s strange behaviour for someone who is gagging for me to pick up the phone and say let’s do it,’ he told The Big Issue. ‘If you think for one minute I am going to share a stage with you after what you’ve said you are f****** more of a moron than you look.’

Asked if it could happen if he stopped stirring things up, Noel added: ‘Of course it could. But there would have to be an extraordinary set of circumstances. But I don’t think he ever will stop. The modern image of him is defined by the angry guy on Twitter.’

Fair.

‘I left the band 10 years ago,’ he continued. ‘I think I’ve seen him twice in 10 years and both times we nearly ended up in a fight for no reason.

‘I can’t envisage the morning I wake up and think I’d like to spend two years on the road, arguing all around the world with Liam.’

Oasis, who have released seven albums, are known for hits like Wonderwall and D’you Know What I Mean?

Their album Be Here Now was the fastest-selling in UK history, until Adele stole their crown with 25 back in 2015.

Meanwhile, despite being in the music industry for 25 years, Liam still knows how to pull it out the bag.

This year he scored a number one album in the UK with Why Me? Why Not, which reached number two in Ireland.
