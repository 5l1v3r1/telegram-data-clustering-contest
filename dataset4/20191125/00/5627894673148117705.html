Southland braces for powerful holiday storm

25 Nov 2019

SOUTHLAND - (INT) - An incoming winter storm is coming into better focus.

KEY POINTS
 A major winter storm will impact Southern California Wednesday morning through Friday
 Significant snowfall will fall in the mountains with high impact to travel - accumulating snow through most mountain passes.
 Light snow accumulations possible in the high desert Thursday afternoon into Friday as the freezing levels drops to 3,000 feet
 Widespread moderate to locally heavy rain; Localized flash flooding and debris flows possible

The Pacific storm is gaining strength and will send two cold fronts into Southern California, one Wednesday and another Thanksgiving Day. A cold air mass will move into SoCal late Wednesday afternoon and evening with significant cooling through Friday.

Snow levels will plunge resulting bringing heavy snow to many mountain communities and mountain passes. A Winter Storm Warning goes up early Wednesday above 3,000 feet and wont come down until Friday night.

Across the foothills, the valleys and into the Los Angeles Basin, its the rain that will impact holiday travel. Isolated thunderstorms could trigger heavy downpours with localized flash flooding and a threat of debris flows in and near the recent wildfire zones.

Latest rainfall totals expected through Friday:

Coast/Valleys: 1.5 to 2 inches, locally up to 3 in some valley locations.
Mountains: 3 to 5 inches
Deserts: 0.5 to 1 inch

Latest snowfall totals expected through Friday:
3000-4000 feet: Trace to 3 inches
4000-4500 feet: 3 to 6 inches
4500-5500 feet: 6 to 12 inches
Above 5500 feet: 12 to 36 inches

Winds on Monday were a precursor to the incoming storm. The strongest gusts hit 74 mph at Cushenbury Grade on the desert side of the San Bernardino Mountains at 72 mph at Whitewater.
