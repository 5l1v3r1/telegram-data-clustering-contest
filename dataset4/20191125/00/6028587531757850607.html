Holiday Gift Guide 2019: The Top Texas Wines For All Wine Lovers

25 Nov 2019 by Michelle Williams

Three-hundred and fifty years after the first vineyards were planted, Texas wine is finally having a moment. Due to numerous successful vintages, word is spreading—it’s time to pay attention to the fifth largest wine producing state. Mediterranean grapes from Spain, southern France, and Italy thrive in Texas. If you are looking for the perfect wine to wow your boss, colleagues, friends, family, or hard to please oenophile, Texas is the answer. There are too many high-quality wines in Texas to list here, but these ten wines will get your holiday gift giving started.

2016 Bending Branch Winery Tannat CM, Tallent Vineyards Texas Hill Country ($50): To achieve maximum concentration this 100% Tannat undergoes cryo-maceration, resulting in a deep penetrating purple in the glass and vibrant aromas of baked black fruit and berries, dried roses, black pepper, dried herbs and leather, roasted espresso beans, and kirsch; a bold wine with finesse; crushed velvet mouth-feel, long tannins and high acidity give ample structure for a wine that will age well. Purchase direct from Bending Branch Winery.

2017 Brennan Vineyards Super Nero Comanche County ($34): This wine is a blend of 58% Nero d’Avola, 25% Carmenere, 8.5% Cabernet Sauvignon, and 8.5% Alicante Bouschet, all sourced from various Texas vineyards. It pours a youthful deep ruby into the glass, with lively aromas of black fruit and bramble berries, warm baking spice, leather bound cedar cigar box, black licorice, dried herbs, and black pepper; youthful tannins are silky upon entry, becoming grippy mid-palate. It contains high acidity, adding structure and a long finish. This wine begs for Texas beef or game. Purchase direct from Brennan Vineyards.

2018 Calais Winery Cuvée du Rocher Texas High Plains ($36.03): Crafted of 100% Sauvignon Blanc from Narra Vineyards and produced in a “Sancerre” style. This wine offers soft fruit notes of under-ripe stone fruit, lemon curd, and key lime zest mingled with orange blossom, dried savory herbs, and crushed stone. Time on the lees and barrel fermentation adds depth and texture, high acidity creates an elegantly tangy mouth-feel and a long mineral-driven finish. Purchase direct from Calais Winery.

2018 Duchman Family Winery Trebbiano Texas Hill Country ($18): Crafted of 100% Trebbiano (aka Ugni Blanc); this wine offers crisp notes of tropical fruit, citrus, and under-ripe stone fruit, wrapped in chalky minerality. It is a bright wine with refreshingly high acidity, elegant—a crowd pleaser; perfect to sip alone or enjoy with poultry, creamy pasta, or a fresh vegetarian meal. Purchase direct from Duchman.

2015 French Connection Wines Roussanne Dessert Wine Texas High Plains ($66.51): Crafted off 100% Roussanne, this port-style wine pours golden amber into the glass. It opens with rich notes of dried apricot and figs, honey comb, orange blossom, and hazelnut dazzle the senses; bright and well-balanced on the palate with a pleasant sweetness and high acidity; long, clean finish; sweet but not cloying; perfect finish to a meal. Purchase direct from French Connection Wines.

2018 Lost Draw Cellars Albariño Texas High Plains ($32): Albarino comes in an array of styles—squeaky clean and gulpable to elegant and thought-provoking; this one is the latter; vibrant notes of ripe stone fruit and citrus mingle with a chalky minerality and lanolin salinity; the salinity follows through on the palate in a medium-bodied, high acidity, elegant wine that begs for food. Purchase direct from Lost Draw Cellars.

2016 Pedernales Cellars Texas Tempranillo Reserve Texas Hill Country ($50): Texas is known for producing high-quality Tempranillo, this is one of the best. It is crafted of 100% Tempranillo from Pedernales best lots in a Ribera Del Duero style. It offeres ample notes of dark fruit and berries; warm baking spice, dried savory herbs and crushed roses, tobacco, leather, and black pepper; aged in American and French oak gives this wine balance, finesse, and a silky mouth-feel, high acidity wraps the palate with a long, earth-driven finish. Decant to drink now; will age beautifully. Purchase direct from Pedernales Cellars.

2016 Ron Yates Winery Sangiovese Tio Pancho Ranch Vineyard Texas Hill Country ($32): This wine is crafted from grapes cultivated in the Farmhouse Vineyard; it pours a deep ruby and offers pleasing notes of red fruit and berries, baking spice, dried roses and savory herbs, leather, tobacco, and trailing vanilla; it wraps the palate in juicy fruit and spice notes with a silky mouth-feel, firm structure, and a long, pleasing finish. Purchase direct from Ron Yates Winery.

2014 Spicewood Vineyards ‘The Good Guy’ Texas Hill Country ($41.99): This wine demonstrates the Texas Hill Country’s capacity to produce a wide range of quality grapes. This proprietary blend of 42% Tempranillo, 17% Graciano, 17% Merlot, 17% Cabernet Sauvignon and 7% Syrah (percentages subject to change depending on the vintage) is sure to please red wine lovers. Ron Yates fell in love with Spanish reds while studying there in college, this wine reminisces of Spain’s top Riojas. It opens with bold aromas of ripe red and blue fruit, loads of warm baking spice, crushed dried roses and savory herbs, a touch of smoked meat and trailing black pepper. Boldness follows through on the palate with firm structure, grippy tannins, high acidity, and a long savory finish. Spicewood recommends pairing with peppercorn venison tenderloin—yes, please! Purchase direct from Spicewood Vineyards.

2016 William Chris Vineyards Mourvèdre Lost Draw Vineyard Texas High Plains ($45): Mourvèdre is often a bold, dark wine; William Chris crafts their Lost Draw Mourvèdre into a thoughtfully finessed wine reminiscent of Pinot Noir. It opens with inviting aromas of red and blue berries mingle with spice, savory herbs, black pepper, and crushed violets; intentionally restrained yet elegant, with a salinity that begs for food; high acidity and silky tannins round out a beautifully complex wine. Purchase direct from William Chris Vineyards.
