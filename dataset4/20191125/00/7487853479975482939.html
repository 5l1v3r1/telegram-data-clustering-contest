Raheem Sterling Nominated for BBC's Sports Personality of the Year Award

25 Nov 2019 by Krishan Davis

​Raheem Sterling has capped a stellar year for both Manchester City and England with a nomination for the BBC's Sports Personality of the Year Award.

While the 24-year-old has excelled on the pitch in Pep Guardiola's title-winning side, he has also become a prominent figure off it in the face of great adversity and a previously hostile media climate.

Sterling has emerged as a leading voice in the fight against racism in football, regularly speaking out and applying pressure on the powers that be to do more to stamp out the kind of bigotry that has reared its ugly head multiple times in recent years in football stadiums across the globe.

Having been abused on several occasions for both club and country, Sterling has taken to social media to call on the FA and UEFA to take strong action against offending teams and nations.
