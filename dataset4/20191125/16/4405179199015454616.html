Best TV shows on Apple TV+

Apple TV+ has arrived, with plenty of new telly shows to binge. Here's our selection of the 7 best series for you to enjoy

25 Nov 2019, 16:12 by Martyn Casserly

Apple TV+ is now available for £4.99/$4.99 per month, taking on the likes of Netflix, Amazon Prime Video, and Disney+ for dominance of our TV screens. So, what does it have to offer? Here's our pick of the 7 best shows to stream.

See

This post-apocalyptic adventure stars Aquaman himself, Jason Momoa, in a bleak tale where most of humanity has been wiped out by a virus, with the survivors all now blind. Into this world comes two babies, both of whom have the gift of sight. This is seen by some as hope for a better future, while others fear it is the portent of a final death knell for mankind.

Momoa leads a tribe who choose to protect the young ones, while religious elders send an army to kill them. Who will prevail?

The Morning Show

Another star-studded series, with Jennifer Aniston taking that lead as a daytime newsroom anchor whose long-term onscreen partner (Steve Carell) is suddenly fired after a sexual abuse scandal. While recovering from this huge setback, Aniston's own job comes under threat from the younger, hungrier journalist played by Reese Witherspoon.

Studio bosses see a possible star in the making, but does Aniston have what it takes to fight for her anchor role or even her sanity? All will be revealed on The Morning Show.

Snoopy in Space

What? It's Snoopy…in space! Good grief, what more do you people want?

Join Snoopy and Woodstock as they head off to NASA with the aim of becoming astronauts. All of the classic Peanuts characters are cheering them on, with Charlie Brown, Lucy, Linus, Marcie and crew manning the controls back on Earth. Plus, there's a piano soundtrack inspired by the evergreen work of Vincent Guaraldi.

For All Mankind

Continuing the astronaut theme is this alternative-history drama in which the Russians won the space race and put the first man on the moon. Never ones to admit defeat, the Americans decide that they will complete the representation of humanity by being the first to land a woman on the moon. Thus, begins a new race with all the personal and technical challenges it brings.

Ghostwriter

Four schoolchildren work to solve mysteries that are triggered when a ghost that haunts the local bookshop frees fictional characters from novels. Chaos and comedy result, in this fun, family-friendly show that's a much gentler alternative to Netflix's Stranger Things.

The Elephant Queen

If all this drama seems a bit too much, sit back and follow the journey of a herd of elephants as they wander across the wilderness of Africa. Led by their matriarch, Athena, the animals must travel far from their waterhole to find a new home, facing dangers and joys along the way.

Narrated by BAFTA winner Chiwetel Ejiofor OBE, the documentary features incredible wildlife footage and is a welcome slice of tranquillity in an otherwise tiring world.

Oprah's Book Club

Oprah Winfrey is a phenomenon. Rising from poverty, she fought her way to become one of the most powerful and influential people in modern media.

One of her many ventures was the Book Club that featured on her talk show for nearly fifteen years. Now this is being reinvented for Apple TV+, with Winfrey selecting a book for each episode then discussing the themes and inspirations with its author. So, buy the book, watch the show, and expand your horizons.
