Bruno Fernandes latest: Jose Mourinho decisive as Spurs target 'practically guaranteed' to leave

Sporting midfielder Bruno Fernandes was heavily linked to Spurs and Manchester United - will Jose Mourinho's arrival in north London prove decisive in his future?

25 Nov 2019, 16:45 by Joe Doyle

Bruno Fernandes was strongly linked with a move to both Spurs and Manchester United in the summer.

However, no move came for the midfielder, who has stayed with Sporting amid a reported €70million asking price.

Real Madrid were also credited with a keen interest in the Portugal international

But with all three sides initially struggling this term, it appears likely that the trio could reignite their interest in January, in what could again be a long and drawn out saga.

Here is what we know so far bout the midfielder's future.

Tottenham's interest

Portuguese publication Record, as reported by Sport Witness, claimed that Spurs were ready to 'move forward with a concrete proposal' worth over €70million to sign Fernandes, while according to Jornal de Noticias a bid of €65million had been submitted.

However, football.london understood early on in the transfer window that Spurs did not see the midfielder as a priority during the summer window.

What Fernandes has said

Fernandes spoke numerous times about his future and suggested that he would like to move to the Premier League but these comments from the playmaker were the most telling in the summer.

"The president decides that, not me," he told A Bola. "I don't care about the market, I've talked about this many times.

"The only thing I can do now is that I need to catch the plane so I don't miss it. Will this be the last match for Sporting? I don't know, it's the coach who decides.

"I said I'd like to play in England, but I don't care about it right now. If it could be this season? It could be when the club decides, I'm not in charge."

Sporting CP's stance

Sporting CP manager Marcel Keizer spoke about the future of Fernandes and explained that any exit would depend on who the club could buy to replace him.

"It depends, of course, on who can enter later if Bruno leaves. Quality players like that we can't replace."

Spurs 'meeting'

Reports from the Portugeuse media claimed that Tottenham sent a delegation to Lisbon to make an offer for Fernandes on August 1.

Public service broadcaster RTP and O Jogo both claimed that Spurs travelled to the city to try and agree a deal.

Record - as translated by Sport Witness - have suggested that a €70milion bid was rejected by Sporting CP president Frederico Varandas at a meeting between the two sides.

Furthermore, the report claimed Spurs and Sporting CP could hold another meeting within the next week, although according to Sky Sports the north London club were not interested in making any further bids.

It was a curious situation. For representatives of Tottenham to fly to the Portuguese capital to make an offer below the asking price for a player they did not see as a priority earlier in the window, and to then apparently end up deciding against taking any further action, seemed an odd move.

Some Spurs fans even suggested that the club was merely trying to use Sporting and Fernandes in a bid to spook Real Betis and get a better deal for Giovani Lo Celso.

'Agreement reached' with the player

According to Portuguese journalist Goncalo Lopes, Tottenham and Fernandes reached an agreement over personal terms although Spurs did not reach an agreement with the player's club.
