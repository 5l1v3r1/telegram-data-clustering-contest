POOCH FEARS

Edinburgh couple’s beloved chihuahua snatched on Princes Street as thief tricked passersby into thinking it was her pet

25 Nov 2019, 16:09 by Blair Meikle

AN EDINBURGH dog owner claims her beloved chihuahua was snatched from her on Princes Street - with the thief tricking passersby into thinking it was their dog.

Laila Hepburn says she was walking with her boyfriend Chris when Tinkerbell was taken outside Boots at around 7pm on Sunday.

When they tried to get her back, the woman tucked the pooch under her jacket and started shouting they were trying to rob her.

This led to other members of the public holding Chris and Laila back - with the thief escaping on a bus.

The family are offering a a £500 reward to anyone that can come forward with information.

Cops have confirmed they received a report of a theft and are investigating.

Laila took to Facebook to urge others to be on the lookout.

She said: "Please if you see a doggy like her please shout 'Tinkerbell’ and if she turns round it’s her, so if that happens please phone police and try to keep track of her til police turn up.

"You cannot mistake her. People we don’t know constantly shout ‘Hi Tinkerbell.

"I’m trying to tell myself that even if I never see her again hopefully she’ll be sold to a nice family who will treat her well but Chris keeps saying ‘imagine what she’s going through right now!

"They’re not Guna feed her, she’ll be missing us and Marley and Diesel’.

 

"I hope she’s found but despite her unique appearance I doubt there’s much hope.

"I’ll even pay for her safe return. I don’t care what it costs, I just want my baby back."

A spokesman for Police Scotland said: "Police were called at around 7.10pm on Sunday November 24 to reports of a theft of a dog at Princes Street, Edinburgh.

"Enquiries are ongoing."
