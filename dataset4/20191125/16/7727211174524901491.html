Fume over 's***show Christmas light switch on 'so awful even Santa left'

"It was a s***show. Embarrassment in all honesty."

25 Nov 2019, 16:27 by Kate Lally

People in Crosby are not happy after a 's***show' Christmas switch-on event on Friday night.

One woman, Mandy Ryan, took to Facebook to post her criticisms, which have since been liked and shared more than 80 times.

Part of her post read: "I am absolutely embarrassed. Having living in Crosby all my life, I have never seen a turnout like [tonight's].

"Santa left before the lights were even switched on.

"I took it upon myself to leave and take my three-year-old daughter to the Thornton switch-on, she was crying by this point because she hadn't seen Santa.

" Thornton had a good crowd and a lovely festive spirit. As for Crosby['s switch on]... you should hang your heads in shame."

Max Dakin replied: "It was a s***show. Embarrassment in all honesty."

Lee Chesworth added: "I took my three-year-old twins and it was awful."

It remains unknown who actually organised the event - but apparently the usual event had to be cancelled due to a "complaint".

Local Labour councillors Michael Roche and Diane Roscoe usually organise the switch-on, but say they had to cancel this year's.

Cllr Roche said: "We arranged everything and then had to cancel all the acts, the market, the stage, lights and generator as someone complained that it was too busy last year."

Local business-owner Ian Scott suggested creating a fundraising page so Crosby can have a better Christmas event next year.

He said: "The people who are moaning should put their money where their mouth is and contribute somehow. If we want better we need to help, not just complain.

"If I set up a [funding] page for next year. I wonder how many of the amazing people of Crosby who love a good moan on here about the turn on would contribute?"

It's not just people in Crosby who are disappointed.

Christmas switch-on events in Ainsdale and Waterloo have been cancelled altogether.

Ainsdale Civic Society said a lack of funds means the tradition event will not be held, although a large tree is on display in the village.

In previous years, the festival has featured live music, children’s activities, fairground rides, Christmas shopping stalls, and entertainment from various local groups.

But after funding cuts took their toll and previous events resulted in financial losses, Ainsdale Civic Society said no event will take place this year.

Last year's event recouped only £1,000 of its £2,500 running costs while this summer’s gala ran at a £3,000 loss.

Waterloo's Christmas evening usually runs in connection with Crosby Housing Association, but CHA say it will not take place this year.

There will still, however, be a lit-up tree in Waterloo.

The ECHO understands Sefton Council has no budget for Council-run Christmas activities. The local authority has previously said 'this was removed as part of the budget setting process a number of years ago due to government cuts'.

Sefton MBC did, however, give some grants to established community groups in all parts of the borough to help with the purchase of their own trees and lights.

This year money (£800 each) was made available to groups in Netherton , Waterloo, Seaforth , Crossens, Birkdale , Lydiate, Ford ward, Thornton, Hightown , Bootle , Melling, Crosby and Hillside.

Formby village also received £800 each from the council - to put towards the clean-up after their event, which was funded by donations from local businesses and residents.

Southport's Christmas switch-on event was funded entirely by Southport Business Improvement District (BID) and received no money from Sefton Council.

All Crosby councillors have been approached for comment.
