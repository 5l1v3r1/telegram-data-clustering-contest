Man stabbed at Blantyre Station in brutal late night attack by two thugs after altercation with large group

25 Nov 2019, 16:31 by Blair Meikle

A MAN was stabbed at Blantyre train station in a horrific late night attack at the weekend.

The victim, in his 50s, was attacked by two thugs at around 1.12am on Sunday morning, following an altercation in Station Road.

The two men were part of a larger group, who all fled in the direction of Station Road.

The victim was rushed to hospital for treatment.

Officers are keen to speak to anyone with information on the two men or the larger group.

BTP can be contacted by texting 61016 or by calling 0800 40 50 40 and quoting reference number 40 of 24/11/19.

Alternatively, call Crimestoppers anonymously on 0800 555 111.

The group are described as:

  • A man, white, between 5ft 10 and 6ft, aged between 20 and 40 years old, of a stocky build and with dark hair. He was wearing a khaki jacket with a patch on the left sleeve, blue jeans and white trainers.
  • A man, white, between 5ft 8 and 5ft 10, aged between 20 and 40 years old, with short dark hair and of slim build. He was wearing a dark jacket with a large white patch on the shoulder area of each sleeve. He was also wearing light blue jeans and white trainers.
  • A man, white, between 5ft 8 and 5ft 10, of a heavy build, and wearing a dark coloured hooded tracksuit top with a white stripe down each sleeve. He was also wearing dark shorts, black socks and white trainers.
  • A woman, white, aged between 18 and 25 years old, of slim build, and with short dark hair tied into a bun. She was wearing a black jacket, light-coloured trousers and white trainers.
  • A woman, white, aged between 18 and 25 years old, of a heavy build, and with short dark hair tied up. She was wearing a black jacket with a fur lined hood, blue jeans and dark shoes.
