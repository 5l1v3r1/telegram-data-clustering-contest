Lewis Morgan wanted for Celtic loan exit as St Mirren eye January return

Jim Goodwin has revealed he wants to bring the winger back to Paisley.

25 Nov 2019, 16:28 by Liam Bryce, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/liam-bryce/

Jim Goodwin has revealed St Mirren want to re-sign Lewis Morgan on-loan from Celtic.

The Buddies boss would jump at the chance to bring the winger back to Paisley in January, two years after he signed for the Premiership champions.

The 22-year-old returned to the Simple Digital Arena for six months before linking up with the Hoops in summer 2018.

But Morgan has been left on the fringes under Neil Lennon and previously spent time on-loan with Sunderland during Brendan Rodgers' tenure.

Goodwin admits he's been given no indication the Scotland international will be made available come the New Year but still plans to open talks with Celtic .

He said: "The January window is approaching, we've been looking at our own squad and looking at different options that are out there.

"Of course Lewis would be a player we'd love to get, he's obviously been here before and been very successful. He's a quality footballer but at this minute in time he's not available.

"We haven't been told by Celtic that he is and he remains part of their squad.Â 

"I wouldn't hide from the fact that if we could have an opportunity of making that one happen then absolutely we would.

"We'll wait until the window opens and we will have those conversations then.

"It's all well and good speaking to Celtic but the player needs to want to come as well and he'll have people advising him on that but Lewis is among a number of players that we're looking at.

"We need to strengthen again in January, it's as simple as that."
