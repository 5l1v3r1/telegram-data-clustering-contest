Duterte in Busan for ASEAN-Korea summit

25 Nov 2019, 10:39

PRESIDENT Rodrigo Duterte is now in Busan, South Korea to attend the ASEAN-Republic of Korea (ASEAN-ROK) Commemorative Summit.

Duterte arrived in the port city past 1 a.m. (South Korea time).

A Malacañang statement said Philippine Ambassador to the Republic of Korea (ROK) Noe Wong, Chief of Presidential Protocol and Presidential Assistant on Foreign Affairs Robert Eric Borje, Korean Ambassador to the Philippines Han Dong-Man, and other ROK officials welcomed Duterte upon his arrival at the Gimhae Air Base.

Duterte is set to participate in the 2019 ASEAN-ROK Commemorative Summit along with the other heads of ASEAN member-states and South Korean President Moon Jae-In.

Presidential spokesperson Salvador Panelo said Duterte is set to hold a bilateral meeting with Moon.

Agreements on education are among those that are expected to be signed between the two countries, he added.

Before meeting the South Korean president, Duterte will attend private meetings in Busan on Monday, according to a follow-up report by Cedric Castillo on Dobol B sa News TV.

Duterte is expected to participate in the summit proper on Tuesday.

Joining the President’s delegation are Foreign Secretary Teodoro L. Locsin, Jr., Finance Secretary Carlos G. Dominguez III, Agriculture Secretary William D. Dar, Education Secretary Leonor M. Briones, Defense Secretary Delfin N. Lorenzana, Trade and Industry Secretary Ramon M. Lopez, Interior and Local Government Secretary Eduardo M. Año, Tourism Secretary Bernadette Romulo-Puyat, Science and Technology Secretary Fortunato T. Dela Peña, Socioeconomic Planning Secretary Ernesto M. Pernia, Communications Secretary Martin M. Andanar, Social Security System President and CEO Aurora C. Ignacio, and this representation.

President Duterte designated Executive Secretary Salvador C. Medialdea as government Officer-in-Charge from November 24 to 26 to take care of the day-to-day operations in the Office of the President and to oversee the general administration of the Executive Department.
