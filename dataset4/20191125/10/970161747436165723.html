Chris Hemsworth is all peace signs and smiles after landing back in Australia

25 Nov 2019, 10:23

Chris Hemsworth was in high spirits as he arrives back home in Australia after a busy trip to Japan for Comic-Con.

The Thor: Ragnorak actor threw up some peace signs as he walked through Sydney Airport wearing his best airport attire.

The 36-year-old donned a plain white T-shirt, jeans and a pair of grey sneakers and hid behind some dark sunglasses.

He didn’t mind stopping for photos with eager fans because Thor is the politest superhero.

Chris enjoyed an extremely short trip to Tokyo for Comic-Con, where he hit the stage with Thor’s hammers.

Not like anyone else can pick it up, right? Oh wait…

The star was obviously there for business, promoting the latest Thor instalment, Thor: Love and Thunder, which has Natalie Portman and Tessa Thompson as his co-stars.

We can’t wait to see muscly Thor make a comeback after his potbelly incident in Avengers: Endgame.

The newest movie will start filming in Sydney in August next year, with the movie set to be released in November 2021.

It was also revealed that Natalie will be playing the first female Thor, and we’re guessing Thor is going to get a little jealous.

Meanwhile, Avengers: Endgame writers Christopher Markus and Stephen McFeely have opened up about avoiding a ‘bloodbath’ and allowing Thor to live.

Well, someone had to.

After we tragically lost heroes including Tony Stark (Robert Downey Jr) and Black Widow (Scarlett Johansson), a tiny ray of hope was presented to mourning Marvel fans in the form of the survival of Thor (Chris Hemsworth) and Hawkeye (Jeremy Renner) among others.

Chatting about the surviving Avengers, Stephen explained: ‘We could have had a bloodbath and all six of them gone down swinging, but that seemed even more depressing.’

Um, no thank you.

Chatting about the surviving duo, he added: ‘It’s not necessarily that we knew they’d get more stories, it’s just that…Thor has sacrificed and sacrificed and lost and lost.

‘It’s not a good ending to kill him. To have him finally, reasonably content with himself—and with his current weight, by the way [is more satisfying].’
