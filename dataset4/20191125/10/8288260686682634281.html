'Just wow!' - Dean Smith explains what Aston Villa have missed without Jack Grealish

Aston Villa v Newcastle United | Dean Smith speaks candidly about his skipper who's set to return to the match-day XI for the visit of Steve Bruce's side at Villa Park this evening

25 Nov 2019, 10:57 by Ashley Preece, https://www.facebook.com/PreeceBirminghamLive/

Dean Smith has lauded Aston Villa captain Jack Grealish ahead of tonight's crucial showdown with Newcastle United, declaring: "We go 'Wow!' at some of the things he does."

The 24-year-old finally looks set to return to the starting XI after missing the last two Premier League matches with a badly-bruised calf.

During his spell on the sidelines, Villa - without their skipper - have lost to Liverpool and then Wolves - and tonight's the night to get back to winning ways against Steve Bruce's Toon.

"He's a top player," Smith explained while discussing Grealish's influence. "I stand with JT sometimes and we go, 'Wow!' at some of the things he does.

"When top players are playing you have a better chance of winning, when top players aren't playing, you've less chance of winning.

"It's the same with any top player at any club."

Smith added: "Against Liverpool we lost his ball retention. I have no doubt that had he been on that pitch being 1-0 up with 10 minutes to go, he'd have bought some fouls and would have retained the ball in the top-half of the pitch for us. He didn't and we ended up losing the game.

"Wolves? Listen, if he'd have played like the others we wouldn't have got anything out of it. But he's a top player, bringing goals to his game as well as assists now and that's all he can keep doing."

Arguably Smith's most-memorable masterstroke to date was giving Grealish the captain's armband on his return from a shin injury at the start of the year.

And, when asked about just how vocal he is around the training ground, the boss said: "He knows the football club, but he's not shy on his opinions as well. That is what I welcome from the players, to talk.

"I'm very inclusive with the players in the debriefs we have and analysis. The vocal side off the pitch, it's something Jack's brought into his game a lot more. On the pitch he's not so vocal but he's a leader by example.

"He'll take the ball even if he's having a bad game. There's bravery in that and, sometimes, that can get other players driven as well."

As for what surely is an inevitable England call-up, Smith added: "We've had three international periods now and each time there has been speculation about him getting in there and he hasn't.

"So the message has been, 'Just keep playing'.

"You saw in October he was nominated for October Player of the Month, such was his form, so he all he can do is that.
