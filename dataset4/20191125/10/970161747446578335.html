Lorry driver accused of killing 39 migrants admits assisting illegal immigration

25 Nov 2019, 10:17

A lorry driver accused over the deaths of 39 migrants has admitted plotting to assist unlawful immigration.

Maurice Robinson, 25, alleged to have been part of a global smuggling ring, is charged with the manslaughter of a group of men, women and children found dead in a refrigerated trailer.

The bodies of eight females and 31 males were discovered in the container attached to his Scania cab in an industrial park in Grays, Essex, early on October 23.

They were later identified as coming from various provinces of Vietnam, with the youngest being two boys aged 15.

Robinson, who is known as Mo, appeared at the Old Bailey this morning where he admitted conspiracy to assist unlawful immigration between 1 May 2018 and 24 October 2019.

The charge states that he plotted with others to do ‘an act or series of acts which facilitated the commission of a breach of immigration law by various persons’.

He also admitted acquiring criminal property – namely cash – on the same dates.

Robinson, from Craigavon in Northern Ireland, is yet to enter pleas to the remaining 39 counts of manslaughter along with offences of concealing criminal property and conspiracy to traffic people.

The judge, Mr Justice Edis, said: ‘This is quite a significant investigation likely to involve investigations into other people.’

Christopher Kennedy, 23, is due to appear at Chelmsford Magistrates’ Court on Monday accused of human trafficking offences.

He was arrested in the early hours of Friday morning on the M40.

Kennedy, from Darkley in Co Armagh, is charged with conspiracy to arrange or facilitate the travel of people with a view to exploitation and conspiracy to facilitate the commission of a breach of UK immigration law.
