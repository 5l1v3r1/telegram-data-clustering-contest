Lindsay Lohan pays tribute to ex-boyfriend and 'best friend' Harry Morton

25 Nov 2019, 10:25

Lindsay Lohan has broken her silence on the death of her ex-boyfriend Harry Morton.

Morton, 38, was found dead at the scene by a family member at his home in Beverly Hills on Saturday afternoon.

According to TMZ, no foul play is suspected in Morton’s death.

In the wake of the tragic news, Lindsay – who dated the Pink Taco founder for three months in 2006 – shared a black and white picture of them as a couple back in the day.

The 33-year-old wrote on Instagram: ‘Best friends. Best life.’

The Mean Girls actress’s mother Dina commented: ‘So sorry my lil princess 🙏🏻he’s always with you , watching over you baby.’

Meanwhile, Lindsay’s dad Michael wrote ‘RIP’, while her siblings Dakota and Aliana left heart and prayer emojis under the pictures.

Morton – the son of Hard Rock Cafe co-founder Peter Morton – was also linked to stars including Demi Moore, Britney Spears, Paris Hilton, Jennifer Aniston and Hayden Panettiere.

He founded LA-based Pink Taco restaurant chain in 1999 aged just 18.

In 2008, he also bought Hollywood nightclub the Viper Room – the club where River Phoenix famously died in 1993 – and recently bought Elvis Presley’s former home in Beverly Hills.

Mr Morton survived by both of his parents, Peter Morton and Paulene Stone.

A Pink Taco spokesman said: ‘We are saddened by the passing of Harry Morton, the founder and former owner of Pink Taco.

‘Harry was a visionary and restaurateur ahead of his time, and his contributions, both professionally to our brand and personally to those he worked with, were numerous.

‘Our thoughts and condolences are with his family and friends during this difficult time.’
