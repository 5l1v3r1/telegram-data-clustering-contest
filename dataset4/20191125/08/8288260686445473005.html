Aston Villa set to make this January decision over loan star

Latest Aston Villa news includes manager confident he can keep AVFC midfielder beyond January transfer window

25 Nov 2019, 08:46 by Shane Ireland, https://www.facebook.com/ShaneIrelandJournalist

Aston Villa midfielder Jake Doyle-Hayes is expected to see out his loan spell at Cheltenham Town.

The 20-year-old joined the League Two side on a season-long switch in the summer after spending the second half of the last campaign at Cambridge United.

He has established himself as one of the first names on the Robins’ team-sheet having impressed in their push for promotion.

Doyle-Hayes has already racked up 18 appearances this term, the most recent of which came in Cheltenham’s 1-1 draw with Colchester United on Saturday as he delivered another solid showing.

Villa have an option to recall the Republic of Ireland youth international when the transfer window re-opens in January but manager Michael Duff is confident his side will be able to keep him until the end of the season.

Discussing his trio of young loanees which also includes Hull City pair Jacob Greaves and Max Sheaf, Duff told GloucestershireLive: "I'd like to think we can keep them because they are here to develop and they are getting minutes so I'd like to think they'd be definites."

Doyle-Hayes made his senior debut for Villa in a League Cup clash against Wigan in August 2017 and signed a three-year contract a month later.

Cheltenham reporter Jon Palmer told BirminghamLive recently: “Cheltenham have had a host of youngsters from Premier League and Championship clubs on loan in recent years and some have not looked ready for senior football, but Doyle-Hayes certainly does look fully equipped to thrive in League Two.

“They brought in Easah Suliman from Villa in 2016 and you could tell he was going to be a useful player, but the move was possibly a little early for him at that stage.

“In contrast, Doyle-Hayes looks absolutely comfortable already and based on what he's done in the opening weeks of the season, I would certainly expect to see him go on to shine higher up the footballing ladder in the near future.”
