Drunk son rang police from Northumberland pub and threatened to smash and burn his parents' home

Thomas Cavanagh wanted to be arrested so made the threats on the phone while he was sat in a Northumberland pub

25 Nov 2019, 08:00 by Sara Nichol

A drunk son rang police and threatened to "smash and burn" his parents' home just so he could be arrested.

Thomas Cavanagh was sat in a pub four miles away from his mum and dad's address, where he also lives, when he made the threats during numerous calls to the emergency services, a court heard.

An officer was sent to locate the 25-year-old, who was at the Village Inn, in Longframlington, but his behaviour didn't stop there.

South East Northumberland Magistrates' Court was told that Cavanagh continued to shout "You better lock me up or I will go round and f*****g destroy the place" and "I'll smash the place up".

His wish was finally granted and Cavanagh was arrested for making threats to damage property.

The court heard that, while in a cell, Cavanagh, of Main Street, Felton, Northumberland, then destroyed a mattress to the value of £150.

He appeared in the dock last Thursday, where he pleaded guilty to one count of making threats to damage property and one of criminal damage.

Prosecutor, James Long, said Cavanagh made the first call on the evening of September 19 this year.

"The defendant said he intended to attend his parents' address and smash the house up and burn their two cars," Mr Long added.

"A police officer is on his way to Longframlington when he gets a message from the control room to say the defendant has been in touch again making the same threats.

"There's then yet another call where the defendant says 'You better get a move on as I have a weapon on me'. He threatens to smash and burn the place."

The court heard that no weapon was ever discovered on Cavanagh when he was eventually arrested outside the pub.

Kyle Patterson, defending, said Cavanagh's parents were oblivious to the threats and that he only made them to the police.

The solicitor added: "He's had too much to drink and there's been an argument and he's cross, so he decides to ring the police.

"His intention is to say silly things and get arrested. It was never his intention to carry out the threats."

Magistrates ordered Cavanagh, who has no past convictions, to pay £150 compensation and gave him a six-month conditional discharge.
