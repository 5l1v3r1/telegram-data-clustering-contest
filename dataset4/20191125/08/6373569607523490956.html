Pop superstars Little Mix are coming to Plymouth

The show will be part of their 21-date Summer 2020 tour

25 Nov 2019, 08:00 by Jess Morcom

Pop superstars Little Mix will be performing at Home Park next summer, it has been revealed.

The world's biggest girl band will perform to fans across England, Scotland and Wales as part of their 21 date Summer 2020 tour, and will be stopping at the Theatre Of Greens on Friday, July 3.

The tour will see Little Mix perform some of their greatest hits including 'Woman Like Me', 'Touch', 'Shout Out to My Ex', 'Black Magic' and 'Wings'.

The four piece band, Perrie Edwards, Jade Thirlwall, Leigh-Ann Pinnock and Jesy Nelson are no strangers to the city, having performed at Plymouth Pavilions in both 2013 and 2014 and MTV Crashes Plymouth in 2015.

Speaking about the Summer 2020 Tour, the group said: “Performing live is our favourite thing to do as a band, we love it.

“Our last Summer tour in 2018 was one of our favourites ever, so we can’t wait for some more brilliant outdoor shows next year. We want everyone to come party with us in the sunshine!”

Speaking about the Summer 2020 tour coming to Home Park, Plymouth Argyle Chief Executive Andrew Parkinson said: “It is superb for us to be able to host one of the most iconic British girl groups of all time for a special concert in Plymouth’s historic Mayflower 400 year.

“Little Mix will be a massive part of a big summer for the club and the city, and we are delighted they are gracing Home Park Stadium.”

“We’re looking forward to welcoming their fans to the venue again for what promises to be another fantastic show.”

Since 2011 Little Mix have not only established themselves as one of British pop's brightest acts, but as a global pop phenonmonon.

They have fully cemented their status as the world’s biggest girl band, with the quartet         asserting dominance in the charts album after album.

They have sold more than 50 million records worldwide, collected more than 17 million Spotify listeners, received more than 3 billion Youtube views, and collated 12 billion streams worldwide.

Record sales have seen them notch up a total of four UK No.1 singles, four platinum-selling albums and nine platinum selling singles, surpassing a record previously held by the Spice Girls.

Their 2016 album 'Glory Days' was the biggest selling album by a female group this millennium in the UK, alongside being named the longest-reigning Top 40 album for a girl group ever.

Tickets go on sale at 9am Thursday November 28 fromwww.ticketmaster.co.uk and www.cuffeandtaylor.com.

At least 75p from every ticket sale will be donated to Nordoff Robbins - a charity that uses the power of music therapy to enrich the lives of people affected by life limiting illness, isolation or disability.
