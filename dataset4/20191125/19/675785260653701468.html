Gary Neville reaches two conclusions with 'fundamentally wrong' Man Utd front three

Manchester United fought back against Sheffield United on Sunday but Gary Neville highlighted problems with Marcus Rashford, Anthony Martial and Daniel James

25 Nov 2019, 19:43 by Liam Prenderville, http://www.facebook.com/mirrorfootball

Gary Neville has criticised the performance of Manchester United's attacking players in their draw with Sheffield United.

United trailed 2-0 at Bramall Lane before mounting a stirring comeback to take the lead, only for the hosts to rescue a point late on.

Marcus Rashford had put United ahead after crossing for Mason Greenwood's equaliser but was very ineffective before then, as were Daniel James and Anthony Martial.

Neville was particularly critical of the trio and suggested Ole Gunnar Solskjaer has work to do to improve things moving forward.

"The lack of penetration and work rate of the front three was particularly disappointing," he said on Sky Sports' Monday Night Football.

"James, Martial, Rashford. They aren't bad lads and they have talent but there is something fundamentally wrong in the first 70 minutes. That cannot happen at that club.

"I can expect inconsistency in terms of performance levels and missing chances, but not work ethic and application and attitude."

Neville went on to suggest Solskjaer must spend big in January to ensure he gets the best out of the trio.

"I come to two conclusions. One, there are no senior players setting the standard and telling them what is required in a football team," he added.

""The other big thing is they don't know or understand how to approach a Premier League away game.

"There are a lot of good young players there but they need experienced players around them.

"Who knows that they could become for the club but they don't know the game at all, particularly away from home."
