ESPN Analyst Releases Score Prediction For Nebraska-Iowa

25 Nov 2019, 19:54 by Matt Hladik

Nebraska and Iowa will meet Friday in their annual post-Thanksgiving rivalry clash. Iowa has won the last four meetings between the two Midwestern programs.

This would be an opportune time for the Huskers to snap their losing streak vs. the Hawkeyes. Nebraska needs a W to finish 6-6 and reach bowl eligibility.

Overall, it has been a disappointing second season for Scott Frost, but a win over Iowa would at least close out the regular season on a high note and send the team to the postseason.

As for Iowa, the Hawkeyes are a workmanlike 8-3, with a shot at their sixth 10-win season under Kirk Ferentz if they can win on Friday and win their bowl game.

ESPN’s Bill Connelly has released his score predictions for this weekend. Using his SP+ formula, he’s made his pick for Iowa-Nebraska.

SP+ projection: Iowa 30, Nebraska 21

Iowa and Nebraska will kick off at 2:30 p.m. ET on Friday from Memorial Stadium in Lincoln.

BTN will broadcast the action.
