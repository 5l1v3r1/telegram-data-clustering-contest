Steven Pressley reveals his last-gasp Hearts manager KO and the Craig Levein phonecall that sealed his fate

The former Gorgie skipper believes he was in line to take the job before a late development.

25 Nov 2019, 19:16 by Liam Bryce, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/liam-bryce/

Steven Pressley has revealed he suffered a last-gasp Hearts manager KO when Craig Levein took the job in 2017.

The former Jambos captain claims he was chief Ann Budge 's preferred candidate in their hunt to replace Ian Cathro two years ago.

Pressley, who refused to criticise Levein for taking on a dual role as director of football, recalled how the now-sacked boss phoned to explain the decision.

Hearts are now searching for the 55-year-old's successor following his sacking in October, with German coach Daniel Stendel among the frontrunners.

Pressley believes he remains a good fit for a return to Tynecastle but also ruled himself out of the running.

Asked if he was a candidate, Pressley told BBC Sportsound : "Not at all, not at all. Just to make this absolutely clear, I'm not on this show to plug myself out.

"What happened was I met the board along with Craig and I felt the interview went exceptionally well.

"I felt there was a really good connection, that my style of management, what I stand for and my beliefs in management were very much suited by the club.

"I was very hopeful of getting the job at that time and the feedback was I was probably Ann's preferred option at that moment in time.

"But it wasn't to happen and Craig took the job. Unfortunately for myself, I didn't get that opportunity.

"But the one thing I must stress is Craig did phone me up on the back of taking on the job and had the courage to explain the decision."

Earlier in November, Pressley was sacked as Carlisle United boss following 10 months in charge.

He left the League Two club in 19th place - although was binned despite winning two of his last three matches.

The 46-year-old believes there were mitigating circumstances behind his failure and was decisive when asked if he would be up to the task in Gorgie.

He said: "I do, very much so. I'm a hugely driven individual and I have huge belief in what I do.

"I think failing as a football manager is part of the journey now, you have to accept and learn from that.

"There's always circumstances to why you fail, they're not always what people believe.

"The truth of the matter is I lost my job, like most managers lose their job, because results were perceived as not good enough and we accept that in the industry we're in.

"There's also reasons to why. When I took over at Carlisle we had to recruit 15 of our 19 players in the summer.

"We had a bottom four or five budget, the players we recruited were players of potential, the geography of the club makes it very difficult.

"But I loved the job there because I had an amazing relationship with the players.

"I'm disappointed because I never seen it coming. I had no inclination at all.

"Two weeks prior to my departure I was told the investors were behind me and there was a pick-up in results.

"But I accept if results are not perceived right then us as managers have to suffer the consequences."
