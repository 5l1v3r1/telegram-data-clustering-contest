BBC Sports Personality of the Year shortlist in full as Raheem Sterling among nominees

The shortlist for BBC Sports Personality of the Year 2019 has been confirmed with the winner of the prestigious prize set to be announced in Aberdeen at a star-studded awards ceremony on December 15

25 Nov 2019, 19:24 by Rich Jones, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/rich-jones/

The full list of nominees for the 2019 BBC Sports Personality of the Year award has been confirmed.

Manchester City and England star Raheem Sterling is among the six-name shortlist - but it is cricketer Ben Stokes who is the firm favourite for the prize after his heroic summer exploits.

Stokes, who captained England to their first ever ICC Cricket World Cup win then played a starring role in The Ashes, is the odds-on favourite to claim the gong on December 15.

They are joined on the shortlist by Wales rugby captain Alun Wyn Jones, Formula One world champion Lewis Hamilton and Athletics stars Dina Asher-Smith and Katarina Johnson-Thompson, who both won gold at the World Championships.

The nominations for the prestigious accolade were unveiled gradually on BBC programmes throughout the day, starting with Stokes on the BBC Radio 1 Greg James Breakfast show and ending with Johnson-Thompson on The One Show.

The six contenders will now be subject to a public vote, opening on the night of the live show in Aberdeen on December 15 with the successor to 2018 winner Geraint Thomas to be unveiled.

Other accolades to be handed out on the night invlude the World Sport Star of the Year, Greatest Sporting Moment of the Year, Team of the Year, Coach of the Year, Lifetime Achievement Award, Helen Rollason Award, Get Inspired Unsung Hero Award.

Boxer Caroline Dubois will also be awarded the Young Sports Personality of the Year after she was named as the winner of the prize last night and presented with the trophy for the first time at BBC Radio 1's Teen Awards.

Stokes' incredible summer in both one-day and Test cricket has made him the long-standing favourite for the award, looking to become the first cricketer to win the prize since Andrew Flintoff in 2005.

Lewis Hamilton, last year's runner-up, is eyeing a repeat of his 2014 win after capturing his sixth Formula One World Championship title, moving clear into outright second place as the most successful driver of all-time.

Sprinter Asher-Smith became the first British woman in history to win a global sprint gold medal in the 200m, adding silver medals in the 100m and 4x100m at the World Championships in Doha.

Team-mate Johnson-Thompson set four personal bests to win heptathlon gold, breaking Dame Jessica Ennis-Hill's British record.

Both are looking to become the first female winner of the prize since Zara Phillips back in 2006.

Sterling, meanwhile, won the historic domestic treble with Manchester City and was also named Football Writers' Association Footballer of the Year, also being praised for his public stance against racism.

The last footballer to win the award was Ryan Giggs a decade ago.

Wales legend Alun Wyn Jones led his country to another Grand Slam victory, as well as becoming Wales' most capped player en route to the World Cup semi-finals in Japan.

Full BBC Sports Personality of the Year shortlist

Ben Stokes, Raheem Sterling, Dina Asher-Smith, Katarina Johnson-Thompson, Lewis Hamiton, Alun Wyn Jones
