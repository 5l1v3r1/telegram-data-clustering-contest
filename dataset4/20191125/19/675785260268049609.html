Unai Emery's time is up at Arsenal and Diego Simeone would be the perfect replacement

Arsenal could - and should - have gone for Diego Simeone when Arsene Wenger left and they must now try and lure him to the Premier League

25 Nov 2019, 19:00 by Stan Collymore, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/stan-collymore/

Arsenal needed revolution after Arsene Wenger left.

They needed someone to be bold, brave, rid the walls of the stench of average, and to take a hammer to the place.

Instead they got Unai Emery who is struggling to stop Arsenal being a Barcelona-light parody act from 2009.

The Arsenal board should now be testing Diego Simeone’s ambition and offering him the chance to test himself against Klopp, Mourinho and Pep.

It is always tough for a manager succeeding a dynasty like Wenger. It could even be two or three bosses down the line who succeeds.

Do Arsenal want to keep playing nice continental passing football... I keep recalling Patrice Evra calling them nice boys.

Or do you want teams turning up at the Emirates thinking: We’re not going to win here. This is a nasty place to come. Nice boys passing, or be an aggressive front-foot side.

Forget going to Germany or Italy for a new manager, with Simeone they have a chance by saying, you have plateaued, eight years in Madrid, we will double your wages, we can parachute you in to compete against Mourinho, Klopp, Pep.

You need a guy with sheer force of will. Klopp has done it at Liverpool. Turning into a modern version of Shankly and their legendary managers.

Emery came in to much fanfare after doing exceptionally well at Sevilla, winning Europa Leagues, but his last job at PSG wasn’t fantastic. You can judge him on two things: his signings and his style of play.

A style of play will take three years to change and get bedded down because Arsenal were basically Barcelona light. They were a parody act of Barca 2009-11.

Fans should understand to unravel 20 years of coaching, philosophy and staff takes a revolution.

It takes the board and a brave manager to say ‘right here we go we are going to do something completely different, we are going to give this man a remit to change things’.

To make that revolution happen the man would be Simeone. He was mooted a few years back the first time Wenger was supposed to be leaving.

He has the hard working attitude and force of character to change that squad. Emery was never going to be a revolutionary.

They brought him in because he plays attractive on-the-deck football. What Arsenal needed was someone to come in and rip it up and create a new identity.

In recent days Emery’s communication has been mocked, and that has been casual xenophobia with people taking the p*** out of his dialect and pronunciation.

People are saying to Arsenal fans that this guy is a parody, a comic, a cartoon. When that sets in he has lost it. There is no way he can turn it back.

The answer is in the next two or three weeks, they will get a draw somewhere and the board will pull the trigger.

Simeone doesn’t speak any English, he signed a long term contract when Atletico Madrid moved into a new stadium, and he seems to be there for the long haul. But unless you ask the question the answer will always be no.

His stock may not be as high as it was two or three years ago but you’d have a manager who is currently bossing a club that is an identikit of Arsenal – third biggest in their league, third most successful, in a new stadium, looking for the next level.

He has a history of getting Atletico Madrid into finals, and if they get him now, at slightly less than his peak, he won’t cost a Jose Mourinho-style salary.

They should - with permission - send the owners over to meet him and say: We know you don’t really fancy leaving Madrid, but here is a vision for you of the potential of Arsenal Football Club.

He would fit what Arsenal need almost perfectly.
