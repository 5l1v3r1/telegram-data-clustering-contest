City Council set to ban sale of flavored e-cigarette products after committee backs bill

25 Nov 2019, 19:25 by Olivia Bensimon and Nolan Hicks

A key City Council committee on Monday unanimously backed legislation that would ban the sale of flavored e-cigarettes products, presaging the bill’s passage by the full Council this week.

The legislation “will finally and belatedly address a health crisis that this country has been ignoring over more than four years,” City Council Member Mark Levine said.

The Health Committee’s vote came just a day before the entire Council is expected to back the ban, amid growing fears about the safety of vaping products — as the usage of the thumb-drive like devices soars.

So far, at least 42 people have died — including two New Yorkers — and more than 2,000 people have been sickened from vaping related illnesses across the country.

Health experts have blasted vaping companies for producing nicotine-laden candy and fruit flavored products — like mango, mint, cotton candy, berry — that they say are helping to hook teenagers.The City Council’s proposed ban would nix every flavor except the traditional tobacco flavor and menthol.

“We don’t want to leave a loophole like that for e-cigarettes,” Levine added. “We want this to be only an alternative to adult smokers who are trying to quit, not an enticement for kids.”

Council consideration of similar legislation that would ban menthol-flavored tobacco products has stalled amid opposition from some key African American political figures, including the Rev. Al Sharpton.
