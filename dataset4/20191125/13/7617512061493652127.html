Man charged in Nanaimo crash that killed retired police officer

25 Nov 2019, 13:36 by Jeff Bell

A 23-year-old man is due in Nanaimo provincial court Dec. 17 after being charged in a collision last December that killed retired police officer Shinder Kirk.

Conrad Niklaus Wetten was charged with driving without due care and attention Nov. 15, said Nanaimo RCMP Const. Gary O’Brien.

Kirk, who had served as both an Abbotsford Police Department media-relations officer and a spokesman with the Combined Forces Special Enforcement Unit, was driving a pickup truck west on Cedar Road on Dec. 22, 2018 when it crashed head-on with a pickup heading east.

He was pronounced dead at the scene, about a kilometre from the Cedar Road bypass. Two passengers in Kirk’s vehicle were airlifted to hospital with serious but non-life-threatening injuries, while two other passengers had minor injuries.

The two occupants in the other truck had minor injuries.

O’Brien said at the time of the incident that investigators did not believe alcohol and speed were contributing factors.

News of Kirk’s death generated tributes across B.C.
