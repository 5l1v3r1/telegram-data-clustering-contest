Phillip Schofield suggests I'm A Celeb time 'created drama' with 90-sec trial

25 Nov 2019, 13:20

Phillip Schofield has weighed in on the I’m A Celebrity Bushtucker trial that caused drama on Sunday night.

In order to win roast dinners for camp, James Haskell and Roman Kemp had to lie on a bed filled with snakes and try to unscrew stars from one side of the bed and screw them onto the other side.

Each star was in a box filled with critters and animals, including a crab that put a spanner in the works and meant they were unable to complete the trial in the 90-second time.

Viewers who tuned in were convinced the trial was ‘impossible’ to do in 90 seconds, but hosts Ant and Dec tweeted that it was successfully completed within the time during the dry run.

James and Roman’s failure meant that three celebs – Ian Wright, Cliff Parisi and Andy Whyment – would miss out on the delicious dinner.

Speaking on Monday’s This Morning, Phillip felt that the show had stirred things up to create drama for the celebs.

He told ex camp mates Georgia ‘Toff’ Toffolo and Stanley Johnson: ‘The last [trial], it’s not possible in 90 seconds.’

‘If they’d gone the other end with the spiders first they would have got it easy,’ Holly said.

Phil went on: ‘The thing is if they’d all done it as well – because they don’t seem to be squeamish all of them – if they’d done that really quickly everyone would have been fed, and where’s the fun in that?

‘So now we have three people at the bottom of that who don’t get their roast dinner.’

Holly added: ‘Including Ian who’s a right grumpy grump at the moment, isn’t he?’

We don’t think Ian, Cliff or Andy are going to be happy bunnies when they return to camp.

The Love Island-themed trial also saw some celebs have to pass pig testicles between them using their mouths, while other gulped down disgusting milkshakes made of blended cockroaches or vomit fruit.

I’m A Celebrity Get Me Out Of Here airs daily at 9pm on ITV.
