Former Bohemains captain Derek Pender appointed head coach of PFA Ireland's annual training camp

The squad will play a match away to League One side Rochdale

25 Nov 2019, 13:43 by Paul O'Hehir

Former Bohs skipper Derek Pender has been appointed head coach of the PFA Ireland annual training camp.

He will be assisted by former League of Ireland stalwart Pat Flynn while St Pat’s Brendan Clarke is the goalkeeping coach.

John Russell will oversee a camp that runs simultaneously in Castlebar for players based in the West of Ireland.

The squad will play a match away to League One side Rochdale, most likely on December 11.

A player exchange facility is also in operation for the first time whereby one member of the squad will be sent to Finland in the hope of earning a contract over there.

In turn, a Finnish player will come the other way in an attempt to land a deal with an Irish club.

PFA Ireland general secretary Stephen McGuinness said: “It's something we hope to build with Norway and Sweden as well.

“Unfortunately the finances around the old tournament are quite significant so that has been curtailed.”

Former St Pat’s player James Doona has been identified as a potential candidate to travel to Finland.

But Pender - who retired from playing last month - will have the final decision on who he sends abroad.

McGuinness added: "We're looking at someone who has played full-time, is available to travel and wants to play abroad and James is definitely one that has spoken to us.

“But Derek will make a decision on the best equipped player to go across and that will be no different to the Finnish player coming here as they have training camps on.

"There are a number of factors including quality of player, whether they have the time and if it's realistic that they would take a contract in Finland if it's offered.”

McGuinness also thanked the FAI for its help behind the scenes having received little or no assistance under the old regime in recent years.

"The facilities here (Abbotstown) have been opened up to us for the first time in quite a few years,” he said.

“We're using the dressing rooms, the pitches. We’re in the indoor arena tonight and were on external on FAI pitches before that.

“We’re being assisted with everything be it washing kit, balls, cones - the FAI have provided everything which is a significant change to what it had been in the past.”

According to McGuinness, that assistance has saved the PFA Ireland between €4,000 and €5,000.
