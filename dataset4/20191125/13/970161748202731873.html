General Election 2019: What do the main parties promise on climate change?

25 Nov 2019, 13:20

Climate change is featuring prominently in this election, with leaders asked to take part in the first ever debate solely on the topic as part of the election debates.

So where do the parties stand on climate change?

Conservatives

The party has pledged to cut greenhouse gas emissions to zero by 2050.

As part of plans to meet this target, the party says it will spend £9.2 billion to improve the energy efficiency of homes, schools and hospitals.

Boris Johnson’s manifesto also vows to plant 75,000 acres of trees a year and ban plastic waste exports to non-OECD countries.

Labour

The Labour party has promised to create 1 million green jobs in a bid to tackle the climate crisis.

Setting out the party’s manifesto, Jeremy Corbyn said Labour would impose an £11 billion windfall tax on oil and gas companies.#

The party also says it will work towards net zero carbon well before 2050, after dropping its initial plans to reach net zero carbon emissions by 2030.

The party’s manifesto also states: ‘We will deliver nearly 90 per cent of electricity and 50 per cent of heat from renewable and low-carbon sources by 2030’, as well promising to build 9,000 new wind turbines.

Liberal Democrats

The Liberal Democrats say they will spend £20 billion a year for five years to tackle climate change.

The party has set itself a target of achieving net-zero carbon status by 2045.

Also among the initiatives are plans to deliver 80 per cent of UK electricity from renewables by 2030 and to plant 60 million trees a year.

Funding of £12 billion has also been promised to support renewable energy.

Firms will also be set climate targets.

Green Party

The party’s main policy is focused on spending £100 billion a year to cut emissions by 2030.

That means all petrol and diesel vehicles would be replaced.

The party also says it will end fossil fuel industry tax breaks and subsidies and offer a nationwide retrofit insulation programme for homeowners and renters.

It is by the far the most radical plan on climate change compared to the other parties.

Brexit Party

Climate change doesn’t feature much in the Brexit Party’s manifesto, though it has pledged to ensure all waste in the UK is recycled.

Nigel Farage has also promised a mass tree planting campaign to capture CO2, though he hasn’t released figures of how many he wants planted yet.
