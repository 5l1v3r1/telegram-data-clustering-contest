Dad brain-damaged after plunging onto concrete while hanging Christmas lights

25 Nov 2019, 13:54

A father of four was left brain damaged after plunging two stories onto concrete while hanging Christmas lights.

Jason Blair suffered a traumatic brain injury, skull fractures, broken ribs and a punctured lung after suffering the fall in Rock Hill, South Carolina, last Thursday.

He is currently on a ventilator, with a GoFundMe page set up to pay medical expenses warning that Blair’s condition had ‘regressed’ on Sunday afternoon.

Blair works as an odd-job man, and was hanging the decorations for client Stephanie Haas at the time of the accident.

He is now in intensive care, with doctors warning Blairs’ family that his prognosis is ‘not good.’

Blair’s brother Roger Eddington told WCNC: ‘He never had a good home.

‘If you needed anything he would help you.’

Eddington explained that Blair had a tough start to life, and was later adopted by his family.

He said that while doctors have warned him to prepare for the worst, he is still hopeful his sibling will pull through.

Eddington said: ‘I think he’s going to make it. My brother is a strong man.’

Homeowner Haas, who set up the GoFundMe page to support Blair, explained that the handyman’s wife Doris is out of work, and that he is sole provider for his family.

And the stricken worker’s friend Chris Perkins said they’d been stunned by the level of goodwill shown towards Blair, saying: ‘The outpouring of support has been absolutely overwhelming.’

Doctors will perform an MRI scan to establish the full extent of Blair’s injuries if and when his condition stabilizes.
