Outrage as Tory says he 'can't do anything' about child poverty in his area

Gordon Henderson, who has been the Conservative MP for Sittingbourne and Sheppey, since 2010, sparked fury after saying he can't do anything about half the children in the area living in poverty

25 Nov 2019, 13:15 by Elaine McCahill, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/elaine-mccahill/

A Tory MP has sparked outrage after saying he "personally can't do anything' about child poverty in his area.

Gordon Henderson appeared on Victoria Derbyshire today as he campaigns to be re-elected as MP for the Sittingbourne and Sheppey constituency in Kent.

Henderson, who has represented the area since 2010, was confronted with the figure that almost half of children on the Isle of Sheppey are living in poverty.

He replied: "This hasn't just happened over the last nine years, there are deep-rooted reasons why we have deprivation in certain areas of this constituency, including in Sheerness, and it goes back 50 years.

"It's been under Labour administrations and Conservative administrations, they are deep-rooted. A lot of it goes back to when they closed the dock yard here on Sheppey many years ago."

Victoria interjected saying: "That was in the 50s!"

Henderson continues: "It was in the 50s and it has never improved. I can't personally do anything about it Victoria."

She hits back: "Why not?"

"On a personal basis there is little I can do, but what I can do and what I am doing is...," he continued.

Victoria then interjected to say: "You're the Conservative MP for nine years, your party has been in government for nine years."

Henderson continues with his point saying he personally can't do anything as a constituent shouts over: "Then why should we vote for you?"

He goes on: "What I'm doing is working with other people. For instance, I am currently working with Swale Borough Council to try and identify...

"I actually undertook an exercise earlier this year to try and identify the people in my area, not just statistics.

"We identified them, we contacted them and we tried to help resolve the problems but I can only do a little bit so I'm working with the council to try and identify who they are so we can help them."

Viewers were outraged by his comments with one saying: "Why would anyone vote for that? He can't do anything, his party who have been in power for a decade can't do anything and have promised more of the same. So why not vote someone who can? Vote for change."

Another said: "The Tories always have their excuses. Always the blame game. It's their fault."
