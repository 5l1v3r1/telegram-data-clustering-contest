Corbyn selfies and Greek storms: Monday's best photos

25 Nov 2019, 13:47 by Arnel Hecimovic

The Guardian’s picture editors select photo highlights from around the world

Builth Wells, Wales

Prime Minister Boris Johnson, visits the Royal Welsh Showground in Llanelwedd whilst on the General Election campaign trail.

Photograph: Stefan Rousseau/PA

Renishaw, UK

The Labour leader, Jeremy Corbyn, snaps a selfie as he arrives for an election event in Derbyshire. Corbyn announced Labour’s housing policy would include a charter of renters’ rights and a national ‘property MOT’

Photograph: Leon Neal/Getty Images

Watford, UK

Liberal Democrats Chuka Umunna (C) joins supporters campaigning. The ruling Conservative Party is currently ahead in opinion polls over the opposition Labour Party as the build-up towards the pre-Christmas general election continues

Photograph: Tolga Akmen/AFP via Getty Images

Kineta, Greece

A man stands among fallen trees after a heavy storm hit the town. The storm, named Gyrionis, triggered heavy rainfall in the region of Attica and other parts of the country

Photograph: Yannis Kolesidis/EPA

Ho Chi Minh City, Vietnam

Leigh Boughton Small hugs her mother, Nguyen Thi Dep, in a hotel room after 44 years apart. Nguyen gave up her then three-year-old daughter during Operation Babylift, in 1975, the mass evacuation of children from south Vietnam to the US and other countries

Photograph: Yen Duong/Reuters

Hong Kong, China

Newly-elected pro-democracy district council members chant slogans near the Polytechnic University. The city’s pro-democracy opposition won a landslide victory in local elections in a clear rebuke to the chief executive, Carrie Lam, over her handling of violent protests that have divided the territory

Photograph: Kin Cheung/AP

Tripoli, Lebanon

Anti-government protesters burn tyres as they block the road leading to the southern entrance of the port city

Photograph: Ibrahim Chalhoub/AFP via Getty Images

Bangkok, Thailand

An animal activist calling for a boycott of Pata zoo

Photograph: Soe Zeya Tun/Reuters

Renishaw, UK

Waspi (Women Against State Pension Inequality) campaigners at an election event hosted by the Labour leader, Jeremy Corbyn

Photograph: Leon Neal/Getty Images

Rajpura, India

A young girl works at a construction site in Patiala district

Photograph: Saqib Majeed/Barcroft Media

Kyiv, Ukraine

Workers scrub swastika symbols daubed on to the Sholem Aleichem monument. The tribute to the Jewish writer is located near a synagogue in the Ukrainian capital

Photograph: Sergey Dolzhenko/EPA

North Korea

The North Korean leader, Kim Jong-un, inspects a female army unit. Independent journalists were not given access to cover the event depicted in this image, distributed by the North Korean government

Photograph: 朝鮮通信社/AP

Allahabad, India

Labourers push wooden boards which will be used to build a temporary pontoon bridge over the Ganges for the upcoming Hindu festival of Magh Mela. The event, held near the confluence of the Ganga, Yamuna and Saraswati rivers, allows pilgrims to atone for past mistakes

Photograph: Sanjay Kanojia/AFP via Getty Images

Tokyo, Japan

Filipino women watch a live stream of Pope Francis leading holy mass in the Tokyo Dome

Photograph: Kim Kyung-Hoon/Reuters

Honiara, Solomon Islands

Local people watch as the Prince of Wales, unseen, inspects the guard at Honiara airport, as he prepares to depart following a three-day visit

Photograph: Victoria Jones/PA

Basra, Iraq

An Iraqi demonstrator shields his face as others burn tyres

Photograph: Hussein Faleh/AFP via Getty Images

London, UK

The former prime minister Tony Blair during an interview with Axel Threlfall, the editor-at-large at Reuters, during an interview on the state of British politics

Photograph: Toby Melville/Reuters
