Deontay Wilder and Luis Ortiz purses confirmed as they weigh-in for title rematch

The pair weigh-in on Friday prior to their heavyweight rematch at the MGM Arena in Las Vegas, and they'll receive a decent sum of money for their time in the ring

22 Nov 2019, 23:27 by Irishmirror.ie

Deontay Wilder and Luis Ortiz are each set to land a handsome figure for their world heavyweight title rematch on Saturday - which is only a portion of their expected earnings for the fight.

Both fighters weighed in on Friday ahead of the bout at the MGM Arena in Las Vegas.

The pair met in March last year, with Ortiz troubling the champion on a couple of occasions before Wilder secured a 10th round TKO win.

Wilder is putting his WBC belt on the line again against Cuban Ortiz, and will collect double that of his opponent in purse fees.

ESPN report that Wilder will be paid $3million (about £2.3m) for the bout, while Ortiz will receive $1.5m (approx £1.1m).

However that is just a fraction of their guaranteed earnings from the clash, with Wilder set to earn a minimum of $20m (£15.5m), while Ortiz gets $7m (£5.4m) in return.

The figures dwarf that of their 2018 bout at the Barclays Center in New York, where Wilder earned a then-career high purse fee of $2.1m.

Ortiz is also quids-in, having received $500,000 from the purses in that fight, just a third of what he is set to earn from Saturday's event.

The bout is due to be screened live via pay-per-view on broadcasters PBC and Fox in the United States.

Ortiz weighed-in at 236.5lbs on Friday, holding the slight advantage over Wilder, who tipped the scales at 219.5lbs.

The 40-year-old comes into the bout the underdog, although his loss to Wilder is the only defeat on his 34-bout record.

Since then he has scored two knockout wins, as well as a unanimous points victory over Christian Hammer.

Wilder meanwhile is unbeaten in 42 bouts, with all-but one of his 41 wins coming by knockout.

His only blemish is a controversial draw with Tyson Fury , while his most recent fight was a first round defeat of Dominic Breazeale.

Wilder said: "He knows what happened the first time, he knows what's going to happen the second time. I'm going to have no mercy on him.

"Just don't blink baby, it's going to be an amazing fight."

Ortiz added: "There's nothing more to talk about. Tomorrow's our night."
