Oklahoma vs. TCU: Game Time, Spread, Channel, Announcers

22 Nov 2019, 23:04 by Dan Lyons

The No. 9 Oklahoma Sooners need to start really impressing people to get into the College Football Playoff picture. Lincoln Riley’s team hosts TCU in Norman tomorrow evening.

Despite being a one-loss conference favorite with plenty of playoff history, the Sooners’ placement in the latest College Football Playoff rankings after a comeback win over previously undefeated Baylor on the road shouldn’t give fans much faith. One-loss Georgia, Alabama, Oregon, Utah, and Penn State teams all stand between Oklahoma and the top four.

Some of those teams are destined to lose, but it will be hard for Oklahoma to jump all of them, if its possible at all. Either way, the Sooners should be plenty motivated to end the season.

TCU is just 5-5 on the year, with a 3-4 Big 12 record. The Horned Frogs’ biggest win of the year came against Texas a few weeks ago, but that doesn’t hold up as well as they probably thought it would, as UT has fallen off.

Gary Patterson’s team has been very competitive in its losses though. The only one of its five losses that came by more than one score was a 49-24 at the hands of Iowa State in Ames. The other four have come by a combined 23 points.
