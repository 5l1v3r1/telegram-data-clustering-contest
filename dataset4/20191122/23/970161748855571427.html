Edward Norton reveals he saved Leonardo DiCaprio's life on scuba diving trip

22 Nov 2019, 23:17

Edward Norton has revealed that he previously saved Leonardo DiCaprio’s life while the two were on a diving trip.

It’s hard to imagine that Leo almost lost his life, but thankfully Edward was on hand to bring him to safety.

The two actors were scuba diving together in the Galapagos Islands, when Leo swam off to chase a pack of eagle rays. The only problem is, is that the 45-year-old did not have enough oxygen in his tank.

Speaking on The Jonathan Ross Show, Edward recalled: ‘We were in the Galapagos Islands. We were scuba diving with Sylvia Earle, the great oceanographer. I’ve known Leo a long time and he loses his mind around animals.

‘He really has a childlike passion for them. This flight of 100 spotted eagle rays went by below us and I saw the look on his face.’

The 50-year-old could sense that there would be a problem when Leo decided to swim off, so made the decision to follow him – and it was this, he claimed, that helped save the actor’s life.

‘I saw him charging off with this camera that he had and I have been diving since I was 16 years old,’ he continued. ‘I reflectively looked at my watch and was like, “We’re very close to the end.”

‘I saw him going off and down and I knew that wasn’t a good thing, so I followed him because I thought to myself, “He’s chasing these things, he’s going to run out of air” – and he did.’

Scuba diving asides, it was previously revealed that the Titanic star was uncomfortable on the set of the 2012 movie Django Unchained, as his character had to use racial slurs.

Speaking in a clip exclusively shared with Metro.co.uk, his co-star, Jamie Foxx said: ‘Leonardo DiCaprio had a problem with saying the word n****r.’

As he began to imitate Leo, he continued: ‘[He said] “Pal, pal, it’s tough for me to say this.”

‘I remember Samuel L Jackson going, “Get over here, motherf****r, this is just another Tuesday motherf****r. I don’t give a f*** about these motherf****rs, look at who I am, motherf****r.”’
