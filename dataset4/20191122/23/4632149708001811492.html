Things to do today in Bengaluru

22 Nov 2019, 23:30 by Bangalore Mirror Bureau

Explore Japan
Earthquake survivor Santhosh Kaliyanaraman will present a solo performance Japan-il Kaliyanaraman and it’s filled with drama, horror, humour, romance, fear, empathy and sadness. It portrays his experiences and challenges of living in Japan, which, he says, has changed his perspective on life. He describes Japan as a land of beautifully honest people with vibrantly unique culture.

WHERE: Atta Galatta, KHB Colony, 5th Block, Koramangala
WHEN: 5.30pm-6.30pm, November 23 CALL: 9448701953

School diaries
Shankar Chugani’s got some new jokes up his sleeves. At the Selling myself short show, he will joke about all aspects of his school life – from living in a city to being a Sindhi, his love for chai and trauma about dancing as a girl on stage. As a kid, Chugani was distressingly short and easily intimidated. He’s still short but where’s the time to care? Because, he’s too busy making people laugh.

WHERE: Grapevine, 22nd Cross Road, Sector 3, HSR Layout
WHEN: 7pm onwards, November 23 ENTRY: `349, bookmyshow.com

Magic tales
Dastangoi is no ordinary form of storytelling. It’s an ancient Urdu folk entertainment, which was popular in the Indian subcontinent till the late 19th Century, and which was then revived during mid to late 2000s. Bringing this to you tonight is Kafeel Jafri, in the form of a story called Dastan-e-Ameer Hamza. Here, Ameer Hamza’s friend Amar Aiyaar, among other pals, decide to fight the magicians of Afraasiyab.

WHERE: Seven Pillars Design Studio, 18th Main, 6th Block, Koramangala
WHEN: 7pm-8pm, November 23 ENTRY: `500 CALL: 9873605652

Watch a science drama
The critically acclaimed science play The Square Root of a Sonnet – The Strange History of Black Holes is back for another run. Written by Nilanjan P Choudhury and directed by Prakash Belawadi, the play explores the dramatic events that accompanied the discovery of collapsing stars that ultimately form black holes, by the Indian-American astrophysicist and Nobel laureate, Subramanyan Chandrasekhar. This is a true story of ambition, friendship and betrayal set against the backdrop of the epoch-making events of the 20th – the two world wars, the Indian freedom struggle and above all, the birth of the strange new sciences of relativity and quantum mechanics.

WHERE: Ranga Shankara, 2nd Phase, JP Nagar
WHEN: 3.30pm-7.30pm, November 23
ENTRY: `200, bookmyshow.com

Movie under the stars
Christmas is coming, so brace yourself for events that will put in you the festive mood. Such as this open-air screening of The Nightmare Before Christmas. The story revolves around Pumpkin King Jack Skellington, who wants to spread the joy of Christmas. But his merry mission puts Santa in jeopardy and creates a nightmare for good little boys and girls everywhere. Bring your own mat to make the magical adventure even more enjoyable.

WHERE: The Courtyard, 105, KH Road, Shanti Nagar WHEN: 7pm onwards,
November 23 ENTRY: `200 CALL: 7338677996

A talk on masculinity
The November edition of ‘Salon with Shree’ is back to engage with the literati and artist community of the city and the topic this time is ‘Re-imagining masculinity in an age of feminism’. To be hosted by novelist Sreemoyee Piu Kundu, the discussion will bring together five powerful men – Charles Ma (Bharatanatyam choreographer and teacher), Subodh Sankar (entrepreneur), Sanjay Rajoura (standup artist and actor), Chetan Ahimsa (actor-political activist) and Aditya Tiwari (social activist).

WHERE: The Whitefield Arms, ITPL Main Road, B Narayanapura, Mahadevapura WHEN: 12pm onwards, November 23 CALL: 9980188938

Coding champs
Chaman Bharatiya School is organising the Create Code Play carnival for children between the ages of six and 12. It aims to introduce children to the fundamentals of coding with various interactive activities, which aim to show that learning technology can be real fun. Each batch will comprise of 20 children.

WHERE: Discovery Centre, Bhartiya city, Devin Paradise Enclave, N Nagenahalli AKC
WHEN: 11am, November 23
CALL: 9832322177

Pitch perfect
Attend a brainstorming session with Sandeep P Gaonkar, an entrepreneur and a product manager, who loves to take on challenging projects. Ideal for budding entrepreneurs or those who are running early-stage startups, he will provide an effective guide to build a strong business, enumerating steps and tools you would need.

WHERE: Dialogues Cafe, 15th Cross Road, Gangadhar Nagar,JP Nagar WHEN: 2pm onwards, November 23 ENTRY: `399
