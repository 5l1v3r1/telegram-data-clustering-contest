Newcastle United evening headlines with Steve Bruce's reaction to Jose Mourinho's return

All the latest Newcastle United news including updates on Steve Bruce, Granit Xhaka, Cheick Tiote and Lee Clark

22 Nov 2019, 20:00 by Chris Knight

Steve Bruce has welcomed Jose Mourinho back to the Premier League - joking he has five months to prepare to preserve the new Tottenham manager's St James' Park hoodoo.

Elsewhere, Ciaran Kelly has continued our end-of-decade series with an emotional look back at the late Cheick Tiote's stint on Tyneside.

Here are the top Newcastle United headlines for November 22:

Bruce on Mourinho

Steve Bruce is delighted 'good-looking b******''  Jose Mourinho  is back in football and joked he has five months to prepare a game plan to prolong the Spurs boss'  St James' Park  hoodoo.

The pair first came up against each other back in 2004 when  Bruce  was in charge of Birmingham City and Mourinho was at  Chelsea  and have remained friendly ever since.

That relationship proved crucial to Mourinho allowing goalkeeper Sam Johnstone to join Aston Villa on loan during his time at  Manchester United  , for example.

While Bruce is certainly looking forward to meeting Mourinho on the touchline again, the  Newcastle  head coach has been keen to pay tribute to Mourinho's predecessor, Mauricio Pochettino, too.

Xhaka latest

Unai Emery has revealed that Granit Xhaka will have 'a long future' with Arsenal following talks with the Newcastle United target.

ChronicleLive previously revealed that Emery planned to sit down with the 27-year-old following his return from international duty with Switzerland.

Xhaka has not played for Arsenal since an angry confrontation with the club's supporters after he was substituted in the 2-2 draw against Crystal Palace last month.

The midfielder has since been stripped of the captaincy but Emery believes there is a way back for Xhaka at the Emirates following Thursday's talks.

Harper's return

Steve Harper has returned to Newcastle United as first-team coach to act as a link between the first team and academy.

The club’s longest-serving player spent 20 years at St James’ Park and has since gone to to work for Newcastle’s academy in a variety of positions.

Harper is currently employed as Northern Ireland’s goalkeeping coach and will combine that role with his new position for the Euro 2020 play-offs and if Michael O’Neill’s side qualify for next summer’s tournament.

The 44-year-old, who played for Steve Bruce at Hull, starts his new role on Saturday and will travel with the squad to Birmingham for Monday night’s game against Aston Villa.

Clark on 'watershed' moment

Lee Clark believes Newcastle's embarrassing 5-0 defeat to Leicester in September was a 'watershed moment' in their season.

Since then, Newcastle have won three of their last five games, have lost only once, and have lifted themselves seven points clear of the relegation zone.

The Magpies head into the clash just two points adrift of fifth place and after a turbulent start to his Newcastle tenure, things are looking up for Steve Bruce.

Newcastle came from behind to beat Bournemouth last time out and Clark believes that showed that they have the character to continue their positive momentum - starting with Monday's trip to Aston Villa.

Remembering Tiote

Moving to Newcastle United was an easy decision for Cheick Tiote . Didier Drogba was among those who advised his friend to test himself at St James' Park and the Chelsea legend rightly predicted his compatriot would relish the cut and thrust of the Premier League.

Tiote could have stayed put at Twente, playing in the Champions League, but he wanted to follow in the footsteps of his idol, Michael Essien, and play in England week in week out. And he quickly made an impact following his £3.5m move to the Magpies in 2010.

Joey Barton never forgot that first day, that first crunching tackle from Tiote at Benton, and it set the tone for the midfielder's time at the club; Tiote played with the same intensity and hunger, whether it was a training session or a competitive game.

Tiote relished the combative nature of the Premier League and while new signings often need time to adjust to playing in England, the midfielder immediately hit the ground running with a fine debut against Everton.

A soft-spoken individual, Tiote turned into something very different when he put on his boots and crossed the white line as Jean Musampa, his agent of five years, recalled.

NUFC in training

Newcastle United finally return to action against Aston Villa on Monday after a long wait since the Bournemouth win.

The Magpies will have to wait over the weekend as they’re the Monday night football match on Sky Sports, but the players have been working hard in preparation for the trip to the West Midlands.

With Jamaal Lascelles and Ciaran Clark missing, United eyes were trained on the return of two defenders from injury – as well as two forwards who they’ll be hoping add some firepower to their attack.
