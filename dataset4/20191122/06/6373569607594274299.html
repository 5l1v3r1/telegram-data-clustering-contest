Bradford City insider's guide to what Plymouth Argyle can expect from Gary Bowyer's Bantams

Third-placed Bradford will be the visitors to Home Park in League Two on Saturday

22 Nov 2019, 06:00 by Chris Errington

Bradford City will return to League Two action after a three-week break when they take on Plymouth Argyle at Home Park on Saturday.

Gary Bowyer's side currently occupy third position, but have played fewer games than the two teams just above them in the table - leaders Swindon Town and Forest Green Rovers.

Bradford's last league game was on November 2 when they beat Exeter City 2-0 at Valley Parade.

Since then the Bantams have played League One outfit Shrewsbury Town twice in the FA Cup first round, losing 1-0 at home in a replay on Tuesday after drawing 1-1 away.

City also lost 2-1 to Rochdale in the EFL Trophy at Valley Parade. Promotion, though, has always been the top priority for Bowyer and Bradford and they are on course to achieve that.

Plymouth Live has got the lowdown on the Bantams from Simon Parker, chief sports writer at the Bradford Telegraph and Argus.

HOW WOULD YOU ASSESS BRADFORD'S SEASON SO FAR?

They are well placed at this stage without really playing to their potential.

The home form has been strong and the squad have gelled well considering the large overhaul in the summer.

There is a question mark, though, over whether they can score enough goals – an issue that has been highlighted even more by the spectacular form of Eoin Doyle on loan for Swindon.

HOW GOOD A JOB HAS GARY BOWYER DONE CONSIDERING HOW BADLY LAST SEASON WENT FOR CITY?

He’s done very well in reuniting the team and the fans after the discontent surrounding their relegation.

Bowyer wanted to restore that bond and the new players have certainly bought into that.

But ultimately he will be judged on where City finish at the end of the season.

WHAT HAVE BEEN THE KEYS TO BRADFORD'S SUCCESS SO FAR THIS SEASON AND WHAT ARE THE TARGETS FOR THE REST OF THE CAMPAIGN?

Bowyer has added height and pace to a squad that was totally lacking in both areas before. They are generally solid defensively and the addition of Callum Cooke has strengthened the midfield.

The target has always been to bounce straight back up and that has not altered.

Being knocked out of the FA Cup this week, while a big disappointment in financial terms, means there are no distractions from the league.

WHAT CAN ARGYLE FANS EXPECT TO SEE FROM BRADFORD AT HOME PARK ON SATURDAY?

They are generally well-organised and well-drilled with the pace to threaten on the break through the likes of Dylan Connolly.

City won’t come for a point, although that wouldn’t be the worst result, and will look to take the game to Plymouth when they can.

BILLY CLARKE HAD TWO SPELLS AT BRADFORD AND IS NOW AT ARGYLE. HOW IS HE REMEMBERED BY THE CITY FANS?

Clarke made his name with City during the FA Cup run to the quarter-finals in 2015, memorably scoring the deflected opener in the fifth-round win over Premier League Sunderland at Valley Parade.

He was also in the team that reached the League One play-off final two years later and had City’s best scoring chance well saved by Millwall keeper Jordan Archer.

It didn’t really happen for Clarke when he returned last season and found game-time limited.

HOW HAVE FORMER ARGYLE PAIR KELVIN MELLOR AND ANTHONY O'CONNOR DONE FOR CITY THIS SEASON?

Anthony O’Connor has recovered from a tough first season at the club to forge a solid partnership at centre half with Ben Richards-Everton.

Kelvin Mellor is another to feature regularly in City’s back four at right back, although he has been edged out recently by Adam Henley.

ARE THERE ANY PLAYERS WHO ARE INJURED OR SUSPENDED?

Striker Clayton Donaldson is out until Christmas after an operation to repair a broken toe. Midfielder Jamie Devitt, on loan from Blackpool, has also been sidelined long term with a torn hamstring.

There is better news with Rotherham loanee Matty Palmer, who is close to returning after two months out.
