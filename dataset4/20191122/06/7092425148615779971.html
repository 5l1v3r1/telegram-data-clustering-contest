Young woman fighting for her life after being knocked down by a truck in Louth

22 Nov 2019, 06:42 by Nick Fitzgerald

Gardai are appealing for witnesses to come forward

A 24-year-old woman has been left fighting for her life after she was knocked down by a truck in County Louth on Monday, November 18.

The young woman was crossing the road at the time of the incident which occurred at approximately 3:30pm in the Mullary area of Dunleer.

She was initially transported to Our Lady of Lourdes Hospital in Drogheda but has since been
transferred to Beaumont Hospital.

Her condition is being described as "critical". Meanwhile, the truck driver was uninjured.

Gardai in Drogheda are now appealing for witnesses of the incident to come forward.

A garda spokesperson said:

"Gardai are appealing to any motorists who may have travelled on the road, which is the R132 (old Drogheda to Dunleer Road), to any motorist who may have dashcam footage of the incident or any witnesses to the collision to contact them at Drogheda Garda station on 041 - 9874200, The GardaÂ Confidential Line 1800 666 111 or any Garda station."

Investigation are ongoing.
