Driver caught doing 69mph in a 30mph zone admits drink driving

Magdalena Lehmann was already facing a speeding offence for driving at more than double the limit when she was stopped for being drunk behind the wheel

22 Nov 2019, 06:00 by Sara Nichol

A drink driver "couldn't stop laughing" when she was stopped by police for swerving all over a main road.

Magdalena Lehmann was more than double the legal limit when she was spotted by officers going across the centre white line, on the Coast Road, in Newcastle, and then braking so hard for a speed camera that her vehicle nearly left the carriageway.

The 36-year-old was pulled over by police but, rather than show remorse, she found the "whole situation funny and couldn't stop laughing", a court heard.

However, the smile soon disappeared when she was arrested for failing a roadside breath test and later gave a reading of 81 microgrammes of alcohol in 100ml of breath, with the legal limit being 35 microgrammes.

It also emerged that, at the time, Lehmann, of Station Road, Wallsend, was also facing a speeding offence where she was caught doing 69mph on a 30mph stretch of the Coast Road.

She appeared at South East Northumberland Magistrates' Court on Thursday and pleaded guilty to one count of driving with excess alcohol and one of speeding.

The court heard that Lehmann was caught driving her Audi A4 at 69mph on a 30mph zone on the Coast Road on December 28 2018.

Whilst still facing that offence on September 20 this year, she was spotted by police driving on the Central Motorway, in Newcastle, at around 1.30am.

James Long, prosecuting, said officers first noticed her when she overtook them at speed "in excess of the limit" before turning on to the Coast Road.

Mr Long added: "The vehicle was seen to be swerving all over the road and breaking the centre white line.

"It nearly went off the road to the nearside after braking so hard for a speed camera.

"As a result, the vehicle is stopped near Newton Road.

"Police speak with the driver, this defendant, and she smells of alcohol and appears drunk and unsteady on her feet.

"The defendant, however, found the whole situation funny and couldn't stop laughing."

Stuart Jamieson, defending, told the court that Lehmann had been the victim of an horrific domestic assault a number of years ago and, as a result, became a helper in an organisation, which supports similar victims.

He said one of those victims had rang her on the night she was caught speeding and asked her to come to her house in an "emergency situation".

However, speaking about the drink driving, Mr Jamieson told magistrates that Lehmann had found out that her attacker was going to be released from prison.

He said: "This was horrendous news for her and she's gone to see a friend, where she's had a couple of glasses of wine.

"She makes an error judgement and attempts to drive back to Wallsend."
