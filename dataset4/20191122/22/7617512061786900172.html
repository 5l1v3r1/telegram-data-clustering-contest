Victoria Royals storm back on Edmonton Oil Kings

22 Nov 2019, 22:01 by Cleve Dheensaw

The Dane was great. And also expressive.

Victoria Royals captain Phillip Schultz looked to the fans and pointed to their hats after scoring his third goal, short-handed on a breakaway, in a 5-3 WHL victory over the Edmonton Oil Kings on Friday night. The headgear came flying over the glass from the 5,215 faithful at the Memorial Centre to celebrate the hat-trick from the two-time Danish world junior championship tournament player.

The Royals could have folded after a disheartening shorthanded goal gave the Oil Kings a 3-2 lead early in the third period. But tallies by defenceman Jacob Herauf at 6:50 and Swiss rookie Keanu Derungs on a deflection at 8:25 gave Victoria a 4-3 lead before Schultz sealed the deal.

The Oil Kings, who suffered through some awful seasons after winning the Memorial Cup in 2013-14, made it back to the playoffs last season and are now the No. 8-ranked team in the CHL national top-10 poll at 14-5-5. So this was a significant win for Victoria (11-8-1), despite being widely outplayed and outshot 39-20 by Edmonton.

Royals goaltender Shane Farkas was the well-deserved second star sandwiched between first-star Schultz and third-star Tarun Fizer with three assists.

But the win was sweetest for Victoria rearguard Will Warm. The three-season Oil King, acquired during the off-season, faced his old club. Warm admitted before the game it was going to be weird but that he was going to treat it as just another game.

Forward Carson Miller, who has two goals in three games, returned to the Victoria lineup after missing 16 games to injury.

The Royals now embark on a three-game road swing through Washington state Wednesday in Kent against the Seattle Thunderbirds, Friday in Kennewick against the Tri-City Americans and next Saturday in Spokane against the Chiefs.
