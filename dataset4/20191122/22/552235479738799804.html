Rangers boss Steven Gerrard delivers quirky quip on Alfredo Morelos

The Rangers manager acknowledges the striker will be desperate to play every game, but stresses he must realise the bigger picture.

22 Nov 2019, 22:29 by Gary Ralston, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/gary-ralston/

Steven Gerrard admits there’s no point telling Alfredo Morelos to calm his jets as he flies high for club and country.

The Rangers boss will make a last-minute decision on starting Morelos at Hamilton tomorrow as he prepares to return in Scotland.

Morelos will land in Glasgow later today after he scored his first goal for Colombia on their two-match tour of the United States.

He netted the only goal against Peru in Miami and then featured for 45 minutes against Ecuador in New York in a game they also won 1-0.

Morelos has scored a staggering 23 goals already this season as Rangers prepare for a run of eight games to the end of December that could make or break their campaign.

Gerrard said: “If Alfredo went straight to Hamilton from the airport he’d be desperate to play, that’s the kind of character he is.

“It wouldn’t surprise me if Alfredo is kicking a ball around the aeroplane right now.

“My job is to decide whether he should play as it’s about finding that balance.

“I’ll pick a team capable of going to Hamilton and winning, but you are always trying to stay ahead of the game because we have so many important matches coming up.

“I am looking at the bigger picture while all the players need to do is focus on Hamilton.”

Gerrard confirmed Jordan Jones has returned to full training and is in line for a first team comeback within 10 days, which could put him into contention for a place in the Betfred Cup final.

The Northern Ireland international has been out for almost three months after injuring himself with a reckless lunge at Moritz Bauer in the last Old Firm game at Ibrox, which Celtic won 2-0.

Gerrard added: “I can’t remember the last time we had everyone fit and available. I don’t believe we’ve ever had one.

“Even in pre-season players can drop out with blisters because you are trying to push them.

“It’s nice to have a clean bill of health because we are heading into a crucial block of games.

“However we all know, given the level of physicality and the tempo we try and play, that injuries will occur.

“But it’s a fantastic time to be here because of the matches that are coming up.

“The players have earned the right because of their performances in reaching the League Cup final and the Europa League.

“We are still in with a chance of qualifying from the group and that’s the reason every game is so important.

“We all signed up for this. That’s why we’re here.”
