2019 Irish Pub of the Year named

22 Nov 2019, 22:51 by James Cox

Paris Texas, a bar and restaurant on High Street in Kilkenny city, has been named as the 2019 Irish Pub of the Year.

The pub, opened by Pat Crotty in 1996, also won the innovation award.

1,700 pubs were in contention in the prestigious awards, which are jointly run by the Vintners Federation of Ireland and the Licensed Vintners Association.

The awards have been running for three years.

Another Kilkenny pub won the Best Tourist Pub 2019.

Malzards Bar in Stoneyford, allows visitors to get a lesson in hurling from proprietor, Fred Malzard.

Malzards Bar, which has been in the same family for five generations, also hosts regular trad sessions.

Two Dublin pubs won at the awards ceremony.

Café en Seine on Dawson Street in Dublin 2 was named Best Late Night Bar, and Harry Byrne’s in Clontarf, Dublin 3 received the Best Local Pub award.

Below is a list of all the winners:

Best Food Pub sponsored by Musgrave MarketPlace: Murph’s Gastro Pub at Derragarra Inn, Butlers Bridge, Cavan.

Best Tourist Pub sponsored by Molson Coors: Malzards Pub, Stoneyford, Kilkenny.

Innovative Pub of the Year sponsored by Bank of Ireland Payment Acceptance: Paris Texas, Kilkenny.

Best Local Pub sponsored by Diageo: Harry Byrne’s Pub, Clontarf, Dublin.

Outstanding Customer Service sponsored by Heineken:  Aunty Lena’s and Adare Court House, Adare, Limerick.

Best Music Pub sponsored by IMRO: Kavanagh’s Bar and Venue, Portlaoise, Laois.

Best Outdoor Space sponsored by Bulmers:  The Courtyard on Sober Lane, Sullivan’s Quay, Cork.

Best Newcomer sponsored by Edward Dillon: The Rusty Mackerel, Teelin, Donegal.

Best Late Night Bar sponsored by Irish Distillers – Pernod Ricard: Café en Seine, Dublin.
