Aston Villa have no intention of selling star players, insists Smith

22 Nov 2019, 22:32

John McGinn scored three goals in two games while on international duty with Scotland recently.

Aston Villa boss Dean Smith has warned circling clubs to forget about trying to poach Jack Grealish and John McGinn when the January transfer window opens.

Both players have been in scintillating form this season following Villa’s return to the Premier League, even if they find themselves in a precarious position just one place and three points above the relegation zone ahead of Monday’s visit of Newcastle.

The duo also have long-term contracts, with skipper Grealish signing a new deal last September through to 2023, while McGinn put pen to paper in August to tie him to Villa until 2024.

The pair are, however, attracting considerable attention, yet a defiant Smith said: “There’s been a lot of speculation about a lot of our players.

“But we’ve two owners who want to build a football club, challenging in the top half of the table, and top six in a few years’ time.

“To do that you have to keep your best players and I’ve no doubts in my mind the owners will want to keep those players.”

Grealish returns to skipper the side against the Magpies after a four-week lay-off with a calf injury, while McGinn heads into the game following a superb week on international duty with Scotland, scoring three goals in two games in the Euro 2020 qualifying wins over Cyprus and Kazakhstan.

A smiling Smith said: “Hopefully John will go and get a few for us now he has hit the goal trail. He said to me, ‘If you need to throw me up front you can do’.”

Newcastle manager and former Villa boss Steve Bruce claimed McGinn was currently performing “at Man United level” such have been his high standards of late.

Smith, who at least credits Bruce for “a great buy” as McGinn cost around £2.75million when he joined Villa from Hibernian last August, feels there is still room to grow.

“He’s improving every game he plays,” said Smith. “He works hard in training, he’s scoring goals at international level as well, so he just needs to keep on with that improvement.

“I’ve no doubt he will because he has a great head on his shoulders. He’s an intelligent boy, but on top of that he’s a really good lad in the dressing room.”
