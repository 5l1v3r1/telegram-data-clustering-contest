Man Utd will never lose their magic, claims Sheffield United boss Chris Wilder

Manchester United might not be the dominant force that they were 10 years ago but Wilder still believes there is something special about them

22 Nov 2019, 22:30 by Irishmirror.ie

Chris Wilder insists Manchester United will never lose their magic.

The Old Trafford giants, by their own high standards, are having a bit of a hard time of it right now.

But they make the trip across the Pennines to take on Sheffield United with Blades boss Wilder tipping them to remain the biggest draw in the business.

He declared: “Manchester United are one of the most iconic teams in the world, and there will always be an aura surrounding them.

“No matter how their fortunes on the pitch may fluctuate, it’s still one of football’s true powerhouses that we are talking about whenever we mention their name.

“They are an incredible football club who are just going through a spell right now where they are not having it all their own way.

“That happens in football. But fortunes can fluctuate very quickly in this game and they will find their way back to the top, that’s for certain.”

Wilder says Harry Maguire will play a key role in the Old Trafford quest for fresh glory, and the England centre-back makes a sentimental return to Bramall Lane tomorrow.

Sheffield-born Maguire launched his career at his home-town team before moving on to Hull and then becoming the world’s most expensive defender with his £80million switch from Leicester to United last summer.

Wilder said: "Harry will get a great reception from our fans, I’m sure, and he can be proud of the way he has progressed and developed his game.

“He was given his chance here by Danny Wilson, then established himself at Hull before doing fantastically well with Leicester and getting that move to United.

“Harry has stayed humble, got on with his job, and we are all delighted for him that he’s getting the success he deserves.

“He’s a Sheffield United boy and he still comes to watch us play when he can. But we don’t intend for this game to turn into the Harry Maguire show.

“He’s in the opposition changing-room this time, so that means he is the enemy for 90 minutes. So it’s up to us to make it the Sheffield United show - and that’s what we want to do to make sure we stay where we are at the top end of the table.”
