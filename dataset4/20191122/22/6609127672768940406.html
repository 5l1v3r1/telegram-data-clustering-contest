MEDALLIST'S STRAIN

Olympic runner Eilidh Doyle slams body image pressure put on athletes

22 Nov 2019, 22:13 by Stuart MacDonald

OLYMPIC medallist Eilidh Doyle has slammed the pressu­re put on athletes over body image.

The 400m hurdles ace, 32, said competitors are now expected to look a certain way and post pictures of their figures on social media.

But the Perth-born runner, who is expecting her first child with hubby Brian in January, said she wouldn’t survive as a rookie now because of online trolls.

Eilidh said: “Everybody has different figures but you are wearing small shorts and a crop top, so your figures are on display and it can be difficult.

"It’s inevitable image can play a factor but I think with social media it’s very difficult.

“There is a lot of people on Twitter who want to say nasty things.

“I started my career when social media wasn’t really big. If I was a 22-year-old starting out now would I stay in the sport?”

Eilidh, who won relay bronze at Rio 2016, said she had sponsorship cut when she turned 30.

She added: “Before, deals were based on your ability. Now it is based on how many followers you’ve got on Twitter or how good you look in the kit.”
