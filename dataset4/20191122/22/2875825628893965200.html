Jurgen Klopp tells Liverpool stars there can be no excuses in Premier League title hunt

The Liverpool boss is hoping his side can continue their impressive form - but knows that there cannot be room for excuses if they are to win the title

22 Nov 2019, 22:30 by Irishmirror.ie

Jurgen Klopp has told Liverpool they have now reached a level where there can be no excuses.

The Reds boss knows his side will face more difficult times in their quest for the title, despite enjoying an eight point lead at the top of the table.

But he believes that no matter what the problems to befall them - through a crowded fixture list and inevitable injury problems - the team has reached such an elite status they must cope with any setback.

Klopp pointed to the historic 4-0 triumph against Barcelona as ultimate proof of their resilience, where they produced one of the greatest European comebacks of all time without Mo Salah and Roberto Firmino.

“If you are on that level we are it is not allowed to look for excuses, you still have to deal with it,” he explained.

“There are always things that happen. Like the Barcelona game at home we all the knocks you could get on top of the Man City v Leicester game and all that stuff.

“No time and no need for excuses. Try whatever you can to be as a successful as you can.”

Liverpool ’s resilience has come into sharp focus after Tottenham boss Mauricio Pochettino was sacked this week.

It is widely held that his Spurs team simply failed to deal with the crushing blow of losing the Champions’ League final and their form this season shattered as a result.

That is in stark contrast to Klopp’s men. They suffered a similar fate in the final the year before but used the experience to grow even stronger, and return a season later to lift the trophy on a glorious Madrid night.

Likewise, they suffered the heartache of missing out on the title by a single point in what is regarded as the greatest season ever, but have returned this time around to open up an impressive gap at the top of the Premier League.

For Klopp, there is no magic bullet, or inspirational speech, just the fact his team know how good they can become. “I said a lot (to keep them hungry), but I don’t know what exactly,” he said.

“I react to what I see. It is not fair to compare it to Tottenham because our squad was for the first time in that moment where we could make the final step.

“With Tottenham, age group wise... four years ago you are very young, then you are not very young anymore. I cannot judge it and do not want to compare it.”

Liverpool visit Crystal Palace tomorrow in what the manager believes is another severe test of his side’s character, given the searing atmosphere at Selhurst Park.

Yet he says the sheer size and legend of the club means his players will always stay hungry: “It’s not too difficult because the expectation level is really high.

“Whatever we did we have to do the next thing. I don’t think anyone thought last year after winning the Champions League we can have a five year break without winning something.

“It is easy to stay hungry at this club. You sign for that when you sign the contract. It is the club. It is the history. It is the supporters, of course.”
