Rivaldo: This Is Why I Left Ajax…

22 Nov 2019, 14:28 by Mazola Molefe

Knowing his place in the starting line-up was always guaranteed is the reason Rivaldo Coetzee left Ajax Cape Town two years ago, the versatile player has said.

A product of the Urban Warriors youth academy, joining the club at 15, Coetzee felt he was too comfortable in the final years of his Ajax stint.

When a potential move to Celtic in the Scottish Premier League collapsed after he failed a medical, Mamelodi Sundowns took a gamble and signed him despite an injury that threatened to cut short his young career.

But Coetzee, now 23-years-old, says he was ready to move away from the Mother City.

“This is why I left Ajax – for me it became too easy. I would go to training knowing already that I would play and that didn’t help my progress,” he said.

“That’s why I decided to come to Sundowns and I knew the competition that they have here in the team, and that’s what I wanted. That is what I was looking for, the reason I came here.”

Coetzee will he hoping he again breaks into the starting line-up tomorrow afternoon when Sundowns face Golden Arrows in the semi-final of the Telkom Knockout for a chance to reach the final and end their four-year domestic cup drought.
