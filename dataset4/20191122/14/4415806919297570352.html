Hunger-striking grandmother ‘unimpressed’ after meeting minister

22 Nov 2019, 14:37

A grandmother on hunger strike in protest at the State's climate change policies says she was “unimpressed” after a meeting with the environment minister Richard Bruton.

Patricia Devlin, 73, who is originally from Australia, but has lived in Monasterevin, Co Kildare, for 20 years, began the four-day strike on Wednesday in the hope of meeting Mr Bruton and calling on him to implement tougher policies to tackle the climate emergency.

Ms Devlin met Mr Bruton on Thursday evening outside the Dáil, where she was protesting, but says the meeting was a disappointment.
