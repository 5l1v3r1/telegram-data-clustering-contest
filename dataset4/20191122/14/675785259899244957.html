Pep Guardiola defends Bernardo Silva following Man City star's racism ban

Manchester City will be without Portugal international Bernardo this weekend, after he was handed a one-match FA ban related to a tweet sent to Benjamin Mendy

22 Nov 2019, 14:26 by David McDonnell, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/david-mcdonnell/

Pep Guardiola claimed Bernardo Silva has been accused of being a racist and defended his player against the charge.

Silva is suspended for City's game against Chelsea tomorrow after the FA hit him with a one-match ban after he admitted a misconduct charge.

The Portugal international was also fined £50,0000 and ordered to undertake an education programme.

The charge related to a tweet Silva sent to City team-mate Benjamin Mendy, comparing him to a character depicted on a packet of Conguitos, a chocolate brand available in Spain and Portugal.

The FA charged Silva after stating the post constituted an “aggravated breach” of its rules on social media behaviour, because it appeared to reference Mendy's race.

Although Silva admitted the charge, Guardiola claimed the player had been accused of being something he is not and re-iterated his support for him.

“In that situation, he’s been accused of something he’s not,” said Guardiola.

“If they want to pick up someone to show something [make an example of] it was the right person.

“He will be careful with social media, but if this can help make a better society then Bernardo will accept it.

"But he’s not what he’s accused of.”

Guardiola also spoke publicly for the first time since City forward Raheem Sterling was dropped by England boss Gareth Southgate for an altercation with Joe Gomez.

“He’s good,” said Guardiola. “I spoke with him, he’s fine, he said Gomez is an excellent guy, they have a good relationship, it sometimes happens.”

City goalkeeper Ederson, who missed the 3-1 defeat at Premier League leaders Liverpool with a thigh injury, is back to face Chelsea.

But City are still missing Aymeric Laporte, Leroy Sane and Alex Zinchenko.
