Everton fans can watch the Merseyside Derby free on Amazon

Six of Everton's seven league fixtures will be broadcast throughout the month

22 Nov 2019, 14:59 by Neil Bennett

When the Premier League fixtures were announced this summer, many Everton eyes were drawn to the month of December.

Along with games against four of the supposed 'Big 6', the Toffees side kick-off the month against high-flying Leicester City.

With eight games in the space of 28 days, it's a month that promises to define the second half of Everton's season - and potentially the future of manager Marco Silva.

Only one of Everton's forthcoming league games - Newcastle United on Saturday December 28th - won't be televised, with the rest being broadcast across Sky Sports, BT Sport and newcomer Amazon .

Amazon have entered the market with a bang having secured the rights to both the Merseyside Derby as well as new Tottenham manager Jose Mourinho's return to Old Trafford.

Below, you'll find a list of all Everton's televised fixtures throughout the month of December as well as information on the cheapest way to watch them.

  • Leicester City (A) - Sunday, December 1st - 4:30pm - Sky Sports
  • Liverpool (A) - Wednesday, December 4th - 8:15pm - Amazon Prime
  • Chelsea (H) - Saturday, December 7th - 12:30pm - BT Sport
  • Manchester United (A) - Sunday, December 15th - 2:00pm - Sky Sports
  • Arsenal (H) - Saturday, December 21st - 12:30pm - BT Sport
  • Burnley (H) - Thursday, December 26th - 3pm - Amazon Prime

Amazon

As well as giving every team in the Premier League airtime, one of the best things about the festive football being on Amazon is you can enjoy it entirely free of charge.

All you have to do is order Amazon Prime from next week and enjoy their free 30 day trial.

To start your today, go to amazon.co.uk/amazonprime

Sky Sports

In the lead-up to the Black Friday sales, Sky are offering 50% discounts on their Sky Sports packages through the online Sky Store .

You can also take advantage of the Now TV Black Friday sales where Now TV Sports Passes have been reduced by 41% between now and the end of the Premier League season.

'1 Month Bundles' have been reduced to £19.99 a month, while the regular Month Passes have gone from £33.99 a month to £20 a month.

BT Sport

Alongside their coverage of the Premier League, BT Sport will also be broadcasting the conclusion of both the Champions League and Europa League group stages throughout the course of December.

The telecommunications giant have just announced their very own deal with Amazon.

Soon to become the only UK TV provider to include Amazon Prime Video, BT customers will receive a free six month Prime Video subscription.
