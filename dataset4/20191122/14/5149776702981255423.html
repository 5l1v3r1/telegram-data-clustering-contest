Where to find a Santa's grotto across Nottingham and beyond this Christmas

From theme parks and garden centres to caves and stately homes

22 Nov 2019, 14:15 by Rachel Gorman

The Christmas lights are on, Winter Wonderland is open and Santa Claus is officially coming to town.

Although you won't be able to visit him in a grotto anywhere in the city, after bosses at intu Broadmarsh decided to give him a break as the multi-million pound redevelopment of the shopping centre continues, he is visiting dozens of locations across Nottinghamshire (and beyond) this Yuletide season.

You'll be able to meet the big man himself at theme parks and garden centres to caves and stately homes.

Wheelgate Park

The family theme park near Mansfield will be transformed into an enchanted wonderland, where children can help the elves on the animal farm and feed Santa’s reindeer.

Illuminated lights, a twinkling snow scene and giant Christmas tree will provide plenty of photo opportunities alongside the festive workshops and trip to Santa's Grotto where kids can choose a gift from Father Christmas' workshop.

The 60,000 sq ft heated indoor adventure zones, including the UK’s largest indoor play centre and 60 acres of attractions will be open as usual from 10am – 5pm, except Christmas Eve when it closes at 3pm.

Tickets to Santa's Grotto are £6.95 per child for a 15 minute visit. A ticket to the theme park needs to be purchased also from wheelgatepark.com.

Rufford Abbey

Good little boys and girls can visit the main man this festive season in the historic grounds of 12th century abbey, once the home to a Cistercian monastery.

It will be open November 16, 17, 23, 24, 30 and every weekend in December (plus Monday, December 23), from 10.30am to 4pm.

Time slots can be booked in advance at parkwoodoutdoors.co.uk.

Tickets are £5 per child.

Sundown Adventureland

Take a magical sleigh ride through an enchanted forest to meet Santa himself at Sundown Adventureland, in Retford, this year.

The park will be given a winter wonderland makeover ahead of the unveiling of the Night Before Christmas ride where families will be greeted by elves, meet Mrs Claus and get up close to real-life reindeer.

There will also be a Christmas marquee, a Snow Queen, dancing bears and entertainment from Andy and Lewis.

Dates are available up to Christmas Eve, with tickets available to book at sundownadventureland.co.uk.

Depending on the age of your child, tickets are between £17.25 and £20.25.

Twinlakes

Travel through the fairy tale forest, ride The Polar Express train and meet Santa in his cosy log cabin at Twinlakes theme park, in Melton Mowbray.

Visits costing £9 per child last 15 minutes and include a special token from Father Christmas which can be taken to the gift shop in exchange for a gift.

A ticket to the theme park needs to be purchased also at twinlakespark.co.uk.

A photographer will also be on hand to snap a picture of the whole family with Santa, which is then available to purchase as you leave the grotto.

Open November, 23-24 & 30, December, 1, 7-8, 12-13, 15 & 18-23.

Creswell Crags

Every Saturday and Sunday from November 30 to December 15, Santa himself will be popping into Creswell Crags to double check his naughty and nice list.

Tickets to see Mr Claus, who will be in his grotto from 11am-4pm, are £4.50 per child. These do not need to be booked in advance but waiting times are expected to fluctuate throughout the day, so it is advised to arrive early.

Newstead Abbey

Santa will be popping into Newstead on weekends throughout December to listen to your Christmas wishes.

He will be sat in his grotto, in the Cafe Courtyard, from 11am to 3pm on Saturdays and Sundays.

Entry is £6 per child with one free accompanying adult and includes a gift.

Timmermans Garden Centre

Families can meet Father Christmas at a special Santa Breakfast.

Starting at 8.30am, once everyone is seated, a freshly cooked breakfast will be served.

Santa will then come up to the Woodborough garden centre restaurant with one of his helpers to walk around and say hello to the children and pose for photographs before returning to his grotto.

Once breakfast is finished visitors can walk through the winter wonderland and see Santa in his grotto, where the children will be given a gift.

Breakfast with Santa will be held on the November, 30, December 1, 7, 8, 14, 15, 21, 22 & 23.

Tickets are £14.99 for children and £8.99 for adults. To book either pop in or call the garden centre restaurant (0115 966 4033 option 2) and speak to either Kirsty or Megan.

Brookfields Garden Centre

Santa's Grotto will be open weekends from Sunday, November 16 to Sunday, December 23.

Ticket are £5 per child and includes a wrapped gift and £1 donation to Nottinghamshire Hospice.

To book a slot visit brookfieldsgardencentre.co.uk.
