Nigerians Battle Senators As Hate Speech Bill Passes Second Reading

22 Nov 2019, 14:38 by Emmanuel Egobiambu

Nigerians have totally condemned the Senate for passing the Hate Speech Bill into its second reading, describing it as an attempt to infringe on the freedom of expression.

Concise News understands that the proposed law which is tagged the Protection from Internet Falsehood and Manipulations Bill seeks to regulate the usage of social media and curb fake news.

The Senate had directed its committee on judiciary to work on the bill and return in four weeks following the second reading.

Senator Mohammed Musa from Niger East under the ruling All Progressives Congress (APC) introduced the bill to the Senate on Tuesday, November 5, 2019.

Nigerians have, however, condemned the bill, saying it is an attempt to muzzle them; stop them from expressing themselves.
