  • Kohli also spoke about taking special care of the Test specialists

KOLKATA: Is day-night cricket the future for Tests? India skipper Virat Kohli does not think so.

"In my opinion, this should not become the only way Test cricket is played,” he said at the Eden Gardens on Thursday. "If that happens, you are losing out on the nervousness of the first session. You can bring excitement, but you can’t have Test cricket only for entertainment."

Kohli has always maintained that Test match is the ultimate form of cricket, and refused to accept it is on the wane. Kohli and many others still feel the battle between bat and ball is the most challenging in Test cricket. "The entertainment of Test cricket lies in the batsman trying to survive a session and bowlers trying to set him up. If people don’t respond to that, too bad. Someone who gets a boost from this battle, in my opinion those are the ones who should come to watch, because they understand the game."
