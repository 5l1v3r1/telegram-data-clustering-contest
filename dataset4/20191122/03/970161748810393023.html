How the Manchester United board reacted to Jose Mourinho's move to Tottenham

22 Nov 2019, 03:44

The Manchester United hierarchy reacted with ‘intrigued silence’ at the news that Jose Mourinho had returned to Premier League management with Tottenham.

The 56-year-old made his return after 11 months out of the game by signing a three-and-a-half year deal with Tottenham on Wednesday.

Mourinho’s been out of work since his tumultuous sacking at Old Trafford last December and a clause in his departure prevented him from speaking about his exit during his stint with Sky Sports.

United, of course, expected him to return to management but the expectation was that he would seek a future away from the Premier League.

And the Independent claim that United’s board were caught by surprise by the news that he’d been appointed by Tottenham and reacted with ‘intrigued silence’.

Reports claim Mourinho was not Spurs’ first choice to replace Pochettino but the Portuguese ‘blew away’ club officials with his pitch for the role.

Mourinho was said to have shown a clear plan for where he sees the team going and what players he plans to buy and sell in the next 18 months.

Spurs take on West Ham at the London Stadium in Mourinho’s first game on Saturday.

Mourinho has taken training in the last two days and all eyes will be on the starting XI he selects in east London.
