Steve Bruce opens up on 'lack of information' at Newcastle United which Steve Harper can now help resolve

22 Nov 2019, 16:02 by Lee Ryder

Steve Bruce insisted a fortnight ago he wanted to have an input into what came next at Newcastle United's Academy following the exit of Neil Redfearn.

The Under-23 coach quit his post after just a few months in the job this month but Bruce has worked with the club's hierarchy to come up with a plan that should help create what he sees as "a bridge" between the club's younger sides and the first team.

In former goalkeeper Steve Harper - handed the title of first team coach - the long-serving ex-Toon star will work between Little Benton and the club's senior training base.

As reported on Chronicle Live on November 10 , Bruce was eager to make some tweaks to the set-up at Newcastle after getting a good feel for the club since being appointed in July.

Harper had been spotted at the last Under-23 game in deep conversation with Bruce during 1-0 loss against West Brom in the Premier League Cup defeat at Whitley Park.

After announcing the change, Bruce said: "Since I came through the door I have always taken a great pleasure in seeing what our Academy is producing.

"With the difficulty of being on two sites it is vitally important we have a link between the two.

"And certainly Steve Harper will give us that link.

"He is somebody who I had the pleasure of managing when I took him to Hull for a couple of years.

"He's went on to do his coaching badges and he is one of the young brigade."

When asked to describe Harper's new role, Bruce, speaking to NUFC's TV channel, said: "He will come in and do a little bit of coaching, not just goalkeeping.

"More importantly it gives us this bridge between the first team and the Academy because it's important I have enough information.

"At the minute I just think we are lacking in that department.

"We welcome Steve aboard and I am sure he will do a great job.

"He's Newcastle through and through and it is the start of a new career for him.

"I hope this can be a platform for him to do bigger and better things."

A club statement summed up Harper's new position and read: "Steve will work alongside first team coaches Steve Agnew and Stephen Clemence and head of goalkeeping Simon Smith as a key part of Steve Bruce's backroom team.

"Part of Harper's role will be to work with Academy staff as the club looks to forge closer links between the first team and its Academy set-up, building on a pathway that has seen the likes of Sean and Matty Longstaff make their Premier League debuts over the past 18 months."

Harper is also set to stay on as part of Northern Ireland's backroom team and will combine his roles.
