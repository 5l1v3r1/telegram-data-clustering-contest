Austin MacPhee blasts BBC Hearts pundit Allan Preston's 'four lies' in withering response

The interim Hearts boss isn't happy with the BBC pundit and agent Allan Preston.

22 Nov 2019, 16:53 by Jonathan McFarlane

Angered Austin MacPhee has laid into Allan Preston after the BBC radio pundit questioned his credentials.

The caretaker Hearts boss is fuming with the former Jambo who launched a verbal attack live on air two weeks ago.

Preston – who is also an agent – left MacPhee without a name as he accused the 40-year-old putting "two fingers up to Craig Levein" – and being "tactically all over the place".

Now the man who could become the new Tynecastle sporting director has hit back with an evisceration of his own.

Seething, MacPhee said: "The results I have had as the manager speak for themselves. I mentioned them last week and prior to the game I spoke with confidence about what I  thought the team could achieve in that game and where I think the club can go.

"I know in particular Allan Preston on the BBC opened the program before the game by trying to discredit me with four different statements in particular which were just lies which I was very disappointed with.

"I thought it was a slight on the players, a slight on the club and for the BBC's voice of Hearts to make these statements I thought was very poor because, for example, he stated that all we were doing was bringing in players from Manchester City's academy.

"I believe the difference between a lie and an error is if it's done with malice and I believe that if you open the show heavily critical of me then back it up with four points which are factually incorrect I believe that's four lies.

"So the first one was that we are bringing in players from Manchester City's academy. That's fabrication, it's not true. Ryo signed for €1m from Gamba Osake, he didn't set foot in Manchester City's Academy.

"The second one was that Craig Wighton was brought in from nowhere so I could stick two fingers up to Craig Levein. That is a complete lie.

"Craig Wighton, in his last two games when he was fit and available started at Parkhead then came on in the Cup Final before suffering a medial ligament injury in preseason at Inverness so the Rangers game was the first game that he could be selected.
