Giroud risks ruining January transfer over wage demands

22 Nov 2019, 16:16 by Buzz Contributor

Olivier Giroud could price himself out of a January exit from Chelsea due to his sky-high wage demands.

Giroud is out-of-favour at Stamord Bridge, with boss Frank Lampard putting his faith in Tammy Abraham this season.

Abraham has rewarded Lampard by netting 11 goals and is could spell the end of Giroud’s spell in west London.

The Frenchman wants out of the club just two years after joining from Arsenal with the 33-year-old keen to play first-team football and boost his chances of a spot in France’s line-up at Euro 2020.

A host of clubs are queuing up to get Giroud - but he’s refusing to leave on the cheap.

The Express report the striker wants a two-year contract worth a mega £7m-a-season to leave Chelsea.

And that could spell the end of interest from the likes of Marseille, Montpellier, Lyon, Crystal Palace and Inter Milan .

Antonio Conte’s Inter are just one side pausing their interest in light of Giroud’s lofty demands.

They’ve got Alexis Sanchez on the books and fighting back to fitness after a dislocated ankle, but are only prepared to put an 18-month deal on the table for the ageing frontman.

Chelsea want around £5m for Giroud, who is available for nothing in the summer when his contract runs down.

Crystal Palace have been keen on the player, but are now eyeing up Celtic’s Odsonne Eduard instead.

The 21-year-old is seen as a better long-term investment, and the 13 goals in 24 games striker is now at the head of their shopping list.

France boss Didier Deschamps explained why he’s kept Giroud in his recent squads despite his lack of action at Chelsea: "He is useful and indispensable. He is very effective and made the decision to shoot the penalty. He allows players around him to have goal chances too.

"Which also explains why he stays with us despite his playing time at Chelsea. He suffers inevitably from this situation on a daily basis.

"If he has an opportunity to move and have more playing time, it's good for him, he knows it."
