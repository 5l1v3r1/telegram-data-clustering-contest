ROAD SMASH

Woman rushed to hospital with serious injuries after being hit by car outside Kirkcaldy Asda

22 Nov 2019, 16:54 by Justin Bowie

A WOMAN was rushed to hospital with serious injuries after being hit by a car outside a Kirkcaldy Asda.

Emergency services rushed to the scene after the horror smash and locked down Carberry Road in the town this afternoon.

Police confirmed that an ambulance was in attendance to treat the woman hurt in the collision, who is in her 50s.

A Scottish Ambulance Service spokesperson said: "We received a call at 15.02pm today to attend an incident in Kirkcaldy.

"We dispatched one ambulance to the scene which arrived at 15.03pm. We transported one female patient in her 50s to Victoria Hospital.”

The road remains closed with diversions in place for drivers.

Pics from the scene showed long traffic queues with the ambulance at the scene.

A Police Scotland spokesperson said: "We were called at 3.05pm to a report of a crash involving a car and a pedestrian on Carberry Road.

"The road is closed.

"Locals diversions have been put in place."
