EXTREMELY PROUD

Donald Trump impeachment – Scots uni chiefs hailed two ex-students for ‘speaking truth to power’ during hearings

22 Nov 2019, 16:49 by Graham Mann

SCOTS uni chiefs have hailed two former students for “speaking truth to power” during the sensational Donald Trump impeachment hearings.

Foreign affairs boffin Dr Fiona Hill spent four years at the University of St Andrews studying Russian and history on her way to becoming a top White House adviser.

And her US state department colleague David Holmes – who also gave evidence - completed a degree in international affairs in 2002.

The diplomats were widely-praised online for the way they performed in front of the house intelligence committee on Thursday - with many describing Dr Hill has a ‘hero’.

A uni spokeswoman said: “Fiona and David are part of long tradition of diplomats, politicians and experts in international relations to have studied here in St Andrews.

“We were extremely proud to watch them speaking truth to power in the clear and confident style which is a hallmark of their professionalism and service.”

Dr Hill – an English-born Russia expert, from County Durham - told of her fears the US was ‘running out of time’ to prevent Vladimir Putin and his intelligence forces from repeating their assault on next year’s US elections.

She delivered the warning after having rubbished President Trump’s repeated claim that Russia’s interference in his 2016 victory was a “hoax”.

The 54-year-old – now a US citizen – said: “This is a fictional narrative that has been perpetrated and propagated by the Russian security services themselves.”

She told the committee: “Right now, Russia’s security services and their proxies have geared up to repeat their interference in the 2020 election.

“We are running out of time to stop them. In the course of this investigation, I would ask that you please not promote politically driven falsehoods that so clearly advance Russian interests.”

The straight-talking Geordie’s testimony - broadcast live around the globe – went down a storm with punters impressed by her performance.

Twitter user Samantha Waterman oozed: “Fiona Hill is my hero. Absolutely phenomenal, in every way.”

Jim Seven said: “Fiona Hill is impressively intelligent. Too bad she can’t be president. The contrast between her and Trump is stunning.”

Frank Stanton agreed, posting: “This person is becoming a hero by virtue of telling the truth, with an edge in her voice. Right on.”

And new-found fan Fred Harding added: “Dr Fiona Hill told a very dangerous truth today. She did it for each and every one of us. She is a hero.”

Dr Hill attended the Fife university between 1984 and 1987 before landing a place on an academic exchange to the then Soviet Union.

Her linked-in profile reveals she earned a master’s degree in Russian studies and modern history before completing a PhD in Soviet studies at Harvard University in the States.

She told the committee of her Brit background, growing up the daughter of a coal miner in the north east of England before moving to Scotland to start her stellar academic career.

Dr Hill told the hearing US ambassador Gordon Sondland carried out a "domestic political errand" for the President and didn’t keep her in the loop over Ukraine.

Mr Trump has been accused of withholding military aid from Ukraine unless it agreed to investigate rival Joe Biden and his son Hunter.

Before Dr Hill’s evidence, Mr Trump posted a tweet urging Republican committee members to “keep fighting” because “you are dealing with human scum”.
