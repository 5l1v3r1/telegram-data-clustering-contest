SNP Health Secretary's future as MSP in doubt as she refuses to confirm election plans

Under-fire minister Jeane Freeman repeatedly refused to say whether she will stand at the next Holyrood election.

22 Nov 2019, 04:30 by Paul Hutcheon, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/paul-hutcheon/

health Secretary Jeane Freeman’s future as an MSP is in “doubt” after she repeatedly refused to commit to standing for Holyrood again.

Freeman, under pressure over a double hospitals crisis, said her plan for the 2021 election is a “matter for another day”.

Labour MSP Monica Lennon said: “It sounds like Jeane Freeman’s future as an MSP is in doubt, but what the public really want to know is whether she is fulfilling her responsibilities as Health Secretary now.”

Elected as an SNP MSP for Carrick, Cumnock and Doon Valley in 2016, Freeman was made a Minister before being promoted to Health Secretary.

However, her tenure has been marked by rows over problems in the NHS she inherited from her predecessors.

She intervened in the summer to block the opening of the new Sick Kids hospital in Edinburgh amid concerns over safety.

 

Freeman has also faced flak over the mounting problems at the £842m Queen Elizabeth University Hospital (QEUH) campus in Glasgow.

As revealed by the Record, a whistleblower alleged that 26 child cancer patients had contracted water-based infections in 2017.

The insider claimed that one of the children, later revealed to be 10-year-old Milly Main, had died.

Freeman has described NHS Greater Glasgow and Clyde’s handling of the row as “appalling” and she has turned on the board for criticising the whistleblower.

The MSP has resisted calls for her resignation, but there are question over whether she will stand for re-election in 16 months' time.

Asked by this newspaper whether she will definitely stand again in 2021, she said: “We are far too early to make those decisions, genuinely far too early. No, I’ve got a number of things I need to do. I need to deliver on our waiting times plan, I need to increase the pace of integration, I need to sort out the things we need to sort out in mental health.

“Most importantly, I need to make sure that these families get to the truth. So that is what I am considering and that is what I am focused on. Whether or not I’m a candidate in 2021 is a matter for another day.”

Asked a variation of the same question on two more occasions, she replied: “It is a matter for another day.”

Pressed again, she said: “I will think about that nearer the time.”

 

It came as Nicola Sturgeon pledged to parents grieving after the death of youngsters at the QEUH that there will be “no stone unturned” in their quest for answers.

The First Minister expressed her “deepest condolences” to the families whose children had died amid concerns over water contamination at the super-hospital.

Sturgeon also said there was a “strong case” for NHS Greater Glasgow and Clyde to be put into “special measures”, with the Scottish Government increasing its level of involvement in the board.

Scottish Tory MSP Miles Briggs said: “Given her botched handling of this latest hospital deaths scandal, it’s no wonder Jeane Freeman is considering her position.

“And for the good of Scotland’s NHS, she should resign as health secretary immediately regardless of whether or not she stands in 2021.”
