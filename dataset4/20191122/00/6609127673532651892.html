MEAL DRIVE

Scottish Labour pledge to give free school meals to every child in General Election manifesto

22 Nov 2019, 00:03 by Chris Musson

SCOTTISH Labour will unveil a General Election pledge today for free school meals for all - even though Jeremy Corbyn would have no power to introduce the policy.

Richard Leonard, the party’s boss in Scotland, will make the promise today as he published his own manifesto ahead of December 12.

But the UK Government has no control over the Scottish education system, which is devolved.

And aides admitted they could only guarantee it become reality if Scottish Labour - currently the Scottish Parliament’s third party - win the 2021 Holyrood elections.

But they said the Scottish Government should use extra cash coming to Scotland via increased public spending down south for the policy.

Mr Leonard said it would benefit about 700,000 children primary, secondary and special educational schools, and cost an extra £190 million per year

He said: “It cannot be right that in the fifth biggest economy in the world, one in every four children in Scotland is living in poverty.

"After a decade of Tory austerity and SNP complacency, it is time for real and radical change.

“That is why I am proud to announce today that a UK Labour government will provide the funds for a free meal for every child 365 days a year, regardless of their social and economic backgrounds.

"Children should not be put in a position where they are left behind through no fault of their own and through no fault of their family.

"Scottish Labour will end the scourge of child poverty once and for all.”
