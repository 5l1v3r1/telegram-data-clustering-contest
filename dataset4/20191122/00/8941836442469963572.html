More deliberations in shotgun killing trial in Maine

22 Nov 2019

PORTLAND, Maine (AP) - A jury is returning for more deliberations in the trial of a man in the shotgun death of his wife in Windham, Maine.

Jurors deliberated for several hours Wednesday and then all day on Thursday in the trial of Noah Gaston. They were set to resume their deliberations Friday morning.

The defense doesn’t dispute that Gaston shot his wife but contends he thought she was an intruder. Prosecutors said he intended to kill Alicia Gaston, or at least acted recklessly.

The jury is weighing whether to convict Gaston of manslaughter or murder, or neither.

Gaston, who did not testify during the trial, has been held at the Cumberland County Jail since his arrest in January 2016.
