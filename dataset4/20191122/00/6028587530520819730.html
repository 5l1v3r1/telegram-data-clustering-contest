Where To Feast On Truffles In Boston This Season

22 Nov 2019 by Jeanne O'Brien Coffey

Want to immediately impress your friends, perfume your meal — and add a couple hundred bucks to your dinner tab in the blink of an eye? It’s truffle season, and this fungus that grows at the base of trees – especially oaks – in Europe and has stubbornly refused cultivation, doesn’t come cheap. But when November arrives, chefs are keen to shave truffles on everything. Whether you want to pick up your own to perfume your kitchen on the cheap, enjoy a truffled ramen noodle cacio e pepe ($145) or splash out on a seven-course truffle menu, including truffled macarons for dessert ($250) – here’s a list of top spots.

Eataly’s gone all out for the tuber with a storewide truffle celebration is intended to offer Bostonians a chance to enjoy them in an accessible, affordable way, aiming to bring joy to everyone. To that end, they are slashing prices for two Truffle Days, Wednesday, November 27 and Sunday, December 22. On Nov. 27th, truffles in retail will be $4.90 per gram – up to 55 percent off, and available in all their restaurants: $25 for 4 grams. Final prices for the second Truffle Day will be announced closer in, when they understand the harvest.

Additionally, enjoy truffles for lunch and dinner every day at La Pizza & La Pasta, La Pescheria, and La Piazza, for a supplement of $45/4g fresh white truffles to add-on to any dish, or a Tajarin con Tartufo Bianco ($45) – egg yolk tajarin pasta with fresh white truffles

 At dinner, every day at Terra try the Chef’s Celebration of Truffle Dinner: three courses for $45 per person or seven courses for $95 per person, with portions of proceeds going to Key West Cares, an organization supporting Hurricane Dorian relief efforts in the Bahamas.

 Sinatra at Encore Boston

A truffle macaron sounds like maybe a bit much, but I can tell you first hand it’s delicious, topping an impressive seven-course meal at Sinatra, the high-end Italian restaurant in Boston’s new gambling destination. See for yourself on December 19th, when Property Executive Chef Joseph Leibowitz, Chef de Cuisine Timothy Hixson and Executive Pastry Chef Joel Reno collaborate for a decadent truffle feast, from a salad with shaved white truffles and a white truffle vinaigrette, to truffle gelato and those Opalys truffle macarons. The meal, paired with a very thoughtful selection of fine Italian wines, costs $250 and is served in the restaurant’s intimate private dining room. 

Chef Daniel Bruce is offering Pasta Ouvo as a nightly special through the months of November and December, served with aged parmesan, cheese sauce, shaved white alba truffles. It is market price based on the weekly rate. 

Executive Chef and Partner Robert Sisca has a special way with truffles, so of course Bistro du Midi is offering a daily special all season: Saffron Tagliatelle Pasta, truffle buratta, wild mushrooms, and fresh alba white truffle, available nightly through the months of November and December. Want more? Bistro is celebrating their 10th anniversary this month with a seasonal tasting menu December-February, featuring a retrospective menu of dishes from the past 10 years, a special list of 2009 vintage champagnes and spirits, and a white truffle martini from their opening menu.

Bar Mezzana, Boston

Chef Colin Lynch is ramping up his coastal Italian menu with truffles shaved tableside over Tagliolini with Parmesan and Butter for $75

No Relation, Boston

Chef Colin Lynch is also behind this new hidden omakase sushi bar, where on a nightly basis the 9 seats and 14 courses make for an exceptional meal. During the season, guests can incorporate two truffle components into the tasting menus- four courses for an additional $85, or for $25, you can add truffles to one bite.

Deuxave, Boston

Deuxave has a daily Alba White Truffle special -currently a fontina cheese tortellini with white truffles shaved to order tableside –that changes nightly. Guests can request either two grams or four grams at market price. The restaurant is also offering a supplement to their Chef’s Tasting Menu to include a white truffle dish for an added cost, based on market price. Or go all out with the five-course White Truffle Tasting Menu, which changes daily. 

UNI, Boston

Chef Tony Messina is sourcing White Alba Truffles for his Back Bay izakaya, offering a Ramen Noodle Cacio e Pepe, $145, a Bay Scallop Risotto Spoon, $45, or for $85, you can supplement to any dish.

Talulla, Boston

For one night only, Thursday, December 2nd, 2019, Conor Dennehy and Danielle Ayer, the husband and wife team behind Talulla, are offering a six-course truffle dinner. Starting with Scallop Crudo, Green Apple, White Truffle, the meal uses truffles in various forms; both black and white varieties, whipped into creams, delicately shaved on top of hand-made pastas, and infused into cream. Link to Truffle Dinner here. Price: $175, wine pairing an additional $95 

Raleigh Wine Bar + Eatery, Portsmouth, NH

Chef Jeremy Glover is featuring three truffle dishes a la carte: Sea Bass en Croute with Crab Mousse, and Black Truffle, Rush Creek Reserve Grilled Cheese, with Braised Collard Greens and  Black Truffle, and a Kohlrabi Gratin with Hazelnut Oil and White Truffle

Ledger Restaurant + Bar, Salem, Mass.

Starting this weekend, Chef Daniel Gursha will be offering truffles shaved tableside as an addition to any dish, plus some special entrees like gnocchi with Parmesan cream and truffles.
