Citrus tree disease appears in San Bernardino County

22 Nov 2019

MONTCLAIR  (INT)  The dreaded Asian Citrus Psyllid and the disease it carries has shown up in San Bernardino County.

The California Department of Agriculture says its the first time Huanglongbing (HLB) has been detected in that county. A single citrus tree in an unincorporated area of San near Montclair was found to be infected.

A 93-square-mile quarantine has been established. It forbids the movement of all citrus nursery stock or plant parts out of the quarantine area. Fruit that is not commercially cleaned and packed, including residential citrus, such as oranges, lemons, grapefruits, and kumquats, must not be moved from the property on which it is grown.

Residents are being urged to take steps to help protect citrus trees.

Once a tree is infected, there is no cure; the tree will produce bitter and misshaped fruit and die within a few years.
