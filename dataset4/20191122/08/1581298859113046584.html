Esperance volunteer firefighters thanked four years on from tragedy

22 Nov 2019, 08:30 by Sarah Makse

On the four-year anniversary of the devastating Esperance region bushfires, local firefighters battled another blaze in Cascade.

The fire on November 17, 2019, tore through more than 750 hectares.

Through a coordinated local response, it was contained and alert was downgraded by nightfall.

Department of Fire and Emergency Services Great Southern superintendent Wayne Green thanked firefighters and the Shire of Esperance.

"Being that it's the anniversary, our thoughts are still with the families involved, the Curnow, Butcher, Sushchova-Winther and Kohrs-Lichte families," he said.

"Also the response crews out there, it's been playing in their minds that this is two years in a row that they have had fires on, or very close to that anniversary date.

"I pass on gratitude and my thanks to everyone who was involved in that fire over the weekend."

Mr Green said the blaze was caused by an accidental header fire, which quickly spread through dry Unallocated Crown Land.

"The fire did take a good run through some pasture, it started on private property and the winds fanned it along," he said.

Mr Green said more than 20 vehicles responded and extra resources were sent to the scene before it escalated.

"We had our DFES officers embedded into the local government incident in support very early, rather than waiting for a request for assistance," he said.

"It was just a fantastic team effort to be able to get on top of these fire so quickly, but also to prepare for the unknown.

"Rather than waiting for it to get bad then ramping up our response, we are sending the response early."

Mr Green said with dry conditions and record breaking temperatures across the state, residents should prepare for the season ahead.

"We need people to be familiar with the Emergency WA website and finding information about how they can best prepare, not only during an event but right now," he said.

"People do need to be prepared, especially around their homesteads or around their properties in neighboring bushland.

"They need to make sure that their gutters are cleaned out, there's no debris and other basic housekeeping around the property."

The 2015 Esperance bushfires burned for 11 days, destroying hundreds of thousands of hectares and claimed the lives of Kim 'Freddy' Curnow, Thomas Butcher, Anna Sushchova-Winther and Julia Kohrs-Lichte.

In a touching tribute, sister of Tom Butcher, Jess Greenaway, said she would always hold on to the memory of her 'hero'.

"November 17, four years on you're still here in my heart and mind bro, still making me laugh because your stories live on," she said.

"I hold you in a thought and I can feel you. I feel you and this gives me strength and courage.

"The tears I have cried for you could flood the earth, and I know you have wiped each one away.

"For you brother, I promise you this, I will go on with my life and make you proud. I will always hold you in my heart.

"You will forever be in our lives, you will forever be a brother, a son, an uncle, a brother in law and friend.

"Love you so much bro, miss you so much, always my hero."
