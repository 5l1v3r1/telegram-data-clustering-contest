Dejan Lovren explains how "unbelievable" Joe Gomez reacted to Raheem Sterling incident

The Croatian defender praised the way his teammate handled the training ground incident which led to Sterling being left out of England's game against Montenegro

22 Nov 2019, 08:58 by Mark Jones, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/mark-jones/

Dejan Lovren has praised his "unbelievable" Liverpool teammate Joe Gomez for the way he reacted to an incident with Raheem Sterling on England duty.

Sterling was dropped by England boss Gareth Southgate after he left Gomez with a scratch around his eye in a seemingly unprovoked altercation just a day after the pair had gone head-to-head in the Premier League.

Gomez and Sterling clashed in the closing stages of Liverpool's 3-1 win over Manchester City at Anfield before the international break, and Sterling seemingly still had that on his mind when he saw Gomez at St. George's Park a day later.

Southgate dropped him from England's clash with Montenegro, with a smattering of England fans then booing Gomez when he came on as a substitute in the same game.

Sterling immediately took to Twitter to criticise that reaction , and Lovren - who played with Sterling for one season at Liverpool - says that his fellow defender has now put it all behind him.

"I think he reacted well, to be honest," the Croatian told Sky Sports .

"I've been speaking with him two or three days ago and asked about it and he was OK, positive and this is what is important for the club and his team-mates.

"We are behind him and that's what he needs at the moment."

Lovren added that Gomez didn't deserve the reaction he received from some England fans.

"He's an unbelievable man and player but it's aggravated everything about that and he didn't deserve it," he added.

"You're playing for the national team and he's there, giving his best so I don't think he deserved the boos."

Lovren and Liverpool go to Crystal Palace on Saturday looking to maintain or improve their eight point lead at the top of the Premier League table.
