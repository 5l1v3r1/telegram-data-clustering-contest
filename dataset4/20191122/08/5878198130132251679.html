Rejected by BBC and ITV, Netflix finally acquires popular Nigerian sitcom, “Meet the Adebanjos”

22 Nov 2019, 08:30 by Novieku-Babatunde Adeola

Netflix has acquired popular British-Nigerian sitcom, “Meet the Adebanjos”. 

The comedy sitcom which has gained popularity across the continent showcases the unusual ups and downs of a first-generation Nigerian couple who are raising their British-born children.

Ranked as “UK’s Number 1 British African sitcom”, it all began in 2011 as a web series that was self-funded by the show’s creators, Osayemi and Deborah Odutayo.

Despite their failure to market the content to major traditional broadcasters at their early stage, they decided to put the show online. The decision paid off as the official trailer attracted 10, 000 views on Youtube in its first week and the numbers increased to 1.4 million viewers over several weeks.

“I pitched to the BBC and they said no, pitched to ITV — NO, Every broadcaster in the UK? NO NO NO. I was at my wit’s end. I was devastated. I had risked all of my investors’ money-making 7 episodes of a TV show and couldn’t find a buyer for it,” Osayemi recounted.
