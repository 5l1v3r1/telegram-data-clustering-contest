Company boss said he knew nothing about millions of smuggled cigarettes inside hollowed-out washing machines

The trial continues

22 Nov 2019, 08:11 by Rod Malcolm

A company boss told a court that he had no idea that 2.6 million smuggled cigarettes were inside washing machines delivered to Nottingham.

"I know nothing of that," said Rafal Szczepanik of Leeway Road, Southwell as he gave evidence at his trial.

Nottingham Crown Court has been told that the cigarettes were of two brands which should never be sold in the UK. The haul evaded tax amounting to Â£756,422 and was found inside hollowed-out washing machines.

Szczepanik, 42, described how he was arrested when he arrived at a storage unit he had leased on Trent South Industrial Park near Little Tennis Street on June 28.

Investigators were waiting and he told a jury: "I was approached by a woman who started shouting 'sir, sir.' She started calling her colleague.

"I was standing next to her and he shouted 'you are arrested.'"

Questioned by Mark Cotter QC, defending, he said that he owns a bonded warehouse near his Polish home, which is six miles from the Ukrainian border.

His customers include people from Russia and the Ukraine. He said: "They buy goods around the whole of Europe and the world. They send them to us in containers and lorries."

He said that he leased storage space in London but this was not a success because of a family illness.

A stepdaughter runs a cafe in Nottingham and so he decided to find one in the city.

Four men have admitted being concerned in the fraudulent evasion of excise duty.

Szczepanik denies the charge.

The court has been told that the case was investigated by HM Revenue and Customs officers. They used a ram to get into the unit.

Gareth Weetman, prosecuting, said: "They found a total of 46 dummy washing machines and 18 of them were still secured on pallets."

The cigarettes were two brands which are illegal in the UK, 2,662,240 Minsk cigarettes and 8,460 carrying the Pect name.

The trial continues.
