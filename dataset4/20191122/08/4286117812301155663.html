Uddhav Thackeray to Shiv Sena MLAs: Maharashtra alliance to be finalised by evening

Shiv Sena chief Uddhav Thackeray told party lawmakers that a coalition with the Congress and the NCP would be finalised by Friday evening. He didn't say if he would be chief minister.

Shiv Sena chief Uddhav Thackeray on Friday accused the BJP of lying, as he blamed his old ally for a recent breakdown in ties and told his MLAs a new alliance would be finalised by evening.

The Shiv Sena has working to cobble together an alliance with the Nationalist Congress Party and the Congress since it fell out with the BJP.

At a meeting with Shiv Sena MLAs, Uddhav Thackeray didn't reveal if he would lead the prospective coalition as chief minister, although there's been a chorus of demands for him to take on the role.

He said he would take a decision at the right time.

Meanwhile the Samajwadi Party, which has 2 MLAs and is allied with the Congress-NCP combine, has asked for reservations for Muslims.

It also wants the Shiv Sena to abandon Hindutva.

Maharashtra has been under President's Rule since November 12, following a nearly three-week-long political deadlock caused by rift in ties between the BJP and the Shiv Sena.

Raj Bhavan sources say Governor Bhagat Singh Koshyari is likely to skip a conference of governors that begins Saturday in Delhi, and remain in Mumbai.
