CARERS' NEGLECT

Vulnerable Glasgow man with capacity of six-year-old left in bath naked for 21 hours by cruel carers

22 Nov 2019, 19:50 by Connor Gordon

TWO cruel carers left a severely disabled man trapped in a bath naked for up to 21 hours.

Scott Sloan, 46, and Paul Campbell, 54, failed to help Andrew McKinnell who slipped while showering in his Glasgow home.

Glasgow Sheriff Court heard Sloan called supervisor Campbell for advice after Andrew, 55, fell at 5.30pm in his home in the city’s Carntyne.

But he was told to leave the vulnerable man — who has the mental capacity of a six-year-old — where he was.

Prosecutor Graham MacDonald said: “Mr McKinnell wanted to get out and didn’t receive assistance.”

Sloan then went to sleep at midnight, leaving Andrew stuck overnight.

He was only rescued when Campbell, of Glasgow’s Bridgeton, turned up at 10am the next day and alerted a fellow supervisor.

The victim was taken to hospital at 2.30pm where he was found to have suffered bruising on his back.

Sloan, of Dennistoun, Glasgow, and Campbell, admitted wilful neglect in November last year.

Sloan was ordered to carry out 200 hours of unpaid work while Campbell received 240 hours.

The pair had been working for non-profit firm Inclusion Glasgow.
