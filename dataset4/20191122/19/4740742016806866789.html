Opening date announced for new Lidl store in Patchway

There'll be bargains galore after the ribbon-cutting

22 Nov 2019, 19:45 by Tristan Cork

It will be the latest supermarket to open in Patchway, and the growing Charlton Hayes suburb - and now the opening date for the state-of-the-art new Lidl has been revealed.

The new branch of the German supermarket will open its doors - with a ribbon-cutting ceremony and everything - at 8am on Thursday, December 5.

And Lidl bosses are offering some of what they are describing as ‘exclusive deals’ for the first three days that the store is open.

These include a travel cot which will have £20 off - just on the Friday - and a toaster and kettle set, which is almost half price on the Saturday.

Where is the new Lidl going to be?

You’ll have seen it being built on Hayes Way in Northfield East, and it forms Lidl’s continued expansion plans right across Bristol and the rest of the West Country.

The store will be a big one - with some 1,325m² of sales area, an instore bakery, toilets, baby-changing and 158 car parking spaces.

It’ll be green too - there are 600 solar panels on the roof, and two electric car rapid charging points in the car park.

What do Lidl say?

Lidl’s regional boss Glen Stidever said the new store has created 40 new jobs, and is designed to mirror a nearby hangar from the old Filton Airfield.

“We would like to thank all those who have played a part in bringing this new Lidl store to Patchway,” he said.

“We have received excellent support from many local residents who currently have to travel out of Patchway, or Charlton Hayes, to complete their weekly food shop.

“We have also worked closely with South Gloucestershire Council to design a store befitting to its surroundings, including taking design inspiration from the neighbouring Belfast Hangar, and constructing artwork designed by Bruce Williams depicting a Concorde in flight at the pedestrian entrance to the site.

“It is great for us to be able to create more jobs and investment opportunities in the area, and we can’t wait to start serving our multi-award winning products to the local community,” he added.

Where will more Lidl stores be opening?

Last month, Bristol Live revealed that Lidl had ambitious plans to expand in Bristol, and were looking for sites for new stores - particularly in more affluent parts of Bristol.
