MUMMY'S BOY

Love Island’s Anton Danyluk can’t wait to team up with his mum at Scottish Sun Sunta store’s opening

22 Nov 2019, 19:31 by Colan Lamont

LOVE Island hunk Anton Danyluk is glad mum Sherie Ann will help him spread some festive cheer at the Scottish Sun Sunta store — saying: “She’s all I want for Christmas.”

The 25-year-old admits his life has been turned upside down since becoming a viewers’ favourite on the hit ITV show.

But it’s meant that the jet-setter hardly gets to spend time at home.
So he can’t wait to team up with his cult hero mum, 55, from Blackride, West Lothian, at our shop — even though she always brings him back to reality with a crash, bang and wallop.

The buff gym owner said: “You know it will be big coming out of Love Island but have no idea the true extent of it.

“It’s life-changing and I’m so glad people liked me because I could have been myself on the show and they disliked me instead.

“I’m happy to be home a bit more now because my mum had to get used to not being able to communicate much with me for a while.

“We are both trying to adapt to this new life. It seems like forever but it’s easy to forget it’s only been four months since I left the villa.

“When I came home I had been that used to staying in hotels that I hadn’t made my bed right away and she said ‘Don’t think you’re a celebrity now.’

"She treats me exactly the same and that’s how it should be.

"People liked me because I was humble and my mum would never let fame get a hold of me.”

Since leaving the reality show, Aidrie gym owner Anton has been on a massive PA tour as well as visiting schools to talk about fitness as part of his role as World Merit Ambassador with the United Nations.

He admits it’s been crazy to see the reaction from fans when they meet him in the flesh.
Anton said: “People ask you to hold their babies. There’s all these different scenarios every day now that are so surreal. Some people cry and then I always try to make them laugh. I tell them

they’re doing what I did when I met Craig David on the show.

“It’s nice, touching moments because I’m the same guy that walked in the villa when nobody knew me.”

And Anton — who recently split with his Love Island beau Belle Hassan, 21 — reveals his busy schedule means he has no time for romance. But that doesn’t stop him being flooded by offers from

women trying to pull him.

He said: “I’ve always been work-orientated and I was so busy when I came out the villa that it was difficult to see anyone.

“Relationships are hard to work out so for now I’m focused on the business side of things.

“If it happens then great, you can’t put a time on it. But I’m single and happy and life is good, I can’t complain.

“It didn’t show on Love Island but before that I always did OK with girls. And now it’s on a different level with the messages you get.

“Then it becomes about who wants to be with you for the right reasons.”

As he ponders what to do next, Anton hopes to back on telly. He’d even be up for doing I’m A Celebrity if the call came next year.

Anton said: “A lot of people said I made Love Island this year because I was so funny. I didn’t expect that.

“I would like to get back on TV so things like Strictly, Dancing On Ice, I’m A Celeb, I’d be up for anything. My main thing is always about helping as many young people as I can so if that means

kids’ TV then I’d do that as well.

 

People liked me because I was humble and my mum would never let fame get a hold of me.

“Presenting would be fun as well. Once you’re on TV it’s hard to give it up. I loved it and want to do it again as soon as I can.”

Before that, Anton also has a new fitness venture being released in the New Year.

He added: “Unfortunately I can’t say too much. But fitness has been my real passion for years and is what I want to do.

“Now I’m looking forward to using my platform to make this new business as big as possible.”

Now the countdown is on until Anton and Sherie Ann open our pop-up charity store on Wednesday.

Our Sunta store is going to be more amazing than ever after Amazon dropped off an incredible £5,000 worth of goodies.

All proceeds will go towards raising vital funds for our chosen charities MND Scotland and Alzheimer’s Scotland.

And Anton can’t think of anything better. He said: “I’ve been fortunate that none of my immediate family has either of these two illnesses.

“But I know people who have and it must be dreadful. With so much going on in our lives, sometimes we forget how lucky we are just to be healthy.

“There are no cures for MND or Alzheimer’s, so we need to keep raising cash until we find them.”
