Climate protesters target COAG energy ministers at Duxton Hotel

22 Nov 2019, 01:22

A small group of climate change protesters have gathered outside Perth’s Duxton Hotel where a meeting of State energy ministers is being held.

About 30 to 40 protesters holding flags and banners and chanting slogans are currently outside the hotel’s main entrance.

There is little police presence, although several plain clothes officers are keeping an eye on the small crowd from a distance.

It’s understood all State and Federal energy ministers are attending Friday’s Council of Australian Governments meeting.

Groups including 350.org, the Australian Conservation Foundation and Extinction Rebellion WA are among the protest group.

The banners they are holding include: “Save our children, government must act now” “listen to the science” and “climate emergency.”

Some of the protesters - many of whom are middle aged - lay on the pavement.
