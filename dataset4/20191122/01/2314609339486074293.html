Senior activists tape mouths shut protesting Brisbane library ban

22 Nov 2019, 01:46 by Lucy Stone

Senior climate activists have staged a silent demonstration outside Brisbane's City Hall, taping their mouths shut in protest of lord mayor Adrian Schrinner's decision to ban them from booking rooms at public libraries.

The Extinction Rebellion subgroup Grey Power stood outside City Hall on Friday morning with their mouths taped shut and holding signs calling for action on climate change, following revelations the Queensland Human Rights Commission is investigating the council's ban.

Cr Schrinner announced in October that public libraries would no longer accept bookings from Extinction Rebellion and other activist groups for meeting rooms, insisting the group were plotting illegal activities.

Activist Miree Le Roy made a complaint to the Human Rights Commission last month, which the commission accepted for investigation earlier this week.

The council and Ms Le Roy will now have to participate in compulsory conciliation to address the issue in late December.

As about 30 activists stood outside City Hall, some passers-by joined in or stopped to ask what was happening.
