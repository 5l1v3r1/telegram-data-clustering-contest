GOP senators gearing up for impeachment trial, may call on Hunter Biden

22 Nov 2019, 01:39 by Carl Campanile and Aaron Feis

Senate Republicans plan to confront the growing likelihood of an impeachment trial of President Trump head-on, fighting allegations of a quid pro quo with Ukraine by calling a slew of witnesses that could potentially include Hunter Biden.

A half-dozen GOP senators huddled with White House counsel Pat Cipollone and Trump’s son-in-law Jared Kushner as the House impeachment hearing wound down Thursday, in an attempt to prepare for the next step, according to Politico.

Republicans hold enough seats in the Senate to, theoretically, quash an impeachment trial in its infancy — but sources told the outlet that there was a consensus in the meeting to let the process play out, both to stand up to the charges and affirm support for due process.

“Different people have different opinions of what to do, but the main message [is] that a motion to dismiss won’t fly,” one attendee told Politico.

A protracted impeachment trial could also allow the White House counsel to call and grill witnesses of their choosing.

Number one on the list could be the younger Biden, who Republicans wanted to question during the House Intelligence Committee hearings, only to be stymied by Democratic control of the witness roster.

“The Senate has great latitude,” said noted attorney Alan Dershowitz, an expert in constitutional law.

“There’s nothing about the impeachment proceedings [in the Constitution] other than you need two-thirds vote in the Senate, and the chief justice [of the Supreme Court] presides. Can they ask Hunter Biden to testify? Yes.”

Republicans could question Biden about his time on the board of the Ukrainian energy company Burisma, a post the GOP says is suspicious considering his father being vice president.

Trump requested in a July 25 phone call that Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelensky probe the Bidens at a time when nearly $400 million in US aid money to the nation hung on the line, an ask that Democrats have alleged to be a quid pro quo.

“They can bring in Hunter Biden and whoever they want to,” said Al D’Amato, who served nearly 20 years as a Republican senator from New York and chaired the Whitewater investigation into President Bill Clinton in the mid-90s.

“[But] I think it would be a political mistake,” D’Amato cautioned. “It would be seen as an obvious political ploy, just like the House proceedings.”

Earlier this month Sens. Rand Paul and John Kennedy, of Kentucky and Louisiana, respectively, discussed the idea, The Washington Post said.

And South Carolina Sen. Lindsey Graham on Thursday asked Secretary of State Mike Pompeo to turn over a trove of documents, including some related to the Bidens and Burisma.

Both Kennedy and Graham were among the Republicans in Thursday’s meeting, according to Politico.
