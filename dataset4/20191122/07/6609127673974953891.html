STROKE OF GENIUS

Ex-Aston Villa boss Bruce says a round of golf helped him steal McGinn from Celtic

22 Nov 2019, 07:33 by John Shields

STEVE BRUCE revealed how a round of GOLF helped him steal John McGinn from Celtic.

The ex-Aston Villa boss has hailed the Scot, as his best signing after snatching him from Hibs for £3million 15 months ago.

And Bruce admitted he had to pull out all the stops to gazump Celtic — including treating McGinn’s golf-mad dad to a round at The Belfry.

The Newcastle boss, whose side tackle Villa on Monday night, said: “Given their financial plight at the time, Villa didn’t have £3m.

“How we got him out of a move to Celtic I don’t really know because his grandfather Jack was Celtic’s former chairman.

“We stuck at it because we knew how good John was and it took a couple of rounds of golf at The Belfry with his dad until we could muster the money together. I didn’t mind paying for those.”

Scotland ace McGinn has now been linked with a £50m move to Manchester United and Bruce added: “John’s at the level of a big club like Manchester United. I’m delighted for him because he is a smashing lad.

“Given what he’s done in the past 12 months I can only see him continuing to improve.

“He has to be one of my best ever signings because he has a determination to do well. With him and Jack Grealish in the middle, they’ll be a handful because they are two top players.”
