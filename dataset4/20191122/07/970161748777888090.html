Floyd Mayweather reveals he is coming out of retirement in 2020

22 Nov 2019, 07:52

Floyd Mayweather has revealed that he is coming out of retirement in 2020 and will be working alongside UFC president Dana White.

The 42-year-old, whose professional boxing record stands at 50-0, last fought in August 2017 when he beat Conor McGregor in Las Vegas.

Since then, Mayweather has participated in an exhibition fight last December against Japanese kickboxer Tenshin Nasukawa.

Mayweather earned $9 million (£7m) for that contest and beat Nasukawa, who was undefeated at that time, in less than three minutes.

Mayweather has now revealed that he will be ending his retirement next year to work with White, which could suggest another exhibition bout could be on the cards.

Speaking earlier this week, Mayweather was adamant that he would not be returning to boxing.

‘I’ve got calls to get back into the ring, but my health is my wealth,’ he told Reuters.

‘Boxing is a very, very brutal sport. In the last few years a lot of fighters have died inside that squared circle.

‘You have got to know when to hang it up. I had a great career.

‘I’ll still travel and do exhibitions. I make great money doing exhibitions; between $10 and $30 million.

‘I think I make more doing that than most fighters make fighting.’
