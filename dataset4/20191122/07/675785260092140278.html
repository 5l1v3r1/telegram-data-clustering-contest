Mason Mount recounts Jose Mourinho advice that will always stay with him

The Chelsea youngster has made a big impression on the pitch this season, but he used to be a regular on the sidelines at Stamford Bridge in Mourinho's days

22 Nov 2019, 07:39 by Mark Jones, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/mark-jones/

Tottenham's ball boys could be in for a shock following the appointment of Jose Mourinho as manager, according to Chelsea's Mason Mount.

Mourinho signed a three-and-a-half year deal to become the new Spurs boss on Wednesday morning, replacing Mauricio Pochettino in the hotseat.

The Portuguese will begin what is his fourth different spell in English football with a trip to West Ham on Saturday lunchtime, and Mount was around for a close look at the third.

The 20-year-old used to act as a ball boy at Stamford Bridge for Blues home games when he was in the club's academy, and he told the Chelsea website about a piece of advice Mourinho - who was then in his second spell at the club - used to give him.

"The first thing I really remember was being a ball boy at the games," he told the Blues website .

"That was a big thing for me. I was so buzzing about that and you can’t really be any closer than that!

"One moment that stands out was under Jose Mourinho.

"If we were winning in Champions League games then he would tell us to keep hold of the ball and not to throw it back so quickly!"

Mourinho will return to the Champions League with Tottenham when the North Londoners host Olympiakos on Tuesday, but before that he'll make his bow at West Ham on Saturday lunchtime.

“I had a feeling I was going to get a club mid-season," he said at his press conference unveiling on Thursday.

"I knew I would be in a situation where I would get only one or two days before my first game. I cannot come here and think it is about myself. It is about the players and going from a base of stability.”
