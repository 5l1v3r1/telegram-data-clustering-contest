HARD TO BUDGE

Ann Budge faces battle to land Daniel Stendel as Hearts boss..because he’d rather stay in England

22 Nov 2019, 07:32 by Robert Martin

ANN BUDGE faces a battle to land Daniel Stendel as Hearts boss — because the German would rather stay in England.

Former Barnsley manager Stendel is the Jambos’ No 1 choice to replace Craig Levein, as SunSport revealed yesterday.

But Tynecastle chief Budge will have to pull out all the stops to persuade the 45-year-old that his future lies in Edinburgh.

Sources close to bookies’ favourite Stendel have indicated he’d rather stay down south than head north.

Stendel led the Tykes to promotion from League One back in May following his first full season in charge of the Yorkshire side.

But he was controversially sacked last month with the club sitting 23rd in the English Championship.

And despite the lowly league position it proved a hugely unpopular decision with the club’s fan base.

Stendel is now determined to restore his reputation at another club in England and is prepared to wait for the right offer to come along.

Unless the Jambos can persuade him to change his mind he is set to remain on gardening leave from Barnsley until the end of his contract in June.

That would be a major blow to the Gorgie club as they continue to search for Levein’s successor.

They have spoken to a vast number of candidates about the vacant post, including Stuart McCall, Jack Ross, Steve Cotterrill and Neil McCann.

In the meantime interim manager Austin MacPhee — who has not given up hope of landing the job himself — will continue to take the team at Kilmarnock tomorrow.

It will be MacPhee’s second game in charge following the 5-2 win over St Mirren ahead of the international break.

And another win would enhance his own chances of persuading Budge to hand him the reins on a permanent basis.

MacPhee was able to welcome back defender Craig Halkett to training on Wednesday ahead of the trip to Rugby Park.

But SunSport understands the trip to Ayrshire will come too soon for the defender as he makes his way back from a serious knee injury.

The 24-year-old is instead expected to make his comeback when the men in maroon visit Rangers a week on Sunday.

And in a further boost for MacPhee midfielder Peter Haring is also hopeful of returning to action before the end of the year.

The Austrian has missed the whole of this season with a pelvic problem dating back to before last season’s Scottish Cup final with Celtic.

But he is now nearing a top-team return ahead of a hectic December for the Tynecastle side.
