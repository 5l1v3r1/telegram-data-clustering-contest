Parliament proceedings: Private Members’ Bills introduced in Rajya Sabha

22 Nov 2019, 05:47 by The Hindu Net Desk

Environment, Forest and Climate Change Minister Prakash Javadekar is responding to questions raised in the Lok Sabha regarding environmental pollution.

The Rajya Sabha discussed the Surrogacy (Regulation) Bill, 2019, the  Transgender Persons (Protection of Rights) Bill, 2019 and the Dam Safety Bill, 2019, yesterday.

The Lok Sabha discussed the National Institute of Design (Amendment) Bill, 2019.

Here are the live updates:

Lok Sabha | 3.40 p.m.

Private Members’ Bills are being taken up in the House now.

Bills introduced include Special Financial Assistance to the State of West Bengal Bill by Adhir Ranjan Chowdhury, Tea Garden Workers (Timely Payment of Dues) Bill by Saugata Roy, National Food Security (Amendment) Bill by Kirit Solanki, Representation of the People (Amendment) Bill by Feroze Varun Gandhi, Central Universities (Amendment)Bill by Riti Pathak.

Lok Sabha | 3.30 p.m.

A Bill to ban production, import and sale of electronic cigarettes and similar products was introduced in the Lok Sabha on Friday.

The Prohibition of Electronic Cigarettes (Production, Manufacture, Import, Export, Transport, Sale, Distribution, Storage and Advertisement) Bill, 2019, seeks to replace an ordinance issued on September 18.

The Bill was introduced by Union Health Minister Harsh Vardhan.

The Bill makes the manufacturing, production, import, export, distribution, transport, sale, storage or advertisements of such alternative smoking devices a cognizable offence, attracting a jail term and a fine.

P.L. Punia (INC) speaks about the lack of clean toilets.

Rajya Sabha | 2.30 p.m.

The House takes up introduction of Private Members’ Bills.

Among Bills introduced are The Right to Universal and free Healthcare Bill by Abhishek Manu Singhvi, The Rights of Dwellers as Owners of the Land Bill by B.K. Hariprasad, The Tribal (Adivasi) Regiment Bill, Reservation for SC/ST in Private Sector Bill by Kirodi Lal Meena, The Constitution (Amendment) Bill, 2019 (amendment of article 343) by Vaiko, The Constitution (Amendment) Bill, 2017 (amendment of article 51A) by Prabhat Jha.

A discussion on Mr. Jha’s Bill begins. Mr. Jha has introduced a Bill on cleanliness.

Lok Sabha | 1:30 p.m.

Rajiv Pratap Rudy, BJP, asks if the government will hold America accountable for the pollution that it creates.

Bhartruhari Mahtab, BJD, asks Mr. Javadekar if Delhi's climate can be controlled within its limits, without having to control the climates of surrounding regions.

Dr. Nishikant Dubey, BJP, says 90% market of lithium batteries lies with China and that those batteries create immense pollution. He also asks the government what long-term policies are in place regarding PNG.

N.K. Premachandran, RSP, asks if single-use plastic is banned, then why policies pertaining to reusing and recycling of plastic continue to exist.

Adhir Ranjan Chowdhury, INC, seeks clarification on the status of hydrogen fuel usage. He also asks what financial aid has been provided to stop stubble burning.

Mr. Javadekar rises to answer the questions.

Lok Sabha | 1:00 p.m.

Adhir Ranjan Chowdhury, INC, speaks on the need for climate justice. "Why shoul common people be the victims of the lackadaisical and cavalier approach of the government?" he asks. "We need a climate smart economy". It is an extremely profitable type of economy, he says, citing an environmentalist.

He commends the efforts of Sweden's Greta Thunberg.

Cimate change was not even mentioned once in the budget speeches of the NDA over the last couple of years, Mr. Chowdhury alleges.

Mr. Javadekar rises to speak. He commends the fact that the discussion on air pollution and climate change has been going on in the House for the last three days.

He expands on the difference between climate change and air pollution.

He talks about the four undertakings in the Paris Climate accord. One of them is to include 40% of renewable energy in the country's energy mix so as to cut down on coal. This will be achieved by or even before 2030, he says.

Reducing emission intensity is another goal, he says. India is one of the only two countries in the world where green cover is increasing, he claims.

He elaborates on Delhi's air quality.

Across industries, energy norms have been changed and enhanced towards lesser pollution, he says.  He says the western and eastern peripheral expressways have been created which reduce pollution in Delhi.

Seven trees are enough for a lifetime's oxygen supply to an individual, he says.

Rajya Sabha | 1:00 p.m.

Question Hour ends. Rajya Sabha is adjourned till 2:30 p.m.

Lok Sabha | 12:30 p.m.

Discussion under rule 193 on air pollution and climate change is underway.

Pratima Mondal, AITC, requests eminent social media personalities to take an initiative to start "challenges" pertaining to purification of air.  She says a stubble-collecting programme should be started.

Rajendra Agrawal is in the Chair.

Balli Durgaprasad Rao, YSRCP, questions the efficiency of the CPCB. He requests the government to take up afforestation with the help of State Governments.

Nihal Chand Chauhan, BJP, speaks on implementation of the NGT order to Punjab to contain its chemically contaminated water. He also mentions that stubble burning is not the primary reason contributing to air pollution.

Jagdambika Pal, BJP, cites a WHO report claiming 22 of the most polluted States are in India, topped by Gurugram. "It not only affects our health but also the GDP," he says.

Adhir Ranjan Chowdhury, INC, says there is not enough scientific effort to deal with the problem of stubble burning. He suggests taking up cycling as an alternative to driving cars as done in Copenhagen.

In India, the highest amount of greenhouse gas emissions come from the energy sector, followed by the agricultural sector, he says.

Pollution is not limited to Delhi, he says, adding that the furore has been created because it is the capital but many other States are suffering silently too.

Rajya Sabha | 12:15 p.m.

Burning of stubble residue in the open is being discussed.

Vijay Goel, BJP, says stubble burning has been happening for a long time. He asks why it has become an issue now when even the Supreme Court has said that local factors in Delhi-NCR are greater contributors to pollution.

Majeed Memon, NCP, asks if the government will provide additional aid to Maharashtra farmers hit by heavy rains and if any special delegation will be sent regarding the matter.

Parshottam Rupala, MoS, Agriculture and Farmers Welfare, responds, saying an interim relief of Rs 600 crore has been allotted so that the State government can help those farmers.

Speaker Om Birla is in the Chair. Question Hour begins.

A question on recycling of plastic is being discussed.

Maneka Sanjay Gandhi, BJP, suggests monetisation of plastic and using a method that is used by Nordic countries to turn it into oil.

Prakash Javadekar, Environment, Forest and Climate Change Minister, says there is a team in place constituted by CPCB and National Institute of Science and Technology who evaluate proposals regarding these matters.

Nama Nageswara Rao, TRS, discusses how a Telangana village Collector had started an exchange of one kg rice for one kg plastic submitted by the villagers.
