Damian McCrory vows to help Notts County overcome 'major blip'

Magpies are looking for a response to heavy Barrow defeat

22 Nov 2019, 15:14 by Leigh Curtis

Damien McCrory is determined to help Notts County respond to a “major blip” as he targeted a much-improved performance against Aldershot tomorrow.

The Magpies suffered their first home defeat since August last weekend when they were beaten 3-0 by Barrow in a performance that boss Neal Ardley admitted was below par.

But Notts have the chance to return to winning ways against struggling Aldershot as they look to cut the gap to leaders Barrow at the summit.

McCrory says the players were shown the video of last weekend’s defeat and they are more than aware of the problems they needed to address.

“The gaffer showed us the video and it was clear that it just was not good enough,” revealed McCrory.

“We were off it in both ways – with the ball and without it. It was a major blip and, as players, we’ve got to make sure it doesn’t happen again.

“We know what we did wrong and what was missing from last Saturday’s performance, so we need to make sure that it is there this weekend.

“A lot of lads were hurt so I think it will be a good learning process to make sure that it doesn’t happen again this season.

“Other teams won’t let us play nice football all the time – some will get in our faces like Barrow and we’ve got to do the horrible stuff as well.

“We have got to learn from last Saturday."

Notts’ task tomorrow has been made increasingly difficult as Aldershot will be fired up after suffering a 6-1 hammering at the hands of Dagenham last week.

Their manager Damon Searle pulled no punches in his criticism of their performance having blasted it as a disgrace and is eager for his players not to produce a repeat showing.

Having been on the end of big defeats before in his career, McCrory says the mindset of the Shots will be to ensure they are tough to beat.

But he says the main priority for Notts is to concentrate on their own game.

“I think they will be working on not conceding,” said the 29-year-old.

“I‘ve been beaten 5-0 in my career and you go back to basics to make sure you are solid and hard to break down.

“I can see that happening on Saturday, which is going to be another test for us.

“I think we have got to play our own game and be solid ourselves while trying to score goals.”
