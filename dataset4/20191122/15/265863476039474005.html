Days Of Our Lives To Be Renewed Much To The Allay Of Fans’ Fears

22 Nov 2019, 15:30 by Charisse Van Horn

Days of our Lives (DOOL), the long-running soap opera is set to be renewed for Season 56, according to a new report from the Daily Mail. Fans were in a state of pandemonium after it was announced the show released the entire cast from their contracts as filming ceased upon the end of Season 55. Days of our Lives first aired on November 8, 1965, and is the longest-running soap opera in television history. The show even has two veteran actors who have been with the program since its inception, met on set, fell in love and were married: Bill Hayes and Susan Seaforth Hayes.

The show has also been home to John Aniston (Jennifer Aniston’s father) who plays the leading villain Victor Kiriakis.

According to the Daily Mail’s reporting, DOOL executive producer Ken Corday informed the show’s production workers on Thursday that the show would be renewed.

Fans were in the early stages of launching a social media campaign to save Days of our Lives if it were to be canceled.

NBC has yet to release an official announcement and the Daily Mail quotes TMZ who spoke to an anonymous source who is close to the situation and says they are still working out the technicalities for the new deal.
