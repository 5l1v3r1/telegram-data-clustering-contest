Stroke patients in South Bristol able to rehabilitate at home thanks to electric bikes

More than 300 patients have benefited from the scheme since April 2017

22 Nov 2019, 15:16 by Ella Buxton

Three hundred stroke patients in South Bristol have been able to have rehabilitation therapy at home since electric bikes were introduced for staff.

Therapists at South Bristol Community Hospital (SBCH) in Hengrove are using the bikes to carry out home visits for stroke patients who have recently been discharged from hospital.

The scheme, which started in 2017, means patients don’t need to stay in hospital for longer than necessary.

The Early Supported Discharge Team for Stroke (EDS) is run by University Hospitals Bristol NHS Foundation Trust and supports patients across the city, with team members visiting up to four patients a day.

Patient Darren Batchelor, 46, suffered two strokes in July and praises the ESD team after they provided support for him in his home in Hartcliffe.

After spending a number of weeks in hospital, including around a month at SBCH, Darren was able to go home once he built up a level of strength.

Once discharged, the ESD team visited him up to twice a day for four weeks and assisted him with speech therapy, consistently checked his blood pressure, provided therapy on his arms and legs and helped him with his exercises.

He said: “They also listened to how I was feeling which was brilliant and really supportive. They were very helpful and it was very good to be able to have this in my own home.

“They helped to set me up with a very positive outlook on my recovery. I don’t know where I would be without their support.

“I have a lot of appreciation for them.”

Since the scheme began in 2017, the team have also worked to tackle climate change.

The scheme was initially a trial, but won an NUS Green Impact Award in 2017 and now, the bikes have travelled nearly 3,000 miles in two years.

James Barber, therapy technician, is a keen cyclist and said: “Since the introduction of the e bikes all members of the ESD team have benefited from shorter journey times and reduced stress due to avoiding traffic.

“It’s great to be out in the fresh air and it’s a really great sustainable way of seeing our patients. The electric bikes mean we’re putting fewer cars on road journeys around Bristol, which reduces traffic and is better for the environment.  It has also saved the Trust significant travel and parking expenses.”
