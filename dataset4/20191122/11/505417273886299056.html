Ireland's Euro 2020 Playoff Path Confirmed

22 Nov 2019, 11:17 by Robert Redmond

The Republic of Ireland’s Euro 2020 playoff path was confirmed at a draw on Friday morning. If they are to reach the tournament, they will have to come through two away games.

Ireland failed to qualify directly for Euro 2020, finishing third in qualification Group D behind Switzerland and Denmark, a result that was confirmed on Monday night following a 1-1 draw with the Danes in Dublin.

Mick McCarthy’s side must now navigate a play-off next spring. They will play Slovakia away on Thursday, March 26 in a one-leg semi-final. The winner of that tie will play either the winner of the other semi-final in Path B, Bosnia and Herzegovina or Northern Ireland, in a final on Tuesday, March 31.

That was all known ahead of Friday’s draw in Nyon, Switzerland, but it has now been confirmed that Ireland – should they reach the playoff final – will have NOT home advantage.

So, if Ireland beat Slovakia away, they will play away to either Bosnia or Northern Ireland in a final on March 31. The winner will advance to Euro 2020. So, it could be the Republic of Ireland versus Northern Ireland in Windsor Park for a place at the tournament.

Ireland’s task looks difficult, and you can currently get odds of 4/1 for them reaching the Euros.

Ireland is one of 12 co-hosts for the tournament next summer as Euro 2020 will be staged in 12 cities throughout Europe – London, Dublin, Glasgow, Rome, Bilbao, Amsterdam, Budapest, Baku, Munich, Copenhagen, Bucharest, and St Petersburg. Dublin will host four games – three group games and a last-16 tie.

Should Ireland qualify, they will play two group matches in the city and a third in Bilbao against Spain. McCarthy’s team are guaranteed to be in a group with Spain. They will also be drawn alongside one of France, Poland, Switzerland or Croatia, who are all in Pot 2 for the draw, and one of Portugal, Turkey, Austria, Sweden or the Czech Republic, who are in Pot 3.

As Ireland and Spain are both host nations, they will not be drawn alongside another host nation. However, Ireland must first qualify for the tournament before they can worry about the makeup of their potential group.

The next step for McCarthy’s team is a one-leg semi-final away to Slovakia on March 26. If they come through that, Ireland will play a final away from home on March 31 against Bosnia or Northern Ireland.

The full Euro 2020 playoff draw is:

Path A: Iceland v Romania. Bulgaria v Hungary. Bulgaria or Hungary will host the final.

Path B: Bosnia and Herzegovina v Northern Ireland. Slovakia v Republic of Ireland. Northern Ireland or Bosnia will host the final.

Path C: Scotland v Israel. Norway v Serbia. Norway or Serbia will host the final.

Path D: Georgia v Belarus. North Macedonia v Kosovo. Georgia or Belarus will host the final.
