Rachel Riley insists backlash is 'price of awareness' as she stands by t-shirt

22 Nov 2019, 11:57

Rachel Riley is standing by her photo-shopped Jeremy Corbyn t-shirt, saying that online hate is ‘the price of awareness’.

The Countdown host sparked widespread fury when she wore a t-shirt showing a picture of Labour leader Corbyn being arrested at an anti-apartheid protest in 1984, with ‘Jeremy Corbyn is a racist endeavour’ edited onto his sign.

The original image showed Corbyn holding a placard reading: ‘Defend the right to demonstrate against apartheid; join this picket.’

After major backlash, Rachel retweeted one of her own posts from October, which said: ‘As we edge towards a December general election, a heartfelt promise: I will do everything in my power to try to ensure my baby, Jewish by blood, will not be born under an antisemitic government.

‘I hope this will never happen, please use your vote wisely.’

She’s now added: ‘Thanks for all the lovely messages of support, & all the silent supporters who’ve followed me, means a lot.

‘Shows how counterproductive nasty pile-ons actually are. I made this promise. I intend to keep it. If this is the price to raise awareness then sobeit. Lots of love.’

Alongside the original upload, she wrote: ‘I didn’t feel comfortable knowing my workplace was to be full of racists tonight.

‘I don’t endorse Boris, but I do endorse #NeverCorbyn. Please see my pinned thread if you still don’t understand why. #LeadersDebate #LabourAntisemitism.’

In the recent debate, Corbyn insisted that he has taken action against anti-Semitism in the Labour party, after he was accused of ‘creating a party machine with protects and normalises anti-Semetic ideas and individuals’.

However, the Countdown star has not been backing down.

Meanwhile, although there have been calls for the star to be sacked from Countdown, she doesn’t seem too fazed, as she was spotted out on a date night with her husband, Strictly Come Dancing’s Pasha Kovalev.
