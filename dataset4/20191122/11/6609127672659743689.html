'WHY WOULD I LIE?'

Youtuber who did Celtic and Rangers tours in rivals’ shirts slams ‘aggressive abuse’ from ‘predominantly’ Gers fans

22 Nov 2019, 11:40 by David Fowler

YOUTUBER Ninh Ly, who did Rangers and Celtic stadium tours in rival jerseys, has slammed the aggressive abuse from 'predominantly' Gers fans after his controversial video.

Last week he visited Ibrox wearing a Hoops shirt before touring Parkhead in a Gers top.

Ly then faced aggressive abuse, in particular from Light Blues supporters, for his controversial stadium tour video.

In a response video about the backlash, Ly said: "There is a lot of misinterpretation about this topic.

"At no point did I ever criticise either team's staff or either football club. I actually went on record that I recommended them both.

"Whilst my video was polarising to some, the vast majority saw the funny side of it and I got praise form both sides.

"But the negative comments I received have been predominantly from one side of the city.

"And whether that is typical or not I thought to myself 'Why are the Rangers fans so salty about all of this?'.

"I actually recommended the Ibrox tour, and I still do, what's the problem?

"I couldn't understand why they were so salty and aggressive given that most of them hadn't watched the video yet.

"But them my old friend told me Stan Collymore had retweeted my video. But I hadn't put my video on Twitter.

"Apparently there is a two minute clip of my video that has been cut specifically to make Rangers look really bad and Celtic look really good.
