Vinnie Jones says he 'owes' Simon Cowell and now wants to win X Factor in huge u-turn

Former footballer Vinnie pleaded media mogul Simon to let him leave the competition after landing in the bottom two with Kevin last week

22 Nov 2019, 11:23 by Irishmirror.ie

Vinnie Jones has admitted that he feels as though he "owes" Simon Cowell and now wants to win The X Factor : Celebrity.

This comes just days after he pleaded the media mogul to let him leave the competition after landing in the bottom two with Kevin McHale.

Speaking to Eamonn Holmes and Ruth Langsford on This Morning today, he said that being a part of the show had stopped him from falling into depression after his late wife Tanya's death.

Tanya died in July, aged 53, after being diagnosed with melanoma in 2013.

"He phoned me in Los Angeles and said he wanted me to come on the show," Vinnie said. "To be honest, I owe Aimon a lot."

He added: "This crazy six, seven or eight weeks has kept my mind off things and kept me focused on work rather falling into a depression and getting on with life.

Vinnie insisted that it was what Tanya would have wanted.

Revealing that he didn't see himself as part of the competition, he described himself someone running alongside the other competitors.

"This to me is great fun. I just felt, last week, Kevin is such a great singer," he said. "This could be a massive impact on his life and his career.

Confirming that he now wanted to win, he said that he had "obligations" now and that if he won a contract or money that all his proceeds would go to charity.
