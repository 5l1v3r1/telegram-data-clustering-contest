Project xCloud review and interview 'it's incredibly important to do this right'

22 Nov 2019, 11:00

GameCentral describes its hands-on time with the Project xCloud preview and talks to the Microsoft execs in charge of video game streaming.

This week saw the launch of Google Stadia and the ushering in of a new era of video game streaming as a viable option for mainstream gamers. It didn’t go that well. Interest before the launch was already muted and most of the attempts to review the service, including our own, complained about a lack of content and missing features. Stadia should become a lot more interesting once it gets more games and things like YouTube integration, but at the moment it’s hard to get excited about.

Everything about Stadia at launch feels rushed and there’s one obvious reason for why that would be: Project xCloud from Microsoft. The Xbox streaming service started its preview phase around the same time as Stadia and although it has no official start date, in terms of being open to everyone, it already seems a more exciting prospect than Stadia. Especially when it was announced last week at X019 that it would get access to all Xbox Game Pass games (which in turn got an additional 50 games added to the service).

The day after X019 we got to speak to Project xCloud bosses Kareem Choudhry and Catherine Gluckstein, who you would have seen talking about it at the livestreamed event. But before that we’d had our chance to try Project xCloud at home. Although, as with Stadia, that’s not as helpful as it sounds because your home, in terms of broadband and distance to the nearest Microsoft data centre will always be different.
