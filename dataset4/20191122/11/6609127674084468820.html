HELPING HAND

Glasgow residents launch ‘give or take’ food box to help locals in need ahead of Christmas

22 Nov 2019, 11:07 by Justin Bowie

KIND-hearted Glasgow residents have launched a “give or take” food box in a bid to help struggling locals ahead of Christmas.

The box, located in a garden on Westray Street in the city’s Milton area, has been lined with tinned food for “anyone facing an emergency”.

Residents are encouraged to leave bites to eat if they can to ensure the charity box remains topped up.

Advertising the food box, a local Facebook group said: “"Give or Take" foodbox in a local garden.

“It might get vandalised, it might not work but we thought we would give it a try especially at this time of the year.

“It’s topped up with tinned food for anyone facing an emergency that needs it.

“People can take food or leave food if it’s running low.”

They added: "We can't fear failure, we want to test it and we will accept whatever happens. Too many people are going hungry."

Social media users hailed the idea.

One Facebook user wrote: “Brilliant Idea.”

Another commented: “This is such a thoughtful thing to do.

“I really hope people don't take the Mick and use it properly, could be a great thing!”

A third person said: “Brilliant idea, I hope the needy use it & not the greedy.

“Well done for giving this a go. Fingers crossed that it's not vandalised & people use it”.

And a fourth Facebook user wrote: “Beautiful idea! Hope it spreads to other areas”.
