Airdrie can return to 35-year dominance over Albion Rovers on derby day says legend Mark Roberts

Airdrie ruled over their Monklands rivals for decades and Roberts hoped to see a return in the Scottish Cup this weekend.

22 Nov 2019, 11:40 by Mark Pirie, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/mark-pirie/

When Mark Roberts was wearing the diamond the side from ML6 ran Monklands Derby day – and the striker expects the current crop of Airdrie players to hit those heights again.

The striker was up for the cup back in 2001 as he scored in the 4-1 rout at Cliftonhill in the Challenge Cup.

Airdrie were flying high in the old First Division as Stuart Taylor, Owen Coyle and Kevin James joined Roberts on the scoresheet for Ian McCall’s team with Rovers in the Third Division doldrums.

It extended their unbeaten record against their local rivals to 25 years, a streak that would remain unbroken until the 7-2 defeat a decade later.

With the Diamonds topping the League One table and boasting full-time players the 44-year-old says there are plenty of similarities between now and the decades of dominance.

He says boss Ian Murray will be wary of conceding an early goal but should “just have too much quality on the day.”

He told the Airdrie and Coatbridge Advertiser: “Airdrie are in a bit of a no-win situation, but that can happen when you are the bigger team in a higher league.

“You have to be confident in your own ability and that you are better than the players at a lower level.

“I’ve been on both sides of it as a player when you get a shock in the cup, these things can happen.

“If you get knocked out by an underdog it can be absolutely terrible so you have to make sure you are doing your job.

“When I was playing for Airdrie there was a big gap between us and Albion Rovers.

“Airdrie are going well so that should be the case again this time around.

“You need to go in with the mindset that you are above their level, but there can be no messing about.

“If you lose an early goal it can change everything, but you have got to have the right approach.

“It will really depend on who scores first. Ian and the Airdrie coaches will be telling the players that; it’s about how they start the game.

“They will be aware that Albion Rovers will be looking for a wee upset. They will need to be fully prepared for that.”

Roberts says there will be weight of expectation on the New Broomfield outfit as they look to secure a potential money-spinning tie as Premiership sides enter the competition in the next round.

He continued: “A derby in the Scottish Cup is always something special. When it’s a league game you don’t get the same feeling as a player.

“It’s a great chance for both teams to progress and get their chance to play some of the big boys in the next round.

“The chairman and boards will be hoping to get through, as well as the players having a chance to showcase their talents.”

The Queen’s Park boss has already faced off against Kevin Harper’s men this term and says they will make it tough for Airdrie to break them down.

Roberts said: “They scored quite early when we drew with them, and made it really difficult for us to break them down.

“They were really defensive after that and made it difficult for us but we controlled the game in my opinion.

“We were confident we would score and that eventually came late on and could have won the game.

“We dictated the game but they made it tough.”
