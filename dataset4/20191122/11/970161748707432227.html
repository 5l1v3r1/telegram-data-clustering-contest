Vinnie Jones's late wife arranged anniversary party before her death

22 Nov 2019, 11:50

Vinnie Jones opened up about his late wife Tanya organising plans to celebrate the couple’s 25th wedding anniversary before her death.

The former footballer’s wife sadly passed away in July this year at the age of just 53.

The hardman is now taking part in The X Factor: Celebrity, managing to make it through to the semi-finals this weekend. He previously said that he feels Tanya is ‘with him’ when he performs on stage.

Appearing on This Morning, the X-Men star told hosts Eamonn Holmes and Ruth Langsford that his wife actually planned a very special surprise for her family to celebrate a momentous anniversary.

‘She planned [something for] New Year’s Eve,’ Vinnie revealed to the presenting duo. ‘I’m flying everybody over to Los Angeles, I have a house in Palm Springs.

‘I’m bringing everyone there to celebrate our 25th anniversary, she wanted to put it on. She made all the arrangements. We have to fulfill them.’

Vinnie was also embroiled in one of the most controversial X Factor moments in years last weekend after he attempted to quit the competition after finding himself in the sing-off.

In the bottom two against Glee’s Kevin McHale, the Lock, Stock and Two Smoking Barrels star pleaded with Simon Cowell to allow him to exit the competition for McHale to continue.

Unfortunately, the music mogul was having none of it and McHale was eliminated.

Despite the controversy, Vinnie has confirmed that he will be returning to the stage this weekend.

He also had the opportunity to perform a surprise duet with ska band Madness after performing a heart-felt tribute to Tanya with one of their songs in the competition.

Vinnie previously tugged at viewers’ heart-strings as he took to the stage two weeks ago with a rendition of hits It Must Be Love and House of Fun. During his performance, he tenderly pointed up to the sky and opened his arms out wide.

The lyrics of the song read: ‘I never thought I’d miss you, half as much as I do, and I never thought I’d feel this way, the way I feel, about you.’

This Morning airs weekdays from 10.30 on ITV.
