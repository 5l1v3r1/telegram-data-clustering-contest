Video shows flames shooting from the engine of a Boeing 777 when it caught fire just minutes after taking off from Los Angeles

22 Nov 2019, 11:05 by Sahar Esfandiari
  • Video shows the moment the engine of a Philippine Airlines Boeing 777 caught fire soon after takeoff from Los Angeles International Airport on Thursday, forcing the plane into an emergency landing.
  • In a statement, Philippine Airlines said the flaming engine was caused by a "technical issue" in one of the engines.
  • All 342 passengers and 18 crew members were unharmed and disembarked safely after the landing.
  • Boeing is under scrutiny following the deadly crashes involving its 737 Max aircraft, which killed a combined 346 people and led to questions over the plane's design.

A Philippine Airlines Boeing 777 plane had to make an emergency landing after it caught fire shortly after takeoff on Thursday.

Video footage of the incident posted to Twitter showed the plane's right engine spewing black smoke before appearing to catch fire and shooting out flames.
