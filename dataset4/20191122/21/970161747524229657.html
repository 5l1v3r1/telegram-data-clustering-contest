Medical examiner got in trouble for gory job ad with jokes about rotting corpses

22 Nov 2019, 21:29

A medical examiner is in trouble for a gory job posting that included jokes about rotting corpses.

Maine Chief Medical Examiner Dr Mark Flomenbaum’s 2017 posting for a new forensic pathologist apparently is a bit too lighthearted for lawmakers, who recently cited the posting in a complaint that calls into question Flomenbaum’s credibility.

The job advertisement starts out like any other posting, saying the job presented a ‘wonderful opportunity to do excellent forensic pathology that is supported by a low stress and high level quality-of-life environment.’

However, the posting takes an odd turn when Flomenbaum wrote that Maine is ‘a winter mecca for skiers, snowmobilers and other sportsmen,’ adding in bold lettering, ‘Translation: really short season of decomposed bodies.’

Flomenbaum seemed to be joking about the long winters and short summers in Maine, which would be preferential for corpse preservation because bodies decompose more rapidly in the heat.
