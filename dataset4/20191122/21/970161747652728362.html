Boris Johnson refuses to apologise for 'letterbox and bum boys' jibes

22 Nov 2019, 21:30

Boris Johnson refused to apologise for offensive language used in his journalism after being accused of contributing to ‘race hate’.

The prime minister defended describing Muslim women wearing the burqa as looking like letterboxes, saying he was making a ‘liberal case for women being allowed to wear what they want.’

Johnson was the last of four party leaders to take to the stage during a BBC Question time special tonight.

An audience member asked: ‘You have contributed to race hate. Will you accept that and apologise?’

He replied: ‘I have written many millions of words in my life as a journalist and I have… genuinely never intended to cause hurt or pain to anybody’.

The PM said he believed the question was a reference to an article in the Telegraph last year, when he was accused of Islamophobia for comparing Muslim women to letterboxes.

However, presenter Fiona Bruce stepped in to remind him of a list of controversial things he has said before and during his political career – including notorious comments about ‘tank-topped bum boys’.

She said: ‘To be fair, there’s a few articles. So there’s the Muslims going around looking like letterboxes, which was last year, you referred to tribal warriors with watermelon smiles and flag-waving pickaninnies and then just to get another demographic in, tank-topped bum boys.’

The PM was laughed at when he tried to skirt the question by saying you can always find things ‘which may look offensive’ when taken out of context.

He faced accusations  that he had ‘personally contributed’ to rampant racism in Britain but refused to apologise, saying: ‘I defend my right to speak out’.

He said: ‘If you go through all my articles with a fine-tooth comb and take out individual phrases there is no doubt that you can find things that can be made to seem offensive and of course I understand that.’

Jeremy Corbyn, Nicola Sturgeon and Jo Swinson all came together to answer 30 minutes of questions from the Question Time audience before the PM was scrutinised.

Boris Johnson has previously faced calls to apologise for his notorious comments and has consistently remained defiant on the matter.

However he said his party had ‘zero tolerance of Islamophobia’ and will have an inquiry into all forms of prejudice in the Conservative Party.

Other issues he was grilled on include alleged Russian interference in British democracy, the NHS, and the importance of the ‘truth’.

He said there was ‘no evidence’ of Russian interference and refused calls to publish a report on the matter.

On the NHS, he said his conservative government were building 20 hospitals, which in time would raise to 40.

These figures were called into question by Fiona Brunce, who said under the current spending plan the Tories could only afford to build six.

The PM was met with cheers and jeers as he exited the stage.
