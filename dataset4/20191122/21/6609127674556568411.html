Guinness World Records: Most valuable whisky collection revealed as tycoon’s £10m hoard kept locked in special room

22 Nov 2019, 21:51 by Douglas Barrie

A TYCOON has been revealed as the owner of the world’s most valuable whisky collection — worth more than £10million.

Viet Nguyen Dinh Tuan has spent 20 years building up his incredible hoard of 535 rare and old bottles which he locks away in a special lounge.

Guinness World Records has now ruled his dram treasure trove is the most expensive around the globe.

The Vietnamese businessman told how it’s his “life’s passion” adding: “I’m really happy and feel honoured.

“Every spare moment I get, I’m searching auction sites and trading websites to find famous and rare whiskies from around the world. I’m proud of my efforts.”

Experts at Rare Whisky 101 valued his collection at a ‘hammer price’ of £10,770,635 — rising to £13,032,468 if it was to be sold through a UK auctioneer.

He is believed to be one of only three collectors to own all three Macallan 1926 bottles — including the Fine and Rare label which recently sold for £1.2million.

His haul also boasts one of only 12 bottles of the oldest Bowmore — worth £300,000.

But Mr Viet, of Ho Chi Minh City, added: “I have no intention of selling any.

“I’ll continue to hunt for more old and rare bottles and add to and enrich it.”

Andy Simpson, co-founder of Rare Whisky 101, said: “We were gobsmacked when we realised the breadth and depth of Mr Viet’s collection.”
