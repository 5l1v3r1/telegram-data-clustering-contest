RUTHLESS

Ruth Davidson mocks Boris Johnson for being sacked by The Times – then reveals she’ll quit as MSP in 2021

22 Nov 2019, 21:37 by Chris Musson

RUTH Davidson cracked a joke about Boris Johnson being fired from a newspaper — then confirmed she would quit politics in 18 months’ time.

The ex-Scots Tory boss mocked his 1988 sacking from The Times at a journalist bash in Glasgow today.

Ms Davidson finally confirmed she’d stand down as an MSP in 2021 as she revealed she’s “terrified” of having a “real job”.

The ex-BBC reporter sparked laughter as she listed politicians who had similar career paths: "Winston Churchill, a journalist who became possibly our greatest ever Prime Minister.

"Michael Foot, lauded editor of the Evening Standard, who led the opposition.

"And Boris Johnson who got sacked by The Times.”

The PM was axed for making up a quote about the discovery of Edward II’s long-lost palace.

Ms Davidson also told the charity lunch as a rookie journo 20 years ago she called Nicola Sturgeon before 8am every Sunday “out of badness”.

She added: “I’ve always had a grudging admiration for her work ethic.”

Last month, Ms Davidson was under pressure to quit as an MSP after she took a £50k-a-year PR advisor role.

She was set to bag the extra cash for just 24 days of work for Tulchan Communications.

But after challenging MSPs to report her to watchdogs if they believed her lobbying firm job broke Holyrood rules - she quit her position with Tulchan.
