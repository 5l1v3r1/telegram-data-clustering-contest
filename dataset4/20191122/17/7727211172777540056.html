Popular wedding venue will close in the new year and could be replaced with a car park

The council plans to knock down the building and replace it with a temporary car park

22 Nov 2019, 17:21 by Christopher McKeon

A Huyton wedding venue will close in the new year and could be knocked down and replaced with a temporary car park.

Knowsley Council’s cabinet agreed in a private session on Wednesday night to close The Venue in Huyton town centre in the face of continued financial losses.

Council documents reveal that The Venue was £87,000 in the red last year and is on course to lose the council £106,000 this year.

The local authority estimated it would also need to invest £2.2 million over the next 10 years in maintaining the building, which is used for a range of events including concerts and weddings.

A council spokesperson said: “Following a thorough review, Cabinet has made the decision to close The Venue in Huyton.

“The building has been running at a financial loss and needs a significant amount of investment in the structure and fabric of the building to keep it open.

“In addition, despite extensive efforts over the last six years to increase usage, it is under-utilised. The Venue will close on 1 January 2020.”

When the possibility of closing The Venue was floated in August , a council spokesperson said the “current financial climate” and continuing cuts from central government meant the council “cannot continue to subsidise The Venue”.

In its place, documents suggest, the council intends to provide a temporary car park at the cost of £450,000 but there is no indication of any longer-term plans for the site.

A council report said: “The demolition of The Venue and the provision of a temporary surface car park will contribute to the proposed regeneration of the Huyton Village centre and thereby enable accelerated business growth and the creation of new jobs and housing.”
