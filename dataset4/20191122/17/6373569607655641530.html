Woman says her dog was stolen from Plymouth street

Diane Green is heartbroken after her beloved shih tzu Kizzy appeared to have been taken

22 Nov 2019, 17:15 by Molly Dowrick

A Plymouth woman is devastated after she believes her beloved dog was stolen from a Plymouth street.

Local lady Diane Green, 73, was walking her dogs along Norwich Avenue in Whitleigh when her shih tzu was allegedly stolen on Saturday, November 16.

Mrs Green told Plymouth Live: "Somebody took her. I was walking along the pavement with my other dog - and she, my shih tzu, was walking behind me," but when Mrs Green turned around, her dog was 'gone'.

Mrs Green believes her dog, named Kizzy, has been stolen, but didn't see anybody abduct her.

Mrs Green is heartbroken and is offering a reward for the safe return of the stolen dog.

Mrs Green told Plymouth Live: "Somebody's taken her, I was walking along the pavement with my other dog - and she, my shih tzu, was walking behind me."

Mrs Green's daughter-in-law, Vicky Green added: "When she turned around, she [Kizzy the dog] was gone."

Kizzy the dog 'never ever runs away from me,' says Mrs Green, and 'always stays behind me and always keeps her eye on me'.

She said: "My dog knows this place [Whitleigh] back to front. If I had left her there, she would be able to get home, [she hasn't] so somebody's taken her."

The incident is thought to have happened at 3.30pm on Saturday, November 16 at Norwich Avenue in Whitleigh.

The dog is a small black and white 'going grey' shih tzu named Kizzy, who is 13 years old and has one eye missing.

Kizzy also has a 'large lump on her side' and wears a pink collar with no tag, Mrs Green says. Kizzy is also chipped.

Mrs Green says she's having panic attacks from the stress of her missing dog.

Mrs Green said: "She's been with me all her life - this is breaking my heart, it could kill me off.

"I'm having panic attacks because of it."

There are posters around the area and everyone knows Kizzy, says Mrs Green, but no one has come forward with her missing dog.

She is offering a reward for her shih tzu's safe return.

"Please, please bring her back, bring her back home," she added.

If you have any information about Mrs Green's missing dog, please contact the police on 101@dc.police.uk, quoting crime reference CR-105337-19.
