Thugs who beat homeless man to death and dumped body in sleeping bag are jailed

Homeless Latvian man Sandris Abimeicevs was brutally battered and dumped in a Birmingham street and left with brain damage, smashed ribs and a broken nose, a court heard

22 Nov 2019, 17:03 by Joseph Wilkes, https://www.facebook.com/dailymirror/, Tom Hitchenor

Three men have been jailed for life after a homeless man was savagely beaten, wrapped in a sleeping bag and dumped in a UK road 'like a bag of rubbish' - before his attackers went off to buy booze.

Homeless Latvian man Sandris Abimeicevs, 55, was brutally battered and left with brain damage, smashed ribs and a broken nose among a total of 50 visible injuries in a booze-fuelled attack.

Russian national Olegas Borisovskis, 60, along with Lithuanian nationals Saulius Lemezonas, 44, and Vytautus Medeckis, 30, left his body before going to buy alcohol.

They have no been jailed for life for murdering Mr Abimeicevs, wrapping him in the sleeping bag on November 6 last year.

Judge Paul Farrer QC ordered Lemezonas and Medeckis to serve a minimum of 20 years and Borisovskis 18 years in jail before being considered for release.

He told Birmingham Crown Court: "All of the defendants were drinking heavily. I have no doubt they were drunk during much if not all of this period. There was a disagreement which lead to violence.

"The precise nature of the falling out is not clear but it appears to have stemmed from the belief that Mr Abimeicevs stole money from Borisovskis.

"Mr Abimeicevs was subjected to a brutal and sustained beating. The violence extended over many hours.

"I have no doubt he was struck numerous times with fists, feet and a weapon which may have been part of a broken chair."

One of his attackers filmed Abimeicevs being taunted.

The judge said there was a pause in the violence when on a video the defendants could be heard discussing the condition of the victim and describing as a "rat."

He went on: "The three of you were involved in beating and humiliating an already seriously injured and therefore helpless man.

"On any view the behaviour by the defendants demonstrated a complete indifference as to whether Mr Abimeicevs died or not."

A horrified neighbour saw the trio dragging his body and alerted emergency services who were unable to save Mr Abimeicevs.

Police arrested the men, who were covered in blood, at the scene and they were found guilty of murder following a three-week trial.

The court heard Mr Abimeicevs was homeless and staying with the other two defendants, who were also homeless, in a bedsit which was let to Borisovskis.

He had allowed Mr Abimeicevs to stay along with Lemezonas and Medeckis.

All four men had been drinking over two to three days, but on the evening of the murder, a neighbour saw three men drag something down the stairs and dump it in the street.

Two of the men were also captured on CCTV going to a local store to buy more alcohol.

When they were interviewed by police they tried to blame each other but the jury found them all guilty of murder.

Rachel Brand QC, prosecuting, said it was not known exactly what went on in the flat between the late afternoon of November 5 and early evening of November 6 and that the beating went on over a protracted period of time.

"When it was clear he was dead or dying they put him in a sleeping bag and took him outside in the street where they dragged him along the pavement and left him like a bag of rubbish," she said.

"There was no good reason to kill Mr Abimeicevs but they were all very drunk, all three of them and maybe that is an explanation."

She said a neighbour, who had heard loud noises, had witnessed the "incredible scene" of the victim's body being dragged down the stairs and had called the police.

The defendants blamed each other when quizzed by the police.

Rossaro Scamardella QC, for Lemezonas, said he had been in the UK for 11 years after coming from Lithuania and had worked in labouring jobs.

His criminal record was indicative of someone who's life had been "blighted by drink" and which had "culminated in this appalling incident".

Detective Inspector Michelle Allen, from West Midlands Police homicide unit, said: "This was a brutal attack on a man outnumbered by the other three, who didn't stand a chance to defend himself.

"He would have known extreme pain before his death and I am pleased the jury recognised that all three are culpable for his murder.

"My thoughts remain with Sandris' family, I hope that this verdict brings them a sense of justice and allows them to start to come to terms with his tragic and senseless death."
