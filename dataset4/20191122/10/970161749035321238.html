Pet groomer filmed throwing cat at wall after it bit him

22 Nov 2019, 10:19

A shocking video has emerged of a pet groomer throwing a cat against a wall after it appeared to bite him.

In the security footage, a topless man can be seen holding down the animal at Carolina Grooming, in Charleston, South Carolina, while another worker tries to cut its fur.

The cat then repeatedly struggles against the groomer, causing him to yell out before losing his temper and violently hurling it against the wall.

He then walks away from the table as a female worker shouts: ‘Babe, you can’t do that. You should have just let him go.’

The incident was captured in May this year, but has only now been released by a previous manager, allegedly because he had been let go.

Marcia Alfaro, who owns the cat in the video, told News 2 the footage made her feel sick to her stomach.

She continued: ‘I can’t even watch it, I can’t see my baby hurt.’

Daryl Kornickey, who owns Carolina Grooming, claims the man in the video was fired immediately after he saw the security footage.

He says he retired three years ago due to poor health, and had left manager David Thomas in charge of hiring staff.

According to Live 5 News, the employee in the video was hired by Mr Thomas, who allegedly leaked the clip because he has since been let go.

Mr Kornickey said: ‘He made some bad hires and because of him being fired, now he wants to try to take down my business.’

He added that he has since resumed the reins of his business, telling customers he will ‘always be watching and visiting’ from now on.

However, Mr Thomas has contested the claims and says Mr Kornickey refused to fire the worker after the incident.

He told ABC News 4: ‘During that incident, I wasn’t there. I was home recovering from getting my teeth pulled. The guy that got bit, I wanted to fire him, and Daryl told me don’t do it.’

He confirmed that he did post the video, but said he had only waited until now because the business owner had been his neighbour up until recently.

Mr Thomas said: ‘I’m not gonna go out there and stir the pot just to watch it bubble in my face.’

Charleston County Sheriff’s Office said his agency say they are now launching an investigation of the incident, after the video emerged online.

Ms Alfaro has stated that she does not plan to press charges.
