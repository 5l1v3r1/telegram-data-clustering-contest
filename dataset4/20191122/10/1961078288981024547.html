Our Best Vegetarian Thanksgiving Recipes

22 Nov 2019, 10:00 by Margaux Laskey

For a full-on meat-free Thanksgiving feast or just some dishes to feed a few vegetarians, here are our best vegetarian Thanksgiving recipes for a memorable holiday meal. (Looking for vegan Thanksgiving recipes or gluten-free Thanksgiving recipes? We’ve got you covered, too.)

Vegetarian Thanksgiving Appetizers

Puff pastry is your friend: This baked brie with quick cranberry jam and this leek, mushroom and goat cheese tart make excellent use of the staple and will keep your guests sated until the big meal is ready. Tangy, bright lemony whipped feta dip is a great counterpoint to all of the rich, brown foods served at the Thanksgiving table, and this roasted garlic white bean dip is a cinch to throw together (and vegan).

Vegetarian Thanksgiving Main Dishes

With it’s crisp puff pastry shell, rich mushroom filling and port wine sauce, Alexa Weibel’s mushroom Wellington may take center stage from this year forward. You could also cook Sam Sifton’s mushroom lasagna, Melissa Clark’s harvest tart with pumpkin and peppers or Yotam Ottolenghi’s butternut squash and fondue pie with pickled red chiles. Or make all four! It’s Thanksgiving after all.

Vegetarian Thanksgiving Side Dishes

Many vegetarians forgo a main dish and instead pile their table with side dishes. We can get on board with that. There’s stuffing (or stuffing-stuffed mushrooms!), mashed potatoes (don’t forget the gravy!) and sweet potatoes. For a jolt of green, Julia Moskin’s green beans with ginger and garlic (above) and Mark Bittman’s roasted brussels sprouts with garlic are perennial favorites. And Yotam Ottolenghi’s roasted butternut squash and red onions with lemony tahini sauce is a riot of flavors and textures.

Vegetarian Thanksgiving Desserts

Many desserts are vegetarian, but we’ll take this opportunity to plug some of our readers favorite dessert recipes anyway. David Tanis’s cranberry curd tart is jaw-droppingly beautiful, an Instagram-worthy crowd favorite that’s also gluten-free. (Pro tip: The secret to achieving that ruby red color every time is puréeing the cooked cranberries in a blender or food processor, then straining the curd at least once through a fine-mesh sieve.) If you think there’s no such thing as a dessert that isn’t chocolate, we have three words for you: pecan pie truffles. For traditionalists, our apple pie, pumpkin pie and pecan pie are tried-and-true. Just add ice cream or whipped cream, or both.
