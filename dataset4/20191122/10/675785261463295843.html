Stealthy python leaps from pond to catch deer as its stops for a drink

Incredible footage captured at the watering hole in Maharashtra, India, shows the snake using it's sneaky hunting skills to devastating effect

22 Nov 2019, 10:30 by Lila Randall

Incredible footage shows the moment an enormous python leaps from a pond to catch a wild deer as it drinks.

The unfortunate animal was with several other members of its herd as they stopped to rehydrate at the pool of water in Maharashtra, India.

The CCTV footage shows the herd of deer sensing danger at the watering hole as they timidly go back and forth to the water’s edge.

But it isn’t long before the serpent seizes its opportunity to ambush the helpless animal by rocketing out of the water at lightening speed.

It then sinks its fangs into the deer as it goes in for the kill on the muddy banks of the swamp in the Karwa forest.

 

The shock of the attack sees the deer squirming but only for a brief moment as the snake quickly coils itself around its prey and wrestles until its lifeless.

For almost a minute, the snake, now tightly knotted around the deer, squeezes as the pair bob in the shallows of the water.

The Central Chanda Forest Department released CCTV footage on November 21 showing the eye-popping incident.

Maharashtra is rich in biodiversity, with the central-western Indian state home to at least seven national parks and wildlife sanctuaries.

Last month, a 15-foot python weighing nearly 70kg was rescued from a sugarcane field in Hapur that took six men to remove it from the village, ANI, an Asian media site, reported.

Gyanendra Tiwari farmer told ANI: While working on the farm we saw a long python, after which we informed the other local villagers who reached the spot and caught the python.”

The Forest Department released the snake near to the river.
