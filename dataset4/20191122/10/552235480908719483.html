David Tennant to play notorious Scots serial killer Dennis Nilsen in new ITV drama

The former Doctor Who Scot will step into the shoes of the twisted monster who butchered at least a dozen people between 1978 and 1983.

22 Nov 2019, 10:53 by Nicholas Keyden, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/nicholas-keyden/

David Tennant is set to play one of Britain's most notorious serial killers in a new TV drama.

The Scot actor will step into the shoes of Dennis Nilsen in ITV's Des which tracks the monster's five-year campaign of terror.

Twisted Nilsen murdered at least a dozen men between 1978 and 1983 and later admitted performing sex acts on some of their corpses.

He lured his victims to two homes in North London, where they were strangled and drowned.

The Scotsman, who initially admitted to killing 15, was convicted of six murders and two attempted murders in 1983, and was jailed for life in November that year.

He was caught after a plumber discovered body parts and bones were causing a blockage to drains around his Muswell Hill home.

Doctor Who star Tennant will lead a cast including Line of Duty star Daniel Mays as Detective Chief Inspector Peter Jay and The Crown's Jason Watkin.

The show, based on Brian ­Masters' book Killing For Company, follows police on their quest for justice in the wake of Nilsen's arrest.

ITV’s head of drama, Polly Hill, said: “This starts with ­Nilsen’s arrest and is looking at him through the eyes of the police officer trying to identify those he killed and deliver ­justice.

"David Tennant is going to be brilliant as Nilsen and, with Daniel and Jason, this has an incredible cast.”

Former civil servant Nilsen burned his victims' bodies, or flushed body parts down the toilet during his sickening series of killings.

If his victim was unconscious after being strangled, he would then drown them in a bathtub.

He died in prison last May at the age of 72.

Des is due to start filming soon and is believed to be aired next year.
