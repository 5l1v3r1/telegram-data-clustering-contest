Leeds to swoop as Dyche green lights January exit for Burnley man

22 Nov 2019, 10:31

Leeds are preparing to swoop after Burnley boss Sean Dyche cleared striker Matej Vydra to leave the club in January to get more game time, according to reports.

Vydra moved to Turf Moor from Derby last summer in an £11m move and having only played 13 Premier League games last season, the Czech Republic international has only played four so far this time round and is yet to start.

According to the Daily Mirror, Dyche realises the striker is unlikely to see his game time increase and is prepared to let him leave for a cut-price fee in January.

The newspaper says that Dyche is willing to see a £7.5m deal go through with Leeds said to be heavily interested in the 27-year-old.

It is claimed that given Vydra has experience in the Championship, Whites boss Marcelo Bielsa wants to add the attacker to his squad in January as they look to mount a serious promotion push.

Vydra has played 22 games for Burnley since his move from the Rams, scoring two goals and assisting four others in that time.

The Clarets make their return from the international break on Saturday when they play Watford at Vicarage Road in Premier League action.
