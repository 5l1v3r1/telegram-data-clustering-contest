'Worries me a lot' - How Bristol City rate Nottingham Forest ahead of Ashton Gate trip

Bristol City v Nottingham Forest | Both sides come into the game looking to build on derby-day wins before the international break

22 Nov 2019, 10:31 by Ben Husband

Bristol City fans have been having their say on a pivotal looking Championship clash against Nottingham Forest.

Sabri Lamouchi's men make the trip to Ashton Gate looking to continue the winning run started before the international break.

Wins over Luton and Derby have left Forest in fifth place, ahead of Saturday's opponents on goal difference alone.

The Robins also beat their rivals before the two week break, after breaking their hoodoo and winning in South Wales against Cardiff City.

Forest have a poor recent record away at Bristol, having failed to win there since 2011.

We took a look at popular fan forum OTIB to get a flavour of how the Robins supporters are feeling ahead of the big game between two play-off hopefuls.

1960maaan: Forest 2 wins , 2 losses in last 4. Don't score too many, don't concede very many. Winnable , but feels like a 1-0 either way.

Robbored: Our home form is patchy at best and our away form is excellent so it wouldn’t be huge surprise to draw with Forest

Mozo: The Forest game has stalemate written all over it. It could be one of those games where neither side want to take too many risks and where if still level in the latter stages, they effectively enact a truce. A draw could keep both teams in the top 6, after all

Davefevs: I see similarities between Forest and us in the way we play
