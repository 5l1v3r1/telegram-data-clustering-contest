Chris Evans Defends Getting Political, Says He ‘Never Felt Limited Or Handcuffed’ By Captain America Role

22 Nov 2019, 10:35 by Brent Furdyk

Chris Evans is one of many celebrities to share his political views on social media, and even visited Capitol Hill in Washington, D.C., earlier this year as part of an upcoming project intended to bridge America’s political divide between left and right.

The erstwhile Captain America has also pulled no punches in his criticism of the Trump administration on Twitter, which has increased in recent months.

Speaking with Yahoo! Entertainment, the “Avengers” star addressed speculation that the increasingly political nature of his tweets is connected with his recent departure from the Marvel Cinematic Universe.

“I did it during Cap, too; it wasn’t like I waited until that run was over,” Evans said of broadcasting his political views on Twitter.

“I was doing it while we were filming those movies.”

Evans also stated that he was never told to tone it down, even when playing arguably America’s most iconic superhero.

“I’ve never felt limited or handcuffed by the roles I play or the studios I worked for,” Evans continued. “I never in my life had a studio tell me to watch what I’m saying.”

However, Evans did admit that navigating the intersection between show-business celebrity and politics can be “tricky” when you run the risk of alienating those who don’t share the same views.

“We live in an industry that’s basically about ticket sales, and you understand a downstream impact could hurt that,” he explained. “But sometimes things matter just too much.”

Meanwhile, as his Twitter followers are well aware, Evans isn’t all about politics, and also spends a lot of time tweeting videos and photos of his beloved rescue dog, Dodger.

“Who doesn’t love the dog?” he quipped. “You gotta balance it, you know what I mean? I get political, but I also got a great dog!”
