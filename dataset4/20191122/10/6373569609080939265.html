Former Plymouth Argyle striker Bradley Wright-Phillips hailed as one of the best forwards in MLS history

Wright-Phillips is leaving the New York Red Bulls after seven seasons and 126 goals

22 Nov 2019, 10:41 by Chris Errington

The goal-laden career of former Plymouth Argyle striker Bradley Wright-Phillips at the New York Red Bulls is over.

Wright-Phillips is out of contract after the end of the 2019 Major League Soccer season and it was announced on Thursday that the 34-year-old will not be offered a new deal.

He scored a total of 126 goals in 240 appearances for the Red Bulls over seven highly successful campaigns.

Red Bulls sporting director Denis Hamlett said: “Brad will go down as one of the best forwards in MLS history, and certainly a major part of the success of this club since he arrived in 2013.”
