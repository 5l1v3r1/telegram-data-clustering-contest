Woman's arm broken in violent mobile phone robbery in Paisley

The 28-year-old was brutally battered by a man and woman who she met to sell a mobile phone.

22 Nov 2019, 10:43 by David Campbell

Two thugs sprayed deodorant in a woman’s face before breaking her arm and stealing her phone in a vicious Paisley street robbery.

The 28-year-old was attacked by a man and woman at the junction of Whitehaugh Avenue and Kelburne Drive at around 10.35pm on Wednesday.

It’s understood the victim was brutally battered when she went to meet them to sell a mobile phone.

The vile duo sprayed her in the face with deodorant before breaking her arm.

She was taken to the Royal Alexandra Hospital, in Paisley, to be treated for her injuries.

She suffered a fractured left forearm and bruising to her face in the assault.

A Police Scotland spokesman said: “A 28-year-old woman was allegedly assaulted by two people at the junction between Whitehaugh Avenue and Kelburne Drive, Paisley at around 10.35pm on Wednesday.

“The victim suffered a fractured arm and bruising and was treated at the Royal Alexandra Hospital for her injuries.

“Our enquiries are ongoing.”

The force spokesman confirmed a man, 39, and a 31-year-old woman have been arrested in connection with assault and robbery charges and a report has been submitted to the procurator fiscal.

They are expected to appear in Paisley sheriff Court at a later date.

Meanwhile, a 45-year-old man has been arrested in connection with a robbery in Paisley.

It’s claimed the man burst into a flat in Speirsfield Court and attacked a 46-year-old man on Monday, November 18.

A safe and its contents were stolen.

It’s understood the victim was injured.

A Police Scotland spokesman said: “We can confirm a 45-year-old man has been arrested in connection with the alleged assault and robbery of a 46-year-old man in Speirsfield Court, Paisley, on Monday, November 18.

“A report has been submitted to the procurator fiscal.”
