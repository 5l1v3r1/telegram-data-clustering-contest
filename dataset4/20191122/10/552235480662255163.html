Puppy left for dead in Hamilton park on coldest night of the year

Beans was found by a member of the public during sub-zero temperatures.

22 Nov 2019, 10:07 by Anthony Blair

A puppy was left for dead in a park in Hamilton on the coldest night of the year.

The SSPCA said the pup , named Beans, was abandoned in a cardboard box and would "almost certainly" have died if it had not been discovered in a park in South Lanarkshire on Monday.

The eight-week-old dog, believed to be a Jack Russell terrier cross, was found during sub-zero temperatures .

Fortunately, it was found by a member of the public, who brought it in for the night before getting in touch with animal welfare groups.

The hunt is on to find the dog's owner.

Sarah Park, a rescue officer for the Scottish SPCA, said: "The dog was found in a cardboard box, lined with a blanket, at a local park near to Quarry Street.

"The young dog, which we believe is a Jack Russell terrier cross, is male and is around eight weeks old.

"We are grateful to the member of the public who took him in and kept him safe overnight.

"The night in question was the coldest so far this year and temperatures were below freezing.

"Had he not been found, Beans would have almost certainly died so we are keen to find out the circumstances surrounding how he came to be in the park.

"He is friendly so we suspect he is used to human contact.

"One of our animal rescue and rehoming centres is providing the care he needs."

Anyone with information is asked to call the charity's confidential animal helpline on 03000 999 999.
