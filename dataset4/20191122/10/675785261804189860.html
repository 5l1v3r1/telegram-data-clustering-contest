I’m A Celeb fans worry for upset Jacqueline Jossa as she fears doing snake trial

Social media was awash with fans worrying over the actress tackling the task in Friday's upcoming episode

22 Nov 2019, 10:16 by Lily Waddell, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/lily-waddell/

I'm A Celeb viewers fear for devastated Jacqueline Jossa after she voiced her concerns over doing the snake trial.

Uncomfortable scenes saw the mother-of-two's eyes grow wide with fear and she laughed nervously after being picked for Snake Hotel.

All the stars in camp had rushed to console her but Jacqueline remained fearful.

Social media was awash with fans worrying over the actress tackling the task in Friday's upcoming episode.

"Jacqueline - I'm already upset for her #ImACeleb ," one fan wrote, leading the troops.

Jacqueline will be checked into Snake Hotel where they will encounter lots of the slithering beasts.

The scaly reptiles will be lurking all over the mock-up hotel room and corridors while she is under pressure to hunt out the stars to get food to camp.

They will be competing to get 10 stars to feed both camps - the original as well as the jailhouse site where four campmates have been exiled to.

Others added: "Awwww I hope Jacqueline absolutely smashes the next trial like she did with the spider one! #ImACeleb ."

" #imaceleb Jacqueline doing a trial please why would anyone vote for that cutie I feel so bad for my favourite."

"Why is everyone being so mean to Jacqueline I don’t get it she’s a cutie pie #ImACeleb ."

But husband Dan Osborne thinks it will be "good" for Jacqueline as he's excited to watch the trial.

Fans watched on with awe as Jacqueline found her courage when she faced her biggest fear spiders in her first I'm A Celeb trial.

The former EastEnders star had 50 spiders crawling around in the head tank with her and 20 in the box where her hands were retrieving the stars.

While she cried watching all her camp mates perform their trials, she put on a brave face and managed to get both her allocated stars.

Meanwhile, Dan has been tasked with looking after their daughters Ella, four, and Mia, one, while he holds down the fort at home.
