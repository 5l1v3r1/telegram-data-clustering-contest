5 minors rescued in anti-human trafficking drive

22 Nov 2019, 10:47 by Alfred P. Dalizon

FIVE female teenagers said to have been recruited by a prostitution ring in Iligan City were rescued by the local police in an anti-human trafficking operation on Thursday, Police Regional Office 10 director, Brigadier General Rolando B. Anduyan announced yesterday.

The teenagers whose names were withheld were about to be sold to sex fiends when they were rescued inside the Sakura Suites in Barangay Ubaldo Laya, Iligan City, the official said. He added that a suspect identified as Mardy Amor Sumalinog alias ‘Mawie Wilson’ was arrested during the operation and will be facing charges for violation of the Expanded Anti-Trafficking in Persons Act of 2012.

According to Iligan City Police Office director, Colonel Michael Pareja, the operation was conducted in line with their Oplan: Salvar Inosente’ aimed at rescuing young human trafficking victims in the city.

Pareja said that during the operation conducted by his men jointly with the PRO10 Regional Mobile Force Battalion 10 and the Women and Children Protection Center-Mindanao Field Unit, suspect Sumalinog was arested during the entrapment operation.

Recovered from the suspect’s possession were P2,000 in marked money.
