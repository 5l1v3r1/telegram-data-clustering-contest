What did Rachel Riley tweet about Jeremy Corbyn?

22 Nov 2019, 12:58

Rachel Riley angered scores of people this week and there are now calls for her to be fired after she posted a picture of herself online wearing a t-shirt that had an edited picture of Jeremy Corbyn being arrested in London while protesting apartheid in South Africa in the 1980s on the front.

The Countdown presenter and former Strictly Come Dancing contestant has since hit back at those criticising her, defending herself via her official Twitter account before stating yesterday that she would not be tweeting any further responses on the matter.

What did Rachel Riley tweet about Jeremy Corbyn?

Rachel Riley’s tweet containing the picture of her wearing the t-shirt in question was sent on November 19, the night of ITV’s leadership debate.

‘I didn’t feel comfortable knowing my workplace was to be full of racists tonight. I don’t endorse Boris, but I do endorse #NeverCorbyn. Please see my pinned thread if you still don’t understand why,’ the 33-year-old tweeted.

She caused uproar because on her top, the photo of Corbyn from the 1980s wearing a sign that read: ‘Defend the Right to Demonstrate Against Apartheid’ had been edited to read ‘Jeremy Corbyn is a racist endeavour.’
