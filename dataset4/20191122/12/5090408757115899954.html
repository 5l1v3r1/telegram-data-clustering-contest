Henry Cavill revealed he once auditioned for James Bond, but a director told him he was too “chubby” for the role.

“The Man of Steel” star said part of his audition process for “Casino Royale” involved walking out of a bathroom with nothing but a towel on.

“I remember the director, Martin Campbell, saying, ‘Looking a little chubby there, Henry,'” Cavill said in an interview with Men’s Health.

But acknowledging he had a poor diet at the time, Cavill, who said he was called “Fat Cavill” and was teased for his weight as a child, said the director’s dismissal was a wake-up that made him rethink his physical health.

“I didn’t know how to train or diet,” he told Men’s Health. “And I’m glad Martin said something, because I respond well to truth. It helps me get better.”

Prior to his James Bond audition, Cavil had parts in “Midsomer Murders” and 2002’s “The Count of Monte Cristo.” He was just 23 when he lost out to Daniel Craig as 007.

Cavill said he also missed out on major parts in “Twilight” and “Tristan + Isolde.”

It wasn’t until he got the lead in the 2011 fantasy film, “Immortals,” that his stardom rose.

But the role of a lifetime didn’t come without discomfort – Cavill said he had to follow a rigourous diet for six months before filming.

“It was my first experience working on a movie and having my shirt off for most of the damn time … It’s very emotionally taxing,” he told the magazine. “When you add the lack of food and pressure on top of that, it’s rough.”

The equally buff roles continued when Cavill scored the role of Superman in “Man of Steel.”

His next major release sees him star as Geralt in the Netflix adaptation of the hugely popular game, “The Witcher,” which premieres in December.
