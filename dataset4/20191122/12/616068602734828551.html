Dog taken, now back home

22 Nov 2019, 12:45 by Nicholas Johansen

UPDATE: 12:45 p.m.

Duke wasn't gone for very long.

The owner of the five-year-old black goldendoodle says he is back home safe and sound.

Apparently, it was all just a misunderstanding.

ORIGINAL 12:10 p.m.

It's every dog owner's nightmare.

Friday morning, about 10 a.m, an SUV pulled up to a home on Kelowna's Abbott Street and stole Duke, a black goldendoodle, from the yard.

“I left for work, I locked the door, and my roommate, whose dog it is, was home upstairs,” Kate Belton said. “When she came downstairs to leave for work, he was nowhere to be found.”

Their home, near the corner of Abbott and Rose Avenue, has a dog door that five-year-old Duke uses to go out into the fenced yard.

“They started knocking on neighbours' doors, and one of the neighbours said they saw a blue SUV come and take him,” Belton said. “There were two people in the SUV, and one was a guy, but that's all we know.”

Belton is hoping someone in the area might have surveillance cameras that captured video of dognapping.

“He's all black with a white spot on his chin and one on his chest,” Belton said. “He has no collar on, no tattoos and no microchip.”

Belton has filed a police report, and called local vet clinics and the RDCO dog control line.

“He is the sweetest boy, and we love him so much. Please help us get him back home.”
