How long is J.J. Watt out? Injury timeline, return date, latest updates on Texans pass rusher

22 Nov 2019, 12:33 by James Crabtree-Hannigan

The Texans are fighting for a playoff spot in 2019 despite being down one of their best players.

Five-time Pro Bowler and three-time Defensive Player of the Year J.J. Watt went down with a pectoral injury after making a tackle Week 8 against the Raiders hasn't been back on the field since.

Watt has been injured multiple times before, missing the final 13 games of the 2016 season when he rushed back from a herniated disk and losing his 2017 year to a Week 5 knee injury that required surgery.

Below is all you need to know about Watt's newest injury, including a timeline of his injury updates and when the Texans might get him back in the lineup.

How long will J.J. Watt be out?

J.J Watt is out for the rest of 2019.

After bringing down Raiders running back Josh Jacobs behind the line of scrimmage in the second quarter of Houston's 27-24 win over Oakland in Week 8, Watt showed discomfort in his left shoulder and eventually ran to the locker room.

He was initially considered questionable to return to the game but was later downgraded to out. He tweeted about the news that night and received season-ending surgery two days later.
