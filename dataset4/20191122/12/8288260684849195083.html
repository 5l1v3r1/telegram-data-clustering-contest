Watch: Dramatic moment lorry ploughs through level crossing - sparking travel chaos across the region

Shocking footage shows the vehicle taking out the barrier in Station Road, Hartlebury, this morning - leading to delays between Kidderminster and Stourbridge

22 Nov 2019, 12:59 by Kelly Ashmore, https://www.facebook.com/dudleybclive/

This is the dramatic moment a lorry smashed through a level crossing - leading to train delays across the region.

Shocking footage - which can be viewed above - shows the vehicle taking out the barrier in Station Road, Hartlebury  - seconds before it had fully closed.

Fire crews from Kidderminster were sent to the scene just before 10am this morning (Friday, November 22) but have since left the incident in the hands of Network Rail.

No injuries have been reported but the windscreen of the van was damaged as a result of the collision.

Debris has now been cleared and engineers are currently on site working to reattach the broken barrier.

Trains are able to run through at a reduced speed until the work has been completed but passengers have been advised to expect delays for the rest of the day, particularly between Kidderminster and Stourbridge .

A fault with the signalling system is also causing travel chaos between Birmingham Moor Street and Birmingham Snow Hill.

Signalling engineers are currently on site working to rectify this issue but National Rail has warned trains may be cancelled, delayed by up to 60 minutes or revised.

West Midlands Railway customers can use tickets on the following alternatives:

  • West Midlands Railway services between Birmingham New Street, Droitwich Spa, Worcester, Great Malvern
  • Chiltern Railways between Birmingham Snow Hill and Leamington Spa, for connecting services to / from Stratford-upon-Avon
  • Midland Metro between Birmingham Snow Hill and West Bromwich Central
  • National Express West Midlands on reasonable routes

Coaches have been requested to run between Stratford-upon-Avon and Shirley, while mini buses have been requested to run between Solihull and Dorridge.

West Midlands Railway is currently sourcing road transport to run between Stourbridge Junction and Worcester.

Passengers are urged to check West Midlands Railway or National Rail before travel.
