Ultraman inks 5-year deal

22 Nov 2019, 12:51 by Colin Dacre

On the heels of a successful test run this year, organizers of Ultraman Canada have inked a deal to hold the race in Penticton for the next five years.

The event will serve as the only qualifying race in Canada for the Ultraman World Championships in Hawaii.

The three-day race is divided into three stages. The first day is a 10-km swim followed by a 145-km bike ride. Day two is a 275-km bike and day three is a 84.4-km double-marathon.

Ultraman Canada was held in Penticton from 1993-2014 under a previous licensee who later broke away to start their own brand, which later ceased to operate in Penticton. 

Rather than see an end to the event in Penticton, former racers approached the Ultraman brand about a return.

“Under race director Brad Sawa, the Ultraman Canada 2019 race was held July 26-28 on a conditional basis. Impressed with the success of the race, positive feedback from the competitors, and the team of leaders that Sawa assembled; Jane Bockus and the Ultraman Board of Directors agreed to grant a new five-year Ultraman Canada license running through 2024,” the event said in a news release Friday.

The Ultraman brand is similar to Ironman in that you must finish an “official” qualifying race in order to have a chance to get to the World Championship in Kona. Next year, Ultraman Canada will take place in Penticton July 24-26. More information can be found here.
