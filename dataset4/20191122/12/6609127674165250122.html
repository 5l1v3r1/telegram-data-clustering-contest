WELL SCOUTED

Scotland’s potential Euro 2020 play-off opponents profiled: Israel, Norway and Serbia

22 Nov 2019, 12:17 by Mark Walker

SCOTLAND are just TWO games away from the Euro 2020 finals and could face Norway or Serbia in the play-off final if they get past Israel.

Steve Clarke's side will take on Israel at Hampden in the one-off semi-final clash.

If Scotland manage to overcome their semi-final opponents they will then play Norway or Serbia in the play-off final.

It will then be a winner takes all as they compete take a place in the competition finals next year.

But how do Scotland's potential opponents compare?

SunSport runs the rule over the three nations.

Formed: 1948.

FIFA ranking: 89.

Manager: Andreas Herzog.

Star man: Moanes Dabour. Sevilla striker who netted a double against Celtic while playing for Red Bull Salzburg. Cost the La Liga side £15 million, but has only featured in the Europa League so far.

EURO 2020 campaign: Finished fifth out of six teams and was their worst campaign for 24 years. Never in with a chance of qualifying.

Interesting fact: They are in the only country in UEFA who have previously qualified for a tournament who have had to endure a longer wait than Scotland to qualify. Their last tournament was the World Cup in 1970.

Formed: 1908.

FIFA ranking: 45.

Manager: Lars Lagerback.

Star man: Joshua King. Bournemouth's former Manchester United striker is a key man for Norway and is their top scorer with 17 goals in 46 games.

EURO 2020 campaign: They finished third in their group behind rivals Sweden and Spain, but ahead of Romania. They drew with Spain in Oslo.

Interesting fact: Norway's manager Lars Lagerback has twice applied to become Scotland manager - as long ago as 2009 he lost out to George Burley.

SERBIA

Formed: 2006 (as Serbia), 1920 as part of Yugoslavia.

FIFA ranking: 33.

Manager: Ljubisa Tumbakovic.

Star man: Sergej Milinkovic-Savic. Classy Lazio playmaker who has been linked with a big money move to the English Premiership.

EURO 2020 campaign: Also finished third in their group behind Ukraine and Portugal. Got a draw away to Portugal but lost at home.

Interesting fact: Former Rangers boss Dick Advocaat was manager of Serbia in 2014, but he lasted just four games before he quit after a disastrous spell with no wins.
