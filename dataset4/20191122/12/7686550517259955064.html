Mum's clever Nectar hack gets her £108 worth of kids clothes for £8

Neat trick could help you save plenty of money ahead of Christmas

22 Nov 2019, 12:49 by Emma Munbodh, Danny Thompson

New research shows it can cost over £75,436 to raise a child up to the age of 18.

With this in mind, families are having to think outside of the box to make the most of their budget and try and save money where possible - and one mum has revealed how using her Nectar card to clothe her children can help save a ton of cash.

Earlier this month, Nicola Shakespeare brought nearly £108 worth of kids' clothes for just £7.70 at Sainsbury's thanks to a handy Nectar trick .

The 33-year-old mum made the most of a double-up offer, picking up a load of bargains in the process, reports The Mirror.

"I’d been notified about the Nectar offer on email and then forgot about it, but saw it on social media and thought 'I really need to do this before I lose the chance to'," the thrifty mum told The Sun .

Nicola, from Porchester in Hampshire, lives with partner Daniel, 41 and their young family.

She recently posted photos of her haul in the Extreme Couponing and Bargains UK Facebook group .

It included kids' pyjamas, trousers, socks, and school dresses for her two kids and Daniel's two children - three girls and one boy between the ages two and 12 years old.

Speaking of the trove, which was 26 items all together, she said: "I was quite lucky, because I caught the end of the sale so a lot of stuff was half-price already.

"It was all the silly bits that you forget about when you’re doing the Christmas shopping - pyjamas, socks, pants - that was on sale."

The mum, who works as a finance manager, said many of the items used as Christmas gifts - putting a huge dent in her annual festive spending, she explained: "The things I bought in the offer were on my list to get for Christmas anyway."

She also bagged a pair Dumbo pyjamas for herself, and a fluffy dinosaur outfit and a grey knitted set for a friend's baby shower.

"The Dumbo pyjamas is a little Christmas treat for myself, the shopping and all the wrapping I've done," Nicola added.

She revealed she is not necessarily a bargain hunter, but is just savvy when it comes to collecting reward points.

Nicola buys nappies and wet wipes for her two-year-old at Boots, -racking up almost £200 worth of points this year. She also fills up her car tank at Tesco or Sainsbury's to gain extra points earning her a free Merlin pass every year, meaning the school holidays are cheaper and more fun when they come around.

She said: "I’ve got loyalty cards for pretty much everywhere.

"I just keep the points and whenever something like this comes through, it's like a little bonus.

"You get rewarded just for doing your food shopping."

The Nectar Double Up scheme allows you to swap 1,000 (normally worth £5) to 10,000 points (normally worth £50) for a voucher worth twice the value.

You can exchange up to 10,000 points per Nectar card account, which means you could turn £50 worth of vouchers into £100.
