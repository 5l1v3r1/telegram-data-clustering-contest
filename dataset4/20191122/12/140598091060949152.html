Bergamot and bay gin recipe

22 Nov 2019, 12:00 by Xanthe Clay

Flavouring your own gin is easy and, done right, delicious. Kate Hawkings, the drinks expert and author of Aperitif (Quadrille, £16.99) suggested this combination – she uses Beefeater gin but any fairly mild one will do.

Bergamots, the aromatic citrus that gives Earl Grey tea its perfumed flavour, are occasionally available at this time of year from greengrocers. Natoora is selling them via Ocado and Abel & Cole stocks them too. Failing that, use grapefruit and a strip of orange (ideally Seville orange if you find an early one) instead.

Prep time: 15 minutes, plus 1 week macerating

MAKES

One 75ml bottle

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 bergamot, or a small grapefruit, and an orange, ideally Seville
  • 1 x 75ml bottle of gin
  • 2 fresh bay leaves
  • Sugar syrup, to taste

METHOD

  1.  Scrub the fruit and peel off the zest in strips using a potato peeler (you’ll only need one strip from the orange). Slice off any white pith that is still attached to the zest, before putting it in a bottle with the gin and the bay leaves. Replace the cap and leave for a week or so before tasting to see if it’s ready – it might need longer maceration.
  2. When the flavour suits you, decant the gin out of the bottle and into a fresh bottle, leaving behind the zest and bay leaves. Taste it and add sugar syrup if you like.
