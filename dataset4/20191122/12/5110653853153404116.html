Myles Garrett addresses report of Mason Rudolph racial slur: 'I know what I heard'

22 Nov 2019, 12:27 by Tom Gatto

Suspended Browns defensive end Myles Garrett responded Thursday night to an ESPN report that said he accused Steelers quarterback Mason Rudolph of using a racial slur prior to a late-game fight between the teams last week.

Garrett addressed the report with a short statement on Twitter. ESPN's Josina Anderson and Adam Schefter reported Garrett made the claim during an appeal Wednesday of his indefinite suspension by the league for ripping Rudolph's helmet off and then hitting Rudolph in the head with it.

The NFL, in upholding the suspension, announced Thursday that it found no evidence Rudolph uttered a slur, and Rudolph, through a team spokesman, denied Garrett's reported allegation.
