The Walking Dead: Avi Nash 'was just about ready to lose it' filming Siddiq exit

22 Nov 2019, 12:40

*Big spoilers from the latest episode of The Walking Dead season 10, Open Your Eyes, are posted below*

So. Farewell, then, Siddiq.

Avi Nash’s time on The Walking Dead (as a living character, anyway) came to an end in this week’s dramatic season 10 episode Open Your Eyes.

The actor play(ed) Alexandria doctor Siddiq on the show, with the character given a rather painful arc this season relating to the severe PTSD he suffered after witnessing the Whisperers’ mass murder of a number of his friends in season nine.

Siddiq’s death came just as he’d finally figured out who the Whisperers’ mole in Alexandria was, too: his fellow doctor Dante.

The evil physician couldn’t let Siddiq stop him from doing Alpha’s dirty work, though, and so he proceeded to rub out Siddiq once and for all.

Due to the show filming out of sequence this season, Siddiq’s death scene wasn’t actually Nash’s final bit of work on the show.

Speaking to EW, Nash revealed that a wholesome scene he filmed with Christian Serratos (who plays Rosita, the mother of Siddiq’s daughter) was actually his last action on The Walking Dead.

‘Well, some of the tears were saved thanks to shooting out of order this year — it’s kind of funny to die and then come back the following week and shoot daddy daycare scenes with your baby strapped to your chest,’ Nash said.

‘But when we finally got to my last scene on my last day — it was the one where Siddiq and Rosita sit in bed — I was just about ready to lose it.’

Nash said it ‘sort of took everything I had to keep from breaking down’, and he was ‘so thankful’ that Serratos was there to ‘keep me from losing my s**t.’

‘I remember just wishing [director Michael Cudlitz] would keep calling for a different shot, or more coverage, or another take… anything to keep it from ending,” Nash added, saying it was ‘really emotional’ to finally wrap his scenes.

‘This cast and crew have become my close friends and my family over these past three years — they gave my gypsy heart a place to call home and it has been really tough to say goodbye.’

The Walking Dead continues Sunday at 9pm on AMC and follows a day later on Fox UK.
