Bayern Munich want to offer Mauricio Pochettino swift return to management after Spurs sacking

22 Nov 2019, 12:48 by Matt Law

Mauricio Pochettino will be offered a swift return to management by Bayern Munich following his Tottenham Hotspur sacking this week.

Bayern want to hold talks with Pochettino, even though they are aware he might prefer to wait until the New Year before considering what his next job will be.

That would suit the German club, who have told caretaker manager Hansi Flick that he will be in temporary charge until at least Christmas and possibly longer.

Bayern’s sporting director, Hasan Salihamidzic, is keen to speak to Pochettino and is believed to be willing to let the Argentine decide when he would like to take over.

Pochettino may want to see what happens at Real Madrid and Manchester United, two clubs who have approached him in the past, but Bayern would be a tempting proposition.

Bayern would represent a good chance for Pochettino to immediately add silverware to his CV and also give him some of the spending power he never had at Tottenham.

Flick was put in temporary charge after Bayern sacked Niko Kovac, who masterminded the stunning 7-2 Champions League victory over Pochettino’s Tottenham at the start of last month.

Despite some encouraging results, Flick has not been given a permanent contract which has allowed Bayern to keep their long-term options open.

Karl-Heinz Rummenigge, Bayern’s CEO, told the club’s annual general meeting: “I have held talks with Hansi and he told me reporters keep asking him if he will be in charge for the next game. I can say that until further notice he will be in charge at least until Christmas, or maybe even beyond.”

Bayern are third in the Bundesliga table, four points behind leaders Borussia Monchengladbach, and are top of Group B in the Champions League ahead of Spurs.

Despite leaving Tottenham in 14th place in the Premier League table and being replaced by Jose Mourinho, Pochettino’s reputation in the game remains very high.

It is recognised that he worked miracles in difficult circumstances to take Spurs into the Champions League and reach last year’s final.

His appointment at Bayern would be popular with the club’s players, particularly on-loan midfielder Philippe Coutinho, who he managed at Espanyol.

Pohettino wanted to sign Coutinho for Tottenham this summer and, on the Brazilian, he said: “As with most Brazilians, Philippe has a special magic in his feet. He has that same quality as Ronaldinho and Messi.

“Aside from the magic that he has, he also has an amazing work rate and that makes us doubt whether he fits the mould of a typical Brazilian player or a European one because his work ethic is outstanding.”
