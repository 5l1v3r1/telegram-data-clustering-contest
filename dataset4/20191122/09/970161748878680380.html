Daniel Craig confirms he's stepping down as James Bond after No Time To Die

22 Nov 2019, 09:44

Daniel Craig has confirmed No Time To Die will mark his last outing as James Bond.

The actor has played 007 since 2006’s Casino Royale, going on to star in Quantum of Solace, Skyfall and Spectre.

But after next year’s No Time To Die – the 25th Bond film – the 51-year-old will be hanging up his tuxedo.

Craig was speaking to German publication Express when he was asked if his era as Bond is really over.

The star said: ‘Yes – in the future someone else has to do it.’

The Knives Out star also shot down claims that he would turn to directing Bond 26 after handing over the reins, saying: ‘For God’s sake, no. I want to come home, eat something and then go to bed.

‘If you’re a director, then at night you’re still with the producers, screenwriters, on the phone. And then after two hours of sleep, you have to go back up to the set. No thanks.’

Daniel previously insisted he wouldn’t return to Bond after 2015’s Spectre, making infamous comments about preferring to ‘slash [his] wrists’ than play the secret agent again.

Speaking to Time Out weeks before Spectre’s release, the father-of-two said: ‘Now? I’d rather … slash my wrists. No, not at the moment. Not at all. That’s fine. I’m over it at the moment. We’re done. All I want to do is move on.

‘I don’t know what the next step is. I’ve no idea. Not because I’m trying to be cagey. Who the f*** knows? At the moment, we’ve done it. I’m not in discussion with anybody about anything. If I did another Bond movie, it would only be for the money.’

He later called it a ‘stupid answer’, but we guess the money was good enough as Craig signed on to play Bond one last time.

Actors rumoured to be taking on the iconic role after Craig’s departure include Idris Elba, Richard Madden, James Norton, Tom Hardy, Aidan Turner and Riz Ahmed.

No Time To Die is in cinemas on 3 April 2020.
