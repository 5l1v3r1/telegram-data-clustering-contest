Thousands of TSB customers waking up with 'no money' after technical problems hit bank

"We're all waking up to discover we have no money - wages and benefits alike, not been paid in, all from different companies."

22 Nov 2019, 09:44 by Sophie McCoid

Thousands of TSB customers have woken up without their wages and other payments, after technical problems rocked the bank.

The bank said payments into some accounts have been delayed and staff are investigating.

Thousands of customers are thought to be affected, with wages, benefits, pensions and other payments not paid - reports Hull Live.

A spokeswoman for TSB said: "Some  payments into TSB accounts have been delayed overnight and we are working to process these as soon as possible today.

"We apologise for the inconvenience this has caused and will ensure customers are not left out of pocket.

"If TSB customers need emergency cash or have any questions they can visit us in branch or contact us via the number on the back of their card.

"Customers can still use their cards to make payments or withdraw cash."

One customer said: "We're all waking up to discover we have no money - wages and benefits alike, not been paid in, all from different companies."

Another said: "I’ve just had my 75-year-old dad on the phone worried sick saying his pension hasn’t gone into his account this morning.

"What is going on and when will it be rectified?"

It is not the first time TSB has experienced technical problems, with a plague of IT failures back in April 2018.

Just this week, an independent report accused the bank’s board of lacking “common sense” in the run up to the problems - which left 1.9 million customers unable to bank online, including some for several weeks.
