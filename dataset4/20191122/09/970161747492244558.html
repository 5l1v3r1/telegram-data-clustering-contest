Harrison Ford to star in true crime series The Staircase

22 Nov 2019, 09:05

Harrison Ford is set to make his long-awaited return to the small screen after a 26-year absence.

According to Variety, Ford is currently attached to the upcoming TV adaptation of the 2004 French docu-series The Staircase.

The true crime series, which gained renewed prominence upon its release on Netflix last year, details the trial of novelist Michael Peterson, who was accused of murdering his wife Kathleen Peterson in December 2001.

The author claimed at the time that Kathleen died after falling down a set of stairs in their home, but police accused him of staging her fall after he bludgeoned her to death.

Michael Peterson, who pleaded not guilty to the charges, was sentenced to life in prison in 2003 after being found guilty in what was one of the longest trials in North Carolina history.

He was later released in 2017 on time served after submitting an Alford plea to reduce his charge to manslaughter.

Peterson’s case will be dramatised in the upcoming series, which is currently being shopped to streaming services and TV networks and is being executively produced by Ford.

It’s not yet clear what acting role Ford will have in The Staircase, but his involvement will end his long exile from TV.

The Indiana Jones and Star Wars actor last appeared in a scripted TV series back in 1993 for The Young Indiana Jones Chronicles, in which he appeared as an adult version of his archaeologist character.

Ford will star as Indiana for the last time in the upcoming fifth instalment of the franchise, which is set for release in 2021.
