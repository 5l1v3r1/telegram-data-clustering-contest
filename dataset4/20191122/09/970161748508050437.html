Kevin Clifton expertly confronts homophobic Strictly fan over same-sex backlash

22 Nov 2019, 09:14

Kevin Clifton has confronted a Strictly Come Dancing fan after they revealed they will boycott the show if there’s any more same-sex routines.

It’s been confirmed the BBC received almost 200 complaints from viewers who found Johannes Radebe and Grazianno Di Primo’s heart-warming performance together ‘offensive’, even though those very same critics would more than likely feel comfortable watching two men play tennis doubles or 11 gents kick a football about together.

Naturally, then, questions need to be asked. Kevin Clifton, rather than attacking one of his followers, did just that.

‘Can i ask why?’ he probed the viewer, who said it was her ‘choice’ to turn away from watching two men or women dance together.

‘Not looking for an argument just interested in why you have that opinion,’ he added.

As it stands the critic is yet to respond. Perhaps there’s nothing more to say.

The BBC refused to comment when approached by Metro.co.uk over the backlash, but it appears while there’s an overriding support for the LGBTQ+ community in the Beeb’s ballroom, there’s a problem with its fan base.

Last weekend Michelle Visage was voted out of the competition after paying tribute to her gay brothers and sisters with a routine inspired by voguing; a dance popularised by underground ballrooms in the LGBTQ+ community where those who had been cast away by the mainstream society could truly be liberated without prejudice.

The RuPaul’s Drag Race star was slammed by thousands of viewers, who claimed ‘voguing isn’t dancing.’

In turn, she fired back with an impassioned post defending her performance, which was without a doubt one of the highlights to come from the Strictly class of 2019.

Strictly Come Dancing continues tomorrow night at 7pm on BBC One.
