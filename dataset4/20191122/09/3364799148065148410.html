Tesla is cracking under pressure, much like the windows on its electric truck.

Shares of the electric-vehicle company fell as much as 4.1% in early trading on Friday after it revealed its electric truck, called the Cybertruck, in a live event on Thursday evening.

CEO Elon Musk has long hyped the truck's futuristic design, inspired by the movie "Blade Runner." During the live demonstration, however, things took an unexpected turn when Tesla's lead designer, Franz von Holzhausen, threw a metal ball at two of the truck's "armor glass" windows, smashing them.

Musk was caught off guard and uttered an expletive before saying that the ball "didn't go through — that's a plus side" and telling the audience that Tesla would fix the problem "in post."

The unveiling of the Tesla Cybertruck comes as competition in electric vehicles heats up. Ford recently unveiled its own Mustang-inspired electric SUV, called the Mach-E, which analysts at Credit Suisse said could threaten Tesla's position as the top EV maker in the US.

In addition, General Motors CEO Mary Barra announced on Thursday that the company's electric pickup truck would launch in 2021 — around the time that Tesla's Cybertruck is set to hit the market — Reuters reported.

Tesla has gained about 6% year-to-date through Thursday's close.
