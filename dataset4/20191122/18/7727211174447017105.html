'I did the best I could for him,' says duty cop after Bala stabbing

Considerable force was used in the stabbing, says Home Office pathologist

22 Nov 2019, 18:57 by Alan Weston

An on-duty police officer described the last moments of murder victim Bala Lloyd-Evans, saying "I did the best I could for him."

PC Philip Parkinson was giving evidence on the fourth day of the trial of two men charged with the murder of Mr Lloyd-Evans, 33, who was stabbed in the heart while out celebrating Liverpool's Champions League win in the early hours of June 2.

Stepbrothers Ramal Edwards, 19, of Emerson Street, Toxteth, and Kieran Perry, 25, of Wendell Street, Toxteth, both deny the murder.

PC Parkinson told Liverpool Crown Court he was on patrol in the early hours of June 2 when he came upon a fight which had erupted in Back Colquitt Street.

Under questioning from David McLachlan, QC, prosecuting, PC Parkinson said the fight had "calmed down" but then flared up again.

He added: "I could see punches being thrown, but I didn't know who by. I didn't see any weapons."

PC Parkinson said a man in a white top had started to run away, leaving another man on the ground. There was a large laceration across Bala’s chest and a lot of blood.

The officer said: "I did the best I could for him."

A crime scene was put in place and people moved out of the area.

Dr Alison Armour, a Home Office pathologist, carried out a post-mortem examination on the body of Bala Lloyd-Evans.

She said he was 5ft 7ins in height and was "of muscular build," weighing around 15 stone.

Dr Armour said there were two significant injuries to the central chest area.

The first was a stab wound which was 5.6cm in length to the central chest area which was directly in line with the nipples.

This was the stab wound that caused Bala Lloyd-Evans' death.

In addition, there was an incised wound, which was 12.2cm in length to the left side of the chest. It did not enter the chest cavity.

Dr Armour explained in detail the path that the knife took as it entered Lloyd-Evans' body, completely severing two ribs.

She estimated the depth of this wound to be up to 14cm, and that it would have required "considerable force" to inflict.

The single stab wound to the chest penetrated the heart, causing catastrophic blood loss.

Edwards has not disputed that he held the knife that caused the death of Mr Lloyd-Evans.

He was chased by police officers before he was arrested, during which time he discarded the knife - a folding lock-knife with a single cutting edge.

The knife was recovered from a location on Cornwallis Street, within the grounds of the Liverpool Workshops for the Outdoor Blind building.

The knife, along with articles of Edwards' clothing, was forensically examined -  within the back left pocket of Edwards’ jeans was contact blood staining from Mr Lloyd Evans’ blood.
