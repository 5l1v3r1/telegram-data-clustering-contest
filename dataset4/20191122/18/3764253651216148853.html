Get closer to citizens, Gov Okowa advises leaders

22 Nov 2019, 18:33

- Governor Ifeanyi Okowa of Delta is of the opinion that leaders shoud get closer to people they lead

- The governor said such move would strengthen the processes of good governance in the country

- He also urged Nigerians to seek the face of God to get a relief from the country's numerous challenges

Delta state governor, Senator Ifeanyi Okowa, on Thursday, November 21, urged Nigerian leaders to be closer to the people to strengthen the processes of good governance.

The governor made the call in a keynote address at the 2019 edition of Christian Association of Nigeria (CAN) Day celebration organised by the state chapter in the state capital, Asaba.

According to Governor Okowa, leaders must be close to their people and identify with them for good policies to be made.

“A godly leader, at whatever level, is empathetic with the pain and suffering of the people he is called to lead.

“There are far too many leaders in our society who are disconnected from the reality on ground and that does not augur well for people-centred policies and good governance,” he lamented.

He emphasised that Nigeria would experience great relief “if we will heed the divine injunction to repent, seek God's face and turn from our wicked ways.”

He commended Chairman of CAN in the state and members of the association’s executive for the programme, which rallied Christians of all denominations for fellowship and prayer.

Chairman of CAN in the state, Senior Apostle Silvester Okorote, while welcoming guests and members earlier, had said, “the state CAN Day remains an annual event which every Christian in Delta earnestly look forward to celebrating.

“It is a day when we gather from all churches and the five blocs across the state to celebrate the oneness of the body of Christ on which the high priestly prayer of Jesus Christ was cantered.”
