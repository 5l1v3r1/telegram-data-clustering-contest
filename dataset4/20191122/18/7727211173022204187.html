Two young men killed in stolen car horror crash that left car flipped on roof

Police confirmed the second victim died in hospital and are now looking for the occupants of a second stolen car

22 Nov 2019, 18:45 by Jenny Kirkham

A teenager has died after the stolen Audi he was travelling in crashed and killed the driver.

The front seat passenger, named as Luke Peaurt-Moran, was travelling along Moss Lane in Burscough in the early hours of Wednesday morning when the car collided with a wall, a telegraph pole and a second wall .

Conner Stevens , 24, from Sefton , who was driving the car at the time of the crash, died at the scene before 18-year-old Luke was rushed to hospital fighting for his life.

Lancashire Police have now confirmed that the teenager has also died as a result of the crash.

Detective Chief Inspector Richard McCutcheon, who is leading the investigation, said: “My thoughts and sympathies are with the family of Conner and Luke.

"Specially trained officers will continue to support them through this extremely sad time."

Officers investigating the crash have also said that a second stolen car, a red Fiat 500 Abarth, is believed to have been travelling in convoy with the Audi at the time of the crash, and was later found on Valley Close in Crosby a few hours later.

Police are currently still trying to trace the driver and occupants of the car.

A spokesperson for Lancashire Police said: "Police are continuing their investigation into a Burscough collision which has resulted in the death of two men.

"It happened at around 4am on Wednesday (November 20) when a white Audi S1 collided with a wall, a telegraph pole and then a second wall on Moss Lane.

"The driver, now named as Conner Stevens, 24, from Sefton , sadly died at the scene.  

"The front seat passenger, Luke Peaurt-Moran, 18, also from Sefton, was taken to Aintree Hospital but sadly later died.

"We now know that the Audi, along with a red Fiat 500 Abarth, were stolen during a burglary prior to the collision at an address in the Towngate area of Eccleston.  

"The Fiat Abarth was later recovered on Valley Close in Crosby , Merseyside.

Dealing with grief and loss

If you have been affected by any of the details mentioned in this story there are people who can help you.

Most people grieve when they lose something or someone important to them.

The way grief affects you depends on lots of things, including what kind of loss you have suffered, your upbringing, your beliefs or religion, your age, your relationships, and your physical and mental health.

Grieving is a totally normal process but there are way to get help if you need support.

Your GP is a good place to start. They can give you advice about other support services, refer you to a counsellor, or prescribe medication if needed.

Or you can contact support organisations directly, such as Cruse Bereavement Care (0808 808 1677) Samaritans (116 123) or Love Jasmine.

Det Ch Insp Richard McCutcheon, who is leading the investigation, said: “Two young men have lost their lives and so we are determined to find out exactly what has happened.

“This very much includes the potential route the Audi and the Fiat Abarth have taken and whether they were travelling together.

"If you saw either of these cars, or indeed any other vehicle that may have been travelling with them, between Towngate in Eccleston, the crash site on Moss Lane and then where the Fiat Abarth was traced on Valley Close in Crosby, we need to speak to you immediately.

“Similarly, if you have any dash cam footage of the cars or the crash itself, please make contact with us.

“Lastly, I would urge anyone who was on board the Fiat Abarth or any other vehicle that may be linked to this incident to search their conscience, do the right thing and come forward. There are two families that deserve answers and you may have vital information.”
