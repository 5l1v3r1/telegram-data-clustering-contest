It is one of the biggest Christmas food purchases you will make and this year you might have to take out a second mortgage to pay for it. It is the festive ham, the centrepiece of many Christmas Day tables and it has never been more expensive.

With African swine fever decimating pig populations in Europe and China, the price of pigs has skyrocketed. By Christmas, it is expected that ham prices will have increased 100 percent.

When you are spending big money on a ham, you want to get it right. But how do you choose?

These days there are rare breed hams, organic hams, mass-produced supermarket hams, hams on the bone and off the bone, hams heavily smoked and others barely smoked — and all at a variety of price points and expectations.

The Weekend West has done all the hard work this year, testing and tasting a range of hams available in WA.

The final result? Our Top 10 Hams for Christmas 2019.

Generally speaking mass produced hams are more wet and salty than traditionally cured artisan hams. They are pumped full of a sugary, salty curing solution and given a rapid cure, aided by nitrites to preserve it and maintain its pink colour.

Traditionally made hams take longer to make — months compared with mere days for a mass-produced ham.

They are drier but still moist, less salty and have a more complex flavour, showcasing the taste of pork rather than the brine. But all this old school artistry comes at a price.

So what’s right for you?

With the help of Australian Pork Limited, we asked ham producers to submit their product for tasting by our four-person judging panel.

We also reached out to the two major supermarket chains, Coles and Woolworths. The retailers sent in two hams at different prices.

Twenty hams were tasted and assessed to arrive at out Top 10 for this Christmas.

The top three hams — from Mondo Butchers, Torre Butchers and The Boatshed Cottesloe — were head-and-shoulders above the rest. They were in a photo finish for podium places, with barely anything between them in terms of quality and taste.

1. Mondo Black Hog Berkshire Free Range, Preservative-free ham. $26.70/kg.

This ham had it all: lovely texture, clean flavours, good salt levels, a balanced brine and incredible silky white fat.

The flavours are delightful — full, rich, clean and just salty enough without being overpowering. A deserved winner of our Top 10. Importantly, a ham of this quality will keep in the fridge a lot longer than a cheap ham, which typically deteriorate more quickly.

2. Linton Batt Black Label Berkshire Ham, The Boatshed Cottesloe. $39.95/kg.

Made by one of the characters and innovators of the WA pork industry, Linton Batt, who manages the entire ham-making process from the paddock to the finished product at his small country abbatoir and butcher shop.

This extraordinary ham is made exclusively for The Boatshed Cottesloe and is luscious, delicate, a good traditional shape, wonderfully fatty and not over-processed or swimming in brine.

3. David Torre Traditional Ham On The Bone. $17.50/kg.

A stunning ham with terrific taste and nicely dry (ie. not dripping with salty, slimy brine that is the hallmark of a cheap ham). It was in many respects as good a ham as that of our winner from Mondo Butchers, so at $17.50/kg it represents value for money.

Torre is one of the city’s finest butchers and their ham is testament to generations of refinement and testing of their cured and smoked meats.

4. P. Princi Nitrite-Free Ham. $30.99/kg

Joe Princi has been making award-winning hams for many years. His hams are consistently at the top of the pack in national ham and pork awards.

His ham is one of the finest and carefully manufactured, leading to a well-balanced ham of distinction.

5. Eurostyle Bone-In Ham. Prices vary.

Eurostyle is a Bibra Lake small-good manufacturer producing hams that routinely score gold and silver prizes in national pork awards.

Their hams are available at selected retailers, butchers and supermarkets.

Their bone-in ham was a lesson in proper European traditional meat curing — a stunner with good mouthfeel, nicely dry but sweet and succulent.

6. P. Princi Free-Range Berkshire Ham. $23.99/kg

Another cracking ham from Joe Princi and his team. Well priced and made with precision.

It has a sumptuous flavour profile, great sugar/salt balance and wonderful mouthfeel.

7. Coles Beechwood Smoked Half Leg Ham on the Bone. $11/kg.

A terrific ham at the price. It may be a supermarket brand but you would be proud to have this ham on your table.

The half leg format means you still get a good looking bone-in ham for the table at about $50 all up. A stunner.

8. Woolworths Gold Triple Smoked Bone In Half Leg Ham. $13/kg.

Another superb ham from one of the nation’s largest supermarkets. As the name suggests, this is one smoky ham.

Too much smoke can often leave a bitter, bushfire taste to the flesh, but Woolies have got the balance right. The flesh is moist without dripping brine and the flavours are clean.

Again, the half leg format gives you all the showbiz you want for your Christmas table without breaking the bank.

9. Re & Sons/ Scarfo Butchers Bone in Ham. $18.99 per kilo.

It is an old-fashioned butcher’s ham made in collaboration with Tony Scarfo at Scarfo’s Meating Place butchers in Myaree. (Re & Sons produces hams in its own right, too). Their hams are made only with female legs — yes, it makes a huge difference to the taste — from free-range porkers. The product is gluten free.

It is not too salty, not pumped with watery brine and cuts superbly. A ripper at a great price.

10. Mondo Doro Leg Ham on the Bone. Prices vary.

Another winner from a large WA manufacturer. This is an everyday, well-made ham.

It is on the bone for the wow factor you want at Christmas.

Prices vary depending on where you buy it from but it is a well-priced ham that has not sacrificed flavour or texture.
