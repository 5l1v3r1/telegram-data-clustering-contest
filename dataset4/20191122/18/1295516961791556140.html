The Maxx: Channing Tatum Adapting Sam Kieth's Cult Comic

22 Nov 2019, 18:11 by L.D. Nolan

A new adaptation of Sam Kieth's cult comic The Maxx is in the works.

According to The Hollywood Reporter, Channing Tatum's Free Association and Roy Lee's Vertigo Entertainment will team up to produce the project. The outlet does not know at this time if the project will be a TV series or a film, and no writers or filmmakers are currently attached, though the producers are holding meetings.

Published by the then fledgeling Image Comics, The Maxx ran from 1993 until 1998. The comic moves between the real world and an alternate one called the Outback. In the former, the title character is homeless, while in the latter he's immensely powerful and works to protect the Jungle Queen. In reality, the Jungle Queen is a social worker named Julie Winters, whose life is being influenced by the Outback without her knowing. MTV released an animated adaptation of the series in 1995, with Michael Haley voicing The Maxx.

In addition to The Maxx, Kieth is known for his work on Zero Girl and Sandman. He won an Inkpot Award in 2013. He'll produce this new The Maxx project alongside Tatum and Lee.

Tatum is an actor known for his roles in such films as G.I.:  Joe: The Rise of Cobra and 21 Jump Street. It's unclear what acting role he might play -- if any -- in this adaptation of The Maxx.
