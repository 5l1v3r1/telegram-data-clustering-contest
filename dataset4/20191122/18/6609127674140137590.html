IT'S OVER

Stewart Milne to step down as Aberdeen chairman with Dave Cormack set to replace him

22 Nov 2019, 18:15 by Robert Thomson

STEWART MILNE is set to call time on his 21-year stint as Aberdeen chairman.

SunSport understands the 69-year-old plans to step aside next month and will be replaced by Dave Cormack.

Milne joined the Pittodrie board in 1994 and became chairman four years later.

With the club’s move to a new £13 million training facility on the outskirts of the city now complete, he has successfully overseen the first phase of the transformation of Aberdeen FC.

And with a new stadium adjacent to Cormack Park unlikely to be opened before at least 2023, Milne has decided to hand over control after more than two decades at the helm.

The Aberdonian housebuilder will be succeeded by US-based multi-millionaire Cormack, currently the club’s vice-chairman.

Cormack was appointed second in command last December after returning the club in 2016 after a spell as chief executive at the start of the century,

He sold his software company before investing in the Dons and has since brought American Tom Crotty on board with further finance.

The 58-year-old, who stays in Atlanta, has been seen as Milne’s successor since he returned.

And after a long and sometimes turbulent reign, Milne will now hand over the keys to the boardroom.
