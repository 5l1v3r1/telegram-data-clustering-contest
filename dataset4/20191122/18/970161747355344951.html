Westlife achieves eighth UK number one album with comeback effort Spectrum

22 Nov 2019, 18:00

Westlife have triumphantly achieved their eighth number one album in the UK with their comeback effort and eleventh studio album, Spectrum.

The album, which is the Irish boyband’s first in nine years, has achieved a total of 63,000 chart sales across physical, download and streaming formats. It is also the band’s first album since they reunited in 2018, following their split in 2012.

‘Thank you so much to everyone in the UK whose made Spectrum Number 1 this week!’ the band told OfficialCharts.com

‘We can’t believe that after 21 years we’ve got another number one – our eighth number one album.

‘The reunion has been going so well, thank you for everything.’

Member Shane Filan added to Metro.co.uk: ‘What a result. We’re genuinely delighted with the way this album has gone.

‘I think we’re all pinching ourselves that how well received the new music has been. We didn’t expect it and can’t thank our fans enough.’

Spectrum is the group’s first UK number one album since their eighth studio album, Back Home, which was released in 2007.

The 11-track collection successfully beat tough competition from Celine Dion, with her twelfth English-language studio album, Courage, hitting number two on the Album Chart – her highest charting album in the UK in 17 years since A New Day Has Come was released in 2002.

Fellow boyband Take That also achieved their thirteenth top ten album, with their live album Odyssey: Greatest Hits Live entering the chart at number five. And football presenter Chris Kamara’s Christmas effort, Here’s To Christmas has made an impressive debut at number eight – Chris’ first appearance ever on the UK Charts.

Over on the Singles Chart, Tones and I continues to dominate as her single Dance Monkey spends an eighth week at the top of the charts, selling 83,000 units and racking up 9.6 million streams.

Meanwhile, Billie Eilish’s newest single, Everything I Wanted, is the highest new entry on the chart, debuting at number three, while Maroon 5 have achieved their eighth top five hit with Memories reaching a new peak of number five.

UK album charts

  1. Westlife – Spectrum
  2. Celine Dion – Courage
  3. Michael Ball and Alfie Boe – Back Together
  4. Lewis Capaldi – Divinely Uninspired To A Hellish Extent
  5. Take That – Odyssey: Greatest Hits Live
