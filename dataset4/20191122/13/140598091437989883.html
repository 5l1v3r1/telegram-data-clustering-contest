Prince Andrew steps down from Pitch@Palace role

22 Nov 2019, 13:33 by James Cook

The Duke of York has stepped down from his role at the start-up competition Pitch@Palace. The event will now continue without any royal involvement, sources said.

The series of events will now be known simply as “Pitch” and will continue to be run by the Duke’s former private secretary Amanda Thirsk.

The competition has now dropped a requirement in its legal terms that entitled the Duke and his company to a stake in the technology businesses which enter the competition following an investigation by The Telegraph.

One entrepreneur said earlier this week that it was “absurd beyond belief” that the company could take such a cut and it was “scandalous” that the terms were buried on the final page of the application process.

A clause in the event's application terms which gave the Duke the ability to claim 2pc of technology businesses was deleted from the Pitch@Palace website on Friday morning.

Royal sources said yesterday the Duke would continue to be allowed to host events “on a commercial basis” at palaces and to maintain his private office at Buckingham Palace. They said Pitch@Palace would move to his “private portfolio”.

However, the Duke has now stepped down from the helm of the scheme he founded in 2014 following pressure from sponsors.

Backers including KPMG, Standard Chartered and Bosch have already pulled out of funding the enterprise.

Last night, the Duke cancelled a planned trip to Bahrain for a Pitch@Palace event amid the furore.

Ms Thirsk, the Duke of York’s private secretary who took the blame for persuading him to record the disastrous Newsnight interview, was informed on Thursday night that her role had been abolished.

It’s understood that Ms Thirsk, a former banker who began working with the Duke in 2004, will continue to run the rebranded “Pitch” event series.
