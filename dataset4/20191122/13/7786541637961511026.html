American Music Awards 2019: What time, what channel, who is nominated, performing

22 Nov 2019, 13:34 by Debbie Lord , Cox Media Group National Content Desk

Taylor Swift will be honored Sunday as the  "artist of the decade" as entertainers from all genres of music come together for the 2019 American Music Awards.

 

>> Read more trending news 

Singer-songwriter Carole King will present the award to Swift, who has sold more than 50 million albums and won 10 Grammys during her career.

The ceremony, to be held in Los Angeles, will feature performances by Camilla Cabello, Billie Eilish,  Lizzo, Thomas Rhett  and others.

The AMAs will celebrate its 45 birthday Sunday. Billboard noted the enduring nature of the show when it reported that Elton John was among the nominees for favorite pop/rock male artist in 1974, the year the awards debuted. This year, 45 years later, he is nominated for tour of the year.

Post Malone tops the list of nominees with seven. Ariana Grande and Billie Eilish each have six nominations. Swift and Lil Nas X are both nominated for five awards.

Here's what you need to know about the show.

What time: The show begins at 8 p.m. ET

What channel: ABC is broadcasting the AMAs

Where is it being held: The ceremony will be held at the Microsoft Theater in Los Angeles.

Who is hosting: Ciara will be hosting the event.

Who is presenting: Presenters at the "2019 American Music Awards" include: Paula Abdul, Kelsea Ballerini, Tyra Banks, Chadwick Boseman, Kane Brown, Misty Copeland, Rivers Cuomo, Jamie Lee Curtis, Dan + Shay, Jenna Dewan, David Dobrik, Michael Ealy, Maddie Hasson, Maya Hawke, Jameela Jamil, Jharrel Jerome, Taran Killam, Carole King, Regina King, Heidi Klum, Katherine Langford, Dan Levy, Megan Thee Stallion, Ben Platt, Billy Porter, Patrick Schwarzenegger, Cobie Smulders, Pete Wentz and Constance Wu.

Who is performing: Scheduled to perform are: Camilla Cabello, Billie Eilish, Selena Gomez, the Jonas Brothers, Dua Lipa, Lizzo, Thomas Rhett and Shania Twain. Look for Taylor Swift to performing a medley of her hits, despite her dispute with Big Machine Records and Scooter Braun. They own the masters of her hits.
