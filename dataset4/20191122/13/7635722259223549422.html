JJ Hanrahan back in the Munster team to play Racing 92 tomorrow

22 Nov 2019, 13:24 by John Duggan

JJ Hanrahan returns from injury to line out for the Munster team in tomorrow's European Champions Cup game against Racing 92 at Thomond Park. 

The out-half has recovered from the hamstring injury that ruled him out of the win over Ospreys last weekend.

There are three changes to the Munster team that won in Wales as Hanrahan, Tadhg Beirne and Jack O’Donoghue all come into the starting side.

Mike Haley starts at full-back with Andrew Conway and Keith Earls on the wings. 

Rory Scannell and Chris Farrell continue their centre partnership with Conor Murray and Hanrahan at scrum-half and out-half respectively. 

Last week’s Man of the Match Jeremy Loughman, Niall Scannell and John Ryan comprise the front row.

Beirne partners Jean Kleyn in the second row, with O’Donoghue named in a back row that also includes skipper Peter O’Mahony and CJ Stander.

Alby Mathewson is among the replacements and is set to play his final game for the province.

Tyler Bleyendaal is injured and wasn't considered.

Munster: Mike Haley; Andrew Conway, Chris Farrell, Rory Scannell, Keith Earls; JJ Hanrahan, Conor Murray; Jeremy Loughman, Niall Scannell, John Ryan; Jean Kleyn, Tadhg Beirne; Peter O’Mahony (C), Jack O’Donoghue, CJ Stander.  

Replacements: Kevin O’Byrne, James Cronin, Stephen Archer, Billy Holland, Fineen Wycherley, Alby Mathewson, Dan Goggin, Arno Botha.
