Mum died after taking cocaine while pregnant with seventh child

22 Nov 2019, 13:54

A mum-of-six who died while pregnant with her seventh child ‘might have survived’ if she hadn’t taken cocaine in the past, an inquest heard.

Lindsey Ann Hughes, 34, became ill in her kitchen on March 14 this year and died in Ysbyty Gwynedd hospital 13 days later.

She was diagnosed with bronchopneumonia, which caused her to collapse and led to respiratory failure, the court was told.

Ms Hughes, from Llangefni, north Wales, was also found to have ‘abnormally high’ levels of the cocaine in her system when she died, and was eight times over drugs limit for driving.

North West Wales coroner Dewi Pritchard Jones stated that the drugs were likely to have ‘contributed’ to her death.

He said: ‘If she had not taken cocaine in the past the probability is that she would have survived the bronchopneumonia, but we cannot be sure.

‘Because of the bronchopneumonia she died of natural causes, this is not a drug death, the cocaine contributed but did not ultimately cause her death.’

Ms Hughes, who was 15 weeks pregnant with a son when she died, was found to have damage to her heart known as cardiac fibrosis, which can be caused by chronic cocaine use.

However, the inquest heard she had been carrying a ‘perfectly healthy male fetus’.

Mr Pritchard Jones issued a warning to the public that cocaine can and does cause long-term issues.

He said: ‘I am seeing far too many cocaine deaths as the use of cocaine in this part of the world is very common.

‘Cocaine damages the heart by killing off patches of heart tissue and causes a tightening of the vessels.

‘Even low levels of cocaine can cause catastrophic results and repeat use causes more and more damage. The public think cocaine doesn’t do any long-term harm but it does.

‘Cocaine use can and does cause long term issues.’

It was also noted that Ms Hughes’ immune system would have been suppressed due to the fact she was pregnant.

A friend of Ms Hughes, Helen Evans, told the inquest she had been ‘full of life’ and was a ‘brilliant mother’.

Ms Evans continued: ‘She was a devoted mother and partner, caring daughter and joyful sister – her death has left a massive hole in so many people’s lives.’
