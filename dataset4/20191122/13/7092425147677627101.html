Harry Potter star to reportedly replace Olivia Colman in The Crown

22 Nov 2019, 13:30 by Ciara Walsh

Imelda Staunton will reportedly take over from Olivia Colman in season five of The Crown. 

It's been reported that Imelda Staunton will take over from Olivia Colman in The Crown season five.

Season three which came out over the weekend, sees Olivia take over from Claire Foy as Queen Elizabeth II.

And it looks like they've already found the next replacement.

According to MailOnline, Imelda was recently approached by The Crown's senior producers and casting directors.

It's believed she'll begin filming the series from 2021 until 2022.

The publication report it's possible filming could continue until 2023.

Imelda, best known for her role as Professor Umbridge in Harry Potter, will become the third actor to take over the role after Claire Foy and Olivia Colman.

This isn't a surprise considering Netflix warned us of a constant change in actors.

It hasn't yet been decided how far into the monarch's life they'll delve into.

According to Metro, Netflix are branding these reports, "Pure speculation".

"We are currently filming season four of The Crown but have not commissioned any further seasons as yet," a source told Metro. "Therefore any news on casting remains pure speculation."

The actress is well known for her role as Lady Bagshaw in Downtown Abbey, she even starred in the Downtown Abbey movie which was a massive success earlier this year.

She is also most popular for her role as Dolores Umbridge in Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix in 2007.

And she later reprised the role in Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows Part One in 2010.

Season three of The Crown is on Netflix now.
