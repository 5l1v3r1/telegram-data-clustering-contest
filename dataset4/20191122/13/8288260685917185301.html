Rangers chief breaks silence on Leeds United and Aston Villa transfer target

Aston Villa transfer news | Glen Kamara has been liked with a move to the Premier League with Crystal Palace, Burnley and Brighton and Hove Albion all said to be interested

22 Nov 2019, 13:25 by Richard Cusack

Rangers manager Steven Gerrard has broken his silence on transfer speculation surrounding midfielder Glen Kamara - and says he is willing to offer him an improved contract.

Kamara has been imperious since moving from Dundee in a bargain £50,000 move in January, with the 24-year-old attracting interest from Aston Villa and Leeds United because of his impressive string of performances.

The Finland international is under contract until 2023, but reports have suggested that Gerrard and his side are willing to thrash out fresh terms to secure the former Arsenal trainee's Ibrox future.

Villa were linked with a move for Kamara in October, along with Leeds and Premier League trio Brighton and Hove Albion, Burnley and Crystal Palace.

Kamara has made 20 appearances for Rangers and has started the past two League matches - and Gerrard wants to reward the midfielder as long as he keeps his performance levels high.

"We haven't heard of any interest down south, we've read tons about it though," said Gerrard.

"But I wouldn't be surprised if there was interest in Glen Kamara because he's an outstanding footballer.

"He's been outstanding since he walked in the door. Sometimes I still have to pinch myself for how we got him and what we got him for.

"Glen is doing fine. He got a real strong contract when he came here from Dundee.

"He's in good form and playing well. The message for Glen and the other players is we always reward our players for consistent performances over a long period of time. Glen will be treated no different.

"All he needs to worry about is bringing success back to the club and I'm sure he will be well looked after."
