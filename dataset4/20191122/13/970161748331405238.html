Pregnant Muslim woman violently attacked by stranger who launched at her in cafe

22 Nov 2019, 13:08

A heavily pregnant Muslim woman was violently punched in the head and stomped on by a stranger while sitting in a cafe in Australia.

The woman, 31, had been having coffee with friends in Bay Vista cafe, on Church Street, Parramatta, New South Wales, when a man approached their table around 10.30pm on November 20.

He allegedly screamed ‘Muslims raped my mum’, before lunging across the table and punching her in the head while other customers desperately tried to pull him back.

Security footage shows the woman, who is 38 weeks pregnant, fall to the ground, with the attacker then beginning to stomp on her before he is dragged away.

Stipe Lozina, 43, has been charged with affray and assault occasioning actual bodily harm in connection with the Islamophobic attack.

He has since been remanded into custody and will appear in court on December 5.

The victim, from Sydney, has chosen to remain anonymous, but thanked Australia for their support through a statement shared by a friend.

She wrote that she had experienced racism and ‘occurrences of verbal abuse and hate’ in the past, but ‘never thought that physical abuse of this nature’ would happen to her.

It was also noted that she is a ‘private person’ but felt the need to speak out to address a few issues and ‘raise awareness’.

The woman added: ‘I hope the justice system lives up to its name. I fear for the world our children will grow up in if this is not addressed.

‘I call all Australians, Muslim and non-Muslim, to stand together in protecting innocent people from any racial or religious attacks.’

The victim told The Telegraph through a Muslim community leader, that the attack had left her feeling ‘very fragile’ and in shock.

She was taken to Westmead Hospital for observation and an assessment of her unborn child and released the next day, having suffered a number of bruises and swelling.

NSW Police Inspector Luke Sywenkyj told reporters in Sydney the victim seemed ‘somewhat emotionally and physically traumatised’.

He noted that the woman did not know her attacker, but said he was known to police and had an extensive criminal record.

Magistrate Tim Keady refused Lozina bail, telling him: ‘After making a comment about Muslims, you approached her and punched her to the head 14 times.

‘For bail purposes I will treat that as a very strong case against you.’
