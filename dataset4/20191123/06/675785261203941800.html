How Liverpool blew their title chance five years ago on a trip to Crystal Palace

The Reds led by three before capitulating against the Eagles, handing Man City a crucial boost in the 2013-14 Premier League

23 Nov 2019, 06:30 by Ned Keating

Liverpool look well on course to end their 30-year wait for a Premier League title this season, but return this weekend to the scene of a famous capitulation that led to one of several near-misses.

With just two games to go in the 2013-14 campaign, the Reds and Manchester City found themselves both locked on 80 points in a titanic title tussle.

In their final away match of the campaign, the Anfield club headed to Crystal Palace on May 5 knowing a win would pile the pressure on City, who would host Aston Villa two days later.

Then manager Brendan Rodgers would have pleased at the break at Selhurst Park with Liverpool leading thanks to Joe Allen's 18th-minute opener.

Things got even better for the Reds early in the second half as they scored twice in two minutes through a Damien Delaney own goal and Luis Suarez to move three ahead by the 55th minute.

All looked to be going swimmingly, with Rodgers' men on course to turn up the heat on City in the final week of the title race.

However, it all began to unravel 11 minutes from time when Delaney netted again, this time in the Liverpool goal, to offer Palace hope of a comeback, even if it seemed remarkably slim at the time.

Dwight Gayle then cut the deficit to one in the 81st minute, before devastating everyone associated with the Reds with his second, the equaliser, two minutes from time.

Come the final whistle, there were scenes that have been become infamous of Liverpool players sinking to their knees in despair, perhaps realising they had blown their title chances.

Uruguayan forward Luis Suarez was the most emotional, with skipper Steven Gerrard having to usher a cameraman away as he tried to film the striker crying.

After the game, Rodgers admitted the title was City's to lose and so it proved, with the Etihad Stadium outfit beating Villa later that week before seeing off West Ham on the final day to lift the trophy.

For Liverpool, though, it was another case of so near, and yet so far as they sought to end their lengthy wait for another league crown.

Suarez would go on to depart that summer and the Reds could only muster a sixth-place finish the following campaign, with Rodgers eventually being sacked in October 2015.

It took Liverpool until last season to truly mount another title challenge and have followed that up again to lead the way this term - it would be a shame now it it were to unravel again because of a trip to Selhurst Park.
