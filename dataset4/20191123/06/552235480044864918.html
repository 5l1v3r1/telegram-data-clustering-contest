Rangers stopper Filip Helander highlights key Martin Odegaard Scotland threat

The big defender reckons Scotland will have to be on their guard with the talented Norwegian schemer.

23 Nov 2019, 06:00 by Gary Ralston, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/gary-ralston/

Filip Helander has warned Scotland to beware Norwegian wonderkid Martin Odegaard.

The Rangers defender has pinpointed the Real Madrid midfielder, currently on loan at Real Sociedad, as the greatest threat to the Tartan Army’s Euro dream.

Steve Clarke ’s side will face the Norwegians or Serbia away from home if they overcome Israel in the semi-final play-off at Hampden in March.

Swedish star Helander watched the Norwegians up close and personal during the qualification campaign.

Sweden qualified for the finals from Group F with Spain and finished four points ahead of Norway, with Helander starring in their 3-0 victory over the Faroes in midweek.

He said: “Norway are a strong side, a good side. They have a few players who are really good - Odegaard is a bit of a player.

“It will be a tough game if Scotland end up playing them.”

Helander won his 11th cap in the 3-0 victory over the Faroes and set up a goal after Janne Andersson’s side successfully sealed qualification for the finals last Friday with a 2-0 win over Romania.

Helander added: “It was my first game since June. You are always proud to represent your country.

“It was a game after we had already qualified and we changed a lot of players, but it was a nice experience to play.

“We had some celebrations in Romania after the game but the manager had already said he would change a lot of players.

“The ones who didn’t play then knew they could be involved against the Faroes so of course you had to handle it wisely.

“I’ve always wanted to play at a major finals so hopefully I can increase my chances of playing for Sweden over the next six months at Rangers .

“As long as the team is playing well then you look better. Your team-mates feel good and if we can continue as well in Europe then I will have a good chance of showing what I am capable of doing.

“Hopefully I can show consistency in my game. You don’t want to play well and then have a bad game after three or four good matches so there is more to come not just from me but from everyone in the dressing room.”
