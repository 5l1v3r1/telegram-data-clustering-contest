The latest urgent product recalls for Waitrose, Cadbury, Asda, Lidl and more

Dozens of products are being recalled

23 Nov 2019, 06:00 by Briana Millett

Every month the Food Standards Agency (FSA) issues product recalls - and this month has been no different.

So far in November 2019 dozens of products have been recalled, with reasons ranging from allergy fears to the presence of salmonella or glass.

In the article below we've rounded up all of the major product recalls issued by the FSA since November 1.

If you have purchased these products, you are advised not to eat them but to return them for a full refund.

The brands include Cadbury, while many of the items are sold at supermarkets including Asda, Lidl, Waitrose and more.

Here's the full list.

Waitrose soup

Waitrose is recalling its Carrot & Coriander Soup because of undeclared allergens.

The vegetable soup contains barley and celery, two ingredients which are not included on the other product’s label.

Product details:

  • Waitrose Carrot & Coriander Soup (600g). Use by: November 27, 2019.

A spokesperson for Waitrose & Partners said: "We are recalling our carrot & coriander soup 600g, use by date 27 November.

"Some of the pots have been filled with the wrong soup (vegetable soup), which contains gluten (barley) and celery that are not declared on the label.

"Any customers with an allergy to gluten or celery should not eat the soup.

"Any customer that has bought the soup please return it to us for a refund. We're very sorry to our customers for this error."

No other Waitrose products are known to be affected.

Spelt & Fruit Cake

Welsh Cottage Cakes is recalling its Spelt & Fruit Cake because it contains wheat which is not mentioned on the label. This means the product is a possible health risk for anyone with an allergy or intolerance to wheat or gluten.

Product details:

  • Spelt & Fruit Cake (550g and 360g). Batch codes: 190920, 191004, 191017, 191025, 191108. Use by: 23 November 2019, 7 December 2019, 20 December 2019, 28 December 2019, 11 January 2020.

No other Welsh Cottage Cakes products are known to be affected.

Cadbury

A Cadbury product is this week being recalled by its parent company Mondelez UK over safety concerns.

The issue concerns some packs of Dairy Milk Little Robins (93g) which contain undeclared almonds and are therefore a possible health risk for anyone with an allergy to nuts.

The FSA have revealed the labelling error is due to some of the product having been incorrectly packed with Cadbury Dairy Milk Little Robins Daim Chocolate.

The affected items have the batch codes of CIS3092812 and CIS3092822, and have a best before date of March 31, 2020.

Little Robins products are currently sold at stores including Tesco, Sainsbury's and Asda.

Waitrose desserts

Pots & Co. is recalling part of its range of products, which are currently stocked at Waitrose & Partners.

The manufacturer recently explained that four of its pot varieties may contain pieces of glass, and are therefore unsafe for consumption.

Colombian Chocolate Pot (65g) which has the identifying barcode of 5060253701920.

Mango & Passionfruit Pot (65g) which has the identifying barcode of 5060253701906.

Mango & Passionfruit Pot (6x65g) which has the identifying barcode of 5060253702224.

Summer Berry Pot (65g) which has the identifying barcode of 5060253701913

The affected products are those with dates codes up to and including November 23, 2019.

Brioche Hot Dog Buns

La Boulangere is recalling its Brioche Hot-Dog Buns because they contain milk and wheat (gluten) which are not mentioned on the label in English. This means the product is a possible health risk for anyone with an allergy or intolerance to milk or milk constituents or an allergy or intolerance to wheat or gluten.

Product details:

  • La Boulangere Brioche Hot-Dog buns (270g). Best before November 8, 23 and 30.

No other La Boulangere products are known to be affected.

Harvey Nichols chocolates

Harvey Nichols is recalling its own brand Dark Chocolate Gingers because the product contains pistachio (nuts) which is not mentioned on the label. The product has been incorrectly packed with Dark Chocolate Pistachios.

Product details:

  • HARVEY NICHOLS Dark Chocolate Gingers (125g). Batch code: 190916. Best before: August 30, 2020

No other Harvey Nichols products are known to be affected.

If you have bought the above product and have an allergy to nuts, do not eat it. Instead return it to the store from where it was bought for a full refund.

Houmous Selection - Aldi, Asda, Sainsbury's, Lidl and more

Dip maker Zorba Delicacies issued an alert as a "precaution" after an ingredient it uses was found to have the possible presence of salmonella.

Shoppers are being urged to return the food items for a refund following a previous warning from the FSA.

It is feared around 83 products could be contaminated.

Zorba Delicacies Limited supplies hummus to several supermarkets including Aldi, Asda, Lidl, Morrisons, and Sainsbury's, as well as Spar, Safeway, Iceland and John Lewis.
