Ludwig Francillette reveals first Newcastle United goal rekindled his days as junior striker

The Magpies may have found a bargain in Ludwig Francillette after he puts in another impressive show for Ben Dawson's second string

23 Nov 2019, 06:00 by Lee Ryder

Newcastle United's big French defender Ludwig Francillette has spoken of his delight at grabbing his first goal in Toon colours - and hopes it can be the first of many.

The 6ft 4in centre-back has impressed in the club's Under-23 side since making a summer move to St James' Park and is starting to look the part for the Magpies.

Francillette's bullet header after 13 minutes against Benfica helped Newcastle earn a draw in their first Premier League International Cup clash of the season at Whitley Park.

Speaking to Chronicle Live, Francillette said: "It was brilliant for me to get my first goal for Newcastle.

"It was incredible for me to see it hit the back of the net.

"I just got a touch on it and looked and the ball had flew into the top corner, so it was great for me.

"I have been working on in training so it was great to see it come off."

The imposing centre-back has looked a real threat in opposition boxes in the last few weeks, when asked if playing as a defender is his only position, the defender said: "Yes I scored a few in France, I'm not sure how many, but not too many like this.

"When I was a kid I played as a striker so I scored a few then!

"The main thing is it was a good performance for the team.

"We're a little bit sad about the draw but we need to keep going.

"We must now see this out in the last two group games."

Francillette's performance came in front of newly appointed first team coach Steve Harper, who will now push reports into the path of head coach Steve Bruce.

The youngster said: "I didn't know if he was watching.

"But I hope he enjoyed the game.

"It is a really positive sign (that he was watching on)

"One day we all dream of playing for the first team, that is the dream for us all."

The goal was further evidence that Francillette is not only finding his feet on Tyneside but also putting himself in contention for first team action.

He said: "I feel at home now.

"My team-mates are helping me and I have made a lot of friends.

"It is all good.

"I don't do English lessons but I am picking it up."
