Jack Ross to take the Aiden McGeady and Lewis Morgan approach to Scott Allan at Hibs

There are some players that need the freedom to go and express themselves and Ross believes Allan falls in to that bracket

23 Nov 2019, 06:00 by Ross Pilcher, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/ross-pilcher/

Jack Ross is well placed to get the best out of Hibs talisman Scott Allan because he’s done it before with his “match winners” at other clubs.

Allan returned to Easter Road for a third spell in the summer after signing a pre-contract agreement back in January, agreed while Neil Lennon was still in charge.

He subsequently left and returned to Celtic – alongside Allan – but there was no question of the midfielder reversing his decision to return to Leith, and he met with Paul Heckingbottom before officially becoming a Hibs player again.

The initial signs were good as Allan netted a late winner on the opening day of the season against St Mirren, suggesting he was set to be a key man for the Hibees once again.

Despite coming in a 6-1 defeat to Rangers and Ibrox and the reaction to it being the subject of some mickey taking, his through pass for Daryl Horgan’s consolation in that game was indeed impressive.

There have been bad days at the office as well though, although not always entirely Allan’s doing.

His now infamous substitution in a home draw against St Johnstone came after an ineffective display, although the fact that Heckingbottom had been playing him on the right of midfield was a contributing factor.

When he was withdrawn in favour of Daryl Horgan with Hibs 2-1 up, the boos rang down from the Easter Road stands.

A last minute Saints equaliser before Heckingbottom responded to questions over his decision to replace Allan with: “I didn’t know there was a rule against taking him off” did not improve the situation.

Playing Allan in his favoured position and giving him the freedom to go and play can result in goals like we saw in Hibs’ last outing, also against St Johnstone.

Allan picked up a loose pass on half way and drove at the St Johnstone defence before finding the far corner to put Hibs 3-0 up in Perth.

It’s those sort of big moments that Ross will endeavour to get from Allan on a regular basis, which means setting up in a way that allows him to flourish.

To do so, he knows that some leeway must be given to those players in the team that can make a difference, in order for them to produce the game changing moments.

"I've had examples of that with Lewis (Morgan) at St Mirren, Aiden (McGeady) at Sunderland,” Ross explained. “Last season he was terrific for me.

“Ideally I find a way to get the best from them, because the players that can make the difference in games and match-winners are difficult to come by.

“Scotty's definitely one of those. I've been really impressed by him this week. Not just on the pitch, but as a young man and speaking to him as well.

“There's a maturity about him and hopefully I can get the best from him on a consistent basis, because he's absolutely got the quality. Not just on the ball, but his all round application and how he is.

“If I can get the best from him then he'll be a major asset for us.

“There are various approaches. I've been fortunate or unfortunate depending on your point of view, with having a squad that's that type in it.

“You balance out everything else accordingly to make sure you get the best from them.

“The people at St Mirren would tell you Lewis was allowed to play in a certain way and Aiden last year, because it’s difficult to find that ability to win games.

“Its about me figuring out as quickly as possible how to get the best from Scotty and others. I'm excited."

Ross is also looking forward to working with Ryan Porteous. The young centre back was handed first senior call-up to the Scotland squad over the last week, although he didn’t feature in the wins over Cyrpus and Kazakhstan.

That meant a delayed return to training this week, but the pair were able to catch up on Thursday.

Ross hopes that Porteous can continue to play well enough to remain in contention for Scotland, and revealed that he tried to secure his services while at St Mirren.

“I think it's fantastic recognition for him and the club, particularly the young players who have progressed through,” he said of the call up.

“I only met him for the first time on Thursday because of that involvement.

“I actually tried to bring Ryan to St Mirren on loan a couple of years ago so he's somebody I've been aware of for a long time.

“Undoubtedly he'll come back from that involvement feeling good about himself.

“So the challenge for him is to make sure he continues that performance level and the next time Steve [Clarke] names a squad, he finds himself back in it."
