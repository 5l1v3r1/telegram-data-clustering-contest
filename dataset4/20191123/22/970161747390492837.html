Elton John forced Robbie Williams into rehab after he arrived drunk at his home

23 Nov 2019, 22:52

Robbie Williams revealed Elton John forced him into rehab after he turned up at the Rocketman’s home covered in wine stains.

The Angels hitmaker appeared on The Jonathan Ross Show where he celebrated being sober for 19 years.

And while discussing his past addiction, the 45-year-old confessed the Honky Cat singer had a hand in getting him to rehab.

Chatting with the host, Robbie said: ‘He really tried to help. In the early days when I was first getting sober, there was a week until I had to go to rehab and I had two vocals to do on my first album, one of them was Angels and one of them was Let Me Entertain You.

‘I was going to do these vocals then go to Rehab. Elton invited me to his house to listen to the work that I’d done so far.

‘That morning I got in a black cab and I had this game that I used to play where I’d stop at every pub I saw and have half a pint.

‘I’d had ten half pints before I’d got to the studio. I ended up under the mixing desk, had a bit of red wine and then I knew I had to get to Elton’s for three o’clock.’

The rocker made it to Elton’s house looking worse for wear with wine stains down his shirt.

And when opening the door, the now 72-year-old told the star that he had to get to rehab, with Robbie agreeing with the icon, saying: ‘I’ve got to go to Rehab now’.

He confessed this exchange went on for a while at the door before Elont and his husband David Furnish put him up for a night at their home in Windsor.

‘I wake up but I haven’t opened my eyes and there’s a knock on my shoulder and I look and there’s… Elton and David Furnish and a doctor and a psychiatrist and they are here to take me away.’

Praising the You Song hitmaker, Robbie continued: ‘Elton is the most loving, generous man you could imagine and he has helped so many people over the years.’

Elton has also battled addiction, and recently celebrated 29-years of being sober.

At the time he confessed: ’29 years ago today, I was a broken man. I finally summoned up the courage to say three words that would change my life: “I need help”.

‘Thank-you to all the selfless people who have helped me on my journey through sobriety. I am eternally grateful.’
