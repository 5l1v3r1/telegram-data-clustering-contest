Ballarat police raid Mount Pleasant home for drugs

23 Nov 2019, 22:00 by Erin Williams

A woman who was arrested after a traffickable quantity of drugs was allegedly found at her Mount Pleasant home on Tuesday remains in custody.

Samantha Broes was refused bail at the Ballarat Magistrates Court on Thursday because she failed to show exceptional circumstances.

Police nominal informant Senior Constable Michelle Cosgrave said Broes was already on bail, charged with trafficking drugs in June, when she was arrested on Tuesday.

She said police searched her home and uncovered 326 grams of cannabis, 30 grams of cocaine, 20 grams of ecstasy, a large amount of capsules, $570 cash and three mobile phones.

Tristan Merakis, 32, who was at Broes' house during the search warrant, was arrested and charged but was granted bail after he showed compelling reasons.

Senior Constable Cosgrave said police considered Broes an unacceptable risk of re-offending while on bail.

She said police were concerned about her children, after a large quantity of cannabis was located in Broes' washing machine.

The court was told Broes refused to be interviewed by police and told them she "uses ice to get by".

Police did not consider Merakis, of Ballarat East, an unacceptable risk of re-offending while he was willing to engage in the Court Integrated Services Program.

He was bailed with conditions, while Broes was remanded in custody. The pair will reappear at the Ballarat Magistrates Court for a mention on December 16.

They have each been charged with eight trafficking and possessing drug offences.
