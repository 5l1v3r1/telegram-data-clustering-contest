Young M.A Explains How 50 Cent Inspired Her To Be A Rapper

23 Nov 2019, 22:01 by Caitlin Kelley

Young M.A just became the first LGBTQ+ rapper to grace the cover of Out magazine, where the 27-year-old spoke about how 50 Cent shaped her aspirations.

The NYC rapper already started writing rhymes at the age of 10, but it was 2003’s Get Rich or Die Tryin' that inspired her to step into the spotlight. “I was like, ‘Who is this? He is fire. And he from New York?’” she told Out’s Culture and Ent. Director Tre’vell Anderson. “That’s what made me want to be a star. That’s what made me be like, ‘Yo, I want to be a rapper. I don’t want to just write.’”

Back in 2016, 50 Cent co-signed the Brooklyn native, calling her “the hottest shit out right now.” He ended up remixing her viral hit “OOOUUU” later that year.
