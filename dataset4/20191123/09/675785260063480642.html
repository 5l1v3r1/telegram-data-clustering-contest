What TV channel is Callum Smith vs John Ryder on? TV and live stream details

Smith and Ryder clash in an all-British clash for the former's world super-middleweight title in Liverpool

23 Nov 2019, 09:00 by Martin Domin, https://www.facebook.com/mirrorfighting

John Ryder faces a huge challenge tonight when he challenges for Callum Smith's super-middleweight world title.

Smith defends his belt for the second time since he won it by beating George Groves in the final of the World Boxing Super Series in Saudi Arabia last year.

He comfortably defeated Hassan N'Dam on the undercard of Anthony Joshua's shock defeat by Andy Ruiz Jr in June but has struggled to secure the super-fights he craves.

Ryder meanwhile comes into tonight's fight as a dark horse having battled from defeats by Billy Joe Saunders, Rocky Fielding, Jack Arnfield and Nick Blackwell to secure a mandatory shot at Smith.

Here is everything you need to know about tonight's fights...

What TV channel is it on?

Latest news

Callum Smith believes he will get the credit he deserves when he ends up having a few wars.

The Liverpool super-middleweight makes the second defence of his WBA title against fellow Brit John Ryder at the M&S Bank Arena on Merseyside.

Smith, 29, is eyeing up a huge 2020 which could include a big fight at Anfield, the home of his beloved Liverpool, in May.

He already reached the top of the 12st division when he dethroned George Groves just over a year ago in the final of the World Boxing Super Series.

But the Scouse scrapper has not been given huge plaudits despite his success in the ring.

He did take time out following the win over Groves when his daughter Alba was born which maybe took some of the momentum away.

But he also feels that sometimes it has been too easy for him on his big nights which left his best wins overlooked.

“Coming through I remember boxed Nikola Sjekloca, he had lost twice on points to world champions Sakio Bika and Arthur Abraham,” ‘Mundo’ said.

“If I beat him in a war people would have said, ‘Fair play for fighting him so early’. Because I whitewashed him on points it was like, ‘Sjekloca wasn’t very good’.

“It was the same when I beat Rocky Fielding. I was 17-0, he was 21-0 and it was a big local derby and I won in a round.

“If I had a war and scraped by I would have gotten more credit and exposure.

“But I’ve got my own goals, my own pressure, my own targets.

“It’s not really frustrating. I got into boxing for myself, not to get the approval of everyone.

“I don’t do anything for people not to like me, but you will still get people who won’t.”
