Police officer describes being confronted by Wetherspoon's burglar clutching a sledgehammer

'It was not a standard night'

23 Nov 2019, 09:32 by Matt Jarram

A police officer has described the moment he chased a masked man brandishing a sledgehammer after a gang of fruit machine burglars broke into a Wetherspoon's pub in Nottingham.

PC David Grassam, who chased the balacava-clad criminal after he smashed up a police car with his weapon, said it wasn't your "standard night" working for Nottinghamshire Police.

PC Grassam and his team were called out to the Samuel Hall pub in Mansfield Road, Sherwood, on February 19 this year following reports a gang had broken into the venue at around 4am.

Burglars Keith Collison and brothers Mark and Lee Carter had just taken more than Â£1,000 from a gaming machine when police arrived.

The gang jumped into an Astra driven by Collison and sped off in the direction of Edwards Lane.

But one of the criminals - Carter - didn't like the fact their plan had been foiled.

"The car came to a stop," PC Grassam explained.

"One of them got out, he had a balaclava and a sledgehammer and he hit the windscreen of the police car. The sledgehammer shattered it. He then hit the passenger side window."

The 29-year-old officer, who only started at Nottinghamshire Police in June 2018, was parked behind the smashed police car.

His team attempted to drive in front of the suspects' car to stop them driving away. The driver rammed their car but crashed into some traffic lights.

Collison, 51, of Beck Crescent, Mansfield, and Lee Carter, 33, of Columbia Street, Huthwaite, were arrested.

But Mark Carter, 37, also of Columbia Street, Huthwaite, tried to make a run for it and raised the sledgehammer towards a pursuing officer.

PC Grassam added: "I got out of the car and I was running towards the man with the sledgehammer.

"At about 10 to 15 metres he turned around to face me and lifted the sledgehammer to swing it.

"I got my baton out but my colleague came to my right-hand side and tasered the man.

"We was wearing a lot of layers of clothing so it was not working and would not make contact with his skin.

"He went to run across the road and into someone's front garden but just as he was about to, he was tasered again and it had some effect.

"He tripped and fell into a heap - and I jumped over the gate and applied the handcuffs.

"It was not a standard night."

Mark Carter received a total of four years in prison for pleading guilty to breaching a suspended prison sentence, burglary, threatening with a sledgehammer, criminal damage and possessing a prohibited weapon - a stun gun at his home.

Lee Carter received a community order for two years and a nine-month drug rehabilitation requirement after he pleaded guilty to burglary.

Collison received three years and two months in prison and a driving ban for six years and two months. He pleaded guilty to burglary, dangerous driving, driving whilst disqualified and having no insurance.

PC Grassam was awarded for his bravery alongside PC Richard Elliott, PC Anna Parkin, PC Daniel Mottishaw, PC James Gill and PC Matthew Leask at the Nottinghamshire Police Awards.

They were held on November 21 at the Albert Hall in the city centre.

PC Grassam added: "When I look back it is tunnel vision. All I can see is the guy with the sledgehammer rather than the stuff around me - that is the permanent vision in my memory.

"These sorts of jobs are few and far between and it is nice we have got acknowledged for it."
