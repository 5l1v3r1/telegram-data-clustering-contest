Bath Christmas Market will close 10 days before Christmas Day

A word of warning for last-minute shoppers...

23 Nov 2019, 09:00 by Emma Grimshaw

Bath Christmas Market, with its beautifully-decorated chalets and head-turning entertainment, is always a hugely popular event.

The attraction offers the perfect opportunity to pick up personal and unusual gifts for friends and family while seeing the odd Ice Queen walking around on stilits.

There's also a chance to get in the festive mood by purchasing mulled wine or tucking into a freshly-made mince pie.

Set out across the picture-perfect city, its estimated 400,000 people visited the market last year and the market is likely to be just as busy when it launches on November 28.

But if you are hoping to visit the sparkling event, make sure you don't leave it too long.

The market is only running for 18 days, finishing on December 15 – 10 days before Christmas Day.

Kathryn Davis, Interim CEO, Visit Bath, said: “Bath Christmas Market runs for 18 days from next Thursday (November 28) to Sunday 15 December.

"151 decorated chalets will line the streets around The Abbey and The Roman Baths, as well as making a return to Milsom Street which was a huge success last year.

"Around 400,000 people visited the Market in 2018 with approximately 27 per cent of visitors staying overnight.

"Over 90 per cent of stall holders are from Bath or the surrounding area, and this year Bath Christmas Market welcomes two new producers from Bristol; Studio Taro who will be selling their wildlife illustrated gifts and homewares, and Jekka’s who will be showcasing their locally cultivated collection of herbs, seeds and herbal infusions.

"We are also delighted to have two charity chalets this year supporting 36 local charities."
