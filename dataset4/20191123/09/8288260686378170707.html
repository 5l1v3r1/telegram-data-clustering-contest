Two people cut free from car following early hours smash in Bilston

The fire service fully removed the roof of a car in order to rescue to people who were trapped

23 Nov 2019, 09:40 by Charlotte Regen, https://www.birminghammail.co.uk/authors/charlotte-regen/

Two people have been left hospitalised after they were cut free from a car following an early morning smash.

Emergency services raced to the scene on Mount Pleasant, Bilston, at around 4.33am, this morning (Saturday, November 23).

The fire service had to fully remove the roof of a car in order to rescue the trapped casualties who were trapped by their injuries.

It is unclear what injuries they have sustained from the crash.

The fire service took to Twitter to warn others about the incident. The tweet said: " @WestMidsFire are in attendance at a two car road traffic collision in Bilston. Two people trapped by injuries. Police, ambulance and fire service in attendance."

A spokeswoman for West Midlands Fire Service told Black Country Live it involved two cars and two people were taken to hospital following a full roof removal. The service then made the cars safe.
