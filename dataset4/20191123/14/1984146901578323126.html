Person brought to safety after emergency service response on the Redheugh Bridge

The southbound carriageway was closed for more than an hour on Saturday afternoon

23 Nov 2019, 14:29 by Katie Collings

A "distressed" person was brought to safety on the Redheugh Bridge by emergency services.

Police were called to the scene shortly before 1pm on Saturday afternoon to reports of a person in distress.

The southbound carriageway was closed for more than one hour while they dealt with the incident and buses and traffic were diverted.

Traffic was heavy around Scotswood Road as drivers had to divert away from the bridge.

The bridge fully reopened shortly after 2pm.

A spokesman for Northumbria Police said: "We were dealing with a person in distress and our response was to ensure their safety and the safety of the public.

"We have given the person the help and support required."

If you are in distress, you can call the Samaritans free at any time, from any phone, on 116 123.
