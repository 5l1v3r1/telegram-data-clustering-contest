European roundup: Barcelona edge to win, Bayern make up ground

23 Nov 2019, 14:20 by Reuters

• Arturo Vidal caps La Liga champions’ comeback at Leganés
• Tight at top of Bundesliga after shock Mönchengladbach loss

Barcelona came from behind to snatch a 2-1 victory at La Liga’s basement club Leganés as the substitute Arturo Vidal struck a scrappy late winner.

It was another unconvincing display away from home from the champions and Leganés had taken the lead in their coach Javier Aguirre’s first home game in charge thanks to a superb strike into the top corner from Youssef En-Nesyri in the 12th minute.

Leganés then spurned two chances to extend their advantage before the break before Barcelona levelled in the 52nd minute when Luis Suárez glanced in Lionel Messi’s free-kick.

Barcelona won the game thanks to a fortunate goal in the 79th minute from the Chile midfielder Vidal, who was in a clear offside position when he knocked in from close range but a VAR review allowed the goal to stand as the ball had bounced off Leganés’ Rubén Pérez.

In Germany Bayern Munich made up some vital ground on Borussia Mönchengladbach to bring themselves within a point of the Bundesliga leaders. Bayern cruised to a 4-0 win over Düsseldorf, Philippe Coutinho and Serge Gnabry among the goals, while Mönchengladbach’s shock 2-0 defeat at Union Berlin leaves one point separating the top three after RB Leipzig’s 3-1 win over FC Koln took them to second.

In Italy Juventus stretched their lead at the top of Serie A to four points for at least a few hours with a 3-1 win at Atalanta, Gonzalo Higuaín’s double and a Paulo Dybala strike all coming in the final 20 minutes to complete a late turnaround. AC Milan and Napoli played out a 1-1 draw, while Inter have the chance to reduce Juventus’ lead to a point at Torino on Saturday evening.
