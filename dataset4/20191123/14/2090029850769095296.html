Thunder kills 17 cows on a farmland in Osun

23 Nov 2019, 14:48 by Sanya Agunbiade

About 17 cows were struck dead thunder in Iba, a town in Ifelodun Local Government Council Area of Osun State.

According to reports, the incident happened when some Fulani herdsmen in the community entered into a farm area with their herd.

However in swift reaction to avoid suspicion, the monarch of the town, Oba Adekunle Oyedeji said he, with the cooperation of the Fulani community in the town are on top of the situation, urging owners to burn the cows to avoid epidemic. He urged calm among indigenes and residents of the town, Vanguard reports.

A statement signed by Prince Gbolagunte Bamigbola said the Fulani community had been living harmoniously with other people in the town.

It reads,

“The people of Iba, have over the years been living harmoniously with Fulani natives living in our domain. The death of the cows belonging to the Fulani cattle rearers living among us was a natural occurrence ocassioned by a thunder that struck in the town.

The cows were killed by thunder at Apala farm in the territory of Iba, headquarters of Ifelodun Area Office and the community with the collaboration of the leadership of the fulani community are on top of the situation.

The Eburu in Council has directed the owners of the cows to burn them to avoid epidemic in the town.

The community is in peace and the people live peacefully with all non indigenes including the Fulanis”.
