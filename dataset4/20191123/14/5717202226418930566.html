West Ham fans critical of Felipe Anderson performance against Tottenham

23 Nov 2019, 14:45 by Dom Toner

Felipe Anderson played just 45 minutes in the defeat to Tottenham on Saturday afternoon – and some West Ham fans on Twitter teared into their player for his performance.

The Hammers were beaten 3-2 at home by Spurs and it means they stay 16th in the Premier League table.

Manuel Pellegrini’s side were second-best in the contest against Spurs, albeit they mounted a late comeback to show some spirit and fight.

Jose Mourinho’s new-look Spurs took a two-goal lead by half-time, as Heung-Min Son and Lucas Moura both got on the scoresheet.

Harry Kane then wrapped up three points early in the second-half with his goal and it lifted his team up the table to sixth.

Michail Antonio did score in the 73rd minute with a clever finish, while Angelo Ogbonna netted in stoppage time which turned out to be the last act of the game.

A selection of West Ham supporters on social media vented their frustration towards Anderson for his individual display.

The 26-year-old once again started in the Hammers XI and was withdrawn at half-time after failing to make very little impact going forward.

The left-winger has still failed to score a single goal this campaign and the Brazilian looks a long way off the standard he set last year.
