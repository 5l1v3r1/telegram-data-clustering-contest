Unsung heroes and heroines honoured at the Royal Sutton Coldfield Town Council Community Awards 2019

"The annual Community Awards are about celebrating, acknowledging and rewarding those who carry out the amazing community and voluntary work that helps make Sutton Coldfield such a wonderful place to live," the Mayor of Sutton Coldfield, Cllr David Allan said

23 Nov 2019, 14:52 by Nick Horner

Heroes and heroines across Sutton Coldfield have been honoured in an annual ceremony highlighting the town’s unsung volunteers.

The third Royal Sutton Coldfield Town Council Community Awards recognised and rewarded the dedicated and hardworking individuals and groups that put themselves to help others at a special ceremony held at the town hall on Thursday (November 21).

More than 100 nominations were made for five awards - Good Citizen/Neighbour of the Year, Young Person of the Year, Volunteer of the Year, Community Event of the Year, Community Group of the Year, with three finalists shortlisted and invited to attend the event.

Winner of Good Citizen/Neighbour of the Year is Lynn Jennings who spends much of her personal time volunteering with Ark Community Church making a positive difference to the lives of people in Mere Green. Runners up were Clare McVerry a school PTA and neighbourhood watch member and Reg Banks, who promotes Neighbourhood Watch, Street Watch and Active Citizens in Sutton.

The Young Person of the Year was Dawn Peters for her role as a young leader of the 6th Sutton Coldfield Scouts.

Runners-up were Cormac Wade who started the brand-new Refill Local Scheme, which is designed to help local people find water on the go and prevent plastic pollution in Sutton Coldfield. And Missy Gillespie, who has continuously made outstanding efforts to benefit the lives of students at Bishop Walsh School, championing a scheme and training a small group of ‘Young Wellbeing Leads’.

Winner of the Volunteer of the Year award was Jackie Benzecry who set up the Breast Friends Charity in 1995, to support those suffering with breast cancer and over the last 25 years has raised £1.6million. After receiving her award, Jackie said: “When I was taking early retirement, I thought I might be a library lady.

“Next thing I knew I had breast cancer and I was in hospital. The last 25 years have been so significant to me. I can’t explain how wonderful it is knowing how much I am helping other people.

“The reason I can do what I do is because of the support around me. All of the ladies in Breast Friends have such a very special bond, because everyone is coming from the same direction and we’re all breast cancer survivors. I’m not sorry for the way my life went, I have got so much out of it since. Being touched by cancer changes your whole perspective and that’s not always a bad thing.

“I can’t believe I am the winner of this award! It was a very emotional evening for me and a really lovely memory.”

Runners-up were Roger Bunting, a volunteer at Our Place and Mary Sutton, a volunteer receptionist at Relate Sutton Coldfield.

The Community Group of the Year winner was CPR Counts, set up by Malcolm Robinson, whose life was saved by a passing off-duty nurse, after he suffered a cardiac arrest. The small and dedicated team has given free two-hour life-saving training to over 1,000 residents in Sutton Coldfield.

Malcolm, who collected the award on behalf of CPR Counts, said:  “My life has completely changed since I had my cardiac arrest. Although I've officially retired, CPR Counts is now a full-time job but I feel so lucky to be alive and to be able to give something back to the community.

“We are feeling highly honoured and proud to receive this award would like to thank all the many people who've helped us along the way to keep our 'show on the road'.

“We are very grateful also to Sutton Coldfield Charitable Trust in Walmley that provides us with sponsorship for room rental and marketing.

Runners-up were Four Oaks Milk Bar, a breast-feeding support group but also a social group for parents to meet other parents, make friends and reduce the risk of post-natal depression. And Friends of Sutton Park Association for its work in looking after Sutton Park and in particular the conservation works and guided walks they carry out on a voluntary basis.

Winner of the Community Event of Year was Carers United for its annual Carers Rights Day event at the United Reformed Church. Designed just for carers, the event offers advice and support and is an opportunity for pampering, lunch and entertainment.

Runners-up were Sutton Coldfield Senior Showcase, which is a free event designed to signpost activities/services for those of post retirement age. And the Sleep Easy event, organised by YMCA Sutton Coldfield, which involved 125 residents and community groups to sleep outdoors to raise funds and awareness to prevent homelessness for young people.

As part of the 2019 Community Awards ceremony, Yaz Benson, founder and volunteer of Footsteps Counselling and Support Services, spoke about the charity’s very special work with local residents. The Mayor’s chosen charity for this year, Footsteps is based in Mere Green and is a free one-to-one counselling service.

Guests of the ceremony were treated upon arrival to the musical talents of students Alfie Bennett (piano) Jack Leatherbarrow (piano), Rebekah Hall (flute) and Szymon Arciszewski (clarinet), all from John Wilmott School.

Mayor of the Royal Town of Sutton Coldfield Councillor David Allan, who hosted the evening, said: “Sutton Coldfield has a long, proud and strong tradition of volunteers supporting every interest and all ages through social activities and support.

"The annual Community Awards are about celebrating, acknowledging and rewarding those who carry out the amazing community and voluntary work that helps make Sutton Coldfield such a wonderful place to live.

"It was a privilege to be able thank some of the hundreds of volunteers across our royal town who so generously give their time, skill and enthusiasm to help others.”

Winners of each category received a glass trophy and certificate.
