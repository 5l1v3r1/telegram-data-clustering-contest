Spurs beat West Ham 3-2 in Mourinho's first game

23 Nov 2019, 14:40 by James Cox

Jose Mourinho's Tottenham reign got off to a winning start as Spurs beat West Ham 3-2 at the London Stadium.

The London Derby was very one-sided in the first-half as Spurs dominated possession and went 2-0 up through goals from Son Heung-min and Lucas Moura.

Son fired home after a Dele Alli pass to make it 1-0 in the 36th minute.

It was well-deserved and it go better for Mourinho just before the break as Moura doubled the lead.

This time Son turned providers as the Brazilian converted his cross.

The second-half started in the same vein and Harry Kane got in on the act in the 49th minute.

The captain headed in Serge Aurier's cross to kill off the game, or so Spurs thought.

A lovely finish from Michail Antonio gave the hosts a bit of hope with 20 minutes left on the clock.

Angelo Ogbonna scored in the 96th minute but it was too late to do anything except take some of the gloss off Spurs' win.

Mourinho didn't look as happy with his team's performance after that but it's still job done and a first away victory since January.
