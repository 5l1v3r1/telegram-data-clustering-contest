James Blunt ‘heats up grub using his armpits’ instead of the microwave

James Blunt admitted his food tastes 'delicious' when heated up in his pits

23 Nov 2019, 14:52 by Lucy Domachowski

James Blunt has admitted he heats his food up by shoving it under his armpits in a gross confession.

The You’re Beautiful singer revealed he can’t stand using microwaves and that he prefers to use his own body heat to warm up his grub.

He even confess his favourite meal to shove up his shirt: baked beans and sausage.

Blunt, 45, uses an old technique he picked up while serving as a ­reconnaissance officer with the Army.

He was taught to heat up his favourite dish by sticking it in a foil bag and then placing it under his arm.

The singer told the Daily Star : “My favourite ration was baked beans and sausage in a silver foil bag.

“If you couldn’t boil it, you’d stick it under your armpit and get heat from your body. I still like it, it’s delicious.”

James joined the army in 1996 and rose to the rank of captain before leaving in 2002.

The five-time Grammy Award nominee and BRIT Award winner has hits like Goodbye My Lover and 1973 and is currently preparing for a world tour next year on the back of his new album Once Upon a Mind.

The record has already been ridiculed by Good Morning Britain presenter Piers Morgan, who joked he had reported it as a “hate crime.”

It’s been 15 years since fans first fell in love with Blunt via his blockbuster 2004 debut album Back to Bedlam, which featured arguably his most famous song, You’re Beautiful.

“I’m back where I started,” Blunt said of his new album,” the songwriter said.

“Writing songs for me and the people around me. And I’m free again.”

James revealed: “This album isn’t for an audience. It’s for my family and the people I need behind me when I go on tour.

“So it’s really just been about exposing myself and that’s why it’s been kind of magical for me to do.”

James’ socialite wife Sofia Wellesley has banned him from using items such as plastic and expensive lighting during the arena tour in a bid to save the planet.
