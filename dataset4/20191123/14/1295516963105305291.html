The Batman: Danny DeVito Reacts to Colin Farrell Becoming The Penguin

23 Nov 2019, 14:11 by John Ridlehoover

Danny DeVito, who famously played The Penguin/Oswald Cobblepot in Tim Burton's Batman Returns, recently shared his opinion on his Dumbo co-star, Colin Farrell, being cast as the famous Gotham rogue in Matt Reeves' The Batman. 

"Colin’s a great actor... He’s a good friend of mine," DeVito told ScreenRant while promoting Jumanji: The Next Level. "I’ve known him for many years and I think he’s going to do a great job as the Penguin. It’s going to be really interesting to see his take on it."

Batman Returns originally premiered on June 19, 1992 and starred Michael Keaton as Batman and Michelle Pfeiffer as Catwoman. The Penguin has also been portrayed by Burgess Meredith in 1966's Batman film and Robin Lord Taylor in Fox's Gotham television series.

There have been several recent casting announcements for The Batman, including one about The Big Lebowski's John Turturro playing Carmine Falcone. Zoe Kravitz, Paul Dano and Andy Serkis are currently all set to appear in the film.

DeVito will star in Jumanji: The Next Level alongside Danny Glover, Dwayne Johnson, Jack Black,Kevin Hart, Karen Gillan and more. The film is set to release on December 13, 2019.

Starring Robert Pattinson, The Batman is directed by Matt Reeves, who will also produce alongside Dylan Clark. While casting is still underway, the film is scheduled to be released on June 25, 2021.
