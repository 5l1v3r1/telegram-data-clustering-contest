Star City fight: Kids waiting to see Frozen 2 flee as armed gangs brawl in cinema

Star City Cinema in Birmingham was plunged into lock down when a group of youth burst into the cinema and started brawling - causing young children dressed as Frozen 2 characters to burst into tearful screams

23 Nov 2019, 20:46 by Milo Boyd, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/milo-boyd/

Terrified film fans have described the moment a major brawl broke-out inside a cinema sending screaming children running into the street.

Star City in Birmingham was locked down this evening after dozens of teenagers ran into the foyer.

According to witness reports they began punching a lone female who was on the floor, before police officers burst into the cinema.

Alarmed punters told West Midlands Police that some of the youths were armed with machetes.

The presence of law enforcement officers caused the brawling teens to flee into the screens in a bid to escape.

Choleigh McGuire was queueing up to get into a screening of Frozen 2 with her 11-year-old sister when the youths charged through the doors.

"This group of girls started beating up this one girl," the 25-year-old said.

"Someone separated it at first but then the other girl's friend started punching her.

"Security came and broke it up then loads of police came."

Choleigh said that the first girl beaten by the group was punched about five times before others intervened.

Her sister was left in a state of shock when police officers armed with Tasers brandished one of the shocking devices towards her face.

She added: "They pointed the Taser at us and said we had to get out.

"They threatened to shoot these innocent boys near us. She just kept waiving it around."

Ashmit Sinha was also waiting to get into a screening when the fight broke out, causing dozens of the young punters dressed as Frozen 2 characters to break into tears.

"A huge fight breaks out and it was really scary," she said.

"Police had Tasers and there were just a bunch of confused little children, who just wanted to watch a movie, crying."

Many took to Birgminghan Live's Facebook page to share their experiences of the horrifying incident.

One woman wrote: "I really hope everyone is okay especially the children.

"My daughter is five and was horrified, screaming and shaking.

"We got locked in the arcade (while) the police throw everyone out. (I'm) still a bit shaken."

Another woman added: " This is the most horrifying thing I’ve been involved in.

"I’m scared and I’m 45. (There were) little girls dressed in Frozen dresses crying and screaming."

Shortly before 8pm the police confirmed that they had made several arrests and that officers had been attacked in the line of duty.

The force tweeted: "Officers arrived to find a group of more than 100 people, and pockets of fighting broke out as police moved to clear the area.

"A number of police officers have been assaulted, and several arrests have been made.

"A dispersal order has been out in place giving officers the power to move on groups of people and arrest those who fail to leave.

"Officers remain at the scene and are liaising closely with management at the venue. Motorists are urged to avoid the area because of traffic build-up."
