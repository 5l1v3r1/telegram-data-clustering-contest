Stephanie Parze’s dad makes heartbreaking pledge to missing daughter: ‘Daddy is trying everything’

23 Nov 2019, 20:38 by Georgett Roberts and Sara Dorn

The distraught dad of a missing New Jersey woman whose ex-boyfriend killed himself after being named a person of interest in the case made a heartbreaking pledge to his vanished daughter Saturday.

“Stephanie, Daddy is trying everything he can. We are not going to stop till we find you,” Edward Parze told The Post from the Freehold firehouse, where hundreds of volunteers gathered Saturday morning to look for the 25-year-old.

“Just keep fighting. You know that we are all out there looking for you. We are not stopping. We will keep turning over rock and stone ’til we find you.”

Stephanie Parze’s ex, John Ozbilgen, was found dead from an apparent suicide in his parents’ home Friday, authorities said. Ozbilgen was once arrested for assaulting Stephanie.

The devastated dad was empathetic toward Ozbilgen’s family.

“Loss of life is a tragedy, so we feel for them,” Parze said.

About 200 volunteers — many clad in white T-shirts with Stephanie Parze’s face on them — searched a wooded area near the station Saturday morning.

Meanwhile, another search was underway in Tottenville on Staten Island, where authorities say cellphone records led them.

Stephanie Parze disappeared after an Oct. 31 night out with friends and family to visit a psychic in New Brunswick, New Jersey.

Ozbilgen — a former Staten Islander who was arrested for allegedly assaulting Parze in September — sent her menacing text messages the night she disappeared, police said.

Ozbilgen, 29, was found dead of a suicide-by-hanging in his parents Freehold home on Friday, police said.

Edward Parze only met Ozbilgen once or twice, he said Saturday.

“It was like a five-minute hi, bye,” he said. “I would imagine she was just dating him on and off. It was nothing serious or I would have known something about that.”

Authorities have refused to say if Ozbilgen left a suicide note.

“They [law enforcement] are not telling us much of anything,” Parze said.
