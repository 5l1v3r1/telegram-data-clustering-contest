'GOING TO SEE CRAIG'

Dying dad of Craig McClelland will have ashes scattered at same beauty spot as murdered son

23 Nov 2019, 20:15 by Chris Taylor

THE dying dad of murder victim Craig McClelland has revealed his ashes will be scattered at the same beauty spot as his son’s.

Michael McClelland, 60, wants his own remains to be cast at a “beautiful” pond where he said a final goodbye to his beloved lad, 31, earlier this year.

He told of his plans for the farewell in Tyndrum, Stirlingshire, after being given 12 months to live during his battle with brain cancer.

The grandad said: “My faith tells me I am leaving here and going to see Craig.

"I genuinely believe that’s what will happen.

“I’ve decided I am just going to be burned and be scattered in the same place as my son.

"We sprinkled his ashes at the most beautiful pond in July.”

Family man Craig McClelland, 31, was knifed by James Wright, 26, in Paisley in July 2017.

Michael also begged law chiefs for answers on why Wright was able to stab his son while on the run after sabotaging his ankle tag.

Wright is serving 20 years for the 2017 killing in Paisley.

Michael has been told a decision on whether to launch a fatal accident inquiry is due within weeks.

He added: “We need answers. This will be my last Christmas.”

A Crown Office spokesman said: “We are aware of the importance of this decision for the family and will inform them as soon as possible.”
