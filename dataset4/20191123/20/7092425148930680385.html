Mahrez cracker helps Man City to 2-1 win over Chelsea

23 Nov 2019, 20:20 by James Cox

Manchester City and Chelsea played out an entertaining clash at the Etihad Stadium tonight.

Chelsea went on the attack from the off and the were rewarded in the 21st minute as N'Golo Kante's effort bobbled over the line.

To Pep Guardiola's credit, his side did not panic after going 1-0 down.

They kept their composure and ha an equaliser seven minutes later.

Kevin De Bruyne didn't need a second invitation to shoot when he picked up the ball on the edge of the box and it flew in after taking a fortunate deflection off Kurt Zouma.

In the 36th minute they had the lead after a piece of individual brilliant from Riyad Mahrez.

The Algerian winger went on a run before cutting inside and firing the ball past Kepa.

The second-half wasn't as lively, unsurprisingly given the frantic pace of the opening 45 minutes.

There were four minutes of additional time at the end, plenty of time for some late drama.

Mason Mount went close with a free-kick before City went down the field and scored moments later.

However, VAR ruled out Raheem Sterling's goal for offside.

The result saw City leapfrog Chelsea into third place.
