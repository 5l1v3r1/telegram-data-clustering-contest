Martin Bashir in talks for new late-night chat show after X Factor: Celebrity

EXCLUSIVE: The veteran journalist has sat down for hard-hitting intensive interviews with Princess Diana, Michael Barrymore, Jeffrey Archer and Michael Jackson

23 Nov 2019, 20:40 by Janine Yaqoob

He’s responsible for some of the past decade’s most memorable interviews.

And now, journalist Martin Bashir is set to grill the biggest names in the world on his own chat show.

Talk TV can reveal he is in talks with ITV bosses about fronting a new programme doing what he does best.

Martin’s jaw-dropping interview with Princess Diana in 1995 was likened to the recent explosive chat with Prince Andrew.

A source said: “Martin’s interview with Princess Diana has gone down in history. The world saw nothing like it until Prince Andrew’s car-crash chat with Emily Maitlis this month.

“Martin is not afraid to ask tough questions and gets revelatory stories from the big names he encounters. ITV know this would make gripping telly and get the world talking.”

Martin, 56, started out as a journalist in 1986 and worked for the BBC until 1999 on programmes including Songs of Praise and Panorama. He also worked on features for ITV’s Tonight with Trevor McDonald.

The pinnacle of his career came when he interviewed Princess Diana for the BBC’s Panorama programme about her failed marriage to Prince Charles.

She secretly invited him into Kensington Palace to give her side of the story and made sensational claims about the royal family.

From spilling the beans about Prince Charles’s affair with Camilla to admitting to her own infidelities, the interview changed the course of royal history.

He also conducted interviews with Michael Barrymore, Jeffrey Archer, Major Charles Ingram and Michael Jackson.

Currently the BBC’s religious affairs correspondent, Martin tried his luck on Celebrity X Factor – but failed to make the final.
