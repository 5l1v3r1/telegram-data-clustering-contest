Scots pub landlord nailed for groping women and making crude comments barred from own boozer

23 Nov 2019, 20:57 by Stuart Patterson

A PUB landlord nailed for groping women and making crude comments has been barred from his own boozer.

George Wood, 56, swerves the bar while heading to an upstairs flat he shares with his wife.

The pervert was put on the sex offenders register after being found guilty of 15 sexual assault charges during a two-year sleaze campaign at the Ship Inn in Banff, Aberdeenshire.

One victim said: “Sometimes you get off-colour things said in pubs but this happened all day, every day.

“He didn’t think there was anything wrong with it and he classed it as banter. But the reality is that he’s a sleazy old man.”

The town’s sheriff court heard Wood sent a barrage of creepy messages inviting women to come to the pub as his wife Margaretann Johnstone slept.

He also tried to snog others and made explicit and suggestive remarks.

One local said of the pest’s conviction earlier this year: “The pub has been quiet ever since. It seems it’s hardly open.

“George was a regular fixture but now he’s not allowed in. He doesn’t even walk through when he takes the dog out.”

As well as being placed on the sex offenders register for three years, Wood was given a community payback order and ordered to take part in a rehabilitation programme.

Sheriff Gary Aitken told him: “There needs to be an adjustment in your attitude towards certain things and your perception of what is just banter or funny.”

Wood’s solicitor Debbie Wilson revealed her client was getting counselling to deal with his behaviour.

She said: “It’s thrown him. The thing that he thought was helping sell the pub was harming it.”

In 2017, we told how Wood admitted assaulting neighbour Paschal Mohan by throwing him against a wall after Margaret­ann slipped out of bed at night to go for coffee with him.

The boozed-up lout also pled guilty to behaving in a threatening and abusive manner towards his wife.
