Chinese spy-turned-defector Wang ‘William’ Liqiang admits to covert operations against Hong Kong

23 Nov 2019, 20:04 by Mary Kay Linge

A self-proclaimed Chinese spy who turned himself over to Australian authorities has confessed to kidnapping a Hong Kong dissident bookseller and meddling in Taiwan’s 2018 elections.

Wang “William” Liqiang, a Chinese defector living in Sydney, may be the first Chinese undercover agent to defect to the West, the Associated Press reported.

Liqiang claims to have worked for a Hong Kong investment firm that was actually a Chinese government front to conduct political and economic espionage.

“These are very disturbing reports,” Australian Treasurer Josh Frydenberg told reporters Saturday. “The matter is now in the hands of the appropriate law enforcement agencies.”

The former head of Australia’s counterespionage agency, Duncan Lewis, said Friday that China is attempting a “takeover” of the Australian government — a worry that Prime Minister Scott Morrison dismissed.
