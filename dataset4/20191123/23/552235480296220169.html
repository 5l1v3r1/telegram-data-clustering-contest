Rangers, Celtic... or Kilmarnock: Queen's Park boss Mark Roberts eyes big draw after Scottish Cup upset

The Spiders are in the hat for this evening's fourth-round draw after stunning Queen of the South at Palmerston yesterday.

23 Nov 2019, 23:50 by Colin Paterson, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/colin-paterson/

Queen of the South 1 Queen's Park 2

Mark Roberts reckons it would be a dream end to Queen’s Park’s Hampden history if they drew Celtic or Rangers in the William Hill Scottish Cup tonight.

A first-half double from Salim Kouider-Aissa deservedly earned the Spiders a fourth-round spot yesterday while Connor Murray netted a consolation for the dismal Doonhamers.

The Glasgow side will embrace a new professional era before flitting from the national stadium to Lesser Hampden at the end of next year.

And delighted boss Roberts would love the club to sign off with a glamour cup clash against one of the Old Firm.

The former Kilmarnock hitman said: “I’m just so happy for the guys and I thought we were excellent.

“We’re a League Two club and we’re not expected to come to places like this and win. They’ve beaten Dundee United and Ayr United here.

“We’re in Hampden until Christmas next year and then we’re moving into Lesser Hampden.

“Rangers or Celtic at Hampden would be magic and that’s what everybody wants but I would love Kilmarnock at home because then I could walk round!

“We will let the guys enjoy this. You get lots of kicks in the b******s in football so when you do well, you’ve got to enjoy it.”

The Spiders stunned Palmerston after 15 minutes when Kouider-Aissa’s glancing header from a Joffrey Lidouren free-kick found the far corner of the net.

Kouider-Aissa then made it 2-0 when he slammed home a low ball back across the face of goal from Ciaran Summers.

The second-half surge from the Championship side didn’t come although Murray pulled a goal back a minute into injury time.

Dejected Doonhamers gaffer Allan Johnston said: “It’s a major blow to go out of the Cup so early.

“We let ourselves down and we let the fans down. We can’t afford to play like that and it’s simply not good enough.”
