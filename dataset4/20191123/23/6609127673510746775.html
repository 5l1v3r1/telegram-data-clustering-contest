BLUE LIGHTERS

Elite Scots cops may have torched files to cover up misuse of cash report claims

23 Nov 2019, 23:06 by Chris Taylor

ELITE cops may have torched files to cover up misuse of cash, a new report claims.

Officers had documents burned just before a probe into their unit began.

Now investigators say the actions by members of the now-defunct Scottish Crime and Drugs Enforcement Agency raise “doubt” over their motives.

Met detectives were brought in to review an earlier inquiry into claims SCDEA chiefs had mismanaged thousands of pounds.

The initial probe claimed “the documents incinerated were not detrimental to the investigation nor did they relate to any financial irregularities”.

But the Met’s report found that the timing so close “to a professional standards investigation” and “lack of any record of destruction throws sufficient doubt that this can be the only conclusion”.

It went on to say one SCDEA cop used funds to play golf and visit Northern Ireland “for false reasons”. A female officer said she was told to burn a pile of documents outside its Paisley HQ.

The SCDEA, set up to fight crime gangs, was axed in 2013.

Police Scotland said last night: “The Metropolitan Police Service concluded that all matters from 2011 are indeed legacy-based.”

The Scottish Police Authority said changes have been made to how archives are managed.
