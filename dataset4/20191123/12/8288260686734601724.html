Investigations continue after CS gas let off in Wetherspoons causing mass evacuation

The incident took place on Tuesday night (November 19). Police say CCTV enquiries are ongoing and the investigation continues

23 Nov 2019, 12:35 by Charlotte Regen, https://www.birminghammail.co.uk/authors/charlotte-regen/

No arrests have been made after a Wetherspoons was evacuated because of a customer letting off a CS gas cannister.

Emergency services swooped to the scene at The Moon Under Water in Lichfield Street, Wolverhampton, at around 8pm on Tuesday night (November 19).

One ambulance, two paramedic officers and the Trust’s Hazardous Area Response Team all attended the scene along with the police and fire service.

West Midlands Police told Black Country Live CCTV enquiries are still ongoing and the investigation continues.

In a previous statement, a spokeswoman for the force said: "Police are investigating after a substance believed to be CS spray or similar was let off in a pub in Wolverhampton.

"Officers were called to the Moon Under Water in Lichfield Street just before 8.50pm yesterday (Tuesday, November 19).

"A cordon was put in place while the area was made safe. A small number of people were treated at the scene after suffering the effects the substance."

Enquiries are ongoing and anyone with information can contact police via Live Chat at  www.west-midlands.pnn.police.uk  between 8am and midnight or call 101 anytime.

Please quote log 2440 of 19 November.
