Parish Notes Nov. 23-29

23 Nov 2019, 12:00 by Jocelyn Bennett, Saskatoon StarPhoenix

Parish events for the coming week.

Please send items for Parish Notes to weekend@thestarphoenix.com with the words “Parish Notes” in the subject line. Submission deadline is the Friday one week before at 10 a.m.

Craft, Bake & Book Sale: Saturday, 8 a.m. to 2 p.m., at McClure United Church, 4025 Taylor St. East. With a pancake breakfast from 7:30 to 10:30 a.m., and a hotdog lunch from 11 a.m. to 1 p.m. Everyone is welcome.

Craft and Bake Sale: Saturday, 10 a.m. to 3 p.m., at Mayfair United Church, 902 33rd St. West. Hot lunch available to purchase. Admission is free.

Advent/Christmas Market and Tea: Saturday, 10 a.m. to 3 p.m., at Redeemer Lutheran Church, 812 Preston Ave. Home-crafted items, new-to-you items, baking tables, and a tea room. Admission is free. Proceeds support Sanctum 1.5. To donate gently used Christmas items, call 306-374-7585.

Pre-Advent Market and Tea: Saturday, 1 to 4 p.m., at St. John’s Anglican Cathedral Parish Hall, 816 Spadina Cres. Christmas baking, homemade turkey pies, jams, jellies, preserves, crafts, plants, books, and a children’s make-and-take area. Tea tickets are $5.

Yoga Retreat: Sunday, 9:45 a.m. to 4 p.m., at 506 Duchess St. A day of gentle and restorative yoga postures, guided meditative practices, and the healing frequencies of the gong. Cost is $119. Pre-registration is required. Registration and information at lynneharley.com.

Mamma Mia! Sing Along Party: Sunday, 7 p.m., at The Refinery, 609 Dufferin Ave. Presented by Emmanuel Anglican Music. Admission is by suggested donation of $10 at the door. Popcorn and drink $5. Everyone is welcome.

Incarnation, Spirituality, and Cultural Transformation: Friday to Nov. 30, at Queen’s House Retreat and Renewal Centre, 601 Taylor St. West. A retreat oriented toward knowing and experiencing the presence of the Christ within every participant. With Simon Lasair. Cost is $95 and includes lunch. Register at 306-242-1916, receptionist@queenshouse.org by Monday. Information at queenshouse.org.

Legacy Christmas Dinner: Tuesday, 10:30 p.m., at Elim Church, 419 Slimmon Rd. With guest speaker and musician Don Balzer. Cost is $20 per person. Tickets at 306-374-1700.

Waiting … Creating: Prayer with Art in Advent: Dec. 3 and 10, 7 to 9 p.m.; and Dec. 4 and 11, 10 a.m. to noon, at Queen’s House Retreat and Renewal Centre, 601 Taylor St. West. Two days of artmaking, creatively exploring your response to waiting for the Messiah. All materials supplied. With Sr. Felicitas Drobig. Cost is $80 for two sessions and $13 for lunch. Register at 306-242-1916, receptionist@queenshouse.org by Tuesday. Information at queenshouse.org.

Gong Immersion Meditation: Thursday, 7:30 to 8:30 p.m., at Queen’s House Retreat and Renewal Centre, 601 Taylor St. West. This is a drop-in session. Cost is $15. Information at lynneharley.com.

All-You-Can-Eat Perogy Supper and Frozen Food Sale: Friday, 5 to 7 p.m., at the Ukrainian Orthodox Auditorium, 919 20th St. West. Includes dessert and beverage. Meat and cabbage rolls available at extra cost. Adults cost $14, children ages five to nine cost $6, and children four and under eat free. Additional food sale Nov. 30, 10 a.m. to noon, at the back door. Five dozen perogies $25, five dozen cabbage rolls $40. Information at bestvenetian@gmail.com.

One Light Chant and Dance: Second Saturday of the month, 7:30 p.m., at Stensrud Lodge, 2202 Mceown Ave. A Dances of Universal Peace event combining singing of sacred phrases from all the world’s spiritual traditions with simple circle dances and live music. No experience necessary. Admission is free. Information at chantndance@sasktel.net.

Sunday Meditation: Sundays, 10 a.m., at Windsor Chapel, 704 Windsor St. Presented by the Centre for Spiritual Living Saskatoon. Learn how to create the life you long for. Sunday celebration follows at 10:30 a.m. Information at 306-975-2022, cslsaskatoon.com.

Buddhist Meditation: Sundays, 4-5:30 p.m. Guided meditation taught by the monks from the Buddha Meditation Centre of Saskatoon. Everyone is welcome. Class is free. Information at meditationsaskatoon.org, 306-374-2840.

Service of Prayer for Post-Trauma Survivors: First Monday of the month, September to June, 5:30 to 8 p.m., at St. Matthew’s Anglican Church, 135 109th St. West. Open to anyone who is post trauma in need of prayerful support. With Rev. Karen Walter.

Celtic Evening Service: Tuesdays, 7 p.m., at St. George’s Anglican Church, 624 Ave. I South. Celtic music, tea and coffee. Each mid-month service will begin with a potluck at 6 p.m.

Ladies’ Bible Study: Wednesdays, 9:30 to 11:30 a.m., at Louise Avenue Congregational Church, 1602 Louise Ave. Study will be “The Holy Spirit.”  With coffee and fellowship. Information at sbwest@sasktel.net.

L’Arche Prayer Nights: Wednesdays, 7 p.m. Prayer and fellowship with L’Arche Saskatoon, an ecumenical Christian community. Everyone is welcome from all faiths. Information at wthiessen@larchesaskatoon.org.

Taizé Service: Second Wednesday of each month, 7 to 8 p.m., A time of music, prayer, silence and contemplation. Everyone is welcome. Information at 306-242-1916, receptionist@queenshouse.org.

GriefShare Recovery Group: Thursdays to Dec. 5, 7-9 p.m., at Louise Avenue Congregational Church, 1602 Louise Ave. Anyone who has lost a loved one is welcome to attend. Information at 306-262-4119, 306-931-1297.

Women in Ministry Friday Luncheon: First Friday of the month, 11:45 a.m. to 1:30 p.m., Queen’s House Retreat and Renewal Centre, 601 Taylor St. West. For women employed in ecclesial ministry; retired from a ministry position; or studying for ecclesial ministry. Cost is $13. Register at 306-242-1916, receptionist@queenshouse.org.

Christmas Bazaar & Bake Sale: Nov. 30, 11 a.m. to 2 p.m., at St. Matthew’s Anglican Church, 135 109th St. West. Snacks, bake table, craft table, new-to-you corner, raffle, and silent auction. With a soup and sandwich luncheon, 11:30 a.m. to 1:30 p.m.

Christmas Tea & Bake Sale: Nov. 30, 2 to 4 p.m., at St. Martin’s United Church, 2617 Clarence Ave. Presented by the St. Martin’s United Church Women’s Group. Admission is by freewill offering.
