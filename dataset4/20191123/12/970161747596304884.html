British man, 25, missing in Australian bush after hitting head

23 Nov 2019, 12:09

A young Briton has been reported missing while camping in the Australian bush, say local police.

Aslan King, 25, has not been seen since around 2am local time on Saturday.

He was camping with his friends near Princetown, Victoria, when he hit his head and suddenly ran away.

Aslan reportedly sprinted away from the camping area into surrounding bushland. His friends tried to look for the injured Briton but couldn’t find him.

Victoria Police said Aslan had a ‘medical episode’ which caused his injury.

Officers believe Aslan may now be disorientated after hitting his head and lost in the bush.

He is described as caucasian, of a medium build, with short dark hair.

Aslan was wearing a grey tracksuit, a hoody and green Reebok trainers, and may have blood on his forehead.

A Foreign Office spokesperson said: ‘Our staff are seeking further information following the disappearance of a British man near Princetown, Australia and are in contact with the Australian police.’
