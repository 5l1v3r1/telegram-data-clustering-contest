Eight beauty spots a short drive away from Bristol

There are plenty of stunning places to choose from

23 Nov 2019, 12:00 by Danny Shaw

One of the great benefits of living in Bristol is the city's fabulous location.

We are surrounded by beauty spots which are just a short drive away - from stunning arboretums and beautiful manor houses to picturesque beaches and dazzling caves.

To celebrate what's on offer, we've put together a list of eight of the best beauty spots you can visit from Bristol.

Some of them could be tricky to visit without a car - but others you can even get to on the train.

So read on and start planning your weekends around visiting these marvellous attractions.

Westonbirt Arboretum

Just a stone’s throw from Bristol, Westonbirt Arboretum is the perfect place for an autumnal escape - although it's special all year round.

Home to 2,500 trees, the woodland explodes into colour in the autumn months.

Follow the meandering seasonal trail through the woodland, or alternatively, wonder through the canopy on the Tree-Top Trail.

The upcoming Enchanted Wood event also promises festive, family fun to entertain all the family.

Tyntesfield

The National Trust’s Tyntesfield is the site of a Victorian Gothic Revival house as well as a garden and parkland.

The estate’s beauty changes with the seasons, offering an abundance of wildlife all year round.

Take the plunge into history with The Victorian Christmas Event. This family event will include choirs, an evening opening and will even be graced by Father Christmas’s presence.

Blaise Castle

The 19th Century mansion that is Blaise Castle forms the centre piece of an estate boasting 400 acres of parkland.

From the top of the castle, featured in Jane Austen’s Northanger Abbey, panoramic views of the neighbouring countryside are visible.

There’s fun for all the family as the museum offers a foray into the past and the adventure playground gives kids a place to let off steam.

Cheddar Gorge

Cheddar Gorge is undoubtedly one of The UK’s most impressive natural landmarks, with towering cliffs and a network of caves providing an insight into both history and the environment.

Rock-climbing, caving and and Escape Rooms are just a few of the exciting adventure activities the Gorge.

There are also plenty of Christmas activities for all the family, including visits from Father Christmas and his team of elves.

Weston-super-Mare

OK, you may not be totally convinced but there are some beautiful places to visit in Weston-super-Mare, Uphill for example.

This North Somerset coastal town lies just 30 minutes from Bristol and has long beaches and an impressive pier.

Just in time for the festive season, Icescape at The Tropicana, is an ice-skating extravaganza that’s great for family fun.

If that’s not enough, this Christmas the town plays host to a Christmas Tree Trail at the end of which you could win £500.

Wookey Hole Caves

Beneath the Mendip Hills lies an underground network of caves.

Wookey Hole is the largest show caves system and is also home to various fascinating rock formations.

Captain Jack’s Restaurant, The Witches Kitchen and The Ice Cream parlour provide plenty of refreshments onsite.

Longleat

The UK’s first safari park lies just an hour south of Bristol, giving customers the opportunity to drive alongside roaming lions and tigers.

You can also take a cruise down the river in the hope of spotting sea lions or the odd meerkat on the banks.

Longleat house also deserves to be explored as it simply is one of the most spectacular stately homes in the country.

Glastonbury Tor

This historical landmark provides magnificent views of the surrounding countryside and on a clear day, you can even see as far as Wales.

The 15th century tower that rests on the top of the hill is all that remains of two churches of St Michael.

The surrounding area provides the perfect escape from the city, with plenty of walking routes to choose from.
