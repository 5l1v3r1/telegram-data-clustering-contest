GM building new plant in Ohio to make engine components

23 Nov 2019, 13:14 by EndPlay

BROOKVILLE, OhioÂ (AP) - General Motors is building a $175 million plant in Ohio to produce truck engine components that will employ about 100 people.

Plant construction is underway and is expected to be completed by the end of next year in the Dayton suburb of Brookville.

The GM-DMAX plant will make components for Duramax diesel truck engines, supplying a nearby DMAX engine plant in Moraine.

The Ohio-built diesel engines will go into the new Chevrolet Silverado and GMC Sierra heavy duty trucks, the Dayton Daily News reported.

GM's executive vice president of global manufacturing, Gerald Johnson, said Thursday that the Moraine DMAX plant has been operating at high capacity to meet growing demand for the Duramax engine.

DMAX is a joint venture of GM and Isuzu.
