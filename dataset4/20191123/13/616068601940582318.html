My faith, your faith, our faith

23 Nov 2019, 13:05 by Darren Handschuh

What happens when representatives of numerous faiths and culture get together?

They have a discussion, share ideas and answer questions.

That's the plan for the interfaith conference Absolute Justice, Kindness and Kinship being held in Vernon Monday from 6-8:30 p.m.

According to organizers, the “event is aimed at inspiring confidence in Canadians and discussing the practical and sustainable paths that can lead the world towards peace.”

Prominent religious representatives from aboriginal spirituality, Baha`I, Buddhism, the Catholic Church, Trinity United Church, The Jesus Christ Latter-day Saints Church, Center for Spiritual Living, Judaism and Islam will be taking part in the event.

The conference will be held at the Vernon recreation centre, 3310 37th Ave.

The event website states:

  • The event aims to explore the teachings and practices of different religious and philosophical traditions, with the hope that such mutual exploration will encourage better understanding and promote harmonious co-existence among the members of Canada’s cultural mosaic.
  • The conference continues to strive to shorten the gap between diverse communities, and to demonstrate that we can live together in the spirit of acceptance, harmony and compassion.
  • The objectives of the event is to promote interfaith respect, harmony, inclusiveness and diversity. It inculcates mutual understanding and respect between different faith traditions and philosophies of the world. It promotes respect, harmony and cohesion among all people.
