The electric Welsh rugby teenager Wayne Pivac's being told to invest in now as he makes history

Louis Rees-Zammit, 18, has been nicknamed 'Speedy' by his coach and been backed to shine in Champions Cup

23 Nov 2019, 13:28 by Andy Howell

Wales Under-18 sensation Louis Rees-Zammit makes history on Sunday when he becomes the youngest player in Gloucester’s illustrious history to start a European match.

It will be some occasion for the youngster, with the Cherry and Whites away to star-studded French big-spenders Montpellier in the showpiece Heineken Champions Cup.

Gloucester head coach Johan Ackermann, in an interview with WalesOnline, advised new Wales boss Wayne Pivac to "invest" in Rees-Zammit.

He has nicknamed the Cardiff-born player ‘Speedy’ after Rees-Zammit, who will be aged 18 and 295 days when he takes the field at the Altrad Stadium on Sunday, clocked blistering times in sprint training.

That beats the previous mark of 18 years and 326 days set by James Simpson-Daniel in a Heineken Cup semi-final defeat to Leicester Tigers in 2001.

Rees-Zammit last season set a record when he came on as a substitute against Bath to become the youngest player to appear for Gloucester in the English Premiership.

He only stepped up to Gloucester’s senior academy from its Under-18 section during the summer but has quickly made a mark.

Rees-Zammit scored two tries in two Premiership Rugby Cup starts and has recorded the fastest sprint times in training.

The Llandaff RFC product demonstrated that searing pace with a blistering run when he came on against Toulouse for his European debut last week.

Rees-Zammit was a late addition to the substitutes bench for that game as Ollie Thorley was ruled out by injury and was picked ahead of the experienced Charlie Sharples.

His selection for the clash with Montpellier, where he will go up directly against Fiji juggernaut Nemani Nadolo, is further proof of how highly he is rated by Ackermann.

“It’s part of our process. We have to believe he’s at that level that he can play and Louis is obviously one of those,” said the former lock, who lifted the 2007 World Cup with South Africa.
