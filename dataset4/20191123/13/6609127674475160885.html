FAMILY SPLIT

Celtic-daft Rod Stewart’s brother is a Rangers fan and Light Blues player is named on his dad’s grave

23 Nov 2019, 13:26 by Justin Bowie

CELTIC-daft Sir Rod Stewart has revealed his oldest brother is a Rangers fan – and that his dad has a Light Blues player named on his grave.

The rocker told how sibling Robert is a dedicated Gers supporter – but he says the pair “don’t have fights about it”.

And he said that ex-Ibrox and Scotland defender Eric Caldow is engraved on his father’s grave.

Speaking to The Herald, he said: “My oldest brother is a Rangers fan and has been all his life.

“We don’t have fights about it. There’s a Rangers player engraved on my dad’s grave, Eric Caldow, Robert must have wangled that!”

Ahead of legendary Sir Rod’s SSE Hydro gigs next week, he said he feels like “one half” of Glasgow loves him.

He said: “I feel at home there and loved by one half of the city perhaps.

“I sometimes wonder if Rangers supporters stay away but like my music.”

In September the Hoops fan revealed he’d beaten prostate cancer after a secret three-year battle with the illness.

He was diagnosed in February 2016 after a routine appointment and at the time he urged men to check their prostates after going into remission.

He said: “I never felt any different [during the illness]. I had treatment for two years and I was working all through that time.

“I went to Harley Street five times a week for a month and no one ever found out, which was a miracle really but I wanted people to know because it’s a horrible cancer to have.

“It gives you no warning whatsoever. You go to your doctor and get a finger up the bum and no harm done.

“That’s why I came out with it; to help other people. I couldn't be fitter than I am right now.”
