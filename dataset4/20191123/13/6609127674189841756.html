Three people hurt after car crashes into Isle of Lewis house setting it on fire as man arrested

23 Nov 2019, 13:33 by Kirsty Feerick

THREE people were hurt after a car crashed into an Isle of Lewis house setting it on fire.

A man has been arrested after the alleged incident happened at around 1.30am this morning.

Mercy crews raced to the scene taking the driver and two passengers to Western Isles Hospital for treatment to serious injuries.

Emergency services also managed to remove three men aged 22, 32 and 36, and a 61-year-old woman from the car after it burst into flames.

Cops claim a blue Vauxhall Zafira crashed on the A857 after the junction with the A858, also known as the Barvas corner.

The road is down to single lane traffic while police carry out their investigations.

A 32-year-old man has been arrested in connection with alleged road traffic offences.

Sergeant Donald Sinclair, of Stornoway’s Church Street station, said: “Our enquiries into the circumstances of the crash are ongoing and I am appealing for anyone who saw the alleged crash or who saw a blue Vauxhall Zafira being driven on the A857 before 1.30am to come forward.

“I’m particularly keen to speak to anyone who may have dash-cam footage which could help with our enquiries.”

Those with information can contact police on 101, quoting reference number 0366 of 23 November 2019.
