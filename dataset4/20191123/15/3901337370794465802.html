'I Won't Celebrate Against Real If I Score'

23 Nov 2019, 15:02 by SL International

On Saturday, Real Madrid will come up against one of the players they loaned out this season, but the midfielder explains why he won't celebrate if he scores against Los Blancos.

Martin Odegaard joined Real Sociedad on loan from Los Blancos in the last transfer window and has registered three assists and two goals in 11 appearances.

The Norwegian is hoping to line-up against his parent club, but insists he has no intention of celebrating if he does find the back of the net.

“My ankle is much better, I hope to be at the Bernabeu on Saturday,” Odegaard tol El Larguero, as per AS.

“I played through the pain against Levante and after I think it was the right decision to stop for a bit. Now I feel better.

“I haven't stopped working in order to return as soon as possible. Playing against Real Madrid is a special match, but I want to play every game.”

“If I score at the Bernabeu, I won't celebrate. If I score against La Real in the future, I won't either,” he said.
