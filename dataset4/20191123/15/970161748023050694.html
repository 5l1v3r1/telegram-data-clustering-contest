Guns N’ Roses tour rakes in $584million as Slash confirms new album is on way

23 Nov 2019, 15:41

Guns N’ Roses’s Not In This Lifetime tour has raked in more than half a billion dollars in the three-year run.

Basically, Axl Rose, Slash and Duff McKagan are smashing it.

According to reports, the Knockin’ On Heaven’s Door singers’ three-year stint on the road has grossed $584.2 million (£455.1m).

The tour, which kicked off way back in 2016, has come to an end and with 5,371,891 tickets sold, making it the third highest-grossing tour, according to Billboard Boxscore.

Not too shabby…

However, GNR have a long way to go to knock Ed Sheeran off the top spot for the highest ever grossing stint.

The Thinking Out Loud singer’s time on the road with his Divide album has taken up the mantel, banking around $750million (£584m).

But if any act can push him off the top spot, we’re guessing it’ll be Guns.

And that may happen sooner than we think, as Slash confirmed the band are planning to bless us with another album full of new music.

‘I think at the end of the day, everybody wants to have a full album released. I don’t think that’s really changed all that much,’ he said during an appearance on Sirius XM.

‘But there’s a different way of putting stuff out initially nowadays more so than … I mean, there’s always been the single, but now you sort of look at that preliminary release a little bit differently now. All things considered, it’s like the Wild West out there; there is no formula for any of it.

‘I haven’t seen any routine kind of thing that works. I mean, you can do any one of a million different things to releasing a record. But at the end of the day, I think that we will ultimately release a full album.’

The 54-year-old also revealed the songwriting process has included the whole band, which has been ‘really exciting’.

‘Obviously, everybody knows that we’re working on new material, which is really exciting, and so, that’s really primarily what the focus is,’ he added.

‘Everybody has been sort of, like, when we’re in town, working on it. And then when we’re out of town, obviously not. So it’s been that kind of thing.’

Bring. It. On.
