Car park thieves target motors parked at RAH

Furious staff say the motors have been targeted as they work and blamed a lack of security for the thefts.

23 Nov 2019, 15:29 by Carla Talbot, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/carla-talbot/

Brazen crooks jacked-up cars parked at the Royal Alexandra Hospital and used angle grinders to steal their catalytic convertors.

Furious staff say the motors have been targeted as they work and blamed a lack of security for the thefts.

According to an internal email sent to hospital workers, a number of vehicles have had parts removed by criminals in a ‘works van’ over the last few weeks.

A doctor’s car was also reportedly towed away by the suspects.

One RAH worker, who asked not to be named, said her car has been written off.

She said: “In our case, the damage done means the car will have to be scrapped because the cost to repair it is over £700, which is just too much.

“It is ridiculous that staff doing their job in a hospital are targeted in a car park covered by cameras.”

Another staff member at the hospital, who also asked to remain anonymous, added: “Staff are really angry about this.

“Someone has actually been able to come in to the car park, jack up a car and physically remove a catalytic converter using heavy machinery.

“This isn’t a two-minute job. It would have taken time to do this and the fact that they were able to do so unchallenged, is unbelievable.

“There have been other incidents and people can’t believe that one car was just blatantly towed out of the car park.”

Staff are now looking for answers from bosses.

The RAH worker added: “The security guards are more concerned with stopping staff from parking there than they are actually monitoring the car park.

“How can someone come into a car park, put a car up on a jack and cut out its catalytic converter with no one asking any questions?”

Police are probing the incidents and appealed for witnesses to come forward.

Chief Inspector Dougie Falconer, area commander for Paisley, said: “We have had a number of reports of parts being stolen from cars parked at the Royal Alexandra
Hospital.

“I would urge people to be vigilant and report anyone acting suspiciously around or under motor vehicles parked at the hospital.

“I’m concerned there may be more victims that have not yet reported thefts of parts from their car as they may not yet have realised.

“If you have any information in relation to these incidents, please get in touch with police.”

A NHS Greater Glasgow and Clyde Health Board spokeswoman said: “We were only made aware of this issue on Monday when one member of staff reported the incident from Sunday, November 17.

“We will give all assistance required to Police Scotland, which is now investigating.

“We would appeal to staff to be vigilant and report any thefts or suspicious activity to our facilities staff.”

Anyone with information should contact police on 101, or Crimestoppers, anonymously, on 0800 555 111.
