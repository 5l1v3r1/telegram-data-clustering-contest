Pink ball Test: India on the verge of big win after Virat Kohli, Ishant Sharma heroics

India vs Bangladesh, Pink ball Test: Ishant Sharma's 4-wicket haul in the 2nd innings meant Bangladesh were reeling at 152 for 6 at Stumps. Earlier in the day, Virat Kohli's 1st hundred with the pink ball put them in a dominant position.

23 Nov 2019, 15:05 by Akshay Ramesh

HIGHLIGHTS

  • Bangladesh were reeling at 152/6, trailing India by 89 runs at Stumps
  • Mushfiqur Rahim's unbeaten 59 helped Bangladesh survive Day 2
  • Ishant Sharma took his tally in the match to 9 wickets

When Ishant Sharma was steaming in and making the pink ball talk in the much-talked-about twilight period on Saturday, it looked like the historic Day-night Test would end inside 2 days.

However, a stiff resistance from Mushfiqur Rahim who remained unbeaten on 59 at Stumps helped Bangladesh take the Pink ball Test to Sunday. Ishant Sharma shone with the ball with a match tally of 9 wickets after Virat Kohli dominated proceedings with the bat, scoring his first hundred with the pink ball.

Bowlers dominated the proceedings in the twilight period at another housefull day at Eden Gardens. India went from 289 for 4 to 331 for 9 but captain Virat Kohli gave his pacers enough time to make use of the conditions as India declared on 347 for 9 with a 241-run lead.
