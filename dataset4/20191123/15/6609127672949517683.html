Heartbroken Highlands family beg lorry drivers to create funeral convoy for beloved trucker granddad as over 90 sign up

23 Nov 2019, 15:23 by Kirsty Feerick

A HEARTBROKEN highland family are begging lorry drivers to create a massive convoy for a beloved granddad's funeral.

Dan Raeburn, 71, from Munlochy passed away suddenly from a blood clot leaving his wife, six children, and four grandkids devastated.

Now they want to honour the long-time lorry driver with one last ride before saying their final goodbyes on Wednesday.

But they were left stunned after their small social media appeal attracted the attention of HGV drivers from all over the country.

His son Ally Raeburn was blown away by the response with already 90 trucks agreeing to take part.

Speaking to the Scottish Sun the 42-year-old says he would be 'speechless' if the drivers all turn up and is desperate to make his dad proud.

He said: "We are really overwhelmed by how far one small Facebook post has gone.

"I had no idea it would, I really didn't expect it to reach the other side of Inverness never mind nationally.

"We lost my dad a week last Wednesday, it was blood clot and happened really sudden.

"He had not been well for a while but still it was a big shock no one saw it coming.

"My dad passed his lorry driving test when he was about the age of 20.

"He spent his whole life driving lorries but had to retire recently because of his health.

"That's all he knew, and he loved it.

"Apart from his family and kids it really was his whole life.

"We want the lorries to show up at his funeral because he deserves it."

Ally's wife Shelia Raeburn, 37, who was like a daughter to Dan, posted the appeal on Facebook.

She said: "Long shot my dad passed away last week suddenly lorry driver all his days went by the name big Dan.

"We are having his funeral this Wednesday coming in his hometown of Munlochy.

"I know I am asking a lot but do any of you fellow HGV drivers have spare time at 10/10.30 so we can have a convoy to take my dad to his final resting place from Knockbeck church to Tore cemetery.

"Thank you from the bottom of my heart."

Ally and Shelia were both amazed at how many people wanted to help their cause.

Ally added: "I knew my dad was well known but I didn't realise quite how well until everyone started responding to the post.

"I didn't realise how much respect everyone had for him it was amazing to see.

"When I was younger I spent a lot of my youth in the lorry with my dad.

"I loved it as well it didn't phase me if he was driving a lorry or a car or if we were on a bus, just the sheer fact I was spending time with my dad was great.

"I grew up surrounded by the lorry driving community to they are all like extended family to me.

"I would really be speechless if lorries turned up, I think there is 90 confirmed right now.

"There's lorries coming from all over including Ireland.

"We are saving all the Facebook responses and printing them off for a little scrap book so we can give my mum or keep for the grand-kids to remember how kind everyone has been."
