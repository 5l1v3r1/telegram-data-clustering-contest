Derby County dealt double injury blow against Preston North End

Derby County v Preston North End | The Rams returned to Championship action at Pride Park Stadium and suffered an injury blow

23 Nov 2019, 15:32 by Steve Nicholson, Mark Wakefield

Derby County have been dealt a double injury blow in their Sky Bet Championship clash against Preston North End.

The Rams returned to league action at Pride Park Stadium in their first game after the November international break.

Phillip Cocu made two changes to the side that lost 1-0 to Nottingham Forest, with Craig Forsyth and Mason Bennett introduced in place of the injured Matt Clarke and striker Jack Marriott.

Cocu was forced into an early change as midfielder Graeme Shinnie required treatment before being replaced by George Evans in the 14th minute, after being unable to carry on.

The Scottish midfielder appeared to pull up with a hamstring problem after playing a pass out wide to Bennett.

Shinnie had treatment on the pitch before he limped off.

Derby were then dealt another injury blow as Bennett was forced to depart just after the half-hour mark, and replaced by Martyn Waghorn who then put the Rams in front against the Lilywhites.

Derby were already without defender Matt Clarke.

The knee injury he suffered in the defeat by Nottingham Forest before the international break will keep him out for "several weeks", which is a blow because he had started the last six matches in a partnership at the back with skipper Curtis Davies.
