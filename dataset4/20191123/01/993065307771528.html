Police to send file on Roma leader to prosecutors in Belfast intimidation probe

23 Nov 2019, 01:00 by Brendan Hughes

POLICE are to send a file on a prominent Roma leader in Northern Ireland to prosecutors over a malicious poster campaign.

Nicolae Nicola (30) was interviewed under caution last week as part of an investigation into alleged exploitation and intimidation affecting the Roma community.

It emerges after The Irish News revealed a man was convicted earlier this month for sending "menacing" online communications.

Mr Nicola was the public face of the Romanian Roma Community Association of Northern Ireland (RRCANI).

The south Belfast charity was accused last year of charging Roma "large sums of money" for what should be free benefits advice.

Whistleblowers raised this claim with the Belfast Trust, which responded by moving its Early Years children's services out of RRCANI's Holylands offices.

RRCANI strongly denied the allegations.

Intimidating posters later appeared on walls which named and made false allegations about two Early Years staff who worked closely with the Roma community.

Mr Nicola was arrested in February and a file was sent to PPS for alleged harassment and attempted intimidation.

However, prosecutors decided not to pursue a case after concluding its evidential test had not been met.

Last week Mr Nicola was questioned again voluntarily after new evidence emerged, BBC Radio Ulster's Nolan Show reported.

In a statement, Detective Inspector Stephen Harvey said: "A 30-year-old male was interviewed under caution last week and a file is currently being prepared for resubmission to the Public Prosecution Service."

Mr Harvey said the PSNI's "investigation into exploitation and criminality affecting the Roma community, led by Criminal Investigation Branch in Belfast, is continuing".

He thanked those who have come forward so far and urged anyone with information to phone police on 101 or Crimestoppers anonymously on 0800 555 111.

Earlier this month Daniel Serban (41), of Tildarg Street in Belfast, was convicted in his absence of improper use of an electronic communications network.

The charge is understood to relate to the sharing online of images of the intimidating posters put up in south Belfast last year.

He was accused of sending in October last year "by means of a public electronic communications network a message or other matter that was of a menacing character".

A second man – Florin Bondor (32), of Thorndale Avenue in the north of the city – has been brought to court charged with the same offence. He denies the charge.

Their cases have been adjourned until January.

Serban, a member of the north's Roma community, was pictured some years ago with Sinn Féin's Máirtín Ó Muilleoir while wearing the Lord Mayor of Belfast's chain of office.

Mr Ó Muilleoir said he had his picture taken with thousands of people when he was Belfast mayor in 2013/14, and does not know Serban.

Last year Mr Ó Muilleoir had posted on Twitter his support for RRCANI when allegations about the organisation began circulating online.

He tweeted a photo of himself outside RRCANI's offices with people including Mr Nicola, and said he was "proud to stand with RRCANI".

The South Belfast MLA had also written to Belfast Trust criticising the decision to move children's services from RRCANI.

He later said that Sinn Féin is "opposed to all forms of racism, exploitation and intimidation" and called for a thorough police investigation.
