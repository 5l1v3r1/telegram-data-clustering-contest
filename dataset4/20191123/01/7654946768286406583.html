Cuomo’s posturing on Long Island natural gas crisis is a real jobs killer

23 Nov 2019, 01:48 by Post Editorial Board

It looks like all of New York will end up paying for Gov. Andrew Cuomo’s scapegoating of National Grid for the Long Island natural-gas shortage that he caused: Moody’s Investor Service just put out an alert that all investor-owned utilities operating in New York now face new “credit-negative” risks.

This accompanies Moody’s launch of a credit-rating “downgrade review” for KeySpan Gas East Corp. and Brooklyn Union Gas Co., both National Grid-owned — a review mandated by the gov’s written threat last week to revoke National Grid’s license to operate in New York.

Moody’s doesn’t see “license revocation as a likely outcome” for any New York utilities, but Cuomo’s over-the-top threat still “represents a weakening of the consistency and predictability of New York’s utility regulatory environment.” In other words: Who knows how far Cuomo might go?

Especially when the utilities are supposed to answer to the independent-by-law Public Service Commission — which Cuomo is now treating like just another arm of his office.

Notably, he last year had the PSC threaten to yank the license of Charter Communications (Spectrum cable), extorting it into making concessions to the International Brotherhood of Electrical Workers.

It’s all the more reason for businesses to think three times before investing in New York — and a guarantee that the state will continue to lag the nation in job growth.
