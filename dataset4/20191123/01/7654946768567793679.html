Michael Blake’s apparent fraud is new test for New York’s ethics watchdogs

23 Nov 2019, 01:41 by Post Editorial Board

It looks like Crain’s New York might’ve just caught Assemblyman Michael Blake robbing the taxpayers. Now let’s see what the state’s various ethics watchdogs (watch-Chihuahuas?) do about it.

State legislators are entitled to per-diem payments to cover expenses in Albany, as well as travel reimbursements. But — spoiler alert! — they can only collect for days they’re actually in Albany.

Blake’s team insists that, for every full per-diem filing, he conducted official business in some way, shape or form, or at least went to sleep in Albany before midnight.

But Crain’s has evidence suggesting that Blake charged for days when he was elsewhere: On April 25, 2017, for example, he missed all Assembly votes, including on five committees he serves on — and video apparently shows him at the Ohio Black Legislative Caucus’ Day of Action in Cleveland. Yet he claimed a full $175 “overnight” per diem, Crain’s reports.

This year, on June 10, he expensed $640 in plane, train and taxi costs to attend a “legislative meeting” in Washington. Yet his own Instagram account appears to show him speaking at the national Democrats’ African American Gala in Atlanta, Georgia, that day.

The state Comptroller’s Office, the Assembly Ethics Committee and the Joint Committee on Public Ethics all have jurisdiction to investigate. Since Blake is running for a US House seat, could one of them resolve this case before next June’s Democratic primary?
