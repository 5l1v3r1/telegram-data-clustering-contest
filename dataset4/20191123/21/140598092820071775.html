BBC Radio 4 will broadcast 10-minute 'soundscape' of Archers characters baking mince pies at Christmas

23 Nov 2019, 21:00 by Phoebe Southworth

BBC Radio 4 will broadcast a 10-minute "soundscape" of Archers characters baking mince pies to give listeners a taste of Christmas in Ambridge.

Patricia Greene, who plays gifted cook and village matriarch Jill Archer, will be rustling up a batch of the sweet treats in her fictional kitchen at Brookfield Farm in December.

Listeners will hear her kneading the dough, rolling it out and cutting it into rounds before filling it with homemade mincemeat and sliding it into the AGA.

Not forgetting the traditional topping, she also be heard whipping up some brandy butter.

The clink of cups will chime through the kitchen as she boils up a well-deserved cup of tea to sip during the baking session.

However, those hoping to scribble down the recipe for her delicious mince pies will be disappointed, as that is never revealed.

The first episode of The Archers was broadcast on January 1, 1951 and it is the world's longest running drama.

It follows the trials and tribulations of the Archer family and their neighbours in the fictional county of Borsetshire.

Greene joined the cast as Jill Patterson in 1957. Her character goes on to marry farmer Phil Archer and they have four children together, becoming the First Family of Ambridge.

She is one of the radio soap's best-loved and most enduring characters, and appeared on Radio 4's Desert Island Discs in 2015 in honour of her longevity.

Greene has admitted she had no experience in radio broadcasting when she auditioned for the role and that her school drama teacher told her she had no future in acting.

But she channeled the sultry tones of actress Fenella Fielding in her audition - a performance which won her the job.

"I was totally useless," she told broadcaster Kirsty Young. "They said you're coming in as a love interest for Philip, six weeks only, a sexy blonde in a tea tent. That's what the director said."  

But the show's creator, Godfrey Baseley, recognised her talent.

"After my first six weeks which were horrible and I couldn't do anything, he came in and said: Congratulations. Cut the sex, you're going to marry him."

Sound effects used during filming of The Archers over the years include a log striking a cabbage to emulate a cow kicking someone, and Alka Seltzer dissolving in a glass of water to replicate the bubbles in a glass of champagne.

Hailed as capturing the "quieter side of life in the village" during a peaceful Saturday, this festive episode will be broadcast on December 7.

On Christmas day, listeners will be treated to a short story on BBC Radio 4 starring double Oscar winner Glenda Jackson, 83, and Game of Thrones heartthrob Kit Harington, 32.

Adapted for the airwaves, Neil Gaiman's 'Chivalry' sees Mrs Whitaker, voiced by former MP Jackson, discover the Holy Grail in a charity shop.

She ends up at the centre of Sir Galahad's (Harington) "most important quest" - and ropes him into doing some chores along the way.
