HUNT IS ON

Nephew of EuroMillions winner Fred Higgins handed boost in hunt for missing mum

23 Nov 2019, 21:25 by Kevin Duguid

THE long-lost nephew of lottery millionaire Fred Higgins was handed a boost in the hunt for his missing mum.

Ian Cooke, 61, was told the £58million jackpot winner and wife Lesley were examining his appeal for info to trace Scots mother Alexandrina, who vanished in 1964.

Hailing the statement, Ian said: “Hopefully this is good news but I don’t want to get my hopes up too much. It’s been going on too long.

“I just want to know where my mum is and have so many questions for them.”

We told last week how Fred’s eldest sister gave Ian up for adoption when he was just six.

Ian, of Notts, was later told Alexandrina died before finding evidence she may be alive.

He also discovered a link to his uncle Fred, 69, from Laurencekirk, Aberdeenshire, and Lesley, 59, before appealing for their help.

A spokesman for the couple, who won the EuroMillions prize last August, said: “We are looking into this.”
