HELPING HAND

East Kilbride girl, 5, begs Nicola Sturgeon to help best pal access cannabis oil to stop life-threatening fits

23 Nov 2019, 21:40 by Chris Taylor

A GIRL of five has begged Nicola Sturgeon to help her best pal access cannabis oil to stop life-threatening fits.

Lucy Park asked the First Minister to meet with the family of epileptic Cole Thomson, seven — and make ‘wonder drug’ Bedrolite available on the NHS.

In a heartfelt letter, Lucy — who met her friend through a disabled kids footie team — wrote: “Cole was very sick and it made me really sad. When he takes his special medicine we can have lots of fun.

“He was too poorly before.

“I’d like Nicola to come to our tea party.

"Then she would see how the medicine makes Cole and his mummy happy.

“Please come and play with us.”

We told how Bedrolite kept Cole, of East Kilbride, seizure-free for 224 days after ex-cop mum Lisa Quarrell, 38, smuggled it into Scotland from Amsterdam.

The oil is not on a list of NHS drugs approved after rule changes by ministers last year.

A Scottish Government spokesman said it was “open to discussing the situation” with the family.
