Shoppers furious after Sainsbury’s doubles cost of plastic bags to 20p

23 Nov 2019, 21:44

Sainsbury’s has doubled the price of its plastic bags to 20p and shoppers are furious.

The supermarket said the move was part of its drive to halve plastic use within five years, with profits going to good causes.

They said the price rise was ‘fair’, but angry shoppers were quick to vent their frustrations on social media.

One called it ‘daylight robbery’ while another questioned why the grocer didn’t offer paper bags rather than ‘profiteering from my forgetfulness’.

A third commented: ‘You’re right, you will cut Sainsbury’s plastic consumption as I won’t be shopping there anymore.’

Sainsbury’s scrapped single use carrier bags back in 2015 after the government imposed a 5p charge.

Asda increased the price of their plastic bags from 9p to 15p in July and Morrisons upped their to 30p the following month.

Tesco have kept theirs at 10p.

A Sainsbury’s spokesperson told The Sun: ‘We think 20p is fair. We’re really hoping it will help reduce our plastic usage.’

In September they removed plastic bags from the bakery, fruit and veg aisles and replaced them with a 30p drawstring carrier.
