Manhunt launched for Brit, 25, missing in Australia bushland after bumping head

Aslan King has not been seen since suffering a medical episode, in which he hit his head before fleeing into nearby bushland, near the iconic Twelve Apostles in Victoria

23 Nov 2019, 10:30 by Ben Weich

A desperate search is underway for a 25-year-old British man who has gone missing in bushland in Australia.

Aslan King was camping with friends near the Twelve Apostles limestone stacks in rural Victoria, before suffering a medical episode.

At about 2am this morning, he hit his head and ran into bushland, the Age reports .

Friends he was camping with launched a search for Mr King but were unable to find him, as officers warned that he may be disorientated and lost in the bush.

Just hours before his disappearance Mr King, an illustrator from Bristol, posted a video of Friday's sunset to Instagram

He also posted clips of his road trip along the Great Ocean Road.

Mr King "suddenly got up and ran from the area into surrounding bushland" in the early hours of Saturday morning, a police spokeswoman said.

Mr King's friends, who are also British, told Australia's Nine News that they had been drinking before he appeared to suffer a seizure.

When his friends tried to restrain him, he started running into bushland.

According to local media, Mr King had only been in the country for weeks before his disappearance.

He was described as a white man of medium build, with short dark hair.

He was wearing tracksuit bottoms, a light grey T-shirt, grey hoodie and green Reebok runners when he went missing.

Police say Mr King may possibly have blood on his forehead.
