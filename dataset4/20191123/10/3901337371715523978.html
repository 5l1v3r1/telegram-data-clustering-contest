Man Utd Coach On Mourinho's 'Circus' Return

23 Nov 2019, 10:00 by SL International

Ole Gunnar Solskjaer was the man who replaced Jose Mourinho at Manchester United, and the Red Devils manager has now commented on the Portuguese's return to the Premier League.

Read: Five Players Mourinho Could Sign In January

Mourinho was appointed manager of Tottenham Hotspur on Thursday morning following the sacking of Mauricio Pochettino the evening before.

The Portuguese endured a difficult third season at Old Trafford, which resulted in a exit in December last year following a 3-1 loss to rivals Liverpool.

"When it's like this, you like Jose coming back," Solskjaer, as per Sky Sports.

"It's going to be a spectacle and a circus and Nemanja has a sense of humour."

Pochettino was heavily linked with a move to United when Mourinho was sacked, but Solskjaer insists he is not worried about his job with the Argentine now a free agent.

"It doesn't bother me at all because I've got the best job in the world.

"I am sure if you are in or out of a job and you are a manager, you would want this job, so it doesn't really matter whatever happens around it.

"I've got to focus on my job at Manchester United and do it as well as I can. I speak with Ed (Woodward) and the owners all the time about how we are going to move the club forward.

"That doesn't change if some other clubs change their managers."
