Vehicle adapted to make 'police-like noises' stopped and has sirens removed

"Safe to say that the person responsible has been dealt with and the ‘sirens’ removed from the vehicle."

23 Nov 2019, 10:35 by Phoebe Ram

Officers out on patrol stopped a vehicle after it was adapted to make 'police-like noises'.

Special constables in Gedling posted to Twitter that a vehicle had been stopped on Friday, November 22.

They reported the 'sirens' were removed and the person responsible was 'dealt with'.

The tweet from Notts Police Specials read: "Stopped: Our Gedling Specials have just identified and stopped a vehicle which was adapted to make police-like noises.

"Safe to say that the person responsible has been dealt with and the ‘sirens’ removed from the vehicle."

Special constables (or "Specials") are volunteer police officers with the same powers as regular officers.
