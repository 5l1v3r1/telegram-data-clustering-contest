'We cleared popcorn with our own hands' - Disgust as Star City Vue cinema not cleaned before showing

The cinema chain has apologised and admitted a rota blunder led to Screen 10 not being cleaned ahead of the Sunday showing of Punjabi film, Daaka

23 Nov 2019, 10:58 by Stephanie Balloo, https://www.facebook.com/MissBalloo/

A family have shamed a popular city cinema at a Birmingham multiplex after they were forced clean their own seats.

Litter, popcorn and empty cups were left strewn across a cinema screen at Star City's Vue Cinema on Sunday, November 17.

Shocking images capture the mess from previous viewers - which was not cleaned as usual due to a rota blunder, Vue later admitted.

A 26-year-old, who had gone to see a Punjabi movie with his family, hit out at the cinema, saying they had to clear others' popcorn from their seats.

The cinema-goer, visiting with his mum and brother, told BirminghamLive: "There were no clean seats.

"We had to move the popcorn with our own hands. It was all up the stairs."

The son had spent £15 to see the late showing of Daaka at the leisure complex's cinema.

But on arrival, they were greeted by popcorn strewn across the floor, seats and stairs.

Empty bottles still filled the cup holders and litter was seen dumped on every row from the last viewers.

"We got there about 11.30pm, went to the counter and was waiting 5-6 minutes, so we got our tickets from the machine.

"We went in about 11.40 - 11.45pm. It was a complete mess. I took pictures and I complained to one of the staff members in the cinema and he apologised. But he didn't say why it happened.

"While I was at the cinema I emailed Vue. I've tried to phone them but it kept getting disconnected."

Vue apologised for the state of the cinema and said Screen 10 had been overlooked due to a mix up with the cleaning rota.

It has since issued the family with three complimentary tickets,

A Vue spokesperson said: “The images taken at our Star City site clearly don’t meet our high standards of cleanliness.

"Following a review of the cleaning rota and system in place screen 10 was unfortunately overlooked on this evening.

"This would normally not occur and management have taken action to ensure this can’t happen again.

"We would like to apologise to customers who were affected.”
