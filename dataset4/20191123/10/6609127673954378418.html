TOUGH CALL

Celtic boss Lennon says he’ll need to talk to Brown if he gets Scotland call-up

23 Nov 2019, 10:02 by Kenny MacDonald

CELTIC boss Neil Lennon has cooled talk of a Scotland recall for Scott Brown.

SunSport revealed that the Hoops captain was refusing to rule out a sensational comeback if Scotland get to Euro 2020.

Israel will be Scotland’s first opponents in the play-offs after the draw was made in Nyon.

That tie will be at Hampden on March 26, with the winners then travelling to face Norway or Serbia for a place at the finals.

But Lenny admitted he’d need to “talk to him” if his captain got the call.

Brown quit Scotland for the second time in February 2018, but has now opened the door to a potential return.

Lennon said: “First of all, there would need to be a conversation between Steve Clarke and Broony, then I’d need to have a conversation with him as well.

“He’s currently getting a benefit from not playing international football, but how much I don’t know.

“He’s certainly playing very well at the moment and there’s no question he could still make a contribution to the Scottish team.

“So it would be down to the chat between those two. I think they’ve had conversations before but I don’t know whether or not that line is still open.

“I wouldn’t be surprised if he was to be called up and I totally get the attraction for Scott of having an appearance at a major finals on his CV, but how would his comeback affect the rest of the group, the people who’ve been performing?

“The Scotland midfield’s functioned very well in their last few games and they have Scott McTominay to come back in as well.

“Ultimately, it will come down to Steve’s call and then we’ll take it from there.”

However, Lennon hinted he would never try to stop a player playing for his country.

He insisted: “There’s too much negativity around players who want to go and play for their country.

“I’ve got a group of Scottish lads who have come back buzzing after winning two games.

“Greg Taylor hasn’t been playing here but he has now got two good international performances under his belt.

“Some people say they play too much football.

"But the flip side of that is Greg and Nir Bitton, who haven’t had much game time, have come back into our group fully match fit and happy.

“Callum McGregor, Ryan Christie and James Forrest were also pleased with the round of games and three wins in a row will be good for them psychologically.

“You can cast aspersions on the quality of the opposition but that doesn’t matter. Winning is vital and now they have the most important matches for years coming in March.”
