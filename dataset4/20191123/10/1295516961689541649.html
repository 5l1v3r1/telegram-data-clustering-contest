Black Adam Producer Discusses Chances of Cavill Superman Appearance

23 Nov 2019, 10:11 by Tom Chapman

Black Adam producer Hiram Garcia is hoping Henry Cavill will reprise his role as Superman for Warner Bros.' upcoming comic book caper starring Dwayne "The Rock" Johnson.

Speaking to ComicBook.com, Garcia discussed Cavill's potential involvement, saying, "I think the DC Universe is a wonderful universe and we're open to everything. We have big aspirations for it. We're friends with Henry. [Dwayne] and Henry are friends, it's a huge comic book brand as well. And I always just loved the idea. Who knows? But man, Black Adam for Superman is really cool. That'd be really powerful."

Following the box office disappointment of Zack Snyder and Joss Whedon's Justice League, the well-laid plans for the DCEU started to fall apart. Ben Affleck later stepped down as Batman, and Cavill has not since reprised his role, seemingly moving on to a new franchise in The Witcher. Cavill was also rumored to cameo, but was unavailable for filming. Instead, the movie featured a Cavill-inspired version of the character that was only shot from the neck down. That being said, Cavill recently said he's not ready to stop playing the Metropolis Marvel.

Directed by Jaume Collet-Serra, Black Adam stars Dwayne Johnson. The film is scheduled to arrive in theaters Dec. 22, 2021. Production is expected to kick off in July.
