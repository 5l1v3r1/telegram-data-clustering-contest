Boy, 8, shot in face during Mormon family massacre in Mexico seen for first time

Cody Langford is the last of seven siblings who survived Mexican cartel massacre to be released from hospital where he was treated for multiple gun wounds

23 Nov 2019, 10:06 by Lila Randall

A mormon boy shot in the jaw pictured for first time with siblings after they miraculously survived Mexican cartel’s brutal massacre.

Cody Langford survived being shot in the jaw and right foot, during the Mexican ambush in the Mexican state of Sonora.

Cody was the last of seven children to be released from a Tuscan, Arizona, hospital last Saturday.

The seven Langford survivors were pictured together after losing their mum Dawna and two of their brothers, Trevor, 11, and Rogan, who were among nine killed by a suspected drug cartel gunmen.

Three mums and six children died in the massacre.

A heartwarming snap shows Cody sitting in a wheelchair with his six siblings surrounding him at their home in Arizona, which was published on the family’s GoFundMe page.

The injured boy was travelling in a convoy of SUVs with seventeen members of the LeBaron Mormon community to visit family when they were attacked around 70 miles south of the US border.

The families were travelling from their home on the La Mora ranch to a settlement along a hostile road controlled by men financed by Mexico’s illegal drugs trade, the BBC reported.

Dawna Ray Langford and her sons Trevor, 11, and Rogan, two, were killed in one car while Christina Langford Johnson, 31, was killed in another. Her seven-month-old baby, Faith Langford, survived and was found on the floor of the vehicle. 

Mum Rhonita Maria LeBaron, 33, was killed along with her six-month-old twin babies, Titus and Tiana, and two other children - Crystal, eight, and Howard, 10. The others were allegedly taken hostage eight miles up the road.

Mexican reports said the attack could have been a case of mistaken identity, as the family were travelling in SUVs similar to those used by cartel.

Others say they could have been targeted in a crossfire.

Cody’s extraordinary recovery has been documented online.

Earlier this month, the youngster had his jaw wired shut and had to undergo further rounds of surgery after medics located yet another bullet.

Video footage shows Cody in pyshio, his leg bandaged, practising to straighten his legs and learning to walk.

So far the GoFundMe page has raised more than £190,000 which the father, David Langford, will use to cover medical expenses and split with the Miller and Johnson families, who were also caught up in the shooting.

Mr Langford told ABC: "Every one of my children that survived that are living miracles," Langford said. "How many bullet holes were fired into that vehicle … at that horrific scene and how many children were involved. It's amazing. It's amazing. It's beyond amazing that they survived."

Mr Langford said he wants the attackers to face justice. He added: "I believe in forgiveness, but I also believe in justice and forgiveness doesn't rob justice. You don't get justice too much in Mexico," he said.

Most of the extended LeBaron family plan to leave Mexico after the shooting.
