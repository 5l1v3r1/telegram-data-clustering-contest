Man arrested for assault at MBTA station wanted on two other warrants

23 Nov 2019, 04:17 by Manoella Macedo

BOSTON - Transit Police have arrested a man in connection to a violent assault that happened on Wednesday.

According to officials, in an unprovoked attack, a man violently assaulted a woman at North Station at around 10:30 p.m.

On Friday, detectives working on a tip in the area of the Park Street MBTA station at around 1:40 p.m., spotted a man, later identified as Robert Gaul, 45, of Boston, who matched the suspect's description.

After confirming Gaul was the suspect they had been looking for, officers arrested him. 

During his booking process, police found out Gaul had two other warrants out for his arrest out of Lowell for assault and battery with a dangerous weapon on a person over 60 and another one out of Brockton for aggravated assault and battery with a dangerous weapon.

Gaul had also been using the alias Robert Booker.

He is being charged with assault and battery with a dangerous weapon, but it is unclear if there could be any additional charges.
