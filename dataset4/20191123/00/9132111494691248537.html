Prove your maturity by inviting me legally – Separatist group leader to govt

23 Nov 2019

Leader of the Homeland Study Group Foundation (HSGF), Charles Komi Kudzordzi has sent a message, from his hideout to the Government of Ghana to desist from sending security personnel after him, and invite him for talks through his legal representatives.

The Ho District Court ordered the arrest of Charles Kwame Kudzordzi and some members of his foundation for “unlawful” declaration of the Volta region as a sovereign state – Western Togoland Separatist Movement

But the wanted separatist movement leader in his video message said he is willing to avail himself on condition that his lawyers are involved and his security assured.

“If government feels I should come back for the case to continue, I am free to do so through my lawyers, but to look for me, I don’t know what they are going to do with me…human as I am, I will not present myself.

“I am in the country anyway, I have not travelled or hidden anywhere, but I want the government to show maturity, invite me legally through my lawyers and I will present myself for the case to continue,” Charles Komi Judjordzi said in the video.

He also chastised government for snooping around his house with security personnel and arresting some members of his family and his supporters.

“What has started happening is not the right thing, going around arresting people in the street, taking them and locking them up in cells and also going into the houses of individuals particularly mine and taking my 70-year old wife and locking her up…no, that is not the right thing.”

He, however, called for calm amongst his people who form the Western Togoland, charging them to not engage in any attack in order to ensure that peace prevails.

‘We want to set a record for peace to prevail so that the whole world will know that we are peace-loving people,” he emphasized.
