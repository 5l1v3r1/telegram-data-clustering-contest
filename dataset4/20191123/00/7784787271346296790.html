Your guide to AFL draft academy and father-son bidding ahead of next week’s National Draft

23 Nov 2019, 00:20 by Matt Balmer

Once again, another draft pool is stacked to the brim with potential academy and father-son selections inside the first round and here’s your guide to how the bidding system works.

In 2015, the AFL created the draft value index which allocated a points value to every possible selection.

Clubs who already have gun academy and father-son youngsters attached to them now have to ‘pay’ to secure these players via multiple picks, because rival clubs have the right to bid on these players. This forces the nominated clubs to match the bid to secure the player. If they don’t, the player goes to the club who made the bid.

SCROLL DOWN FOR A FULL EXPLANATION ON THE BIDDING SYSTEM

Each draft selection now has a points value attached to it, starting with Pick 1 (worth 3000 points) down to Pick 73 (nine points). Picks from 74 onwards are worth nothing.

The best way to explain the scenario is via a hypothetical example.

  • LET’s say Brisbane bid on Hawthorn father-son prospect Finn Maginness at Pick 21 (878 pts) **
  • IN order to snare him, the Hawks now have to match the Lions’ bid using the draft picks they already hold.
  • FIRST, we must take away the 197 point discount (which is automatically given to clubs for bids outside the first round) that would be given to the Hawks, meaning they now have to find 681 points to secure Henry.
  • With Hawthorn already using Pick 11, their next three selections are Pick 42 (395), Pick 50 (273) and Pick 54 (220), cover the bid
  • However, as the value of those three picks adds up to 888, they have 207 points left over. This means the Hawks’ Pick 54 would slide back to Pick 55 in the draft
  • Now Finn Maginness is officially selected by the Hawks at Pick 21, but the Hawks have lost Pick 42 and 50.
  • The Lions select again at Pick 22 and the predetermined draft order continues from there.

** - Note it is likely Pick 21 would slide back to Pick 23 after GWS match a bid on Tom Green and Fremantle match a bid on Liam Henry.
