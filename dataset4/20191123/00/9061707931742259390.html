Remembrance-themed bake off at Frederick Gent

School raised more than £100 for Poppy Appeal

23 Nov 2019 by Jane McFarlane

Frederick Gent School held the Great Freddie's Bake Off and raised over £100 for the Poppy Appeal, in honour of Remembrance Day.

Students and staff from the school based in South Normanton, were asked to create remembrance themed cakes and other treats, and the top three bakes were rewarded with prizes. The school was inundated with exceptional bakes and throughout the day students were able to sell their cake creations. By the end of the day a grand total of £138 was raised from the cake sales.

Headteacher, Mr Woollard, and the Chair of Governors, Sandra Fitton-Wilde had the honour of choosing the winners of the bake off. The winners were Daisy Naylor, Evie Farrer and Poppy and Nicole Wood.

Students who were highly commended include, Olivia Wheatcroft in year 8, Millie Riley in year 8, Emily Rosindell in year 9, and Shaiyena Rawson-Brooks in year 8.

Finally, the best staff bake was won by Miss Skelton.

South Normanton Parish Councillors presented the top three winners with a prize and the winning cakes were sent to the South Normanton Parish Council for a fundraiser over the weekend to raise even more proceeds towards the Poppy Appeal.

Christopher Woollard, Headteacher of Frederick Gent School, said: “I would like to thank all the students who created such unique and delightful cakes, as well as the staff for organising the remarkable event. The event was a huge success and I am thrilled with the amount the school have raised for such a worthy and important cause. At this time of year, it is important to reflect on how privileged we are to have so much good in our and events like this are such an great way to bring the school community together and remind everyone how lucky we are.”
