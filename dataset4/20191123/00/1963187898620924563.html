Egypt possess strong infrastructure meeting needs of investors: Nasr

23 Nov 2019 by MENA

CAIRO - Nov 23 (MENA) – Egypt possess a concrete infrastructure that enables it to meet all the requirements of potential investors, said Investment Minister Sahar Nasr on Saturday.

The country has outlined a legislative framework meant to stimulate investment and find solutions to investors with a view to creating a favorable business climate and paving the way for investments of the private sector, the minister added.

While addressing a session of the "Investment for Africa Forum 2019" that takes place in the New Administrative Capital under the auspice of President Abdel Fattah El Sisi, Nasr pointed out to the structural reforms implemented by the country in light of an inclusive economic program carried out in partnership with the private sector.

The minister further highlighted the legislative updates and the national economic reform program which have been carried out by the government to improve the country’s investment climate.
