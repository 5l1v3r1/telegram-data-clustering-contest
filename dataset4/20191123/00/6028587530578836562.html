UFC Light Heavyweight Champ Jon Jones Opens As Monster Favorite Over Dominick Reyes

23 Nov 2019 by Trent Reinsmith

UFC light heavyweight champion Jon Jones has his next fight booked. Jones, who last defended his title in July, meets No. 4 ranked 205-pound contender Dominick Reyes on February 8 in Houston, Texas.Chris Taylor of BJPenn.com first reported news of the bout. ESPN's Brett Okamoto later confirmed the fight with UFC President Dana White. Jones wrote on Twitter that he had the contract in his inbox, waiting for his signature.

Jones fought twice in 2019. In his first bout of the year, he successfully defended his title with a unanimous decision win over Anthony Smith in March. His July title defense saw him score a split decision win over Thiago Santos. The victory over Santos was the first time Jones did not win a fight by unanimous decision or stoppage.Â 

Reyes is 12-0 as a pro. He joined the UFC in 2017 on the heels of three consecutive first-round knockout victories. Two of those wins came via head kick. Reyes also ended his first three fights with the UFC via first-round stoppage. After tow decision wins, Reyes got back on track with a "Performance of the Night" bonus-winning knockout of former UFC middleweight champion Chris Weidman. That win came in October.

Before this fight was signed, there were questions if Jones would face Reyes or Corey Anderson. Earlier this month, Jones seemed to make a decision when he wrote on Twitter, "Just finished a coaches meeting studying Dominick and Cory's careers. Established Dominicks definitely the more dangerous fighter. Beating his ass next."

The bout between Jones and Reyes is scheduled for February 8 at Toyota Center in Houston, Texas. The fight headlines the pay-per-view event. The co-main event is another title fight. In that contest, UFC women's flyweight Champion Valentina Shevchenko faces Katlyn Chookagian.

The early odds for the Jones vs. Reyes matchup are heavily in the champion's favor. Jones is a -550 favorite over Reyes, who comes in as a +390 underdog.
