Best Wines To Pour At Thanksgiving Dinner

23 Nov 2019 by Aly Walansky

Thanksgiving is the Super Bowl of cooking and entertaining. And while a lot of the focus is on the perfect roast turkey and all the trimmings, an extended family get together calls for wine. Lots and lots of wine.

While at some point during dinner with the family one might be reaching for everything and anything available, these are some great wines to pour during Thanksgiving dinner. Here’s some of the best options for a hearty and festive holiday dinner.

Diving Into Hampton Water Rose Wine 2018 

At some point in Thanksgiving dinner, you’ll hit anywhere-but-here mode. And while fantasizing about being anywhere else, you might fantasize you are with Jon Bon Jovi. Here’s the next best thing: Diving Into Hampton Water Rose Wine has a clean and fresh taste with with bright red fruit flavors like cherries, strawberries, and watermelon, a balanced acidity, and a long, dry and refreshing finish. It’s a perfect welcome wine at Thanksgiving dinner but you just may want to enjoy it the whole night.

Planeta Frappato

This is a popular Sicilian wine, and it’s delicious when served chilled. Frappato is perfect for anyone who enjoys bold flavorful reds that are fruit-forward and light-bodied. Think of it as an alternative to Pinot Noir, and it’ll be perfect for sipping as people arrive or throughout dinner.

Toscolo Chianti DOCG 

A bold Italian wine made from 100% Sangiovese grapes, the wine is a bright red color and rich and flavorful. While this one will work as a perfect all-around dinner wine, it’s also great to pair with the cheese or pasta course, or dessert.

Monte Antico Toscana IGT

Another fabulous Italian wine, this one is 85% Sangiovese, 5% Merlot and 10% Cabernet Sauvignon. The wine is a dark ruby color, with flavors of black cherries, licorice and plums. It’s a great choice for those who want a medium to full-bodied palate – and it has a bit of a spicy feel to it that will hold up against hearty meat and pasta dishes. 

Primitivo di Manduria “Il Matané” DOC 

This Italian wine is 100% Primitivo and tastes of ripe cherries and plums, with notes of chocolate and vanilla. This wine has a long, velvety finish which will work great with Thanksgiving dinner menus that include lamb and hard cheeses.

Pinot Nero

We all love Pinot Noir, but you have you tasted it from Alto Adige, Italy? “This Northeastern region of Italy has its founding origins in the Austria-Hungarian empire and most of its inhabitants speak German. Almost every car zipping around is a BMW or a Mercedes, and that Austrian influenced attention-to-detail inherent in the region really helps the cultivation of such a fickle grape. Put a little chill on it and serve with a deep-fried or smoked turkey at Thanksgiving or even game hen, veal, or venison,” said Matthew Kaner, co-founder of Bar Covell and Augustine and one of Wine Enthusiast Magazine's 40 Under 40 Tastemakers 2019.

Elena Walch Ludwig Pinot Nero $42

Cantina Terlano Pinot Noir Monticol $45

2013 Villa Antinori Chianti Classico Riserva 

This wine is an intense ruby red in color varietal. A glass of Villa Antinori 2013 has notes of red fruit that’s spicy with a touch of balsamic. This will be great with the turkey and trimmings or to enjoy as the night goes on.

Terrazas de los Andes Reserva Malbec 2017 

Another rich and hearty red wine, this one has very intense colors, with aromas of fresh fruit, flowers, and spices. Enjoy this all night long (especially as the conversations get more intense. We’ve all been there!)
