Sir Alex Ferguson speaks out on Man Utd's struggles under Ole Gunnar Solskjaer

23 Nov 2019, 11:49

Sir Alex Ferguson admits Manchester United are going through a ‘challenging’ spell under Ole Gunnar Solskjaer.

Solskjaer enjoyed a superb start to his managerial career at Old Trafford but results and performances dropped off after he was named as Jose Mourinho’s permanent successor.

The Red Devils have endured a poor start to the 2019-20 campaign, winning just a third of their Premier League fixtures to sit seventh in the table after 12 matches.

Manchester United’s current struggles stand in stark contract to the monumental success Ferguson enjoyed at the club, with the Scot winning 13 league titles and two Champions Leagues in a remarkable spell at the club.

Asked what the future holds for his former club, Ferguson told Manchester United’s official website: ‘Well, we’ve got a great structure at the club.

‘We’ve got a lot of good young players coming through, that’s never changed.

‘There’s always that challenge, and what’s happening at the moment is a challenge, but with a bit of luck – and a bit of perseverance – we’ll sort it out.’

United’s struggles under Solskjaer have sparked fears that the best young talent will be reluctant to join the Premier League giants but Ferguson insists this is not a concern.

‘The young players at United are doing well,’ he told talkSPORT. ‘It’s a good set up at United.

‘For any young kid going there, it’s a great club to go to. And I think England are producing great young players who are representing their country and that can only be a good thing.

‘Hopefully Scotland can follow that!’

On Sunday, Manchester United travel to Premier League new-boys Sheffield United, who are currently fifth in the top-flight.
