Porcini mushroom risotto recipe 

23 Nov 2019, 11:00 by Angela Hartnett

This sustaining supper requires very little preparation; simply soak the porcini mushrooms and start cooking. It's perfect for a midweek meal or satisfying weekend supper. 

Risotto shouldn’t be too solid. It shouldn’t be like a soup, but it should pour easily. Adding one ladleful of stock at a time and allowing it to absorb is the best way. It’s critical not to overcook it, and essential to use a great home-made stock.

Prep time: 5 minutes | Cooking time: 25 minutes 

SERVES 

four to six

INGREDIENTS 

  • 2 tbsp oil
  • A few knobs of butter for cooking, plus 75g cold butter, diced
  • 1 large shallot, finely chopped to make about 2 tbsp
  • 1 garlic clove, finely chopped
  • 350g risotto rice
  • 50g dried porcini mushrooms (the lighter in colour the better), soaked in hot water to rehydrate
  • 1 litre hot light chicken stock
  • Handful of chopped flat leaf parsley
  • Grated parmesan, added to taste
  • Handful of sautéed ceps, to finish

METHOD 

  •  Heat the oil and butter in a large heavy-bottomed pan. Add the shallot and garlic and cook for five minutes, or until the shallot is cooked and soft without any colour.
  • Add another couple of knobs of butter along with the risotto rice and toast it in the bubbling butter for a few minutes. Drain and chop the soaked porcini and add to the rice.
  • Start to add the hot chicken stock a ladleful at a time, adding the next lot only when it has cooked away in the pan. Cook for 16 to 18 minutes in total, until the rice is tender with a bite.
  •  Remove the pan from the heat and stir in the cold diced butter and parmesan to taste. The risotto should be loose and pourable – add more butter if necessary and check the seasoning.
  • Finish with the chopped parsley and some sautéed ceps.
