Police allegedly find ecstasy, cannabis in teenager’s car at schoolies, Victor Harbor

23 Nov 2019, 11:22

A teenager has been charged with drug trafficking during schoolies’ celebrations at Victor Harbor.

Police allegedly discovered 34 ecstasy pills, cannabis and a set of scales in a backpack in the back of the car at 6.30pm yesterday. He was bailed to appear in court on January 6 next year.

Police have handed out almost 30 fines, with 27 expiation notices for various offences, including drinking in a dry zone, urinating in public and disorderly behaviour.

One police barring order was also issued. Seven people were taken to hospital.

A Wynn Vale teenager, 17, was arrested just after 9pm on Friday for allegedly acting disorderly and damaging property.

However, Superintendent Mark Fairney said there were no major incidents during the evening.

“Police are pleased with the behaviour of schoolies so far,” he said.

“It’s great to see so many schoolies have taken advice from parents and police to be responsible and enjoy themselves.”
