Bulldozer at homeless camp

23 Nov 2019, 19:54 by Chelsea Powrie

Bulldozers cleared loads of garbage out of a homeless camp in Surrey Saturday morning, following a previous warning from the city to the campers that they would be coming in. 

Workers were accompanied by RCMP as they moved debris from the so-called "Sanctuary Tent City" next to King George Boulevard located on private property. 

Some campers still remained, despite the offer of a warm bed at a new shelter called "The Cove," CTV News reports. 

A woman named Robin was one of those who stayed, worried about how her beloved dog Shelby would get along with others in a confined shelter. 

Another couple, Dan and Kim, who are older, have lived at the camp for five years. 

"Finally broke down,” Kim said. “We have to move out of here, we realize that.”

Dave Diewert of Alliance Against Displacement likened the experience to an assault. 

"Watching them destroy people’s places and slash through this with massive equipment.”

Crews will continue to clean up the area over the next few days, but for now are letting remaining campers take a bit more time to pack up and head out.
