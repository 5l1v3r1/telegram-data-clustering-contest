Klopp and Van Dijk react to Liverpool's dramatic victory over Crystal Palace

23 Nov 2019, 19:01

Jurgen Klopp hailed the hunger of his team after Liverpool maintained their eight-point gap at the top of the Premier League with a thrilling 2-1 victory over Crystal Palace.

Wilfried Zaha appeared to have rescued a point for the Eagles when he cancelled out Sadio Mane’s 49th-minute opener at Selhurst Park with just eight minutes remaining.

But, three minutes later, Roberto Firmino scored from a corner to ensure Liverpool left south London with three points and have now gone 30 games unbeaten in the Premier League.

Klopp, reacting to the win, said: ‘I’m not surprised at all that it was tough, to be honest. You probably saw all my games [against Palace], they were always similar.

‘We just know it’s a tough place – the atmosphere is good, they don’t give up. Crystal Palace doesn’t need a lot of situations to stay in the game.

‘We knew before that they can play really good football. They were more often direct and did that pretty well, had moments after set-pieces.

‘We knew at half-time we had to do better. The challenges – I said it now to pretty much everybody and their dogs – that they [the players] come back from so many different national teams.

‘So today I have absolutely no problem that we were not brilliant because in a game like this you just have to make sure that you fight for the right result.’

Klopp, whose Liverpool side have dropped just two points this season, added: ‘Could we have played better? Yes.

‘But we had players on the pitch that were on Thursday morning still in a plane back from Abu Dhabi, so that’s not too cool.

‘Getting a result at Crystal Palace is something we never take for granted. It feels good.’

Star defender Virgil van Dijk, meanwhile, described Liverpool’s win over Palace as ‘massive’.

‘Obviously everyone knows how tough it is to come here against a very well organised side after the international break, and we were very happy with the three points,’ he said.

‘If you are prepared to suffer setbacks, because in the games that will happen, it’s about how you react and so far we have been doing that pretty well.

‘Obviously we try to minimise the setbacks in the game, we tried to not concede and today they scored the goal.

‘We had opportunities to score a second and it’s how you react, and so far we have been doing pretty well.’
