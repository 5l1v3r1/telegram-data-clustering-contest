Warriors end long drought

23 Nov 2019, 19:00 by Wayne Moore

The five week, 10-game slump is finally over.

The West Kelowna Warriors put a six-spot on the board in one of their best 60-minutes all season, beating the visiting Salmon Arm Silverbacks 6-2 Saturday at Royal LePage Place.

It was their first win since edging the same Silverbacks 2-1 at home back on Oct. 18.

"Everything that we've been talking about finally fell into place," said Warriors head coach Brandon West.

"I thought we played really well with the lead. Even when they started pouring it on, I saw some good things from our group."

West said the coaches put some 17-year-olds in some situations they normally wouldn't be.

Two of those, Tyler Cristall and John Evans were dominant all night with linemate Brendan Pigeon.

The line was dangerous every time they stepped on the ice, combining for 4 goals and 10 points.

They got things going right off the opening faceoff, playing tic-tac-toe down low before working it to Evans who beat Ethan Langenegger from in close 14 seconds in.

It was the first time the Warriors had opened the scoring in 11 games, and the first time they had held a lead of any kind over that same stretch.

Evans made it 2-0 late in the first when his shot from the bottom of the left circle found a way through Langenegger, who argued briefly he had been bumped before the puck went in.

"John and Tyler have some history playing together and I thought Brendan has been one of our most consistent guys from day one.

"He's a winger playing centre and he compliments those guys real well. Those three shared the puck all night long, making great unselfish plays, and finding ways to be leaders."

The Warriors dominated the first period and the early going of the second.

But, a fight four minutes into the second seemed to give the Silverbacks a bit of life, then a goal three minutes later tipped the momentum in their direction.

Instead of giving into the pressure, the Warriors were able to push back, and were rewarded when Max Bulawka crashed the net and deflected home a pass from Lucas Cullen.

Salmon Arm again cut the deficit to one three minutes into the third, but again, it was the Warriors who were able to respond.

Cristall rang a shot off the goal post, but the rebound went straight to Pigeon who deposited his third on a power play.

Cristall and Cullen put it away with two more power play goals.

Cullen's brought everyone to their feet. After leaving the penalty box, he raced into the Salmon Arm end, stripped a defender of the puck then from in close, pulled the puck back between his legs and flipped it past Langenegger.

Cullen had gone 12 games without a goal.

The six goal outburst also matched their total from the last four games combined.

The Warriors, who completed a run of three games in four nights, and endured a lot of travel over the past four weeks, get a well deserved break.

They don't return to the ice until next Friday when Powell River come calling.
