Helen Flanagan's defiant response after being urged to 'stop moaning' about her kids

The Coronation Street actress shares two young daughters with former Aston Villa star Scott Sinclair

23 Nov 2019, 19:33 by James Rodger, Katie Fitzpatrick, https://www.facebook.com/jamesrodgerjournalist

Helen Flanagan has passionately defended herself after being told: "Stop moaning about your kids".

The Coronation Street actress shares two young daughters with former Aston Villa star Scott Sinclair.

And Helen, who plays Rosie Webster on the ITV1 soap, issued a passionate response as she clapped back at a social media user.

Helen's staunch defence came after being accused of 'moaning' about her children.

One Instagram user told Helen to 'buckle up and get on with it' - but the soap star says she's being honest so that her followers can identify with her.

Helen, a former I'm A Celebrity campmate, posted the row on her Instagram stories for others to see, reports the M.E.N.

"Direct messages like this," she said.

"I have friends very close to me who have struggled to conceive."

Helen was told: "Stop ranting about your kids! Buckle up and get on with it.

"There are some people who are unable to conceive then you have people like yourself moaning.

"It's part of the process. Get on with it and stop ranting which is what you're doing."

Helen, from Bury, replied: "Actually I am honest on my Instagram so other mums can identify with me and feel less on their own.

"I am supportive of other women."

Helen, 29, shared on her Instagram stories a photo of herself looking glum with the words: "To get any peace at the moment I have to lock myself in the toilet ... any other mums do the same?"

In another frank post she shared a picture of a chocolate cake with the caption: "Delilah screamed so much in the car today I was so stressed I had to stop at Costa drive thru and inhale this."

Helen's eldest daughter Matilda is four and she welcomed her second child with Celtic footballer Scott Sinclair Delilah in June last year.
