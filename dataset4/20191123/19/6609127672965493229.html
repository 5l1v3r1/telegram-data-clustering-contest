SHOP DRAMA

Cops swoop in on Glenrothes shopping centre following reports of man with ‘knife and firearm’

23 Nov 2019, 19:58 by Rachel Macpherson

POLICE swooped in on a Glenrothes shopping centre following reports of a man with a "knife and a firearm".

Emergency services attended the scene at Kingdom Shopping Centre this evening.

Cops were called out at around 5.25pm this evening following the reports.

A 16-year-old has now been arrested following the incident.

A Police Scotland spokeswoman said: "Police were called out to Kingdom Shopping Centre in Glenrothes at around 5.25pm on Saturday, November 23 following reports of a man with a knife and a firearm.

"A knife and a replica of a firearm were recovered by police.

"A 16-year-old has now been arrested.

"There was no risk to the wider public's safety."

The Scottish Sun previously reported how a man had been rushed to hospital following an attack in Belshills on Friday night.

And earlier we told how cops had launched an urgent appeal for a missing Dumfries teen who vanished in September.

Maraj Karimi, 15, was last seen on Sunday 22 September 2019 in the Newton Stewart area.

Cops now believe he may have travelled to Belfast in Northern Ireland.
