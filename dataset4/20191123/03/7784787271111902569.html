Rabbit out of a hat! Van Gisbergen snatches Race 31 pole position in Newcastle

23 Nov 2019, 03:10 by James Pavey

Shane van Gisbergen pulled a rabbit out of the hat to clinch pole position for Race 31 at the Coates Hire Newcastle 500.

Van Gisbergen was the first driver out in the fight for pole, and as rivals came and went, the 2016 champion’s 1:10.2817s lap proved too far out of reach.

Series champion Scott McLaughlin was third man out, and missed van Gisbergen’s time by just 0.0627s to be the meat in a Red Bull Holden Racing Team sandwich.

Provisional pole winner Jamie Whincup missed top spot by just 0.1070s and was a tenth down on his qualifying time set earlier in the day.

Cameron Waters (#6 Monster Energy Mustang) slotted into fourth ahead of Anton De Pasquale (#99 Penrite Racing ZB Commodore), with both drivers within two tenths of van Gisbergen’s benchmark.

Fabian Coulthard outqualified McLaughlin earlier in the day, but managed sixth and was over two tenths down on his Shell V-Power Racing Team stablemate.

Second-man-out David Reynolds (#9 Penrite Racing ZB Commodore) was seventh ahead of James Courtney (#22 Walkinshaw Andretti United ZB Commodore), Tim Slade (#14 Freightliner Racing ZB Commodore) and Lee Holdsworth (#5 The Bottle-O Racing Team Mustang).

It was a sublime performance by van Gisbergen, who nearly missed the Shootout altogether after sneaking into the top 10 in a thrilling qualifying session.

The result marks the first time since 2009 (Garth Tander, Tasmania) that the first driver out in the Shootout walked away with pole.

“The car has been awesome all weekend, just no good on new tyres,” said van Gisbergen, praising his team for turning the car around on set-up.

“I was just stoked with that, they did a great job tuning it, getting the tyre phasing better.

“I just can’t believe we went from 10th there to the front. It’s critical here.

“Beside my mate Scotty, so it’ll be good.”

The 95-lap Race 31 will commence at 4:15pm AEDT.
