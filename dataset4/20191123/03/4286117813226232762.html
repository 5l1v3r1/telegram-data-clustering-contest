Sharad Pawar says his Nationalist Congress Party (NCP) had no hand in his nephew Ajit's astounding decision to join a BJP-led government.

Ajit Pawar himself said he reached the decision on his own.

Other NCP leaders -- Chhagan Bhujbal, Nawab Malik, Jayant Patil and Jitendra Awahad -- said they had no idea of what was to come. Praful Patel said Sharad Pawar had nothing to do with it.

Indians watched open-mouthed on Saturday as their TV screens showed Devendra Fadnavis of the BJP and Ajit Pawar taking oath as Maharashtra's chief minister and deputy chief minister, respectively.

That image -- which appears to have left the political establishment just as shellshocked -- came very much against the run of play: for days now, the NCP, the Congress and the Shiv Sena have been working on a prospective alliance, irrespective of their differing ideologies.

As recently as Friday evening, Sharad Pawar said the new alliance wanted Shiv Sena leader Uddhav Thackeray to be its chief minister.

Well, it looks like the Sena can forget about that for now.
