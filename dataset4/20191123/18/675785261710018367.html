Humble Jose Mourinho won't take any credit but that was Dele Alli back to his best

Dele Alli impressed for Tottenham as Jose Mourinho enjoyed a winning start against West Ham with a 3-2 victory at the London Stadium on Saturday

23 Nov 2019, 18:45 by Andy Dunn, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/andy-dunn/

He will not take any of the credit because he is so, so humble, of course.

When a sprinkling of the away support acknowledged him in song long after the final whistle, he acknowledged it with mock sheepishness.

That well-documented humility will prevent Jose Mourinho from suggesting he has had some sort of immediate, transformative effect on Tottenham Hotspur.

And let’s face it, West Ham were - for three-quarters of this odd match - utterly dismal, with a goalkeeper who must have won some sort of competition.

But as he pointed and shouted his approval towards Dele Alli in the celebratory wake of Spurs’ second, Mourinho would have been giving himself a familiar, mental pat on the back.

In fact, a great big self-hug.

Forget the fact the player has been in pretty decent nick over the past couple of months anyway, Mourinho’s one-liner asking him if he was Dele or Dele’s brother obviously hit a mark.

And never mind that Dele’s brother, or mother, would have fancied his chances against Manuel Pellegrini’s lot, genius Jose is back.

One quip and prime Dele returns, assisting in the opener for Son Heung-Min and then impudently pre-assisting in the Lucas Moura close-ranger that doubled Tottenham’s lead.

Quite why West Ham and Pellegrini afforded him the freedom of the London Stadium for most of the afternoon is anybody’s guess.

But the basic truth is that this was, arguably, one of Dele’s best performances of the season.

This was Dele back to his cheeky best, back to his England standard, back to his roaming, adventurous, creative norm.

Call it coincidence, if you like, but to not give Mourinho any sliver of credit for Dele’s contribution would be churlish.

Just as it would not be to give him any credit for Tottenham’s first Premier League away win since January 20.

Again, the caveat has to be that West Ham, for large swathes of the game, were insipid, half-committed and, overall, a tactical joke.

But Mourinho had a plan - he always does - and it worked.

Take Tottenham’s third goal. In the first half, it had been evident the often-maligned Serge Aurier had been given a licence to bomb forward from the right-back position whereas Ben Davies had been told to hold his station at left-back.

Sure enough, early in the second half, Aurier careered into opposition territory again and sent over the cross from which Harry Kane, with a crushing inevitability, beat the truly hapless Roberto with his header.

Not exactly a masterstroke from Mourinho but a ploy that paid off, as so many of his ploys used to, certainly back in the day.

He will not have been happy that Michail Antonio and Angelo Ogbonna - courtesy of decent finishes and unacceptable penalty box freedom - were able to give the scoreline a semblance of respectability West Ham did not really deserve.

There was, indeed, some late sloppiness that reminded everyone just how underwhelming Spurs have been this season.

But this was pretty much the perfect start for Mourinho, allowing him to go through a fair chunk of his extensive, technical area repertoire.

The finger-pointing, the low fist pumps, the hands-in-pockets pacing and an old, old favourite from his motivation manual.

With 11 minutes of regulation time left, he withdrew Dele, for tactical reasons obviously but also enabling him to give one of those familiar, one-to-one public displays of affection.

Just like the hanging humbly around at the end so the fans eventually feel pretty much compelled to chant your name, it is a well-used Jose trick.

Brace yourself for a lot more of them.

As the world knows, as Dele knows, it is not going to be dull.
