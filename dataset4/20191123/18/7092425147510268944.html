Leinster come out on top in tough Lyon encounter

23 Nov 2019, 18:01 by James Cox

Lyon 6-13 Leinster

Leinster dug deep to come out on top in a tough away clash against Lyon today.

Lyon out-half Jonathan Wisniewski missed an early drop-goal attempt before Leinster went ahead through a Johnny Sexton penalty.

Lyon were putting heavy pressure on the visitors but Leinster's defence came up with the goods for what proved to be the game's only try.

Josh van der Flier won the ball before giving it to James Ryan.

Tadhg Furlong and Rob Kearney were also involved in the move which eventually saw Max Deegan touch down.

Sexton converted before Lyon went in search of a try of their own.

Leinster were under pressure for much of the game but they managed to hold firm for a crucial win.

Next up in the Champions Cup for Leo Cullen's side is Northampton Saints, who beat Benetton today.
