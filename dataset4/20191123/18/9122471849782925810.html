ESPN’s Final Prediction For Today’s Nebraska-Maryland Game

23 Nov 2019, 18:30 by Chris Rosvoglou

Two programs that are reeling will have the chance to snap their losing streak this afternoon. Later today, the Nebraska Cornhuskers will take on the Maryland Terrapins.

Many analysts were confident that Nebraska would be a real threat in the Big Ten this year. Scott Frost is widely considered one of the better offensive minds in college football.

Nebraska had a 4-2 record midway through the season and seemed well on its way to building something special. All that momentum went away very quickly though, as the team is currently on a four-game losing streak.

If there was ever a time for the Cornhuskers to snap their losing streak, it’d be this afternoon against the Terrapins. Mike Locksley began the season with back-to-back wins, but the program has now lost its last five games.

Although betting odds have Nebraska favored by 6.5 points, the ‘Worldwide Leader in Sports’ has Maryland winning the game.

Per ESPN’s Football Power Index, the Terrapins have a 51.1 percent chance of defeating the Cornhuskers.

Nebraska needs to defeat Maryland in order to remain eligible for a bowl game this season.

Kickoff for this game is at 3:30 p.m. ET from Maryland Stadium.
