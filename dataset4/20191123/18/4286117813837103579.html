Ajit Pawar sidelined, NCP-Cong-Sena knock SC's door against Maha Guv: Top developments

In a mega turn of events on Saturday morning, Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP) leader Devendra Fadnavis was sworn in as the 19th Chief Minister of Maharashtra. The development was reported at a time when the Nationalist Congress Party (NCP), Congress and Shiv Sena were still engaged in talks to arrive at a power-sharing formula before staking claim to form government in Maharashtra. In fact, just a few hours before Devendra Fadnavis took oath as the chief minister, Sharad Pawar had said that the consensus for the chief minister's post was on Uddhav Thackeray.

Sources have told India Today TV how the BJP, along with the NCP's Ajit Pawar, who has now been sworn in as the state's deputy chief minister, executed the midnight coup in Maharashtra.

It all started on Friday afternoon when Ajit Pawar, Sharad Pawar's nephew, approached Maharashtra Governor Bhagat Singh Koshiyari in Mumbai with signed letters from all 54 of NCP's winning candidates in the Maharashtra assembly elections, extending his support to Devendra Fadnavis and the BJP.

Following this, Devendra Fadnavis, who'd been keeping a low profile since the Shiv Sena broke ties with the BJP, reached Raj Bhawan (Governor House) late evening. Devendra Fadnavis staked claim to form the government along with the support of NCP's 54 MLAs and 15 others.

Having received the NCP's letter of support, Governor Koshiyari informed the Centre, recommended the lifting of President's Rule in the state, paving way for a democratically-elected government.

On Saturday morning, Governor decided to invite Devendra Fadnavis to form the next government in Maharashtra. While Devendra Fadnavis was sworn-in as the chief minister, Ajit Pawar took oath as the BJP leader's deputy.

AJIT'S DECISION TO ALIGN WITH BJP AGAINST PARTY LINE: SHARAD PAWAR

NCP supremo Sharad Pawar, who had announced on Friday that there was agreement on making Uddhav Thackeray the chief minister, held a joint press conference with the Shiv Sena leader and said that the decision of his nephew Ajit Pawar to align with BJP was against the party line. He also made it clear that any MLA who aligns with Ajit Pawar would face anti-defection law.
