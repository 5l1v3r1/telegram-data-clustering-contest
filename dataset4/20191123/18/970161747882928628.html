Three seriously injured after car crashes into house and sets on fire

23 Nov 2019, 18:13

Three people have suffered severe injuries when their car crashed into a house and drove up the side of it.

The car became engulfed in flames as it got stuck upside down, with the back end of the car almost touching the roof of the home.

The incident occurred at around 1.30am on Saturday on Barvas Corner, on the Isle of Lewis.

A blue Vauxhall Zafira crashed into a house, which resulted in both being set on fire, before emergency services and police were scrambled to the scene.

They successfully rescued the occupants of the car – three men aged 22, 32 and 36 – and a 61-year-old woman who was in the house.

The driver and two passengers were taken to Western Isles Hospital for treatment to serious injuries.

A 32-year-old man was arrested in connection with road traffic offences.The road is down to single-lane traffic while police investigate.

Sergeant Donald Sinclair, of Stornoway’s Church Street station, said: ‘Our inquiries into the circumstances of the crash are ongoing and I am appealing for anyone who saw the crash or who saw a blue Vauxhall Zafira being driven on the A857 before 1.30am to come forward.

‘I’m particularly keen to speak to anyone who may have dashcam footage which could help with our inquiries.’
