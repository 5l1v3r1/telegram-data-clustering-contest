'New coach' - Tony Adams goes on passionate rant about Unai Emery and Arsenal

Tony Adams has been speaking about Unai Emery and Arsenal after the clash against Southampton

23 Nov 2019, 18:30 by Paul Clarke

Tony Adams has issued a strong response to Arsenal's 'disjointed' 2-2 draw at home to Southampton which saw the Gunners winless run in the Premier League stretch to five games.

Arsenal have dropped down to seventh in the top tier and the result has only added to the pressure on the shoulders of boss Unai Emery.

The Spaniard is enduring a tough second season at the Emirates and has been accused of not improving the team since Arsene Wenger left the club last year.

Fans of the Gunners are becoming increasingly tired of the inconsistent play under Emery and in many ways, Adams has now perfectly summed up the discontent around the club.

During an appearance on BT Sport's results show, Adams was quick to heavily criticise the performance from Arsenal and Emery himself.

"The boos are deserved. It was a terrible performance and very disjointed," Adams told BT Sport. "There was no defending in sight and they changed the system three times. He (Emery) is not doing himself any favours.

"That was a really poor performance and I don't want any coach losing his job but the club is not going forward and progressing.

"The recruitment has been poor and we got rid of (Laurent) Koscielny and (Aaron) Ramsey and the team is weaker for that.

"I could put a team together that is more organised and enthusiastic than that. There is nobody actually going out in that game to get a clean sheet. There doesn't look like a clean sheet in sight. We kept 24 clean sheets in one season once."

Whilst Adams knows some of the new signings including Nicolas Pepe need time to prove their worth, the club legend gave a stark verdict on Emery.
