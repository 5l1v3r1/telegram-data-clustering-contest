Every TV General Election debate still to come in 2019 - BBC, Sky, Channel 4 and more

Their policies split opinion with neither leader coming out as the clear winner - but they have several more opportunities to try and gain support from the public

23 Nov 2019, 18:29 by James Rodger, Fionnula Hainey, https://www.facebook.com/jamesrodgerjournalist

Jeremy Corbyn and Boris Johnson are returning to the campaign trail after a tough TV grilling from a live audience on a BBC Question Time special.

The Labour leader told the audience he would take a "neutral stance" in an EU referendum while the Prime Minister was challenged over "racist rhetoric" and trustworthiness during the debate.

Their policies split opinion with neither leader coming out as the clear winner - but they have several more opportunities to try and gain support from the public.

The BBC will host another head-to-head debate, as well as one involving all the main party leaders, while Sky News as plans for a three-way battle and Channel 4 will focus solely on the environment.

Here's everything you need to know about the upcoming TV election debates, reports the M.E.N .

Sky News debate

What is it?

A three-way debate involving the leaders of the three main political parties in England. The Scottish National Party has argued that its leader should also be part of it - but Sky is yet to confirm if Nicola Sturgeon will be joining in.

Who's taking part?

The debate will see the prime minister Boris Johnson take on Labour leader Jeremy Corbyn and Liberal Democrats leader Jo Swinson.

When is it?

Thursday, November 28. Time to be announced.

What channel?

Sky News (Freeview channel 233)

BBC leaders debate

What is it?

A seven-way podium debate with representatives from all the major parties invited to take part. Nick Robinson will be hosting the show which will be broadcast live from Cardiff.

Who's taking part?

The Conservatives, Labour, Liberal Democrats, Plaid Cymru,Â Greens, Scottish National Party and the Brexit Party have all been invited to take part.

When is it?

Friday, November 29 at 7pm.

What channel?

BBC One and online on the BBC iPlayer

BBC prime ministerial debate

What is it?

A head-to-head debate between current prime minister Boris Johnson and opposition party leader Jeremy Corbyn. It will be chaired by Nick Robinson and broadcast live from Southampton.

Who's taking part?

Just the leaders of the two main parties.

When is it?

Friday, December 6 at 8.30pm.

What channel?

BBC One and online on the BBC iPlayer

Under 30s debate

What is it?

Emma Barnett will be the host of a Question Time special, focused on an audience made up of young voters. The show will be filmed in Hull and panelists will include representatives of all major parties.

Who's taking part?

The panelists have not yet been announced.

When is it?

Monday, December 9 at 8.30pm.

What channel?

BBC One and online on the BBC iPlayer

Channel 4 climate debate

What is it?

A leaders debate focusing only on the issue of climate change. All of the major party leaders have been invited to take part but Channel 4 has not yet received confirmation from the prime minister on whether he will be attending.

Who's taking part?

The leaders of the Labour Party, the Liberal Democrats, the Green Party and the Scottish National Party have so far all confirmed they will be there.

When is it?

Date to be announced. 7pm.

What channel?

Channel 4
