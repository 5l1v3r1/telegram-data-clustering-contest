Drug reform activists protest

23 Nov 2019, 18:18 by Chelsea Powrie

Protesters gathered outside the Victoria Conference Centre Saturday morning to try to push the BC NDP toward more progressive policies with regard to the province's overdose crisis. 

The NDP are holding their 2019 convention there this weekend. Protesters hailed from community activist groups around Vancouver Island, CTV News reports. 

"We're a collective of agencies in the Victoria community who are committed to seeing drug policy changes and an end to the deaths that continue," said Jennifer Howard, who held a sign reading "safe supply saves lives. Do something NOW."

Howard said the goal was to push the NDP to decriminalize drugs and provide a safe supply for those who are addicted. 

"We're asking - and demanding - that they get down to work and pass this resolution," Howard said.

"4,500 British Columbians have died, including my son, since 2016. Many families are mourning the loss of a loved one. We can't continue to sit back and not do more, and the way to end this crisis is to enact safe supply and decriminalization for those who struggle."

Premier John Horgan did not commit to supporting the resolution, only saying they will be "debating" the issue during the convention. 

"We've been working tirelessly," Horgan said. "We've seen a decline in overdose deaths, but it's still not acceptable. We're going to work and look at new therapies, new ideas, new suggestions all the time."

Howard said that while the NDP has expanded services and programs in response to the opioid crisis since taking office, there is much more they need to do. 

"We don't want to see the NDP sitting back, patting themselves on the back saying, 'Our work is done,' around the overdose crisis," she said. "Their work is far from done."
