Man threatened to set fire to nightclub because it didn't sell Red Bull

23 Nov 2019, 18:57 by James Cox

A man has been jailed after he threatened to burn down a nightclub because it didn't sell Red Bull.

Michael Day, 38, screamed: "I'm going to burn every last one of you."

Shush nightclub in Wantage, Oxfordshire, had to be evacuated due to the incident.

Day was set to stand trial for the incident but later admitted to attempted arson and two counts of making threats to kill.

He flew into a rage after bar staff had served him an alternative drink, as they didn't sell Red Bull, on April 28, heard Oxford Crown Court.

Day was thrown out of the club after becoming aggressive.

He went home before returning to the club, where CCTV footage showed him armed with a 20-litre white plastic container party filled with petrol.

He was seen throwing it towards the door and launching himself in the path of the door staff.

Petrol was spilt on the ground and on staff members' clothing before Day flicked a lighter and screamed death threats, the court heard.

Day had a cigarette in his mouth at the time and also shouted racist abuse towards one worker and threatened to burn the nightclub down.

As the smell of the fumes wafted inside the club, all 177 punters had to be evacuated.

After being arrested, Day told police he had drank up to seven bottles of Corona and didn't really intend to start a fire, simply aiming to 'scare people'.

Day's barrister said he couldn't remember the incident.

Judge Maria Lamb jailed him for five years and two months over the "terrifying" incident.
