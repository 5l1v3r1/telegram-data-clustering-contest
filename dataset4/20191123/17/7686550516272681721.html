Steve Cooper's brutally honest assessment of Millwall defeat and Swansea City's home form

Cooper pulled no punches after what was a grim defeat

23 Nov 2019, 17:44 by Ian Mitchelmore

Steve Cooper admits Swansea City will struggle to win matches this season if they replicate the performance levels they showed in the 1-0 loss to Millwall.

The Swans went into the clash with Gary Rowett's side unbeaten in their last three outings, claiming seven points from their fixtures with Cardiff City, Wigan Athletic and Sheffield Wednesday.

But they produced a lacklustre display against the Lions who claimed their first away Championship win of the season thanks to Jed Wallace's stunning 65th minute free-kick.

And Swansea head coach Cooper admits his troops fell well below the levels required against Millwall.

"We certainly fell short, that’s for sure," he said.

"We always look at ourselves first and foremost, and we all know we were not at the level today in any area of the pitch, truth be told. It’s a disappointing day.

"It’s difficult to pay too much attention to form because this league is so indifferent week in and week out.

"The players know too many parts of our game were not where they should have been in terms of performance levels.

"If you do that – unless you are really lucky and get away with it – you will not win games."

It was Swansea's fourth league defeat of the campaign, with each of those coming at the Liberty Stadium.

Cooper's men are also without a win in their first match after an international break this term.

However, despite the poor return of points on home soil, Cooper does not believe his side's home struggled have put added pressure on the players.

"It’s not a good return, those four losses. We know that, but I don’t think it’s about momentum or there being any added pressure. We just did not play well," he added.

"When we did have moments and we did not capitalise on them, and we have conceded a disappointing goal. So, lots of things accumulated into it being a poor performance.

"We have got to be good at dealing with what comes our way and we work to make sure we have solutions.

"They put a lot of bodies around their box, but we have good enough players to play through and around that, but we did not do that.

“If you do not do that you will not create chances and score goals."

Swansea return to action against Huddersfield Town at the John Smith's Stadium on Tuesday night before hosting Fulham at the Liberty Stadium three days later.
