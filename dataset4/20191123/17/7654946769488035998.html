Cops bust teen who stole, crashed car with two kids inside

23 Nov 2019, 17:27 by Ruth Weissmann

The teen car thief who went on a joyride in Queens with two children in the backseat and then crashed into a tree has been arrested, police said Saturday.

The 16-year-old suspect, whose name was not released, swiped the car near Liberty Avenue and 92nd Street in Ozone Park at about 7:45 p.m. Friday night, officials said.

He sped off in the stolen vehicle toward Brooklyn — with two frightened boys under the age of 10 in the backseat, police said.

Shortly after, the teenager lost control of the car, crashing into a tree on Norwood Avenue in Cypress Hills.

The children suffered minor injuries and were treated at the scene, then reunited with their mother, officials said.

The joyriding thief was taken into custody and charged with grand larceny auto, endangering the welfare of a child, obstruction of justice, and two counts of reckless endangerment.

A witness told The Post the rogue teen was speeding the wrong way down a one-way street.

“It was going very fast. It was like Grand Theft Auto,” said witness Kenny Placencio, 27.
