Elon Musk's net worth drops by $768m one day after cybertruck fail

23 Nov 2019, 17:25 by James Cox

Tesla boss Elon Musk has seen his net worth plummet one day after the botched launch of his latest electric vehicle.

Shares in the firm dropped after the embarrassing unveiling of the electric pickup truck.

The supposedly unbreakable window glass on the vehicle shattered twice during a demonstration.

The failed stunt came after Mr Musk had talked at length about the strength of the glass on the futuristic, stainless steel-skinned Cybertruck.

The vehicle was unveiled amid lasers and flames to build up its grand entrance.

Mr Musk said: "You want a truck that's really tough, not fake tough.

"You want a truck you can take a sledgehammer to, a truck that won't scratch, doesn't dent."

He then asked a Tesla designer to hit the door with a sledgehammer, with no resulting damage.

However, when they threw a metal ball at the driver's side window it smashed.

A visibly shocked Mr Musk exclaimed: "Oh my f****** God."

Attempting to save face, he said: "Maybe that was a little too hard."

When the stunt was repeated, the passenger window also cracked.

Mr Musk said: "At least it didn't go through, that's a plus side. We'll fix in in post."

The gaffe became the main talking point of the show's launch while the radical design of the vehicle has also been the subject of jokes.

Deutsche Bank said some "important unanswered questions" surround the vehicle, including whether the design was "too polarising".

As a direct result of the failure, Tesla stocks fell by more than 6%, translating to a $768 million hit to Mr Musk's net worth (to $23.6 billion) according to Forbes.

A year ago Mr Musk's behaviour resulted in a 6% hit to Tesla stock when he smoked marijuana on a live web show.

The Cybertruck has been getting hammered online following the launch fiasco.
