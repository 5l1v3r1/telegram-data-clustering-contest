LIRR tracks were inspected hours before Queens derailment

23 Nov 2019, 17:32 by Jacob Henry and Ruth Weissmann

The tracks where a packed Long Island Rail Road train derailed in Queens — leaving 600 passengers stranded — had been inspected just hours before the transit snafu, MTA officials said Saturday.

Two rear cars of the Jamaica Station-bound train jumped off the track at about 9:30 p.m. Friday night, forcing the railroad to dispatch a rescue train to evacuate the hundreds of trapped passengers, authorities said.

It was not clear what had caused the derailment, which occurred just after the Babylon line tracks were inspected Friday afternoon, LIRR senior Vice President of Operations Rod Brooks told reporters.

“We will go through an exhaustive evaluation and investigation process,” Brooks said. “We do know that there is a rail that was broken. We don’t know whether that was a cause or an effect.”

The tracks are inspected for safety every week, he added.

The ill-fated train had left Penn Station at 8:55 p.m. that night and was traveling at about 13 miles per hour when it jumped the track, the MTA said.

Fuming riders were left waiting over an hour before an evacuation train arrived at about 11:15 p.m.

“We have been trapped on this derailed train for well over an hour now. I’m going to need a refund for my ticket…” tweeted passenger Stephanie Tatton.

No injuries were reported in the mishap, officials said.

The derailment sparked cancellations and delays on the Babylon track, though officials said scheduled service had resumed Saturday and would have no impact on Monday morning’s commute.

“We’ve worked through the night and around the clock,” Brooks said. “There’s no indication of any human error.”
