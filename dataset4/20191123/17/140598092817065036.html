Sean Dyche joked about how former Watford chairman Sir Elton John used to message him, back when the Burnley manager was in charge at Vicarage Road - but might not do after this result.

Quique Sanchez Flores, currently in charge of Watford, should be far more worried about his phone buzzing and an awkward conversation with a member of the club’s hierarchy. 

The pressure on Flores looked to have eased after their previous game - a win at Norwich - but this result has turned the heat back on in terms of the relegation battle.

Beleaguered Watford have now been condemned to 11 consecutive home league games without winning - for the first time in their history - and have slumped back to bottom place.

Dyche’s Burnley finally ended their winless away start to the Premier League season with second-half goals from Chris Wood, Ashley Barnes and James Tarkowski.

Flores said: “After beating Norwich, we didn’t think everything was fixed. But, after defeat here, we don't think every thing is wrong.

“Football is unpredictable - Norwich lost 2-0 to us then beat Everton 2-0. 

“I don't have doubts in the players. But, I knew from the first day, it would be difficult. It is difficult to change things so quick.”

Burnley had lost 3-0 themselves at Sheffield United three weeks ago - but have followed that up with successive 3-0 wins, here and at home against West Ham, and are now sixth. 

Dyche’s men managed their first away league clean sheet since January - when they did it at Watford.

The first period had actually been dominated by Watford but their lack of killer instinct is a major factor in why they are last. In contrast, Burnley kept it tight then broke their opponents clinically. 

Burnley goalkeeper Nick Pope - a week after playing 90 minutes for England - made saves to deny Gerard Deulofeu.

The visitors scored from their first shot on target when Wood hooked the ball in, inside the six-yard box.

Jay Rodriguez struck the bar for Burnley, then the killer blow then arrived when VAR awarded a penalty to Barnes - judging he was caught by Jose Holebas - and the striker struck in. 

Tarkowski then scored at the second attempt after being initially denied by Ben Foster. Deulofeu struck against the bar but, by then, his side had been buried.

Dyche said: “It wasn’t a 3-0 performance but you take the 3-0.”
