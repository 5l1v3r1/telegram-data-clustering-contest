Arsenal 2-2 Southampton: 5 talking points as pressure mounts on Unai Emery

Arsenal's winless streak stretched to five games after they were held by the struggling Saints at the Emirates on Saturday afternoon

23 Nov 2019, 17:00 by Irishmirror.ie

Arsenal were booed off despite striker Alexandre Lacazette's last-gasp leveller against Southampton.

The Gunners' winless run extended to five games to leave them off the pace in the race for the top four.

James Ward-Prowse's late effort looked like being enough to earn the Saints victory before Lacazette's late intervention.

Arsenal had to recover from an early deficit as they were caught out by Ryan Bertrand's quick free-kick and Danny Ings fired Southampton in front.

Lacazette levelled in the 18th minute, though, after strike partner Pierre-Emerick Aubameyang's shot was blocked by Bertrand.

Emery losing support

A changing of the guard up the Seven Sisters Road has brought a new dynamism to the Premier League sacking season.

And Arsenal manager Unai Emery's body language has all the joy of a condemned man prowling the exercise yard the night before he has an appointment with the firing squad.

After nearly 18 months in charge, the only thing Emery does with conviction is to greet the media “Good ebening” - and he is in danger of matching the gendarme's “Good moaning” in spoof sitcom 'Allo 'Allo.

Two points from a possible 12, before the Saints came to town, had left fans teetering on the brink of mutiny – or, even worse, apathy.

Noticeably, there were no chants in support of Emery, no ringing endorsements of his leadership from the posh seats (they are all posh seats at the Emirates).

Xhaka is a symptom, not the problem

Ever since Granit Xhaka was hounded out of the 2-2 draw with Crystal Palace last month, Arsenal's fortunes have not improved.

Despite Emery's pre-match hints that his captain would be phased back into the first team picture, Xhaka was not in the 18-man matchday squad – but his absence did not remove the toxic atmosphere swirling around the Emirates when Arsenal fell behind early.

Picking your captain by conducting a dressing room poll always smacked of a cop-out, a manager shirking his responsibility to make a fundamental decision.

Xhaka's removal from the firing line may not have turned him into a martyr, but he was an expedient scapegoat.

No sympathy, just apathy

By unpopular demand, empty red seats are back in fashion at the Emirates.

The abundance of no-shows was not surprising, given Arsenal's spluttering autumn form, and it was by no means as conspicuous as the half-empty arena which greeted Arsene Wenger's team during the vicious cold snap of March 2018.

But empty seats is never a healthy sign for a Gunners manager under pressure – because every unoccupied place is an expression of apathy, a statement that shopping at IKEA or staying at home is preferable to watching average players in an average team coached by an unloved manager.

Arsenal need more Pepe

Anaemic and disjointed for 45 minutes, Arsenal's performance was galvanised immediately by the half-time introduction of record signing Nicolas Pepe.

When you pay £72 million for a winger, instead of meeting the asking price for Wilfried Zaha, you are entitled to get some bang for your bucks.

And Pepe's arrival in place of Calum Chambers immediately signalled a change of gear, with the Gunners showing more urgency. Pepe's scissor-kick, which bounced down and against the bar, was desperately close to only his second Premier League goal of the season.

Saints staying alive

If Southampton boss Ralph Hasenhuttl was on borrowed time after that record nine-goal mauling by Leicester , this performance must have earned him a stay of execution.

Saints pressed high, they pressed hard, they committed men forward and bravery brought its rewards.

Their 'game management' – yes, that means timewasting – was transparently obvious, but the stats don't lie: They had more shots, better chances and, for long periods, they were better than Arsenal.
