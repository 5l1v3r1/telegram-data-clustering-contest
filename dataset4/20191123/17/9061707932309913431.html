Two Chaddesden pubs closed after a police incident

Fire and ambulance services also reported at scene

23 Nov 2019, 17:34 by Carl Slater

Two Derby pubs were closed on Saturday tea-time after reports of a police incident.

The Toby Carvery at Chaddesden and the Oak and Acorn at Oakwood were both shut.

There were reports that other licensed premises across the Chaddesden area were also closed.

There were claims that police, fire and ambulance had been called to The Toby Carvery, in Nottingham Road, and it had been evacuated.

One witness posted a message just before 4pm which said: "Something big is cracking off now at Toby Carvery.

"12 police cars, fire engine, ambulances, it's been evacuated, and there are loads of people outside all dressed up."

A spokesman at the pub confirmed it was currently shut but could not give any further details.

A second pub confirmed that it had closed.

The Oak and Acorn Sizzling Pub and Grill in Danebridge Crescent, Oakwood, said it was currently shut and no one was available to say when it might reopen.

A spokesman for Derbyshire police said he was aware of no major incidents in the area.
