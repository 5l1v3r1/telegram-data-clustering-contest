Cheryl denies voting for voting Nadine Coyle to do I'm A Celebrity trials

23 Nov 2019, 08:56

Girls Aloud members Cheryl, Kimberley Walsh and Nicola Roberts have denied claims they’re voting for former bandmate Nadine Coyle to take on I’m A Celebrity…Get Me Out Of Here’s Bush Tucker trials.

Nadine is just one of several high-profile names in the Australian jungle this year, including reality TV icon Caitlyn Jenner and EastEnders actress Jacqueline Jossa.

Speaking on claims that she could be voting for the 34-year-old to take part in the trials in revenge for their long-touted feud, Cheryl insisted that it was all love (machine).

‘We only want to see Nadine do well, and regardless of any differences we might have had, we will always all have love for each other,’ she said.

‘We did not enjoy watching her distressed when jumping out of the plane. And we don’t have time for group chats regarding the trials on the show!’

Kimberley also refuted the rumours, insisting that she is watching the show, and her heart goes out to the Insatiable singer.

‘I felt really emotional watching Nadine jump out of the plane and texted Cheryl saying how hard it was to watch. I think she’s doing great in there,’ she said.

Speaking about social media-fuelled rumours that the trio have a group chat dedicated to Nadine’s stint on the show, Nicola added to MailOnline:’We don’t have a group chat about I’m A Celebrity.

‘This just isn’t true. We only wish Nadine the best in the jungle and hope she does well on the show.’

As members of Girls Aloud alongside Sarah Harding, the girls scored 20 consecutive top 10 hits on the UK chart, a record for a UK act. They formed in 2002 on Pop Stars: The Rivals before splitting for good after their tenth anniversary tour.

We previously started our very own deep-dive into the fractured history between Nadine and Cheryl and their apparent, long-standing feud.

The Northern Irish singer has already rubbished claims that she and Cheryl don’t get on, telling campmates in the jungle that the rumours were ‘fake news.’

And even though a Girls Aloud reunion isn’t looking possible at the moment, a new itineration of the girlband may have already been born as Nadine taught some of her campmates the dance routine to the band’s last number one single, The Promise.
