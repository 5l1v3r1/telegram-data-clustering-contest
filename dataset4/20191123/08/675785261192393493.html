James Corden looks amazing as he shows off his one stone weight loss while filming

The funnyman slimmed down to a trim 16st 7lbs from more than 17st earlier this year as he showed off his handsome looks

23 Nov 2019, 08:00 by Lily Waddell, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/lily-waddell/

James Corden looks incredible as he showed off his slimmed-down figure.

The TV host, 41, looked his best when he filmed new scenes for a laugh-out-loud parody of Frozen 2 for The Late Late Show in recent weeks.

Gavin & Stacey's James divulged he had lost more than one stone since the start of this year.

And the results were amazing as he looked handsome while dressed as the Disney character Elsa for the epic TV scenes set to air.

It comes after James previously touched on his weight struggles while he shared his determination to get into shape.

In June, the funnyman slimmed down to a trim 16st 7lbs from more than 17st earlier this year.

He told The Daily Star : “I think the lightest I have been is 212 pounds (just over 15st), something like that. Right now I’m like 234lb (16st 7lb).

“I am not tall enough to be 234lb. And then my biggest I think I was like 280 and I have been also, even this year, I’ve been 250 (more than 17st)."

James admitted bread was his downfall when it come to his weight struggles because he complained it is "everywhere".

He added: "Bread is the absolute destroyer of me and it’s everywhere. You can’t move for it. It’s everywhere. I’d do anything to [stop].”

More recently in September, James hit out at Bill Maher for wanting to bring back "fat-shaming" as he said the TV host was encouraging bullying.

Maher, 63, caused a stir with his controversial view fat-shaming needed to make a "comeback" which riled up the comedian.

He said: "Fat-shaming doesn't need to end, it needs to make a comeback."

James had a witty response for Maher as he reminded him fat-shaming hadn't gone away.

He explained: "We're not all as lucky as Bill Maher, we don't all have a sense of superiority that burns 35,000 calories a day.

"It's proven that fat-shaming only does one thing - it makes people feel ashamed. And shame leads to depression, anxiety and self-destructive behaviour. Self-destructive behaviour like over-eating.

"Fat-shaming is just bullying and bullying only makes the problem worse."
