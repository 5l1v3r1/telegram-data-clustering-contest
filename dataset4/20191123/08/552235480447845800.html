Hamilton boss Brian Rice says his side must starting winning again - starting with Rangers

Rice says Accies have to treat the visit of Steven Gerrard's men like any other Premiership game.

23 Nov 2019, 08:00 by Andy McGilvray, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/andy-mcgilvray/

Brian Rice says Hamilton need to start winning games again – starting with Rangers on Sunday.

Rice knows it’s not going to be easy against Steven Gerrard’s side in the lunchtime show-down, but insists his side must treat it like any other game.

It’s the start of a tough run of four games which also see Accies travel to Celtic Park – with a visit at Livingston in between – before entertaining St Mirren on December 7.

Accies drew 2-2 at Kilmarnock a fortnight ago, despite being 2-0 up, drew with Ross County from a winning position and lost at St Johnstone, having taken the lead.

Rice said: “It’s time for us to start winning games.

“It’s OK drawing, away from home, but we need to try and turn a few of the draws into wins to give ourselves a wee bit of breathing space. Against Ross County we could have got three points, and we would have deserved them, in my opinion, and at Kilmarnock we were 2-0 up and ended up with another draw.

“It’s good not to lose, but we need to start turning some of these draws into wins.

“It’s Rangers on Sunday but we have to treat it like any other game; it’s three points at stake.

“We know how difficult it’s going to be, we’re under no illusions about that, with the season Rangers are having.

“It’s going to be very difficult but we need to come up with a game-plan that gives us a chance of taking something from the match.

“It is a tough run, but we take it as it comes. Rangers, Livingston, Celtic, is very difficult, but we have to try and take something from the games.”

Rice added: “Celtic and Rangers aren’t really our fight. Obviously we will try as much as we can to take something out of these matches, and I’m a great believer that if you set up properly and do things correctly, then you have a chance of getting something.

“We’ve seen how good they are with the goals they’ve scored and their results at home and in Europe, our fight is against the nine other teams.”

Hamilton go into the clash with Rangers minus suspended duo Sam Stubbs and Scott Martin, while there are injury problems, including defender Ciaran McKenna undergoing a ‘tidy-up’ operation on a cartilage problem.

Rice said: “We’re down to the bare bones but there are no excuses. We’re a squad for a reason. If you’re part of the squad you should be ready to play, so we just need to get on with it.”

The game kicks-off at 12.15pm on Sunday and is live on Sky Sports.
