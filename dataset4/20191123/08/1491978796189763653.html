20 photographs of the week

23 Nov 2019, 08:17 by Jim Powell

Escalating violence in Hong Kong, protests in Colombia, Chile and Bolivia, Prince Andrew’s interview and Cleveland Browns’ Myles Garrett losing his cool – the best photography in news, culture and sport from around the world this week

Aegean Sea

Migrants and refugees on an inflatable boat are rescued by members of the Turkish coastguard in the Aegean, between the Turkish resort town of Bodrum and the Greek island of Kos. More than 53,000 migrants and refugees have been intercepted by the Turkish coastguard since January.

Photograph: Burak Kara/Getty Images

Bogotá, Colombia

Police detain an anti-government demonstrator in Bogotá during a nationwide strike. Colombia’s main union groups and student activists called for a strike to protest against the economic policies of the country’s president, Iván Duque.

Photograph: Ivan Valencia/AP

Hong Kong

A protester lowers himself down a rope from a bridge to a highway to escape from the Hong Kong Polytechnic University campus and from the police. Dozens of Hong Kong protesters escaped the police siege at the campus by shimmying down a rope from a bridge to awaiting motorbikes in a dramatic and perilous breakout that followed a renewed warning by Beijing of intervention to end the crisis engulfing the city. Police said the demonstrators inside Polytechnic University had no option but to come out and surrender.

Photograph: Ye Aung Thu/AFP/Getty Images

Hong Kong

An anti-government protester uses a bow during clashes with police at the Hong Kong Polytechnic University. Hong Kong police fought running battles with protesters trying to break a security cordon around the university in the city, firing teargas both at activists trying to escape the besieged campus and at crowds trying to reach it from outside.

Photograph: Athit Perawongmetha/Reuters

Hong Kong

Police detain protesters and students after they tried to flee the Hong Kong Polytechnic University campus. The university siege stretched into a sixth day on Friday as medics warned of a humanitarian crisis and the city prepared for weekend elections that will be a key barometer of public support for protesters.

Photograph: Nicolas Asfouri/AFP/Getty Images

Santiago, Chile

A demonstrator using a street mirror as a makeshift shield takes part in a protest against the government in Santiago. Chilean political parties agreed to a referendum on replacing the country’s Pinochet-era constitution, in a deal that could help end nearly a month of sometimes violent political unrest. Much of the protesters’ anger has focused on Chile’s 1980 constitution, which is seen as the embodiment of Augusto Pinochet’s neoliberal model, prioritising a market-driven economy but failing to adequately guarantee healthcare, education and pensions.

Photograph: Claudio Reyes/AFP/Getty Images

El Alto, Bolivia

Women sit on a traffic barrier, one of many used by supporters of the former president, Evo Morales, to block a highway in El Alto. Morales stepped down following nationwide protests over suspected vote-rigging in the 11 October election in which he claimed to have won a fourth term in office. But his departure, far from resolving Bolivia’s crisis, has only deepened it. After his allies in the legislature resigned, the next in the line of succession was a rightwing Christian who served as deputy president of the senate, Jeanine Áñez.

Photograph: Natacha Pisarenko/AP

La Paz, Bolivia

Supporters of the former president, Evo Morales, take to the streets demanding his return. He resigned last week under pressure from the military after a disputed election that he claimed to have won. Morales supporters have denounced the heavy-handed policing, accusing authorities and opponents of targeting them for being indigenous in appearance or dress.

Photograph: Natacha Pisarenko/AP

London

Emily Maitlis talks to Prince Andrew about his relationship with Jeffrey Epstein in an interview for the BBC at Buckingham Palace. The royal’s defiant Newsnight interview triggered a disbelieving reaction from the public and the media. Critics in the UK and US demanded an apology for his conduct and said that his defence of his actions was simply not credible.

Photograph: Mark Harrison/BBC

Washington DC

US Ambassador to the European Union Gordon Sondland leaves a public hearing on the impeachment inquiry into President Donald Trump at the House permanent select committee on intelligence. Sondland delivered a devastating blow to Donald Trump, testifying about the existence of a quid pro quo with Ukraine and insisting: ‘We followed the president’s orders.’

Photograph: Anna Moneymaker/Pool/EPA

Cleveland, Ohio

Cleveland Browns defensive end Myles Garrett prepares to hit Pittsburgh Steelers quarterback Mason Rudolph with his helmet during the second half of the match. ‘I lost my cool and I regret it,’ Garrett said. ‘It’s going to come back to hurt our team. The guys who jumped into the scrum, I appreciate my team having my back, but it never should have gotten to that point.’

Photograph: Jason Miller/Getty Images

Venice, Italy

People sit in a flooded bar amid rising water levels in Venice. High tidal waters returned to Venice last Saturday, four days after the city experienced its worst flooding in more than 50 years.

Photograph: Andrea Merola/EPA

London

Two women look at an immersive jellyfish installation by Rimini Protokoll in the Eco-Visionaries exhibition at the Royal Academy of Arts. The work, titled win> <win, is a tank of 50 moon jellyfish, one of the few species that actually benefit from the effects of global warming.

Photograph: Hollie Adams/Getty Images

Athens, Greece

Students shout slogans during the annual march towards the US embassy in Athens to commemorate the 1973 student uprising against the US-backed military junta.

Photograph: Louisa Gouliamaki/AFP via Getty Images

North Korea

North Korean leader Kim Jong-un poses with members of the air and anti-aircraft forces of the Korean People’s Army during training at an undisclosed location.

Photograph: KCNA/KNS/AFP/Getty Images

Tal Nasri, Syria

A member of the Khabour Guards Assyrian Syrian militia sits in the ruins of the Assyrian Church of the Virgin Mary, which was destroyed by Islamic State fighters. The few Assyrian Christians who escaped the Islamic State invasion in 2015, and did not choose to emigrate, now anxiously watch Turkish forces advance towards their villages in the province of Hassakeh.

Photograph: Delil Souleiman/AFP/Getty Images

Harare, Zimbabwe

An anti-riot policeman kicks out at a woman as police disperse a crowd gathered to hear an address by the leader of the Movement for Democratic Change Alliance, Nelson Chamisa, at Morgan Tsvangirai House in Harare.

Photograph: Jekesai Njikizana/AFP/Getty Images

Ahmedabad, India

A stray dog stands on a burning rubbish dump on the outskirts of Ahmedabad, Gujarat.

Photograph: Amit Dave/Reuters

Minya, Egypt

A labourer carries a stone block while working late at a limestone extraction quarry site.

Photograph: Khaled Desouki/AFP/Getty Images

Xuyi, China

A foggy day on Tianquan Lake in Xuyi.

Photograph: AFP/Getty Images
