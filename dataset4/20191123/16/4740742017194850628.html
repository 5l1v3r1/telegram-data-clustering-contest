Old care home being sold as a 20-bedroom development opportunity

Hollis Morgan says the property has 'huge potential'

23 Nov 2019, 16:00 by Alexander Brock

An old care home in Clifton is being sold as a 20-bedroom development opportunity with "huge potential".

Hollis Morgan, who is marketing the property, say it presents "scope to convert into eight flats and a detached coach house".

The period property, which previously operated as nursing home, is just a short walk away from Whiteladies Road and is expected to sell for a whopping £1.5million.

The online advert describes the property as an "imposing detached period property occupying a prime position on a substantial plot."

It goes on to say: "The property has over 8,000sqft of accommodation arranged over four floors and retains many of the original period features.

"There are excellent amenities on the nearby Whiteladies Road which provide a wide range of supermarkets, shops, restaurants and pubs.

"A choice of primary and secondary schools are nearby and, in addition, there is excellent access to the city, as well as to Cribbs Causeway and the motorway network."

According to Hollis Morgan, the property is ideally suited for a residential conversion and there is currently a proposed scheme of eight flats as well as a detached coach house.

Alternatively, the estate agents say the property could be converted into a nursery or school.

The property will be auctioned off on Wednesday (November 27) at All Saints Church in Pembroke Road, Clifton.
