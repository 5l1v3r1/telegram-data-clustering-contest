Jose Mourinho apologises to Tottenham fans after West Ham win

23 Nov 2019, 16:15

Jose Mourinho has apologised to Tottenham supporters after he refused to approach the away section after his side’s victory over West Ham on Saturday.

Mourinho got off to a winning start having replaced Mauricio Pochettino earlier this week as Spurs sealed a 3-2 victory against Manuel Pellegrini’s men at the London Stadium.

Son Heung-Min and Lucas Moura had put Spurs two goals ahead in the first half before Harry Kane added a third in the 49th minute.

West Ham scored twice through Michail Antonio and Angelo Ogbonna but left it too late to find an equaliser.

After the match, Spurs’ players went over to their travelling supporters but Mourinho opted to hang back and applaud fans from afar.

‘At the end of the game I didn’t want to go to our fans because I felt I shouldn’t,’ Mourinho explained to Spurs TV.

‘It’s about the players, not about me.

‘Apologies for that, I stayed away.

‘But I appreciate the few thousand who were there behind the team and I think really enjoying and understanding the difficulties and supporting, and probably now as happy as we are.’

Mourinho also revealed that Spurs second goal was a direct result of working on Lucas Moura’s positioning in training earlier this week.

‘It’s not just passion,’ said Mourinho.

‘We did something that we did in training, Lucas arrived in a position as a sequence of a dynamic that we did in training.

And for a coach, for my staff, we thought about it, we trained it and when it happens it’s the best feeling a coach can have.’
