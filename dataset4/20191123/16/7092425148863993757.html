Fans gobsmacked at ‘corrupt’ VAR decision to deny Crystal Palace goal

23 Nov 2019, 16:53 by Buzz Contributor

Liverpool’s Dejan Lovren was deemed to have been pushed by Crystal Palace’s Jordan Ayew as James Tomkins saw his opener from a corner chalked off by VAR.

Liverpool were on the lucky end of another decision from VAR, this time against Crystal Palace at Selhurst Park.

The Eagles thought they had taken the lead just before half time of this afternoon’s Premier League clash.

But the Premier League leaders escaped following the intervention of the technology and officials.

James Tomkins tapped the ball in at the far post from a corner.

However, it was decided that Jordan Ayew had intervened enough in the build up to the goal.

Ayew was deemed to have pushed defender Dejan Lovren over when the ball came in.

Fans of rival clubs were left raging as Jurgen Klopp’s title chasers have been seen to receive some favourable decisions with tight VAR calls this term.

An Arsenal fan tweeted: “liVARpool”.

And another said: “liverpool cheating as per”.

A Man Utd fan said: “Had to be. It just had to be”

A Man City fan: “Name a more iconic duo”.

And another posted: “Let’s not bother. This league is an embarrassment to the sport.”

A Chelsea branded VAR: “Corrupt”.

And another suggested: “See the meeting went well then”.

The call from VAR proved pivotal as Liverpool took the lead shortly after the restart.

Sadio Mane's strike counted at the other end just after the second half.

The Tomkins decision, meanwhile, left Paul Merson seething in the Sky Sports studio as he commentated on the match.
