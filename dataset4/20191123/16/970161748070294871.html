Avengers: Endgame writers explain why Thor survived as they avoided 'bloodbath'

23 Nov 2019, 16:44

Avengers: Endgame writers Christopher Markus and Stephen McFeely have opened up about avoiding a ‘bloodbath’ and allowing Thor to live.

Well, someone had to.

After we tragically lost heroes including Tony Stark (Robert Downey Jr) and Black Widow (Scarlett Johansson), a tiny ray of hope was presented to mourning Marvel fans in the form of the survival of Thor (Chris Hemsworth) and Hawkeye (Jeremy Renner) among others.

Chatting about the surviving Avengers, Stephen explained: ‘We could have had a bloodbath and all six of them gone down swinging, but that seemed even more depressing.’

Um, no thank you.

Chatting about the surviving duo, he added: ‘It’s not necessarily that we knew they’d get more stories, it’s just that…Thor has sacrificed and sacrificed and lost and lost.

‘It’s not a good ending to kill him. To have him finally, reasonably content with himself—and with his current weight, by the way [is more satisfying].’

He went on to tell Vanity Fair: ‘There’s a joy in letting him go off aimlessly into the ends of the universe. And Clint got his family back, and that’s something. That’s the whole reason he was doing it.’

Don’t get us all emotional again. We’ve still not fully recovered.

However, it’s been made clear that the fates of the heroes aren’t always set in stone, as casting director Sarah Finn previously revealed Black Widow was only meant to feature in one film.

And then Scarlett came along.

‘Maybe it would have just been in one or two films,’ Finn told The Hollywood Reporter.

‘But I think her take on the character was so compelling and thoughtful that it grew to where she’s really becoming a selfless leader, which is not where Natasha starts.’

The character went on to become a beloved staple in the MCU, with Scarlett starring in her solo movie Black Widow, which is set for release in May 2020.

We’ll be needing it immediately, please.
