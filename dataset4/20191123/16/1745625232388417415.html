Donald Trump Says The FBI Tried To Overthrow His Government

Kristine Lofgren

Donald Trump said on Friday night that he believes the FBI tried to overthrow his presidency. According to The Guardian, the president claimed that the FBI was “spying” on him during his 2016 campaign in response to a report that surfaced claiming that an FBI lawyer may have altered a document related to surveillance efforts on former campaign adviser Carter Page.

The FBI obtained a warrant to monitor the communications from Page in 2016 and 2017 as part of the Russia investigation. It’s alleged that one individual may have changed some information. While speaking to Fox & Friends, Trump focused on the allegation to repeat his frequent claim that he was spied on and that the Russia investigation is a “witch hunt” against him.

“This was spying on my campaign – something that has never been done in the history of our country,” Trump said. “They tried to overthrow the presidency.”

Trump argued that the situation is a continuation of an attempt to prevent him from winning the election.

“Personally, I think it goes all the way … I think this goes to the highest level,” he added. “I hate to say it. I think it’s a disgrace. They thought I was going to win and they said, ‘How can we stop him?”

The Justice department is conducting a review of the Russia investigation, led by Robert Mueller, which has so far led to charges against six people involved in Trump’s campaign, including Paul Manafort, Rick Gates, Michael Cohen, Michael Flynn, George Papadopoulos, and most recently, Roger Stone. Trump has been accused last week of possibly lying to the FBI during the investigation.

Justice department head Michael Horowitz will likely release his findings on December 9, which are expected to focus in part on the use of surveillance against a former Trump adviser.

Early reports on the findings have trickled in from several news sources and they indicate that the investigation will likely find some mistakes made by some lower-level FBI officials, but nothing egregiously political from higher-up officials.
