Christine McGuinness and kids cheer on Liverpool during Crystal Palace clash

'Come on Red Men'

23 Nov 2019, 16:40 by Sophie McCoid

Christine McGuinness cheered on Liverpool this afternoon, with her three children also getting into the spirit.

Christine s hared a photo of her and the kids dressed in their Liverpool kits, as the Reds took on Crystal Palace away.

The Halewood native and Leo, Penelope and Felicity looked to be enjoying their own game of football before Liverpool kicked off.

The children can be seen holding footballs in the air, while Christine, 31, stood in a makeshift goal in the hallway.

She captioned the cute post: "Kiddies making up their own rules, whatever keeps them happy! ❤️⚽

"Send help, mummy’s in goal!!!.. #YNWA#liverpool@liverpoolfc#ComeOnRedMen#DreamTeam "

Liverpool beat Crystal Palace 2-1 after a last minute goal from Roberto Firmino.

Christine and husband Paddy have been very open about their children's autism condition, regularly sharing updates of how their condition affects their lives.

Earlier this year the family were able to enjoy their first holiday abroad - with the kids wearing their beloved Liverpool kits for the plane journey.

The Real Housewives of Cheshire star has previously talked about how the trip was "six years in the making" and how they practised by taking their children, two of whom have been diagnosed with autism, on a short flight to Southampton.

The trip was a success and Christine posted at the time: "I don’t think it will be long until we have our first holiday abroad!"

And sure enough she posted photos of the holiday tweeting: "Finally, our first family holiday abroad!

"I am so immensely proud of my three babies, they coped so well. Such a magical weekend.

"Lots of photos to follow, sorry for the 'holiday spam'. I am just the proudest mummy!"
