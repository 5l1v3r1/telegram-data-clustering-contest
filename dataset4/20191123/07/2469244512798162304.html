Urgent flood warning to residents and businesses as River Teign levels rise

The Environment Agency has issued a warning for Saturday

23 Nov 2019, 07:49 by Paul Greaves

A flood warning has gone out residents living by the River Teign that their homes will be threatened on Saturday.

The Environment Agency has issued the warning.

It says: "Flooding is expected on Saturday morning.

"Heavy rain falling in the River Teign area will continue throughout Saturday. This is causing the River Teign to rise and river levels will remain high throughout Saturday."

One driver was rescued by firefighters after becoming stuck in a river in Kingsteignton on Saturday morning.

The warning adds.

"Properties and low lying areas around Ventiford Cottages and properties around Ventiford Bridge, School Cottages at Teigngrace, businesses at Teignbridge Level Crossing, Sandford Orleigh Farm and the Exeter Road will start to flood first from around 6am on Saturday.

"River levels will continue to rise and will be at their highest towards midday. Flood waters may be deep and fast flowing in these areas. Residents are strongly urged to take action now.

"Remain safe and be aware of your local surroundings. We will be closely monitoring the situation and this message will be updated as the situation changes."
