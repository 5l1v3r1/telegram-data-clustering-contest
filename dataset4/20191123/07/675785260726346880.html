Two sisters aged 1 and 2 die after being left inside hot car on 30C day

The girls' devastated mother carried their bodies into the family home near Brisbane, Australia, in a desperate attempt to cool their bodies, it was reported

23 Nov 2019, 07:43 by Chris Kitching, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/chris-kitching/

Two sisters aged just one and two are dead after they were left inside a hot car as the outside temperature soared to 31C.

The girls' devastated mum discovered them inside the vehicle and carried them into the family home in a desperate attempt to cool their bodies, according to witnesses.

A neighbour said her granddaughters later saw the sisters' bodies being carried out of the home, near Brisbane, Australia, and they were "soaking wet".

The inside of the car would have been like an oven - experts say the temperature inside a parked car on a warm day can be more than 30C hotter than that outside the car.

Police and paramedics were called to the home in Logan at about 1.35pm local time on Saturday.

The sisters were unresponsive when they were discovered and paramedics desperately tried to revive them.

Police told 9News that the girls died after being left inside the car on a hot spring day.

The sisters' mother found them and rushed them inside in an attempt to cool their bodies, the report added.

A neighbour told the Courier Mail: "My granddaughters saw the babies being carried out of the house soaking wet.

"It is a tragic accident, she was a good mum."

Describing the scene as "tragic" and "distressing", Detective Inspector Mark White said: "Two young children were located at that particular location.

"QAs (paramedics) made attempts to revive those children, unfortunately they were both declared deceased at the scene.

"The two children showed evidence of being exposed to extreme heat."

Police have spoken to the girls' parents.

No-one has been arrested.

Officers cordoned off the home with crime scene tape and forensics officers were brought in to examine the car.

The temperature inside a hot car can be more than 30C hotter than outside the car on a typical Australian summer day, said Australia's National Roads and Motorists' Association.

Within minutes, a child left in a parked car in that kind of extreme heat can become dehydrated and can die from organ failure, it said.
