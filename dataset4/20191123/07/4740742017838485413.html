How your number plate could land you with a £1,000 fine - the rules you need to know

Here are 7 car number plate rules every driver should know

23 Nov 2019, 07:00 by Alexander Brock

Road safety experts have revealed number plate rules which every driver should know.

In a bid to make the country's roads safer, they are encouraging motorists to ensure their number plates are legally sound.

According to LeaseCar.uk, it's becoming increasingly common to see registration numbers displayed with quirky fonts, sizes and even the incorrect gapping.

However, number plate offences could see individuals dealt a hefty fine of £1,000, with the vehicle also failing its MOT test.

A spokesperson from LeaseCar.uk said: “Customising cars can be part of the fun of owning one, being able to choose the make, model and colour to match your needs.

“There is, however, a line which must not be crossed.

"A registration number is essentially a car’s passport – and you wouldn’t change the format of your own.

“They carry lots of important information to do with tax and licencing, and so not following the rules can result in some very large fines.”

These are seven rules every motorist should follow when it comes to number plates:

1. Background

Depending on where your number plates are placed, the background colours should be different.

Those at the front of a vehicle should have a white background, with the rear number plate having a yellow background.

2. Font

There is a standard font which must be used for number plates.

Characters cannot be in italics – as was previously allowed before 2001 – but you can personalise the font, only in making the characters 3D.

3. Visibility

Number plates must be displayed at all times on the vehicle – both front and back – and the letters and numbers must be visible at all times, so make sure they’re kept clean.

Theft resistant plates are available to make it more difficult for them to be removed.

4. Size

In order to ensure number plates are easy to read, there are lots of specific measurements with regards to the individual characters.

They must be 79mm tall and the thickness of the black print 14mm. More details can be found on the government website.

5. Flags

You are allowed to display a flag on the left-hand side of the number plate, but only if it relates to the destination the car’s from.

They must be smaller than 5mm wide. A GB sticker must be displayed if you plan to travel in Europe – a flag on the number plate will suffice.

6. Formation

Each car is issued with a regular formation of two letters, which identifies where the car was originally registered, two numbers which show the age of the car, followed by three random letters.

It is illegal to put plates on a car which makes it look younger than it is.

7. Personalised plates

Personalised number plates can also be purchased. These adhere to all of the above rules, apart from they can follow different formations.

These plates don’t show the age of the car, one of the reasons individuals may choose to buy them.
