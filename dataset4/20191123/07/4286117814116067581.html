Nationalist Congress Party (NCP) chief Sharad Pawar on Saturday said his party didn't support his nephew Ajit Pawar's decision to join a BJP-led government in Maharashtra, and warned his MLAs against offering support to the rebel leader.

On Saturday morning, the nation watched in disbelief as Devendra Fadnavis of the BJP and Ajit Pawar took oath at Raj Bhavan in Mumbai -- a development that went very much against the run of play, for the NCP had been in talks with the Shiv Sena and the Congress for days.

At a joint press conference with the Shiv Sena, Sharad Pawar reminded NCP MLAs of the anti-defection law and said they could lose their membership. He said he'd learned that 10-11 lawmakers had gone with Ajit Pawar, but also that some of them had begun contacting the parent faction.

NCP MLAs present at the press meet said they didn't know they were being taken to Raj Bhavan for a swearing-in ceremony.

Later, Shiv Sena leader Sanjay Raut said Ajit Pawar would be "alone".

Sources have said the NCP could expel Ajit Pawar.

Sharad Pawar said at the press meet that a decision on replacing Ajit as the NCP's legislature party leader would be taken at a later meeting.

Shiv Sena chief Uddhav Thackeray, who spoke after Sharad Pawar, described the morning's developments as a "surgical strike" against Maharashtra and dared the BJP to try and break his party.

The Congress, which was part of the prospective opposition alliance taking shape in recent days, did not have representatives present at the press meet.

Sharad Pawar said a legislature party meeting of the Congress was under way at the time.
