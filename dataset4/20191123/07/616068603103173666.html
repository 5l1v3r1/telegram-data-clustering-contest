More forestry woes

23 Nov 2019, 07:13 by Darren Handschuh

There are more woes for the beleaguered BC forest industry.

CTV News Vancouver Island is reporting forest management company Mosaic plans to temporarily shutter its logging operations.

The company said harvesting operations will cease on Monday, Nov. 25, a move that will impact roughly 2,000 workers.

“We are currently experiencing very challenging pricing and market conditions,” said Pam Agnew, communications manager for Mosaic Forest Management. “As a result, we are shutting down earlier, ahead of a usual winter shutdown.”

The company’s website says it employs people throughout Vancouver Island and Coastal B.C. 

“The temporary curtailment impacts contractors, both union and non-union – approximately 2,000 people across the coast,” said Agnew. 

In the Okanagan, Tolko Industries has recently announced plant closures, slow downs and layoffs.
