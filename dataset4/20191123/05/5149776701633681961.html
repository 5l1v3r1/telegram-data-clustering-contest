Former nurse follows her dream to open vintage tea room in Nottinghamshire

She has taken what she likes when visiting a tea shop and applied it to the business

23 Nov 2019, 05:00 by Lynette Pinchess

A nurse of 20 years has achieved a long-term dream to open her own tea room with vintage crockery, bunting and delicious home-made cakes.

Alice's Tea Shop - named after owner Allison Morris' late grandmother - has already proved popular with villagers since opening this week.

The traditional menu covers breakfast, light lunches, cakes, coffee - and obviously tea, either a pot of Yorkshire tea or one of the loose leaf teas, from English breakfast and Earl Grey to peppermint and spicy chai.

After taking over an empty property, which had been the Village Cafe and Bistro in Keyworth until its closure in June, Allison and her landscaper husband Steve gave it a full vintage makeover with pastel colours, birds and flowers wallpaper, bunting, wooden dressers and upcycled tables and chairs.

Lunches of soup, toasted sandwiches, paninis, sandwiches and jacket potatoes are served. The array of cakes, currently lemon with lavender, Victoria sponge, and carrot, will always have vegan and gluten-free options.

Toasted tea cakes, cream teas, cheese scones and seasonal treats, including mince pies and chocolate log are also there to tempt customers who have just popped in for a coffee.

And what's a tea room without afternoon tea? Here the pre-bookable feast comes with sandwiches with fillings such as smoked salmon and ham, scones with Mr Pitchfork's strawberry and champagne jam and clotted cream, and a selection of cake - with an abundance of tea or glass of fizz.

Allison, who lives in Wymeswold, had been a practice nurse in West Bridgford but quit to look after her sick father. She said the untimely deaths of her grandmother Alice and mother Barbara prompted her to take a leap of faith to live her dream.

"I decided to give up nursing and try something else. This is something I have always wanted to do. I love tea shops, the vintage feel, the crockery and tea pots.

"My grandmother died when I was young but I remember her fondly so I named it after her. I lost my mum Barbara suddenly when I was very young. She was only in her forties so I thought if I don't do it now, you never know what's around the corner.

"I lost my dad a few years ago. I think they would be very proud of me."

Allison hopes the tea room in Main Street will have all the perfect ingredients so that customers leave happy. She said: "I have been to so many that are not that nice and thought I can do that. There should be good customer service, that welcoming feel and a nice selection of local food."

Bacon and sausage comes from RM & J. Green butcher's shop a few doors away, home-produced honey is from Willoughby, Mr Pitchfork's Pickles is based in Nottingham, while produce is sourced from Kerry's Fresh in Cotgrave.

"It is important we keep it as local as possible, We very much want to reduce the plastic and keep that to a minimum," said Allison, whose husband is helping to get the cafe off the ground before returning to his landscaping business.

The 22-seater tea shop, which has created three jobs including one for her long-time friend Hannah Hallett, had a busy first day.

"We keep asking for feedback. People can give us an honest opinion because I have never done it before," said Allison.

"We were asked about takeaway a few times yesterday so that is something we need to think about. People were taking cake home and some people have come in for breakfast cobs to take away today

"It's very changeable if there is something people would like."

Alice's Tea Shop is open Monday to Friday from 8am to 3.30pm and Saturdays 8am to 2pm.
