Birmingham city centre parking space you can use for 95 YEARS - but it costs £50k

It's yours for nearly a century - but it costs more than some houses

23 Nov 2019, 05:30 by Kamal Sultan

It's one of the bugbears of modern city centre life - cruising the streets at slow speed looking for that elusive empty parking space.

But salvation could be at hand with a solution that means one driver will never have to worry about finding a vacant spot in central Birmingham ever again.

There's just one catch - it will cost a bit more than the loose change most drivers have rattling around in the glovebox.

Almost £50,000 to be precise - and that's more than some houses currently on the market.

Because the space up for grabs at Millennium apartments in Newhall Street is on the market at £49,950 - more than some houses on the market in the city.

In contrast, a three-bedroom terrace in Dunstall Grove, Selly Oak, is going under the hammer at auction on December 11 with a guide price of just £5,000.

So what will the buyer get for all that cash?

Exclusive use of space number 74 and a short walk from Snow Hill and New Street stations.

It is guarded by CCTV and there's apartment block concierge at hand to assist with any problems.

A gated secure entrance provides extra security for the - presumably - high-end car parked in the space and the belongings inside.

The 125-year lease began on March 1, 1988 and has 95 years remaining.

That works out to be £1.44 per day for the remainder of the lease.

Parking around the city centre would usually set you back around £3 for two hours.

The new owner will, however, have to pay £50-a-year for ground rent.

The ad on Rightmove stresses no apartment comes with the space but describes it as an “extremely rare opportunity with excellent access to the city area”.
