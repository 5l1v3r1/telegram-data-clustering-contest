Steelers' Anthony Chickillo won't receive NFL discipline for domestic charges, report says

23 Nov 2019, 05:15 by Tom Gatto

Steelers linebacker Anthony Chickillo will not be punished by the NFL for being charged with misdemeanor assault in October, The Athletic's Mark Kaboly reported Friday on Twitter.

The league placed Chickillo on the Commissioner's Exempt ListOct. 20 after his arrest in Pennsylvania on charges he assaulted his girlfriend, Canadian pole vaulter Alysha Newman, during an altercation at a casino hotel. It removed him from the list Oct. 31, a day after the charges were dropped.

Steelers general manager Kevin Colbert said when Chickillo came off the list that the NFL would continue to investigate the matter. The league, according to Kaboly's tweet, determined there was insufficient evidence to punish Chickillo, who could have been suspendedunder the league's personal conduct policy.

Chickillo, a fifth-year player out of Miami,was charged with simple assault, criminal mischief and harassment. Newman was cited for summary harassment, the (Pittsburgh) Post-Gazette reported. Prosecutors in Fayette County, Pa., dropped the charges against Chickillo and Newman after the twoappeared in court separately on Oct. 30.

Chickillo, 26, apologized in a statement Nov. 1 for his involvement in the incident. Newman, 25, said in an Instagram post that "(t)here were no payoffs, no behind the scene deals" for her to drop charges against Chickillo.
