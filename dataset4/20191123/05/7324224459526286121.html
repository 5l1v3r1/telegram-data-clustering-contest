Wolverhampton's HÜDS and West Bromwich's The Pagans S.O.H join Birmingham's MeMe Detroit for single launches

Three former The Star Unsigned acts are uniting to launch their latest singles in Birmingham this month.

23 Nov 2019, 05:00 by Leigh Sanders

Birmingham's queen of grunge MeMe Detroit will lead the night with her new-look band, being joined by West Bromwich's The Pagans S.O.H and HÜDS, from Wolverhampton.

HÜDS will open the night before MeMe, Pagans and London band The Great Malarkey all play equal headline sets.

The gig at the Second City's The Sunflower Lounge is being put on by MeMe's own label and publisher SoulRock Central next Saturday.

HÜDS will be releasing their new single - Copicat. It is their first full release having previously dropped four demo tracks. Produced by Gavin Monaghan at Wolverhampton's Magic Garden Studios it will be released via Fade to Dust Records on all major streaming platforms the day before the gig.

It will also come with their debut music video, filmed by Paul Husband and Trust A Fox Photography.

Black Country Music Award winners this year for Best Live Act, The Pagans S.O.H will be releasing their new track Black Jesus - following on from previous singles Banananah and Pagan Pilgrimage. It was also produced by Monaghan and comes out via Fade to Dust the day before the show.

Me Me Detroit's latest single is Can't Get You Out Of My Mind - something she has dedicated to "all the heartbroken souls out there". It was recorded with Thomas Mitchener and drummer Will Taylor. SoulRock Central Records will release this one the day before the show.

Speaking via her SoulRock Central label, MeMe said: "Join us for the party of the year as three killer acts take to the stage for this triple headline bill. It's all to celebrate and launch brand new singles from The Pagans S.O.H, MeMe Detroit and HÜDS, who are also joining the single launch train.

"This will be a show you won't want to miss as it shakes you to your core and leaves you buzzing for weeks after the final note. Set in intimate venue The Sunflower Lounge, this show has a very limited number of tickets that will sell quickly. Book early to avoid disappointment."

Those tickets can be snapped up via the SoulRock Central website.

All three local acts can be found on Twitter - HÜDS @_HUDSBABY, The Pagans S.O.H @the_pagans, and MeMe Detoit @memedetroit
