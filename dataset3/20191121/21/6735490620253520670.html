Harry Styles Gets Asked If He Has ‘Women Rolling In And Out’ Of His Place

21 Nov 2019, 21:20 by Jamie Samhan

Harry Styles eloquently avoided an awkward question during a recent interview.

While speaking on Australia’s KIIS, Styles was asked about his romantic life.

“When I think of you I think you’re like Vinnie from ‘Entourage’, Vinnie Chase, you’ve got a couple of friends around, you’re doing your thing there’s women rolling in and out,” the co-presenter Kyle Sandilands said. “Is that what your life is like? Or is it a lot more normal than we think?”

“I like to split the two terms of working and not working,” the “Watermelon Sugar” singer responded.

“I’m definitely aware my working life can be extraordinary at times and isn’t the average thing, but I’d say my personal life is pretty normal,” he added.

The answer wasn’t enough for Sandilands who pressed on. “I thought there’d be models and girls fighting [over you], and you’d be like, ‘ladies please, one at a time.’ It feels like such a waste.”

“No, I just don’t do that, I’m sorry,” Styles politely, but firmly said while putting an end to the conservation.

And sure enough, Styles’ fan came to his defence calling out the radio host for making the singer into a “womanizer.”
