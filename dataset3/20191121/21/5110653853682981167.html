T.J. Brodie injury update: Calgary Flames defenseman skates on his own for first time since episode

21 Nov 2019, 21:59 by Tommy McArdle

Calgary Flames general manager Brad Treliving on Thursday issued a statement saying the team is optimistic thatdefenseman T.J. Brodie will return to in-game actionin the near future.

Brodie, 29,collapsed on the ice during a team practice on Nov. 14 and was rushed to the hospital. The team said Thursday that all medical evaluations and tests performed on Brodie in the past week have provenreassuring,but the defenseman has been placed on injured reserve retroactive to the date of the episode.

Still, the Flames confirmed that Brodie skated on his own Thursday under the supervision of medical staff and had been working out for a number of days prior — easing worries about Brodie's health after last week's scary situation.

It was reported Wednesday that Flames head coach Bill Peters said Brodie will not start with the team on an upcoming four-game road trip, but had not yet ruled out his joining before they return for their nexthome gameon Nov. 30.

The team had previously held a press conference with GM Treliving and team doctor Ian Auld on Nov. 15 to explain what they then knew about Brodie's episode.

"Allthe early indications are it’s very likely more related to a fainting episode than something significant and inside the brain," Auld had told reporters.

Team captain Mark Giordano told reporters that Brodie had mentioned feeling "a little bit off, lightheaded maybe" moments before he collapsed and was seen convulsing on the ice. According to Auld, the team's on-ice medical staff reached Brodie within seconds andan ambulance was called "in about 45 seconds" to take Brodie to a local hospital. Those convulsions, Auld said, certainly can becommon with mere fainting episodes — and not necessarily signs of anything more serious.
