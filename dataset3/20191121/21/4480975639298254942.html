Wall Street close: US Benchmarks logging longest streak of declines in months

21 Nov 2019, 21:17 by Ross J Burland
  • The Dow Jones Industrial Average, DJIA, -0.20% lost 54.8 points
  • The S&P 500 SPX fell back 4.92 points.
  • The Nasdaq Composite index COMP dropped 20.52 points. 

US benchmarks slipped again in a continuation of the downside corrections,  losing ground for a third consecutive losing session. Investors were subjected to forever conflicting trade- deal headlines with some reports saying that China is confident of a Phase-One deal and even had invited Washington's negotiating team to Beijing for face-to-face talks despite the Hong Kong bill. Then, in other reports, headlines came quite to the contrary, suggesting China will not do a deal if the HK bill passes into law.

  • Forex today: DXY testing 98 on conflicting US/China trade headlines

Investor appetite soured by South China Morning Post headlines

The news came from the South China Morning Post, in an article entitled, "China watching Donald Trump’s response to US Hong Kong bill as it threatens to become new barrier to trade deal," and bullet points as follows: 

   •  One source says Beijing may decide to ‘fight and talk alternatively’ and is now closely monitoring the US president’s next move following the vote by Congress
   •  China reacted angrily to the proposals, accusing Washington of interfering in its internal affairs, and may feel obliged to respond.

As a result, stocks closed modestly lower with the Dow Jones Industrial Average, DJIA, losing 54.8 points, or 0.2%, to 27,766.29 while the S&P 500 dropped back 4.92 points, or 0.2%, to 3,103.54. The Nasdaq Composite index fell 20.52 points, or 0.2%, to 8,506.21 having closed at its highest levels at the start of the week.
