Slovakia believe they've drawn the toughest possible play-off in Ireland

They host Mick McCarthy’s Ireland on March 26 in a one-off semi-final

21 Nov 2019, 06:00 by Paul O'Hehir

Slovakia reckon they have landed the toughest playoff draw possible.

They host Mick McCarthy’s Ireland on March 26 in a one-off semi-final with the winner facing Northern Ireland or Bosnia in a final five days later.

A draw will take place in Switzerland tomorrow to determine who is at home in that decider.

Slovakia finished third in their qualifying group behind Croatia and Wales and closed out with a 2-0 home win over Azerbaijan on Tuesday.

They only finished a point behind Wales and Slovakia boss Pavel Hapal has bemoaned last month’s home draw with Ryan Giggs’ side.

Skipper Marek Hamsik, the long-time Napoli stalwart who is now with Dalian Yifang in China, knows Slovakia have a tough test on their hands.

“It’s a pity we didn’t qualify automatically but we have to get our heads up to play Ireland,” he said.

“For the playoff, it’s important that we’re all playing regularly for our clubs.”

Hertha Berlin defender Peter Pekarík said: “We’ll be disappointed for a few days that we didn’t qualify automatically but our problem was drawing at home with Wales.

“We have another chance in the playoff but I watched Ireland’s game against Denmark and they played very well - they are a really tough opponent.”

Slovakia boss Hapal claims his side is in transition but insists his players will be fired up for the playoff.

Hapal said: “The team is young and we’re rebuilding but there is quality there. The players will be well prepared for a playoff.

“We’ve a positive atmosphere in the team and we’re gaining valuable experience all the time.”
