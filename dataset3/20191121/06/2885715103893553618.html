Explained: Sweden drops rape probe against Julian Assange. What happens now?

From the beginning, Julian Assange has maintained that the allegations were motivated, and that his arrest in Sweden would mean extradition to the US.

21 Nov 2019, 06:41 by Explained Desk

On Tuesday, Swedish prosecutors said that would be dropping a nearly decade-old rape investigation against Julian Assange, the founder of WikiLeaks who has been under arrest in the United Kingdom since April.

Assange, who lived inside the Ecuadorian embassy in London since 2012 after being granted asylum while on the run from extradition to Sweden over accusations of sexual assault, was arrested by British police in April after Ecuadorian officials revoked his asylum.
