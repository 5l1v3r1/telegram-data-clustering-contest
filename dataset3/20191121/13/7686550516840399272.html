The Tories have created a bogus website called labourmanifesto.co.uk and are using it to attack Jeremy Corbyn

The fake website is appearing at the top of many people's Google search results

21 Nov 2019, 13:06 by Marcus Hughes

The Conservative Party have bought a website called "labourmanifesto.co.uk" and are using it to attack the opponent party on the day of their manifesto launch.

The website, which some commentators suggest may appear to be an official Labour Party domain, has appeared at the top of some people's Google search results as a paid advertisement.

But the content of the site contains criticisms and smears on Labour policies related to Brexit and tax reform.

The website opens on a single page with an unflattering image of Jeremy Corbyn captioned "Labour's 2019 Manifesto".

Below that it reads: "A website by the Conservative Party" followed by "no plan for Brexit", "higher taxes" and "two more referendums".

The whois.net website shows that the domain name labourmanifesto.co.uk was bought on Monday this week.

The Labour Party launched its General Election manifesto on Thursday morning.

The document includes plans for a windfall tax on oil firms, a pledge to make the UK carbon neutral by 2030, and promises to scrap tuition fees and introduce a "real living wage" of at least £10 an hour.

A WalesOnline reporter entered the search term "labour" into the Google search engine shortly after the official launch of the Labour Party manifesto.

The top result, appearing as an advertisement, listed the Conservative-run site along with the line "£2,400 More Tax, Every Year".

Commentators writing on Twitter have suggested the website may be misleading potential voters.

It comes after the Conservative Party were widely citicised for changing the name of their communication HQ Twiitter handle to "factcheck UK" during Tuesday's televised debate .
