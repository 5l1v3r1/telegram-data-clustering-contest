Bengal: 2 lynched by mob in Coochbehar over cattle smuggling suspicion

They were taken to a hospital after they were beaten up by a mob in Coochbehar but due to their serious condition they succumbed to their injuries.

21 Nov 2019, 13:44 by Indrajit Kundu

Two persons died in West Bengal's Coochbehar after they were allegedly beaten up by a mob over a suspicion of cattle smuggling.

The alleged lynching took place on Wednesday at Putimari Fuleswari village in Coochbehar.

The locals spotted the two persons -- Bablu Milan and Prakash Das -- carrying cattle in a van. They were stopped and questioned. When the locals were not satisfied with the answers, they thrashed the two persons and even set their van on fire.

Police from Kotwali police station rushed to the spot and rescued Bablu and Prakash. They were soon taken to a hospital but due to their serious condition, they succumbed to their injuries.

An investigation has been launched into the matter.

The incident comes to light just after the Bengal Assembly passed an anti-lynching legislation.
