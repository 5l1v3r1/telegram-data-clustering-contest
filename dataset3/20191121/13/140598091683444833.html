Euro 2020 play-offs explained: the Nations League route to qualification

21 Nov 2019, 13:06 by Telegraph Sport

When Uefa decided that its 55 members deserved a European Championship finals tournament comprising 24 as opposed to 16 teams for the 2016 tournament and beyond, its initial strategy was to expand qualification in the traditional manner.

Consequently the host, nine group winners and runners-up plus Turkey as the best third-placed team progressed to the finals. The final four spots were decided by play-offs between the other eight third-placed teams and the winners - Hungary, Republic of Ireland, Sweden and Ukraine - made it to France.

Already qualified

Euro 2020 has multiple hosts and, influenced by the spirit of Kraftwek’s Trans-Europ Express, will take place across 12 cities from Baku in the east to Dublin in the west. After the final round of fixtures on Nov 19 and Wales' victory over Hungary in particular, all 20 of the teams who qualify as group winners and runners-up have been determined: England, Czech Republic, Ukraine, Portugal, Germany, Holland, Switzerland, Denmark, Croatia, Spain, Sweden, Poland, Austria, France, Turkey, Belgium, Russia, Italy, Finland and Wales. 

Nations League to the rescue

That leaves four finals places up for grabs and they will be resolved between 16 of the remaining teams, split into four groups of four in which the highest-ranked team in each play-off pot hosts the fourth-ranked, and second hosts third, in one-leg semi-finals on March 26 and the winners of those matches will meet in a do-or-die final five days later.

Eligibility for the play-offs is based on each country’s performance in the inaugural Nations League groups, with a qualifier from each of the four tiers: A, B, C and D. Each team that finished top of their Nations League group was guaranteed a place in the play-offs. If a team with a play-off spot had also qualified by finishing in the top two positions in their Euro 2020 qualifying group, their place goes to the next best-ranked team in their Nations League tier.

So, Switzerland, Portugal, Holland and England, who won the four groups in League A, were guaranteed at least a play-off spot. Since all have now qualified for the Euro 2020 finals, their play-off spots are passed down the line, to those with the next-best record in Nations League A should they need it. If every team in League A qualifies for Euro 2020 through the usual path (and all have apart from Iceland), their play-off positions will cascade down to the best-ranked team in League B that hasn't already qualified or made the play-offs in that pathway and so on from League B to C and League C to D.

The play-off paths

Play-off groups were decided after the final round of fixtures.

We know Iceland are definitely on Path A; Bosnia and Herzegovina, Slovakia, Republic of Ireland and Northern Ireland on Path B and Scotland, Norway and Serbia Path C; and Georgia, North Macedonia, Kosovo and Belarus from Path D. Other teams that have also qualified are all from Path C: Bulgaria, Israel, Romania and Hungary and it is those four who are yet to learn their fate.

Given there is only one open berth in Path C but three in Path A, the four unallocated teams will be drawn - three in to Path A and one into Path C - at Uefa HQ in Nyon on November 22. Only then will Scotland learn who they will be playing at Hampden on March 26 - it could be any one of Bulgaria, Israel, Hungary or Romania. All will be revealed on Friday ...
