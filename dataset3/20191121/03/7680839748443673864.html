Congress will hear today from three more officials set to publicly testify about their knowledge of President Donald Trump’s efforts to strongarm Ukraine into delivering him political dirt while withholding military aid and a White House meeting.

Gordon Sondland, the US’s ambassador to the EU, testified beginning at 9 a.m. ET.

Laura Cooper, a deputy assistant secretary at the State Department, and David Hale, the undersecretary of state for political affairs at the State Department, were scheduled to jointly testify at 2:30 p.m. but their hearing got pushed back to 5:30 when Sondland’s hearing went over the scheduled time limit.

The hearings were broadcast live on C-SPAN and the major cable news networks.

Hale: Ousted ambassador Masha Yovanovitch ‘should have been able to stay at post and continue to do the outstanding work’

Yovanovitch was abruptly recalled from her position in May after what she characterized as a “smear campaign” that Trump and his lawyer, Rudy Giuliani, carried out against her.

Yovanovitch testified last week that she repeatedly requested that the State Department release a statement supporting her amid Trump’s and Giuliani’s attacks but that top brass at the agency refused because they didn’t want to risk angering the president.

In the end, Yovanovitch said, she was removed based on “false allegations by people with clearly questionable motives.” Specifically, she said she was recalled because she wouldn’t help Giuliani pressure Ukraine to deliver Trump the investigations she wanted.

Democratic Rep. Denny Heck asked Hale if he agreed Yovanovitch served with dignity and grace in the face of “the smear campaign.”

Hale replied, “Yes, she did.”

Heck then asked Hale if he agreed Yovanovitch is a “dedicated and courageous patriot.”

“I endorse what you say exactly,” Hale said.

Referring to Yovanovitch’s dismissal, Hale said, “I believe that she should have been able to stay at post and continued to do the outstanding work.”

Hale: State Department advocated ‘for proceeding with all of the assistance’ to Ukraine

Hale said that when he and other State Department officials learned that Trump had ordered the Office of Management and Budget to withhold aid, they vigorously pushed back.

“The State Department advocated, as I did in that meeting, for proceeding with all of the assistance, consistent with our policies and interests in Ukraine,” Hale testified. He was referring to an interagency meeting that took place at the end of July.

“You believed what you said? You believed in the release of the hold?” Democratic Rep. Sewell asked Hale.

“Yes, I did,” he said.

Sewell asked whether anyone at the meeting supported the hold.

“The only agency represented in the meeting that indicated that they supported the hold was OMB,” Hale testified.

Trump’s acting chief of staff, Mick Mulvaney, heads up the OMB and is also one of the key figures in Trump’s push to strongarm Ukraine into delivering dirt while withholding aid.

Mulvaney also confirmed in a news conference last month that Trump withheld aid in part because he wanted Ukraine to investigate a bogus conspiracy theory suggesting it was Ukraine, not Russia, that interfered in the 2016 election.

The conspiracy has no factual basis and stems from a frequent talking point pushed by Russian President Vladimir Putin, who ordered the Kremlin to meddle in the US election.
