Congress says ‘complete unanimity’ with NCP, to meet pre-poll allies, Shiv Sena tomorrow

The briefing on the composition of the government and the architecture will happen after this, says Prithviraj Chavan.

21 Nov 2019, 11:37 by Sandeep Phukan

Congress leader and former Maharashtra Chief Minister Prithviraj Chavan said on November 21 that discussions between the NCP and his party were over and that there was “complete unanimity”.

Both the parties will meet pre-poll allies in Mumbai on November 22, and then the Shiv Sena. The briefing on the composition of the government and the architecture would happen after this, he said.

Top Congress leaders and core members of the Congress Working Committee (CWC) met on Thursday at party chief Sonia Gandhi’s residence over the Common Minimum Programme (CMP) to support a Shiv Sena-led government in Maharashtra.

On Thursday, The Hindu reported how the use of the word “secularism” was proving tricky for the parties to come to an agreement.

Sources claimed that the Congress was insisting on the inclusion of ‘secularism’ in the CMP, while the Sena does not want it so.

NCP chief Sharad Pawar is learnt to have suggested that the CMP could simply refer to abiding by the Preamble of the Constitution without explicitly mentioning it.

“Yesterday’s [Wednesday] meeting was on CMP and leaders of the NCP and the Congress have said the deliberations were positive. In a day or two, the three parties will reach a consensus. Maharashtra will get a stable government led by the Shiv Sena before December,” Sena MP Sanjay Raut told reporters.

The formulation on secularism is going to be a crucial one for the Congress as one of its allies in Kerala, the Indian Union Muslim League (IUML), met Ms. Gandhi over the possible tie-up with the Sena.

On Wednesday, for the first time, the Congress-NCP alliance said that they in-principle agreed to be part of a Sena-led alliance.
