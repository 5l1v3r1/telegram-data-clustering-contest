Coldplay will not tour for two years due to environmental concerns

'Our dream is to have a show with no single-use plastic and have it largely solar-powered'

21 Nov 2019, 11:49 by Howard Lloyd

Coldplay have announced they will not go back on tour for the next two years until their concerts are more environmentally friendly.

Lead singer Chris Martin, who grew up in the Exeter area, says it is the band's dream to 'have a show with no single-use plastic and have it largely solar-powered'.

"We’re taking time over the next year or two to work out how our tour can be not only sustainable but actively beneficial," the 42-year-old singer said.

"All of us in every industry have to just work out what the best way of doing our job is.

"How can we harness the resources that our tour creates and make it have a positive impact? Our dream is to have a show with no single-use plastic and have it largely solar-powered.

"I think it is a question of accepting you have to do your best and not be over-zealous of criticising others because everyone will catch up if you prove that it is easy to do it the right way."

The band - who are currently in Jordan performing - hit the headlines last month after unveiling the track listing for their latest album "Everyday Life" in a series of local newspapers, including the Express & Echo.

The album is due for release tomorrow.

The ad, which appears on page 50 of Thursday’s paper, shows a list of tracks underneath the two sections of the new album.

Chris was educated at Hylton School and Exeter Cathedral School, which is where he found his passion for music, and keeps close ties to the area.
