'Rest in perfect peace my mate' tributes paid to man, 24, killed in horror crash

The man has been named locally as 'Connor'

21 Nov 2019, 11:29 by Max Clements

Friends and loved ones have paid tribute to a 24-year-old man after he was killed in a horror crash.

Police were called to the scene, shortly after 4am on November 20, after a car flipped on to its roof in a crash on Moss Lane , Burscough .

A white Audi A1 is believed to have crashed into a wall and flipped over during the incident, which closed the A59 Moss Lane temporarily closed.

Lancashire Police has today confirmed that the car had earlier been stolen from Eccleston, near Chorley .

The driver, a 24-year-old man from the Sefton area died at the scene, despite the best efforts of the emergency services.

An 18-year-old, understood to have been a passenger in the car, was taken to hospital and is today still described as in a critical condition.

On social media many have paid tribute to the driver, who has been named locally as 'Connor' and offered their condolences to his family.

Following the crash Lancashire Police put out an appeal to trace aÂ  red Abarth car which they believe was travelling in a convoy with the Audi.

The car is thought to have been traced in the Merseyside area.

Any witnesses or anyone with information is urged to come forward.

Sgt Paul Maguire said: "Firstly my thoughts are with the family and friends of both of these men at this incredibly distressing time. We have specially trained officers supporting them.

"I am asking anyone who saw the Audi and also the Abarth travelling in the area, or indeed the collision itself, to come forward. Similarly, if you have any dash cam footage that could assist our investigation, please make contact with us.

"Lastly, I would like to directly appeal to the people who were in the Abarth to speak to the police. A man has died and another is extremely ill in hospital so it's important we find out exactly what happened."

Anyone with information can call 101 quoting incident reference 114 of November 20th.
