I'm A Celeb's Andy Whyment faces first Bushtucker Trial

21 Nov 2019, 11:07

Andy Whyment might be a late arrival to the I’m A Celebrity… Get Me Out Of Here! jungle but he’s certainly not getting an easy ride.

In scenes due to air tonight, the Coronation Street star is put through his paces as he’s confronted with his first-ever Bushtucker Trial, which sees him trapped in a shed packed with ‘loads of guts and worms’ and with pigeons thrown in for good measure, because why not?

Named the Frontier Of Fear, Andy, who’s joined the show alongside EastEnders’ Cliff Parisi, was tasked with finding stars around the house of horrors while presenters Ant McPartlin and Declan Donnelly observe from a safe space.

‘How does it smell?’ Ant asked from a distance.

‘Absolutely disgusting,’ replied Andy. ‘This absolutely stinks.’

‘Everything is covered in offal and fish guts,’ Dec added as a nice little welcome reminder.

But that wasn’t the only obstacle as there were also birds flying over Andy’s head.

‘There are 25 pigeons in there with you,’ Ant revealed.

‘I bet you’re having a right good laugh out there aren’t you?’ Any replied.

Andy’s challenge comes just one day after Adele Roberts and Roman Kemp faced camel anus and vomit fruit for their Bushtucker Trial.

Each campmate was presented with a different ‘dessert’ which they had to scoff to win meals for the camp, with Roman taking on a meal of dead cockroaches, crickets and mealworms, eating three of each to gain a star for camp.

He described the crickets as being like ‘quite cheesy, cheesy eggs’ which sounded pretty grim.

Adele was then given vomit fruit to eat, and as she gagged and retched, she said: ‘It’s like your dog’s been sick and you have to eat it.’
