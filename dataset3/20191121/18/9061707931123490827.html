Update on sexual assault investigation in Derbyshire park

Can you help police with their investigation?

21 Nov 2019, 18:01 by Rachel Sutherland

A man has been arrested in connection with a sexual assault alleged to have taken place in a Derbyshire park in the early hours of the morning.

Derbyshire police officers were called to reports of a sexual assault in St Giles Park, in Sandiacre, at 1am on the morning of Thursday, November 21.

Derbyshire police were at the scene this morning and a section of the park next to a children's play area was cordoned off.

Here's how we covered the incident earlier here.

The victim was not physically harmed during the incident and officers believe it was an isolated event.

Officers would like to hear from anyone who was in the area between midnight and 1am and anyone who may have information that could help them with their investigation.

If you have any information, you can contact police on 101, quoting reference number 39-211119.

Alternatively, you can call Crimestoppers anonymously on 0800 555 111.

Derbyshire police can also be contacted via their Facebook or Twitter pages.
