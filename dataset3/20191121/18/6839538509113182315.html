WA Police unveil dual-trained police dogs for catching criminals and detecting drugs

21 Nov 2019, 18:00

He might look cuddly but this nine-week-old Belgian Malinois puppy is the newest weapon in crime-fighting for WA Police.

If he makes the cut, Joker will be one of WA Police’s unique dual-trained police dogs — able to track and catch offenders and detect drugs.

“Other police forces around Australia are looking at our program here, some of them quite enviously, and they’re seeing the benefits of dual-trained dogs,” Police Minister Michelle Roberts said.

Seven-year-old PD Whiskey will be the first dual-trained dog to help to fight crime when he is deployed in Kalgoorlie with his handler, First Class Constable Barry McDonald later this year.

“You need a really close bond, but he also needs to know that I’m in charge,” he said.

“We work in dangerous environments so I need to know that he’s got my back and I’ve got his.”
