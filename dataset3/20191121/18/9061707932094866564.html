'Different' - Preston boss Alex Neil offers his take on Derby County struggles

Derby County latest - The Rams have endured a tough start to the season and this weekend go up against a high-flying Preston North End

21 Nov 2019, 18:00 by Jamie Kemble

Preston North End boss Alex Neil has described Dery County as 'different from last season'.

Neil's high-flying Lilywhites take on the Rams on Saturday afternoon at Pride Park.

Preston head into the clash high in confidence, sitting second in the table, just two points behind leaders West Bromwich Albion.

Meanwhile, Derby are down in 16th and with just one win from their last three games.

The transition has proved difficult for the Rams after the arrival of new boss Phillip Cocu in the summer, and they look a long way from the play-off final side of last season.

But why have they struggled? Preston boss Neil had his say during his press conference this week.

He said: "They are different from last season, they have lost a lot of their loan players which was always going to make it difficult for them.

“I think if they had the same players they had last year they would be higher up in the league now.”
