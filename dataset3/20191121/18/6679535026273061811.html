102 shops sealed for not paying rent arrears

21 Nov 2019, 18:24 by Staff Reporter

The Salem Corporation on Thursday sealed 102 shops at various places in its limits as the shop owners failed to pay rent arrears within stipulated time as directed by High Court recently.

The corporation in 2016 revised rents and deposit rates of its commercial spaces and existing tenants moved the High Court against the price revision and the Corporation later reduced its rate by 25%. According to officials, it has not revised its rates since 2007.

Hearing the case, the Court on October 15 permitted Corporation to collect the revised rates and advised tenants to pay arrears for 36 months within four weeks of receipt of copies of judgment. According to Corporation officials, it would get ₹17.86 crore in the form of arrears. The Court also permitted the corporation to conduct fresh auction for shops if they failed to pay the arrears within stipulated time.

On Wednesday, Corporation officials sealed 11 shops in New Bus stand and Sathram areas, 56 shops at Sandhapettai and 36 at Kunduchetti lake premises. From these areas, the Corporation has also collected ₹ 1.20 crore in the form of arrears from 33 shop owners.
