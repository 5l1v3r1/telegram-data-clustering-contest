Myles Garrett Accuses Mason Rudolph Of Using “Racial Slur” Before Fight

21 Nov 2019, 18:33 by Andrew Holleran

Cleveland Browns defensive lineman Myles Garrett has reportedly accused Pittsburgh Steelers quarterback Mason Rudolph of using a racial slur leading up to last Thursday’s melee between the two teams.

Garrett slammed his helmet into Rudolph’s head at the end of last week’s Browns-Steelers game. The two players were engaged in a scuffle on the ground with time running out in Cleveland’s win. Garrett has been suspended indefinitely by the NFL.

ESPN is reporting that Garrett accused Rudolph of using a racial slur. This accusation reportedly came during his appeal hearing.

In an appeal hearing with the NFL on Wednesday, Cleveland Browns defensive end Myles Garrett alleged that Mason Rudolph called him a racial slur just prior to last week’s brawl on “Thursday Night Football,” sources told ESPN’s Josina Anderson and Adam Schefter, an accusation the Pittsburgh Steelers quarterback strongly denies.

Rudolph apologized for his role in the brawl on Wednesday, though he denies using a racial slur.

“Mason vehemently denies the report of being accused of using a racial slur during the incident Thursday night in Cleveland,” a Steelers spokesman said in a statement. “He will not discuss this accusation any further and his focus remains on preparation for Sunday’s game against the Cincinnati Bengals.”

Rudolph said he should have handled things better while speaking to reporters on Wednesday.

“I should’ve done a better job handling that situation,” Rudolph said. “I have no ill will towards Myles Garrett. Great respect for his ability as a player. And I know that if Myles could go back, he would handle the situation differently.

“As for my involvement last week, there’s no acceptable excuse. The bottom line is I should’ve done a better job keeping my composure in that situation and [not] fall short of what I believe it means to be a Pittsburgh Steeler and a member of the NFL.”

Rudolph’s agent has since accused Garrett of lying.
