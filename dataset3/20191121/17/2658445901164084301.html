INAC expo toast of exhibitors - The Nation Newspaper

21 Nov 2019, 17:35

Ozolua Uhakheme

As this year’s International Art and Craft Expo (INAC) enters its day two, exhibitors from about 30 countries and 11 states of the federation have opened fresh vistas for the promotion of art and crafts.

FCT Exhibition Pavilion, venue of the 12th International Arts and Crafts Expo holding in Abuja, has transformed into a big expo ground recording impressive turnout of participants.

The participants are displaying unique art and crafts while organisers of the expo, Natonal Councol for Arts and Cilture provides free skill acquisition and free medical services to members of the public.

The Exhibition Pavilion which has become a beehive of activities since the Expo opened on Wednesday, is designed to showcase, entertain and impart skills in 4 days of non-stop fiesta.

Since it opened on Wednesday, INAC has registered an unprecedented number of Nigerians and foreign nationals who are storming the venue to witness the best of art and craft from every continent of the world.

Conceptualised by the Director-General, National Council for Arts and Culture, Otunba Segun Runsewe in 2017, this year’s INAC will also feature special country days in what has become an annual feature aimed at adding extra finesse to the event. On Thursday, the Peoples Republic of China had its day marked with musical performances by Chinesè artists as well as students of secondary schools in Abuja.

INAC is one of the flagship programs held annually by the National Council for Arts and Culture in what has become a major Exhibition that seeks to replicate other global trade fares.
