Quiz your parliamentary candidates at Tiverton & Honiton hustings events

The Halberton Community Association is organising hustings which will be held on Saturday, November 30

21 Nov 2019, 17:34 by Lewis Clarke

The contenders for the Tiverton and Honiton constituency are set to face each other in a Halberton hustings.

The Halberton Community Association is organising hustings which will be held on Saturday, November 30, 2019, at Halberton Village Hall.

On the night, all the parliamentary candidates in the Tiverton and Honiton Constituency will be present to answer questions, including the sitting MP Neil Parish (Conservative Party), plus Colin Reed (Green Party), Elizabeth Pole (Labour Party) and John Timperley (Liberal Democrat Party).

George Parker, political editor of the Financial Times, will chair the event.

The Village Hall will be open from 6:00 pm with the event beginning at 7:15 pm when those who have booked via  hca@halberton.org  will be admitted. Those without priority admission will be admitted from 7:30pm if there is room. The hustings will begin at 8:00 pm.

Questions from the audience to all of the candidates, submitted in advance through  hca@halberton.org  and vetted by our HCA Hustings Sub- Committee will then be heard. 

The event will finish at around 9:30 pm.

Terrance Payne from the HCA said: “The address of the Halberton Village Hall is 18 High Street, Halberton, Tiverton EX16 7AF.

“Parking is available behind the Village Hall. Candidates may wish to arrive and depart earlier or later than the public and are invited to park opposite the Hall in Chapel Close - courtesy of the residents - to make this easier. If the hall car park is full, there is parking available on Greenway.

“We look forward to welcoming the public, candidates, agents and other members of the parties who will wish to support the event. Please advise everyone to book their seats in advance by simply emailing  hca@halberton.org  as soon as possible.”

Speaking about the chair of the evening, George Parker, Mr Payne added: “He has covered British politics at Westminster since 1990, with a front-row seat in the political demise of Margaret Thatcher, the turbulent John Major years, the Blair-Brown era and David Cameron’s coalition. Prior to taking his current job in 2007, he was the FT’s bureau chief in Brussels, in charge of covering European Union politics.

“George grew up in a farmhouse in Devon and went to Tiverton comprehensive school. He read Geography at Queen Mary’s University of London. He previously worked on the Western Morning News and nowadays, as well as working for the FT, he presents BBC Radio 4’s Week in Westminster and What the Papers Say.

“George lives in Kew with his wife Gabrielle and three children. He loves cycling, football and cricket and supports Crystal Palace FC and Somerset cricket club.”

Before the Halberton hustings, there will be a climate and biodiversity emergency debate in Honiton on Thursday, November 28.

Residents will have the chance to ask what action their prospective political representatives are proposing or supporting to best protect the interests of the local community, and the wider world.

You can submit questions for the candidates to answer on the night by emailing: climatehustings@gmail.com
