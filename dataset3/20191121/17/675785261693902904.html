Sarah Ferguson pictured dashing to palace for 'crisis summit with Prince Andrew'

The Duchess of York has previously shown support for her ex husband the Duke of York by calling him a 'true and real gentleman' ahead of his Newsnight interview

21 Nov 2019, 17:54 by Danya Bazaraa, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/danya-bazaraa/

Sarah Ferguson has been pictured arriving at Buckingham Palace where it is reported she is having a 'crisis summit' with Prince Andrew.

The Duchess of York dashed to the palace after the Duke, who was earlier seen in public for the first time since announcing he would be stepping away from royal duties.

Fergie appeared to be in good spirits as she smiled and chatted to a Metropolitan Police officer this afternoon.

Photos showed her in the back of a car after being driven to the palace.

The Duchess of York has previously confirmed her support for her 'true gentleman' ex-husband.

In a tweet the day before he gave his famous interview about the Jeffrey Epstein scandal, she wrote: "It is so rare to meet people that are able to speak from their hearts with honesty+pure real truth, that remain steadfast and strong to their beliefs.

"Andrew is a true+real gentleman and is stoically steadfast to not only his duty but also his kindness + goodness @TheDukeOfYork."

Today she is attending a 'crisis summit' with the Duke, according to the Daily Mail.

Fergie's comments came ahead of the BBC Newsnight special in which journalist Emily Maitlis sat down with Prince Andrew and quizzed him over his friendship with paedophile Jeffrey Epstein.

Days after the interview, the Duke of York yesterday announced he would be quitting royal duties for the "foreseeable future".

Less than 24-hours later, he was pictured smiling and waving behind the wheel of his car.

Andrew, wearing a suit and tie, was snapped leaving his home in Windsor this morning as the fallout from the Royal Family's unprecedented crisis continued.

The Duke will no longer receive his £249,000 royal allowance which is taxpayers' money from the Sovereign Grant and he has been ditched by a number of companies and charities.

He has been forced to step back from his more than 200 charity patronages.

A senior Palace source confirmed to the Mirror that the Duke had vowed to “not return to public life until the Epstein case is closed or he has cleared his name”.

The palace insider said “that could be a considerable amount of time”.

Following Saturday night's interview, Andrew was criticised for showing a lack of empathy towards Epstein's alleged victims and a lack of remorse over his friendship with the pervert, who killed himself in a New York prison while facing child sex trafficking charges.

Prince Andrew has repeatedly denied claims that he had sex with Epstein accuser Virgina Giuffre (formerly Roberts) on three occasions when she was a teen.

Prince Andrew's statement in full last night said: "It has become clear to me over the last few days that the circumstances relating to my former association with Jeffrey Epstein has become a major disruption to my family's work and the valuable work going on in the many organisations and charities that I am proud to support.

"Therefore, I have asked Her Majesty if I may step back from public duties for the foreseeable future, and she has given her permission. 

"I continue to unequivocally regret my ill-judged association with Jeffrey Epstein.

"His suicide has left many unanswered questions, particularly for his victims, and I deeply sympathise with everyone who has been affected and wants some form of closure.

"I can only hope that, in time, they will be able to rebuild their lives. Of course, I am willing to help any appropriate law enforcement agency with their investigations, if required."
