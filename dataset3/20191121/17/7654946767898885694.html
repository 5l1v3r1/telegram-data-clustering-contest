Ephraim Sykes cast as ‘MJ’ in Broadway’s Michael Jackson musical

21 Nov 2019, 17:46 by Barbara Hoffman

Broadway found its Gloved One — and he’s coming fresh from the Temptations.

Ephraim Sykes, currently kicking up his heels in Broadway’s “Ain’t Too Proud — the Life and Times of The Temptations,” will play Michael Jackson in the upcoming musical “MJ,” producer Lia Vollack and The Michael Jackson Estate announced Thursday.

It’s a solid choice. Sykes, who played the impossibly limber Seaweed on “Hairspray Live!,” snagged a Tony nomination for his performance as David Ruffin, the microphone-twirling tenor of the Temps.

“I’ve never been somebody who really loved the spotlight,” Sykes told Gold Derby when he was cast as the cocky Ruffin. With any luck, he’s learned how before he slips on that glove.

“MJ The Musical” starts previews July 6 at the Neil Simon Theatre, where it opens Aug. 13. Two-time Pulitzer Prize winner Lynne Nottage is writing the book. Given the recent and shocking Jackson documentary, it should be quite a challenge.
