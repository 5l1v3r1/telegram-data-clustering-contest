Channing Tatum and Jenna Dewan’s divorce finalised

21 Nov 2019, 07:52

Channing Tatum and Jenna Dewan are officially free from each other as a judge has finalised their divorce almost two years after their split.

The former couple, who married in 2009 three years after meeting on the set of Step Up, announced their split in April 2018.

It’s now reported that a judge has granted their request to legally separate while they discuss custody agreements and a property settlement. Channing and Jenna recently filed papers asking for the divorce so that they have the option to settle down with their new partners.

According to TMZ, documents filed by Jenna’s lawyer Samantha Spector indicate the divorce judgement was announced on Tuesday.

The former spouses now have to work out a custody agreement for their six-year-old daughter Everly whom they are currently co-parenting.

Jenna, 38, was the first to file for the divorce.

Both Channing, 39, and Jenna have moved on with other people – the Magic Mike actor is currently dating British singer Jessie J, while his ex-wife is in a relationship wit Shameless actor Steve Kazee, and is currently pregnant with their first child.

The divorce has come at the right time for Jenna, who reportedly wants to marry Steve, 43.

A source told E! News: ‘Jenna plans to marry Steve and is so in love with him. They have had many long and open talks about that and it’s definitely the plan.

‘What matters most though is that she and Steve are fully committed and so excited to have a baby and grow their family. She knows they will be engaged and married soon enough.’

Jenna opened up about her split from Channing in her new wellness book, explaining: ‘I’d come to realise the dynamic I was in wasn’t serving me nor was it serving my daughter.

‘First and foremost I had to accept the realisation this isn’t working and had moved into hurting. The public saw Channing and I in this idealised romanticised light. This made things difficult for me because I like being as honest and real as I can.’
