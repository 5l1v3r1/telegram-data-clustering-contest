DAN THE MAN

Hearts set to move for Stendel with MacPhee to become sporting director

21 Nov 2019, 07:55 by Andy Devlin

DANIEL STENDEL has emerged as the new favourite to become the next Hearts manager.

The 45-year-old German tops Jambos owner Ann Budge’s wish-list after an exhaustive round of interviews.

It’s understood the Tynecastle chief is finally ready to make her move, three weeks after Craig Levein’s sacking.

Current caretaker boss Austin MacPhee is set to move upstairs to become the new sporting director.

SunSport revealed that Hearts were eyeing a foreign candidate.

Former Barnsley boss Stendel is that mystery man and the Jambos are prepared to spend big to get him.

He is on gardening leave following his dismissal from Oakwell last month — a sacking roundly condemned by fans.

Budge has previously stated she’d be willing to pay compensation to get the right man in.

A six-figure fee could be required to buy out the remaining seven months of his Tykes contract.

Stendel must also now decide whether he wants to resurrect his career north of the border.

The German, who grew up close to the Polish border and spoke Russian as a kid, had a successful stint as manager of Hannover before moving to Barnsley in June 2018.

He was a popular figure with the club’s fans, who even threw a party for him after he left the club.

Stendel is just one of several candidates the Jambos spoke with, including Stuart McCall, Neil McCann and Steve Cotterill.

However, the early favourite, Motherwell manager Stephen Robinson, has not yet been approached.

MacPhee will continue in the role of interim manager for Saturday’s visit to Kilmarnock.

Meanwhile, defender Craig Halkett has handed the Jambos a boost by returning to training.

The former Livingston captain has not played since hobbling out of the 0-0 draw at St Mirren on September 28 with a knee injury.

Saturday’s trip to Rugby Park is likely to come too soon for the 24-year-old, but the following week’s visit to Ibrox is a more likely target.

Halkett joined Hearts during the summer on a three-year deal and has made 12 appearances so far this season, scoring four goals in the process.

The defender’s return will be welcomed by MacPhee as Hearts’ injury list finally shows signs of easing.

Scotland striker Steven Naismith and playmaker Jamie Walker were among those who returned to first-team action before the international break.

However, John Souttar, Conor Washington and Ben Garuccio all remain sidelined for now.
