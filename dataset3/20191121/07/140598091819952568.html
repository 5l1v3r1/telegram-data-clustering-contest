British Airways passengers hit with delays of 'up to 22 hours' after 'technical issue'

21 Nov 2019, 07:00 by Gareth Davies

British Airways passengers have claimed to have been hit with delays of up to 22 hours after the airline struggled to deal with what it described as a "technical issue". 

Flights to and from the UK's two busiest airports, Heathrow and Gatwick, were among those worse affected.

Some 97 flights to and from Heathrow were more than 45 minutes late, and two were cancelled. A further 17 Gatwick flights were also delayed by the same length of time.

The worst was Flight BA170 from Pittsburgh, in the US, which was more than 12 hours behind schedule.

Responding to Matthew Perry, who said on Twitter he had been delayed by 22 hours and was left "13 hours from home", British Airways said: "We're sorry for any disruption. Our teams are working hard to resolve a technical issue which is affecting some of our flights, and we are rebooking customers onto alternative flights and offering hotel accommodation where they are unable to continue their journeys tonight."

Mr Perry was one of dozens complaining to the airline on Twitter, which by Thursday morning had become littered with angry messages directed at the airline.

Another passenger, giving his name as Paul, said he had his flight from Gatwick to Orlando cancelled without warning. 

He said: "So, no update from BA on here aside from replies to other people stating a 'technical issue', but no mention of what that issue is. Some more detail would make people less angry.

"Getting no reply when you reply to others faster, having to go to the airport to sort and having to find out more information via newspapers isn’t very good for a company the size of BA. Almost as if they don’t want you to know what the problems actually are."

It is the latest in a long line of damaging incidents for the airline which has seen it slide down the airline rankings.

Rory Boland, editor of Which? Travel magazine, said: "This is the latest in a long line of British Airways technical glitches causing delays and cancellations and yet again it's thousands of passengers who are paying the price - left tired, frustrated and with a lack of information and assistance from the airline.

"BA must do the right thing and reroute passengers as quickly as possible, using other airlines where necessary, as well as informing customers facing disruption about their entitlement to compensation."

The passengers stranded miles from home

Those trying to get home to loved ones have been reduced to messaging British Airways directly on social media in a desperate attempt for answers. 

Anger appears to be building, with the airline directing those delayed to a news page of its website with no general update on flight status nor an explanation of what is happening at its airport. 

Many of the responses ask the passengers to privately message British Airways, a common tactic used by companies who do not want their dirty laundry aired in public.
