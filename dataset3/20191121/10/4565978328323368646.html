Shrimp Breeders of "Colombia" Fulfill this Year’s Commitment

21 Nov 2019, 10:24 by Arbelio Alfonso González

The shrimp farmers of the SanRos basic business unit, in the southern municipality of Colombia, fulfilled the capture's plan of the crustacean for the current year more than a month before the scheduled date.

"Colombia", Las Tunas.- Reymundo Mendoza, director of the entity, said that despite the climatic adversities and the worsening of the U.S. blockade, the conversion factor was improved, which translates into the use of less food, and consequently the decrease in cost per ton produced.

Another favorable indicator was the survival rate in the ponds, which reached 75 percent, while the use for export reached 63, which represents higher incomes for the country.

The capture will continue until mid-December, which allow exceeding the 610 tons planned and provide an extra amount to the national program. The result was dedicated to the anniversary of the Triumph of the Revolution, an event whose provincial celebration will take place in this municipality.
