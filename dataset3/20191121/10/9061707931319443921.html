Burton firm's giveaway pledge to people with homes left devastated by floods

Bosses were moved by the story of a Repton woman whose home was left inhabitable

21 Nov 2019, 10:51 by Ben Waldron

A Burton business is offering aid to any household impacted by the devastating floods that hit the area last week.

Bosses at Mick's Carpets, in Curzon Street, Burton, has vowed to give free rugs and offer discounts for new carpeting to those who have suffered.

It comes after torrential rainfall throughout Thursday, November 14, that saw homes flooded and streets underwater across Burton and South Derbyshire.

And it was the story of pensioner Carol Checkett that caught the attention of Caroline Heath, of Mick's Carpets.

She said Mrs Checkett, who was forced out of her Repton home, in Brook End, was a long-time customer of theirs.

Many of her belongings were wrecked when eight inches of water seeped into the ground floor.

Mrs Heath said her loyal customer had only recently had carpeting on her stairs done by Mick's.

She said: "I read it in the Burton Mail and felt so terrible for her.

"I thought of how devastated I'd be if it were to happen to us, we just want to help.

"As a family business, based in Burton since the 1960s and being a small business, we want to help how we can, we feel sympathy for our customers.

"Following Carol's predicament, we have realised there are also a lot of people in Rolleston affected by the flooding.

"As a company, we would like, for anyone affected by the flooding, to offer them free rugs to protect the floor and then offer a discount for carpetting, particularly for those that are self-funded."

The firm will be handing out 5x3ft rugs to those who need them and can prove they have been impacted by flooding in their homes.
