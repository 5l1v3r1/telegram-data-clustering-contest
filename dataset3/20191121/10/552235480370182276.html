Full details of Alex Salmond's alleged sex assaults on 10 victims as ex-First Minister appears in court

The former SNP leader denies all 14 charges against him and said he will defend himself "to the utmost" in court.

21 Nov 2019, 10:23 by Peter Davidson, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/peter-davidson/

Alex Salmond is set to go on trial next year to face 14 charges, including sexual assault and attempted rape - one where he is said to have pinned a woman to a bed and laid naked over her.

The former First Minister appeared at the High Court in Edinburgh earlier today to hear the charges against him.

Salmond , who denies all the allegations, is facing charges which are said to have occurred over a six year period from June, 2008 to November, 2014.

The 64-year-old, who was Scotland 's First Minister from 2007 to 2014 , is expected to appear at a full trial early next year.

The former SNP leader stepped down from high office following the result of the independence referendum in 2014.

In June 2014, Salmond is accused of attempted rape of a woman by "repeatedly block her path, pin her against a wall, pull at her clothing, touch her breasts and body with your hands, remove her clothing, touch her breasts and body with your hands, remove her clothing and underwear, remove your clothing and underwear, push her onto a bed, kneel over her, pin her to the bed by her shoulder, lie naked on top of her."

He is accused of intent to rape of another woman after it's believed, in December 2013 , he lay on top of her, touched her buttocks, thighs and breasts over her clothes, repeatedly kissed her face with the intent to rape.
