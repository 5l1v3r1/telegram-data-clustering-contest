OECD: Global growth will be worst since financial crisis in 2019

21 Nov 2019, 10:19 by Harry Robertson

The global economy is set to grow at its slowest pace since the financial crisis this year, the OECD has said, with governments failing to adequately tackle the problem.

In a gloomy update to its forecasts, the Paris-based organisation said countries were also failing on tackling climate change, the digitisation of economies and a breakdown of the rules-based global order.

The world economy is set to grow 2.9 per cent this year and in 2020, the OECD said, the weakest expansions for 10 years. In 2021, growth is predicted to rise to just three per cent.

OECD chief economist Laurence Boone said clear policies were needed to address the problem, otherwise “uncertainty will continue to loom high, damaging growth prospects”.
