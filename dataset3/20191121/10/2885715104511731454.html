Forever Fit: The Flying Sikh turns 90

For Milkha Singh, age is just a number that does not stop Milkha Singh from playing 18 holes at the 7,202-yard long Chandigarh Golf Course

21 Nov 2019, 10:29 by Nitin Sharma

It’s 1 p.m in the afternoon and Flying Sikh Milkha Singh makes his way to his Sector 8 home in his golf cart. For Singh, who turned 90 on Wednesday, age is just a number that does not stop him from playing 18 holes at the 7,202-yard long Chandigarh Golf Course.

Greeted by fans at his home, the former Commonwealth and Asian Games champion says, “It’s a special day for me and the whole family but celebrations can wait. The reason I am here to see this day is my fitness regime and that’s what I followed at the start of the day,’’ says Singh, who plays golf thrice a week during which he walks the first nine holes before getting on to the cart.

Not just that, he also jogs for more than 2 km at the Sukhna. “I want people to be passionate about their fitness. Everyone must take ten minutes of physical exercise daily at home or outside,” declares Singh.

He attributes his present form to the six hours of training which he used to do in his youth.

“I start my day with two eggs and two slices of toast along with juice and take 1-2 chapatis with dal and curd rice at lunch. Doctors have advised me not to take milk so I have curd from the desi cows at our farm.

It has been close to three decades since I took a full meal during dinner. Instead, have a light snack, but this diet has kept me fit all these years,” adds Singh.
