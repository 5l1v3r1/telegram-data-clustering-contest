Scotland face potential Euro 2020 group of death with England, France and Portugal

21 Nov 2019, 10:34 by Alan Galindo

SCOTLAND are just two games away from securing a spot at the Euro 2020 finals - but they could find themselves in a 'group of death'.

Steve Clarke's men face a play-off semi-final and final to make it to the tournament proper.

But they will find out their potential group stage opponents next week - and one scenario doesn't make for good reading.

The Dark Blues could come up against England, France AND Portugal in the early stages of the major finals.

Scotland are guaranteed to be paired with England in Group D - who are top seeds - due to Hampden and Wembley being host pairings.

A slightly less daunting route for the Scots would be facing England, Poland and Czech Republic.

The 24 teams that qualify for Euro 2020 are seeded based on their performances during the qualifiers.

The ten group winners take the first ten positions, followed by the ten runners up, all ranked by points, goal difference and goals scored to determine their final pot.

The draw for the Euro 2020 finals takes place on Saturday November 30 and Scotland will be part of the draw.

Euro 2020 finals draw pots

POT 1 - Belgium, Italy (hosts, Group A), England (hosts, Group D), Germany (hosts, Group F), Spain (hosts, Group E), Ukraine.

POT 2 - France, Poland, Switzerland, Croatia, Netherlands (hosts, Group C), Russia (hosts, Group B)

POT 3 - Portugal, Turkey, Denmark (hosts, Group B), Austria, Sweden, Czech Republic

POT 4 - Wales, Finland, Winner play-off Path A, Winner play-off Path B, Winner play-off Path C, Winner play-off Path D

Clarke and Co will discover their play-off semi-final opponents tomorrow with one of Bulgaria, Israel, Hungary or Romania making the trip to Hampden in the last four.

If the Dark Blues reach the final they will face one of Norway or Serbia - with a draw determining who hosts the one-off game.

The overall winner secures a spot at Euro 2020 and will be seeded in Pot 4.
