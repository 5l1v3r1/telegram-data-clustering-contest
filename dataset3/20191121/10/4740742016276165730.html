Bristol Rovers chairman Steve Hamer leaves club after 15-word statement

He has been at the Gas for three years

21 Nov 2019, 10:39 by David Byrom

Bristol Rovers chairman Steve Hamer has left the club.

The Gas announced the news this morning in a short statement on their website.

Hamer joined the Gas in February 2016, being installed after the al-Qadi family took charge of the club, after previously having a three-year stint as chairman of Swansea City between 1997 and 2000.

The 68-year-old was at Rovers' FA Cup replay victory over Bromley on Tuesday evening but has been out of the spotlight over the last 12 months, with CEO Martyn Starnes taking a more prominent role after being appointed in March 2018.

Rovers' statement is notable in its shortness, with just 15 words being dedicated to the man who has been at the Gas for over three years.

It said: "The club has parted company with the non-executive chairman, Steve Hamer, with immediate effect."
