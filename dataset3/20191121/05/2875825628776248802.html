Stephen Fry and Lenny Henry confirmed as guest stars in new series of Doctor Who

The legendary big-name stars will join Jodie Whittaker as she reprises her role as the thirteenth Doctor in the twelve series opener of Doctor Who

21 Nov 2019, 05:05 by Irishmirror.ie

TV legends Stephen Fry and Lenny Henry have been confirmed as guest stars in the new series of BBC's hit sci-fi show Doctor Who .

Two of the UK's biggest stars have reportedly signed up for 'major' roles in the show and will join the thirteenth Doctor Jodie Whittaker for some time-travelling adventures in early 2020.

While it will be a debut stint for both Sir Lenny, 61, and Stephen, 62, in Doctor Who, the pair's characters are being kept totally under wraps.

Speaking ahead of his Doctor Who appearance Stephen Fry said: "Short of being picked for a British space exploration programme and I readily concede that I’m past the age where I’d be considered (if I was ever the right age for such a posting) – then being in an episode of Doctor Who will certainly do as a very sweet second-place excitement."

Sir Lenny Henry CBE added: “It was absolutely brilliant to be welcomed into the fantastical world of Doctor Who.

"The nearest I have been to the TARDIS was when I played the Caribbean Doctor in the Lenny Henry Show, so as a life-long (hiding behind the sofa type) Doctor Who fan this is a very special moment for me."

Meanwhile Chris Chibnall, Showrunner spoke about the show's proud decision to welcome the new additions, saying: "Doctor Who is coming back with a bang, with two great British icons in major roles. One of the great joys of Doctor Who is getting to work with actors from your wish list.

“Stephen and Lenny are two of my absolute favourite actors and to be able to bring them into Doctor Who , in our one of our biggest ever stories to kick off the new series, is an absolute thrill."

The news of Stephen and Lenny's appearances come after it was revealed there is no festive special planned for the second year running.

The highly-anticipated 12th series is due to start on New Year’s Day, with one of the biggest episodes in the show’s history.

Whittaker and her time-travelling pals Bradley Walsh as Graham O’Brien, Tosin Cole as his step grandson Ryan Sinclair and Mandip Gill as Yasmin Khan, will all return for the new 10-part run.

The second half of the story is then expected to air on January 4, as the series shifts back to its traditional Saturday evening teatime slot.

Jodie Whittaker was last seen in the role in the New Year's Day special entitled Resolution, which saw the Doctor and her friends face off with a Dalek.

The show, which has just finished filming, has since dropped hints that Whittaker’s second run will feature some 'other' familiar faces.

The next run is also due to feature returning aliens the Judoon , along with more historical stories, one of which will see the TARDIS crew encounter scientist Mary Shelley.

Last week BBC also revealed the new writers and directors set to jump aboard the TARDIS for The Doctor's latest adventures, which will bring a new feel to the show.

A recent rumour teased that Jodie's Time Lord clashes with them in the 19th century in the “darker” new series.

An insider previously revealed: "This new run is much scarier than Jodie’s first year and will ­definitely put the frighteners on fans. Things are about to get darker for the Doctor."
