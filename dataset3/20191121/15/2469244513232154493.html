Full list of polling stations in Torbay to vote in the December 2019 General Election

The bar at Torquay United Football Club’s ground will see a different set of supporters than usual on Thursday, December 12

21 Nov 2019, 15:34 by Edward Oldfield

The bar at Torquay United Football Club’s ground will see a different set of supporters than usual on Thursday, December 12.

The Boots and Laces Sports Bar at Marnham Road in Plainmoor will become one of the 48 polling stations across Torquay and Paignton for the 2019 General Election.

The stations in the Torbay constituency include churches, cafes, community centres and two Torquay schools - Sherwell Valley Primary in Hawkins Avenue, and Torre CofE Academy in Barton Road.

The more unusual locations include the unused council-owned building known as The Old Monastery in Berry Drive, Paignton.

Polling stations will be open for people to cast their votes from 7am to 10pm.

The deadline to register to vote in the election is at midnight on Tuesday, November 26.

Anyone who is unable to get to a polling station can ask for a postal vote. The deadline to apply, amend or cancel a postal vote is 5pm on Tuesday, November 26.

Voters can also appoint someone to vote on their behalf, called a proxy vote, for example if they are ill or away on polling day. 

The deadline to apply for a proxy vote is 5pm on Wednesday, December 4.

In some circumstances people can apply for an emergency proxy vote up to 5pm on polling day. 

More information about voting is on Torbay Council’s website, with links to register and apply for postal and proxy votes online.

Five candidates are standing to become the MP for Torbay at the General Election.

They are Independent James Channer, Conservative Kevin Foster, Liberal Democrat Lee Howgate, Labour’s Michele Middleditch and Sam Moss for the Green Party.
