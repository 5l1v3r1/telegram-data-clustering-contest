'Better than expected' Pep Clotet surprised by this Birmingham City star's test results

After going through the summer as a free agent the midfielder signed a two-year deal last month and has made just one first team appearance so far

21 Nov 2019, 15:18 by Brian Dick, https://www.facebook.com/blueswriterbriandick/?fref=ts

Pep Clotet admits Josh McEachran has surprised him with the progress he has made with his conditioning.

The Birmingham City caretaker head coach praised the former Brentford midfielder for his attempts to prove himself at his new club.

After going through the summer as a free agent McEachran signed a two-year deal last month and has made just one first team appearance so far.

Whenever he has been asked about the 26-year-old Clotet has referred to the exacting fitness levels the former Chelsea starlet needs to meet for more regular involvement.

And the signs are that after a fortnight of heavy conditioning and testing, McEachran has done well and could be ready to contribute in the run-up to the festive period.

“He did the test and he did very well,” Clotet said. “Actually better than expected. I had a good talk with him.

“He’s putting in all the effort that he can to make sure he’s ready, so that’s good. We have a lot of games now from now until Christmas so everyone will make a difference.

 

“It was a long time since he last played regularly. I see him very focused on using this opportunity to get back as strong as possible and he’s putting in the right steps in the right direction.”

Another central midfielder doing that is Gary Gardner, who has not played for five weeks since injuring his knee at Leeds.

 

Clotet hopes to have Gardner back in the main squad by next week.

“Gary is already working outside on the pitch with the group. Then in the main part of the session he is working with the medical staff.

“We expect him to be back with us next week for normal training and then it’s about how he feels.

“I do know that he’s been quicker and he feels much better than we expected. I hope we can get him back to fitness as quick as possible and he can be in contention again.”
