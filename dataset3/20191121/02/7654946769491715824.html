10-year-old shot during NJ high school football game dies of injuries

21 Nov 2019, 02:36 by Ben Feuerherd

A 10-year-old boy who was struck by gunfire last Friday night during a high school football game in New Jersey died of his injuries Wednesday.

Micah Tennant was one of three people wounded when a gunman opened fire at the game between Pleasantville High School and Camden High School in Pleasantville, outside of Atlantic City, the county prosecutor’s office said.

Tennant, who was shot in the neck, died of his injuries Wednesday, authorities said. A 27-year-old man and a 15-year-old boy were also wounded in the shooting, prosecutors said.

The suspected gunman, Alvin Wyatt, was hit with an upgraded charge of murder after Tennant died Wednesday, authorities said.

“Words at this time seem so insufficient to portray the anger and outrage that our community feels regarding his loss,” Atlantic County Prosecutor Damon G. Tyner said in a statement.

“However, his spirit will live on in so many people that he inspired.”

Four other people who were carrying weapons at the game were also charged in connection to the shooting.
