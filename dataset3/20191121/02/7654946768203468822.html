De Blasio slams ‘vampire’ Hiram Monserrate for seeking comeback

21 Nov 2019, 02:06 by Julia Marsh, Bernadette Hogan and Carl Campanile

Mayor Bill de Blasio slammed disgraced felon and ex-state Sen. Hiram Monserrate Wednesday as a shameless “vampire” for mounting another bid for public office — and wished he’d just go away.

“God help us all. It’s like — what is it? The vampire that keeps coming back,” de Blasio said of Monserrate filing paperwork to run for state Assembly against veteran incumbent Jeff Aubry.

“We have to — well, we’re not allowed to put a stake through someone’s heart,” de Blasio chuckled.

“He says he wants to run against Jeff Aubry who’s one of the — I think — most important voices of conscience in Albany, and I think is just an exemplary public servant.”

Aubry, first elected in 1992, is one of the top leaders in the Assembly and one of the most prominent African-lawmakers in New York politics.

Monserrate is feeling confident after he was elected a Democratic district leader last year within the 35th Assembly district Aubry represents, covering East Elmhurst and North Corona.

His critics are taking him seriously by purchasing domain names for a potential campaign.

Tap in the name “VoteHiram.com” and the New York Times story appears from when Monserrate pled guilty to misusing $100,000 in public funds as a city councilman. He was sentenced to two years in prison.

His candidacy could also spur momentum in Albany to pass legislation sponsored by Sen. Todd Kaminsky (D-Nassau) and Assemblywoman Catalina Cruz (D-Queens) to bar convicted felons from running for elective office for at least ten years.

“My bill is based on the simple premise that out of 100,000-plus people in an Assembly district, we can surely find one qualified
person to represent the public who is not a convicted felon,” said Kaminsky, a former federal prosecutor.

“Monserrate’s history of violating the public trust in such a gross and clear way, should disqualify him from being able to represent that, or any other district,” he added.

Meanwhile, Assembly Speaker Carl Heastie (D-Bronx) issued a statement saying he is totally behind his pal Aubry and refused to even utter Monserrate’s name.

“Throughout the course of his career, Jeff has proven to be a man of integrity, a strong voice for his constituents and a tireless advocate for all New Yorkers,” Heastie said.

The ex-con has already lost three previous comeback races for the Senate, Assembly and City Council.

Monserrate has been trying to return to elected office since he was expelled from the state Senate in 2010 after being convicted of a misdemeanor assault charge stemming from the brutal 2008 attack on his girlfriend, Karla Giraldo.

The disgraced pol was caught on tape dragging a battered and bleeding Giraldo through the lobby of his apartment building as she desperately tried to seek help from a neighbor.

He was acquitted of charges that he first flew into a rage and slashed her face with a broken glass after discovering a police officer’s union card in her wallet.

Monserrate’s lawyers claimed the cut came from a “freak accident” when he tripped while giving Giraldo a glass of water.

Years later, Giraldo was sentenced to three years behind bars for attacking her new fiancé with a samurai sword.
