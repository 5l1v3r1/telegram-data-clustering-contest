VATA BHOY

Ex-Celtic ace Rudi Vata’s son Rocco scores for Ireland in Poland victory

21 Nov 2019, 14:59 by Ewan Paton

EX-CELTIC ace Rudi Vata's son Rocco has scored on his start for the Republic of Ireland.

The Hoops youngster netted in a 2-1 win over Poland in the match in Galway.

It was youngster Rocco's first start for the under-15s.

SunSport exclusively revealed on Tuesday night that Rocco was set to make his debut.

And he did as he helped his country to two wins in two games at Eamonn Deacy Park.

Jason Donohue's team had previously beaten the same opponent on Tuesday.

Kevin Zefi netted his second in successive games after number ten Rocco Vata had opened the scoring for Ireland.

Vata Snr would have been proud of his son and the rest of the boys as they came from behind to win.

Poland pounced from a corner kick on six minutes and opened the scoring.

However, towards the end of the first half, Ireland missed a penalty but Rocco was on hand to tap into the net on the rebound to level proceedings.

Then on 64 minutes the Irish completed the comeback when Zefi found himself in the right place to fire in from around 22 yards out.

Dad and former Celtic hero Vata won 59 caps for Albania and lifted the Scottish Cup with the Hoops in 1995.

Rocco 14 is in the Hoops academy. He also qualifies for Albania, Scotland and Montenegro.

He can represent the Irish through his Dublin-born gran Ann on his mother’s side.

Vata, 50, now an agent and TV pundit, said: “When I joined Celtic, I met my new team-mates for the first time in Dublin.

“My debut was against Sligo Rovers because we were on a pre-season tour.

“This is part of my history so it is special for my son to be in Ireland now.

“Nobody in the Albanian FA contacted me about Rocco. I know that Scotland have watched him too, but the Irish association was the first to get in contact.

“His granny is the happiest Irish woman in Scotland!"
