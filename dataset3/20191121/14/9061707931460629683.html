Big news for Burton brewery as agreement is reached with American firm

It's great news for business in town

21 Nov 2019, 14:15 by Jenny Moody

A long-term deal with an America brewery comes with a big role for Burton over the next 15 years.

The Marston's Beer Company has reached a long-term trade agreement with Shipyard Brewing Co, which is based in Portland, Maine, to produce one of their beers in the UK.

The new multi-million-pound agreement for 15 years follows a previous successful contract and means the two companies will now be working together until 2034.

It comes as good news for workers in the Burton Marston's factory, in Shobnall Road, as they will be responsible for managing the packaging of bottles and cans.

Tom Spencer, site manager at Marston's Brewery, said: "The long-term commitment between Marston's and Shipyard is fantastic news.

"The packaging of bottles and cans for Shipyard takes place right here at Burton and we will continue to manage this for the next 15 years."

It comes after Shipyard co-founder Fred Forsley visited Marston's head office to sign the deal, according to The Business Desk.com.

The collaboration already has a 12-year history and has proved successful, as the popularity of the American-styled craft beers has grown in the UK.

Shipyard American Pale Ales is the first beer to be brewed solely in the UK for Shipyard.

It is now the UK's biggest-selling craft keg beer and Shipyard Indian Pale Ale is still growing.

In June 2019, Marston's launched Low Tide, Shipyard's first low-alcohol alternative in the UK market.
