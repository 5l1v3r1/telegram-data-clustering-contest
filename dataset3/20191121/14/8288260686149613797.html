The parking charge changes for Solihull village's Christmas lights switch-on

The council has introduced temporary measures for the Knowle event

21 Nov 2019, 14:23 by David Irwin, https://www.facebook.com/David-Irwin-Local-Democracy-Reporter-792811574238027/?ref=settings

Parking restrictions will be relaxed in Knowle to support the village's Christmas lights switch-on next week.

The council has agreed to special arrangements on Wednesday, November 27, when the festive decorations will be officially unveiled and many traders will be offering late night shopping.

Visit Knowle, a group of businesses and residents which got together earlier this year, believes the community-led event will be a big boost in the crucial pre-Christmas period.

But there has been a push locally to make parking easier for shoppers and avoid issues with cars being left in nearby residential streets.

This week the council agreed to suspend charges in its free and display car parks between 4-6pm and keep the Knowle Park facilities open later, to provide extra spaces.

Kim Hulse, from Visit Knowle, said the aim was to get shoppers to stop out a bit longer and support independent businesses.

"The feedback from some of the shoppers [last year] was it was quite difficult to park, so we have been working to take that barrier away and make it easier," said the self-employed businesswoman.

Karen Blondel, from the Indigo Art Gallery, said there would be a "great atmosphere" at the event and the later opening was a boon in terms of attracting customers into outlets along the High Street.

Visit Knowle was launched around six months ago and was inspired by the proactive approach of residents who had previously set up the Knowle, Dorridge and Bentley Heath Neighbourhood Forum.

"Some local people are really [passionate] about Knowle and love the village but were concerned about all the empty units, concerned about parking and thought it was a good time to take action," said Ms Hulse.

The group is looking at a number of ways to promote the village and has urged others who would like to get involved to get in touch.

The temporary parking measures were rubber-stamped at a council decision session this week.

 

Paul Tovey, from the highways team, said the proposals were "reasonable, practical and affordable", with the council expected to lose less than £500 from stopping the charges at 4pm (they would usually continue until 6pm).

The same arrangements would be expected to apply in future years, although this would be subject to there being no significant financial burden on the local authority.

Cllr Ken Hawkins, cabinet member for the environment and highways, said: "It's a nice bit of good news for Knowle."

The lights ceremony will start at 4.15pm, with singers providing entertainment and - as an added attraction this year -  pop-up shops will be set up in the Greswolde Arms.Knowle
