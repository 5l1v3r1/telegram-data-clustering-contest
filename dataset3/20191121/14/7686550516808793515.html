Gardens and garages top house-hunters' wish-list

Property website Zoopla has issued a list of the most commonly searched features throughout the country

21 Nov 2019, 14:59 by Paul Smith

Britain's house-hunters have named gardens and garages as their top two 'must have' items according to property website Zoopla.

Site activity over the last year also revealed large family homes and property with a seaside or rural aspect among the most searched items.

In addition, simplicity and ease of access made bungalows another popular search for those using Zoopla to help source a new property.

The website's consumer expert, Laura Howard, believes this is consistent with a population now living longer than ever before.

"The once unfashionable bungalow continues to make a comeback highlighting an ageing population that wants to enjoy the benefits that single-floor living provides," she said.

Unsurprisingly, the research found house hunting has a regional aspect, with cottages in particularly demand in the Midlands, northern England, the East of England, the South West and Scotland.

Similarly, city-dwelling Londoners were most likely to seek out green space, with gardens comfortably topping their search list. Interestingly, Scotland was the only other part of the country to exhibit this trend.

With property prices in the capital being higher than elsewhere, Help to Buy was another popular search term.

In Wales and Scotland, house hunters were particularly likely to seek privacy, with the search term 'secluded' appearing on their top 10 lists.
