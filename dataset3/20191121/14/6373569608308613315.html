How much money Plymouth Argyle have made from cup competitions so far this season

The Pilgrims will be away to Bristol Rovers in the FA Cup second round on December 1

21 Nov 2019, 14:35 by Chris Errington

Plymouth Argyle have picked up £88,500 in prize money from three cup competitions this season.

But the real riches can still be made by Ryan Lowe's Pilgrims if they can progress further in the FA Cup.

Argyle will be away to Bristol Rovers, managed by former Argyle defender Graham Coughlan, in the second round next weekend.

The game at the Memorial Stadium will be played on Sunday, December 1 with a 2pm kick-off so it can form part of the BBC Final Score programme.

That will earn both clubs £12,500 in TV fees as a result, while the winners of the tie will receive £54,000 in prize money.

A televised second round replay between Argyle and Rovers at Home Park would be worth £36,000 apiece.

However, it is in third round of the FA Cup where the prize money and TV fees become very considerable.

Third round winners receive £135,000 in prize money, and the broadcast fee for a televised tie at that stage of the competition is £114,000 per club.

So, for example, if Argyle were to beat Rovers in a televised replay and then be chosen for a live broadcast of a third round tie against a top Premier League club at Home Park, they would earn £204,000.

That is excluding the gate receipts from what would probably be an 18,000 sell-out at the Theatre of Greens.

Here is a breakdown of the prize money that Argyle have received so far this season:

EFL Cup

First round winners vs Leyton Orient: £5,000

Total: £5,000 + gate receipts

EFL Trophy

Participation fee: £20,000

Draw vs Bristol Rovers £5,000

Win vs Swindon Town: £10,000

Total: £35,000 + gate receipts

FA Cup

First round winners vs Bolton Wanderers: £36,000

Bristol Rovers tie moved to Sunday: £12,500

Total so far: £48,500 + gate receipts

Overall total: £88,500 + gate receipts
