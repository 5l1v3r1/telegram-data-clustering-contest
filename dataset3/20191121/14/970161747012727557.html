Strictly's Anton Du Beke defends same-sex routine amid complaints storm

21 Nov 2019, 14:17

Strictly Come Dancing’s Anton Du Beke has defended Johannes Radebe and Graziano Di Prima’s same-sex routine after the BBC was stung by almost 200 complaints.

In a report of their most complained-about moments of the month so far, the broadcaster revealed 189 viewers took the time to criticise the duo’s spellbinding performance to Emili Sandi, which, like us, surprised the professional dancer.

‘As a dancer, you just dance. It’s a sort of creative process, you’re just dancing,’ Anton said of Joahennes and Graziano’s routine. ‘That’s what you do as a performer, you create, we’re creating dance.’

He continued: ‘I don’t think there’s anything controversial in the number itself.

‘I danced with Vincent Simone years ago on Strictly and I also did a number with Brucey [Forsyth].

‘I can’t say why they complained, it’s their own personal opinion about it but dance is dance, they’re just dancing. That’s all it is. All you’re doing is performing and you hope people enjoy it.’

When asked about his opinion on whether Strictly should welcome same-sex partners on the show Anton, who’s touring the UK and Ireland with Erin Boag next year, admitted he’d be happy either way.
