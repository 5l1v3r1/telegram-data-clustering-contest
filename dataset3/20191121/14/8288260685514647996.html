The 20 Solihull sites where traffic restrictions will be placed and what it will mean

There is a 'daily invasion' of cars during the school run, according to locals

21 Nov 2019, 14:15 by David Irwin, https://www.facebook.com/David-Irwin-Local-Democracy-Reporter-792811574238027/?ref=settings

Fed-up residents in a Solihull suburb have made a fresh appeal for the council to deal with the "daily invasion" of cars during the school run.

Those living in Grandborough Drive and its various side streets have called for parking restrictions to be imposed to put a stop to the problems they say are blighting their lives.

Their latest call for action comes as the highways department sets out plans to roll out new traffic regulation orders (TROs) at 20 sites around the borough.

An extra £20,000 has been allocated to deal with troublesome locations in 2020/21, allowing restrictions to be introduced in more places than normal.

But with more than 100 separate requests, the increased budget still means that almost five in six sites, including Grandborough Drive, are not deemed a high enough priority at this stage.

Road chiefs admitted this week that it was impossible to please everyone, although those in the cluster of cul-de-sacs, off Whitefields Road, argue that the number of schools in the vicinity makes their area a special case.

 

Judy Middleton, speaking on behalf of 78 local households, said that introducing restrictions from 8am-4pm, Monday to Friday, would improve road safety and reduce air pollution.

"The current parking situation is intolerable, it's got steadily worse year-on-year as the schools have increased their pupil numbers.

"Our roads are reduced to narrow single-lane highways, with virtually no passing places, compelling vehicles to illegally drive along pavements for long stretches to clear the gridlock. It is so dangerous.

"So I urge you, please give your support to our request and release us from this daily misery."

 

Ward councillor Joe Tildesley (Con, St Alphege) said residents were "plagued" by congestion and dangerous manoeuvres and the situation was "only getting worse."

"They are trapped in their homes during the school opening and closing times and the school holidays are a blessed relief," he said.

Cllr Ken Hawkins, cabinet member for the environment and highways, said he sympathised with residents who bore the brunt of school parking problems.

"I've got on my iPad cars parked on double yellow lines [and] in front of people's houses in my ward and I've been with fellow councillors and challenged them and they've just walked straight past us."

He said that the comments made by those in the Grandborough Drive area would be considered and an update was expected in January.

School Close, Smith's Wood - obstructive school parking

In a report to Cllr Hawkins' latest decision session -  in which all 108 requests were ranked by order of priority - the location was No 51, putting it at near the bottom of the council's "reserve list".

Paul Tovey, from the highways team, said that the current system for scoring TRO requests was now in its tenth year but it could "only please some".

Usually around £30,000 is allocated to deal with the highest priority projects, enough for between 10-12 schemes. Although an increased budget of £50,000 for next year has meant that slightly more will move forward.
