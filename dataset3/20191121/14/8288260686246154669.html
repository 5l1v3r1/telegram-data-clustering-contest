Caitlyn Jenner's family Kylie and Kendall ignore I'm A Celebrity - leaving fans 'heartbroken'

The star is being paid £500,000 to appear on the series, which is hosted by Ant McPartlin and Declan Donnelly

21 Nov 2019, 14:26 by James Rodger, https://www.facebook.com/jamesrodgerjournalist

Caitlyn Jenner’s daughters Kylie and Kendall are still ignoring I’m A Celebrity... Get Me Out Of Here! as they fail to show support.

Jenner is one of 12 campmates on the ITV1 reality show, which also features Ian Wright, James Haskell and Jacqueline Jossa.

The star is being paid £500,000 to appear on the series, which is hosted by Ant McPartlin and Declan Donnelly.

Myles Stephenson, Adele Roberts, Roman Kemp, Kate Garraway, Nadine Coyle, Andy Whyment and Cliff Parisi are also in the camp.

Andrew Maxwell completes the line-up.

None of Caitlyn's family have publicly discussed or supported her during her time on the ITV show.

One fan wrote on Twitter: "I’m not saying that you have to put anything on social media to prove you care etc but not one of @Caitlyn_Jenner’s family members have said anything about her stint on #ImACeleb and its bothering the hell out of me."

"Its literally breaking my heart that not a single member of Caitlyns family appear to be supporting her (sic). You're doing great Caitlyn! I am rooting for you!!" a second added.

"Are the Kardashian/Jenner clan even going to acknowledge @Caitlyn_Jenner being in the #ImaCeleb jungle orrr.....?" asked a third.

Last night, Jenner said she was enjoying having people do things for her on I'm A Celebrity.

The US star competed in the American version of the series in 2003, when she was still Bruce, and is now on the ITV series of the show.

 

Jenner, who came out as a trans women in 2015, told her campmates it was nice having people do things like cook for her.

"The last time I was in here, old Bruce, I was doing everything for everybody," she said.
