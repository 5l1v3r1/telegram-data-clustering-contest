CJ rejects Okowa’s ex-aide’s clemency plea - The Nation Newspaper

21 Nov 2019, 01:04

Okungbowa Aiwerie, Asaba

There was drama yesterday at the Kwale Custodial Centre, Ndokwa West Local Government Area of Delta State after a plea by Friday Eluhu, a sacked security aide to Governor Ifeanyi Okowa, was turned down by the state  Chief Judge, Justice Marshal Umukoro.

Eluhu and his accomplice were allegedly caught in a viral video footage earlier this year assaulting a lady after stripping her naked for allegedly insulting him.

Justice Umukoro turned down Eluhu’s plea for mercy, saying “a woman was stripped of her dignity and reduced to a state of inhumanity.”

Eluhu, 47, is charged along with two others Onyeachukwu Nebo France, 22, and Anom Victor Ewere, 33,  for alleged conspiracy, kidnapping, unlawful detention and attempted murder.

The trio were remanded at the Kwale Custodial Centre since February 18, 2019.

Eluhu, who was on his knees, attributed his travails to the handiwork of his enemies as well as an alleged high-level conspiracy against him.

He told Justice Umukoro he had already initiated an out-of-court settlement with the victim’s family.

Eluhu said his children lacked parental care in his absence.
