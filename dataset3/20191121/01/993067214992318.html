Historians to host series of talks at Ulster Historical Foundation event in Belfast

21 Nov 2019, 01:00 by SUZANNE MCGONAGLE

THREE renowned historians are to host a series of talks in Belfast next week.

Dr Francis Costello, Dr Éamon Phoenix and Dr James Dingley will speak at the event, entitled 'Ulster, America and the Enlightenment: From Feux de Joie to the Belfast Harp Festival'.

Hosted by the Ulster Historical Foundation, it aims to capture the "cultural atmosphere of Belfast's radical Golden Age".

Dr Costello will discuss the enlightenment, the American Revolution and the society of the United Irishmen during his talk, while Dr Phoenix will examine the critical social, cultural and political atmosphere in Belfast and the province of Ulster from 1789 to 1795.

Dr Dingley, who is chairman of the Francis Hutcheson Institute, will examine the shaping of Enlightenment thinking in Ireland, Britain and Europe during his talk.

It will feature music by Belfast harpist Edel Brady, who will reprise the traditional airs of the Belfast Harp Festival of 1792.

The event will be held at First Presbyterian Church, Rosemary Street, Belfast on November 27 at 7pm.
