Ballarat Cup 2019: fashion judges share their tips

21 Nov 2019, 01:00 by Greg Gliddon

A LEADING stylist and Central Square Fashions on the Field judge for Saturday's Ballarat Cup says poise, confidence and dressing for the weather are the keys to a successful day at the races.

Skipton-based stylist Ema Smith, one of four judges who will decide the four categories at the fashions on the field events, says some golden rules need to be followed.

"Confidence is key," Ms Smith said. "If the person if not confident, it shows in their outfit.

"Attention to detail is what I look for. How does the handbag match in with the shoes?"

"One of the tips I would definitely make is that I always dress for the weather. We're fortunate the weather will be nice, but always consider the wind, check your accessories, perhaps take a light jacket."

Ms Smith said she was honoured to be chosen as one of the judges for what will be her first Ballarat Cup.

"I've judged at Ararat Cup before and I'm also an adviser up at Avoca as well," she said.

Ms Smith said trends this year included one shouldered dresses "bold colour, very bright pops of colour, teals and greens suits".

"Also total dressing, a full all-white outfit is incredibly striking.

"For men, three-piece suits were all the rage at the Melbourne Cup carnival, but an attention to detail can really make a difference - perhaps an umbrella or a cane really adds to the whole outfit."

However, there was one trend that she's going to leave up to the gents.

"The sock or no socks trend is a European trend as the Europeans tend to wear loafers more," she said.

"If you're going to go the no socks lock, you need to wear the right shoes or you can look like a fool. For me, it's a matter of what works for the outfit - if it works, it can look good."

The four categories of Central Square Ballarat Fashions on the Field are: Traditional Lady of the Day, Contemporary Lady of the Day, Most Fabulous Millinery and Gent of the Day.

Registrations from 11.30am with judging following race 1 at 12.25pm.
