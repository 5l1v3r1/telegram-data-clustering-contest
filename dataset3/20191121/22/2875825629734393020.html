John McGinn told he's good enough for Man Utd - by ex-Aston Villa boss Steve Bruce

Bruce signed Scotland international McGinn for a bargain £2million last year when Villa boss and believes he is among his best ever bargains

21 Nov 2019, 22:30 by Irishmirror.ie

John McGinn has been told he’s Manchester United quality – by the boss who signed him for a bargain £2million.

The Aston Villa midfielder is a red-hot £50million-rated property and is being monitored by Ole Gunnar Solskjaer.

But former Villa manager Steve Bruce has revealed how he lured McGinn to the Midlands from Hibernian for a knock-down price thanks to some persuasive rounds of golf at the former Ryder Cup venue The Belfry.

Bruce, who takes his Newcastle side to Villa on Monday night, said: “We nearly didn’t sign him because we couldn’t afford £2m, but we whipped him away from Celtic just at the right time.

"I always knew he’d be very good, but he’s surpassed expectations.

“How we kept him away from Celtic I don’t know because his grandfather was the chairman!

“It took a couple of games of golf at the Belfry with his dad until we could muster the money together. I am delighted for the kid because he is smashing lad.”

Bruce rates McGinn as one of his best cut price signings; high praise indeed given his landed Manchester United ’s £80m defender Harry Maguire for £2.5m and Liverpool full-back Andy Robertson for not much more while at Hull.

“I wish I could find a few more of him,” admitted Bruce of McGinn. “He has to be one of my best ever signings because he has a determination to do well.

“It took him a minute to win over the supporters at Villa when I was manager because he crashed into a tackle, sprayed a 50-yard pass and had a shot in his first few minutes.”

McGinn continued to catch the eye during Villa’s promotion campaign and has shone on their return to the top flight.

Villa say it would take an eye-watering sum to lure him away mid-season.

But Bruce has no doubt McGinn has what it takes to play for a side challenging for a top-six spot.

Bruce added: “Man Utd? From what he has done in 12 months, he can only improve. He is that level, yeah.

"He scored twice again for Scotland the other night, so that’s six in three games for them.”
