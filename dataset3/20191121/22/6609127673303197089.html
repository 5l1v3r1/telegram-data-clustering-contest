DUMPED

Argyll and Bute Council ‘flytipped’ marine plastic cleared by volunteers from beaches

21 Nov 2019, 22:58 by Moira Kerr

VOLUNTEERS told of their shock after a mountain of marine plastic they had cleared from beaches was “flytipped” by a council.

Rubbish including nets and fish farm piping — which can’t be recycled at normal sites — has been dumped on local authority land beside Oban Airport in Argyll.

The haul also included the same plastic taping that led to the death of a stag after it got caught up in it and had to be put down on Jura last week.

Argyll and Bute Council had agreed to store the waste, which had been carefully sorted by volunteers, until it could be moved on to be recycled.

But it was dumped earlier this month after space filled up at the council’s yard.

Today, beach clean-up organiser Janie Steele said: “It’s been flytipped. I was really upset when I saw it.”

Argyll and Bute Council said: “We hoped to have a container ready but one was not available. We are doing our best to arrange one now.”
