Controversial housing development between Derby suburbs gets approval

Residents have voiced their concerns

21 Nov 2019, 22:08 by Paige Oldfield

Plans for a massive 800-home development between Alvaston and Chellaston have been given the green light despite objections.

An application for the housing development, known as Snelsmoor Grange, was granted by Derby City Council’s planning committee tonight, Thursday, November 21.

It is set to be built in phases and the developers are looking for full planning permission to start work on the first 245 homes.

They are also seeking outline permission for the remaining 555 houses as part of what is called a hybrid planning application.

It will be sited on land to the north of Snelsmoor Lane and south of Field Lane at Boulton Moor.

To the east of the site, within South Derbyshire District Council's area, is another proposed housing site, known as Boulton Moor 2.

The development also comprises of a country park and a primary school and will be accessed from a new roundabout on Snelsmoor Lane.

The school will be sited on the edge of the green wedge and accessed from a new junction off Field Lane.

The proposed country park will form the retained green wedge and will be located between the proposed housing and the recently completed Fellow Lands Way development.

It will also contain a series of footpaths, cycleways, play areas, wildflower meadows and public orchards.

Tony Chambers-Kirk, owner of End Cottage Kennels, in Alvaston, addressed his concerns over the planned development at the meeting.

He said: “The use of the footpath by the public will disturb the dogs and cause them to bark.

“If this happens in the early morning, late evening or in the night this may disturb our new neighbours which may force them to complain about noise.”

He also raised concerns over potential security issues.

Another member of the public, Miss Warrent, urged members of the committee to build new houses away from green land.

She said: “When you say affordable, what do you mean? I can’t afford them.

“This site will rip up the environment and we are all worried about it. We are concerned.

“Why don’t you develop social housing that we can actually rent?

“We all know there is lots of traffic happening, if you would just reject these 800 houses a lot of people would be on your side.

“Things are changing out there. Please support us.”

The planning application was first lodged with Derby City Council six years ago.

Since the original application, dating from 2013, in response to the then draft allocation of the emerging local housing plan, the proposal has undergone a lengthy period of negotiations and amendments.

During that time, the council has received 41 public representations, one in support of the proposal and the others citing objections.

These objections included the detrimental impact of the development on the green wedge and Green Belt; further development in the area cannot be sustained; loss of hedgerows and impact on wildlife; insufficient community facilities such as health centres; out-of-date traffic study; detrimental impact on traffic in "already congested Chellaston" and the impact of drainage plans on existing homes.
