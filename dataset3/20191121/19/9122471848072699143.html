ESPN Addresses The Big 12’s College Football Playoff Hopes

21 Nov 2019, 19:48 by Andrew McCarty

Earlier this week, the College Football Playoff committee released its rankings following Week 12 of the college football season.

LSU held on to the top spot, while Ohio State, Clemson and Georgia rounded out the top four.

One of the most interesting story-lines following the release of the rankings was where the Big 12 sits. Oklahoma came in as the conference’s top-ranked program at No. 9.

It’s a bad spot for the Sooners, who are well outside of the top four and don’t really have a way to get back into the conversation.

ESPN college football analyst Heather Dinich broke down the Big 12’s chances of making the playoff.

“If beating undefeated Baylor on the road wasn’t enough to give the No. 9 Sooners a significant boost during the regular season, why would beating a one-loss, No. 14 Baylor in the Big 12 title game be worth much more? Oklahoma remains the Big 12’s best hope at a semifinal spot, but it’s going to need some chaos to go along with a conference title.”

Her comments are in line with what much of the national conversation is. Oklahoma needs massive chaos to have any chance of making the playoff this season.

Can the Sooners win-out and get some luck along the way?
