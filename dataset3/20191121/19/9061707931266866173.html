Latest on village Co-op targeted in coach crash ram raid

It was the third attempted break in at the store

21 Nov 2019, 19:15 by Beth Pridding

A shop that was targeted by ram raiders this morning has reopened to customers.

Police said that the raiders used a coach and a telescopic handler to ram into the front of the Moira Co-op, in Shortheath Road, in the early hours of this morning, Thursday, November 21.

They had apparently been trying to steal the cash machine from the store.

However, they left empty handed and abandoned the coach and telescopic handler at the scene.

Now, despite suffering significant damage, the store has reopened.

A post on social media from a member of staff at the store reads: "Moira Co-op will be open this evening.

"We do not have a cash machine due to the damage it sustained.

"Everything in store is safe, including the colleagues, which is our priority.

"I would like to personally thank everyone in the community for their support."

Anyone with information should call Leicestershire Police on 101, quoting reference 19*623150.
