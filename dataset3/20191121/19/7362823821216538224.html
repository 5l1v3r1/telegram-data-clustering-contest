Nikki Haley: Dems want it 'really bad,' but impeachment hearings have not laid out 'impeachable' offenses

21 Nov 2019, 19:39 by Nick Givas

INSERT CLIP

Former U.S. Ambassador to the United Nations Nikki Haley told the "Fox News Rundown" Thursday that congressional Democrats have not reached the threshold proof necessary to impeach President Trump, despite wanting to do so "really bad."

"There is nothing that is impeachable there," Haley said. "There is no secret he wanted an investigation opened up. There was no aid withheld."

"I know the Dems want this really bad, but there is nothing impeachable there," she added. "And they would have maybe a slight bit of, you know, credibility had they not been trying to impeach the president since his very first day [in office]."

The former South Carolina governor compared impeachment to a political "death penalty" and said Democrats are unsure of where to go next.

"I think impeachment, I have said it is the death penalty for an elected official," she said earlier in the interview.

"It's the highest, highest offense you can do against someone. And I just think it's hard to find where the Democrats are trying to go with this because if you just look at the facts, there's nothing impeachable there."

Haley also said she has already started helping Trump with his 2020 reelection campaign and plans to assist GOP Senate candidates as well.
