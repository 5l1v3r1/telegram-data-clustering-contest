Derby's oldest woman celebrates her 106th birthday with whiskey

The great-great grandmother was in great spirits

21 Nov 2019, 19:57 by Rachel Sutherland

A great-great grandmother from Derby celebrated her 106th lap around the sun today with a glass of whiskey in her hand.

Gladys Gunn is thought to be the city's oldest resident.

She said the key to longevity is "to take each day at a time and to have a drop of whiskey each day in your coffee."

During her birthday celebrations at her sheltered housing accommodation in Alvaston she needed help blowing out her birthday candles but saw the funny side.

Gladys has two children, six grandchildren, 11 great-grandchildren and one great-great grandson.

The mum-of-two has lived through both World Wars and reminisced about the time she arrived home from work to her address in Findern Street, to find two soldiers in her porch.

She then took them in and looked after them for a couple of weeks.

For all of her life she has loved dancing, both old time and modern dance, saying that it used to be "her life".

She used to dance with her husband Bill who passed away in 1967.

She chuckled: "His (Bill's) mother was a teacher in old time dancing. I tried to teach him modern but he wouldn't do it!"

She was born in 1913 in Northampton and met Bill while on holiday in Derby - which she said looks exactly the same as it did when she first visited.

The lovebirds worked together at an off-licence in Bainbridge Street, Normanton.

Gladys' eldest daughter, Glenyce Corns, 74, said her mum had various jobs but stopped working when she had her two daughters.

She worked in Derby's knitting mills, as a home help and as a cook at St Christopher's Orphanage, to name a few.

When working at the orphanage she would regularly invite one of the children back to her home, a girl called Valerie Hills, whom still visits her now.

In her 60s Gladys did college courses in pottery and flower arranging.

Her youngest daughter, Celia Houghton, 71, said they often went on railway trips as a family to the seaside.

The gene for long life runs in the family as Gladys' great-grandmother reached the ripe old age of 104.
