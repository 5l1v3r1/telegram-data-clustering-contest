6 Legit Money Saving Life Hacks We Learned From Our Favourite TV Shows

21 Nov 2019, 19:00 by Katie Cunningham

TV is full of life advice, some better than others. Arrested Development taught us to always leave a note. Michael Scott told us that we miss 100 percent of the shots we don’t take (or was that Wayne Gretsky?). Jean-Ralphio from Parks and Rec… actually, don’t listen to him.

But when it comes to money, there’s actually a lot of helpful hacks to be found on the small screen, from characters who’ve dealt with the same budgeting woes as us all. Here are the best money-saving tips ripped straight from TV.

Make It Instead

Pawnee’s resident libertarian Ron Swanson dispensed a lot of wisdom over Parks and Recreation’s seven seasons. But between his takes on breakfast (the best meal of the day), skim milk (it’s just water that’s lying), and clear alcohol (only for rich women on diets) lay a handy financial tip: DIY, baby.

Once, instead of shelling out for a pair of expensive wedding bands, Swanson forged some himself out of a lamp he ripped from the wall — something “any moron with a crucible, an acetylene torch, and a cast iron waffle maker” could have done.

Now, we’re not suggesting you actually start liquefying your own metal (please don’t, actually) but, from making homemade face masks out of the things in your pantry to learning to fix busted clothes instead of replacing them with new ones, getting crafty really can save you some serious bucks.

As Ron says: “People who buy things are suckers”.
