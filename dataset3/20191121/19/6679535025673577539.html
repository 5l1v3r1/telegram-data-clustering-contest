Vizag to go ‘pink’ on December 8

‘Pinkathon’ aims at promoting women’s health and active lifestyle

21 Nov 2019, 19:30 by Special Correspondent

The fifth edition of the ‘Vizag Going Pink’ run, popularly known as ‘Pinkathon’, will be organised on Beach Road on December 8. The women-only run aims at creating awareness on women’s health and promoting a healthy lifestyle for prevention of diseases.

Actor and fitness enthusiast Milind Soman, who is the ambassador for the ‘Pinkathon’, was in the city on Thursday to promote the event that will be held in 3km, 5km and 10km categories. ‘Pinkathon’ was started in Mumbai in 2012 and subsequently moved to other cities such as Bengaluru, Chennai and Visakhapatnam.

“It is a memorable journey which inspired thousands of women to choose a healthy lifestyle. Running makes the mind stronger and drives out negativity,” Mr. Milind told the media here on Thursday.

Describing women as ‘central to the family’, he said when they understand the value of keeping themselves healthy, they can contribute to a healthier society and country.

Mr. Milind was of the view that everyone should do whatever they could as an individual to raise awareness about a healthy lifestyle to keep diseases at bay. “Running as a fitness regimen can help check various diseases including breast cancer. Women need to step out of the house and adopt a healthy lifestyle practices including physical exercise,” said Mr. Milind.

Breast cancer

Referring to breast cancer, he said the disease was among the leading causes of cancer deaths among women worldwide. “However, it can be cured if detected early. The United Sisters Foundation (USF) started its activities in Mumbai in 2012 to create awareness among women on the need for an active lifestyle to stay fit,” he said.

participating in the promotional event, Sheela, a young cancer survivor, recalled that she was shocked when she was diagnosed with uterine cancer. “I spoke to my friends and the survivors. I decided to fight it out. Now, I am completely cured. My regular running helped a lot,” she said.

Regional Chief Operating Officer of HCG Cancer Centre Sailesh Guntu said that breast cancer accounts for 30% of all cancers among women. The technological advances in medicine ensures early detection and cure,” he said.
