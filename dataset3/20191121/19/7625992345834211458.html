Japanese hotel Asahi Ryokan offers $1 rooms for guests who live-stream everything

21 Nov 2019, 19:10 by Irina Ivanova

Visitors to Fukuoka, Japan can get a hotel room at the bargain price of $1 — but there's a catch.

To take advantage of the deal, visitors must agree to live-stream almost everything. That doesn't include using the bathroom, which is outside the range of the table-mounted camera, and the guests are permitted to turn out the lights during sleep, the Washington Post reported.

Everything else is fair game for 24 hours.

"Young people nowadays don't care much about the privacy," 27-year-old Tetsuya Inoue, the hotel's manager, told CNN.

Inoue was looking for ideas to broaden revenues for the 30-year-old guesthouse, which his grandmother owns, when he hit upon the idea of streaming after an English visitor livestreamed his own stay, according to the Washington Post. 

While a $1 a night price is clearly unprofitable, Inoue hopes to monetize the gimmick by selling advertising against the live stream, according to CNN. YouTube allows streams to sell ads once they reach 4,000 views.
