For the love of money… $200 swimming competition ends in tragedy as two men drown in dam

A $200 swimming competition ended in tragedy after two competitors drowned in Musinje Dam in Juru Growth Point, Mashonaland East Province.

According to police reports Marshal Dhlamini (25) and Benjamin Dick (25) in the company of three other colleagues had gone to the dam to take a bath before they decided to place $200 wager for a swimming competition across the dam.

Acting police spokesperson for Mashonaland East Province Assistant Inspector Misheck Denhere confirmed the incident.

“Circumstances are that on November 12, around 1700 hours, Dhlamini and Dick in the company of three others went to Musinje Dam to take a bath.
“Upon arrival at the dam, Dhlamini challenged Dick for a swimming race across the dam and suggested that the winner will get $200, Dick accepted the challenge and the two managed to swim across and they decided to complete the race by swimming back. However, they got exhausted while in the middle of the dam and drowned,” he said.
