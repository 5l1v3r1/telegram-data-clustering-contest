Night shifts for women at factories

21 Nov 2019, 00:30 by Bangalore Mirror Bureau

The Karnataka government issued a notification on Wednesday allowing women to work in night shifts (7 pm to 6 am) in all factories. The state government has in the notification cited Madras High Court judgement striking down Section 66(1)(b) of the Factories Act, 1948, which prohibited employment of women to work in night shifts, and certain conditions laid down by it. Currently, women are allowed to work night shifts only in the IT and IT-enabled service sectors. With this, women can work during the night in other sectors including manufacturing.


The notified rules also specify that the employer shall provide from their residence and back facility to the women workers female security guard) and (for night shift) and security guards (including ) each transportation vehicle shall also be equipped with CCTV Camera. The establishments should also keep a separate vehicle be kept ready to meet the emergent situation such as hospitalisation, in case of injury or incidental acts of harassment etc. The factories should also ensure that not less than one third of strength of the supervisor shift-in-charge or foreman or other supervisory staff shall be women. The employer should also appoint not less than two female wardens per night shift who shall go around and work as Special welfare Assistants.
