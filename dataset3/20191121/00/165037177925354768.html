"My challenge is to triumph at Madrid"

21 Nov 2019 by BeSoccer

Martin Odeegard, on loan at Real Sociedad from Real Madrid, spoke to 'El Larguero' and the Norweigan stated that his main objective is none other than to triumph at Real Madrid.

Despite only being 20-years-old, Odegaard is one of the names to look out for in the next few years. The attacker, who currently plays at Real, hopes to contnue evolving.

After not having a place in the Madrid squad, the team decided to loan him to Real Sociedad, where the footballer feels very happy and loved by all. 

"I feel very comfortable here, like I am at home. It's very nice and friendly and it has been easy to come here with the team. I am very happy here. It is a very beautiful city," he started saying to 'El Larguero'.

Curiously, the Norweigan will against the team who actually own him this Saturday, Madrid. The attacker, having recovered from an ankle injury, showed his future ambition at Real Madrid.

"My challenge is to go back and triumph at Madrid. That's why I signed with them, to play there. But now I am happy here. I am not in a hurry. If I play in two or five years at Madrid, it's all the same. The time doesn't matter. But one day I want to play in Madrid," he said.

On the current situation of Madrid, this is what Odegaard said: "They are looking good. They are better now. It will be a difficult match."

In relation to if he will celebrate a goal against Madrid, he made it quite clear: "I would not do so because it is against the team that I belong to".

Finally, Odegaard did not rule out being on loan at Real for two years. "Yes, that is the plan. I'm clear. Then in football you never know what will happen, but the idea is to stay here for two years," he concluded.
