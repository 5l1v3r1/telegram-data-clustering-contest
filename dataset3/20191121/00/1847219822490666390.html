Greece to close overcrowded migrant camps

21 Nov 2019 by BNN

The Greek government has announced plans to shut down overcrowded open migrant camps to open closed and smaller centres.

British public broadcaster BBC reports on Wednesday, November 20, the camps on five Greek islands off the Turkish coast are overcrowded. In September a woman died when a fire broke out at the Moria camp in Lesbos, where more than 15,000 asylum seekers live on a site designed for fewer than 3,000.

The proposed sites are being described as «closed pre-departure centres», an announcement which the government in Athens hopes will be noticed by traffickers and potential asylum seekers alike.

«A clear message should be sent to those planning or thinking of coming to the country illegally when they aren’t entitled to asylum,» explained Greek government spokesman Stelios Petsas.

The past few months have seen a big increase in numbers, with 10,551 arrivals by sea in Greece in September and a further 8,996 in October, BBC reports.
