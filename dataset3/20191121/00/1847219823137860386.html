Rats in Estonia cut e-governance services

21 Nov 2019 by BNN

In Estonia, which sees itself as the world’s first digital nation, has experienced an interruption of state e-services, caused, apparently, by rats in Harju County around Tallinn.

Estonian public broadcaster ERR reports on Thursday, November 21, that the interruption came in the afternoon on Wednesday, November 20.

The damage meant the state portal Eesti.ee, services provided by the health insurance fund (EHIF) including issuing digital prescriptions, and several other e-services provided by the Estonian State Information System Agency (RIA) were not working.

Head of the Estonian State Network Department Kaido Plovits commented that the network was not fully protected from such incidents. «We apologize for any inconvenience and will work hard to fully restore the services soon,» Plovits stated on November 20.

Most e-services were restored by Wednesday evening, ERR reports.
