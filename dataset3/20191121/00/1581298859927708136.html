Esperance disability draft plan open to public feedback

21 Nov 2019, 00:47 by Jake Dietsch

The Shire of Esperance's draft Disability Access and Inclusion Plan for 2019-2024 is open for input from the public.

Related story: Disability access plan's total rewrite

All local governments must have a plan to outline how they will make their services, buildings and information accessible for people with disabilities.

Once the plan has been endorsed by council, a reference group will be formed to assist in the development of an implementation plan to outline specific actions and improvement measures.

The shire has said those strategies would not be 'set in stone' for the next five years.

The plan is reviewed annually and can be updated at any time to reflect needs.

If you would like to be part of the reference group call 9042 0346 or email shire@esperance.wa.gov.au

A copy of the plan is available online here and can be obtained from the Shire's Administration Building.

Submissions on the plan can be sent to the shire's PO Box at 507, Esperance, WA 6450 or emailed to shire@esperance.wa.gov.au by 4pm Tuesday, December 10.
