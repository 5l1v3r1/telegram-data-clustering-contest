Cuadrado extends Juve contract until 2022

21 Nov 2019 by BeSoccer

Juan Cuadrado has followed Leonardo Bonucci in committing his future to Juventus by penning a new deal.

Winger Juan Cuadrado has signed a two-and-a-half-year contract extension at Juventus, the Serie A champions have confirmed.

Juventus have now made two great incorporations to their squad. In fact, despite the approaching winter transfer window, the club, owned by Andre Agnelli, has been concentrating on keeping some of their players at the club.

And, after Bonucci, one of those was Cuadrado, as was already known. The last negotiations had progressed very well, so it was hoped that the official announcement would arrive soon, as happened this Thursday. 

The Colombian arrived at Juventus as a striker four years ago and now, at the hands of Sarri, has established himself as one of the key men at the Italian club. For this reason, his new contract with the improved conditions is already a reality.

Despite other players, such as Cristiano, Dybala and De Ligt taking all the attention, at the moment, the Colombian's time at Juventus has been impressive, as he has won four league titles, three Copas and one SuperCup.

Moreover, this makes him one of the most loved players by Juventus fans.
