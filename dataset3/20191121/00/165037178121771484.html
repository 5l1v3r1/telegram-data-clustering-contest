Las Palmas rule out signing Barca's Riqui Puig to replace Viera

21 Nov 2019 by BeSoccer

Las Palmas are working on the signing of a player to substitute Jonathan Viera in January 2020. However, they are not planning on signing Riqui Puig from Barca B, Miguel Angel Ramirez, the Las Palmas president, confirmed in a press conference.

Viera, on loan from Beijing Guoan until 31st December, will almost certainly return to China. Although Las Palmas are leaving the door open for him to stay on, it looks very unlikely. They are looking for a bigger name to replace the local player. 

Ramirez surprised people by publicly revealing that Jonathan Viera earns 10 million euros gross at the Chinese outfit, "more than the entire Las Palmas budget. It was difficult for them to send him on loan to us so we are very grateful to Beijing Guoan," he highlighted. 

"We will have him until 31st December and when the date comes nearer, we will see what happens. I cannot be publicly airing the club's strategy. The player has helped us to get victories, but with him we are 15th in the league. If he leaves, we will bring another footballer and we are already working on that," the president said.

On the other hand, Ramirez has confirmed that Tana will return after his loan spell at Zheijang Greentown FC from the Chinese second division. He will probably rejoin the group in December so that Pepe Mel can decide whether he stays or looks for a new club in January.
