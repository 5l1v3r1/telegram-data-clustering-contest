Things to do today in Bengaluru

21 Nov 2019, 00:30 by Bangalore Mirror Bureau

n the rocks
Forget your weekday blues and head to a gig of pop and rock songs by Rachna Ramdin and Joshua Paulmer. Ramdin is a vocalist, born and brought up in Mauritius. She has been singing since the age of 10, and hails from a family of musicians, who sing in English and French. While she might be new to Bengaluru, she has already done a number of acoustic gigs in the city. As for Paulmer, he has been instrumental in innovating with new styles of music, and covering different genres. His repertoire has more than 600 songs, ranging from the 60s to current hits.

WHERE: 1522 The Pub, opposite Sindhoor Convention Center, 6th Phase, 100 Feet Road,
JP Nagar WHEN: 8pm, November 21 CALL: 7829118909

The truth about fast fashion
Do you know what goes into making all the clothes we buy and hoard? Find out at this movie screening. Riverblue uncovers the secrets behind the apparel industry and shows how the irresponsible manufacturing is leading to disposal of toxic waste and how effluents affect our water bodies and human health. The event is presented by Looking Glass, a platform where individuals from different walks of life come together to learn and discuss topics around sustainability.
WHERE: Shoonya, Lal Bagh Main Road
WHEN: 5.30pm onwards, November 21
ENTRY: Free
CALL: 7760832226

Attend a group show
Myths and legends are an integral part of human existence. They have been around us all the time. Though their validity is susceptible, nobody seems to mind them as they stand as proof of the human belief in divine powers. A new show has unveiled a collection of popular mythological stories in form of art works that will take you to times when gods co-habited with humans, divine voices could be heard and visions seen. Fourteen artists are displaying their work at Stories of Mytholofy.

WHERE: Art Houz, 63, Palace Road
WHEN: 10.30am-6.30pm, November 21-30 (closed on Sundays) CALL: 41148662

They’ll sing it all
Thayirsadam Blues are your friendly neighbourhood blues/pop-rock/R&B band. They are a nine-piece band, who are inspired by the elegance and simplicity of Thayir Sadam (curd rice) – that’s why the name. At a gig tonight, they will belt out a soulful blend of acoustic, modern pop, rock, jazz, funk and reggae music with a bluesy touch to it. That’s because they are inspired by a whole lot of musicians, from Coldplay to Eagles, Eric Clapton to Etta James, Bruno Mars to James brown, and Frank Sinatra to Jamiroquai. And opening the evening for them will be neo-soul band Paula And The Associates.

WHERE: Fandom at Gilly’s Redefined, 1st floor, 4th Block, Koramangala
WHEN: 8pm, November 21
ENTRY: `300, insider.in

Channel your inner artist
Leading artist Dr MS Murthy will conduct a year-long workshop called ‘Kalasanskar – Watercolour Workshop – Indian Wash Method 2019’. This is to educate artists and art lovers about the various aspects of visual arts and its centrality in our lives. He will teach you everything from drawing to painting to working with water colours, and composition. All the workshops are carefully planned and well structured so that the participants get sufficient time to reflect on the concepts and practice them too.

WHERE: Bhoomi The Centre For Art Studies, Thalaghattapura Post, Banashankari 6th Stage
WHEN: 10am-5pm, November 21
CALL: 9844157982

Watch a film
If you are looking around for some inspiration, this movie screening will do you good. Directed by Clémentine Deroudille, the film Robert Doisneaue features previously unissued photographs and video archives as well as interviews of Robert’s friends and partners in crime. It tells how this kid from the poor suburbs became a superstar photographer, a determined artist and a purveyor of happiness.

WHERE: Church Street Social, No-46/1, Cobalt Building WHEN: 7pm onwards, November 21
ENTRY: `300
CALL: 9886673090

Roll the dice
Find a whole new world of wonderful board games beyond Monopoly and Scrabble at ‘Table-Top Thursday’. You don’t need to have played any board games before – you will be introduced to new ones here. They will also help you pick a game, set up, and explain the rules. Go solo, with a date or in a group – they’ve got something for everyone.

WHERE: Lahe Lahe, 80 Feet Road, Indiranagar
WHEN: 7pm-10pm, November 21
ENTRY: `200, insider.in

New in town
Hobby artists Shyla Prasad, Geetha Shantaram and Anuradha G will mount their works in an exhibition called Simplicity in creativity. Their artwork covers a gamut of skills, from folk art to figurative, zentangle and one-stroke, and the themes of nature and Lord Ganesha on canvas.

WHERE: Hall No 1, Karnataka Chitrakala Parishath, Kumara Krupa Road WHEN: 11am-7pm,

November 22-24 CALL: 22261816
