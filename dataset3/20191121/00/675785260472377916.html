British Airways hit by 'technical problems' as flights cancelled and delayed

BA says some flights have been delayed by a 'technical issue', although it denied that a global power outage was causing the problem - as some customers claimed

21 Nov 2019, 00:21 by Matthew Dresch, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/matthew-dresch/

British Airways customers are facing cancellations and delays after a 'technical issue' affected some flights.

Passengers around the world have reported delays to their flights, with some being told that a power outage is resposible.

However, BA dismissed these reports and said a 'small number' of flights have been pushed back because of a 'technical issue'.

Some passengers said their trips were rescheduled for the next day, while others claimed their flights were cancelled.

Flyer Liz Yates, from London, said her journey from Orlando, US, was pushed back because of a supposed 'power outage' at Gatwick.

She told Mirror Online: "The pilot said they don’t want us to board until they have a flight plan and because all the computers at Gatwick have gone down.

"He came and made the announcement personally because they were mid attempting to board people in wheelchairs when they got the word.

"They have to make some sort of arrangement with the civil aviation authority."

Ms Yates originally tweeted: "Apparently there’s a power outage at Gatwick and no flight plans are being released. Was supposed to take off ten minutes ago. Now no boarding for up to an hour.

 

"Could’ve spent this time with my mum.

"The one before us supposedly got out in time. Waiting for civil aviation to give a flight plan old school instead of email by the sounds of."

Tony Murrell told Mirror Online he was one of hundreds of flyers facing delays in Las Vegas.

He said: "My wife and I and hundreds of others are stuck at Las Vegas airport.

"We were first told at 10.34am there was a delay, the time now is 6.37pm (we're eight hours behind the UK).

"We were due to go at 2.30pm, flight BA2276. Our plane is here and the captain has told us he cannot file his flight plan with Gatwick so we cannot take off.

"There is another plane due to leave at 9pm for Heathrow, that will not be going either.

"What we want to know is when will BA draw a line under it and organise buses to a hotel for night."

Some passengers said their flight with BA had been cancelled early this morning.

Michael Miller tweeted: "@British_Airways just had an email to say our flight has been cancelled less than 24hours before we are due to depart, flight number ba2036 from Orlando mco to London Gatwick.

"No alternative options given and no reason for the cancellation either!!! Please explain???"

Sarah Davidson complained to about delays at Edinburgh Airport.

She tweeted: "Currently at Edinburgh Airport standing in a queue waiting for information about our cancelled onward 2037 to Orlando International Airport."

A British Airways spokesman responded to Ms Davidson, saying: "Due to technical issues a number of flights have been disrupted both yesterday and today.

"The decision to cancel the flight didn't come soon enough for us to notify customers ahead of their arrival at the airport, which has put additional pressure on the resources we have at some our smaller outstations."

Another Twitter user uploaded a picture showing their 11.40am flight from London to Orlando today had been cancelled.

A spokesman for BA told Mirror Online: "Our teams are working hard to fix a technical issue causing delays to a small number of flights this evening​. 

"We apologise to customers affected and we're assisting them with alternative arrangements."

The spokesman denied that computers at Gatwick had gone down.

Ross Beattie tweeted: "@British_Airways leaving or trying to leave Orlando but outage says no.

"Could be another hour leaving #orlandoairport. Credit to British Airways captain who spoke to us directly and #honestly #transparency giving us the truth."

Peter Dampier, who is flying to Heathrow Airport, added: "Their global flight management system seems to be down. Global outage. Stuck at Newark NYC here.

"We were told that by a BA gate manager.... Global BA IT outage."

One passenger told Mirror Online: "Sitting in Austin Texas because BA has stated they are having to do all their flight plans manually because their computers are down."

Another Twitter user said: "Can't print off a piece of paper to allow flights to depart - Antigua to Gatwick STUCK...The national airline... What a disgrace."

Michael Walker tweeted that flights from New York's John F. Kennedy International Airport were also affected.
