Oklahomans Alfre Woodard, Mary Kay Place, James Montague and Craig T. Sanger nominated for 2020 Film Independent Spirit Awards

21 Nov 2019, 23:43 by Brandy McDonnell

LOS ANGELES - Oklahomans Alfre Woodard, Mary Kay Place, James Montague and Craig T. Sanger have been nominated for the 2020 Film Independent Spirit Awards.

The 2020 Film Independent Spirit Award nominees were revealed today at The LINE LA in Los Angeles, presented by Zazie Beetz ("Atlanta, "Joker") and Natasha Lyonne ("Orange is the New Black," "Russian Doll").

The 2020 Spirit Awards honoring independent cinema storytellers will take place Feb. 8 and be broadcast live on IFC, according to a news release.

Tulsa native Alfre Woodard, a 1983 Academy Award nominee, is nominated for best female lead her performance as a prison warden worn down after years of carrying out death row executions in "Clemency."

As previously reported, writer-director Chinonye Chukwu earned the Grand Jury Prize in the U.S. Dramatic Competition at the this year's Sundance Film Festival with the hard-hitting film. "Clemency" also is nominated for best feature and Chukwu for best screenplay.

Tulsa native Mary Kay Place also is nominated best female lead for her performance in “Diane” in which she plays on a widowed, septuagenarian who fills her days serving food to the homeless, visiting ailing friends and desperately attempting to reach her drug-addicted son. But as these pieces of her existence begin to wither and fade, she is forced to look at her own identity—and memories she’d rather forget than face.

As previously reported, "Diane" was helmed by Kent Jones, a first-time narrative director and writer who won the Founders Award for Best Narrative Feature and Best Screenplay in a U.S. Narrative Feature Film at the 2018 Tribeca Film Festival. In addition, "Diane" is nominated for best first feature.

As previously reported, Woodard and Place and their respective films recently received similar nods for New York's Gotham Awards, which will be handed out Dec. 2.

And Oklahoma City-area filmmakers James Montague and Craig T. Sanger earned an Independent Spirit nod for best first screenplay for penning the sci-fi mystery "The Vast of Night," Edmond filmmaker Andrew Patterson's feature film directorial debut.

As previously reported, "The Vast of Night" earned strong reviews and was a hit at the Toronto International Film Festival, where the alien contact film in the Midnight Madness section. Amazon Studios has acquired worldwide rights to "The Vast of Night," which is set in the late 1950s in New Mexico and centers on a young switchboard operator (Sierra McCormick) and radio DJ (Jake Horowitz) seeking the source of a mysterious frequency.

In addition, Bartlesville-bred director Terrence Malick's new film "A Hidden Life" received a Film Independent Spirit nod for best feature film. The award will go to producers Elisabeth Bentley, Dario Bergesio, Grant Hill and Josh Jeter if the film wins.

As previously reported, the latest film from the three-time Oscar-nominated writer-director, “A Hidden Life” recently premiered in competition at the 72nd Cannes Film Festival in France, where it was nominated for the esteemed Palme d'Or (which went to South Korean director Bong Joon-ho for his satirical smash “Parasite”). The fact-based drama stars August Diehl (“Inglourious Basterds”) as Austrian Franz Jägerstätter, a devoted husband and father who refuses to fight for the Nazis in World War II, even as the possibility of execution threatens to tear him apart from his family.
