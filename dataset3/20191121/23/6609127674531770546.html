SCOTTISH SUN SAYS

Jeremy Corbyn’s plans for £11bn raid on North Sea oil sector could not be more idiotic

21 Nov 2019, 23:34 by The Scottish Sun

LABOUR were in turmoil in Scotland before today’s manifesto launch.

If anything, they’re now in even deeper trouble. Jeremy Corbyn’s manifesto rabbit, it turned out, was an £11billion raid on the North Sea oil sector.

The timing of this insane, short-sighted cash grab could not be more idiotic.

The struggling industry is still on its knees following the slump that started in 2014 and ravaged jobs and the north-east economy in the years that followed.

More than 100,000 livelihoods were lost then, at a conservative estimate.

And now the sector is just about picking itself back up, in part thanks to tax cuts and investment from the UK and Scottish Governments to encourage innovation and exploration.

Getting firms to invest — and not simply cut their losses and flee — has been hard enough in recent years.

This windfall tax will only make things worse.

And it risks the recovery that’s needed to put firms in a financially sound place, necessary for the decades of decommissioning that will follow the industry’s inevitable decline.

As Aberdeen University’s Professor Alex Kemp points out, the policy is also based on a mistaken belief that firms should have been taxed harder historically in line with countries like Norway.

This is comparing apples and pears, with taxes lower in the UK basin because the oil has been, generally, more expensive to extract in our waters than in Scandinavia.

It’s this kind of schoolboy error — or fundamental misunderstanding of reality — that has become a hallmark of Corbynomics.

Just like his pledges to boost public spending, while splurging billions on misguided nationalisations.

It doesn’t add up.

But you have to wonder if Mr Corbyn knows this — and simply wants to virtue signal and pursue his wrecking-ball ideology.

Because there appears to be a warping of reality with Labour.

Take their pledge for free school dinners in Scotland. It may sound nice, but education is devolved.

People have enough trouble understanding the complex political landscape without red herrings like this.

It’s no wonder polls show no sign of budging in Mr Corbyn’s favour.

He’s detached from reality.

And this manifesto isn’t going to change his woes.
