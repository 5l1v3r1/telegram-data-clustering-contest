PIE-EYED

World Championship Scotch Pie Awards 2019: Hungry Scottish Sun man on a munch mission to find the winning pie

21 Nov 2019, 23:21 by Graham Mann

AS a crusty old reporter with a bit of a gut I’ve been teased with the odd jibe of “Who ate all the pies?”

Now, I can happily confirm that yes, it was indeed me.

I’ve honed my taste buds at countless footie matches up and down the country for the best part of three decades.

And when I’m pie-eyed after a few pints I love nothing more than to tackle a steak bake.

In fact, I dread to think how much grease has dribbled down my arm over the years — but I reckon it could fill a good-sized Scottish Cup or three.

To get the chance to become an expert on my favourite subject seemed no more than pie in the sky.

So I was literally drooling when I was invited to join the seasoned pros judging contenders at the World Championship Scotch Pie Awards.

But my self-appointed authority quickly turned to mince.

It was a meaty learning curve that started with a word of caution from my judging team-mate Simon Young, a pie guru who’s been at 19 of the 21 annual competitions run by Scottish Bakers.

He warned: “This is a serious responsibility — bakers and butchers put a lot of time and effort into it.”

Words of wisdom from Simon — and our expert team leader — taught me there was more to judging pies than wolfing them down before unleashing a satisfied burp.

We mulled over a raft of characteristics such as ‘the bake’ and ‘boil out’ as well as shape, texture, taste and the filling-to-pastry ratio.

Before I knew it, I had set aside my carnivorous instinct of scooping dollops into my mouth and was now carefully wafting each pie’s aroma towards my twitching nostrils with a pretentious pinky stuck out.

I’m pretty sure it’s a look that would not go down well with your average footie punter.

But I’m absolutely certain they would give every one of the pies I tasted a resounding seal of approval.

The identity of the artisans behind the 450 bakery products showcased this week at Carnegie Conference Centre in Dunfermline remains a closely guarded secret.

The winners will be announced at a glittering awards ceremony in January when guests will tuck into pie, beans and chips served up by last year’s champ, The Little Bakery.

Alasdair Smith, chief executive of Scottish Bakers, revealed: “The top prize — to be named the World Scotch Pie Champion — is the single most coveted prize among pie-makers across the country.”

I will happily raise a Scotch pie — or two — to that.
