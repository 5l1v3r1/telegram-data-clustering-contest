One Mic Stand mentors want to collaborate with Shah Rukh Khan, Sachin Tendulkar and Priyanka Chopra

One Mic Stand mentor Zakir Khan said Shah Rukh Khan is not only intelligent but also knows what to speak while interacting with people.

21 Nov 2019, 09:40 by Sana Farzeen

Amazon Prime Original’s One Mic Stand has been receiving mixed reviews. The unscripted show created by Sapan Verma features five celebrities – Shashi Tharoor, Richa Chadha, Bhuvan Bam, Taapsee Pannu and Vishal Dadlani trying their hand at stand-up comedy for the first time. And, mentoring them are ace comedians Zakir Khan, Rohan Joshi, Angad Singh Ranyal, Ashish Shakya and Kunal Kamra.

Recently, host Sapan and the mentors spoke exclusively to indianexpress.com. While they revealed how interesting it was for them to mentor the celebrities, we asked who would they want to collaborate with in Season 2 of One Mic Stand. And pat came the replies:

Zakir Khan: It has to be Shah Rukh Khan. I feel he should just release an hour of his interviews and people should pay to watch him. He is not only intelligent but also knows what to speak while interacting with people.

Sapan Verma: Yes, SRK would be the best choice. We have worked with him on various award shows, and if he finds out you are into comedy, he will actually discuss the space with you. And he is so insightful, not just about contemporary films but he has followed all the comedians of the 80s and 90s. I don’t know if he was serious, but he once told me that he wanted to do stand-up. But that time, the culture hadn’t developed much in India. I hope he can feature in season 2 and I promise, all of us would be more than happy to mentor him.

Rohan Joshi: I would pick Sachin Tendulkar. He has lived through the entire dramatic Indian cricket journey for 25 years. I don’t think there’s anyone who would have more stories than him.

Angad Singh Ranyal: Priyanka Chopra. We recently shot a video with her and she is genuinely so funny. Also, she has this middle-class upbringing which increases her relatability factor.
