Best Superfast Fibre Deals

Superfast fibre is currently the most popular form of broadband on the market. Boasting speeds of 36Mbps and up, these are great for larger homes that use a variety of different devices at once.

There is plenty of choice at the moment as well, with the UK's broadband network constantly improving and thriving in many areas. Some providers such as Virgin or Hyperoptic can be geographically restricted, so you won't always be able to get coverage, but most areas can get some form of superfast broadband at the moment.

The government is promising a heavy expansion of the broadband network over the next few years, but here's what you can pick up right now.

Best Mobile Phone Deals

Mobile phone deals will include monthly contracts, Pay As You Go SIM cards, and everything in between.

What to expect on Black Friday

Outside of Black Friday, providers such as Vodafone and Virgin Media have offered incentives such as free 4K TVs or Amazon Echo speakers. Right now, Virgin is offering a whole month of Amazon Prime in its TV and broadband bundles. This ends on November 13, but similar deals could appear closer to Black Friday and Cyber Monday.

For mobiles, the recent launch of the iPhone 11 will continue to grab a lot of attention, while retailers will continue to drop monthly prices on the Galaxy S10 series as we gear up for an updated device in 2020.

5G could be a big topic of discussion during the sales, with most UK providers bidding to have some 5G coverage by the end of 2019, this could be the perfect opportunity to get a few more early adopters on board for the advanced networks rolling out across the UK. This means we could see some deals on 5G phones from the likes of Samsung, Huawei, OnePlus, and more.

Three is pledging to build the UK's fastest 5G network, effectively taking away EE's crown as the fastest 4G network. Unlike EE, Three is also promising no prise rises on its SIM cards and applicable contracts. That means if your phone is 5G ready, your Three SIM card could be as well. This is vastly different to EE who offer premium 5G contracts at an increased cost in comparison to the regular 4G SIM cards.
