Hailey Baldwin slays on solo Berlin trip after slamming Selena Gomez drama

21 Nov 2019, 12:16

Hailey Baldwin slayed in Berlin as she headed to a swish Calvin Klein party without husband Justin Bieber.

Despite the Sorry singer also appearing in a campaign for the brand alongside his wife, he was notably absent at the bash, held in the German capital.

Not that the model seemed too fussed, as she rocked up solo at Musikbrauerei in an off-the-shoulder, black and white top, paired with fitted dark skinny jeans.

The 22-year-old completed the look with a pair of the highest, deep red heels.

Hailey’s appearance comes after she was forced to hit back at claims she had shaded Justin’s ex, Selena Gomez, on Instagram.

Because we’re back in 2015, apparently.

It all kicked off when Selena released her new song, Lose You To Love Me, last month – with fans suggesting the bop was about her ex.

She can be heard singing about being ‘replaced’ in two months ‘like it was easy’, which some took as a dig at Justin, who got engaged to Kendall Jenner’s best friend last July, shortly after he broke things off with Selena.

Soon after the song dropped, Stephen Baldwin’s daughter shared a screenshot to her Instagram page of the song I’ll Kill You, by Summer Walker.

Side note: A total banger.

But her Instagram antics put her directly in the firing line, with fans adamant she was throwing shade at Selena, threatening to ‘kill her’.

Yes, seriously.

Hailey soon hit back at all the drama and denied the accusations in a now-deleted comment, insisting: ‘Please stop with this nonsense… there is no “response”, this is complete BS.’

Discussing the emotional song, the Wolves singer told Radio Disney she never wants to be inauthentic.

‘I wrote this song over a year ago and it’s obviously a very emotional song for me but it’s interesting to see how far I’ve come from that point to now,’ she explained.

‘And me sharing my story is exactly what I’ve always done, because I can’t be inauthentic, I can’t pretend that I’m not going through something when it’s obvious that I had been.

‘It’s one of those songs where I feel it’s out, it’s released, and I hope that girls and guys all over the world can feel supported. Because that’s a feeling everyone has felt.

‘That was something that I needed to do for myself, regardless of what was going to come after.’

So, that’s that then.
