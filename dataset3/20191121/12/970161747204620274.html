Tories launch 'fake Labour manifesto' website despite 'FactCheckUK' backlash

21 Nov 2019, 12:29

The Conservative Party has been accused of more ‘dirty tricks’ after creating a website which at first glance looks like it belongs to their political rivals.

Labourmanifesto.co.uk was launched moments before Jeremy Corbyn unveiled his party’s manifesto in Birmingham today.

The Tories have apparently paid Google advertising fees so their site appears at the of the page when some users search ‘Labour’.

It has a bright red background, a picture of Corbyn and slogans including ‘no plan for Brexit’, ‘higher taxes’ and ‘two more referendums’.

Negative headlines about the Labour leader and a message near the top saying it is a ‘website by the Conservative party’ means it’s easy enough to see who’s really running it.
