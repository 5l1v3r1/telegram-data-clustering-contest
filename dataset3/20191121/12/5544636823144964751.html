Senate Approves Buhari’s Request To Increase VAT, Six Other Taxes

21 Nov 2019, 12:33 by Olugbenga Ige

The Nigerian Senate has approved the request by President Muhammadu Buhari to increase Value-Added Tax (VAT) from 5 percent to 7.5 percent.

Concise News had reported that the Federal Executive Council (FEC) in September approved an increase in VAT and sent a bill to the National Assembly for consent.

However, the Red chamber passed the Finance bill during Thursday’s plenary after it scaled through the third reading on the floor of the Senate.

The Senate also approved the amendment of six tax provisions and make them more responsive to tax reform policies.

It also seeks to amend the Customs and Excise Tariff Act to encourage local manufacturers.

The legislation under the bill includes Companies Income Tax, Value Added Tax, Customs and Excise Tariff, Capital Gains Tax Act, Petroleum Profit Tax, Personal Income Tax, and Stamp Duties Act.

The Minority Leader, Enyinnaya Abaribe, and Senators Ifeanyi Ubah, Gabriel Suswan and Abba Moro, however, raised objections to the bill.

According to the lawmakers, the increment in taxes would further compound the sufferings and pains of Nigerians.
