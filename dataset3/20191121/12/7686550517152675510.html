Man charged with murder after 22-year-old dies in hospital from his injuries

Shafiul Islam was found with serious head injuries a week ago

21 Nov 2019, 12:06 by Cathy Owen

A man has been charged with murder after the death of a 22-year-old man in hospital.

Shafiul Islam, from the Shaftesbury area of Newport, was found with serious head injuries on Tewkesbury Walk, in the city around 11.55pm on Thursday, November 14. He died from his injuries at the Royal Gwent Hospital.

A spokesman for Gwent Police said: "Our thoughts remain with his family, who are being supported by specialist officers."

He added that a 22-year-old man from the Newport area has been charged with murder.

Conlan Dunnion appeared at Newport Magistrates' Court on Thursday and was remanded in custody until Friday when he is due to appear at Newport Crown Court.

There have also been other arrests in connection with the investigation:

On Saturday, November 16, three people were arrested on suspicion of attempted murder, including a 46-year-old man and a 37-year-old woman, both from the Neath area and a 41 year old man from the Pontardawe area. All three have since been released under investigation.

Following police enquiries, further arrests were made.

  • On Tuesday, a 41-year-old man from the Cardiff area was arrested on suspicion of attempted murder.
  • On Wednesday a 32-year-old man from the Newport area was also arrested on suspicion of attempted murder.

After Mr Islam's death were re-arrested on suspicion of the murder.
