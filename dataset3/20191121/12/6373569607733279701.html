Why Plymouth Argyle's new date for Grimsby Town home game might not be until 2020

The fixture was postponed on November 2 due to structural damage to the Barn Park End roof

21 Nov 2019, 12:21 by Chris Errington

Plymouth Argyle’s postponed League Two game against Grimsby Town from earlier this month is unlikely to be played until the New Year.

The Home Park fixture on November 2 was called off less than three hours before kick-off due to structural damage to the Barn Park End roof after overnight gales.

Rearranging a new date for the game has been complicated by the fact that Grimsby have had three matches postponed over the past month.

Argyle’s continued involvement in the FA Cup has ruled out the possibility of playing the Mariners next Saturday, which would otherwise have been a free date for both clubs.

Grimsby lost 2-0 away to Newport County in their first round replay on Wednesday night.

The Pilgrims are in second round action away to Bristol Rovers at the Memorial Stadium on Sunday December 1.

That leaves the possibility of a replay at Home Park on the week commencing December 9, ruling out another potential date to take on Grimsby.

The Mariners had their league game at home to Cheltenham Town on October 26 postponed due to a waterlogged pitch.

Then, after the Argyle fixture was called off, Grimsby also had a free Saturday last weekend.

Their scheduled home match against Newport County was postponed due to international call-ups for their Welsh opponents.

Grimsby have now rearranged to host Cheltenham at Blundell Park next Tuesday, November 26, but a date to play Argyle is still to be agreed.

Pilgrims’ boss Ryan Lowe said: “I don’t think anything has been sorted just yet because we have to be careful of the FA Cup, in case there is a replay.

“I think they were trying to get it in that week but obviously we can’t. I believe it’s going to be in the New Year.”
