What the anti-apartheid poster in Rachel Riley's t-shirt actually said

21 Nov 2019, 12:04

Countdown presenter Rachel Riley has been criticised for posing in a t-shirt showing a doctored photo of Jeremy Corbyn.

In the picture, the Labour leader holds a placard saying: ‘Jeremy Corbyn is a racist endeavour’.

But what did the poster say before it was edited?

The original photo was taken outside the South African embassy in London in 1984, during a protest demanding Nelson Mandela’s release from prison.

It shows Corbyn being led away by two police officers for defying a ban on demonstrating outside.

The original placard reads: ‘Defend the right to protest against apartheid. Join this picket.’
