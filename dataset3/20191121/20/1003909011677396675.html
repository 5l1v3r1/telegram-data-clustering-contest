Block Heater to melt February cold with focus on African, Indigenous artists

21 Nov 2019, 20:39 by Eric Volmers

The Calgary Folk Music Festival will focus on artists of African descent and Indigenous performers for its fifth Block Heater this February, offering a wide array of performers and genres.

Now it its fifth year, the February festival will run from Feb. 20 to 22 in various venues in Inglewood and East Village. Spoken word star Shane Koyczan, whose father is First Nations, is among the headliners as is Mohawk Powwow pioneer DJ Shub. February’s Black Future Month will be represented by Chadian-born Afrobeat performer AfrotroniX, New York Afro-roots singer-songwriter Cécile Doo-Kingué, Senegalese-born percussionist and songwriter Élage Diouf, Los Angeles punk-blues singer songwriter Sunny War, Philadelphia R & B performer Son Little, Calgary R & B singer Lynn Olagundoye and Edmonton neo-soul singer Karimah.

Edmonton’s Plains Cree band nehiyawak, Winnipeg First Nations singer-songwriter Richard Inman, and Saskatchewan’s Onion Lake Cree Nation DJ Tchutchu will also perform.

“I set out to make sure we had a nice range of artists of African descent and styles,” said Calgary Folk Music Festival artistic director Kerry Clarke. “In general, with programming in the two festivals I’m aiming to have a really significant number of Indigenous artists and that’s an ongoing strategy.”

Koyczan is one of the country’s most renowned poets and spoken-word artists. A 2017 documentary chronicled the Vancouver-based artist’s reconciliation with his Indigenous father.

“You can’t really pigeonhole him into spoken-word,” Clarke says. “He’s a storyteller. There’s something infinitely powerful about how he presents and what he says.”

Also scheduled to perform this year is Calgary indie-rocker Chad VanGaalen, Latin-grooved Montreal-based Mexican-Canadian hip-hop artist Boogat, scratch DJ Kid Koala, Vancouver-based indie-pop singer Hanna Georgas, alt-country songwriter Justin Rutledge, Toronto experimental folkies The Weather Station and Winnipeg native Del Barber.

Local artists include 36?, Amy Nelson, Bella White, Jess Knights, Sunglaciers and Wyatt Louis.

Block Heater will be held at Festival Hall, Studio Bell, King Eddy, the Central Library, the Ironwood and Gorilla Whale.

Block Heater runs from Feb. 20 to 22. For a full lineup, visit calgaryfolkfest.com.
