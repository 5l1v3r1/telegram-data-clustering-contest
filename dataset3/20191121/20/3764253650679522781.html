Confusion as Kano residents take to streets over sacking of 4 new emirs installed by Ganduje

21 Nov 2019, 20:18

- Protest is reportedly going on in some communities in Kano state over court ruling on new emirates

- Governor Ganduje had in May created four new first-class emirates in the state

- The Kano state high court had on Thursday, November 21, sacked all the new emirs

Hours after the sacking of four emirs installed by Governor Abdullahi Ganduje of Kano state, some residents of Kano communities are currently embarking on a protest, according to the report by Premium times.

Legit.ng reported how the Kano high court nullified the creation of four new first-class emirates by the state government.

The four new emirates for which Governor Ganduje named first-class emirs in the state in May are Bichi, Rano, Karaye and Gaya.

Justice Usman Na’Abba ruled on Thursday, November 21, that the law creating the new emirates did not follow due process.

After the judgement, scores of residents of Karaye, one of the affected areas, trooped out to protest the judgement.

It was gathered that a similar protest is ongoing in Gaya.

According to the report, there are unconfirmed reports that similar protests were holding in other emirates.

The online news platform said that in Karaye, the residents carried placards, whose contents were in Hausa, to show their grievances at the judgment.

“Leave us in our new emirate, the Karaye emirate,” one of the placards reads.

“The people of Karaye are in support of the new emirate,” another placard reads.

Meanwhile, Legit.ng had previously reported that the appointment and creation of four additional emirates in the state was on Thursday, November 21, nullified by a state high court.

It was reported that while delivering the verdict, Justice Usman Na’abba, the presiding judge, declared the creation of the four emirates did not follow due process.
