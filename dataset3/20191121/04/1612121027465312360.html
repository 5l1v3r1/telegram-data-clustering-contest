Samsung quickly releases an improved Galaxy Fold for China

21 Nov 2019, 04:52 by Jared Peters

Meet the Samsung W20 5G, an incrementally improved Galaxy Fold set to release in China. It’s pretty strange for Samsung to launch a better model of their daring folding phone so soon, but it’s not quite the Galaxy Fold 2 that you might be expecting.

Small changes, better 5G

The W20 5G isn’t a fully new device, and it’s pretty much keeping the same design as the Galaxy Fold. It does, however, have a stronger, more reinforced frame, which should alleviate some of the things that caused the Galaxy Fold to be recalled in the first place.

It’s also a 5G phone that’s capable of utilizing China’s non-standard 5G networks, which keeps Samsung in a competitive position with Huawei going forward.

There are some internal bumps, too, but nothing major. The phone sports a Snapdragon 855+ CPU, plus 12GB of RAM and 512GB of internal storage. The battery is actually barely smaller than in the regular Galaxy Fold, but ideally the better CPU should offset that.
