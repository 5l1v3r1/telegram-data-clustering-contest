Camera leaks hints at 8K recording, 108MP sensor for the Galaxy S11

21 Nov 2019, 04:30 by Jared Peters

Galaxy S11 leaks have started to ramp up, and now Samsung’s own camera software is starting to hint at what’s to come with their 2020 flagship.

The latest versions of the camera application have some references to higher-resolution video recording, as well as to a phone with a massive megapixel count. These kinds of things aren’t always accurate, but if Samsung is building software to reference new hardware, we can make some pretty good guesses.

The app now has references to brand new photography features, like Director’s View, Single Take Photo, Night Hyperlapse, and a few other things, plus strings that mention whopping 8K video recording. The Galaxy S10 can handle 4K recording, so doubling that up to 8K recording would make for a killer marketing feature. Samsung’s newest Exynos chip also supports 8K recording, so everything lines up for next year.

Additionally, the app also mentions a phone with a 108MP sensor. What phone is Samsung likely to release soon with a megapixel count that high? I’ll let you guess.

There’s one last fuzzy detail that references a device with a 20:9 aspect ratio. Since Samsung uses this app across multiple devices, this isn’t a guarantee that the Galaxy S11 will be adopting the taller aspect ratio. It’s certainly a possibility, but with how many phones that they launch every year, it could very well be a specific device launched in South America at a very specific demographic, too. Hard to tell this early on.

source: XDA Developers
