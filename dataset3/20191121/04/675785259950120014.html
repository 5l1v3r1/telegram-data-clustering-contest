Bride 'dreading' wedding dress fitting loses 8st by cutting one thing from diet

Kylie Mulcahy, 30, from Wollongong, near Sydney, Australia, started shedding the pounds after she was diagnosed with Polycystic Ovary Syndrome and handed a special diet to follow

21 Nov 2019, 04:11 by Matthew Dresch, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/matthew-dresch/

A bride who was 'dreading' trying on a wedding dress lost an incredible eight stone by cutting one thing from her diet.

Kylie Mulcahy, 30, decided to shed the pounds because she feared her size 20 frame would not fit into a white gown.

Ms Mulcahy, from Wollongong, near Sydney, Australia, was 120kg at her heaviest, although her weight plummeted after changing her exercise routine and eating habits.

When she finally started visiting bridal shops in 2018 she slipped into her size eight dress with ease.

The bride, who wed her husband in 2019, has now revealed that the key to her weight loss was ditching dairy from her diet.

She told the Daily Mail : "I remember thinking if I ever got married the thought of walking into a wedding dress shop would scare me because I would never fit into anything, the people would judge me.

"But when I went into a store a year after losing all of the weight and I could pretty much wear any of them, it was a great feeling.

"I was exercising with a personal trainer at the gym in 2016 - who wrote me a diet plan filled with protein and vegetables - and I still wasn't able to lose weight."

Ms Mulcahy began gaining weight in her 20s, even though she led a healthy life.

She lost the first 15kg with meal replacement shakes and Lite N' Easy healthy dishes.

However, she started shedding the serious pounds after being diagnosed with Polycystic Ovary Syndrome in 2017.

A nutritionist told her to cut out dairy and gluten to help deal with her insulin levels.

Her new low carb and sugar diet, combined with a exercise routine inspired by YouTube workout videos, resulted in her dramatic weight loss.

When she finally went wedding dress shopping, she was overwhelmed with relief after falling in love with the first gown she tried on.

The bride said: "It's so cool to be able to try something on and have it fit perfectly."

She added that her aunties burst into tears and said she looked beautiful when they saw her on her wedding day in March.
