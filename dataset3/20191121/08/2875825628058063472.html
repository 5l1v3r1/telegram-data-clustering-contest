Moise Kean told he can make Italy's Euro 2020 squad- but he may need Everton exit

The 19-year-old has struggled to make an impression at the start of his spell at Goodison Park and speculation has grown suggesting he could return to Italy

21 Nov 2019, 08:20 by Irishmirror.ie

Everton forward Moise Kean has been advised to seek a club who will give him more playing time than the Toffees are currently doing by Italy manager Roberto Mancini.

Kean joined the Merseysiders from Juventus in the summer for a fee of Â£24.75m but has yet to open his account at the club in nine Premier League appearances.

He was dropped from the squad altogether for the win against Southampton after turning up late for a team meeting and his father also said he thought the move to Everton was a "mistake" .

Kean has won three caps for Italy, scoring two goals, but has not appeared for the Azzurri since March, and speaking to Rai Sport manager Mancini said he may need to seek a move from Everton if he wants to make his Euro 2020 squad.

"I hope he can find a club that lets him always play: if so, he's one of the players who can come to Euro 2020, but it depends on what he does from now until May," the ex Manchester City boss said.

Kean impressed for Italy Under-21s on Tuesday, scoring twice in a 6-0 win over Armenia, and Toffees fans will be hoping he can bring his form at international level to the Premier League.

He impressed at Juventus last season, scoring six goals in 13 Serie A appearances, but so far his move to England has not worked out.

He hit out at his father's comments over his move , insisting in a post on Instagram that he was capable of turning his poor form around.

He wrote in Italian: "Don't talk about my life when yours is not an example. I don't fear the hard times when the best comes (after) them."

It remains to be seen whether Kean will return to the Everton squad for the game against Norwich on Saturday.
