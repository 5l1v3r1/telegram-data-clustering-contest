PLEASE FIND HIM

Scots pensioner missing overnight as police launch frantic search amid growing fears for his safety

21 Nov 2019, 08:09 by Blair Meikle

A PENSIONER has been missing overnight in Dumfries and Galloway as fears grow for his safety.

Thomas McKee, 71, was last seen at about 2.30pm yesterday on Main Street, Newton Stewart.

He has not been heard from since and his whereabouts are unknown.

Thomas' family and friends are becoming increasingly concerned for his safety.

He is described as being 5ft10in, of medium build, and clean shaven with short thinning grey hair in a comb over pattern.

Thomas was last seen wearing a dark blue knitted jumper, shirt, dark corduroy trousers and black shoes.

He may be driving his green Skoda Fabia with the registration SA69EEV.

Inspector Cameron from Dumfries Police station said: "Thomas has been missing for a number of hours now, his friends and family are understandably worried about him.

"We have a number of police resources out looking for Thomas and I am now looking for the assistance from the public. "

If you believe you have seen Thomas, his vehicle or have any information that you think may assist us please contact 101 quoting incident no. 2575 of the 20/11/2019.

We told yesterday how a body was found in the search for an Oban man.

Alex Carter, 49, disappeared without a trace on Tuesday morning.

Cops launched a frantic hunt to find him, but they confirmed a body has now been found in their search.

And a Scots hillwalker has been missing since Sunday in freezing temperatures.

Paul Murphy, 51, was last seen leaving his home in Stenhousemuir, Falkirk, at around 7am, as he set off to go hillwalking.

He left in his car which has since been found at the Cairngorms Ski Centre in Aviemore.

He has not been seen or heard from since, and concerns are growing for his safety amid freezing temperatures.
