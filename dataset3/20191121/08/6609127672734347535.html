Rangers ‘being greedy’ over star Alfredo Morelos as Saint Etienne cool interest

21 Nov 2019, 08:15 by David Fowler

A MOVE for Rangers star Alfredo Morelos is now unlikely for Saint Etienne in January, according to reports.

The Gers ace has been on incredible form for club and country this campaign, bagging 23 goals in all competitions.

The Colombian's red-hot strike rate has attracted attention from clubs around Europe, and yesterday it was said that French outfit Saint Etienne were the latest to be keen.

However, according to Le10sport, the Ibrox club have been labelled as 'being greedy' over their main man.

The media outlet in France say that a summer move is far more viable as they cooled talk of an advance for the hitman in the winter window.

It is believed that the Ligue One club have scouted the striker on a number of occasions, most recently against Porto where Steven Gerrard's men won 2-1 at Ibrox.

Morelos has bagged 11 goals in the Europa League for the Light Blues this term against the likes of the Portuguese giants, Young Boys and Legia Warsaw.

And its those performances on the continental stage which are earning him rave reviews as the attention grows in the 23-year-old.

SunSport exclusively told you that EPL side Crystal Palace are preparing a £20million January bid.

However, other clubs such as Aston Villa and Bordeaux have been credited with interest too.

Both Rangers gaffer Steven Gerrard and director of football Ross Wilson are adamant that the Ibrox club do not need to sell their prized assets in January.

Wilson's "categorical" statement came after the club's financial figures uncovered that they'd made an annual loss of £11.3million.
