Logan Paul’s appeal against controversial KSI loss being formally investigated

21 Nov 2019, 08:02

Logan Paul recently announced that he would be launching an appeal over the decision which meant he lost his boxing rematch with KSI – and it is now being formally investigated.

The YouTuber, 24, had a crucial two points deducted for holding and punching KSI, which ultimately cost him the fight.

Logan filed an appeal with the California State Athletic Commission, who have confirmed they will be formally investigating.

A spokesperson for CSAC told TMZ: ‘The executive director Andy Foster is evaluating. If there’s sufficient grounds, it’ll go to a hearing at a commission meeting.’

KSI ended up winning the fight, after the referee issued Logan with the two-point deduction in the fourth round after he threw a shot at KSI’s head after he was knocked down.

Logan claimed he wasn’t planning on making excuses for the loss, but decided to appeal after watching the fight back later.

He explained: ‘The 2 point deduction was the reason I full out split decision lost the fight. My team and I have decided to file an official appeal for the fight because we just don’t believe that the outcome was right.

‘It just resonates with me to my core. One of my biggest fears about this is being a sore loser and that’s why I made this video a week after the fight.

‘I really had to check myself and make sure I wasn’t doing this because I was afraid of losing.

‘Historically I’ve taken big Ls. By no means am I afraid of losing. I welcome it because it’s the only way I tend to experience growth in my life. But I truthfully, 100 per cent believe I did not lost this fight.

‘This is just a plea to the California State Athletic commission, I would just hope that you guys give this the weight it deserves.’

With confirmation the Athletic Commission is going to investigate whether the two-point deduction was indeed fair, or too harsh a punishment, Logan will soon find out whether the decision may be changed.
