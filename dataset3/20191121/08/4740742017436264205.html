What Portishead Town Council would spend the money on if tax rise is approved

People in Portishead are being asked whether they want a hike in council tax bills to fund better services for the town

21 Nov 2019, 08:37 by Heather Pickstock

Portishead could get its own traffic warden, new CCTV system and community fridge project – if plans to increase spending in the growing town get the green light,

Portishead Town Council is currently consulting on moves to increase its annual precept, the money it collects on council tax bills for the services it runs.

Currently the town council precept is one of the lowest in North Somerset at just 50 pence per week per band D household and is dramatically lower than other neighbouring towns and villages.

The precept in neighbouring Clevedon is 90 pence a week and in Yatton homeowners pay £1.43 for services provided by the parish council.

In Nailsea the town council precept is £1.48 a week and in Weston-super-Mare it is £1.47.

The town council is proposing to increase the precept to ensure it is put on a firm financial footing and can plan for the future.

The council is putting forward three options – from increasing the precept from between 70 and 80 pence per week per household to a rise of up to £1.60 a week.

The first option – a rise to between 70 and 80 pence a week – would be ‘enough to keep the lights on’ and keep the council and its services running and slowly build up the council’s cash reserves.

But more ambitious plans are included in the second option to increase the precept to between £1.05 and £1.20 per household per week.

Money collected under this option could be spent on a number of new projects for the growing town including employing a traffic enforcement officer and renewing Portishead’s CCTV system.

Other plans include more bins, a yearly event to celebrate the work of volunteers in the town and funding for a new, major community project every year.

There would also be ‘seed’ funding to help with the development of a new skate and wheels park and a ‘peoples’ budget’ with cash up to £500 which community groups could bid for each year.

Under this option the council would also use cash to build up its cash reserves and run its current services.

The third option – a precept of between £1.30 and £1.60 a week would see reserves built up at a faster rate and used in the future to consider investment options for the town.

Under this option the money could be used for a ‘major strategic community project’ such as a new community orchard or to refurbish current community buildings.

Money raised would also be used to promote Portishead as a key destination to visitors.

Cash could also be put aside to develop a community resilience project.

Money would also be earmarked for a fighting fund to fight off unwanted development. Cash could also be put in a ‘land fund’ to allow the council to purchase sites to be used as green spaces and play areas in a bid to protect the environment.

Work on the development of a Neighbourhood Plan would also be sped up.

A community fridge could also be developed to help tackle food poverty in the town. There are also plans for a ‘library of things’ which would be stocked with useful items local residents could borrow.

Initiatives which promote positive mental health, physical fitness and prevent social isolation could be launched.

Full details of all the options are to be discussed at a public meeting tonight (Wednesday) at 7.30pm at the Folk Hall.

A Portishead Town Council spokesman said: “This is only the first part of our consultation work on this year’s budget.

“We are sure the people of Portishead will have many other ideas and some better ones.

“It is important people take part as these views will help shape the future of the town.”

We will also have our public meeting on Wednesday 20th November 7.30pm at the Folk Hall. A leaflet insert with the options will also be going in the North Somerset Times. Other events may be scheduled depending on demand.
