ITV Good Morning Britain uproar at Bake Off's Prue Leith who wants to ban packed lunches

" Woman who eats cakes for a living tries to ban packed lunches as they're 'unhealthy'"

21 Nov 2019, 08:52 by Rebecca Koncienzcy

Breakfast TV viewers have slammed Great British Bake Off judge Prue Leith, who wants to ban packed lunches.

The 79-year-old chef appeared on ITV's Good Morning Britain telling hosts Susanna Reid and Ben Shephard kids' lunch boxes are 'full of junk'.

She said: "Some mums do a great job, but a lot fill lunch boxes with junk. I feel we should ban packed lunches."

Prue said parents should not put treats in lunch boxes as they should be given once a week, not every day.

Susanna said: "Once a week?"

Prue said: "It depends, but I think if you have more than one sweet thing a day you are adding 400 cal to your days.

"Chocolate and chips aren't bad, you just don't want it every day."

Sian Griffiths, who works in schools was also on the show and did not agree with the former Great British Menu judge.

She said: "I don't think banning packed lunches is a good idea.

"A lot of school food is absolutely terrible. I go to schools a lot and just this week I was offered fish fingers, pizza, fried fish in batter and sausages and that's not health food."

Prue said it should be up to schools to teach children about healthy food, but Sian said: "I think schools have got so much on their plate."

Susanna asked: "Prue, do you not see some conflict here, as you are a judge on The Great British Bake Off?"

Prue said: "Of course it is a very obvious one, but that is a competition.

"Mothers shouldn't put a treat in to the lunches every week. I want to see lunches as a lesson.

"In Finland the children sit down and are taught a lot about food, they are sitting down with teachers and they are learning, they don't know it is a lesson - it should be relaxing."

But people watching at home were not impressed with her argument.

One, @DoubleTreble_ said: " Everyday in school dinners they get a pudding, but kids on packed lunches should get a sweet treat once a week? What a load of rubbish."

Another, @angryg21said: " Ban packed lunches? Have you seen the price of school dinners!????"

A third, @cfitzy69 said: " @GMB Our sons school doesn’t allow chocolate or fizzy drinks in packed lunch. He has 2 pieces of fruit, a sandwich, fresh orange juice or water and a small bag of crisps. School dinners include pizza and chips. Which is more healthy?"

Meanwhile @linsullivan610 said: " For many parents, it’s the cost of school meals that forces them to send packed lunches, my daughter had 3 having school meals and at the time it came to £30 per week, this eventually caused her to change to packed lunches as she could not afford over £100 per month."

Many pointed out Prue's job, as one, @KimberleyW1983 said: " Woman who eats cakes for a living tries to ban packed lunches as they're "unhealthy"."

Another, @AngryBritain said: " BREAKING: Woman who has made a living from baking calorie and sugar laden cakes tells Britain on #GMB we should only eat something sweet once a week #irony"
