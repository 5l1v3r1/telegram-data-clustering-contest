Conservative Party Criticised Over 'Fake' Labour Website

21 Nov 2019, 16:30 by Shabana Arif

The conservatives are in hot water again, this time over a reported Google ad that supposedly hijacked search results.

The party's Twitter account has been harping on about Labour's manifesto for at least as far back as I can be bothered to scroll, accompanied by the hashtag #CostOfCorbyn. It's even knocked up a website to tell everyone how shit it thinks Labour's proposed policies are, dubbed Labourmanifesto.co.uk. In the context of its Tweets, it's quite evident that the site has nothing to do with Labour, which would probably shy away from summing up its own manifesto as "no plan for Brexit", "higher taxes", and "two more referendums".

The landing page makes it abundantly clear too, boldly pronouncing that it's "a website by the conservative party," but the move is being viewed as another underhanded tactic, especially in light of the party's silly buggering this week, when it changed its branding and username to factcheckUK during the live TV debate between Boris Johnson and Jeremy Corbyn.
