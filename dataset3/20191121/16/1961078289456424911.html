After Trauma , a Silenced Vocalist Sings Again

21 Nov 2019, 16:18 by Corinna da Fonseca-Wollheim

In the fall of 2013, the vocalist Lucy Dhegrae performed a solo called “Dithyramb.” The five-minute piece, written by Jason Eckardt, consists of cries, truncated syllables and sharp sibilant sounds that appear to resemble someone angrily shushing herself.

At the end, Ms. Dhegrae forced a single breath into a painfully long note that turned into a stifled hiss, held until her face changed color and her eyes teared up. From the audience, it looked like self-asphyxiation.

“It’s supposed to be a prayer to Dionysus, the god of ecstasy,” Ms. Dhegrae said of the piece in a recent interview. “And the entire time I thought about what had happened and how I had not talked about it — and would it ever go away.”
