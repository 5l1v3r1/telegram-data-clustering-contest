English rugby player Joe Marler imitates 'Irish horse' in bizarre and hilarious interview ahead of Harlequins clash with Bath

The video has been viewed 500k times online and baffled Twitter users

21 Nov 2019, 16:21 by Andrew Byrne

A pro rugby player imitated an "Irish horse" in a bizarre interview on Wednesday.

The Harlequins FC and English international star, Joe Marler, compared himself to an "Irish horse" in an interview with his club's media channel after a recent loss.

Marler describes the hurt he felt after the recent defeat and said the team has to "get back on the horse".

Marler takes the classic cliche answer and embarks on a tale about a horse who wants to "give a better performance" this weekend against Bath.

He said: "I wasn't hurting as much as the lads who were out there but I definitely felt it and I know how hard the boys have taken that.

"They'll be disappointed with the account that we put out but we have got another week to get back on the horse and take that horse to the water and you can ask that horse and say, 'Hey, horsey, do you want to have a drink or do you want to swim?'

"And it's up to that horse to then realise what he wants to do in his life.

"And that horse, at the moment, wants to go out on Saturday.

"He wants to clippidy-clop all the way to The Stoop and he wants to say 'hello' to those fans.

"And he goes: 'I'm sorry about the result last week, but I'm going to give a better performance here at home against Bath.'

After attempting to bizarrely imitate a 'horse' voice in that last sentence, he continued: "He's a slightly Irish horse."

The bizarre video has been viewed 597k times on the Harlequins Twitter channel, with fans and viewers loving the interview.

One fan said: "Absolutely brilliant! Funny as, Joe. Thanks for making us laugh.

"It's great to see a professional sportsman still show his character.

"Go well against Bath."

Another added: "If there's no actual horse at the game on Saturday I'm going to be very upset now."

Another Twitter user said: "Joe Marler needs to do a 'one man show'. He was first class on Wossy the other night."

Harlequins play Bath at 5.30pm on Saturday evening.
