How Kieffer Moore has revitalised Wales and why Hungary will be the biggest night of his career

The striker is expected to lead the line in Cardiff having scored two goals in four games for Wales

18 Nov 2019, 05:00 by Ian Mitchelmore

Kieffer Moore says Wales' winner takes all clash with Hungary on Tuesday night will be the biggest game of his career.

The Wigan striker scored the opening goal in his side's 2-0 win over Azerbaijan, a result that - along with Croatia's 3-1 triumph against Slovakia - means Wales' destiny is in their own hands.

Ryan Giggs' men will qualify for Euro 2020 if they beat Hungary at Cardiff City Stadium.

And Moore admits the occasion will be the greatest of his career to date.

"It will rank the biggest. There is everything on the line," he said.

“We have done our job and now it is all preparation and recovery for a massive game.

“I know we are going to get supported by the fans because they are amazing at home, and away.

“If I get to play I will give it my all when I step over the line and hopefully Tuesday is going to be successful and we qualify."

Moore's early header in Baku means he now has two goals in his four caps for Wales.

Giggs' troops are yet to lose a game in which the towering striker has played, with Wales currently on a five-game unbeaten streak ahead of Tuesday's crunch qualifier at Cardiff City Stadium.

And Moore admits his style has been hugely beneficial for the likes of Daniel James and Gareth Bale.

“They say some nice things. I just need to keep going and keep bringing the ball down and supplying them with the ball," he added.

“Obviously they are massive attacking threats if I can giving the ball to them, it stands us in good stead.

“I am very suited to the way this team sets up and plays.

“As you can see I am reaping the rewards and working hard for the team as well.

“There are better players around me, no disrespect, and the runs I am making and the balls there, it is a lot easier for me.

“I will do anything for the team, put my body around.”

Wales host Hungary in Cardiff at 7:45pm on Tuesday.
