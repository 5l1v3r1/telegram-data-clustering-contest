Thousands of Devon homes and businesses at risk of flooding

More than 18,000 properties in Devon postcodes are at a high long-term risk

18 Nov 2019, 06:00 by Joel Cooper

More than 45,000 properties in Devon are at medium or high risk of being flooded.

That’s according to the latest figures from an Environment Agency database.

The figures - updated last month - show that 18,203 properties in Devon postcodes are at a high long term risk - meaning there is a one in 30 chance or higher that they will be flooded within a year.

Of those, 50% (9,155) are residential properties.

A further 26,965 properties are considered to be at medium risk - meaning there is a chance of between one in 30 and one in 100 that they will experience flooding each year.

The postcode unit with the highest proportion of high risk properties is EX8 1R in East Devon.

Some 269 properties there have a high risk of flooding - 100% of the total.

That was followed by EX34 8Q (16 of 16 properties at high risk), and EX8 1S (528 of 532 properties).

That's looking at postcodes with 10 or more properties.

Despite numbering in the thousands, medium and high flood risk only applies to 5% of all properties in Devon, and 5% of residential properties.

You can check whether your property is among those at long term risk using the government's online tool here:  https://flood-warning-information.service.gov.uk/long-term-flood-risk

How many floods were there last year?

Devon and Somerset Fire and Rescue Service attended 186 incidents of flooding in 2018/19.

That compares to 344 the year before.

Not all flooding incidents attended by fire crews are caused by the weather.

While many will be due to storms and heavy rain, some might be caused by burst water mains and properties where people have accidentally left taps running.

In England as a whole, the figure fell from 15,670 flooding incidents to 13,360 in 2018/19.

What is the current situation?

As of 2pm on Sunday (November 17) 59 flood warnings were in force - meaning flooding is expected - and there were 90 flood alerts in areas across the country.

A flood alert means that people should be prepared for the possibility of flooding.

You can check if your area is at immediate risk using the government's flood information service here  https://flood-warning-information.service.gov.uk/warnings .
