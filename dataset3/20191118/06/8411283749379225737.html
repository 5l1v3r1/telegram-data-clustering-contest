Foxsemicon sees revenue rebound

18 Nov 2019, 06:10 by Julian Ho, Taipei; Jessie Shen, DIGITIMES

Semiconductor equipment maker Foxsemicon Integrated Technology, an affiliate of the Foxconn Group, has seen its monthly revenues return to an on-year growth track with the company's sales outlook turning more optimistic.

Foxsemicon has reported consolidated revenues increased 2.2% from a year ago to NT$683 million (US$22.4 million) in October 2019, ending a 11-month streak of on-year decreases.

Foxsemicon's cumulative 2019 revenues through October totaled NT$5.71 billion, down 29.1% from a year earlier. Foxsemicon generated earnings per share of NT$2.10 in the third quarter, the highest level for 2019. EPS for the first three quarters of this year came to NT$4.54.

Market observers have expressed optimism about Foxsemicon's sales outlook for the rest of this year and 2020, citing a rebound in orders particularly OEM orders placed by Applied Materials.

Applied Materials has disclosed impressive results for its fourth quarter ended October 27, 2019. "Applied Materials' fourth quarter results reflect a healthy uptick in demand for semiconductor equipment, combined with strong execution across the company," said company president and CEO Gary Dickerson. "The semiconductor industry is increasingly adopting a new playbook for improving chip performance, power, area and cost, and we are investing in unique solutions to enable our customers' success in the AI-Big Data era."

Applied Materials announced revenues for fiscal 2019 slid 13% on year to US$14.61 billion. On a non-GAAP adjusted basis, the company reported gross margin of 44% and EPS of US$3.04 for the year.
