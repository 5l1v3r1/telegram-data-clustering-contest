Four dead and gunman on the loose after shooting at football party

18 Nov 2019, 06:33

A gunman is on the loose after four people were shot dead and another six injured in a shooting in Fresno, California, last night.

Three people died instantly and a fourth later died at hospital after unknown suspects sneaked into the garden where they were watching a football match.

Police said the shooting took place at around 6pm last night and the people at the party were all family and friends.

Deputy Chief Michael Reid said the six people who were injured are expected to survive and are recovering at the hospital.

Fresno Police Lt Bill Dooley said: ‘What we do know is that this was a gathering, a family and friend gathering in the backyard.

‘Everyone was watching football this evening when unknown suspects approached the residence, snuck into the backyard and opened fire.’

Agents from the U.S. Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, Firearms and Explosives field office in San Francisco are responding to the shooting, the Bee reported.

It was at least the second fatal gun attack Sunday in southeast Fresno, the Bee reported. A man in his 20s was shot to death early Sunday at a home in another part of the city.

Police have not said whether the incidents could be connected but the hunt is still on for suspects.
