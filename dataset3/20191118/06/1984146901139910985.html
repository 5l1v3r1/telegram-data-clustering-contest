Drunk thug attacked ex when she said she never wanted to see him again after wake

David Groves, of Ashington, was already on a suspended prison sentence for attacking another former partner

18 Nov 2019, 06:00 by Rob Kennedy

A thug attacked his ex partner when she told him she never wanted to see him again when he turned up drunk after a wake.

David Groves was already on a suspended prison sentence for attacking another former partner when he lashed out again.

A court heard the 34-year-old had been allowed to get showered and changed at the home of his recent ex that day as he had to attend the funeral of a close family member.

But Newcastle Crown Court heard he returned to the house "drunk" later that night and, after he smashed a glass over his own head, was asked to leave.

Prosecutor Kevin Wardlaw said it was when victim told Groves she "never wanted to see him again" that he lashed out.

Mr Wardlaw added: "This agitated the defendant, at which point he swung a blow with an open hand which collided with her nose, causing injury."

The court heard the woman's nose was left cut and swollen from the attack. 

She said in a victim impact statement: "I have never seen this side of him. I am terrified of what he will do next."

Mr Wardlaw said Groves was bleeding after the violence as a result of his own injury, which he caused with the glass.

While at the police station he spat blood at an officer, who was left "disgusted" and worried about health implications for himself.

Groves, of Maple Street, Ashington, admitted assault, assault on an emergency worker and possession of a small amount of cannabis.

Recorder David Gordon sentenced him to a total of 14 months behind bars with a ten year restraining order to keep him away from the victim. 

The judge told him: "By committing these offences you are in breach of a suspended sentence order imposed in February for offences of criminal damage and battery, committed against a former partner. 

"There are features common between what you did to her and what you did in the present case."

The judge said Groves poses a "high risk of serious harm" to people known to him.

Andrew Walker, defending, said Groves had stayed away from alcohol for two months before the offence and added: "The funeral was that day. As is often the case, there was the presence of a lot of alcohol. 

"To his great regret, a single drink led to more and he finds himself back before the courts."

Mr Walker said Groves has completed courses in custody in a bid to have a more positive future.
