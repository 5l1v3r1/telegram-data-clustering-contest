At least four people were killed and six others were injured on Sunday evening after unknown gunmen opened fire at a backyard football viewing party in Fresno, California.

Fresno Police say at least 10 people were shot just before 8 p.m. (11 p.m. ET) in the backyard of a home in southeast Fresno, according to The Fresno Bee. Police say the “unknown suspects” remain at large.

All of the victims were “Asian males” between the ages of 25 and 30, police said.

“What we do know is that this was a gathering, a family and friend gathering in the backyard,” Lieutenant Bill Dooley told a press conference, calling the event a “mass casualty shooting.”

“Everyone was watching football this evening when unknown suspects approached the residence, snuck into the backyard and opened fire.”

Dooley said victims are in the Community Regional Medical Centre and range from “critical” to “critical but stable” conditions. He added that they have no information on the suspects, the weapons used, or the vehicle used to flee after the shooting.

In a separate statement to TV crews at the scene, Deputy Chief Michael Reid said at least 35 people were at the home when the shooting occurred.

“Thank God that no kids were hurt,” he said.
