Hong Kong protests: Security tight at PolyU with hundreds of protesters trapped on campus

18 Nov 2019, 06:50

HONG KONG: Hong Kong police fired tear gas at protesters as they tried to escape a besieged university on Monday (Nov 18) while hundreds more activists stayed inside armed with petrol bombs and other weapons awaiting an expected operation to oust them.

The bloody standoff between security forces and activists at Polytechnic University entered its second day after a night of mayhem in which a police van was set alight and a police officer was shot with an arrow.

Police threatened to fire live bullets if "rioters" did not stop using lethal weapons. They later said three live rounds were fired but that no one appeared to have been hit.

Thirty eight people were injured overnight, the city's Hospital Authority said. Reuters witnesses saw some protesters suffer burns from chemicals in the jets fired from police water cannons.

An armoured police vehicle that was set ablaze by petrol bombs in Sunday's violence was towed away early on Monday.

"Remember you have life in your hands. Why do you need to push us to death?" one person shouted at police from a campus rooftop as protesters wearing gas masks and clutching umbrellas looked for ways to escape the university.
