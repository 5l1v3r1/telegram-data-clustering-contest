Where to find mental health support in Devon

There are a number of groups around Devon offering help-in a variety of ways

18 Nov 2019, 06:00 by Charlotte Vowles

Many of us will experience mental illness in our life time-either directly or through loved ones. Talking openly about mental health struggles can be incredibly difficult, and it can be hard to know where to go for support.

Fortunately, there are many groups in Devon offering support to people struggling-often run by people with lived experience.

Many of these groups offer support in lots of different ways, including help online and over the phone, with some offering a non-referral, drop-in option.

Here are some of the places in Devon you can seek support for yourself or loved ones.

Andy's Man Club- Exeter, Plymouth, Newton Abbot & Torbay

Andy's Man Club was set up specifically to support men with their mental health. They describe themselves as "real, non judgemental, talking groups for men." They have groups nationwide, and currently have three groups in Devon- with a fourth launching in Torbay, soon.

Exeter City Football Club, St James Park, Stadium Way, Exeter, EX4 6PX

The Exeter group meets here every Monday at 7pm-except Bank Holidays

On Course South West, Hyde Park House, 74 Mutley Plain, Plymouth, PL4 6LF

The Plymouth group meets here every Monday at 7pm-except Bank Holidays

Coffee #1, 46 Courtenay Street, Newton Abbot, TQ12 2ED

The Newton Abbot group meets here every Monday at 7pm-except Bank Holidays

KindaKafe, 10-12 Marine Parade, Paignton, TQ3 2NU

The Torbay group will launch November, 25 and will meet 7-9pm-except Bank Holidays

To find out more about Andy's Man Club visit their website, here.

 

Fight Club Talks-Newton Abbot

FCT are a mental health support group as well as a combat sport club organised to help release aggression and frustration.
Sessions are at 5.45pm every Friday, at Premier Kickboxing, Unit 24, Olympus Business Park, Kingsteignton Road, Newton Abbot, TQ12 2SN.

To find out more, drop them an E-mail: fightclubtalks@gmail.com

Males Allowed, Plymouth

Males Allowed offer mental health support to men in a number of ways, including mental health car pool chats and walk and talk events. They currently meet at the Shekinah Project, Bath Street, Plymouth, PL1 3LT, Wednesdays 6.15pm-8pm.

To find out more, drop them an E-mail: malesallowed@outlook.com, find them on Facebook or visit their website, here.

Open Arms East Devon- Honiton and surrounding areas

This group was set up by Garry Wakeham, who has lived experience of mental health difficulties and welcomes anybody struggling with their mental health and those who support them.

People can chat about their mental health in a supportive environment, and can also choose to take part in arts and crafts and cookery lessons.

Garry said: "There's a great camaraderie-we're very accepting of anybody that comes in, it's about having a laugh. If someone can smile once, it's worthwhile-it's as simple as that."

They meet at Dunning Court Community Centre, Honiton, EX14 1FQ, Tuesdays and Fridays 10.30-4pm.  People can just drop-in, but Garry's happy to meet anybody that's anxious beforehand to put them at ease.

For more info: you can contact Garry on: 07716519608 or E-mail: openarmseastdevon@gmail.com.

Women's Wellbeing Club, Exeter, Paignton & Newton Abbot

The Women's Wellbeing Clubs are relaxed, with no pressure to talk or obligation to attend. Ladies can come along for a cup of tea and some company, or to talk about mental health in a safe environment. There are currently three groups running in Devon.

The Paignton group meets every Tuesday from 7pm

The Salvation Army, 30-32 Union Street, Newton Abbot, TQ12 2JS.

The Newton Abbot group meets every Tuesday 7pm-9pm

Ilfracombe Depression and Anxiety Group

The group meets at Belles Place, Lee Place, Ilfracombe, EX34 9BQ, Wednesday evenings from 7pm-9pm. The meetings are free, with a request for donations towards the cost of tea/coffee and biscuits.

To find out more, E-mail Paul Quinn: I.D.A.G@outlook.com or telephone: 07874 845 465. They are also on Facebook.

Bipolar UK-Torquay & Plymouth

These groups are for anybody affected by bipolar including those with a diagnosis, pre-diagnosis, their family and friends. No referral is needed, people can just turn up.

Central Church, Tor Hill Road, Torquay, TQ2 5RF

The Torbay group meets on the 1st Thursday of every month, 7-9pm.

Stoke Dameral Parish Centre, Paradise Road, Plymouth, PL1 5QL

The Plymouth group meets on the 2nd Wednesday of every month, 7-9pm

To find out more visit their website, here.

Rethink Mental Illness- various places throughout Devon (Including Plymouth, Dartmouth and Exmouth)

These support groups are run by people with experience of mental illness. Each one offers a welcoming, non-judgemental space in which you can talk openly about your experiences and receive support. Rethink mental illness has a large number of support groups in Devon, including groups offering support for carers. To find out more, visit their website, here.

CHIL-Plymouth

CHIL offers support through one to one and group work, including informal drop-ins. They also offer craft and gardening groups- all with the aim of supporting mental health. People can self-refer or be referred by another organisation or medical professional. To set up an initial appointment or to find out more info, visit their website, here. You can also call them on: 07505435345

Mind Devon-various places throughout Devon

Mind runs a range of mental health support groups including groups for those living with bipolar, OCD and groups especially for men. To make an initial appointment to join a group E-mail: admin@devonmind.com or call: 01752 512280. You can also find out more about the groups by visiting their website, here.

Headlight East Devon-Axminster and Ottery St Mary

The Headlight service provides mental and emotional health support to young people 13-24 years and their families.

To find out more about referrals, visit their website, here.

Anybody struggling with their mental health can also get support through The Samaritans, any time, for free, by calling: 116 123. Further details of support are also available on their website, here.

Details correct at time of publication, but please check with individual groups in case of any time or venue changes.
