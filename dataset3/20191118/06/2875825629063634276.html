Glenn Whelan insists tonight won't his last Ireland game - regardless of result

Until about a year ago, Whelan feared the game thrill was a thing of the past

18 Nov 2019, 06:00 by Paul O'Hehir

Glenn Whelan's international career was prematurely written off once before. And whatever happens tonight, this won’t be his last game either.

Beat Denmark and he will play at the Euros next summer. Any other result and he will be playoff bound in March.

Mick McCarthy wouldn’t have it any other way for one of his most trusted lieutenants who is ringing every last drop out of his green shirt the second time around.

But last night, Whelan still acknowledged: “With the young lads we have coming through and the talent we have, this is the be all and end all for me.

“If I can qualify and go out on a high note in the summer that will be great, but I’m not looking anywhere other than Denmark.”

Whelan, 35, has faced into these big November nights in the past, of course.

He revelled in the highs of playoff wins over Estonia and Bosnia. He also shared in the despair of infamous playoff defeats to France and Denmark.

And memories of those encounters have been a talking point amongst the players in the team hotel this week as they chance victory and reward tonight.

Midfielder general Whelan said: “We’ve been talking about the good times, the nights we got the fans behind us and when the stadium was rocking.

“A few of the lads have not been involved in nights like that, so this is the most important game they will have played for Ireland.

“There have been a few shouts of ‘go and make a name for yourself’ and there are players in our squad who can do that tonight.

“These are the nights you want to be involved in. Please God this will be another one. We don’t go into any game feeling scared, or thinking we can’t get a result.”

But until McCarthy arrived back on the scene a year ago, Whelan feared the game thrill was a thing of the past.

In the wake of the horrorshow to the Danes two years ago, he was frozen out of the international picture by Martin O’Neill.

He was briefly brought in from the cold last November for a friendly against Northern Ireland in which he was made captain.

It was billed as his farewell, although Whelan stressed throughout that particular week that he would never retire from international football.

Clearly, retirement was being forced upon him. He certainly wasn’t consciously packing in a decade long love affair with the green shirt.

Whelan said: “You think it's been taken away from you, that you're never going to wear the jersey again, to get a second chance, a second bite of the bullet.

“There were two times where myself and Martin clashed, really clashed. But off the pitch he's the manager and he wins. We shook hands and got on with it.”

That drab draw with the North just happened to be O’Neill’s penultimate game. Fast forward 12 months and Whelan is one of McCarthy’s most trusted lieutenants.

“Martin had a go, Roy had a go, Mick has a go too. Martin and Roy had a way of doing things, discipline that hey were on top of.

“But it’s down to performances and results. It didn’t happen for them in the Nations League and that’s why there was a change.

“But confrontations happen all the time and Mick and his staff are no different. You only have to look at Mick on the sidelines, he’s a bit of a mad man!

Whelan continued: “Black is black and white is white with him. The lads really appreciated him coming in and stripping everything back.

“He has given the lads a lift. There were a few lads who were really low after the Nations League.

“Not because of Martin, but because of the way results had gone and their own performances.

“Mick has kept everything simple. He knows what he wants and it’s not rocket science.

“We got a bit leaky at times come the end of the Nations League, conceding a lot of goals and he has steadied the ship.

“We know we need to let the reins off a little to attack and score more goals but defensively he's come in and improved on that.”

And Whelan added: “We’re one game away and Mick has given us something to fight for.”
