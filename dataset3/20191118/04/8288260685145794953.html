Striker 'target' signs new contract

Wolverhampton Wanderers have been one of several Premier League sides linked with a move for highly-rated Wigan Athletic youngster Joe Gelhardt.

The 17-year-old - who has been fast-tracked into the Latics’ first team after impressing manager Paul Cook - has now signed a new deal with the Championship outfit.

A report from Goal.com last month claimed Wolves , Liverpool, Everton and Leicester City were all interested in the striker.

But despite that interest, Gelhardt has penned a new, long-term deal with the Latics, meaning that any prospective suitor would now need to offer a lot more cash for his services, with a reported £5m valuation attached to him.

Wolves close in on Buta deal

Wolves are closing in on an agreement for Royal Antwerp bull-back Aurelio Buta, according to reports.

The 22-year-old has been linked with a number of top flight clubs, including Newcastle United and Watford after an impressive season in Belgium.

The Sunday Mirror (via The Express) have claimed that Wolves are close to finalising a deal that will see him move to Molineux, initially on loan.

Buta is seen as vital competition for Matt Doherty, with Adama Traore now being utilised in a more traditional attacking role.

'Gutted for Diogo'

Wolves fans are having their say after Cristiano Ronaldo's 99th international goal helped Portugal secure their qualification for next summer's European Championship finals.

The reigning European champions won 2-0 away to Luxembourg in group B to make sure they will be in the running to defend their title in 2020 - and there were a quartet of Wolves stars on the field by the end of the game.

Rui Patricio played the full 90 minutes in goal, while Ruben Neves, Joao Moutinho and Diogo Jota all came off the bench.

It was Jota's involvement that got Wolves supporters talking in the immediate aftermath, as the forward came close to registering his first goal for Portugal's senior side with a close-range chance, only for captain Ronaldo to tap the loose ball home from barely a yard out in the 86th minute.

Ant, Dec and Carl

"Which one of them is it?”

That's the bizarre question, Wolverhampton Wanderers legend Carl Ikeme has been asking after revealing a shock familial link with either Ant or Dec.

The iconic TV duo tracked their family tree on a recent ITV show, which discovered both had ancestral roots in Ireland, with the pair even finding themselves distantly related.

And now Ikeme, who has family in England and Nigeria, whom he represented at international level, has revealed he is a distant relative of either Anthony McPartlin or Declan Donnelly.
