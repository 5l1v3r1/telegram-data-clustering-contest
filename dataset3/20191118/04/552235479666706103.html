Health Secretary Jeane Freeman facing toughest test of political career

The Daily Record says the SNP minister needs to start sorting out the chaos in NHS or make way for someone who can.

18 Nov 2019, 04:30 by Record View, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/record-view/

Health Secretary Jeane Freeman is facing the toughest test of her political career.

She has to answer for a health service rocked by serious allegations of hospital infections, cover-ups and construction scandals.

Last week, the Daily Record exposed shocking claims a child in remission from cancer died after picking up an infection from a hospital water supply in Glasgow’s new super-hospital.

Today, Freeman can add to her list a damning report on staff shortages putting cancer survival at risk.

And that’s not all of it.

 

A children’s ward in Livingston is not clinically safe to open as promised.

A brand new children’s hospital in Edinburgh hasn’t opened at all.

Unlike Westminster, the Scottish Parliament is still open for business during the General Election
campaign.

That means there can be absolutely no excuse for any distraction. None at all.

The structural and management ­failings are not the fault of doctors, nurses and the wider NHS workforce.

It would be wrong for a front-line medic to face public anger at ­management and government mistakes.

Health boards and SNP ministers are in charge of funding and steering the NHS through ever more challenging circumstances, including the impact of damaging Tory austerity.

But that’s where the party politics should end.

Everyone in the country relies on a properly funded, competently managed NHS. The public are not getting what they deserve – and the health secretary needs to dramatically turn that around.

 

Tomorrow, when Holyrood starts its week, Freeman needs to stand up in front of parliament and show she ­understands just how bad this is getting.

More than that, Freeman needs to clearly set out what she’s going to do about it.

Patient confidence is paramount – and it has been sadly neglected.
