NFL Week 11: Lamar Jackson’s dominance, Cardinals-49ers insanity

18 Nov 2019, 04:33 by Peter Botte

Lamar Jackson distinguished himself again in another impressive win for the Ravens, but Baltimore’s defense made sure Deshaun Watson couldn’t say the same in what turned out to be a lopsided battle of two of the best multi-threat quarterbacks in the NFL.

Jackson threw for four touchdowns and added 86 more rushing yards to his case for league MVP in a 41-7 rout of the Texans on Sunday, the rolling Ravens’ sixth straight victory to remain one game behind New England (9-1) for the best record in the AFC. This marks Baltimore’s longest winning streak since a seven-game surge in 2000, their first Super Bowl championship season.

“Anytime you are 8-2, you have a special group of guys. We have a goal of being champions,” said running back Mark Ingram, who caught two passes. “[Jackson] just keeps one-upping himself.”

With six games remaining, Jackson is on pace for 1,261 rushing yards for the season, which would easily surpass Michael Vick’s single-season record for quarterbacks of 1,039 yards in 2006. The former Heisman Trophy winner also became the first Ravens quarterback to have two games in the same season with at least four touchdown passes.

“I like that stat, I’d rather throw them than run them,” Jackson said afterward.

Still, the second-year breakout star’s highlight play against the Texans was when he broke several tackles on a weaving 39-yard run in the third quarter, especially on the heels of his 47-yard scoring burst last Sunday against Cincinnati.

“That was remarkable. That was incredible, I haven’t seen anything like it … since last week,” Ravens coach John Harbaugh said.

Just as important in the Ravens’ ascension to contention, their defense limited Watson to 169 passing yards, forced two turnovers and sacked him six times (of seven overall), including two apiece by Matt Judon and Tyus Bowser. Houston didn’t score until Carlos Hyde’s 41-yard touchdown run with 7:10 remaining in the fourth quarter made the score 34-7.

The Ravens opened the season by splitting their first four games, but their six-game win streak also features victories over two other 8-2 teams, Seattle and New England.

“Today was a little surprising to me. I hope no one on this team takes offense, but I didn’t think we would do them like that,” veteran safety Earl Thomas said. “I think Lamar definitely separated himself again against another Hall of Fame-type quarterback. We definitely want him to win the MVP award and that definitely was a motivation.”

The Ravens totaled 263 yards on the ground, including 112 from Gus Edwards, who became the first runner to reach at least 100 against Houston since Indianapolis’ Frank Gore in the final game of the 2017 season. The Texans (6-4) slipped into a first-place tie with the Colts in the AFC South ahead of Sunday’s head-to-head matchup in Houston.

“It didn’t go our way. They executed, we didn’t. That’s it,” Watson said. “Flush it and move forward.”
