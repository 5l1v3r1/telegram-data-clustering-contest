Topsham by-election candidates for Exeter City Council election confirmed

The by-election has been triggered after the resignation of Conservative Catherine Pierce, who was first elected to the council in 2018

18 Nov 2019, 09:46 by Daniel Clark

Candidates for the by-election in Topsham which will take place on the same day as the upcoming General Election have been announced.

Residents in the town will go to the polls to elect an Exeter City Council member, as well as their MP.

The by-election has been triggered after the resignation of Conservative Catherine Pierce, who was first elected to the council in 2018. No reason for her resignation has been given.

Four candidates have been confirmed as taking part in the election which will happen on Thursday, December 12.

They are:

  • Christine Campion (Liberal Democrats)
  • Ivan Jordan (Labour)
  • Frankie Rufolo (The For Britain Movement)
  • Keith Sparkes (Conservatives)

The composition of the council currently stands as Labour (29), Conservative (5), Liberal Democrat (2), Green Party (1), Independent (1), and Vacant (1).

Topsham has only returned Conservative councillors in the 21 century, and in May’s election, Cllr Rob Newby held onto his seat in the ward with 47 per cent of the vote.
