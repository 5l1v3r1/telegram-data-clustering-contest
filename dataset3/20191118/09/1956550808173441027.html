Dubai Airshow 2019 set to be biggest exhibition yet

The biggest aerospace event in the Middle East will see 165 aircrafts on display from Emirates, Air Arabia and more

18 Nov 2019, 09:07 by Darragh Murphy

Don’t be surprised to see jets whizzing through the sky over the next five days, as the biggest aerospace event in the Middle East has returned.

The Dubai Airshow 2019 kicked off yesterday (Sunday November 17), and it boasts spectacular air displays, free talks from the UAE Space Agency including a ‘Women in Space’ and tech talk, along with 165 of the latest aircrafts to ogle at.

Taking place at the purpose built Dubai Airshow venue at DWC, visitors can expect the event’s famed flying display from 2pm until 5pm each day. If it’s anything like 2017’s show, you won’t want to miss it.

Throughout the five-day event (taking place until Thursday November 21), more than13,000 exhibitors will be showing off the latest in airline tech, along with plenty of planes from Emirates, Air Arabia, Jazeera Airways, SalamAir and plenty more.

Airlines will be displaying aircrafts that are set to be used over the next 20 years, so now’s a good chance for aviation fans to get a first glimpse of what they’ll be taking off in when going abroad.

The Dubai Airshow 2017 was the biggest show ever with 1,200 exhibitors from 63 countries, over 79,380 trade visitors and 159 Aircraft on display. This year is set to be even bigger.

Tickets to the event are priced from $85 (around Dhs312), but it’s completely free to attend the Women in Space conference and tech talks taking place.

For those looking for a spot to stay during the Dubai Airshow 2019, Aloft Dubai South is setting up a host of special deals, including dining and travel deal to and from the site. Visit www.aloftdubaisouth.com for more info.

Prefer to take aircrafts into your own hands (but aren’t exactly sure how to fly)? Emirates has launched A380 simulator at The Dubai Mall, and it will teach you all about flying a plane.
From $85 (Dhs312, visitor pass). Sun Nov 17-Thu Nov 21, 10am-5.30pm. Dubai Airshow Site, DWC, www.dubaiairshow.aero.
