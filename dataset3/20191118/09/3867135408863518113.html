Climate talk and action

A Climate Reality presentation and conversation by WSU Snohomish County Extension Sustainable Community Stewards is scheduled for 6:30 to 8 p.m. Wednesday at 600 128th St. SE, Everett.

Talk about what the climate crisis means for this community as well as the solutions and actions to make a difference. For each audience member who attends the presentation (and fills out the form), Climate Reality will plant a tree on their behalf.

This event is free.

Risks of vaping

The Mukilteo Police Department is hosting a community forum Tuesday about the risks of vaping. Health educator Mary O’Leary, of the Snohomish County Health District, is the speaker.

The subject has been in the spotlight recently, with two reported cases of people becoming ill from vaping in Snohomish County. And in October, Gov. Jay Inslee issued an executive order to have flavored vape products banned.

“It is important that teens and community members learn factual information about vaping and e-cigarettes so responsible health-related decisions can be made. Too many times these devices can encourage the use of more dangerous substances,” said Mukilteo Police Chief Cheol Kang.

More info: Contact Crime Prevention Officer Myron Travis at 425-263-8104 or email crimeprevention@mukilteowa.gov

Deck the halls with holiday art

The opening reception for Schack Art Center’s annual Holiday Exhibit is set for 5 to 8 p.m. Thursday at the art center, 2921 Hoyt Ave.

The reception features the Colored Pencil Society of America, as well as glass, pottery and other art from more than 70 regional artists.

The show is open to the public through Jan. 2.

More info: www.schack.org

Meet an astronaut

South Korea’s first and only astronaut will share her story at Stanwood High School on Saturday.

Yi So-yeon left Earth in 2008 to spend nine days at the International Space Station, where she conducted experiments and medical tests for the Korea Aerospace Research Institute.

She almost died on her way back, when her spacecraft’s equipment went awry. She and two other astronauts experienced nearly 16Gs of force and a rough landing 260 miles from their intended destination.

Yi left the South Korean space program in 2014, and had a stint as a physics teacher at Everett Community College in 2016. She now lives in Puyallup.

A limited number of free tickets are available at Sno-Isle Libraries branches in Stanwood, Camano Island, Lakewood/Smokey Point and Arlington.

The event will take place from 4 to 6 p.m. Saturday at the Stanwood High School Performing Arts Center, 7400 272nd St. NW, Stanwood. Doors open at 3 p.m.

More info: www.sno-isle.org
