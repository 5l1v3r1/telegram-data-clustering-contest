Triple murder trial due to start in Exeter

Alexander Lewis-Ranwell is accused of killing three men in February

18 Nov 2019, 09:40 by Paul Greaves

The trial is due to begin today of a man accused of a murdering three men in Exeter.

Alexander Lewis Ranwell, 28, is charged with killing Anthony Payne, 80, and 84-year-old twin brothers, Roger and Dick Carter in February.

Their bodies were found within 24 hours of each other at two addresses in the city. The defendant, from Croyde in North Devon, is due to stand trial at Exeter Crown Court on Monday.

Mr Richard Smith QC will prosecute the case for the Crown and Mr Andrew Langdon QC will appear for the defendant. High Court judge Mrs Justice May will preside over the case.

The discovery of three bodies in February sparked one of the biggest police investigations in Exeter in recent years.

Mr Payne was discovered dead at his home in Bonhay Road on February 11. Police found Roger and Dick Carter dead at their detached home in Cowick Lane on February 12.
