Ridiculous: McAfee Gives Away 10% Supply Of Epstein Didn’t Kill Himself Token (WHACKD)

18 Nov 2019, 09:10 by George Georgiev

Well-known entrepreneur and cryptocurrency proponent, John McAfee, recently launched an ERC-20 based token called WHACKD. The full rubric of the token is “Epstein Didn’t Kill Himself,” and it was announced back on November 11th. Now, however, McAfee has said that he will give away 10% of the total supply of WHACKD to the winners of a new meme contest.

Gifting WHACKD To Meme Contest Winners

The circumstances surrounding Jeffrey Epstein’s death caught the attention of the people around the world. Many of them – including McAfee himself – are convinced that the disgraced American financier and convicted sex offender did not commit suicide, which was reported as the “official” cause of death.

The creator of what is arguably one of the most popular and commonly used antivirus software on the planet openly said that there is no conclusive evidence that Epstein killed himself, as is the original version.

Moreover, McAfee decided to voice his opinion through a cryptocurrency. On November 11th, he launched a token called Epstein Didn’t Kill Himself using the ticker symbol WHACKD.

Just a few days later, he launched an “Epstein Didn’t Kill Himself” meme contest and announced that the winners will receive 10% of the total supply of WHACKD.
