Metronomy return to their 'spiritual home' to play Rock City

Nottingham is also the birthplace of keyboardist Oscar Cash

18 Nov 2019, 09:13 by Paul Drury

I wasn’t sure which way Metronomy would go tonight. Their new album, as fantastic as it is, has just as many ambient soundscapes and experimental doodles as perfect electro-pop songs. Would they stick to the bangers or risk confusion on the dancefloor?

As it turned out, they played some of the less ‘live friendly’ tracks through the PA before they came on stage.

Clever that.

They open with Lately from the new record followed by The Bay from their 2011 breakthrough album The English Riviera, a magnificent pair that could easily have been their encore.

This is a band that knows they have quality in depth.

Half way through the hour and a half set they have an instrumental interlude, involving a coming together of keyboards in a Kraftwerk-style tableau, then return for a beautifully stripped back version of Aquarius and have the confidence to slow things down with the funny and touching acoustic Upset My Girlfriend.

And they look as stylish as they sound, visions in white jumpsuits, clearly enjoying delivering old favourites like The Look alongside the irresistible Salted Caramel Ice Cream.

If they ever release a greatest hits album (is that still a thing?) it really would be the definition of all killer, no filler, though that sounds far too crass for this quintessentially British band – polite, wildly creative and a delicious mix of genders, ethnicities and sexualities.

Makes you proud.

“Nottingham is our spiritual home,” says singer Joseph Mount, explaining his parents met in our city and it’s the birthplace of keyboardist Oscar Cash. “We’ll be visiting you again soon.”

Promises, promises.
