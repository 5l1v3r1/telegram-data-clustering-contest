Val Kilmer looks healthy and happy at art exhibition in LA after battling cancer

18 Nov 2019, 20:29

Val Kilmer looked healthy and happy during an appearance at his own art studio exhibition after battling throat cancer for two years.

The 59-year-old attended the exhibition at HelMel studios in Los Angeles on Friday after slowly returning to the spotlight since getting surgery on his throat.

Val was posing with artist Tamie Adaya as he rocked his a scarf covering his throat, a colourful flannel shirt and jeans.

His appearance comes after news broke of the actor starring alongside Tom Cruise in Top Gun 2, where he will reprise his role as Iceman, which hits cinemas next summer.

The actor admitted he had been secretly battling throat cancer for two years in an interview with The Hollywood Reporter in 2017.

He had a procedure on his trachea that made his voice raspy and left him short of breath.

The disease took a huge toll on the Tombstone actor but he believes his Christian Scientist faith and the chemotherapy helped him fight his cancer.

Despite battling the deadly disease, he still continued to work and starred in the 2017 horror movie The Super before briefly showing up in Michael Fassbender’s film The Snowman last year.

It looks the actor is slowly getting better, and we are excited to watch him return to our screens.

Joseph Kosinski – who is directing Top Gun: Maverick – has previously teased fans about what they can expect from the follow-up.

Hinting that that film will focus on Maverick’s time in the modern armed forces, he recently said: ‘The Navy is very different now than it was in 1986. Back then, they hadn’t been in any war for 15 or 20 years at that point.

‘Now, here in 2017, the Navy’s been at war for 20 years. It’s just a different world now, so you can’t remake the first movie. It has to adapt.’

Top Gun: Maverick will hit cinemas on 26 June, 2020.
