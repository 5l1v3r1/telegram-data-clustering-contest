Double trouble for owner of £215k Lamborghini Huracan spotted parked illegally in Birmingham city centre

The motor, which has a 5.2L engine, also drove through a no-entry sign

18 Nov 2019, 20:37 by Jordan Coussins, https://www.birminghammail.co.uk/authors/jordan-coussins/

A Lamborghini owner has been blasted by police for parking illegally in a taxi rank in the heart of Birmingham city centre.

The owner of the £215,000 Huracan Performante was slapped with a £130 fine by officers from West Midlands Police after the supercar was spotted in a taxi space in  Waterloo Street, off Temple Row,   on Friday, November 15.

Birmingham Taxi and Private Hire Officers called out the parking and publicly criticised the motorist on social media.

 

The expensive vehicle – which can do more than 200mph – also failed to display a front registration plate.

They said: “I don’t need to blank this plate on the basis anyway – that’s two offences the owner is being prosecuted for!”

Many have taken to social media to also slam the driver for their actions.

One said: “A couple of hundred bucks at the most. Can’t imagine them being that bothered when they can afford a car like that.”

 

While another said: “Nail the plate on with a nail gun.”

And a third wrote: “If they are from a reputable company or football club I'm sure they would like to know about their irresponsible behaviour.”

The fine comes exactly a year after a £271,000 Aventador parked in the exact same position on the same street.

They tweeted: “It really does annoy me that the driver has parked on a rank – but even more so by driving through a no entry!”

John Humpherson, who replied to the police's tweet, said: “I don’t suppose he will be too concerned by the fine.”

But officers said the driver was ‘more concerned with it [the car] being lifted’.
