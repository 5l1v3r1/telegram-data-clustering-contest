2022 FIFA World Cup Qualifiers: Yemen vs Singapore live stream, updates, when and where to watch

18 Nov 2019, 20:00

Tatsuma Yoshida’s Singapore will travel to Bahrain to face Yemen on the sixth matchday of the 2022 FIFA World Cup Qualifiers at the Sheikh Ali bin Mohammed Al Khalifa Stadium in Muharraq on Tuesday. 

Singapore and Yemen are in Group D of the 2022 FIFA World Cup and 2023 AFC Asian Cup Joint-Qualifiers second round where they are joined by Palestine, Uzbekistan and Saudi Arabia.

As many as 40 teams from Asia have been drawn into eight groups of five teams each for the second round of the qualifiers which will run until June 9, 2020.

Eight group winners as well as the four best runners-up will qualify for the AFC Asian Cup 2023 to be held in China as well as the third and final round of the 2022 World Cup Qualifiers which is scheduled to begin in September 2020.Then, the four best teams in the third round will qualify for the showpiece event in Qatar while a fifth team will take part in an intercontinental play-off.

Meanwhile, the next best 24 teams from the second round of the joint-qualifiers will take part in a separate competition to decide the 12 remaining slots in the 24-team 2023 Asian Cup to be held in China PR.

When to watch?

The 2022 FIFA World Cup Qualifiers match between Yemen and Singapore will take place on November 19, 2019 and kicks off at 11:00 PM HKT.

The Sheikh Ali bin Mohammed Al Khalifa Stadium in Muharraq, Bahrain will play host to the game.

Where to watch?

Viewers from Singapore can tune into the Toggle.sg to watch the 2022 FIFA World Cup Qualifiers match between Singapore and Yemen.
