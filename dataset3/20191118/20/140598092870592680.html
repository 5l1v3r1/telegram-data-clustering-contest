Somerset docked 12 points for next County Championship season after poor Taunton pitch in title decider

18 Nov 2019, 20:34 by Tim Wigmore

Somerset have been handed a 12-point deduction for next season's County Championship after being found guilty of breaching pitch regulations.

A Cricket Discipline Committee Disciplinary Panel ruled against the county over the poor state of the pitch for their match against Essex at Taunton in September.

Somerset needed to win the match to secure their maiden County Championship title. In the event the match was decimated by rain and, despite a sterling final-day effort from the hosts - whose trio of spinners claimed all 10 Essex first-innings wickets between them - the game was drawn. In total, 21 wickets fell in 148 overs - of which 17 were to spin - during the game.

Taunton, Somerset's home ground, was long regarded as a batting haven but in recent years has become known as one of the grounds most conducive to spin in the country. The decision by a Cricket Discipline Committee panel means that Somerset begin next season on minus 12 points. This amounts to half of the potential points a team could gain by winning a match with full bonus points, and so represents a significant disadvantage over the course of the 14-game County Championship season.

Somerset's full punishment is a 24-point deduction, of which 12 are suspended for two years. They have 14 days to appeal against the decision. A club statement said that they would "await the full report from the Cricket Discipline Commission before considering our next course of action".
