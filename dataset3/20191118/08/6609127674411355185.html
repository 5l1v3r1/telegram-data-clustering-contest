COBBLES EXIT

Coronation Street’s Beverley Callard reveals she’s quitting the soap after 30 years as Liz McDonald

18 Nov 2019, 08:18 by Beth Allcock

CORONATION Street legend Beverley Callard - known for playing feisty barmaid Liz McDonald - is quitting the ITV soap.

The actress, who has been on the ITV series for 30 years, made the sensational confession and admitted her character would go out with "one hell of a bang."

Bev, 62, told OK! Magazine her final scenes would air next year, in news set to devastate her loyal fans.

Revealing her reaction after telling bosses of the Weatherfield soap of her intention to quit, she added: “I was in shock, I felt totally numb."

She added: “I’ve been told Liz is going to go with one hell of a bang. I cant wait!”

The Leeds-born actress did tell how she had experienced many "sleepless nights" deliberating with the major decision.

Yet Corrie fans can take some comfort from the fact Liz will remain on screen until summer 2020.

Bev, who joined Corrie in 1989, will leave screens in order to go on tour with comedy play, The Thunder Girls.

She plays character Roxanne in the show, which stars four women over the age of 50.

Viewers will miss Liz's bubbly nature and passionate streak on screen, particularly as Steve McDonald's mum has been at the heart of recent storylines.
