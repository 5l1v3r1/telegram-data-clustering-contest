Forcecon introduces new cooling solutions for notebooks, smartphones

18 Nov 2019, 08:12 by Aaron Lee, Taipei; Steve Shen, DIGITIMES

Taiwan-based heat dissipation solution provider Forcecon Technology has introduced a new form of heat pipe, thermal ground plane (TGP), thickened 0.2mm for notebook and smartphone applications, according to company chairman Yao Cheng-chi.

The company has delivered its TGP devices to notebook and smartphone clients for verification, likely paving the way for volume shipments in the first quarter of 2020, Yao said.

Its initial production of TGPs will reach 200,000 units, with output to be ramped up depending on market demand, Yao added.

Since TGP products can come with a thickness of 0.2mm or even 0.1mm, the TGP-based cooling solutions boast an advantage when applied to slim consumer electronics products as compared to vaper chamber (VC)-based modules, which are now available with a thickness of 0.35mm, Yao said.

In the third quarter of 2019, Forcecon posted revenues of NT$3.564 billion (US$116.88 million), net profit of NT$117 million and gross margin of 18.3%.
