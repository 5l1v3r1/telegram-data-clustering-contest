Red-Hot Kane Sets England Records Tumbling

18 Nov 2019, 08:28 by SL International

England captain Harry Kane climbed to the top of the goalscoring standings in the 2020 UEFA European Championship qualifiers on Sunday, ahead of Cristiano Ronaldo, and in doing so sent a number of records tumbling.

Read: Ronaldo Moves To 99 Goals As Portugal Qualify For EURO 2020

Kane played a starring role in England's 4-0 win over Kosovo on Sunday, during which he scored his side's second goal on the night.

In doing so, the Tottenham Hotspur star took his tally in qualifying to 12 for the Three Lions, which is the most by any player in this year's qualification campaign, ahead of Portugal's Ronaldo and Israel's Eran Zahavi.

According to Opta Sports, his goal against Kosovo saw Kane equal the record for the most goals by an England player in a calendar year, matching the mark of 12 set by George Hilsdon in 1908 and Dixie Dean in 1927.

The 26-year-old also became the first player from the nation to score in every match during a single qualification campaign, having netted in each of England's previous fixtures against Bulgaria, Czech Republic, Kosovo and Montenegro.
