Coronation Street exit confirmed for Liz McDonald as Beverley Callard quits after 30 years

Coronation Street actress Beverley Callard will bow out as Liz McDonald from the ITV soap after 30 years in the role

18 Nov 2019, 07:41 by Charlotte Tutton

Coronation Street actress Beverley Callard has confirmed she is leaving the ITV soap having appeared as her character Liz McDonald over the past 30 years.

An exit has been announced for the character with her set to leave Weatherfield "with a bang".

The character is the mother of fellow Corrie resident Steve McDonald, with her granddaughter Amy also on the Cobbles.

Now, speaking to OK! magazine soap favourite Beverley has announced she will depart from the show next year, with a huge storyline lined up for her.

She told bosses of her plans earlier this month, while her final scenes will air in 2020.

 

The actress confessed: "I was in shock, I felt totally numb".

She added she had experienced "so many sleepless nights" over her decision, but knew she was making the right choice.

Teasing her exit plot, she said: "I’ve been told Liz is going to go with one hell of a bang. I cant wait!"

Beverley, who first appeared on the soap in 1989, will soon go on tour with the comedy The Thunder Girls.

She joined the theatre show based on the best-selling novel alongside Coleen Nolan and Emmerdale's Sandra Marvin earlier this year.

Beverley also spoke of her frustrations over the lack of roles for older women when discussing her decision to leave the soap.

She said: "There’s very little work in our industry for women over 50, but I’m 62 and recently I was doing three jobs at once.

"It made me realise how I was privileged to have the opportunity to do so when so many women my age no longer get the chance."

She added: "I’m sick of ageism in the industry.

"Although I’ve had to leave the safety net of being in Coronation Street to do this, I’m hoping that ageist bosses, of which there are many, take note of how successful this show is and it starts the ball rolling on helping other women my age, get the roles they still deserve."

Coronation Street airs Mondays, Wednesdays and Fridays at 7:30pm and 8:30pm on ITV.
