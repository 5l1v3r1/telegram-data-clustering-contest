GST collection stabilising, says official in Puducherry

Raj Kumar Barthwal, Member (Customs), Central Board of Indirect Taxes and Customs said the tax collection was being fine-tuned according to the emerging economic situation

18 Nov 2019, 07:26 by Special Correspondent

The Goods and Services Tax (GST) collection has almost stabilised and is being fine-tuned as per the emerging economic situation, Raj Kumar Barthwal, Member (Customs), Central Board of Indirect Taxes and Customs (CBIC), said on Monday.

“GST is a one-of-its-kind of transformational tax regime that has happened in the world. It’s been on a zig-zag course and it has almost stabilised. And it’s been fine-tuned as the economic situation demanded,” he told reporters on the sidelines of a three-day conference organised by CBIC at Poornankuppam near here.

Senior customs officials from 33 countries, including China, Australia, Japan, Iran and Cambodia are participating in the programme.
