YOUNG GUN

Rangers kid Young-Coombes says Lovenkrands inspired him to Ibrox goal

18 Nov 2019, 07:20 by Colin Duncan

NATHAN YOUNG-COOMBES revealed an inspirational pep talk from Peter Lovenkrands inspired him to a first Ibrox goal.

The 16-year-old hit the winner as Rangers Colts beat Wrexham to book their place in the semi-final.

Jamie Barjonas was also on target as Greame Murty’s kids saw off the National League outfit in front of a 5000 crowd.

Young-Coombes came off the bench to put the seal on a terrific afternoon for the Gers kids, slamming home a rebound after the keeper spilled a Barjonas effort.

It was the scenario the teenager had been working on for the previous 48 hours with former Gers favourite Lovenkrands, who is now part of the academy coaching staff.

He said: “Peter and I had been going over his goals for Rangers, Newcastle and Birmingham on Thursday and Friday.

“We were honing in on how to get into the six yard box, being alive and picking up scraps because that’s where you get joy.

“I was lucky enough to pick up on what we were speaking about at half-time — getting in that box and get that goal.

"I had noticed when I was on the bench that the keeper spilled one and I was hoping to get on and be alive to that.

“Peter was telling me, any shots at the goalie, make sure I was following in. And I was lucky enough to get my goal today.”

Young-Coombes joined Rangers from Chelsea earlier this year as he felt there was more opportunity to fast-track his career.

The likeable youngster believes he made the right decision and is confident he will continue to make rapid progress under Lovenkrands and Murty.

He said: “I came up here to eventually get first team football. All I can do is score. The managers are very good.

“They know where I am in my development. I am only 16, so getting U21 football against a first team like Wrexham, you’d think: ‘wow, hold on a second’.

"I have faith in what our coaches will do for me in the future.

“Peter and Murts have been great for me. We are always talking about how I can take my game to the next level.

“We were playing against a first team here and I’m only 16, so realistically I’m not going to bully them or outpace them.

“Don’t get me wrong, I’m very confident in my own ability, but it’s about how I can beat a defender who is bigger than me, stronger than me.

"I can do it by being alive in the box and that’s what happened.”

Rangers joined Partick Thistle, Inverness and Raith in the hat for the draw and are the first colts side to reach the last four.

Given the feel-good factor swirling around the club at the moment Young-Coombes sees no reason why they cannot go all the way.

He said: “Can we win it? Yes, we think so. There’s a real aura about the boys, from our Youth Champions League nights to playing in this tournament.

“Everyone’s buzzing and it pushes our training sessions on because everyone wants to start and they are disappointed when they don’t start.

“So when you do get on the park you want to prove you deserve to be in the starting 11.

“It was a good victory. You could see what their game plan was from the outset, to try to catch us on the counter.

“They were very frustrating and we needed to move the ball quickly, side to side, and look for little gaps which we could exploit.”

Due to the international break Rangers allowed their Under-21 side to strut their stuff at Ibrox and they made the most of the unexpected opportunity.

And Young-Coombes added: “The atmosphere was everything I hoped it would be.

“I have been watching games from the stands, hoping to be on the pitch and it was a tremendous honour. Allowing us to play at Ibrox was great and we always want to play at Ibrox.

“That’s why we play football, to play on the big occasions with big crowds and big atmosphere. That’s every little boy’s dream, to play in games like that.”
