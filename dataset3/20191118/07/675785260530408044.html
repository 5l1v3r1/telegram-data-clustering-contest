Man Utd and Arsenal send scouts to watch South American talent ahead of transfer window

Arsenal and Man Utd are both looking far afield in a big to strengthen their squads in January after tough starts to the Premier League season

18 Nov 2019, 07:02 by Rich Jones, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/rich-jones/

Manchester United and Arsenal are reportedly amongst the clubs taking the chance to monitor the next generation of South American talent.

According to Portuguese newspaper Record , both clubs had scouts in attendance in Gran Canaria last week to watch the Tenerife Tournament.

The four-day event was being used by Under-23 teams from Brazil, Argentina, Chile and the USA to prepare for the 2020 CONMEBOL Pre-Olympic Tournament.

And whilst no specific names have been mentioned, both were keen to run the rule over any potential gems who could strengthen their side moving forwards.

For Arsenal , it was also a chance to keep a close eye on their youngster, Gabriel Martinelli, as he featured for Brazil after earning his first international call-up.

Arsenal have already hit the jackpot by raiding Brazil, picking up Martinelli from lowly Ituano for a bargain £5m fee and they will hope he could help convince some team-mates to join him.

They appear keen to repeat the trick with reports suggesting they have met with the father of Flamengo prospect Reinier to try and convince him to join the club.

They do face competition, however, with Paris Saint-Germain, Manchester City , Real Madrid and Juventus amongst those all said to be interested in the £17million-rated 17-year-old.

United, meanwhile, have had a mixed record with Brazilian players in the past, with the likes of Kleberson, Anderson, and current midfielders Fred and Andreas Pereira having limited success at Old Trafford.

But that hasn't stopped them monitoring some highly-rated players, as well as seeing the best that Argentina, Chile and USA have to offer at that age group.

It was Argentina who emerged as winners in the tournament, with Boca Juniors midfielder Nicolas Capaldo, 19, scoring the winner in a 1-0 triumph over Brazil in the final.
