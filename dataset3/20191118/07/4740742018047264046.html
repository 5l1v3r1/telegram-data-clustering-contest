Man raging at 'shocking' state of Bristol pavements reveals mission to get them all fixed

Raymond Rose says he got every footpath fixed in Basingstoke - and plans to do the same in Bristol

18 Nov 2019, 07:00 by Conor Gogarty

A Southmead pensioner says he is on a mission to get every last pavement in Bristol repaired.

Raymond Rose, of Bradwell Grove, claims he has inundated Bristol City Council with complaints about the condition of footpaths over the last 12 months, but says none have been addressed.

Though the 71-year-old believes “the whole of Bristol is in a shocking state with its pavements”, he deems the following four to be the worst:

  • Union Street in the city centre
  • The Horsefair in Broadmead
  • Greystoke Avenue in Southmead
  • Doncaster Road in Southmead.

Mr Rose said: “I came to Bristol nine years ago from Basingstoke. I got every footpath that you could think of fixed there.”

But the retired landscape gardener has accused Bristol City Council of being less responsive to complaints than Hampshire County Council.

“Bristol has the worst council in the whole country,” he said.

“When I call up, they just fob me off and never seem to do anything.”

'Worse by the day'

He believes the stretch of pavement most in need of repair is that on Union Street near the Royal British Legion shop.

Mr Rose said: “The slabs are all broken and sticking up. It slants, so the water drains down and there is a big puddle all the time.

“There are broken slabs all the way around Primark in Broadmead. All they seem to do is put a little bit of tarmac in. It needs to be all ripped up and redone properly.”

Asked if it puts him off coming into the city centre, he replied: “Not half.

“They did all that work around Temple Meads but it seems like they don’t care about the city centre. It’s getting worse by the day.”

Pavements in disrepair are particularly problematic for Mr Rose because his wife Susan, 70, uses a wheelchair due to hip problems.

He said: “I have to push her everywhere. It’s very, very difficult all around Bristol.

“My wife is really disgusted by the whole issue. I am finding it hard because I’m 71 and I have to push her over these obstacles.

“It’s a big problem for people in wheelchairs and on scooters going across the pavements.”

Bristol City Council was approached for comment.
