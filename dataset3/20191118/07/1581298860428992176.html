Commonwealth Bank Esperance donates $500 to Restore Hope Foundation

18 Nov 2019, 07:30 by Sarah Makse

The Restore Hope Foundation received a $500 donation from Commonwealth Bank Esperance on Thursday, November 14, to help support struggling families over Christmas.

The volunteer led organisation runs a soup kitchen providing free food deliveries twice a week, and in-kind support to local families doing it tough.

Commonwealth Bank Esperance branch manager Larissa Beeby said the branch voted to support the Restore Hope Foundation because of their dedication to the local community.

"With the festive season approaching, it's a timely opportunity for us to show our support for Restore Hope Foundation," Ms Beeby said.

"In particular, we are thrilled to be able to make a contribution towards their annual toy drive, especially at this time of year.

"It's great to be able to give back and support this work."

Restore Hope Foundation director Ben Pratt said the donation would assist volunteers to distribute toys collected from the annual Esperance Toy Run and Christmas hampers to families in need.

"We get a good team together to wrap the toys and we get some monetary donations to go towards hampers for the Christmas period and we do a delivery right before Christmas," he said.

Foundation chairman Mick Robertson said the donation allowed the group to reach more families over the festive season.

"We are always pretty grateful to people who donate to Restore Hope because that's our lifeblood," he said.

"We don't get anything other than the satisfaction of helping people."

The donation was part of a national program from Commonwealth Bank which pledged to donate $500,000 to organisations making a difference in their community.
