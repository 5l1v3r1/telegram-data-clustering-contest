Port Adelaide has made a bold draft call. It will help them secure a son of a gun

18 Nov 2019, 02:13 by Matt Balmer

Port Adelaide has made a bold decision to hand over their 2020 first round pick to gain an extra selection at next week’s national draft.

The Power struck a deal with Brisbane in order to move up the draft order, resulting in the club walking into the draft with Pick 12, 16 and 18 at their disposal.

But the Lions will be backing the club to do poorly in 2020, given Port Adelaide has handed over their first-round selection for next year.

Brisbane receives Port’s 2020 first-round pick, Pick 29 in 2019 and Pick 71 in 2019, handing over Pick 16, 52, 55 and 72.

The trade helps Port Adelaide move up the draft order before a potential bid on father-son prospect Jackson Mead — which is likely to come in the second round — allowing the club to take three selections on night one and match a bid with their stockpiled fourth round selections.

“We were eager to secure an extra first-round pick in this year’s draft, which is less compromised than next year’s, and this fits our strategy over the last couple of years of securing more top-end talent,” said Port Adelaide general manager of football Chris Davies.

“We are also mindful of having access to a number of potential father-son and NGA recruits both this year and next, so we wanted to give ourselves the best opportunity to select them if we choose to do so.

“This swap has left us in a strong position to attack the draft later this month.”

Brisbane’s first selection in the 2019 draft is now currently Pick 21, with the club offloading late selections they would’ve been unable to use due to their list size.

The 2019 national draft will be broadcast live on FOX FOOTY on November 27 and 28.
