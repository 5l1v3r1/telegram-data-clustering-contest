Prince Andrew interview and Harry rows 'show Queen has lost control of her family'

Royal correspondent Nicholas Witchell claims the Queen's advisers were not consulted about Prince Andrew's Saturday's BBC Newsnight interview

18 Nov 2019, 02:04 by Matthew Dresch, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/matthew-dresch/

The Queen no longer has 'strong central control' of her family, according to the BBC's royal correspondent.

Nicholas Witchell claims Prince Harry's recent attack on the tabloid press and Prince Andrew's Jeffrey Epstein disastrous interview this weekend showed a 'lack of strong central control' in the royal family.

He claimed the Queen's advisers were not consulted over the Duke of York's BBC Newsnight interview, in which he addressed his relationship with his paedophile former pal Epstein.

The journalist said: "The Queen was informed but she is 93 years old now, and she is not exercising the strong control she had, if she ever did.

   

"We have had two episodes within just a couple of months, of senior members of the royal family doing it their way.

"We had Prince Harry with his rant against the tabloid media which was absolutely against the advice of his communications officials who were in despair over it.

“We have a similar situation now. The mainstream advisors of the Queen at the Palace were not a part of this Prince Andrew debate.”

Mr Witchell - once called 'an awful man' by Prince Charles - claims the Queen would have asked her second son 'are you sure' if he told her about his interview plan.

He added that the Duke discussed the Newsnight sit down with his daughters and close advisers.

According to the Express, ex-Buckingham Palace press officer Dickie Arbiter described Prince Andrew's interview on Saturday night as 'excruciating'.

There are conflicting reports over whether the Duke received his mother's permission to go ahead with the interview.

Newsnight presenter Emily Maitlis believed the monarch signed off on the interview after he sought 'approval from higher up'.

However, sources previously told the Express that while the Queen was “aware” of his interview, she had not approved it.

 

Pressure mounted on the Duke to address his friendship with Epstein after the billionaire financier was arrested on sex trafficking charges in July and killed himself in prison in August.

One of Epstein's victims, Virginia Roberts - now Giuffre, claimed she had been trafficked to have sex with Prince Andrew three times.

She said she had sex with the royal when she was 17-years-old.

 

 

The Prince categorically denied having sex with Mrs Giuffre and said he could not even remember meeting her. 

He also denied recalling the infamous picture of him and Mrs Giuffre cosying up to each other being taken, suggesting it could be a fake.
