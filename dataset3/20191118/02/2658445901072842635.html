‘Don’t plunge Kogi into violence’ - The Nation Newspaper

18 Nov 2019, 02:44

By James Azania, Lokoja

 

Bello/Onoja Governorship Campaign Council has called on the Peoples Democratic Party (PDP) to avoid a situation that can plunge the state into violence by desisting from spreading falsehood.

The council, in a statement in Lokoja by the Chairman of the APC Media and Publicity Committee, Kingsley Fanwo, urged the opposition party to await the conclusion of the entire (election) process, which it said had been free, fair and peaceful.

The statement reads: “We have a credible intelligence that the leadership of the Peoples Democratic Party (PDP) has directed the National Publicity Secretary Of the party, Mr. Kola Ologbondiyan, to quickly address a press conference to condemn an ongoing process that has been adjudged free, fair, credible and peaceful, just because they are seeing the handwriting of people’s rejection on the wall.

“The pockets of crisis during the election were caused by the stand of the people against the rigging machinations of the PDP.

“Mr. Ologbondiyan was caught in the same satanic plan as he has been alleged to have carried ballot boxes and papers to his private residence, a situation that was staunchly resisted by the people of his Ward, who insisted on the power of the ballot.

“How will such a person garner enough moral strength to address a press conference to condemn a largely credible process?

“He and other PDP leaders should accept the verdict of the people and clean their names in the face of heavy accusations against them.”

“We commend the Independent National Electoral Commission (INEC) and security agencies, for their excellent performance in ensuring fair conduct.

“To the PDP, they should prepare for future elections with seriousness and stop claiming danger to democracy, when things don’t go their way.

“We also congratulate Kogites for coming out en masse on Saturday to elect their next governor.”
