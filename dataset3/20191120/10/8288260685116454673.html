'Commitment' - The gesture that impressed Birmingham City players - and it involves Aston Villa

The 30-year-old made an interesting candidate for the EFL’s QFQ feature in which he identified Birmingham City's biggest joker and gave one typically modest response

20 Nov 2019, 10:52 by Brian Dick, https://www.facebook.com/blueswriterbriandick/?fref=ts

Lukas Jutkiewicz has been a professional footballer for 13 years, there isn’t much the Birmingham City talisman hasn’t seen or done in a career that has spanned 400 league matches.

So the 30-year-old made an interesting candidate for the EFL’s QFQ feature, where stars are asked a series of quickfire questions.

There’s the standard ones like tea or coffee, night in or night out, Messi or Ronaldo and sweets or chocolate

And there are more revealing questions such as the best pre-match superstition he has witnessed, or his biggest inspiration in football.

For the record it’s this 90s great: “I grew up in Southampton so you can’t really look any further than Matt Le Tissier,” Jutkiewicz revealed.

“I went down to The Dell to watch him and he was just different from everyone else, he could win a game on his own.”

But Birmingham City fans will be more interested in the joker in the Blues pack:

“I think what would have to be David Davis, he’s always pulling pranks everywhere.

“He’s nicking pieces of clothing, usually mine, it then ends up on a hanger or a mannequin out on the training pitch.

“It’s good to have those guys in the changing room, it keeps spirits high and keep it light.”

 

And his response to the most skilful player he has played with, is typically self-effacing.

“To be honest everyone I’ve played with seems to be more skilful than me so it’s a little bit of a tough one that. Scotty Arfield is pretty skilful at Burnley.”

Blues supporters will recall his candidate for best haircut.

“It’s got to be Ryan Shotton before the Blues-Villa derby, he dyed his hair blue so that was pretty good commitment to the cause I thought.”

 

That was in October 2016, the first league meeting between the rivals for six years, in which Davis cancelled out Gary Gardner’s opener for the visitors.

The game ended 1-1 and Shotton’s blue rinse clearly lived long in some memories.

Finally, asked the best thing about football, Jutkiewicz replied:

“For me personally I get to do what I love doing as my job every day and I think football is very inclusive, anyone can go and play football, no matter where you’re from, no matter what your upbringing is like, I would like to think that football is for everybody.”
