Astronomers brand Elon Musk 'man who polluted heavens' after Starlink launch

20 Nov 2019, 10:42

Elon Musk is forging ahead with a plan to make fast internet available to everyone via a network of interconnected satellites.

But he might be doing it at the expense of the night sky.

SpaceX, the private company that Musk founded, is in the middle of pursuing the ‘Starlink’ project. In its entirety, the project will consist of 12,000 mass-produced satellites all orbiting the Earth and beaming internet signal down to us.

The company sent 60 satellites into space earlier this month to join 60 that were previously launched in the summer.

But Earth-based astronomers have already come out to criticise the endevour as the satellites have begun to interfere with observations of the night sky.
