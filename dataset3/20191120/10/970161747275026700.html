Mourinho an 'angry man who will ram words down critics' throats,' says Souness

20 Nov 2019, 10:21

Jose Mourinho’s anger and frustration at criticism will lead him to be a success as Tottenham manager, believes Graeme Souness.

Mourinho’s appointment as Spurs boss on Wednesday morning was met with mixed reactions from Tottenham fans and the wider football world.

The 56-year-old has been out of management since leaving Manchester United in December last year and came under fire for his spell at Old Trafford, despite winning the Europa League, EFL Cup and finishing second in the Premier League during his two-and-a-half years in charge.

The Portuguese was sacked with United sixth in the table, having been criticised for a negative style of play and getting little return from nearly £400m spent on 11 players.

However, since winning the Portuguese title and UEFA Cup with Porto in 2003, Mourinho has gone on to win trophies with Chelsea, Inter Milan, Real Madrid and Manchester United.

It is this consistent success, along with the drive to prove his critics wrong, that will make him a hit at Spurs, believes Souness.

‘He is a guy that has won trophies wherever he has been. I would expect him to do the same at Spurs,’ said the former Liverpool manager.

‘Spurs have won three trophies in 28 years. For a big club that’s just not good enough.

‘Spurs are genuinely a big football club and they need someone that can turn what is perceived to be attacking, attractive football into maybe a slightly more pragmatic approach that will put some silverware in the boardroom, and I think he is the perfect fit for them.

‘He is an angry man, deep inside. He’s a frustrated man, the way it turned out at United. I think Spurs will reap the benefit of that.

‘He knew he was going to go back and he was just waiting for the right one. He loves the English game.

‘I think Spurs are getting the right man at the right time. He feels he has a point to prove to people who doubted him or were critical of him. He will want to ram those words down his critics’ throats.’

Tottenham’s three trophies in 28 years are the 1991 FA Cup and League Cup wins in 1999 and 2008.

They have not won a league title since 1961 or a European competition since the UEFA Cup triumph in 1984.
