FG Queries NFF Over Nigerian Teams Performance

20 Nov 2019, 10:11 by Emmanuel Egobiambu

The Federal Government has queried the Nigeria Football Federation (NFF) over the country’s performances at major competitions.

Concise News understands that the Minister of Sports and Youth Development Sunday Dare met the NFF on Monday over the poor showing of the national teams.

The Super Falcons, as well as the U-23 Eagles, will not take part in the football event of the 2020 Tokyo Olympics after failing to qualify for the sporting event.

Nigeria failed to make it past the Group B of the Africa U-23 Cup of Nations (AFCON) in Egypt which serves as the qualifier for Tokyo 2020.

On the other hand, the female team were beaten by Ivory Coast in the qualifying game for the biggest sporting event.

Also, Nigeria’s Golden Eaglets, six-time winners, were knocked out of the U-17 World Cup 2019 in the second round by The Netherlands.

Earlier in June, the Golden Eaglets stumbled into the Round of 16 at the 2019 U-20 World Cup before crashing out.

In October, the Super Eagles Team B failed to qualify for the 2020 African Nations Championship (CHAN) after Togo defeated them.

These developments have left the Nigerian government concerned with Dare asking the NFF to explain why the country’s teams have performed woefully this year.

The meeting was attended by the NFF President Amaju Pinnick, First Vice-President Seyi Akinwunmi, second Vice-President Shehu Dikko and General Secretary Mohammed Sanusi.

“Yesterday I led the Ministry of Youth and Sports in a meeting with the NFF,” Dare tweeted.

“A single agenda meeting: to provide an explanation to the country as to why we suffered several failures recently – towards taking appropriate and far-reaching steps to improve Nigerian football.”
