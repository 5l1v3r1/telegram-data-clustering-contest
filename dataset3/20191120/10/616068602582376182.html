Vernon a lot safer

20 Nov 2019, 10:23 by Darren Handschuh

It was just two years ago when Vernon was named the ninth most dangerous city in all the nation.

According to a McLeans report, Vernon cracked the top 10 following a series of shootings and other crimes in 2016 that lead to the ranking in 2017.

But last year, Vernon was ranked at No. 25 in the nation and this year it is even better.

The McLeans crime severity index has Vernon at No. 44 for 2019.

According to McLeans, “The report ranks communities according to the Cime Severity Index (CSI), a Statistics Canada measure of all police-reported crime, which takes into consideration both the volume and seriousness of offences. The 2018 data, the most current available, was released July 23, 2019.”

The ranking takes a number of factors into consideration, but RCMP do not put much stock in the survey results.

RCMP Const. Jocelyn Noseworthy said police are happy to see the city now ranked at 44, but noted a single incident in the city can boost the ranking.

The rankings are based on crimes per 100,000 and a single incident can have a big impact on the end results.

“We are super happy we are further down on the list, but I am not sure how much stock we put into it either,” said Noseworthy. “But it's great to see that we are going down.”

Penticton was ranked No. 20, Kelowna 139 and Kamloops came in at 67.

The top three most dangerous communities in Canada according to McLeans is Thompson, Man. at No. 1, a single point ahead of North Battleford, Sask. at No 2 and Portage La Prairie, Man. at No. 3.
