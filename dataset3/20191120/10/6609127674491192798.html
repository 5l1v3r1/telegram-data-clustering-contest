STILL PUSHING

Paisley dealer’s Snapchat still advertising high purity cocaine as he’s in jail over drugs that killed Chelsea Bruce

20 Nov 2019, 10:54 by Blair Meikle

A DRUG pusher’s social media is still offering deals - even though he’s locked up after supplying fatal ecstasy.

Lewis Reid, 22, sold crystallised MDMA to pals of Chelsea Bruce, 16, who collapsed at a party and died after taking a hit.

But despite serving time we can reveal his Snapchat account is being used to promote high-purity cocaine - known as Prop - at £80 a gram.

Tonight, Scottish Conservative shadow justice secretary Liam Kerr said: “This is a completely unacceptable state of affairs.

“When this drug dealer was convicted and sentenced, his criminal operation should have gone down with him.

“Instead, it’s still up-and-running and causing untold damage to vulnerable young people.

“This must cause even more misery to the family of the victim who have suffered enough.”

It’s understood the account named 'Gucci' is being run by a pal on the outside.

A message posted last night read: “Prop their £80 a gram. More weights call number if need about Glasgow/Pollok/Paisley all night. Save number.”

And a message on Twitter from Reid, of Paisley, advertises the Snapchat account and offers “1g for 40, 2 for 70, 3 for 100, 2g of prop £140.”

He even mocked cops with a crude “F*** the CID” spelled out in white powder.

Reid - nicknamed Gucci - is behind bars after admitting 22 offences between August 2018 and July.

Chelsea, from Glasgow, died in July after taking MDMA supplied by Reid.
