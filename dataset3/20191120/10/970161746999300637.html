Chadwick Boseman doesn’t want Marvel’s Black Panther to become a Disney+ TV Show

20 Nov 2019, 10:03

Black Panther star Chadwick Boseman has quashed any hopes of a Disney+ adaptation of the hit movie… because he doesn’t want to do a TV show.

While the likes of Tom Hiddleston, Elizabeth Olsen and Anthony Mackie might be willing to reprise their roles on the small screen as Disney+ plans the rollout of new Marvel TV shows, we’re not going to see Black Panther as one of them.

Chadwick, who plays the lead role of T’Challa aka Black Panther, is focussing on movies rather than working in TV.

When asked which Avengers co-star he would choose to team up with for another Marvel adventure, Chadwick told MTV: ‘I wouldn’t because I don’t want to do a TV show.

‘I wouldn’t choose one so I can’t answer that. But I have a whole list of things that I’m excited about and none of those are franchises.’

While we won’t get the TV show we were hoping for, Chadwick will be returning to the iconic role for Black Panther 2, slated for a 2022 release.

He revealed what he hoped to see from the sequel: ‘Hopefully we can maintain the foundation that we set and build upon it and expand and explore.

‘That’s what I hope, but I think first you need to maintain the foundation. We built a culture and a world and so you have to make sure that you keep that in place.’

Ryan Coogler will be returning to direct the sequel and previously admitted there was some pressure to match the success of the first film, which grossed $1.34billion (£1.03billion) at the box office.

He told IndieWire: ‘I think the pressure is kind of always going to be there. I’ve had a chance to make three feature films, each one of them had its own very specific type of pressure. In the process of it, it feels insurmountable each time.

‘When it comes to making a sequel, I’ve never done it before, a sequel to something that I’ve directed myself.

‘So I think there’s gonna be a lot of pressure there, but what we’re going to try to do is just focus on the work, like we always do.

‘Really try to go step by step and try to quiet everything else around us, really focus on trying to make something that has some type of meaning.’

While details of the storyline are a closely-guarded secret, Marvel Studios president Kevin Feige previously revealed the studios have decided on a ‘pretty solid direction’ for the second film.

Black Panther 2 will be released in May 2022.
