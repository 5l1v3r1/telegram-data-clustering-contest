Lorraine Kelly delivers brutal ITV Leaders Debate verdict and it's absolutely perfect

The Scottish TV presenter was among millions of Brits tuning into the hour-long debate on ITV1 last night

20 Nov 2019, 10:45 by James Rodger, https://www.facebook.com/jamesrodgerjournalist

Lorraine Kelly was left unimpressed with the "underwhelming" ITV Leaders Debate.

The Scottish TV presenter was among millions of Brits tuning into the hour-long debate on ITV1 last night.

The debate, on Tuesday, November 19, saw Jeremy Corbyn and Boris Johnson do battle - with YouGov calling it a virtual "tie".

Today, Lorraine spoke out on her ITV1 daytime show, giving her savage verdict on the Labour and Tory leaders' contest.

The 59-year-old, speaking to Susanna Reid and Piers Morgan on Good Morning Britain, classed it as "underwhelming" and asked Ranvir Singh: "Who do you think was the least worst?"

The GMB reporter replied: "I think if you were undecided before watching the debate, you probably ended an hour later still undecided - I don't think it necessarily helped many people make a decision.

"And the point of those debates is to help undecided voters think one way or the other."

 

"That handshake though," Lorraine added.

"How ghastly was that.

"That kind of summed it all up really, it was so excruciating. I don't know why they had to do that, it was horrible."

 

The Scottish mum-of-one then said: "I just felt like I hadn't learnt anything!"

"And then at the end when they were talking about Christmas presents - you just think 'for the love of God'.
