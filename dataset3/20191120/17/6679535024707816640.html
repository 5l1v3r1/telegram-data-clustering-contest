CBI files supplementary charge sheet against 2 in Narendra Dabholkar murder case

PTI

20 Nov 2019, 17:29 by PTI

The CBI on Wednesday filed a supplementary charge sheet against accused Sanjeev Punalekar and Vikram Bhave in the 2013 murder case of rationalist Narendra Dabholkar.

While Mr. Bhave is in Yerawada jail here under judicial custody, Mr. Punalekar, a lawyer, is out on bail.

The CBI, through its special public prosecutor Prakash Suryawanshi, filed the supplementary charge sheet in the court of assistant sessions judge S.R. Navandar, presiding over a special court set up under the Unlawful Activities (Prevention) Act (UAPA).

Till now, charge sheet has been filed against five accused in the high-profile murder case suspected to be handiwork of right-wing activists.

Mr. Punalekar and his assistant Mr. Bhave were arrested by the CBI on May 25. In July, Punalekar was released on bail.

Mr. Punalekar is accused of advising Sharad Kalaskar, one of the two alleged motorcycle-borne assailants who gunned down Dabholkar here on August 20, 2013, to destroy the weapons used in the crime.

According to the CBI, Mr. Bhave helped the two alleged shooters - Sachin Andure and Mr. Kalaskar - in conducting a survey of the spot where the well-known anti-superstition activist was shot dead while on a morning walk on the Onkareshwar Bridge here.

Mr. Bhave is also accused of helping the gunmen escape from the murder spot and assisting them to abandon the motorcycle used in the crime.
