Rangers yob fined £750 for chanting sectarian support of UVF outside Rugby Park

20 Nov 2019, 17:23 by Campbell Thomas

A RANGERS yob has been fined for chanting sectarian support of the UVF.

Mark Ashe, 23, was with other away fans who were singing as they waited to enter Kilmarnock's Rugby Park ground in Ayrshire on February 9.

Ashe was originally accused of swearing and "singing sectarian lyrics and lyrics in support of a terrorist organisation".

Prosecutors did not specify any particular armed group in the statutory breach of the peace charge at Kilmarnock Sheriff Court.

Ashe, who denied the offence, was found guilty of an amended charge of threatening or abusive behaviour likely to cause fear and alarm, aggravated by religious prejudice.

Sheriff Shirley Foran cleared him of swearing and replaced the words "singing sectarian lyrics and lyrics in support of a terrorist organisation" with "repeatedly chanted lyrics in support of the UVF".

The only lyric that could be identified clearly was "UVF", the court heard.

Ashe, of Winchburgh, West Lothian, was fined £750.

Sheriff Foran ruled the first offender should not be given a football banning order.
