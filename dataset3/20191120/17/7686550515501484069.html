Couple 'humiliated' when Aldi made them unpack shopping after querying bill for £40 grill

Daniel and Pamela Murray claim the retail company didn't even apologise

20 Nov 2019, 17:12 by Kelly Williams, Katie-Ann Gupwell

A couple claim they have been left "embarrassed and humiliated" after they were forced to empty their shopping bags to prove they hadn't purchased a portable gas grill that they were charged for incorrectly.

Daniel and Pamela Murray visited the Aldi store at Rhyl 's Marina Quay for a few items, but were left bewildered when the bill totaled at over £66.

They later queried the bill, and say the cashier said she had put everything through the scanner that resulted in the total price.

The couple, who are from Rhyl and are in their 60s, then asked for a receipt to be printed.

It was later found out that they'd been charged £39.99 for a portable gas grill that they hadn't even purchased.

Following the incident, a manager was called to attend and they were made to empty their bags in front of a queue of people waiting to be served.

Speaking to North Wales Live , Daniel said: "We'd only popped in for a few things and there was only one till open at the time.

"We loaded our items onto the belt but we got a shock when the cashier told us the bill was £66.

"I said: 'That can't be right, we've only got two carrier bags.'

"I asked her to print a receipt and it was showing £39.99 for a portable gas grill we hadn't even bought.

"The manager and a security guard were called over and we had to unpack all our shopping so they could check the item wasn't there.

"By this time a big queue was forming and we felt so embarrassed and humiliated. We didn't even get an apology."

Daniel went on to explain that, eventually, the £39.99 was deducted from the bill with the total expected only to come in at £26.

He added: "After the incident I decided to write a letter to the store to say I wasn't happy with how the matter had been handled having not been given an apology.

"I also wanted to warn them that maybe something had been coded up wrong, in case this happens to someone else who might not notice being overcharged before it's too late.

"How would people who've left the store prove they hadn't bought it?

"Anyway, I didn't get any response to my letter, nor to a further email that was sent to the Aldi head office.

"We don't want anything, we just don't think it's very good customer service."

The incident is not the first to make the press as the store, which opened in August, has hit the headlines before.

Customers were left with lump sums coming out of their accounts two months after making purchases after a technical glitch led to card payments not taking instantly during the stores first five weeks of trading.

The issue allegedly led some families into financial difficulty.

An Aldi spokesman told North Wales Live: "We have apologised to the family for their experience at the Rhyl store.”
