[BREAKING] Govt withdraws appointment of Baffour Awuah as new Ghana Health Service Boss

20 Nov 2019, 05:39 by MyNewsGH

President Akufo-Addo has withdrawn the appointment of Dr. Baffour Awuah as Director General of the Ghana Health Service (GHS) MyNewsGh.com has been authoritatively informed.

The termination follows information forwarded to the seat of government by a Medical Team loyal to the New Patriotic Party(NPP) that revealed the new GHS boss is an opposition NDC loyalist since 1992 but managed to warm his way into NPP after January 7th 2017.

Dr. Baffour Awuah was appointed after the contract of Dr Anthony Nsiah- Asare, the former Director General was terminated even though it had been extended to 22nd May 2020 earlier.

A deep-throat source at the seat of government told MyNewsGh.com that Dr. Baffour Awuah has been officially notified of the decision to terminate his appointment with immediate effect. Before the termination, his appointment was already hanging pending the approval of the Public Service Commission.

Health Minister Kwaku Agyeman Manu is said to be livid about the development as Dr Baffour Awuah before his elevation served him as a Personal Assistant.

Also instrumental in the decision, MyNewsGH.com was told, was Kofi Ofosu Nkansah (NPP footsoldiers’ leader) who carried social media grievances against the new GHS boss to the top following complaints he received on social media.

Komfo Anokye Hospital NPP caucus petition and counter petition

A team of doctors, pharmacists, nurses etc led by one Albert Opoku all working at the Komfo Anokye Hospital wrote a petition to the presidency detailing how Dr. Baffour Awuah who is a former Komfo Anokye Hospital Director frustrated them because of their NPP affiliation between 2008 to 2016 when he rose to head the top Ashanti regional medical facility.

Following the petition, Dr. Baffour Awuah is alleged to have also instigated the formation of his own Komfo Anokye NPP Caucus to issue a counter statement to declare support for him which caught the attention of the presidency.
