Bookworms at Tidcombe win Summer Reading Challenge for third year in a row

The popular event takes place every year over the six week summer holiday

20 Nov 2019, 12:42 by Lewis Clarke

Pupils at Tidcombe Primary have proven to be the best bookworms in Tiverton once again.

The school has won the Summer Reading Challenge for the third consecutive year.

Head of English and assistant headteacher, Jill Mallender said: “Summer may seem like a distant memory, but for children and staff we are still celebrating.

“The popular event takes place every year over the six week summer holiday, and it is organised by Tiverton Library.

“Tidcombe School is one of the smaller Primary schools in Tiverton with just 194 children currently on roll, but, size obviously does not matter for these little bookworms.

“We are all so proud of our children, and as a school, we are very passionate about encouraging our children to read. We have super supportive parents who share our drive to promote reading every evening and would like to say a special thank you to them, for helping the children to achieve this award for the third year running.”
