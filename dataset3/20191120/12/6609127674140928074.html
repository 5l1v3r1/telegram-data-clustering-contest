TREASURE HUNT

Medieval ring and Roman brooch worth £1,500 ‘stolen’ from Edinburgh train

20 Nov 2019, 12:05 by Justin Bowie

MEDIEVAL and Roman treasure worth £1,500 was “stolen” from an Edinburgh train as cops launch a hunt to find it.

A ring worth £500 and a brooch valued at £1,000 vanished after being left on a train at Durham.

It is understood the items were then “nicked” as the train made its way to Edinburgh Waverley since they were not handed into lost property.

And now police are appealing to the public for help as they attempt to trace the treasure.

Cops believe the theft would have occurred between the evening of Friday October 4 and 12.30am on Saturday October 5 when the train arrived in the Scots capital.

The historic ring is believed to date from the mid to post-medieval period.

And the brooch is believed to date from the late Roman era.

Anyone with information that can help locate the treasure should call British Transport Police on 0800 40 50 or text them via 61016, quoting reference number 1900086888.

Alternatively, you can contact Crimestoppers anonymously on 0800 555 111.
