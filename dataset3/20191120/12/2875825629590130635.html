Netflix announce Vikings spin-off series which will be filmed in Ashford, Co. Wicklow

Vikings creator Michael Hirst has expressed his joy to be returning to 'glorious' Wicklow where the spin-off series will be filmed

20 Nov 2019, 12:57 by Ian Mangan

Netflix has announced it has commissioned a spin-off of the hit TV series Vikings which is set to be filmed in Ireland.

The streaming service and MGM announced that the new show, entitled Vikings Valhalla, will take place 100 years after the original History Channel series.

It will follow the story of some of the most famous Vikings in history Leif Erickon, Freydis, Harald Harada and the Norman King William the Conqueror in what promises to be an "explosive new chapter" in the saga Netflix says.

Vikings creator Michael Hirst has expressed his joy to be returning to 'glorious' Wicklow where the spin-off series will be filmed.

The news comes as a major boost to Ashford Studios where the original series was also shot.

Hirst, who also worked as a writer on The Tudors, expressed his happiness to be returning to "glorious" Wicklow.

He said: "I am so happy that we are returning to Ireland and Ashford Studios in glorious County Wicklow, which has been our home for the last eight years."

"Our Irish crew, in my opinion, is the best and most professional crew in the world. And it has been a particular joy to me, shared with my producer Morgan O’Sullivan, that we have been able to give continuous employment to many hundreds of Irish men and women."

Hirst also hailed Ireland for "its support of writers and drama" and said the show will benefit both from "cultural and financial support."

He added: "Making a great show isn’t a mechanical activity; it’s a human one!”

This year it was announced that Vikings which ran between 2012 and 2018 will air its sixth and final season in December this year.

The series featured performances from a number of Irish stars including the likes of Gabriel Byrne, Moe Dunford, Ferdia Walsh-Peelo, Jonathan Rhys Meyers and Leah McNamara.

Its continuation is not the only Netflix project that is being produced in Ireland.
