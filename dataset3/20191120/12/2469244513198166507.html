Devon manufacturer HepcoMotion praised in national awards

The company took plaudits in the regional heats of a national manufacturing awards programme organised by Make UK

20 Nov 2019, 12:42 by Lewis Clarke

HepcoMotion has been named runner-up in three categories recognising excellence in manufacturing.

The company took plaudits in the regional heats of a national manufacturing awards programme organised by Make UK, the manufacturers’ organisation.

HepcoMotion based in Tiverton were runners up in three different categories with Maurice Porter, group training manager runner up in the leadership award and Andrew Crew a final year engineering apprentice also receiving recognition. The third award saw HepcoMotion runners-up in the developing future talent category.

Judges said: “HepcoMotion’s approach to talent development gives back to the community by taking unemployed people, training them and injecting a huge level of enthusiasm.”

The awards which recognise excellence among manufacturing companies in six categories, as well as two apprentice categories, were presented at the south annual dinner of Make UK which was sponsored by Japanese IT solutions provider Hulft and Menzies LLP.

Caroline Milton, partner and manufacturing specialist at Menzies, said: “UK manufacturing is a great British success story. A healthy sector is instrumental in driving economic growth and plays a critical role in rebalancing the economy.

“The Future Manufacturing awards recognise the importance of manufacturers and apprentices across Britain, reminding us of their contribution and the hard work, dedication and ambition going on in every part of this vibrant sector. We congratulate all winners for their achievements and wish them the best of luck at the national finals.”

All the winners will now go forward to the national finals to be judged in London in December.
