The Crown's Josh O'Connor praises Emma Corrin they film as Diana and Charles

20 Nov 2019, 12:08

The Crown’s Josh O’Connor has heaped praise on his new co-star Emma Corrin as the pair prepare to retell the love story of Prince Charles and Diana, Princess of Wales in season 4.

Josh was introduced into the iconic Netflix series in the newly-released season three, and is now filming for season four – which dictates the time period he meets and marries Diana Spencer (played by Emma).

It’s speculated that The Crown season 4 will pick up in 1977 and run through to 1990 – during which time the pair met, married, and had their sons Prince William and Prince Harry.

Speaking to The Wrap, Josh said: ‘I obviously won’t go into exactly what we’re doing, but we’re shooting Season 4 at the moment and Emma’s doing an extraordinary job.’

‘I think what will be interesting is that, you know, ultimately, we know so much about the Charles and Diana relationship,’ he added. ‘We specifically know a lot about Diana’s side of things, we also know an awful lot about Charles’ side of things.

‘I think what will be interesting is how Peter [Morgan, showrunner] navigates both sides of things and tells that story from what we know.’

The breakout star of season three added that whatever happens in the plotline, the show will always return to the two main focal points – the crown, and the power it holds.

Emma has been seen filming on set as Princess Diana, looking like the spitting image of the beloved princess as they recreated the couple’s 1983 tour to Australia.

Charles and Diana divorced in 1996, which is likely to be documented in season 5, by an entirely new cast as the show ages up once again.

Season three saw the introduction of Camilla Shand (Emerald Fennell) into Prince Charles’s life as the pair began dating.

However, the pair soon split, despite Charles’s protests, and Camilla is seen marrying Andrew Parker-Bowles as Charles is sent to the Caribbean as part of his duty in the Navy.

Following their respective divorces, and Princess Diana’s death in 1997, the pair reunited, and married in 2005, becoming the Duke and Duchess of Cornwall.
