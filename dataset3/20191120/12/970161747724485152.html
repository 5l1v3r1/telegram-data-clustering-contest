Doctor Who may return sooner than expected for New Year's Day special

20 Nov 2019, 12:00

Doctor Who fans will be sorely disappointed when for the second year running there will be no Christmas Day special – but they could be in for a New Year’s Day treat instead.

Earlier this year, fans were treated to the first New Year’s Day special for the long-running sci-fi show, which saw Thirteenth Doctor Jodie Whittaker face the Daleks for the first time.

Now that filming on the up-coming series 12 has finished, Whovians are eagerly awaiting the Time Lord’s return – and they could be in for less of a wait than they initially thought.

According to The Mirror, the show will return with a blockbuster, two-part special to utter in its new run of episodes, with the first instalment airing at the turn of 2020.

A source spilled: ‘It’s going to be huge. The scale is just epic. No one will be disappointed.’

A rep for Doctor Who refused to comment.

TARDIS companion Bradley Walsh has already teased that fans can expect to see some ‘terrifying’ new monsters on the 12th run of the re-booted series.

‘I’ve got to say, be prepared for the monsters,’ he said. ‘There are some monsters that will absolutely make your hair curl.

‘There’s a couple of monsters they’ve come up with that are absolutely terrifying,’ he added to Radio Times. ‘Absolutely terrifying.’

Considering the legacy the show has for creating scary delights – from the Daleks to the Weeping Angels – we’ll prepare ourselves for the nightmares.

Hopefully, viewers will get the news they’ve been craving soon as the Doctor Who’s official Twitter has already teased a massive announcement is coming in just three days time.

The date of November 23 is very significant, especially for the hard-core fans, who will recognise this as the day when the show’s first-ever episode was aired all the way back in 1963.

And speaking of the new series – A former star of the show has also claimed that the next cycle could be the best yet, and that Whittaker herself will be ‘better than ever’ in her second run, just like David Tennant and Matt Smith before her.
