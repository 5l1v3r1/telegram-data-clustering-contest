Alleged ‘womb raider’ gives birth while being held on murder charges

20 Nov 2019, 01:47 by Tamar Lapin

One of the women accused of slaughtering a pregnant Chicago teenager and ripping the baby from her womb gave birth to her own child this month, authorities said.

Desiree Figueroa — who has been held on murder charges since May 16 in the death of 19-year-old Marlen Ochoa-Lopez — gave birth Nov. 1 at Stroger Hospital and was back in jail Monday, according to the Cook County sheriff’s office.

It’s unclear what agency has custody of the newborn or whether Figueroa, 25, will be able to visit her infant.

Authorities said the infant’s gender, and other details, could not be released, The Chicago Tribune reported.

Figueroa and her 49-year-old mother, Clarisa Figueroa, allegedly lured Ochoa-Lopez to their home April 23 with the promise of free baby clothes.

The nine-months-pregnant teen was missing for about three weeks, until her decaying body was found May 15, dumped in a garbage can behind a home on the city’s Southwest Side.

The mother-daughter duo allegedly strangled Ochoa-Lopez, cut her baby out of her womb and pretended Clarisa had just given birth to the boy when they called 911 to report that he was not breathing.

The baby, named Yovanny Yadiel Lopez, suffered severe brain damage during the grisly incident and died in June.

The women have pleaded not guilty to first-degree murder charges in the deaths of Ochoa-Lopez and her baby.
