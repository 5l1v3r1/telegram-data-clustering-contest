Third period surge leads Storm past Rangers

Kitchener has now lost eight of their last 10 games, and remain last in the Western Conference

20 Nov 2019, 02:32 by Mark Pare

Give the Kitchener Rangers credit.

Coming in against the hottest team and best defense in the OHL, it was going to be an uphill battle.

But with the game tied at one, the Guelph Storm scored a backbreaker with a second left in the second period and put up three more in the final frame to take down the Blueshirts 5-1 Tuesday night at The Aud.

Riley Damiani tallied a power play marker off a beauty of a backhand feed from Jonathan Yantsis in the second, extending Damiani's point streak to six games.

But that's all they could get by Nico Daws, who made 30 saves to claim the victory.

Pavel Gogolev had a three-point night, putting him up to 19 points in his last 10 games.

Cam Hillis had a goal and an assist, while Keegan Stevenson, Josh Wainman and Cedric Ralph scored the others for the visitors.

Lucas Pfeil stopped 20 shots in the loss.

Kitchener has now lost eight of their last 10 games, and things don't get any easier with London paying a visit to Kitchener Friday night.

Pregame on 570 NEWS is 7:00 p.m.
