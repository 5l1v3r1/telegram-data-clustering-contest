Chandigarh: Re-transplantation of trees begins to make way for Sector 29 flyover

Horticulturist Rahul Mahajan said that the trees will be transplanted in four phases, beginning from Sector 29, which will be continued at Sector 31, Industrial Area Phase 1 and Hallomajra.

20 Nov 2019, 02:37 by Express News Service

The work of re-transplantation of as many as 180 trees out of 445 that will face the axe for the construction of Sector 29 flyover began on Tuesday. The trees re-transplantation will be done under the supervision of horticulturist Rahul Mahajan.

Speaking to Chandigarh Newsline, Mahajan said that the trees will be transplanted in four phases, beginning from Sector 29, which will be continued at Sector 31, Industrial Area Phase 1 and Hallomajra.

“It will take us around 90 to 100 days to transplant the trees. All the trees will be transplanted in nagar van,” he said.

The work of Sector 29 flyover, the first flyover in the region, will begin this year.

How transplantation will be done

For the re-transplantion of trees, which are to be axed for the construction of the flyover, first, the trenches will be dug. The trenches will be dug slightly away from the root zone, in a triangular shape and it will be watered every day to acclimatize the root system. It will be followed by clearing the soil after 30 to 40 days. The same will be pruned as per need and later it will be lifted with a Hydra. The tree will then be transported to the new pit made for the tree.
