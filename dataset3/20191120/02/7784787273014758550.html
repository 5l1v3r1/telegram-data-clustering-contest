Young Carlton pair Zac Fisher and Will Setterfield have burst out of the blocks this pre-season

20 Nov 2019, 02:07

Two young Carlton midfielders are poised for breakout seasons after bursting out of the gates at the start of the pre-season.

Zac Fisher and Will Setterfield are two names who emerged on Tuesday, firstly via Blues co-captain Patrick Cripps at a press conference and then from the mouth of assistant coach Dale Amos on SEN.

Fisher was linked to a few clubs during the trade period, where the Blues failed to land Sydney small forward Tom Papley or Gold Coast Suns midfielder-forward Jack Martin.

The West Australian played a career-best 21 games in 2019 but could fight for midfield minutes with Martin, who is expected to find his way to Princes Park via the Pre-Season Draft next week.

Setterfield arrived at Carlton this time last year still recovering from the anterior cruciate ligament tear that ruined his second season at Greater Western Sydney.

Despite a limited maiden pre-season at Princes Park, the inside midfielder played 18 games, building momentum as the season progressed.

“There’s probably two that have jumped out. Zac Fisher has come back in really good shape,” Amos told SEN on Tuesday.

“He looks really strong and in really good shape ready to start to elevate himself.

“Will Setterfield off the back of joining us after having done his ACL looks like a different athlete to what he was 12 months ago.

“Those guys have done all the work to put themselves in position to have a great pre-season. Typically that transfers into a big season.”

Carlton unveiled their new Puma guernseys for 2020 in a warehouse in Brunswick East on Tuesday afternoon, after signing a ten-year deal with the German company earlier this year.
