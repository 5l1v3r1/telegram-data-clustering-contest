Hurts worked on ball security during post-game workout

20 Nov 2019, 06:05

NORMAN — Oklahoma quarterback Jalen Hurts was doing his usual post-game workout in the early hours of Sunday morning, but, this time, he had an extra focus.

When the Sooners landed in Norman around 2 a.m. Sunday morning after beating Baylor 34-31, Hurts was practicing his ball security while he went through his now-routine, post-game workout.

Hurts fumbled the ball three times against the Bears. He recovered the ball once, but Baylor recovered the other two.

"It ain't the first time I've fumbled, and it probably won't be the last," Hurts said.

One of Hurts' fumbles against Baylor came in the third quarter as he was trying to get into the end zone. The ball was stripped out of his arm before he crossed the goal line, and the Bears recovered. Afterward, Hurts acknowledged his struggle with ball security in the game.

"My ball security, it sucks," Hurts said. "So I'll try to fix it."

Hurts said practice and focus are the only ways to keep from fumbling. Hurts has fumbled six times this season and lost it four times, according to CBSSports.

"What do shooters do when they really wanna hit shots? They keep shooting," Hurts said. "So (I) gotta keep going."

Hurts named Big 12 Offensive Player of Week

Jalen Hurts was named the Big 12 Offensive Player of the Week, the conference announced on Monday.

Hurts threw for 297 yards and four touchdowns and ran for 114 yards in Oklahoma's 34-31 win over Baylor on Saturday.

Hurts led Oklahoma's second half comeback on offense, after the Sooners had struggled to get going on the defense.

This is the third time this season Hurts has earned conference player of the week honors.

Sooners' O-line named semifinalist for Joe Moore Award

Oklahoma's offensive line is a semifinalist for the Joe Moore Award, it was announced Tuesday.

The Joe Moore Award is given annually to the nation's top offensive line unit, and the Sooners are one of 10 semifinalists.

The Sooners' offense leads the nation in yards per play, averaging 8.8 yards so far this season and ranks No. 3 nationally with 6.5 yards per rush.

Oklahoma won the Joe Moore Award last season.

Other semifinalists for the award include Alabama, Clemson, Georgia, LSU and Ohio State.

Finalists will be announced on Dec. 10, and the winning unit will be announced after a surprise visit to the winning team's campus in late December.

Sooners-Baylor highest rated game on Saturday

Oklahoma's comeback over Baylor was historical, and it made for great TV.

The Sooners' 34-31 win over the Bears was the most watched game of the week across all networks, attracting 6,785,000 viewers. It was the eighth most viewed game of the season across all networks.

Among full national windows only, the game was ABC's highest viewed Big 12 intra-conference game since Oklahoma played Oklahoma State in 2013.
