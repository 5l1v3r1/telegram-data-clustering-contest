General Election 2019: Voters say Boris Johnson edged live ITV debate with Jeremy Corbyn by 51% to 49%

20 Nov 2019, 06:14 by Natasha Clark

BORIS JOHNSON and Jeremy Corbyn traded blows over Brexit and the NHS in last night’s ITV leaders’ debate.

The Labour leader was laughed at by the live audience as he swerved questions on if he wants to remain in the EU and how he would campaign in a second Brexit referendum.

A YouGov poll of viewers after last night’s hour-long head-to-head showed Mr Johnson edging it by 51 per cent to 49 per cent.

Meanwhile, The Liberal Democrats launch their manifesto today, and have promised 20,000 more teachers for England and a £10bn a year spend on schools if Jo Swinson’s party is elected.
