Rag'n'Bone Man 'splits with wife Beth Rouy six months after getting married'

The singer, real name Rory Graham, got hitched wearing a tracksuit but now it appears the pair have ended their relationship ten years after they met before he found fame

20 Nov 2019, 06:44 by Sam Elliott, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/sam-elliott/

Rag'n'Bone Man and his wife Beth Rouy have reportedly called it quits - six months after getting married.

The Sun say that it's all over between the singer and his new wife.

The music star, real name Rory Graham, wed Beth in Sussex in May. They wore tracksuits, shunning shoes, ties and shirts and ordered guests to dress relaxed.

But it appears they couldn't make it work despite being together for more than ten years.

The pair share son Reuben, two, are it is claimed they have decided to just be 'close friends'.

 

A source has claimed: "It’s a great shame, but these things happen. Everyone is staying amicable, and they have a son together who is their top priority.

"He likes to keep a low profile so doesn’t want to make a big deal out of it, but there are no explosive rows and he isn’t likely to say anything about it.

"It looks like it’s a very sad end to what was a very loving and long-term relationship.

 

"The wedding pictures show what a quirky couple they were, which makes how this has ended so soon even more heartbreaking."

In June he spoke about married life, saying: “It’s great, man.

“We’ve been together for ten years anyway so it’s exactly the same. We’ve just got the same name now.”

The Human hitmaker is now worth almost £3.1million - even after splashing out £1million on a new home.

Figures for his company, Ne Oublie Limited – which is French for “Don’t Forget” – show assets in the firm rocketed from £1.8million to £4.2million.

Rag’n’Bone Man is known as one of the hardest-working men in show business and has steadily built a massive fan base in the UK and across Europe.

He is known for charitable gestures including giving a wedding cake to a drug addiction clinic and helping local kids with music charity  AudioActive .
