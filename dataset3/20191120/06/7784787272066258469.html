This shrewd trade means Hawthorn avoids an AFL draft snag. It helps North Melbourne, too

20 Nov 2019, 06:44 by Ben Waterworth and Matt Balmer

Hawthorn has orchestrated a clever pre-draft trade that helps the club avoid a potentially sticky draft situation and ensures its gun father-son prospect will don a brown and gold jumper in 2020.

The AFL on Wednesday afternoon confirmed a third pick swap between clubs had been lodged in three days, with the Hawks and North Melbourne striking a deal.

Hawthorn gave up Pick 30 to the Kangaroos, but acquired the latter’s future second-round selection, as well as Picks 50 and 73.

The Hawks are understood to be keen to move their 2020 first-round selection into this year’s draft pool to complement their current Pick 11.

But until Wednesday, they’d been hamstrung due to tight AFL regulations, which prohibit clubs from trading out their future first-round pick unless they have second, third, fourth and fifth-round future selections at their disposal.

Also, clubs can’t trade their second to fifth-round selections unless they replenish their first-round selection.

But with Hawthorn now receiving the Roos’ 2020 second-round selection, it offsets the loss of their own future second-round pick, which was traded to Melbourne as part of the Sam Frost deal last month.

Hawthorn now has full a full book of second to fifth-round 2020 selections, meaning they can trade their future first.

It now means the Hawks could have two selections before a bid comes on father-son prospect Finn Maginness, who’s the son of dual premiership Hawk Scott Maginness. A bid on the Sandringham Dragons midfielder is tipped to come in the later stages of the first round or the early part of the second round.

The addition of the later draft selections, courtesy of the Kangaroos, means the Hawks should have no issues matching a rival club’s bid on Maginness.

The Hawks are now armed with over Picks 42, 50, 54, 63 and 73, which equates to 1009 points according to the AFL draft index. That means if a bid on Maginness comes at Pick 13 or later, the Hawks will be able to cover the value cost, thanks to the 20 per cent discount for matching father-son/academy prospects.

“We feel like this pick swap strengthens our position in both this year and next year’s draft,” Hawks football boss Graham Wright said.

“We now look forward to next week’s drafts.”

As for North Melbourne, it now holds three picks inside the top 30 of this year’s draft (Picks 26, 27 and 30), as well as another selection at Pick 47.

And with just spots left on North’s primary list for 2020, club recruiting manager Mark Finnigan said it was a no-brainer deal to strike with Hawthorn.

“By having two first-round selections next year already, we had the ability to trade out our future second pick, and strengthen our hand this year,” Finnigan said.

“With such an even draft this year, we’re confident we can bring in three high-quality players within the first 30 selections.”

The Hawks-Kangaroos trade is the third one that’s been lodged between the end of the exchange period and the national draft.

Adelaide and the Giants struck a deal that saw the latter move up to Pick 4 in the draft, which will likely allow the Giants to pick up another gun draftee before being forced to match a bid for their academy gun Tom Green, who’ll likely be called at around Pick 5 or 6. The Crows moved down the draft order two spots, but gained the Giants’ future first-round pick.

On Monday, Port gained an extra first-round selection in this year’s draft in a deal with Brisbane, who gained a future first-round selection from the Power.

Clubs can continue to trade picks until November 22.
