Brexit, Prince Andrew and Christmas presents - 5 things we learned from ITV Leaders Debate

Johnson said a vote for the Tories is a vote to "get Brexit done" as his party has a deal that is "ready to go" and backed by all 635 Conservative candidates at the election

20 Nov 2019, 06:43 by James Rodger, https://www.facebook.com/jamesrodgerjournalist

Prime Minister Boris Johnson and Labour leader Jeremy Corbyn went head-to-head in the first of three election debates tonight.

Both leaders received rounds of applause as they answered a series of questions from the audience on a range of subjects including Brexit, the NHS and the monarchy.

Here are five things we have learned from ITV's head-to-head debate between Boris Johnson and Jeremy Corbyn.

- Brexit reigns supreme in the election debate - despite differing opinions

Mr Johnson said a vote for the Tories is a vote to "get Brexit done" as his party has a deal that is "ready to go" and backed by all 635 Conservative candidates at the election.

Mr Corbyn said the Prime Minister's pledge was "nonsense" as he repeated his promise to negotiate a new deal and hold a second referendum to let the public decide which path to follow.

- Opposing politicians can unite

Host Julie Etchingham invited the two leaders to shake hands to make a gesture to improve the nature of debate in the country.

Mr Johnson stepped towards Mr Corbyn's podium and the pair shook hands - a rare moment of unity in the debate.

 

- They differ royally on royalty amid criticism of the Duke of York

With Andrew under continued criticism over his friendship with convicted sex offender Jeffrey Epstein, both candidates were asked whether the monarchy is fit for purpose.

Appearing to show restraint, Mr Corbyn replied: "Needs a bit of improvement."

Mr Johnson said: "The institution of the monarchy is beyond reproach."

- Both can be the butt of the joke

The PM - whose "do or die" Brexit deadline passed nearly three weeks ago - was met with laughter when he asked the audience to "look at what I've said I'm going to do as a politician and look at what I've delivered". More chuckles ensued when he said he believes the truth matters.

Mr Corbyn, who wants to hold a fresh referendum on a new Brexit deal but would not say whether he would campaign for it, tickled the audience when he said: "I've made the position clear."

 

- The leaders would gift literary Christmas presents

Asked what gifts they would leave each other under the Christmas tree, Mr Corbyn said he would give Mr Johnson a copy of A Christmas Carol by Charles Dickens to "understand how nasty Scrooge was".

The Prime Minister said he would give the Labour leader a copy of his Brexit deal. But, when Etchingham urged him to give a non-political present, he said he would gift some damson jam.
