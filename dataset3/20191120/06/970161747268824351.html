Dad performs citizen's arrest on on-the-run paedophile he saw on a bus

20 Nov 2019, 06:48

A paedophile who was released by mistake is back in prison after he was caught by a father on a bus.

Yesterday we ran a police appeal about Valerijs Gordijs who was wanted after being let out by accident while serving a sentence for sex offences against children.

He was spotted by Graham Lamb, 45, who performed a citizen’s arrest after recognising him thanks to that appeal then posed for a selfie with him.

Graham said: ‘He got on and I thought “t can’t be. It can’t be that much of a coincidence”, but he was right in front of me.

‘I was checking Facebook and it popped up saying convicted paedophile was out of prison, and he was right in front of me on the bus.

‘I kept looking at him and he kept looking at me. I brought up his picture on my phone and that was when I realised it was the same guy.’

Police had warned people not to approach Gordijs, 46, but Graham said he couldn’t just sit back and let him go free.

He said: ‘I said to the bus driver it was him and that she needed to stop the bus so I could ring the police.

‘I showed her the picture and he looked at the phone and tried getting off the bus so I went after him. He tried to avoid me so I said “stay there” and I rang the police.

‘I was ready to grab him and pin him down but I did not need to. He was only a small guy and would not have put up much of a fight.

‘As soon as I said “stay there” he had a worried look on his face. He had the same scar above his left eye. I knew it was him.

‘As soon as the bus pulled away there was a police car right next to it so I flagged it down.

‘He deserves to be back in prison and I am glad that he is off the streets and is no longer a danger to anyone else or any other children.’

The Ministry of Justice said: ‘Releases in error are very rare but we take them extremely seriously and are working closely with police to bring offenders back into custody quickly.’

A Humberside Police spokesman added: ‘Following our media appeal, Valerijs Gordijs was located on Hessle Road this morning. He was arrested and will be returned to prison.

‘We want to thank everyone who shared our appeal.’
