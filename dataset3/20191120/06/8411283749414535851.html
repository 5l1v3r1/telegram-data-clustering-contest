EgisTec expects slight revenue drop in 4Q19

20 Nov 2019, 06:19 by Cage Chao, Taipei; Jessie Shen, DIGITIMES

Taiwan-based Egis Technology (EgisTec) expects to post a slight sequential decrease in fourth-quarter revenues. Revenues will return to sequential growth in the first quarter of 2020, according to the fingerprint sensor specialist.

EgisTec expects its shipments to be lower-than-expected in the fourth quarter, due to customers' intention to work off their existing inventory at quarter-end to settle accounts. Customers' transition from old to new products will also drag down EgisTec's shipments during the fourth quarter, said the fingerprint sensor supplier.

EgisTec posted October revenues of NT$836 million (US$27.4 million), down 1.6% from the record high set in September. The company's cumulative 2019 revenues through October totaled NT$6.2 billion, rising 18.8% on year.

EgisTec's net profit surged 22.5% sequentially to NT$289 million in the third quarter of 2019, while revenues climbed to a record high of NT$2.075 billion. EPS for the quarter came to NT$4.23.

Samsung Electronics, and several China-based first- and second-tier smartphone vendors are reportedly among EgisTec's customers.
