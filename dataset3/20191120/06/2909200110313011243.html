Deal for 6 new aircrafts is greed before nationalism – Wereko Brobbey ‘Tarzan’

20 Nov 2019, 06:36 by MyNewsGH

Dr Charles Wereko-Brobbey (alias Tarzan) , a former Chief Executive Officer (CEO) of the Volta River Authority has criticized government for signing a deal to purchase six aircraft from Canada without a decision on a national airline, describing the decision has “putting private greed ahead of the public good”.

The government of Ghana on Tuesday signed a deal with De Havilland Aircraft of Canada at the on-going Dubai Air Show to buy six Dash8-400 aircraft to reestablish a national airline.

But Mr Wereko Brobbey argues that a decision on the reestablishment of the national airline should have been reached before the signing of the deal.

“Buying PLANES Ahead of DECISION On  NATIONAL AIRLINE Is NAKED Abuse Of POWER In Putting  PRIVATE GREED Ahead Of  D PUBLIC GOOD “, he wrote on social media after news of the signed was broadcast sighted by MyNewsGh.com

Ghana has had failed national carriers over the years, but has always had the dream to restart another one.

A collapsed Ghana Airways was replaced with a Ghana International Airline that eventually also went down.

The newly signed deal by the Aviation Minister Joseph Kofi Addah is expected to actualize the dream of having new national airline for the country.
