High school notebook: Broken Arrow's Andrew Raym selected for Under Armour All-America Game

20 Nov 2019, 06:05 by Cameron Jourdan

Broken Arrow star offensive lineman Andrew Raym was selected to participate in the 2020 Under Armour All-America Game, former Oklahoma star and Houston Texans wide receiver Kenny Stills announced Monday on Twitter.

Raym, an Oklahoma commit, will play for Team Charge. The 13th-annual All-America Game will take place Jan. 2, 2020, at Camping World Stadium in Orlando, Florida. He is the only prospect from Oklahoma playing in the contest.

Raym is No. 1 on The Oklahoman’s Super 30 rankings of the state’s top recruits of the 2020 class. His stellar play is being awarded, and he'll play alongside 99 of the nation's top college football recruits.

Broken Arrow (9-2) faces Owasso (11-0) at 7 p.m. Saturday in the Class 6A-II semifinals at Jenks High School. The schools have split the past two championships, with Owasso winning in 2017 while Broken Arrow took the crown last season.

Sasakwa's Thomas Rompf continuing magical season

Thomas Rompf is running wild on every field he steps on.

The junior running back at Sasakwa's big season continued Friday in a 70-60 win against Coyle in the first round of the Class C playoffs. Rompf rushed for 389 yards on 35 carries with five touchdowns while helping the Vikings secure their first playoff win in school history.

Coach Bruce Harrell said Rompf's season has been special. Sasakwa's nine wins are also the most in school history.

For the season, Rompf has rushed for 2,405 yards and 46 touchdowns. Sasawka will need another big performance come Friday when it travels to face three-time defending state champion Tipton at 7 p.m. Friday.

Ford runs away with Player of the Week honors

Southwest Covenant sophomore quarterback Bryson Ford won The Oklahoman’s Fans’ Choice Player of the Week poll, taking just more than 50 percent of the vote.

Ford, who received 313 votes, won the honor after accounting for seven touchdowns in his first career start at quarterback in the Patriots’ 50-18 win against Webbers Falls in the opening round of the Class C playoffs.

Moore running back Jayce Gardner finished second in the voting. Make sure to check oklahoman.com/sports/high-school for the poll Saturday following the second round of the playoffs.

Here are the results of the first-round poll:

Bryson Ford, Southwest Covenant, 313 votes (50.4 percent)

Jayce Gardner, Moore, 190 votes (30.6 percent)

Cade Stephenson, Kingfisher, 85 votes (13.69 percent)

Anthony Bland, Stillwater, 14 votes (2.25 percent)

Cavin Russell, Minco, 12 votes (1.93 percent)

Ayron Lawson, Perkins-Tryon, 7 votes (1.13 percent)

Total votes: 621
