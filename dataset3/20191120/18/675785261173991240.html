Oxford Circus incident: Man critical after 'falling onto tracks during medical episode'

Witnesses claimed the London station was overcrowded but the British Transport Police have said the rush hour incident is not being treated as suspicious as the man remains in hospital

20 Nov 2019, 18:06 by Danya Bazaraa, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/danya-bazaraa/

A man in a critical condition after being struck by a Tube train suffered a "medical episode" before he fell onto the tracks in front of horrified commuters, police believe.

The man is in a critical but stable condition and has been taken to a major trauma centre with serious leg injuries, emergency services have now said.

British Transport Police were at the scene along with paramedics treating the injured man at London's Oxford Circus Tube station which was evacuated and closed at rush hour.

Witnesses claimed the station was 'overcrowded' with one saying people were 'pushing and shoving each other'.

The incident happened at around 5.35pm, the busiest time for commuters.

Photos from the scene showed huge crowds building outside the popular central London station.

BTP said the incident is not being treated as suspicious.

Former England cricketer Ebony Rainford-Brent said she witnessed a man fall under a train right in front of her.

Documenting what she saw in a series of tweets, she wrote: "1. I cannot believe what I have just witnessed. Absolutely horric scenes at Oxford Circus and I just watched a man fall under the tube 2m in front of me. @TfL this is UNACCEPTABLE that in one of the most sophisticated cities in the world stations are allowed to get this RAMMED. (sic)

"2. People pushing & shoving each other & putting life at risk just trying to get home from work. @TfL something needs to be DONE URGENTLY to create safety. Sending prayers out to the family & friends of the young man, some horrible conversations to be had. Still shaking. So sad."

She added: "For clarity it was overcrowded and not managed at all."

Ms Rainford-Brent told the PA News Agency the platform was the "most packed" she had ever seen in her 35 years living in London.

A Londoner wrote on Twitter: "Was just leaving the platform at #oxfordcircus and suddenly people start screaming/ crying and running towards me."

One commuter warned people to avoid the station saying there were "lots of police around".

Another said he didn't see what happened but was on the platform at the time and heard calls for doctors.

He added: "Huge number of ambulances and emergency services now."

A British Transport Police spokesman said: "BTP officers were called to Oxford Circus today at 5.33pm following a report that a person had been struck by a train.

"It's believed a man suffered a medical episode and fell onto the tracks. He is in a critical condition in hospital."

They have since said he is in a critical but stable condition.

A London Ambulance Service spokesman added: "We sent a number of resources to the scene including an incident response officer and our hazardous area response team.

"We also dispatched a trauma team from London's Air Ambulance.

"Crews treated a man at the scene with leg injuries and took him to a major trauma centre by road."

A spokesman from TfL earlier told Mirror Online: "There's a casualty on the tracks on the Victoria line."

BTP London tweeted: "Please be aware, we’re currently at Oxford Circus station following a report of a casualty in the tracks. Paramedics are also at the scene and a man is being treated for injuries."

Victoria Line earlier said: "The station is currently closed while we deal with a customer incident. Tubes are currently not stopping."
