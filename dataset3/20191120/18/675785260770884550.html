5 things we noticed as Jose Mourinho takes Tottenham training for first time

Jose Mourinho held his first training session as Tottenham manager on Wednesday and was keen to make a good first impression

20 Nov 2019, 18:15 by Rich Jones, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/rich-jones/

Jose Mourinho began life as Tottenham Hotspur manager by leading his new charges in their first training session on Wednesday afternoon.

The Portuguese was announced as Mauricio Pochettino’s successor at 6:30am, following the Argentine’s sacking on Tuesday night.

Between the departure of Pochettino and the welcoming of the ex-Manchester United and Chelsea boss, Spurs chiefs made the decision to change their training schedule.

Spurs stars back from international duty had been due to get down to work at Hotspur Way on the morning, but the session was subsequently moved to the afternoon.

That decision was made so that Mourinho and members of his new-look training staff could immediately get down to work, ahead of their debut in Saturday’s London derby against West Ham .

Here’s five things we spotted from Mourinho’s first session at the club’s Enfield base…

1) Getting the captain on side

Harry Kane is clearly a senior figure in the Tottenham dressing room.

So it is no surprise Jose Mourinho is keen to get the captain on side.

Kane paid tribute to Mauricio Pochettino after his departure, but Mourinho was spotted in deep talks with Kane at the training ground.

Getting the England front-man on side will not only be key to dressing room morale, but also on-field success as they need their talisman firing.

2) Keeping a close eye

Jose Mourinho was seen getting right up close to the action as he runs the rule over his new players.

Mourinho says he has inherited a squad any manager would want due to its young talent.

But he still has plenty to learn about his new squad.

His first session saw him getting stuck in and keeping a very close eye on proceedings as his players were put through their paces.

3) Plenty of plans.

Mourinho walked onto the training ground with clipboard in hand - and he seems armed with ideas.

It is hoped the legendary manager will provide a fresh impetus on the squad which reached the Champions League final last year.

His clipboard already seemed thick and containing plenty of plans for his very first training session.

Did it contain the wisdom they need to get their Premier League campaign back on track?

I guess we'll soon find out...

4) Words of wisdom for Dele

When Jose Mourinho walked into Chelsea back in 2004, he told Frank Lampard: “You are the best player in the world.”

You have to wonder whether he has said something similar to Dele Alli upon their first meeting today.

It’s been a difficult season for Alli so far, in and out of the side, having largely sacrificed himself in a deeper midfield role during 2018-19.

Last year he scored just seven times, well down on the 14 of 2017-18 and the 22 of 2016-17.

So the question now is how does Mourinho see him?

And can he make him into his new Lampard?

5) Building relationships

Mourinho was seen giving his players a drinks break to stay hydrated during their training session.

But he was keen to stay close by and take the chance for a casual chat with his players.

The Portuguese boss knows he will need to build morale quickly if he's to make an instant impact.

It's easy to see why he wants to make a positive first impression.
