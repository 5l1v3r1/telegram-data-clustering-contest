Coach O Has Update On When Veteran LB Michael Divinity Could Return To Team

20 Nov 2019, 18:56 by Chris Rosvoglou

Prior to LSU’s showdown with Alabama, senior linebacker Michael Divinity left the team due to personal reasons. He’s not set to return in the regular season, but Ed Orgeron did reveal the earliest possible date for his return.

The loss of Divinity is a pretty significant blow for LSU’s defense, which has struggled in back-to-back games without him.

Divinity had 22 tackles and three sacks this season for the Tigers. His best performances came against marquee opponents, such as Auburn and Texas.

LSU is currently on track to play in the SEC Championship. Although the defense would benefit from having Divinity on the field for that game, Coach O made it clear that won’t happen.

However, Orgeron said it’s possible that Divinity would be eligible to play in the national championship game

From The Advocate:

“Maybe he will be eligible for that,” Orgeron said on the Southeastern Conference coach’s teleconference. “Up until then, Mike will not be eligible to play.”

Orgeron didn’t fully commit to playing Divinity in a potential national title game, but he’ll cross that bridge if he gets there.

At the very least, the LSU Tigers won’t have Divinity for two regular season games against Arkansas and Texas A&M. He could also miss a conference championship game against Georgia and College Football Playoff semifinal.
