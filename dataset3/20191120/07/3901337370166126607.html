How Pirates Plan For League Restart...    

20 Nov 2019, 07:44 by Soccer Laduma

Orlando Pirates will want to get their Absa Premiership campaign back on track with a good result against Polokwane City. 

The Buccaneers will host Rise and Shine at the Orlando Stadium next Tuesday in their first match after the FIFA international break.

In preparing for the match, Bucs played against Highlands Park on Saturday in one of their friendly matches during the break.

Owen da Gama’s side emerged as 2-0 winners at the Rand Stadium, with both goals scored by Rodney Ramagalela.

Rulani Mokwena’s side went into the break on the back of a disappointing run of only one win in their last six matches in the league.

Bucs are 9th on the log with three wins from 11 matches, having collected 13 points out of a possible 33.   

Meanwhile, Pirates’ reserves won 1-0 against Kaizer Chiefs in the MultiChoice Diski Challenge Soweto Derby in Dobsonville on the same day.
