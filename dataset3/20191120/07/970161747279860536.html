Spurs have been planning Mourinho appointment 'for some time now,' says McCoist

20 Nov 2019, 07:46

Tottenham will have been planning to hire Jose Mourinho as manager ‘for some time now’ believes former Scotland striker Ally McCoist.

Mourinho confirmed the appointment of the former Manchester United and Chelsea manager on Wednesday morning, after Mauricio Pochettino was sacked on Tuesday night.

The rapid turnaround makes McCoist believe that Mourinho had been waiting in the wings for a while before taking over at the Tottenham Hotspur Stadium.

‘It’s one of these that you’re half of expecting it but still reasonably shocked and surprised when it happens,’ McCoist told talkSPORT.

‘Obviously they’ve had this plan for some time now, you don’t just sack somebody and appoint somebody within 24 hours if you’ve not got a plan.’

The Rangers legend is a big fan of the Portuguese manager but noted that he would bring a completely different approach to his predecessor at Spurs, which could ruffle feathers in north London.

‘I’m interested in the contrasting styles of both managers,’ McCoist continued. ‘There’s one thing with Mourinho, he’s an absolute winner, I love him.

‘It’ll be interesting to see how the players handle it because, make no mistake about it, they are completely different characters, Pochettino and Mourinho.

‘One thing Spurs supporters will know they are getting is a man with a ruthless streak that wins things, he did it at United as well.

‘He went on record and said one of his biggest achievements was at United, finishing second, which raised a few eyebrows, but you look back now and it makes a lot of sense.’

TalkSPORT host Alan Brazil noted believes that Mourinho may well have a more settled time at Spurs than at Manchester United, due to already having a home in London and loving the city.

‘He loves London, by the way,’ said Brazil. ‘Nothing wrong with Manchester, but he was away from his family, London is his home.’
