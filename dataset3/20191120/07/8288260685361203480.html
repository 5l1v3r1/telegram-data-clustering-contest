'Philosophy' - Inside track on how West Brom are hoping to maintain promotion dream

Latest West Bromwich Albion news | The Baggies are on course to secure promotion to the Premier League, here's a behind-the-scenes look at how they're aiming to do it

20 Nov 2019, 07:55 by Mark Wakefield

West Bromwich Albion are enjoying a good start to the season in the Sky Bet Championship.

The Baggies are currently top of the league table as we approach the end of the November international break.

Slaven Bilic’s side will be hoping that they can maintain their bright start to the campaign and secure promotion to the Premier League.

One member of staff at The Hawthorns who will be vital to Albion mounting a promotion push is first-team fitness coach Nick Davies.

 

Davies, who joined Albion’s coaching staff in 2018, is responsible for making sure the players are available for selection game in, game out.

The 40-year-old recently spoke about how he keeps the Albion players in check, and has revealed the process behind making sure Bilic has as many players fit and ready to play.

“All the players have got an app on their phone,” Davies told the Express & Star.

He added: “They enter information on how well they have slept, if they feel any fatigue and if they have any soreness.

“What we are trying to do, before they have walked into the building, is build up a picture to see if anyone has any problems. It’s real time information for us.

“If a player is struggling to sleep for example, we will sit down with them and work out why. Maybe we can help with nutrition or simple guidelines like bed time routines.

“If someone says they have got some muscle tightness, it doesn’t mean they aren’t going to train but it does mean they will have extra interventions before training. Maybe a massage or a specialised stretching routine.

“All these things are to ensure they can train rather than give them reasons not to train – that has always been my philosophy.

“We want to build robust players that can withstand the rigours of Championship football. You don’t get that by wrapping people up in cotton wool.”
