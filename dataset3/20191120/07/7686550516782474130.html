Wayne Pivac holds talks with Liam Williams about returning to Wales from crisis club Saracens

Warren Gatland's successor wants the full-back to join a Welsh region and says new Wales recruit Willis Halaholo has the dancing feet to evade a defender in a telephone box

20 Nov 2019, 07:04 by Andy Howell

Wayne Pivac has had talks with Liam Williams about returning to Wales full-time from English crisis club Saracens.

The 28-year-old full-back’s contract with the Heineken Champions Cup and controversial Gallagher Premiership winners is up at the end of the season.

Saracens are facing a battle to avoid relegation from the Premiership after being docked 35 points and fined over £5m for breaches of the competition’s salary cap regulations.

And last month, WalesOnline exclusively revealed Williams could now be coming home.

Williams has reportedly visited his former region Scarlets, while his signature is also being pursued by former European club champions Toulon, Ospreys and others.

Pivac revealed: “I met with Liam pre the World Cup and there have been discussions.

“Liam will make his decision no doubt very shortly and it’s one that, from a selfish Welsh point of view, I’d love to see him back here in Wales.

“That’s a decision ultimately he will make given that he can play both inside and outside the country, given the number of Test matches he’s played.”

The Kiwi disclosed Alun Wyn Jones, who misses Pivac’s debut Wales match as coach against the Barbarians through a groin injury, will remain captain.

“At the moment the leadership of Alun Wyn is very important to us. I’ve had several conversations with Alun Wyn pre and post World Cup and he’s a big part of our plans going forward at this stage, hence why he was re-contracted,” he said.

Pivac revealed he telephoned centre Scott Williams to explain his omission from the squad and the inclusion of Welsh-qualified New Zealander Willis Halaholo for the uncapped clash with the Barbarians.

“As I mentioned to Scott I know a lot about him. I coached him for four years and he’s been a very, very good player for me in the past.

“We’ve won a championship together. He’s played some fantastic rugby. Willis we don’t know as much about in the northern hemisphere.

“From what I’ve seen in the games he’s played against the Scarlets sides I’ve coached he can beat a guy in a phone box.

“He’s got a skill-set not many people have, but we want to see the other side of his game up close and personal in our camp.

“It’s not a Test cap game, so it’s a great opportunity to get some questions answered hence some of the selections.

“We’ve done them with a view of let’s learn as much as we can about the players that will be available for the Six Nations.”
