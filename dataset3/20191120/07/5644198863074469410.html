US Senate unanimously passes Hong Kong rights Bill

20 Nov 2019, 07:17

WASHINGTON: The US Senate, in a unanimous vote, passed legislation on Tuesday (Nov 19) aimed at protecting human rights in Hong Kong amid China's crackdown on a protest movement that has gripped the vital financial centre for months.

Following the voice vote by senators, the "Hong Kong Human Rights and Democracy Act" now goes to the House of Representatives, which earlier approved its own version of the measure.

The two chambers will have to work out their differences before any legislation can be sent to President Donald Trump for his consideration.

"The people of Hong Kong see what's coming - they see the steady effort to erode the autonomy and their freedoms," Republican Senator Marco Rubio said at the start of the brief Senate debate, accusing Beijing of being behind the "violence and repression" in the Asian financial hub.
