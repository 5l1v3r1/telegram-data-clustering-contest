JNU protest: Shiv Sena takes students' side after azadi from BJP

A senior Shiv Sena leader says the protests by JNU students are valid as long as they're peaceful. Surprised? You have some catching up to do. Read on.

20 Nov 2019, 07:10

HIGHLIGHTS

  • Sena's Priyanka Chaturvedi slams police action against students
  • Says fairness of protesting fee hike debatable
  • Shiv Sena, BJP fell out after recent Maharashtra state election

What does the Shiv Sena, until recently a coalition partner in the Modi government, make of the ongoing protests at Jawaharlal Nehru University?

One of its highest-ranking members, deputy leader Priyanka Chaturvedi, thinks they're valid as long as they're peaceful.

What about the fact that they're trying get a hostel fee hike rolled back?

In a tweet she posted on Wednesday, Chaturvedi said the fairness of the motive was "debatable", but "for the Delhi police to resort to violent lathi charge to stop the protests is sign of a system that is unable to handle dissent".

Ouch.
