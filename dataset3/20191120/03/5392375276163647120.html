A few years ago when the manufacturers began making full-screen smartphones and replacing metal and plastic back panels with glass and ceramics, many were scared of the repair price. Not accidentally, if you have to repair such a handset, it would cost you a fortune. Now, when we are entering the era of folding screen smartphones, it’s logical to see a familiar picture. Today, Huawei officially announced the repair price of Huawei Mate X (protection or man-made damage).

It turns out the company’s service centers will charge from you 278 yuan ($40) for battery replacement, 7,080 yuan ($1,000) for screen replacement/repair, 3,579 yuan ($509) for motherboard replacement/repair, 361 yuan ($51) for battery cover replacement/repair, and 698 yuan ($99) for rear camera replacement/repair.

If you think these maintenance prices are too high, just recall its original price of 16,999 yuan ($2,403). And if you think this phone is overpriced, just get acquainted with its selling points.

As said, the Huawei Mate X uses a flexible folding screen. The latter adopts an eagle-wing folding technology that can reach up to 8 inches when unfolded. But if you don’t need a large screen, you can fold it, turning the phone into a 6.6-inch handset. So the single-hand operations will be realized more easily. And the other screen size is 6.38 inches. The thickness of the body is only 5.4mm.

As for the core configuration, the Huawei Mate X is equipped with the company’s first 7nm 5G multimode chip Barong 5000 + Kirin 980. The subsequent batches have been upgraded to Kirin 990 5G. The phone also supports a 55W super-fast charge. Thus, it can charge to 85% in 30 minutes. And take into account that it is packed with a 4500mAh battery.
