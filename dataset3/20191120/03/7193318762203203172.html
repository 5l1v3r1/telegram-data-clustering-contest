Teen injures himself in jump from jet bridge at Oklahoma City airport while trying to escape sheriff's deputies

20 Nov 2019, 03:13 by Adam Kemp

Law enforcement said a teenager is in the hospital with two broken legs after leaping from a jet bridge at Will Rogers World Airport while trying elude police.

The 16-year-old boy, who police said won't be identified due to him being a minor, was on a United Airlines flight from Houston to Oklahoma City on Monday when he made the attempt to escape sheriff's deputies who were there to arrest him.

The boy was wanted on a second-degree burglary charge out of Cleveland County.

Officials said he made his way off the plane and onto the tarmac after his flight landed in Oklahoma City, trying to evade sheriff deputies. Deputies began chasing the teen who eventually climbed on the jet bridge of another terminal where he then leaped to another jet bridge of the adjacent terminal.

There, the boy jumped to the ground and injured both legs in the fall. The teen was then taken to a hospital for treatment.
