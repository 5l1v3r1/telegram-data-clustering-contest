Breaking: Jose Mourinho's Tottenham backroom staff confirmed

The Portuguese has added two names to his backroom team at Tottenham Hotspur

20 Nov 2019, 14:24 by Lee Wilmot

Jose Mourinho has arrived at Tottenham to replace Mauricio Pochettino.

But one of the next questions has been who will join him and make up his backroom staff at Tottenham Hotspur Stadium.

And that has been confirmed, or at least two members have, by French club Lille.

Joao Sacramento and Nuno Santos, both members of Lille's technical staff, have joined Mourinho in N17 following talks between the two clubs.

Sacramento will become Mourinho's assistant head coach.

Sacramento is a Portuguese coach who has been assistant manager to Lille boss Christophe Galtier for almost three years. In his previous role, he served as a technical assistant to Leonardo Jardim and Claudio Ranieri at Monaco, where he became the manager of opposition analysis for the first team.

Before those two jobs in professional first team football, Sacramento spent time in Wales.

He worked in the scouting department and as an Under-15s assistant coach for Cardiff City, as well as an assistant coach at the University of Glamorgan and as a performance analyst for the Football Association of Wales, working under Gary Speed and Chris Coleman.

The other new recruits at Spurs will be fitness trainer Carlos Lalin, Giovanni Cerra who will join the analysis team and scout Ricardo Formoshino.
