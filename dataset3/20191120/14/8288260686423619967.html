Gladiators star Ace 'went from earning £2,000 an hour to £20' after 'crashing into the real world'

Ace - real name Warren Furman - spoke on This Morning on Wednesday, November 20

20 Nov 2019, 14:54 by James Rodger, https://www.facebook.com/jamesrodgerjournalist

Ace from Gladiators went from earning £2,000 an hour to £20 after "crashing down to reality".

The star of the show, which was a household favourite in the 1990s and filmed right here in Birmingham, opened up today.

Ace - real name Warren Furman - spoke on This Morning on Wednesday, November 20.

The star of the hit show spoke about his ‘crash down into the real world’ as he adjusted to life away from the hit Saturday night gameshow.

The programme ran from 1992 until 2000 and racked up a total of eight series.

Ace joined in series five until the end and enjoyed a jet-set lifestyle full of sports cars, women and first class travel.

He was also engaged to Katie Price in the late 90s.

Today, Warren said: "When you've had something like that and it's taken away from you, and you're not earning a living out of it anymore, and all people want to know is daft stuff.

"Well it seemed daft because now I wasn't earning any money. I came crashing down into the real world. I could earn £2,000 an hour, now I'm lucky if I could get £20 an hour.

 

"And then people are coming over like, 'What's Ulrika like? What's Wolf like?' and in the end I'm like, 'I'm not actually public property any more, I'm just trying to make a living so I don't really want to talk about it anymore.'

"I know that sounds conceited, but I felt like I was living in the past."
