Paul McCartney to headline Glastonbury festival

20 Nov 2019, 14:31

London - Paul McCartney will take star billing at next year's Glastonbury when the legendary British music festival marks its 50th anniversary, organisers said.

Glastonbury co-organiser Emily Eavis said the former Beatle would headline the Pyramid Stage on 27 June - the traditional highlight of the event in Somerset, south-west England.

"Having Paul McCartney coming back to headline the Pyramid next year is an absolute dream come true," she said in a statement on the festival website on Monday.

"There really was no one that we wanted more for the 50th anniversary." McCartney previously headlined Glastonbury in 2004. This year's headliner was grime star Stormzy.
