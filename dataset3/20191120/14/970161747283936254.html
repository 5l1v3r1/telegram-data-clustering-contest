Stewardess 'was almost seven times over alcohol limit' at Heathrow

20 Nov 2019, 14:43

A cabin crew member has been accused of being nearly seven times the alcohol limit while working at Heathrow Airport.

Lemara Thompson, 27, allegedly had 135mg of alcohol per 100ml of blood in her body while on duty for US-based Delta Airlines on June 16 this year.

She faces one charge of performing an aviation function with a blood alcohol level over the prescribed limit.

The limit is currently 20mg of alcohol per 100ml of blood, according to Civil Aviation Authority rules and the Railways and Transport Safety Act 2003.

Thompson, from Brooklyn, New York, did not appear at Uxbridge Magistrates’ Court for a hearing today but responded to the court via post.

Chair Magistrate Alison White adjourned the case for two weeks to give Thompson notice to travel to court or set up a video link.
