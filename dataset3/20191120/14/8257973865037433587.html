Ariana Grande, Lizzo, Billie Eilish top Grammy nominations

20 Nov 2019, 14:06 by Wade Sheridan

Nov. 20 (UPI) -- The nominees for the 62nd annual Grammy nominations were announced on Wednesday with Ariana Grande, Lizzo and Billie Eilish leading the field.

Record of the Year will be decided between "7 Rings" by Grande, "Truth Hurts" by Lizzo, "Bad Guy" by Eilish, "Hey Ma" by Bon Iver, "Hard Place" by H.E.R., "Talk" by Khalid, "Old Town Road" by Lil Nas X featuring Billy Ray Cyrus and "Sunflower" by Post Malone and Swae Lee.

Grande's Thank U, Next, Lizzo's Cuz I Love You, Eilish's When We All Fall Asleep, Where Do We Go?, Lana Del Rey's Norman F***ing Rockwell!, H.E.R.'s I Used To Know Her, Lil Nas X's 7, Vampire Weekend's Father of the Bride and Iver's I,I are nominated for Album of the Year.

"Always Remember Us This Way" performed by Lady Gaga, "Bad Guy" performed by Eilish, "Bring My Flowers Now" performed by Tanya Tucker, "Hard Place" performed by H.E.R., "Lover" performed by Taylor Swift, "Norman F***ing Rockwell" performed by Del Rey, "Someone You Loved" performed by Lewis Capaldi and "Truth Hurts" performed by Lizzo are up for Song of the Year.

Eilish, Lizzo, Lil Nas X, Black Pumas, Maggie Rogers, Rosalia, Tank and the Bangas and Yola are nominated for Best New Artist.

The Grammys will be awarded Jan. 26 at the Staples Center in Los Angeles. The event will be aired live on CBS at 8 p.m. EDT.

Alicia Keys is returning to host for the second year in a row.

Kacey Musgraves dominated last year's awards, winning Album of the Year and Best Country Album for Golden Hour and Best Country Solo Performance for "Butterflies." Childish Gambino won Record and Song of the Year for "This is America."
