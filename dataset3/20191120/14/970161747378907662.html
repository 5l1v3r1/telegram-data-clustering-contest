Man Utd handed huge injury boost by six injured stars including Shaw & Bailly

20 Nov 2019, 14:00

Manchester United received a host of injury boosts on Wednesday as Eric Bailly, Luke Shaw, Axel Tuanzebe, Nemanja Matic, Diogo Dalot and Timothy Fosu-Mensah all took part in training.

Ole Gunnar Solskjaer took charge of training at Carrington ahead of United’s Premier League clash with Sheffield United on Sunday.

The United boss welcomed back some of his players on international duty and several first-team stars who have been struggling with injuries.

The club confirmed defensive trio Bailly, Fosu-Mensah and Dalot were back in training, but conducted their own session away from the main group.

Bailly and Fosu-Mensah are yet to play this season while Dalot has not featured for United since October.

While that defensive trio continued their recovery, Matic, Shaw and Tuanzebe were able to take part in the main session.

Matic has barely played this season as he has struggled to break into Solskjaer’s team, while Shaw has been nursing a hamstring issue and Tuanzebe a hip problem.

While Matic has been continually overlooked by Solskjaer, he may get a recall due to an injury to Scott McTominay.

The Scotsman did not train with the group and was forced to pull out of international duty this month due to an ankle injury.

Ashley Young also trained and he will be available for selection against Sheffield following his suspension for United’s previous game.

United are nine points off fourth place, with Sheffield flying high in fifth place, two spots above the Red Devils.
