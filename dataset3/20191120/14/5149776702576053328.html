Nottingham students reveal what will sway their vote in the general election

"I need to look into it all more before I actually vote, I’ll admit that.”

20 Nov 2019, 14:28 by Gurjeet Nanrah

With more than 60,000 students in the Nottingham area in full-time education, this demographic could have a big say locally in the upcoming general election.

That is if they choose to register to vote at their term-time addresses, as students are able to register back at their permanent address and wherever they are studying for their degree - it is a criminal offence to vote in more than one place.

Nottinghamshire Live spoke to some students in the city to find out more about where they plan to vote on December 12 and the big issues swaying their decision.

Callum Berkinshaw, a 19-year-old international business student at Nottingham Trent University (NTU) from Coventry, said: “A lot of posters and signs are around campus telling us we should now register to vote.

“I’m registered back home. I know I’ll be back home that close to Christmas.

“Brexit is a big issue and the NHS. Not everyone is that aware of how certain things could impact them.”

Another business student at NTU, 19-year-old Zoe Schonn, who is in her second year, added: “I’m registered to vote here and back home in Coventry.

"I think it’s a good thing that we can register at both because we break up around then so a lot of students will go home. I knew that I’m only allowed to vote in one place though.

“It’s mostly Brexit influencing my vote too. My mum is from Germany so it’s a big problem for her."

Another student admitted she has 'not registered yet', but knows she has the option of voting here or back home in Surrey.

Sarosh Patel, a 19-year-old economics student at the University of Nottingham, added: “For me student finance is a big issue and the need for greater assurances about it all.

"I didn't know you could only vote in one of the places you've registered though so I'll probably just register back home."

Emma Cawley, a 19-year-old Criminology student at NTU from Hertfordshire, said: “I have registered back home. I didn’t know you could register in both places to be honest.

“The environment is a big issue. It’s our future it will impact in that sense and a lot of the older people won’t be around to see the impact of climate change."

Rosie Soroka, a BA History first year student at NTU from Leeds added: “I’m registered in Nottingham, but agree it’s a good thing to be able to register in both places because students are more likely to move at short notice.

"It’s sad though because a lot of people at university will just not vote.

“More money needs to be put into schools too and the scare of the NHS being privatised needs to be stopped because people don't want that.”

Ed Holderness, 20, and a civil engineering student at NTU from near Watford, spoke honestly and said: "I need to look into it all more before I actually vote, I’ll admit that.”

Bradley Fox, President of Nottingham Trent Students' Union, said: "In the lead up to the general election, we have been running a high-visibility campaign on campus encouraging students to register to vote at their home and term-time addresses.

"We have also collaborated with the University of Nottingham Students' Union to create a Nottingham Student Manifesto, which we hope will put student-related issues on the agenda of election candidates."

A spokesperson for the University of Nottingham's Students' Union said: "We believe that it is of great importance that students exercise their democratic rights. We are using our physical, social, and online channels to raise awareness of the voter registration deadline.

"The choice is down to students as to where they vote; our message reminds students that they can register at both their home and term time addresses but that they can only vote from one of them."
