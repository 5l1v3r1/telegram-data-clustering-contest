Woman dies after incident at Tesco store

The circumstances surrounding the incident are not believed to be suspicious

20 Nov 2019, 14:57 by Jamie Hawkins

A woman in her 60s has died following an incident at a Tesco store in Devon.

Emergency services were called to the Honiton branch on Wednesday following a concern of welfare of a woman.

Police, the air ambulance and land ambulances all attended the Battishorne Way store, which remains closed to the public.

She was declared dead at the scene. The death is not believed to be suspicious.

A police spokesperson said: "Police were notified at around 12pm to a Tesco Store on Battishorne Way in Honiton following a concern of welfare of a woman.

"The Air Ambulance were in attendance.

"A woman in her 60s was confirmed deceased at the scene.

"The circumstances surrounding the incident are not believed to be suspicious and a file will be prepared for the coroner."

A South Western Ambulance Service spokesperson added: "We were called at around 11.05am about a medical incident on Battishorne Way, Honiton. We sent various resources to attend the including land ambulance crews and an air ambulance crew."

Duncan Sheridan-Shaw, community manager at Tesco Honiton, said: “There is a customer who has been taken very poorly and we are currently dealing with the emergency services.

“For the purposes of the customer and their family, we have taken the decision to close the store until further notice.”

The store has reportedly been shut since 11am, with staff turning customers away.
