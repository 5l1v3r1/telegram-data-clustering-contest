Two seen running from large fire which badly damaged detached Burry Port house

Police are appealing for witnesses

20 Nov 2019, 14:00 by Robert Harries

Police are appealing for witnesses after two people were seen running from a large fire which totally destroyed a three-storey property in Carmarthenshire .

The blaze broke out in the early hours of Tuesday morning at an empty house in the Graig area of Burry Port .

Fire crews from Llanelli, Kidwelly and Tumble tackled the blaze and confirmed that the house - consisting of two floors and a basement - was in an abandoned state.

The fire was so serious investigators were unable to enter the detached house at all on Tuesday due to the temperature inside making conditions unsafe.

Police, who were at the scene on Wednesday, are not treating the incident as arson, but are asking for anyone who saw “people acting suspiciously” to come forward.

A spokeswoman for Dyfed-Powys Police said: “We are investigating a fire at an abandoned property in Burry Port.

“A call was made at around 1.20am today (Tuesday, November 19) reporting that a detached house in Graig was well alight.

“The house is not lived in, and has sustained substantial fire damage.

“There were no witnesses to indicate who has started the fire, but two people were seen running away from the scene.”

The fire is being investigated by both the police and the fire service.

Anyone with information about the incident is asked to contact Dyfed-Powys Police by calling 101, or by reporting it online .
