Strictly's Tess Daly eats her own split ends, hates germs and doesn't work under real name

Strictly Come Dancing host Tess Daly, who is married to Vernon Kay, has a pretty eccentric outlook on life

20 Nov 2019, 14:56 by Irishmirror.ie

Tess Daly may come across as a glittering, cool-as-a-cucumber host on Strictly Come Dancing , but she's secretly got a whole range of eccentric dislikes.

The 50-year-old TV presenter, who co-hosts the hit BBC show alongside Claudia Winkleman, rarely gives interviews, but has admitted in the past that Tess isn't her real name.

The blonde bombshell was actually born Helen Elizabeth Daly, but goes by a diminutive of her middle name for work.

She's never explained why her stage name is different from her real one, but started out her modelling career as a 17-year-old as 'Tess'.

Tess is married to Vernon Kay and has two daughters - Phoebe Elizabeth and Amber Isabella - with him.

The pair went through a rocky patch in 2010 when Vernon was caught sexting glamour model Rhian Sugden and exchanging inappropriate messages with her behind Tess's back.

He was forced to issue a grovelling apology live on Radio 1, telling listeners that he'd made "some stupid decisions".

"I have disappointed and I have let down a lot of people. I am very sorry," he said.

"Tess is extremely upset. Hopefully I can clear the slate and start again."

While Tess has never explicitly spoken about the time her marriage nearly imploded, she did hint in December 2018 that it was their two daughters who kept them going.

"There are so many highs with children to celebrate. It’s the good times that keep you together," she told the Daily Star.

The couple married in 2003 and celebrated their 16th wedding anniversary earlier this year.

They live with their children in a plush Buckinghamshire mansion - but Tess retains a fear of germs that regularly disrupts daily life.

"I have an extreme germ phobia," she told the Guardian in 2016.

"Toilet door handles, the ice-cream seller holding your cone with his bare hands, touching raw meat or chicken," she told the Guardian.

Tess opened up about her most unappealing habit, admitting she can't stop eating her own hair.

"I bite off my split ends. There’s probably a fur ball in my stomach," she confessed.

Tess also explained why she no longer drives black car, out of a mix of superstition and practicality.

"[The closest I've come to death was] on a motorway three years ago," she revealed.

"An HGV hadn't seen me and pulled out. My father-in-law's a truck driver and he said black cars are not very visible. I don't drive them any more."
