Pro Golfer Gives Fan With Down Syndrome A Hug After Ill-Timed Yelp Botched His Putt And Cost Him A Win

20 Nov 2019, 16:43 by Brent Furdyk

A tense moment that could have gone bad turned unexpectedly sweet, proving that kindness goes a long way.

As The Golf Channel reports, pro golfer Brandon Matthews was competing at the VISA Open de Argentina in Buenos Aires on Sunday and was on the third playoff hole about to make a crucial putt that could win him the tournament.

Just as he was about to make his putt, recounts the 25-year-old golfer, he became distracted by a noise.

“I got over the putt, took the putter back and heard kind of a yelp or a scream,” he said. “I kind of flinched on the putt and immediately knew I missed it.”

Matthews admits he was understandably upset, thinking that someone deliberately tried to sabotage his putt.

“I thought someone had done it intentionally. I was frustrated,” he adds. “Really, I was in shock that that just happened.”

As it turned out, the noise wasn’t intentional at all, but the sound of excitement from an enthusiastic golf fan with Down syndrome.

When Matthews learned what took place, he asked to meet the fan — so he could give him a hug and let him know it was okay.

“There’s nothing you can do about that. I mean, the guy was out there, he was enjoying it,” Matthews said in video posted on the PGA Instagram page.

“I feel for the guy, ‘cause I’m sure he felt terrible,” Matthews added. “That’s why I went and gave him a glove and a ball afterwards and gave him a hug. It’s just one of those things that happens.”
