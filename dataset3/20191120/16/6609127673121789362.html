'POOR WEE SOUL'

Glasgow kitten left blinded by horror eye wounds after being neglected and sold online at just four weeks old

20 Nov 2019, 16:33 by Chelsea Rocks

A TINY kitten has been left blind after being neglected by its owner and sold online at just four weeks-old.

The cat, named Cookie, was sold to a buyer who was unaware of the animal's critical eye condition.

The malnourished moggy has now been left with horror wounds and unable to see.

Cookie was brought to the centre last Thursday to be treated immediately, after the new owner was told Cookie's condition.

The owner is believed to have bought him for £150, exchanging the money cash-in-hand and receiving Cookie in a cat carrier then driving home before noticing the severity of the situation.

The poorly pet then underwent two weeks of vet checks and treatment, with the new owner footing the £200 vet bills, before being handed over to the Cat Adoption Centre who are now nursing him back to health.

When he arrived at the centre he weighed as little as a 2-3 week old kitten, though vets recon he was closer to six weeks old.

This means the frail kitten was detached from his mother well before he should have been, as cats tend to wean six weeks after birth.

He has also missed out on vital skill development like eating solids, self-grooming and using a litter tray.

One staff member took to Facebook to highlight what had happened to their "poor wee soul".

They said: "What I can say about Cookie is that he is a wee fighter & he is acting like any other kitten.

"With help, he is eating well, finding his way to his litter tray & using it, playing with toys we give him, grooming himself & purring like a wee machine."

However it is not known whether the kitty will make a full recovery and it is uncertain when he will be re-homed due to the string of health issues he has to overcome.

Shaming the original owner for their actions, the Facebook post continued: "It’s utterly devastating to think that human neglect could mean he never sees the world around him but we will do absolutely everything we can for him & we are keeping everything crossed that his sight can be saved."

The adoption centre have now vowed to keep their followers updated on his recovery and hope to restore his sight.

Many of the Centre's Facebook fans weighed in on the case, one disgraced follower wrote: "This is beyond heartbreaking. I'm glad this wee soul was picked up as he is now in the safest of hands. I hope he makes a full recovery and is able to live a happy life in a loving home. Thank goodness for cats protection and all those kind people who look after these poor souls x"

While another was reduced to tears, writing: "I am in tears here. Greeder breeders...all I want to do is swear ...heartbroken that humans can actually treat animals with such disrespect. May they rot in hell. I hope they get caught and locked up. Well done to everyone involved with helping this wee soul you are all heaven sent xx".
