Teacher adopts student with Down's syndrome after his mother died of cancer

20 Nov 2019, 16:03

A teacher adopted one of her own students after the boy’s mother died of cancer.

Kerry Bremmer decided to take care of Jake Manning, who has Down syndrome, after his single mom Jean succumbed to terminal breast cancer on last Wednesday.

Bremmer, from Ayer in Massachusetts, said she ‘fell in love with (Jake) instantly’ when they first met four years ago.

She told WCVB: ‘As she got sicker that year, I just thought: “What is she going to do?”‘

Bremmer discussed Jake’s predicament with their husband Dave – then made his ailing mother her incredible offer.

Recalling the conversation, Bremmer said: ‘If you need a backup plan for Jake, then our family is happy to make him part of our family.

‘She said: “I’ll sleep better tonight than I have in a long time.”‘

Jake then spent time with Kerry, Dave and their three children Kaitlyn, Kristen and Jonathan, who fell in love with the youngster’s happy and joyful nature.

Dave said: ‘When I first met him when he was in Kerry’s class, it was fun.

‘But when we first decided to do this and I met him again, he said: “You’re the dad? You’re Dave the dad?”

‘That was it for a very long time. I was Dave the dad.’

Jean Manning died last Wednesday after taking a nap following a chemotherapy treatment.

Jake said: ‘My mom went to heaven. She’s always in my heart.’

And while heartbroken by Jean’s loss, Bremmer is delighted to have Jake as part of her family, saying: ‘We shared our boy and she will live on here in this house.’

A GoFundMe page has been set up to help support Bremmer and her newly-extended family.
