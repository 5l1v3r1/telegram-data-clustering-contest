Prince Andrew reportedly considering a second interview to 'put things right'

A source claimed he wants another chat

20 Nov 2019, 16:02 by Richard Bell

Prince Andrew is reportedly considering a second interview to set the record straight over his controversial friendship with billionaire paedophile Jeffrey Epstein.

The Duke of York, 59, said in an interview with Newsnight he "kicks himself" every day for remaining friends with Epstein after his conviction.

But the Queen's second son received a huge backlash over the conversation, with Good Morning Britain's Piers Morgan branding it "desperate" and "toe curling" to watch.

Now, Vanity Fair has reported that the royal is looking to do a second interview as he wants to "put right" things he failed to mention in the first.

According to the publication, an insider said: "There are rumors swirling that Andrew wants to do another interview. Andrew hasn't drawn a line under it.

"He wants the chance to put right the things he didn't say. Newsnight gave him the chance to show some remorse. He was asked if there was anything else he wanted to say. It was an open goal, he didn't grasp it, and I think he sees that now."

The Mirror reported that a spokesperson for Buckingham Palace said they were "not aware" of any official plans for a second interview.

In his Newsnight chat, the duke told journalist Emily Maitlis he cannot recall the woman who has accused him of sleeping with her on three occasions, including in 2001 when she was 17.

Reacting to the programme on Twitter, GMB presenter Piers Morgan said it was among the "most astonishing" interviews he had ever seen.

He wants the chance to put right the things he didn't say.

He Tweeted to his 6.9 million followers: "This is one of the most astonishing interviews I've ever watched. Brilliant forensic dissection by @maitlis - desperate, toe-curling [bleep] from Prince Andrew.

"Why on earth did he do this? Insane."

But from Prince Andrew's perspective, the interview went well - and he is even reported to have told the Queen it was a "great success".

Someone claiming to be close to the duke told The Sun: "The duke went to church with the Queen [on Sunday] and was heard telling her it's all been a great success."
