'The ground shook' Birmingham City fans pick their childhood Blues heroes

Birmingham City latest | All the latest Blues news and views from BirminghamLive as Bluenoses identify their childhood favourites

20 Nov 2019, 16:44 by Brian Dick, https://www.facebook.com/blueswriterbriandick/?fref=ts

Birmingham City have had some absolute superstars pull on the famous Royal Blue over the decades.

Trevor Francis, Christophe Dugarry, Mikael Forssell and for those of an older vintage the likes of Alex Govan and Gil Merrick - legends to a man.

However, favourite players are a very personal thing and it doesn’t have to be a megastar who fires the imagination of a young supporter.

We asked Bluenoses: “Who was your Blues childhood hero and why?” One man featured prominently - but his wasn’t the only one among some real club legends.

Kevin Harley: My first was Fred Pickering, an old fashioned centre forward whose goals in the 1968 FA Cup got us through to the Semi-Final against WBA at Villa Park. I was expecting so much from him in that game, but a header from Pickering that hit the bar was all he could muster, the rest is history. Francis arrived on the scene four years later, a different kind of hero altogether.

Max Bradley: Loved Martin Grainger

Ian Evans: Trevor Francis, best player the Blues have ever had
