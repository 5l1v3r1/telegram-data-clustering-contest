Man found collapsed in his doorway was unlawfully killed, inquest rules

Cowardly gunman shot Leroy Ottey in the back in the hallway of his own home

20 Nov 2019, 16:54 by Alan Weston

A man gunned down in his own hallway was unlawfully killed, an inquest concluded.

Jamaican-born Leroy Ottey, 25, was shot from behind by an unknown gunman after entering his home in Alderson Road, Wavertree , on the night of October 17 last year.

Area coroner Anita Bhardwaj was told Mr Ottey had just got out of a taxi when the gun attack occurred.

DCI John Middleton of Merseyside Police, in a written statement made to the coroner's court, said Mr Ottey was found collapsed in the doorway.

A black Audi saloon made off from the scene "at speed" and collided with two other cars during the getaway, but failed to stop.

The car was later found burnt-out in Abbeystead Road nearby.

A post mortem found that Mr Ottey died from multiple gunshot wounds.

In his statement, DCI Middleton said the investigation into the murder of Mr Ottey was ongoing but there was "no immediate prospect of charges" being brought.

Ms Bhardwaj concluded Mr Ottey was unlawfully killed.

No family members were present for the brief hearing.

In an appeal made on the first anniversary of his death last month, Mr Ottey's sisters Lerisha and Dana spoke of the devastating impact of his murder on the family.

Lerisha said the family now have "no happy occasions" since the tragic death of her brother.

The family has stopped running the business the 25-year-old helped them run,  Lodge Lane  restaurant Sista Sue's, and they have moved out of their old home.

One year into the investigation, six people have been arrested on suspicion of murder, all of whom remain under investigation.

They include a 25-year-old man from Toxteth, a 30-year-old man from Wavertree, a 31-year-old man from Rainhill, a 30-year-old man from Toxteth, a 24-year-old man from Toxteth and 23-year-old man from Toxteth.
