The £35million question hanging over Aston Villa as 13 players fight for their future

Aston Villa transfers | Dean Smith signed 12 players in the summer yet as many as 13 could be out the door come the summer of 2021 as contracts tick down by the month

20 Nov 2019, 16:30 by Ashley Preece, https://www.facebook.com/PreeceBirminghamLive/

Aston Villa already have a relatively-small squad with just 25 - including FOUR goalkeepers - to choose from.

Dean Smith and the club's hierarchy went about their business efficiently in the summer having signed 12 players for an initial £130m.

And that summer spending spree was very much needed after a huge cull of ageing - and limited - playing staff not fit for the Premier League.

In total, a whopping 17 players departed Bodymoor Heath, including five out on-loan: Scott Hogan, Andre Green, Callum O'Hare, James Bree and Rushian Hepburn-Murphy.

However, with the January transfer window fast approaching, we list the Villa players who are, in effect, currently playing for their futures.

Club captain James Chester and striker Jonathan Kodjia are among a list of six whose deals are just seven months away from expiring.

Youngsters Keinan Davis, Green, O'Hare and Hepburn-Murphy make up that six-some and it remains to be seen if any are offered an extension.

Alarmingly and, if Villa somehow fail in their attempts to land a new striker in January, they could have just ONE senior striker at the club come June, Wesley.

Villa, though, could look to cash-in on Kodjia while they can amid mooted Qatari and Turkish interest in January while Davis is currently out sidelined with a hamstring injury.

The 21-year-old is yet to start in the Premier League but he'll be hoping to make an impact on his return in order to force Smith's hand in offering him a new deal, especially given the stern Championship interest in him during the summer.

Green and Hepburn-Murphy, you'd expect, will be told their surplus to requirements after failing to make an impact at Preston and Tranmere respectively.

Looking ahead to the following summer of 2021, five of Villa's current first-team squad will be out of contract.

The likes of Henri Lansbury, Ahmed Elmohamady and Neil Taylor could, in the summer, have their eyes on a: securing a more long-term contract and b: playing more regularly elsewhere.

One thing is for certain, though, and that the next couple of transfer windows will be busy yet again as the Aston Villa rebuild continues.
