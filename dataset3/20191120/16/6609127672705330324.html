Euro dream

Can Scotland follow Denmark route to Euro 2020 glory with Russia fearing doping ban?

20 Nov 2019, 16:35 by Gary Taylor

RUSSIA face being banned from the Euro 2020 finals next summer due to alleged inconsistencies in anti-doping date - which could hand Scotland a place at the tournament.

The world's largest nation are also at risk of being suspended from next summer's Olympic Games in Tokyo.

Uefa haven't stated who would replace the Russians in the tournament should they be sanction but Denmark famously profited in 1992 after failing to qualify.

Former Rangers winger Brian Laudrup was part of the squad who went all the way and lifted the trophy.

The Danes finished runners up in qualifying but found themselves in the finals after group winners Yugoslavia were not allowed to participate due to being in a state of civil war.

Steve Clarke's Dark Blues ended their automatic bid to reach the finals in third spot - behind Russia -  but could land a similar free pass to the big event just like the Denmark class of '92.

Richard Moller-Nielsen's men were given just over a week's notice to get a squad prepared for the Euro finals.

Less than one month later, the underdogs had completed the miracle and were champions of Europe!

Denmark qualified from their finals group section in second spot after a win over France, draw with England and a defeat to Sweden.

The tournament finals only featured eight teams - with the top two from each section of four reaching the semi-finals.

More heroics followed in the last four as the Danes knocked out the Netherlands 5-4 on penalties following a 2-2 draw.

Footballing icon Marco Van Basten missed the crucial penalty for Holland.

Then it was on to the final and it was a 2-0 success over Germany which arguably sparked the biggest fairytale in world football.

Scotland faced off against Russia twice in their Euro qualifying group, with the Russians winning both 2-1 and 4-0 respectively.

They are one of 12 host countries for next summer's Euro 2020 tournament - which kicks off on June 12.

Russia's potential punishment comes as the country's anti-doping agency is expected to be declared non-compliant when the World Anti-Doping Agency's executive committee meet on December 9 to discuss the issue.

Scotland have the cushion of the play-offs because they topped the Nations League group - and will discover their semi-final opponents on Friday.

The winner of the one-off game at Hampden will face either Norway or Serbia in the final with a spot at Euro 2020 up for grabs.

But after Denmark's Euro '92 success, the Tartan Army could be forgiven for dreaming of a different route to glory.
