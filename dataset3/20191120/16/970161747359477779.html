Priest told funeral boy, 18, had killed himself then condemned 'sin' of suicide

20 Nov 2019, 16:53

A Catholic priest was blasted for telling a teenage boy’s funeral that the youngster had killed himself, before condemning him for it.

Linda Hullibarger had not disclosed her 18 year-old son Maison Hullibarger’s cause of death when the Reverend Don LaCuesta decided to tell a packed church what had happened.

A lawsuit filed by Hullibarger says LaCuesta ignored her request for a ‘positive, uplifting and loving message,’ at the service in Temperance, Michigan.

The priest instead ranted about the ‘sin’ of suicide, refused to allow Maison’s family to speak and banned them from participating in a ceremony called the laying of the cloth, it is claimed.

LaCuesta reportedly said: ‘I think that we must not call what is bad good, what is wrong right.

‘Because we are Christians, we must say what we know is the truth — that taking your own life is against God who made us and against everyone who loves us.

‘The finality of suicide makes this all the worse. You cannot make things right again.’

His revelation about how Maison had died left many who attended the funeral ‘visibly distraught,’ the lawsuit alleges.

The youngster’s dad Jeff tried to halt the rogue priest on two occasions, only for LaCuesta to rebuff his efforts, court papers say.

The Hullibargers complained about LaCuesta, and met with Archbishop Allen Vigneron, NBC News.

But he reportedly told them that ‘he wasn’t there to discuss father LaCuesta,’ and told them to ‘let it go’ when they pressed the issue.

They have now sued at Wayne County Circuit Court, 50 miles south of Detroit, for an undisclosed amount for emotional distress, misrepresentation and invasion of privacy.

LaCuesta has since been barred from speaking at funerals at the Archdiocese of Detroit for failing to ‘display appropriate sensitivity to the situation.’

He apologized shortly after the disastrous funeral, admitting he fell ‘well short of providing them the comfort they so desperately needed.’

The priest continued: ‘Instead, I added to their pain.

‘I deeply regret that, and I am sorry.’

A court hearing has been scheduled in for February 12.
