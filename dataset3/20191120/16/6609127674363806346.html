'VINDICTIVE'

Ex-Aberdeen star glassed by ex in club for kissing another woman spiked her drink with medication after he caught an STD

20 Nov 2019, 16:59 by Alexander Lawrie

A LOVE rat ex-Aberdeen footballer who was glassed by his ex for kissing another woman previously spiked her drink with medication after catching an STD.

Josh Walker, 30, subjected Lauren Dinse, 31, to “emotional and physical abuse”, according to her solicitor.

Neil Martin told the court Walker had been “vindictive” towards her and that his ex was involved in an abusive relationship with him.

It came as Dinse escaped jail for the bloody Edinburgh nightclub attack which left Walker needing 11 stitches after he was taken to hospital.

The footie had been glassed by his then partner after she spotted him in a romantic clinch in trendy club Lulu.

But Mr Martin said Dinse was “remorseful” about the attack and had been “under the influence of alcohol at the time”.

Mr Martin told the court she had spent a “harrowing” weekend in the cells following the incident.

And he added that Dinse is now seeking help from the Women’s Aid Organisation.

He said: “The narrative in the case was that the complainer Mr Walker was standing speaking to another female but what can be seen clearly [in the CCTV] is that he is kissing another female in the nightclub.

“This does not justify what she did but it adds some context. But the relationship was clearly toxic and is now over.”

Sheriff Adrian Cottam said: “The information that has come out since does not excuse your actions which could have been even worse for Mr Walker but fortunately they weren’t.”

The sheriff ordered the receptionist to be placed on an offenders supervision requirement for the next 12 months as an alternative to custody.

Previously prosecutor Alistair Millar gave a short narration to the court and said Dinse was in a relationship with Walker at the time of last December’s assault.

Mr Millar said: “This incident took place in a nightclub where the accused has witnessed the complainer speaking to another female.

“Her reaction to this was to hit him over the head with a glass.”

The fiscal added Mr Walker’s injury was described as “a deep laceration to the head which was five centimetres in length” and required 11 stitches.

Walker recently retired from football due to a serious knee injury.

He is remembered for scoring the opening goal for Aberdeen against Bayern Munich in a UEFA Cup match at Pittodrie.

He also played in the English Premiership for Middlesbrough at just 17 and captained England at the 2009 FIFA U-20 World Cup in Egypt.

Walker also played for Watford, Bournemouth, Rotherham and Indian side Bengaluru and was captain of Scottish League Two side Edinburgh City after joining the club in 2017.
