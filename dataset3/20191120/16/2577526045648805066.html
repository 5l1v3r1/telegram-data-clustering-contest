A Pokémon Shield Player Beat the Entire Game Using Only a Wooloo

20 Nov 2019, 16:19 by Alistair Jones

In the opening hours of Pokémon Sword and Shield, I have somehow managed to almost entirely avoid wild Wooloo. There’s rival Hop's ovine pal, of course, and a number of other sheep Pokémon that seem intent on disrupting public transport throughout the Galar region, but they’ve remained strangely absent from my adventure so far.

For one player, however, Wooloo has made up the entirety of their trip through the latest game. In a post on Reddit last night, player Celvx announced that they’d beaten Shield using nothing but a solitary sheep, posting a video of their Wooloo triumphing over Champion Leon’s Charizard.

Celvx says “I went into this with zero planning or expectations and while I’m sure I didn’t do it in the most efficient or optimal way, it was a fun challenge nonetheless.” Players were quick to acknowledge that this was most definitely not the best way to have tackled the game, as Celvx’s Wooloo, nicknamed Ares, had a nature which limited its attack stat, and arguably a non-optimal ability too. One comment suggested that Celvx had done “a really hard mode even by Wooloo standards.”

At level 100, you’d probably expect Ares to triumph over Leon’s significantly weaker Charizard with relative ease, but Celvx says they were severely held up by dragon-type gym leader Raihan’s double battles (for which he subbed in a pretty useless Magikarp) and dark-type leader Piers. A few lucky critical hits undoubtedly helped them through the challenge but, either way, it's an impressive feat.
