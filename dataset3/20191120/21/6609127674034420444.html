SNP Councillor Frank Anderson suspended after sharing ‘anti-semitic’ blog about female trade unionist

20 Nov 2019, 21:42 by Chelsea Rocks

AN SNP Councillor has been suspended for three months after allegedly sharing an "anti-semitic" blog.

Frank Anderson, a West-Lothian councillor, was suspended after a watchdog probe.

 

The commission sanctioned the Nat for "disrespectful conduct" after he shared a blog post which claimed GMB Scotland leader Rhea Wolfson was taking advantage of Hitler's fascist ideology for her own gain.

Jewish Wolfston had been made the subject of abuse on contentious pro-independence website Grouse Beater.

The blog post named "GMB - a cockney clique" referenced Hitler's autobiography Mein Kampf and his views that Jews gradually had took leadership of the trade union movement.

A post said: “Whether or not Wolfson is intellectually aware of Hitler’s outlook is unknown, but she certainly knows how to make the most of it”.

The blog's author, Edinburgh-based Gareth Wardell, has since been expelled from the SNP.

In light of the investigation, Wolfston took to Twitter to air her views of the controversial post.

The trade-union leader said she "honestly never expected to see such outright support for such obviously anti-Semitic views".

A complaint was later made about Anderson and the Standards Commission for Scotland held a hearing today.

Anderson sought to defend his part in supporting the blog, which he shared on his social media.

The West-Lothian councillor denied it was anti-semitic and claimed: "I don’t accept that it was an anti-Semitic article, nor was I aware of any of Miss Wolfson’s Jewish heritage.

“The article was, in context, about the self-serving attitude of Labour.”

He has since backtracked on his denial, offering his "unreserved" apologies

Following the  Standards Commission's verdict, Anderson has been banned from council meetings for three months.
