Dr Holden will see you now! Amanda gets honorary doctorate from drama school

20 Nov 2019, 21:50

Amanda Holden was beaming with pride as she picked up her Honorary Doctorate.

The Britain’s Got Talent star received the Doctorate from drama school Mountview on Wednesday in recognition of her outstanding contribution to the UK’s arts industry.

Amanda picked up her degree at the Union Chapel with the class of 2019.

The radio host was all smiles while posing with her sister Debbie on the steps of the chapel, holding her Doctorate with pride.

Posting a picture of them on social media, Amanda wrote: ‘My sister @deblholden never missed a show I did’.

They celebrated with sweet treats at a nearby cafe with the 48-year-old sharing a pic of her dessert that had ‘Congratulations’ written on it.

She soon changed her name on Instagram, adding a Dr in front.

Reflecting on the momentous day, Amanda shared: ‘Today I had the great privilege of being awarded an honorary degree -Doctor of Letters from my incredible drama school @mountviewldn where I graduated in 1992…thrilled to have shared the day with these talented students ♥️ Dr Mandy Holden will see you now.’

She explained that she was happy that the arts ‘gets the acknowledgement it deserves’, and added ‘so go out there and show the world what you’ve got.’

In her acceptance speech, Amanda joked that when she heard she would be given the achievement ‘I was almost as surprised as when I watched Prince Andrew mention he popped into Pizza Express in Woking on the 10th March 2001!

‘Life is tough and what we do is provide people with an escape… just for a short time.

‘I’m not claiming that our craft is the panacea for all ills, I’d never do that, mainly because I don’t know what panacea means, but from the beginning of time there has been a need for people to express themselves artistically – yes, we are the modern day equivalent of cave drawings – but that need is what allows the world to breathe, to take some time to interpret the chaos and, quite simply, to just experience some joy. As Picasso said, Art washes away from the soul the dust of everyday life.’
