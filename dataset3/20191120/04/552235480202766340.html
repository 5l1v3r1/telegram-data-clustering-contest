Scots conman who fleeced victims out £840K is ordered to pay back just £25,000

Swindler Stewart Kennedy fleeced his victims and battered one with a baseball bat.

20 Nov 2019, 04:30 by Rory Cassidy

A callous conman who raked in £840,000 through crime was ordered to hand over just £25,000 yesterday.

Swindler Stewart Kennedy fleeced his victims and battered one with a baseball bat.

The 54-year-old told investors that a franchise in his business, was “the financial opportunity of a lifetime” for them.

But he used their money to go on two trips to the US, costing nearly £30,000, and paid for his son’s £1400-a-month flat in London.

He took £144,267 in cash and spent the money in clothes shops, outlets in London’s Camden area, supermarkets, jewellers and the upmarket Corinthian Club in Glasgow.

 

He went on a Disney Cruise, visited New York, Florida and Greece, and bought tickets to Rangers games, jewellery for his wife and meals in restaurants.

Prosecutors had been seeking to recover £151,452 from Kennedy, claiming he made £661,350 from his crimes.

Kennedy had previously submitted a letter denying he lived “a criminal lifestyle”.

But at Paisley Sheriff Court yesterday, it emerged he had agreed to pay back £25,000 after prosecutors discovered he had made £840,000 through crime.

That figure was revealed through Proceeds of Crime laws, which allow the authorities to examine a criminal’s finances in the years before they committed their offence.

 

At the hearing yesterday, Sheriff David Pender gave Kennedy six months to hand over the cash.

Kennedy, who was caged for four years in January last year, has already been released from jail.

Among those duped was plumber Stuart Caldwell, who was battered with a baseball bat by Kennedy when he went to his home in Quarrier’s Village, Kilmacolm, Renfrewshire, to find out what had happened to his £33,000 investment.

Crook Kennedy told investors they would receive a minimum of £4600 per month for 10 months as a return on each £15,000 sum they invested.
