The Irishman final trailer: Martin Scorsese promises a grand crime drama

The Irishman is the longest Martin Scorsese feature yet, running at 3.5 hours. Also, starring Al Pacino and Ray Romano, the film has been penned by Steven Zaillian.

20 Nov 2019, 04:34 by Entertainment Desk

Martin Scorsese directorial The Irishman, which is already playing in select US theaters, will get a wider release on Netflix on November 27. The streaming giant recently released a final trailer for the movie which is being touted as the ultimate Scorsese epic.

The almost three-minute-long video gives us a glimpse of what happens when the tricky worlds of the mafia and politics collide. People are campaigning for John F Kennedy as the US President, while slowly, within the closed doors of the American-Irish underworld, Robert De Niro has entered in the form of a ‘carpenter’ who also happens to ‘take out people’ when needed.

Unlike the previous trailers, the final clip from the movie shows us the multiple timelines within which The Irishman operates. We get to see a relatively younger De Niro with his little daughter and an older De Niro asking for forgiveness from his grown-up child.
