Fears for Ammi, a puppy who ran off after being hit by a taxi

He hasn't been seen for 18 hours and counting

20 Nov 2019, 19:11 by Tristan Cork

A woman has launched a massive search for a missing puppy, after it was hit by a taxi in Bristol and ran off.

Ammi, a nine-month-old male Staffordshire cross, has not been seen since he ran into the darkness after being hit by a taxi while crossing the road in St George.

The brindle-coloured male was being walked on a lead by his owner Toni Teale across Church Road at around 1.30am in the early hours of Wednesday morning.

Police confirmed they attended the scene, and are reviewing dashcam footage of the incident.

Toni said she has been left devastated by Ammi’s disappearance, and appealed to everyone in Bristol to look out for him.

A major search has been carried out in St George’s Park, but so far - at 6pm on Wednesday, November 20, and with Ammi missing for almost 17 hours - he has not turned up yet.

Fears are growing for the puppy, with the incident happening so quickly that no one was able to ascertain how badly he was injured.

He was last seen wearing a reflective harness with a red lead attached.

“We were crossing the road when the car came speeding up and hit my dog,” said Toni.

“Ammi hasn’t been seen by anyone yet - dog wardens and local vets have been contacted, and I’m just praying he’s ok,” she added.

“Devastated is not the word - I don’t know if he’s ok or not, but I’ve been looking for him ever since and still haven’t found him,” she said.

If anyone has any information about where Ammi might be, they should message Toni on Facebook or text her on 07548053587.

“If anyone has him or knows anyone who has, please take him to the local vets so he can come home,” she added.

A spokesperson for Avon and Somerset policee confirmed that they were called to an incident at around 1.30am on Church Road. He said officers were reviewing dashcam footage to ascertain whether any offences had been committed.
