STREET HORROR

Woman struck by taxi outside Edinburgh’s Usher Hall rushed to hospital by paramedics

20 Nov 2019, 19:08 by Katy Pagan

A WOMAN has been rushed to hospital after being struck by a taxi outside Edinburgh's Usher Hall theatre.

The pedestrian was crossing the capital's Lothian Road at around 6.05pm when the ordeal unfolded.

Cops and ambulance crews raced to the scene after the alarm was raised, with a stretch of the road closed off.

The condition of the woman remains unknown at this time.

An eyewitness said: "It looked like the woman has been badly injured.

"Paramedics were carrying her away on a stretcher, and you could see the private taxi's windscreen was all smashed."

A police spokeswoman confirmed they received reports of a female pedestrian being struck by a motor at 6.05pm.
