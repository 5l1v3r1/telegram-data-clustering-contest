Motorcyclist seriously injured after Wales' Euro qualifier match

Police said the 25-year-old biker was later arrested following the crash

20 Nov 2019, 19:56 by Ffion Lewis

A motorcyclist suffered serious injuries after a crash involving a car and a motorbike as Wales fans made their way back from last night's 2-0 win over Hungary.

The accident happened on Cornwall Road, near Cardiff City Stadium at around 10.15pm last night, Tuesday November 11.

The area was particularly busy at the time of the incident due to crowds leaving the ground.

Police and paramedics attended the scene and a 25-year-old male motorcyclist was taken to the University Hospital of Wales with serious injuries.

Officer said that the motorcyclist was subsequently arrested on suspicion of driving under the influence of alcohol, taking a motor vehicle without consent and driving without insurance.

An eyewitness at the scene said: "The driver was lying in the middle of the road with a number of people helping him.

"There was substantial debris on the road and the motorbike was lying on its side.

"Passers by, many who'd left the Wales game, were offering help and directing traffic including buses around the man.

"We saw two ambulance response vehicles and an ambulance arrive."

A spokeswoman for South Wales Police said: "Police are investigating a collision between a motorbike and car near the junction of Cornwall Street and North Clive Street, Grangetown, at around 10.15pm last night.

"The motorcyclist was arrested for driving a motor vehicle whilst under the influence of excess alcohol, taking a motor vehicle without consent and driving without insurance.

"The 25-year-old from Riverside was treated by paramedics at the scene and taken to the University Hospital of Wales, Cardiff, with serious injuries."

Anyone with information on the incident is asked to contact South Wales Police on 101.
