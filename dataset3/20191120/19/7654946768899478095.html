Former Green Bay Packers tight end Colt Lyerla gets 2 years for attacking girlfriend

20 Nov 2019, 19:16 by Joshua Rhett Miller

A former Green Bay Packers tight end has been sentenced to more than two years in prison for choking his girlfriend earlier this year, according to reports.

Colt Lyerla, 27, jettisoned by the Packers in 2014 before ever playing in a regular-season game, pleaded guilty Tuesday to charges including fourth-degree assault and unlawful possession of heroin for the June 17 attack, The Oregonian reports.

Police said Lyerla — a onetime 5-star recruit out of high school who joined the University of Oregon Ducks in 2011 — choked the 24-year-old woman at her home in Eugene and fled by the time cops arrived.

Lyerla was taken into custody the following day after cops used a stun gun on him. He also had heroin on him at the time, the newspaper reports.

The sentence is the latest legal setback for Lyerla, who was added as a junior in 2013 to the “watch list” for the John Mackey Award, which is given annually to college football’s most outstanding tight end, KVAL reports.

Later that season, Lyerla missed two games for unspecified violations of team rules before leaving the team in October to pursue a career in the NFL. But Lyerla was arrested in Eugene days later for snorting cocaine and pleaded guilty to cocaine possession, the station reports.

Lyerla also had several other criminal convictions since that time, including for possession of heroin, cocaine and forgery, the Register-Guard reports.

Most recently, Lyerla was busted in December at a Target in Oregon, where police said he stole about $120 of items and was found in possession of heroin when taken into custody. A judge sentenced him to four months in prison after he pleaded guilty to unlawful possession of heroin, according to The Oregonian.
