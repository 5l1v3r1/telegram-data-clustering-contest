The Cranberries have finally been nominated for their first-ever Grammy award

20 Nov 2019, 19:45

It only took 30 years, but The Cranberries have been nominated for their first-ever Grammy award.

The Irish rock band have been shortlisted for Best Rock Album at the Grammy Awards 2020 – and we’re literally crying with joy.

Earlier this year, the band released their final album as a tribute to their late singer Dolores O’Rioirdan and we know she’d be so proud.

The album, In The End, has been nominated along with albums from Bring Me The Horizon, Cage The Elephant, I Prevail and Rival Sons.

Guitarist Noel Hogan, bassist Mike Hogan, and drummer Fergal Lawler conjured up vocal demos that were recorded by the lead singer before she died in January 2018.

Their first single All Over Now from the album was released on the anniversary of her death.

Meanwhile, pop artists like Billie Eilish, Lizzo, and Taylor Swift are among the stars who are leading the Grammy nominations ahead of the anticipated ceremony.
