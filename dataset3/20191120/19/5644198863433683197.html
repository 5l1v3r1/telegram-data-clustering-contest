Prosus urges Just Eat shareholders to accept its offer, reject Takeaway bid

20 Nov 2019, 19:05

Dutch technology company Prosus NV on Wednesday urged shareholders of Britain's Just Eat PLC to accept its unsolicited 4.9 billion pound (US$6.3 billion) cash bid for the company by Dec. 10, and spurn an alternative offer as too risky.

AMSTERDAM: Dutch technology company Prosus NV on Wednesday urged shareholders of Britain's Just Eat PLC to accept its unsolicited 4.9 billion pound (US$6.3 billion) cash bid for the company by Dec. 10, and spurn an alternative offer as too risky.

"We believe that the Takeaway.com offer underestimates the substantial investment required in Just Eat to recapture market share and improve performance," Prosus CEO Bob van Dijk said in a statement of the rival proposal.

Takeaway's all-share offer, which is supported by Just Eat's board, currently values Just Eat at around 4.7 billion pounds.

Prosus is controlled by South Africa's Naspers Ltd.
