Halsey 'unsurprised' BTS weren't nominated for a Grammy

20 Nov 2019, 19:34

Halsey has asked BTS fans to ease off after the K-Pop group were not nominated for a Grammy award.

The south Korean group’s passionate fanbase, called ARMY, have been left disappointed by the nominations for 2020, with the likes of Lizzo and Ariana Grande up for multiple gongs.

Halsey, who worked with BTS on the single Boy With Luv, responded on Twitter to an ARMY who asked why she hadn’t spoken out about the snub.

In a now-deleted tweet, the Without Me singer said: ‘I just woke up and the biggest song of my career wasn’t nominated either. Can u guys not take ur anger out on me for once or’.

She later added: ‘Deleting and ignoring all negativity. BTS deserved many nominations.

‘I am however, unsurprised that they weren’t acknowledged. the US is so far behind on the whole movement. the time will come.[sic]’

The boy group’s fans wasted no time in blasting the Grammys as ‘disgraceful’ when the nominations were revealed on Wednesday.

The K-Pop group – made up of RM, Jin, Suga, V, Jungkook, J-Hope and Jimin – are currently far too busy working on their comeback, and recently asked fans for their patience while they cook up something amazing.

In an interview with Paper magazine, the group revealed: ‘We are currently practicing and working on new songs so we can show you the best sides of ourselves. Please look forward to it.’

The comeback will follow their latest album Map Of The Soul: Persona.

BTS have three nominations at the upcoming American Music Awards for tour of the year, favourite social artist and favourite duo or group – pop/rock.

For Tour of the Year, the boys will be up against Ariana Grande, Ed Sheeran, Elton John, and Pink.

They recently wrapped up their Love Yourself: Speak Yourself tour in Seoul.
