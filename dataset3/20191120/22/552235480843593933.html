Daniel Stendel 'top Hearts target' as Austin MacPhee is set for new role at Tynecastle

The German left his role as Barnsley manager earlier this season and has been interviewed.

20 Nov 2019, 22:45 by Gabriel McKay

Daniel Stendel has reportedly been identified as Hearts ' number one target for the vacant head coach role.

The German was sacked as Barnsley boss earlier this season and has already been interviewed for the position, which has been vacant since Craig Levein was sacked.

According to the Sun owner Ann Budge has identified Stendel has the top target to replace the former Scotland boss and is prepared to push the boat out financially to get him.

Stendel is still under contract with Barnsley and is on gardening leave, so the Jambos would have to pay compensation to bring him to Tynecastle.

Budge has previously stated that would not be an issue if the right man were to be identified.

She said earlier this month: "We have to compete with the top clubs in Scotland and to do that we want a very experienced and high profile manager.

"Finances (to pay compensation) are not a problem."

Up to six figures could be required to extricate Stendel from his contract, and it remains to be seen if he will accept the job.

It's also reported that current interim boss Austin MacPhee will move upstairs to become the new sporting director at Tynecastle.

Levein previously acted as both head coach and director of football, but Hearts are looking to shake up the existing structure.

MacPhee will be in the dugout for Saturday's game at Kilmarnock but could be appointed to the newly-created sporting director role after that.
