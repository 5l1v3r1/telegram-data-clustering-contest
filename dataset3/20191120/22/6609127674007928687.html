JACK AND CHILL

Scotland star Jack feared nasty challenge would rule him out of vital Rangers games

20 Nov 2019, 22:28 by Colin Duncan

RYAN JACK feared a nasty Hampden tackle would rule him out of the run of games which could make or break Rangers' season.

The Scotland midfielder was relieved to emerge unhurt after Kazakhstan sub Islambek Kuat rammed his studs into his shin.

Kuat escaped with just a yellow card, but Jack felt it should easily have been red.

Fortunately for Gers he suffered no lasting damage and will be fit to face Hamilton on Sunday.

That clash at the Fountain of Youth Stadium is the first of ten matches for Steven Gerrard’s side between now and the winter break.

The Light Blues face Europa League clashes with Feyenoord and Young Boys and a Betfred Cup Final with holders Celtic.

There’s also the small matter of away Premiership trips to face Aberdeen, Celtic, Motherwell and Hibs during December

Jack said: “My leg is fine. Obviously it was a bad one, but the ref has only seen it as a yellow.

“There were a few naughty challenges throughout the match. I don’t know what was behind that, but the referee is there to protect us. Thankfully no one is injured.”

Jack knows only too well the significance of the games that lie ahead over the next five weeks.

They will place huge mental and physical demands on the Ibrox side.

But Jack reckons the fact there is no time to think about them is a big advantage given what is at stake.

He said: “It’s a really tough schedule and there’s no doubt there’s a lot of high-pressure games. But you’re at a big club and that’s how you want it.

“Maybe at the end of the season I’ll notice if it’s tiring, but it’s just game, train, recover, game, train, recover and your body gets into a routine.

“You don’t really have time to think about it. It’s playing on a Thursday, recover, then play on a Sunday, recover, and then play midweek.

“You just take every game as it comes. If you have a bad result, you want to put it right and you’ve got games so close to each other that you get the chance to do that.

“There’s tough games coming up in Europe as well. Feyenoord away will be very tough, then Young Boys at home. They have been doing very well in the group.

“We’ve got a lot of work to do before we can say we’ve achieved anything.”

Jack impressed for Scotland in both victories against Cyprus and Kazakhstan.

The 27-year-old dovetailed well alongside Celtic’s Callum McGregor in the middle of the park as Steve Clarke’s team ended a poor qualifying campaign with a third straight win.

Jack is convinced the wins have given the national team massive momentum ahead of the Euro 2020 play-offs in March.

And he also feels the boost generated from that will benefit him at club level.

Jack said: “The last three wins really help us.

"We’re playing alongside each other more often, but it’s frustrating that there’s going to be a wee bit of a break now.

“If we had these games next month there would be a feel-good factor from the last games.

“We’ll all go back to our clubs and look to play well and stay in the manager’s thoughts. I’m sure come March everyone will be involved.

“You want to win matches. You don’t want to come away and get beat and then go back to your club not feeling good about yourself.

“Thankfully on this trip we’ve been able to get two wins, so that helps your confidence going back to your club.

“There’s lots of big games coming up and lots to look forward to.

“The next two games will be massive. The games I’ve had over the last two years have helped me get to this stage and playing for my country.

“At club level, as soon as you put that jersey on there’s an expectation to win.

“Then you come to Scotland and play alongside top-class players and there’s an expectancy, from the supporters and staff, to go and win games as well.

"Since I’ve come along to the squad the manager has said he wants positivity and everyone wanting to be here to play for Scotland.

“He doesn’t want any negativity and this week we’ve not had that.

“We’ve managed to end it in a good way with three wins in three games and that takes us into the play-offs in March.”

Next up for Jack is a game on Hamilton Accies’ plastic pitch at the weekend and he is adamant there will be no problem adjusting to the least glamorous trip in the Premiership.

He added: “Not at all. There is different pressure for each of the games we are involved in.

“But going back to your club, it’s back to getting three points and keeping that momentum going that we’ve had.

“We’ve had a great month with great results and Sunday will be a big test for us.”
