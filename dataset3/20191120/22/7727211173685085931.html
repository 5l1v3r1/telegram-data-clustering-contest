I'm A Celebrity late campmates confirmed as Andrew Whyment and Cliff Parisi

The soap stars will be arriving in camp soon

20 Nov 2019, 22:03 by Sophie McCoid

Coronation Street star Andrew Whyment and actor Cliff Parisi were confirmed as the late entries to the Jungle at the end of tonight's episode.

The pair have been widely rumoured to enter I'm A Celebrity and now they have been confirmed as the show's late arrivals.

Ant and Dec said the pair would be entering the jungle, but before they got to camp they'd be taking part in a bushtucker trial to try and win the camp dinner.

The pair will become Outlaws in the Wild West! They will meet for the first time at a Bushtucker Trial in Ol' Dingo Town - a wild west style town and I'm A Celebrity's biggest trial set to date.

Arriving dressed head to toe as cowboys they will each face a trial behind one of the buildings in Ol' Dingo Town. 

Over the coming days all Bushtucker Trials will take part in Ol' Dingo Town and there are plenty of twists and turns along the way.

Last year Noel Edmunds was the late arrival, as he lorded over the camp in his Emperor role, and left after just nine days.

But this year Corrie favourite Andrew Whyment, who plays Kirk Sutherland, will be joined by former EastEnders star Cliff Parisi.

When the line-up was announced, many fans were furious that Andy was not included after he was heavily rumoured to appear - but now they are sure to be thrilled.

Cliff, 59, is known to millions as 'Minty' from his eight years on Albert Square and also now as handyman and gardener Fred Buckle on Call The Midwife.

Cliff grew up in north London and is married to BBC production assistant Tara Wyer.

He has previously spoken of how his school days were tough, because he was dyslexic but dis­­missed as “thick” and he left at 13.

Unable to follow his dream of attending stage school because he lacked the necessary qualifications, Cliff became a stand-up comic.

He was offered a bit-part in the 1989 film Queen of Hearts then landed the role of Lunchbox in the TV series Chancer.

He also appeared in The Darling Buds of May and Drop the Dead Donkey and landed bigger parts in Kavanagh QC, Bramwell and A Prince Among Men before being cast as Minty in EastEnders in 2002.

The actor has four children – daughter Mandy, 37, and sons Dean, 31, and Jack 27, are from previous relationships. Tara gave birth to the couple’s fourth child, a son named Arthur, now nine.
