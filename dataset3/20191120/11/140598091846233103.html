Classic trifle with poached pears recipe

20 Nov 2019, 11:39 by Angela Hartnett

This is a simple recipe but it makes an impressive trifle to serve your guests. Slice the poached pears neatly to get a nice even layer. 

Prep time: 20 minutes | Cooking time: 10 minutes

SERVES

Six to eight

INGREDIENTS

For the poached pears

  • 500ml water
  • 300g caster sugar
  • Juice of ½ lemon
  • 1 wide strip lemon zest
  • 1 wide strip orange zest
  • 1 star anise
  • 2.5cm piece fresh ginger, peeled
  • 4 William or conference pears

For the trifle

  • 6 ready-made trifle sponges or savoiardi biscuits
  • 200ml sherry, dry ideally
  • 300ml vanilla custard, home-made ideally
  • 300ml double cream
  • Caster sugar, to taste
  • 50g flaked almonds, toasted

METHOD

  1. First, poach the pears. Put all the ingredients (apart from the pears) into a saucepan and bring to the boil.
  2.  Reduce to a simmer and stir until the sugar has dissolved. Remove from the heat and allow to infuse while it cools. This can be kept in the fridge until you need it.
  3. Peel and carefully core each pear, leaving them whole and ideally with the stalks on. Lay them in a pan, along with the stock syrup.
  4.  Cover with a circle of parchment paper and sit a plate (smaller than the circumference of the pan) on top – this keeps the pears in the syrup while they cook.
  5. Bring to the boil then turn down to a simmer and cook for 10 minutes, or a little less if the pears are really ripe – check them with the tip of a knife. Remove from the heat and allow to cool in the syrup, then slice.
  6. Line the base of a large (ideally see-through) bowl with the sponge fingers or biscuits.
  7. Top with a layer of sliced pears to cover then sprinkle over the sherry to soak through both layers. Pour the custard over the pears.
  8. Whip the cream with a touch of sugar to taste, until you have medium to firm peaks.
  9. Pipe or spoon the cream over the trifle then use a fork to whip up the surface of the cream into peaks. Scatter with the almonds to finish.
  10.  Serve immediately or leave in the fridge until ready to serve.
