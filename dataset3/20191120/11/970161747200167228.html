What channel is Rick and Morty season 4 on in the UK and can you watch online?

20 Nov 2019, 11:30

Wubba lubba dub dub! It’s almost time for the first episode of Rick and Morty series 4 to land in the UK.

The UK premiere of the season is set to air Wednesday, 20 November.

Here’s what you need to know about where you can tune in and catch up.

What channel is Rick and Morty season 4 being shown on?

Episodes of season 4 will air in the UK on E4 and All 4, with the very first episode – Edge of ToMorty: Rick Die Rickpeat – airing on E4 at 10pm.

This means you’ll be able to watch episodes of Rick and Morty for free on Channel 4’s website after they’ve aired on TV.

At first, the new series was set for release in the UK in January 2020.

Fans were not ok with that however, and demand from them led to season 4’s new November premiere.

Ian Katz, Director of Programmes at Channel 4 said: ‘When we announced we would be airing Rick and Morty Series 4 on free to air TV in January we thought fans would be delighted.

‘Instead they told us – in their droves – that it wasn’t nearly soon enough, so we’ve listened and brought forward the UK premiere to November 20th on E4 and All 4. Enjoy!’

This season, we can look forward to celebrity cameos from the likes of Taika Waititi, Christian Slater, Sam Niell, Matthew Broderick and Paul Giamatti.

The reviews for the new series have been promising, and suffice it to say that we can’t wait to see what’s in store for Rick, Morty and the rest of the Smiths this time around.

The first episode of Rick and Morty season 4 will air at 10pm on E4 on 20 November.
