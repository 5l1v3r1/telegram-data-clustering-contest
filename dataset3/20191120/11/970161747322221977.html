Jorja Smith performing Shelter charity gig ahead of Christmas

20 Nov 2019, 11:13

Jorja Smith will be doing her bit for charity in the run up to Christmas by performing a charity gig for Shelter.

The Be Honest singer will raise awareness for the rise in homelessness around the festive holiday with a performance at London’s Union Chapel on Monday 9 December.

Sharing her anticipation ahead of the performance, Jorja said in a statement: ‘I’m thrilled to perform at this year’s Shelter music gig, at such an iconic and intimate venue, and help raise money that supports the amazing work the charity does fighting homelessness.

‘Everyone should have a safe place to call home and buying a ticket will make a massive difference to people facing homelessness this Christmas.’

Tickets cost £45 and will be allocated on a ballot basis. Every penny will go towards Shelter’s work helping those struggling with homelessness and poor housing conditions with around 320,000 people currently homeless in the UK.

The ticket ballot opens today at 11am and will remain so until 9am on Monday 25 November. Those lucky enough to bag tickets will be notified from 1pm that day.

Polly Neate, Shelter’s chief executive said: ‘We’re really grateful that Jorja is giving up her time to help people who won’t have a home this Christmas.

‘The country is in the grips of a housing emergency that is pushing thousands into homelessness and leaving millions on the verge of losing their home. So, money raised from ticket sales will help us provide vital advice and support for people facing the misery of homelessness this festive season.’

Fans can expect Jorja to perform hits from her critically-acclaimed debut album Lost & Found, with songs including Blue Lights and Teenage Fantasy as well as her recent summer hit Be Honest featuring Burna Boy.

Until then, the 22-year-old singer is currently living her best life on holiday in Jamaica and it’s ultimate holiday goals.

Jorja has been giving fans travel envy with stunning photos of her lounging around on the beach and climbing trees in the water.
