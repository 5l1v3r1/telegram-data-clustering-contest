Katie Price's son Harvey is now an autism charity ambassador

Harvey will help raise awareness of autism

20 Nov 2019, 11:41 by Charlotte Vowles, Tiffany Wallis

Katie Price's eldest son, Harvey, has been announced as an ambassador for an autism charity.

Harvey is autistic, partially blind and has a genetic disorder called Prader- Willi Syndrome.

The 17 year old, has been the target of online trolls over the years, and will now represent autism awareness charity Anna Kennedy Online.

The former glamour model, 41 and Harvey are set to work together for the charity.

OBE of the charity, Anna Kennedy, announced Harvey's new position by posting a proud photo of the three of them to Instagram on Friday.

She wrote a welcoming caption alongside the snap: "I am pleased to announce that Harvey Price is now one of my Charity Ambassadors and joined the AKO family.

"Welcome Harvey :) #autism #charity #fridayfeeling @officialkatieprice @officialharveyprice."

Anna previously welcomed Harvey on board, saying: "I have met Harvey and Katie quite a few times now when they have both attended our charity events.

"Harvey was pleased to meet other autistic young people like himself and loved Autism's got Talent shouting out words of encouragement to our performers.

"He also has supported our Anti Bullying campaign this week sharing our message ‘We say no to Bullying!’

"We welcome with open arms Harvey to our AKO family and look forward to Harvey helping me raise autism awareness and acceptance.”

The charity is famous for running huge events such as Autism's Got Talent and it's website now lists Harvey as part of its team.

The website tells of Harvey's incredible keyboard skills and recalls his performance at The Mermaid Theatre in London, after he was inspired to audition for their talent contest.

The post also touches on Harvey's numerous telly appearances which led to him becoming the subject of vile social media posts.

It reads: "Harvey deals with online bullying and trolling. He was subject to horrendous online abuse after sharing a drawing of his mum on the Gatwick Express on his  Instagram  page.

"Fans commented pleaded with Katie to remove the account to protect Harvey, but she chose not to and instead deleted the harmful comments.

"On 22 January 2019, it was revealed that Parliament would back Katie’s ‘Harvey’s Law’ efforts.

"She started an online petition in March 2017 to criminalise social media trolling, after Harvey suffered at the hands of Instagram trolls."

Last month, Harvey's straight-talking businesswoman mum named and shamed a troll who called her son a "fat s**t" and Katie a "s**g."

The former glamour model shared with her fans a screen shot of the troll's profile which showed they were a health and social care nursing consultant.

Katie penned: "If anybody knows this person or works with this person, I would question her job."

She added an appeal to fans: "This is exactly what she said about my son Harvey. Name and shame her."

Recently Katie made another swipe at trolls as she filmed Harvey asking them to not target him.

In his hand, Harvey can be seen clutching a piece of paper with the slogan: "Give Us A Break! Bullying is WRONG. End of."
