Who is going to win the 2019 General Election? Conservatives ahead in odds

20 Nov 2019, 11:06

As the General Election draws closer, latest odds suggest that the Conservatives are on course for a win following the ITV leaders debate between Boris Johnson and Jeremy Corbyn last night.

According to bookmakers Coral, the Tories – who were already ahead in the polls before the debate – now have odds of just 1-20 of winning the most seats at the polls on 12 December – with Labour’s odds of an overall majority having drifted.

Since the debate – which left viewers divided as to who won – the Labour party’s odds on that front are now a distant 25-1 – behind no majority being reached, which is second in the betting at 7-4.

‘Johnson and Corbyn locked horns last night for the first leadership debate but the reaction from a betting point of view is that the Conservatives are continuing to motor towards a majority,’ said Coral’s Harry Aitkenhead.

‘We have them as the overwhelming favourites to win the most seats and they are odds on to claim the majority that Johnson is desperate for.’

With odds having shortened on a Conservative win, the odds on Brexit happening have also dropped, with the bookmaker now offering odds of 1-2 on the UK having left the EU by 1 February.

‘Brexit was a major talking point last night, unavoidably’ Aitkenhead added, ‘and we make it odds on that the UK has finally left the EU by February the 1st, 2020.’
