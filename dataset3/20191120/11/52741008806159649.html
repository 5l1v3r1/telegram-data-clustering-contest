Byelection to be held in Cambridge

Residents in Ward 7 can expect to cast a ballot in March 2020

20 Nov 2019, 11:17 by Mark Pare

Back to the polls, we go!

Cambridge city council has decided to hold a byelection to fill the vacant seat in Ward 7, left behind after the passing of three-term councillor Frank Monteiro on October 15.

The two main things that came up during discussions around the horseshoe centred around time and money.

"When you've got a three-year term left, I think the voters and the people of Ward 7 should be able to have an election, have their say on who they want as their representative," says Councillor Shannon Adshade.

Councillor Nicholas Ermeta agreed with those sentiments.

"I believe a byelection is the fairest way possible," he said.

"There is a cost to democracy, there really is.  People have lost their lives for it."

The cost for a byelection could come in at up to $100,000.

Those in favour believe it's money well spent, while Councillors Donna Reid and Pam Wolf --- the two arguing against the motion in what turned into a 6-2 vote --- say the money could be better used on something else.

The two of them pushed for council-led process to appoint someone to the post instead.

"We can use the Municipal Act as it is written for us to use, and provide Ward 7 with a councillor by January, rather than the end of March," Reid says.

Nomination day is February 7, 2020, with a proposed byelection date coming March 23, 2020.
