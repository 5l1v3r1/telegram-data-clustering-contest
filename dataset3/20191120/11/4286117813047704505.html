Blind students from JNU protest against police lathicharge, demand apology from commissioner

A group of students protested outside the new Delhi Police headquarters demanding an apology from the commissioner against the alleged lathicharge on blind students.

20 Nov 2019, 11:34 by Pooja Shali

Around 50-60 students from Jawaharlal Nehru University (JNU) on Wednesday shouted slogans outside the new Delhi Police headquarters demanding an apology from the commissioner against the alleged lathicharge on blind students.

The protest was an escalation to the ongoing demonstrations for the demand of fee hike's rollback.

Most protestors were students with disabilities from JNU, angry at the police action.

"Most students belong to marginalised families with low income. I could study further only due to subsidised education. Inclusive education should be for all. How will we study further with hike in fee?" asked Pankaj Khushwaha, who is pursuing PhD.

A delegation led by the PhD student went inside the police headquarters to submit a list of demands.

"Police lathicharged a blind student and asked why he is participating in a protest if he is blind. Protest is a democratic right for all. We demand an unconditional apology from the police commissioner," said a student of Russian studies.

Shweta, JNU MPhil Society Medicine and Community Health, asked, "Our bigger issue is against fee-hike. Plus, on Monday we faced brutal behaviour by the police. Why were students lathicharged?"
