Jose Mourinho's comments praising 'powerful' Tottenham under Daniel Levy

20 Nov 2019, 08:38

Jose Mourinho spoke in glowing terms of the rise of Tottenham Hotspur, even when the Portuguese was in charge at Manchester United.

The 56-year-old has been confirmed as the new manager of Spurs on Wednesday morning after Mauricio Pochettino was sacked on Tuesday evening.

The former Chelsea, Real Madrid and Inter Milan boss brings a wealth of top level experience to north London, and also a lot of respect for the club he is joining.

When in charge at Old Trafford, Mourinho was asked about the difficulties of signing players, and cited Spurs as a team you could no longer bully into selling their best players.

‘Is Manchester United by its history, by its dimension, bigger than Tottenham? With all respect, Tottenham is an amazing club. I think everybody would say yes,’ Mourinho said in 2017.

‘Can you buy Tottenham’s best players? No, because they don’t sell. Of course, they are so powerful that they can say no.

‘A few years ago, who was Tottenham’s best player? Michael Carrick. And a few years later, who was the best player? Berbatov. Can we go there now and bring Harry Kane? Dele Alli, [Christian] Eriksen, Son [Heung-min]? Can we go there and bring those players here? No.

‘So who is more powerful now? Them or us? Football’s changed. It’s more difficult to buy players of a high, high, high level. The clubs are more powerful. The clubs don’t want to sell, and to sell is to go to absolutely incredible levels.

‘Before, the smaller clubs were almost begging the big clubs: “Get my best players. I need to sell. Please, you are powerful. Buy my best player.” At this moment, they don’t want to sell. They don’t want to sell.’
