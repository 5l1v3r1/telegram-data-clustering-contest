Rebel Wilson writes K-pop comedy Seoul Girls and the internet has feelings

20 Nov 2019, 08:53

K-pop is only just starting to be recognised in the Western charts and awards show, with artists like BTS, BLACKPINK and EXO smashing records.

And it seems now the movie industry is jumping on the bandwagon

A K-pop comedy written by Rebel Wilson is in the works, and yup – the internet has some feelings.

According to Deadline, Lionsgate has obtained the rights to Seoul Girls, which was written by Pitch Perfect star Rebel, who will also have a role in the movie.

Seoul Girls will follow a Korean American high school student who, along with her friends, decides to enter a global talent competition to be the opening act for the world’s biggest K-pop boyband (three guesses on who that is based on).

Advertisement Advertisement

The ‘Seoul Girls’ are given help in their quest to open for BTS – er, no, we mean a totally unrelated boyband – by a former K-pop trainee and a former member of a British girl group.

We’re getting throwbacks to Simon Cowell’s UK-pop comments…

While we have no further details on what Seoul Girls will entail, people aren’t exactly enthusiastic about the project.

The Ringer’s Kate Halliwell tweeted: ‘well there’s no way this could go wrong’, while one person added: ‘Why is this happening and who can I pay to make it stop lmao?’

And the hits just kept coming:

In fairness, none of us know what the actual content of the movie will be yet, and many people on Twitter are waiting to see a trailer to give the benefit of the doubt.

Plus, it has been noted that Rebel’s script has been given a recent revision by Korean screenwriter Young Il Kim, who has tweeted: ‘This will be a fun project. Hope you all see it if/when it comes out. Also, I hope this leads to me getting backstage passes for my kids to a BTS concert or something.’

He also joked: ‘Years of attending K-CON paid off!

‘This Kpop project is gonna get me at least a 10% discount in K-town restaurants!’

Seoul Girls doesn’t yet have a release date.
