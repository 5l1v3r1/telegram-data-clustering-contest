GMB presenters 'could take cut' as Piers Morgan's contract negotiations continue

20 Nov 2019, 09:17

Good Morning Britain’s Susanna Reid has joked that the rest of the presenters could face having their wages cut to accommodate Piers Morgan.

Earlier this week, Piers confirmed that he could be leaving the breakfast programme ahead of the show’s time extension and has previously disclosed that his contract runs out on New Year’s Eve.

Discussing Piers’ future as co-host, Susanna suggested that he could soon be replaced by longstanding rival BBC Breakfast’s Dan Walker.

‘If your contract negotiations don’t work out perhaps he’ll be sitting in your seat,’ she remarked.

‘Or perhaps I’ll be sitting in his seat. BBC…,’ Piers clapped back.

Susanna then joked: ‘BBC can’t afford you, sorry… We can barely afford you. The rest of us might have to take a cut if we want you back here in January.’

Piers then explained to Susanna and viewers at home that they must comprehend their worth during contract negotiations.

‘You have to understand your own value and then you have to stick to it,’ Piers said. ‘I value myself at X You value me at Y therefore, I’m taking my toys out of the pram and going to Z.’

Ever the diva, eh?

Good Morning Britain is due to get an extended time slot in the new year, adding half an hour to fill in the gap in an overhaul of the ITV morning schedule.

From 6 January 2020, Good Morning Britain will be extended by half an hour to run from 6am until 9am – that’s three whole hours of Piers Morgan (should he stick around).

Lorraine will move to 9am, and will be followed by This Morning at 10am, extended by 30 minutes, before Loose Women at 12.30pm, ending at 1.30pm.
