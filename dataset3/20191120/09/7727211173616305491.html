Community proves that Liverpool Cares

Read the latest news from Semble - the UK's most active platform for community projects

20 Nov 2019, 09:00 by Liverpool Echo

Liverpool Cares is a community network of younger and older neighbours hanging out and helping one another out.

They are currently running a Winter Wellbeing project that will help over 65s to stay warm, active and connected over the winter months ahead.

The project sees the Liverpool Cares team delivering warm items such as blankets, hats and hot water bottles to older neighbours across Liverpool, providing a little extra warmth as we move into these colder months.

Liverpool Cares will also be putting older neighbours in touch with local organisations that can offer guidance, such as ways of reducing fuel bills and tips and tricks to help insulate a cold home.

By getting out across Liverpool, visiting local community groups and GP surgeries, Liverpool Cares are able to build deep connections with older neighbours who are in need of some support.

June , 76, heard about the Winter Wellbeing project on BBC Radio Merseyside, and reached out personally to Liverpool Cares to ask for some warm items to help her get through the winter months ahead.

Whilst dropping off a thermal blanket and some new gloves at June’s home the Liverpool Cares team were able to tell June all about the free Social Clubs that they run across the city - helping her stay connected and active- and over a nice cup of tea they also shared stories about how Liverpool has changed over the years.

Liverpool Cares will be out and about across the city over the coming months, connecting with more older neighbours and telling them all about their free programmes.

If you know someone who could benefit from a warm item, or you would like to hear more about their Winter Wellbeing project, please get in touch on 0151 659 1789.
