80s festival featuring Marc Almond, Altered Images, Heather Small and more coming to Notts in 2020

'Retro revival at its finest'

20 Nov 2019, 09:00 by Simon Wilson

An 80s festival will be bringing some of the biggest stars of the decade to Nottinghamshire next summer.

Marc Almond, Heather Small, Hue & Cry, China Crisis and Altered Images have been confirmed to appear at Discover the 80s, part of the 2020 Newark Festival at Riverside Park.

The retro all-dayer, where gates open at 3pm, is on Saturday, August 29.

Organisers LHG Events describe it as "an unforgettable evening of fun and some of the best music from the much-loved decade - retro revival at its finest."

Liz Doogan-Hobbs MBE, CEO of LHG Events said “Festival-goers, get ready for our finest year yet, with a new date and an incredible line-up of artists who will be bringing retro fever to Newark.

"We’re thrilled to present yet another year of music and entertainment and are sure that revellers will welcome the new August date at the height of the summer. We have some amazing acts and urge everyone to get into the spirit of the decade and dress up for the occasion."

It is part of the annual three-day festival. Details of the Friday and Sunday events have yet to be revealed.

Dennis Jones, leader of Newark Town Council said: “I hope that the local community will once again give its full support. We are working to produce a fantastic weekend of music and entertainments and details of a bigger and better, free, Brass Explosion event on the August Bank Holiday Sunday will be announced shortly.”

Marc Almond has sold over 30 million records with Soft Cell and as a solo artist. Hits include Tainted Love, Say Hello Wave Goodbye, Bedsitter, Something’s Gotten Hold of My Heart (with Gene Pitney), Jacky and The Days of Pearly Spencer. He played the Royal Concert in Nottingham in May with a show described by NottinghamshireLive as "a triumph".

As part of M People, hits such as Moving On Up, One Night In Heaven and Search For The Hero and albums like Elegant Slumming, Bizarre Fruit and Fresco made Heather Small a household name. The band won Best British Dance Act Award at the Brits in 1994 and 1995, as well as the Mercury Music Prize for Elegant Slumming.

Small has since had success with two solo albums, while her song Proud became the soundtrack to a number of major events including London's successful 2012 Olympic bid, the 60th anniversary of VE Day in Trafalgar Square, the launch of Queen Mary 2, England's victory at the Rugby World Cup celebrations, and the official ceremony marking the handover of the Olympic Games from Beijing to London.

Hue & Cry - brothers Patrick and Gregory Kane - are still best known for just two hit singles, Looking For Linda and Labour of Love but their fan base is such that they've now released 12 studio albums and continue to tour in their own right. They've also played concerts alongside U2, James Brown, Madonna, Al Green and Van Morrison.

Merseyside duo China Crisis - aka vocalist/keyboardist Gary Daly and guitarist/vocalist Eddie Lundon - notched up eleven UK Top 50 singles and three UK Top 40 albums during the decade and continue to tour across the world.

Altered Images emerged from Scotland during the new wave era and enjoyed a strong of hits including Happy Birthday, I Could be Happy, See Those Eyes and Don’t Talk to Me About Love. After a lead role in the 1981 film Gregory’s Girl, when the band split singer Clare Grogan pursued her acting career with appearances in Red Dwarf, Eastenders, Father Ted and Skins.

She has also been a TV presenter and children’s novelist.
