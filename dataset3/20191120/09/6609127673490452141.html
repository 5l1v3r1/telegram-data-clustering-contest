'HE WILL NEVER BE FORGOTTEN'

Family of Scots chef Jonathon Donaldson who died ‘falling in river after night out’ launch funeral GoFundMe

20 Nov 2019, 09:07 by Blair Meikle

THE cousin of a tragic West Lothian chef who died on his way home from a night out has launched a fundraiser in his honour.

Jonathon Donaldson, 26, vanished last Monday and a body was found at the weekend after cops launched a frantic search.

We told earlier how devastated family members paid tribute to the popular chef who was "the life of the party".

And his thoughtful cousin Becca Graham, 16, has organised a fundraiser in a bid to raise much needed funds to support the family with funeral costs.

She told the Scottish Sun: "I just wanted to help support the family.

"He was a great person, he was just so caring, even to people he didn't know - he would always make sure you were OK.

"He was really popular, he worked in a lot of places and got to know a lot of people."

Becca said she has been touched by the response to Jonathon's shock death, and also to her fundraiser.

She added: "It made me feel really proud and it's so good that it's going to help.

"It's a small comfort to the family.

"Originally I just wanted to raise £200 but it's at £300 and people are still donating.

"So I'm just going to keep it going until we need to pay for the funeral."

She added on the gofundme page: "His death was such a surprise to all of us and will be a hard one to recover from, he will never be forgotten.

"Any donations are useful, thank you all."
