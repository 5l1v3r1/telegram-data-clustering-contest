Inside London's Billionaires Row: The grand home of Britain's first porn baron, who quit school at 15, built a media empire, then sold it, Business Insider - Business Insider Singapore

20 Nov 2019, 09:10 by Bill Bostock
  • A mile-long road in London has been the city’s “Billionaires Row” since World War II: a destination for luxury, scandal, murder, and secrecy.
  • The newspaper baron Richard Desmond has lived on The Bishops Avenue since 2004, one of very few residents to own a home under his own name.
  • This post is part of an interactive Insider series profiling Billionaires Row.

In 1966, Richard Desmond quit school in north London at the age of 15 to become a drummer.

In May 2019, the now-67-year-old has a mansion on The Bishops Avenue – London’s Billionaires Row – and commands a personal fortune estimated around $1 billion.

Desmond lives in a mansion called Dane Court, one of the avenue’s 66 properties.

Desmond is the owner of Northern & Shell, once a giant of UK media which published the UK’s Daily Express and Daily Star newspapers until their sale last year.

As the media business went sour, Desmond sold off Britain’s Channel 5 TV station, his newspapers, as well as celebrity magazines like OK! and New, pivoting his company to property and gambling.

Desmond bought Dane Court, a neo-Jacobean mansion, for £9.2 million ($14 million) in 2004.

It is one of 40% of the mansions on the road registered to a named person rather than a shell corporation.

On the right side of the house, the derelict Kenmore House rots away behind gates sliding from their hinges, while on the left stands the shining Jersey House, complete with safe room, sold for £34 million ($44 million) in December 2015.

Desmond is famous in the UK for being the nation’s first “porn baron.”

In the 1980s, his early magazines had names like International Musician and Home Organist but these soon gave way to Asian Babes, Forty Plus, and Posh Housewives.

In 1982, Desmond bought the UK license for the x-rated US TV channel Penthouse. He controlled it until 1991.

In his autobiography “The Real Deal” Desmond called the decision “nine years of grief,” as Penthouse’s US founder Bob Gucci transpired to be a terrible business partner.

Desmond also received death threats from John Gotti’s “Gambino” crime family when a porn advertising scam they hoped to run through Desmond’s magazines collapsed around them.

The Gambinos planned to put dodgy adverts in Desmond’s porn magazines, but the deal collapsed, according to FBI testimony used in the Gambino trial.

Satirists at Private Eye magazine dubbed Desmonds “Dirty Des.”

Desmond sold off his x-rated magazine titles for £20 million($36 million) in 2004, the same year he bought Dane Court.

Desmond also bought a second property on The Bishops Avenue, Fairways, which became the most expensive new-build home in the UK after it was listed for £22 million ($28 million) in 2014.

Details of Dane Court’s interior are not easy to come by. However, Desmond’s unusual style in his company boardroom has been well-documented.

He gets bananas delivered by his butler on a silver tray, rings a bell in meetings when someone makes a good point, and blows an old-fashioned car horn when it’s bad, Management Today reported.

Desmond declined to comment for this article through a representative.
