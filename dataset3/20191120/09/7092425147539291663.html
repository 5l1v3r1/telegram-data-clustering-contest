Jose Mourinho named as new Tottenham manager

20 Nov 2019, 09:34 by Buzz Contributor

Tottenham Hotspur have confirmed Jose Mourinho is their new head coach on a three-year deal after the shock sacking of Mauricio Pochettino last night.

Tottenham have confirmed Jose Mourinho will take charge in north London following Mauricio Pochettino's dismissal.

Mourinho has signed a contract until the end of the 2022/23 season.

Spurs sensationally sacked Pochettino yesterday after a series of poor results.

The 47-year-old had guided Spurs to the Champions League final last season.

But they underwhelmed in the Madrid showpiece and lost 2-0 to Liverpool.

Mourinho, who was axed by Manchester United 12 months ago, has now been tasked with reviving the club's fortunes.

“In Jose we have one of the most successful managers in football. He has a wealth of experience, can inspire teams and is a great tactician," Daniel Levy said.

"He has won honours at every club he has coached. We believe he will bring energy and belief to the dressing room.”

Having been given the nod, Mourinho explained that he's delighted to take charge.

“I am excited to be joining a club with such a great heritage and such passionate supporters," he said.

"The quality in both the squad and the academy excites me. Working with these players is what has attracted me.”

Mourinho's first game will be against West Ham at the London Stadium in east London on Saturday.

Spurs have not won an away fixture since January.

The Portuguese is one of the most decorated coaches in world football - although he struggled at Old Trafford.

The 56-year-old won league titles with Porto, Chelsea, Inter Milan and Real Madrid.

He is also one of only three managers to have bagged the Champions League twice with two clubs.

Mourinho won Europe’s elite competition with Porto in 2004 and Inter Milan six years later in 2010.
