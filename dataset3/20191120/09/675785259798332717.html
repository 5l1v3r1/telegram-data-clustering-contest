Granit Xhaka set for Arsenal exit as two clubs 'eye January loan transfer'

Granit Xhaka's future at Arsenal is in plenty of doubt with both Inter Milan and AC Milan interest in the Swiss midfielder

20 Nov 2019, 09:46 by Sam Meade

Granit Xhaka is eyeing a loan move away from Arsenal come January with Italy his likely destination.

Inter Milan and AC Milan are both interested in the Swiss midfielder whose future at the Gunners is up in the air.

Xhaka has also been linked with a move to Newcastle should he look to stay in England with Fabian Schar, his team-mate at international level, seen as a major player in convincing his compatriot to head north.

The midfielder hit back at fans when he was substituted against Crystal Palace last month which has caused a huge divide between them and the player.

The 27-year-old has not played since his public outburst and he is seeking a way out, but a move back to Borussia Monchengladbach has been ruled out.

It leaves the two Milan clubs in pole position to secure the midfielder's signature until the end of the season with an option to make the deal permanent, reports Sky .

And Unai Emery has been unable to confirm whether the former skipper will play for Arsenal again should he stay put.

“As a coach I have a responsibility for the team: we need a player like Xhaka. But also, I don’t know if he’s going to play [for us] again," Emery said after the incident.

Xhaka has endured a difficult relationship with the Arsenal fans since joining after Euro 2016 and issued a public statement after his outburst citing mass abuse suffered on social media as the reason behind his emotions boiling over.

His appointment as captain earlier this season was met with plenty of criticism.

The Swiss star won a vote to be given the armband, but Emery's reluctance to name him without seeking the approval of the players angered Xhaka.

Pierre-Emerick Aubameyang has since been given the armband at the Emirates.
