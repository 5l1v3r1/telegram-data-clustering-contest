MAC ATTACK

Amy Macdonald blasts ‘charlatan’ Jo Swinson for nuclear weapons comments after ITV leaders’ debate

20 Nov 2019, 09:40 by Ewan Mowat

AMY Macdonald has blasted Jo Swinson for her comments on using nuclear weapons in an interview after last night's ITV leaders' debate.

The Scots singer, 32, also branded the Lib Dem leader a "charlatan" for her time as East Dunbartonshire MP.

Last night saw the first in a series of head-to-head debates between party leaders in the run-up to the general election.

Labour chief Jeremy Corbyn faced off one-on-one against Tory leader Boris Johnson.

The SNP and Lib Dems were not part of the main debate after losing a legal battle to be included.

But party chiefs Nicola Sturgeon and Jo Swinson, as well as Nigel Farage of the Brexit Party and Greens co-leader Sian Berry, were interviewed live by host Nina Hossain afterwards.

The Lib Dem leader repeated her promise to revoke Article 50 if her party win on December 12 - and confirmed she would not put Corbyn or Johnson into power.

But it was her quick-fire response to a question about nuclear weapons that irked Macdonald.

When asked if she would be prepared to use nukes, Swinson swiftly replied: "yes.".

And the This Is The Life singer - who has had Swinson as her MP for more than a decade - was left fuming by her answer.

In a now-deleted tweet, she wrote: "Swinson has been my MP for pretty much my entire voting life. She has been a charlatan the entire time.

"It took her one second to say that she'd happily drop nuclear weapons. Imagine voting her her?!?

"Liberal?! Hang on till I drop my nukes. Then we'll talk."

Her tweet racked up around 1,000 likes and more than 300 retweets before the singer pulled it from the site.

And responding to another social media user's comment about Swinson, she said: "I actually can't believe it. It's madness.

"What's wrong with the world that it's an acceptable answer to say you'd happily kill thousands of people and potentially start a nuclear war without a second thought."

The Scottish Sun has approached the Lib Dems for comment.
