Kejriwal says Paswan misleading public on water, Vardhan hits back

CM raises question on BIS report authenticity; health minister claims water-borne diseases going up.

20 Nov 2019, 20:00 by Express News Service

Delhi Chief Minister Arvind Kejriwal Wednesday accused Union minister Ram Vilas Paswan of lying and deceiving people over the issue of water quality in the capital. The latest attack escalates the war of words over quality of water provided by the Delhi Jal Board, a department headed by Kejriwal, which broke out after a Bureau of Indian Standards report stated that Delhi’s water quality was the worst among 20 states. Earlier in the day, Paswan got the report hand-delivered to Kejriwal’s house, after the CM said he had not seen a copy of it.

As per the report, shared by Paswan, 11 samples were picked up from different parts of the city and they failed on all 19 parameters, including taste, colour, odour and suspended solids.
