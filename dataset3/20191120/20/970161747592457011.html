Why has I'm A Celebrity banned eating live bugs in Bushtucker Trials?

20 Nov 2019, 20:12

I’m A Celebrity bosses have banned campmates from consuming live insects as part of Bushtucker Trials.

Throughout the show’s history, celebrities have been faced with the challenge of eating creepy crawlies – but that’s all set to end.

Instead, campmates will now only be served up dead insects as part of the grisly trials, with no wiggly worms or crawling spiders allowed near the celebs’ mouths.

That means in Wednesday night’s Bushtucker Trial, which will see Adele Roberts and Roman Kemp tackle the eating challenge, they won’t have to catch their dinner before they can eat it.

Why has eating live bugs on I’m A Celebrity been banned?

Banning live bugs from being devoured on I’m A Celebrity is a decision that is said to have been discussed by producers for some time.

A show source told BBC Radio 1 Newsbeat: ‘I’m A Celebrity producers have taken a look at the trials and decided that no live critters would be eaten in the trials this year.

‘They have been planning this for some time and actually last year beach worms were the only critters eaten live but this time around they’ve decided to implement the change fully and permanently.’
