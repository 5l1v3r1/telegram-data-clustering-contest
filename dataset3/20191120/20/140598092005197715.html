Burnley claim unwanted top spot for reported hate crimes

20 Nov 2019, 20:39 by Telegraph Sport
Follow your club now for first access to all our news, views and analysis Burnley

Burnley supporters were reported for alleged hate crimes at football matches more often than any other club in England or Wales over the past two completed seasons, ­according to Home Office data.

There were 17 instances where the Premier League club were reported, while Manchester United, Sunderland and West Ham United were the subject of 13 reports each, a request under the Freedom of ­Information Act revealed.

Fifteen of the 17 reports concerning Burnley related to discrimination on the grounds of race, while two related to sexual orientation. 

However, the club believe their self-policing approach might ­account for the proliferation of the reports.

“Burnley is aware of the figure highlighted in the data and believes the number of instances reported reflects the club’s proactive stance on such issues,” a statement read. 

“As a club we operate a zero-tolerance policy towards any form of discrimination at Turf Moor and among our supporters at away matches.

“We actively encourage our supporters to report any relevant incidents, including those suspected from our own fans, which we believe is reflected by the number of reports made.”
