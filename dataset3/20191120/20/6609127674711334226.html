FOR KYLE

Rangers vs Hearts – Minute’s applause planned for 20th minute in honour of tragic Blantyre man Kyle Monteith

20 Nov 2019, 20:06 by Katy Pagan

RANGERS fans have planned a minute's applause during their clash with Hearts in honour of a tragic Blantyre man.

Kyle Monteith, who died on November 7, was a huge Light Blues supporter.

Now, an emotional tribute is set to be held in his honour during the 20th minute of next month's game at Ibrox.

Spreading the word on social media, Kyle's heartbroken girlfriend Elle Jean said: "Rangers vs Hearts game on the 1st December there will be a minute's applause on the 20th minute of the game in memory of Kyle Monteith."

It is understood officials from the club even reached out to Kyle's loved ones about the plans.

The touching gesture comes after mourners were asked to wear red, white and blue at his funeral on November 15.

Kyle's death was not treated as suspicious.

Speaking at the time, Elle Jean said: "Should not be helping plan your boyfriend's funeral at 20 years of age.

"Life is so f***ing cruel.

"Never felt heartache like this, if only he could see how loved he really was."

We told earlier how mourners gathered to release balloons in Kyle's memory.

It was reported that around 600 people congregated at Kirkton Park on November 9.

As the balloons were let go the park fell silent and then fireworks lit up the sky.

The crowd broke out into a lengthy applause as the display came to an end.
