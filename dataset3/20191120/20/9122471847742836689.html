AAC Commissioner Calls Out The CFB Playoff Selection Committee

20 Nov 2019, 20:51 by Tzvi Machlin

It’s no secret that the American Athletic Conference and the rest of the Group of Five conferences basically need a miracle to have a hope that one of their teams gets into the College Football Playoff in any given year.

But AAC commissioner Mike Aresco has gotten tired of seeing the teams in his conference disrespected with each College Football Playoff poll.

In a recent interview, Aresco said he questions the “methodology” and “conclusions” that the selection committee makes in their decision. He ultimately feels that the AAC and Group of Five are fighting a “double standard” in evaluating their teams.

Via ESPN:

“Each time I’ve watched the release of the rankings, I’ve seen an unfortunate predictability, and why wouldn’t I be upset? I’m not criticizing the committee personally, I’ve never done that, but what I’m doing is I’m trying to take the committee to task on their methodology and their conclusions. I want to point out the facts. My feeling is this conference has been undervalued and disrespected since the CFP began.”

“We’ve been fighting what I think is a P5 preference We’ve been fighting it for years and we’re fighting now a double standard when it comes to evaluating G5 teams.”

The AAC currently has three one-loss teams: Cincinnati, SMU and Memphis. All of them have at least one win over a Power Five team, and only one has a Power Five loss.

But even in the best possible campaigns from teams in the conference, it hasn’t led to College Football Playoff consideration.

In 2017 UCF went 12-0, winning the AAC title, but did not get into the Playoff over four one-loss teams. They ultimately proved their worth by upsetting Auburn in the Peach Bowl to finish 13-0.

UCF replicated the feat in 2018, going 13-0 with an AAC title, but were once again snubbed. This time, though, they lost in the Fiesta Bowl to LSU.

Regardless of the outcome in the Fiesta Bowl, if a Group of Five team can win 25 games in a row and still not get CFP consideration, that lends a lot of weight to Aresco’s claim of a double-standard.
