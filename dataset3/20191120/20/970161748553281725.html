Donald Trump defenders exchange worried glance over bombshell impeachment claims

20 Nov 2019, 20:15

A top Republican’s expression said everything viewers need to know about Wednesday’s bombshell impeachment testimony.

Senator Devin Nunes, the ranking Republican on the House Intelligence Committee, was caught on camera glaring at his party’s lawyer, Steve Castor, after US Ambassador to the EU Gordon Sondland’s explosive claims that ‘there was quid pro quo’ involving Donald Trump and Ukraine.

As the hearing broke up for a recess, Nunes, who has been Trump’s fiercest defender during the proceedings, gave Castor a wide-eyed look that appeared to convey either anger, panic, frustration, shock – or all of the above.

Sondland testified that he helped other US diplomats prepare a statement for Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelensky. It announced investigations into Trump’s political rival and 2020 Democratic presidential front-runner Joe Biden, his son Hunter, natural gas firm Burisma, and debunked conspiracies that Ukraine meddled in the 2016 election.
