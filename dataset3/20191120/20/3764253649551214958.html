Igbo community in Liverpool founded in 1935 celebrates their traditional culture

20 Nov 2019, 20:14

- The Igbo community in Liverpool founded in 1935 always comes together to celebrate their culture with food, dress, and music

- The community makes great effort in making sure that their culture is not lost despite the fact that they are far away from home

- With a community building that was acquired in 1988, the Igbos pass the uniqueness of their roots from generations to generations

Igbos in the UK have created a hub to keep celebrating their culture and ensuring that it is passed on from generations to generations even though they are far from home.

It is in this vein that the Igbo community created a group called Igbo Community in Liverpool which was founded in 1935.

In a video interview with the BBC, the head of the community, Chief Angus Chukwuemeka, said when they get together, it is like home from home as they eat the food peculiar to the culture, dance to their music, and dress in Igbo attire.
