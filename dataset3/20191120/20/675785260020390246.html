Lorraine Kelly shares miscarriage pain and 'struggles in life like anyone else'

Telly favourite Lorraine Kelly summarises on life as she approaches her big birthday later this month and says she'd love to go on SAS: Who Dares Wins

20 Nov 2019, 20:57 by Nick Harding, Gabrielle Fagan

Unlike many of her ITV colleagues, Lorraine Kelly vows she’ll never eat a kangaroo’s nether regions in the jungle or dance with Anton du Beke. Or impress a panel with her baking.

There is one exception to her blanket ban on reality TV, though.

“The only one I’d love to go on is SAS: Who Dares Wins,” Lorraine says. “I think I’d be gritty enough to cope.”

Lorraine turns 60 this month and that grittiness, and the love of husband Steve Smith, has got her through hard times.

“I’ve had struggles just like everyone else,” admits the TV presenter, who has a daughter, Rosie, 25 with Steve.

The couple weathered the tragedy of a miscarriage in 2001.

“It was very hard,” recalls Lorraine. “We had very much wanted another baby but it wasn’t to be.”

“I wanted to go back to work to get routine back, but I went far too soon.”

And a few years ago Lorraine entered the menopause and struggled to cope.

“It started when I was around 54 and got gradually worse until it was unbearable. I just wasn’t enjoying life and was constantly knackered.

“I found it hard to get out of bed. I had to paint on a smile.”

Eventually cameraman Steve told her to seek help and she went to TV health expert Dr Hilary Jones.

“The hormonal imbalance had triggered anxiety,” reveals Lorraine. “It was such a relief to know what was wrong.”

Steve’s support is key to Lorraine’s happiness, she admits.

“I’ve been very lucky to find somebody that gets me, puts up with me and makes me laugh,” she says.

“He’s a good man. We are a proper partnership which is totally equal – except he takes the bins out.”

Now she has written a self-help book, Shine: Discover a Brighter You, in which she gives advice on everything from ageing to self-confidence.

And she refuses to hanker after her youth, saying: “I’m proud to be 60. I have got a lot more comfortable in my skin.”
