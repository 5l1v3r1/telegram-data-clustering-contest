Popular Bold Street brand Utility show off huge new premises after store closure

Fans of the brand were gutted when they announced the closure of one city centre store - but it's not all bad news

20 Nov 2019, 20:21 by Jenny Kirkham

Popular brand Utility have shown off their huge new premises in Liverpool city centre.

Fans of the independent store, which opened on Bold Street in 2004, were gutted when the company announced they would be shutting one of their stores at the end of August.

At the time, shoppers were told the store would be moving to a larger, more suitable premises, by the end of the year.

Today, Utility have revealed that new store will be located inside a former warehouse on Flint Street in the Baltic Triangle.

It is expected to be open to the public by spring 2020.

A spokesperson for Utility told the ECHO: "Liverpool based design store Utility has purchased new premises in the city’s Baltic Triangle.

"The Flint Street building comprises a two storey brick built former warehouse in Liverpool’s creative hub.

"The building will be the new home to the company’s furniture and lighting store as well as their growing web team.

Director of Utility, Richard Skelton, said: “We have been on the hunt for our own building in the city which is more suitable to our current needs.

"Our new building – which has a working title of ‘The Flint’ will not be a furniture showroom or store in the conventional sense.

"Not only are we creating a home for our web team, we are also expanding our contract team to serve our growing number of contract clients.

"The building will give us a home to host events, meeting for architects and designers, product launches and so on. We want to create a space which lends itself to anything and – going back to our Coffee Union roots, which we sold in 2002 – the building will also a have a coffee shop,  as well as a potential element of co-working space to rent.

"This is a really exciting project for us; it’s a big building for which we have big plans.”

Established in 1999, Utility is an independent Liverpool brand with currently two stores on Bold Street, one in Liverpool ONE and another on Oxford Road in Manchester.

Talking back in August about the decision to close one store, Richard Skelton, director of Utility, said: "We have long outgrown our Bold Street furniture store and it’s no longer fit for purpose in the modern retailing landscape.

"38% of our turnover is now online and we are seeking space complementary to our online offer where customers can see larger furniture ranges and view a more comprehensive offer."
