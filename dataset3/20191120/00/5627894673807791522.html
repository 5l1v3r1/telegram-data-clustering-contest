Camp Fire clean-up reaches milestone

20 Nov 2019

PARADISE  (INT)  The fire-stricken Northern California community of Paradise has reached a milestone in its recovery.

It was announced Tuesday on the 1-year anniversary of the devastating Camp Fire that all debris has been removed from 11,000 properties.

The clean-up was the largest of its type in state history. Crews removed more than 3.66 million tonsor 7.3 billion poundsof ash, debris, metal, concrete, and contaminated soil. The total tonnage removed from the Camp Fire is equivalent to 10 Empire State Buildings.

It took 9-months under Californias Consolidated Debris Removal Program.
