Whether You’re Naughty Or Nice, Miracle Bar Atlanta Is The Holiday Pop-Up You Can’t Miss

20 Nov 2019 by Danielle Harling

Holiday season is upon us and so is the stress that merrily comes with gift shopping, party planning and overall fa la la la la’ing. Thankfully, a series of pop-up bars is offering a boozy reprieve from the cheery chaos. Returning for the fourth year is Miracle Bar Atlanta, a Christmas-themed pop-up that features over-the-top holiday decor and cocktails like the Gingerbread Flip and the Yippie Ki Yay Mother F****r! Consisting of three locations (Miracle On Monroe, Miracle On Highland and Sippin’ Santa at Bon Ton), Miracle Bar Atlanta will open its doors on the week of November 25.With three years under its belt and three locations, Miracle Bar Atlanta has become an anticipated attraction in the city, according to Eric Simpkins, co-founder of Big Citizen.

“If we’ve learned anything the past three years, it’s that Atlanta is overflowing with Christmas spirit,” said Eric Simpkins. “With our fourth year here, we’re excited to once again host the best Christmas bar pop-ups in the city, and we hope everyone comes out for many more holiday seasons to come.”

This year’s menu will include a slew of favorites, as well as a handful of new sips. Among the new additions on the menu at Miracle On Monroe and Miracle On Highland is the SanTaRex cocktail and the Mistletoe shot. A potential new favorite for the apple cider crowd, the SanTaRex is a lush-worthy combination of whiskey, oloroso sherry, spiced apple cider concentrate, lemon juice, hard cider and baked apple bitters. Another whiskey offering, the Mistletoe is a shot-sized portion of gingerbread spiced rye.

In addition to holiday-themed cocktails, Miracle On Monroe will offer Christmas-themed beer, wines and non-alcoholic drinks like hot tea and apple cider.

While Miracle On Monroe and Miracle On Highland will primarily serve the same set of cocktails, Miracle on Monroe also features a special Santa’s Snacks and Elf Workshop Plates food menus. Comprised of small, bar bites, the Elf Workshop Plates include the Merry Little Burger, Frosty Fries, Another Cheesy Christmas Bun and several other options.

Sippin’ Santa, which is located at Bon Ton, a Louisianan-Vietnamese fusion restaurant in Midtown Atlanta, will boast its own unique food and holiday-inspired drink menu.

For more information on opening dates, hours of business and reservations, visit the Miracle Bar Atlanta website.
