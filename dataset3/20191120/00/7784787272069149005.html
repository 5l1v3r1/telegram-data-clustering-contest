It’s a ‘truly historic and landmark day’ for the Dogs. Now all eyes are on seven rival clubs

20 Nov 2019, 00:08 by Ben Waterworth

The Western Bulldogs have become the latest AFL club to officially step away from the gaming industry, finalising a deal to sell their two venues in a bold statement to seven rival clubs.

Club chief executive Ameet Bains revealed on Wednesday the licences for Club Leeds in Footscray and the Peninsula Club in Dromana — which hold 65 gaming machines across both venues — had recently been purchased by the Melbourne Racing Club, with settlements to occur over coming weeks.

In a passionate video to club members and supporters, Bains thanked club president Peter Gordon and the board for their “vision and resolve” in pursuing the outcome, as well as the Dogs’ financial team for their diligence.
