WAIT CRISIS?

Scottish Government rapped as number of patients kept waiting 12 hours in A&E rockets to 215 a week

20 Nov 2019, 00:06 by Katrine Bussey

THE number of patients kept waiting over 12 hours in A&E has rocketed to 215 a week, latest figures show.

The shock stat was up from 117 for the previous seven days.

Another 793 had to hang around for more than eight hours in the week ending November 10.

The Scottish Government’s target of having 95 per cent of patients dealt with inside four hours has not been hit since August 2017.

Lib Dem MSP Alex Cole-Hamilton said Health Secretary Jeane Freeman made “big promises” on tackling waiting times but “can’t escape the fact the SNP Government hasn’t met the A&E target for years”.

Mr Cole-Hamilton, the party’s health spokesman, added: “To offer patients a bright future, staff must have the resources they need to do their job.”

Just 84.6 per cent of people were seen in under four hours — the lowest rate since March last year. It meant a whopping 4,338 people spent over four hours in A&E.

The largest health board, NHS Greater Gla- sgow and Clyde, hit the target. in just 80.9 per cent of cases.

An SNP Government spokeswoman said: “Scotland’s core A&E departments have been the best performing in the UK for more than four years despite high attendance levels.”
