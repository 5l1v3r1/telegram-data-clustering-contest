Swedish TV: U.S. investigates Swedbank over violations of Russia sanctions

20 Nov 2019 by BNN

With U.S. and European Union’s sanctions banned from cooperating with Russian weapon maker Kalashnikov, Swedbank has been involved in sending large sums between the shareholders of the Russian firm and the owner of Kalashnikov USA, Swedish television SVT reports.

According to a report published this week in the news portal of the SVT, Swedbank is now under investigation by U.S. financial authorities.

Swedish journalists also wrote that the business network of Russian oligarch Iskander Mahkmudov – shareholder of Kalashnikov Group in Russia and the biggest client of Swedbank Estonia, have transferred over 1 million euros to counterparts at Kalashnikov USA.

According to the report, the payments were sent in four transactions, from companies registered in the tax haven of the British Virgin Islands and the money was deposited in accounts at Bank of America controlled by key people at Kalashnikov USA.

Comment by Swedbank

In a press release on Wednesday, November 20, Jens Henriksson, President and CEO of Swedbank, said: «It is a good thing that the bank is being investigated and I welcome [SVT news programme – BNN] Uppdrag granskning’s reporting».

The bank went on to write that was not aware of any U.S. financial watchdog OFAC’s violations arising out of the continuing Swedbank’s internal investigation, which would end in 2020.
