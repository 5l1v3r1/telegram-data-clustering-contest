Indoor marijuana growers cant hide, seven arrested

20 Nov 2019

JURUPA VALLEY  (INT)  Marijuana eradication teams say they are waging a full court press on those bent on converting rented homes for indoor marijuana cultivation.

Search warrants were served this week at three homes in Jurupa Valley and neighboring Eastvale.

The results included 2,632 marijuana plants with an estimated full harvest street value of $4.1 million.

Seven people were arrested on suspicion of illegal marijuana cultivation and possession with intent to sell. Four of the suspects were residents of Los Angeles County.

Indoor marijuana cultivation in Eastvale, the Inland Empires newest city, has become well-known in recent years.
