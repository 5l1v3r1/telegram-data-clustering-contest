Family of elderly victim plead for help after brutal attack at Sydney retirement village

20 Nov 2019, 00:03 by Jenny Noyes

Warning: Graphic imagery

The daughter of an 84-year-old woman who was brutally assaulted in the bedroom of her retirement village home on Saturday is joining police in pleading for help to find her mother's attacker.

The elderly woman, named by police as Patricia, was found with multiple serious injuries and lacerations to her face, chest and arm after emergency services were called to her unit in Sydney's upper north shore on Bobbin Head Road, North Turramurra, about 12.30pm on Saturday.

Police were told Patricia had arrived home to find an unknown man in her bedroom, and that he attacked her before fleeing out the front door.

Patricia was taken to Sydney Adventist Hospital by ambulance, where on Wednesday she remains in a serious but stable condition.

Her daughter Caroline will join police on Wednesday in making a public appeal for assistance to find the person responsible for her mother's injuries.

Following the assault, a crime scene was established and the scene was forensically examined. Detectives from Kuring-Gai Police Area Command established Strike Force Calculus to investigate the assault and the break-in.

Strike force detectives conducted an extensive canvass of the local area on Tuesday, which included speaking with residents within the retirement village.

They would like to speak with an unknown man who was seen in the area at the time of the incident. He is described as being aged between 30 and 50, about 175-180cm tall, possibly of Asian or Indian sub-continental appearance, with a medium build, brown eyes, dark hair, and clean shaven.

The man was wearing light-coloured pants and a jacket over a button-up shirt and tie at the time of the incident, police said.

Detectives would also like to speak with anyone who may have noticed anything suspicious in the area and have asked motorists who travelled through the area to review dash-cam vision.
