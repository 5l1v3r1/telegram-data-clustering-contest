Germany, Belgium & Dutch Win Big In Qualifiers

20 Nov 2019, 00:27 by SL International

Belgium 6-1 Cyprus

Belgium sealed a perfect qualifying campaign with a 6-1 demolition of Cyprus and topped Group I with maximum points courtesy of two goals each from at Stade Roi Baudouin.

Christian Benteke and Kevin De Bruyne both bagged two goals each while Yannick Carrasco and an own goal from Kypros Christoforou also got on the score-sheet for the hosts , while Nikolas Ioannou scored the only goal for Cyprus.

Germany 6-1 Northern Ireland

Germany clinched top spot in Group C after thumping Northern Ireland 6-1 thanks to a hat-trick by Serge Gnabry and a brace by Leon Goretzka at Commerzbank Arena.

Serge Gnabry grabbed the headlines after bagging a ha-trick his hat-trick on the hour-mark, while a brace from Leon Goretzka and a Julian Brandt strike rounded off the thrashing in stoppage time. Michael Smith got the Northern Ireland's only goal.

Wales 2-0 Hungary

Wales sealed a spot in the 2020 European Championship after beating Hungary 2-0 thanks to a brace from Juventus star Aaron Ramsey.

Ryan Giggs' charges qualify as the second best team in Group E with 14 points, three points behind group winners Croatia.

San Marino 0-5 Russia

Russia confirmed second spot in Group I with a 5-0 walloping of San Marino at Stadio Olimpico Di Serravalle.

Daler Kuzyaev, Sergey Petrov, Aleksey Miranchuk, Aleksey Ionov and Nikolay Komlichenko were all on target as Russia cruised to victory, while San Marino lost all their matches in the group.

Poland 3-2 Slovenia

Poland, who had long secured their ticket to the 2020 tournament, confirmed top spot in Group G with a 3-2 victory over Slovenia at Stadion Narodowy.

Sebastian Szymanski, Robert Lewandowski and a winner from Jacek Goralski gave Poland the three points to win Group G six points clear, while goals from Tim Matavz and Josip IIicic and red-card for Jasmin Kurtic saw Slovenia fail to qualify in fourth place.

Netherlands 5-0 Estonia

Netherlands qualified as the second best team in Group C behind Germany after thrashing Estonia 5-0 thanks at Johan Cruijff Arena.

Georginio Wijnaldum's hat-trick was complemented by goals from Nathan Ake and Myron Boadu as the Dutch ran out emphatic winners on the night.
