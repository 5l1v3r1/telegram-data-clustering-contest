3 easy Thanksgiving Brussels sprouts recipes from NYC chefs

20 Nov 2019, 00:25 by Marisa Dellatto

This Thanksgiving, it’s all about the sprouts.

Back in the day, no foodie would dream of serving up controversial, cruciferous Brussels sprouts at their annual feast. That’s because people used to misguidedly boil the leafy spheres — a technique that emphasized their bitter flavors and created a stench that’d put anyone off their dinner. But a few years ago, chefs had a collective aha moment and started preparing the maligned veggies differently: roasted, thin-shaved, stir-fried. Now diners can’t get enough of these green queens.

Here, NYC restaurants share their crowd-pleasing recipes for the star sprout.

Ilili’s creamy Lebanese Brussels sprouts

Trim and halve 1.25 pounds of Brussels sprouts; toss in oil and roast cut-side down at 500 degrees until crispy, about 20 minutes. Salt to taste and coat sprouts with 4 tablespoons sherry vinegar and 1/2 cup fig jam. In a bowl, combine 1 cup yogurt, 1 tablespoon minced mint leaves and 3/4 teaspoon salt. Spoon yogurt sauce over sprouts and top with 1/2 cup of halved grapes and 1/2 cup crushed walnuts.

Don Angie’s spicy Italian Brussels sprouts

Sautee the cloves of 1 head of garlic in a small pan over low heat until golden brown, about 10 minutes; set aside garlic and let cool. Trim and halve 4 cups Brussels sprouts, toss in olive oil and salt and roast cut-side down on a baking sheet at 400 degrees, about 20 minutes. Meanwhile, in a blender, combine cooked garlic with 1 cup lemon juice, 3/4 cup sugar, 1 tablespoon crushed chilies in oil, 2 tablespoons honey, 1/4 cup sriracha and 1/4 cup colatura, an Italian anchovy-based fish sauce (at specialty grocers). Pour dressing over roasted sprouts and toss.

Made Nice’s Brussels Caesar salad

Trim and halve 1 cup Brussels sprouts, toss with salt and olive oil and roast cut-side down at 400 degrees until golden brown, about 20 minutes. In a large bowl, toss sprouts with 1 bunch stemmed, chopped kale, 2 cups raw, shaved Brussels sprouts, 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese and 1 cup Caesar dressing. Garnish salad with Parmesan crisps (available at specialty stores or make your own).
