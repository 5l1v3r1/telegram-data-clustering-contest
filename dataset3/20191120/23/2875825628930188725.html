I'm a Celeb newbies Cliff Parisi and Andy Whyment face first Bushtucker Trial

A first look at the celeb jungle's newest recruits facing their first Bushtucker trial

20 Nov 2019, 23:04 by Irishmirror.ie

Two new celebs are joining the I'm a Celebrity camp tomorrow night, and they are being thrown in at the deep end.

Coronation Street actor Andy Whyment and EastEnders actor Cliff Parisi are entering the jungle for a Wild West themed trial.

Ol' Dingo Town is a special town built for the trial, and will see Andy and Cliff become outlaws.

Andy, who played Kirk Sutherland, and Cliff, who played Minty Peterson, will be dressed head to toe as cowboys.

Over the coming days, all Bushtucker Trials will take part in Ol' Dingo Town and there are plenty of twists and turns along the way.

Cliff admitted he was worried that the camp mates had already bonded and would ostracise him when he joined them.

He seemed ready for the jungle's critters, declaring: "Come on you spiders! Come on you crocodiles!"

Brave Andy claimed he did not have any fears or phobias, but neither did he have any survival skills.

Cliff has admitted that he initially turned down the job until he was shown a huge tax bill by his accountant, which changed his mind.

Cliff said: “What made me sign up? The taxman. I have got a bill I need to pay.

"Like everyone at this time of year, you go, ‘Oh no. Again...' You would think I earn a lot of money but I don’t.

"I work for the BBC, so it ain’t that big.

“I had a meeting about this earlier on in the year, and I thought about it long and hard and said no.

"And then as I got an estimate for my tax, I phoned them up and said, is it too late to say yes?

“So here I am. But it could be an amazing experience and I hope it is.

“Life throws up things like this for you, and if you don’t take them you are a bit of a mug.

So I give myself a bit of a talking to, and then I spoke to the accountant and he made my mind up.

What is the worse-case scenario? A big snake is gonna bite you or the taxman is gonna bite you. Which do you want?”

Andy is not perfectly equipped to take on the jungle liftstyle and trials. He admitted: “Last time I went camping was at V Festival '99 So I’ve not been camping since 2000 and that was actually in a tent.

"Now I’m actually in the outdoors in the Australian outback so that’s completely different.”

But he has some experience of creepy crawlies, and said: “You could come face to face with a spider or a snake but my brother actually had a tarantula when I was a kid so I’m used to holding spiders and stuff like that.

“I don’t mind snakes but if there’s a snake rearing up at you that’s a bit different.

"Another one I’m a bit wary about is if you have to put your hand in and you don’t know what’s in there.

"Like when Amir Khan had to put his hand in and pulled the snake out. But I’m going to have a go at everything. I am looking forward to it and just want to get in there and get on with it.”
