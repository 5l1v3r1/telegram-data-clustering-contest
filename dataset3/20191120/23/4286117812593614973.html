WATCH | Currency notes shower from Kolkata building as DRI conducts search

There was a startling shower of crisp currency notes from the sixth floor of a building in Kolkata when DRI officials were conducting a search in a private office on that floor.

20 Nov 2019, 23:50

HIGHLIGHTS

  • Currency notes started raining down a building in Kolkata on Wednesday
  • The incident took place when DRI officials were searching a private office
  • Bystanders started running around to collect Rs 2000, 500 and 100 notes

There was a startling shower of crisp currency notes from the sixth floor of a building in the city's central business district on Wednesday afternoon when DRI officials were conducting a search in a private office on that floor.

Bystanders made hay as bundles of currency notes of denominations of Rs 2000, Rs 500 and Rs 100 were seen being pushed out of a window in the sixth floor with the help of a broom.

DRI sources told PTI that its sleuths had gone to the office of a private company engaged in export-import activities located on that floor to conduct search operations for alleged evasion of duty.

"Later reinforcements were also sent," he said adding searches are on.
