27-year-old in Blaine Lake charged with identity theft and fraud

20 Nov 2019, 23:52 by Saskatoon StarPhoenix

Officers seized 200 stolen cheques and 144 personal identification cards — the majority being Saskatchewan health cards and driver's licences.

A 27-year-old from Blaine Lake, Sask. has been arrested and charged after a search warrant at a home uncovered a vast number of identification cards and stolen cheques.

Latecia Juba-Arcand faces a list of charges including assault causing bodily harm, identity theft, fraud, forgery, possession of stolen property obtained by crime and possession of a controlled substance.

According to an RCMP news release, officers from the Blaine Lake detachment received information on Sunday about a possible fraud and identity theft situation and after a search of Juba-Arcand’s home.

Officers seized 200 stolen cheques, stolen tools, a small amount of what is believed to be methamphetamine and 144 personal identification cards — the majority being Saskatchewan health cards and driver’s licences.

Juba-Arcand made a first appearance in Saskatoon provincial court on Tuesday.
