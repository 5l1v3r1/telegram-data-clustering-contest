Todd McShay Updates His Draft Grade For QB Tua Tagovailoa

20 Nov 2019, 23:43 by Chris Rosvoglou

Over the weekend Alabama quarterback Tua Tagovailoa suffered a dislocated hip that will not only end his season, but keep him out of action for roughly six-to-eight months. As a result, the talented prospect could see his stock go down as the 2020 NFL Draft approaches.

Prior to his injury, the thought around college football was that Tagovailoa would be the first quarterback taken off the board. Now, the top-rated quarterback on most draft boards is LSU’s Joe Burrow.

ESPN analyst Todd McShay made a few changes to his latest draft rankings due to Tagovailoa’s injury.

McShay revealed on Twitter that he lists Tagovailoa as the No. 13 prospect in his draft class. He originally had the Alabama gunslinger as the second-best prospect heading into last weekend.
