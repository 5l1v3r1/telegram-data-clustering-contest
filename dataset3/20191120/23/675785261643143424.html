I'm A Celebrity new arrival Cliff Parisi's plan to smuggle in contraband rumbled

Former EastEnders star Cliff Parisi had planned a unique way of making friends with the current I'm A Celebrity campmates as he fears they have all already bonded

20 Nov 2019, 23:37 by Kyle O'Sullivan, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/kyle-osullivan/

New I'm A Celebrity arrival Cliff Parisi had planned a genius way to make friends in the jungle by bringing in contraband.

But his cunning scheme has been rumbled - so he will have to get the current campmates on side in the traditional way.

The former EastEnders actor, who is filling up on a big breakfast before he enters the jungle, fears it will be a struggle to form friendships as a newbie.

Cliff said: "It is going to be tricky going in late though because they will have bonded and made friendships.

"How will I make friends with them? I am going to try and bring in a bag of sweets!"

The Call the Midwife is feeling guilty going into the camp late with a full stomach when everyone else will be starving.

He had a long think about taking part in the show - but realised he needs the money.

Cliff said: "I had always said ‘no’ to anything with the word ‘celebrity’ tagged to it,” he explains. “And I have avoided those shows like the plague!

"But everyone on last year’s series was so much fun and I loved watching it. I realised it would be great fun to do. And now I am going to do it, I am hoping it will be cathartic."

The Londoner jokes that "we don't have animals" and he is allergic to cats so may struggle with the critters.

He added: "I do think the trials will be scary but I will still give them a go. I will try my best to win the stars."

Cliff is a seasoned camper but think he may bore everyone with all of his stories.

He admits: "I will be the camp bore! I am looking forward to doing it now but I know it is going to be tough.

"When you have a crucial time in your life, you always have a cup of tea. To go without tea, no telly and not much food will be tough. It’s going to be hard being away from my creature comforts."

As for what his colleagues on Call the Midwife will think when they find out he is swapping the popular drama for snakes and rats, he says: "They will be screaming with delight once they find out. I know they won’t be disappointed and they will be rooting for me.

"In fact, I know they will be phoning up for me to do the trials! Doing I’m A Celebrity is going to be so much fun. It is going to be the best year!"
