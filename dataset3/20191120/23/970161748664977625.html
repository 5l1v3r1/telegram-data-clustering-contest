I'm A Celebrity's Ian Wright and Andrew Maxwell clash over crocodile feet

20 Nov 2019, 23:07

I’m A Celebrity…Get Me Out Of Here saw it’s second clash of the series – this time it was over crocodile feet – yes you heard that right.

Ian Wright and Andrew Maxwell had a little disagreement over a crocodile foot (which looked like a chicken wing), as Adele Roberts and Roman Kemp won the camp a luxury meal for collecting 10 stars during the bushtucker trial.

‘Everyone is a bit cautious on how they think their food should be cooked, if it was on the outside world I may get annoyed but you can’t in here,’ Myles Stephenson began.

But as the camp tucked into their meal, they all had something to say about it.

‘It’s a bit like chicken-fish,’ Adele Roberts described.

While Jacqueline Jossa added: ‘It was a mixture between pork and chicken but just quite hard and tough.’

It was when Jacqueline offered Ian an extra bit of ‘chicken’ when things got a bit heated.

‘Ian everyone has had some there’s two there, go,’ Andrew added.

He continued: ‘Come on seriously Ian.’

The former footballer calmly responded: ‘I honestly don’t want no more.’

It was when Andrew exclaimed: ‘Jesus the drama’ when Ian got a bit snappy (see what I did there) as he raged: ‘I said I don’t want no more f***ing crocodile.’

Roman Kemp and Adele Roberts had the first clash of the series over the iconic Jane McDonald photo, which Adele brought in as her luxury item.

Talking together in the main camp, Kate Garraway and Roman are talking about the picture, which Adele brought in as her ‘luxury item’.

While Adele’s girlfriend Kate Holderness has since cleared up why she brought in the framed photo, the campmates where nonethewiser.

But Kate saw the plus side, as Kate asked: ‘Jane McDonald is looking over us, can you think of a song of hers?’

Admitting she didn’t know any, Adele said: ‘I know her off Loose Women, I don’t know her actual songs but I know her from the cruising show when she does covers.’

Adele then feels cornered by Myles and Roman who laugh at her, with Roman declared: ‘No one knows what she does.’
