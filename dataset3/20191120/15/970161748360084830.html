Lil Nas X is just excited as we are as he reacts to six Grammy nods

20 Nov 2019, 15:09

Lil Nas X has had one heck of a year, and is now wrapping up with decade with six whole Grammy nominations.

It seems he’s just excited as we are over the news as he celebrated with his fans on Twitter.

Following the announcement, which saw him tie with Billie Eilish, while Lizzo earned eight nods, the 20-year-old was quick to take to his social media platforms.

With barely any explanation to his outburst, the singer (whose real name is Montero Lamar Hill), wrote: ‘NO F**KING WAY.’

And yes, the capitals are more than necessary.

Fans were quick to congratulate the star as they showered him with praise under his post.

‘Congrats King. I’m so proud of you!!!’ one wrote while another added: ‘GRAMMY NOMINATED NAS IS WHAT THEY CALLIN YOU NOW.’

Someone else said: ‘Congrats bro, proud to see how far you’ve come in such a short time.’

The Panini hitmaker has been entered for both his single and album, including record of the year for Old Town Road and album of the year for 7.

He joins the likes of Lizzo who is steaming ahead of the competition with an impressive eight nominations to her name including album of the year for Cuz I Love You, best new artist and song of the year for Truth Hurts.

Lewis Capaldi has also received a nomination for song of the year with Someone You Loved.

Alicia Keys helped do the honours of announcing the list of nominations, on Wednesday, as she’s set to host the awards for the second year in a row at the Staples Center in Los Angeles on 27 January.

The 38-year-old revealed she would be presenting the prestigious ceremony earlier this year in a statement.

Here she said: ‘At first I did think last year was a one-time thing but when the opportunity came back around there was no question about returning as host of the GRAMMY Awards.

‘Last year was such a powerful experience for me. Not only did I feel the love in the room, but I felt it from around the world and it confirmed the healing and unifying power of music.’
