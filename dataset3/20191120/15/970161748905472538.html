Jose Mourinho instructs Tottenham to sign Lille sporting director Luis Campos

20 Nov 2019, 15:28

Tottenham are set to begin talks with Lille to sign highly respected sporting director Luis Campos, a long-time friend of Jose Mourinho credited with unearthing a host of superstars.

The Portuguese coach was officially announced as Mauricio Pochettino’s successor on Wednesday morning, with the Argentine dismissed just five months after the Champions League final following a poor start to the campaign.

Mourinho has already raided Lille twice to bring in new assistant Joao Sacramento and goalkeeping coach Nuno Santos, with the French side confirming the news.

But Spurs may not stop there, with ESPN reporting that Mourinho wants the club to prise Campos away from Lille and head up recruitment at Tottenham.

The two men are close friends and, during his time out of work, Mourinho frequently made the trip from London to Lille to watch the Ligue 1 side in the company of Campos.

He was a key scout under Mourinho at Real Madrid before becoming sporting director at Monaco, where he oversaw an impressive recruitment drive that culminated in a run to the Champions League semi-final.

He bought the likes of Kylian Mbappe, Fabinho, Bernardo Silva, James Rodriguez and Benjamin Mendy, turning over massive profits while remaining competitive on the pitch.

That continued at Lille, making massive profits on Nicolas Pepe and Rafael Leao over the summer after assembling a side that finished second in Ligue 1 last season and qualified for the Champions League.

Mourinho wanted Campos at Manchester United and is eager for a reunion, while Spurs are believed to have been keen to appoint such a figure while Pochettino was in charge but he rejected the idea.

Campos has previously said he would be open to moving to the Premier League – and even working with Mourinho again – if the project was right for him.
