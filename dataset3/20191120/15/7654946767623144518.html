Tiny, ‘New York City-sized’ Cracker Barrel rolls into Manhattan for Thanksgiving

20 Nov 2019, 15:44 by Christopher Cameron

Cracker Barrel, America’s favorite country-fried stop along the interstate, is celebrating a big milestone with a little country store in NYC, ahead of the Macy’s Thanksgiving Day Parade.

The Lebanon, Tenn.-based restaurant chain — known for antique-covered walls, peg games and biscuits — is honoring its 50th anniversary with a 269-square-foot, “New York City-sized” Cracker Barrel store that will appear in the holiday parade, according to Fox News.

The functioning “Tiny Home Away from Home” will feature Southern fixin’s like a front porch, rocking chairs, a glowing fireplace and even reclaimed wood from the first Cracker Barrel store, which debuted in Tennessee on Sept. 19, 1969. The day before the parade, the mini-Barrel will set up shop in NYC’s Foley Square for city folk to check out.

“As we celebrate our golden anniversary, this is a big moment for Cracker Barrel, and we wanted to do something extra special to mark our first-ever appearance in the Macy’s Thanksgiving Day Parade by creating a miniature Cracker Barrel experience that recalls the unique atmosphere of hospitality enjoyed by guests,” Cracker Barrel Vice President of Marketing Jeff Sigel said in a statement.

Alas, Cracker Barrel does not actually have a location in New York, so fans will want to take the opportunity to stop by the little ol’ country store to pick up vintage toys, as well as packaged foods.
