When is Sunderland vs Coventry, is it on TV, can you still buy tickets and who is in the teams?

The Black Cats are back in League One action and face a vital game at the Stadium of Light ahead third-place City

20 Nov 2019, 15:12 by Stuart Jamieson

After four cup games - all without a win - Sunderland get back to the bread and butter of their League One campaign.

And it couldn't be a bigger fixture with third-placed Coventry City at the Stadium of Light for a vital match.

Promotion is, of course, the only target for Phil Parkinson and Co and, with games in hand, wins on Saturday and against Burton Albion next Tuesday, could change the picture dramatically.

Here's all you need to know.

What are the injury concerns? Who is in the teams?

Sunderland will have Jon McLaughlin, Tom Flanagan, Conor McLaughlin and Benji Kimpioka back from international duty, but face a wait of several injury concerns. Jordan Willis, Joel Lynch and George Dobson sat out the FA Cup defeat at Gillingham, while Lynden Gooch and Charlie Wyke are nearing fitness.

Sunderland likely XI: Burge; C McLaughlin, Ozturk, Lynch, Hume; Power, Dobson, Watmore, Maguire, McGeady, Grigg.

Coventry likely XI: Marosi; Mason, Rose, Hyam, Dabo, Drysdale; Walsh, Westbrooke, Shipley, J Eccles; Bakayoko.

Who is the ref?

Scott Oldham

It's the first time referee Oldham has taken charge of the Black Cats this season. He was last on Wearside for the 1-1 draw with Burton Albion last April, while he also officiated the draw at Oxford in February, also a 1-1 scoreline.
