Spanish media slam Gareth Bale for 'Wales. Golf. Madrid' flag

20 Nov 2019, 15:46 by James Cox

Gareth Bale's days at Real Madrid appear to be numbered after he posed with a flag captioned, 'Wales. Golf. Madrid', following Wales' 2-0 victory over Hungary last night.

Bale, 30, has won three Champions League titles among other honours in his six years at the Bernabeu but he's not a popular figure in Madrid.

This isn't helped by the fact he's openly admitted he prefers playing for Wales, while his passion for golf is often used as another criticism of the winger.

Former Real star Pedja Mijatovic underline this when he criticised Bale on Spanish radio, claiming that the former Tottenham star's priorities were Wales, golf, Madrid, in that order.

Bale didn't help matters when he admitted to finding chants about it amusing, and he's sparked fury in Spain by posing with the flag after Wales secured Euro 2020 qualification.

The majority of readers in a MARCA poll stated that Bale showed "a lack of respect for the club and fans".

The popular Spanish outlet added that the relationship between Bale and Real manager Zinedine Zidan was "broken for good".

The headline in AS read:  "Bale is 'mocking' Madrid with the 'Wales. Golf. Madrid' flag."

Meanwhile, SPORT suggested Bale should be punished by the club.

Real are yet to comment on the incident.

Bale's relationship with Zidane has been poor for some time now and he came close to joining Chinese club Jiangsu Suning in the summer transfer window.

Bale was reportedly set to earn £1m-a-week in the Chinese Super League.
