MISSISSAUGA, Ont. — A man has died after an officer used a stun gun on him west of Toronto, police said Wednesday, prompting Ontario's police watchdog to launch an investigation.

Peel Regional Police said they were called to a home in Mississauga, Ont., around 3 a.m. for reports of a suspicious person in the area.

Const. Akhil Mooken said the officers found a man in a backyard.

"Officers located the male and had an interaction, at which point a conducted energy weapon was deployed," Mooken told reporters at the scene.

He said the man was taken to a local hospital, where he died shortly after 4 a.m.

The Special Investigations Unit, a civilian agency that looks into cases where there is a death, injury or allegation of sexual assault involving police, has invoked its mandate.

This is the second stun-gun death in Mississauga in recent months.

The SIU is also investigating the death of a 34-year-old man who died after police used a stun gun on him on Sept. 10.

In that case, the watchdog said Peel police officers responded to a domestic call in the Malton area around 9 p.m.

The SIU said the "officers became involved in an interaction with a man which involved the use of a conducted energy weapon."

The agency said the man stopped breathing, his heart stopped beating and he was later pronounced dead in hospital.

This report by The Canadian Press was first published Nov. 20, 2019.
