How to keep your robotic vacuum from wandering out of control

20 Nov 2019, 13:42 by Consumer Reports and Connie Thompson

Smart vacuums might be good at picking up dirt, but they're not always so smart when it comes to getting out of tight places. So how do you keep your robotic vacuum from wandering into uncharted territory?

Some robotic vacuums come with magnetic strips that you place along doorways to keep the robot from crossing. Others come with a device that creates a virtual boundary using infrared beams to keep the robot in or out. Newer models come with an app that lets you create boundaries with a swipe of your finger.

Other ways to keep a robot from roaming? Make sure the docking station is placed against a wall with a couple of feet of open space around it. If you move the station, the robot may have difficulty finding it and could wander around your house until its battery dies.

CR tests the ability of robotic vacuums to pass over rug fringe without getting tangled. But thick bathroom mats should be avoided. And even pros can find a glitch by chance. A CR tester noticed that one model was missing from its dock every morning. It turns out the robot was unintentionally set to clean every morning at 4 a.m. It just took a couple of clicks on the remote to get it back on schedule.
