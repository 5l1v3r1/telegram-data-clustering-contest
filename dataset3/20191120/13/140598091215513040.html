What to put in a homemade Christmas hamper for 2019

20 Nov 2019, 13:52 by Saffron Alexander, Tomé Morrissy-Swan

Christmas hampers, packed to the brim with indulgent treats you otherwise wouldn't consider purchasing outside the festive season, are a seasonal tradition for many of us. Stores like Fortnum & Mason report record sales years thanks, at least in part, to the continued popularity of their luxury hampers, and the trend looks set to continue.

But hampers, filled with the best festive and seasonal goodies on the market, don't always come cheap – Fortnum's Classic Christmas Hamper retails at £175 while their Imperial Hamper demands a staggering £6,000 investment. At ultra luxe retailers, like Harrods, you'll be hard pressed to wine and dine a family without spending a few hundred of the Queen's English. 

So, if you've blown your budget on gifts (we Brits, on average, spend much more on Christmas than our European neighbours) or you're simply looking for a personal way to let someone know you care this holiday season, why not go the homemade route and make a hamper yourself? With a little time and effort, it could save you a lot of money.

For more inspiration, see our best hampers guide for Christmas 2019.

The basket

The presentation of your hamper is just as important as the contents. Choose a nice wicker hamper – it doesn't need to be expensive – and fill it with shredded tissue paper (£2.86, Amazon) or straw from your local pet shop (£2.49, Pets at Home), before topping it all off with a festive bow (£1.50, Paperchase).

1. 'Build Your Own' hamper

£7, The Works

This wicker tray from The Works comes with a tartan bow, cellophane wrap and wood wool filling to make your hamper look like it was made by a professional.

2. Personalised Hamper by DIBOR

£33, Not on the High Street

This country-style hamper is woven with natural willow and will look absolutely gorgeous filled with colourful Christmas treats. Leather straps also keep all of your contents secure, for practical and presentational use. 

The food

Christmas hampers typically contain tasty food items; seasonal favourites like mince pies, Christmas pudding, spiced preserves, biscuits, chocolate, and cheese. Among the sweets and savouries, many also contain bottles of wine or, for non-alcoholic hampers, tea, coffee or cocoa.

Mince pies

1. Gluten Free Mince Pies

£2 for two, Waitrose

The Telegraph taste testers said: "These palm-sized tarts should satisfy all the family, free-from or no. The pastry is crisp and moreish, the mincemeat sharp with cranberries and sweet, releasing a citrus scent. Hard to tell they are gluten-free."

2. Finest All-Butter Mince Pies 

£2 for six, Tesco

The Telegraph taste testers said: "Packed full with mincemeat that’s gloriously glossy and fiercely sweet, finishing with a sharp orange note. The pastry is firm and finished nicely with a sprinkle of sugar."

Yule logs

Yule logs always go down a treat in the Christmas hamper and the Telegraph taste testing team thought these chocolatey treats were some of the very best.

1. Daylesford Organic Chocolate & Orange Yule Log

£18, Abel & Cole

The Telegraph food writers said: "Impressing from the off with an intense aroma and mirror glaze, this has the strongest chocolate taste, delivered via layers of red-velvet-esque sponge, shot through with sharp citrus curd."

2. Woodland Yule Log

£15, M&S

Marks and Sparks has really outdone themselves this year. This all-chocolate yule log can not only double as a Christmas cake, serving up to 10, but all of it can be eaten - even the woodland decor.

Christmas pudding

1. Heston from Waitrose Golden Delicious Christmas Pudding

£16, Waitrose

As you'd expect Heston has put another twist on the Christmas pudding - this time, adding fresh apple, cider, apple brandy and pine nuts, plus there's a honey and apple brandy sauce centre. 

2. The Collection Perfectly Matured Christmas Pudding

£12, Marks & Spencer

This is an intensely fruity, classic pudding enriched with brandy, cognac, port and stout. It's best served steamed, of course, but you can get it from microwave to plate in under three minutes.

Biscuits

What Christmas hamper is complete without a few suitably spiced biscuits sprinkled throughout?

1. Christmas Spiced Biscuits

£5.95, Fortnum & Mason

These biscuits are made with ginger, nutmeg and cloves, so are basically Christmas in a tin. Delicious.

2. Christmas Pudding Biscuits

£8, Whittard

Christmas pudding in biscuit form? Yes, please. Imagine these dunked into a fresh cup of coffee.

3. Spiced Christmas Shortbread Box

£7.50, Bettys

Bring the cosiness of a Bettys tearoom into their larder with these spiced shortbread biscuits from the Yorkshire bakers. With stem ginger and cinnamon, they'll make tea time just a bit more seasonal. 

4. Christmas Stained Glass Gingerbread Stars

£5.99, Waitrose and Partners

The adorable and truly scrumptious gingerbread biscuits can be enjoyed with a cup of cocoa. Perfect for Christmas, they can even be hung on your tree with some ribbon.

Smoked salmon

1. Chestnut Smoked Scottish Salmon

£5.99, Waitrose

The team said: "Warming but medium-strength smokiness through flesh that is not at all oily, with a buttery bite. The deep, dark colour has a nice glow at the edges, making this stand-out salmon a good choice for Christmas morning."

2. Speybay Oak-Smoked Scottish Lochmuir Salmon

£10, Marks & Spencer

The taste testers said: "Narrow slices and neat, too, making for easy serving. The smell is immediately sweet, and the fish is rich and luxurious to eat, slightly saline, with a mild please- all smoke."

Drinks

1. Taste the Difference Gavi 2017, Italy

12%, Sainsbury’s, £8

2. Paul Mas Reserve Grenache-Syrah-Carignan 2017 Languedoc, France

13.5%, Waitrose, £6.99 down from £9.39 until Jan 1

Moore said: "A Rhône-like old favourite that is very hard to beat when on offer. Generous and reassuring, with a whiff of figs, dried herbs and raspberry jelly, and a touch of oak spice." 

3. Les Pionniers Champagne Brut NV, France

12%, Co-op, £18.99

"Year in, year out, this outstanding supermarket champagne puts others in the shade. A magnificent blend of pinot noir (50 per cent), pinot meunier (30 per cent) and chardonnay (20 per cent) made by Piper Heidseick," says Moore. 

Preserves

Preserves are a quick and easy way to ward off the winter chill and add some flavour to your Christmas hamper.

1. Cottage Delight Gin Preserve Cracker

£8.95, John Lewis & Partners

Gin is having a moment. So naturally you can now find it in marmalade and lemon curd, both presented in this fancy festive cracker.

2. Festive Boxing Day Chutney

£6.99, Selfridges

Boxing Day is the day we all eat up all those left over meats and cheeses, and this tangy chutney is sure to spruce them up a bit.

3. Christmas Strawberry and Champagne Preserve

£7.95, Fortnum & Mason

As if all those sumptuous strawberries weren't enough, this new preserve from Fortnum & Mason is soaked in Marc de Champagne, so you are assured a sparkling addition to your Christmas breakfast table.

4. Organic Fig Apple & Balsamic Chutney

£6.99, Daylesford

This organic chutney is a great mix of sweet and sour and works beautifully with cheese and ham.

Something different 

If you want to include something a little less traditional British Christmas, why not go for one of the following: 

1. CollaGin

£35, Not on the High Street

Collagen has been hailed for helping us look youthful and its health benefits, particularly to joints. Gin has been praised for, well, being tasty and alcoholic. Mix the two, add some star anise, pink grapefruit and orris and you have a unique drink that's great in a Martini, a G&T or on its own.

2. Zymurgorium Sweet Violet Gin, 50cl

£25, John Lewis & Partners

This is like drinking Parma Violet sweets. And it's purple. Everyone's in for a treat.

3. Guiseppe Guisti Monolegni Balsamic Vinegar Set

£39, Selfridges

One for the foodies, this attractive gift set includes three variations of balsamic vinegar, aged in oak, juniper and cherry wood barrels for notes ranging from vanilla to a sweet spice.
