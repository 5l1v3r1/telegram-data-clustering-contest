John Barnes opens school sports hall on his birthday

The former England and Liverpool Footballer cut the ribbon at New Brighton Primary School

20 Nov 2019, 13:34 by Daniel Noble

Pupils from a Wirral primary school welcomed a star visitor to open their new sports hall on Thursday, November 7.

Former England and Liverpool footballer John Barnes cut the ribbon to officially open the new facility at New Brighton Primary School, Vaughan Road, on what was his 56th and his son Alexander’s ninth birthday.

The children welcomed John and sang happy birthday as head teacher Coleen Hibbard presented him with a fantastic red velvet cake, complete with LFC crest, that she had baked for him at home.

John was happy to take time out of his big day to help promote sport in school: “Sport is very important for children as it teaches them teamwork, it teaches them discipline, it teaches them togetherness,” he said.

“So, when you can have facilities which endorse all aspects of teamwork, it is fantastic.”

He added that sport in schools is vital for developing the next generation of sports stars: “Before you are in an academy or before you become a 16-year old footballer, you do sport in school.

“So, it is the first step of the ladder for you to become a sportsperson.”

The school sports hall, which was recently built by Wirral council, provides the pupils, aged 3-11, with a dedicated place to play sports in school.

Children, staff, chair of governors Lin Cook, Wirral Council Estates Strategy and Projects Manager Mike Woosey, architect Christopher Michael and local residents watched as John cut the ribbon.

After the opening ceremony, head teacher Mrs Hibbard took John on a tour of the school, which has nearly 650 pupils.

John took time out to meet the children in each class and even signed a few autographs.

Mrs Hibbard said: “John is a great role model for the children.

“They are intrigued by anyone who is a celebrity and he has been great, going around talking to the children and sharing important things like tolerance of others and hard work.

“He was the right choice of person.”

John took his personalised birthday cake to share with his family.

He said: “I’m honoured to receive the cake on my birthday and it is my son’s birthday as well, so I will take some home for him.”

When asked about her choice of cake, Mrs Hibbard said she believed the toffee in banoffee - John’s favourite flavour - would be too closely associated with Liverpool’s rivals Everton.

“For a Liverpool FC cake with red icing, a toffee cake just seemed wrong in so many ways.

“Red velvet was much more fitting,” she said.
