Moseley double death - Man 'stabbed woman to death and killed himself', police believe

The bodies of Michael Tesfamariam and Tsegereda Gebremariam were found at an address on Oxford Road on November 9.

20 Nov 2019, 13:13 by Charlotte Paxton, https://www.facebook.com/charlottepaxtonbm/

Police investigating the deaths of a man and woman believe he stabbed her to death before killing himself.

The bodies of Michael Tesfamariam and Tsegereda Gebremariam , both 28, were discovered at an address on Oxford Road, Moseley , on November 9.

Police officers, alerted by a concerned man who 'heard screams', forced their way in and discovered Mr Tesfamariam hanged, an opening inquest at Birmingham Coroner's Court heard.

Mrs Gebremariam was found with stab wounds to her chest and stomach.

Despite the efforts of police and paramedics, the pair - originally from Eritrea - both died at the scene.

 

Coroner Louise Hunt heard brief evidence from police officers, who said they did not believe anyone else had been involved in the deaths.

The pair had lived at separate addresses, with Mr Tesfamariam a married delivery driver, living in Sycamore Road, Handsworth.

Mrs Gebremariam was married to a warehouse worker and lived in Oxford Road.

Det Sgt John Garbett said: "Police were called on November 9 at around 5.30pm.

"Officers arrived and attempted to gain accessr.

 

"They found Mr Tesfamariam, who had hanged himself.

"Officers immediately commenced CPR, and paramedics took over on arrival.

"Officers carrying out further searches of the flat found Mrs Gebremariam, who had sustained stab wounds to her chest and abdomen.

"Extensive efforts were made to save both of their lives, but they were unsuccessful.

"It is our hypothesis that Mr Tesfamariam stabbed Mrs Gebremariam, then killed himself."

Full inquests into both of the deaths will be heard separately on April 6 next year.

Ms Hunt will hear evidence from GPs, mental health services, forensic pathologists and witnesses.

The news of the double deaths sent shockwaves through the  vibrant local community  .

Coun Kerry Jenkins (Lab, Moseley) said: “It is terrible to have had something like this happen in our community.

“To have the deaths of two people like this is awful and there will be a lot of people who are shocked and saddened by this news.”

The property on Oxford Road is said to have been converted into flats and is directly opposite a city primary school.
