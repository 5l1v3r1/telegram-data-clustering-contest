James Haskell's Bellator MMA boss delighted to see rugby star in the I'm A Celebrity jungle

The former England rugby star joined fellow ex-athletes Ian Wright and Caitlyn Jenner in the jungle

19 Nov 2019, 12:27 by Donagh Corby

James Haskell's MMA promoter is delighted to see his fighter in the I'm A Celebrity jungle, despite the potential hit to his training that it could cause.

The former England rugby star joined fellow ex-athletes Ian Wright and Caitlyn Jenner in the jungle this year, just months after he announced his signing with top fight promotion Bellator MMA.

And speaking exclusively with Irish Mirror, Bellator's head of European operations David Green said that he knew about the 34-year-old's reality TV opportunity before he went in.

"I'd known for a while, but there was a strict Non-Disclosure Agreement," said Green. "I don't think it affects our plans."

"To be honest, James is a very big personality and that was one of the big attractions to us when we signed him and this will further his profile.

"I'm A Celebrity is one of the biggest reality shows out there and if he gets to show a bit of his prowess in the jungle and come back to us that's great.

"You have to look at these things in the round and the opportunities it presents, we brought him in because of who he is and his profile.

"This can only increase his appeal and reach, which is never going to be a bad thing for MMA and I think he's going to be very successful here."

Haskell made headlines when it was announced he had signed with the MMA promotion just months after his retirement from rugby after a glittering career for England and Wasps.

But since announcing his intention to fight an as-yet unconfirmed opponent next year, Haskell first jetted across the world to cover the Rugby World Cup in Japan for Sky Sports.

And he is now away from training for a potential three weeks while competing to be crowned king of the jungle.

But Green insists Haskell trained while in Japan, saying: "I think some people would be very surprised with how hard he is training.

"Since we signed him he's been in the gym constantly, and for Japan in particular I was helping him find MMA gyms there because he wanted to train every day.

"So if anything, he was improving the possibilities of people he could work with so I have no concerns about his commitment.

"He takes great care of himself, he's a professional sportsman and he will make sure he is fully prepared for this fight."

In fact, Green claims his only worry is that Haskell could drop a few weight classes thanks to the dietary options available in the jungle.

"My only concern is that I signed a heavyweight, but if Caitlyn Jenner doesn't improve on these Bushtucker trials I might end up with a middleweight!"

Tickets to Bellator London, which features Irish MMA star Charlie Ward, are on sale and can be purchased online from axs.com/uk and Bellator.com.
