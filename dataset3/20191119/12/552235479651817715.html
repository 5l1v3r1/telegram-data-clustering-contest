Man in court charged with murder of Edinburgh mum Nicola Stevenson

The mum-of-two's body was found in a wheelie bin by a dog walker.

19 Nov 2019, 12:05 by Michael Drummond

A man charged with the murder of Edinburgh mum Nicola Stevenson, whose body was found in a wheelie bin, has appeared in court.

Nicola , 39, was found dead by a dog walker last Wednesday at 12.30pm.

The mum-of-two's body had been stuffed into a wheelie bin, near recreation ground.

 

Nicola, who was understood to be a recovering cancer patient, had fatal head wounds.

Officers probing her brutal murder think the green household bin could have been lying in the undergrowth for two weeks.

The grim discovery was made in off Landport Road in Lewes, East Sussex.

 

Richard Canlin, 41, of no fixed address, was charged with murder on Sunday and appeared at Hove Crown Court on Tuesday.

He did not enter a plea and will appear again at Crown Court on November 26 for a further hearing.

A preliminary trial date has been set for April 2020
