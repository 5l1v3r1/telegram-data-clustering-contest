‘Kaldero’ cost ‘reasonable’

19 Nov 2019, 12:08 by Jester P. Manalastas

IN defense of the House Speaker, Albay Rep. Joey Salceda said the P55 million cauldron for the torch-lighting ceremony of the 2019 Southeast Asian Games (SEA Games) is “reasonable.” Cayetano heads the Philippine Southeast Asian Games Organizing Committe.

In a television interview, Salceda said the cauldron describes “the new and bolder character of the Filipino as coming of age and breaking into the field of emerging tigers.”

“It personifies the new character of the Philippines being a strong economy,” said the Saceda said.

“Yung P55 million pag tiningnan mo yung cauldron mukhang reasonable naman kaso ‘imeldific’ yung dating, may grandiosity,” he added.

The chairman of the House Committee on Ways and Means said the SEA Games is a lump sum item in2019 national budget, amounting to P6 billion.

Senate Minority Leader Franklin Drilon had questioned the P55 million cost of the design, foundation and construction of a stadium cauldron for the SEA Games.

The Philippines is hosting the SEA Games from November 30 to December 11, 2019.

According to Drilon, the P50 million could have been used to build at least 50 classrooms for the public schools.

But Salceda corrected Drilon saying each classroom costs P2.5 million.

“Wala namang classroom na P1 million ngayon. Karamihann ng classroom P2.5 million,” he said.

He further said that the P6-billion budget is also “reasonable” and actually “small” for funding the hosting of the SEA Games.

“Mukhang reasonable naman ‘yung P6 billion to stage games like that. P6 billion is already small,” the Albay representative added.
