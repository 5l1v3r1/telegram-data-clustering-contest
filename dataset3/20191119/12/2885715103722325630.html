Yes Bank promoter Rana Kapoor, Yes Capital and Morgan Credits sell remaining 0.8% stake

According to the information disclosed to the exchanges, the promoters of Yes Bank sold 2.04 crore shares (2,04,24,100 shares) which account for 0.80 per cent of the total shares of the bank in the open market during November 13-14, 2019.

19 Nov 2019, 12:05 by Express Web Desk

Yes Bank founder and former managing director (MD) Rana Kapoor and promoter firms Yes Capital (India) and Morgan Credits have sold their remaining 0.8 per cent stake in the bank, the private sector bank announced in an exchange filing Tuesday.

According to the information disclosed to the exchanges, the promoters sold 2.04 crore shares (2,04,24,100 shares), which account for 0.80 per cent of the total shares of the bank in the open market during November 13-14, 2019.

Following the selloff, the promoter group now holds only 900 shares of the private sector lender, according to the information provided in the exchange filing to the BSE.

The announcement came during the market hours on Tuesday after which, the shares of Yes Bank fell 2.66 per cent to settle at Rs 64.15 apiece on the BSE. On the National Stock Exchange (NSE), the stock settled at Rs 64.20, down 2.51 per cent.

Earlier this month, ace investor Rakesh Jhunjhunwala had purchased approximately 1.3 crore shares of YES Bank at a price of Rs 67.10 per share, which valued the total amount of the deal to around Rs 86.89 crore.
