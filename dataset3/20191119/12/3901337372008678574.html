The Top Goalscorers In South American History Revealed, Only One Above Messi

19 Nov 2019, 12:26 by David Kappel

With Lionel Messi adding another international goal to his Argentina tally on Monday, only one player is now ahead of him among South America's record goalscorers in history.

The Argentine netted a late penalty to salvage a 2-2 draw for his side against Uruguay this week. With the strike being his 70th at international level, only legendary Brazil forward Pele has netted more among South America's leading international goalscorers.

Messi scored his 70 strikes in 138 games for Argentina, while Pele needed just 93 appearances for the Selecao for his 77 goals.

Another Brazil great in Ronaldo Nazario completes the podium after finding the back of the net 62 times for his country, and he is followed by Neymar, who has scored just one goal less.
