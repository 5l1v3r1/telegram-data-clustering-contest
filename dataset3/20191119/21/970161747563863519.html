Boris Johnson and Corbyn share awkward handshake during live TV clash

19 Nov 2019, 21:11

Jeremy Corbyn offered to give Boris Johnson a copy of A Christmas Carol so the PM could understand how ‘nasty’ Scrooge was.

The jibe came after the rivals were asked what they would give each other for Christmas during the ITV general election debate.

Boris retorted by saying he’d give Corbyn a copy of his Brexit deal – or a pot of damsel jam.

It came after they were forced to share an awkward handshake by presenter Julie Etchingham.

The sour tone of politics dominated much of tonight’s leaders debate, with the pair both facing accusations of degrading debate and making the political climate toxic.

The two leaders were challenged to step up their game on tackling abuse and poor behaviour in politics, after a tumultuous few years that has seen a rise in hate crime towards MPs, including the murder of Labour MP Jo Cox.

The audience applauded after one member of the public asked the party leaders how the nation could ‘trust’ either of them.

Responding, Johnson blamed the lack of trust in politicians on parliament repeatedly blocking Brexit and said the way to restore trust was to ‘get Brexit done’.

Corbyn said ‘trust has to be earned’ and that this could only be done by ‘listening to people’ – from Chief Executives to rough sleepers.

The pair then obliged somewhat awkwardly to a request from Julie Etchingham to pledge to ‘raise the tone’ of politics and to shake hands on it.

She said: ‘There are many people, many politicians indeed, who feel that the nastiness of political debate in this country has got out of control.

‘Will you two tonight look at one another and no matter who becomes prime minister make a gesture to improve things today – make a pledge and promise?.

But the rare moment of ‘unity’  left some viewers cringing.

Others felt like it was insincere given the rest of the debate saw them clash over issues like Brexit and the NHS.
