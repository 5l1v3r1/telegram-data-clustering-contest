I'm a Celeb's Ian Wright was 'mugged off' by his wife when they first met

Former footballer Ian Wright has made the surprise confession that his wife rebuffed him when they first met - but that was how he knew she was the one

19 Nov 2019, 21:45 by Irishmirror.ie

I'm a Celebrity star and former footballer Ian Wright opened up to his camp mates about the first time he met his wife - and revealed it was a disaster.

He was chatting to radio presenter Roman Kemp and boy band member Myles Stephenson about relationships.

Rak Su singer Myles confessed he was always scared women were only after him for his fame.

Ian advised him: “You have to go to places that are more obscure.

"You can’t be going to places where the girls all like your music."

He added that when he first met his wife Nancy Hallam, who he married in 2011, she had no idea who he was.

"My missus didn’t know anything about football. I met her in a pub in Notting Hill", he said, revealing she rebuffed his advances at first.

"It was the biggest kind of mugging off in respect of meeting someone", he said.

"I’m not saying I‘m Brad Pitt, but she mugged me off so beautifully I knew she was the one.”

Myles was clearly touched by the chat, and said in the Bush Telegraph: "When we was in the creek, we got speaking about our love lives and how we adjusted to fame.

"Ian’s my big brother", he added.

"He’s my family.”

Myles wasn't the only one who is a big fan of Ian.

Roman has been one for a very long time, as he supports Arsenal, where Ian played for seven years.

He told Ian: “I’m not going to lie, them pulling me away from you on day one was quite tough. Especially when I know I can live in the Jungle with a legend.”

He also showed Ian his Arsenal tattoo on his leg, with Myles calling it his "super fan moment".

Roman confessed to Ian he had a plastic toy of him which he’d signed for him when his Dad Martin, from Spandau Ballet, appeared on Ian’s show when Roman was about seven years old.

Talking in the Bush Telegraph, Roman said: “Imagine Ian’s head as a bobble head with a little Arsenal kit on, little tiny boots.

"That’s what I’ve still got at home, and I’ve just said that to the man himself.”
