Natalie Sciver hopes pay gap is reduced as she aims to be someone's David Beckham at Trent Rockets

The England all-rounder is one of the star picks in The Hundred as she moves to Trent Bridge alongside fiancee Katherine Brunt

19 Nov 2019, 21:27 by Matt Davies

When Natalie Sciver was growing up she had a poster of David Beckham on her wall, no doubt like thousands of other children of the late 90s.

In Sciver's case he was her sporting hero thanks to his exploits for Manchester United, Real Madrid and England.

These days, Sciver hopes to be someone else's David Beckham, including next summer when she plays for Trent Rockets women's team in The Hundred.

The 27-year-old England all-rounder and World Cup winner is perhaps at her sporting peak and women's cricket - and sport in general - is flourishing, affording more scope for female athletes to be role models than ever before.

For the next generation of women cricketers, the picture will only get better, but she is happy with her lot now as she aims to inspire.

"I was so lucky with how I started. I finished university and pretty much got handed a contract," she said.

"I've never had to worry about the real world in my life, which is amazing. To see women's sport growing and where it can go is really exciting.

"It's really important to have those role models for young girls.

"The England team has really connected to that, to make sure we're accessible to young girls and boys for that matter.

"They love women's cricket. If we can make that connection in the crowd and after the game it's really important to us.

"When I was younger I looked up to David Beckham. Wrong sport I know, but having that male role model was really important to me.

"Hopefully I can be someone's David Beckham in years to come."

No matter how inspirational Beckham and Sciver are there will always be a huge difference in their bank balances.

No matter how much women's sport has grown there is still a huge difference in salaries between her male counterparts.

In the men's tournament next summer salaries range from £30,000 to £125,000. For the women it is £3,600 to £15,000.

Sciver admits she would like to see the gap reduced and believes that will happen in time.

"It's a difficult subject really. In terms of international cricket, England have come such a long way already," she said.

"From where we started to where we are now, we're so much further ahead. We're only going to improve from there.

"Obviously we'd love to be a bit closer in terms of pay gaps and things. Sometimes it's not realistic for those things to happen so soon.

"I'm assured things like that will get smaller in time. We can always hope for more. At the moment, at 27, I'm doing pretty well not thinking about the real world."

At present, Sciver is looking forward to coming to the East Midlands to play for Trent Rockets alongside England teammate Katherine Brunt, with the couple also announcing their engagement last month.

Sciver is certain the tournament will be a positive step for women's cricket with the team's games to be played in Derby and Leicester, as well as at Trent Bridge.

"I had a few options I could have gone for but I wanted to be part of this team and be reunited with a former coach in Salliannn Briggs," she said.

"I actually live in the Midlands so this a home team for me.

"The Hundred is a chance for our game to go to the next level and make sure we have a bigger pool of players ready for the international game.

"From a women's side of things it's great there are eight teams, two teams more than the KSL. That's more players being exposed to different pressures and making a step up from county cricket.

"I think it will still look a lot like T20. We had a couple of practice games last summer and apart from getting the hang of counting to five not six it's pretty similar!

"In terms of playing, you always think you have less time than you have. It will be the same with The Hundred.

"There will be a few tactical things to learn though."
