Sherlock star Louise Brealey's fury as dad's 'most precious possession' stolen

Louise Brealey, who plays morgue registrar Molly Hooper in the BBC series, revealed her 71-year-old dad Keith’s garage was broken into last week.

19 Nov 2019, 21:20 by James Rodger, https://www.facebook.com/jamesrodgerjournalist

One of the stars of TV's Sherlock is trying to solve her own real-life crime mystery – the theft of her father’s rare antique pedal car.

Louise Brealey, who plays morgue registrar Molly Hooper in the BBC series, revealed her 71-year-old dad Keith’s garage was broken into last week.

Thieves took a 1949 Austin Pathfinder, which Louise says is the “Penny Black” of the pedal car world along with power tools.

Northamptonshire Police are now appealing for information about the theft which happened in Raunds, Northants., overnight on November 12.

Louise, 40, said: "It's worse than losing something yourself. He's heartbroken.

"His mum, who died when dad was a teen, had saved up for months to buy him an Austin Pathfinder pedal car. He has owned it for 70 years.

“It is basically the Penny Black of the pedal car world.”

 

She said that her father's toy was a "very rusty, trusty, old friend" and was also "loved hard and showed it".

She added: "Police are trying their best, but he's desolate.”

Since the actress put out the appeal on Twitter it has been shared over 9,500 times.

She has received support from the likes of poet Pam Ayres, writer Caitlin Moran and actor Prasanna Puwanarajah.

A police spokeswoman said: “The incident happened between 7pm on Tuesday, November 12, and 8.10am on Wednesday, November 13, when the unknown offender/s broken into a garage in the area and stole an Austin Pathfinder and a number of power tools.

“It is thought that the offenders made off in a Peugeot SUV.”
