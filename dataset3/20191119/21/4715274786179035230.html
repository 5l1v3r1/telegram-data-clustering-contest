UST tablenetters head straight to UAAP finals

19 Nov 2019, 21:40 by Manila Standard Sports

University of Santo Tomas jumped straight to the Finals of the UAAP Season 82 Men’s Table Tennis Tournament after completing a 14-0 elimination sweep Monday at the Blue Eagle Gym.

The Tiger Paddlers scored a 3-0 romp of University of the Philippines before returning in the afternoon session with a 3-1 victory over National University.

UST remains on course of winning back-to-back and a record 28th crown.

Despite losing twice in the final day of the eliminations, the Bulldogs (11-3) will still have the twice-to-beat bonus in the step-ladder semifinals for being ranked No. 2.

NU suffered a shock 2-3 loss to Adamson University in the morning session.

De La Salle finished strong in the eliminations after identical 3-0 wins over Ateneo and University of the East.

The Falcons then tied with the Green Paddlers at 9-5 following a 3-0 tripping of the Blue Eagles.

De La Salle and Adamson University clash in a do-or-die tie at 9 a.m. Tuesday. The winner will face the Bulldogs at 1 p.m. Tuesday.

The best-of-three Finals will be played on Wednesday starting at 9 a.m.

Meanwhile, back-to-back title-seeking University of Santo Tomas avenged its lone loss of the season with a 3-1 conquest of Ateneo Monday at the Blue Eagle Gym.

The Tigress Paddlers swept the second round of the eliminations and finished with a league-best 13-1 record.

UST, which defeated University of the East, 3-0, earlier in the day, will face Far Eastern University in the Final Four at 9 a.m. on Tuesday.

Ateneo stunned De La Salle, 3-0, in a playoff to secure the remaining twice-to-beat slot in the Final Four. The vastly-improved Lady Eagles and the Lady Paddlers ended the elims with identical 11-3 records.

Ateneo placed sixth last year.

The Tigresses and the Lady Eagles need only to win once to make it to the best-of-three championship series on Wednesday. The deciding Final Four tie, if needed, will be played at 1 p.m. on Tuesday.

The Lady Tamaraws claimed the last semifinals berth after hurdling the last two elimination round matches.

Prior to scoring a narrow 3-2 win over De La Salle that sealed a semis duel with UST, FEU opened the day with a 3-0 decision of Adamson University.

In the morning ties, the Lady Eagles beat National University, 3-0, while the Lady Paddlers downed University of the Philippines, 3-0.

The Tigresses are gunning for a 14th championship.
