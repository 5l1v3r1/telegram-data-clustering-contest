Aaron Ramsey sends Wales to Euro 2020 Finals as Juventus star's double downs Hungary

WALES 2-0 HUNGARY: Juventus midfielder Ramsey came up trumps after Ryan Giggs decided to select the ex-Arsenal star in the starting lineup despite recent injury troubles

19 Nov 2019, 21:49 by Irishmirror.ie

Ryan Giggs masterminded a famous Wales win last night as his side continued their dramatic upturn in form.

Wales had only twice previously reached the finals of a major competition - the 1958 World Cup and Euro 2016.

But they secured a memorable hat-trick infront of their own fans at an ecstatic Cardiff City Stadium.

Perhaps now sceptical Wales fans will warm to boss Giggs after a lukewarm reception to his reign until now.

The Manchester United legend wasn't everyone's favourite choice to replace Chris Coleman in January 2018 due to his record of rarely playing in friendlies.

It saw the knives sharpened for Giggs when successive summer losses left Wales languishing off the pace in Group E.

Yet Giggs, 45, wasn't too proud to change his philosophy of passing out from the back in favour of a more direct approach to 6ft 5in targetman Kieffer Moore, who only made his competitive debut last month.

Wales' change in style continued to pay off in their sixth game without defeat since losing to Hungary in June.

Giggs got his tactics right again as the recalled Aaron Ramsey ghosted in to head Wales into a 15th minute lead from skipper Gareth Bale's cross.

Moore then caused further problems as he found Ramsey to fire in his 16th international goal after the break to move up to joint seventh in Wales' all-time goal-scoring charts.

Giggs admitted he faced tough decisions after Wales impressively swotted aside Azerbaijan 2-0 in Baku on Saturday.

But he showed his ruthless streak dropping Harry Wilson despite his goal and assist at the weekend to bring in Ramsey.

While Joe Allen returned from suspension to take the place of Ethan Ampadu in midfield.

Hungary were without their key centre-back Willi Orban due to injury and had other suspensions.

It handed the initiative to Wales who started well as the visitors struggled to retain possession.

Wales had their first shot on target in the 12th minute when Bale cut in from the left on to Ramsey's pass before curling a shot straight at Peter Gulacsi. The hosts then took the lead in the 15th minute to take control of the match.

Connor Roberts released Bale down the right and his inswinging left-foot cross was headed in by Ramsey from five yards out. Hungary complained Roberts committed a foul in the build up but the goal stood.

Wales had another great chance on the break in the 29th minute when Ramsey released Bale down the right again but Moore this time headed the cross just wide.

The only scare for the hosts was when they had keeper Wayne Hennessey to thank for preserving their advantage in the 34th minute with a fantastic double save.

First Hennessey made a brilliant left-handed reaction save to his left to deny Dominik Szoboszlai's shot before getting down low to his right to foil Roland Sallai's follow-up.

Wales are 24th in the FIFA men's world rankings, 26 places higher than Hungary in 50th.

The gulf in class was underlined after the break when Ramsey made it 2-0 with a clinical right-foot finish.

Ben Davies curled in a free-kick from the left which Moore steered back across goal and Ramsey buried.

Ramsey was only denied a hat-trick by the outstretched leg of keeper Peter Gulacsi late on.

Wales were unfortunate not to add to their advantage as dangermen like Daniel James threatened.

When Wales qualified previously Bale scored seven and assisted two of their 11 goals.

Yet this time they have more attacking threats and are less reliant on the Real Madrid star as they highlighted replacing him with the talented Wilson off the bench in the closing stages.
