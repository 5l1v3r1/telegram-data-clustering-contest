Lucile I. Lasher

19 Nov 2019, 21:38

Lucile I. Schroeder was born January 12, 1933, in Watertown, Wisconsin, the daughter of Irene E. (Stoflet) and Lawrence A. Schroeder.

She grew up in the area of Lake Mills, Wisconsin, and attended country schools at Townline and Plain View. Beginning in seventh grade she attended Lake Mills Public School and graduated from the Lake Mills High School in 1950. Immediately following graduation she began working as a secretary at the Creamery Package Manufacturing Company in Lake Mills.

On February 10, 1951, Lucile was united in marriage to Robert H. Lasher at the Congregational "Old Stone Church" in Rockton, Illinois, after which they made their home in Fort Atkinson, Wisconsin.

It was in 1977 that she began working as a part-time secretary in the office of the First Congregational Church, United Church of Christ in Fort Atkinson. That led to a "calling" to become the full-time Director of Parish Services at the church on January 1, 1979. In 1980, Lucile was installed by the Reverend Bob Cedar as a Commissioned Worker in the United Church of Christ. She remained the Director of Parish Services until her retirement on January 25, 1998. During these years she became known as "Mother Church" to some and the "Hug Lady" to many more. 

Through the years she was active in the Fort Atkinson Hostess Club, was a leader of Girl Scout Troop 314 for six years and served two more years as the Girl Scout Community Cookie Sales chairperson. Most importantly though, Lucile was always quick to say that being an active member of the church and being involved in its activities, committees and boards gave her the most joy and greatly enriched her life.

Preceding her in death was her husband Robert "Bob" Lasher on February 14, 1978. Surviving are a daughter, Sharyl Hendriksen (Ron) of Fort Atkinson; a grandson, Erich Rusch (Ashley) of Green Bay; a step-son, Eric Hendriksen of Edgerton. Additional survivors include a brother, Robert Schroeder (Olgui) of Tucson, Arizona; three sisters, Shirley Radloff (Ken), Marilyn Trieloff, Elizabeth "Bette" Hopmann (Roger), all of Lake Mills, Wisconsin. She is also survived by many nieces, nephews, great-nieces and nephews.

Lucile peacefully departed this life on November 18, 2019 at Rainbow Hospice Care in Johnson Creek, Wisconsin.

A celebration of life will be held on Friday, November 22, at 11:00, with visitation from 9:30 until the time of service at the First Congregational Church, United Church of Christ in Fort Atkinson, Wisconsin.

In lieu of flowers, memorials may be given to the First Congregational Church, United Church of Christ at 31 W. Milwaukee Avenue in Fort Atkinson, Wisconsin 53538 or to Rainbow Hospice Care at 147 Rockwell Street in Jefferson, Wisconsin 53549.
