South Australia Braces For Catastrophic Bushfire Danger And Record-Breaking Temperatures

19 Nov 2019, 21:38

It’s set to be a hot and windy day through South Australia with temperatures above 40 degrees forecast across the State.

With firefighters preparing for the extreme weather, ‘catastrophic’ fire danger warnings remain in place for the West Coast, Eastern Eyre Peninsula, Lower Eyre Peninsula, Mid North, Mount Lofty Ranges, Yorke Peninsula and Kangaroo Island districts.

Total fire bans have been declared in 16 districts and no part of South Australia has a fire risk rating lower than ‘severe’.

The CFS urges residents to action their Bushfire Survival plans and contact 000 in an emergency.
