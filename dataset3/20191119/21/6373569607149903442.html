'I was trying to melt a plastic bag' says man who burned down his ex's shed

Brian Brooks, aged 51, denies that he meant to put the sleeping woman at risk

19 Nov 2019, 21:05 by Stuart Abel

A man who started a fire in a shed next to the home of his sleeping ex-partner has denied he meant to put her at any risk.

Brian Brooks, aged 51, told a jury he only intended to melt a plastic bag on top of her tumble drier.

But a court has heard that the fire spread after he ran away from the house in Plymstock.

The woman said she ran in a panic to a neighbour’s house at about 10pm at night. She had been dozing in front of the television when the fire tripped the electricity.

Brooks is on trial at Plymouth Crown Court after he denied arson reckless as to whether life was endangered on April 5 this year. He has admitted arson.

Brooks, of Quarry Park Road in Plymstock, has also denied leaving her in fear with unwanted calls and social media messages between February and April.

Asked what he wanted when he started the fire, Brooks said: “I wanted the bag to melt and leave a puddle of plastic on top of the tumble drier and that’s it.”

His barrister Ali Rafati asked: “Did you foresee any risk to life when you started the fire?”

Brooks replied that he did not.

Mr Rafati asked whether he knew she was asleep in the lounge at the time.

He admitted peering through her window but added: “I did not see anyone in there.”

The court heard that the couple had been together for about 14 months and lived together at her house in Jenkins Close for six.

The woman said they split on February 9 and claimed Brooks messaged her and phoned her despite saying she wanted no contact.

She added she was “terrified” and worried what he would do next.

The ex-partner told the jury: “I was really concerned for my safety and I did not know where this would lead.”

The woman said she was dozing in front of the television in her lounge when the set went off. She awoke to find all the power had gone off.

She added: “I heard a huge bang and I looked out of the window to see a huge ball of flame.”

The trial continues.
