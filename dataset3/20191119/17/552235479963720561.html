Iceland threaten to walk away from Kilmarnock store over booze rules

The supermerket chain plan to take over the old Aldi unit in town.

19 Nov 2019, 17:20 by Kilmarnock Standard, Sarah Hilley, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/kilmarnock-standard/, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/sarah-hilley/

Up to 30 jobs will be up for grabs at a new Food Warehouse store in Kilmarnock.

The Iceland supermarket chain got the green light to sell alcohol at premises in the Glencairn Retail Park during a licensing board meeting today.

The application to sell booze was granted despite a tough policy on getting new alcohol licences in East Ayrshire.

 

Members heard Iceland might walk away from the Kilmarnock unit if it was barred from selling liquor.

The decision went to a tied vote among councillors, with three refusing the bid for the bulk buy shop to offer booze.

Licensing depute clerk Stuart McCall described the council’s licensing policy as a “critical issue” and a “major obstacle” to allowing alcohol to be sold by the Food Warehouse.

Solicitor Niall Hassard told councillors the application was “for an Iceland concept known as the Food Warehouse”.

He called the sites “flagship new stores” which are more than double the size of a typical Iceland.

 

Describing it as a “coup” for Iceland to bag the retail space in Kilmarnock, he also pointed out the new business would fill an empty shop.

The board heard there would be no zero-hour contracts and staff are paid above the living wage.

There would be between 25 to 30 new posts, including nine full-time jobs.

While deliberating over the application Councillor Lillian Jones said: “You’re saying if the licence is refused you could walk away from the store.”

Mr Hassard confirmed Iceland would rethink the matter if it was turned down.

 

The meeting heard alcohol makes up about 3 per cent of their sales.

Three councillors voted to reject the application while the same number voted to approve it.

Councillor George Mair, who moved to grant the licence, said: “The benefits outweigh the presumption for refusal.”

Board chair Tom Cook had the deciding vote and ruled the alcohol licence would be granted.

 

Councillor Ian Grant could not vote as he arrived late to the meeting.

Iceland applied for the provisional grant of a premises licence for off-sales during Monday to Friday from 10am to 10pm. The alcohol display area will measure 22 square metres.

There are 14 Food Warehouse stores in Scotland.
