RIP JAMIE

First picture of Glasgow dad who died in hospital after taking unwell in NCP car park on coldest night of the year

19 Nov 2019, 17:17 by Katy Pagan

A DAD who died after taking unwell in a Glasgow multi-storey car park on the coldest night of the year has been named as Jamie Kearney.

The 43-year-old was discovered having taken unwell at the NCP car park on Mitchell Street at around 5.50pm on Sunday.

We told earlier how Sunday was recorded as the coldest night of the year so far - with temperatures plummeting to -8.6C in some parts of the country.

Emergency services rushed to the scene after Jamie was discovered unwell.

He was rushed to Glasgow Royal Infirmary but was sadly pronounced dead after arrival.

Cops say the death is not being treated as suspicious.

A spokeswoman for the force said: "Around 5.50pm on Sunday 17 November 2019, police were called to a report of a man unwell within a car park in Mitchell Lane, Glasgow.

"The 43 year-old man was taken to Glasgow Royal Infirmary, where he was pronounced dead.

"A post mortem examination will take place in due course, however at this time the death is not being treated as suspicious."

Initial reports suggested the man who died was homeless.

But a spokeswoman for Glasgow’s Health & Social Care Partnership said: “Homelessness services have not been officially notified of the death of anyone they have been working with.

“We await formal identification of the deceased and, of course, will fully assist the police and emergency services in their investigation.”

Anyone with concerns about a rough sleeper or homeless person is asked to contact the council's RSVP service on 0800 027 7466.
