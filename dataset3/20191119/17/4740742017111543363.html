Bristol City coach expects Neil Warnock to return to management after Cardiff City exit

Assistant head coach explained no-show following Robins win in Welsh capital

19 Nov 2019, 17:54 by Gregor MacGregor

Bristol City's Dean Holden has explained that Neil Warnock gave no hint of his imminent departure from Cardiff City the weekend before last.

After the Robins triumphed 1-0 at the Cardiff City Stadium on the Sunday, Neil Warnock did not have a drink with the Robins coaching staff following the game.

The Robins assistant head coach revealed that instead the coaching team had a beer with Warnock's assistants Kevin Blackwell, Ronnie Jepson and Andy Dibble following a first win in 17 years over the bridge.

And Holden said there was no hint of what was to come following the match: that Warnock was about to leave the Welsh capital after three years in charge, a quirk of fate seeing the spell book-ended by a win and a loss against Bristol City.

The veteran coach is not expected to be out of work for long.

"The couple of times we've played them, he's not been available to come in afterwards," explained Holden following the game.

"We had a beer after the game with some of their staff: Kevin Blackwell and Ronnie Jepson and Andy Dibble.

"I was chatting to Kevin Blackwell about what they might do in the summer - because obviously they'd already announced that he was set to retire in the summer.

"Kevin was joking around that he's done this more times than Sir Alex Ferguson has.

"Obviously that night or the next day he left the job but I watched the BT thing where they mic'ed him up - have you seen it? A doucumentary. It's only a 20-minute thing and it's really interesting.

"And at the end of that it said that he's 26 games short of 1,500 [league games managed] I noticed. I thought to myself straightaway that there's no way - and nor should he - retire with 26 games to go to what could be a huge massive milestone for him.

"I'm sure that someone will come calling for him at some point. He's obviously a very colourful character in the game, for 30-40 years. I'm sure the game will miss him and he'll miss the game.

"I expect him to come back at some point for sure," explained Holden, at the pre-Nottingham Forest press conference.

We make it 1,655 games for Warnock across all levels including non-league matches, for the veteran boss who has been linked with the Hearts job recently - along with former City boss Steve Cotterill.

The assistant head coach explained that Jay Dasilva's scan on his tibia last week was positive but the full-back is still expected to be out for a few weeks yet.

Jack Hunt will be assessed later this week as to whether he can return against Nottingham Forest on Saturday.
