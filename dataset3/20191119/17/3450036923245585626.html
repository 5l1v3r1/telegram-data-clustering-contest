Kirksville's MREIC reflects on successful year

19 Nov 2019, 17:29 by Ashley Hoak

KIRKSVILLE, Mo. â€” One northeast Missouri organization aimed at helping entrepreneurs is reflecting on its accomplishments.

Missouri Rural Enterprise and Innovation Center, or MREIC Director Carolyn Chrisman says the past year has been a successful one.

"We are given a goal of trying to help 15 new businesses start in our region, and we blew that out of the water. We actually helped 18 businesses in our region start. We also helped retain over 200 different jobs for those businesses."

During a meeting on Tuesday, MREIC Vice President Tony Keene announced his resignation from the board.

He'll be taking a position at Sullivan County Hospital in Milan as CEO starting in December.

Until November 19, Keene represented Northeast Regional Medical Center on the MREIC board.

Keene serves as the assistant CEO at the hospital in Kirksville.
