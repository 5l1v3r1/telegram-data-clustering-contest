Frozen 2 writer-director Jennifer Lee: Challenges were greater with the sequel

Jennifer Lee and Chris Buck directorial Frozen 2 releases in India on November 22.

19 Nov 2019, 02:30 by A. Kameshwari

Frozen 2 director duo Jennifer Lee and Chris Buck along with producer Peter Del Vecho talk to indianexpress.com about the making of the much-awaited Disney film, the need for a sequel to Frozen and much more.

Here are excerpts from the conversation:

When did you decide to do Frozen 2?

Jennifer: Unless it came from us, no one was going tell us we had to do Frozen 2. When we got talking a year after Frozen hit screens, we got so emotional. We realized that we had all these questions that we asked each other. We realised we were not ready to leave the Frozen universe. So, the minute we mentioned sequel, there was a lot of happiness. There was a complete embracing of it (from Disney) because people loved the characters and what we wanted to do with it. The great thing is that we have got a lot of support in a beautiful way.

Was it a challenge to make a sequel?

Chris: We wanted to have our characters go to a different place and have them go outside Arendelle. We wanted to have new characters to expand this world. The challenge was how do you keep true to the characters and let them grow. There’s a challenge to keep them true.

Peter: When Frozen came out, it wasn’t our movie anymore. The film became the world’s movie and that was exciting to us. But with that comes a lot of expectations. Truthfully, we agreed that we would keep the success outside the room and really build Frozen 2 the same way we built the earlier one, which is really talking about characters first, then plot and then get the songwriters involved. If you let that outside pressure into the room, it can actually hinder the decisions we make for Anna and Elsa.
