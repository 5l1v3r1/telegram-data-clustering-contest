Cutrone's agent slams speculation

Patrick Cutrone’s agent has quashed rumours the striker could be about to quit Wolverhampton Wanderers.

Italy striker Cutrone, who scored twice for his nation’s under-21s in a 3-0 win over Iceland on Saturday, only joined Wolves from AC Milan in the summer.

The 21-year-old signed a four-year contract at Molineux and has already become a hugely popular figure with supporters.

Cutrone has scored just two goals in 19 appearances, but most of those outings have come from the bench under Nuno Espirito Santo.

And rumours have surprisingly surfaced to suggest Cutrone could return to Italy with Sampdoria reportedly interested.

But Cutrone’s agent, Donato Orgnoni, has laughed off those claims and insists his client is only focused on life at his new club.

“We’ve never thought of leaving Wolves,” he told SampNews. “I don’t understand why certain news comes out. He remains in England one hundred percent.”

Olmo hasn't closed the door...

Dani Olmo is among the many names to have been linked with Wolverhampton Wanderers in the past 12 months. 

The Dinamo Zagreb midfielder, 21, scored on his international debut for Spain on Friday night to add to his growing reputation. 

His stock has risen considerably this season due to his involvement in the Champions League with Dinamo and Wolves aren’t the only club to have been linked.

Olmo’s boyhood club Barcelona are also said to be keen on re-signing him due to his fine form in Croatia, which has seen him net 19 goals in 31 league matches. 

And a return to the Camp Nou appears to be Olmo’s preferred destination.

“I hope I can come back, but we've just started the season really well, we're doing well in the Champions League,” Olmo told SPORT. “There are two games left and we depend on ourselves to get through. There's a lot of work to be done.”

Pressed on where his futures lies, Olmo added: "I don't close the door on any club, but I'm a Dinamo player. What happens with Dinamo, we will see.”

United want a Loader Danny

Manchester United have joined the race to sign Reading star Danny Loader, according to Sky Sports News.

Loader, 19, is reportedly a target for a host of Premier League clubs with Wolverhampton Wanderers known to be among his admirers.

Wolves made a move to sign Loader late in the summer transfer window and agreed a deal before Reading pulled the plug before the deadline.

And Wolves continue to keep tabs on Loader with his contract due to expire at the end of the current season.
