INVESTMENT LATEST

Mel Morris says the challenge to succeed at Derby County remains a "constant quest".

Derby's owner and chairman, who bought the Rams in 2015, has invested tens of millions of pounds in the club and he has been searching for new investment.

That investment could be in place by the end of the year, DerbyshireLive understands.

ACOSTA NO GO

Wayne Rooney's former team-mate at DC United, Luciano Acosta , is not a transfer target for Derby County .

The 25-year-old attacking midfielder claimed in an interview in Argentina that Rooney, who joins Derby as player-coach in January, had told him to join him at Pride Park.

There was a suggestion that Acosta is "waiting for the call" from Derby over a potential move, but DerbyshireLive understands the player is not on their 'wanted list'.

DEFENDER WANTED

Former Derby County defender Efe Ambrose is reportedly wanted by two clubs in Scotland

The experienced centre-back signed for the Rams on a short-term deal in February as a free agent, shortly after his contract at Hibernian has expired.

Ambrose was signed as defensive cover to centre-backs Richard Keogh and Fikayo Tomori, but left Pride Park in the summer without a single first-team appearance to his name.
