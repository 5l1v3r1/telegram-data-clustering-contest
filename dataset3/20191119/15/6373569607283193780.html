Plymouth woman wants to give away her wedding dress to someone in need

The Good Samaritan is looking to pass the Mori Lee gown to another couple

19 Nov 2019, 15:50 by Jess Morcom

A generous Plymouth woman is looking to give away her wedding dress to someone in need.

The lady, who does not wish to be named, got married a few years ago in a Â£1,300 Mori Lee designer dress.

And now, she feels the time is right to give the dress to someone who would benefit from the stunning gown.

"I got married back in 2016, and I know for most people, especially for the first couple of years, keeping your wedding dress is a very sentimental thing," she told Plymouth Live.

"But now, myself and my husband are looking to go abroad for a few months, and I just thought to myself, actually I could really put this dress to good use.

"It's always been important to me that people share good fortune, so I just feel like giving it to someone in need is the right thing to do.

"Originally, I looked into giving the gown to charities that make gowns for still borns, but that seems to be a really popular route as they're inundated with donations at the moment.

"So now I'm seeking to find another couple that would really benefit from this dress. Perhaps they've had a tough year, or they're experiencing illness and want to marry while they still can.

"It would be great to be able to see the dress make a difference to someone else."

The dress is around a UK Size 10, but could be adjusted accordingly.

There are also a pair of shoes in size 7 which could go with the dress should they be required.

Anyone interested in the dress is urged to either comment, contact Plymouth Live or email jess.morcom@reachplc.com and we can put you in touch with the Good Samaritan.
