Rich and famous to get major tax refund after victory against HMRC

19 Nov 2019, 15:47

Wayne Rooney is among hundreds of famous faces in line for a major tax refund after winning a battle against HMRC.

A host of celebrities ploughed money into a £263m scheme, which allowed them to claim tax relief in return for investing into a project to build two data centres.

675 people paid a combined total of £79million towards the Tyneside based development in 2011, and got £131million in relief.

But the taxman argued that those involved – including comedian Jimmy Carr and football manager Arsene Wenger – benefited excessively from tax cuts.

In 2016, HMRC sent so-called ‘accelerated payment notices’ (APN) demanding those involved to hand over more money in tax.

After some appealed to the tax tribunal the case went to the High Court, which ruled against HMRC.

The ruling yesterday means some of the investors who have already paid tax demands will be able to claim money back.

It is not clear how much each celebrity had put into the scheme, and there was no suggestion of wrongdoing by any of those involved.

The tax relief was meant to start economic growth in deprived areas – but HMRC said it amounted to ‘tax profit’.

The scheme’s allowances meant investors could claim 50% tax relief on the full £263million cost of the project, even though their total investment was only £79 million.

Many said at the time that were pleased to be investing in schemes they hoped would create jobs in the North East.

Two judges, Mr Justice Zacarol and Judge Jonathan Richards, ruled that the schemes did qualify for relief.

The sites were completed in 2012 and 2013 and are designed to house computer servers belonging to businesses and government organisations.

However, the court heard that since the data centres have been completed, efforts to find a tenant have been ‘unsuccessful’.

The judges said: ‘At no point since the appellants acquired their interests in the Data Centres have those Data Centres been let.’

Other celebrities who had invested into the scheme include Roy Hodgson, the late Status Quo guitarist Rick Parfitt, and Lady Elizabeth-Ann Redgrave, wife of Olympic legend Sir Steve.

The case threatens to drag on further, with judges giving HMRC the right to appeal.

Mr Justice Zacaroli said: ‘We invited further submissions from the parties as to how the apportionment of the price that which does and that which does not qualify for EZAs is to be calculated.’
