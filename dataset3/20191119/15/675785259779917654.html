Mario Balotelli paid £100k in Man City's fine funds after failing to follow rules

Micah Richards was in charge of fines at Man City during their first Premier League success and Mario Balotelli was a regular visitor to the fine shop - when he turned up

19 Nov 2019, 15:54 by Alex Smith

Mario Balotelli had a bad boy reputation at Manchester City - and it turns out he shelled out around £100,000-a-season in fines during his time at the Etihad.

Balotelli's former teammate Micah Richards has revealed all about the fine system at City during their famous maiden Premier League title winning season.

Defender Richards was in charge of collecting the fines, which come from being late, messy or generally anti-social, from inside the dressing room - with the funds being handed to charity.

"Everything else seems to be within sync. Balotelli was always late," Richards said on the BBC.

 

"We used to give all of our money to charities and by Christmas time we'd have £100,000 or £150,000 in there all from Balotelli being late. 

"He would be in the building. Say we had a meeting upstairs - he'd be downstairs just relaxing. He'd come to the meeting but he was just a bit dozy."

Richards was at City from 2001 and 2015 before moving to Aston Villa. He has not had a club since the end of last season.

The 13-cap England ace went on to joke about club legend Vincent Kompany and his diligent ways in keeping track of the fines and sticking to the letter of the law.

"Kompany, he's busy isn't he?," Richards continued. "He just looks like that person anyway. He always kept a track of the fines and because I was sort of vice-captain, one of the leaders in the dressing room, he'd ask me to take charge of it. 

"But I wasn't going to say to the manager 'Oh player was late today, oh player didn't put his bib in the washing machine'.

"So it was only if Vincent Kompany caught you. I'm not a snitch.

"You could appeal it as well. You had to go with Vincent Kompany into the manager's office. He had a fine sheet logged of all the fines. He's like a schoolteacher, headmaster. One of those guys that when you see him, you turn the other way.

"I tried to be friends with everyone. I told (Balotelli), "I'm going to let you off today". With him it was every other week so I could only do so much. 

"Kompany is looking over my shoulder making sure I was doing my job right. He undermined me a little bit, Kompany did'.
