History lesson that points to serious concern for Derby County's top-six hopes

The Rams have collected 21 points from a possible 48 in the Championship so far this season

19 Nov 2019, 14:47 by Steve Nicholson

Derby County will need to up their current points' return rate if they are to finish in the top six this season.

That is clear from the Championship table and from Derby's tally of 21 points from a possible 48.

Only twice in the past 10 seasons have Derby had fewer points at this stage.

They had 20 points in 2016-17, and finished ninth. They had 17 points in 2009-10, and finished 14th.

Closer scrutiny of Derby's current stats, however, show how Phillip Cocu's team will have to buck a trend if the season is to extend into the play-offs.

Only three teams in the past 10 seasons have secured a top-six finish from a position of 21 points or fewer after 16 matches.

Aston Villa, conquerors of Derby in last season's play-off final, had 21 points at this stage and yet they went on to finish fifth on 76.

They were helped, of course, by a run of 10 consecutive wins.

Villa's 4-0 thumping of Derby at the beginning of March sparked the run, and Dean Smith's team showcased a perfect example of hitting form at just the right time and going into the play-offs with momentum.

The previous season, 2017-18, Fulham had 19 points from 16 games - two fewer than Derby's current tally - and yet they rallied to finish third on 88 points. They went on to beat Villa in the play-off final and win promotion.

Reading are the only other team in the past 10 seasons to finish in the top six from 21 points or less at this stage. They had 21 in 2009-10 - and finished as champions on 89!

Derby have so far this season collected 44% of the points on offer.

The figure is significantly lower than the percentage they collected on each of the four occasions they finished in the top six in the past six seasons.

Last season, they finished sixth on 74 points (54%). In 2017-18, they also gathered 54% of the points available to finish sixth, while the figure was 57% (78 points) in 2015-16.

Derby finished third on 85 points in 2013-14 - a points return of 62%.
