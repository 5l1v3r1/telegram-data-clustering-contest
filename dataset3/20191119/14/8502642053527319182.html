CBOT Trends - Wheat, corn steady to up 1 cent; soy up 3-5 cents

19 Nov 2019, 14:13

CHICAGO, Nov 19 (Reuters) - Following are U.S. trade expectations for the opening of grain and soy complex trading at the Chicago Board of Trade at 8:30 a.m. CST (1430 GMT) on Tuesday.

WHEAT - Steady to up 1 cent per bushel

* Mild round of short-covering expected wheat after most-active CBOT soft red winter wheat contract hit lowest on a continuous basis since Oct. 11 on Monday. Technical resistance expected to curtail rally attempts.

* Benchmark CBOT December soft red winter wheat contract failed to hold support above five-day moving average during overnight trading session.

* CBOT December soft red winter wheat last traded up 1/4 cent at $5.07-1/2 per bushel. K.C. December hard red winter wheat was last up 1/4 cent at $4.18-3/4, and MGEX December spring wheat was last down 1/4 cent at $5.03.

CORN - Steady to up 1 cent per bushel

* Slow harvest pace supports corn but light demand limits strength in the market. Forecasts for rain in Brazil and Argentina pressuring corn.

* The U.S. Agriculture Department said that U.S. corn harvest was 76% complete as of Nov. 17. Analysts had been expecting 77%. The five-year average is 92%.

* CBOT December corn last traded up 1 cent at $3.68-3/4 per bushel.

SOYBEANS - Up 3 cents to 5 cents per bushel

* Soybean market firming on bargain buying after most-active contract dropped to lowest since Oct. 4 on Monday. Uncertainty about progress in U.S.-China trade negotiations, South American weather outlook keep gains in check.

* Benchmark CBOT January soybean futures contract rose above 100-day moving average overnight.

* USDA said soybean harvest was 91% complete, matching market expectations. The five-year average is 95%.

* CBOT January soybeans last traded up 3-1/4 cents at $9.13-1/2 per bushel. (Reporting by Mark Weinraub Editing by Nick Zieminski)
