Doctor Sleep review: The sequel does justice to The Shining

19 Nov 2019, 14:00 by James Cox

If you're a fan of Stanley Kubrick's cult classic The Shining, Doctor Sleep is a must-watch.

Director Mikey Flanagan gets it spot on despite the various challenges of such a project.

First, there's the difficulty in adapting a Stephen King novel.

His work is so prolific that countless books have been adopted for film or TV, normally they're terrible or excellent.

We felt Doctor Sleep fell into the later category.

Flanagan also had the challenge of doing justice to Kubrick's classic film.

He did it perfectly, paying tribute to the original and its characters at the right time while also making sure Doctor Sleep had its own identity.

It follows a grown-up Danny Torrence (Ewan McGregor) as he reluctantly returns to his 'Shine' powers to mentor a young girl named Abra (Kyleigh Curran) who has even stronger telepathic abilities than his own.

The old ghouls and monsters from The Shining rear their ugly heads once again but Flanagan resists the urge to be too indulgent with these established characters.

Instead, they simply supplement the new evil haunting the protagonists, a sinister cult called the True Knot.

The True Knot are led by the a brilliant, but terrifying, character called Rose the Hat.

An excellent performance from Rebecca Ferguson brings Rose's character to life.

And...... *slight spoiler alert if you prefer going into a film completely blind*... the return of the Overlook Hotel tops it off!

So often contemporary sequels to classics fail to get it right but that's not the case with Doctor Sleep.

Despite it's poor intake at the box office so far, it's well worth a watch and we give it four stars.

    ****
