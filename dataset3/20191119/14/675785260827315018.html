Woman suffers horrific injuries and almost loses eye after being mauled by kangaroo

The victim, only identified as Dina, was walking her dog in Victoria, Australia, when she was mauled by the animal, forcing her to play dead in a bid to survive

19 Nov 2019, 14:43 by Lorraine King

A woman suffered horrific facial injuries after she was attacked by a kangaroo while out walking her dog.

The victim, only identified as Dina, said she had to "play dead" to stop the mauling which took place in Victoria, Australia.

She had just let her pet off its leash when she found herself face to face with the giant creature.

She was viciously attacked when she intervened to stop her dog chasing the kangaroo.

She told 9News : "I ran up the embankment to try and get my dog away but not realising that I was only three or four metres behind the roo.

 

"And that's when I sensed the roo was panicking and thinking 'I'm being attacked on all sides.

"He looked at me and I knew what was going to happen.

"Something made me turn around. He attacked me from behind and he knocked me to the ground.

 

"He gouged out the back of my leg, I know now if I hadn't turned around, I would probably have been disembowelled."

Adding that there was nothing she could do to stop the mauling, she said: " I just thought this is happening, just wait until it is over.

 

"He started pounding on me and jumping on me and I played dead."

The area where Dina was attacked is metres from a local walking track.

There are warning signs from the council advising pet owners to keep their dogs on their leads.

 

Dina needed surgery for leg wounds and had 25 stitches on her face but despite it all she doesn't blame the kangaroo but her dog.

"If anything, we're encroaching on their ecology," she said.

 

"I could sense his panic I really felt for him the poor thing."

She said the attack serves as a warning to take care around wild animals and have a fearful respect for them.

Councillor Danny Lowe has issued a warning to residents advising them to be extra vigilant over summer.

He said the dry conditions are forcing kangaroos closer to homes. 

He added: "They are wild animals, they will defend themselves and they are territorial."
