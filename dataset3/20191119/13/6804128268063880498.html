Arsenal youth coach explains how club aims to emulate Ajax and produce the next Frenkie De Jong

Marcel Lucassen has discussed how he implemented a recognised structure into Arsenal's youth ranks

19 Nov 2019, 13:45 by Josh Challies

Arsenal's head of development Marcel Lucassen has explained how he has implemented a structure into the club's youth ranks, as they aim to emulate the likes of Barcelona and Ajax.

The Spanish and Dutch giants have become widely recognised for their ability to continuously churn out ready made players for the first-team, saving their clubs millions in the market.

Ajax's success last season in the Champions League made their development of young players mainstream news, with the Eredivisie side raising significant funds from the sales of Matthijs de Ligt and Frenkie De Jong.

Rather than reinvesting those profits, Ajax have stuck by their values by keeping faith in their young talents behind the scenes - and Lucassen has similar plans in North London.

“When I arrived at Arsenal, I firstly wanted to know, what the club stands for in general. The problem was that no coach could answer me that exactly," he told TransferMarkt.
