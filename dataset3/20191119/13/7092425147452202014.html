Sweden drops Julian Assange rape investigation

19 Nov 2019, 13:25 by James Cox

A Swedish prosecutor has confirmed the alleged rape investigation involving WikiLeaks founder Julian Assange, who is currently in prison in Britain, has been dropped.

Deputy Chief Prosecutor Eva-Marie Persson offered the update on the Swedish case on Tuesday.

In June, a Swedish court ruled that Assange shouldn't be detained. This meant that a preliminary investigation in Sweden continued, but he could be questioned in Britain.

Assange had been evicted from the Ecuadorian embassy in London two months beforehand.

The 48-year-old is currently serving a 50-week sentence in Britain for jumping bail in 2012.

He's also fighting extradition to the US, where he's accused of publishing secret documents.
