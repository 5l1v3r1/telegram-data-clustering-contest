Boris Johnson spars in boxing ring as Jeremy Corbyn gets 'quick trim' before ITV debate

As the Prime Minister got into the boxing ring this afternoon on a visit to Manchester, Corbyn took a more relaxed approach and headed for a haircut at the barbers

19 Nov 2019, 13:08 by Abigail O'Leary

Boris Johnson and Jeremy Corbyn have spent the day preparing for their first TV debate - in very different ways.

As the Prime Minister got into a boxing ring this afternoon while on a visit to Manchester, Corbyn took a more relaxed approach and headed for a "quick trim" at the barbers.

The Labour leader took to Instagram where he uploaded a clip of him in the barber shop chair, captioning it: "Quick trim before the first TV debate of the #GE2019. You can watch it tonight at 8pm on ITV. #RealChange."

Meanwhile on his visit to the Manchester boxing gym, Boris said he "loves" the sport.

The Prime Minister entered the ring with the head coach of Jimmy Egan's Boxing Academy in Wythenshawe to practice his punches.

Mr Johnson, who removed his suit jacket and rolled up his sleeves for the occasion, donned a navy blue pair of "Get Brexit Done" gloves in the ring.

 

The PM said he should not be "aggressive" and instead said it was "more therapeutic boxing" as he joked about punching photographers on the side.

Mr Johnson left the ring as two more serious boxers entered, saying he would "quit while he's ahead".

Steve Egan, the boxing coach who sparred with the Prime Minister in the ring, told PA news agency Mr Johnson was "strong" but a "bit rusty".

Tonight's debate will be hosted by ITV regular host  Julie Etchingham , who previously hosted the Conservative party leader debate between Johnson and  Jeremy Hunt  .

Corbyn and Johnson will each have a minute for opening statements and then 45 seconds for closing statements, but in between they will be asked questions from ITV viewers from varying political beliefs and socio-economic backgrounds.

It is the first time the leaders of the two main parties have squared off since 2015 - and it is set to be dramatic.

 

Brexit  , the NHS, the economy, relations with the US, and each of the pair's own personal leadership troubles will likely be up for debate.

The Lib Dems and  SNP  took legal action against ITV for keeping them out of its first debate. - but lost their court battle yesterday.

Two leading judges ruled there was "no arguable breach of the Broadcasting Code".

Lord Justice Davis, sitting with Mr Justice Warby, said: "The clear conclusion of both members of this court is that, viewed overall, these claims are not realistically arguable.

"It follows that the television debate scheduled for tomorrow evening between the leader of the Conservative Party and the leader of the  Labour  Party may lawfully go ahead."

ITV said that following the debate between Boris Johnson and Jeremy Corbyn it will broadcast a live programme where Jo Swinson, Nicola Sturgeon, Nigel Farage and Sian Berry will be interviewed in consecutive face-to-face interviews.

Scottish First Minister Ms Sturgeon said last week that it is "fundamentally unfair" for other parties to be cut out of the debate.

Sky News has proposed a November 28 date for its debate, while the BBC has confirmed it will host two debates, on November 29 and December 6, in addition to a series of Question Time specials.
