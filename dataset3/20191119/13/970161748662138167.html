Man Utd's Daniel James compared to PSG superstar Kylian Mbappe by Hungary boss

19 Nov 2019, 13:03

Daniel James might be the quickest player in world football, with only Kylian Mbappe able to challenge him for pace, reckons Hungary manager Marco Rossi.

Hungary are preparing to take on Wales in an all-important Euro 2020 qualifier in Cardiff on Tuesday night, with the winners qualifying for the tournament next summer.

A draw could be no good for either side, as if Slovakia can beat Azerbaijan then they will overtake both Hungary and Wales to grab second place.

Rossi will be looking for all three points in Cardiff, but is well aware of the threat that James poses, having been very impressed with the flying Manchester United winger.

‘James is a player that is playing with a motorbike because he is really fast,’ said Rossi ahead of the Wales clash.

‘I have never seen someone in the last period as fast as him, maybe Mbappe. He’s unbelievable.

‘If we leave him space to run he could be dangerous. This could be the key, but we work a lot on the defensive line and will try to give confidence to our players.’

James arrived at Old Trafford from Swansea for £15m and has made an impressive step up in his first season in the Premier League.

The 22-year-old has featured in all 12 of Manchester United’s Premier League games this campaign, scoring three goals and registering two assists.

He has also been key for Wales, starting all seven qualification matches, and scoring the crucial only goal in their 1-0 win over Slovakia in March.

Wales boss Ryan Giggs is a big fan of the winger, and even compared him to another former Manchester United superstar, Cristiano Ronaldo.

‘Dan takes a lot of knocks but gets up, gets on with it and demands the ball back,’ said Giggs.

‘Ronaldo, especially earlier on in his career, took a lot of knocks and would still demand the ball. It shows huge bravery.

‘United fans like players who like to attack, who gets the fans off their feet — and he’s somebody who does that.

‘With the form he’s in, he has been outstanding and a constant threat. He’s added goals and assists, which is a wingers’ game. He will get better and better — and I’m pleased for him.’
