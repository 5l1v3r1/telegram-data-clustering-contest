Louis Saha reveals Gary Neville predicted he’d miss penalty against Celtic

19 Nov 2019, 13:14 by David Fowler

LOUIS SAHA has revealed Gary Neville predicted he'd miss from the spot against Celtic in the Champions League.

The duo were starring for Manchester United on that famous night in 2006 that Hoops fans will never forget.

Shunsuke Nakamura netted an unforgettable free-kick before a late Artur Boruc penalty save secured three huge European points at Parkhead.

Glasgow's East End was rocking as 60,000 fans celebrated the missed effort from 12-yards.

And Saha admits he was raging after reading what his team-mate has said in the newspaper.

He told FourFourTwo: "The worst memory about it is that my own team-mate, Gary Neville, actually predicted I'd miss the penalty!

"He said that to someone, then I read it in a newspaper - I was fuming!

"But with Gary, we argued a lot, so no change there.

"It's about how you react, and the following weekend I scored against Chelsea , one of the best goals I scored in my career."

That last-minute penalty save earned Gordon Strachan's men a place in the Champions League knockout stages.

And 13 years on from the cagey affair at Parkhead, Saha has praised Boruc for his stop.

He added: "Of course, I have to credit Celtic 's goalkeeper for the penalty.

"It was a good save."
