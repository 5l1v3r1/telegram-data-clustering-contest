Next steps for Skaha Park?

19 Nov 2019, 05:00 by Colin Dacre

The City of Penticton is getting ready to kick off public consultations on the future of Skaha Lake Park, four years after the park's waterslide debacle rocked city hall. 

Council will hear a presentation Tuesday from staff on the creation of a new master plan for the east end of the park.

“When looking at the entire park, city staff and several key stakeholders now hold the common view that any long-term planning for Skaha Park should focus on the east side,” said acting director of development services, Ben Johnson. 

“Much of the park is functioning well and is in good condition, however there are challenges and opportunities to explore in this part of the park, including the need for a long-term vision for the marina, aging facilities like the spray park and boat house, a desire to improve walking connections between areas of the park, and beach erosion concerns.”

The city says the creation of the plan will be “informed through a public engagement process,” that will “temperature check” the public on the issue of commercial operations in parks. 

Primarily, the city will gauge support for the Skaha Lake Marina and what types of accompanying amenities (ie. cafe or restaurant) that the community will tolerate. 

The master plan will aim to create a foundation for a long-term lease at the marina, where the existing operator is entering year two of a two-year lease.

The Gallagher family is currently operating the marina after the City of Penticton paid $200,000 to exit the 29-lease with the Trio Marine Group that outraged the community.

“Council is looking forward to receiving the presentation staff have prepared outlining the process by which a much needed plan for the east side of Skaha Park will be created with the public’s involvement,” said Mayor John Vassilaki.
