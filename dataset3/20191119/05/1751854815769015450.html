In India, the base of the Redmi Note 8 that is available for purchase online has 4 GB of RAM. Now, Xiaomi has introduced a new starting variant of the Note 8 for the offline markets in the country. It comes with 3 GB of RAM.

The Redmi Note 8 that was introduced in October in India comes in two models. The 4 GB RAM + 64 GB storage base model and 6 GB RAM + 128 GB storage higher variant are respectively priced at Rs. 9,999 (~$139) and Rs. 12,999 (~$181). These models can be availed through Amazon.in and Mi.com. According to 91mobiles, the new 3 GB RAM + 32 GB storage base model for the Redmi Note 8 is exclusively for the offline markets of India. It carries a price tag of Rs. 9,799 (~$136). Apart from the amount of RAM and storage on the new model, there is no other difference between the new and existing models.

Redmi Note 8 Specifications

The Redmi Note 8 has a 6.3-inch IPS LCD full HD+ resolution that offers 19.5:9 aspect ratio. The display of the phone is protected by Gorilla Glass 5. The same reinforced glass safeguards the backside of the device. The MIUI 10 based Android 9 Pie OS runs on the device.

The Snapdragon 665 mobile platform that includes 2.0GHz octa-core processor powers the Redmi Note 8 smartphone along with Adreno 610 graphics. The rear side of the phone has 48-megapixel (Sony IMX586) + 8-megapixel (ultrawide) + 2-megapixel (macro) and 2-megapixel (depth assist) quad camera system is found on the rear side of the Redmi Note 8. For capturing selfies, the Redmi Note 8 has a front-mounted camera of 13-megapixel.

The Redmi Note 8 has a rear-positioned fingerprint reader. The device is backed by a 4,000mAh battery that supports 18W fast charging through USB-C. It comes in colors like Neptune Blue, Moonlight White, Space Black and Nebula Purple in India.

In related news, Xiaomi introduced the Redmi Note 8T smartphone in Europe in November. The Note 8T and Note 8 have identical specs and the only major difference is that the Note 8T has support for NFC connectivity.
