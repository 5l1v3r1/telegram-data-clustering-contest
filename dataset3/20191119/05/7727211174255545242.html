Are these the worst and best toilets ever in Liverpool?

This World Toilet Day people across Liverpool have flushed out public conveniences to praise and condemn

19 Nov 2019, 05:00 by Jenny Kirkham

People across Liverpool have had their say on the best and worst toilets in the city.

According to United Nations Water, 4.5 billion people live without a safe toilet. Which is why World Toilet Day aims to raise awareness about the billions of people across the world don't have access to toilets and sanitation.

World Toilet Day, celebrated on November 19, is about taking action to ensure that everyone has a safe toilet by 2030.

So whether it's a tip top toilet or bog standard here are some of the best and worst in Liverpool from down the years - decided by you.

Krazyhouse

On Facebook, Jason Walsh made his feelings about the former Krazyhouse toilets known to everyone.

The venue has now changed hands and returned with a facelift but the memories of the old toilets clearly live on.

Speaking about the ex-Wood Street club, he said: "Nowhere will ever be worse than the Krazyhouse toilets."

Le Bateau

Despite the love for former Duke Street venue Le Bateau, a former Liverpool student described the club's toilets as a "death trap" before it closed.

They said: "Le Bateau was an amazing venue but the toilets on the top floor felt like a death trap - and often smelled like something had already died in there"

Pleasure Rooms

The Pleasure Rooms are nostalgically remembered by many people across Liverpool - but some would soon rather forget the state of its bathrooms.

Ste Welsby said: "The pleasure rooms toilets at 3am onwards. Hands down the worst toilers since time began."

No love for toilets at city ASDA supermarkets

A number of ECHO readers commented on the cleanliness of a number of supermarket toilets across Liverpool.

Fred Holtham said: "Asda Smithdown Road are the worst ever. The smell alone stops you from going in."

Antonia Curran also responded about the toilets at the ASDA supermarket in Hunts Cross.

She said: "Asda Huntscross are the worst."

In reply, Wendy Moulton said: "I swear they are never cleaned."

Jemma Simpson added: "Ahh yeah I agree they smell so bad x"

Lime Street Station

The toilets at Lime Street Station are clearly an area of tension due to the cleanliness and the fact you have to pay to use them.

Wendy Aston said: "Lime street station toilets are a nightmare!

"The cubicles are tiny and the sinks are ridiculous, you can't wash your hands properly."

And now for some of the best...

Clayton Square and Metquarter came out on top as the people's choice.

Emma Bowles said: "Met quarter hands down the best."

Dan Ambrose also said: "Clayton Square or Met Quarter."

Laura Jane added: "Clayton Square! Never a queue, pristine and there’s a ledge for your shopping."

A number of newcomers also appeared in the comments.

Thomas Johnston said: "Excellent toilets in the Liverpool Central Library and all of the Merseyside Museums."

Dave Nimmo added: "Heebie jeebies ladies loos are lovely according to my Mrs."

James Downes also said: "The Club House on Chavasse Park. Absolutely brilliant."
