New York man pleads guilty to making death threats against Rep. Ilhan Omar

19 Nov 2019, 05:01 by Ben Feuerherd

The upstate New York man who called Rep. Ilhan Omar’s office and threatened to shoot her after calling her a “terrorist” pleaded guilty to the crime Monday.

Patrick Carlineo, 55, faces up to 10 years in prison for the March 21 threat. He’s scheduled to be sentenced on Feb. 13.

Carlineo was arrested after the incident and later told federal investigators that he is a “patriot” and that he hates radical Muslims who are critical of President Trump.

“He has taken responsibility for using threatening and inappropriate language to express [his] beliefs in this instance,” his lawyer said in a statement.

Omar, one of the first Muslim women elected to Congress, has been the target of harsh anti-Muslim criticism.

Trump has been a vocal critic of Omar, most recently calling her a “fraud” and “disgrace” at a rally in her Minnesota district.

Omar’s office did not have a comment on Carlineo’s admission.
