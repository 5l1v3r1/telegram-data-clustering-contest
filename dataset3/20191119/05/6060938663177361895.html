Davis Cup: Nur-Sultan may host India-Pakistan tie

19 Nov 2019, 05:25 by K Kumaraswamy

PUNE: Nur-Sultan, the capital city of Kazakhstan, has emerged as the most probable venue for India's Davis Cup tie against Pakistan to be played on November 29-30.

Although the International Tennis Federation was yet to issue an official statement, the All India Tennis Association has initiated the visa process for the team members and the rest of the travelling contingent.

"It could be indoor hard courts in Astana. But we cannot say anything until we receive an official communication from the International Tennis Federation," Hironmoy Chatterjee, AITA honorary secretary, said on Monday. "Our boys are used to the hard courts as they play all year around on that surface," Chatterjee added, when asked if the new venue could prompt a change in the team.

Nur-Sultan, previously known as Astana, is the second largest city of Kazakhstan after Almaty, the country's former capital.

Bopanna pulls out

In another development, Jeevan Nedunchezhiyan was drafted into the main squad on Monday after Rohan Bopanna withdrew from the squad citing a shoulder injury. "I have pulled out due to a supraspinatus tendinopathy with an intrasubstance tear," Bopanna told TOI.

Bopanna was expected to partner Paes in the doubles rubber and Rajpal hinted that Jeevan could combine with the veteran.

Aisam to boycott tie

The Pakistan team received a bigger setback with their star player Aisam-ul-Haq Qureishi writing to his parent association (PTF) that he would boycott the tie if it was moved out of his home soil.

On Monday, Qureshi tweeted a link to a news report which said he had written to PTF president Saleem Saifullah conveying his decision to opt out of the tie.
