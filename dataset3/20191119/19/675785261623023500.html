Mauricio Pochettino sacked by Tottenham Hotspur after disappointing start to season

Mauricio Pochettino has left Tottenham Hotspur, having led the club to the Champions League final last season, after struggling so far this term

19 Nov 2019, 19:40 by Alex Richards, https://www.facebook.com/AARichards/

Mauricio Pochettino's Tottenham Hotspur five-year reign is no more after he was sacked by Spurs on Tuesday night.

A club statement declared that the Argentine, along with his coaching staff of Jesus Perez, Miguel D’Agostino and Antoni Jimenez, have been relieved of their duties.

Just over five months ago, Pochettino was on the brink of Champions League glory following Spurs' remarkable run to the final, the culmination of a season in which he was also heavily linked with the jobs at Manchester United and Real Madrid.

But after defeat to Liverpool in Madrid, Spurs have endured a wretched start to the current campaign.

Spurs have won just three league games so far this season and only six of their last 24. Pochettino has cut a frustrated figure for much of the current season.

 

Of the 14 Premier League sides to spend the entirety of 2019 in the top flight, no side suffered more than the 18 defeats managed by Tottenham and since summer, he has cut a frustrated figure

Chairman, Daniel Levy, said: "We were extremely reluctant to make this change and it is not a decision the Board has taken lightly, nor in haste.

“Regrettably domestic results at the end of last season and beginning of this season have been extremely disappointing.

“It falls to the Board to make the difficult decisions - this one made more so given the many memorable moments we have had with Mauricio and his coaching staff - but we do so in the Club's best interests.

 

“Mauricio and his coaching staff will always be part of our history. I have the utmost admiration for the manner in which he dealt with the difficult times away from a home ground whilst we built the new stadium and for the warmth and positivity he brought to us.

"I should like to thank him and his coaching staff for all they have contributed. They will always be welcome here.

“We have a talented squad. We need to re-energise and look to deliver a positive season for our supporters.” 

 

Levy has had to pay big money to part with Pochettino and his staff, all of whom signed new five-year contracts in May 2018.

That number could be as much as the £19.6million that Manchester United paid to dismiss Jose Mourinho and his staff in December 2018.

Pochettino was at the helm for 293 games and delivered four successive top-four Premier League finishes, challenging for the title in 2015/16 and 2016/17.

Pochettino will have no shortage of suitors when he decides to return to the game.

As well as having long-term admirers in the shape of United and Real Madrid, he is also admired by Bayern Munich chiefs, with the Bavarians currently without a manager following the exit of Niko Kovac.
