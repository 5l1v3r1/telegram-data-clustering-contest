Alabama vs. Furman: Underdog Paladins can win outright

19 Nov 2019, 19:00 by Greg Peterson, VSiN

Since the 2014-15 season began, the best against-the-spread team has been the Furman Paladins. In this span, they are 89-50-1 against the number as they hit the road Tuesday to play a new-look Alabama team that is not off to a solid start.

The Crimson Tide rank 302nd in the country in points per game allowed with 77.7, but are 155th in defensive efficiency, which is points allowed on a per-possession basis. This means Alabama is trying to play much faster, which makes sense with new coach Nate Oats coming in from Buffalo.

Furman entered Monday ranked 13th in the country in points scored per possession, thanks to 64.9 percent accuracy on 2-point shots. Alabama rates 261st in 2-point shooting percentage on defense.

The Paladins are built around a pair of forwards in Noah Gurley and Clay Mounce who both are averaging at least 15.8 points and 5.8 rebounds per game.
