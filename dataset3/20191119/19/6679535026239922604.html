Don’t bow to Syndicates’ pressure, CM tells VCs

19 Nov 2019, 19:44 by Staff Reporter

Chief Minister Pinarayi Vijayan has counselled Vice Chancellors against bowing to the pressure exerted by Syndicates to indulge in unlawful activities that could potentially tarnish the reputation of universities.

Mr. Vijayan issued the advice while convening a meeting of the Vice Chancellors of all 14 State universities and senior officials of the Higher Education Department here on Tuesday.

His remarks assumed importance as they were made against the backdrop of controversies surrounding marks being moderated in certain varsities at the behest of Syndicates.

During the meeting, he also raised the oft-repeated complaint of shortage of skilled employees that was commonly cited by industrial houses that have commenced business in the State. He exhorted the universities to commence industry-oriented courses to address the lacunae.

Migration

The Chief Minister mooted a proposal to enable students to migrate from one university to others midway through their courses. Referring to the bright job prospects for nurses abroad, he also suggested measures to teach various foreign languages in addition to English.

In view of the controversies that have cropped up regarding the conduct of examinations, Mr. Vijayan directed the Vice Chancellors to expedite the automation process that were under way at the universities.

As part of such efforts, a biometric system must be introduced in the examination wings to prevent malpractices and unauthorised access.

Besides, barcoding must be introduced for answer sheets in universities that were yet to adopt the practice.

Academic reforms

The meeting deliberated the feasibility of implementing various academic reforms, including the introduction of training in basic life support in the curriculum of all universities with the assistance of experts from medical colleges.

At the meeting

Higher Education Minister K.T. Jaleel, Higher Education Principal Secretary Usha Titus, Kerala State Higher Education Council vice chairman Rajan Gurukkal, Chief Minister’s Principal Secretary V.S. Senthil and Officer on Special Duty M. Sivasankar were also present at the meeting.
