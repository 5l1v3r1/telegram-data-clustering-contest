Mauricio Pochettino sacked by Tottenham

19 Nov 2019, 19:57 by James Cox

Tottenham have made the bold call to sack Mauricio Pochettino and his coaching staff.

Pochettino led Spurs to top four finishes in four of his five seasons in charge while they reached their first Champions League final last season.

However, the club has suffered a huge slump in form this season with just five victories so far.

Spurs are currently 14th in the Premier League table.

Club chairman Daniel Levy said a change had to be made in the "club's best interests".

Levy said: "Regrettably domestic results at the end of last season and beginning of this season have been extremely disappointing.

"It falls to the Board to make the difficult decisions - this one made more so given the many memorable moments we have had with Mauricio and his coaching staff - but we do so in the Club's best interests.

"Mauricio and his coaching staff will always be part of our history.

"I have the utmost admiration for the manner in which he dealt with the difficult times away from a home ground whilst we built the new stadium and for the warmth and positivity he brought to us.

"I should like to thank him and his coaching staff for all they have contributed. They will always be welcome here.

"We have a talented squad. We need to re-energise and look to deliver a positive season for our supporters."

Pochettino has previously been heavily linked with Manchester United and Real Madrid, both of whom have managers at the moment.

It'll probably be a while until Spurs announce his successor but former United manager Jose Mourinho is the bookies favourite.
