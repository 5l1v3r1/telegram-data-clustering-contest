57 children receive intestinal infections at Smiltene’s secondary school

19 Nov 2019 by LETA

According to deputy principal of Secondary School in Smiltene Inese Raiskuma, 57 children have received virus-induced intestinal infections.

She said that last week children had experienced vomiting and diarrhoea at home in the evening. This condition remained on the next day at school. This prompted the school to call in epidemiologists and cancel all celebrations associated with the national holiday.

«Samples of food products and samples of different surfaces were taken. We are waiting for answers. Additional disinfection was performed over the weekends. Parents have been advised on measures to the performed for affected children,» said Raiskuma.

Smiltene Secondary School’s principal Ilze Vergina reports that all infected children may return to school only once their family doctor declares they are healthy.

There are 314 children, including 185 primary school children and 129 pre-school children, study at this school.

Disease Prevention and Control Centre (SPKC) public relations head Ilze Arāja has confirmed the centre is aware of the incident in Smiltene. Results of laboratory tests will be reported within one day’s time.

Food and Veterinary Service expanded control measures following the incident in Sigulda, when intestinal infections were found in multiple kindergartens, where infected poultry produced by Polish Cebrob S.A. was used to prepare meals.

Salmonella enteritidis bacteria were found in samples taken from poultry served by SIA Gaļsaimnieks, as well as SIA EVBL.

As previously reported, while performing increased control of intestinal infections in Sigulda, SPKC also uncovered cases of salmonella infections. Children who went to two kindergartens in Sigulda (Ieviņa and Tornīši) were infected with salmonella.

At the beginning of October, stomach infections were found for approximately 50 employees of Stockman supermarket. Catering services for Stockman employees during Trakajās dienas were provided by SIA Baltic Restaurants Latvia.
