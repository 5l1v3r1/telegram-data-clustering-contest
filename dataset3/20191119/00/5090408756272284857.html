A new 'Half-Life' game is finally coming out after 12 years — but it seems headed to virtual reality only, Business Insider - Business Insider Singapore

19 Nov 2019, 00:04 by Matt Weinberger, Business Insider US
  • The good news: Valve has confirmed that a new “Half-Life” game is in the works, with a formal debut to come Thursday morning.
  • The weird part: Valve says it’s a “flagship VR game,” a strong indication that it’ll be available only for SteamVR, its PC virtual-reality platform.
  • Regardless, the confirmation of a new “Half-Life” game ends a 12-year drought for the many fans of the series.

In 2007, the mega-hit first-person shooter “Half-Life 2: Episode 2” ended on a massive cliffhanger, with the evil alien Combine having achieved a massive victory that left the heroic Gordon Freeman and his sidekick Alyx in an impossibly sticky situation.

Fans have since been waiting 12 long years and multiple console and PC hardware generations since for any update on the “Half-Life” franchise.

The good news, then, is that the wait is finally coming to an end: The developer Valve Software has confirmed on Twitter that a new game called “Half-Life: Alyx” will be formally announced on a livestream Thursday morning.

The catch is that Valve also says it will be a “flagship VR game.” While we’ll have to wait for more details, this is a strong indication that “Half-Life: Alyx” will be available only for SteamVR, Valve’s PC virtual-reality platform.
