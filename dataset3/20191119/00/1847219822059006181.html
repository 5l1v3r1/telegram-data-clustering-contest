Moscow returns seized Ukrainian ships

19 Nov 2019 by BNN

Russian coast guard has returned three Ukrainian navy ships that Moscow seized in an incident in the Kerch Strait in 2018.

German public broadcaster Deutsche Welle reports, that three Ukrainian ships were returned on Monday, November 18, almost a year after the Russian navy seized the vessels in the Kerch Strait.

Ukrainian authorities confirmed the return, a day after tug boats were seen towing the vessels through the strait that separates Crimea from the Russian mainland. Russia’s Ministry of Foreign Affairs previously announced the handover on Monday.

The step was «in accordance with the agreements reached with the Ukrainian side,» sources from Russian intelligence agency FSB’s Crimean branch told Russian news agency Interfax.

DW reports that the news comes ahead of a crucial summit between the leaders of Ukraine and Russia. The handover is seen as an important step in resolving the ongoing five-year conflict in eastern Ukraine.
