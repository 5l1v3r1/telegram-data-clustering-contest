Paqueta then Coutinho: Brazil's two great goals against Korea

19 Nov 2019 by BeSoccer

Brazil opened the scoring after just nine minutes against South Korea, with a good header from Paqueta. Coutinho then made it two thanks to a great free-kick which went into the top corner.

In the first half of the encounter between Brazil and South Korea, Brazil showed their quality and attacking threat against a South Korean side who could not hit on the target. Alisson had next to nothing to do early on. 

After just nine minutes of the fame, Coutinho, with the ball at his boots, cut inside the box, laid it off for Renan Lodi with a pass with the inside of his foot and Paqueta headed his cross brilliantly past goalkeeper Cho Hyun-Woo.
