SCOTTISH SUN SAYS

The SNP’s climate crusade is undermined by the number of domestic flights taken by government transport officials

19 Nov 2019, 00:29 by Scottish Sun

BARELY a day goes by without the SNP hailing their climate crusade.

Net-zero carbon emissions are promised by 2045. They’re “world-leading” targets, we’re repeatedly told.

And a major part of achieving this aim will come from cutting transport emissions.

Well, let’s hope someone has told the rest of the Scottish Government.

Because, as ever, it also seems like a case of one rule for them, and another for the rest of us.

We learned yesterday that Scottish Government transport officials had taken nearly 900 domestic flights in five years.

That’s despite its own internal guidance encouraging staff to use the “greenest and most cost-effective” transport methods available.

And the numbers of domestic flights by the Government shows no sign of coming down.

This all comes a matter of months after The Scottish Sun revealed how chauffeur-driven trips by SNP ministers had hit a record high.

It doesn’t look great, at a time when the green agenda permeates every major statement from the First Minister.

In response, we got a load of waffle from Transport Scotland about how officials were always “mindful of their carbon footprint” and “try to strike a balance”.

Crucially, there was no explanation of why so many flights were needed for travelling within the UK, as opposed to, say, taking the train.

Predictably, at a time when emissions need to come down,  we get nothing but hot air.

Bad choice

WE already knew Labour were struggling for reputable election candidates.

Earlier this month we told how a dozen wannabe MPs had already become embroiled in slur scandals. But it seems the desperation runs even deeper.

Now, we find Scottish Labour’s latest poster boy — fronting an election video on social media  — has a dubious track record, including a conviction for thuggish behaviour during rioting in London in 2010.

Bryan Simpson also threw an infamous Margaret Thatcher “death party” in Glasgow city centre in 2013.

Many, many, Scots — understandably — had little time, if any, for Mrs Thatcher.

But hundreds of people doing a conga around George Square to celebrate the death of an old woman seems as unseemly today as it did six years ago.

It’s this kind of extreme and unsettling behaviour that dogs Labour under Jeremy Corbyn. The aggressive brand of politics employed by hard-left firebrands is alien to the moderate majority.

It’s one of the reasons Labour are polling at 12 per cent in Scotland — and could lose all but one of their seven MPs here.

And why the bookies make Mr Corbyn a rank outsider to win a majority at the December 12 election.
