Prosecutors In Sweden Drop Probe Into Julian Assange Rape Allegation

19 Nov 2019 by Isabel Togoh

Topline: Swedish prosecutors have dropped their investigation into an allegation of sexual assault made against WikiLeaks founder Julian Assange in 2010.

Crucial quote: Deputy chief prosecutor Eva-Marie Persson said in a statement on Tuesday: "I want to inform about my decision to discontinue the preliminary investigation."

Key background: Prosecutors in Sweden had been investigating allegations of rape against Assange until they dropped the case in 2017 and reopened it after he was arrested earlier this year. Meanwhile, lawyers for the U.S. government have been fighting to extradite Assange to the States to face trial for conspiracy to commit computer intrusion, and face extra charges of breaching the Espionage Act by publicising classified information through WikiLeaks.
