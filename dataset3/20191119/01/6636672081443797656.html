Noise Is A Problem In Transistors

19 Nov 2019, 01:07 by David Manners

NOISE in transistors is a far more complex problem than in thermionic devices.

So, 59 years ago, started a story in Electronics Weekly’s edition of November 2nd 1960

The story continues:

Mullard Ltd. are working on the problem of specifying noise and identifying it by parameters.

“There is need for a relatively simple system of noise parameters,” say Mullard, “which will enable the noise factor obtained when a transistor is fed from any particular source impedance to be calculated.”

It is hoped for most purposes to reduce these parameters to two, dependent on collector current, and also on collector voltage.

Work is also being carried out to isolate the components of out- put, due to a drift resulting from temperature change, and those due to noise at extremely low fre- quencies.

To enable this work to be carried out Mullard have further developed their two-stage temperature controller to the point where a stability, short term, of 100 deg. C is obtainable.
