'I stabbed my grandparents to death after I told them my dad had sexually abused me'

David Barnett was sentenced to death when he was convicted of brutally murdering his grandparents but speaking to I Am A Killer he claims he has no memory of the murder

19 Nov 2019, 10:55 by Jane Lavender, https://www.facebook.com/dailymirror/

Pensioners Clifford and Leona Barnett were found in a pool of their own blood having been stabbed more than 20 times in a brutal killing in their own home.

But it was no intruder who had sadistically killed the elderly couple - it was their own grandson, David Barnett.

Barnett, who was sentenced to death for the murders, used five different knives to ensure both Leona and Clifford were dead.

He calmly walked into his grandparents home while the God-fearing couple were at a church service at the nearby Kirkwood Baptist Church.

Barnett even fell asleep while watching TV on the sofa as he lay in wait for his unsuspecting grandparents to return home.

 

But the orgy of violence that followed when they returned home at 1pm was anything but calm.

First, Barnett shoved his grandmother down the hall and shoved his grandfather to the floor and grabbed a knife.

He stabbed Clifford 10 times in his face neck and head before he was satisfied he was dead.

Barnett then turned his attention to his grandmother.

He grabbed another two knives and stabbed her 12 times on the neck and face.

The attack was so savage several of the knives used to stab them to death, snapped.

Certain they were both dead, Barnett the hid one of the knives under a mattress, washed the blood off his hands, pulled the blinds in the house and took the cars keys and money from his grandmother's purse and fled the house.

Police arrested Barnett the following morning when they found the victims' car in a residential area.

He immediately confessed to the murders and was sentenced to death.

For years, Barnett refused to say why he had carried out the seemingly senseless crimes.

But now, in Crime+Investigation's I Am A Killer, he has revealed the appalling abuse he says he suffered at the hands of his adopted father, their son, John Barnett.

Barnett had a troubled upbringing from the moment he was born.

His mother rejected him and he lived with a man who beat him, molested him and showed him no love.

Barnett said: "I had to find my own food, I wasn't washed. I was like a stuffed toy kept on the shelf. I was alone."

When he was five, Barnett was taken into care and by the time he was seven he was living with Rita Reames and her family - and finally had to love a little boy needs.

But then Rita was given the chance to study abroad and because Barnett was her foster son was unable to take him with her.

He said: "I thought I had done something wrong. I thought they were getting rid of me because I hadn't done something right."

Back in the care system, Barnett went to live with John Barnett when he was eight.

It wasn't long before the computer teacher adopted the little boy.

Barnett said: "I was ecstatic. That was one of the happiest days of my whole childhood."

Within just a few months Barnett says he began to notice things were going very wrong.

He said: "He had high expectations and he would hit me if I didn't meet them. But then he would comfort me by hugging me and kissing my ears.

"He would touch me inappropriately and I started to black out when I was sitting on his lap. I felt numb and I wanted to die."

Then John adopted two more boys, with the youngest, Eric, becoming the focus of his attention.

Barnett said: "I was the oldest and I was supposed to protect them but I was also glad because it wasn't happening to me any more."

The three boys and John lived on the same street as their grandparents, Clifford and Leona.

While Barnett adored his grandmother he couldn't separate Clifford from his son.

Then, when he was 15, Barnett and his friend, Jason Kingdon, claim they found a photo in John's bedside cabinet of him holding a young boys genitals.

They took it straight to the police but Jason says their concerns were dismissed.

Then John fostered a fourth child, Secil Shodroski, who had been having a hard time at home.

While she says she didn't see John abusing the boys physically or sexually, she has her own harrowing story.

Secil explained: "He would wait for the boys to go to sleep and he would rub me and touch me. I thought it was OK because I loved him but I was just a little girl.

"He was a paedophile and a predator."

Secil was also growing close to Barnett as the pair spent more time together and they became a couple.

She fell pregnant with their son, Seth, and the couple set up home.

Secil said: "David was so proud of Seth, he wanted to hold and feed him all the time."

But as a young man, Barnett was often out late with his friends and Secil couldn't cope.

She said: "I didn't know how to handle it and I know I pushed him away, which hurt him because people had been pushing him away his whole life."

Less than a year later, Barnett had stabbed his grandparents to death.

He finally confided in Jason that he had gone to Clifford and Leona's home that day to tell them about the abuse he had suffered at the hands of their son.

 

Jason said: "He wanted them to know what John was doing and he told them everything.

"They were hurt and offended and didn't want to hear it."

To this day, Barnett says he can't remember what happened as he stabbed his grandparents to death.

He explained: "I remember the beginning and the result was horrifying. There isn't a day that goes by when I don't think of them, they live in my heart.

"I think Leona would give be a hug and forgive me, so would Clifford."

 

At Barnett's trial, no details emerged about the abuse he had suffered and John has never been prosecuted.

But his death sentence was taken on by a legal team who were adamant his harrowing story should be heard.

Earlier this year, Barnett's death sentence was reduced to life without parole.

And he hopes one day that he will be allowed to apply for parole and live a normal life.

He said: "I believe I deserve to be where I am and that I deserve that situation and am blessed to have it.

"The more support I had the more I was willing to live. I believe I will be out and hopefully I can be a productive member of society."

Amazingly, he also has no anger towards the adoptive father who caused him so much pain when he was a child and who died in 2017.

Barnett said: "I don't hate John, I still love him for what he tried to do. He had his own problems that he couldn't overcome."
