Meet the woman, 87, banned from YMCA charity shop - after causing 'too much trouble'

Brenda, from Liverpool, said she visited her local YMCA charity shop on Three Tuns Lane in Formby Village on Sunday

19 Nov 2019, 10:38 by James Rodger, Charlotte Hadfield, https://www.facebook.com/jamesrodgerjournalist

Meet the 87-year-old who has been barred from a charity shop - because she 'causes too much trouble'.

The pensioner says she was told police would be called when she visited her local shop.

Brenda, from Liverpool, said she visited her local YMCA charity shop on Three Tuns Lane in Formby Village on Sunday, reports the Liverpool ECHO.

But the OAP was told by a manager that she was "barred" from the store and if she did not leave they would "phone the police."

Brenda, who is deaf and partially sighted, lives with her 91-year-old husband Lesley.

She she was left feeling "pertrified" and "angry" at being told to leave, because she had "done nothing wrong."

"It's the first time I've been into the shop since August," she said.

 

"So I walked into the shop yesterday and after I'd walked three quarters of the way around because I was looking for something for my husband - that's all, I wasn't being intrusive - this woman comes over all of a sudden.

"She said 'you're barred from here, you shouldn't be in this shop.'

"I said 'I beg your pardon?' and she said 'you know you're barred from this shop because you come in here and you cause trouble'."

She said: "I never cause any trouble, I'm an old lady.

"I went back to the counter and I said 'can I have a quiet word with you or someone senior?'

"I just wanted to tell her that I can't see the prices so I have to get people to help me read things.

 

"She said 'get out now or I will call the police'.

"I was petrified. I was on my way out of the door anyway but it made me feel angry because I do not go around causing trouble at all.

"My family were livid when I got home and told them."

 

Brenda said it is not the first time she has been approached by the manager in the charity shop.

In the summer, Brenda had purchased a chest of drawers to be delivered to her house at the time, at a cost of £20, and said she was confronted by a manager at the charity shop for "discussing the costs of delivering furniture with other customers."

Brenda added: "She had cornered me in the summer and said I was discussing the costs of delivering furniture with other customers.

"She said I believe you come in here every week causing trouble and it's offending the customers.

"I paid for a chest and I paid for the £20 for it to be delivered with no objection whatsoever."
