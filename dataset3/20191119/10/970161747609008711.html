Line Of Duty casts Trainspotting star Kelly Macdonald as new detective

19 Nov 2019, 10:43

Line Of Duty has confirmed that Kelly Macdonald has been added to the cast just days after Jed Mercurio teased guest arrivals.

The infamous Trainspotting actress, who’s also starred in State Of Play, Boardwalk Empire and more recently The Victim, will play Detective Chief Inspector Joanne Davidson, the senior investigating officer of an unsolved murder.

Joanne’s unconventional conduct raises suspicions at AC-12 and no will no doubt have Vicky McClure, Martin Compston and Adrian Dunbar’s characters trying to uncover whatever it is she’s up to.

‘We’re honoured that Kelly Macdonald will join Martin, Vicky and Adrian in series six of Line of Duty,’ Jed enthused. ‘DCI Joanne Davidson will prove the most enigmatic adversary AC-12 have ever faced.’

CEO and Creative Director of World Productions Simon Heath, added: ‘The Line of Duty team have always been huge fans of her work, so we’re thrilled Kelly has agreed to join us this series to pit her wits against AC-12.’

Meanwhile, Controller of BBC Drama Piers Wenger, raved: ‘We’re delighted that Kelly Macdonald is coming on board for series six. She’s one of the UK’s most versatile actors and we’re excited for viewers to see what Jed’s scripts have in store for her when she joins the Line of Duty team.’

Line Of Duty is available for catch-up on BBC iPlayer.
