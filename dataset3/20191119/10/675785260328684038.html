Man 'shot in the head' before body was found in burnt out car

Police believe the victim was coerced into the car by someone he knew and was then driven off and killed before the hatchback was set alight in Mount Andrew in Dublin

19 Nov 2019, 10:32 by Lorraine King

A murder probe has been launched after a man's body was found in a burnt out car on a housing estate.

The victim was discovered in the passenger seat by emergency services in Mount Andrew Rise in Dublin, on Monday night.

Following an overnight forensic examination, Gardai have announced they are now treating his death as a murder case, Dublin Live reports.

Detectives believe the victim may have been shot in his head after he got into the hatchback car with a person or people he trusted and may have been driven to the scene.

They also suspect he suffered head injuries too.

 

The area around the murder scene is sealed off as Gardai continue to try to identify the dead man and the details of the car.

A Garda spokeswoman said: "Gardai are attending the scene of a fatal incident in the Mount Andrew area, Lucan this evening Monday, 18th November 2019.

"Emergency services attended the scene of a car fire earlier this evening.

"A body was subsequently discovered in the car. The scene has been sealed off for examination. No further information is available at this time."

 

Local councillor Anne-Marie McNally said the community is shocked at the news which comes just months after a man was shot multiple times in nearby Griffeen Glen Park.

The Social Democrat told Dublin Live: "People are shocked and terrified. It's a very quiet estate, and everyone's very shaken.

"It's still early in the investigation so we don't really know or completely understand what's going on.

"Mount Andrew is your typical quiet estate, full of nice families. The area is a popular cut-through route from the bus stop off the N4. A lot of people would go through it.

"There's been some extremely horrible incidents in Lucan lately, and it's come up when speaking to people while canvassing."

Councillor Andrew Moynihan also spoke of his horror describing violent crime as "unacceptable".

He said: "Horrified to hear of a violent shooting in Mount Andrew.

"This estate is home to many young families. Particularly violent crime like this is unacceptable, and we will not tolerate it in our community. Thinking of the victim and all those affected."
