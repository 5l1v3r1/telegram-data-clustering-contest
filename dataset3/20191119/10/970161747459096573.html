Greens launch manifesto with £100,000,000 to fight climate change

19 Nov 2019, 10:58

The Green Party has set out plans to invest a £100 billion a year in tackling climate change.

Its General Election manifesto includes legislation for a ‘Green New Deal’, intended to get Britain to reduce carbon emissions to net zero by 2030.

The party – which is strongly pro-Remain – is also promising a ‘People’s Vote’ bill for a fresh referendum on Britain’s relationship with the EU.

Under its plans, the Greens would raise £91 billion a year for the next decade for capital expenditure on tackling the climate crisis.

A further £9 billion a year in operational spending would be funded through raising taxes, including corporation tax, which would rise to 24%.

The party argues that the borrowing is both justified – given environmental concerns – and a way to kick start economic and social regeneration. It added that public sector investment would act as a catalyst for private sector investment, as private investors seek to share in the financial rewards of a transition to a low carbon economy.

Co-leader Jonathan Bartley explained: ‘We’re proud to put forward a manifesto which puts us on track to remain in the European Union and make the whole country carbon neutral by 2030, while delivering social justice across Britain.

‘It’s the most ambitious Green new deal proposed anywhere in the world. While the other parties are trying to catch up, we’re still racing ahead, reaching new horizons.’

The Green New Deal has been attracting attention in the USA, where it is seen as a potential modern-day version of the famous ‘New Deal’ reforms undertaken by President Franklin D. Roosevelt.

The Greens say a British version ‘will unleash an urgently needed green economic and social revolution’.

The manifesto launch comes amid a shift in polling suggesting climate is seen as increasingly important to the British public, with the ‘school strikes’ and activism by Extinction Rebellion pushing the issue into the spotlight.

In other measures, the party said it would increase NHS funding by at least £6 billion a year, scrap university tuition fees and introduce a universal basic income (UBI) with a ‘phased-in unconditional payment to everyone at a level above their subsistence needs’.

Its UBI scheme would eventually see everyone in the country – regardless of their wealth or employment status – paid a lump sum.

The party would also scrap the first-past-the-post system in elections, replacing it with proportional representation, create a fully elected House of Lords and extend voting rights to 16 and 17 year-olds.

Former leader and Green MP Caroline Lucas tweeted: ‘Our Green New Deal will address both the climate crisis & crisis of social justice.

‘Don’t settle for less’.

The Greens are currently on around 3%, according to the latest polls.
