Munster Issue Injury Update Ahead Of Racing 92 Clash

19 Nov 2019, 11:57 by Sean McMahon

Munster have issued an injury update ahead of their Heineken Champions Cup clash with Racing 92 at Thomond Park on Saturday (KO 5.30 pm).

The southern province enter this game off the back of a bonus-point 32-13 over the Ospreys at the Liberty Stadium.

The Parisian side also secured a bonus-point in their match with Saracens at the Paris La Défense Arena which sets up a mouth-watering encounter with Munster on Saturday.

This weekend should also see the return of Simon Zebo and Donnacha Ryan to the Limerick venue which adds another level of spice to the occasion.

Munster will be going into this game sweating on the fitness of JJ Hanrahan. The Kerry native missed the win over the Ospreys with a hamstring injury he picked the week previously against Ulster.

However, the southern province have confirmed that Hanrahan will be reintegrated into training this week which puts him in contention for Saturday’s evening kick-off.

The same applies to hooker Rhys Marshall who has been struggling with a knee problem.

Players continuing to rehab include Chris Cloete (RTPP), Dave Kilcoyne (calf), Joey Carbery (Ankle) Ciaran Parker (calf) and Darren Sweetnam (hamstring).

Saturday’s match will also be the last game for Alby Mathewson. The southern province confirmed that the Kiwi scrum-half will be departing the southern province at the end of the month.
