Unions slam Tata Steel after ‘shocking’ job cuts announcement

The company said it wanted to come to terms with a ‘severe’ international steel market, with up to 3,000 jobs at risk across its European operations.

19 Nov 2019, 11:56

Unions are seeking urgent assurances about the future of steel plants in the UK after Tata Steel announced plans to axe thousands of jobs.

The company, which employs around 750 staff at Wednesfield Steel Park in Wolverhampton, said it wanted to come to terms with a “severe” international steel market, with up to 3,000 jobs at risk across its European operations.

Tata added that there would be no plant closures, but unions have voiced concern about the impact in the UK.
