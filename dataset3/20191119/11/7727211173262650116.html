Liverpool FC launch new 'Hello Kitty' range through their official online store

The collection is available now

19 Nov 2019, 11:44 by Neil Bennett

Liverpool FC have set up a new global collaboration with Sanrio, the Japanese company behind 'Hello Kitty'.

The new range - available today from LFC's official online store - includes a variety t-shirts, sweatshirts, hoodies, track pants and pjyamas suitable for women, girls and babies.

To launch the new collection, Hello Kitty herself made a special appearance at Liverpool's Anfield store this past Sunday alongside the Mighty Red mascot.

The items depicted below, and more, will be available through both of the club's Liverpool city stores as well as online at store.liverpoolfc.com/lfc-hello-kitty

The Range

T-shirts in the range start from £12 with hoodies priced at £22.

Stationery and other items are also available.

Hello Kitty History

In Japan, Hello Kitty is described as a gijinka - an anthropomorphic 'non-human' with human traits.

Described as a 'symbol of happiness, kindness and friendship', Hello Kitty was first created by Japanese designed Yuko Shimizu in 1974.

The now infamous character first appeared on a vinyl coin purse and within two years made it's first appearance in the United States.

Originally marketed towards a child and preteen audience, Hello Kitty broadened its appeal to cater towards a more mature audience.

By 1999, Hello Kitty appeared on 12,000 different products yearly. Throughout the early noughties and up to 2008, that number increased to over 50,000 different branded products in over 60 countries across the world.

This year, 2019, Hello Kitty is said to be the second highest-grossing franchise of all time behind Pokemon. Throughout it's history, it's sales are said to have totalled somewhere in the region of £75bn.
