Woman dies in M5 crash as police appeal for witnesses

The collision closed the motorway for nine hours

19 Nov 2019, 11:22 by Jamie Hawkins

A woman has died following a crash on the M5.

The incident involved a car and a lorry, with firefighters having to cut the female car driver out of the vehicle after it became wedged underneath the lorry on Saturday morning (November 16).

Police, fire and ambulance crews were called to the southbound carriageway between junctions 22 and 23 following a report of a two-vehicle crash just before 6am.

It caused severe delays to Exeter throughout the day.

The woman - the occupant of the car - was taken to hospital in a critical condition and police have today confirmed she has died.

A spokesperson for Avon and Somerset Police said: "We’re appealing for witnesses after a woman died following a collision on the M5 on Saturday morning (November 16).

"The collision happened at about 6am on the M5 southbound between junctions 22-23.

"A car was in collision with a lorry.

"The driver of the car received serious head injuries and was taken to hospital but sadly died over the weekend.

"We are keen to speak to anyone who was on the M5 on Saturday morning who may have seen the incident or has dash cam footage of the collision or shortly before it happened.

"If you’re able to help, please call 101 and provide the call handler with the reference 5219265362."

All traffic had to be stopped, resulting in hue tailbacks up to six miles long - with some drivers reporting being stranded for up to five hours.

A spokesman for  Devon and Somerset Fire and Rescue  said they were called to the scene at 5.56am following a call from a member of the public.

He said: "Devon and Somerset Fire and Rescue Service received a call from a member of the public reporting a road traffic collision at the above location.

"Two fire appliances from Burnham-on-Sea and a heavy rescue vehicle from Taunton were immediately mobilised by Fire Control.

"On arrival the Incident Commander confirmed that a car had run in to the back of a lorry and become stuck under the rear of the lorry with one female casualty still inside the car.

"Crews quickly got to work to stabilise the vehicle and then set about extricating the casualty from the car using Hydraulic Rescue Equipment.

"The female was taken by ambulance to hospital and the driver of the lorry was treated for shock."
