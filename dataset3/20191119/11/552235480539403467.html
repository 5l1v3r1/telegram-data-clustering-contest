Scottish Labour leader leads push to win back West Dunbartonshire in election

Richard Leonard says he is focused on helping Jean Anne Mitchell defeat the SNP’s Martin Docherty-Hughes.

19 Nov 2019, 11:04 by Fraser Clarke, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/fraser-clarke/

Scottish Labour leader Richard Leonard says the party are determined to win back West Dunbartonshire at next month’s general election.

Speaking as he campaigned in Balloch last week, Leonard exclusively told the Lennox Herald he is focused on helping Jean Anne Mitchell defeat the SNP’s Martin Docherty-Hughes in what historically has been one of his party’s safest constituencies in the country.

He said: “West Dunbartonshire is one of our main target seats. Jean Anne came close to winning last time as she took a huge chunk out of the SNP majority, but we don’t take for granted that it’s just a case of one more heave.

“We will be campaigning flat out to make sure that Jean Anne is the next member of parliament for this seat.

“Locally we are hugely concerned about the continued cuts to the funding which West Dunbartonshire Council gets from the Scottish Government.

“Over time there has been a continued intensity of pressure on the council, forcing them to make some really difficult decisions.

“We want greater investment in public services and communities.”

Jean Anne said: “I’ve spent a lot of time knocking on doors of people who are suffering the most dreadful austerity and the number of people who have come into total crisis because of Universal Credit is disgraceful.

“I really want to focus on helping those people.

“Voting SNP will not get anything other than another Tory government.”

Meanwhile, the Liberal Democrats and Green Party have revealed the identity of their candidates for next month’s vote.

Jenni Lang will stand for the Lib Dems, who finished a distant fourth place in 2017 with just 2.3 percent of the vote, whilst Peter Connolly will become the Greens first ever parliamentary candidate in West Dunbartonshire.

Also standing is independent candidate Andrew Muir whose wife, Claire, stood in the 2015 election – also as an independent – polling just over 500 votes.

The SNP’s Martin Docherty-Hughes, Labour’s Jean Anne Mitchell and the Conservatives’ Alix Mathieson are the other candidates who complete the ballot paper.

The election takes place on December 12 and anyone who wants to vote but is not yet registered has until 5pm on November 26 to ensure they do so www.gov.uk/register-to-vote.
