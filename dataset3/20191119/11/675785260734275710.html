Man Utd eyeing transfer swoop for Reading starlet Danny Loader

Danny Loader has caught the attention of plenty of Premier League clubs at Reading including Man Utd - he had a summer transfer to Wolves fall down

19 Nov 2019, 11:11 by Alex Smith

Manchester United are keeping an eye on Reading starlet Danny Loader.

Hot shot striker Loader has turned heads with his performances for Reading in the Championship since making his debut in the Carabao Cup in 2017.

The 19-year-old has netted once in 29 first team appearances for the Royals having progressed through their Berkshire academy.

United have proved in recent times they aren't afraid to dip into the lower leagues for talent - having picked up Dan James from Swansea this summer.

 

Loader is out of contract at the end of the season, with plenty of Premier League interest in him, along with rumours Juventus are keen.

"There are ongoing discussions between the club, him, his agent and his family," said Reading boss Mark Bowen.

"He is very happy with his football and yes he wants more game time but he is a great professional, has a smile on his face and works hard. That’s all I can ask for.

"He is a confident lad and has got a talent. At times he will be down but he has to be patient and he will get his chance."

 

Loader almost moved to Wolves in the summer but the transfer fell through on deadline day.

“Everyone knows a Premier League club tried to sign me in the transfer window, but it wasn’t to be," Loader said.

"I’m very ambitious, my dream is to play on the biggest stage and you never know when these opportunities will come around again.”

Loader comes with high praise from Red Devils legend Jaap Stam - who handed him his professional debut.

“He’s a very, very promising player, everybody knows that,” said Stam.

“When the opportunity is there he trains with us (first team). You can see he’s a killer around the box and his finishing is very clinical.

“It’s very tempting to take him into the first team but we need to be very careful how he feels.

“We need to take care of these youngsters but if he keeps on playing how he is then he’s going to be very close to the first-team squad and maybe onto the pitch.”
