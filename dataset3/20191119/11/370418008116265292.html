Which nu-metal album is the all-time best? Have your say!

19 Nov 2019, 11:37

If we trace back nu-metal’s genesis to Korn’s debut album, then the subgenre celebrated its 25th anniversary this year. The self-titled effort from the Bakersfield dreadlock merchants laid the foundations for an entire movement within heavy music, mixing heavy bass and hip-hop sensibilities with crunching riffs and uncontrollable angst.

Korn gave way to countless bands, some of which went on to achieve greatness within the world of metal. Slipknot, Linkin Park, Deftones, Limp Bizkit… household names amongst those who like their music heavy, all pushing boundaries of what it meant to be ‘metal’ in the late ’90s/early ’00s.

The scene is much maligned these days, seen as a bit of a dirty mark in the rich history of metal. But it’s hard to ignore the impact and influence some of these albums had. Everyone knows the dance to Rollin’, everyone knows all the words to In The End, everyone was terrified and incensed by the Wait And Bleed video.
