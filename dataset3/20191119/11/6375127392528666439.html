Chinese kidnapper falls

19 Nov 2019, 11:08 by Alfred P. Dalizon

THE Philippine National Police Anti-Kidnapping Group (PNP-AKG) yesterday announced the capture of a female Chinese national wanted for her involvement in the kidnapping-for-ransom of a Chinese-Australian citizen inside the Resorts World Casino last May 27.

PNP-AKG Officer-in-Charge, Colonel Molave H. Dueñas, identified the suspect as Zhenzhen Cheng, a 28-year-old resident of no. 437-D Tower Shell Residence located at EDSA Road, SM Mall of Asia in Pasay City.

The official said that the suspect was arrested by a team of AKG Luzon Field Unit operatives led by Major Erving Chan near the City of Dreams in  Parañaque City last Saturday on the strength of a warrant of arrest for kidnapping-for-ransom issued by Judge Restituto Mangalindan Jr. of the Pasay City Regional Trial Court Branch 114.

No bail was recommended for the temporary liberty of the Chinese woman and her co-accused.

The PNP-AKG said that their investigation showed that the suspects seized  Jianting Chen, a Chinese-Australian citizen inside the Resorts World Casino last May 27.

The kidnappers demanded 90,000 RMB from the family of the victim in exchange for his safe release. After the victim’s wife wired the money to a bank account given by the suspects, the victim was released.

The arrested suspect is now being held at the PNP-AKG lock-up facility in Camp Crame pending the issuance of a commitment order for her by the Pasay court.
