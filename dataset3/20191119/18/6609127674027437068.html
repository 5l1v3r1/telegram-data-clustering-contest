BE SAFE

University of Glasgow issue warning to students after woman, 20, raped and three ‘sex attacks’ in city’s west end

19 Nov 2019, 18:24 by Katy Pagan

GLASGOW students have been warned to stay safe after a woman was raped and separately three others were allegedly sexually assaulted in the city's west end.

University of Glasgow chiefs issued an "important security message" tonight after the spate of alleged attacks.

On October 17 two women were allegedly sexually assaulted - one in Lawrence Street, Partick, and the other in Napier Halls Street, North Woodside.

Another woman was allegedly sexually assaulted on October 24 in Cranworth Street, Hillhead.

Cops have arrested and charged a man, 34,  in connection with the three alleged attacks.

In a separate incident, in the early hours of October 31, a 20-year-old woman was raped in Sutherland Lane, near the university's library.
