The players who could save Bristol City millions in the January transfer window

The Robins have recall options for the start of 2020

19 Nov 2019, 18:43 by James Piercy

With Joe Morrell starring for Wales and Liam Walsh transforming into "Scouse Messi", it's emphasised the level of talent Bristol City possess beyond the confines of the first-team squad Lee Johnson has opted to work with this season.

Midfielders Morrell and Walsh are two of 12 players out on loan across the Football League and Scotland, with the Robins ensuring plenty of their players are earning consistent minutes outside the limitations of what Johnson can offer them.

With season-long loans, City have the provision to recall any players they feel could be useful in the second-half of the season.

The club are increasingly focusing on the January transfer window but incomings could mean more than just new signings, with assistant head coach Dean Holden admitting the prospect of bringing one or two back into the fold is a possibility.

"I'm not sure about the contractual obligations of the loans but it's something we're always looking at, with January fast approaching," Holden said.

"All those conversations are going on in terms of players in and out of the club and loans in and out of the club; those two (Morrell and Walsh) will come into the reckoning, two lads who are doing fantastic.
