Marcus Rashford excited by England's attacking trio

19 Nov 2019, 18:53 by Shambhu Ajith

Marcus Rashford, Harry Kane and Raheem Sterling are in the form of their lives for England at the moment, and the Manchester United star is excited by the partnership they have struck up.

Rashford was talking to Sky Sports when he heaped praise on Kane, Sterling and himself, stating:

"I think it is obviously a good combination but we have played games without that particular front three and it is frightening what we can do as a group of attackers.”

Aside from Rashford, Kane and Sterling, the England squad has plenty of quality attackers, with the likes of Jadon Sancho, Callum Hudson-Odoi and Tammy Abraham ready to step in when required.

Rashford went on to state that England will have to take advantage of the depth they have in the squad.

“You have to use it to your advantage and maybe it isn't working in one game for that three and someone has to come in and make the difference - we have to use that to our advantage, 100 per cent!”

England managed to score 37 goals in 8 EURO 2020 qualifying games while conceding just 3. They scored 7 against Montenegro last week at Wembley in what was the country's 1000th international game.

In their last 3 matches, against Bulgaria, Montenegro and Kosovo, England scored 17 goals without reply.
