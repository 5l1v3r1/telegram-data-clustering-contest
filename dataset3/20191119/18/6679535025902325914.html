H1N1 cases on a decline, says BBMP

19 Nov 2019, 18:50 by Staff Reporter

The number of H1N1 cases reported in the city has dropped this year when compared to the previous two years. According to the Public Health Information and Epidemiological Cell (PHIEC) of the BBMP, there have have been 1,144 cases of H1N1 reported so far in the city from January to November 16.

BBMP Commissioner B.H. Anil Kumar on Tuesday tweeted that 1,773 H1N1 cases were reported in 2018 and 1,356 in 2017.

B.K. Vijayendra, Chief Health Officer, Public Health, BBMP, said in October 2018, there were 814 cases of H1N1 reported and this year in October there were 19. In November last year, there were 585 cases and till November 16 this year, only 16 cases have been reported. “As per the current trend, the number of H1N1 cases this year has been low. We have taken various precautionary measures as well,” he said.

Mr. Viyayendra said people need to take precautions and maintain hand hygiene to keep the infection away. “H1N1 is contagious and hence people should follow basic hygiene measures, drink plenty of water and have nutritious food,” he said.
