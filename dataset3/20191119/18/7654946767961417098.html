Man bites worker during attempted U-Haul robbery: cops

19 Nov 2019, 18:49 by Daniel Cassady and Natalie Musumeci

A man tried to steal two sets of truck keys at a Brooklyn U-Haul facility – and then bit an employee who confronted him, police said.

The thief made his way into the U-Haul rental outpost on Washington Avenue in Prospect Heights last Thursday at around 8:30 a.m., cops said.

A 51-year-old employee confronted the intruder, and during the struggle, the suspect chomped down on a finger on the worker’s left hand,  according to police. The suspect then fled without taking the keys, cops added.

The victim was not seriously injured and refused medical attention at the scene.

The NYPD released photos and surveillance footage of the suspect in the hopes that the public may be able to help authorities track him down.
