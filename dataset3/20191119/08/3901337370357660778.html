Ex-Liverpool Star Slams Klopp

19 Nov 2019, 08:29 by Kurt Buckerfield

Despite a century of appearances under Jurgen Klopp at Liverpool, one of the German's former players has seemingly taken a swipe at the manager four months after his exit from the Reds.

Belgium goalkeeper Simon Mignolet spent six years at Anfield after his arrival from Sunderland in 2013.

The 31-year-old made 204 appearances for the club, 100 of which came under Klopp, but he appears to have a bitter taste in his mouth regarding his last season in England.

Mignolet played just twice for the Reds in the 2018/19 campaign following the big-money signing of Brazil goalkeeper Alisson, and the Belgian seemingly feels that Klopp was never going to give him a fair chance to compete with the former Roma shot-stopper for the No. 1 spot. Â 

"If I could really fight for my place, I might have stayed," he told Belgian newspaper Het Belang van Limburg.

"But if I had ended up in goal if Alisson picked up injury, I would be allowed to push 10 shots out of the box.

"Then Alisson could immediately claim his place again. And even if he pushed the ball into his own goal, he would still retain (his place in the starting XI).

"What sense did it make for me?"

The Belgian also explained why he does not regret leaving Liverpool.

"No, because I knew that the opportunity existed," Mignolet conitnued.

"I haven't thought once: 'S**t, now Liverpool is in the Premier League race and I'm gone.'

"Of course, I could have stayed with Liverpool to earn my money, challenge for the league title and the Champions League.

"However, as long as you are not on the starting XI yourself, a price feels different. So I would rather play as a starter at Club Brugge than grabbing the English title as a substitute at Liverpool."
