Trauma surgeon offers advice for deer hunters

19 Nov 2019, 08:13 by Riley Fannon

Kirksville, Mo. — November 16 was the start of Missouri’s firearms deer season and one health care provider wants you to stay safe this season.

Speaking with Dr. Jacob Quick, trauma surgeon for MU Health Care, he’s seen an average of 30 major hunting accidents happen every year.

But some key precautions can limit a hunter’s risk of injury.

Quick is a hunter and says gunshot injuries are rare. We wanted to know what the injuries he sees the most.

“We see everything from orthopedic injuries like broken legs and arms to intra-abdominal injuries – injuries to the bowel, liver, spleen, kidneys, those kinds of things - to chest injuries. You name it, it can happen," said Quick.

Most of the injuries occur after a hunter falls from a tree stand and according to Quick, 30% of all hunters will fall and suffer a serious injury at some point.

That can be easily avoided, as 80% of those falls come from hunters who don’t wear a harness.

Other than wearing a full body harness, Quick says hunters should keep 3 limbs in contact with the ladder at all times, make sure someone knows your location and always carry your cellphone.
