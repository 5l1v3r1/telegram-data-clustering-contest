Jada Pinkett Smith tears up as she recalls how Tupac felt ‘threatened’

19 Nov 2019, 08:29

Jada Pinkett Smith was overwhelmed with emotion while recalling how her close friend, Tupac Shakur, felt ‘threatened’ by the thought of their friendship breaking down.

It’s no secret that the Girls Trip actress had a very close friendship with Tupac, who died in September 1996 at the age of 25 following a drive-by shooting.

They were the best of friends while studying at Baltimore School of the Arts before finding fame separately.

According to Jada, the California Love rapper feared they would drift apart as he found ‘stability’ in their friendship.

Jada, 47, tearfully opened up about Tupac in the episode of Red Table Talk featuring Whitney Houston’s lover Robyn Crawford.

‘I understood because of the complex relationship I’ve had with ‘Pac and those moments of “Who’s that?” Knowing damn well there ain’t nothing like that between us.

‘Him feeling like, “You’re the only stability I got, I can’t afford for you to put that attention elsewhere”,’ Jada explained.

‘For him, it was, we were an anchor for each other. Anytime he felt like that anchor was threatened, oh my God.’
