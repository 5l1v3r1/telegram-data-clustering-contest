FIGHT FOR LIFE

Beast who launched vicious machete attack on girl, 13, holding baby sliced so deep her lung was cut open

19 Nov 2019, 08:42 by Jason Johnson

THE beast who launched a vicious machete attack on a teenage girl holding a baby sliced so deep that her lung was cut open.

Geraldine Maughan, 13, was struck on the legs and back when three men burst into her family home in Lisnaskea, Co Fermanagh on Saturday night.

She threw herself on top of her 11-month-old nephew as a “laughing” middle aged man belted her with the blade. Another man, believed to be one of his sons, hacked at her legs with the machete while recording the obscene attack on a mobile phone.

Slightly-built Geraldine, who had been cuddling godson John Joyce, is being treated for a range of wounds, including the deep, six inch gash.

Her sister Pauline, 20, the child’s mother, told how air was leaking from her back as she tried to breathe after the savage assault.

She said: “Her lung collapsed right away. The way she has been cut means she can’t walk now and has to learn to walk again.

“She’ll be in hospital for weeks. Her back won’t be the same for about 12 months.

“She’s very frightened from what happened. She was just sitting there and they just walked in and two of them started hitting at her.

“It’s disgraceful, grown men doing that to a girl and baby.”

Police are treating the bloody assault as attempted murder.

Lizzy Joyce, the boy’s grandmother, appealed for “no retaliation”, saying she was looking forward to “smiling when they go to jail”.

She said her sons had left the house in Trasna Way just minutes before the machete mob arrived at around 9.15pm.

The 41-year-old was also wounded in the vicious onslaught, but managed to strike one attacker in the face with a picture frame.

She said: “I think they waited and saw my lads going out of the estate and then they came.

“They thought my husband would be here and came for him but he wasn’t here either.”

The mum-of-six, who has four sons, went on: “They came through the door and two of them were attacking at a 13-year-old girl, my daughter-in-law’s sister.

“All of them had the machetes. I’ll never forget because that man, a grown man, was laughing as he hit that girl.

"One of them came at me and I stood up because I knew they would be slicing me up.

“I got up to stop him getting any proper hits at me. I started fighting back because the instinct for survival got in my head.

"When I started fighting back I grabbed for a picture frame and hit him hard in the face.”

She added: “He pulled his mask up then, a balaclava, and I could see who he was. He was backing away to the door.

“The other two had black hats but I could see their faces.”

She said the men “ran out” to where they had left their blue car at the top of a hill.

Lizzy gave chase but the men, who had taped over their car’s registration number, sped off.

She said of Geraldine: “She’s a legend, she’s a hero, a fighter.

“When I came back to the house I grabbed the little fella first and checked him over and he was fine.

“Then I checked Geraldine and she was destroyed. You could see the blood spitting out of the back of the girl where her lung was split.

“I got on the phone to the boys and said ‘we’re cut up’ and called an ambulance.”

Her husband Gramsey Joyce, a bare knuckle boxer with a fearsome reputation in the Travelling community, was a distance away in the estate — but told how he “heard the screams.”

Mr Joyce, who is recovering from a cancer operation, ran to the house.

He said: “They were already gone when I got here. I came in and I was just thinking it was the smell of death.

“It made me think of a car crash we were in and there had been blood and it was like that, blood everywhere and I could smell death.

“But thankfully no one was killed.”

He said he believed that the attackers “came to kill me”.

Lizzy said: “The PSNI are working with the Gardai and they have promised me faithfully these men will be caught.

“They would be better off to just hand themselves in instead of hiding because it’s only going to be a matter of weeks before the police come and get them.”

She added: “We want no retaliation. We want justice, the best retaliation will be in the court when I’m smiling as they go to jail.

“And I have my boys told that. That will be the best retaliation.”

She said that brave Geraldine is “only a little skinny one, about seven stone weight six, six”, and continued: “If men like that do that to her, they’d do that to anyone.

“I’m not bothered about the wounds I have but that’s a girl in the hospital who is sitting up and trying to sleep because they can’t even lie her down on her back.

"It’ll be a long recovery for a little girl.”

It’s understood that the men suspected of involvement in the attack have told pals they have an alibi from the night.

Meanwhile, police are in possession of the video recording made by the attackers as they made their way to the house and began their brutal assault.

Det Sgt Keith Monaghan said the incident was a terrifying ordeal for those involved.

He said: “We are determined to find the men responsible.”

He added detectives were investigating several lines of inquiry.
