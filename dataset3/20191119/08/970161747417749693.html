Coldplay celebrating new album with Natural History Museum gig

19 Nov 2019, 08:21

Coldplay are celebrating the release of their new album, Everyday Life, by performing at London’s Natural History Museum next week.

And fans are absolutely beside themselves at the news.

The Fix You singers will be blessing us with their eighth album on 22 November, before whacking out some of their biggest hits at the prestigious museum.

Not only that, but the proceeds will be going towards environmental charity Client Earth.

Your favourites could never.

The band announced the gig on social media, telling fans: ‘Coldplay, Live at the Natural History Museum, London 25 November, 2019.

‘Exclusive ticket access to UK fans who pre-order (or have already pre-ordered) Everyday Life from the Official Coldplay Store, before Friday, 22 November.

‘All proceeds from the Natural History Museum show on 25 November will go towards the work of @ClientEarth, an environmental charity which uses the power of the law to protect the planet and its people.’

Right on cue, fans went into total meltdown at the news, with one replying: ‘So exciting, thank you for this. Just got to try and get a ticket now.’

‘This makes so much sense! Art playing in a museum,’ another commented.

While one screamed: ‘OMG YES THANK YOU I’VE BEEN WAITING FOR THIS FOR YEARS.’

And many, many more begged the band to perform in their home towns.

The news comes after Chris Martin broke all our hearts when he confirmed that Coldplay would not be playing at Glastonbury 2020 – and we are utterly devastated.

The 42-year-old frontman appeared on Australian radio breakfast show Nova 96.6 when he told listeners: ‘We won’t be playing at Glastonbury 50 – although, I’ll probably be there because I love going.

‘It’s like our home…but we won’t be playing.’

So if you were lucky enough to snag tickets, you still might be able to see the band – just not on stage.

While Coldplay won’t be performing next June, Glastonbury-goers will be watching Paul McCartney headline the festival at Worthy Farm.

Emily Eavis shared the news on Twitter, after months of speculation, telling fans the Beatles legend will be performing on the Saturday night.

‘IT’S REAL!! So excited.. Having Paul McCartney coming back to headline the Pyramid next year is an absolute dream come true,’ she wrote on Instagram
