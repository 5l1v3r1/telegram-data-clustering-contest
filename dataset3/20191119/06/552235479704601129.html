Graeme Murty's Rangers manager stint broke him but he's back to his best with Colts - Gavin Berry

Gavin reckons he looked a bit out of his depth when in caretaker charge but his work with the reserves and youths shouldn't go unnoticed.

19 Nov 2019, 06:00 by Gavin Berry, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/gavin-berry/

Graeme Murty joked he had to wipe the custard from his face after Daniel Candeias snubbed him during a goal celebration.

The Portuguese winger netted the winner against Hearts at Ibrox in April 2018.

Murty said Candeias “ran straight past me” as he made a beeline for sub Andy Halliday who had suffered Hampden humiliation the previous week when he was hooked before half-time in a 4-0 Scottish Cup semi-final loss to Celtic.

That game was the last time Murty was in the Ibrox dugout before his Rangers Colts side’s 2-0 win over Wrexham in the Challenge Cup on Saturday.

It was an eventul 19-month period with the Candeias snub nothing compared to the humiliation he suffered the following week after a 5-0 mauling at the hands of Celtic at Parkhead.

That proved to be his last match in caretaker charge of Rangers with Jimmy Nicholl stepping up for the final three games before Steven Gerrard’s arrival.

It was a tough learning curve for the former Scotland full-back who answered the club’s call after Pedro Caixinha’s sacking – the second time he’d stepped up after an interim spell following Mark Warburton’s axing.

That led to chairman Dave King calling Murty his “man of the year” at the club’s agm.

Lee Wallace and Kenny Miller might beg to differ after their alleged bust-up with Murty in the aftermath of that Scottish Cup semi-final loss. It remains a mystery what took place inside the dressing room that day with conflicting rumours of just how heated it got.

Ironically, Murty’s victorious return could see him on a collision course with Miller after the veteran striker scored for Partick Thistle in their 4-1 Challenge Cup win at Stenhousemuir to secure a semi-final spot.

But Murty’s success in guiding his young Light Blues into the semis is just a reminder of his own career rehabilitation since that traumatic end to his time in the hot-seat.

Many believed a clean break from the club would have been the easiest solution – it couldn’t have been easy walking the corridors of the Rangers Training Centre when Wallace and Miller were still around – for all.

But to his credit the 45-year-old returned to the Academy where he was first appointed three years ago after forging an impressive reputation with Norwich’s youths.

Under Academy chief Craig Mulholland and alongside Brian Gilmour, Peter Lovenkrands, David McCallum and Kevin Thomson he is helping to create a sense of excite-ment for the future – the job he was brought in to do in the first place.
