Teenager's family speak of 'heart-breaking' time in court as he is cleared of one punch incident

Dylan Marriott says he was acting in self defence

19 Nov 2019, 06:00 by Rod Malcolm

A teenager's family spoke with joy as he was cleared of GBH when facing a man who threatened to stab him.

Mum Danielle Marriott saw 19-year-old Dylan walk free from court and told his barrister: "You have given me my boy back."

Relatives and friends spent a week in Nottingham Crown Court listening to evidence and legal debate about the clash in Long Row on February 23.

Mr Marriott told how he went up to help a man, who was clearly drunk. He was faced with threats and abusive remarks.

As the man leaned back as if to strike, Mr Marriott punched him once and saw him fall to the ground. The man suffered head injuries.

Outside court, Mr Marriott said: "It was self-defence. He went down.

"I only went up to him because I was trying to help," said Mr Marriott, of Vernon Park Drive, Basford.

Mother-of four Mrs Marriott, 38, said: "We have been here every day and it has been heart-breaking.

"The other guy had a knife. Police found it on him later.

"Since this happened, it has been a very emotional time, a rollercoaster. I have never been in this position before and it has been terrible.

"It was a one punch incident, that's what people say."

Mr Marriott denied causing grievous bodily harm and was defended by barrister Sarah Phelan.

He was cleared by a jury which began its deliberations on Friday.

On Monday it delivered a "not guilty" verdict on a charge of maliciously inflicting grievous bodily harm.

The injured man was put into an induced coma at the Queen's Medical Centre. He has now been released.

Mr Marriott's 22-year-old sister Amy said: "He's the glue that holds us together. He's a lovely boy. He would do anything for anybody."

Mrs Marriott first heard of the incident when her son was working in Lincolnshire the next day. He discovered that police wanted to interview him.

He returned immediately, spoke to police officers and handed over the clothing he was wearing that night to help their enquiries.

Mrs Marriott went on: "Dylan rang to say 'police are looking for me.' He said he wanted to help them.

"They didn't even need to cuff him in custody.

"But it has been so hard on us. It is out of your control, thinking of your child being punished by someone else."

After the jury returned its verdict, Recorder Jennifer Jones thanked them for their work.

She then told Mr Marriott: "You are free to leave the dock and free to leave court thereafter."
