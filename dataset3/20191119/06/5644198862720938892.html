Louisiana government computers hit by ransomware - sources

19 Nov 2019, 06:35

REUTERS: Louisiana state government computers were hit by ransomware, two people familiar with the matter said on Monday, as results from the closely-fought election for governor in the southern state are still awaiting certification.

The extent of the outbreak is not yet clear, although Louisiana Secretary of State spokesman Tyler Brey said the tallying of Saturday's vote, which happens on a different system, was not affected.

The Secretary of State's website was briefly down earlier on Monday, "as were all state websites that ended in LA.gov," Brey said.

The vote, which saw Louisiana Governor John Bel Edwards win re-election, drew national attention following U.S. President Donald Trump's well-publicized endorsement of the Republican challenger, Eddie Rispone.

Louisiana's candidates have until next week to contest the results and ask for a recount.

Ransomware works by scrambling data held on vulnerable computers and demanding a payment to unlock it.

A person familiar with the matter identified the particular breed of ransomware that struck Louisiana as Ryuk, a variant which cybersecurity firm CrowdStrike said has earned its operators US$3.7 million since its appearance last year. Experts believe that Ryuk is used by financially motivated criminals rather than foreign governments.

Messages seeking comment from Louisiana's Division of Administration and the Louisiana branches of the Democratic and Republican parties were not immediately returned.

(Reporting by Raphael Satter and Christopher Bing; Editing by Bill Berkrot)
