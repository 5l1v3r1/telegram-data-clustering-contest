19 Nov 2019, 16:56 by Katie Russell

It’s the Godfather of Black Friday. Amazon, the online retailing giant that brought the November sales to the UK, consistently has some of the best deals on electricals, beauty and Amazon-branded products.

This year, the company will be holding their official Black Friday sales from November 22 — the week before the official Black Friday sales date, on November 29. In a statement, the online retailer claimed it would be their "biggest ever Black Friday sale" and that shoppers will be able to save "tens of millions on thousands of new deals every day".

Yesterday, Amazon has launched an early round of sales called the Black Friday Countdown. With electric toothbrushes, phones and vacuum cleaners on offer, there is something for everyone. These deals have been split into Deals of the Day (available for 24 hours), Best Deals (promotions that can run for up to two weeks) and Lightning Deals (flash sales that usually last for around six hours.) Yet there are also a number of other sales floating outside these categories — including impressive deals on Amazon products, such as the Echo Dot and the Echo Plus.

While there are more details of these Black Friday Countdown deals below, we have also analysed trends from last year’s sales to predict some of the best Amazon offers for the main event: Black Friday 2019.

So, what can we expect the best deals to be in the sales? We anticipate that, as in previous years, Amazon will primarily champion deals on their own branded products (Echo, Kindle, Fire TV stick, etc). However, they usually offer great discounts on home tech, laptops, phones and beauty products - so we have included our predictions for these categories, too.

How to shop on Amazon this Black Friday

To prepare you for the upcoming havoc of Black Friday, we have collated some tips on how to check in and check out of Amazon, with your basket of deals, as quickly as possible.

  • Create a membership. Be sure to register as a new customer so you can check out your items as quickly as possible when the deals start. You could even save your card details to really speed up the process.
  • Sign up for Amazon Prime. Amazon often incentivise their customers by giving Prime members exclusive deals and early access to thousands of ‘lightning deals’ (the ones that usually sell out the fastest) 30 minutes before the rest of the internet.
  • Get there early. Amazon have been known to launch their Black Friday deals as much as two weeks in advance. Many last-generation tech products pop up during this time, whereas newer ones usually emerge nearer to Cyber Weekend (this year, November 30). 

Keep track of the Telegraph’slive coverage of Black Friday - so you can find the best deals as soon as they’re on offer. 

Check out the ‘Upcoming’ page. When the 2019 offers are released, Amazon’s official Black Friday micro-site should have an ‘Upcoming’ page filled with future offers and the exact time of their launch. If you clikc the ‘Watch this deal’ button on anything listed, the Amazon app will send you a notification for each item’s deal launch.

Pre-fill your basket. Some savvy shoppers swear by pre-filling their baskets with rumoured products so they can check them out as soon as the deals go live. 

Shop around. While Amazon does usually have great deals, it doesn’t hurt to browse the Shopping tab on Google for a better deal. 

Check Flubit for a better deal. If you've found a product you like on Amazon, and you have a spare couple of hours, enter the URL into the Flubit generator - it will tell you if there's a better deal elsewhere on the internet. This can take up to three hours and Flubit, which receives a commission on each deal it facilitates, does sometimes admit that Amazon has the best price on an item.
