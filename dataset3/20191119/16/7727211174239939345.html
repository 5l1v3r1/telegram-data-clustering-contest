What time is the ITV General Election debate and where can I stream it?

Everything you need to know for the crunch leaders' debate

19 Nov 2019, 16:45 by Liam Thorp

The General Election is just a few weeks away and the campaigns are well underway.

Tonight the first live debate of the election campaign will take place - with ITV hosting Prime Minister Boris Johnson and Labour leader Jeremy Corbyn in a face to face stand-off.

Hosted by Julie Etchingham at ITV's studios in Media City, Salford - it will be the first time since 2015 that both the Labour and Conservative party leaders have taken part in a TV debate.

It is the first TV debate of the campaign - with further events set to be broadcast on Sky and the BBC in the coming weeks.

What channel is it on? Can I stream it?

The hour-long showdown will be broadcast on ITV1 from 8pm, with a live stream available on the  ITV Hub  for UK-based viewers.

Both leaders will stand behind podiums to deliver a minute-long statement before the debate, followed by a closing statement of 45 seconds.

The Labour leader has chosen to speak first, after Labour and the Conservatives drew lots to see who would open the debate.

What time does it start?

The ITV leaders debate will kick off at 8pm and will last for one hour until 9pm.

The event will take place at the MediaCityUK studios in Salford, in front of an audience of about 200 people.

The ECHO will be bringing you all the drama, action and reaction from inside the Salford studios where our political editor Liam Thorp will be watching the debate unfold live.
