Remembrance service for Devon woman found dead in caves

Peta Harper, 58, was last seen in her red Mini car at nearby Hope Cove

19 Nov 2019, 16:15 by Colleen Smith

A short service of remembrance will be held this week for Peta Harper, 58, whose body was found in caves at Hope Cove.

It will be held at St Andrews Church in Aveton Gifford. A major search and rescue operation was called off last Tuesday two days after the last sighting of her in a car park nearby.

Kingsbridge Police said: "There will be a short service at St Andrews Church, Aveton Gifford on Thursday at 12.30pm for all those who would like to pay remembrance to Peta Harper."

Peta, from Aveton Gifford, was reported missing reported missing from the Hope Cove/ Bolt Tail area on Monday (November 11).

Devon and Cornwall Police confirmed that a body was found in a system of caves at Hope Cove. Although formal identification has not yet taken place, her family have been informed.

Kingsbridge Police released the following statement on their Facebook page: "Today, searches have been carried out around a system of caves within the cliffs at Hope Cove.

"It is with great sadness to report that the body of a female was located within the cave structure.

"Formal identification has not yet taken place but the family of Peta Harper, who was reported as missing from Aveton Gifford on Monday have been informed.

"A file will now be prepared for HM Coroner.

"Our sincere condolences to the friends and family of Peta."

The last known movements of a 58-year-old were released by emergency services as concerns grew for her safety.

A major air and sea search for Peta Harper was called off on Tuesday afternoon.

Police revealed this week that the Kingsbridge woman was last seen in the car park at Hope Cove on Sunday at around 6.30pm. They believe she parked her car and then slept in the red Mini overnight.

She was officially reported missing from the Hope Cove/ Bolt Tail area at around 1.20pm on Monday 11 November.

A police spokesperson said at the time: “The last confirmed sighting of Peta was at around 6.30pm on Sunday 10 November. We believe she parked her red Mini in the car park at Hope Cove and slept there overnight.

“I’d appeal for anyone who saw a red Mini in the car park after 6.30pm on the 10th and anyone who saw Peta in the area, to contact police.”
