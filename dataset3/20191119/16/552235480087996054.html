Got the bottle to help build Perth panto stage set?

Perth Theatre on the hunt for plastic bottles

19 Nov 2019, 16:21 by Melanie Bonn, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/melanie-bonn/

Perth Theatre’s box office is expecting to soon be awash with plastic bottles if the Fair City public takes up a request for drinks containers to get a second life as part of the Perth panto.

The theatre is on the lookout for used plastic bottles, asking for them to be handed in to be recycled as part of the set for the festive adventure, ‘Sinbad’.

The panto team needs rinsed-out, clear, blue or green 2lt size plastic bottles and smaller.

They will play a starring role in the panto which opens on November 29.

“We wanted to highlight the importance of recycling, reusing and repurposing,” explained Barrie Hunter, who not only plays Dame Jackie Alltrades, but has written the script and is directing Sinbad.

“We have done this in elements of the set and costumes.

“And I’ve most definitely done so in the script - I’ve been recycling the same ‘trumpet tree’ joke for the last eight years!”

Barrie’s Dame Jackie Alltrades is joined by daughter Sinbad, son Finn Lad and young nature-lover Greta on a journey to save the Tayside beavers, with all the usual high spirits in this year’s family panto.

Designer Becky Minto said: “We have re-purposed and recycled from last year’s panto where we could.

“Our wardrobe department have been having a great time, digging out some costumes from pantos past, and working their magic on them, giving them a Sinbad-themed look and feel.”

Sinbad is in Perth Theatre from Friday, November 29 until Saturday, January 4.

Tickets can be bought from the box office on 01738 621031 or www.horsecross.co.uk
