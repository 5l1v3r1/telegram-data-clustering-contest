How getting a tattoo saved Swadlincote mum whose life was wrecked by tragedy

'I dread to think where I would be now if I'd not got that tattoo that day'

19 Nov 2019, 16:23 by Rhea Turner

A Swadlincote mum who fell to pieces after a "whirlwind" year has told how deciding to get a tattoo turned her life around.

Simply Needlecraft boss Ami James was a successful businesswoman when her life started to fall apart.

The 38-year-old, who has bipolar disorder, was left reeling when she broke up with her husband and her stepdad died from cancer.

Ami, said: "I was diagnosed with bipolar disorder 10 years ago. It doesn’t really affect me with mood swings like it does others but it gives me really bad anxiety.

"I have always been stable but a while ago I started to go downhill.

"I separated from my husband, had to move house and downsize and my stepdad passed away from cancer.

"It was a whirlwind year and my mind just ran away. It seemed like everything was going wrong, it was one thing after another."

Ami says her circumstances destroyed her confidence and she was full of self-doubt.

She said: "I lost motivation and all confidence in my business and things got really bad.

"I just had to carry on because I have a son (Ben, 15) and I have no choice. You get up out of bed in the morning and do it all again but it was a struggle.

"I went from being someone who was quite ambitious to someone who started questioning everything.

"I was wondering whether I was good enough.

"It felt like things were stacking up on top of me and they couldn’t get any worse.

"I was becoming someone who was the polar opposite to what I am really like."

A major turning point came when Ami decided to get a tattoo, courtesy of Swadlincote artist Kevin Paul, in November last year.

Kevin has worked with major stars included Rihanna and Ed Sheeran and agreed to tattoo a key on Ami's foot to represent "opening new doors".

She said: "My life was changing, so I went for a new tattoo on a whim.

"I first met Kevin a couple of years ago when he did a tattoo for me.

"I spent five hours sitting in the chair. We got talking and he really helped me.

"He has had anxiety in the past so he knew where I was coming from and he just told me to look at what I have and what I have achieved.

"He taught me that things are much simpler than they seem. It was a breath of fresh air.

"Kevin made a big difference, because instead of looking at the bad things, he just focused on what I did have and he got straight to the point.

"Family and friends tried to make me feel better, but the pity from them just made me feel worse.

"I know they meant well, but I felt like I was worrying them which only made me feel worse."

Swadlincote Haberdashery Simply Needlecraft had to close last year as a result of Ami's problems.

"But she is now back to her old self and will reopen it in January.

She said: "I had a complete change of mind and since getting my tattoo and things are on the up.

"I have moved house and I am setting my business up in January.

"I have got my confidence back and I am starting to feel more like myself again.

"I am also teaching sewing around the country which is my passion.

"I dread to think where I would be now if I'd not got that tattoo that day. Kevin helped change my life."
