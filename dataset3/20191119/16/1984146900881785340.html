Newcastle City Council reveals changes to what rubbish should go in recycling bins

Yoghurt pots can now be recycled by Newcastle City Council following these changes to what waste and rubbish can be put in what bin

19 Nov 2019, 16:51 by Simon Duke

It's something of a grey area when it comes to putting the rubbish out and Newcastle City Council has revealed some changes as to what rubbish goes into what bin right in time for Christmas, when we're all sure to have plenty of extra things to dispose of.

According to the council website , all residents of Newcastle have a responsibility to ensure that all of their rubbish and waste is disposed of properly. Under the Household Waste Duty of Care (section 34(2A) of the Environmental Protection Act 1990), residents are required to take reasonable measures to ensure that waste produced on their property is only passed on to an authorised person.

And changes have recently been made as to what can be recycled, so here is a full break down of what waste should go in what colour bin...

Green bin: household waste

Your green wheelie bin is for household and food waste, and waste that cannot be recycled. Please bag all waste that goes in this bin in either black bags , carrier bags or bin liner bags.

DO NOT place the following items in your green bin:

Rubble and bricks

Vehicle parts

Items which may make the bin too heavy

Commercial or trade waste

Liquids

Small electrical items - please take these to your nearest Household Waste Recycling Centre

Carpets or furniture

Blue bin: recycling

Use the main part of your blue bin to recycle the following items, placed inside loose without bags:

Washed and dried margarine tubs, yoghurt pots, meat trays or fruit punnets (no black plastic)

Empty aerosol cans

Cartons and cardboard egg boxes (flattened)

Plastic bottles (flattened, with tops replaced)

Cardboard (flattened)

Newspaper, magazines and paper (no plastic coated paper, no shredded paper or bound files)

White and brown envelopes - it doesn't matter if they have windows or not

Telephone directories and Yellow Pages

Food and drink cans (rinsed and squashed)

Foil and foil trays (rinsed)

Biscuit tins and plastic chocolate tubs

Use the black caddy inside your blue bin to recycle:

Rinsed glass bottles and jars (  not pyrex glass, drinking glasses, broken window panes, mirrors)

DO NOT not place the following items in your blue bin: 

Compostable films, plastics or items like compostable cutlery and plates

Small electrical items -  old or broken things like toasters, kettles or irons, please take these to your nearest Household Waste Recycling Centre.

Brown bin: garden waste

To help you keep a tidy garden your brown bin garden waste collection will accept all your garden trimmings, cuttings, prunings, leaves and weeds.

Please place this waste loosely in your bin, not in plastic bags or sacks.

The following vegetation SHOULD NOT be placed in your bin:

Branches, logs, or roots more than 10cm (4 inches) in diameter

Japanese Knotweed

DO NOT put the following items in your brown bin:

Turf

Stones

Rubble

Plant pots

Dog and cat faeces

Straw and hay bedding used for pets

Food waste or refuse

Compostable films, plastics or items like cutlery and plates*

Anything in black bags or plastic bags.
