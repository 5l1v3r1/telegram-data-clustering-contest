Samsung unveils Galaxy Note10+ Star Wars edition

19 Nov 2019, 16:19 by Rohit KVN

Samsung, earlier in the year, unveiled Galaxy S10+ Tokyo Olympic limited model, to mark the 2020 edition of the foremost global sporting event. Now, the company is bringing a similar, but colorful phone next month.

Samsung in collaboration with Hollywood movie production firm Lucasfilm has announced the Galaxy Note10+ Star Wars Special Edition to commemorate 'Star Wars: The Rise of Skywalker' motion picture, which is slated to hit theaters worldwide on December 20.

The limited-edition Galaxy Note10+ flaunts raven-hued device with crimson accents on camera module, power, volume rockers and it accompanies red S Pen stylus. 

On the back, it sports a grey First Order logo in the center and Star Wars engraved at the base.

The company is also offering the Sith-inspired retail package, which includes Kylo Ren-themed leather cover, Galaxy Buds with the red-and-black colourway case, and an exclusive metal badge as a collector’s item. 

Inside, the Galaxy Note10+ phone's UI comes with custom Star Wars-inspired animation, themes, icons, wallpaper, ringtones and more.
However, the internal hardware remains the same as the original Galaxy Note10+ series.

Samsung Galaxy Note10+ sports a 6.8-inch Quad HD+ dynamic AMOLED screen (498 ppi) with nearly bezel-less front-panel. On the back, it comes with a premium shell made of good blend of glass-and-metal.

Under-the-hood, the device, depending on the region of sale, comes with either Qualcomm Snapdragon 855 or Exynos  9825 octa-core processor backed by 12GB RAM, 512GB storage and a  4,300mAh battery with fast and wireless charging capability.

As far as the photography is concerned, it features a quad-camera module, with primary 16MP F2.2 (123-degree), wide-angle 12MP sensor with  OIS (77-degree), 12MP Telephoto lens and a depth camera. On the front, it houses a 10MP dual-pixel (F2.2 aperture and AF).

The Galaxy Note10+ Star Wars edition costs $1,299 (approx. Rs 93,255) and slated to hit stores in select global regions on December 13.
