Jack Grealish to boost Aston Villa ahead of Monday night's Steve Bruce reunion

Villa talisman Grealish missed the defeats to Liverpool and Wolves due to injury but is set to return against his former manager when Newcastle head to Villa Park

19 Nov 2019, 16:30 by Irishmirror.ie

Jack Grealish is on course to return for Aston Villa in Monday's crunch Premier League home clash with Newcastle when former boss Steve Bruce returns.

Villa skipper Grealish has missed the club's last two top-flight games as Dean Smith's men have lost to Liverpool and Wolves .

He also missed the club's Carabao Cup win over a weakened Wolves side due to a calf injury sustained in the 3-0 loss at Manchester City last month.

It saw Grealish, 24, leave the Etihad Stadium on crutches but he is due back in training later this week after some time off in the international break.

Grealish has been in fine form this term after inspiring his boyhood club to promotion last season.

He has two goals and three assists and is the most fouled player in the top-flight ahead of Wilfried Zaha after also being singled out in the Championship last term.

But the uncapped former England U21 ace continues to be overlooked by Three Lions boss Gareth Southgate who has selected Leicester's James Maddison instead.

Villa have badly missed Grealish after slipping to 17th in the Premier League – four points below Newcastle who are bossed by Bruce.

Bruce coached Villa from October 2016–October 2018 but was sacked after being hounded out by fans who even threw a cabbage at him.

Former Villa manager Tim Sherwood reckons it is a mistake overlooking Grealish ahead of next summer's European Championships after comparing his ability to England legend Gazza.

Sherwood, in charge at Villa Park from February 2015-October 2015, said: “You have two friendlies in March but I think it may be a bit late for Jack.

“I think we've missed a trick because this kid is special - 100% they missed the boat on him.

"Jack takes the ball to the opposition, he goes right at the heart of people, he dribbles like Paul Gascoigne.”
