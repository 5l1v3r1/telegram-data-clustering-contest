Cardi B Shares A New Freestyle To Tide Fans Over Until Her Sophomore Album

19 Nov 2019, 16:28 by Eddie Fu

On Monday, Cardi B shared a new freestyle on Instagram to give fans “a little something until I finish the album.” Over the Magnum P.I. theme song, Cardi takes aim at her haters and boasts about her come-up.

Cardi opens up about losing some friends during her rise to fame:

These bum bitches is broke, and I don’t respect that
Lot of sugar daddy talk, but they don’t reflect that
You ain’t open up no business, got no Benzes
I be in a mansion and you be in my mentions
I came right out the trenches to top of the charts
Lost friends on the way, this shit is breakin' my heart

She continues by referencing her role in the movie Hustlers:

I don’t act, I’m a hustler, just playin' my part
What you spend on your whip, bitch, I spend on my fit

As Cam'ron fans would point out, Cardi isn’t the first MC to rap over the Magnum P.I. theme. Back in 1998, the Dipset leader dropped a track titled “357” that samples the detective show’s theme song, which is created by Mike Post.

Cardi’s freestyle is meant to tide over fans until her sophomore album, tentatively titled Tiger Woods. Earlier this year, the Bronx rapper said it would be arriving in 2020.

In a recent Billboard interview, Cardi spoke about not chasing trends on the project.

“There’s certain music that I want to do, but I feel like, ‘Is people interested in that?’” she explained. “I feel like it’s more like a twerk sound going on right now. It’s just like, ‘Should I just do my music around that?’ But I cannot just go with what’s hot. I still gotta go with what I want to do.”
