Liverpool's main sponsor Standard Chartered cut business ties with Prince Andrew over Jeffrey Epstein links

Standard Chartered will no longer be a partner in the Duke of York's business start-up project

19 Nov 2019, 16:39 by Kathryn Batte

Liverpool's main sponsor Standard Chartered have become the latest major business to cut ties with Prince Andrew over the Royal's links to sex offender Jeffrey Epstein.

The company have been the main sponsor of the Reds since 2010 and their relationship with club will extend until the end of the 2022-23 season, making it the second-longest sponsorship deal in Liverpool 's history.

Standard Chartered revealed to MailOnline that its bosses have decided it will no longer partner the Duke of York's business start-up project: Pitch@Palace.

A spokesman said: "We won't be renewing the sponsorship when it ends in February."

Prince Andrew has faced further scrutiny and backlash after he defended his friendship with Epstein in an interview with BBC Newsnight.
