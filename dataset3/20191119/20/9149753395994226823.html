Death Stranding and Control lead The Game Awards 2019 nominations

19 Nov 2019, 20:15 by Andy Chalk

The Game Awards is an annual Geoff Keighley-produced event during which the videogame industry and its many fans get together to watch trailers, snark about sponsorships, and yes, hand out a few trophies in various "best of" categories. 107 different games were nominated in those categories for the 2019 show, led by Death Stranding with eight, and Control with seven.

The nominations were picked by a jury of 80 "media and influencer outlets," PC Gamer included. After the nomination phase, winners are selected via a fan vote, which is open now. (Given Keighley's friendship with Hideo Kojima and cameo in Death Stranding, we can guess which game he's pulling for.)

Other big games this year include Sekiro: Shadows Die Twice, with five nominations, and Resident Evil 2 and The Outer Worlds, which drew four each. The "Best Performance" category features two nominations each from Control and Death Stranding, which is interesting (Gears 5 and The Outer Worlds round out the category, and for the record I'm all-in on Matthew Porretta as Dr. Casper Darling), but I'm a little confused by the "Fresh Indie Game Presented by Subway" award, which stands separate from the Best Independent Game category. 

The Game Awards website says that the Fresh by Subway category is for new indie studios that released their first game in 2019, but Outer Wilds studio Mobius 38 has two previous games to its credit—Beacon 38 and Terra Chrome—while Untitled Goose Game developer House House put out Push Me Pull You in 2016. DeadToast Entertainment also has nearly a dozen releases to its credit: Mostly web games, which might seem a little tenuous, but that's where My Friend Pedro got its start.
