Johnson and Corbyn clash in General Election debate

19 Nov 2019, 20:23

Boris Johnson and Jeremy Corbyn are going head-to-head in a General Election TV debate.

The Prime Minister and Labour leader are live on ITV until 9pm, in the first of a series of political debates ahead of the December 12 election.

The two men most likely to lead the country after the December 12 vote have already clashed on Brexit, the union and coalitions.

The Prime Minister blamed a ‘deadlocked’ parliament for the election and blocking Brexit, while warning against a coalition between Mr Corbyn and Nicola Sturgeon. He suggested such a government would lead to two referendums – one on Brexit and Scottish independence.

Mr Corbyn argued that the Conservatives are failing on the economy, climate crisis, NHS and Brexit. He added that there had already been nine years of chaotic coalitions. 

The hour-long ITV debate is being followed by another round of political interviews with the minor party leaders at 10pm.
