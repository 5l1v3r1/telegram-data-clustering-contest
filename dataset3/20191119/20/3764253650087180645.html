Student, 21, works part-time on weekends as a young Queen Elizabeth lookalike

19 Nov 2019, 20:16

- Harriet Ball, a 21-year-old actress, is making a fortune acting the lookalike of Queen Elizabeth II

- After starring in The Crown, a popular Netflix series, she became so popular for how she bears a striking resemblance to the queen

- On weekends, Harriet charges at least £50 (N23,476.50) to put up show where she would act like a young version of the monarch

Harriet Ball, a 21-year-old lady, has built a career serving as a royal replica to Queen Elizabeth. She first started when she acted in a Neflix series titled The Crown.

A final year student studying screenwriting, Harriet has been able to make her facial semblance with the queen into a profitable venture since the series she acted in became very popular, Daily Mail reports.

She now practices the lookalike business on weekends. In one of her appearance shows, the Duke of Kent made a commentary on obvious likeness to the royal mother.

Harriet charges from £50 for her appearance while she also does charity shows. She said she has very huge respect for the queen and she thinks the series has even added to her popularity.

The final year student also spoke about how she has had gigs when she was asked to shake hands with the public like the queen would do, recite the queen’s famous Children’s Hours Speech and put up a show of how the royal mother inspects the Home Guard at the 1940s weekends.

“Earlier this year, I performed at a local Veterans café for the D-Day commemorations as HRH The Duke of Kent was visiting.

"It's so rewarding when people go ‘Oh my goodness, you look just like the Queen when she was younger’, it always makes me smile, as I feel I'm doing a good job,” she said.

The young actress also said that people stop her in the street to comment on her striking resemblance even at times when she is not putting up a replica show.

The 21-year-old said she also invested so much into the kind of clothes the queen wore during World War II (WW2), so she can achieve an accurate portrayal.

“When I started university, I invested in the exact ATS Uniform that the queen wore during WW2 so I can do an accurate portrayal of Her Majesty.

“By this point, The Crown had been released on Netflix, and more and more people were tagging me, messaging me and sharing stills from the show going 'You look SO much like her! You have to become a lookalike!’” she said.

The young woman also commended the queen on how she has been a great queen and monarch.
