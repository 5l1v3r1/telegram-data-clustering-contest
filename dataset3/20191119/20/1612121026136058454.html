Galaxy Watch Active2 UX features get backported to Galaxy Watch, original Galaxy Watch Active

19 Nov 2019, 20:30 by Jared Peters

Samsung has gotten much better about updating older devices with newer software and features lately, which helps keep their current lineup of phones and wearables in sync. Many devices were updated to One UI with Android 9.0 Pie, and several of them will be getting One UI 2.0 with their Android 10 update. The Galaxy Watch family also sees some feature updates from time to time.

Galaxy Watch Active2 Features

The latest update takes some of the new UX stuff from the Galaxy Watch Active2 and brings it back to the original Galaxy Watch Active as well as the standard Galaxy Watch.

Bixby spearheads the list, as Samsung has added some new tricks to its digital assistant. You’ll be able to ask Bixby to start exercise routines or check the time difference between two places, and it’s expanded to support many more languages as well.

The watches will also be getting much more customizable with 24 new sub-dials and complications on top of the 17 already-existing complications available. Additionally, the My Style feature is getting back ported to both watches so you can take a photo of your outfit and have a custom watch face generated to match.

The interface itself is changing up a bit to match some of the small improvements that were made to the Galaxy Watch Active2 that should make your watch a little easier to navigate and manage. Samsung Health also learns a few new tricks and can alert you to a low heart rate, track lap times while running or cycling, and manage fitness goals a little better.

Expect to see this update rollout gradually over the next few weeks.
