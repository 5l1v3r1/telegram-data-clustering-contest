Amur tiger at Calgary Zoo put down due to ovarian cancer and health complications

19 Nov 2019, 23:06 by Stephanie Babych

A female tiger at the Calgary Zoo has been euthanized due to ovarian cancer and related health complications, according to zoo officials.

Alarms were raised when the zoo’s animal care team noticed a change in the tiger’s behaviour over the past several days. The 19-year-old Amur tiger’s activity level and appetite dropped and, after she was examined under anesthesia Monday evening, the care team determined her poor health would severely affect her quality of life, according to a news release from the zoo.

“The difficult decision was made to humanely euthanize Katja,” the Zoo’s statement read.
