ENDO PAIN

Hayley McQueen was found passed out on floor of Sky Sports loos due to excruciating endometriosis period pains

19 Nov 2019, 09:41 by Jennifer Hale

TV presenter Hayley McQueen was found passed out on the floor of the Sky Sports studio toilets after agonising period pains.

The new mum, 39, told how her battle with chronic condition endometriosis saw her faint minutes before going on air.

Hayley wasn’t diagnosed with endometriosis until she was 36 - after two decades of being told by doctors she had everything from just bad periods to constipation and IBS.

Endometriosis caused when cells like the ones in the lining of the womb are found elsewhere in the body. 

But instead of leaving the body as a period the blood has no way to escape leading to inflammation, pain and formation of scar tissue - which can eventually cause infertility.

One frightening episode saw her passed out in the Sky Sports studio ahead of going on air.

She said: “The episodes I’ve had have been quite bad at Sky- I’ve not been able to go on air before.

“I remember coming into work and I was white as a ghost and I felt terrible.

“I had really horrific cramps and I’d wandered off to the toilet and then passed out.

“I was just lying there on the floor, in an increasing amount of pain.

“One of the makeup ladies came in and put a towel on my head and helped look after me and calmed me down. 

“They didn’t know what was wrong with me. 

“It was my female boss at the time who basically came in and put me in a taxi and sent me home.”

The episodes were so severe she later found herself worrying if a Scottish matchday coincides with the day she was due to start her period.

She added: “I used to get nervous, thinking - if I have one of these episodes I can’t even pull myself off the bathroom floor let alone sit in a chair and broadcast live.

What are the symptoms of endometriosis?

There are many different symptoms of endometriosis and they can vary in intensity. Some women with endometriosis will have no symptoms at all.

The most common symptoms are:

  • Painful periods
  • Painful sex
  • Infertility
  • Painful bowel movements
  • Fatigue

Other symptoms include:

  • Lethargy
  • Nausea
  • Extreme tiredness
  • Depression
  • Frequent infections such as thrush
  • Feeling faint/fainting during periods

But when Hayley — the daughter of Scotland footie legend Gordon McQueen — did get pregnant, she was plagued with worry over fears that she would lose her baby.

She explained: “When I first found out that I was expecting all I was worried about was having an ectopic pregnancy or miscarriage because of endometriosis.
