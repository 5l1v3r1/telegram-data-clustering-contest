Flock of turkeys destined for Christmas dinner escape from poultry farm

19 Nov 2019, 09:54

A flock of turkeys appear to have made a desperate last attempt for freedom before they become someone’s Christmas dinner.

Around 50 birds were filmed running near Red Lodge, in Suffolk, on Saturday.

At first, it was thought they may have escaped from the nearby Traditional Norfolk Poultry farm but the owners have said they cannot confirm if the flock belongs to them.

A spokeswoman for the firm said: ‘We’ve had no reports of any turkeys escaping.’

A woman who spotted the turkeys said: ‘I was just surprised to see that many.

‘I think they escaped from a nearby farm, but I think turkeys tend to go back, so they might have done.’

Another walker, who asked not to be named, filmed the gang of birds as it wandered through woodland clearings and posted the footage on Facebook.

Members of the public were so concerned about the birds’ welfare one person even offered up their land for the turkeys to stay on.

It’s not clear whether or not the turkeys have been returned to their home.

Traditional Norfolk Poultry boast of breeding free-range and organic Norfolk Black and Norfolk Bronze turkeys which are renowned for their succulent meat and gamey flavour.
