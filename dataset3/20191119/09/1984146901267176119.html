DWP Universal Credit claimants eligible to get Christmas bonus in December

The one-off tax payment will land in benefit claimants' bank accounts before December 25

19 Nov 2019, 09:13 by Katie Collings

Some Universal Credit claimants will notice a special Christmas bonus in their bank accounts this winter. 

The one-off payments will appear in the accounts of those claiming certain kinds of benefits.

Below is everything you need to know about the Christmas Bonus for 2019 - who's eligible, how much it's worth and how to claim.

Also, find out the benefit payment dates this Christmas.

What is the Christmas Bonus?

The Christmas Bonus is a one-off £10 payment that will land in the accounts of those eligible to receive it in December.

The bonus is usually paid in the first week of December, and is given to households claiming specific benefits.

Am I eligible to receive the Christmas Bonus?

To be eligible for the bonus, you'll need to be claiming one of the following benefits when December pay week falls:

  • Armed Forces Independence Payment
  • Attendance Allowance
  • Carer’s Allowance
  • Constant Attendance Allowance (paid under Industrial Injuries or War Pensions schemes)
  • Contribution-based Employment and Support Allowance (once the main phase of the benefit is entered after the first 13 weeks of claim)
  • Disability Living Allowance
  • Incapacity Benefit at the long-term rate
  • Industrial Death Benefit (for widows or widowers)
  • Mobility Supplement
  • Pension Credit - the guarantee element
  • Personal Independence Payment (PIP)
  • State Pension (including Graduated Retirement Benefit)
  • Severe Disablement Allowance (transitionally protected)
  • Unemployability Supplement or Allowance (paid under Industrial Injuries or War Pensions schemes)
  • War Disablement Pension at State Pension age
  • War Widow’s Pension
  • Widowed Mother’s Allowance
  • Widowed Parent’s Allowance
  • Widow’s Pension

Those on Universal Credit  won't be automatically entitled to the bonus, and must meet one of the categories outlined above to receive it.

Couples who are married, in a civil partnership or are living together will both receive the bonus if they both fall under one of the categories outlined above.

How do I claim the Christmas Bonus?

If you're eligible, you won't need to do anything as the money will be automatically transferred into the same account that your usual benefits are paid into.

If you do receive it, the Christmas Bonus may show up with a transaction ID of  'DWP XB'  in your bank account.

When does Universal Credit get paid over Christmas?

Benefits paid on a bi-weekly basis include support allowance, income support, or jobseekers' allowance.

Universal Credit and all other benefits are usually paid into accounts every two weeks.

Due dates differ for each individual, so check if your usual payment date falls on a Wednesday or a Thursday.

If so your payment should be due on Christmas or Boxing Day, meaning you can expect it to hit your account on Tuesday, December 24.

If your due date falls on New Year's Day (Wednesday, January 1), you can expect your payment to go in on Tuesday, December 31.

Tax Credit payment dates for Christmas and New Year 2019

Due date: December 25, 2019 | Actual pay date: December 24

Due date: December 26, 2019 | Actual pay date: December 24

Due date: January 1, 2020 | Actual pay date: December 31

Child Benefit payment dates for Christmas and New Year 2019

The usual pay dates should stay the same, as neither Christmas nor New Year falls on a Monday or a Tuesday (the usual days Child Benefit is paid) this year.

Child Benefit is usually paid every four weeks in England.
