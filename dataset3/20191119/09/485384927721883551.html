Australian cricket team has reacquainted itself with the line ahead of Pakistan Test series

19 Nov 2019, 09:20 by Richard Hinds

For Australian cricket, the line is there again in plain sight.

Yes, the old behavioural line that was drawn with good intentions, head-butted by those used it to justify dodgy tactics as well as avoid them, scandalously ignored in a Cape Town dressing room and seen only far off on the horizon ever since.

Australian fast bowler James Pattinson did not merely find the line by directing a homophobic slur at an opponent during Victoria's Sheffield Shield match against Queensland, he sailed clean over it.

Pattinson will miss the first Test against Pakistan starting Thursday as the result of an accumulation of conduct code violations including this latest and most serious offence.

But given the nature of the abuse, it would have been quite understandable had Pattinson been given a match on the sidelines to contemplate his intolerable outburst for this offence alone.

Surely by now, even the most foul-mouthed suburban park cricket sledger understands the use of homophobic and racial taunts are not to be tolerated by opponents, onlookers and, you would hope, teammates.

And even if we have not come that far we have the right to expect high-profile players on colossal Cricket Australia contracts will set the example, not succumb to such a base instinct in the heat of the moment.

Pattinson, a hugely popular player among teammates, was at least quick to own his behaviour. "I'm gutted to miss a Test match, but the standards are there for a reason and the fault is mine," he said via a Cricket Australia statement.

Steve Smith did not cross the line. But a man who has worked hard on and off the field to restore a tattered reputation got an abrupt reminder of the standards by which he will be judged for the remainder of his career.

Smith's mistake was to demonstrably question a caught behind decision. He lingered at the crease, waved his bat and in so doing embarrassed the umpire who had raised his finger.
