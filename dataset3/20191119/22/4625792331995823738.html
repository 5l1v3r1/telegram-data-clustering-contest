Ilhan Omar asks judge to go easy on man who pleaded guilty to threatening to kill her

19 Nov 2019, 22:31 by Caitlin Yilek

Rep. Ilhan Omar urged a judge for leniency in sentencing a New York man who threatened to kill her.

The Minnesota Democrat wrote a letter on Tuesday to U.S. District Court Judge Frank Geraci asking for “compassion to be applied” in Patrick Carlineo’s sentencing.

Carlineo, 55, pleaded guilty Monday to calling Omar's office on March 21, telling a staffer that Omar was a “terrorist,” and threatening to shoot her. He faces up to 10 years in prison.

He told the FBI he loved President Trump and hates people he believes are radical Muslims in the United States government, according to the criminal complaint.

“He has taken responsibility for using threatening and inappropriate language to express those beliefs in this instance,” Carlineo’s lawyer, Sonya Zoghlin, told the Star Tribune, adding that her client never intended to harm Omar and never made any plans to do so.

His plea included the admission that he illegally had guns. A 1998 felony criminal mischief conviction prohibited him from possessing guns, prosecutors said.

“There is no doubt that Mr. Carlineo’s crime is grave,” Omar wrote in the two-page letter.

“But we must ask: who are we as a nation if we respond to threats of political retribution with retribution ourselves?” she said. “Punishing the defendant with a lengthy prison sentence or a burdensome financial fine would not rehabilitate him. It would not repair the harm he has caused. It would only increase his anger and resentment.”

Omar said Carlineo should be given a chance “to make amends and seek redemption.”
