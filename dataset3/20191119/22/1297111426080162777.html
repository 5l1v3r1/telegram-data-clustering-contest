Rob Vanstone: How I met my crush at the Crushed Can

19 Nov 2019, 22:29 by Rob Vanstone

Breaking news from the too-cheap-to-buy-a-card and too-lazy-to-think-of-a-different-intro departments: Happy 25th anniversary to my wife, Chryssoula Filippakopoulos.

We celebrated our 20th anniversary on May 15.

None of this is even remotely sensible.

Please permit me to explain.

It was Nov. 19, 1994. The Regina Pats were in Moose Jaw to play the Warriors at the Civic Centre — the Crushed Can. As the Regina Leader-Post’s hockey writer of the day, I was in The Friendly City (or whatever it is called now) to cover the spectacle.

The buildup was a marketer’s dream. The night before, when the Pats and Warriors had met at the edifice formerly known as the Agridome, the opposing head coaches — Norm Johnston (Regina) and Al Tuer (Moose Jaw) — nearly came to blows between the benches.

Those were the days when there was genuine enmity between the Pats and Warriors. The rivalry, barely detectable now, was at its apex.

Hence the attendance — 3,317 — at the 2,969-seat Civic Centre, which was hardly the Civil Centre when the Pats were in town.

Little did I know that one of the 3,317 attendees would change my life.

After the game, won 5-2 by Moose Jaw, I ventured downstairs to interview interim head coach Bill Hicke (substituting for the suspended Johnston) and the Pats’ players.

The visiting team’s dressing room was located downstairs near the Hot Stove Lounge, where the elite would meet and greet after each Warriors home game.

While walking hurriedly down the hallway, I said hello to a friend, who was standing beside a special someone whom I had never met.

This complete stranger smiled at me … and I died a little.

I had never seen such a beautiful smile. (To this day, that has not changed. I write this at the not-inconsiderable risk of offending all the other avid smilers out there.)

Somehow, I spluttered my way through the post-game interviews, whereupon I returned to my sprawling, 62-square-foot south Regina estate to confide in the dog.

During the drive home from Moose Jaw, I kept thinking, “Who WAS that?”

It was, honestly, love at first sight.

Over the next few days, I made a few inquiries and discovered that her name was (how do I spell that again?) Chryssoula Filippakopoulos.

She had just started working at the Moose Jaw Times-Herald after earning a journalism degree at Ryerson in her hometown of Toronto.

“You should know,” one of my well-placed sources told me, “that she will only date Greek guys.”

Like the can, I was crushed.

Time went by. We were not introduced until the following summer, when our paths crossed at a Moose Jaw Diamond Dogs baseball game. (Murray Brace, we miss thee.)

I was a nervous disaster. I had no idea what to say to Chryssoula, although it did occur to me to inform her that my surname was, ohhhh, Metropoulos?

In any event, nothing was accomplished in 1995. Or 1996. For me, that was a hot streak, because I was more accustomed to accomplishing absolutely nothing over a span of decades.

Then came Valentine’s Day, 1997.

How romantic … right?

There was only one problem. I spent Feb. 14 in the Coronary Care Unit at the Plains Health Centre.

I woke up that morning with severe tightness in my chest … and shooting pains down both arms … and I was sweating profusely.

Ill-advisedly, but with some urgency, I drove myself to the hospital — but not before taking what I thought was one last look at my Scottish terrier and warbling, “Well, it’s been fun, Oscar.”

Before I knew it, I was on an examining table in Emergency, being examined by multiple cardiologists. (This is not recommended, as much as I revere cardiologists. When there is a team of cardiologists, it is not a good sign.)

The doctors’ first suspicion was that I had an arterial blockage. Oh, great …

However, further tests revealed that I had (how do I spell that again?) pericarditis — an inflammation of the lining of the heart.

I ended up spending the night in hospital. Thanks to excellent medical care and some very large pills, I was back at work within two days.

Shortly thereafter, I ran into (swoon) Chryssoula at (where else?) a Warriors game. She was surprised to see me, considering the widespread rumour that I had suffered a heart attack.

I told her of my valiant, heroic struggle, showed her the pill bottle, and made a joke: “I actually feel OK. But if I owe you any money, get it from me soon, because I might not make it to my next birthday.”

“When is that?” she inquired.

“March 30,” I responded, demonstrating a command of the subject matter.

Little did I suspect that, on March 30, 1997, I would receive a birthday card, postmarked “Moose Jaw.”

That card, from Chryssoula, was the impetus for FINALLY asking her out.

The rest, like the dear old Crushed Can, is history.

(Rob Vanstone is the Regina Leader-Post’s sports editor.)
