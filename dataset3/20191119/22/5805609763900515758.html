How to Prioritize Notifications in the Windows 10 Action Center

19 Nov 2019, 22:15 by Karanpreet Singh

Well, if you are using the Windows 10 operating system for a while, then you might be well aware of the Action Center. Action Center is just like a Notification Panel on Android which acts as a central location for all your notifications to Windows. Before Windows 10, there was no Action Center.

With the 2016’s Anniversary Update of Windows 10, Microsoft has introduced a lot of changes to the Action Center like you can easily dismiss a group of notifications, reply to messaging services directly, prioritize apps, etc. Out of all those, the Notification Priorities are the most noticeable one as it ensures that the notifications from your most-used apps are not buried deep down in the Action Center.

How to Set Notification Priorities for Apps in Windows 10 Action Center

So, in this article, we have decided to share a working method that would help you set Notification Priorities for Apps in Windows 10 action center. So, let’s check out how to prioritize which notifications you see from the Action center on Windows 10 computer.

The method is quite straightforward and easy, and you just need to follow some simple steps that will set the priorities of all the notifications. So follow the steps below to proceed.

Set Notification Priorities for Apps in Windows 10

Step 1. First of all, open the Settings app by pressing on the Start button and click on the Settings icon. From there, click on ‘System’

Step 2. Under the System Settings, you need to click on the ‘Notifications & Actions’

Step 3. There you will see the list of apps installed on your device.

Step 4. In the next step, click on the app whose notification priority you want to set.

Step 5. Now you will see three Priority options. Set the Priority level as per your wish.

That’s it, you are done! This is how you can Prioritize Notifications in the Windows 10 Action Center.
