Joe Marler dismisses England boss Eddie Jones' concerns over Saracens scandal

England boss Jones is worried that the salary-cap scandal could be a problem when his squad comes together for the Six Nations but Harlequins ace Marler disagrees

19 Nov 2019, 22:00 by Alex Spink, https://www.facebook.com/alexspinkmirror/

Joe Marler has dismissed Eddie Jones’ concerns that the Saracens salary-cap scandal could fracture the national squad ahead of the Six Nations.

The England forward says he does not expect team mates to fall out over it - nor for Sarries stars to prioritise the pressing needs of their club over those of the country.

Jones voiced fears on Monday that there may be “some dislocation” between stars from an organisation found to have cheated the system for three seasons and the rest - describing it as a “reality”.

Marler’s Harlequins club mates have been critical of Saracens, who have been slapped with a £5.36 million fine and 35-point deduction which leaves them 26 adrift of Premiership safety.

But the prop disagreed with England coach Jones that the scandal would have a “significant impact” when the World Cup finalists reassemble.

“There might be a little bit more banter,” said Marler. “I’ve teased the Saracens boys about it for the last few years!

  

"Just as they’ve teased me about my s**t hair, how bad we’ve been here in the past, my breath, my wonky nose.

“But you come in with the national side, put your club hat to one side and you all buy into what we’re about playing for England.

"You can’t change the past. It’s been dealt with. I don’t think any bridges will need to be built there.”

Marler, who indicated he will continue his Red Rose career, knows how big a call it is to make yourself unavailable to your country - having done so twice before.

He said: “There is always that choice but I would be surprised if boys did choose to based on Sarries having a strong enough and big enough squad to cope anyway.”

Quins boss Paul Gustard agreed, saying such a call would be tantamount to players choosing to retire from international rugby over “something that a club has done”.

Saracens’ week went from bad to worse yesterday when they were fined an undisclosed amount for failing to attend the Champions Cup launch a fortnight ago.
