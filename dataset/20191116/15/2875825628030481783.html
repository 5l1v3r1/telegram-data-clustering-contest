Strictly stars ditch lace and sequins for leopard print and sunnies in Blackpool

The contestants are in the brightly lit English city for a weekend of celebrations and dance routines as Strictly takes over the Blackpool Tower Ballroom

16 Nov 2019, 15:19 by Irishmirror.ie

The stars and professionals of Strictly Come Dancing have descended on Blackpool for the annual live episode from the iconic Blackpool Tower Ballroom - and they all look to be in high spirits indeed.

Seven lucky celebrities have made it thus far in the 2019 competition - and are joined by their dance partners for what will no doubt be a breathtaking night of fancy footwork.

Former England footballer Alex Scott, 35, comedian Chris Ramsey, 33, EastEnders star Emma Barton, 42, CBBC presenter Karim Zeroual, 20, soap hunk Kelvin Fletcher, 35, TV judge Michelle Visage, 51, and YouTube star Saffron Barker, 19, have all made it to the landmark episode.

And they are joined by respective dance partners Neil Jones, Karen Hauer, Anton du Beke, Amy Dowden, Oti Mabuse, Giovanni Pernice and AJ Pritchard for the big night.

Landing at the iconic Blackpool Tower Ballroom building on Saturday, the stars and were met by well wishers and Strictly superfans who embraced chilly weather for an up close look at the contestants.

And the stars of the show didn’t disappoint as they signed autographs and posed for selfies with the hordes of adoring fans.

Show host Tess Daly, 50, looked radiant (and decades younger than her age would suggest) as she arrived wrapped in a chic, grey, Louis Vuitton scarf and power suit.

American star Michelle (who is a judge on popular reality show Ru Paul’s Drag Race) showcased her absorption into UK culture by channelling her inner Pat Butcher and wearing a thigh leopard print coat.

While Anton Du Beke looked something like a Poundland version of Del Boy as he sauntered up in a wooden coat and a flatcap.

Karim and Amy were all smiles as they arrived together in puffy black jackets.

While adorable dance pro Oti fully embraced the Blackpool way of life as she arrived carrying a stick of rock and wearing an “I heart Blackpool” hoodie.

Dianne Buswell was impossible to miss as she sauntered up to the tower in an offensively bright orange coat that looked as comfortable and warm as a sleeping bag.

While married dance star Aljaž Skorjanec camped things up in a garish pair of Blackpool sunglasses.

Elsewhere, show judges Bruno Tonioli and Craig Revel-Horwood looked slightly harassed as they arrived ahead of Saturday night’s show.

Craig glared at photographers with a strained expression while dressed in black.

And Bruno scowled behind dark sunglasses as he marched into the iconic venue.

Strictly Come Dancing continues tonight, Saturday 16 November 2019, from 7:05pm on BBC One.
