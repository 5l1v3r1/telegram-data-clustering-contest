Desperate plea from family of mum who went missing from mental health hospital

16 Nov 2019, 15:08

The daughter of a woman who has been missing from a mental health unit for over two weeks is begging for help in finding her.

Georgina Fletcher, 50, known as Gina, was last seen on October 31 before escaping from Lakeside Mental Health Unit in Isleworth, west London.

Her daughter Leah and sister Siobhian have been worried sick since she vanished and are desperate for updates.

Police are getting increasingly concerned over Georgina’s welfare and are trying to track her down.

Leah told Metro.co.uk: ‘She’s gone missing before but she would always let us know where she was or the police would be able to track her phone.

‘She was admitted six weeks ago when the police could see she was unwell and they took her to the mental health unit.’

The family said they’d been trying to get officers to intervene three months ago as they feared for Georgina’s safety.

Leah added: ‘They have searched every inch of nearby Crane Park over the last seven days and they are planning to expand their search.

‘I just really want to find my mum. I’d like anyone with information to get in touch. The missing persons post on my Facebook account I have done has been shared over 10,000 times and quite a few people have come and volunteered to look for her.’

In a message to her mother, Leah added: ‘The police want you to know you are not in any trouble whatsoever. You know what we are talking about.’

The Lakeside Mental Health Unit is run by the West London NHS Trust who said they were unable to comment.

Metro.co.uk has contacted the Metropolitan Police.
