Almost 100 flood warnings and alerts still in place across the Midlands

The Environmental Agency has issued 94 flood warnings and alerts across Birmingham, Staffordshire, Warwickshire and Worcestershire

16 Nov 2019, 13:31 by Oprah Flash, https://www.birminghammail.co.uk/authors/oprah-flash/

Almost 100 flood alerts and wanring are still in place across the Midlands this weekend after the region was battered by torrential rain.

There were 42 warnings - signalling a requirement for immediate action - in Birmingham, Staffordshire, Warwickshire and Worcestershire on Saturday, November 16, according to the Environment Agency.

There were also 50 flood alerts, warning of possible flooding.

Birmingham

Across Birmingham and the Black Country there were two flood alerts in place.

For residents living near the River Tame, the Environmental Agency said: "River levels are high but steady at the Tamworth river gauge as a result of recent heavy rainfall. Consequently, the risk of flooding remains.

"Flooding is affecting low-lying land and roads adjacent to the River Tame between Horseley Heath and Castle Vale and the Ford Brook between Walsall and Bescot. We expect river levels to remain high. We are closely monitoring the situation."

 

Residents and motorists closer to the River Blythe have been advised that river levels remain high but are beginning to fall at the Castle Farm river gauge after recent heavy rainfall.

Consequently, the risk of flooding remains. Flooding is affecting low-lying land and roads adjacent to the River Blythe between Cheswick Green and Blyth End. River levels are falling, but still pose a flood risk.

Staffordshire

In Staffordshire there are currently two flood warnings and 12 flood alerts in place.

For the River Blythe at Blythe End, river levels remain high and continue to fall at the Castle Farm river gauge as a result of recent rainfall. Consequently, the risk of property, roads and farmland flooding remains.

The weather outlook is improving and further light rainfall is forecast over the next 24 hours.
