Conor McGregor reflects on Irish Mirror's coverage of his famous double world title win

The Irishman became the first ever two-weight UFC champion just over three years ago

16 Nov 2019, 13:13 by Mirror Sport

Conor McGregor has reflected on the Irish Mirror's coverage of his historic Lightweight title win in New York three years ago.

The Irishman became the first ever two-weight UFC champion at Madison Square Garden, in what was the first UFC event to be held in the state after the sport was unbanned.

And responding to journalist Petesy Carroll sharing a photo of the two-page spread featured on the Mirror on Twitter, McGregor said: "Ah the aul mirror".

It was, to date, McGregor's last win inside the octagon - he lost a boxing match to Floyd Mayweather in 2017 and then was submitted last year in a UFC lightweight title fight with Khabib Nurmagomedov.

But Conor McGregor's striking coach has declared that the Irishman is in "phenomenal shape" ahead of his January return to the UFC .
