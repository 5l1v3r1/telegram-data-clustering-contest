Tributes paid to Liverpool's oldest living captain, Johnny Wheeler, who has died aged 91

Crosby-born Johnny Wheeler spent seven years at Anfield and once scored a memorable quickfire hat-trick in just over four minutes

16 Nov 2019, 13:50 by Dan Kay

Tributes have been paid to the oldest living former captain of Liverpool FC, Johnny Wheeler, who has died at the age of 91.

Crosby-born Wheeler played 177 games for the Reds between 1956 and 1961, scoring 23 goals, but did not arrive at Anfield until he was 28 years old having begun his career with Tranmere Rovers in 1944.

He played 101 league games for the Prenton Park side before joining Bolton Wanderers in 1951 and featured in the famous 1953 'Stanley Matthews' FA Cup final which the Trotters lost 4-3 to a last minute goal having led 3-1.

Phil Taylor brought him to Anfield for a fee of £9,000 in the summer of 1956 and he made an immediate impact, scoring one of the quickest hat-tricks ever recorded when bagging a treble against Port Vale that November, notching in the 81st, 82nd and 85th minutes in a 4-1 Reds win.

Taylor appointed Wheeler, who was capped once by England, skipper at the beginning of the 1958/59 season but by the start of Bill Shankly's first full season, 1960/61, he had to share the number 4 shirt with Gordon Milne and was gradually phased out of the team, making his final appearance against Plymouth Argyle in December 1961.

Wheeler was appointed player-manager of former Football League club New Brighton but never took up the post and became assistant-trainer at Bury where he stayed until 1969.

A tweet from Liverpool's official account said: "We are deeply saddened by the passing of former captain Johnny Wheeler at the age of 91.

"The thoughts of everybody at Liverpool Football Club are with Johnny’s family and friends at this sad time.

"RIP Johnny 1928-2019."
