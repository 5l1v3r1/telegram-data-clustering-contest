Chelsea 'issue warning' to former Derby County assistant over social media - report

Derby County latest | Jody Morris regularly posted on social media while assistant manager at Derby - and Chelsea have reportedly had something to say about his activity after a recent tweet

16 Nov 2019, 13:48 by Josh Murray

Former Derby County assistant manager Jody Morris has reportedly been warned by Chelsea about his social media activity after a post in reference to ex-Blues boss Jose Mourinho.

Morris was Frank Lampard's assistant at Pride Park last season, before moving back to Stamford Bridge with his former teammate when Lampard became the new Chelsea head coach in July.

Morris is active on Instagram and Twitter, as he was during his Rams spell.

According to the Daily Mail, he has been told by Chelsea "to tone down his social media activity" following the Mourinho post.

The ex-Blues and Leeds United midfielder retweeted a video clip of Mourinho analysing Chelsea's start to the season, adding the comment: "Jose's still worried", with three laughing face emojis.

The report adds: "Chelsea feel such activity is unbecoming and inappropriate for a senior member of their coaching team."

Lampard's side currently sit third in the Premier League table, with 26 points from their opening 12 games.

Among the regulars in the Chelsea team have been Mason Mount and Fikayo Tomori, who both enjoyed eye-catching loan spells at Pride Park in 2018-19.

Mount has four goals in 18 appearances, while Tomori has notched 14 appearances, scoring in a win away at Wolverhampton Wanderers.
