Police storm houses across Merseyside in Friday night drug raids

Pictures show huge quantities of drugs which officers found at the homes

16 Nov 2019, 17:00 by Max Clements

A stun gun and large quantities cannabis were among items seized from addresses across the city last night as armed police carried out a series of raids.

Five people were arrested in police raids in Old Swan, Croxteth and Kirkby that took place last night.

The raids are part of Merseyside Police's 'Operation Target', a large scale crackdown against serious and violent crime.

A man and a woman were detained after a large quantity of cannabis was seized from an address in Old Swan during a police raid.

A man tried to escape from police through the back of the property during the bust but he was detained by officers.

A 22-year-old man from Garston and a 24-year-old woman from Old Swan were arrested on suspicion of possession with intent to supply cannabis.

In Kirkby, police recovered a stun gun and suspected Class A drugs from an address in Overdene Walk.

Officers searched the address due to the smell of cannabis and later arrested two men.

 A 40 year-old man and a 54-year old man on suspicion of possession with intent to supply Class A drugs, possession of Class B drugs and possession of a firearm.

Police also stormed an address in the Croxteth area where they found cannabis and other related paraphernalia.

A 23-year-old man from Croxteth was arrested on suspicion of possession with intent to supply Class B drugs.

A police spokesperson said that officers are continuing to carry out stop searches in these areas and will 'maintain an increased' police presence in these communities.

Chief superintendent Matt Boyle, Head of local policing, said: "Our increased disruption activity across Merseyside continues this weekend. We'll keep acting on all information received to bring about positive results like you see here last night, and remove drugs, weapons and those people who cause harm from your communities.

"Key to this work are the residents most affected, so help us make your streets safe to live, and hostile places for criminals to operate.

"The action continues on a daily basis, and we’ll use everything within our powers to bring anyone responsible for drug supply and other serious and organised crime to justice.

"This includes warrants, the continued use of stop searches to disrupt criminals, and land searches in areas we believe they may be storing weapons.
