'Massively cruel' - Former Leeds United striker gives verdict on Aston Villa injury crisis

Latest Aston Villa news | AVFC will be without their first and second choice goalkeepers due to injury when they resume their Premier League campaign after the international break

16 Nov 2019, 17:46 by Mark Wakefield

Former Leeds United striker Noel Whelan has been discussing the recent injuries to two Aston Villa goalkeepers.

Tom Heaton, who signed by the Lions for £8m from Burnley in the summer, picked up an injury in the build-up to last weekend’s defeat to Wolverhampton Wanderers.

The injury forced the stopper to withdraw from the England squad for this month’s round of international fixtures.

And to make matters worse, stand-in keeper Jed Steer was forced off early in the loss at Molineux with a calf injury, and replaced by Orjan Nyland .

 

With timescales on Heaton and Steer’s injuries unclear, it appears that Nyland will be Villa’s preferred keeper for the foreseeable future.

Whelan says it is “massively cruel” what has happened to Villa with the injuries, especially for Steer who has been waiting in the wings behind Heaton all season so far.

“Rarely do you see injuries to goalkeepers but you’ve got two within the space of one game for Aston Villa,” Whelan told Football Insider.
