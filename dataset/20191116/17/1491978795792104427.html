Czechoslovakia’s Velvet Revolution, 1989 - in pictures

16 Nov 2019, 17:15

Thirty years ago, Czech photographer Bohumil Eichler was working for a dissident student-run news agency when the Velvet Revolution began. His work from Prague has rarely been seen, until now.

21 August 1989

A young woman protester is arrested by a riot policeman at a demonstration in Prague’s Wenceslas Square commemorating the anniversary of the 1968 invasion of Czechoslovakia by Warsaw Pact armies, which crushed the Prague Spring

Photograph: The Bohumil Eichler Collection

17 November 1989, 18:30

Police confront student demonstrators on Národní třída in Prague, a clash that sparked the Velvet Revolution
