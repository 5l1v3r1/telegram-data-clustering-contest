Premier League football clubs cashing in on Christmas with expensive fan merch

EXCLUSIVE: Premier League football clubs are looking for a festive footie windfall, charging astronomical prices for branded gifts. Fans can choose from a £4600 Man Utd watch, or a Chelsea tree bauble for £8

16 Nov 2019, 20:21 by Irishmirror.ie

Manchester United are back on top of the league – for flogging football’s most expensive Christmas gifts.

As well as replica kits, hats, scarves and t-shirts, United lead the way in the Premier League when it comes to pricey pressies.

Dearest of the lot is a £4,600 TAG Heuer Carrera watch – twice the price of arch-rivals Manchester City ’s £1,900 Triumph watch.

The TAG Heuer Carrera timepiece has a red strap and the club logo on the face and reverse.

The club also have a tie-in with fashion designer Paul Smith selling his trendy team scarves for £105 and club ties at £110.

Man City are second place for pricey gifts. Fans can splash out on the £1,900 Triumph watch, £100 cufflinks or a £500 toy robot.

Liverpool , currently riding high in the Premier League, are third in the super-expensive presents league.

A “dream scene canvas” by international sports artist Jamie Cooper, featuring Anfield legends and painted to celebrate the club's 125th anniversary costs £1,850.

Fans with £325 can also buy an old stand seat signed by ex-player and manager Kenny Dalglish and a signed Robbie Fowler boot for £350.

Clubs say the festive gifts bring in much-needed extra income but critics have branded the charges as “astronomical”.

West Ham have a £1,500 shirt signed by goal scorers in the final game at their Upton Park ground when they beat Man United 3-2 in 2016.

Spurs are selling a £350 cockerel 9ct gold pendant, a £200 watch and £70 earrings as well as gifts including a Tottenham themed Toblerone chocolate bar for £12.99.

Not to be outdone, rivals Arsenal have signed photos of Thierry Henry for £250 and watches for £95.

At the other end of the scale, Chelsea flog a pack of two Christmas tree decorations for £10 and Christmas baubles for a mere £8 and Burnley have a Lego-style football for £15.

Fans of new boys Norwich, struggling at the bottom of the league don’t need lofty ambitions when it comes to gift ideas. They’ve got novelty items that include a dog coat in club colours for £19 and £31 brollies.

And fans of fellow newcomers Sheffield United can buy a brick in their Bramall Lane ground for £35.

Jamie Vardy’s Leicester City also don’t go crazy when it comes to stretching the bank balance of fans. A club watch will set them back £90 and cufflinks £60.

Burnley also know their fans have modest budgets - they’ve got a Lego-style football for £15.

Southampton fans can buy a personalised St Mary’s Stadium brick for £50 while south coast rivals Bournemouth have club earrings for £123.50.

Meanwhile, Wolves have a £30 engraved tankard and Brighton have stud earrings with the team’s crest for just £4.

Everton fans can buy gifts including a club washing bag for £11.20 while Aston Villa has a team Monopoly game for £36.

Newcastle United owner Mike Ashley has made a fortune selling discounted sports kit and there are few items to break the bank on Magpie’s club shop site.

But a Gazza signed photograph is £350 and one by Kevin Keegan is £300.

Crystal Palace offers a garden gnome at £18.99 while Watford are selling a Hornets hip flask for £30.

Martyn James of consumer rights website Resolver said: “Some of the prices clubs are charging for Christmas gifts - just because they feature a team badge or logo - are absolutely astronomical.

“Fans would do well to stop and think before shelling out and ending up out of pocket. It’s also a shame to see wealthy sides overcharging for small stocking fillers you could easily source from a local market for just a few quid.”
