Video: ESPN Analysts Debate Nick Saban’s Tua Tagovailoa Decision

16 Nov 2019, 20:19 by Dan Lyons

Typically, most teams would be thrilled to be beating a conference opponent 38-7. Unfortunately for Alabama, the main storyline from this game is the brutal injury suffered by Tua Tagovailoa, when things already seemed well in hand for Nick Saban’s team.

Tagovailoa, who has been dealing with an ankle injury that kept him out of the game against Arkansas a few weeks ago, remained in the game up 35-7, to attempt a three-minute drill at the end of the half.

Some coaches would have pulled their banged up Heisman candidate quarterback  by that point. Saban wanted to get him that valuable end of half drill experience, which you can’t always replicate in a true game situation.

On a pass attempt, Tua rolled left, and came down awkwardly after being hit by two Bulldog defenders. The new injury is reportedly a hip issue, and the quarterback was heard “screaming in pain” as he was taken off the field.
