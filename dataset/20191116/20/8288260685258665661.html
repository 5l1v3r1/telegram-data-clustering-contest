'Spot on' - Former Birmingham City star re-signs for League Two club and makes second debut

Latest Birmingham City news | The 31-year-old made more than 150 appearances during his time with Blues

16 Nov 2019, 20:33 by Mark Wakefield

League Two side Swindon Town have confirmed they have re-signed former Birmingham City defender Paul Caddis.

Caddis rejoins the Robins on a short-term deal, six years after leaving the club to sign for Blues in 2013.

The experienced right-back made more than 150 appearances in two spells for Blues, with his first stint coming as a loan spell from Swindon in the 2012-13 season.

His move to St Andrew’s was then made permanent that summer, and he went on to spend three-and-a-half more seasons with Blues before signing for Bury in 2017.

 

Since leaving Birmingham, Caddis has had spells at Bury, Blackburn Rovers, Bradford City, before rejoining the club he initially signed for in 2010.

His first stint with Swindon saw Caddis make 92 appearances and has now re-signed for the club on a short-term deal.

Prior to joining Swindon, the 31-year-old has been training with the club in recent weeks to keep fit and sharp and manager.
