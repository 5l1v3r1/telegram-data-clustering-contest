'Vision from teammates was lacking' - Kyle Wooton's Barrow performance analysed

Magpies forward endured a frustrating afternoon

16 Nov 2019, 20:04 by Noah Abrahams, Leigh Curtis

Notts County were beaten 3-0 by Barrow this afternoon in what was their first home defeat since August.

Here Noah Abrahams assessed the performance of Kyle Wootton as Neal Ardley's side experienced frustration against the Bluebirds.

Playing furthest forward in black and white, Magpies attacker Kyle Wootton piled the pressure on when he could as a flustered Notts County lost 3-0 at Meadow Lane.

Having started every game since September, the Scunthorpe United loanee had it all to do from early on, his side trailing from two quick goals as he attempted to penetrate a high Barrow defensive line.

Seeing limited action in the opening 45 minutes in what promised to be a competitive fixture, Wootton spent more time in his own half than the opposition's for the most part, dropping back regularly to aid his struggling colleagues.

Aware that the serial goal-scorer had found form already this campaign, Barrow defenders Connor Brown and Matt Platt stuck to the number 14 like glue in the second period.

Proving to fans that he had “more to give” was looking a challenge.

On six goals this season so far, the 23-year-old struggled as he pondered around the six-yard box with little to work on in the final third.

A talented centre forward, the he looked frustrated with a lacklustre performance.

Remaining at the heart of the action, Wootton knocked on Joel Dixon’s door when coming whiskers away from meeting and converting a beautiful cross from the right-hand side.

Taking instructions regularly from Notts boss Neal Ardley, it became quickly apparent that any goals scored would be as a result of the man who netted twice in the FA Cup last week.

Thus however, was not to be. Signalling for the ball and sneaking through cracks all the way through today’s encounter, vision from teammates was lacking massively – as did a goal scoring opportunities.

Previously unbeaten at home since August, the hosts today were on the receiving end of a 3-0 defeat.

In one of Wootton’s “favourite loan spells”, the result will not be a highlight in what has been a so far positive stint.
