Maharashtra govt formation: Draft CMP ready, Shiv Sena, Cong, NCP shift focus to power-sharing formula

The Congress, which won lesser seats as compared to NCP and Sena, has been insisting on an equitable power distribution model on the ground that each party needs the other to come to power.

16 Nov 2019, 03:05 by Sandeep A Ashar

After formulating a draft common minimum programme (CMP), the Shiv Sena, Congress and NCP have now shifted focus to the power-sharing formula between them in Maharashtra.

Even as a final decision on whether the three parties can join hands to form a non-BJP government is pending, sources told The Indian Express that preliminary talks regarding the likely power-sharing arrangement are being carried out at the state level.

Sources added that the initial proposal being discussed is regarding proportionate distribution of key ministerial portfolios between the three parties. While 42 ministerial berths are up for grabs, sources said the preliminary talks are regarding distribution of nine key portfolios — home, finance, revenue, cooperatives, public works, water resources, urban development, rural development and agriculture.

The Congress, which won lesser seats as compared to NCP and Sena, has been insisting on an equitable power distribution model on the ground that each party needs the other to come to power. In the Assembly polls, Sena won 56 seats, NCP 54, and Congress 44.
