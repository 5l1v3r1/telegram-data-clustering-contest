Dominic Matteo undergoes surgery on brain tumour as Liverpool support former defender

Dominic Matteo, 45, fell seriously ill this week and has undergone an operation on a brain tumour in hospital

16 Nov 2019, 10:10 by Liam Prenderville, http://www.facebook.com/mirrorfootball

Former Liverpool and Leeds defender Dominic Matteo has undergone an operation on a brain tumour.

Matteo, 45, is currently being treated in hospital after falling seriously ill earlier this week.

The former Scotland international is now coming out of intensive care.

A statement from Liverpool read: "The thoughts of everyone at the club are with Dom, his family and friends and we will be offering our support to them throughout this difficult time.

"We wish Dom a full and speedy recovery and will offer any support we can to his wife and family.

"Dom's family would like to place on record their gratitude to all staff at Leeds General Infirmary and express their thanks for all of the messages of support they have received."

 

Matteo made his Liverpool debut in 1993 and went on to make over 100 appearances for the club.

Speaking last month, Matteo said: "I didn’t want to leave Liverpool, that’s the truth. I had five years left on my contract. Me and Houllier didn’t get on.”

“It’s very hard to compare with Liverpool. I’d come through the ranks, so you’re brought up a certain way – The Liverpool Way. I’ve never really changed from that. Wherever I went that was always going to be in me."

 

The defender joined Leeds in 2000 and was later named captain as the Elland Road side made it to the semi-finals of the Champions Legaue.

He also had spells with Blackburn and Stoke and won six caps for his country.

Since retiring Matteo has turned his hand at punditry and is a regular on Premier League matchday coverage.
