'Our mistake' - Leeds United man's message after Derby County setback

Derby County latest | The Rams currently trail the Championship play-off places by seven points - but their top-six finish last season was followed by a play-off semi-final triumph over Leeds

16 Nov 2019, 10:25 by Josh Murray

Leeds United striker Tyler Roberts says Elland Road has regained its fortress status after last season's Championship play-off defeat to Derby County.

Marcelo Bielsa's side currently sit third in the table, two points adrift of leaders West Bromwich Albion.

Despite an indifferent start on home soil in 2019-20, Leeds have now won their last four games at Elland Road to strengthen their promotion credentials.

They looked set for a return to the Premier League for much of last season, only to fall just short of the top two.

Bielsa's men faced Derby - who currently trail the top six by seven points - in the play-off semi-finals, and after a 1-0 win at Pride Park, they lost 4-2 in a breathless return leg to end their promotion hopes.

And Roberts - who has one goal from eight appearances this season - believes they are now showing just how they have bounced back from that disappointment at the hands of the Rams.

“The boys have forgot about last year and tried to move on from it,” he told the Yorkshire Evening Post.

“Of course, it was our mistake when we came back to Elland Road (against Derby).

"But Elland Road is a fortress this season, the fans have shown that and kept supporting and believing and it is a great place to be.

"Missing out last season was not a nice feeling.
