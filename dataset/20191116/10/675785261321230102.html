Twerking Take Me Out contestant lifts lid on what goes on behind the scenes of ITV show

Shan, 22, says that one man who rejected her on the show by turning off her light later text her to tell her he'd made a mistake and wished he'd picked her instead

16 Nov 2019, 10:42 by Stephanie Balloo

A Take Me Out contestant who twerked in a bid to impress a potential date has revealed what goes on behind the scenes on the ITV show.

Shan, 22, from Wolverhampton, is one of 30 hopeful women who are looking for a chance at romance - and a trip to Fernando's for a sun-soaked date.

But the former baker-turned-saleswoman, who has even brought her cake creations onto the show, says that one potential love interest got back in touch with her, despite rejecting her on national television.

She told Birmingham Live : "The second episode that aired, the guy who turned my light off messaged me, a couple of weeks later, telling me he wished he'd picked me.

"He felt like we would have had a better vibe."

But Shan was no longer interested.

She laughed: "I was like, 'It's too late now!'

"That was last year after filming. He's got a girlfriend now."

Since appearing on the show, Shan says she has been bombarded with offers from men asking her on a date.

 

She said: "I had a lot of people message me saying, 'If you don't get taken out, I'll have to take you out.'

"There's people messaging me saying, 'You didn't need to go on there, I would have taken you to Fernando's'."

Shan hasn't revealed whether she managed to find love, but did say the experience she had on the show boosted her confidence.

She said: "It's an experience of a lifetime, so I was really thankful for the opportunity.

 

"I found it really interesting with all these strangers, most people would be concerned, I'm a very empowering lady so I knew I would get on with all the girls.

"We made friends for life, we still keep in touch as well, we've got a group chat. We all meet up when we can."

Shan admitted that standing out on the show wasn't always easy, but said that her accent definitely helped matters.

She said: "My accent makes me stand out because it's so broad and common, so people always remember me for that.

"I remember host Paddy McGuinness said I sounded like I was from Peaky Blinders!"

 

Shan, who has never had a boyfriend, said she thought the show might be a good way to find her 'one person' and jumped at the chance after she was approached by show bosses on Instagram .

She said: "For the longest time, I wanted to live life to its fullest and have all kinds of experiences, so I set myself some goals.

"I've never been in a relationship, not to sound arrogant but it is out of choice - because nowadays relationships are taken for granted.

"I don't think there's much respect with boys and girls nowadays. I'm just looking for that one person that stands out for me, I don't want to invest my time in people for it to be a waste."

Shan added that viewers should expect 'a bit of a change' in this series, with the girls being "a bit more brutal".

"I'm a bit bonkers to be honest, I just say whatever is on my mind.

"But you have to be careful when you're on TV, because I've got a bit of a foul mouth!"

*Take Me Out, featuring Shan, airs on Saturday at 7.10pm on ITV.
