Virgil van Dijk explains why Liverpool manager Jurgen Klopp's criticism makes him better

Virgil van Dijk recently took a break from concentrating on Liverpool's Premier League crunch clash with Man City to have dinner with Dutch monarch King Willem-Alexander

16 Nov 2019, 22:30 by Irishmirror.ie

Liverpool fans claim Virgil van Dijk is already football ­royalty – but the Dutch defender felt like the real deal when he was invited to dinner by the king of the Netherlands.

Van Dijk, the £75million centre-back who is the bookmakers’ ­favourite to win the Ballon d’Or on December 2, was flown to ­Amsterdam at the request of King Willem-Alexander and Queen Maxima earlier this month.

The annual VIP event celebrates ‘outstanding’ Dutch people, and was attended by elite scientists, musicians, business people, ­authors – and one very special footballer.

It says much about Van Dijk’s growing profile that he held court for much of the evening after his king asked for the low-down on Liverpool ’s Champions League coronation and their march to the top of the Premier League.

Van Dijk said: “I sat next to the King for the most part, until after the main course.

“We talked about a variety of subjects – but definitely about football!

“As soon as I received the ­invitation I asked Liverpool if I could attend the event.

“It came at a very awkward ­moment as it was in between our Champions League game against Genk and our match against ­ Manchester City !

“Fortunately Pepijn Lijnders [Liverpool’s assistant manager] put a good word in for me.

“As a Dutchman, Pep knew that this was something huge. It was a really special experience.”

Van Dijk boarded a private jet to fly him back to Merseyside as soon as the event was finished.

And he hinted that the first thing Kop boss Jurgen Klopp did on his return to Melwood was to keep him humble.

Van Dijk said: “I have a very similar connection with Jurgen Klopp at Liverpool as I had earlier with Ronald Koeman at Southampton and I still have with Koeman with the national team.

“Like Koeman, Klopp knows ­exactly how to get the best out of me – by being critical. When the media are hyping me and being very positive, he will downplay the praise all that – often with a wink.

“When I was voted UEFA Player of the Year recently and had to go to the awards ceremony, Jurgen told all the lads at Liverpool that I was picking up the trophy on ­behalf of the entire team.

“All the boys in the squad know I can take that sort of stuff very well.

“I know what he means. At the same time, the boss gives me a lot of respect and responsibility.”

Van Dijk’s royal visit had no ­effect on his performance as the Reds beat City 3-1 to go eight points clear at the top of the ­Premier League.

But the Dutchman insists that all title talk has been banned from the Anfield dressing room.

He said: “Of course I can sense how much winning the title is ­spoken about.

“For our fans, winning the championship has been way too long, so they place the Premier League title now above everything.

“No competition is more ­important than this one, but we need to keep the fever outside.

“Klopp tells us every week, ‘Focus on the next match’.

“We are doing alright, but it is only November.

“The hardest months of football are still waiting.”
