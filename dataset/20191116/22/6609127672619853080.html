GREEN-SPIRITED

Scottish Greens blast STV for excluding them from leaders’ election debate with Labour, SNP, Tory and Lib Dem chiefs

16 Nov 2019, 22:07 by Tom Eden

SCOTTISH Greens today slammed a decision by STV to exclude the party from its leaders’ election debate.

The crucial showdown will be aired on December 3 with input from Labour, SNP, Conservative and Lib Dem chiefs.

But the party, led by Patrick Harvie, was left out of the hour-long grilling nine days before the vote — prompting a furious claim it’s handing the big parties an advantage based on their deeper coffers.

A spokesman said last: “We’re extremely disappointed that STV has chosen to include just the four establishment parties in its General Election leaders debate.

“We are in the midst of a climate emergency and while our candidates demand climate action, the other parties are part of a cosy political consensus on this key issue, all agreeing with one another.

“Our record as Scotland’s most influential opposition party includes transforming Scotland’s income tax system. We led the way on children’s rights by providing them with equal protection from assault.

“And we scrapped unnecessary invasive social security assessments. This shows the impact Scottish Greens politicians have.”

But the spokesman continued: “Unlike the other parties we don’t have unlimited resources and millionaire donors.

“That’s why our local branches need to decide carefully how to deploy resources and unfortunately that means we can’t stand everywhere.

“It’s deeply depressing that STV has allowed an unfair voting system and financial advantage, rather than actual political influence, to drive its decision.”

The Greens are the only political party with seats in the Scottish Parliament to be excluded from the hour-long debate.

The Brexit Party and Ukip, who are standing in 15 and seven Scottish seats respectively, are also excluded.

STV says it is also planning a series of interviews with party leaders including those from the Brexit Party and Scottish Greens, due to be aired at an unspecified date. BBC Scotland chiefs have already announced a televised debate involving leaders from the SNP, Conservatives, Labour and Lib Dems to be shown on December 10.

And UK-wide political broadcasts are also planned, starting on Tuesday with a head-to-head debate between Tory boss Boris Johnson and Labour leader Jeremy Corbyn being aired on ITV, followed by a similar format on BBC One on December 6.

The SNP and Lib Dems — the third and fourth biggest Westminster parties in terms of seats — threatened legal action against ITV for their exclusion from the national debates.

Nicola Sturgeon said it was “fundamentally unfair” not to include her party, the third-largest in the UK.

STV confirmed the format of its broadcast will see the party leaders debate each other directly, and face questions from host Colin Mackay, the channel’s political editor.
