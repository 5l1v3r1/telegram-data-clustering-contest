Scott Frost Reveals What Needs To Change For Nebraska Moving Forward

16 Nov 2019, 22:07 by Andrew Holleran

Scott Frost and the Nebraska football program suffered another tough loss today. The Huskers were beaten by Wisconsin in Lincoln, dropping to 4-6 on the season.

It was an eventful day for Frost, who received a two-year contract extension prior to kickoff. He’s now under contract through the 2026 season.

Year 2 was supposed to feature a big step up for the Huskers, but that hasn’t happened. Adrian Martinez is far from the Heisman Trophy contender some projected him to be, the defense gives up an insane amount of big plays and mistakes are made by just about everyone.

Nebraska remains committed to Frost, though, and that is probably the smart move. If anyone is going to turn this program around, it’ll probably be him.

Frost was direct following the loss to Wisconsin. He knows what needs to change – get better players.

“We’ve got to keep bringing more talent into this program so that we are going into gun fights with fully loaded guns, and we’ve got some really good bullets on this team. We have some really good pieces, some really good weapons, but we need more,” Frost said, per Parker Gabriel.
