Unai Emery has month to save job - but Arsenal chiefs don't think sacking would end crisis

Arsenal haven't won a Premier League match since October 6 and are currently on a five game winless streak, which has piled the pressure on manager Unai Emery

16 Nov 2019, 22:30 by Simon Mullock, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/simon-mullock/

Unai Emery has a month to save his job at Arsenal – but there is a belief inside the club that sacking the Spaniard will still not solve the crisis at the ­Emirates.

Emery was given a vote of ­confidence by the Gunners’ head of football Raul Sanllehi in the ­aftermath of a 2-0 defeat at Leicester last week, which saw them slump to sixth in the Premier League after a run of just two wins from 10 games.

But Emery’s fate will be decided by ­the run of Premier League games, against struggling Southampton , Norwich, Brighton and West Ham – as well as the visit of Manchester City on ­December 15.

Arsenal must also negotiate ­Europa League ties against ­Eintracht Frankfurt and Standard Liege during that period.

But some staff fear that Emery now faces an impossible task to ­revive a club that has lost its heart and soul since Arsene Wenger’s ­near 22-year reign came to an end.

Wenger transformed the Gunners by building on traditional Highbury virtues with the innovative modern methods he imported from abroad.

Just 18 months after leaving the club, insiders claim the Frenchman’s legacy has been destroyed by a string of disastrous high-level ­appointments.

One source said: “The DNA that made ­Arsenal the club it was has slowly but surely drained away.

“Appointments have been made – both before and after Wenger’s departure – that either didn’t work out or aren’t working out.

“Long-serving members of staff have been shocked by what’s ­happening to the club.”

Bringing in former midfielder Edu in the summer to work as technical director alongside Sanllehi and academy manager Pep Mertesacker was supposed to herald a new beginning.

But all of Wenger’s most trusted people have now left.

And Emery has found himself struggling to stabilise the club on the pitch as Arsenal undergo their biggest upheaval since Wenger’s ­arrival in 1996.

Steve Morrow, the former ­midfielder who became a highly ­respected head of youth ­recruitment, was the highest-profile ­casualty of a clear-out rubber-stamped by Edu and Mertesacker this month as they look to revamp the academy ­scouting set-up.

The insider added: “To say it was a shock to see so many good people leave so suddenly is an ­understatement.

“Steve Morrow, in particular, is a huge loss. He is someone who is ­respected throughout football and is seen as having the potential to be a director of football.”

Chief scout Steve Rowley, the man who unearthed a succession of top talent for the Gunners over more than 30 years, was forced out of the club two years ago by then-chief ­executive Ivan Gazidis.

Gazidis himself left the Emirates last year to become chief football ­officer at AC Milan. His departure saw Sanllehi promoted into his ­current role. The former director of football at ­ Barcelona had initially ­arrived at Arsenal 12 months earlier as head of football relations.

It has not gone well for Gazidis, 55, in Italy.

Last month, he was ­accused by angry fans of “destroying” Milan when manager Marco Giampaolo was axed after a poor start to the season.

Arsenal fans have also got reasons to be unhappy with him. He was ­instrumental in appointing Emery as manager last May.

Former Arsenal midfielder Mikel Arteta was in pole position to succeed Wenger, after furthering his coaching expertise under Pep Guardiola at Manchester City.

But, with City bracing themselves for an official approach, Gazidis turned to Emery.

Gazidis also got his way when Andries Jonker ­arrived as academy ­director in 2014.

The Dutchman was an ­unmitigated failure and now works back in his homeland for second division club Telstar after a short
ill-fated spell in charge of Wolfsburg. ­German Sven Mislintat lasted just 14 months after being made head of scouting in November 2017.

He was part of a three-man panel, including Gazidis and ­Sanllehi, which voted to bring in Emery.

Gazidis’s belief that the club should invest in information and data specialists also led to the appointment of Hendrik ­Almstadt as head of technical data.

The German shared Gazidis’s belief that top players could be identified by compiling statistical databases.

But Wenger was ­unconvinced by Almstadt’s methods and he left after five unsuccessful years before also failing to make an impression at Aston Villa.

When Huss Fahmy was brought in to handle player contracts in 2017, it prompted the departure of transfer fixer Dick Law, another of Wenger’s most trusted confidants.

Emery is in the firing line, but, unless Arsenal’s decline is ­reversed, pressure will mount on Sanllehi and those he surrounded himself with after becoming the main man at the club.
