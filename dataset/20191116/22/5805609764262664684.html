How to Schedule Startup and Shutdown on MAC

16 Nov 2019, 22:15 by Karanpreet Singh

Well, if we look around, we will find that Windows and Mac are the two most used desktop operating system. Although macOS is not as popular as Windows, still it has some of its own unique features which makes it different from its competitor.

Let’s admit, we are now highly dependent on our personal computers. In fact, most of our works are not done online. In our busy schedule, we sometimes forget to shut down our computers. Well, Macs are power efficient, but it’s still a waste of electricity to have a MAc operating 24 hours a day.

So, to deal with power issues, Mac has an ‘Energy Saver’ feature. It’s basically a scheduling feature of which allows users to set a time for their Mac to automatically start up, wake, sleep, restart or shutdown.

How to Schedule Startup and Shutdown on MAC

So, in this article, we have decided to share a working method that could help you to schedule startup and shutdown time on Mac computer 2019. Setting a schedule for startup and shutdown on Mac is easy, you just need to follow some of the simple steps given below. So, let’s check out how to schedule a startup and shutdown on Mac 2019.

Step 1. First of all, hold down the command key and press the space bar. This will launch the Spotlight search on your device.

Step 2. Just type in the “Energy Saver” in the Spotlight panel and then select the energy saver system preference. This will open the Energy Saver System on your device. You can also open it on your device through the Menu, select the System Preferences from the options and then click on the Energy Saver.

Step 3. From the Energy Saver panel look for the option “Schedule” that could be placed as a button either, just click on it. On the next screen choose the Startup or Wake option and hence you will be recalled with some set of settings that you can customize and insert by yourself.

Step 4. Just set up the weekdays, time for wake-up and shut-down, etc through the various settings and then after that you can save all those settings by clicking the “OK” button.

After the above process, you can see that your Mac will automatically shut down, start-up at the time and weekdays that you have set up before. You can easily disable this functionality from the same settings only. Go to the settings and then from the options remove the start-up and shut-down schedules!

So, this is all about how to schedule startup and shutdown on Mac. Scheduling startup and shutdown on Mac is easy and you can do that by following the steps given in this article.
