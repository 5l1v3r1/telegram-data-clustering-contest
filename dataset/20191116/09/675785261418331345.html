Devastated parents moved by touching shrine set up by friends of dead teenage son

Morgan Symcox's friends have transformed a bench at Wolstanton Marsh into a shrine to the teenager whose cause of death remains a mystery

16 Nov 2019, 09:27 by Richard Ault

The parents of an 18-year-old who suddenly died have said how moved they are by the actions of his friends.

Relatives of Morgan Symcox have been "overwhelmed" by the support they have received from the teenager’s friends since he ‘fell asleep’ earlier this month.

They have transformed a bench into a stunning shrine in his memory, reports Stoke-on-Trent Live .

It has been covered with dozens of bouquets of flowers, balloons, photographs and messages to Morgan who used to attend Alpha School, in Hanley.

His friends have also been keeping a vigil at the site.

Now the friends want to raise money for a plaque and a second bench – which Morgan always said he wanted so friends could sit opposite each other and chat.

 

Dad Damon Symcox, 42, said: "We go to the bench because that’s where he was happiest. He loved his mates.

"Sometimes I’d say, ‘can’t you stay in tonight’. I didn’t understand why he always wanted to go to the bench, but I understand it now.

"You realise that his mates care about him as much as we do.

"I have been to the bench every day, it feels closer to him.

 

"Whenever you go there’s always someone there, it’s amazing.

"When you read that someone has passed away in the paper, everyone says that they were the heart and soul, but Morgan really was.

"If you met Morgan for five minutes, then six-months later you would still remember him.

"You don’t realise how popular someone is until you lose them."

Mum Liz, 42, said: "Morgan was a very popular lad,

"We are living in hell, but his friends lost him too and they have made that lovely bench.

"It has been their meeting point since they were about 13.

"They must have put about 40 or 50 bouquets of flowers, and 60 or 70 photographs.

"Morgan loved going out and socialising, everything was about his friends.

 

"His friends have done a memory box which they are going to bury. They want to pay for a plaque on the bench.

"When we go to the bench and see his friends and cry and give them a hug they hug you back.

"They will never know how much it means to us."

Morgan was found pronounced dead in the bedroom of the family's May Bank home on Saturday, November 2.

The exact cause of his death is still not known.

Liz added: "He just fell asleep.

"Morgan was about to start an apprenticeship as a plumber.

"We are a really close family. Earlier this year we went on holiday for five days in Poland, then we went to Turkey for two weeks."

 

Morgan had two sisters - Keely, 23, and three-year-old Annie - who he doted on.

Keely said: "He loved documentaries and history. He knew everything worth knowing. He loved tanning and sun beds, he loved his food and designer clothes.

"He loved life, that’s why it’s so cruel and sad.

"He was my best friend. He was lovable and funny, he would  make anyone laugh, and he was very loyal.

"All his friends say they are living in Morgan’s world. Everything they do now, they say it’s for Morgan’s world. That’s their way of coping.

"When we go to the bench and see his friends there’s no awkwardness, it’s like we are all together."

Morgan’s girlfriend, Lily Mason, 17, of Chesterton, said: "He would have done anything for those close to him.

"He faced every single day with a smile on his face. He was so intelligent and funny.

"I will never meet a more generous and lovely person. He was so loved by everyone around him.

"He was one in a million."

Friend Dionne Dale, 18, of May Bank, said: “Anyone who knew Morg will know that he was the most positive, funny, wildest person to ever walk this Earth.

"He never let anything faze him and always found a bright side to every situation."
