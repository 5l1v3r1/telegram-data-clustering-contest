UBC urges exchange students in Hong Kong to return to B.C. due to violent protests

16 Nov 2019, 09:41 by Taran Parmar

Summary

Exchange students in Hong Kong are being urged to come home by the University of British Columbia

32 students at four universities in Hong Kong have been contacted by UBC

UBC has reached out to partner schools to confirm measures are in place to ensure the safety of exchange students

VANCOUVER (NEWS 1130) – The University of British Columbia is urging 32 exchange students to leave Hong Kong, and return to B.C.

Joesphine Chiu-Duke is a professor with the Department of Asian Studies at UBC and says safety is top of mind for the university, as it recommends students return home immediately.

“The obvious reason is that we don’t feel that the students staying there are safe because the violence being escalated by the police and the students in Hong Kong, trying to resist and fight back,” she says.

“The campus in the Chinese University of Hong Kong has become a small battleground between protesters and police.”

The students overseas are attending four universities in Hong Kong and as of Friday, they were all urged to leave as violence between police and protesters continues.

UBC will be working with those who have decided to return home and will help make travel arrangements.
