Silvertips blow late lead, fall to Ice in overtime

Everett rallies from a two-goal deficit, then sees a two-goal lead disappear in the 5-4 loss.

16 Nov 2019, 09:30 by Joshua Horton

EVERETT — The Everett Silvertips climbed out of an early two-goal deficit, only to see their own two-goal lead dissipate in the third period in a 5-4 overtime loss to the Winnipeg Ice on Saturday at Angel of the Winds Arena.

Winnipeg’s Chase Hartje batted in a rebound for the game-winning goal just 39 seconds into overtime.

The loss snapped a four-game win streak for the Silvertips (13-4-1), who still lead the U.S. Division with 27 points.

The Ice cut Everett’s lead to 4-3 with Jackson Leppard’s goal at the 7:58 mark in the third period. Nino Kinder snapped a shot past Silvertips goaltender Keegan Karki with just 40.6 seconds remaining in regulation to tie the game.

Silvertips linemates Michal Gut, Jackson Berezowski and Brendan Lee each recorded a goal and an assist in the loss.

Next up

The Silvertips host the Spokane Chiefs at 4:05 p.m. on Sunday.
