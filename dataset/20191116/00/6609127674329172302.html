Scottish Tories to lose seats at general election, predicts polling expert Sir John Curtice

16 Nov 2019, 00:22 by Andrew Nicoll

SIR John Curtice believes the Tories are on course to lose some of their 13 Scots seats on December 12.

The polling guru’s forecast came as the SNP won a trio of council by-elections yesterday

The Nats took Dunfermline Central from the Conservatives and held seats in Rosyth and Inverness Central.

Curtice believes the SNP could benefit further as support for Labour crumbles north of the border — reflected in their poor showings in the three local ballots.

Speaking after the first full week of General Election campaigning, Sir John said: “A slight question mark over the Conservatives, recent polls still had them down about seven points in Scotland.

“But given they have been making some ground across the UK and there’s something of a Brexit Party vote to squeeze in Scotland, it would be surprising if they were not at risk of losing some of their seats.

"The SNP look to be around 40 per cent, about three points up on where they were in 2017, so they are at least holding on, probably going up.”

The Strathclyde University professor also believes Jeremy Corbyn’s perceived muddle over IndyRef2 policy is hurting support for Labour here.

He said: “Unsurprisingly, given the state of the party’s polling position in the UK as a whole, they are well down in Scotland.

“They are probably heading for aro­und 20 per cent of the vote, therefore down seven, eight or nine per cent.”

Although the Nats won both Fife by-elections, sources within the party have admitted they fear a Lib Dem surge in the area.

Jo Swinson's party upped their support by over 13 per cent in Dunfermline.

SNP's Stephen Gethins won North East Fife seat from the Lib Dems by just TWO votes in 2017 - and some are worried it might slip from their grasp again.
