2019 Holiday Gift Guide: Travel Journals

16 Nov 2019 by Michele Herrmann

Even in this digital age, it’s still nice to take a pen to paper and write down experiences rather than capturing every moment on a smartphone. With the holiday gift giving season approaching, consider buying a travel journal for your adventure-seeking loved ones to record and reflect on their memories from the road. Here is a selection of travel journals that keep on giving as keepsakes.

This sturdy journal becomes your literary travel sidekick, in keeping track of your itinerary, making lists, sketching maps and, of course, writing down impressions and discoveries. Having a lay-flat binding and a storage pocket, the journal also includes helpful advice relating to time zones, weights and measures and seasonal climate charts along with a brief guide to finding hidden wonders in a dozen of the world’s most interesting cities.

For those seeking to check off visiting every state in the U.S., this hand-screen printed journal is to help keep track of your progress. It has questions asking just about everything relating to your trip, from the date of your arrival to your deep thoughts about your experiences. However, they can be taken in any direction you please.

Designed as both a road trip planner and entry taker, this uber-luxury journal is described as having lists of hidden locations to discover. Its curated pages and quotes will get your sense of wanderlust started easily.

This tan leather bound journal from The Black Canvas, and sold through Fancy, entices notetakers with an engraved world map illustration on its cover. Its antique finish snap button on the cover flap closes up your daily entries and its 80 pages of white handmade paper makes an account of your adventures solid.

Created by Kate Pocrass, a San Francisco Bay Area artist and illustrator, this colorfully illustrated travel journal encourages its owners to explore everyday details they encounter on their journey. It contains a good amount of space for inserting addresses, itineraries, reviews and tips from locals, a reference section with time zones, measurements and other useful details and a back pocket for collecting ticket stubs or other small papers.

Even the younger set might be curious about having adventures, so get them started with some assistance from KeeKee, a globe-trotting calico kitty with a picture book series. A 28-page diary that equals out to seven days of adventures, this child-friendly journal enables young scribers to draft a daily account of what they saw and did.

This Moleskine notebook is a handy dandy travel partner, in that it’s packed with pertinent information for making the most out of your journey. It has detachable packing and to-do lists, along with other resources for mapping out each day in detail before heading out the door.

This journal is made to spark creativity by inspiring its owner to engage with their surroundings through not only the written word but also drawing. With a lightweight, durable and flexi-bound cover, each page spread features a different activity, from sketching what you see outside your window, to marking down what’s on your travel bucket list. And its elastic strap protects its pages from being damaged while being on the move.  

This portable trip planner and diary doubles as a before and during trip tool. Its features not only encompass everything from A to Z – a packing list, metric conversions chart, foreign phrases guide and so forth - but also has diary pages for adding in entries while traveling. Its inside back cover pocket can store mementos, tickets, maps, receipts and other papers worth holding onto.

As a travel journal or a daily diary, this linen hardcover book becomes your very own chronicle. Each double page is lined so you can write all about your happenings – and then re-read them often.

This journal is a source of advice for adventurous females, with lined pages for journaling and sketching, and quite useful information on topics extending to personal safety, avoiding jet lag and cultural etiquette. An inside pocket holds postcards, receipts, mementos and other documents for safekeeping.
