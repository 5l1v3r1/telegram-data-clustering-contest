Here’s How Much Money Optometrists Make In Every State

16 Nov 2019 by Andrew DePietro

The career of an optometrist is one that’s gaining some serious ground in the United States lately. Employment numbers have been increasing and, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics’ Occupational Outlook Handbook, employment of optometrists is projected to increase faster than average, with approximately 4,000 optometrist jobs being added from 2018 to 2028, an increase of roughly 10%. Using occupational data from the Bureau of Labor Statistics, we've analyzed and compiled a round-up of the average optometrist salary by state in the U.S. Read on for a full breakdown of where optometrists make the most money, and where they're making the least.

10 States Where Optometrists Earn the Most Money

The national average annual wage of an optometrist is $119,980, according to the BLS, well above the e average annual salary for all occupations, $51,960. The average optometrist salary can vary significantly depending on the state. In the best-paying state for optometrists, the average salary is $55,110 more than the national average.

Below is a list of the top-10 highest-paying states for optometrists.

  1. North Dakota average optometrist salary: $175,090
  2. Vermont average optometrist salary: $170,550
  3. Alaska average optometrist salary: $165,640
  4. South Carolina average optometrist salary: $142,290
  5. Massachusetts average optometrist salary: $141,890
  6. Connecticut average optometrist salary: $138,140
  7. Maine average optometrist salary: $136,510
  8. North Carolina average optometrist salary: $136,260
  9. Wyoming average optometrist salary: $135,220
  10. Minnesota average optometrist salary: $134,270

It’s interesting to note that several of best-paying states also have a low supply of optometrists: No. 3 Alaska has the fewest, with only 70 optometrists; No. 9 Wyoming has 80; No. 2 Vermont has 90; No. 1 North Dakota has 160; and No. 7 Maine has only 170. This low supply combined with demand for such a fundamental medical service helps boost optometrist salaries in those states.

10 States Where Optometrists Earn the Least Money

The bottom-10 states where optometrists make the least money are a geographically mixed, though several of the two worst-paying states can be found in the U.S. West region, as designated by the Census Bureau. In the worst-paying state for optometrists, Utah, the average salary is nearly 38% less than the U.S. average salary. Here’s a look at the 10 worst states for optometrist’s salaries:

  1. Utah average optometrist salary: $74,490
  2. New Mexico average optometrist salary: $86,870
  3. Arkansas average optometrist salary: $95,500
  4. Idaho average optometrist salary: $96,070
  5. South Dakota average optometrist salary: $96,540
  6. Oregon average optometrist salary: $99,560
  7. Florida average optometrist salary: $102,060
  8. Nebraska average optometrist salary: $104,010
  9. Rhode Island average optometrist salary: $104,320
  10. Louisiana average optometrist salary: $104,360

What’s worse about these states is that, with the exception of two, all of them are seeing their optometrist salaries fall over the years. In 2013, the average annual wage for an optometrist in Utah was $118,050. By 2018, it had plummeted by 36.9% down to $74,490. New Mexico saw a drop of 33.8% over the last five years, from $131,250 down to $86,870. In South Dakota, the decline was even worse, with the average optometrist salary plummeting by 37.7%, from $154,890 in 2013 down to $96,540 in 2018.
