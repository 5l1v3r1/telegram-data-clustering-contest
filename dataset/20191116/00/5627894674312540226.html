Weather changes are blowin' in the wind

16 Nov 2019

SOUTHLAND - (INT) - Novembers status quo is about to get a weather readjustment.

Two events in the next 5-days could signal a defining change from autumn to winter.

Initially, the first critical fire weather conditions of the month are likely this weekend.

Santa Ana winds are predicted Saturday night through Sunday afternoon. Winds gusting to 35 miles an hour will combine with humidity of 10%-15% with temperatures rising 15 degrees above normal.

Second, autumn weather begins a dramatic cool down starting Tuesday as a Pacific storm system approaches from the northwest, the first of the season. By Wednesday and Thursday, the National Weather Service is predicting rain, mountain snow (starting at 8,000 ft and lowering to 6,500 ft), mountain fog, and gusty winds in the mountains and deserts of Southern California.
