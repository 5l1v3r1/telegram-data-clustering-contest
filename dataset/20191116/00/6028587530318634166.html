Stefanos Tsitsipas Beats Roger Federer To Advance To Championship Of Nitto ATP Finals

16 Nov 2019 by Adam Zagoria

Stefanos Tsitsipas is now one win away from capturing the Nitto ATP Finals in his first appearance in the event.The 21-year-old Greek sensation advanced to Sunday’s championship match when he knocked off six-time champion Roger Federer, 6-3, 6-4, in the first semifinal on Saturday.

After closing the match with an ace, Tsitsipas will face the winner between Dominic Thiem and defending champion Alexander Zverev in Sunday’s final. Thiem leads Tsitsipas 4-2, while Tsitsipas leads Zverev 4-1.

The winner will take home $1,354,000, with the runner-up landing $657,000.

“I’m really proud of myself because I went through a lot of difficulties in today’s match,” Tsitsipas said in his on-court interview. “It’s something that I think I deserve because I was in the right state of mind and did things almost up to perfection.”

As for the final, he said, “Both of them play great tennis, I’ve played both of them and I need to be really prepared for tomorrow because it’s going to be one of the most important finals of my career so far. I need to be mentally there from the very beginning and I honestly have no preference if it’s going to be Zverev or Thiem. I hope they play a long, tiring semifinal.”

The “Big Three” of Federer, Rafael Nadal and Novak Djokovic are now all out of the ATP Finals. Nadal and Djokovic did not qualify for the semifinals.

Tsitsipas improved to 2-2 against Federer, including a massive win in the Australian Open in January en route to a semifinal appearance there.

After taking the first set, Tsitsipas broke Federer early in the second. Federer broke back to tie it at 2-all, but quickly surrendered the break right back to go down 3-2. Federer did not serve nearly as well against the Greek as he did in his last match against Djokovic on Friday.

He was also 1-for-12 on break points after going 0-for-12 in their Australian Open match.

“I was just trying to survive each one of them,” Tsitsipas said. “I had a similar situation with him at the Australian Open this year when I did save also a lot of break points. But I wasn’t really thinking much. I was sticking to my game plan, just trying to be as aggressive as possible, trying not to give him time and space to execute his own game plan.”

While the “Big Three” have combined to win 55 total majors and 12 straight, the younger generation now appears poised for a breakthrough.

Tsitsipas is 21, Zverev 22, Daniil Medvedev 23 and Dominic Thiem 26.

Thiem has lost in back-to-back French Open finals to Nadal, while Medvedev lost an epic five-setter to Nadal in the 2019 U.S. Open.

With 2020 around the corner, it’s possible one of the younger guys will break through and win his first major – and the first major from that group.

As for the “Big Three,” they will head into 2020 trying to augment their major totals. Federer has a slim 20-19 lead on Nadal, who could potentially tie and pass the Swiss next year, while Djokovic is lurking close behind at 16.
