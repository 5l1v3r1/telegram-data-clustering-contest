HOLD UP

Scots criminal record staff snowed under with perv checks for wannabee Santas and elves

16 Nov 2019, 21:52 by Chris Taylor

CRIMINAL record checks staff are snowed under with a backlog in the run-up to Christmas — due to the high number of wannabe Santas and elves.

Firms are having to wait for weeks to start non-seasonal staff because Disclosure Scotland is so swamped with perv checks on people wanting to work with kids.

Employees at the Glasgow-based vetting agency are currently taking longer than two weeks to clear hundreds of applicants for jobs.

A source whose business has been hit by the delay said: “We’ve been waiting for ages to get back checks on people that are waiting to start work with our company.

“Disclosure Scotland is normally able to turn these things around really efficiently so we phoned them to find out what the issue was.

“We were stunned when we were told it was due to the massive number of people who have applied to be Santa and his helpers this year.”

A Father Christmas can earn £8-£14 an hour working long days in malls and stores.

Disclosure Scotland last night said it is processing 94.3 per cent of basic background applications within 14 days.

Chiefs say the average processing time is 6.4 days.

A spokeswoman said: “This time of year is traditionally busy due to the number of seasonal workers being taken on.

"We are unable to provide figures for those seeking to play the role of Santa as there is no requirement for a job title on basic applications.”

We told in October how authorities were accused of risking kids’ safety by placing no obligation on footie coaches to pass vetting checks before taking one-to-one sessions.
