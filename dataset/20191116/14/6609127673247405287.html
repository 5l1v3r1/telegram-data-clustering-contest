'CONSISTENT ABUSE'

Ross County and ex-Rangers star Richard Foster lifts the lid on relationship with fans and says he’s ‘abused everywhere’

16 Nov 2019, 14:01 by Gary Taylor

EX-RANGERS star Richard Foster has revealed he gets "abused everywhere" as he reflected on his career.

Foster, who now plays for Ross County, opened up about his relationship with fans and told how he gets booed and jeered just about wherever he plays.

Speaking to The Times, the 34-year-old said: "I get it from Aberdeen fans for going to Rangers.

“I get it from Rangers fans for being crap in my second spell there. I get it from Celtic fans because I played for Rangers. I get it from the Hamilton fans, from Motherwell fans; I’ve had it from the Dundee fans.

"I mean, do I have one of those faces that everyone loves to hate?”

Foster is known to have a loaded relationship with Dons fans - despite playing over 270 games for the team over an 11-year period.

He said: "To this day Aberdeen fans boo me for the full 90 minutes, whether I’m visiting Pittodrie, or at any other ground.

"The first time I went back to Pittodrie after leaving Aberdeen I couldn’t believe the level of consistent abuse they gave me. I mean, I got the ball in the 89th minute and they were still booing me. I thought: ‘surely you guys have given up by now?’ You just think, ‘Jesus Christ, guys, get over it.’"

It comes after the footballer recently returned to Ross County - following a flare-up with St Johnstone gaffer Tommy Wright.

We previously reported how Foster had left Saints after the training-ground bust-up in the summer - sparking a heated exchange between Wright and Foster's wife pop star Amy Macdonald.

Talking about the incident, the footballer revealed that maybe the "disagreement" went a "step too far".

He said: "I had a training ground disagreement with Tommy that you’ll see many times at clubs. But maybe we all took it a step too far; it sort of went too far to be brought back.

"It was heated but not abusive. When I went to Ross County and we played St Johnstone a couple of weeks after I’d left, I made a point of shaking Tommy’s hand, and he texted me later saying ‘all the best’.

“It was unfortunate the way it ended at St Johnstone, but I leapt at the chance to go back to County. I get on well with everyone there.”

We previously told how Amy had a cheeky dig at St Johnstone fans who called her a "w***e" following her hubby's controversial exit from the Saints during the summer.

It followed her hit back at St Johnstone boss Tommy Wright over his "casual misogyny" in his "Mrs Foster" remark.

The war of words between the pair kicked off after Macdonald branded the Northern Irishman "hot-headed" during an appearance on Radio Scotland's Off The Ball. Wright later claimed her version of events were miles away from the truth.
