The best books of the week

16 Nov 2019, 14:26 by Mackenzie Dawson

Crusaders: The Epic History of the Wars for the Holy Lands
Dan Jones (nonfiction, Viking)
Bestselling author Dan Jones is a bit of a rock star in the historical-nonfiction space, and for his latest title he turns to 1099, when Christian armies seized Jerusalem, kicking off a period of intense conflict between Christians and Muslims.

The Zookeepers’ War: An Incredible True Story from the Cold War
J.W. Mohnhaupt (nonfiction, Simon & Schuster)
In a divided Cold War Berlin, one of the stranger proxy wars of the era was fought in the city’s two zoos — when the zookeepers on each side began an animal arms race.

The Problem with Everything: My Journey Through the New Culture Wars
Meghan Daum (Essays, Gallery Books)
Meghan Daum is over the 24/7 outrage culture, and the result is this book of thoughtful, provocative essays on everything from the Trump presidency to identity politics to generational differences and beyond.

The Fall of Richard Nixon: A Reporter Remembers Watergate
Tom Brokaw (nonfiction, Random House)
Tom Brokaw was a young NBC News White House correspondent in August 1974 and gives a thoughtful overview of a confusing time, introducing readers to the various players.

Wild Life: Dispatches from a Childhood of Baboons and Button-Downs
Keena Roberts (memoir, Grand Central Publishing)
A sort of real-life “Mean Girls,” Keena Roberts split her adolescence between Botswana and an elite Philadelphia private school, moving between the two wildly different locations every six months with her parents, prominent primatologists.

Under Occupation
Alan Furst (fiction, Random House)
From the master spy novelist comes a thriller about French Resistance members working to stop Hitler.
