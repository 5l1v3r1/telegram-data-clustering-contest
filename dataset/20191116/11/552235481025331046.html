Cops release images of men in connection with Remembrance Day attack on East Kilbride war veteran

Former Royal Marine Christopher Smith said he ‘feared for his life’ during the vicious attack after he paid tribute to fallen comrades.

16 Nov 2019, 11:50 by Fraser N Wilson, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/fraser-n-wilson/

Police in East Kilbride have released CCTV images of two men they want to question over the assault of a veteran on Remembrance Sunday.

The East Kilbride News told you earlier this week how Christopher Smith required hospital treatment after he was beaten up by two men around 11pm last weekend.

Now police have stepped up their search for those responsible.

Chief Inspector Lex Baillie told the News: “This has been a serious act of violence that has left the victim with serious injuries.

“As part of our ongoing inquiries we’re eager to identify and trace the men pictured and would ask anyone who can help us to get in touch as soon as possible.”

 

The 61-year-old victim of the assault told the News how how he ‘feared for his life’ during the vicious attack.

And, shockingly, Christopher, from the Calderwood area, is adamant that one of his attackers is a fellow veteran spotting medals on one of the assailants.

Christopher, who served with the Royal Marines during the height of the troubles in Northern Ireland, has been left with a bleed on the brain, cracked ribs and bruised kidneys after the attack on Sunday just after 11pm on Stuart Street in the Village.

The veteran is still recovering from a major heart attack earlier this year.

He had spent the day paying his respects to the fallen and friends who didn’t make it home and was heading home after a couple of drinks when two men in their 30s approached him out of the blue.

“I have no idea who they were, I have never met them in my life before,” he told the News.

“One of them said something but he was so out of it and I couldn’t understand him.  I just said to them to go away and leave me alone. I didn’t want any trouble.

“The guy who was wearing an Afghanistan medal said something about his friend dying over there, I said I had lost friends everywhere and the next thing I know I am being battered and face down on the ground.

“I just don’t understand why this happened to me, I didn’t do anyhing. It’s horrific, especially as was of them was another veteran.”

Police say the incident happened around 11.10pm on Sunday, November 10 on Stuart Street in the Village.

The first man is described as white, aged 30-35, 5ft10in with shaved head and a Scottish accent. He is shown in the image wearing dark clothing.

The second man is described as white, of large build, 5ft8in with brown hair. He is shown in the image wearing a grey tracksuit.

Anyone with any information is asked to call Police Scotland on 101, Information can be left anonymously with Crimestoppers by calling 0800 555 111.
