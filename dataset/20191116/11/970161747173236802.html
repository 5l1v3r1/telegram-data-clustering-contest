Millie Bobby Brown shrugs off ‘fitted clothes’ controversy with movie night

16 Nov 2019, 11:04

Millie Bobby Brown shrugged off the controversy around her latest comments about wanting to wear fitted clothes as she spent a night watching chick flicks with her friends.

The Stranger Things star, 15, made headlines after she told Harper’s Bazaar that she wanted to wear more fitted clothes to embrace her ‘womanhood’.

She said: ‘I would really enjoy wearing more fitted clothes now. I feel like I’m becoming someone and being a woman so I just want to show that.

‘I think about the girls who are going to look at the picture – those young girls that are going to look at me and if they think, “Wow that’s what I should dress up as”.’

She added: ‘They are my age, so those are the ones I think of. I want to make sure that I’m influencing being your age and being who you are in that time of your life.

‘It’s always difficult to dress for a red carpet event because a lot of people have opinions and unfortunately you say you won’t listen to it, but you actually kind of have to.

‘For me, I sit there and think, I’m not going to listen to what they have to say, journalists or whoever wants to write badly about my inappropriate outfit.’

People had criticised the young actress for dressing ‘older’ than her age, and shared their opinions of her choice of red carpet outfits.

Millie didn’t let the controversy over what she should be wearing get to her as she enjoyed a chilled night in with her friends and watched classic rom-com Bridget Jones’s Diary.

She shared a video of her friends crying as Renee Zellweger and Colin Firth embraced on camera as the girls all relaxed on the sofa together.
