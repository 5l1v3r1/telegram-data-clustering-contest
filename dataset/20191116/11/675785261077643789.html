Thug tried to throw pregnant girlfriend out of window after man 'liked' her Facebook photo

Ashley Brazier, 31, of Whitstable, Kent, tried to chuck his partner out the window after throwing away her recently deceased father's ashes

16 Nov 2019, 11:16 by Gregory Kirby, Sean Axtell

A violent bully tried to hurl his pregnant girlfriend out of a first-floor window after he noticed a man “liked” her Facebook photo.

Ashley Brazier picked up his 7st 2lbs partner and tried to chuck her out of the window, after throwing away her recently deceased father's ashes.

The victim only managed to prevent the fall by extending her body across the window frame in the second of two vicious rages.

Brazier, 31, of Whitstable, Kent, struck her in the face with her phone after accusing her of having an affair.

Prosecutor Amy Nicholson said Brazier’s anger intensified after his attempt to hurl the victim out of the window failed.

He then pinned her to the wall by the throat and lifted her off the floor to set up his next attack before a “petrifying” game of cat-and-mouse ensued.

 

Brazier repeatedly spat in his victim’s face as she fought to escape and slapped her with such force it caused ringing in her ear.

Brazier delivered an uppercut to the pregnant woman’s torso, dislodging a rib and puncturing a lung - which doctors later had to inflate twice.

He then stripped her and locked her out of the room, only relenting after realising the damage he had caused.

She was sent to the Queen Elizabeth The Queen Mother Hospital in Margate, Kent, where doctors had to install a drainage tap.

The victim did not know she was in the early stages of pregnancy at the time of the assault.

In a previous attack, Brazier became suspicious of one of his partner’s texts while drinking at a friends’ home in Ramsgate, Kent.

He called the woman "a slag", slapped her, pulled clumps of hair from her head and spat in her face in front of a seven-month-old baby in June.

Miss Nicholson said the victim branded Brazier “spiteful, cruel” and told how on one occasion he “threw away her father’s ashes”.

She added: “She says what happened has ruined her life.”

Brazier had 41 previous convictions including two for harassment, six for battery, 10 for criminal damage as well as multiple breaches of court orders.

Judge Rupert Lowe scalded Brazier for his campaign of violence and humiliating the woman with “signature attacks” of stripping her.

The judge said Brazier posed a “significant risk to the public” but could not impose an extended sentence because his sentencing powers were restricted.

He jailed Brazier for three years and nine months for grievous bodily harm and actual bodily harm at Canterbury Crown Court on Thursday.
