'I'll punch your c*** in' Gerry Cinnamon threatens to batter bottle yob at London gig

The Scots star was belting out a tune at Brixton Academy when a "f*****g daftie" lobbed a bottle towards him. (Warning: Contains strong language)

16 Nov 2019, 19:44 by Graeme Thomson

Scots star Gerry Cinnamon lost the rag mid song at his latest gig as he threatened to batter a mindless thug who threw a bottle towards him while he was on stage.

The Glaswegian signing sensation was belting out a tune at the 02 Brixton Academy in London when a bottle whizzed past him on Friday night.

The raging star dropped his guitar and headed towards the crowd in a bid to identify the yob who threw the missile.

After returning to the stage he then shouted "See this c*** who tried to hit me with a bottle, I will walk off here and rip you limb from limb you f******g idiot.

"Ya f*****g daftie, I'll punch your c*** in."

Gerry then started pointing at members of the crowd and asked "Who was it? Was it you?" before calming down, laughing and continuing with his set.

The Caslemilk singer is in the middle of a tour and will be in Aberdeen next Saturday at the P&J Live Arena before doing two homecoming shows at Glasgow’s SSE Hydro Arena in December.

He has also just become the first unsigned act to ever sell out Glasgow’s  Hampden Park .

On Friday the 34-year-old folk star sold 50,000 tickets in just four hours for his mega-gig at the National Stadium in July next year.
