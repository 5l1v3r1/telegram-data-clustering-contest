The reason why this dad-of-four buys hundreds of Christmas presents

'We could never imagine what it would be like for families and it just breaks your heart'

16 Nov 2019, 19:00 by Olivia Williams

A dad-of-four buys hundreds of Christmas presents every year for children as the thought of them going without "breaks his heart".

Kevin Wilcox, from Lydiate, raises the funds every November for the presents and goes out with his wife and four children to buy the gifts for different age ranges.

The 38-year-old then donates the hundreds of presents to the Salvation Army for their Christmas present appeal, who then in turn deliver the gifts on Christmas day.

Kevin told the ECHO: "I don't do it for the recognition, but I have been doing it for about seven years now.

"We have kids ourselves and we know what is like at Christmas and is expensive.

"We're lucky - we have been pretty comfortable on the financial side and we could never imagine what it would be like for families and it just breaks your heart.

"It just started off really, I don't even know how it started, I just thought of the kids waking up with nothing and couldn't bear the thought.

"We go out ourselves and the kids help pick the presents for different age ranges that we have to pick for - the kids love it, it's nice to give."

The business owner also believes the number of children living in poverty in Merseyside is on the rise and the amount of gifts Kevin needs to buy and cash he needs to raise increases each year.

He added: "Last year the total number was around 180 and some of them are big toys and each year it keeps going up.

"Each year I am devoted and sometimes I think 'do I have the time to do it', but then I know that people rely on it.

"You can hear the joy in the kids' voices when you ring up to see how the kids got on - and that's what makes me do it each year."

Kevin's wife, Claire Wilcox set up a GoFundMe page to help raise the money needed to buy the presents and sponsor Kevin doing 'Movember'.
