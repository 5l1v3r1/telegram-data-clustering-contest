Ryan Lowe praises defensive resilience of his Plymouth Argyle players after win at Forest Green Rovers

The Pilgrims beat the League Two leaders 1-0 with a goal from midfielder Antoni Sarcevic

16 Nov 2019, 19:00 by Chris Errington

Manager Ryan Lowe highlighted the way his Plymouth Argyle players put their bodies on the line in the 1-0 win away to League Two leaders Forest Green Rovers.

The Pilgrims had to withstand heavy late pressure from Forest Green, including a flurry of corners and goalmouth scrambles.

Argyle’s third league win in their last four games saw them move up to 11th place in the table, only a couple of points outside the play-off positions.

It came after they went to Bolton Wanderers in the FA Cup first round last weekend and also triumphed 1-0.

Argyle’s winning goal was a move off the training ground as Antoni Sarcevic played a short corner with Danny Mayor in the 24th minute.

Sarcevic fired a fierce deflected shot past Forest Green goalkeeper Joe Wollacott and that proved to be the difference between the two sides at the final whistle.

Lowe said: “It was a fantastic performance, especially in the second half with backs against the wall and dogged defending.

“What I’m seeing of these boys, they are putting their bodies on the line.

“We have gone a goal up with a well-worked set piece, and that’s pleasing. I would have liked us to keep the ball a little bit better in certain areas.

“But sometimes you can’t do that. You are coming to the league leaders and they are a very good side.

“They had wide free-kicks, throw-ins and corners and my players were coming out and clearing and taking whacks, and whatever else.

“That showed me the boys are determined to keep clean sheets and they want to make sure they keep the points on the board.”

Argyle lost Callum McFadzean and substitute Joe Riley to leg injuries in what was a fiercely competitive contest.

McFadzean was replaced for the start of the second half by Riley, who did not last long before he had to be taken off too.

Lowe did not want to dwell on those injuries, though, after Argyle’s hard-fought win.

The Pilgrims’ boss revealed that Sarcevic’s goal had not come by chance but it had been planned for in training.

He said: “It’s your work, it’s what we do. My players certainly do the work, and my staff certainly do the work. When it pays off like that it’s pleasing.”

The back-to-back victories over Bolton and Forest Green are an encouraging sign for Argyle but Lowe believes there is still a lot of room for improvement from his side.

Lowe said: "We can get better at everything. We are getting better at game management, we are getting better at putting bodies on the line, we are getting better at seeing games out.

"We are getting better at passing the ball and moving it through the thirds, through the lines.

"Every day is a learning day for us, and every game is a learning game for us. We are not going to get carried away.

"But, listen, I will enjoy tonight because we have come to the league leaders and beat them 1-0."

Lowe continued: "Some of them are in there (away dressing room) now bruised, battered and they are hobbling, but they have put their bodies in the line for three points.

"It will taste nicer tonight when they get home to their families and have a glass of red wine or pint of beer, whatever it may be. They have worked hard all week for a result on Saturday afternoon."
