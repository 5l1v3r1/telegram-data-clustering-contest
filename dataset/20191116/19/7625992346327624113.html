Five charged in high school football game shooting described as "petty vengeance"

16 Nov 2019, 19:32 by Victoria Albert

Five people have been charged in connection with a Friday night shooting at a high school football game in New Jersey, prosecutors said Saturday. Three people, including a 10-year-old child, were shot after a gunman opened fire at the game, in what authorities described as an act of "petty vengeance."

"This incident had nothing to do with the students of Pleasantville High School or Camden High School," Atlantic County Prosecutor Damon G. Tyner said in a press release. "The venue simply presented an opportunity for criminals to pursue their own form of petty vengeance against one another."
