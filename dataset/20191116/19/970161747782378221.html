How old is Jacqueline Jossa and who did she play in EastEnders?

16 Nov 2019, 19:30

The wait for I’m A Celebrity 2019 is almost over, with Jacqueline Jossa and the other celebrities in this year’s line-up about to head into the jungle.

Even though the former EastEnders star admitted that she’s ‘scared of everything’, Emily Atack has thrown her support behind Jacqueline, telling The Mirror: ‘I’m really supportive of Jacqueline Jossa, I think we’re a similar age and she’s been through quite a lot.

‘I think she could really find some positives, so we’re rooting for her.’

Ahead of her appearance in the I’m A Celebrity jungle, here’s everything you need to know about Jacqueline’s time on EastEnders.

Who did Jacqueline Jossa play in EastEnders?

Jacqueline, 27, played Lauren Branning on the BBC soap for eight years from the time she took over the role from Madeline Duggan in 2010 until 2018.

Lauren is the eldest daughter of Tanya Cross (Jo Joyner) and Max Branning (Jake Wood).

Over the years, her storylines have included hitting her father with her car, helping her mother through cervical cancer, having a son with Peter Beale (Ben Hardy), and falling from the roof of the Queen Vic with her sister Abi (Lorna Fitzgerald).
