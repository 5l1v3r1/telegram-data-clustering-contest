Notts County beaten for the first time at home since August as Barrow secure 3-0 win

Two goals in three minutes left Magpies with too much to do

16 Nov 2019, 16:52 by Leigh Curtis

Notts County suffered their first home defeat since August as Barrow secured a convincing 3-0 win at Meadow Lane.

The Magpies got off to a poor start falling behind after just 10 minutes as they found themselves a goal down for the third consecutive game.

It was a well worked move as Patrick Brough's incisive pass from full-back made its way through to Josh Kay who cut the ball back for Dior Angus who slid the ball under Sam Slocombe.

Before Notts could even regain their senses, they found themselves two down just three minutes later when an underhit back pass from Enzio Boldewijn was pounced on by Dior Angus.

The striker still had much to do but kept his composure to pick out John Rooney who converted at the near post.

Notts tried to engineer a fight back as Boldewijn saw a shot blocked while Wes Thomas couldn't convert from two yards after Ben Turner had headed goalwards from a corner.

Ardley resisted making changes at half-time as he searched for a way back into the game but Notts' carelessness in possession at times made life even tougher.

Thomas did flash a shot across goal, but goalkeeper Joel Dixon was rarely called into action while Slocombe made a fine save to thwart Angus.

But Barrow did have their third goal when substitute Jack Hindle converted at the near post.

It what was a disappointing afternoon for Notts who lost for the first time at home since being beaten by Barnet in the opening weeks of the season.
