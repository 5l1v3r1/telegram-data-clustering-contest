Cristiano Ronaldo's Ballon d'Or credentials questioned by former teammate

Cristiano Ronaldo is on the 30-man shortlist to claim his sixth Ballon d'Or but is behind the likes of Virgil van Dijk and Lionel Messi in the race for the prestigious award

16 Nov 2019, 16:15 by Irishmirror.ie

Iker Casillas reckons that it wouldn't be "very logical" if former Real Madrid teammate Cristiano Ronaldo was awarded the Ballon d'Or this year.

Juventus superstar Ronaldo was included on the 30-man shortlist for the prestigious gong - which will be handed out at France Football's annual ceremony in Paris on December 2.

The 34-year-old had a lean season by his incredibly high standards, as he helped Juve lift the Serie A.

Ronaldo produced his worst goal return since his last season at Manchester United - as he only managed 28 goals in 43 appearances, having scored between 42 and 61 in his previous eight seasons at Real.

Because of that he is not seen as one of the front runners for the Ballon d'Or, with Liverpool defender Virgil van Dijk the bookmaker's favourite to walk about with silverware.

And keeper Casillas thinks the award should be between van Dijk and Lionel Messi.

"Van Dijk was the best player in UEFA. Messi was the best for FIFA. Imagine Cristiano (on his merit) winning the Golden Ball," Casillas wrote on Twitter .

"It seems to me that the criteria we follow in football to give prizes are not very logical, right? Friday's reflection."

Ronaldo has already won five Ballon d'Ors during his glittering career - but according to his mother he should have had even more.

"There is a mafia. That's the right word to define it. Yes, there is a mafia in football," she told the Portuguese press.

"If there wasn’t a football mafia, my son would have more [individual] international trophies. If you see everything that happened, you will realise that it is the mafia.

"If he was Spanish or English they would not do what they did to him. But since he's Portuguese and from Madeira... well, this happens.”
