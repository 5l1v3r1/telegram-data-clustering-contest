Movie producer Evander grabs 9 nominations for Zafaa Awards 2019 with “Asantewaa” movie

16 Nov 2019, 16:10 by Adu Sarpeah

“I don’t set out to win or get worldwide recognition, but whenever my name is cited by any recognized awards’ institution, it motivates me to push a little harder” has been the philosophy of Evander Agyemang who doubles as a film producer and director.

READ ALSO:– The Movie Industry Needs Investors, Not Unity – Director Evander Kwame Agyeman

Lo and behold, the philosophy of Evander has been actualized once again as his name has been cited by Zulu Africa Film Academy Awards (ZAFAA). Thus, Evander’s self-directed movie christened “Asantewaa” has bagged 9 nominations in this year’s ZAFAA Awards.

For the purpose of clarity, the movie “Asantewaa”, speaks of a young energetic African woman who out of an African mother feeling puts her life to risk after surviving from a kidnapper who trade in female virgin’s parts and infringe on their rights by raping those that are not virgins.

“Asantewaa” the true heart of an African lady is a must watch movie. You can watch full movie of “Asantewaa” on Amazon prime as “Virgins in Danger”. Below are the Nine (9) nominations from ZAFAA 2019 in the category of;

BEST SCREENPLAY                                                             AMA ODUMA ODOOM

BEST ACTOR INDIGENOUS FEMALE                               EMELIA BROBBEY

BEST ACTOR INDIGENOUS MALE                                  KWADWO NKANSAH

BEST SUPPORTING ACTOR INDIGENOUS FEMALE     CYNTHIA TIMA YEBOAH

BEST SUPPORTING ACTOR INDIGENOUS MALE         YAW DABO

BEST PRODUCER INDIGENOUS                                      NANCY TORTO

BEST INDIGENOUS FILM                                                  EVANDER KWAME AGYEMANG

BEST CINEMATOGRAPHER                                    EVANDER KWAME AGYEMANG

BEST DIRECTOR INDIGENOUS                            EVANDER KWAME AGYEMANG

It’s worth knowing that Zulu Africa Film Academy Awards (ZAFAA), over the years has given nominations and awards a number of filmmakers around the world not excluding countries like Ghana, USA, Nigeria, Uganda, Cameroon. UK, Burkina Faso, etc.

As a reminder, Evander Kwame Agyeman is a Ghanaian filmmaker who has over the years prove beyond reasonable doubt that the popular saying “experience is the best teacher” is indeed true. Evander as popularly called has humbly served and learned film making for 18yrs with experience from both international and local bodies in terms of theory (School) and practical (Work).

More importantly, the award under discussion takes off at Transcorp Hilton hotel, Abuja Nigeria, 24th November 2019. Let’s meet and celebrate African film.
