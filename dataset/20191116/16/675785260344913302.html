Conor McGregor trains two-year-old son in adorable UFC session

Conor McGregor had some advice for two-year-old son Conor Jnr as he put him through his paces in the octagon

16 Nov 2019, 16:11 by Liam Prenderville, http://www.facebook.com/mirrorfootball

Conor McGregor has offered his two-year-old son a glimpse into life as a UFC superstar.

McGregor is working towards his UFC return and took some time out from training to put Conor Jnr through his paces.

In an adorable video, the two-year-old is seen donning the heavy MMA gloves and throwing some punches at his superstar father.

After slipping over following a wild swing, McGregor tells the toddler: "On you feet, don't fall in. Stay on your feet!"

McGregor's fiancee Dee Devlin and his daughter Croia are also seen in the ring during the session.

 

The Irishman uploaded a number of images alongside the clip as Conor Jnr aims some kicks and punches.

McGregor captioned the posts: "We reap what we sow."

McGregor is expected to take on Donald Cerrone when he returns to the octagon on January 218.

The bout will be the 31-year-old's first fight since he was choked out by Khabib Nurmagomedov.

 

His striking coach Owen Roddy is confident McGregor will be back as good as ever.

"I know he’s working very hard,"  he told MMA Junkie  . "There’s no official date set yet, but Conor’s been training very hard, as you can see - he’s in good nick.

"If you're looking at any of the pictures, he's in phenomenal shape, he’s ready to go. I’m sure everybody’s excited to see him back.

 

"If it is 'Cowboy', it would be an exciting fight. I said this a couple of months ago: 'Cowboy' and Conor is a great fight for the fans.

"Two guys that are exciting strikers – although ‘Cowboy’ is phenomenal on the ground, as well, but I think if it was a fight between them, it would be a striking battle, and I think it would be one for the fans, so if it is that, it’ll be an exciting one.

"I just want to see Conor back in. I think everybody just wants to see Conor back in the UFC, bring back that spectacle that is his fights.
