Iowa Will Be Wearing A Special Helmet For Its Showdown With Minnesota

16 Nov 2019, 16:31 by Matt Lombardi

The Iowa Hawkeyes have a huge game on Saturday afternoon against the Minnesota Golden Gophers. It looks like the team will be wearing a special helmet for the contest.

Iowa will be hosting a blackout for the game, which could help decide the Big Ten West. The Hawkeyes will also be wearing a black helmet with a red, white and blue hawkeye logo. It features stars and stripes like the American flag.

It’s not the first time Iowa has worn the helmet. But there’s no doubt that it’s one of their better looks. Hopefully fans agree.

Iowa announced the news on social media.

Minnesota, surprisingly, is off to a 9-0 start to the season after a huge upset win over Penn State last week. The Golden Gophers are ranked No. 8 in the College Football Playoff rankings as a result.

Iowa, meanwhile, is in the midst of a 6-3 season that includes a 3-3 mark in the Big Ten. The Hawkeyes are probably out of the Big Ten West race, but they can still achieve a nine or 10-win season if they win out.

The Hawkeyes and the Golden Gophers kick off at 4:00 PM ET on FOX.
