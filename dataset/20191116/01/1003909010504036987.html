Inaugural Green Line committee meeting shows council divided

16 Nov 2019, 01:31 by Meghan Potkins, Calgary Herald

It was a meeting that was supposed to “set the tone” and bolster public confidence in the city’s largest infrastructure project, but Friday’s inaugural gathering of the Green Line committee saw council members more divided than ever on the $4.9-billion transit project.

Coun. Shane Keating began the meeting with an appeal to his colleagues to “stop the churn” and “focus” on finalizing the route for Stage 1.

“It is the responsibility of this committee to bring confidence and credibility back to the Green Line project,” exhorted Keating. “We must cease and desist from criticizing administration as we move forward and we must work together as a committee.”

But within moments of Keating’s opening remarks, city councillors were arguing in circles over public engagement and bickering over the wording of a proposal from Coun. Jeff Davison to establish new terms to guide the Green Line project.

A controversial part of Davison’s pitch was the suggestion that the city should consider “options to split the line” to avoid crossing the existing downtown transit mall along 7 Avenue S.W. — a move that would effectively quash the original vision for a single north-south line with a tunnel beneath the downtown.

Committee members couldn’t come to an agreement on Davison’s proposal and opted instead to refer it back to city staff for further work — but not before Coun. Evan Woolley was asked to apologize for telling his colleague, Coun. Jyoti Gondek, that she didn’t “have to be so livid all the time” during a particularly heated exchange.

Woolley apologized and the meeting continued under a cloud of tension, with Coun. Druh Farrell lamenting at one point, “I don’t know how this committee is going to accomplish anything, frankly, based on the last two and a half hours.”
