Bobby Duncan explains decision to leave Liverpool for Fiorentina wasn't for money

Steven Gerrard's cousin says he 'always loved Liverpool' but couldn't see a path into first team with world's best front three ahead of him

16 Nov 2019, 01:16 by Gareth Bicknell

Bobby Duncan has opened up on his decision to leave Liverpool for Fiorentina, insisting his decision to make the Serie A switch wasn't for money.

Duncan, who is Steven Gerrard's cousin, joined Fiorentina in a controversial Â£1.8million transfer from Liverpool in the summer.

The 18-year-old forward had been prolific for Liverpool's youth sides last season, and helped the Reds win the FA Youth Cup for the first time in 12 years.

But with Sadio Mane, Mo Salah and Roberto Firmino forming what Duncan described as "the best front three in the world" and Rhian Brewster knocking on the door behind them, the teenager knew his chances of first-team football at Liverpool were limited.

"It was a difficult decision to be honest," Duncan told ViolaNation.com . "I was in a difficult moment at that time.

"Something was missing. It wasn't over money. It wasn't over anything else other than I want to play first team football soon.

"Liverpool has Mohamed Salah, Roberto Firmino, Sadio Mane, Rhian Brewster. That front three is the best front three in the world. It was hard to knock them down.

"In my mind, I thought I would go and play abroad. Serie A is just below the Premier League.

"I wanted to make a name for myself, show the Liverpool and Fiorentina fans who I am."

Duncan added: "I always loved Liverpool, but my love now is to the Fiorentina fans. I need to repay them for showing me the love since I arrived.

"Leaving Liverpool was a difficult decision. I was only there for a year, but I didn't see the path forward for me there."

Duncan featured in Jurgen Klopp's first team for their pre-season friendlies in the summer, but an almighty row broke out shortly before the closure of the summer transfer window when Duncan's agent Saif Rubie accused the Reds of "mental bullying" in blocking potential moves away .

Liverpool denied the accusations, and a stand-off was avoided as Duncan was allowed to move to Fiorentina.

Duncan is yet to play for Fiorentina's first team, but has scored four goals in 10 appearances for their under-19s.
