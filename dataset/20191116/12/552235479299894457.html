What channel is Cyprus vs Scotland? Live stream, TV and kick-off details

It's the first game of Scotland's Euro 2020 double header as Steve Clarke's side look to build some momentum.

16 Nov 2019, 12:31 by Chris Doyle, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/chris-doyle/

Scotland are in Cyprus for what is essentially a dead rubber yet Steve Clarke needs a result as much as ever.

The national side are in Nicosia for the second last of their Euro 2020 qualifying group fixtures before Tuesday's match with Kazakhstan.

And with the crucial Euro 2020 playoff match in March on the horizon, six points from six is a must if they are to build any momentum after a disappointing spell since Clarke took over.

Scotland ran out 2-1 winners thanks to a late Oliver Burke goal in the first meeting between these two sides, but a trip to Cyprus is a different proposition for Clarke's men after their recent woes away from home.

Here's all the details you need to know ahead of the clash.

When and where will the game take place?

The match will take place on Saturday, November 16 at the GSP Stadium.

Kick-off is at 2pm.

What channel is it on?

You can watch the game live on Sky Sports Football.

Is it being streamed online?

Yes, subscribers can watch on the Sky Go app.

Where else can I follow the game?

You can follow it right here on dailyrecord.co.uk of course! We'll be running a live blog well ahead of the TV coverage, bringing you all the key moments as well as post-match reaction and analysis.

A match report and much, much more will also be available on our website following the final whistle.
