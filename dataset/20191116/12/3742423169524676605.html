How To Do A One-Hand Dumbbell Snatch

16 Nov 2019, 12:30 by Beth Skwarecki

I’m glad that more than zero of you joined me for the one hand deadlift last week. Weird but cool, right? We’ve got another one handed lift this week, and this one goes overhead, which makes it extra badass. It’s a one hand dumbbell snatch.

The basic idea is this: you grab the weight and rip it off the ground so hard that it flies up past waist level, maybe higher (with your hand still on it, of course), and then you dip your body down to catch it overhead with a straight arm.

Special thanks to commenter Ash78 for pointing out that the dumbbell version of this lift is easier for a lot of folks than the barbell version I prefer. Once again, I’m just hoping for everyone to have fun experimenting with new lifts, so the more accessible a lift is, the better.

(If you work out with kettlebells, or if you do Crossfit, you’ve probably seen the kettlebell version of this lift. With a kettlebell you have to flip the bell over in mid-air. I always bang it into my forearm, because I suck at the kettlebell version of this move, although I’ve heard that if you do it with proper form, you don’t have this problem. A dumbbell gets around this problem, but please feel free to use a kettlebell if that’s your thing.)
