Welsh rugby coach rips his players to shreds in expletive-ridden tirade caught on live TV even though they won

Merthyr RFC beat Llandovery in dramatic fashion but their coach was in no mood to celebrate

16 Nov 2019, 12:17 by Jon Doel

Merthyr RFC's players could be forgiven for expecting a celebratory Friday night after securing a dramatic last-minute victory over Llandovery in the Welsh Premiership.

Sadly, they were mistaken.

Despite Matthew Jarvis' late penalty ensuring a 33-31 win at The Wern, coach Dale McIntosh was far from happy. In fact, he was furious.

As the final whistle blew, McIntosh, who recently returned from helping to coach Namibia at the Rugby World Cup, could be seen menacingly pacing the touchline shaking his head in anger.

And, as the BBC Scrum V punditry team picked through the bones of the game, McIntosh could be seen, and partly heard, in the background tearing into his players as if they'd just been demolished by 50 points.

There were more than a few expletives as the players huddled together to face the wrath of their coach who was angry about the ill-disciplined performance he had just witnessed.

Asked how he was feeling by Scrum V's Phil Steele immediately afterwards, he said: "Angry. I'm angry. They gave a hell of a lot of effort and Llandovery were excellent tonight. They just wouldn't go away.
