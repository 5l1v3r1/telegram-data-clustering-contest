Indrani’s medical condition irreversible, says her lawyer

Ms. Mukerjea had filed a bail application nearly six months ago on health grounds before special judge for CBI cases J.C. Jagdale

16 Nov 2019, 02:50 by PTI

The medical condition of Indrani Mukerjea, prime accused in the murder of her daughter Sheena Bora, is “irreversible” and requires treatment by specialists, her lawyer told a court on Friday while seeking bail.

Ms. Mukerjea filed the bail application nearly six months ago on health grounds before special judge for CBI cases J.C. Jagdale. “Her medical condition is severe, deteriorating and aggravating. Path is not towards cure... Path is towards deterioration,” said her lawyer Tanveer Ahmed.

“Medical experts have opined that the infirmity with the lady is of an irreversible condition... So, deterioration is something which is inevitably going to happen,” he said.

She cannot be treated in jail and requires better medical facilities and specialist doctors. No quarterly medical check-up of his client, as directed by the court while rejecting her previous bail plea in November 2018, has been carried out yet, the lawyer claimed.

The CBI had earlier contended while opposing bail for Ms. Mukerjea that her release could hamper the trial, and it was yet to examine Rahul, former media baron Peter Mukerjea’s son from earlier marriage, who is a crucial witness.

Mr. Ahmed said the agency’s apprehension was mere speculation. “On the date of last order (in November 2018) it was contended that one vital witness Rahul is yet to testify. Since then 36 more witnesses have testified but Rahul is yet to be examined.”

The court will continue the hearing on the bail plea on November 19.
