20 photographs of the week

16 Nov 2019, 08:45 by Jim Powell

Bushfires in New South Wales, protests in Chile and Iraq, escalating violence in Hong Kong and floods in Venice – the best photography in news, culture and sport from around the world this week

Hong Kong

A man reacts as he tries to extinguish a burning Christmas tree at the Festival Walk mall in Kowloon Tong.

Photograph: Thomas Peter/Reuters

Hong Kong

Riot police detain two men in the central district of Hong Kong. A policeman shot a masked protester in the torso this week, igniting clashes across the city as crowds took to the streets to block roads and hurl insults at officers.

Photograph: Dale de la Rey/AFP/Getty Images

Hong Kong

Pro-democracy protesters stand on a bridge over the Tolo highway as they gather at the Chinese University of Hong Kong. Demonstrators stepped up their campaign of road blocks and vandalism, crippling the international financial hub this week and igniting some of the worst violence in five months of unrest.

Photograph: Philip Fong/AFP/Getty Images

La Paz, Bolivia

A woman cries in front of soldiers during a march by supporters of former president Evo Morales in downtown La Paz. Morales resigned this week after massive nationwide protests over suspected vote-rigging in an election in which he claimed to have won a fourth term in office.

Photograph: Natacha Pisarenko/AP

Washington, DC

Members of the press gather in the hallway of the Longworth House office building during the first public impeachment hearing before the House intelligence committee on Capitol Hill. House democrats are trying to build a case that President Donald Trump committed extortion, bribery or coercion by trying to enlist Ukraine to investigate political rival Joe Biden in exchange for military aid.

Photograph: Sarah Silbiger/Getty Images

Colo Heights, Australia

Fire and rescue officers watch the Gospers Mountain fire near Colo Heights in New South Wales. A fresh emergency warning was issued at the end of this week for a fast-moving fire, as hot, dry and windy conditions returned to bushfire-hit areas of New South Wales.

Photograph: Dan Himbrechts/EPA

Coffs Harbour, Australia

Sharnie Moren and her 18-month-old daughter Charlotte look on as thick smoke rises from bushfires. Three people have reportedly been killed, five are missing and 150 homes have been destroyed as more than 80 bushfires are burning uncontrolled around New South Wales.

Photograph: Dan Peled/AAP/Reuters

Venice

A woman sits in a chair in a flooded St Mark’s Square. The high-water mark hit 187cm in Venice this week, meaning more than 85% of the city was flooded. The highest level ever recorded was 194cm during the infamous flooding of 1966.

Photograph: Luca Bruno/AP

San Francisco

A mural on a side of a building depicts Swedish teenage climate activist Greta Thunberg. It was painted by Argentinian muralist Andres Iglesias, who is also known as Cobre.

Photograph: John G Mabanglo/EPA

Santiago, Chile

A Chilean police officer fires teargas at anti-government demonstrators during a protest in Santiago. Students began protesting nearly a month ago over a rise in subway fares. The demonstrations have morphed into a massive protest movement demanding improvements in basic services and benefits, including pensions, health, and education.

Photograph: Esteban Félix/AP

Santiago, Chile

Anti-government protesters clash with the police during protests in Santiago. The government announced this week that it has agreed to start the process to write a new constitution for the country, one of the most repeated demands of protesters who have taken to the streets in often violent demonstrations in recent weeks.

Photograph: Esteban Félix/AP

Baghdad, Iraq

An Iraqi protester returns a teargas canister thrown by security forces during clashes in the capital’s Khallani square during anti-government demonstrations.

Photograph: Ahmad Al-Rubaye/AFP/Getty Images

Darbasiyah, Syria

Syrians throw stones towards Turkish military vehicles during a Turkish-Russian army patrol near the town of Darbasiyah along the border between Syria and Turkey.

Photograph: Delil Souleiman/AFP/Getty Images

Soweto, South Africa

A student from the Soweto rugby school academy waits before practice. The academy is one of the three rugby schools of Soweto, a black township of Johannesburg that houses more than 1.3 million people. It was founded in 2016 and welcomes some 250 students between 8 and 19 years old, of which almost 50 are girls.

Photograph: Guillem Sartorio/AFP/Getty Images

Berlin

A tourist from Greece looks through the hole in remains of the Berlin Wall after commemorations celebrating the 30th anniversary of the fall of the Berlin Wall at Bernauer Strasse.

Photograph: Markus Schreiber/AP

Nicosia, Cyprus

A Cyprus airways aircraft lies abandoned at Nicosia airport inside the UN buffer zone that divides the Greek Cypriot south and the Turkish Cypriot north. As the world commemorates 30 years since the fall of the Berlin Wall, the bullet-riddled sandstone walls of abandoned, crumbling homes and concrete machine gun nests in Cyprus’ no-man’s land are a reminder of Europe’s last divided country.

Photograph: Petros Karadjias/AP

Salt, Spain

Pro-Catalan independence demonstrators block a major highway in Salt. French police detained 18 pro-Catalan independence protesters and used pepper spray and batons to push back demonstrators who blocked a major highway border crossing between France and Spain for more than 24 hours.

Photograph: Joan Mateu/AP

Le Barón, Mexico

A relative reacts during the burial of Rhonita Miller and her children Howard, Kristal, Titus, and Teana, who were killed by unknown assailants. Three families were traveling in separate SUVs when they were ambushed in two separate attacks by cartel gunmen hidden in the mountains along the dirt road. As a result, three women and six children died. All family members had dual Mexican-American citizenship and belonged to a Mormon offshoot group not affiliated with The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints.

Photograph: José Luis González/Reuters

Bangkok, Thailand

People place krathongs, or floating baskets, in a pond to mark the annual Loy Krathong festival in Bangkok. Loy Krathong takes place on the evening of the full moon of the 12th month in the traditional Thai calendar.

Photograph: Lillian Suwanrumpha/AFP/Getty Images

Fort William, Scotland

Current servicemen and veterans gather at the Commando Memorial at Spean Bridge for the annual Remembrance Sunday ceremony.

Photograph: Jane Barlow/PA
