Heartbroken family desperate for news of West Lothian chef who disappeared five days ago

Jonathon 'Johny' Donaldson's family and friends will join a makeshift search party in Armadale today in the hope of finding the missing 26-year-old.

16 Nov 2019, 08:24 by Daniel Morrow, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/daniel-morrow/

A heartbroken family are growing concerned for the welfare of a West Lothian chef who has been missing for five days.

Jonathon Donaldson was last spotted leaving his work at The Oatridge Hotel in Uphall at around 4pm on Monday, November 11.

An eyewitness then spotted the 26-year-old heading into a nearby Scotmid before boarding a number 600 bus heading towards Whitburn about 15 minutes later.

Police have been unable to trace the hotel sous chef since.

 

 

Friends and family of Jonathon are set to gather at Armadale Community Education Centre at around 10am on this morning.

More than 30 people are expected to join the makeshift search party at the centre, as his family grow desperate for news of his whereabouts.

Allanna Simpson said on the Missing Person Scotland Facebook page: “This is so heartbreaking for myself and my family and we are extremely concerned for his welfare.

“Concern is growing by the minute and we just need him home.”

Police Scotland have issued a fresh appeal for information.

He has been described as 5ft 2ins, of slim build with short light brown hair.

Jonathon was wearing a black jumper with a grey hood and dark coloured trousers, dark coloured shows and carrying a black Adidas rucksack.

He also has links to the Armadale and Uphall area of West Lothian.

A spokesperson for the force said: “Anyone who may have seen Jonathon since this time, or who has any information on his whereabouts, is urged to contact Police Scotland via 101 quoting incident number 1521 of November 12.”
