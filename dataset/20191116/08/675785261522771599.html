Chelsea warn Jody Morris after he mocked Jose Mourinho on social media

Chelsea have reportedly spoken to Jody Morris about his social media behaviour after he mocked former manager Jose Mourinho

16 Nov 2019, 08:19 by Rich Jones, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/rich-jones/

Chelsea have reportedly warned assistant manager Jody Morris over his use of social media.

It comes after he hit the headlines for posting a video on his Instagram story last week mocking former manager Jose Mourinho in response to comments made by the Portuguese boss about the Blues.

Mourinho stated he was worried about Chelsea's defence despite their fine form in recent weeks.

Morris quoted a tweet of the video, adding: "Jose is still worried" followed by a string of laughing emojis before posting a video pretending to cry on Instagram accompanied by Bitter Sweet Symphony by The Verve.

The former player, now a key member of Frank Lampard's backroom staff, is active on social media and, according to the Daily Mail, Chelsea believe some of his activity is inappropriate for a senior member of coaching staff.

He has been asked to show more restraint moving forwards, although they have no plans to ban him from social media.

Morris, who played over 100 games for Chelsea between 1996 and 2003, returned to Stamford Bridge to begin his coaching career in the youth system after retiring in 2013.

He worked his way into the role of Under 18s head coach in 2016/17 and led the club to their fifth straight FA Youth Cup win in 2018.

He departed for Derby, working as assistant manager under Frank Lampard last season, before the pair returned to Chelsea this season.

They have enjoyed a strong start to life in charge at Stamford Bridge, with their youthful side sitting third in the table after a run of six straight Premier League wins.
