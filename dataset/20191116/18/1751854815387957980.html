The foldable smartphones are now a reality. After the Samsung and Huawei, now Motorola has finally unveiled the iconic Motorola Razr foldable flip smartphone with dual displays and dual cameras. It is the first flip foldable smartphone which has once again flashed the old memories of old flip phones.

Read More: Motorola Razr vs Samsung Galaxy Fold vs Huawei Mate X: Specs Comparison

The Motorola Razr houses two displays as like the Galaxy Fold. The primary 6.2-inch P-OLED FLex View display is present on the inner side. It features the resolution of 2142 x 876 pixels flaunting a 21:9 aspect ratio, On the outer side, there;’s a 2.7-inch gOLED display with a resolution of 800 x 600 pixels and 4:3 aspect ratio. The exterior surface display is meant to respond and show notifications quickly.

Motorola Razr is powered by Qualcomm Snapdragon 710 SoC coupled with Adreno 616 GPU for graphics processing. The smartphone is available only in a 6 GB RAM and 128 GB internal storage variant. In optics, the users will get a 16-megapixel unit having an f/1.7 aperture and dual-pixel autofocus. Apart from the 5-megapixel front camera on the top of Flexview, the primary camera can also act as a selfie camera as it comes to the front when unfolded.

A 2510 mAh battery is present to withstand the whole day, and it supports 15W TurboPower fast charge. The device runs on Android 9.0 Pie. Unfortunately, the Motorola Razr does not carry a 3.5mm headphone port. In connectivity, the smartphone features e-SIM, Wi-F-, Bluetooth 5.0, NFC, GPS, and USB-C. It will become available in the US market by January 2020 for the price of $1499.
