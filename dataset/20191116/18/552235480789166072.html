Rangers fans baited with Celtic scarves and banners during Colts' win over Wrexham

The National League side's fans travelled in their numbers for the tie and seemed keen to spark some banter.

16 Nov 2019, 18:34 by Jonathan McFarlane

They travelled down in their hundreds for a taste of the big time as Wrexham fans headed to Ibrox determined to create a big game atmosphere for their Tunnockâ€™s Caramel Wafer Challenge Cup semi-final with Rangers Colts .

Part of their plan appeared to be siding with their opponents bitter Glasgow rivals Celtic after flags and even a banner featuring the Parkhead club's crest was held up in the away end of Ibrox .

Nearly 1000 punters from the Welsh town made the long journey to Glasgow as they attempted to create a carnival atmosphere in their little corner of the ground.

The game ended in a 2-0 victory for Rangers' Colts after goals from Jamie Barjonas and 16-year-old Nathan Young-Coombes dampened the travelling support's enthusiasm.

It sends the youngsters into the semi-final of the competition, the first time a Colts team has ever reached such a lofty stage of the tournament.

Rangers have already dispatched the likes of Solihul Moors, Stranraer and Ballymena United on their journey to date.
