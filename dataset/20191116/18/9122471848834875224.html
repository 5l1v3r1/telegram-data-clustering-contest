Injury Update For Alabama QB Tua Tagovailoa vs. Mississippi State

16 Nov 2019, 18:37 by Andrew McCarty

Earlier Saturday morning, college football analyst Tim Tebow suggested Alabama should not play Tua Tagovailoa this weekend – for safety reasons.

Tua, who played last weekend in a limited capacity against LSU, is recovering from an ankle injury. While he played last weekend, it was clear he wasn’t at 100-percent – he even said after the game that he played on one foot.

However, Tua got the start on Saturday afternoon and got off to a hot start. Tagovailoa completed 14-of-18 passing for 256 yards an two touchdowns.

Tebow’s words of caution came back into play near the end of the first half. Tagovailoa took a nasty shot and had to be carted off of the field.

It looked like he suffered some type of injury as his helmet popped off.
