Why QB Tua Tagovailoa Was Still Playing In Blowout vs. Mississippi State

16 Nov 2019, 18:49 by Andrew Holleran

Alabama quarterback Tua Tagovailoa was carted off the field with an injury late in the first half of the Crimson Tide’s afternoon contest against Mississippi State. The injury came with Nick Saban’s team leading, 35-7.

Tagovailoa, who played last week vs. LSU on a non-100 percent ankle, appeared to injure his hip on a play late in the first half.

Alabama’s quarterback was reportedly “screaming in pain” and had to be carted off the field.

Saban has since been criticized for leaving the less-than 100 percent quarterback in for too long, especially with the game in hand.

“Why in the hail is Tua in the game when you are up 35-7? That makes no sense. None!” ESPN’s David Pollack tweeted. He certainly wasn’t alone with this line of thinking, either.
