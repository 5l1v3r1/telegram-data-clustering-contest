Former Nottingham Forest striker shows he can still cut it with late winner

Nottingham Forest latest - Murphy departed the City Ground at the end of last season after being released following two years at the club.

16 Nov 2019, 18:51 by PA, Jamie Kemble

Bolton have wiped out their points deduction after former Nottingham Forest striker Daryl Murphy's stoppage-time winner saw off 10-man MK Dons 1-0.

The victory made it a third straight win for Wanderers - who started the campaign on -12 points after going into administration - and moved them on to one point for the campaign, 12 points from safety.

MK Dons appeared to have done enough to stop their three-match losing run when goalkeeper Lee Nicholls saved Murphy's 90th-minute penalty.

But three minutes into added time, Murphy turned in Thibaud Verlinden's cross to take Wanderers from negative numbers into the black.
