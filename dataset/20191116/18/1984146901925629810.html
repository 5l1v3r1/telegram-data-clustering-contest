Life in Ouseburn's The Malings where people rent their homes to the neighbours

The Malings has won awards for it’s unique design and community focus. Now, residents share their thoughts on what it is like to live there

16 Nov 2019, 18:00 by Nicole Goodwin

It was once voted one of the trendiest places to live in the UK thanks to its luxury homes and eye-catching developments.

But what is life like living at the quirky The Malings development on the bank of the River Ouseburn?

All 76 homes have been designed with the individual in mind, boasting high ceilings, cheerful brightly coloured doors and convenient commercial spaces for local businesses to thrive.

One resident said: "I don’t exaggerate when I say it’s everything we wished for."

Nestled in the heart of the community lives Jill Lamb, who is on her way to take two-year-old daughter, Stella, to ballet class.

Jill moved to the area from Gateshead to be among the lively Ouseburn lifestyle.

However, since moving to the area, Jill has become a parent and now finds a number of features impractical for life with a toddler.

The lack of garden space, and living area on the top floor of the house while her kitchen sits on the ground floor has proven particularly difficult for the family.

Despite this, she enjoys living at The Malings and highlights that there is a good sense of community among residents who remain in regular contact through resident Whatsapp and Facebook groups.

Resident and Malings Residents Association Chairman, Stuart Wannop, moved to the area from the Quayside three years ago.

While he agrees that the The Malings isn’t the best place to live if you desire a quiet life, he echoes that there is a strong sense of community embedded in The Malings.

The 43-year-old explains: “If somebody needs to borrow something, a quick post on the Facebook group and someone will usually come up trumps.

“Some have even been known to loan their house to a neighbour for a weekend when relatives come to stay.”

Stepping into the neighbouring Brinkburn Street Bar and Kitchen bar, which hosts Malings residents association meetings, we meet Jackie Christer who is visiting her son, Will.

Will Christer lives on Ouseburn Wharf, and is currently entertaining his brother on a staycation in Newcastle.

They have visited the new Ghetto Golf on Hoults Yard and local businesses, Proven Goods Co and Kiln.

Jackie explains that despite living in Prudhoe, she has only recently discovered Ouseburn and has fallen in love with the area.

The 61-year-old said: “I love it in Ouseburn. I love the mix of people – young and old – and the atmosphere is really inclusive.”

The Malings was designed by architect, Ash Sakula, to nurture a genuine sense of cohesion and engagement among the inhabitants.

And speaking to people in Ouseburn, it appears Ash Sakula’s vision has been accomplished.

Following The Malings development, a second phase situated on Lower Steenberg's Yard in Ouseburn has been granted.

This will feature 28 new riverside apartments and four new commercial spaces.
