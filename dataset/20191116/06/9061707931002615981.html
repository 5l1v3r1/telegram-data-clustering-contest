Where to see Santa in his grotto this Christmas

This is where to take the children this year

16 Nov 2019, 06:00 by Ben Waldron

There are few things that get you in the mood for Christmas more than a visit to the big man himself - Santa.

There is nothing more magical than taking the children or even the grandchildren along the little ones, as they excitedly tell the man in red all about what gifts they would like for Christmas. And visiting Santa in his grotto is the perfect location.

And there is a whole list of events coming up in and around Burton and the surrounding areas. where you can meet the man in the red suit.

From meeting Father Christmas at the Grade I listed Calke Abbey, near Ticknall, to Burton's very own National Forest Adventure Farm, if you are looking for somewhere to see Santa then there are plenty of options.

This is not a definitive list, so if you have an event coming up where little ones can meet Santa, get in touch. At ll venues there is a cost for meeting Santa.

Where to meet Santa near Burton

  1. National Forest Adventure Farm, Tatenhill

    The National Forest Adventure Farm at Tatenhill, near Burton, is bringing Christmas to life with an immersive festive event called the "North Pole Adventure".

    Visitors can take in all the sights, sounds and smells of Christmas in a journey to the North Pole, transporting them to Santa's home.

    There will even be a Victorian high street, complete with a sweet shop and a pub.

    Meet a talking polar bear, make gingerbread men and stop off at the elves workshop to collect a gift.

    One of the owners of the venue, Ivor Robinson said: "We are so excited to open the North Pole Adventure again this year.

    "Christmas is such a magical time of year and we wanted to create something that really captures the essence of Christmas spirit.

    "We didn't want to rest on our laurels but continue to improve in an effort to delight our visitors and offer a magical experience which creates memories for all the family."

    The event will open on Saturday, November 23. Tickets are priced from £22 for children and £13 for adults and includes entrance to the farm.

    Further information can be found by visiting https://www.adventurefarm.co.uk/northpoleadventure/.

  2. Alton Towers, Staffordshire

    Staffordshire theme park Alton Towers is hosting a Santa Sleepover this year.

    The festive event will allow children to not only meet Father Christmas himself, but also receive a gift.

    These events will take place on Sunday, November 24 and throughout December on 1, 8, 14 and 23.

    Visitors could also chose to stay overnight in one of the park's themed hotels, tickets for the Alton Towers pantomime, Robin Hood and tickets to the theme park.

    The ticket also includes passage into the water park and crazy golf, as well as a festive evening meal, a buffet breakfast and evening entertainment.

    More information can be found online at https://www.altontowers.com/activities/events/christmas/.

  3. Trentham Estate, near Stoke

    Children can meet Santa at Trentham Garden Centre each weekend from Sunday, December 1 until Tuesday, December 24.

    The cost per child is £6 with more information on booking available on the Trentham Estate webiste at https://www.trentham.co.uk/whats-on/shopping-village/santa-trentham-garden-centre/.

  4. Drayton Manor Theme Park, near Tamworth

    Sticking to the subject of theme parks, Drayton Manor will also be hosting a Christmas event.

    Young visitors can meet Father Christmas in the "Castle of Dreams", as well as having fun on more than 25 child-friendly rides and attractions in the park.

    They can also experience magical character parades, live Christmas shows, falling snow and spectacular fireworks.

    The event begins on Saturday, November 16, and runs until Tuesday, December 1.

    More information, including opening times, prices and a full list of events can be found by visiting https://www.draytonmanor.co.uk/magical-christmas.

  5. Calke Abbey, near Ticknall

    Where better to meet the spirit of Christmas himself than the Grade I listed country house of Calke Abbey, near Ticknall.

    Although the popular magical stable grotto is sold out, more dates have been added for "breakfast with Father Christmas".

    Little ones can get their festive time mornings off to a great start and enjoy a full English breakfast with Father Christmas, who will swoop by with presents.

    Due to popular demand, an additional date has been set for Saturday, November 30, starting at 9.15am.

    Further information can be found online at https://www.nationaltrust.org.uk/calke-abbey/features/father-christmas-in-his-grotto/.

  6. Conkers, Moira

    Evenings at visitors attraction Conkers, off Rawdon Road in Ashby, will be turned into Christmas magic.

    The Winter Wonderland event will run from Saturday, November 30, where Santa's chief elf and his team will lead you through the enchanted forest and the valley of Christmas lights to really get you into the festive mood.

    On arrival, adults will receive mulled wine and a mince pie, while the little ones can choose from a selection of Mrs Clauses freshly baked cookies.

    There will also be a balloon modeller, Christmas shows and films viewings and a "twinkle toes" train journey.

    Tickets and further information can be found online at https://www.trentham.co.uk/whats-on/shopping-village/santa-trentham-garden-centre/.
