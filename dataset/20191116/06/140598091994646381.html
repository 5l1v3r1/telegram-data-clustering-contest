Bagnetto with bitter crudités recipe

16 Nov 2019, 06:00 by Catherine Phipps

The flavour of this dip is incredible – richly savoury, spicy, salty, tinged with sweetness – the best way to completely satisfy the palate is to serve it with something succulent and bitter.

Prep time: 10 minutes

SERVES

Four

INGREDIENTS

  • 40g flat leaf parsley
  • A few sprigs each of tarragon, basil and chervil (optional)
  • 50g capers
  • A few caper leaves (optional)
  • 1 small tin of anchovies and their oil
  • 2 tbsp Dijon mustard
  • 2 tbsp tomato purée
  • 1 tbsp red wine vinegar
  • 1 tsp dried thyme or a few dried thyme flower heads
  • ½ tsp chilli flakes, plus a little more to taste, if desired
  • ½ tsp freshly ground white pepper
  • 4 tbsp olive oil
  • A pinch of caster sugar (optional)
  • Sea salt (optional)

To serve

  • A few heads of Belgian endive, separated into leaves, or cut into thin wedges, or any other kind of crisp winter leaves, such as slender, curling tardivo (fiori d’Inverno) or puntarelle.

METHOD

  1. Finely chop all the herbs, capers, caper leaves, if using, and the anchovies.
  2. Put in a bowl and add the mustard, tomato purée, vinegar, thyme, chilli flakes and white pepper. Stir to combine, then whisk in the olive oil.
  3.  Leave to stand for a few minutes, then taste. If you haven’t used the sweeter herbs you may need to add a pinch of sugar, and if you haven’t used salted capers or anchovies, you may want to add salt.
  4.  Adjust according to your taste and transfer to a serving bowl, or for future use, a sterilised jar.
  5. Arrange the endives on a platter and serve with the dip. If you have any left, store in a sterilised jar in the fridge, covered with a little olive oil.
