Steamed sprout tops recipe

16 Nov 2019, 06:00 by Catherine Phipps

This is the top of the dense, woody stalk of Brussels sprouts, where the stem, still dotted with tiny nuggets of nutty-flavoured sprouts, broadens into a perfect head of tightly furled leaves surrounded by a loose crown of floppy green leaves.

  1. Nick off the sprouts with a sharp knife and pull off all of the looser leaves. Discard the woodiest part of the stem, then cut the head into wedges. To cook, put a large slice of butter in a pan with a splash of water.
  2. When the butter has melted, add everything together and season with salt.
  3. Toss gently, then tightly cover and leave for around 10 to 12 minutes over a gentle heat, checking regularly.
  4. When you can easily pierce the sprouts with a knife tip, they are done. Serve immediately with the buttery juices.
