Fagron to pay US$22.5 million to settle US allegations over inflated prices

08 Nov 2019, 01:55

WASHINGTON: Pharmaceutical supplier Fagron Holding USA LLC will pay US$22.5 million to resolve allegations that its subsidiaries inflated average wholesale prices for compound drug ingredients and submitted fraudulent claims to federal healthcare programs, the U.S. Department of Justice said on Thursday.

The settlement will resolve legal actions filed against Fagron units Freedom Pharmaceuticals, B&B pharmaceuticals and Pharmacy Services Inc, the department said in a statement.
