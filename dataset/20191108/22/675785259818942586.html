Jurgen Klopp insists there is "absolutely no chance" of Liverpool sealing Kylian Mbappe transfer

Liverpool have been linked with a a transfer swoop for PSG superstar Mbappe but Klopp has quashed the rumours once and for all

08 Nov 2019, 22:30 by David Maddock, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/david-maddock/

Jurgen Klopp has dashed the hopes of millions of Liverpool fans… by insisting there is “absolutely no chance” of the club signing Kylian Mbappe.

The Reds have long been linked with the French star, after first making an approach for him back in 2017 when he was at Monaco. And a recent link up with Nike as the Merseyside club’s kit sponsor has added fuel to the fire.

Mbappe is one of Nike’s biggest stars, and there have been suggestions this week that Real Madrid president Florentino Perez is genuinely worried the English Champions League winners have the resources to outbid even his own club.

Klopp though, was blunt in response. As he plots the downfall of super-rich Manchester City this weekend, he revealed the prospect of buying Mbappe is simply not part of the longer term strategy.

"OK, from a sporting point of view, there are not a lot of reasons to not sign Mbappe, what a player he is,” Klopp said.

“But it is about the money of course. No chance. Absolutely no chance, sorry for killing that story.

"I don't think there is a club in the world that can buy Kylian Mbappe from PSG. And we are involved in that too. We cannot do it, it is as easy as that.

"We have nothing (more) to say about it to be honest, but [buying] this calibre of player is difficult."

Klopp though, believes it is possible for Liverpool to rival City, even without the endless resources now required to sign the likes of Mbappe that only a super-rich club backed by oil money can provide.

“This team is in a wonderful age and they really still have space for improvement,” he said.

The Reds boss revealed that both Virgil van Dijk and Jordan Henderson are available for the showdown with City, but Joel Matip is ruled out until after the international break.
