Joleon Lescott infuriates Aston Villa fans - and Wolves are loving it

Aston Villa latest news | The build-up for Villa's clash with Wolverhampton Wanderers on Sunday is starting as both sides meet in the top flight for the first time in over seven years

08 Nov 2019, 10:07 by Richard Cusack

Joleon Lescott says he will be supporting Wolverhampton Wanderers when Aston Villa head to Molineux on Sunday (2pm).

Lescott came through the ranks at Wolves , spending six years with the club before being bought by Everton.

He then went on to Manchester City, winning two Premier League titles, a pair of FA Cup trophies and a League cup.

In the latter years of his career he had stints at West Bromwich Albion and Villa , with his one season at Villa Park ending in relegation - the first time the club had been demoted from the top flight.

Now a pundit, Lescott revealed on Sunday that he will be supporting Wolves this weekend, not Villa who he grew up supporting.

He was on duty for BT Sport's coverage of Wolves' 1-0 win over Slovan Bratislava in the Europa League last night when Paul Ince questioned where his allegiances lie.

"I will be supporting Wolves at the weekend," he said.

"I was a Villa fan but not any more."

 

Unsurprisingly, his comments drew plenty of reaction from both sets of fans.
