Ole Gunnar Solskjaer issues challenge to Man Utd starlet Mason Greenwood

Greenwood underlined his vast potential with a fine goal in the Europa League on Thursday night and is looking to become a regular at Old Trafford

08 Nov 2019, 10:00 by David McDonnell, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/david-mcdonnell/

Ole Gunnar Solskjaer has told Mason Greenwood he must 'man up' before he can become a Manchester United regular.

Greenwood, 18, underlined his huge potential with the opening goal in United's 3-0 win over Partizan Belgrade, which secured their place in the Europa League knockout stage.

It was Greenwood's third United goal this season, but the young striker has had to patient, with Solskjaer determined to ease him into the first-team set-up.

“Mason is going to have a very big career, but sometimes it's not the right time to play him,” said Solskjaer. “He knows why he hasn’t played as much.

“He needs to learn the man’s game. He’s gone up from the under-18s last year, now he’s training with the first-team every day and you can’t just turn that switch on and off.

“We’re taking our time with him. It’s been a difficult period. Anthony Martial has been away, and to put all the weight on his shoulder wasn’t right, either.

“I think we're finding the right balance between playing him, resting him and training him.

“You’ve got to perform every day if you are going to be accepted by a group like this."

Solskjaer deployed Greenwood on the right of a front three against Partizan, a position new signing Dan James has made his own with an impressive start to his United career.

But Solskjaer sees Greenwood as another option there, with Martial now preferred as a central striker and Marcus Rashford on the left.

“Mason's definitely one option on the right,” said Solskjaer.

“I rested Dan against Partizan because he’s played too much football for my liking, so maybe Mason hasn’t played enough.

“If he’d really stamped his authority in training every single day and been the best option, he would have played every game.

“But as a young boy you expect ups and downs and he’s had some downs. But I think he's on his way up again and I think we'll see a top, top player there one day.”

Solskjaer was asked if Greenwood's finishing – and in particular his deadly left foot - reminded him of former United striker Robin van Persie.

“Definitely,” said Solskjaer. “He's very composed as well with his finishing. It's just what he does, it's natural to him.

“Right foot, left foot – I don’t think he still knows which is the best one, which is very difficult for defenders to defend against.

“He’s doing his finishing practice and I can see it every day. The good thing is he can do it at Old Trafford in front of a full crowd or 62,000.

“He doesn’t get fazed by the occasion. He’s still composed. Whether that’s a game with his mates in the park or a cup final, he'll still finish the same, hopefully.”

Solskjaer, whose side host Brighton tomorrow, has centred his rebuild around young players and drafted several into the first-team squad, including Greenwood, Brandon Williams and James Garner.

“You always get assessed on results now, but the DNA of this club – and it’s always been in our philosophy and my philosophy - is to give young players with the right attitude a chance.” said Solskjaer.

'If that takes a couple of years for Mason to really, really come through, it’s worth it for us.

“I’m managing for Manchester United, I’m not managing for me. I'm managing for the best way I think we can get the best out of Mason.”
