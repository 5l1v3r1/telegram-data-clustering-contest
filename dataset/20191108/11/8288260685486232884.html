'I agree' - Ex-Aston Villa man weighs in on Jack Grealish decision

Aston Villa latest news | Jack Grealish was left out of the England squad for the next batch of upcoming Euro 2020 qualifiers later this month in a surprise decision

08 Nov 2019, 11:44 by Richard Cusack

Darren Bent says Jack Grealish is unlucky not to be named in the England squad for upcoming Euro 2020 qualifiers.

Eyebrows were raised yesterday when the Villa captain was left out of Gareth Southgate's side for clashes with Montenegro and Kosovo later this month.

Southgate explained that Grealish was competing with wingers because Dean Smith has played him out wide this season, with Leicester City's James Maddison and Chelsea's Mason Mount ahead of him in the pecking order of attacking midfielders.

Grealish has missed the past couple of games because of a calf injury, but Smith confirmed he would be OK to play for England if he was called up.

Villa fans and neutrals were expecting him to make the cut for the 27-man squad after two goals and one assist in his last three matches.

And former Villa striker Bent has weighed in with his thoughts.

"Immediately the one that jumps out if the Grealish one, because I think he's playing really well at the minute," he told talkSPORT.

"But I also agree with what Gareth is saying in that in the first time in a long time we've got that creative midfield role stacked with talent.

 

"We've got so many young players who are playing really well in the minute.

"We've got some who are not even playing that consistently, like Phil Foden, who can come in and slot into that role.

"As much as I think it's harsh on Grealish, he's up against some stiff coimpetition."
