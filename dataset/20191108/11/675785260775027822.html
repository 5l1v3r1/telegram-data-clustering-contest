Liverpool fans urge Sadio Mane to do special celebration if he scores against Man City

The Senegalese forward has been in the news in the build-up to the game, as City boss Pep Guardiola accused him of diving before later backtracking on his comments

08 Nov 2019, 11:35 by Mark Jones, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/mark-jones/

The build-up to the titanic tussle between Liverpool and Manchester City on Sunday seems to have been all about one man.

Or rather two.

Pep Guardiola's "diving" comments about Sadio Mane shortly after his side's 2-1 win over Southampton last weekend have been everywhere in the run-up to the Anfield meeting.

The City boss was discussing the Liverpool forward shortly after he'd been told that Mane had just scored a last-gasp winner for the Reds at Aston Villa, and so he was perhaps understandably frustrated.

Mane seems to have taken it all in his stride though, even joking that he would be more than ready to dive if it helped his team win.

“If it could be a penalty for sure I will ‘dive’ again,” he joked after the Reds' 2-1 Champions League win over Genk in midweek.

“If the dive will give me a penalty then I will do it to get it back. Why not?

“It’s a bit clever from him (Guardiola) to get the attention of the referee but I will just play my football.

“I don’t pay attention to what he’s saying because it’s part of football.

“Even though I saw him commenting about it, what he said, for me, I think it’s positive."

And it seems that there are plenty of Liverpool fans online who are hoping to see a Mane dive at the weekend, but a dive that comes after a goal rather than a dive that will help score one.

That's because the whole Guardiola-Mane 'row' has evoked memories of a similar one between then Everton manager David Moyes and then Liverpool forward Luis Suarez before a Merseyside derby in October 2012.

Moyes had claimed that Suarez "often went down too easily" in the run-up to the game at Goodison Park, and so when Suarez's cross-shot was turned into his own net by Everton's Leighton Baines 14 minutes into the match, the Uruguayan tore off and launched into a comedy dive right in front of where Moyes was standing.

It was a moment Reds fans loved, and many of them hope to see something similar from Mane in front of Guardiola on Sunday.

Suarez later scored again in that 2012 clash, but Everton battled back to a earn a 2-2 draw from a thrilling Merseyside derby.

With many expecting a similarly thrilling game at Anfield on Sunday, will Mane 'do a Suarez?'
