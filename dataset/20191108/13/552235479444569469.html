GTG Glasgow is recruiting automotive instructors to train the new generation

Got what it takes to inspire a new generation to work in the automotive industry? One of these jobs could be for you

08 Nov 2019, 13:16 by Dailyrecord.co.uk

Training and development firm GTG is searching for instructors to fill several automotive roles.

With facilities in Glasgow, Edinburgh and Wolverhampton and more than 40 years of experience, GTG is one of the UK’s leading training groups.

The company provides classes in a huge range of areas, from business skills to health and safety and they have a strong heritage in automotive training with hundreds of apprentices starting at GTG every year.

The company, which is owned by Arnold Clark, is searching for skilled individuals to become motoring industry instructors at their Glasgow South Street training centre.

A spokesperson for GTG said: ‘We’re currently looking to expand our highly skilled teaching staff to help train the next generation of apprentices working in the automotive sector.

“We can offer excellent facilities and the latest technology to provide an outstanding working environment.

“It’s a hugely satisfying career and offers a great opportunity to add further skills to the knowledge and experience you have already accumulated over the years. The most important thing you need is passion for making a difference to learner’s lives.’

The following roles are currently available at GTG Glasgow:

Automotive Technical Trainer – Heavy Vehicle / Bus & Coach

This role is perfect for those who have enjoyed a career as a technician in an automotive environment and are looking to pass on their knowledge to apprentices. The role also requires strong interpersonal skills and the intitiative to work independently.

Automotive Technical Trainer – Body Repair

Candidates for the Automotive Technical Trainer role should have knowledge of best practice in accident repairs and health and safety regulations, as well as a Level 3 qualification in Light Vehicle Repair. The job involves working as part of a team in order to inspire apprentices to succeed in the repair sector.

LVM Technical Trainer

An LVM technical trainer is responsible for training both apprentices and commercial delegates. The role requires experience in the light vehicle sector with a Level 3 qualification as well as a good understanding of apprenticeships. A teaching qualification is helpful but not essential.

All of these jobs will include training and development to help new employees learn new skills and to and provide them with the best working environment. There is also a range of great employee benefits, including private healthcare and life assurance.
