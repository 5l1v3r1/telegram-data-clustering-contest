All you need to know about the referee for Plymouth Argyle's FA Cup tie at Bolton Wanderers

Rob Lewis has already taken charge of the Pilgrims once this season

08 Nov 2019, 13:30 by Chris Errington

Experienced EFL referee Rob Lewis will take charge of Plymouth Argyle’s FA Cup first round tie at Bolton Wanderers tomorrow.

He has shown 50 yellow cards and sent-of three players in 12 games this season.

One of those matches was Argyle’s come-from-behind 1-1 League Two draw away to Swindon Town on October 12.

Lewis booked seven players in that game, including the Pilgrims’ trio of Niall Canavan, Danny Mayor and Joe Riley.

He has also refereed Bolton once this season, when they lost 5-0 away to Gillingham in League One on August 31.

His last match was Port Vale’s 0-0 League Two draw at home to Oldham Athletic on October 26.

Bolton are four times winners of the FA Cup and were beaten finalists on three other occasions.

They reached the semi-finals in 2010/11 when still a Premier League club but lost 5-0 to Stoke City at Wembley.
