Neil Lennon makes Celtic transfer pledge as he pays tribute to fans after Lazio heroics

The Northern Irishman was full of praise for his players but also had a special mention for the travelling support.

08 Nov 2019, 15:06 by Chris Doyle, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/chris-doyle/

Neil Lennon has paid tribute to the travelling Celtic fans after last night's heroics in Rome.

The Parkhead side made a remarkable comeback to take all three points against Lazio and qualify for the Europa League last-32 with two games to spare.

James Forrest cancelled out Ciro Immobile's opener, before super sub Olivier Ntcham snatched the victory in stoppage time.

Lennon was also full of praise for his players, but had a special mention for the away support who made it all the way to Italy.

The Northern Irishman also insisted the club will strengthen in the January transfer window to help them prepare for the knockout stages.

He said: "I've not really had a chance to reflect on the result, we got in very late this morning. The immediate feelings are of immense pride and happiness."

"The qualification is a huge feather in our caps and the performances in the four games from the players have been immense.

"I didn't think we'd qualify as quickly. They showed great resilience last night. Some of the football we played and the chances we created, it was a real step in the right direction.

"A huge credit goes to the supporters. It felt like the real Celtic. That connection with the fans and the players was there. There was a real pride for me last night. To send them home happy was as good for me as getting the result.

"It's the sum of all parts. We've got a great young and dynamic coaching staff here. Their work ethic is fantastic and the players themselves are playing brilliantly.

"It's a long way off, it's after the January window and we'll look to strengthen again. Going forward we are a threat against anyone."

And the Hoops boss also provided an injury update on a host of stars and admits Leigh Griffiths could return against Motherwell on Sunday.

He added: "Griffiths could feature. Rogic we're hoping will be back and Mikey has a small chance too. Hatem will get a scan tomorrow but we don't think he'll be fit for the game."
