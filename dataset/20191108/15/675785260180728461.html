Michael Schumacher's wife and daughter give rare interview paying tribute to F1 legend

Corinna Schumacher has paid tribute to her husband, the seven-time Formula One world champion, in an interview in Germany

08 Nov 2019, 15:02 by Alex Richards, https://www.facebook.com/AARichards/

Michael Schumacher's wife, Corinna has paid tribute to her husband in a rare interview, declaring: "I don't forget who I have to thank."

Formula 1 legend Schumacher suffered a serious brain injury in December 2013 in a skiing accident in the French Alps, which left him in a coma.

Since then, little has been revealed about the condition of the seven-time world champion since he moved to being treated at home in September 2014.

Schumacher's son, Mick, now 20, is following in his father's footsteps with a racing career of his own.

But Corinna, 50, and daughter Gina Maria, 22, have another passion: horseback riding.

And in an interview with She Magazine in Germany, she paid tribute to Michael and credited him for the love that both have for horses.

The family have a ranch in the Swiss town of Givrins which was given to his wife by Michael Schumacher in 2005 for their 10th wedding anniversary, and mother and daughter are also organising riding events there.

"When I was 30, I very much wanted to have a horse and Michael went with me to Dubai, where I intended to buy an Arabian horse," she said.

"He did everything for me. I will never forget who I have to thank. That would be my husband Michael."

Corinna, who married Michael in 1995, now has two ranches in Switzerland and Texas.

She currently has 40 horses, and Michael used to boast about how he would act as the hired help, known as a Turniertrottel in Germany, where he would much out stables and drive the truck.

She added: "I am glad that I have such a great team that keeps everything going. I feel the greatest pride when I see Gina.

"What she has already achieved and how much fun she is - that makes me happy."

Earlier this year, Gina helped Germany secure a gold medal in the SVAG FEI Reining European Championship in Switzerland.

And she revealed the advice her father once gave her, stating: "When you drive a car, you can stick it in the garage afterwards. But a horse needs to be looked after all the time, even on Sundays."

According to Corinna, their daughter's success was something Michael had once prophesied.

She said: "When my husband told me that one day Gina would be much better than me, I was not thrilled.

"I thought 'hey, how did he come to that conclusion?' I mean I work with horses from morning to night, trying to learn everything."

"He then told me: 'You are too nice. Gina however gives everything to the horses, but she is also able to say no and to see it through.'"
