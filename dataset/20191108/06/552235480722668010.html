Angelo Alessio reveals the Kilmarnock study sessions that have turned his fortunes around

The Italian was named Manager of the Month for October after a difficult start to his time in Scotland.

08 Nov 2019, 06:00 by Gordon Parks, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/gordon-parks/

Angelo Alessio admits hiring an English tutor and engaging a tactical rethink have transformed his fortunes at Kilmarnock.

The Rugby Park boss landed the Ladbrokes manager of the month award for October after a haul of nine points from a possible twelve.

It’s a far cry from a start to his reign which had some calling for the Italian to be sacked after Europa Cup elimination to Welsh minnows Connah’s Quay was followed by two Premiership defeats on the bounce.

But with the help of his teacher, the language barrier’s been lifted and Alessio insists all the talk his now translating into future success.

He said: "The beginning was difficult with my language but now it's much better. I still have to improve it, the Scottish accent is very difficult to understand.

"I have a teacher and watch programmes here in English. I prefer of course sport, but also documentaries and movies but I don’t have a lot of time. I have to think about the next training, the next game.

"Now It's much better because I have more confidence more fluency.

"We changed the method of training and the bad results didn’t help me with this.

“I have a lot of players from last season and I have to consider this. I wanted to change some things about the style, I believe in my methods, but I have to be flexible because I have these players.

“I wanted to keep the same style as last season and I changed some situations on the pitch but now we have to continue, in the future I want to put other things in place that I believe in."

It's been a dramatic improvement for Killie after the doom which surrounded the club in the summer and a manager of the month award is evidence of a man growing into the job and he insists there hasn't been a moment when he's had doubts over his decision to move to Scottish football.

He said: "I'm very proud of the award because it comes after a difficult start. So I consider it an award shared with my staff and my players and all colleagues at Kilmarnock.

"I have never had a doubt or regret over coming to the club as I knew it’d be difficult as it was a great season last season at Kilmarnock. It was a big challenge of course but I prefer a big challenge than comfort.

“It's important to work in the right way and believe in my methods.

"Kilmarnock chose me for this job. It was a difficult start for many reasons after being eliminated from the Europa League and losing the first two matches.

“But that's the past, we have to forget the bad start. Our focus is on the next game.

“It's difficult because I have won it after two defeats but we were going very well until Motherwell and then had some problems in terms of injuries which complicated our progress.

“But now we gave another important game on Saturday against Hamilton and we can re-start our campaign."

Despite the early trials and tribulations, Alessio believes everyone from the players, fans and board are now buying into his methods and style of play.

He said: "Billy bowie and the board were always with me and he helped me a lot. They understand my job and of course and everything depends on the result.

"I have my idea but the players have to think of my idea, they have to follow my instruction, follow me in everything.

“I wanted to keep the same style as last season and I changed some situations on the pitch but now we have to continue, in the future I want to put other things I believe.

"I think winning our first league game at St Johnstone was when I saw the team compact, they work hard in training sessions, they show the right attitude and concentration.

Alessio confirmed Scotland defender Stuart Findlay faces a lengthy spell on the sidelines but 24-year-old won’t require surgery on a tendon problem and backed the player to come back better than ever.

He said: “It's complicated but at the moment I can say minimum three months. I'm sorry for him because he was playing very well, more confident and in the national team too.

“At the moment he is devastated but he will come back stronger and more hungry."
