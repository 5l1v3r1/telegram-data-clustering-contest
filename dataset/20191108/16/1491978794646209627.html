The week in wildlife – in pictures

08 Nov 2019, 16:42

Red squirrels, a laughing kookaburra and a dehydrated koala

Bohemian waxwings eating rowan berries in the Kostroma region of Russia.

Photograph: Vladimir Smirnov/TASS/Getty Images

A bear with cubs at an observatory near Băile Tușnad in central Romania. The country has Europe’s highest number of brown bears.

Photograph: Daniel Mihăilescu/AFP via Getty Images

Starlings on a telegraph wire in Shawbost (Scottish Gaelic: Siabost) in the Outer Hebrides, Scotland.

Photograph: Murdo MacLeod/The Guardian

The Widdale red squirrel reserve in North Yorkshire. The UK Squirrel Accord estimates that there are about 140,000 red squirrels in the UK, where years ago there were several million

Photograph: Danny Lawson/PA

A red squirrel forages for food ahead of winter in the Widdale red squirrel reserve.

Photograph: Danny Lawson/PA

Two squat lobsters ( Galathea squamifera) in a rock pool at West Runton, north Norfolk. The crustaceans are closely related to hermit crabs and swim or scuttle backwards when disturbed.

Photograph: Rupert Higgins

A yellow-headed blackbird perches on a stalk of grass in a wetland near Sterling, North Dakota.

Photograph: Charlie Riedel/AP

A three-year-old male baby elephant plays with its mother as it stands in a line during the re-opening of Pobitora wildlife sanctuary, which was closed to tourists due to the monsoon season, on the outskirts of Guwahati, India.

Photograph: Anupam Nath/AP

A National Trust ranger holds a pair of hazel dormice during monthly monitoring check on Cotehele estate in Cornwall. The National Trust is asking people to report any sightings of the endangered species.

Photograph: George Holmes/National Trust

A rescued young Sunda pangolin takes his first tentative steps after being released back into the wild in Thailand. The critically endangered animal was being illegally kept in cramped conditions and constant darkness by a poacher, before being saved by Zoological Society of London staff and local park rangers.

Photograph: Yingboon Chongsomchai/ZSL

Dead seabirds lie on a pier with plastic debris on Midway Atoll in the Hawaiian archipelago. Midway is littered with deads birds that have brightly coloured plastic protruding from their decomposing intestines.

Photograph: Caleb Jones/AP

A young protester holding a placard featuring a photograph of broadcaster David Attenborough during a Friday strike protest in Melbourne, Australia.

Photograph: Asanka Ratnayake/Getty Images

A laughing kookaburra perched on a tree branch. The bird is currently in third place in the competition to find the Australian bird of the year 2019.

Photograph: Ken Griffiths/Alamy

A shark caught in a fishing net. Approximately 46% of ocean plastic in the Great Pacific Garbage Patch is made up of fishing nets, some as large as football fields, according to a study published in March 2018 in Scientific Reports.

Photograph: Csaba Tokolyi/Sea Shepherd

A crab walks in a mangrove in Cabo de Santo Agostinho, Pernambuco state, Brazil. Fishermen affected by oil spills along the beaches of Brazil have helped clean the coastal ecosystem, which is essential for the survival of numerous species and of families who depend on fishing.

Photograph: Nelson Almeida/AFP via Getty Images

A young jackdaw rests on a fence post in Altheim, western Germany.

Photograph: Thomas Warnack/DPA/AFP via Getty Images

A lone deer grazes on a charred ridge in the aftermath of the Kincade fire near Healdsburg, California.

Photograph: Charlie Riedel/AP

A dehydrated and injured koala receives treatment at the Port Macquarie koala hospital in New South Wales after its rescue from a bushfire that ravaged a 2,000-hectare area.

Photograph: Saeed Khan/AFP via Getty Images

The Ghost, by Eduardo Blanco Mendizabal, was the overall winning entry in the 2019 GDT European wildlife photographer of the year. The night portrait shows the most endangered species of wildcat in Europe, the Iberian lynx, in the Sierra de Andújar nature park in Andalusia, Spain.

Photograph: Eduardo Blanco Mendizabal/2019 GDT European Wildlife Photographer of the Year

A young male sperm whale found stranded on Hell’s Mouth beach in Gwynedd, Wales – the first stranding of this species recorded in Wales. This whale was 6.7 metres (22ft) long.

Photograph: CSIP-ZSL

A baboon sitting under a tree in Mana Pools national park in Zimbabwe. Buffaloes, elephants, hippos, impalas, zebras and many other creatures are stressed by lack of food and water in the park.

Photograph: Tsvangirayi Mukwazhi/AP

A tiny jacana chick strides confidently over Amazonian waterlilies on a lake next to the Rewa river in Guyana.

Photograph: Kevin Rushby/The Guardian
