Logan Paul recalls being ridiculed at school for wanting to be famous ahead of KSI rematch

Logan Paul clashes with KSI on Saturday night in Los Angeles in their long-awaited rematch as professional boxers

08 Nov 2019, 08:00 by Martin Domin, https://www.facebook.com/mirrorfighting

Logan Paul has revealed his life-long ambition to be famous - and how his class-mates ridiculed him for it.

The social media influencer has been in the public eye since he was a teenager when he first started creating six-second videos on Vine.

He then migrated to YouTube where he attracted a following of almost 20million to establish himself as one of the most popular influencers in the world.

And on Saturday night Paul will take his earnings and perhaps his reputation into the stratosphere when he takes on KSI in their long-awaited rematch.

And the Ohio native is confident he will emerge victorious because he's been proving people wrong his whole life.

"In high school I wanted to be famous and everyone laughed," he said.

"But everything has seemed in reach for me as long as I grab for it. This is not a one-off; it cannot be. I am too good at it to not use this skill set more."

Paul fought to a draw with KSI in Manchester last year in a white-collar bout.

More than a year on and the rivals have turned professional after being granted licences by the California State Athletic Commission.

Anthony Joshua's promoter Eddie Hearn is behind this weekend's show with the fight dividing the boxing world.

 

But Paul insists he has taken his preparation seriously after recruiting former heavyweight world champion Shannon Briggs as his trainer.

"I am taking it very seriously, like everything," he added.

"This camp was more strategic and I work my ass off better than anything. The strategy last year was to work hard but I over-trained and was weak.

"I was focused in travelling to travelling to Paris for a holiday with my former girlfriend.  I sparred amateurs last year and I boxed like an amateur.

"This time I am sparring pros and fighting like a pro. We have not sparred a single amateur, all unbeaten pros and everyone has left on the canvas."
