Jeff Rosenstock doing Trans-Pecos residency of benefit shows in December

08 Nov 2019, 12:30 by Jeremy Nifras

Jeff Rosenstock just released his killer live LP Thanks, Sorry! last month, which was recorded over four nights at NYC’s Bowery Ballroom earlier this year, and now Jeff is set to bring his energetic live show to an even more intimate NYC venue before the year ends. He’s playing the first four Mondays in December at Trans-Pecos in Ridgewood, NY, with 100% of the proceeds each night going to a different cause.

There are no advance tickets for these shows, but you do have to RSVP in advance, and then arrive at the venue starting at 6 PM to make a suggested donation and receive a wristband for entry. Even with RSVP, admission is first come, first serve, and only 200 people will be admitted each night.

On night one (12/2), proceeds go towards victim services nonprofit Safe Horizon (RSVP); night two (12/9) benefits Brooklyn’s long-running and consistently awesome Willie Mae Rock Camp For Girls (RSVP); night three (12/16) benefits Educated Little Monsters, the “music and arts group serving Bushwick youth whose communities are severely impacted by gentrification” that used to run classes out of Silent Barn (RSVP); and night four (12/23) supports Black Lives Matter (RSVP).
