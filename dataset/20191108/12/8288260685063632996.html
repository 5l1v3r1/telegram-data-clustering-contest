'Underdogs' - Birmingham City star makes promotion declaration and reveals Pep Clotet's true impact

Latest Birmingham City news | The 22-year-old moved to St Andrew's in the summer and has been a regular in Pep Clotet's starting line-up

08 Nov 2019, 12:36 by Mark Wakefield

Birmingham City midfielder Dan Crowley has certainly made an impact at St Andrew’s following his summer move from Willem II.

The 22-year-old has featured in all but one of Blues ’ Sky Bet Championship matches this season, and has quickly settled in to Pep Clotet ’s methods.

The stats do not do Crowley justice, which read three assists and no goals to his name thus far, but the former Arsenal man is often a handful for opposition defences.

Crowley has spoken about the influence of Blues’ two Gardners, midfielder Gary and player-coach Craig, who helped persuade him to reject moves to PSV Eindhoven and Feyenoord in favour of a move to St Andrew’s.

 

The Blues playmaker is also confident that the club can succeed the season, and are “underdogs” in the race for promotion.

“Craig’s been a great help with his coaching,” Crowley told The Times .

“He’s the link between the players and the staff, the middle man, and I think that helps in a team.

“The Gaffer (Pep Clotet) is a really intelligent guy. The stuff we work on in training during the week, you can really feel it working on a Saturday.

“I think we can get promoted. We’re the underdogs as well — no one thinks we’ll go up.”

Crowley came through the youth ranks at Arsenal, but failed to make a first-team appearance for the Gunners before signing for Eredivisie outfit Willem II on a free transfer in 2017.

Loan spells at Barnsley, Oxford and Dutch club Go A Eagles haven’t dented Crowley’s confidence.

The Blues midfielder is determined to go to the very top of the game and play at the highest level.

 

“No one else is going to believe it if you don’t, are they?” Crowley added.

“I think I’m good with the ball at my feet, and I can make a difference to the team — that little something extra — where maybe not everyone can.

“When I was at Arsenal and I trained with the first team, I would think, ‘I am the best player here’. Maybe that mentality was my way of pushing myself. But I did think it as well. I always believed I was the best.

 

“Santi Cazorla was unbelievable, his technique — left and right — was probably the best I’ve seen. [Alexis] Sanchez was so sharp and worked so hard. Wilshere was someone I was compared with, but I don’t want to be compared to anyone. That might sound really big time, but I’m me. And I know that still means nothing really.

“I want to play in the Premier League, at the top. I want to play in the Champions League. I believe I will.”
