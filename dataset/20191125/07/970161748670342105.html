Ashes and World Cup star Jofra Archer warned after New Zealand thump England

25 Nov 2019, 07:29

Joe Root admits he wants to see improvements from Jofra Archer after New Zealand smashed England in Tauranga.

Archer took 1-107 from 42 overs in New Zealand’s sole innings as the hosts wrapped up an emphatic victory to take a 1-0 lead in the two-match series.

The 24-year-old burst into the international scene last summer, starting in England’s World Cup triumph and the subsequent Ashes series.

But Root insists Archer is a work in progress, telling Sky Sports after the defeat: ‘This has been a frustrating Test for Archer.

‘He’s a hugely exciting talent and he has a huge amount of skill but he has to learn that there are certain spells where he needs to ramp it up more.

‘He may have to bowl shorter spells. The energy and effort have to be there all the time.

‘Jofra is a young guy at the start of his career. He’s never bowled with this ball before, these are foreign conditions and it will take time to adjust to that.

‘He’s taken international cricket by storm but having too high an expectation of him at the start of his career is a dangerous thing.’

New Zealand sailed past England’s first innings total of 353, with BJ Watling scoring a double century and Mitchell Santner posting his maiden Test ton as the Black Caps declared on 615-9 and took control of the match.

England spent a gruelling 201 overs in the field before being bowled out for 197 on the final day.

‘I can’t fault the effort levels,’ Root added. ‘We were trying to create pressure by bowling maidens when there wasn’t much in the surface.

‘There were a few half chances that slipped away from us but we have to give credit to the oppositon who played very well on this surface.

‘It will be important to see how the bowlers pull up as there has been a big workload.’
