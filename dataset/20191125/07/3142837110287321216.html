Trustmark Corp (NASDAQ:TRMK) Announces $0.23 Quarterly Dividend

25 Nov 2019, 07:47 by Scott Moore

Trustmark Corp (NASDAQ:TRMK) announced a quarterly dividend on Tuesday, October 22nd, Zacks reports. Stockholders of record on Sunday, December 1st will be given a dividend of 0.23 per share by the financial services provider on Sunday, December 15th. This represents a $0.92 dividend on an annualized basis and a dividend yield of 2.69%. The ex-dividend date is Wednesday, November 27th.

NASDAQ TRMK opened at $34.24 on Monday. Trustmark has a 52-week low of $26.84 and a 52-week high of $36.63. The company has a debt-to-equity ratio of 0.04, a quick ratio of 0.83 and a current ratio of 0.85. The company has a market capitalization of $2.20 billion, a P/E ratio of 15.49 and a beta of 1.09. The company has a fifty day moving average of $34.51 and a 200-day moving average of $33.72.

Trustmark (NASDAQ:TRMK) last posted its quarterly earnings data on Tuesday, October 22nd. The financial services provider reported $0.64 earnings per share (EPS) for the quarter, topping analysts’ consensus estimates of $0.62 by $0.02. Trustmark had a net margin of 21.68% and a return on equity of 9.51%. The company had revenue of $160.05 million during the quarter, compared to the consensus estimate of $160.21 million. During the same period in the previous year, the firm posted $0.54 EPS. The firm’s revenue was up 1.8% compared to the same quarter last year. As a group, analysts forecast that Trustmark will post 2.37 EPS for the current fiscal year.

About Trustmark

Trustmark Corporation operates as the bank holding company for Trustmark National Bank that provides banking and other financial solutions to individuals and corporate institutions in the United States. The company offers checking, savings, and money market accounts; individual retirement accounts; certificates of deposits; financing for commercial and industrial projects, income producing commercial real estate, owner-occupied real estate, and construction and land development; and installment and real estate loans, and lines of credit.
