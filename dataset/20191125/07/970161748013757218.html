Wanted man, 18, arrested after mum killed in hit-and-run

25 Nov 2019, 07:47

A man has been arrested on suspicion of the murder of a mum-of-one killed in a hit-and-run last week.

Kaylib Connolly, 18, was wanted in connection with the death of Alison McBlaine, 36, who was hit in Blackburn, Lancashire, on Tuesday evening.

She died in hospital two days later with her family, including her son Reagan, 12, by her bedside.

Police have said Ms McBlaine was hit by a Fiat Punto Grande they believe deliberately mounted the pavement at about 7.45pm on Tuesday.

The car failed to stop at the scene and was later found with significant front end damage.

Attempts had been made to set the vehicle on fire, a spokesman for Lancashire Police said.

Detectives investigating the murder are continuing to search for Dean Qayum, 20.

The force spokesman said a 26-year-old man and a second person arrested on suspicion of murder remain in custody.

A 25-year-old man who was also arrested on suspicion of the charge has been bailed to 19 December.

A second pedestrian, a man aged 26, was also hit in the collision with Ms McBlaine and suffered serious injuries, police said.

Police searching for Qayum, who may be using crutches due to a leg injury, have warned anyone hiding him will also be arrested.

Detective Chief Inspector Lee Wilson said: ‘Dean Qayum remains wanted in connection with this extremely serious offence.

‘Police want to speak to him and I believe he is being harboured in and around the Blackburn area at this time.

‘I would warn anyone who is protecting him, or helping to hide him, that we will identify this during our investigation and they will also be arrested and prosecuted.

‘My thoughts and those of the team engaged on this inquiry remain with Alison’s loved ones, in particular her 12-year-old son Reagan.

‘I would urge anyone with information about what has happened, or knows where Qayum is, to do the right thing – please come forward and speak to police immediately.’

Anyone with information should contact police on 101 or call Crimestoppers, anonymously, on 0800 555 111.
