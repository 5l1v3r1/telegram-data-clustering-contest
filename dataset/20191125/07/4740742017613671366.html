Bristol Sounds 2020: Noel Gallagher, Jack Savoretti, Supergrass and more announced for concert series

The summer concert series is returning with a bang

25 Nov 2019, 07:00 by Robin Murray

A massive lineup has been revealed for Bristol Sounds 2020.

The concert series has become a firm favourite for many in the city's summer calendar and this year included the likes of Elbow, Tom Misch and Bloc Party.

In 2020 music fans will be treated to more big performances, this time from the likes of Noel Gallagher's High Flying Birds, Supergrass, Jack Savoretti, The Levellers, Reef and more.

And for the first time ever there will be an all-day Saturday show as part of next year's series, which will run from midday until 11pm, curated by the Levellers.

Who's playing on what day?

Wednesday, June 24 will see Noel Gallagher’s High Flying Birds return to Bristol having recently released their latest single, Wandering Star, taken from the forthcoming EP Blue Moon Rising.

Fans of Gallagher will no doubt be delighted with his return to the city, following his incredible headline set at The Downs Festival in 2018.

Supporting Noel are the Australian indie electro-pop outfit Confidence Man, whose debut album Confident Music for Confident People was named the best independent dance album in the Australian Independent Record Label Music Awards. 

On Thursday, June 25, Supergrass take to the stage. The Britpop four piece reunited in September of 2019, performing a 'surprise' performance at the annual Pilton Party in the same month, which means they are likely to perform at the festival's 50th anniversary.

While the lineup details for Friday are still to be announced, there's plenty to look forward to at the all-day event on Saturday.

Headlined and curated by the Levellers, the all-day bill will also feature local Pilton legends Reef, perennial festival favourites Goldie Lookin Chain, Scottish three-piece Peat & Diesel and a rare performance from Gaz Brookfield & The Company of Thieves, with many more acts still to be announced.

Bringing proceedings to a close on Sunday, June 28 is Jack Savoretti, returning to the west country after selling out Westonbirt Arboretum to 9,000 people this summer.

His recent sixth album Singing to Strangers entered at number one in the album charts earlier this year and this will be his biggest Bristol show to date.
