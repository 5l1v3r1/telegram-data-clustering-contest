Leeds United boss drops hint

Jack Clarke appears to be firmly in Leeds United's plans this season after making his second League debut for the club yesterday.

He made a 19-minute cameo for Marcelo Bielsa's side in a 2-1 win at Luton Town, a result that keeps his side firmly in the Championship promotion race.

They sit in second place, on 34 points and three ahead of Preston North End in third.

Clarke came through the Whites' academy and was sold to Tottenham Hotspur in the summer for around £10 million - but he was loaned back to Leeds for the whole of this season.

Derby County taunted

Preston North End fans delivered a short, but cutting, message to Derby County supporters yesterday.

Alex Neil's side were visiting Pride Park for a Championship clash, which they lost 1-0 thanks to a first-half effort from Martyn Waghorn.

It was Derby's first game since the defeat to Nottingham Forest before the international break, with that match also ending 1-0 with Lewis Grabban capitalising on a stray pass from Jayden Bogle to make himself the hero in front of the Trent End.

That was Forest 's third consecutive win over Derby in all competitions and already their second this season after a 3-0 pasting of Phillip Cocu's side in the Carabao Cup second round tie at the City Ground in August.

How the opposition reported it

Nottingham Forest battled to earn a point in a goalless draw with Bristol City yesterday.

Despite Ryan Yates being sent off 11 minutes into the second half, the Reds still picked up what could turn out to be a vital point in the Championship promotion race.

Forest did have chances, though, with Yates himself having an effort cleared off the line and Joe Lolley testing Daniel Bentley in the hosts' goal.

Lewis Grabban, too, spurned a brilliant opportunity with 15 minutes to go as Forest threatened to sneak a win in a second half that had turned into a defensive battle with the visitors hoping to earn a point.
