I'm A Celebrity campmates rage over food hoarding discovery - with two to blame

I'm A Celebrity's James Haskell, Andrew Maxwell and Ian Wright were pretty annoyed to learn two campmates hadn't been providing enough food

25 Nov 2019, 21:40 by Irishmirror.ie

I'm A Celebrity aired a food hoarding discovery on Monday that left a number of campmates raging.

Two of the gang, Nadine Coyle and Myles Stephenson, were blamed for the awkward mix-up that had left the stars super hungry this week, significantly the day before.

Nadine and Myles had been placed in charge of the cooking by camp leader James Haskell hence the blame laying with them, as it emerged a number of bags of food hadn't been used.

Instead, they'd been kept in the trunk, with no one appearing to even know they were there - despite the stars complaining of hunger.

As James had a search through the trunk in the latest episode, he suddenly found extra food they didn't realise existed.

But it wasn't just one, two, maybe three bags - James found 21 extra bags of beans uneaten.

Not only that, but there wasn't the same amount of rice to match.

While they tend to keep a few back in case the camp have a smaller meal another day, or generally get hungry around lunchtime, 21 is far too much to be hoarding.

James told his campmates: "We have got to be way better at this food organisation, we’ve got 21 beans in here."

A hungry Ian shouted: "What?! And we are getting the same amount of rice to beans?”

Jacqueline Jossa admitted people weren't eating their beans in favour of the rice.

She confessed: "When we are putting them on we are putting just three of four bags on instead of the amount of people there are.”

Andrew Maxwell complained: "It’s madness. We had such a tiny portion of beans yesterday.”

James went on: "It’s because people aren’t organising them properly by putting them in a box so you can see what you’ve got….I’m not going to have a go at Myles because he’s doing such a brilliant job or Nadine because she’s so lovely but we’ve got to have a conversation.

“I’d delegated catering admin to a couple of other people but I’m now going to take over it myself to make sure we don’t find hoarded food because yesterday everyone was flagging.”

Ian later said in the Bush Telegraph: "Everyone was sitting there hungry yesterday not realising that we’ve got 21 packets of beans!" with Andrew adding: "We didn’t need to go hungry at all yesterday."
