Woman trapped underneath bus after city centre crash

Police, fire and ambulance crews have been working at the scene since 7.40pm

25 Nov 2019, 21:22 by Emma Grimshaw, Natasha Davies

A woman was trapped underneath a bus in Bristol city centre this evening.

Firefighters, police and ambulance crews were called to the scene on Broad Quay, near to Sainsbury's, shortly after 7.30pm on Monday, November 25.

Dozens of emergency services vehicles were seen blocking the road, with queues of buses behind.

Passengers on the buses in the queue abandoned the vehicles.

A spokesperson for Avon Fire and Rescue Service confirmed crews from Temple, Bath and Avonmouth attended the incident.

They added that firefighters used a scoop stretcher to free a female casualty who is being treated by paramedics.

The spokesperson was not able to comment on the woman's condition or injuries.

It is not yet clear when the road might reopen.

First Bus advised passengers requiring services to Kingswood, Hanham and Brislington to use Rupert Street or Broadmead.
