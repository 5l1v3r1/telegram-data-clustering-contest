Man dies after car rams tree in Dwarka

The victim, Dwarka resident Apurv Pandey, was returning home in his Honda City after attending a function from NH8, said police.

25 Nov 2019, 21:11 by Express News Service

A 36-year-old man died after he allegedly rammed the sedan car he was driving into a tree in South West Delhi Monday morning. The incident took place around 1.10 am outside Devam farmhouse on Dwarka Link Road.

The victim, Dwarka resident Apurv Pandey, was returning home in his Honda City after attending a function from NH8, said police. “Prima facie it appears that Pandey was speeding,” said a police officer. DCP (South West) Devender Arya said, “A PCR call was received at 1.29 am about fire in a car and when a police team reached there, it was found that a car had collided into a tree and had caught fire.”

Irshad Khan (55), a guard on duty outside the farmhouse, told The Indian Express, “From a distance, I saw a speeding car hit the tree. A few of us rushed there and took the driver out… Suddenly the car caught fire.” Pandey was rushed to Venkateshwar hospital, where he declared dead on arrival.

“His body was handed over to the family after post-mortem at DDU,” said Arya.

Pandey held a private job and is survived by his parents and an elder brother who lives in Mumbai.

A case under IPC sections 279 and 304 has been filed. Police said the cause of the accident is yet to be ascertained.
