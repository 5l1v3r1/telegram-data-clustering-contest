Italian company produces robot that will carry your groceries home from the shops

25 Nov 2019, 21:54

People can now have their groceries carried for them by a robot that will follow their every move, all for the cost of a little under $5,000. But experts are asking, why?

Key points:

  • The Gita robot is designed to follow its owner and assist them with carrying goods
  • The product is thus far only available in the United States
  • The Gita retails for over $4,700, leading experts to question whether it is worth it

According to one technology analyst, Italian company Piaggio — famous for the Vespa scooter — might be onto a loser with its "Gita" (pronounced Jee-tah) if all the robot does is carry groceries, not other applications such as lugging tools around warehouses, hospitals or factory floors.

"That's a lot of money for what is in effect just a cargo-carrying robot that's going to carry your groceries," Forrester's JP Gownder told the Associated Press.

However, in the eyes of Piaggio's tech arm Fast Forward, the Gita, named after the Italian word for a short, pleasurable excursion, is a way for people to get "connected" with their local areas — rather than take a car to the shops, they can walk with a robot.

"We're trying to get you out into the world and connected to that neighbourhood you decided to move to because it was so walkable," said Greg Lynn, chief executive of Piaggio Fast Forward.

On a recent November morning, Mr Lynn was hunched over in a Boston waterfront park, pushing a button that triggered a Gita to "see" him with its cameras and sensors. Then came a musical whirring sound as the device — a squarish, bright red bucket with two oversized wheels — rose up and signalled it was ready for a neighbourhood stroll.
