Weather advisory issued by Met Eireann for three counties

25 Nov 2019, 21:23 by Ruairi Scott Byrne

Met Eireann has issued a weather advisory warning for three counties as Storm Sebastien looks set to finally land in Ireland.

The Tropical Storm is moving north-east from the Atlantic and is set to arrive on these shores on Monday and continue into Tuesday, possibly bringing heavy rain and a risk of flooding.

The US National Hurricane Centre (NHC) confirmed the Atlantic’s 18th named storm of 2019, which formed last week, had suddenly intensified in strength after it had initially been predicted to weaken quite quickly.

Earlier this evening Cork City Council issued a tidal flood warning and now the national forecaster has issued a Weather Advisory for Wexford, Cork and Waterford.

The warning, which will be valid from 3am until 8pm, on Tuesday states: "A combination of high tides, together with appreciable, but generally sub-warning levels of wind and rain, will produce localised impacts along the south coast during Tuesday."
