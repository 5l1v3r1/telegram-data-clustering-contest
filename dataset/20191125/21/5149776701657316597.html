Manager keen to extend Nottingham Forest youngster's loan spell but admits others could show interest

Latest Nottingham Forest transfer news | The 21-year-old midfielder is on loan at the League Two club until January

25 Nov 2019, 21:04 by Mark Wakefield

Port Vale boss John Askey is hopeful Nottingham Forest will extend Jake Taylor’s loan deal at the club.

The 21-year-old joined the League Two side on a short-term loan deal in the summer which currently runs until January.

So far this season, the versatile midfielder has scored five goals and provided one assist for his Vale team mates in 17 appearances in all competitions.

His last outing saw the young midfielder score his side’s only goal in the Valiants’ 2-1 defeat at Scunthorpe United.

Vale are hoping to persuade Forest to extend the loan until the end of the campaign but Askey says other clubs could take an interest.

"Hopefully. It will depend if anybody comes in for him,” Askey told Stoke-on-Trent Live, when asked if he hoped to keep told of Taylor for the remainder of the season.
