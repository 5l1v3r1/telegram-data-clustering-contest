FIREMAN PALM

Renfrewshire reveller rescued by firefighters after getting his hand stuck in pub radiator

25 Nov 2019, 21:07 by Rebecca Gray

A REVELLER was rescued by firefighters after getting his hand stuck in a pub radiator.

The mortified drinker had to be freed with hydraulic cutting gear when his digits got lodged between the heater and the boozer’s wall.

Tickled onlookers took a series of snaps at the Boarding House in Howwood, Renfrewshire, before dialling 999 on Sunday.

One pub-goer said: “The guy was really starting to panic so the fire brigade were called.

"I’m not sure why his hand was down there — maybe he dropped something and was trying to retrieve it.

"But he soon regretted his decision.”

The unlucky punter was treated with oxygen therapy before being taken home.

A Scottish Fire and Rescue Service spokesman said: “We were alerted at 6.40pm on Sunday to reports of a person in need of assistance within a pub.

“Operations control mobilised one fire appliance to the town’s Main Street, where firefighters used small cutting gear to help a casualty whose hand had become trapped in a radiator.”

“Crews removed the casualty to safety and provided the individual with oxygen therapy before leaving the scene.”

We told how a pensioner was rescued by 12 firefighters after getting stuck up a tree in Insch, Aberdeenshire, in 2013.

The 70-year-old worker was stranded 20ft up when his ladder crashed to the ground.
