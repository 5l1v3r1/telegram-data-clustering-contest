Ravens vs. Rams betting decision: How much Baltimore’s big wins should sway you

25 Nov 2019, 21:32 by VSiN

Have the Baltimore Ravens become the best team in pro football?

Betting markets aren’t quite pricing them that way heading into Monday night’s TV attraction on the road at the Los Angeles Rams (8:20 p.m., ESPN).

But, if you’re strictly looking at recent on-field performance, there’s little debate. In their past four games, the Ravens have crushed a fairly difficult schedule.

Sure, the Cincinnati Bengals didn’t represent much of a test. Baltimore’s other three wins came against playoff contenders with a combined record of 19-4 at kickoff. Those three victories over top opposition came by a combined 108-43 margin. The Ravens just beat teams playing .826 football by an average of three touchdowns per game!

A quick review of the Ravens past month:

  • Baltimore (+3) won at Seattle 30-16. The final score is a bit of a red herring. The Ravens had two defensive touchdowns. But, they did rush for 199 yards and gain 6.1 yards per play on the road at a loud site.
  • Baltimore (+3) beat defending Super Bowl champion and projected top AFC seed New England, 37-20. The Ravens took full advantage of a bye week to prepare, winning total yardage 372-342, yards-per-play 5.7 to 5.3, and rushing yards 210-74. Baltimore turned all four red-zone trips into touchdowns, then added another defensive score.
  • Baltimore (-10¹/₂) stomped Cincinnati, 49-13. How about 8.2 yards-per-play, 67 percent on third-down conversion attempts, and 100 percent once again in the red zone?
  • Baltimore (-4) humbled Houston, 41-7. Some of this may be attributed to Houston thinking more about a huge divisional game scheduled a few days later. But, the Ravens put on a true clinic, winning yardage 491-232, yards-per-play 7.7 to 4.1, rushing yards 263-122, while cashing in five touchdown drives of 70 yards or more.

That’s four double-digit wins that covered their point spreads by 19, 20, 25¹/₂, and 30 points.

When handicapping Ravens-Rams, or thinking about the futures board, should bettors focus on the gaudy stats above, or extenuating circumstances such as a 4-0 edge in non-offensive touchdowns … a bye week ahead of New England … catching Houston distracted … and so on?

The point spread spent a week expressing that very dilemma, hopping between Ravens -3 and -3¹/₂ .

Quants see an offensive mismatch against the recently struggling Rams, who have turned the ball over six times while converting just four of 24 third-down tries their past two games. And, the public loves betting hot teams at cheap prices.

Dog lovers make their case with money whenever the hook shows up. Long-time bettors are always on the lookout for overreactions. They’d point out that the Rams would have been favored by -3 or -3¹/₂ if this game were played a month ago (the price range for Ravens-Seahawks), and closer to a touchdown in September. They’ll take a defensive ’dog at +3¹/₂ against a regression-due favorite that had to travel cross-country in an emotional letdown spot.

No trophies are given for being the best team in November. Let’s see if Baltimore is Lombardi ready.
