England will support Jofra Archer after racist taunts – Ashley Giles

25 Nov 2019, 23:14

England’s managing director says the abuse has no place in sport.

Ashley Giles has backed Jofra Archer’s England team-mates to rally round the paceman after the racist abuse he suffered during the first Test in New Zealand.

Archer took to Twitter shortly after the tourists were routed by an innings in Mount Maunganui to say he found it “a bit disturbing” to hear racial taunts during day five.

Giles has been liaising with an apologetic home board in his role as managing director of the England men’s team and is also checking in with the 24-year-old Archer.

“It was obviously emotional, it hurts,” said Giles.
