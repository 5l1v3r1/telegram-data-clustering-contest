CAUGHT LIVE

Yungblud took Glasgow’s SWG3 by storm as he gave an electric performance

25 Nov 2019, 23:41 by Jessie Wilson

YUNGBLUD took Glasgow’s SWG3 by storm as he gave an electric performance.

The 22-year-old, whose real name in Dominic Harrison, bounced relentlessly across the stage as the vibrant crowd screamed every word to hit tune 21st Century Liability.

He only stopped briefly to pick up a bra launched towards him by a fan.

He told the Glasgow crowd: “Are you ready to lose your mind Glasgow?

"Or have you already lost it?”

He added: ““You can look like whatever you want to look like, you can love whoever you want to love, and you can identify however you want to identify.

“No one can f***ing touch us.

He followed on from support band Saint Phnx, comprised of brothers Stevie and Alan Jukes from Wishaw.

They soared through their set while the crowd echoed the band’s infectious energy in true Glasgow style.

Yungblud’s latest EP, the Underrated Youth, came out in October this year.

He is a figurehead for his own pop-punk protest movement.

And as the adoring crowd screamed Kill Somebody along with him as he took centre stage in a mosh pit, there was a tangible feeling of love, defiant rage, and above all, hope.

He reappeared on the stage in a black mini skirt and an acoustic guitar before belting out Casual Sabotage - an anthem for the “freaks” and the “rejects”.

And he finished his encore with every hand in the air as Glasgow sang: “There’s hope for the underrated youth”.
