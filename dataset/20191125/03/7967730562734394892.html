Missing Queensland diver was on trip with mates

25 Nov 2019, 03:23 by Michael Doyle

Crews have widened their search area for a missing Bundaberg man who failed to surface after going for a dive off Elliott Heads.

A search resumed at first light on Monday for the 38-year-old, who was reported missing by the skipper of a six-metre vessel, 14 nautical miles off the coast on Saturday.

The initial search area covered about 100 nautical miles off the Bundaberg coast but has now been extended to 350 nautical miles, Senior Constable Mark Gardiner said.

"We have a helicopter up in the air doing a search along the shoreline," he said.

"At this stage, conditions are very favourable for a search.

"As the days progress, we will have our search assets spreading further out."

The 38-year-old man was returning from an overnight trip with two mates when police believe he decided to have one last descent into the water.

He has been described as a competent diver and spear fisherman.

Rescue divers have searched the last known location of the missing man, but have found no sign of him.

"From all the information we have received, he has done this trip a few times," Snr Const Gardiner said.

"It is all assumptions at this stage, what has caused him not to come up."

Shallow-water blackout, which occurs when a diver does not have enough oxygen for a dive, is one possible reason given for the disappearance.
