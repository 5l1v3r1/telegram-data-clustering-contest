Simba SC lucky to beat Ruvu Shooting, says Bwire

25 Nov 2019, 03:21 by Michael Mwebe

SIMBA Sports Club was fortunate to emerge victorious against Ruvu Shooting in the Vodacom Premier League clash, which took place in Dar es Salaam on Saturday, according to the Coast Region squad’s information officer Masau Bwire.

Bwire felt the game was tough for both sides. Contrary to what the official claimed, Simba secured a comfortable 3-0 win thanks to a brace by Miraji Athuman and one goal by Tyrone Santos.

The Msimbazi Street-based club have reclaimed their top spot in the process in the ongoing league season.

Miraji beautifully flicked header from Shomari Kapombe’s cross gave Simba, nicknamed the Msimbazi Reds, the lead on the 37th minute.

Brazilian defender Santos doubled his side's advantage two minutes after the restart, powering home from Francis Kahata’s corner kick delivery that was not well dealt by Ruvu Shooting defense.

Miraji grabbed his brace and his sixth goal of the season when he connected home another good delivery from Kahata 16 minutes from stoppage time.

The results extended Ruvu Shooting’s winless run against Simba to 17 games since September 2010 which is a run of 16 losses and one draw.

In a post-match interview, Bwire was left feeling hard done by after the results. He expressed his belief that luck has played a huge part in Simba's latest victory.

“It is true we have lost against Simba. The game was tough for both sides, not just Ruvu Shooting. I don’t think they have been much better than us. It’s a fortunate result for Simba,’’ Bwire noted.

“Was there any beauty? Did you see any build up in their goals? They scored through counter attacks, I think we matched them in every department but we were unfortunate to lose.’’

The Coast Region-based side has 72 hours to dust themselves off and regroup for Monday’s fierce derby against JKT Tanzania, who also lost their Friday’s match against Young Africans (Yanga).
