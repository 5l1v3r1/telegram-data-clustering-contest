S&P sees recession worries drop

25 Nov 2019, 18:17 by Niv Elis

The chances of a recession in the coming 12 months have dropped, according to S&P Global, which tracks the issue.

"Our recession model, which is based on key financial market indicators up to mid-November, indicates that the probability of a U.S. recession starting in the coming 12 months has moved down to 30%. The probability of recession risk based on this model was 35% in August," said Beth Ann Bovino, the companyâ€™s chief economist for the U.S.

Over the summer, bond markets flashed one of the best-known indicators of a coming recession, sending jitters through the markets. But as bond markets calmed and companies continued to post strong earnings in light of strong consumer demand, anxiety over a downturn eased.

Rate cuts from the Federal Reserve and some signs that the manufacturing sector may be recovering boosted the outlook as well.

Economic growth is still expected to slow in 2020, but the prospect of a recession in a presidential election year would spell trouble for President Trump, who has made economic growth central to his reelection campaign.
