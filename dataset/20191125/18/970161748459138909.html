$9m lottery winner sues for another $10m because draw before his win was rigged

25 Nov 2019, 18:57

A $9 million lottery winner sued for another $10 million after discovering the draw the week before his win was rigged.

Larry Dawson, of Iowa City, filed the lawsuit against The Multi-State Lottery Association after security boss Eddie Tipton installed cheating software.

That allowed Tipton to find out certain winning number combinations ahead of time – including the $16.5 million up for grabs the week before Dawson won in December 2010.

Tipton worked with his brother and others to buy tickets and scoop the sums, with the fraudster jailed for up to 25 years in 2017.

Dawson, 66, argued that that cash could have rolled over into his week had Tipton not stolen it, meaning he was deprived of a far bigger win.

He sued for the lump sum he could have gotten after tax had the $16.5 million been added to his prize pot.

Dawson took home $6 million after tax on the $9 million lump sum.

He has now settled for an undisclosed sum, with all details of the settlement withheld.

Iowa Lottery’s previous CEO Terry Rich tried defending the lawsuit by claiming Dawson wanted to ‘rewrite history.’

He said there was no way of knowing whether someone else could have theoretically chosen to pick the winning numbers the week of Tipton’s fraud

Tipton’s fraud unraveled thanks to his own stupidity, after he bought the $16.5 million winning ticket at a gas station close to lottery headquarters.

He passed the ticket to associates, but the Iowa Lottery failed to pay out after lawyers representing a trust refused to identify the purchase.

Surveillance footage of the winning ticket being bought was then released, with Tipton’s colleagues stunned to realize that he was the buyer.

The cash fraudulently won by Tipton was later returned to the 16 states that participate in the multi-state contest.
