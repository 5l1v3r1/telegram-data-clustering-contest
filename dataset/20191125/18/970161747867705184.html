Emily Eavis drops Glastonbury headliner hints: 'One male, one female'

25 Nov 2019, 18:13

Emily Eavis has dropped major hints about who the remaining Glastonbury Festival headliners will be, and we can’t deal.

The festival organiser has teased it’ll be the pair’s ‘first time’ at Glasto, which actually narrows things down quite a lot.

Virtually every big name in music has played at one time or another.

‘Interestingly, for the other two headliners [in addition to Paul McCartney] it’s their first time at Glastonbury,’ Eavis recently told BBC Radio 2.

‘And we’ve got one male and one female,’ she went on. ‘So there are a couple of clues there.’

‘I’m sure people will guess,’ she then added. ‘So don’t put them to me!’

The bookies favourites to join Beatles icon Paul McCartney as the second and third headliners are Taylor Swift and Kendrick Lamar, according to reports.

Just imagine.

We at least know Taylor will be in Europe around the time of the music extravaganza in Somerset.

Glastonbury 2020 takes place from Wednesday 24 to Sunday 28 June.

Meanwhile, Taylor will be in Berlin, Germany on 24 June and Oslo, Norway on 26 June for her hotly-anticipated Lover Fest tour.

Some 14 shows have been scheduled so far across Europe, the US and Brazil.

Meanwhile Kendrick hasn’t hit the road for a world tour since last year, with the Damn Tour culminating in July last year.

In her interview, Eavis also expressed her excitement about Sir Paul’s appearance for the 50th anniversary of the music extravaganza – his first since 2004.

‘It’s amazing having him back,’ she said. ‘It’s the ultimate way to celebrate.’

We literally can’t wait.
