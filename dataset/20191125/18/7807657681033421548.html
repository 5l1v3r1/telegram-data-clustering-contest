Let's break down what you should search out in a laptop, what's important to look for on Black Friday, and how you can score the best deal.

How do I pick the right laptop?

There are a number of considerations to make when buying a laptop, no matter the day, because you don't want to get stuck with a device that doesn't work for you. Other than avoiding five major mistakes people make on Black Friday, you want to first decide on a form factor. If you're perfectly happy with a clamshell notebook, you can usually save a bit of money by avoiding a convertible or 2-in-1. If you'd rather sometimes (or mostly) use your laptop as a tablet, the extra money usually tacked onto a convertible will be well worth the money.

The next thing you want to consider is display size and resolution. If you're a multitasker, a larger display is generally preferred. It's tough to keep multiple windows open on a 13-inch laptop, whereas a 15- or 17-inch model offers considerably more screen real estate. It's recommended you don't go for less than a 1080p (FHD) resolution no matter how great the deal is, while 1440p (QHD) is a great middle-ground leading up to 4K (UHD). You're going to get the best picture with 4K, but keep in mind that it's generally a significant drain on battery life. If you want your device to last as long as possible between charges, consider a lower resolution and pay attention to battery size and in-depth reviews.

Internal hardware is paramount to a great laptop, but you don't want to buy the most expensive model available in your budget and hope it fits. Buying too much hardware is as frustrating as buying too little hardware. If you don't plan on any specialized tasks like gaming or editing, you probably don't need to spend extra on a laptop with a dedicated graphics card (GPU). Just want to tackle productivity work? Get a modern processor (CPU), about 8GB of RAM, and enough storage to handle your data. Pay attention to whether the laptop includes a hard-disk drive (HDD) or solid-state drive (SSD); the latter is much faster than the former, though prices will rise accordingly.

Keyboard and touchpad can be tough to gauge, especially if you're shopping online, which again is where laptop reviews come into play. Try to find a keyboard with a backlight — consider a number pad if you're working on spreadsheets — and keep in mind that a touchpad with Precision drivers will offer the best pointing experience. As for ports, make sure you have the right I/O for your accessories. If the laptop in question includes USB-C or Thunderbolt 3, you can always check out a quality docking station to expand connectivity.

What do I need to look for in a gaming PC?

Source: Windows Central

Gaming PCs are a special breed of laptop, usually with stylized designs and RGB lighting, high-performance display, plenty of ports, and powerful internal hardware. First, consider how aggressive of a design you're comfortable with. If you're not one for flashing lights and tons of plastic, go with a more reserved build. You'll likely be taking this with you when you hit the road, so be sure to get something you want to show off.

A 1440p display with a 200Hz refresh rate might sound amazing (and it is), but can the performance hardware inside keep up? There's no sense paying for a premium display (especially 4K) if you're going to outfit the laptop with hardware that's better suited for 1080p at a 144Hz refresh rate. FHD and QHD displays are most popular for PC gaming; they'll offer the smoothest picture, especially with G-Sync or FreeSync tech included. 4K might look the best, but your refresh rate will likely top out at 60Hz and you'll need to include powerful hardware to push the resolution.

When buying a gaming laptop, ask yourself what types of games you plan on playing. If you generally stick to indie titles or older games, you can save some money on a lesser dedicated GPU with 2GB or 4GB of VRAM. On the other hand, if you're concerned with modern AAA games that push performance, you'll likely want to add the best hardware possible in your budget.

What are the most important things to look for when comparing models?

New laptops are released constantly, often with only slightly refreshed components to keep up with trends. For example, Microsoft's Surface Pro 7 is a formidable 2-in-1 device with the latest 10th-Gen Intel Core CPUs, a USB-C port, and Wi-Fi 6 connectivity, but otherwise it's basically the same as the older Surface Pro 6. You're still going to get a high-res touch display, premium design, and stellar performance, albeit generally for less than the latest model.

When comparing laptops, especially similar devices from different generations, make sure you're not paying extra for the name alone. Watch out for minor performance refreshes, observe port selection, and note if there are any differences in the display. This goes for laptop models from different manufacturers as well. You might pay more for a laptop with the identical performance from, say, Dell as you would from HP, only because it was released more recently.
