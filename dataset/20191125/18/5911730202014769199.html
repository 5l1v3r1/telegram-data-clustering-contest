At least 3 hurt in partial building collapse in Cincinnati

25 Nov 2019, 18:46 by WCPO Staff

CINCINNATI, Ohio — First responders transported at least three people and are searching for more victims after a development partially collapsed in downtown Cincinnati Monday afternoon, according to Cincinnati police and fire.

Three contractors working on the fifth floor of the 4th and Race Street development were injured after crews on the floor above them poured three feet of concrete, which caused part of the building to collapse, according to Chief Roy Winston.

4th Street is closed between Vine Street and Central Avenue, and Elm Street is closed from 3rd to 5th Streets. The roads will be closed through rush hour and could possibly be closed through the weekend, police said.

The collapse occurred at about 1:15 p.m. As of 2:30 p.m., crews were still chipping at concrete, searching for potential victims.
