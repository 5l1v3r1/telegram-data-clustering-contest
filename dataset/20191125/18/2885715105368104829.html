Forty Years Ago, November 26, 1979: Pakistan’s H-bomb

The Times said that the claim that Pakistan may be just a few months away from the test was made to it by a high-level military source from Islamabad.

25 Nov 2019, 18:46 by Express News Service

Pakistan plans to test a Hydrogen bomb device by next April in spite of the American leaders’ appeal to General Ziaul Haq not to do so, according to the Sunday Times. The Times said that the claim that Pakistan may be just a few months away from the test was made to it by a high-level military source from Islamabad. The paper said evidence about a secret operation by Pakistan in three countries to acquire the necessary nuclear material and know-how has been uncovered. The Americans had estimated that Pakistan was two to three years away from possessing a bomb. Now they say Pakistanis may explode it in the next three or four months.

Mecca Mosque Ablaze

Troops of the Saudi National Guard stormed the Grand Mosque in Mecca during the night, and the remaining gunmen who had been holding out inside the building gave themselves up, Riyadh radio reported. Other reports from Saudi Arabia said there had been heavy fighting with many dead on both sides. There was no information on the fate of hostages still believed to have been held by the gunmen since they took over the mosque early Tuesday. Part of the huge mosque was set to be in flames after the storming. The Saudi troops used heavy weapons in their final assault, diplomatic sources said.

Defection Rumours

Congress President Devraj Urs wrote to Union Industries Minister Brahmananda Reddy, requesting him to clarify if the reports about him joining the Congress-I had any basis. According to a Congress source, Urs said in his letter that he would not like to “give credence” to the rumours and would be grateful if Reddy let him know his position. Reports have appeared in the past two days about Swaran Singh, Reddy and Congress leaders like Shafi Qureshi and K C Pant planning to join the Congress-I. Both Pant and Qureshi have said they will issue statements contradicting the reports, the source said.
