Derry man (35) found unanimously not guilty of raping teen during Spanish holiday

25 Nov 2019, 14:43

A man has been found unanimously not guilty of raping a 17-year-old in the bedroom of a hotel they shared during a holiday in Spain.

Following just 28 minutes of deliberations, a jury at Derry Crown Court acquitted Michael Cregan (35), from Ferguson Street in the city, of a single charge of rape.

The complainant alleged that he raped her as she slept, following a day's drinking, in the room they shared with two other adults in the resort of Torremolinos in August 2014.

The defendant told the jury that they twice had consensual intercourse on the night in question.

It was the first case of its kind in the north in which a UK resident went on trial for an offence allegedly committed outside the jurisdiction.

Following the not guilty verdict, Judge Philip Babington told the defendant he was free to go.

Members of the complainant's family were visibly upset when the jury forewoman announced the not guilty verdict.
