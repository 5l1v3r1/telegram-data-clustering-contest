GrafTech International Ltd (EAF) to Issue Quarterly Dividend of $0.09 on December 31st

25 Nov 2019, 14:19 by Sarita Garza

GrafTech International Ltd (NYSE:EAF) announced a quarterly dividend on Wednesday, November 6th, Zacks reports. Shareholders of record on Friday, November 29th will be paid a dividend of 0.085 per share on Tuesday, December 31st. This represents a $0.34 dividend on an annualized basis and a yield of 2.34%. The ex-dividend date is Wednesday, November 27th.

Shares of NYSE:EAF opened at $14.50 on Monday. The business’s fifty day moving average price is $12.51 and its two-hundred day moving average price is $11.77. The stock has a market cap of $4.18 billion, a PE ratio of 5.05 and a beta of 1.71. GrafTech International has a one year low of $9.60 and a one year high of $16.35.

GrafTech International (NYSE:EAF) last announced its earnings results on Thursday, November 7th. The company reported $0.61 EPS for the quarter, missing analysts’ consensus estimates of $0.65 by ($0.04). The company had revenue of $420.80 million for the quarter, compared to analyst estimates of $441.85 million. GrafTech International had a net margin of 41.87% and a negative return on equity of 97.65%. GrafTech International’s quarterly revenue was down 7.5% compared to the same quarter last year. During the same quarter last year, the company posted $0.67 EPS. As a group, equities analysts anticipate that GrafTech International will post 2.56 EPS for the current year.

A number of equities research analysts have commented on EAF shares. ValuEngine cut GrafTech International from a “hold” rating to a “sell” rating in a report on Saturday. Zacks Investment Research lowered GrafTech International from a “hold” rating to a “strong sell” rating in a research note on Monday, November 11th.

GrafTech International Company Profile

GrafTech International Ltd. researches, develops, manufactures, and sells graphite and carbon based products worldwide. It offers graphite electrodes, which requires for the production of electric arc furnace steel, ferrous, and non-ferrous metals; and petroleum needle coke, a crystalline form of carbon used in the production of graphite electrodes.
