NY inn to pardon turkey named Trump

25 Nov 2019, 14:56 by Judy Kurtz

Trump is getting pardoned this Thanksgiving — by a historic New York inn.

The Milleridge Inn on Long Island will pardon a turkey named Trump at a ceremony this week, it announced Monday.

The gobbler bearing the same name as the commander in chief "will be spared this Thanksgiving holiday to spend the rest of his days on a turkey farm in Virginia, several miles from the White House," according to the inn.

“Trump will be spared from getting his head chopped off and from any impeachment process as he is loved by everyone,” the inn's owner, Butch Yamali, said in a statement.

President Trump and his namesake bird — which is described as a "red-orange headed turkey" — both share a June 14 birthday.

Like the White House, the centuries-old inn has been hosting a turkey pardoning event for years.

The president is poised to pardon a pair of turkeys this year in a Rose Garden ceremony on Tuesday.

Former President George H.W. Bush started the tradition of pardoning turkeys in 1989.
