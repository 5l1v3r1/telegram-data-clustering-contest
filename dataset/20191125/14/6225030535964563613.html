Bodexpress leads the list of late-developing 3-year-olds

25 Nov 2019, 14:25 by Reinier Macatangay

While the older horse division appears depleted, the current 3-year-old crop poised to join those ranks year has showed promise lately, with returning division leaders such as Maximum Security and Code of Honor to be joined by some talented late developers.

Here, we'll look at four sophomores who won recently and should be positioned to make more noise soon.

Bodexpress

After losing his first seven starts, with the streak culminating with those antics at the start of the Preakness Stakes, Bodexpress took a break until an Oct. 14 maiden at Gulfstream Park West. He won easily by three lengths over a soft field.

Next, Bodexpress stepped up to an allowance race over the same track. This time, the field contained notable names in Moretti and Union's Destiny.

Moretti won a maiden race at Aqueduct impressively last December before finishing sixth in the Withers Stakes (G3). Union's Destiny ran sixth in the Fountain of Youth Stakes (G2) and seventh in the Florida Derby, but experienced trouble. On paper, he seemed like a late developer.

Bodexpress blew the field away, though, by 6 3/4 lengths. He won under a vigorous hand ride, while the others spun their wheels.

Bodexpress earned a 111 TimeformUS Speed Figure, adjusted from a raw 115 because of the slow pace. It is notable that Bodexpress earned an unadjusted 116 when second to Maximum Security in the Florida Derby (G1), too.

Given normal growth and maturity, Bodexpress should become even faster next year as a 4-year-old. He is ready for Grade 1 races.
