Football fan stretchered out after being hit face by ball during Albion's match against Sheffield Wednesday

The man is set to receive Conor Townsend's shirt after he was hit in the face by the ball during Saturday's match (November 23)

25 Nov 2019, 14:04 by Charlotte Regen, https://www.birminghammail.co.uk/authors/charlotte-regen/

A man had to be 'stretchered' out of a crowd at a football match after he was smacked in the face by the ball when it was smashed by defender Conor Townsend.

It happened on Saturday (November 23) at The Hawthorns, during West Bromwich Albion's game against Sheffield Wednesday.

Thankfully, the fan hadn't suffered any significant injuries and was discharged at the scene, according to the ambulance service.

An eyewitness told Black Country Live: "There was a fan who was stretchered away from the game after being hit in the face by the ball.

"He was in the Halfords Lane stand near the dug outs. A player belted the ball into the crowd during the second half and it hit him flush in the face. Physios from both teams attended and then paramedics arrived."

Now, West Bromwich Albion are organising for the man to receive the responsible player's shirt for his trouble.

A spokesman for West Bromwich Albion said: "He is fine and left the stadium as normal after being attended to. We are organising to get him Conor Townsend’s shirt for his trouble - it was Conor’s clearance that hit him in the face."

A Spokesman for West Midlands Ambulance Service said: "During Saturday's game between West Bromwich Albion and Sheffield Wednesday a member of the crowd was struck in the face the by the ball.

"He was assessed by ambulance staff who were already at the ground and thankfully hadn't suffered any significant injuries and was discharged at the scene."
