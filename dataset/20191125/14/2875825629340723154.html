Dele Alli says he blamed himself for Mauricio Pochettino getting sacked at Tottenham

Tottenham midfielder Dele Alli says he was very upset when he heard the surprising news that Mauricio Pochettino had been sacked, and couldn't wait to see him for a chat

25 Nov 2019, 14:54 by Irishmirror.ie

Dele Alli says he blamed himself when Mauricio Pochettino was sacked as Tottenham boss.

Pochettino was relieved of his duties at Spurs last Tuesday, after five years at the club, and replaced by Jose Mourinho.

The Tottenham players went to see Pochettino at his house after the news was announced, and Alli said he and the Argentine sat down and spoke as friends.

"I think it's so clear what Mauricio meant to the players," said Alli.

"He helped me grow a lot. I came here as an 18-year-old and had my ups and downs here. When I found out I was upset and I wanted to speak to him. I went to see him.

"I've seen him more than my family over the past five years. After I heard the news I couldn't wait to see him. I was very upset.

"It wasn't so much a conversation about football more two friends having a chat."

Pochettino was sacked following a poor run of results, with Tottenham floundering outside of the top four places having picked up just 14 points in 12.

Alli featured just five times under Pochettino in the league this season, but says he initially felt partly responsible for the Argentine getting the sack.

Alli added: "I would be lying if I didn't blame myself when it first happened rather than looking at the bigger picture. We had a great journey. You can't help but feel like you're to blame because you're out there but I realised I gave 110 per cent always.

"We've worked with Mauricio for so long. It's new to a lot of us. The squad we had was a lot of young players coming in and it's the first time we've had a change in manager.

"We know where we should be and the talent we have. It's important that we show everyone. This season doesn't show how good we are as a squad and that's why we're not worried.

"You want to impress but at the same time you just want to win games. We've always given everything to win."
