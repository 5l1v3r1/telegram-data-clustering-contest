The fascinating tale of woman who lived in the ruins of her beloved seaside village

Elizabeth Prettejohn refused to leave Hallsalls even while sea wiped away the vast majority of her village

25 Nov 2019, 14:08 by Colleen Smith, Lea Nakache

This is the story of an incredible woman who lost everything to the elements… but decided to stay and live in the ruins of her seaside village.

Hallsands was a small fishing village on the Devon coast.

A community of 79 villagers inhabited the hamlet, made up of a small number of houses, a shop and a tiny pub, the London Inn.

Elizabeth Prettejohn was born in this pub up on the cliff, reports Devon Live. She had lived a peaceful life of fishing alongside the men, following in the footsteps of generations of women before her.

But on the fateful night of January 26, 1917, everything fell apart.

Around 8pm, a terrible storm battered Hallsands and its inhabitants were forced to flee, only to watch their beloved village crumble to pieces, washed out into the ocean.

By midnight, 37 houses had been destroyed, as well as the shop… and the London Inn.

Miraculously, all the inhabitants were safe and sound.

Most of the villagers were relocated by the government, but one stubborn family decided to stay - the Prettejohns.

Elizabeth, then 33, decided to stay with her family, and to live in the only relatively safe cottage, which she renamed Sea View.

Old British Pathe footage even described her as the “last inhabitant and keeper of memories [...] refusing to give away to the irresistible advance of the sea”.
