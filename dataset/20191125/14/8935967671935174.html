Orange Spain officially launches bank service

25 Nov 2019, 14:49

Orange has announced the commercial launch of its mobile banking service in Spain following a trial period and around two years after the platform was rolled out in France. In a statement, the operator said Orange Bank combines the attributes of a traditional bank with a mobile offering delivered via a dedicated app for Android and Apple smartphones. All Orange Spain mobile customers who sign up to the service will have access to a Spanish IBAN, a Mastercard debit card with Apple Pay compatibility and the possibility of earning a 1 percent interest rate on savings of up to EUR 20,000. Orange said it also plans to eventually offer customer loans of up to EUR 10,000 at an attractive interest rate.

Other advantages include mobile payment via NFC-enabled phones, free SEPA transfers and the availability of bank agents 24 hours a day, all via the Orange Bank app. By way of a promotion, Orange Spain is offering subscribers to its mobile (Go) and convergent (Love) plans a discount of EUR 30 on their subsequent invoice if they sign up to Orange Bank.

Orange said the banking subsidiary will have around 100 employees and will be headed by Narciso Perales, who previously held several positions at Bankinter, including head of digital transformation. The operator is aiming for profitability in less than five years and a million Orange Bank customers within 10 years. 

In France, the parent company recently announced that Orange Bank has passed the milestone of 500,000 active customers and is signing up new customers at the rate of over 20,000 per month.
