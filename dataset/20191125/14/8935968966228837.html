Last week in telecoms: another disastrous week for Intelsat; European incumbents seeking to list more units

25 Nov 2019, 14:30

Diminishing chances for international satellite operators for a windfall on US 5G spectrum holdings drove Intelsat (-47%), SES (-15%) and Eutelsat (-10%) down for a second straight week. The incumbent operators appear to be looking increasingly at spinning off networks and country operations into listed subsidiaries, including Deutsche Telekom, Orange and Telecom Italia/TIM. The Telecompaper European Telecom Services index was down 0.1 percent in week 47, against a loss of 0.7 percent for the EuroStoxx 50. The German conglomerate 3U was the biggest winner, up 10 percent.

First of all, the earnings seasons was coming to an end with the Q3 results from A3 (Sweden) and MTS (Russia).

5G spectrum hopes imploding

The FCC proposed a public auction for 5G spectrum currently held by Intelsat, SES, Eutelsat and Telesat, bypassing offers from these operators to share auction proceeds. Intelsat was the biggest casualty (-47%), as in week 46 (-44%). The stock now trades at around USD 7, a level not seen since March 2018. It peaked at almost USD 35 in October 2018.

Incumbents listing units

Asset sales and consolidation appear to increasingly go hand in hand with the creation of listed subsidiaries. The large incumbents (Deutsche Telekom, Orange, TIM) already have a number of these and more could be added. Inwit, the tower unit controlled by TIM, approved the proposed merger with Vodafone Italy's towers. Orange was considering listing its African and Middle Eastern operations, and Deutsche Telekom could seek an IPO of its Dutch subsidiary, if rumours are true. DT's Deutsche Funkturm (mobile towers) could also be spun off.

The Zayo Group, itself subject to a bid, was considering the sale of its colocation business.

YTD: infrastructure winning

Looking ahead to the 2019 performance of the European telecom stocks, we so far have Greek ISP Forthnet (+307%) in the lead, followed by Altice Europe (+206%). Next come Turk Telekom (+82%), 3U (+80%), Intred (+73%) and Cellnex (+67%). Among the weakest shares are the aforementioned satellite operators and a number of German challengers.

Looking at a number of segments, we notice that most include both winners and losers, whether challengers, fixed operators, incumbents, mobile operators, FMC plays or even satellite groups. There is just a single exception, the group of infrastructure plays. All the stocks in this segment have positive year-to-date performances, which are also ahead of our index (+9.3% YTD). This segment contains:

  • Data centres: Digital Realty (+12%), Interxion (+58%), Equinix (+58%)
  • Fibre: Retelit (+35%), Zayo (+50%), Intred (+73%)
  • Masts: Inwit (+48%), Cellnex (+67%)
