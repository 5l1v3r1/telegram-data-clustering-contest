Jury selected to try teenager over killing of Arnold teenager Jaden Moodie

The teenager had moved from Arnold to London six months before his death

25 Nov 2019, 14:00 by Emily Pennink, PA Old Bailey Correspondent, Matthew Bunn

A jury panel has been selected to try a teenager for the murder of a 14-year-old boy from Arnold.

Jaden Moodie was fatally injured in Bickley Road, Leyton, east London, on January 8. The former Redhill Academy student had moved to London six months previously to be closer to his grandmother.

Ayoub Majdouline, 19, from Wembley, north London, faces trial at the Old Bailey charged with Jaden's murder and possession of a knife.

On Monday, prosecutor Oliver Glasgow QC told potential jurors: "The allegation in this case concerns the murder of a 14-year-old boy, Jaden Moodie.

"The incident took place on January 8 this year in Bickley Road, Leyton in E10."

After being selected, Judge Richard Marks QC sent the jury panel away until Wednesday morning when Mr Glasgow is expected to open the case for the prosecution.

The defendant has denied the charges against him.
