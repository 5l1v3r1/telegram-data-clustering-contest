Liverpool boss Jurgen Klopp only saw Vincent Kompany screamer because of his wife

The former Manchester City captain's stunning strike against Leicester City last season effectively won his side the Premier League at Liverpool's expense

25 Nov 2019, 14:20 by Irishmirror.ie

Liverpool manager Jurgen Klopp admitted he had not intended to watch Manchester City's crucial game against Leicester last season but was convinced to do so by his wife.

City were two points behind the Reds with a game in hand going into the last week of the campaign, meaning their game against the Foxes was effectively a title-decider.

Until the 70th minute it looked as though Klopp's side may have been handed the advantage in the race, with the scores level at 0-0.

But Vincent Kompany then stepped up and smashed in a 25-yard screamer to break Liverpool hearts, and the 1-0 win coupled with City's 4-1 victory over Brighton on the final day of the season meant the Merseysider's wait for a top flight title was extended to 29 years.

Klopp attended the Football Writers’ Association northern branch managers’ awards dinner in Manchester on Sunday evening, and according to The Athletic the German admitted it was the most nerve-wracking game he experienced all season.

He allegedly only watched the match because his wife had suggested he should do so, and said: "this game, second by second by second I started... hoping."

Although their title race ended in heartbreak, there was to be a bright side for Klopp and his side as they went on to beat Tottenham to win the Champions League in June.

And at the same awards dinner Pep Guardiola reportedly joked "maybe we can swap?" with regard to the two trophies, with he and his City side known to be desperate for European glory while Liverpool are more focused on winning the league.

Both Liverpool and City won at the weekend, with Klopp's team overcoming Crystal Palace and City beating Crystal Palace, and they are both in action in the Champions League this week against Napoli and Shakhtar Donetsk respectively.
