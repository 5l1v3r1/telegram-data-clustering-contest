Man arrested for string of offences in Radford including possession of a firearm

'Offences like these can have a huge impact on victims'

25 Nov 2019, 14:26 by Phoebe Ram

A man has been arrested after a string of incidents including possession of a firearm, assault and witness intimidation.

Officers arrested the 31-year-old man on suspicion of possession of a firearm and other offences in connection with multiple incidents.

All the incidents happened in Radford throughout November 2019.

On Saturday, November 9 a witness intimidation report was made to Nottinghamshire Police after an incident in St Peter's Street, Radford.

The firearms offence relates to a report of a man possessing a firearm in Radford on Sunday, November 17.

On November 19, officers were called to Midland Way in Radford after reports of assault following a theft in the same street.

The theft from motor vehicle offence is in connection with a report of a man allegedly taking the tailgate off of a Mitsubishi L200 in Manston Mews, Radford on Monday, November 25.

Inspector Ruby Burrow, of Nottinghamshire Police, said: “Offences like these can have a huge impact on victims and we are committed to investigating all reports of crime thoroughly as a result.

“It’s important that we find those responsible for violent crime as soon as possible to make sure that appropriate action is taken.”
