ITV I'm A Celebrity... Get Me Out Of Here! starving stars on the brink as tears, missing beans and low morale threaten camp

"People are going crazy."

25 Nov 2019, 22:00 by Rebecca Koncienzcy

Morale of the I'm A Celebrity... Get Me Out Of Here! campmates had fallen to new lows in tonight's episode.

The ITV reality show set in the jungles of Australia started off with tears from Coronation Street actor Andrew Whyment, 38, who said how much he was missing his kids.

Tonight we saw him wiping away tears in the Bush Telegraph as he pinned his hopes on the team getting care packages so he could have a picture from home.

He said: "I hope that we get the 12 stars and the 12 packages and today will be a different day and everyone will be happy.

"I'm right at the bottom of the roast dinner list but to just get a picture from home. I miss my kids so much.

"Hopefully we'll get the 12 packages because I think everyone would like a picture of home so if that could happen I think it would be the best day in here by far."

And he wasn't the only one struggling with the pressures of camp life.

Girls Aloud singer Nadine Coyle, 34, said people are "going crazy" with difficulties of living with the pure basics.

She said: "I sense if we do get it people are going to be really happy because it's now day 765 in the jungle and people are going crazy."

Presenter Kate Garraway echoed Nadine's thoughts as the team psyched themselves up for the live trial.

The 52-year-old said: "I think we all need a boost basically so if we could win these care packages I think it would be just what the camp needs."

While Andy, Kate, Nadine and Rak-Su singer Myles Stephenson set out on a mission to get the care packages to help bring the camp morle levels up, the other hungry campmates discovered missing food.

Andrew Maxwell, 44, was hoping the team win all 12 stars so that he can fill up on a roast dinner.

He has had a particularly difficult few days after he came to blows with former footballer Ian Wright, 56, and questioned his decision to take part in the show.

But he and Ian were shocked when  ex-England rugby player James Haskell found 21 packets of beans in the ration chest.

Andrew said: "It’s madness. We had such a tiny portion of beans yesterday.

"We didn’t need to go hungry at all yesterday."

Luckily for the celebs, the team did amazingly well in the challenges and all 12 care packages were delivered, including a full roast dinner.

After seeing the feast, Capital Radio DJ Roman Kemp said: "I thought it was a mirage. I can’t help but think about Ian, Cliff and Andy [who missed out on the dinner] but I also can’t help but think about cauliflower cheese."

When they got back to camp James handed out the care packages and the celebrities read each other’s notes from home, looked at family photos and enjoyed little food treats which resulted in tears, laughter and joy at the sight of their chosen sugar or savoury bites.
