Westlife grant terminally ill fan's wish to meet them and it's a moving story

25 Nov 2019, 22:51

Westlife have granted the wish of a terminally ill fan battling cancer by inviting her backstage to meet them at a recent show.

The boys – Markus Feehily, Shane Filan, Nicky Byrne and Kian Egan – met Mairead Nolan in Dublin on Friday night.

The super fan was diagnosed with terminal ovarian cancer last October.

Mairead says she was invited backstage at RTE Studios, where the guys – known for hits like Mandy and Flying Without Wings – were rehearsing for their Late Late Show performance.

Speaking to the Irish Sunday Mirror, Mairead, from Cork, said: ‘I am in floods of tears after meeting them, I was just so excited. It was better than my wildest dreams and it has given me a lift and the push I need to fight the cancer.’

She furthermore explained: ‘I was shaking with nerves, I was worried about what way they might be towards me but they were calling my name, they knew all about me.

‘They came over and were hugging me, kissing me and taking selfies. It was the best day of my life.

‘I can die happy now, if something happens to me at least I know I have met them and they were so nice to me.’

What an inspiring story. And they say you should never meet your idols.

The guys’ act of kindness follows a blazing music comeback for the four-piece, which last week culminated in them scoring Ireland’s year’s fastest-selling album.

Spectrum is their 11th studio LP and first since 2010. It was preceded by the hit singles Hello My Love and Better Man, released earlier this year.

Congratulations, guys. All this success couldn’t be happening to nicer people.
