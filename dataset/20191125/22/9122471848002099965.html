ESPN’s Prediction For Tonight’s Rams-Ravens Game

25 Nov 2019, 22:45 by Matt Hladik

Week 12 of the NFL season concludes tonight with a Monday Night Football matchup between the Baltimore Ravens and Los Angeles Rams.

Tonight’s game features two teams with quarterbacks playing on opposite ends of the spectrum. Baltimore’s Lamar Jackson is performing at an MVP-level, while the Rams’ Jared Goff has been struggling immensely.

At 8-2, the Ravens are right now the No. 2 seed in the AFC, and they have a win over the top-seeded Patriots on their resume. The Rams are 6-4 after beating the Bears last week and must win to stay in the thick of a tight NFC playoff race.

Translation: there’s plenty at stake tonight. ESPN has released its prediction for the game.

Despite being on the road, the Ravens are slightly favored by the computer.

Baltimore has a 52.5 percent chance of winning, according to the FPI. The Rams are given a 47.1 percent shot, with a 0.4 percent chance of a tie.

Rams-Ravens will kick off at 8:15 p.m. ET from Los Angeles.

You can catch the action on ESPN.
