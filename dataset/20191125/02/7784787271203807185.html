Wests Tigers sweat on Latrell Mitchell as NRL rival enters the race

25 Nov 2019, 02:14 by Staff writers

The Wests Tigers are now facing competition to sign Roosters star Latrell Mitchell despite the North Queensland Cowboys effectively pulling out of the race.

Mitchell had been heavily-linked with a switch to either the Tigers or the Cowboys after meeting with North Queensland officials in Townsville last week.

On Sunday, however, the Cowboys confirmed Valentine Holmes would be joining the club on a six-year deal. It means they have no space in their salary cap to accommodate the sort of money Mitchell is after.

But with Tigers in the box seat, having tabled a four-year, $3.8 million offer for Mitchell, a new rival has entered the race.

The Daily Telegraph reports NRL basket case Gold Coast Titans want to lure Mitchell north and make him their marquee player.

While the Titans are yet to meet with Mitchell, head of performance and culture Mal Meninga has publicly declared the club is in the market for a big-name signing as they look to put a disastrous 2019 season behind them.

Despite the competition, the Tigers are still confident of getting their man. They will grant the 22-year-old hiswish and hand him the No. 1 jersey and believe the club could offer the type of off-field stability to take his game to the next level.

But there could be a late twist.

The Tigers fear South Sydney could enter the race at the 11th hour and poach Mitchell from under their noses.

The Rabbitohs are seeking salary cap compensation following the retirement of Sam Burgess and, if granted, could use the cash to make a play for Mitchell.

A decision on his future is expected this week.
