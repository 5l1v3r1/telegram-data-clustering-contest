Gigastorage focuses on PV power generation projects

25 Nov 2019, 02:55 by Nuying Huang, Taipei; Adam Hwang, DIGITIMES

Gigastorage has transformed from a solar-grade poli-Si wafer producer into a company handling EPC (engineering, procurement, construction) for PV power generation projects, according to company chairman Jimmy Chen.

So far in 2019, Gigastorage has obtained EPC contracts with total installation capacity of 27.461MWp and completed 19.416MWp, of which 17.828MWp has been on grid, Chen said.

In Taiwan, new PV power generation projects to come into construction in 2020 are estimated to add total installation caapcity of 2,500MWp, and Gigastorage aims to share 50-60MWp (2-2.4%), Chen noted.

Gigastorage has also begun production of semiconductor-grade test and dummy wafers - mainly 8-inch ones, Chen indicated.

Gigastorage posted consolidated revenues of NT$1.905 billion (US$62.5 million), gross margin of 12.48%, net operating loss of NT$49.3 million, net loss of NT$15.4 million and net loss per share of NT$0.07 for third-quarter 2019, leading to consolidated revenues of NT$6.368 billion, gross margin of 11.81%, net operating loss of NT$21.2 million, net profit of NT$217.2 million and net EPS of NT$1.05 for January-September.

Gigastorage has reported consolidated revenues of NT$635.1 million for October, slipping 14.78% sequentially but growing 15.03% on year, and those of NT$7.007 billion for January-October dipped 13.70% on year.
