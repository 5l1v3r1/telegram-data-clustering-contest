UK weather forecast: Storm Sebastien to lash Britain with 22ft waves

The Met Office has issued a 'danger to life' flooding warning and two other rain alerts for the next three days - south-west England will see the worst of the weather

25 Nov 2019, 05:34 by Matthew Dresch, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/matthew-dresch/

Storm Sebastien will reportedly bring 22ft waves, at least two inches of rain and 50mph winds to the UK over the next few days.

Huge waves are expected to crash into the coast of south-west England today as remnants of the storm make landfall.

The Met Office has issued three rain warnings for the next three days.

The first, which started yesterday afternoon and is due to end at 10am today, affects south-west England and south Wales, with up to 25mm of rain expected in areas.

Another alert will be in force across south-west, south and south-east England from noon tomorrow to 3pm on Wednesday.

Forecasters have predicted up to 60mm of rain in some spots, equating to roughly two weeks of rain.

The final 'danger to life' flooding warning will be in place in Yorkshire from the midnight to 6pm on Wednesday.

As much as 60mm of rain could hit parts of the region, almost half of the 121mm average total for November.

Met Office forecaster Marco Petagna told The Sun : “Remnants of Tropical Storm Sebastien mean it will be windy on Tuesday into Wednesday with gales in the South and South-West, and high spring tides.

“It's not a pretty picture, with warnings for rain for the next few days after some places had three times their normal rain in the first half of the month.

“Colder air arrives at the end of the week, perhaps generally hanging on for the first half of December.”

Tonight (Monday night): A misty and cloudy night, with showers about. These showers will be scattered throughout the UK, but will remain mostly light in nature.

"Into the early hours of the morning, rain will move into the south west of the UK, with some heavier showers in Northern Scotland as well. Moderate southerly winds.

Tomorrow (Tuesday): Rain will move north-east across many areas tomorrow. Some of this will be heavy, especially in southern England, where thunder us possible. Showers will follow into the south-west, where it may become very windy for a time. Drier for northern Scotland.

UK Outlook (Wednesday and Thursday): Further outbreaks of rain on Wednesday in places, especially in southern England and northern Scotland.. Thursday will showery rain sinking slowly southwards across England and Wales.

"Turning colder across Scotland with a few wintry showers in the north and north-east."

The five-day forecast

Today

Mostly cloudy with rain moving northeast across Britain, this heavy at first across southern England and Wales, and followed by some bright or sunny spells and showers. Probably staying dry in far north of Scotland.

Tonight

Outbreaks of rain continuing across northern Britain, with showers dying out after midnight across central and southern areas. However, further heavy rain and strong winds reaching the southwest by dawn.

Tuesday

A band of occasionally heavy rain will push north across the UK. Scattered heavy and possibly thundery showers follow with strong winds in south and west.

Outlook for Wednesday to Friday

Wet and windy weather gradually clearing away eastwards during Wednesday night and Thursday morning. Becoming drier for most by Friday and turning colder with some sunshine.
