Commodity outlook: Moong dives 3%; here’s how other commodities will fare today

Here is how SMC Global expects commodities to fare today.

25 Nov 2019, 05:23 by ETMarkets.com

New Delhi: Commodity futures on domestic bourses were trading mixed on Monday after the United States and China expressed willingness to sign an initial trade deal by the year-end.

Energy and precious metals were in the red on MCX while base metals advanced. Crude oil was down 0.43 per cent to Rs 4,151 while silver slipped 0.5 per cent to Rs 44,265. Among base metals, copper added 0.29 per cent to Rs 431.25. Zinc was up 0.4 per cent to Rs 188.45.

Agri commodities were also mixed with castor seed, cottonseed, guar, moong and chana losing in early trade on NCDEX. On the other hand, jeera, turmeric, soybean and mustard seed were trading with gains.

Here is how SMC Global expects commodities to fare today:

Bullion: Gold can move lower towards Rs 37,600 while facing resistance near Rs 38,100 and silver can test Rs 44,000 while facing resistance near Rs 44,600.

Base metals: Base metals may trade with a sideways-to-weak bias. Copper may dip towards Rs 428 while facing resistance near Rs 435. Zinc can dip towards Rs 186 as it can face resistance near Rs 190. Lead may trade sideways as it can take support near Rs 153 and face resistance at Rs 155. Nickel may dip lower towards Rs 1,060 while facing resistance near Rs 1,090. Aluminum may slip lower Rs 128 while facing resistance near Rs 131.

Energy: Crude oil may witness further upside movement. The commodity can move upwards to Rs 4,200 while taking support near Rs 4,120. Natural gas can open on a positive note testing Rs 195 while taking support near Rs 186.

Spices: The correction phase of turmeric futures (Dec) would possibly take a pause and find support near Rs 5,530-5,500. Jeera futures (Dec) may witness consolidation in a wider range of Rs 15,700-16,200. Dhaniya futures (Dec) are likely to consolidate in the range of Rs 6,920-7,310. Cardamom futures (Dec) are expected to come down towards Rs 2,650.

Oilseeds: Soybean futures (Dec) are expected to consolidate in the range of Rs 3,950-4,065 with upside getting capped. The outlook of mustard futures (Dec) is bullish as it may trade higher to test Rs 4,380-4,420, taking support near Rs 4,275. CPO futures (Dec) are expected to trade sideways in the broader range of Rs 660-680. Soy oil futures (Dec) are expected to trade sideways in the range of Rs 801-818.

Other commodities: Cotton futures (Nov) will most probably witness correction towards Rs 18,930 tracking bearishness in the overseas market. Chana futures (Dec) are expected to consolidate in the range of Rs 4,470-4,590. Mentha oil futures (Nov) are likely to trade with a weak bias to test Rs 1,230-1,240.
