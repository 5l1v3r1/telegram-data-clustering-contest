Sharad Pawar reveals disagreement with Sena, Congress over sharing CM post led to delay in govt formation

Sharad Pawar said on Monday that the three parties couldn't agree on sharing the CM post equally and there were differences.

25 Nov 2019, 05:21 by Pankaj P. Khelkar

HIGHLIGHTS

  • Sharad Pawar has said there was no consensus between the 3 parties over power sharing
  • Sharad Pawar also said he would never betray Shiv Sena because he made a promise
  • Pawar also said three parties forming govt need time to iron out differences

NCP chief Sharad Pawar has finally come clean on why the three parties took so much time to arrive at a consensus on government formation in Maharashtra. Pawar said on Monday the three parties couldn't agree on sharing the CM post equally and there were differences.

Speaking at an event in Maharashtra's Karad, Sharad Pawar said, "We had demanded 50-50 sharing of the CM's post and there were differences among the parties. There was no consensus."

Two days after his nephew sprung a surprise and foiled all plans of the NCP to form the government with Shiv Sena and Congress, Sharad Pawar has finally spoken on the delay in staking claim, which gave enough time for Ajit Pawar to orchestrate the coup that left everyone shocked.

On being asked if he Ajit Pawar's move has his backing, Sharad Pawar said, "I have come much ahead with Shiv Sena. I would have taken my partymen in confidence if I had such plans."

He added, "I cannot do anything bad with Shiv Sena as I promised them. We have to run the government for 5 years with three parties, we couldn't act in a hurry."

NCP and Congress leaders held several rounds of meetings over the past week since the President's rule was imposed in Maharashtra on November 12. Soon after BJP refused to form the government, Shiv Sena reached out to NCP-Congress, however, even after almost two weeks, the three parties couldn't stake claim to form the government.

While insiders from the meetings suggested Congress demanded the Deputy CM's post and consensus on the Common Minimum Programme (CMP), it is clear now that NCP and Shiv Sena too couldn't agree on the power sharing formula.

Sharad Pawar made the big revelation at the death anniversary event of former Maharashtra Chief Minister Yashwantrao Balwantrao Chavan at Karad on Monday.

Meanwhile, NCP, Congress and Shiv Sena leaders submitted a letter of support to Governor BS Koshyari on Monday morning ahead of the crucial Supreme Court hearing of their petition.

The parties had moved the court seeking an immediate floor test in the Maharashtra assembly.
