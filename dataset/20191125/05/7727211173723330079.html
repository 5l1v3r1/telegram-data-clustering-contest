'How dare that teacher sexually assault my daughter!' says outraged mum of girl abused by school paedophile

Charles Ellis, who abused six children, would brazenly sneak his hands down girls' clothing during lessons

25 Nov 2019, 05:00 by Luke Traynor

A mum told of her horror after her primary school daughter was sexually assaulted in front of other children during lessons by her teacher.

On Thursday, Charles Ellis, now 60, was found guilty of molesting six girls, over an eight-year period, when they were aged eight or nine.

The teacher, who was employed at a primary school in Wirral, targeted the pupils during class.

The abuse happened when the pupils were with Ellis at his desk or at their own desks.

Several pupils were assaulted on a number of occasions.

The pervert touched girls inappropriately by putting his hand on their backs under their clothing, touching bottoms under and over clothing and touching their legs, prosecutors at Liverpool Crown Court said.

Today, the mum who first blew the whistle on Ellis and whose daughter first complained to the school and police about the teacher's conduct, described him as a "danger to children".

She told the ECHO: "Ellis kept on singling her out.

"He would ask why she was not going to after school club.

"I would have regular arguments with him in the playground about his behaviour.

"I went to the headteacher with my concerns."

Her daughter confessed to her mum years later what Ellis had done.

The teacher would approach her in class, putting his hand down the back of her dress and inside her knickers.

But the child was too young to understand what was happening, so she remained silent.

The parent added: "Ellis is not getting away with what he did to those children.

"How dare he do these things?

"I want everyone to know what he has done."

Ellis, of Oakfield Drive, Upton, Chester, was warned to expect a jail sentence when he returns for his sentencing next month.

He was employed at the school since 2002.

One of the victims complained to her mum about his behaviour and in 2013 he was spoken to by police.

But no prosecution was brought and he was given further training about not touching children inappropriately.

The paedophile was "motivated by sexual gratification” and "abused his position of trust", the trial heard.

A member of staff had also twice reported feeling uncomfortable about his touching some of the girl pupils.

He was suspended by the school in April 2017.

Charlotte Kenny, prosecuting, said that Ellis, who has no previous convictions, was “a man who appears not to be able to control himself”.

The mum added: "If it wasn't for my daughter coming forward, Ellis would still be abusing children now.

"She finally found the courage to speak out when she saw Ellis outside our house, talking to the caretaker, and she blurted out 'I can't believe he's still a teacher at that school'."

The girl confided in her mum, who says she was "devastated" by the allegations.

She added: "How can Ellis live with himself, knowing what he's done to these kids?

"If he had those urges, he should have never become a teacher.

"He should be taken out of society.

"My daughter has had to go to counselling and she has self-harmed.

"She's on anti-depressant tablets.

"How can he sleep at night?"
