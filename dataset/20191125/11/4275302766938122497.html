Seriously-injured man rescued from Kirriemuir factory building

A man is being treated for a serious injury in Ninewells Hospital after being rescued from a factory building in Kirriemuir.

25 Nov 2019, 11:01 by Peter John Meiklem

Police closed Marywell Brae, near the J&D Wilkie factory, after 6pm on Sunday evening after reports of a man inside the building.

Firefighters attended the scene and deployed a 13.5 metre ladder in the rescue, before using oxygen, and passing the injured man to waiting paramedics.

The ambulance transported him to Ninewell’s Hospital, where he was treated for a serious leg injury.

A police spokesman said: “A man was rescued from a window by emergency services at factory premises in Marywell Brae, Kirriemuir.

“He was taken to Ninewells Hospital where he is being treated for a serious leg injury.

A Scottish fire and rescue spokeswoman said one appliance had been sent to the incident.

“We received the call at 17:58. We worked with Scottish Ambulance Service colleagues. One male casualty was removed from the scene,” she added.
