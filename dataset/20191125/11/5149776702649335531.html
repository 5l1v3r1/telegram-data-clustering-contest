Mansfield General Election candidates explain why you should vote for them

All five candidates have explained why they should get your support

25 Nov 2019, 11:57 by Kit Sandeman

The General Election candidates standing in Mansfield have set out why people should vote for them, and what they would like to achieve if elected.

Conservative Ben Bradley pulled off a major upset here last time round, gaining a seat which in living memory had only ever been held by Labour.

This time, there are five candidates, two of whom are standing as independents in a heavily Leave-leaning area.

We’ve asked all the candidates standing why people should vote for them.

Ben Bradley, Conservative

I’ve been the Member of Parliament for Mansfield since the 2017 election, when I won an historic victory as the first ever Conservative to win the seat, ending nearly 100 years of Labour representation.

Since entering Parliament I’ve been a vocal campaigner for Brexit and have always voted to leave the EU at the earliest opportunity, backing my constituents who voted 71 percent to leave.

My key local priorities have been town centre regeneration, transport links and education, and I’ve made major progress in all three areas.

I secured an [up to] £25m ‘Town Deal’ to boost Mansfield’s local economy along with a commitment to hundreds of new police officers to keep the town safe, over £20m of Government cash for the A38, A617 and A614 as well as big improvement plans on the Robin Hood Rail line, and a £14bn increase in funding for schools while serving on the Education Select Committee in Parliament.

Sarah Brown, Liberal Democrat

I am an HR Professional who joined the Liberal Democrats in 2013.

I’ve been campaigning for several years to remain in the European Union and am strongly behind the Liberal Democrats policy to revoke Article 50 and stop Brexit.

People who voted remain in Mansfield and across the UK are being ignored by both Labour and the Conservatives who are determined to delivery Brexit at any cost.

Brexit has proved itself to be far from what was promised by Boris Johnson and the leave campaign and will cause chaos up and down our country.

Stephen Harvey, Independent

In common with many previous Mansfield MPs I was a local coal miner, then a Mansfield District Councillor before I became Chairman of Mansfield District Council.

I’m also one of only two candidates that actually live in Mansfield.

I’ve consistently campaigned for the delivery of Brexit and am ashamed of our current Parliamentarians.

I have a strong record of defending Mansfield and district taxpayers’ money.

I have directly changed major government policies that have positively affected multi-million pound investments locally.

As the two main parties push more towards the extremities the vast majority of Mansfield and District residents tell me they desperately need a centre ground politician.

This election is not just about Brexit, but also about the NHS, social care, crime, the environment and education all require a huge amount of work.

A vote for an independent means that I can put Mansfield before any political party interests.

Sid Pepper, Independent

Like it or not, the reality is that this election is about Brexit.

All because dishonourable MPs across parliament decided not to honour our democratic vote.

I have stood twice before on a Brexit platform, to see my core voters ‘lend’ their votes in 2017 to the Conservatives, who promised to deliver Brexit.

So I have to stand again, if only to remind voters of the promises made and not delivered by the Tory government.

Again their main pitch is to deliver Brexit. But Johnson’s treaty is not Brexit, so for a second time they are promising something they won’t deliver.

Nationally, I pledge to support the Brexit you voted for and make the Conservatives deliver on this promise.

Locally, I would work to stop the decline of Mansfield, making our market future proof, by moving the stalls undercover – the Old Town Hall, then using the Square for free central parking.

Sonya Ward, Labour

I have lived in Mansfield for more than three decades, and worked across Mansfield, Woodhouse and Warsop as a youth worker, for the NHS and for the Open University.

Mansfield is a great place to live in so many ways, we’ve got wonderful people and great community spirit.

But, I’m concerned nothing has replaced what was lost with the decline of industry.

Wages here are £6,400 below the national average.

Low pay means less disposable income in people’s pockets, and that impacts on our town centres and marketplaces.

It’s hard for young people to find good quality employment here, but under a Labour government, with a new focus on manufacturing in Britain, there will be new opportunities.

We need to be able to look forward with hope, and we can only do that with a government and an MP who will fight for investment not further cuts.

Mansfield was once a thriving district, and it can be again. Back me to back Mansfield.
