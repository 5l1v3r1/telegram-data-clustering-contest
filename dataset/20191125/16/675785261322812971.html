Jose Mourinho makes request to Jorge Mendes after discussing Tottenham's transfer plans

Tottenham boss Jose Mourinho is already eyeing transfer targets - and he's made a request to Jorge Mendes regarding Bruno Fernandes and Ruben Dias

25 Nov 2019, 16:05 by Rich Jones, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/rich-jones/

Jose Mourinho has reportedly asked Jorge Mendes to keep him informed over Portuguese duo Ruben Dias and Bruno Fernandes as he plots January transfer business.

Mourinho is monitoring potential targets after taking over at Tottenham despite getting his reign off to a winning start at West Ham on Saturday.

Portugal internationals Dias and Fernandes have both been linked with Premier League moves - and Mourinho is keen to use his connections to monitor the chances of bringing them to north London.

Both are represented by super agent Mendes, who also represents Mourinho, and 90min claim Mendes has been asked to keep him informed on the future plans of both players.

Fernandes was heavily linked with a move to Manchester United in the summer before a late bid from Spurs was rejected by Sporting Lisbon.

But he is still being tipped to move to the Premier League having been vocal about his desire to play in England in the past.

With Christian Eriksen's future still uncertain, Fernandes could fit the bill as Mourinho eyes another creative midfielder.

Centre-back Dias, 22, has been linked with Manchester United, Manchester City and Arsenal in recent months.

His current deal, until 2024, includes a release clause worth around £59million, although Benfica have looked to improve his terms to raise the buyout to around £79m.

With both players in-demand, Mourinho has asked Mendes to provide him with the latest updates so he can decide whether to pull the trigger on a move, or risk waiting until the summer to conduct major transfer business.

Reports have suggested both players feature on a three-man transfer shortlist handed to Daniel Levy by Mourinho when he took the job, along with Manchester United midfielder Nemanja Matic.

His business could also be effected by whether he can successfully convince Christian Eriksen, Jan Vertonghen and Toby Alderweireld their futures lie at Tottenham as they resist signing new deals.
