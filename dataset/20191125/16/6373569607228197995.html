Make a difference with South Western Ambulance Service

The service is currently recruiting for various roles across the south west

25 Nov 2019, 16:24 by Leena Sidat

If you’re looking to make a positive difference to people’s lives, then a job with South Western Ambulance Service NHS Foundation Trust can help you do just that.

An emergency call handler with South Western Ambulance Service, Gavin is one of the voices that callers hear when they dial 999 for ambulance assistance, and has dealt with almost every conceivable emergency.

Here, Gavin speaks about the demands and benefits of such a vital role.

Q) What is it like to be on the other end of a 999 call?

“You never know what type of call you’re going to get next, so you need to be prepared at all times. The responsibility of answering a 999 call is extremely high as you’re giving life-saving instructions and also having to get important information from a highly stressed caller.”

Q) What’s the best thing about working as a 999 emergency call handler?

“I can’t say any day is ever the same. Can’t predict what’s going to happen. It’s exciting and rewarding, the adrenaline – you can’t describe it. I look forward to going to work every day knowing that I am going to make a difference to someone.”

Q) What’s the most challenging part of the job?

“For me, it’s the fast pace and you need to be able to stay calm and reassuring when on a 999 call.”

Q) What type of person does it take to be a 999 emergency call handler?

“You need to be strong minded, resilient, a strong communicator and have the ability to remain calm and composed when making critical decisions in emergency situations.”

Q) What would you say to someone considering this career path?

“Go for it, you won’t regret it. If you like a fast-paced environment and want to make a difference to people’s lives, and don’t mind working shifts, this is a great way to do so. The fulfilment you get knowing you’ve helped somebody when they really need it – that’s what it’s all about.”
