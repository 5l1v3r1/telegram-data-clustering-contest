GERS IN THE DOCK

Five Rangers fans deny charges as they face trial over chaotic scenes at Kilmarnock match

25 Nov 2019, 16:52 by Campbell Thomas

FIVE Rangers fans are facing trial after major disruption at their away match at Kilmarnock on the opening day of the season.

The Scottish Premiership game against Killie on August 4, ended 2-1 to Rangers after a stoppage time header from Connor Goldson.

It ended with a pitch invasion and chaotic scenes at the Ayrshire side's Rugby Park stadium.

Richard Bambridge, 29, Daniel McHugh, 25, Mattthew Brannigan, 27, and Jamie McEwan, 21, are accused of entering the field, embracing players, struggling with stewards, trying to take a photo with a player and gesticulating at rival fans.

Bambridge, of Baillieston and McHugh, of Blackhill, both Glasgow, Brannigan, of Bishopton, Renfrewshire and McEwan, of Bishopbriggs, Dunbartonshire, all deny breach of the peace.

They were freed on bail at Kilmarnock Sheriff Court for trial in April.

At a separate hearing in the same court, Rangers fan Dean Jackson, 18, was accused of singing a song with sectarian lyrics at the game.

Jackson, of Larbert, Stirlingshire, denies threatening or abusive behaviour aggravated by religious prejudice.

He was bailed for trial in April and banned from attending any regulated football match in Scotland meantime.

Police Scotland put out a public appeal for information after the match disruption, which caused the roof of a sheltered disabled section to cave in.

Officers later said 13 men and boys had been charged with offences connected to the disorder.
