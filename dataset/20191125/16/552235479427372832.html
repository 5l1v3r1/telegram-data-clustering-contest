Victim rushed to hospital after stab attack at Blantyre railway station

British Transport Police have released descriptions of five people they would like to speak to.

25 Nov 2019, 16:55 by Kirsten McStay

British Transport Police are appealing for witnesses after a man was stabbed in Blantyre.

The victim, aged in his 50s, was attacked by two men following an altercation in nearby Station Road.

And the incident happened at 1.12am on Sunday, November 24, in the car park at Blantyre railway station.

The two men were part of a larger group and, following the attack, the men and the group ran away in the direction of Station Road.

The victim was rushed to hospital for treatment. His injury was not life-threatening, and he has since been released.

Officers would like to speak to anyone who knows the two men, or who knows anyone who was in the group.

They are looking to speak to those in the group with the following descriptions:

■ A man, white, between 5ft 10ins and 6ft, aged between 20 and 40, of a stocky build and with dark hair. He was wearing a khaki jacket with a patch on the left sleeve, blue jeans and white trainers.

■ A man, white, between 5ft 8ins and 5ft 10ins, aged between 20 and 40, with short dark hair and of slim build. He was wearing a dark jacket with a large white patch on the shoulder area of each sleeve. He was also wearing light blue jeans and white trainers.

■ A man, white, between 5ft 8ins and 5ft 10ins, of a heavy build, and wearing a dark coloured hooded tracksuit top with a white stripe down each sleeve. He was also wearing dark shorts, black socks and white trainers.

■ A woman, white, aged between 18 and 25, of slim build, and with short dark hair tied into a bun. She was wearing a black jacket, light-coloured trousers and white trainers.

■ A woman, white, aged between 18 and 25, of a heavy build, and with short dark hair tied up. She was wearing a black jacket with a fur lined hood, blue jeans and dark shoes.

Anyone with information can contact BTP by texting 61016 or by calling 0800 40 50 40 and quoting reference number 40 of 24/11/19.

Alternatively, call Crimestoppers anonymously on 0800 555 111.
