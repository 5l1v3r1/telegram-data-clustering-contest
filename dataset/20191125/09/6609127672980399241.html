COP PROBE

Police watchdog probe Isle of Lewis crash which saw car smash into house ‘after cop chase’ as man arrested

25 Nov 2019, 09:00 by Ewan Mowat

THE police watchdog has launched an investigation after a car smashed into a house on the Isle of Lewis following an alleged cop chase.

Three people were hurt after the horror crash which saw the motor burst into flames in the early hours of Saturday morning.

A 32-year-old man has been arrested in connection with alleged road traffic offences.

Mercy crews raced to the scene on the A857 after the junction with the A858, also known as the Barvas corner, around 1.30am.

The driver, 32, and two male passengers, 22 and 36, were rushed to Western Isles Hospital to be treated for serious injuries.

A 61-year-old woman had to be evacuated from her home during the incident.

Cops closed one lane on the road as they probed the crash.

It is believed the car involved was a blue Vauxhall Zafira, which reportedly caught fire as it left the road before smashing into the house.

Three fire crews battled the flames, which had taken hold in the house, before extinguishing the blaze and leaving the scne around 3.20am.

The Police Investigations and Review Commissioner (Pirc) is now probing the involvement of officers in the lead up to the smash.

A spokesperson for the force said: "We can confirm the vehicle was involved in an earlier pursuit that morning.

“As such, in line with the standard procedure, the incident has been reported to Pirc.”

A spokesman for Pirc’s investigation team said: “We are carrying out an assessment to determine whether a full investigation is required.”

At the weekend, Sergeant Donald Sinclair, of Stornoway’s Church Street station, said: “Our enquiries into the circumstances of the crash are ongoing and I am appealing for anyone who saw the alleged crash or who saw a blue Vauxhall Zafira being driven on the A857 before 1.30am to come forward.

“I’m particularly keen to speak to anyone who may have dash-cam footage which could help with our enquiries.”

Those with information can contact police on 101, quoting reference number 0366 of 23 November 2019.
