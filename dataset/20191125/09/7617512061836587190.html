Winter shelter opens today in Nanaimo; freezing temperatures in forecast this week

25 Nov 2019, 09:52 by Carla Wilson

Nanaimo’s winter shelter is opening today as freezing temperatures are forecast for this week.

The 35-bed facility, open daily between 6 p.m. and 7 a.m., will run until the end of March. It is located at St. Peter’s Hall, 301 Machleary St., part of St. Peter’s Parish. Hot dinners are served and social services are available.

The shelter is operated by the Society for Equity, Inclusion and Advocacy. B.C. Housing is covering the cost.

A total of 2,927 beds were filled at St. Peter’s last winter.

Homelessness is a concern in Nanaimo, where people are living in camps in the woods, alone or in groups. B.C. has funded two supportive housing facilities to provide homes and supports for people in need.
