Jofra Archer claims he was racially abused by a spectator during New Zealand Test

25 Nov 2019, 09:19 by Telegraph Sport

The England and Wales Cricket Board is looking into Jofra Archer's claim that he was racially insulted while batting against New Zealand in the first Test.

Archer scored a second-innings 30 as England failed to bat out the final day in Mount Maunganui, losing by an innings and 65 runs.

The 24-year-old bowler said on Twitter: "A bit disturbing hearing racial insults today while battling to help save my team.

"The crowd has been amazing this week except for that one guy. The Barmy Army was good as usual also."

Archer, who was born in Barbados and represented West Indies at Under-19 level, has been fast-tracked into the England side and made an impressive test debut during the Ashes series against Australia.

New Zealand Cricket said they plan on "apologising to Archer" in a statement released on Monday.
