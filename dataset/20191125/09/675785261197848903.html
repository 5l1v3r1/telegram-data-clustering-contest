Mum fighting for life after she's caught in crossfire of mafia 'godfather' hit

Waitress Dominika Akirtava, 22, was hit in the head by a ricocheting bullet when gunmen armed with Kalashnikov rifles assassinated two mafia bosses at a restaurant in the Georgian breakaway region of Abkhazia

25 Nov 2019, 09:43 by Will Stewart, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/will-stewart/

A young waitress is fighting for her life in hospital after she was caught in the crossfire of a gangland shooting that left two mafia bosses dead.

Mum-of-one Dominika Akirtava, 22, had brought drinks to an outside table at a restaurant when gunmen Kalashnikov rifles opened fire in the double assassination of a local 'godfather' and another mob figure.

Ms Akirtava took cover near a table but was hit in the head by a ricocheting bullet in the Georgian breakaway region of Abkhazia, say reports.

A woman inadvertently filmed the shooting as she recorded video of her daughter walking in the street.

The child is seen running in fear towards her mother as gunfire is heard.

Local ‘godfather’ Astamur Shamba, nicknamed Astik, aged 38, was gunned down while he was eating lunch at an outside table at a restaurant in regional capital Sukhumi, say reports.

Another crime boss, Alkhas Avidzba, nicknamed Khasik, 38, was shot dead.

Two relatives of the local barons, who were members of the Gudauta criminal gang, were wounded in the gun attack, which so far has led to no arrests.

The waitress - the mother of a two-year-old boy called Alan - was in a “grave” condition in hospital.

A hospital source said the young woman was in a coma as doctors wait for her condition to change before performing surgery.

Eyewitnesses described a chaotic and distressing scene as the gunmen attacked.

An eyewitness said of the young mother: "She was in the wrong place at the wrong time.

“She is in a coma,” said a hospital source.

“Her blood pressure constantly drops, so they cannot start the surgery.”

Ms Akirtava had worked at the restaurant for just two months, said locals.

Officers launched a search of the area after the shooting.

A burned-out Toyota car was found with four Kalashnikov rifles.

It is believed the mob bosses were attacked as part of a gangland war.

Rival criminal gangs are battling for control of the de facto sovereign state, which borders Russia in the South Caucasus on the eastern coast of the Black Sea.

There were fears the gunmen had fled to Georgia.

The Foreign and Commonwealth Office has told Britons not to travel to the Russian-occupied breakaway region of Abkhazia or to areas immediately adjacent to the Administrative Boundary Lines due to the situation there.

It said: "The British government does not recognise the unilateral declarations of independence made by the de facto authorities in the breakaway regions of Abkhazia and South Ossetia."

Following the Georgian-Russian war in 2008, the Kremlin recognised the region as an independent state and Georgia declared Abkhazia "occupied" by Moscow forces.
