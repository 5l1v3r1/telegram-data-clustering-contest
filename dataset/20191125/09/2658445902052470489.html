Players' union praises Wike over academy - The Nation Newspaper

25 Nov 2019, 09:26 by https://www.facebook.com/alao.h.joshua

Our Reporter

The National Association of Nigeria Footballers (NANPF) has called for the establishment of more soccer academies affiliated to European club sides in the country.

President of NANPF, Tijani Babangida while taking a cursory look at the Real Madrid Football Academy in Port-Harcourt praised the Rivers State Governor Nyesom Wike for such a laudable idea that would take Rivers’ youths out of the street.

“We want other state governors to emulate Nyesom Wike in establishing football academies attached to European clubs. This is the best way to produce world’s best footballers, “Babangida said in a release signed by NANPF ‘s Secretary General Austin Popo. “Rivers state will turn out good players in the nearest future that will compete with the best in Europe and the rest of the world. In fact, when this happens Wike is the winner.

NANPF has equally commended Wike for putting a round peg in a round hole with the appointment of former Nigeria Football Federation (NFF) technical committee chairman, Christopher Green to manage the Real Madrid Football Academy in Port-Harcourt.

The erstwhile Rivers commissioner for sports and NFF board member was appointed this month to handle the affairs of the academy affiliated to its parents club in Spain.

Babangida explained that the best way to sustain such a project was appointment of such a high level football official like Green and placing the academy under his direct supervision.

The former Super Eagles’ winger also praised governor Wike for providing high class facilities, which he said are second to none in the country and would ensure high performance of the youths.
