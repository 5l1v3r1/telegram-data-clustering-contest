I'm A Celebrity's Andy Whyment 'never been happier' in jungle admits wife Nicola

Coronation Street star Andy Whyment 'hasn't laughed like that in so long' as his long-suffering wife watches him in the I'm A Celebrity jungle

25 Nov 2019, 09:31 by Irishmirror.ie

While Andrew Maxwell and Ian Wright are butting heads, there's one I'm A Celebrity star who seems to be lapping up every single minute - cockroaches and all.

Coronation Street star Andy Whyment was a late arrival to the jungle camp, but has never been happier, says his long-suffering wife Nicola.

"Andy is living his best life in the jungle," she told OK! magazine.

"I think he just showed everyone how excited he is to be there. I've not seen him laugh that much in so long. I hope he doesn't struggle too much in the jail camp as he loves his food and sleep."

Andy went in at the same time as Cliff Parisi, and was in paroxysms of laughter as the pair did their first Bushtucker trial in Ol' Dingo Town.

And while some of his I'm A Celebrity campmates have already grown weary of the lack of food and sleep, Andy's had a smile on his face ever since he arrived.

"I think the hardest part will be the hunger," he had said before jetting Down Under.

"My co-star Jennie [McAlpine] did the show in 2017 and warned me about how hungry you get when you're on basic rations. Antony Cotton also did the show in 2011 and he said I was going to feel starving."

He added to OK!: "I just love my food! There's a joke on the Corrie set that as soon as they announce it's lunchtime, I bomb it straight to the canteen. Sometimes the cast have physically held me back to stop me getting to the queue first!

"I've got a little pot belly, so maybe it'll be good for getting rid of that. As for the gross eating challenges, I think that's a lot about mindset. When you want to do well for your camp and you’re starving, I'm sure you find a way to power through it."

But Andy admitted the hardest part of his jungle stint would be the time away from his family.

"Even when I work away from home, I can call Nic and FaceTime the kids," he added.

Andy, who plays Kirk Sutherland on the ITV soap, has previously opened up about the horrific bullying he experienced as a child.

He began ballet aged eight, but other kids at his Bolton school were jealous and would relentlessly tease him.

"It was ­verbal rather than physical, but once one started a few of them would join in," Andy said.

"It was jealousy more than anything else. I was ­doing something with my life and they were just hanging about the street corners getting hammered every week.

"At one stage my dad was thinking of pulling me out of the school.

"But I told him I didn't want him to, because if I left then they'd have won. So I stayed and got on with it."

Andy has been on TV since he was a teenager, starting with roles on shows including Where The Heart Is and The Royle Family.

He also sang in a rock band and busked in Manchester for three years so he didn't have to "sponge off" his parents.

Andy only entered the jungle camp on Thursday, but he's already become the new favourite to win the show.

He won the hearts of the viewers with his rolling commentary during a Bushtucker Trial which saw Cliff Parisi try to avoid buffaloes, and lucky dipped his way through fish guts to find his five stars.

Whyment's commentary and comparing it to "just like watching it on the telly" had hosts Ant and Dec in stitches throughout before he was sent to Jungle Jail by the sherif.
