Midfielder Antoni Sarcevic tops Plymouth Argyle player ratings for November

Sarcevic scored the winning goal for the Pilgrims in their 1-0 win at Forest Green Rovers

25 Nov 2019, 09:34 by Chris Errington

Midfielder Antoni Sarcevic is Plymouth Live’s Argyle Player-of-the-Month for November.

The Pilgrims have only had three matches this month, winning all of them, and Sarcevic has been in excellent form.

Their next game is not until Sunday, December 1, when they travel to Bristol Rovers in the FA Cup second round.

Sarcevic’s average rating for November was 8.00, which included a mark of nine out of 10 in the 2-1 League Two victory over Bradford City at Home Park on Saturday.

The 27-year-old was also the Star Man for Argyle when he scored the winning goal in the 1-0 triumph at Forest Green Rovers the previous weekend.

Sarcevic had been the runner-up in the Plymouth Live Argyle ratings to Will Aimson in September and George Cooper in October.

Now his consistent form has seen him come out on top in the November rankings.

The runner-up to Sarcevic was Joe Edwards, who has averaged 7.66 from three games after moving to right wing-back from the holding midfield role.

Edwards, who recently became the father to twin boys Alfie and Louie, grabbed the Pilgrims’ second goal in the defeat of Bradford.

In joint third place with an average mark of 7.33 for November were defenders Niall Canavan and Scott Wootton, plus Josh Grant.

The latter has impressed in the holding midfield role vacated by Edwards over the last three matches.
