Taylor Swift Performs Her Old Catalog Of Songs At The AMAs After Spat With Scooter Braun & Big Machine

25 Nov 2019, 12:24 by Caitlin Kelley

Taylor Swift was honored as the Artist of the Decade at the American Music Awards, where she performed a medley of her biggest hits following a licensing dispute with Scooter Braun and former label Big Machine Records.

The singer started off with her Lover cut, “The Man,” before launching into songs like “Blank Space,” “Love Story,” and “I Knew You Were Trouble.” Although she didn’t directly address Braun or Big Machine Records, she wore a shirt with all her old album titles written on it. During her acceptance speech, she thanked her new record label for their support without mentioning her longtime home Big Machine.

“This album really felt like a new beginning, and I also really love my record label, Universal and Republic,” Swift said. “Thank you for being so generous to me and allowing me to make whatever music I want to make. As a songwriter, it’s so thrilling to me that I get to keep doing that.”
