Medics called to help man lying on pavement outside Burton Wetherspoons

Scores of shoppers saw him on the floor

25 Nov 2019, 12:34 by Beth Pridding

Paramedics were called to help a man who was lying on the ground outside a popular Burton boozer.

Scores of shoppers saw the man on the floor outside the Lord Burton, in High Street, at around 1.30pm on Saturday afternoon.

Ambulance bosses have today told Burton Live he had fallen and injured himself.

An ambulance was called to help the man and he was later taken to hospital, paramedics have revealed.

A spokesman for West Midlands Ambulance Service said: "We were called at 1.20pm on Saturday, November 23, to reports of a patient who had fallen outside the Lord Burton, on High Street.

"We sent an ambulance to the scene and paramedics treated one patient, a man, for injuries that were not believed to be serious.

"He was then taken to Queen's Hospital in Burton for further treatment."
