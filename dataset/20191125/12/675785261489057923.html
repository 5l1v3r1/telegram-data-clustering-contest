BREAKING Boris Johnson plans to hold stripped-back Queen's Speech days before Christmas

If you were hoping for a break from politics over Christmas, you'll be disappointed if the Tories win the election - because Boris Johnson isn't slowing down for the holidays

25 Nov 2019, 12:28 by Mikey Smith, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/mikey-smith/

Boris Johnson plans to hold a stripped-back Queen's Speech just days before Christmas if he wins the election.

It will fuel expectations the arrogant Tory leader wants to ruin Christmas by pushing his Brexit bill to a vote while Brits are trying to settle down for a festive break.

Under current plans - which could change if Labour take power - MPs will return to the Commons on Tuesday, December 17th for a speeded-up swearing in process.

The Queen's Speech is currently planned for the following Thursday, December 19th.

But it will be a 'dressed down' Queen's Speech - stripped of some of the pomp and ceremony as it was after the snap 2017 election.

In the 2017 Queen's Speech, the monarch arrived in a car rather than the guilded carriage she usually uses for the journey from Buckingham Palace to Parliament.

And for the first time since 1974 she wore "day dress" and a hat rather than robes of state and Imperial State Crown to deliver her speech.

The crown was still very much a part of proceedings though - travelling in its own car to Parliament from the Tower of London, where it is kept as part of the Crown Jewels collection.

It's possible Prince Charles could deliver the speech in her place if Queen Elizabeth is unable to attend in person.

The news has sparked speculation Mr Johnson could be plotting to bring his Brexit Withdrawal Agreement back for a vote as late as Monday, December 23rd - the day before Christmas Eve.

Johnson yesterday suggested he wanted to push another vote as an "early Christmas present" for Brexiteers.

"As families sit down to carve up their turkeys this Christmas, I want them to enjoy their festive-season free from the seemingly unending Brexit box-set drama," he said in a statement ahead of the Tory manifesto launch.

But keeping the Commons sitting until the 23rd means MPs could still be banging on about Brexit while families are wrapping their presents, preparing the starters for Christmas dinner and wanting to settle down with a mulled wine to watch Die Hard.
