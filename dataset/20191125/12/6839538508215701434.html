The Courier-Mail/The Sunday Mail reader numbers jump 13 per cent

25 Nov 2019, 12:26

The total monthly digital and print audience of The Courier-Mail and The Sunday Mail has grown to more than 3 million readers.

The official September audience figures from emma – Enhanced Media Metrics Australia – reveal the total print and digital audience of The Courier Mail, The Sunday Mail and ­couriermail.com.au soared by 13 per cent to 3.008 million readers.

The Saturday edition of The Courier-Mail and TheSunday Mail both posted strong readership gains and were among only a handful of major newspapers in Australia to grow their print audiences.

The Courier-Mail re­inforced its importance as an essential way to buy and sell property, with standout readership growth for Saturday’s Real Estate and Home magazine. Friday’s Motoring section surged 5.2 per cent over the past three months and Careers also posted gains in both Friday and Saturday’s editions.

Business Daily’s readership jumped all days of the week and Saturday’s sport section, led by Queensland’s No.1 team of newsbreakers and experts, was another highlight.

The Courier-Mail’s editor Sam Weir said: “It’s fantastic to see the print readership of our flagship newspapers growing at the same time we’re recording record digital audiences.

“The fact more than three million readers place their trust in our authoritative journalism is credit to the dedication and passion of Queensland’s biggest, best and most respected newsroom.”
