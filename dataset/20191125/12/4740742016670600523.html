Drivers urge Bristol City Council to put more signs on 'unclear' bus lane after fines

The road is a bus lane but drivers are complaining it's not clear

25 Nov 2019, 12:00 by Hannah Baker

A number of Bristol drivers are urging the local authority to put up more signs on a road in the city that’s just for buses after being accidentally caught out and served with penalty charge notices.

Romney Avenue in Bristol has a section which is closed off to vehicles except buses but some drivers believe there are not enough signs to make it clear that cars can’t travel along that stretch of road.

The road markings say 'Bus Gate' but drivers are asking why the road doesn’t say 'Buses Only' - and more than 4,000 people have been fined already this year.

The council insists signs are clear but told Bristol Live drivers can appeal if they feel they were issued a notice incorrectly.

Drivers caught by the bus cameras on Romney Avenue will receive a fine of £60, which is reduced to £30 if paid within 14 days.

However, if the charge is not paid within 28 days, the cost rises to £90.

Lisa Goodfellow, who lives in Filton, says she and her partner got caught out and are now appealing the fine.

She said: “We were on our way to view a flat as we are being made homeless due to our landlord not paying the mortgage.

“We took a wrong turning which the sat nav told us to take. We did not know anything about it until the fine showed up.”

Another driver agreed that it is an “easy mistake to make” having driven up there accidentally.

They said: “A no entry except buses sign would be good - and more signs in the lead up to the bus lane.”

A Bristol City spokesperson said: “We made signage clear to indicate what vehicles are allowed to use the bus gate.

"From the start of 2019 until November 20, we have served 4,819 penalty charge notices to people who have incorrectly driven down it.

"If anyone feels they received a penalty charge notice that was incorrectly issued, they can make an appeal via our website.”
