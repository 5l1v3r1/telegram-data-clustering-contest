Woman charged over suspected arson attack at Derby flat

Emergency services rushed to the scene

25 Nov 2019, 12:44 by Nigel Slater

A woman has been charged in connection with a suspected arson attack at a Derby flat over the weekend.

Firefighters from Kingsway, Nottingham Road and Ascot Drive were called to the scene in London Road, Alvaston at 9.21pm on Saturday.

Police also attended and the road was closed for a time while the fire was dealt with.

This morning, Derbyshire Fire and Rescue Service confirmed investigations had concluded the fire was caused deliberately and police were investigating the matter.

The fire service added that the blaze caused extensive damage to the top floor of the building.

A Derbyshire police spokeswoman said a 33-year-old woman had been arrested and charged in connection with the fire but have not released her name yet due to concerns over her mental health.

A nearby resident, who did not want to be named, said she had seen the emergency services on Saturday. She said: "I don't know what has happened but there were police cars and fire engines here until quite late."

Another local person. who also did not wish to be named, added: "I live down Wisgreaves Road and heard a lot of sirens but I don't know what has happened. I just saw the police van there this morning (Sunday) that's all."
