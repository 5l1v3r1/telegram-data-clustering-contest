Alliance leader Naomi Long defends decision not to take part in pro-Remain pact

25 Nov 2019, 12:02 by Rebecca Black, Press Association

Alliance leader Naomi Long has defended her party's decision not to stand aside for other pro Remain election candidates.

The cross-community party has refused to take part in the election co-operation efforts between Sinn Féin, the SDLP and Greens in constituencies where tight races are expected at next month's General Election.

It is the only party to stand candidates in all of Northern Ireland's 18 parliamentary constituencies.

Sinn Féin has stood aside in South Belfast in support of the pro Remain SDLP candidate Claire Hanna against the DUP's Emma Little Pengelly, while the SDLP has stood aside in North Belfast to back the pro Remain Sinn Féin candidate John Finucane against DUP deputy leader Nigel Dodds.
