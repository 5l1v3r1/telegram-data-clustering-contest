After Saina, Srikanth Pulls Out of PBL to Focus on International Events

The 26-year-old shuttler, who had guided Bangalore Raptors to their maiden title win in the last PBL, will not feature in the PBL which is scheduled to be held from January 20 to February 9.

25 Nov 2019, 12:34

The upcoming edition of Premier Badminton League (PBL) is already losing star power due to the jam-packed and hectic international schedule. After Saina Nehwal choosing to sit out of the fifth edition of one of the top Badminton leagues in the world, it is now the turn of former world number one Kidambi Srikanth to pull of the tournament to focus on international assignments ahead of the 2020 Tokyo Olympics.

The 26-year-old shuttler, who had guided Bangalore Raptors to their maiden title win in the last PBL, will not feature in the PBL which is scheduled to be held from January 20 to February 9.

“It’s a tough road ahead. Need to go full throttle and fulfil the expectations that lie on me. Hence, I won’t be playing PBL this year to focus more on international events. Wish @blr_raptors all the very best and hope for a smashing season this year as well,” Srikanth wrote on his twitter handle.

Srikanth is currently enduring a winless streak since claiming four titles in 2017. He had reached the finals of India Open earlier this year.
