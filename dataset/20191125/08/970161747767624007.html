Rashford explains how Greenwood and Williams personify Man Utd

25 Nov 2019, 08:20

Mason Greenwood and Brandon Williams both scored for Manchester United on Sunday and their attacking intentions epitomise what the club is about, according to Marcus Rashford.

The Red Devils found themselves 2-0 behind against Sheffield United in the Premier League before Williams, Greenwood and then Rashford scored to give them an unlikely lead.

A late Oli McBurnie goal saw the spoils shared at Bramall Lane, which was the least the South Yorkshire side deserved after dominating much of the match.

Rashford is thoroughly impressed with his fellow academy graduates and how they play the ‘Man Utd way’ although saying that full-back Williams only knows how to attack may be damning with faint praise.

‘For Brandon, he has done brilliantly since he’s come up with us and obviously for Mason, for any forward, it’s beautiful to score a goal,’ Rashford told MUTV.

‘All they know is how to attack and play forward-thinking football and you can see with the way they attack they just bring that into their game and that is Man United.’

The draw leaves Manchester United ninth in the Premier League with just four wins from their opening 13 games in the competition this season.

The Red Devils have not won back-to-back Premier League games since 3 March and are really struggling to find any consistency under Ole Gunnar Solskjaer.

Goalkeeper David de Gea was to thank for the score only being 1-0 to the Blades at half-time on Saturday in an appalling first half display from the Red Devils, which Rashford admits was unacceptable.

‘We know that we didn’t play well enough in the first half,’ said the striker.

‘But we managed to find it inside us to get back into the game and get in a good, strong position in the game.

‘It’s important that we understand the bigger picture and the current situation we’re in and to show the fight that we did today.

‘Credit to the team and to the manager and to the fans, it was a good spell to get back into the game.’

Solskjaer could not defend his team’s first half performance, although he was keen to point out that it was not his tactics that were to blame.

‘I was angry, disappointed, you had to wake them up,’ Solskjaer said. ‘You had to do something about it. I could have changed every player at half-time, apart from the keeper.

‘It wasn’t about tactics. It was about the desire first half, getting first to the ball, tackling, winning challenges. Sometimes tactics go out of the window.

‘You have to earn the right to win a game and we didn’t have any right to win the game after 70 minutes. We were nowhere near a Man United team.’

United are back in Europa League action on Thursday away to Astana before hosting Aston Villa in the Premier League on Sunday.
