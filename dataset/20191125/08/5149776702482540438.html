Legacy of much-loved Nottingham rugby player honoured with fundraiser

Ben Hallam of the Nottingham Casuals rugby football club died in July 2018

25 Nov 2019, 08:12 by Phoebe Ram

Loved ones of a popular rugby player who died after a short battle with cancer have spoken of how they are keeping his memory alive.

Ben Hallam had been club captain at Nottingham Casuals, where he was one of the top try scorers, and was known among teammates as "The Beeston Bullet".

Ben grew up up in Attenborough and returned to the area to live permanently after studying away at university.

The Rolls-Royce worker was diagnosed with cancer in Spring 2018 but died just a few months later. He was 41.

Club chairman Oli Mott, 40, said: "It just happened really, really quick.

"We wanted some way to keep his memory alive, so we renamed an award after him.

"The Ben Hallam Clubman of the Year award will be given annually to anyone who helps throughout the year.

"It's perfect for Ben as he did so much for the club.

"We considered retiring his number 15 shirt as he played in the full back position. But instead, it will now carry his name as a lasting tribute to him."

Oli described Ben as "naturally quiet but still very social".

"He did some really good work for the Casuals especially as club captain. He had a friendly face and was easy to approach," he said.

"He was a great guy and a very good friend."

Speaking about the funeral and the wake that followed back at the club, Oli said people were all "sharing stories about Ben".

He said: "It ended up inspiring an event because it ended up being such an enjoyable day."

On the anniversary of Ben's death, July 13, 2019, Ben's family and friends held the first ever Hallam Fest, combining his three loves of rugby, rock music and fancy dress.

Oli added: "We had live bands on an open air stage, held a touch rugby tournament and used ticket sales and a raffle to raise money.

"It was really well received and went on late into the night. We have now considered it as a regular event.

"It was designed as something Ben would have enjoyed and it couldn't have been done without the support of his family.

"It has been a great way to keep us connected."

Ben's sister, Tori Hallam, 44 worked with Oli and the club to organise the festival event as a way to remember her brother.

She said: "A year on I just thought let's turn this awful day into something nice and raise some money.

"It was so nice to pull people together to remember Ben and to see how much he meant to people.

"There is such a long history at the Nottingham Casuals for Ben and they've all been so lovely and supportive.

"We're all really grateful to the club and it's been great to see how much Ben meant to people.

"He was good humoured and as someone else said, 'predictably joyful and joyfully predictable!'

"Family and friends meant everything to him. He was very loyal and a great uncle to my children.

"I grew up just thinking he'd be around forever. Losing him has left a big hole in our family."
