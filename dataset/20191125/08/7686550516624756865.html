Storm Sebastien to lash UK with high winds and heavy rain for days

Waves could be up to 22ft in places

25 Nov 2019, 08:13 by Neil Shaw

Storm Sebastien is set to bring gale-force winds and torrential rain to the UK for days on end this week, according to the latest forecast.

Waves could by 22ft in places as the remains of the tropical storm lash the UK.

Three weather warnings have been issued by the Met Office for the first three days of the week, forecasting heavy rain across parts of the country.

The first, which ends at 10am today, was for up to 25mm of rain across the South West and South Wales.

The second, tomorrow, covers South Wales, the South West and the South East and lasts until 3pm on Wednesday.

We could see up to two weeks of rain in a few hours.

And a final warning will be in place in Yorkshire from midnight on Tuesday to 6pm on Wednesday.

As much as 60mm of rain could hit parts of the region, almost half of the 121mm average total for November.

Met Office forecaster Marco Petagna told The Sun : “Remnants of Tropical Storm Sebastien mean it will be windy on Tuesday into Wednesday with gales in the South and South-West, and high spring tides.

“It's not a pretty picture, with warnings for rain for the next few days after some places had three times their normal rain in the first half of the month."

Tropical Storm Sebastien was feared to be on the verge of becoming a hurricane, with swirling 60mph winds.

It has broken up as the last storm of the season crossed the Atlantic, but will still bring foul weather to the country over the next few days.
