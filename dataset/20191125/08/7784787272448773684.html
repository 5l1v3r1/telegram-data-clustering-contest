RLPA on the hunt for a new CEO as Ian Prendergast announces intention to quit

25 Nov 2019, 08:33

Rugby League Players’ Association (RLPA) CEO, Ian Prendergast, has announced that he will quit the organisation in 2020.

He is due to leave his post in April with the players’ union now on the lookout for a replacement to take over from the departing former Carlton AFL player.

Prendergast’s legacy includes the most recent collective bargaining agreement, rolling out an accredited agent scheme and helping the game establish a set international structure along with inviting female players into the association.

In the last year, he has led the RLPA’s oposition to the NRL’s no-fault stand down ruling and will now return to Melbourne.

“When I joined the RLPA as CEO in 2015, I set myself a goal of building a strong and respected association that could properly represent the interests of its members” Prendergast said.

“I’m really proud of what the team and our members have achieved and believe the RLPA is in great shape.
