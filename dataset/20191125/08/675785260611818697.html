Jofra Archer claims he was racially abused during England's defeat to New Zealand

England cricket star Jofra Archer claims he suffered racist abuse while batting during the defeat to New Zealand on the fifth day of the first Test

25 Nov 2019, 08:09 by Matt Maltby, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/matt-maltby/

Jofra Archer has claimed that he was racially abused by spectators during England's Test against New Zealand.

England were crushed by New Zealand as the tourists lost the first Test by an innings and 65 runs at the Bay Oval in Mount Maunganui, Tauranga.

The game, however, was overshadowed after Archer alleged that he was the subject of racial abuse while batting for England.

He wrote on Twitter: A bit disturbing hearing racial insults today while battling to help save my team.

"The crowd has been amazing this week except for that one guy. The Barmy Army was good as usual also."

Neil Wagner's five for 44 included two wickets in as many balls to bring an abrupt conclusion to proceedings as the Black Caps took a 1-0 lead in the two-Test series, but a number of England batsmen were culpable for their own downfall.

There was some brief resistance from Sam Curran (29no) and Jofra Archer (30), both of whom rode their luck in a 59-run stand that was England's highest of the second innings.

But Archer strangely hooked to deep backward square-leg off Wagner, whose low full toss trapped Stuart Broad plumb in front, leaving the Kiwis buoyant.
