Model railway display destroyed by vandals rebuilt using £107,000 of donations

25 Nov 2019, 08:34

A model railway exhibition that was destroyed by vandals earlier this year has now been rebuilt thanks to more than £107,000 in donations.

Members of The Market Deeping Model Railway Club had been left in tears after four teenagers trashed the display as part of a ‘pre-exam night out’ earlier this year.

Exhibits which had taken as long as 25 years to make were thrown against walls, smashed and stamped on, including a locomotive unit worth about £8,500.

It has now taken 30 members of the club around 1,000 hours to restore their displays, remaking everything by hand.

The sets are now on display at the Warley Show, at the Birmingham NEC, after a fundraising page was backed by singer Sir Rod Stewart.

Sir Rod donated £10,000 towards the club, telling them he ‘felt their pain’ after taking 23 years to build his his own model railway.

Altogether, the page raised more than £107,000 from 5,500 people across the world.

Club chairman Peter Davies, 70, described Sir Rod’s donation as ‘absolutely amazing’, adding that he was pleased to see the club up and running once more.

He said: ‘We could have just easily walked away. That’s not the thing to do, we’re a club, we work together and it’s been satisfying getting it back again.’

Mr Davies previously described how the teenagers had caused ‘total devastation and destruction’ by wrecking the exhibition.

Three 16-year-old boys and one 15-year-old boy had broken into Welland Academy in Stamford, Lincolnshire at around 3.50am on May 18.

They then shared a bottle of vodka, before going on a ‘rampage’, overturning tables and throwing models across the room.

The boys were later found hiding in the toilets after setting off an alarm.

In August, they all admitted criminal damage at Lincoln Youth Court, and three of them were handed 12-month referral orders.

Their parents were also ordered to pay £500 in compensation.

At the time Mr Davies, a grandfather-of-five, said: ‘We are devastated and distraught. It’s heartbreaking.

‘There were grown men there in tears because of what had been done, and I admit I was one of them.

‘Can you imagine your life’s work wrecked? One guy spent 25 years on his and it’s wrecked, it’s just horrendous. They left it like a bomb site.

‘There were many different models of locomotives across the hall that were smashed, trodden and kicked about.’

The club have now made a Sir Rod an honorary member and he has his own t-shirt waiting for him to pick up.

The Warley National Model Railway Exhibition, open on Saturday and Sunday, includes 90 model railway layouts and a full-size locomotive display.
