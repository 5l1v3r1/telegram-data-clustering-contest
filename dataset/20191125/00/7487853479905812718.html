Tottenham Line Up Bournemouth's Nathan Aké to Strengthen Their Struggling Defence

25 Nov 2019 by Ewan Murray

​Tottenham are keeping a very close eye on Bournemouth defender Nathan Aké as they look to bolster their defence in the near future.

Spurs are planning to overhaul their back line and are aiming to bring in at least one centre-back over the next two transfer windows, but could recruit more if needed. 

As reported by ​Football Insider, the club are particularly keen to bring in highly-rated Dutchman Aké as a left-sided centre-half, who could also play as a left-back.

The London side are keen to replace 32-year-old Jan Vertonghen who, alongside fellow defender ​Toby Alderweireld, is out of contract this summer.

​Spurs have great interest in ​Aké and are set to send scouts to keep an eye on his progress over the coming months, before making a final decision on whether to pursue a transfer. 

Aké has worked with new Spurs boss José Mourinho during his time at ​Chelsea, and has spoken out about his frustration after being forced to play for the reserves at Stamford Bridge under the 56-year-old.

However, the defender has shown drastic improvements since his last stint in London, starting all of Bournemouth's league games last season and 13 out of 13 so far this campaign.

Tottenham are struggling for homegrown players and are looking to change this over the next few transfer windows. While Aké is a Dutch international, he counts as a homegrown player as he joined Chelsea from Feyenoord at only 16 years of age. 

During his first game against West Ham last Saturday, Jose Mourinho named Davinson Sanchez and Alderweireld at centre-back, while ​Eric Dier moved in to midfield despite starting the previous two games in defence.

Spurs are also in the market for full-backs following the shaky early-season form of Danny Rose and Serge Aurier, and Ake's versatility across the back line could be pivotal in a potential move to the Tottenham Hotspur Stadium.
