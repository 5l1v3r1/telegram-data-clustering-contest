Poll: Lithuanians losing trust in political parties and prosecutors

25 Nov 2019 by BNN

In Lithuania, less than a quarter of the population trust political parties and prosecutors, standing out in loss of confidence since October, an opinion poll by Vilmorus found.

Lithuanian public broadcaster LRT reports on Monday, November 24, that the survey, published by the daily Lietuvos Rytas on Saturday, shows that 22.2 percent of the public trust the prosecution service, down from 26.3 percent in October. And 23.4 percent said they mistrusted the service, up from 19.8 percent.

The survey also indicated the public’s growing distrust of political parties, the parliament and the government.

Close to two thirds, 63.5 percent, of the respondents said they didn’t trust political parties in November, up from 57.2 percent in October. The parliament, Seimas, is distrusted by 60.9 percent (51.7 percent in October), the courts by 31.5 percent (29.6 percent in October), and the government, by 29 percent (23.7 percent in October).

People in Lithuania have most trust in firefighters (91.6 percent in November, up from 88.8 percent in October), followed by the police (65.8 percent and 63.3 percent respectively), and the president (64 percent and 71.2 percent respectively).

The army is trusted by 60.8 percent of the public (63.3 percent in October) and the social insurance fund SoDra by 52.3 percent (53.3 percent in October).

The Vilmorus survey of 1,020 people was carried out on November 8-16.
