Inside The Glitzy, Star-Studded Diamond Museum In Antwerp

25 Nov 2019 by Nadja Sayej

Antwerp is a city with a storied history, from fashion to royalty and art. But this Belgian city is also renowned for it's precious stones and diamond history, which dates back 600 years.One of the best ways to experience the diamond history of Antwerp is through the city’s diamond museum, the Diva Antwerp. The museum, which opened last year, features over 500 objects that detail the story of Antwerp's history with diamonds and gemstones.

One highlight is the Egyptomania craze that took Hollywood by storm after Elizabeth Taylor starred as Cleopatra in 1963, as a flurry of Egyptian-inspired brooches, rings and headpieces followed on the commercial market.

Other highlights include medieval chain-link purses, diamond-encrusted tableware from European royalty, diamond-encrusted tennis raquets to star-studded high fashion boots, Sri Lankan sapphires and 18th century tiaras.

Among the themes in the museum’s collection, there is everything from men’s jewellery to animal-themed items, pepper shakers and religious items. including items that have a special significance to Belgian heritage, like diamond-encrusted portraits of King Leopold II. There is also diamond items that are inspired by stars in the sky, celestial bodies; like a star-shaped brooch to tiaras, rings and tableware.

Inside the museum, the rooms range from the Wonder Room, which is filled with luxurious objects, while the Dining Room details the rules around diamond and gem collecting among the Belgian noble families. Other rooms detail the history of jewellery-making and precious stones with multimedia and interactive displays.

The current, temporary exhibition is Room of Wonder II: Wouters & Hendrix, is on view until February 16, 2020. Showing the works of Belgian jewellery designers Wouters & Hendrix, which was co-founded by Katrin Wouters and Karen Hendrix over 30 years ago, their masterpieces in gold, silver and gems are being shown alongside key pieces chosen from DIVA’s own design collection.

As the designers say in their own words, the magic of jewellery is all in the details. “For some unknown reason, a small detail or object can make appear, as by magic, fragrant memories of love, sounds and sights,” they say. “Those are wonderful moments and these objects are treasures.”
