Cyberpunk 2077 Enters Final Stage of Development

25 Nov 2019 by Sillicur

Every time CD Projekt Red shows off something about Cyberpunk 2077, the game just looks fantastic and we all can’t wait to play it when it releases on 16 April 2020. Despite initial delays, it looks like Cyberpunk 2077 development is going well and it shouldn’t be delayed further, as the game has entered its final, critical stage of development.

RELATED: Half-Life: Alyx Minimum System Requirements Revealed

This news comes from the Q3 2019 financial results of CD Projekt Red and you can watch the highlights of the open financial report at the bottom of this article. Headlining the developer’s financial report is none-other than Cyberpunk 2077 development.

The third quarter of the year was dominated by further intensive development and promotion of Cyberpunk, which has now entered its most crucial, final stage of creation. We have begun internal and external testing of the game and started to localize its content into each supported language. We are also ramping up our global promotional campaign.

In late August, we took part in Gamescom in Cologne – the largest European gaming industry event, where we ran presentations of Cyberpunk for over 12 thousand participants. Besides Gamescom, our newest videogame universe was also showcased at key industry fairs throughout the world.

CD Projekt Red continues by saying that they are “acutely aware” of the expectations surrounding the game and that these expectations spur them on to carry on with their hard work. In case you were wondering, CD Projekt Red is sitting pretty with roughly 38% increase in worth from last year, with their cumulative net profit for 2019 at 66 million PLN, which is nearly R250 million.
