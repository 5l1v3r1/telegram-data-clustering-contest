They have also mounted artworks from Shaun Heffernan and Vijit Pillai.

WHERE: Floor L1, JW Marriott Hotel, Vittal Mallya Road, Shanthala Nagar, Ashok Nagar
WHEN: 11am-11pm, ongoing
CALL: 8067189999

A talk on human behaviour
Tarun Khanna, a Harvard professor, will deliver a talk about the use of basic analytics to better ‘see’ some overlooked regularities in human behaviour. He will draw on examples from recent Indian history (crowdsourced memories of the Partition of British India to timeless religious gatherings such as the Maha Kumbh Mela) as well as from contemporary social phenomena (ubiquity of unskilled youth and the demographic bulge; an unmanageable epidemic of chronic disease across the developing world).

WHERE: International Centre for Theoretical Sciences, Shivakote, Hesaraghatta Hobli
WHEN: 4pm-5.30pm, November 25

Once upon a time

Fourteen artists will take you on a journey of divinity.

Black & white
Mani M Nagasubramanian is a man of many talents. He is an artist, actor, designer and author. And now, he’s mounted an exhibition called Black On White. His black & white works have the ability to transform the mundane into the special, creating lyrical images that manage to retain a native earthiness.

Break the taboo
Men Against Violence and Abuse in collaboration with Christ (deemed-to-be university’s) department of media studies & department of sociology and social work brings you the third edition of the travelling film festival ‘Samabhav’. Over two days, the selected films will address key issues such as discrimination against women, queer and trans individuals, toxic masculinity, misogyny and sexism. Plus, gender rights activists will hold discussions after every film screening with.

WHERE: Room No 911, Central Block, Christ, Main Campus, Hosur Road
WHEN: 9am-5pm, November 25-26
ENTRY: Free CALL: 9175488760

The groom hunt

Pravara art studio’s comedy play Beg Borrow Aliya has completed its 25th show and is coming back again this weekend.
Written by renowned writer MS Narasimhamurthy and directed by Hanu Ramasanjeeva, the play is plotted on the thoughts of vasudhaiva kutumbakam, which means ‘the world is one family’. The story revolves Vishwa and his wife Vishalu, who are nervously looking for a groom for their daughter Laila. Things take a funny turn when not one but three potential grooms enter their lives, namely Babu, Ramu and Somu. If that was not enough, inspectors Vikram and PC Basya enter the play to investigate a murder case. What unfolds is an experience
that will have you rolling on the floor with laughter.

WHERE: KH Kalasoudha, Hanumanthanagara WHEN: 5pm & 7.30pm, November 30
