Hong Kong elections amid protests end with big democratic win

25 Nov 2019 by BNN

In the Chinese semi-autonomous metropolis of Hong Kong, where large protests and violent clashes with police have continued since summer, local elections have been held. Close to 90% of mandates in the local power have been won by pro-democracy candidates.

British news portal The Guardian reports that the elections were held on Sunday, November 24. People turned out in record numbers to eject pro-Beijing politicians from district councils they had controlled across the city. They held on to little over 10 percent of the 452 openly contested seats.

Pro-democracy candidates won a majority of seats on all 18 councils, although they will only control 17 because a large number of government appointees shifted the balance of power in the Islands district.

«Whatever happens, Hong Kong is always a part of China and any attempts to create chaos in Hong Kong or to jeopardise its prosperity and stability will not be successful,»

said the Chinese foreign minister, Wang Yi, according to the Guardian.
