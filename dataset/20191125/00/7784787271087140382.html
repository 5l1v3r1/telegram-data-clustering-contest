Current 2020 NFL Draft order: Cincinnati Bengals build lead in race for No. 1 pick

25 Nov 2019, 00:19 by staff writers

Things looked good, or bad — it depends how you look at it — for the Cincinnati Bengals on Monday (AEDT).

Cincinnati was locked in an AFC North slugfest with the Pittsburgh Steelers, with their first win of the season within reach.

That Bengals defence was proving so effective Mike Tomlin was forced to bench Mason Rudolph. But his replacement, Devlin Hodges, threw the game-defining 79-yard touchdown pass, and like all the times before this one, Cincinnati was ultimately outclassed.

Now 0-11, Zac Taylor’s team have succeeded in one thing: building what might be an insurmountable lead for the No. 1 pick in the 2020 NFL Draft.

Of course, we know leads are anything but safe when the Bengals are involved, but in this context, you probably fancy their chances.

The New York Giants, Miami Dolphins, and Washington Redskins are all in the mix, but will need to find a new level of ‘bad’ to somehow end up with the worst record.

Here’s how it currently looks for those hunting a top pick in next year’s draft; whether that be for a quarterback like Joe Burrow, or a gun defensive star in Chase Young.
