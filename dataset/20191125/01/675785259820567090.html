Mum, 33, describes the constant agony of living with 'suicide disease'

Toni Lea Lawrence went without medication when she was pregnant with her first child, however the pain from her trigeminal neuralgia condition is now too severe for her to have another baby

25 Nov 2019, 01:40 by Matthew Dresch, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/matthew-dresch/

A mum has spoken out about the excruciating pain she suffers on a daily basis because of a condition that's been dubbed 'suicide disease'.

Toni Lea Lawrence, 33, has to take powerful medication to deal with the chronic pain, leaving her unable to have another baby.

Mrs Lawrence, from Australia, weaned herself off the pain pills in order to fulfil her dream of becoming a mum when she had her first child.

However, the pain is now so bad she cannot face another pregnancy without any drugs.

The senior paralegal had to give up her job because of her agonising condition.

It all started in March 2010, when she suffered a wave of searing pain and lost control of her left side at work.

According to Yahoo News , she said: “I was sitting at my desk reading emails and my first thought was that I was having a stroke because I was in so much pain and my left side was struggling to work, and I could barely talk."

She rushed to a nearby GP surgery in a panic after a colleague said her face looked different.

A doctor gave her pain relief and sent her for an MRI scan, believing she had a brain tumour.

However, Mrs Lawrence later found out she had trigeminal neuralgia (TN), which is commonly known as 'suicide disease'.

She said the condition caused agony on her left side and compared the pain to her teeth being ripped out with fishing wire.

The mum added: "It is triggered by stress, touching my face, really cold weather , really hot weather and wind hitting my face.”

The pain associated with the disease means that most sufferers are unable to have children - because they must come off medication for their pregnancy.

However, Mrs Lawrence was so keen to have a baby with her husband Kieran, 32, that she came off her medication.

She suffered increasingly severe pain after the fifth week of her pregnancy.

Her contractions began after she fell down the stairs at 35 weeks, although doctors held off Theo's birth for one more week.

The tot, now two, was delivered via c-section and his mum was finally able to take pain relief again after he was born.

Although she would love to have more children, Mrs Lawrence says her pain has now become too much to consider a drug-free pregnancy.
