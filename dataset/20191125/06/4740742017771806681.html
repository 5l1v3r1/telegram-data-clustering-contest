Pregnant women set for free swimming in South Gloucestershire

The scheme is expected to begin in a few months' time

25 Nov 2019, 06:00 by Andrew McQuarrie

Pregnant women will soon be allowed to swim for free at South Gloucestershire leisure centres.

The Circadian Trust Board has approved a year-long pilot scheme expected to begin in early 2020.

The decision comes following a campaign by the Conservative parliamentary candidate for Thornbury and Yate, Luke Hall.

Mr Hall said: “As a father living in Yate, it is great news that we have been able to secure free swimming for pregnant women."

Mr Hall set up a petition calling for the scheme in response to a finding that women can boost their chances of a healthy pregnancy by keeping active.

Mum-of-three Liz Brennan branded the success of the campaign "amazing news".

The former swimming teacher said: "As a local mum, I think it is important for pregnant women to have access to vital exercise opportunities such as this.”

Free swims are expected to be introduced in January or February 2020, according to South Gloucestershire Council.

Mr Hall said: "I am grateful to everyone who took the time to sign my petition. This is a very exciting initiative and I am grateful for the overwhelming support this campaign has received."
