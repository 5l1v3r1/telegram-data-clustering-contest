'Matilda' director Danny DeVito says he wants to make a sequel

25 Nov 2019, 20:18 by Ruairi Scott Byrne

The 1996 family film Matilda remains a staple of many people's childhood - and now the director behind the beloved comedy has revealed he wants to make a sequel.

While many of you will remember that Danny DeVito starred in Matilda, which is based on the Roald Dahl novel, as the catty and arrogant car seller Harry Wormwood, many people are unaware that the Always Sunny in Philadelphia star also directed the film.

Matilda proved hugely popular when it was released over 23 years ago as it struck a chord with those who loved reading books larger than their body frames, shy kids, introverts, and anyone who felt like an outcast in their families/communities.

And now DeVito has revealed that he'd be up for making a follow-up to the 90s classic which could revolve around the child of the now grown-up Matilda.

While promoting his new film Jumanji: The Next Level, DeVito was asked which of his previous films he'd most like to revisit, with the 75-year-old actor revealing his desire to produce a sequel to the iconic fantasy film.
