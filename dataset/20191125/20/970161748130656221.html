UFC fighter Walt Harris's missing stepdaughter Aniah Blanchard, 19, 'found dead'

25 Nov 2019, 20:37

UFC fighter Walt Harris’s missing stepdaughter, Aniah Blanchard, 19, is believed to have been found dead, police said.

Blanchard’s body is believed to have been discovered Monday morning in Macon County, Alabama, more than a month after she disappeared from Auburn, which is in the same state, on October 24.

Lee County District Attorney Brandon Hughes said: ‘I can confirm that human remains have been found in Macon County and we have good reason to believe they are that of Aniah Blanchard.

Auburn police told AL.com that investigators found the remains after they ‘obtained information regarding the possible location of a body.’

A suspect in Blanchard’s kidnapping, Antwain ‘Squirmy’ Fisher, 35, was arrested on Friday made his first court appearance Monday in the case of Aniah’s disappearance, according to CBS.

Prosecutors say Fisher helped 29-year-old Ibraheem Yazeed, a second suspect, kidnap Blanchard by giving him ‘material assistance to Yazeed by providing transportation to Yazeed and disposing of evidence,’ according to court records made public Monday.

Yazeed was arrested on November 7 and charged with kidnapping after he was seen in surveillance footage at the same convenience store as Blanchard shortly before she disappeared.

A witness told police that Yazeed forced Blanchard into a car at the store, although it is unclear if the witness was referring to Yazeed’s vehicle or Blanchard’s SUV, which was later found abandoned and damaged.

Auburn police said evidence inside the SUV contained the ‘blood of someone suffering a life-threatening injury. The evidence was submitted to the Alabama Department of Forensic Sciences and was found to contain Blanchard’s DNA.

The vehicle was also found to contain a man’s DNA and Yazeed has recently been ordered by a judge to submit a DNA sample.
