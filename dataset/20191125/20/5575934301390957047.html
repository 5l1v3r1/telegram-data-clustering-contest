A 6-year-old who fractured her skull in a car accident has been regaining strength by painting a high school quarterback's nails before games

25 Nov 2019, 20:54 by Mack DeGeurin
  • A tragic accident brought together 17-year-old Omaha, Nebraska, quarterback Elliott Brown and 6-year-old McKinley Blue, “Good Morning America” reported.
  • The two met in a hospital while Blue was recovering from a car accident.
  • The accident put Blue in a coma for 10 days and left her with brain damage. She reportedly had to relearn how to walk, talk and eat.
  • While Brown visited her, Blue would paint his fingernails to help her regain some strength in her arms.
  • Blue continued painting Brown’s nails even after she was released from the hospital.
  • Now, Blue regularly attends Brown’s games and is one of his biggest fans.

Though neither of them could have predicted it, 17-year-old Omaha, Nebraska, high school quarterback Elliott Brown, and 6-year-old McKinley Blue have become a formidable team.

As first reported by “Good Morning America,” the two met while Blue was in the hospital recovering from the after-effects of a 2018 car accident. That accident left Blue with a left skull fracture that put her in a coma for 10 days. The 6-year old had to have a piece of her skull removed and was confined to a wheelchair during her recovery, KMTV reported. Blue’s brain trauma was so severe she reportedly had to relearn how to walk, talk, and eat. It was during those long months of recovery that Brown started visiting.
