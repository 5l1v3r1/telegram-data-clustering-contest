Bill Cosby gives interview from behind bars after conviction for sexual assault

25 Nov 2019, 15:51

Bill Cosby has said he will not show remorse after being jailed for three counts of aggravated indecent assault.

The 82-year-old is serving from three to 10 years in a Pennsylvania prison for the sexual assault of Andrea Constand, and if he serves his full sentence, he will be 91 when he is released.

But the comedian has said he will never admit wrongdoing to help him get parole, speaking in his first interview from behind bars.

Via a series of 15 minute prison phone calls, Cosby told National Newspaper Publishers Association’s BlackPressUSA.com: ‘I have eight years and nine months left. When I come up for parole, they’re not going to hear me say that I have remorse.

‘I was there. I don’t care what group of people come along and talk about this when they weren’t there. They don’t know.’

The former The Cosby Show star then claimed that his trial was unjust, saying: ‘It’s all a set up. That whole jury thing. They were imposters.’

Since his incarceration, the star has been a featured speaker on numerous occasions at the Mann Up Association reform programme in prison, which strives to ‘encourage and empower African American men to strive for self-respect and dignity’.

Cosby referred to his cell in general population as ‘the penthouse’, before adding: ‘I am a privileged man in prison.’

At his sentencing in October 2018, Judge Steven T Judge O’Neill deemed it appropriate to classify Cosby as a sexually violent predator.

Dozens of women have accused Cosby of sexual misconduct, with allegations spanning from 1965 to 2008.

However, Constand’s allegations are the only ones that have resulted in criminal charges.
