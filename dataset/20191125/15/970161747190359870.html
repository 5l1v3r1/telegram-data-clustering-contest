Ricky Gervais teases After Life season 2 release date

25 Nov 2019, 15:44

Ricky Gervais has hinted at the release date for After Life season two.

Ricky had a hit on his hands with the black comedy After Life so naturally he’s coming back for more.

He’s currently working away on new episodes and hopes to be done and dusted with everything by the end of the year.

After filming and then letting Netflix work its magic with subtitles, we should see season two in the first half of 2020.

Asked about the release date on The Graham Norton Show, he said: ‘That will be next spring. I’m editing now and I’ll finish that after Christmas.

‘Then it takes two months to put it into, like, 120 languages. That’s why it takes so long.’

The Derek star also spoke about how warmly viewers had received After Life.

He explained: ‘I’ve never had a reaction like it and with social media now, you hear about it. It has been overwhelming and it’s the type of reaction as well.

‘People have been coming up to me on the street and saying “Oh, I lost my mum, or I lost my buddy”. I’m there thinking “Oh my God, everyone is grieving”.’

After Life follows a man called Tony, played by Ricky, who considers taking own life after the death of his wife. He then decides to live and get his own back on the world by saying whatever he likes.

Season two will focus on what happens to Tony when he discovers there’s things around him that make life worth living.

It’s not yet known which of his co-stars will be making a comeback, but Roisin Conarty, Diane Morgan and Ashley Jensen featured prominently in the previous series.

After Life is available to watch on Netflix.
