Jose Mourinho's reaction to Roy Keane bluntly telling Man Utd to sign Harry Kane

Back in October Red Devils legend Roy Keane bluntly announced in the Sky Sports studio that Manchester United should go out and sign the Premier League's best forward, Harry Kane

25 Nov 2019, 15:55 by Jake Polden, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/jake-polden/

Jose Mourinho looked on with a grin in October when Roy Keane bluntly announced in the Sky Sports studio that Manchester United should "just go and get Harry Kane".

The Red Devils were struggling in front of goal at the time, and Gary Neville and Graeme Souness became embroiled in a row over the club's decision to offload Romelu Lukaku.

"[Mason] Greenwood at 17? That's how desperate Man United are," said Sounnes. "Go and get goalscorers who will win you games."

Neville responded: "He had a goalscorer in Lukaku - he's a proven goalscorer at the highest level - he didn't want him." And Souness replied: "So that's been a smart decision to start the season without a replacement for him, wasn't it? Gary, it's Man United!"

Former United right-back Neville hit back once more: "That's fine, but you're saying just get anyone in." And Souness concluded: "I'm saying a goalscorer, I'm not just saying anybody."

Listening to the debate in front of him, Keane then piped up.

He responded with a simple solution to the problem: "Go and get [Harry] Kane.

"Just go and get Kane from Spurs, easy. He'll score 20 goals a season with his eyes shut. They're in disarray, so go and get him."

His comment was met with silence as the rest of the room looked at him, prompting Keane to say: "I don't know what are you all staring at," which drew laughter from the pundits.

Mourinho was sitting closest to Keane, and looked at the Irishman with a smirk on his face.

Of course he had no idea he would seen become the Tottenham boss, but his reaction to Keane hailing Kane the league's best striker speaks volumes following his Monday press conference.

Ruling out signing Zlatan Ibrahimovic in January, Mourinho said: "Amazing player, amazing guy, but I would say no chance.

"We have the best striker in England. It doesn't make sense to sign him when we have Harry Kane."
