Defense a question mark for Barcelona

25 Nov 2019, 15:48 by Ernesto Hernandez

Barcelona set to go with makeshift defense ahead of crucial Champions League clash.

Barcelona find themselves in a precarious situation defensively ahead of their match against Borussia Dortmund in the Champions League.

The Catalan side is set to be without a number of their first-choice defenders and concerns whether others will be able to recover from their injuries in time.

Gerard Pique is set to miss the game serving a suspension for accumulation of yellow cards.

Nelson Semedo suffered an injury a few weeks back and is still has a few weeks left until he returns. Same for Jordi Alba who suffered an injury in their match against Slavia Prague.

With both starting fullbacks out, Junior Firpo and Moussa Wague played in Barcelona’s win over the weekend against Leganes.

Fortunately for Ernesto Valverde, Sergi Roberto will be back as he missed the Leganes match due to suspension. Expect Roberto to be deployed as the starting right-back midweek.

Besides the fullbacks, the other concern has to do with the center-backs.

As mentioned, Pique is suspended but the other question mark has to do with the availability of Clement Lenglet.

He, too, missed their last fixture after picking up a calf injury. The team got some good news with regards to Lenglet as the Frenchman was back in training. Whether he’ll start will be up to Valverde.

Samuel Umtiti was able to take advantage of Lenglet’s injury and started against Leganes and had a good performance. Ability has never been a question for Umtiti. Rather the knee injury is what’s kept him sidelines. If he stays healthy, he should play against Dortmund regardless of whether Lenglet is deemed fit.

Should he be deemed fit, the likely partnership should be Lenglet and Umtiti. But Jean-Clair Todibo and Ronald Araujo are also options. Araujo was called up to the first team and was preferred over Todibo who has been rumored with a move away to Bayer Leverkusen.

With the draw against Slavia Prague, Barcelona have put themselves in a less than envious position.

Had they won, Lenglet would not need to be rushed back and the younger defenders could have received playing time.

Now, because they are only a point in front of Dortmund, Valverde will need to field a strong line-up. The defense has long been a concern this season. Considering how poor Barcelona have looked in the Champions League, expect the defense to once again be under a microscope.
