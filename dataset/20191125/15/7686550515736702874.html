Brian O'Driscoll's perfect analysis of how Dan Biggar pulled off the sublime moment that has rugby fans drooling

'He knew exactly what he was doing', says O'Driscoll

25 Nov 2019, 15:53 by Simon Thomas

It’s the pass everyone has been talking about and now one of the legends of the game has provided a fascinating insight into exactly how Dan Biggar pulled it off.

The Wales outside-half produced one of the moments of the weekend during Northampton’s dramatic Champions Cup victory over Benetton.

His flicked pass behind his back to set up a try for winger Tom Collins was a thing of absolute beauty and a masterful piece of skill.

But, according to Ireland and Lions great Brian O’Driscoll, it was more than just showboating.

Analysing the incident on BT Sport, O’Driscoll outlined precisely what Biggar was thinking.

“It might seem full of trickery, but there’s a little bit more to it than meets the eye,” he said.

“It’s the line that he runs.

“Rather than fading on the pass and inviting the defence to drift with him, what he did was attack it short.

“He invites the defender in, making him think he’s going to challenge to the line himself.

“As soon as he takes it to the line, that defender feels as though he’s going to carry.

“That’s when he’s able to play it out the back and create a simple two-on-one.

“He knows exactly where his support player - Tuala - is, then it’s straight to Collins, great try.

“It’s not just showboating. He knew exactly what he was doing.
