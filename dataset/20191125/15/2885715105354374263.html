Video of theyyam artiste ‘beating’ people goes viral, Kerala SHRC takes suo motu case

Raghavan Payyanad, former HOD at Calicut University, who has researched extensively on different forms of theyyam, wondered why the rights panel would file a case when no one had any complaints about the incident.

25 Nov 2019, 15:37 by Vishnu Varma

Based on a viral video of a theyyam artiste chasing and beating people during a performance in Kanjangad in Kasaragod district, the Kerala State Human Rights Commission registered a suo motu case last week and asked the district collector and the police for a report within 30 days.

The filing of the case, without even a complaint, has stirred debates once again on the extent to which rights panels can interfere in religious customs and rituals. Theyyam is northern Kerala’s ancient ritual dance form and encompasses over 400 different forms of gods, goddesses, war heroes and spirits performed at temples, sacred groves and upper-caste households across Kannur, Kasaragod districts and certain parts of Kozhikode and Wayanad districts.
