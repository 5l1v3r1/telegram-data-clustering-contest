Cost of informal cremations with no mourners will be cut in RCT to help low income families

The fee for "direct" or "simplicity" cremations will be cut from £710 to £560 if cabinet agrees to it

25 Nov 2019, 15:50 by Anthony Lewis

Fees for simpler and less formal cremations in Rhondda Cynon Taf will be cut to help people on low incomes struggling with funeral costs.

Cabinet has approved a year-long pilot of a reduced cremation fee for funeral directors offering a “direct” or “simplicity” cremation in Rhondda Cynon Taf.

The fee will be reduced to £560 from its current level of £710 and come into force from December 1 this year.

Many funeral directors are now offering alternative options to families that are lower in cost and allow for those who wish to have a simpler, less formal funeral and these are known as “direct” or “simplicity” cremations.

These involve a funeral director arranging a service with no mourners and bringing the body directly to the crematorium with no religious service or attendees.

The cabinet report says funeral costs have increased substantially and are becoming increasingly unaffordable to those on the lowest incomes.

The fees and charges to the family are significantly less with direct or simplicity cremations making the service more affordable, particularly for those on low incomes.

Councillor Rhys Lewis, the cabinet member for well-being and cultural services, said at the cabinet meeting on Thursday, November 21: “We are offering a greater choice to residents.

“There are often prohibitive costs and this will assist in that. There is an increasing demand [for direct cremations] which reflects changing tastes.”

Research has shown that the cost of a funeral will come to nearly 40 per cent of the annual expenditure of someone on the lowest income.

The option would be available at both Glyntaff and Llwydcoed crematoria.

These services are already available at crematoria in Cardiff , Margam, Swansea , Narberth , St Asaph, Colwyn Bay and the privately-run crematoria in the Vale of Glamorgan.

The average cost of the essential elements of a funeral in the UK was estimated to be nearly £4,300 in 2018 with the costs coming in at £3,744 for a cremation and £4,798 for a burial.

On average another £2,000 was spent on discretionary items such as flowers and catering.

In Wales the average cost of a burial is £3,934 and the average cost for a cremation is £3,142.
