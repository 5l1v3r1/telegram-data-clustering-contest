Huge new baby goods store to open in Nottingham on Black Friday

Yes, there will be offers available for customers

25 Nov 2019, 15:21 by Matthew Bunn

A huge new baby goods store filled with prams, car seats and nursery furniture will open in Nottingham later this week.

Offers and goodie bags are being promised to customers as Little Angels prepares to launch in the city on Black Friday, November 29.

It will be located in a 5,000 sq ft site in Prospect Street, next to the Castle Retail Park, and will become the company's second branch.

It comes just weeks after national retailer Mothercare announced it was closing down, including its Nottingham branch.

Kelly Carter, 31, who set up the company in Ilkeston five years ago, said: "I am super excited. We have been looking to move to Nottingham for the past two years. It is such a big area so we always aimed to move here."

Little Angels will stock brands including iCandy and Silver Cross and there will be games and colouring books to keep children busy while parents are shopping.

Mrs Carter believes the store could potentially boast the biggest range of pushchairs in the UK, with more than 80 expected to be on display.

On the day of the opening, there will be offers on products in-store, while goodie bags will be on offer and representatives from suppliers will also be on hand to help.

Ahead of the first day, she added: "It is a bit overwhelming because it is just me.

"We are either going to sink or swim and we will soon find out. Every time you open a new business you take a massive chance and I have put my blood, sweat and tears into this, so I am really looking forward to it."

The move comes at a difficult time on the high street, which has seen national retailer Mothercare announce it will close all its stores.

The retailer has a branch at Nottingham's Castle Marina Retail Park.

Mrs Carter said: “It’s so sad to see such a household name disappearing, and devastating for all the staff.

"As an independent business, we aim to provide a professional and personal service for Nottinghamshire’s parents and parents-to-be.”

The Ilkeston branch will remain open but is expected to become more of a factory retail outlet.

Since opening five years ago, Little Angels has a most recent turnover figure of more than £2 million.
