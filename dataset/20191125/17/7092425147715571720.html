Cork City Council issues 'tidal flood warning' as Storm Sebastien closes in

25 Nov 2019, 17:51 by Ruairi Scott Byrne

Cork City Council has issued a tidal flood warning as the remnants of Storm Sebastien look set to hit Ireland this week.

Tropical Storm Sebastien is moving north-east from the Atlantic and is set to land in Ireland on Monday and continue into Tuesday, possibly bringing heavy rain and a risk of flooding.

The US National Hurricane Centre (NHC) confirmed the Atlantic’s 18th named storm of 2019, which formed last week, had suddenly intensified in strength after it had initially been predicted to weaken quite quickly.

Due to the impending arrival of Storm Sebastien, the Cork City Council (CCC) say that there will be a period of very high astronomical Spring Tides from Tuesday until Wednesday.
