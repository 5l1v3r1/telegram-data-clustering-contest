Sadness in Kilmarnock as iconic town pub closes its doors to public

The Howard Arms at Glencairn Square, on the corner of West Shaw Street and High Glencairn Street, had its final night operating in its current form last night.

25 Nov 2019, 17:16 by Lochlin Highet, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/lochlin-highet/

An iconic Kilmarnock pub has closed its doors to the public.

The Howard Arms at Glencairn Square, on the corner of West Shaw Street and High Glencairn Street, had its final night operating in its current form last night.

The current lease agreement has not been renewed, and the brewers in charge of the building are still looking for new management.

As it stands the pub will remain closed for the foreseeable future, with no estimated reopening date.

The pub is advertised 'to let' on Star Pubs and Bars.

The advert says: "The newly refurbished Howard Arms will appeal to a broad range of customers in the local area, establishing itself as a community hub.

"Located on a prominent main road just outside of Kilmarnock town centre and in close proximity to supermarkets and a variety of shops, the Howard Arms benefits from high passing footfall.

"Its position next to the local Kilmarnock Football Club ensures great trading on match days, whilst the improved offer will extend its appeal throughout quieter periods.

"A wide variety of premium drinks including beer, cider, coffee and soft drinks will make the Howard Arms indispensable to the local area.

"The new, improved food offer, supported by the attractive lounge and dining area, and family-friendly covered beer garden will see the pub become a welcoming and versatile venue for the entire community."
