Best Help to Buy ISAs - last chance for savers to get £3,000 Government bonus

If you open a tax-free Help to Buy ISA, the government will chip in an extra 25% of what you stash away - but the account is closing to new savers on November, 30. If you want to take advantage, these are the best accounts online and in branch

25 Nov 2019, 17:41 by Emma Munbodh, https://www.facebook.com/EmmaMunbodh

The clock is ticking for savers to open a Help to Buy ISA account for a £3,000 Government bonus on their first home anywhere in the UK.

Anyone over the age of 16 can apply for one - but you only have until Saturday, November 30, when the scheme closes to new savers for good.

The account allows you to save up to £200 a month, after an initial deposit of up to £1,200 - but you can open one with just £1.

And you can earn interest on top - with banks offering up to 3% a year on the cash you stash, on top of the 25% state reward.

Once you've then built up £1,600 savings, you can cash the money in, apply for the bonus, and then use it to buy a home worth up to £250,000 across the UK, or £450,000 in London.

This money can be put towards your deposit or legal fees for your first home - but you won't actually 'see' the reward. Your solicitor will have to apply for it around 30 days before completion - in other words, they'll have to unlock the bonus on your behalf.

Help to Buy ISAs will be on sale until November 30, 2019. If you've opened one before then, you can keep it running until 1 December 2030.

Help to Buy ISA eligibility - who can open one?

  • First time buyers. If you’ve owned a property in the past, or part of one, you won’t qualify. You also won’t be eligible if you've ever inherited a property.
  • Anyone aged 16 or over
  • Anyone looking to buy a home worth up to £250,000 (or up to £450,000 in London)
  • But you can’t use a Help to Buy ISA if you’re going to rent out the property
  • You also can’t have more than one Help to Buy ISA

What do I need to open a Help to Buy ISA?

The minimum opening balance to open an account is £0 if the Help to Buy ISA is being funded by a cash ISA transfer-in, otherwise the minimum deposit is £1.

To open an account, you need to be a first-time buyer aged 16+ with a valid national insurance (NI) number.

In branch, you'll need at least a single form of ID (such as a passport, driving licence and proof of address, ie utility bill from the past three months).

How to choose a Help to Buy ISA

You can open a Help to Buy ISA account with most well-known high street banks and lenders but the right one for you will depend on the accounts you’re eligible for, what you need and even where you live.

Bear in mind it's also not just the big banks that offer them - regional building societies and even some credit unions have Help to Buy ISA options.

It's important to look at details such as the rate of interest you can earn and how flexible the Help to Buy ISA is.

Right now, Help to Buy ISA accounts can generate interest at a rate of between 2.5% and 3%, although this could change at any time.

You won’t earn interest on your government bonus because you don’t actually get the money until you buy your property.
