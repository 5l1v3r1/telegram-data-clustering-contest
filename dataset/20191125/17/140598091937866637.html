Rugby World Cup player says man he rescued from burning building ran away without saying thanks

25 Nov 2019, 17:19 by Telegraph Reporters

An international rugby player rescued a man from a burning building, who then ran away.

Prop Gordon Reid, 32, pulled the man from a house in the village of Coylton, South Ayrshire, early on Sunday morning.

The Scotland international, who featured at the rugby world cup in Japan, later posted on Twitter: "I am really angry because I put my life at risk."

Police confirmed they believe the fire was started deliberately and have launched an investigation.

Reid dialled 999 before breaking into the house. Firefighters arrived and put out the blaze, while also giving oxygen treatment to Reid, who had suffered from smoke inhalation.

The rugby player said on Twitter: "Just a normal day. Wake up, have breakfast, save a guy's life by pulling him out of his house, which was on fire.

"Then the p---- ran off - didn't even say thanks.

"Now am stuck here sucking on air. Thanks to the firemen for the back up."
