General Election 2019: What are the parties promising on crime and justice?

25 Nov 2019, 17:05

Law and order is a key priority for many voters during elections.

The parties have been keen to tackle voters’ concerns in their election manifestos, so here’s what their plans are on crime and justice.

Conservative

The Conservative Party has pledged to recruit 20,000 new police officers and create 10,000 more prison places.

Boris Johnson has also set out plans to make it easier for police officers to stop and search those convicted of knife crime.

The Tories also say they will introduce tougher sentencing for the worst offenders and end automatic halfway release for serious crimes.

The party has also said it will pass the Domestic Abuse Bill, which will legally require councils to provide secure homes for those who are fleeing domestic violence.

Labour

The Labour party says it will recruit 2,000 more police officers than the Conservatives and restore prison officer numbers.

It also vowed to end the disproportionate targeting of black and ethnic minority communities in police stop and search.

The Labour manifesto also says it will review the controversial Prevent programme, which aims to prevent radicalisation, but which critics have called a blunt tool that disproportionately targets Muslims.

Liberal Democrats

The Lib Dems say they will set aside £1 billion to restore community policing and will develop a public health approach to serious violence.

Jo Swinson says her party will also invest £500 million in youth services to help prevent crime and will recruit 2,000 more prison officers.

A major policy announcement is the legalisation of cannabis, which the party says will bring in £1.5billion in duty and savings through sale in licenced shops to those over 18.

Green Party

The Green Party says it will boost police powers to tackle hate crime, end the war on drugs and halve the prison population.

The party says it is also committed to restorative justice and will significantly reduce the number of short-term prison sentences, in the hope of reducing reoffending.

According to the party’s manifesto, it will also ‘invest in youth services and centres, to help turn at-risk children away from crime’,

Brexit Party

Nigel Farage says his party will focus on increasing police numbers, with a particular focus on tackling violent crime.
