Showcase Cinemas becomes second chain to ban Blue Story after mass brawl

25 Nov 2019, 13:15

Showcase Cinemas has become the second chain to pull gangland movie Blue Story from its venues after a mass brawl broke out in Birmingham.

Over the weekend Vue Cinemas decided to remove the film from 91 of their outlets, sparking accusations of ‘blatant racism’ on social media.

The company said they wanted to protect the ‘safety and welfare’ of customers after a mass brawl broke out in the Star City entertainment complex on November 23.

Around 100 people were involved in the incident, with Birmingham Police seizing two machetes and a knife.

Five teenagers were arrested, including a 13-year-old girl, while seven officers sustained injuries at the scene.

Blue Story depicts the lives of two friends growing up in rival south London postcodes.

The film is rated 15 and warns of very strong language, strong violence, threat, sex, and drug misuse.

Writer and director Rapman, real name Andrew Onwubolu, said on Sunday: ‘It’s truly unfortunate that a small group of people can ruin things for everybody.

‘Blue Story is a film about love not violence.’

Yesterday the phrase ‘No Blue No Vue’ began trending on Twitter as social media users voiced their outrage at the film’s ban.

Adefolahan Oshinloye, a business professional in corporate law, described the ban as ‘institutional racism in practice’.

He told Metro.co.uk: ‘The fact that the perpetrators of the violence were not even from the Black British community – although annoying – is less important than the fact that for some reason Vue has seen fit to punish the creators of Blue Story for the actions of others.

‘Had the violence been committed by black children, it would still not be a good enough reason to issue a blanket ban of a film that promotes an anti-gang violence message at its core.

‘There are clearly issues with youth violence in Birmingham, as there are across the country. It is clearly not race specific, but largely denotes the socio-economic problems across the nation.’

A Vue spokesperson has refuted allegations of racism, stating: ‘The safety and welfare of our customers and staff is always our first priority.

‘We are saddened to have had to take this step, which is not made lightly, particularly as the film itself seeks to strike a strong message against violence.’

A spokesperson for Showcase confirmed that Blue Story has been removed from ‘all of participating cinemas’.

They continued: ‘Any guests that have purchased tickets in advance can receive a full refund at the cinema box office.

‘We remain in discussions with the distributor with regards to the possibility of re-introducing the film in due course.

‘We apologise for any inconvenience but guest safety remains our top priority.’
