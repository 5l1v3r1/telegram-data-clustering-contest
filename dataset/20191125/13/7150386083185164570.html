Jofra Archer Claims Racial Abuse During 1st Test, New Zealand Cricket Offers Apology

The England team and management were caught unawares by Archer’s tweets and that it had not been raised immediately post match.

25 Nov 2019, 13:08

In a shocking revelation on Monday, England pacer Jofra Archer claimed that he was racially insulted by a spectator during the fifth and final day of the first Test against New Zealand at the Bay Oval. A fiery five-wicket burst from Neil Wagner sealed comprehensive innings and 65-run victory for New Zealand over England on Monday.

“A bit disturbing hearing racial insults today whilst battling to help save my team,” tweeted Archer after the completion of the match.
