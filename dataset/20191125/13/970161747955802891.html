Granny and her two young grandsons died after she drove into path of train

25 Nov 2019, 13:49

A grandmother and her two grandsons were killed after a train collided with their SUV, authorities say.

Valery Jo Rintamaki, 58, Trystan Prestano, 10, and Skyler Prestano, 8, were killed in the crash as they drove to a campground near the JW Corbett Wildlife Management area in Jupiter, Florida on Saturday.

Police said Rintamaki failed to yield to a northbound Amtrak train that struck her 2018 Subaru Outback and dragged it a half-mile down the tracks. The family’s deaths are being treated as a tragic accident.

David Peat, the boys’ grandfather and caretaker, told the Palm Beach Post that the train crossing did not have any guard gates or flashing lights to warn of approaching trains.

Peat said he was shocked by the lack of safety precautions and added that the crossing was marked with a yield sign instead of a stop sign.
