Fundraising page started for Sidney family that lost everything in fire

25 Nov 2019, 13:32 by Jeff Bell

A fundraiser has been started for a Sidney family whose house was extensively damaged in a Saturday evening fire.

Janine Taverner and five-year-old daughter Georgia were at home at the time of the fire, but escaped without injury. Janine’s husband, Bob, arrived minutes later.

“You never expect what you hear on the news could happen to you,” says a message posted on the Taverner Fire Fund webpage.

“This GoFundMe page has been set up to help get this wonderful family [started] from scratch, cover any wage loss and ensure Christmas can be celebrated.”

Bob works as a long-haul truck driver and Janine is a stay-at-home mom who runs her own illustration business.

Sidney firefighters could see the blaze from the fire hall, the page said.

“By the time they arrived and were able to contain the fire it was quickly determined that anything that wasn’t burnt was either ruined by water damage or smoke damage.”

The blaze started just before 4 p.m. in the home on Beacon Avenue West, said fire chief Brett Mikkelsen of the Sidney Volunteer Fire Department.

Crews arrived to find large flames coming from the garage area of the one-storey home.

Firefighters extinguished most of the fire from the outside, before entering to put out flames in the attic.

They rescued a hamster, and retrieved passports and Christmas gifts.

The fundraising page is online at gofundme.com/f/taverner-fire-fun.
