Cyberpunk 2077 is an FPS

Cyberpunk 2077 is undoubtedly an FPS game as it includes a chunk of first-person shooting even though the cutscenes and driving often switch to the third person. There are several actions involving the guns, and everything moves with blazing speed although the scenes appear less expensive when compared with Doom. During the opening moments of the demo, the firefight shows the primary character V bent quickly around the cover only to shower bullets on a gang of organ scavengers. Similar to Destiny 2, the numbers indicate how the damage explodes with each shot, although the enemies do not feel like the bullet sponges. The agility of V is an added attraction.

There is a lot of versatility to be noticed in the potential of V in the demo that shows her as a talented and fast-paced cyber ninja. While she could slide and run to cover besides triggering a slow-motion bullet-time mode. A quick dash allowed her to burst into a targeted direction, and with the amalgamation of the dash and the bullet time, she could put the enemies on to one side and deliver those deadly shots. In the demo, we have also encountered some damn cool weapons apart from the guns even though the guns are in plenty. With a Tech Shotgun that is street-modified, it is easy to penetrate through the enemies and the cover that appear to be a deadly combination when paired with the upgraded optic implant of V that showed her the enemies through the objects. Another one is a Smart Rifle that exhibits its ability to shoot by firing bullets that track the enemies. When you aim into a general direction, it will automatically lock multiple targets for firing the rounds on the tracked targets.
