Canucks’ Alex Edler dodges injury bullet, plans to play vs. Flyers

25 Nov 2019, 13:38 by Iain MacIntyre

PHILADELPHIA – As unlucky as they’ve been with injuries at forward, the Vancouver Canucks continue to be favoured on defence by the hockey gods.

Top defenceman Alex Edler skated this morning with teammates and plans to play Monday night against the Philadelphia Flyers, two days after abandoning the Canucks’ win in Washington when he sustained an undisclosed first-period injury.

“I felt good yesterday, so going into today I felt good and the skate went well,” Edler told reporters. “I’ve had a lot of injuries, so I feel like I know my body really well. Today the skate went well.”

Edler, whose average ice time of 24:48 before the Washington game easily led the Canucks, missed 26 games last season due to injuries and 52 over the last three years.
