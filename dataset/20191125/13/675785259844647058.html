Woman, 30, dies after 'slipping into water' returning to Italian yacht from disco

Breffni O’Connor from Co Limerick, is thought to have slipped into the sea when she returned to the yacht in the port of Ancona after a late night disco

25 Nov 2019, 13:45 by Sam Roberts, Lila Randall

An Irishwoman who died in an accident while working on a yacht in Italy has been named as Breffni O’Connor from Co Limerick.

It is believed the 30-year-old slipped into the sea after returning from a late night disco in the early hours of Saturday morning.

Ms O’Connor had been working as crewhand on the yacht in the port of Ancona on the Adriatic Sea, Irish Mirror reports.

Italian media reports that police are currently investigating the incident but it is being treated as an accident with no suspicious circumstances.

Investigators are looking at video surveillance in the port area to try and piece together her last movements, the Irish times reports.

Limerick councillor Emmett O'Brien said that the young woman's family were very well-known in the area, and locals have been left stunned by the news.

He told the Irish Mirror: "The community state of shock significant tragedy young parishioner passing away in such unfortunate circumstances and express my condolences to the family."

Abbeyside Ballinacourty GAA club in Co Waterford also paid tribute to Ms O'Connor.

A spokesperson said: "On behalf of all in the club, we would like to offer our deepest sympathies to the O'Connor family, Limerick and Abbeyside on the tragic death of Breffni O'Connor in Italy on Saturday morning last.

"Breffni's father, Liam regularly travels back to the village to watch our club teams in championship action and has always been a great supporter of every and any fundraising initiative we have undertaken.

"Indeed, Liam was a chief organiser of the very successful and enjoyable sponsored cycle from Limerick to Abbeyside a few years ago.

"Our condolences to Liam and his family at this most difficult time. Ar dheis Dé go raibh sí."

The Department of Foreign Affairs are understood to be providing assistance to the family.
