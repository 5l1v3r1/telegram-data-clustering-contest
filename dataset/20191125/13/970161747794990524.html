What is pidgin English as Prince Charles ditches English during royal tour?

25 Nov 2019, 13:24

Prince Charles won applause from crowds in the Solomon Islands after addressing guests with a speech conducted in pidgin English.

Charles, who has also visited India and New Zealand on his tour, was presented with a chupu – a traditional offering involving a live pig tied up – when he arrived at the Lawson Tama in Honiara, before delivering his speech which spoke of the importance of the environment and his welcome there.

His use of the language was met with laughter and applause – but just what is pidgin English and where is it spoken?

Here’s what you need to know…

What is pidgin as spoken by Prince Charles in the Solomon Islands?

A pidgin language is a grammatically simplified means of communication which develops between two or more groups who do not share a common language.
