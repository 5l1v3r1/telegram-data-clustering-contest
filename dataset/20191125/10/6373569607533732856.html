Joe Edwards: Plymouth Argyle's players cannot wait to see new grandstand full with fans

The £8m-plus grandstand will open for the first time against Morecambe on December 14

25 Nov 2019, 10:32 by Chris Errington

Joe Edwards says he and his Plymouth Argyle team-mates are ‘lucky to be a part of this club’ as the £8million-plus Mayflower Grandstand is close to reopening.

Home Park has been a three-sided stadium since the start of the 2018/19 season due to the redevelopment work.

However, the rebuilt 5,403-seat grandstand will be open to 50 per cent capacity for the League Two game against Derek Adams’ Morecambe on December 14.

That will increase to 75 per cent for the visit of Stevenage on December 29.

The grandstand will then be fully open for the New Year’s Day game against current league leaders Swindon Town, taking the capacity of Home Park to 18,200.

The crowd for that fixture could be close to that limit given the current form of both teams.

The new dug-outs at the front of the grandstand were used for the first time when Edwards scored one of the goals in Argyle’s 2-1 win against Bradford City on Saturday.

Edwards said: “It’s exciting even just walking out and seeing it (the grandstand). It looks fantastic and it will look even better when it’s full.

“Hopefully we will can get it full and really rocking. If we are up near the top of the table the fans will be there in their numbers.

“Everyone has done a fantastic job on it and we are just lucky to be a part of this club at the moment when it has been built. We are really looking forward to it being open.”

Argyle’s average home attendance after nine league games at Home Park this season is 9,453.

That is second best in the division, behind only Bradford (14,105).

Swindon are third (7,326) followed by Leyton Orient (5,736), Northampton Town (5,182) and Exeter City (5,124).

Argyle have won four of their last five league games to move up to eighth position.

With the return of Adams to Home Park coming up, followed by two post-Christmas fixtures there is every chance of 10,000-plus crowds for the next three matches there.

Edwards said: “We are on a good run and I think if we are in and around it at Christmas we can really kick on after that. Who knows where it can take us.”

For now, though, Argyle’s attention turns to the FA Cup and their second round tie away to Bristol Rovers on Sunday.

Argyle lost 1-0 to Rovers in a pre-season match at Home Park at the end of July.

The teams then drew 1-1 in an EFL Trophy group game in early September, with the Pilgrims winning the subsequent penalty shoot-out 5-3.

Now they are set to meet again, but this time at the Memorial Stadium.
