Elon Musk makes Rick and Morty cameo and now his Twitter alter ego makes sense

25 Nov 2019, 10:42

It finally makes sense why Elon Musk changed his name to ‘Elon Tusk’ on Twitter earlier this year and the answer lies in his recent Rick and Morty cameo.

The 48-year-old co-founder and CEO of Tesla appeared on season four, episode three of the hit cartoon, entitled One Crew Over the Crewcoo’s Morty.

He played, you guessed it, Elon Tusk – basically exactly like Elon Musk, but with tusks.

Warning: Spoilers ahead!

During the episode, Rick and Morty surprise Elon at his company headquarters, aptly titled Tuskla.

Morty hurriedly told Elon: ‘Listen, we need your help!’

Then Mr Tusk replied: ‘Rick, Morty – why me? Did something happen to Elon Musk in your own reality?’

He went on to explain Elon Tusk and Elon Musk ‘aren’t opposites’, one just has tusks.

The rest of the episode sees the team complete a series of random tasks, like Elon taking out a turkey from the oven and putting it in the fridge, as well as playing Minecraft.

Probably easier to explain if you just watch the episode…

Back in February, the billionaire mysteriously changed his Twitter name to Elon Tusk, complete with an elephant emoji and now we know why.

At the time, fans speculated the alter ego (which, of course, used the initials ET) had something to do with aliens.

And they were kinda right.

People also had a hunch the name change could have had something to do with Fleetwood Mac’s twelfth studio album, Tusk.

Alas, that guess was way off.
