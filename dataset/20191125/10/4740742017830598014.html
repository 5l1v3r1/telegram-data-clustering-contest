Former Bristol City and Manchester United man linked with Everton return as pressure on Marco Silva grows

It would be a sensational return to Goodison Park

25 Nov 2019, 10:59 by David Byrom

David Moyes has been linked with a sensational return to Everton.

The former Bristol City defender is out of work at the moment having not managed since leaving West Ham United in 2018.

Yet he has regularly been linked to jobs in that period, most recently being hotly-tipped to take over at former club Preston North End should they lose Alex Neil.

However, it now looks like he might make a return to another of his former clubs.

The Mirror report that the Goodison Park hierarchy have held talks with Moyes over the prospect of him replacing Marco Silva at Everton.

Silva has underachieved as Toffees boss with an underwhelming campaign so far seeing them in 15th in the Premier League, just four points above the relegation zone.

A 2-0 defeat to Norwich City over the weekend could well have sealed Silva's fate, although a Goodison Park return for Moyes would raise eyebrows, with the Scot having enraged a number of Everton fans following his move to Manchester United in 2013.

Moyes, who spent two years at the Robins and is still held in high regard by many fans at Ashton Gate, would likely return to the Toffees on an initial short-term deal to try to steer the club away from relegation.

The 56-year-old has previously told Bristol Live that he would fancy taking charge of the Robins one day if he was ever offered the job.

He said: ""I would never say no, that's for sure. I was very close to becoming Bristol City manager before Tony Pulis went - I was close to it. I was manager of Preston and I was having quite regular contact with Bristol City at the time.

"And I said okay I would do it but in the end it didn't quite materialise or I would have been manager at that time.

"But it's something that I would never say never to because it was such a great part of my career and I had a great rapport with the supporters and the people in the city so it was something I really enjoyed."
