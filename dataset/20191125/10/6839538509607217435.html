Torana key to cold case 'murder' of teens

25 Nov 2019, 10:56

A green Holden Torana sedan could be key to solving the suspected murders of three Lake Macquarie teenage girls with police saying the pain of the families "lingers to today".

Detectives believe Robyn Hickie, 18, and Amanda Robinson, 14, were killed in 1979 before Gordana Kotevski, 16, was murdered in 1994.

Robyn left her home on the evening of April 7 1979 and was last seen standing at a bus stop on the Pacific Highway in Belmont North.

Amanda Robinson, 14, was last seen a fortnight later on April 21 1979 near a bus stop in Swansea after attending a high school dance.

Ms Gordana was forced into a vehicle while walking from Charlestown Square Shopping Centre - also in Lake Macquarie - on November 24 1994.

Police on Monday made a public plea for information on the cases saying the mid-1970s Torana was central to the disappearance of the two girls in 1979.

It's unclear if the green sedan was connected to Ms Gordana's 1994 disappearance.

Despite investigations at the time, and over the years, none of the girls has been located.

A subsequent coronial inquest into their disappearances found the three girls were deceased - most likely as a result of foul play.

NSW Police Detective Chief Inspector Greg Thomas hopes photos of a similar green Torana will jog memories in the Lake Macquarie area.

The car is thought to have travelled between the area and Queensland.

"Whether it's still even on the road we don't know - but it's a very specific car," Det Chief Insp Thomas told reporters in Belmont on Monday.

"Going back 40 years to when these disappearances occurred in this area, there could be someone who has knowledge of that vehicle.

"The car has become a focus, not the sole focus, but a focus of investigators."

Det Chief Insp Thomas said the families of all three missing girls continue to suffer.

"It (the pain) lingers to today, and that's why the investigators treat every inquiry to its highest," he said.

"What it gives them is the confidence ... that police are continuing to investigate these disappearances."
