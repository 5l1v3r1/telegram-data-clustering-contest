25 Nov 2019, 10:58 by Gautam Kapoor

CAN Vs CCC Dream11 Team And Match Report: Out of the reckoning for a spot in the next round, today’s opponents left with little to fight for.

For both Canada and Combined Colleges and Campuses, this instalment of the Super50 Cup couldn’t have been any more horrendous. Both sit slumped in the trenches of Group A of the showpiece tournamnet, befittingly so with the outfits being routed and thumped in their sorties to see their sojourns being wrapped up at an early stage.

What will especially perturb the two units is not just the fact that the failed to make the most of a pristine chance to showcase their capabilities but completely faltered in their aspirations. The two have fallen flat of their faces, left latching at little else than straws with the writing on the wall clear for them ever since their tournamnet openers.

For Canada in particular, the backlash the side have faced for their showings is completely justified. The side have been taken swipes and jibes at with their gaffe showings unbecoming of a side of the calibre of an international setup with the team managing to muster up a sole win in their seven outings with their howler of a run extending to four successive defeats as things stand.

The come into this clash having been routed by 7 wickets by Barbados who needed just 23 runs to peel off the sorry 155 run target laid out infront of them with 7 wickets to spare.. Elsewhere, Combined Colleges and Campuses haven’t fared any better with the side being ousted out of the tournamnet as well with the five defeats from seven matches although they did manage to defeat Jamaica by two wickets the last time around as they chased down 273.

Pitch Conditions And Toss

A reasonably good wicket for the bats, this is a surface where the team batting first is going to come out and look to post anything above 250. With the team chasing winning on a mere two occasions out of the 12 matches contested over here, it’s apparent that the team winning the toss will be looking to come out and set a target today.
