The entire humanity together ought to start working, concentrating on underpinning and strengthening the part of compassion, love, and sympathy from enriched Islamic philosophy into the overall human psyche. 

Islamophobia may not be confused with the so-called xenophobia. In the ideological debate, the term Islamophobia become a popular term in 2000, referred to as the dread, abhorrence of, or prejudice against the Islamic religion particularly, or Muslims generally, mainly when seen as a geopolitical force or the cause of extremism. Very recently, in Norway’s southern city of Kristiansand over the weekend, some extremist people of the right-wing group Stop the Islamization of Norway (SION) burned and tor apart the copy of the Muslim’s Holy Book Qur’an (a central religious text of Islam). The action has been condemned and upsurges from top to toe annoyance in the Muslim world.

Besides, it is not the single case that the SION has hurt Muslim feelings around the world.In June 2019, in a speech by the SION’s leaders during the event in town Drammen, Anna Braten, the leader clearly said that Islam has no place in Norway and all their Qur’an and Masjids (Mosques) must be destroyed at any cost. In Norway, most of the time, the feeling of Muslims have been hurt either through the burning of the Qur’an, destroying their worship-places, or insulting them in public.

On August 11, 2019, an unknown gunman opened fire on the Al-Noor Islamic Centre (ANIC) in the capital Oslo, some people died on the scene, while the rest were afraid to go back to their desired places.  The above recent actions have prompted debates over whether the Muslim population is getting their fundamental rights in the country or not?.
