House okays 3rd package of tax reform plan

25 Nov 2019, 10:42 by Jester P. Manalastas

THE House of Representatives has approved on final reading the 3rd package of the comprehensive tax reform program of the administration.

House Bill 4664 or the Valuation Reform Bill got 224 affirmative votes, seven negative and one abstension.

The bill seeks to reform the government’s  property valuation and assessment system which  is expected to provide additional revenues of P30.5 billion.

Albay Rep. Joey Salceda, chairman of the House Committee on Ways and Means, is the principal author of the bill.

According to Salceda, the measure will enable LGUs to achieve stable financial resources and a strengthened local real property tax base to fund service delivery requirements of the LGUs toward transparent governance and increased public performance.

The VRB aims to improve real property valuation of government by adopting a single market-based schedule market value (SMV) -- a table of base unit values for all kinds of real properties within an LGU prepared by assessors -- to be used as basis of local and national real property taxation.

It also seeks to address the perennial problems in real property valuation attributed to failure of LGUs to update their SMVs that leads to values not reflective of real market.

The House leader added that the measure will benefit not just the LGUs but also the national government in terms of valuation of right-of-way acquisitions.
