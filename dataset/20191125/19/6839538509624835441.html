Man dies after three vehicles crash on Dukes Highway in state's South East

25 Nov 2019, 19:23

The state's road toll has risen to 100 after a man, involved in a three-vehicle crash near Tintinara, has died. Four more are injured.

At 5.15pm on Monday, the car collided with a truck and ute 6km south of Tintinara, on the Dukes Hwy.

The driver of the car, a 78-year-old man from Victoria, died at the scene while the driver and three passengers of the ute sustained serious injuries.

A helicopter with specialist MedStar personnel on board airlifted two of those people to Adelaide and the other two were taken to the Murray Bridge Hospital in an ambulance.

The truck driver wasn't injured in the crash.

The Dukes Highway was closed at Keith with traffic being diverted around the scene of the crash, but has since reopened.

The road toll was 69 at the same time last year.
