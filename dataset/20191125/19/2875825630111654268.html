Ed Woodward told he must contact Mauricio Pochettino to 'do his job' at Man Utd

Man Utd chief Ed Woodward has been warned he must contact Mauricio Pochettino whilst he has the chance if their form under Ole Gunnar Solskjaer does not improve

25 Nov 2019, 19:57 by Irishmirror.ie

Manchester United chief Ed Woodward has been warned he would not be doing his job if he didn't contact Mauricio Pochettino whilst he is out of work.

Pochettino, heavily linked with United after Jose Mourinho's sacking last December, is on the market after being replaced by Mourinho as Tottenham boss last week.

The Argentinian manager is already attracting plenty of interest as he plots his next move, whilst United are still languishing in mid-table after a 3-3 draw with Sheffield United on Sunday.

Ole Gunnar Solskjaer is expected to be given time to rebuild the Red Devils despite mixed results as he approaches 12 months at the helm.

But former Liverpool star Jamie Carragher has told United bosses they at least make contact with Pochettino over the festive period if their form does not improve.

"The top six teams, all believe they should be challenging for the league," Carragher told Sky Sports Monday Night Football.

"There will always be two or three who are being criticised for what they are doing.

"There is no doubt Pochettino is the one they wanted and Solskjaer did that well, it forced United to give him the job.

"He should be allowed in January to get what he needs.

"At the top of Manchester United , when they get to Christmas and January and this continues, and there is a guy like Pochettino who would take them forward.

"They would not be doing their job if they weren't in contact with him."

United legend Gary Neville, meanwhile, urged Solskjaer to "protect himself" by splashing the cash on the players he needs in January.

"It was the most predictable thing in the world," Neville said.

"Two and a half years ago, I said Mauricio Pochettino should be appointed before Jose Mourinho.

"I can't say I don't think he would be the right person.

"But Ole has been appointed and he has to be selfish now. He has to have that ruthless streak that Mourinho or Conte has and spend money to protect himself.
