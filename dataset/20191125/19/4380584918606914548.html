Tigers land RHP Agrazal from Pirates for cash

25 Nov 2019, 19:02

DETROIT -- The Tigers announced on Monday they have acquired right-hander Dario Agrazal from the Pirates in exchange for cash considerations.

Agrazal, 24, saw time at three levels of the Pirates' organization in 2019, including his first Major League action. Agrazal had a 4-5 record with a 4.91 ERA and 41 strikeouts in 15 appearances (14 starts) with the Pirates. The 6-foot-2, 240-pound starter also combined to make 16 starts between Double-A Altoona and Triple-A Indianapolis, compiling a 5-5 record with a 4.45 ERA and 74 strikeouts, while walking just 12. He was named the International League Player of the Week for the week ending May 5 after going 1-0 with a 1.32 ERA and 13 strikeouts over two starts.

A native of Aguadulce, Panama, Agrazal has made 114 appearances (111 starts) in his Minor League career, and he has a 38-36 record with a 3.62 ERA and 394 strikeouts. He was ranked as the No. 26 prospect in the Pirates' organization by MLB Pipeline following the 2018 season. Agrazal was selected to the New York-Penn League All-Star team in '15 and the Florida State League All-Star team in '17.

With the acquisition of Agrazal, the Tigers 40-man roster is now at 40.
