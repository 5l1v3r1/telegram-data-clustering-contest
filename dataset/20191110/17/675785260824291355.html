Ole Gunnar Solskjaer lauds Man Utd's best performance of season against Brighton

Manchester United moved up to seventh in the Premier League table with a 3-1 victory over Brighton at Old Trafford

10 Nov 2019, 17:27 by David McDonnell, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/david-mcdonnell/

Ole Gunnar Solskjaer hailed his “exhilarating and entertaining” Manchester United side after their goal glut against Brighton .

Solskjaer said he did not know how his United side failed to score more after what he described as their best performance this season.

United moved up to seventh with strikes from Andreas Pereira, Marcus Rashford and an own goal from Brighton's Davey Propper, with Lewis Dunk scoring for the visitors.

“That was our best performance of the season,” said Solskjaer. 'It was an excellent performance from the lads. When you see them going forward, it's a joy to watch.

“How it didn't end up five, six or seven, we don't know, but it was exhilarating and entertaining and great to see the boys enjoy themselves.

“These six weeks since the last international break, when we started against Liverpool , we've had five wins and a defeat.

“Losing at Bournemouth was disappointing, but we've had loads of answers to what we're trying to do.

“It's been great these two games, with loads of attacking play. We should have had more goals, but the intentions are there and they're enjoying themselves

“They young players are performing and learning. They've had some tough lessons, but they've learned from them.

"The table doesn't really matter at the moment. We need to keep improving, keep winning games and see where it takes us.”

United winger Dan James said: “The high press paid off, that’s what we have been about all season, playing with energy right from the start.

“We were a little bit edgy when they scored and it was good to score again on the break. But we could have scored seven or eight goals.”

Rashford missed an open goal from a James delivery, moments after making it 3-1, but the Wales winger said: “Marcus normally scores a lot of those goals, but thankfully when he missed we were already 3-1 up.”

The only downside for United was an ankle injury to Scott McTominay, which saw the midfielder taken off on a stretcher late on.

McTominay will have a scan today but is unlikely to go on international duty with Scotland, while the full extent of the damage to his injured ankle is assessed.
