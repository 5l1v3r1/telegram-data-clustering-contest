Slaughtneil beat Dunloy to win third Ulster Hurling Championship in four years

10 Nov 2019, 17:18 by Diarmuid Pepper

Slaughtneil have overcome Dunloy Cuchullains to get their hands on the Four Seasons Cup for the third time in four years.

The Derry champions defeated Antrim’s Dunloy by 1-15 to 0-10 in today’s Ulster Hurling Senior Club Championship final at Pairc Esler.

And while an eight-point victory may look comfortable on paper, Slaughtneil were forced to dig deep to see off the Antrim champions.

In the first half both goalkeepers proved their worth and pulled off acrobatic saves when a goal seemed certain.

With little more than five minutes on the clock, a smart handpass was fed into Slaughtneil's Brendan Rogers. He found a gap in the Dunloy defence and drove the ball towards the bottom corner of the net from close range.

But Dunloy ‘keeper Ryan Elliot was up to the challenge, extending his hurl to tip the sliotar around the post and out of play.

At the other end of the field, Slaughtneil's Oisin O’Doherty made an equally good save. O’Doherty was due to start on the bench but from himself starting between the sticks instead.

On the 17th minute, he had to be at his best to divert a Ronan Molloy shot onto the post and away from goal.

At the end of the first half, Slaughtneil lead 0-8 to 0-6 but soon after the restart their task was made somewhat easier.

In the 38th minute, Dunloy’s Neil Elliot received his marching orders for a second yellow card.

However, Dunloy kept battling and with a quarter of the game remaining they were still in contention, trailing 0-8 to 0-11.

But as the game was about to enter the final 10 minutes, Slaughtneil were awarded a penalty.

Cormac O’Doherty struck the penalty and found the back of the net, giving Slaughtneil a 1-11 to 0-8 lead.

Dunloy tried their best to bounce back and scored the next two points.

But Slaughtneil struck over four unanswered points to pull away from Dunloy and finish out the game with a scoreline of 1-15 to 0-10.
