Starting XI: Liverpool v Man City

10 Nov 2019, 17:39 by SL International

Manchester City will be out to end Liverpool's unbeaten in the Premier League when the two sides clash in an eagerly awaited clash at Anfield - Here's how they line up.

English Premier League

Teams: Liverpool v Manchester City

Date: 10 November 2019

Venue: Anfield

The match is set to get underway at 18h30.

The Citizens have already lost twice this season and they can’t afford to drop further points especially against their rivals after coming from behind to beat Southampton 2-1 last weekend.

City come six points adrift of the Reds and their concern will be the unavailability of goalkeeper Ederson, who was ruled out with a thigh injury, as his replacement Claudio Bravo will be making his first Premier League start in 18 months.

Meanwhile, Liverpool are yet to lose a league match so far this season, with their 1-1 draw Manchester United towards the end of October being the only blemish.

The Reds will be without defender Joel Matip who has not recovered from a knee injury, although Dejan Lovren will line-up alongside Virgil van Dijk.

Liverpool’s only league defeat last season came at the hands of Man City at the Etihad Stadium, after the sides played to a goalless draw in the first round clash in October 2018 at Anfield.

The Citizen don't have the best of records at Anfield, having lost tasted victory in Merseyside back in 2005.

Here's how they line up:

Liverpool: Alisson; Alexander-Arnold, Lovren, Van Dijk, Robertson; Fabinho, Wijnaldum, Henderson; Mané, Salah, Firmino.

Manchester City: Bravo; Walker, Stones, Fernandinho, Angeliño; Rodri, Gundogan, De Bruyne; Bernardo, Sterling, Agüero.
