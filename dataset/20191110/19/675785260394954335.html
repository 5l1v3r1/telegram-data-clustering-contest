Man City's defensive disarray will cost them the title - not VAR and referees

Manchester City's miserable run at Anfield - one win in 29 visits - continued in a match which left Pep Guardiola raging at some controversial VAR calls

10 Nov 2019, 19:14 by Andy Dunn, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/andy-dunn/

As he slumped into his bucket seat, Pep Guardiola spat something out.

It was hard to tell what it was so let’s just call it his disgust.

Mohamed Salah’s head had just added insult to the injury inflicted by Fabinho’s sweet right boot and the game was up for City.

The vagaries of VAR and Liverpool’s ruthlessness on the counter-attack had combined to put Guardiola’s hopes for this particular contest to the sword.

And in truth, the title game was probably up a while ago. Up when Pep failed to sign a high-quality central defender in the summer after Vincent Kompany left, up when Aymeric Laporte collided with Brighton’s Adam Webster at the end of August.

 

Forget any perceived injustices here. In the grand scheme of things, they are largely irrelevant.

The simple fact is that whatever defence Guardiola fields is not a defence truly befitting of such stellar champions.

No matter how eye-pleasing they are in possession - and they were again here - their defending is best watched from behind a sofa.

The snapshots of their disarray were scattered across Anfield.

Angelino clearly believing his inclusion was some sort of daydream, Kyle Walker and John Stones arguing as though they were a couple of hungover pub players.

 

Fernandinho wasting his energy hectoring Michael Oliver, Claudio Bravo only ever looking remotely comfortable when the ball was at his feet.

Good for an outfield player, trouble for a man with big gloves.

But while City might have been better off with Walker in goal, a fit Ederson would not have saved this game for City.

This is a very good team with one serious issue. They look like conceding against any decent attacking outfit they come up against.

Raul Jimenez, Adama Traore and Patrick Cutrone gave them the runaround last month - no wonder this crack Liverpool unit got such joy.

No wonder Sadio Mane appeared to be the only body and mind in the City box able to chart the flight of Jordan Henderson’s cross in adding the third.

 

There were other contributing factors in this defeat.

Raheem Sterling’s persistence was exceptional and came with the normal amount of invention but his final choice of option was unusually flawed.

Throw in the fact that Oliver gave him very little sympathy and it was a predictably frustrating afternoon for him even though he was a constant menace.

Ditto Sergio Aguero, a quarter-stride from his standard sharpness. It was that quarter-stride that saw him pass up several invitations from Kevin De Bruyne.

And in general, their finishing was a long way from the clinical level until Bernardo Silva’s sidefooted effort set up an interesting finale.

 

Of 18 attempts at goal, City managed to get just three on target.

Compare that with Liverpool getting five of their 12 attempts on target.

That would have been as much a source of Guardiola’s exasperation as the failure of Oliver and VAR to do City and favours.

It has to be said that while this was a deserved win for Liverpool, they would be very happy to welcome Oliver back in the future, I’m sure.

And inevitably, that Guardiola exasperation boiled over and took him out of that bucket seat to confront fourth official Mike Dean.

He held up his fingers to count the number of injustices he considered had been inflicted on him and his team.

Then, to accompany his handshake of Oliver, he thanked the referee "soooo much".

Who said sarcasm was an English peculiarity?

But if he wanted to sarcastically thank anyone, it should have been his defence.

It cost him this football match, it will cost him the title.
