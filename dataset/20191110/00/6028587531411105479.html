Only Watch Auction Raises Over $38-Million for Charity, Including $31-Million Patek Philippe, A New World Record

10 Nov 2019 by Carol Besler

A one-of-a-kind Patek Philippe Grandmaster Chime Ref. 6300A sold for a hammer price of CHF 31-million ($31,117,400) today at Only Watch, a charity auction conducted by Christie’s and held in Geneva. Watch brands donate special editions, usually one-of-a-kind, to the auction. (The watches were sold in Swiss francs, which is roughly equivalent to US dollars). The next four top lots after the Patek included the F.P. Journe Astronomic Blue, which sold for  CHF 1.8-million (the watches were sold in Swiss francs, a currency that is roughly the equivalent in US dollars); the A.P. Code 11.59 Tourbillon for CHF 1-million; the Akrivia Chronometre Contemporain for CHF 360,000; and a Tudor Black Bay Ceramic One for  CHF 350,000.

The Only Watch auction has been held biennially since 2005, with 100% of proceeds going to benefit research towards a cure for Duchenne muscular dystrophy, a genetic disorder that strikes children. To date, Only auctions have raised more than $40 million, but today’s sale will add CHF 38,593,000 to that total. The auction was started by Luc Pettavino after his son, Paul, was diagnosed with the disease and passed away in 2016 at the age of 21. Luc Pettavino received the Special Jury prize at the Grand Prix d’Horlogerie de Genève two days ago. Here are the top five watches from today’s sale:

1. The CHF 31-million Patek Philippe Grandmaster Chime reference 6300A-010 was created specially for Only Watch, and it is the only one that will ever be made in stainless steel. It has four spring barrels driving 20 complications, including a grande and petite sonnerie, a minute repeater, instantaneous perpetual calendar with a four-digit year display, second time zone, day/night indicator, day/date (on both dials), month, leap-year cycle, four-digit year display and 24-hour and minute subdial.

2. The F.P. Journe Astronomic Blue, which sold for CHF 1.8-million, over an estimate of CHF 300,000-600,000. It’s an astronomical complication, with sidereal time, dual time zone, day/night and sunrise/sunset indicators, moon phase, equation of time, and a one-minute tourbillon. It is also a minute repeater. And the movement is made of 18k gold.

3. The Audemars Piguet Code 11:59, a unique piece, is an openworked tourbillon with a two-tone case emphasizing its architectural complexity, with an octagonal middle case and an 18k gold bezel, lugs and caseback. It sold for CHF 1-million over an estimate of CHF 190,000-$240,000.

4. The Akrivia Rexhep Rexhepi Chronometre Contemporain, a platinum watch with a manual wound chronometer movement with hours, minutes, hacking seconds and automatic zero reset. It has a 100 hour power reserve from one mainspring barrel. The dial is hand-hammered enamel. It sold for CHF 360,000.

5. The all-black Tudor Black Bay Ceramic One, a diving watch with a case made of matte black ceramic with a bead-blasted finish. The unidirectional rotating bezel is PVD-treated titanium with a matte-black ceramic disk. It is water resistant to 200 meters. It sold for CHF 350,000.
