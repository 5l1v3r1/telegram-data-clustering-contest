Holiday Gift Guide 2019: The Best Books For Armchair Travelers

10 Nov 2019 by Lois Alter Mark

Studies show that anticipating a trip can make you happier than actually taking it, so giving these travel books may be even more of a gift than you thought.These books – memoirs, travelogues, guides, cookbooks – will transport readers around the world, providing a rich experience whether they’re seasoned travelers or simply dreaming from their couch.

In the 16 years since “1,000 Places to See Before You Die” was originally published, millions of travelers have turned it into their ultimate bucket list, checking off places one by one and using it to plan their next trips.

This new coffee table book, weighing in at a whopping five pounds, has been completely redone for the Instagram age, with an emphasis on stunning photography that will make you want to see every place in person. Covering locations from Abu Dhabi to Zurich, this is the gift for travelers this year. Price: $50

The next best thing to actually spending the holidays at Highclere, this beautiful book takes you inside the iconic castle so you can imagine you’re celebrating with Lord and Lady Grantham.

The Countess of Carnarvon – Highclere’s actual resident – generously shares photos, personal stories and recipes, welcoming you for a country weekend as if you were an old friend. You’ll get a real insider’s view of a place that already feels like home. If only the book could be hand-delivered by Mr. Carson or Mrs. Hughes. Price: $49.95

This delightful little volume is a bibliophile’s dream, focusing on 25 places that have been featured in classic novels and remain in the hearts and imaginations of readers.

Travel to Holden Caulfield’s mid-century Manhattan and Cathy and Heathcliff’s Yorkshire Moors as well as Don Quixote’s La Mancha and Atticus Finch’s Monroeville. Sarah Baxter provides fascinating context to Amy Grimes’ lovely illustrations as well as proof that reading can truly be a journey in itself. Price: $19.99

Scuba divers and those aspiring to be will be tempted to don their wetsuits and grab their gear after turning the pages of this breathtaking book.

This underwater travel guide from National Geographic is filled with awe-inspiring images. Colors pop and marine life amazes. The book is organized by diving experience and certification level, and offers tips from expert divers, but even if you never get in to the ocean, you’ll feel like you’ve been granted VIP access to explore a world most of us will never get to see. Price: $35

French cookie makers extraordinaire Michel et Augustin have put together a fun, easy-to-follow guide to baking your favorite pastries including eclairs, pralines and millefeuilles.

They take all the intimidation out of French baking, offering step-by-step, illustrated instructions for making flaky layers, perfectly textured croissants and the creamiest creams. If you can’t get to Paris, this is the next best thing. Well, after buying their cookies. Price: $29.95

At the age of 24, after losing her mom to breast cancer, Sarah Jane Douglas was struggling and often in trouble. When she decided to start climbing Scotland’s highest peaks in honor of her mother, her life turned around.

This inspiring memoir follows her journeys as she ultimately becomes Munroist number 5764 (meaning she has climbed all of Scotland’s mountains that are more than 3,000 feet high). Douglas, who writes the popular blog, Smashing Cancer in the Face, is down-to-earth and relatable, and her book has a breezy tone that sucks you in. Her hillwalking actually grounds her and reminds all of us that sometimes all you can do is put one foot in front of the other and just keep going. Price: $16.53

Monisha Rajesh spent seven months circumnavigating the globe via rail with her fiancé, reliving the romance of train travel along with its quirkiness. Along the way, they meet a wild variety of passengers and learn valuable lessons in history, culture and patience.

Because not every train is the luxurious Venice Simplon-Orient-Express on which they indulge in chicken oysters and damask sheets, the couple experience different levels of comfort and safety as they make their way from London to Russia to the United States. “Americans who have never ridden on their railways have no idea what they are missing,” writes Rajesh. Her first-hand account changes that and is likely to cause a surge in Amtrak ridership. Price: $28

A real treat for foodies, this stunning art book is as much about restaurants’ design as it is about their cuisine.

Delicious Places is like a culinary trip around the world, where you get to visit eclectic eateries like the Pink Zebra in Kanpur, India, whose décor is rosy and striped, and the striking, nautically-inspired Bazaar Mar in Miami. It’s a joy to be able to learn about these restaurants you may never get to see in real life – and start to make a list of the ones that just may warrant a trip. Price: $55

This mouth-watering cookbook transports you to Canada’s backcountry, and will warm you up on a cold winter night even if you never make any of the recipes.

Katie Mitzel spent 20 years as a chef in some of the most remote and beautiful lodges nestled in and around the Rocky Mountains, and her cozy book makes you feel right at home. There’s a quiet elegance to both her words and photos, and you will wish these dishes – oh, the breads, the soups, the Chocolate with Chocolate on Chocolate Salted Cookies! – could pop off the page and onto your plate. All of her lovingly-created recipes reflect the beauty of their surroundings, and will leave you feeling full and content. Price: $25

This charming little tome should be on every New Yorker’s shelf and distributed to every book lover headed that way.

A celebration of everything literary, from the city’s best bookshops and libraries to the homes and haunts of world-famous writers, the book itself is a piece of art, peppered with drawings by New Yorker cover artist, Pierre Le-Tan. Find a quiet place to sit and read (Le-Tan has some delightfully surprising recommendations) and immerse yourself in a literary land where anything is possible. Price: $40
