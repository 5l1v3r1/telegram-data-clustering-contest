New Surface Details Confirm Microsoft’s Louder Sound

10 Nov 2019 by Ewan Spence

Following the innovation packed into the Surface Pro X and the Surface Laptop 3, what is Microsoft planning for the next generation of Surface hardware? As always, the published patents from the R&D teams at Redmond give us a pretty good idea, and it sounds like the Surface Pro could receive an audible upgrade. The recently published patent, titled “Expandable Enclosure For Electronic Device Resonance Box” describes a way to increase the effectiveness of a speaker. Mayank Parmar has taken a closer look:

"The idea described in the patent would definitely make sense and it could significantly improve the speaker quality of thin devices like Surface Pro.

“An electronic device, comprises a display and an expandable enclosure positioned opposite the display. The expandable enclosure is operable between at least a closed configuration and an open configuration, such that in the open configuration, the expandable enclosure defines faces of a resonance box,” the filing reads.” 

Naturally, the appearance of a patent does not mean that a feature will be included in the retail release - and a glance at the specifications of the newly announced Surface hardware shows that it is not present.

But a second glade at the new Surace hardware shows that the internal design is changing. The Surface Pro X is geared towards easier repair and includes an easily removed SSD, while the Surface Laptop 3’s key components (such as the screen and the keyboard) are also easier to switch out by an authorised repair centre.

The Surface Pro 7 has not received as extensive a redesign as the Laptop 3 or picked up the features that have arrived in the Surface X. I’d expect the customer feedback to influence the presumptively named Surface Pro 8 to carry the same repairability influence.

And that redesign leaves scope for hardware features such as the aforementioned speaker.
