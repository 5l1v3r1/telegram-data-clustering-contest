Celtic fan stabbed in Rome says 'cowardly' Lazio ultras 'just wanted to hurt somebody'

Conor Weir spoke of the terrifying moment masked men jumped out of a car, grabbed him from behind and knifed him the back.

10 Nov 2019, 12:33 by Mark McGivern, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/mark-mcgivern/

Celtic fan Conor Weir reveals he's lucky not to to have been badly hurt after he was jumped by cowardly ultra fans.

The 20-year-old, one of three fans to be attacked in Rome, was treated in hospital but still managed to finish off the celebrations of a historic win on Friday night.

His flight was delayed because of the hospital stay and Conor, of Bo’ness, West Lothian , escaped with just three stitches in a back wound after masked men jumped out of a car and ran up behind him.

He said: “I wasn’t wearing a Celtic strip, just a green t-shirt, but they must have been driving round looking for people to target and I was unlucky enough to get it.

“It was cowardly. They weren’t looking for a fight, just to hurt somebody and escape without getting any comeback.

 

“It was over so fast I hardly knew what was happening until I felt a sensation in my back and I felt blood on my top.

“The other three people I was with scattered in panic and I tried to run myself but they grabbed me and stabbed me before I could break free.”

Conor said he became aware of a car stopping as he approached a McDonald’s restaurant on the main Viale Giulio Cesare street, near the Vatican at 2.30am on Friday.

He had been partying and in high spirits but was preparing to get to bed when the attack happened.

He said: “I’m ok and I’m lucky to escape with what I did.

“I got a local person to phone an ambulance and they were brilliant, very calm and they got it sorted right away.

 

“After I got to hospital I was given a few stitches and I was out within a few hours. I phoned home to say I was ok then I went back and had some rest.

“But I was determined not to let some idiots spoil my trip, so I was out again on Friday night and had a great time. It was one of the best ever results for Celtic in recent times and it was great to be there to see it in person.”

Conor, who works for construction form Norbord Europe in Cowie, which makes MDF boards, was the third of three Celtic fans to be stabbed.

Two other fans were jumped at the Flann O’Brien bar on Via Nazionale, where travelling supporters were enjoying a drink on Wednesday night.

Another bar in the trendy Trastevere part of the city was raided by ultras on Wednesday night, smashing chairs against a window as Celtic fans enjoyed a peaceful drink inside.

Celtic’s 2-1 win was their first in Europe against an Italian team.

The tie looked certain to bring trouble after ultra fans marched through Glasgow a fortnight ago, making fascist salutes in tribute to former dictator Benito Mussolini.

In turn, Celtic ultra made their own anti-fascist protest, erecting a banner that depicted Mussolini hanging upside down and telling ultras top “follow your leader”.

The Green Brigade and many other fans also repeatedly chanted “F*** Lazio” and another “Brigate Verde” banner appeared to be based on the logo of a left-wing terrorist organisation.
