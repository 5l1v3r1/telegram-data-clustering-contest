Coca-Cola confirms Christmas truck tour is returning for 2019

It's official, holidays are coming and the Coca-Cola Christmas truck will once again be doing the rounds in the UK spreading festive cheer

10 Nov 2019, 12:20 by Courtney Pochin, https://www.facebook.com/courtneypochinmirror/?modal=admin_todo_tour

It's as synonymous with Christmas as the John Lewis advert.

Each year, when you hear the jingle and see the illuminated red truck drive across your television screen, you know that holidays are most certainly coming.

We're talking of course about the Coca-Cola Christmas truck, which features in the company's festive ads each year and goes out on a tour of the UK.

And if you're yet to see the joly sight in person, then we've got some very good news for you - it's returning once again for Christmas 2019.

 

Coca-Cola have confirmed that the truck, which brings free drinks to cities across the UK, will be setting off on tour in December 2019.

No official tour dates or stops have been released as of yet.

However in a message on their website, a spokesperson said: "Holidays are coming... soon!

"The iconic Coca-Cola Christmas truck tour will shortly be back for 2019, spreading holiday magic across the UK! Details will be revealed very soon."

 

Coca-Cola's iconic trucks were first created in 1995 by the W.B. Doner Agency and back then they were known as 'Christmas Caravans'.

The illuminated lorries were made more enchanting with special effects by the world-famous Industrial Light and Magic, the company behind the Star Wars films.

Images of Santa Claus adorned the sides of the trucks, which were created by artist Haddon Sundblom.

Since then, the iconic image has undergone various changes to become the fleet of trucks you see today.

Last year the truck visited 24 different locations on its tour, including Glasgow, Exeter, Newcastle, Bristol, Manchester and London.

To find out where it will stop this year, keep an eye on www.coca-cola.co.uk/christmas
