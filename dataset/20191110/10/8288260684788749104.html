Fire crews rescue trio following late night car crash in Sheldon

West Midlands Fire Service sent two fire engines with eight crew to the scene of the smash in Sheldon. One woman suffered minor injuries in the late night crash

10 Nov 2019, 10:52 by Cathrina Hulse-BM

Three people who were involved in a late night car crash have escaped with minor injuries.

The trio were in a car travelling along Sheldon Heath Road on Saturday (November 8) when the car crashed.

West Midlands Fire Service sent two fire engines with eight crew on board.

Eyewitnesses said one or more of the occupants had to be cut from the vehicle but West Midlands Fire Service were unable to confirm this.

The drama happened at 9.15pm.

A spokeswoman for West Midlands Police said: "We were called to a single car collision at the junction of The Radleys and Sheldon Heath Road in Sheldon , at about 9.10pm on Saturday, 9 November. No injuries reported."

West Midlands Fire Service said: "We were called at 9.15pm to three people in a vehicle. 

"One man and a woman had self-extricated when we got there and a woman in the vehicle had minor injuries."
