Families terrorised by prowler wearing balaclava who peers through their windows

Several homes have been targeted by the prowler who has scared his victims by staring into their homes in Stoke-on-Trent

10 Nov 2019, 10:46 by Hayley Parker, Lorraine King

A balaclava clad man is terrorising families by staring at them through their windows.

Police have launched a manhunt in Stoke-on-Trent following reports from petrified victims of a man behaving suspiciously outside their homes.

They initially received one report but since then other residents told StokeonTrent Live they too were victms.

A family member of a witness told StokeonTrentLive: "I believe he is targeting specific houses and he is studying the occupants of these homes.

"My granddaughter walked into the kitchen to see a man wearing a balaclava watching her through the kitchen window.

"Then on another night they went out on the spur of the moment and immediately after they left someone dressed in black wearing a balaclava was going around the house looking through windows.

"He was only scared away by a neighbour across the road who he then targeted two days later.

"My granddaughter was scared witless and hid behind the couch for ages before getting the courage to go go upstairs to tell her mum what happened."

It is believed at least three houses were targeted before 7.30am on November 6.

Another victim said: "When I saw him, he was wearing a dark duffle coat. It was really unnerving. It's like he gets a kick of scaring people."

Police are now appealing for witnesses to get in touch.

A  Staffordshire Police  spokesman said: "Police received a report of a man behaving suspiciously on Stansmore Road in Meir.

"He was seen peering through the windows of three houses."

Anyone with information should message Staffordshire Police on Facebook or Twitter quoting incident number 84 of November 9. Alternatively, contact 101 or call Crimestoppers, anonymously, on 0800 555 111
