Plymouth man, 25, dies after late-night crash into wall

The crash happened at the junction of North Prospect Road and Ferndale Road

10 Nov 2019, 10:11 by Katie Timms

A 25-year-old man from Plymouth has died after a late-night crash.

An emergency rescue was carried out by firefighters at the scene after a red Ford C-Max, which was being driven by the male, crashed into a wall.

Devon and Cornwall Police was called to the single-vehicle collision at 1.38am today (Sunday, November 10) at the junction of Ferndale Road and North Prospect Road.

Fire crews from Camels Head and Crownhill were sent to the incident and the man was taken to  Derriford Hospital.

A Devon and Somerset Fire Service spokesperson said: "Crews from Camels Head and Crownhill in Plymouth were mobilised following a request from the police to assist after a car had collided with a wall trapping the driver.

"Due to the medical condition of the casualty the crew had to carry out an immediate extrication shortly after arriving, alongside ambulance paramedics, whereby they were conveyed to hospital via land ambulance.

"Once extricated crews made the vehicle and scene safe, before leaving duty of care with the police."

Devon and Cornwall Police has now confirmed that the man died as a result of the collision.

A police spokesperson said: "At 01:38 hours on Sunday 10th November 2019, Police were notified of a single vehicle road traffic collision at the junction of Ferndale Road with North Prospect Road, Plymouth (OSGR 045973, 057381).

"The vehicle involved, a red Ford C-Max, was being driven by a 25-year old local male. No other vehicles are thought to have been involved in this collision.

"Police thank the public for their patience while they investigated the scene."

Officers from the Alliance Roads Policing Team attended the scene and a full investigation took place.

Officers are working to establish the cause of this collision.

Anyone with information, or may have witnessed the collision or seen the vehicle in the Ferndale Road area at this time is asked to contact Devon and Cornwall Police on 101, quoting log 84 of November 10, 2019, or email 101@dcpolice.co.uk
