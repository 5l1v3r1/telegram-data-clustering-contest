Reports: Arsenal Contact Emery's Replacement

10 Nov 2019, 10:31 by SL International

With the pressure mounting on Arsenal manager Unai Emery, the North London club have reportedly contacted a possible replacement.

Read: Emery Responds To Rumours Mourinho Could Replace Him

The Gunners had another disappointing away day after suffering a 2-0 loss to Leicester City at the King Power Stadium on Saturday evening.

Former Manchester United manager Jose Mourinho has been linked as a possible replacement, but reports in Spain claim Arsenal chief Raul Sanllehi has made contact Former FC Barcelona tactician Luis Enrique.

Following the poor the run off form recently, El Confidencial reported that Sanllehi has drawn up a list of possible replacements but has been in contact with Enrique.

Enrique has been out of work since resigning as head coach Spain in June this year for personal reasons.

However, Emery refused to believe that his position is under threat as the club extended their winless run to five matches across all competitions.

"For me, as a coach, it's about continuing stronger and improving with our players," he said.

"I am speaking with the club to stay calm and to stay patient to improve and recover some confidence with some circumstances.

"We are receiving criticism but I know that's my job. I've had lots of moments like that but I've recovered by working to get confidence, being together with the club and players.

"I accept all (of the criticism). I accept the applause and I accept it when they criticise us. I know when we win, they are going to be happy and when we lose, they are going to be sad. It's normal.

"We need to stay calm and also keep improving things. Today we got one step ahead by being together and being strong defensively. But we lost because we were playing a very strong team in a good moment."
