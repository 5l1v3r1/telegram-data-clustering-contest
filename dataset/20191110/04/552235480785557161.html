Celtic fan's terror after being stabbed in back by masked Lazio thug in Rome ambush

Conor Weir was knifed while in the Italian capital by a Lazio hooligan following Thursday night's Europa League victory.

10 Nov 2019, 04:30 by Mark McGivern, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/mark-mcgivern/

A Celtic fan has told how he was ambushed and stabbed by Lazio hooligans as he celebrated his team’s win in Rome.

Conor Weir, 20, was with friends at a McDonald’s near the Vatican hours after Thursday night’s match when a car pulled up and masked thugs jumped out.

He was stabbed in the back and the yobs fled.

Conor, of Bo’ness, West Lothian, was rushed to hospital in an ambulance.

He phoned his dad Derek from there on Friday morning to tell him what happened and was due to arrive home last night.

Derek said: “The first I heard of it was when Conor rang. His phone had been off overnight while he was getting treatment.

 

“He said he’d gone to a McDonald’s at the end of the night with his pals and a car pulled up and guys with masks and hoods on jumped out. They stabbed him and then got away in a car.

“The whole thing was over very quickly. Conor was treated in hospital and the doctors were happy to release him the next day.

“He hasn’t wanted to make a big deal out of it.”

Another source said of Conor: “The last thing he was looking for was aggro. Conor and his mates were getting to the end of their night and weren’t expecting any bother.

“Their digs were quite close to the Vatican and they’d gone for a McDonald’s on a big street so they didn’t think that would be a likely location for getting jumped.

 

“It was obviously planned because the guy who stabbed him had a hood up and a mask on. The knife had a short blade and didn’t pierce any important areas.”

Conor – who works for wood panel makers Norbord Europe in Cowie, Stirlingshire – was attacked on the Viale Giulio Cesare, in the Prati area of Rome.

He was the third Celtic fan to be stabbed in the city.

On Wednesday night, two were knifed at a bar three miles away on Via Nazionale.

Lazio “ultras” also targeted Hoops supporters who had to walk into the city centre after the game – which Celtic won 2-1 – when their shuttle bus broke down. At least 12 Italian fans were arrested.
