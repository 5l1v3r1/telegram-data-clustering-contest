Jose Mourinho explains why he was "upset" after Liverpool's win over Man City

Former Manchester United boss Jose Mourinho hit out at the VAR decision from the fifth minute of Liverpool's Premier League win over Manchester City

10 Nov 2019, 21:38 by Jake Polden, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/jake-polden/

Jose Mourinho said the inconsistency of VAR left him feeling upset following Liverpool's Premier League win over Manchester City.

VAR was called into action in just the sixth minute of Sunday's title crunch match at Anfield after Liverpool opened the scoring through Fabinho.

Trent Alexander-Arnold had handled the ball in his own area in the build-up to the goal, but a VAR check ruled his arm wasn't in an unnatural position and no penalty was awarded.

Manchester City boss Pep Guardiola was incensed by the decision, and sarcastically thanked referee Mike Riley after the final whistle.

Mourinho discussed VAR in the Sky Sports studio following the game and sympathised with the Spaniard, admitting the technology's inconsistency upsets him.

"I think we've spoken enough about it and I think the main question is just consistency," said the former Manchester United boss.

"That's what really upsets me in the game, in this moment, that one weekend it's not a penalty and next week it's going to be a penalty. For me that's the crucial point of it.

"The game was so good and there is so much to speak about the game that I think enough is enough.

On Liverpool's impressive counter-attack move, which directly lead to the goal, the Portuguese added: "Yeah of course [City could have prevented the goal], there's no doubt about it.

"And I think everybody knows how strong Liverpool is in these situations.

"Everybody knows how they play and the way they manage to only leave [Mohamed] Salah and [Sadio] Mane in privileged positions for these transitions.

"Everybody knows the positions they like to attack and you see the way they score both goals, the second and the third.

"City is too good to be now crying for a week about an incident, they should focus also on the game, what they could do better."
