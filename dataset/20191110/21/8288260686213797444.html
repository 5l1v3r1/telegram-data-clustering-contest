Liverpool fans will love this Premier League prediction - but Manchester City fans won't

Premier League prediction latest | Jurgen Klopp's side widened the gap to second-placed Leicester City to eight points with the Reds hoping to end a 30-year wait for a title

10 Nov 2019, 21:04 by Josh Challies

The latest international break is upon us, with 12 Premier League matches gone and the table starting to take shape.

The final weekend of action before the break saw a number of eye-catching results, with Liverpool extending their lead at the top by defeating Manchester City, Leicester City beating fellow top-four rivals Arsenal and Newcastle United picking up another valuable three points.

Now, the significance of those results has been determined by a supercomputer created by FiveThirtyEight , which has predicted the outcome of all the vital battles in the Premier League this season.

Overall, it’s great news for Liverpool as Jurgen Klopp’s side are given a 67% likelihood of finally bringing an end to a 30-year wait for a league title, with City now just 29% likely to lift the title for the third season in a row.

Beneath them, Frank Lampard’s stellar work at Chelsea is predicted to be fruitful come May as the Blues are given an 81% likelihood of securing Champions League football, with Leicester 63% likely of booking their spot in Europe’s elite competition.

That means it’s not good news for Tottenham, Manchester United and Arsenal, all of whom are given less than a 20% likelihood to secure their spot in midweek European action next term.

Meanwhile at the bottom of the table, the prediction clearly believes Norwich City are doomed as they are handed a 74% likelihood to suffer an immediate return to the Championship, with Southampton 43% likely to join them.

The final spot is claimed by Watford, handed a 39% likelihood of relegation, with Aston Villa at 30%, West Ham at 28% and Newcastle at 20% all surviving to enjoy another campaign in the top-flight.

Wolverhampton Wanderers are backed for a mid-table campaign, with their chances of being relegated and qualifying for the Champions League at five per cent and three per cent respectively.
