MH360 in air-turn back due to landing gear issue, says Malaysia Airlines

10 Nov 2019, 07:37

PUTRAJAYA, Nov 10 — The Malaysia Airlines flight MH360, which departed from Kuala Lumpur to Beijing yesterday evening, experienced an air-turn back due to a landing gear issue.

In a statement today, Malaysia Airlines said in the interest of passenger safety, the operating captain made a decision to return to Kuala Lumpur International Airport (KLIA).

“The aircraft landed safely at 9.03 pm on the same day and is currently being rectified by engineers.

“The flight MH360, which will be serviced by another aircraft, departed Kuala Lumpur at 12.30 am on Nov 10 and arrived in Beijing at 6.35 am on the same day,” the airline said.

Malaysia Airlines added refreshments were provided to all passengers and all onward connections were arranged.

“Safety is Malaysia Airlines’ number one priority at all times and it regrets for the inconvenience caused to its passengers,” it added.

It was reported that the flight MH360 departed KLIA at 6pm yesterday and was scheduled to arrive in Beijing, China at 12.20 am today. — Bernama
