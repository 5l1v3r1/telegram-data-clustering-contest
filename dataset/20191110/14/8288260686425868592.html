Unemployed mum in shock after total stranger gives her £32,000 at Jobcentre - for this incredible reason

'In the long run it’s going to be a complete change to my life' - woman reveals the amazing gift given to her just because she was in exactly the right place at the right time

10 Nov 2019, 14:28 by David Bentley, https://www.facebook.com/davidbentleybm/

An unemployed single mum is in total shock after an amazing gift was handed to her when she went to her local Jobcentre.

Jodie Graygoose was handed £32,000 by a total stranger as she waited for a routine appointment.

And it was all because the 30-year-old mum, who lives with her eight-year-old son Riley, sat in the right seat at the right time (10.10am to be exact) on that particular day.

The time slot had been decided in advance and it was all down to who sat in the chair at that moment.

Jodie says she was suddenly approached by a man who turned out to be Peter Kelly, CEO of Imployable, and left stunned by what he did.

Jodie told our sister title Plymouth Live : "It was half term, we got to the Jobcentre in the pouring rain, and this guy came over and asked if we could have a chat.

"I didn’t mind, I was there early anyway. I thought it would waste some time while waiting.

"We went in and I was like, ‘Wait, what?’ I’m thinking, this isn’t happening. Surely he’s going to say he’s just joking. Apparently it wasn’t a joke.

"He said ‘I want to gift you 1 per cent' and I was in shock. I wouldn’t claim to know anything about shares and don’t know what one per cent means. I’m terrible with numbers, so when he was writing it down I was like, ‘Come on, dumb it down for me.’"

She added: "When I went to the offices to meet the team, we sat down and he talked me through it. When he told me I was like, ‘Oh my God, you must be crazy to give somebody you don’t know that kind of money.’

"He said it was his way of giving back. People ask me how I feel and I still don’t think it’s really happening. I had to go to my Jobcentre appointment after, then rang my sister. She was like, ‘Are you sure?' And I didn’t know. We were very sceptical about it.

"Obviously now I know it's real. I speak to Pete quite often and ask how it’s going. I don’t know who these people are half the time but it’s nice to know what’s going on."

 

"In the long run it’s going to be a complete change to my life.  I’m not ready to give it away yet, though. I think it could make a lot more and he’s capable of doing very big things. I can put money into it and get more shares, but I’m just letting it sit."

"I just happen to be the person who sat in that seat. Wow. The Jobcentre was quite busy, it could’ve been anyone that sat there. I’m in shock really, it’s surreal."

'It’s £32k. The possibilities are endless, it’s crazy. Maybe I’d buy this house? I know people my age who are married and own a house, I’m like ‘Hang on?! How have you managed that?’

Her day-to-day life hasn't changed yet, though.

"I drop to Riley to school, do a bit of housework and some shopping. Obviously I’m unemployed at the minute, which majorly sucks. I am hoping to get hired. I volunteer at a children’s centre."

Peter Kelly said: "It is very rare that you get the opportunity to change someone's life directly, this campaign was about giving a gift to someone who is deserving of a break whilst inspiring entrepreneurs to increase their social value and close the gap between businesses and society.

"I hope that people in the same position as Jodie can be inspired, be open to opportunity and achieve more than their circumstances dictate."

Six other companies have already signed up to follow Peter Kelly's example and are set to gift equity totalling over £300,000.
