Strictly producers 'ditch Michelle Visage from arena tour' in last minute snub

Strictly Come Dancing's contestants are hitting the road next year for a tour, but some of the cast have been left out

10 Nov 2019, 09:30 by Tiffany Wallis

Michelle Visage will not appear on Strictly Come Dancing 's glitzy arena tour next year after reportedly being snubbed by producers.

According to the Daily Star Sunday , the contestant, 51, was given the news over email after telly bosses decided they didn't need her.

A Strictly insider told the publication: "Michelle was approached by the producers working on the tour a while ago."

"They talked her through the dates and asked if she could be available.

"But then suddenly she got an email saying she wasn’t needed. The message was short and blunt."

After the 51-year-old telly star was told she wasn't going to be included on the tour, the source told the publication Michelle was obviously "upset."

They added: "The tour is a chance for the celebs to cash in on their success on the show.

"Michelle is going to miss out on all of that."

 

The decision is likely to disappoint many Strictly fans as the mother-of-two continues to fight for the Glitterball trophy with partner Giovanni Pernice.

The judge on RuPaul's Drag Race was the first contestant to reach a lofty score of 39 when she performed the foxtrot on  Halloween .

In last weekend's show the couple once again flew close to the top of the leaderboard when they netted 34 for their stunning Paso Doble.

In last night's show , Michelle and Giovanni danced the American Smooth to I Just Want To Make Love To You by Etta James and received a standing ovation.

The judges praised the star for her sexy housewife performance.

Former Strictly champion Stacey Dooley , 32, is making her comeback to the dance floor as she returns as the new host of the live tour.

Stacey will be joined for 32 shows around the country by many of the celebrities and professional dancers from the current series of the BBC One show.

The tour will open in Birmingham on January 16, before travelling to Aberdeen, Leeds, Manchester, Dublin, Belfast, Glasgow and Nottingham, before ending at London's O2 Arena on February 9.
