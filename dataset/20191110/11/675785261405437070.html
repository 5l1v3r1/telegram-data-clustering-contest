Remembrance Day: The Queen wipes tear from cheek as Kate Middleton stands next to her

During the two minutes silence at 11am Her Majesty, who was dressed all in black, was seen with a tear on her cheek as she stood on a balcony with Kate Middleton and Camilla Parker-Bowles

10 Nov 2019, 11:45 by Brendan McFadden, Danya Bazaraa

The Queen was seen wiping away a tear from her cheek during a special wreath-laying service this morning on Remembrance Day.

The Royal Family lead the nation in paying respects at the traditional service at the Cenotaph on Whitehall in central London.

During the two minutes silence at 11am Her Majesty, who was dressed all in black, was seen with a tear on her cheek as she stood on a balcony with Kate Middleton and Camilla Parker-Bowles.

Princes William and Harry followed their father in laying wreaths during the ceremony, as Kate Middleton and Meghan Markle watched the ceremony from balconies.

Political leaders, including Prime Minister Boris Johnson and Labour's Jeremy Corbyn , took a break from election campaigning to attend the service at the memorial.

Hundreds of armed forces personnel were present at the event, alongside Cabinet ministers, religious leaders and representatives of Commonwealth nations.

After the two minutes silence the King's Troop Royal Horse Artillery fired a gun.

Following the ceremony, thousands of veterans and servicemen and women are to march past the Cenotaph to pay their respects to those killed in past and present conflicts.

In a video released on his  Twitter  account before the ceremony, Mr Corbyn paid tribute to the work of the Armed Forces both current and throughout history.

He said: "We remember the many brave people from Britain and all across the world who put their lives on the line making huge sacrifices in two world wars which cost the lives of millions, and in all other conflicts since.

"And we stand together to say: Never again."

During the ceremony thousands of veterans and servicemen and women marched past the Cenotaph to pay their respects to those killed in past and present conflicts.

Buglers of the Royal Marines sounded the Last Post before the wreaths were laid by members of the royal family, politicians, foreign representatives and senior armed forces personnel.

Five former prime ministers, Sir John Major, Tony Blair , Gordon Brown, David Cameron and Theresa May were at the event.
