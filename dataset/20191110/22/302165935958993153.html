WWII film ‘Midway’ tops N. American box office

10 Nov 2019, 22:55

LOS ANGELES, Nov 11 — Midway, a new take on the pivotal naval battle between the US and Japan in the Pacific during World War II, opened atop the North American box office this weekend with an estimated US$17.5 million (RM72.3 million) in ticket sales, an industry group said yesterday.

Director Roland Emmerich’s PG-rated film from Lionsgate looks at the four-day battle between Japanese and US aircraft carrier groups in June 1942 that is widely considered a turning point in the war in the Pacific.

Also in its debut week, the Warner Bros. horror flick Doctor Sleep, based on a novel of the same name by Stephen King, came in second with US$14.1 million in revenue, Exhibitor Relations said.

In this followup to King’s horror classic The Shining, Ewan McGregor plays the now-adult Danny Torrance, who grapples with alcoholism as he endures the trauma of his creepy childhood at the Overlook Hotel and tries to help a young girl with the same “shining” sensory power. 

In third place for the Friday-to-Sunday period, with US$12.8 million, was Playing With Fire, a Paramount comedy about a crew of tough firefighters who rescue three feisty siblings and end up having to babysit for them. It stars John Cena, Keegan-Michael Key and John Leguizamo.

Last Christmas, a rom-com from Universal about a British woman who works as an elf in a year-round Christmas store and falls for a guy who is charming, good-looking and almost suspiciously cheerful, finished fourth with an US$11.6 million take at the box office.

In fifth was Paramount’s Terminator: Dark Fate the sixth and latest instalment in the storied franchise. It reunites Arnold Schwarzenegger and Linda Hamilton with series creator James Cameron and cost US$185 million to make.

Rounding out the top 10 were:

Joker (US$9.2 million)

Maleficent: Mistress of Evil (US$8.0 million)

Harriet (US$7.2 million)

Zombieland: Double Tap (US$4.3 million)

The Addams Family (US$4.0 million) — AFP
