Violent thug who battered pensioner, 71, on the loose after escaping prison

Police have issued an urgent public appeal to find Shane Douglas, 41, who was serving 10 years for aggravated burglary at HMP Springhill in Buckinghamshire

10 Nov 2019, 15:18 by Brendan McFadden

Police are urgently appealing for help to capture a violent thug who has escaped from prison in Buckinghamshire and have warned people 'not to approach him'.

Shane Douglas, 41, was serving 10 years for aggravated burglary after battering a 71-year-old man in his own home during a burglary .

Douglas who fled from HMP Springhill in Buckinghamshire yesterday is described as black, around 6ft 5ins tall with a stocky build.

He is known to frequent the Hammersmith and Brixton areas of London and is clean shaven, with a cropped haircut or shaved head.

Investigating officer Detective Sergeant Justin Harris said: “I am appealing for information which will help us find Shane Douglas.

 

“If you see him, please do not approach him, instead call us on 999.

“Anyone with any other details should call us on the non-emergency number 101, quoting reference 43190350864.”

Douglas was convicted of breaking into an elderly couple's home in Fullham, west London and stealing a gold Rolex watch of great sentimental value to the man he attacked.

In the raid he demanded cash and jewellery from the elderly man and his 63-year-old wife.

When the man confronted him,  Douglas threw him to the ground and punched and kicked him, the Sun Online reports

He also stole a gold Rolex watch from the victim, which holds great sentimental value.
