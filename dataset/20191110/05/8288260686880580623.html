Remembrance Day in Birmingham: How the city and surrounding areas have remembered the fallen since the end of World War II

We've trawled our picture archive to share moments of remembrance from the city and surrounding towns and areas since the end of World War II

10 Nov 2019, 05:30 by Scott Edwards, https://www.facebook.com/scottedwardsjournalist/

The video above shows moments from history captured on camera from Remembrance Day memorials in and around Birmingham since the end of World War II.

At BirminghamLive, we have trawled the picture archives to pull some of our most powerful and telling pictures captured from the Hall of Memory in Birmingham's Centenary Square in 1945, to soldiers paying their respects at the National Memorial Arboretum in Staffordshire in 2018.

This year, the annual Remembrance Day Service in Birmingham will take place on Colmore Row, opposite St Philip’s Cathedral, on Sunday, November 10, attended by military veterans and Armed Forces representatives.

November 11 marks the day in which the Armistice agreement of 1918 was signed, signalling the end of the Great War, World War I, on land, sea and air between the allies and Germany.

Since then, the date and the significance of the poppy have been used to remember the fallen and wounded from every major conflict.

 

Hundreds turn out every year for the Remembrance Day services held in and around the city.
