Logan Paul vows to "contest the commission" after being docked points in KSI defeat

The American YouTube star was left raging after the decision cost him victory in the main event fight from Staples Center in Los Angeles

10 Nov 2019, 06:28 by Aaron Flanagan, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/aaron-flanagan/

Logan Paul has been left furious by the referee's decision to controversially dock him points in his defeat to KSI.

The American YouTuber was two points in the fourth round after being found to have punched KSI after he had been knocked down.

It turned out that the two points were crucial, as he lost 56-55 on the third and final judges' decisive scorecard.

One judge scored the fight 56-55 in favour of Paul, the other scored it 57-54 in favour of KSI, before the final and decisive card ensured the split decision result.

After paying tribute to KSI and congratulating him for his victory, Paul fumed at the official for the costly decision.

"I thought it was a legitimate knockdown," Paul said. "I didn't get a warning.

"I got two points taken away from me and that's the reason I lost tonight. I would love to contest the commission because I think I should have got a warning. I don't think it was fair at all."

Paul was then asked whether he wants to fight KSI for a third time.

He said: "I'd love to fight KSI again, these are the best moments of my life.

"I just want to say fair play to KSI, you are one of the toughest people I know and I wish you the best. I can talk trash but it's all for show."

However, KSI ruled out a third fight, claiming: "It's done."

KSI added: “I'm on to the next thing. You should do MMA. You should fight CM Punk.

“I moved around quite a bit. I got him a lot to the body, to slow him down.

“I felt good and I was happy. It's different and I took some big shots.”
