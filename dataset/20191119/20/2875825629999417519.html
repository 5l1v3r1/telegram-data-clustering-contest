Jose Mourinho's stance on replacing Mauricio Pochettino as Tottenham Hotspur manager

Tottenham chief Daniel Levy sacked Mauricio Pochettino on Tuesday night after a disappointing start to the season, and Mourinho is the bookmakers' favourite

19 Nov 2019, 20:38 by Irishmirror.ie

Jose Mourinho is ‘open’ to returning to Premier League management as Tottenham Hotspur boss, following the exit of Mauricio Pochettino.

The Portuguese is the bookmakers’ favourite to replace the Argentine, after he was unceremoniously dumped by club chief Daniel Levy.

Just five months after leading Spurs to the Champions League final, Pochettino held crisis talks with Levy during the international break.

And Levy declared that the decision was made "in the club's best interests.”

"We were extremely reluctant to make this change and it is not a decision the Board has taken lightly, nor in haste,” said Levy in a club statement.

“Regrettably domestic results at the end of last season and beginning of this season have been extremely disappointing.

“It falls to the Board to make the difficult decisions - this one made more so given the many memorable moments we have had with Mauricio and his coaching staff - but we do so in the Club's best interests.

“Mauricio and his coaching staff will always be part of our history. I have the utmost admiration for the manner in which he dealt with the difficult times away from a home ground whilst we built the new stadium and for the warmth and positivity he brought to us.

"I should like to thank him and his coaching staff for all they have contributed. They will always be welcome here.

“We have a talented squad. We need to re-energise and look to deliver a positive season for our supporters.”

Mourinho has been without a club since leaving Manchester United last December but had initially wanted to return to action in the summer.

And it’s understood that Mourinho would be keen on taking charge in north London, having been snubbed by Bayern Munich recently.

Bayern for their part currently have Hansi Flick in caretaker charge but are admirers of Pochettino.

They could now turn to the Argentine on a permanent basis, but if Pochettino doesn’t wish to take charge in Bavaria immediately, then they will likely stick with Flick until the end of the season.

Bayern had little interest in turning power at the Allianz Arena over to Mourinho.

The question now is whether Daniel Levy will be willing to do so at the Tottenham Hotspur Stadium.
