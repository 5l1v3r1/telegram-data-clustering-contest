Over 40,000 pairs of socks collected during ToastyToes campaign

Since the campaign got its start in 2013, close to 127,000 pairs of socks have been donated

19 Nov 2019, 20:42 by KitchenerToday Staff

It was another successful ToastyToes campaign in Waterloo Region.

The founder of the campaign says 40,500 pairs of socks were collected from October 10 to November 10, the most the campaign has ever brought it.

And since the campaign got its start, close to 127,000 pairs of socks have been donated.

Sharon Gilroy-Dreher launched the campaign in 2013 in memory of her mother, who always had cold feet and loved giving socks to people.

The socks will now be distributed to shelters in the area.

Here are some more highlights from the 2019 campaign Gilroy-Dreher shared with KitchenerToday.com:

  • Over 100 organizations participated (businesses, schools, groups) - the most that have ever participated
  • 27 tech companies collected 16,038 pairs. That’s an increase from 25 tech companies last year with 15,932 pairs. The most tech companies and the largest donation ever from this sector
  • Biggest donation this year was (tech company) Smile Rewards with 3,496 pairs. This is not our single largest donation since starting, but our largest donation this year
  • 19 schools participated (private, elementary, secondary, post-secondary) collecting 9,324 pairs. That’s an increase from 13 schools last year with 5,027 pairs.  The most schools we’ve had participate and the biggest donation from the education sector
  • Biggest school donation was Empire Public with 1,509 pairs - the largest school donation ever

The campaign will run again in 2020 from October 10 to November 10.
