'SEX ATTACK' PROBE

Man charged with raping tragic Ayr mum Kirsty Robertson before her ‘unexplained’ death

19 Nov 2019, 20:10 by Ben Archibald

A MAN has appeared in court after he was charged with raping a tragic mum-of-two before her “unexplained death”.

David Hose, 29, was arrested in connection with the suspected serious sexual assault of Kirsty Robertson, 29, later found dead in her home in Ayr.

He appeared on petition at the town’s Sheriff Court in connection with the alleged incident in October, the same month she died.

Separately detectives launched a probe into Kirsty’s death after a postmortem was carried out.

A Police Scotland spokeswoman said: “Police can confirm that a 29-year-old man has been arrested in connection with a serious sexual assault of a 29-year-old woman in Ayr.”

And she added on the death probe: “Our enquiries are ongoing and the death is being treated currently as unexplained.”

Hose appeared on petition on 4 November. He made no plea and was granted bail.

No future dates have yet been set.

It’s understood Kirsty had no immediate family other than her two children after her parents and brother died.

A close pal started a fundraiser to pay for her send-off - and raised a whopping £3,000.

Samantha Hart wrote on JustGiving: “Kirsty was an absolute diamond and an amazing mum to her two kids who meant the world to her.”

Officers made door-to-door enquiries and were seen searching her property and the street for clues after her death.
