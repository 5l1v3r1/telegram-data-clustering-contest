Josh McDaniels Addresses Tom Brady’s Frustration With The Offense

19 Nov 2019, 20:19 by Andrew McCarty

On Sunday afternoon, the New England Patriots exacted their revenge over the Philadelphia Eagles from their Super Bowl loss.

However, even after the 17-10 win, quarterback Tom Brady and company were less-than-pleased with their play on the field. Specifically, Brady was unhappy with the play of the offense.

Brady shared his “frustration” with the offense after the win.

Offensive coordinator Josh McDaniels responded to Brady’s comments on Tuesday. McDaniels said he doesn’t take Brady’s comments as “negative.”

Here’s what he had to say via Pro Football Talk:

“I’ve been with Tom a long time and I appreciate his competitive spirit, his desire to be perfect in everything he does and with everything that our unit does. I don’t ever take that as a negative. I understand he wants to go out there and do what we all do which is to put a lot of points on the board every week.”

Brady’s frustrations should begin and end with himself. The 42-year-old quarterback consistently missed open throws, completing just 26-of-47 passing for 216 yards.

The only offensive touchdown for the Patriots came on a touchdown pass from wide receiver Julian Edelman to fellow receiver Phillip Dorsett.

Next up for the Patriots is a home game against the Dallas Cowboys.
