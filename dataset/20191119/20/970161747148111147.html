Painter told police that wind blew bag of cocaine into his car

19 Nov 2019, 20:31

A painter caught with cocaine in his car tried to blame the wind for blowing the powdered drug into his vehicle, police say.

Joseph Zak, 37, reportedly offered the unusual excuse after being pulled over by police in Fort Pierce, Florida, on November 15.

Zak caught their attention after he failed to yield at a stop sign that evening, according to The Smoking Gun.

After flashing their lights, an officer reportedly noticed Zak making ‘furtive movements’ inside his moving sedan.

He was reportedly seen ‘leaning over towards the center console as (if) attempting to discard an item out of the passenger window.’

Zak eventually pulled over, with an officer who got out of the car spotting him trying to hide an open can of Budweiser as he walked up to its window.

The vehicle was then searched, with officers finding a glass crack pipe, as well as a clear baggie filled with white residue, which later tested positive for crack cocaine.

Zak denied the drugs were his, and said ‘the police or the wind must have placed it there.’

He was subsequently charged with multiple offenses, including drug crimes, and was booked into St Lucie County Jail.

Zak is now free in bond, and will appear before a court for an arraignment hearing on December 3.
