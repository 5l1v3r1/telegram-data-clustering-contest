Emily Triggs offers resilience and hope on sophomore album, Middletown

19 Nov 2019, 13:04 by Eric Volmers

When a friend first heard the songs on Emily Triggs’ new album, Middletown, she immediately reached out to the singer-songwriter.

“She sent me an email asking me if I was OK,” says Triggs with a laugh. “Because a lot of the songs are about loss. She knows me very well. So I guess she was just concerned.”

An initial spin of the Calgary-based artist’s 10-song sophomore record does suggest there is more than a little darkness lurking beneath the explorations of love, loss and heartbreak. On the mid-tempo country-blues lament Baby Don’t, the weary narrator sings “my hands keep on burning from trying to relight this flame” to an aloof partner. Triggs evokes ghostly menace on the haunting, slow-burn folk ballad Run From Me, singing “with everyone listening and all the eyes stare, you wrap up your poison and take it again.” But there is light at the end of the tunnel, or at least a vow to persevere. “If it breaks my heart, I pick up the broken parts,” she sings on the achingly beautiful If It Breaks My Heart.
