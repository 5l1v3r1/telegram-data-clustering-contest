Projected 2020 NFL draft order: Who has the No. 1 pick?

19 Nov 2019, 13:15 by ESPN.com

The two-win Miami Dolphins are no longer projected by the ESPN Football Power Index (FPI) to pick No. 1 overall in the 2020 NFL draft. The Cincinnati Bengals are the new favorites to get the top pick, with the Dolphins now projected to pick No. 3. Miami also is projected to have the Nos. 17 and 24 picks, according to our 1-32 draft order projections.

The Dolphins traded cornerback Minkah Fitzpatrick to the Steelers to add another first-round pick, and they already had an extra first-rounder after trading Laremy Tunsil to the Texans.

The Jacksonville Jaguars, meanwhile, own the Los Angeles Rams' first-round pick after the Jalen Ramsey trade. That pick is projected to be No. 19.

Each week during the season, the FPI projects the draft order by simulating the remainder of the season 10,000 times. Game probabilities are based largely on the model's ratings for individual teams and game locations. The order is based on each team's average draft position, which is determined by the record the model believes the teams will have after 16 games.
