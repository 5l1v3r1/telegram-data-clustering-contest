Court date for teacher who denies charges of indecent assault and sexual assault of children

Richard Giles Taylor will have his case heard in August

19 Nov 2019, 13:52 by Matthew Bunn

A primary school teacher has pleaded not guilty to 18 charges relating to the sexual touching of children.

Richard Giles Taylor, 53, appeared at Nottingham Crown Court which was told his trial is due to start on August 10.

Three charges are of indecent assault and are dated between April 1999 and August 2000.

Another 15 are allegations of sexual assault of children under 13. These carry dates from September 2004 to August 2017.

Taylor also denied two counts of making indecent photographs of a child on August 8, 2016.

Dawn Pritchard, prosecuting, told the court: "This matter needs to be adjourned for trial. We are looking at about three weeks."

Judge Gregory Dickinson QC, the Recorder of Nottingham, said the August 10 date had been found with a trial lasting "three to four weeks."

Adrian Reynolds, defending, said they were awaiting a summary of the case.

The judge set a timetable for the prosecution to compile its case and hand it to lawyers representing Taylor, whose team will then draw up "a defence document."

The matter is due back before the crown court on March 27 as part of the preparations for the trial.

Bail was extended to Taylor. When he initially appeared before city magistrates, he gave his address as Exchange Road, West Bridgford.

Children named in the charges have their identities protected for life.
