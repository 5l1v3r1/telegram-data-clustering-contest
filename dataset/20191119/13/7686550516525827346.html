Man admits manslaughter over death of charity worker Mark Bloomfield in Swansea

Colin Payne denies murdering Mark Bloomfield in an incident near the Full Moon pub and will stand trial next month

19 Nov 2019, 13:26 by Nino Williams

A man has pleaded guilty to manslaughter following the death of a charity worker in Swansea.

Mark Bloomfield died a few days after the alleged assault near the Full Moon pub in High Street on July 18.

The 54-year-old had carried out charity work around the globe, including India and Africa, and had worked as a special assistant to Mother Teresa in Calcutta.

Colin Thomas Payne, from Swansea, appeared in Swansea Crown Court on Tuesday where he admitted the manslaughter of Mr Bloomfield as well as perverting the course of justice by removing CCTV footage from outside the pub.

The 61-year-old denies a charge of murder and a trial date of December 2 was set.

Judge Paul Thomas QC said: “For obvious reasons he can’t be sentenced until the trial has been concluded but in the meantime he will be remanded in custody."
