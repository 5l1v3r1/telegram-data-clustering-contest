Chelsea preparing new contracts for four of Frank Lampard's young stars

19 Nov 2019, 09:25

Chelsea are looking to give new deals to Tammy Abraham, Fikayo Tomori, Reece James and Tino Anjorin as Frank Lampard continues to build his team around the exciting young prospects at Stamford Bridge.

Abraham and Tomori have been regulars in the Blues first team in the Premier League and Champions League this season, while James has also been called into first team action.

17-year-old midfielder Anjorin is the least recognisable name of the four and has made just one appearance in the senior side, in the 7-1 EFL Cup win over Grimsby.

Lampard is a big fan of all four and wants to tie their futures down at Chelsea for the foreseeable future, reports Goal, as they continue to impress early in their careers.

Abraham has been in spectacular form so far this season, scoring 10 Premier League goals, second only to Jamie Vardy in the race for the Golden Boot.

Tomori has stepped up to the first team and made 14 appearances across all competitions, also making his England debut on Sunday against Kosovo.

Given he plays in the same position as club captain Cesar Azpilicueta, right-back James has been more limited in terms of first team options, but has still made nine appearances this campaign, scoring two goals.

Also set for a new contract offer is Willian who has been in tremendous form since Lampard took over as manager in the summer.

The 31-year-old Brazilian captained the side in their last outing against Crystal Palace and has been integral to the Blues’ fine run of results in recent weeks.

Willian is out of contract in the summer and has previously been linked with Barcelona, who could make a move to sign him in January, but he given his resurgence under Lampard, he is now likely to stay in west London.

He told DAZN: ‘The only real offer I had to leave Chelsea came from Barcelona last year, after the World Cup. Barca presented an offer to Chelsea but it was rejected.

‘That was the only time I ever had to discuss something like that with my family, but we really love it here [in London]. So there were several factors that helped me stay.’

Lampard has also made it clear he wants the play-maker to stay, saying earlier this month: ‘The word isn’t ‘no-brainer’ because I can’t get involved in the money, that’s up to the club to do.

‘But everyone sees how much I’m relying on him and what he’s producing. I love him at this football club, so there’s my answer.’
