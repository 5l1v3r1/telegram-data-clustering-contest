FIR registered in connection with protest march at JNU

Sources said that the FIR had been registered against unknown members who took part in the protest for allegedly violating prohibitory orders

19 Nov 2019, 09:10 by Staff Reporter

Police on Tuesday registered two FIRs, one in South district and another in South West district, in connection with the protest march held by students of the Jawaharlal Nehru University on Monday.

A senior police officer confirmed the development.

Sources said that the FIR had been registered against unknown members who took part in the protest for allegedly violating prohibitory orders, obstructing police officers from discharging their duties and causing hurt among other charges.

On Monday, students of the JNU and their supporters held a protest march from the University and they were stopped at Safdarjung Tomb. Students alleged they were lathicharged but police denied the claim.
