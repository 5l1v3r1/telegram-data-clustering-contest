Spoilers: EastEnders' Jake Wood says 35th anniversary will be 'jaw-dropping'

19 Nov 2019, 09:51

EastEnders legend Jake Wood has promised that the soap’s storylines will be filled to the brim with drama in celebration of its upcoming 35th anniversary in February 2020. The actor, who plays Max Branning, told Piers Morgan, Susanna Reid and Good Morning Britain audiences that the programme is on ‘real high’ at the moment and won’t be slowing down anytime soon.

Susanna probed: ‘So EastEnders, 35 years, a significant anniversary next year. Are there going to be some extraordinary, jaw-dropping, edge of your seat, duff duff moments?’

Jake confirmed: ‘Yes, undoubtedly there will be.’

He continued: ‘I think we’re on a real high at the moment, EastEnders. We’ve got a new boss Kate Oates and Jon Sen. She’s [Kate] got an amazing great track record of turning these shows around.

‘At the moment, we’ve got great actors, great characters, great storylines. I think when you have a combination of all those three things, we’ve got a great soap opera.’

This exciting news comes just days after Jake exclusively revealed to Metro.co.uk that EastEnders has got a phenomenal 12 months lined up for us.

This year’s really strengthened what we’ve got, and Christmas and the 35thanniversary next year — it’s going to be an incredible six months, or year, ahead,’ he enthused.

Speaking about the upcoming milestone, Jake told us: ‘I think what Kate has done has really upped the game and really brought us to the top — and as far as I can see it’s going to continue, the scripts we’re getting are just amazing, I know what the plans are for the 35th— they’re going to be some of the most exciting scenes we’ve ever done.

‘I’m really looking forward to it!’

Roll on February!
