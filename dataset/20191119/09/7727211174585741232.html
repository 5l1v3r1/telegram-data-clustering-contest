BBC Strictly Come Dancing's Michelle Visage tells ITV Lorraine Kelly she 'wasn't asked to do Strictly tour'

"I was gutted"

19 Nov 2019, 09:35 by Rebecca Koncienzcy

Michelle Visage has said she was "gutted" when she wasn't asked to take part in the Strictly Come Dancing tour.

The 51-year-old became the latest celebrity to leave the BBC show on Sunda y when the judges chose to save YouTube star Saffron Barker and her partner AJ Pritchard over Michelle and her professional partner Giovanni Pernice.

Since her departure there have been a number of stories about her in the press, including the decision for her not to be on the Strictly tour next year.

She told Lorraine Kelly: "There is a lot of controversy about this.

"I wanted to do it, I was not asked to do it, there was a problem with dates at first, but I am gutted. But that is just the way it goes."

There were also reports that her and Gio clashed, arguing backstage and that she had stormed off the BBC show on Saturday.

Happy to answer questions, the Drag Race UK judge said: "They [the press] said I stormed out of actual Blackbpool - I was at the party till 3am dancing with Kelvin and Oti and Gio.

"None of it's true. I am a party girl and I love people, I would stay to the end."

Speaking about rumours that her and Gio had come to blows during rehearsals, she said she had prayed to get him as her professional partner: "We have such and incredible relationship and this thing has been so much richer because of him.

"I rang him yesterday, and he is busy with the pro stuff but I said, 'any hours you have can we dance?'"

After she was voted off the reality show, following her final performance to Madonna's 'Vogue', she told Tess Daily: "This whole experience has been amazing.

"At 51 years old and never danced in my life, I never thought I'd be able to do what I have done and to have such an incredible partner.

"To be here for my husband and my kids and the entire community, that's why I did what I did.

"I am so honoured to be welcomed by my new friends here."

Pernice admitted that the couple had been through a range of emotions during their time together, but said he has respect for his partner despite their arguments.

He said: "We laugh, we cry, we fight and we argue and we've done everything but I've loved every single second. I think you are inspirational."
