CCTV released after pregnant mum and two daughters went missing

They were last seen in a hotel

19 Nov 2019, 09:03 by Neil Shaw

Police have released CCTV images of a heavily pregnant mum and her two young daughters who have been missing for more than two months.

Detectives are becoming “increasingly concerned” for Hajar Mohammed (Al-Shammani), 35, who vanished with daughters Reem, eight, and six-year-old Marjam.

They were last seen at the Travelodge hotel in Lower Street, Newcastle-under-Lyme, Staffordshire, at approximately 5.30pm on September 10.

Despite enquiries being made by Staffordshire Police’s Missing Person Enquiry Team, the family have not been found.

A police spokesperson said: “We are becoming increasingly concerned for a mother and her two children.

“Detectives from Force CID are now investigating and want help to locate the family to ensure they are safe and well.

“Hajar is described as being 5ft 3ins tall, of Middle Eastern appearance and was heavily pregnant when she was last seen.

“She was wearing a beige head scarf and a black floor-length dress.

“Marjam and Reem were last seen wearing matching clothing including a pink and light-grey rain coat, a grey t-shirt with unicorn details, light-blue jeans with a unicorn at the top of left leg and trainers that matched their coats.”
