PSG owners 'close in on Leeds United takeover' as boardroom talks continue

PSG owners Qatari Sports Investment are interested in taking control of Championship side Leeds and recent talks are thought to have becoming increasingly positive

19 Nov 2019, 09:27 by James Whaling, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/james-whaling/

The owners of Paris Saint-Germain are reportedly closing in on a takeover of Leeds United.

Qatari Sports Investment (QSI) are interested in investing in the Championship side who are currently bidding for a first return to the Premier League since being relegated in 2004.

Discussions between the would-be investors and Leeds have been ongoing since before the summer and - while nothing is finalised - recent talks have been positive, report ESPN FC.

Leeds owner Andrea Radrizzani also has interest from two other parties but his close relationship with PSG chief Nasser al-Khelaifi means QSI are in pole position to strike a deal.

An agreement is not thought to be imminent, with uncertainty over Brexit , the difficult situation at Al-Khelaifi-owned beIN Sport and the diplomatic landscape in Qatar all thought to be contributing factors to the delay.

QSI have reportedly been interested in getting involved in an English club for some time, considering a bid to take over Queens Park Rangers before deciding Leeds would be a better opportunity.

The worldwide appeal of the Premier League - where Leeds see themselves returning - and the astronomical TV revenue that comes with it make the Yorkshire club an attractive proposition.

Leeds and Qatar have already collaborated in a number of ways including a partnership with Doha's ultra-modern football academy Aspire where Whites youth teams travel for training and tournaments.

Leeds currently sit third in the Championship under boss Marcelo Bielsa, only out of the top two on goal difference and just two points adrift of table-topping West Brom.

They missed out on promotion to the top flight last term as faltering late-season form saw them slip into the playoffs where they were beaten by Derby over two legs in the semi-finals.
