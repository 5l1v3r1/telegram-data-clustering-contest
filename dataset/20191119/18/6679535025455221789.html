NMPT gives new pair of spectacles to commercial drivers transiting port

19 Nov 2019, 18:46 by Special Correspondent

The New Mangalore Port Trust, in association with Shell India and VisionSpring, launched on Tuesday the programme of testing eyes and giving spectacles to commercial drivers and allied transportation workers transiting through the port.

Shell India, as part of its corporate social responsibility, has joined VisionSpring’s “DriveSafeIndia” initiative to help nearly 3.65 lakh commercial vehicle drivers, mechanics and other allied transport workers in Gujarat, Maharashtra, Tamil Nadu, and Karnataka, by the end of 2020 . Apart from free vision screening, there workers will get low-cost eyeglasses, and are also referred to hospitals for cataracts and other major corrections.

In a function held at the NMPT Training Centre, Ekta K., Country Lead-CSR, Shell India, said of the 5 lakh fatalities in the road accidents 26 % (1.5 lakh) of fatalities were contributed by commercial drivers. Poor eyesight has lot of bearing on the accident.

The NMPT was among the organisations that Shell India is associated in coastal Karnataka. Eye testing and issuance of spectacles in NMPT will go on till end of December.

NMPT Chairman A.V. Ramana said the NMPT was sensitising their employees and those in transport sector about health care that included maintaining good sight.

The NMPT was offering free medical check-up and medicines to truck drivers and cleaners. The new vision programme will help commercial drivers to a great extent and yield good results, he said.
