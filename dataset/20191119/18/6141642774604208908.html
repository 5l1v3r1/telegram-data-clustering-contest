New arrival at Jersey Zoo tops off anniversary year

DURRELL has announced the birth of a baby gorilla.

19 Nov 2019, 18:10

The new arrival, whose gender is not yet known, was born in the early hours of Friday to mother Bahasha and father Badongo.

Staff said on Tuesday that mother and baby were both doing well.

In a statement posted on social media alongside photos and a video of Bahasha and the new arrival, the zoo said: 'We certainly couldn't have topped off our 60th anniversary and a wonderful year celebrating gorillas in a more perfect way!'

The zoo's gorillas have been in the limelight this year after a series of statues were placed across the Island during the summer.

The 44 statues that made up the Go Wild Gorillas were sited at a range of locations across the Island before being auctioned earlier this month.

The auction raised a total of £1,146,500 towards a new enclosure for the gorillas at the zoo in Trinity. One statue called The Space Between Us – designed by artist Andy Coutanche – sold for £72,000.
