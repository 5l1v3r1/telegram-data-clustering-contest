Mike Tomlin Has Strong Comment On Incident With Browns

19 Nov 2019, 18:48 by Alek Arend

It’s not too often a Thursday Night Football is still getting discussed following a full weekend of football. The  Browns-Steelers fiasco isn’t going away any time soon, especially as Mike Tomlin’s team is set to play Cleveland again in just a few weeks.

Myles Garrett’s actions will never be forgotten – some are calling his helmet swing on Mason Rudolph one of the dirtiest things in the history of football. But several Steelers players took part in the brawl as well. There’s blame to be placed on both Rudolph and Garrett.

It’s pretty clear that Rudolph played a role in the incident, but Pittsburgh head coach Mike Tomlin doesn’t seem to agree. In fact, he feels his team doesn’t have much to learn from the whole ordeal.

“I don’t know that we did anything to make it happen in the first place,” Tomlin told the media on Tuesday. “That’s why I said we didn’t have anything to learn from it.”
