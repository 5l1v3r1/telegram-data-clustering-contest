BTS's Jungkook teases new music as group reveal how they spent their time off

19 Nov 2019, 18:41

BTS recently took an extended holiday before closing off their Speak Yourself tour in Seoul, South Korea.

But it turns out these boys just couldn’t rest, as both Suga and Jungkook spent their days off working on new material.

That’s right, despite their extremely busy year, taking a break is just not an option for the seven-piece, who recently opened up to Paper Magazine about their time away from the stage.

When chatting to the publication, the boy band were asked what they’d been getting up to.

‘I slept, worked out and went to art museums quite a lot. I went to Jeju Island, Venice, Vienna and Copenhagen,’ RM said, while V added: ‘I took a good rest. It was an eat-play-sleep routine.’

But while V was sleeping, Jimin was doing a lot of time thinking – and we don’t blame him!

‘I just kept on the move and went to a bunch of places,’ he said: ‘It was an opportunity to think about [the group] in the past and in the future.’

Jin also seemed to have a pretty relaxing time, as he added: ‘I played games at home. I also went fishing with Suga.’

And even though he enjoyed fishing with his bestie, it seems Suga also managed to put time aside to work on some new tunes.

The 26-year-old said: ‘I focused on resting and worked on some songs. It was a time [for] looking back at myself.’

And he wasn’t on his own when it came to getting on with other projects, as J-Hope went to film the music video for Chicken Noodle Soup.

‘I felt and learned a lot of things,’ he said: ‘I can’t call it a rest time, but it was a meaningful time. After that, I came back home, I had good food and rested well. I also played with my puppy.’

Jungkook kept his answer simple, as he confessed: ‘I worked on music.’

Earlier this year, the 22-year-old was crowned the longest-charting male idol on Billboard World’s Digital Song Sales.

The star’s smash hit Euphoria re-entered the charts at number 20, setting a new record for BTS’s solo songs with 22 charting weeks.
