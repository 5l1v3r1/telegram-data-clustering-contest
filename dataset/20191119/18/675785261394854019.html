UK weather forecast: Cold warning as temperatures to plunge below freezing again

Public Health England have urged people to look out for the elderly as freezing temperatures are expected again tonight, possibly as low as -3C in parts of Scotland

19 Nov 2019, 18:03 by Danya Bazaraa, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/danya-bazaraa/

Health chiefs are urging people to look after those at risk amid the freezing cold weather with temperatures set to plunge again.

The Met Office said the low temperatures will continue until Wednesday, after -9.9C was recorded in Braemar, north-east Scotland, last night.

Below freezing temperatures are expected again tonight, as cold as -3C in parts of Scotland.

With warnings that more cold weather is on the way - particularly for the north east, north west and Yorkshire and Humber - Public Health England has a cold weather alert in place until tomorrow.

This comes as the government issues 24 flood warnings and 63 flood alerts around the country.

 

Dr Emer OConnell, Consultant in Public Health at Public Health England, said: "Older people and those with heart and lung problems are at risk of getting sick in cold weather.

"Keep an eye out for those who may need help staying warm, ensure they wear lots of thin layers and have everything they need.

"Below 18 degrees, changes to the body mean that the risk of strokes, heart attacks and chest infections increase so heating homes to this temperature is particularly important to stay well."

 

Areas which have been issued with a flood warning include Barrow upon Humber, the River Severn at South Worcester and the River Severn at Ashleworth.

Flood alerts have been given for a number of areas including the Welney Causeway in Norfolk, the River Trent in Derbyshire and the River Thames for the Abingdon area.

Tonight there will be cloud and patchy rain for western parts of England, Wales and Scotland.

Central and eastern areas will be mostly dry with clear spells. Clear skies will develop in Northern Ireland.

Tomorrow there will be cloud and outbreaks of light rain at times in Northern Ireland, Wales and western parts of England and Scotland. It will be mostly dry with sunny spells for much of England, Scotland and eastern parts of Wales, especially in the afternoon.

On Thursday bands of cloud and showers will spread northwards across the country. There will be bright spells too, mainly in the south during the afternoon.

Friday will be cloudy with rain at times in the south and west. Northern and eastern areas should be dry with sunny spells during a breezy day.

Showers are believed to sweep in from the west at the end of the wintry week.

The coldest spots will again be in Scotland, most notably on Thursday.

Met Office 5-day forecast

This Evening and Tonight:

Windy in the west with rain edging slowly eastwards. Clearer across Northern Ireland for a time with some frost and fog. Central and eastern areas mostly dry with some frost in places, though less widespread than last night.

Wednesday:

Western areas often cloudy with occasional rain. Mostly dry and bright elsewhere. Feeling cold in brisk southeasterly winds, with coastal gales in the west.

Outlook for Thursday to Saturday:

Northern and eastern areas should be dry on Thursday. Bands of rain, already in the southwest, will move slowly and erratically northeast to much of the UK through the period.
