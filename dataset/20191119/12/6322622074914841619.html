Batu Kawan 4Q net profit up 25%, ends FY19 on a flat note

19 Nov 2019, 12:50 by Justin Lim

KUALA LUMPUR (Nov 19): Batu Kawan Bhd, which counts Kuala Lumpur Kepong Bhd as a main subsidiary, saw its net profit for the fourth quarter ended Sept 30, 2019 (4QFY19) rise 25% to RM96.70 million from RM77.25 million a year ago, lifted by its manufacturing segment, higher other operating income, better share of results from associates, and lower income tax expenses.

Consequently, earnings per share grew to 24.38 sen from 19.25 sen last year, Batu Kawan's stock exchange filing showed today, despite revenue falling 9% to RM3.92 billion from RM4.32 billion.

Its manufacturing segment reported a 64% jump in profit to RM108.37 million from RM65.94 million, despite a 16% drop in revenue to RM2.15 billion from RM2.55 billion. Its plantation segment, however, saw profit sink 24% to RM133.41 million from RM176.37 million, though revenue grew 2% to RM1.69 billion from RM1.65 billion, no thanks to lower crude palm oil and palm kernel prices.

The group, which had warned in August that it could see reduced profit in FY19, following a dismal 3QFY19, closed the year with earnings little changed from a year ago. Net profit came in at RM363.50 million versus RM365.68 million previously, though revenue dropped 15% to RM16.05 billion from RM18.95 billion.

On prospect, the group said today that it expects a higher profit for the financial year ending Sept 30, 2020 (FY20), as its plantation segment is expected to improve with the recovery of CPO and PK prices.

Meanwhile, the performance of the group’s oleochemical division should be sustained with some additional capacities coming onstream. "As for the group’s industrial chemical division, profits from both chlor-alkali and sulphuric acid businesses are projected to be satisfactory," it added.

Batu Kawan shares slipped two sen to RM15.40 today, bringing a market capitalisation of RM6.04 billion. The counter saw some 15,200 shares transacted. Year-to-date, its share price has retreated some 8% from RM16.68.
