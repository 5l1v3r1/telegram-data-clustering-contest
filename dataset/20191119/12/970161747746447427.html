Mel B prefers lesbian porn because it’s ‘kinder and sensual'

19 Nov 2019, 12:49

Mel B has shared her porn preferences, citing lesbian scenes as being ‘kinder’ in the grand scheme of things.

Hey, whatever floats your boat, Mel…so to speak.

The Spice Girls star has revealed she’s had relationships with both men and women, but seems when it comes to her theatrical proclivities it’s all about the ladies.

In the world of adult entertainment, you see, she prefers to watch something ‘more sensual’, and when there is momentous chatter about sexism in heterosexual porn she sort of has a point.

Speaking on podcast The Truth Flirts – which she hosts in partnership with dating app Badoo – Scary Spice said: ‘Lesbian porn is more sensual, I think anyway. It’s nicer and kinder.’

She’s not alone – a few years ago we asked a whole bunch of you why you prefer lesbian porn after a PornHub and Buzzfeed study found it the most popular genre for women.

Mel – who has three daughters from previous relationships – also discussed the differences between men and women when it comes to dating, and admitted her time with former flame Christine Crokos was ‘really beautiful’ with hardly any arguments.

She added: ‘I do think that dating women is very different to dating men…

‘I had a five-year relationship with a woman and it was an intense five years but it was actually a really beautiful five years.

‘We barely kind of argued, because we both thought the same, wanted the same out of life. It was really interesting when I actually compare it to my other relationships.’

The star may be more than ok with voicing her views on the podcast, but outspoken Mel previously admitted she found it a little ‘cringey’ talking to her daughter Phoenix, 18, about the birds and the bees.

‘When Phoenix was about 16, I was like, we need to sit down and have a word about safe sex and contraception, and she was like “what?”’ Mel said.

‘And I think all her other friends had never even had that conversation with their mothers. I’m so glad, as cringey as it was, I was very to the point, I told her about boys’ intentions, or the main intentions of boys at that age.

‘We spoke about contraception thoroughly, about condoms, what happens when you don’t use a condom, which I think terrified her to her core.’
