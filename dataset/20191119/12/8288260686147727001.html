Tesco's discount supermarket Jack's launches Christmas range - and the items are ludicrously cheap

Jack's offers customers top quality products at great value prices, across its nine UK stores

19 Nov 2019, 12:42 by James Rodger, https://www.facebook.com/jamesrodgerjournalist

Jack's, part of the Tesco family, has unveiled its Christmas food range for 2019.

With tasty delights such as profiteroles filled with fresh cream and topped with Belgian chocolate (£4 for a pack of 32), baked camembert with a caramelised onion and balsamic herb chutney (£2, 290g) , and a Christmas pudding filled with juicy sultanas and a citrus peel (£1.50, 400g), there is something for everyone to feast on this festive season.

Other delicious Christmas treats include Jack's award winning mince pies (85p for a pack of six), made in Britain from crumbly shortcrust pastry and voted a winner on quality, taste and price, and pigs in blankets - sausages made from British pork hand trimmed by skilled butchers and wrapped in British smoked streaky bacon.

Jack's offers customers top quality products at great value prices, across its nine UK stores.

Other key Christmas products include:

- Jack's sausage roll garland: seasoned pork sausage meat baked in a golden puff pastry (£4, 700g)

- Jack's Chocolate Yule Log - deliciously rich and chocolatey, made with fresh British cream and finished with chocolate flakes (£4)

- Jack's sage and onion stuffing balls - blended with aromatic sage, parsley and onion, delicious hot or cold. (£2, 350g)

 

Alex Edwards, Head of Fresh & Frozen Foods at Jack’s said: “The Jack’s team have spent months developing our Christmas range and are excited to present customers with the very best quality products at some of the best prices in the market.

"Christmas allows us to celebrate the Best of British, aligned with our customer commitment to offer ‘8 out of 10 Jack’s products are British’. We’re very proud of our festive food range and the value it offers. We can’t wait for customers to enjoy it with their families.”
