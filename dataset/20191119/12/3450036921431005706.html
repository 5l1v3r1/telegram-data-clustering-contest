Army veteran receives motorized chair that has him moving through life at a new speed

19 Nov 2019, 12:44 by Romelo Styles

ALBIA,Iowa â€” Army veteran Scott Miller of Albia, Iowa, has a new way to get around after receiving a motorized chair on Tuesday.

"It's awesome pretty overwhelming really," Miller said. " It's quite the chair. It will be a lot for somebody to afford on their own. ITC doing this really helps a guy out to get out in the woods, and get back out and be active.

Electric company, ITC Midwest, donated this chair to Miller. The Iowa chapter of the Paralyzed Veterans of America who chose Miller to receive one of the motorized chairs.

"This is the sixth chair that they have purchased for the Iowa chapter of Paralyzed Veterans of America. Each chair is upwards of about $13,000 and so they have donated a lot of money and energy and time into providing these chairs for us," says Executive Director of the Iowa chapter of Paralyzed Veterans of America Kim Gould.

The president of ITC Midwest is happy the company could be a part of something so meaningful.

"It does make us feel good as employees we can do a small part to help these veterans who have served our country and sacrificed their time," said Dusky Terry, ITC Midwest president. "It's a way for us to show our appreciation for that.

Miller says he enjoys spending time in the woods, and that's one of the first places he will be heading with his new chair.
