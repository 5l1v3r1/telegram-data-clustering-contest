Jeane Freeman confirms Glasgow super hospital inquiry will investigate dirty water linked to child's death

The Health Secretary is set to make a statement on the Queen Elizabeth University Hospital scandal tomorrow.

19 Nov 2019, 12:58 by Paul Hutcheon, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/paul-hutcheon/

Health Secretary Jeane Freeman has said a public inquiry into Glasgow ’s super hospital will investigate water contamination problems linked to the death of Milly Main.

Freeman, who will also make a statement to Holyrood on the scandal, told MSPs she is “clear” the probe will examine the deadly infections at the Queen Elizabeth University Hospital (QEUH) campus.

It came as figures showed the national performance against the accident and emergency waiting time target had fallen to its lowest level since March last year.

The Daily Record revealed claims by a whistleblower last week that 26 child cancer patients contracted infections at the hospital in 2017.

 

The insider alleged that one child, who we later revealed to be Milly, had died as a result of the bacteria.

Milly's mum Kimberly Darroch told this newspaper she was “100%” sure water contamination at the hospital had caused Milly’s death.

She called on NHS Greater Glasgow and Clyde to “stop lying” and urged Freeman to tell the truth about the case.

Kimberly also revealed Milly, who had leukaemia, was in remission at the time she got the infection.

An independent review is underway and a public inquiry will look at a raft of issues at the QEUH and the new Sick Kids hospital in Edinburgh, which remains unopened.

Asked at the Health and Sport committee whether the review and the inquiry would examine water contamination, Freeman said:

“It is my understanding the current independent review has taken evidence on that, and therefore that is part of what they are considering.”

“I would expect that the public inquiry will also take that into account.”

She added: “It’s clear that will be part of both pieces of work.”

Freeman is also facing calls by Labour MSP Anas Sarwar, who was contacted by the whistleblower over the the QEUH infection, to put NHSGGC into special measures.

The Health Secretary has pointedly failed to rule out the move, which would mean the Government effectively taking on a more hands-on approach with the organsation.

 

Meanwhile, the Scottish Tories have been campaigning outside the new Sick Kids hospital, which Freeman stopped from opening in the summer over safety concerns.

Conservative MSP Miles Briggs said: “Five years ago, Nicola Sturgeon was elected First Minister . Five years on, the Scottish NHS is lurching from crisis to crisis.”

“Nothing symbolizes her government’s failures better than the new Sick Kids Hospital in Edinburgh: over-budget and still not delivered.”
