Couple 'disappointed' to find Aldi label on Waitrose vegetables

The incident brought up questions over whether customers are paying for food quality or just the brand's label

19 Nov 2019, 12:07 by Sarah McGee

When a couple's Ocado delivery arrived, they were surprised to see their Waitrose 'Mediterranean Roasting Vegetables' had an Aldi label on it.

The front of the packaging appears completely normal but the underside was sporting the Aldi logo, saying the vegetables had been 'specially prepared' for the budget supermarket.

Speaking to Mail Online , the husband who wishes to remain anonymous, said: "I was really disappointed. I use Waitrose and Ocado for good reasons - reputation and home delivery.

"And I pay for the privilege.

"No disrespect to Aldi or their customers but if I wanted their products I'd go to one of their stores."

Aldi do sell a similar product to Waitrose, called Ready to Roast Winter Roast/Mediterranean Roast; Aldi's cost £1.29 while the product from Waitrose retails at £2. Both products are the same weight.
