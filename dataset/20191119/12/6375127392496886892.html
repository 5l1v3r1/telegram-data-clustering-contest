Libel suit vs Ressa, reporter thrown out

19 Nov 2019, 12:11 by Hector Lawas

THE Quezon City prosecutor’s office yesterday junked the libel complaint filed against Rappler’s Maria Ressa and reporter Rambo Talabong by Agrarian Reform Secretary John Castriciones for lack of probable cause.

“The statements made by respondents in their online website are not in (themselves) defamatory,” Assistant City Prosecutor Arnel Pabellar said in a resolution.

Citing Yabut and Tamargo vs Office of the Ombudsman, the resolution said: “A public official, more especially an elected one, should not be onion-skinned…Always, he is expected to act and serve with the highest degree of responsibility, integrity, loyalty, and efficiency, and shall remain accountable for his counduct to the people.”

In 2017, Castriciones filed the libel complaint as undersecretary of the Interior and Local Government Department.

Rappler reported that Castriciones along with two other DILG undersecretaries were divested of their powers by then DILG secretary Ismael Sueno in 2017.

Also, concerned DILG officials and employees sent a letter to President Duterte asking him to fire Castriciones and the other undersecretaries.

However, the President did not fire them and just transferred them to other offices.

The public Quezon City prosecutor ruled there was no libel because Rappler “merely summarized the contents of the letter to the President’ without any remarks or comment.”
