19 Nov 2019, 12:03 by Fraser N Wilson, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/fraser-n-wilson/

Two men have appeared in private in connection with the alleged assault of a veteran on Remembrance Sunday.

We told you last week how Christopher Smith, pictured, had allegedly been attacked on Stuart Street in the Village.

Police believe the incident happened around 11.10pm on Sunday, November 10.

Now a 32 and 38-year-old have appeared at Hamilton Sheriff Court in relation to the matter.

Brian Kelly and Derek Gallagher, both East Kilbride, made no plea or declaration at court on Monday.

Both are charged with assault to severe injury, but only Mr Kelly was released on bail.

Mr Gallagher was remanded in custody following his court appearance.

It is expected the pair will return to court in the next eight days.

Chief Inspector Lex Baillie told the News the pair had been arrested following a public appeal for information and thanked those who came forward to help police with their investigation.

Mr Smith, who served with the Royal Marines during the height of the troubles in Northern Ireland, was said to have suffered a bleed on the brain, cracked ribs and bruised kidneys following the alleged attack.

Following the publicity over the alleged incident, he is now being helped by the South Lanarkshire Council’s  veterans’ support liaison.
