Dockers secure young gun Luke Ryan with new long-term deal

19 Nov 2019, 01:51 by Ben Waterworth

Fremantle has locked away arguably its biggest name that was out of contract at the end of next year, with Luke Ryan recommitting to the club.

The Dockers confirmed on Tuesday that Ryan had signed a three-year contract extension on top of the season he already had left to run, therefore tying him to the club until at least the end of 2023.

Ryan, 23, has become one of the key cogs in Fremantle’s backline since it drafted him with Pick 66 in 2016 as a mature-age prospect.

Rated elite for intercept possessions and intercept marks according to Champion Data, Ryan said he was keen to repay the faith Fremantle had shown in him.

“I’ve got my family back home but when I spoke to them, we came to the conclusion that Fremantle gave me my chance – if it wasn’t for Fremantle I probably wouldn’t have been playing AFL.

“(Before) I was called out with pick 66, I felt it was my last chance and, luckily enough, they called me. I owe it to Fremantle and what they’ve done for me, so I wanted to sign on.”

Ryan, who was ranked second in the AFL for average intercepts in 2019, has played 49 games in three seasons.

Fremantle list manager Mark Micallef said Ryan had established himself the club’s defensive set-up and had high hopes for him.

“With Luke’s intercept ability and ability to use the ball by foot, he adds to the defensive pillars we have in our defence,” Micallef said.

“We see Luke as being a really strong and hopefully elite player of the competition. He has the football characteristics that are vital for our success and we hope he can lock in that role as a strong leader of our defence.”
