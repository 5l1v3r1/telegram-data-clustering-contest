ScotRail tell customer to ‘arrive earlier’ over ticket queue issue as fare dodger campaign sparks furious backlash

19 Nov 2019, 08:17 by Jonathan Whitelaw

SCOTRAIL told a customer to "arrive earlier" after a complaint about ticket queues as a campaign against fare-dodging sparked fury.

Rail bosses took to social media last night to announce they were cracking down on those who don't pay for journeys.

They shared a Tweet that featured a ticket wearing a robber's mask - urging punters to "buy before you board".

But some responses from ScotRail were panned by fuming punters.

The firm said: "Does your station have a ticket machine or a ticket office? Buy Before You Board.

"Travelling without buying a ticket is fare evasion.

"We’re clamping down".

A number of customers complained about facilities at stations, including automated ticket machines.
