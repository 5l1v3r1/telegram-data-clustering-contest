Marvel's Runaways to end after season 3 - is Disney+ to blame?

19 Nov 2019, 08:22

It’s been announced that Marvel’s Runaways will be ending after its third season.

The TV series was based on the comic book of the same name, and followed a group of six tearaway teenagers from different walks of life who have to learn to work together to fight their parents, who they learn all moonlight as supervillains.

According to Variety, bosses behind the show agreed that the story for the upcoming third run brought the series to its natural conclusion.

The show was created and written by the creative team of Josh Schwartz and Stephanie Savage, who were also the minds behind iconic teen drama Gossip Girl.

It is worth noting, however, that Runaways was one of the last live-action series in production under the Marvel Television brand. All six of Netflix’s MCU tie-ins – including Daredevil and Jessica Jones – have been culled to make way for original series for Disney+.

There are a number of series in the works to air on the streaming service, including WandaVision and a Hawkeye spin-off, and with the news of Runaway’s imminent demise, Agents of S.H.I.E.L.D will be the last show standing when it airs its seventh (and also final) season next year.

Kevin Feige, the head honcho and guiding light of the MCU, was recently named as Marvel’s new Chief Creative Officer, and with that news came the warning that all Marvel TV series had either been axed or were in danger of being axed to make way for Disney+’s limited series content.

Unfortunately, this news does seem to have come true.

Runaway’s third and final season will start airing on Hulu in the States on 13 December.

In other Marvel related news, deleted scenes from Avengers: Endgame finally gave fans a glimpse at 13 Reasons Why’s Katherine Langford as Tony Stark’s daughter.

It was also announced that Star Wars icon Mark Hamill may not be joining James Gunn for the next Guardians of the Galaxy after all.

Oh, and just in case you forgot, everyone’s favourite Star-Lord also originally auditioned to be Captain America.
