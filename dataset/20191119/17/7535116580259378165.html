Malaysia’s Flying Car Will Have Its Very First Test Flight This Thursday!

That’s right folks, if you’re looking for excitement, you’re about to get it. Keep yourself fastened to your seat as the nation takes a huge step into the new age.
As reported by Careta, the Minister of Entrepreneur Development, Datuk Seri Mohd Redzuan Yusof told the Dewan Rakyat today (Nov 19th) that the very first Malaysian flying car test drive will happen on Thursday (Nov 21st) in Subang.

The minister emphasised that he himself would participate in the test drive of the vehicle. Reportedly, the car can only fit two passengers as of now.

Datuk Seri Mohd Redzuan Yusof also mentioned that he would invite a representative from the opposition to witness the flying car test drive together.

“The Honorable Paya Besar (Mohd Shahar Abdullah-BN) will be invited and if it is to his liking, we can sit together in the car and snap a few pictures together while flying it.” he continued.

The minister said this during the wrapping-up session of the committee-level Supply Bill 2020 on behalf of the ministry.

Mohd Redzuan has explained before this that the flying car prototype is expected to launch by the end of this year. The production cost will be fully covered by the private sector and it will not need any kind of funds from the government.
