Corvette, powered by GM Tonawanda, named MotorTrend Car of the Year

19 Nov 2019, 17:26

The new Chevrolet Corvette Stingray, powered by engines from General Motors' Town of Tonawanda plant, was named MotorTrend's 2020 Car of the Year.

GM Tonawanda is making small volumes of the 6.2-liter, small block V-8 engines for test vehicles of the new Corvette. Full-scale engine production is expected to begin in early 2020, when a GM assembly plant in Bowling Green, Ky., starts producing the vehicles.

The Corvette won MotorTrend Car of the Year in 1984 and 1998. The redesigned Corvette was unveiled with fanfare earlier this year, including an event at the Tonawanda plant featuring GM president Mark Reuss.
