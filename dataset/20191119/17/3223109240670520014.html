Geoff Keighley’s ‘Death Stranding’ Leads Geoff Keighley’s Game Awards Nominations

19 Nov 2019, 17:55 by Jordan Minor

If you want to see a really cool video game awards show with new announcements and big guests like Reggie Fils-Aime, check out the New York Video Game Awards featuring me and other members of the New York Video Game Critics Circle next January 21.

In the meantime though, you can watch an arguably bigger show with some arguably bigger announcements at this year’s Game Awards. Geoff Keighley just revealed the nominations for “The Oscars with more commercials” and we already can’t wait to yell at the screen when the wrong games win. The game of the year nominees are Control, Death Stranding, Resident Evil 2, Super Smash Bros. Ultimate, Sekiro: Shadows Die Twice, and The Outer Worlds.

Although Keighley isn’t a voting member, it is weird to see Death Stranding, a game he cameos in on behest of his master Kojima, leading in nominations for his awards show. It’s also weird how the arbitrary November cut off means that Super Smash Bros. Ultimate, a game from December 2018, is up for Game of the Year 2019. Not that the nomination isn’t deserved, but that also means games like Star Wars Jedi: Fallen Order and Pokemon Sword and Shield won’t be considered until 2020. And in a world where Apple Arcade is giving us good premium mobile games again, Call of Duty Mobile getting nominated is a farce. At least Tetris 99 got in there for best multiplayer.
