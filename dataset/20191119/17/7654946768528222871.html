Mark Walton cut by Dolphins after allegedly punching pregnant girlfriend

19 Nov 2019, 17:26 by Justin Terranova

The Dolphins cut running back Mark Walton on Tuesday morning after he was accused of attacking a woman who was five weeks pregnant with his child.

According to a police report obtained by ESPN, it is alleged that Walton pushed the woman into a wall and punched her several times in her face and head. Walton found out he was the father on Sunday, according to Miami television station Local 10.

“We were made aware of a police matter earlier this morning regarding Mark Walton,” Dolphins general manager Chris Grier said in a statement. “We hold our players to a high standard and take these matters very seriously. We will have no further comment at this time.”

Walton, 22, was released by the Bengals last season following three arrests and was in the midst of serving a four-game suspension for Miami as a result of his past problems.

Walton’s three prior arrests came in a three-month span last year, and included weapons, drugs and battery charges. 

Walton, a Miami native, rushed for 201 yards on 53 carries in his seven games with the team.
