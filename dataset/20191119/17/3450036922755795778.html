Holiday season can take toll on immune system, tips to maintain health

19 Nov 2019, 17:24 by Ashley Hoak

KIRKSVILLE, Mo. â€” The holidays are fast approaching, and one northeast Missouri physician is offering tips to stay healthy through all the hustle and bustle.

Dr. Justin Puckett, of Complete Family Medicine, says the holiday season can take a toll on our immune system.

He adds that while it's important all the time, covering sneezes and coughs is especially important this time of year, due to being around an increased number of people.

He says that those everyday good habits can sometimes be forgotten, resulting in illnesses being spread at a faster rate.

Puckett explains that if you are ill, you should stay home instead of sharing germs with others, no matter how fun the holiday event may be.

"If somebody has a fever or has been actively sick, then you just need to stay home no matter how much you want to be at that gathering -- it's just not the right thing to do to carry those germs."

If out shopping prior to the holidays, Puckett also stresses the importance of sanitizing shopping carts and baskets before touching them.

He adds that you should also wash your hands frequently.
