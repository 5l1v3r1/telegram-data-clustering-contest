Volunteers wanted

19 Nov 2019, 04:15

Want to volunteer? Explore these opportunities and find more at Volunteer Victoria: 250-386-2269 or volunteervictoria.bc.ca

CFAX Santas Anonymous: The Christmas Giving Network needs volunteer list makers to take information shared by families and input it into Santa’s computer system. Volunteers needed until Dec. 22. Contact Evans@sashcf.com 250-516-8842.

The Victoria Stroke Recovery Association: Provides weekly programming and activities that are designed for stroke survivors and their caregivers. We are looking for a treasurer and secretary to assist us in monthly reports. Additionally, we would welcome any interested volunteers for our program. Contact Karol Sanderson at 250-383-4865.

Boys and Girls Club is looking for a passionate, dedicated, and committed board director with a CPA designation who would oversee the financial planning, management, and reporting for the agency. Visit bgcvic.org/career-opportunities/volunteer/ for more information.

Habitat for Humanity Victoria is seeking volunteer Gingerbread Ambassadors for the annual Gingerbread Showcase — Parkside Hotel — daily 9 a.m. to 9 p.m. through Sunday, Jan. 5. Three-hour shifts. Volunteers greet and support the Gingerbread Showcase’s many visitors. To sign up, visit habitatvictoria.com, call Jenessa at 250-480-7688 ext. 106, or email volunteer@habitatvictoria.com

Sendial Volunteer Shopping Program (Thrifty Foods): This program does the shopping for customers who cannot easily shop independently. Seeking kind and caring volunteers — Colwood and Saanichton locations. Volunteers are needed to take and shop grocery orders. Good hearing and attention to detail is required. Grocery product knowledge is an asset. For info: 250-544-1234 or email sendial@thriftyfoods.com

Lifetime Networks is seeking a volunteer to build a friendship with a man with autism in his 40s. He is a coffee connoisseur and a foodie and he is looking for a friend who shares his love of computer programming. He is also interested in going for walks in nature/at the beach, yoga, tai chi, photography, art and pottery. For information, please contact Emily at 250-477-4112 or volunteer@Lnv.ca

Pacific Centre Family Services Association: Volunteer drivers. Help seniors living in your community of View Royal by providing reliable transportation to their medical appointments, or assisting them with grocery shopping. A six-month commitment providing two to three transports per month makes a difference in a senior’s well being. Contact: Dori Bodman dbodman@pcfsa.org or 778-677-3540.

Explore your volunteer options: Need help finding that perfect volunteer position? Meet with one of Volunteer Victoria’s advisers. Call 250-386-2269 to make an appointment.

Our Place Society: Volunteer nurse — medical outreach services program. Become part of a team of compassionate people assisting Victoria’s most vulnerable citizens. For more information, visit our website, ourplacesociety.com, or call our volunteer program manager, Patricia O’Byrne at 250-388-7112, Ext. 263.

Recreation Integration Victoria: Recreation volunteer — leisure assistance program. This service involves matching a person with a disability on a one-to-one basis with a volunteer who has similar recreation and leisure interests. No experience is required. For more information contact: volunteers@rivonline.org or 250-477-6314, Ext. 205.

Anawim House: Physiotherapist. The physiotherapist supports the free physiotherapy services to guests, including those from the recovery population and homeless population, and to residents who live on site. Anawin House is geared toward community and recovery and strives to support those in the community who are in need. For more information contact: Stacey, stacey@anawimhouse.com
250-382-0283.

Victoria Chinatown Care Society: Activity assistant. As an activity assistant, you will work with senior residents and adult day program members who have cognitive and physical challenges. You will also collaborate with various health care team members to plan, implement, and participate in activities for seniors. Contact: Annie Wu 250-381-4322 Ext. 203.

Action Committee of People with Disabilities: Volunteer reception and office staff program. ACPD relies on volunteers to keep our busy office open. Administration skills are considered a benefit but are definitely not a requirement. Contact: Shannon 250-383-4105.
