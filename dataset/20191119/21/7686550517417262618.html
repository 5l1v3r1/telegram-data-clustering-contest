Where is Euro 2020 being held?

This time round the competition will be very different

19 Nov 2019, 21:49 by Elliott Jackson

Euro 2020 is one step closer with the group stage of qualification coming to an end on Tuesday night.

Although there is the small matter of the Nations League play-offs to sort in March next year, fans will be busy planning their journeys to support their national sides.

This time round the tournament has a very different format, so supporters should be aware of big changes from the last competition held in France in 2016.

So, how will it all work? Here are all the key details you need to know.

Who is hosting EURO2020?

For the first time in the competition's history, the Euros will be held across 12 different cities, in 12 different countries across Europe.

Of the 12 venues, all will stage at least three group stage games and a knock-out round tie each. The breakdown of each venue and games can be seen in the table below.

Group stage & Round of 16 - Denmark (Copenhagen) - Parken Stadium

Group stage & Round of 16 - Hungary (Budapest) - Ferenc Puskas Stadium

Group stage & Round of 16 - Ireland (Dublin) - Aviva Stadium

Group stage & Round of 16 Netherlands (Amsterdam) - Johann Cruijff Arena

Group stage & Round of 16 - Romania (Bucharest) - Arena National

Group stage & Round of 16 - Scotland (Glasgow) - Hampden Park

Group stage & Round of 16 - Spain (Bilbao) - San Mames Stadium

Group stage & Quarter-final - Azerbaijan (Baku) - National Stadium

Group stage & Quarter-final - Germany (Munich) - Allianz Arena

Group stage & Quarter-final - Italy (Rome) - Stadio Olimpico

Group stage & Quarter-final - Russia (Saint Petersburg) - Krestovsky Stadium

Group stage, Round of 16, Semi-finals & Final - England (London) - Wembley Stadium

Why is EURO2020 being held across 12 venues?

The decision to change the format of EURO2020 to include several cities, rather than one or two host capitals was decided by UEFA in 2012.

The change is to honour the tournament's 60-year anniversary with FIFA President Gianni Infantino explaining that "instead of having a party in one country, we will have a party all over Europe in the summer of 2020."

Whilst it's unclear if this is going to be a regular format, Gianni did imply this one would be a one-off change, although who knows what the future may hold.
