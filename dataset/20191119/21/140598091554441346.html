Substitute jockey wins Lingfield race after answering last-minute call on Twitter

19 Nov 2019, 21:32 by Marcus Armytage

A jockey who answered an owner’s Twitter SOS to ride his horse at Lingfield ended up riding her first winner of the season on Tuesday.

Racheal Kneller was riding out in Lambourn when Vaughan Lewis, owner of Ruacana, put out a message for a jockey to partner his horse in the novelty annual handicap hurdle in which the rider is required to have a Flat jockey’s licence, after Trevor Whelan’s flight from Ireland had been cancelled.

Until Kneller, 32, replied he had had no takers, partly because there were two all-weather Flat meetings yesterday and not all Flat jockeys are prepared to risk their necks over obstacles.

“I came up through the Pony Club riding eventers and show-jumping and have a retired racehorse which I jump, so that wasn’t a worry,” she told Telegraph Sport last night. “But if I’d known I was going to ride in it I’d have had a practice over the weekend.”

She added: “My agent said Ruacana had 11st 4lb and I thought, ‘Poor thing having to carry so much lead’ so I stopped at a McDonald’s and had a chocolate milkshake on the way. I thought that would help with a couple of pounds. Maybe I should do it more often!
