Boris Johnson on the foreign leader he admires most: ‘The EU27, all of them’

Favorite leaders and Christmas gifts in the UK election debate.

19 Nov 2019, 21:23 by Paul Dallison

Boris Johnson doesn't have a favorite world leader but 27 of them!

Asked in a televised election debate on Tuesday "which current foreign leader do you most admire?" the British prime minister said: "I would like the EU27, all of them, because they did me a fantastic deal which enables us to come out of the EU."

Jeremy Corbyn went for António Guterres, the secretary-general of the United Nations.

The Conservative and Labour leaders were taking part in a debate broadcast on ITV ahead of the U.K.'s public poll on December 12.

The final question of the debate also featured Johnson's Brexit deal. Asked what gift each leader would get the other, Corbyn said a copy of Charles Dickens' "A Christmas Carol" so he could "understand how nasty Scrooge was."

Despite being told to pick a non-political gift, Johnson said he would get Corbyn a copy of "my brilliant Brexit deal." Corbyn looked disgusted at the prospect.
