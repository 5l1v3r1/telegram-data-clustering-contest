Election debate: Boris Johnson and Jeremy Corby reveal what they'd get each other for Christmas

Lets be honest you basically wouldn't want to be getting a gift from either of these two if this was anything to go by

19 Nov 2019, 21:15 by Oliver Milne, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/oliver-milne/

Not even the festive spirit can soothe the tense relationship between Boris Johnson and Jeremy Corbyn.

If we had any doubt about how the pair really felt about each other, they were dispelled in tonight's fractious debate.

But in an attempt to bring some Christmas reconciliation at the end of the studio showdown in Salford, one audience member thought he'd get them both to think generously.

Asked what they would leave under the Christmas tree for one another - both decided to double down on their political messages.

Jeremy Corbyn said he would give the Prime Minister "'A Christmas Carol' by Charles Dickens" so that he could "learn about Scrooge".

 

Boris Johnson on the other hand was even less imaginary and said he would leave the Labour leade a copy of his Brexit deal.

Pushed by Julie Etchingham to come up with a better answer he eventually offered up "some damson jam.”

The leaders of the UK's two major parties went head-to-head in an hour-long debate hosted by Julie Etchingham in Salford's MediaCityUK.

It gave Mr Corbyn an opportunity to make up ground on the Conservatives as opinion polls showed Labour trailing behind. 

But Scottish National Party leader Nicola Sturgeon branded Mr Johnson a "scaredy-cat" over his refusal to debate her, saying she would face him "any time, any place".

The Lib Dems and the SNP yesterday lost a High Court battle to have their leaders included in the debate.

In his closing remarks, Mr Corbyn urged viewers to register to vote in a "once-in-a-generation election".

He added: "This is a once-in-a-generation election to end privatisation and give the National Health Service the funding it needs. To give people the final say and get Brexit sorted.

"To tackle the climate emergency that threatens our futures. To invest good jobs in every region and nation of our country.

"I ask that you vote for hope and vote Labour on December 12."
