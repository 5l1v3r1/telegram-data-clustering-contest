Gary Lineker slams Tottenham for sacking irreplaceable Mauricio Pochettino

19 Nov 2019, 21:23

Gary Lineker has slammed Tottenham for sacking Mauricio Pochettino and insists the club will fail to find a manager more suitable for the position than the Argentine.

Spurs released a statement on Tuesday evening confirming that Pochettino had been ‘relieved of his duties’ after five years in the hot seat, along with his coaching staff Jesus Perez, Miguel D’Agostino and Antoni Jimenez.

Pochettino found himself under pressure after a dismal start to the season in which Tottenham have struggled to live up to their heights of last term when they reached the Champions League final and finished fourth in the Premier League.

He departs with Spurs sitting just six points above the relegation down in 14th. Former Manchester United and Chelsea boss Jose Mourinho is the overwhelming favourite to take over the reins in north London.

Big-name managers Carlo Ancelotti, Max Allegri and Brendan Rodgers are also in the running for the job, but Lineker is adamant Pochettino remained Spurs’ best option.

‘Mauricio Pochettino has been sacked by Spurs. He helped the club to punch massively above their weight for years,’ the ex-Spurs and England striker tweeted following Tottenham’s statement.

‘Good luck with finding a better replacement….ain’t gonna happen.’
