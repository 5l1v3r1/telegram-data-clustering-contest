Mauricio Pochettino pleaded for 'time to build' in final Tottenham press conference

The Argentine appeared to accept he faced the possibility of being sacked after the draw with Sheffield United on November 9

19 Nov 2019, 21:35 by Irishmirror.ie

Mauricio Pochettino predicted that he would not be given the time to rebuild Tottenham in his final press conference as Spurs boss.

Spurs’ horrible start to the season continued as they were again sub-standard in stuttering to a 1-1 draw against Sheffield United before the international break, leaving them with just three wins from their opening 12 league games.

Pochettino had previously spoken at length about how the club were entering a “painful rebuild” after their run to the Champions League final last term, but no one envisaged just how painful things would be on the pitch as well as off it.

They led at the Tottenham Hotspur Stadium through Son Heung-min, but George Baldock’s 78th-minute leveller earned the Blades, who also had a goal marginally ruled out by VAR, a deserved point.

Pochettino said in the build up to the Blades clash that he would have liked to see out the remaining three years of his contract.

But following what proved to be his last game in charge, he ultimately accepted that he was running out of time.

“I think you can see that yes, we need to find the balance,” he said.

“We are building a team during the season, while competing in the Champions League and Premier League, the toughest league in the world, and always that is dangerous, this type of situation can happen.

“You expect better results than you get. Of course we are in a process to build and we’ll see if we have time to build what we want.”

The relationship between Pochettino and chairman Daniel Levy had become fraught in recent months, with the pair having ceased speaking to each other.

That in part led to the Argentine's departure as Spurs manager, with the 47-year-old being denied the chance to rebuild his squad.
