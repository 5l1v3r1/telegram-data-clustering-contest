Trump slams ‘sick’ media for speculating about his health

19 Nov 2019, 19:18 by Lia Eustachewich

President Trump bashed the media on Tuesday for speculating that his weekend visit to Walter Reed National Military Medical Center was due to major health problems.

“These people are sick,” Trump said at a Cabinet meeting in the White House Tuesday while bringing up coverage of his doctor’s visit.

“They are sick. We don’t have freedom of the press in this country. We have the opposite. We have a very corrupt media, and I hope they can get their act straight.”

“I went for a physical on Saturday,” he said.

He said his trip to Walter Reed was short but included a tour of the hospital and a visit with the family of a “young soldier who was very badly injured who in the operating room.”

The routine checkup will continue sometime in January, Trump added.

Once he got back to the White House on Saturday, however, he said he was “greeted with news that, ‘We understand you had a heart attack'” and recalled being asked by his public relations team, “Sir, are you OK?'”

“I said OK for what?” Trump continued.
