Derby care home to close due over 'minuscule' council funding

Residents and staff have been notified today

19 Nov 2019, 19:59 by John Grant

Residents and staff at a Derby care home have received notice that the facility is to close.

The Florence Nightingale Care Home, on Village Street, Derby will close in four weeks after its director Neil Vickers said running it had become unsustainable.

He said he had attended a meeting with Derby City Council officials hoping for more money to help keep the care home, which he says has been losing money for the last three to four years, afloat.

Mr Vickers, who has run the care home since 2013, said: "We were hanging on to see what the council would give us for next year. What they've given us is minuscule really. We've always asked for increased funding."

The council has said it has not been formally notified about the closure, but its annual consultation with care home owners is ongoing.

The businessman has been running care homes for decades. He said: "I've never seen it this bad in 28 years.

"They have not increased the funding properly to make it profitable. I told them, I'm not a charity or a council, I am a businessman.

"I have to make money or I'll go skint. I support that care home with my other businesses.

"If you don't fund these care homes we won't have anywhere for our elderly people. If all the care homes in Derby close down, where would you put these people?"

Mr Vickers said the Florence Nightingale is constantly losing money despite currently being full.

He said: "We are full with 20 residents and we've still had to shut it, I cannot sustain that business anymore. Care homes are massively underfunded and we will all pay the price for it.

"I'm really sad to give this notice."

Along with the residents, 20 staff members have been given notice of the closure, which will take place in four weeks.

A spokesperson for Derby City Council said: “The council has not been formally advised of any notice of closure for the Florence Nightingale Care Home.

"We are in a consultation phase with care homes about the standard residential and nursing fees for 2020/21. This is an annual process the council engages in with the sector.

"This does not propose any cuts to fees, in fact no fee rates for 2020/21 have been proposed to the sector as yet.

“The care home owners have been asked to raise their cost pressures with the council and they have until November 26 to do that.

"We are happy to open up discussions about how the council can support the sector with their cost pressures, which is the purpose of the consultation period we are engaged in.”
