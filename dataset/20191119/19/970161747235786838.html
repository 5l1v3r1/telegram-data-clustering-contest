Big Bang Theory creator jokes Russia should help with Young Sheldon's ratings

19 Nov 2019, 19:59

The Big Bang Theory creator Chuck Lorre has asked an unlikely source to help with Young Sheldon’s falling ratings (although we are pretty sure he’s joking).

The spin-off show has been successful in its own right, yet it has still failed to match the high ratings of The Big Bang Theory, which ended in May this year.

Chuck recently joked that he wished Russia would hack the Nielsen ratings to make Young Sheldon and his other show Mom’s viewership look a little better.

At the end of each of his programs, the show creator is allowed one second to display a vanity card (a card which allows him to say anything he wants).

With this week’s instalment, he joked about the falling ratings of both of his hit TV shows.

The card read: ‘Russia, if you’re reading this, hack into the Nielsen computers and make our ratings higher.’

The news follows after Young Sheldon was faced with a $272,000 (£219,000) for using an emergency alert tone sound effect.

The series, which focuses on the growing pains and family life of a young Sheldon Cooper, used an altered version of the Emergency Alert System tone which has been banned in the US from being used outside of legitimate emergencies.

As a result, the Federal Communications Commission (FCC) wants to slap a fine on the show for not adapting the sound enough to be not considered the actual alert.

They stated: ‘On April 12, 2018, CBS transmitted an episode of Young Sheldon via at least 227 television stations, including 15 of CBS’s owned-and-operated television stations.

‘The episode included a sound effect accompanying a tornado warning, which the producers modified, but still audibly resembled actual EAS tones.’

The Big Bang Theory airs on E4 and is available to watch on Netflix in the UK.
