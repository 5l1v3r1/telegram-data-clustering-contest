Arlington council member rebuked for calling mayor ‘corrupt’

Sue Weiss’ unsubstantiated claim about Mayor Barb Tolbert prompted the ire of other council members.

19 Nov 2019, 19:59 by Douglas Buell

ARLINGTON — A remark by an Arlington city councilwoman in a news article, in which she called the mayor “corrupt,” led council members to explore rules for civility at a workshop Tuesday — but not before the elected official who made the accusation left the room, upset.

In comments in a Nov. 8 election story in the Arlington Times, incumbent Sue Weiss, who is losing to Michele Blythe in a race to retain her seat, said she was disappointed that citizens would have to contend with more of the same for another four years with someone she deemed corrupt. Her unsubstantiated claim was in reference to Mayor Barb Tolbert, who on Tuesday was ahead by 35 votes in her bid for re-election.

According to the Times, Weiss wrote: “I am most disappointed for the citizens of Arlington that will have to contend with more of the same for the next four years from the most corrupt mayor I have ever known! My hope for Arlington is that in a couple years, more people will take a stand and run for City Council.”

The accusation drew the ire of council members.

Councilwoman Debora Nelson introduced an item not on the agenda at the end of the meeting that served the purpose of calling out Weiss publicly for her remark and exploring codes of conduct to prevent similar situations in the future.

“Council member Weiss made unsubstantiated accusations of corruption within our city government in the Arlington Times last week,” Nelson said. “I’m unaware of her reporting any alleged wrongdoings to the state auditor during her four-year term on the council.”

Weiss, sitting across from Nelson, spoke over her, saying that the workshop was not the venue for a discussion on conduct. She added that she was under no obligation to run by others what she said to the press. Meanwhile, the mayor tried to call for order.

Weiss then stood up, grabbed her things and walked out of the chambers.
