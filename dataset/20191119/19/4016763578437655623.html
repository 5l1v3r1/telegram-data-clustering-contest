Tyler, The Creator Vents About Pop Stars & Industry Gatekeepers Not Trusting His Ideas

19 Nov 2019, 19:38 by Eddie Fu

Tyler, The Creator’s latest album, IGOR, debuted at No. 1 on the Billboard 200 and earned him widespread critical praise, but the California rapper isn’t fully satisfied with his success. In a new GQ cover story, Tyler opened up about his frustration with not yet earning the approval of pop stars and gatekeepers like Hot 97 DJ Funkmaster Flex.

He revisited the experience of being turned down by both Justin Bieber and Rihanna for “EARFQUAKE,” the breakout hit from IGOR.

“I wanted him to have it, and it didn’t work out,” Tyler remembered about sending the song to Bieber. “I be trying to send people songs and it didn’t work.”

Tyler also recalled a sit down with Funk Flex in which he seemed to open the hip-hop traditionalist’s ears to his music. According to GQ, the rapper said Flex never followed up with his thoughts on IGOR after the interview.

“It’s just, like, ‘Trust me, bro,'” Tyler said about the gatekeepers and figureheads of mainstream pop. “I know this isn’t your type of thing, but I know that this end result will be a good idea if you just let me cook.”

However, the rapper seems determined to figure out how he can tackle the challenge—regardless of how long it takes.

“That’s the one thing I can’t crack,” he said. “I don’t know what the fuck it is. To just trust me on little shit like that. It’s still a learning curve. I’ve figured everything else out thus far. It might take long. It might be next week. It might be another three years.”

Elsewhere in the interview, Tyler spoke about the delicate balancing act between humor and art that he continues to navigate with his latest album. “The IGOR shit plays a thin line between goofy and funny and art,” he explained. “I always have to be careful so it doesn’t fuck everyone’s perception up. It’s art, and an idea.”

All of these comments align with a recent Wall Street Journal Magazine interview in which Tyler explained how he made a concerted effort to change his online image in order to win the same respect as his idols.
