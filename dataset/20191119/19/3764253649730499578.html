Kogi election: APC cautions INEC against verdict of observers

19 Nov 2019, 19:56

- The APC has dimissed the verdicts of observers in the just concluded governorship election in Kogi state

- The ruling party described the election observers as corrupt elements

- It is on record that majority of the election observers called for the cancellation of the exercise

The All Progressive Congress (APC) has cautioned the Independent National Electoral Commission (NEC) to be wary of certain election observers who sought financial inducement in the performance of their duty.

The party was reacting to several calls by independent election observers for the electoral body to cancel the Kogi governorship election.

The APC described the observers as 'corrupt elements,' accused them of taking the position, because it refused to dance to their tunes.

In a statement sent to journaists on Tuesday, November 18, the spokesperson of the APC campaign council, Kingsley Fanwo, stated that it was out of place to adopt a general evaluation of events with reported incidences of violence in some areas.

He asserted that some observers instead of going to the field to monitor the exercise thronged the houses of candidates for logistics to give a favourable report of the elections.

The party spokesperson affirmed that as a government they were not bothered by the damning reports by the obsevers.

He opined that true and genuine observation of elections was geared towards pointing areas where electoral umpire or security agents needed to improve in future elections

He said the observers should hide their faces in shame, calling them 'cash and carry observers.'

The group made the call hours after a civil society organisation, YIAGA AFRICA, alleged that the credibility of Kogi elections has been compromised by political parties and security agencies and as a result called for the cancellation of the polls.
