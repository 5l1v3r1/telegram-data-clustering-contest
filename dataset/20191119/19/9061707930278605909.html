When Derby County fans can see Rams on TV over Christmas period

Derby County latest | A regularly-updated guide of when the Rams will next be on television and how to watch both live action and highlights, as Phillip Cocu's side look to climb the Championship table

19 Nov 2019, 19:00 by Steve Nicholson

Derby County continue to be a regular presence on Sky Sports' coverage of the Championship - and there are more televised games on the way for Phillip Cocu's side.

The Rams have already been on the box five times in the league this season, the most recent being the East Midlands derby defeat to Nottingham Forest.

Before that, the opening-day win over Huddersfield Town was followed by draws against West Bromwich Albion, Cardiff City and Leeds United on the box.

We will keep this story regularly updated so you know when Derby are next on your screens.

The next selected TV match for the Rams is home to Sheffield Wednesday on Wednesday December 11.

Derby will also feature live on Sky Sports a couple of times over the Christmas period, including their first match of 2020 against Barnsley, which could see new signing Wayne Rooney make his Rams debut.

All midweek Championship games are screened on the red button as part of a deal with the EFL which began last season.

Red button events are not available via Sky Go or the Sky Q app - but you can watch midweek Sky Bet Championship matches via the Sky Sports app.

Here are the Sky Sports matches currently pencilled in for the Rams:

Derby County's television games

Derby County v Sheffield Wednesday (Sky Sports) - Wednesday, 11th December (7.45pm)

Derby County v Charlton Athletic (Sky Sports) - Monday, 30th December (7.45pm)

Derby County v Barnsley (Sky Sports) - Thursday, 2nd January (7.45pm)

2019 mid-week red button games (Sky Sports)

Fulham v Derby County - Tuesday, 26th November (7.45pm)

Watching Championship highlights

Where can we watch Rams highlights?

Championship highlights are shown on Quest.

It is part of a four-year deal which will bring 90 minutes of match highlights, hosted by Colin Murray, at 9pm every Saturday night.

Quest are also broadcasting 10 hour-long highlights shows capturing the action from every round of midweek fixtures.
