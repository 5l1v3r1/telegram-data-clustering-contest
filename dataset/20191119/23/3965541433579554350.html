Joyous reunion at PDX: Cat back with owner after 1,200-mile journey

19 Nov 2019, 23:27 by KATU News

An incredible tale of lost and found ended right here in Portland.

Viktor Usov, who lives in our area, lost his cat Sasha five years ago.

He reported him missing, but as time went on, he started to lose hope that he would ever see him again.

Then he got a call from an animal shelter in New Mexico.

Somehow, Sasha had traveled more than 1,000 miles and ended up in Santa Fe.

He was microchipped, which is how the shelter figured out who he belonged to.

After word got out, American Airlines offered to fly Sasha back to Portland, along with someone from the shelter.

On Tuesday night, the cat and the owner were reunited at PDX.

"It's crazy. I've been at the shelter for five years. I’ve talked to people that’s worked in the shelter business for over a decade, and they've never heard of an animal traveling 12-hundred miles," said Murad Kirdar with Santa Fe Animal Shelter.

Viktor thinks Sasha must have hitched a ride with someone to get so far away.

He said he’s just glad to have the cat back.
