Farage fails to name a single thing he has done to help environment

19 Nov 2019, 23:32

Nigel Farage has failed to name a single thing he has done to help the climate crisis.

The Brexit Party leader was pressured to come up with examples of changes he has made to his personal behaviour and could only admit that he is not a ‘leading example’ on environmental issues.

Mr Farage called for more green action and a massive increase in tree planting, in the second part of ITV’s General Election debate coverage this evening.

But he conceded that he still drove and flew, which meant he could not claim to have made changes to his own life to help the environment.

He explained: ‘I drive all over the country, I catch a lot of aeroplane flights, I am not a leading example.

‘But I think the UK should start a global initiative on planting trees on a massive scale.’

Earlier today, Mr Farage called for a net migration cap of 50,000.

He also said if he had to pick one political leader to go on I’m A Celeb with, it would be Boris Johnson – so that he could ‘share a bottle of something’ with the Prime Minister and find out what he ‘really believes’.

The leaders of the Liberal Democrats, Scottish National Party and Greens also appeared, following the debate between Boris Johnson and Jeremy Corbyn.
