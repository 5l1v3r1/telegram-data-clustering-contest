SCOTTISH SUN SAYS

Boris Johnson is utterly terrified of being eaten alive by Nicola Sturgeon in a debate

19 Nov 2019, 23:48 by The Scottish Sun

ELECTION telly debates almost always give off more heat than light.

And there was no change tonight as Boris Johnson and Jeremy Corbyn squared up for the first head-to-head.

For all the hype about who won or who messed up, it’s unlikely the debate will change the minds of many voters.

Watching the pair slug it out would have been an especially odd spectacle for many Scottish voters, with no place for Nicola Sturgeon, despite the SNP being streets ahead north of the border.

It also looks unlikely Mr Johnson will debate the First Minister any time soon.

Despite his bluster about the First Minister not standing as PM, it’s hard to conclude he’s not utterly terrified of being eaten alive by Ms Sturgeon.

The alternative TV battle was on the other channel, of course, as Scotland took on Kazakhstan.

But Johnson v Corbyn was enough to make a meaningless Euro qualifier at chilly Hampden look like the World Cup Final.

Greens goof

THE Greens and SNP are locked in a perpetual battle at Holyrood over who’s the most “progressive”.

In terms of tax, this vacuous buzzword means one thing — clobbering the wealthy as hard as possible.

Well, there’s bad news for the SNP.

It looks like the Greens might have won.

Their proposal for a rates overhaul, set down in a parliamentary amendment, would lead to — quite literally — astronomical bills for big businesses.

The Greens now insist their plans were an error. Quite how they made this mistake is a mystery, given the step-by-step calculation was carefully spelled out in legislative format.

On one hand, the bungle is funny. Until you remember the Greens have the minority SNP Government under the thumb.

Come Budget time it will be the Greens — complete with their crackpot calculations — who have the whip hand over the Nats.

Bleak diagnosis

FIGURES out yesterday for A&E waiting times do not bode well.

The number of Scots spending more than 12 hours in accident and emergency is up, while overall performance against the four-hour aim is at its worst since March 2018.

At the troubled Queen Elizabeth University Hospital in Glasgow, almost a third of patients spent longer than the target time in the emergency department.

As ever, the SNP will seek to claim performance is better than in other parts of the UK.

But these are targets set by Nats ministers, and they are meant to be achievable. It looks like we’re in for a long winter.

And ministers will not be forgiven if our hospitals are unprepared.
