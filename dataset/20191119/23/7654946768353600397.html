O’Neill defends stop-and-frisk after Bloomberg apology

19 Nov 2019, 23:21 by Craig McCarthy

Outgoing NYPD Commissioner James O’Neill defended stop-and-frisk as a “constitutionally tested tool” Tuesday — just days after one of the tactic’s biggest boosters, former Mayor Michael Bloomberg, publicly apologized for it.

“It helps us get weapons off the street, it helps keep the city safe,” the commissioner said in an interview with 1010 WINS’s Juliet Papa. “But it has to be used correctly, and obviously it has to be used constitutionally.”

The contentious policing tactic, an integral issue in the former mayor’s three-term legacy, eventually led to a civil-rights suit — Floyd v. City of New York.

The case ended with a judge ruling that stop-and-frisk led to “indirect racial profiling” and police were using it in an “unconstitutional” manner — leading to the NYPD to significantly scale back on the tactic, going from nearly 700,000 stops at its peak to just over 11,000 last year.

“It’s something that we still employ. We are concentrating on the people driving the crime. It’s a constitutionally-tested tool that has to be used, needs to be used,” O’Neill said in the interview, adding that he respects Bloomberg for taking the stance.

“It’s not easy to apologize,” he said. “If that’s what’s in his heart, and he felt it necessary to do… I think it was a good step for him.”
