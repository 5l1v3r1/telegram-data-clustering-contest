Customers claim Ulta’s Beautyfest was full of empty promises

19 Nov 2019, 03:44 by Lisa Fickenscher

Ulta Beauty’s recent ploy to boost foot traffic amid sagging sales is backfiring on social media.

The retailer told eager consumers last week that its 1,200 stores would open one hour earlier than usual — at 9 a.m. — on Saturday and Sunday to celebrate its Beautyfest promotion. The chain promised free giveaways, coupons, $100 gift cards and other perks.

But customers quickly flooded social media with complaints that some stores failed to open their doors on time and that they waited in freezing temperatures for chintzy freebies.

“@ultabeauty I was here an hour early and they aren’t open!” one upstate New York consumer wrote.

“#ultabeautyfest was the Fyre Fest but for makeup lovers,” tweeted another disgruntled customer.

Customers complained that they waited in line only to be handed a measly $5 coupon, or discounts and goodie bags with unexpected fine print, including approval of an Ulta credit card.

Another tweet: “That was disappointing this morning @ultabeauty. Show up early to get a cheap plastic bag and a coupon that I can’t use because I’m not a first time customer. #ultabeautyfest #Sweepstakes.”

Excitement over the promotion was strong to start, but as word about the disappointing giveaways spread on social media, “traffic trailed off,” according to retail consultant Gabriella Santaniello, whose A-Line firm dispatched retail checkers to the stores.

Ulta on Monday apologized to its customers, saying it “missed the mark.”

“While most of our guests were thrilled, we realize that some were not and we apologize to those who thought we missed the mark,” an Ulta spokesperson said in a statement. “We’ve listened to our guest feedback and learned from what they have shared with us.”
