Stars face Libya in AFCON qualifiers today

19 Nov 2019, 03:25 by By Guardian Reporter

SENIOR national football team, Taifa Stars, will be out to stay within reach of progression to the 2021 Africa Cup of Nations AFCON finals, as they confront Libya in Group J duel of the continental tournament’s qualifiers in Tunis today.

Taifa Stars had an impressive start to their campaign in the qualifiers, notching 2-1 win over Equatorial Guinea at the National Stadium in Dar es Salaam weekend.

Striker Simon Msuva and midfielder Salum Abubakar netted for Taifa Stars in the clash, forward Pedro Obiang scored what turned out to be a consolation for Equatorial Guinea’s consolation.

Taifa Stars definitely need to be at their best against Libya, as the latter will be keen on getting their campaign in the qualifiers back on track.

Libya experienced frustrating start to their campaign, conceding a 4-1 loss to Tunisia, who are widely expected to sail through to the 2021 AFCON finals.

Wahbi Khazri and Saif-Eddine Khaoui netted two goals apiece for Tunisia, the semi-finalists in the last AFCON finals which was hosted by Egypt.

Taifa Stars are currently placed second in the Group J of the qualifiers behind the group favourites, Tunisia, whereas Equatorial Guinea and Libya, who lost their respective opening matches, are placed third and fourth.

Much as both Taifa Stars and Tunisia have collected three points, the North Africa giants boast of better goal aggregate, thanks to their impressive victory over Libya.

Taifa Stars will, today, miss the services of utility player Erasto Nyoni that picked an injury in the last weekend clash with Equatorial Guinea.

Nyoni played in the defensive midfield in the first half of the clash, whereas Kelvin Yondani partnered Bakari Mwamnyeto in the central defense role,

With Ndayiragije seeking to bolster Stars’ striking force, Nyoni was, in the second half, deployed as central defender after right fullback, Hassan Ramadhan, had been rested for striker Ditram Nchimbi.

Yondani was, for that matter, deployed as right fullback in the second half in a tactical substitution which ultimately paid off.
Juma Kaseja, Stars’ keeper, requested soccer followers in Tanzania to pray for the team in an effort to see to it the side comes out with good results.

Kaseja promised Taifa Stars’ players will play their hearts out against Libya with a view to notching victory and boost their pursuit of progression to the AFCON finals.

Taifa Stars will then host Tunisia at the National Stadium on September 8 next year.
