Baldivis Chinese BBQ Restaurant fined $20,000 for food safety convictions

19 Nov 2019, 06:29

A suburban Chinese restaurant in Perth’s south has been hit with more than $20,000 in fines and legal costs after being convicted of three food safety breaches.

Baldivis Chinese BBQ Restaurant on Atwick Terrace was hit with the infringements after an inspection by City of Rockingham health and safety officers.

The offences included failing to store food in a way that protected it from contamination, and failing to store potentially hazardous food under temperature control.

The restaurant was also caught out not maintaining the premises “to an acceptable standard of cleanliness”.

The owner of the restaurant, who refused to give her full name, said the fines were for “a very small matter”.

She said she did not want to elaborate on the fines.

A spokesman said the City of Rockingham was “unable to provide any further comment” on the issue.

The restaurant was fined $20,000 in total and forced to pay $1813 in costs.

The news follows revelations in October that the now-defunct Metro Indian Restaurant in Subiaco copped the biggest fine in WA history for some of the worst breaches local food inspectors had seen.

In May, Nedlands Vietnamese eatery TQR Restaurant was described as having an “extensive history” of food complaints as it copped a $25,000 fine for a filthy kitchen and cockroach droppings.

The City of Fremantle also released details of the Hilton Curry Club Indian restaurant’s food safety breaches in May.

Krusty Kob bakery in Thornlie was fined more than $10,000 for selling a mouldy birthday cake and in February the IGA in Rockingham copped a $45,000 fine for selling food after its use-by date.

And “underground” barbecue business Big Don’s Smoked Meats was convicted of Food Act breaches in January and fined $30,000.
