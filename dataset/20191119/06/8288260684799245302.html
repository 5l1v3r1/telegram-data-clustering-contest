Ian Wright bravely reveals he cried 'for days' after not making England's Euro 96 squad

Last night, Wright left viewers saddened - by admitting he cried for days following his omission from the England squad for Euro 1996

19 Nov 2019, 06:55 by James Rodger, https://www.facebook.com/jamesrodgerjournalist

Ian Wright has bravely opened up on I'm A Celebrity... Get Me Out Of Here! over his biggest sporting heartbreak.

The former footballer turned broadcaster is now a frequent pundit on BBC One highlights show Match of the Day.

And Wright, who was awarded an MBE, is currently competing on I'm A Celeb alongside nine other stars.

His campmates include Caitlyn Jenner, Kate Garraway and James Haskell.

Last night, Wright left viewers saddened - by admitting he cried for days following his omission from the England squad for Euro 1996.

 

Ian said: "To be honest I just wanted to win the FA Cup and the League and then in the end I won the FA Cup a couple of times, won the Premier League and played for England.”

But Ian added: “I couldn’t believe I got left out of (Euro) 96, I cried for days. He (Glenn Hoddle) took Robbie Fowler and Les Ferdinand who are obviously top players, unbelievable players but I was devastated.”

 

Caitlyn said: “Ian, James and myself all some from a sports background so for me, I’m always intrigued by people who have achieved so much in sport and I want to hear their story.”

Rugby union star James said: “I think everyone, especially the three of us appreciates what it took to get to the levels we did.  We have definitely got a common ground, it’s nice to share that and hear from two people I really admire.”
