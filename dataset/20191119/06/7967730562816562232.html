Warning issued as prescribed burns blanket Perth in smoke

19 Nov 2019, 06:02 by Tabarak Al Jrood

Emergency services have warned residents to shut their doors and windows after a smoke alert was issued stretching from Perth to Bunbury.

The Parks and Wildlife Service issued the alert as a result of prescribed burns being carried out in the South West.

More than 30 prescribed burns are under way across the state, with several burning around the Perth metro area.

As the smoke may reduce visibility on roads, motorists are warned to take extra care, turn on their headlights and travel at appropriate speeds.

People with asthma and pre-existing respiratory or cardiovascular illnesses are advised to follow a prepared treatment plan while those with conditions exacerbated by smoke should take precautions in line with their medical advice.

Bureau of Meteorology duty forecaster Steph Bond said the smoke was expected to clear overnight.

“There’s going to be strong winds increasing in strength tonight and also showers developing over the metro area, so that will help clear the smoke,” she said.
