Fireside Fest ready to rock

19 Nov 2019, 10:18 by Nicholas Johansen

A home-grown Kelowna music festival returnsÂ for 2020, expanding to three days this year.

Fireside Festival began in the living room of Aaron DeSilva's Upper Mission home in the winter of 2014, as a chance to show off the musical talents of his friends for a night.

The festival quickly outgrew the home, moving to BNA Brewing Co. in 2016, and expanding to two days of music in 2018.

DeSilva, who DJs locally under the name Deepsounds, released the lineup for the seventh iteration of the festival Monday night.

The festival will run Jan. 24-26, taking place across Kelowna at Dunnenzies Pizza's Mission location Friday night, the new Rustic Reel Brewing Co. Saturday afternoon, and BNA Saturday night and all day Sunday.

Saturday night at BNA will be headlined by Vancouver's Little Destroyer, who played the festival in 2018, while Sunday will headlined by Snotty Nose Rez Kids. The hip-hop duo, originally from Kitimaat Village on B.C.'s coast, were nominated for a Juno Award for Indigenous Music Album of the Year in 2019.

Friday night at Dunnenzies will feature a hip-hop showcase, while acoustic sets will take over Rustic Reel Saturday afternoon.
