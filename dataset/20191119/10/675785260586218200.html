Violent thug who repeatedly stabbed victim is on the run after escaping prison

Aiden Morrissey was jailed in Birmingham for almost nine years for stabbing a man who tried to stop him assaulting a woman at a cashpoint

19 Nov 2019, 10:55 by James Rodger, Lila Randall

A prisoner who was jailed for knife crime and battery is on the run after escaping an open prison.

Aiden Morrissey, 21, was jailed for eight years and eight months for wounding with intent and possessing a knife in public in February 2017.

He was also sentenced for two counts of battery against a woman who he punched in the eye and pulled her hair, Birmingham Live reported.

The public are being warned not to approach the dangerous knifeman after he fled a prison close to the Staffordshire border.

He left HMP Sudbury, an open prison in Ashbourne, yesterday and has not returned.

He is white, five foot 10 inches tall and has grey eyes and brown hair. He also has the word "mom" tattooed on his right arm.

Morrissey was jailed after he repeatedly stabbed Will Flint, an award-winning fitness competitor, when Flint intervened in the teenager attacking a woman at a cashpoint.

Mr Flint - who was 27 at the time - had heard a commotion in the early hours of New Years Day and went to help when Morrissey attacked, leaving him with a dozen separate knife wounds and 60 stitches.

Speaking at the time, Mr Flint said the severity of his wounds meant he would no longer be able to compete in the professional fitness sphere.

A police spokesman said: "If you have seen Morrissey or know of his whereabouts, please do not approach him, instead contact the police immediately quoting reference number 19561817."

Anyone with any information can also call Crimestoppers anonymously on 0800 555 111.
