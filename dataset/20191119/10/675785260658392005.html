Callum Smith admits Anfield dream is on the line against John Ryder

Callum Smith takes on John Ryder at Liverpool's Liverpool’s M&S Bank Arena on Saturday with the hope of a future bout at his beloved Anfield

19 Nov 2019, 10:18 by David Anderson, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/david-anderson/

Callum Smith claims he will blow fighting at Anfield and all his other dreams if he loses to John Ryder on Saturday.

Smith makes the second defence of his WBA Super super-middleweight title against Ryder in Liverpool’s M&S Bank Arena and the Kopite is in line to fight at his beloved Anfield if he wins.

The unbeaten Smith also dreams of fighting Gennady Golovkin or Canelo Alvarez in Las Vegas and knows none of this will happen if he slips up against Ryder.

“Listen I’m switched on,” he said. “The fights that have been mentioned with Canelo, Golovkin, the big unification fights, there’re still there providing I come through this fight.

“But if I lose this fight, they disappear. It’s a must win for me and it’s one more step towards those fights.

“I know if I slip up, Anfield is gone. I’m fully focused on this one and if I get this one out of the way, I’ll speak to Eddie Hearn and see if he can deliver me a big fight in 2020.

“I’ve trained hard. I’ve worked hard to become a world champion and I don’t plan on losing it just yet.”

Smith, who is 26-0, is a big Liverpool fan, like his fighting brothers, and says he has imagined what it would be like to walk out at Anfield.

“The last few times I’ve been there, I have sat in the crowd and just tried to imagine what the atmosphere would be like for me,” he said.

“It would be massive for me. It is possible, but the timing has to be right and there’s no time like now because I’m a world champion.”

Smith, 29, is relishing his homecoming and his last fight in Liverpool was in September 2017, a year before he became world champion.

“I’m excited about that because when I was coming through as a young professional, I wasn’t known all over the country, but the people here they backed me from the start,” he said.

“It’s nice to be able to bring a world title back to the city, which is something I always wanted to do. It’s a dream for me. It will be a special night for me, but the win is the most important thing.”
