Magic Days are Coming

19 Nov 2019, 10:27 by Yelaine Martínez Herrera

The 24th edition of the Ánfora Magic Festival, from November 20 to 24, will bring together renowned representatives of that ancient art on the Island and foreign talents from Nicaragua, the United States, Costa Rica and Canada in Las Tunas.

Las Tunas, Cuba.- As reported at a press conference, companies from the Cuban provinces of Santiago de Cuba, Villa Clara and Camagüey will participate; in addition to independent magicians from those territories, and others from Pinar del Río, Havana, Matanzas, Ciego de Ávila, Holguín, Granma and the hosts.

The jury, chaired by Fernando Rodríguez Vázquez, will reward the most prominent in the modalities of card, close-up, general and comic magic, mentalism, small and big illusions.

"One of the most special moments will be the tribute to José Reynerio Valdivia Valdivia, Magician Píter, who from here exalted that art, creating a school, a company, and this festival, with prestige beyond our borders," said Alberto Carlos Estrada, a specialist of the Performing Arts Council.

"Magician Merlina will also perform the Broken Wand Ceremony to pay homage to Magician Ayra, who died last April and was a faithful participant in the event," he added.

The program, in addition to the competitions, includes conferences, galas, and tributes to prominent personalities of the guild, among other activities. Several premieres stand out in the venues of the Tunas Theater, the Raúl Gómez García Hall and the Teatro Tuyo Cultural Center.

The host Company, Huracán Mágico (Magic Hurricane), will perform on November 21 at 9:00 p.m. The debut of Granmagia (Granma) will be the next day, at the same hour, with the Génesis show. The presentation of El Teatro Cubano de la Magia (Cuban Theater of Magic), from Camagüey, is reserved for Saturday, with the premiere of the Dejavú show. And Sunday, at 5:00 p.m., will be the award ceremony, with the presence on the stage of international guests.

The magicians Raúl Romero (Havana), Leonel Leyva (from Santiago, with residence in Ciego de Ávila) and Rodelay (director of the Huracán Mágico Company), with an outstanding work in the "universe of cards and top hats," will be decorated as part of the activities scheduled for the closing day of the Ánfora Festival.
