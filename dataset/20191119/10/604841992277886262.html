Round O stun Lawless's

19 Nov 2019, 10:11

Top of the table Lawless's Bar travelled to Navan to take on the Round O in round six of the Navan & District Darts League, but they came away empty-handed following a 2-4 loss.
After defeating Lynch's the previous week to climb to the top of the table Laless travelled in expectation of another win.
The Round O's Mark Costello took on Keith Cummins in the opening match and he claimed the opening spoils, 3-2.
Second up saw the hosts John McLoughlin take on Eoin Hand and with both lads hitting a 180 each it was Hand that took out a 110 fora win 3-2 . 
Round O's Declan Dowdall faced Paul Hand and played the best game of the year to win 3-0 with a 91 checkout. 
Paul O'Rourke cruised to a 3-0 win over Davy donnelly, Starting the last leg with a 180 and taking the double out to guarantee at least a draw for the Round O.
The fifth game saw Ross Clarke take on Ian Hand and it was a close game 2-2 going into the last game.
In the sixth and final game Des Keegan lost out 2-3 to Geoff Power, but  it was the Round O who took the points and win 4-2 on the night 
Round O - Mark Costello, John McLoughlin, Declan Dowdall, Paul O'Rourke, Ross Clarke, Des Keegan, Sean McGillic, Noel Byrne, Shane Kerrigan.
Lawless's - Keith Cummins, Eoin Hand, Paul Hand (jr), Davy Donnelly, Ian Hand,  Geoff Power, Paul Hand, Paul Pepperd 

Teach Na Teamhrach travelled to the Foundry and got off to a bad start as the hosts raced into a 3-0 with wins for Derek Dowdall, Jim Quinn and Robbie Gibney.
Teach na Teamhrach pulled the next two games back when Patrick Darby won 3-1 and Chris Moffett won 3-2, but it was Derry Farrelly that got the points for the Foundry that returned them back to the top of the league.
In form Paddy's found the going tough in Tara na Ri going down 2-4. Paddy's started well and raced into a 2-1 lead, but wins for Darren Lanny and Pat Callan put Tara na Ri in control.
Jack Quinn's made the long journey from Scurlogstown to Slane to take on Lynch's and they looked like going home empty-hnanded when they trailed 1-3. However a win for Simon Cox in game five made it 2-3 and in the final match Bobby Brannigan came up with the goods to seal a 3-3 draw.
Lantern headed to Lawless' lounge and they won 6-0 to move up to joint second along with their team mates Lawless' bar and the Round O
