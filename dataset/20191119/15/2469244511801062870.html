Talks restart on 'eyesore' Crossways centre at Paignton

Torbay Council is trying to reach a deal to buy the centre so it can get on with proposals to redevelop the site

19 Nov 2019, 15:19 by Edward Oldfield

Torbay Council has restarted talks with the owner of the run-down Crossways shopping centre in Paignton in an effort to speed up regeneration plans.

The council is trying to reach a deal to buy the centre so it can get on with proposals to redevelop the site.

It has now requested an independent valuation of the property to form the basis of negotiations with the owner.

Agreeing a deal on the price would be quicker than opting for compulsory purchase, a legal process which could take up to 18 months.

In September councillors gave senior officers powers to start the compulsory purchase if necessary.

A meeting of Torbay Council heard that it would provide a back-up if negotiations with the owner failed.

Plans have been put forward to redevelop the near-empty concrete complex with an estimated Â£22.5m scheme.

It would include shops on the ground floor and "extra-care" affordable housing on the upper levels.

The shopping centre has a walkway which links Torquay Road and Hyde Road, but most of the shops are now empty and it has been described as an eyesore.

The site has been zoned for redevelopment in local planning policies in recent years but the owner Gaelic Investments has so far held on to it.

Councillors were told at a meeting it September that the owner said early this year it had its own redevelopment plans.

But because there was no sign of progress, such as a planning application, the council was now intending to get on with the job.

A report said officers would restart talks with the owner to buy the land while preparing the compulsory purchase order as a back-up.

A planning application for the site would also be brought forward, councillors heard.
