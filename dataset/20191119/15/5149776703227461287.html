'Wool Hurts' - PETA's bizarre Christmas jumper features 'bruised and bloodied' 3D sheep

An animal rights group wants to encourage people to switch to cruelty-free knits

19 Nov 2019, 15:37 by Andrew Forgrave

A Christmas jumper that is unlike anything you've seen has gone on sale.

An animal rights group has revealed its festive sweater for 2019, which features 3D 'bruised and bloodied' sheep.

The plush animals appear to burst through the green and red jumper, says NorthWalesLive .

People for the Ethical Treatment of Animal (PETA) says it hopes the design will shock Christmas revellers into ditching wool in their clothes and homes.

The controversial group calls the look “a show-stopping, conversation-starting” design.

Featuring the words “Wool Hurts” on the front, PETA says wearers can “join in on the holiday fun while making a statement for animals.”

However the price tag is an eye-watering $149.99 from PETA USA – though it can be ordered internationally.

Available in one size, it’s made in Germany using 100% acrylic yarn.

The PETA sweater forms part of the group’s investigative campaigning: since 2014, the group says it has released 11 exposés of 99 wool-industry facilities on four continents, including in the UK.

A description on the PETA website reads: “This is part of PETA's campaign against the wool industry, where workers have been caught beating, kicking, stomping on, and even killing gentle sheep in shearing sheds.

“With the help of celebrities such as Billie Eilish and P!nk, PETA is calling on shoppers (and retailers) to help sheep by switching to cruelty-free knits.”
