The Crown star says Prince Charles has spent his whole life waiting for his mum to die

Prince Charles Actor Josh O'Connor has spoken about how he believes the future king's whole life has been lived in 'purgatory'

19 Nov 2019, 15:51 by Tiffany Wallis

Josh O’Connor, who plays the future king in season three of Netflix series The Crown , has spoken out about how Prince Charles lives in the shadow of his mother.

The Prince Of Wales actor, 29, delved beneath the surface of Prince Charles to truly capture his character in the hit royal series, but discovered some sad realities.

During an interview with GQ Hype , the rising star insisted that Prince Charles' whole life has been lived in "purgatory" and said that he is "waiting for his mother to die."

He shockingly told the publication: "What I’m gradually finding is a guy waiting for his mother to die for his life to have meaning. His whole life has been in purgatory.

"Waiting for your parent to die is pretty messed up."

Josh said the Queen is responsible for whether or not Charles' life can have meaning.

He continued: "That the one person who should be unconditional is the person that holds the power for you to have any meaning in your life.

"It’s quite nice to play that. They’re huge stakes"

This certainly runs true, as Queen Elizabeth is the longest ever serving monarch, aged 93, with Prince Charles remaining the next in line for the throne, aged 71.

The young prince makes his series debut in episode six, where he endures a semester 'abroad' to learn Welsh at the order of his family and government.

Netflix's hit series The Crown has already begun taking over the lives of its fans as season three arrived at the weekend with a bang.

The third series of The Crown opened in 1964 and covers the Queen’s Silver Jubilee, the moon landing and Prince Charles’s relationship with a young Camilla Shand.

The season delve into the mysterious complicated romances between Charles, Camilla, Andrew Parker Bowles and Princess Anne .

Helena Bonham Carter, Olivia Colman and Tobias Menzies also star in the new series.

To get his portrayal of Prince Charles right, Josh did a lot of research on his character before acting him out.

He learned to ride a horse and started eating a lot of boiled eggs to resemble Prince Charles' eating habits.

The star also told GQ Hype that he had to mimic the prince's habits correctly, even down to finicky details such as how he holds a horse's reins in an unusual way.

The Crown season three is available on Netflix.
