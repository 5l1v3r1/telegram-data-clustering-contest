Rihanna is 'bad at taking pictures' says Seth Meyers

19 Nov 2019, 15:59

Rihanna might seem absolutely flawless, but it turns out there’s one thing she’s bad at – well, according to Seth Meyer’s anyway.

When appearing on The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon, the 45-year-old opened up on his iconic day drinking session with the Umbrella hitmaker – which saw her dish out some X-rated advice.

But while gushing over the pop star, he also threw some shade in her direction, as he teased that she’s bad at taking pictures.

Jimmy prompted his confession when he asked the comedian whether or not there’s anything she’s bad at.

‘Yes,’ Seth smirked: ‘At one point she took my phone and took a bunch of pictures while we were day drinking.’

He added: ‘And then when I went through them, I was like, “Rihanna is bad at taking pictures.”‘

You know, despite those seemingly perfect selfies!

But despite not getting a good shot to remember the day by, it seems the star’s memories were more than enough to keep him going.

‘It was beyond my wildest dreams,’ he said of their time together: ‘We went day drinking. Why would you say yes to that? But it was amazing.’

The father-of-two added: ‘People have asked, “What was it like drinking with Rihanna?”

‘You know, like there’s some people where the more you drink over the course of the night, the more they start looking like Rihanna, imagine starting there!’

He laughed: ‘You know what I mean? But she was so much fun and is the nicest person in the world and it was a blast.’

When asked if he managed to get her phone number, Seth chuckled: ‘I wouldn’t want it, I think it would be too dangerous to have, I think I’d spend all day like, “Whats a funny thing to send Rihanna?”‘

But while she might be bad at taking photos, it seems the 31-year-old is even worse at dropping new music, as lo and behold we’re still waiting on an album.

Teasing a little bit about her ninth studio album, the singer recently revealed that it will be reggae-inspired.

‘I like to look at it as a reggae-inspired or reggae-infused album,’ she explained to Vogue. ‘It’s not gonna be typical of what you know as reggae. But you’re going to feel the elements in all of the tracks.’
