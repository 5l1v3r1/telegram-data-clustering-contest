Andy Murray and Great Britain Davis Cup team quote Frozen in press conference

Leon Smith's men had a plan for their answers as they met the media in Spain and they were in no mood to Let It Go

19 Nov 2019, 16:29 by Neil McLeman, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/neil-mcleman/

Andy Murray and the British team warmed up for the Davis Cup by quoting lyrics from Frozen as they seek their own sequel this weekend.

Dan Evans held back giggles as the double Olympic champion told critics of the revamped competition to “let it go”.

Kyle Edmund , Evans and new boy Neal Skupski all dropped lines but Jamie Murray failed to deliver his quip before facing the Netherlands tomorrow.

British number one Evans said: “We have both had a good week's practice, can't hold it back anymore, and get it going on the match court.” Edmund said: “Fear will be our enemy.”

And Skupski marked his first appearance at a pres conference in a British tracksuit said: “if we do get called upon, we'll just have to be the player we always have to be.”

The older Murray brother was supposed to say: “The cold never bothered me anyway”.

The European premiere of Frozen 2 was in London on Sunday six years after the original films broke all box office records for an animated film.

Great Britain won the Davis Cup in 2015 under Smith and are a good outside bet to win back the trophy in the new week-long format in the Spanish capital this week.

At the 1998 FIFA World Cup in France, England players had bets on who could get most song titles into interviews.

Asked about the England training camp, Gareth Southgate told Bob Wilson: “It's not exactly Club Tropicana, Bob. You are not going to get any Careless Whispers from me about the team.”

Alan Shearer said: “We are not exactly Dancing on the Ceiling.”

Andy Murray said he would be reluctant to play five back-to-back singles this week but reported he had lost weight since piling on 10 pounds after his Antwerp win last month.

"I've lost a little bit of weight - not as much as I would like. But obviously getting to practice here and hopefully getting to play a few matches can also help with the last couple of kilos.

“Physically, my elbow's been really good so that's been positive. And then, yeah, I mean, obviously, if you're playing matches, there's obviously a bit of added intensity there.

"So how you recover from them and feel after them is slightly different to the practice. But the elbow has been good.”
