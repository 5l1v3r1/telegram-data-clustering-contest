Charge laid in Tuesday morning collision

It happened shortly after 5:30 at Homer Watson and Conestoga College Boulevard

19 Nov 2019, 16:05 by KitchenerToday Staff

NEWS RELEASE
WATERLOO REGIONAL POLICE
*************************
On November 19, 2019, at approximately 5:35 a.m., Waterloo Regional Police responded to the intersection of Homer Watson Boulevard and Conestoga College Boulevard in Kitchener, for a collision involving two Tractor Trailers trucks. 

A 26-year-old male driver from Oshawa was driving a 2019 International Tractor Trailer, travelling north on Homer Watson Boulevard when he made a left turn onto Conestoga College Boulevard. 

A 40-year-old Cambridge male was driving a 2020 Volvo Tractor-trailer, travelling south on Homer Watson Boulevard. 

The 40-year-old driver was unable to stop and struck the Tractor Trailer turning left, causing one of the trailers to dislodge from the truck. 

The trailer came to rest blocking all southbound lanes of Homer Watson Boulevard. The intersection was closed for several hours while investigators remained on scene.

There were no injuries as a result of the collision. 

The investigation is ongoing. 

Anyone who witnessed the collision is asked to contact the Waterloo Regional Police Service's Traffic Services Unit. 

As a result of the investigation, the 26-year-old male driver was charged with making an Improper Turn.
