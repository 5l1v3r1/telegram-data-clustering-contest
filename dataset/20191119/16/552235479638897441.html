Pots of local talent on show at Perthshire design fair

Annual market held in November

19 Nov 2019, 16:48 by Rachel Clark, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/rachel-clark/

Makers and artists from across Perthshire are showing their wares at a design market.

Perthshire Creates’ annual Christmas Design Market will return to the Civic Hall in Perth’s 2 High Street on Saturday, November 23 and Sunday, November 24.

Over 50 different makers will have stalls, including 24 brand new artists.

The work on sale is hugely varied, featuring original designs and handcrafts, helping to keep Christmas shopping local and support independent makers in the region.

Jenna McDonald, a jeweller from Crieff, has been taking part in the Christmas Design Market since it began in 2015.

She said: “It’s a joy to exhibit in my home town alongside so many talented local artisans.

“As a maker, I love having the opportunity to get out of my studio to meet new and existing customers, chatting about my jewellery making workshops (which I will be hosting from my new studio space within the Perth Creative Exchange from early next year), and catching up with other stallholders.”

There will be a range of crafts on offer at the market, including glass, prints, textiles, paintings, ceramics, woodwork and contemporary crafts.

Perth-based jewellery Diana King said: “As a stallholder with the Perthshire Creates Design Market, I can rest assured that the overall feel of the design market is one of the quality products lovingly handmade to the highest standard.

“The location of the Civic Hall is a beautiful space to set up the market, it has great natural and artificial lighting to enhance displays and plenty of room for visitors to browse and stroll around.

“It is perfect for welcoming new and existing customers visiting the city centre and enjoying the market’s riverside location.

“I love to use markets and fairs to meet my customers, bringing the shopping experience back to how it should be, face-to-face with a person.”

John Bishop from The Grey Earl in Comrie is exhibiting at the design market for the first time this year.

He said: “I’m hugely excited. Having met some of the other makers and artists throughout the year, I am excited to be sharing a platform with some really talented creatives.

“It feels like a very supportive creative community which the Perthshire Creates team represent, all coming together at this year’s Christmas Design Market.

“Also, it’s an opportunity for me to do some festive shopping whilst supporting other local independent businesses.”

The Christmas Design Market will be open from 10am until 4.30pm on both Saturday, November 23 and Sunday, November 24 in the Civic Hall.

Entry is free, although there is a suggested donation of £1 to Culture Perth and Kinross, the creative organisation behind the region’s museums, galleries and libraries.
