Get on your bike 'n' ride - Perthshire woman writes book about biking across North America

Lynn hopes book will inspire other women

19 Nov 2019, 16:16 by Rachel Clark, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/rachel-clark/

A Perthshire woman is hoping to encourage others to follow their dreams, after motorcycling across North America.

Lynn Aitken (58) from Almondbank only got her motorcycle licence at the age of 49, and wanted to go on an epic adventure.

In two parts, Lynn and her husband Mike took on the trip of a lifetime by traversing from Mexico, travelling through the US and Canada, and finishing up in Alaska.

She said: “I only got my motorbike licence at 49.

“My husband and I were on holiday in Canada and we saw a signpost for the scenic route to Alaska and we both said ‘one day we will have to go down that road’.

“It took a couple of years of planning and saving up, but in 2016 we headed out to Mexico.

“When it came to saying goodbye to my kids, that was the biggest step for me to leave my family.

“It was not just a two week all-inclusive vacation, we were literally riding off and taking on this big challenge.

“It was a bit of a journey of discovery for me.

“At first I was absolutely terrified and when we landed I went straight to the departures board to try and go back home.

“I still don’t know why I did it.”

The pair travelled all the way to Canada, and had almost made it into Alaska when disaster struck back home.

Due to a family crisis back in Perthshire, they had to cut their journey short - but they were determined to finish the challenge, and last year went out and completed the leg of Alaska.

Lynn said: “When we were on our first trip, we got about 20 miles short of Alaska and we had to turn around and come back home.

“We headed down to Vancouver as quickly as we could.

“I knew we needed to finish the journey, so we went about saving up to get back out there again.

“It was niggling at me, I had to finish what we started.

“All in all it took about six months to do.”

She continued: “My favourite bit was meeting all the incredible people along the way.

“They were all so friendly and we have made lifelong friends, we met some of the most amazing people.

“We tried to do mostly camping, but when we started the first leg it was early in the season and a lot of the campsites weren’t open yet, so we stayed in budget hotels as well.

“The second part last year was completely camping.

“The wildlife was amazing as well.

“In all the places we’ve travelled to, you can’t beat Scotland for scenery, but the wildlife in America was amazing.

“We saw bears, moose and there was a lone wolf watching us at one point.

“We went whale watching in a protected area as well and the whales brought their babies up to the boat to show everyone.

“This was in their birthing area, so only small boats were allowed to approach the whales.

“I ended up covered in whale snot, but I didn’t care.”

Since finishing her epic adventure, Lynn has written and published a book on her trip called ‘Long Roads and Lipstick’.

She hopes she can encourage other women to follow their dreams and take a leap into the unknown while they still can.

Lynn explained: “There are not many books written by women, particularly women of my age, on motorcycling.

“It started off just as a thought.

“All along the way I wrote notes most days and then I thought ‘you know what, I will write a book’.

“Everyone knows me for always having lipstick on - if I have my lipstick on, I am ready for it all.

“Other women who write these kinds of books don’t talk about their family, but I am just a normal woman with a family and I want to inspire other people.

“The book is the story of what happened, but also hints and tips on what we found out along the way.

“Anyone can do something like this, but the hardest step is the first step, just going out there and doing it.

“Whatever your dream is, follow it.

“Before I went out there, my husband had loads of books to read.

“I started to read one woman’s book as well and it was a good book, but the woman who wrote it was very gung-ho and ‘I can do this’ whereas I was a bit hesitant.

“I would have loved to have read this book before I went out.

“There are sad bits in it, but funny bits as well that will have people laughing out loud.”

She added: “At first I couldn’t get it published so I have done it myself.

“My daughter said it is perfectly imperfect and now my story is out there.

“The book is now out on Amazon for £8.99 or on Kindle for £2.99.”

Lynn and her husband are now planning to do another trip, and are hoping to go east and motorcycle to Russia.
