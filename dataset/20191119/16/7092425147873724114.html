Troy Parrott back in Ireland U21 squad for Sweden clash

19 Nov 2019, 16:08 by James Cox

Stephen Kenny's Ireland Under-21s have received a huge boost ahead of their clash with Sweden tonight as Troy Parrott has returned to the squad.

Parrott made his senior debut in the 3-1 victory over New Zealand last week but he was an unused substitute for the 1-1 draw with Denmark last night.

This means he's fresh for the crunch clash against Sweden tonight.

Parrott, 17, has scored three goals in three games at Under-21 level.

Two of those goals came in the 3-1 win over tonight's opponents back in September.

Lee O'Connor had already rejoined the squad after making his senior debut against New Zealand.

Ireland's clash with Sweden kicks off at 8pm at Tallaght Stadium, with Kenny's side top of Group 1.

However, they've played two more games than second-placed Italy who are three points behind Ireland on 10.

Sweden are fourth with six points but they've played three fewer games.
