Gigi Masin's new album, Calypso, inspired by the Greek islands

The ambient LP is out February 28th via Apollo.

19 Nov 2019, 16:55 by Carlos Hawthorn

Gigi Masin's new album, Calypso, was inspired by the Greek islands.

Calypso is the unofficial name for Gavdos, the southernmost Greek island, AKA Ogygia in ancient mythology. (In Homer's Odyssey, the nymph Calypso imprisoned Odysseus on Ogygia for seven years.) The 16-track ambient album is out February 28th via R&S subsidiary Apollo.

"Gavdos gives you profound sensations in every corner, as here you can find enchantment, mystery, the getting lost to forget, to erase and drown the memories in a light and salty water," says Masin.

On his production methods, he adds: "[Calypso was made using] the same old software, a simple, sort of a child-proof game from the mid-'90s or what experts call a 'beta.' It's a kind of mixer where you have to insert the music files as they are, naked and simply as recorded. But it works like I have six or eight tape recorders where my sounds are not locked, so my chance of giving them a perfect position isn't always certain. Often it seems the sound found its own way without human logic, and the music appears as a revelation."
