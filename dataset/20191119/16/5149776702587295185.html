Rocky road - Stan Collymore recalls Forest fight after Liverpool and Man City stars clash

Nottingham Forest latest | Collymore gives insight into possible response to England incident with a story from his time as a Forest player

19 Nov 2019, 16:26 by Josh Murray

Stan Collymore has explained how a "training-ground fight" with Alf Inge Haaland during his Nottingham Forest days means he can relate to Raheem Sterling and Joe Gomez after their recent altercation while on England duty.

Manchester City forward Sterling was dropped for the Three Lions' European Championship qualifying win over Montenegro last week "as a result of a disturbance in a private team area".

Gomez was later pictured at training with a small cut under his eye, although it was not confirmed whether that came from the incident.

Former Forest and Liverpool star Collymore has given his response to the England talking point - and suggesting the sort of welcome back into the changing room players can get after similar incidents.

"The day after I knocked out Alf Inge Haaland in a training-ground fight, I walked back into the dressing-room to be greeted by Stuart Pearce and the lads singing the Rocky theme tune," Collymore wrote in his Daily Mirror column.

"I mention this because people will think life will be all dark and sullen for Raheem Sterling after his bust-up with Joe Gomez.

"But, trust me, footballers have a great way of welcoming one of their team-mates back into the group and it’s usually by giving you a good, old sledging.

"Pep Guardiola will perhaps grab Sterling for a quick debrief on the situation and tell him to get his head down, and there’s no better game for him to do that than Saturday’s against a young Chelsea side."

Collymore netted an eye-catching 41 goals in 65 League appearances for Forest between 1993 and 1995, before a big-money move to Anfield.

He won three England caps.
