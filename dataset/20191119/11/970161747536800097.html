Star Wars' Carrie Fisher looked out for Warwick Davis on set

19 Nov 2019, 11:51

Warwick Davis has revealed some of his favourite memories from working on several films in the Star Wars franchise, including the times the late Carrie Fisher looked after him on set.

The actor and presenter, 49, first starred in Return of the Jedi when he was just 11 years old, after his grandmother heard a radio advert calling for people who were less than four feet tall to be in the movie.

He revealed how special it was to work alongside the greats like Mark Hamill and Carrie Fisher as a big fan of Star Wars.

Warwick told Metro.co.uk: ‘I first met them when I was 11. Carrie, Mark Hamill and Harrison Ford were my heroes at that age.

‘I had trouble differentiating between them as the actors and their characters, so Harrison Ford was always Han Solo and Carrie was always Princess Leia, and I couldn’t see them any other way.’

Warwick, who had teamed up with Argos to celebrate the launch of the new video game Star Wars Jedi: Fallen Order, also shared a touching story of how Carrie would look out for him on set.

He explained: ‘She was such a lovely lady, it’s so sad she’s no longer with us. I enjoyed working with her and she was so lovely to me when I was in my Ewok costume.
