Big cat fear after foal found dead in field

The horse appears to have been partially eaten

19 Nov 2019, 11:50 by Neil Shaw

Experts fear a big cat could be on the loose after a foal was found dead in a field - with a huge wound in his stomach.

A ferocious feline may be responsible for killing the colt and disembowelling it, after he was found dead in a field in Kent with a giant hole in its stomach.

The young horse was found lying on his side in his field in Greenhithe last week.

The foal was seen alive the day before when he was in good health.

Rik Snook, formerly of Big Cats of Britain, has a long history of tracking big cats across Kent and Sussex.

He says the foal was probably killed and eaten by a big cat, but would not like to speculate which type.

Rik said: “It is definitely possible a big cat is responsible.

“There have been reported sightings in the area.

“It is pretty common a big cat would want to go after a young horse. It would be the upper size of what the cat would go after, but it’s definitely not unusual.”

The RSPCA was alerted after a four-month-old piebald foal was found gutted in a field in Greenhithe on November 10.

RSPCA inspector Kirsten Ormerod said: “There was no obvious signs of ill health from what I could see looked in good body condition.

“It is possible he had died from natural causes and then unfortunately been scavenged on but his owner is understandably concerned so we are appealing for anyone with information to come forward.

“We urge anyone with information to contact our appeals line in complete confidence.”

Many believe Britain has an elusive population of wild big cats, with many people reporting sightings in rural areas.

Often described as panthers or pumas, others are unconvinced with many sightings often turning out to be misidentified large domestic cats.

However on several occasions, genuine big cats have been captured and identified in Britain.

Sceptics say these were likely animals that had been kept illegally and escaped, or even released after the animals became too difficult to manage.
