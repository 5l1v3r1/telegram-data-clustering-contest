Zoe Ball ‘splits from boyfriend Michael Reed’ after nearly two years

19 Nov 2019, 11:22

Zoe Ball has split from her boyfriend Michael Reed after nearly two years together, according to reports.

It marked the first public relationship the Radio 2 presenter had since the tragic suicide of her boyfriend Billy Yates in 2017.

According to sources, Zoe called time on the romance with construction boss Michael amid claims she was ‘growing uncomfortable’ with claims he was using her fame for his own benefit.

‘Zoe knew deep down the relationship wasn’t going anywhere,’ a friend told The Sun.

‘She enjoyed her time with Michael but came to the conclusion he wasn’t a keeper, despite him believing they were getting serious.

‘There were certain things she grew uncomfortable with, such as him wanting to become a model and act. Her close circle feared he was using her to enhance his own career.’

The relationship followed the tragic death of Zoe’s former partner Billy, who died by suicide two years ago following a long battle with depression. She had previously been married to Fatboy Slim Norman Cook, with whom she shares two children, Woody and Nelly.

When it comes to her split with Michael however, according to a source close to the Strictly It Takes Two presenter, all was amicable – things just didn’t work out.

‘They have split but it is very amicable,’ the insider told the publication.

‘With all Zoe’s had going on with Billy, work and Norman she’s had so much to deal with and sadly a new relationship was too much. It wasn’t possible.

‘They remain friends but she’s happily single and focusing on her kids and work.’

In September Zoe paid tribute to Billy to mark his birthday.

She posted a snap of Billy lighting a fire lantern to her Instagram page, admitting that his ‘star still shines the brightest up there’.

‘Beautiful full moon for your birthday eve Billface. Your star still shines the brightest up there,’ she said. ‘Fly forever free. Always in this old ♥ of mine.’

The 48-year-old had previously spoken about her grief during her radio show, while reaching out to listeners going through similar situations.

‘So tomorrow it’s going to be two-years since our dear Billy Yates died,’ she said.

‘Anyone who has lost someone dear to them, be it a friend or a family member, will know that these anniversaries have a tendency to make your knees wobble and your heart hurt.

‘I just wanted to reach out with thoughts and an extra dollop of love to anyone going through anything at the moment, whether it be mental health problems, perhaps a loved one is poorly or maybe you’re just having a right old tricky time of it at the moment.’

Metro.co.uk contacted Zoe’s reps for comment.
