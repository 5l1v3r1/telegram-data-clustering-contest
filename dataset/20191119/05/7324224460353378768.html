Wolverhampton's Pluviam drop single and debut EP

Wolverhampton's Pluviam are back with a new single and their debut EP.

19 Nov 2019, 05:00 by Leigh Sanders

The track Minimalism and EP The Rain were released on November 8 and follows on from their debut track Searching this summer.

They are available to hear on their Spotify and Soundcloud pages.

Pluviam formed in February 2018 when vocalist James Riley and drummer Mike Tabone which led them to discover their signature sound which mixes metal with indie rock and modern experimental music. It wasn’t long before the duo became a band, when they were joined by guitarist Tom Boddison and bassist Calvert Stevens.

Since then, their growing reputation has seen them enjoy radio play from Janice Long on BBC Radio Wales, as well as features on many online magazines including Loud Horizon, who described the band’s sound as "combining the vocal style of Tom Yorke with a heavy raucous Linkin Park-inspired backing".
