Scots travel chiefs took 900 domestic flights in five years despite SNP climate crusade

19 Nov 2019, 00:46 by Christine Lavelle

TRAVEL chiefs came in for flak after it emerged officials took 900 domestic flights in five years — despite the SNP’s green crusade.

Figures show staff at government agency Transport Scotland have flown around the UK 872 times since 2014.

Tory shadow environment secretary Maurice Golden branded the official stats “embarrassing”

He added: “It goes to show the SNP likes talking about a climate emergency, but has no intention of acting on one.”

And Lib Dem transport spokesman Mike Rumbles said: “News of a climate emergency seems not to have reached Transport Scotland.

“Every public body should be working out how they can reduce their impact on the planet.”

The row comes after Nats vowed to cut Scotland’s carbon emissions to net-zero by 2045.

And First Minister Nicola Sturgeon also declared a “climate emergency” back in April.

The SNP Government’s sustainable travel strategy says flying should be a “last resort” for staff due to the high emissions.

Earlier this year the Scottish Government u-turned on a 2016 SNP manifesto pledge to cut and eventually scrap air passenger tax.

It said the policy was “no longer compatible” with its “more ambitious” climate change targets.

A Transport Scotland spokesman said: “Any business flights taken by ministers or employees are offset by a carbon levy.

“The levy collects £40,000 per year, and is spent in a transparent way to deliver value and compensate emissions generated.”
