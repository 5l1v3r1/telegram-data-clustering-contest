‘The Red Lantern’ Looks Certain To Maintain Xbox Game Pass’ Momentum In 2020

19 Nov 2019 by Matt Gardner

Xbox Game Pass subscribers have been given yet another reason to bless their investment. The Red Lantern–an upcoming dog-sledding, rogue-lite narrative-driven first-person adventure–is dropping straight onto the service on the day it’s released.In the Timberline Studio-produced game, which was announced earlier this year and has been scheduled for early 2020, was this month confirmed as the newest addition to Game Pass' future line-up. In it, you're put in the shoes of The Musher as she and her team of five sled dogs navigate the Alaskan wilderness in order to find their way home.

The protagonist, voiced by the incredible Ashly Burch–best known for Horizon Zero Dawn’s Aloy, Borderlands’ Tiny Tina and, most recently, the fantastic Parvati Holcomb from The Outer Worlds–initially sets out to train for the world-famous Iditarod race, only to get lost, turning an initially exciting experience into a matter of life and death.

With a firm focus on story, The Red Lantern places a massive focus on “hundreds of different events” that can occur, including “fending off bears, resisting frostbite, attending your dogs, or receiving a signature moose-licking.” You can choose how you interact with the world around you, whether you’re looking to hunt everything on your route with a trusty firearm, or take a more tempered approach and conserve your energy.

The game’s preview content unsurprisingly highlights the bond between the player and their five dogs. Given that huskies can be infamously aggressive with even long-term owners, it’ll be interesting to see if neglect of ownership duties could also be a potential danger in the game, or if Timberline has created a softer bond between gamers and their canine companions.

Alongside the Xbox One, The Red Lantern is also coming to the Nintendo Switch, where the game is given gyro controls in handheld mode, letting players look around at the exciting environments by turning the system.

If it’s as good as it looks, The Red Lantern could be a signal that Microsoft is planning to maintain Game Pass’ impressive momentum and continuing to provide a platform for unique, brilliant and otherwise-overlooked titles. In 2019 alone, popular indie games made available upon release to subscribers include Void Bastards, Creature in the Well, Descenders, For the King and Felix the Reaper.
