The Crown's Tobias Menzies is happy to be paid less than Olivia Colman

19 Nov 2019, 00:10

The Crown’s Tobias Menzies confessed he gets paid less than Olivia Colman and is happy about it considering Claire Foy and Matt Smith’s pay gap scandal.

Tobias, 45, understood that Olivia is ‘much more established’ than him but found it ‘hard to swallow’ when he discovered lead star Claire Foy was earning quite a bit less than Matt Smith.

Speaking to Harper’s Bazaar UK, the Game of Thrones star said: ‘It’s an area that, as you may appreciate, I’m wary to step into. I think broadly speaking, yes, greater transparency will lead to greater equality and that is absolutely the direction of travel that I think is necessary and good.

‘It’s interesting… my understanding is, that this time round, I’m paid less than Olivia. Of course I think that feels proportionate because Olivia’s much more established than me and has a much higher profile that she’s bringing to the show and the product.’

Tobias continued: ‘There are complexities within it that are sometimes hard to articulate. Someone’s not always coming in with the exact same amount of previous work, [but] the optics of that particular thing were hard to swallow because obviously Claire was doing the larger part. They got there in the end I suppose.’

Claire, who plays a younger Queen Elizabeth II, earned £29,000 per hour-long episode while her more famous counterpart Matt allegedly earned an extra £10,000 or so more.

However, Netflix apparently agreed to backpay Claire £200,000 after the scandal went viral, but the actress denies the report.

Tobias replaced Matt as Prince Phillip opposite Oscar-winner Olivia as the new Queen, and producer Mackie stated that ‘going forward, no one gets paid more than the Queen.’

The star also revealed that he was third-in-line for the role of Prince Philip in Season Three.

Outlander star Tobias was left with some pretty big shoes to fill as he joined the franchise.

Netflix bosses originally had their eye on both Kingsman’s Mark Strong and Avengers Endgame star Paul Bettany for the role.

‘Initially, I think they went after Mark Strong, then Paul Bettany, and then it came back around to me,’ Tobias told The Sunday Times Magazine.
