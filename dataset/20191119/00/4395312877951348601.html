The Art of Letting go

We have to have the strength to let go of friendships that no longer serve our greater purpose...

19 Nov 2019 by CK Prenci

Learning to let go of friendships that no longer serve our greater purpose…

The greatest step towards a life of simplicity is to learn to let goSteve Maraboli

My life has always been a series of “letting go”. I can recall a time where practicing letting go changed the trajectory of my life. I had made the decision at age 31 to move to London and get my Masters.The decision was quite scary and with it came many rejections from those around me. In this moment I had to let go a great toxicity that I allowed to control my life for so long. I had to learn to let go of the opinions of others, and what they thought of my life choices.

 I’ve always viewed London as being the door opener in which I find my confidence, my voice. A voice that grew stronger and louder over time. Learning to say yes to myself and no to others was a huge moment in my life.

I am a firm believer that letting go affords us a new beginning, away of leaving behind bad behaviors perhaps of our own fruition, and/or walking away from toxic relationships that have held us back from becoming the best version of ourselves.

With that said, there comes a time to let go of relationships in our lives.

Fast-forward to the present where I’ve now found myself at a place where I had to make a decision, a not so easy decision… letting go of a friendship. This friend has been apart of my life for over a decade. In this case, a major life change, an awakening, and avoidable truths all surfaced at once. In a split moments time whether I was ready or not, life’s signal insisted it was now time to let go. My aspirations and life path had changed, I was no longer the same person, I knew this would require adjustments in certain areas of my life.

Sometimes connections and friendships no longer serve who we’ve become, and it can be a double-edged sword, when we begin to change, our purpose and life may no longer serve our friend either. No longer being on the same page; we could possibly be doing a disservice to them or to each other. As difficult as it is to accept such truths, it is even harder to make the decision to no longer carry on. A spiritual companion of mine led me to such a beautiful revelation just recently. She said, “ Someone doesn’t have to be a bad person for you to move on from them, and do not look for reasons to vilify them either. She said, “ Look at it this way, it is like looking into your wardrobe to discover that the things hanging in your closet are no longer your style, your taste.” This can happen in friendships, when life or circumstances change in our lives…. some friendships may no longer fit into our life, and that’s ok.

 Is there an easy way to end such relationships, no, but there is a right way. It is never ok to make the person feel worthless, or belittle, or that they are responsible for our bad decision making.even if the person participated in certain behaviors or decision making with you, do not look down upon them in order to feel better about yourself. Also, if you truly care or love the person, be honest, no matter how hard it may be, have “that talk”, share your feelings not in a condemning manner, but in a manner where an open discussion is able to occur. Again, this is never an easy process. Some may abruptly end leaving someone confused or heartbroken, and some may end with a big blow out of some sort. And as cruel as it seems, some friendships are left with no goodbyes. However it may end, again it is not easy.

Let it die…

Let it die, because perhaps a higher power is putting you on notice, that the best version of yourself is ready to come forth, ready to be birthed in its place. And if that’s the case, learn to embrace the letting go fully. Allow yourself time to heal. The best thing you can do is part ways keeping the truth of the best memories you shared. We must forgive. Forgive them, because when you forgive them you heal, and more importantly forgive yourself, and forgive what might of went wrong.

Lastly, just know in time you will see the beauty of the moment you chose to let go and move on. As you walk into the new dimensions of your new found self- awareness and being…you leave room for more suitable relationships to enter your life, for where you are now and where you are going. Letting go isn’t easy, but it will be the greatest path to living a free and fulfilling life.
