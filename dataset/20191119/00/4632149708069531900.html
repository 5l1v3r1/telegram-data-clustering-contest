Things to do today in Bengaluru

19 Nov 2019, 00:30 by Bangalore Mirror Bureau

A play on Indira Gandhi
This play by Sarvam theatre came under the attack of right-wing outfits and had to be cancelled early this year. It’s back tonight, albeit with a few self-censored changes such as the disclaimer: “Not a political statement’. Called My Wife Mrs Gandhi, it’s the first Kannada play on prime minister Indira Gandhi and is told from the point of view of her husband Feroze Gandhi. Written and directed by Divya Karanth, the play is set from 1965 to 1984, tangling between the present and past scenarios of India’s politics.

WHERE: KH Kalasoudha, Hanumantha Nagar
WHEN: 7.30pm, November 19
ENTRY: `150, bookmyshow.com

A show of energy
Who’s not intrigued by the forces of universe that define our lives? In an exhibition, artist Nita GK has put up paintings that reflect her strong faith in the law of karma and destiny. Each of her pieces, she says, is a manifestation of the surging energies that lie within her.

WHERE: Fidelitus Gallery, Brigade Software Park, Banashankari Stage II
WHEN: 10am-7pm, on view till November 24
CALL: 68073700

A day for men
Prepare yourself for a special edition of ‘Poetry from the Heart’, as it is dedicated to ‘International Men’s Day’. On the lineup are 11 prolific male poets, namely Pramod Sankar, Vijay Karthik, Pradeep Dharmapalan, Navoneil Bhattacharyya, Malcolm Carvalho, Nithin Jacob, Mehul Jain, Stephen Deepak, Samantak Bhadra, Prashant Sankaran and Jeremy Solomon. They will throw light on men’s health, the need to improve gender equality in society, and highlight male role models.

WHERE: Urban Solace Café, 32 Annaswamy Mudaliar Road, opposite Ulsoor Lake
WHEN: 8pm-9.30pm, November 19
ENTRY: `249, at the venue
CALL: 9945022177

Laugh out loud
It’s just Tuesday and you are tired of the week already?
Don’t worry. Chill out at standup comic Karunesh Talwar’s gig tonight. He is a young comedian and writer, who made his way into the local comedy scene by winning several open-mic competitions, including the Comedy Store’s RAW.

WHERE: That Comedy Club, 5th Block, Koramangala
WHEN: 9.45pm-10.45pm, November 19
ENTRY: `826, bookmyshow.com

Jokes in progress
If you want to see how a joke is developed and how a standup comic wings it when he or she forgets his or her joke in between the performance, then grab this opportunity. Make your Tuesday happening with some upcoming artists such as Harman Preet Singh, Gaurav Purohit, Vijeth VS, Hari Krishna and Neelaksh Mathur.

WHERE: Woke, 2nd Cross Road, Indiranagar
WHEN: 8pm, November 19
ENTRY: `99, bookmyshow.com

Revenge time
Follow the story of Riyaz in a play called The Edge Of Heaven. In a fit of rage during a football match, he shoots a boy called Ayidh. Just when he is about to be presented for his public lynching, Ayidh’s mother is asked if she would forgive Riyaz. What follows next, will make you think about what it means to neutralise the essence of revenge, rage and retribution.

WHERE: Ranga Shankara, 8th Cross Road, JP Nagar
WHEN: 7.30pm-8.50pm, November 19
ENTRY: `200, bookmyshow.com

Girls, go party
DJ Nash and DJ Raghu Achar will spin your Tuesday night into a fun one. At this Ladies Night party, they will churn out hit tracks of the season. So call up your friends and be there to let your hair down.

WHERE: The Leela Palace, 23, HAL Old Airport Road, Kodihalli
WHEN: 9pm onwards, November 19
CALL: 8884285111

Museum watch
Immerse yourself in India’s rich music heritage at this interactive museum. With themes ranging from the legends of Bollywood to folk music, to classical maestros and indie rock bands, it’s got everything under one roof.

WHERE: Indian Music Experience, Brigade Millenium Road, JP Nagar 7th Phase WHEN: 10am-6pm, November 19 ENTRY: `150, bookmyshow.com

Health first
Eating healthy is all the rage today and for good reason. So Dr Neerja Hajela, head of science and regulatory affairs at Yakult, will discuss the importance of a healthy digestive system and how probiotics help.

WHERE: Tinystep, AVS Compound, 4th Block, Koramangala
WHEN: 3pm-4pm, November 19
CALL: 7760504608

Big canvas
Ode To Krishna is an exhibition of Pichwai paintings, which are made to narrate tales of Lord Krishna.

WHERE: Art Cafe, Sheraton Grand at Brigade Gateway
WHEN: 11am onwards, November 19
CALL: 9972793154

Rock on
This Thursday, Rachna Ramdin and Joshua Paulmer will come together to croon some iconic rock and pop tunes for you. Ramdin
is a vocalist, born and brought up in Mauritius. She has been singing since the age of 10, and hails from a family of musicians, who sing in English and French. While she might be new to Bengaluru, she has already done a number of acoustic gigs in the city. As for Paulmer, he has been instrumental in innovating with new styles of music, and covering different genres. His repertoire has more than 600 songs, ranging from the 60s to now.

WHERE: 1522 the Pub, opposite Sindhoor Convention Centre, 6th Phase, 100 Feet Road, JP Nagar WHEN: 8pm, November 21 CALL: 7829118909
