Latvian government decides to divert hospitals’ profits for infrastructure development

19 Nov 2019 by BNN

The government has decided to allow capital associations in which Healthcare Ministry is a shareholder to divert clean profits and dividends for 2018 towards improvement of infrastructure, as confirmed by Healthcare Ministry.

The ministry explains this step will allow certain hospitals to improve their environment’s accessibility, develop services and help resolve infrastructure-related matters.

Aknīste Psychoneurological Hospital will be able to divert profits of EUR 1 377 from 2018 towards repairs of its boiler room to ensure unhindered economic operations of the hospital.

Daugavpils Psychoneurological Hospital’s profits of EUR 2 134 will be diverted towards development and expansion of accessibility of rehabilitation services.

Ģintermuiža Hospital will be able to divert its profits of EUR 352 towards procurement of a gas-powered boiler (total costs planned at EUR 45 000), considering the old boiler is too used up.

Piejūra Hospital will be able to diver its profits of EUR 4 512 towards the project Successful Psychological and Sensory Rehabilitation for Children and Adults Suffering from Mental and Behavioural Disorders to help improve service quality.

National Rehabilitation Centre Vaivari will use its profits of EUR 3 859 to help restore walkways on its territory (total costs estimated at EUR 4 000) to help improve environmental accessibility for patients.

Considering limited state budget availability for healthcare, diverting profits from Healthcare Ministry’s capital associations towards infrastructure and service development, which is a normal practice supported by the government, the ministry reminds.
