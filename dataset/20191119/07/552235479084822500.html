Blantyre woman stabbed her partner

The accused phoned the police and confessed to what she had done.

19 Nov 2019, 07:00 by Hamilton Advertiser, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/hamilton-advertiser/

A Blantyre woman stabbed her abusive partner then phoned the police and confessed what she’d done.

Lorraine Hatcher, 52, of Alpine Path, attacked Steven Roberts after a drinking session that went on into the early morning.

Hatcher appeared at Hamilton Sheriff Court on Friday and admitted stabbing her partner twice on the leg to his severe injury at her home on April 6.

Scott King, prosecuting, said the couple had been in an on-off relationship for two or three years.

There was a background of domestic abuse and Roberts had been convicted of offences against Hatcher.

Mr King told the court: “On April 5 the couple were drinking together. This went on until 7.30 the following morning and both were under the influence.

“There was then a heated argument and the accused took possession of a kitchen knife and stabbed Roberts twice on the left thigh.

“This caused cuts which were about an inch wide.

“The accused then dialled 101 and told police she stabbed ‘Stevie’ because he had tried to attack her.

“Officers arrived and found Hatcher agitated and upset. She told them repeatedly that she had stabbed her partner.

“Roberts was taken to Hairmyres Hospital where three staples were put in each wound.”

Sheriff Thomas Millar called for background reports and deferred sentence until December 16.

Hatcher had her bail continued.
