VACSWIM launches Summer 2020 program to teach 12,000 SA students how to swim

19 Nov 2019, 07:48

Summer is fast approaching, and VACSWIM has 12,000 students to teach. But they’ll have some help from our own Olympic swimmer Kyle Chalmers.

VACSWIM launched its summer campaign on Tuesday, with Olympic gold medallist Kyle Chalmers as its new ambassador.

The annual program will teach water safety to 12,000 primary school-aged children at 120 beaches and pools over the summer school holidays.

Learning to swim is just not an option for young South Australians, Chalmers said.

“As an Aussie kid it’s important to be confident around water,” he said.

“If being a part of this program can help prevent drownings and encourage kids to get out, safely and have fun in the water, then that’s a wonderful thing.”

Brianna McLay, 8, has done VACSWIM and enjoyed demonstrating water safety yesterday when she “rescued” her brother, carefully going through the process.

“I really like going in the water,” she said.
