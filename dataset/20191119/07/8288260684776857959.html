Leeds United sent Aston Villa transfer warning as Arsenal agent drops Lucas Torreira bombshell - Premier League rumours

Premier League transfer rumours | With the January transfer window re-opening in the New Year Dean Smith will be keeping tabs on what deals his side could complete

19 Nov 2019, 07:26 by Richard Cusack

The January transfer window is nearing with the one-month period offering a timely chance for clubs to beef up their squad as they bid to keep their season afloat.

With that in mind, here are all the latest transfer rumours surrounding Aston Villa and their rivals...

Leeds sent Villa warning

Leeds United have been warned to expect ‘vultures to come swooping’ for Kalvin Phillips once the transfer window reopens.

The midfielder was a summer target for Villa after Dean Smith’s side achieved promotion.

With Phillips having excelled in his debut campaign under Marcelo Bielsa – earning himself a  place in the Championship’s team of the year – Villa were among a number of admirers from the Premier League.

But they couldn’t agree a fee with Leeds to take the 23-year-old to Villa Park and looked elsewhere.

Everton fans on Morelos

Everton fans have had their say after the Toffees were urged to sign Villa-linked striker Alfredo Morelos.

Villa have been claimed to hold an interest in the Rangers forward who is enjoying the most prolific campaign of his career at Ibrox.

He’s scored 22 times this season in just 26 appearances, leading many to tip the Colombia international for a move to the Premier League.

Dean Smith has hinted Villa will considering adding to their options upfront when the transfer window reopens in January but Darren Bent believes it’s Everton who should swoop for Morelos.

Championship trial

Villa are reportedly one of a number of clubs chasing highly rated Carlisle United defender Jarrad Branthwaite.

The 17-year-old has only this season broken into the first team at Brunton Park, but is already turning heads with a number of impressive performances.

According to Football Insider, Villa, along with several other top flight clubs have been monitoring Branthwaite ahead of a potential move in the January window.

The central defender has started the last five League Two games and was even given the honour of the captain's armband in a recent league fixture.

Read more HERE .

Man United drop transfer hint

Ed Woodward says Manchester United have a ‘clear vision’ for recruitment – and hinted the club are ready to spend amid reports linking them with Villa midfielder John McGinn.

United were first credited with an interest in McGinn in the summer transfer window after the Scotland international played a key role in Villa’s promotion to the Premier League.

The 25-year-old signed a new five-year deal at Villa Park ahead of the first game of the season but it is understood United have continued to monitor his performances.

Old Trafford executive vice-chairman Woodward addressed the New York Stock Exchange to inform shareholders of results from the first quarter of the financial year.

Arsenal agent drops bombshell

Lucas Torreira's agent has declared that the Arsenal midfielder is "no longer at ease" under Unai Emery, putting his long-term Emirates future into doubt.

The Uruguay international was signed from Sampdoria in a £26million deal in 2018, and was seen as something of a coup after impressing at the World Cup.

But after being a regular last term, he hasn’t been first choice for Emery so far in 2019-20.

Torreira, 23, has often seen Matteo Guendouzi, Dani Ceballos and Joe Willock preferred by the Spaniard, as well as former skipper Granit Xhaka.

Everton transfer claim made

Former Everton director of football Steve Walsh has revealed the Toffees rejected to sign Harry Maguire and Andy Robertson during his tenure - for a combined fee of £20million.

Maguire became the most expensive defender in history when Manchester United paid £80million to sign him from Leicester over the summer.

Robertson has gone on to establish himself as one of the world's premier full-backs since joining Liverpool from Hull and helped the Reds to a sixth European crown last term.

And Walsh claims - while the duo were both at Hull - he offered Everton the chance to sign the pair - but the Toffees hierarchy decided against the move.

Man City to hijack Arsenal move

Manchester City are weighing up a bid for Red Bull Leipzig defender Dayot Upamecano who was considered by Arsenal in the summer.

The Premier League champions are struggling in defence having lost Aymeric Laporte until the New Year.

Pep Guardiola's cover has not impressed with John Stones and Nicolas Otamendi both making errors which could push him into the transfer market.

The Catalan has said he won't buy in January, but should injuries force his hand he wants to sign players who can benefit the club beyond his tenure.
