WANTED MAN

Swansea and Stoke City want Celtic ace Jonny Hayes as Parkhead contract comes to an end

19 Nov 2019, 07:37 by Alan Potts

SWANSEA are set to make a move to lure Jonny Hayes from Celtic.

But SunSport understands Championship rivals Stoke City could foil the Swans’ pursuit of the Republic of Ireland international, who is out of contract at the end of the season.

Celtic don’t want to lose the 32-year-old utility man and are looking for him to commit his future to Parkhead.

But the clock is ticking for the Hoops as Hayes’ representatives will be free to open talks with other clubs in the New Year.

There’s also the prospect that Celtic could lose Hayes for nothing in the summer if he were to agree a pre-contract switch.

New Stoke manager Michael O’Neill is keen to tempt Hayes, who started his career at Reading before joining Leicester in 2007, back down south.

He also had loan spells at Northampton and then Cheltenham Town before he headed north to sign for Inverness Caley Thistle in 2009 and Aberdeen in 2012.

After five years at Pittodrie, where has was a League Cup winner in 2014, he was signed for Celtic by Brendan Rodgers.

Hayes has made 16 appearances for Celts this season and scored in September’s 2-0 win over Rangers at Ibrox.
