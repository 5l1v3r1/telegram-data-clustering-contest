Jane McDonald's stunned response at becoming Adele Roberts' luxury item on I'm A Celeb

The Radio 1 DJ picked a photo of the Loose Women star to be her luxury item during her stint in the jungle

19 Nov 2019, 07:02 by Lucy Needham, https://www.facebook.com/dailymirror/

Jane McDonald has responded after she became the unlikely inspiration for Adele Roberts' luxury item on this year's I'm A Celebrity.

On Tuesday night's episode, the famous faces in the main camp were tasked with retrieving the goods they selected prior to show over a pond - with nothing but oars to help them.

As they tried to grab each parcel in turn the items each celebrity had picked to bring them comfort for the foreseeable future were revealed.

Kate Garraway chose concealer, James Haskell an orthopaedic pillow and Roman Kemp plumped for a mug with a picture of his family on it.

Radio 1 DJ Adele plumped for something a little more bizarre.

She told her fellow campmates that she'd selected a picture of Loose Women star and cruise ship legend Jane McDonald to bring her support during her stint Down Under.

Adele said she was huge fan of Jane's work and after the photo was successfully obtained encouraged her pals to 'give Jane a rub for luck.'

Jane has now responded after her surprise cameo in the jungle.

Taking to Twitter , Jane wrote: "Absolutely thrilled to see my picture in the jungle. Adele Roberts I flipping love you," followed by a red love heart.

She then revealed that she'd be supporting her fellow Notherner in the jungle, "Best of luck," added Jane.

Adele's pick had come as a surprise to her campmates and viewers at home with celebs usually opting for something practical to make their time in the jungle that little bit easier.

The broadcaster was one of only two celebs to receive their goodies from home - with Kate Garraway also getting her hands on her make-up - after the team failed miserably at their attempts to save the luxury items before they sank.

The celebs in the spin off camp, who have been living on basic rations, were particularly upset to see hear of the watery demise of their home comforts.
