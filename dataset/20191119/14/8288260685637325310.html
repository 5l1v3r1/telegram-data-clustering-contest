Schoolboy, 13, arrested after suspect stolen Ford Fiesta flips during police chase in Oldbury

The motor lost control and flipped on its side near to Birchley Island, Oldbury

19 Nov 2019, 14:49 by Jordan Coussins, https://www.birminghammail.co.uk/authors/jordan-coussins/

A schoolboy has been arrested after a suspected stolen car flipped on its side after a police pursuit in Oldbury.

West Midlands Police told Black Country Live that officers on patrol spotted the Ford Fiesta on Wolverhampton Road at 2.15am on Monday, November 18.

But following a short pursuit, which was spotted by locals, the motor lost control and flipped on its side near to Birchley Island.

Two teenagers were arrested at the scene; a 13-year-old boy was arrested on suspicion of burglary and has been released while enquiries continue.

A 16-year-old boy, who can’t be named for legal reasons, was charged with burglary and driving offences and will appear before Birmingham Magistrates’ Court today (November 19).

A spokesman for the force added: “Officers on patrol spotted a suspected stolen car on the Wolverhampton Road, Oldbury at 2.15am yesterday morning (18 November).

“Following a short pursuit, the Ford Fiesta lost control and collided near to the Birchley Island.

 

“Two teenagers were arrested at the scene; a 13-year-old boy was arrested on suspicion of burglary and has been released while enquiries continue.

“A 16-year-old boy, who can’t be named for legal reasons, was charged with burglary and driving offences. He is due to appear before Birmingham Magistrates’ Court today (November 19).”
