Top stories of the day: Rajya Sabha passes Jallianwala Bagh National Memorial (Amendment) Bill, FIR registered in connection with protest march at JNU, and more

The major news headlines of the day, and more.

19 Nov 2019, 14:05 by The Hindu Net Desk

Rajya Sabha passes the Jallianwala Bagh National Memorial (Amendment), Bill

On day two of the winter session of the Parliament, the Rajya Sabha  discussed the Surrogacy (Regulation) Bill, 2019. It passed the Jallianwala Bagh National Memorial (Amendment), Bill, 2019. Here is a recap of the day's discussions.

FIR registered in connection with protest march at JNU

Police on Tuesday registered two FIRs, one in South district and another in South West district, in connection with the protest march held by students of the Jawaharlal Nehru University on Monday.

Congress dominates Rajasthan local body polls, wins over 961 wards while BJP gets 737

The ruling Congress won over 961 wards, leaving the BJP trailing with 737 places in the elections to the urban local bodies held last week. The results declared on Tuesday showed that the party dominated most of the 49 civic bodies which went to poll. The Congress won nearly half of the 2,105 wards in the contest in the first local elections held in Rajasthan after the party came to power in the State last December.

Fathima Latheef suicide: IIT Madras students call off hunger strike

A section of students who went on a hunger fast on Tuesday have called it off, following assurances that the Indian Institute of Technology Madras would concede to two of their three demands.

Sweden discontinues Assange rape investigation

A Swedish prosecutor said the alleged rape investigation involving WikiLeaks founder Julian Assange, who is currently in prison in Britain, has been discontinued.

Bengaluru couple allege daughters ‘illegally confined’ at Nithyananda’s ashram in Ahmedabad

Controversial self-styled godman Nithyananda finds himself in another controversy with allegations of “illegally confining” two daughters of a Bengaluru-based couple. A petition has been filed in the Gujarat High Court by the couple seeking the court’s help to get back the daughters allegedly “illegally confined” at an ashram set up by Nithyananda, who faces rape charges.

Sea level along Indian coast rose by 8.5 cm in last 50 years: Environment Minister

Sea level along the Indian coast has risen by 8.5 cm in the last five decades, Union Minister Babul Supriyo said in the Rajya Sabha on Tuesday. Responding to a question on whether several cities will be submerged as temperatures are rising due to global warming, the Minister of State for environment said that the rate of increase of sea level due to climate change cannot be attributed with certainty.

12-year-old barred from proceeding to Sabarimala temple

A 12-year-old girl from Puducherry, who had come for darshan to Lord Ayyappa temple here along with her father, was not allowed to proceed to the shrine from Pamba on Tuesday morning. The age of the girl, with “irrumudikettu” (offerings to Lord Ayyappa) had been shown as 10 in the virutal Q booking.

Sri Lanka may face snap Parliamentary poll: Speaker

Sri Lanka faces the possibility of a snap Parliamentary poll, Speaker Karu Jayasuriya said on Tuesday, a day after Gotabaya Rajapaksa took over as the country’s President.

Already dreaming about historic pink ball test: Rahane

India vice-captain Ajinkya Rahane says he is already “dreaming” about the first-ever Day-Night Test in the country, which is starting from Friday against Bangladesh at Eden Gardens here. “Already dreaming about the historic pink ball test,” Rahane said in a Twitter post along with a picture of him sleeping and a pink ball lying next to him.
