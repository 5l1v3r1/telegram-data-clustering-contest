You'll never walk alone, Gordon Brown tells Jews in anti-racism speech

The former Prime Minister also hit out at allegations of Islamophobia

19 Nov 2019, 14:47 by Ben Glaze, https://www.facebook.com/MirrorPolitics

Gordon Brown promised Jews “you will never walk alone” as Labour battles the ongoing anti-Semitism row.

Jeremy Corbyn ’s election campaign has been marred by revelations over some candidates’ allegedly anti-Semitic social media posts, while the Tories have been hit by claims of Islamophobia.

Former Prime Minister Mr Brown told a Hope Not Hate dinner: “To those who suffer from Islamophobia, we have got to say - we will never tolerate the intolerable, we will never accept the unacceptable, we will never equivocate on what is unequivocally wrong.

“To the Jewish community, I believe we have got to say this – you will never walk alone, we will always walk by your side in fighting anti-Semitism.”

He added: “We can’t just stand up to some forms of discrimination, some forms of prejudice, some forms of bullying, some forms of bigotry and some forms of racism.

“We must stand up against all forms.”

Former Labour leader Mr Brown demanded “new laws and new protections against anti-Semitism and against Islamophobia”, adding: “That’s essential for a decent country.”

In a wide-ranging speech to the anti-fascist campaign group in London, he raised fresh warnings about the UK failing to strike a post-Brexit deal with Brussels before a transition ends in December 2020.

“It seems to me unlikely that, as present circumstances go, we are going to have anything other than a no-deal Brexit ,” he said.

“That means Britain an evermore intolerant, an evermore introverted, an evermore inward-looking and isolationist country - the opposite of the country we want to be.”
