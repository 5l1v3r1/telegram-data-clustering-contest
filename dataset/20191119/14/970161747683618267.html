Yorkie died 'when courier threw box of crystal over garden wall and it hit her'

19 Nov 2019, 14:08

A family’s beloved Yorkshire terrier died bloodied and in pain after a delivery driver allegedly threw a package that landed on top of her.

Cooper the dog was sunbathing in the back of her home in Venice, Los Angeles, last week when a FedEx driver pulled up outside.

That driver is said to have tossed a large box filled with crystal over the fence, only for it to land on Cooper and fatally damage her lungs and liver.

Mitchell Galin, who owned Cooper, told CBSLA: ‘So I immediately picked up the box, and Cooper was in a puddle of blood.’

He and partner Keiko Napier rushed Cooper to a vet, where they were told that the four-pound dog would have to be put to sleep.

Galin, who said Cooper had helped soothe him to sleep, said: ‘It was painful to watch your dog in pain.’

And Napier told of the agony the ordeal had caused, saying: ‘We’ve just had a horrible weekend.’

FedEx issued a statement extending its ‘deepest sympathies’ to Galin and Napier.

Their fence is so tall it is unlikely that the driver would have seen Cooper laying on the other side.

The courier giant said it had launched an urgent investigation into Cooper’s death, and would ‘take appropriate action’ once it had concluded.

But Napier has now called for a company wide rule banning couriers from throwing packages.

He explained: ‘To me that’s not satisfactory.

‘I feel they need to say they’re going to institute a corporate-wide mandate that drivers cannot throw packages; that’s what I want to hear.

‘I don’t want to hear we’ll look into it.”

The couple said Cooper used to sleep between them at night, and said they want to prevent drivers from taking shortcuts that could have a lasting impact.

“Our grandchild pays out here.

‘My mother is out here gardening. I’m here gardening.

‘And the package was so heavy, should it have struck one of us, it would’ve caused damage — very very severe damage.’
