Teenager stabbed after being attacked by three men in street

16-year-old rushed to hospital after attack in Coronation Drive

19 Nov 2019, 14:21 by Max Clements

A 16-year old boy suffered serious injuries after he was stabbed in an attack in Prescot last night.

Police were called to Coronation Drive following at 10pm last night to reports that a 16-year-old boy had been assaulted by three males.

Emergency services arrived on the scene shortly after and the teenager has been taken to hospital for treatment for treatment to injuries to his hands and legs.

An investigation is currently underway into the incident.

The injuries are not thought to be life threatening.

CCTV, witness and forensic enquiries are ongoing as police work to identify those involved.

A police spokesperson said: "We are appealing for information following a stabbing in Prescot last night, Monday, 18 November.

"Shortly after 10pm, officers were called to Coronation Drive following reports a 16-year-old boy had been assaulted by three males.

"Emergency services attended and the victim was taken to hospital for treatment to non-life threatening injuries to his hands and legs.

"CCTV, witness and forensic enquiries are ongoing in a bid to identify the offenders and anyone with information is asked to come forward."

Detective Chief Inspector Craig Summer said: "An investigation is underway to establish the full circumstances and we are keen to hear from anyone with information.

"I would like to reassure the local community that we are doing all that we can to find those responsible and I would like to ask anyone with CCTV or dashcam footage from the area, or who saw any suspicious behaviour or people making off from the scene, to please let us know.

"Knife crime will not be tolerated by Merseyside Police and for a 16 year-old boy to be left injured in this way is simply not acceptable.

"We will be carrying out a number of enquiries in the area and anyone with information is asked to contact @MerPolCC, 101 quoting ref 19100675691 or @CrimestoppersUK, anonymously on 0800 555 111."
