Food, gasoline shortages reported in Bolivian cities

Bolivian interim government said Monday that its efforts to resupply La Paz faced challenges because demonstrators cut off some transport routes

19 Nov 2019, 02:19 by AP

Residents in several Bolivian cities are reporting food and gasoline shortages because of protests by supporters of former President Evo Morales, who resigned and fled to Mexico after a disputed election and nationwide unrest.

Bolivian interim government said Monday that its efforts to resupply La Paz faced challenges because demonstrators cut off some transport routes. The new leadership is also struggling to open dialogue with opponents, particularly after the shooting deaths of nine pro-Morales coca growers during a confrontation with security forces Friday in the central town of Sacaba.

On Monday, Mr. Morales’ supporters in Sacaba threw rocks at police, who fired back with tear gas trying to disperse the protesters.

Bolivian church leaders announced plans to begin talks Monday involving U.N. envoy Jean Arnault. They appealed for the participation of Morales’ Movement Toward Socialism party and said topics would include new elections and calls for a new election panel. Former President Carlos Mesa, who finished second to Morales in the disputed Oct.20 election, said he would participate.
