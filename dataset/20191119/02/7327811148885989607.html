Steam leak, eruption shuts down block in Midtown during rush hour

19 Nov 2019, 02:14 by WABC

MIDTOWN, Manhattan (WABC) -- Crews responded to the scene of an underground water and steam leak in Midtown during rush hour on Monday evening.

The incident was reported at 49th Street and 8th Avenue around 5:30 p.m.

Because of the situation, West 49th Street was shut down between Broadway and 8th Avenue.

Officials say the underground leak somehow led to an eruption of steam. In some areas, dust debris and mud from the ground flew onto nearby cars in the neighborhood.

No injuries have been reported, but crews are working to determine what was in that dust.

DEP and Con Edison also responded to the scene.

Three buildings in the area were without steam service while DEP made repairs.

8th Avenue reopened Monday night though some lanes remained closed with potentially contaminated dirt in need of a cleanup.
