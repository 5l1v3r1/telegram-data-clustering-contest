Die Orakel launches bleep techno tribute series

Frankfurt artists 386i, Benjamin Milz and Koga will pay tribute to the Sheffield sound across three white-label 12-inches.

19 Nov 2019, 02:24 by Matt McDermott

Oliver Hafenbauer's Die Orakel label has launched the ORKL-0114-Series.

The first of three white-label 12-inches, from the Frankfurt producers 386i, Benjamin Milz and Koga, is out today. The triptych will pay tribute to the influential bleep techno sound which swept British clubs on the cusp of the '90s. The era has experienced a resurgence in interest recently, thanks to bleep-influenced productions by artists like Luca Lozano as well as a recently-published book by Matt Anniss.

Check out Anniss's 2014 RA feature, Bleep: The Story Of Britain's First Bass Revolution.
