I'm a Celeb's new campmate Cliff Parisi joined jungle due to financial woes

EXCLUSIVE: Cliff Parisi, who played Minty on EastEnders, has revealed he said yes to going into the jungle because of money worries

19 Nov 2019, 22:30 by Irishmirror.ie

Cliff Parisi, known to millions as Minty from EastEnders , is not minted, and says he is going into the jungle because he needs the money.

The actor, 59, has admitted that he initially turned down the job until he was shown a huge tax bill by his accountant, which changed his mind.

Cliff said: “What made me sign up? The taxman. I have got a bill I need to pay.

"Like everyone at this time of year, you go, ‘Oh no. Again...' You would think I earn a lot of money but I don’t.

"I work for the BBC, so it ain’t that big.

“I had a meeting about this earlier on in the year, and I thought about it long and hard and said no.

"And then as I got an estimate for my tax, I phoned them up and said, is it too late to say yes?

“So here I am. But it could be an amazing experience and I hope it is.

“Life throws up things like this for you, and if you don’t take them you are a bit of a mug.

So I give myself a bit of a talking to, and then I spoke to the accountant and he made my mind up.

What is the worse-case scenario? A big snake is gonna bite you or the taxman is gonna bite you. Which do you want?”

Cliff, who quit EastEnders in 2010 and has since starred regularly in BBC drama Call The Midwife, will go into the jungle as a late entry tomorrow, alongside Coronation Street actor Andy Whyment, who plays Kirk Sutherland on the soap.

Cliff started off as a stand-up comic,, but soon appeared as an actor in The Darling Buds of May and Drop the Dead Donkey.

He then landed bigger parts in Kavanagh QC, Bramwell and A Prince Among Men, before being cast as Minty in EastEnders in 2002, a role he played for eight years.

Cliff admitted he has not watched the show since he left.

He said: “I loved working there, but I think that in life you’ve got to move forward and do different things.

"Although I do miss the people there, I’m happy doing what I’m doing. I love Call the Midwife. It’s a new family.”

Asked if he would ever return, he said: “Never say never. If there was a time that financially I needed to do it and they offered it to me and it was an interesting enough storyline, I’d consider it.”

Cliff admitted that he had some concerns about the famously meagre jungle rations.

He said: “I obviously like my food, so this might be a bit of a struggle for me.

"But, I like challenges, so let’s just see. I’d love to lose a couple of stone – that’d be incredible.”

The star also said he would miss his family during his time Down Under. Cliff has son Arthur, nine, with wife Tara Wyer, a BBC production assistant.

He said he told Arthur: “I said, Daddy’s going away for a while and it’s going to be an adventure. I’m going to miss you and I love you.”

Cliff also has daughter Mandy, 37, and sons Dean, 31, and Jack 27, from previous relationships.

And he said Tara offered some helpful hints for his time in the jungle.

He said: “My wife’s written a long list out of things I mustn’t do.

"Like scratching my bum, picking my nose, farting – all the disgusting things men do – scratching your nuts.

"I’m sure I’ll forget from time to time and I’ll have to have manners.”
