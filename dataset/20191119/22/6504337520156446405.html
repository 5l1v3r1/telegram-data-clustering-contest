South Hayward Station reopens after rider killed in stabbing aboard train

19 Nov 2019, 22:53 by Dianne de Guzman

Updated 8:28 pm PST, Tuesday, November 19, 2019

BART police confirmed a rider was stabbed and killed by another patron aboard one of its trains, forcing a temporary shutdown of the South Hayward Station.

The suspect was later arrested a block from the station, according to BART police.

The incident was first reported as a fight between two men in their 40s on a train, between the Bay Fair and Warm Springs stations just before 1 p.m., said BART Interim Police Chief Ed Alvarez. The fight reportedly started when the suspect attempted to steal a pair of shoes and was confronted, The Chronicle reported.

As the train approached the South Hayward Station, police were on the platform and the victim was found to be fatally stabbed.

A man matching the suspect's description was found on Tennyson Road and was "involved in an issue at a local dealership," but Alvarez didn't elaborate further. The suspect was then arrested.

Alvarez said the incident was not a random attack, stating that riders have a safe system and the stabbing is a "rare occurrence" on BART. He said that video aboard the train and at the station will be reviewed as part of the investigation.

"Our thoughts are with the deceased and his loved ones," BART said in a tweet.

BART General Manager Robert Powers promised to increase BART police presence at its stations in the aftermath of the stabbing, saying in a statement that police officers, non-sworn community service officers and fare inspectors will be "in the system, on station platforms, and riding the trains."

“We are heartbroken that a person has lost his life due to violence on one of our trains this afternoon. We extend our sincerest condolences to the family and friends of the victim," Powers said.

"BART is taking immediate steps to address concerns many of our riders may have as a result of the tragic death. ... The safety of our customers and employees is, and always will be, our top priority."

The South Hayward Station was closed at 1 p.m. as BART police and the Hayward Police Department investigated the fatal incident, said BART spokesman Jim Allison. Trains did not stop at the station and were forced to single track-through South Hayward during the shutdown.

The station was re-opened at about 5:20 p.m.

Riders can continue to expect a residual 20-minute delay on the Warm Springs line, in the Warm Springs, Richmond and Daly City directions.
