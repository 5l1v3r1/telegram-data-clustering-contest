Ireland's Euro 2020 play-off semi-final opponent confirmed as Slovakia

Mick McCarthy's men will face Slovakia in March - and then either Northern Ireland or Bosnia if they progress - for Euro 2020 qualification

19 Nov 2019, 22:00 by Paul O'Hehir

Ireland will play Slovakia away in their Euro 2020 playoff semi-final in March.

And the winner of that one-legged tie will meet either Northern Ireland or Bosnia in a one-off final for a spot at the finals,

UEFA have to officially rubber-stamp the fixtures today, before holding a draw in Switzerland on Friday to determine who is at home in the final.

And that decider could be an All-Island battle between north and south at either Windsor Park or Aviva Stadium.

But the Boys in Green will have to negotiate Slovakia first and that game will be played in either Bratislava or Trnava.

March 26 and 31 have been earmaked as the semi-final and final dates, but UEFA has yet to confirm match details

Ireland have played Slovakia five times before, winning once and drawing the other four games.

Four of those matches were competitive. Kevin Doyle scored a winner for Steve Staunton’s Ireland at Croke Park in the first ever meeting in 2007.

The return leg at the Tehelne Pole Stadion in Bratislava was a 2-2 draw but best remembered in the build-up for Stephen Ireland’s ‘Grannygate’ shenaningans.

Under Giovanni Trapattoni, Ireland drew 1-1 away and 0-0 at home with Slovakia on their way to Euro 2012.

And the most recent meeting was a 2-2 friendly draw at Aviva Stadium under Martin O’Neill three years ago.

In this Euro 2020 qualifying campaign, Slovakia finished third in their group behind table-toppers Croatia and second-placed Wales, winning four of their eight games and losing three.

They scored 13 goals in the process and conceded 11, losing heavily home and away to Croatia while also going down 1-0 to Wales in Cardiff but holding them to a draw at home.

Like Ireland, Slovakia - who are ranked 31st in the world - last featured at a major tournament at Euro 2016.

In France, they lost to Germany in the second round having qualified for the knockout stages as one of the best third-placed teams in a group behind England, Wales and Russia.
