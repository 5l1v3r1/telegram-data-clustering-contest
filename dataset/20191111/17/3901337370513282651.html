'Ronaldo Hasn't Dribbled Past Anyone In Years!'

11 Nov 2019, 17:45 by Kurt Buckerfield

After superstar Cristiano Ronaldo reacted angrily to being substituted on Sunday night, one of the most legendary managers in football slammed his lack of professionalism and claimed he has not dribbled past a defender in three years.

Click through the mini-gallery above to see some pictures of the incident.

The Portuguese forward has endured a tough time in Italy of late, having been substituted in both of Juventus' past two matches.

On Sunday night, in the Bianconeri's 1-0 Serie A win over AC Milan, Ronaldo once again reacted furiously to being taken off by Maurizio Sarri in the 55th minute, heading straight down the tunnel to the dressing room after making his way off the pitch.

Following the match and the 34-year-old's reaction, former Juve boss Fabio Capello praised Sarri's decision to take off the Portugal talisman.

"I didn't like this, it wasn't nice," he told Sky Sport Italia.

"He (Ronaldo) must be a champion even when he comes off the pitch.

"The truth is that Cristiano Ronaldo hasn't dribbled (past) an opponent for three years. I did the La Liga commentary when he usually took the double-step and left you there.

"Inside him is a super-champion, the best of all, but now there is Dybala and Douglas Costa, who made two sensational goals. Dybala, in excellent condition, can make the difference, as can Douglas Costa.

"They win games without Ronaldo. Juve seemed dependent on him, instead the great squad and the quality of the players helps them win anyway.

"Bravo Sarri, who had the courage to take him off, it takes personality, especially thinking that all the players in the squad can play and make a difference.

"Cristiano did it for a while, but now it's not him and he must recover, especially on a physical level. He does not have the speed and dynamism he has shown at other times."
