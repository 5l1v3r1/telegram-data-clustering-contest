Children across Plymouth mark Remembrance Day

The city fell silent at 11am

11 Nov 2019, 17:51 by Rom Preston-Ellis

Young children from across Plymouth have come together to mark Armistice Day.

The Royal Naval Preschool Learning Organisation that runs seven childcare nurseries - known as Jack and Jill’s Childcare in Plymouth and Torpoint - today were able to join Goosewell Primary Academy’s children in paying their respects and remembering those serving who have died during both world wars and other conflicts around the world.

A spokesperson for the nursery said: "Children from Jack and Jill’s Plymstock attended the service at the war memorial at Burrow Hill, Plymstock, along with representatives from various military organisations and councillors to hold a two-minute silence and lay wreaths and poppy crosses.

"The Jack and Jill’s children attending were aged between three and four years old and were accompanied by members of the preschool staff and Trustees.

"During the last week children have undertaken activities involving remembering those who fought for us and those no longer with us."

Plymouth fell silent at 11am to remember those who have lost their lives in past conflicts.

This year marks a number of significant anniversaries, including the 80th anniversary of the Battle of River Plate and the 75th anniversary of D-Day and Battle of Arnhem. It also marks the 70th anniversary of the Berlin Airlift.

On Remembrance Sunday and Armistice Day on Monday, and further into November, events will take place all over Plymouth in honour of the military and sacrifices made by armed forces personnel.
