Why the police helicopter was over Jowett in Lakeside, Tamworth

The National Police Air Service helicopter was called in, along with colleagues from the fire and rescue service, to use heat seeking equipment to search the area as a precaution.

11 Nov 2019, 17:11 by Daniel Newbould, https://www.birminghammail.co.uk/authors/daniel-newbould/

The National Police Air Service helicopter was called to Tamworth after reports of a driver falling in water following a crash.

The incident happened off Jowett in Lakeside on Friday night (November 8).

Staffordshire Police said: “We received a report at around 9pm on November 8 of a collision involving a single vehicle on Glascote Road in Tamworth.

“The driver left the scene before officers arrived and was thought to have fallen into a body of water nearby.

“The National Police Air Service helicopter was called in, along with colleagues from the fire and rescue service, to use heat seeking equipment to search the area as a precaution.

   

“Despite a thorough search, no trace of the driver was found.

“Enquiries to find the driver continue.”

West Midlands Ambulance Service confirmed: “We were called at 9.38pm to a location off Jowett to reports of someone falling in a lake or body of water.

“We sent one ambulance, two paramedic officers and the Hazardous Area Response Team (HART). After an extensive search of the area nothing was found and we were stood down.”
