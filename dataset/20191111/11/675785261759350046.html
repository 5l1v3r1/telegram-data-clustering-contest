Premier League team of the weekend as Liverpool pair joined by Leicester duo

Liverpool bolstered their title hopes with a 3-1 win at Anfield, while there were also home wins for Wolves, Burnley, Manchester United, Leicester and Newcastle

11 Nov 2019, 11:00 by Alex Richards, https://www.facebook.com/AARichards/

Another round of Premier League fixtures has been and gone, and now we're left staring towards the final international break of 2019 (shudders).

The top-flight season is now almost a third of the way through, and Jurgen Klopp's Liverpool are flying high, a 3-1 win over champions Manchester City on Sunday sending them eight points clear of second place.

That position is now held by Leicester City, with Brendan Rodgers' men having eased past a bedraggled Arsenal side on Saturday, while Chelsea are third after victory over Crystal Palace.

At the other end of the table, both Watford and Everton claimed much-needed victories, while Newcastle , Burnley , Wolves and Manchester United were also winners.

Sheffield United are now fifth, after Chris Wilder's side took a point off Tottenham in the weekend's only draw.

However, despite an impressive team display from the Blades, none of their star men are in this week's Mirror Football XI.

With that being said, here's our team of the weekend, ready to shine in a pretty straightforward 4-2-3-1 formation.

Nick Pope (Burnley)

Nothing spectacular was required from the Clarets No.1 in the comfortable win over West Ham .

But he made a couple of smart stops, came and made claims to relieve pressure when necessary and looks back to his best. Will hope to rival Jordan Pickford with England now.

 

Ricardo Pereira (Leicester City)

The Portuguese played a decisive role in Leicester's comfortable win over Arsenal .

Pereira, having nailed down a starting spot at right-back this term, defended well against Pierre-Emerick Aubameyang whenever he drifted left and had a hand in both goals; has a knack of finding space in dangerous positions the final third.

Kurt Zouma (Chelsea)

The Frenchman was challenged by Frank Lampard to make himself first choice and he's done just that in the absence of Antonio Rudiger.

Zouma had a fine game against Crystal Palace , enjoying and winning the physical battle with Jordan Ayew, while using his speed - on a number of occasions - to close out Palace runners or help Reece James on Wilf Zaha. Read the game well throughout.

 

Ciaran Clark (Newcastle United)

The Irishman scored the winner as Steve Bruce's men made it back-to-back victories at home to Bournemouth .

But Clark was tremendous throughout as part of the Magpies' back three, dealing well with Callum Wilson and winning more than his fair share in the air.

Andy Robertson (Liverpool)

Social media continues to be awash with gifs of Trent Alexander-Arnold pinging balls from right to left, but the best ball at Anfield this weekend came from Liverpool's left-back; the Scot's delivery for Mohamed Salah's header was perfect.

During the champions vs champions clash, Robertson did well with the ball and was defensively sound, doing well up against Bernardo Silva.

 

Fabinho (Liverpool)

The Liverpool man mountain produced a towering performance in the 3-1 win over Manchester City .

Scored a tremendous opening goal from distance, but also provided calm amid the Anfield chaos, defending smartly at the base of midfield and moving the ball to good effect throughout.

Ruben Neves (Wolves)

The Portuguese playmaker has stepped up in recent weeks, and opened the scoring at Molineux in the West Midlands' derby with a fine effort from 22 yards.

Was typically calm and smart in possession throughout, as well as making a number of smart interceptions, while a fine, brave, sliding challenge directly led to Wolves ' second goal.

Adama Traore (Wolves)

Keeps getting better and better under Nuno Espirito Santo.

Traore terrorised Aston Villa throughout, hitting double figures for completed dribbles (13/17), and showed composure in teeing up Raul Jimenez for Wolves' killer second goal after a late, lung-bursting 60-yard run.

It was his eighth assist of the campaign in all competitions.

Gerard Deulofeu (Watford)

Every so often, the Spaniard shows just why he was once so highly-rated when dancing his way through La Masia; Friday night was one of those occasions.

From his first touch of the game, which saw him rob Emilio Buendia before racing clear, drop a step over and score, Norwich couldn't get near him.

He ended the night with a goal and an assist, inspiring the Hornets to a first win of the season.

Richarlison (Everton)

The Brazilian scored the winner as Marco Silva's Toffees edged past Southampton .

And throughout the Saints had no answer to Richarlison's movement, speed and power, cutting in from the left side and generally wreaking havoc.

Jamie Vardy (Leicester)

Brendan Rodgers has spoken of channelling Vardy's running for the moments that matter, and this was the perfect example.

The Premier League's top scorer was a constant threat to the Arsenal defence because he stayed central, sniffing opportunities in behind, rather than chasing across the field to close fullbacks.

He duly scored one, teed up another, and has the Foxes second at the international break.
