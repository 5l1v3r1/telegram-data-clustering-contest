Liam Cooper delivers Scotland blow as Leeds star pulls out of squad

The 28-year-old limped out of Leeds' win over Blackburn and has withdrawn from the Scotland squad.

11 Nov 2019, 11:36 by Mark McDougall, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/mark-mcdougall/

Scotland will be without defender Liam Cooper after he pulled out of the squad to face Cyprus and Kazakhstan through injury.

The Leeds captain limped out of his side's win over Blackburn Rovers with a groin injury, having just returned from a similar problem that saw him miss three games.

And now the 28-year-old has withdrawn from the Scotland squad and it leaves Steve Clarke with just three recognised centre backs, although it's likely he will call someone else in.

Mikey Devlin, Scott McKenna and Declan Gallagher are the only centre backs available although Scott McTominay has played in that role for Manchester United in the past.

Charlie Mulgrew has been a regular at the back but wasn't included in the current squad and could now be handed a reprieve.

Sheffield Wednesday's David Bates and Stoke's Liam Lindsay are other options while U21 defender Ryan Porteous could also be be promoted if needed.

Cooper has been a regular in Scotland squads since his first call-up in 2016 but only made his debut in the 2-1 defeat to Russia in September and also played in the 4-0 hammering to Belgium three days later.
