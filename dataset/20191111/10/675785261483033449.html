Gary Neville's Premier League title verdict after Liverpool's win over Man City

The 3-1 victory over Man City on Sunday put Jurgen Klopp's side nine points clear of the defending champions, in fourth, and eight ahead of Leicester and Chelsea

11 Nov 2019, 10:08 by Matt Maltby, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/matt-maltby/

Gary Neville believes it is only a matter of time until Liverpool lift the Premier League title - and he reckons this season could be their year.

The 3-1 victory over Manchester City on Sunday put Jurgen Klopp's side nine points clear of the defending champions, in fourth, and eight ahead of Leicester and Chelsea .

They have taken 34 points from a possible 36 since the start of the season and the only other two clubs to have been in a similar position - Manchester United in 1993 and City in 2017 - both went on to win the title.

And Neville, the Manchester United legend, believes this form is why the Anfield giants are rightly being lauded as the favourites for the crown.

 

Speaking on his Gary Neville podcast , he said: "To win the Champions League you have to be some team, to get to back-to-back Champions League finals you have to be some team.

"To do what they did last year and chase Man City down relentlessly to the end - they are playing championship football, Premier League title-winning football.

"They have been now for 18 months.

"They could blow this, yes, City could come back, yes there could be a freak from Chelsea or Leicester, but it’s becoming more unlikely."

Neville says an injury crisis could cause a huge headache for Klopp, like it has done for City manager Pep Guardiola - but feels the Reds have what it takes to lift the trophy.

"The biggest risk to Liverpool is getting caught up with the Champions League, with injuries to [Mo] Salah, [Sadio] Mane, [Virgil] Van Dijk - that would be a big problem for them, losing one of those players for a couple of months at a time, just like it has been for City losing [Aymeric] Laporte.

"But all things point towards Liverpool being big, big favourites for the title.

"And it’s not just because they’re nine points clear (of City).

"It’s the steps that they’ve been taking in the seasons before to get closer and closer and closer and closer.

"And eventually they will get there and it’s looking like, at this moment in time, they’re going to take some real stopping."
