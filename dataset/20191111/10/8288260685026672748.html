Long-serving Wolverhampton city councillor granted leave

Long-serving Wolverhampton city councillor is unable to attend meetings and is granted special dispensation by Labour colleagues on the grounds of his ill health

11 Nov 2019, 10:31 by Joe Sweeney, https://www.birminghammail.co.uk/authors/joe-sweeney/

One of Wolverhampton's longest-serving councillors has been granted special dispensation from attending committee meetings on the grounds of ill health, his colleagues agreed.

Councillor John Rowley (Lab. Blakenhall), who was first elected to the council in 1982, but has been unable to attend meetings and functions since May 15 this year - sending apologies for medical reasons.

A meeting of the full city council agreed the move unanimously. It will be recognised until May 2020.

Moving the recommendation, Leader of the Council, Cllr Ian Brookfield (Lab. Fallings Park), said: "I'm sure I speak for everyone on the council in sending our very best wishes to Cllr John Rowley and his wife Judith."

Leader of the opposition, Cllr Wendy Thompson (Con. Tettenhall Wightwick), who seconded the resolution, added: "We are all in full agreement of this resolution. And we certainly join everyone else in sending our very best wishes to Cllr Rowley and Judith in very difficult circumstances."

Cllr Rowley, who worked as a college lecturer, served the council alongside his wife, Judith, who was also a Labour representative for the Blakenhall ward for 23 years. He was de-selected from his seat in 2016 but later regained it.

His main interests are planning, the environment and community safety. Cllr Rowley is also a Justice of the Peace on the Walsall Bench.

In a report to members, the council's Democratic Services Manager, Jaswinder Kaur, said: "Unfortunately, Cllr Rowley has been unable to attend any council meetings, committees or other outside bodies on which he represents the authority.

"If a member of the local authority (LA) fails to attend any meetings for a period of six months consecutively, then they will cease to be a member - unless the absence has been approved by the council within that time frame."
