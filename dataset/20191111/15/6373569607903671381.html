Joe Riley has been playing through the pain barrier for Plymouth Argyle, reveals Ryan Lowe

The right wing-back was ruled out of the FA Cup tie at Bolton Wanderers, however

11 Nov 2019, 15:55 by Chris Errington

Plymouth Argyle wing-back Joe Riley has been playing through the pain barrier to try and avoid an operation, manager Ryan Lowe has revealed.

The 28-year-old is still struggling with the after effects of the broken left arm he suffered in an away game against Blackpool at the end of March.

Riley has made 14 starts and two substitute appearances for the Pilgrims this season, and scored two goals.

However, he was ruled out of the 1-0 FA Cup first round win away to Bolton Wanderers, the club where he began his career, after falling on the arm in training on Friday.

Riley needed injections to play in home games against Salford City and Walsall earlier in the season because of the pain he was in.

Lowe said: “He has had a few whacks on it. He has had a bandage on it a few times, and a strapping and what not.

“In all honesty, I think it’s broke still but he doesn’t want to get the operation because it could rule him out for six to eight weeks.

“Then by the six to eight weeks he could find himself out of the team, and that’s what he doesn’t want to do.

“To be fair to him, he’s trying to go through the pain barrier with it but that’s why we have a squad.”

In the absence of Riley against Bolton, Joe Edwards to right wing-back and Josh Grant impressively played the midfield holding role.

Lowe said: “Joe was gutted not to play because it (Bolton) is where he came through but you have to take the decision out of his hands.

“We sit down – the physio (Paul Atkinson) and John (Lucas, Argyle’s fitness and conditioning coach) – and say ‘Look, it’s not going to work’.

“Joe Edwards has gone in there and was fantastic, and Josh has gone in the holding midfield role and was fantastic.”
