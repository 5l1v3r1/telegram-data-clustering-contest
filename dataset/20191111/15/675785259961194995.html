Love Island star Tommy Fury challenges KSI ahead of boxing return

KSI won his six-round cruiserweight bout against fellow YouTube personality Logan Paul in Los Angeles on Saturday night

11 Nov 2019, 15:56 by Matt Maltby, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/matt-maltby/

Tommy Fury has thrown his hat into the ring to take on YouTube sensation KSI in a money-spinning boxing showdown.

KSI won his six-round cruiserweight bout against fellow YouTube personality Logan Paul in Los Angeles on Saturday night.

The fight at the Staples Centre was won by the Watford man, whose real name is Olajide Olayinka Williams JJ Olatunji, in a split decision - 55-56 57-54 56-55.

Professional boxer Fury, another self-proclaimed influencer after starring on ITV hit show Love Island this year, has revealed that he stayed up until the early hours to watch the bout.

And the British light-heavyweight, whose older brother is lineal world heavyweight champion Tyson, has challenged KSI to a showdown.

He told Seconds Out : "I was sat in the hotel room, it was about 10 o'clock and I thought, 'do you know what, Logan Paul and KSI are fighting tonight. Ok, a lot of people are jumping on it. I'll have a watch.'

"So I went on the Sky Sports app, I bought it and I sat down and watched it all the way through.

"I thought it was a very entertaining fight, a very entertaining fight, and I wasn't aware that KSI still wanted to fight afterwards. I thought he was just gonna fight Logan afterwards and that was it.

"But I've heard that he does want to keep fighting.

"You know, I'm a fighting man, I'm no amateur. We're both from the influencer background, he's from the YouTube side, I'm from the Love Island/reality TV side. Let's get it on.

"If he wants a real fight, he knows where I am."

Fury had two professional contests before entering the reality dating show earlier this year.

He will fight for the first time since appearing on Love Island at London's Copper Box Arena on 21 December, with a fight pencilled in on the undercard of Daniel Dubois v Kyotaro Fujimoto.

And, despite have a professional boxing background, the Manchester bruiser believes it would be an even contest against KSI.

"Do you know what it is, right, I'm sure a lot of people will jump on this saying 'Tommy will do this, Tommy will do that' and obviously I back myself 100 per cent in any fight I go into," he added.

"But if you actually look at the statistics, what is it? I've had 12 amateur fights when I was 15 years old and I've had three pro fights. There's not a lot in it.

"Yeah I've been in the gym sparring around, but actually fighting wise, I've not had a lot of experience fighting and he's not had a lot of experience.

"I'm 20, he's a young man, I've had three fights, he's had two fights.

"If he wants to keep boxing, why not? Why not make it a battle of Britain."
