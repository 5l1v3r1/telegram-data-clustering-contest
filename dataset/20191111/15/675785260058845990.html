Lewes Castle: Medieval wall collapses as emergency services search for casualties

The wall is said to have crumbled onto buildings in Castle Ditch Lane in the Sussex town - with a helicopter and dog unit searching the area for casualties

11 Nov 2019, 15:07 by Abigail O'Leary

Emergency services are searching through rubble after part of Lewes Castle in Sussex collapsed - sending 500 tonnes of Medieval wall crumble onto to nearby buildings.

Sussex Police confirmed its officers were assisting East Sussex Fire and Rescue Service with the ongoing incident.

Witnesses reported multiple emergency vehicles at the scene, as well as a helicopter and dog unit.

According to East Sussex Fire and Rescue teams, the area of wall collapse is not part of the main castle and not immediately accessible by the public.

They said there were no immediate reports of anyone missing but a "multi-agency search and rescue operation" is ongoing.

A spokesperson said: "A search of the area is not yet underway, due to the instability of the Old Coach House. ESFRS is working with other emergency services to gain a safe access.

"Currently, there are no reports of anyone missing, or any casualties.

"The area of wall collapse is not part of the main castle and not immediately accessible by the public. This is expected to be a protracted incident, involving a multi-agency search and rescue operation.

"East Sussex Fire & Rescue Service were mobilised to Lewes Castle, following reports of a wall collapse in the Old Coach House.

"Lewes firefighters were initially mobilised at 12.22 today (11 November) and called further assistance from Sussex Police, SECamb, HART, Building Control, the Red Cross and specialist teams, including both Kent and West Sussex Urban Search & Rescue."

 

A spokesman for Sussex Police has said: “Police are responding to a report of part of Lewes Castle collapsing on to buildings in Castle Ditch Lane, Lewes."

"Fire service colleagues have requested the assistance of search dogs and a drone having been alerted at 12.22pm on Monday (November 11).

"It is not known at this time if anyone is trapped as a result."

Councillor Edward Crouch said: "Something pretty major going on at #Lewes Castle, helicopter, three ambulances and a police."

A spokesman from Lewes District Council said officers from its building control team are at the scene and "liaising with emergency services".

An eyewitness said five police cars, a dog unit and a number of ambulances were at the scene, reports the Sussex Express.

The Norman Castle dates back 1000 years and occupies a commanding position guarding the gap in the South Downs cut by the River Ouse.
