Plymouth Argyle drawn away to Bristol Rovers or Bromley in FA Cup second round

The tie will be played on the weekend of November 30 and December 1

11 Nov 2019, 19:33 by Chris Errington

Plymouth Argyle will have to wait a little longer to find out their opponents in the FA Cup second round.

Ryan Lowe’s Pilgrims have tonight been drawn away to Bristol Rovers or National League leaders Bromley.

Those two teams drew 1-1 at Rovers’ Memorial Stadium in their first round tie on Saturday, after Bromley grabbed an 83 minute equaliser.

The replay will take place at Bromley’s Hayes Lane ground on Tuesday, November 19 and be televised live on BT Sport 1.

Bromley have lost on the past 13 occasions they have reached the FA Cup first round proper.

Their replay against Rovers gives them the chance to reach round two for the first time since 1945/46.

Argyle will travel to either Graham Coughlan’s Rovers or Bromley on the weekend of November 30 and December 1.

The winners of the tie will pick up £54,000 in prize money and secure a place in the third round, when all of the Premier League and Championship clubs will enter.

Rovers, who are ninth in League One under the leadership of Coughlan, had taken a 78th minute lead against Bromley with a goal from substitute Luke Leahy.

Bromley forced the replay, though, after Chris Bush netted the equaliser from close range.
