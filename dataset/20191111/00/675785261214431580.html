Declan Donnelly reduced to tears at performance of song played at dad's funeral

Ant & Dec's DNA Journey saw the pair travel to Ireland, where an emotion-heavy performance saw Dec shed a few tears

11 Nov 2019, 00:48 by Will Twigger, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/will-twigger/

The intensely emotional Ant & Dec's DNA Journey followed the pair as they traced back their ancestry and chatted candidly about the ups and downs of their lives.

Ant even spoke openly about his time in rehab and the rough couple of years he had - but it was something else entirely that reduced presenting partner Dec to tears.

During a segment in which both were found to have Irish ancestry, Ant and Dec jetted off the Emerald Isle to retrace their ancestors' footsteps.

They made their way to a pub in Drumkeeran in County Leitrim, where a local sang for the guests something very familiar to Dec.

As tears rolled down his cheeks, Dec revealed he "loved" the song and that it had also been performed at his dad's funeral.

Alphonsus Donnelly died in 2011, aged 78, after a battle with cancer.

Fans were quick to take to social media to discuss how powerful the show was.

One wrote: "Crying all the way through Ant & Dec's #DNAJourney wishing they were my cousins!"

"Ant crying, Dec crying I'm crying #AntAndDecsDNAourney," another blubbed.

A third penned: "The boys crying left me a bit in tears...Especially when Dec told that he knew that song from his dad's funeral. Wow, this is getting not only better but so emotional. #DNAJourney."

It won't be long before the Geordie duo are back on our telly screens, as they've already touched down in Australia for the next series of I'm A Celebrity .

The show - which kicks off on 17th November - will be the first time Ant and Dec have reunited in the Aussie jungle after Ant sat out last year's show to focus on his rehabiliation.

This Morning's Holly Willoughby stood in for him during last year's show, which saw football icon Harry Redknapp crowned the latest King of the Jungle.
