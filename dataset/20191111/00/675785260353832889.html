British Steel saved as it agrees £70million rescue deal with Chinese firm Jingye

The deal is expected to secure thousands of jobs. Staff at Scunthorpe steelworks had been told talks were progressing well and an announcement could be made this morning.

11 Nov 2019, 00:36 by Elaine McCahill, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/elaine-mccahill/

British Steel is set to announce a rescue deal to the tune of £70million with a Chinese firm.

The deal with the Jingye Group is expected to save around 4,000 jobs in the UK.

Staff had been told in an email that talks with the industrial giant were progressing well and an update would be issued on Monday.

The Chinese firm have in principal agreed to absorb the company and staff into its huge existing steel and manufacturing division. British Steel employs around 3,000 in Scunthorpe and another 800 on Teesside.

British Steel also supports around 20,000 jobs in the supply chain.

It's believed the firm has promised an increase in production but also warned costs may need to be cut.

Jingye are a privately owned Chinese corporation founded and led by Li Ganpo a former senior Communist Party official who became a self-made industrial tycoon.

The group has interests in steel and manufacturing, chemicals, real estate, finance, trade, pharmaceutical, hotels and tourism.

It has more than 22,000 employees and assets valued at more than £3bn.

British Steel plunged into liquidation in May, triggering fears for major job losses.

The taxpayer is propping up the business at a cost reported to be £1m a day.

It's understood the government will help with loan guarantees.
