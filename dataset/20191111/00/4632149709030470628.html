Things to do today in Bengaluru

11 Nov 2019, 00:30 by Bangalore Mirror Bureau

Every picture tells a story
Artist George Martin’s new show So Far... So Good is inspired from the classic, satirical expression, which we use to pause and gauge our performance in the absurd theatre of life, where we put up an act of ‘being’ and ‘existing’. In all these artworks, some sort of conversation is happening through the images, illusions, forms, colours, flatness, symbolisms, pastiche and visual textures.

WHERE: Gallery Sumukha, BTS Depot Road, Wilson Garden
WHEN: 10.30am-6.30pm, till November 30
CALL: 22292230

Colour me happy
Confluence has brought together the recent works of Priya Bharat, Shipra Gupta, and Sucharita Senapati. Sucharita’s style is realism and she loves to work with watercolour, oil, and charcoal, and she is currently experimenting with textures and ink. She’s made a series on Ganesha, Shiva, elephants and fairies. Bharat’s subjects are varied – landscape, cityscape, still life, each drawn from observations of everyday life. Gupta’s paintings are characterised by nostalgic images of childhood and joys of the simple, rural life.

WHERE: Karnataka Chitrakala Parishath
WHEN: 10.30am-7pm, till November 12

Shoe talk
Comic Aakash Mehta is in town with his act Dirty stans and other stories. What should you expect? Is it a story about shoes? No. About capitalism? No, no. Is it about the travel tales he’s picked up in the eight years he was on the road. Drop by and find out yourself.

WHERE: That Comedy Club, 5th Block, Koramangala
WHEN: 9.45pm, November 11

Jokes apart
Mondays are heavy to deal with. We know it. But good for you that, Bangalore Comedy Club has the perfect plan to beat your Monday blues. They have lined up Neha Bobde, Arun Kiran, Ashwin Srinivasan, Kaushal Dayani, Amrita Chanda, Rupen Paul and Aditya Giri for a standup comedy show tonight.

WHERE: The Beer Cafe, 4th Block, Koramangala
WHEN: 8.30pm, November 11
ENTRY: `150, insider.in

A comedy of errors
Humans have been exploring the unknown since time immemorial. Relive this journey with The Boy with No Shadow tonight. It is a comic tale of a peace-loving kingdom on the planet Venus, ruled by an absent-minded king and a very patient queen, and their assistant – a military chief, who is preoccupied with making a magic potion. A little boy passing by their world drinks a bottle of one of the potions ordered by the Commander out of curiosity and turns invisible, which leads to a comedy of
errors and endless entertainment.

WHERE: MLR Convention Centre, JP Nagar, 7th Phase
WHEN: 7.30pm onwards, November 11
ENTRY: bookmyshow.com

Finishing touch
So you are intrigued by fondant,
but are too scared to use the material? Join this workshop and let the experts walk you through the usage of fondant

and how to give your cakes that neat finish. Over two days, you will learn how to work with ganache, achieve those sharp edges, drape the cake with fondant, and various other decorations such as frills, bows, ruffles, figurines and more.

WHERE: Flourish, School of Baking Cake Decor, Brigade Road, Ashok Nagar
WHEN: 3pm, November 11

FEE: `5,544, eventshigh.com
