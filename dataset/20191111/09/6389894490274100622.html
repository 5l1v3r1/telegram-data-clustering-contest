UK escapes recession but annual growth slowest in a decade

11 Nov 2019, 09:36 by Harry Robertson

The UK economy avoided falling into recession in the third quarter of the year, official figures have shown today, with the giant services sector lifting growth.

British GDP grew by 0.3 per cent between July and September compared to the previous quarter, the Office for National Statistics (ONS) said, marginally below economists’ expectations of a 0.4 per cent expansion.

The UK economy contracted in the second quarter, meaning another contraction in the third quarter would have plunged Britain into technical recession, causing a major problem for the Conservative government during the General Election campaign.

Nonetheless, growth over the last year has “slowed to its lowest rate in almost a decade,” said an ONS spokesperson.
