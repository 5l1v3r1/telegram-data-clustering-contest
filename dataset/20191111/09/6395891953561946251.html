Georgia sheriff's deputy charged with murder after man shot, killed, police say

11 Nov 2019, 09:48 by Michelle Ewing, Cox Media Group National Content Desk

ATHENS, Ga. - A Georgia sheriff's deputy is facing a murder charge after a deadly shooting in Athens, authorities said.

According to Athens-Clarke County police, Madison County sheriff's Deputy Winford "Trey" Terrell Adams III was arrested early Monday after a 26-year-old man was found with multiple gunshot wounds Sunday evening in the 6000 block of Old Jefferson Road. The victim, whose name was not released, was taken to a nearby hospital, where he died.

Adams, 32, "was off-duty and in plain clothes at the time of the incident," police said in a news release. The shooting stemmed from a domestic dispute, according to the release.

No further information was immediately available.
