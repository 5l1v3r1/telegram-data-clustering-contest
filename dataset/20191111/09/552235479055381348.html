Death threat racist brute glassed shopkeeper

Aston Brown, 28, head-butted Mohammed Amin and plunged a Buckfast bottle into his face and head after racially abusing him over his ethnicity

11 Nov 2019, 09:44 by Ron Moore, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/ron-moore/

A former boxer attacked a shopkeeper with a Buckfast bottle in a frenzied racist attack.

Aston Brown, 28, head-butted Mohammed Amin and plunged a Buckfast bottle into his face and head after racially abusing him over his ethnicity.

Paisley Sheriff Court heard former prize fighter Brown had been thrown out of the Post Office shop – after loitering around and pestering staff – before he exploded in violence.

Prosecutor Keri Marshall said: “Mohammed Amin, witnesses Mohammed Sarwar and Fahid Amin were working in the shop along with another witness Kenneth Ogilvy, who was carrying out work outside the shop.

“At around 7.20pm, the complainer was within the shop when the accused entered. The accused asked Fahid if he could charge his mobile phone. He was told no.

“The accused then left and made his way down the side of the shop, where he continued to pace up and down.”

Brown slinked back into the shop and was again asked to leave.

The court heard Brown then lost the plot and started racially abusing staff at the store in Greenock Road, Inchinnan.

 

Ms Marshall added: “The accused was shouting and swearing such phrases as ‘f****** black b*******’ and ‘P*** b*******’ as well as ‘I will f****** kill you all’ and ‘You are all dead’.

“The complainer went to the shop door along with witness Ogilvy.

“The accused ran back towards the complainer and headbutted him.

“The complainer and Ogilvy grabbed hold of him. They were struggling with him and trying to restrain him.

“The accused repeatedly punched the complainer on the body. The complainer seized hold of the accused, but stumbled and fell.

“The accused then struck the complainer on the head with a Buckfast bottle, which he had removed from his jacket pocket.

“The bottle broke as a result of this assault.

“The accused then repeatedly tried to strike the complainer on the body with the broken bottle.”

 

Witnesses including Fahid, who is Mohammed’s brother, and Mr Sarwar, went to the victim’s aid to help fight off Brown, who legged it from Inchinnan in the direction of Renfrew.

The court heard Mr Amin was taken to the Royal Alexandra Hospital, in Paisley, were he received seven stitches to a wound above his left eye and four stitches to another gash on the back of his head.

The doctor who treated the victim said the injuries will result in permanent scarring.

Brown, who used to box professionally, pleaded guilty to charges of assault to severe injury and permanent disfigurement, and making racially-prejudiced threats and comments to his victim on December 3, 2017.

Brown, from Govan, also admitted further charges of resisting police and arrest and taking a vehicle without the owner’s permission, when he was pulled over by cops on the A8 near Bishopton on December 27, 2018.

Defence agent Paul Lynch said his client realises “these are very serious matters,” and reserved his submission in defence of Brown until after the court had obtained background reports.

Sheriff Tom McCartney warned “all options remain open” in sentencing him and Brown will discover his fate on December 10.
