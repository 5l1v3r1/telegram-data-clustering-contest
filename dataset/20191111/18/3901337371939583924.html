Messi Breaks Another Record, Opens Gap Over Ronaldo

11 Nov 2019, 18:30 by SL International

Lionel Messi's incredible hat-trick against Celta Vigo on Saturday saw him break a host of records, one of which has seen him open a gap over long-time rival Cristiano Ronaldo.

The FC Barcelona superstar inspired his side to a 4-1 LaLiga victory over Celta with yet another hat-trick, included in which were two well-taken free-kick goals.

Messi's treble saw him become the first player to reach the milestone of 40 goals in a calendar year (across club and country) for the 11th straight time, having first done so back in 2009.

In doing so, the Argentina superstar also extended the gap over arch-rival Ronaldo, who has scored 40 or more goals in each of the past nine calendar years.

The Juventus forward, whose own record stretches back to 2010, currently has 29 goals to his name in 2019, and looks unlikely to reach the milestone a 10th time before the year draws to a close.

Messi's treble also saw him equal Ronaldo's all-time record for the most hat-tricks in LaLiga history, with 34, and took his overall career tally to 52.
