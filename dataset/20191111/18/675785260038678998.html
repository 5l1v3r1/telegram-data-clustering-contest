Chelsea assistant Jody Morris mocks Jose Mourinho over latest comments

Frank Lampard's no.2 reacted sarcastically to Mourinho's suggestion that Chelsea could come unstuck against other big sides after some recent results

11 Nov 2019, 18:17 by Darren Wells, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/darren-wells/

Chelsea's assistant manager Jody Morris has mocked the club's former boss Jose Mourinho following his latest comments about the Blues.

The Stamford Bridge club have enjoyed a decent run of form which has propelled them to joint-second in the table.

Alongside manager Frank Lampard, no.2 Morris has seen the Blues win their last seven league matches, including a well-earned victory against Crystal Palace on Saturday.

Yet Mourinho still had concerns for his former employers, expressing his fears they struggle against the other top sides in the division.

Mourinho said on Sky Sports: "In the first weekend I was worried and I'm still worried with the big matches."

He then cited Chelsea 's two defeats to Man Utd, their loss at home to Liverpool , and the midweek draw with Ajax where they conceded four, as question marks over whether Chelsea had the required pragmatism to challenge the top teams.

But Morris clearly disagreed with the Portuguese coach, sharing the video of his comments on Twitter , adding: "Jose is still worried," followed by laughing emoji's.

Mourinho claimed he was "curious" to see how Chelsea would get on in their next fixture - a difficult trip to defending champions Man City at the Etihad.

It is not the first time this season that Mourinho has expressed a slightly negative opinion of the work of his former player Lampard - who admitted they had been in contact.

Lampard told talkSPORT last month: "With Jose we would have texts from time to time. We had some at the start of the season around the Manchester United game where he had a little go.

"It's all good. I've sat in the punditry chair for a year or so. I understand the need to say things so I'm certainly not going to bang on too much."

However Mourinho did concede that he can see success for Lampard and his young side.

He added: "I'm curious to see if they found that great empathy between the good football they play and the pragmatism that the top teams must have to play against top teams.

"When they find that I think they have a great team for the future. I don't believe in them to finish top two, but I believe very seriously for them to finish top four."
