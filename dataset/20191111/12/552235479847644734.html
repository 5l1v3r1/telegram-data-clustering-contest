Green Brigade are disrespecting lost Celtic heroes and should be ashamed of their vile poppy chants - Hotline

Gavin Berry answered your calls and many Celtic fans were unimpressed with the behaviour of their ultras group.

11 Nov 2019, 12:07 by Sports Hotline, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/sports-hotline/

The annual poppy row involving Celtic fans erupted again as the Green Brigade came under fire for their conduct during their side’s clash with Motherwell. 

Celtic’s ultras group opted to stay away during the Remembrance Day minute’s silence before singing an anti-poppy chant when they finally entered Parkhead. 

The Hoops won 2-0 against the Steelmen to remain a goal ahead of rivals Rangers, who beat Livingston by the same scoreline, at the top of the Scottish Premiership. 

But yet again a section of the support came under fire over a lack of respect to those who died in conflict with one Celtic supporter on the Hotline even criticising his fellow fans. 

Tomas Marshall emailed: “I went to the Motherwell game with a poppy on and was slaughtered by my fellow Celtic fans. How disrespectful and ignorant these people are. 

“They have no place in society, never mind football. This must be highlighted because it’s a total disgrace and happens every year.”

John Thomson emailed: “At football matches all over the country at the weekend a minute’s silence silence was observed with the respect and gratitude it merited and deserved. 

“The vast majority at Celtic Park also observed the tribute with dignity but not long into the game we could hear the Green Brigade signing vile anti-poppy chants.

“This is the same group of people who unfurled a banner a few years ago stating that they did not respect the British army or their poppies. 

“At the time Peter Lawwell stated the club would weed out these people and ban them from Celtic Park but to date nobody has ever been banned. 

“Obviously money is more important than the memory of those who died in two world conflicts.”

James Forsyth, Bellshill, said: “How long can Celtic put up with the Green Brigade? Celts died in the Great War and many fans are in the forces today. 

“Stay away if they want but cut out the vile chants. I wonder what Sir Rod Stewart thinks of them now.”

Samuel Logan, Peterborough, said: “For a team that lost players in the Great War it is unbelievable that a section of the Celtic support disrespect Remembrance Day.”

Jock Gray, Falkirk, said: “Do the morons in the Green Brigade not realise that their poppy protest is an insult to all the Celtic fans and former players who died for them.”

Mark Lambert, Elgin, said: “I’m disgusted with the anti-poppy song that came from the Green Brigade during the Motherwell match. 

“They need to remember ex-Celtic players gave their lives for us during world wars.”

Mark Watson, Rutherglen, said: “Can someone explain why Celtic are practically the only team in Britain who don’t wear poppies on their shirts? It’s a disgrace.”

On the park, it’s nip and tuck at the top of the table but Steve McSherry, Uddingston, said: “As a Celtic fan I’m disappointed that Hibs, Hearts and Aberdeen have fallen away. We need teams to present a challenge to keep us on our toes. 

“Rangers are giving us a bit of a challenge but I can’t see them sustaining that throughout the season.”

But Gers fans are confident their team can keep pace with their rivals and Alan McGaw, Alloa, said: “It was a fantastic seven days for Rangers with three wins in three different competitions and no goals conceded. 

“Alfredo Morelos was once again the golden boy as he continues to do his talking with his feet. He’s doing what he’s paid to do which is score goals.

“He was maybe slightly offside but I couldn’t believe he scored from what looked like an impossible angle.”

Steven Gerrard called for referee protection before the trip to West Lothian after Joe Aribo picked up a nasty head knock on their previous visit. 

Harry Fitzpatrick, Canada, emailed: “When will the SFA have a word with Steven Gerrard? His statements before the Livingston game regarding protection from his players were out of order. 

“He was trying to intimidate the referee. Who does this guy think he is?”

Jim Robertson emailed: “In the space of a week we’ve had Connor Goldson and Joe Aribo saying that Scottish football is physical. 

“If we want to succeed in Europe at club and international level then we have to, not abandon that aspect of our game but concentrate more on skill and quality. 

“People may say that is part of the Scottish game but it is also what is holding us back.”
