Spider-Man Far From Home Blu-ray special features: Are they worth a look?

Spider-Man Far From Home is out on Blu-ray now - but do the special features provide all that a fan desires

11 Nov 2019, 20:39 by Lewis Knight, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/lewis-knight/

The latest Spidey outing was very popular with MCU fans.

Spider-Man Far From Home continued the story of Tom Holland as Spider-Man/Peter Parker and saw him join MJ (Zendaya) and his friends go on a trip around Europe but face disaster from the inter-dimensional threat of The Elementals.

However, the threat seemed to face being extinguished by the actions of Mysterio/Quentin Beck (Jake Gyllenhaal).

But this new hero wasn't all he seemed.

The reviews were glowing for the film, but does the new Blu-ray release provide anything extra to the experience for Spider-Man fans?

Here's what you need to know about the special features.

Spider-Man Far From Home Blu-ray special features

Original Short: “Peter’s To-Do List”:  A special short film that takes place during Far From Home and sees Peter run some errands before the class trip.

"Teacher’s Travel Tips" Featurette:  Mr. Harrington & Mr. Dell give a guide to travelling around Europe.

"Far, Far, Far From Home" Featurette:  Go behind the scenes on all the exciting locations in the film and how they played such an important role.

"It Takes Two" Featurette:  Looking at the chemistry between director Jon Watts and Tom Holland.

"Fury & Hill" Featurette:  MCU regulars Cobie Smulders and Samuel L. Jackson look at the return of Agent Maria Hill & Nick Fury - or do they?!

"The Ginter-Riva Effect" Featurette:  How character William Ginter-Riva ties Spider-Man: Far From Home all the way back to the beginning of the MCU.

"Thank You, Mrs. Parker" Featurette:  The journey of adapting the character of Aunt May, as played by Marisa Tomei.

"Stepping Up" Featurette:  Tom Holland explores how Spider-Man was introduced into the MCU and where the character goes next.

"Suit Up" Featurette:  A special look at all the costumes Spidey wears in the film

"The Brother’s Trust" Featurette:  A look at the charity The Brother's Trust and the work of Tom Holland and his brothers with it.

"The Jump-Off" Featurette:  The cast and crew go behind the scenes on the stunt work." “Now You See Me” Featurette:  The evolution of Mysterio and casting Jake Gyllenhaal.

Stealthy Easter Eggs:  E.D.I.T.H. reveals some of the hidden Easter Eggs you may have missed!

Select Scene Pre-Vis:  A side-by-side comparison of the Pre-Vis with the film.

Gag Reel & Outtakes

Never-Before-Seen Alternate & Extended Scenes

Are they worth it?

If you are a fan of Spider-Man, particularly Tom Holland's iteration of Peter Parker then this will be a must-have.

“Peter’s To-Do List” is a particularly fun little episode of Parker’s everyday double-life as Spider-Man, as Peter goes about errands for his upcoming trip, but we are then also treated to a colourful action scene previously only glimpsed in trailers. Seeing Spidey interact with the police is also a fun change-up and explores the cockier side of the character.

Of the many featurettes on the disc, we also get the informative and intriguing discussions in “Stepping Up” which see Marvel Studios chief Kevin Feige, Tom Holland, and other architects of this version of Spider-Man discuss his place in the Marvel Cinematic Universe going into Far From Home and where the character will go next. This future that is teased by Holland and co also feels more gratifying now that we know his place in the MCU is assured.

Some of the other clips are exactly what they say on the tin, but it is exciting for fans of characters such as Aunt May Parker and Mysterio aka Quentin Beck get attention from producers and actors as they discuss their transition from comics to the film.

Sadly, the features are let down by rather forgettable deleted scenes which expand on some of the comedy elements but provide no extra insight into plot or character.

Thankfully, the biggest treat will be the hilarious Outtakes and Gag reel which especially utilises the fantastic chemistry Tom Holland and Jake Gyllenhaal.

Overall, the Spider-Man Far From Home will be worth purchasing for fans of MCU Spidey as they will get the latest chapter of his story alongside a whole host of features that will add to understanding the web-slinger's adventure and its characters.
