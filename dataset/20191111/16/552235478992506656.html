Brexit party in chaos as former MEP says Nigel Farage ignored Scotland

Former MEP David Coburn said he's ditched support for the party in a row over support for the union.

11 Nov 2019, 16:43 by Andy Philip, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/andy-philip/

 Scotland’s first Brexit party politician has turned against his former allies in a brutal verdict on Nigel Farage’s leadership.

David Coburn said the party had ignored Scotland and the union - and threatened to campaign against them.

Coburn exposed a party in meltdown over how to deliver its key commitment to leaving the EU .

 

And he revealed a split on where to let Tories have a free run in constituencies.

In an online backlash, the former MEP said: “I am sick and tired of Scotland being ignored and sidelined within the Brexit party and I no longer have to keep quiet.”

He spoke out shortly before Farage revealed the Brexit Party won’t contest 317 seats won by Tories at the last election.

But they will still contest Labour seats, potentially helping the SNP in Scottish marginals.

Corburn urged voters to back the Labour candidate in his home patch, Kirkcaldy and Cowdenbeath, previously held by Gordon Brown.

Coburn said there is “no evidence” of a Labour-SNP pact to allow a second independence referendum.

Asked if he no longer supports Farage’s single-issue party, he replied: “Definitely I don’t.”

 

Humiliated Farage caved in during a hasty press conference, claiming standing against Tories in their 317 seats would lead to a hung parliament, where no party has overall control.

Farage said: "That action, our announcement today, prevents a second (EU) referendum. That right now is the most important single thing in our country.

“In a sense we now have a Leave alliance. We’ve just done it unilaterally."

Coburn was elected as a Ukip MEP under Farage’s leadership in 2014.

He joined the Brexit Party in February but did not stand at the European election.
