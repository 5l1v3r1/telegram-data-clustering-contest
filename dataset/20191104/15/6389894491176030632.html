US factory orders fall in September as trade war bites

04 Nov 2019, 15:13 by Harry Robertson

US factories suffered a steeper-than-expected fall in orders in September, survey data has shown, suggesting the country’s manufacturing sector is being held back by the US-China trade war.

Factory goods orders fell 0.6 per cent last month, following a 0.1 per cent drop in August, the US Commerce Department said today. The reading was below analysts’ forecast of 0.5 per cent.

Manufacturing and business investment in the US have been hard-hit by the trade war President Donald Trump has waged on China, which he accuses of abusing the international trade system.
