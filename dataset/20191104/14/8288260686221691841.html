'Dominated' - Aston Villa boss makes this Wolves claim

Aston Villa latest news | Here's what Dean Smith has said about Wolves ahead of the Premier League meeting between the two sides at Molineux on Sunday afternoon

04 Nov 2019, 14:23 by Ashley Preece, https://www.facebook.com/PreeceBirminghamLive/, Richard Cusack

Aston Villa make the short 21-mile trip to West Midlands rivals Wolverhampton Wanderers on Sunday (2pm) for their latest Premier League clash.

Dean Smith 's side will be hoping to put an end to their two-match losing run following defeats to Manchester City and Liverpool, the latter a gut-wrenching 2-1 defeat at Villa Park on Saturday.

Villa and Wolves are well-acquainted after their recent dalliances in the Championship, both sides earning promotion one year after the other back to the Premier League.

And now they will meet in the top flight for the first time since 2012, when Villa secured a 3-2 win at Molineux.

The rivalry was stoked last week, when Villa dumped Wolves out of the Carabao Cup in the fourth round win to set up a quarter-final tie with Liverpool.

And Smith offered his verdict on the match when he was quizzed about Wolves in the press conference afterward.

"We dominated the football, both teams made a lot changes," he said .

"My only gripe is we didn't put the game to bed earlier with all the possession we've had and, as can be just one goal ahead, we gave them opportunities to score and they took that and had a helping hand, we had a crazy two minutes.

"Jed (Steer) made a good save and we saw the game out comfortably."
