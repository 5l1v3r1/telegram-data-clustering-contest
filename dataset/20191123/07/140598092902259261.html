Where to find the best Black Friday 2019 deals, including top offers from Amazon, John Lewis and Currys

23 Nov 2019, 07:58 by Telegraph Reporters

Even though Black Friday 2019 officially starts next Friday, November 29, the retailers have got a jump-start this year. Yesterday, exactly a week before the official start date, Amazon, Currys PC World and John Lewis launched their Black Friday deals, and there some are serious savings to be made.

So far, there are big discounts on TVs, tech, fashion and everything in between. Last year, customers saved £40 million on Amazon alone, and, from looking at the deals that have launched today, it is easy to see why. 

With so many deals already on offer, sale-loving shoppers are truly spoilt for choice this year. But before you start feeling overwhelmed by this choice, we have prepared the following guide on where to find the best Black Friday deals this year.

Where can I find the best Black Friday deals?

It's hardly surprising that Amazon leads the pack. The retail giant is practically synonymous with Black Friday as it not only brought Black Friday to the UK, but it offers the widest range of deals in the widest range of categories - including TVs, tech, mobile phones, appliances, laptops, tablets, consoles, smart devices and more.

Both John Lewis and Currys PC World price-match many of their competitors' best deals, making Currys an easy one-stop-shop for tech and John Lewis a worthwhile destination for higher-end products.

Argos is a good go-to for smart tech and consoles, and last year's Black Friday sale marked their biggest-ever day for video gaming. It's also worth keeping an eye on other cross-category retailers, such as Very and AO, which work hard to beat Amazon's prices every year - especially for home appliances. 

Even British supermarkets have joined in on the hype. Asda, Tesco, Morrisons, Sainsbury's, Aldi and Lidl have all held their own events in the past, offering a wide range of deals on groceries, clothing, tech and homeware. In fact, Asda's Black Friday sale in 2013 resulted in chaos as customers physically fought for televisions and gadgets. This has since led the supermarket, owned by American retailer Walmart, to offer promotions over a longer period instead. 

Last year, Tesco reduced the price of toys, TVs and mobile phones, while Morrisons offered discounts on perfume, wine and spirits. Sainsbury's also ran deals on their TU clothing range, so we can expect similar deals to appear in the 2019 sales.
