Ballarat Cup 2019 | fashion, races, crowd, live coverage

23 Nov 2019, 07:00

That's a wrap

Thousands of people dressed up in their fanciest frocks and suits to attend the Ballarat Cup on Saturday afternoon.

As the champagne was poured, the best dressed took to the stage to vie for the much sought after prizes and others keenly watched each race, the atmosphere was merry and everyone enjoyed themselves.

See who was at the cup here.

Police praised punter behaviour, with the crowds well-behaved throughout the day.

However, there was one arrest for public drunkenness after the last race.

Police had a visible presence at the event, based out of the police bus, with almost 30 police officers patrolling on foot throughout the day.

The police presence has now moved into Ballarat CBD, while Ballarat Highway Patrol are conducting breath and drug tests on roads around Ballarat, to ensure everybody gets home safely.

Food vendors reported slightly less business than last year.

Cafe Buzz owner Kylie Bullen takes her coffee cart to all of the events throughout the year but believes there were less people wandering around than last year's cup.

She poured about 400 cups of coffee for people throughout the day and less donuts than usual.

Over at the Hamburger Cart, about 300 trays of chips, 150 hamburgers, 300 dim sims and 200 donuts were packaged.

Kiwia wins again

Locally-trained Kiwia has won a second successive Ballarat Cup in a result that left his trainer Archie Alexander a little stunned.

Kiwia won last year's Ballarat Cup over 2200m for Darren Weir but was transferred to Alexander in February after a four-year disqualification handed his previous trainer.

The gelding was having his sixth start for Alexander, fifth for the preparation, with the trainer searching for answers prior to Saturday's Listed race over 2000m.

As he did last last year, John Allen guided Kiwia ($16) to victory, this time defeating Double You Tee ($9.50) by a short-head with Another Dollar ($20) a half-length away third.

"It's a special win as it's a hometown cup," Alexander said.

"And it's with a horse that we came here with not overly confident about. He hadn't won in 12 months and he's had his issues.

"I refer to him as the Roger Federer of the stable as it's hard to keep him in one piece but he came here today into the mounting yard bouncing.

"I don't know why he peaked on this day, but for whatever reason he was flying."
