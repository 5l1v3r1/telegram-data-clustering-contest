Boxing tonight: Schedule, TV channels, live streams, fight times, undercards and odds

Callum Smith and Deontay Wilder defend their respective world titles tonight against John Ryder and Luis Ortiz

23 Nov 2019, 07:00 by Martin Domin, https://www.facebook.com/mirrorfighting

There are world title fights galore tonight as boxing fans are treated to two championship bouts on either side of the Atlantic.

In Liverpool, Callum Smith defends his super-middleweight title against domestic rival John Ryder

It is just Smith's second fight since he won his belt from George Groves in the final of the World Boxing Super Series in Saudi Arabia last year.

A few hours later in Las Vegas, Wilder Deontay Wider will take on Luis Ortiz for a second time in a risky heavyweight rematch.

Wilder is not only putting his world title on the line but also a second fight with Tyson Fury which is scheduled for February 22.

Here is everything you need to know about tonight's fights...

Callum Smith vs John Ryder

Fight time

The fight will start between 10pm and 10.30pm but may start earlier if the undercard fights finish prematurely.

The fights can be streamed on Sky Sports via the Sky Go app either on mobile devices or through your browser.

Deontay Wilder vs Luis Ortiz

Fight time

The fight will start at 5am UK time which is 9pm in Las Vegas where the fight is being held. Ring walks are unlikely to take place earlier, even if the undercard fights finish ahead of schedule.

TV channel

The fight is being broadcast on Sky Sports in the UK. Sky Sports Action will show the fight with the programme starting at 2am.

Is there a live stream?

The fight can be streamed via the Sky Go app, either on mobile devices or on the Sky Sports website.
