How Jose Mourinho fared in his first games in charge of previous clubs

The Portuguese will take charge of Tottenham for the first time since replacing Mauricio Pochettino when they go to West Ham in Saturday's early kick-off

23 Nov 2019, 07:30 by Mark Jones, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/mark-jones/

Guess who's back? Back again.

You can't have missed the news the week, as Jose Mourinho makes his first return to Premier League football following his sacking by Manchester United in December last year.

Mourinho has replaced Mauricio Pochettino in the Tottenham Hotspur hotseat following the Argentinean's somewhat surprise departure on Tuesday.

And as the former Chelsea and United boss returns to the English top-flight in a London derby at West Ham in Saturday's early kick-off, we thought we'd take a look back at how he fared in his first games in charge of his previous clubs.

As you'd expect, they've been pretty eventful.

Benfica

Boavista 1-0 Benfica - September 23, 2000

The Lisbon giants somewhat surprisingly turned to assistant Mourinho to replace German boss Jupp Heynckes in 2000, giving the 37-year-old his first job as a No.1.

He got off to an inauspicious start though, losing to an early Duda goal as his future Chelsea midfielder Maniche was sent off.

Leiria

Leiria 2-2 Santa Clara - August 19, 2001

He was to last just three months at Benfica though, before returning the following season with unfashionable Leiria.

They would start the 2001/02 campaign with a draw at home to Santa Clara, before eventually finishing seventh in the league despite Mourinho leaving halfway through the season.

Porto

Porto 2-1 Maritimo - January 26, 2002

He caught the eye of Porto after impressing at Leiria, and began with a win after replacing Octavio Machado in the hotseat.

Soon-to-be Tottenham flop Helder Postiga grabbed the winner in a 2-1 success.

Chelsea (first spell)

Chelsea 1-0 Manchester United - August 15, 2004

After anointing himself as 'The Special One' Mourinho arrived in England with a bang as Chelsea served notice to Sir Alex Ferguson's Manchester United and the rest of the Premier League.

It took Eidur Gudjohnsen just 14 minutes to grab the game's only goal.

Inter Milan

Inter Milan 2-2 Roma (won 6-5 on penalties) - August 24, 2008

Mario Balotelli was on target as Mourinho began life in Italy by picking up the Italian Super Cup on penalties in Milan.

A week later he'd make his Serie A bow, with Zlatan Ibrahimovic striking in a 1-1 draw at Sampdoria.

Real Madrid

Mallorca 0-0 Real Madrid - August 29, 2010

The Portuguese had to settle for a point on his La Liga debut at Mallorca, who ground out a goalless draw.

Mourinho's Madrid would go on to win 29 of the following 37 league games, but they missed out on the title to Barcelona by four points.

Chelsea (second spell)

Chelsea 2-0 Hull City - August 18, 2013

Oscar and Frank Lampard were the goal heroes as Mourinho came back to Chelsea with a victory in 2013, when he was feted like a returning hero.

The Blues would go on to finish third that season behind Manchester City and Liverpool .

Manchester United

Manchester United 2-1 Leicester - August 7, 2016

Mourinho was walking into the reigning FA Cup holders when he joined Manchester United in 2016, and one of his summer signings proved the difference against Leicester in the Community Shield.

Ibrahimovic grabbed the winner with seven minutes remaining, with the Swede also scoring a week later as United beat Bournemouth 3-1 in the Premier League.
