Jeremy Corbyn defends 'neutral' Brexit stance and calls it a 'sign of strength'

23 Nov 2019, 19:24

Jeremy Corbyn has defended his ‘neutral stance’ towards a second Brexit referendum, calling it a sign of ‘strength and maturity’.

The Labour leader shrugged off Tory criticism accusing him of ‘deciding to be indecisive’ on the issue at the heart of next month’s general election.

During a stop on the campaign trail, Mr Corbyn told voters in Sheffield he was offering them a ‘sensible way forward’ which could unite communities.

‘I think being an honest broker and listening to everyone is actually a sign of strength and a sign of maturity,’ he said.

‘My role as the Labour prime minister would be to ensure that is carried out in a fair way, that the offers put are fair, and that I will carry out the result of that referendum.

‘I think this is actually a sensible way forward that actually can bring people together.’

However, Health Secretary Matt Hancock said the Labour leader’s stance ‘won’t wash’ with the electorate.
