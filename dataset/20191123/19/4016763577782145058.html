DJ Toomp Talks About His Unreleased Collaboration With JAY-Z & Rihanna

23 Nov 2019, 19:00 by Caitlin Kelley

DJ Toomp has worked with some of the biggest artists in hip-hop, including T.I., Kanye West, and Wale. But the Atlanta producer just revealed that he has a joint track with JAY-Z and Rihanna in the vault.

During an interview with Revolt TV, the hitmaker was asked about his biggest unreleased track. “Rihanna and JAY-Z,” he responded. “I did a Rihanna and JAY-Z record that’s so fucking hard. This was made between 2009-2010."

He elaborated on the sound of the stashed “hit.” “There are guitars that remind you of this Beanie Sigel record, and Jay is rapping on the guitar part,” he said. “The beat breaks down with some guitars and then that’s when Rihanna comes on. They’re protecting that shit like a precious emerald.”

Toomp previously worked with Jay on “Say Hello.” Meanwhile, Rihanna’s “Numb” sampled West’s “Can’t Tell Me Nothing,” which Toomp co-produced. JAY-Z and Rihanna also worked together on “Umbrella,” “Run This Town,” and “Talk That Talk.”

Although the DJ worked on the unreleased song, he doesn’t have access to it. “What’s so crazy is [Def Jam’s] Karen only wanted to play it for me one time,” Toomp said. “She was like, ‘I’m really not supposed to be playing this for you, Toomp.’ I was like, ‘Please, let me hear this damn record.’ She played it, and I was like, ‘Play it one more time’ and she played half of it. That was my first and last time hearing it.”

Elsewhere in the interview, he was asked if he believes his frequent collaborator T.I. pioneered trap. “Come on, man. You already know I do,” he said. “I didn’t know what we were starting, but wasn’t really nobody talking about trapping on any records.”

In a recent cover story for Vogue, Rihanna described the sound of her next album. “I like to look at it as a reggae-inspired or reggae-infused album,” she said. “It’s not gonna be typical of what you know as reggae. But you’re going to feel the elements in all of the tracks.”

Back in August, JAY-Z and Roc Nation partnered with the NFL to produce the Super Bowl halftime show.
