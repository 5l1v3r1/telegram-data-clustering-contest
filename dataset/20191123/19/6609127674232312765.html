Celtic-daft Rod Stewart curls up in Hoops top after performing at Memories That Matter charity bash in London

23 Nov 2019, 19:54 by Oliver Norton

SIR ROD Stewart curled up in bed after setting the dance-floor alight with his missus.

The rock-star, 74, performed at the Memories That Matter charity bash in London to raise money for the Teenage Cancer Trust.

He also showed off his moves with wife and model Penny Lancaster, 48, who joined him on the floor.

She later took to Instagram and posted an image of her hubby in bed with his favourite Celtic top on.

She wrote: “The Celtic loving boy having a lay in after an incredible night, raising 1 million for @teenage_cancer.

“Now he’s off to Manchester for his first show of the UK tour ending just before Christmas.”

Sir Rod is set to perform three sell out shows at the SSE Hydro in Glasgow this week (on November 26, 28 and 30).

We told how Celtic-daft Sir Rod revealed his oldest brother is a Rangers fan – and that his dad has a Light Blues player named on his grave.

The rocker told how sibling Robert is a dedicated Gers supporter – but he says the pair “don’t have fights about it”.

He also said that ex-Ibrox and Scotland defender Eric Caldow is engraved on his father’s grave.
