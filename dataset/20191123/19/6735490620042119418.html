Larry King Reveals He Suffered A ‘Slight Stroke’ Earlier This Year: ‘They Thought I Was Going To Die’

23 Nov 2019, 19:29 by Jamie Samhan

Larry King has revealed that he suffered some health issues earlier this year.

While talking to Page Six, the TV host and interviewer said he had a “slight stroke” earlier this year. Reports at the time suggested King had a heart attack, a claim that his production company had denied.

“I had what they call a slight stroke in March,” King, 86, revealed. “Everything is back to normal, except my left foot. I can’t walk on my left foot, so I’m walking with a walker. It’s making me feel old. But at least it helps me get around. Another thing I can’t stand is having people do things for me. I was always someone who did things for themselves.”

King has turned to a physical therapist to help him walk on his own again.

“They thought I was going to die in March,” he added. “They told my two boys to come home. It was really touch-and-go. I don’t remember any of it, because I was out during the whole time. I had a sepsis infection, I had a new stent put on the heart. It’s been amazing—everyone tells me they can’t believe I made it through.”

But that isn’t going to slow down King who is back at work. “Being in [a] wheelchair doesn’t affect you, hosting a show,” he continued. “I’m proud to be able to do that.”
