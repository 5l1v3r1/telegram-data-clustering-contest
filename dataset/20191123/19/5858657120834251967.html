Teen shot, critically wounded by Winnipeg police during attempted holdup

23 Nov 2019, 19:36 by The Canadian Press

Summary

A 16-year-old boy is in critical, but stable condition after being shot multiple times by Winnipeg police

The shooting on Thursday, which was caught on camera, comes as police deal with a spike in violent crimes

Manitoba's police watchdog is investigating

WINNIPEG (NEWS 1130) – Winnipeg police say a 16-year-old boy is in critical condition but stable after he was shot by an officer during an attempted armed robbery at a convenience store.

The teen was wounded after officers responded to a report of a holdup at the central-area store on Thursday.

Police say a suspect was still inside when they arrived, but he left through the front door as he was carrying a weapon and was shot when he refused to obey an order to drop it.

One officer suffered a minor injury.

He was taken to hospital where he was treated and released.

The Independent Investigation Unit, Manitoba’s police watchdog agency, is looking into the shooting.
