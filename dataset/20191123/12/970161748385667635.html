First picture of British businessman gunned down outside Costa del Sol villa

23 Nov 2019, 12:05

A British expat who died instantly after being shot in the heart at a Costa del Sol holiday resort has been named.

Peter Andrew Williamson, 39, from Salford, Greater Manchester, was gunned down on Thursday as he sat in his parked car outside his luxury villa.

It is thought he was shot from another vehicle which drew up beside him and sped away from the scene.

Friends and relatives have paid tribute to Mr Williamson, nicknamed Snaggle, who is believed to have two children.

Writing on social media his aunt Carol Williamson said: ‘My caring beautiful nephew little Peter.

‘Taken from us in horrible circumstances. Gone too soon. Love you Snaggle. Always in my heart. Will never be forgotten.’

Sarah Kay Moores added: ‘Absolutely devastated and heartbroken. Still doesn’t seem real that he’s gone.’

Mr Williamson had just returned from the gym and was sitting in his UK-plated two-seater Audi when he was killed at around 3.15pm.

He was outside his home in the hills on the north side of the AP-7 motorway, running along the Costa del Sol.

A notice in Spanish at the front gate of the large detached villa says: ‘CCTV operating in this area. You may be being recorded.’

Business records show the victim was registered as a self-employed agricultural sector intermediary specialising in ‘international trade’.

Detectives are looking into the theory that the British expat’s murder was drugs related and said he had been arrested in March for using a transport firm to send cannabis resin from Malaga to an unidentified destination, according to local paper Diario Sur.

There has been no official comment from Costa del Sol police about the reports and officers have confirmed no arrests have been made.

Mr Williamson’s murder was the area’s second in less than a week.

On Friday last week, a man was shot dead on a residential estate called Andasol, a short drive from Marbella town centre and close to a beach named Alvarito Playa.

Detectives believe the killer fled in a getaway car and have not made any official comment on the victims’ nationalities, but local reports at the time said they were of ‘Arabic origin’.

The incident is being linked to a gangland drugs feud.

In January a millionaire businessman who was close to the stars of reality TV series TOWIE was shot dead outside his luxury Costa del Sol home.

Marco Yaqout, who parties with the likes of Mohamed Al Fayed , was gunned down as he drove his garage in his trademark UK-plated Bentley.

The killer was waiting outside his villa on a quiet residential street in an upmarket neighbourhood in San Pedro de Alcantara near Marbella.

At the time of his murder, the English-speaking entrepreneur was said to own at least five well known nightspots in Puerto Banus, including Linekers and TOWIE favourite TIBU.

Other violent incidents in recent months on the Costa del Sol include two attacks on Brits, the shooting of a Dutchman in a sushi restaurant and the machine-gun murder of a Frenchman outside his home.

Last September a 24-year-old Brit was stabbed twice in the legs by an assailant who attacked him in the street without warning in the popular resort of Puerto Banus.

It came ten days after a 35-year-old Brit was shot, stabbed, beaten and given a ‘Glasgow smile’ close to the same area after being abducted by a gang of criminals over a drug deal.

The torture victim was later identified as Craig Moran, jailed for 13 years for his part in a September 2003 armed raid on a jeweller’s in Nottingham in which shop owner Marian Bates was killed.
