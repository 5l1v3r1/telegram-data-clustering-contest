Verdict due on Muddler expansion plan, amid fears Grey Street will become 'another Bigg Market'

The restaurant wants to extend into a building next door and has insisted that allowing it will not see it "banging out drinks" on Grey Street

23 Nov 2019, 12:00 by Daniel Holland

Controversial plans for a city centre restaurant to double in size face a final verdict next week.

The Muddler wants to expand into an empty unit next door to its Grey Street site, opposite the Theatre Royal.

But a rival operator has warned that allowing the move will mean that Muddler “can and will become a bar”, echoing fears from residents that the historic street is becoming “another Bigg Market”.

Newcastle City Council was due to decide whether the extension could go ahead last month, but was forced to delay when a licensing hearing was postponed due to a medical emergency.

A rescheduled meeting of the authority’s licensing sub-committee is set for next Thursday, November 28, when Muddler will discover its fate.

Barrister Charles Holland, representing Muddler owners Danieli Holdings at October’s hearing, said that an expanded Muddler would have to abide by conditions ensuring that it remained a restaurant rather than a bar “banging out drinks”.

He claimed that The Muddler “is not and could not become” a bar, but wants more flexibility to allow customers to drink alcohol without having a meal.

Mr Holland said: “We have carefully thought about what to do with this very important site. It is opposite what is perhaps the most culturally important building in the city.”

Richard Arnot, representing Easteye Ltd, claimed there would be “nothing” stopping Muddler becoming a bar if these plans are approved.

Easteye’s directors, the Ladhar family, run nearby Pleased to Meet You.

One objecting resident told the council: “Grey Street, as I feared, is simply becoming another Bigg Market, which is tragic for one of the finest streets in the UK if not Europe.

“It was a prestigious street with high class shopping, banks and other establishments, now it is fast becoming one large pub with a plethora of similar establishments nearby and I don’t believe there is sufficient demand for yet another large bar in this vicinity.

“In fact, I believe the council should refuse all future applications for new licences on Grey Street.”

Northumbria Police are also objecting to the plans, saying that Grey Street already suffers from “noise, crime and disorder and anti-social behaviour”.

But Mr Holland argued that The Muddler has been “incident-free” since opening last December.
