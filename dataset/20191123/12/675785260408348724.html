What TV channel is Deontay Wilder vs Luis Ortiz on? TV and live stream details

Ortiz is bidding to upset Wilder's plans to rematch Tyson Fury next year by beating the heavyweight world champion

23 Nov 2019, 12:00 by Martin Domin, https://www.facebook.com/mirrorfighting

Deontay Wilder puts his heavyweight world title on the line tonight in his rematch with dangerous Cuban Luis Ortiz.

The pair first met 18 months ago when Ortiz rocked the champion before being stopped in the 10th round.

The challenger has vowed to make Wilder pay for accepting a second fight with him but the champion insists he has learned his lesson and will have his hand raised again.

Should he come through, Wilder will book another sequel, this time with Tyson Fury with whom he fought to a controversial draw last December.

Here is everything you need to know about tonight's fights...

What TV channel is it on?

The fight is being broadcast on Sky Sports in the UK. Sky Sports Action will show the fight with the programme starting at 2am.

What time is the fight?

The fight will start at 5am UK time which is 9pm in Las Vegas where the fight is being held. Ring walks are unlikely to take place earlier, even if the undercard fights finish ahead of schedule.

Is there a live stream?

The fight can be streamed via the Sky Go app, either on mobile devices or on the Sky Sports website.

Latest news

Tyson Fury has warned Deontay Wilder to avoid any injuries or slip-ups against Luis Ortiz this weekend.

The WBC king defends his strap in a rematch against experienced Cuban Ortiz.

But Fury is more concerned with his own money-spinning second fight with Wilder, pencilled in for February 2020.

Having suffered a nasty cut in his last outing against Otto Wallin, Fury knows the dangers faced in the ring no matter who the opponent.

He has just started training ahead of his Wilder rematch and, whilst out on a run, warned Wilder he must do his bit to keep things on track for the start of next year.

In a video message on Instagram , Fury said: "Just out running, doing a six-miler.

"February 22nd, Las Vegas, I'm coming for you baby. Training hard, training right through Christmas.

"Wilder, don't be taking any days off mush.

"Don't be getting injured in your return fight.

"Get it done and meet me for battle.

"February 22nd, Las Vegas, Nevada, 2020."
