'A fight formed' - Leeds United star reveals his role in that Aston Villa incident

23 Nov 2019, 12:00 by Shane Ireland, https://www.facebook.com/ShaneIrelandJournalist

Leeds United midfielder Pablo Hernandez has revealed the role he played in the bizarre goal Aston Villa scored at Elland Road.

In what was a vital Championship promotion clash in April, Villa went behind when Mateusz Klich scored despite Jonathan Kodjia being injured and awaiting treatment.

The goal sparked fury and Anwar El-Ghazi was shown a red card after a clash with Patrick Bamford – although if was later rescinded with the Leeds striker being handed a ban.

In response to his players’ unsportsmanlike behaviour Leeds head coach Marcelo Bielsa demanded they allow Villa to walk the ball into the net to equalise.

Albert Adomah ran from the halfway line and slotted home, despite the best efforts of Swedish defender Pontus Jansson to prevent him doing so.

Hernandez, it turns out, was the player who revealed Bielsa’s decision to the rest of his team-mates.

“There was a lot of tension,” he said in an interview with Marca. “At the moment we got the goal a fight formed. I had a yellow and I didn’t want to take a chance.

“At that time, Bielsa called me and transmitted to me, since we speak the same language, what he thought, what he felt we had to do at that time. I passed it to the teammates.

“Also the captain, who through the assistants was given that instruction from the bench.

“Bielsa is our leader, the coach, we support his decision and we decided to let them score a goal to tie. They are things that also define your personality, our mentality and that have a lot of repercussion. It was a moment in a game, that decision was made and we did the right thing.”

The game ended 1-1 and Villa went on to achieve promotion the following month by beating Derby County at Wembley, after Leeds had lost against the Rams over two legs in the play-off semi-final.
