Joe Schmidt was delighted when Tubridy presented him with a special guest

23 Nov 2019, 12:53 by Nick Fitzgerald

Speaking on The Late Late Show last night, the Kiwi admitted he was nervous returning to Ireland following their defeat to Japan

Last night Joe Schmidt sat down with Ryan Tubridy for Friday's airing of The Late Late Show. The New Zealander was visibly delighted when the Late Late host brought out a special guest.

In particular, Schmidt discussed sport and in his time with Ireland in the Rugby World Cup.

After Ireland's disappointing defeat to Japan, the Kiwi admitted he was quite nervous returning to the Emerald Isle and he feared what the public would think.

Schmidt's leadership over the Irish rugby team ended in a crushing fashion but overall, he was a huge success and will go down as a legend of Irish rugby.

Speaking about the nerves, Schmidt told Ryan:

"I was disappointed. I didn't really want to meet people and have them disappointed as well.

"You know when you get off the flight you're going to walk straight into cameras that are at the airport. We'd been up at 3am to catch our flight and I didn't really catch our flight.

"You get in - and I'm not sure what I said in the interview now - but you're probably not at your peak.

"I did hide away for a while. And my wife said let's get out of town then. We went to Spain for a few days which was great."

As the conversation was coming to a close, Ryan said "I just don't feel like finishing this just yet."

A special guest then popped up out of the blue, much to the delight of Joe Schmidt. The guest was none other than the man himself Brian O'Driscoll.

The sports legend praised the impact Schmidt has had on Irish rugby over his career and presented the former Ireland coach with a signed copy of Seamus Heaney's poetry as a farewell gift.
