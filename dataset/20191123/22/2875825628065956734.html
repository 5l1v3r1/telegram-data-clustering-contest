Michael Owen and Alan Shearer set to appear on TV together months after furious Twitter row

EXCLUSIVE: The two former Newcastle players clashed on social media following the release of Owen's autobiography after he admitted he regretted his move to Tyneside

23 Nov 2019, 22:30 by John Richardson

Warring pundits Alan Shearer and Michael Owen are set to go head-to-head in what could prove to be a Boxing Day bust-up.

The former Newcastle United team-mates fell out after Shearer accused Owen of being reluctant to play in the finale to the 2008-09 season when Toon were relegated from the Premier League.

Owen hit back in his autobiography insisting he was being blamed for then Toon caretaker boss Shearer’s managerial shortcomings.

A flurry of strongly-worded text messages between the feuding pair followed and the fall-out still hasn’t been repaired.

But Amazon Prime, who are televising 20 Premier League games in December, are now trying to line up the pair to be studio pundits for the Boxing Day clash between Manchester United and Newcastle.

Bosses are braced for sparks to fly but know it could prove compulsive viewing, with the broadcaster keen to make a big impact during their month in the spotlight.

An Amazon insider said: “It would be great to have both of them in the studio but we would settle for one being in the studio and the other one alongside the match commentator.”

While Shearer is a legend at Newcastle after 206 goals in 405 games for his boyhood club, Owen made nowhere near the same impression, only making 79 appearances in four seasons, with a return of 30 goals.

After linking to a video of Owen explaining to BT Sport how much he hated his time at Newcastle and wanted to retire, Shearer wrote on Twitter : "Yes Michael, we thought that also, whilst on £120k a week....."

Owen responded: "Not sure you are as loyal to Newcastle and you make out mate. I distinctly remember you being inches away from signing for Liverpool after Sir Bobby Robson put you on the bench You tried everything to get out."
