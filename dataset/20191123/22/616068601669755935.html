Rockets sweep U.S. trip

23 Nov 2019, 22:20 by Wayne Moore

The Kelowna Rockets returned home from a brief two-game swing through Washington State with two more marks in the win column.

Leif Mattson provided all the offence the Rockets would need Saturday in a 2-1 victory over Tri-City.

Things happened fast and furiously. In fact, fans who showed up a few ticks late missed most of the offence these two teams produced.

The action started after Tri-City was handed a penalty just 83 seconds in.

Just 15 seconds after the call, Krystof Hrabik beat Roman Basran with a shorthanded goal, putting the home side up a goal.

Mattson tied it moments later on the same power play. He jumped out of the way of a point shot, turned and banged at the loose puck.

It eventually went off a Tri-City defender and goaltender Beck Warm and into the net.

The game stayed that way until the the early seconds of the third.

With the Rockets shorthanded to start the third, Mattson poked the puck away from a Tri-City player at center.

Mattson broke in all alone and beat Warm with a wrist shot from in close.

Basran shut the door the rest of the way, handing the Rockets their fourth win in a row.

The veteran netminder turned away 24 shots as the Rockets outshot Try-City 29-25.

The Rockets will host Edmonton Tuesday and Prince George Saturday.

The Cougars did the Rockets and the rest of the BC Division a big favour, taking two from Kamloops on the weekend.

Those two defeat, coupled with the Rockets two victories moves Kelowna to within two points of the division-leading Blazers.
