'ROSIE WAS LET DOWN'

Mum of cancer patient who died in scandal-hit Glasgow hospital brands facility as ‘sick’

23 Nov 2019, 22:23 by Kevin Duguid and Oliver Norton

A CANCER treatment room at Glasgow’s crisis-hit Royal Hospital for Children has still not opened — more than four years after it was built.

And now the mum of a cancer patient who died at the scandal-hit hospital has branded the facility “sick”.

Donna-Louise Hurrell, 40, believes daughter Rosie Veronica Mitchell, who passed away in January aged seven, could have benefited from care at a hub designed to tackle neuroblastoma.

But we can reveal the MIBG cancer treatment room will not open until at least the spring at Glasgow’s Royal Hospital for Children — part of the Queen Elizabeth University campus plagued by infection outbreaks.

Donna-Louise, of Port Glasgow, Renfrewshire, claimed vital chemotherapy for Rosie was delayed at least twice after bug problems at the facility, which opened in June 2015.

The mum said: “Rosie fought as hard as she could but in the end she was let down.

“She was treated latterly in a ‘sick building’ where she was so busy having to deal with the effects of infection issues that she didn’t always have the energy or support needed to fight the cancer.”

Donna-Louise claims the lead-lined room is in ward 2A — one of two paediatric cancer wards at the Royal Hospital for Children.

The sites have been closed since last September following a string of infections and are not expected to reopen until at least March — along with the treatment room.

The mum said: “Rosie potentially could have benefited from it but instead had to go on the waiting list to get it elsewhere.

“Rosie was put on the waiting list in London. By the time she got there in January her cancer had progressed so far she couldn’t get the treatment.

“If that room had been available in that hospital she would have got treatment quicker. Would that have made a difference to her outcome?”

The £842million superhospital plunged into government control this week.

Donna-Louise blasted the lack of transparency and care by NHS Greater Glasgow and Clyde.

She said: “The health board are not being as open and transparent as they should be.

“I don’t think the Government are doing a good enough of holding them to account at all.

“Nobody has written to me to apologise for what Rosie had to go through in that hospital. It should never have happened.”

The MIBG room was built with the hospital in 2015. It will see nuclear medicine scans used to check for changes in the adrenal glands and surrounding tissues.

It’s understood that the therapy is only available currently at the University College Hospital and the Royal Marsden hospitals in London.

Rosie potentially could have benefited from it but instead had to go on the waiting list to get it elsewhere.Donna-Louise Hurrell

Another parent said the special room “has never been in use”. They claimed funding was approved last year to train staff and carry out work.

A source added: “The point of having it in the new hospital was so children wouldn’t have to travel to London for it. It would have reduced the waiting list for kids.”

Labour’s health spokeswoman Monica Lennon said: “The MIBG room must be fully resourced and the safe reopening of the children’s cancer wards should be the Government’s top priority.”

A spokeswoman for the health board said the MIBG room was built as a “potential new” service.

She added: “Work is under way to upgrade the MIBG room in readiness for the launch of the service in spring next year when wards 2A and 2B reopen.”

A Scottish Government spokesman said: “We understand NHS Greater Glasgow and Clyde are working to ensure that both the room and the surrounding environment are ready to admit patients both safely and as soon as possible.”
