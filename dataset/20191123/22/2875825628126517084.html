Borussia Dortmund join transfer race to sign Tottenham teenager Troy Parrott

The 17-year-old made his first-team debut for Spurs in their Carabao Cup game against Colchester in September and also won his first Republic of Ireland cap earlier this month

23 Nov 2019, 22:30 by Neil Fissler

Borussia Dortmund want to get a jump on arch-rivals Bayern Munich in the race to land Tottenham Hotspur youngster Troy Parrott.

Dortmund have been boosted by the successful signing of England international Jadon Sancho from Manchester City two years ago, and are looking to potentially repeat the trick with the signing of the highly-rated Parrott.

They are ready to go head-to-head with Bayern, Juventus and Real Madrid to sign the 17-year-old, who Spurs bought as a schoolboy from Irish side Belvedere two years ago.

He also helped Ireland to a win against New Zealand on his senior debut for his country earlier this month, creating an assist in their 2-1 win.

He has impressed at youth level, and signed his first professional contract with Spurs in February.

That runs out in 2021, however, and some of Europe's elite clubs are looking to take advantage of the situation.

The top German clubs are keeping a close eye on young English talent, and Bayern tried to swoop for Chelsea's Callum Hudson-Odoi in the last two windows before he eventually signed a new long-term deal with the Blues in September.

It remains to be seen what new Spurs manager Jose Mourinho makes of talent, and whether he sees him as a potential challenger for first-team places in the near future.

Parrott did not get off to the best of starts with Mourinho, however, when he allegedly 'liked' a post on Twitter criticising the Portuguese manager.

Metro reported that a post which read: "Troy Parrott come to Manchester United you're 'f****** with Jose" was 'liked' by Parrott, before he seemingly quickly changed his mind and undid the action.
