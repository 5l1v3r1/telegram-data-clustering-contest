Carrie Underwood Celebrates Barnes & Noble Vinyl Weekend With Her ‘Cry Pretty’ Picture Album Exclusive

Jane Lasky

On Halloween, Carrie Underwood completed her “Cry Pretty 360 Tour” which drew sellout crowds all over the nation and in Canada. Now, the country crooner is inviting those fans and others who weren’t able to snag a ticket to any of her recent concerts to listen to the music she performed in a special way. Her latest Instagram post offered Carrie’s 9.2 million fans and followers the chance to celebrate Barnes & Noble’s three-day vinyl weekend ending on November 24 by offering her Cry Pretty picture album in an exclusive deal.

The popular chain of bookstores, stocking everything from bestselling novels to turntables to popular magazines, praised the American Idol winner who has become a worldwide superstar. On its site, a Barnes & Noble blog raved about Carrie’s current album.

“Cry Pretty is Carrie Underwood’s sixth album, but it’s very much an album of firsts: it is her first album with Capitol Records Nashville and marks her first time co-producing her own work. Notably, her performance of the title track at this year’s Academy of Country Music Awards received much critical praise, and that song also topped the Digital Songs chart the week it was released—another first for the artist… ‘Cry Pretty’ is a soulful, dynamic song about not being able to hide one’s emotions forever, and it is just one track on what is possibly Carrie’s best record yet.”

The Barnes & Noble exclusive version of Cry Pretty features full-color vinyl art, with Carrie standing at a mic while dressed in her typical garb of short shorts, a skintight, long-sleeved top, and over the knee stiletto boots. Her long blond hair, parted on the side, hits the top of her breasts while she puts one hand up to fool with her tresses while her other hand caresses the mic.

On the other side of the album, a close-up picture of Carrie is featured. She has mascara and eyeliner smeared on her face as if she had been crying and she holds one hand up, with a finger delicately touching a point underneath one eye. She definitely looks pretty even though she had apparently shed some tears before the snap was taken. The B side of the vinyl disc also names a complete list of the album’s songs

A number of Carrie’s admirers were keen on her most recent social media share. After being uploaded for less than half an hour, she earned more than 2,800 likes and dozens of comments. Many fans used emoji to convey their thoughts, with crying-laughing face, heart-face, and heart emoji being popular.
