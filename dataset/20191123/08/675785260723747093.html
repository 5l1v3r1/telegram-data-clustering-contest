English football's 10 current longest serving managers after Mauricio Pochettino axe

The Tottenham Hotspur boss was sacked earlier this week and replaced by Jose Mourinho, as the Argentinean's reign as the fifth longest-serving boss in England came to a close

23 Nov 2019, 08:30 by Mark Jones, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/mark-jones/

Mauricio Pochettino's sacking as Tottenham Hotspur manager wasn't just good news for Spurs incumbent Jose Mourinho.

Because the surprise Tuesday evening departure of the Argentinean also meant that English football's established managerial order was shaken up.

Ex-Southampton manager Pochettino was sitting fifth in the list of durable bosses up and down the four divisions when the axe fell on him, with his five-and-half year stint at Spurs marking him out as the third longest-serving Premier League manager currently in a job.

But his shock departure from North London meant an entry into the top five for Liverpool's Jurgen Klopp, just a few short months after he bested Pochettino to win the Champions League.

Meanwhile, a new top-flight name battled his way into the elite which features some less heralded lower league names.

Here's the new-look top 10 as Mourinho takes his place all the way down at No.91.

10. Chris Wilder (Sheffield United) - 3 years, 6 months, 11 days

9. Mark Cooper (Forest Green Rovers) - 3 years, 6 months, 14 days

8. John McGreal (Colchester United) - 3 years, 6 months, 19 days

7. Lee Johnson (Bristol City) - 3 years, 9 months, 17 days

6. Nigel Clough (Burton Albion) - 3 years, 11 months, 16 days

5. Jurgen Klopp (Liverpool) - 4 years, 1 month, 15 days

4. John Coleman (Accrington Stanley) - 5 years, 2 months, 5 days

3. Sean Dyche (Burnley) - 7 years, 24 days

2. Eddie Howe (Bournemouth) - 7 years, 1 month, 11 days

1. Gareth Ainsworth (Wycombe) - 7 years, 1 month, 30 days
