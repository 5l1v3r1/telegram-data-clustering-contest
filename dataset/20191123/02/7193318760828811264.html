OSU basketball: Cowboys edge Western Michigan to remain unbeaten

23 Nov 2019, 02:07 by Jacob Unruh

Related coverage: OSU men's basketball

STILLWATER — Oklahoma State center Yor Anei grabbed the rebound, dribbled to the center of the lane and spun as he dropped the basketball into the goal with his left hand.

He nearly sprinted to halfcourt in celebration as Cowboys faithful went wild.

OSU’s big man delivered late to alleviate any worries on a slog of a night for the Cowboys with four straight points — an alley-oop dunk and then a putback — in the final 2:03.

It was enough to help the Cowboys (5-0) remain perfect to start the season with a 70-63 victory over Western Michigan on Friday night.

The win also left a better feeling the same day OSU received a Notice of Allegations from the NCAA.

The NCAA informed OSU it faced a Level 1 allegation from an investigation into former associate coach Lamont Evans, who was found by an FBI probe to have accepted bribes to link top players with bribe-paying managers and financial advisers from April 2016 through September 2017.

OSU plans to appeal and has 90 days to respond.

But that wasn’t going to overshadow OSU’s best start since going 7-0 to open the 2014-15 season.

Anei recorded his third career double double with 13 points and 12 rebounds. He also had five blocks.

Point guard Isaac Likekele also scored 10 points to go along with four assists and three rebounds.
