Injured man pulled from Scarborough Beach surf

23 Nov 2019, 02:54

A 50-year-old man is fighting for his life in Royal Perth Hospital after he was dragged from the water at Scarborough beach on Saturday morning.

Surf Life Saving WA members were conducting training on jetskis around 8am when they were alerted by members of the public about a man who was unconscious in the water about 500m north of Scarborough Surf Life Saving Club.

A spokesperson from Surf Life Saving WA said life savers begun CPR on the man who was unresponsive and not breathing, while waiting for St John Ambulance to arrive.

The man was transported to Royal Perth Hospital by St John Ambulance in a critical condition, where he remains.

The spokesperson praised members of the public for their quick thinking and assistance in helping the man.

Members from Trigg Surf Life Saving Club and Scarborough Patrols supported the rescue.

A witness at the popular Perth beach said he heard screams coming from the water.

“I saw instructors from the surf school drag a man out of the water and started doing CPR,” he said.
