In Bob we trust: Documentary follows strange history of the Church of the SubGenius

23 Nov 2019, 13:01 by Eric Volmers

Midway through Sandy K. Boone’s J.R. “Bob” Dobbs and The Church of the SubGenius, viewers finally get a description of the religion at the heart of the documentary.

Sporting the same smart-alecky grin he wears throughout the film, artist and charter associate Paul Mavrides offers a definition of The Church of the SubGenius as it evolved in the 1980s, when the parody religion first began to gain traction among America’s weirdos.

“We actually are a religion that seems like an art piece that seems like performance art that seems like a joke that seems like a religion,” Mavrides explains.

As a documentary filmmaker attempting to unravel the convoluted history and beliefs of the Church of the SubGenius, one might think Boone would be a bit frustrated by the cryptic nature of Mavrides’ definition. As with everyone else involved, he seems incapable of giving a clear answer about the true nature of this phenomenon.

“No, that was part of the fun and it needed to be fun,” says Boone, in an interview from her office in Austin, Texas.
