Maharashtra midnight stunner and day of political drama: All that happened in last 24 hours

Saturday was an action-packed day in Maharashtra politics as the BJP managed to rope-in NCP's Ajit Pawar and a letter than pledged support of 54 NCP MLAs. But the battle for Maharashtra is far from over. Read on to find why.

23 Nov 2019, 13:53

When people in Maharashtra went to sleep last night, they knew Shiv Sena president Uddhav Thackeray was likely to be their next chief minister with the Nationalist Congress Party (NCP) and Congress supporting him. Uddhav too must have retired for the day in this hope. After all, after days of hectic parleys his party was on the verge of ironing out an agreement with the Congress and NCP, an agreement that would pave the way for government formation with Shiv Sena at the helm, and more importantly, the BJP out of power.

On Saturday, the three parties were to meet again and finalise their deal.

BUT...

Hidden under the cloud of these talks was another deal taking shape, albeit with utmost secrecy.

It all started on Friday afternoon. While the entire media's focus was on Shiv Sena, NCP and Congress, Ajit Pawar, NCP chief Sharad Pawar's nephew, approached Maharashtra Governor Bhagat Singh Koshiyari in Mumbai with signed letters from all 54 of NCP's winning candidates in the Maharashtra assembly elections, extending his support to Devendra Fadnavis and the BJP.

News of this letter of support being delivered to Governor Koshiyari remained a secret for nearly 20 hours.

Later that night, BJP's Devendra Fadnavis quietly met Governor Bhagat Singh Koshiyari and staked claim to form government, along with the support of 54 of NCP's MLAs, 15 others and, of course, the BJP.

CAUGHT BY SURPRISE

As Mumbai slept peacefully, the action swiftly shifted to New Delhi. Around midnight, Governor Bhagat Singh Koshiyari informed the Centre, and through it the Rashtrapati Bhawan, of the latest developments in Maharashtra.

At 5.47am on Saturday, the Centre lifted the President's rule that had been imposed in Maharashtra on November 12 when no party had been able to stake claim to government formation.

This decision was apparently taken without a Cabinet meeting. Quoting sources, news agency PTI reported that the Prime Minister invoked Rule 12 of government's transaction of business to go ahead with this decision. The rule gives the PM special powers to bypass the need for Union cabinet's recommendation.

At 5.47am when President's Rule was lifted, no one knew about it. Headlines in the Saturday edition of most newspapers announced that Uddhav Thackeray was set to become Maharashtra's chief minister.

But around 7.30 am, news flashed saying Devendra Fadnavis had taken oath as Maharashtra's chief minister at the Raj Bhawan in Mumbai. Not just this, NCP's Ajit Pawar too had taken oath as the deputy chief minister. Ajit Pawar had managed to ensure that 10-12 NCP MLAs attended his oath-taking ceremony.
