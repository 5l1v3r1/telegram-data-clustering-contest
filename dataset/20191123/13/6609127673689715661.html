Never say never

Ex-Scotland star Fletcher has not ruled out playing return but sees future in management

23 Nov 2019, 13:02 by David Fowler

FORMER Scotland star Darren Fletcher has not ruled out a return to club football but sees his future in management.

The ex-Manchester United star left Stoke City at the end of last season, but has not officially retired from the game.

Hibs recently failed in a bid to hold talks with Fletcher over their managerial post before Jack Ross landed the top job.

And he admits he could be tempted to pull his boots back on.

He told BBC Scotland: "It would have to be something that was really appealing.

"I've not officially retired yet for a few different reasons.

"There would have to be a number of different factors - I'm very happy with what I've achieved in my career."

The 35-year-old doesn't feel quite ready to go into management just yet.

But he insists it's something he definitely sees for his future.

He added: "It's always something I thought I would go into.

"I love the game, would love to still be involved in the game, coaching and speaking in the dressing rooms and motivating players.

"Inspiring players comes naturally to me so hopefully that transcends to management.

"A managerial role is something I definitely see in my future but at the same time I don't feel I'm quite ready yet.

"I need to go and define what I would be as a manager, finish my coaching badges, try and take in as much experience as I can from different clubs around the world, the UK, and speak to as many people as possible to try and prepare myself for when I do make that step.

"Hopefully it's not too far in the future, but as of now any opportunities that came round I wouldn't be ready to take."

Fletcher learned his trade under legend Sir Alex Ferguson at Old Trafford, and revealed his ultimate goal is to follow his pal Ole Gunnar Solskjaer and eventually boss the Reds.

He said: “It’s no different to being a player - when I was a player I wanted to play for the biggest and best club in the world, and I did that at United.

“So when I take my steps on the management ladder my goal is to become Manchester United manager because that’s the pinnacle for me. Nothing tops that.

“There’s thousands of players who play, but there’s not that many manager’s jobs, and United is the biggest job in the world for me.
