Scots rocker's secret album found in auntie's kitchen drawer after his death

When singer and guitarist, Shaun Scott, 27, passed away earlier this year his loved ones and bandmates from punk pop group The Ronaines were left devastated.

23 Nov 2019, 13:21 by Kathleen Speirs

A musician's legacy has been uncovered following his death after his grieving bandmates found a secret album he’d written while battling mental illness, with help from a Scots rock legend.

When singer and guitarist, Shaun Scott, 27, died on August 19 his loved ones and bandmates from punk pop group The Ronaines were left devastated.

Weeks after the Glasgow dad’s tragic death, an album written, performed and produced by Shaun in 2015 was discovered in his aunt’s kitchen drawer.

Blown away by the 11 tracks and determined to share their late friend’s talent, The Ronains are on a mission to release the record.

Bass guitarist, Jim Reid told the Record: “The album is called Satellite Tears.

“Shaun was always so quiet and private.

“He battled Tourette’s syndrome from a young age but to us he was just Shaun.

“These songs are like a letter to himself and loved ones about his mental struggles.

“We couldn’t listen to it all at first because it brought us to tears.

“But it’s an incredible album that people need to hear.”

Satellite Tears takes the same name as Shaun, from Glasgow’s previous band with music pals, Liam Byrne and Chris Sweeney.

Scottish rock legend, Gordy Goudie, of Simple Minds fame , helped produce two of the ‘powerful’ tracks.

The group disbanded in 2016, leaving Satellite Tears gathering dust in a drawer on one disc and one USB stick.

Jim, along with singer, Debbie Ronain, both from Clydebank, and drummer, Linzi Ronain, from Paisley, want the album out by Christmas.

All proceeds from record sales will go towards setting up a mental heath charity in Shaun’s name, and his three-year-old son, Jaxson.

Amidst the shock and grief of losing their friend, the band say Satellite Tears was a “gift from the grave”.

Jim said: “What’s happened with Shaun and his private struggles is heartbreaking.

“One of the songs is called Failure To Launch where Shaun sings, ‘son you’re too young too die’.

“It’s a cry for help.

“But in a way, this is almost like his parting gift as the music is so beautiful.”

A secret CD isn’t the only sign Jim believes the Ronain’s were sent surrounding Shaun’s death.

He said: “Not long before Shaun passed away we recorded the video for our latest track, Fun Time Frankie, which features ex-footballer Frank McAvennie.

“Shaun always wore black.

“By chance, the video director wanted to shoot a scene where Shaun was dressed in white, waking as girls surround him.

“Watching that back now, it’s like he’s walking to heaven.

“It’s eerie but amazing at the same time.”
