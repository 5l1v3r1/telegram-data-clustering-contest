Magmatic and Zoono see Ellis easy winner of Shares Race

23 Nov 2019, 13:00 by John Collett

Angie Ellis, of 80-20 Investments, has won the six-week Shares Race by a big margin of almost $13,000, mostly thanks to the outstanding performances Magmatic Resources and Zoono Group.

Her original $10,000 investment in Magmatic, a gold and copper explorer, ended the competition worth $30,000 – by far the best-performing tip of all.

Her $10,000 investment in Zoono Group, an anti-microbial products company, was almost as good at $29,070 – the second-best performing stock.

It was an emphatic win by the Melbourne accountant, who has won the Shares Race more times than any other competitor.

Credit also goes to second-placed Varun Ratra, of the newsletter Eye between Bulls & Bears, who ended the race with a portfolio value of $123,556. His selection of Oncosil Medical – the third-best performer – saw his original $10,000 investment in the medical device company grow to $27,692.

Dan Silver, co-chief executive of share trading platform Stake, also picked Magmatic and also did well from his stock selection of Michael Hill, with his original $10,000 in the jewellery retailer ending the competition worth $12,105.

However, Silver was let down by his tip of the worst-performing stock, Botanix Pharmaceuticals, with his original $10,000 in the cannabis pharmaceutical company dropping to just $4,458.

The sharemarket, as measured by the S&P/ASX 300 index, finished the week down 1 per cent to 6,627 points on Thursday, the day the competition ended.

The Shares Race is a fantasy shares game that runs for six weeks. Players start with $100,000, divided evenly across 10 stocks. Their progress is reported weekly.
