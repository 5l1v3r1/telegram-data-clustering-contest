Pierre-Emerick Aubameyang sends message to Granit Xhaka in Arsenal programme notes

The Gabonese forward has taken over from Xhaka as the new club captain following the reaction to the Swiss midfielder's substitution against Crystal Palace last month

23 Nov 2019, 13:18 by Mark Jones, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/mark-jones/

Pierre-Emerick Aubameyang had words for Granit Xhaka in his captain's programme notes for Arsenal's clash with Southampton on Saturday.

The forward has taken over the armband from the Swiss midfielder following the reaction to the latter's substitution against Crystal Palace last month.

Xhaka was jeered by home fans when he was replaced after an hour of the 2-2 draw, and was seen mouthing "f*** off" to the fans as he left the pitch.

The subsequent reaction eventually lead to Xhaka issuing a statement apologising if he had caused any offence, but the row had already turned toxic and eventually led to him losing the club captaincy.

Xhaka hasn't played for the club since the incident, but is set to return to the squad for Saturday's clash with the Saints.

And Aubameyang's captain's column called for support for both Xhaka and the rest of the team.

"I took over from Granit in tough circumstances but I am very proud to be wearing the armband," said Aubameyang.

"It is an honour. It comes with a lot of responsibilities but I feel I can do a great job.

"I would like to also say a word for Granit.

"I understand that sometimes one can make mistakes. It happens to all of us. Granit is one of us. We are all here for him and I know how strong he is.

"It was tough to see all of this happening but we need to move forward together."

"This is my message for you Arsenal fans. We need you. You are amazing, your support is amazing and we need to all pull in the same direction.

"I really hope you will be massively behind us today and for the rest of the season."
