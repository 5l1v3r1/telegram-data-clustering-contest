'Cry of the Curlew' to be launched at Scrub Hill 1869

23 Nov 2019, 21:00 by Ben Hopkins

A collection of artistic talent from around the Ballarat region is set to be showcased in Newlyn as the Moorabool Writers Craft launch their new anthology book, 'Cry of the Curlew'.

Contributing writer and musician Vanessa Craven said she is excited to launch the project which features fiction, non-fiction and a collection of poems.

"It's so exciting to be releasing it because it's been born of hard work... people who are enthusiastic and want to create," she said.

The free festival will feature live readings from the new book, along with music, art, food and drinks at Newlyn's Scrub Hill 1869.

Ms Craven said having a free festival on offer is very important in promoting art to the wider community and encourage involvement within groups like the Moorabool Writers Craft.

"I think making events like this accessible is really important... having a free event is a great way of introducing people to the various groups that will be there which will hopefully encourage people to get involved."
