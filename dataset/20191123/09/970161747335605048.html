Mark Ramprakash criticises England's use of Jofra Archer as BJ Watling cashes in

23 Nov 2019, 09:05

Former England batting coach Mark Ramprakash fears England risk wasting Jofra Archer’s natural talent if he is restricted to the role of enforcer in their new-look bowling attack.

New Zealand assumed control of the first Test in Maunganui after a patient batting display saw them reach 394/6 at the close with an unbeaten 119 from BJ Watling anchoring their innings.

The hosts established a 41-run after Joe Root’s side had wasted a commanding position earlier in the match having been 277/4 at one stage, before a familiar collapse.

Archer, who shot to prominence during the summer following his World Cup and Ashes heroics, remained wicketless from 30 overs on his first Test appearance abroad, failing to disrupt New Zealand’s batsmen with a barrage of short-pitched bowling.

‘We have to be very careful with our expectations just because he has great natural ability and great natural pace,’ said Ramprakash reflecting on Archer’s toils.

‘It doesn’t mean that at his very young age he’s going to turn up and get five wickets every time.

‘We have to be very patient and understand he’s a young man who is learning even though he has great success at one-day tournaments.

‘He had a tough day of Test cricket today he will have learned a lot about how to bowl in those conditions. How to physically understand his body and he’ll feel it in the morning.

‘I’ve got to be honest, he’s been used in the wrong way he’s not someone who just comes in and bowls loads of bouncers.

‘I think he’s better than that. I want to see more balls at a fuller length. The great West Indian bowlers of the past, yes they’ve played the chin music but they’ve followed that up with really fast, accurate bowling.

‘I think he’s better than what he showed today but I wonder if he’s being asked to play a role that’s not getting the best out of him.’
