The last episode of Russi Taylor voicing The Simpson’s Martin Prince is ‘epic'

23 Nov 2019, 09:37

Russi Taylor, the voice actor behind The Simpson’s Martin Prince passed away earlier this year, and the final episode of her voicing the character is set to air on Sunday.

The Simpsons’ executive producer Matt Selman confirmed that the final episode will air on Sunday, telling fans: ‘This Sunday’s episode of @TheSimpsons features the last vocal appearance of the wonderful, hilarious Russi Taylor as Martin Prince.

‘It turned out to be an epic farewell, one Russi would have loved.’

Despite it being the last episode voiced by Russi, the character of Martin will live on, and will be voiced by Grey DeLisle-Griffin, who is best known for voicing Daphne in Scooby Doo and Vicky on Nickelodeon’s The Fairly OddParents.

As well as her role as Martin Prince, Russi also was the voice of Minnie Mouse in several Mickey Mouse movies, and Huey, Dewey, Louie and Webby in DuckTales.

She passed away from colon cancer in July 2019 at the age of 75, and had voice acted on 193 episodes of The Simpsons between 1989 and 2019.

While there’s no word on what her final appearance as Martin Prince will entail, fans of The Simpsons will be able to find out on Sunday.

The episode, entitled Thanksgiving of Horror in homage to the Treehouse of Horror series, will also be the longest single Simpsons episode ever at 25 minutes.

According to Entertainment Weekly: ‘The Simpsons face various Thanksgiving nightmares, including the first Thanksgiving, an A.I. mishap and a dangerous space mission complicated by a sentient cranberry sauce. ‘

Matt Selman added: ‘Russi’s Martin goes out in memorable style.’

Martin Prince has been a staple of Springfield’s cast of characters, being a bit of an overachiever and Nelson Muntz’s favourite target to bully.

As well as Martin, Russi was also the voice behind several other characters including twins Sherri and Terri, Uter Zorker and Dr. Hibbert’s daughter.
