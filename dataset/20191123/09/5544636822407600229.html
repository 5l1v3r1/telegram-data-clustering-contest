Nine-Year-Old Belgian Set To Graduate From University

23 Nov 2019, 09:34 by John Andah

Belgian Laurent Simons is on the verge of bagging a degree in electrical engineering from the Eindhoven University of Technology in the Netherlands at the age of nine.

Concise News understands that Simons, who desires to make artificial organs, will in December become one of the world’s youngest graduates.

It was learned that the nine-year-old completed the aforementioned course in just nine months instead of the usual three years.

“I’m currently studying electrical engineering. It’s about designing circuits and things like that. So chips, actually,” AFP quoted him as saying in the Dutch capital Amsterdam.

“I really like karting. And I like playing with my dog and watching Netflix,” he added.

And excited Sjoerd Hulshof, programme director for electrical engineering at the university, said Laurent’s qualities were extraordinary.

“Laurent’s qualities are all simply extraordinary,” Hulshof told AFP, adding that he is “the quickest student we’ve ever seen. And he’s not just hyper-intelligent, but very nice.”

Laurent was raised in Ostend by his grandparents until the start of 2019, as his parents, Alexander and Lydia, were “busy with work” in the Netherlands, according to AFP.

He started school at the age of four, and completed primary in a year and a half.

Come to think of it, it has taken him just five years to go through primary and secondary school and university.
