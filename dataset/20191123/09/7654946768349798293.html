Kyrie Irving to miss 3-game road trip, won’t face Knicks or Celtics

23 Nov 2019, 09:13 by Brian Lewis

Kyrie Irving is going to miss the Nets’ entire three-game road trip, a salivating slate that begins against the Knicks on Sunday at the Garden, and continues against the All-Star guard’s two former teams, the Cavaliers and the Celtics.

When Irving wasn’t present on the Nets’ bench during Friday’s 116-97 win over Sacramento, fans began to wonder why. But he was in the building, and after having his shoulder evaluated, it has been determined that he’ll sit out the upcoming trip.

Irving will be reassessed once the Nets return to Brooklyn. They host the Celtics in a rematch on Nov. 29.

“I think he’s concerned about one thing — getting back to being as close to 100 percent as possible. It’s as simple as that,” coach Kenny Atkinson said of Irving before Friday’s game.

Irving suffered a shoulder impingement during the game at Utah on Nov. 12, and played two days later at Denver, with Atkinson admitting that outing aggravated the injury. A specialist told The Post at the time the guard’s injury would require from several days to a couple of weeks of rest. The Black Friday game against the Celtics at Barclays Center would be exactly two weeks of rest following the game against the Nuggets.

Atkinson has been on record saying he would prefer Irving have some practice time before returning to the court.

The Nets aren’t practicing Saturday, aren’t allowed to practice Tuesday after a back-to-back, and are unlikely to practice on Thanksgiving after flying back from Boston before a noon matinee Friday.

They could conceivably concoct a suitable workout for Irving if his shoulder improves enough.

“I wouldn’t say ‘have to’ [practice]. We’d like to see some evidence, not just with Kyrie. That’s with Spencer [Dinwiddie], that’s with anybody,” Atkinson said. “We definitely don’t want to throw him out there without observing where he is.

“But luckily with the staff we have, our whole staff is ex-players, we can create a practice on an off day. We obviously have the facilities and we have the personnel to be able to evaluate in a three-on-three or a four-on-four if we have to.”

Friday marked the Nets’ first wire-to-wire victory of the season and most lopsided win of 2019-20.

The Nets shot 50.6 percent (41-of-81) from the field, their second-best mark this season.

The Nets outrebounded the Kings 48-33, with the plus-15 rebounding differential matching their highest of the season.
