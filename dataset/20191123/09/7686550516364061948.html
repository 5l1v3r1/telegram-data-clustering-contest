Schmidt could walk away

Former Ireland coach Joe Schmidt has revealed he may never return to rugby coaching.

Schmidt stepped down after Ireland's Rugby World Cup failure, bringing to an end a largely successful six-year reign.

He announced he was leaving the Irish job prior to the World Cup to spend more time with his family and help look after his son who has epilepsy.

But there's no doubt the manner of the bruising 46-14 World Cup quarter-final defeat to New Zealand and the pressure of being in charge of the national team have taken their toll.

In an interview with the Telegraph to promote his new book, Schmidt revealed he had not taken a day off in more than six years and barely slept in Japan.

"Yeah, I'm not sure I'll ever go back to coaching," he said.

"I just don't know. I want to give myself a bit of distance. I'm emotionally attached. I do take it home with me. I do feel a sense of responsibility to the people who support us, the people who came out to Japan, the people whose hopes we maybe inspired. To let them down was… really tough."

England Six Nations blow

England have suffered a potential Six Nations blow after winger Joe Cokanasiga was ruled out for months with an unspecified knee injury he picked up at the World Cup.

His club Bath released a vague statement, saying: “In order to provide Joe the best possible management plan and rehabilitation process, Bath Rugby’s medical department are enlisting the services of world-leading specialists to ensure expert opinion is considered to deliver an optimal path to recovery.

“The club’s priority is to maximise his chances to perform at the very highest level.”

Director of rugby Stuart Hooper added: “Joe is a phenomenal talent and a powerful athlete, and we must manage him very carefully in the coming months to allow for the issues he came back from Japan with to be resolved.”

Scarlets suffer heartbreak

The Scarlets were downed in heartbreaking fashion at the Stade Felix Mayol, losing 17-16 to Toulon in the European Challenge Cup.

A 90th minute conversion from centre Anthony Belleau saw the hosts creep ahead by a point, snatching victory from the jaws of defeat in the last play.

It was a remarkable performance from the Scarlets, who played the entire second half with 14 men following the sending off of Tevita Ratuva late in the opening period.

Greenwood loves Dan Biggar

TV pundit, Telegraph columnist and World Cup winner Will Greenwood has named Dan Biggar as one of his 10 standout players in the European Champions Cup so far this season.

Biggar was at the heart of Northampton's opening weekend win over Lyon, stepping back into the breach just a fortnight after the Rugby World Cup.

And, picking his top stars in the Telegraph , Greenwood wrote: "I had to pick out Biggar. I admire this lad immensely. Totally committed. A little 'wordy' with refs occasionally, perhaps one too many throws of the arms, but I live in a glass house so will not throw stones.

"Truly brilliant goal-kicker, excellent reader of the game when defending and scrambling, so good in the air. And lovely distribution."
