Woman hit by ambulance as she crossed the road in Exeter

She was seriously injured as a result

23 Nov 2019, 09:27 by Chris Matthews

A pensioner has been left seriously injured after being struck by an ambulance, it has been confirmed.

Devon Live yesterday reported how a pedestrian was involved in a collision with a vehicle on Cowley Bridge Road in Exeter on Friday afternoon.

Seven police and ambulance vehicles were called to the scene of the crash near the Esso petrol station forecourt at 3.20pm.

The road was initially closed in both directions causing long tailbacks between Exeter and the Crediton road.

Police remained at the scene on the petrol station forecourt after the road reopened.

Today (Saturday) a Devon and Cornwall Police spokesman has confirmed that the accident involved an elderly woman who was involved in collision by an ambulance.

A spokesman said: “At 3:20pm on Friday November 22 Devon and Cornwall Police were notified of a road traffic Incident on the A377 Cowley Bridge Road, Exeter, Devon.

 “The incident was attended by personnel from the police and ambulance services. A 78-year-old female pedestrian crossing the road was injured by an emergency ambulance responding to a call for medical assistance.

“Officers from the Exeter Roads Policing Unit are appealing for witnesses to come forward quoting log number 430 of 22/11/19.”

A force spokesman went on to thank members of the public for their patience during the scene closure.
