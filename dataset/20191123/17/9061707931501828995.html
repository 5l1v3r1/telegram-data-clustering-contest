New landlord promises revamped pub is 'the best in Derby'

He's moved to Normanton from Leeds

23 Nov 2019, 17:01 by Anna Whittaker

A Derby landlord says his new pub is the best in Derby.

Kelvin Garvey, the new landlord at the Loudon Arms, in Normanton said: “Most pubs in Derby don’t have what we’ve got.

“Everything has been changed inside the whole pub. We’ve got Sky Sports, live music, DJs on a Friday and Saturday and Caribbean food on a Sunday.

“I’m appealing to everybody. People are coming from town just to come here. It’s the best pub in Derby."

The 46-year-old, who has moved to Normanton from Leeds for the business venture, said he’s enjoying the area.

He said: “I’ve been getting to know the customers and I’m like a local now. There are lots of regulars.

“It’s very multicultural and I feel more at home here."

Mr Garvey, who owns two pubs in Crewe, also claims to be selling the cheapest lager in the city.

He said: “Our Carlsberg and John Smith's are £2.50 and our Carling is £2.80.

“It’s the cheapest pint in Derby.”

He added: “We’re very busy at weekends and we’ve got lots of events coming up for Christmas, from December 24 into the New Year.”

The Loudon Arms is open from 11am – 11pm Monday to Wednesday and Sundays and 11am – 12pm Thursday to Saturday.
