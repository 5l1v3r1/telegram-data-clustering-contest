Plymouth Argyle beat high-flying Bradford City 2-1 to record another League Two win

Ryan Lowe's Pilgrims are up to eighth in the table after a 2-1 victory at Home Park

23 Nov 2019, 17:36 by Chris Errington

Plymouth Argyle made it four wins in their last five League Two games as they beat Bradford City 2-1 at Home Park.

Ryan Lowe’s Pilgrims are up to eighth in the table, only a point outside the play-off positions, after an excellent victory in an exciting match.

Striker Byron Moore and midfielder Joe Edwards scored first half goals for Argyle, who could easily have been more than 2-0 up at the interval.

However, Bradford were much improved at the start of the second period after a double substitution by manager Gary Bowyer.

Their pressure was rewarded as Argyle defender Niall Canavan conceded an own goal in the 51st minute.

The Pilgrims then showed good character to dig deep and come back into the contest as they limited the opportunities for a Bradford equaliser.

Argyle man-of-the-match Antoni Sarcevic almost grabbed a late goal which would have made the five minutes of stoppage time much more comfortable for the hosts.

Nonetheless, the Pilgrims held onto their slender lead despite late pressure from City.

Argyle had not played a league game at Home Park since they beat Leyton Orient 4-0 on October 22.

After such a long wait, it did not take them long to got the opening goal against Bradford.

Sarcevic made a strong run down the right and showed good determination to cut inside the challenge of Bradford’s Harry Pritchard.

He then rolled the ball across to Moore, whose first attempt to put it into the net was blocked by ‘keeper Richard O’Donnell.

However, Moore was alert to the rebound and applied the finishing touch at the secod time of trying.

Bradford responded well to falling behind and had three decent chances to draw level, the first when Harry Pritchard headed over the bar after a Connor Wood cross.

Callum Cooke sent a powerful 25-yard strike narrowly wide of Alex Palmer’s right post before Pritchard’s forced a diving save out of the Argyle ‘keeper.

The Pilgrims then doubled their lead in the 25th minute thanks to the tenacity and finishing ability of Edwards.

O’Donnell was put under pressure by Edwards when he went to clear the ball from inside the Bradford penalty area.

It went only as far as Joel Grant, who moved forward with it before passing inside to Edwards, who had taken up position next to him.

Edwards then came up with a well-placed low left-foot finish to give O’Donnell no chance of making a save.

It could have been 3-0 to the Pilgrims in the 35th minute when Grant showed good strength to win the ball off Bradford defender Ben Richards-Everton in a tackle.

Grant raced towards goal and cut inside the attempted sliding block of former Pilgrims’ defender Anthony O’Connor.

Having done all the hard work, Grant could come up with the finish though as his weak shot was stopped by O’Donnell.

The Bradford ‘keeper also kicked away a deflected shot from Sarcevic on the stroke of half-time as Argyle were again denied a third goal.

Bowyer made a double substitution at the start of the second half, with former Pilgrims’ defender Kelvin Mellor one of those sent on.

The visitors showed much more urgency and were given a lifeline back into the match in the 51st minute.

After Argyle could not clear a free-kick into their penalty area, Mellor touched the ball outside to O’Connor.

Canavan tried to cut out O’Connor’s cross-shot but could only redirect it into his own goal, past Palmer.

Bradford’s other half-time substitute, Zeli Ismail, almost came up with an equaliser in the 65th minute.

Ismail made a run in between Argyle defenders Scott Wootton and Gary Sawyer and flicked the ball narrowly wide of Palmer’s right post.

Canavan then had the chance to make amends for his earlier own goal with a clearcut scoring chance in the 73rd minute.

The defender rose well at the far post to meet a cross from Edwards but sent his header wide of the target from close range.

With Dom Telford now on as a substitute after replacing Moore, Argyle found a renewed lease of energy.

Sarcevic almost put them 3-1 up in the 84th minute with a low shot which was blocked on the line by a combination of O’Donnell and Wood after a run and cross from Callum McFadzean.

Inevitably, Argyle came under late pressure from Bradford but just about managed to deal with a couple of long throw-ins from Mellor deep into stoppage time in a tense finale.

ARGYLE (4-2-3-1): Alex Palmer; Scott Wootton, Niall Canavan, Gary Sawyer; Josh Grant; Joe Edwards, Antoni Sarcevic, Danny Mayor, Callum McFadzean; Joel Grant (Ryan Taylor 84 mins), Byron Moore (Dom Telford 70 mins).
