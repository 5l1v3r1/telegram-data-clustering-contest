WATCH: Doctor saves airline passenger by sucking urine from bladder

23 Nov 2019, 17:34 by Zachary Halaschak

A quick-thinking doctor is being hailed as a hero after he used an improvised catheter to suck urine from an ailing man’s bladder.

While on a nine-hour flight from China, a 70-year-old man began to experience pain and collapsed, according to the Sun. He told flight attendants that he was unable to urinate and that his bladder was close to rupturing.

Dr. Zhang Hong and Dr. Xiao Zhangxiang sprung into action and fashioned a catheter out of a syringe, a milk straw, and a plastic tube. Zhang then spent 37 minutes sucking the man’s urine from his bladder into his mouth.

“He was going into shock and may have suffered a risk to his life if we didn't tend to him urgently,” Zhang said. “His family said he had a history of prostate enlargement, so we suspected this was causing urinary retention.”

“Saving lives is a doctor's instinct,” he added.
