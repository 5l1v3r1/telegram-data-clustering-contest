Star Wars: This Key TLJ Moment Was Former Rise of Skywalker Director's Idea

23 Nov 2019, 17:11 by Dan Conner

It was recently revealed that a big moment from Star Wars: The Last Jedi actually came from the original Star Wars: The Rise of Skywalker director, Colin Trevorrow.

During The Last Jedi's production, Trevorrow asked if a scene from his own script could be included in the film. Trevorrow told Empire about his conversation with The Last Jedi's director, Rian Johnson. "I just asked Rian if he could include a little moment where Rey and Poe meet for the first time," he said.

"They’re such beloved characters," Trevorrow continued, "It felt right for them to have some history in the next movie. I thought the way he did it was perfect." While Trevorrow, who also is the overseer of Jurassic World, did not end up directing Rise of Skywalker, he did earn a story credit for his contributions to the film.
