Urban Meyer Reacts To Jim Harbaugh’s Season At Michigan

23 Nov 2019, 17:59 by Chris Rosvoglou

It took time for Michigan to establish an identity this season, but Jim Harbaugh finally has his players competing at a high level. In fact, the Wolverines have been so impressive of late that even Urban Meyer had to give them credit.

Michigan suffered two losses early in the season to Penn State and Wisconsin. The entire team looked flat when it played at Madison back in September.

The Wolverines even went through growing pains in October, but they’ve responded over the past few weeks with a trio of double-digit victories.

During FOX’s Big Noon Kickoff show this morning, Meyer praised Harbaugh’s team for its resiliency this season.

Here’s what he had to say, via 247Sports:

“We had them (on FOX) against Army and also against Wisconsin, and I’m not sure I’ve seen a Wolverines team look that defeated, that deflated,” Meyer said. “But you have to give credit to the leaders on that team. This is a very good team. They’re playing excellent defensive football.”

Meyer isn’t one to shy away from being brutally honest about how he feels when it comes to Michigan football. The fact that he’s commending the team for having a bounce-back season puts into perspective how well the Wolverines have played since their loss to the Nittany Lions.

Harbaugh has to be proud of his players and the way they’ve responded to adversity this season as well.

We’ll see if Michigan can continue its winning ways on the road this afternoon against Indiana.
