This Week In XR: Ubiquity 6 Makes The Smartphone Spatial, Why IAPPA Is A VR Showcase, Magic Leap’s New Games

23 Nov 2019 by Charlie Fink

We just spent two long days of checking out 70 VR attractions offered for sale at the world’s largest amusement park conference and expo, IAAPA, which is best described as a milea of moon bounces, punctuated by cotton candy machines. The most original location based VR (LBVR) we saw at IAAPA was Barron Games’ VR Foosball Table, with runner up going to underwater VR, which will soon monetize a swimming pool near you. Otherwise, the best LBVR at IAPPA this year was the best VR last year: Hologate, VR Studios, and VRsenal, none of whom put you on a motion platform and blow wind in your face. We’ll drop all the details in the next couple of day but, in the meantime, here a look at VR Foosball.

We’re excited that our demand for more AAA titles from Magic Leap One was immediately fulfilled by two top shelf titles from Weta Workshop and Resolution Games, who have already delivered the best two spatial titles for the Magic Leap One, Dr. Grodbort’s Invaders and Angry Birds, respectively.

Weta Workshop launches new Dr. Grordbort’s game for Magic Leap One. Dr. Grordbort’s Boosters is all about controlling a rocket with your hands. The experience was built from a game jam the group held at the Weta studio in Wellington, New Zealand to explore the Magic Leap One SDK. Learn more about the game on Magic Leap’s blog here.

Creators of first Magic Leap game announce new detective game on the way. The game, Glimt, is stated as “coming soon” and will build on all the lessons Resolution Games learned from their previous Magic Leap One and VR titles: Angry Birds VR: Isle of Pigs, Bait! and Acron: Attack of the Squirrels! \

Ubiquity6 launches Display.land for scanning and sharing augmented realities. The company’s new app uses the smartphone’s camera and graphics processing power to scan a recreate real world spaces. The photogrammetry and its location is synced to the cloud allowing different user’s scans to be stitched together. In other words, with the Display.land app, Ubiquity 6 is building a network of 3D places. Users can also add augmentations such as annotations, artwork, or games to their scans. The app is available on iOS and Android at Display.land.

Vader Immortal Final Episode 3 released this week. If ILMxLab’s first person VR triology isn’t the best VR content every created for a home system, it’s damn close. They just delivered the third and final chapter. Oculus likes it so much Vader Immortal has been bundled with their new Quest this holiday season.

Oculus Link beta begins. Owners of the Oculus Quest can now connect their headsets to a PC via an Oculus approved USB-C cable that will effectively turn their standalone headset into a PCVR headset. In theory, this means any PCVR title playable on a Rift-S will now be playable on the Oculus Quest when linked to a VR ready PC. Learn more about what cables will work on the Oculus blog post here.

Valve unveils first VR title Half-Life: Alyx, the first Half-Life game in 13 years. The trailer for the new VR only title arrived on Thursday. The game, set sometime between the events of the first Half-Life and Half-Life 2, is built specifically for VR and though Valve suggests players play on the Valve Index HMD, they assure that the game will play on any PCVR headset. Owners and new buyers of Valve Index hardware will receive a copy of the game along with their hardware. The game will be available in March 2020 and can be pre-ordered now. Gamer dudes have been effusing over the Interwebs.

Lenovo and Varjo partner to provide VR ready hardware. The partnership, announced this week, will lead to Lenovo offering high end computers labeled as “Certified for Varjo”. 

Adidas launches XR experience showcasing the impact of plastic waste on the oceans. The app, created in partnership with eyecandylab and French creative agency MONOCHROME, is available at the Adidas flagship store at Champs Elysees in Paris. Users can see into an AR powered ocean world and help collect plastic floating there. The app shows the process of turning plastic waste into usable threads and ultimately into Adidas products. 

Wave launches multi-platform service for streaming live concerts. The technology transforms performers into digital avatars in real time, and streams the experience to multiple channels such as Youtube, Twitch, and Facebook, as well as Wave’s VR app. 

You can now pre-order the Nreal Light Developer Kit. Nreal announced pre-orders for its mixed reality headset are now open. The company also shared its partnership with LGUplus to bring 5G ready devices to the Korean market. The developer package costs $1,199 and comes with the headset, a 3 DoF controller, and Nreal’s computing pack. A consumer version is slated for 2020. 

Sacramento Kings adding AR to their app for an enhanced fan experience during games. The new AR features, created by Imagination Park Technologies, will include AR scavenger hunts, in-arena photo opportunities with digital players, giveaways, and more. The new features are set to launch in early 2020. 

Mixed reality streaming company LIV secures $2.6 million series A. The company behind mixed reality streaming of VR games previously secured $1 million in seed funding from investors including HTC Vive and Palmer Luckey. This round of funding was led by Hiro Capital.

AT&T announced multiple 5G deployments. including Tyndall Air Force Base, Purdue University’s College of Engineering is working with AT&T to create a test bed for 5G-based research and development, and the Washington Post newsroom. In December, AT&T will launch its 5G deployment at University of Miami in order to drive new scholarly pursuits and experiences, focusing first on architecture, engineering and library sciences, where it will be prototyping spatial computing applications. 

NextMind unveiled brain sensing wearable for controlling devices hands-free. “The small, light-weight, round device fits into the back of a cap or headband, and rests gently on the user's head. It captures data from the electrical signals created by the user's neuron activity in the visual cortex, and using machine learning algorithms, transforms that output into communication that enables easier interaction and control with a computer, AR/VR headset or any device within the Internet of Things.” The wait list for a development kit can be found here. 

Rickard Steiber leaves HTC. The well-liked and visible President of HTC’s Viveport VR distribution platform is the first to step down following the appointment of X as the new CEO of the Taiwan based VR leader. A Google alumni, Steiber is likely to land on his feet in the C suite of one of XR’s leading companies.

DigiLens welcomes Nima Shams to the team. Shams has been working in augmented reality for over a decade and recently held a position at RealWare as their Head of Advance Product Development and Chief Engineer. Shams joins the DigiLens team as Vice President of Product Engineering and Management.
