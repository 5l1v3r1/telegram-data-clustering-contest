Friday Box Office: ‘Charlie’s Angels’ Drops 69% As ‘Joker’ Tops ‘Deadpool 2’ And ‘Suicide Squad’

23 Nov 2019 by Scott Mendelson

In holdover news for Friday, Fox’s Ford v Ferrari earned $4.5 million (-59%) on its second Friday for a $15.4 million (-51%) second weekend and $57.44 million ten-day total. That’s not a great hold, but it should still end its run, Oscar bumps notwithstanding, with around $90 million domestic. How the $98 million Christian Bale/Matt Damon racing flick performs overseas will determine its profitability. It’s still in better shape than Ad Astra, but we’ll see where the overseas figures stand tomorrow. Paramount and Walden’s Playing with Fire earned another $1.06 million (-44%) on Friday for a likely $4.98 million (-28%) third weekend and $32 million 7-day cume. That’s decent for a live-action kid pick in 2019, although the $29 million budget means it needs strong overseas grosses. Lionsgate’s Midway earned $1.38 million (-45%) on Friday for a likely $4.75 million (-44%) third weekend and a $43.1 million 17-day total. The $100 million Roland Emmerich movie is a global bust, but Lionsgate should make money since it was only on the hook for domestic/UK distribution fees and expenses.

Warner Bros.’ The Good Liar, which is a damn good time at the movies, earned another $1.03 million (-37%) on Friday for a likely $3.62 million (-35%) second weekend and $12 million ten-day total. Bill Condon’s Ian McKellen/Helen Mirren con man thriller isn’t exactly a hit, but it did cost only $10 million to produce. Universal’s Last Christmas earned $940,000 (-53%) on Friday for a $3.14 million (-52%) third weekend and a poor $27.9 million 17-day cume.

Sony’s Charlie’s Angels earned just $970,000 (-69%) on Friday for a likely $3.13 million (-63%) second weekend and miserable $13.896 million ten-day cume. The Elizabeth Banks actioner may not have deserved to be a super-hit, but it damn well deserved better than this. Warner Bros.’ Joker earned another $760,000 (-48%) to bring its cume to $324.8 million domestic and $1.025 billion worldwide. It has passed/will pass Deadpool 2 ($324 million) and Suicide Squad ($325 million) in raw domestic earnings, with Batman v Superman next on the proverbial chopping block. Focus Features’ Harriet earned $660,000 (-47%) on Friday for a $2.3 million (-50%) fourth weekend and $36 million 24-day total. It has tripled its opening weekend and doubled its $17 million budget. Terminator: Dark Fate will earn $1.29 million (-70%) for a $59 million 24-day total.
