Forecasters warn of high impact, major winter storm

23 Nov 2019

SOUTHLAND - (INT) - Mother Nature is putting winter at our doorstep. A Thanksgiving storm should complete the seasonal transition.

Forecasters are ramping up their outlook warning that Wednesday through Friday will feature a high impact, major winter storm.

Precursor showers may begin Tuesday evening followed by widespread rain that could be heavy at times Wednesday mixed with isolated thunderstorms. Localized flooding will be possible, and there may be a threat of debris flows in and near recent burn scars.

A cold air mass settles over the area Thanksgiving Day into Friday pushing down the freezing levels with heavy snow in many mountain communities.

All this carries a potential impact on the busy holiday travel period with widespread hazardous conditions on the roadways throughout Southern California.

Partly cloudy, cool weather will finish off the holiday weekend.
