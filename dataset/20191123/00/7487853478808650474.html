West Ham 2-3 Tottenham: Report, Ratings & Reaction as Jose Mourinho Wins First Game in Charge

23 Nov 2019 by Ben Carter

​Jose Mourinho won his first game in charge of Tottenham thanks to goals from Son Heung-min, Lucas Moura and Harry Kane away at West Ham, surviving a late comeback to pick up three points on Saturday.

Harry Kane had the ball in the back of the net inside the opening three minutes but his goal was correctly ruled out for offside by the linesman, however even match official couldn't stop Son Heung-min from putting the visitors into the lead with a left-footed shot across the goalkeeper half an hour later.

Alli played a big role in the opening goal, and the England international was pivotal again for Tottenham's second as an improvised pass released Son, who squared a pass for Lucas Moura who had the simple tap in at the far post.

Kane finally got in on the act just after the break to move clear as Tottenham's third-highest goalscorer of all time, racing onto the end of an inch-perfect cross from Serge Aurier to put the game out of sight.

Substitute Michail Antonio pulled one goal back for West Ham, who even added a second goal with their last kick of the game, but it was too little too late for the hosts and Tottenham held on to pick up their first win under Mourinho.

Key Talking Point

There were spells of the match where West Ham looked dangerous, but for the most part it was a performance that has all-but handed Manuel Pellegrini his P45.

It was all too easy for Tottenham to work their way into attacking areas and into positions where they could get shots off at an out of form Sergi Roberto, but it was a crippling lack of quality going forward that ensured West Ham always had an impossible mountain to climb.
