Sebastopol man rejects sentence indication on firearm charges

23 Nov 2019 by Erin Williams

A Sebastopol man will fight an allegation he was the owner of an unregistered homemade handgun, which was located on his kitchen bench with a spent shell inside, along with ammunition.

Kye Fulton fronted the Ballarat Magistrates Court on Thursday for a contested mention over the 2018 incident.

The 28-year-old sought a sentence indication on five charges, including possessing an unregistered handgun, possessing ammunition, failing to answer bail and possessing methamphetamine.

Police prosecutor Sergeant Aimee Heal said police searched the accused's Sebastopol home on October 9, 2018, where they uncovered a homemade unregistered handgun with a spent shell inside and ammunition on the kitchen bench.

She said after a safety and evidentiary search on Fulton, police found a zip lock bag containing methamphetamine in his wallet, which was in his pocket.

Sergeant Heal said Fulton did not have a gun licence. He was arrested and transported to the Ballarat Police Station where he made a no comment interview.

The concreter was bailed to appear at the Ballarat Magistrates Court on February 7, but he allegedly failed to appear.

Defence lawyer David Tamanika said the firearm charges were in dispute because his client did not know the gun was there.

He said the Sebastopol property, which Fulton owned, was being used as a drop-in centre by his brother and his brother's friend.

Mr Tamanika said Fulton had briefly called into the house the day police arrived and the names of Fulton's brother and his friend were also on the police search warrant.

"He said he had no knowledge of the item on the bench," Mr Tamanika said.

"It's a difficult one, Your Honour. He is concerned about prison, he's never been there before. He is 28 and may not cope well in custody."

Mr Tamanika urged the court to consider a non-custodial sentence if Fulton pleaded guilty.

Magistrate Michelle Mykytowycz reminded Fulton he would be admitting he owned the gun if he accepted her sentence indication.

She said the alleged crime warranted an immediate term of imprisonment.

"This is one where there has been firing in the community because it had a spent shell in it and there was ammunition nearby," Ms Mykytowcycz said.

"On this sentence indication, it would be a term of imprisonment."

Fulton rejected the sentence indication and his case was adjourned to another contested mention at the Ballarat Magistrates Court on January 15.
