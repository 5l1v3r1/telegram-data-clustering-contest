The World’s Largest Indigenous Tourism Conference Takes Place In Kelowna, BC

23 Nov 2019 by Fiona Tapp

Conferences are not usually described as emotional, inspiring or thought provoking. But not all conferences are the largest Indigenous tourism conference in the world, either.Bringing together travel professionals, business owners, artists, creatives and culinary experts from across Canada, the 8th annual International Indigenous Tourism Conference (IITC) was held on the traditional and unceded territory of the Syilx Nation in beautiful Kelowna, British Columbia.

Jointly hosted by The Indigenous Tourism Association of Canada (ITAC) and Indigenous Tourism BC (ITBC) the conference spanned three days and included local cultural tours, rousing key note speaker addresses, music, dance, an awards ceremony and plenty of exciting news from the world’s largest growing travel market.

Indigenous owned, operated and led travel companies are a booming industry offering authentic experiences and education with the latest travel trends showing visitors want more mindful tours and activities that offer a transformative travel experience.

In fact, Indigenous tourism has seen phenomenal growth and acceleration beyond overall tourism activity. This fact was lauded during the conference along with these other key special moments.

A Cultural Celebration

ITAC exists to champion and empower Indigenous people to own, operate, and excel in tourism roles and enterprises in 10 provinces and 3 territories across Canada.

These businesses include restaurants, hotels, art galleries, cultural experiences, tours and more. During the course of the conference delegates and guests were treated to a range of performances and were led on cultural tours to community centers and businesses to listen and learn.

Two outstanding performances that led to a standing ovation included a boot tapping musical fiddle show from Double The Trouble. These twin 15-year-old Cree and French- Metis boys delivered an energetic performance of traditional and modern songs.

Another favorite performance during the event was by PIQSIQ. Sisters Kayley Inuksuk Mackay and Tiffany Kuliktana Ayalik are Inuit throat singers, and members of the Juno Award winning band, Quantum Tangle.

Their performance was haunting and totally thrilled the crowd.

Award Winning Indigenous Tourism

Special awards were given to outstanding Indigenous entrepreneurs and business owners across Canada.

“As we grow, we hope these awards serve as examples of best practices and we encourage our stakeholders to share and learn from each other,” said Keith Henry, CEO and President of ITAC.

The nominees in each category were chosen from majority Indigenous-owned and controlled travel companies and tourism businesses.

The winners represented the most innovative businesses and tours from diverse Indigenous nations across the land.

The winners included:

  • Best Marketing Campaign- Vacances Essipit, QC
  • Most Improved Business-Site Interpretation Micmac de Gespeg​, QC
  • Most Inspiring Cultural Ambassador-Levy Uttaq, ​Igloo Tourism​, NU
  • Indigenous Adventure Award – Presented by Adventure Travel Trade Association (ATTA)-Wikwemikong Tourism​, ON
  • Inspiring Indigenous Culinary Award -Buffalo Point Lodge​, MB
  • ITBC Awards- Remarkable Indoor Cultural Experiences- St. Eugene Resort & Casino
  • Exceptional Outdoor Cultural Experiences -Homalco Wildlife and Cultural Tours
  • Outstanding Indigenous Accommodations -The Lund resort at Kla ah men
  • Customer Service Award -Sierra Hall-​ Spirit Bear Lodge

Taste Of The Nations

Indigenous cuisine continues to grow in popularity all over the world with more and more Indigenous owned food businesses opening every year.

With the New Indigenous Culinary Tourism Strategy even more people across Canada and the world will have the opportunity to try the huge range of delicious Indigenous traditional and modern fusion foods.

Co-chair of the Indigenous Culinary of Associated Nations, Bill Alexander, is the executive chef at Calgary’s Grey Eagle Resort & Casino and a consulting chef for WestJet Airlines. He says, “Working with ITAC, together we will help foster the entrepreneurial spirit of Indigenous people to create more genuine Indigenous culinary offerings and connect visitors with the types of culinary tourism experiences they will not find in any other country in the world.”

Creating a One Stop Shop To Book Indigenous Experiences

The Indigenous Tourism Association of Canada announced at the conference a new agreement with Indigeno Travel, which will become their travel agency of choice.

This decision makes planning, organizing and booking indigenous travel experiences easier than ever.
