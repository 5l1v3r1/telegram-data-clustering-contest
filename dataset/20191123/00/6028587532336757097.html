Box Office: ‘It Chapter Two’ Petering Out After Barely Cracking $50,000

23 Nov 2019 by Travis Bean

As It Chapter Two heads into its 12th weekend in theaters, it looks as though the end is near. After opening to a stellar $91.1 million weekend back on Sept. 6-8, this past weekend was barely able to drum up $50,000 from its scattered showings across the U.S.While It Chapter Two has undoubtedly been a huge success for Warner Bros., it hasn’t been able to mimic the record-shattering presence the first film had back in 2017. On opening weekend, It opened to a $123.4 million weekend, which was followed by a $60.1 million weekend. During those two weeks, It played in about 4,100 theaters.

However, It Chapter Two’s second weekend—which saw 4,570 theaters across the country—only pulled in $39.6 million. That put Chapter Two’s two-week revenue a full $74.4 million behind its predecessor.

Surprising, It’s run in theaters was short compared to most blockbusters producing nine-figure grosses. Sans inflation, It is easily the highest-grossing horror movie of all time. However, the film only spent 14 weeks in theaters. After weekly revenue dipped down to $1.3 million during Week 9, its theater count fells from 1,181 to just 578. After that, its quick exit from theaters before the holiday season became inevitable.

So with Christmastime approaching once again, we’re seeing the end of It’s sequel as well. Chapter Two also saw a big drop in weekly revenue during Week 9 when it pulled in $249,618. After that, its theater count dropped dramatically from 1,101 to just 126.

Then this past weekend, Chapter Two was only able to muster $59,361 from 132 theaters. That puts its Weekend 11 gross nearly $100,000 behind the first film.

At this point, It Chapter Two is just narrowly beating out movies like Once Upon a Time in Hollywood (currently in its 18th week in theaters), The Lion King (in its 19th week in theaters) and Toy Story 4 (in its 23rd week in theaters). So before the barrage of holiday films hits the market, we’ll likely see the much-anticipated sequel to It exit theaters. Considering you can now stream the film on Amazon, that isn’t too much of a surprise.
