Frank Lampard Explains Why His Spell at Manchester City Helped Open Door to Managerial Career

23 Nov 2019 by Ben Carter

​Frank Lampard claims that his one-season spell at Manchester City at the end of his playing career helped him to pave his way in management.

The Chelsea boss is only in his second season as a head coach, but Lampard's side head into matchday 13 as the most in-form team in Europe's top five leagues - winning their last six games in a row in the Premier League.

Lampard, who actually scored against Chelsea when he was on the books at Manchester City as a player, returns to Etihad Stadium for the first time this weekend.

And speaking ahead of the game, Lampard praised his time in the north-west for helping him transition into life as a manager.

"The year in Manchester, in terms of what I'm doing here now, was perfect for me because it opened my eyes to another club, another structure, another dressing-room, another coaching philosophy, another idea," Lampard said, quoted by The Daily Mail.

"And then at New York [Red Bulls], again that continued and that starts to shape what you're thinking next.

"At the time I was trying to look around me more and see things from a different side, which was less as a player and more about how I'd work with people around me.

"I can't look back on my career now with any negative feelings even about leaving Chelsea now. I just look back with a thankful feeling that I had such a long career at great places."

Lampard's managerial career started last season with Championship side Derby County, where The Rams went on to reach the play-off final but ultimately fell short against Aston Villa.

Since taking over at Chelsea, who will be under a transfer ban until next summer, Lampard has lost five of his 19 games across all competitions - including the UEFA Super Cup against Liverpool.

But the Blues have been one of the surprise packages in the Premier League this season and they could move four points clear of Manchester City this weekend.
