900,000 cigarette packs seized for not having valid tax stamps

23 Nov 2019, 16:48

Okaz/Saudi Gazette

JEDDAH — Inspection teams have seized over 900,000 cigarette packets of various brands for violating a ban on sale of cigarette packets not bearing valid and activated tax stamps, the General Authority of Zakat and Tax (GAZT) announced on Friday.

The ban came into effect on Monday, Nov. 18.

On its Twitter account, GAZT added that the cigarettes were seized during 1,200 visits to shops and sales outlets in different regions of the Kingdom.

GAZT further said: “This decision is based on the provisions of the Executive Regulations of the Selective Tax Law related to tax stamps within the sphere of the Unified Agreement on Selective Tax for GCC States.”

A tax stamp is defined as a “distinctive mark” in the form of a physical label or symbol containing encrypted digital data, placed on selective commodity products and activated electronically. Producers and importers of selective goods have to follow this system before displaying them for consumption.

GAZT said that by implementing the tax stamp regulation they would enhance the imposition of the supervisory controls on collecting taxes on selective commodities being imported to the Kingdom.

The measure will also boost the capability to verify whether the selective commodities have entered the Kingdom legally or not. The objective is to ensure collection of all due taxes on such a commodity.

The taxes fall under the category of selective taxes on products deemed harmful to public health. The application of tax stamps ensures that the standards of compliance set out in the WHO Convention on Tobacco Control are met.

GAZT has confirmed that a consumer can verify the validity of the tax stamps via smart phone app “Tahaqqaq” (Verify), which the authority launched simultaneously with the implementation of the tax stamp regulation, enables a user to scan the barcode on the cigarette pack.

GAZT has called upon all consumers to report on illegal products via its official website gazt.gov.sa; the Unified Call Center number 19993 or the “Tahaqqaq” (Verify) smart phone app.

The decision on tax stamps on tobacco products will be extended later to include other tobacco products, like shisha (hubble-bubble), and then to soft drinks and energy drinks subject to selective tax in the Kingdom.

GAZT announced earlier that 100 percent tax would be levied on e-cigarettes and products used in them, and a 50 percent tax on sugared drinks.
