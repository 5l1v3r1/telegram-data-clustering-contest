'Liverpool's best signing of the season' - Rival fans have their say on Palace VAR controversy

Rival supporters left thinking VAR is always going in Liverpool's favour this season

23 Nov 2019, 16:53 by Lee Wilmot

There was controversy in the game between Crystal Palace and Liverpool at Selhurst Park on Saturday.

Liverpool led the way at the top of the Premier League table at the start of the day's play - eight points clear of Leicester and Chelsea.

But the looked to be in trouble as the match with the Eagles headed towards half-time when Palace took the lead.

Or so they thought.

A free-kick from the far left-hand side was nodded on and headed home at the back post by James Tomkins in the 42nd minute.

But as has often been the case this season, VAR intervened.

After a long delay, the video assistant decided to disallow the goal because of a foul in the build-up. Palace striker Jordan Ayew was adjudged to have pushed Dejan Lovren in the area as the free-kick was delivered into the box.

It is not the first time the Reds have benefited from VAR interventions this season and with them far out in front at the top of the table, rival fans from Manchester United, Chelsea, Tottenham and others have been having their say on the decisions that seem to be going the league leaders' way.
