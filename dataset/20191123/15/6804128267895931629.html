Dele Alli opens up on his talk with Jose Mourinho before impressive performance vs West Ham

Mourinho got off to the perfect start as Tottenham beat West Ham 3-2 on Saturday in his first match

23 Nov 2019, 15:15 by Elliott Jackson

Tottenham Hotspur star Dele Alli has revealed that Jose Mourinho made a joke about him playing "like his brother" after he was appointed as the club's new manager.

The Portuguese coach replaced Mauricio Pochettino this week after he was sacked after five years in charge of Spurs.

Mourinho enjoyed the perfect start to management as he watched his side race into a 3-0 lead away at West Ham in his first match.

The final score ended 3-2 but the win secured a first league away victory since late January for Spurs, who have struggled on the road in 2019.

Alli was central to all good things about Tottenham's play, setting up the first goal for Heung-Min Son before showcasing an audacious bit of skill in the build-up for Lucas Moura to double their advantage.

Playing in a more tailored number ten role behind Harry Kane, Alli looked back to his best after being afforded the license to express himself by Mourinho.

However, after the match, Alli confirmed that reports detailing a discussion between the pair where Mourinho asked the Englishman if he was his brother or the real Dele Alli were true, in a bid to spur on Alli after his dip in form over the last 18 months.

"Yeah, he made a joke about it but a lot of people have been talking about that for a few years so it's nothing new," Alli told BT Sport after the full-time whistle against West Ham.

"Like I said, I think I've performed well this season despite the results. I haven't been playing that bad but I have to just keep getting better.

"A lot of people have asked me or said 'when am I going to find my old form?' but I don't want to find that old form, I want to get better than that, improve and reach my full potential."

He added: "You never know, people might think I played bad today, people might think I played good but, for me, we won, so I'm happy."
