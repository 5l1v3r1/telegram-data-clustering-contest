'Risk of flooding' as 18 hours of rain forecast for Nottinghamshire

The yellow weather warning for rain is in place from the Met Office from midnight on Wednesday November 27 to 6pm on the same day

23 Nov 2019, 15:05 by Ben Reid

The wet autumnal weather is set to continue as the Met Office issued another warning for Nottinghamshire.

Eighteen hours of rain is predicted to batter the county and Yorkshire on Wednesday, November 27.

The yellow weather warning for rain is in place from the Met Office from midnight on November 27 to 6pm on the same day, with a risk of flooding possible.

A warning had previously been put in place for Saturday, November 23, but this was later cancelled by the Met Office.

In a statement on its website about the Wednesday warning the weather forecaster said: "Further heavy rain is possible on Wednesday with a risk of flooding.

"Spray and flooding could lead to difficult driving conditions and some road closures.

"Where flooding occurs, there is a slight chance of delays or cancellations to train and bus services.

"There is a small chance that homes and businesses could be flooded, causing damage to some buildings.

"There is a small chance that some communities become cut off by flooded roads.

"There is a small chance of fast flowing or deep floodwater causing danger to life."

Large parts of Nottinghamshire were hit hard by severe flooding on Thursday and Friday November 14/15 including Nottingham city centre streets.
