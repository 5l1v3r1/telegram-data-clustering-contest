Mourinho breathes new life into Spurs as reinvigorated Alli runs riot

23 Nov 2019, 15:33

After a whirlwind week that started with Mauricio Pochettino but will end with Jose Mourinho, there was a palpable sense of relief among the Tottenham players at the London Stadium that the focus had shifted back to on-field matters.

For a club so prone to upheavel during the early years of Daniel Levy’s tenure, Pochettino’s reign brought a welcomed calmness for players and supporters alike but that script was ripped up this week as the club turned to Mourinho, a manager that guarantees drama above and before anything else.

The manner of Mourinho’s exit from Manchester United and indeed Chelsea before that means there’s valid question marks over his capabilities but there was enough evidence on display against West Ham to suggest he retains some of the man-management that once made him the most coveted manager in world football

Mourinho was only afforded three training sessions with the group before his side’s trip to the London Stadium, meaning any tactical changes would be tweaks rather than grand overhauls. Even then, there’s simply not been enough time for him to imprint his tactical vision on a Spurs side that have heard the same voices for the last five-and-a-half seasons.

But even small changes can make a difference and the evidence of that was all too apparent in Deli Alli’s performance. Alli’s been a shadow of his former self in the last 18 months as he moved deeper into midfield, further away from goal and further still from the player that made him one of the most exciting talents in world football.

As Spurs fans pored over every piece of footage from Mourinho’s first 72 hours at the club, there was evidence of a marked difference in his approach to certain players. His first encounter with Heung-Min Son, for example, was soft and friendly. There was an arm around the shoulder for the South Korean, who was one of the few players who didn’t allow his performance level to dip too far below the required standard in Pochettino’s final days at the club.
