Jose Mourinho doing Jose things: joking with the ball-boys in the tunnel before the game in full view of the television camera, greeting Manuel Pellegrini as if he, Mourinho, were the home manager welcoming an overawed lower league boss on FA Cup third round day. The ball-boys loved every moment of it, and soon so too did Mourinho.

By half-time Tottenham Hotspur were two goals up, by 50 minutes they should have been four up. Dele Alli, of whom Mourinho had asked the question whether he had been replaced by a less talented sibling, played the first half like the classic older brother lauding it over the little ones. Spurs controlled the game for an hour but another ten minutes at the end and West Ham, who were dismal in the first half, might have grabbed a draw.

Mourinho’s team selection on the occasion of the fourth coming of Jose was as expected, with Eric Dier back in the team and a golden front four of Son Heung-min, Lucas Moura, Harry Kane and Dele who all played within a few yards of each other. Dele was everywhere, the best of it an improvised flick with the inside of his left boot as he was falling over backwards on the touchline to play in Son to cross to Moura for the second. Dele had made the first for Son too. Moura missed a simple chance for the third on the counterattack before Harry Kane nodded one in from Serge Aurier’s cross.

West Ham were dreadful in the first half and many of their support had gone home by the time they rallied in the last 30 minutes. The half-time substitute Michail Antonio scored on 73 minutes and they made a game of it in the final stages. Nevertheless this is a run of five defeats and two draws in their last seven league games. It is Chelsea away next Saturday and Manuel Pellegrini looks like a man on the brink.

“How s--- must you be, we’re winning away” sang the Spurs fans celebrating their first away win in the league since Fulham in January. In the last of six minutes of time added on, Angelo Ogbonna scored West Ham’s second with barely enough time to re-start the game. It gave Mourinho plenty to think about.
