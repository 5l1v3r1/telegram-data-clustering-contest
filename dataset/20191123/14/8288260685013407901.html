Primark issues statement as excited rumours of Tamworth store circulate

Shoppers have moved to social media to reveal their excitement over the speculative plans for Ventura Park - but nothing has been confirmed by Primark

23 Nov 2019, 14:48 by Stephanie Balloo, https://www.facebook.com/MissBalloo/

Primark has responded to frenzied speculation that an announcement is imminent on a new store rumoured to be opening in Tamworth.

Social media rumours - not citing any proof - that the budget fashion chain would be moving into Ventura Park in the near future have sparked excitement in the town.

It was said to be taking over the former Matalan unit - with many happy shoppers welcoming the idea.

JaymeLeigh Silver said: "yesssss! Love the place and don’t care what anyone else thinks."

Jo Cope Davis added: "Really hope they do bring a Primark here..people can call the store down all they like but fab store for kids clothes especially the way my daughter grows..

"Cheap and cheerful store for those of us who refuse to pay triple the price elsewhere when a child grows so quick."

But others were sent into a panic over the potential traffic problems caused by the alleged opening.

One said: "Traffic is already a nightmare there despite the changes to add exits on two sites, let's hope people learn the road lanes a bit better before someone dies if the traffic increases due to Primark!"

However, the social media speculation has not been confirmed by Primark, with no sign of the potential store listed as one of their 19 new stores either.

A Primark spokesperson said when approached by BirminghamLive: "Unfortunately, we don't have immediate plans to locate a new store in Tamworth in the near future."

BirminghamLive has also approached Ventura Park for a comment.

Primark has, however, confirmed it is opening 19 new stores in the coming year - after a successful launch of  the world's biggest shop in Birmingham .

It will launch new UK stores everywhere from Hastings and Bluewater to Belfast and Milton Keynes.

Primark chief executive George Weston previously said: "In the next financial year, we are planning to add a net 1million sq ft of additional selling space.

"We expect to open 19 new stores together with a number of relocations and extensions."

The company have also recently celebrated their 50th anniversary along with their 20 millionth social media follower as customers continue to flock to stores around the world.

Sales were up 4.3% and operating profit rose by 8%.
