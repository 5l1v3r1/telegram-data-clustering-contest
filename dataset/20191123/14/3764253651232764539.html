Alleged N3billion election largesse causes rancour within PDP

23 Nov 2019, 14:35

A report by The Nation indicates that the governorship elections in Kogi and Bayelsa states have pitched senior PDP members against themselves.

According to the report, a N3 billion largesse doled out by one of the party's governors to some influential members of the PDP in Abuja has triggered crisis within its fold.

Already, some aggrieved party members are clamouring for sanctions against the recipients of the said sum.

The party chieftains were said to have demanded for N6 billion to facilitate the victory of the PDP’s candidates but got N3 billion as advanced payment.

It was not clear whether the governor in question would be willing to pay the outstanding sum with the party’s loss of the election or demand a refund of the sum already paid.

“The governor who gave out the money is said to be peeved that the party’s National Working Committee has been unduly quiet about the results of the elections,” the report stated.

Meanwhile, Bayelsa governor, Seriake Dickson, has described the just concluded governorship election in the state as a military coup.

Addressing journalists in Yenagoa on Tuesday, November 19, Governor Dickson said the election was a charade and a carefully orchestrated plan to forcefully take over Bayelsa state towards entrenching a one-party system in Nigeria.
