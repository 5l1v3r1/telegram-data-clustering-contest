Manuel Pellegrini may regret Adrian comments after another goalkeeping horror show

The Reds number two left the Hammers for free in the summer

23 Nov 2019, 14:06 by Elliott Jackson

West Ham United manager Manuel Pellegrini may begin to rue his latest comments about Liverpool goalkeeper Adrian.

The Spaniard left the Hammers in the summer on a free transfer and has proved a handy addition for the Reds after an early-season injury to Alisson.

Adrian deputised fantastically, earning himself an instant spot in the hearts of the Liverpool supporters.

However, West Ham are now suffering a goalkeeping crisis of their own, with first-choice shot-stopper Lukas Fabianski ruled out until 2020.

Following the departure of Adrian, West Ham pumped for Roberto as their new number two from Espanyol.

However, the Spaniard has suffered a rocky start to life in goal with a number of high-profile mistakes, including a comical own goal where he punched a corner into his own net.

Despite that, Pellegrini has insisted he doesn't regret letting Adrian leave the club in the summer.

"No regrets because we did everything we could to keep Adrian,” he told the Evening Standard.

"He was a player who played six or seven years at West Ham and was a goalkeeper I trusted a lot, but for different reasons, he did not arrive to reach a new contract.

"I do not have any regrets about that because I have a lot of trust in Roberto."

However, Roberto's latest blunder may have the former Manchester City boss reconsidering his goalkeeping options in January.

Roberto was at fault yet against on Saturday as Heung-Min Son fired the ball through the goalkeeper for Tottenham's opening goal against the Hammers.

After yet another high-profile mistakes, West Ham fans are calling for Pellegrini, and their new goalkeeper, to be axed, with both badly out of form.

With that in mind, Liverpool fans are all saying the same thing about Adrian's summer move.
