Scooter Braun Makes A Public Appeal To Taylor Swift As He And His Family Receive Death Threats

23 Nov 2019, 14:54 by Gretel Sequeira

Taylor Swift has been winning hearts for years with her interesting and gripping music. However, she has been fighting for the rights to her music with The Machine Label Group. Now, Scooter Braun took to his social media to publicly appeal to the Shake It Off singer to end the dispute over her music catalog

Taylor Swift has carved a niche for herself in the music industry with her entertaining songs. The singer rose to fame because of her songs based mainly on her life, and Taylor has been soaring high since then. The Shake It Off singer has managed to win many Grammys as well as other notable accolades due to her contribution to music. As Taylor’s fans might be well aware of the post she shared over her dispute with the music label ‘The Machine Label Group’ a few days ago, the music manager Scooter Braun has now released a public appeal to the singer via a social media, to end the dispute due to concerning reasons.

Music manager Scooter Braun of ‘The Machine Label Group’ recently took to his social media to publicly appeal to Taylor Swift to end  her nearly six-months-old dispute over her music catalog. Scooter took to his Instagram to share a long-written note and requested the singer to put an end to the dispute. In his social media post, Braun spoke at length about Taylor’s statement made last week and mentioned about receiving several anonymous death threats directed at his family. The music manager also mentioned how he broke his stoic silence for the first time publicly at an entertainment conference on Thursday and shared that he wouldn’t participate in a social media war with the singer. Talking about the death threats, Braun shared that when he arrived at home, he found out about the phone call his wife received, threatening the safety of his children as well as many other threats.

Meanwhile, Taylor Swift had publicly claimed last week that Braun who had purchased her former music label, Big Machine Label Group, along with Scott Borchetta, was blocking the addition of her songs in an upcoming Netflix special as well as were depriving her of singing her old classics at the upcoming AMAs. The Red the singer wrote, “Neither of these men had a hand in the writing of those songs. They did nothing to create the relationship I have with my fans. So this is where I'm asking for your help. Please let Scott Borchetta and Scooter Braun know how you feel about this.” While Swift has left the label group last year to move on further with the Universal group, the Big Machine Label Group has clearly denied the allegations made by the singer.
