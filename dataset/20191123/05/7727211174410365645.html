Everton prepare to regenerate as David Unsworth names six teenagers in starting XI for Tottenham defeat

Everton's winless Premier League run extended to a seventh game but it felt like the start of something new, says Sam Carroll

23 Nov 2019, 05:00 by Sam Carroll

It was a night that will leave David Unsworth disappointed but looking to the future. 

Everton, defending their Premier League 2 crown but winless in six games, ensured the streak continued to seven with a 3-1 defeat against Tottenham Hotspur at Southport.

First-half goals from Dilan Markanday and Armando Shashoua left the under-23s with an uphill challenge at the break but Shashoua's second with over half-an-hour remaining dashed any hopes of a comeback despite Dennis Adeniran's consolation and a flurry of late pressure. 

Unsworth will, obviously, hope it heads in a different direction results-wise from here but it felt, in some ways, like the start of something new.

With Morgan Feeney and Lewis Gibson both missing from the heart of defence, the manager was forced to start a back five that has not started a competitive game together.

Harry Tyrer, the goalkeeper, is just 17 while centre-back Ryan Astley is a year older. Kyle John, at left-back, is the same age, Alex Denny 19 and Matty Foulds, expected to move on at the end of the campaign when his contract expires, the eldest at 21.

All this while being screened by a 16-year-old midfielder in the form of Tyler Onyango. It is not to say it was a totally new side. Antony Evans, the skipper, is another who will be eyeing a move away from Goodison Park in the near future while Harry Charsley's short-team deal expires in January.

Nathangelo Markelo will have suitors if his first-team chances remain distant while Dennis Adeniran, who trains with Marco Silva's squad, could seek a temporary switch in January if Everton move for a senior midfielder.

But with six teenagers starting - and another, in the form of Manasse Mampala, being introduced from the bench alongside 20-year-old Con Ouzounidis - the time seems right for Unsworth and his team to regenerate.

The manager has been here before after winning the title in 2017.

Jonjoe Kenny, Tom Davies, Dominic Calvert-Lewin and Ademola Lookman all made appearances that season as well as the likes of Liam Walsh, Matthew Pennington and David Henen.

Unsworth must now ensure the progression of Tyrer, Denny, Onyango and co. heads in the same direction as the stars plying their trade in Everton's first-team, the Football League and beyond.

The loss to Tottenham was by no means an audition and, in fairness, by no means did the new-look defence let anybody down. They will, without a doubt, have more chances to impress. 

But lapses at this level will be punished more severely than what the young stars are used to and getting up to speed will take some time.

Hopefully a cold Southport evening can be their first lesson on the way to the Premier League, to Goodison Park and senior football.

For now, Unsworth has been given the initial tools for a new challenge.

He will hope they have the drive and character of former graduates that enables them not only to compete and gain results for his side, but to eventually make that next - and hardest - step to the professional game.
