Maharashtra power twist: Devendra Fadnavis takes oath as CM, Ajit Pawar as Deputy CM | Who said what?

The month-long political impasse ended dramatically with Devendra Fadnavis returning as chief minister of Maharashtra for the second term and NCP leader Ajit Pawar taking oath as the deputy chief minister at the Raj Bhavan on Saturday morning.

23 Nov 2019, 05:28

While the Shiv Sena was trying to canvas support from the Congress and the NCP to keep BJP out of power in Maharashtra, a stunning move by the Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP) wherein it roped in NCP's Ajit Pawar and gave him the deputy chief minister's post, has stirred the state's politics and triggered reactions from top leaders across the country.

The month-long political impasse ended dramatically with Devendra Fadnavis returning as chief minister of Maharashtra for the second term and NCP leader Ajit Pawar taking oath as the deputy chief minister at the Raj Bhavan on Saturday morning.

While the surprising move by the BJP has left everyone wide-eyed, Ajit Pawar, who is the nephew of NCP chief Sharad Pawar, is now being accused of backstabbing the party and Shiv Sena by joining hands with the BJP to form government in Maharashtra.

Reactions from leaders across the country are pouring in as the Maharashtra deal got sealed today. Here are LIVE updates:

PM Modi congratulates

Prime Minister Narendra Modi was the first to congratulate BJP's Devendra Fadnavis and NCP's Ajit Pawar on taking oath as Maharashtra's Chief Minister and Deputy Chief Minister respectively.

Taking to Twitter, PM Modi said, "Congratulations to Devendra Fadnavis Ji and Ajit Pawar Ji on taking oath as the CM and Deputy CM of Maharashtra respectively. I am confident they will work diligently for the bright future of Maharashtra."
