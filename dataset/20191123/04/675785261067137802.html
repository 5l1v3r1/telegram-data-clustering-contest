Chelsea 'to make January move' for Fedor Chalov if transfer ban lifted

Frank Lampard's side expected to target striker who has been linked with Arsenal, Liverpool and Man Utd - if club's appeal against ban is successful next month

23 Nov 2019, 04:46 by Gareth Bicknell

Chelsea are reportedly weighing up a January move for striker Fedor Chalov if the club's transfer ban is lifted next month.

Frank Lampard's Chelsea will find out in mid-December whether their appeal against the ban has been successful.

And if the ban is lifted, Chelsea will target CSKA Moscow striker Chalov, has previously been linked with Arsenal, Liverpool and Manchester United .

That is according to Sky Sports , who report that Chelsea owner Roman Abramovic is a fan of his fellow Russian.

Chelsea, who play champions Manchester City in a huge Premier League clash on Saturday, have blooded several of the club's talented young players this season after they were banned from making signings.

Tammy Abraham, Mason Mount and Fikayo Tomori have all shown that the future is bright at Stamford Bridge - but Chelsea are still expected to target new recruits in January if their appeal against their transfer ban is successful.

But they will face competition for Chalov, who has scored five goals so far this season for CSKA in the Russian Premier Liga.

The 21-year-old has been tipped as Russian football's star of the future with a goal record and lethal finishing ability that is attracting Germany's leading clubs Bayern Munich, Borussia Dortmund and RB Leipzig, as well as French side Monaco and Sevilla of Spain.

Chalov, who has been at CSKA since he was eight, finished last season as the Russian Premier League's top scorer on 15 goals – two ahead of Zenit St Petersburg's Sardar Azmoun.

He also weighed in with seven assists to enhance his growing reputation as an attacker who can score and make goals with his eye for a pass, smart decoy runs and awareness of space.

Chalov signed an improved deal in January that runs until June 2022 – but the talented striker is expected to be a hot property in January and in the summer.
