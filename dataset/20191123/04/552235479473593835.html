Baby blinded in one eye after sickening assault by woman in Falkirk

The baby suffered rib fractures, bleeding on the brain, a broken right wrist, fractures to the base of both big toes and a detached retina at the hands of Caroline Thomson.

23 Nov 2019, 04:30 by Wilma Riley

A baby was blinded in one eye after a woman attacked him, a court heard yesterday.

Caroline Thomson, 30, of Falkirk, admitted assaulting the tiny tot to his permanent impairment, permanent disfigurement and to the danger of his life.

The offence was committed on various occasions between May 2 and June 16 last year, when the boy was aged between three weeks and nine weeks, at addresses in Larbert, Stirlingshire, and Falkirk.

The baby suffered multiple rib fractures, bleeding on the brain, a broken right wrist, fractures to the base of both big toes and a detached retina, the High Court in Glasgow heard.

Prosecutor Kath Harper said: “The accused told the police the injuries may have been caused when she dropped the baby and said some of the injuries could have been caused by a two-year-old child.”

The court heard consultant ophthalmologist Jennifer Ann Gillen examined the boy’s eyes.

She said she had never seen a detached retina in a young baby and such an injury would require some form of blunt force trauma.

The boy, who is now 19 months old, can’t speak and is unable to walk, although he can crawl.

Judge Lady Rae remanded Thomson in custody and deferred sentence until next month.

Defence QC Bert Kerrigan will give his plea in mitigation then.
