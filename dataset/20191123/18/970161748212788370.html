Iconic props from Friends are being auctioned off amid rumours of series reboot

23 Nov 2019, 18:38

Iconic props from Friends are set to be auctioned off – and could we be anymore excited about it?

From Joey’s Hugsy penguin to the Central Perk sofa and the famous Turkey that Monica wore on her head (as if we could forget) – yes that’s right you could own them – but at a hefty price.

The auction, organised by Prop Store, will offer more than 100 items that span across all 10 seasons of the sitcom at auction houses based out of London, England and Valencia, California, next month.

Hugsy and the wedding invitation are expected to fetch between $1,000 and $1,500 (£750-£1,150) with Chandler’s wood canoe potentially selling for $6,000 (£4,600).

Brandon Alinger, COO of Prop Store, said: ‘Prop Store is thrilled to be working with Warner Bros to auction some incredible material from all 10 seasons of Friends.

‘The series gave us so many laughs during its original run and continues to entertain and inspire fans around the world.

‘It’s an honour to be able to bring some amazing original pieces to the fans, such as Joey’s original Hugsy doll.’

Some items are being estimated to sell for prices as high as $8,000, for the famous orange Central Perk couch, or even $14,800 for Ross’s giant holiday armadillo costume.

The best part about the auction is that the VHS of the spoof porn film made by Phoebe’s sister Ursula, Buffay the Vampire Layer, is also up for sale.

Also, in case you need a costume to teach your son about Hannukah, the Armadillo costume Ross wore might be the one for you.

Proceeds from the auction will benefit The Trevor Project, the world’s largest suicide prevention and crisis intervention organisation for LGBTQ+ young people.

The news follows after the cast announced that they would be up for a reboot of Friends.

The Hollywood Reporter has said that all six cast members – Jennifer Aniston, Courtney Cox, Lisa Kudrow, Matt LeBlanc, David Schwimmer and Matthew Perry – are all set to be involved, but this is unconfirmed.

The publication made it clear that a deal ‘is far from done and agreements with cast and creatives still need to be hammered out’.

Jennifer Aniston previously whipped everybody into a frenzy after she joined Instagram – and made her first post a picture of herself and her co-stars back together.

The actress later confirmed to Ellen DeGeneres that on the show’s 25th anniversary there might be something in the making.

‘We would love for there to be something, but we don’t know what that something is. So we’re just trying,’ she said, adding: ‘We’re working on something.’

Friends originally began in September 1994 and ran for 10 years, with the final ever episode airing on 6 May 2004.

As it stands there are no plans for Friends to be spun off on to the big screen – and Jennifer Aniston recently spoke about why it’s unlikely to happen.

Bidding will begin on 3 December, known as Giving Tuesday, and runs until 17 December.
