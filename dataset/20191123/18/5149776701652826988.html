'It's got to change' - Neal Ardley concerned as Notts County are beaten at Aldershot

Magpies suffered back to back defeats for first time this season

23 Nov 2019, 18:26 by Leigh Curtis

Neal Ardley delivered a withering assessment of Notts County's defeat at Aldershot as the anniversary of his first year in charge at Meadow Lane was one to forget.

On the date he was appointed as Harry Kewell's successor last year, the Magpies failed to give him the celebration he was looking for as they were beaten 2-1 at the EBB Stadium.

Notts were the masters of their own downfall as they gifted possession away in midfield which led to a counter attack that ended with Mohamed Bettamer crashing a header beyond Sam Slocombe.

Kyle Wootton's seventh goal of the season put Notts back on level terms, but a poor pass from Ben Turner into the feet of Michael Doyle saw him lose possession to Alfy Whittingham who slipped the ball beyond Slocombe.

Notts could not haul themselves level for a second time and Ardley was disappointed with his side's desire.

"The mistakes are costing us but that’s not the bit that really gets to me,” he said.

“The rest of the game wasn’t intense enough and it’s got to change.

“The one thing that bothers me more than anything is it looked like the opposition wanted it more – and that was the case last week, too.

“Our levels have dropped. We’ve not had the spice and intensity we had earlier in the season, we haven’t won 50/50s and we’ve not done the nitty-gritty well enough in the last few matches.”

With Notts having grown a reputation as being one of the best teams in the league, Ardley feels that Notts have become the side everybody wants to beat.

And he says his side need to get used to dealing with the pressure of being a scalp.

“At the start of the season, we tried to recruit players we thought could handle the pressure of playing for Notts County,” he explained.

“People raise their game against us and if we let our levels drop like we have done the last two weeks, we get what we deserve – nothing.

“We have to expect teams to be aggressive against us and need to stand up, win our 50/50s and do the nitty-gritty stuff properly.

“Maybe teams are working us out but there are solutions to everything,” Ardley added.

“For 90 per cent of the season, the players have got it right and we’ll work to solve the problems.

“There’s a challenge on our hands. We’re a good team that is capable of getting in the play-offs but not unless we bring the side of the game that earns results when you don’t play well.”
