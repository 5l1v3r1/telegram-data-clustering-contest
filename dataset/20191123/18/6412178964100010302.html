Jurgen Klopp reveals why Liverpool struggled against Crystal Palace

23 Nov 2019, 18:10

Jurgen Klopp says Liverpool struggled against Crystal Palace as they weren’t compact enough following his side’s narrow 2-1 win.

Sadio Mane gave the league leaders the lead early in the second half before Wilfried Zaha equalised for Crystal Palace. A late penalty from Roberto Firmino though was enough to give Liverpool all three points.

“They [Crystal Palace] worked hard, I can only imagine what Roy Hodgson is feeling like because they would have deserved a point today. But we won the game and it was deserved today as well,” Klopp said after the match.

“We weren’t compact enough. Our distances between the full-backs and midfielders were too big. The set pieces were really dangerous and gave them the time to go there.

“It was a tough job to do today and at the end of the day I am happy with the result and big parts of the performances as well.”

It could’ve easily been a different result for Liverpool, with James Tomkins’s initial equaliser for Palace being harshly ruled out by VAR for a foul on Dejan Lovren.

“I’m not surprised at all that it was tough,” Klopp added.

“We knew at half time that we had to do better. We said we just have to make sure you are ready to fight right from the very start.

Getting a result at Crystal Palace, we will never take it for granted.”
