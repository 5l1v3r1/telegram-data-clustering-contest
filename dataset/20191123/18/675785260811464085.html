Customers forced to clean 'filthy' cinema seats with their own hands

Customers at Star City's Vue Cinema in Birmingham were disgusted to find a screen in an awful state with popcorn strewn across the floor and empty food and drink containers left on the seats

23 Nov 2019, 18:56 by Stephanie Balloo

Customers were left to clean popcorn off seats with their hands when they arrived at a filthy cinema.

Rubbish, abandoned empty cups and large amounts of popcorn were strewn across Star City's Vue Cinema in Birmingham on November 17.

Film-goers tried to clean some of the mess away so they could settle down and take in the showing without being immediately affected by the squalor, Birmingham Live reported .

A Vue spokesperson later admitted that a rota blunder meant the screen had not been cleaned.

A 26-year-old, who asked not to be named, described entering the room with his mother and brother.

"There were no clean seats," he said.

"We had to move the popcorn with our own hands. It was all up the stairs."

The man and his family members had spent £15 to see the late showing of Daaka, a Punjabi film.

But on arrival, they were greeted by popcorn strewn across the floor, seats and stairs.

Empty bottles still filled the cup holders and litter was seen dumped on every row from the last viewers.

"We got there about 11.30pm, went to the counter and was waiting 5-6 minutes, so we got our tickets from the machine," he added.

"We went in about 11.40 - 11.45pm. It was a complete mess. I took pictures and I complained to one of the staff members in the cinema and he apologised. But he didn't say why it happened.

"While I was at the cinema I emailed Vue. I've tried to phone them but it kept getting disconnected."

Vue issued an apology for the mess and sent the family three complimentary tickets.

A company spokesperson said: “The images taken at our Star City site clearly don’t meet our high standards of cleanliness.

"Following a review of the cleaning rota and system in place screen 10 was unfortunately overlooked on this evening.

"This would normally not occur and management have taken action to ensure this can’t happen again.

"We would like to apologise to customers who were affected.”
