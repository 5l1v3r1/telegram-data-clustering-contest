These are the shops you want to move to Burton

Disney and Debenhams are among them

23 Nov 2019, 06:30 by Helen Kreft

There always seems to be one shop or another closing down as news business continue to move into Burton of late.

Among big names to close in the town have been travel agent Thomas Cook, which had stores in Coopers Square shopping centre and Station Street, Toni and Guy hairdressers in Station Street and the Body Shop in Cooper's Square.

New to Burton though, were Menkind and Ronnie's Patisserie in Cooper's Square. Ronnie's replaced Patisserie Valerie, which closed in the centre of the shopping centre as the national chain went into administration.

All the changes have led to Burton Live readers having their say on which shops they would like to see open in the town - and there were lots well-known names on the wish list.

One top contender readers have said they would love to see the Disney toy store open in Burton as well as famous department store Debenhams.

The Disney store, which stocks all things connected to the famous film brand, is a popular shopping destinations in most cities, including Derby, but has never set up home in Burton. Readers say they would love to buy their Frozen II, Aladdin and Mickey Mouse goods right here in town.

Also on the wish list ws high-end underwear chain Victoria Secrets.

One reader, David Croxall, even suggested a Hooters, which is an American-style restaurant famous for its scantily-clad waitresses.

Cosmetics retailer and home of the hand-made bath bomb, Lush, was another popular suggestion among readers.

There were many of you who suggested a Debenhams, including Asma Khan, while Jenny Gidlow wants to see stores such as Wallis, Principles, and Phase Eight.

Antony Churchill would like to see jeans firm Levi's in town, while Ester Morris Nejedlova wants a Selfridges, and Vincy Plum opted for Victoria's Secret.

Kate Hickebottom wants to see clothing shop Superdry.

While Burton does not have a Superdry shop, in the town centre, it does boast the chain's warehouse at Clipper Logistics, in Wellington Road. However, apart from several families and friends discount events - it is not generally open to the public.

Meanwhile, Victoria Egginton has asked for an Oasis.

Bethany Whitby also suggested a Debenhams but was among others who wanted to see a TGI Fridays restaurant and a Disney store.

Lauren Stock, who also suggested a Disney store, even said Burton needs an Anime Japanese animation cartoons shop.

Joe Delacy-Lenton said he wants to see a comic book store after Burton lost Supa-Shakes, in Station Street in 2017.

Other readers want to see more toy shops in the town, with Steve McComish hoping to see his favourite shop of his childhood - Toyland - back.

James Bedford would like to see a Lego store, and Amy Whetton wants to see Mothercare brought back some years after it left Coopers Square shopping centre.
