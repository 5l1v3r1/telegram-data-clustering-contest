Sunderland academy product Brandon Taylor can cut it at first-team level, says Phil Parkinson

Sunderland boss Phil Parkinson was impressed with Brandon Taylor's man of the match performance in the FA Cup at Gillingham

23 Nov 2019, 06:00 by James Hunter

Brandon Taylor has proved he can cut it at first-team level, according to Phil Parkinson.

With injuries and international call-ups severely depleting Sunderland's options for the midweek FA Cup first round replay at Gillingham, 20-year-old academy centre-back Taylor stepped up from the U23 squad to start the biggest game so far in his fledgling career, with his previous first-team experience extending to just one start and one sub appearance in the EFL Trophy.

And Taylor demonstrated he was up to the task, turning in an excellent performance as part of a back three - with manager Parkinson singling him out as arguably the Black Cats' man of the match, despite seeing his side lose 1-0 in extra-time.

Parkinson says youngsters such as Taylor, and 21-year-old central defender Jack Bainbridge who played half-an-hour as a substitute against the Gills, are at the stage where they should be ready to break out of the U23 squad if they are to make the grade in senior football.

"I was very pleased with Brandon. I thought he was probably our best player on the night," said Parkinson, whose Sunderland side takes on League One promotion rivals Coventry City at the Stadium of Light this afternoon.

"He loved every minute of it, but it was a tough game to play in because Gillingham play a certain way where they get the ball forward very early, long throws coming into the box, a lot of set-plays, and that's a totally different type of game to the ones he has experienced at U23 level.

"But I don't think he could do a lot more to show that he can play.

"When you look at these lads like Brandon or Jack [Bainbridge], they are not 17 or 18 they are 20, 21, or 22 - they are at a point where they are either ready, and they are going to be good enough, or they are not.

"Brandon and Jack are in that category and all they can do is take their chance when they get it, and they did that on Tuesday.

"And Brandon certainly did that over the full 120 minutes - he played like a man."

With Conor McLaughlin and Tom Flanagan returning from international duty, Jordan Willis and Joel Lynch fit again after missing the Gillingham game due to injury, and Alim Ozturk - who played on Tuesday night despite being nowhere near match-fit - edging his way back, there is plenty of competition in central defence.

But Taylor trained with the first-team squad yesterday and is now seen as a player who can be called upon when needed.

Parkinson said: "He trained with us this morning. With the lads coming back from international duty we have more options, and I have to give Alim credit as well because we asked him to play the other night when he was way off match fitness and yet he did very well."
