Party and family split, NCP’s Supriya Sule announces on WhatsApp

Hours after NCP leader Ajit Pawar took oath as the Maharashtra deputy chief minister, party chief Sharad Pawar's daughter Supriya Sula expressed displeasure over WhatsApp.  

23 Nov 2019, 06:25

HIGHLIGHTS

  • Party and family split: NCP leader Supriya Sule said on WhatsApp
  • Supriya Sule's cousin Ajit Pawar sworn in as deputy CM of Maharashtra on Saturday
  • BJP leader Devendra Fadnavis on Saturday took oath as Maharashtra CM

Hours after Nationalist Congress Party (NCP) leader Ajit Pawar took oath as the Maharashtra deputy chief minister, party chief Sharad Pawar's daughter Supriya Sula expressed displeasure over WhatsApp.

In her message, NCP leader Supriya Sula said, "Party and family split,"

On Saturday morning, Supriya Sule's cousin Ajit Pawar sworn in as deputy chief minister of Maharashtra while BJP's Devendra Fadnavis became the chief minister of the state for the second time.

The unexpected development comes a day after Shiv Sena chief Uddhav Thackeray emerged as the Sena-NCP-Congress' consensus candidate for the chief minister's post.

NCP chief Sharad Pawar, however, said it was his nephew Ajit Pawar's own decision, and not that of the party, to support the BJP to form the government.

Shiv Sena leader Sanjay Raut accused Ajit Pawar of stabbing the Sena in the back by deciding to join hands with the BJP. He said Sharad Pawar and Uddhav Thackeray would address a joint press conference in Mumbai soon.

The protracted political impasse ended dramatically with Devendra Fadnavis returning as the chief minister after the President's rule, imposed in the state on November 12, was revoked early on Saturday.

The development raised eyebrows not only in the political circles, but also among the people of the state as the Shiv Sena, NCP and the Congress had been holding talks over forming the government in the state.

It was only on Friday evening that Sharad Pawar had said the new government would be led by Uddhav Thackeray, which signalled the end of speculation on who would occupy the chief minister's post.

The three parties had even prepared a draft common minimum programme (CMP) to guide the actions of the new government.

However, after the oath-taking ceremony was held early on Saturday, Sharad Pawar tweeted: "Ajit Pawar's decision to support the BJP to form the Maharashtra Government is his personal decision and not that of the NCP. We place on record that we do not support or endorse this decision of his."

Talking to reporters, Raut said: "Ajit Pawar has stabbed the Shiv Sena in the back. Joining hands with the BJP to form the government is a betrayal."

The BJP and the Sena, which fought the assembly polls in an alliance, had secured a comfortable majority by winning 105 and 56 seats respectively in the 288-member House. However, the Sena broke its three-decade-long ties with the BJP after the latter declined to share the chief minister's post.

The Congress and the NCP, pre-poll allies, won 44 and 54 seats respectively.
