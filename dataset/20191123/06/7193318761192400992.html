Oklahoma Scene: Energy FC hires head coach; OCU runners win national title

23 Nov 2019, 06:05

Energy FC on Friday announced the hiring of Minnesota United FC assistant coach John Pascarella as the team's new head coach.

“We couldn’t be more excited for John to join our club,” said owner Bob Funk, Jr. “John not only has an impressive resume, but everyone he’s worked with speaks very highly about him. It became clear early in our discussions that John has a vision for Energy FC both on and off the pitch, and we look forward to building a winning culture with him.”

Pascarella replaces Steve Cooke, who was fired after two seasons with Energy FC. Pascarella is the third coach in the club’s seven-year history.

Pascarella has been an assistant coach at Minnesota United FC of Major League Soccer since January 2018. Prior to his time in Minnesota, Pascarella managed the Des Moines Menace of USL League Two during the 2017 season and was an assistant and goalkeeper coach at Sporting Kansas City from 2009 to 2016, winning the MLS Cup in 2013 and the US Open Cup in 2012 and 2015.

He played professional soccer for nine seasons in the United States and Peru before beginning his coaching career at the University of Maryland in 1998. He also spent time with the Northern Virginia Royals of USL League Two and Virginia’s U.S. Olympic Development Program.

OCU men win cross country national crown

Top-ranked Oklahoma City University captured its fifth NAIA men's cross country championship Friday at Fort Vancouver National Historic Site in Vancouver, Washington.

OCU earned in its 69th national championship in all sports. The Stars won a national championship for the 27th year in a row to continue a streak that dates to 1993-94.

Mark Shaw, a senior from England, nabbed his second individual national title for the Stars. Oscar Kipkoros. a senior from Kenya took fifth, and senior Max McNeill of Scotland finished 13th to seize all-American honors for the third year in a row.

In the women's race, OCU freshman Sarah Olamide from Nigeria placed ninth and to become an All-American. As a team, the Stars finished 16th at the meet.

Santa Clara boots OSU from soccer tournament

Santa Clara has ended No. 3 seeded Oklahoma State's run in the NCAA women's soccer tournament with a stunning 3-1 loss Friday afternoon at McAlister Field in Los Angeles.

The Broncos (15-5-2) scored twice in 48-second span in the first half and never trailed the Cowgirls (16-3-3).

Kelsey Turnbow started Santa Clara's scoring at the 19:32 mark off an assist by Kelcie Hedge. Julie Doyle made it 2-0 Broncos less than a minute later.

Izzy D'Aquila put Santa Clara up 3-0 on a goal assisted by Alex Loera and Hedge.

Gabriella Coleman gave OSU its lone goal at 88:11 on an assist by Charme Morgan.

OCU benefactor Jim Wade dies at age 94

Oklahoma City University benefactor and former athlete Gen. James L. Wade died Wednesday. He was 94.

Wade competed for OCU in baseball, basketball and football before attaining the rank of brigadier general in the U.S. Air Force. Memorial services for Wade will be held 10 a.m. Tuesday at Southpark Baptist Church, 2900 SW 119th Street in Oklahoma City.

The Wade family requests those desiring to commemorate Wade’s memory make a gift in his honor to the Jim Wade Endowment for Athletics. To make a gift, go to okcu.edu/championsclub or call Chris Maxon, OCU associate athletic director for development, at (405) 208-5431.

The Stars hold the annual Jim Wade Awards to celebrate OCU’s athletic accomplishments. Wade entered the OCU Athletics Hall of Fame as part of the 1979 induction class.

His financial contributions helped complete additions and renovations to OCU’s baseball facility, Jim Wade Stadium, as well as at Abe Lemons Arena inside the Freede Center and Brian Harvey Field, the Stars’ soccer facility.

He established the Jim Wade Endowment and an endowed scholarship fund to provide financial support for the Stars, winners of the past three NAIA Directors’ Cup titles.The Talihina native entered the OCU Athletics Hall of Fame in 1979. He was decorated for service in World War II, the Korean War and the Vietnam War. He flew 55 missions in Korea. He last piloted a Boeing 727 at age 78.
