Jose Mourinho, John McGinn, Rangers and 27 things you can do for the price of a Scotland ticket

Kris Boyd Special? Hampden binoculars? OldFirmFacts helps you spend £27

23 Nov 2019, 06:00 by Old Firm Facts

At 7.45pm On Tuesday night, the temperature in Glasgow was 3°. It was raining. You’d probably had a tough day at work, seeing as Tuesday is the only day of the week worse than Monday.

Those conditions scream ‘straight home, heating up, staunch union jack jammies on’ to most people, but there is a subset of society with a more perverse outlook. Thousands of them could be found in Glasgow’s south side, having paid £27 for the privilege of watching the sides ranked 53rd and 121st in the world contest a dead rubber.

A total of 19,500 people chose to spend their Tuesday night this way. As unpalatable a method of spending an evening as it might seem to many, there’s no doubt that considerably more tickets would have been sold if the price was more reasonable.

I mean, you COULD have spent £27 for the right to have a running track between you and Scott McKenna backing off Baktiyar Zaynutdinov. Or, you could have spent it on any of these 27 things that cost the same or less than a Scotland v Kazakhstan ticket.

1. Borat on DVD, so you can watch something involving Kazakhstan that’s entertaining from the start rather than just the last 45 minutes. Treat yourself to a green mankini for the full experience. That still leaves £19.58 for a takeaway.

2. One ticket for The Shining sequel Doctor Sleep at Cineworld, if you’re looking for a less harrowing experience than watching the Scottish national team. Throw in a large popcorn, large soft drink, nachos and an alcoholic beverage.

3. Tickets for some of the Scotland Women’s Euro 2021 qualifiers. They’re the ones who qualify for tournaments.

4. Launch a consortium to take over Adidas, after which you can burn down the warehouse which stores the new international kit.

5. Rangers, 27 times (Look, if I didn’t say it here there would be 20 guys with ‘67’ at the end of their Twitter handles saying it in the replies).

6. A nice bouquet of flowers for Ethel Smith to say thanks for letting her husband Walter take the Scotland job again later this year.

7. A France ‘98 sticker album plus a set of 13 Scotland stickers. It’s a handy reminder of the last time the men’s team qualified for a major tournament, and also brings into sharp focus the fact that Christian Dailly has played in more major international tournaments than Champions League-winner Andy Robertson. Throw in Archie McPherson’s book ‘Adventures in the Golden Age: Scotland in the World Cup Finals 1974-1998’ and then use the remaining £4 to buy a meal deal you can enjoy while reading it on your lunch break.

8. Therapy to teach you coping strategies for your Euro 2020 qualifying campaign PTSD.

9. Save the £27 and use it to buy a half-pint at next year’s TRNSMT festival.

10. Train to Edinburgh and an underground ghost tour.

11. A ticket for the Scotland game which you then give to Katie Hopkins, who must attend and stay for the full 90 minutes otherwise an alternative punishment will be found.

12. Get a taxi to Hampden, mount an SFA coup and instigate a reasonable pricing structure for the Nations League playoff

13. Failing that, at least pay for two SFA members to visit Edinburgh and find out what Easter Road and Tynecastle look like.

14. A round of golf for Scott McKenna at Trump International Scotland on March 26th. No, hang on, he’s already an ambassador. An ambassador for Trump.

15. 0.00027% of Scott McKenna’s £10m transfer fee.

16. Supplies for the underground bunker you’ve set up in preparation for Jo Swinson’s nuclear strike.

17. Binoculars, so if you are going to attend a game at Hampden you can at least see the action.

18. Pay the Conservative Party fact check account to debunk the idea that £27 is an acceptable price to pay for Scotland v Kazakhstan.

19. Hire Prince Andrew’s PR team. There’s no way they can charge more than £27 with a straight face.

20. Stick £27 on the date of Jose Mourinho’s first passive-aggressive comment about Spurs chairman Daniel Levy. I’ve got March 3rd 2020.

21. Enough plasters and ibuprofen to keep Stevie Naismith safe between now and the playoffs.

22. Pay for a carryout on your way to a rugby match. On the plus side you won’t get it confiscated what with it not being a football match, but on the down side you’re going to a rugby match.

23. Keep warm at the football with one of the discounted items from Derek McInnes’ Autumn/Winter 2019 Gilet Collection.

24. Taxis for the Scotrail employees who this week tweeted “I’m not going to discuss ‘what ifs’ with you, Josh” and “Is it a major issue buying from the ticket machine or booking offices before getting on the train, Fraser?” in response to perfectly reasonable questions from commuters.

25. Or give them both a £13.50 bonus, depending on your perspective.

26. 0.225% of the ‘Kris Boyd Special’ at a hair transplant clinic

27. Save the £27 until next summer and buy a congratulatory round for John McGinn after his hat-trick in the Euro 2020 final.
