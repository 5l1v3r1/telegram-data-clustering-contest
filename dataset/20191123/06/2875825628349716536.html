Lyon clash will be a test of Leinster's manhood, says Leo Cullen

With that in mind, Cullen has turned to the experience of Rob Kearney, Tadhg Furlong, Scott Fardy and Rob Henshaw

23 Nov 2019, 06:00 by Michael Scully

Leo Cullen claims Leinster's Lyon visit will be a test of his team's manhood.

With that in mind, Cullen has turned to the experience of Rob Kearney, Tadhg Furlong, Scott Fardy and Rob Henshaw, who all return to the starting line-up for today's encounter at the Matmut Stadium de Gerland.

"We love the home games, but when you go to France it's a bit more of a test of our manhood, so to speak," stated the Leinster head coach.

"Can we stay controlled in the environment with all the chaotic bits going around; trumpets and bands and crowd noise and cheering?

Opposition flying into you, maximum pressure all the time. Them playing on the edge. Can we think clearly with all of those going on in the background?".

Having said that, Lyon have lost their last four home games in Europe - having won nine of their previous 11.

Leinster, by contrast, have only lost once in their last eight games on French soil.

But Cullen is wary of the fact that Lyon, who are the Top 14 pace-setters, lost their pool opener in Northampton last week. This is a must-win for the French club.

"It can be a turnaround game - and that's when French teams are very dangerous," he surmised.

"Last weekend, they rested some of their key guys. They have such a deep squad anyway.

"They've signed guys like (Xavier) Chiocci, crafty operators from the Top 14 who have been around the block and know how to get the job done."

"Their tournament lies on the line and that's how dangerous they can be."

That one loss in eight on french soil happened on their last visit a year ago, so there's been no problem with the Blues' mindset this week after their opening bonus point home win over Benetton.

Leinster went to Toulouse as reigning champions but there were celebratory scenes at the end between the team and their supporters after winning by a single point.

"We'd seen a lot of footage of Toulouse and seen some of the threats they have and said there was actually lots of opportunities," recalled Cullen.

"Then, you actually get there and there's the cauldron atmosphere and it's a very different team to what you analysed on the footage.

"Then, when Toulouse win at the end, you see Ugo Mola's reaction and the players scoring their tries, how they celebrate. The crowd, the noise, everything.

"This is a big deal, it is a big deal to them, it looks like, to play against us which is in one way a compliment because you can see how much it actually does mean to them, that they're not making it up.

"That's what we've tried to focus on this week, understanding what it means to these guys when they win against Leinster.

"It's a positive sign that they're treating the game like that to that extent.

"For some of our guys it is going to be a great step, a great game. This is why you get involved in the game".
