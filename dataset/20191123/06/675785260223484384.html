UK weather forecast: Britain faces 17 hours of heavy rain and 92 flood warnings

The Met Office has issued four yellow warnings for rain, including areas in already flood-hit South Yorkshire. Residents are warned of travel disruptions for the weekend

23 Nov 2019, 06:13 by Tiffany Lo

Flood-hit areas will face a 'disruptive' wet weekend with nearly two weeks' worth of rainfall in just a few hours.

Forecaster has issued a total of four yellow weather warnings for rain that cover the southwest, east midlands and the Scottish highlands.

Two of them will last from 4pm on Saturday until 9am on Sunday - that's 17 hours of downpours.

The Environment Agency has 92 flood warnings and alerts in place in parts of the Midlands and northern England.

Saturday will start off dry before persistent and heavy rain hit in the afternoon.

Met Office meteorologist Helen Willetts said: "It will be really very wet for the Balearic Islands as we go through Saturday.

"It will push into the United Kingdom and into France as well.

"Large waves on the coast and more flooding rains are expected. The ground is already so saturated."

Britons living in affected areas are expected to see cuts to power supplies and delays to public transport.

The Environment Agency said that river flooding was possible in southwest England and Wales this weekend, and across parts of southern England on Monday.

Weekend holidaymakers are warned of travel disruptions and advised to check for updates.

Fog patches are also likely in parts of central, southern and eastern England.

The weekend forecast will heap trouble to those in South Yorkshire, one of the worst-hit areas that sees 655 homes flooded and more than 1,300 people evacuated.

Five-day weather forecast:

Today:

Most parts cloudy with rain at times, heaviest in the southwest this morning then in the northeast later. Temperatures close to the seasonal average but feeling chilly in the rain, and with a brisk breeze in the northeast.

Tonight:

Heavy rain becoming confined to northeast Scotland. Becoming drier elsewhere but with mist, low cloud and a few fog patches.

Sunday:

Heavy rain clearing northeast Scotland. Elsewhere, a murky start with some drizzle and a few fog patches. Brighter skies developing in places during the afternoon. Milder in the south.

Outlook for Monday to Wednesday:

Further spells of rain or showers for most, heavy in places, and some brief drier interludes. Often windy, especially around coasts. Milder than of late although patchy overnight frost possible.
