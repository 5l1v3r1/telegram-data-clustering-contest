Arsenal: 3 positives it’s important not to lose track of

Arsenal may be burning to pieces, but that doesn’t mean that every last thing has fallen to bits. Don’t lose track of the small niblets of joy here.

23 Nov 2019, 23:59 by Josh Sippie

Arsenal could not take home three points against the worst defense in the Premier League. Instead of dominating Southampton in front of a home crowd, they left the field to a cascade of boos following a 2-2 draw that was only scraped together at the last moment by Alexandre Lacazette.

It’s bad. There’s no hiding from that. And it’s very easy to take this recent string of results and live in the negatives. It’s probably even natural. And while I will always clash with Arsenal fans not to be so overly sensational, it’s hard to avoid the truth here—things are bad.

But the key here is that some things are bad. Most things, I guess you could say. But that doesn’t mean all things. And as is my calling here, I want to take a moment to get through to the crux of the positives here at the club, even though there aren’t that many.

If nothing else, consider it an exercise in keeping hold of your own blood pressure because, spoiler alert, this club is not good for it.

So come with me on this journey to positivity. I’ve got three for you right now.

3. Functional fullbacks

I have no clue what Unai Emery is doing with this defense. I don’t think he has any clue either. He doesn’t seem to understand how to put together what I find to be obvious pieces. But all that said, the fullback situation is incredibly positive, despite what we saw against Southampton.

Kieran Tierney gave up a penalty and Hector Bellerin looked sluggish at best, but the key takeaway that I found here was that we know Bellerin is better than he played here and Tierney was almost entirely positive and the penalty given was rather soft.

These are two capable, they are deadly, and when they fully hit their stride, they will turn the tide in matches. It’s all just a matter of time.

Onwards to No. 2.
