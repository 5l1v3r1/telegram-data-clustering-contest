Ole Gunnar Solskjaer rates Manchester United's chances of January signings

23 Nov 2019, 23:08

Manchester United boss Ole Gunnar Solskjaer has suggested he could make loan signings in the January transfer window due to the unavailability of his long-term targets.

The Norwegian has been given around £100m to spend in January following the club’s inability to replace Romelu Lukaku last summer after his £74m move to Inter Milan.

United agreed a deal to sign Juventus star Paulo Dybala but refused to meet the Argentine’s wage demands, while a move for team-mate Mario Mandzukic failed to get over the line.

The Red Devils have a long-term interest in Dortmund star Jadon Sancho and Red Bull Salzburg ace Erling Haaland but neither player is likely to be available in January.

That could revive the Mandzukic option and Solskjaer says it’s more likely he’ll seek loan deals in the winter market.

‘It is not about ‘X’ amount of money we have to spend,’ said Solskjaer.

‘It is about who do we think will be good for the club in the long-term, not just three or four months.

‘The targets we have are probably not available in January.

‘Not many clubs want to sell players that they would otherwise want to keep in January. Maybe one or two could be a loan deal but that is not a big money thing, it is just to help the team.’

United take on Sheffield United at Bramall Lane on Sunday but will be without a number of key players.

Scott McTominay will miss the fixture as he’s still a fortnight away from recovering from an ankle injury.

Paul Pogba and Marcos Rojo are also set to miss out, while there are doubts over Axel Tuanzebe.
