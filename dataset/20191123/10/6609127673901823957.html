ABANDONED CAT

Cat found dumped in Ayr street next to dog poo with netting wrapped around its neck

23 Nov 2019, 10:32 by Justin Bowie

A CAT was found dumped next to dog poo with netting wrapped around its neck in Ayr.

The poor feline was abandoned in a carrier by a member of the public in Maybole Road in the town, near Halfords, on November 19.

After finding her the kind-hearted local gave the cat some chicken while waiting for the Scottish SPCA to arrive.

The moggy, who is now affectionately known as Bessie, was left with “scabby skin” after being dumped.

But rescuers say she was found “just in the time” as the netting she was entangled in had not yet broken her skin.

The Scottish SPCA launched a probe after the horrific discovery as they appeal to the public for information.

Animal rescue officer Sheena McTaggart said: “The female cat was found in a carrier with no door.

“She was in a sorry state, with scabby skin and a large piece of netting wrapped around her neck.

“Luckily, the netting had only rubbed away her fur and not broken the skin so we found her just in time.

“We are grateful to the member of the public who found her and fed her some chicken while he waited for us to arrive.

“One of our animal rescue and rehoming centres is providing the care she needs.

“The team have affectionately named her Bessie.

“We’re keen to find out more about the circumstances that led to her becoming entangled in the net and subsequently ending up abandoned.

“If anyone has any information, we would urge them to call our confidential animal helpline on 03000 999 999.”

We told on Wednesday how a tiny kitten was left blind after being neglected by its owner and sold online at just four-weeks-old.

The cat, named Cookie, was sold to a buyer who was unaware of the animal's critical eye condition.
