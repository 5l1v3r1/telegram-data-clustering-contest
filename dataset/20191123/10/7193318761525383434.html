Oklahoman business cover briefs for Nov. 23, 2019

23 Nov 2019, 10:00

OK rig count holds

Oklahoma’s rig count remained unchanged this week as the nationwide number of working rigs declined, Baker Hughes reported Friday.

This week's national working rig count is 803, off three for the week and off 276 from the 1,079 that were drilling a year ago.

In Oklahoma, the count remained at 52, compared to 144 a year ago. The number of rigs working in the Cana Woodford basin declined by one week-over-week, to 27.

The most active basins this week, Baker Hughes said, are the Permian in west Texas and southeast New Mexico at 405, the south Texas Eagle Ford at 60, the Haynesville at 53, the Williston at 51 and the Marcellus at 36.

Continental appoints new board member

Continental Resources appointed a long-time oil and gas executive to its board, the company announced this week. Tim Taylor, president of Phillips 66 from 2014 until he retired in 2017, began as a Continental director on Nov. 20.

Taylor also worked for Phillips 66 as its executive vice president of commercial, marketing, transportation and business development from 2012 until he was promoted to president.

He has more than 35 years of experience in the oil and gas industry, serving in various executive management positions in midstream and downstream businesses.

He holds a Bachelor of Science in Chemical Engineering from Kansas State University.

Harold Hamm, Continental Resources’ chairman and CEO, said Taylor’s extensive experience provides the company “a tremendous asset.”
