House pressing higher vape taxes

23 Nov 2019, 10:53 by Jester P. Manalastas

WITH or without the ban, the House of Representatives is pushing for higher taxes on vape products.

Albay Rep. Joey Salceda, chairman of the House Committee on Ways and Means, said that the House will retain the taxes on vape and might even go for higher rates.

“We will retain it pero given the logic of the initial reported ban -- precautionary principle -- we might  go for a higher rate than P25/ml to P45/ml,” Salceda said.

According to the House leader, the 1 million vape users are almost totally upper middle to high income class versus the 23 million smokers with 7 million in the lowest 50 percent.  

The entry cost to vaping is relatively high at P1,600.

President Rodrigo Duterte has ordered a ban on vaping in public and even ordered the arrest of violators.

Salceda said they will repeal Section 2 of Republic Act 11346, passed by the previous (17th) Congress, even if it means revenue loss of P1.4 billion for the government.

In August, the House of Representatives approved on third and final reading HB 1026 raising anew the excise taxes on alcohol products, heated tobacco and vapor products.
