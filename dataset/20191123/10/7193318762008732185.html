23 Nov 2019, 10:00

EDMOND

Renovations planned for Edmond Library building

The Edmond Library building, 10 S Boulevard, will be closed from Dec. 9 through March 31 for $2.126 million in renovations, with limited-service library space open at 1030 S Bryant Ave. in the meantime.

The city-owned library building houses the Edmond Library, which is managed by the Metropolitan Library System. It was built in 1973 and last renovated in 2016, said city spokesman Casey Moore.

Renovations will include significant updates to the HVAC system, roof, ceiling, lighting and flooring, Moore said. The contractor is A.C. Owen Construction.

The temporary library space, “Edmond Express Library at Pelican Bay,” will open Dec. 16 for patrons to pick up holds and return items. Patrons can also visit any of the other Metropolitan Library System facilities in the Oklahoma City metro area.

OKLAHOMA CITY

Manuel Perez Park to be upgraded

Mayor David Holt and other civic leaders will break ground on new improvements at Manuel Perez Park, 21 SE 17, in a ceremony at 10 a.m. Dec. 2 presented by OKC Parks.

Improvements will include a new memorial plaza dedicated to Perez and other Oklahomans who earned the Medal of Honor.

A large new pavilion will be constructed with Hispanic-inspired architecture next to a memorial plaza. A new playground and new covered benches along the park trail, two new futsal courts, a new basketball court, enhancements to the skate park, and a new parking lot will be installed.

Manuel Perez Jr., 1923-1945, was awarded the Medal of Honor for actions as a U.S. Army private first class on Feb. 28, 1945, during World War II as a member of the 511th Parachute Infantry Regiment of the 11th Airborne Division. He led attacks that destroyed 11 Japanese pillboxes in fighting in the Philippines. He was killed in action a few weeks later.

Mesta Park Holiday Tour set

The Mesta Park Holiday Home Tour will be from 4 to 8 p.m. Dec. 7 and 1 to 5 p.m. Dec. 8, featuring six homes, a festive refreshment porch, holiday music by neighborhood students and a visit from Santa Claus.

Advance tickets are $12 for adults and come with a free $5 gift card to Parlor OKC. Tickets are available at www.MestaPark.org, Event Brite, and the Mesta Park Facebook page. Tickets also will be available at the door of each home, $15 for adults and $5 for children. Children 3 and under admitted free. Ticket purchases include coffee, hot cider and cookies.

Stops are at the homes of Jimmie and Susan Ownbey, 628 NW 21; Mat and McKenzie Clouse, 900 NW 20; Pete and Whitney Marrs, 1008 NW 18; Lauren Warkentine, 901 NW 16; Tommy and Molly Cooke, 516 NW 16; Floyd Simon, 907 NW 18; and with the refreshment porch, Andy and Jennifer Bowman, 704 NW 18.
