23 Nov 2019, 10:00

OKLAHOMA CITY

Holiday homes sought for shelter dogs

Oklahoma City Animal Welfare is seeking families who will open their homes to a shelter dog during the Thanksgiving holiday.

Participants can visit the shelter Monday through Wednesday to choose their dog and then return them starting Nov 30.

Those wanting to share their homes with a shelter dog must be at least 18 years old and need to be sure a pet is allowed at their residence.

All adoptable dogs qualify for the "Home for the Holidays" program. The shelter is open from noon to 5:30 p.m. daily at 2811 SE 29. All dogs have been spayed or neutered and are microchipped and up to date on shots and preventives.

MACU wins federal grant

Mid-America Christian University has been awarded a federal grant to benefit science, technology, engineering and math (STEM) education in three rural Oklahoma school districts.

The grant is part of the U.S. Department of Agriculture's Access to Online Math and Science (ATOMS) rural development project.

The funding will allow the university to provide STEM distance education to three districts: Alex Public Schools, Peggs Public Schools and Stillwell Public Schools.

Using video conferencing technology, ATOMS will deliver content for teachers and students through live interactions. The project offers funding for devices such as iPads, smart boards and other equipment with real-time connectivity to the schools.
