'I was called a monster after my nose fell into my face - but I still found love'

A series of tragic illnesses ravaged the 30-year-old's face, forcing her to seek treatment abroad after facing cruel taunts that she was a 'monster'

23 Nov 2019, 10:44 by Chris Jaffray, Ben Weich

A mother-of-two met and married the love of her life after a rare disease tragically ravaged her face, causing her nose to fall into her face..

Ana Roman, 30, was diagnosed with cancer in November 2017 after she collapsed, with medics later discovering a mass in the base of her skull.

The cancer weakened her immune system and she then developed microscopic polyangitis - a rare condition which causes blood vessel inflammation and left her nose sunken.

The mum said was forced to face cruel taunts that she was a 'monster'.

Ana, the manager of a car auction, underwent a complex, six-hour long operation to remove the back and lower parts of her skull, leaving her with no palate.

It also caused her nose to recede into her face.

Despite this, Ana met her current partner, 30-year-old Florin Petrescu, in the midst of all her health issues in January this year.

The couple found each other through a mutual friend, before tying the knot last month.

Florin proposed to Ana just 10 months after they met.

She has now discovered she is pregnant with the couple's first child - after fearing the gruelling round of chemotherapy would leave her unable to bear any more children.

Ana said: "After the first operation which left me with no palate I felt that a lot of people were looking at my face, some people even wrote on Facebook that I look like a monster.

"It was so hard to take my kids to school. But now after what it did to my face I can't believe I found love, I feel like the happiest woman in the world.

"I am really lucky to have Florin, he supports me so much. Just the week after we met I was back in hospital.

"I wouldn't be here now if it wasn't for him. At one point I wanted to kill myself because of the pain."

Ana was first diagnosed with a cancer of the palate in November 2017 after collapsing while visiting her brother in Germany.

After undergoing three months of draining chemotherapy, which compromised her immune system, she was told in May 2018 that she also the rare condition known as microscopic polyangitis.

Ana then battled through more chemotherapy, spending time in hospital in Romania and Belgium, before returning to the UK in January.

Amid her personal turmoil, she found happiness last month when she married Florin in a bar in Manchester, in front of 50 friends and family.

Florin, a self-employed valeter, added: "When I met Ana and discovered what was happening to her, I just wanted to be supportive.

"If you love someone you go through good and bad times. I just want her to be healthy again."

After being taken back Manchester Royal Infirmary in August this year, when the pain in her neck got worse, Ana was rushed to A&E for an operation on her neck to clear up the scar tissue from a previous operation.

She claims that nerve damage she allegedly suffered during surgery has left her unable to walk, and causes pain in her hand, which has left her unable to grasp items.

She has now left the UK to be treated at a hospital in Romania - spending more than £1,500.

A new procedure, which will involve operations on her neck and face, will leave her temporarily unable to walk.

The family have been forced to leave the UK, despite Ana's ten-year-old son, David living in the country for most of his life.

His three-year-old brother, Selim was born in Britain.

Ana said: "I had pain in my neck before, but none of the other new symptoms. The constant pain is terrible. I have never been offered compensation even though the doctors messed up.

"Last week I went back to hospital but was discharged, it was then I decided to leave the country. It is terrible, I don't want to bring my kids up here.

"I have played by the rules but I don't want to live here anymore."

A spokesperson for Manchester Royal Infirmary, which is managed by Manchester University NHS Foundation Trust, said: "We are not aware of the allegations made by this patient.

"We encourage the patient to get in touch with our dedicated Patient Advice and Liaison Team who can provide support and we can discuss any concerns they have.

"We are unable to comment further due to patient confidentiality."
