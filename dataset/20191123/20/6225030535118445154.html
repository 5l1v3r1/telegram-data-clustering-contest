Asmussen-trained 2-year-old Halo Again takes Coronation Futurity

23 Nov 2019, 20:40 by Brittney Mayotte/Woodbine

Undefeated two-year-old colt Halo Again captured the 116th running of the $225,000 Coronation Futurity for Canadian-foaled two-year-olds Saturday at Woodbine Racetrack.

The Coronation Futurity, contested over 1 1/8 miles on the Tapeta, is considered a stepping-stone to Canada’s most famous race, the Queen’s Plate. The first jewel of the Canadian Triple Crown of Thoroughbred Racing is set for June 27, 2020 at Woodbine.

Trained by Steve Asmussen, Halo Again arrived at Woodbine after making a victorious seven-furlong debut at Churchill Downs on Oct. 27.

With Rafael Hernandez aboard for the Coronation Futurity, Halo Again raced along the rail behind pacesetter Green Growth with Tomcat Black tracking most of the way through panels of :25.29, :50.15 and 1:14.76.

Despite losing position in the final turn, Halo Again re-rallied down the stretch and shot through late to score the victory. He prevailed in 1:51.82 by a head over Malibu Mambo, who closed from last on the far outside into the win photo.

Finishing in between the top pair, favorite Muskoka Gold closed within three-quarters of a length while pacesetter Green Growth was a close fourth. Tomcat Black finished fifth with Northern Thunder, Pleasecallmeback and Kunal completing the field.
