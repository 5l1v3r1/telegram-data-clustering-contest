DRIVER BLAST

Glasgow mum blasts driver who killed son in horror crash amid claims he compared 90mph police chase to Grand Theft Auto

23 Nov 2019, 20:51 by Kevin Duguid

A MUM whose son died in a horror road crash blasted the killer driver amid claims he compared his 90mph police chase to Grand Theft Auto.

Caroline Kelly, 45, said Ramsay Stevenson, 21, “bragged” the tree smash which left her lad Cameron Donaldson, 20, dead was like a real-life version of the popular computer game crime caper.

She told of her agony after the learner driver, who was high on a cocktail of drugs, spoke on social media about the tragedy in Baillieston, Glasgow.

Caroline, of nearby Easterhouse, said: “Stevenson got out of hospital and was on video chat boasting that it was like Grand Theft Auto getting chased all over the place.

"A boy has been killed. How can he do that?

"This is no game, we’re in a living hell. I can’t imagine the fear Cameron endured during that chase.”

Cameron was in the back seat of the Vauxhall Meriva when Stevenson lost control and hit a tree last October.

He suffered internal injuries and died eight days after the crash.

Front seat passenger Pauline McDowell, 27, was seriously hurt.

Stevenson — who had necked ecstasy, amphetamines and etizolam — fled the mangled motor and was found hiding from cops in a garden.

He admitted causing death by dangerous driving at the High Court in Glasgow last week. Sentence was deferred and he was remanded in custody.

Caroline said the loss of Cameron has devastated brothers Kieran and Calvin Donaldson, both 24, and Kayden Sutherland, six, who has never been told how his sibling died.

She added: “It has shattered me. Cameron didn’t know he was under the influence of drugs and they weren’t friends.

“Stevenson is a yob. He does not fear danger.

“You expect your children to get a job and settle down with a wee family. We won’t get the chance.”
