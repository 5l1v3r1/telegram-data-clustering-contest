North Shields salon owner 'lost for words' at 'gangster's wife's' reply to appointment change

Stacey Ward, from Sparkling Beautique, couldn't believe her eyes when she received a torrent of abuse

23 Nov 2019, 11:47 by Amber Hicks

When salon owner Stacey Ward messaged a client to reschedule her appointment, she was not prepared for the expletive-ridden respond that followed.

The North Shields beautician has shared the bizarre text exchange between her and the woman, who she has now banned.

The series of messages began when Stacey's colleague informed the client she would be unable to perform her lip treatment as she had the winter vomiting bug, norovirus.

She wrote: "Hi [name], it's Charnie from Sparkling Beautique, I have caught the Norovirus and Stacey's daughter has it too. Could I reschedule you this week if possible for your lips hun? X'.

Within seconds, the woman replied - and it wasn't pleasant, the Mirror reports.

She wrote: "Nar f*** off you dirty s***. Get to me f***ing door."

She continued: "Am going rip you f***ing face off for reschedule."

The woman added: "CHARNIE. S***."

Absolutely stunned, the beautician replied" "What?".

To which another barrage of abuse followed.

The woman said: "Reschedule my appointment come fight me. I'll kick you up you f**** lips."

Stacey then decided to get involved and sent her own message.

She wrote: "This is Stacey, the salon owner. My daughter isn't well so I have asked to reschedule your appointment and this is your reply?"

The woman quickly responded: "Listen live [sic]. Am a gangsters wife am not someone to be messed around. Me fellas a real villain."

Stacey posted the messages on Facebook where thousands of people have shared and commented on the post.

She wrote: "I mean some customers haven’t been happy with what’s going on today with the salon being closed but this is taking it to a whole new level.

"I’m actually lost for words, think it’s safe to say this ones now barred from rebooking."

One social media user replied: "She sounds like a real delight".

Another added: "Ma’am it’s 2019. Don’t be the gangsters wife, be the gangster."

A third commented: "Omg that lady is nuts. I wouldn’t even respond to the message."

Others couldn't believe it was genuine.

One remarked: "I would bet you have the wrong number on file. This sounds like a joke."

Another added: "It’s got to be a joke, you got the wrong number I bet, someone’s got the text and they’re responding to it as a joke. Can’t be real! The way she’s talking."
