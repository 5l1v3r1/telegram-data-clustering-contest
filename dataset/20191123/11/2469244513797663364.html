Met Office issues five days of weather warnings for Devon and Cornwall

Weather warnings will be in place every day from now until Wednesday

23 Nov 2019, 11:39 by Gayle McDonald

Met Office weather warnings will be in place across Devon and Cornwall from today until Wednesday evening.

Forecasters say there is more rain to come with a risk of flooding in some areas and disruption to travel.

A yellow warning for rain came into force at 12pm yesterday and is due to end at 6pm on Saturday.

Today the Exeter-based weather agency has issued two new warnings which cover the next four days.

The first comes into force at 4pm on Sunday and is scheduled to end at 10am on Monday.

The second starts at 12pm on Tuesday and covers up until 3pm on Wednesday.

What the warnings say

The Met Office says a combination of a spell of persistent heavy rain and heavy showers that follow will bring a risk of flooding.

What to expect 

  • Spray and flooding on roads probably making journey times longer

     

  • Bus and train services probably affected with journey times taking longer

     

  • Flooding of a few homes and businesses is likely

     

Met Office forecast for the south west in full

Today: Heavy rain set to continue throughout the morning. However, it will begin to ease later into the afternoon with a few showers remaining. There will also be a risk of hill fog during the day. Milder than of late. Maximum temperature 12 °C.

Tonight: A dry night with only a few showers possible. Hill and coastal fog will continue to linger throughout the night and into the early hours. Minimum temperature 3 °C.

Sunday: A dry day on Sunday, with a few coastal showers possible. However, a band of heavy rain will push in from the southwest later in the afternoon. Maximum temperature 12 °C.

Outlook for Monday to Wednesday: Showers, often heavy, across the region on Monday. Heavy rain expected on Tuesday which should begin to clear into Wednesday, leaving a drier day.
