Peter Crouch reacts to Tottenham's "strange" Jose Mourinho appointment

The former Tottenham forward gave his thoughts on the sacking of Mauricio Pochettino and the arrival of the former Chelsea and Manchester United boss

23 Nov 2019, 11:48 by Mark Jones, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/mark-jones/

Peter Crouch has admitted that he finds the appointment of Jose Mourinho at Tottenham as "a strange fit" but added that his former club made the decision out of a determination to get into the Champions League.

Tottenham sacked Mauricio Pochettino on Tueday after five-and-a-half years in charge, before acting swiftly to appoint former Chelsea and Manchester United boss Mourinho on a three-and-a-half year deal.

And Crouch - who started out at Spurs before returning for a second spell in 2009 - says that the situation his former club found themselves in this season made the appointment understandable.

"I don’t think at the start of the season Spurs would have hired him," Crouch told Metro .

"In a lot of ways… sometimes clubs are close to relegation and they get someone in that doesn’t play the way the club wants to but it’s about surviving.

"I think this is a similar situation but at the top end of the league.

"The situation is clear; the club need to get into the Champions League. Mourinho is not the usual fit for Tottenham . Levy wants young players who have a resale value, that can improve and be able to improve the club.

"Mourinho traditionally buys 30+ players who do the job now because he knows he’ll only be there one or two years. That’s against the philosophy of Tottenham so it’s a strange fit but it’s needs must."

Despite some reservations, Crouch is backing Mourinho to do a good job ahead of his first game in charge at West Ham on Saturday.

"I feel like they still have a top squad, they’ve got top players," he added.

"This is the same team that got to the Champions League final, they can beat anyone on their day."
