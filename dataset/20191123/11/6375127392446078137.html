Beermen, Aces fight for dear lives

23 Nov 2019, 11:28 by Arman Carandang

ONE of the toughest challenges for a team seeking a semifinal spot is if it is pitted against a powerhouse team.

That’s the situation now for Barangay Ginebra which will play sister team and grandslam-seeking San Miguel Beer at the start of the quarterfinal round in the 2019 PBA Governors’ Cup at the Smart Araneta Coliseum.

“We certainly would have chosen a different opponent for the quarterfinals if it was within our power, but SMB it is,” said Gin Kings coach Tim Cone.

“Control what you can control, and that is preparing ourselves the best we can,” added Cone.

The Gin Kings and the Beermen meet in the 6:45 p.m. main offering of an explosive Sunday double-header that will also see Meralco collide with Alaska in the curtain-raiser at 4:30 p.m.

“It is such always a great challenge going against them (Beermen). Both teams are extremely motivated. It should be a great fight,” stressed Cone.

The Gin Kings finished within magic four at 7-4, thus earning the luxury of winning only once – which they could do it today —  to make the semis right away.

With the Gin Kings in the magic four are NLEX (8-3), no. 2 Meralco (8-3) and third seed Talk ‘N  Text. The four have to win only once to advance to the next round.

The Bolts meanwhile are also expecting a tough fight against the Aces, who won all their last four outings in the elimination round to finish 7th at 5-6.

“With the elimination now over, our total concentration is on reaching the semis. Alaska has been playing good basketball so we are expecting a tough challenge,” said Meralco coach Norman Black.

Black is particularly wary of the Aces’ vaunted strong start which could have catapulted the team to the playoff round after a 0-5 start in the elims.

“We need to be ready for the fact that they have been thriving with early offense and a strong team defense,” said Black.  

“On our end, we just want to continue to execute and play the type of basketball that has gotten us to this point,” added Black.

The other half of the quarterfinals pitting top seed NLEX against NorthPort and Talk ‘N Text against Magnolia will be played on Monday also at the Big Dome.
