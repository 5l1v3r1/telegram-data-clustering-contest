British EuroMillions winner comes forward to claim huge £105million jackpot

The lucky EuroMillions ticket-holder is one of the biggest-ever British lottery winners after scooping a £105million jackpot in the draw last Tuesday night

23 Nov 2019, 11:09 by Chris Kitching, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/chris-kitching/

A single ticket-holder has come forward to claim the £105million EuroMillions jackpot prize won on Tuesday night.

The win makes the lucky Brit one of the biggest-ever lottery winners in the UK.

The winner's identity is still a mystery - it could stay that way if they choose to remain anonymous - and their prize has not yet been paid out as the ticket cannot be validated until Monday morning.

Six lucky Brits have won big in the EuroMilllions so far this year in what has been a highly successful year for UK punters.

Andy Carter, senior winners' adviser at the National Lottery, said: "This is the sixth massive EuroMillions jackpot win in 2019 and follows the nation's biggest ever win of £170million just last month.

"We now focus on supporting the ticket-holder as they start to enjoy their new-found wealth."

A EuroMillions spokesperson said: "The prize will be paid out at the ticket validation appointment.

"The ticket-holder will then decide whether or not to go public and share their news.

"There will be no information on whether it is an individual or syndicate winner, or where the ticket was purchased, unless the ticket-holder decides to go public."

Britain could have another big winner on Saturday night, with a £25million Lotto jackpot that must be won.

The EuroMillions spokesperson added: "This is the sixth EuroMillions jackpot win in the UK this year and further adds to the 5,500 millionaires made by the National Lottery since it launched."

Patrick and Frances Connolly, from Northern Ireland, won £114.9million on EuroMillions in the draw on New Year’s Day.

Ade Goodchild scooped £71million in March and an anonymous ticket-holder bagged £38.2million in April.

In June, a ticket-holder who chose to remain anonymous won £123million.

Last month the country’s biggest ever winner was made when an anonymous ticketholder scooped £170million.

The previous biggest winners in the UK were Colin and Chris Weir, from Largs, North Ayrshire, who won £161million in July 2011.
