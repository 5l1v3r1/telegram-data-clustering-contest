Blackcurrant condensed milk ice cream recipe 

23 Nov 2019, 11:00 by Xanthe Clay

I’m not sure who invented condensed milk ice cream – I first came across it on the side of a Carnation milk tin decades ago. It makes a brilliant no-churn ice cream: on the sweet side and so good with a tangy ripple.

If you can’t get frozen blackcurrants (whole food shops often have them in the freezer section) then half a jarful of jam, mixed with a spoonful or two of lemon juice will work just fine, as will any fruit compote.

Prep time: 10 minutes, plus freezing time | Cooking time: 5 minutes 

SERVES 

eight

INGREDIENTS 

  • 250g frozen blackcurrants
  • 4 tbsp sugar
  • 600ml double cream
  • 1 x 397g tin condensed milk

METHOD 

  1. Heat the blackcurrants gently in a pan with two tablespoons of water and the sugar until the sugar dissolves. Allow to bubble briefly then leave to cool.
  2. Put the cream and condensed milk in a large bowl and beat with an electric whisk until very thick. Scrape the mixture into a plastic freezerproof box.
  3. Drizzle the blackcurrant compote over the top, then give the ice cream a single s-shaped stir. Put the lid on the box and freeze for at least six hours.
