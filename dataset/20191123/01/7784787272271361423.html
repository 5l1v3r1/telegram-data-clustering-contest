Adelaide’s 2018 first-round picks Chayce Jones and Ned McHenry finish top-two in Crows’ 2km time-trial

23 Nov 2019, 01:43 by Josh Gabelich

This time last year, Chayce Jones and Ned McHenry were hours away from being selected by Adelaide in the first-round of the National Draft.

On Saturday morning, the teenage pair finished first and second in the Crows’ 2km time-trial to make an early statement this pre-season, under the watch of new senior coach Matthew Nicks.

Defender Jake Kelly finished behind Jones and McHenry, with ruckman Reilly O’Brien and half-back Brodie Smith rounding out the top five.

While McHenry is yet to make his debut, Jones received his first taste of league football in Round 1 and finished his maiden season with eight senior games under his belt.

Jones, who the Crows selected at Pick 9 last November before taking McHenry at Pick 16, relishes the competition with the small forward who could fill the void left by departed star Eddie Betts in the coming years.

“We’ve run a couple of times together, it’s probably about even now,” Jones told reporters in North Adelaide on Saturday.

“It’s good competition and it makes you better out of it all.”

After arriving from Tasmania weighing in at 77kg, Jones has added a few kilograms in his first 12 months at the club and will continue to focus on building his frame over the summer.

He finished the season with career-high 18 disposals, seven marks four tackles and two goals against the Western Bulldogs and is now eyeing more time in the middle in 2020.

“It’s nice to keep (my time) relatively similar to last year while putting on a few kilos,” he said.

“I definitely want to push myself to get in the (midfield) so it’s all about putting your foot forward in the pre-season.”
