ITV7 guide: Highlind fling | Ascot, Haydock, Gowran free-to-play jackpot £50,000

22 Nov 2019, 18:03 by David Smith

There's £50,000 on offer in this weekend's free-to-play ITV7 and Dave Smith has the guide you need for the crucial races.

13:50 Haydock - Betfair Exchange Handicap Hurdle (Class 2)

Despite falling last time out, WHOSHOTTHESHERIFF was travelling well for a long way. The handicapper has nudged him up 2lb for this better race but Sean Quinlan is a good booking. Kateson will be well found in the market having been tried in better company and Project Bluebrook will be worth keeping an eye on.

14:05 Ascot - Christy 1965 Chase (Grade 2) (Class 1)

In what is set to be a titanic clash, the two-highest rated chasers in training go head-to-head in Berkshire. ALTIOR has never gone over this far and there is a slight quirk with some of his jumping, if you want to be overly critical. Yet the speed in which he pulls away on the flat should see him re-calibrate the ratings with the classy Cyrname.

14:25 Haydock - Betfair Best Odds On ITV Races Stayers' Handicap Hurdle (Grade 3) (Class 1)

Harry Skelton has deserted Diomede Des Mottes after he seemed certain to win last time out but found next to nothing off-the bridle. The one of most interest is HIGHLAND HUNTER who now has Paul Nichols as his trainer. He'll be happy on the soft ground and it's interesting that Sam Twiston-Davies gets the ride.

14:45 Gowran Park - Irish Stallion Farms EBF Beginners Chase

LAURINA comes up against the boys having shown some admirable form in the Champion Hurdle and she will be a warm order to take this. Opposition looks thin on the ground and with the trainer having a 50% record in the last ten runnings of this race, the selection tightens up.

15:00 Haydock - Betfair Chase (Grade 1) (Registered As The Lancashire Chase) (Class 1)

Despite there being only four runners, a case can be made for all four. Bristol De Mai loves it around here and will surely head the betting, whilst Frodon is only rated a pound lower but significantly wider in the market. Ballyoptic won the Charlie Hall Chase well but LOSTINTRANSLATION looks a real Gold Cup horse and can well and truly frank the form of a red-hot JLT at the Festival by taking this with a stellar round of jumping.

15:20 Ascot - Gerard Bertrand Hurst Park Handicap Chase (Class 2)

Quite a competitive race in which Capeland will surely be competitive after his unfortunate disqualification last time out. He re-opposes Diego Du Charmil here but the selection is another they both meet again in the shape of CAID DU LIN. His record around Ascot is decent and with a slight lowering in the weights he can make the most of the difference.

15:40 Haydock - Better Value On The Betfair Exchange Stayers' Handicap Chase (Class 3)

Fairly uninspiring stuff to complete leg 7. With a run under his belt at a course not too dissimilar to this MOVING IN STYLE may just eke out enough improvement to take this. Know the Score has had two wind operations so that marks as a negative but Lake Romantic looks like a thorough stayer and could be a big player.
