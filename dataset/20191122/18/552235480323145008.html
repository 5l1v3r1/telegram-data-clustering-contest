Ruth Davidson reveals she will not stand as an MSP again in 2021

The 41-year-old stepped down as Scottish Tory leader in August and now Davidson says she will soon be an ex politician.

22 Nov 2019, 18:34 by Torcuil Crichton, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/torcuil-crichton/

Ruth Davidson has confirmed for the first time that she will not stand for the Scottish parliament again in 2021.

At an event on Friday the former Scottish Conservative leader described herself as “an ex-journalist and soon to be ex politician”.

Speaking at a charity lunch hosted by journalists, Davidson said: “I enjoyed being a journalist and I loved being leader of the Scottish Conservative Party so I would like to thank everyone in this room for the last 20 years, the first decade on one side of the wall, and the second on the other and I am terrified of having to try and find a real job in 18 months time.”

Previously Davidson said it was “unlikely” that she would continue at Holyrood, although this still leaves the door open for a return to frontline politics in the future as an MP at Westminster.

Last month Davidson faced mounting calls to quit as an MSP over her £50,000 a year second job with a PR firm which she later gave up.

It has since emerged that she has set up her own consultancy business and will be paid to deliver TV analysis on election night.

Davidson stepped down as leader of the Scottish Conservative Party in August after eight years in charge.

The mum of one was widely credited for turning round the fortunes of the Tories north of the border and she has been tipped as a future UK Tory party leader.

The MSP for  Edinburgh Central  stepped down as leader after disagreeing with Boris Johnson over his initial pursuit of a no-deal Brexit although she stated her decision to quit was due to personal reasons.

The 41-year-old had backed Remain in the 2016 EU referendum and clashed Johnson during a TV debate before the vote.

The rift between Davidson and Johnson deepened in July when  David Mundell was sacked  as Scottish Secretary by the new Prime Minister and replaced with Brexiteer Tory MP Alister Jack.
