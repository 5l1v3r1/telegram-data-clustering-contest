Cyclist rushed to hospital after hit-and-run in Falkirk as cops search for driver of silver Mercedes

22 Nov 2019, 18:16 by Paul Smith

A CYCLIST has been rushed to hospital after a hit-and-run in Falkirk this morning.

Police and ambulance crews raced to Carron Road in the town at around 11:14am after reports a person had been hit by a car.

Officers say the car failed to stop at the scene.

They're now on the hunt for a silver Mercedes that's believed to have been involved in the incident.

The condition of the man, believed to be in his 40s, is not known.

He was taken to Forth Valley Royal Hospital to be treated by medics.

Posting on Twitter, a Police Scotland spokesperson said: "We are appealing for information in relation to an incident that happened on Carron Road, Falkirk today at the bridge at the river Carron.

"A cyclist was struck by a silver Mercedes that failed to stop.

"If you have any info please call 101 and quote incident 1205 of 22/11/19".
