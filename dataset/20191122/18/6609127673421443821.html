Man charged with Glasgow ‘rape’ on Halloween after ‘west end sex attacks’

22 Nov 2019, 18:46 by Paul Smith

A MAN has appeared in court after a woman was allegedly raped in the west end of Glasgow on Halloween.

Parvinder Singh, who has also been charged with two alleged sexual assaults, made no plea at the city’s sheriff court yesterday.

It is alleged the 34-year-old raped a woman, 20, in Sutherland Lane, near the University of Glasgow’s library in the early hours of October 31.

He has also been charged with sexually assaulting another woman on October 17, and a third on October 24.

Detective Inspector Suzanne McNulty of the Public Protection Unit in Govan said: “Officers in Glasgow carried out enquiries following a reports three woman had been the victim of sexual assaults

in the West End between 17 and 31 October.

“A 34-year-old man has now been charged in connection with two sexual assaults on 17 and 24 October, and a serious sexual assault on 31 October.

“In every serious crime reported to police, officers carefully consider whether or not it is appropriate to proactively make the circumstances public.

“This is continually assessed throughout the investigation to ensure the safety of the wider public is protected.”

“We will continue to liaise with Glasgow University and the surrounding communities as part of our continued investigations into this.”

The case was committed for further examination and Singh was remanded in custody.

His court appearance comes after Glasgow students were warned to “stay safe” around campus following alleged sexual assaults.

University of Glasgow chiefs issued an "important security message" during the week to reassure students that security procedures were being put in place.

Increased guard patrols have now been brought in around the campus and improved lighting has been installed.

In a social media warning, uni bosses said: "Incidents like this are rare and student and staff safety is of the utmost importance to the University.

"We work extremely closely with Police Scotland and we have increased security patrols and improved lighting on campus.

"If you have any concerns or information to report, please contact our security team on 0141 330 4282 or Police Scotland 101."
