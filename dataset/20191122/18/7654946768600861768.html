Big political donor faces new charges in Manhattan federal court

22 Nov 2019, 18:56 by Emily Saul

Los Angeles venture capitalist Imaad Zuberi — a big donor to both Democrats and Republicans who’s been charged in California with campaign finance violations — is set to face new allegations in New York, according to court papers.

Federal prosecutors in Manhattan plan to charge Zuberi with obstruction of justice and failing to register as a foreign agent, according to a letter filed late Thursday by his own attorneys.

The news comes as Zuberi is scheduled to plead guilty Friday in California federal court to campaign finance violations and tax evasion relating to allegations he traded donations for political access.

The 49-year-old venture capitalist has for years given millions to both Democrats and Republicans — shelling out $900,000 for Trump’s inaugural and also donating to Hillary Clinton.  It was not said in the California case what candidates the illegal funds went too.

Lawyers for Zuberi asked a California judge Thursday to reschedule their client’s guilty plea to December, in order to “allow the U.S. Department of Justice (“DOJ”) to find a single voice – not New York and Los Angeles voices speaking over each other – so he can globally resolve all potential charges instead of being subject to serial prosecutions for the identical conduct by different offices of the DOJ.”

They also accused the DOJ of “last-minute goalpost-shifting” in Zuberi’s case.

The judge has not yet responded to the filing.

A spokesman with the Manhattan U.S. Attorney’s office declined to comment, and the DOJ did not immediately return a message.
