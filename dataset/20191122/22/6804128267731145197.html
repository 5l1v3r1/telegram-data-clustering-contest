Jack Wilshere a West Ham injury doubt to face Jose Mourinho's Spurs despite training return

The Hammers midfielder has been back in training but suffered a setback

22 Nov 2019, 22:30 by Sam Inkersole

West Ham boss Manuel Pellegrini revealed Jack Wilshere is an injury concern for West Ham and their hosting of Jose Mourinho's Spurs at London Stadium on Saturday.

Wilshere had returned to training having been out for a month with what was initially a "small" problem which turned out to actually be quite a significant one, missing the last three games.

The Hammers midfielder hasn't started in the Premier League since the second game of the season, a 1-1 draw with Brighton, and was hauled off at half time in that game as injuries yet again hamper the former Arsenal man.

Pellegrini was asked about the midfielder's fitness after some confusion initially in his first answer about the availability of Wilshere, Michail Antonio and Mark Noble - the latter two are fit for selection.

And West Ham's manager was less than convincing in his answer on Wilshere, saying: "Wilshere was working normally until yesterday (Wednesday) when he had a small problem.

"We will see tomorrow (Friday) if he is able to be on the squad list.

"It's a small pain that he has behind his stomach. It's a small thing but it bothers him a little bit so he must be 100 per cent [to play].

As mentioned, the Hammers have been handed a boost as Antonio and Noble have come through their respective injuries and could feature on Saturday against Spurs.

Antonio has been out since August with a torn hamstring that required surgery while Noble twisted his ankle in the dire 3-0 defeat to Burnley prior to the international break but has recovered.

On that duo, the manager added: "They are important players for us, Antonio had a long injury and makes things happen in attacking, Mark Noble is the captain of the team.

"They are able to play."
