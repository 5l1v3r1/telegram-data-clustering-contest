Emma Faulds ‘murder’: Suspect to appear in court two days after Ayrshire care worker’s 40th birthday

22 Nov 2019, 22:58 by Douglas Walker

A SUSPECT charged with killing care worker Emma Faulds will appear in the dock just two days after what would have been her 40th birthday.

Ross Willox will appear for a preliminary hearing at the High Court in Glasgow on December 4 accused of murder.

Willox, 40, of Monkton, Ayrshire, also faces a charge of trying to defeat the ends of justice.

Emma vanished on April 28, sparking a desperate search.

Her remains were found in Galloway Forest on June 13.

At her funeral in July Rev Cameron told the packed church Emma’s death was a “shock” to everyone who knew her.

Parents Ian, 68, and Margaret, 69, heard her described as “inspirational” for her work with vulnerable kids.

Last month, Emma's beloved dog Maverick died.

She got the West Highland Terrier for the 30th birthday and they were inseparable.

Maverick even led her coffin from the church in the arms of a weeping family friend.
