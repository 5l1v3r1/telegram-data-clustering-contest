Dean Smith fires Aston Villa transfer warning to Manchester United

Aston Villa v Newcastle United | Dean Smith talks about the rumoured interest in super John McGinn after the 25-year-old's goal-scoring exploits for Scotland during the break

22 Nov 2019, 22:30 by Ashley Preece, https://www.facebook.com/PreeceBirminghamLive/

Dean Smith believes Aston Villa can - and will - keep hold of Scottish goal-scoring sensation John McGinn, beaming: "I have no doubt in my mind."

McGinn has long been touted with a potential move to Manchester United, with Newcastle United boss Steve Bruce - the man who brought him to Villa Park last summer - admitting this week he's Old Trafford-quality.

The 25-year-old, signed for around £2.75m, is in the form of his life having scored 10 goals in his last 20 games for club and country.

Ginny, as he's known around Bodymoor Heath, fired in another two goals for Scotland during the international break to take his season-tally to double-figures, including three in 12 for Aston Villa.

The all-action midfielder is believed to be worth more than £50m in today's market and signed a new five-year deal at Villa Park as recently as September.

Smith, when asked if Villa are expected to keep hold of the likes of McGinn, said: "There's been a lot of speculation about a lot of players, but we've two owners who want to build a football club, challenging at the top half of this table, and top six in a few years' time.

"To do that you have to keep your best players, and I've no doubts in my mind the owners will want to keep those players."

Speaking of McGinn, who'll once again start in Villa's engine room on Monday night against Newcastle, Smith added: "He's improving every game he plays, he works hard in training, he's scoring goals at international level as well, so he just need to keep improving.

"I've no doubt he will because he has a great head on his shoulders, he's an intelligent boy, but on top that he's a really good lad in the dressing room."

The gaffer, meanwhile, reserved special mention to Monday's counterpart Bruce for signing the midfielder for peanuts in August 2018.

"A great buy," Smith exlaimed. "I credited Steve at the end of the season at Wembley because both goal-scorers were brought in by Steve.

"[McGinn] started to get accolades at Hibs, and Celtic were in for him, but Villa got ahead, and I'm very grateful for that."

Villa are live on Sky Sports on Monday before trips to Old Trafford and Stamford Bridge before the TV cameras are at Villa Park again for the visit of bang-in-form Leicester City.

When asked if such a platform could come at the wrong time given the window opens in less than six weeks, Smith said: "If they're showcasing their talents then it's the right time because they are playing well, and that's all I want from them."
