Warriors struggles continue

22 Nov 2019, 22:35 by Wayne Moore

Frustrating!

That's the best way to describe the mood around the West Kelowna Warriors these days.

Frustration at not being able to score, frustration at always having to chase the game from behind, frustration at not getting a call, frustration at not being able to get a win when you might deserve a better fate.

The Warriors established a dubious franchise record Friday night, dropping their 10th in a row - a 5-1 defeat at the hands of the Wenatchee Wild.

And, it came on their first game back before the hometown faithful after nearly a month, and seven straight games away from Royal LePage Place.

"I thought we competed. I thought we showed some character and some resilience," said head coach Brandon West.

"We lose a top guy in Trevor Adams (upper body) during the game, we lose a top guy in Brandon Dent during the first five minutes of the game. It's hard to recover...it's frustrating to kill those penalties."

As has been the case in each of their previous nine losses during this long streak, the Warriors gave up the first goal. Matt Dorsey banged home a rebound on Wenatchee's third power play of the period with just 90 seconds left on the clock.

Harrison Scott doubled the lead 24 seconds into the second, deflecting home a shot from the right wing.

Tyler Young made it 3-0 five minutes later on another power play before Tanner Main got a cheap one off a Warrior skate to up the lead to four.

The Warriors finally got one back when Deegan Mofford scored his first career BCHL goal with a hard wrist shot from the left circle on a two-on-one break.

The Warriors came close to getting back into it, but newcomer Tyler Cristall rang a shot of the post with 12 minutes still left in the third.

It was one of a handful of chances the Warriors had, but were unable to capitalize on. A common theme during their 10-game slide.

West says it's no secret, the Warriors are creating good scoring chances, but are struggling mightily to finish them off.

"Wednesday night we outchance a team, last Saturday we outchanceÂ  team and we fall on the wrong side of the scoreboard both of those nights.

"Our chances for and against are either on par...we haven't been outchanced a lot in this skid, but we have to find ways to score goals."

During this losing streak, not only have the Warriors given up the first goal in each game, they have never held the lead in any of those 10 contests.

The Warriors went 0-3 with the man advantage, as that part of their game also struggled. Wenatchee was 2-7, with all seven chances coming during the first 40 minutes.

Things won't be any easier Saturday when they host the Salmon Arm Silverbacks, minus Adams who will; miss at least the next game due to injury.

The current slide has also allowed the Merritt Centennials to creep ahead in the standings. The Centennials doubled Prince George 4-2 to move two points up on the Warriors and out of the Interior Division basement.

Saturday's game between the Warriors and Silverbacks gets underway at 6 p.m. at Royal LePage Place.
