Straphanger hit with hammer, thrown onto Bronx subway tracks by homophobic man

22 Nov 2019, 22:25 by Anabel Sosa

An unhinged man smacked an innocent straphanger with a hammer Friday and then threw him onto the subway tracks in the Bronx after shouting a homophobic slur at him, according to police.

The 21-year-old victim was waiting for the D train at Tremont Avenue near Grand Concourse just after 1 a.m. when the man came up to him, called him an anti-gay slur and then whacked him with a hammer, police said.

The victim was able to grab ahold of the hammer but, in response, the suspect picked him up and thew him onto the subway tracks, police said.

The suspect fled the subway station, and the victim was able to climb back onto the platform, police added.

The victim was taken to Saint Barnabas and is in stable condition.

Investigators are treating this as a possible bias incident, according to police sources.
