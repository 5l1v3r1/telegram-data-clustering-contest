Ben Dawson hails 'valuable experience' for Newcastle United U23s and Ludwig Francillette's first goal

The young Magpies picked up a point in their first group game of the Premier League International Cup

22 Nov 2019, 22:37 by Lee Ryder

Ben Dawson believes his side will gain some much-needed experience from their opening Premier League International Cup clash of the season against Benfica.

The Magpies were held to a 1-1 draw against the Portuguese side at Whitley Park on a night which Ludwig Francillette netted his first goal for the club.

Dawson felt the young Magpies done enough in the game to take three points out of their group game, and emerged afterwards to tell Chronicle Live: "I look back at the great example this summer where four or five lads from the Under-23s went out to China for the Premier League Trophy with the first team.

"They grasped the chance.

"We have also played in the Hong Kong 7s tournament so it helps you then go on to deal with different opponents.

"There's other things like travelling and the quick turnaround and then playing.

"The lads had a different experience again to two years ago in this competition.

"Back then we had Sparta Prague who travelled here and just sat back.

"That was totally different to whatever the lads experienced but they might need it at some point.

"It's all valuable for them."

Dawson has taken on the responsibility of selection and tactics since the departure of Neil Redfearn earlier this month.

Looking back on the game Dawson said: "A bit of a mixture really, I was pleased with the lads' effort and application.

"I am disappointed we haven't made the most of it and taken three points to be honest.

"We probably do feel fortunate that Jake Turner made a great save at the end to keep it at 1-1.

"But in general terms we did enough to just about edge it.

"We need to tighten up on some bits in the attacking half to create more opportunities and chances.

"Although, I am pleased with how we dealt with a strong international opponents.

"I can't grumble too much."

Francillette's goal is further evidence that the 6ft 4in defender is settling in by the day on Tyneside.

Dawson said: "He is a real threat as you can imagine with the size of him.

"With any set-play, no matter what the set-up or what the opponent, if there is a good enough delivery, if you've got somebody willing to put their head on it.

"He was and it was a great finish.

"I just wish we could do that a bit more often."
