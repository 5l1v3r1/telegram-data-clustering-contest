Photos of Dwayne 'The Rock' Johnson's Career Evolution

22 Nov 2019, 22:07 by Charlotte Chilton

1994: A College Athlete Johnson received a full scholarship to play football for the University of Miami. As defensive tackle, he helped Miami secure a National Championship win in 1991, but was replaced in the starting lineup soon after due to an injury. @therock - Instagram

1995: The NFL Draft Johnson entered the NFL draft in 1995, but was not drafted. The star shared this photo of his college football days on Instagram , and laughed about it, saying, "[I was] 22yrs old. 280lbs. Ran a 4.69 40 yard dash. Never accomplished my dreams of playing in the NFL...but I sure as hell never missed a meal." @therock - Instagram

1995: Joining the Calgary Stampeders After missing out on the NFL, Johnson signed on to the practice roster for the Calgary Stampeders in the Canadian Football League as a linebacker. The team recently shared a throwback picture of Johnson during his time on the teamwhich only lasted a few months before he was cut. @calstampeders - Twitter

1999: Moving on to WWE After his hopes of playing professional football were dashed, Johnson pivoted towards professional wrestling. After a series of successful fights, Johnson signed a contract with the WWE in 1996. By 1999, he'd become a staple in the sport, winning his first WWF Championship in 1998. Frank Micelotta Archive - Getty Images

2000: Third Generation Wrestler It may seem like a far jump from football to wrestling, but Johnson's father, Rocky Johnson, and his grandfather, Peter Maivia, were both professional wrestlers. Getty Images - Getty Images

2000: The Rock is Born His booming career, as one of the biggest names in wrestling and the face of the sport's Attitude Era athletes, lead to many opportunities to expand his brand as "The Rock." He even released a New York Times best-selling book, called (what else?) The Rock Says... Arnaldo Magnani - Getty Images

2001: His First Film As his wrestling career boosted his public image, Johnson transitioned into acting. He made his film debut in 2001 with a role in The Mummy Returns. J. P. Aussenard - Getty Images

2002: A Big Gig Johnson's acting career reached another level when he hosted Saturday Night Live for the first time in 2002. He's since joined the elite club of actors who've hosted the show five times. NBC - Getty Images

2002: Taking on Hulkamania Johnson was known for his trash-talking persona in the ring and in 2002 he faced off against the '80s and '90s icon Hulk Hogan for the first time ever at WrestleMania X8. The event was a historic matchup of icon vs. icon. George Pimentel - Getty Images

2002: Seeing Double You know you've made it when you get your own wax figure at Madame Tussauds. In 2002, the wrestling champion attended the unveiling of his, which featured his signature eyebrow lift. Mario Tama - Getty Images

2003: Becoming Mainstream By 2003, Johnson was no longer just a professional wrestler, but a household name. That same year, he showed just how big his fan base had grown by presenting an award at the Nickelodeon Kids' Choice Awards with Brittany Murphy. Steve Grayson - Getty Images

2003: A Big Role In 2003, Johnson secured a lead role in the film The Rundown, proving that he could do more than just cameo roles. The actor poses on the red carpet with then-wife Dany Garcia at the premiere. Kevin Winter - Getty Images

2003: Action Hero of the Year Johnson solidified his new career path as an action star when he earned GQ's Action Hero of the Year award in 2003. KMazur - Getty Images

2004: Stepping Out of the Ring In 2004, the star decided to retire from WWE to pursue his burgeoning acting career. During his wrestling career, he was a two time international champion, a five time tag team champion, and a ten time world champion, as well as WWE's sixth triple crown champion. Djamilla Rosa Cochran - Getty Images

2004: Another Starring Role After his retirement from the WWE, Johnson's film career took off. He appeared in Walking Tall that same year. Frederick M. Brown - Getty Images

2005: A Fearless Reputation Given his tough guy personal, the actor regularly attended events that reinforced his fearless action star image, like the Taurus Stunt Awards in 2005, as seen here. Jon Kopaloff - Getty Images

2006: A Transition in Film The actor headed to France for the Cannes Film Festival in 2006 for his role in the drama/fantasy film, Southland Tales, which also featured Sarah Michelle Gellar. VALERY HACHE - Getty Images

2007: A Gentler Rock In 2007, Johnson continued to push his range as an actor with the role of Joe Kingman in the Disney film, The Game Plan. The movie was a family-friendly hit that not only increased his fan base and brand recognition, but proved he is more than just muscle. Jason Merritt/TERM - Getty Images

2008: The Academy Awards In true movie star form, Johnson looked dapper in a tuxedo at the 80th Annual Academy Awards, during which he presented an award. Steve Granitz - Getty Images

2008: WWE Hall of Fame For his record-breaking matchups and countless champion titles, Dwayne "The Rock" Johnson was inducted into the WWE Hall of Fame in 2008. George Napolitano - Getty Images

2008: A Major Film Talk about star power: Johnson joined costars Anne Hathaway, Alan Arkin, and Steve Carell at the premiere of their film Get Smart. Kevin Winter - Getty Images

2009: The Rock is Everywhere By 2009, it seemed like Johnson was everywherefrom sports to television to filmand even presented at the 51st Annual Grammy Awards. John Shearer - Getty Images

2009: Turning to Producing Johnson and his ex-wife, Dany Garcia (the couple divorced in 2007), attend the premiere of Racing Dreams, a film they coproduced together. The pair would later cofound the production company, Seven Bucks Productions, in 2012. Bryan Bedder - Getty Images

2009: Back at his Alma Mater The actor was asked to deliver the December commencement address at his alma mater, the University of Miami, in 2009. He offered words of wisdom about never letting setbacks get you down . John Parra - Getty Images

2010: A Flop In 2010, Johnson returned to a familiar role as the star of the action movie Faster. The film ended up flopping, and is Johnson's lowest-grossing wide release ever. Kevin Winter - Getty Images

2011: Back to the WWE Johnson announced he was coming out of wrestling retirement to challenge the WWE's newest star, John Cena, at WrestleMania XXVIII. Dimitrios Kambouris - Getty Images

2011: A Prestigious Award Everyone knew it already, but in 2011 the Guys Choice Awards made it official by presenting The Rock with The Biggest Ass Kicker Award. Kevin Winter - Getty Images

2011: Joining 'The Fast and the Furious' Crew In 2011, Johnson joined the successful The Fast and the Furious franchise for their latest film, Fast Five. BORIS HORVAT - Getty Images

2012: Taking on Cena WrestleMania XXVIII's The Rock Vs. John Cena matchup was hyped and highly-anticipated for an entire year. The match final came to blows in 2012, when The Rock ultimately defeated his younger opponent. Ron Elkman/Sports Imagery - Getty Images

2012: Back to Filming In 2012, Johnson was spotted on the set of Pain & Gain in Miami. The actor costarred alongside Mark Wahlberg and Anthony Mackie. Uri Schanker - Getty Images

2014: Hosting a Holiday Special Johnson was tapped to host TNT's Christmas in Washington special in 2014, during which he met President Barack Obama and First Lady Michelle Obama. Kevin Mazur - Getty Images

2015: A Blockbuster Premiere Joined by his mother, daughter Simone, and girlfriend Lauren Hashian at the premiere of Furious 7, his third film for the franchise. The film ended up earning $1.516 billion at the box office and is the ninth highest-grossing film of all time. Gregg DeGuire - Getty Images

2016: A Breakout Hit Johnson's HBO show Ballers was one of the biggest breakout hits of the 2015 season. The show is loosely based on the star's life as a failed football player who ultimately becomes successful. Aaron Davidson - Getty Images

2016: Movie of the Year At the premiere of Disney Pixar's Moana, for which Johnson did voice work. The movie featured music composed by Lin-Manuel Miranda and received two Academy Award nominations. Alberto E. Rodriguez - Getty Images

2017: The Return of 'Baywatch' Who else would star in the Baywatch reboot other than The Rock and Zac Efron? The two actors arrive on the red carpet, alongside original cast member David Hasselhoff, at the premiere. Franziska Krug - Getty Images

2017: A Hollywood Star In 2017, Johnson earned a star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame. The actor was joined at the ceremony by his wife, Lauren Hashian, and their daughter, Jasmine. JB Lacroix - Getty Images

2018: Game Show Host Johnson turned his charisma and athletic prowess into a game show job. The actor hosted the first season of The Titan Games for NBC in 2018, where he inspired people to become the titan version of themselves. NBC - Getty Images

2019: A Pop Culture Icon Johnson accepted MTV's Generation Award in 2019, which is for "beloved actors whose diverse contributions to both film and television have turned them into household names in the industry." The actor took the opportunity to talk about showing kindness to one another . Kevin Winter - Getty Images

2019: Another Successful Year This has potentially been Johnson's biggest year yet. The actor starred in Fast & Furious Presents: Hobbs & Shaw, which is a spinoff of the The Fast and the Furious films, he tied the knot to longtime partner Lauren Hashian this summer, and he announced his latest business venture: a tequila company. Well done, sir. Lintao Zhang - Getty Images
