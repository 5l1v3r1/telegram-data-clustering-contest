Mauricio Pochettino 'confident' Jose Mourinho will fail at Tottenham

22 Nov 2019, 19:31 by Buzz Contributor

Mauricio Pochettino is supposedly ‘confident’ his Tottenham Hotspur replacement Jose Mourinho will not be a success.

Mourinho took over from Pochettino in the space of just 11 hours , after Spurs chief Daniel Levy chose to part ways with the Argentinian.

Tottenham had enjoyed a hugely successful time under Pochettino, establishing themselves as genuine Premier League title challengers and reaching last season’s Champions League final.

However, it all seemed to go wrong this season, with the club languishing in 14th when Levy decided to wield the axe.

In a shock move, former Chelsea and Manchester United boss Mourinho has been appointed as Spurs’ new head coach in a bid to turn their fortunes around.

The Portuguese tactician ended his time at Old Trafford on a sour note, facing suggestions he had lost his mojo.

It has now emerged that Pochettino seems to think along the same lines, with ESPN reporting that he is banking on Mourinho’s failure.

According to their sources, Pochettino was not prepared to resign or leave via mutual consent.

Instead, he was only willing to leave if another side approached him or he was sacked.

Had he left the club any other way, Pochettino thinks it would have appeared as though he had failed and would have discouraged other elite sides from offering him a job.

He supposedly believes Mourinho will fail in the role and has gambled on this belief coming true.

If that becomes the case then no blame will fall at Pochettino’s door, with Levy getting the brunt of the criticism for giving up on the manager too soon.

What is for sure now is that the former Southampton boss will be in the frame for every single top managerial job going.

It has already been suggested that he could end up at Manchester United , while a move to Bayern Munich and a shock switch to Arsenal have also been mooted.
