Back On The Field, PSL Next Target For Aghahowa

22 Nov 2019, 19:45 by Soccer Laduma

Now that he is back on the field and appearing to be regaining his confidence and fitness, Junior ‘Aghahowa’ Khanye has sights set on returning to the PSL.  

In September, it was reported by the Siya crew that Khanye finally made a return to football after a long absence, after he signed with Limpopo-based ABC Motsepe League side Ngwaabe City Motors.

The former Kaizer Chiefs star, who is now 34 years old, has always reiterated that he would like to play at the top level again, before he officially retires from the game.  

Speaking to the Siya crew, Khanye confirmed that he is targeting January for a potential move to the PSL.

“I am very fit and oozing with confidence right now. We are playing against Mikhado tomorrow (Saturday) in Venda and I am looking forward to it. The support I have been getting from everyone here since I arrived at the club is amazing and encouraging. I’m getting the support from the chairman, technical team, players and everyone associated with the club, and that’s pushing me to do well. The confidence is back and my aim is to move a step up in January to either an NFD (GladAfrica Championship) club or straight to the PSL (Absa Premiership). The confidence and fitness is what I want to tell you about, and as for the skill, you and everyone know,” Khanye boasts.
