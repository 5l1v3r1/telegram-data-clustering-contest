Citi Bike say e-bikes are returning to NYC this winter

22 Nov 2019, 19:05 by Natalie Musumeci

Bike lovers, rejoice!

Citi Bike is bringing back its electric pedal-assist rides this winter after pulling them from service across the Big Apple in April due to brake issues.

Lyft, which owns Citi Bike, once hoped to have the e-bikes back this fall, but said the company is “taking extra time to make sure they’re safe and ready to go for New York City streets.”

The pedal-assist bikes were yanked from service earlier this year after some riders “experienced stronger-than-expected braking force” on the front wheel, the company said.

Battery problems then struck similar e-bikes in San Francisco, which forced the company to find a new battery supplier as it reworked the bike’s troublesome brake setup.

“It’s taken longer than anticipated to get all the necessary components for the new bikes and complete safety testing, but we’re making progress,” Lyft said.

Citi Bike plans to initially launch several hundred e-bikes this winter “and will gradually ramp up to a larger fleet.”

The company is also ditching its previous plan to charge $2 fee for every e-bike ride, opting to charge 10 cents per minute for annual Citi Bike members and 15 cents per minute for non-members.

“We’ve done the math, and the majority of riders will pay less for e-bike rides with per-minute pricing,” the company claimed. “When e-bikes were in service, 80 percent of member e-bike rides were under 20 minutes.”

Citi Bike will also cap e-bike charges at $2 for member rides that begin and/or end outside of Manhattan and will remove the two-minute wait period between rides.

Additionally, starting Jan. 15, Citi Bike will cut its extra time fees on its classic bikes.

Members will now pay 15 cents per minute after the included 45 minutes, instead of $2.50 every 15 minutes. Riders with a single ride pass will also pay 15 cents per minute after the included 30 minutes.

“These changes will eliminate a point of frustration and save riders money by only charging them for the time they use,” said the company.
