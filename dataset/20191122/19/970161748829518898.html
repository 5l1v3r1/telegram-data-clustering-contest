McDonald's worker, 17, battered with brick 'for refusing to give boys free food'

22 Nov 2019, 19:04

A young McDonald’s worker was viciously assaulted by four boys with a brick for refusing to give them free food, it is claimed.

Charm Sullivan, 17, was left with a badly-swollen face that required hospital treatment after the attack at a McDonald’s near Hunting Park in Philadelphia on Wednesday night.

Charm’s cousin Khadijah Lovett said: ‘She’s hurt,” Lovett said. “The only thing she keeps saying is she doesn’t know. Why her. Why her?

‘They assaulted her several times.

‘They hit her with a brick in front of McDonald’s.

‘She’s all beat up. She had to go to the hospital.’

Charm says she was serving behind the counter when the boys started pestering her, and that they jumped her when she went to use the bathroom.

The assault continued outside, and ended with the teenager laying unconscious between two parked cars, it is claimed.

Charm suffered swollen lips and a busted right eye in the attack, and has now vowed never to return to the McDonald’s where she was assaulted.

Philadelphia Police are hunting for her attackers, but have yet to release a description of the suspects, or make any arrests.

A manager at the McDonald’s refused to comment on the attack when contacted by NBC10.
