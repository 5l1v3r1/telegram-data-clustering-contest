Chip shop in Swansea is selling battered mince pies this Christmas

And they've also done their own version of pigs in blankets too

22 Nov 2019, 19:23 by Katie Bellis

Christmas food is one of the best things about the festive season.

You can't have a dinner without pigs in blankets and it's not Christmas without a mince pie.

But what about having them deep fried in batter?

It might sound like a fishy idea, but that's exactly what Copperfish in Mumbles Pier in Swansea has just started doing.

Mumbles Pier general manager Hannah Hunter reckons you can batter any food.

"We had battered Creme eggs at Easter and they went down well which is why we decided to sell mince pies and pigs in blankets," she said.

"We started selling the battered mince pies last Sunday and people have been divided. There's been a mixed reaction. A lot of people want them but some people will say that they can't put that in their mouth.

The battered mince pies cost £1.10 and the pigs in blanket cost £1.70. Both are dipped in flour and batter before being deep fried.

But what do they really taste like? I tried them both out to find out.

The verdict

My first impression of a battered mince pie was that it would, quite frankly, be disgusting. The thought of eating the sweet festive treat covered in batter made me feel slightly sick at first.

It felt wrong mixing the batter with a mince pie and cream, which is served on the side but, after taking my first bite, I was pleasantly surprised.

The coating was fresh and crispy and enveloped the warm pie which had a generous filling but after a couple of bites I couldn't eat any more.

Was it the best thing I have ever tasted? No. Would I order it again? No. But I was prepared for it to taste a lot worse than what it actually did. That being said though, while I wasn't a fan, my photographer definitely was - who had gobbled his battered mince pie faster than you could say "cheese".

However, the chippy's festive version of pigs in blankets, definitely sounded more appealing. I mean, after all, a battered sausage at a chippy is always a good idea.

The pork sausage came wrapped in rashers of streaky bacon and was then coated in the signature batter and was served with gravy and cranberry sauce - the perfect combo.

I definitely preferred this to the mince pie.
