XXXTentacion to release fourth and final studio album Bad Vibes Forever

22 Nov 2019, 23:39

XXXTentacion is set to release his final studio album, just one year after his death.

The late rapper’s second posthumous record, Bad Vibes Forever, will drop in just two weeks’ time on 6 December.

Bad Vibes Forever is executive produced by the star’s mother, Cleopatra Bernard, and features a whole host of guest artists, including Lil Wayne and Old Town Road hitmaker Lil Nas X, who features on the country-tinged hip hop song School Globes.

The album is supported by the release of previous singles, Royalty and Hearteater, with the latter’s music video guest starring XXX’s ex-girlfriend.

As well as these two singles, the album’s title track was also released to streaming platforms today.

And although the tracklisting is yet to be confirmed, the artwork has been revealed, showing a picture of the rapper with the word ‘ugly’ scrawled across his face.
