WHAT COULD'VE BEEN

Aberdeen star Considine had chance to join Rangers as youth player, reveals dad Doug

22 Nov 2019, 23:04 by Derek McGregor

ABERDEEN star Andrew Considine will run out at McDiarmid Park this weekend for his 500th game for the club.

But his father and former Don Doug has revealed his son almost didn’t manage one.

Rangers, under former Reds great Alex McLeish, tried to sign Considine Jr in 2003.

But the move ultimately jolted the Pittodrie outfit into ensuring the academy graduate stayed put.

The rest, as they say, is history.

Considine Sr told SunSport: “It’s true, Rangers tried to sign Andrew while he was a youth player at Aberdeen and still waiting for his breakthrough.

“George Adams was head of youth at Rangers and someone I knew well from my time at Aberdeen and he was a big fan of Andrew’s.

"As a family we have a high regard for George and we knew he would look after him if he’d gone to Glasgow.

“Andrew was 16 and I thought he was doing quite well at Aberdeen.

“But very few actually get signed up. In fact, I’m not sure if there was anyone else signed from Andrew’s year.

“At one point, we thought he could leave. We didn’t know which way it would go. Aberdeen were playing their cards close to their chest.

“All the 16-year-olds at the time were waiting to hear of their fate. Then Rangers made known their interest in signing Andrew.

“After that it didn’t take long for the phone call to come from Aberdeen to go back in and talk.

“Andrew signed and it was great for him and all of the family. We were all very proud.

“But, honestly, I didn’t think he would be with Aberdeen all his life. It’s remarkable how things have worked out.

“It’s great credit to Andrew for his dedication, professionalism and ability to have consistently proved himself over 15 years for various managers.”

Considine Jr is under contract until 2021 and there’s every chance the 32-year-old will see out his career with the Dons.

Doug, a member of the 1980 Premier League-winning squad, said: “Aberdeen’s one of the biggest clubs in the country and he should be very proud of himself for what he’s done so far.

“I see no reason why he can’t carry on for a good bit yet. Playing for Aberdeen means so much to him.

“I still feel emotional every time watching him come out the tunnel with the Aberdeen strip on. It’s a club that will always be close to my heart.”

Asked how he feels things would have worked out for his boy if he had signed for Rangers, Considine Sr added: “It’s very hard to tell.

“I like to think he’d have still gone on to be a success for Rangers or anywhere else he would have gone.

“Andrew has a very good football brain and he uses it well. He’s shown that by the number of games he’s managed to play for Aberdeen.”
