RCMP officer gets conditional discharge after northern off-duty assault case waived to Saskatoon

22 Nov 2019, 23:19 by Bre McAdam, Saskatoon StarPhoenix

While on probation, Cpl. Luay Toma cannot possess or consume alcohol or cannabis and must take anger management if required by his probation officer.

A northern Saskatchewan RCMP officer has been given a 12-month conditional discharge after pleading guilty to an off-duty assault.

Cpl. Luay Toma, 30, was charged on May 30 with assaulting someone in a home in Wollaston Lake on May 20. The case was proceeding through the northern community’s circuit court but was waived to Saskatoon on Oct. 22, court documents show.

A publication ban issued during Toma’s first court appearance prevents the reporting of any information that could identify the victim, including details of the assault.

While on probation, Toma must report to a probation officer in Saskatoon and cannot possess or consume alcohol, cannabis or non-prescribed drugs or be in any place that sells alcohol or cannabis.

He is also ordered to take any personal counselling or programs for alternatives to violence and anger management as required by his probation officer.

Toma’s conditional discharge means he will not have a criminal record if he abides by his conditions for 12 months.

The RCMP officer remains suspended with pay pending the conclusion of an internal code of conduct investigation, RCMP spokesman Rob King confirmed.

Wollaston Lake is a remote community located approximately 700 kilometres northeast of Prince Albert.
