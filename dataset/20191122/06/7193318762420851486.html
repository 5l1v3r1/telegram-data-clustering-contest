Oklahoma Hall of Fame inducts 8

22 Nov 2019, 06:04 by Staff Reports

The Oklahoma Hall of Fame inducted its 2019 class Thursday night. Induction into the Hall is Oklahoma's highest honor, according to the Hall with 698 individuals who have been recognized since the first class in 1928.

The 2019 honorees were:

• James Day, an oilman from Pond Creek.

• Tricia Everest, a philanthropist and community leader from Oklahoma City.

• Gray Frederickson, an Oscar- and Emmy-winning producer from Oklahoma City.

• John Herrington, Native American astronaut from Wetumka.

• John Nickel, a businessman and philanthropist from Muskogee.

• J.C. Watts Jr., a former University of Oklahoma and CFL football player and a U.S. Congressman from Eufaula.

• Steve Largent, a former University of Tulsa and NFL football player and a U.S. Representative from Tulsa.

• Allen Wright (Kiliahote), Choctaw chief known for suggesting the name Oklahoma in 1866 (posthumous induction).
