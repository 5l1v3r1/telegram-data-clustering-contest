Stokes and Curran put England on top in first New Zealand Test

22 Nov 2019, 06:57

Ben Stokes scored 91 and Sam Curran took two wickets to put England on top in the first Test against New Zealand.

Resuming on 241-4, Stokes’ innings and a ninth-wicket stand of 52 between Jos Buttler and Jack Leach took England to 353.

Kane Williamson hit fifty in New Zealand’s reply before becoming the second of Curran’s victims and the home side had reached 144-4 by the close of play on day two.

England had aspirations of surpassing 400 in their first-innings after Stokes, Joe Denly and Rory Burns all scored fifties on day one of the match in Tauranga.

World Cup hero Stokes and young batsman Ollie Pope made a bright start this morning, adding 36 runs in the first ten overs before Ross Taylor produced a superb catch to dismiss Stokes nine runs short of his century.

Stokes’ departure sparked a collapse of 4-18 in 21 balls as Surrey’s Pope fell for 29 while Curran and Jofra Archer contributed just four runs between them.

Somerset team-mates and close friends Buttler and Leach combined to frustrate New Zealand, however, with their ninth-wicket partnership almost taking the tourists past 350.

Buttler eventually departed for 43 and Neil Wagner also dismissed Stuart Broad to finish with figures of 3-90, bettered only by Tim Southee (4-88).

In New Zealand’s reply, Curran – selected ahead of experienced all-rounder Chris Woakes – came on first change and immediately found the breakthrough, trapping Tom Latham LBW.

Fellow opener Jeet Raval and Kiwi skipper Williamson then added 54 before the former was removed by Leach.

Stokes continued to impress by dismissing Ross Taylor and Curran claimed the prize wicket of Williamson in the final hour of the day.

Henry Nicholls and BJ Watling survived a tricky period before the close of play but New Zealand will still resume on day three trailing by 209 runs.

Reflecting on the day and the state of the match, England bowler Leach said: ‘It was a good day for us.

‘We would have liked an extra wicket at the end but if we can get a couple early, we’ll be right on top.

‘The ball from Sam Curran to get Kane Williamson misbehaved and that’s a good sign when you’ve got runs on the board.’

New Zealand’s Southee, meanwhile, added: ‘We’ve still got a lot of batting to come so we just need some partnerships and eke out some more runs.

‘We’ll turn up tomorrow, approach the first session and then reassess where we are at lunch and then the same at tea.’
