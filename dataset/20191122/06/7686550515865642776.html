British Bake Off inspires Britain's kids - but many don't know pork comes from pigs

New cooking school will bring weekly food education classes to primary schools across the UK

22 Nov 2019, 06:00 by Paul Smith

Trendy ingredients such as avocado and passion fruit are readily identified by over 50 per cent of youngsters, but almost a third are unaware that pork comes from pigs.

Those were the findings of a study commissioned by Zanussi to celebrate its newly-launched partnership with the charity Cook School.

Perhaps sparked by prime-time favourite the Great British Bake Off, the survey also concluded that one in five of those involved bake at least once a week.

Seven in 10 have put together batches of cupcakes, with pizza, rice-krispy cakes and pancakes also topping the list of children's signature dishes.

However, the survey of 1,000 youngsters aged between six and 11 has also revealed some alarming gaps in their basic knowledge of food and nutrition.

When shown a courgette, 42 per cent mistook it for a cucumber and 19 per cent reported never having tried one.

And while 86 per cent of those quizzed by OnePoll have visited a farm to see the link between animals and the food on their plates, two in five do not know that chips come from potatoes.

Chris George, UK head of marketing for Zanussi, said: “Based on these results we can see that today’s youngsters are likely to be more engaged with food than any previous generation.

“However, it is also clear there is a significant task ahead to ensure the fundamentals of food and nutrition, as well as sustainable eating, are taught early enough to allow healthy habits to form.

"With this in mind, we are delighted that Zanussi and Cook School have teamed up to host weekly cooking classes in primary schools and in turn make a real, positive difference to childhood obesity, diabetes and food wastage."

Ahead of the launch, Cook Scool's founder Amanda Grant viewed the findings that 80 per cent of children would like to learn more about cooking at school as "fantastic news"

”The cooking school will bring weekly food education classes to primary schools across the UK," she said.

"Children will be taught how to cook well-balanced, sustainable meals from scratch and educate their families on the importance of nutrition for long-term health.

“By the end of 2020 we’ll deliver weekly Zanussi Cook School classes reaching 30,000 children nationwide to empower them and their families to make healthier and more sustainable eating choices."
