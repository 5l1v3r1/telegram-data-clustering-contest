Scooter Braun finally speaks out about ‘toxic division’ with Taylor Swift

22 Nov 2019, 06:48

Scooter Braun has finally spoken out about his feud with Taylor Swift, after admitting it was hard not to make a statement for the past six months.

Taylor slammed Scooter earlier this year after he purchased Big Machine Records, which owned all of her back catalogue of songs.

She claimed she wasn’t allowed to buy the masters of her work and was ‘banned’ from performing some of her old songs at the American Music Awards, which Scooter denied to be true.

While Taylor has publicly attacked him on social media, he remained silent until now, when he revealed: ‘I haven’t talked about this in six months, not once.

‘I haven’t made a statement about it and that’s hard, because when you have a lot of things being said and a lot of different opinions — yet the principals haven’t had a chance to speak to each other — there’s a lot of confusion.

‘I think that I’m not gonna go into details here because it’s just not my style,’ he told the Hollywood Chamber of Commerce State of the Entertainment Industry Conference.

Scooter, 38, added: ‘I just think we live in a time with toxic division and people thinking that social media is the appropriate place to air out on each other and not have conversations.
