UltraSoC joins OpenHW Group

22 Nov 2019, 06:01 by David Manners

UltraSoC, the Cambridge on-chip analytics IP specialist, has joined the Open Hardware Group (OpenHW) to further the adoption of open source processors  for high volume SoCs.

UltraSoC will contribute its experience and IP in system-level debug and processor trace.

The OpenHW Group has announced a range of cores, dubbed CORE-V, based on the RISC-V open ISA.

Both UltraSoC and the OpenHW Group are active members of the RISC-V Foundation, and development in this area will be a key part of UltraSoC’s initial contribution to the group.

The Open

HWGroup, launched in June, is comprised of 25 member and partner organisations and operates as a non-profit, driven by its members and individual contributors, where hardware and software designers collaborate in the development of open-source cores, related IP, tools and software.

“The OpenHW Group is just what the industry needs to make it easier to develop real systems using open-source hardware – these developments are increasingly presenting an equal or better option to proprietary CPU-based alternatives,” says UltraSoC CEO Rupert Baines (pictured).
