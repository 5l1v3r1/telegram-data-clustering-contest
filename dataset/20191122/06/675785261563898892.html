Diego Maradona causes Roger Federer to break down in tears with heartwarming message

Legendary footballer Maradona hailed Federer as 'the greatest' - and told 38-year-old to call him if he has any trouble in Argentina

22 Nov 2019, 06:32 by Gareth Bicknell

Legendary footballer Diego Maradona caused Roger Federer to break down in tears with a heartwarming message calling him 'the greatest'.

Federer leads the all-time list of the most Grand Slam wins with 20 - but his record is under threat with Rafa Nadal just behind, while Novak Djokovic is fast catching his rivals up on 16.

Swiss star Federer is currently touring South America playing a series of exhibition matches with Alexander Zverev.

Federer had invited Maradona to his match in the 1986 World Cup winner's native Argentina, but the 59-year-old was unable to attend.

However, Maradona instead sent a message by video, which was relayed on a big screen after Federer's match against his German opponent.

Maradona has long been considered to be one of the greatest footballers of all-time, although many would argue that his compatriot Lionel Messi has surpassed him.

And Federer appeared to be overwhelmed with emotion when Maradona referred to the tennis star as "the greatest" in his video.

In the clip, Maradona said: “Hello master, machine, like I call you.

"You were, you are, and will always be the greatest. There is no other like you.

"I want you to know that if you have any kind of trouble in my country, you can call me and tell me what you need.”

Maradona this week quit as boss of Argentine top-flight club Gimnasia after two months in the job .

Meanwhile Federer, still playing at 38, insisted he had no plans to retire.

"My retirement is going to depend on my health. At this moment I see no reason to stop," he said in quotes reported by the Sun .

"I think in 2009 I questioned it for the first time. [It's been] ten years and here I am. I'm exactly where I wanted to be at this age.

"It is a lot of work. I expected to play until 35-36 and here I am, in a new dimension."
