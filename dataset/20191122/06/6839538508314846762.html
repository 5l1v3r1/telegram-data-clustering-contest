Gateway Motorway hit by major traffic congestion

22 Nov 2019, 06:50

One of Brisbane’s busiest motorways is jammed for more than 15km after a trio of traffic incidents this afternoon. Motorists are advised to avoid the area if possible.

The first incident, a serious crash, occurred about 1.30pm at Bald Hills on the Bruce Hwy near the motorway exit.

It caused a massive traffic jam that extended more than 15km.

A mattress caught under a truck and caused it to break down at about 2.30pm, adding to the delays.

Finally, a breakdown in the centre northbound lane near the Deagon exit has further exacerbated conditions.

As of 3.50pm traffic was very slow moving from Mango Hill to Banyo.

Down on the Gold Coast, a multi-vehicle crash involving a bus on the Gold Coast has left more than a dozen people needing the attention of paramedics.

Emergency services were called to a crash involving a bus and two vehicles on Brisbane Street in Biggera Waters at 3.26pm.

Paramedics assessed 15 patients, with two women in their 30s, one man in his 30s and an elderly man transported to Gold Coast University Hospital in a stable condition.

One lane of Brisbane Street is blocked with police directing the flow of traffic.
