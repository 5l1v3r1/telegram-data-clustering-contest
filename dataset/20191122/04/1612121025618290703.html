Google probably shouldn’t kill any products on Stadia launch week, but they did it anyway

22 Nov 2019, 04:25 by Jared Peters

Google has announced that Cloud Print will be sailing into the sunset beginning in 2020. Honestly, this probably isn’t a big deal, since I doubt many people use it, enterprise customers never adopted it, and other pieces of Google’s ecosystem will pick up the slack. But come on, Google, this looks terrible.

After a year of slaughtering services left and right, and tons of apprehension over how long Google will actually support Stadia, the last thing they needed to do was announce anything at all getting removed from their portfolio. Stadia can’t even get out of the gate in a functional matter, so it really doesn’t need any help to make the whole situation look worse.

Since Cloud Print is going to be supported until 2020 anyway, why wouldn’t you wait until the end of the year to make this announcement? Give Stadia a little bit of time to stand on its own before you recommit to filling your graveyard.

Killing Cloud Print is fine, but the timing could not have been worse. Get a better PR department, Google.
