Police nab 7 million pills 'bound for Queensland' ahead of festival season

22 Nov 2019, 01:46 by Lydia Lynch

Police have seized 700 kilograms of crystalline MDMA with the potential to make 7 million ecstasy pills that was believed to be bound for Queensland ahead of the summer music festival season.

The first batch of drugs was uncovered in August, as part of an international investigation into a Dutch-based organised-crime syndicate.

Following the first raid at Rotterdam, a further 15 search warrants were conducted in the Netherlands and Belgium this month that saw 11 people charged.

"These search warrants uncovered concealed laboratories and 50 tonnes of precursor chemicals for the production of MDMA," an Australian Border Force statment read.

Queensland Police Deputy Commissioner Tracy Linford said the drugs destined for Australian communities would "be diluted with other contaminants and then falsely pedalled as a safe drug".

"They are anything but this; in fact, they are made by unsophisticated and unqualified people in clandestine conditions whose priority is not the authenticity of their product but profit," she said.

The raids uncovered enough MDMA crystals and oils to produce 15 million pills.

Two alleged ringleaders were arrested in the Netherlands and Belgium, a 58-year-old and a 37-year-old man. The arrested Dutch nationals have been charged by Dutch authorities and are awaiting trial in the Netherlands.

A 48-year-old woman from Bass Hill in NSW was arrested in Sydney on November 11 and has been extradited to Brisbane.

She was charged with an attempt to possess a commercial quantity of border controlled drugs, an offence that carries a maximum penalty of life imprisonment.

She was next due to appear in Brisbane Magistrates Court on November 29.
