NFL's Mason Rudolph 'racial slur' verdict after Myles Garrett hit him with helmet

Cleveland Browns defensive end Garrett's ban was upheld following his appeal hearing after he ripped Pittsburgh Steelers quarterback's helmet off and hit him with it

22 Nov 2019, 01:40 by Gareth Bicknell

The NFL 'found no such evidence' that Mason Rudolph directed a racial slur at Myles Garrett before the latter ripped the Pittsburgh Steelers helmet off him and struck him with it.

NFL spokesman Brian McCarthy said the league had looked into the claim made by Cleveland Browns defensive end Garrett during the appeal hearing against his indefinite suspension.

But McCarthy said they had found no evidence of a racial slur, with Rudolph himself also denying the accusation.

Garrett's suspension for his role in the brawl with the Pittsburgh Steelers last week was upheld on Thursday, ensuring he will miss at least the rest of the 2019 season and postseason.

Appeals officer James Thrash made the ruling, which will keep Garrett out of Cleveland's final six regular-season games - and any postseason games if the Browns qualify - before having to apply for reinstatement from commissioner Roger Goodell in 2020.

 

In a statement released on Thursday, the league also announced that Steelers center Maurkice Pouncey's three-game suspension for his part in the incident was reduced to two games.

Appeals officer Derrick Brooks ruled on Pouncey, who will miss Sunday's contest at Cincinnati and a Week 13 rematch with the Browns.

In addition to forfeited game checks during their suspensions, Garrett will be fined $45,623, and Pouncey will be fined $35,096. Browns defensive tackle Larry Ogunjobi's one-game suspension was upheld a day earlier, though his fine was dropped.

The brawl began in the waning seconds of the Browns' win last week, when Garrett got into a scrap with Steelers quarterback Rudolph, removed the QB's helmet and struck Rudolph's unprotected head with it . Pouncey then jumped on Garrett and threw several punches and kicks, and Ogunjobi shoved Rudolph from behind.

 

ESPN reported earlier on Thursday that Garrett said at his appeal hearing that Rudolph had used a racial slur prior to the melee. Garrett's hearing, which was held Wednesday in New York, reportedly lasted less than two hours.

Garrett acknowledged the report - which was confirmed by the Washington Post - in a statement posted Thursday evening on Twitter , reading: "I was assured that the hearing was space that afforded the opportunity to speak openly and honestly about the incident that led to my suspension.

"This was not meant for public dissemination, nor was it a convenient attempt to justify my actions or restore my image in the eyes of those I disappointed.

"I know what I heard. Whether my opponent's comment was born out of frustration or ignorance, I cannot say.

 

"But his actions do not excuse my lack of restraint in the moment, and I truly regret the impact this has had on the league, the Browns and our devoted fans."

Through the team and his lawyer, Rudolph strongly denied Garrett's accusation, branding it "a lie" and "an assault on Mason's integrity".

The quarterback's media session scheduled for Thursday was cancelled.

NFL spokesman Brian McCarthy told Pro Football Talk later on Thursday that the league "looked into it and found no such evidence."

It is not known whether on-field microphones caught any exchange between the players. Teams typically have a microphone in the shoulder pads of the center or guards, allowing for audio of the quarterback's cadence on a broadcast.

According to ESPN's Jake Trotter, multiple Browns said on Thursday that it was the first they had heard about the accusation of a racial slur.

Rudolph is expected to be fined for his involvement in the scuffle. He appeared to grab at Garrett's helmet and facemask while the two scrapped on the turf.

Garrett, 23, was the No. 1 overall pick in the 2017 draft. He has 30.5 career sacks in 37 games, including 10 through 10 games this season.
