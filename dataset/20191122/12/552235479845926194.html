Brute who attacked his pregnant partner twice is behind bars

Six days before she was due to give birth, he returned home drunk and became aggressive.

22 Nov 2019, 12:54 by Hamilton Advertiser, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/hamilton-advertiser/

A brute who attacked his partner twice when she was pregnant has been jailed for nearly three years.

Steffanie Grady was first assaulted by Ian Murphy on New Year’s Day 2008, but she was so scared of him that she didn’t report his violence until ten years later.

Murphy, 38, of Redwood Crescent, Viewpark, was sentenced at Hamilton Sheriff Court last Thursday.

He admitted four charges of assaulting Ms Grady between 2008 and 2017.

Jennifer Guy, prosecuting, said the first attack happened after a Hogmanay celebration.

Murphy became angry because his partner stayed out later.

Ms Guy told the court: “As soon as she got home, the accused went mad.

“It was the first time she had seen him acting like this and she was scared.

“He kept shouting and bawling at her and slapped her forcefully across the face. He then dragged her into the living room where he continued to strike her to the head.”

Ms Grady was treated for a perforated eardrum, cuts and bruises, including a black eye.

At hospital she found out she was pregnant and told Murphy who promised he would never hit her again.

That promise was broken in August 2008 when she was heavily pregnant.

Six days before she was due to give birth, Murphy returned home drunk and became aggressive.

He kicked Ms Grady, causing her to fall head first down the stairs.

Ms Guy said she had “obvious concerns” for her unborn baby, but hospital staff reassured her the baby was fine.

Afraid of repercussions from Murphy, she told nurses she had fallen by accident.

Ms Guy also told the court of an occasion in January 2015 when Murphy lost his temper, pulled his partner from a chair and dragged her across the room “in full view of a witness”.

Then, in December 2017, Ms Grady went to a work’s Christmas party and when she got home Murphy started shouting at her for staying out late.

Ms Guy said: “The accused grabbed her by the throat, restricting her breathing.

“He then shook her and pushed her, causing her to fall back and strike her head off a bannister.

“This was witnessed by a child.

“Ms Grady was bleeding profusely from a cut to the back of the head and the accused was shouting about blood on the carpet.

“He went with her to hospital. Staff asked her if he was responsible for her injury, but she was so scared she said she had simply fallen.

“The wound required four stitches and will likely result in scarring.”

Eventually Ms Grady confided to a work colleague that Murphy had attacked her.

She went to the police in the summer of last year.

Ms Guy added: “She said she felt in constant fear and had no way to escape. She had been terrified to tell anyone because it would make things worse.

“At one point she took an overdose but, fortunately, did not require hospital treatment.

“In March last year Ms Grady told Murphy their relationship was over. He pleaded with her, promising he would change and nothing further would happen.”

Defence agent Laura Kiernan said Murphy was “ashamed” of his behaviour, but suggested he could be spared a jail sentence.

She said: “I don’t want to take away from the serious nature of the charges, but a supervision order would allow social workers to get to the crux of his issues.

“This would benefit him and the wider community.”

However, Sheriff Thomas Millar said each of the four charges was serious enough on is own to merit a prison sentence.

He jailed Murphy for a total of 34 months.
