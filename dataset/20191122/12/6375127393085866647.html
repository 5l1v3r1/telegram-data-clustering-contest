Vapers can fight ban in court — IBP

22 Nov 2019, 12:42 by Hector Lawas

THE Integrated Bar of the Philippines yesterday said that the legality of President Duterte’s order banning the use of e-cigarettes in public, including its importation, can be challenged before the courts.

IBP national president Domingo Cayosa made the statement despite Duterte’s statement that he would not honor any court order stopping his bid to curtail vaping.

“It’s a strong position by the President. He’s requesting the courts na huwag pakialaman ‘yung kaniyang paninindigan diyan, “ Cayosa said.

“But at the end of the day, any citizen who feels aggrieved can actually go to the court,” he continued.

Recently, Duterte said that he would soon issue an executive order on the vaping ban but noted that police officers can already arrest those vaping in public on the basis of a law that prohibits the distribution of toxic materials in public.

He, however, did not name the specific law.

“I do not want anyone in the Philippines vaping. The executive order will come,” he said in a speech during the 80th anniversary of the Department of National Defense.

The President made the statement as the Philippine National Police began its crackdown on e-cigarette users.

Duterte said he banned vaping to protect the health of Filipinos.

“The e-cigarette is making it worse. There are chemicals that are added to nicotine which we do not know. And I am not about ready to allow the young people of the Philippines to get sick and to die,” he said.
