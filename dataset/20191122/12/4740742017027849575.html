First look inside the festive Après Ski Bar in Bristol city centre

The pop up attraction also includes a roller disco and street food, with more to come

22 Nov 2019, 12:16 by Robin Murray

Bristol has become very festive in recent weeks, with the German market launching in Broadmead and Christmas lights going up all over the city.

In addition, the Après Ski Bar has opened in Millennium Square, as part of multi-use attraction The Square.

Back for its fourth year, the Christmas pop up also features a roller disco run by BUMP and numerous street food vendors, with more to come including screenings on We The Curious' big screen and an immersive light installation.

The roller disco is a replacement for the ice rink which has not gone ahead this year for environmental reasons.

Visitors can choose from warming beverages such as mulled cider and wine, a selection of craft ales or cocktails, including a blood orange negroni and rhubarb ginger collins, which can all be enjoyed while wrapped in blankets supplied by the venue.

Food on offer includes Pepe Nero, who are serving mouth-watering pizzas including vegan and gluten-free options and kitchen takeover kings Le Boeuf & Lobster, who are rustling up indulgent mac & cheese, burgers and more.

Jon Ojo, Operations Manager of Pure Spaces, the team behind the attraction, said: "We are delighted to be working with We The Curious again to create Winter at the Square.

"After the success of the Bristol Beach Club and last year's Apres Bar, we're coming back bigger and better for the people of Bristol over the festive period.

"Our good friends Bump Roller Disco are back on the square to offer a more sustainable alternative to the ice rink of previous years and we have lots of great surprises in store!"

The Square will run through until January 5.

What's coming at The Square?

New for 2019, the free immersive and interactive art installation, Submergence by Squidsoup, will be joining the Apres Ski Bar, taking centre stage in Millennium Square on December 12.

The piece uses thousands of individual points of suspended light to create feelings of presence and movement within a physical space.

Designers say the work combines sound, light, physical space and virtual worlds to produce alluring and emotive headspaces.

On November 30, Big Screen Bristol will host a free screening of Harry Potter and the Philosophers Stone.

The event will be a fancy dress led event and the best dressed will receive a bar tab for the night.

For more information and to find out what else is coming up, visit The Square's website.
