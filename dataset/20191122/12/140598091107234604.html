Chocolate hazelnut salami with figs recipe

22 Nov 2019, 12:00 by Xanthe Clay

A sort of chocolate tiffin roll to slice at the end of a meal. You can vary the biscuits and the fruit as you like. This one is for Nutella lovers.

Prep time: 20 minutes, plus overnight chilling time | Cooking time: 5 minutes

MAKES

Three

INGREDIENTS

  • 100g dried figs, soaked in boiling water for 10 minutes
  • 200g plain chocolate
  • 100g unsalted butter
  • 200g crushed amaretto biscuits or shortbread
  • 100g pistachios
  • 100g ground toasted hazelnuts
  • 3 tbsp icing sugar

METHOD

  1. Drain the figs well, and chop each into four strips, getting rid of the tough stem at the same time.
  2. Melt the chocolate and butter together. Stir in the figs, biscuits or shortbread, pistachios and ground hazelnuts.
  3.  Lay out a 20 x 30cm piece of foil. Spoon a third of the mix along the long end, in a 15cm sausage. Roll tightly, twisting up the sides as you go, and patting it into a neatish cylinder, so you have a stubby salami. Repeat with the rest of the mixture to make three rolls, and refrigerate overnight.
  4.  Unroll the salamis and dust lightly with icing sugar, then rewrap in greaseproof paper. Twist the ends and tie with string. Store in a sealed container in the fridge.

Chocolate chorizo recipe

A spicy take on the classic chocolate salami, with paprika and chilli giving a grown-up kick, although you could use red and orange food colouring if you prefer. Goji berries are available from supermarkets and health food shops. Use orange juice instead of the brandy for an alcohol-free treat.

Prep time: 15 minutes, plus overnight chilling time 

MAKES

Three

INGREDIENTS

  • 10g butter
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • ½ tsp cayenne pepper
  • 100g (5 balls) stem ginger in syrup
  • 100g shortbread or amaretti biscuits
  • 200g white chocolate, chopped
  • 60g goji berries soaked overnight in 30ml brandy
  • 100g almonds
  • 1 tbsp double cream
  • 3 tbsp icing sugar

METHOD

  1. In the microwave or in a bowl over a pan of hot water, melt the butter, paprika and cayenne together. Leave to cool.
  2. Slice the ginger ½cm thick, then into batons. Crush biscuits into pea-sized bits.
  3. Add half the chocolate to the paprika butter bowl, and heat again until melted, stirring it into a smooth gloop. Stir in the goji berries, biscuits, almonds and ginger, then the double cream. Leave to cool before adding the last of the chocolate. Shape, chill and dust as for the chocolate salami above.
