Rugby rumours and transfer news: Harlequins set to beat Bath and Northampton Saints to Alex Dombrandt; Manu Tuilagi rugby league swap latest; Bristol Bears add New Zealand flanker

All the latest rugby rumours and transfer news from around the Gallagher Premiership

22 Nov 2019, 12:53 by John Evely

The new rugby season is well underway but with injuries beginning to mount up and with one eye on next season directors of rugby up and down the land are turning their attention to rugby transfer market.

With Bristol Bears in their second season back at the top table of European rugby, playing in the Gallagher Premiership and European Challenge Cup, we take a look at all the latest transfer news and rumours from them and their rivals.

Here is all the latest.

Quins win race to keep Dombrandt 

Harlequins have reportedly fought off interest from Northampton Saints and Bath Rugby to keep hold of breakout back row star Alex Dombrandt. 

The powerful 22-year-old number eight is out of contract at The Twickenham Stoop at the end of the season and big things are expected of him having been called up to train as part of England’s preliminary Rugby World Cup training squad. 

He missed the cut for the 31-man squad for Japan but there is an obvious vacancy for a back-up number eight in the England squad behind Saracens star Billy Vunipola, and Dombrandt, along with Bath’s Zach Mercer, Bristol Bears’ Nathan Hughes and Exeter Chiefs’ Sam Simmomds are all leading contenders for the job 

The Rugby Paper reports Quins have secured Dombrandt, who they signed as an amateur from Cardiff Met University, on a three year contract.

Leicester Tigers given Manu Tuilagi boost amongst rugby league rumours

England star centre Manu Tuilagi says he is planning to see out his current contract at Leicester Tigers despite a potential huge money offer to join newly promoted Super League club Toronto Wolfpack.

The 28-year-old centre, who was a key part of England's run to the World Cup final after final overcoming injury problems which have plagued much of his career, signed a new deal with Tigers in March.

Speaking about his plans, Tuilagi said: "At the moment, I don't want to look to the future too far ahead,"

Before signing a new contract with Leicester Tuilagi rejected an offer from Paris-based Top-14 side Racing 92.

"You have to focus on the now, and for me, I've two years at Tigers," Tuilagi added.

Toronto Wolfpack have confirmed they have spoken to Tuilagi's representatives and it is widely known that his agents will at least have a discussion with any club willing to make an offer.

Speaking to The Rugby Paper, Toronto coach Brian McDermott said: “I know David [Argyle, the Toronto Wolfpack owner] is talking to Manu’s people.

“He hasn’t spoken to Manu but with his representatives and it’s where it was with Sonny [Bill Williams, who has signed a two-year deal worth a reported £5m] to start with. It’s not a no.

Tuilagi reportedly discussed switching to Rugby League earlier this year, holding talks with Warrington Wolves before agreeing a new deal with Leicester, keeping him at Welford Road until 2021.

However Toronto are reportedly willing to pay off the rest of Tuilagi’s contract to immediately land the rugby union superstar.

Super League clubs are able to spend huge sums on players outside of their salary cap if they have never played professional rugby league before, which is how Wolfpack would be able to go after Tuilagi.

Sale Sharks on the hunt for second row reinforcements

Sale Sharks director of rugby Steve Diamond is looking for some midseason reinforcements in the second row after two serious injuries to key men in his squad. 

Josh Beaumont (knee) and South African World Champion Lood de Jäger have both suffered long-term injuries, with the Springbok expected to miss six months with his shoulder injury. 

Diamond is now looking to add additional recruits reports The Rugby Paper.

Bristol Bears sign kiwi flanker

Bristol Bears have signed highly rated New Zealand U20s back rower Adrian Choat as injury cover until the end of season.

With Sam Jeffries taking a break from the professional game, Choat – who is English qualified through a grandparent – makes the switch to the West Country in November.

A versatile loose forward who can play across the back row, the 21-year-old represented New Zealand U20s in the 2017 Junior World Championship and played for Auckland in the 2019 Mitre 10 Cup.

Director of Rugby Pat Lam said: “We were eager to bring in somebody of Adrian’s attitude and ability to give us cover in a key area for the season ahead.

“With Sam Jeffries unavailable to play and Jordan Crane and Sam Graham returning to fitness, Adrian [Choat] comes in to bolster our back-row options, alongside Luke Hamilton, who arrived earlier this month.

“Adrian is English qualified with a good pedigree, so we look forward to welcoming him to the Bears.”

Choat will return to Auckland at the end of Bristol Bears’ 2019/20 campaign.

Leicester Tigers boss says Ellis Genge wants to stay

England prop Ellis Genge's club future appears to still be up in the air despite the latest reassurances from Geordan Murphy

Last week The Rugby Paper reported Genge's former club Bristol Bears had made an offer for the Leicester Tigers loosehead to return to Ashton Gate next season but it fell short of expectations.

The publication reports he is close to signing a new three-year deal at Welford Road.

Genge, who now has 14 caps for England, left Bristol for Leicester Tigers midway through the 2015/16 season, following a series of problems both on the field, and off it.

However Bears director of rugby Pat Lam, who arrived after the frontrower had moved on, has made no secret of the fact that he is a big admirer of the prop and would like to bring him back to the West Country.

Speaking back in May as a guest on BT Sport's Rugby Tonight , Lam said: “Would we like Ellis Genge to come home? Of course we would.”

In the past Genge has also expressed his desire to one day return to Bristol.

Bristol currently have four senior loosehead props at the club in Samoan international brothers James, 25, and Jordan Lay, 27, Yann Thomas, 29 and Jake Woolmore, 28.

On Tuesday Leicester Tigers director of rugby Geordan Murphy confirmed contract negotiations with Genge are "ongoing" with the Midlands club keen to keep hold of one of their stand-out stars.

Speaking to the Leicester Mercury , Murphy said: "We have been in contract negotiations with Ellis since before the World Cup, as with some of the other guys who there has been huge speculation about.

"When the guys were away we stepped down our chat in order to let them focus on the World Cup.

"Ellis has come back and has been very vocal about telling us he wants to re-sign and contract negotiations are ongoing."

However the situation has been confused further by regular social media user Genge's tweet to BBC Radio Leicester journalist Adam Whitty.
