Defiant Rachel Riley hits back after calls for her to be sacked from Countdown

Pregnant Rachel Riley came under fire for sharing a doctored photo of Jeremy Corbyn being arrested, and faced calls to be removed as Countdown presenter

22 Nov 2019, 12:25 by Emmeline Saunders

Rachel Riley has sent a defiant message after coming under fire yesterday for her message about Jeremy Corbyn .

The heavily pregnant TV star faced calls to be sacked from Countdown after tweeting an image of herself wearing a t-shirt with a doctored image of the Labour leader being arrested at an anti-racism protest.

The historic photograph originally shows Mr Corbyn being led away by two police officers while protesting against apartheid outside South Africa House in London in 1984.

But his placard - which originally stated "Defend the right to protest against apartheid. Join this picket" - had been Photoshopped on Rachel's top to bear the words: "Jeremy Corbyn is a racist endeavour."

Her image sent Twitter into meltdown, with calls for her to be fired from the Channel 4 show.

But defiant Rachel has since tweeted to thank her supporters for their messages, saying: "Thanks for all the lovely messages of support, & all the silent supporters who've followed me, means a lot.

"Shows how counterproductive nasty pile-ons actually are," she went on.

"I made this promise I intend to keep it. If this is the price to raise awareness then sobeit. Lots of love."

The tweet she signposted to was published on October 29 and read: "As we edge towards a December general election , a heartfelt promise: I will do everything in my power to try to ensure my baby, Jewish by blood, will not be born under an antisemitic government. I hope this will never happen, please use your vote wisely."

It comes as the increasingly toxic election campaign goes into overdrive, with the Conservative party accused of tweeting out a 'fake' video of Labour candidate Jess Phillips talking about her party's manifesto on Good Morning Britain.

The Tories sent out the short clip yesterday morning, but doctored the kicker to remove the original date of Jess's interview - October 3 - and added "Labour manifesto to launch" to make it look like she was on the ITV show yesterday morning.

They captioned the clip: "MUST WATCH: Labour's Jess Phillips undermines Corbyn's manifesto by admitting they can't or won't deliver on their promises. With no clear plan for Brexit , Labour just can't deliver."

A slogan on the video reads: "Labour: Not sure we can deliver on our promises".

Ms Phillips condemned the video, telling the Independent: "It is totally misleading to share it in this context, but I can’t say I’m shocked because Boris Johnson and his party have shown themselves to be serial liars.

"I realise they wouldn't recognise a politician speaking honestly, so it must be difficult for them.

"Nothing that was in the Tory manifesto has been delivered, so they really don't have a leg to stand on anyway."

Mirror Online has contacted Channel 4 for comment.
