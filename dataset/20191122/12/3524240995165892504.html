Police confirm release of abducted Delta resident doctors’ president

22 Nov 2019, 12:11 by Onyekachukwu Meluwa

Onyekachukwu Meluwa – Warri.

The Delta State Police Command has confirmed the release of the President of Delta Resident Doctors at the Delta State University Teaching Hospital, in Oghara, Dr. Jerry Isogun.

PUNCH learnt that an undisclosed sum of money was said to have been paid to the hoodlums to secure Dr. Jerry Isogun’s release around 8pm on Thursday, November 21.

Sources at the hospital confirmed the development on Friday morning to newsmen that Dr Jerry has regained freedom from the hands of his kidnappers.

They said, ”The abductors reportedly dumped Dr Jerry in a bush near Eku in Ethiope East Local Government Area of the state.”

It was further learnt that the medical practitioner is correctly receiving treatment for injuries sustained during the incident at DELSUTH.

When contacted for comments, the Delta State Commissioner of Police, Mr Adeyinka Adeleke, confirmed the development on Friday.

The police boss said, “Yes, he (Isogun) has been released. He was released last night.”

Dr Jerry Isogun who is the president of the Resident Doctors of DELSUTH was reportedly kidnapped late Wednesday on his way home from work.
