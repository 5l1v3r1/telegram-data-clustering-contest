Jurgen Klopp sends message to "desperate" Jose Mourinho after Tottenham appointment

Liverpool boss Jurgen Klopp has had his say on Jose Mourinho's surprise Premier League return with Tottenham - and claimed he knew his old rival was "desperate" to return to work

22 Nov 2019, 12:13 by Rich Jones, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/rich-jones/

Liverpool boss Jurgen Klopp has welcomed Jose Mourinho back to the Premier League - and joked he knew he was desperate to get back in the dugout.

Mourinho has sealed a return to management at Tottenham , replacing Mauricio Pochettino less than 24 hours after the Argentinian was sacked.

Klopp admits the situation serves as a reminder of how quickly the game moves having beaten Spurs in the Champions League final less than six months ago.

Despite initially being shocked by the news, he has sent a message to former rival Mourinho and claimed it will be "interesting" to see how he performs in the hotseat in north London.

Speaking at his press conference ahead of Saturday's clash with Crystal Palace , Klopp said: "Welcome back Jose! It's nice to have him back.

"You could see he was desperate when he was not in (work)!

"Poch is not there anymore so it shows how quick things change. He did a brilliant job at Tottenham.

"Outstanding coach, great guy. I enjoyed the games against him, we have a history together.

"When I first heard it, I couldn't believe it in that moment. I really hope he can enjoy a few days, weeks or months off.

"Jose is motivated highly, so it will be interesting."

Whilst Klopp has called on Pochettino to enjoy some time off, speculation is already mounting over his next job.

He has long been linked with Liverpool's arch rivals, Manchester United , but current Red Devils boss Ole Gunnar Solskjaer insists he does not feel any pressure with Pochettino now on the market.

“No, it doesn't bother me at all,” said Solskjaer. “I've got the best job in the world and if you're in or out of a job, you want this job, so it doesn't really matter whatever happens around it.

“I've got to focus on my job at Manchester United and do it as well as we can.

“I speak with Ed [Woodward, United's executive vice-chairman] and the owners all the time about how we move forward, and that doesn't change if others change managers.

“It's good to have Jose back, definitely, especially for you guys and maybe for me, because you can talk and write about everything else.

“For Mauricio, it's always sad when one of your colleagues, a good man, loses his job, especially before Christmas, so I wish him all the best."
