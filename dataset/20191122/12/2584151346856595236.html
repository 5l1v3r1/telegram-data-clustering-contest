Germany’s Kramp-Karrenbauer calls to set up digital ministry

Similar ideas had been dropped because of resistance from Chancellor Angela Merkel.

22 Nov 2019, 12:56 by Matthew Karnitschnig, Janosch Delcker

LEIPZIG, Germany — Annegret Kramp-Karrenbauer, the leader of Germany's Christian Democrats, said the country should have a digital ministry to make it fit for the digital age.

“I’ve come to believe there is no way around establishing a ministry for digitalization,” she told a party summit in Leipzig.

Kramp-Karrenbauer's comments are set to revive a discussion that arose ahead of the country's general election in 2017 over whether Berlin needs a dedicated ministry to coordinate digital policy efforts.

Eventually, such ideas were dropped because of resistance from Chancellor Angela Merkel, who instead appointed a high-ranking official inside her chancellery to coordinate digital efforts.

Merkel has announced she will not run for chancellor in Germany's next general election in 2021. As CDU chief, Kramp-Karrenbauer is expected to be the party's candidate for the post instead.
