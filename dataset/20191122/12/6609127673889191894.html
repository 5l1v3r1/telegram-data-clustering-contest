OSLO FORTRESS

Scotland face formidable Norway record if they meet in Nations League play-off final

22 Nov 2019, 12:15 by Mark Walker

SCOTLAND will come up against a formidable record if they meet Norway in the Euro 2020 play-off final...they haven't lost a game at their Oslo fortress for over three years and 15 games.

Steve Clarke's side could face the Scandinavian nation if the Scots defeat Israel and Norway overcome Serbia in the other semi-final.

But Scotland would have to beat a Norway side who have proved to be unbeatable at home recently.

Their last defeat at the Ullevaal Stadion came in September 2016 and that was against Germany in a World Cup 2018 qualifier where they crashed to a 3-0 loss.

But since then, Lars Lagerback's side have been impossible to crack in their own backyard.

They've defeated San Marino, Azerbaijan, Northern Ireland, Australia, Panama, Cyprus, Slovenia, Bulgaria, Malta and the Faroe Islands.

And they've also drawn with Czech Republic, Sweden twice, Romania and Spain.

Although, as SunSport revealed yesterday, Scotland face a nightmare group of death if they make the tournament proper.

The Dark Blues could come up against England, France AND Portugal in the early stages of the major finals.
