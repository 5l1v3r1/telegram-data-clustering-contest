Celtic captain Scott Brown is only interested in his ego and not Scotland’s progress- Hotline

Gavin Berry takes your calls as Scott Brown's comments refusing to rule out a Scotland return have brought criticism.

22 Nov 2019, 12:39 by Sports Hotline, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/sports-hotline/

Scott Brown put the cat among the pigeons after refusing to rule out another Scotland comeback with the nation just 180 minutes away from qualifying for Euro 2020.

But Hotline callers have told national team boss Steve Clarke in no uncertain terms that the Celtic captain shouldn’t be allowed to return to the squad.

Brown will celebrate his 35th birthday just a couple of days after Hampden hosts its final group game at next summer’s finals and a week before a last-16 tie comes to Glasgow.

And, if Scotland can get past Israel at Hampden and Norway in Oslo next March, the former captain refused to rule out being a part of the Euro football extravaganza.

Brown earned 55 caps for his country before calling it quits for a second time under Alex McLeish in February 2018, having initially ended his international career in August 2016 only to U-turn on that decision two months later to help Gordon Strachan.

His last appearance was a 2-0 win over Malta at Hampden in the failed World Cup 2018 campaign but believes the rest during internatinal breaks has been crucial in maintaining his sparkling club form.

But James Forsyth, Bellshill, said: “Scott Brown shouldn’t be allowed to come out of internatinal retirment if we qualify for the finals.

“Where was he when we needed players and were getting thrashed?”

Kenny Wilson, Moffat, said: “I’m a big Scott Brown fan but there’s absolutely no way he should be allowed back into the Scotland squad if we qualify for the Euros.”

Alan Milligan emailed: “Scott Brown has no shame. He lets the other players do the spade work and thinks he can come back when there is a chance of a place in the finals. Unbelievable.”

George Grant, Banff, said: “So Scott Brown is considering coming out of international retirement yet again.

“He has walked way twice already and is only thinking about a return because we have a chance of reaching the finals.

“Brown didn’t want to play through the qualifying group so he shouldn’t seen to play in the rest of the games.

“He’s only interested in his ego and not the national team’s progress.

“We have managed without him up until now so we don’t need him.”

Davy Matson emailed: “So Scott Brown says he might return to the Scotland squad if we qualify for Euro 2020.

“We don’t need players who don’t want to play in the earlier games but want all the glory. Stick with the boys who get us there.”

Jim Kerr, Renfrew, said: “It would be the wrong move for Scott Brown to return to the Scotland team.

“He’s had his time and I’d love him to take the rest of the Celtic players out of the squad as well to give them a break.”

The picture is clearer for Scotland following the play-off draw in Nyon and Record Sport revealed a group containing France, Portugal and England could await if Clarke’s men are successful.

But Alfie Mullin, London, said: “Let me ease everyone’s minds by reassuring them that we won’t face that nightmare scenario. “We won’t make it through the play-offs. We haven’t beaten any team of note for years and I don’t see this changing. The only thing like to change is the manager when he fails to negotiate the play-offs successfully.”

Recent Scotland managers have found it difficult to find international class defenders and Andrew Considine has missed out on a call-up as he prepares to play his 500th game for Aberdeen against St Johnstone on Sunday.

Arthur Wyllie, Aberdeen, said: “I can’t remember Andrew Considine ever having a poor game for Aberdeen. He is so consistent and usually brilliant.

“There is only one thing missing - a Scotland cap. Why has such a dependable player never been capped for his country? He must be the best uncapped player in Scotland.

“Come on Steve Clarke, please give him at least one cap which he thoroughly deserves even if it is in some meaningless friendly.”
