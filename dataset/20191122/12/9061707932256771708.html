Burton paper girl left to walk in torrential rain as yobs steal her bike

She was doing extra newspaper deliveries because of the heavy rain that day

22 Nov 2019, 12:35 by Ben Waldron

A Burton student had her bike cruelly stolen while on her paper round - leaving her to walk for an hour in the pouring rain.

Wendy Roper-Knight has issued an appeal to the public to locate her missing bike.

The 46-year-old NHS worker's daughter, Freya was using it for her paper round in Rolleston on Thursday, November 14, because her own cycle had a flat tyre.

It came the day torrential rain flooded the area, leaving roads closed and houses wrecked.

Freya, 16, was doing three rounds, covering for colleagues who had to get to school because the rain had made the paper delivery van late.

The Burton and South Derbyshire College student had a day off and volunteered to fill in on an unfamiliar route in Hall Road, Rolleston.

Mrs Roper-Knight, who has used the bike in triathlons, including in Derby, said the bike was taken between 8am and 8.20am.

The mum of two said: "She'd left the bike resting against a house and gone to deliver some papers, but when she came back it was gone.

"We thought at first maybe she'd just forgotten where she'd left it.

"I thought maybe because of the rain someone might have thought it was abandoned and taken it in, but we've knocked around and nobody has seen anything.

"It was raining at the time - absolutely hammering it down - but even after losing the bike she still had another round to do, so was out there for another hour."

Freya has held a paper round for more than a year, but has never had an experience like this before.

Mrs Roper-Knight said: "She was drenched when she got back, absolutely soaked.

"She was a bit confused. No-one can understand why someone would take it.

"It's a small road, a lovely area. It feels so unlikely that anyone would take it; no one can believe it was stolen.

"Hopefully it still is the case that someone's just taken it in thinking it was abandoned."

Luckily, Freya is still able to do her paper round, as her own bike's tyre has now been fixed.
