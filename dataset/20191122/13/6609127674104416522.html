Mekala Osborne – Scots personal trainer who was in coma on Vietnam ‘set to return home’ after two month ordeal

22 Nov 2019, 13:52 by Jonathan Whitelaw

A SCOTS personal trainer left fighting for her life in Vietnam after falling ill is finally set to return home, it's reported.

Mekala Osborne, 22, was placed in an induced coma after developing pneumonia while travelling in South East Asia.

Mekala has spent months in intensive care in a hospital in Hoi An, Da Nang.

Mum Yvonne and stepdad Jimmy McAulay, 64, both jetted out to be at her side.

And it emerged Mekala, from Wallyford, East Lothian, was to be transported to Singapore “as a matter of urgency”.

Now it's understood that she will finally fly back to the UK this weekend, after months in hospital abroad.

The BBC reports that her condition deteriorated so badly her family was told she had a 5% chance of survival.

And that she's now been moved onto a portable ventilator and is well enough to come home.

Jimmy said: " It has taken a long time to get her well enough to travel and cope with the cabin pressure.

"When she gets back she is likely to be in intensive care for two weeks.

"She will need intense physio due to muscle wastage.
"They say it will be a year before she is back to full health."

Well-wishers have raised over £12,000 to help support Mekala's family with the costs of travel and treatment.
