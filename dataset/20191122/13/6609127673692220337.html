ROAD SMASH

Man taken to hospital after crash between school bus and car on A89 in West Lothian

22 Nov 2019, 13:18 by Justin Bowie

A MAN was taken to hospital after a crash between a school bus and a car on the A89 in West Lothian.

Emergency services raced to the two-vehicle collision this morning at around 8am after the alarm was raised.

Debris was strewn across the road with the motor involved in the smash badly damaged.

The road was also briefly locked down as emergency crews dealt with the incident.

A Scottish Ambulance Service spokesperson said: "We received a call today at 7.56am to attend an incident on West Main Street.

"We dispatched a rapid response unit and an ambulance to the scene.

"We transported one male patient in their 20s to Wishaw General.”

A pic on social media showed police at the scene as they taped it off.

But the road was later reopened.

A spokesperson said: “Police were called around 8am on Friday November 22 following reports of a two-vehicle road traffic collision involving a car and bus on the A89.”

“The road was briefly closed and has now reopened."

Scottish Fire and Rescue also confirmed they were called to the smash on West Main Street in Armadale at 8.01am this morning.

A spokesperson said: "Scottish Fire and Rescue teams were called to reports of a crash between an empty school bus and a car around 8.01am this morning.

"Scottish Fire and Rescue teams were on hand to make the vehicles safe."
