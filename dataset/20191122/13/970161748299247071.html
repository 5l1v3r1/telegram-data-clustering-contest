I’m A Celebrity’s Stanley Johnson makes dig at women for crying in jungle

22 Nov 2019, 13:42

I’m A Celebrity alum Stanley Johnson has made a huge dig at Jacqueline Jossa and Kate Garraway for getting emotional in the jungle.

During Thursday night’s instalment, the pair were seen tearing up as they discussed how much they were missing their children, four days after the show started.

And Boris Johnson’s dad is having absolutely none of it.

During an appearance on Loose Women, he told the panel: ‘I do wonder about the question of people getting teary when they’re missing their children. Women seem to do it…

‘You signed up to go off to the jungle for four or five weeks…’

When Jane Moore reminded him that he became visibly emotional when receiving a poem from his wife, he added: ‘I teared up for half a second.’

Stanley’s words come after Jacqueline broke down in tears on Thursday, admitting she was homesick and missing daughters Ella and Mia, as well as husband Dan Osborne.

Heading to the bush telegraph, she said: ‘I just woke up feeling a bit like “mmmm” when you’re just looking up at pure Jungle and you think, “wow, I’m so far away from home.”

‘The thing is, everyone is missing their families, I just feel like it’s too soon to be crying missing your family…and we all smell so bad, we just stink, I stink.

‘The smelliest I’ve ever been in my life.’

It was up to Ian Wright to comfort the EastEnders actress, reassuring her that everything was okay.

‘She misses her kids which is natural, she misses her husband, everything like that,’ he told the camera afterwards.

Kate Garraway later explained she was also missing her family, getting emotional as she could no longer ‘remember what their heads smelled like’.

They were then joined by Caitlyn Jenner, who opened up about life with her famous family.

‘My kids all call me dad, Kendall asked me first and I said, “dad”,’ she explained.

‘I’m your dad and I’ll always be your dad till the day I die.

‘But what they’re really good at which shocks me all the time is when they’re talking about me, [they say] “my dad, she”, and it’s tough to change the pronoun in the middle of it.

‘Bruce raised them and Caitlyn is enjoying their life with them.’

Loose Women continues on weekdays, at 12.30, on ITV.
