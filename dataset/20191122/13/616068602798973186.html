More cuts at Tolko

22 Nov 2019, 13:29 by Darren Handschuh

Tolko Industries announced more job losses Friday.

Castanet was sent a message from Brad Thorlakson, president and CEO of Tolko Industries, to employees stating more jobs are being cut.

“Today we announce the elimination of 44 positions within woodlands, operations and the Vernon office,” Thorlakson said in the message.

“The decision was made after a long and careful review of the options, realizing that many good people would be affected.”

Market conditions were cited as the reason for the cuts.

“It is a tough day, but we believe we have right-sized the organization,” said Thorlakson. “We may still have short-term adjustments, but long term we now have the resources to match our footprint in B.C.”

The cuts are the latest blow to the besieged forestry giant that has been struggling with high costs and a poor market.

Earlier this month, Tolko announced the closure of its Kelowna operation, putting close to 200 people out of work.

Tolko also announced slow downs at all of its BC operations Dec. 21 to Jan. 6.

Castanet was also told the CN Rail strike is making it difficult for Tolko to ship its product.

When contacted by Castanet, a spokesperson for the company refused to comment on the situation.
