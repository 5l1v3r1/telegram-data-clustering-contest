GER ON

Rangers boss Steven Gerrard willing to move fixtures to help Scotland get to Euro 2020

22 Nov 2019, 13:57 by Gary Taylor

RANGERS are willing to move fixtures in order to help Scotland's bid to reach Euro 2020.

Gers boss Steven Gerrard revealed that the Ibrox club will do whatever is necessary to aid the national side.

Steve Clarke's men learned their Nations League play-off fate this afternoon.

The Dark Blues will take on Israel at Hampden in the semi-finals.

If they come through that, then it will be a one-off away trip to either Norway or Serbia to battle it out for a spot at the major tournament.

It would be a first in 21 years if Scotland were to make it, and Gerrard is happy to help out Steve Clarke.

Speaking ahead of their clash with Hamilton, he said: "I certainly won't be standing in the way.

"I represent this club in this country so for me I give it the support I need to give it. But we don't make those decisions. Ultimately it will be down to the SFA and stuff.

"We'll go with the flow and if it means us skipping a game or adjusting a game we'll do everything we can to support (Scotland). That's what you've got to do."

It comes after ex-Kilmarnock boss Clarke has urged Scottish football's governing bodies to do all that they can to ensure the national team is helped.

Meanwhile, Stevie G admits he still has to "pinch himself" every time he thinks about how cheap Rangers bought Glen Kamara for.

The 24-year-old was initially snapped up on a pre-contract agreement last January from Dundee, along with Jordan Jones at Killie.

However, unlike with Jones, the Light Blues opted to bring Kamara in early - signing him for a mere £50,000.

Gerrard said: "We haven't heard of any interest down south, we've read tons about it though.

"But I wouldn't be surprised if there was interest in Glen Kamara because he's been outstanding since he walked in the door.

"Sometimes I still have to pinch myself for how we got him and what we got him for.

"He got a real strong contract when he came here from Dundee.

"The message for Glen and the other players is we always reward our players for consistent performances over a long period of time. Glen will be treated no different."
