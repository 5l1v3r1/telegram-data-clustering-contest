The Premier League title claim that Chelsea fans will relish ahead of Man City clash

Man City host Chelsea on Saturday evening in the Premier League

22 Nov 2019, 20:00 by Paul Clarke

Former Chelsea defender Frank Sinclair has made a Premier League title related claim about the clash between Frank Lampard's men and Manchester City.

Pep Guardiola's side host Chelsea on Saturday in battle between two clubs comfortably in the top four.

But so much more was expected of two-time champions City, who trail league leaders Liverpool by nine points after losing 3-1 at Anfield earlier this month.

Chelsea, however, have surprised many by seamlessly dealing with Frank Lampard's debut season and a transfer ban, with the Londoners having won their last six league games.

Sinclair, however, feels the true test of Lampard's team will come at the Etihad Stadium and he says only if they successfully pass the challenge will they be truly in a title race.

"Many people say that Chelsea have not really been tested this season. When they have played against one of the so called big clubs they struggled. They lost 4-0 at Manchester United," Sinclair, who was speaking on behalf of GentingBet, told football.london.

"The question that needs to be answered is whether they can step it up against one of the top sides. We should learn what they are about.

"There will be some small margins in the game. It will not be easy but if they do win this game, they can look at challenging for the title.

"The pressure is on Man City after their match in the Premier League when they went down at Liverpool.

"Chelsea have got an opportunity to go to Man City when it is really good to play them and go for a win.

"We have not been able to say that confidently over the last couple of seasons that Chelsea can go to Man City and get a result. The way they're playing, they can certainly do that and I think Lampard's side will win 2-1."

Only Arsenal inside the top nine teams in the Premier League have conceded as many goals as Chelsea, though the Blues' defence has improved of late.

Meanwhile, City have scored six more goals than any other top-tier team, with Sinclair well aware of the challenge his old side face.
