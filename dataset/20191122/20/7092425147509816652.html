Donald Trump on impeachment: 'I want a trial'

22 Nov 2019, 20:53 by James Cox

US President Donald Trump's latest comments on the impeachment inquiry were typically bullish as he claimed he'd welcome a trial.

"Frankly, I want a trial," Trump told the Fox and Friends programme.

However, he doesn't think the House of Representatives will impeach him.

"I think it's very hard for them to impeach you if they have absolutely nothing," Trump said.

His comments follow two weeks of public hearings with 12 witnesses involved in the impeachment inquiry.

The Democratic-led House is investigating whether Trump abused the power of office by pushing Ukraine to investigate a political rival.

The trial is centred on a phone call between Trump and Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelensky on July 25.

Trump asked his counterpart to launch an investigation into former US Vice President Joe Biden and his son, Hunter, who had served on the board of a Ukrainian gas company.

Biden is a 2020 Democratic presidential frontrunner.

Testimony revealed that the administration was also pushing for an investigation into a debunked conspiracy theory that it was Ukraine, not Russia, that meddled in the 2016 US presidential election.

Fiona Hill, a former Russia expert in the White House, debunked the Ukrainian theory today.
