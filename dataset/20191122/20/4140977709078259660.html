Whilst the ongoing edition of the Women’s Big Bash League has engrossed and exhilarated us in enthralling and intriguing fixtures, the focus shifts to the younger pile of players partaking in the league. Starting today, Lanning XII and Perry XII will be going at loggerheads in the Australian U19 Women’ Big Bash League to not only get some much needed time out on the field but also test their calibre infront of worthy players.

Initially conceptualised to spur and spruce women’s cricket in the domestic fraternity of the country, the league makes an onset with not only giving the upbringing players from the grassroots level a platform to showcase their credentials but also a chance to identity players for the next stage. Separated into two outfits under the tutelage of Australia’s worldclass players Meg Lanning and Elyse Perry, this league promises to be an enterprising and engrossing one as players from Australia’s arena look to stamp their authority with enigmatic and quirky displays.

Opening day of the league will see two matches being played inside the place of 8 hours as the two sides partake in a hectic and congested schedule.
