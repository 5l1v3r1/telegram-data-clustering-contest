Biggest I'm A Celebrity success stories - and those who flopped after the jungle

This year's crop of stars have now arrived in the jungle, where they're being served up gruesome 'treats' and facing horrifying challenges

22 Nov 2019, 20:30 by Poppy Danby, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/poppy-danby/

I'm A Celebrity is one of the most perilous reality TV shows on our screens, with celebs pitting their wits against creepy crawlies, treacherous tasks and unsavoury snacks live from Australia.

But after our favourite stars have fought it out to become King or Queen of the jungle, their careers can take a dramatic turn in the real world.

Now, the latest crop of stars have made their way into the jungle we take a look at the biggest I'm A Celeb success stories and those who didn't fare so well, when they got out of there...

Stacey Solomon (2010)

Stacey is one of the most successful stars to have come out of the I'm A Celeb jungle.

The former X Factor singer won the show in 2010, with a huge 80 per cent of the vote.

And she's continued to woo the public with a number of lucrative deals. For example, not only is she now a regular on the Loose Women panel but she's also had massive collaborations with brands such as Iceland and Primark.

On top of this, Stacey also started dating fellow King of the Jungle, Joe Swash, and the pair now have baby son, Rex, together.

Vicky Pattinson (2015)

Vicky is no stranger to reality TV after first hitting our screens in Georgie Shore from 2011 to 2014 and Ex On The Beach in 2015.

But her stint on I'm a Celebrity made her a household name.

Similarly to Stacey, the brunette stunner had a regular place as a panelist on Loose Women.

And she has also written two novels and an autobiography called Nothing But The Truth.

She also worked on a campaign with lingerie brand, Ann Summers.

However, Vicky's career took a blow when she was hired to present I'm A Celeb spin-off show, Extra Camp in 2016, but was swiftly fired after being ridiculed for her presenting skills.

She was then also fired from co-hosting Blind Date before it even aired.

Vicky seems to have bounced back with her own reality show and is now dating the man of her dreams.

Scarlett Moffatt (2016)

Scarlett's career seems to have gone from strength to strength since she won I'm A Celeb in 2016.

And it's been reported that her earning are now over £1million.

Since leaving the jungle, Scarlett has presented a number of show, including Ant & Dec's Saturday Night Takeaway and co-hosting the National Television Awards.

And, aside from her lucrative TV work, she also released her own exercise DVD and a book.

Scarlett has recently starred in controversial reality TV show, The British Tribe Next Door – which saw her and her family spend a month in a near-exact replica of their County Durham home amid the mud huts of the Himba tribe in Namibia.

Gino D'ACampo (2009)

Following his stint on I'm A Celeb, Gino landed a regular cooking spot on This Morning and he even launched his first day-time cookery show, Let's Do Lunch With Gino and Mel in 2011 to 2014.

He's since worked on numerous cookery programmes and books.

And, in 2013, he also launched his own chain of Italian restaurants across the UK.

Georgia Toffolo (2017)

Georgia was the celebrity who was paid the least for her appearance on the show in 2017.

However, she arguably came out of it with the most and was tipped to earn £5million after leaving the jungle.

This is mainly down to her TV work – such as her reporting work for This Morning and coverage from backstage at the National Television Awards.

She also starred as Mrs Toffee Apple in The Grinch in 2018.

Toff has also remained close friends with fellow jungle campmate, Stanley Johnson, and the pair competed together in Celebrity Hunted for Stand Up To Cancer, in October this year.

Phil Tufnell (2003)

Life in the I'm A Celeb jungle kickstarted England cricketer Phil's reality TV career after he was crowned King of the Jungle in 2003.

The 53-year-old went on to compete in a range of shows from Strictly Come Dancing, in 2009 to The Jump in 2015.

He has also enjoyed a long career as a team captain on A Question of Sport.

Katie Price (2004)

Katie first met Peter Andre in the jungle in 2004 and their flirtatious antics were the talk of the nation.

Shortly after the show ended, the pair married in 2005 and had two children Junior and Princess together.

The pair split up in 2009 but this wasn't the end of Katie's I'm A Celeb journey, as the glamour model returned to the jungle later that year to get “closure” on her dramatic breakup with Peter.

However, she left after just nine days, claiming: “It's not about the money, it's not about winning for me – I just don't want to be here any more.”

And even more drama followed, as Katie, 41, famously dumped her second husband, Alex Reid, live on air during her exit chat from the show.

He gave her another chance but, needless to say, their relationship didn't last much longer and they parted in 2012.

Tony Blackburn (2002)

Radio DJ Tony Blackburn became the first ever winner of I'm A Celeb back in 2002.

He was abruptly sacked by the BBC in February 2016 following a sex abuse probe.

Tony was later cleared of any wrongdoing and reinstated by the BBC by October that year.

Kerry Katona (2004)

Atomic Kitten's Kerry Katona was the winner of series three in 2004 and was the first woman to be crowned Queen of the Jungle.

But despite her success on the show Kerry's personal life has suffered a number of ups and downs since coming out of the jungle – including financial woes and problems with her love life.

She has featured on other reality TV shows, including Dancing On Ice and Celebrity Big Brother since leaving the jungle and is often founding voicing her opinions on Loose Women.

Carol Thatcher (2005)

Journalist, Carol, proved that she was just as tough as her mother (the late Prime Minister, Margaret Thatcher) when she defeated the jungle tasks and was crowned queen in 2005.

She enjoyed four years of TV stardom – appearing on Most Haunted and presenting a documentary about that Faulkland Wars.

She also had a stint as a correspondent on The One show but was reportedly fired for making a racist remark.

She's since retired from the media spotlight.

Matt Willis (2006)

Matt entered the show at a time when his career was waining – Busted had split up and his solo career had struggled to take off.

However, he proved a big hit with viewers and was crowned King of The Jungle in 2006 and his career got straight back on the right path.

Not only did Busted collaborate with McFly to become McBusted but the original band also reunited in their own right.

Meanwhile, Matt also tried his hand at acting on stage in Wicked, and on TV in Birds of a Feather and EastEnders.

Christopher Biggins (2007)

Initially, the jungle sparked a career revival for Biggins when he won the show in 2007.

However, in 2016, he hit the rocks after a disastrous appearance on Celebrity Big Brother – where he upset viewers by making derogatory comments about bisexuals and was kicked out after he was accused of making anti-semitic and pro-nazi comments.

Last year he received the Lifetime Achievement Award at the Great British Pantomime Awards.
