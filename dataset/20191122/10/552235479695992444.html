Thousands of Scots left without money after TSB glitch fails to pay wages

Customers have taken to social media to claim they have not been paid - with earnings, benefits, pensions and other payments still outstanding.

22 Nov 2019, 10:10 by Dailyrecord.co.uk

Thousands of Scots have been left without access to their wages after payments failed to go through.

Customers have taken to social media to claim they have not been paid - with earnings, benefits, pensions and other payments still outstanding, the Mirror reports.

The glitch is affecting TSB customers with the bank saying it is experiencing some 'delays' to payments.

The bank said a "small number" of customers failed to receive payments due in on Friday, and it has been forced to offer emergency cash to those affected.

Customers who may have incurred extra costs due to the late payments have to keep receipts and can claim once the issue has been resolved.

 

A TSB statement reads: "Some payments into TSB accounts have been delayed overnight and we are working to process these as soon as possible today.

"We apologise for the inconvenience this has caused and will ensure customers are not left out of pocket."

If you've incurred any extra costs due to today's outage, keep hold of any receipts, call logs and invoices which you can use to make a claim once the issue has been resolved.

"If TSB customers need emergency cash or have any questions they can visit us in branch or contact us via the number on the back of their card," a statement added.

"Customers can still use their cards to make payments or withdraw cash."

Reader Kerry Louise said she's been unable to pay for a deposit on a flat this morning, waking up to "nothing" on the day her £900 payment is due.

 

"I was expecting my wages this morning and woke up with nothing. I have an appointment before work to put a deposit down for a flat and now cannot do this.

"TSB have ruined everything. I am heartbroken. There's been no explanation, no time limit or anything. This is happening too often and I will be changing banks after this. I cannot believe it," the customer explained.
