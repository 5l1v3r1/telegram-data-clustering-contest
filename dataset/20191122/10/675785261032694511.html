Murdered Brit doctor and fiancée 'found with throats slashed and hands cuffed'

Dr Richard Field, 49, and Dr Lina Bolanos, 38, were stabbed to death by former doorman Bampumim Teixeira at their luxury penthouse in Boston, Massachusetts, say prosecutors

22 Nov 2019, 10:36 by Chris Kitching, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/chris-kitching/

A British doctor and his fiancée were found with their throats slashed and their hands cuffed behind their backs after being murdered by a former doorman, a court heard.

Police shot and wounded suspect Bampumim Teixeira as they discovered the bodies of Dr Richard Field, 49, and Dr Lina Bolanos, 38, inside their luxury penthouse.

Teixeira, 32, is accused of murdering the couple, who worked as anaesthesiologists, in a planned burglary at their home in the US city of Boston, Massachusetts, on May 5, 2017.

Jurors were told that Teixeira lurked outside the Macallen Building for more than two hours until he seized on an opportunity to make it past security without being noticed.

 

Opening the case on Thursday, John Pappas, prosecuting, told the court that Teixeira followed a car into the building's garage just before 4pm and then went to the couple's penthouse on the 11th floor through an unlocked stairway.

Dr Bolanos arrived home just under an hour later and was ambushed at her front door, the Boston Globe reported.

Her partner was stabbed to death after he arrived home about 90 minutes later while Teixeira was still inside, Mr Pappas said.

With members of the victims' families sitting in the public gallery, the court heard that text messages were sent from Dr Field's mobile phone urging a friend to call 911 because a gunman was in his home.

A number of 911 calls were attempted from the phone as well.

Dr Field's friend didn't see the messages immediately, but he eventually raised the alarm with the police and the building's concierge.

Officers found two packages and a set of keys on the floor in front of the door.

When they used the keys to open the door, Teixeira emerged and was shot in the leg as one of the officers shouted "gun".

Teixeira, who worked at the building for several weeks in 2016, was in possession of a duffel bag filled with Dr Bolanos' stolen jewellery and belongings, the court heard.

Fake guns, knives, pliers, handcuffs, duct tape and a mask - all allegedly belonging to Teixeira - were found in a second bag.

Dr Bolanos, a Harvard anaesthesia instructor originally from Colombia, was found dead with stab wounds to the neck in one of the bedrooms.

Dr Field was found dead in a pool of his own blood in a second bedroom.

Pointing at Teixeira, Mr Pappas told the jury: “These murders were committed by one person acting alone, one person with a purpose and plan.

“Although the why to this story may never fully be explained to your satisfaction, the who will absolutely never be in doubt.”

In his opening speech, Steven Sack, defending, told the court that there was no credible or scientific evidence to prove that his client broke into the home.

Mr Sack said Teixeira was not armed with a gun when he was shot at seven times by police.

Jurors will visit the murder scene in South Boston during the trial.

Teixeira, originally from Guinea-Bissau in West Africa, denies first-degree murder, armed robbery, armed home invasion and kidnapping.

He was formally charged in his hospital room as he recovered from his gunshot injuries.

The murder suspect avoided deportation a year before the double murder when he struck a plea deal on a bank robbery charge.

The trial continues.
