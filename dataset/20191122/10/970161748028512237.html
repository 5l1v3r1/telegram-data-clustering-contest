Man's sinister threats to wife after being jailed for saying he'd kill her

22 Nov 2019, 10:11

A prisoner jailed for making death threats against his wife has been given more time behind bars after admitting he’d cut her throat if he met her again.

Stephen Kennedy, 58, said he would kill his wife if he ever saw her, adding that he would be willing to ‘do life’ for the crime, Teesside Crown Court heard.

Kennedy was already serving an 18-month sentence, along with a five year restraining order, for making death threats against his former partner.

When a probation officer came to visit him at Holme House Prison on July 1 to discuss the terms of his release, he was asked how he felt about his wife.

He replied saying: ‘If I saw her, I would kill her’.

Kennedy went on to say he wanted ‘revenge’, adding ‘I would do life’ and ‘I would just cut her throat’, prosecutor Paul Newcombe said.

He said the threats were genuine, he felt angry, and it would not bother him to return to prison.

When his comments were reported to police, he confessed, adding that his 25-year relationship was over and mentioning a dispute over money.

The prisoner said he thought his wife would be ‘gobsmacked’ to hear of his comments.

He had been prescribed medication for mental health and anger problems, but had not been taking them in prison.

His wife was ‘concerned’ about the threats, the court was told.

Kennedy was jailed for 20 months after admitting making threats to kill, an offence that carries a maximum of 10 years’ imprisonment.

A pre-sentence report raised the concern that he might be a dangerous offender, which could have led to an extended sentence.

Kelleigh Lodge, defending, said his client had been referred to a psychiatrist in prison and was working with the mental health charity Mind.

He had previously been diagnosed with psychosis and was said to suffer from mild depression.

Assessments were said to be ‘ongoing’.

The case had been postponed for three months for the Crown to try to speak to the victim about the effects the death threats had had on her.

However, inquiries had not led to anything and Judge Deborah Sherwin had decided Kennedy should be allowed to learn his fate for his ‘serious and nasty’ offence.

She said: ‘He’s been left festering away in prison… Things can’t go on forever.

‘I have to regard this case as being a case where she’s been caused some distress by the fact that you’ve made further threats to her.

‘The best mitigation you have is that you pleaded guilty to these matters at the first opportunity.’
