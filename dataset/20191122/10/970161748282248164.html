How to register to vote in the 2019 General Election

22 Nov 2019, 10:45

It’s déjà vu all over again as we ready ourselves for the third General Election in four years.

Prime Minister Boris Johnson previously said he wanted the vote – which is scheduled for 12 December – to take place because there’s a need for a ‘new and revitalised’ Parliament to see Brexit through.

As the election approaches, so too does the deadline to register to vote in time to cast your ballot.

How to register to vote in the upcoming election

You’ll need to register to vote if you are a first-time voter or if you have moved house since the last time you voted.

Registering to vote takes minutes and all you need to do is go to the Government’s website.

You must be over 16 to register to vote, but you won’t actually be able to vote until you turn 18. You must also be either a UK citizen or an Irish, Commonwealth or EU citizen with a permanent address in the UK.
