Indonesia plans to cut corporate tax rate in stages to reach 20% starting 2023 — finmin

22 Nov 2019, 10:50 by Reuters

JAKARTA (Nov 22): Indonesia's finance minister said on Friday the government would submit to parliament a bill on tax reform next month, proposing to cut the corporate tax rate to 22% in 2021-2022 and 20% starting 2023, from 25% currently.

Sri Mulyani Indrawati told reporters the corporate tax rate for publicly listed companies meeting certain criteria would also be trimmed to 19% in 2021-2022 and 17% from 2023. Companies with at least 40% of their shares sold to the public in the Indonesia Stock Exchange currently pay a 20% tax on profits.
