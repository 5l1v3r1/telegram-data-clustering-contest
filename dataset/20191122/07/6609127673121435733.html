SHANKED IT

Dundee United ace Lawrence Shankland ‘facing weeks out’ with injury

22 Nov 2019, 07:59 by Ewan Paton

DUNDEE UNITED star Lawrence Shankland faces weeks out injured, according to reports.

The Arabs hitman has been in sensational form at club level this season, following on from two great seasons at Ayr.

The striker also made his Scotland debut against Russia last month, and even scored versus San Marino to bag his first for his country.

However, according to the Daily Record, the 24-year-old is now set for a spell on the sidelines.

They say that he's torn a thigh muscle while away on Scotland duty with Steve Clarke's men.

It's a huge blow for Robbie Neilson at United, with the bagsman netting 17 times already this term.

He picked up the injury during the warm-up ahead of Saturday's 2-1 win over Cyprus.

Scans have now revealed that the attacker has suffered a small tear on his upper leg.

Shankland subsequently pulled out of the squad and was replaced by Kilmarnock's Eamonn Brophy.

It is believed he will be out for at least two weeks, depending on how treatment goes for the injury.

It means he will miss games against Morton and Alloa.

The good thing for United head coach Robbie Neilson is that his team managed to see off Queen of the South without Shankland at the weekend.

Meanwhile, Scott Brown has refused to rule out a sensational Scotland return at the Euros.

The Celtic skipper quit the international scene for a second time in February 2018.

But Brown, 34, has left the door open to pulling on the national shirt again if Steve Clarke’s side make it through March’s play-offs.

Asked if Clarke was to sound him out, Brown said: “They still have two games to get through but you never say never.

“It would be phenomenal to get to a finals after 22 years. Football’s a funny game and you never know.

“I’m in a good place fitness-wise in terms of my body and I make sure I keep myself ticking over."
