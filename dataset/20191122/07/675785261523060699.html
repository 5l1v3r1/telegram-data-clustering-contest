Jeremy Corbyn and Piers Morgan agree on something - you have to register to vote

Millions of young people are still missing from the electoral register - and everyone from Jeremy Corbyn to Piers Morgan is demanding you sign up before the midnight deadline on Tuesday 26 November

22 Nov 2019, 07:27 by Jason Beattie, https://www.facebook.com/Jason-Beattie-148163672552769/

Millions could miss out on the chance to shape the country’s future unless they register to vote – and the majority are young people with 33% of them not signed up.

These young voters could prove key to deciding the outcome of the election.

As many as nine million people in all have yet to register or are registered at the wrong address, the elections watchdog has warned.

Regardless of party loyalty, politicians, TV stars and celebrities called for people to make sure they are on the electoral roll before the November 26 deadline.

Labour leader Jeremy Corbyn said ahead of today’s National Voter Registration Day: “Register to vote. And if you are registered get your friends and family registered too.”

 

   

Since the election was called more than two million people have applied for a vote. But the Electoral Commission says between 8.3million and 9.4million people – 17% of the electorate – have not registered or are not registered at their current address.

TV presenter Piers Morgan said: “It is vitally important everyone exercises their right to vote. If you don’t, you can’t complain if you get the Government you didn’t want. So register, then get out and vote.”

X Factor and Love Island’s No Love Lost added: “It’s so important to make your voice heard for the future.”

Comedian Jason Manford said: “This is a great campaign by the Mirror. Register to vote, you only have until November 26. Do it today.”

 

   

Operation Black Vote said one in four black and minority ethnic people are not registered – four times higher than the national average. Only 74% of those aged 25-34 are correctly registered, falling to 68% among those aged 20-24 and 66% among those aged 18-19.

By contrast 94% of the over 65s have registered.

Operation Black Vote co-founder, Ashok Viswanathan said: “If we want our politicians to look like us and talk like us, we need to use our voice.”

Craig Westwood of the Electoral Commission said: “It only takes five minutes to register to vote online – time that you might otherwise spend waiting for the kettle to boil or for a gym class to start.”

Electoral Commission figures also reveal that 91% of home owners are registered to vote compared with 58% of people in rental accommodation.

A study by the BBC found non-voters could have changed the result in 551 of the 650 Westminster constituencies if they had taken part in the 2017 election.

This includes Prime Minister Boris Johnson ’s Uxbridge seat where the number of non-voters, 22,798, was more than four times his majority of 5,034.

Darren Hughes of the Electoral Reform Society said the missing millions of voters “makes our democracy unfair and we need to put that right through moving to universal voter registration.”
