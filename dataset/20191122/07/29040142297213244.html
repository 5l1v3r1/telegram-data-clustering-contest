Samsung Galaxy A71 renders show hole-punch design, quad camera setup

The Galaxy A71 is likely to be launched sometime early next year.

22 Nov 2019, 07:23 by Babu Mohan

What you need to know

  • Popular leaker @OnLeaks has collaborated with CashKaro to release the first CAD-based renders of the Samsung Galaxy A71.
  • The renders reveal an Infinity-O display and an L-shaped quad camera array on the back.
  • It is rumored that the upcoming mid-range smartphone will sport a 6.7-inch FHD+ display and Qualcomm's Snapdragon 675 chipset.

Earlier this month, we got our first look at Samsung's upcoming Galaxy A51 mid-range smartphone, courtesy of leaker @OnLeaks. The reliable leaker has now teamed up with CashKaro to give us our first clear look at another upcoming A-series smartphone from Samsung, the Galaxy A71.

The renders suggest the Galaxy A71 will not be very different from the Galaxy A51 in terms of design. It will have a single hole-punch cutout at the top of the display, similar to the Galaxy Note 10 series. The phone will also have minimal bezels on all sides.

On the back of the phone, we see an L-shaped quad camera array with an LED flash. If rumors are to be believed, the Galaxy A71 will come with a 48MP primary sensor, a 12MP ultrawide lens, 12MP telephoto lens, and a ToF sensor for portrait shots. The hole-punch cutout on the front of the phone is tipped to house a 32MP selfie camera.
