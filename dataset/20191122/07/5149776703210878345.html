Club will 'listen to offers' for Nottingham Forest reported target as Leeds star is chased by Liverpool and Man United - Championship rumours

With just over a month until the January window opens we will keep you up to date with all of the latest rumours coming from Championship

22 Nov 2019, 07:27 by Ben Husband

The January transfer window may still feel like a few months away but it will soon be here as teams look to add to their squads again.

Trading will resume in the New Year with plenty of major transfers expected as Championship sides look to prepare for the business end of the season.

And below is the latest gossip from around the division.

Gayle playing the waiting game

Newcastle United will “listen to offers” for reported Nottingham Forest target Dwight Gayle in January.

The Reds were said to be keen on the striker in the summer, and it has been claimed they could reignite their interest when the window opens in a few weeks.

Gayle has featured just twice for the Magpies under Steve Bruce this season, coming off the bench against both Chelsea and Wolves last month.

And though boss Bruce has previously said the 29-year-old will get his chance, it has been suggested United will be willing to let him leave midway through the campaign.

Chronicle Live report: “They’ll be listening to offers for Dwight Gayle - although don’t expect that to be advertised publicly - and if he leaves, a replacement would be possible.”

In the race

Lawrence Shankland continues to be linked with a potential January move to Nottingham Forest.

The Scotland international striker is reportedly interesting several clubs ahead of the transfer window reopening in the new year, including Celtic and Rangers.

A remarkable 17 goals in 13 Scottish Championship appearances for Dundee United so far this season has caught the eye - and Forest could yet consider a January bid for the 24-year-old, following reported summer interest from the City Ground.

Putting the rumours to rest

Joe Worrall has been a key player for Lamouchi this season, following his loan spell at Rangers last term.

The 22-year-old was the subject of some transfer speculation in the summer, with the Reds understood to have turned down more than one bid.

Hull City and Wigan Athletic were both believed to have shown an interest, though it is unclear whether either club were involved in making an offer.

Worrall, however, has made clear his love for Forest as they prepare to return to action after the international break with a trip to Bristol City on Saturday.

“Of course there’s always speculation. You see it all the time on Twitter,” said the Nigel Doughty Academy product.

“But at the end of the day I’m playing for Forest - the team I love. I love wearing the red shirt.

“It’s something that I’m focused on for the next game.

Elsewhere in the division (via The Mirror)

White in demand

Leeds defender Ben White has attracted the interest of both Liverpool and Manchester United after impressing on loan at Elland Road.

The 19-year-old moved north from Brighton for the season in July and has become an integral part of Marcelo Bielsa's defence.

So much so that two of the Premier League big boys are weighing up a move reports the Athletic .

Liverpool have a real interest in the central defender while United have scouted White as they continue their recruitment policy that places an emphasis on young homegrown talent.

Clarets out of the race for Phillips

Burnley are not expected to reignite their interest in Leeds United midfielder Kalvin Phillips.

Sean Dyche’s side were strongly linked with a move for the Leeds academy graduate and were believed to have made a bid for him.

A number of top flight clubs were linked with the 23-year-old, with Aston Villa, Wolves and Bournemouth all credited with an interest.

Phillips did sign a new deal at Elland Road in the summer in order to fend off interest and that looks to have done the trick at Turf Moor, with reports now claiming they won’t try and sign him in January.
