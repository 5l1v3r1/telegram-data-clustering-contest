Mompha Sues EFCC, Demands N5 Million Compensation

22 Nov 2019, 07:40 by Oyindamola Olubajo

Instagram big boy and Bureau De’ Change operator, Ismaila Mustapha aka Mompha has filed a suit against anti-graft agency, Economic and Financial Crimes Commission (EFCC) alleging unlawful detention and violation of his rights.

Concise News reports that EFCC arrested Mompha on October 19, in Abuja, over alleged cyber-crime and money laundering.

Mompha has now filed a fundamental rights enforcement suit, asking the court to award N5m against the EFCC for detaining him beyond the constitutionally-allowed period without being charged to court.

He prayed the court to order the EFCC to either immediately charge him to court or release him if it was not ready to charge him to court.

The Instagram big boy’s lawyer, Mr Gboyega Oyewole (SAN), says his client, as a Nigerian, was entitled to the protection of his fundamental rights under sections 34, 35 and 36 of the 1999 constitution.

Meanwhile, the acting chairman EFFC, Ibrahim Magu had described the arrest of the Bureau De Change operator as a testimony in fight against internet related fraud.

“Investigation has also revealed that Mompha operates 51 bank accounts in Nigeria with which he acquires properties in Dubai and had laundered about N14billion through a firm known as Ismalob Global Investments Limited.” he said

Mompha, is renowned for his luxurious lifestyle which is well portrayed in his flagrant display of expensive cars, wristwatches, and bewildering landscapes and properties on Instagram.
