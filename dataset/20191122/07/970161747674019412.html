Bus driver attacked by man armed with a bike and a piece of perspex

22 Nov 2019, 07:11

A manhunt is underway after a bus driver was attacked by a passenger following an argument.

The attacker flew into a rage and ripped off a piece of perspex from the driver’s booth.

He then made a stabbing motion with it, before throwing it at the booth and continued to hurl abuse.

He then left the bus and picked up a bike that used it as a battering ram on the number 48 bus in Bishopsgate, City of London.

The incident happened at around 3.15pm on August 19 and police have just released footage to try and track down the aggressor.

PC Sam Lowe said: ‘This was a terrifying and violent incident which happened in the middle of the day, with many witnesses around.

‘Thankfully, the bus driver was physically unhurt, but it was obviously traumatic for someone to be subjected to this level of abuse and violence while at work.

‘We would encourage anyone who saw this incident, or who has information about the identity of this dangerous individual, to get in touch with us.’

Anyone with information is being urged to contact City of London Police.
