Rajasthan: Lady sarpanch climbs on JCB machine to stop demolition. Watch crazy video

In Jalore, Rajasthan, a lady sarpanch clung tenaciously to a JCB machine to oppose an anti-encroachment drive. It goes without saying that you shouldn't try this at home. Video below. Read on.

22 Nov 2019, 09:05

HIGHLIGHTS

  • Sarpanch attempts to stop anti-encroachment drive
  • Hangs on to JCB machine, ignoring cries of alarm
  • Incident took place on Thursday, November 21

Rekha Devi hung tight when the loader's bright, yellow arm jerked as if to goad her into disembarking.

She clung on -- drawing her legs closer for strength, ignoring the cries piercing the air-- as the machine retreated across the unpaved ground, its driver steering frantically.

She still had a hand on the loader when it rose to seek refuge in height. A turbaned villager who had stayed behind her wisely helped her down.

This was an anti-encroachment drive: one about which Rekha Devi, the sarpach of Mandawala village (Jalore) in Rajasthan, obviously had strong feelings.

Back on terra firma, she was still pointing in the direction of the JCB machine as it fell back further.

Then it suddenly braked. Its front end rose and crashed with a thunderous peal, almost sounding furious for having been thwarted.
