HELP FIND HER

Mystery surrounding late night sighting of ‘frail’ Dundee pensioner in freezing temperatures as police try to trace her

22 Nov 2019, 09:35 by Justin Bowie

COPS are trying to trace a “frail” pensioner who was sighted alone late last night in Dundee.

Police say the vulnerable woman, believed to be aged between 70-80, was spotted at around 10pm last night.

She was seen in the area of Strathdon Place in Broughty Ferry, walking in the direction of Dawson Road.

Police say they are “concerned” for her as she was wearing a jumper described as not being appropriate for the cold weather.

And they have now made an appeal to the public in a bid to find her.

Inspector Ahmad said: “We are concerned for the female as she was described as approximately 70 to 80 years old, frail, and wearing a light purple jumper which was not appropriate for the weather conditions.

“If anyone saw the female at the time, or knows who she is, please contact Police quoting incident 3914-on November 21, 2019.”

We told yesterday how a desperate search was launched for two young brothers who disappeared after being dropped off at school in Midlothian.

But Michael O'Brien, 9, and brother Jamie, 6, were traced safe and well.

And police also traced a pensioner who went missing in Dumfries and Galloway.

Thomas McKie, 71, had disappeared from Newton Stewart.

But cops later confirmed that he was found safe and well.
