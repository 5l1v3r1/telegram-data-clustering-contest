Dido is coming to Arena Birmingham - here's how to get tickets

Her date at Arena Birmingham will be opening night of UK tour

22 Nov 2019, 09:39 by Graham Young, David Bentley, https://www.facebook.com/grahamyoungfilm?ref=hl, https://www.facebook.com/davidbentleybm/

Oscar-nominated singer-songwriter Dido has announced a Birmingham concert date.

The news comes just as the No Angel star releases her new album, Still on my Mind.

Last year her first tour for 15 years was announced but included just three UK dates.

But a new gig at the Arena Birmingham - formerly the Barclaycard Arena and before that the National Indoor Arena - has now been confirmed for Thursday, November 28, 2019.

And it will be the opening night of a seven-date UK tour.

The London-born star - real name Florian Cloud de Bounevialle Armstrong but known as Dido since childhood - burst on to the scene with her debut album No Angel (1999), which has sold more than 21 million copies.

Follow-up album Life for Rent (2003) included the singles White Flag and Life for Rent.

Other albums followed - Safe Trip Home (2008) and Girl Who Got Away (2013).

Her song Thank You was sampled on Eminem's No 1 single Stan, which brought her to the attention of US audiences.

She has also been a regular contributor to Faithless, an electronica band whose members include her brother, Rollo.

Her song If I Rise, performed with co-writer A R Rahman, was Oscar-nominated after featuring in the Danny Boyle film, 127 Hours - the category was won by Randy Newman's We Belong Together from Toy Story 3.

When will tickets go on sale?

The Arena Birmingham date on Thursday, November 28 is the first of seven UK dates in the autumn.
