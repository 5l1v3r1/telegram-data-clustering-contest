Tesla unveils bold design for ‘Cybertruck’

Elon Musk hopes Bond-inspired electric vehicle will be a hit with pick-up drivers

22 Nov 2019, 09:01 by Richard Waters

Elon Musk has taken the wraps off a new vehicle designed to put a dent in one of Detroit’s most profitable markets, unveiling an angular wedge-shaped Tesla electric pick-up truck.

Called the Cybertruck, the latest model from Tesla takes aim at a market currently dominated by Ford and General Motors and accounting for the three best selling vehicles in the US this year.

Mr Musk surprised an invited audience at Tesla’s design studio in Los Angeles on Thursday evening by disclosing an entry price for the hotly anticipated vehicle that was $10,000 below expectations, with three versions that have base prices ranging from $39,900-$69,000.

This marks a departure from the company’s normal tactic of breaking into a market with high-priced models and racing to force prices down as production volumes rise. But putting it within reach of most pick-up truck buyers also raises the spectre of renewed pressure on margins, at a time when Tesla is enjoying a rare respite from Wall Street unhappiness over flagging profitability.

The pick-up market presents a new test of Mr Musk’s ability to generate buzz and break the mould. With its entrenched brands, conservative buyers and a high proportion of fleet sales, Tesla will be forced to focus either on a narrow niche or find a way of expanding the market by drawing new buyers to the category, analysts said.
