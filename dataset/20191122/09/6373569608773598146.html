Niall Canavan: Plymouth Argyle starting to repay Green Army after Devon Derby drubbing

The Pilgrims have bounced back from a 4-0 defeat at Exeter with back-to-back 1-0 away wins

22 Nov 2019, 09:42 by Chris Errington

Defender Niall Canavan believes Plymouth Argyle have begun to repay the Green Army after the 4-0 Devon Derby drubbing by Exeter City last month.

The Pilgrims have bounced back from the debacle at St James Parek with back-to-back 1-0 away wins.

First, they defeated Bolton Wanderers in the FA Cup first round, and then Argyle went to League Two leaders Forest Green Rovers and also beat them.

Next up for the Pilgrims is the visit of third-placed Bradford City to Home Park on Saturday.

That will be another test of the Pilgrims’ credentials to make a serious promotion push over the rest of the season.

The mood around Home Park has improved considerably since losing so emphatically to Exeter.

Canavan said: “We were all hurting after the game. We knew we were going to get booed by the fans.

“You are well aware of what has happened and what is going to come.

“It’s hard to go and try to applaud them because you know really that they don’t want you to do it.

“But we are thankful for the support we have had everywhere.

“We have said from there we need to repay them and the two good defensive displays away from home are hopefully the start of doing that.”

Argyle have had their issues defensively this season, particularly when defending set pieces in their own penalty area.

However, they have kept three clean sheets in their last four league games with Exeter the notable exception.

Canavan said: “It’s hard work on the training ground and pointing out a few principles that we maybe let slip at certain times in games.

“Or there is games that we have maybe lost control of when we have not been at our best.

“We have got a good record for clean sheets this season and in some games there has been a glut of goals at times that have hurt us, and that’s something we have got to address.

“We have worked on things on the training ground, we have had meetings and we all understand our roles and what’s expected of us.

“The last two results in particular away from home we have really dug in and got the gritty bit done.”

Manager Ryan Lowe has settled on a defensive trio in ther last couple of matches which has seen Canavan in the centre of defence, with Scott Wootton and Gary Sawyer either side of him.

Canavan said: “Obviously, we know each other from last season but didn’t play as a three.

“It is something that the more work you do the more you build on those relationships and you get familiar with what people tend to do in certain situations.

“You get to pick up on little bits and we spend a lot of time here (at Home Park) together. That will just continue to build the more we play together.”

The visit of Bradford to Home Park will see Leeds-born Canavan line up against the club which released him at the age of 12.

Canavan said: “It’s a club I spent a bit of time at and knew some good people there.

“They are a big team up there. They are well supported and I’m sure they will be coming down in good numbers so it should make it a good game.”

After being released by Bradford, Canavan played for local ataeur side Leeds City before being spotted by Scunthorpe United and signed up.
