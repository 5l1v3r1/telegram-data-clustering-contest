Remainers offer to sell thebrexitparty.com to Nigel Farage for £1,000,000

22 Nov 2019, 09:05

Anti-Brexit campaigners who bought thebrexitparty.com are hoping to make a handsome profit at the expense of Nigel Farage.

Led by Donkeys are offering to sell it to him for over a million pounds and said the fee, which will increase by £50,000 each day, would be donated to the Joint Council for the Welfare of Immigrants (JCWI)

It comes after Brexit Party lawyers demanded the activist group remove their logo from the site, launched in May during the European Parliament elections.

But Led by Donkeys are refusing to transfer the domain name, which is similar to the party’s thebrexitparty.org.

The spoof website features a rising total of the cost and an ‘election advent calendar’ – offering examples of ‘lies, lunacy and hypocrisy’ of the the party as well as the Tories.

Behind the first door is a video of the Brexit Party leader saying ‘we’re going to have to move to an insurance-based system of healthcare’.

Led by Donkeys co-founder Oliver Knowles said: ‘When Farage and his millionaire backers set up the Brexit Party they didn’t have the foresight to buy up all of the websites with their own name – and we did.

‘With the advent calendar, we are telling the story of the Brexit Party and the Tory Party who are now election partners – and the threat they represent to the country.’

He said he thought donating to the JCWI would be a ‘good and just cause’ given Farage peddling an ‘ideology of hatred and division’.

Led by Donkeys ridiculed the recent approach from Brexit Party lawyers and said their eight-page legal letter cites European Union law five times as justification of its claim.

The group tweeted: ‘Who knew Nigel Farage was such a fan of European law?’

All Brexit Party materials and logos have been removed from the current version of the website following the cease and desist letter issued by lawyers Wedlake Bell.

But a party spokesman said Led by Donkeys are ‘so far refusing to transfer the domain name’.

The pro-remain campaigners made a name for itself with large displays and billboards in public spaces designed to make Brexiteer’s pasts come back to haunt them.
