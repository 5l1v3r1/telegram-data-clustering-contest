Why Downs Star Wants To Change Positions

22 Nov 2019, 17:31 by Mazola Molefe

Rivaldo Coetzee has officially made up his mind about his best position, revealing that he has no real intentions to return to playing as a centre back.  

Even his Mamelodi Sundowns coach Pitso Mosimane has publicly argued that the only way the 23-year-old can attract interest abroad is if he switches to defensive midfield.

Mosimane began experimenting with Coetzee late last season and the Bafana Bafana international, whose dream of moving to Scottish Premier League outfit Celtic two years ago collapsed after he failed a medical, collected a few Man of the Match awards along the way and received rave reviews from fans, media and pundits.   

“If it’s up to me, then yes I would like to play there,” said Coetzee.

“I think I can contribute a lot to the team in that position, especially now that I am training in that position almost every day and that helps me to improve on it. There’s obviously a few things I need to work on myself, which the coaching staff is helping me with.”

‘Riva’ said he doesn’t mind the competition – alongside captain Hlompho Kekana, Tiyani Mabunda, Andile Jali and Sammy Seabi.

“I am really enjoying playing there. The competition is always good for the team because that’s how I can improve. I don’t really want to go back to playing centre back. I want to be further forward,” he added.
