How do I register to vote, and how to get a postal vote?

22 Nov 2019, 17:15 by Jonathan Walker, https://www.facebook.com/JonWalkerPolitics?pnref=story

The general election is on Thursday December 12  - but you have just days to apply for a vote, as the deadline to register is Tuesday 26 November.

You  can check whether you are already registered with your local electoral registration office. That will be a department of your local council, so give the council a call or look on their website for information.

If you live in the Birmingham City Council area, you can find out whether you are registered to vote by calling 0121 303 2731 or e-mailing electionsoffice@birmingham.gov.uk.

How do I register to vote?

You can register online by visiting gov.uk/register-to-vote and answering a small number of questions.

They include your name, address and National Insurance number. However, note that you can register even if you don't know your National Insurance number.

How long does it take?

About 5 minutes if you're not too speedy on a computer. Less if you have a quick mouse hand. It doesn't take long.

When is the deadline?

You must register to vote by midnight on Tuesday 26 November.

If you want a postal vote, you must apply by 5pm on Tuesday 26 November .

If you want a proxy vote (where you appoint someone to vote for you), you must apply by Wednesday 4 December.

For an emergency proxy vote, for example if you end up in hospital, it's 5pm on the day of the election, on December 12.

How does postal voting work?

If you can't get to your polling station, you can apply for a postal vote (although if you never get round to putting your completed ballot paper in the post, you can actually deliver it to the polling station on the day).

A ballot paper will be sent out to any address you choose.

You just need to fill it in, choose which way you want to vote and send it back so it arrives before 10pm on the day of the election.

All the instructions are on the ballot paper.

To apply for a postal vote, you need to download a form which you can find here: https://www.electoralcommission.org.uk/i-am-a/voter/voting-person-post-or-proxy/voting-post

The form must be printed out and completed, and signed.

You then need to send the form to your local electoral registration office. As we said before, that's a department of your local council, so contact your council or look at their website to find out exactly where you need to take the form.

If you are not sure who your local council is then the Electoral Commission website will tell you.

If Birmingham is your local council then the form can be handed in to council's offices in Victoria Square; posted; sent by fax on 0121 212 1065, or scanned and then emailed in PDF form to absentvotes@birmingham.gov.uk . There are more details on the council website at https://www.birmingham.gov.uk/info/20097/elections_and_voting/699/voting_by_post/3 .

However if Birmingham is not your local council then you must check with your council to find out where the form needs to go.

What about proxy voting?

This means if you're unable to go to the polling station for reasons such as going on holiday or having a disability then you can have somebody else vote on your behalf.

The deadline to apply for a proxy vote in the UK Parliamentary general election is 5pm on 4 December.

If you need an emergency proxy vote then you can apply for one up to 5pm on polling day. But this is only possible if you have a problem preventing you from going to the polling station that you didn't know about before December 4.

How do I vote by proxy?

You must give a reason why you need to vote by proxy and may need a qualified person to sign your application.

You may be able to deliver the form by hand or send a scan of the completed and signed form via email to your local authority.

The person you nominate must then vote in person for you.

Who can vote for me?

Any person you appoint if they are 18 or over and are registered individually.

A person cannot be a proxy for more than two people at one election, unless they are a close relative.

Close relatives are the spouse, civil partner, parent, grandparent, brother, sister, child or grandchild of the applicant.
