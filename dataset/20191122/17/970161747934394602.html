Avengers: Endgame cut 'huge battle sequence' with Thanos' army

22 Nov 2019, 17:33

Avengers: Endgame could have had another huge battle scene featuring Thanos’ army, but had to be cut – because, you know, the film was already three hours long.

In the new artbook for the movie, concept illustrator Josh Nizzi spoke about the prospect of squeezing in another fight.

Alongside a string of drawings, he explained how it was hoped the purple one would be seen going up against a military alien race, reports Comicbook.com.

However, it didn’t come to fruition.

He said: ‘Marvel wanted to show how powerful Thanos was even before he had any of the Stones.

‘There was going to be a huge battle sequence where he fights a military alien race. The aliens actually did show up in the film but only for a few seconds before being killed by Gamora and Nebula.’

Not quite the epic battle scene originally thought out, then.

Jerad Marantz, costume concept artist, described the army and called coming up with the design ‘challenging’.

‘After looking at the source material, I wanted them to have a biomechanics quality to them,’ he said.

‘There was concern that the Outriders may look too much like a Venom symbiote, and the challenge was to ensure they had a unique look.

‘To achieve that, I decided to go biomechanical with them and incorporate the same shapes that are used in Thanos’ armor and Proxima Midnight’s and Corvus Glaive’s.’

This isn’t the only scene that had been cut out of the epic superhero flick, as Katherine Langford was set to appear as the grown-up version of Tony Stark’s (Robert Downey Jr) daughter Morgan, who was played by Lexi Rabe in the movie.

The deleted scene, revealed by Disney+, shows Tony encountering the adult version of his daughter, saying: ‘Guess it worked.’

Morgan goes on to tell him: ‘Yeah, it worked for me. I got to live, grow up. For you, I can’t answer.’

Then, as if we weren’t sobbing enough, she tells her dad: ‘I love you’, with him (of course) answering: ‘I love you 3000’.
