East Village welcomes M2 to the neighbourhood

22 Nov 2019, 17:35 by Claire Young, Calgary Herald

East Village’s latest building is getting ready for its new tenants, who will enjoy four floors of unparalleled views of the Bow River and Riverwalk.

M2 is a four-floor building built by local firm XYC Design + Development that emcompasses retail on the ground level with office space on the second and third floors, topped with a very special residence on the top floor.
Its urban black and white design fits neatly against one neighbour, Alt Hotel, while its modernity is grounded by the red brick of the historic Simmons Building next door.

XYC Design + Development’s president Kate MacGregor is the mind behind the new building — and it’s her first development. XYC, incidentally, is a combination of her hometown YYC, Calgary’s airport code, and NYC, for New York City, where she lived while attending Columbia University for a master’s degree in architecture, following her degree in civil engineering at University of Calgary.

“I don’t think there could have been a better project on this site,” says Kate Thompson, vice-president development for Calgary Municipal Land Corp., the developer of East Village. MacGregor’s pitch during the request for proposals for the spot hit all the right notes for the community.

The design of the building was a unique combination that challenged MacGregor’s creativity from both her civil engineering training as well as her architectural side. The site has a unique shape, being long through the middle and bookended with both a wide and a narrow end. The Calgary rule that the building can’t cast a shadow on the Bow River dictates a stepped-back shape as it gets taller. The building also has no “back” side to it, as it’s accessible on all sides and doesn’t have a back alley.

M2 was designed by Brooklyn-based nArchitects, where some of MacGregor’s architecture professors and she worked for two years.

The main floor of M2 features 7,500 square feet of space that will be home to a bike store by Bow Cycle and a restaurant by the Concorde Group, with operations starting in 2020.

MacGregor, who grew up in Bowness, remembers her parents buying her first bicycle at age 5 at Bow Cycle.
“Bowness made a similar transition as East Village, and Bow Cycle was an important part of that transformative effect,” says MacGregor.

The bike store will activate the RiverWalk side of the building, with the business able to expand onto the patio when weather permits. It will focus on e-bike sales and rentals.

The Concorde Entertainment Group — the company behind such restaurants as Model Milk and Anju — will host a restaurant with 2,000 square feet of patio spilling out onto RiverWalk Plaza.

“In 2008 I left for New York and that was real transformation in the restaurant and entertainment industry in Calgary. When I came back, I went to Model Milk, and I couldn’t believe it — I could have been in New York,” MacGregor says.

M2’s second floor will be home to Harvest, a prairies-based venture group that helps start-ups with disruptive ideas and helps develop them into globally competitive companies. Behind Harvest is one of the founders of SkipTheDishes.

The third floor will be shared by North West Capital, a Calgary venture investment firm specializing in the energy industry, a cleantech startup called Quebe Technologies Inc., and the new offices of XYC Design + Development.

The fourth floor completes the building’s live-work-play mission — a private live-work home with about 1,800 square feet, two bedrooms, 2.5 bathrooms and a private deck that enjoys the flowing views of the Bow River and people on RiverWalk.

The home’s long and unique shape lends to tall ceilings in the great room and kitchen at one end, and tapers to a more intimate eight feet in the master bedroom at the other end of the home.

There are thoughtful design elements in the home — a nesting spot for pet dogs to curl up beside the linear fireplace, a chandelier that references the shape of Bloom, an art installation by Michel de Broin on St. Patrick’s Island that is part of the view from the living room, and a jewel of a half-bathroom off the kitchen.
