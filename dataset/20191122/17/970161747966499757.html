Dimitar Berbatov's weekend predictions including Arsenal, Man Utd, & Chelsea

22 Nov 2019, 17:00

The Premier League is back after a seemingly never-ending international break and there’s no doubt where the majority of the focus will be following Jose Mourinho’s shock appointment at Tottenham.

Spurs travel to West Ham for what is likely to be a typically feisty London derby in what will be Mourinho’s first match in charge since replacing Mauricio Pochettino. Liverpool, meanwhile, will hope to maintain their advantage at the top with Jurgen Klopp’s table toppers facing a trip to Crystal Palace, while rapidly improving Chelsea face Manchester City.

Betfair Ambassador Dimitar Bebatov reviews the action exclusively for Metro.co.uk…

West Ham vs Spurs

All the eyes are going to be on this game and all the photographers will be queuing up to get that picture of Mourinho in the Spurs dugout. The players are going to want to show him how good they are but for me time is a little bit short to get a big reaction from them straight away so I can see them having a few difficulties at West Ham.

Arsenal vs Southampton

For me Arsenal need the win for sure. It is the same story with them, they play good football, they keep the ball but in the end no trophies. Last year they didn’t manage to get in the top four and if they want to get there this season these are the games that they need to get results from and build some consistency.

Bournemouth are playing well but I like Wolves as well – they are playing good football and putting themselves about in the Europa League. Honestly, I think this one will be close but in the end I think home advantage will see Bournemouth nick this one.

Man City vs Chelsea

City need to bounce back, they are now nine points behind Liverpool and if they lose this one, Chelsea will become title contenders. Frank Lampard’s side are on a great run and they can cause City problems. But saying that, Guardiola will prepare his team to the maximum and they can’t afford any more slip ups whilst Liverpool are flying. They need the win and I think they will get it at the Etihad.

Sheffield United vs Man United

What the **** are Sheffield United doing in fifth place in the Premier League, can someone explain that to me!? Joking aside, they are playing some really good football, their 3-5-2 formation is slick, going to five at the back when someone is attacking them is so effective, they are a solid team and for me they are one of the surprises of the season. Of course I want United to win, but sometimes you have to look at things from the perspective of how things are, it will be a tough game and I think both teams will get a point.
