BETTRENDS SELECTION: LISNAGAR OSCAR

This is a race which favours a young and up-and-coming, lightly-raced hurdler and there are plenty towards the head of the market that fit the profile. However, the one who matches all four key trends is LISNAGAR OSCAR, who reverts back to hurdles after a disappointing run at Stratford. He still managed to finish third on that occasion and won over course-and-distance in February, when he had several of Saturday’s opponents in behind.

This looks a really competitive renewal – as you would expect for the money – and Umbrigado is another to take very seriously. He bids to provide David Pipe with a fourth win in the race since 2010 and like six of the past 10 winners, he will be having his first start at the trip.
