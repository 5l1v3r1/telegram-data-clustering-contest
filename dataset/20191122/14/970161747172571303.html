David Walliams makes £100million from his children's books

22 Nov 2019, 14:50

David Walliams’s foray into writing books for children has proved to be extremely lucrative – he’s made over £100million from his novels.

The actor, comedian and Britain’s Got Talent judge, 48, wrote his first book in 2008 after signing a deal with HarperCollins, and in just 11 years has become one of the UK’s highest-earning authors.

Although it’s estimated around £10million of the £100million earnings go to David, it still puts him in an exclusive club with the likes of Harry Potter creator JK Rowling, Gruffalo writer Julia Donaldson and Tracy Beaker writer Jacqueline Wilson.

The Bookseller magazine claims he made £17million in the last year alone, and has written 13 children’s story books, four collections of short stories and seven picture books.

Some of his titles, including Gangsta Granny and Mr Stink have also been made into films for the BBC, too.

His latest novel, The Beast Of Buckingham Palace, was also released this week, though he admits that he doesn’t just read his own books to his six-year-old son.

David shares his son Alfred with ex-wife Lara Stone, and told The Sun: ‘I love being a dad. It is the best thing of all and totally life-changing.

‘You feel there is finally some meaning to your life. Everything you do after they come along is for them or relates to them.’

While reading is a big part of their lives, David likes to vary what his son reads: ‘I don’t push my books on to him. I don’t want him to grow up and feel like, “We never read any books other than my dad’s”.

‘But reading with my son is one of the best parts of being a father. It is a pleasure to share the stories I loved as a child, like the [Roald] Dahl and Dr Seuss books, and discover new ones.

‘I’d urge all parents to make time to read with their children, as it is a joy for both of you. What could be better than firing their imagination just before they drift off to sleep?’
