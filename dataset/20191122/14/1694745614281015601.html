If you love nail art, you might have a folder dedicated to photos of ornate looks by your favorite celebrities on your Instagram account. If you're in search of an eye-catching design to wear to your next holiday party, we're here to help. The pros at Essie and influencer Yana Sheptovetskaya have teamed up to create some of Winter's biggest nail trends in three easy steps.

If you aren't familiar with Sheptovetskaya, you have likely seen close-ups of her perfectly manicured hands clutching some of the most coveted and 'grammable beauty products on her Instagram account, Gelcream. Instead of a face cream or a bottle of perfume, this time Sheptovetskaya clutched onto bottles from the Essie Winter 2019 collection to demonstrate how simple techniques can create some of the most eye-catching holiday nail art looks.

Keep reading ahead to find three of the season's coolest nail art trends.

Winter Nail Art Trend: Champagne Bubbles

To recreate this "Champagne Bubbles" nail art look, first apply a base coat. Next, using a dotting tool, create dots on each nail using Essie two polishes: Caught on Tape ($9) and Making Spirits Bright ($9). After your dots have dried down, apply a long-lasting top coat.

Winter Nail Art Trend: Classic Sparkle

To create this classic, sparkling nail look, prep your nails with a base coat. Then, apply two coats of the brand's nail polish in Let It Bow ($9), a shimmering white nail polish with a rainbow shift. Seal the manicure using a glossy top coat.

Winter Nail Art Trend: Classic Christmas Lights

Sheptovetskaya dubs this look "Classic Christmas Lights," and uses a different hue from the brand's Winter 2019 shades across all 10 fingers. To begin this look, first paint on a base coat, followed by a nude nail polish color. Then, apply a different hue in a straight line down each nail. Sheptovetskaya's iteration of the look uses the brand's Making Spirits Bright ($9), Knotty or Nice ($9), Tied and Blue ($9), Caught on Tape ($9), and Under Wraps ($9) (from top to bottom in this photo).

Seal the look with a glossy top coat. Sheptovetskaya suggests securing a tiny rhinestone near your cuticle for an extra touch of sparkle.
