'Bank robber in Donald Trump T-Shirt blasts worker for giving him too much cash'

22 Nov 2019, 14:40

A bank robber dressed in a Donald Trump T-Shirt hit out at a teller he targeted for giving him too much cash, police say.

Sandy Hawkins, 73, reportedly held-up the Wells Fargo bank in Boca Raton, Florida, on Tuesday after passing a note saying ‘Give me $1,100 now.’

The worried teller passed him $2,000, only for Hawkins – who was also wearing a ‘Make America Great Again Hat,’ to again demand he be given $1,100.

Bizarrely, retired air conditioning worker Hawkins’ note is said to have added: ‘No alarms. Hope to get caught.’

And his wish came true shortly after, when deputies from Boca Raton County Sheriff’s Office arrested him and charged him with robbery.

He visited a branch where staff recognized him as a regular customer, making it much easier for police to arrest him.

Staff initially believed he was asking for a regular withdrawal, and requested Hawkins debit card, it is alleged.

But he told them ‘This is a robbery. I have a weapon.’ and reached into the waistband of his pants.

Sheriff’s have since said that Hawkins was unarmed.

Hawkins’ ex-landlord Scott Vail painted a sympathetic picture of the alleged robber, saying he’d been left devastated by the death of his wife from cancer in 2013.

He told the Panama City News-Herald: ‘I knew it was pretty devastating.

‘He was just an affable kind of person. He probably reached some kind of breaking point.’

Hawkins confirmed he was the man who had robbed the bank when he was arrested at his home shortly afterwards, it is claimed.

No motive for the bizarre crime has been given.

Hawkins has been charged with robbery without a weapon, and is being held in Palm Beach County Jail in lieu of $50,000 bond.
