Woman who sent naked photo of ex to his daughter guilty of revenge porn

22 Nov 2019, 14:18

A bitter ex-girlfriend become one of the first women to be convicted of sharing revenge porn after sharing intimate photos of her former partner.

Jacqueline Doyle, 55, used a fake Facebook profile to send pictures of her victim’s genitals to his boss and daughter.

She also complained to one of her partner’s senior managers to say her ex-boyfriend had taken the images himself, Plymouth Crown Court heard.

This is one of very few cases brought under new revenge porn legislation, and one of the first where the perpetrator was a woman.

Doyle, of Plympton, Devon, pleaded guilty to two counts of disclosing private sexual photographs without consent with the intention of causing distress on June 8 and June 10.

The court heard how the defendant broke up with her partner, with whom she lived, in June last year.

That same month Doyle contacted the victims daughter on Facebook, posing as a man using a fake profile.

To her horror she opened the message and saw one picture of her father’s face and another of his genitalia.

She forwarded it to her dad who confirmed that the photos were taken while he was in a relationship.

Judge James Townsend said: ‘It is particularly unpleasant after you involved a member of the victim’s family.’

The Crown Prosecution Service’s Nick Lewin said told the court how Doyle emailed the same pictures to her partner’s senior manager at work.

Acting as if she was an aggrieved customer, she said: ‘I am not happy with your staff. One of them sent these to my wife.’

Mr Lewin added: ‘I am glad to say that the company kept things under wraps and the only other people who know are that manager and the victim’s direct line manager.’

The court heard how the victim has since kept in touch with Doyle, who has ‘expressed a high level of remorse’.

Michael Green, defending, said drinking alcohol ‘seemed to be the build-up to this level of offending’.

Doyle, who has no previous convictions, was handed a 12 month community order and a two month home curfew.

She was also told to pay an £85 victim surcharge and must undertake rehabilitation activity.

The judge declined to pass a restraining order to keep Doyle away from the victim.

It became a specific criminal offence to disclose private sexual photographs and films with intent to cause distress in April 2015.

But the number of prosecutions and convictions resulting from the new law are still low.

A support service for victims said the low numbers were ‘no surprise’ as victims often fear going through the legal process would be too traumatic.

Local news site Plymouth Live succeeded in removing a court order which effectively prevented media reporting Doyle’s identity.
