New film on 2018 Georgian club raids and protest raves to screen at London's Tate Modern

Watch Stephan Polivanov's debut feature, Raving Riot, at the Southbank gallery on Friday, November 29th.

22 Nov 2019, 14:15 by Marissa Cetin

A new film about the 2018 Georgian club raids and ensuing protest raves will screen next week at the Tate Modern in London.

Stephan Polivanov's first-ever feature film, Raving Riot, explores the so-called "raveolution" "beyond the news and beautiful pictures," he says. He'll participate in a discussion and Q&A session following the screening on Friday, November 29th, at 7 PM, as part of the November edition of Tate Lates. (Find more details about the event here.)

"We decided to make a movie... explor[ing] the generation that made that protest possible," Polivanov says. "This is a coming-of-age story of people who have become aware of themselves being a political force and having a voice, but do not yet understand what to do with it."
