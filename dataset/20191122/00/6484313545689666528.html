TSB customers left without wages as payments to 1,000s of accounts delayed

22 Nov 2019 by Naomi Schraer

Payments into thousands of TSB accounts were delayed overnight in another major banking failure, leaving some customers without wages or cash.

Update 12pm 22 November: TSB says the issue has now been fixed, and that all delayed payments have now been completed. 

We've heard from a number of TSB customers whose wages were delayed as a result of the issues, leaving them overdrawn or struggling to pay for essentials. Some also reported difficulties logging into online banking. 

The latest problems came just days after the publication of a report into the two months of chaos experienced by TSB customers last year, when an IT meltdown hit the bank's online and app services. 

TSB has apologised for the latest issues, saying they were due to a "processing error". It says no customers will be left out of pocket, and has confirmed that customers who went into their overdrafts as a result will be refunded any interest or charges.

It said that customers' cards still worked for payments and withdrawals during the problems, and it offered emergency cash to customers who needed it.
