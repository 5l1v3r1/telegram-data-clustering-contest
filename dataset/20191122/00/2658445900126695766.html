Truck with 'illegal' diesel impounded in Imo - The Nation Newspaper

22 Nov 2019, 00:31

Chris Njoku, Owerri

 

THE Imo State command of Nigeria Security and Civil Defence Corps (NSCDC) Owerri has said a truck carrying over 900 bags of salt and eight drums of illegally sourced Automated Gas Oil or diesel have been intercepted.

The corps also said three suspects were arrested.

The suspects are Ibrahim Babayo, Yunusa Nuhu and Murtala Abdullahi.

It was learnt that the bags of salt were used to conceal the illegal petroleum product.

The arrest was said to be made on October 30, 2019 at about 4.00pm at Okuzi checkpoint in Ohaji/Egbema Local government Area of the state.

Parading the suspects on Tuesday, the state Commandant of NSCDC, Mr. Raji Ibrahim said military operatives of the 34 Artillery Brigade, Obinze Imo State in a joint operation with officers of Civil Defence, arrested the suspects and handed them over to the operatives of the corps for further investigation and necessary actions.

He said, “It is unlawful dealing on petroleum products, sales, distribution without license and adulteration of petroleum products. It contradicts section 17 (a) and 18(b) of miscellaneous offences Act”.
