vivo U20 debuts in India, is a rebranded U3

22 Nov 2019 by Sagar

vivo launched the U10 in India back in September with a Snapdragon 665 SoC, triple camera and 5,000 mAh battery, and today the company followed it up with the vivo U20 which is a rebranded U3 announced in China last month.

The vivo U20 is powered by the Snapdragon 675 SoC that's paired with up to 6GB RAM and comes with 64GB of onboard storage. However, the phone also has a microSD card slot, allowing storage expansion by up to 256GB.

It sports a 6.53" FHD+ display that has a notch up top for the 16MP selfie camera. Around the back, you get a fingerprint reader and a triple camera setup which is a combination of 16MP main, 8MP super-wide (120 degrees FOV) and 2MP macro units.

On the software side, the vivo U20 runs Android Pie-based Funtouch OS 9.2 out of the box and comes with Widevine L1 certification, meaning you can stream Amazon Prime Video and Netflix videos in HD.

The U20 comes in Racing Black and Blaze Blue colors and packs a 5,000 mAh battery having 18W Dual-Engine Fast Charging support.

The base variant of the U20 with 4GB RAM is priced at INR10,990 ($155/€140) and you get 2GB extra RAM for INR1,000 ($14/€13) more.

The smartphone goes on sale in India from November 28 through Amazon.in and vivo's official India site.
