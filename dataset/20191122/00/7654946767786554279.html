Man accused of unprovoked attack on elderly man flips out in court

22 Nov 2019, 00:38 by Andrew Denney and Daniel Cassady

A 37-year-old man with a lengthy rap sheet threw a fit in Brooklyn Criminal Court Thursday and had to be dragged out by guards as he was brought to face charges for an unprovoked beating of a 77-year-old man.

Romeil Moore, who has been charged with second-degree assault for the Oct. 8 attack in Clinton Hill, couldn’t keep quiet as Judge Hilary Gingold walked him through his charges and he insisted that he was the real victim.

Even his own attorney asked him to be quiet.

“I was assaulted by the Asian man!” Moore blurted out, saying there are audio recordings taken from a 99-cent store near the scene that support him.

“The court reporter is sitting right there and taking down everything you say,” warned Moore’s lawyer, Michelle Riedel of Brooklyn Defender Services.

Moore, who is tall and lanky and who appeared in court wearing a denim jacket and jeans, ignored the warning and kept trying to make his case.

“Can I issue to the grand jury?” he repeated several times as two court officers took him by both arms and led him back to the holding cells.

“That went worse than I expected,” Riedel said as she headed back to her seat.

Moore was remanded and is being held without bail.

Assistant District Attorney Wilfredo Cotto said that there is video footage of the alleged incident.

Moore has an arrest record dating back to 1998 that includes multiple robbery counts and criminal possession of a weapon.
