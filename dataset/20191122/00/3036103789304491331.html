Facebook Dating lets users show Stories on their profiles

22 Nov 2019 by Bradley Prior

Facebook will let users display content from their Facebook or Instagram stories to populate their Facebook Dating profiles, Engadget reports.

These Stories will be viewable to other users as long as one party hasn’t blocked or passed on the other.

Using this feature will not change users’ settings across their Facebook or Instagram accounts – meaning that users won’t have to compromise their privacy on other platforms to use this feature.

Tinder already lets Snapchat users add their Snapchat Stories to their profiles, and it has also allowed users to link their Instagram account to their Tinder profile.

Facebook Dating

Facebook launched its dating platform in August 2019 as competition to the popular Tinder dating app.

Unlike Tinder, Facebook Dating doesn’t rely on users swiping left or right; instead, users are able to comment directly on another user’s profile or press the Like button to let them know that they would like to engage further.

Facebook Dating will not match you with any of your Facebook friends unless you have both tagged each other as secret crushes.

The platform also does not offer any equivalent paid plans to Tinder’s Gold product, meaning that all features are available to all users.

It also does not show adverts – Facebook is currently not monetising the product, and instead views it as a way to encourage more users to be active on the primary Facebook platform.
