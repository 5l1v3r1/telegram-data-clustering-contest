Serbia blames Russia for military spying

22 Nov 2019 by BNN

Serbian President Aleksandar Vučić has blamed Russia of spying after a man described as former Russian deputy military attache was filmed secretly giving cash to a man in Serbia.

Serbian public broadcaster RTS and British news portal The Guardian report that on Thursday, November 21, said that Serbia’s intelligence agencies have uncovered a Russian intelligence plot involving members of the Serbian army and summoned the Russian ambassador to explain the situation.

Serbian officials have identified the agent as Georgy Kleban, who worked as deputy military attache at Russia’s embassy in Belgrade.

It is believed he left his post last year.

Vučić named the Serbian man in the video only as Z.K and said he was a retired member of the Serbian military.

The Serbian President also stated that that relations with Moscow would not suffer. «We will not change our policy towards Russia, which we see as a brotherly and friendly country … but we will strengthen our own intelligence defences,» noted Vučić.
