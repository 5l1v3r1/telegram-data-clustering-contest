Things to do today in Bengaluru

22 Nov 2019, 00:30 by Bangalore Mirror Bureau

Bend the rules
In her exhibition I would rather be a lover than a fighter, German artist Nadin Reschke focuses on how to navigate gendered spaces and roles within familial environments and employ personal tactics to deal with them. She has employed saree as a sculptural material and collaborated with a local screen printer to print the individual narratives as text onto these textiles. The work has culminated in a textile installation bringing together a pluralistic view that recognises gendered realities.

WHERE: 1 Shanthi Road Studio Gallery, Bheemanna Garden, Shanti Nagar WHEN: 6.30pm onwards, November 22
ENTRY: Free
CALL: 8022511300

Age is just a number
Catch the screening of RV Ramani’s new film ‘Oh That’s Bhanu’, featuring the dance and theatre personality Bhanumathi Rao. The 92- year-old Bharatanatyam and Kathakali dancer has proven time and again, that age is just a number , and this one only reinforces her prowess. Filming Bhanumathi in Delhi and Bengaluru, with her daughters nudging her, the filmmaker takes the audience on a journey of life and performance, through the spasms of memory, complexities of relationships, love and a reflection of what could constitute a beautiful mind.

WHERE: Bangalore International Centre, Stage 2, Domlur WHEN: 7pm onwards, November 22
CALL: 25359680

Let your hair down
DJ Avisha Sati is back to the console with some great company in the form of DJ Soul Tokes and DJ Insowmya. Sati is one the upcoming female DJs in the scene and her energtic sets of commercial, dance and hiphop music keep the crowd swaying and jumping. Those who’ve seen her play will tell you she is a whirlwind in the DJ booth.

WHERE: Liquid Bar, Hyatt Centric MG Road, 1/1 Swami Vivekanada Road, Ulsoor
WHEN: 8pm onwards, November 22 ENTRY: `1,000 CALL: 8040187200

Save the dying art forms

Art For Concern is holding the ‘Traditional and Folk Art’ exhibition to showcase indigenous art forms from across India. The show aims at preserving India’s artistic heritage and that’s why, it’s got an array of artworks – from Gond works of Madhya Pradesh to Madhubani paintings of Bihar, from Tanjore paintings of Tamil Nadu to Phads and Pichwais of Rajasthan, from Mata Ni Pachedis of Gujrat to Kalighat paintings and Pattachitra of eastern India. Proceeds from the sale will go towards grassroots programmes that work on social development programs.

WHERE: The Art Corridor, Taj West End, 25 Race Course Road
WHEN: 7pm-9.30pm, November 22

CALL: 8971965922
