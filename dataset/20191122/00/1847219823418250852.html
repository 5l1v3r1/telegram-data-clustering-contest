Saeima committee urges feedback about NEPLP candidates

22 Nov 2019 by BNN

Saeima’s Human Rights and Public Affairs Committee invites associations and foundations that work in mass media, education, culture, science and human rights sectors, and other interested people to provide feedback about proposed candidates for posts in the National Electronic Mass Media Council (NEPLP), as confirmed by Saeima’s press-service.

So far there are 17 candidates for two posts in the media council.

Candidates include ex-Saeima deputy Kārlis Seržants, journalist Jānis Eglītis, publicist Ilmārs Šlāpins, director Jānis Ābele, researcher Oļģerts Tipāns, ex-Latvijas Radio sound engineer Andis Plakans, corporate and teacher Ilmārs Jargans, Jekaterina Jerofejeva-Āboliņa, journalist Ieva Kalderauska, Latvian Christian Radio news editor Ginta Linmeijere, writer and journalist and Riga City Council deputy Eva Mārtuža, journalist Ilze Pētersone, journalist Jānis Zariņš and former NEPLP member Aija Dulevska. Other candidates who have applied include Lavijas Radio 2 music programme manager Daiga Mazvērsīte, politician Roberts Putnis and Latvijas Regional Television development director and Riga TV24 content editor Inese Šuļžanoka.

In accordance with the Law on Electronic Mass Media, the candidate needs to be a citizen of Latvia with residence declared in Latvia. The candidate also needs to have higher education, at least five years of professional or academic experience in mass media, education, culture, science or human rights sector, as well as have a good reputation.

The law also states – candidates are not allowed to be members of political parties or political party unions, shareholder in a mass media, as well as have a past criminal record. There is also a requirement for candidates to be able to receive permit to access official secrets.

NEPLP members are picked by the Human Rights and Public Affairs Committee and approved for a five-year term by the Saeima.
