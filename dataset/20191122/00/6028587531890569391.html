Holiday Gift Guide 2019: The Best Napa Valley Cabernets For Oenophiles

22 Nov 2019 by Michelle Williams

Holiday gift giving can be a challenge, especially for the person who has everything. Wine is the ideal solution. What better way to spread holiday joy to your boss, in-laws, clients, friends, and loved ones than with a stunning bottle of Napa Valley Cabernet Sauvignon? Napa produces many high-quality cabs; however, this curated list steps away from the well-known, to bring you ten Napa cabs sure to please the oenophiles on your holiday gift list.

2017 Barnett Vineyards ‘Rattlesnake’ Cabernet Sauvignon Spring Mountain District ($190): This 100% Cabernet Sauvignon is crafted of small lots of vineyards grown Rattlesnake Hill, a small rocky knoll on the estate. Due to record breaking heat during the summer months, this represents one of the biggest Rattlesnake Cabernets produced by Barnett Vineyards. Bold aromas of black fruit, baking spice, cassis, graphite, ground espresso, dusty cocoa, and dried tobacco leap from the glass. Ripe, grippy tannins give way to a crush velvet mouth-feel. A perfect wine for the collector with patience to age. Purchase direct from Barnett.

2015 Chateau Montelena ‘The Montelena Estate’ Cabernet Sauvignon Calistoga ($175): As Montelena approaches forty vintages of their Estate Cabernet, winemaker Matt Crafton honors the harmony that links the early wines with their wines of today. A blend of 97% Cabernet Sauvignon, 2% Cabernet Franc, and 1% Petit Verdot, this wine demonstrates the juxtaposition of a warm vintage with refined winemaking, illustrating elegant tension. Notes of fresh and dried black and red fruit, warm baking spice, dried savory herbs and roses, leather cigar box, and roasted espresso beans leap from the glass. This wine is fresh, young, and exuberant, proper cellaring will further integrate its vibrant tannins and firm acidity. Purchase direct from Chateau Montelena.

2016 Cliff Lede ‘Poetry’ Cabernet Sauvignon Stags Leap District ($250): This flagship wine reflects the pinnacle that is the Poetry Vineyard, carved into a steep west-facing hillside, reaching from the highest elevation of the Stags Leap District appellation to the valley floor. A blend of 86% Cabernet Sauvignon, 6% Cabernet Franc, 6% Merlot, and 2% Petit Verdot; this Bordeaux style blend delivers rich aromas of fresh and baked red and black fruit, lavender, cassis, cedar cigar box, fresh tobacco, black pepper, chocolate covered espresso beans, black licorice, and slate. Bold and powerful, yet elegant and refined. This wine coats the palate like crushed velvet—firm tannins, high acidity, and bold structure give way to a long, luscious finish. An alluring wine that rewards the patient, cellar properly for highest rewards. Purchase direct from Cliff Lede.

2016 Gamble Family Vineyards ‘G. Thomas’ Cabernet Sauvignon Oakville ($205): This wine was named in honor of those who inspired Tom Gamble—his father, grandfather, and uncle—who taught him the value of hard work and persistence. This 100% Cabernet Sauvignon is distinctively Oakville, with only the best fruit sourced from Family Home and Cairo Vineyards. Loads of dark berries, dried herbs, baking spice, cassis, leather cigar box, and dark chocolate leap from the glass. This is a bold wine, with energy and broad shoulders. Dusty tannins and firm acidity provide great structure. Patience will be rewarded. Purchase direct from Gamble.

2016 Quintessa Red Wine Rutherford ($190): Each vintage of Quintessa illuminates the evolution of the property and the winemaking team. For Quintessa, 2016 was a profound vintage, providing generosity in the glass today, which will continue to evolve over time. A blend of 89% Cabernet Sauvignon, 6% Merlot, 3% Cabernet Franc, and 1% each of Petit Verdot and Carménère, this inky purple wine is elegant, vibrant, and seamless. Vibrant layers of endless aromas include fresh black and red berries mingled with cassis, crushed violets, fig preserve, warm baking spice, leather cigar box, dried Herbs de Provence, black truffles, licorice, black pepper, and eucalyptus. Long and lean on the palate with a silky mouth-feel and a mineral driven finish. A truly stunning wine any collector will cherish. Purchase direct from Quintessa.

2016 Signorello Estate ‘Padrone’ Cabernet Sauvignon Napa Valley ($175): This flagship wine is only made in exceptional vintages, like 2016. Crafted of 100% Cabernet Sauvignon from their estate vineyards on Silverado Trail, this sophisticated wine offers a mosaic of aromas, included black and red fruit, warm baking spice, cassis, black licorice, wild herbs, fresh tobacco, and a graphite driven minerality. It’s elegance is exemplified by its silky mouth-feel, lean tannins, pronounced acidity, and firm structure. This wine will age well for many years. Purchase direct from Signorello.

2015 Sill Family Vineyards ‘tres’ Cabernet Sauvignon Atlas Peak ($195): Crafted of 75% Cabernet Sauvignon, 16% Merlot, and 9% Cabernet Franc, this new world Bordeaux style wine offers the power of Atlas Peak with the silkiness of Napa Valley. Dark chocolate covered cherries, black berries, cassis, baking spice, dried tobacco, and crème de menthe envelop the senses, while lush tannins with firm structure and high acidity coat the palate. This wine can be enjoyed now with a thorough decanting, but patience will heighten the experience. Purchase direct from Sill.

2015 Trefethen ‘Halo’ Cabernet Sauvignon Oak Knoll District ($220): This 100% Cabernet Sauvignon is named for Trefethen’s third generation—Hailey and Lorenzo—who grew up in the middle of the estate’s Hillspring Vineyard. The wine is classic Oak Knoll, demonstrating power and elegance. Notes of dark fruit mingle with dried roses, wild herbs, cocoa, dried tobacco, and spice. This is a collector’s wine, built to age. Purchase direct from Trefethen.

2017 Tor Beckstoffer To Kalon Vineyard Cabernet Sauvignon ($225): Winemaker Jeff Ames believes this 2017 vintage To Kalon Cab to be one of their best yet, reminiscent of the beloved 2012 vintage. Enticing the senses with aromas of black fruit and berries, warm baking spice, crème de cassis, graphite, umami underbrush, fresh tobacco, and dusty cocoa. Bold yet refined, with dusty tannins, high acidity, proud structure, polished purity, and length for days. This is a contemplative wine that will age for decades, an oenophile’s dream. Purchase direct from Tor.

2016 Viader Red Wine Howell Mountain ($150): A proprietary blend of Cabernet Sauvignon and Cabernet Franc sourced from Viader estate vineyards on the eastern slopes of Howell Mountain. This blend put Delia Viader on the map as a Napa pioneer for showcasing Cab Franc alongside Cabernet Sauvignon. Aromas of dark and red fruit and berries, cassis, warm baking spice, fresh herbs, dusty earth, tobacco, and black pepper arouse the senses. Its silky texture is accentuated by smooth tannins, high acidity, and a bold structure. A wine to enjoy now with a long decant, or age for more concentration. Purchase direct from Viader.
