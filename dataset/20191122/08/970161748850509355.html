Manny Pacquiao's camp responds to Floyd Mayweather return announcement

22 Nov 2019, 08:29

Manny Pacquiao’s camp has welcomed back Floyd Mayweather after he revealed his plans to come out of retirement in 2020.

Mayweather has announced that he will be ending his retirement next year to work alongside UFC president Dana White in what could be another exhibition fight.

Last year, Mayweather earned $9 million (£7m) for his exhibition contest against Japanese kickboxer Tenshin Nasukawa, who he beat inside three minutes.

Mayweather has not yet revealed what his next move will be but it appears the prospect of a rematch against Manny Pacquiao is off the table.

Responding to Mayweather’s latest plan to come out of retirement next year, Pacquiao’s manager Sean Gibbons told The Athletic: ‘Viva Floyd !! Let’s Go Champ.’

Mayweather beat Pacquiao in their highly anticipated fight in May 2015 and a rematch has been touted ever since that event.

But speaking earlier this week, Mayweather was adamant that he would not be returning to boxing.

‘I’ve got calls to get back into the ring, but my health is my wealth,’ he told Reuters.

‘Boxing is a very, very brutal sport. In the last few years a lot of fighters have died inside that squared circle.

‘You have got to know when to hang it up. I had a great career.

‘I’ll still travel and do exhibitions. I make great money doing exhibitions; between $10 and $30 million.

‘I think I make more doing that than most fighters make fighting.’
