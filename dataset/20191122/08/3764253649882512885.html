Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie honoured for fighting gender inequality

22 Nov 2019, 08:16

- International organization Equality Now honoured Nigerian author Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie on Thursday, November 21

- Adiche was recognised for fighting gender inequality with her words

- English writer and activist, Scarlett Curtis was also honoured alongside Adiche

Prolific Nigerian author Chimamanda Adiche has been recognized alongside English writer and activist, Scarlett Curtis for fighting gender inequality with written words.

Both authors were honoured by international organization Equality Now on Thursday, November 21 at the annual Make Equality Reality Gala in New York City.

According to reports, the night was filled with art, advocacy, and entertainment to inspire activism, with an interactive musical number by the Resistance Revival Chorus, a satirical political sketch by Stacey Kramer, and a riveting spoken-word performance about sexual assault by Katie Capiello.

Adichie is the critically acclaimed author of five books, a MacArthur Genius Grant winner, and is even featured on Beyoncé’s hit song “Flawless.” While accepting th honour, Adiche stated that she was a feminist long before she knew what the word meant.
