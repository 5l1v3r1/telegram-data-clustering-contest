Robbie Williams says it took nearly dying in health scare to force him to change

Brit pop star Robbie says despite years of battling booze and drugs, it was the health scare which left him close to death in 2017 that changed his ways

22 Nov 2019, 11:50 by Tiffany Wallis

Robbie Williams has revealed it took nearly dying in huge 2017 health scare to change his wild lifestyle.

The pop star, 45, spent seven days in an intensive care unit after abnormalities were found in his brain in September two years ago.

The Let Me Entertain You hitmaker now reveals he felt like a "cat with a billion lives" and worryingly believed he was "immortal" before suffering from the severe heath panic.

During a new interview with The Sun , Robbie recalls the scary experience and admits he "knows how lucky he is" after being close to death.

He described his attitude before the incident: "I’ve always thought I was protected and I’ve gone out of my way to test that hypothesis. Like a cat, I’ve had a billion lives.

"But this was the closest and scariest. I also had this f***ed up feeling that I was immortal. But all is OK with my brain now."

The former Take That frontman went on to reveal how the health scare caused him to re-evaluate his life and thinks the experience is the reason he is "on such good form today."

He continued: "I’ve refocused my life and all the extremities in it. It takes nearly dying for someone like me to change the way they live."

The singer famously spent a week in intensive care after falling seriously ill while he was backstage at a gig in Zurich on September 2.

He previously described feeling his arm going numb and suffering from a severe headache.

The Angels singer was then forced to cancel his Russian tour dates due to the illness and spent spent two months recovering at his home in Los Angeles .

According to reports, Robbie took up yoga, meditation and a healthier lifestyle.

During the interview, Robbie also revealed how he finally feels happy, confident and at ease with being in the spotlight.

He has a busy month coming up with the release of his album No12 and his festive hit 'The Christmas Present', featuring an unusual collaboration with boxer Tyson Fury .

The singer - who has children Teddy, seven, Charlie, five, and Coco, 14 months, with wife Ayda Field , 40 - infamously checked into rehab in 2007 after taking speed, acid, heroin, cocaine and 'heart-stopping' amounts of prescription drugs.

The Take that star has now successfully been sober for 19 years and is no longer tempted by these addictions and jokes that he is now addicted to boxing and golf.
