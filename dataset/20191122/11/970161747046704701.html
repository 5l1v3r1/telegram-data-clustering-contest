Jon Voight awkwardly dances in front of Donald Trump at medal presentation

22 Nov 2019, 11:35

Jon Voight is one of the few big names in Hollywood to have voiced support for Donald Trump’s presidency – and now the President has repaid the favour.

The Mission: Impossible and Pearl Harbor actor was invited to The White House yesterday (21 November) to be presented with the prestigious National Medal of Arts and Humanities in a ceremony which took place against the backdrop of the impeachment hearings happening across town.

Trump praised Voight’s Oscar and award nominations during a speech which briefly – and rather unexpectedly, judging by POTUS’ initially confused reaction – saw the theme from Midnight Cowboy being played.

Voight, presumably swept up by all the excitement, proceeded to get up from his chair and awkwardly shimmy, throw shapes and grandad-dance in front of the President, the guests and the press.

Sick moves, Jon.

Trump appeared to approve of the impromptu dancing, chuckling away as Voight shimmered towards him before shaking his fist in approval (we guess?) and pointing at the veteran actor.

‘I wanted to hear that whole song,’ Trump told the audience after that strange interlude. ‘But I dunno, Jon, maybe we gotta keep things moving a little bit?’

Which presumably was Presidential Code for ‘please don’t dance again’.

‘Jon captures the imagination of audiences and dominates almost every single scene,’ Trump later said in gushing praise. ‘He’s a special person.

‘Jon Voight, you are an amazing artist and a beloved icon of American film.

‘We love having you here: especially as you’re someone I really like.’

Things later returned to Level Awkward, though, when Trump presented Voight with his medal, as Trump managed to ignore Voight’s outstretched hand when the actor went in for a handshake.

Just another day in the Trump White House, we guess.
