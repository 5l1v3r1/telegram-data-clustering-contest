WNY Maritime/Health Sciences Falcons basking in semifinal spotlight

22 Nov 2019, 11:00

This week of practice has been like no other for the Western New York Maritime/Health Sciences football team.

In addition to the increased spotlight on the Falcons as a first-time state semifinalist, they have been preparing for their biggest game yet on an official field. With lines and everything.

They’ve been allowed to use Johnnie B. Wiley Pavilion, as a favor from the Buffalo Public Schools, which runs the facility.

Excuse the second-year Falcons, if they suddenly feel like they’re being spoiled.

Just last week, they practiced at dusk in snow deep enough to make snow angels within sight of Johnnie B.

Coach Tyree Parker tweeted a video with the caption, "Rain sleet or snow @MHSFalconsFB working!!!" and noted that the Falcons didn't have a stadium with lights. For most of the season, they practiced on a patch of grass near the old Rockpile.

Not this week. Consider it a perk for making the New York State Public High School Athletic Association Final Four.
