House of Fraser slammed after fur appears back on the shelves

22 Nov 2019, 11:39

A high street store has been slated for apparently overturning its ban on animal fur.

House of Fraser appears to have started to sell fur products again, despite saying in 2017 that it had a ‘strict no-fur policy’.

The store – which was taken over by Sports Direct CEO Mike Ashley last year – attracted widespread criticism after garments were found lined with rabbit fur on shelves.

Animal charities have slammed the revelations, which also include reports of a hats with ‘100% racoon fur’ available on the company website. The item appears to have since been removed.

People for the Ethical Treatment of Animals Director Elisa Allen told Metro.co.uk: ‘Has House of Fraser’s new owner lost his mind, or does he just not mind losing customers?

‘At a time when designers, retailers, entire councils, and high-profile individuals – including Her Majesty the Queen – are turning their backs on real animal fur, the company’s decision to start hawking it is both shameful and perplexing.

‘Today’s shoppers wouldn’t be seen dead in the skins of animals. 95% per cent of the British public refuses to wear animal fur, and PETA urges House of Fraser to come to its senses, do the decent thing, and reinstate its no-fur policy immediately.’

Dominic Dyer, CEO of the Badger Trust, labelled the move ‘exploitation and cruelty for profit’, while other animal charity added to the chorus of condemnation.

Earlier this year, it was revealed that the Queen has switched to fake fur.

House of Fraser have been involved in controversy surrounding fur before.

In 2017, an investigation found items marked as fake fur in the store were actually made with real fur.

The company apologised and a spokesperson told HuffPost UK: ‘House of Fraser has a strict no-fur policy and we ensure all of our suppliers and brand partners are aware of this.’

Sports Direct Ltd, which owns House of Fraser, have not responded to multiple requests for comment.
