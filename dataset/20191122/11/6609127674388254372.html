EUROS PUSH

Euro 2020: Scotland draw Israel in Nations League semi-final play-off

22 Nov 2019, 11:13 by Ewan Paton

SCOTLAND'S Nations League play-off fate has been decided.

Steve Clarke's side will take on Israel as they continue their battle to make it to Euro 2020.

The one-off match will take place at Hampden in a bid to reach the Nations League play-off final for a place in the Euros.

The game will take place on Thursday, March 26 during the next international break.

The Tartan Army are just two games off a spot at their first major tournament in 21 years.

Their potential opponents for the play-off semi included Bulgaria, Hungary, Israel and Romania.

However, we now know that it's Israel that will travel to Hampden.

Although, Scotland will be away to either Norway or Serbia in the Nations League final should they make it.

Boss Steve Clarke said: "We are looking forward to the home semi-final against Israel in what is a hugely exciting challenge for us with a massive reward. The players know Israel well having faced them in the Nations League.

"It was a tough match over there but we showed our capabilities with a good win at Hampden last year.

“This is the time to believe. We have just finished what was a difficult campaign, having experienced a turbulent few years for players, fans and managers alike.

"It’s now time to channel the positivity following our recent results and focus on the semi-final in March first and foremost.

“We have three wins in a row and the benefit of momentum. We need to channel that positivity and focus on the many attributes we have.

"To have more than 50,000 people cheering us on at Hampden for the semi-final and the whole country watching at home is something that really excites me and will only serve as further inspiration to the players.

“We want to be active participants at EURO 2020, not just hosts, and we will give our all in what I believe will be two games.

"The Scotland fans always travel in their numbers and I hope they will do so once again if we were to reach the final, but for the moment our focus will be on the semi-final first and foremost."

Although, as SunSport revealed yesterday, they face a nightmare group of death if they make the tournament proper.

The Dark Blues could come up against England, France AND Portugal in the early stages of the major finals.

Scotland are guaranteed to be paired with England in Group D - who are top seeds - due to Hampden and Wembley being host pairings.

A slightly less daunting route for the Scots would be facing England, Poland and Czech Republic.

The 24 teams that qualify for Euro 2020 are seeded based on their performances during the qualifiers.

The ten group winners take the first ten positions, followed by the ten runners up, all ranked by points, goal difference and goals scored to determine their final pot.

Elsewhere, Northern Ireland will host the Republic of Ireland for a spot at Euro 2020 if they both with their play-off semi-finals.

NI will travel to Bosnia-Herzegovina first of all though while Mick McCarthy's side will take on Slovakia away.

Meanwhile, Celtic skipper Scott Brown has refused to rule out a sensational Scotland return at the Euros.

The Hoops skipper quit the international scene for a second time in February 2018.

But Brown, 34, has left the door open to pulling on the national shirt again if Steve Clarke’s side make it through March’s play-offs.

Asked if Clarke was to sound him out, Brown said: “They still have two games to get through but you never say never.

“It would be phenomenal to get to a finals after 22 years. Football’s a funny game and you never know.

“I’m in a good place fitness-wise in terms of my body and I make sure I keep myself ticking over."
