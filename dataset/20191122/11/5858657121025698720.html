White Rock's rainbow decal program to provide safe places for LGBTQ2S+ community

WHITE ROCK (NEWS 1130) – The City of White Rock is teaming up with the RCMP to launch an initiative aimed at increasing safety for the LGBTQ2S+ community.

The Safe Place program has participating businesses and schools place a rainbow decal on their window to show they are a haven for anyone being harassed because of their sexuality or gender identity.

The location commits to remaining a safe space while police are called and the victim waits for officers to arrive.

The RCMP says in a release that: “By placing a decal in their window, they will be clearly identifying the premise as a safe haven and ally for members of the LGBTQ2S+ community.”

Vancouver launched a similair initiative 3 years ago.
