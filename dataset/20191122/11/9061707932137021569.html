Ford Fiesta driver taken to hospital as car flips off road near Burton

He ended up getting trapped between a tree and a telegraph pole

22 Nov 2019, 11:54 by Helen Kreft, Jenny Moody

A driver had to be rescued by firefighters after an accident saw him trapped in his car in a South Derbyshire country lane.

The vehicle rolled on the lanes in Drakelow, at the junction of Cadley Lane and Caldwell Road, and ended up trapped between a tree and a telegraph pole.

A spokesman for Derbyshire police said they were called at 9.35am today, November 22, by East Midlands Ambulance Service as one man was trapped in the vehicle.

A spokesman for East Midlands Ambulance Service said: "We received a call at 9.23am on November 22 to Caldwell Road in Drakelow.

"The caller reported that there had been a road traffic collision.

"We sent a paramedic in a car and a crewed ambulance.‚Äù

The spokesman said the patient had been taken to Queen's Medical Centre, Nottingham.

One motorist, who did not wish to be named but uses that road every day, said: "That bend in the lanes is probably one of the tightest bends around here.

"And considering it is on a road with a 60mph limit, I think there should be warnings about how tight it is.

"I think a lot of people could misjudge it if they are new to the area, so there should be signs warning people that it is a tighter bend than they might think.

"I don't know if that had anything to do with what happened here - I just hope the driver is Okay."
