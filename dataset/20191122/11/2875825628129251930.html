Harry Maguire wanted to be a midfielder before becoming world's most expensive defender

Maguire became the world's most expensive defender when he signed for Manchester United for £80m from Leicester this summer but it could have been very different

22 Nov 2019, 11:20 by Irishmirror.ie

Former Sheffield United academy manager John Pemberton has revealed Harry Maguire wanted to be a centre midfielder before his transformation into the centre-back he is today.

Pemberton helped develop Maguire during his time at Bramall Lane before he eventually moved on to Hull in 2014, and is a big part of the reason why he eventually made his eyewatering £80m move to Old Trafford.

Speaking to The Athletic Pemberton said he did not feel Maguire was a good enough midfielder to make it at the top level, and convinced him to change into a defensive role.

"Harry was playing centre midfield when I first met him," Pemberton said. "The problem for me was I just didn’t see his future there. He was a centre-half to me.

"I remember sitting him down in my office. We had quite a few chats over the years, mainly because he liked asking questions about the game. Anyway, this time I ended up asking him, ‘Where do you see yourself in three years’ time?’ He said the first team but I pushed him on a position. He thought centre midfield but I said, ‘No’."

Pemberton told Maguire he would never make it to become one of the top midfielders in the country, and convinced him to try his hand at the back.

"Steven Gerrard and Frank Lampard were the elite midfielders in the Premier League at the time and I told Harry he would never be as good at that position as them," he added.

"I said there were a lot of young midfielders out there who could do the same as him. But I then went on to say, ‘If you go to centre-half, you can be the best in that position’. He understood what I was saying, agreed to move back, and worked as hard as he could to become the player he is today.”

Maguire has had a solid start to his career at United, and will next be in action when they take on his former club Sheffield United on Sunday.
