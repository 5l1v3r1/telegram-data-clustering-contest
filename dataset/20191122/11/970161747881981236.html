Pakistan bowler Naseem tipped to become 'superstar' by England hero Vaughan

22 Nov 2019, 11:25

Pakistan teenage bowler Naseem Shah has the potential to become a ‘superstar’, according to former England captain Michael Vaughan.

Naseem, 16, made his international debut in the opening Test against Australia in Brisbane this week.

The seamer has only played seven first-class matches and is yet to take a wicket against Australia as David Warner’s unbeaten 151 saw the hosts dominate Pakistan in Brisbane.

Tim Paine’s Australia bowled Pakistan out for 214 at the Gabba and the home side had reached 312-1 by the close of play on day two.

Despite his unenviable welcome to Test cricket, Pakistan’s new bowling sensation Naseem has been backed to develop into a ‘superstar’ by England hero Vaughan and Australia opener Warner.

Vaughan tweeted: ‘1 over into his Test Career… but already you can see Naseem Shah is going to be a superstar! 16 yrs old and clocking 147kph with a fantastic action.’
