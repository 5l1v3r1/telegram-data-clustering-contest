Driver denies causing death of teenager in Mid Devon tree stump crash

Brandon Martin died in the accident on the A3181 between Cullompton and Willand when he was the front seat passenger in a Toyota Celica car which left the road and hit the stump on October 21, 2018

22 Nov 2019, 11:30 by Ted Davenport

A 20-year-old man has pleaded not guilty to causing the death of a 16-year-old boy who was a passenger in his car when it crashed into a tree stump.

Brandon Martin died in the accident on the A3181 between Cullompton and Willand when he was the front seat passenger in a Toyota Celica car which left the road and hit the stump on October 21, 2018.

Jordan Sturcbecher, aged 20, of Orchard Way, Cullompton, denied causing death by dangerous driving when he appeared at Exeter Crown Court.

Judge Timothy Rose adjourned the case for a four-day trial on May 4  next year and set a timetable for the exchange of prosecution and defence papers, which will include specialist collision investigator and vehicle examiner reports.

Sturcbecher was released on bail.

Emergency services were called to an area known as Five Bridges at around 1.50 am on Sunday October 21 last year after the car left the road, hit a tree stump and overturned.

Front seat passenger Brandon, from Cullompton, sustained fatal injuries and was pronounced dead at the scene. The driver was treated for minor injuries.

A large number of floral tributes were left at the scene of the accident, which was close to the Diggerland tourist attraction, where Brandon had worked since leaving school.

His family set up a fund raising page on the internet to raise money for a permanent memorial.
