Queen shows support to Prince Andrew as they ride horses together in Windsor

22 Nov 2019, 15:43

The Queen has been seen out with Prince Andrew for the first time since she effectively sacked him.

The monarch and her ‘favourite son’ were pictured riding together in the grounds of Windsor Castle earlier today.

The pair were covered to protect from themselves from the rain on the Berkshire estate and accompanied by two equerries.

The Duke and his mother regularly ride horses together but the image is likely to be seen as a very public show of support towards her embattled son.

The monarch and her heir, Prince Charles, are believed to have forced Andrew to stand down in the wake of the Jeffrey Epstein paedophile scandal.

The Duke has repeatedly maintained he did not have sex with one of the financier’s ‘slaves’ when she was 17.

But he came under intense after a ‘car crash’ BBC interview in which he failed to show sympathy for the victims and defended his friendship with Epstein.

Charles – who is currently on a tour of New Zealand – is believed to have personally stepped in and persuaded his mother that Andrew could no longer continue royal duties.

The final straw was said to have been when the question ‘is the monarch fit for purpose’ formed part of the General Election debate.

The Queen is said to have summoned Andrew to Buckingham Palace on Wednesday and demanded he step down from public duties.

The eighth-in-line to the throne was told he could write his own statement in an attempt to allow him to bow out gracefully.

A source told the Daily Mail: ‘When the Queen and the Prince of Wales stand firm together they are a pretty formidable combination in terms of getting things done.’

The wider Royal Family were informed and the Duke put out a statement just before 6pm on Wednesday announcing his departure.

Since then, Outward Bound, the University of Huddersfield and the Royal Philharmonic Orchestra have announced they are parting company with him.

Yesterday Buckingham Palace said he would still be involved in Pitch@Palace because it was a private venture.

The Duke was said to have been planning to go to Bahrain this weekend for an event but a furious Charles is reported to have stepped in and forced him to cancel the trip.

A source said: ‘He was persuaded by his family that it was not a good idea in light of this week’s events.’

The Duke of York has long been rumoured to be the Queen’s favourite and he was named after Prince Philip’s father Prince Andrew of Greece.

A year earlier the Queen and Philip were said to have gone through a ‘rocky patch’ and Andrew’s birth marked a fresh start for the family.

He has appeared in public a handful of times since stepping back from royal life and is said to be remaining upbeat.

His ex-wife Sarah Ferguson, with whom he still lives, was all smiles when she arrived at Buckingham Palace yesterday while his daughters Beatrice and Eugenie have also been attending events.

A source told the Mail: ‘Despite everything the duke is getting a lot of support. The entrepreneurs he helps have been tremendously sweet and kind.

‘This really feels like a witch-hunt. He’d been hounded about this since 2011 non-stop. So he’s very positive about it
