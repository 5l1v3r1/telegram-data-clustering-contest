Katie Price completely botches ad on Twitter and unleashes fan's fury

Katie tagged the wrong company when promoting a florist on Twitter and a follower had some harsh words for her in the comments section

22 Nov 2019, 15:50 by Will Twigger, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/will-twigger/

Katie Price has utterly messed up an ad she was trying to do for a florist.

The former glamour model, 41, seems to have enraged a fan by tagging the wrong company in her Twitter post.

Katie posed in her tracksuit bottoms and hoodie on the sofa, admiring a lavishly curated bouquet of flowers.

She wrote: "#ad | Still receiving these beautiful flowers from @londonblooms. They send a unique selection of gorgeous flowers every week, and you can get started for just Â£11 per your first two boxes using code KATIE50."

@londonblooms isn't the Twitter handle of the company she meant, something that someone in the comments picked up on.

"That's a pretty s**t 'Ad'," they wrote, "Since she's STILL using the Twitter handle of an American jewellery maker.

"If she gets paid for these tweets then London Blooms should [ask] for their money back."

In the same ad on Instagram, she got the tag right.

Katie's no stranger to coming up against adversity online - she's even facing down trolls who've targeted eldest son Harvey.

In a video she shared with her Instagram followers, Katie filmed Harvey imploring trolls to stop their online bullying.

17 year old Harvey, who suffers from Prader-Willi Syndrome, is partially blind and autistic, said: "Please don't be horrible to Harvey."

Later, his mum prompted: "And what don't you want them to do?"

"Bully people," Harvey insisted.

She also took down a cruel troll last month who branded Harvey a "fat s**t" and her a "s**g."

"If anybody knows this person or works with this person," Katie wrote of the troll, who's profile said she was a health and social care nursing consultant, "I would question her job."

"This is exactly what she said about my son Harvey," Katie continued, "Name and shame her."

She was also attacked for her appearance following a radio interview, and for taking a picture of five year old daughter Bunny holding a Louis Vuitton bag.

She does have many adoring fans though, who often gush over Katie's skills as a parent, particularly in dealing with Harvey's condition.

"Beautiful picture of mother and son," one fan wrote on a recent throwback snap of Katie and Harvey.

Another said: "This photo is magical, soooo much love in those faces."
