Amitabh Bachchan is forever indebted to his fans for their love and affection

22 Nov 2019, 15:00 by Filmfare

The 50th edition of IFFI (International Film Festival Of India) was inaugurated by the legendary Amitabh Bachchan a few days ago in Goa. He was felicitated at the opening ceremony by superstar Rajinikanth. Bachchan gave a speech at the function crediting his success to his fans. He said, "I would like to thank my fans the most. In highs and lows, you have been with me and I have always been saying this -- I am in debt of your love and affection. I do not want to repay the debt, because I would like to keep it forever."

An Amitabh Bachchan Retrospective in which a set of his films like Sholay, Deewar, Piku, Bala and others was showcased at IFFI.

During an interaction with the audience present there, Big B mentioned that film as a medium transcends borders of language, colour caste and religion. "There are few mediums left like cinema in this fast-paced disintegrating world that can claim such integration.” He expressed hope that we continue to make films that will bring people together.

The veteran actor shot for his debut film, Saat Hindustani (1969) in Goa, where he played the role of one of the seven satyagrahis, who take on the Portuguese to free the coastal state.

He also shared his experience of visiting Goa. "Coming to Goa has always been like coming home. My very first film was shot here. And ever since then, I’ve had many opportunities to work in this wonderful place and receive the hospitality from the people of Goa during our various stints of shooting."
