Grace Millane's devastated parents' say lives are 'ripped apart' by her murder

British backpacker Grace Millane's body was found stuffed in a suitcase in New Zealand as parents pay tribute to 'talented and beautiful' girl

22 Nov 2019, 05:47 by Matthew Dresch, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/matthew-dresch/

Grace Millane's parents have paid tribute to their 'talented and beautiful' girl after a 27-year-old man was convicted of her murder.

Her dad David Millane delivered an heartbreaking speech to reporters outside Auckland High Court, following the unanimous verdict.

Speaking to the media as he stood next to his wife, Gillian, Mr Millane described his 22-year-old daughter as their 'sunshine' and said she did not deserve to be murdered in such a 'barbaric' way.

The British backpacker's parents have sat in court for the past three weeks listening to the harrowing details of their daughter's case.

They both broke down in tears - along with members of the jury - when the guilty verdict was announced today.

Speaking outside the court, David said: "The verdict of murder today will be welcomed by every member of the Millane family and friends of Grace.

"It will not reduce the pain and suffering we have had to endure over the past year. Grace was taken in the most brutal fashion a year ago and our lives have been ripped apart.

"This will be with us for the rest of our lives. Grace was a beautiful, talented, loving daughter. Grace was our sunshine and she will be missed forever. She did not deserve to be murdered in such a barbaric way on her gap year.

"We must return home and try and pick up the pieces of our lives... without our beloved Grace."

He thanked the people of New Zealand for 'opening their hearts' to Grace and her family.

Grace was killed when she returned to her murderer's hotel room in Auckland, New Zealand, last December.

Prosecutors claimed he deliberately strangled her, although he argued that Grace had died accidentally during rough sex.

Auckland High Court heard how the man Googled 'flesh-eating birds' and 'are there vultures in New Zealand' as he researched how to dispose of her body.

He then bought two suitcases and stuffed her body into one of them, before burying it in a shallow grave outside Auckland.

The defendant also arranged another Tinder date while Grace lay dead in his hotel room.

His defence team said he panicked after finding Grace dead in his room and buried her body because he did not think anyone would believe he was innocent of murder.

The jury at Auckland High Court pronounced their verdict after little more than five hours deliberations at the end of a three week trial.

At 5.45pm local time, they filed into the jury box.

The killer, whose identity remains protected, remained impassive before he was led away to the cells.

He was then brought back to the dock, wearing a dark suit and black open necked shirt, to be told by Judge Simon Moore that he would be sentenced on February 21 next year.
