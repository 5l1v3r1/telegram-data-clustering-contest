Brandon Lee Harris charged with manslaughter after shaking baby in 2018

22 Nov 2019, 05:22

A man who left a five-week-old baby blind and paralysed after shaking it has now been charged with manslaughter after the boy died in October.

Brandon Lee Harris, 23, was jailed for four years, with a non-parole period of 18 months, for shaking the child, leaving the five-week-old with critical injuries.

The baby died in August this year after being kept alive on life support.

Police have charged Harris with manslaughter.

Harris took the baby to Wallaroo Hospital in May 2018, telling doctors that the child had hit his head on the arm of a chair during a feed.

A doctor noted that the child “looked unwell” and had dilated pupils, irregular breathing and a bruise on his wrist.

The child was later treated at the Women’s and Children’s Hospital where a paediatrician found that the baby had suffered a “significant neurological impairment” and required resuscitation as a result of the heard trauma.

The doctor concluded that the injuries, which included haemorrhaging, swelling and bleeding on the brain, were likely caused by forceful shaking.

Harris admitted that he had twice shaken the baby because he would not settle at night.

He also said he had shoved a bottle into the baby’s mouth in frustration, possibly causing an injury to his tongue and lip as well as his collarbone.

The child was diagnosed with quadriplegia, epilepsy and clinical blindness as a result of the shaking and was kept alive on life support.

Harris was sentenced in July after pleading guilty to aggravated recklessly causing serious harm.

He was charged with manslaughter shortly after the child died in August.

He will face court in the new year.
