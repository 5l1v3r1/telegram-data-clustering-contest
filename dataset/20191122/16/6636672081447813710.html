Crowdfunding Watch: Mochi Robot provides LEGO-compatible, screenless coding for kids

22 Nov 2019, 16:14 by Alun Williams

This one has smashed its target on Kickstarter – the Mochi Robot that’s aimed at kids aged between 3 and 6. The big selling point, aside from being LEGO-compatible is that it supports a ‘screenless’ approach to learning coding.

If the significance of this passes you by, its true to say that a lot of STEM toys drive kids towards smartphones and tablets with their use of apps. Not a situation completely to be desired, as the said phones and iPads then self-defeatingly provide plenty more distracting and attention-grabbing diversions…

The aim of Mochi Robot, its creators say, is to teach children coding concepts while learning their ABCs and 123s and colours. And Mochi himself is a cute brown bear.

Using story books and maps, and control tokens and a control board, you determine the movement of the Bear-on-wheels around the maps, as part of the story.
