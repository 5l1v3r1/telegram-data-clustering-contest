After facing four break-ins over the past two weeks, this Scotswood charity needs your help

Scotswood Natural Community Garden is devastated after loosing thousands of pounds worth of tools and being left with the cost of repairs following several break ins

22 Nov 2019, 16:00 by Rachael Nichol

A charity has been left counting the cost after FOUR break-ins in just two weeks.

Thieves have repeatedly targeted Scotswood Natural Community Garden in Newcastle, stealing tools and leaving the charity with a repair bill of thousands of pounds.

Finding it difficult to continue their work due to lack of resources, members of the initiative are now pleading for help.

Sean Clay, the charity's manager, said: "It’s awful to feel that we are being targeted, we feel so vulnerable.

"Many people who come along to our garden are vulnerable and see it as a place of sanctuary. Now after it has been targeted it has really upset everyone."

Providing educational programmes, training and workshops to residents, the charity is at the heart of Scotswood’s community.

Starting in 1995, the project teaches thousands of people of all ages, abilities and backgrounds about the environment while having a positive impact on mental health and well-being.

The charity's first break in occurred two weeks ago, when sheds were raided and power tools, drills, sanders and multi-tools were taken.

After seeing what other tools were in the shed, two nights later the thieves returned and targeted the shed but failed to get in.

However, through their attempt to get inside they caused over £200 worth of damage to the shed.

Five nights later, the thieves came back with better tools and managed to successfully break into a tool shed, cut the locks off the other sheds and stole strimmers and other small power tools.

After getting new locks and paying for the damages, the charity believed that the thieves wouldn't come back.

To much devastation, last weekend the charity found the new locks cut off and their lawn mowers chippers were missing.

Robert Milne has been volunteering at the community garden for over a year and is shocked at the thieves actions.

The 30-year-old said: “It’s shocking that people can steal from such a lovely place. I am really disappointed.”

Following the break-ins the charity has found it difficult to fund fixing fences, locks, sheds and to replace tools.

With the garden being located on an isolated site, it means the charity is vulnerable to crimes.

However, local firm Armour Door Systems has offered to install security shutters and JCS Ltd has offered to install a CCTV system.

Northumbria Police has collected evidence and is trying to find those responsible.

PC John Loraine said: “This is a much-loved community garden that is the heartbeat of the neighbourhood in Scotswood.

“It is run by volunteers and they put on a host of events for local families and residents from the surrounding area.

“They have been targeted four times in less than three weeks and the value of the goods stolen and damage caused is now in excess of £1,000.

“Nobody has been arrested at this time but we are conducting extensive inquiries and have put a patrol plan in place in the area."
