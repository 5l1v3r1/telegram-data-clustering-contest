Albert Adomah - Why next five weeks are so vital to Nottingham Forest's promotion bid

The Reds return to Championship action at Bristol City on Saturday looking for their fifth away win of the season

22 Nov 2019, 16:41 by Barry Cooper

The next month could prove to be a crucial period in Nottingham Forest’s season, a campaign they hope will end in promotion to the Premier League.

That’s the verdict of Albert Adomah, who believes the busy period leading up to Christmas may will be a significant one in the context of the league season.

Now the final international break of the calendar year is out of the way, the Reds start their hectic run of 10 games in the space of five weeks at one of his former clubs, Bristol City on Saturday.

Like Forest, the Robins have designs of their own on seeking a top flight berth, with both clubs locked together on 28 points inside the top six and only separated by goal difference, the Ashton Gate meeting is a set to be a pulsating encounter.

“As players, you know November and December is when there are lots of games,” Adomah said.

“I think there is 10 games in less than five weeks and you look forward to the challenge as you want to play lots of games.”

Bristol City are first up followed by a trip to QPR on Wednesday night, before Cardiff visit the City Ground next Saturday.

A Friday night jaunt to Millwall is in the diary on December 6 and then Reds fans can look forward to a home double header against Middlesbrough and Sheffield Wednesday.

Trips to Huddersfield and Hull either side of Christmas are followed by a home clash with Wigan to round off 2019 before Blackburn arrive on Trentside to see in the New Year.

“This is when you can make a big difference; if you get more points in these months then you will be up there,” said the experienced winger.

“For us, we are in the top five now so we just want to maintain that and get as many points as we can.

“We are in a good position at the moment but we are not taking anything for granted.

“We will take each game as it comes and the more points that we can get, the better and hopefully we can stay up there too.”
