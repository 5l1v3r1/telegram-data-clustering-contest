The week in wildlife – in pictures

22 Nov 2019, 16:27 by Eric Hilaire

The pick of the best flora and fauna photos from around the world, including foraging sparrows and a swimming beaver

A crested ibis flies over the Muma River in Hanzhong, north-west China’s Shaanxi province. Crested ibises, with their red crests and long black beaks, were thought to be extinct in the country until seven wild birds were observed in Shaanxi in 1981, a discovery that prompted captive breeding and enhanced protection of the species

Photograph: Tao Ming/Barcroft Media

A young nutria rat at the Mönchbruch nature reserve in Kelsterbach, western Germany

Photograph: Dorothee Barth/DP/AFP/Getty

Sparrows forage in a park in Bole, Xinjiang, China

Photograph: CostFoto/Barcroft Media

Fly agarics grow next to autumn-coloured plants in Ruppertstal, Germany

Photograph: Soeren Stache/DPA-Zentralbild/ZB

The National Trust is releasing beavers into large enclosures at two sites in the south of England next spring to bolster the region’s wildlife and help combat flooding

Photograph: David Chapman/PA

Common seal pups Buckbeak and Pixie have been released back into the wild near Sutton Bridge in Lincolnshire, UK, after being nursed to full health at an RSPCA sanctuary in Norfolk. Pixie was found in Happisburgh village, Norfolk, with puncture wounds around her mouth and muzzle after being attacked by a dog. Buckbeak was orphaned at Burnham-on-Crouch in Essex

Photograph: Joe Giddens/PA

Two grasshoppers mating at night in Spain. Scientists say light pollution is a significant but often overlooked driver of the rapid decline of insect populations

Photograph: Pedro Luna/Alamy

A lineated barbet (right) clashes with a jungle myna in the Morigaon district of Assam, India

Photograph: EPA

An injured koala receives treatment after being rescued from a bushfire at the Port Macquarie koala hospital, Australia

Photograph: Tao Shelan/VCG/Getty Images

A domestic reindeer, a species increasingly threatened by warming temperatures in Mongolia

Photograph: Julia Clark/Flinders University/PA

Egrets search for food in the polluted Brahmaputra River in Guwahati, India

Photograph: Anuwar Ali Hazarika/Barcroft Media

A recently emerged adult female western glacier stonefly in Glacier National Park, Montana. Warming temperatures are melting the glaciers and year-round snowfields the species relies on

Photograph: Joe Giersch/AP

Kangaroo survivors of a bushfire graze at Wollemi National Park in New South Wales, Australia. Rising temperatures and hot winds are again expected to fan wildfires in the state next week

Photograph: Jeremy Piper/EPA

A cape vulture eats a chick at the International Centre for Birds of Prey, a conservation centre in Newent, Gloucestershire

Photograph: Ben Birchall/PA

Kalmyk camels in a pasture in Yustinsky district, Russia. The Polynny farm is home to 400 camels, the largest population of the species in Kalmykia

Photograph: Valery Matytsin/TASS

A mass coral spawning has begun on Australia’s Great Barrier Reef, with early indications the annual event could be among the biggest in recent years

Photograph: Pablo Cogollos/Sunlover Moore Reef Marine Base/AFP/Getty Images

Gelada monkeys watch as the sun sets over the Simien Mountains, Ethiopia. A third of plant species in Africa are threatened with extinction, a study says. Ethiopia, west Africa, central Tanzania, and southern Democratic Republic of the Congo have a worryingly high proportion (40%) of potentially threatened species

Photograph: Giedrius Stakauskas/Golden Turtle/Bav Media

A wedge-tailed eagle walks at Alice Springs Desert Park, Australia. Conservationists have called for a Victorian landowner to face charges under the Wildlife Act after he admitted to killing 420 wedge-tailed eagles in 18 months

Photograph: Samantha Ohlsen/Alamy

Workers dump dying birds in a truck at Sambhar Salt Lake in India’s northern state of Rajasthan. Thousands of migratory birds were found dead recently from suspected botulism

Photograph: Himanshu Sharma/AFP via Getty Images

Hunter, a young Belgian malinois, keeps an eye on Jazz, a nine-day-old giraffe, at the Rhino Orphanage in the Limpopo province of South Africa. Jazz, who was brought in after being abandoned at birth by her mother, is being looked after at the orphanage and has been befriended by Hunter and his sibling Duke

Photograph: Jérôme Delay/AP
