Community officers praised after mum and baby rescued from floodwater in Kirkby-in-Ashfield

The three CPOs have been praised for their actions

22 Nov 2019, 16:11 by Phoebe Ram

Community protection officers (CPO) in Kirkby-in-Ashfield have been praised for their quick thinking in rescuing residents trapped by floodwater.

As rain battered Nottinghamshire on Thursday, November 14, many roads were affected by flooding.

On the Thursday evening around 5pm three Ashfield District Council CPOs rescued two residents trapped in their cars on Mill Lane in Kirkby-in-Ashfield.

While on patrol they came across the flooded area where water was approximately three foot deep.

One resident was stuck in her car in the water and the two CPOS managed to get the resident to safety.

A colleague was called to assist and Nottinghamshire Police were contacted to block the road.

During this time another resident drove into the flood water.

The car was submerged up to the windows with a woman and her 17-month-old baby trapped inside.

The CPOs rescued both from the car and got them to the safety of their patrol car until they could be transported home.

They then returned and recovered the car after the floodwater had gone down.

Theresa Hodgkinson, director of place and communities at Ashfield District Council, said: “The quick thinking of the CPOs saved those residents from getting into real trouble.

"They showed courage and acted on instinct. All three are a credit to Ashfield District Council, we are extremely proud of them and thankful for their actions.”
