Tranmere Rovers boss Micky Mellon sends message ahead of Fleetwood Town clash

Tranmere Rovers take on Fleetwood Town in League One this weekend

22 Nov 2019, 16:24 by Tom Beattie

Tranmere Rovers manager Micky Mellon feels his side’s incredible FA Cup first round victory can spur the club on against Fleetwood Town.

Rovers overcame league leaders Wycombe Wanderers on Wednesday with a 2-1 extra time win despite being reduced to 10 men.

Mellon said: “We had to show great character, [just as] Wycombe had to do when they came to Prenton Park. It was a sort of role reversal.

"When the questions got asked the players answered. So it was really pleasing.

“We’re trying to keep things moving forward and as a club [the result] will certainly do no harm.”

This weekend’s opponents Fleetwood are currently 8th in League One but have the advantage of games in hand on the sides above them.

The Fylde Coast outfit’s boss Joey Barton has called for Mellon to receive a hero’s welcome ahead of his return to the club he led into the Football League for the first time in their history, winning the Conference National in 2011/12.

However, the Rovers manager was quick to point out that sentiment will not enter his mind as he prepares to face his former employers.

Mellon said: “It was a time in my managerial career that I made so many friends and had such an incredible time.

"To have the moments we did as a team was fantastic and will stay with us forever.

“But I’m with Tranmere now and it will be about making sure that my players are ready.”

He added: “I respect the club that Fleetwood is and the great job that Joey [Barton] is doing. I know a lot of them there. There are still people there from my time there.

“I’m an experienced manager and I think you have to keep your focus on the job at hand rather than get into competing against people.”

Mellon insisted his preparation for this match will not differ despite his links with his old club.

He said: “It’s just the next game. We look at their strengths, we look at how we can nullify them. We look at areas maybe we can get them in.

“I look forward to the challenge, absolutely."

Despite the performance in midweek, Mellon acknowledged that the club are still developing and getting used to the intensity of League One following back-to-back promotions. And he was quick to keep expectations at realistic levels ahead of the clash against Fleetwood.

Mellon said: “[Fleetwood] have been at this level longer than us so they’re a wee bit more experienced than us.

“We’re growing as a group all the time. Always representing this fantastic club really well and [Saturday] will be no different.

"So it should make for an interesting afternoon for all of us.”
