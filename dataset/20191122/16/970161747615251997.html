Tories hold solid lead over Labour with 20 days until General Election

22 Nov 2019, 16:29

The Conservative Party has increased its lead to 14 points over Labour as the December 12 General Election approaches.

Jeremy Corbyn’s party is straggling behind on 29 points, according to an average of polls over the past week.

The Tories have gained an extra percentage point over their main rivals over the past three days.

Meanwhile the Liberal Democrats have gained an extra point, sitting at 15 per cent, while the Brexit Party has dropped from six to four points.

The Green Party has been flat-lining at three per cent and all other parties are at six.

Today’s figures come after the Liberal Democrats, Labour and the Green Party revealed their election manifestos.

Corbyn has divided opinion with his radical taxation and spending plans, which Chancellor Sajid Javid brands ‘totally unsustainable’, despite claims they are fully costed.

Lib Dem leader Jo Swinson promised to stop Brexit altogether if they win and vowed to build 300,000 homes a year by 2024.

The Conservatives are still yet to reveal when their manifesto will be unveiled, but Treasury Chief Secretary Rishi Sunak gave assurances it would happen ‘very shortly’.

Rejecting the traditional manifesto, Nigel Farage launched a ‘contract with the people’ this morning, including a 50 per cent cut in the foreign aid budget and a ‘clean-break Brexit’.
