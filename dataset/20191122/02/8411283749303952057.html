ATrack lands GPS orders from Japan

22 Nov 2019, 02:35 by Max Wang, Taipei; Adam Hwang, DIGITIMES

GPS car-use tracking device maker ATrack Technology has disclosed it has become an ODM for a major Japan-based maker of automotive air-conditioning systems.

ATrack will provide a customized GPS tacking device for the client to monitor operating conditions of its refrigerator vans' air-conditioning systems as a basis for predicative maintenance, ATrack said.

The customized device is being tested, and the testing will complete in first-quarter 2020, ATrack said, adding the device will also be introduced to the Hong Kong and Singapore markets.

ATrack posted consolidated revenues of NT$55.1 million (US$1.8 million), gross margin of 40.74%, net operating loss of NT$9.0 million, net profit of NT$5.7 million and net EPS of NT$0.17 for third-quarter 2019, leading to consolidated revenues of NT$174.8 million, gross margin of 38.50%, net operating loss of NT$26.3 million, net profit of NT$4.6 million and net EPS of NT$0.14 for January-September.

4G-based models accounted for nearly 50% of the third-quarter revenues. North America was the largest market, taking up 48.6% of the consolidated revenues.

ATrack has reported consolidated revenues of NT$28.6 million for October, the highest monthly level since August 2017 with increases of 138.55% sequentially and 26.69% on year, and those of NT$203.4 million for January-October grew 21.98% on year.
