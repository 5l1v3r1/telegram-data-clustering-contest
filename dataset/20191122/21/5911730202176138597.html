Student accused of repeatedly saying he will 'shoot up' Gibbs High School

22 Nov 2019, 21:49 by WFTS Digital Staff

ST. PETERSBURG, Fla. -- Police arrested a 10th grader for threatening to shoot up a high school in St. Petersburg on Friday.

St. Pete police responded to a call of a threat at Gibbs High School. The student, a 16-year-old boy repeatedly told students and adults on campus that he would "shoot up the whole school."

The student was charged with making a false report concerning the use of a firearm/bomb to conduct bodily hard.

No one was hurt in the threats.
