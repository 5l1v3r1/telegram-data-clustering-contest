Deontay Wilder puts legacy on line in heated Luis Ortiz rematch

22 Nov 2019, 21:34 by George Willis

There were plenty of skeptics when Deontay Wilder first became a serious contender for the heavyweight championship. He started boxing at an advanced age, he hadn’t fought quality competition and he was too unorthodox. He won a bronze medal at the 2008 Olympics, but no one was paying attention.

Fast forward a decade, and Wilder has become instrumental in reviving interest in a division that had been forgotten. He will be making the 10th defense of his WBC heavyweight championship Saturday night in a rematch with Luis “King Kong” Ortiz at the MGM Grand Garden Arena in Las Vegas and on FOX Sports pay-per-view.

Leo Santa Cruz seeks a title in a fourth weight division when he faces Miguel Flores for the WBA “Super” super featherweight championship in the featured undercard bout.

Andy Ruiz holds the other major belts in the heavyweight division after his stunning upset of Anthony Joshua in June at Madison Square Garden, and Tyson Fury is considered the lineal champion, having never lost a title in the ring. But the 34-year-old Wilder (41-0-1, 40 KOs) is the most established of them all, looking to add to his legacy with a second victory over Ortiz (31-1, 26 KOs)

“It’s a great accomplishment for myself and for my career,” Wilder said of making his milestone 10th defense. “It’s a great honor, especially when many thought I was never going to be the heavyweight champion. It’s amazing to be at this point in time, and right now all I could do for my career is just rack up numbers. I have always fought for a legend’s legacy, and I’m still fighting to this day for that. I just want to be the best in the world. I want to be the best that has ever done it.”

Wilder handed Ortiz the first loss of his career on March 3, 2018, at Barclays Center. After nearly being knocked out in the seventh round by the hard fists of Ortiz, Wilder rallied to stop the Cuban in a dramatic 10th round.
