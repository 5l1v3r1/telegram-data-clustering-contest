Three semi-trucks collide on highway near Estevan

Three people were sent to hospital after three semi trucks were involved in a collision on near the village of  Roche Percee on Thursday. 

22 Nov 2019, 21:47 by Regina Leader-Post

Three people were sent to hospital after three semi trucks were involved in a collision on Highway 39 near the village of Roche Percee on Thursday.

According to an RCMP news release, the crash happened at approximately 4:08 a.m. when a semi-truck travelling from North Portal jack knifed. Soon after, a second semi-truck driving southeast collided with the truck and split the trailer from the tractor.

Minutes after the collision, a third semi-truck travelling southeast collided with the second truck, pushing it into a ditch. The third semi-truck then caught fire.

All three drivers and a single passenger were quickly located. Two of the drivers and the passenger were transported to hospital in Estevan and released later that morning.

Police said road conditions at the time were icy and rutted, and visibility was poor due to fog. The crash is still under investigation.

Roche Percee is located approximately 19 kilometres southeast of Estevan.
