BHU: Students to end protest against Muslim prof in Sanskrit dept

The protesters feel only a Hindu can teach Sanskrit at BHU. But they've decided to end their agitation after meeting the vice-chancellor, who they say has pledged corrective measures.

22 Nov 2019, 03:08

HIGHLIGHTS

  • BHU professor Firoze Khan hasn't been able to take classes
  • BHU chancellor, top netas, some students defend him
  • V-C pledged corrective measures within 10 days: Protesters

Students protesting the appointment of a Muslim professor in Banaras Hindu University's Sanskrit department will end their demonstration today.

They reached their decision after meeting BHU Vice-Chancellor Rakesh Bhatnagar, who they say pledged corrective measures within 10 days.

BHU's Chancellor, Giridhar Malviya (the grandson of BHU founder Madan Mohan Malviya), and a number of senior politicians have defended the professor, Firoze Khan.

Sanskrit "has vastness", Congress leader Priyanka Gandhi Vadra said on Thursday.

"The Constitution of our country has vastness. Any teacher can teach Sanskrit in a university," she said.

The protesters, on the other hand, say only a Hindu can do so.

But on Wednesday, some students marched on campus in support of Firoze Khan, who has so far been unable to take classes.
