'Attention' - Liverpool star blasts Manchester City boss over diving accusation

Aston Villa latest news | Etihad Stadium boss made the comments about Sadio Mane's dive in the 2-1 win at Villa Park on Saturday after his side's defeat of Southampton

07 Nov 2019, 07:23 by Richard Cusack

Liverpool star Sadio Mane has hit back a Manchester City boss Pep Guardiola after being accused of diving by the Barcelona legend.

Guardiola bizarrely rounded on Mane on Saturday evening in his post-match press conference after watching his side come from 1-0 down to beat Southampton 2-1 at the Etihad Stadium.

Mane and Liverpool had been in action at the same time, beating Aston Villa by the same scoreline at Villa Park.

It was a similar situation in B6, Mane's side behind before storming back to grab the three points late on.

The Senegal international crossed in for Andy Robertson's headed equaliser before bundling in Trent Alexander-Arnold's corner four minutes into added time.

There was controversy surrounding Mane in the clash because he was booked for simulation, leading to Guardiola to accuse him of diving.

Critics have said Guardiola was trying to get into Mane and Liverpool's heads in the build-up to the Premier League clash between the two sides on Sunday, with City six points behind Jurgen Klopp's league leaders.

But Mane says he takes no notice.

 

“I think it's a bit clever from him to get the attention of the referee but I will just play my football like I'm always doing," he says.

"I don't pay attention to what he's saying because it's part of football.

“It doesn't make anything for me.

"Even though I saw him commenting about it, what he said, for me, I think it's positive!”
