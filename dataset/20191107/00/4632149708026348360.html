Things to do today in Bengaluru

07 Nov 2019, 00:30 by Bangalore Mirror Bureau

Save the snow leopard
Charudutt Mishra, science and conservation director at The Snow Leopard Trust, will be in town to deliver a talk today. He will cover aspects of the work that go into science-based conservation of these rare animals. Plus, he will elaborate on the new threats that have emerged in the recent past and the acute need for green development in the areas where snow leopards are found.

WHERE: Bangalore International Centre, 4th Main Road, Domlur II Stage WHEN: 6.30pm-8pm, November 7 ENTRY: Free CALL: 9886599675

Watch a film
Written and directed by Chaintanya Tamhane, Court is an engaging courtroom drama, about a trial of a social activist who is accused of inciting a sewage worker’s
suicide. It gives an insight into the harsh realities and biases that underscore the judicial system. The film was also India’s official entry for the 88th Academy Awards.

WHERE: 7/1, Edward Road, off Queens Rd, Vasanth Nagar
WHEN: 6.30pm onwards, November 7 ENTRY: champaca.in

Health first
Eating healthy is all the rage today and for good reason. To shed light on all the dos and don’t of healthy eating, Shubha Dharmana, a nutritionist and cosmetologist, will host a talk. She will recommend a diet that is based on the principles of anti-ageing medicine that cuts out all processed foods, refined sugars, carbohydrates and replaces them with healthy, home-cooked meals with lots of fruits and vegetables.

WHERE: Phalada Pure & Sure Cafe, 7th Main Rd, 4th Block, Jayanagar WHEN: 3pm-5pm, November 7 CALL: 9538191551

Candle light veg dinners
Are you looking for a multi-cuisine, vegetarian restaurant to go out to? Look no further than Paakashala, a chain started by restaurateur KN Vasudev Adiga. It’s a big plus that they have six outlets in the city and the next is coming up along Kanakapura Road. Each outlet offers something different – the RR Nagar is a fine diner and will start candle light dinners on Tuesdays from now on, whereas their Chandra Layout, Malleshwaram and Vidyaranyapura branches are QSR formats.

WHERE: RR Nagar, Chandra Layout, Malleshwaram, Vidyaranyapura, JP Nagar, MG Road
CALL: 28604460

FIVESIXZERO is back
Wondering what’s that? Inspired by the pin code of Bengaluru, FiveSixZero (FSZ) was launched in 2016 to celebrate the city’s status of being the ‘music capital of India’, and since then, it’s attracted many thousands to the festival ground.

The good news is, it’s back for the third time and will be held in association with Bangalore Mirror. Over three days, it will celebrate every facet of Indian dance music with an intersection of global dance music, and will explore aspects of space, history, communities, talent, uprisings and more, all through the lens of the music industry. It will feature a two-day-long music summit and after-parties. And of course, the big music festival night, which is divided into three parts.

The ‘Main Stage’ will be dedicated to electronic music and will be headlined by Krewella and Nucleya, and supported by Third Party, Justin Oh, Tom Tyger, TANK, and BOHB. ‘De:Vision – The Underground Stage’, will cover the underground side of techno, deep-house and tech-house music. This will be headlined by Shal O Cin and supported by Anytrixx, Mia Mendi, Audio Units, Answer After Hours, and Dramatiq. The third one, called ‘Homegrown Stage’, will give a platform to the famous DJs of Bengaluru, such as Ivan, Martin D Souza, Shine, Rishab Chabra, Ashtrix, and Roan.


And if you aren’t convinced already, let Nocturnal Networks, the curator of FSZ, an initiative of Times Red Cell, tell you, “With the summit being a fulcrum to get the music industry discussing its opportunities and challenges with the global diaspora, the fest is touted to be the first-of-its kind in India”.

WHERE: The Lalit Ashok, Kumarakrupa Road WHEN: November 22-24 ENTRY: bookmyshow.com DETAILS: fivesixzero.in
