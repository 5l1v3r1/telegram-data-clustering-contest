20 photographs of the week

09 Nov 2019, 08:26 by Jim Powell

Day of the Dead in Mexico, protests in Chile and Iraq, South Africa celebrate winning the Rugby World Cup and the New York marathon – the best photography in news, culture and sport from around the world this week

Monroe, Louisiana

Supporters of Donald Trump listen as he speaks during a campaign rally at the Monroe civic centre.

Photograph: Evan Vucci/AP

County Armagh, Northern Ireland

Boris Johnson helps quality control workers during a general election campaign visit to the Tayto Castle crisp factory in County Armagh.

Photograph: Daniel Leal-Olivas/AFP/Getty

Yokohama, Japan

A bloodied and dejected Billy Vunipola reacts after England’s defeat by South Africa in the Rugby World Cup final.

Photograph: Tom Jenkins/The Guardian

Soweto, South Africa

South Africa captain Siya Kolisi holds up the Web Ellis trophy as the Rugby World Cup winners parade on an open top bus in Soweto.

Photograph: Michele Spatari/AFP/Getty

Melbourne, Australia

A general view of the Macca’s Run during Melbourne Cup day at Flemington racecourse.

Photograph: Scott Barbour/EPA

Eagle Butte, South Dakota

A Lakota boy places a sacred staff on a horse during a ride to meet Brad Upton, descendant of the commander of the Wounded Knee massacre on the Cheyenne River reservation in Eagle Butte.

Photograph: Stephanie Keith/Reuters

Baghdad, Iraq

Anti-government protestors start fires, while security forces fire teargas in Baghdad. Tens of thousands of protesters gathered in Baghdad and across southern Iraq calling for the overhaul of the political system established after the US-led 2003 invasion.

Photograph: Hadi Mizban/AP

Baghdad, Iraq

A protester rides a tuk-tuk during clashes with riot police forces near Al-Ahrar bridge in central Baghdad.

Photograph: Murtaja Lateef/EPA

Darbasiyah, Syria

A Kurdish man tosses his shoes at a Turkish military vehicle on patrol in the countryside of Syria’s north-eastern Hasakeh province. Turkey started joint patrols with Russia in northern Syria to verify whether Kurdish forces have withdrawn from a key border zone in compliance with a deal reached between the two governments.

Photograph: Delil Souleiman/AFP/Getty

Santiago, Chile

A riot police officer reacts having been engulfed in flames after a series of molotov cocktails were thrown in the direction of the police during protests in Santiago.

Photograph: Jorge Silva/Reuters

Santiago, Chile

A dog barks as demonstrators cover themselves against water cannons used on them by riot police during clashes in Santiago.

Photograph: Martin Bernetti/AFP/Getty

Hong Kong

A riot police officer detains a protester at a shopping mall in Tai Po.

Photograph: Kim Kyung Hoon/Reuters

New York City

Runners cross the Verrazano-Narrows bridge during the New York City marathon.

Photograph: Johannes Eisele/AFP/Getty

Oaxaca, Mexico

A group of people dressed up for the Day of the Dead parade pose in Oaxaca.

Photograph: Cristopher Rogel Blanquet/Getty

Bavispe, Mexico

Members of the Lebaron family mourn near the car where nine family members were burned and killed during an ambush in Bavispe. US President Donald Trump offered to help Mexico ‘wage war’ on its cartels after three women and six children from an American Mormon community were murdered in an area notorious for drug trafficking.

Photograph: Hérika Martínez/AFP/Getty

Moscow

Soldiers are seen through a truck window as they take part in a military parade in Red Square as part of the ceremonies marking the 78th anniversary of the 1941 historical procession, when Red Army soldiers marched past the Kremlin walls towards the frontline to fight Nazi Germany troops during the second world war.

Photograph: Dimitar Dilkoff/AFP/Getty Images

Setermoen, Norway

Norwegian soldiers stand guard in front of a temporary command centre camp during ‘reindeer 2’, a Norwegian-US military drill in Setermoen.

Photograph: Stoyan Nenov/Reuters

Pibor, South Sudan

A displaced woman looks at her child, who is hiding behind her dress, in a school now occupied by internally displaced people after heavy rains and floods forced hundreds of thousands of people to leave their homes in the town of Pibor.

Photograph: Andreea Câmpeanu/Reuters

Nice, France

Clouds pass over the Mediterranean sea in the French Riviera city of Nice.

Photograph: Valéry Hache/AFP/Getty

Rotherham, England

A member of the fire and rescue service wades through flood water as he passes an abandoned car in Rotherham, after over a month’s worth of rain fell in a day on parts of England.

Photograph: Oli Scarff/AFP/Getty

Tultepec, Mexico

The bones of at least 14 mammoths, who would have lived more than 14,000 years ago, have been found in what is believed to be the first find of a mammoth trap set by humans.

Photograph: National Institute of Anthropology/AFP/Getty Images
