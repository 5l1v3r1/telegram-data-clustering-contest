New UUP leader attacks Boris Johnson over ‘sell-out’ Brexit deal

09 Nov 2019, 12:44

The new Ulster Unionist leader has accused Boris Johnson of proposing a “sell-out” Brexit deal.

The British Prime Minister and his DUP allies made “false promises” over the UK’s departure from the EU, Steve AIken added.

No unionist voted for England, Scotland and Wales to leave but Northern Ireland to stay in the bloc, he added.

The proposed deal would involve Northern Ireland following some of the rules of the EU’s single market to avoid a hard border on the island of Ireland but including a new regime for goods crossing the Irish Sea.
