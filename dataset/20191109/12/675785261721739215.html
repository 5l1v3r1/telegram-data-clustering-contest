UK weather: Woman swept away by floodwater was High Sheriff appointed by Queen

Annie Hall, a former High Sheriff of Derbyshire who was appointed to the ceremonial position by the Queen, was swept away in floodwater in Rowsley and found two miles away

09 Nov 2019, 12:49 by Chris Kitching, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/chris-kitching/

A businesswoman who died after being swept away in raging floodwater was a former High Sheriff of Derbyshire.

Mum Annie Hall was appointed to the position, which dates back more than 1,000 years, by the Queen in 2017 and was very active in the county for decades.

She was swept away in the rain-swollen and debris-filled River Derwent in Rowsley, near Matlock, in the early hours of Friday and her body was reportedly found about two miles downstream in Darley Dale.

Mrs Hall's devastated family, including her husband Michael and children, said in a statement: “It is with great sadness that we, the family of Annie Hall, report her sudden passing.

“We are in great shock and grieving. Please allow us to mourn privately for the time being.

 

“If you would like to contact us, please send any messages to condolencesforhallfamily@hotmail.com. We will publish details of the funeral arrangements when they are known.”

Mrs Hall, who lived in Ashford in the Water in the Peak District, had run a number of businesses and was involved in a number of local charities.

Derbyshire Police Chief Constable Peter Goodman said: “I am shocked and deeply saddened by the untimely and tragic death of my friend, and former High Sheriff, Annie Hall.

“Annie was a great leader in Derbyshire in both industry and on the civic front. She will be hugely missed.

“My deepest sympathies go to Michael and all the family at this time.”

A spokesperson for the force added: "Emergency services were called to the River Derwent in Darley Dale, near Matlock, in the early hours of Friday, 8 November to reports that a woman had been swept away by the water.

"Tragically a body was found further downstream a short time later.

"Mrs Hall served as the county’s High Sheriff in 2017, and struck up a close bond with our Chief Constable Peter Goodman during her tenure."

Originally from Wincanton, Somerset, Mrs Hall was sworn into the non-political role in April 2017, serving for a year until a new sheriff took over from her.

At the time, she pledged to promote Derbyshire to the rest of the UK and the interests of the county’s "incredible" women and people who were doing great work.

When she was younger she began training to be a psychiatric nurse, but she and her husband at the time moved around, ending up in Derbyshire, and set up a business making industrial winches, according to a May 2017 profile by Derbyshire Life.

Mrs Hall told the publication they arrived "with no money, no business qualifications and frankly, no plan".

She added: "But I had married someone who was an entrepreneur and I always say my role was the sweeper.

"He would have the idea and I would look at it to see if it would work – it couldn’t be a leap into the unknown."

The business, Northern Industrial and Marine Services, later became NIM Group.

The business ran for 20 years until it was sold it to a competitor in 1994 and a new company called Pickup Holdings was established.

Mrs Hall was chairman of Pickup Holdings, which ran a plastic coating business and a marina, and manufactured luxury narrowboats.

Her son and daughter, David and Joanna, eventually took over the company.

The businesswoman was the first female president of the Derbyshire Chamber of Commerce, a founding member of the Training Enterprise Council, a non-executive director and vice-chairman of Derbyshire Mental Health Trust, and a Foundation Derbyshire trustee.

The Hall Family Fund helps community groups working in rural areas or groups addressing rural issues.

Members of the emergency services, mountain rescuers and a Coastguard helicopter were involved in the search and rescue mission.

As the county paid tribute to Mrs Hall, Derby Cathedral tweeted: "Shocked and saddened by this tragic news. Annie Hall was Chair of Derby Cathedral Council and a good friend to the whole Cathedral community. Our prayers are with her and her family as they grieve."

Mrs Hall was swept away as more than a month's worth of rain fell in Yorkshire and the Midlands and rivers surged to record levels, inundating homes and leaving cars submerged.

The heaviest rainfall was at Swineshaw in the Peak District, which had 112mm in 24 hours.

Parts of Sheffield recorded 85mm.

Hundreds of homes were evacuated and more than 100 residents were rescued by firefighters in South Yorkshire.

Severe flood warnings remained in place in Doncaster on Saturday, along with dozens of warnings and alerts across Yorkshire and the Midlands.
