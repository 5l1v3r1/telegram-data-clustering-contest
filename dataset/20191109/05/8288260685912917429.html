House on Benefits Street up for sale for £100k - and it's a right tip

You can now bag yourself a bargain terrace on the road that was thrust into the spotlight during a Channel 4 reality show that made residents unlikely celebrities

09 Nov 2019, 05:30 by Cathrina Hulse-BM

It's the famous road that made unlikely stars of residents including White Dee, Fungi and Smoggy.

Now a buyer with cash to spare and forward vision can snap up a two-bedroom terrace on James Turner Street in Winson Green for offers over £100,000.

But there's a snag - the house is in a complete and utter state.

The road hit the headlines in 2014 when Benefits Street burst onto our screens.

The reality TV shone the spotlight on the lives of people living in James Turner Street. It showed the struggles of Brummies living on the poverty line but also revealed the camaraderie of residents.

Now potential buyers have the chance to by their very own home on Benefits Street after details were posted on the Rightmove website. 

Yet from the wild, overgrown rear garden to the grubby, stained bathroom, this property needs plenty of TLC.

The end terrace has two bedrooms, a lounge, dining room, kitchen and a downstairs bathroom.

Take a look below to see the keenly priced property in all its glory.

Lounge

 

The wallpaper is peeling and the room has been used as a bedroom with a double bed still in situ.

If the new owner can look past the old-fashioned fireplace, battered chests of drawers and rubbish littering the frayed carpet, the room is a decent size.

Kitchen

 

A renovation nightmare! An off-white paint adorns the walls of the typical terrace-type kitchen which has some built in cupboards and black worktop.

There is a radiator, cupboards and a sink. But there is clutter galore, with the kitchen littered with plates, food and rubbish.

Downstairs bathroom

 

The kitchen leads through to the downstairs bathroom which, again, needs a good paint job.

For your money, you will inherit a filthy bath - and the room is in need of another BIG clean-up. 

Dining room

 

This main room is filled with furniture and clutter including a black chair, table and black bags.

The ceiling is peeling and the room feels in need of a proper makeover.

Bedroom One

Holes in walls and a missing door.

This is the kind of bedroom that could give you nightmares!

But size is a bonus.

Rear garden

Another case of DIY SOS!

Overgrown and with carpets and chairs dumped in it, this garden is also in need of serious TLC.

Yet it does have potential and is a nice size.
