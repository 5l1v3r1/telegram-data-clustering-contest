Adama Traore interview

Adama Traore was doomed from the very start at Aston Villa. 

A 19-year-old boy with limited English was plucked from Barcelona - the only club he’d ever known - and expected to hit the ground running alongside 10 other new signings. 

He didn’t and a perception of Traore was created which the winger has been unable to shake. Until now.  

A rip-roaring six months at Middlesbrough led Traore to Wolves and his career is taking off under Nuno Espirito Santo.

That decisive cross for Raul Jimenez’s winner against Slovan Bratislava on Thursday was Traore’s ninth direct goal contribution (two goals, seven assists) of what’s been an electric season so far. 

Villa fans aren’t familiar with this souped-up version of Traore. Many of the 3,000 Villans at Molineux on Sunday will remember a novice with raw speed and power that he couldn’t control. 

But Traore, now a man and captivating a room full of journalists, isn’t fussed about proving them wrong.

“I don’t think I have to prove anything to anyone,” says the 23-year-old. “I’m not thinking about anything outside of our team or what other people think. 

“I have to prove it to the manager, Nuno, who has trusted me, and the team and myself.”

A surprise transfer link

James Rodriguez is reportedly on the trade block with Real Madrid eyeing Wolverhampton Wanderers midfielder Ruben Neves.

Reports in the Spanish press say Manchester United's Paul Pogba and Tottenham Hotspur's Christian Eriksen are also of interest to Zinedine Zidane's side.

Rodriguez, who spent last season on loan at Bundesliga giants Bayern Munich, has started just five matches in all competitions this season.

He hasn't featured at all since October 22, the 2014 summer signing out of the picture under Zidane who is in his second spell in charge at the Santiago Bernabeu.

He was brought to the club from AS Monaco on the back of an excellent World Cup campaign with Colombia, for a fee reported to be in the region of £63 million.

Neves is a key cog in Nuno Espirito Santo's Wolves machine, making 101 appearances since his £15.8m move from FC Porto in 2017.

Nuno's Villa promise

Nuno Espirito Santo has promised Wolverhampton Wanderers’ supporters that his players understand the meaning of Sunday’s derby clash with Aston Villa.

A section of Wolves fans were frustrated with Nuno’s team selection in the 2-1 Carabao Cup defeat to Villa last week and are keen for revenge this weekend.

And Nuno will go all out at Molineux with Premier League points the order of the day after Wolves edged closer to the Europa League knockout stages on Thursday.

“We’re going to work and start thinking about Villa,” Nuno said. “It’s a big game, here at Molineux, a big derby. We are going to be ready for it.

“We played (Villa) already - we know about it already. We are the same people, we know what it means.”

Prior to this season Nuno and Villa head coach Dean Smith faced each other twice in 2017/18 when the latter was in charge of Brentford. Smith’s Bees team held Wolves to a point at Griffin Park before being walloped 3-0 at Molineux.

And Nuno is full of admiration for the job Smith has done since taking charge at Villa Park last October.

“Fantastic - he’s done an amazing job. We played against Dean in the Championship and he’s a very good manager.”
