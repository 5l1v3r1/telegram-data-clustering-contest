KSI vs Logan Paul: Fight time, pay-per-view details and live stream ahead of YouTube boxing match

YouTube superstars KSI and Logan Paul clash for a second time when they meet in the professional ring in Los Angeles

09 Nov 2019, 07:00 by Martin Domin, https://www.facebook.com/mirrorfighting

The waiting is over and in a few short hours KSI and Logan Paul will settle their beef once and for all.

The YouTube stars first fought in Manchester over a year ago but the stakes have been raised significantly tonight.

The pair have turned professional for the rematch in Los Angeles which means they have dispensed with the headguards and will pull on 10oz gloves.

Both men have promised to knock the other out and send them into social media retirement at the famed Staples Center.

But before they walk to the ring, there is world title action with Billy Joe Saunders and Devin Haney defending their super-middleweight and lightweight belts respectively.

Here is everything you need to know about tonight's fights...

What time is the fight?

The fights are expected to start at around 4pm local time, which is midnight in the UK. Billy Joe Saunders should walk to the ring at around 6pm (2am UK) and will be followed by Devin Haney.

The main event is scheduled to start at 8pm local time (4am UK).

What TV channel is it on?

The fights are being shown on subscription service DAZN in the US and on Sky Sports Box Office in the UK.

Are the fights on YouTube?

No, unlike the first fight, the rematch will not be shown on YouTube despite KSI and Logan Paul having 40 million followers between them.

How many rounds is the fight?

The fight will be contested over six three-minute rounds in the cruiserweight division which means both KSI and Logan Paul weighed in less than 200lb at yesterday's weigh-in.

What are the odds?

When the fight was first announced, both men were around evens to win but Logan Paul is now the odds-on favourite with the bookmakes. He is priced at 8/15 with KSI at 7/4 while another draw is 8/1.

Latest news

Logan Paul has revealed his life-long ambition to be famous - and how his class-mates ridiculed him for it.

The social media influencer has been in the public eye since he was a teenager when he first started creating six-second videos on Vine.

He then migrated to YouTube where he attracted a following of almost 20million to establish himself as one of the most popular influencers in the world.

And on Saturday night Paul will take his earnings and perhaps his reputation into the stratosphere when he takes on KSI in their long-awaited rematch.

And the Ohio native is confident he will emerge victorious because he's been proving people wrong his whole life.

"In high school I wanted to be famous and everyone laughed," he said.

"But everything has seemed in reach for me as long as I grab for it. This is not a one-off; it cannot be. I am too good at it to not use this skill set more."

Trainers almost come to blows

KSI and Logan Paul took a back seat as their trainers almost came to blows in Hollywood.

The YouTube stars came together for the penultimate time before the first bell of their rematch on Saturday.

But it was former heavyweight world champion Shannon Briggs who stole the limelight.

Briggs will be in Paul's corner for the sequel at the Staples Center while KSI has been trained by professional fighter Viddal Riley.

And it was the coaches who traded insults across the stage.

Briggs said: "This is great for boxing, it's going to introduce new boxing fans to the sport.

"I had to be part of it, I've been connected to Logan for 25 years through a guy I met on the street, Paulo, who is now his manager.

"This is a great opportunity but my man has the ability and I know he can be the champ because I did it twice

"This is the pro ranks and you came to this fight with a butter knife of a trainer.

"This is war, you're going to get beat up real bad. I said to Logan you better put this guy in hospital."

Paul admits to breaking self-imposed sex ban

Logan Paul has admitted he broke his own sex ban in the build up to his rematch with KSI.

The American YouTube star will settle his feud with his fellow social media sensation when the pair clash in Los Angeles on Saturday.

Paul has been training with former heavyweight world champion Shannon Briggs after turning professional for the sequel which will be held 14 months after the first fight ended in a draw.

Paul had previously claimed that he was abstaining from sex for the duration of this training camp and that he had banned girls from his 'Maverick Mansion'.

But he has now conceded: "There were no girlfriends, just tunnel vision, no sex all camp but just one issue a month ago... so we did four weeks.

"In this camp I have never been more calm focused and confident. I truly feel like the best version of myself and better than I have ever felt and boxing is the vehicle for that."
