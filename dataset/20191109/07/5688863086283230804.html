Sandeep Chaudhary, Sumit Antil smash world records at World Para Athletics Championships

09 Nov 2019, 07:09 by SportsCafe Desk

Indian duo of Sandeep Chaudhary and Sumit created world records on way to their gold and silver medals respectively in the F64 javelin event of the World Para Athletics Championships on Friday. Ukraine's Roman Novak bagged the bronze medal as the F44 and F64 categories were combined into one event.

While the F44 category athletes have leg amputations or reduced function in one or both legs and compete without a prosthesis, the F64 category athletes have limb deficiency and leg length difference and compete with a prosthesis.

Sandeep bettered his own world record of 65.80m by hurling the javelin to a distance of 66.18m in F44 category, while Sumit sent the spear to a distance of 62.88m to clinch the silver and better his own F64 world record of 60.45m. The records will be counted according to the classification of the athletes.

“Roman Novak of Ukraine, a F44 athlete, took the bronze with a best throw of 57.36m. #TOPSAthlete #SandeepChaudhary and #Sumit set new world records in Men’s #Javelin F64 (42-44, 61-64) at the#ParaWorldChampionships. Sandeep won gold with a throw of 66.18m (F44) while Sumit won silver with a throw of 62.88m (F64). Many congratulations to both. #KheloIndia,” SAIMedia tweeted, reported PTI.

Congratulations for the Indian duo was in order from Union Minister of Sports Kiren Rijiju via his social media page.

“Sandeep Chaudhary and Sumit set new world records in Men’s Javelin F64 (42-44, 61-64) at the Para World Championships. Sandeep won gold with a throw of 66.18m (F44) while Sumit won silver with a throw of 62.88m (F64). @KirenRijiju  congratulates them both on this achievement,” Rijiju tweeted.
