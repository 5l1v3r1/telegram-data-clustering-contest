What channel is Leicester vs Arsenal on? TV and live stream information

Arsenal have been on a poor run of form and it gets no easier as they travel to the King Power Stadium to face Leicester in the Premier League

09 Nov 2019, 07:20 by Sam Fletcher

Arsenal are back in Premier League action against Leicester City - and are desperate for a win before the international break.

But they face a stern task as Brendan Rodgers’ high-flying Foxes aim to maintain their unbeaten record at the King Power Stadium and Arsenal haven’t won away from home since the opening day of the season.

Unai Emery’s job has come under severe pressure in recent weeks with odds already being taken over who will succeed him as Arsenal manager.

In the opposite dugout is Rodgers is one of the men tipped to replace Emery as Arsenal manager, despite only taking charge of the Foxes in February.

Striker Jamie Vardy is in fine goalscoring form having netted seven times in the last five matches and scored twice when the sides last met in April.

Here’s everything you need to know before the match...

When is the game?

The game is on Saturday 9 November.

It will kick-off at 5:30pm.

Where is the game being played?

The game is being played at Leicester City’s King Power Stadium.

Since the draw with Wolves on opening day of the season, Leicester have won all four of their Premier League home matches.

What TV channel is the game on?

UK viewers will need a Sky Sports subscription to watch the match with the game being shown on both their Main Event and Premier League channels.

Coverage starts from 5pm.

Can you live stream the game?

UK subscribers to Sky Sports can stream the game through their website and the Sky Sports app.

Alternatively, you can follow all of the action on the Mirror Football live blog.

Leicester team news

Brendan Rodgers stated in his pre-match press conference he has no fresh injury concerns ahead of the Arsenal game with the Northern Irishman boasting a fully-fit squad.

Arsenal team news

Dani Ceballos is unavailable for the match having picked up a thigh injury in Wednesday’s Europa League draw with Vitoria SC.

The Spaniard was substituted in the 54th-minute with Unai Emery already ruling him out of Saturday’s fixture.

Granit Xhaka is also expected to miss out, having not featured since his angry reaction to being substituted against Crystal Palace two weeks ago. Xhaka was also stripped of the captaincy.
