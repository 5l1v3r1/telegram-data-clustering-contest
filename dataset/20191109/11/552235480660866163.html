Teenager beaten and tortured for six hours by man she met on dating app

Emily Harvey, 19, travelled 50 miles to meet the man she had been chatting with on Badoo.

09 Nov 2019, 11:51 by Lynn Love

A young woman was brutally beaten and tortured for six hours by a man she met on a dating website

Emily Harvey, 19, travelled 50 miles to meet the man she had been chatting with on dating app Badoo .

The pair laughed and joked in his flat in Loughborough before he subjected her to hours of torture.

The 17-year-old man on Emily, beat her with a baseball bat until it broke, hit her with a frying pan and a hoover pipe and strangled her.

 

As Emily's attacker tightened his grip around her neck with both hands, she could feel herself about to lose consciousness,  LeicestershireLive reports.

"At that moment, I thought, ‘This is it. He’s going to kill me.’ Then it all went black," the teenager said.

Emily regained consciousness on the bedroom floor of the flat of her attacker and knew she needed to get out of the flat or she might be killed.

The brave teenager is now recalling the horrifying night and sharing her story.

She said: "He had beaten me with a baseball bat, and when he broke that on me, he got a frying pan and hit me on the back of the head with it, then picked up a Hoover pipe and  started to rain blows on me with that.

 

"Now, bizarrely, he was accusing me of stabbing him. He screamed at me for attacking him and told me to get out.

"I managed to get to my feet and I ran as fast as I could out of the door.

"As I tried to get out of the building I bumped into one of his neighbours. She took me in and called the police."

That was the end of an ordeal that had lasted six hours.

In that time, Emily’s date, a boy she had just met for the first time face-to-face after chatting online for a month, thought he had killed her.

At one point during the prolonged and sadistic attack, the Loughborough teenager took photographs of Emily, terrified and cowering on his settee, and shared them with a friend and his half-brother.

Despite her horrific ordeal, Emily found the courage to give a full statement to the police, and detectives praised her brave testimony which helped them build a successful case against the youth.

The boy, who cannot be named for legal reasons, pleaded guilty to wounding with intent to cause grievous bodily harm on their date on August 24.

He was given  a nine-year extended sentence,  with five years of detention and four years on licence, at Leicester Crown Court last month.

'He attacked me for no reason'

"I don’t think that is justice after what he did to me.

"They should lock him away and never let him out.

"One minute he was okay with me, then he just flipped.

"He attacked me for no reason. I really thought he was going to kill me there in his flat."

Emily said she had first made contact with the boy on a dating website.

"We got chatting and he seemed really nice," she said. "He was funny and said he liked me.

 

"We chatted for about a month. We sent messages on Snapchat too.

"He asked me to go over to meet him and said he would pay for the taxi.

"I felt like I sort of knew him, so I agreed to go.

"At first he was lovely. It was fun. Then he just changed.

"He damaged my phone so I couldn't contact anyone. Then he attacked me."

Today, Emily is slowly trying to find some normality in her life in the wake of the attack.

"The biggest problem I've got physically is the ligament in my right hand," she said.

"It got badly damaged as I tried to fend off the blows as he hit me, and kept on hitting me, with the baseball bat until it broke on me.

"I can’t do much for another couple of months.

"I hope  that it will heal  and then I can start to look for a job."

Emily has been offered counselling, but said she felt she did not need it.

"I want to just get on with things," she said.

"I haven’t had any nightmares about this, which is good.

"I've been out a couple of times with friends and that helps."

But she added: "I don’t feel like I can trust anyone I don’t know properly now."

Emily said she should have told her parents where she was going, "but I just didn’t".

She added: "I’m not sure how I might feel about meeting another man and going on a date – that’s so far off in the future I can’t even think about it."
