‘Asin law’ bucked

09 Nov 2019, 11:32 by Jester P. Manalastas

AN opposition solon has asked the Department of Health (DOH) to study carefully the proposed Asin Tax Law.

According to Marikina Rep. Stella Quimbo, this tax bill will hit hard the poor  who are the ones consuming dried fish (daing) and other salted fish.

“Kung ipapataw ang Asin Tax sa salted fish (tulad ng daing) at instant noodles, ang mahihirap ang matatamaan dito,” Quimbo said.

Citing the Family Income and Expenditure Survey, Quimbo said 4.9 percent of total food expenditure of every poor family is spent on dried fish, instant noodles and sardines.

Rich Filipinos are only spending 1.6 percent of their income on salted fish and the like, the lawmaker noted.

The DOH is pushing for an Asin Tax Law pointing out that too much salt is causing non-communicable diseases (NCDs).

According to the World Health Organization (WHO), 68 percent of  Filipinos  suffer from NCDs due to unhealthy diet.

But Quimbo said it is not right to impose higher tax on salted food.

“Ang tanong: tama bang magpataw ng Asin Tax? Sa totoo lang, kakaunti lang ang bansang mayroong Asin Tax. Mayroong junk food tax sa Hungary at Mexico,” she said.
