Man Utd target Jadon Sancho hauled off in Borussia Dortmund defeat to Bayern Munich

The England star was substituted 36 minutes into his side's 4-0 defeat in 'Der Klassiker'

09 Nov 2019, 22:04 by Hassan Rashed

England star Jadon Sancho was substituted after just 36 minutes during Borussia Dortmund's heavy defeat to Bundesliga rivals Bayern Munich.

The 19-year-old was replaced by Portugal international Raphael Guerreiro during the first half of Saturday's 4-0 thrashing in 'Der Klassiker' - while Dortmund were one goal down.

Sancho had been a doubt for the match after he suffered a hamstring injury during his side's 3-2 win over Inter Milan in the Champions League on Tuesday.

It was initially feared that the former Manchester City youngster had aggravated the same problem, but manager Lucien Favre insisted that his decision to replace him was due to tactical reasons.

"He was not injured, he trained yesterday," Favre said after the game (via The Mail ).

"I had to go on what I was seeing and, to be honest, it was not good enough."

Sancho, who has been heavily linked with the likes of Chelsea and Manchester United , was recently included in Gareth Southgate's squad for the upcoming European Qualifiers against Montenegro and Kosovo.

A week after being convincingly beaten 5-1 by Eintracht Frankfurt, Bayern followed-up Wednesday's 2-0 European win over Olympiacos with a dominant display in their first league game since Hansi Flick took over as interim boss after Niko Kovac's sacking.

Robert Lewandowski continued his stunning start to the season in front of goal, scoring twice to take his tally to 16 goals from his first 11 Bundesliga games.

The Polish forward opened the scoring after 17 minutes at Allianz Arena to give his side a 1-0 lead at the break.

And the defending champions took full control of the game after the break, with Serge Gnabry doubling the lead just two minutes after the restart.

Lewandowski added his second with a quarter of an hour to go, and Dortmund defender Mats Hummels confirmed the three points for his former club by putting the ball into his own net four minutes later.

The result leaves Bayern third in the table, one point behind leaders Monchengladbach and trailing RB Leipzig on goal difference, while Dortmund drip to fifth with 19 points.
