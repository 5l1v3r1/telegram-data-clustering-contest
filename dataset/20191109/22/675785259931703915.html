Man City boss Pep Guardiola dismisses Liverpool favourites tag ahead of Anfield clash

Liverpool can go nine points clear of Manchester City with victory at Anfield but Pep Guardiola insists the title won't be over

09 Nov 2019, 22:30 by Simon Mullock, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/simon-mullock/

A defiant Pep Guardiola has warned Jurgen Klopp that he is not going to Anfield as an underdog.

The Manchester City boss ­refuses to accept that the ­champions will be handing over the title to Liverpool after already falling six points behind Klopp’s men at the top of the ­Premier League.

City’s team has been ravaged by injuries – with goalkeeper Ederson the latest player to join a casualty list that includes David Silva and long-term absentees Leroy Sane and Aymeric Laporte.

Guardiola is aware of the size of his task at a ground where City have won just twice in the last 63 years.

But the Catalan has declared his intention to go to Merseyside to win and blow the title race wide open.

Guardiola said: “I’ve never gone into a game feeling like an ­outsider and feeling like I’m not going to win.

“Of course, Liverpool are in a position six points ahead and they are playing ­fantastic all season.

“We also have many problems in some ­departments.

“But I’m not going to take the bus to Anfield on Sunday thinking I’m going to lose the game.

“That has never ­happened in my career. I have always had the feeling that if we do the special things we plan to do, then we will have our chance to win.

“People keep talking about what will happen if we lose. But what if we win?”

Guardiola’s four trips to Anfield brought three defeats before a goalless draw last season.

Riyad Mahrez squandered the chance to blow away the Blues’ bogey when he missed a last-­minute penalty.

But the result still provided the solitary point that was to prove decisive for City in their bid to retain the title.

Yet City’s struggles at Liverpool run deep. Their only Premier League win was back in 2003 – and Kevin Keegan is the only man still alive to have managed the Blues to victory there.

Guardiola admits he is at a loss to explain why Anfield has become such a graveyard for City. He said: No, I cannot ­explain it. I can explain my period here, when we have played there.

“Last season, in the last ­minute, we could have won but we missed a penalty.

“Sometimes it happens. It shows how difficult it is. It is not easy to go to Camp Nou or ­Madrid or Bayern Munich or the biggest stages to win away.

“I will try to tell the players what they have to do to win the game in terms of the ­mental approach and tactical ­decisions. Maybe it happens this time and it is done.”

Guardiola added: “To win these kind of games you have to be at the top level.

“We can’t be half and half ­because the way Liverpool play demands incredible attention in all the details for 95 minutes.

“The only chance we have is if we play well and create chances.

“Not just at Anfield, but all the big stadiums around the world.”
