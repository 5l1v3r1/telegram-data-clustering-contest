Saudi Aramco targets sale of 0.5per cent of oil firm to retail investors in IPO: sources

09 Nov 2019, 22:50

Saudi Aramco is looking to sell up to 0.5per cent of the state oil giant to retail investors in its planned initial public offering (IPO), three sources familiar with the matter told Reuters.

DUBAI: Saudi Aramco is looking to sell up to 0.5per cent of the state oil giant to retail investors in its planned initial public offering (IPO), three sources familiar with the matter told Reuters.

The Saudi oil group has not yet revealed the size of its planned IPO or what proportion of the company it will float, although sources have previously said this could be 1-2per cent.

Aramco declined to comment.

It is expected to release more details about the company in an IPO prospectus document later on Saturday.

Assuming Aramco achieves a total valuation of US$2 trillion, the retail tranche could be worth around US$10 billion, the sources said on Saturday.

Aramco fired the starting gun on the domestic IPO last week after a series of false starts. It did not give details on how much would be sold, or when the listing would happen, while expert valuations vary from US$1.2 to US$2.3 trillion.

A government committee has met in the past few months with dozens of wealthy Saudi individuals to secure pre-sale agreements, sources told Reuters last month.

And the government has encouraged investors to repatriate cash held overseas to buy into the IPO to avoid draining too much liquidity from the Saudi banking system, they said.

Reuters reported on Oct. 17 that Aramco can take advantage of new market rules that allow issuers the flexibility to sell more shares to retail investors, likely exceeding the usual 10per cent seen in recent IPOs.

"Local demand is strong," a second source said on Saturday, adding that this would lead institutional investors to think that up to 75per cent of the IPO would be available for them.
