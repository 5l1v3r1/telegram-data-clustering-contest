Majority of Democratic voters in swing states prefer moderate nominee: Poll

09 Nov 2019, 16:49 by Zachary Halaschak

Most Democrats in the country’s battleground states want to vote for a candidate who is more centrist than liberal, according to a new survey.

A New York Times-Siena College poll released Friday found that voters in Michigan, Wisconsin, Pennsylvania, North Carolina, Arizona, and Florida preferred a candidate who would work with the other side of the aisle. Of those surveyed, 62% said they want a nominee who promises “to find common ground with Republicans,” compared to 33% who said they want a nominee who fights “for a bold progressive agenda.”

Additionally, 55% said they want the candidate to be “more moderate than most Democrats,” while 39% preferred the candidate to swing further to the left than most. Respondents were split on how they want to see the candidate handle the role, with 49% preferring that the nominee bring “politics in Washington back to normal” and 45% hoping for a “fundamental, systemic change to American society.”

Joe Biden led in five of the six states polled, with Elizabeth Warren enjoying a slight edge in Wisconsin. The only three candidates who brought in double-digit support in any of the states were Biden, Warren, and Bernie Sanders. Pete Buttigieg and Kamala Harris fell far behind the trio in all six states.

Nationally, Biden is the front-runner, with a RealClearPolitics average putting him at 28.3% support and Warren trailing him 20.6%.
