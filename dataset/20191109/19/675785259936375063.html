England Women 1-2 Germany: Lionesses suffer late heartbreak at packed Wembley

Ellen White's goal for England Women proved in vain as Germany claimed victory at Wembley. Klara Buhl popped up with a late winner as the visitors took the bragging rights

09 Nov 2019, 19:25 by Jacob Murtagh, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/jacob-murtagh/

Klara Buhl struck a last-gasp winner to earn Germany victory at Wembley.

Alexandra Popp gave the visitors an early lead before Ellen White levelled on 44 minutes for England Women.

However, Buhl found the net at the death as Phil Neville's Lionesses suffered late heartbreak.

It was Germany who came closest at the start of the game.

Lena Oberdorf drove forwards to create the space before passing to captain Popp, whose effort forced Earps to tip the ball onto the crossbar.

With their second chance of the match it was the visitors who took the lead in the ninth minute.

Popp flicked a header past Earps from a Kathrin Hendrich cross to put the second-ranked team in the world ahead.

Beth Mead, who had looked one of England's brightest options going forward, went down under a heavy tackle in the 28th minute.

The 24-year-old was involved in a challenge with Sara Doorsoun, who was shown a yellow card, but was able to continue.

The Lionesses were awarded a penalty in the 35th minute, with referee Stephanie Frappart ruling Mead was brought down by goalkeeper Mere Frohms.

White had threaded a ball through for Mead before she was fouled, with the referee awarding a spot-kick and a yellow card to the diving goalkeeper.

With a chance to level the tie, Nikita Parris stepped up to take the penalty, having scored just one out of three at the World Cup in the summer.

The Lyon forward's low drive was saved by the goalkeeper, as the Germans kept their 1-0 lead.

Ellen White levelled the score for the Lionesses just before the half-time whistle.

The experienced forward beat the offside trap and got on the end of a clever lofted cross from Kiera Walsh to score her 36th international goal.

Parris went down at the start of of the second half after Sophia Kleinherne appeared to catch her Achilles. Kleinherne was shown a yellow card for the attempted tackle, and Parris had to receive treatment before continuing.

Just as the game looked to be lacking in intensity, Germany stormed forward forcing Earps into a low diving save to deny Klara Buhl.

Lina Magull followed up from an offside position, and did have the ball in the back of the net, but the flag had been raised.

Just as the game looked to be heading towards a draw, Germany started piling on the pressure, and captain Steph Houghton managed to make to important blocks to deny the visitors.

But in the 89th minute, Klara Buhl retook the lead for Germany, with a calm finish as she shot across goal into the far corner.

Jodie Taylor came close to finding an equaliser seconds later, but her outstretched effort was straight at Frohms.
