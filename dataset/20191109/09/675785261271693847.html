Arsenal predicted lineup as Unai Emery's men face Leicester

Arsenal are hoping to arrest a slide of four straight draws in all competitions in their Premier League clash with Leicester - with a defeat meaning they could go nine points adrift of the top four to pile the pressure on Unai Emery

09 Nov 2019, 09:00 by Alex Smith

Unai Emery is set to stick with the back-three formation which struggled to a draw in the Europa League on Wednesday.

Arsenal suffered a 1-1 draw with Vitoria as Bruno Duarte bagged a 90th minute equaliser to cancel out Shkodran Mustafi's strike.

Under fire Emery utilised three centre backs - Sokratis, Rob Holding and Mustafi - for the first time in Portugal, and is likely to do the same against high flying Leicester on Saturday evening.

Calum Chambers, David Luiz and Sokratis are expected to be the trio to line-up in front of Bernd Leno.

They will probably have defensive width provided by Hector Bellerin, who will make his first Premier League appearance since his injury - having been left on the bench against Vitoria - and Sead Kolasinac.

The Gunners have drawn their last four matches in all competitions and haven't won in the Premier League since a 1-0 victory over Bournemouth on October 6.

Dani Ceballos is the only injured player after the Spain international suffered a hamstring injury in the Europa League.

Granit Xhaka will once again miss out as he continues to sit out following his bust up with Arsenal fans against Crystal Palace last month.

But Emery is expected to name a strong XI - with Mesut Ozil keeping his place in midfield.

The German is set for back-to-back Premier League starts for the first time this season, having previously been frozen out by Emery.

He should provide the tip to a midfield alongside Matteo Guendouzi and Lucas Torreira.

Up front, Pierre-Emerick Aubameyang will be partnered by Nicolas Pepe.

If Arsenal were to lose to the Foxes they will be left nine points adrift of the top four, and Emery has branded the fixture a must-win.

“This a very big opportunity. After two matches at home that we drew, tomorrow is a very good opportunity. Yesterday the players worked and trained very well with a good spirit and they give me confidence,” said Emery.

“The most important is our mentality and going there thinking: ‘We are Arsenal.’ And we will go there with confidence and also strong in our mentality to be together to go to work against them.

“The circumstances - Ozil, Kolasinac, the Laurent Koscielny circumstances and if he was still here then he would still be captain and there would be no problem. But the team inside now, they are strong.

"Is this must-win? Yes. But in our mind, for each match it is only to win.”

Arsenal likely XI: Leno; Bellerin, Chambers, Luiz, Sokratis, Kolasinac; Guendouzi, Torreira, Ozil; Pepe, Aubameyang
