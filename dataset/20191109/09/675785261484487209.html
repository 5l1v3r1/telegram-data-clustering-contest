Vincent Kompany explains how Liverpool fans attacking Man City bus started rivalry

Liverpool fans caused damage to the Man City team coach as it arrived at Anfield for the two club's Champions League quarter-final in April 2018 - with the Reds winning the fixture

09 Nov 2019, 09:53 by Alex Smith

Vincent Kompany believes Liverpool fans attacking Manchester City's team bus was the starting point for the two club's rivalry.

City were on their way to Anfield for 2018's Champions League quarter-final first leg when Reds supporters started to throw items, such as bottles and flares, on their approach to the ground.

The blitz on the vehicle forced Liverpool to issue an apology, with the club handed a £17,500 fine by UEFA - although despite trawling through 130 hours of footage, the police were unable to make any arrests.

But that incident, which smashed windows and required a replacement coach to take the players home, is seen as the flash point which turned the two sides to enemy status.

That was intensified by last season's title race - which saw Liverpool lose once all season, to City, and Pep Guardiola's men lift the crown having won the battle by a point.

It looks set to be another close tussle for top spot in the Premier League, and former City skipper Kompany, who is now a player-manager at Anderlecht, reckons that evening in April on Anfield Road was the flash point.

“The rivalry increased and they became our No. 1 team to beat," Kompany recalled in his book 'Treble Triumph', which is currently being serialised in the Sun. "The mood among City fans towards these games against Liverpool had changed.

“They’d always been seen as tough fixtures but now there was more tension.

“None of us were sitting on that coach saying they were scared. But it was very unpleasant and should never have been allowed to happen.

“I do think that incident changed the way that a lot of neutrals looked at Liverpool. To give them some credit, they had also become the hardest team to win against. But I could see it becoming a deep rivalry.

“But it changed the way we saw games against them from that moment. It became even more important to us to win our games against Liverpool."
