Messi Nets Sublime Hat-Trick As Barca Cement Top Spot

09 Nov 2019, 23:56 by SL International

LaLiga

Result: Barcelona 4-1 Celta Vigo

Date: 09 November 2019

Venue: Camp Nou

Lionel Messi stole the show after netting a hat-trick, which included two free-kicks, as FC Barcelona thumped Celta Vigo 4-1 on Saturday night.

Ernesto Valverde recalled Anssu Fati in place of Ousmane Dembele who dropped to the bench while Samuel Umtiti started alongside Gerard Pique in the heart of defence

Barca hit the front at the midway point after Messi fired in from the penalty spot following a handball in the area after Junior Firpo handled Nelson Semedo’s cross on the byline.

After spearheading the opener, Semedo pulled a hamstring after delivering the ball and was duly removed for Sergio Busquets who filled the Portuguese international’s void.

Celta looked for a strong finish three minutes to halftime and found their just rewards after Lucas Olaza found the bottom corner with a well-taken free-kick caused by Messi.

Barca restored the tally on the stroke of halftime when Messi nestled the far corner of the net with a similarly taken free-kick from the edge of the area.

The Catalans brought more speed up top following the halftime break, as Fati made way for Frenchman Dembele.

Messi wasn’t done though and went on to complete his hat-trick with an identical top corner free-kick near the danger area two minutes from the interval.

Antoine Griezmann closed in to give Barca a third following the 61st minute, but good goalkeeping from Ruben Blanco narrowed the forward’s angle and attempt as well.

Barca stayed in charge following the final minutes despite a few danger signs in and around the final third from the visiting side.

Busquets wrapped up the contest five minutes to the end when he collected a looping ball on the area to riffle into the bottom near corner.

Barca returned to winning ways following two dismal results in their two last matches, as Celta drop to the bottom-three.
