Trent Alexander-Arnold tipped to leave Liverpool in summer transfer battle

Legendary former Brazil right-back Cafu reckons Alexander-Arnold will be wanted by clubs across Europe this summer

09 Nov 2019, 23:48 by Gareth Bicknell

Liverpool right-back Trent Alexander-Arnold could be the subject of a big-money transfer this summer, according to legendary Brazil right-back Cafu.

Alexander-Arnold has provided 28 assists in his 101 games for Liverpool since making his top-flight debut three years ago, and already has three assists and one goal in the Premier League this season.

Last season he was the Premier League's joint-third highest provider with 12 assists, while Reds left-back Andy Robertson was just one behind on 11.

Liverpool's attacking full-backs have been crucial in Liverpool's rise to the top, with the European champions and Premier League leaders hosting Manchester City today in a huge title-race showdown at Anfield.

Former Brazil star Cafu played a similar attacking role to Alexander-Arnold , with Roberto Carlos providing similar impetus from left-back as the Selecao won the 2002 World Cup.

And Cafu, who won 142 caps for Brazil and also won the World Cup in 1994, reckons 21-year-old Alexander-Arnold will be one of this summer's hottest transfer properties as big clubs across Europe bid to tempt Liverpool into letting him go.

“Trent has been one of the better players in the league and he is one of the main reasons why Liverpool are where they are," Cafu told The Sun .

“At the end of the season we could see a big trade with him, as he is not only a great defender but he can also help and move forward and sideways with and without the ball.

“For that reason he has been great in scoring goals and is what I would call a ‘quarterback scorer’."

Cafu, 49, has been impressed with Alexander-Arnold's commitment to the right-back position.

Both Gary Lineker and Jamie Carragher have both recently said they would like to see the England international play further forward as a creative midfielder - but Alexander-Arnold's response was to tweet "One position at a time" along with a smirking emoji.

Cafu said: “To any young player, the key word is always commitment.

“Young players need to have a strong commitment to the position they take on.

“This is my No 1 piece of advice. It’s what I teach to others.

Alexander-Arnold's current transfer value is estimated at £72million by football stats website Transfermarkt.

However, given his importance to the team and the fact he is contracted to Liverpool until 2024, the Reds would most likely stand firm amid any interest and it would take a much greater offer to tempt them into selling.
