KSI vs Logan Paul rematch free live stream warning about unofficial boxing channels

The highly-anticipated rematch between KSI and Logan Paul will be watched by millions of people around the world - and live streaming could become an issue

09 Nov 2019, 23:00 by Aaron Flanagan, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/aaron-flanagan/

Over one year has passed since KSI and Logan Paul last step into the ring - and now they can finally settle their grudge once and for all.

The pair fought out a draw when they clashed in August 2018, leading to Saturday night's rematch, taking place in Los Angeles, California.

Millions of people around the world have been showing an interest and are keen to catch the action.

Those in the UK can see the fight on Sky Sports Box Office, with it purchasable for a price of £9.95, with the option to watch on either TV or live stream.

In the USA, it can be watched and streamed on DAZN, with a monthly fee of $19.99 for their subscribers.

 

However, fans are warned that they must not watch the action on illegal streaming sites - as the damage could be costly.

Some streaming sites have even asked for small payments over PayPal to access the fights - but those streams risk being taken off air due to copyright infringement.

There is also a worry that dodgy streaming websites create the risk of malware and other computer viruses, if adequate protection is not already in place.

But perhaps the biggest warning is to fans who are looking to use the live streaming and recording services available on Facebook , Twitter and Instagram to share the fight with their friends and followers.

In 2017, Bristol-based Yusuf Mohammed was slapped with a £16,000 fine after he was found guilty of broadcasting Sky Sports content, filmed from a remote device, on his social media channels.

One year later, Craig Foster from Scarborough was handed a staggering £85,000 fine after it was claimed that his friend broadcast the Anthony Joshua vs Wladimir Klitchsko fight.

But Foster was the one guilty after it was found that he was the man who paid for the Sky Sports Box Office fight.

The 34-year-old claims that the friend illegally broadcast it via Facebook to 4,250 people via Foster's iPad.

A watermark of his account number allowed Sky to track down Foster, and they hit him with the bill due to loss of earnings.

"I’d paid for the boxing, it wasn’t like I was making any money," Foster told the Mirror exclusively at the time.

"My iPad was signed in to my Facebook account and my friend just started streaming the fight.

“I didn’t think anything of it, then a few days later they cut my subscription.

“They’re demanding the names and addresses of all my mates who were round that night but I’m not going to give them up. I said I'd take the rap.”
