X Factor: Celebrity judges blown away by Megan McKenna's moving song about heartache

The reality TV star touched the judges' hearts with her moving original song as it related to romance and heartbreak as she performed ahead of another double elimination

09 Nov 2019, 23:10 by Brogan-Leigh Hurst

Megan McKenna left the X Factor: Celebrity judges moved by the song she wrote and sang tonight as it referenced relationship heartbreak.

The former Celebrity Big Brother star, 27, took to the stage and sang her heart out to Simon Cowell, Nicole Scherzinger and Louis Walsh as she hopes to win the singing show.

Her original track Half of My Heart also wowed the stunned audience as she vowed to steal a vote before tonight's sing off and double elimination.

Simon Cowell, 60, asked: "What was that song driven by, what was it about?"

She responded: "That song for me I wrote literally a few weeks ago and I wanted to put in that... the message in that song is the next thing I go into just to not put my full heart in - just to keep a little back for myself."

Then Simon quizzed: "Are you talking about relationships?" and she said: "It could be anything, but," before Simon repeated the question again.

Megan laughed: "Maybe a little bit," before the music mogul replied: "Yeah, thought so!"

It then went to Louis - Megan's mentor, who said: "Megan what a lot of people probably don't know is your dedication to music, your song-writing, your singing - you are so serious about this.

"And when you sing, you touch people and everybody relates to the lyrics of your songs."

He then confessed she ticks all the boxes and is fit for a recording career, stating "it has to be you".

And Nicole Scherzinger, 41, who watched in awe, gushed: "I love the message of that song, I second you on that.

"Protect your heart, because you have to ultimately love yourself first."

An overwhelmed Megan was voted for by the audience in tonight's double elimination and will be performing on next week's show.

*The X Factor: Celebrity continues Saturday evenings on ITV.
