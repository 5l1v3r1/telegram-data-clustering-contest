Things to do today in Bengaluru

09 Nov 2019, 00:30 by Bangalore Mirror Bureau

It’s book O’clock
It’s a great weekend for book lovers in the city, because the 2019 edition of Bangalore Literature Festival opens today. It will feature a host of distinguished speakers, authors and artistes such as Pankaj Kapur, Rajat Gupta, Shubha Mudgal, Rohini Nilekani, Akshay Gandhi, among others.
And some stellar music performances in the evening – from Shakespeare to Shishunala: A Poetic Journey by Manasi and Friends to a concert by the
Thayir Sadam Project.

WHERE: The Lalit Ashok, Kumarakrupa Road, High Grounds
WHEN: 10am onwards, November 9
DETAILS: bangaloreliteraturefestival.org

Let the dogs out
Silicon City Kennel Club will conduct All-India Championship Dog over the weekend. About 450-500 pedigreed dogs of more than 50 different breeds will be exhibited. Special breeds such as Akita, Maltese, Schnauzer, Siberian Husky, Belgian Shepherd Dog, Afghan Hound and popular breeds such as German Shepherd, Doberman, Labrador Retriever, Golden Retriever, Boxer, Great Dane, Cocker Spaniel and others will enthral the hearts of spectators with their grace, sheer and beauty.

WHERE: Veterinary College, opposite CBI office, Hebbal WHEN: 9am-5pm, November 9
CALL: 9036259062

Flower power
Ethnic Crafts from Udaipur is coming to this boutique for the first time and it will showcase clothes with unique block-prints such as the carnation, marigold flower, palm tree, mogul leaf, different takes on the classic paisley and many others. Choose from their range of pure cotton kurtas, palazzos, and Chanderi silks and upgrade your wardrobe.

WHERE: Sakhi by Chandra’s, HAL 2nd Stage, Defence Colony, Indiranagar
WHEN: 10am-8pm, November 9
CALL: 9900033636

Find your balance
In Sense is a one-day workshop designed to help one understand the power of thought and to learn to deal with the stressful situations through a process of inquiry or self-questioning. Here, participants will be introduced to powerful questions that can help them find clarity, inner strength, self-love, freedom, and zest for life. Sign up for this workshop, which will ne facilitated by Jaishree Sellamuthu.

WHERE: Atta Galatta, #134, KHB Colony, 5th Block, Koramangala
WHEN: 10am-5pm, November 9 CALL: 8056200888

Watch a cartoon show
This gallery has mounted a new exhibition of cartoons, featuring the works of YS Nanjunda Swamy. He has practiced both commercial and traditional art in Shivamogga, and has created over a thousand cartoons and over two hundred caricatures within a short span of four years. On display will be caricatures of tennis legends Roger Federer and Serena Williams, physicist Stephen William Hawking, political leaders Donald Trump and Kim Jong-un, and others.

WHERE: Indian Cartoon Gallery, Midford House, Midford Garden Road WHEN: 10am-6pm,
November 9 CALL: 9241658361

The best of 25
Continuum is a selection of art from 25 years of India Foundation for the Arts (IFA’s) grant-making programme. So if you are an arts enthusiast, this show is not to be missed, as it brings together the works of artists Alakananda Nag, Soumya Sankar Bose, Anitha Balachandran, Dhruv Jani, Avik Mukhopadhyay, Sarbajit Sen, Pallavi Paul and Sahej Rahal and Abul Kalam Azad.
The show includes photographs, video projection,
gaming projection and film screening.

WHERE: The Gallery, The Bay, Campus 8, RMZ Ecoworld, Sarjapur Marathahalli
WHEN: 11am-6pm, November 9
CALL: 9886021715

Hit the road
Celebrated on the day of Shankar Nag’s birthday, a movie star and a cult hero figure amongst city’s auto drivers, Auto Rickshaw Day is started with an aim to bring dignity to the auto driver community. They will host health camps, blood donation, issuance of driver uniforms, performances by auto rickshaw community and honour many of them. You can also join the event and hail their efforts to keep the city moving.

WHERE: Shalini Ground, 5th Block, Jayanagar WHEN: 4pm onwards, November 9
CALL: 9591106263

Arts for all
Surchitranam is no ordinary exhibition. It brings together 40 to 50 artworks by a wide range of folks, from school kids to a middle-aged people. Since the theme of the art show is ‘Celebrating Indian Art Forms’, these paintings give glimpses of Indian folk art, tribal art, temple art, gods and goddesses, and art forms tracing back to the 18th Century. The exhibits are done in a mix of acrylic, charcoal, pencil shadings and soft pastels.

WHERE: Hotel Aloft, Cessna Business Park, Sarjapur-Marathahalli, Outer Ring Road
WHEN: 10am-6.30pm, November 9
CALL: 9903813988

The dance of democracy
Bandar Ke Haath Champagne, translated from the original work by Marathi playwright Dr Vivek Bele, talks about the one thread that binds the people of India. It’s the politics, more precisely the elections, where people are the audience, politicians are (supposedly) the performers and media is the
narrator of this grand festival. Directed by Sandeep Paranjape, it narrates the story of three friends who have very distinct personalities. Their lives change when a girl walks into their lives and all of them fall for her. Some seek attention, some adventure, and some just chaos.

This story through its life events and interactions is a take on how the stakeholders of our democracy woo the public and how the public in turn responds to these advances. Expectations, promises, tactics, rifts, alliances, manipulation – all find their way to this hilarious story.

WHERE: Bangalore International Centre, Stage 2, Domlur
WHEN: 4.30pm & 7pm, November 10 ENTRY: `300, bookmyshow.com
