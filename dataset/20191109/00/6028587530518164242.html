Talking Shop With Jyothi Rao, The President of INTERMIX

09 Nov 2019 by Felicity Carter

Jyothi Rao, president of INTERMIX is here to help you build a wardrobe, and a very stylish, informed and elevated one at that.

A collection of specialty boutiques, INTERMIX opened shop in 1993 and can be found across America. It’s a brand known for bringing together sought-after, established labels (McQueen, Balmain, Dolce & Gabbana) with new designers on the scene (such as the favorite, Alighieri), making for a fresh and compelling edit.

As for Rao, she joined INTERMIX in 2014 having built up a wealth of knowledge in the industry. Her work has taken her to Africa, India, and Europe and she’s served in senior roles at Gilt.com, Calvin Klein and before that she spent 16 years at Gap Inc., where she held several leadership positions and was responsible for successfully growing domestic and international businesses for both the Gap and Banana Republic brands. Impressive.

Combining all this consumer and retail know-how it, has made her the perfect woman to craft the vision for INTERMIX, developing the overall business strategy tapping into contemporary culture, because as Rao says herself, “fashion is a reflection of culture.”

FC: What was your first fashionable memory?

Jyothi Rao: I remember spending my weekends as a teenager in London in the ‘80s visiting all the boutiques on South Molton street, New Bond Street, Covent Gardens and Knightsbridge, Harrods, Harvey Nicks, Liberty and so many more. I couldn’t afford to buy anything from most of these stores but I loved to look at the clothes. I would pour through fashion magazines, discovering who Karl Lagerfeld and Jean Paul Gaultier were and I was very influenced by the music scene in London; a mashup of punk and ‘80s glamour. It was an exciting time for fashion.

FC: How, when, why did you get into the fashion industry?

JR: I was 21 when I graduated from college and was open to a myriad of career options from investment banking to marketing roles. I interviewed with several companies who came to my college to recruit graduates and I landed on a role in merchandising because it combined my love for fashion and my love for numbers. 

FC: How would you sum up the aesthetic of Intermix?

JR: The INTERMIX point of view is feminine, elevated, confident and effortlessly chic. We strive to offer a selection of versatile styles that help women stay current while getting a lot of mileage from their wardrobe. We don't believe in disposable fashion or the endless aisle. We want to help women build a great wardrobe.

FC: What do qualities do you look for when it comes to buying a brand for the store?

At INTERMIX, we know our customers very well. Our talented buying team makes sure that we select brands that are relevant to our customers and that they fit within the INTERMIX aesthetic. Supporting emerging designers is an important part of our business model. A few of the up-and-coming brands we’re excited about are ROTATE BIRGER CHRISTENSEN, Acler, Paris Texas, Aje and Sabina Musayev.

FC: What is luxury to you?

JR: For us, luxury is about personalization and knowing our customer on an intimate level so that we can customize the product edit and the shopping experience for her. One way we do this is by offering complimentary styling services in our boutiques and online.

FC: Who is your customer?

JR: The INTERMIX woman is informed, confident and fun. She sees fashion as a form of self-expression and doesn’t take it too seriously. I am endlessly inspired by the wonderful community of women we serve because they are interesting, generous and supportive of one another.

FC: How is the retail landscape changing for you, and how are you adapting to this?

JR: Customers are at the center of everything in retail today. They are setting the standards of what they expect from the brands they purchase, they are the content creators and the influencers. At INTERMIX, we are very proud of having a diverse team from the leadership level to the most junior level of the brand, we put our customers at the center of every decision we make, and we continue to look for ways to bring our community further into the brand.

FC: What's the best piece of advice that you've been given when it comes to handling the industry?

JR: Fashion is a reflection of culture. You have to stay in tune with where the customer is going and constantly evolve while staying true to your brand ethos.
