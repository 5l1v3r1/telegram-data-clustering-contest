Friday Box Office: ‘Terminator: Dark Fate’ Plunges 73% As ‘Joker’ Nears $1 Billion

09 Nov 2019 by Scott Mendelson

Even with better reviews, a stronger fan reception and a decent B+ Cinemascore grade, Terminator: Dark Fate met Judgment Day on Friday. The $185 million Paramount/Disney/Fox release earned just $2.8 million (-73%) on Friday to set the stage for a $10.21 million (-65%) second-weekend gross. That will give the Tim Miller-directed and James Cameron-produced sci-fi sequel just $47.9 million in ten days, or just slightly above what Terminator: Genisys earned in five days in 2015 and what Terminator: Rise of the Machines earned in its $43 million Fri-Sun weekend (in a $73 million Wed-Sun debut) in 2003. The 74% Friday-to-Friday drop isn’t as bad as Batman v Superman (-81%) or Dark Phoenix (-83%), but it’s worse than any previous Terminator sequel (all of which opened on Thursday or Friday).

Terminator: Salvation opened on a Thursday in 2009 and its second Thursday saw an 80% drop from its first. Either way, It’s a sign that making a better sequel couldn’t save a franchise for which general audiences stopped caring decades ago. The Linda Hamilton/Arnold Schwarzenegger/Natalia Reyes/Mackenzie Davis/Gabriel Luna flick will end its domestic run with around $65-$70 million domestic, or about what Rise of the Machines earned in its first five days and what Terminator: Salvation earned ($65 million) in its Thurs-Mon Memorial Day launch back in 2009. Yes, I’m been beating the drum on this one’s failure, because, like Dark Phoenix, it’s an example of the studios looking at the threat posed by VOD and streaming and giving theatrical audiences exactly what they don’t want.

In better news, Warner Bros.’ Joker is continuing to do its thing. It earned $2.51 million (-34%) on Friday for a new $306.802 million domestic cume. Presuming it has maintained that 32%/68% domestic/overseas split, the film has earned, on a $62.5 million budget, $958.7 million worldwide. As noted yesterday, that makes it the most profitable comic book movie, in terms of “rate of return” budget versus global gross, of all time. It’ll earn $9.22 million (-32%) in weekend six for a $313.5 million domestic cume, just shy of Thor: Ragnarok ($315 million in 2017), a new $980 million global cume.  The DC Films flick is essentially holding up Warner Bros.’ entire year-end dramatic slate. And yeah, it should be over the $1 billion mark in a week.

Walt Disney’s Maleficent: Mistress of Evil crossed $400 million worldwide earlier this week, and it grossed $2.002 million (-35%) for a $91.3 million cume. The $185 million Angelina Jolie sequel will earn $8.27 million (-37%) in weekend four for a $97.57 million 24-day cume as it tops $100 million sometime next week. Presuming the 22/78 split holds, it’ll have around $443.5 million worldwide by the end of tomorrow night. Buoyed by an A+ Cinemascore grade, Focus Features’ Harriet earned $1.99 million (49%) on Friday for a likely $6.8 million (-42%) weekend and $23 million ten-day cume. That’s not huge for Cynthia Erivos’ $17 million Harriet Tubman biopic, but it’s a solid performance thus far that points to a $40 million domestic finish.  

Jojo Rabbit will earn around $4 million this weekend (+71%) as Fox Searchlight expanded it to 802 theaters, giving Taika Waititi’s World War II/Nazi comedy a decent $9.2 million 24-day domestic cume. Neon’s Parasite continued to pack them in, relatively speaking. The Bong Joon Ho-directed thriller will earn $2.53 million (+0%) in 603 theaters for a terrific $11.3 million 31-day domestic cume. The South Korean thriller that has already earned $100 million worldwide. The Addams Family will have $91 million domestic after a $3.71 million (-55%) weekend as it’ll just miss $100 million domestic. Warner Bros.’ Motherless Brooklyn held surprisingly well in weekend two, with a $639,000 (-39%) Friday and a $2.27 million (-35%) weekend. That’s still a terrible $7.427 million ten-day cume, but I digress.
