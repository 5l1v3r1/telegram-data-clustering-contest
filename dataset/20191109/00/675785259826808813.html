Cat owner's horror as she finds her kitten decapitated 'with sharp blade' near home

Katie Ross said she had to 'scoop up' the body of her ten-month-old kitten, Ru, from the pavement in Bristol with her pet's head was 'nowhere to be seen'

09 Nov 2019, 00:49 by Tiffany Lo, Tristan Cork

A cat owner was horrified to discover her pet decapitated near her home.

Owner Katie Ross said her 10-month-old Main Coon kitten, Ru, went missing overnight from home in the St Anne's Park area of Bristol on Thursday.

She appealed on local social media in her neighbourhood and went out searching - but was soon told the headless body of a cat was lying on the pavement near her home in the Wyatt’s View area.

She described the horrific scene to Bristol Live: "Instantly I went out to see and as I walked up I knew it was him, even with the head gone, it was nowhere to be seen.

 

"I scooped him up and waited for my partner to get home.

We took him to the vets, praying it was an animal attack, as it is more digestible than thinking a human could do something so vile."

She said the vet confirmed the worst - that Ru had been killed and decapitated with a sharp instrument or blade, and had also had an area of fur on his tail shaved off.

Katie said Ru lived with his brother Morty - who is now being kept in.

"He's lost without him," she added.

Police has launched an investigation into what happened, and have been conducting door-to-door inquiries.

The attack must have happened at some point after 3pm on Thursday, November 7, when Ru first went out, although probably after 7pm when a search of the area found no sign of him, Katie said.

A spokesperson for  Avon and Somerset police  said: "We're investigating after the mutilated body of a cat was found in the St Anne's Park area earlier today, Friday, November 8.

"The incident is being treated as a criminal offence and our inquiries are continuing."

If anyone has any information about what happened to Ru, in St Anne's Park overnight on Thursday November 7, they should call the police on 101 and quote reference number:  0165 of today's date, 8/11/19.
