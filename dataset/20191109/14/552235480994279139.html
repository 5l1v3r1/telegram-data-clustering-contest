Eddie May in Hibs 'commitment' poser as he makes veiled Paul Heckingbottom dig

The academy chief is in the dugout for today's clash with St Johnstone but doesn't want the job permanently.

09 Nov 2019, 14:54 by Gabriel McKay

Hibs caretaker manager Eddie May has offered a veiled jibe about Paul Heckingbottom 's style of play as he appeared to question the commitment of some of his signings.

The Englishman was sacked by the club this week, with the academy boss taking charge for today's trip to St Johnstone .

He's made five changes from the team which lost 5-2 to Celtic in Heckingbottom's last game, with Thomas James, Adam Jackson, Daryl Horgan, Stevie Mallan and Josh Vela dropping out.

James, Jackson and Vela were all summer signings, and Florian Kamberi has been restored to the XI for only his second start since September.

May said: "I've looked at the games where we've been competitive and I want players who are committed to the club.

"I want players who are good on the ball and people who are attacking. It's just unfortunate that some guys have missed out today but they're part of a squad and everyone will get their opportunity. If things aren't going well we can put some of these players on.

"We have to take responsibility on the park in terms of delivering a more pleasing on the eye product but also more attacking.

" Hibs have probably been a little bit unfortunate in some games, they could be a few points better off, but we're not and we have to make sure that we go out and make a difference on the park.

"We've picked a team that suits us."

May is in caretaker charge for today's game but made clear he has no interest in taking the job permanently.

He added: "I think I've already stated that. I'm in for a reason, someone has lost their job which is unfortunate and someone else will come in and take the club forward.

"It's not about me it's about what's best for the club and there are some very good candidates who want the job so I think they have to go and look at everyone and then make the right decision."
