Fan floored by single punch from police officer following Wolves' win over Slovan Bratislava

Footage shows an officer floor a Slovan Bratislava fan with one punch following their defeat to Wolves at Molineux in the Europa League on Thursday evening

09 Nov 2019, 10:04 by James Whaling, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/james-whaling/

A police officer punched a Slovan Bratislava fan to the floor with one shot amid crowd trouble following their defeat to Wolves in the Europa League at Molineux.

Around 1,000 Slovan fans made the trip to the West Midlands and three arrests were made.

Allegations included coin-throwing, a racial comment and assault of an officer.

Footage shows several Slovan fans charge at cops, before one is struck in the face and floored by the single punch.

Officers also use batons to stop the onrushing fans following the match which Wolves won 2-1 to leave them on the brink of qualification for the knockout stages.

A spokesman for West Midlands Police told the Express and Star: "There were pockets of disorder during the evening which included a group of away supporters surging towards home fans forcing police officers to intervene.

"An officer was assaulted as officers contained the volatile away following.

"A 36-year-old man from Slovakia was arrested on suspicion of assaulting an officer and remains in police custody for questioning."

Wolves' victory was the second over their Slovakian opponents this season after also claiming the three points in the reverse fixture.

The match in Bratislava was played 'behind closed doors' - although thousands of supporters still attended owing to a UEFA rule that allowed children under the age of 14 to attend accompanied by an adult.

Nuno's men host Aston Villa in a West Midlands derby at Molineux on Sunday.
