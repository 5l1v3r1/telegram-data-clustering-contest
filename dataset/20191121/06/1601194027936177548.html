UFC News: Leon Edwards has been waiting for Tyron Woodley fight for months, waiting on former champion to accept

21 Nov 2019, 06:06 by R. Nath

The British #4 ranked Welterweight Contender Leon Edwards has been on the rise for the last few years. He's currently riding an 8-fight win streak that includes wins over Donald "Cowboy" Cerrone, Vicente Luque and more recently, Rafael Dos Anjos.

He's in an upward trajectory despite not having gotten a finish in one and a half years. The logical next step for him would be to either face a top-5 ranked contender or a former champion. He is expected to get both in one go as Tyron Woodley seems to be the next man in line for him.

Woodley has been open about the fact that he wants to get back in the mix as soon as January, but it seems as though he hasn't accepted a fight as yet. Damon Martin of MMAFighting reported on Twitter that Edwards' manager said that his team has spoken to UFC at length for the last three months about a fight against Tyron Woodley. However, The Chosen One has yet to accept the fight.

Martin further stated that Edwards is determined not to take a step back, so Woodley is the logical choice as his next opponent.
