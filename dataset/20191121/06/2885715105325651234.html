By the end of George Reddy, I am sure audience will be teary-eyed: Sandeep Madhav

George Reddy, starring Sandeep Madhav in the titular role, is based on the Osmania University student leader of the same name.

21 Nov 2019, 06:07 by Gabbeta Ranjith Kumar

Actor Sandeep Madhav aka Sandy, who made his acting debut with Ram Gopal Varma’s Vangaveeti, returns to the screens this week with George Reddy, based on the Osmania University student leader of the same name.

Ahead of the film’s release, Sandeep talked to indianexpress.com about his role in the biopic.

Excerpts from the conversation:

How did you prepare for George Reddy?

The prep was to look like George Reddy. The movie covers his life from the age of 22 to 25. So to look that age, I had to put on some weight and later, shed some too. Other than that, I learnt and practised a few other things, including boxing.

What aspect of this story inspired you the most?

I liked the character. Right after listening to the script, I started reading books and articles about him. I always thought of doing a character of a student leader, but I never thought that it would come so early in my career.

How did you approach the character of George Reddy?

In the case of George Reddy, we found only one video of him on YouTube. It was a BBC documentary named Crisis in the Campus. So, except that video and two photos of him, I had nothing to learn or imbibe about his personality. I tried to understand his body-language through these limited resources and implemented it based on my understanding. I also interacted with people who were well aware of his behaviour and thoughts.
