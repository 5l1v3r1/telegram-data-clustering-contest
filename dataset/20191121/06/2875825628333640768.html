Jose Mourinho's appointment could be blessing in disguise for Troy Parrott

It certainly doesn’t have to be the nightmare scenario that some were depicting yesterday

21 Nov 2019, 06:00 by Paul O'Hehir

Jose Mourinho's appointment at Tottenham could be a blessing in disguise for Ireland starlet Troy Parrott.

It certainly doesn’t have to be the nightmare scenario that some were depicting yesterday.

Cracking the Spurs first team-squad is all well and good, but unless he is getting game-time, the Ireland newcomer would be as well off on loan.

It is no secret that Mourinho has a peculiar attitude towards promoting young players. It would appear that he just doesn’t trust them.

Christian Pulisic - according to one of his old coaches - snubbed Manchester United for that reason and later signed for Chelsea where he is now thriving.

At United, the likes of Marcus Rashford and Anthony Martial seemed to be in a permanent state of flux under the Portuguese.

His reputation is one of splashing the cash on established players who can make immediate impacts, rather than putting time into shaping what is already available.

The initial suggestion is that Mourinho does not feel the need to spend heavily in January, but we’ll see.

If that transpires, Parrott , 17, could yet have a role to play having already become a regular around the first-team squad after prolific goal scoring at underage level.

But if Mourinho says that Parrott - reportedly on Bayern Munich’s radar - is not in his plans, a loan move beckons.

For Ireland, the value of such an experience would be enormous heading into the Euro 2020 playoff in Slovakia.

As Robbie Keane, the Ireland assistant manager and former Tottenham striker, said this summer: “Harry Kane went to four teams on loan.”

Keane was answering a question in June as to whether Parrott would benefit from some time away from White Hart Lane.

And his advice?

“Go and play because you get so much experience being in front of a crowd instead of 25 people,” he said.

“That feeling of the crowd shouting, whether it’s 5,000 people or 10,000 - it’s pressure.. Is there pressure playing Under-23s, when nobody is watching you?.”

Keane continued: “It’s all about playing for young players, it doesn’t matter where you are.

“As long as you’re playing every week in the Championship, League One, wherever it may be, just go and play.”

But a penny for Parrott’s thoughts on Tuesday night as he checked his phone in the dressing room after scoring in the Irish Under-21’s rout of Sweden.

The axe fell on his old gaffer just before kick-off and it wasn’t a shock to see him kept away from any media duties afterwards.

Parrot owes Mauricio Pochettino everything. The Argentine blooded him in pre-season and then handed him a debut in the League Cup eight weeks ago.

He was then on the bench away to Everton the day Andre Gomes suffered a horror ankle injury. Harry Kane missed that game due to illness and Parrott was the fallback.

That’s how highly they rate him. But not even Parrott himself has a handle yet on what Mourinho makes of him.

But as England captain Kane discovered at Leyton Orient, Millwall, Norwich and Leicester, a taste of regular first-team football elsewhere can be the making of you.

And that wasn’t lost on Ireland boss Mick McCarthy who, after Monday’s draw with Denmark, was already turning his sights to the playoff in March.

McCarthy said of Parrott: “Let’s hope he plays somewhere and gets some football, and then we would have another (striker playing regularly).”
