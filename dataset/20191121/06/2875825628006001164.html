Padraic Joyce will bring attractive style of football as Galway boss says Kieran Fitzgerald

The two men played alongside each other when Galway won their last All-Ireland in 2001

21 Nov 2019, 06:00 by Pat Nolan

Padraic Joyce will bring an attractive style of football as the new Galway manager, says his former teammate Kieran Fitzgerald.

The two men played alongside each other when Galway won their last All-Ireland in 2001 but it has been pretty barren for them since, with last year’s All-Ireland semi-final appearance their first at that stage in 17 years.

Another ex-teammate, Kevin Walsh, oversaw an upturn in the team’s fortunes in recent years but he shipped criticism for what was viewed as a negative style of play, particularly at a time when Fitzgerald’s Corofin were earning rave reviews for their displays.

Fitzgerald said: “I remember Padraic, when I was playing with him, very knowledgeable guy.

“When he speaks, he talks, he says the right stuff. It was a kind of thing where he didn’t say an awful lot in the dressing room but when he was talking, he was talking sense.

“I have heard him on radio commentary since then and I think he is very good as well. He knows his football. He loves playing the go forward game.

“He wants kicking into the full-forward. He played full-forward for years and he wanted that ball in quick and early and he’ll definitely try and do that.”

Fitzgerald appreciates why Walsh was more conservative, however.

“We used to play attractive football but we used to concede huge, cricket scores, and I was part of it.

“We weren't getting anywhere, and I know where Kevin was coming from with that, because I know he commented before that the Kerry guys used to love playing Galway.

“Of course they did, because we used to let them play football.

“From a supporter’s point of view, I understood that you have to be defensively sound, and I know what Kevin was trying to do there. You have to be like that.

“But maybe with the players like Damien Comer, Shane Walsh, Brannigan, Ian Burke, we could have been a little bit more expansive, pushed the boat out a little bit more.”
