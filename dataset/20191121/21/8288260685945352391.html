Aston Villa transfer news | Speculation is starting to ramp up ahead of the re-opening of the transfer window in January when Dean Smith's side will be able to sign players

21 Nov 2019, 21:10 by Ben Husband, https://www.birminghammail.co.uk/authors/ben-husband/, Richard Cusack

January could be a crucial month for Aston Villa's hopes of surviving relegation back to the Championship.

After 12 matches - and ahead of a crucial Premier League clash with Newcastle United on Monday night - Dean Smith's side sit in 17th place in the table and three points ahead of Watford in the final relegation spot.

The New Year represents a chance for Smith and Villa to improve on their current squad and ship players out who are not needed.

Villa have been perennially linked with Alfredo Morelos of Rangers, with the Colombian hot-shot impressing in recent weeks.

But a couple of unfamiliar names have been touted with possible moves to Villa Park as speculation begins to ramp up ahead of the re-opening of the transfer window.

Here's every player linked with a move to Villa...

Nico Gaitan

Reports in The Guardian on Thursday claimed the Argentina international is on the radar of Villa and Premier League rivals West Ham United and Sheffield United.

It is said the Hammers hierarchy have held preliminary talks with Gaitan's representatives about a move, with the 31-year-old most recently of MLS side Chicago Fire.

He will be a free agent when his contract is up in December and there is an option to stay with Chicago for the 2020 season.

Christoph Monschein

Reports on the continent suggested the Austria international could be in line for a move to Villa for a cut-price €5 million fee.

He has scored 13 goals in 18 games this season for Austria Wien - and it is claimed that Dean Smith's side have sent scouts to watch him in action.

 

Alfredo Morelos

Morelos has been heavily linked with a move to Villa, his impressive performances north of the border highlighting a goal threat that Villa currently lack up front.

Rangers are desperate to keep hold of him, though, with boss Steven Gerrard and recruitment chief Ross Wilson issuing a firm 'not for sale' warning.

Glen Kamara

Villa are one of a number of clubs linked with the Finnish international. After arriving at Ibrox in January for £50k, the midfielder has flourished under Gerrard's leadership and they are expecting a major profit on their investment.

Along with Villa - Brighton, Burnley, Crystal Palace and Leeds United have all been credited with an interest.

Jarrod Bowen

A transfer rumour that could only happen in the 21st century.

The Hull City forward got tongues wagging, when he followed the club on Instagram last week.

The Leominster-born 22-year-old has been linked with big-money moves to Tottenham Hotspur, Newcastle United and Southampton.

Omar Elabdellaoui

Reports in the Daily Mail state that Villa are among a number of clubs chasing the Olympiacos defender, along with Leicester City, Watford, Bournemouth and Burnley.

Elabdellaoui's contract with the Greek top flight side is running out in the summer and the 27-year-old will be a free agent when it expires.

As things stand, the Norway international can sign a pre-contract agreement at the turn of the year with an English side.

Elabdellaoui left Manchester City to join Olympiacos in 2014 without making a single appearance for the current Premier League champions in a five-year stay.

Sam McCallum

The young full-back has impressed for Coventry City this season and has admitted to being 'flattered' by speculation linking him with a move out of League One.

Villa, Leeds United and West Bromwich Albion are all rumoured to be interested in the 19-year-old.

Eric Dier

Dier has been linked with Villa after Smith's side were placed among the favourites to sign him by the bookies.

The midfielder has struggled for minutes at Tottenham this season, making just three Premier League appearances so far.

Amid suggestions he could be made available for transfer, former Spurs boss Mauricio Pochettino told the Telegraph in September: "It has been difficult [for Dier]. Of course it is tough for him.

“He has to wait through training and then of course he has the opportunity. He has to try to fight for his place.

“We have a big squad, I need to take the decision performance by performance. The plan is the plan. We are going to assess the player and then make the decision.”

Manchester United are said to be Dier's most likely destination, followed by Everton, with Villa rated as third favourites to sign him.

 

Ben Chrisen

Chrisen is one for the future.

The 15-year-old midfielder, currently with Exeter City, is also said to be wanted by Chelsea , Liverpool and Bayern Munich.

Dennis Politic

Villa have been keen to sign young players with the potential to grow and 19-year-old Politic is on the radar according to Football Insider.

The former Manchester United academy talent has been catching the eye in League One this term for troubled Bolton, who are rooted to the bottom of the table.

The Trotters signed nine new players after their ownership crisis with the Romanian among the very few to have kept a place in the starting XI.

He has previously represented Romania at under-17 level and spent six years at United before joining Bolton's under-15s.

Politic has two goals to his name so far this campaign having played on both wings.

Olivier Giroud

Not a player linked with Villa necessarily, but the claret and blue faithful want to bring the Chelsea striker to Villa Park after hearing he will be available for a cut-price fee of £6m.

He would certainly be a good back-up to Wesley and bolster the forward department, but a move to Inter Milan and a reunion with former Chelsea coach Antonio Conte looks most likely.

RUMOURED DEPARTURES

John McGinn

McGinn's excellent early-season form saw calls for Manchester United and Liverpool to sign the Scotland international.

He has taken to the Premier League well, scoring three goals in 12 games including Villa's first back in the Premier League in the 3-1 defeat to Tottenham Hotspur.

Jack Grealish

All the talk about Grealish now is whether he will be able to shake off a calf injury in time for the Newcastle United match on November 25.

He was in fine form until he suffered the injury against Manchester City in October.

Jonathan Kodjia

Besiktas have been linked with a move for the out-of-favour striker.

The forward has seen his game-time limited upon Villa's return to the Premier League, the 30-year-old playing just 20 minutes of League football this season.

He's spent most of 2019-20 out injured with a cheekbone problem, but he returned to full fitness last month and managed just four minutes in two games.

Lovre Kalinic

Smith has been given the green light to sell Lovre Kalinic this summer, according to reports.

It is less than a year since the Croatia international made the move to B6, but he has endured a nightmare spell at Villa Park, with both parties now willing to cut their losses.

Football Insider have reported that Smith is planning to shift the 29-year-old, who Villa signed for £7m last January.

WHAT IS VILLA'S RECORD TRANSFER FEE PAID?

This is a bone of contention because of the way transfers are structured with add-ons and the like. In terms of an up-front payment, Wesley's move from Club Brugge is the current record fee paid by Villa at £22m.

WHAT IS VILLA'S RECORD TRANSFER FEE RECEIVED?

The reported £32.5m that Liverpool paid for Christian Benteke is by far and away the most the claret and blues have received for a player.
