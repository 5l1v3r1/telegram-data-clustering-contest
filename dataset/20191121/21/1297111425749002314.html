Teen charged in Regina coffee-throwing attacks posted online

A 14-year-old boy is now charged in connection with two coffee-throwing attacks that happened in east Regina during the summer.

21 Nov 2019, 21:54 by Regina Leader-Post

A 14-year-old boy from Wadena is now charged in connection with two coffee-throwing attacks that happened in east Regina during the summer.

Video of the attacks began circulating on Facebook at the time, which lead to a police investigation.

The attacks happened on July 30 in a parking lot near the stores Walmart and Winners. Falgun Vaviya, a university student from India who works at the Walmart, was sitting outside on a bench when a group of young men approached him just before 9 p.m. and threw coffee in his face. 

In the widely-distributed video, one of the boys says “excuse me” and when Vaviya looks up, the coffee is splashed on him.

The coffee was not hot, but the incident was still shocking for the 19-year-old Vaviya.

Shortly after the first attack, a 54-year-old man also had coffee thrown at him. Police said this attack happened at a nearby shopping center parking lot.

On Thursday, Regina police issued a release stating members of the Wadena RCMP detachment located and charged a 14-year-old boy with two counts of assault with a weapon.

The teen is scheduled to appear in Provincial Youth Court in Regina on Dec. 5. The youth cannot be named in accordance with provisions under the Youth Criminal Justice Act.

Wadena is located approximately 207 kilometres northeast of Regina.
