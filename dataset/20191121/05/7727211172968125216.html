Special surprise for 7-year-old who asked Santa for food and a house

So many people have responded to the heartbreaking letter - and now Christmas is going to be a lot better for the family

21 Nov 2019, 05:00 by Liam Thorp

A 7-year-old child who wrote a letter to Father Christmas asking for a house and some food for her family is set to get some amazing Christmas surprises after a staggering amount of money was raised.

This week the ECHO reported how Liverpool Councillor Gerard Woodhouse - who runs the L6 Community Centre in Everton - found the heartbreaking letter in the Santa Postbox in the centre.

It said: "Dear Father Christmas. Can you help? Can we have a home for Christmas?

"Mam wants us to be all together, can you give us some food and can I just have just nice doll for Christmas. Thank you."

Since the note was made public, a huge number of people have reached out to offer support to the family and to the L6 Centre that helps many struggling people in north Liverpool.

Cllr Woodhouse said that since the letter was featured in the ECHO and shared on social media, the centre has received close to Â£10,000 in donations.

He explained how this money will be used to help the child's family and others.

He said: "We cannot thank the public enough.

"All the money raised will be spend on supporting families who are referred to us to try and make there Christmas special.

"We are supporting the family of the child who wrote the letter with presents and a 3-day stay in a hotel over the Christmas period for the mum and kids, who do not usually live together."

The plight of the child and their family has touched hearts all across the country.

Amongst those offering support have been a local footballer and reality television star Gemma Collins.
