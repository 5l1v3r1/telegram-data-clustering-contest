AFL draft 2019, pick swaps, AFL trades, Fremantle, Melbourne, Adelaide, Liam Henry

21 Nov 2019, 05:11 by Matt Balmer

Fremantle has avoided a potential draft night disaster after taking part in a three-way trade along with Melbourne and Adelaide.

With next generation academy prospect Liam Henry drawing interest from a number of clubs, namely Melbourne (Pick 8) and Carlton (Pick 9), the Dockers were stuck in a hard place with Pick 10 in the draft.

While the Dockers had the option of trading Pick 10 when on the clock, the club has elected to do a deal before the night.

First, the Crows and Dockers struck a deal which allowed for the Dockers to receive 119 extra points which will help in matching Henry’s bid.

The Crows received Pick 22 and a 2020 third-round pick (tied to Collingwood) in exchange for Pick 28, 49 and a 2020 fourth-round pick (tied to Carlton).

“We weren’t going to use pick 49 on the night so it was great we could improve our draft position in 2019 and 2020,” Adelaide list manager Justin Reid explained.

Then the Dockers dealt Pick 10, Pick 28 and a 2020 fourth-round pick to the Demons, in return for Pick 8.

It now means Fremantle will walk into the draft armed with back-to-back picks, with the club likely to consider Victorians Hayden Young and Caleb Serong at Pick 7 and 8.

The Demons’ third selection in the draft was previously Pick 97, but now is Pick 28, allowing for the club to take three selections inside the top 30.

Trading of selections will close for all AFL clubs on Friday November 22.
