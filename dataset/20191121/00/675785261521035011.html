Love Island's Jourdan 'signs six figure contract' with Kendall Jenner's modelling agency

The brunette beauty will embark on a new adventure and model with the same agency as Hollywood stars

21 Nov 2019, 00:01 by Brogan-Leigh Hurst

Love Island's Jourdan Riane has taken her new-found fame to the next level and bagged a contract with Kendall Jenner's modelling agency.

The social media sensation, 24, will be working with top modelling agency Elite - who work with the KUWTK star - in a "huge deal".

Jourdan signed the contract after returning from Las Vegas where she completed her first presenting job at the red carpet of the BET Soul Train Awards.

She shared the huge experience with her family who joined her on the red carpet.

On Instagram , the overwhelmed star wrote: "Where I go, they go. When I win, they win. So blessed to have been able to take my family with me whilst hosting the  @soultrain  awards red carpet with  @bet_uk ! Blessings. My Nan would be so proud️".

 

The beauty icon's new venture has come just weeks after she split with Danny Williams, 21.

Commenting on the new signing, an Elite representative said: "Elite London are proud to sign Jourdan Riane to their board.

"Jourdan's vibrant style and infectious personality is perfect for our client base.

 

"We're very excited about the future with Jourdan."

Jourdan and Danny met in the villa during 2019's series of Love Island and became one of the quickest couples to go official.

They even moved in together, but have since split because of "lack of respect, disloyalty and dishonesty."

 

Sharing a snap of herself looking sensational , she penned a lengthy caption to explain her position and reassure fans she's doing fine.

"I have to respect me first," Jourdan wrote, "I genuinely had such a great time in my relationship and I don't regret the decisions I've made as far as choosing to give someone a chance however, I have boundaries.

"In a relationship there are things I won't tolerate. Lack of respect, disloyalty & dishonesty are amongst those things.

 

"I know people will come with the 'I told you so', 'you should've listened' (maybe it's true) & more than likely snigger/laugh at my situation.

"The reality is my mum raised me to be a strong woman and always give the benefit of the doubt and let people prove themselves to you. Which is what I did.

"I went into this with a genuine heart & real intention to make it work. The love was as real as the hurt is now. But...I will continue to grow and love myself first.

"If it doesn't feel right, it's not right."
