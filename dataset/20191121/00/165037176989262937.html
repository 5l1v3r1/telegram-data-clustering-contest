"Mr. Chelsea? I am also Mr. Porto and Mr. Real Madrid"

21 Nov 2019 by BeSoccer

New Tottenham coach, Jose Mourinho, was presented to the London team and was questioned about his 'Spurs' phrase when he was Chelsea manager, but he also sent a caring message to Mauricio Pochettino.

"I come with a bit of sadness for his exit. Pochettino can come when he wants. When he misses the players, when he misses the people he works with. The door is always open for him.” He will find another great club and will have a great future," he explained.

Mourinho told reporters that he had a good break: "I was a little lost, but I did well. I even learned to be an expert on television."

Regarding his phrase ("I would never coach Tottenham")m Mourinho stated that he said this "before being fired" from Chelsea. "Mr. Chelsea? I am Mr. Porto and Mr. Real Madrid and other clubs as well, I am whatever club I am at", he said.

Moreover, he analysed his competitive gene and didn't blame anybody for his failures. "If you are happy by losing football matches, it’s difficult to be a winner in any moment of your career. I was always humble but it was in my way. The problem was that you didn’t understand that", he stated.
