3 charged in high school football game shooting due in court

21 Nov 2019

MAYS LANDING, N.J. (AP) - Three of the six men charged in a shooting at a high school football game that led to the death of a 10-year-old spectator are due to make their initial court appearances.

Michael Mack and Tyrell Dorn, both of Atlantic City, and Vance Golden, of Pleasantville, all face weapons charges and will be in court Thursday.

The counts stem from Friday’s shooting in Pleasantville, which left a man and two children wounded. One of the children, Micah Tennant, died Wednesday from his wounds, while a 27-year-old man - who authorities have said was the target of the shooting - remains hospitalized in critical condition.

A 15-year-old boy was treated for a graze wound.

The suspected gunman is now facing murder and weapons charges. The wounded man also faces weapons charges.
