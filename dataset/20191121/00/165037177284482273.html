"I will retire at Barça, I see myself playing until 2022"

21 Nov 2019 by BeSoccer

Gerard Piqué gave an extensive interview to 'Mundo Deportivo'. The FC Barcelona central defender went over all Barcelona related news: his retirement, Griezmann, Valverde, the Davis Cup, his more personal side...

Before returning to the pitch after the international break, Gerard Pique is promoting the new Davis Cup that is currently being played in Madrid. A key player for Spain and one of Barcelona's captains, the player spoke at length in a personal interview.

The central defender, beloved by fan, openly recognized he is thinking about the end of his time as a footballer: "I've always said, my last team will be Barça. I don't see myself wearing any other shirt than Barça's. For me, being in Barça is the greatest dream of all, no matter how much the creating f the new Davis Cup is also one of my dreams."

"My intention is to stop playing by 2022, but I do not have a crystal ball and I do not know if I will be able to. If I see that I lose importance or I see that I am not at the level I have no problem in leaving before. I think it will not happen because I know me and I see myself able to hold until 2022, but you never know, "he added.

The captain showed his love for Barcelona, but he's never been secretive about the fact that he has other interests. In this regard, Davis Cup is his new hobby: "There are players who have restaurants or other businesses, others play video games, spend their days on their phones, others devote their time to being with their families ... each devotes his time to what he thinks fills him most."

Also, Piqué continued in the same line: "We know that our careers are short, limited and when it is finished I would like to be able to do other things and make sure that it's not all over with "Pique the player", I would like there to be more than just football".

Finally, Barça's '4' showed his more personal side and spoke of his ritual before the games. "On match days I sleep 10 hours at night and nap for two hours. If it's true that there are some days I sleep less, but not just to organize the Davis Cup, it's also because I like the NBA and the games are late, I'm sure other people do it," he concluded.
