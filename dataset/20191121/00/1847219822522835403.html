Google, Facebook business incompatible with privacy rights, says Amnesty International

21 Nov 2019 by BNN

Human rights organisation Amnesty International has found in a study that the «surveillance-based business model» of Facebook and Google’s is incompatible with the right to privacy. The U.S. companies disagree.

German public broadcaster Deutsche Welle reports on Thursday, November 21, that Amnesty International in a report has said tech giants Facebook and Google should be forced to abandon what it calls a «surveillance-based business model» that is «predicated on human rights abuse.»

«Despite the real value of the services they provide, Google and Facebook’s platforms come at a systemic cost,» the human rights group said in its 60-page report this week.

Amnesty believes that by gathering up personal data to feed advertising businesses, the two firms carry out an unprecedented assault on privacy rights.

U.S. firms disagree

Facebook disagreed with the report’s findings. Steve Satterfield, the company’s public policy director, rejected the notion that the business model was «surveillance-based» and noted that users sign up voluntarily for the service.

Google also disputed Amnesty’s findings, DW reports.
