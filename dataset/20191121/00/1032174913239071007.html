More Tram Strikes to Hit Melbourne Next Week

Expect short-term shutdowns in November and December.

21 Nov 2019 by Nick Buckley

The pay dispute between Melbourne tram workers and Yarra Trams continues, as negotiations on a new workplace agreement remain at an impasse.

Tram workers walked off the job in August, led by the Rail, Tram and Bus Union (RTBU). They will do so again the next two Thursdays.

On November 28, and again on December 5, about 1500 drivers and customer-service personnel will stop working between 10am and 2pm.

The RTBU is currently seeking a six per cent annual pay rise over three years, while Yarra Trams is offering a four per cent pay rise over four years. Yarra Trams also wants to lift the cap on the number of part-time workers in its workforce.

Buses will replace trams during the strikes but will run less frequently.

For updates on train and tram services, head to ptv.vic.gov.au.
