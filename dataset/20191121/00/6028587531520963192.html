2020 Michelin Stars in Spain and Portugal: Who Got the New Stars?

21 Nov 2019 by Ann Abel

In the weeks and days leading up to the biggest night of the year for the Iberian fine-dining world, rumors and predictions were bubbling up. Spanish and Portuguese food critics and bloggers had lots of expectations (and suggestions) for the Michelin evaluators.Would one of Spain’s three- or even two-star restaurants lose its luster? (I don’t know—I’m here to celebrate the winners.) Would a Portuguese chef finally get a third star? (Sadly no.) Would local critics’ darlings or popular favorites like the dazzling, sky-high Fifty Seconds by Martín Berasategui in Lisbon be recognized? (Yes!)

So it was with great expectation that hundreds of leading figures in culture and gastronomy, journalists, food critics, and—of course—chefs in gathered tonight in Seville for the presentation of the 2020 Michelin Guide to Spain and Portugal.

The choice of location reflects Andalusia’s rise on the fine-dining scene: In 2009, the region had only eight restaurants with Michelin stars. In 2019, the number was 18 with stars, although there were 173 restaurants in the guide. The gala dinner featured dishes prepared by Michelin-star chefs in Andalusia, coordinated by Ángel León, whose restaurant Aponiente holds three stars.

The biggest news was that Cenador de Amos in Cantabria deservedly won its third star. Gaining their second star were Angle in Barcelona, Bardal in Málaga, cas in Valencia and Casa de Chá da Boa Nova in Porto.

And a diverse collection of restaurants were awarded with their first star this year.

Congratulations to all of the restaurants. The newly starred restaurants are listed below. (For a full rundown of the ceremony and a complete list of Michelin star restaurants in Spain and Portugal, see this report from Barcelona-based Bon Vivant, experts in all things gastronomical.)
