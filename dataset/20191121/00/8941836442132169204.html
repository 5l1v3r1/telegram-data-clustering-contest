Coldplay stops touring until concerts are more environmentally friendl

21 Nov 2019 by Jessica Chasmar

British band Coldplay won’t be touring for their new album “Everyday Life” while they take a break to try to develop more environmentally friendly concerts.

“We’ve done a lot of big tours at this point. How do we turn it around so it’s not so much taking as giving?” frontman Chris Martin told BBC News. “All of us have to work out the best way of doing our job.”

“We’re taking time over the next year or two, to work out how our tour can not only be sustainable [but] how can it be actively beneficial,” he added.

Coldplay’s last tour for their 2015 “A Head Full of Dreams” album included 122 shows and spanned five continents.

Mr. Martin said their goal for the next major tour is to be carbon neutral.

“Our next tour will be the best possible version of a tour like that environmentally,” he told the BBC. “We would be disappointed if it’s not carbon neutral.

“The hardest thing is the flying side of things,” he said. “But, for example, our dream is to have a show with no single use plastic, to have it largely solar powered.”

Friday’s release of “Everyday Life” will be followed by two concerts on the same day in Amman, Jordan — one at sunrise and one at sunset — which will be broadcast for free on YouTube.

Coldplay will also be performing an environmental awareness benefit at the Natural History Museum in London on Monday.
