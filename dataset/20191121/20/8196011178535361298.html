Evening Round Up: Migrants ferry in Rosslare; Arrests in Lunney case; Israeli PM

21 Nov 2019, 20:55

So that’s Thursday nearly wrapped up. Here’s some of the stories we published on irishexaminer.com today which we hope will help you make sense of it all this evening.

TO INFORM

Migrants: Gardaí have announced that they are investigating "potential breaches of Immigration Legislation" after 16 people were found aboard a ferry which has arrived in Rosslare.

Lunney: Gardaí have arrested an additional two men in relation to the abduction of Kevin Lunney, bringing the total number to four.

Body: The body of a man in his 50s was discovered on November 20, after Gardaí received a report of a sudden death at a house in Killarney.

Child abuse: Six women and five men have been remanded on bail to appear before the Central Criminal Court for trial, on charges of more than 200 counts of sexual offences and neglect of children.

Isreali PM: Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu has been charged in a series of corruption scandals.

Schmidt: Joe Schmidt pinpointed a failure to prioritise last season’s Six Nations as a mistake that sowed the seeds of Ireland’s World Cup disappointment nine months later.
