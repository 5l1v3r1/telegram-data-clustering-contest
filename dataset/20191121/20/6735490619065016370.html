Kathie Lee Gifford Moved To Nashville Because She Was ‘Dying Of Loneliness’ After Death Of Frank Gifford

21 Nov 2019, 20:36 by Shakiel Mahjouri

Kathie Lee Gifford was looking for a sense of community following the death of her husband.

The former “Today” show host opened up about her life after Frank Gifford’s passing in an interview with Nashville Tennessean. Gifford, 66, said she yearned for a sense of community in the aftermath of her husband’s death in 2015. That prompted her to move from Connecticut to Nashville.

“I moved here because I was dying of loneliness,” she explained. “It’s a culture of kindness in Nashville,” she continued, describing locals as “authentically kind” and “joyful.” Of her Connecticut mansion, she said, “that huge beautiful memory-filled home was like a morgue to me.”

Gifford also touched on how her family life, or lack thereof, allows her more time to pursue writing and other projects.

“Here’s the bad news – I’m a widow, an orphan, and an empty-nester. The good news is, I have the freedom of a widow, an orphan and an empty-nester,” she said. “I’ve got all the time to spend my days writing. I’m having the life I could’ve only ever dreamed of.”

Frank died of natural causes at his Greenwich, Connecticut home, a week before his 85th birthday. Gifford and her husband share two children: Cody, 29, and Cassidy Erin, 26.
