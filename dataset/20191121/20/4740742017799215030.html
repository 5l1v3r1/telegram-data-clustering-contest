What top stars are being paid on iTV's I'm a Celebrity 2019

Ian Wright and Nadine Coyle are among the highest-paid celebrities

21 Nov 2019, 20:22 by Nathan Bevan, Emma Grimshaw

We all love to watch famous faces being scared to death as they have to eat live cockroaches or walk through a tank of snakes.

But don't have too much sympathy for them because it turns out the stars on I'm A Celebrity are actually being paid a small fortune to take on through the gruelling challenges.

Indeed, like the tasks set before the celebs in the regular Bushtucker Trails, some of the amounts are eye-watering, reports Wales Online.

American television personality Caitlyn Jenner will be earning around £500,000 – one of the highest figures ever paid - Bristol's Noel Edmonds was the only other participant to match that sum.

Close behind Jenner is ex-footballer-turned-sports pundit Ian Wright, who's reportedly netting £400,000.

And Girls Aloud songstress Nadine Coyle is taking home a whopping £250,000.

Good Morning Britain presenter Kate Garraway and former EastEnders acrtress Jacqueline Jossa are apparently making £75,000 each, with £50k going to both DJ Adele Roberts and retired England rugby player James Haskell.

Some of the lowest earners include Capital FM DJ Roman Kemp who is being paid  £40,000 and X Factor winner Myles Stephenson gets £25,000.

Meanwhile, it's thought Coronation Street star Andrew 'Kirk' Whyment will be entering the jungle later this week, thereby pocketing £100k for his trouble.

I'm a Celebrity hosts Ant McPartlin and Declan Donnelly joked: “Most people come into the jungle to lose a few pounds, but [Caitlyn’s] going to be gaining 500,000 of them according to the papers.”
