I'm A Celebrity contestant being paid £25,000 a day for jungle stint

The contestants on I'm A Celebrity are getting taking home different pay cheques but one is getting substantially more

21 Nov 2019, 20:37 by Simon Duke

It's common knowledge that the pay cheques for I'm A Celebrity differ vastly from star to star but there is one this year who is on significantly more than the rest.

You might be surprised by just how little - or how much - some of the celebrities earn from their stint in the jungle.

A TV insider confessed to The Sun that ITV bosses have splurged over £1.4 million on the 2019 line-up and while Kate Garraway and Jacqueline Jossa have reportedly agreed on £75,000 for their fees.

Rugby star James Haskell and DJ Adele Roberts fall slightly behind with their rumoured £50,000 cheques, there is one star who the channel is said to have broken the bank to get into the jungle.

And it might not come as too much shock to learn that celeb is Caitlyn Jenner, who is thought to be taking home around £500,000 - which works out at around £25,000 a day.

Nearest to Caitlyn on the I'm A Celebrity earnings table is said to be Ian Wright, who is getting £400,000, while Nadine Coyle isn't too far behind on £250,000.

While he has just gone into the jungle as a new arrival, Andrew Whyment, better known to millions of viewers as Kirk Sutherland on Coronation Street, is said to be on £100,000.

This year's lowest paid celeb is thought to be Myles Stephenson, a member of Rak-Su, who won The X Factor in 2017. His total cheque is said to be £25,000, what Caitlyn is earning per day!

ITV have declined to comment on any of the pay cheque reports.
