ESPN Analyst Thinks Tua Tagovailoa Should Return To Alabama

21 Nov 2019, 20:20 by Tzvi Machlin

After a hip injury ended Tua Tagovailoa’s season last week, just about everyone thinks it’s a no-brainer for him to forego his senior year and enter the NFL draft. But at least one ESPN analyst thinks that there’s some merit to returning to Alabama in 2020.

On Thursday’s edition of Get Up!, ESPN analyst Emmanuel Acho asserted that from a monetary standpoint, it might make sense for him to go back. Acho explained that Tua has already potentially lost draft position – and money by extension – because of his injuries.

Acho ultimately concluded that if Tua plays another year and doesn’t get injured in 2020, he could regain top prospect status the season after.

Via Saturday Down South:

“It pains me to say this, I think Tua has to go back to school,” Emmanuel Acho said. “And it is monetarily speaking, what does Tua have to lose by going back to school? If he gets hurt again, he already has the injury notion that he is injury prone but if he goes back and he plays clean, you are talking about 15-plus million dollars guaranteed … I’m not for college athletes going back typically but in this case, Tua has more to gain by going back.”
