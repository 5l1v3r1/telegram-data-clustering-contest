Aston Villa latest news | Dean Smith's side will be in action on Boxing Day when they welcome Norwich City to Villa Park but they won't be on Sky Sports or BT Sport

21 Nov 2019, 19:19 by Elliott Jackson

With the Christmas football period growing ever closer, fans hoping to tuck into a feast of Boxing Day football may have been frustrated to discover Amazon have the right to all 10 Premier League matches.

Clashes such as Leicester City vs Liverpool and Wolves vs Manchester City will all be shown on Amazon this year.

Aston Villa's Boxing Day date with Norwich City at home will also be screened on the platform.

They became the first streaming service to enter the Premier League rights market, claiming all ten fixtures on matchday 15 and 19, including the Boxing Day football.

With supporters paying out for subscription services for Sky Sports and BT Sport already, another competitor in the market has only complicated matters for fans wanting the ultimate football package.

However, you can watch all of Amazon matches for free and we've got all the details on how to do so.

How can I watch the games for free?

If like most football fans, you're already forking out for Sky Sports and BT Sport, the last thing you need is an extra expense, especially around Christmas.

No bother, as it stands, you can watch all of Amazon's fixtures for free by taking out their 30-day Amazon Prime trial.

After the free trial expires, you will be charged £7.99, so make sure to set a reminder if you're not bothered about those next-day-delivery perks.

Of course, if you time it right, you can start your trial before their first batch of matches start and cancel after Boxing Day when all the football has finished.

What presenters and pundits are on?

Whilst there is yet to be an official announcement on who will head Amazon’s Premier League coverage, there have been some star-studded names linked.

Gabby Logan could be in line to anchor as Amazon’s main presenter, with former footballers such as Peter Crouch and comedian John Bishop also linked.

To add the X-factor, former Arsenal striker and new Montreal Impact manager Thierry Henry could join the panel.

How can I watch on Amazon Prime?

The beauty of an online streaming service is that there are multiple ways to watch Amazon Prime and not miss a kick of the action.

You can stream Amazon through any tablet, laptop and most Smart TVs, all you need is a device that can connect to the internet.

You can also watch Amazon Prime directly through your TV if you’re a Virgin TV customer with the use of the V6 TV set top box, however, this isn’t available via Freeview or through Sky or BT.
