Anu Malik resigns from ‘Indian Idol 11’

21 Nov 2019, 19:48 by Raj Baddhan

After much furore about his return to the Sony Entertainment Television series, ‘Indian Idol’, musician Anu Malik has stepped down from his role as judge.

Malik had been under huge pressure to resign after he was accused of sexual allegations by a number of singers including Sona Mohapatra, Neha Bhasin and Shweta Pandit. Malik walked away from the series half-way last year due to the claims. However, his position was reinstated this year.

Mohapatra had regularly campaigned for him to leave the show. Upon hearing the news today, Mohapatra branded the decision, a “symbolic victory”. She added, “It’s great news. Sony TV took a long time to do this but I am happy that he has finally stepped down from the show. It’s a battle of the whole country. There are so many people who did not want to see this person (Anu Malik) flaunting himself on national television because it gives a lot of wrong messages to predators that they can also get away with such a thing.”

She said, “I was fighting for fairness and justice. Now, after hearing this news, I think it is a victory for everybody – not just me but also for all other women who were badly treated by him. It is a symbolic victory. Our fight is not over yet, it’s just a start. We are not going to sit here and let people to take us for granted.”
