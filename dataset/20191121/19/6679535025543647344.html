Protests, delays mark Day Three of arts fete

Thiruvananthapuram North leads in HSS category

21 Nov 2019, 19:21 by Staff Reporter

Thiruvananthapuram North sub-district led the points tally in the higher secondary school (HSS) category at the Revenue District School Arts Festival on Thursday.

With the curtain set to come down on the festival on Friday, Thiruvananthapuram South sub-district was ahead in the high school, and Kilimanoor in the upper primary segment. As many as 239 events were completed.

The third day of the festival too was not without its share of protests.

In the evening, the group dance high schoool event was marred by protests after the declaration of results. The police had to be called in to diffuse the situation and an appeal was accepted from the protesters.

Corruption alleged

The folk song higher secondary event saw corruption allegations being levelled against the judges by a section of students.

Delayed starts continued to plague the events as did the rain.

In the higher secondary category, Thiruvananthapuram North, with 320 points, had a slight edge over Thiruvananthapuram South that had 311 points. Kilimanoor followed with 286 points.

Thiruvananthapuram South led the table in the high school segment with 260 points.

Thiruvananthapuram North was close on its heels with 258 points and Kilimanooor was in third position with 246 points.

Kilimanoor was ahead in the upper primary category though with 116 points.

Government Girls Higher Secondary School, Cotton Hill, was leading among the schools with 98 points, followed by Carmel Girls Higher Secondary School, Vazhuthacaud, at 93 points.
