Spurs Transfer News: Seven In, Five Out?

21 Nov 2019, 19:16 by David Kappel

With Jose Mourinho taking over at Tottenham Hotspur, the Portuguese tactician is expected shake things up in his new squad. Several players are now being linked with Spurs, while there are some who could be set to leave.

The 56-year-old has replaced Mauricio Pochettino following a string of poor results that saw the Argentine, who guided Spurs to the UEFA Champions League final last season, being relieved of his duties.

Mourinho is well known for splashing the cash in the transfer market, and is expected to make some changes to his new squad over the next two windows.

It remains to be seen whether or not he can convince chairman Daniel Levy, who is known for not spending too much, to open the cheque book for new players, but who are the stars on the club's radar at the moment?

Early reports suggest that Mourinho could attempt to improve in three areas, namely the right-back position, at centre-back and up front.

Kieran Tipper, who left for Atletico Madrid at the beginning of this season, has never been replaced on the right side of defence, and Mourinho could look to Youcef Atal as a perfect fit for his system. The OGC Nice and Algeria defender was one of the standout performers during his country's 2019 Africa Cup of Nations triumph, and has been making positive headlines in Ligue 1 too.

Further up front, Paulo Dybala could become the club's No. 1 priority after Spurs reportedly looked at him in the previous window, and the Argentine seemed open to the idea of leaving Juventus. Free agent Zlatan Ibrahimovic could also be a short-term solution as a back-up for Harry Kane, and add some much-needed experience to the young squad.
