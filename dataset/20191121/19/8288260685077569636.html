Bournemouth suffer key injury blow ahead of Wolves clash - report

Wolverhampton Wanderers latest - Nuno Espirito Santo's men are back in action this Saturday against Bournemouth following the international break

21 Nov 2019, 19:23 by Jamie Kemble

AFC Bournemouth appear to have suffered a key injury blow ahead of their clash with Wolverhampton Wanderers this weekend.

The Cherries and Wolves go head-to-head on the South Coast on Saturday with the two sides sitting side-by-side in the table, separated by a solitary goal.

The clash should prove evenly-matched, but Wolves' chances of picking up a valuable away three points may have been boosted.

That's because reports claim Bournemouth's starting striker Joshua King has suffered a hamstring injury.

King was described as 'a little sore' by local media after exiting Norway's Euro 2020 qualifier against Malta with just one minute left on the clock.

The former Manchester United frontman scored the opener as Norway won 2-1, but they failed to achieve automatic qualification, finishing third.

King has scored three times for Bournemouth this season, including a winner against former club United.

If reports are true and he has suffered a hamstring injury, he may not be able to inflict the same level of damage on Wolves this weekend.
