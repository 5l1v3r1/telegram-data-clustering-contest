James Maddison makes Manchester United transfer decision as Arsenal dealt blow - Premier League rumours

Latest Premier League transfer news | With the New Year on the horizon that means the January transfer window will open Aston Villa and their top flight rivals can start spending again

21 Nov 2019, 19:44 by Connor O’Neill

The Premier League returns this weekend, with points to be gained at both the top and bottom end of the table.

While the January transfer window is also just around the corner, with plenty of clubs expected to be looking to add to their squads in the New Year.

Aston Villa are one of them and they have seen speculation around their side ramp up over the past few days, with Nico Gaitan and Christoph Monschein linked with moves to B6 on Thursday.

Gaitan will be a free agent in December when his contract with MLS side Chicago Fire expires while Monschein has netted 13 goals in the Austrian top flight this season.

But what of their top flight rivals?

And here’s a round-up of the latest transfer news, rumours and gossip around the Premier League ...

Maddison to snub Manchester United

James Maddison is considering signing a new contract with Leicester despite strong interest from Manchester United.

United have earmarked Maddison as a long-term target but the MEN are reporting that the England international could re-sign with Leicester.

The report also states that a new deal with Leicester is not close and Maddison could wait until the end of the season before deciding on his future.

Mourinho eyes European pair

Bruno Fernandes and Pablo Dybala could be back on Spurs' radar in January.

Dybala was close to joining Tottenham in the summer under Mauricio Pochettino but a deal collapsed towards the end of the window, while Fernandes was also linked with north London club throughout the summer.

And now the Express are reporting that new Spurs boss Jose Mourinho is keen on the pair in the winter window.

Aubameyang to reject new Arsenal deal

Pierre-Emerick Aubameyang is set to reject a new Arsenal deal.

According to Spanish newspaper Sport, Aubameyang is waiting to see whether Barcelona make a move for him.

The Gabon striker's current deal runs until 2021, but the Gunners are keen to extend.

 

Tottenham considered both Rodgers and Nagelsmann

Tottenham considered both Brendan Rodgers and Julian Nagelsmann as candidates to replace Mauricio Pochettino, before appointing Jose Mourinho.

Mourinho was appointed as Tottenham manager on Wednesday morning, but it according to reports, he was not the club’s first choice.

But according to the Mail , Rodgers and Nagelsmann were both considered for the role, but

Spurs thought it would be too difficult to secure them.

Pochettino on Bayern’s radar

Mauricio Pochettino is being considered by Bayern Munich to take over as manager at the Allianz Arena.

Although, according to Sky Sports , no contact has been made with the Argentine following his sacking by Tottenham on Tuesday, it is believed he is on their shortlist of candidates.

Bayern have been without a permanent manager after Niko Kovac was sacked by the German giants at the start of November.
