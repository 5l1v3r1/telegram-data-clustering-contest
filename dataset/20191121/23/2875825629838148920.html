Terrified Jacqueline Jossa forced to face next trial and chooses Myles to join

Jacqueline Jossa looked visibly shaken as she found out she would be completing her next Bushtucker trial tomorrow

21 Nov 2019, 23:31 by Irishmirror.ie

Jacqueline Jossa looked terrified in tonight's I'm a Celebrity... Get Me Out Of Here!

She found out the horrifying news that she would be completing tomorrow's Bushtucker Trial, Snake Hotel.

Her eyes went wide before she laughed nervously.

Her camp mates immediately rushed to comfort her as she threw her head in her hands and exclaimed "No! On my own!?"

Rallying around her, her campmates rubbed her back and assured her she would be fine.

Myles Stephenson hugged her as she complained she was especially gutted to be doing the task alone.

But this isn't the case.

Extra Camp revealed that Jacqueline was given a choice of campmate to do the task with her, and she chose Myles.

The pair will hunt for stars tomorrow night in a snake-infested hotel room in order to win meals for their campmates.

The night saw some tension in the famously friendly camp.

After achieving eight stars in their Bushtucker Trial, new campmates Cliff Parisi and Andy Whyment became Outlaws and were sent to the Jungle Jailhouse.

The main camp had the opportunity to be "good, bad or downright ugly" - and either keep all the food themselves, or choose to feed the outlaws with their share of food.

The twist with the second option was that two campmates would have to join the outlaws in the Jailhouse - but tempers flared over a difference in opinion.

Andrew Maxwell and James Haskell both agreed they were happy to go to the jailhouse, while Kate Garraway and Ian Wright also said they would sacrifice themselves.

Former footballer Ian suggested they should draw straws, so the group picked out named water bottles to decide who would go.

However, Jacqueline said it was "scary" and admitted she did not want to leave the main camp.

Kate asked if she would rather not go, while Ian said she didn't have to, but Jacqueline confessed: "Yeah but it’s not very heroic."

Comedian Andrew Maxwell did not see the funny side and insisted everyone should be in the hat.

He said: "I think it has to be everyone in, I don’t think it’s fair to count yourself out."

Rugby star James said Jacqueline should not be forced to go, but Andrew argued: "It’s an ordeal that has to be shared."
