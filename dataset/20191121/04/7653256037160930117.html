Infy says dealt with plaint as per law

In October, Infosys told the stock exchanges that it had received whistleblowers' complaints.

21 Nov 2019, 04:35 by ET Bureau

Infosys has complied with rules by India's markets regulator and its internal guidelines, on a whistleblower complaint, it said, adding fresh media reports were creating a mistaken impression.

"It is clarified that the media reports refer to substantially the same allegations made in the undated whistleblower complaint referred to in the disclosure made by the Company on October 22, 2019 to the stock exchanges," Infosys told stock exchanges, adding it had previously provided all the necessary information.

If required, the company can provide any additional information or clarification, it noted. In October, Infosys told the stock exchanges that it had received anonymous whistleblowers' complaints alleging certain unethical practices by the top management.

Infosys Chairman Nandan Nilekani said the whistleblower complaint dated September 20, as well as an undated complaint, were received by one of the board members on September 30.

ET reported on October 21 that a letter signed by 'Ethical Employees' had alleged that Infosys CEO Salil Parikh and chief financial officer Nilanjan Roy were involved in financial malfaesance.
