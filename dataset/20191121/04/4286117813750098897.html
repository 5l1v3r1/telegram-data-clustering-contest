Wording woes: Secularism in CMP? Congress keen, NCP says think again

Sources say the Congress was keen on using "secularism" in the prospective Maharashtra alliance's common minimum programme. But one NCP leader stepped in and offered an alternative, with the Shiv Sena in mind.

21 Nov 2019, 04:51

HIGHLIGHTS

  • Congress, NCP hold meet as govt formation talks continue
  • NCP leader suggests avoiding use of word 'secularism' in CMP: Sources
  • Congress's Jairam Ramesh, brought in to prepare draft, accepts: Sources

A Nationalist Congress Party (NCP) leader pre-empted a possible point of contention between the Congress and the Shiv Sena on Thursday, when he suggested replacing the word "secularism" with more indirect phrasing in a key document.

The three parties are working to form a government together in Maharashtra, which was placed under President's Rule this month.

Sources say the Congress was keen on using "secularism" in the prospective alliance's common minimum programme (CMP). But at a meeting of Congress and NCP leaders on Wednesday, one NCP leader suggested a wordier alternative: "...will adhere to Preamble of Indian Constitution".

The Preamble, of course, says India is a sovereign, socialist, secular and democratic republic.

Congress leader Jairam Ramesh (far right in the photo above, sitting with a laptop) accepted the suggestion, sources say. He was brought in by his party to help prepare the draft of the CMP.

Congress leaders left after the meeting. Shiv Sena leader Sanjay Raut was then given a briefing about the draft by NCP leaders. Shiv Sena president Uddhav Thackeray was also informed.

President's Rule was imposed in Maharashtra this month following a prolonged delay in government formation in the wake of an October state election that the Shiv Sena and the BJP won together.

The two former allies fell out bitterly, unable to agree that the chief minister's job should be shared using a rotation policy.

'DECISION ON MAHA GOVT IN 2 DAYS'

Shiv Sena leader Sanjay Raut on Thursday said a final decision on a new Maharashtra government would be taken in two days.

He told reporters there were no plans of any meeting between Shiv Sena chief Uddhav Thackeray and Congress president Sonia Gandhi this week.

ANI quoted Sanjay Raut as saying the process to form a government would be completed before December 1, and that the three parties concerned would hold a meeting in Mumbai.
