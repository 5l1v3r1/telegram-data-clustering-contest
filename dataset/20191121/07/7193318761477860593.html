Nonprofit notes for Nov. 21, 2019

21 Nov 2019, 07:01

Vintage Market Days helps empower women

Vintage Market Days, an upscale vintage-inspired market, is Dec. 6-8 in Oklahoma Expo Hall at Oklahoma State Fair Park.

Co-owners and event coordinators Brandi Nollin and Erica Parker are empowering women entrepreneurs while creating a charming family atmosphere for shoppers. With the theme “A Splendid Christmas,” the event will feature about 80 vendors and 200 booths along with live music, food trucks and photo booths.

Tickets on Dec. 6 are $11, and $6 Dec. 7-8. Admission is free for ages 12 and younger. For more information or tickets, go to vintagemarketdays.com/market/oklahoma-city.

Libraries can apply for funding opportunity

The Oklahoma Partnership for School Readiness is accepting applications for its Public Library Family Engagement Project.

The funding will allow libraries to implement several different programs encouraging infant and toddler development and promoting lifelong literacy. In total, 112 recipients will be funded with a maximum award of $2,000 per recipient.

To apply, go to okschoolreadiness.org/okfutures/maximizing-parent-choice#public_library_project by 5 p.m. Dec. 6.

For more information, call 429-4219 or email contact@okschoolreadiness.org.

Santa Market to benefit Alzheimer’s research

The 10th Annual Santa Market is 9 a.m. to 5 p.m. Nov. 23 in the Pavilion Building at Oklahoma State Fair Park. Admission and parking are free.

Almost 200 vendors will be selling homemade creations, including clothing, children’s items, toys, jewelry, home decor, holiday items and more. Photos with Santa will be available. The first 1,000 shoppers will receive a free shopping bag filled with goodies. There will also be a silent auction.

Part of the proceeds goes to the Alzheimer’s Association of Oklahoma. For more information, go to facebook.com/thesantamarket.

Gift drive to benefit The Children’s Center

BETHANY — The Children’s Center Rehabilitation Hospital is preparing for its 13th annual Holiday Helper’s campaign. Individuals and groups looking for ways to donate this season can choose from a list of basic needs items and a special toy catalog to provide patients holiday cheer. The campaign kicks off Nov. 21 and will run through Jan. 1.

Items can be dropped off from 9 a.m. to 9 p.m. daily. For more information, including the basic needs list, go to miracleshappenhere.org or call 789-6711, option 4.

Scouts collect 52,000 pounds of food

To help alleviate hunger in the community, Scouting for Food collected more than 52,000 pounds of food on Nov. 9 and 16.

Scouting for Food is an annual collection of nonperishable food items, coordinated across the 24 counties served by the Last Frontier Council. Various food pantries across the community will receive the food collected, and in turn, give the food to hundreds of individuals or families in need.

For more information or to make a donation, contact Sarah Spinks at 840-1114.

— Erica Smith, for The Oklahoman
