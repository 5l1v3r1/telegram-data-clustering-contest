ON THE ROPES

Highland Games chiefs fear tug o’ war is under threat with popularity dwindling as they appeal for government cash

21 Nov 2019, 07:38 by John Jeffay

HIGHLAND Games chiefs warn the tug o’ war is under threat — as the historic strength battle isn’t the pull it once was.

Bosses fear it could vanish and appealed for government cash to boost participation.

Scott Fraser of the Grampian Highland Games Association revealed just one team from outside the north-east consistently goes to the ten official tournaments each year.

He said: “Tug o’ war is amazing to play and watch but it is a struggle to get enough teams to travel to the tournaments.

“Highland Games do a huge amount to bring tourism and money to local economies and I don’t think that’s reflected by the Government.

“More funding would certainly help.”

Tug o’ war, an Olympic event between 1900 and 1920, involves two teams of eight trying to pull each other over a line.

GHGA organisers will meet Scottish Highland Games Association bosses on Sunday to plot how to drag the sport back to its former glory.

Aberdeenshire Tory MSP Alexander Burnett said: “The value of Highland Games in strengthening links with the Scottish diaspora is incalculable.”

The Scottish Government said it has invested £140,000 in Highland Games and gave £2,500 in 2018/19 to the Scottish Tug of War Association.

A spokesman said: “We are providing funding and support to ensure the Games thrive.”
