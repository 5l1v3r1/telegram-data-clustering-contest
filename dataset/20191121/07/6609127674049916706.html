Gers look at Gazza

Paul Gascoigne’s nephew Cameron ‘returns to Rangers for second trial’

21 Nov 2019, 07:55 by Gary Taylor

RANGERS have offered Cameron Gascoigne a second trial following an impressive training stint earlier this season, according to reports.

The Daily Record claim Paul Gascoigne's nephew is edging closer to landing a chance to follow in his uncle's footsteps.

The 18-year-old, who has reportedly returned to Auchenhowie, scored in a 3-0 development team win over Partick Thistle on Tuesday.

It's claimed the former Newcastle youngster could now be offered a full-time deal to move to Scotland after his release from the EPL side.

Former England star Gazza is a legendary figure around Ibrox.

He joined the club from Italian outfit Lazio and spent three years with the Light Blues before joining Middlesbrough.

Meanwhile, Scottish boxing legend Billy Nelson has praised Gers ace Jon Flanagan and even offered to train all of his team-mates too.

The defender trained with renowned boxing coach Nelson yesterday as he stepped up extra fitness work to get back into the first team.

Opportunities in Steven Gerrard's side have been few and far between for the 26-year-old as he battles back from injury.

He recently featured in the Rangers Colts side who beat Wrexham 2-0 in the Tunnock's Caramel Wafer Cup last week.

But Nelson, was full of kind words for the Scouser, and admits he would love to help out Gerrard's men gain an extra edge in the title race over Celtic.
