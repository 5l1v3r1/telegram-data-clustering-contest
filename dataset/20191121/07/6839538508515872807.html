$7 Aldi wine wins top award at Great Australian Shiraz Challenge 2019

21 Nov 2019, 07:34

A $7 bottle of wine has taken out international Gold at one of Australia’s biggest wine competitions.

Aldi’s $6.99 One Road South Australian and Heathcote Shiraz won the Great Australian Shiraz Challenge 2019, in a huge win for both Aldi and those of us too cheap to pay more than $10 a bottle.

The supermarket’s One Road Gouldburn & King Valley Cabernet Merlot 2018 - also an absolute steal for $6.99 - and A.C Byrne & Co Margaret River Cabernet Merlot 2018 - just three dollars more at $9.99 - also took out Blue Gold in the Sydney International Wine Competition 2020.

Aldi’s popular champagne, the Veuve Monsigny Brut Champagne, on the shelf for just $21.99, also managed to land itself a spot in the top 100 wines of the competition.

Earlier this week, the supermarket giant released a list of its top products as voted by Australian customers.

While the aforementioned champagne was expected to take out the alcohol category, Aldi shoppers instead dubbed the South Point Estate Rosé their top pick, retailing for just $4.99.

Looks like now is the perfect time to shop for some award-winning wine to bring to Christmas dinner.
