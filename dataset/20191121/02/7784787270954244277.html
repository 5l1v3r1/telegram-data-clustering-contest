He’s playing on! Cameron Smith confirms he’ll be a Melbourne Storm player in 2020

21 Nov 2019, 02:45

Cameron Smith will play on in the NRL in 2020, allaying fears he would retire from the game with a year left on his contract.

The 36-year-old was reportedly considering quitting the game in the wake of the Storm’s finals exit to the Roosters earlier this year, but on Thursday he declared his intention to carry on with Craig Bellamy.

“I don’t know mate I’ll have a think about it over the next few weeks and decide then,” Smith said after the Storm were knocked out by the Chooks.

“I’ve said all along, especially over the last two or three seasons even thought I’m getting on a bit and the end is near, I’ve been enjoying my football thoroughly.

“I’m playing a different style of football in the last couple of years, now we have a lot of younger and inexperienced players.

“In a way that’s given me a new lease of life in footy. There’s a lot of things to think about, the kids are growing up and we’ve got a lot of things on.”

A veteran of 414 NRL games, the former Queensland and Kangaroos captain has spent time away and on Thursday released a statement via the Storm, confirming his plans to play on.

“I’ve had a good break after another pretty big season in 2019. I’ve had time to relax and put my feet up and I’m feeling pretty fresh now,” Smith said.
