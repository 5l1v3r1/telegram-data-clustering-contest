Jeffrey Epstein accusers are pressuring Prince Andrew to give evidence to the FBI after he pledged to help investigate his former friend

21 Nov 2019, 11:49 by Sahar Esfandiari, Business Insider US
  • Lawyers representing the accusers of Jeffrey Epstein have spoken out urging Prince Andrew to offer information to law enforcement in the US about any evidence he may have related to the sex-trafficking claims against his former friend.
  • Prince Andrew on Wednesday announced he would step back from his royal duties following a disastrous interview with the BBC.
  • In a statement, the Duke of York said he was “willing to help any appropriate law-enforcement agency with their investigations, if required.”
  • Lisa Bloom and Gloria Allred, two lawyers representing some of Epstein’s accusers, urged the Duke of York to come forward voluntarily and offer any relevant information.
  • “It’s great that he’s stepping away from his royal duties, but it’s really not about that, it’s about justice and accountability for the victims, so it’s important that he says he’s going to cooperate with law enforcement,” Bloom said.

The lawyers of Jeffrey Epstein’s accusers are pressuring Prince Andrew to give evidence to US law enforcement including the FBI after he pledged to help investigate his former friend.

The Duke of York announced Wednesday that he would be taking a step back from his royal duties for the “foreseeable future” following his disasterous interview with the BBC.

“I am willing to help any appropriate law-enforcement agency with their investigations, if required,” he said in a statement.
