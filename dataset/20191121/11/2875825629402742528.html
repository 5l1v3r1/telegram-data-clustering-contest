Love Island's Hannah Elizabeth defends her glam makeover after giving birth

In good faith, the reality star had uploaded a series of photographs shortly after she had given birth

21 Nov 2019, 11:51 by Irishmirror.ie

A Love Island star has taken aim at online trolls.

Hannah Elizabeth come under fire for having a full face of make-up and a blow dry after she gave birth to her son Reggie.

In good faith, the reality star had uploaded a series of photographs shortly after she had welcomed her first child into the world.

But when her dolled up photos were met with a backlash, Hannah spiralled into a furious tirade justifying why she wanted to look her best.

The new mum owned the fact she was wearing make-up after giving birth as well as having had a curly blow dry.

She wrote: “Yes haters I did have make-up on after giving birth. Actually I had had a curly blow, pins curls the lot.

“I wasn't gonna meet my son for the first time and best day of my life rough was I?...

“I had a C-section so birth was planned chilled and AMAZING.”

Hannah blasted the trolls for branding her make-up transformation as "offensive".

She wrote: “Because apparently u shouldn’t take time from your baby to ‘cake makeup on’ so when the nurse says to get up n go for a shower freshen up your meant to just lie in the bed sweating?

"And its ‘offensive’ to other mums lol wtf.” (sic)

Hannah gave birth to her first child on November 5 before she shared the slew of social media snaps with her followers.

The Love Island finalist split from her fiancé George Andreetti when she found out she was pregnant.

In October, the reality star shared the break up was the "best and healthiest" thing she could do for her baby as she claimed her ex wasn't "well".

She told MailOnline : "I left George when I found out I was pregnant, I had to do what is right for me and my baby.

"He isn't well at the minute and I don't agree with what he is posting online.

"I want my baby to have the best and healthiest upbringing and I am not going to let anyone come between that."

The star found fame on the first series of Love Island and garnered a loyal fanbase.
