Southern Steel kicks off FY20 with loss-making quarter as selling price, sales fall

21 Nov 2019, 11:46 by Tan Xue Ying

KUALA LUMPUR (Nov 21): Southern Steel Bhd started its financial year ending June 30, 2020 (FY20) in the red, as its first quarter ended Sept 30, 2019 recorded a net loss of RM45.59 million, dragged by lower sales volume and selling prices.

This is the company’s fourth consecutive quarterly net loss, which brings its cumulative losses to RM166.42 million since 2QFY19.

In the corresponding quarter a year ago of 1QFY19, the company, which is a member of the Hong Leong Group, recorded a net profit of RM1.79 million, onrevenue of RM929.04 million. Revenue has now fallen 30% to RM653.74 million.

Southern Steel said the lower revenue and losses recorded in 1QFY20 were “largely due to lower selling prices which eroded margin and lower sales volume”.

The company said it will continue to undertake measures to adopt cost and to maintain its market position, as the market continues to be “severely oversupplied”.

Shares of Southern Steel closed unchanged today at 74 sen, for a market capitalisation of RM320.90 million. The stock has lost 35% over the past one year.
