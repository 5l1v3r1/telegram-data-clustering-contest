DOG ABANDONED

Puppy dumped and left for dead in a box in Hamilton car park on coldest night of the year

21 Nov 2019, 11:19 by Justin Bowie

A PUPPY was dumped and left for dead in a Hamilton car park on the coldest night of the year.

The Scottish SPCA was alerted after the vulnerable Jack Russell terrier cross, believed to be around eight-weeks-old, was discovered alone on November 18.

The dog, who has been named Beans, was found in a cardboard box lined with a blanket near Quarry Street in the Lanarkshire town.

Rescuers say he would have almost certainly died had it not been for a member of the public finding him.

And the Scottish SPCA are now making an appeal to the public for information.

Animal rescue officer Sarah Parker said: “The dog was found in a cardboard box, lined with a blanket, at a local park near to Quarry Street.

“The young dog, which we believe is a Jack Russell terrier cross, is male and is around eight weeks old.

“We are grateful to the member of the public who took him in and kept him safe overnight.

“He is friendly so we suspect he is used to human contact.

“One of our animal rescue and rehoming centres is providing the care he needs.

“The team have affectionately named him Beans.

“The night in question was the coldest so far this year and temperatures were below freezing.

“Had he not been found, Beans would have almost certainly died so we are keen to find out the circumstances surrounding how he came to be in the park.

“If anyone has any information, we would urge them to call our confidential animal helpline on 03000 999 999.”
