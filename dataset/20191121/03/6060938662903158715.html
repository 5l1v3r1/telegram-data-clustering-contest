All out for 7! Rivals win by 754 runs

21 Nov 2019, 03:10 by Gaurav Gupta

Highlights

  • In a Harris Shield match in Mumbai, all the batsmen of Children's Welfare school were out for a duck and the final total of 7 was all due to extras in the innings that lasted just six overs.
  • Batting first, Swami Vivekanand International School had made 761/4 in 39 overs, fired by an unbeaten triple hundred from Meet Mayekar (338*).

MUMBAI: It's a day Children’s Welfare school would like to forget in a hurry. The school from Andheri ‘achieved’ a dubious record in history books when all its batsmen were out for a duck in their Harris Shield first round knockout match against Swami Vivekanand International School (SVIS), Borivali.

Thanks to seven extras (six wides and one bye) bowled by the SVIS bowlers, Children’s Welfare managed to aggregate 7, as they were all out in merely six overs. The wrecker-in-chief for SVIS was medium-pacer Alok Pal, who took six for three in three overs. Skipper Varod Vaze took two for three, while the other two batsmen were run out.
