Morrisons-mad woman visits 148 stores - and vows to do all 493

Janine Schofield, 27, from Guernsey, wants to visit all branches in homage to gran and grandad who used to take her to the supermarket's cafe as a treat

21 Nov 2019, 03:56 by Jack Williams

A woman is bidding to visit every Morrisons store in honour of her late grandparents.

Janine Schofield, 27, has been to 148 of the 493 branches.

Her gran and grandad, Pam and Ken, used to treat her at Morrisons cafes.

Janine’s bizarre quest is made even more difficult because she lives on Guernsey - which is technically not in the UK.

The accountant uses trips to the mainland to cram in as many supermarkets as she can.

She said: “Friends think I’m mad but are also very supportive.

 

“For example if I visit them and there is a store near where they live that I haven’t visited they’re happy to take me.”

Whenever she visits one she takes a photo in the store and buys something so she can keep the receipt as proof.

Her hobby began in 2007 when she was 14 and taken to the cafe of Morrisons in Cleveleys, near Blackpool, by her grandparents, Pam and Ken.

Janine and her younger sister Elise often accompanied the elderly couple.

The furthest north her hobby has taken her is Glasgow while the furthest south on the mainland is Penzance.

The most she has visited in a single day is seven, which she achieved in the Midlands.

She said her dream would be to take a year off work and spend 12 months travelling the country to visit the rest.

Such is the extent of Janine’s strange hobby, in 2014 she was given a behind-the-scenes tour of the company’s headquarters in Bradford.

In 2014 Janine had the opportunity to visit the headquarters in Bradford and got a behind-the-scenes tour where she met the CEO at the time, Dalton Phillips.

She also uses a large Morrisons flag as a blanket and has her own name badge and fleece.

She also has an extensive collection of memorabilia her home has Morrisons carrier bags on some of the walls.
