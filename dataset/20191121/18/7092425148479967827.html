Netflix's You season two set to be gorier and scarier than ever before

21 Nov 2019, 18:45 by Ciara Walsh

Is Joe up to his old tricks again? 

Netflix have just released first look images from the upcoming second season of You.

In this new chapter, Joe leaves New York for LA as he runs from his long-lost ex-girlfriend Candace, but will he fall in love again after the death of his former flame?

The new season sees Joe running from his ex-girlfriend Candace who is hell-bent on getting revenge on the psychopathic bookstore owner.

Season one of the drama, based on Caroline Kepnes' 2014 book of the same name, follows Penn Badgley's Joe and his dogged obsession with a woman named Guienevere Beck, played by Elizabeth Lail.

Although the follow-up will see Joe move to LA in the hope of a fresh start and "fully intending to start over a new leaf", a brand new image from You's second season reveals that nothing much appears to have changed.

In Entertainment Weekly's exclusive look at season two, Joe can be seen watching a young woman doing her shopping - newcomer Victoria Pedretti's Love Quinn - from a distance.

You's showrunner Sera Gamble spoke to EW about Joe's relationship with Love, insisting that he is "a different guy" from the one viewers watched unravel in the first season.

"It's not that simple love-at-first-sight Joe might've been looking for a couple of years ago," Sera explained. "He's a different guy. He's been through more. The circumstances of his encounter with Love are very informed by what he went through with Beck."

While Joe might have learned a lesson over his pursuit of Beck, You is still going to be a bleak watch, with Sera Gamble recently singling out her favourite scene in an upcoming episode as something "gorier and scarier" than ever before.

"Joe is always trying to be a good guy but yet somehow he's finding himself in these thriller, horror kind of situations," she said. "It's terrible for him but I think it's going to be so much fun for the audience."

You season one is streaming now on Netflix. Season two will premiere on Stephens' Day, December 26.
