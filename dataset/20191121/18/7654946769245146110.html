Record number of NYC high school grads are heading to college

21 Nov 2019, 18:27 by Rich Calder

More city public high school graduates are heading to college than ever with nearly two-thirds heading to college, Mayor de Bill Blasio announced Tuesday.

A record 48,782 high school graduates in 2018 — 62 percent of the class — are heading to higher education, DOE stats show.

“This idea would have been considered impossible not so long ago in New York City, but it’s happening now,” Hizzoner said at the Brooklyn announcement, where he was joined by Schools Chancellor Richard Carranza.

The two spoke at the High School of Telecommunication Arts and Technology in Bay Ridge, where a whopping 82 percent of its 2018 seniors went on to college.

Carranza credited de Blasio’s “equity and excellence” agenda for public schools as the key factor in driving up the numbers.

“It doesn’t matter what your zip code is,” he added. “It doesn’t matter where you’re from. All that matters is what you want to do. “

In 2013, the year before de Blasio took office, only about 50 percent of New York City’s public high school graduates went on to college.

De Blasio said he expected two-thirds of graduating seniors to head straight to college on average by the time he leaves office in 2021.

Data for all 2019 graduates is not yet available.
