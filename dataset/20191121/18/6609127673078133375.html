BURGER, C*** AND FRIES

Serial flasher exposes himself at Edinburgh McDonald’s staff a year after ‘helicopter’ stunt with genitals at same spot

21 Nov 2019, 18:27 by Alexander Lawrie

A SERIAL flasher has been caught exposing himself to Edinburgh McDonald's staff - just a year after performing "helicopter-like moves" with his genitals in the same restaurant.

Shameless Ryan Dolan, 30, flashed his tackle at shocked workers and members of the public as he stood outside the capital's South St Andrew Street branch.

Dolan banged on the window to get everyone's attention before dropping his trousers for a full 18 seconds at around 5am on July 27 this year.

Staff said he then ran away.

Dolan, from Musselburgh, East Lothian, pleaded guilty to the charge of public indecency at Edinburgh Sheriff Court today.

It's the second time the garage worker has targeted staff at the same McDonald’s fast food store by exposing himself.

He was caught flashing his genitals when he staggered into the restaurant and made “helicopter-like moves” with his exposed tackle on July 22 last year.

Dolan jumped up on the counter and dropped his trousers and performed a bizarre dance while waving his genitals around.

He was sentenced in November last year where the court heard Dolan had two previous convictions for similar offences. He was fined £500.

On Wednesday prosecutor Eilidh Yates said: “Staff saw the accused standing outside knocking on the window.

"He knocked on the window again and then exposed his genitals to the staff and to members of the public.

“He did so by dropping his trousers.”

The court heard “CCTV showed it was for around 18 seconds” that Dolan had exposed himself for before “pulling up his trousers and leaving the area”.

The fiscal said the Crown did not believe the offence was sexually motivated and was not seeking a conviction that could lead to Dolan having to register as a sex offender.

Dolan had previously pleaded guilty by letter and defended himself during his court appearance.

Sheriff Peter McCormack said: “You have an analogous conviction from 2018 so I am going to impose a relatively higher fine than I otherwise would have.

“It is not the first time something of this nature has happened.”

The sheriff said he would have fined Dolan £900 but reduced the fine to £600 due to his early guilty plea.
