After adding Grandal, is this SP next for White Sox?

21 Nov 2019, 18:01

After emerging as one of the National League's better power pitchers over the last two seasons (23-15, 3.65 ERA, 3.37 FIP, 8.9 K/9), Zack Wheeleris now a free agent. He is tied to Draft-pick compensation if he signs with a new team after being one of 10 players to receive the $17.8 million qualifying offer.

Below is a list of the latest news and rumors surrounding the 29-year-old right-hander.

White Sox add Grandal; is Wheeler next?

Nov. 21: The White Sox acted quickly to sign catcher Yasmani Grandal to a four-year, $73 million contract on Thursday, and the team could stay aggressive to address its other needs.

Earlier this week, MLB.com's Jon Paul Morosi listed the Sox as one of four frontrunners for Wheeler, along with the Angels, Padres and Twins.

SNY's Andy Martino reports that Chicago still plans to pursue Wheeler even after adding Grandal on the largest contract in franchise history.

Grandal's record might not stand for long, as a five-year, $100 million deal for Wheeler is believed to be plausible, according to Morosi.
