Cleland man Millar Jukes is making waves down under with his new music project

Millar, 32, has just released his latest single 'Don't Worry' on all major platforms.

21 Nov 2019, 10:12 by Gregor Farquharson, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/gregor-farquharson/

An ex-Coltness High pupil has been making waves down under with his new music project.

Millar Jukes, 32, moved to Australia in 2011 with his ex-girlfriend and has stayed there ever since.

He has just released his latest single “Don’t Worry” onto all major platforms, a song inspired by a conversation with his mum.

He said: “I was back home a wee while ago and everything in my life was just not going well.

“My girlfriend had broken up with me, I had lost my job and flat. It all just seemed to be going downhill.

“I was at home with my mum and she was watching Frasier and I was complaining and she turned the sound down, looked me in the eye and basically just said 'Don’t worry about things you can’t change'.

“That’s where the inspiration comes from, my mum.”

Millar is from a musical family, with his brothers Stevie and Al in the popular band Saint PHNX .

 

“I was quite late to the whole music thing, later than my brothers for sure,” said Millar.

“I learned guitar on a year I took out travelling and I’ve been playing since.

“Stevie told me I couldn’t sing so I went and got lessons and wrote some songs and released my EP five years ago.

“My brothers helped me a lot. They always seem to be on tour now but I speak to them every few weeks.

“I’ve been trying to get them to come out here and play, I think that would be amazing.”

Despite being out in Australia, Millar still remains very much a Wishaw man, with his accent as strong as when he left.

 

He added: “Wishaw made me who I am. I do miss certain things, like the banter and the chat and even the Scottish music scene, which is booming.

“I know I’ll stay over here forever now, I love it, but I enjoy coming home every so often and seeing my family.

“Last time I played in Scotland was at the Vigo Thieves final gig in Broadcast, Glasgow, and it was one of the best gigs I’ve ever played.

“I’d love to play my own show there in the future.”

You can keep up to date with what Millar is up to on Facebook and Twitter and stream his new single on all major platforms.
