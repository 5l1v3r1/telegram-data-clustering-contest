Agent confirms Liverpool interest in Trabzonspor goalkeeper Ugurcan Cakir

21 Nov 2019, 10:40

Liverpool’s interest in Trabzonspor goalkeeper Ugurcan Cakir has been confirmed by the player’s agent.

The 23-year-old only established himself as number one choice for the Turkish side midway through last season, but has done enough to catch the eye of sides across Europe.

Cakir has picked up two caps for Turkey, making his international debut in May, but is yet to displace Mert Gunok as the first choice in the national team.

Despite this relative lack of experience, the 6’3″ keeper is attracting interest, specifically in England, where his agent says a number of clubs have been keeping track of his progress.

‘Liverpool are one of four English sides keeping close tabs on Ugurcan,’ Engin Kirkpinar told Turkish-football.com.

‘Liverpool are among the sides interested in Cakir, and they have been monitoring his progress.

‘Trabzonspor value Cakir as being worth a minimum £18m, and will not sell him in January. An end-of-season move could however be on the cards.’

This is not the first time Kirkpinar has mentioned Liverpool’s interest, as he also name-dropped Lille and Schalke earlier this month.

‘Every team have scouts and some of them are following Ugurcan’s matches,’ the agent told Ajansspor.

‘Schalke haven’t made an official offer for him. I know Lille and Schalke are following him.

‘Liverpool are also interested in him. Liverpool officials have talked with us, we had some contacts, but no official offer has come from them.’

Alisson Becker is firmly entrenched as the number one goalkeeper at Anfield, and at 27-years-old, is likely to remain so for years to come.

Adrian was brought in as back-up over the summer and has made 11 appearances so far this season due to Alisson picking up an injury on the opening day of the season.

The Spanish keeper was signed on a free transfer and given a two-year contract, but Cakir may be seen as a more long-term number two.

Caoimhín Kelleher has also made two first team appearances this campaign, both coming in the League Cup.
