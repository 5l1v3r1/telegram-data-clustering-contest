Babcock Sex Tape: Nigerians Divided As University Expels Student In Viral Video

21 Nov 2019, 10:20 by Emmanuel Egobiambu

Nigerians are divided as Babcock University, Remo, Ogun State, expelled the student in the viral sex tape video, Concise News reports.

Babcock University had in a statement described the viral sex tape of ex-students as a “sad commentary on the moral decadence that has assailed our society at large.”

It added that it is a behaviour alien and unethical to the values of the University, saying that the school has zero-tolerance for such an act.

“According to the girl, the incident took place in April this year during school vacation, when she visited the boy at the hospital,” the statement added. “The act did not take place in the University.”

The University said it remains committed to highest moral standards and will be a standard-bearer for quality education in character, learning and service to humanity.

It also adds, “We are happy to be saddled with this challenge as an institution because it propels us to contribute to the greatness of this nation, and the security of bright future for our children.”

The expulsion of the student by Babcock University over the leaked sex tape that has gone viral has drawn parallel comments from Nigerians.

Some said the school was morally wrong to expel the student over the video, adding that the act was not committed in the varsity’s premises.

According to them, the student involved should seek legal action against the management of the University for the expulsion.

Others, however, commended Babcock University for protecting its name, adding that the student deserved to be expelled from school over the viral sex tape.
