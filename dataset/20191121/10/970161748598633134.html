Crazy Rich Asians director hits back at Brenda Song’s claims of audition snub

21 Nov 2019, 10:26

The Crazy Rich Asians director has responded to Brenda Song’s claims she was denied an audition for the film for ‘not being Asian enough’.

In a new interview, Brenda, 31, said she was never invited to audition for the hit rom-com which stars Constance Wu, Henry Golding and Gemma Chan.

Brenda explained to Teen Vogue that she was disappointed in not being given the opportunity to star in a movie embracing her Asian heritage, but refused to ‘change her past’ to ‘fit in’.

However, director Jon M Chu has hit back at the claims and says he’s unaware if another member of the production team made the comment about her ‘not being Asian enough’.

Responding in a series of tweets, the 40-year-old filmmaker said: ‘Nope. I love @BrendaSong and that sucks if anything of that nature was ever communicated. It’s gross actually. The fact is, obviously I know who she is and didn’t need her to audition. I’m a fan of hers! Nothing more nothing less. Bums me out she thought it was anything but.’

He added: ‘would these words ever come out of my mouth? Nope makes no sense. I feel horrible she thinks this is the reason. The fact is I love Brenda Song and am a fan. I didn’t need her to audition because I already knew who she was!’

Speaking to the publication, Brenda had said: ‘A lot of people don’t know this, but I never got to read for Crazy Rich Asians, ever.

‘Their reasoning behind that, what they said was that my image was basically not Asian enough, in not so many words.’

Brenda felt she was able to relate to the character – presumably the lead protagonist Rachel Chu played by Constance – due to her being in her mid-late 20s and an Asian-American woman.

The actress told the producers: ‘I’ve auditioned for Caucasian roles my entire career, but this specific role, you’re not going to let me do it?

‘You’re going to fault me for having worked my whole life? I was like, ”Where do I fit?”’

The Secret Obsession actress detailed how she then ‘got [herself] together’ and said: ‘“Brenda, there is only one you, and you can’t change who you are. You can’t change your past”.’

Crazy Rich Asians went on to become one of the biggest box office hits of 2017 and grossed an impressive $238.5 million (£183 million) worldwide. Two sequels are said to be in the works based on the novels China Rich Girlfriend and Rich People Problems, all written by author Kevin Kwan.
