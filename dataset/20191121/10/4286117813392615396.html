BSNL will give you money for every SMS you send: Here's how you can get it

Extending the 6 paise cashback offer to SMSes, the company will now credit money for every SMS you send from your BSNL phone number. 

21 Nov 2019, 10:15 by India Today Tech

HIGHLIGHTS

  • BSNL last month announced cashback of 6 paise for every five minutes of voice calls .
  • BSNL will now credit money for every SMS you send from your BSNL phone number. 
  • This offer is available for landline, broadband and fiber till 31 December 2019. 

BSNL is on a cashback spree these days. The company last month announced cashback of 6 paise for every five minutes of voice calls placed by the subscribers and now the same is being offered for the SMSes. Extending the 6 paise cashback offer to SMSes, the company will now credit money for every SMS you send from your BSNL phone number.

To activate the BSNL cashback offer, type "ACT 6 paisa" in the SMS compose box and send it to 9478053334. You will start getting a cashback of six paise with each SMS. This offer is available for landline, broadband and fiber till 31 December 2019.

A lot is changing at BSNL. The company is giving cashback with calls too. It is giving 6 paise for every five minutes of voice calls placed by the subscribers. The offer is applicable on BSNL's wireline, broadband and FTTH customers. The money is credited in the form of cashback to the subscriber.

BSNL free calls in Mumbai and Delhi: In one of its latest moves, BSNL has announced free calls to MTNL users. BSNL has three plans that come with a free call facility to MTNL number. These are - Rs 429, Rs 485 and Rs 666. The Rs 429 plan offers free call voice calling benefits on all networks including MTNL along with 1GB data per day for 81 days. The Rs 485 plan offers free calls and 1.5GB data per day for 90 days. The Rs 666 plan also offers free calling and same data benefits as Rs 485 plan but the validity is more. It comes with 122 days of validity.

The government's yes to BSNL-MTNL merger: The government has finally given said yes to the merger of debt-ridden companies-- MTNL and BSNL-- along with also the 4G spectrum allocation to the two. It plans to invest Rs 29,937 crore in this revival plan with the combined assets of the two companies worth Rs 38,000 crore which is to be monetised.
