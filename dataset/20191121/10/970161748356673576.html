Elton John needed a teleprompter for iconic performance at Princess Di’s funeral

21 Nov 2019, 10:48

Even though it’s his tune, Sir Elton John has admitted he needed a teleprompter when performing Candle In The Wind at Princess Diana’s funeral in 1997.

The performer took the grand piano to sing a revised version of his 1973 hit to honour his dear friend, who died following a car crash in a Paris tunnel.

With not a dry eye in the house, the Rocketman performed the stirring tune (written with Bernie Taupin) however now Elton has revealed he needed a teleprompter, hidden beside his instrument.

‘It’s the first time I had a teleprompter, a teleprompter down on my left-hand side because I thought if I sing “Goodbye Norma Jean” I’m gonna get hung, drawn and quartered,’ Elton said this week.

Speaking at the Royal Academy of Music, the 72-year-old added: ‘So just for precaution, I had the teleprompter there.’
