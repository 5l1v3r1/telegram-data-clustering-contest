BIKE HORROR

Edinburgh rugby player killed in Nepal after motorbike plunges 1000ft from mountainside road

21 Nov 2019, 10:19 by Blair Meikle

A POPULAR and well-known Edinburgh rugby player has died in a motorbike accident in Nepal.

Iain Finnie, 63, was involved in the horror smash in the Mustang tourist district, 150 miles outside Kathmandu.

His bike plunged almost 1000ft from the mountainside road, according the the Edinburgh Evening News.

Iain was rushed to Manipal Hospital in Pokhara for treatment, but was later pronounced dead.

He starred for Watsonians rugby team between 1973 and 1986, for the first XV and several other teams.

He played almost 150 games, at hooker and in the back row, for the first team.

A spokesperson from the Foreign and Commonwealth Office said: “We are assisting the family and friends of a British man who died in Nepal."

A spokesperson for Watsonian rugby club told the Evening News: "He was a formidable 7-a-side player at hooker and had an impressive turn of speed with great handling skills."
