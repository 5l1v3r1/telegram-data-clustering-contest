Man dies after assault that began with dispute at Courtenay library

21 Nov 2019, 10:48 by Cindy E. Harnett

A Courtenay man has died after an assault in downtown Courtenay on Tuesday.

Family have confirmed Sean Parker, 46, died at Victoria General Hospital hours after an altercation.

Sister Natalie Parker confirmed he died at 4:08 p.m. Tuesday.

At about 1:25 p.m. on Tuesday, Comox Valley RCMP officers responded to a report of an assault on the 350-block of 6th Street in Courtenay and found a 46-year old man unconscious on the sidewalk. He was rushed to hospital by ambulance with life-threatening injuries.

“The victim and the suspect were at the Courtenay library shortly before getting into an altercation, which resulted in the victim suffering a serious head injury,” said Staff Sgt. Glen Breckon of the Comox Valley RCMP’s major crime unit.

Breckon, who described the area on 6th street as busy, put a call out for witnesses to the dispute at the library, or anyone with video or dashcam footage from around the time of the assault.

Police do not believe the incident to be random. Police said they had identified a person of interest on Tuesday afternoon.

Anyone with related information is asked to call Comox Valley RCMP at 250-338-1321 and refer to file number 2019-17839.

Video footage can be sent directly to the Comox Valley RCMP at comoxvalleyrcmp@rcmp-grc.gc.ca

Those who wish to remain anonymous can call Crime Stoppers at 1-800-222-8477 (TIPS) or go online at http://comoxvalleycrimestoppers.bc.ca/.
