Tories rake in £5 million more in big ticket donations than any other party

Donations ahead of the General Election have been published and the Conservative Party has pocketed more big cash gifts in the first week of the campaign than the other main parties combined

21 Nov 2019, 15:39 by Mikey Smith, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/mikey-smith/

The Tories have raked in £5 million more in big ticket donors than any other political party in the first week of the General Election .

The party pocketed more big cash gifts than all the other main parties combined - and 26 times more than Labour - according to new figures published by the Electoral Commission.

In the same period, Labour took in just £218,500 - though Jeremy Corbyn's party traditionally raises more money through small donations, which are not declared in the same way.

The watchdog publishes details of any donations made to parties over £7,500 for each week of the campaign.

In total, £6,507,146 in donations was reported to the Commission between November 6 and 12 - less than the  £7,054,844 for the same period in 2017.

 

The Brexit Party took a single donation of £250,000 from businessman Jeremy Hosking - a former Tory donor who has been a major backer of Nigel Farage's operation.

Tory donors include Russian banker Lubov Chernukhin.

Mrs Chernukhin's husband Vladimir Chernikhin, was formerly an ally of Russian President Vladimir Putin, although he says he has fallen out with the leader.

She has frequently made headlines as a big-ticket donor to the Tories - regularly bidding in auctions for access to senior ministers at Tory fundraisers.

Earlier this year she paid £135,000 for a "girls' night out" with Theresa May and other senior cabinet ministers.

In 2018 the Tories accepted her bid of £30,000 in return for a private dinner with defence secretary Gavin Williamson in the Churchill War Rooms.

 

And she paid £160,000 at a previous event to play tennis with Boris Johnson .

The biggest backer of the Conservatives was theatre producer John Gore who gave £1 million.

He was also the party's biggest donor last year.

The next three largest donors were the travel company Trailfinders, Countrywide Developers and WA Capital, who each gave £500,000 pounds to the party.

The Lib Dems accepted £275,000 in large donations during the first week of the campaign.

And the Green Party accepted £30,000.
