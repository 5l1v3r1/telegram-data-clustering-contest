Viewers are saying the latest Netflix documentary is the best true crime film they've seen

21 Nov 2019, 15:42 by Fiona Kelly

Netflix has just dropped a new true crime documentary and people are absolutely raving about it.

The film, titled Dream/Killer landed on Netflix yesterday and focuses on a case represented by none other than Kathleen Zellner — the powerhouse lawyer that has taken over Steven Avery's case.

Dream/Killer is based on a man who took on the US justice system after his son Ryan was sentenced to prison for a murder he claimed not to have committed.

In the 2015 documentary, Bill Ferguson took on the judiciary, alongside his attorney Kathleen Zellner, who became an international sensation when she feature in season two of hit docs-series Making a Murderer.

We don't want to give too much away so as not to spoil the movie for you but here's the basic outline.

The film investigates the 2001 murder Kent Heitholt, who was found dead in his work car park and had been strangled with his own belt.

Two years after his death, the case was still unsolved but police were offered a fresh lead after an anonymous call tipped them off to a local man called Charles 'Chuck' Erickson.

Chuck was said to have been high on drugs and had met his 17-year-old friend Ryan Ferguson at about 10.30pm but he said he couldn't remember anything else afterwards.

During a police interview, footage of which was shown in the film, Erickson incriminated himself and his friend Ryan in the murder. Ferguson was later picked up by the FBI.

He later claimed: "I'm innocent of even being there. I'm not involved in this, in any way."

However, state prosecutor Kevin Crane issued a statement revealing Erickson was being charged with murder in the second degree, and Ferguson was being charged with murder in the first.

There was no physical evidence linking either one of them to the crime.

The documentary has been receiving rave reviews from true crime fanatics, with some claiming it is the best they've ever watched.

Sure to be honest, we'd watch a film about paint drying if Kathleen was in it.

Dream/Killer is available to watch on Netflix now.
