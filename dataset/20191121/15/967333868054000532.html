Major Russian Bank Accidently Offers Client to 'Cry, Kill Jews' in SMS Advertisement

21 Nov 2019, 15:55 by by Tim Korso

The bank explained that the mishap happened due to a "purely accidental" glitch in a fully automatic algorithm generating passphrases for special offers for clients. It has vowed to review how the mechanism works to avoid similar incidents in the future.

A client of one of Russia’s major banks, Sberbank, was unpleasantly surprised when he received a credit offer from the financial institution that at some point basically suggested that he "cry, kill Jews". The "proposal" came in the form of a passphrase that the client needed to use to get the credit on more favourable terms.
