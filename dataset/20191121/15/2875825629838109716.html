Marty Moore signs new two-year deal with Ulster

The 28-year-old former Leinster tighthead prop joined Ulster from Wasps in the summer of 2018

21 Nov 2019, 15:55 by Michael Scully

Following the announcement of Billy Burns' contract extension, Ulster are further boosted ahead of tomorrow's Champions Cup clash with Clermont tomorrow night with Marty Moore's decision to sign a new two-year deal.

Moore, the 28-year-old former Leinster tighthead prop, joined Ulster from Wasps in the summer of 2018 and has played 22 times for the province.

Having missed the start of the season through injury, Moore made an impressive return in the most recent games against Munster and Bath.

"I’ve settled in really well at Ulster and have thoroughly enjoyed my time at the club, so I’m delighted to be able to get a deal sorted for another two years," said the Ireland front row. 

"Working with the coaches and wider support staff, I feel like I still have a lot of potential to improve, and I’m enjoying that process. We’re a club with big ambitions and I’m looking forward to playing my part in helping to achieve those."

Head Coach, Dan McFarland commented: "Marty has been a great addition to the squad. 

"His ability and experience at set piece time has been demonstrated time and again, and has helped all our props make significant progress. 

"His performances over the last two games have been excellent and he is keen to keep developing as part of our squad going forward."
