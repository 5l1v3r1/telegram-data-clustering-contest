Doctor sucks urine from elderly man in mid-air emergency on flight

21 Nov 2019, 15:51

This doctor has been hailed a hero after sucking the urine out of an elderly man.

Zhang Hong used a catheter to extract 800ml of urine during a 37-minute incident on board an aeroplane.

Zhang was on board a China Southern Airlines flight from Guangzhou to New York on Tuesday.

While in mid-air, a passenger in his 70s suddenly suffered dysuria, a painful condition characterised by difficult and painful urination.

Two passengers – Zhang and another man named Xiao Zhanxiang – came to the unnamed man’s aid.

Xiao made a device with materials that were available onboard to try to help the elderly man urinate but it failed.

Then Zhang decided to use a catheter to suck urine from the man and spat it out into a cup.

After photos and videos of the incident circulated online, internet users in China praised the two doctors for their heroics.

Zhang afterwards said he did not think much at the time because ‘saving people is the instinct of a doctor.’

Zhang works for The First Affiliated Hospital of Jinan University (Guangzhou Overseas Chinese Hospital) while Xiao is attached Hainan Central Hospital.
