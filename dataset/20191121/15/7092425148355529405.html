Here's the line-up for this week's Late Late Show

21 Nov 2019, 15:42 by Ciara Walsh

The Late Late Show is back with a stellar line-up this week. 

Tomorrow evening's show is set to be jam-packed, kicking off this week with Westlife, who will be performing a number of songs, including new single My Blood, as well as chatting to Ryan about their pop career and amazing comeback.

Earlier this week, The Late Late Show was on the lookout for Westlife fans to join the boyband in the studio audience.

Over 10,000 fans applied for the chance to be a part of The Late Late Show audience following the call-out for their most devoted followers.

The exciting news comes after Tubs confirmed Westlife would lead this week's line-up at the end of last week's show.

Also joining them will be legendary singer Christy Moore to celebrate and reflect on 50 years in the business, and to perform some of his most beloved songs.

Ahead of stepping down from his role as Ireland rugby coach next week, Joe Schmidt will join Ryan to look back on his time with the team, as well as to discuss what went wrong for Ireland in Japan.

Iconic newscaster Trevor McDonald also joins Tubs to talk about interviewing some of the most famous people in the world, from beloved statesmen like Nelson Mandela, to sitting down opposite dictators like Saddam Hussein and Colonel Gaddafi.

Trevor will also be sharing how he went from growing up on a small Carribean island to becoming the definitive face of the news for a generation.

And finally, to celebrate 20 years of Gift Grub, Mario Rosenstock will be bringing some of his best-known characters to life.

The Late Late Show airs Friday's at 9.35 pm on RTE One.
