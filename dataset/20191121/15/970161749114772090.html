Inside Jeffrey Epstein's notorious 'baby-making ranch' in the desert

21 Nov 2019, 15:25

Dozens of photos have emerged giving a look inside Jeffrey Epstein’s sprawling ‘baby-making ranch’ in the New Mexico desert.

The disgraced financier entertained VIP guests at the secluded estate, outside Santa Fe, which reportedly boasted an eight-person ‘party’ shower and an underground strip club.

Other photos display an indoor bathing area complete with huge pool, a life-sized sculpture of Jesus crucified on a cross and a lavish well-stocked library.

Drone footage shows outhouses and farming equipment dotted around the Zorro Ranch’s neglected flatlands as well as the private airstrip the sex offender used to traffic underage girls on a private jet dubbed the ‘Lolita Express’, DailyMail.com reported.

It is one of several of Epstein’s homes where underage girls were flown in from all over the world.

Earlier this year the New York Times reported that the convicted paedophile, who killed himself in custody awaiting trial on further child sex charges, wished to impregnate scores of women at a time there to improve the human race with his genes.

An anonymous contractor, who claimed to have worked at the ranch until 2006, told Albuquerque radio station owner Eddy Aragon the estate usually stayed quiet most of the year.

However, when Epstein would turn up two or three times each year ‘with an entourage of 30 people or so’ the group ‘would party there’ and ‘pull out all the toys’.

‘I had seen things in the house, on the computers, which made me evaluate whether I should be working for him or not,’ the former worker added.

‘There was numerous photographs in frames, in his library, his office, on the walls, of young underage girls topless and him and various other powerful people.’

The Mail also reported the station owner as having spoken to another ex-employee who revealed the paedophile built a space underground that was used ‘like a nightclub, with stripper poles’ where there would be ‘dancing’ and guests would hang out.

Prince Andrew has come under intense media scrutiny over his association with the tycoon following a ‘car-crash’ BBC interview over the weekend.

The Duke of York faced criticism for showing a lack of empathy towards Epstein’s victims and remorse over his friendship with the businessman.

He took the dramatic decision of stepping down from official royal duties in the wake of the furore after a number of multi-million-pound businesses distanced themselves from him and his Pitch@Palace scheme.

In his statement, the duke said he was ‘willing to help any appropriate law enforcement agency with their investigations, if required’.
