Tories 'misleading' voters again with old video of Labour MP

21 Nov 2019, 15:13

The Conservative Party has been accused of misleading voters again with a video of Labour candidate Jess Phillips.

The video was tweeted by the party’s official account – @CCHQPress – to coincide with the launch of Labour’s manifesto.

In it, Labour’s candidate for Birmingham Yardley, 37, says: ‘You can never, ever deliver all of those things that you are pretending to deliver when you go to the electorate.’

Ms Phillips’ statement was edited out of an interview she gave on Good Morning Britain on 3 October, while promoting her new book.

The interview was re-aired on the ITV breakfast show this morning during an interview with shadow education secretary Angela Rayner.

The @CCHQPress account – which earlier this week caused outrage by changing its name to ‘FactcheckUK’ – used a snippet of it in a tweet that dated the video 21 November, 2019.

During the original interview, Ms Phillips was questioned by Susanna Reid about keeping manifesto promises – before the General Election was even announced.

Ms Phillips said: ‘I think there is an argument to be said that you can never, ever deliver all of those things that you are pretending to deliver when you go to the electorate.

‘In reality, things change. Globally things change, situations change. Facts change. [We can’t deliver it] in all cases.

‘I can’t control the trade war between America and China and I have to, each and every day, and at the moment it is on a day-by-day basis and it is not good, and I have to say, “what is the best thing, the best decision I can make today to make sure my constituents are better off?”‘

The @CCHQPress video edit also labelled Ms Phillips a ‘Corbyn ally’ despite her being a vocal critic of the Labour leader in the past, going as far as to say he wasn’t the ‘practical choice’ for leader because people would not vote for him.

But it’s not the first time the Tories have been accused of misleading the public with a video on its Twitter account.

On 5 November, they were accused of unfairly editing a video of Labour’s Sir Keir Starmer, to make him appear unable to answer a question on Brexit.

And today, as Mr Corbyn announced his manifesto, the Conservatives launched a website to attack the party’s policies.

The Tory run Labourmanifesto.co.uk website was launched moments before Mr Corbyn took to the stage in Birmingham today.

It accuses the party of promising ‘higher taxes’ with ‘no plan for Brexit’.

The Tories have apparently paid Google advertising fees so their site appears at the of the page when some users search ‘Labour’.
