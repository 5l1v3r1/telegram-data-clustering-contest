Who was Mahatma Gandhi, what did he do and what are his best quotes?

21 Nov 2019, 14:51

An organisation called Alliance Against Gandhi Statues are demonstrating today against a planned nine-foot statue of Mahatma Gandhi because they claim the peaceful protestor was ‘a racist, misogynist and apologist for fascism’.

In the wake of the Manchester Arena bombing which killed 22 people in May 2017, an Indian spiritual mission called Shrimad Rajchandra Mission Dharampur donated the Gandhi statue to the city.

Gandhi has become something of a complicated figure in the many years since his death, and it seems that not everyone in Manchester is happy with having a statue of him in their city.

Who was Gandhi?

Gandhi was born in Porbandar, India, on 2 October 1869.

His initial career was as a lawyer, but he is respected and beloved by many worldwide to this day for leading a peaceful campaign to end the rule of the British Empire in India.

The honorific ‘Mahatma’, which is Sanskrit for ‘great-souled’ and ‘venerable’, was first applied to him in 1914.

In 1948, a year after his country gained independence, Gandhi was assassinated at the age of 78.

After his wife’s death, Gandhi began to test his celibacy and, in April 1945, he referenced being naked with several ‘women or girls’ as part of these experiments in a letter.

Last month, an open letter from students planning to protest said that they ‘demand that Manchester City Council reconsider this decision on the basis of Gandhi’s well-documented anti-black racism and complicity in the British Empire’s actions in Africa.

‘Gandhi referred to Africans as “savages”, “half-heathen Natives”, “uncivilised”, “dirty” and “like animals”, to reference only a few of his comments.

‘He saw himself as a “fellow-colonist”, theorising Indians as a superior race. Gandhi was a virulent anti-black racist. This statue would not promote peace but instead promote Gandhi’s racist and anti-black ideology, and promote continued violence in Kashmir.’

On the subject of the protest, Professor Vinita Damodaran, Indian history expert of Sussex University, previously told Mail Online: ‘This campaign focuses on Gandhi’s views when he lived in South Africa and ignores everything else.

‘He moved on to become a very much more humane, much more inclusive person in his later years and he redacted these views.’

A spokesman for Manchester City Council said: ‘Although we aware that there is some debate about Gandhi’s life, most people in the city will see the statue in the context in which it was intended – to spread a message of peace, love and harmony.’

Some of Gandhi’s best quotes

‘You must not lose faith in humanity. Humanity is an ocean; if a few drops of the ocean are dirty, the ocean does not become dirty.’

‘Strength does not come from physical capacity. It comes from an indomitable will.’

‘Freedom is not worth having if it does not include the freedom to make mistakes.’

‘I will not let anyone walk through my mind with their dirty feet.’

‘In a gentle way, you can shake the world.’
