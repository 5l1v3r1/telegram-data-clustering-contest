Month of rail strikes over Christmas set to go ahead as talks collapse

21 Nov 2019, 14:09

Millions of commuters are set to be hit with Christmas misery as rail workers plan to strike for 27 days over the festive period.

Talks have failed to resolve a long-running dispute over guards on South Western Railway (SWR) trains.

Workers are now planning to walk out from December 2 until New Year’s Day.

The only non-strike dates are December 12 – General Election day – and Christmas and Boxing Day, when trains do not usually operate anyway.

The strike action was ordered by the Rail, Maritime and Transport union (RMT) and will hit commuters, shoppers and those wanting to see friends and family.

The union claims that leaving drivers, rather than guards, to open and close train doors is dangerous.

SWR and the rail watchdog say that driver-only controls are safe.

SWR runs services to and from London Waterloo, the country’s busiest station.

It is now drawing up an amended timetable, saying it plans to run more than half of the normal Monday to Friday services.

They acknowledged the strike would be ‘deeply frustrating for customers.’

RMT general secretary Mick Cash said: ‘RMT has attended discussions under the auspices of Acas for the past two days to discuss the issues in dispute.

‘No proposals that would enable the development of a resolution to the dispute have been put forward by SWR.

‘Throughout these talks SWR have not shown any intention of moving the issues at the heart of the dispute forwards, despite verbal assurances in earlier discussions.

‘It has become increasingly clear that they are not interested in reaching a settlement at this time.

‘As a result of the company attitude all planned action remains on, and the union is committed to ensuring the safest possible method of operation.

‘RMT will remain available for discussions with the company at any time.’

SWR said they remained committed to finding a solution and would be open to more talks with the union.

A spokesman said: ‘We’re very disappointed. Despite promising to keep guards on all our trains and promising that they will have a safety-critical role, the talks broke down today.

‘We believe these promises deliver on what the RMT has been asking for, so these strikes are unnecessary.

‘We remain committed to finding a solution that works for our customers and we remain open to talks if the RMT decides to reconsider its position.

‘In the meantime, our focus has to be on planning and delivering a train service for our customers.’

Prime Minister Boris Johnson has previously criticised the RMT strike action, saying: ‘Every train which does not run as a result of these unnecessary strikes impacts thousands of passengers at a very busy time when families and friends want to come together.’
