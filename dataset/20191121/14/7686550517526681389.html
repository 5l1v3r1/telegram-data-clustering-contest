A hairdresser did his salon up for Christmas and you've never seen anything like it

Rhys Jones spent three days and more than £3,000 to create his Alice in Wonderland themed Christmas decorations

21 Nov 2019, 14:29 by Anna Lewis

We all know that one person who takes decorating for Christmas to another level.

But a Valleys salon has gone above and beyond and has transformed itself into the set of Alice in Wonderland.

Rhys Jones, 32, spent three days and more than £3,000 to get the magical look - complete with a 12 foot Cheshire cat tail and furniture stuck to the ceiling.

According to the Christmas-mad hairdresser, though, it's all worth it to see the look on his customers' faces.

Rhys, who owns Vanity Hair Lounge in Gelli, said: "As soon as the salon closed the family came over to help. It took three days.

"I put the final touches on last night and literally collapsed into bed."

The fairytale wonderland isn't the first time Rhys's Christmas efforts have impressed passers by.

Last year his icy snow scene complete with hundreds of fairy lights and a giant glittery unicorn attracted hundreds of visitors from near and far.

He said: "[Last year] people were in the bus driving past and would say they needed to come in and see it. We gained so many new clients.

"I wanted to follow on from last year and the only thing that made sense was to do Alice in Wonderland this year and go for the complete opposite - so really colourful rather than icy."

He added: "It's taken a whole year, I would go look now and again and we would go shopping and looking for things.

"We bought a wrought iron air balloon from an antiques shop and we have a 12 foot Cheshire cat tail which a client made. It's stuffed with a mattress. We have pink chairs hanging from the ceiling.

"We wanted to go with an upside down theme to feel like everything is suspended.

"For the tea party there's fake food which have been made from polysterene."

For Rhys, his masterpiece wouldn't be possible without the help of his mam, stepdad, brother and sister.

Together, with the help of some Christmas music and a drink or two, the family worked around the clock, and until midnight on Saturday, to get the display ready on time.

Rhys, who lives in a flat above the hairdressers, said: "It's not easy as we all work such long hours but we all chip in. It's got to be done and you need some alcohol!

"It only opened [on Monday] but everyone who's been in looking has been asking if they can take videos and pictures.

"I definitely need upstairs to match downstairs now, but maybe not to the same extent!

"I'm always thinking of different themes, I've already planned next year's. Next year we will have been there 10 years so it needs to be even bigger."

So far Rhys hasn't sat down to add up the cost of his creation - but puts it at more than £3,000.

He said: "It sounds like a lot of money but when you spread it over the year it's worth it."

Rhys's mum Maria added: "People always ask me what Rhys is doing this year. I get it from around October because sine we work in the same area his clients are my clients.

"He's been working on it since March. He never uses the same decorations twice."
