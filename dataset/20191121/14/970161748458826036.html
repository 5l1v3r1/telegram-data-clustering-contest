Police floor woman called Christmas Carol for blowing kisses to passing motorist

21 Nov 2019, 14:31

Disturbing footage shows the aftermath of a police officer flooring a woman called Christmas Carol after telling her to stop blowing kisses to passing cars.

Carol, whose real name is Carol Christensen, also suffered a dislocated shoulder after her arms were yanked behind her back by the cop in South Lake Tahoe, California.

Monday’s incident began when the cop ordered Christmas Carol – who is well known for waving and blowing kisses to locals – to stop what she was doing.

The situation escalated when she refused to do so, with eyewitness Jessica Barackman’s video of Carol laying on the ground sparking fury online.

Barackman told CBS13: ‘I was like sick to my stomach that they would hurt her like that.

‘She’s like one of the most nice ladies you’ll ever meet.’

South Lake Tahoe Police have since released bodycam footage they say vindicates their officer’s conduct.

He can be heard telling Christmas Carol ‘You need to get out of the road… you’re going to get hurt.’

She responds: ‘Stop ordering people around.’

Cops say their footage shows Christmas Carol was asked to get back on the sidewalk 13 times before the situation escalated.

They say she was taken into custody ‘for her own safety’ before being given a citation and released.
