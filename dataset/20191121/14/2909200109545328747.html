BNI probes GH¢80k bribe against KMA staff

21 Nov 2019, 14:05 by MyNewsGH

The Bureau of National Investigations (BNI) is set to probe GH¢80,000 bribe allegations levelled against some staff of the Kumasi Metropolitan Assembly (KMA)

The Chief Executive Officer of  Kumasi Metropolitan Assembly (KMA), Hon. Osei Assiby Antwi  called for the security agencies to invite a trader in the metropolis over allegations some staff of the Assembly took huge sum of monies as bribe before allocating shops to traders at the newly Kejetia redeveloped market.

“As a matter of fighting corruption at KMA, I will ask the BNI and other security agencies to invite this trader to help us identify any staff who took money as bribe before allocating any a shop”, the Mayor vowed.

An anonymous trader, who spoke on Nhyira FM morning show monitored by MyNewsGh.com reporter, alleged, a certain KMA staff told her to pay GH¢80,000.00 to secure her a shop at the newly market.

“A man at KMA has told me that if I bring him Eighty Thousand Ghana Cedis, he will assist me own a shop and I am selling my car currently to enable me get that money”, the female trader who spoke on Nhyira FM  on condition of strict anonymity alleged.

Speaking immediately on the same network after the allegation, Hon. Osei Assiby Antwi indicated his readiness to fish out corrupt staff whom actions and inactions has tendency to bring the name of the government into disrupt.

“I will ask you [host] to help me get this trader’s contact so that she will help the security agencies to deal with staff doing corrupt things” the Mayor appealed to host.

Hon. Osei Assiby Antwi also sent a caution to traders who go to radio stations to malign people without evidence to desist from such practices since that ruins the relationships between staff and traders.

“Sometimes if you don’t understand our work come to us for explanations. You don’t have to go on air and use unprintable words on us. Once you do that, you can’t redeem back those words used if it turns that what you are saying is not true”, the Mayor advised.
