ScotRail will dump human waste on tracks next year despite stopping the process two years ago

21 Nov 2019, 22:06 by Chris Musson

UNDER-fire ScotRail will dump sewage onto tracks next year even though the practice should have been flushed out two years ago.

Nine old trains will drop human waste on lines in 2020 it emerged due to delays on carriages equipped with “retention tanks”.

Union bosses have slammed the firm over fears track workers could get sick from the poo.

ScotRail ended the process in 2017 but reintroduced old stock as a stop-gap due to a new fleet of refurbed InterCity trains being postponed.

The RMT union’s organiser Mick Hogg said: “We’re livid this promise has been broken.

“Why should our members be subjected to these disgusting conditions, being spra-yed by foul waste, as they do their work.”

All UK rail operators were meant to halt the practice by 2020 but ScotRail blamed suppliers after they admitted they will miss the deadline.

Tory Jamie Greene said: “It’s important we consider the impact this has on the track workers and the wider effects on the environment.”

The Dutch-based firm — who’ve got flak over delays, cancellations and service — was meant to have 26 renovated trains by December 2018. But only eight arrived.

ScotRail spokesman said: “We’re working with suppliers to ensure our fleet’s completed as soon as possible.”

Network Rail added: “There are a few companies given a bit more time. We are tracking their actions closely.”

We told last month how ScotRail bosses apologised after a passenger complained about "human zoo" toilets on the trains.

Scottish Labour said ScotRail had cancelled 75,000 trains since Abellio took over.
