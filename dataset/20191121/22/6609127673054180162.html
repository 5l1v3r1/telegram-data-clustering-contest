GOLDEN HANDSHAKE

Dundee Uni principal Andrew Atherton walks away with £127k pay-off after rent row

21 Nov 2019, 22:47 by John Jeffay

A UNIVERSITY chief who quit in a row over claims he didn’t pay his rent walked away with £127,250.

Prof Andrew Atherton, 53, received six months’ salary as well as £26,903 in pension contributions and £8,565 in outstanding leave payments.

The principal and vice-chancellor was suspended for allegedly failing to pay rent on a luxury flat provided to him by Dundee University.

It also emerged staff had made complaints about bullying to bosses. Investigations were launched into both claims.

Tory MSP Bill Bowman said: “This saga has been hanging over a brilliant institution for months now.

"Perhaps it’s best to draw a line under the matter and move on.”

Dundee said Prof Atherton — who took over the role in January — stepped down as its “vision for the future and values it holds are different to his aspirations”.

After tax and repayment of part of the £40,000 “disruption allowance” he got for his move from Lancaster University to Dundee, he will have around £70,000.
