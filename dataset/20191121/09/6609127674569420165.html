LIFE-THREATENING

Driver fighting for life in hospital after horror smash with articulated lorry on A92 in Fife

21 Nov 2019, 09:13 by Blair Meikle

A DRIVER is fighting for his life in hospital after collided with an HGV in a horror smash on the A92 in Fife.

Emergency services raced out at 5.20pm after a red Vauxhall collided with an articulated lorry between the Bankhead and Redhouse roundabouts near Kirkcaldy.

The car ended up being wedged beneath the lorry, and the driver had to be cut free by firefighters.

The 41-year-old man who was driving the Vauxhall was rushed to Ninewells Hospital, where he is being treated for life-threatening injuries.

The road was closed until late last night as an investigation was carried out.

Cops probing the crash are appealing to witnesses to come forward.

Sergeant Ewan Pearce, from the Glenrothes Road Policing station, said: “We are asking for anyone who witnessed the crash, or was driving through the area around the time, to come forward with any information they have, which could help our enquiries.

“Anyone with information can contact Police Scotland on 101, quoting incident 2547 of 20 November."
