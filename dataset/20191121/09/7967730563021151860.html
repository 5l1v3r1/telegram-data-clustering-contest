Man to face court after allegedly trying to lure girls into bush at Mandurah park

21 Nov 2019, 09:54

A 48-year-old man has been arrested and charged after allegedly approaching young girls and attempting to lure them into bushland at a Mandurah park.

The man appeared in Mandurah Magistrates Court on Thursday over the incident, which was alleged to have taken place on Friday, November 15 at the Quarry Adventure Park in Meadow Springs.

Police allege the man approached two young girls aged 11 and 12 outside the toilet block of the park, took a small note with a written message and dollar coin attached to it from his pocket, and dropped the note, coin and an open condom packed in front of them.

He allegedly the walked into the bush area of the park and behind a tree.

After reading the note, the girls left and spoke to their parents, who then contacted police.

The 48-year-old Lakelands man has been charged with procure, incite, encourage a child under 13-years to do an indecent act.

Mandurah detectives are still investigating the incident and are encouraging anyone who knows of any similar approaches made to children in recent weeks in the Lakelands and Meadow Springs area to contact police.

Anyone with any information is asked to call Crime Stoppers on 1800 333 000 or report online at www.crimestopperswa.com.au.
