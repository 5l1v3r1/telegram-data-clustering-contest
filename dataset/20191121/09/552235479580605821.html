Police conduct woodland search in desperate bid to find body of mum missing for two years

Patricia, who was known as Patti, vanished in November 2017.

21 Nov 2019, 09:48 by Ayrshire Post, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/ayrshire-post/

Police were yesterday combing the Gleniffer Braes in Paisley in a bid to find the body of missing mum Patricia Henry.

Patricia, who was known as Patti, vanished in November 2017.

Her family reported her missing have since made several heartfelt appeals for information.

Cops spent months searching a property in Burnfoot Crescent, Glenburn, and the flat she moved to in Girvan, but found no trace.

Now our sister paper the Paisley Daily Express understands the search for her body is focused on an area of the Gleniffer Braes.

A team of officers spent around an hour in sub-zero temperatures digging through the undergrowth of a dense pocket of woodland on Braehead Road near to Paisley Golf Club.

Police tape was tied to trees as markers during the morning search, which failed to uncover any further clues.

A spokesman for Police Scotland said: “A search has been taking place in the Paisley area which relates to the death of 47-year-old Paisley woman Patricia

Henry.

“She was last seen in her home in November 2017.

“A 69-year-old man was arrested in July this year in connection with this death.”

Patti was officially reported missing by family 20 months ago but was last seen on November 13, 2017 in the Girvan area.

Family members, including Patti’s daughter Alannah, handed out leaflets to members of the public in April in a bid to generate fresh information about her whereabouts.

Detectives have also spoken to hundreds of people in both Girvan and Paisley in an effort to establish where she could be.

As her family marked two years since they last saw Patti last week, her devastated brother Stephen told the Paisley Daily Express: “We all miss her so much. We miss her all the time.

“She was a pain in the backside but she was our pain in the backside.

“The last two years without her have been so difficult for the whole family.

“It’s been torture really. It doesn’t get any easier with time, that’s for sure.”

Stephen, from Paisley, explained how the whole family has been affected.

He said: “The knock-on effect has been massive for everyone.

“We think about her all the time.”
