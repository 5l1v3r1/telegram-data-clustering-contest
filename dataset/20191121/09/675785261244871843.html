Girl, 11, 'body-slammed' by 225lb Primark security guard after 'stealing socks'

Footage shows Leeajah Williams being pinned down by 36-year-old Mohammad Khan as his colleagues and shoppers plead with him during the seven-minute incident in Boston

21 Nov 2019, 09:37 by Lorraine King

Shocking footage has emerged that shows a 225lb security guard appearing to body-slam a child suspected of stealing socks at a Primark store.

The clip, which was posted on social media, shows Leeajah Williams being pinned down by 36-year-old Mohammad Khan inside the branch in Boston.

Khan is seen wrestling Leeajah to the ground after he allegedly caught her lifting a pack of socks and trying to walk out of the budget clothing store.

A witness screams: "He just punched her in the face" while a man says: "Don't hit her, don't hit her."

The 11-year-old, who is believed to have admitted theft, was left swollen and bruised following her seven-minute ordeal which took place in June, Sun Online reports.

 

The footage of the incident has come to light following Khan being charged with assault and battery on a child under 14 this week.

Khan and his bosses - New Jersey-based Securitas Security Services USA- are also accused of civil right violations.

Leeajah cannot be charged due to her being under the age of criminal responsibility under state laws, according to the  Boston Globe .

Her mum Sherrice Williams, who she says admits trying to steal clothing worth $175, shared photos of her with bruises and a swollen lip.

 

Sherrice also claims Leeajah has been left so traumatised by the incident on June 9 that she is having counselling.

The mum told  WCVB  News: "She stole a pack of socks, was walking out the door and a guy grabbed her and threw her against the wall - the security guard.

"She was screaming, 'Get off of me!'

"She didn't know who he was because he wasn't dressed in any type of security clothing, so she thought he was just a random man grabbing her."

Authorities have claimed that 6ft 1in Khan dragged the schoolgirl into a corner of the store that has no CCTV before punching, choking and leaping on her.

Khan, who works as a special part-time police officer, is claimed to have ignored pleas from his colleagues and shoppers and has been pulled up by his bosses four times before for using excessive force.

Suffolk District Attorney Rachael Rollins said: "The Boston Police were exemplary in de-escalating the situation when they arrived, and the other Securitas employees in the store behaved appropriately during the encounter.

"Being a Special Police Officer allows someone the power of arrest, but with that power comes great responsibility.

"I applaud the vast majority of Boston Police officers and SPOs who behave professionally and with restraint."

Khan is due before Suffolk Superior Court on December 17.
