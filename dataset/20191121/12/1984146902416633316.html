Newcastle United quietly confident Matty Longstaff will sign new contract in next few weeks

Newcastle United player Matty Longstaff's contract runs out next summer but Steve Bruce is hopeful a breakthrough in talks is imminent

21 Nov 2019, 12:21 by Ciaran Kelly

Steve Bruce is 'quietly confident' Matty Longstaff will sign a new contract with Newcastle United in the next few weeks.

Longstaff's current deal runs out next summer and one of Bruce's immediate priorities has been to tie down the 19-year-old to a new long-term deal.

It was been a remarkable few months for Longstaff, who left a big impression on Bruce during the club's pre-season tour of China, and just a few weeks after scoring on his Premier League debut against Manchester United , the midifelder made his international bow for England's under-20s.

Lee Charnley, the Magpies' managing director, has been speaking to a number of players' representatives about new deals in recent months and Bruce is confident a breakthrough is imminent.

"All of them things are still ongoing. Young Matty has been away, now played for England, represented his country, which we're all delighted about," he told reporters at his pre-match press conference ahead of Monday's trip to Aston Villa.

"We're quietly confident we'll get that tied up over the next few weeks but, as I've said many times now, it seems to take forever - even with a young lad like Matty - but we're quietly confident that it will be OK."

Longstaff has been an ever-present in the match day squad since firing Newcastle to victory against Manchester United last month and, with Isaac Hayden suspended, the midfielder kept his place against Chelsea and Wolves.

The youngster's older brother, Sean, will miss the trip to Villa as he is serving the final game of his three-match ban after being sent off against Wolves.
