Sword and Shield Introduce an Even Rarer Form of Shiny Pokémon

21 Nov 2019, 12:56 by Alistair Jones

In all my years of playing Pokémon, I've encountered just one shiny. The odds of such a meeting are already so small (1:4096) that, in 20 years, my shiny-hunting efforts amount to a single brown Liepard, a creature that's about to become even more worthless thanks to the discovery of a whole new, ultra-rare variant.

In a tweet yesterday, well-known dataminer Kaphotics showed off a snippet of code from Sword and Shield, saying "Shiny Pokémon rarity has been split." While the odds of finding a shiny remain the same, there are now two different types. If you're relying on breeding or random encounters, one in every 16 shinies will have square or diamond-shaped sparkles, rather than the traditional star-shaped ones. According to Kaphotics, that puts the odds of finding a square shiny at 1:65536.
