Football Hustlers: PSL Stars With Side Hustles

21 Nov 2019, 13:00 by SL Social

Footballers are predominantly known for their flashy lifestyles, which consists of the latest cars and the most expensive clothing.

However, there are also those who have planned for life after football and run successful businesses. Check out the PSL’s side hustle gang.

As the old adage goes, ‘football is a short career,’ is clearly something these stars abide by and have started making provision to maintain their lifestyles beyond their playing careers.

Whether it is starting a fashion brand or running your own farm, these players have established relatively successful side hustles.

Thabiso Semenya

The veteran Baroka FC defender owns a fleet of cars, which form part of his shuttle service, Kgaleke Tours. The 37-year-old can often be seen posting images of his impressive fleet on his Instagram page.

Kgotso Moleko

The Kaizer Chiefs defender is a proud farmer when he is not doing duty for Amakhosi. He owns a large variety of animals and does not mind getting his hands dirty on his Free State-based farm

Michael Morton

The AmaZulu star is a certified personal trainer and nutritional coach. When he is not marshalling the Usuthu midfield, the 30-year-old is putting together fitness regimes of whipping up strict diet plans.

Reneilwe Letsholonyane & Siphiwe Tshabalala

The veteran former Kaizer Chiefs stars earlier this year launched their joint business venture ShaYe Lounge, a modern rooftop hangout spot in the heart of Soweto.

Morgan Gould

Gould’s ‘MG’ fashion brand has quickly become one of the most sought after brands in Mzansi. Offering a host of fashion items, the veteran Stellenbosch defender is well on his way to fashion mogul status.
