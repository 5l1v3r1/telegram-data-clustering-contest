Exeter is named most expensive burger city in the country and includes one that costs £54

If you don't succeed your name appears on its Wall of Shame

21 Nov 2019, 13:41 by Anita Merritt

The hunt for the best-value burger city has seen Exeter named as being the home of the most expensive burgers in the UK, including one that will set you back a whopping £54.

Since the mid-2010s, more and more eateries, bars and gastropubs have been adding trendy burgers to their menus due to customer demand.

Exeter is well-known for having a particularly large number of burger restaurants, not all of which have stood the test of time.

It has now been named as the priciest city for a meat burger, with the average price coming in at £17.40, while Wolverhampton is a frugal burger lover’s paradise with the lowest average burger cost at £9.20 - a 87 per cent difference in price.

Exeter's most expensive burger is the Burger as Big as your Face which is served up at The Pig and Pallet in Topsham.

It consists of two 6oz beef patties, pulled pork, beef chuck, king bacon, pastrami, black pudding, onion rings, remoulade, swiss cheese, Big Jim’s BBQ Sauce, egg and pickles.

If you eat the meal all by yourself, including the fries, pickles and slaw, your name and picture will be put on its Hall of Fame and you get a t-shirt to prove you completed the challenge.

However, don't expect a free feed. The restaurant states you will not get the meal free no matter how fast you eat it.

And if you don't succeed your name appears on its Wall of Shame. So far there are 13 people who have tried and failed the challenge.

But on the Hall of Shame board is 11 people and among them is Marcus Davey who works as a chef at The Pig and Pallet.

The burger was added to its menu just over a year ago, and Marcus, who is known as The Food Dude at the company, says not conquering the burger mountain was never an option.

He admitted: "I was a bit sick afterwards. I did it in just under hour and I had friends with me and they were just like 'you animal'. I’m not a big eater but I had to do it to prove a point."

Explaining how you get what you pay for when it comes to burgers, he said: “We champion local produce. We have to pay more for free range and organic ingredients which are closer to home.

"One of our core beliefs is find the best and serve the best, and it does cost more for better things.

“Our burgers come served with fries in a red basket, but you can pimp it up if you want to such as adding cheese."

Exeter has also been named as the second worst value city for meat-free burgers at a cost of £13 - just 10p less than the worst city, Southampton.
