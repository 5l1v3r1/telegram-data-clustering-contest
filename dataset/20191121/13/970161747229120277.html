Step-mum charged with sex crime for cleaning topless in front of kids

21 Nov 2019, 13:34

A woman may have to register as a sex offender for walking around with her breasts out in front of her step-children at home.

Tilli Buchanan and her husband took off their shirts to stop them getting dusty while doing garage work in late 2017 or early 2018.

The children, then aged nine and 13, walked past them at their home in Salt Lake City, Utah.

She told a court Tuesday: ‘It was in the privacy of my own home. My husband was right next to me in the same exact manner that I was, and he’s not being prosecuted.’

Buchanan’s lawyers said ‘she considers herself a feminist and wanted to make a point that everybody should be fine with walking around their house or elsewhere with skin showing.’

Buchanan was charged with three counts of misdemeanor lewdness in January after a separate child welfare investigation was launched but was not linked to her.

Buchanan volunteered the information to officials because she was ‘alarmed’ by the proceedings, and was later arrested.

Her  lawyers are seeking the charges overturned because they are sexist and unfair.

Prosecutors have counter-argued that nudity is commonly understood to include women’s breasts, and that the courts must uphold morality laws.

Presiding Judge Kara Pettit said the case was ‘too important an issue’ to decide in a hurry, and has said she will issue a ruling in the coming months.

Buchanan’s case comes amid an ongoing ‘free the nipple’ movement urging women to go topless to challenge similar laws in other US states.
