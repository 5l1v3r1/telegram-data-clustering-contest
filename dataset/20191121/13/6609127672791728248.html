Celtic loan star Elyounoussi says future is down to Southampton and Hoops but ‘he’s happy’ at Parkhead

21 Nov 2019, 13:50 by Alan Galindo

CELTIC loan star Mohamed Elyounoussi says his future is down to Southampton and Hoops - but he's happy.

The Norwegian international has been a standout performer at Parkhead since his arrival on a season-long loan deal.

The attacker, who has played 12 games in all competitions, has scored seven goals and provided six assists so far this campaign.

Elyounoussi, who was named Ladbrokes Player of the Month for October, admits he hasn't really thought about his long-term future.

But says he's enjoying his time with Neil Lennon's men.

Speaking to the Ladbrokes social club, he said: "Yeah I mean for now it's not up to me. The clubs have to talk.

"I am really happy here. Celtic is an amazing club but I haven't really thought about my future yet.

"I just think about the next training, next game and do my best.

"In the summer we will see where we go."

Elyounoussi was sent home by Norway over the international break with a foot injury.

He is a doubt for the clash with Livingston on Saturday but should be ready for Rennes in the Europa League next week.

He added: "I picked it up a couple of weeks ago against Hibs in the semi-final.

"It's been a bit painful since then and hasn't really had the time to heal.

"With a little rest and some treatments I should be alright."

The 25-year-old, who arrived at Celtic on summer deadline day, has revealed ex-Celt Stuart Armstrong helped him decide to join the club. 

The Scotland international was gushing in his praise for his old club as he and Elyounoussi sat next to each other at the club's Staplewood training base.

He also sought advice from Norway team-mate Kris Ajer and Fraser Forster.

He said: “Stuart told me so many good things about Celtic.

“I had also spoken with Kris Ajer so I had a good knowledge of what I was coming into. I didn’t look at it as a step down. I needed to get minutes on the pitch.

“I feel Celtic have brought my confidence back. I am scoring goals and I’m really happy. I know the quality I have and I think the manager knows that as well.

“So with his belief and trust in me and the help of the coaches too, I feel I am bringing what I can to the team.”
