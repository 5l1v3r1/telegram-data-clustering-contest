'LIGHTS OUT'

Rangers legend Ally McCoist reveals cheeky response over Aberdeen Christmas lights invite

21 Nov 2019, 13:45 by Gary Taylor

RANGERS idol Ally McCoist has revealed that he cheekily turned down an offer to turn on Aberdeen's Christmas lights.

The Light Blues idol was invited by the city's Lord Provost for the grand switch on a number of years ago.

Speaking on talkSPORT, the 57-year-old admitted he couldn't resist a light-hearted dig at the Dons in his response.

The ex-striker lifted the lid that he received the request when he was in his Ibrox heyday.

Although he wrote to the city council chief to inform them that he wouldn't be in attendance due to a "prior engagement".

He said: "They asked me to turn the lights on, on Union Street.

"I sent a letter back. Laura, the secretary at Rangers and I, we sent a lovely letter back.

"I said: 'Dear Lord Provost, thank you very much for your wonderful invitation to turn on the Christmas lights on Union Street in Aberdeen.

"Unfortunately, due to a prior engagement, I will not be able to take you up on your very, very kind offer.

"P.S. The irony isn't lost on me, as I normally come up to Aberdeen and put your lights out!'"

Meanwhile, Gers star Ryan Jack has been pictured in a hilarious throwback picture with ex-team-mate Fraser Fyvie.

Jack played at Aberdeen for seven years between 2010 and 2017.

The midfield ace turned out to be a great player for the Dons, as he came through the youth ranks to make his mark on the first team and eventually skipper them.

The Scotland international played with some great players such as Adam Rooney, Niall McGinn and Jonny Hayes.

However, its one other familiar name who has popped up to give fans a laugh with a baby-faced throwback picture.

Cove Rangers ace Fraser Fyvie, who played with Jack at the Dons for three years in the first team, shared the snap on social media.
