Aston Villa monitoring international striker ahead of €5m January bid- reports

Aston Villa transfer news | With just over a month until the January window opens, rumours linking a number of players with a move to B6 are beginning to surface

21 Nov 2019, 13:55 by Ben Husband, https://www.birminghammail.co.uk/authors/ben-husband/

Aston Villa are keen on Austria Wien striker Christoph Monschein, according to reports.

The 27-year-old, who has 13 goals in 18 appearances for club and country this season, is Wien’s most saleable asset and his sale could help to alleviate some financial concerns.

Sport 24 (via Sport Witness ) have suggested that Villa are interested in the striker, but have been put off by his high asking price.

The report claims that his club are looking for €5m, for the player described as their ‘super striker’.

 

Villa have reportedly been scouting Monschein ahead of the January window, with Dean Smith keen to bolster his attacking ranks.

Austria Wien are currently enduring some financial difficulties and could look to offload the striker, although management believe his sale would cause relegation.

Smith has hinted that he will look to bring a striker in when the January window opens, with Keinan Davis’ injury leaving only Wesley and Jonathan Kodjia to compete for the centre forward role.
