Prince Andrew’s ‘alibi’ doubts royal’s explanation of key trip in Jeffrey Epstein scandal

21 Nov 2019, 13:34 by Lee Brown

Prince Andrew faced further humiliation Thursday when his alibi for a key New York trip in the Jeffrey Epstein scandal was shot down by the former diplomat whom he said he had stayed with, according to a report.

The Duke of York has been accused by Epstein accuser Virginia Roberts Giuffre of having sex with her in the late moneyman’s Upper East Side mansion in April 2001 when she was just 17.

Andrew insisted during his “car crash” of an interview with the BBC that the allegations were impossible — because he was “staying with the consul-general” during that particular Manhattan trip.

However, Sir Thomas Harris, the consul-general in New York at the time, told the Daily Mail that he had “no recollection” of the prince staying at his official residence during that trip.

“It doesn’t sound as if he stayed with me,” Harris, who served in New York from 1999 to 2004, told the paper. “I don’t recall him staying with me.”

Harris, 74, admitted he no longer had a copy of his 2001 diary. However, he stressed that such overnight stays by senior royals tend to be formally registered in the Court Circular.

No such stays are listed for that period, which “makes me suspect he wasn’t with me that night,” Harris told the paper.

“If you go through the Court Circular, you will come across the other visits he paid to New York. That was the typical pattern,” he said.

“It doesn’t ring any bell whatsoever,” he said of the 2001 trip.

Giuffre has insisted in court filings that convicted pedophile Epstein forced her to have sex with Andrew at least three times, including the April 2001 New York trip.

The Court Circular from the time shows the prince flew to New York on the morning of April 9, a flight that would have landed in New York just after lunchtime — with no duties listed for the rest of the day, according to the Telegraph, noting gaps in his schedule during the trip.

The same day Andrew arrived, his accuser, Giuffre, also flew into New York on Epstein’s private jet, the Telegraph said, citing flight logs.

Andrew admitted he “probably did” visit close pal Epstein during the trip, but said there was “definitely no activity” because he did not sleep over.

“Because of what I was doing, I was staying with the consul-general, which is further down the street on Fifth,” Andrew told the BBC in the interview that aired Saturday.

“So I wasn’t staying there,” he said of Epstein’s mansion. “I may have visited, but no, definitely didn’t, definitely, definitely, no, no, no activity.”

Andrew used his interview to once again adamantly deny the accusations against him.
