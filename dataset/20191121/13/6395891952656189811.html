Suspect in fatal stabbing at Brookline park heads to court

21 Nov 2019, 13:13 by EndPlay

BROOKLINE, Mass.Â (AP) - The suspect in Brookline's first homicide in 13 years is heading to court.

Norfolk District Attorney Michael Morrissey says 21-year-old Singha Oeun is scheduled to be arraigned on a murder charge Thursday in the death of Nicolas Kern.

The 20-year-old victim was stabbed multiple times in Amory Park at about 2:30 a.m. Tuesday and died at the hospital.

Police Chief Andrew Lipson did not disclose a motive but says the men knew each other and there is no threat to the public. Both men lived in Brookline.

Kern's death is the first homicide in the Boston suburb since 2006.

It could not be determined if the suspect had an attorney.

Amory Park is a family-friendly facility of about eight acres with ballfields, walking trails and tennis courts.
