Prue Leith slammed for hypocritical stance on limiting treats

21 Nov 2019, 08:50

Great British Bake Off’s Prue Leith has come under fire on Good Morning Britain for suggesting that treats in lunchboxes should be limited to once a week.

The television judge joined Susanna Reid on the breakfast programme to debate that she thinks schools should ban packed lunches because she believes they contribute to childhood obesity.

Beginning the debate, Prue said most parents ‘fill lunch boxes with junk’ and said that items with high-fat contents should be restricted.

However, Susanna noted that Prue might not be in the best position to preach the notion because of her role on the Channel 4 baking show, and asked: ‘Do you not see some conflict in your role as a host of a programme which is all about cake?’

‘Of course there’s a very obvious one, there I am sugar every day on Bake Off but this is competition,’ Prue clapped back. ‘Nobody is recommending that you do that every day.’

She continued: ‘What happens is and what I like about is that most people get into cooking through baking. You start with a brownie or cupcakes, that’s the best way to get into cooking at all.’

But Susanna continued: ‘So we can’t reduce people to a treat per week because then they wouldn’t cook a full Sunday roast?’

Prue then cleared up her previous comment, telling the host: ‘I never said you should have a treat a week, I said mothers shouldn’t put a treat into the lunchbox every week, they’re going to get dozens of treats at home.’

Viewers were conflicted over Prue’s remarks, with many disagreeing with her stance on packed lunches on Twitter.

‘Should we ban pack lunches? No. If schools are under financial pressure, asking them to find more money to fund meals for all would be ridiculous,’ scorned one.
