Italy boss Roberto Mancini's bizarre transfer advice for Everton's Moise Kean

Roberto Mancini thinks Moise Kean should leave Everton ahead of EURO 2020

21 Nov 2019, 08:08 by Caoimhe O'Neill

Former Man City coach Roberto Mancini thinks Moise Kean needs to leave Everton.

Speaking after the forward scored twice for Italy Under-21s, the national team manager revealed his hopes for the 19-year-old ahead of next summer's EURO 2020 finals.

"I hope he finds a club that allows him to play regularly,” he told Rai Sport as cited by Forza Italian Football.

"A lot will depend on what he does from now until May for the Euros."

Kean, who joined the Blues from Juventus in the summer, scored twice for Italy's Under-21s on Tuesday in their 6-0 rout of Armenia.

The teenager is still awaiting his first Everton goal having featured 11 times this term.

But speaking after the win with the young Azzurri, Kean believes he has provided the perfect response to any critics after his well-taken brace.

"The only thing I need to do is show a response out on the pitch," the striker said.

"We played excellent football and need to continue in this manner.

"Do I hope to be called up to the senior side? Of course, it’s everyone’s dream."
