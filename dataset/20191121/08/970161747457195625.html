Coldplay won't tour unless it's carbon netural as they release new album

21 Nov 2019, 08:36

Coldplay have revealed they’re not going to be touring to promote their latest album, explaining that they would want any tour to be carbon neutral.

Frontman Chris Martin explained that the lads are keen to do their bit for the environment.

‘We’re taking time to see how our tour can be actively beneficial,’ he said. ‘All of us have to work out the best way of doing our job.

‘We’re taking time over the next year or two to work out how can, not only our tour be sustainable, but how can it be actively beneficial,’ the star added to BBC News.

The band’s last tour saw them put on a whopping 122 shows across four continents in 2016 and 2017.

Chris pointed out: ‘Our next tour will be the best possible version of a tour like that environmentally. We would be disappointed if it’s not carbon neutral.’

Instead, the band will take to Amman, the capital of Jordan, to play two shows which will be streamed on YouTube.

Meanwhile, they’re also set to stage a show at the Natural History Museum, with the proceeds going towards environmental charity Client Earth.

Despite turning down a tour, the lads are definitely keeping themselves busy as they look to be clashing with Robbie Williams for Christmas number one.

This week will see Robbie release his first ever Christmas album The Christmas Present, while Coldplay are also dropping their anticipated new album Everyday Life on Friday.

Both releases come one month before Christmas meaning they’ll have to hold onto the top spot for four weeks, but sources say they’re expected to enjoy big sales.

A source told The Sun: ‘Coldplay and Robbie are both massive forces when it comes to selling albums so the numbers they will shift next week will be huge.

‘They have strong track records and very dedicated fan bases who will fight for their act to get to number one.’

They added: ‘At the moment Coldplay are looking likely to pip him to the post, but with a week and a half to go it’s anyone’s game, especially given all of the publicity Robbie has done.’
