How Northumberland proved a stunning subject for photography competition

Northern Lights, dramatic waves and a less than sheepish sheepdog among entries showing diversity of Northumberland in all its glory

21 Nov 2019, 08:00 by Tony Henderson

Bill the collie certainly wasn’t sheepish when the camera pointed in his direction – and he ended up providing one of the top pictures for a photography competition.

The image of Bill rounding up the sheep in the College Valley in Northumberland against a Cheviots background won a runner-up award for photographer Colin Balmbro, from Wooler.

It was one of 200 entries in Northumberland County Council’s Discover our Land “Days Out” photo competition, which celebrated places to visit in the county.

The winner of the competition, which attracted more than 5,500 public votes on the Discover our Land Facebook page, was Sunderland Nissan worker Ashley Corr, of Houghton-le-Spring, for his stunning picture of the Northern Lights at Holy Island.

The other runner-up was David Southern of Warkworth for his dramatic Waves at Howick, with Dunstanburgh Castle as the brooding backdrop.

Charlotte Christian, aged 12, who lives in Prudhoe, was the winner of the under-16s section for her autumnal shot of the River Tyne from Ovingham Bridge. The pupil at Ovingham Middle School won a prize of a three-hour photography class with professional photographer Neil Denham, also one of the judges.

“I have been taking photos, just on my phone, for a little while now and I really enjoy it,” Charlotte said. “I was on my way home from school when I took this one and just loved all the different colours and the way the water was moving in currents.

“My mum entered me in the competition and I was so happy when she told me I had won. It’s really exciting to now get the chance to go out with a professional photographer.

“I would love to do it as a career when I’m older and I’m hoping to get a camera for Christmas so when I go out with Neil I can take some really good photos.”

Charlotte’s mum Louise said: “She is absolutely over the moon to win and so is excited to get the opportunity to learn some tricks of the trade from a professional photographer.”

Ashley meanwhile only entered his picture minutes before the competition closed after hearing about it from a friend.

The amateur photographer, who won an overnight stay for two at Langley Castle in Northumberland, said: “I have been taking photos in our region for quite a few years now just as a hobby, but this is the first time I have entered a competition so I am delighted to have won.

“The photo is a nod to the fact that the beauty and splendour of Northumberland isn’t just apparent during daylight. Quite often the magic begins when darkness falls – and on this occasion Mother Nature played her part and put on a stunning light show from the refuge hut at Holy Island.

“It’s a memory that will stay with me for a long time, and another reason why my hobby is so enjoyable.”

Neil said: “Once again the standard of entries was outstanding and we were particularly excited by the standard of the youngsters who entered. It’s fantastic to see young talent coming through and I am very much looking forward to passing on my knowledge to Charlotte.

“Northumberland has so many stunning locations. It’s a real playground for photographers, and all those shortlisted show the breadth of things on offer and the great days out you can have in the county.

“The Discover our Land campaign is really bringing the county to the fore and sharing the county’s best stories.”

The campaign was launched in January to raise the profile of Northumberland as a place to live, work, visit and invest. For more information go to www.discoverourland.co.uk
