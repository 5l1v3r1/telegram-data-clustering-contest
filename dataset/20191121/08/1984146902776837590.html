Teen seriously injured pensioners when he crashed his parents' Range Rover into their car

Charles Elliott, who had only passed his test four-months previously, failed to give way at a junction and crashed his parents' Range Rover into elderly couple's car

21 Nov 2019, 08:00 by Sara Nichol

An A-Level student seriously injured two pensioners when he crashed his parents' Range Rover into their car.

Charles Elliott had only passed his test four months earlier when he failed to give way at a junction "at speed" and smashed the high-powered vehicle into an elderly couple's Honda Civic, a court heard.

The 18-year-old, who was 17 at the time and still in school, was then heard shouting "F**k, what have I done?" by witnesses when he stepped out of his car and saw the scale of the accident on the Military Road, near Rudchester, in Northumberland.

The pensioners, a man and wife, had to be cut-free from their vehicle and were both taken to hospital, one via the Great North Air Ambulance, with serious injuries.

A court heard that the male suffered numerous pelvic fractures, fractured ribs, a broken collarbone, a broken shoulder and a cut to the liver, while the woman suffered a ruptured diaphragm, multiple fractures to the sternum, multiple fractures to the pelvis and a cut liver.

Both were in hospital for several weeks.

Now, Elliott, of Loweswood Close, in Jesmond Dene, Newcastle, has been fined Â£300 and banned from the roads for six months after he admitted one count of careless driving at North Tyneside Magistrates' Court.

District Judge Bernard Begley told the teen: "I do question the wisdom of anyone making the decision in these particular circumstances to allow you to drive a vehicle that powerful, particularly on rural roads in an area you're not familiar with and you had only driven the vehicle a couple of times before."

The judge added: "You are so, so lucky that this has not turned into a tragedy and, what I mean is potentially death, because some of the injuries sustained, particularly [the male victim], were quite severe."

The court was told that Elliott had been at a a school rugby fundraising event at Close House Hotel, in Heddon-on-the-Wall, on April 22 this year when he was asked by his parents to take his friend home in their car

James Long, prosecuting, said: "The defendant has failed to give way joining the Military Road from a minor road and he's struck an elderly couple's car, which was travelling from west to east on the Military Road.

"He's effectively driving north and hit them on the left side."

The court heard the couple were returning from an 80th birthday party when they were hit by Elliott.

Mr Long added: "One witness saw the Range Rover enter the junction at speed and making no attempt to stop, slow or give way.

"It collided with another vehicle and he saw the Range Rover lifting high in the air and spinning around and coming to rest on the opposite side of the junction near a field boundary fence."

The same witness then saw Elliott get out and heard him say "F**k, what have I done? I didn't mean to do it".

Mr Long said that the pensioners' car had been "catapulted" into the air and landed in a field several metres away.

In a statement, the male victim said: "The strain and impact on my family has been immense.

"The anger, frustration and worry has been unbearable. Providing a police statement is about as traumatic as the incident itself."

Charles Weidner, defending, said it was hard to tell what had caused the accident.

He added: "It could have been the powered-up vehicle, it could have been a lapse in concentration, but he's full of guilt about causing the accident.

"It's greatly effected his school work and exams and he's had to have counselling."

District Judge Begley told Elliott that he now needed to move on with his life as he'd made a mistake and seemed like an "independent and capable young man".
