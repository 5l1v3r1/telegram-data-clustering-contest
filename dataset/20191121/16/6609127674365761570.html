REJECTED

‘Attractive’ blonde carer who engaged in sexual activity with young boy in her care has appeal rejected

21 Nov 2019, 16:01 by David Meikle

AN ATTRACTIVE care worker who engaged in sexual activity with a teenage boy she was supposed to be looking after has lost a bid to overturn her conviction.

Kayleigh Martin, 27, met the 16-year-old boy, who cannot be named for legal reasons, at a residential centre which offers support for troubled teenagers and young adults.

They exchanged messages and began an 'inappropriate relationship' until the affair came to light and care home bosses called in police.

Martin denied allegations of engaging in sexual activity while in a position of trust and sending a sexual communication as she went on trial at Lanark Sheriff Court.

She was convicted by Sheriff Nikola Stewart and in April was placed on the sex offenders register for two years.

Martin was also banned from contacting the teenager and was ordered to perform 250 hours of unpaid work.

The 27-year-old launched a bid to have the conviction overturned at the Sheriff Appeal Court in Edinburgh but that was thrown out before any full hearing could take place.

Martin, of Hamilton, Lanarkshire, has now appeared at Hamilton Sheriff Court where she was granted a further nine months to complete her unpaid work by Sheriff Vincent Smith.

Ian Scott, defending, added: "Her appeal in this matter is at an end because it failed to make it through the sift stage.

"We are asking the court to grant her an extension to allow her to complete the court order she was given."

The Sheriff Appeal Court confirmed Martin's case had been refused at the 'second sift stage' of the appeal process.

Sentencing her at a previous trial, Sheriff Stewart blasted Martin's 'appalling behaviour'.

He said: "This was a serious relationship regardless of what she thought it was.

"He is a young man who has an attractive, caring young woman who was giving him a highly unusual amount of attention and the warning bells should have been ringing much earlier.

"I don't think she is predatory but is someone who is naive and foolish.

"I have no doubt that this will have undoubted serious repercussions on his life and frankly this is a dreadful thing she has done.

"The benefit of her education has now been entirely wasted.

"I regard this appalling behaviour very seriously and can't say enough how worried I am about the repercussions of this on this young man's life."
