Vikings' Alicia Agneson shares character transformation as she returns to show

21 Nov 2019, 16:34

Alicia Agneson is returning to Vikings despite her character Freydis being killed off at the end of season five.

And she’s just gone and teased her comeback to the History Channel hit on Instagram, with a video of her transformation.

Leading up to her death scene, where she was brutally murdered by Ivar The Boneless, the goddess-like Nordic sported long blonde hair that was usually braided in some incredibly intricate design.

However, in recent trailers, Freydis has been spotted alive and kicking, but this time around she’s a Russian brunette.

Now, we’re pretty sure there was no such thing as hair dye back in 800AD, so we can’t help but wonder how director Michael Hirst has managed to bring the star back.

But she is returning, alright, and Alicia couldn’t help but hint at her ‘new’ role just weeks before season 6 is set to kick off.

‘HONESTLY GUYS I can’t contain my excitement for the 4th of December when Season 6 of @historyvikings airs,’ she wrote to her 142,000 followers.

‘Here’s a little sneak for you of me, @mucy_lartin , @zuelikadelaney & @aideenjo . This is how we roll at 5am in the morning.’

In the short clip, the 23-year-old can be seen in hair and makeup singing along to Lynn Anderson’s song, Rose Garden.

And as she pans the camera around, she shoots to newcomer Lucy Martin who will be playing Ingrid in the next installment of the historical saga.

Fans were quick to comment and couldn’t contain their excitement over the impending episodes.

‘Beauties. I am ridiculously excited to see Freydis again and her look a like. Oh And obviously Ingrid,’ one wrote while another said: ‘OMG!!!! this season it’s gonna be AMAZING! can’t wait to see.’

Someone else questioned the look, adding: ‘It’s fake blonde hair?’

Alicia is yet to confirm how she managed to make a comeback and whether or not she will be playing Freydis or will return to a new role.

However, we don’t have long to find out as Vikings season six is set to drop on the History Channel and Amazon Prime 4 December.
