Nursery nurse Demi Harris has jail term cut for killing partner Christopher Pearson

Judges said the sentence was too long

21 Nov 2019, 16:48 by Champion News Service

A nursery nurse who killed her partner after a drunken row at a hot-tub party at her Nottinghamshire home had her jail term slashed by top judges.

Demi Harris, 22, knifed Christopher Pearson, 39, to death in the bedroom at their home, in Hucknall, in February last year.

Both were "quite drunk" and Harris was angry when her partner stripped to get into a hot-tub with other friends.

She went to challenge him and was dragged fully-clothed into the water, sparking a row which led to her stabbing him twice with a knife.

She claimed it was self-defence and was acquitted of murder, but convicted of manslaughter at Nottingham City Crown court last October.

Harris, of Strathmore Close, was jailed for eight years.

But after a challenge at the Court of Appeal in London, top judges have now slashed Harris' jail term to six years.

"We have concluded that the judge imposed a sentence which was too long," said Mrs Justice Cockerill.

Ruling, the judge said Harris and Mr Pearson had been together for three years in a "somewhat volatile" relationship.

There was evidence before the jury at the trial that he was "on occasion aggressive and violent" towards his younger partner.

On February 17, 2018, they went out drinking around Hucknall and ended up back at their house with friends.

At 4.30am, Harris looked out of a window and saw Mr Pearson in his pants near their hot-tub.

She shouted at him and he responded by dropping his boxer shorts to the floor.

Harris went outside to confront him, but a male guest pulled her into the tub, fully-clothed.

Angry, she got out and poured pool cleaner into the hot-tub before turning it off.

She was followed inside by Mr Pearson, who she claimed then attacked her.

She claimed that, in fear, she had taken hold of a knife, and he was stabbed twice.

Later, she said she "wanted to poke him" to get him away.

By its verdict, the jury accepted that she did not intend to cause him serious injury, but also rejected her self-defence claim.

Appealing against her sentence, Harris claimed the trial judge had not fully taken into account her mitigation.

And he had placed too much emphasis on her use of a knife, her lawyers claimed.

Giving judgment, Mrs Justice Cockerill said Harris had been "remorseful" immediately after the incident and done what she could to help.

The attack was not premeditated and she was still young, the judge added.

It was "wrong in principle" to count the use of the knife as an aggravating feature, it having already been taken into account in the sentencing.

"Accordingly, we quash the sentence imposed and substitute a sentence of six years' imprisonment," she concluded.
