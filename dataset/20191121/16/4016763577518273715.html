Eminem Mimicked Comedian Chris D’Elia’s Impression Of Him When They Met

21 Nov 2019, 16:44 by Eddie Fu

In September 2018, comedian Chris D'Elia went viral for his parody of Eminem rapping. He caught the Detroit MC’s attention earlier this year with another impersonation, which led to a recent meeting between the two celebrities. On a recent episode of his Congratulations podcast, D'Elia reminisced about visiting Em in the studio and recalled how the rapper gave him the ultimate compliment by mimicking the comedian’s impression.

“We show up and Eminem’s just sitting there like, ‘Come on in,’” D'Elia remembered. “Eminem stands up and goes like this, ‘Flappin’ around like a bapkin'‘ to me, and I’m like, 'Okay, this is the fucking craziest thing to me already.’ He’s doing my impression [of him] to me.”

On D'Elia’s first Eminem parody, he closes it out by rapping:

You using way too many napkins
Bapkins
Lapkins and Chapki-
You using ChapStick and napkins while I’m bapkin'
Flappin' around like a bapkin'

Logic later sampled the viral video on the outro of his Eminem collaboration, “Homicide,” which was released earlier this year as the third single from the Maryland rapper’s album, Confessions of a Dangerous Mind.
