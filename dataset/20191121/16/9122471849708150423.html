ESPN Predicts What’ll Happen If Penn State Beats Ohio State

21 Nov 2019, 16:47 by Dan Lyons

Right now, the College Football Playoff appears to be heading towards a field of LSU, Ohio State, Clemson, and one from the group of Georgia, Oregon, Alabama, Utah, with a few others waiting in the wings. It would only take one upset to send things topsy-turvy as we near the end of the season.

On Tuesday night, during ESPN’s rankings release show, Rece Davis introduced a “nightmare scenario,” where Minnesota wins out and beats undefeated Ohio State for the Big Ten title, and Georgia beats LSU for the SEC title. That would really send thing for a spin, and it isn’t that unreasonable, given how explosive the Golden Gophers have looked at times this year.

We might not have to wait for the Big Ten Championship Game for things to get weird, though. Penn State travels to Ohio State this weekend.

The Nittany Lions are big 18-point underdogs in Columbus, so a win isn’t particularly likely. James Franklin’s team is one of the best in the country though, and upsets of that magnitude happen all the time in college football. A win would likely send PSU to the Big Ten title, setting up either a game against Wisconsin, or a rematch with the Minnesota team that handed it its only loss.

On today’s episode of Get Up on ESPN, college football reporter Maria Taylor and former Texas star Emmanuel Acho debated the impact of a potential Penn State win on Saturday.
