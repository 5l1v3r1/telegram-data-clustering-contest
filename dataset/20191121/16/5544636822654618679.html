Jonathan – Dickson Feud: Here’s What Nnamdi Kanu’s Counsel Says

21 Nov 2019, 16:14 by Ridwan Yusuf

A Counsel to the leader of the outlawed Indigenous People of Biafra (IPOB), Nnamdi Kanu, Barrister Aloy Ejimakor has expressed his reservations about the reported feud between former President Goodluck Jonathan and sitting governor of Bayelsa state, Seriake Dickson, Concise News reports.

The legal practitioner went proverbial, warning that “when two grasshoppers fight to death, it is the crow that gets to feast on their carcass.”

Ejimakor wrote on his known Twitter handle on Thursday: “Goodluck Jonathan & Gov Dickson should’ve known that when 2 brothers fight to death, a STRANGER inherits their property or when 2 grasshoppers fight to death, it is the crow that gets to feast on their carcass”. That’s the summary of the electoral self-immolation in Bayelsa.”
