Beyonce, Ed Sheeran, Billie Eilish, Taylor Swift And Ariana Grande Are Up For Best Pop Vocal Album Grammy Award

21 Nov 2019, 16:46 by Charisse Van Horn

The 62nd Grammy nominations were announced and there’s some serious competition for Best Pop Vocal Album. To qualify for a vocal album at least 51 percent of the album must be comprised of original vocals. The category was once known as Best Contemporary Album then it was simply Best Pop Album. The 51 percent of vocal tracks ensure that the nominees are separate from instrumental type albums and ones that consist primarily of music in various formats.

This is relatively a new category and only two artists have won two Grammys in it. They are Adele and Kelly Clarkson.

The nominees for Best Pop Solo Album are all women. The Best Pop Vocal Album category were almost all female save for four-time Grammy winner Ed Sheeran who scored his 14th Grammy nomination with Best Pop Vocal Album.

Here is the complete list of those nominated for Best Pop Vocal Album.

  1. “The Lion King: The Gift” by Beyonce
  2. “When We All Fall Asleep, Where Do We Go” by Billie Eilish
  3. “Thank U, Next” By Ariana Grande
  4. “Lover” by Taylor Swift
  5. “No. 6 Collaborations Project” by Ed Sheeran
