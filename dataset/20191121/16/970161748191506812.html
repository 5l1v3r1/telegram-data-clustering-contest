Making a Murderer's lawyer bids to free man jailed over murder seen in a dream

21 Nov 2019, 16:33

Netflix has been upping its true crime game over the past year, but this is the one that may blow your mind the most.

2015 film Dream/Killer has been bought by the streaming service, and sees Making a Murderer’s Kathleen Zellner of a man who was convicted of murder – based on the testimony of a dream someone had.

Ryan Ferguson was 20 years old when he was convicted for the 2001 murder of 48-year-old Kent Heitholt, a sports reporter found dead outside his offices in Columbia, Missouri.

Heitholt was found on the floor having been strangled by his own belt.

Just 17 at the time of the murder, in 2005 he Ferguson was sentenced to 40 years in jail for the killing – and triggered a 10 year battle spearheaded by his father Bill to get him freed.

The biggest piece of evidence that sold the jury? An anonymous tip-off to Charles ‘Chuck’ Erickson, who had taken drugs the night of the crime, and after spending time with Ryan that evening, had dreamed they may have committed it.

Yup. That’s it. That’s the evidence.

‘I didn’t know if it was memory, or if it was a dream. I assumed that if I’d done it, Ryan and I did it to get more money for drink,’ Erickson is seen telling cameras in the documentary.

Spurred on by police evidence and ‘remembering things’ while they were interrogating him, Chuck implicated himself in the crimes as well as Ryan.

But Bill Ferguson knew his son was innocent, and as a result when on his own mission for justice.

Hiring Kathleen Zellner to support his son, Bill goes on a mission to get Ryan freed, but was repeatedly refused a retrial despite the growing among of evidence supporting his innocence.

In 2013, Ryan was finally released, and continues to support those who also insist they’re innocent after he himself was exonerated.

He now works as a personal trainer and health and fitness guru, and temporarily hosted an MTV series called Unlocking The Truth, which focuses on the potential innocence of prisoners.

Dream/Killer is available to watch now on Netflix.
