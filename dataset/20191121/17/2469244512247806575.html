Here's how much the I'm A Celebrity campmates are being paid this year

The new series of I'm A Celebrity..Get Me Out Of Here! is in full swing, and here is what the stars are rumoured to be raking in for their jungle debut

21 Nov 2019, 17:08 by Jess Morcom

I'm A Celebrity... Get Me Out Of Here! is back for 2019 with an all new line-up of stars looking to face their fears in the jungle over the next few weeks.

Of course, most of us can assume that they haven't volunteered to survive on a diet of rice and beans and eat kangaroo balls for free.

And enrolling celebrities to participate in Bushtucker trials doesn't come cheap.

The highest paid contestant this year is raking in a whopping £500,000, and a TV insider has revealed to The Mirror that ITV bosses have splashed out as much a 1.4 million on fees this year.

Here's a look at what it is thought that the jungle entrants are being paid, and some are earning up to ten times less than their co-stars.

70-year-old Caitlyn Jenner is unsurprisingly the highest paid 2019 camp mate, with a reported £500,000 fee.

If it's true then it would make her one of the highest earners in the show ever, with Noel Edmonds rumoured to have been paid between £500,000 and £600,000 for his appearance last year.

Ant and Dec referred to the rumours in the opening show.

Introducing the show, Dec said: "We've got reality TV royalty Caitlyn Jenner. Most people come into the jungle to lose a few pounds," with Ant adding: "But she's going to be gaining 500,000 of them according to the papers."

Dec then responded, telling Ant, "Don't believe everything you read in the papers, Ant. You know that," suggesting that Caitlyn might not be earning quite so much.

Ex-Arsenal player and football legend Ian Wright is the next best paid star, who has been flown Down Under for a reported fee of £400,000 followed by ex-Girls Aloud singer Nadine Coyle, who will apparently be paid £250,000.

Andy Whyment, who is known for playing factory worker Kirk on Coronation Street, is reportedly the only other campmate to be taking a six-figure pay cheque home too after negotiating a fee of £100,000.

Ex-Eastenders actor Jacqueline Jossa is said to be receiving £75,000 for her jungle stint, alongside Good Morning Britain host Kate Garraway, reports Leeds Live .

Meanwhile, rugby player James Haskell and Radio One DJ Adele Roberts trail slightly behind with their rumoured £50,000 fees.

According to reports, the two celebs with the lowest pay cheques are two of the youngest in the group.

Spandau Ballet bassist Martin Kemp son, Roman Kemp - Capital FM DJ - is thought to be earning £40,000 for his jungle adventure.

Last up is X Factor star Myles Stephenson who won the singing competition back in 2017 while being in British boy band Rak-Su.

He is expected to take home £25,000 for his jungle debut.

It's not known what Irish comedian Andrew Maxwell will be paid.

And apparently, even if they decide that the jungle life isn't for them, they still get paid their full paycheck - well, if history is anything to go by.

When The Only Way Is Essex's Gemma Collins dropped out of the jungle in 2014's series after just three days, she reportedly got paid her full fee anyway.

I'm A Celebrity representatives have declined to comment.
