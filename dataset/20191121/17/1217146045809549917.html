Google Adds $1.5 Million Top Reward to Android Bug Bounty Program

21 Nov 2019, 17:38 by Lawrence Abrams

Google is expanding the Android bug bounty program with new data exfiltration and lockscreen bypass categories as well as a $1 million reward for critical vulnerabilities targeting the Titan M chip.

Over the past 12 months, Google has paid out over $1.5 million USD in bug bounty rewards and over $4 million USD for over 1,800 reports since the program was launched in 2015.

The largest reward has gone to Guang Gong (@oldfresher) of Alpha Lab, Qihoo 360 Technology Co. Ltd for a "remote code execution exploit chain on the Pixel 3 device". Gong was awarded $161,337 fom Android Security Rewards program and addition $40,000 from Chrome Rewards for a total of $201,337.

Starting today, Google is expanding the program by offering a $1 million reward for developing a "full chain remote code execution exploit with persistence" that compromises the Titan M secure chip on Pixel devices. This is up from the previously top award of $200,000 for a critical vulnerability that compromises a Pixel's Trusted Execution Environment (TEE).

In addition to the $1 million bounty, Google is also offering an additional 50% bounty for vulnerabilities found on certain developer previews of Android, bringing the top potential reward to $1.5 million USD.

Titan M is a hardware chip introduced to Pixel phones in 2018 that Android uses to secure sensitive data and operations including the bootloader, disk encryption, passcodes, and secure transactions.

"Today, we’re expanding the program and increasing reward amounts," Google stated in their announcement. "We are introducing a top prize of $1 million for a full chain remote code execution exploit with persistence which compromises the Titan M secure element on Pixel devices. Additionally, we will be launching a specific program offering a 50% bonus for exploits found on specific developer preview versions of Android, meaning our top prize is now $1.5 million."

New data exfiltration and lockscreen bypass categories

In addition to the above reward increases, Google has also announced two new categories that researchers can pursue for vulnerability rewards.

The first category is data exfiltration vulnerabilities, which allow attackers to access data on a device and steal it by transferring it to an attacker controlled location.

The second category being added is lockscreen bypasses, which is used to access a locked device without knowing the user's passcode.

These new categories will award researchers up to $500,000 depending on the exploit category.
