All The New Movies & TV Shows On Amazon Prime This Month

21 Nov 2019, 17:33

If your streaming service of choice is Amazon Prime, then you know that there are new movies and shows added each month to both stream and rent or buy. In November 2019, the new movies and TV shows on Amazon Prime include four Amazon original series, a few films that were released in theaters this year, and a ton of James Bond movies. It's really Bond month over at Amazon Prime. As always, there’s also the option to watch sports and TV series, particularly if you're subscribed to Prime Video Channels.

There are a lot of options. The original series include the fourth season of The Man in the High Castle and the Costume Quest Christmas special. There's also a couple Amazon Original movies, including The Report (which is in theaters now) and a documentary called One Child Nation.

As for the films that were released in theaters earlier this year, there's Brittany Runs a Marathon, Bottom of the 9th, and The Souvenir.

Read on to find out a little more about some of the movies and shows hitting Amazon in November and see their exact release dates. There's a full list of everything that will be available this month at the end.

'Die Another Day' — Streaming November 1

Movieclips Classic Trailers on YouTube

First up, we have our first James Bond movie, Die Another Day, with Pierce Brosnan in the title role and co-staring Halle Berry.

'Double Jeopardy' — Streaming November 1

Movieclips Classic Trailers on YouTube

In Double Jeopardy, Ashley Judd plays a woman who is wrongly convicted of killing her husband. But she then finds out that you can't be convicted of the exact same crime twice...

'Dr. No' — Streaming November 1

Movieclips Classic Trailers on YouTube

If Sean Connery is your Bond of choice — or if you just want to start at the beginning — you'll be interested to know that the first James Bond movie, Dr. No, is available this month.

'Flashdance' — Streaming November 1

Flashdance - Trailer on YouTube

Flashdance stars Jennifer Beals as an aspiring professional dancer, who is working her way up while working in a steel mill.

'Goldfinger' — Streaming November 1

Movieclips Classic Trailers on YouTube

Goldfinger is the third James Bond movie and, again, stars Connery. This time he wears a terry cloth romper!

'On Her Majesty's Secret Service' — Streaming November 1

Movieclips Classic Trailers on YouTube

Any George Lazenby Bond fans in the house? On Her Majesty's Secret Service, the only Bond film starring the actor, is also on Amazon Prime. And this one goes where no Bond film went before: a bobsled course.

'The Ring' — Streaming November 1

UniversTrailers on YouTube

If you're looking for a horror film to watch this month, Amazon is streaming The Ring, about a cursed videotape. You might end up being scared for the next seven days after watching it.

'Save The Last Dance 2' — Streaming November 1

HipDanceJunkie on YouTube

Yep, there was a straight-to-DVD Save the Last Dance sequel. It came out in 2006 and was about the same character Julia Stiles played in the first one, but with another actor, Izabella Miko.

'The Spy Who Loved Me' — Streaming November 1

Movieclips Classic Trailers on YouTube

And now we have Roger Moore as James Bond in 1977's The Spy Who Loved Me. This was his third time as Bond out of seven films total.

'Texas Chainsaw 3D' — Streaming November 6

Lionsgate Movies on YouTube

Texas Chainsaw 3D answers the question: What happens when you inherit a home that has a murderous cannibal living in the basement?

'One Child Nation' — Streaming November 8

Amazon Studios on YouTube

This Amazon Original documentary is about China's One-Child Policy, which ended in 2015. It includes interviews with people who had various experiences with their families under the law.

'Anna And The Apocalypse' — Streaming November 13

Orion Pictures on YouTube

Anna and the Apocalypse is "a zombie Christmas musical". And that's probably about all you need to know to tell whether this is your thing or not.

'Instant Family' — Streaming November 14

Paramount Pictures on YouTube

Instant Family is a comedy starring Rose Byrne and Mark Wahlberg as a couple who adopt three children from foster care, a teenager (Isabela Merced) and her two younger siblings (Gustavo Escobar, Julianna Gamiz).

'The Souvenir' — Streaming November 14

A24 on YouTube

The Souvenir follows a college student (Honor Swinton Byrne) as she goes through a very tumultuous relationship and tries to make her first film in 1980s England.

'Creed 2' — Streaming November 15

MGM on YouTube

This Rocky franchise sequel from 2018 shows Adonis Creed (Michael B. Jordan) facing off against Viktor Drago (Florian Munteanu), the son of the man who killed his father.

'The Man In The High Castle' Season 4 — Streaming November 15

Amazon Prime Video on YouTube

The fourth and final season of The Man in the High Castle, about a parallel universe in which the Axis has won World War 2 and divided the U.S. into Japanese and German occupied sections, premieres on November 15.

'Bottom of the 9th' — Streaming November 19

Movieclips Indie on YouTube

Real life couple Sofía Vergara and Joe Manganiello star in Bottom of the 9th, a drama about a man who is released from prison after 17 years and who was set to become a professional baseball player prior to his sentencing.

'Costume Quest: Christmas Special' — Streaming November 22

Prime Video Kids on YouTube

Costume Quest is an Amazon Original series for kids about a group of children with magic costumes, who have to save the world. But this time, it's Christmas.

'The Report' — Streaming November 29

Amazon Prime Video on YouTube

Based on real events, The Report stars Adam Driver as Daniel J. Jones, the Senate staffer who put together the Senate Intelligence Committee's CIA torture report.

'Brittany Runs A Marathon' — Streaming In November (Date TBD)

Amazon Studios on YouTube

Brittany Runs a Marathon stars Jillian Bell as a woman who decides to run the New York City Marathon. But, you know, it's not just about the marathon, it's about living her best life.
