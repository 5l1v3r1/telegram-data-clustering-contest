Joker director Todd Phillips responds to sequel reports

21 Nov 2019, 17:58 by Ciara Walsh

Rumours of a Joker sequel have been circulating since the film's release. 

Joker director Todd Phillips has addressed the confusing and conflicting reports of a sequel.

Yesterday, The Hollywood Reporter and Deadline published different pieces regarding the future of Joaquin Phoenix's Joker, following the movie's success at the box office.

The Hollywood Reporter said that a Joker sequel was in the works with Phillips potentially directing again, however Deadline later stated that the meeting between Phillips and Warner Bros about the DC movie universe never happened.

The Playlist asked Phillips to clarify the reports - and it's not looking like there are any plans set in stone for the time being.

"That was written about today, and I have to be honest, it came out of nowhere," Phillips said, discussing The Hollywood Reporter's story. "It referred to a meeting that was never had.

"I thought it was anticipatory at best. Obviously, sequels have been discussed when a movie that cost $60 million made $1 billion, but we have not had any serious conversations about it." 

Joker has been setting box office records since its release and has made more than $1 billion worldwide.

Set in 1981, the film also stars Robert De Niro, Zazie Beetz and Frances Conroy.

Joker is now playing in cinemas.
