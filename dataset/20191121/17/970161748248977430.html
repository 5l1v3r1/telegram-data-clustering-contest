Stephen Fry reveals 'sweet excitement' after being cast Doctor Who role

21 Nov 2019, 17:16

Stephen Fry has expressed his excitement after being cast for a guest role in Doctor Who – alongside Lenny Henry.

Jodie Whittaker’s Time Lord will be travelling the universe once again and in series 12 she will meet the pair along the way.

And while both of their characters are being kept a secret for now – we are yet to discover whether they’ll be a friend or foe

Stephen has exclaimed how happy he is to be cast the role describing it as a ‘very sweet second-place excitement’.

He explained: ‘Short of being picked for a British space exploration programme and I readily concede that I’m past the age where I’d be considered (if I was ever the right age for such a posting) – then being in an episode of Doctor Who will certainly do as a very sweet second-place excitement.’

Sir Lenny, equally excited, added: ‘It was absolutely brilliant to be welcomed into the fantastical world of Doctor Who. The nearest I have been to the TARDIS was when I played the Caribbean Doctor in the Lenny Henry Show , so as a life-long (hiding behind the sofa type) Doctor Who fan this is a very special moment for me.’

Showrunner Chris Chibnall said he is buzzing to be working with ‘two of my absolute favourite actors’.

‘Doctor Who is coming back with a bang – with two great British icons in major roles,’ he said.

‘One of the great joys of Doctor Who is getting to work with actors from your wish list. Stephen and Lenny are two of my absolute favourite actors – and to be able to bring them into Doctor Who, in our one of our biggest ever stories to kick off the new series, is an absolute thrill.’

Series 12, which has just finished filming, will reunite Chris with Lenny, who appeared in series three of his crime drama Broadchurch.

The news comes after reports that Doctor Who is set to kick off with a New Year’s Day special in place of a Christmas episode for the second year running.

Doctor Who returns to BBC One in 2020.
