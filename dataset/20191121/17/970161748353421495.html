I'm A Celebrity's top earners revealed as Scarlett Moffatt tops the list

21 Nov 2019, 17:57

I’m A Celebrity…Get Me Out Of Here is in full swing and over the years we have seen a host of famous faces battle it out down under to become King or Queen of the jungle – but who has made the most money from the show?

An odds insights platform, which gives analysis on the latest odds on sports, TV and Celebrity news, has compiled a list of the I’m A Celebrity…Get Me Out Of Here contestants who have gone on to earn the most after leaving the show.

Googlebox star Scarlett Moffatt has topped the list as her stint in the jungle gave her the biggest boost to her career with a net worth increase of 1567%, followed by Emily Atack with a 710% and Georgia Toffolo with a 567% increase.

Scarlett, who won the show in 2016, has an estimated net worth of £1 million, after landing multiple TV presenting gigs and brand partnership deals as a result of winning the show.

Despite recent events that have seen her career take a nosedive, over the last three years Scarlett’s enjoyed presenting stints on Ant & Dec’s Saturday Night Takeaway, Beauty School Cop Outs and the widely-criticised Channel 4’s The British Tribe Next Door.

Inbetweeners actress Emily Atack has seen her net worth increase by 710% to £6 million after she came second to Harry Redknapp in 2018. Emily is currently hosting this years’ I’m A Celebrity: Extra Camp and has presented other ITV shows including This Morning.

Made in Chelsea’s Georgia Toffolo takes the third spot in the list with a net worth of £800,000 – an increase of 567%. While Georgia is from a wealthy family, her own personal net worth before appearing on the show wasn’t a substantial amount.

Other famous faces who made the list include the likes of Myleene Klass, who has been a presenter on The One Show, Lorraine and a panellist on Loose Women and Mark Wright, who has been a presenter on Take Me Out, Surprise Surprise and he even has had his own TV show Mark Wright’s Hollywood Nights.

And not forgetting Peter Andre, Katie Price, Vicky Pattison and Kerry Katona.

I’m a Celeb’s nineteenth series is heating up already and we have seen clashes between Adele Roberts and Roman Kemp and Ian Wright and Andrew Maxwell – and there’s even more to come.

Spencer James, founder of Whataretheodds.co.uk explained: ‘Since the first season in 2002, I’m a Celeb has continued to increase in popularity, becoming one of the most watched British TV shows of all time. There just seems to be something about watching celebrities in discomfort that captivates the nation.

‘So, why do these celebs put themselves in this position for three weeks? Our research shows just how much of a positive impact appearing on the show has on a celeb’s career, even if, as in Scarlett’s case, it ends up being short-lived! Some contestants have gone on to do absolutely nothing since the show while others have completely transformed flagging careers. It will be interesting to see how the contestants fair after their stint in the jungle this year.’
