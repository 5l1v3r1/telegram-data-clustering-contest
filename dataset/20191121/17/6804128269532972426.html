Diego Simeone replaces Unai Emery & 9 trophies - Football Manager predicts Arsenal's next decade

We used the hugely popular football management simulation game Football Manager 2020 to predict how the next decade could look for Arsenal

21 Nov 2019, 17:45 by Nathan Bliss

Arsenal have had incredible stability over the last decade, with is unique in modern football, with stable league finishes and impressive financial results.

They've had just two managers, Arsene Wenger and Unai Emery, and have finished every season in the top six places in the Premier League, finishing in the top four seven times since the 2009/20 season.

But the Gunners haven't actually made the top four since the 2015/16 season, and don't look like doing so again this season under Emery, who is coming under increasing pressure after some disappointing performances.

The question is, how will Arsenal perform over the next decade?

What will the first XI look like in 2030? How long will Emery remain as manager?

And, how many trophies will they have won?

We used Football Manager 2020 to take a look at how the next decade could pan out for Arsenal.

Arsenal in 2019/20

Arsenal transfers made in 2019/20

Arsenal aren't predicted to do much business in January, with youngster Mattia Novella the only signing.

Arsenal best eleven in 2019/20

This is how Emery's best eleven is predicted to look in the 2019/20 season, with Pierre-Emerick Aubameyang both the highest rated player and the top scorer, with 30 goals.

2019/20 Competition results

Arsenal are predicted to a good season domestically and finish third in the Premier League table. Bear in mind that when we started this simulation, we couldn't input the results that have already happened!

Emery's side are also predicted to win the Europa League in 2019/20, beating Italian side Roma on penalties in the final, with Hector Bellerin scoring the winning spot kick.

All in all, a very good season.

Arsenal in 2020/21

Arsenal transfers made in 2020/21

Emery spent a whopping £216 million in the summer of 2020, with Milan Skriniar, Houssem Aouar, Chiquinho, Luiz Felipe and Callum McGregor among those arriving.

Henrikh Mkhitaryan secured his move to Roma, with Shkrodran Mustafi leaving for Fiorentina, Sead Kolasinac moving to China and Calum Chambers going to Wolves, as Arsenal recouped £80 million.

Arsenal best eleven and stats in 2020/21

Young talents Reiss Nelson and Bukayo Saka forced their way into Emery's best eleven, as did new signings Luiz Felipe, Skriniar and Aouar.

Keiran Tierney was the club's highest rated player, with Aubameyang again top scorer with 25 goals in all competitions.

2020/21 Competition results

These performances led to a fourth place Premier League finish in 2020/21, but 13 points behind champions Man City.

In the Champions League, Arsenal reached the quarter-final stage, eventually losing to Antonio Conte's Inter Milan on away goals.

Arsenal in 2021/22

Arsenal transfers made in 2021/22

Emery was allowed to splash the cash once again in the summer of 2021, signing the likes of Ferland Mendy, Youri Tielemans and Ryan Fraser for £153 million.

Grant Xhaka, Mesut Ozil, David Luiz, Rob Holding, Emiliano Martinez and Fabricio Bustos all left the club, with Arsenal recouping just £39 million.

Arsenal best eleven and stats in 2021/22

Despite Mendy's arrival, Tierney retained his place, with Aouar being pushed up into a number ten position, with Saka and Nelson on the flanks.

Alexandre Lacazette had a great season, scoring 24 goals in 47 appearances, although Aubameyang did miss a lot of the season with injury.

French midfielder Aouar scored 18 goals in his first season and was the club's highest rated player.

2021/22 Competition results

But it was a poor Premier League season for Emery and Arsenal, as the Gunners finished 5th in the league with 68 points, just one point off a top four spot.

But, Arsenal won the two domestic cup competitions, beating Man City in the Carabao Cup Final.

And beating bitter rivals Spurs in the FA Cup Final!

Those two trophy wins meant Emery kept his job for the following year...

Arsenal in 2022/23

Arsenal transfers made in 2022/23

Another £130 million was spent in the summer of 2022, with Ben Godfrey, Ibe Pieters, Enzo Millot, Angus Gunn and Adam Garratt all being brought in.

Aubameyang, now 32 years old and losing some of his pace, was sold for a meagre £10.5 million, with Joe Willock being sold for decent money, Xhaka, McGregor and Elneny also left on loan.

Arsenal best eleven and stats in 2022/23

It was a familar front four, with Torreira and Tielemans forming a great midfield partnership.

William Saliba returned to the club and made a first team spot his own, partnering Skriniar, who was the club's highest rated player.

Midfielder Aouar was the club's top scorer with 18 goals, with Lacazette and Nketiah finishing on 16 goals each.

2022/23 Competition results

It was a great season for Arsenal, as Unai Emery's side won the Premier League title ahead of Man City, on goal difference!

The Gunners also won the Europa League, with Saka and Pepe scoring in the final against Norwich City, of all opponents!

A fantastic season for Arsenal.

Arsenal in 2023/24

Arsenal transfers made in 2023/24

Fresh from their title success, Emery spent £128 million on four young talents, Edson Alvarez, Galeno, Mike Maignan and Amadou Haidara.

But, the club did lose Nelson to Man City for £47.5 million, also selling Chiquino, McGregor, Xhaka and Jed Steer, recouping a total of £108m.

Arsenal best eleven and stats in 2023/24

Pepe replaced Nelson in the best eleven, scoring 14 goals in 41 appearances, with Nketiah also replacing Lacazette, finishing as the club's top scorer with 23 goals in 49 appearances.

Mike Maignan replaced Bernd Leno in goal for the first time, with the youngster making 55 appearances in all competitions.

2023/24 Competition results

But, Arsenal couldn't replicate their title winning form from the previous year, finishing in 5th in the Premier League.

Emery's side did get to the Champions League semi-final, but were knocked out by Napoli on away goals.

A poor season overall.

Arsenal in 2024/25

Arsenal transfers made in 2024/25

Emery spent his lowest total yet in the summer of 2024, just £77 million, on Jamal Lewis, Luiz Ricardo, Erik Coronel and Kasper Schmeichel on a free transfer.

Galeno, Angus Gunn, Tyreece John-Jules and a now 33-year-old Lacazette, were all sold.

Arsenal best eleven and stats in 2024/25

New signing Luiz Ricardo played his way into the best eleven, pushing Aouar back into a deeper role alongside Tielemans.

Nketiah was now the club's number one striker and was the club's top scorer, with 21 goals in 36 appearances.

Aouar was the highest rated player, with a rating of 7.31.

2024/25 Competition results

But, to say Arsenal had a shocker in 2024/25 would be an understatement.

The Gunners finished in 7th place in the Premier League, below West Ham, Chelsea and Tottenham.

Arsenal also faced disapointment in the cup competitions, losing to Liverpool in the Carabao Cup Final.

And, going out of the Europa League at the semi-final stage on penalties.

This poor season resulted in Unai Emery being sacked as Arsenal manager at the end of May, with the club now looking for their second manager this decade.

Arsenal in 2025/26

Arsenal's new manager?

Former Atletico Madrid manager Diego Simeone.

The Simeone era was here.

Arsenal transfers made in 2025/26

But, the Argentine didn't go mad in the tranfer market, actually making a profit overall.

Striker Tammy Abraham was brought in from Chelsea, joining Sergi Roberto, Sebastian Jurado, Jesus Pinero, Agustin Benitez, Roman Acre and Moses Dladla as new signings.

The Gunners made £89 million selling Emile Smith-Rowe, Ferland Mendy, Mike Maignon, Gabriel Martinelli and Bernd Leno.

Arsenal best eleven and stats in 2025/26

Simeone kept with the 4-2-3-1 formation, with roughly the same lineup.

Nketiah had another great season, scoring 25 goals in 45 appearance and was also the highest rated player.

2025/26 Competition results

In the Argentine's first season at the club, Arsenal were Premier League champions once again, finishing three points ahead of Man City.

Talk about having an instant impact.

They also nearly won the Europa League, but were beaten in the final by RB Leipzig.

Arsenal in 2026/27

Arsenal transfers made in 2026/27

Simeone went big in the summer of 2026, spending £136 million on Alex Meret, Achraf Hakimi, Sacha Smits, Bodi Tielemans and more.

But, Simeone did suffer a blow when Nketiah was sold to Real Madrid for £75 million.

Arsenal best eleven and stats in 2026/27

Despite bringing in Abraham, Simeone made Matteo Guendouzi a false nine (yes, really) to replace Nketiah, and the Frenchman repaid him with a 17 goal haul, getting the highest rating out of anyone that season.

A masterstroke.

2026/27 Competition results

Arsenal couldn't replicate their league win this season, securing fourth place, with Liverpool having a phenomenal season.

But, Auour did break a club record for most assists in a season.

And Arsenal only went and won the biggest club trophy in the world too, beating Liverpool in the Champions League Final, with Nicolas Pepe and Abraham scoring.

Arsenal in 2027/28

In the summer of 2027, Inter Milan approached Simeone about becoming manager, and the Argentine left Arsenal for Italy in July, but not before securing one last big signing.

Arsenal transfers made in 2027/28

That signing was wonderkid Tony Unsworth, who cost £68 million from Everton.

Abraham was allowed to go out on loan to Monaco.

In late July, Arsenal announced their new manager as former Liverpool boss Jurgen Klopp!

A hugely popular appointment.

Arsenal best eleven and stats in 2027/28

Klopp didn't deviate from the 4-2-3-1, starting Unsworth up front, who scored 22 goals in his debut season, and the rest of the team looking very familar.

Alex Meret had now made the goalkeeping position his own.

2027/28 Competition results

Klopp had a good first season, finishing second in the Premier League and winning a trophy.

Arsenal won the FA Cup thanks to a 1-0 win over Cardiff at Wembley, with Lucas Torreira scoring a late winner.

Arsenal in 2028/29

Arsenal transfers made in 2028/29

£100 million was spent by Klopp in the summer of 2028, with the German bringing in defensive midfielder Nohan Kenneh, midfielder Jose Andres, Brazilian defender Ederson and young goalkeeper Pasquale Bono.

Augustin Benitez, Lewis, Bellerin and Bodi Tielemans were all sold, meaning Arsenal actually made a £4 million profit.

Arsenal best eleven and stats in 2028/29

Jose Andres replaced Pepe in the best eleven, with Ibe Pieters breaking into the first team and Luiz Ricardo playing a bit deeper.

Unsworth had another stormer of a season, scoring 24 in 47 appearances and earning the player of the year award.

2028/29 Competition results

But, Arsenal were poor in Klopp's second season, finishing 5th in the league, with just 71 points.

Klopp did win the European Super Cup for the Gunners, about a month after his appointment, but also lost an FA Cup Final to Wolves.

Arsenal in 2029/30

Arsenal transfers made in 2029/30

Klopp was retained as manager and given £90 to spend in the summer of 2029, with right-back Tomas Perdigao his biggest signing for £55 million from Sporting CP.

Arsenal best eleven and stats in 2029/30

Unsworth didn't have a great season, scoring just 10 goals, and youngster Cesar Vinicio was named in the best eleven in his place.

Number ten Luiz Ricardo was the club's best player, with new signing Perdigao made the right-back spot his own.

2029/30 Competition results

But, Klopp's third season did little to excite. A 3rd place league finish was good, but his side were poor in the cup competitions.

In fact, the only cup Arsenal won 2029/20 was Matteo Guendouzi's testimonial!

Klopp resigned at the end of the season, with Arsenal replacing Klopp with former Man City manager Pep Guardiola for 2030!
