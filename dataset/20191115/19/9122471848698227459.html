Minnesota Vikings Announce Adam Thielen’s Status For Sunday’s Game

15 Nov 2019, 19:02 by Dan Lyons

The Minnesota Vikings host the Denver Broncos this weekend. The team is in the middle of a playoff race, and will once again be without star wide receiver Adam Thielen on Sunday.

The Vikings sit at 7-3 and have looked very strong, winning five of their last six games. The sole loss came to the Kansas City Chiefs.

With the first place Green Bay Packers on bye, Minnesota can get within a half-game of Aaron Rodgers and company with a win. The Denver Broncos are one of the worst teams in the AFC, so this is a prime opportunity for Minnesota.

Thielen is a big absence, though. He’s been dealing with a nagging hamstring injury for a few weeks now. He did play in the Chiefs game, but left early after aggravating it.

Thielen initially missed the Week 8 game against Washington, as well as last weekend’s win over the Dallas Cowboys. He hasn’t recorded a catch since Week 7, when he had one reception for a 25 yard touchdown against the Detroit Lions.

Now, according to Adam Schefter, Adam Thielen is out yet again tomorrow.
