Port Vale hoping 'decent' relationship with Nottingham Forest will help them keep youngster

Young Reds midfielder Jake Taylor has been a regular for Vale since making the short-term loan move along the A50

15 Nov 2019, 19:44 by Barry Cooper

Port Vale are hoping to extend Jake Taylor’s loan deal from Nottingham Forest, after the midfielder’s impressive start to life at Vale Park.

The Forest youngster has caught the eye in the Potteries, scoring four times in 15 games including the winner against Crewe last month, with the League Two outfit hopeful of keeping him.

Valiants boss John Askey hopes the good relationship the club have with Forest will reflect well on their chances of securing a deal for the 21-year-old beyond the current one which runs until January.

Askey has also revealed Vale owners Carol and Kevin Shanahan arranged a car for Taylor to use, after his was broken into during the week, further evidence, Askey believes, that Vale are doing right by their players and making them feel valued.

"I know there will be one or two clubs looking but we have a decent relationship with Gary Brazil who played here who looks after Jake at Forest, so they can see he is being looked after,” he told Stoke-on-Trent Live.

"That's where Carol has come in this week.

"Jake had his car broken into and Carol fixed him up with a car within two hours of it happening. Things like that can go a long way.

"So, where a lot of football clubs would just say 'get on with it', we are seeing that we are taking care of players now.

"It means a lot when you are at work and people care about you, you go out of your way to do that little bit extra."
