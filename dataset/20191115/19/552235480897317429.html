Manchester United line up £200m double January transfer move for Jadon Sancho and Erling Haaland

The Red Devils have placed Borussia Dortmund winger Jadon Sancho and RB Salzburg's high rated hit man Erling-Braut Haaland high on their January transfer window wish list.

15 Nov 2019, 19:40 by Adam Thomas

Manchester United are weighing up a big January transfer swoop for highly rated youngsters Jadon Sancho and Erling-Braut Haaland.

Ole Gunnar Solskjaer has identified the teenage duo as his top priorities in the upcoming transfer window, as United look to get their business done earl doors when the window re-opens.

Both are likely to cost the Old Trafford outfit around £200m in total, with Borussia Dortmund valuing England international Sancho at £100m, while RB Salzburg striker Haaland is rated as an £85m purchase by his club.

Sancho has made 21 appearances for club and country this season, finding the back of the net six times, while Haaland has scored 22 goals in 18 appearances, scoring a hat-trick for Austrian outfit Salzburg against Wolfsberger at the weekend.

United tried to sign Sancho last summer, but their failure to make the Champions League scuppered any potential deal, with the former Manchester City star singing a new contract with Dortmund.

With Haaland, United hope his relationship with Solskjaer, who managed him at Molde, can give them a head start over their rivals in the race to sign Europe's most prolific young striker.

Haaland is the son of former Manchester City and Leeds midfielder Alf-Inge, and United have had Haaland watched several times this season, with Solskjaer sending Simon Wells, one of his most senior and trusted scouts, to assess the youngster in recent weeks.

Haaland's exploits have made him one of the most sought-after young players in the world, with one hurdle for United being his apparent favouring of a move to Barcelona and Real Madrid .

 

Both Spanish clubs are also tracking Haaland, but United will not be put off by interest from their more illustrious rivals and their ability to guarantee Champions League football.
