Man Utd consider Erling Haaland loan back to RB Salzburg if January transfer completed

The teenage scoring sensation has attracted interest from a number of top European clubs but United have sent their top scout to keep tabs on him as they weigh up an offer

15 Nov 2019, 19:41 by Darren Wells, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/darren-wells/

Man Utd are open to loaning Erling Haaland back to Red Bull Salzburg providing they can get a deal done for the striker in January.

The Red Devils are hot on the heels of the 19-year-old after his impressive start to the season which has seen him net 26 goals in 18 appearances.

Haaland has made no secret of his desire to play in the Premier League, and is attracting interest from elsewhere, including Liverpool , Bayern Munich and Juventus .

United boss Ole Gunnar Solskjaer has sent his most trusted scout, Steve Wells, to report back on the youngster, as they consider a move to sign him.

Any transfer was likely to go ahead next summer once the current season has ended, however United are hoping to steal a march on their rivals by finalising a deal in the next transfer window.

They are considering allowing Haaland to then return to Salzburg until the end of the season, before making his move to Old Trafford permanent.

Haaland's form has attracted a number of clubs keen on acquiring his services, after hitting five hat-tricks already this campaign.

He struck nine goals in one match at the summers under-20 World Cup, and has also been on the scoresheet at Anfield this season.

The son of former Man City and Leeds midfielder Alfe-Inge Haaland, he is represented by super agent Mino Raiola, who knows the United hierarchy well from his dealings with Paul Pogba.

However despite interest from Europe's elite, Haaland has been warned off of making a move this soon, by Alf Ingve Berntsen, who worked with him in the early stages of his career at Bryne FK.

Speaking of the interest from other clubs, Berntsen told Goal and SPOX : "The tactical requirements at Bayern and Liverpool would be a bit too high for now.

"After all, he has only played in Norway and Austria so far. And also there for a short time.

“Joining Bayern would be difficult for him at the moment. Because, of course, he still some way to go before he reaches the level of Robert Lewandowski.

“Erling is a dominant character, sitting on the bench would not be easy for him."

He added: "What many forget: Haaland was not even a regular player at Salzburg recently," he added. "He did not play regularly until August this year.

"Of course he caused hype with his goals, but it would probably be advisable to stay at Salzburg for another year.

“The most important thing for Erling is to play regularly at the highest possible level. It would be bad for him not to play at his new club.

"He needs playing time at the highest level in order to evolve. To be a substitute at a top club would be a hindrance to this development.”
