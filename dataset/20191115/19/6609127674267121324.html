BLAZE DRAMA

Fire breaks out in Glasgow block of flats as emergency services swoop with residents treated for smoke inhalation

15 Nov 2019, 19:22 by Ewan Mowat

A FIRE has broken out in a block of flats in Glasgow with several residents needing ambulance treatment.

Emergency services raced to Leadburn Road in Barmulloch around 5.10pm.

Four fire crews battled the blaze.

Meanwhile a number of locals were treated by paramedics for smoke inhalation.

The fire has reportedly been extinguished.

But emergency services are still on the scene two hours later.

A spokesperson for the Scottish Fire and Rescue Service said: "We were called at 5.08pm to reports of a fire in a residence on Leadburn Road, Glasgow.

"Operations control mobilised four appliances - three pumps and an aerial ladder.

"Two appliances are still on the scene."

A spokesperson for Police Scotland said: "We received report of a fire at an address on Leadburn Road in Glasgow around 5.15pm.

"Emergency services at the scene. Ambulance treating several residents for smoke inhalation."
