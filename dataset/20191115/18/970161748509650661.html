The Mandalorian episode 2 finally reveals significance of mind-blowing twist

15 Nov 2019, 18:33

The Mandalorian has finally revealed the significance of that mind-blowing twist at the end of its first episode.

The series is the first live-action TV series set in the Star Wars universe, tracking the life of the titular bounty hunter (played by Game of Thrones’ Pedro Pascal) as he journeys throughout a galaxy in chaos.

We were already told that the serial – which is set after the events of Return of the Jedi but before The Force Awakens – would feature a ‘major’ Star Wars spoiler in its first episode, and it didn’t disappoint.

During the closing moments of the first installment of the space opera, we were treated to a brief glimpse of what appeared to be…Baby Yoda!

Now, the second episode of the series expands on this tantalising twist – and reveals why this significant moment is tied in with creator Jon Favareau’s classification of the show as a ‘space-western.’

According to io9, the new ep will be titled The Child and see our favourite helmeted bounty-hunter (sorry Boba Fett!) take on the responsibility for looking after this mysterious child and try to take the child to safety as they escape a hostile planet.

The theme of a lone gunman finding a helpless soul to care after (and thereby redeem himself for past wrongs) is a common trope in the Western genre, which The Mandalorian emulates just with a lot more aliens.

A few famous westerns the show could be referencing include True Grit and Unforgiven.

So, Baby Yoda (we’re calling it Baby Yoda because that seems like the general consensus right now – Yoda of course died before the events of the show) could well help set The Mandalorian on the path to redemption.

But what does it all mean? Is he the actual reincarnation of the Jedi Master? Another member of his species? Or a weapon to fight the emerging First Order?

If this isn’t enough Star Wars content for you – we still have Ewan McGregor’s return as Obi-Wan Kenobi to look forward to, as well as the release of Rise of Skywalker, which apparently will still leave fans with ‘questions.’
