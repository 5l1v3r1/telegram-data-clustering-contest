Here’s Everything We Know About Camila Cabello’s Upcoming Album ‘Romance’

15 Nov 2019, 18:08 by Caitlin Kelley

Camila Cabello will return with her sophomore album, Romance, this holiday season. The love-themed LP has already gotten an extensive rollout, as she’s already released six tracks, including her hit, “Señorita.” This is her first album since last year’s self-titled debut.

Genius rounded up everything we know about the project ahead of its release.

The Release Date

In an Instagram post, Cabello announced the album would arrive on Dec. 6.

The Features

So far, we know her No. 1 hit with Shawn Mendes, “Señorita,” will make the cut.

The Producers

Her producers include benny blanco, Cashmere Cat, watt, Louis Bell, Carter Lang, Westen Weiss, and Frank Dunes.

The Songwriters

Some big names appear in the liner notes, including Charli XCX, Ed Sheeran, and FINNEAS.

The Promotion

She spoke about dropping “Liar” and “Shameless.” at once in an interview with the Zach Sang Show. “I think it’s too much pressure when I haven’t released an album in a year and a half to just put out one song,” the singer said. “That’s a bit much for me. I don’t like it… I’m not really like a drop one single kinda gal. I feel like I want to put out more music, more visuals, more stuff because I feel like then it makes you appreciate the one song more because you have context for it.”

The Recording Process

Cabello posted about how the album is a reflection of her experience with falling in love. “I just wanted this album to sound like what falling in love feels like, pretty impossible thing to do but I can say I gave it all I had,” she wrote. “I’ve never lived as much life as I did writing this album. It was messy, and beautiful, unforgettable and at times so painful I wish I could forget, it was excruciatingly consuming and impossible not to get lost in.”

She elaborated on how the album captures her “essence” at this moment. “There was a time when I was listening to these songs that I had to turn the album off,” she told Elle. “I had to let go emotionally—I sat there and cried so much. I feel an undeniable truth that the album has captured the essence of me at this point in my life. I always think, “Could I be more honest? Could I be more open?” I feel like I’ve done that even better than on my first album.”

The Songs

According to an Apple Music preview, the LP contains 14 tracks. She’s already dropped six songs: “Shameless,” “Señorita,” “Liar,” “Easy,” “Cry for Me,” and “Living Proof.”
