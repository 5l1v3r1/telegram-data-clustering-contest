Browns Fans Are Furious With NFL’s Decision To Not Suspend Mason Rudolph

15 Nov 2019, 18:05 by Andrew McCarty

On Friday morning, the NFL announced suspensions for players involved in a fight during the final seconds of Thursday night’s game between the Cleveland Browns and Pittsburgh Steelers.

Myles Garrett received an indefinite suspension – for at least the rest of the 2019 season, including playoffs. The suspension comes as a result of Garrett using Mason Rudolph’s helmet as a weapon against the Steelers quarterback.

The NFL did not suspend Rudolph – although the league left further punishment open.

Most NFL analysts and former players agreed Garrett deserved a lengthy suspension. However, most also agreed Rudolph deserved to be punished for his part in the scuffle.

Browns fans were not happy with the league’s decision regarding the lack of punishment for the Steelers quarterback.
