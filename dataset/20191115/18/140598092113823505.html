Greggs boss skips his sausage rolls and steak bakes in attempt to 'go vegan'

15 Nov 2019, 18:00 by Patrick Sawer

He is known as the nation’s most famous purveyor of sausage rolls, but now the boss of Greggs has turned his back on one of his firm’s best selling products.

Roger Whiteside, the chief executive of the Newcastle-based bakery chain, has declared he is trying to go vegan.

Which means he will no longer be able to savour the delights of Greggs’ sausage rolls or bacon and cheese wraps, never mind its legendary steak bake, a pastry parcel packed with “diced cuts of prime beef in a rich and tasty gravy”.

Mr Whiteside said he his attempting to make the switch and cut out animal produce from his own diet, at least for a while, after watching a Netflix documentary called The Gamechangers.

Speaking after being crowned this year's North East Business Executive of the Year he said: “If you'd asked me three weeks ago which roll I preferred I would have said the original sausage roll, but I'm now attempting to live on a vegan diet.

"Obviously there are arguments based around animal welfare and the environment, but this was all about the health benefits, and I thought I should give it a go.

"Avoiding meat is easy. The problem is avoiding dairy, avoiding milk and cheese is almost impossible. It would be okay if I had time to cook, but I don't, so I have to have things that are quick and easy."

Greggs public relations department, keen to avoid a Ratner-style PR disaster - in which the businessman Gerald Ratner in 1991 described the products his jewellery firm sold as “total crap” - was swift to point out that their stores provided many tasty and nutritious vegan alternatives for their boss.

These include an award-winning Mexican bean and sweet potato wrap, fruit pots, and of course their now famous vegan sausage roll, launched in January to the apoplectic fury of a number of commentators, including Good Morning Britain presenter Piers Morgan.

Which means Mr Whiteside need not go hungry when he finds himself passing one of his 2,000 branches.

"We've got the award-winning vegan wrap which I have quite frequently and the vegan sausage roll which I'll have about once a week, and now we have the sweet choice with the vegan sweet mince pie,” he said.
