Sex worker group says gardaí previously knew about criminal gang

15 Nov 2019, 06:47

A sex worker representative group say gardaí have known about a gang targeting workers for some time.

Gardaí say seven targeted attacks have taken place on sex workers, mainly in the in the Dublin area who advertise services online since mid-October.

Sex workers have been attacked and robbed by a number of men after a fake meeting was organised online.

Sex Workers Alliance Ireland (SWAI) say both sex workers and gardaí have known about the criminals involved for some time, but sex workers refuse to contact the garda due to lack of trust.

Ireland adopted the “Nordic Model” on March 27 2017, criminalising the purchase of sex, not the selling of sex, which the Government says is aimed at tackling trafficking and protecting vulnerable people in prostitution.

Sex worker organisations have railed against the change since it was implemented, saying the new laws make workers more vulnerable.
