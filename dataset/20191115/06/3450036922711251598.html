Abuse trial of Bloomfield mom delayed one more time

15 Nov 2019, 06:31 by KTVO News Desk

BLOOMFIELD, Iowa — The trial of a Bloomfield, Iowa, mother accused of being complicit in the sexual abuse of her daughter has been delayed again.

Kendra Hoover will now face a Davis County jury in February.

Hoover is charged with 14 counts of child endangerment and neglect.

This is the second time in less than a month the trial has been pushed back.

In March 2018, Hoover’s live-in boyfriend, Steven Crook, was arrested for videotaping the rape of her daughter. Hoover is accused of allowing her child to be around Crook, even after she saw the abuse on tape.

Crook pleaded guilty to federal charges, and is currently serving a 120-year prison sentence.

Hoover is currently free on bond as she awaits trial.
