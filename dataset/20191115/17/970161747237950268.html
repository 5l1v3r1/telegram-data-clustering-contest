Mum left kids, 3, 5, and 7, locked in room with bucket and spent night with man

15 Nov 2019, 17:12

A mum left her three young children locked in a bedroom on her son’s birthday, so she could spend the night at a hotel with her partner, a court heard.

The children – aged three, five and seven – were locked in the room with bucket, a roll of toilet paper and a plastic bag filled with snacks on the evening of September 15, 2017.

They were also unable to leave the room, at the home in Blackburn, Lancashire, as the door handle had been removed, Preston Crown Court was told.

Judge Philip Barry said the only source of light in the bedroom was a lamp with exposed wires, plugged into a damaged socket, causing a ‘clear fire hazard’.

When officers were alerted to the situation and forced their way into the house, the five-year-old little girl told them: ‘Mummy has left us alone again’.

Prosecutors said the alarm was raised by the children’s father, who is separated from their mother, after he became concerned she had left them alone.

The 32-year-old mum, who cannot be named for legal reasons, returned the next morning and was arrested at around 8am.

She pleaded guilty to three offences of child neglect on November 14 and was given an 18-month prison sentence, suspended for two years.

Prosecutors said police found text messages on her phone that made it clear she had booked a room at a Holiday Inn Express, in Burnley, with a man she referred to as ‘babe’.

She claimed she had asked her sister to look after the children but dumped them at home when the arrangements fell through.

When officers arrived on the scene they could see a light on in a back bedroom so they broke in through the front door.

Charles Brown, prosecuting, said the police could not immediately open the bedroom door and the youngsters could be heard crying inside.

Their father, who was with the officers, asked them to stand clear of the door so police could break it down, the court heard.

The children then spent the night with their father, but they are now cared for by the mother’s aunt and uncle, the court was told.

Kelly Cyples, defending, said her client had been involved in an abusive relationship with the children’s father, which she had ended.

But because she had married him against her family’s wishes, she was effectively isolated and had struggled with bringing the children up, she claimed.

The mother must also complete 150 hours of community service and undertake 30 rehabilitation activity days.
