'Shifted' - the numbers that reveal Nottingham Forest fans are on the march

The Reds are fifth in the Championship and right in the thick of the Premier League promotion race

15 Nov 2019, 17:00 by Barry Cooper

Demand continues to soar among Nottingham Forest supporters keen to watch their side’s promotion push.

Forest fans have already sold-out their allocation for next Saturday’s visit to fellow top-six hopefuls Bristol City, and now all 1,241 tickets have been shifted for the reunion with former manager Mark Warburton at QPR on November 27.

After selling their allocation of tickets in the upper tier of the School End at the Kiyan Prince Foundation Stadium, another 1,000, this time in the lower tier, have also been given to Forest, with those expected to go in advance of the midweek visit to west London.

Children under the age of eight can get in for free, with other prices ranging from £13 to £28 for adults.

Meanwhile, the Reds have been handed just over 1,000 tickets for the trip to Millwall on December 6. They are on sale now – and prices range from £9 to £29.

Just before Christmas, on December 21, Sabri Lamouchi will take his Forest side to west Yorkshire to face Huddersfield at the John Smith’s Staduim.

A full allocation of 2,400 tickets has been requested with prices ranging from £10 to £30, and they go on sale to season card holders next Thursday, November 21.
