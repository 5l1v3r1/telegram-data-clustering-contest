Judge rules Isis bride condemned by Donald Trump should be barred from US

15 Nov 2019, 17:23

A judge ruled that a US-born Isis bride condemned by Donald Trump is not a US citizen and should be barred from reentering the US.

On Thursday, Reggie Watson, a Federal District Court Judge for the District of Columbia dismissed a lawsuit brought in February by Hoda Muthana’s family, who hoped to retrieve Muthana and her 2-year-old son from a refugee camp in Syria.

Muthana, 25, was born in New Jersey to a diplomat from Yemen, but ran away to join ISIS in 2014 after apparently becoming radicalized online. She married three different ISIS fighters while overseas before she was captured by Kurdish forces in January.

Watson said Muthana’s father, Ahmed Ali Muthana, cannot provide financial support to Muthana or her son without facing possible charges of providing material support to terrorism.

In February, President Donald Trump tweeted: ‘I have instructed Secretary of State Mike Pompeo, and he fully agrees, not to allow Hoda Muthana back into the Country!’

While Muthana was overseas, the US government determined that she was not a US citizen and revoked her passport because her father was a diplomat at the time of her birth.

The Department of Justice said that because Muthana’s father was a former Yemeni diplomat at the time of her birth, he was under the diplomatic protection of his home country. Because of this, Muthana did not obtain a US citizenship by birth on US soil, the department said.

On Friday, Muthana’s family said they would be appealing the ruling.

Christina Jump, a lawyer with the Constitutional Law Center for Muslims in America, said: ‘While we are disappointed with and disagree with the Court’s ruling today, this is not the end of our client’s legal options.’
