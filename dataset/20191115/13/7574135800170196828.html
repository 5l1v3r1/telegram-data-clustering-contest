Brooklyn Bazaar, which closes in two weeks, is having a liquidation sale

15 Nov 2019, 13:00 by Bill Pearis

Brooklyn Bazaar will close its Greenpoint Ave location at the end of the month and if you’re looking for a souvenir, or looking to buy some used kitchen equipment or security cameras, the venue is trying to recoup some money with a liquidation sale:

As you may know, Brooklyn Bazaar will be closing our doors at our 150 Greenpoint Ave location at the expiration of our current lease on Dec 1.

We have a pretty enormous inventory which we are liquidating and putting up for sale. Restaurant and venue furniture, banquets, tables, high tops, chairs, security camera system, kitchen equipment, bars, walk-ins, fridges, reach-ins, tap system, shelving, flat screens, speakers, multiple sets of sound systems and backlines, and tons more.

If you’re interested in sound systems, staging, backlines, lighting rigs, etc head here for the list of available items; if you’re looking for bar/restaurant/furniture/office etc, head here for the list of stuff. And head here for more info on the sale.

Meanwhile there are still shows happening, including The Ergs tonight (sold out), E-Town Concrete and Lion of Judah (both playing their first NYC shows in 13 years) on 11/20,  Weatherbox/The Hotelier (solo) on 11/21, Jonathan Toubin’s Soul Clap and Dance-Off with Nobunny and more on 11/23, So-So Glos, Wild Yaks and more on 11/29, and the final show on 11/30 with Hank Wood, The Warthogs and more.
