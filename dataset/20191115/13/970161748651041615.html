Tories increase lead over Labour in General Election polls

15 Nov 2019, 13:47

Public support for the Conservative Party has been steadily growing throughout the week, according to an opinion polls average.

The Tories have increased their lead from 34 to 40 per cent over the past two weeks as parties gear up for the December 12 General Election.

Yesterday the December 12 election looked like it could be shaping into a two horse race as Jeremy Corbyn’s party was catching up with the Conservatives.

But today Labour remained stagnant at 29 per cent while the Tories kept on making gains.

It comes Boris Johnson said this morning that his party ‘have got to do better’ on the NHS after figures suggested the service’s performance is at its worst of all time.

Support for the Liberal Democrats has fallen to 16 per cent, compared to 19 per cent when the election was first called.

The Brexit Party was sitting at 12 per cent two weeks ago but has since plummeted to seven per cent.

It comes as its leader Nigel Farage decided to stand down 317 candidates in Tory-held seats.

Meanwhile support for the Green Party, whose members want a second EU referendum, has fallen from five to three per cent.

In his first keynote General Election campaign speech this week, the Prime Minister promised a clean energy revolution, to put more money into the NHS and to help people cope with the cost of living.

Meanwhile the Labour Party have promised free broadband for all by bringing parts of BT into public ownership if it wins.
