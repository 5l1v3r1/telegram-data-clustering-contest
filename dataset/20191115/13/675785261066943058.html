Mum charges family £40 a head for Christmas dinner after ending up broke in 2018

A mum from Essex has taken to billing guests £40 a head to eat at hers on Christmas Day, but insists she's actually undercharging them compared with how much she spends herself

15 Nov 2019, 13:03 by James Andrews, http://www.facebook.com/jameswritesmoney/

Mum of two Mandie Holgate had a shock when she looked at her bank statement in January 2018.

After buying presents and food and for her extended family, she entered the new year broke.

So the 46-year-old from Essex decided that next year she would charge.

"I realised - especially as the kids in the family were getting bigger - that I was spending an absolute fortune on Christmas Day," Mandie told The Sun .

"It all adds up and you wouldn't get free food in a restaurant."

 

Chrismas day, she worked out, had cost her £700 - as she catered for more than a dozen people.

"Don't get me wrong, I love Christmas, I love hosting and I love my family," she said.

"But it is so expensive. In total I've worked out it costs more than £700 to buy lunch for 16 people... I'm not made of money."

Mandie admitted that part of the problem was how much she chose to spend to put on a "big show"

"When I told my colleagues about it they laughed but then admitted it sounded amazing - way better than their Christmas Days," she said.

And, despite the cost, she insists she's offering good value.

"One person - I won't say who - disputed how much everything cost so I actually created an Excel spreadsheet outlining my expenditure," Mandie said.

"When I showed them it they were stunned. And gladly handed over the £40.

"Because it emerged that actually I was undercharging people."

She also doesn't make her children pay for themselves, covering the cost for her son Harry, 18, and daughter Sophie, 15 - oh, and Harry's girlfriend too.

"I know some people think it is bonkers how much I spend on just one day, and I know other people will kick up an absolute fuss that I'm charging my family," Mandie said.

"I love to host and I want to. I'm happy to cook and clear up. I just want people to pay for what they eat and drink so come January I'm not existing on beans on toast."

What you get for your £40

  • On arrival: Fresh pastries, salmon and olives
  • Drinks: Coffee, Bucks Fizz, fizz, beer, selection of cocktails
  • Main: Organic turkey from a local butcher, bread sauce, pigs in blankets, sprouts - "the lot".
  • Extras: Crackers, party hats and more
  • Desert: Aged, home-made Christmas pudding, from nan's old recipe
