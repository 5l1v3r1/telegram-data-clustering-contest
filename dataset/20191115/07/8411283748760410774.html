CPT owes workers up to NT$300 million in salary

15 Nov 2019, 07:49 by Rebecca Kuo, Tainan; Steve Shen, DIGITIMES

The ill-fated panel maker Chunghwa Picture Tubes (CPT) is believed to be owing about 2,000 of its employees nearly NT$300 million (US$9.82 million) in salary, as it seeks to sell its 4.5G and 6G LCD lines to raise funds to cover the payments, according to industry sources.

But there are no signs that the company could sell the two older-generation LCD fabs in the near future after it announced bankruptcy in September, said the sources.

Additionally, the company still owes NT$160 million in electricity fees to Taipower, the sources added.

Fellow LCD makers Innolux and HannStar Display reportedly have made assessment for possible acquisition of the plants, but no deals have been signed, according to sources familiar with the deal.

Some CPT workers have urged the panel maker's parent company, the Tatung Group, to take up the responsibility to clear the underpayments. Tatung and its subsidiaries hold a 40% stake in CPT.
