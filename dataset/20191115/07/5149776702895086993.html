Notts woman left 'baffled' after spotting crystals in Morrisons cheese

It happens when cheese does not reach full maturation

15 Nov 2019, 07:37 by Phoebe Ram

A woman has described her concern after finding 'lactate crystals' embedded in cheese slices she bought from Morrisons.

This has been confirmed to be completely harmless and is caused when cheese "has not quite reached the end of its maturation process".

Alex Beavis of Colwick said she was "completely baffled" to see "small shards" within the slices after she and her housemate had already eaten a few.

"They came in a pack of 10 but there were only two left when I went opened one on Tuesday night (November 12)," Alex said.

The 24-year-old added: "I saw something sticking in the slice of cheese, but then realised it was throughout.

"It looked like small shards, and I thought it was horrific considering they're really meant for children."

Alex bought the Morrison's 'Cheesy Slices' on Friday, November 8 at the Netherfield branch. Th packet had an expiry date of January 2020.

As well as the opened slice, she checked another unopened piece of the cheese and saw the same issue.

Alex contacted customer services through Facebook, believing it to be a serious matter and was told it would be investigated.

After Nottinghamshire Live contacted Morrisons to find out what the cause was behind the unusual defect, Alex was asked to send the product back to be tested.

It was confirmed that 'lactate crystals' had formed which happens when cheese does not fully mature, which is not harmful to eat.

Alex responded to the information by saying: "I still wouldn't want to eat it, because it had sharp edges.

"And it might not be good for a small child to eat. You definitely wouldn't expect it in a soft, cheese slice.

"I think a small warning on the packet would be helpful to stop any other concern in future especially if it's not bad for you."

A Morrisons spokeswoman said: "We have tested the cheese.

"These are lactate crystals which can occur when cheese has not quite reached the end of its maturation process."
