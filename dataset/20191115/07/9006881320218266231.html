Women have the best sex in this type of relationship, study finds

But there's a catch.

15 Nov 2019, 07:39

Ever since apps like Tinder took over, and rom-com worthy meet-cutes became an object of nostalgia, a hook up is never really further than a right swipe away. But if you’re looking for more between the sheets, science may have the answer, and it starts with the letter ‘C’: commitment.

According to new research, women have better sex in committed relationships compared to hook ups. The study, published in The Journal of Sex Research surveyed 1,084 sexually active women between the ages of 18 and 71 about orgasms, sexual satisfaction and monogamy.

The findings showed that women experienced greater sexual outcomes with a significant other than a one-night-stand. But there’s a catch – it comes down to attitude. The study found women who were more open-minded and up for short-term sex-scapades tended to experience similar satisfaction and the ability to orgasm, regardless of whether the sex was casual or not.

“I became interested in this topic because female orgasm is very complex and can be affected by a wide array of factors including psychological, physiological, and environmental factors,” said study author Val Wongsomboon.

“It’s also well known that women tend to orgasm less often than men do, hence the word ‘orgasm gap.’ Many researchers have focused on orgasm gap between men and women. However, I realised there is also an orgasm gap among women themselves.”

“Some women orgasm all or most of the time while some rarely or never do, and this is greatly affected by contexts. Therefore, I started examining the orgasm/sexual satisfaction gap among women as I hope it will help us better understand women’s sexual function and satisfaction as a whole.”

So by all means, swipe right – just make sure you go in with the right attitude.
