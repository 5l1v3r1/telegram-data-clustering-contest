Raw sewage was in flood water after heavy rainfall hit Nottinghamshire village

The heavy downpours led to water spilling out of the sewage pumping station with flood waters forming on the road

15 Nov 2019, 12:43 by Ben Reid

Residents were left "disgusted" as raw sewage was detected in flood water in a Nottinghamshire village.

Large parts of East Leake flooded on Thursday, November 14 after 13 hours of rainfall hit the county.

The heavy downpours led to water spilling out of the sewage pumping station, in Gotham Road, with flood waters forming on the road.

Severn Trent Water explained what happened was known as a "hydraulic overload" - where there’s so much rain in such a short period of time that the sewer system can’t cope.

The company sent a tanker to the pumping station overnight to prevent any more sewage escaping into the water.

Linzi Barford, 43, has lived in the village for four-and-a-half years and said the sewage in the floods was a "public health risk".

"I was disgusted by it," she added.

"We all have big wellies and coats, but you shouldn't have to be walking through that and bringing it into your homes.

"I just want awareness raising on the issue. This has happened before in the village as well.

"Flooding was everywhere in the village. I had to ring my other half to work out how I was going to get home. It was horrendous.

"There are more houses approved for the village, which isn't going to help the situation."

Another East Leake resident, Tom Kilman, 24, added: "It's gross thinking that there's raw sewage in the water.

"Obviously no one wants that. The village suffers quite a lot from floods. The brook gets really high."

A spokesman for Severn Trent told Nottinghamshire Live: "After the huge amount of rain yesterday, we became aware of issues at the pumping station during the night and immediately got a tanker to site so we could remove waste before any more escaped.

"Due to the sheer quantity of issues caused by the heavy rain, we’re prioritising internal flooding but will get clean-up crews out as soon as we can."
