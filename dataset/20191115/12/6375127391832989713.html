Red lions bounce back

15 Nov 2019, 12:19 by Theodore Jurado

NEWLY-MINTED season MVP Calvin Oftana powered San Beda to a 79-76 win over Letran to send the NCAA men's basketball championship to a deciding game last night before 18,407 fans at the Mall of Asia Arena.

When the chips were down, the Red Lions turned to Oftana to keep their dynasty alive.

San Beda regained the driver's seat, 77-76, after Oftana  converted a three-point play with 19.1 seconds left.

The Knights tried to put a storybook ending to their season, but Bonbon Batiller, receiving a pass from Jerrick Balanza, muffed a lay-up as the ball seemed to have slipped away from his hands.

James Canlas then sealed the deal for the Lions with two free throws to close out the thrilling contest.

The winner-take-all Game 3 is set at 4 p.m. on Tuesday at the same Pasay venue.

Instead of becoming the second team after 2017 Lyceum of the Philippines squad to fail to clinch the throne after completing a 18-0 elimination round sweep, San Beda will have a chance to clinch its fourth straight crown, and the 23rd overall.

Letran, for its part, hopes to shrug off the painful endgame as it tries to annex its championship banner No. 18.

History is still on the Knights' side, as the previous 13 champions all won Game 1 of the Finals.

Larry Muyang hit a fadeaway jumper off Kenmark Cariño to give Letran a 76-74 lead with 55.4 seconds remaining.

Oftana scored on a putback that capped the Lions' telling 13-0 run for a 72-66 advantage with 3:51 left.

But the Knights countered with their own 8-0 run for a 74-72 lead courtesy of Muyang's two free throws.   

Fran Yu, continuing his solid post-elimination round play  beat the buzzer, an off-balanced jumper that gave Letran a 55-53 at the end of the third period.

In its the first do-or-die game since Game 3 of the championship against Letran on October 29, 2015, San Beda, stung by its shock 64-65 series-opening loss, played with a sense of urgency.

The Lions roared to a 15-0 start at the 6:04 mark and posted an 18-7 lead at the end of first period.

San Beda was still up, 37-25, at the half before Letran made a huge fightback at the start of second half, taking the lead for the first time at 44-41 after Muyang completed a three-point play.

In the juniors division, McLaude Guadaña shot 19 points as Lyceum of the Philippines University lived another day with a 79-74 win over San Beda.  The Finals decider is set on Tuesday.
