Met Office has its say on chances of more snow for Devon and Cornwall

The latest weather forecast is in

15 Nov 2019, 12:27 by Jamie Hawkins

The Met Office has had its say on the chances of more snow falling across the south west following heavy showers.

Dartmoor, Exmoor and Okehampton were all hit by heavy snow earlier this week, with at least two inches falling in some places.

It led to disruption on major roads across the region, with several accidents reported.

Stunning pictures taken on Thursday morning show snow settled on Dartmoor with further wintry showers expected falling later on.

However, most of the snow has since melted, with the Met Office playing down any further snow for the next few days.

Press officer Oli Claydon told Devon Live: "It doesn't look like there will be anymore snow for the south west for the foreseeable future.

"Any snow on Dartmoor looks to be disappearing throughout Friday, leaving much drier conditions.

"It is looking much drier generally for the weekend, but there are a band of persistent rain showers moving in from Ireland to the tip of Cornwall.

"Dartmoor and Devon overall is looking dry, if not slightly drizzly.

"It will be Brighter Saturday afternoon and into Sunday and Monday.

"But Tuesday afternoon is looking wet."

Temperatures have plummeted across the country this week, with overnight frosts, but it will be getting slightly milder.

Oli added: "We are looking at highs of 9C on Saturday, potentially 10C in Camborne.

"Overnight tonight we are looking at 6C in Plymouth Plymouth but it could be lower in some parts."

So it's bad news for those hoping to see some wintry showers this weekend.

"Unless you're on the high moors of Scotland," Oli added.
