Henman backs Roger Federer to win ATP Finals after 'amazing' Novak Djokovic win

15 Nov 2019, 22:30

Tim Henman rated Roger Federer’s performance against Novak Djokovic as ‘one of his very best’ and backed the 20-time Grand Slam champion to go on and win the ATP Finals.

Federer was at his ruthless best as he downed his great rival in straight sets, serving meticulously in a 6-4 6-3 win on Thursday night at the O2.

He will take on Stefanos Tsitsipas in the next round, with Dominic Thiem facing Alexander Zverev in the other semi-final.

And former British No. 1 Henman believes Federer is the man to beat at the O2.

‘Yeah, I think he probably is [the favourite],’ said Henman. ‘It was an amazing performance.

‘I was sort of disappointed in myself almost that we sit in the commentary box and try and write him off. On an indoor court that’s playing a bit quick, that’s borderline naïve. The way he played, serving the quality, the consistency – he was up six or seven miles per hour on speed. I think it was hotter in there last night, made it a bit quicker.

‘Every time he got the chance to pull the trigger from the baseline, he did. And he didn’t make many unforced errors. One of his very best performances I have seen. Now getting through to the semis he takes huge confidence from that.’

It’s been one of the most thrilling ATP Finals tournaments in recent memory and Henman has relished the unpredictable nature of the event.

‘What I like about this year, in previous years we have seen good tennis but it has been quite predictable,’ he added.

‘You look at the results that we have had. First day we had [Stefanos] Tsitsipas, who had never beaten [Daniil] Medvedev, [Alexander] Zverev had never beaten [Rafael] Nadal – they were up sets.

‘Thiem playing as well as he did beating Federer and Djokovic. Still Djokovic I thought was the favourite and Federer plays like that. Nadal 5-1 down – it has been brilliant.
