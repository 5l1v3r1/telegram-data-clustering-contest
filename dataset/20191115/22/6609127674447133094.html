BOXING CLEVER

Jack Ross ticks ALL the boxes to be Hibs boss but he has questions to answer to after Sunderland, says Michael Stewart

15 Nov 2019, 22:28 by Michael Stewart

HIBS might have looked outside the box when they added Darren Fletcher and John Kennedy to their wishlist.

But in Jack Ross they have someone who ticks ALL the boxes for the Easter Road club right now.

Look, with any managerial appointment there’s an element of risk, even with the very top bosses, but on the face of it this looks like another progressive appointment by Leeann Dempster.

There’s no doubt that in recent times Hibs’ most successful managers have been either young and hungry and on the way up, or people with a point to prove.

Tony Mowbray was a left-field choice that worked out big time. Alan Stubbs was another.

Even Neil Lennon, to a degree, was a move that not a lot of people saw coming at the time.

They tried something similar with Paul Heckingbottom and in the end that was too left-field, even for them.

Mowbray, Stubbs and Lennon, though, all knew the Scottish game, having played here.

And having that sort of history is a clear advantage that Heckingbottom didn’t have, but Ross clearly does.

His critics will point to the fact that he had a good budget at Sunderland and didn’t get them up. That’s a valid argument and something Ross will have to answer to.

But it doesn’t disqualify him from being in the running for a job like Hibs — just as Derek McInnes’ time at Bristol City didn’t stop Aberdeen being interested in him.

Del’s time in England didn’t make him a bad manager, likewise Ross’ time at the Stadium of Light.

But at the same time just because going for McInnes worked for the Dons it’s not a given that Ross will be a success at Hibs.

What I will say is it’s not like Sunderland’s form has suddenly picked up since Ross has moved on.

The job Ross did at St Mirren cannot be underestimated — they were on their way to League One when he went in there.

He turned them around and got them promoted to the Premiership with a squad of players you wouldn’t say was that good. That team, remember, had to get ripped up and improved just to have a chance of staying in the top flight.

Ross is fresh, he has good ideas, he’ll get Hibs playing football the way their fans want to see the game being played.

So it seems like a good fit.

What is clear is that Hibs went into this process with a clear idea of what they wanted. Sure, they may have ultimately come up short in their attempts to speak to Kennedy and Fletcher.

But they were looking to the future. There was a profile there, a clarity of thinking.

It was always going to be difficult for them to get Kennedy in such a pivotal season for Celtic as they chase nine in a row.

I have heard nothing but good things about big John as a coach and, who knows, he may well have designs on being a manager someday soon.

The Hibs job could have been a fantastic opportunity in that respect but I can also understand why Lennon was so keen to keep him.

It’s a similar situation with Fletcher, who is another who wants to be a manager at some point, just not yet.

My old team-mate has already been touted for a role at Manchester United, otherwise he might have kept playing on this season.

But he has made it known he wants to get some experience before throwing himself into a manager’s job.

What Ross, Kennedy and Fletcher all have in common is they are young, hungry and desperate to prove themselves.

And the positive for Hibs is there are boys there Ross has worked with before and others he will inevitably have a good understanding of.

As I have said all along Hibs have the makings of a very good squad but there are also some gaping holes that need to be filled.

Ross has six weeks to get through to the winter break before he can start to plug those gaps.

Centre-half and centre midfield are the two positions where they are crying out for reinforcements.

And while Dylan McGeouch might not have played for him at Sunderland he’d certainly do a job for him at Hibs.

Dylan would help straight away, he would be someone the Hibs fans could relate to straight away as well, unlike some of Heckingbottom’s signings.

I don’t know what the working relationship between the two is like — I’m sure Dylan wouldn’t have been too happy not playing at Sunderland.

But if he was to come to Hibs, knowing he was going to get game time, it would be a win-win situation for both parties.
