Guilty plea in head-on crash that killed 11-year-old girl near Warman

15 Nov 2019, 22:48 by Bre McAdam, Saskatoon StarPhoenix

Anthony McClelland has pleaded guilty to impaired driving causing death and two counts of impaired driving causing bodily harm.

A Martensville man admits he was intoxicated when he caused a head-on collision near Warman that killed an 11-year-old girl.

In Saskatoon provincial court this week, Anthony McClelland pleaded guilty to three charges: impaired driving causing death and two counts of impaired driving causing bodily harm.

On Dec. 21, 2018, McClelland crashed his truck into a truck carrying three people at the intersection of Highways 11 and 305. Sophie Mae Schnurr, 11, died in the crash and two others were injured.

McClelland was also charged with dangerous driving causing death and bodily harm and driving with a blood-alcohol level over the legal limit causing death and bodily harm, but those charges were withdrawn after the impaired driving pleas were entered.
