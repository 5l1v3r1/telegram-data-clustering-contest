Esperance local who received state's first heart transplant celebrates milestone

15 Nov 2019, 05:25 by Sarah Makse

The state's first heart transplant patient travelled to Perth last month to celebrate 25 years of the Advanced Heart Failure and Cardiac Transplant Service.

Esperance local Rodney Western was the first patient to receive a heart following the opening of the service at Royal Perth Hospital in November 1995.

The service marked the first time West Australians could receive heart treatment and care in their own state.

Mr Western's successful procedure paved the way for 198 WA patients to receive healthy heart transplants.

He has been visiting the team at Fiona Stanley Hospital for the past 24 years and enjoyed a healthy life.

Mr Western said his transplant enabled him to live a full life of work, hobbies, time with his wife, three children and eight grandchildren.

"Although it's been 24 years since my transplant I remember clearly the day I received the phone call asking me to come Perth for the transplant, I was excited and nervous at the same time," he said.

"It all worked out really well with the team having me up and walking quite soon after the operation."

On average, there are 10 heart transplants and seven Ventricular Assists Devices implanted each year in WA.
