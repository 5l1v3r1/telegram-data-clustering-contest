“I Stand With Yuendumu”: More Than $65,000 Raised To Help The Warlpiri Seek Justice

15 Nov 2019, 05:50 by Rachael Conaghan

Thousands of dollars has been raised for the Yuendumu community as they fight for an independent investigation into the death of Kumanjayi Walker.

It took less than two days for $65,000 to be raised on GoFundMe, which will help pay for lawyers and other experts.

While people are mourning the death of the 19-year-old, for many the shooting is also a symptom of the institutionalised racism that still exists in Australia. Kumanjayi Walker was shot by police in his home last Saturday after they came to arrest him for breaching parole.

The Warlpiri people of Yuendumu hope an independent investigation will ensure this never happens again.

“This is the time to address the broader systemic issues that so deeply affect our Aboriginal communities,” the campaign reads.

“The families and communities affected will be better served having separate legal representation as there is clearly a need for system change; especially around policing and other broader issues that have come to the forefront as a result of this tragic event.”
