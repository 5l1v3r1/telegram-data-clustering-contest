Toton school where only rule is to 'be kind' achieves outstanding praise

It is one of the first in the country to be rated under the new 2019 Ofsted framework

15 Nov 2019, 16:27 by Phoebe Ram

Staff and pupils of a school described as a having 'a culture of kindness' have celebrated after receiving an outstanding report.

Chetwynd Primary Academy in Toton has been highly complimented by inspectors who said pupil behaviour was "exemplary".

Head teacher Lorraine Tonks, who joined the school in 2014, said the feedback had been "a dream come true" adding the only rule for the school was "be kind".

"We were awarded gold status for our anti-bullying work last year," Lorraine added.

"I think it's just about creating a culture out of it. It's simple for children if you only have one thing to remember. It's also modelled through staff and parents."

Inspectors from Ofsted, the education watchdog, visited the school over two days on October 1 and 2.

All inspectors agreed the school showcased outstanding qualities, improving on its last inspection in 2014 when it was rated good.

Inspectors said pupils are "extremely happy" and "cannot remember when any bullying last happened".

The school was also praised for giving pupils "the best possible education".

The report described Chetwynd as "a hive for learning" with teachers demonstrating "excellent subject knowledge".

Pupils were encouraged to learn to read from their first week and as a result they "soon become fluent readers".

Lorraine added there is an ethos of "keep up not catch up" that ensures children are supported in areas such as reading.

"I've always strongly believed education is the key and that all school children, private or state, deserve the best possible teaching," she said.

"We are lucky to be surrounded by brilliant staff. The first couple of years I was here were tricky. It has been a journey for everyone involved."

The report stated the broader development of pupils was "exceptional" with leaders providing a "vast array of experiences".

These include sports-based activities, drama and music clubs as well as a unique opportunity for students to broadcast the school's news in a podcast, known as Chetwynd Radio.

For pupils with special educational needs and/or disabilities (SEND) the inspectors also noted that expectations were not lowered by staff and they went on to "achieve exceptionally well".

Lorraine said the inspectors "couldn't give us one thing to get better at" but that has not meant the school is not looking for ways to improve.

"After the results came through, staff were already looking at more great ideas to develop," Lorraine said.

"There are always elements we want to improve and we have also had an influx of requests for people to visit and learn from us.

"We want to make sure other children get the same experience as Chetwynd, and will help to spread it widely."

Chetwynd Primary is a Spencer Academies Trust school.

Spencer Academies Trust director of primary education Angela O’Brien said: "This is one of the strongest school inspection reports we can recall.

"Chetwynd has been recognised as something more than outstanding – as a genuinely exceptional and very special place to learn."

Trust chief executive Paul West said: "We are delighted and very proud that Chetwynd has been recognised by Ofsted as an outstanding school

"Lorraine and her team work incredibly hard to deliver the vision for the academy, all children achieve and succeed and Chetwynd has an incredibly supportive parent body."
