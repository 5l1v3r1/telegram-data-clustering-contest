Senior leader at troubled Plymouth school suspended amid 'bullying claims'

It comes after the CEO of ACE Schools Multi Academy Trust was suspended and left with immediate effect

15 Nov 2019, 16:18 by Katie Timms

A senior leader at a troubled Plymouth school has been suspended amid 'bullying and mismanagement' claims.

Chris Humphries, director of performance at ACE Schools Multi Academy Trust, which is based at Martins Gate, Bretonside, was suspended earlier this week and an investigation is underway.

In an email, seen by Plymouth Live, staff at ACE were informed of Mr Humphries' suspension and asked to send "submissions with respect to bullying and mismanagement" to the board of governors.

A source, who wished to remain anonymous, told Plymouth Live that there 'were a number of issues surrounding senior leaders' at the trust.

Ace Schools confirmed that it had a "formal process running currently" and it was "not in a position to comment" at this time.

It comes after Sarah Gillet, CEO of ACE MAT was suspended and left with immediate effect in July this year.

School staff have previously said that they feel "mistreated" and have gone out on strike to voice their concerns with staff treatment.

Mr Humphries' profile on the trust website states: "I worked for fourteen years running Outdoor Education Centres around the UK before retraining as a geography teacher and combining my outdoor education qualifications with teaching. 

"I have led an outstanding pupil referral unit, been shortlisted for a TES Leadership award, hold an NPQH qualification and also am currently a specialist leader in education for behaviour and leadership. 

"My core values are based on helping make a difference not just to our students but also to our staff and partners.  I am currently undertaking a masters in coaching and mentoring to help embed a coaching philosophy across our organisation and through the training we offer other organisations. 

"Much of my personal time is devoted to my love of sailing which has been a lifelong passion."

The trust specialises in providing for children of both primary and secondary school age who have needs outside mainstream education and operates from a number of locations around Plymouth as well as satellite bases in Devon and Cornwall.

In 2017, Courtlands Special School joined the trust.
