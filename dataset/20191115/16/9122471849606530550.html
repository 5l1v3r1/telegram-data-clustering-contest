Detroit Lions Announce Matthew Stafford’s Status vs. Cowboys

15 Nov 2019, 16:59 by Tzvi Machlin

After seeing his 136-game start streak snapped last week against the Chicago Bears with an injury, Detroit Lions quarterback Matthew Stafford has had his status up in the air for Sunday’s game against the Dallas Cowboys.

Lions head coach Matt Patricia updated Stafford’s status – and it doesn’t bode well for the team’s chances.

Patricia said in his Friday press conference that Stafford has been ruled out for the game. He is currently dealing with broken bones in his back as well as a hip injury.

As a result, backup Jeff Driskel will start for the second straight week. Driskell went 27 of 46 for 269 yards, one touchdown and one interception in last week’s 20-13 loss to the Bears.
