41-year-old man pleads guilty to cannabis charges

Mouniam Elfidh, of no fixed addresses, appeared at Nottingham Crown Court

15 Nov 2019, 16:25 by Rebecca Sherdley

A 41-year-old man pleaded guilty to producing cannabis and possessing the drug with intent to supply.

Mouniam Elfidh, of no fixed addresses, entered his pleas at Nottingham Crown Court.

He denied possessing a quantity of amphetamine with intent to supply but pleaded guilty to possession.

He also admitted abstracting electricity.

The offences happened on May 9.

The court heard he had been convicted of two different charges at Lincoln Crown Court and that hearing had been adjourned until December 19.

The prosecution said it seemed custody was likely to be inevitable, and the pleas were acceptable.

Judge Stuart Rafferty QC sent the latest case to be sentenced by the same judge at Lincoln.

Elfidh remains in custody.
