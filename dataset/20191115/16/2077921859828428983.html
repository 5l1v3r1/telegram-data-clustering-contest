New on Hulu: All the Movies and TV Coming in December

15 Nov 2019, 16:59 by Scott Russell  

The longer 2019 lasts, the faster it moves—we’re already staring down the barrel of December, with 2020 lurking on the other side. Time’s inexorable march, however, won’t steal any further focus from Hulu, specifically the streamer’s feature film and television additions coming next month. Allow us to walk you through the highlights, won’t you?

December’s film standout is an easy call: Julia Hart’s sci-fi family drama Fast Color (Dec. 11), by all accounts one of the year’s best films that no one saw—though acclaimed by critics and audiences alike, the film flopped at the box office, so if you missed it, don’t make the same mistake twice. Gugu Mbatha-Raw (A Wrinkle in Time) stars as a young woman with the ability to control the earth’s energy—her superhuman powers make her a target, forcing her to return to the home she abandoned long ago, where she must reconnect with her mother (Lorraine Toussaint, Orange Is the New Black) and daughter (Saniyya Sidney, Fences), evade the local sheriff (David Strathairn, Good Night, and Good Luck), and sound the depths of her power. Other film additions worth watching out for include Martin Scorsese’s 2004 Howard Hughes epic The Aviator (Dec. 1), mid-’80s dance classic Footloose (Dec. 1), Quentin Tarantino’s martial arts revenge saga Kill Bill (Dec. 1), Cameron Crowe’s rock ‘n’ roll tale Almost Famous (Dec. 1) and Oliver Stone Oscar winner Wall Street (Dec. 1), followed later in the month by indelible killer whale doc Blackfish (Dec. 12), acclaimed ‘80s-set Transformers prequel Bumblebee (Dec. 13), Ridley Scott’s Denzel Washington- and Russell Crowe-led crime drama American Gangster (Dec. 16) and Jessie Buckley-starring musical drama Wild Rose (Dec. 16). The first four Fast and Furious films are scattered throughout the month, as well, if you’re into those.

We move now to the small screen, where the foremost highlight is equally obvious: Season 2 of AMC’s Killing Eve (Dec. 18), the Sandra Oh- and Jodie Comer-starring, Emmy-winning spy series from Fleabag mastermind Phoebe Waller-Bridge. Oh and Comer star as MI6 operative Eve and psychopathic assassin Villanelle, respectively—bound by mutual obsession and one brutal act, the two women play a game of cat and mouse that Paste so enjoyed watching play out, we named Killing Eve one of the year’s best shows (so far). Elsewhere in TV, you might keep an eye out for: Season 6 of TV Land’s long-running, Hilary Duff-starring romantic comedy series Younger (Dec. 3), the debut season of Hulu’s original new “hyper-noir” revenge series Reprisal (Dec. 6), Season 3 of Hulu’s superhero fan-favorite Marvel’s Runaways (Dec. 13), new Christmas- and New Year’s Eve-themed episodes of Hulu horror anthology Into the Dark (“A Nasty Piece of Work” on Dec. 6, “Midnight Kiss” on Dec. 27, respectively), the complete third season of Pamela Adlon’s FX dramedy series Better Things (Dec. 28) and the complete fourth season of AMC’s acclaimed adaptation of Garth Ennis graphic novel series Preacher (Dec. 29).

Don’t overlook what’s leaving Hulu at the end of the year, either, folks: Titles departing on Dec. 31 include must-sees like Boyz N’ The Hood, Platoon, Chinatown, Interview with the Vampire, Total Recall, Varsity Blues, Event Horizon, True Grit (1969), Requiem for a Dream, Magic Mike, Get Shorty, Heathers, Cloverfield and Saturday Night Fever. It’s not all bad news, though: At least American Beauty, Crash, Hot Tub Time Machine and Terminator Salvation are all about to be that much easier to ignore.
