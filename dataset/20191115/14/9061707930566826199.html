The cosy seaside caravan site which is perfect for a weekend away

It's perfect for families

15 Nov 2019, 14:30 by Anna Whittaker

The homely caravans, breathtaking sea views and stunning scenery are just a few of the factors which makes Holgates holiday park so amazing.

Situated in an Area of Outstanding Beauty just next to Morecambe Bay are a flock of inviting green caravans which make up Holgates Silverdale Holiday Park.

Perfect for nature lovers, the site is set in the middle of a woodland with stunning sea views and beach walks to be enjoyed.

Warming gastropub food is just a 20 minute walk away, or you can choose to cook in your caravan which is docked out with everything you need for cooking.

What are the caravans like?

Simply amazing.

The caravan was absolutely pristine and smelt brand new.

It was spacious too - the lounge had plenty of space for the four of us and the three bedrooms provided us with room to relax.

We stayed in the three bedroom caravan which was totally luxorious. It also had a sofa bed which means it could house even more guests.

The site has more than 350 caravans altogether, with the majority being holiday home owners and the rest available for hire.

It also has camping pods, touring pitches and tents.

What can you do there?

There is an endless list of things to do just a small walk away from the caravans - making the park perfect for families.

Children can enjoy the indoor heated swimming pool, bowling alley, soft play area and outdoor playground.

And for the adults, a sauna, steam room, gym and nine hole pitch and putt can be enjoyed on the site.

The hot tub was a particular highlight, especially with its views looking out into the beautiful setting of the caravan site.

The site itself itself is situated in 100 acres of grounds, meaning you don't even need to leave the site for a scenic walk.

Outside of the site there are also plenty of ways to stay entertained.

A short walk away is the beach front where you can take a stroll while watching the sun go down.

Holgates is also within an hour's drive of the Lakes. On a rainy day we drove half an hour to Kendal, which was perfect for a day of shopping.

The coastal town of Morecambe was also a nice day trip from the site. We had a beach walk and there's a play area there which is great for kids.

Further afield, you can also drive to the Yorkshire Dales and the Forest of Bowland.

What can you eat there?

The caravan is equipped with everything you need to cook meals.

But if you fancy a meal out, you can visit the on-site restaurant and bar which serves delicious freshly prepared meals as well as snacks and cakes throughout the day.

The restaurant is situated right next to the swimming pool, meaning you can have a morning swim and tuck in to a hearty lunch afterwards.

Amazingly, there's also an ice cream parlour stocked up with locally made ice creams.

Just down the road is the centre of Silverdale, where you can find shops, a takeaway and restaurants.

We loved The Royal Silverdale, a cosy gastropub serving fresh food and a range of locally sourced beers and a huge list of flavoured gins.

Overall, it was a unforgettable weekend and we will definitely be back at Holgates in the near future.
