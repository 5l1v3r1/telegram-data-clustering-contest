I'm A Celebrity's Myles Stephenson to grill Caitlin Jenner over OJ Simpson trial

15 Nov 2019, 14:22

I’m A Celebrity star Myles Stephenson has revealed how he’s desperate to find out from fellow campmate Caitlin Jenner if OJ Simpson was really a murderer.

Robert Kardashian, who was married to Caitlin’s ex Kris Jenner was part of OJ’s defence team when he was initially cleared of killing his wife Nicole Brown and her friend Ron Goldman.

During the infamous trial, OJ was asked by the prosecution to try on a pair of gloves that was connected to the murders – and now the Rak-Su singer is eager to find out more.

Myles told The Sun: ‘The first thing I’m going to ask Caitlyn is, “Did OJ do it? Does the glove really fit?”‘

Caitlin has always been quite vocal about the OJ trial and in extracts from her memoir The Secrets Of My Life, she admits that during a car ride with Robert he once told her that he ‘would’ve been okay with it if they had gotten him in the first trial.

‘The implication was obvious that he believed OJ was guilty.’

Myles and Caitlin will hit our screens on Sunday along with an all-star I’m A Celebrity line-up, which includes the likes of Nadine Coyle, Ian Wright and Jacqueline Jossa.

The singer, who rose to fame on The X Factor, has admitted that he doesn’t want to be ‘held back’ if other career prospects come his way.

The 28-year-old went on to explain that while he is unsure of what will happen after the show, his fellow bandmates have been nothing but supportive of him.

Speaking ahead of entering the jungle on Sunday during the hotly-anticipated launch, Myles explained: ‘They are really supportive and they totally understand.

‘I would love to stay in the band but if other opportunities come along my way as well after this, you have to take it by the scruff of the neck.

‘They would do the same. I would never hold them back.’

Rak-Su, a four-piece band from Watford, won The X Factor in 2017, becoming the first boy band to win the show after beating runner-up Grace Davies.

The band, comprised of Myles Stephenson, Ashley Fongho, Jamaal Shurland and Mustafa Rahimtulla, have yet to release a full-length debut album – and earlier this year it was reported the boys had been dropped by their record label.

I’m A Celebrity begins on Sunday, 17 November, at 9pm, on ITV.
