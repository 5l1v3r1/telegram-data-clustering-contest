Browns' Odell Beckham Jr., Jarvis Landry troll Mike Tomlin with yawns

15 Nov 2019, 03:11 by Jeremy Fowler

CLEVELAND -- Browns receivers Odell Beckham Jr. and Jarvis Landry let Pittsburgh Steelers coach Mike Tomlin know they didn't sleep on his offseason gesture about Beckham.

Both receivers pretended to yawn in the end zone after big plays in Thursday night's game, an apparent nod to a Tomlin on-camera ESPN interview, during which he made the same yawning gesture when asked about playing the newly acquired Beckham twice a season.

"You know, I see great players, or we see great players, week in and week out," Tomlin said in July from a fundraising event in his hometown of Virginia Beach, Virginia. "We've seen him before. We respect his talent and the talents of everyone they've acquired. But it doesn't change our agenda or our focus."

The clip of a yawning Tomlin circulated online.

After Beckham broke free for a 42-yard gain to the goal line in the first quarter Thursday night, he turned toward the Steelers bench and yawned several times before celebrating with teammates. Beckham thought he had scored a touchdown, but the ball was ruled down at the 1. The Browns scored on the next play with a quarterback sneak by Baker Mayfield.
