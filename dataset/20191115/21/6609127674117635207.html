Rod Stewart reveals he’d back Scottish independence – but would still be sad to see the Union split

15 Nov 2019, 21:53 by Craig Paton

SIR Rod Stewart says he would back Scotland going independent — despite reckoning a UK split would be sad.

The Maggie May singer, 74, admitted he would be pleased to see us go it alone if it's “good for the country”.

But the Celtic-daft superstar, who spoke out against a Yes vote in 2014 along with other British celebs, insisted he was passionate about the Union.

Sir Rod, who was born in London to a Scots dad and English mum, told BBC Scotland’s The Nine show: “I’m somewhat of a traditionalist.

“It would be a shame to see the British Isles break up. It would be a shame to see that blue off the Union Jack. But if it is good for Scotland then I’m happy.”

Ahead of the first indy poll five years ago, Sir Rod said of Scotland: “It’s always been a spiritual home but as I don’t live there I shouldn’t comment on independence.

"I hope it’s not a lot of kids thinking ‘Braveheart’.”

In The Nine interview Sir Rod also claimed he had faith in PM Boris Johnson to end the Brexit deadlock.

He said: “We’re in a muddle but I think Boris will sort it out. We have to be patient.

“I was always a Remainer. I didn’t see why we had to leave.

“Maybe it’s something that I’m missing, I don’t know, but I’m still a Remainer.”

And the music legend insisted December 12’s General Election is of the “utmost importance”.

He said: “It’s a once in a lifetime, this election. I don’t know what the outcome is going to be, I really don’t.”
