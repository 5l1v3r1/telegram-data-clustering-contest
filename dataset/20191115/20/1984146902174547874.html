Meat thief with 231 convictions caught smuggling drugs into prison given chance to go straight

Paul Cox, of Durham, was told he has six months to prove he can stay offence-free or he will be jailed

15 Nov 2019, 20:27 by Rob Kennedy

A drug addict with 231 previous convictions who was caught smuggling narcotics into a prison has been given six months to prove he can stay out of trouble.

Paul Cox was visiting an inmate at HMP Northumberland when he was spotted handing over a package containing 50 xanax tablets.

The 42-year-old went on to steal hundreds of pounds worth of meat and chocolates during a series of shop thefts, Newcastle Crown Court.

He was facing a prison sentence but a judge decided to give him until next March to see if he can remain offence free - promising if he does, he may consider sparing him custody.

Recorder Paul Reid told Cox: "You are facing a period inside again and that is the almost inevitable outcome or would have been were I to sentence you today.

"But the sentence for this lengthy period of appalling behaviour cannot be measured in years, rather in months.

"I'm going to defer the sentencing hearing until March 6.

"You have to stay out of trouble. If you do, when you come back, having had a period free of offending, the chances are I will impose a custodial sentence but will suspend it. So it's a matter entirely for you."

The court heard it was on March 21 last year that Cox went to HMP Northumberland.

During a visit, he was seen reaching down into his trousers, bringing something out and placing it on a table, which the prisoner slid across the table to himself.

Cox, of Redwood Flats, Brandon, County Durham, told police pressure was being put on the inmate, whose relative he was living with, and he agreed to help.

He pleaded guilty to conveying a banned article into prison and nine offences of theft from shops - including high value meat and chocolates.

The court heard of his 231 previous convictions, 136 are for acquisitive crime, mainly shop thefts and he also previously served a seven year sentence for supplying heroin.

Michael Hodson, defending, said: "His antecedents are quite frankly appalling.

"He was stealing items which were easily sold and are attributable to his addiction.

"He owed debts because he is a drug addict and he was encouraged to take those drugs into prison, no doubt to pay off some drug debt."

Mr Hodson added that Cox was the carer for his mentally ill partner and he had also been attacked with pepper or CS gas.
