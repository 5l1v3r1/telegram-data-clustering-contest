Gareth Bale says playing for Wales 'more exciting' than Real Madrid

15 Nov 2019, 20:49 by James Candy

The Wales fans who have traversed Europe with hopes of witnessing their team take a leap towards another major tournament will unfurl a brand new banner in honour of their talisman Gareth Bale.

A national flag emblazoned with “Wales, Golf, Madrid – In that order” will make its international debut in Baku referencing Real Madrid legend Predrag Mijatovic’s claim that the 30-year-old firmly prioritised country over club.

Bale last played for the Spanish giants on Oct 5 and did not return to full training under Zinedine Zidane following last month’s Euro 2020 qualifiers against Slovakia and Croatia.

But he looks set to play another leading role for Ryan Giggs’s side in the decisive games against Azerbaijan and Hungary, and Bale admits his fires burns brightest for Wales.

“It’s the goal that will come first,” said Bale on qualifying for another European Championship. “It’s something I think everyone knows - how passionate I am about Wales.

“We want to qualify for multiple tournaments. It’s not just important for the here and now but also for the future of Welsh football and make our national team better for the future.
