When is the next episode of See on Apple TV and how can you watch it?

15 Nov 2019, 20:00

Jason Momoa’s new Apple TV Plus series See has been getting rave reviews and the post-apocalyptic show has already been renewed for a second season.

The 40-year-old actor stars as Baba Voss, a father of twins born with the ability to see in a world 400 years in the future where humans have long lost their sight.

With a budget of $15 million per episode, the show had a lot to live up to, but luckily the series, which also stars the likes of Alfre Woodard, Sylvia Hoeks and Hera Hilmar in the cast, doesn’t disappoint.

So when can we tune in to see the next installment of See and learn the fate of Baba Voss, his twins and their clan?

When is the next episode of See on Apple TV?

The show’s first three episodes, which Metro.co.uk’s Adam Miller described in his review as: ‘three hours of unbroken tension’ were made available on November 1 when the show launched and have continued to roll out weekly since.

So yes, that means that another episode of the TV show will be available to stream today, on Friday 15 November.

How can you watch See?

Along with The Morning Show, starring Jennifer Aniston and Reese Witherspoon, See was part of the opening offering to entice people to try out Apple’s new streaming service Apple TV Plus.

Apple TV Plus can be accessed through Apple’s TV app. You need to sign up to the monthly subscription service, which costs £4.99 a month, but there’s also a free seven-day trial – if you want to try before you buy.

For those thinking of purchasing a new iPad, iPhone, iPod touch, Mac or Apple TV2, you’ll also snag yourself free access to Apple TV Plus for a year as a bonus.
