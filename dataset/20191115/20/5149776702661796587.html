£8m McDonald's, Greggs and Coffee Coffee development approved for site off the A52

"It's just a lost game," one campaigner said

15 Nov 2019, 20:27 by Jamie Barlow

Controversial £8m plans for a McDonald's, a Costa Coffee and a Greggs in a village off the A52 have been given the go-ahead.

Eight Rushcliffe borough councillors voted in favour of the plans for a site next to Wheatcroft Island, on land between the A52 and Landmere Lane, Edwalton. Two councillors voted against.

Among objectors to the scheme was Hetvi Parekh, 36, a civil servant and a member of the Edwalton Community Group, who said she was 'disappointed' with the outcome.

"It's just a lost game. We're all very disappointed, I would say. We're not happy about it," she said.

"It's not just about the traffic. We are also worried that the employment that it's going to create, it's not going to be high-end employment, it's all low wages."

McDonald's bosses say the new fast-food outlet is likely to open next summer.

A spokesman for the company said: "We’re excited to be opening a new restaurant in Edwalton following the decision of the planning committee last night.

"We aim to start building works as soon as possible and look to open sometime in 2020.

“As well as offering great-tasting food for our customers, the new restaurant will create at least 65 full- and part-time jobs for local people and we look forward to playing a positive role in the local community.”

A Costa Coffee spokeswoman said: “As the UK’s favourite coffee shop brand, we work closely with local planning departments when opening new drive-thru stores to ensure we comply with all building and planning requirements.

"We can confirm that planning permission has been approved for the drive-thru store on Landmere Lane, Edwalton, Nottinghamshire, and we look forward to sharing more information soon.”

The borough council's planning committee, meeting on Thursday, November 14, approved West Bridgford-based Henry Davidson Developments' proposals for two drive-through outlets - to be occupied by McDonald's and Costa - along with a day nursery and three shop units, one of which will be occupied by Greggs.

Members considered a report detailing objections from 136 members of the public, on grounds such as traffic congestion, light pollution and likely litter and anti-social behaviour. Four members of the public backed the plans.

Speaking previously, Graeme Cosgrove, director of Henry Davidson Developments, said the project had been reviewed by Highways England and Nottinghamshire County Council.

"They are both happy that the road network has a substantial amount of available capacity for additional traffic," he said.

The scheme is located on land between an Aldi and care home, which already have planning permission.

Meanwhile, elsewhere a retail park expected to deliver a new gym, Lidl supermarket and Starbucks coffee shop is planned in West Bridgford.
