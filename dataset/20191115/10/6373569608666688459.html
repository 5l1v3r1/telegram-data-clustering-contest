Royal Navy launch four F-35 fighter jets from aircraft carrier HMS Queen Elizabeth off coast of United States

It happened for the first time ever

15 Nov 2019, 10:30 by Rom Preston-Ellis

Four jets have been launched in quick succession from HMS Queen Elizabeth for the first time ever.

The four F-35 Lightning jets took off from the giant aircraft carrier off the coast of the USA.

The operation took place as part of Westlant 19 -  a three-month exercise where aircraft carrier HMS Queen Elizabeth, F-35s and supporting units are being rigorously tested under realistic warfighting scenarios, while visiting Canada and the USA.

The exercise was described as "absolutely awe inspiring to watch, hear and feel."

HMS Queen Elizabeth's official Twitter account said: "We have flown our first UKF35 multi-ship, launching 4 jets in quick succession and recovering on completion of their ‘mission’.

"Absolutely awe inspiring to watch, hear and feel."

Earlier this week HMS Queen Elizabeth welcomed two new ships to its 'strike group'.

USS Philippine Sea and USS Truxtun, the Ticonderoga-class guided missile cruiser and Arleigh Burke-class destroyer have joined us as UKF35 jet trials continue in the Atlantic off the coast of the USA.

HMS QE also brought its renewed Carrier Strike Group capability online with a refuel at sea involving bridge work with HMS Northumberland.

"Welcome to the Queen Elizabeth Carrier Strike Group, USS Philippine Sea and USS Truxtun," said HMS Queen Elizabeth's official Twitter account.

"The Ticonderoga-class guided missile cruiser and Arleigh Burke-class destroyer have joined us as #UKF35 jet trials continue in the USA #WESTLANT19 #jointcapability."

"A great shot to add and another first for WESTLANT19 as we bring our renewed Carrier Strike Group capability online.

"A double Replenishment at Sea (RAS), with some fantastic bridge work from FATideforce, HMSNORT and our own great team here."
