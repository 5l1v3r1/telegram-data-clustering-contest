Andy Ruiz Jr almost KOs trainer as he prepares for Anthony Joshua rematch

Ruiz Jr showed off his power during trainer as his coach staggered after the heavyweight thumped the pad

15 Nov 2019, 08:30 by Martin Domin, https://www.facebook.com/mirrorfighting

Andy Ruiz Jr showed off his power as he almost knocked out his coach in training.

The heavyweight world champion is preparing for his rematch with Anthony Joshua as the Brit bids to win back his belts.

Ruiz shocked the world in June when he dropped Joshua four times before stopping him in the seventh round.

The rivals will clash in Saudi Arabia on December 7 in one of the most eagerly-awaited fights of the year.

And the Mexican-American posted a video of himself hitting pads with the caption, "I barely touched him."

In the footage, Ruiz throws a left hook which catches his trainer and sends him staggering backwards before the coach signals he is OK and is hugged by the champion.

Ruiz Jr fought Joshua in June at just six weeks' notice and was mocked for being chubby.

But having shocked the world, he has had three months to prepare for his sequel with Joshua.

And the champion plans to slim down to make best use of his speed in Saudi Arabia.

"Right now, I'm only three pounds less than when I fought Anthony Joshua," Ruiz Jr told iFLTV.

"A lot of people are saying 'he looks too skinny, he won't be strong enough for the fight', but those are people who don't really know about boxing.

"We've been looking really good inside the ring.

"I just want to be 6lbs, 8lbs lighter than I was on June 1.

"That's the goal, to be 8lbs lighter, and that's going to make me feel a lot better.

"It's going to make me a lot slicker, a lot faster, and that's going to tell on December 7.

"I weighed in at 268 for the first Anthony Joshua fight, and I'm planning to weigh in at 260 for the next fight.

"I don't want to lose too much weight because I want to be strong for December 7. We know what we're doing and what we're doing it for."
