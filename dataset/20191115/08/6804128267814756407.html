Gary Lineker makes big prediction about Chelsea star Tammy Abraham after his first England goal

The Chelsea striker scored his first England goal as the Three Lions thrashed Montenegro at Wembley

15 Nov 2019, 08:58 by James Findlater

Tammy Abraham’s maiden goal for England will be the “first of many” for the Chelsea striker, Gary Lineker has predicted.

Abraham came off the bench to score his first goal for England during last night’s 7-0 thrashing of Montenegro.

The Three Lions secured their qualification for next summer’s Euro 2020 finals with ease as Tottenham’s Harry Kane scored a hat-trick at Wembley.

Abraham came on as a second half substitute for the England skipper – who overtook Alan Shearer to sit fifth on the all-time England goals list on 31 – and marked his fourth appearance for the national team in style.

The 22-year-old latched on to Jadon Sancho’s ball across the face of goal to tuck away his first England strike.

And the Chelsea striker was congratulated by Lineker, who sits third on the all-time leading scorers’ list, and expects big things from Abraham.

He tweeted: "Congratulations to @tammyabraham on his first @England goal. Always a truly special moment. First of many for his country, I suspect."

Alex Oxlade-Chamberlain opened the scoring on his return for England, with Marcus Rashford also on the scoresheet as the Three Lions ran riot in the first half at Wembley.

And Lineker was also full of praise for Gareth Southgate’s youthful side – England’s youngest in 60 years – and backed them to have a say in the 2022 World Cup in Qatar.
