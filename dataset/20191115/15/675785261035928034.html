British Gas apologises for sending a woman letter calling her 'Ms Nightmare'

The letter was sent to a great grandmother after she asked why she wasn't able to access her account online, however British Gas is saying it's a genuine mistake

15 Nov 2019, 15:49 by James Andrews, http://www.facebook.com/jameswritesmoney/

British Gas has apologised for sending an 79-year-old  woman in which they called her 'Ms Nightmare'.

Luckily for them, great-grandmother Janet Nightingale saw the funny side.

After Janet moved into a flat in Overstrand Road, Cromer, Norfolk, she wrote to the company explaining she was unable to access her online account.

She got a reply from the complaints department at British Gas reading in bold letters: "We're sorry you've had cause to complain" and then in smaller text "Dear Ms Nightmare".

The grandmother-of-six and great-grandmother-of-one said: "My initial thought was 'bloody cheek'.

"But then I thought 'What the hell. What's the point of complaining?'

"A neighbour had helped me write the letter and he thought it was quite funny too.

"It has given many of my friends some amusement."

 

The neighbour, Stephen Scott-Fawcett, was equally amused by the response.

He said "Janet moved into a flat below me in Cromer and asked for help to complain to British Gas about their service.

"She received a standard complaints reply addressed to, wait for it, Ms Nightmare.

"You couldn't make it up."

Further on in the response, British Gas said they had been able to resolve her complaint.

She is now set up as the new tenant for the property and can select a tariff and payment method, it added.

A British Gas spokesman said: "Unfortunately our complaint handler made a typo in the spelling of Ms Nightingale's name, which he promptly corrected.

"We've been in touch to apologise for the genuine mistake."

Janet said she's proud of her surname and her second Christian name...Florence.
