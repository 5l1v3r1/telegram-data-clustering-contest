Flooding disruption in Nottinghamshire - What to expect over the next few days

The Environment Agency is asking people to "remain vigilant" in the coming days as river levels on the Trent remain high

15 Nov 2019, 15:26 by Ben Reid

Residents have been warned to remain vigilant due to high river levels and flooding after heavy rain caused disruption across large parts of Nottinghamshire.

The wet weather created travel disruption on Friday morning, with road closures in place and rail services being delayed or cancelled.

Trains running from Nottingham to Derby were cancelled for much of Friday due to the flooding.

Flood warnings remain along the River Trent on Friday, November 15 including at Collingham, Lenton and Newark.

And the Environment Agency is asking people to be vigilant in the coming days as river levels on the Trent remain high.

This is due to recent high levels of rain moving down the river to its mouth in the Humber Estuary.

More than 40 roads were closed across Nottinghamshire on Friday including the A52 Radcliffe Road and the A617 Kirklington Road.

Nottingham City Council confirmed the Queens Drive Park & Ride Site will remain closed on Saturday, November 16 due to the standing water.

Rushcliffe Borough Council said more than 2,600 sandbags were delivered to 80 locations across the borough on Thursday by the council’s waste and streetwise teams.

Crews from both organisations worked continually from midday on Thursday through to the early hours of Friday morning to distribute the bags.

The council’s housing team also found bed and breakfast accommodation for families who needed to be evacuated from their homes in Tollerton, with 15 properties affected in the area.

Rushcliffe School had to close 11 of its classrooms due to the flooding and St Peter's C of E Junior School in Ruddington was closed on Friday.

Sewage was reported to have formed in flood water in a Nottinghamshire village and gardens were also underwater from the deluge.

A spokesman for the Environment Agency told Nottinghamshire Live: "River levels on the Trent remain high and we ask people to remain vigilant and to check their flood risk.

"The levels in Nottingham are steady at the Colwick Gauge and at around 1pm (Friday, November 15) were measured at 4.11m.

"Our staff continue to monitor the levels as the water from the recent rain moves down the river.

"We are working closely with partner agencies to protect people and property.

"We will issue further alerts and warnings when necessary.

"River levels are set to remain high and people should be vigilant across the East Midlands."

The Met Office weather station at Watnall - the closest one to Nottingham - recorded 29.0mm of rainfall throughout Thursday.

Meanwhile the station at Sutton Bonington had 27.6mm.

However the wettest place in the UK was at Tredegar in Wales, which recorded 42mm.

How is the forecast looking over the next few days?

Fortunately, the Met Office is predicting better conditions over the weekend and into next week for the county.

While there is a 60 percent chance of light rain from 7pm on Friday, through to 1am on Saturday morning, Nottingham's forecast is overcast for Saturday, but dry.

Temperatures will reach highs of 8 degrees C in the city.

Further rain is predicted across northern England and southern Scotland.

Outlook for Sunday to Tuesday (November 17-19)

Sunshine and dry weather is forecast for the start of next week, with highs of around seven degrees C predicted on Monday in the city.

Tuesday is looking cloudier at this stage, but dry.
