Disgraceful scenes in NFL as Cleveland's Myles Garrett rips helmet off Pittsburgh quarterback Mason Rudolph and attacks him with it

15 Nov 2019, 09:35 by Reuters News Agency

A violent incident marred the end of the NFL game between Cleveland Browns and Pittsburgh Steelers on Thursday when Cleveland's defensive end, Myles Garrett, ripped the helmet off Pittsburgh's young quarterback Mason Rudolph and used it to strike Rudolph on the head.

Commentators were aghast, with Fox pundit Joe Buck describing the incident as "one of the worst things I've ever seen on a professional sports field".

Garrett was ejected after the ensuing melee, with just eight seconds left in the game and Cleveland already assured of the victory. Garrett now faces a multi-game suspension by the league.

The incident started when Garrett tackled Rudolph well after the quarterback had thrown the ball.

Rudolph, however, was not happy, and grabbed at Garrett's helmet. Garrett then did likewise and managed to rip Rudolph's off his head before using it to strike the quarterback.
