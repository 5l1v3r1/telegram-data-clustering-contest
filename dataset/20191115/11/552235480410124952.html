Violent 'control freak' shatters girlfriends eye socket after he's caught cheating

Terrified Joanne Mason, 33, was terrorised by Luke Patrice, 32, for almost two years.

15 Nov 2019, 11:29 by Robert Sutcliffe

A violent 'control freak' left his girlfriend with a shattered eye socket after going into a fit of rage when she caught him with another woman.

Terrified Joanne Mason, 33, was terrorised by Luke Patrice, 32, for almost two years.

She suffered unbearable abuse almost from the start of their relationship, reports the Examiner Live .

She said his possessive streak surfaced soon after they started dating in Heckmondwike, West Yorkshire, in December 2016 when he demanded her Facebook password.

After she caught Patrice with another woman he attacked her and then prevented her from getting medical treatment for four days.

Traumatised Ms Mason has bravely spoken out about the abuse and her injuries - her eyesight will be "imperfect" for the rest of her life - after Patrice was jailed.

She said: "He pretended to be me to see if I had any relationships with other men.

"I thought he liked me. But he was a complete control freak.

"I was supposed to have a court protection order in place to stop him from harming me but it didn't get sent off in time."

Matters came to a head when she caught him with another woman in Golcar.

Patrice left her with severe injuries in an attack on January 26, but she says he prevented her getting any medical treatment until four days later.

He treated the wound himself with a frozen pack of ice at the home they shared in Scholes, near Cleckheaton.

Ms Mason, who has a teenage daughter, said: "I had been looking forward to going out with some friends and had started to curl my hair.

"He was going out too and couldn't find his trousers and flew into a rage. I went into the living room and lit a cig.

"The next thing was he spat in my face and pushed me to the floor and called me a slag.

"I went to run down the stairs and he smashed me in the face with the palm of his hand.

"I was on the floor shouting at him: 'why do you have to hit me so hard?'"

She went to Calderdale Royal Hospital and was referred to Bradford Royal Infirmary.

Ms Mason said: "I phoned my mum. I needed to tell her because I was scared.

 

"On February 4 she underwent a three and a half hour operation to try to repair the optical nerve damage to her left eye with metal plates to hold her eyeball in situ.

The mum said: "Luke was saying I didn't need it but I have been left with no feeling in the left hand side of my face. Quite a lot of my face is now numb.

"The medics did a brilliant job but I've been left with a life-changing injury and my eyesight will be imperfect for the rest of my life.

"And I have post-traumatic stress disorder now.

"I remember looking at the surgeon saying 'help me'. I had had enough.

"Every time he used to hurt me he would say it was an accident.

"I had never grown up with domestic violence, it was a new and horrifying experience for me.
"I feel like my life has been torn apart. I was a happy, outgoing person.

"My teenage daughter thinks what has happened to me is disgusting."

She added: "I loved going out with my friends and spending time with my family. I was a happy person.

 

"I loved my job working in the kitchen of a high school in West Yorkshire thought they had to send me home sometimes because of the damage he had done to me."

Patrice went on the run earlier this year and West Yorkshire Police issued a plea for his whereabouts following allegations regarding a number of offences allegedly committed across the West Yorkshire area.

Ms Mason said: "He was not man enough to hand himself in. He was bailed on 12 March.
"He was in Leeds but was caught in Huddersfield."

Yesterday at Leeds Crown Court, Patrice, of no fixed abode, was sentenced by Judge Robin Mairs to 34 months in prison.

He was convicted of unlawfully wounding Miss Mason and received 24 months imprisonment.
In addition he pleaded guilty to eight months for dangerous driving on Leeds Road and Lower Quarry Road, Huddersfield, in April 2018.

He was also sentenced to two months for possession of Class B drugs in December 2017.

Patrice received two months imprisonment for assaulting an emergency worker in Huddersfield He was also disqualified from driving for four years and five months.

Asked how she felt about the length of his imprisonment she said it was not long enough.

She said: "I feel heartbroken. How can someone who is meant to love you hurt you?

"My daughter thinks what he has done is disgusting and all my family are upset.

"I have had the absolute s*** kicked out of me. All I did was love him.

It's not the physical abuse, it's the mental abuse.

"He is a kind of Jekyll and Hyde character."
