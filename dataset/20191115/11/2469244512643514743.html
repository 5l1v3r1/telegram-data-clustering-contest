Number of councillors who failed to fill in register of interest has reduced

Last month Devon Live reported that up to 13 councillors could face prosecution

15 Nov 2019, 11:08 by Lewis Clarke

The number of councillors who have failed to hand in their register of interests has ‘reduced dramatically.’

Last month The Gazette reported that up to 13 councillors could face prosecution for not completing their register of interest.

Section 34 of the Localism Act 2011 stated that it was an offence without reasonable excuse to fail to comply with the obligation to submit a register of interests. The register should be submitted within 28-days of a person being elected.

Speaking at a Full Council meeting on Wednesday, November 6, Councillor Clive Eginton said: “I am pleased to say that following the monitoring officer having written to a number of parish councillors who had outstanding matters relating to the register of interests, the number has reduced dramatically.

“We are heading in the right direction, but having said that, there are still several who are outstanding.”
