Notts County players risk fine for bad parking as boss Neal Ardley reveals desire for 'structure and discipline'

Ardley eager for high standards off the pitch as well as on it

15 Nov 2019, 11:01 by Leigh Curtis

Neal Ardley has revealed his Notts County players are fined double if they are late on matchdays - and says even bad parking will see them hit in the pocket.

Earlier this week, it emerged how Chelsea’s squad are fined huge amounts of money if they break Frank Lampard’s rules which includes a whopping £20,000 for being late for training.

That kind of money will not be changing hands at Meadow Lane given the gulf in earning power between the National League and the top flight.

But Ardley is still eager to have high standards on and off the pitch this season with Magpies defender Ben Turner tasked with collecting money from the offenders.

That is then saved, totted up and used for a team night out in the season and Ardley says it is a useful way of imposing discipline particularly in terms of players’ timekeeping.

“Every time you set the fines it’s about getting that structurer and discipline,” he said.

“The obvious one is being late to try and make sure the boys are always on time.

“We are quite ruthless with that. It doubles on matchday.

“So being late is a big one. There are also little ones like parking outside of the bays at the ground.

“If you are over a line and somebody else can’t park in the other space, then it’s annoying for others.

“Not having a drinks bottle out on the training pitch is also a fine. There’s many others on the list.

“But with Big Ben collecting them, there shouldn’t be too many arguments.

“I am sure I am on the list of people who can be fined, but I think they have to be very careful if they want to come for the manager!”

As the man responsible for collecting the fines, Turner joked: "I am a nice guy and the lads know I am fair.

"It doesn't matter if it's an 18-year-old who is fined £10 for whatever, or somebody like Michael Doyle who is one of our most senior players, a fine is a fine.

"If you break the rules, take your medicine and get it paid. Then we will have a decent pot for all of us to do something socially."
