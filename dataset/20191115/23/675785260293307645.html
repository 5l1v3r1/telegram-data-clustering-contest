Thom Evans has 'sworn off sex' so he's not distracted from X Factor dream

Rugby Hunk Thom has decided to abstain from 'indulging in women' until the end of the celebrity singing competition

15 Nov 2019, 23:43 by Tiffany Wallis

Rugby player Thom Evans is reportedly abstaining from sex throughout his Celebrity X Factor experience.

The hunky sportsman, 34, is currently competing in Simon Cowell 's spin-off singing competition alongside fellow Rugby stars Ben Foden and Levi Davis in their band Try Star.

Thom recently told The Sun that he is so dedicated to Try Star's singing success, that he has decided to steer clear of any romance.

He also admitted that he continues to receive a lot of sexual and romantic advances from both men and women.

The band member confessed to the publication: "I have lots of men and women sliding into my DMs. I always try to reply."

However, the star revealed he is only into the old-school way of dating and refuses to "date anyone from Instagram or an app."

Shockingly, the X Factor hopeful also hinted at potential romances with his fellow singing contestants on the show, but said he is choosing not to "indulge".

He teased: "I’m single and there are lots of lovely girls on X Factor but I’ve been focused on the band.

"It’s been pretty stressful learning the songs so I’ve no time to indulge in women."

Thom's severe dedication has come at a cost as he decided to give up his love of women in hope of winning the show and earning a record contract.

Try Star have been wowing the judges from day one, when they stole the show during auditions with a very British and very unexpected rendition of Blackstreet's iconic No Diggity.

The rugby boys are adamant they are in the competition to win it and want to be taken seriously.

Thom's honesty about his love life comes after the star suffered an awkward clash with Louis Walsh.

Last week, Thom didn't take too kindly to being called a "backing singer" along with Ben, over their latest performance with co-member Levi Davis.

The trio were forced to change their song last minute as mentor Simon Cowell wasn't a fan, so they instead performed No Diggity for the second time.

Louis blasted this decision, before claiming Levi was the star and his co-members were just "backing singers".

The X Factor: Celebrity continues Saturday evenings on ITV.
