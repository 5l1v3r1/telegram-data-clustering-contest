Plymouth stabbing: Young man seriously injured as police shut down area

A 23-year-old man has been arrested following the incident in Alexandra Road

15 Nov 2019, 23:34 by Katie Timms

A young man in his 20s is being treated for a "serious stab wound" after a large-scale police incident this evening.

Multiple residents contacted Plymouth Live after the National Police Air Service helicopter was seen and heard over the city at around 10.30pm.

Devon and Cornwall Police has now confirmed that officers were called to an address in the Devonport area at around 10.15pm this evening, after "one male was reported to have been stabbed".

Live updates on the incident can be found here.

The police Force Incident Manager, who oversees all live incidents in the region, said: "On arrival, officers found an injured male who appeared to have a serious stab wound.

"He is being treated at the scene by paramedics."

He confirmed that a 23-year-old man, local to Plymouth, was arrested in relation to the incident and has been taken into custody.

The FIM added: "The male fled the scene and was eventually detained at a licensed premises close by."

The helicopter, which was heard over Stoke, St Levan Park, over the Tamar Bridge, Plympton, St Budeaux, Keyham and North Prospect, has been stood down.

Officers remain at the scene, securing the area and carrying out their enquiries.

Anyone with information regarding the incident can contact police via phone on 101, or via email on 101@dcpolice.uk, quoting log 919 of November 15.

Alternatively, you can call Crimestoppers on 0800 555 111 and report information anonymously.
