'FULL FORCE 100MPH'

Burnistoun was tame compared to new sketch show The State of It, says Glasgow writer Robert Florence

15 Nov 2019, 23:16 by Colan Lamont

ROBERT Florence admits that Burnistoun was a bit mad - but he reckons his new TV show is truly mental.

The State Of It sees the Glasgow-based comedy writer ripping into the state of today’s Scotland and the world at large.

Featuring the type of sketches that made him famous, it would be easy to compare it with the hit that made he and pal Iain Connell famous a decade ago.

But the 42-year-old insists Jolly Boy John and The Quality Polis will look tame in comparison when his new effort hits the box next week.

Robert says: “We are looking at stuff going on and making jokes about it. We go quite hard in some places but pick and choose our targets.

“It’s one of silliest shows. Burnistoun is really daft in places but The State Of It does some really different things

“The show is an odd one, full force 100mph stuff.”

In the first episode, The State of It sets its sights on technology and meets cutting edge beauty influencer, Kendra, who shares her corned beef master plan for world domination.

Also, the team look at modern day sperm donation, genetic engineering by post and an ingenious music streaming service. Elsewhere, resident animators Will and Ainsley consider crypto currency.

Scot Squad creator Joe Hullait is involved on the show both behind and in front of the camera.

And Robert loved being able to act out sketches that he didn’t write himself.

He says: “I loved being in sketches written by other people because it’s quite a freeing thing to not be thinking that you should have wrote it another way after the fact.

“Burnistoun was a weird one beacuse with a sketch show like that, you feel pressure when you’re doing it.

“It’s always fun but there’s pressure because it was established and people liked the sketches.

“But the lucky thing with a long running sketch show like us is people forget all the s***e ones and just remember all the stuff as being brilliant and forget the ones that failed.

“The idea in their head is that episodes were much better than they actually were.

“That’s why it was nice to do something brand new. It was a clean slate and I loved it.”

When the new BBC Scotland channel launched in February, it was promised that it would be bursting with new Scottish talent.

Since then, some people have claimed its shows featured the same old faces instead of newbies.

But The State Of It really does give the stars of tomorrow their first big break on telly.

Alongside Robert, comic talent like Susan Riddell, Nathan Byrne, Rachel Jackson, Laura Lovemore and Bigg Taj plus many more are mainstays.

When Burnistoun was at its peak, Robert and Iain never accepted submissions from aspiring writers.

Now he wants to make up for what he reckons is one of his only regrets from the show.

He says: “This is ultimately about developing new talent, that’s why I got involved. It wasn’t my idea. They came to me.

“What interested me was helping with that. It’s a massively important thing when you get on the telly for the first time as a writer or performer.

“Just the fact that your family sees you on telly justifies the work you’re doing.

“The show is an odd one, full force 100mph stuff.”Robert Florence

“I’ve been doing this 20 years now which is too long. I should be long gone by now so I end up fans of all the people coming in.

“When I got into the game there was a radio show Velvet Cabaret on BBC Radio Scotland looking for writers and Chewin’ the Fat were also accepting submissions.

“But then there was a long spell without any opportunities.

“And I have to shoulder part of the blame myself because me and Iain wrote Burnistoun ourselves and werent accepting any submissions which I reflect on a wee bit and think it’s ashame.”

Robert adds: “For me it’s not so much about making the next big show like Burnistoun, it’s more about giving other people a chance to make that dream a reality and get on the telly.

“People bang on about ratings but miss the big picture, there’s all these hours on the telly that need filled by people working.

“I hope people watch enough of The State Of It so we can get to other people in a second season.

“I’d do this show forever if we keep bringing new people through.

“People shouldn’t be jumping through hopes, we need to recognise talent and push them.

“I hope people see this show and realise they could definitely be on it.

“Watch us, look at the credits and contact the producers and names you see.

“Anybody who wants to do comedy, particulary women and people from different backgrounds, we need to broaden the diversity out.”

Away from telly screens, Robert is also dominating the airwaves.

He recently launched the Wrestling Daft podcast alongside Scot Squad and wrestling star co-host Grado.

The well known wrestling fan - whose niece Isla Dawn peforms in WWE - couldn’t turn down the opportunity.

Robert says: “I’ve known Grado for years. He started the football daft podcast which has been going down really well and they said to him about a wrestling and he suggested me as co-presenter.

“The minute he asked me I said aye. I just love talking about it.”

The State of It will be on BBC Scotland on Friday November 22 at 10.30pm.
