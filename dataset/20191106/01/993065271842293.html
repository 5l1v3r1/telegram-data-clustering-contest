BACK IN THE DAY

The Irish News, November 6 2009: Jamie Conlan’s decision to switch pro after a multi-title winning amateur career faces a first exam with a scheduled four-round debut at flyweight against Kuwait-born Anwar Alfadi at the Odyssey Arena, Belfast tonight.

He campaigns out of Sheffield and is without a win. Alfadi has drawn two of five contests.

One of the contests shared was with Belfast’s Luke Wilton over six rounds.

Wilton, who opened with three wins, is 1-2-1 in his last four and is matched with fellow novice Kevin Colgan (1-0).

Conlan’s super-middle stable mate at Breen’s Gym, Joe Rea (6-1-1-1NC), opens the show. Rea’s only loss to date was to Martin Murray in the Prizefighter series last November. His opponent is debut-maker Philip Townley.

Neil Sinclair (32-7) contests his 40th pro bout. Budapest’s Janos Petrovics (18-28) opposes.

ON THIS DAY

1986: Manchester United appointed Alex Ferguson as manager. He went on to become the most successful manager in English football history and was knighted for services to football after leading the club to an unprecedented treble in 1999. He retired in 2013.

1998: The Football Association fined Paul Ince for his V-sign as he was sent off in England's 2-1 Euro 2000 qualifying defeat to Sweden.

2003: Dwain Chambers was facing a minimum two-year ban from athletics after it was revealed his B sample test for designer steroid THG was positive.

2006: Paul Azinger was named United States Ryder Cup captain for the 2008 event at Valhalla in Louisville, Kentucky.

2007: Yossi Benayoun scored a hat-trick as Liverpool thumped Turkish side Besiktas 8-0 at Anfield in a Champions League group game.

2011: Hibernian sacked manager Colin Calderwood following a poor run of results. Pat Fenlon was later confirmed as his replacement.

2012: Ronnie O'Sullivan announced his decision not to play on the professional snooker tour for the season due to personal reasons.

2017: Former Italy, AC Milan and Juventus midfielder Andrea Pirlo formally announced his retirement from professional football after a trophy-laden 22-year career.

2018: England debutant Ben Foakes hit an unbeaten 87 as England recovered from 103 for five to reach stumps on 321 for eight at the end of day one of the first Test against Sri Lanka in Galle.
