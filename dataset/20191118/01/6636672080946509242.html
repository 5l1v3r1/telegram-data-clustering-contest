Ed Gets The Bum’s Rush

18 Nov 2019, 01:06 by David Manners

We have the works on our opponents – policies, strategies, campaign appearances, dirty tricks, media events, scandal revelations – the lot, Ed confides to his diary, it’s like cracking Enigma – we know exactly what the opposition are going to do next.

All thanks to Greaser’s cyber-spy outfit Scunthorpe Systematica which penetrated their system while feinting a Denial of Service attack. The PM promises me a Dukedom if this works out but the PM’s promises are for show not to go.

Thanks to a heads-up from SS about the opposition’s broadband plans, Greaser is now preparing to IPO his trench digging subsidiary, FossLux, which, thanks to me, has received government approval for fibre installation. 

The publicity from both main parties’ broadband initiatives is going to push FossLux’s value sky-high.  Whoever wins this election – gigabit broadband is now seen by the public as a right.

I get Greaser on the blower.

“If we lose the election, Greaser, I shall be available to help with the FossLux IPO.”

“How, Ed?” asks Greaser.

“I would be prepared to serve on the FossLux board.”

“And how would that help the FossLux IPO, Ed?”

“Having a Peer of the Realm, a former Cabinet Minister and an ex technology CEO on the board would give the IPO greater credibility, Greaser.”

“Really Ed? Are pols seen as trustworthy? Or are they seen as opportunistic, on the make, chancers? Having you on the board would be a negative.” 

“Steady on Greaser, I have contacts, influence, friends . . . . . .”

“Who will all be useless if you lose the election, Ed. We’ll need friends in the new government. Your association with us would be harmful.”

“But we’re old muckers, Greaser.”

“True Ed, but business is business.”
