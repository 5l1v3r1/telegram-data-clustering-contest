Dad arrested after son, 5, "told teachers Spiderman heroin turned him into a superhero"

Benny Garcia, of Massachusetts, US, was arrested when police officers searched his flat - having been called to his son's school where the boy had reportedly shown off a bag of white powder

18 Nov 2019, 01:54 by Milo Boyd, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/milo-boyd/

A five-year-old went into school with a bag of heroin and told his teachers he becomes a superhero when he takes it, it's claimed.

On Thursday police officers were called to H.B. Lawrence Elementary School in Massachusetts, US, to reports a child had brought a powdery substance to school.

The young boy had been showing off a plastic bag stamped with a picture of Spider-Man, with an unknown substance inside.

The boy told his teacher that when he eats or tastes the powder he turns into a superhero, Assistant District Attorney Matthew Green later told a court.

He allegedly told the members of staff that he found the bag in his living room at home.

At one point the child put the bag in his mouth, prompting an adult to take him to the Baystate Medical centre in Springfield.

Luckily his health turned out to be in good order.

While the boy was receiving medical treatment officers contacted the pupil's mother who gave them permission to search her apartment.

There they found Benny Garcia sleeping on his bed, which had bags containing white powder underneath, police say.

More than 200 bags of heroin and cocaine were taken from the property, court documents state.

Four walkie-talkies and chargers were also found in a kitchen pantry.

"The officers located the same sort of baggie (of) heroin stamped ‘Spider-Man’ that the child brought to school in a dresser drawer of the defendant’s bedroom," Mr Green said.

"When Garcia discovered why officers were inside home and the incident involving his son, he became ‘very emotional."

The man's son and another child were taken in by the Department of Children and Families.

"He loves Spider-Man," Holyoke Police Lt. James Albert told the Daily Hampshire Gazette.

"Our officers were quite taken by it all. You don’t often deal with this."

Garcia, 29, pleaded not guilty in Holyoke District Court on drug possession and distribution charges and a charge of reckless endangerment of a child.

He was arrested Thursday on a warrant on a 2018 drug possession charge.

Judge William Rota ordered Garcia to be held until a dangerousness hearing scheduled for November 20 and revoked his bail on the previous charges.
