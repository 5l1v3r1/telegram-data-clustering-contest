First Quantum Minerals restart sees hundreds of jobs

18 Nov 2019, 02:00 by Jake Dietsch

More than 100 employees have been recruited and are working at the restarted Ravensthorpe Nickel Operation.

The recruitment campaign is continuing with more than 350 roles still to be filled at the site.

A spokesperson for Canadian-based mining company First Quantum Minerals said the company had received a "substantial response" to its recruitment campaign.

The company has appointed Anthony Mukutuma to be the general manager of the restart.

Mr Mukutuma said he was delighted with the progress towards the restart.

"There is a lot of work involved in recruiting a substantial team and preparing the operations for production again, even though the plant and equipment has been kept in excellent condition by our maintenance team," he said.

Mr Mukutuma said local businesses in the region would start to feel the benefits with new families arriving into Hopetoun and other parts of the region.

"We hope that nearby towns like Hopetoun and Ravensthorpe will soon benefit from our activity and that there is also a noticeable positive impact on larger communities like Esperance and Albany," he said.

"Our goal is to ensure we are locally focused and benefit the entire region by making the nickel mine cost-efficient and commercially resilient."

First Quantum Minerals is expected to resume mining operations in Ravensthorpe in early 2020.

The mine is located about 35 kilometres east of Ravensthorpe and was placed into care and maintenance in October of 2017.

The closure was a major blow to the Esperance community. There were 500 jobs lost as a result and a quarter of these were at the Esperance Port.
