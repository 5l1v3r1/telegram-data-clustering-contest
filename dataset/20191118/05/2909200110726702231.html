Photo credits: femalebossclub.com, rebellesociety.com

Last year, an article on Entrepreneur.com brought the question of women working with fellow women to the fore by suggesting many women tend to avoid working with their fellow women for many reasons, the main one being perceived incivility.

There are so many forms incivility can take in the workplace, and while men report experiencing incivility perpetrated by women and men at the same rate, women say that they experience incivility from their own sex more often. Across three studies focused on gender and workplace incivility, researchers found that women were 5 to 9 percent more likely to experience incivility from other women than from men, according to Marcus Butts, an associate professor in the Cox School of Business at Southern Methodist University, who co-authored that research.

People tend to compare themselves to others who are similar to them in terms of demographics. As a result, women tend to be hyper-aware of the actions of other women, compared to the actions of their male counterparts.

92% women disapprove of women bosses/supervisors

Over 90 percent of hundreds of women sampled in a social media survey strongly opposed working under a female boss but would rather work with a male boss.

92% of the women said if they had their way, they would never have to work under a female boss or supervisor ever in their lives.

Out of this, only 3 percent of the women polled said they wouldn’t mind working under a female, but if only they had no option.
