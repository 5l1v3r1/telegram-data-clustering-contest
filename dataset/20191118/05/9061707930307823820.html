All you need to know about Christmas lights switch-on events across the area

Wrap up warm to usher in the start of the festive season

18 Nov 2019, 05:00 by Jenny Moody

Christmas is just around the corner and soon towns and villages across the country will be filled with brightly coloured lights.

The annual switching on of the festive lights is always an occasion to look forward to as people gather to see the button pressed for the official start of Christmas.

Some places bring in celebrities to turn on the lights, while in others enlist the mayor or another prominent character to flick the switch honour.

Across East Staffordshire and South Derbyshire, we will see fireworks, performers, fairs and Christmas markets adding to the celebrations as families wrap up warm to brave the cold.

And we have done a round-up of when towns and villages across the area will be switching on.

Anyone who would like their lights included in the round-up can email jennifer.moody@reachplc.com.

Light switch-ons in 2019

  1. Burton

    The family of young mum and student paramedic Tammy Minshall will be switching on the Burton Christmas lights on Sunday, November 24.

    Lynn and Andrew Minshall, of Stretton, were left bereft when their daughter Tammy was killed in July this year while on duty in an ambulance she was travelling in.

    The 31-year-old was a devoted mum to Eva and was in her first year of training as a student paramedic.

    She died doing what she loved.

    Although the family is facing the pain of their first Christmas without "Tam", as she was known, they have said they are "overwhelmed" to have been asked to kick off the festive celebrations in Burton by turning on the lights.

    The Christmas switch-on will take place in the Market Place.

    The day will start with a Christmas market and entertainment from 2pm, followed by the lights switch on and fireworks display at 5pm.

  2. Swadlincote

    Plans are gathering pace for this year's Christmas lights switch-on in Swadlincote.

    The date has been set for Friday, November 29 and organisers promise a "sparkling programme of entertainment".

    The full details of the event have not yet been revealed.

  3. Barton Marina

    Hollyoaks heart throb Danny Mac, who also appeared on Strictly Come Dancing with Oti Mabuse in 2016, will be dreaming of a white Christmas as he heads to Barton Marina to switch on the lights.

    They will be turned on at 5pm on Sunday, December 1.

    The star will also strolling the marina promenade, welcoming Father Christmas and one of his reindeer, Dasher, before flicking the switch.

    Caroline Kerr, the marketing manager at Barton Marina, said: "With his insane talent, nothing stands still in Danny's world.

    "Since reaching the finals of Strictly Come Dancing, his career has skyrocketed.

    "He's just finished a run in Amelie the Musical and from mid-November he'll be starring in London's West End revival of White Christmas at the Dominion Theatre.

    "No doubt he'll be popping into the marina shops looking for seasonal gifts."

  4. Tutbury

    The East Staffordshire village will be holding its annual Christmas Festival on Friday, November 22. The lights will be switched on during the event.

    The event will include a festive markets filled with stalls and organisers say it is always a popular event in the village.

    Drivers are warned High Street will be closed from noon until midnight on the day for the event so it can be set up and, later, cleared away.

  5. Uttoxeter

    The switching on of Uttoxeter's Christmas lights will take place at Uttoxeter Town Hall at 6pm on Friday, November 29.

    The lights will be switched on by town mayor Councillor Sue McGarry, assisted by children from Uttoxeter's first schools.

    Father Christmas and his reindeer will be in attendance, along with carol singing from Fountains Signing Choir.

    High Street will host a Christmas fair from 5pm to 7pm, weather permitting, which has been organised by the Uttoxeter Knights.
