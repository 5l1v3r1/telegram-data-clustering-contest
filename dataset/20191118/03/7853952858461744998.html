Li Ka Shing Foundation launches 2nd phase grants for retailers

18 Nov 2019, 03:01 by Raymond Tsoi

The Li Ka Shing Foundation, the charitable organization founded by Hong Kong’s richest man Li Ka-shing, will start accepting applications on Monday for the second batch of grants under a HK$1 billion program to help small businesses and entrepreneurs that have suffered due to the months-long social unrest in Hong Kong.

Aiming to assist small and medium-scale enterprises, complementing the government’s own relief measures, the Li Ka Shing Foundation, after discussions with industry representatives. has identified five categories of retailers most affected by the civic disturbances: 

1. Clothing, footwear and allied products

2. Electrical goods and other consumer durable goods

3. Books, newspapers, stationery and gifts

4. Chinese drugs and herbs

5. Medicines and cosmetics

Retailers involved in these of business segments can apply for HK$60,000 grants, along with paperwork that includes Mandatory Provident Fund contributions statements. Those that cannot provide such statements and retailers who fall outside the five categories can apply for a lower amount of aid, at HK$30,000.

Total amount of grants available will be HK$600 million under the second phase of the relief program from the Li charitable foundation. Only retail outlets having physical stores can apply for the grants.

If the number of eligible applicants exceeds the quota, successful applicants will be randomly selected by computer.

Online applications can be submitted from 9 am today, until 5 pm on Nov. 24. Successful applicants will receive the grants before mid-December, according to a statement from the foundation.

Phase one of the program that targeted the food and beverage industry had received more than 10,000 applicants as of Sunday noon.

As the eligible applications exceeded the quota of HK$200 million under that phase, the winners will be selected by computer randomly, and the money will be disbursed beginning Nov. 26.

Successful applicants will receive an SMS notification, and a cashier’s order will be posted or couriered to the registered address.
