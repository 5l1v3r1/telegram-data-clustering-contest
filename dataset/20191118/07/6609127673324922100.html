TOP SCOT

Ayrshire dad claims top Euro spot at World’s Toughest Mudder after entering just FIVE days before race

18 Nov 2019, 07:40 by Ewan Paton

A SUPER FIT Scots dad finished fifth at the famously gruelling World's Toughest Mudder – entering just FIVE days before racing.

Scott Brown, 37, from Irvine smashed 90 miles in 24 continuous hours of energy-sapping competition in America over Saturday and Sunday.

He finished fifth overall in the world standings, but claimed the number one spot for the top European performance among the hundreds taking part.

The dad of three had NO endurance training prior to taking part in Atlanta because he was recovering from a knee injury, ruling him out of running for months.

However, a little bit of adversity is nothing for the Ayrshireman, who trains hard at his gym in Tarbolton.

He told The Scottish Sun how the decision came about to jump straight into the notoriously intense, ultimate fitness test.

He said: “It couldn’t have gone better and it was worth every pain I’m feeling now.

“It was all about who was willing to suffer the most over 24 hours.

“I’m proud of myself and I can’t thank everyone at home enough for getting behind me.

“I’m glad to put Scotland on the map once more.

“I only decided to go on Monday (November 11), so wasn’t sure how it would go. It exceeded my expectations.

“This year I wasn’t going to go at all as I did the West Highland Race in June, which was 97 miles and I finished 7th which I was over the moon with.

“The next thing I rushed back into running and hurt my knee because I was stupid and didn’t allow myself the proper rest.

“So I spent about 13 weeks just doing gym work on my upper body.

"The last five weeks I’ve been injury free.

“I was looking through Facebook memories on Monday and it came up with pictures from last year.

“I tore my pec seven hours into the competition but I carried on and finished eighth, which I think was a great result in the circumstances.

“But I looked through the comments and I messaged my wife Kerri to ask if I could go again, and she said ‘you’re off your head’.

“She thought the voice of reason would be my running coach Kasey.

“So I messaged him to say ‘you’re gonna think I’m mad but can I go and do World’s Toughest Mudder, you know I’m pretty good at it’ and he told me to go for it.

“We knew I wasn’t going to be in great condition for it but I wanted to enjoy it.

“So I booked the flights, got my gym covered for the weekend and off I went.”

The gym and fitness bootcamp fanatic managed 18 laps of the five-mile assault course.

The winner, Trevor Cichosz, from USA, won with 21 laps, completing 105 miles in total.

However, Scott, who served nine years in the British Army, was amazed by the generosity of one of his competitors.

He explained: “I usually take friends and family with me but because it was such short notice I couldn’t organise that.

“Although one of the American athletes, his team helped me out with my pit, which is a small tented area to get changed and to eat.

“I’ve known him for some time from previous runs, so even although we were rivals for the top places, it was good to have that kind support.”

Scott runs his gym on a full-time basis, along with his SBF bootcamp in Dundonald some weeknights.

His social media post about his fantastic news raked up over 1,000 likes on Facebook.

While he knows to learn from past mistakes, he isn’t ruling out a return to compete for a fifth time next year.

He said: “I started the race too fast I think – I was flying. I started getting cramps through the lack of training.

“But it goes past fitness and it’s down to who is willing to suffer the most, and I don’t think I left the top seven spots the full race.

“I feel physically fine and in good health – just aches and pains.

“In 2015 I finished fifth as well, 2012 which was my first year, I was tenth and then last year I went and ended eighth.

“I say I’m not going back every year because it’s in America but I’m not going to think like that this time.

“I’ve got a 214-mile race in June 2020, it’s a race across Scotland, so that takes my next focus.

“I’ll give my body recover over Christmas first and enjoy some family time with my wife and daughters and then I’ll get back into the swing of things to prepare.”
