Illegal rave in disused Carpetright warehouse raided by police

18 Nov 2019, 07:24

A man has been arrested after more than 1,000 people descended on a disused Carpetright warehouse store for an illegal rave.

Police were called to the premises in Strood Retail Park, Kent, at 11.30pm on Saturday, following reports of anti-social behaviour.

Up to 10 police vehicles were spotted outside, and officers remained on-site to prevent any ‘potential breach of peace’ throughout the evening.

A video circulating on social media showed huge crowds dancing inside the rave, which had been kitted out with professional sound and lights systems.

One person can be heard saying in the clip: ‘We’ve finished work, gone to McDonald’s in Strood and hear about this illegal rave at Carpetright – this is crazy.’

Another video showed revellers being moved along from where they were sitting on the ground inside the retail park.

A Kent Police spokesperson confirmed that a man, 39, from Ashford was arrested on suspicion of conspiring to commit a public order offence.

He was also arrested on suspicion of supplying a psychoactive substance (nitrous oxide).

The spokesperson said: ‘It was established that a suspected unlicensed music event was being held inside a disused warehouse with a significant number of people in attendance.

‘Officers remained at the location to prevent any potential breach of the peace, ensure public safety and identify if any criminal offences had been committed.’

Enquiries are still ongoing.
