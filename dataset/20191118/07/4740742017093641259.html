Council could lose £1.6million if Portishead road widening cannot be completed by March

The project to widen the major commuter route in and out of Portishead has been delayed by five months

18 Nov 2019, 07:00 by Heather Pickstock

A £1.6 million pound scheme to ease congestion in and out of Portishead needs to be completed in the next four months – or funding for the project will be lost.

North Somerset Council is facing a race against time to complete the scheme to widen Wyndham Way by March next year.

The scheme has been mainly funded by the Government as part of its national congestion relief scheme.

A caveat of the funding was the work had to be completed by the end of March 2020.

Work on the widening was due to start in May but was delayed.

And North Somerset Council has only just agreed to appoint Skanska to carry out the work.

Contractors now have just four months – with the threat of bad weather and Christmas holidays in the way – to meet the deadline.

The scheme will see the A369 Wyndham Way widened between the Sheepway and the 'sail' roundabout to create a second lane for traffic heading into the town.

The move will increase capacity for traffic heading into Portishead at rush hour and reduce journey times.

Large tailbacks can often build up into the town from commuters who work out of the area but live in Portishead.

The outbound road leaving the town would remain as a single carriageway.

Council bosses have admitted the delays and tight timescale which has ensued is ‘not ideal’ but are ‘confident’ the work will be completed in time.

North Somerset Council spokesman Richard Turner said: “A decision notice has been approved for our contractor Skanska to carry out the work.

“We are now working with Skanska to agree a detailed schedule of work.

“We expect work to start in early December and are confident it will be completed by the end of March.”

The delay in work commencing is due to changes being made to the plan following a consultation on the improvements earlier this year.

Initially it was expected the scheme would cost £1 million.

But the costs have now increased as a result of the changes to £1.6 million.

Extra funding of £380,000 has come from Highways England with the other money coming from 106 contributions from local developers and from the Local Transport Plan.

The work to Wyndham Way is part of a wider scheme which will also see improvements to the M5 Portishead motorway junction carried out by Highways England.

A scheme is currently being drawn up to put in an additional lane on the M5 sliproad onto the junction 19 roundabout.

The work will see the number of lanes on the southbound motorway exit slip road at junction 19 increased from two to three.

The project is being drawn up to tackle the large queues that form on the inside lane of the southbound carriageway on the Avonmouth Bridge.

At peak times - such as morning and evening rush hour - queues can often stretch the length of the bridge and as far back as the Avonmouth junction.

As a result some motorists are trying to cut in from other lanes, causing accidents.

The work to junction 19 is due to start this autumn and be completed by March 2020.
