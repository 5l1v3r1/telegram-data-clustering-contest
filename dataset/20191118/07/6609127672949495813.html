FRESH START

Rangers star Ryan Jack hopes Cyprus performance will kickstart Scotland career

18 Nov 2019, 07:18 by David Friel

RYAN JACK won his first Scotland cap in November 2017.

Two years on, he hopes his international career is really starting now.

The Rangers midfielder was excellent as Scotland secured a 2-1 win in Cyprus.

Jack has had to be patient over the last 24 months — due to fierce competition in his position.

But he looked the part as he won his third cap and now wants to nail down a place in Steve Clarke’s side.

Jack said: “I hope it is starting now. I’ve always said I want to play for Scotland and be a part of it so I hope that is the case.

“I hope I did enough here to get the nod again in the future. Obviously I can’t decide that, but I enjoyed every minute of it against Cyprus. It was good to be in there.

"I’ve come away with the squad, worked hard and the gaffer has been really good with me. It’s obviously a proud moment every time you get called up or get a cap.

“Growing up, you always want to play for your country. I am proud to do so. You see games at Hampden when there are full houses and the atmosphere that creates.

“You want to be part of it and I don’t want to stop. I want to keep going and I want more.

“Hopefully, this can be a box ticked and I get the nod in the future. It was good, I enjoyed it.

“It was obviously a tough night and a tough test. But it was job done and we got the win we wanted.

“There are no easy games at this level but I felt I settled in fine. First half, we did well. Second half, they scored and that was a bit of a sickener.

“But we showed a great attitude to come back and get in front again. The manager did say that if we face adversity we have to stand up to it.

“We did that so, all round, I’m delighted we got the job done.”

Jack combined well with Celtic rival Callum McGregor in central midfield, with John McGinn playing as a No 10.

Scott McTominay was injured and seems his direct competitor but the Rangers man doesn’t think it’s that simple.

He said: “I have played with Callum before for the Under-21s. It was good. I enjoyed playing with everyone to be honest.

“I felt we dug in well when we had to and we showed a bit of resilience. Is it a case of me v Scott? Not at all. If you look at the midfield there is talent right through it.

“Kenny McLean and Scott are out with injuries. Then you have myself, Callum, John McGinn, John Fleck, Stuart Armstrong.

“The list is endless because there is that much talent in the one position.

“Every time I get a chance, I just want to stake a claim. That’s all I can do.

“I’m sure the other boys are the same when they play.

“We are all working for the same goal and want to get to a major tournament.”

Jack has excelled against Porto and Feyenoord for Rangers this season — so Cyprus were never going to scare him.

But the Ibrox midfielder believes more Europa League games have developed him.

He said: “The experience I have gained playing in Europe always helps, of course it does.

"I am playing for a good team at club level and I am facing good opposition in the likes of Porto and Feyenoord.

“That does help when I come away on these trips. I am hoping for a successful season for club and country. Everything is going well so far.

“Club level has been very good. I have been doing well in Europe and now I’ve got a start for my country after being called up.

“As I said, I really enjoyed it and I am just glad that we got the win.”

Jack looks set to keep his place for Kazakhstan tomorrow as Scotland attempt to secure third place in Group I.

Another win would help boost confidence ahead of the March play-offs but Clarke’s men aren’t taking anything for granted.

Jack said: “It’s all positive. And we are looking to now take that into Tuesday night. We all want to build momentum and get wins.

“It has not all been positive in this campaign, but hopefully we can go three wins on the spin on Tuesday night.

“Now you want to put in the performances to stake a claim for the huge games in March.

“If we can get the wins and build momentum then hopefully we will go into the play-offs with a real chance.

“Getting the win in Cyprus was great but in football you know that everything can turn very quickly.

“You can’t rest on it. It’s about more hard work and keeping things going. We can’t get carried away by anything now.

"We have won two on the bounce.

“But we want to get three and then try and get Scotland to a major tournament, which would be a great achievement for us.”
