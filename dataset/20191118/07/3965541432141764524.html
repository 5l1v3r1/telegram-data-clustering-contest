'Secret Sister' online gift exchanges are a scam, BBB warns

18 Nov 2019, 07:35 by MCKENZIE STAUFFER | KUTV Staff

SALT LAKE CITY (KUTV) -- It's almost that time of year to buy gifts for "Secret Santa."

But, beware of secret sister gift exchanges with friends you haven't met.

These gift exchanges may seem like innocent fun, but they are really pyramid schemes, according to the Better Business Bureau.

The "Secret Sister" gift exchange became popular in 2015 through posts on Facebook. Participants were promised up to 36 gifts, in exchange for a single gift, valued at $10.

Users were encouraged to invite others to participate in the holiday gift exchange and promised information on where to send the gifts.

The cycle continued and participants were left buying and shipping gifts, in the hopes of the favor being reciprocated. Unfortunately, that doesn't happen, according to the BBB.

The U.S. Postal Inspection Services says these gift exchanges are considered a form of gambling and that participants could be subject to penalties such as jail time, fines or a lawsuit for mail fraud.
