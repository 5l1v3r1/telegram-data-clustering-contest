Prince Andrew got Queen's approval for interview, says Emily Maitlis

18 Nov 2019, 08:50

Prince Andrew may have got the Queen’s approval for his explosive Newsnight interview, Emily Maitlis has said.

The Duke of York allegedly told the presenter he had to ‘seek approval from higher up’ before sitting down to talk about his friendship with convicted paedophile Jeffrey Epstein.

Writing in The Times today, Ms Maitlis stated that he then came back to the team at ‘8am the next day’ to say that the interview could go ahead.

She said: ‘We have finished laying out our pitch. An awkward moment of silence falls. And the duke tells us he must “seek approval from higher up”.

‘It dawns on us then that he means the Queen herself. At 8am the next day we have a message telling us to call his office. The Queen, it seems, is on board.’

Ms Maitlis then later mused about the Queen’s apparent approval as she arrived at Buckingham Palace, where the interview took place.

She questioned ‘the significance’ of the monarch lending ‘her own formal quarters’ for the filming, stating: ‘It feels like a code I do not properly understand.

‘Is she endorsing her son? The need for this interview? Or am I reading way too much into every step, merely because there are so many of them?’

Ms Maitlis’ article comes just as The Telegraph reported that the Duke of York had operated ‘in silo’ without the Queen’s approval.

A source said Her Majesty was only made aware of the interview after it had already been set up.

They continued: ‘The statement Buckingham Palace issued said the Queen was aware of the interview but not that she approved it.

‘It does seem as if the Duke of York’s private office is operating in silo, which is really quite dangerous because there is a lack of accountability there. Internally, this is being seen as a f**k up.’

According to Ms Maitlis, Newsnight approached Prince Andrew for an interview a year ago, but the Palace only returned their interest in May this year.

However, they declined the offer ‘out of principle’ at first, after the duke’s team vetoed any conversation about Epstein.

It wasn’t until the sex offender and entrepreneur was found dead in his cell in August that talks of the interview resumed.

Prince Andrew was named in the legal deposition of Virginia Roberts, who claimed she was trafficked to him for sex at the age of 17.

Ms Maitlis said of the accusations: ‘The Palace knew they had a problem. They had sent out statements vigorously denying the claims.

‘But those perhaps lacked the conviction of a human voice behind them.’

During the interview, broadcast on Saturday, the duke denied having sex with Ms Roberts, telling Ms Maitlis: ‘I have no recollection of meeting her.’

He refuted her claims that he’d been ‘profusely sweating’ during their encounter, telling the presenter it was ‘almost impossible’ for him to perspire due to a medical condition.

The duke said: ‘I have a peculiar medical condition which is that I don’t sweat or I didn’t sweat at the time.

‘…I didn’t sweat at the time because I had suffered what I would describe as an overdose of adrenaline in the Falklands War when I was shot at and I simply… it was almost impossible for me to sweat.’

He also said he had been at ‘Pizza Express in Woking’ on March 10 2001 – the date the allegations were said to have taken place.
