Police investigate after van is set on fire

Fire service called out in early hours of this morning

18 Nov 2019, 08:18 by Max Channon

Police in Plymouth are investigating a van fire in the early hours of this morning.

Firefighters were called to Northesk Street at Stoke Village at 12.33am by a resident who initially thought a house was on fire.

A spokesperson for Devon and Somerset Fire and Rescue Service said: "A fire appliance from Camels Head dealt with a van fire on a private driveway, just after midnight, following a call from a concerned resident of Northesk Street who reported smelling smoke and initially thought a house was on fire.

"Our crew were on scene within minutes of the call and confirmed at 01:00hrs that they had extinguished it using a hose reel jet, small tools and a thermal imaging camera to check for hotspots.

"After making the vehicle and area safe, duty of care was left with the Police as the cause of the fire was confirmed as deliberate ignition."

Devon and Cornwall Police have been approached for comment.
