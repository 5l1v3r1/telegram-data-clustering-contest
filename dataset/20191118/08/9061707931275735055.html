These flood-hit roads are open again this morning

The ease in rain has led to areas starting to clear of surface water

18 Nov 2019, 08:29 by Jenny Moody

Busy roads in and around Burton and Swadlincote are once again open this morning after flooding hit the area hard last week.

The constant rain had led to problems across the area, with drivers having to find alternative routes to get about as many roads were closed due to being under water.

Drivers heading through Walton again experienced major problems as Station Lane flooded at the weekend and was not passable for those wanting to get through.

Those trying to find another route also had to avoid Croxhall Road, near Catton Park, due to flood water, which led to busy traffic through Stapenhill with drivers avoiding the flood water.

Last week there were also issues in Rolleston, Repton and Barton as the downpours led to brooks bursting their bans and major problems with standing surface water.

The good news this morning is that all roads are now open as the water has receded and drivers can go about their normal Monday morning routine.

However, Conkers Discovery Park, in Moira, remains closed until Wednesday, November 20, due to the "natural lake breaching its banks".
