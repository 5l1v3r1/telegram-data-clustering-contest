Samoa shuts schools, declares emergency as measles kills 6

New Zealand Prime Minister Jacinda Ardern said on Monday it was sending 3,000 vaccines to Samoa as well as nurses and medical supplies.

18 Nov 2019, 08:10 by AP

Samoa has closed all its schools, banned children from public gatherings and mandated that everybody get vaccinated after declaring an emergency due to a measles outbreak that has so far killed six people.

For the past three weeks, the Pacific island nation of 200,000 people has been in the grip of a measles epidemic that has been exacerbated by low immunization rates.

Schools were closed from Monday after the government declared an emergency on Saturday. The National University of Samoa also told students to stay home and said exams scheduled for this week had been indefinitely postponed.

Samoa Director General of Health Leausa Take Naseri said in a news conference last week that he expects the epidemic will get worse. He said that only about two-thirds of Samoans had been vaccinated, leaving the others vulnerable to the virus.

But figures from the World Health Organization and UNICEF indicate that measles immunization rates among Samoan infants have fallen steeply from over 70% in 2013 to under 30% last year.

New Zealand Prime Minister Jacinda Ardern said Monday it was sending 3,000 vaccines to Samoa as well as nurses and medical supplies.

Ms. Ardern said Samoan authorities believe the outbreak was started by a traveler from New Zealand.

We, of course, have an open flow of people, Ms. Ardern said. But we see our responsibility as supporting Samoa as they deal with the outbreak, and we are doing that actively.

Dr. Petousis-Harris of University of Auckland said it was disappointing that people in New Zealand who were carrying the virus had traveled to Samoa. She said New Zealand has for years known it has immunity gaps.
