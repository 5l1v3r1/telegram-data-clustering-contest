'AMAZING MAN'

Real-life Scots Santa Claus who decorated home with 3,000 Christmas lights to honour late wife dies

18 Nov 2019, 20:36 by Thomas Brown

A REAL-life Santa Claus who decorated his house with 3000 lights to honour his wife’s dying wish has lost his own fight for life.

Davie Brown, 63, first dressed as Santa and put on the show in 2007 to cheer up Ann, who had lung disease COPD.

Every year families from all over would visit the couple’s wonderland in Bonnyrigg, Midlothian.

And despite reeling from the loss of Ann at 68 in August last year, Davie made sure he continued the annual tradition after she begged him to keep it going.

But Christmas 2018 was to be the last time the charity champion thrilled locals with his festive extravaganza after loved ones announced his death last week aged 64. It’s believed he had been ill for some time.

Laura Jane Rushforth said: “My family would like to thank everyone for the kind words and support! The street will never be as bright as it was with him in it!”

Hundreds of tributes poured in for the ex-miner - whose efforts helped raise thousands for Marie Curie cancer charity.

Visitor Jenn Rae said: “This is heartbreaking. Davie was such an amazing man and did massive amounts for Marie Curie.

“I was absolutely gutted when I found out last year would be the last of his lights. I hope he can rest in peace now he is reunited with his wife.

“Such sad news.

“RIP Davie you will be sadly missed by those who knew your kindness.”

And Penny Andrews said: “You made hundreds of children smile. Such a lovely man and he has left behind a legacy.

Thank you for everything you did for a very worthwhile cause. Rest in peace Davie. Bonnyrigg won’t be the same without your Christmas spirit “

Dedicated Davie would hand out sweets to children and collect their letters bound for the North Pole.

In December 2018, he put on his largest display ever and wore his Santa costumer every day in December as a tribute to Anne.

He pulled out all the stops and built a Cinderella-style carriage from scratch, complete with six rocking horses, which were named by children living nearby.

He also stepped up the Christmas lights by another 1,400 on top of the 1,600 which were lit up in 2017.

It’s believed his decorations have been placed elsewhere in the community so locals can continue to enjoy his work.
