CRISIS HOSPITAL

Patients given extra blankets to keep warm after heating packs in at scandal-hit Glasgow superhospital

18 Nov 2019, 20:54 by Graham Mann

PATIENTS were given extra blankets to keep warm after the heating broke down at Scotland’s scandal-hit superhospital.

Bosses ordered in mobile heaters as engineers battled for hours to fix the system at the £842million site.

Staff on every ward at the Queen Elizabeth University Hospital in Glasgow battled to keep the winter chill at bay in bone-chilling conditions on Saturday.

The latest problem to hit the NHS hub, which opened just four years ago, comes as prosecutors confirmed they are investigating the death of a boy aged three at the Royal Hospital for Children, also on the campus.

One patient in an A&E unit when the system broke said: “I couldn’t believe it when I heard staff talk about wards without heating.

“I heard a doctor say it was affecting patients in the high dependency unit and they had to use space heating to stabilise the problem.

“The thought of this happening at a multi-million pound facility defies belief. It’s an outrage.”

A spokeswoman for NHS Greater Glasgow and Clyde said: “There were some issues with the heating system on Saturday.

“These were rectified by our senior Estates staff, who successfully carried out urgent repairs.

“Temporary mobile heaters and additional blankets were quickly made available to ensure patients were comfortable. We would like to apologise to any patients affected by this.”

In a blast at Health Secretary Jeane Freeman, Scots Tory health spokesman Miles Briggs said: “The number of scandals totting up on her watch has become unsustainable. She is no longer fit to be health secretary.

Labour’s Monica Lennon added: “SNP ministers have allowed issues to build up and the maintenance and repairs backlog across the NHS is close to £1billion. Patients deserve better.”
