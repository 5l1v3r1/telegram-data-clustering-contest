Two, including minor, held for stabbing man to death

‘Victim used to abuse, thrash accused’

18 Nov 2019, 20:09 by Staff Reporter

A 21-year-old man has been arrested and a minor detained for allegedly stabbing a youth to death in south Delhi’s Ambedkar Nagar on Sunday night, the police said on Monday.

The accused told the police that the victim used to abuse and thrash them regularly.

Deputy Commissioner of Police (South) Atul Kumar Thakur said that the victim has been identified as Manoj alias Lavi (21), a resident of Madangir. The 21-year-old accused is Sooraj, who lives in the same area.

The PCR received a call at 11.12 p.m. regarding an incident of stabbing in Madangir. When a police team reached the spot, it was informed that the injured person had been taken to a private hospital by his relatives. “It was found that the victim was declared brought dead by doctors. Stab injuries were found on his body,” the officer said.

A case under Section 302 (punishment for murder) of the Indian Penal Code was registered and an investigation was taken up.

During investigation, it was revealed that the victim was stabbed by three persons following an argument. The accused were identified after a police inquiry.

“The accused, Sooraj, was arrested and a 16-year-old juvenile was apprehended on Monday. Teams are looking for the other accused involved in the case,” the officer said.

During interrogation, the accused told police that the victim, under the influence of alcohol, used to abuse them regularly. “They also said that Manoj used to thrash them reguarly. They decided to take revenge and bought a knife from Haridwar. The knife has been seized,” Mr. Thakur said.
