Peter Grant brands Michael Stewart a liar in astonishing Leigh Griffiths bust-up

The two clashed on the BBC radio show Sportsound over comments made by Stewart when Alex McLeish was Scotland boss.

18 Nov 2019, 20:21 by Jonathan McFarlane

Former Scotland assistant manager Peter Grant has branded pundit Michael Stewart a liar in an astonishing radio bust-up.

The Alloa boss launched a furious attack on Stewart over comments claiming Leigh Griffiths had been "hammered" in the dressing room after a defeat and his general "negativity" about the Alex McLeish regime.

McLeish and Grant were sacked by the SFA after a 3-0 defeat in Kazakhstan and an unconvincing 2-0 win over San Marino started Euro 2020 Group I in disastrous fashion.

Below is a transcript of the heated exchange between Stewart, Grant and BBC journalist Tom English on Sportsound.

Grant begins by referring directly to the claims about Griffiths.

Peter Grant: "I think I said it to Michael. I'd never say it by text or phone. I wanted to say it face to face and I was fortunate enough to see him a couple of weeks ago. It was a total lie and I was always led to believe if you are not 100 per cent sure, don't go with what someone else has said, always check before you say anything.

"I said to Michael and that was it, the end for me because it was a complete and utter lie. It wasn't the fact that he said it, it was the fact that everybody wrote it afterwards."

"(Michael said Leigh Griffiths) had been slaughtered by the staff, that was the words that were used I think.

Michael Stewart: "Peter brought it up at a Ladbrokes event. I didn't respond due to the fact that he brought it up in front of other people so I appreciate he didn't do it over the phone or a text, but to bring it up in front of some other people who had absolutely no idea what the conversation was about, I decided not to get involved.

"There's a big difference between telling lies and I'm quite interested to hear him there say you shouldn't say something unless you are 100 percent sure, I'm not a journalist and would never claim to be a journalist but in the journalistic handbook, if you hear something from two independent sources that is confirmation of a story and that is what happened.

"I heard days after that something had happened in the changing room, that Leigh Griffiths had been slaughtered. I then thought nothing of it.

"Then three weeks later when there was talk of Griffiths not wanting to be in the squad, I was talking to somebody else and they told me again that something happened in the changing room.

 

"This is not me fabricating a story, if that was the case I'd hold up my hands and apologise but if I'm recanting a story that two independent sources have told me then that's not a slight on my character and that's what I take umbrage with.

"When Peter said, 'Michael told lies', he was using it to undermine my opinion on the show about Craig Levein.

"Now everybody knows I was open and honest about what I thought of Craig Levein . I didn't like the guy personally but I offered my professional assessment. Me being honest about that undermined my professional assessment with a lot of people but that was me being honest. I could have lied and tried to hide that but I didn't.

"It wasn't a lie, if it didn't happen...well it's not a lie Peter, you're rolling your eyes there, it's not a lie.

Peter Grant: "If it didn't happen and you said it, it is a lie. Why come out and say it?

Tom English: There's a difference between a lie and an error."

Peter Grant: "No it's a lie is the same thing Tom. Michael has got my number."

Tom English: "A lie is implying malicious intent..."

Peter Grant: "Yes. I think it was."

Michael Stewart: "So you think I was campaigning against Alex McLeish?"

Peter Grant: "No but there was no positives from you in the whole time he was there."

Michael Stewart: "Because I don't think it was positive, my professional assesment. You're suggesting I lied with malicious intent..."

Peter Grant: "I said you lied because you did lie."

Michael Stewart: "I've just explained to you Peter the difference been a lie and...

Peter Grant: "I've worked with you on the TV and we got on fantastically well and I'm thinking, 'Why did he not phone me?'. A problem with Leigh Griffiths in the dressing room? Michael, don't even go there."

The debate continued back and forth before Grant is asked about an incident where McLeish, "had a problem remembering the team in front of the players".

Grant denied the story as "rubbish", explaining that McLeish had simply taken unwell. 

He said: "The team was named. We were ready to leave for the match. Alex was going to have a couple of minutes with the boys.

"The team is already named and everything has been done.  Five minutes before we go we go in to have a final talk with three members of staff and the players.

"Alex stood up, said something then. 'Excuse me a minute guys' and walked off. He said to me, 'I'm just not feeling right.'

"It was nothing to do with the team being named or the organisation.

"The thing that frightened me was by the time we got back the story was out."
