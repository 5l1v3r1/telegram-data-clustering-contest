It’s back! International Rules to return in 2020

18 Nov 2019, 20:42 by Staff writers

The International Rules series will return in 2020 after the AFL and Ireland’s Gaelic Athletic Association finally reached an agreement.

While venues are yet to be confirmed, the series is set to be held on Sunday, November 15 and Saturday, November 21.

Australia will be in line to host the series after the 2022 AFL season.

The AFL’s general manager of football operations, Steve Hocking said Australia would look to field as a strong a side as possible upon the series return.

“We want to take the best of the best to the two-Test series in Ireland next year and our All Australian players will be the first players selected,” Hocking said.

“The IRS Tests allows players from both codes the only opportunity to represent their countries and have traditionally been great contests that have showcased their athleticism.

“We saw in 2017, two high-standard matches that drew great attendances. I look forward to the rivalry continuing when the Test series returns next year.”

Australia triumphed in the last instalment, winning the 2017 series 2-0 on home soil.

But the contest was not without controversy with Geelong skipper Joel Selwood black carded for a late hit in the second match. It lit the fuse and sparked an ugly melee between both sides.

There have been 20 International Rules series held between Australia and Ireland. The two nations split the first four meetings between 1984 and 1990, before it became a two-Test series in 1998. They were mainly close encounters, with Ireland winning seven of the next 13 meetings. The last two series have been one-off Tests, with Australia taking the honours in 2014 and Ireland in 2015. Overall, Ireland leads the head-to-head encounters over Australia 21-18.
