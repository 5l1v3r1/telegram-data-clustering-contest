Myles Garrett was ‘defending himself’ against Mason Rudolph: Sheldon Richardson

18 Nov 2019, 20:14 by Justin Tasch

A former Jet and current Browns teammate of Myles Garrett said the defensive end was “defending himself” when he bashed Steelers quarterback Mason Rudolph in the head with Rudolph’s helmet.

“Of course he was,” Sheldon Richardson said Monday, according to ESPN. “If a guy [is] scratching at your face, what you going to [do]? The helmet is overboard, let’s get that clear. But defending yourself, I don’t blame him for defending [himself] at all.”

Garrett was suspended indefinitely but at least for the rest of this season, including potential postseason games. The 23-year-old is appealing that suspension.

Richardson, a 2013 first-round pick by the Jets who joined the Browns this year on a three-year, $37 million deal, believes Rudolph should’ve been suspended for grabbing at Garrett’s helmet before Garrett snapped.

“You can’t antagonize a fight and then claim bullying. … you get what’s coming to you,” Richardson said. “Of course Myles overreacted. … He was protecting himself. I don’t blame him. Guy keeps rushing me, even with the helmet off, he’s asking for it. Just leave it at that.”
