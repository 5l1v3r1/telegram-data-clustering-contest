Submit sealed reply to Subodh Gupta’s plea: HC

Subodh Gupta seeks ₹5 crore for damage due to alleged defamatory posts

18 Nov 2019, 20:01 by Staff Reporter

The Delhi High Court on Monday allowed Instagram handle — Herdsceneand — to submit, in a sealed cover, its response to artist Subodh Gupta’s plea seeking removal of alleged defamatory content made against him on the photo-sharing app.

Justice Rajiv Sahai Endlaw also allowed the Instagram account handle to file in a sealed cover its ‘vakalatnama’ (document empowering a lawyer to act for and on behalf of his client) to maintain its interim anonymity and furnish a redacted copy of its response to Mr. Gupta.

The anonymous account holder will have to submit its response to Mr. Gupta’s suit within two weeks.

Mr. Gupta, in his suit, has sought ₹5 crore for damage caused to him and his family due to the alleged defamatory posts.

The HC had earlier directed social media giants, including Google, Facebook and Instagram, to remove anonymous posts containing sexual harassment accusations against Mr Gupta that emerged during last year’s #MeToo movement. It has listed the case for further hearing on January 22, next year.
