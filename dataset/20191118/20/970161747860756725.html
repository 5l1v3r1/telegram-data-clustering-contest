Mother rescues missing daughter after spotting her on TV report about snow

18 Nov 2019, 20:31

A mother was able to rescue her missing daughter after seeing her appear in a TV report about snowy weather.

Jill Rosenow has been searching for her daughter Alice Crawford since she vanished from her home in North Dakota over the summer.

And she was stunned to discover that Alice had appeared on a TV news station in Denver, Colorado, late last month.

Alice, who was living on the streets of the Mile High City, told a crew that its freezing, snowy conditions were ‘death weather’ and that she feared for her safety.

Despite not having access to the TV station which broadcast the interview, Jill learned about the clip, and immediately jetted out to Denver.

Jill told 9News: ‘It was death weather. It was cold. She was cold.

‘It broke my heart. I cried. Seeing my daughter on the streets, homeless … a mother is only as happy as her saddest child.

‘As you can imagine, I’m a train wreck. I cry. I think about her.

‘I don’t sleep at night. I just want her back. She needs to be safe.

‘She needs to be off the streets… It’s a mother’s worst nightmare.’

Jill and other relatives arrived in Denver from as far afield as California and Seattle, before chatting to the reporter who’d spoken to Alice in a bid to track her down.

They spent two days chatting to hundreds of people, and showing Alice’s photo to everyone who would listen.

Jill and her relatives were beginning to give up hope of finding Alice when they visited the city’s Union Station.

But they were reunited unexpectedly when Alice’s aunt Theresa Brown went to use the restroom, and heard Alice’s voice coming from a locked stall.

Theresa recalled: ‘It was really by accident, well, I had to use the restroom.

‘We walked into the restroom thinking it was empty and the last stall was occupied.

‘As I was getting ready to go into my stall, we heard her voice.’

Jill was thrilled to see her daughter, who has battled drug addiction, and was covered in scabs after months on the street.

She said: ‘It was just over. Just like that. In a wonderful way it was over. The search was over.’

Alice has spent the last three days throwing up, but Jill is hopeful about her daughter’s future, and said: ‘She’s alive, and that counts for more than anything.’

Jill plans to get Alice into rehab as soon as her daughter is well enough to fly.

She and her family plan to take Alice out of their home state, so she can focus on sobriety without unhelpful distractions.
