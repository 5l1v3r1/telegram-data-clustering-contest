Danielle Panabaker says she “felt more confident” during her second time directing an episode for season six of “The Flash.”

“The first time you do anything, it’s a little nerve-wracking,” Panabaker, who stars as Caitlin Snow/Killer Frost on The CW show, told Insider. “You can be as prepared as you want, but you never know.”

The actress made her directorial debut during season five, episode 18 of the comic-book series (titled “Godspeed”) and will once again get behind the camera for Tuesday’s episode (“License to Elongate”).

“I definitely felt more confident this season,” Panabaker told us.

She continued: “Going into my second time directing, I felt like I also had less to prove, because the first time I directed, I really wanted to show everyone how seriously I was taking it and how prepared I was.”

“I think, rightfully so, a few people were skeptical at first,” Panabaker added. “And now that it was my second time around, people really trusted that I knew what I was doing, and was capable of hopefully giving the best version of that script possible.”
