16 Real Pictures of Princess Margaret’s Insane Trip to the U.S.

18 Nov 2019, 09:31

In 1965, Princess Margaret embarked on a three-week trip to the United States for a historic royal tour. She and her husband, Antony Armstrong-Jones, started in California and then went to Tucson, Arizona, made a quick stop in Washington, D.C., where they went to a ball at the White House, and finished their trip in New York City for the launch of his book.

The trip was a huge success. Like, imagine if Meghan Markle came to the U.S. on a royal tour today. That’s how people treated Princess Margaret. Fans waited at the airport with signs, paparazzi followed them everywhere, it was a whole thing. The president at the time, Lyndon B. Johnson, gave her rave reviews after the ball she attended at the White House. The Crown depicts (a slightly exaggerated version of) the whole thing.
