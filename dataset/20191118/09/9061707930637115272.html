What the weather has in store for Burton and Swadlincote areas this week

Many communities are still waterlogged from recent downpours

18 Nov 2019, 09:53 by Jenny Moody

Big parts of Burton and South Derbyshire have been underwater since heavy rain started battering the region.

As standing water struggles drain, flooding has made roads impassible and left drivers looking for alternative routes.

But with a new week ahead, it appears that we might be in for some better news, according to the Met Office.

Today, November 18, the sun will be making an appearance, but it will still be cold, so keep those gloves and scarves handy.

Temperatures will hit highs of 7C and it will be dry throughout the day.

Tonight it will be cold, with clear skies and frost making way for fog patches in the morning.

Tomorrow will be cold and frosty to start with, but the fog will slowly clear to make way for the sun.

The maximum temperature will be 8C.

It will be a cold and cloudy day on Wednesday, as temperatures hit highs of 7C, but it will stay dry.

Thursday will be dry most of the day, but it will also be breezy, with patchy rain developing later in the day.

Heavier rain will be back on Friday, with scattered blustery showers.

Saturday and Sunday will also be dry, with some light rain overnight, forecasters have predicted.
