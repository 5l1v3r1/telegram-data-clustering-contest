1 killed, 3 deputies injured in shooting in rural Franklin County

18 Nov 2019, 09:24 by KOMO News Staff

FRANKLIN COUNTY, Wash. - One man was killed and three deputies were injured in a shooting incident and police pursuit early Monday in rural Franklin County, sheriff's officials said.

The incident unfolded at around 3 a.m. when Franklin County sheriff's deputies responded to a report of a prowler in the area of Sagehill Road and Hollingsworth Road, according to the sheriff's office.

During the call, the deputies attempted to stop a vehicle they spotted heading north on Sagehill Road at high speed, and a police chase followed.

One of the deputies attempted to make contact with the fleeing suspect in the area of R-170 and Davis Lane. According to sheriff's officials, no other details are are known except that one of the deputies reports shots being fired.

From here, the suspect continued up the road at high speed, then drove out into the middle of a field near Sagehill Road and Wahluke Road, where he collided with a deputy's patrol car, according to the Franklin County sheriff's command staff.

According to police, deputies administered first aid to the suspect, who was taken from the scene in an ambulance but later died.

The three deputies, identified as Sgt. Gordon Thomasson, Deputy Andrew Gardner and Deputy Cody Quantrell, were taken to the hospital for treatment of injuries then released. The nature of their injuries were not disclosed.

The Franklin County Sheriff's Office has turned the investigation over to the Special Investigation Unit and Coroner, according to the Franklin County Sheriff Command Staff.
