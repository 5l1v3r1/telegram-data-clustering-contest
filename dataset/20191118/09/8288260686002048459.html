Liam Gallagher issues appeal after teen Joseph Hogg goes missing after gig

The Oasis singer, who last week played Arena Birmingham, retweeted an appeal which says Joseph went missing after the show at the city's Utilita Arena

18 Nov 2019, 09:52 by James Rodger, https://www.facebook.com/jamesrodgerjournalist

Liam Gallagher has shared an urgent appeal for a missing teenager who disappeared after attending a gig in the North East.

Joseph Hogg went missing after Liam rocked Newcastle last night.

The Oasis singer, who last week played Arena Birmingham, retweeted an appeal which says Joseph went missing after the show at the city's Utilita Arena.

Joseph was last seen at about 11.30pm with 'no phone or money and without his shirt on'.

Gallagher shared an appeal from a Twitter user called HOGG, who said he was Joseph's cousin.

The tweet said: "My cousin Joseph Hogg has gone missing after last nights Liam Gallagher gig.

"Last seen around half 11 in Newcastle with no phone or money and without his shirt on.

"No sign of him on CCTV yet last seen somewhere near the Life centre. Please RT #LiamGallagher #newcastle."

 

A spokesperson for Northumbria Police said: "We can confirm we are searching for missing 17-year-old Joseph Hogg who was reported missing from the Utilita Arena shortly after 11.30pm on November 17.

"Enquiries to locate him are ongoing and anyone with information about his whereabouts is asked to contact police on 101 quoting log 1128 171119."

The appeal has been retweeted hundreds of times.

Katy Stobbs also tweeted: "MISSING! Twitter please do you thing!

 

"His name is Joe Hogg, and he was at the Liam Gallagher concert last night in Newcastle, he stormed off from the gig with no phone, no too on and no money at all, and hasn’t been seen since, it was around 11pm.

"Everyone please share."
