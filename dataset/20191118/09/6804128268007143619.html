Chelsea fans will love John Terry's message to Fikayo Tomori and Mason Mount

Both Blues' players featured in England's Euro qualifier vs Kosovo

18 Nov 2019, 09:08 by Amie Wilson

England's final Euro 2020 qualifier against Kosovo is not likely to be one that both Fikayo Tomori and Mason Mount will forget anytime soon.

The Chelsea duo were both bought on as second half substitutes in the 4-0 win, with Tomori replacing Trent Alexander-Arnold to make his senior international debut with six minutes to play.

Mount was bought on in the 72nd minute replacing Alex Oxlade-Chamerlain. It was the midfielder's fourth appearance as an England player.

The 20-year-old had come close to scoring his first international goal in the 7-0 win vs Montenegro on Thursday, but made no mistake when the opportunity presented itself this time, bagging England's fourth goal of the game with a good finish beyond Kosovo goalkeeper Arijanet Muric.

Chelsea fans were full of pride after the Blues' player first goal for his country , and there was someone else who took to social media to send a message to both Tomori and Mount during the match.

Former Chelsea captain John Terry played 78 times for the Three Lions, and also captained the country on many occasions.

The Blues legend, posted on his Instagram stories as the Chelsea youngsters both reached significant milestones in their careers.

"Congratulations. Yes mate," was his message when Tomori took to the pitch.

While the former defender put a blue heart and England flag when Mount found the net.

Both players will now return to Chelsea, where they will start their preparations for the big upcoming match vs Manchester City this weekend.
