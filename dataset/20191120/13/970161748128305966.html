Man Utd suffer blow in James Maddison chase as Leicester star considers future

20 Nov 2019, 13:49

James Maddison is considering a new contract at Leicester City, which would throw a spanner in the works of Manchester United’s pursuit of the midfielder.

The England international is one of United’s top transfer targets with the Red Devils preparing to test Leicester’s resolve in January or next summer.

The Foxes do not want to let one of their star players leave and are trying to secure him to a deal beyond the end of his current contract, which expires in 2023.

Although his future is far from decided, the MEN reports that Maddison is considering re-signing with Leicester, despite the interest from elsewhere.

Whether that means a contract extension, an improved salary, or most likely both, is not known.

Manchester United are reportedly confident of getting their man, nonetheless, as Maddison grew up as a fan of the club and is good friends with Harry Maguire who made the switch from Leicester to Old Trafford over the summer.

With the midfielder’s contract still running for another three-and-a-half years, and Leicester not being under any financial need to sell the player, he would be extremely expensive.

The Foxes held out for £80m when they sold Maguire to the Red Devils and may well expect a figure in the same region for their latest bright young English talent.

England manager Gareth Southgate was full of praise for Maddison as he gave him his international debut last week against Montenegro.

‘He’s a player of outstanding technical ability. He has an eye for an incisive pass, his set-play delivery is world class – absolutely top,’ said Southgate.

‘With his club now he is in a position as a number eight that he’s really learning more and more about work off the ball.

‘His club are pressing in the way we want to press, pretty much. I know Brendan has a focus on that.

‘In those areas, work off the ball is as important as with it. Especially the higher level of opponents and tighter games.

‘He’s developing well and he has a lot to offer us.’
