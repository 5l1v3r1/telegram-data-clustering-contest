Sandbag thrown at lorry on M5 motorway southbound - teens arrested

The incident happened near Tewkesbury

20 Nov 2019, 13:56 by Jessica Mercer, Jasper King

A sandbag was thrown at a lorry on the M5, smashing its window and narrowly missing the driver.

Two teenage boys from Gloucestershire have been arrested in connection with the incident, Gloucestershire Live reports.

The incident happened shortly before 11pm on Thursday October 31 on the southbound carriageway nearby junction 9 towards Tewkesbury.

Luckily the lorry driver was shocked but not physically injured and managed to steer the lorry to safety.

The two boys, both aged 16 and from the Tewkesbury area, were arrested yesterday morning, (November 19), on suspicion of conspiracy to commit grievous bodily harm and conspiracy to cause criminal damage recklessly to endanger life.

They have been released under investigation and police enquiries continue.
