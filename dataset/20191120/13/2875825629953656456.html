How to get to the Republic of Ireland's Euro 2020 play-off with Slovakia for under €90

Mick McCarthy's men are set to face Pavel Hapal's side in either Bratislava or Trnava

20 Nov 2019, 13:31 by Robert Hynes

The Republic of Ireland will travel to Slovakia for their Euro 2020 play-off in March.

Mick McCarthy's men are set to face Pavel Hapal's side in either Bratislava or Trnava, which is under an hour away from the capital.

The match is set to take place on Thursday, March 26 and direct flights in and out of Bratislava from Dublin are already costing a couple of hundred quid.

With that in mind, we decided to look at alternative ways of getting there.

We do warn that there is plenty of travelling involved in this route, but it will get you to Slovakia for under €90.

Flying out of Dublin the day before the game, you can catch a flight to Milan for €29.07 with Ryanair at 1.30pm.

You'll then have to wait over four hours in Italy before getting a flight to Vienna with Wizz Air for €9.99.

It's just 90 minutes from Vienna to Bratislava and you can get a bus ticket online with Flixbus for only €1.

Coming home, you will be getting back on board the €1 bus to Austria and catching a flight with Laudamotion for €14.99 to Copenhagen.

You'll have two hours to kill in Denmark before getting a Ryanair flight to Dublin for €33.19.

Full itinerary:

Out

Dublin to Milan Malpensa Leave: 1.30pm. Arrive: 4.55pm March 25 Ryanair €29.07

Milan Malpensa to Vienna Leave: 9.25pm. Arrive: 10.55pm March 25 Wizz Air €9.99

Return

Vienna to Copenhagen Leave: 6.20pm. Arrive: 8.05pm March 27 Laudamotion €14.99

Copenhagen to Dublin Leave: 10.10pm. Arrive: 11.30pm March 27 Ryanair €33.19

Total cost (including buses): €89.24

*Prices are correct at the time of writing and are subject to change.
