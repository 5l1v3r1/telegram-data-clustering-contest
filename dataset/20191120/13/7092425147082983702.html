UEFA confirm draw will decide home advantage for Euro 2020 play-off finals

20 Nov 2019, 13:38 by James Cox

UEFA have revealed that a draw will decide which team plays at home in the Euro 2020 play-off finals.

The draw takes place in Switzerland on Friday and it will confirm if Ireland will play at the Aviva Stadium, Windsor Park or in Bosnia & Herzegovina, should they get past Slovakia in the play-off semi-final.

Wales' 2-0 victory over Hungary last night confirmed Ireland will face Slovakia, with that game set to take place on Thursday, March 26 at 7.45pm.

Slovakia must declare the home venue within a month, they've played all their recent games in Trnava but the reopened stadium in Bratislava could also play host to the game.

The venue must be confirmed before December 20.

If Ireland beat Slovakia, they will face the winner of Bosnia v Northern Ireland on Thursday, March 31, for a place at Euro 2020.

UEFA have also confirmed that the draw for the group stages of Euro 2020 will take place on Saturday, November 30.

Ireland will be in Group E with Spain if they qualify due to their status as tournament hosts.

The seeds have been confirmed for the main draw, meaning Ireland would play Spain along with one team from France, Poland, Switzerland or Croatia.

The other team in the group would be either Portugal, Turkey, Austria, Sweden or the Czech Republic.
