Ghislaine Maxwell is too loyal to Prince Andrew to implicate him in FBI interview, insider says

20 Nov 2019, 13:42 by Lee Brown

Jeffrey Epstein’s accused madam, Ghislaine Maxwell, is so loyal to Prince Andrew, she would never implicate him if interviewed by the FBI, according to a report.

“It is impossible,” a source familiar with Maxwell told the Telegraph of the woman who has been repeatedly named in court filings as being central to Epstein’s depraved activities.

Maxwell is so close to the prince that he once even asked his mother, Queen Elizabeth II, for permission to take her on an official work trip, according to the Times of London, which said the request was denied.

As well as not wanting to drag a longtime friend into further trouble, the Telegraph’s source also insisted that Maxwell maintains that the Duke of York is innocent of the claims against him.

“Her position is very clear: Prince Andrew did nothing wrong,” she said of the accusations that Andrew again denied during his ill-fated interview with the BBC.

Maxwell is now assumed to be in hiding after seemingly becoming central in an ever-widening investigation into Epstein’s accused accomplices since the convicted pedophile’s death in a Manhattan lockup in August.

The Sun on Wednesday even offered a reward of almost $13,000 for information on the whereabouts of the daughter of the late media tycoon Robert Maxwell.
