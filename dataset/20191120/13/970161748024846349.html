Cara Delevingne talks feeling 'rotten' over mental health battle

20 Nov 2019, 13:02

Cara Delevigne bluntly discussed how she felt like she was ‘rotting’ in an emotional chat on Running Wild with Bear Grylls.

The Carnival Row star doesn’t hold back as she discusses her personal struggles, which included looking after her mum, her mum’s heroin addiction, and being thrust into the limelight as a model.

As the pair cooked rats over a campfire, Bear asked her questions about her life, leading to a heart-breaking confession from Cara in which she admits that she felt like she was ‘selling herself’ to fit what other people wanted.

‘I had no sense of who I was. I had no identity,’ she told him. ‘All I had been was a mental illness, a child, a caretaker, or a supermodel, which just seemed so ridiculous to me.

‘It was like my integrity, my morals, everything was rotting, like I just had nothing left, because I honestly was just selling myself….because I wanted to belong, and I thought that by people hiring me, that was giving me a sense of who I was.’

Thankfully the star decided to focus on her own happiness and now seems to be better than ever – and said if she could tell her younger self anything, it was to have more confidence in herself, and not be afraid to communicate her feelings when she was in trouble.

‘If you can have any confidence, to be confident in your vulnerability is the best thing you can ever ask her,’ before acknowledging that she didn’t realise how strong she was, or how much she had grown up over the years.

‘You’re a strong, remarkable lady,’ Bear told her.
