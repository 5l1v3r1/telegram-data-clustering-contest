Maharashtra govt formation: Congress-NCP discuss common minimum programme blueprint in meeting in Delhi

Congress-NCP leaders seem to be finalising key agendas, keeping their respective manifestos in mind.

20 Nov 2019, 13:48 by Mausami Singh

Senior leaders of the Congress and the Nationalist Congress Party (NCP) held talks at the residence of NCP chief Sharad Pawar in New Delhi on Tuesday. The meeting was to take a shot at government formation in Maharashtra.

Jairam Ramesh was present at the meeting. Sources have informed India Today that Jairam Ramesh has been tasked with drawing the blueprint of the alliance.

Congress-NCP leaders seem to be finalising key agendas, keeping their respective manifestos in mind.

The meeting took place at the residence of Sharad Pawar. Congress leaders Ahmed Patel, Jairam Ramesh, Mallikarjun Kharge, Prithviraj Chavan, KC Venugopal and Balasaheb Thorat attended the meeting.

Those representing the NCP included Supriya Sule, Ajit Pawar, Jayant Patil and Nawab Malik.
