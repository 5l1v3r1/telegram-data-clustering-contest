Transhumanist 'cyborg' to run against Trump in 2020 US presidential election

20 Nov 2019, 11:02

President Donald Trump has a new Republican competitor in the race to become US President: a transhumanist ‘cyborg’ called Zoltan Istvan.

We’re used to hearing politicians promise the world and make huge promises they can never keep.

But Istvan has perhaps the greatest ambition of any non-religious public figure in recent history because he wants to make death a curable disease and encourage humanity to meld with technology.

He’ll be standing as a Republican presidential candidate – the second time he’s joined the race.

He told Metro: ‘My team and I aim to make conservatives more open-minded about transhumanism and the future in the US.

‘Right now, they are some of the most closed-minded folks on the planet.

‘However, they’re pretty smart with their money, so now it’s my goal to get them to be open to the future too. It’s a very different way to take on Trump. It’s almost like we’re a Trojan Horse, but in a good way that both sides can appreciate.’
