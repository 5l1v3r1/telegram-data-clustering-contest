Matthew McConaughey Launches Eco-Friendly Cabin With Hopes Of ‘Inspiring Australians To Reconnect With Nature’

20 Nov 2019, 11:09 by Becca Longmire

Matthew McConaughey just launched an eco-friendly cabin, dubbed The Reserve, in Australia.

The actor, who spent a year living in the country as a Rotary exchange student in the 1980s, teamed up with Wild Turkey’s charity initiative, With Thanks, to design the home. The launch was held at Sydney’s Royal Botanic Gardens Wednesday.

Aussie twins Chris and Cam Grant, founders of travel startup Unyoked, which offers private cabins in the wilderness with “minimum footprint and maximum chill,” helped design the hut with McConaughey.

The cabin will be rented out once it is moved to a wilderness area in New South Wales.

McConaughey said in a statement, according to Just Jared: “I’ve always been in awe of Australia’s natural beauty.

“My hope now is that The Reserve by Wild Turkey x Unyoked cabin will inspire Australians to reconnect with nature as an antidote to the frenetic pace of life. I’m also proud that the cabin will form part of the Australian Wild Turkey ‘With Thanks’ initiative that will support people and organizations who demonstrate unwavering conviction to preserving and protecting Australia’s great wilderness and wildlife.”
