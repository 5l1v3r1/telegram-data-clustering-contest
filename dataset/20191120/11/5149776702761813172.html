'Low-lifes' steal safe and 40 bottles of spirits from Hucknall Town FC

'Any money is a loss to a club at our level,' said the club chairman

20 Nov 2019, 11:47 by Jamie Barlow

Thieves raided and smashed through the clubhouse of non-league Hucknall Town - causing thousands of pounds worth of damage.

Chairman Bob Scotney said the culprits removed a panel below a window at ground level to get in and broke several locks in a raid believed to have happened between 9pm on Monday, November 18, and 8.20am on Tuesday, November 20.

A safe from the cellar was taken, along with about 40 full bottles of spirits. Boxes of sweets, including bags of Haribo, were also stolen.

Mr Scotney, 60, said the 'old-fashioned' safe was taken using a trolley used by staff to move around barrels of beer.

"There was cash in the safe," he said. "Any money is a loss to a club at our level. It's got to be replaced."

Storage containers full of alcohol were also taken from the cellar and behind the bar at the ground in Watnall Road.

"We probably estimate in the region of 40 [bottles]," said Mr Scotney, who has been the club chairman for about four years. "All your regular brands - your vodka, your Southern Comfort, Jack Daniels, gin.

"It's all branded, we don't sell any non-branded types of spirit."

The thieves only took full bottles.

The club is run by volunteers and Mr Scotney said it came as a disappointment to find "some low-lifes have caused that much damage".

Several doors were smashed and have had to be repaired.

"It will run into thousands of pounds for the damage and everything we have got to put right, even small things like padlocks."

Tills were also damaged but Mr Scotney said there was no money in them.

The club posted pictures of the damage on Facebook, with images showing smashed glass on the floor.

A club statement, published on Tuesday (November 19), read: "This is what we have walked into after a break in at the club last night.

"A safe was stolen and taken in a sack trolley along with equipment and spirits.

"Did anyone see anything suspicious between 9pm last night and 8am?

"If so please contact the club or the police."

All the damage has been repaired.
