Sarcastic note left on car parked on pavement

20 Nov 2019, 11:33

A resident at their wits end over parked cars hogging space on the pavement made their feelings known with this passive aggressive note.

In a bid to stop the driver of the white car from ever mounting the curb again, they printed a message on A4 paper and stuck it behind the rear wiper.

The note read: ‘I PARK ON THE PAVEMENT BECAUSE I AM FAR MORE IMPORTANT THAN OTHER PEOPLE.’

As he walked through the a residential suburb of Handbridge in Chester, Cheshire, Twitter user @kevbrackenbury spotted the angry message and shared a picture of it with the caption ‘I park therefore I am’.

He revealed the area the car was parked in but refused to say which street it was on.

People were quick to call the note ‘hilarious’ and ‘brilliant’ and an example of ‘people power’ in action.

Another said this sort of tactic ‘might be good for those that feel the need to take up 1.5 spaces in car parks too’.

Parking on a pavement has been illegal in London since 1974 and can result in a £70 fine for offenders.

There is no general ban anywhere else in England, Wales or Northern Ireland, but MPs have called for the law to be extended to the rest of the UK.

Last month Scottish Parliament passed a bill which will ban pavement parking across the country.
