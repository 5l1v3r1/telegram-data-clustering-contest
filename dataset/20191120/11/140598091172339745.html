Ham, pea and spelt risotto recipe

20 Nov 2019, 11:29 by Angela Hartnett

This recipe requires a little bit of preparation, but once done it’s very simple and any excess can be frozen for a later date.

Prep time: 15 minutes, plus overnight soaking time | Cooking time: 2 hours 20 minutes

SERVES 

Four

INGREDIENTS

For the ham hock:

  • 1 ham hock
  • 1 carrot
  • 1 stick of celery
  • 1 onion, halved
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 sprig of thyme

For the risotto:

  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 30g butter
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 garlic clove, crushed then chopped
  • 300g pearled spelt
  • 125ml white wine
  • 1 litre ham stock, reserved from cooking the hock
  • 50g frozen peas
  • 50g Wensleydale, grated

METHOD

  1.  Soak the ham hock overnight in cold water in the fridge.
  2. The next day, rinse off and place in a pan covered with fresh cold water. Bring to the boil. Skim off any scum that rises to the surface then add the carrot, celery, onion, bay leaf and thyme.
  3. Leave to simmer for about one-and-a-half hours until you can pick the hock up and the ham falls away from it.
  4. When cooked, reserve the stock and pick all the meat away from the bone. Cover the meat with a touch of stock and store both meat and reserved stock in the fridge.
  5. To make the risotto, heat the oil and butter in a pan and cook the onion and garlic until soft and translucent.
  6.  Add the spelt and cook for two to three minutes, then add the wine to deglaze the pan.
  7.  Cook until the wine disappears then start to add the ham stock a ladleful at a time. Wait until the liquid has disappeared before adding more, and stir regularly. It should take about 35 minutes to cook.
  8. About 20 minutes into the cooking time, add 200g of the shredded ham. To finish, stir through the peas and the wensleydale and serve immediately.
