Wild boar pappardelle recipe

20 Nov 2019, 11:15 by Angela Hartnett

Lots of wild boar comes from Europe but you can look out for it from Scottish meat suppliers. You can easily replace it here with venison, too.

Prep time: 10 minutes | Cooking time: 2 hours 15 minutes

SERVES

Four

INGREDIENTS

  • Oil, for cooking
  • 300g mixed roughly chopped carrot, onion and celery
  • 2 garlic cloves, finely chopped
  • 500g wild boar meat, minced or finely chopped
  • 1 tsp tomato purée
  • 100ml red wine
  • 200ml chicken stock
  • 1 bay leaf

To serve:

  • Freshly cooked pappardelle
  • Chopped parsley
  • Freshly grated Parmesan

METHOD

  1.  Using a pan large enough to hold all the ingredients, start by heating three tablespoons of oil and add the chopped vegetables and garlic. Sauté until the vegetables are soft but haven’t taken on any colour.
  2. Add the meat and sauté briefly just until it loses its redness – it does not need to be cooked through.
  3. Add the tomato purée and cook for four minutes, then add the wine and reduce the liquid to a glaze before adding the stock, bay leaf and enough water to cover. Put the lid on and cook for a couple of hours, checking every once in a while to stir, until the meat is coated in a nice thick sauce.
  4. The sauce can be stored in the fridge for a couple of days, or served on the day with fresh pappardelle. Finish with chopped parsley and freshly grated Parmesan.
