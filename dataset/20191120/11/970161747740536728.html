Erling Haaland's father wants son to snub Man Utd transfer in January

20 Nov 2019, 11:36

Manchester United’s chances of signing Erling Haaland in January have suffered a blow because the striker’s father wants him to join a Bundesliga side, reports say.

The RB Salzburg star is one of the most coveted players in Europe after scoring 26 goals in just 18 appearances this season.

United, Manchester City, Real Madrid and Juventus have all been credited with a transfer interest in Haaland.

However, Bild claim the 19-year-old’s father is unconvinced a move to a major European club is the right decision.

United want to agree a deal to sign Haaland in January, but Alf-Inge Haaland has advised his son to snub the move to Old Trafford in favour of a switch to Germany next summer.

Haaland’s father wants his son to play regularly and is keen for him to join a smaller side to ensure a starting place.

The preferences of his father could be a big boost for RB Leipzig, who are owned by the same company as Salzburg and have a history of swapping players with the Austrian side.

Leipzig reportedly have the option to sign Haaland for £27million and remain eager to land the teenager even with bigger clubs circling.

Ole Gunnar Solskjaer is keen to spend in January and has earmarked a new forward as his priority target.

United are prepared to back their manager in the winter transfer window, but will only sanction mega-deals if their key targets become available.

As well as Haaland, United are tipped to make a formal approach for Borussia Dortmund’s Jadon Sancho in January.
