Devon beach cleaner picks up 100,000th plastic piece this year

Catherine Jeffs has been counting the number of plastic pieces collected in 2019

20 Nov 2019, 09:07 by Lewis Clarke

A beach cleaner has picker her 100,000th piece of plastic at Woolacombe.

Catherine Jeffs has been helping clean the beach at Woolacombe for many years, but since retiring has been able to do it every day, rain or shine.

She said: “Most of my beach cleaning is on beautiful Woolacombe beach, between Mill Rock and Black Rock which is very clean.

“Last year I set myself the target to collect 250 pieces of plastic each day, but this year out of interest I recorded each day the number of items I have picked up, and I’ve just picked up my 100,000th piece.

“Some days there is very little to collect, but on days of very high tides along with stormy weather, there is much more. Even I have been amazed at how many items I have collected.”

She added the items fall into three main categories; fishing industry debris such as rope and net, single-use plastic, for bottles and wrapping, and microplastics, which are tiny pieces of broken-down plastic and nurdles.

“Some days I just collect one bag of debris, other days can be three or four, which gets very heavy,” she said.

“I am just one of several people who do beach cleaning at Woolacombe. Although it is good to clear up the plastic, it would be even better if it wasn’t there.”

Catherine is also a member of Plastic Free Woolacombe, which is encouraging everyone to reduce the amount of single-use plastic they use and where possible reuse or recycle it.
