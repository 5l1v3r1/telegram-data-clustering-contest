Lemonheigh-Evans provides Torquay United with timely injury boost

Midfielder ahead of schedule in his return from injury

20 Nov 2019, 09:58 by David Thomas, Stuart James

Midfielder Connor Lemonheigh-Evans is expected to regain fitness earlier than expected, giving a timely boost to Torquay United as they try to defy a daunting injury-list and stay in the National League promotion race.

Bristol City loanee Lemonheigh-Evans, one of the stars of the Gulls' NL South championship last season, has been out for more than two-and-a-half months with an ankle injury which required surgery.

He was not due to return until Christmas or the New Year.

But manager Gary Johnson has  that the Wales Under-21 international's recovery is ahead of schedule, and he could be back in training with United within the next two weeks.

"Connor keeps in touch all the time, he is going well and he thinks he will be back playing early next month.  That's better than we thought originally," said Johnson.

"He had a similar injury to his other ankle in the past, so he's using that experience in his recovery now.

"He may even be back with us in two weeks' time - he might not be quite ready to play then, but he will be close."

Torquay came away from their 1-0 FA Cup defeat at Maidstone United ten days ago with seven players injured - Lemonheigh-Evans, Asa Hall, Armani Little, Opi Edwards, Jake Andrews, Liam Davis and teenage defender Louie Slough.

Davis' ankle fracture prompted Johnson to sign 21-year-old Reading left-back Ramarni Medford-Smith on loan until January.

Midfielders Frank Vincent and Ben Whitfield limped off in last Saturday's 2-1 home league defeat by Sutton United, while United were also without loanee defender Joe Lewis, on Wales Under-21 duty, and Ryan Dickson, who is on loan at Truro City.

Nineteen-year-old Swansea City loanee Lewis will be available for this Saturday's trip to bottom club Chorley, Vincent and Whitfield, - they both sustained 'kicks' against Sutton - are battling to be fit for that game and so is Andrews.

Johnson stressed: "We seem to have had important players out for a long time, and I am really proud of the boys who have got us in the hunt.

"We were playing that Sutton game to go top of the league, and that's what made it more disappointing for everyone.

"It's very tight and competitive at the top, but we are part of that competition now.

"When you look at our starting XI last Saturday, very few have any real experience at senior level.

"But most of them have been winning games for us, and I think they can keep doing it."

Meanwhile, a hat-trick by 17-year-old striker Olaf Koszela led Torquay United to a 4-0 win over Peninsula League Elmore in the Devon St Lukes Challenge Cup second round at Horsdon Park.

Koszela scored in the 24th, 41st and 68th minutes, with Manny Duku chipping in with the Gulls' second goal in the 34th minute.
