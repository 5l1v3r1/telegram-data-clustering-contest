Barracuda band members get together for charity acoustic album

Fred, Doug, Mike Unplugged has been released

20 Nov 2019, 09:08 by Lewis Clarke

A popular Devon band have come together once more to record an acoustic CD Album.

Fred Harris, Doug Parish and Mike Barkworth, who have all been members of the band Barracuda, have released Fred, Doug, Mike Unplugged.

The band recorded the album at Greenvale studio, Withleigh, with Fred on lead and harmony vocals, bass and acoustic guitar, kazoo and claves. Dough on lead and backing, vocals, acoustic bass and kazoo and Mike on lead vocals, acoustic guitar, shaker and dobro. The album was produced, mixed and mastered by Fred Harris and Mike Barkworth and the photo design and cover art is by Lucy Warren.

The album includes 14 songs of covers songs of various artists such as Steve Earle’s My Old Friend The Blues, Eric Clapton’s Before You Accuse Me and Norwegian Wood. The final song on the album is Ken Dodd’s Absent Friends dedicated to all past members of Barracuda; Benny Radford, Ronnie Graham, Gerald Orchard, Barry Sowden, Tony Ruby and Frank Bond.

The trio has put their very own stamp with their own arrangements on such songs as the old folksong Working On The Rail Road, and the Thin Lizzy hit Dancing In The Moonlight. The album covers musical styles from country to folk to pop with some airplay lined up on local radio stations Tonefm, North Devon’s the Voice, Somerset’s10radio and Tiverton’s TCR Radio.

The trio is also planning to play gigs across the region to promote their CD album Fred, Doug, Mike Unplugged. For more information or to buy the album, which costs £8, call 01884 253989. Any profit from the album will be donated to the Tiverton Hospital League of Friends.
