Prince Andrew ditched by Australian universities over ties to Jeffrey Epstein

20 Nov 2019, 09:31

Universities in Australia are distancing themselves from the Duke of York after a ‘car crash’ interview about his involvement with sex offender Jeffrey Epstein.

Prince Andrew was accused of showing a lack of empathy towards victims of the disgraced financier, who took his own life while imprisoned earlier this year.

In the interview he denied sleeping with Virginia Giuffre, one of Epstein’s victims, on three separate occasions between 2001 and 2002, twice while she was underage.

In an attempt to rebut her account of what happened the duke told the BBC’s Emily Maitlis that he ‘can’t sweat’ due to a medical condition.

Eyebrows were also raised when he said he distinctly remembered being at a Pizza Express in Woking on the night of one of the alleged incidents.

Multi million-pound businesses and British universities have have begun severing ties or moving away from the royal and are being followed by institutions in Australia.

Bond University, the University of Wollongong (UoW) and the Royal Melbourne Institute of Technology (RMIT) have either ended their relationship with the duke’s Pitch@Palace entrepreneurial initiative or will not be continuing it in the future.

A spokesman for Perth’s Murdoch University, also listed online as a partner, said it was ‘currently reviewing its support of the Pitch@Palace event in 2020’.

In a statement, Bond University said: ‘We have previously said any new agreement would be considered in 2020.

‘However, in light of recent events, the university does not intend to seek any further involvement.’

It comes after London Metropolitan University said it would consider the duke’s role as its patron, while a student panel at Huddersfield University passed a motion to lobby Andrew to resign as its chancellor.

Asian-focused bank Standard Chartered has joined KPMG in deciding not to renew its sponsorship of the Pitch@Palace scheme.

AstraZeneca’s three-year partnership with the initiative is due to expire at the end of the year ans is being reviewed.

Meanwhile the Outward Bound Trust, of which Andrew is a patron, is to hold a board meeting in the next few days to discuss the matter.

The duke has cancelled a visit to flood-ravaged communities in South Yorkshire today and was summoned for crisis talks at Buckingham Palace, according to the Sun.

As the scandal unravelled, former home secretary Jacqui Smith claimed prince made ‘racist comments about Arabs that were unbelievable’ at a Buckingham Palace state banquet in 2007.

A spokesman for the palace said: ‘HRH has undertaken a considerable amount of work in the Middle East over a period of years and has many friends from the region. He does not tolerate racism in any form.’

The palace has also ‘strenuously denied’ claims Prince Andrew used the term ‘n****r in the woodpile’ during a Downing Street discussion about trade policy.
