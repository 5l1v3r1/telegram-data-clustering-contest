Larkhall thug who tried to rob a shop assistant claims it was a prank

Brandon Watson fled empty-handed, but immediately went into a church and stole an elderly woman’s purse.

20 Nov 2019, 09:00 by Hamilton Advertiser, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/hamilton-advertiser/

A Larkhall thug who tried to rob a shop assistant at knifepoint claimed the frightening incident was only a prank.

Brandon Watson fled empty-handed, but immediately went into a church and stole an elderly woman’s purse.

Watson, 22, of Balfour Wynd, was jailed for 15 months at Hamilton Sheriff Court on Friday.

He admitted presenting a knife and demanding money from Kim Thain at Ashbridge Antiques in Union Street, Larkhall, on February 6.

Watson also admitted stealing a purse containing cash and cards from St Machan’s Parish Church in nearby Church Street.

Scott King, prosecuting, said the shop assistant was behind the counter when Watson arrived and demanded £40.

He told the court: “She refused to hand over any money and told the accused to leave.

“However, he stood where he was and lowered his right arm, exposing the tip of a knife concealed in his sleeve.

“He directed the witness’s attention to this and again asked for money.

“She refused again and Watson then said he was only joking, that he was filming proceedings to see what her reaction would be as part of a prank.

“He then ran from the shop.”

Watson went into St Machan’s Church, claiming he needed to see a minister because his dad had died.

A prayer meeting was taking place and he was asked to wait.

Mr King said: “There was a table beside him at the back of the church.

“A woman aged 73 had left her handbag on the table and the accused took the purse from it.

“People heard him leaving quickly and bags were checked. The woman realised her purse had been taken.

“Police were in the area after being called about the attempted robbery at the shop.

“They saw the accused, who matched the description of the suspect. He was searched and the purse was recovered.

“The woman’s bank cards were still there, but £20 was missing.”

Defence agent Diarmid Bruce said Watson has had difficulties with crack cocaine, heroin and valium. He had been taking drugs that day.

Mr Bruce stated: “He doesn’t know where he was going. He can’t remember having a knife. The lady in the shop was quite brave and the explanation he gave was bizarre.

“He has a record but this is, unfortunately, a step up for him.”

Watson is currently serving a 13-month prison sentence for other offences and his earliest release date is February 19.

Sheriff Thomas Millar said the 15-month stretch will start then.

The sheriff noted that Watson had previous convictions for crimes of dishonesty and described the purse theft as “pretty mean”.
