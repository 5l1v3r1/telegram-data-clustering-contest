Glastonbury 2020: Organisers announce new glamping option for festival

Sticklinch is home to yurts, bell tents and more

20 Nov 2019, 14:33 by Robin Murray

Glastonbury Festival has announced a new glamping option which is perfect for those who aren't the keenest campers.

Sticklinch will be located on the former site of the West Campervan Fields at the 2020 festival, which is the event's 50th anniversary.

It will house pre-erected festival accommodation a short walk from the festival site including a range of podpads, yurts and bell tents, as well as a limited number of the festival's 'signature scout-style tents', and offers a more gentle walk to the festival than the existing Worthy View glamping site.

Glastonbury organisers described the new campsite as 'unpretentious accommodation', adding it's perfect for those who would 'like to have a tent ready and waiting for them on arrival at the festival, and nothing to carry home but their bags'.

The car park for Sticklinch is located in the adjacent field, a short walk away, but organisers encourage guests to travel by public transport where possible.

Prices range from £325 for a two-person scout tent up to £1,150 for the six-person octopad, while a four-person yurt will set you back £850.

A spokesperson said Sticklinch is part of the festival's continued commitment to reducing waste.

How to book

To make a booking, you will be required to enter the registration number you used when booking your festival ticket.

Bookings for Sticklinch Campsite will be taken exclusively by the festival’s nominated booking agency, See Tickets.

Coach and ticket package holders will have access to a pre-sale for Sticklinch accommodation from 6pm on Thursday, November 21 2019.

The pre-sale will be followed by a general release for coach package and general admission ticket holders at 7pm on the same day.
