BMW India makes its petrol cars BS-VI-compliant; diesel ones next

Company to increase the price of BS-VI-compliant vehicles by up to 6% starting next year

20 Nov 2019, 14:45 by Gireesh Babu

Luxury car maker BMW India has announced that its entire petrol portfolio has been tuned to comply with BS-VI emission standards mandated in the country from April 2020. The diesel portfolio would also be converted ahead of time, it said, adding that it planned to increase the price of BS-VI models by up to 6 per cent beginning next year.

The BMW Group Plant in Chennai had started local production of BS-VI diesel variants of the BMW 5 Series and the BMW 6 Series Gran Turismo, the company said. The new BS-VI version of the BMW X1 would also hit the shop-floor of the Chennai plant soon. The company claims to be the first in its segment to offer BS-VI petrol variants of all its models.

Rudratej Singh, president and chief executive of BMW Group India, said the company's new product portfolio had triggered remarkable traction in the luxury car market. "Early start of BS-VI production is a strategic call we are taking to meet the unprecedented demand for our new products. Today, BMW India customers have the choice of buying a BS-VI version across the entire product portfolio.
