KIOKO and RISCAS celebrate launch of Birmingham Music Awards 2020

Birmingham Music Awards launches for 2020 to celebrate the best of the city's music talent

20 Nov 2019, 14:29 by Scott Dickinson, https://www.birminghammail.co.uk/authors/scott-dickinson/

The staple event of Birmingham talent is returning to the city once more to uncover a new wave of musical performers.

Last year’s ceremony proved to be an overwhelming success, pulling in over three thousand applications for a show stopping celebration of a city brimming with undiscovered musicians.

Uniting performers and song writers with record Labels, producers, managers, radio/TV, legals, publishers and agents, the evening is set to be a valuable source of networking for all corners for music.

For applicants, the categories include Best Song, Best Male, Best Female, Best Band, Best Live Venue, Best Rap/Grime Act, Rising Star, Best Radio Station and Best DJ.

We caught up with the BMA founder, Jo Jeffries, to discuss the BMA’s and all things music in Brum.

What can we expect at Birmingham Music Awards 2020?

 
Jo said “At our last ceremony, the incredible band KIOKO were spotted by UB40 and invited to join them on a huge tour. They’ll be performing at the press launch on Wednesday 20th November fresh from their trip.”
“The amazing quartet Riscas will also be performing for us as well.”
Jo remains determined to steer the spotlight from the capital city onto the wealth of talent in Birmingham.
“We need events like this in Birmingham because people are prone to either quitting or moving to London. There are no big labels or publishers over here.”

Trending Talent

Jo is a big fan of Birmingham's musical talent praises a host of bands including:

Flares - Birmingham based indie rock band quartet

North Parade - Indie Alt-pop 4-piece from Birmingham

MC T.Roadz - 13-year-old spitter from Birmingham

The Clause - Alternative indie group born in Birmingham

Mahalia - 21-year-old soul singer

Events in Birmingham’s music scene

The founder of BMA also recommends a host of events coming to the city over the coming months.

5th December, BIMM Birmingham’s end of term gig - she says is “always awesome platforms for new talent; we love working with the students at BIMM and seeing what they can do.”

18th December, BMA Music Network London - this is Christmas party at Mama Roux in which “The Brum music community comes together for one massive festive jolly, dressing for the part and networking.”

January 2020, Discover Birmingham 2020 - she says this initiative throughout January at the sunflower lounge will “exhibit the very best emerging talent from across the Midlands.”

Changes UK and other sponsors

Jo has also ensured to incorporate the work of  ‘Changes UK’ into the event. Identifying themes of addiction and mental health issues in music, she’s determined to make a change.

Speaking of the charity she added “I came across it three years ago, the work they do is astounding. They work to recapture people’s identities and even rehouse them.”

There will also be a corporate presence at the show, with the likes of Tileyard Music and CD Baby set to return as well.
