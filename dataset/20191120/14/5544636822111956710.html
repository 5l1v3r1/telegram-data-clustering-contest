Former Eagles Star Issues Advice To Red-Hot Victor Osimhen

20 Nov 2019, 14:30 by Emmanuel Egobiambu

Super Eagles star Victor Osimhen must be focussed on his game instead of trying to match Rashidi Yekini’s records, says a former international Victor Ikpeba.

Concise News understands that Osimhen has been in a rich form for the West Africans lately, scoring four goals in four matches.

The former U-17 World Cup winner scored twice as Nigeria mauled Lesotho 2-4 in a 2021 Africa Cup of Nations (AFCON) qualifiers last weekend.

Before then, he got a goal as Nigeria saw off the Benin Republic 2-1 in Uyo last week.

Also, he has notched up nine strikes for Lille in seven matches in France but Ikpeba has advised him not to be carried away.

Ikpeba who represented Nigeria in the 1998 world Cup believed it would be too early for the youngster to start comparing himself with Yekini.

“I don’t think it will be right for Victor Osimhen to start comparing himself with late Rashidi Yekini when it comes to goal scoring in the Super Eagles,” Ikpeba told Brila FM.

“Yes, he’s a young striker who has shown so much potentials to be great if he maintains the height that he’s going.

“However, it will be good for him to focus more on developing his goal-scoring skills first.

“As you have known, he started his football career on a wrong foot by joining VfL Wolfsburg and Charleroi after his success at the FIFA U-17 World Cup.

“However, I am delighted that he has gotten it right by joining Lille in Ligue 1, where he is banging in the goal.”
