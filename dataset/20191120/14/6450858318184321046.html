Hunting out the best Fitbit discounts

There are six activity trackers in Fitbit’s current range: Inspire, Inspire HR, Charge 3, Ace 2 (for children), the Versa range (with the Versa 2, Versa Lite and Special editions) and Ionic Fitbit smartwatches. You may also find older models heavily reduced. Their features vary significantly, as do their prices.

This year, Fitbit reduced the price of its top-range Fitbit Ionic by £30 to £249.99, down from £279.99. However, the cheapest we've seen the Fitbit Ionic smartwatch elsewhere is £199.99 on Scan, which is £50 off the usual retail price.

The Fitbit Versa (it lacks only the Ionic's built-in GPS but can connect to your phone's GPS) now has £45 off from Amazon. The latest Versa 2 also has £14 off on Amazon right now.

The Charge 3 is also available with £30 off at Amazon - and we have seen it there for as much as £50 off at £79.99, although this deal seems limited so snap it up if you see it. We also spotted the Special Edition Charge 3 – which comes with an NFC chip, so you’ll be able to use Fitbit Pay and leave your wallet at home – for £125 on Amazon, which is £24.99 off the retail price (available in Frost White or Lavender).

You can also find recently discontinued models such as the Flex 2, Alta, Alta HR, Charge 2, and Zip (clip-on) online – see below.

Fitbit has updated its tracker for kids, the Fitbit Ace. The Ace 2 (based on the Inspire) is now available for £69.99 at the Fitbit Store, while Amazon has it for £64.99.

On the Fitbit accessory side, the Aria 2 wireless scale that syncs with your Fitbit tracker and app are down to £85.99 on Amazon and £97.99 at Scan. It typically costs £119.99.

Discontinued Fitbits offer great value

Consider just-discontinued models, too, for bargains. For example, the Charge 3 was released only in October 2018, so the Charge 2 is at the top of the bargains. The Charge 3 may be newer but the Charge 2 was our top-rated fitness tracker last September, so is well worth considering at a cut-price, and is available in a wide array of colours.

Scan is offering the Blue/Grey Rose Gold Charge 2 (Limited Edition) for £83.99, while Amazon is offering the Lavender Rose Gold version of the Charge 2 Special Edition for £97.76. The Charge 2 (regular edition) is also available at Amazon for £92.97.

Fitbit is still selling the original Versa, but we expect this to be discontinued in the next couple of months.

The minimalist Flex 2 has also been discontinued on the Fitbit Store and there aren't many retailers offering at the moment. The only place where it's available for new is Fruugo, where it's £149.99 though for this price you can get a far more advanced Fitbit. We also haven't used this retailer before.

The Inspire Fitbits have replaced the Alta and Alta HR models, so look out for bargains there, too. Right now the Alta HR is available for £59.98 on eBuyer, which is the cheapest price around apart from Scan where its £71.15.
