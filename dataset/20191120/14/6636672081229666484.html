Man Bites Dog

20 Nov 2019, 14:21 by David Manners

Here’s a yarn of the man bites dog variety: China is giving an American takeover deal a regulatory going over.

CFIUS, the Committee for Foreign Investment in the US, has turned down many an attempt by Chinese companies to buy US companies.

Now the boot is on the other foot.

Diodes Inc of Texas, which bought Zetex, Ferranti Semiconductors’ discretes business in 2008- and, earlier this year – bought the old Natsemi/TI fab at Greenock,  made a $428 million offer, in August,  to buy Lite-On Semiconductor of Taiwan.

Lite-On has a Shanghai affiliate company called On-Bright Electronics with a market value of  $324 million.

Diodes’ take-over of Lite-On would mean control of On-Bright would pass to Diodes.

Lite-On is a power discretes company and On-Bright is an analogue and mixed signal IC company with CMOS, BiCMOS and BCD processes.

It is now being rumoured that the Chinese regulator will only allow the takeover to go ahead if OnBright is cut out of the deal.

The newly aggressive regulatory stance by China has come as a surprise to many.

We keep being told that a US-China trade peace deal is around the corner but this looks like escalated tit-for-tat.
