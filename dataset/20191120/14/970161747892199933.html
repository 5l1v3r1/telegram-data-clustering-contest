BTS will not be exempt from mandatory military service

20 Nov 2019, 14:04

BTS will not be exempt from mandatory military service despite the guidelines being revamped, the Korean culture minister has said.

Bad news for ARMYs everywhere.

While it’s mandatory for able-bodied young men in South Korea to undertake military service, there has previously been an exception for global award-winning athletes and classical musicians.

However, it seems it’s not going to be possible for the K-Pop heroes – especially since the guidelines are being altered to make them more fair.

‘In the case of BTS, I personally wish I could allow exemptions for them under certain standards, but the Military Manpower Administration and the Ministry of National Defense [in charge of conscription] are inclined to downsize the overall scope [of exemption],’ Culture Minister Park Yang-woo said, according to Yonhap News.

He added: ‘Unlike classical arts or sports, it is difficult to fix the criteria of the selection in the popular culture and arts fields, which makes it difficult to institutionalise [a waiver system].’

The eldest member of BTS, Jin, will be required to enlist by the end of 2020, when he turns 28.

However, it doesn’t seem like a disastrous result for the boys, who recently spoke out about the pressure to be perfect, as Jin previously told CBS that they’re fully prepared to be called up.

‘As a Korean, it’s natural, and someday, when duty calls we’ll be ready to respond and do our best,’ he said.

And hopefully they’re getting some inspiration from fellow K-Pop star, Big Bang’s G-Dragon, who recently returned from serving.

The star was officially discharged from his mandatory service on 26 October.

After G-Dragon completed his service, almost 3000 fans waited to see him as he stepped out in Yongin, Gyeonggi Province.

In his uniform, he bowed and said: ‘I’ve returned after completing my military service. Thank you for waiting for me and for coming to see me today.

‘I will be returning to my original occupation, and I will work hard.’
