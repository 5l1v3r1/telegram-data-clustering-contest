Latest General Election 2019 poll after ITV leaders' debate

20 Nov 2019, 14:36

The Tories solid lead over Labour took a hit in the latest general election poll conducted just before the live TV leaders debate.

The latest YouGov poll for Sky News puts the Conservatives on 42%, down three points compared to a poll which closed last Friday.

It means the party has a smaller advantage over the opposition party as the election campaign enters a critical stage of debates and manifestos.

On Friday, a YouGov poll put the Tories at a 17 point advantage over Labour at 45%.

But just ahead of Boris Johnson’s head-to-head with Jeremy Corbyn, the party had slipped while Labour narrowed the gap by two points at 30%.

The YouGov voting intention poll saw the Lib Dems unchanged on 15% and the Brexit Party staying on 4%.

Previous calculations reported on at Metro.co.uk used data from the Britain Elects poll tracker, which weights data from various British polling companies to arrive at an industry average.

The latest Britain Elects figures, up until 18 November, put the Tories at 11 points ahead of Labour with the smaller parties trailing behind.

Following last night’s TV debate between the prime minister and Labour leader Jeremy Corbyn, YouGov’s snap survey suggested it was a close call on who won voters over.

It revealed 51% of Britons believed Mr Johnson won the first leaders’ debate of the General Election compared with 49% for Mr Corbyn.

Those who answered ‘don’t know’ were removed from the result.

Further questioning showed 45% regarded Labour’s Mr Corbyn was more trustworthy compared with 40% for the Prime Minister.

On who came across as more likeable, 54% said Mr Johnson and 37% Mr Corbyn.

But when asked who came across as more in touch with ordinary people, 25% said Mr Johnson and 59% answered Mr Corbyn.
