Lil Nas X, Nipsey Hussle, Tyler, The Creator and More Nominated for 2020 Grammy Awards

20 Nov 2019, 14:07 by Peter A. Berry

The 2020 Grammy Awards nominations are in. On Wednesday morning (Nov. 20), the Recording Academy revealed the full list of nominees for the 62nd annual Grammy Awards. Revered producer Harvey Mason Jr., journalist Gayle King and the host of the 2020 Grammy Awards Alicia Keys introduced the nominees during a special broadcast on CBS This Morning.

Among the full list of talented artists, Meek Mill (Championships), YBN Cordae (The Lost Boy), Tyler, The Creator (Igor), 21 Savage (I Am > I Was), the Dreamville roster (Revenge of the Dreamers III) have all been nominated for Best Rap Album. Meanwhile, Lil Nas X has earned Grammy nominations for Best New Artist, Album of the Year, Record of the Year and more largely thanks to his record-breaking song "Old Town Road (Remix)."

Cordae and Savage were also nominated for Best Rap Song. Cordae's "Bad Idea" featuring Chance The Rapper and Savage's "A Lot" featuring J. Cole join the likes of other major hip-hop records like DaBaby's "Suge," Rick Ross' "Gold Roses" featuring Drake and the late Nipsey Hussle's "Racks in the Middle" featuring Roddy Ricch and Hit-Boy.

The 2020 Grammy Awards will air Jan. 26, 2020 on CBS. Check out the full list of nominations for the 2020 Grammy Awards.
