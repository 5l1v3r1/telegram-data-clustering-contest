Pregnant woman mauled to death by pack of dogs during deer hunt

20 Nov 2019, 14:18

A six-months pregnant woman was mauled to death by dogs while walking in woodland during a deer hunt, investigators said.

Elisa Pilaski, 29, was found dead in the Forest of Retz, around 55 miles northeast of Paris, on Saturday afternoon while a hunt was underway with a pack of hounds.

An autopsy showed she died at some time between 1pm and 1.30pm after suffering ‘bleeding after several dog bites to the upper and lower limbs and the head’, prosecutor Frederic Trinh said.

Some of those wounds had been inflicted ‘post-mortem’, The Guardian reported him adding.

Tests have been carried out on 93 dogs so far, including some from the hunt as well as five of Ms Pilaski’s, in order to try and determine which, if any, were responsible.

Police have reportedly launched a manslaughter probe.

Ms Pilaski telephoned her husband at work at Charles de Gaulle airport during the walk to say she was worried about being attacked by a pack of dogs, according to MailOnline.

He got to the woods 45 minutes later where he discovered her body.

Police have said she may possibly have been attacked by stray dogs or even her own one and have refused to rule out any possibilities.
