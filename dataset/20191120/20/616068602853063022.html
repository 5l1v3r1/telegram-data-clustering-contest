6.1 quake rattles Thailand

20 Nov 2019, 20:06 by The Canadian Press

A strong earthquake shook a border area between northern Thailand and Laos on Thursday morning, swaying Bangkok highrises.

Residents in the northern Thai city of Chiang Mai felt a long period of shaking but saw no major damage. High-rise buildings swayed slowly for at least half a minute in the capital Bangkok, startling residents.

The U.S. Geological Survey said the 6.1 magnitude quake Thursday morning was about 10 kilometres below the surface. Moderate quakes of 4.6 and 5.7 magnitude shook the same area overnight.

It was centred about 31 km from Chaloem Phra Kiat, which is about 280 km northeast of Bangkok.
