Jose Mourinho takes first training session as he makes promise to Spurs fans

20 Nov 2019, 20:23 by Buzz Contributor

Jose Mourinho put his Tottenham stars through their paces today for the first time since replacing Mauricio Pochettino as their new head coach.

The Portuguese, 56, officially took charge of his third English club early this morning after Pochettino saw his five-and-a-half year reign come to an end last night.

It has only been six months since the 47-year-old guided Spurs to their first ever Champions League final, but three wins from 12 in the Premier League this term was labelled “extremely disappointing” in a statement from chairman Daniel Levy confirming his sacking.

And Levy has now turned to Mourinho, who carried out his very first Tottenham training session this afternoon.

In a video posted on Spurs’ official Twitter page, the former Manchester United and Chelsea manager is seen working alongside the likes of Harry Kane, Son Heung-Min and Toby Alderweireld.

The footage is also accompanied by Mourinho’s first interview as head coach, wherein he makes a promise to supporters.

“I couldn’t be happier, and if I was not as happy as I am I wouldn’t be here,” he said.

“What can I promise? Passion - for my job, but also passion for my club.

“It’s a privilege when a manager goes to a club and feels that happiness in relation to the squad he is going to have.

“These are not words of the moment. They are not words of me being the Tottenham head coach.

“These are words that I've been told and I've repeated in the last three, four, five years even as an opponent.”

Mourinho was also full of praise for the facilities on offer, labelling Tottenham’s stadium the best on the planet.

“When you say beautiful stadium you are too humble. You have to say the best stadium in the world,” he said.

“I think that is the reality. The training ground is second to none. It probably can only be compared with some American Football training grounds.”

Tottenham’s woeful start to the 2019/20 campaign has left them down in 14th place, 11 points adrift of the top four.

But Mourinho is adamant he will pull the club out of their slump, while insisting he is relishing the challenge.

“In relation to the Premier League we know where we are and we know that we don't belong there,” he said.

“We should just play match after match, the next match we want to win and that's the same about the next, and the next, and the next until the last.

“At the end of the season we will see where we are but i know we are going to be in a different position to where we are now.

“I look forward to the challenge, to the responsibility to bring happiness to everyone that loves the club.”
