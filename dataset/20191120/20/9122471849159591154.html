Chicago Bears Issue Injury Update On QB Mitch Trubisky

20 Nov 2019, 20:45 by Chris Rosvoglou

Matt Nagy created quite the controversy on Sunday night, when the head coach of the Chicago Bears took out Mitch Trubisky with only a few minutes remaining in the fourth quarter. It generated a bunch of questions about whether or not the former No. 2 pick would remain the starter.

Trubisky is struggling mightily this season for the Bears as he only has 1,580 passing yards and nine touchdowns in nine starts.

Chicago used Chase Daniel earlier this year because of a shoulder injury to Trubisky. However, the coaching staff went right back to the former UNC star once he was healthy.

Even though many people thought Nagy benched Trubisky because of his lack of production on Sunday Night Football, he said that it due to a hip injury.

Despite suffering a hip injury just three days ago, the Bears listed Trubisky as a full participant during Wednesday’s practice.

As you’d expect, there are a bunch of football fans on social media questioning the legitimacy of his injury.
