Nottingham Forest’s Dwight Gayle interest, Newcastle United’s stance & January transfer chances

Nottingham Forest transfer news | The Reds could be looking to bolster their attacking ranks during the January window - and Dwight Gayle has been continually linked

20 Nov 2019, 20:07 by Josh Murray

Nottingham Forest were interested in a summer move for Newcastle United striker Dwight Gayle - but that interest might not be at an end yet.

Forest could reportedly be tempted to make a move for the 29-year-old when the window reopens in January, as Sabri Lamouchi looks to bolster his ranks for a promotion push in the second half of the campaign.

Lewis Grabban currently leads the Reds' top scorer list for the campaign, with seven to his name so far.

But could Gayle provide a deadly strike partner for Grabban at the City Ground?

We take a look at all the latest around Forest's Gayle interest.

'Front of the queue'

Gayle enjoyed a stunning loan season in the Championship in 2018-19 with West Bromwich Albion.

He scored 24 goals in 41 appearances as the Baggies made it to the play-off semi-finals.

West Brom were linked with a second move for Gayle in the summer, when Forest were also interested.

But The Athletic's report last month suggested the Reds could look to "position themselves at the front of the queue for the Newcastle striker" once January comes around.

Steve Bruce on Gayle

While Gayle has made only two appearances so far in this Premier League season.

Both were substitute outings, in the 1-0 win over Chelsea and a 1-1 draw with Wolverhampton Wanderers.

But Newcastle boss Steve Bruce still insists the 29-year-old forward get his chance at St James' Park.

"What happened last year or the past, I am not really interested in when it comes to Dwight," Bruce told The Chronicle last month.

"The thing for Dwight is that whatever number he has on his back he's got the ability to score goals.

"I am delighted that he's around us because he is a very, very good player.

"And I am delighted he's here. He can knuckle down now and fight for his chance."

Lamouchi on January

“January, for me, is so far away. There’s no point talking about players who are not in my squad."

China move a possibility

Reported Forest target Gayle has been linked with a big money move to China.

While Forest and second-tier rivals West Brom could yet look at a January move themselves, the Sunday People report that a transfer to the Chinese Super League could be a possibility.

The report says Gayle and teammate Jonjo Shelvey are "willing to consider" a switch to the Far East.

West Bromwich Albion, where Gayle had a successful loan spell last season, are also said to be eyeing up the striker again.
