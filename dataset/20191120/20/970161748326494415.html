School worker sent note home with boy, 5, telling mother to put him on a diet

20 Nov 2019, 20:41

A cruel school worker was fired for sending a note home with a five year-old boy telling his mom to put him on a diet.

Francesca Easdon, from Kingwood in Texas, was stunned by the note in son Kyler’s bag, which she had originally sent in to try and cheer Kyler up.

Francesca had written a note to Rocking Horse Day Care saying: ‘Please tell Kyler that his mommy loves him so much and I’m thinking about him.’

But an unidentified person – whose handwriting looked too advanced to belong to one of Kyler’s classmates – annotated it with ‘No! Put him on a diet & go away!’

Francesca told KTRK: ‘It took a minute to register, what I even saw and I just kept re-reading it and I just stood there for a minute.’

She said that Kyler is a picky eater, and that she had started putting healthy options in his lunch, but wanted to include the note to cheer him up.

Francesca added: ‘For the record, I feel that Kyler is absolutely perfect the way he is, I’m just helping him make healthier choices.

‘Instead of his school being supportive I am in absolute shock at what happened.’

Rocking Horse are believed to have identified the worker who wrote the note, and fired them, but have not named the worker involved.

But Rocking Horse spokesman Jacques Verron later refused to confirm the dismissal, saying he did not want to be sued.

Easdon said the facility, which Kyler attended for three years, has shown ‘zero remorse for their actions.’

She added: ‘I am disgusted that I put my trust in these people to care for my child and this is what I get in return.’

The mother is now considering withdrawing Kyler from Rocking Horse.
