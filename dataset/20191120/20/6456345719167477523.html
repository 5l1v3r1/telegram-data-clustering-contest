Yarra River is the tonic for Melbourne gin distillery

20 Nov 2019, 20:00

A gin distillery situated in a former brothel in the CBD plans to make a new batch using water from the Yarra River.

The innovative idea from Little Lon Distilling emerged when it was named as one of the finalists in the City of Melbourne’s small business and social enterprise grants scheme.

Other inspired ideas to make the shortlist include a podiatry business designing football boots for women’s feet, a skills development agency for disadvantaged women and a program to authenticate Aboriginal art.

Lord Mayor Sally Capp said it was fantastic to see so many ideas come to life.

“From new ideas that need investment to get off the ground, to established businesses wishing

to expand, these grants help foster our city’s strong culture of enterprise and innovation across

many sectors,” Ms Capp said.

She highlighted the more unusual idea from a Melbourne-based distillery, which would see Yarra River water used as an ingredient in a new taste of gin.

“The team from Little Lon Distilling have proven before that they have the right ingredients for success and we are proud to be supporting the development of this new distinctively

Melbourne product,” the Lord Mayor said.

A record 274 groups put forward ideas this year, with the winner to snap up a share in $500,000 to go towards their project.
