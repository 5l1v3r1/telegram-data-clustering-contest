Mo Salah and Andy Robertson doubtful for Liverpool's trip to Crystal Palace

20 Nov 2019, 20:26 by James Ducker
Follow your club now for first access to all our news, views and analysis Liverpool

Liverpool could be without Mohamed Salah and Andrew Robertson for Saturday’s Premier League game at Crystal Palace.

The pair were forced to pull out of international duty with ankle injuries and are now serious doubts for the trip to Selhurst Park.​

But Jurgen Klopp is expected to have Joe Gomez available after the defender withdrew from the England squad before their 4-0 win over Kosovo on Sunday after suffering a minor knee injury in training.​

Midfielders Jordan Henderson and Georginio Wijnaldum, both of whom suffered some illness during the international break, are also expected to play.​

Salah has been struggling with an ankle problem since a heavy challenge from Leicester’s Hamza Choudhury during Liverpool’s 2-1 win early last month.​

It forced the Egypt forward out of Liverpool’s 1-1 draw against Manchester United at Old Trafford – the only time they have dropped points in the Premier League this season – and he aggravated the problem after being caught by Fernandinho, the Manchester City midfielder, during the 3-1 win at Anfield this month.​

Salah flew to Egypt to be assessed and returned to Merseyside wearing a protective boot after withdrawing from his country’s matches against Kenya and Comoros.​

Robertson was also forced to withdraw from Scotland’s international games against Cyprus and Kazakhstan with a swollen ankle sustained in the City win.​

The potential loss of two highly influential players will be a blow as Liverpool bid to maintain or extend their eight point lead at the top of the Premier League.
