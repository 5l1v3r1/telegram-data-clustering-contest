NEMO Senior Services held ribbon cutting ceremony Wednesday for renovated dining room

20 Nov 2019, 12:05 by Matt Gunn

KIRKSVILLE, Mo. — On Wednesday, the NEMO Senior Services, Inc. and the Kirksville Housing Authority held a ribbon cutting ceremony for their brand new dining room.

The dining room had not seen any substantial renovations in almost two decades.

KTVO spoke with Patti Preston, executive director of Kirksville Housing Authority, and Bridget Morton, executive director of NEMO Senior Services, Inc., about what the accomplishment of having this renovated dining room means to them.

“My goal was to make it a pleasant atmosphere so that folks in the community would feel comfortable coming in and enjoying a great meal from an organization that provides a great service to our community,” Preston said.
“We were so pleased that Patti and the housing authority and her board were able to complete this remodel for us. To watch our tenant or our customers and clients come in and look at all the different new things on the walls they smile. We wanted this to be a place called home,” Morton said.

After the ribbon cutting ceremony, the crowd was able to stick around and enjoy a nice Thanksgiving dinner.
