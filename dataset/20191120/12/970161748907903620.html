Ederson injury update ahead of Man City's huge clash with Chelsea

20 Nov 2019, 12:21

Ederson is set to return to the Manchester City team for their Premier League clash with Chelsea on Saturday after recovering from injury.

The injury, which was only described as a ‘muscular problem’ by City boss Pep Guardiola, kept Ederson out of the 3-1 defeat to Liverpool.

It also saw him substituted during the Champions League draw with Atalanta which ended with Kyle Walker in goal after Claudio Bravo was sent off.

However, City’s goalkeeping woes appear to be at an end after Ederson was pictured in training on Tuesday.

The Brazilian now looks like he will be back in first team action, as expected, at home to Chelsea in the 5.30pm kick-off on Saturday.

Guardiola said after the Liverpool defeat on 10 November: ‘Now he’s going to Brazil to recover there, when he comes back hopefully he’ll be fit.’

The Premier League champions are fourth in the table, one place and one point behind Chelsea, and will be desperate to get back to winning ways against the Blues.

They remain without long-term injury concerns Leroy Sane, Aymeric Laporte and Oleksandr Zinchenko for the game at the Etihad, and will also be missing Bernardo Silva through suspension.

However, Guardiola has Rodri and David Silva available to him again after injury.

City are already nine points behind Liverpool in the title race and cannot afford another slip up as they host a side that has won their last six Premier League games on the spin.
