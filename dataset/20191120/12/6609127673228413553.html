ALF TALK

Rangers striker Alfredo Morelos is a ‘January target’ for French side St Etienne

20 Nov 2019, 12:00 by Gary Taylor

RANGERS striker Alfredo Morelos is a transfer target for French side St Etienne, according to reports.

The Rangers hitman has been in great form so far this campaign, scoring 23 goals in all competitions for club and country.

His red-hot form is attracting plenty of attention from outfits around Europe, and the Ligue One side are believed to be keen on a January transfer bid.

According to Le Progres, a media site in France, Claude Puel wants the Colombian to add to his side's firepower up front.

The former Southampton and Leicester City boss is believed to be ready to sanction a move for the 23-year-old when the next window opens in just over a month.

Last season's Scottish Premiership top goalscorer has continued to impress under Rangers manager Steven Gerrard this season, with 22 of his goals coming at club level for the Light Blues.

This includes 11 goals in the Europa League, with the frontman shining against the likes of Porto, Young Boys and Legia Warsaw.

He notched his first international goal for Colombia on Friday night against Peru, and started last night too against Ecuador.

It's not the first French team to have registered their interest in the attacker, with Bordeaux too monitoring his progress.

SunSport also exclusively revealed that EPL side Cyrstal Palace are preparing a £20million bid.

Fellow English outfit Aston Villa have also been credited with an interest in Morelos.

Both Rangers gaffer Steven Gerrard and director of football Ross Wilson are adamant that the Ibrox club do not need to sell their prized assets in January.

This came after the club's financial figures uncovered that they'd made an annual loss of £11.3million.
