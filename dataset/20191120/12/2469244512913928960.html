Cambridge United boss slams 'absolute nonsense' defending for Grecians winning goal

“I have to say, if we can’t defend that movement for their goal, we’re in real trouble."

20 Nov 2019, 12:46 by Andrew Georgeson, Daniel Clark

Cambridge United boss Colin Calderwood slammed his side's defending that led to Alex Fisher's winner for Exeter City in last night's FA Cup replay as “absolute nonsense.”

Fisher grabbed the winner with 20 minutes to go to book the Grecians spot in the Second Round in a game that saw few chances for either side.

He volleyed the ball in at the near post from an Ben Seymour cross, after the City youngster had been played in by Archie Collins.

Calderwood, speaking after the game, said his side didn't do enough going forward but that the defending for the goal was a nonsense, CambridgeshireLive reports.

“I don’t think we had enough of those moments where we produced anything with any reasonable level of quality that might have troubled them,” the U’s head coach said.

“Certainly there were two or three moments first half where we found a nice move – one of which was called offside – but in the second half everything came from so deep in our own half and we haven’t troubled their goalie at all.

“I have to say, if we can’t defend that movement for their goal, we’re in real trouble. That is nonsense, absolute nonsense. Everyone is in a shape, everyone is in a position and it can’t go in, but it does.

“Listen, we’ve frustrated them, we were waiting for a moment of inspiration and [it didn’t come].  Through that period, you got a feeling they started to get a little bit more on top, and a little bit more dangerous, in the end the team with the greater attacking quality tonight has won the game.”

He continued: “It can’t, shouldn’t [be a goal]. You stand here with your fingers crossed that it won’t go in, in the future, because it’s next to rubbish in the alphabet.”

Exeter City will now host Hartlepool United in the Second Round, with the game kicking off at 2pm on Sunday, December 1.
