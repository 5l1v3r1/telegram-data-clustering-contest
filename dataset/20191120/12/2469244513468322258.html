Budding bards get into books and reading with professional poet

Professional poet Rachel Rooney ran a workshop with pupils at Halberton and Uplowman Primary School

20 Nov 2019, 12:42 by Lewis Clarke

Young Shakespeares and Wordsworths have been honing their poetry prowess.

Professional poet Rachel Rooney ran a workshop with pupils at Halberton and Uplowman Primary School on Friday, November 8 as well as heading to see a performance at the Northcott Theatre in Exeter thanks to The Children’s Bookshow charity.

Pupils also received free poetry books and the opportunity to take part in the workshop. The children learnt about how poems are written, structured and presented.

Head of teaching and learning at Uplowman, Rebecca Hart, said: “It has been fantastic to see the children working together and learning about writing poetry.

“They have had a chance to work alongside a professional poet and understand more about playing with language and creating entertaining poems. They have worked well and shared some great ideas.”

Christine Campion from Halberton added: “I have been impressed with the poetry our children have created. I think they have been inspired, and for many, it will have been their first time seeing a performance poet. We will be creating a book with the poetry they have produced, and we look forward to sharing them with parents and pupils.”

Rachel Rooney said: “I had a fabulous time at Halberton Primary. They’ve built a lovely library for the pupils there, and the staff have a love of books and reading.

“I always enjoy visiting schools and engaging pupils with poetry. I was honoured to read poems that their pupils were inspired to write after my visit. They are brilliant - and I better watch out as I clearly have some competition.”
