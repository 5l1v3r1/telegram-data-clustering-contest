Lyric Theatre planning 'Grease,' 'Kinky Boots,' 'Matilda,' world premiere Native American musical 'Distant Thunder' for 2020 season

20 Nov 2019, 12:03 by Brandy McDonnell

From a world premiere musical steeped in Native American culture and a rollicking royal show based on the songs of The Go-Go's to the family-friendly magic of Roald Dahl's "Matilda" and the high-heeled hilarity of the Tony-winning "Kinky Boots," Lyric Theatre of Oklahoma is ratcheting up for an eclectic 2020 season.

And if all that isn't enough, the venerable Oklahoma City professional nonprofit theater will "Grease" the gears with a tuneful theater and movie classic.

The 2020 subscription season will include three productions at Lyric's Plaza Theatre, 1725 NW 16, plus three summer shows at the Civic Center Music Hall, 201 N Walker.

Season subscriptions are on sale at lyrictheatreokc.com/season-subscription, by phone at 524-9312 or at the Lyric Theatre offices from noon to 5:30 p.m. Monday-Friday at 1727 NW 16 in the Plaza District.

Also, the theater is planning two 2020 extra productions at the Plaza Theatre that can be added on to season subscriptions: Dr. Seuss' "The Cat in the Hat," the fourth installment of Lyric’s for Young Audiences Series, and "A Christmas Carol," the 2020 edition of Lyric's annual holiday favorite.

For more information, go to lyrictheatreokc.com.

Lyric Theatre 2020 subscription season

'HAVING OUR SAY'

LYRIC AT THE PLAZA

Feb. 19-March 8

By Emily Mann

Adapted from the book by Sarah L. Delany and A. Elizabeth Delany with Amy Hill Hearth

Directed by Monique Midgett

Celebrate the story of a century with 103-year-old Sadie and 101-year-old Bessie, famously known as the Delany sisters. From the old Jim Crow South, to renaissance Harlem, and ultimately as a professional teacher and a dentist, respectively, their stories weave a rich tapestry of lives lived triumphantly. While making dinner (live, on stage) these two African American sisters tell us of the last century as they lived it – with true stories that touch the soul and your funny bone – filled with humor, faith, love, and family. You’ll just love your date with these feisty sisters at Lyric’s intimate Plaza Theatre.

'DISTANT THUNDER'

LYRIC AT THE PLAZA

March 25-April 11

WORLD PREMIERE

Book by Lynne Taylor-Corbett and Shaun Taylor-Corbett

Music and Lyrics by Chris Wiseman and Shaun Taylor-Corbett

Additional Music and Lyrics by Robert Lindsey-Nassif and Michael Moricz

Directed by Lynne Taylor-Corbett

This story steeped in American Indian culture is the 2020 installment in Lyric's ongoing New Works Initiative. In this new American musical, Darrell Waters, a successful young attorney, returns to his childhood home in Montana to broker a deal between a big energy company and the impoverished Blackfeet Nation. He soon faces his reclusive father about their painful past and grapples with the paradigm of what it means to be Native American in the United States. Cultures collide and unite through music, dance, stories, and faith as the audience pays witness the dawning of a "Distant Thunder."

ROALD DAHL'S 'MATILDA'

SUMMER AT THE CIVIC

June 23-28

Book for the musical by Dennis Kelly

Based on the book by Roald Dahl

Music and lyrics by Tim Minchin

Directed by Ashley Wells

The inspiring story of an extraordinary little girl, who, gifted with a vivid imagination and a sharp wit, dares to prove that you can change your destiny despite all odds. While surviving a tumultuous life with her unscrupulous parents and tyrannical headmistress, Matilda discovers a secret that will liberate her fellow students. Packed with high-energy dance and songs, this Tony Award-winning musical revels in the anarchy of childhood, the power of imagination, and the inspiring story of a girl who dreams of a better life while becoming the greatest little hero. Appropriate for the whole family.

'GREASE'

SUMMER AT THE CIVIC

July 7-12

Book, music and lyrics by Jim Jacobs and Warren Casey

Directed by Michael Baron

“Grease is the word!” Hand-jive the night away with the hit musical in a new Lyric Theatre production. A secret summer romance between the new girl in town and the school’s coolest cat finds its way into Rydell High’s school year. These were “the good times” where hula-hoops, bouffants and hot rods were all the rage, and rock 'n' roll was king. Have a blast with Danny, Sandy, Rizzo and the gang in this production featuring all the unforgettable songs from the show and the hit movie, including “You’re The One That I Want,” “Grease is the Word,” “Summer Nights,” “Hopelessly Devoted to You” and more. Occasionally contains strong language and situations.

'KINKY BOOTS'

SUMMER AT THE CIVIC

July 21-26

Book by Harvey Fierstein

Music and lyrics by Cyndi Lauper

Based on the Miramax motion picture "Kinky Boots"

Written by Geoff Deane and Tim Firth

“Step into a dream where glamour is extreme!” This exhilarating Tony Award winner is inspired by the true story of a struggling shoe factory that will lift audiences' spirits to high-heeled heights. Charlie Price has reluctantly inherited his father’s shoe factory, which is on the verge of bankruptcy. While trying to save his family business, Charlie finds inspiration from Lola, a fabulous drag entertainer in need of some sturdy stilettos. As they work to turn the factory around, this unlikely pair find that they have more in common than they ever dreamed possible, and discover that when you change your mind, you can change your world. Occasionally contains strong language and situations.

'HEAD OVER HEELS'

LYRIC AT THE PLAZA

Sept. 23-Oct. 11

Songs by The Go-Go’s

Conceived by Jeff Whitty

Original Book by Jeff Whitty
Book adapted by James Magruder

Based on "The Arcadia" by Sir Philip Sidney

Directed by Michael Baron

A fearlessly fresh, new musical mash-up of posh and punk, this unpredictable Elizabethan romp follows the escapades of a royal family on an outrageous journey to save their beloved kingdom from extinction – only to discover the key to their realm’s survival lies within each of their own hearts. It’s a laugh-out-loud adventure of dramatic irony and comical courtship told through verse set to the iconic pop music of 1980s all-female rock band The Go-Go’s, featuring dance-in-the-aisle hit songs like “We Got the Beat,” “Vacation” and more. Occasionally contains strong language and situations.

Lyric Theatre 2020 add-on productions

DR. SEUSS' 'THE CAT IN THE HAT'

LYRIC AT THE PLAZA

Jan. 23-Feb. 9

Based on the book by Dr. Seuss

Play originally produced by the National Theatre of Great Britain

Adapted and originally directed by Katie Mitchell

Lyric Theatre brings the classic children’s book to life in a wild ride of physical comedy. Sally and her brother are stuck home in the rain, when a Cat comes knocking at the window pane. But the Cat has brought friends, Thing One and Thing Two, who make messes and problems and oh, what to do?! Mischief and mayhem and laughter galore, bring the kids – they’ll clamor for more!

LYRIC'S 'A CHRISTMAS CAROL'

LYRIC AT THE PLAZA

Nov. 27-Dec. 24, 2020

By Charles Dickens

Directed by Michael Baron

Hark! Oklahoma City’s favorite holiday tradition returns with its 10th anniversary edition guaranteed to delight audiences of all ages. Get into the holiday spirit and “come a-caroling.”
