Gold rate today: Here is how the yellow metal will trade today

Bullion counter may remain positive, said brokerage house SMC Global.

20 Nov 2019, 04:35 by ETMarkets.com

Precious metals were trading flat with a negative bias amid increasing tensions between the US and China and a weaker rupee.

The prospect for progress on trade seemed to dim when China condemned a US Senate measure on Hong Kong, vowing to take the steps necessary to safeguard its sovereignty and security. The Senate unanimously passed legislation aimed at protecting human rights in Hong Kong.

MCX Gold (Dec) futures were 0.05 per cent to Rs 38,187 while MCX Silver (Dec) futures shed 0.03 per cent to Rs 44,848.

Bullion counter may remain positive, said brokerage house SMC Global. Gold can move higher towards Rs 38,300 while taking support near Rs 38,000 and silver can test Rs 45,000 while taking support near Rs 44,600, it added.

In the US markets, gold prices rose on Wednesday on heightened worries that a Sino-US trade deal could hit a snag after President Donald Trump threatened fresh tariffs on Beijing as the US Senate passed a bill supporting Hong Kong anti-government protesters.

Spot gold rose 0.2 per cent to $1,474.42 per ounce at 0144 GMT, reported Reuters. US gold futures were little changed at $1,474.80 per ounce.

According to technical charts, spot gold may rise into a range of $1,480-$1,485 per ounce, driven by a wave c, said a Reuters technical analyst.

Indeed, the metal has more or less broken $1,474. The next resistance will be at $1,480, the 50 per cent retracement of the downtrend from $1,515.41 to $1,445.18.

A break below $1,462 could confirm the completion of the bounce and the resumption of the downtrend. On the daily chart, gold seems to be edging up towards $1,500, the channel line resistance.
