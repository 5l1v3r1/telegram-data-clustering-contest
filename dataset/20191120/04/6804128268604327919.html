Spurs news: Mauricio Pochettino sacked, perfect January transfer, Giovani Lo Celso boost

Giovani Lo Celso, Mauricio Pochettino, Daniel Levy and Tim Sherwood feature in our round-up of Tottenham news

20 Nov 2019, 04:00 by Lee Wilmot, Amie Wilson

The countdown to the weekend has begun, and it has been a turbulent start to the week for Spurs

Tottenham travel across London to take on rivals West Ham United in the lunchtime kick-off on Saturday, hoping to finally kickstart their ailing season, but the will do so without manager Mauricio Pochettino after he was sacked.

It has been a tough campaign for the Tottenham supporters, to watch their side struggle just months after a Champions League final.

Rumours around the Spurs manager had been circulating in recent days, and it all came to a head early on Tuesday evening.

Read all that and more in our round-up of the biggest news stories from around Tottenham Hotspur Football Club.

Mauricio Pochettino sacked

Where else to start? There were a plethora of stories suggesting Mauricio Pochettino was nearing the Spurs exit on Monday night.

And them reports were founded, when the club announced the dismissal of manager Pochettino on Tuesday night.

The Club can today announce that Mauricio Pochettino and his coaching staff Jesus Perez, Miguel D’Agostino and Antoni Jimenez have been relieved of their duties," read a statement on the club website.

"Chairman, Daniel Levy, said: "We were extremely reluctant to make this change and it is not a decision the Board has taken lightly, nor in haste.

“Regrettably domestic results at the end of last season and beginning of this season have been extremely disappointing.

“It falls to the Board to make the difficult decisions - this one made more so given the many memorable moments we have had with Mauricio and his coaching staff - but we do so in the Club's best interests

“Mauricio and his coaching staff will always be part of our history. I have the utmost admiration for the manner in which he dealt with the difficult times away from a home ground whilst we built the new stadium and for the warmth and positivity he brought to us. I should like to thank him and his coaching staff for all they have contributed. They will always be welcome here.

"We have a talented squad. We need to re-energise and look to deliver a positive season for our supporters.

"We shall provide an update on new coaching staff in due course."

Jose Mourinho is the runaway favourite to take over the role from the Argentine, with some reports suggesting that a deal with the former Chelsea and Manchester United manager is close.

Spurs are in a terrible run of form, with just three wins from their 12 Premier League matches so far.

The perfect January signing

Tottenham fans will be hoping to see the team busy in the January transfer window.

Fourteenth place in the Premier League after 12 games is not exactly what the supporters would have been expecting or hoping for ahead of the campaign.

And if they are to get out of their current predicament and move up the table, new signings could help that.

So who should Tottenham sign?

We take a look at the perfect person.

Lo Celso boost

Tottenham are hoping Giovani Lo Celso can play a big role between now and the end of the season.

Lo Celso joined on a season-long loan from Real Betis on transfer deadline day and Spurs have an option to make the move permanent in January or the summer.

The midfielder was signed as a potential replacement for Christian Eriksen, who, although did not leave in the summer, is expected to move on next summer.

And Spurs have been handed a boost in terms of Lo Celso's ability to make an impact between now and the rest of the season.

Blame the players

Former Tottenham manager Tim Sherwood has defended his successor Mauricio Pochettino from criticism, stating that the players must carry the responsibility for the club's struggles.

Spurs return from the international break with an 11-point deficit to make up in the hunt for Champions League football again next season, with the gap potentially being extended this weekend if they cannot defeat West Ham, form which has led to Pochettino losing his job.
