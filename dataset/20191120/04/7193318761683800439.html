Trisha Yearwood to appear Wednesday on 'Good Morning America'

20 Nov 2019, 04:27 by Brandy McDonnell

Country music superstar Trisha Yearwood, a former Owasso resident, will appear Wednesday on "Good Morning America."

"GMA" airs from 7 to 9 p.m. weekdays on ABC.

As previously reported, Yearwood will host the 10th annual “CMA Country Christmas,” which will air at 8 p.m. Tuesday, Dec. 3 on ABC.

Yearwood will also perform on the Country Music Association’s holiday special, with Broken Arrow native Kristin Chenoweth, Rascal Flatts (which includes guitarist Joe Don Rooney, who grew up in Picher), For King & Country, Chris Janson, Tori Kelly, Lady Antebellum, Runaway June, CeCe Winans, Brett Young and Chris Young joining her on the lineup.

Also as previously reported, Yearwood released her new album “Every Girl,” her first full-length country music collection in more than a decade, in August. The long-awaited follow-up to 2007’s critically acclaimed “Heaven, Heartache and the Power of Love," her new country album debuted less than six months after the bow of "Let's Be Frank," a Frank Sinatra covers collection she had wanted to record for 20 years.
