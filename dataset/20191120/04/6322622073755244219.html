Malaysia Oct CPI up 1.1%, driven by food and beverages

20 Nov 2019, 04:00 by Surin Murugiah

KUALA LUMPUR (Nov 20): Malaysia's inflation, as measured by the consumer price index (CPI), rose 1.1% year-on-year (y-o-y) in October to 122 from 120.7 previously, according to the Department of Statistics Malaysia.

In a statement today, chief statistician Datuk Seri Dr Mohd Uzir Mahidin said out of 552 items covered in CPI, 351 items showed a y-o-y increase in October 2019.

"On the contrary, 150 items declined while 51 items were unchanged," he said.

The increase in the overall index was driven by the index of alcoholic beverages and tobacco (2.2%), miscellaneous goods and services (2.2%), education (1.9%), and food and non-alcoholic beverages (1.8%).

On a monthly basis, the CPI increased 0.2% to 122.

The increase was contributed by communication (1.6%), transport (0.2%), food and non-alcoholic beverages (0.1%), health (0.1%), and education (0.1%).

Meanwhile, the CPI for the period of January to October 2019 increased 0.6% as compared to the same period last year.

The department said three states, namely Wilayah Persekutuan Kuala Lumpur (1.3%), Pulau Pinang (1.3%), and Selangor and Wilayah Persekutuan Putrajaya (1.2%), surpassed the national CPI rate of 1.1% in October 2019 as compared to the same month in the preceding year.

It said all states registered an increase in the index of food and non-alcoholic beverages. The highest increases were recorded by Johor (2.5%), followed by Wilayah Persekutuan Kuala Lumpur (2.4%), Pulau Pinang (2.1%), and Selangor and Wilayah Persekutuan Putrajaya (2%).

The increase surpassed the national index of food and non-alcoholic beverages in October 2019.
