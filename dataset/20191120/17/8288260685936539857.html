Fresh Manchester United injury update provided - including Paul Pogba hint for Aston Villa

Latest Aston Villa news | Dean Smith's AVFC take on Man Utd at Old Trafford on December 1

20 Nov 2019, 17:09 by Shane Ireland, https://www.facebook.com/ShaneIrelandJournalist

Manchester United have a number of injury concerns ahead of a busy run of fixtures which includes Aston Villa’s visit to Old Trafford.

Villa face Ole Gunnar Solskjaer’s side on Sunday, December 1 after United have taken on Sheffield United in the Premier League and Astana in the Europa League.

Midfielders Paul Pogba and Scott McTominay are among the players who have been nursing injuries recently, while former Villa loanee Axel Tuanzebe has also been sidelined.

And it looks likely that United will be without Pogba against Villa as he continues his recovery from an ankle problem.

The France star has featured in just two of the last 14 matches for his club and the Manchester Evening News reports he isn’t likely to return to action until early December, which could rule him out of the clash with Dean Smith’s side.

Diogo Dalot, Eric Bailly and Timothy Fosu-Mensah can also be considered doubts for Villa even though there is still 10 days to recover, while the severity of McTominay’s problem is unclear.

McTominay pulled out of the Scotland squad for European Championships qualifiers and Solskjaer said the ankle injury could keep him out for between two and eight weeks.
