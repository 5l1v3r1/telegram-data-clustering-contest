Hearts-daft Lee McGregor wants Kash Farooq rematch as he holds Tynecastle dream

The capital boxer won a contentious split decision over his Glasgow opponent last weekend.

20 Nov 2019, 17:23 by Ross Pilcher, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/ross-pilcher/

British bantamweight boxing champion Lee McGregor wants a rematch with Glasgow’s Kash Farooq at Tynecastle and believes he could sell out the famous old ground.

McGregor, a lifelong Jambo, won a controversial split decision over Farooq at the Emirates Arena last Saturday to add the Lonsdale belt to the Commonwealth title he already held.

Many fight fans believed the decision should have gone to Farooq, but ringside judges scored the Edinburgh boxer as the winner.

McGregor has spoken before about his dream of fighting at Tynecastle, but it could now be a reality after a meteoric rise to prominence over the last year.

"A year ago I would have said no, but now it is crazy - the support and the fanbase I am building is really exciting.

"Everyone knows I am diehard Jambo and Hearts are right on board with me.

"I am sure the fight at Tynecastle will happen one day. I think Kash is the perfect opponent to sell that fight out. It will probably be for European or world honours.

"I'm not counting anything out at the moment, I'll keep preparing and training hard and I'll be ready for whatever my team bring.”

Despite his allegiances lying in the maroon half of the capital, McGregor, who is a former Cyclone stablemate of world champion and Hibs fan Josh Taylor , wants the whole of Auld Reekie behind him.

“I also want the whole city behind me - the whole of Edinburgh and the whole of Scotland behind me,” he told STV .

"Although I'm a Hearts fan and although Tynecastle is my dream venue I want Hibs fans to get behind me and support me as well.

"I want to gather the whole city, the two teams, together and let's make some special nights come to Edinburgh."

On his fight with Farooq, McGregor can see why there were questions based on the margins of the scorecards, rather than the outright result.

He added: "At the end of the day it was a close, grueling fight that could have gone either way and the judges preferred my work - it is as simple as that.

"I think the scorecard is more what the outcry was about, rather than me winning.

"It's just making me more hungry, so if the re-match gets made then let's get it on - I am more than happy because I was born to do this."
