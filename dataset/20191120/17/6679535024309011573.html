SASTRA gets UGC nod to offer courses

Through open and distance learning mode

20 Nov 2019, 17:20 by Special Correspondent

SASTRA (Shanmugha Arts, Science, Technology and Research Academy), Thanjavur, figures among two other deemed to be universities and a State university in the updated list of higher educational institutions released on Wednesday by the University Grants Commission (UGC) for grant of graded autonomy, entitling them to offer programmes through open and distance learning.

The other three institutions are Alagappa University, Karaikudi; SRM Institute of Science and Technology, Kattankulathur; and Sri Ramachandra Institute of Higher Education and Research, Chennai.

The UGC had published a list of 25 higher educational institutions countrywide for grant of graded autonomy.

These institutions have been permitted to conduct admissions in two sessions: from January to February end, and from July to August end.

List

The UGC had initially published a list of higher educational institutions on July 31 specifying their recognition to offer programmes in ODL mode.

The UGC notice states that these institutions cannot start or run any professional courses governed by specific Acts of Parliament except with prior and specific approval of the authority constituted thereunder. Courses in engineering, medicine, dental, pharmacy, nursing, architecture, physiotherapy, agriculture, hotel management cannot be offered in ODL mode.

Likewise, no university, institution, deemed to be a university and college can conduct M.Phil. and Ph.D. programmes through distance education mode.
