Cambuslang baker lands awards at cake show - thanks to Sylvester Stallone

Anna Dillon won gold and silver at the Cake International competition at the NEC in Birmingham.

20 Nov 2019, 17:13 by Emylie Howie

A Cambuslang baker’s remarkable confectionery creations resulted in sweet success at the world’s biggest cake show.

Anna Dillon rose to the challenge and iced competition from around the globe to land herself not one, but two prestigious accolades at the glittering event.

The Drumsagard resident’s extraordinary cakes bagged her a gold and silver award at the Cake International competition at the NEC in Birmingham.

Anna’s incredibly lifelike Sylvester Stallone creation complete with “muscles and wrinkles” put her on the Rocky road to gold at the international competition.

And an equally impressive replica of Poppy, the family pet, scooped silver.

A delighted Anna told the Reformer: “To get gold and silver is an absolute dream come true.

“I didn’t expect it at all.

“Poppy, our Beagle pup, is only eight months old and we all love her, so I thought, ‘I’m going to design a cake of her’.

“She’s standing on one leg with those crazy eyes in an action shot because that shows her at her best.

“Plus no-one has ever done a dog standing on one leg before so I went for it. I am so happy my wee Poppy won a prize.

“As for my Sylvester Stallone , the inspiration for that came because the characteristics of his face are really good to recreate.

“He looks amazing, so it was really good to get his wrinkles and muscles included.

“To beat people from China, Japan, Italy and Germany left me totally gobsmacked, but also delighted.”

The 51-year-old care worker started baking eight years ago when she owned a snack van and made tray bakes to sell.

From there, Anna was asked to make birthday cakes for friends and family which then snowballed into baking the elaborate efforts she enters for competitions.

Other famous faces Anna has recreated include the late George Michael, Bruce Forsyth and Freddie Mercury, as well as Wolverine and even ET.

“From baking to decorating something like Sylvester Stallone takes about four days but I really love it, it’s a great passion of mine,” Anna explained.

“I have another competition in April in London that I’m looking forward to.

“My husband is so supportive of me competing; he isn’t creative but he certainly enjoys the fact that I am always baking at home.”

Anna’s recent success has landed her a plum role on the judging panel of a competition next September.

The organisers of the Scottish Baking Awards have asked her to judge the novelty cake category next year alongside some celebrity judges.

Anna added: “I am honestly so excited. Especially when we have Phil and Christine Jenson from Extreme Cake Makers judging the same class as me.

“I introduced myself to Phil earlier in the month and he said he knew who I was.

“I thought I was dreaming. It was surreal. I couldn’t believe Phil from Extreme Cake Makers knew little me and my cakes.”
