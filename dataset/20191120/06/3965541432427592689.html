Happy World Vegan Month!

20 Nov 2019, 06:30 by Herb Weisbaum

Do you find yourself eating less meat and more plant-based foods than you did a few years ago?

If so, you're not alone. Retail sales of plant-based foods in the U.S. are up 11 percent in the past year.

A vegan diet – with no animal products, including no eggs or dairy products – is also growing in popularity.

"With that transition and going completely plant-based, you have less of an intake of saturated fat, you have a lot of fruits, you have a lot of vegetables, whole grains, legumes, soy products, nuts, seeds, all of those are rich fiber and phytochemicals,” said Amy Kimberlain, a spokesperson for the Academy of Nutrition and Dietetics.

A vegan diet can help lower your cholesterol level, improve blood sugar and reduce the risk of some forms of cancer.

Because they don’t eat any meat, fish, poultry, eggs or cheese, vegans need to make sure they’re fulfilling their nutritional needs.

"Certain key nutrients that you would want to pay attention to when going vegan would be overall protein intake, iron, calcium, vitamin D, vitamin B-12, zinc and omega 3s,” Kimberlain explained. “When planned properly and consumed throughout the day, you're able to receive enough, but it takes careful planning."
