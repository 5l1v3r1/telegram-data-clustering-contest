Intel to regain semiconductor crown in 2019

20 Nov 2019, 06:50 by Jessie Shen, DIGITIMES, Taipei

Intel is expected to rank as the largest semiconductor supplier and have sales that are 26% larger than Samsung in 2019, according to IC Insights.

The memory market is set to fall 34% this year, which will affect negatively revenues at Samsung, SK Hynix and Micron. The top-3 memory chipmakers are each forecast to register 29% and more than 29% on-year declines in 2019, IC Insights indicated.

The world's top-15 semiconductor companies will see their combined sales decrease 15% in 2019 compared to 2018, 2pp lower than the expected total worldwide semiconductor industry decline of 13%, IC Insights noted.

SK Hynix will log the biggest decline among the top-15 companies with a 38% plunge in sales this year, while other major memory vendors (Samsung, Micron and Toshiba/Kioxia) are also expected to suffer double-digit sales declines, IC Insights said.

Only three of the top-15 companies are forecast to register year-over-year growth in 2019 - Sony, TSMC and MediaTek, according to IC Insights.
