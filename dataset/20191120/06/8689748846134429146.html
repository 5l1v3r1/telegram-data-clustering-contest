‘D-Black Bit More Than He Could Chew’- Joey B

20 Nov 2019, 06:52 by BB Jones

Rapper Joey B has blamed his friend and colleague D-Back for signing too many artists onto his record label which resulted in a mass exodus.

Rapper and business mogul, D-Black had the likes of Wisa, Dahlin Gage, Freda Rhymz and Koblah Jnr leaving his Black Avenue Muzik citing lack of progress for their decision.

The other artists left are not flourishing and from the look of things, they could all leave sooner than later.

On this note, Joey B believes the avoidable problem started when D-Black decided to sign a lot of artists at the same time.

“I think that’s where he fell short. He should have done it one or two at a time. He did too much with too many artists, unless he had some serious funding from somewhere but Ghana is hard,” he revealed to Nana Adwoa Sarkodie on Citi TV.

“I feel like it’s something he should not have done. I feel he bit more than he could chew. Sometimes you need to take your time

Joey also noted that it appeared D-Black was wasting the artistes’ time.

“What you said you would do and what you promised them you could not do it because you are busy yourself.

He didn’t really interpret what he wanted to do properly. We are all human beings and everyone wants to move nobody wants to be stagnant,” he further stated.
