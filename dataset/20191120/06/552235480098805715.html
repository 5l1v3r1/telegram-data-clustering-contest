Film Rangers and Celtic dressing rooms before powderkeg clash, Scottish football needs Netflix-style drama - Michael Gannon

Michael reckons the madness of our game would benefit from an access-all-areas approach.

20 Nov 2019, 06:00 by Michael Gannon, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/michael-gannon/

American jazz poet Gil Scott-Heron said the revolution will not be televised but that was before the big man could devour box sets on Netflix.

The small screen has never been bigger, where hundreds of channels are still not enough for us so we top up our viewing with streaming stations for a few quid a month.

The couch has never been more comfortable and we can bombard our eyes with millions of hours of shows, films and every kind of sport.

Broadcasters are determined to tap in to whatever branch of entertainment sees a surge. Right now it’s true crime documentaries and fire-breathing dragons.

But it’s also behind-the-scenes sports docu-dramas, where fans have been able to peek around the curtains at Man City, Sunderland, Leeds United and the All Blacks.

BBC’s look at David Robertson’s Real Kashmir was unmissable TV, even if some of the language would make a brickie blush. We lap all this stuff up – but it’s time for Scottish football to tap in to the market.

New Hibs boss Jack Ross has admitted he won’t miss the cameras poking around after going through it all at Sunderland but that’s exactly what we need.

What we might lack in quality we make up for in drama and a warts-and-all look behind the scenes would make Narcos look a bit like Emmerdale. Get the cameras in the dressing room before an Old Firm clash. Let’s see training bust-ups, the magic worked on shoestring budgets, rows with refs, players stepping out of line and those in dodgy clobber. Get the Go-Pro on an Arbroath player to see Dick Campbell react to losing.

Opening up the doors and letting everyone see the madness would be box office and could drive more to actually watch the games.

The Scottish football revolution should be televised. Fly on the wall? We’d be on to it like flies on a s****.
