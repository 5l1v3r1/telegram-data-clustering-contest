Wishaw woman set for fire walk as she continues battle against cancer

Sam Shing has organised a charity fire walk to take place at Wishaw Bowling Club on Saturday.

20 Nov 2019, 06:00 by Ross Thomson

Final preparations are being made as a Wishaw woman gets set to fight fire with fire in her ongoing cancer battle.

In June last year, just 16 months after giving birth to baby daughter Luana, Sam Shing’s world was turned upside down when she found out she had been diagnosed with Choriocarcinoma – a cancer affecting one in 50,000 pregnancies.

Sam is now on immunotherapy and is fundraising for the Harris & Trotter Charitable Trust, a charity which provides funding for the treatment to cancer patients when the treatment cannot be obtained from the NHS.

To give something back to the charity, Sam, who also has a 17-year-old son Kyle, has organised a charity fire walk to take place at Wishaw Bowling Club on Saturday.

Sam said: “We have over 30 people doing the fire walk which is brilliant.

“People are still able to come along and be part of it if they wish.

“I have already smashed my target of £5,000 so I am pleased with that but would love to raise more money for the charity.”

Sam, who works as a social worker, has already had three different types of chemotherapy as well as a hysterectomy.

She is continuing her treatment down in London at the Charing Cross Hospital.

And she continues to display a positive attitude as she continues her battle against the disease.

She added: “I am down in London this week for treatment. It’s tough but having to organise something like this has helped me take my mind off it.

“I try to be as positive as I can be whenever I can be. There have been some false dawns before so I don’t want to get too ahead of myself.”
