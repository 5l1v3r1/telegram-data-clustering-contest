One of biggest AFL names off-contract at the end of 2020 just signed a new deal with Geelong

20 Nov 2019, 06:40 by Ben Waterworth

Geelong has locked away arguably its biggest name that was out of contract at the end of 2020, with star midfielder Mitch Duncan signing a long-term extension.

The Cats confirmed on Wednesday that Duncan had added another four years to his contract, which expires at the end of next year.

It means Duncan is now contracted to the Cats for another five years, tying him to the club until the end of 2024 and, essentially, making the 28-year-old a Cat for life.

Duncan was the second Cat to sign a new deal on Wednesday, with forward Gary Rohan signing until the end of 2021.

“The club is delighted to see Mitch commit to the club,” Geelong football boss Simon Lloyd said.

“Mitch has been a mainstay of our team over 10 seasons, a dependable, intelligent teammate and a quality player.

“Our members and supporters have watched Mitch’s career unfold and we all look forward to it continuing over the next five seasons.”

Amid a star-studded on-ball brigade, Duncan has often played second fiddle at the Cats behind the likes of Patrick Dangerfield, Gary Ablett, Joel Selwood and, most recently, Tim Kelly.

But Duncan has still established himself as a star of the AFL, becoming renowned for his excellent kicking efficiency and workrate that allows to win ample uncontested possessions.

Duncan joins a long list of Cats who are contracted beyond 2020.

It means Geelong will now turn its attention to the likes of Jack Henry, Quinton Narkle and Brandan Parfitt, whose respective deals expire next year.

Veteran Tom Hawkins, also contracted until 2020, is expected to re-sign, but interesting decisions loom around Sam Menegola, Nakia Cockatoo, Rhys Stanley and Zach Tuohy, whose deals all expire at the end of next season.

After being drafted with Pick 28 in the 2009 national draft, Duncan has been one of Geelong’s most durable players, registering 203 games, including the successful 2011 Grand Final.
