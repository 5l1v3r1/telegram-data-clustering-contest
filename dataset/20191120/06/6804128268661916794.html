Jose Mourinho appointed new Tottenham Hotspur head coach

The Portuguese manager is back in the Premier League

20 Nov 2019, 06:47 by Sam Inkersole

Tottenham Hotspur have announced Jose Mourinho as their new head coach.

Mourinho has replaced Mauricio Pochettino at the helm in north London on a contract until the end of the 2022/23 season after Pochettino was sacked on Tuesday evening.

Spurs announced the news of Mourinho's appointment at 6.30am on Wednesday morning.

Mourinho said: â€œI am excited to be joining a Club with such a great heritage and such passionate supporters. The quality in both the squad and the academy excites me. Working with these players is what has attracted me."

Pochettino was sacked as manager at Spurs after picking up just three wins in the 2019/20 season but also only five and a half months after leading the team to the Champions League final, where they were beaten by Liverpool in Madrid in May.

Spurs haven't won away from home in the Premier League since January this year. Reports have claimed that the former boss had lost the dressing room and that a meeting was held earlier this week with chairman Daniel Levy where Pochettino was given his marching orders.

Speaking about the decision, chairman Daniel Levey said: "In Jose we have one of the most successful managers in football. He has a wealth of experience, can inspire teams and is a great tactician. He has won honours at every club he has coached. We believe he will bring energy and belief to the dressing room."
