NATO Chief Confirms $1bn Deal To Extend Life Of AWACS Fleet

Jens Stoltenberg was speaking at a meeting of Foreign Ministers in Brussels.

20 Nov 2019, 19:56

NATO's Secretary General has confirmed a $1 billion (£774 million) investment in the AWACS (Airborne Warning and Control Surveillance) reconnaissance fleet.

Jens Stoltenberg says the contract will help to modernise the alliance's capabilities, and he also mentioned replacing the fleet after 2035.

Also known as the "eyes in the sky", the fleet of E-3A aircraft has helped to protect NATO airspace close to Russia since it annexed Crimea in 2014.

Inside, the planes are kitted out with technology capable of pinpointing any ship or aircraft more than 250 miles away.
