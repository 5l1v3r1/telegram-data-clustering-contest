Analyst Calls Out College Football Playoff For Its “Alabama Privilege”

20 Nov 2019, 19:21 by Andrew Holleran

The latest College Football Playoff rankings were released on Tuesday night and Alabama continued to sit at No. 5 overall. The Crimson Tide don’t really have any impressive wins and they’re now without quarterback Tua Tagovailoa, but the selection committee decided to not drop Nick Saban’s team.

Alabama will most likely drop in the weeks to come, barring a blowout win over Auburn with Mac Jones at quarterback.

Still, there are some who think Alabama is unfairly getting the benefit of the doubt from the selection committee. Namely, Tim Brando is not happy.

The college football analyst thinks the committee has an “Alabama privilege.”

He doesn’t think Alabama is alone in that category, either.

“Teams that are considered blue bloods,” Brando said today. “Teams that have had success in the last 30, 35, 40 years, multiple conference championships. This cabal of teams that have made this event the great Sun Belt Invitational. If I can tell you in July that these are your teams and I’m gonna be right on at least 3 out of the 4 that I name, then we’ve removed all of the joy of the regular season of college football.”

Brando had tweeted about Alabama’s ranking earlier in the month.

“The CFB Playoff Committee has all their cronies on TV all day preparing everyone for Alabama privilege tonight,” Brandon tweeted. “There’s no doubt they’ll drop only 1 or 2 spots. Committee will claim “eye test” and forget their non Conference schedule. An embarrassment! I dropped em’ 5 spots.”
