Donald Trump screeches from scrawled notes while stony-faced Ivanka looks on

20 Nov 2019, 19:30

Donald Trump launched a vocal defense of explosive impeachment testimony against him from a notebook scrawled with black felt tip.

The President of the United States read from his book – embossed with ‘Aboard Air Force One’ – as US Ambassador to the EU Gordon Sondland accused him of corruption Wednesday.

Trump was flanked by daughter and special adviser Ivanka, who kept a stony expression throughout.

One page of the book was snapped by a photographer, and read: ‘I want nothing, I want nothing.

‘I want no quid pro quo.

‘Tell Zellinsky (sic) to do the right thing.

‘This is the final word from the Pres of the US.’

Trump used the notes as an aid while denying claims he’d made lawyer Rudy Giuliani press Ukraine to launch an investigation into his political rivals.

The president reportedly wanted Ukraine’s newly elected President Volodymyr Zelensky to probe Joe Biden’s son Hunter’s links to Ukrainian natural gas firm Burisma.

He is also said to have pressed for a probe into alleged Ukrainian meddling in the 2016 US presidential election which he ended up winning.

Conspiracy theories about that interference have already been debunked.

Sondland further suggested that $400million in military aid the US promised Ukraine to guard itself from Russian aggression had been held up until Zelensky agreed to Giuliani’s terms.

He claims that is the ‘quid pro quo’ – a favor for a favor – that proves Trump abused his office for personal gain.

Trump also used Wednesday’s appearance to distance himself from Sondland, despite appointing the millionaire hotelier – who donated to his campaign – to the key ambassadorial role.

The president said: ‘I don’t know him very well. this is not a man I know well. Seems like a nice guy.’ Prosecutor Ken Starr, who impeached then-President Bill Clinton in 1999, told Fox News Wednesday that he now believes Trump committed bribery, and that the president will be impeached.

Trump spoke out amid fresh questions over his health after he paid an unexpected visit to the Walter Reed Medical Center National Military Medical Center on Saturday.

White House spokesman Stephanie Grisham claimed it was part of the president’s annual medical.

However, Barack Obama’s ex-doctor Dr David Scheiner told CNN he suspects the president may be suffering from a series of small strokes.

Scheiner said: ‘These aren’t words, these are slips of the tongue.

‘These are words he can’t find and this is happening over and over again.

‘Comedians joke about it, but it’s not a joking matter. I think there is a neurological issue that is not being addressed.

‘And if he’s having an MRI of his head over there, I would be very pleased because I think he needs it.’
