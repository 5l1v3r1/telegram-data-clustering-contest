Everton legend Andy Gray says club must stand by Marco Silva

EXCLUSIVE Under-fire Everton boss Marco Silva has been given the backing of club legend Andy Gray, who draws parallels with the situation of Howard Kendall in the 1980s

20 Nov 2019, 22:30 by David Anderson, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/david-anderson/

For Marco Silva in November 2019, read Howard Kendall in November 1983.

Andy Gray can see parallels between Silva’s plight at Everton and the situation facing Kendall when he arrived at Goodison Park back in that winter of discontent.

Kendall was facing fan boycotts and calls for his head because the Blues were going nowhere.

The then chairman, Philip Carter, stuck by Kendall and Gray, coupled with Kevin Brock’s infamous backpass in the League Cup, helped spark Everton’s remarkable revival.

The rest, as Evertonians joyfully know, is history as Kendall embarked on the club’s most-successful era, winning two league titles, the FA Cup and the European Cup Winners’ Cup.

Gray says Everton must also stand by Silva, claiming they must break the cycle of hiring and firing managers.

“When I arrived at Everton, Howard was under huge pressure,” said the former Blues striker. “There were petitions saying ‘Kendall must go’. Philip Carter, in fairness to him, stuck by him.

“It was staggering that we could turn from what we were to what we became in such a short period, it was unbelievable.

“I hope Silva can also turn it round. I don’t want Everton to become a club that sacks managers every 18 months. At some time, someone has to be given time.

“Since Martinez left, who got almost three years, there has been a collection of managers who have come and gone, like Koeman and Sam Allardyce, so somebody has to be given a chance.

“If they feel Silva is the man, then they’ve got to stick with him.”

Gray, who won the FA Cup, League title and Cup Winners’ Cup in his 20 months at Goodison, was part of their feared frontline with Graeme Sharp and Adrian Heath.

What Evertonians would give to have one of them now and Gray was reunited with Sharp and his old team-mates for the launch of the film ‘Everton - Howard’s Way’, which chronicles that golden era.

Sitting underneath a picture of himself holding the Cup Winners’ Cup in the ‘85 lounge at Goodison, Gray lamented Everton’s need of a proper No 9.

“They’re lacking a genuine goalscorer,” said the Scot. “That’s a big problem for Everton.

“We don’t know if Moise Kean is going to be good enough yet. He’s still young, they’ve taken a gamble on him and he’s going to need time.

“Dominic Calvert-Lewin does a lot of good things, maybe works too hard - if that sounds stupid - outside the box trying to make up for his deficiencies inside the box. If he’s going to be top bracket, he needs to up his goal ratio dramatically.

“Cenk Tosun, his goalscoring record was good. But that was Turkey and this is a different league altogether.”

Gray, 63, claims Everton must follow up their win over Southampton , which was only their second in eight league games, by beating Norwich on Saturday.

“It has to change some time, there has to be a turning point this season for Everton or else they’re going to struggle,” he said.

“I just hope we can put back-to-back wins together now. We had a good win before the international break against Southampton.

“If we could beat Norwich, that would be a good six points. It would move us up the league and then things might begin to look a bit better.”

‘Everton - Howard’s Way’ is out now on DVD and on all the usual digital download platforms.
