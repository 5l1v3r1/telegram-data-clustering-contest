Anthony Joshua insists he is "still a champion" ahead of Andy Ruiz Jr rematch

Anthony Joshua has insisted he won't "become a p***** overnight" ahead of his huge rematch with Andy Ruiz Jr next month

20 Nov 2019, 22:30 by Irishmirror.ie

Anthony Joshua insists he is still a champion, claiming: “I’m not about to become a p**** overnight.”

Joshua has dismissed suggestions he has become psychologically damaged since his sensational first pro loss to Andy Ruiz Jr.

He claims he is still the same fighter who won Olympic gold and unified the WBA Super, IBF and WBO heavyweight titles before losing to Ruiz in June.

Joshua is adamant one defeat cannot wipe away all that he achieved in the previous seven years as he prepares to face Ruiz again in Saudi Arabia on December 7.

“We haven’t done anything differently mentally, I’m a champion,” he said defiantly. “I walked into the gym and three years later I was Olympic champion.

“I’ve had one loss, but I’m not about to become a p***** overnight and not be able to fight.

“I can fight, that’s it. I don’t need to reflect on that, I just need to win.”

Joshua has had to listen to all sorts of criticisms in the months since his shattering loss to Ruiz at Madison Square Garden and Tyson Fury claims he looked to him like he did not want to be there.

The former heavyweight world king rejected Fury’s claim and says he does not know what’s going on inside his head because they are different fighters.

“Of course I wanted to be there,” he said. “Tyson is at a different stage in his career.

“I’ve always said he turned pro the year I had my first fight and he’s been pro 11 years, doing his thing.

“What me and him deal with, he would never understand what I’m going through. That’s another entitled opinion, I was ready, I was in a good place.”

Joshua, 30, appeared before the media yesterday at his training base at the English Institute of Sport in Sheffield, sporting a beard and longer hair, signs that he is totally focused on work.

He looked leaner, but claimed he has not deliberately lost weight to be more mobile against the stocky Ruiz.

He says he is using his sparring against the likes of Dereck Chisora and Bryant Jennings to gauge how heavy he should be for the rematch in Diriyah.

“I leave it down to my sparring and how I prepare for the fight,” said Joshua, who is due to fly to Saudi Arabia at the weekend.

“I’m not really focused on my weight, I’m just focused on my sparring.

“I could weigh anything, but it just comes down to how I perform in the ring. I’m not interested in getting down or up to 110kg and then thinking I’m fit.

“If I’m sparring 10 or 15 rounds at 120kg, then I’m good. The sparring gives me all the information I need.”
