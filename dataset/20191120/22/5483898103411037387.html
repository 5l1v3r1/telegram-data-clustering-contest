UPDATE, 11:21am: 11 properties damaged and 33 people injured in Yorketown fire.

According to Police Commissioner Grant Stevens:

"We believe there are 11 properties that have been affected.

"There are only minor reports of injuries with some 33 people being treated for eye washing and minor burns and smoke inhalation…

"Crews are holding the fire on the outskirts of Edithburgh (and are) doing a great job there.

"I commend the crews for their efforts yesterday.”

*

UPDATE, 8:54am: CFS acting deputy chief Rob Sanford says the Yorketown fire’s “forward rates of spread have been halted on the outskirts of Edithburgh”.

He said the CFS could not yet confirm whether any properties had been lost in the fire and that he was “not aware” of any loss of human life.

He said 40 appliances and crews were on the ground and “still have a huge amount of work to do”.

“I’m sure they would’ve put themselves in harm’s way to make every effort to protect the communities.”

EARLIER...

The CFS has released a Watch and Act message for an “uncontrolled” bushfire burning on the Yorke Peninsula.

According to the CFS:

Issued for Honiton, Edithburgh,Troubridge Point, Wattle Point, Sultana Point, Hungry Point, Coobowie, Port Giles & Seven Roads  in the Lower Yorke Peninsula

Take action now as this bushfire may threaten your safety. If you are not prepared, leave now and if the path is clear, go to a safer place. Do not enter this area as conditions are dangerous.

The STANSBURY ROAD, YORKETOWN fire is uncontrolled. This stubble fire is burning in a North-Easterly direction towards Edithburgh and Coobwie. Conditions are continually changing.

Earlier Edithburgh residents were evacuated to the beach as fire threatened the township amid fears the town hall had been burned down. An eyewitness report on FIVEaa Breakfast later suggested the hall was still standing.
