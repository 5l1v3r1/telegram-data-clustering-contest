Sydney Choked in Smoke as Wildfires Rage in New South Wales

20 Nov 2019, 22:30 by Tom McKay

The city of Sydney – Australia’s most populous and the capital of New South Wales (NSW) state – was swathed in thick smoke on Tuesday morning amid more than 50 bushfires on the state’s east coast, the Guardian reported.

According to the Guardian, the smoke over Sydney mostly came “from a huge fire at Gospers Mountain” northwest of the city, with the coming days looking little better with temperatures expected to rise to around 40 degrees Celsius and accompanied by little or no rainfall. 28 of the fires were classified as uncontained on Tuesday morning, the paper wrote, with the “Greater Sydney, Greater Hunter, Illawarra/Shoalhaven, Southern Ranges and Central Ranges fire regions” under severe fire risk. Wide swathes of the rest of eastern NSW and Australian Capital Territory were classified as facing very high fire risk.
