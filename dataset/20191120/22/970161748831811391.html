The Apprentice's Ryan-Mark backs calls to go on I'm A Celebrity 2020

20 Nov 2019, 22:01

The Apprentice’s Ryan-Mark Parsons has revealed he would definitely give I’m A Celebrity…Get Me Out Of Here a go if he got approached by TV bosses – and it would be pure comedy gold.

The 19-year-old who was the latest candidate to be fired from the BBC show told Metro.co.uk that he’s ‘always up for a challenge’.

‘I mean if ITV got in touch and wanted me in I would say yes, I am always up for a challenge and it would be comedy TV gold,’ Ryan-Mark began.

The womenswear consultant continued that he’s already faced worse in The Apprentice than what he would come up against in the jungle.

He laughed: ‘I was dealing with worse sharing a bed with Thomas Skinner that is worse than eating a kangaroo anus.

‘Dealing with Thomas has actually prepared me for the jungle if it was to happen and I think it should happen.’

The ITV show, hosted by Ant McPartlin and Declan Donnelly has already started and the line-up includes the likes of Caitlyn Jenner, Adele Roberts and Ian Wright.

Ryan-Mark’s pal Lottie Lion has also recently fuelled rumours that she would go on I’m A Celeb.

The 19-year-old, who was in the centre of a ‘racism’ storm at the beginning of this Apprentice series, has been responding to fans on social media who are calling for her to go into the Australian jungle in 2020.

And while the librarian has been retweeting the calls to ‘get her in the jungle’, she hasn’t responded as to whether she would take part or not.

One questioned: ‘You like the idea then Lottie?’

Another tweeted: ‘Lottie needs to be in I’m A Celeb next year’

The Apprentice continues Wednesday at 9pm on BBC One and I’m A Celeb airs on 17 November on ITV.
