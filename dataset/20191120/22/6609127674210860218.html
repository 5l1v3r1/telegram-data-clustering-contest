STREET BLAST

Fife mum shot in chest with BB gun during gang bust-up involving her drug dealer sons

20 Nov 2019, 22:36 by John Jeffay

A MUM was shot in the chest with a BB gun during a gangland bust-up in the street involving her drug-dealing sons.

Shelagh Rashid, 47, was also struck with a golf club in the attack outside a busy pub.

A court heard Robert Speed, 36, drove his brother-in-law Ryan Brown, 22, and two others to return a mobile to two Rashid brothers when a row broke out in Kirkcaldy.

Prosecutor Ronnie Hay told the town’s sheriff court: “A loud bang was heard and the complainer said ‘I’ve been shot’.”

Ms Rashid collapsed and was rushed to the nearby Victoria Hospital.

A pellet found near the base of the neck was too dangerous to remove.

The court was told Speed clubbed Ms Rashid before returning to his Citroen Berlingo van for the gun, which he said he didn’t know was loaded.

One of the Rashids was also seen with a baseball bat near the Novar Bar.

Slamming the Sunday afternoon horror, Sheriff Jamie Gilchrist QC said: “This was gangsterism played out in public in broad daylight.”

Perth prisoner Speed admitted assault to severe injury and danger of life on August 4. He was jailed for three years and four months on Tuesday.
