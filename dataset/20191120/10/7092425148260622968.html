Homeless child's heartbreaking letter to Santa goes viral

20 Nov 2019, 10:44 by Ciara Walsh

A child's heartbreaking letter to Santa Claus has gone viral on Twitter. 

The image was shared by Labour Councillor Gerard Woodhouse, who was visiting the L6 Centre in Liverpool to help with Christmas food hampers for those in need.

While there, Woodhouse stumbled upon a child's letter to Santa which was being stored in the centre's Santa post box before it was sent to his workshop in the North Pole.

The heartbreaking letter was from a seven-year-old child, who asked Santa for a home for her family and some food. Woodhouse explains "Last week @L6 Centre we started getting down Xmas decorations we placed The Santa postbox in the cafe until grotto is set up. Today I went in to sort food parcels for residents while passing post box I looked in it. Just read a seven-year-old's letter."

The letter reads: "Dear Father Christmas, can you help? Can we have a home for Christmas? Mam wants us to be all together can you give us some food and can I have just a nice doll for Christmas. Thank you."

The letter is getting a massive reaction, standing as a strong reminder as the difficulties many people face all year round, particularly in the colder months.

On person says "I'm in tears reading it." Another says "Tried to read it out to my husband, couldn't finish it."

While the identity of the family has been kept anonymous, many have reached out to ask if they could help in this way.
