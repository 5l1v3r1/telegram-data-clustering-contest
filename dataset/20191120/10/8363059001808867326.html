Conservatives Play Silly Buggers on Twitter but Escape Actual Consequences

20 Nov 2019, 10:30 by Shabana Arif

During the live TV debate between Boris Johnson and Jeremy Corbyn last night, the party's Twitter account underwent a sneaky rebrand.

It replaced its username - usually just the account's Twitter handle @CCHQPress - and usual branding with the username factcheckUK, complete with an overhauled aesthetic. The issue is that - despite retaining its Twitter handle - the overhaul of the username and branding seems like a fairly obvious attempt at masquerading as an impartial, independent fact checking account.

Twitter did call out the bullshit and threatened "decisive corrective action" if the Convervative's social media team decided to have another crack at it, explaining that it has “global rules in place that prohibit behavior that can mislead people, including those with verified accounts.”

The party's Twitter account is still displaying 'factcheckUK's' verdict on the debate, crowning Boris as the winner.
