Changing face of South Derbyshire towns and villages showcased in new book

Magic Attic publication features then and now images

20 Nov 2019, 10:15 by Jane Goddard

Ever wondered what the streets around where you live used to look like?

Well, if you live in South Derbyshire a new book could well have the answer.

“How Times Have Changed in South Derbyshire” is the latest publication from the Magic Attic Archive in Swadlincote.

It contains a series of vintage photographs taken from the Magic Attic database, alongside present day views taken in the same spot by volunteers.

Captions describe the images and also contain interesting historical information.

Alan Hiley, a Magic Attic volunteer, said: “Many of our more mature readers will recognise scenes from the past and the younger readers may find it hard to realise that this is how it was.

“Looking at the present-day photographs it shows a way of life that has gone forever.”

The book covers numerous villages in South Derbyshire and many of the newer images show just how much demolition of buildings has gone on in the intervening years.

Happily, some of the older buildings have survived the test of time.

The format is A5 landscape, extending to 161 pages, with black and white and colour photos.

Priced at £8.95, the book is on sale at the Magic Attic and Sharpes Pottery Museum.
