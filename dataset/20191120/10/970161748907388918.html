Titans season 2: Hawk replaced by dangerous fraud & heroes confront their demons

20 Nov 2019, 10:56

Titans season 2’s penultimate episode will see Hank in a whole new heap of trouble as someone takes over his life as he spirals out of control.

The complicated vigilante, played by Alan Ritchson, has turned to cage-fighting for money to support his unhealthy habits as his violent nature gets the better of him.

However, in new episode Faux-Hawk, he wakes up one day to find that someone has stolen his suit, and now there’s a new Hawk in town as an imposter takes his place.

With a decision to make, Hank needs to get himself together in order to reclaim his title as the true Hawk of the DC Universe.

He’s not the only one with troubles, as Donna (Conor Leslie) and Dawn (Minka Kelly) head out on a mission to find out more information about Gar (Ryan Potter) and Conner (Joshua Orpin).

Heading to Cadmus, they meet a mysterious Cadmus operative (Raoul Bhaneja) in order to get information, but the Lex Luthor- run laboratories is about to start its Phase One plan.

Which could be lethal, as it plans to experiment with Gar’s superhuman abilities.

Dick Grayson (Brenton Thwaites) has its own problems to be dealing with after Jericho’s (Chella Man) so-called “death”.

Except, he realises he has somehow managed to survive his death at his father’s hands, and may not be in even bigger trouble than he initially thought.

Escaping from jail, Dick resides to return to his Robin alter-ego, and goes on a mission to find a new suit in time to go and save Jericho.

Finally, Rose (Chelsea Zhang) has something to confess to Jason (Curran Walters) and is later seen battling father Slade/Deathstroke (Esai Morales).

With only two episodes left of the season, and with no guarantee of season three, it could be anyone’s guess what’s to come for our heroes…

The show’s second season production was marked with tragedy back in July when one of their stunt co-ordinators were killed in a freak accident on set.

It was reported by TMZ that a car stunt rehearsal that went wrong on the set of the comic-book series in Toronto, where a piece of the vehicle flew off while flipping, and hit him in the head.

Confirming the news of Warren Appleby, whose previous work has included effects co-ordinator on IT, The Shape of Water and Repo Men, the team on the show temporarily shut down production.

Titans airs on DC Universe in the US and will air on Netflix in the UK at a later date.
