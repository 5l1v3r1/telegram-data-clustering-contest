Yvonne Connolly "grateful to be alive" after horse kicked her in the face and left her with broken bones

She suffered four fractures to her eye socket and cheek bone, a broken nose and a broken arm

20 Nov 2019, 10:20 by Mikie O'Loughlin

Yvonne Connolly said that she is "grateful to be alive" following a serious horse riding accident.

The TV personality was rushed for emergency maxillofacial surgery in St. James's Hospital, Dublin after she suffered four fractures to her eye socket and cheek bone.

She was also suffered a severe broken nose after being kicked in the face by a horse last week, RSVP Live reports.

Explaining what happened during the traumatic episode, Yvonne said: "So grateful to be alive after a serious riding accident.

"Four fractures to my eye socket and cheek bone and my nose was completely smashed. I also broke my arm.

"Two surgeries over the past 10 days under the magical hands of Dr Kumara Ekanayake and the incredible staff at St James's."

The mother of three went on to thank those close to her for their support and kind thoughts during this difficult time for her.

Yvonne added: "Thanks too to all my friends and family and to those who sent such lovely messages of support."

Yvonne recently took part in a series of races to raise money for injured jockeys. In July, she won in the Corinthian Challenge Charity Race Series at the Curragh, riding Neverushacon to victory after being trained by Jessica Harrington.

Following the event, she told RSVP Magazine: "I sat on my first racehorse in April and it was a pretty daunting experience. I very quickly got fit and got my confidence and Jessica is an amazing woman."

Yvonne went on to say that injuries are part and parcel of the sports of horse racing and show jumping.

"Ali got a new pony last summer and she broke her wrist and needed surgery, it was heartbreaking," she explained.

"Some people might say 'Why do you let her do it?' but when you can’t stop somebody when they have a passion like that.

"You could slip walking down the street or get hit by a car, you can’t wrap kids up in cotton wool. For me too, I love it and I take every precaution, but you have got to expect that you might fall or have an injury."
