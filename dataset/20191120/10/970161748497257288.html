Around 4,000,000 at risk of asthma attacks triggered by flu and colds in winter

20 Nov 2019, 10:04

Around 4,000,000 people are at risk of suffering asthma attacks triggered by flu and colds this winter, a charity estimates.

A survey by Asthma UK found that three quarters of sufferers said their conditions worsened when they became ill over the colder months.

They are now urging anyone with the condition to get vaccinated this winter, after one man was left in a coma due to a flu-related asthma attack.

Adam Blackman, 47, had been struggling to breathe, stating that it felt as though someone had his hands around his neck.

He ended up spending over a month in hospital, where doctors put him in a coma for a week due to complications which led to pneumonia and a collapsed lung.

Adam said: ‘It was nearly catastrophic for me, but my friends and family were also at risk. My brother-in-law who has asthma got ill shortly afterwards and ended up in hospital too.

‘Given how contagious flu can be I would urge everyone to get the flu vaccine, so they don’t have to go through the hell I’ve been through.

‘I’m not risking it again and have already got the flu vaccine. I am urging everyone with asthma to get it as soon as possible, it could save your life.’

Flu viruses can increase inflammation in the airways, causing people to cough and wheeze for breath.

The narrowing of airway then has the potential to trigger a bronchospasm, otherwise known as an asthma attack.

Asthma attacks can be fatal, with around three people dying in the UK every day.

Yet only half of people with chronic respiratory disease, including asthma, received their free jab last year, Public Health England reports.

The vaccine is available for free to asthmatics who use preventer medicine, have been on a course of oral or systemic steroids or who have been previously hospitalised because of their condition.

Anyone who is not eligible can be assessed on a case-by-case basis by their GP or pay privately for a jab from their local surgery or pharmacy.

Previous research by Asthma UK suggests people put off getting the vaccine because they are worried about side effects or are sceptical about its effectiveness.

Dr Andy Whittamore, a GP and the clinical lead at Asthma UK, said: ‘Asthma and flu can be a potentially lethal combination, and the flu vaccine is the best way to prevent someone falling ill.

‘Flu can make people’s asthma symptoms worse and trigger a life-threatening asthma attack.

‘People with asthma are also at higher risk of developing other serious flu-related complications as the flu can make their asthma symptoms worse.’
