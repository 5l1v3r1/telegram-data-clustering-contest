Morrisons is launching vegan pigs in blankets for Christmas - and they're only £2.50

Pigs in blankets are officially back on the menu for vegans

20 Nov 2019, 10:10 by Simon Duke, Hayley Watson

Veggies and vegans have often been the afterthought at Christmas - but not anymore.

There's more on the shelves for vegetarians and vegans at Christmas than ever before, from dairy-free cheeseboards to no-turkey roasts.

Now, pigs in blankets have been given the meatless treatment, as Morrisons prepares to launch their version of the Christmas dinner favourite.

The 'Vegan No-Pigs in Blankets' are made from a blend of jackfruit and chickpeas, wrapped in a streaky pastry blanket.

They will be available to buy in packs of 10 for £2.50 from Wednesday, November 27, according to ChronicleLive .

Morrisons' party food expert, Leanne Walters, said: “Pigs in blankets are one of the UK’s favourite Christmas treats so we have created a meat-free version that can be enjoyed by vegans or anybody else.”

As well as going on Christmas dinner plates around the country, the vegan pigs in blankets are being billed as a perfect canape for festive get-togethers.

And they're not the only vegan nibbles in Morrisons' Christmas 2019 range.

Other highlights include Korean Jackfruit Bao Buns – soft, fluffy bao buns with a BBQ pulled jackfruit filling and mixed vegetables with a spicy gochujang sauce.

Also on the menu are Katsu Croquettes – crispy croquettes filled with sweet potato, carrot and red pepper in a creamy and warming Katsu curry sauce.

Both of those will be available in store from December 11.
