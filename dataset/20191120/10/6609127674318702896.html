PUNTING PUPS

Glasgow drug lord Stephen ‘Jimmy’ Jamieson dealing designer dogs to fund luxury lifestyle

20 Nov 2019, 10:32 by Ewan Mowat

DRUGS baron Stephen "Jimmy" Jamieson is now dealing in designer dogs in Glasgow's south side to fund his luxury lifestyle.

The hood, 36, is punting English and French bulldogs online, selling puppies and offering a stud service.

Jamieson regularly posts pictures of young dogs on Facebook, often promoting his Instagram page BulliesSouthside.

An insider claims he imports the designer pooches, selling them for thousands or offering them out as studs for £600 a pop.

The source told the Daily Record: "He buys top-end French bulldogs and English bulldogs and breeds them.

“He sells semen and studs them out at a minimum of £600 a go.

“The rest are kept at the homes of friends and family members and associates, on the basis they do two litters for him then the dogs become their pet after he gets that.

“He’s got the best dogs as they’re mainly imported. His dogs sell from £3500 upwards.”

Last month, Jamieson — previously jailed for his part in a £9million drugs gang — took a luxury holiday with feared crime boss Jamie ‘Iceman’ Stevenson, 54.

The underworld associates travelled to millionaires’ paradise Dubai, posing for photos in designer gear and pricey watches in a swanky nightclub.

The gangster pals stayed at the five-star Five Palm Jumeirah hotel — whose past guests include pop star Liam Payne.

Jamieson, of Paisley, is currently shacked up in a plush south side pad owned by millionaire taxi tycoon Stevie Malcolm.

He has been living in the £260,000 mortgage-free house for over a year with his girlfriend and kids.

Two new £100,000 Range Rovers with blacked out windows are regularly parked in the driveway of the four-bed gaff.

The house, built in 2013, has French doors, two en suite bathrooms and a garage.

Jamieson served half of his 8½-year sentence and was freed in 2013.

 

Malcolm, nicknamed The Fat Controller, owns a string of properties including his £9million mansion near Gleneagles, Perthshire.

In 2018, Malcolm stepped down as boss of one of his biggest firms, Edinburgh City Private Hire.
