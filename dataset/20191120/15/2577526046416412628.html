Infinity Ward is Still Trying to Fix Modern Warfare's Busted-Ass Shotgun

20 Nov 2019, 15:05 by Alistair Jones

Infinity Ward is still trying to bring Modern Warfare's monstrous shotgun under its beck and call. A new patch, released early this week, saw the company hit the hyper-powerful 725 shotgun with yet another round of nerfs - the third set since the game launched less than a month ago.

When the game first released, it didn't take long before players realised that a few choice attachments could turn the 725 into a some monstrous sniper/shotgun combo, capable of one-shotting opponents from across the map. It already did a lot of damage, but with an already impressive range, adding a scope, a suppressor, and a barrel attachment, it soon had the Call of Duty community up in arms.

Infinity Ward has already taken action twice before, but the 725 remains comically powerful. The latest nerf reduces long-range damage reduction, as well as adjusting the impact of attachments on the weapon's range. That should help with those long-range snipes, but the developer is also trying to reduce the gun's overall stats, reducing its damage and accuracy when fired from the hip. Given that this has been a thorn in players' sides for weeks, I'm not 100% sure the changes will do enough to fix the issue, but perhaps it'll be a classic case of third time lucky.
