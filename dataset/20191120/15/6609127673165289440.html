TASTE TEST

Glasgow punters sample new Scottish drink Alba Cola featuring Scots water and heather extract

20 Nov 2019, 15:11 by Aidan Smith

A NEW Scottish cola is hitting the shelves just in time for St Andrew's Day.

Glaswegians Chris Ewing and Niall Holmes of the Caledonian Cola Company have launched the aptly named Alba Cola.

The drink is designed for people looking for a new cola drinking experience and a contemporary change from the big brands.

Made with lightly carbonated Scottish water and a buzz of heather botanicals, Alba Cola contains less sugar than large brands and comes packaged in a sleek black can with a gold unicorn, Scotland’s national animal on the front.

We took to the streets of Glasgow to give punters a taste of the new craft drink and get their thoughts on the fresh taste.

One person said: "Anything is better than normal coke especially if it's Scottish. I really liked it.

Another said: "It tastes alright, it's like Coca-Cola, but it's not.

"It tastes a bit like those weird botanical drinks from Tesco.

"It tastes dead sweet but it doesn't make your mouth all rotten like normal coke."

A third person added: "I can't quite work out the taste of it. It's not as sweet as normal coke which I like.

"There's like a floral herby feeling. It's nice I like it."

Another came to the conclusion: "It's different, it tastes a wee bit flat. Maybe it's not cold enough."

Another member of the public agreed with the flat verdict: "It's actually not bad. It's flatter than normal coke but it's alright."

And our final sampler thought it was a bit like cider: "It tastes different, it has a kick to it. It is flatter but I think it's more sour.

"It's not as sweet. Do I like it? Not so much.

"It has a more cider taste, but a non-alcoholic version. It's more like a juice than a something with fizz to it."

The idea for the soft drink was born when entrepreneur and ex-Motherwell footballer Chris noticed the popularity of regional colas in France.

He got together with musician Niall who had been working in the food and drink industry for over 15 years.

Together they tasted and tweaked the recipe to perfectly balance the heather botanicals in the cola and are now launching what they deem the best cola around.

Chris explained: “People in France would frequently ask for a cola made from their region over the big brands and I knew there would be a market for the same kind of thing from Scotland with its own wonderful food and drink and culture.

“Consumers are drinking less alcohol and looking for new premium soft drinks or mixer experiences.

“It’s a drink to be shared and enjoyed with friends made using some of Scotland’s best natural ingredients, our untouched water and botanicals from one of our most iconic plants.

"They work so well together in a cola!”

Niall added: “We are very excited to introduce Alba Cola to the thriving food and drink industry here in Scotland.

"The reception we are getting when people see and taste it is fantastic.

“It really is such a versatile cola with a refreshing taste and we look forward to seeing what people think of the world’s favourite soft drink reinvented with a premium Scottish twist.”

Alba Cola is currently stocked in a range of independent retailers, cafes and bars around Scotland.
