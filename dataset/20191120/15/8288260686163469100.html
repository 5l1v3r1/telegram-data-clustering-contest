'Over the moon', 'One of us' - Tamworth FC joy at Chris Lait's return

The winger had rejoined Tamworth FC from AFC Telford after leaving in the summer. He scored 21 goals for the Lambs during the 2018-19 campaign

20 Nov 2019, 15:11 by Matt Panter

Andrew  Danylyszyn says the Lambs wanted to ‘strike while the iron is hot’ after snapping up former star Chris Lait.

The flying winger has rejoined Tamworth after a stint at National North side AFC Telford.

He scored 21 goals last season and was named Player of the Year by supporters and the Tamworth Herald.

And Danylyszyn said: “We are over the moon to get Chris back on board at the club.

“For Gaz (Smith) and I, it was about striking while the iron is hot."

“In terms of where we are as a team, at the top of the table, it’s a fantastic position.

“You tend to find though that teams react when they are not doing so well. That tends to be how it works.

“But we want to push on and decided to strengthen from the positive position we are in.”

The Lambs already have an embarrassment of riches in attack but Danylyszyn feels Lait adds to the group rather than takes away from it.

“We just feels he’s a great signing,” he added. “We were really gutted to lose him and (right back) Kristian Green at the end of last season."

 

“We understood the reasons though and to get Chris back, it’s excellent news.

“He knows the system and how we play the game and he’s a very good player.

“He scores goals and he has that ability to change a game at any time.

“I can’t wait to see him on the pitch although he knows he has to earn the right to be on the field by what his does in training.”

Joint-boss Gary Smith added: "We were delighted to get Laity back.

“He’s one of us, he’s back at home.

“Everyone knows what a top-class player he is. As a signing, I don’t think they come much bigger."
