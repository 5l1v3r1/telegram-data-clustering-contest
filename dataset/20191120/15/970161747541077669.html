Queen Elizabeth 'found out truth of Prince Andrew interview at breakfast'

20 Nov 2019, 15:29

Former Royal aide Paul Burrell has said that he feels sorry for Queen Elizabeth II amid the Prince Andrew interview fallout – as she would’ve found out the truth in the papers over breakfast.

The 93-year-old reigning monarch has a daily routine which includes going through the daily newspapers each day over her morning meal.

So despite claims Prince Andrew told the Queen the now infamous interview with Emily Maitlis an incredible ‘success’, the truth would have come out the next morning.

Speaking on Loose Women about his time in the palace, and of Prince Andrew’s friendship with the now-dead sex offender and trafficker Jeffrey Epstein, Paul said that she was the one he felt sympathy for most.

‘I’ve never really known him and that’s the honest truth. I feel sorry for the victims of this paedophile [Epstein], that’s where my sympathy goes,’ he told the panel.

‘I also feel sorry for Her Majesty who’s 93 years old. Every morning I would set out her newspapers on her breakfast table, still happens to this day.’

‘She sees the papers, all of them, they’re not censored from her. She sees everything, and I feel sorry that she has to see this,’ he added.

‘She’s a 93-year-old mum and she has to put up with this.’

Prince Andrew’s Newnight interview, in which he discussed his friendship with Epstein as well as defended accusations of his own, has become the national talking point.

In the discussion, Andrew attempted to debunk claims that he had sexually engaged with then 17-year-old Virginia Roberts (now Giuffre) – claiming he was with his daughter at Pizza Express that day.

He also stated her accusations were false because in her statement she claimed he was ‘sweaty’ in a nightclub – and ‘due to an overdose of adrenaline in the Falklands he was unable to sweat at the time’.

The Duke also came under fire for downplaying his attachment to Epstein, saying he ‘let the side down’ by staying with the convicted sex offender after his release from prison.

Emily Maitlis, who conducted the interview, has since stated that the Duke of York would have got permission from his mother prior to filming.
