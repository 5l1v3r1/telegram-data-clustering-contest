TRIAL DRAMA

Glasgow mum of slain Owen Hassan tried to save him outside pub while he lay ‘covered in blood’ after ‘machete attack’

20 Nov 2019, 15:18 by Grant McCabe

A MUM told a trial how she tried to save her dying son after he was allegedly murdered in a "machete attack" outside her pub.

Anne Marie Lynch recalled Owen Hassan “covered in blood” in the street beside the Old Stag Inn bar in Glasgow's Pollokshaws in November 2018.

The 55-year-old initially chased after a man before realising he was with another individual.

Miss Lynch told jurors how her son would “stand up for himself” - but was not aware if he had a “lot of enemies”.

She was giving evidence at the trial of David Callaghan,30, and Craig Corrall, 39, at the High Court in Glasgow.

The pair deny murdering 30-year-old Mr Hassan.

The court heard how the dad would often work in his mum's pub, where she was the licensee.

Mr Hassan had gone there on November 7 last year, the day of the alleged murder.

Miss Lynch told the court: “He came in and said: 'I am exhausted. I am just going to go and get a shower'. He got the keys for the house. He said he was very tired and was going home.”

She said her son left and she later heard “a bang”.

Miss Lynch ran from behind the bar and spotted Mr Hassan against the pub door.

She added: “I thought he was doing something funny then I could hear thumping and realised something was wrong.”

As the mum ran out, Mr Hassan was on the ground. Miss Lynch told the court she then grabbed hold of another man there to “try and stop what was happening”.

She said the man then broke free, but she initially went after him.

She told prosecutor Lynsey MacDonald: “I just thought: “Who are you...what have you done?'.”

Miss Lynch told the court she believed the man was armed. She said he then shouted on another person nearby who also appeared to have a weapon.

Miss Lynch said she was told at one stage: “F*** off, missus.”

The mum then recalled: “I ran back to the pub. He was just lying there, covered in blood. I was trying to get a phone to call an ambulance. I was just trying to help him.”

Paramedics arrived to treat Mr Hassan and took him to hospital, but he later died.

The court heard Miss Lynch later identified two individuals she believed were involved.

Callaghan's QC Brian McConnachie quizzed Miss Lynch on the men she claimed to have seen.

He asked did she shout 'I know who you are' as the mum went after them that night.

Miss Lynch: “I said 'I know who you are', but I did not have a clue who they were. I only said that to frighten them.”

Mr McConnachie also asked the witness about two brothers who lived in the area.

The QC said: “Are they people who Owen had issues with?”

Miss Lynch replied: “Don't think so.”

The advocate suggested Mr Hassan was a person who had a “lot of enemies”.

His mum replied: “Maybe he had enemies. I don't know.”

She also stated during evidence: “Owen stood up for himself, but that does not give them reason for what they had done.”

Mr McConnacie said after the death people were putting forward “a number of names” for apparently being involved.

Miss Lynch said: “We were all thinking who could have done it, but I was not interested in suggestions.”

The QC added photos of individuals had been sent to the mum – including of Callaghan.

Mr McConnachie said: “Before the identification parade, David Callaghan was given to you as one of the names?”

She replied: “Yes.”

Mr McConnachie said: “His photo had been sent to you?”

Miss Lynch: “Yes, but it was like a different guy. The photo was completely different to the person I picked out at the identification parade.”

Prosecutors claim Mr Hassan was chased, seized by the body and repeatedly struck with knives or machetes.

The trial before Lord Woolman continues.
