Pensioner who tried to set her own bungalow on fire escapes jail

82-year-old was found not fit to stand trial after being remanded in prison

20 Nov 2019, 15:30 by Lucy Webber

An elderly woman has been spared jail after attempting to set her bungalow on fire.

On November 27 last year, Maureen Walsh tried to start the fire in the kitchen of her semi-detached home in Ellesmere Port .

Walsh, 82, had made the sink overflow by stuffing a tea towel on the plughole with both taps on full.

She also placed packets of food on each of the four electric cooker hobs which were all on.

It was also noted a kettle power cord was deliberately placed trailing in the water-filled sink whilst it was turned on, with the risk it could start an electrical fire.

Walsh was remanded in custody to Styal prison in the February of this year, where she was cared for by prison staff as her health began to fail significantly.

She was found not fit to plead or stand trial after medical reports were taken out on Ms Walsh.

At Chester Crown Court on November 19 Walsh was given a conditional discharge for 12 months.

Peter Hussey, from the Crown Prosecution Service, said: “This has been a complicated and sad case.

“Ms Walsh’s social conditions and health needs meant she had carers visiting her daily. Her behaviour was challenging, and the carers visited in pairs as she often became abusive and aggressive.”

Walsh had previously received a short prison sentence in February earlier this year for other, less serious offences.

Mr Hussey added: “She initially tried to blame the carers for the fire but also admitted that she’d done it on purpose.

“The special verdict by the jury meant that only three types of sentences were available to the court – a hospital order, a supervision order, or a conditional discharge.

"As her mental ill-health was not treatable, and her residence was uncertain, the sentencing options for the Court were very limited.”
