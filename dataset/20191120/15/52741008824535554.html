School bus driver charged with stopping in wrong place at railway crossing

Police allege a bus got close to the tracks with a train approaching, but there was no contact between the bus and train

20 Nov 2019, 15:18 by Blair Adams

A charge has been laid following an alarming incident in Cambridge.

Waterloo Regional Police allege a school bus, which had children on it, got too close to a railway crossing at Coronation Boulevard and King Street East the afternoon of October 29.

They say the driver did perform all safety checks, as required.

But as those checks were being done, the driver noticed a train approaching in the distance at a slow speed.

That's when police say the bus came into close proximity to the tracks.

There was no contact between the bus and train, as well no one was injured.

Police say they launched their investigation the following day, when they were made aware of the incident.

On Wednesday, November 20, police announced they had charged a 72-year-old woman - under the Highway Traffic Act - with stopping a school bus at the wrong place at a railway crossing.
