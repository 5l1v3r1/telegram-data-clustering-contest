BTS Army drags ‘disgraceful’ Grammys as K-pop stars fail to get nominated

20 Nov 2019, 15:05

BTS fans have dragged the Grammys after the K-pop group failed to get nominated for any awards.

While Ariana Grande, Lizzo, Billie Eilish and H.E.R dominated the nominations, the Boy With Luv stars were snubbed from the list.

And, to say Army is raging for Jimin, V, RM, Jin, Jungkook, Suga and J-Hope is definitely an understatement.

Flocking to Twitter, one fumed: ‘I’m sorry but BTS deserved better once again… I didn’t want to get my hopes up in case of disappointment but this was down-right disgraceful…

‘They deserved that Grammy nomination for album of the year especially as after everything that albums + our boys achieved.’

‘Literally everyone knows that BTS deserved a nomination, America isn’t the only country in the world and their artists aren’t the only talented ones out there,’ another posted.

‘No I‘m fr so frustrated,’ a follower said. ‘This was BTS’ biggest year this was BTS’ most successful year with BWL getting certified platinum, top 10 hot100, 4.2 million sales for MOTSP, there is no trying harder next year when this was their biggest year, bigger than many other artists nominated.’

While a fan suggested: ‘The world’s still not ready for these talented young men. Soonest, my babies, you will have that award. Nothing is impossible with all of you and ARMYs.’

And a Twitter user added: ‘I might not stan BTS but HOW the f**k they ain’t get a Grammy nomination….

‘The hype America gave these boys but didn’t even give them a f**king nomination when they repeatedly broke records with their albums…..’

Lizzo has been tipped for a whopping eight gongs at the Grammys – which takes place at the Staples Center in LA, on 27 January.

She is up for album of the year for Cuz I Love You, as well as best new artist and song of the year for Truth Hurts.

Side note: Still a total banger.

While Billie Eilish and Lil Nas X aren’t far behind, scooping six nominations each, while H.E.R is up for four, including record of the year for Hard Place.
