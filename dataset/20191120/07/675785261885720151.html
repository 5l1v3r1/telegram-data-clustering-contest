Jose Mourinho's Tottenham prediction highlights how far they have fallen

Jose Mourinho claimed Tottenham were capable of mounting a Premier League title challenge this season, but he takes over with them down in 14th spot

20 Nov 2019, 07:42 by Jacob Murtagh, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/jacob-murtagh/

Jose Mourinho believed Tottenham were one of four teams who could have challenged for the Premier League title this season.

Instead, the new Spurs boss inherits a side languishing down in the bottom half, 20 points behind runaway leaders Liverpool.

The Special One faces a huge task to get Tottenham back among the Champions League spots as they also trail fourth-placed Manchester City by 11 points.

And during his punditry debut for Sky Sports earlier this season, Mourinho believed they were good enough to compete for top honours.

When asked how many clubs could challenge for the title, he replied: "Four. Manchester City , Tottenham , Liverpool and Manchester City B team."

Mourinho replaced Mauricio Pochettino at the helm after Daniel Levy decided to wield the axe on the Argentine.

“I am excited to be joining a club with such a great heritage and such passionate supporters," said Mourinho.

"The quality in both the squad and the academy excites me. Working with these players is what has attracted me.”

Levy has explained why he turned to Mourinho, who returns to the dugout 11 months after leaving Manchester United .

He said: “In Jose we have one of the most successful managers in football. He has a wealth of experience, can inspire teams and is a great tactician.

"He has won honours at every club he has coached. We believe he will bring energy and belief to the dressing room.”

Jamie Carragher believes Mourinho is just the type of manager Spurs need to change their fortunes.

He told Sky Sports News : "You look at his C.V., people question the Manchester United job, but you have to look at his career as a whole.

“He’s been hugely successful and that is the one thing Tottenham lack at this moment, no matter how well Pochettino’s done.

“They haven’t quite got over the line in getting a trophy and normally that’s what Jose Mourinho does when he goes to a club.”
