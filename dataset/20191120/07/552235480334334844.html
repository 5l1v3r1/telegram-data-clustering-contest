Man gets jail after showing off a knife in Hamilton store

The serial offender was sentenced at Hamilton Sheriff Court.

20 Nov 2019, 07:00 by Hamilton Advertiser, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/hamilton-advertiser/

A serial offender who showed off a knife in a Hamilton store has been sent to prison for 16 months.

William McInulty revealed the blade when he stripped to the waist after he heard staff talking about him.

McInulty, 28, a prisoner, was sentenced at Hamilton Sheriff Court on Friday.

He admitted having a knife at B&M Bargains in Douglas Park Retail Park on September 12.

McInulty also admitted resisting arrest, shouting abuse and making threats.

Scott King, prosecuting, said staff were warned in a phone call from a neighbouring store that the accused had entered B&M Bargains and might have a knife.

Mr King explained: “McInulty appeared to overhear this conversation. He took off his jacket and t-shirt, exposing a knife that had been concealed in the waistband of his trousers.

“Staff pressed the panic button immediately and a member of the public also contacted the police.

“Officers arrived and approached the accused from different areas of the store.

“He was arrested and began to resist and tried to break free. Five officers were involved.

“He was searched and a kitchen knife with a six-inch blade was seized.

“McAnulty then began to shout, swear and threaten the police officers.”

Solicitor Jackson Bateman said his client has mental health difficulties and was at a “particularly low point” at the time.

Mr Bateman added: “There was an issue with homelessness and he was misusing heroin and valium.”

Sheriff Thomas Millar noted that the accused was jailed for four years in 2014 for assault and robbery involving a knife.

The sheriff told him: “There has been further offending since and you have been put under supervision and given a restriction of liberty order, measures which are designed to prevent further offending.

“You are clearly a person who is not averse to using a knife.

“This must have been a very frightening experience for those in the shop.”
