The Crown's Helena Bonham Carter 'didn't understand' Rihanna on Ocean's 8

20 Nov 2019, 07:36

Rihanna is one of the most well-known celebrities around, thanks to her massive singing career, constantly sold-out beauty range, history-making clothing line – and she can act, too.

However, Helena Bonham Carter revealed there were some communication issues between the pair while starring on Ocean’s 8 together.

During an appearance on The Late Show, The Crown actress took a second to spill all the tea on some of her biggest co-stars so far.

And, while the 53-year-old was full of praise for the We Found Love singer, she didn’t quite understand some of what she said.

‘She’s amazing, she’s a goddess. She looks extraordinary,’ she told host Stephen Colbert. ‘I could never understand what she said though. We speak totally different languages.

‘But I love her, and she’s amazing to look at — really good actress too, and then she’s a great designer.’

Colin Firth, who starred opposite her in The King’s Speech, didn’t fare any better.

‘Love dearly, talks too much,’ she explained.

And Helena revealed that Harry Potter’s Daniel Radcliffe recites poetry backstage and is very useful for ‘holding her drinks’.

Discussing how sensitive the 30-year-old is, she continued: ‘He’s got nice manners, too. He loves poems, and he writes them. He also quotes poems.

‘He’s really handy because I like my tea and my coffee and my Diet Coke and things, and he’d hold them all for me. So he was really helpful.’

Good to know…

Helena recently made headlines as she opened up about her role as Princess Margaret in series three of The Crown – which is on Netflix now.

In fact, she claimed that she spoke to the ghost of the late Royal before taking on the part.

Clearing up the rumours while appearing on The Graham Norton Show, she admitted: ‘I actually went to see a friend about something else, but she has a talent for mediumship and she suddenly said, “Margaret is here. Does that mean anything?”

‘I hadn’t actually said yes [to the part] yet, but thought I’d ask her if I should take the role and she said, “You’ll be better than the other one”.

‘There was actually someone else up for the part and it was typical of Margaret.

‘I’d met her a few times and you never really knew where you were with her, she had a way of complimenting and putting you down at the same time.’

No communication issues there, then…
