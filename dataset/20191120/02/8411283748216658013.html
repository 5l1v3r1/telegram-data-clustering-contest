Taiwan not to release 3.5 GHz frequency bands for private 5G vertical applications

20 Nov 2019, 02:03 by Bryan Chuang, Taipei; Steve Shen, DIGITIMES

Taiwan will not reserve a dedicated frequency band range in 3.5 GHz spectrum for private enterprises or entities to implement their respective 5G vertical applications, according to government sources.

Prior to biddings set to begin in December for 5G frequency bands, the Ministry of Economic Affairs (MOEA) earlier suggested the government release the 3,700-3,800 MHz frequency band range under the 3.5 GHz spectrum to private enterprises or other entities for developing their own 5G vertical applications.

However, officials from the National Communications Commission (NCC) have noted the 3,700-3,800MHz frequency band range is too close to the 3,300-3,570MHz range to be auctioned off and it will result in interferences if former is also released.

Government sources said that transportation and communications minister Lin Chia-lung has rejected the MOEA proposal.

Instead, the sources said that as the government is likely to adopt a two-pronged approach to solve the issue: private businesses or entities will be allowed to lease frequency bands available within the 3.5 GHz spectrum from telecom operators who have won the bid to use the specturm; or to lease frequency bands of non-3.5 GHz spectrum from the government to develop their own 5G vertical applications.

The government is likely to release frequency bands available within the 4.8-4.9 GHz spectrum to private enterprises, indicated the sources.
