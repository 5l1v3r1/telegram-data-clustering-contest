Prince Andrew statement as he will 'step back from public duties' following Jeffrey Epstein scandal

The Queen has given her permission to let Prince Andrew step down from public duties after he faced widespread criticism

20 Nov 2019, 18:14 by Kathryn Riddell

Prince Andrew will "step back from public duties" it was announced days after a disastrous TV interview on his friendship with a sex offender.

The Queen has given permission for the Duke of York to “step back from public duties for the foreseeable future” amid criticism over his relationship with Jeffrey Epstein, the duke said.

In the statement released on Wednesday night, the Duke, 59, said that the scandal over his friendship with American financier Epstein had become "a major disruption" to the Royal family 's work.

He said: "Therefore, I have asked Her Majesty if I may step back from public duties for the foreseeable future, and she has given her permission."

He has also agreed to speak to the forces investigating Epstein's offences involving underage girls.

Addressing the criticism he has faced in the wake of the Newsnight interview with Emily Maitlis, he added: "I continue to unequivocally regret my ill-judged association with Jeffrey Epstein.

"His suicide has led many unanswered questions, particularly for his victims , and I deeply sympathise with everyone who has been affected and wants some form of closure.

"I can only hope that, in time, they will be able to rebuild their lives.

"Of course, I am willing to help any appropriate law enforcement agency with their investigations, if required."

The Queen's second son was interviewed on Saturday night and made a series of bizarre claims including being in a "Pizza Express in Woking" on the night he allegedly met Epstein victim Virginia Roberts.

The Prince also said during the interview that despite his former pal’s crimes he did not regret their friendship as it helped him professionally.

He was later widely condemned for failing to sympathise with the victims of Epstein, who killed himself in prison earlier this year.
