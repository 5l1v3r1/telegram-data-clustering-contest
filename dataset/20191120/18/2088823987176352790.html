Column: Bills fans can shout about No. 1 TV ratings for second straight week

20 Nov 2019, 18:46

For the second straight week, Buffalo Bills fans can shout "We're No.1."

Ratings for Buffalo Bills games are routinely hitting season highs every week largely due to the team’s 7-3 record.

The Bills’ 37-20 victory over the Miami Dolphins Sunday had a season-high 43.9 rating on WIVB-TV, the local CBS affiliate. Buffalo ranked No. 1 among all CBS markets carrying NFL games Sunday.

The rating Sunday was seven-tenths of a point higher than the previous high 43.2 rating for the Bills’ 19-16 loss to the Cleveland Browns a week earlier when the Buffalo market also had the highest-rated game on CBS that Sunday.

The rating for the Cleveland game was a point higher than the previous high of 42.2 for the Bills’ 24-9 victory over Washington and their 31-13 loss to Philadelphia. Both games were carried on WUTV, the local Fox affiliate.

Bills games are easily the highest-rated programs in Western New York at a time few prime-time entertainment programs get double digit-ratings.

By comparison, the Bills-Dolphins game had an 11.3 rating on the CBS affiliate in Miami.

However, Buffalo is one of the smallest markets in the NFL, which means lower ratings in bigger markets can have considerably more viewers.

The Buffalo market is No. 52 in the country, with each rating point equaling 5,767 households. The Miami-Fort Lauderdale market is No. 16, with each point equaling 16,422 households.

That means about 253,700 households watched the game in the Buffalo market and about 185, 570 households watched in the Miami market.
