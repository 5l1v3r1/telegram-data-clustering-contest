Newcastle United star and Nottingham Forest's long-time target - the players linked with the Reds

Nottingham Forest transfer latest | With just over a month until the January window opens, we've put together a list of every player that has been linked with a move to the City Ground

20 Nov 2019, 18:00 by Josh Murray, Mark Wakefield, Ben Husband

The January transfer window opens is fast approaching for Nottingham Forest and their Championship rivals.

Forest signed 12 players during the summer, but could they look to dip back into the market in the New Year to strengthen their squad and their bid for promotion to the Premier League?

Sabri Lamouchi’s side are in fifth place in the table after the final international break of 2019, and they have been beaten only three times to show a push for the top flight could be on the cards.

Here, we take a look at the players who have been linked with a move to the City Ground during the January transfer window in recent weeks.

Jack Clarke

Forest have been linked with a January move for Leeds United winger Jack Clarke .

The 18-year-old is currently on loan at Elland Road from Premier League side Tottenham Hotspur but has seen little game time.

And according to Sporting Life , he is “likely” to be recalled by his parent club in January as, it is claimed, a couple of other Championship clubs “have expressed an interest in taking him for the second half of the season”.

It is said the Reds could be one of those teams interested as they may look to add another winger when the window opens.

Dwight Gayle

Forest could reignite their interest in Newcastle United striker Dwight Gayle when the transfer window opens.  

Lamouchi’s Reds were keen to try and tempt the prolific Championship striker to the City Ground in the summer along with a clutch of other clubs, but were unable to complete the deal.

Now, according to the Athletic, Forest may well be tempted to move for the 29-year-old, who netted with such regularity at West Brom last season .

Billy Gilmour

Forest are said to have been eyeing up promising Chelsea youngster Billy Gilmour .

The 18-year-old midfielder came up through the academy at Scottish giants Rangers before making the move to Stamford Bridge two years ago.

According to London Football News, the Reds and Championship rivals Leeds “have been tracking the best players in Chelsea’s development side, for which Gilmour has caught the eye since being signed from Glasgow Rangers”.

Emil Bohinen

Although the 20-year-old hasn't been officially linked with the City Ground, his dad is keen for him to make the move.

Lars, who was part of Frank Clark's exciting squad from the early 90s is managing in his native Norway, but has revealed he hopes his son Emil returns to the Trent.

“I was hoping to bring him to Forest at some point actually, I think he can manage the standard (Championship). Like me, maybe the Premier League isn’t the first place to go but we will see," he said.

Emil, like his old man, operates in midfield and is a regular representative of his national side's U21 team.

Lawrence Shankland

Forest are set to rekindle their interest in long-term transfer target Lawrence Shankland , according to reports.

The Reds were extensively linked with the striker in the summer, before he joined Dundee United.

The Sun reported that Sabri Lamouchi is looking to add another striker in January to provide competition for top scorer Lewis Grabban.

Hugo Cuypers

Forest have been linked with a move for Belgian striker Hugo Cuypers when the transfer window opens in January.

The 22-year-old is on loan at French second division club Ajaccio from Olympiacos, the Greek giants, like Forest , owned by Evangelos Marinakis .

According to Greek media outlet SDNA , the Reds have been impressed by the Under-19 international’s blistering start to the French league season, and are considering a move in January.

Randell Williams

Forest are reportedly chasing Exeter City winger Randell Williams with a view to a January transfer swoop.

Cardiff City, QPR and Huddersfield Town have all been linked with the midfielder, who has been instrumental in Exeter's charge to the very top of the fourth tier, according to TeamTalk.

The former Tottenham Hotspur youth star, 22, has turned out 19 times this season for the Grecians in League Two, scoring once and impressing during his time in Devon.
