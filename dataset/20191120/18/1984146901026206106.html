Newcastle United's team of the next decade predicted with Longstaff and Saint-Maximin and Lascelles

We used popular football management simulation game Football Manager 2020 to simulate the next decade and get a prediction on how it might pan out for Newcastle United.

20 Nov 2019, 18:00 by Nathan Bliss

At the beginning of the decade, in the 2011/12 season, Newcastle United were on cloud nine after finishing in fifth position in the Premier League.

It was a fantastic season for the club, with Demba Ba the club's top goalscorer, featuring alongside players like Moussa Sissoko, Hatem Ben Arfa, Papiss Cisse, Yohan Cabaye and Fabricio Coluccini.

But, that was as good as it got for the Magpies, who were relegated just four seasons later, before being promoted back to the Premier League in 2017/18.

As we approach the next decade, you can't help but wonder how the next decade could turn out for Newcastle.

What will the team look like in 2030? And, who will make the Team of the Decade?

We used Football Manager 2020 to predict what might happen over the next decade. You know, for a bit of fun.

Newcastle United's Team of the next Decade (2020-2030)

 

Newcastle United's Team of the next Decade, as predicted by Football Manager 2020, is as follows:

First XI: Alexander Nubel; Sergino Dest, Jamaal Lascelles, Jonathan Panzo, Brandon Williams; Miguel Almiron, Sean Longstaff; Allan Saint-Maximin, Takefusa Kubo, Hakon Evjen; Joelinton.

Subs: Martin Dubravka, Mason Holgate, Tosin Adarabioyo, Charly Musonda, Pedro Neto, Andrew Kavanagh, Lukas Nmecha.

According to Football Manager 2020, five of the players currently in the 2019/20 side will be consistently strong performers over the next decade, earning a place in the Team of the next Decade.

Jamaal Lascelles is predicted to make another 227 appearances for the club, before leaving in the 2025/26 season.

Sean Longstaff is predicted to make the most appearances of anyone over the next decade, making an incredible 305 appearances, scoring 31 goals, becoming a true Newcastle legend, among the likes of Kevin Keegan, Alan Shearer and Jackie Milburn.

Miguel Almiron is predicted to have a long career at St James Park, spending a further six seasons at the club clocking up 207 appearances, scoring 35 goals.

Allan Saint-Maximin is also named in the best eleven and is predicted to spend another four seasons at the club, before leaving in a big money move to Juventus in 2023/24.

Joelinton's goal record over the next decade is predicted to be very, very strong. In 223 appearances over the next decade, the Brazilian will score 89 goals, according to Football Manager 2020, before moving to Chinese side SIPG in 2025 for a massive £54 million.
