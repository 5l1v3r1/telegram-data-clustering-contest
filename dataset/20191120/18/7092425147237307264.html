Prince Andrew scaling back on royal duties

20 Nov 2019, 18:05 by James Cox

Prince Andrew will be stepping back from royal duties for the "foreseeable future" following the Jeffrey Epstein scandal.

Prince Andrew said his links with the convicted sex offender have become a "major disruption" to the royal family.

He said he deeply sympathised with Epstein's victims and "everyone who has been affected and wants some form of closure" in a statement.

The Duke of York has faced backlash after a BBC interview in which he discussed his relationship with Epstein.

Prince Andrew said he had asked the Queen if he could step back from public duties, and she had given her permission.

"I continue to unequivocally regret my ill-judged association with Jeffrey Epstein. His suicide has left many unanswered questions, particularly for his victims, and I deeply sympathise with everyone who has been affected and wants some form of closure.

"I can only hope that, in time, they will be able to rebuild their lives."

He said he was "willing to help any appropriate law enforcement agency with their investigations, if required".

BT are one of the organisations to have distanced themselves from Prince Andrew since the BBC interview.

Murdoch University and RMIT University in Melbourne also said they would no longer work with "Pitch@Palace" - a charity founded by Prince Andrew to promote entrepreneurship.

Murdoch University said it had "advised Buckingham Palace it would not continue its participation" in the Pitch@Palace project.

Several UK-based businesses, including Standard Chartered and KPMG, have also distanced themselves from the prince and his charities.
