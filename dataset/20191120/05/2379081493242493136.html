Yuan slips to fresh 2-week low on Trump's tariff warning

20 Nov 2019, 05:20 by Reuters

SHANGHAI — China’s yuan slipped to a new

two-week low against the dollar on Wednesday morning, in

response to a much weaker midpoint and signs of tension between

Beijing and Washington.

Prospects for a trade deal turned bleaker after U.S.

President Donald Trump threatened to raise fresh tariffs on

Chinese goods if negotiations fail.

Speaking at a cabinet meeting at the White House, Trump said

he had a good relationship with China, noting that China was

“moving along.” However, he said China would have to make a deal

“I like.”

China on Wednesday also condemned the U.S. Senate passing

legislation aimed at protecting human rights in Hong Kong,

vowing to take the steps necessary to safeguard its sovereignty

and security.

Uncertainty around Sino-U.S. relations dented market

sentiment, with both corporate clients and proprietary traders

unwilling to pick a direction for the yuan for now.

Prior to market opening, the People’s Bank of China (PBOC)

set the midpoint rate at 7.0118 per dollar, 88 pips

weaker than the previous fix of 7.003, and was the weakest since

Nov. 5.

In the spot market, onshore yuan opened at 7.0330

per dollar and eased to a low of 7.0345 at one point in morning

trade, the weakest since Nov. 4.

As of midday, the spot yuan was changing hands at 7.0304, 32

pips weaker than the previous late session close.

“Any tiny news around the trade talks could bring volatility

to the market for now,” said a trader at a Chinese bank, adding

investors were awaiting confirmation before making huge bets on

the yuan.

OCBC Wing Hang Bank said in a note that the yuan’s

short-term movements will largely depend on the dollar’s

performance and the developments in the Sino-U.S. trade talks,

“especially if the two sides will roll back existing tariffs and

how big the size would be.”

Separately, China announced a lower new benchmark lending

rate on Wednesday, as widely expected, moving to drive down

funding costs and shore up an economy hurt by slowing demand and

trade tariffs.

The global dollar index rose to 97.884 at midday from

the previous close of 97.856.

The offshore yuan was trading at 7.0333 per dollar

as of midday.
