BlueVine Gets $102M For SMB Banking

20 Nov 2019, 05:44 by PYMNTS

Small business banking provider BlueVine announced Tuesday (Nov. 19) it has raised $102.5 million in a Series F round of equity financing.

California-based BlueVine offers a suite of products for business owners including BlueVine Business Checking, Line of Credit, Term Loan, and Invoice Factoring.

The round was led by ION Crossover Partners, with participation from all major existing investors, including Lightspeed Venture Partners, Menlo Ventures, 83North, SVB Capital, Nationwide, Citi Ventures, Microsoft’s venture fund, M12, and private investors, as well as new investors including MUFG Innovation Partners Co., Ltd, O.G. Tech – Eyal Ofer’s VC, Vintage Investment Partners, ION Group, Maor Investments and other private investors.

The funding will be used to further build and scale BlueVine’s banking platform, which boasts a business checking account that integrates with BlueVine’s existing suite of online financing products. The infusion will also be used to develop future banking features, such as integrations with BlueVine’s existing working capital solutions, as well as expand the BlueVine team.

“The recent launch of BlueVine Checking demonstrates our commitment to revolutionize banking for small business owners with a full suite of services designed specifically to meet their unique needs,” Eyal Lifshitz, CEO and co-founder of BlueVine, said in a press release. “This funding further validates our mission and will help democratize true business-grade banking for small businesses who have been underserved for so long.”

Investors say BlueVine fulfills a need among small business and helps them manage their financial needs.

“BlueVine has demonstrated a track record of success with their multiple financing products and set themselves apart with their vision of a complete platform of innovative banking products for small businesses,” added Jonathan Kolodny, partner at ION Crossover Partners.
