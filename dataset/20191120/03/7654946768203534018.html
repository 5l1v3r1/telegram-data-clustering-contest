Cops search for masked man who vandalized more than 50 cars in Bay Ridge

20 Nov 2019, 03:57 by Ben Feuerherd

Police on Tuesday released an image of the person suspected in vandalizing more than 50 cars parked on a leafy Brooklyn street this weekend.

The suspect, dressed in all black and wearing a face mask, scratched up and slashed the tires of the vehicles on Saturday night and early Sunday morning in Bay Ridge, cops said.

The vandal struck all of the cars when they were parked on Shore Road between Bayridge Avenue and Bayridge Parkway, cops said.

The NYPD has upped patrols in the area since the vandal struck, with the commanding officer of the local precinct deploying “Neighborhood Coordination Officers, patrol officers and anti-crime personnel to the area to conduct patrols to monitor the area and search for a suspect(s) during every tour,” an NYPD spokesperson said.

City councilman Justin Brannan, who represents the neighborhood, has offered a $5,000 reward for information that leads to the arrest of the vandal.
