Region soaks up first autumn rain & snow

20 Nov 2019

INLAND EMPIRE - (INT) - Southern California will be drying off following a double dose of rain.

Tuesdays incursion of tropical moisture was followed by a cold front Wednesday that chilled the Southland. The rain, thunderstorms, snow and hail came in the wake of a series of record-setting warm days.

The first storm of the season made its mark. Rainfall exceeded 4-inches at Valley Center in north San Diego County. In the San Bernardino Mountains where chain requirements were posted, nearly 5-inches of early season snow was measured at Green Valley Lake.

The storm is winding down with diminishing shower chances. Dry weather returns Friday accompanied by a muted warming trend that should boost weekend temperatures back into the 70s.

Just ahead of Thanksgiving, an ensemble of forecast computer models brings back another troughing pattern over the Eastern Pacific with an increasing likelihood of precipitation.
