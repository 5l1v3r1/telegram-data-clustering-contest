Woodman was 'someone I believed would come and buy tickets': Pakula

20 Nov 2019, 00:23 by Sumeyya Ilanbey

Senior Victorian minister Martin Pakula says the property developer at the centre of the Casey land scandal was invited to his campaign fundraiser last year because he was "someone who I believed would come and buy tickets".

John Woodman, the developer and key witness in public hearings about alleged corruption at the City of Casey, contributed $3300 to Mr Pakula’s fundraiser in 2018 and had attended two other fundraisers in the past seven years.

Mr Pakula has denied claims made in Independent and Broad-based Anti-corruption Commission hearings this week that he received a separate donation of $20,800.

Under pressure to explain his involvement with the developer, Mr Pakula took to ABC radio on Wednesday morning to defend his conduct during the last election.

“Developers, unions, companies, businesspeople, all sorts of people were invited to attend fundraisers or invited to attend lunches or invited to make a donation,” Mr Pakula told ABC Radio.

“I always took the view, why should a developer be any less able to be invited to a fundraiser than a bank or law firm or trade union?”

He said there was “no evidence whatsoever” that Mr Woodman had influenced government decisions.

“It’s all good and well to run general smear, but unless there’s direct observable benefits, people shouldn’t be put through this kind of suspicion.”

Along with "a couple of other MPs" Mr Pakula had run a fundraiser at ZINC at Federation Square, attended by 100 people, in the lead up to the election.

“And Watsons [the planning firm where Mr Woodman is director] bought a few tickets to the value of $3300,” he said.

When pressed why he invited Mr Woodman, Mr Pakula said: “Because by definition he was someone who attended fundraisers of mine in the past.

“[He was] someone who I believed would come and buy tickets. I’ve never approached people for donation because I didn’t run my fundraisers.

“It’s quite normal when you run a fundraiser, either yourself, or someone else, will send out invitations to fundraisers and he was someone who an invitation was sent to.”

Mr Woodman contributed to local campaigns for the then-roads minister Luke Donnellan and aspiring Cranbourne MP Pauline Richards.

There is no suggestion that the donations and fundraisers have influenced any particular planning decisions by the Andrews government to favour Mr Woodman or his clients.

Mr Pakula said he understood why Mr Woodman’s donations to his fundraising campaign were a “troubling perception” but said donation laws had changed to prevent undue influence, and perceptions of undue influence.

He said Mr Woodman’s links to the Casey land scandal had only been revealed in the Independent and Broad-based Anti-corruption Commission hearings over the past two days, and that he wouldn’t have known about it last year.
