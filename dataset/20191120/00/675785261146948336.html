Brave dad catches paedophile released by mistake then takes victory selfie

Graham Lamb was on the bus in Hull when he noticed a familiar face getting on - after checking on his phone he felt confident enough it was wanted paedophile Valerijs Gordijis to make a citizen arrest

20 Nov 2019, 00:32 by Milo Boyd, Michael Mutch, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/milo-boyd/

A man detained a convicted paedophile who was released from prison by mistake after spotting his wanted notice on Facebook .

Graham Lamb was sitting on a bus when he clocked a familiar figure boarding the vehicle.

After checking the Hull Live website on his phone, the 45-year-old felt certain it was Valerijs Gordijis - a convicted paedophile wrongly released just the day before.

“He got on and I thought 'it can’t be',” said Mr Lamb.

“It can’t be that much of a coincidence but he was right in front of me.

“I was checking Facebook and it popped up saying convicted paedophile was out of prison, and he was right in front of me on the bus.

 

“I kept looking at him and he kept looking at me. I brought up his picture on my phone and that was when I realised it was the same guy.”

Gordijis had been serving a sentence at HMP Hull for sexual offences against children.

On Tuesday morning police issued an appeal for the 46-year-old after he was released by accident.

Realising he was in the pervert's presence, Mr Lamb decided he could not let him go free.

Mr Lamb said: “I said to the bus driver it was him and that she needed to stop the bus so I could ring the police.

“I showed her the picture and he looked at the phone and tried getting off the bus so I went after him. He tried to avoid me so I said ‘stay there’ and I rang the police.

 

“I was ready to grab him and pin him down but I did not need to. He was only a small guy and would not have put up much of a fight.

“As soon as I said 'stay there' he had a worried look on his face. He had the same scar above his left eye. I knew it was him.”

As luck would have it there was a police car parked nearby.

"As soon as the bus pulled away there was a police car right next to it so I flagged it down," the dad continued.

“He deserves to be back in prison and I am glad that he is off the streets and is no longer a danger to anyone else or any other children.”

 

The Ministry of Justice confirmed that  Gordijs was released by mistake : "Valerijs Gordijs was released in error from HMP Hull yesterday but has since been recaptured.

“Releases in error are very rare but we take them extremely seriously and working closely with police to bring offenders back into custody quickly.

A  Humberside Police  spokesman added: "Following our media appeal, Valerijs Gordijis was located on Hessle Road this morning. He was arrested and will be returned to prison.

"We want to thank everyone who shared our appeal."
