Netflix’s December Release Schedule Is Here & There’s A Sneaky New Mulaney Special

20 Nov 2019, 00:27 by By Cam Tyeson

The kind and gentle folk at Netflix Australia have made their list, checked it twice, and decided we’ve all been very, very nice this year, because not only have they unveiled their full list of December releases this morning, but contained within is a very sneaky, very surprise, very adorable-sounding new comedy special from your friend and mine, John Mulaney.

Come Christmas Eve, right as we’ll all be on the verge of unwrapping various gifts, Netflix will unwrap the new special, John Mulaney and the Sack Lunch Bunch: a variety comedy special reportedly featuring Mulaney and “The Sack Lunch Bunch,” a bunch of literal children, as they comically tackle grown-up problems together. You’d be hard pressed to find a more wholesome-sounding thing out there, I tell you what.

Beyond that, it’s a good-ass month for comedy specials all around. Michelle Wolf, Tiffany Haddish, and Jack Whitehall all have brand new specials set for release throughout December. As does “our” very own Ronny Chieng, whose debut Netflix special Asian Comedian Destroys America! is set to arrive on December 17th.

Not keen on stand-up? That’s cool, man. Other stuff floating around this coming December includes the streaming arrival of the critically acclaimed Lady Bird (December 8) and I, Tonya (December 21).

Then there’s the back half of Vikings season five, crime docos The Confession Killer and Don’t Fuck With Cats: Hunting An Internet Killer, and a throng of tip top flicks including the likes of Raiders of the Lost Ark, Rush Hour, Tropic Thunder, Honey, Knocked Up, Baby Mama, and the sorely underrated and deeply funny Accepted.

December! Christmas! New shit to watch!
