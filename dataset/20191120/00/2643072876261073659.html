Nigeria Breaking News, Today, Wednesday, 20th, November 2019

20 Nov 2019 by Enioluwa Adeniyi

This is a Round-up of some breaking news for Today, 20th, November 2019.

PDP Holds Emergency NWC Meeting Today

The Peoples Democratic Party will hold an emergency meeting today, Wednesday, to deliberate on Saturday’s governorship elections in Kogi and Bayelsa States.

The PDP lost both elections conducted by the Independent National Electoral Commission (INEC).

INEC Reveals When Certificate Of Return Would Be Presented To Lyon

The Independent National Electoral Commission (INEC) has announced that the Bayelsa State Governor-Elect, Chief David Lyon will be presented a Certificate of Return on Thursday.

Lyon defeated 44 candidates including the Peoples Democratic Party (PDP)‘s flag bearer, Senator Douye Diri, to emerge the winner of the polls last Saturday.

Jonathan’s Team Speaks On His Defection To APC

The alleged report claiming that the former President, Goodluck Jonathan has concluded plans to defect from the Peoples Democratic Party to the All Progressives Congress has been debunked by his media team.

Following the reported disagreement between the Bayelsa State Governor, Seriake Dickson and the former President, there have been speculations that Jonathan supported the candidate of APC, David Lyon in the November 16 governorship election and also celebrated his victory.

Bayelsa: Dickson Reveals Next Move, Says Jonathan Remains His ‘Oga’

Governor Seriake Dickson has said the Peoples Democratic Party (PDP) will approach the Election Petition Tribunal to challenged the result of the Bayelsa Governorship election.

Naija News reports that the National Electoral Commission (INEC), had declared David Lyon the winner of the Bayelsa Governorship Election.

Kogi’s Election Was A Civil War – Dino Melaye

Senator Dino Melaye, the lawmaker representing Kogi west senatorial district, has described as a civil war, the senatorial rerun and gubernatorial elections held on Saturday, November 16th in Kogi state.

The lawmaker said so while disclosing that 16 lives were lost during the election.
