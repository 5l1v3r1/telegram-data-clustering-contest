Ex-Poly Prep student claims he was sexually abused by Latin teacher

20 Nov 2019, 00:30 by Andrew Denney and Ben Feuerherd

A former student at Brooklyn’s tony Poly Prep Country Day School filed a lawsuit against the school claiming he was sexually abused by a teacher and witnessed other abuse that was covered up.

The former student, who was identified as John Doe in the court documents, claims in the suit that longtime Latin teacher John Miller sexually abused him on a number of occasions from 1974 to 1976.

During his junior year of high school, Miller — who was also an Episcopal priest — asked the boy to stay after class, shoved him against a wall and pressed up against his private parts, the suit in Brooklyn supreme court alleges.

Terrified, the boy began to cry — and Miller responded by forcibly kissing him and licking his tears off his face, according to the suit.

On another occasion, Miller, who is no longer alive, invited the boy to the Yale Club in Manhattan to discuss a college recommendation letter, the suit states.

He then brought the boy to his Park Avenue apartment and tried to coerce him into sex, according to the suit.

“Miller made aggressive advances and tried to have oral sex with Doe,” the suit states.

The defendant also claims he witnessed abuse by football coach Phillip Foglietta, who was at the center of a previous sexual abuse lawsuit brought against the school.

The man claims that while he was on the football team, Foglietta groped and abused children in plain sight to brag that he could get away with it.

In one instance, Foglietta groped a boy in front of the defendant while giving him “defiant” stares, which he “understood to mean, ‘What the fuck are you going to do about it’?” the suit says.

Poly Prep did not immediately respond to request for comment.
