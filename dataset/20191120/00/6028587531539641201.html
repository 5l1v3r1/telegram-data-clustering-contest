Three In Ten Young Trans People Attempt Suicide

20 Nov 2019 by Jamie Wareham

Three in 10 young trans people in America have tried to take their life in the last year, according to a new report. The new report also says 6 in 10 trans and non-binary young people have seriously considered taking their lives too. 

Over 34,000 young LGBTQ people were surveyed for the report, the largest survey of LGBTQ youth mental health ever done. 

Released to mark Transgender Day of Remembrance, The Trevor Project has released its inaugural National Survey on LGBTQ Youth Mental Health. 

A vast 8 in 10 of the trans and non-binary respondents have faced discrimination due to their gender identity.

The figures of trans young people who have tried to take their life are double their cisgender, people born in the gender they identify with, LGB counterparts.

Only 15% of young lesbian, gay and bisexual people will try to take their life, compared to 29% of young trans people.

Both figures are significantly higher when compared to the average in the wider population. Compared to the average among high school aged youth, just 5.4% of straight-identified students reported attempting suicide in the past year.

This leaves lesbian, gay, bisexual, and transgender youth over four times more likely to attempt suicide than their peers.

This latest report is in line with other studies that have shown a range of two in ten to nearly half of all trans people will attempt suicide at some point in their lives.

The Centre for Suicide Prevention reports, based on multiple studies, that the number is somewhere between two and four in 10 trans people will try to take their life. 

Marking the significance of these mental health numbers, just days after it was revealed 331 trans people had been killed in the last year, Dr Amy E. Green, Director of Research for The Trevor Project says: 

“Transgender and non-binary youth may face a range of unique stressors associated with their adolescent development as well as increased experiences of internalized stigma, discrimination, and rejection from others.

“No one factor causes an individual to attempt suicide, but the cumulative effect of these stressors can be detrimental to one’s mental wellness and increase the risk for suicidal ideation.”

Two in three young LGBTQ report someone trying to convince them to ‘convert’ or change their identity

The study also reveals a staggering 2 in 3 American LGBTQ youth have reported that someone tried to convince them to change their sexual orientation or gender identity.

Of the LGBTQ young people who have been through conversion therapy, their likelihood of attempting suicide doubles from 2 in ten to 4 in ten. 

The therapy, now banned in many countries and some states in America, promises the impossible of ‘changing people’s sexualities’ often with cruel and inhumane tactics.

The reality for many young LGBTQ Americans, many of whom will come from religious backgrounds is their families will send them to these types of therapies.

But Dr Green says “research shows that LGBTQ youth who report having at least one accepting adult were 40% less likely to report a suicide attempt in the past year.”

The Trevor Project, which provides crisis services, also noted that their respondents were much more likely to ask for help with digital support services, over phone or ‘IRL’ support. 

The trend is what’s sparked many youth charities to invest in text support services like ‘Shout,’ the mental health text service supported by the Duke and Duchess of Cambridge.

Their Royal Highnesses met with volunteers, including trans and non-binary activists, at an event in London, UK, for the charity this week.

When is Transgender Day Of Remembrance 2019?

The statistics were released today to mark Transgender Day of Remembrance.

Trans Day Of Remembrance (TDoR) is a day which remembers those trans and gender-diverse people who have been victims of homicide, held each year on November 20, 2019.

Across the world, the LGBTQ community and their allies will be gathering for vigils to remember those lost this year. 

Many will read names from the Trans Murder Monitoring report list which tracks trans homicides across the world.

Juliana Ferreira was the first death recorded this year on October 3 2018. She was found dead and in a state of decomposition near a large stone which local media posed as the cause of death. The local media reports deadname Juliana, referring to her with a male name. 

Júnia Franco, the last name on the list this year, was stabbed 13 times and left in a pool of blood. They were killed on September 29 2019. 

Juliana and Júnia will be the first and last names of over three hundred, which will be read out at vigils that will take place across the world today, led by the LGBTQ community. 

Globally, use a search engine and type ‘mental health help’ for local support or call the emergency services if your life is at risk.
