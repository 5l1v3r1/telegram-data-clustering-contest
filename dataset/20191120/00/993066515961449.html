Baillie Gifford Prize won by biography of Jack the Ripper victims

20 Nov 2019, 00:31

A biography of the victims of Jack the Ripper has won a major literary award.

Hallie Rubenhold’s The Five has been awarded the Baillie Gifford Prize for Non-Fiction, organisers announced on Tuesday.

The British historian takes home £50,00 for winning the award, with chair of judges and editor of The Times Literary Supplement Stig Abell describing The Five as “brilliantly written and “brilliantly researched”.

He added: “It’s a great story and it’s a great moral act, reclaiming the voice of these women.”
