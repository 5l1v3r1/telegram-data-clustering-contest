At Least 81% Of Americans Are Making Holiday Plans This Year, New Survey Finds

20 Nov 2019 by Laura Parker

In good news for the U.S. economy, a new survey has found that at at least 81% of Americans plan on traveling for one or more holidays this season. The survey, conducted by HotelPlanner, group travel booking website, surveyed Americans across a range of demographics to learn about their holiday travel plans this Thanksgiving and Christmas.

In what could be perceived as a sign that the American economy is growing stronger, 65% of respondents with household incomes of less than $50,000 say they are planning on traveling this holiday season, compared with 80% of respondents with a household income of $125,000. (The caveat is that only 30% of the first group are actually going on vacation; the other 70% say their primary reason for travel is to visit family.)
