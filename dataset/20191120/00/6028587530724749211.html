Why BorgWarner Stock Is Up 40% Over The Last Three Months

20 Nov 2019 by Trefis Team

BorgWarner (NYSE: BWA), an automotive components company that is best known for its power train components, has seen its stock rally by close to 40% over the last three months. Part of the increase was driven by the company’s stronger-than-expected Q3 2019 results, with EPS coming in at $0.96 per share, well ahead of consensus of $0.85. The company also raised the lower end of its full-year guidance. While the auto market has been facing some headwinds with growth cooling, BorgWarner sales have been outperforming the industry-driven partly by its higher exposure to hybrid and electric. The company’s exposure to the trade war between the U.S and China is also relatively limited. We ‘step back’ from these recent swings to review BorgWarner’s performance over the last few years, as a context for what might come next. A closer look At BorgWarner’s Total Revenues over the last few years and the outlook

Total Revenues for BorgWarner moderately increased from $9.80 Bil in 2017 to $10.5 Bil in 2018; an increase of 7.45%.

This compares with Total Revenues growth of:

  • -3.39% in 2015
  • 13.1% in 2016
  • 8.03% in 2017

We expect Total Revenues to decline by 4% to $10.1 billion in 2019.

A closer look At BorgWarner’s Total Expenses over the last few years and the outlook

Total Expense for BorgWarner moderately increased from $8.74 Bil in 2017 to $9.33 Bil in 2018; an increase of 6.85%.

This compares with Total Expense growth of:

  • -3.12% in 2015
  • 14.5% in 2016
  • 7.47% in 2017

We expect Total Expenses to decline by 5% to $9 billion in 2019.
