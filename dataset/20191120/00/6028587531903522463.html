Holiday Gift Guide 2019: The Best British Stocking Stuffers

20 Nov 2019 by Bridget Arsenault

Do you have a friend who could reel off the last three designers Meghan Markle has worn? Or who lives for a Bake Off marathon? All around the world - not just in Old Blighty - English products and brands are beloved. Who could resist a Fortnum & Mason Christmas hamper, stuffed to the brim with jams, biscuits and bubbles? And while it’s wonderful to receive those big ticket holiday items, there is also something delicious about unloading a stocking filled with unexpected gems. A mix of high and low, male and female, these British-made pieces are sure to delight.Word Merge By Clarendon Games, £6.50

The holidays are the ideal time to crack open a new game. Bright, bold and beautifully packaged, Word Merge is a brand-new product from London-based Clarendon Games. The ultimate word game for friends and family, players compete to find words as letter cards are dealt out onto the table. The first person to shout out a word using three cards or more, wins those cards! It’s frenzied and fast-paced - a game doesn’t tend to take more than 20 minutes - and it’s unadulterated fun!

Studs By Bear Basics, £40

East London-based jeweller Bear Brooksbank launched her Bear Basics collection last year, in order to offer a more affordable everyday option to her loyal client base. The Bear character is her brand staple, and these 20-21ct yellow-gold studs are made with all the same attention to detail and design as her core collection. Suiting styles both young and old, these are sold as a pair but can be worn individually alongside Bear’s delightful huggies or hoops if one so pleases.

Photo Frame By The White Company, £20

Printed photographs make such a lovely gift. This silver-plated stainless steel double photo frame from The White Company is sure to look at home atop any surface. The tarnish-proof exterior means it never needs a polish, a fact that will be much appreciated by the recipient. And it even comes stocking-ready in a gift box.

Sparkling Wine By Nutbourne, £24.50

Surely no grown-up stocking is complete without a bottle of bubbles? In recent years, producers in England have been scooping up awards and accolades for their complex and fruity sparkling wines. Vines were first planted at Nutbourne in 1980. In 1991 the vineyards were passed onto the Gladwin family - who are also purveyors of a raft of abominably good London restaurants. Made with Pinot Noir and Chardonnay grapes, their traditional fermented sparkling wine competes with all the well-known marques.

Travel Accessories Case By Stow London, £80

The Compass leather accessories case by Stow London is ideal both for the home and to snatch when travelling. Lined with suede, it fits everything from earrings to small necklaces, rings, cufflinks or travel essentials such as headphones, charging cables and USB sticks. Inspired by the aesthetic of a classic compass case, the leather exterior comes in a rainbow of colors to suit every style.

Mayfair Sock Set By London Sock Company, £60

Everyone loves to hate the gift of socks. But this three-pack of merino wool socks from the London Sock Company are just delightful. And they’re the ideal gift - entirely useful but perhaps too indulgent to buy one’s self. Designed in smart navy blue, these mid-calf, medium-thickness socks are breathable and long-lasting.

Napkins By Lottie Day, £35

Lottie Day designs and produces products from her own original illustrations, inspired by her home county of Norfolk. Day then screen-prints the drawings onto home textiles - napkins, dish cloths, place mats, lampshades and more. Handmade in the UK on natural cotton, these rustic accessories are a delight.

Skincare By Sarah, £55

Sisters and entrepreneurs Sarah and Lauren have created a unisex UK-made skincare line out of entirely natural and organic, plant-based ingredients. At By Sarah they consciously include the full list of ingredients on the front label across the entire collection, so users know exactly what they’re putting on their skin. This festive box - containing a signature body and hair oil - has a sleeve made entirely from crushed grapes - a UK skincare first - and all the packaging is recyclable.

Shirt By WNU, £85

Behind-the-scenes for UK charity Smart Work, Meghan Markle appeared in her first official Instagram story as @SussexRoyal. In the photos Markle looked at ease, wearing a blue-and-white striped linen shirt by British brand With Nothing Underneath. Founded in 2017 by Pip Durrell, an ex Vogue stylist and former Tatler jewelry editor, Durrell wanted a simple easy-going boyfriend-style shirt, but she was stumped. So Durrell set out to create one herself. Designed to withstand trends and generations of wear, this 100 percent poplin cotton shirt proves the perfect addition to every woman’s wardrobe.

Sauna Gift Card By Glow Bar, From £40

Sasha Sabapathy grew up between Malaysia, London and New York and in 2018 founded Glow Bar in central London to help women manage their stress. A haven of calm and relaxation, it’s part store, part nourishing cafe and part spa. Downstairs, in addition to a menu of facial treatments, clients can book in for a sweat session. Based on ancient South American and India healing practices, Glow Bar offers up sessions in infrared saunas to help the body repair and recharge. It’s the perfect wellness gift and someone to enjoy in their own time.
