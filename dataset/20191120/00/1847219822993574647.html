Riga Mayor ready to resign, if no new majority in City Council

20 Nov 2019 by BNN

Riga Mayor Oļegs Burovs (Honour to Serve Riga) is looking for a new majority after cooperation with his party has been stopped by Harmony party. If no new majority is established, Burovs would resign, he said in an interview with Latvijas Radio.

In the interview on Wednesday, November 20, Burovs, who is the head of the Riga City Council since August 2019, said he plans to find coalition partners to form a majority in up to two weeks. If the plan fails, the politician would be ready to resign and call a snap municipal election.

Burovs said he seeks to find a stable majority, which would be more than 32 councilmembers in the 60-seat Riga City Council.
