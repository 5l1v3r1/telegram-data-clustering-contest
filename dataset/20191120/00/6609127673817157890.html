XMAS TREAT

Love Island’s Anton Danyluk set to open the Scottish Sun’s Sunta Store next month

20 Nov 2019, 00:48 by Matt Bendoris

THE Scottish Sun’s celebrity packed charity shop is returning to spread more Christmas cheer — and this year is going to Trump them all.

Love Island hunk Anton Danyluk will open our Sunta Store at the end of this month.

That’s before we get a very special visit by none other than the President of the United States Of America — aka the UK’s No1 impressionist Lewis MacLeod.

Last night “Trump” said: “I’m half-Scotch you know so I can’t wait to return to Scotchland. So come along and meet me at The Scotch-ish Sun charity shop and help Make Christmas Great Again.”

All money raised this year with go to MND Scotland and Alzheimer Scotland.

And they’re already on to a winner after The People’s Postcode Lottery donated £20,000 to our store.

Clara Govier, managing director of People’s Postcode Lottery, said: “Supporting charities is at the heart of what we do at People’s Postcode Lottery.

“Alzheimer Scotland and MND Scotland are two charities doing stand-out work caring for people and families affected by these diseases throughout the country.

"I’m delighted we’ve been able to kick-start the fundraising and get the Scottish Sun’s Christmas Shop off to a flying start for another year.”

Stagecoach chairman Sir Brian Souter has vowed to match every penny raised over the counter from the shop in Glasgow’s St Enoch Centre.

He’ll have to dig deep if last year’s record-breaking amount is anything to go by — our wonderful readers helped us rake in an incredible £13,000 at the tills.

Last night, Sir Brian said: “I’m delighted that Stagecoach will be matching the generous donations of Scottish Sun readers.

“MND Scotland and Alzheimer Scotland are incredible charities. Their work not only helps fund vital research into finding cures, it also means nobody needs to face these terrible conditions alone.”

Our Sunta Store opens its doors at 11am on Wednesday, November 27, on the first floor of the St Enoch Centre in Glasgow.

Generous big brands — including Amazon, HarperCollins, Lidl, Coop and United Wholesale (Scotland) Ltd — have donated thousands of items for sale, which will make perfect last minute Christmas gifts.

As if that wasn’t enough, a galaxy of star guests will be behind the cash desk every day to greet shoppers. To kick off the week, Anton and his bum-shaving mum Sherie Ann will officially open the store.

And celebs including Clyde One DJ George Bowie, wrestling heavyweight Grado, River City favourite Stephen Purdon and footie hero Frank McAvennie will be popping in throughout the week.

Scottish country legend Sydney Devine will be on song when he pops in and we’ll also have some incredible money-can’t-buy prizes at our Tombola stalls.

Scottish Sun editor Alan Muir said: “Anton is one of the hottest stars today thanks to his antics on Love Island. He’ll have plenty of fans desperate to meet him.

“But we shall also be paid a visit by one of the most important men in the world who will hopefully leave our customers with a warm, orange glow for Christmas.”

He added: “This the fourth year in the row we’ve opened our shop and we genuinely look forward to it every Christmas — it really kicks-off the whole Festive season.

“We have the most generous readers in the country and hopefully this year will bring in record-breaking amounts of money for two extremely worthwhile charities.”

Alzheimer Scotland work hard to make sure nobody faces dementia alone.

Not only do they offer support but they are also involved in research into the incurable condition which affects an estimated 90,000 people in Scotland — around 3,000 of whom are aged under 65.

Sa Craig, head of corporate partnerships & events at the charity, said: “We are delighted to be chosen by the Scottish Sun as part of their Sunta Store fundraising.

“All funds raised for us will help ensure that the charity can continue supporting people living with dementia, their carers and families, and aiding our mission that nobody should face dementia alone.”

MND Scotland provides care and support to people affected by motor neurone disease (MND), as well as funding vital research.

Iain McWhirter, MND Scotland’s Head of Fundraising, said: “I want to thank everyone at the Scottish Sun for choosing to support MND Scotland. This will raise an incredible amount of awareness of Motor Neurone Disease, and also raise money to continue funding ground-breaking research to find a cure, and provide vital support to families across Scotland.

Don’t miss the shop opening at 11am next Wednesday — with fantastic bargains to be had from as little as 50p, you would be crazy not to pop in.
