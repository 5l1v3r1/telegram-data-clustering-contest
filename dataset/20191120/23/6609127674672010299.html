XMAS CAST

Wrestler Grado & his Pinocchio panto castmates set to visit Scottish Sun’s Sunta Shop when it opens next week in Glasgow

20 Nov 2019, 23:44 by Matt Bendoris

THE Scottish Sun nose we’re on to a good thing after the cast of Pinocchio backed our pop-up charity shop.

Stars from Glasgow’s Pavilion Theatre’s panto — including telly wrestler Grado and River City favourites Stephen Purdon and Holly Jack — will be helping to spread some Christmas cheer when they appear in store next Saturday.

And they will all help raise vital funds for our chosen charities MND Scotland and Alzheimer Scotland.

Pavilion Theatre manager Iain Gordon — who also donated a family ticket for his show’s opening night — said: “The panto cast are delighted to support The Scottish Sun charity shop for a fourth year in a row.

“They always look forward to being in store to help raise money for such good causes.”

And this year our Sunta store — which opens next Wednesday — is going to be more amazing than ever after Amazon dropped off an incredible £5,000 worth of goodies.

David Freeman, General Manager at Amazon’s Development Centre in Scotland, said: “Amazon is thrilled to support the Scottish Sun’s Christmas Charity Shop for the second year in a row.

“The Sunta Store is always a fun event where shoppers can grab some great bargains and spread the magic of Christmas while raising money for good causes.

“And The Scottish Sun’s chosen charities, MND Scotland and Alzheimers Scotland, carry out important work for individuals and families across the country.

“We hope our donation of £5,000 worth of Amazon electronics, sports equipment and other gifts goes a long way to raising much needed funds to give people help and hope at this time of year.”

The incredible gifts include sought after electrical goodies including 4k Ultra HD Fire Sticks, Echo Show with Alexa, Echo Dots and new Fire TV Cube video streaming gizmos.

But Amazon is not the only company who have really stepped up to the plate for our Sunta store.

United Wholesalers donated £1,000 worth of chocolates and toys while Golden Casket Sweets also weighed in with toffees and other tasty treats.

Supermarkets Lidl and Co-op have supplied wine and food stuffs by donating Christmas hampers as did National Trust For Scotland.

And we have incredible Tombola prizes including a family ticket for Glasgow’s Flip Out which boasts 63,000 square feet of trampolines.

We have four carnival tickets for the Irn-Bru Festival and tickets for Scotland’s theme park M&D’s including for their carnival, crazy golf and Amazonia attractions.

Tennent’s have given us two tours of their world famous Wellpark Brewery.

And the SEC donated four tickets for their Snow White panto, which features Gary: Tank Commander and Two Doors Down favourite Doon Mackichan.

We have loads of fine dining on offer too including lunch for two at Halloumi Glasgow or Southside, dinner for two at Kilmurry & Co.

You can also win a Spanish feast at Rioja or afternoon tea for two at the Hilton.

Quiz will be also supplying the latest in high fashion, with scented Christmas favourites supplied by Shearer Candles and Duff Christmas Trees have helped decorate our store.

Aulds the Bakers will be supplying us with a Christmas cake decorated by a different celebrity each day and sold and Glasgow Distillery Company gave us a crate of their award-winning Makar Gin.

And not only did St Enoch Centre donate the shop space for our pop-up Sunta Store, they have also donated a £50 gift card to spend inside the mall.

Scottish Sun editor Alan Muir said: “Our Sunta store really does have something for everyone.
