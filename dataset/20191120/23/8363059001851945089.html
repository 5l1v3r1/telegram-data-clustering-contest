Tiny Goggles Turn Nintendo's R.O.B. Accessory Into the Worst Smartphone-Controlled Personal Assistant

20 Nov 2019, 23:00 by Andrew Liszewski

To help sell people on its new console after the 1983 video game crash, Nintendo branded the NES as a complete “Entertainment System”, which included a robotic accessory that could play along with gamers. Thirty years later, some hackers at Croxel have found a clever way to revive that long-forgotten accessory.

Nintendo’s claims about what R.O.B. (Robotic Operating Buddy) would bring to the console gaming worked, and the company’s efforts to rebrand its Japanese Famicom console for the Western market that had soured on video games was a huge success. R.O.B. was an effective trojan horse for getting the NES into Western households, but ultimately just two compatible games were ever made for it, and the accessory quickly gathered dust for most gamers, before being abandoned by Nintendo just a few years after its debut.

3D-printing R.O.B.’s fancy new specs. (Photo: Croxel)

R.O.B. is now more of a curiosity than anything; a fun retro collectible for nostalgic gamers because it relied on old-school CRT TVs to work, receiving signals that were rapidly flashed between screens of gameplay. Modern flat-screen TVs work differently, rendering accessories like R.O.B. and the NES light gun unusable, but instead of turning to outdated TV technology we’re all happy is long gone, the folks at Croxel fitted their still functional R.O.B. with a fancy pair of specs that have given him a new life.
