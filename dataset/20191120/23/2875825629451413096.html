Harry Redknapp admits he sent Daniel Levy cheeky text to replace Mauricio Pochettino

Former Spurs boss threw his hat in the ring for his old job back before Jose Mourinho was announced as Mauricio Pochettino's successor

20 Nov 2019, 23:30 by Irishmirror.ie

Harry Redknapp admitted he threw his hat into the ring to be the new Tottenham boss the day before Jose Mourinho replaced sacked Mauricio Pochettino as manager.

Mourinho was appointed as Tottenham manager early on Wednesday morning, less than 12 hours after Pochettino's five-and-a-half years in charge was brought to an end.

Redknapp, who managed Spurs from 2008 to 2012, said that after learning Pochettino had been sacked he expected the next man in charge to be Mourinho or Carlo Ancelotti.

But that didn't stop him sending Tottenham chairman Daniel Levy a cheeky text to ask for his old job back.

"I thought Mourinho, Ancelotti, who's it going to be?" Redknapp said on The Debate on Sky Sports.

"I text Daniel last night, I said 'I'm here Daniel, I'm just going for an Italian with my wife. A couple of hundred quid a week - I'm ready, I'm coming.

"He text me back and said 'You're a star. You're my special guest at a Tottenham home game.' I thought 'thanks Daniel.'"

Meanwhile Redknapp urged Mourinho to make Gareth Bale his first signing as Tottenham manager in the January transfer window.

Despite helping them deliver four Champions League titles and scoring over 100 goals Bale has never truly settled in Spain.

The 30-year-old continues to be linked with a move away from the Bernabeu, and Redknapp said a return to his former club could be the perfect move.

"It could be couldn't it, yeah," Redknapp told Ally McCoist on talkSPORT on Wednesday morning.

"What a signing he'd be for the club. What a signing. That would get him on side straight away, the fans would love that. That would be an amazing move for him."
