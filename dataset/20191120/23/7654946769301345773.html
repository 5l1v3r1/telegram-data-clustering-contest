‘Most’ Fed board members agree to wait on rates cuts

20 Nov 2019, 23:46 by Richard Morgan

Minutes from the Fed’s October policy meeting, which resulted in the third interest-rate cut this year, revealed that “most” members of the Federal Open Market Committee don’t see a need for additional rate cuts.

Fed officials voted at the meeting to cut their short-term benchmark to its current range between 1.5 percent and 1.75 percent. They later modified a post-meeting statement to play down prospects of a fourth rate cut in December.

“Most participants judged that the stance of policy, after a 25 basis point reduction at this meeting, would be well calibrated to support the outlook of moderate growth, a strong labor market, and inflation near the Committee’s symmetric 2 percent objective,” according to the minutes, released on Wednesday.

The minutes also noted that Fed officials were worried about weakness in manufacturing, trade and business investment when they cut interest rates last month. But they elected to take a wait-and-see attitude before addressing whether the economy needed more stimulus, the minutes said.

After meeting with the President on Monday, Federal Reserve Chairman Jerome Powell said he told Trump about his sense of comfort with current monetary policy — just as he did at Congressional hearings a week ago.

Trump, who has long pressed the Fed for more monetary easing, complained in a tweet after the meeting that “our Fed Rate is set too high relative to the interest rates of other competitor countries.”
