What Lille sporting director Luis Campos has said about reuniting with Jose Mourinho

Jose Mourinho has been named as Mauricio Pochettino's successor at Tottenham, signing a three-and-a-half-year deal with the club

20 Nov 2019, 21:55 by Matt Maltby, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/matt-maltby/

Jose Mourinho's former right-hand man, Luis Campos, is reportedly keen on linking up with the Portuguese manager at Tottenham .

Mourinho, 56, has been named as Mauricio Pochettino's successor, signing a three-and-a-half-year deal at the north London club.

The Portuguese, who has been out of work since losing his job at Manchester United last December, inherits a squad that are languishing in the bottom half of the Premier League, having taken 14 points from their 12 games.

Mourinho moved quickly to appoint his backroom staff, with Joao Sacramento and Nuno Santos joining from Lille as assistant manager and goalkeeping coach, respectively.

And now Sky Sports has claimed that Lille chief Campos, who was Mourinho's assistant manager at Real Madrid , is open to taking up a sporting director role with the Premier League club.

Rebecca Caplehorn is currently in the role of director of football, with chief scout Steve Hitchen and chairman Daniel Levy also taking a leading role in transfers.

But it was only last month that Campos opened up on a potential reunion with Mourinho.

He said: "Mourinho is like a brother to me.

"I know him very well and for a long time. He's a wonderful person and the best coach in the world. So, of course, if he called me I would speak.

"In my opinion - football needs someone like Jose. Football needs this special coach with this energy and strong personality. I believe the next work of Jose will be his top work."

Soon after Mourinho's appointment, Tottenham also confirmed the arrival of Carlos Lalin as fitness coach, Ricardo Formosinho as tactical analyst and Giovanni Cerra as technical analyst.

All three worked under Mourinho at Old Trafford.
