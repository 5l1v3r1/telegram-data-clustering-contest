Child Dies After Shooting At High School Football Game

20 Nov 2019, 21:43 by Andrew McCarty

Over the weekend, a truly terrifying scene unfolded during a high school football game in New Jersey. Three people were injured when gunshots were fired during a playoff game in Pleasantville.

According to a new report from ESPN, a young boy injured in the shooting has passed away from his injuries.

ESPN revealed a 10-year-old boy passed away just days after the shooting. A 15-year-old boy suffered a graze wound from the shooting. Six men have been charged – one of whom was also shot.

The 10-year-old boy, Micah Tennant, died five days after he lapsed into a coma.

From the report:

Authorities said Wednesday that a 10-year-old boy has died from injuries sustained in a shooting at a New Jersey high school football playoff game between Pleasantville and Camden on Friday night that also injured two others and suspended play.

The officials said that Micah Tennant died five days after lapsing into a coma after the shooting while being treated at Cooper University Hospital in Camden.

The game was stopped with 17 minutes remaining, but is expected to resume on Wednesday at 4:00 p.m. ET at Lincoln Financial Field. Camden led 6-0 when the shooting occurred.

According to ESPN’s report, the game will be closed to the public.
