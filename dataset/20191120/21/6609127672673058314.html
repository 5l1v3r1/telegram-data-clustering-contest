TICKETS PLEASE

Tartan Army could face tickets scramble if Scotland reach Euro 2020 play-off final

20 Nov 2019, 21:08 by Aidan Scott

SCOTLAND fans could face a scramble for tickets if they reach the Euro 2020 play-off final.

Steve Clarke's side are set to host the semi-final at Hampden in March against one of Bulgaria, Israel, Hungary or Romania.

Should they win on the night, they will then take on the winner of the game between Norway and Serbia for a place in next summer's finals.

However, it won't be known until the draw this Friday whether a potential final for Scotland would be played at home or away.

And that could lead to a scramble for tickets with the Tartan Army potentially only having 1,400 briefs available to them.

Uefa have confirmed to SunSport that as the play-offs are a continuation of the qualifiers, regular host nation rules apply.

That means only as little as five per cent of tickets need to be allocated to away supporters.

If Scotland reach the final and be drawn away against the winner of the clash between Norway and Serbia, this means as little as 1,400 briefs could be up for grabs.

That figure refers to the 28,000-capacity Ullevaal Stadion in Oslo where Norway play their home games.

If the Tartan Army were forced to travel to Serbia, they would have at least a slightly improved 2,777 tickets available to them at the 55,538-capacity Rajko Mitic Stadium in Belgrade.

Meanwhile, Russia could be banned from Euro 2020 over doping allegations - and Scotland could take their place.

Uefa haven't stated who would replace the world's largest nation at the tournament if they are banned.

But it is worth noting that Denmark famously profited from Yugoslavia not being allowed to participate at Euro 92 due to being in a state of civil war.

Like Scotland finishing third behind Russia in our qualifying group, the Danes also finished behind the former Balkan nation in their qualifiers that year.
