Wall Street falls on concerns of US-China trade deal delay

20 Nov 2019, 21:10

Wall Street edged lower on Wednesday, as hopes of a trade deal receded following an escalation in tensions between Washington and Beijing that also overshadowed upbeat forecasts from retailers Target and Lowe's.

NEW YORK: Wall Street's main indexes closed lower on Wednesday on concerns a "phase one" trade deal between Washington and Beijing may not be completed this year.

The Dow Jones Industrial Average fell 113.61 points, or 0.41per cent, to 27,820.41, the S&P 500 lost 11.78 points, or 0.38per cent, to 3,108.4 and the Nasdaq Composite dropped 43.93 points, or 0.51per cent, to 8,526.73.
