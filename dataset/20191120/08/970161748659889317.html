Driver fined £120 for pulling over while having diabetic episode

20 Nov 2019, 08:40

A diabetic driver has been handed a £120 fine after he pulled over when his blood sugar levels dropped.

Andy Lindsay, 52, suffered a hypoglycaemic attack during his journey, and so stopped at Calder Park Industrial State, next to the M1 in Wakefield.

His car was photographed and he received a £100 fine from Excel Parking a few days later in March this year.

Despite him submitting medical evidence the fine has gone up to £120 and he is now being threatened with court.

Andy’s wife, Bronwyn O’Brien, 47, said the firm have shown ‘no common sense’ in their handling of the issue.

She said: ‘We understand the fine, but what we don’t understand is that we have appealed it three times now and there seems to be no common sense.

‘Imagine if he had carried on driving and killed someone – I’m sure the family of that person would have asked, “why didn’t you pull over?”

‘I don’t think they would have understood if he had said, “well I didn’t want to get a fine”.’

Andy, a sales representative, was diagnosed with type one diabetes at the age of 11.

Those with the condition are advised by the DVLA not to drive when blood sugar levels drop below 5mmols (millimoles per litre).

Symptoms of hypoglycaemic attacks include shakiness, dizziness, drowsiness and confusion, making it potentially dangerous for sufferers to remain behind the wheel.

Bronwyn added: ‘If it had been a council parking ticket I’m sure they would have used common sense and let us off by now.

‘This company feels like it’s just a money-making scheme and nothing to do with parking or safety. He could have been dead at the wheel for all they cared.’

Excel Parking has been contacted for comment.
