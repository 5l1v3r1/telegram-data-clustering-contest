The Greg Taylor touch of class as Celtic star protects mascot from the big Hampden freeze

The left-back had a fine evening as Steve Clarke's side defeated Kazakhstan on Tuesday night.

20 Nov 2019, 08:10 by Liam Bryce, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/liam-bryce/

The absence of Andy Robertson and Kieran Tierney has been to undoubted benefit of Greg Taylor.

While the Celtic left-back wouldn't wish injury upon either of his international team-mates, it's allowed him to show what he's all about.

Not that Steve Clarke needs convincing, having worked with Taylor extensively at Kilmarnock.

The 22-year-old produced a fine display in the 2-1 win over Kazakhstan, bagging an assist for John McGinn's second.

But it was before kick-off he displayed a real touch of class.

On a freezing night at Hampden, mascots were left shivering as they lined up with the team for the national anthem.

One youngster looked particularly baltic and it didn't go unnoticed.

Taylor promptly removed his tracksuit top and passed it on to the delighted kid.

Having hardly featured under Neil Lennon since a summer move to Parkhead, Taylor is grateful for the minutes with Scotland.

And he hopes he's done enough to remain in Clarke's thoughts even when Premier League stars Tierney and Robertson return.

He said earlier this week: "In football, it's about taking opportunities and, when you least expect it, you get them.
