City to fund doctor housing

20 Nov 2019, 16:41 by Colin Dacre

The City of Penticton is putting up $30,000 to provide subsidized housing for locum doctors filling in at the hospital.

City councillors heard a presentation Tuesday from Dr. Brian Forzley and Dr. David Stoll, members of the Medical Staff Association at PRH.

The pair said housing has become one of the largest barriers to attracting physicians, particularly locums, who travel to fill in for doctors on leave for periods of a few weeks to a month.

Recruiting locums is usually the first step to bringing the doctors to the community on a permanent basis, said Dr. Stoll.

“It’s unusual to get a physician to come to a community without them actually be able to check out the site, work a little bit in the department, test the waters.”

Dr. Forzley noted Penticton is competing against other Interior Health hospitals for doctors. He used an example of a locum anesthetist last summer who tried to come to Penticton with her husband and three children but could not find a rental below $3,000 a month.

Instead, she took a position in Kamloops, which offers free housing for locums and doctors moving to the community. 

Council heard Penticton is currently short seven psychologists and the average age of doctors in the hospital is over 50.

The District of Summerland and Regional District of Okanagan Similkameen will both be approached for $5,000. The combined $40,000 will act as seed funding to secure three units of housing for locum doctors — one for a family and two smaller. Rent would still be collected, but the municipal funds will act as a subsidy for 3-5 years. 

Council voted 5-2 to accept the doctor’s proposal and take the funds out of this year’s budget.

The Town's of Oliver and Osoyoos put forward money earlier this year for a similar initiative at South Okanagan General Hospital.
