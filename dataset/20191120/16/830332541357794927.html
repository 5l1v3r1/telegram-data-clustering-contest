2 arrested for disorderly conduct while armed after bringing guns to 'fistfight,' police say

20 Nov 2019, 16:25 by Stephen Cohn

MADISON, Wis. - Two people were arrested on tentative charges of disorderly conduct while armed Tuesday night, Madison police said. 

According to an incident report, Larry T. Spinks, 34, and Lakisha M. Crawford, 31, were arrested after DeForest police officers and Dane County Sheriff's deputies performed a high-risk traffic stop in the DeForest area, where the suspects live. 

Officials said people arrived in three separate cars to the 4000 block of East Washington Avenue shortly before 8 p.m. 

The report said one car had a man and woman from Sun Prairie. They reportedly called police when they realized the other people involved brought two guns and a pipe to what they thought was a "fistfight," according to the report. 

Police said the two other cars drove away after police were called. 

Madison police said they searched Spknks' and Crawford's car and found two pipes and a loaded handgun. 

The driver of the other car, who the victims said also brought a gun to the fight, remains at large, according to the report.
