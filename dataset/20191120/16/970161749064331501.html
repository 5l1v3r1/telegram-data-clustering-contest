Jussie Smollett suing city of Chicago over hoax attack claims

20 Nov 2019, 16:08

Jussie Smollett is now suing the city of Chicago for malicious prosecution, after it was claimed he staged a fake racist and homophobic attack on himself earlier this year.

Back in January, the Empire actor claimed he was the victim of a racist and homophobic attack, alleging he was beaten by two men who put a noose around his neck and screamed racial slurs.

He was later charged with making a false police report after officials said the attack was staged, with brothers Abel and Ola Osundairo coming forward to claim he paid them to help fake his ordeal.

Cheques the 37-year-old made out to the pair later emerged, and he was charged with 16 felonies.

Despite being indicted by a grand jury, the State Attorney later dropped all charges.

Jussie has always pleaded his innocence, and has now accused officials and police officers in Chicago of a smear campaign against him.

According to Washington Post, the lawsuit states he was the victim of a malicious prosecution that caused him extreme distress and humiliation.

TMZ – who obtained the lawsuit – reports that it states the brothers only made the allegations once they were promised immunity.

He also insisted they were lured into a smear campaign by police, claiming his attacker was a white male.

Jussie also maintained the cheque he gave the Osundairos was for their services as personal trainers, helping him get into shape for a music video.

However, the City previously started legal action against Taraji P Henson’s co-star, suing him for the cost of the investigation.

Officials have requested $130,000 (£99k) to cover their funds, including the ‘overtime worked by more than two dozen detectives and officers’.

After he missed the deadline, the city is now requesting nearly $400,000 from him.

Metro.co.uk has contacted reps for Jussie for a comment.
