Bayern Munich linked with Troy Parrott move

20 Nov 2019, 16:24 by James Cox

Troy Parrott's form has not gone unnoticed and it'll be interesting to see if he gets any first-team involvement under new Tottenham manager Jose Mourinho.

The talented 17-year-old made his senior debut against Colchester United in the Carabao Cup earlier on this season while he's also featured on the bench in a number of Premier League games.

Parrott's international form should help his cause as he made his senior debut in the 3-1 win over New Zealand before a tidy finish in the Ireland Under-21s' 4-1 victory over Sweden last night.

Mourinho may even have some competition to hold on to Parrott.

German giants Bayern Munich are "monitoring his situation having noted his continual progress this season" according to a report in the Daily Mail.

Bayern's interest in Parrott reportedly stems from their desire to find a young player in the English market who can go on to become a key player, like Jadon Sancho did after joining Borussia Dortmund from Manchester City in 2017.

The fact that Parrott's deal expires in 2021 has added to Bayern's interest, as they eye a bargain deal.

Bayern made a number of attempts to sign Callum Hudson-Odoi in the last two windows before he eventually signed a new five-year deal at Stamford Bridge in September.

The report adds that Bayern are keeping tabs on Manchester United goalkeeper Dean Henderson (currently on loan at Sheffield United) who they see as a potential long-term successor to Manuel Neuer.
