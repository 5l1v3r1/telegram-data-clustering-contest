Rick and Morty season 4 UK release date, how to watch, episode titles and more

20 Nov 2019, 16:41

The first episode of the fourth season of hit animated sci-fi sitcom Rick and Morty is just hours away from dropping.

After two years of waiting, we’re finally getting season 4, and, with some great reviews under its belt, the hype is very real.

For anyone not in the know, the series follows a drunken and amoral genius as he goes on darkly comic adventures with his timid non-genius grandson.

Ahead of the series 4 premiere, here’s everything you need to know about the new season…

Rick and Morty UK release date

The first episode of Rick and Morty season 4 is coming out today, 20 November.

How to watch Rick and Morty season 4 in the UK

Advertisement Advertisement

Episode one of season four is kicking off at 10pm on E4.

The initial UK release date was set to be in January 2020, however fans did not take kindly to the delay.

This led to season 4’s new November premiere, with Ian Katz, Director of Programmes at Channel 4 saying: ‘When we announced we would be airing Rick and Morty Series 4 on free to air TV in January we thought fans would be delighted.

‘Instead they told us – in their droves – that it wasn’t nearly soon enough, so we’ve listened and brought forward the UK premiere to November 20th on E4 and All 4. Enjoy!’

Stream Rick and Morty online for free

Since the new season of Rick and Morty will be airing on E4, you’ll be able to watch episodes for free on Channel 4’s website after they air on TV.

Which Rick and Morty characters are back in season 4?

Judging by the trailer, season 4 will unsurprisingly feature the return of titular characters Rick and Morty, along with Summer, Beth and Jerry Smith.

In a throwback to season 1, a group of Mr Meeseeks can be seen, and Mr Poopybutthole, who first appeared in season 2, will be making a comeback too.

Gearhead, aka Revolio Clockberg Jr., who also appeared for the first time in season 1, can be seen briefly in the trailer.

The fourth series of Rick and Morty will be ten episodes long and split between two parts, with the first five episodes starting in November and the rest dropping in 2020.

Where to buy a Rick and Morty Christmas jumper

What with the new season landing just in time for the holiday season, you’ll no doubt be relieved to know that a variety of retailers sell Rick and Morty Christmas jumpers.

From Ebay and Geek Store to Amazon and Grindstore, there’s plenty of festive options to choose from.
