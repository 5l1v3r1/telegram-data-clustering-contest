Women Dominated the 2020 Grammy Award Nominations

20 Nov 2019, 16:17 by Nerisha Penrose

Lizzo isn't just 100 percent that bitch, she's also 100 percent a Grammy nominee—eight-time Grammy nominee, to be exact.

At the crack of dawn on Wednesday, November 20, the Recording Academy unveiled the nominees for the 2020 Grammy Awards. To the surprise of no one, women are dominating this year's noms, especially the four major categories: record of the year, album of the year, song of the year, and best new artist.

Let's start with ELLE's Women in Music cover stars, Lizzo and Billie Eilish, who are first-time Grammy nominees and are leading the pack with eight and six nominations respectively. Lizzo bagged nods in the Big Four categories for "Truth Hurts" (record of the year and song of the year), her album Cuz I Love You (album of the year), and is listed in the best new artist category. Eilish's "Bad Guy" was also nominated for record of the year and song of the year, and joins Lizzo in the best new artist lineup alongside fellow newcomers Rosalía, Yola, Maggie Rogers, Lil Nas X, and more.

Newbies aside, pop's most famous faces also returned to the race. Ariana Grande garnered five noms this year, including two in the major categories—record of the year and album of the year—as well as best pop solo performance for "7 Rings," best pop/duo performance for her "Boyfriend" collaboration with Social House, and best pop vocal album for Thank U, Next. H.E.R. also grabbed five nominations, including two in the major categories.

Beyonce is up next with four nominations, including best pop solo performance and best song written for visual for "Spirit." The Lion King: The Gift is up for best pop vocal album, and Homecoming, her music documentary about her 2018 Beychella performance at Coachella, is nominated for best music film.
