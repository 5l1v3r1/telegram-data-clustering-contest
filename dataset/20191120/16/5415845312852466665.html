Arsenal: Jose Mourinho reveals accepting irrelevance

The news of Jose Mourinho’s appointment at Tottenham is a shock, but it does serve as a reminder of Arsenal’s dwindling relevance and their acceptance of it.

20 Nov 2019, 16:00 by William Dodds

Daniel Levy decided to burn everything to the ground on Wednesday night when he appointed Jose Mourinho as Spurs manager almost immediately after disposing of Mauricio Pochettino.

While it seems weird that he would decide to replace one of the best managers in the world with someone that guarantees to have your club in ruins within three years, I am not going to argue too much with someone that seems intent on destroying Spurs from within.

However, what this move does signal is that Spurs are no longer satisfied with mediocrity. As soon as they fell down the table, regardless of whether it was with one of the best managers they have ever had or a mere Joe Bloggs, they took action and decided to make a change.

Now, the execution and timing was poor, and the man they have turned to is not the correct appointment either, but it highlights a cautionary air at Arsenal that is seeing them slip into mediocrity without any action to make a change. Sadly, under Unai Emery, they are losing relevance by the week. But what is even more concerning is that they are doing nothing about it.

With every season spent playing poor, indistinguishable and identity-less football, the less anyone around the world will care what they are doing. And that is even more troubling than losing matches. Apathy is more dangerous than criticism.
