Most Valuable African XI, Salah & Mane Feature

13 Nov 2019, 12:30 by SL International

The market values of African stars continue to rise as the continent's brightest talents leave their mark around the world. Here is Africa's most-valuable XI!

Click through the gallery above to see Africa's most valuable XI, according to Transfermarkt, in a 4-2-3-1 formation!

Ajax Amsterdam shot-stopper Andre Onana is the most valuable African goalkeeper in the world at present. The Cameroon international, who is an undisputed starter for the reigning Eredivisie champions, is valued at a reported €35 million (R574 million).

Onana is supported by a backline that includes countryman Joel Matip, who shares the same valuation, and Senegal defender Kalidou Koulibaly, who has a remarkable market value of €75 million (R1.2 billion).

The pair of centre-backs are flanked by €30 million-rated (R492 million) Morocco right-back Achraf Hakimi and Algeria left-back Faouzi Ghoulam, who is valued at €23 million (R377 million), although the latter's value is influenced by the short length of time remaining on his Napoli contract.

Guinea's Naby Keita and Ghana's Thomas Partey, who are both valued at €50 million (R820 million), make up a two-man midfield, while Mali's Moussa Marega takes up an advanced role. The Porto star is said to be worth €30 million.

Liverpool's duo of Sadio Mane and Mohamed Salah are the two most valuable players in the XI. Senegal star Mane features on the left wing at €120 million (R1.9 billion) while right-sided Salah is rated at €150 million (R2.4 billion).

Up front, Arsenal and Gabon striker Pierre-Emerick Aubameyang features as the lone striker with a reported market value of €70 million (R1.1 billion).
