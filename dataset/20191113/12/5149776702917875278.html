Man in court on video link as trial date is set over alleged murder of Daniel Mock in Bulwell

Shane Seymour made a 23-minute court appearance

13 Nov 2019, 12:15 by Rod Malcolm

A Bulwell man has made a 23-minute court appearance via video on a charge of the murder of neighbour Daniel Mock.

The charge was not read to Shane Seymour, 44, who was represented by QC Michael Auty at Nottingham Crown Court.

A trial has been set for January 13 with an estimate that it will last for five days.

Another preparation hearing will be held on December 12. Mr Auty told the court that he was working on a list of witnesses required for the trial.

Mary Loram QC, for the Crown Prosecution Service, said they were getting ready to hand case papers to the defence team.

The charge is that Mr Mock, 37, was murdered in Spruce Gardens, Bulwell, on August 2. Both men lived on the street.

Judge John Burgess told Seymour that barristers had been dealing with details of the case during the hearing.

He added: "We have discussed everything we need to and there will be another hearing on December 12."

Seymour, who wore a grey top and a ring in his left ear, appeared to have followed the hearing.

His only word was "yes" when asked to confirm his name.

It was his second appearance at the crown court. Before that he was brought before three magistrates in the city.
