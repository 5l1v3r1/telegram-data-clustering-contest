Lanzini, Noble, Wilshere - latest West Ham injury news and potential return dates

West Ham news | When could some of the Hammers injured stars be looking at a return?

13 Nov 2019, 17:00 by Sam Inkersole

West Ham manager Manuel Pellegrini's problems mounted prior to the international break as he lost two midfielders to injury during the 3-0 thumping at Burnley.

Both Manuel Lanzini and Mark Noble left the field, the latter in the first half and the former in injury time, to reduce the manager's options in midfield for the foreseeable future in the case of Lanzini.

The Argentine number ten has undergone surgery on a fractured collarbone on Monday and is expected to be out for around six to eight weeks which would rule him out for the rest of 2019.

Noble had an MRI scan on an ankle sprain earlier this week and it is hoped he will be fit for the visit of Spurs in ten days time though it might only be enough for the bench.

Jack Wilshere missed the Burnley defeat as well, his third game in a row he's been absent for, after picking up his injury prior to the 1-1 draw with Sheffield United.

Michail Antonio is expected to be fit for the Spurs game as he is due to return to training over the international break having been out since August after hamstring surgery.

Winston Reid meanwhile made his comeback for the Hammers U23s last midweek.

The Kiwi centre back played an hour of the U23s defeat to Wolfsburg B in the Premier League International Cup and has been called into New Zealand's squad for their friendly against Ireland this week.

Here is the latest injury news from the treatment room at Rush Green and some potential return dates.

Manuel Lanzini

Type of injury: Fractured collarbone

Date of injury: November 9

Potential return date: January 2020

Comments: Lanzini underwent surgery on Monday and is now in the recuperation stage from going under the knife. The average absence for this injury is around 60 days.

Mark Noble

Type of inury: Ankle sprain

Date of injury: November 9

Potential return date: November 23

Comment: "Mark twisted his ankle. We'll see how he is."

Jack Wilshere

Type of injury: Groin

Date of injury: October 25

Potential return date: November 23

Comments: "Jack has a small problem but he is not 100 per cent fit so I think, for him, it is a risk for him to come back before he has fully recovered.

Lukasz Fabianski

Type of injury: torn hip muscle

Date of injury: October 5

Potential return date: January 2020

Comments: "Lukasz had his surgery and now he must be patient, he needs some time to recover. You cannot do it faster than you need. As we said at the beginning, he will probably return next year."

Michail Antonio

Type of injury: hamstring tear

Date of injury: August 27

Potential return date: November 23

Comments: "The first time I did it, I did it at the bottom on my left, then I've done it at the top of my left, I've done the top of my right now Hopefully, I can stay fit, it's not the muscle I'm injuring, it's the tendons," Antonio said.

Winston Reid
