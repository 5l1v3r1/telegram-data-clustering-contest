'Different class' - Nottingham Forest fans all in agreement on their player of the year

Latest Nottingham Forest news | The Reds are in fifth place in the Sky Bet Championship going into the November international break - and there are a number of contenders for the star player of the campaign so far

13 Nov 2019, 17:00 by Mark Wakefield

We have reached the final international break of the calendar year, and Nottingham Forest finds themselves in a healthy position in the Sky Bet Championship.

The Reds are in fifth place in the league table following their win over arch rivals Derby County at the weekend, and are three points outside of the automatic promotion places with a game in hand.

Sabri Lamouchi’s side will have confidence going into the remainder of the campaign that they can maintain their form and mount a serious promotion challenge with Premier League football is real possibility.

Forest have played 18 matches so far this season – 15 in the league and three in the Carabao Cup – but which individual player has stood out above the rest.

We asked Reds fans who their player of the season so far would be, and the overwhelming majority went for the versatile Matty Cash.

The 22-year-old has been filling in at right-back for much of the campaign, with injuries to Tendayi Darikwa and Carl Jenkinson leaving Lamouchi short of options in that department.

Here is what Reds fans had to say about Cash’s performances this season, when asked who their player of the season would be.

Michael Guthrie: “Matty Cash, change of position and quickly becoming the best right back in the Championship.”

Joshua Booth: “Cash all day long made some important tackles this season and shown much improvement one of the best right backs in the championship.”

Mark Doughty: “It’s great to have so many worthy winners for a change - Samba, Cash, Worrall, Watson. I’d go for Matty Cash but by a whisker!”

Debra Smith: “Soooo tough!!!! I think I might have to stick my neck out and say Cash. He’s been consistently great and it’s a new position for him which makes it even better. So many good ones though; Samba (both), Watson, Dawson before injury, Worrall, Grabban, Ribeiro.”

Craig Leigh: “Cash for me. All the others are playing their natural position and doing a cracking job. But Cash has been a different class in his new role.”

Lee Tilston: “Very tough. Could make a fair case for at least 5 but for me it has to be Matty Cash. Kind of forced by injury to play a position alien to him - a little bit ropey defensively first game...how far has he come in just 10-12 games!? Outstanding at both ends of the pitch, consistent and looks like the quintessential modern full back.”

Andy Middleton: “Between Cash and Samba for me. Cash definitely most improved playing in a new position but can’t wait for his energy to be used further up the pitch later in the season.”

Eli Denton: “Jayden Bogle for me, that assist for Grabban on Saturday was sublime. But in reality I’d go with Matty Cash, great transformation.”
