Andy Ruiz Jr's diet ahead of heavyweight rematch against Anthony Joshua

Andy Ruiz Jr is set to trim down ahead of his second clash with Anthony Joshua on December 7 with a strict diet fuelling his training camp

13 Nov 2019, 17:27 by Rich Jones, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/rich-jones/

Andy Ruiz Jr is looking to arrive in the shape of his life when he takes on Anthony Joshua in their huge rematch in Saudi Arabia next month.

The world heavyweight champion, who stunned Joshua in New York in June, is already looking trimmed down as he aims to weigh in eight pounds lighter for their second bout.

He has already showed off his new, leaner physique after being ridiculed by some ahead of his stunning win over Joshua.

But a gruelling training camp is not the only thing behind his new look - there's also a strict diet ahead of the biggest fight of his life.

And his personal nutritionist, Zo's Meal Plans, told Sky Sports exactly what he is eating to ensure he is in the shape of his life on December 7.

Breakfast - Omelette with mushrooms, spinach and avocados, hash browns.

A big breakfast is needed to fuel Ruiz for the day ahead.

This is said to be Ruiz's typical breakfast, with the egg omelette providing high-quality protein as well as vitamin D, B6 and B12 plus minerals such as zinc, iron and copper.

The mushrooms offer good protein and fibre, with spinach adding iron to his diet to give him energy and avocados providing a healthy source of monounsaturated fats.

Lunch - Chicken Salad or Chicken Wrap

Chicken is a staple in Ruiz's diet, providing high protein, low fat and ideal muscle fuel.

Whether he eats the meat in a salad or a wrap depends on the day - but it's almost always Ruiz's lunchtime choice.

Dinner - Pasta

Ruiz prefers to catch up on his carbs in the evening to top-up his energy levels after a hard day of training.

Pasta is the perfect dish - but he has also taken a liking to Saudi Arabian food ahead of his 'Clash of the Dunes' fight, particularly their rice.

Ideal Meal - Steak

Given a choice, Ruiz reportedly goes for a steak whenever he gets the chance.

It isn't quite as healthy as chicken and fish, which he eats more regularly, but he does regularly go for leaner cuts of steak when he can.

Snack - Wraps, Sandwiches, Fruit, Nuts.

Ruiz tends to have a snack or two in between meals, and these vary from wraps and sandwiches to fruits and nuts to top up his daily quota.

If it is something he can travel with, then it can be a snack when Ruiz is out and about between meal times.

Cheat Meal - El Pastor con Queso Tacos

In a gruelling training camp, everyone is entitled to a cheat meal every once in a while.

So what is Ruiz's choice? According to his nutritionist, it's tacos - with cheese inside and melted with marinated pork.

The Destroyer is a big fan of the food from his Mexican roots as his choice of cheat meal shows.
