Joe Gomez scratch 'caused by Raheem Sterling's jewellery' in England canteen bust-up

FA reported to be considering Sterling misconduct charge after he was axed from England qualifier following row with Gomez

13 Nov 2019, 03:25 by Gareth Bicknell

The scratch sported by Joe Gomez following his England bust-up with Raheem Sterling was reportedly caused by the Manchester City man's jewellery.

The fallout from Monday's row continued on Tuesday when Liverpool defender Gomez turned up for England training with a scratch under his right eye.

The day before, Three Lions boss Gareth Southgate had axed Sterling from England's Euro 2020 qualifier against Montenegro on Thursday.

That came after Sterling reignited a row with Gomez which had started on the pitch in the closing stages of Liverpool's 3-1 win over City at Anfield on Sunday.

When England players reported for training at St George's park on Monday, Gomez is believed to have approached Sterling in the squad canteen.

Sterling said to Gomez "So you think you're the big man?" before reportedly trying to get the Liverpool defender in a headlock.

After Gomez turned up to training the following day with a scar under his eye, Southgate was asked at a press conference whether Sterling had caused the scratch.

The England manager would not divulge any details, but it appears the scratch was caused by jewellery Sterling was wearing, according to the Sun .

The two players reportedly had to be pulled apart by team-mates, sending food flying from tables.

The FA are reported to be considering hitting Sterling with a misconduct charge.

Sterling later apologised to Gomez and the rest of the England players at a squad meeting, and has stayed with them amid the possibility of returning to action in Sunday's qualifier against Kosovo.

Gomez had reportedly asked Southgate not to banish Sterling from the squad.

After the shameful incident, Sterling posted on social media to admit that emotions had got the better of him.

Defending Premier League champions City are now nine points behind Liverpool following their defeat to the leaders on Sunday.

Sterling said via Instagram : "We are in a sport where emotions run high and I am man enough to admit when emotions got the better of me.

“Both Joe and I have had words, figured things out and moved on.

“Me and Joe are good, we both understand it was a 5-10 second thing. It’s done, we move forward and not make this bigger than it is.

“Let’s get focus on our game on Thursday.”
