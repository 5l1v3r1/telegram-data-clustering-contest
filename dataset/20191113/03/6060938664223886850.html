Is Sourav Ganguly in conflict zone?

  • They also allege that this is a case of ambush marketing since Dream11 is an official IPL partner and Ganguly tweeted about a rival company.

INDORE: A few months after he was adjudged to be conflicted by BCCI ombudsman, Sourav Ganguly finds himself stuck on the same wicket. However, he is likely to emerge 'not out' on this occasion.

The former India captain is again being questioned in some quarters on the conflict-of-interest issue after he tweeted on My11circle, a fantasy cricket league which gives a chance to its users to win a reward for predicting the best playing eleven for a match.

His critics point out that a BCCI president promoting such a venture is unprecedented. They also allege that this is a case of ambush marketing since Dream11 is an official IPL partner and Ganguly tweeted about a rival company.

"You're bringing this issue to my knowledge for the first time. For the first time, you've a Board president who's a celebrity too. You can't compare his case to other presidents. After quitting cricket, he's been endorsing brands and earning his livelihood. We can't deprive him of that," BCCI treasurer Arun Thakur told TOI.

When told about Ganguly endorsing a rival product of the Board sponsor, Thakur said: "We'll have to see what commitment he made when signing that contract. You don't sign contracts in the anticipation that you'll become the BCCI president one day."

Ratnakar Shetty, the BCCI's former Game development manager, says that "ambush marketing' rules apply to the players, and not the officials," as per the Board constitution..

"Nike was the Indian team's sponsor for several years, and Sachin Tendulkar had a contract with Adidas. There was no conflict, because he had to disclose in the contract that he has a deal with Adidas."

On Monday, in Mumbai, Ganguly said he didn't see any conflict in his deal.
