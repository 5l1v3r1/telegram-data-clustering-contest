Hong Kong Open: PV Sindhu, HS Prannoy, Parupalli Kashyap advance; Saina Nehwal, Sameer Verma, Sai Praneeth ousted in first round

13 Nov 2019, 03:47 by Suhas Nayse

Highlights

  1. World Champion PV Sindhu took 36 minutes to see off world No. 19 Kim Ga Eun of Korea in the opening round
  2. The Olympic silver medallist will face Thailand's Busanan Ongbamrungphan next
  3. Saina Nehwal put up a fight a little too late against China's Cai Yan Yan and lost the match 13-21, 20-22 in just 30 minutes

NAGPUR: The second day of the Hong Kong Open BWF World Tour Super 500 badminton tournament turned out to be a mixed bag for the Indian shuttlers. While five singles players including PV Sindhu entered the second round, another first round defeat of Saina Nehwal puts a serious question mark on her future.

Qualifier Sourabh Verma continued his sparkling show to join HS Prannoy, Parupalli Kashyap and Kidambi Srikanth in the last 16. But it was curtains for B Sai Praneeth and Sameer Verma. Both lost in three tough games.

In a shocking result, the in-form men’s doubles pair of Satwiksairaj Rankireddy and Chirag Shetty squandered away a bright start and went down fighting against Takuro Hoki and Yugo Kobayashi of Japan 21-17, 16-21, 17-21. The world No. 7 Indian duo of Satwik and Chirag were surprised by world No. 14 Japanese pair in a marathon match which lasted for an hour and two minutes.

Overcoming her first round exit in the China Open last week, Sindhu carved out a clinical 21-15, 21-16 win against Kim Ga Eun. Sixth seed Sindhu needed just 36 minutes to get past world No. 19 Korean in their first ever meeting.

Sindhu began well and established a four-point cushion but Kim bounced back to take a slender 11-10 lead at the break. The tall Indian then reeled off six successive points to clinch the first game.

In the second game too, Sindhu was in control most of the time. A seven-point burst at the start helped her down her opponent.

The world champion will now take on Busanan Ongbamrungphan of Thailand, ranked 18th in the world for a spot in the last eight on Thursday. Sindhu has a perfect 10-0 head-to-head record against the Thai girl.

World No. 9 Saina came up with a far better fight than their last meeting in the China Open but goes down in straight games against Cai Yan Yan, world No. 22, in the first round.

In the men’s singles, Kashyap kept his 2020 Olympic Games chances alive by registering a brilliant win over world No. 12 Kenta Nishimoto. World No. 25 Kashyap knocked out Japanese 21-18, 16-21, 21-10 in their first meeting on the circuit.

Prannoy ousts China’s Huang Yu Xiang in straight games for his first win after returning from dengue. The former national champion will next face 6th seed Jonatan Christie of Indonesia.

In an all-India second round encounter, Srikanth will lock horns with Sourabh Verma, who shocked Frenchman Brice Leverdez 21-11, 21-15.

Indian Results (Round 1)

Men’s singles: HS Prannoy bt Huang Yu Xiang (China) 21-17, 21-17; Parupalli Kashyap bt Kenta Nishimoto (Japan) 21-18, 16-21, 21-10; Sourabh Verma bt Brice Leverdez (France) 21-11, 21-15; Kidambi Srikanth walkover 1-Kento Momota (Japan); B Sai Praneeth lost to 3-Shi Yu Qi (China) 21-11, 18-21, 12-21; Sameer Verma lost to Wang Tzu Wei (Chinese Taipei) 11-21, 21-13, 8-21.

Women’s singles: 6-PV Sindhu bt Kim Ga Eun (Korea) 21-15, 21-16; 8-Saina Nehwal lost to Cai Yan Yan (China) 13-21, 20-22.

Men’s doubles: Satwiksairaj Rankireddy-Chirag Shetty lost to Takuro Hoki-Yugo Kobayashi (Japan) 21-17, 16-21, 17-21.

Women’s doubles: Ashwini Ponnappa-N Sikki Reddy lost to Maiken Fruergaard-Sara Thygesen (Denmark) 13-21, 12-21.
