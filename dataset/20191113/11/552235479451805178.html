Scottish Parliament boost for bid to create Galloway National Park

MSPs have backed the creation of new national parks during a debate to mark the Forestry Commission's centenary

13 Nov 2019, 11:55 by Stephen Norris

Hopes that Galloway could host Scotland’s third national park have received a boost from Holyrood.

MSPs backed creating new national parks during a routine debate to mark the Forestry Commission’s centenary.

The Scottish Government had previously indicated no further national parks were planned for the country at this time.

The unexpected development came via an amendment to the forestry motion tabled by South of Scotland MSP Colin Smyth.

It stated: “Parliament ... recognises the contribution that national parks make to protecting forestry and widening the natural environment and therefore believes that new national parks should be designated.”

 

MSPs unanimously approved the proposition – raising hopes of a full government consultation on the Galloway plan.

Mr Smyth hoped the decision meant the government’s position was changing.

He said: “I also hope they are being convinced to come on board with the idea of new national parks in Scotland including here in Galloway.

“The Government now needs to listen to parliament and the public and work with the community to develop a plan for a Galloway National Park.

“The momentum behind the campaign for a Galloway national park is growing all the time.”

News of the move delighted GNPA president Barbara Kelly.

She said: “The Galloway National Park Association very much welcomes the agreement to Colin Smyth’s amendment.

 

“We recognise the vital role of forestry in the rural economy. But national parks have an equally important role to play.

“We need a feasibility study which would be a very welcome development.

“We look forward to discussing this further when we meet Mairi Gougeon later this month.”

Galloway and West Dumfries MSP Finlay Carson also welcomed the decision.

Speaking after the debate, he said: “The Scottish Parliament has backed the calls from myself, colleagues and campaigners.

“I believe the SNP Government now need to seriously look at the what the Galloway National Park campaigners need to do to trigger the consultation and feasibility study process.

“Today’s vote should reinforce the positive case for ensuring Galloway is the next designated national park.”
