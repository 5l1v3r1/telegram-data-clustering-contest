'Don't Compare Me To Ronaldo Yet'

13 Nov 2019, 11:25 by SL International

Real Madrid's latest wonderkid has called for calm from the media over recent comparisons to Los Blancos legend Cristiano Ronaldo.

Read: Ronaldo Facing Two-Year Ban?

Eighteen-year-old starlet Rodrygo has been making headlines in recent weeks after breaking into the first team at the Santiago Bernabeu.

The former Santos prodigy has already scored five goals in six appearances for the LaLiga giants, including a hat-trick in the UEFA Champions League against Galatasaray last week, and earned a call-up to the Brazil national team.

His form for Real has earned him comparisons to Ronaldo, with some even claiming he is the player to fill the void left behind by the club's all-time record goalscorer.

Rodrygo has since revealed that he would have loved to have played alongside the Portugal star, who moved to Juventus at the beginning of the 2018/19 campaign, but aaded that it is too soon to see him as the 34-year-old's replacement in Madrid.

"Being able to play alongside Cristiano Ronaldo at Real Madrid was a dream of mine and it's a shame that he left because now I think it would be very difficult to play with him," Rodrygo said, as reported by Marca.

"I am still a very young player to talk about filling the place left by someone like Cristiano Ronaldo, who is one of the greatest players in the history of the club – if not the greatest.

"I don't want to weigh myself down with the pressure of replacing Cristiano Ronaldo."
