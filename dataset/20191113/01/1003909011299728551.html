2020 budget: City tax scenarios include cuts to police and fire, loss of 236 jobs

13 Nov 2019, 01:28 by Meghan Potkins, Calgary Herald

City council got its first glimpse Tuesday of some big changes that could be in store for next year’s municipal budget.

Council initially approved a 3.03 per cent tax hike on homeowners in 2020 as part of the previously approved four-year budget — but council members have since signalled that they could be pushing for a budgetary freeze and cuts to services in the year ahead.

The budget debate comes as Calgary continues to grapple with the economic pain of depressed property values in the downtown and significant cuts in funding to municipalities from the new provincial government.

City staff revealed Tuesday that eliminating the 2020 tax increase entirely would have big implications for already stretched municipal budgets.

A zero-per-cent tax increase would mean monthly savings of $5.10 for the average home, but would result in an $8.45-million cut to police budgets and the elimination of 236 full-time jobs at the city, and potential further job losses for the Calgary Police Service. The job-reduction figures include the elimination of vacant positions as well as layoffs, city staff clarified Tuesday.

“The biggest challenge with zero is that it means a pretty big cut to the police service,” said Mayor Naheed Nenshi. “There’s no other way to do it. You can’t rely on the other services to keep covering for the largest service. And so council really has to (decide) are they more wedded to the zero or are they more wedded to protecting the police service.”

Under the zero-tax-increase scenario, there would also be a $6.7-million cut to fire and emergency response budgets, resulting in the postponement of a new fire station in the north-central neighbourhood of Livingston; the cut to fire would entail the temporary cancellation of a fire engine and 20 new firefighters to service Calgary’s growing suburbs.
