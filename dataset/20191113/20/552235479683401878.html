Cadbury urgently recalls Dairy Milk Little Robins over allergy fears

Customers with nut allergies have been told not to eat the sweet treat.

13 Nov 2019, 20:40 by Rachel Pugh, Kathleen Speirs

Cadbury has urgently recalled a Christmas product over allergy fears.

Some bags of Cadbury Dairy Milk Little Robins, have been filled with the Daim version of the sweet treat.

The Daim chocolates may contain almonds, whereas the original Robins do not.

 

This has posed a health risk for nut allergy sufferers, as packet could contain almonds, but does not say so on the label.

Cadbury are now warning those with nut allergies to avoid the recalled products, Manchester Evening News reports.

The recall applies to 93g bags with a best before date of 31 March 2020.

 

The manufacturer has warned that the mistake might not be immediately obvious because both products are wrapped in similar foil.

Any customer who has bought the product and has a nut allergy is advised not to eat it, and to return it to the store from where it was bought for a full refund, or to contact the Mondelez consumer care line on 0800 818181. 

No other Cadbury products are affected.
