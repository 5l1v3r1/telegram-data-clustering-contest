Former Wales coach Warren Gatland opens up on heartbreak of daughter's death aged just four months

Ex-Lions boss Gatland and wife Trudi were forced to make the gut-wrenching decision of "letting nature take its course" when daughter Shauna was born with spina bifida

13 Nov 2019, 10:11 by James Whaling, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/james-whaling/

Former Wales boss Warren Gatland has opened up on the heartbreaking death of his first daughter, aged just four months.

Gatland's wife Trudi gave birth to baby Shauna in Ireland in January 1992 - but she was tragically born with spina bifida.

Wales boss Gatland reveals in his upcoming autobiography Pride and Passion that he and Trudi were given an "awful dilemma" to either subject Shauna to a series of long and gruelling operations - or to "let nature take its course".

“When Shauna was born, in the early hours of 17 January 1992, our world changed,” he said.

“Whatever problems she had didn’t register with me at the moment of birth. But there were tears in the eyes of the nursing staff.

“The nurses knew deep down her condition was serious and an immediate scan confirmed the worst. We were devastated."

Gatland said of the heartbreaking decision he and his wife had to make: "That was the hardest, most gut-wrenching conversation of my life.

 

“Even if the medical side went to plan, even if she fought like a lion to survive, what kind of life would be on offer to her?” said Gatland.

Gatland and Trudi opted to take the "kinder" approach and Shauna died on May 13 1992.

They moved back to New Zealand following the birth but Gatland admits "profound and lasting" regret at being in Australia with the Waikato provincial side when Shauna died.

Gatland has two other children - daughter Gabby and rugby-playing son Bryn - of whom he is immensely proud.

The 56-year-old became Wales coach in 2007 and was Lions boss for their tours in 2013 and 2017.
