Weirdest requests made by Nottingham hotel guests revealed

'Can you arrange for me to meet David Attenborough at Attenborough Nature Reserve?'

13 Nov 2019, 14:52 by Rachel Gorman

If you've ever felt naughty pocketing the toiletries following a hotel stay, or worried it was too cheeky to ask the reception for a tea and biscuits top up then stop!

Instead, console yourself with the knowledge that this year one Travelodge guest asked staff if they could arrange a meet-and-greet with David Attenborough at Attenborough Nature Reserve.

The quirky request is one of 10, revealed by the hotel chain, as some of the weirdest its Nottingham staff have dealt with over the last 12 months.

Others include:

"Where can I meet Robin Hood?"

"Can you arrange for a white horse to take my son to his wedding?"

"Can you call our Shanghai office and pretend to be me as they think I can speak Mandarin?"

"Will I see the golden goose at the Nottingham Goose Fair?".

Travelodge spokeswoman, Shakila Ahmed, says there has also been a spike in guests asking staff for help with wedding proposals.

"One customer asked for a unicorn pedal boat and for a rainbow to appear above the hotel at 4.16pm for a wedding proposal at Thurrock Travelodge," she says.

"The team at Rhyl Travelodge were asked to set up a romantic candlelit dinner on Rhyl beach with a path of red rose petals from the hotel to the table.

"At Leamington Spa Travelodge the hotel team were tasked to get a full moon outside the customer’s room window in order to create a moonlight setting.”

The hotel chain annually welcomes around 19 million customers across 570 hotels, including three in Nottingham.

Shakila says: "Our hotel teams receive thousands of interesting requests from business and leisure guests. Where possible, our hotel teams will go above and beyond to help customers as they relish a good challenge.

"However there are some requests beyond their control, such as getting a raft of ducks to go to sleep, stopping the traffic on the M5, getting the Northern Lights to make an appearance and getting a herd of sheep as a sleep aid.”
