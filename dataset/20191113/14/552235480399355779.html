Our Lady and St Patrick's top in West Dunbartonshire school tables

The school was ranked 98th in Scotland above both Dumbarton Academy and Vale of Leven Academy.

13 Nov 2019, 14:31 by Lorraine Weir

The school league tables have been published showing Our Lady and St Patrick’s High as the highest rated school in the region.

Ranked as 98th in the area, 41 per cent of students achieved five or more Highers.

Dumbarton Academy came in 199th place with 31 per cent of students achieving five or more Highers.

 

And the Vale of Leven Academy was ranked 284th out of 339 schools in the country. Some 23 per cent of students got five or more Highers.

When the exam results were received in August, students in West Dunbartonshire were celebrating after achieving their best ever National Five exam results.

Schools in the region are above the national average with a local pass rate of 81.63 percent for National Five.

 

Of those who sat exams, 99 per cent achieved one or more qualifications, and of the 925 sitting Highers, 84 per cent achieved one or more passes and of the 159 Advanced Higher pupils, 78 per cent achieved one or more passes — an increase of two percent on last year’s results.
