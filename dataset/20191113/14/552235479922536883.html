West Lothian taekwon-do stars strike gold at the World Championships

Three members of Howden School of Taekwon-do in Livingston scooped top spot on the international stage.

13 Nov 2019, 14:23 by Callum Carson, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/callum-carson/

Three members of Howden School of Taekwon-do proved they could mix it with the best by scooping gold medals on the international stage.

Morgan McQuade, Blair Nelson and Callum Schooler, all from Livingston, were in fine form at the ITF Taekwon-do World Championships at Ravenscraig.

The event boasted more than 20 competing nations and over 600 competitors, with the Livi trio all taking home gold medals.

Morgan clinched two golds and a silver, which also saw her pick up the overall top competitor award for the 15-17 age category.

 

She progressed through several rounds to win her hard-earned silver medal in patterns before comprehensively winning the gold in sparring with a fine victory in the final.

Morgan scooped a second gold as she won the special technique discipline kicking a high target.

Blair matched his team-mate with a pair of gold medals while also adding a bronze to his tally, which saw him crowned overall top competitor in the 10-13 age category.

He clinched gold in the sparring division before sealing another gold for the special technique discipline hitting a high target.

He showed tremendous focus in the patterns to pick up a bronze medal to complete his remarkable tally.

 

Callum made sure it was a trio of golden success as he grabbed top spot in his sparring division.

David Lupton, 6th degree black belt instructor at Howden, said: “Our young champions have been training now for over seven years and have been supported by each other and their families.

“They have all shown great dedication and always give 100 per cent effort in training.

“Senior coach Andrew McQuade and I are immensely proud of the students and can see a bright future for them.

“Hopefully they will represent Scotland in major championships in the future.”

He added: “The club won five gold medals, one silver medal and one bronze medal in the first World Championships for the young students. It was a fantastic achievement.”
