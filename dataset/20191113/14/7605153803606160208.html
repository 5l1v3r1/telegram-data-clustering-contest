KISUMU, Kenya, Nov 13 – Transport Principal Secretary Esther Koimett Wednesday announced the modernization of Kisumu port was now complete paving way for the commissioning of the facility by President Kenyatta.

Renovation works cost the taxpayer Sh3 billion.

ADVERTISEMENT

Koimett who led colleague PSs who are members of a national technical committee charged with overseeing the regeneration of the lake port in touring the port said works at the port are complete.

“We have toured the port and I can say the renovation works are now complete and a dry run has already been done,” she said.

Speaking to journalists in Kisumu, Koimett said the technical committee will now notify President Uhuru Kenyatta on the project’s completion.

PSs who accompanied Koimett included Mwangi Maringa (Infrastructure), Joseph Njoroge (Energy), Gordon Kilahangwa (Public Works) and John Omenge (Mining).

The national government carried out the rehabilitation of the port to open up trade between Kenya and neighboring countries.

Kisumu will Thursday host a blue economy conference, where the discussion on sustainable exploitation of Lake Victoria as a shared resource among East African countries is expected to dominate.

Nyanza Regional Commissioner James Kianda announced that plans are also underway to put up a market to accommodate traders in a bid to spur economic growth in the region.

He said a marketplace will be set up in a 23-acre parcel of land owned by Kenya Railways after it was recovered from a private developer.

Kianda said that the county government has been directed to relocate traders affected by recent demolitions to a temporary location awaiting the completion of the new market.

“What we have told the county government is that we have a relocation place where they have done some structures, in the next one week we have agreed with the county government that those traders are going to be relocated there temporarily,” he said.
