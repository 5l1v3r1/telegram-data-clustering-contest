Rangers fan saves dad's life after cardiac arrest behind the wheel on way home from Ibrox

Karl Zimmerman spoke of the CPR battle to save his ill father and praised the "amazing" ambulance service.

13 Nov 2019, 14:29 by Alan McEwen

A Rangers fan saved the life of his father when he suffered a cardiac arrest after leaving a game at Ibrox .

Karl Zimmerman used CPR on stricken 64-year-old after pulling him from their crashed car.

The 29-year-old had to crawl over Carl to free him as the vehicle had accelerated towards another car on a Glasgow roadside.

With guidance from an ambulance call taker, Karl began giving CPR in a desperate bid to save him.

Karl said: “I brought him back once and I will never forget it. He took a breath, and then I just kept going and going.”

Doctors later warned Carl’s devastated family that he had only a six per cent chance of survival.

But the grandad pulled through and now Karl has passed on his thanks to the Scottish Ambulance Service for their “amazing” support.

 

Karl, a joiner from the city’s Govan, said: “We’d just been to see Rangers beat Livingston at Ibrox and were driving away from the ground.

“It was crazy, pure luck – by the grace of God, his front headlight was out, he had pulled over and I was checking it.

“When I came back he was unconscious at the wheel.

“It was an automatic, and the car just started rolling into another car. I managed to turn the wheel and pull it into a hedge.

“I then climbed over him and dragged him out of the car. I was a bit panicked.”

Karl, who had learned first aid at work, started CPR. He added: “It was all a bit of a blur, instinct just kicked in. The 999 call taker talked me through it." 

Ambulance call taker Celeste Hill was put on loudspeaker on Karl’s phone while a couple passing by stopped to help relay instructions.

 

Karl said: “They helped me as I continued doing CPR. The whole thing seemed like a minute, and then the ambulance response arrived. They were brilliant. 

“I could not believe how calm they were. I thought it was the most incredible thing I have ever seen – the ambulance response right through until the aftercare. 

“I only did what I had to do. I don’t think it was heroic. 

“If I hadn’t had the call taker speaking to me, I would’ve gone into panic mode.

“I knew I had to do something. I knew he was not going to just wake up. Once the ambulance arrived, they took over, and took him into the ambulance.”

Carl, also from Govan, praised his son’s actions.

He said: “He was fantastic. The work of everyone was fantastic.

“I cannot even remember the game to be honest. I cannot remember leaving Ibrox and I cannot remember watching the game.

“Next time I woke up was five days later.”

After months of recovery following the incident last November, the father and son met one of the attending crew members based at Kilmarnock Ambulance Station.

Along with Karl’s daughter, Eva, eight, and Carl’s wife Christine, they thanked Martin Moore in person. 

Technician Martin said: “This incident puts into perspective the importance of good call handling, nearby resources and most importantly bystander CPR.”
