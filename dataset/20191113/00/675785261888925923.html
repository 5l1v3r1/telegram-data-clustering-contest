Inside Royal Caribbean's Allure of the Seas and its epic £128million makeover

Royal Caribbean's Allure of the Seas is getting a £128million makeover with heaps of epic features including a water park, laser tag and new dining options

13 Nov 2019, 00:01 by Julie Delahaye, https://www.facebook.com/juliedjourno

Royal Caribbean's mega cruise ship Allure of the Seas is getting a seriously glamorous update in 2020 ahead of her return in May, and it's sure to be a hit with families.

Once the biggest cruise ship in the world, Allure of the Seas will undergo an epic $165million makeover (approximately £128million) which includes heaps of thrilling features.

We're talking a karaoke bar, glow-in-the-dark laser tag, a space-themed escape room, a rock wall, wave simulator FlowRider, a zipline and plenty of new dining offerings.

However, what's sure to be a hit with families is the water park on board that has everything from splash pools to thrilling racer slides.

In fact, those looking for an adrenaline rush will want to take on the Ultimate Abyss Slide which, at 10 storeys high, is the world's tallest slide at sea!

Have a bit of a competitive streak? Then challenge your family and head to the Perfect Storm slides, where you'll find two racer slides - Typhoon and Cyclone - sure to get the heart pumping.

For something a bit different, Supercell will see you propelled by the water as you spin down the huge champagne bowl!

Little ones who aren't quite ready to take on the daring rides can explore Splashaway Bay which offers splash pools, fountains, and drench buckets (not to mention mischievous kids are sure to love the water cannons).

It's not just the entertainment on board Allure of the Seas which will be amplified; foodies will be spoiled for choice thanks to the plethora of dining options available on the ship following its new look.

Highlights include El Loco Fresh where you'll find mouthwatering Mexican dishes from tacos to burritos, as well as Porkside BBQ where you can tuck into tender slices of brisket, pulled pork and juicy turkey legs.

Meanwhile, kids are sure to be big fans of the huge range of ice creams and sweets on the menu at Sugar Beach!

The fun doesn't stop once dinner is over as the ship also offers heaps of evening entertainment across its bars and lounges.

For a memorable night out make sure to check out the new Bionic Bar where the robot bartenders will whip up your favourite cocktails!

Allure of the Seas will also boast an opulent Music Hall where you can find everything from live music and shows, to fun nights where you yourself can take to the dancefloor.

Oh, and sports fans haven't been forgotten; the Playmakers Sports Bar & Arcade will show the biggest games on its huge TV screens, not to mention it will offer a wide array of bar snacks and drinks.

There's a lot to explore, but there does come a point where you need to rest up and luckily Allure's cabins are ideal as they offer heaps of comfort, space and elegance.

There's everything from cosy interior staterooms to breathtaking balcony cabins, as well as lavish suites offering up whole new levels of luxury.

 

Want to book a stay?

Allure of the Seas will be returning from May 2020 when she will embark on a series of glamorous Mediterranean itineraries, with ports of call including Palma de Mallorca, Marseille, Florence, Rome, Naples and Barcelona.
