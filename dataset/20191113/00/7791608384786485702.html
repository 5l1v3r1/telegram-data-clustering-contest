Disney+ Subscribers Are Now Discovering ‘Racist’ Overtones In Old Disney Films

13 Nov 2019 by Mikelle Leow

Disney has launched Disney+, its answer to streaming platforms like Netflix and Hulu. Subscribers are treated to an entire archive called the Disney Vault that not only includes all-time favorites like Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs and Cinderella, but also features 80 years worth of movies, toons and television shows produced by Walt Disney Co.
As it turns out, not every film is as kid-friendly as expected, at least for today’s standards. Some of Disney’s older movies have been discovered to feature “racist” imagery, and the company is fully aware of this. It’s inserted historical notes into programs that are likely to be deemed insensitive and problematic today.
Even Walt Disney’s grandniece Abigail Disney wrote in a 2014 Facebook post that she had “mixed feelings” about her famous relative. “Anti-Semite? Check. Misogynist? OF COURSE!” she described of her granduncle. “Racist? C’mon, he made a film [The Jungle Book] about how you should stay ‘with your own kind’ at the height of the fight over segregation!”
However, she admitted that the entertainment mogul “was hella good at making films, and his work has made billions of people happy. There’s no denying it.”
If you don’t own a Disney+ subscription yet, simply rewatch classics like Dumbo, Lady and the Tramp, The Jungle Book and Peter Pan to have a grasp of the less politically-correct nuances that had flown over your head as a child.
According to Mic, in 1941’s Dumbo, a flock of crows can be seen singing When I See an Elephant Fly; this scene has been interpreted to represent Black minstrelsy. To top things off, the lead singer’s name is Jim Crow.
