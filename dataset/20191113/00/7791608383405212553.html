PrettyLittleThing’s Little Mix Range Gets Backlash For ‘Oriental’ Fashion Items

13 Nov 2019 by Yimin Huang

PrettyLittleThing’s Little Mix collection has been widely criticized for cultural appropriation as its outfits loosely mimic the traditional qipao from China. Some items have even been described as “oriental” by the official website.
Many of the outfits, such as the red crop top, and skirts and dresses in a variety of colors, have been made sexier than the traditional garments with higher slit. Each Little Mix member is seen wearing an outfit of a different color, namely red, green, pink and shimmering black.
There are also bags, sandals and boots designed similarly to match the skirts and dresses.
Many Twitter users voiced out their objection to the brand, with one user Emma Kent pointing out that the brand is trying to profit off traditional costumes and make them sexy and “trendy.”
A Twitter account called “Stop appropriating my culture” specifically goes after incidents of cultural appropriation, and has tagged @LittleMix and @OfficialPLT to its post denouncing the clothing range. The post calls the design and “oriental” description as a “reduction” of Chinese culture to “exoticism” and reinforces the objectification of East Asian women.
Another Twitter user tuquynh also wrote a lengthy thread explaining how “Asian-inspired” clothing contributes to the “fetishization” of Asian women.
So far, the collection is still available on the PrettyLittleThing website.
