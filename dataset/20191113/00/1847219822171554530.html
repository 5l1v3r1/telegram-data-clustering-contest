Minimal pensions to increase in Latvia next year

13 Nov 2019 by BNN

On Wednesday, 13 November, Latvia’s Saeima approved in the final reading amendments to the State Pensions Law. These amendments provide for increasing minimal retirement pension amounts in 2020.

Law amendments are included in the package of the country’s budget for 2020, as confirmed by Saeima’s press-service.

Amendments provide for using a new calculation base set by the government to calculate minimal pensions. In general cases it will be EUR 80. For people suffering from disabilities since childhood this amount will be EUR 122.69. The Cabinet of Ministers will set minimal pension amounts, as well as payment and re-calculation order.

Until now minimal pension amounts were set using state social insurance benefit amount – EUR 64.03, as well as EUR 106.72 for people suffering from disability since childhood.

Retirement pension’s minimal amount will be applied with a specific coefficient depending on the person’s work experience.

Minimal retirement pension increase will apply to 20.5 thousand people, and it is planned the allocated pension amount will be increased by an average of nearly EUR 7, and it will be necessary to allocate EUR 1.8 million for this, as mentioned in the annotation.

Information from the State Social Insurance Agency shows that in Q2 2019 retirement pensions were paid to 449 000 people.
