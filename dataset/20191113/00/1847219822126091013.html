Netherlands planning lower speed limits to tackle pollution

13 Nov 2019 by BNN

In the Netherlands, the government seeks to considerably reduce the emissions of nitrogen oxide by reducing daytime speed limit on roads from 130 to 100 km/h, leaked government plans to the press say.

British public broadcaster BBC reports that details of the leaked measures are to be revealed on Wednesday, November 13, but reports say the existing limit of up to 130km/h would still be permitted at night from 2020.

Why is it needed?

The Dutch nitrogen oxide pollution crisis is so severe that big infrastructure projects have been put on hold. A ruling in May by the top court in the Netherlands on nitrogen oxide emissions affected thousands of plans for roads, housing and airports.

The Council of State said Dutch rules for granting building and farming permits breached EU law protecting nature from emissions such as ammonia and nitrous oxide.

The government wants to build 75,000 homes in 2020, so for the past week the cabinet has tried to find a solution to cutting the pollutants.
