Airbnb’s First-Ever Royal Listing Lets You Stay At A 300-Year-Old City Palace

13 Nov 2019 by Thanussha Priyah

Now, everyone can feel like royalty with Airbnb’s latest ritzy listing. The homestay company is offering its first royal property at the heart of Rajasthan, India. The 300-year-old City Palace of Jaipur is being offered up by the city’s royal family for US$7,983 nightly.
The monument was built by Jaipur’s royal founder Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II in 1727, and it has since been graced by illustrious guests including Princess Diana, Oprah Winfrey, Bill Clinton and Jackie Kennedy.
The chateau once exclusive to royalties and their special guests will now open its majestic doors to Airbnb bookings from 23 November 2019 onwards.
Maharaja Padmanabh Singh, the 21-year-old monarch of Jaipur, will play host to travelers from all over the world. Though India is now a democratic republic, the family still holds its significance in Jaipur’s history and traditions.
Maharaja Padmanabh Singh shared that he is “thrilled” to “bring the splendor of Rajasthan to life” for guests from around the world.
The Gudliya Suite comes with everything one needs—lounge, kitchen, bedroom and bathroom—and more, such as a private indoor swimming pool and your very own butler.
The royal treatment kicks off right when guests arrive at the airport. With a chauffeured car, visitors will make their way to the City Palace, where a private butler will assist to usher them in.
A staffer from the palace will guide a tour for the guests to learn more about the abode’s architecture, art and décor.
They can also indulge in Rajasthan’s authentic cuisine for breakfast and dinner by the royal residence’s in-house restaurant, Baradari.
From the terrace, visitors will be treated to breathtaking views of the Pink City, known for its terracotta textures dusted with rustic pink and red velvet-like architecture.

If the grandiose dwelling isn’t enough, guests can enjoy the curated lineup of city excursions, museum tours and shopping spots.
Airbnb will donate the proceeds from the mansion’s bookings to Princess Diya Kumari Foundation, a nonprofit organization, that aids underprivileged communities in Rajasthan, according to CNN.
