Things to do today in Bengaluru

13 Nov 2019, 00:30 by Bangalore Mirror Bureau

All for anime
That Bengalureans love Japanese pop culture, particularly anime, is an open secret. Take the case of this group, which meets once in a while to watch an anime movie together. Today, it will screen a horror and mystery film, the name of which will be revealed to you only if you contact them. What are you waiting for?

WHERE: Dialogues Café, 100 Feet Road, 4th Block, Koramangala
WHEN: 7.30pm-9.30pm, November 13
ENTRY: `250, eventshigh.com

The extinction theory
Catch the screening of A Prayer for Compassion, a documentary that explores the spiritual side of veganism. After the screening, Dr Sailesh Rao, founder of a non-profit dedicated towards healing the climate, will talk about climate change, biodiversity loss, ecosystems collapse, and chemical pollution and other factors that he believes will cause the premature extinction of Earth.

WHERE: Justbe Cafe, Sadashivanagar
WHEN: 5pm, November 13 CALL: 48538672

The makers’ club
Pop-Arc workshop is ideal for those who love tinkering with design and fabrication tools. Meant for students from class of 8 to 12 or those involved in the fields of industrial design and architecture, participants will learn to use CNC technologies, 3D printing, and industrial robots. The workshop will be conducted by the faculties of the Southern California Institute of Architecture.

WHERE: Workbench Projects, Halasuru Metro Station WHEN: 9.30am-3pm, November 13
ENTRY: `5,900; pay at the venue CALL: 9036001411

She’s got you covered

WHERE: Kazé Bar & Kitchen, Lavelle Road, Ashok Nagar

Grab your tickets
FIVESIXZERO, Bengaluru’s very own dance music fest, is coming up. To be held in association with Bangalore Mirror, the three-day event will explore aspects of space, history, communities, talent, uprisings and more, all through the lens of the music industry at a summit, and then at a big concert. The concert is divided into three categories – the ‘Main Stage’ is dedicated to commercial music and will be headlined by Krewella and Nucleya, ‘De:Vision – The Underground Stage’ will cover the underground side of techno, deep-house and tech-house music and will be headlined by Sal O Cin, and the third one, called ‘Homegrown Stage’, will feature the famous DJs of Bengaluru. The fest is an initiative of Times Red Cell.

WHERE: The Lalit Ashok, Kumarakrupa Road WHEN: November 22-24
DETAILS: fivesixzero.in
TICKETS: bookmyshow.com

Connect the dots

WHERE: Art Studio & Gallery, 15th Cross, 6th Main, Sterling Sharda Nivas
WHEN: 2.30pm onwards, November 13
ENTRY: `1,620, insider.in

The life and times of a dancer
Indian Music Experience will open a special show this week. Called Credo of a Dancer: An in-depth exhibition on the life and art of T Balasaraswati, the show will feature photographs and press clippings, along with costumes, ornaments and other personal collectibles of the legendary Bharatanatyam dancer. It will also provide insights about the socio-economic context of her time, all of which shaped her artistic expression.
The inaugural day will host a dance performance by Aniruddha Knight, grandson of Balasaraswati. The following day, a panel discussion and a series of short performances in connection with the exhibition, will be presented by the World Dance Alliance, Karnataka Chapter. The panel discussion will be chaired by dance scholar Nandini Ramani, and will feature dance gurus Veena Murthy Vijay and Kiran Subramanyam and dance critic Jyothi Raghuram. Eminent dancers Lakshmi Gopalaswamy, Praveen Kumar, Shama Krishna and Anuradha Vikranth will also perform.

WHERE: Indian Music Experience, Brigade Millenium, JP Nagar WHEN: 5pm, November 16-17; the exhibition will continue till December 1 (closed on Mondays) ENTRY: Free
