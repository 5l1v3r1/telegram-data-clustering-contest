Elenger starts supplying Latvia with natural gas from Klaipeda terminal

13 Nov 2019 by BNN

Estonian Eesti Gaas subsidiary SIA EG Energija, which operates under Elenger brand, has commenced supplying Latvia with gas from Klaipeda floating gas terminal, as reported by Elenger Latvija board member Dāvis Skulte.

The company has signed a contract with Lithuania’s company Klaipedos nafta on gas supplies and transit to the gas network from Klaipeda floating terminal ship Independence.

«By commencing supplies from Klaipeda floating terminal, we increase resource variety and independence for our customers. For us this means access to the global market and four our clients – access to better prices, because there are more supply sources,» comments Dāvis Skulte.

So far the company has procured some gas from Klaipeda liquefied natural gas terminal and has transported it to clients in Estonia and Latvia using specialized cargo trucks.

The objective of the new contract is gasifying the procured liquefied gas and diverting it to the network so that it is possible to deliver the gas via pipelines.

The contract for supplies for small amounts of liquefied gas using Klaipeda terminal was signed at the end of 2017. Over the course of two years, Lithuania has procured 2 600 tonnes of liquefied natural gas.

The company supplies natural gas and liquefied natural gas from Russia, Latvia, Finland and Poland.
