Some SGX-listed companies to watch as Hong Kong unrest escalates

13 Nov 2019, 09:48 by Goola Warden

SINGAPORE (Nov 13): Alarming news that a Christmas tree was set on fire at Festival Walk caused unit price in Mapletree North Asia Commercial Trust to fall. Festival Walk contributed S$104.9 million or 61.7% to MNACT’s net property income of S$169.8 million in 1HFY2020 the six months to Sept 30. On Nov 13, MNACT’s manager announced that the mall has been closed for operations since 2pm on Nov 12 amid city-wide disruptions and the manager will update on developments. Since Nov 7, MNACT’s unit price is down 8%.

Sister real estate investment trust Mapletree Logistics Trust’s Hong Kong properties contributed 23.4% to 2QFY2020 (three months to Sept 30) gross revenue of S$127.5 million. Logistics properties are not very visible.

However, Bloomberg carried a news item that nine OCBC Wing Hang branches were closed on the morning of Nov 13 because of traffic disruptions. OCBC Wing Hang contributes 9% to Oversea-Chinese Banking Corp’s operating profit for the nine months to Sept 30.
