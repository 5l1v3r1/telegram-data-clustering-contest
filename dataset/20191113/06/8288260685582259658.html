Stella McCartney's Meghan Markle post on Remembrance Sunday blasted over being 'disrespectful'

Stella, who is the daughter of Paul McCartney, posted an image of Meghan, who is married to Prince Harry, at this year's Remembrance Sunday service

13 Nov 2019, 06:57 by James Rodger, https://www.facebook.com/jamesrodgerjournalist

Stella McCartney has sparked a fierce backlash for an 'insensitive' Meghan Markle Remembrance Sunday post.

The British designer took to social media to share a photograph of the Duchess of Sussex.

Stella, who is the daughter of Paul McCartney, posted an image of Meghan, who is married to Prince Harry, at this year's Remembrance Sunday service.

In the photograph, Meghan - who gave birth to baby Archie earlier this year - was seen wearing a matching navy woolen coat and hat ensemble.

Stella captioned the image: "So honoured to have  HRH Duchess of Sussex in our Autumn '19 coat at Remembrance Day service. x Stella #RemembranceDay."

Instagram users subsequently slammed Stella over her "disrespectful" choice of words.

"Using remembrance day to advertise your clothes is a little disrespectful!! People died and you take pictures of people showing respect for advertising purposes. Just wrong," said one critic.

A second added: "Remembrance day is to honour those that fought for us not to use for advertising purposes. Pretty shocked."

"Nice - but not really a time to be advertising your clothes, Stella," wrote a third.

 

Stella is yet to issue a comment on the backlash it caused.

The image has been removed from Instagram.
