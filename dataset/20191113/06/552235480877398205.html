Hibs manager favourites profiled as Paul Kane gives his verdict on the top candidates

The Hibs legend gives his thoughts on the men who could be king at Easter Road.

13 Nov 2019, 06:00 by Scott Burns, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/scott-burns/

Hibs have upped their search for a new head coach this week with Jack Ross and John Kennedy at the forefront of Easter Road thoughts.

A lot of big names from Pep Guardiola to Neil Lennon have had their say on the Hibs managerial search in recent days.

The legendary Manchester City boss rang Easter Road sporting director Graeme Mathie to recommend Spanish coach Raul Caneda Perez for the vacant position.

Celtic boss Neil Lennon also issued a firm hands-off message to his former club when they were linked with a move for his No. 2 John Kennedy.

So Record Sport has asked Hibs legend and life-long supporter Paul Kane to give his thoughts on some of the main runners and riders who are in the mix to succeed Paul Heckingbottom.

JACK ROSS

“Jack knows the Scottish game.

“He has played up here and managed St Mirren before he went down to Sunderland.

“Jack will know what you need to be successful up here and that is what we need.

“We can’t afford to appoint another Heckingbottom who comes up and misjudges the standard of the Scottish game.

“We don’t have time to do that again because we need to get this team up and running quickly so we can start pulling away from danger.

“Jack has come back up from Sunderland where it didn’t really work out the way he had hoped and he be looking to get back on track.”

NEIL WARNOCK

“For me, the way Neil Warnock’s teams play football would work against him.

“His football is pretty direct and defensive and he is not going to change his style football now he is coming to the end of his car

“Right or wrong, the Hibs fans demand their team play in a certain style and that comes from the 1960 and 70s.

“There is a demand to entertainment and that was shown when we won the Scottish Cup under Alan Stubbs or came back into the Premiership with Neil Lennon.

“We had good attacking teams that not only excited but were also successful!”

DANIEL STENDEL

“The suggestion is that they have already interviewed the former Barnsley manager, Stendel.

“In fairness, you are going to have to look at all the markets because Hibs is a big job.

“You have to listen and talk to all the main candidates but the position we are in at the minute I think we need to go with someone who is experienced in the Scottish game.”

RAUL CANEDA PEREZ

“He doesn’t have any real experience of the British game.

“I know Pep Guardiola has put him forward but it would be too much of a step into the unknown for me.

“He might be an experienced coach but it is too much of a risk for me.”

JOHN KENNEDY

“John has been at Celtic for a lot of years as a player, coach and assistant manager.

“Does he always want to be classed as a No.2 or does he want to become his own man?

“Maybe he is at that stage just now. “He has had 10 years coaching and has a lot of experience.

“I would imagine that if he has an ambition to become a future Celtic manager then he will need to cut his teeth somewhere else to become that manager down the line.

“I don’t know John at all.

“I can only go with what other people say.

“I am friendly with Scott Brown and he can’t speak any more highly of him.

“I have never heard anyone say a bad word about John.

“We all saw the winning mentality that Neil Lennon brought to Hibs and I would think Kennedy will instil something similar if Hibs were able to get him.”

STATEMENT OF INTENT

“I had initially put my hat on John Hughes but he is not even getting an interview, so what do I know I about football!

“I would say that from all the guys that have been mentioned it is a two-horse race between John Kennedy and Jack Ross.

“I would probably edge towards Kennedy. “If you were able to get Kennedy away from Celtic I think that would show some ambition.

“It is also the first big appointment of the new owner and I am sure he will want to put his stamp on things.

“People outside and the Hibs fans will be looking for some statement of intent and if they could get Kennedy then I think that would make everybody sit up and take notice.

“Unless the board pull a rabbit out of the hat and make another appointment that has gone under the radar!”
