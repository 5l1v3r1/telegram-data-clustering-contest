Fred explains how Ole Gunnar Solskjaer has finally got the best out of him for Man Utd

The Brazilian midfielder had a tough start to life at Old Trafford but his performances have seen a huge improvement in recent weeks

13 Nov 2019, 18:13 by Alex Milne

Fred has praised manager Ole Gunnar Solskjaer for giving him the confidence to play his best football after a difficult start to his Manchester United career.

The 26-year-old signed from Shakhtar Donetsk for £52m in 2018, and much was expected of the central midfielder.

He struggled to get in the first-team under Jose Mourinho, however, and only managed to make 17 Premier League appearances last season.

During the current campaign he has been much-improved, and put in a brilliant performance during United's 3-1 win against Brighton on Sunday.

Speaking to The Athletic , Fred credited Solskjaer for his upturn in form.

"The trust from the manager is important and I feel I have this," he said.

"I just want to continue doing what I’m doing, playing well and starting the games — but I want to score, too. I came close (against Brighton) and hit the bar but I’ll keep working until this happens.

"I’m very happy, firstly because we won but also because we’re getting some rhythm and we’re doing well in the cups.

"But we needed to do better in the league. We just have to carry on fighting so that we reach the Champions League."

Fred said he is the happiest he has been since moving to United from Ukraine, and thinks a run of consecutive games has helped him get into his rhythm.

He added: "I’m feeling better than I’ve ever felt since moving to England. All I ever wanted was to play regularly and now that I’m doing that, there’s no doubt my confidence is higher and that helps any player."

Fred will be looking to help United continue their recent fine form when they take on Sheffield United after the international break on November 24.
