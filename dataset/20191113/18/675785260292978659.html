Gylfi Sigurdsson issues rallying cry to Everton teammates after win against Southampton

The Toffees have climbed up to 15th in the table after two positive results and next face bottom club Norwich ahead of a tricky run of fixtures in December

13 Nov 2019, 18:06 by David Anderson, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/david-anderson/

Gylfi Sigurdsson claims it is time for Everton to climb the Premier League table.

Everton have risen out of the drop zone to 15th on the back of their mini-revival and Sigurdsson claims they must build on this momentum ahead of their next game against Norwich.

Norwich visit Goodison Park on Saturday week and Daniel Farke’s Canaries have the worst away record in the Premier League with just one point from six games.

Sigurdsson, 30, was heartened by Everton’s positive results in their last two games before the international break and says they must keep going.

“It’s extremely important to build from the Southampton game going forward,” said the Icelandic star.

“It was a strong showing to get the equaliser against Tottenham, followed by three points against Southampton.

“There are some tough games ahead of us. We have Norwich at home next and then four very tough games on the way.

“There’s not much to choose between many of the clubs.

“It’s time for us to start climbing up the table.”

Everton are only three points behind fifth-placed Sheffield United and Sigurdsson claims they could shoot up the table on the back of a run of wins.

“There are only three points between ourselves and fifth place, which is really amazing considering how things have been with us,” said the playmaker.

“The league is such that if you put together three results in a row you will jump up four or five places.

“There are lots of games ahead and it's a fun time when there is little time for training but plenty of games.”

Pressure had mounted on manager Marco Silva after they lost four Premier League games in a row, but the Toffees are now unbeaten in their last three in all competitions.
