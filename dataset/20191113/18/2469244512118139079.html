Burglar jailed after two pensioners found him in their bungalows

He had been out of prison for only a few weeks after a sentence for an Exeter burglary where he left his false teeth at the scene

13 Nov 2019, 18:07 by Ted Davenport

A burglar has been jailed after he was caught inside the bungalows of two pensioners in a seaside town during the middle of the night.

Matthew Jackman came face to face with an 89-year-old grandmother and an 80-year-old man after breaking into their homes in Seaton, East Devon, on the same night in September.

He is a serial burglar with a history of targeting elderly victims, who used a coal bin and a water butt to reach windows which he tried to climb into open windows.

He had the effrontery to advise one of the victims to lock her doors after she found him inside her home. In the other raid, he pretended he was looking for a woman called Emma after coming face to face with the pensioner.

His excuse did not fool the householder who chased him down the road and tried to recover a cash tin which Jackman had snatched from a windowsill. He refused to hand it over but gave him £2 and begged him not to call the police.

Jackman was identified by DNA from the burglaries. He has an appalling criminal record of 139 offences including 20 previous dwelling house break-ins.

He had been out of prison for only a few weeks after being released half way through a four year, six months sentence for a burglary in Exeter where he was caught because he left his false teeth at the scene.

Jackman, aged 57, of no fixed address, admitted two burglaries and was jailed for four years and eight months by Judge Peter Johnson at Exeter Crown Court.

He told him:"You have many convictions for dwelling house burglaries and were last sentenced in July 2017. You had been released on licence when you committed these burglaries in Seaton.

"They were at night, when you were heavily in drink, and on each occasion the occupier has been confronted by you."

Mr Robert Yates, prosecuting, said the first offence happened at around 11 pm on September 14 when the 89-year-old woman heard someone in her bungalow in Townsend Road.

She thought it may be her grandson visiting her but instead found Jackman who told her 'Sorry, I'll come back tomorrow. Make sure you lock your doors."

He struck again 45 minutes later at a bungalow about 100 metres away at Newlands Park, where the 80-year-old was in bed.

He heard a house outside and noticed a moneybox containing £14 was missing from his window ledge and that a water butt had been moved to under the window.

There was a ring on his door and he saw Jackman, who said he was 'looking for Emma'. The owner spotted the cash box and chased him, at which point Jackman handed back £2 of his money and pleaded with him not to call the police.

He was tracked down and linked to the break ins by DNA. He told police he had drunk a bottle of gin and a bottle of wine on the night and he had little memory of what he had done.

Mr Lee Bremridge, defending, said Jackman is a different person when he is not drinking or taking drugs and is now studying creative writing and has started to write a book.

He said:"On this occasion, he had lost his accommodation and was drinking, that is when he got things wrong. When things go right, he is a very different character."
