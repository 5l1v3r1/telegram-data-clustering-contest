'Overwhelming': Veterans food bank swamped with donations after plea

13 Nov 2019, 23:43 by Bill Kaufmann

A plea for donations to avert a crisis has been answered “amazingly” by the public, say operators of a Calgary veterans’ food bank.

Last week, those who run the Veterans’ Association Food Bank said their shelves were becoming bare, with only enough supplies to last into the New Year.

Demand, they said, had recently skyrocketed among military veteran clients.

But that request for help resonated with donors who were probably also inspired by Remembrance Day, they said.

“It’s been absolutely overwhelming, the response we’ve received, we’ve gotten calls from Edmonton,” said Mike Nelson, a military veteran who works at the charity.

“We were here late last night, we had four pallets of food that flooded our warehouse . . . it’ll see us through for a bit.”

Nelson said the food bank was receiving the kinds of items most in demand, such as canned fruit, meat and seafood.

A GoFundMe page purportedly set up to raise money for the cause reached its goal of $10,000 in six days.

However, food bank founder Marie Blackburn said she hasn’t been in contact with anyone operating a GoFundMe page.

But she said the days immediately following media coverage of the food bank’s needs brought a huge surge in donations.

They’ve also taken in a considerable amount of cash, she said, that’s used to keep the charity running and to assist veterans in their homes.

“We’ve spent a lot of money to prevent homelessness and suicide,” said Blackburn.

One cash donor, she said, was a 13-year-old who brought in $1,300 from collecting neighbours’ cans and bottles.

“He said, ‘I’ve got to help the soldiers,’ ” said Blackburn.

Many of those donating are other veterans, she said, which is consistent with the mandate of the food bank, at 820 28th St. N.E.

Meanwhile, the Calgary Food Bank said demand for its services is continuing to increase and is up by nearly nine per cent over last year.

On Wednesday, the 31st annual Mayor’s Christmas Food Drive kicked off to collect donations for the Calgary Food Bank in a campaign that runs through Dec. 20.

“We wish we could say that there was no need for a food bank and that there was no hunger in Calgary,” said food bank CEO James McAra.

“Demand is still at an all-time high and we know first-hand that those accessing us for food support are still reeling from the Alberta downturn.”
