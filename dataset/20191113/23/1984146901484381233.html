Angry pest threatened to kill ex and her new man after they announced relationship on Facebook

Storm Simpson, of South Shields, has been jailed after leaving voice messages saying 'you are dead' and flouting a restraining order

13 Nov 2019, 23:00 by Rob Kennedy

A new couple received death threats from an ex-partner after announcing their relationship on Facebook.

Storm Simpson had already flouted a court order aimed at keeping him away from his former girlfriend when he left voice messages warning her and her new man "you are dead".

A court heard the woman believed her 39-year-old ex would carry out his threats and she was left feeling frightened and "on edge".

Now Simpson, of Marshall Wallis Road, South Shields, has been jailed for 12 months at Newcastle Crown Court.

In a victim impact statement, his ex said: "He has made threats to kill my partner and me and I honestly believe he his capable of carrying out the threats.

"He was angry. I fear he will come to my home and harm me and my partner. I am living in fear.

"I am scared by the threats he made. They have left me distressed and on edge. I don't want any contact from him."

The court heard Simpson was given a restraining order in May after he smashed a Virgin Media box worth £110 at his ex's home.

Since then he had been convicted four times of breaching it by turning up at her house or speaking to her in the street.

Just a month before the death threats he had been given a suspended prison term as a result of the repeated breaches. 

Simpson admitted two charges of making a threat to kill and one of breach of a restraining order.

Recorder David Kelly told him: "It is clear you have found it difficult to accept the end of the relationship and you have repeatedly breached the restraining order.

"I have concluded, I am afraid, this breach, set against the background of previous breaches, is so serious only a custodial sentence can be justified."

Liam O'Brien, defending, said Simpson, who has been employed as a cleaner while in custody on remand, has worked well and completed courses to try and help him stay out of trouble in future.

Mr O'Brien added: "This is clearly a man who has deficits in his thinking skills.

"He doesn't want to go out and keep breaching the order. He doesn't think it's a good idea to do so but he just can't stop himself."
