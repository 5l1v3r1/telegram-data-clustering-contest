Grieving mum of stabbed son begged him to open his eyes when she viewed his body

Tina Moss lost her 30-year-old son Ronald Portz when he was stabbed in the chest in a house in Hull last Saturday

13 Nov 2019, 13:47 by Hannah Robinson, Lorraine King

The distraught mother of a dad-of-three who was stabbed to death said she begged him to open his eyes when she went to view his body.

Tina Moss has spoken out about her devastation after Ronald Portz was knifed in the chest in Hull last Saturday.

Ms Ross tole Hull Live: “I just can’t believe this has happened. I’m heartbroken.

“Ronald’s brother died when he was six weeks old, so now I have lost two of my children.”

One of nine children, the 30-year-old has been described as a “big brother” to his brothers and sisters.

 

“I went to see his body yesterday and I was begging him to open his eyes,” said Ms Moss said.

“I want him to come home. I just want Ronald back.

"He was a lovely son.

“He did a lot for me. Two years ago I had a brain operation and he was by my side the entire time.

 

“He would always come and see me, and he looked out for all of my kids. If any harm came to them he sorted it all out.

“He came out of prison a couple of weeks ago, after being inside for 14 days, and he rang me and said, ‘Mam, I’m out’.

“I was laughing at him and joked when his next holiday would be. That was the last time I got to speak with him properly.”

Ms Moss celebrated her 50th birthday three days before Ronald died and he left her a voicemail wishing her a happy birthday, but she said she cannot bring herself to listen to it.

“I was watching a DVD of him last night acting about and being silly,” Ms Moss said.

“He would dress up like a woman when we were at parties and make everyone laugh.

"He always had the biggest grin on his face.

“That’s why we’re having him cremated and I want his ashes with me. I want him to be near me all the time.”

Sally Godwin, who Mr Portz had children with, has been charged with murder.
