UK weather forecast: Snow blast of up to 5cm to hit amid 'danger to life' floods

The Met Office has issued a rain warning for south-west England and south Wales today, with snowfall possible in these areas

13 Nov 2019, 05:49 by Matthew Dresch, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/matthew-dresch/

Up to 5cm of snow could fall in parts of the UK today as rain and sleet ravage the country.

The Met Office has issued a rain weather warning for south Wales and parts of south-west England, with snowfall possible in these regions today.

The weather alert, which is in force from 5pm to midnight tonight, states that these areas may see flooding today.

Another 'danger to life' flooding warning, covering much of England and Wales, has been issued for tomorrow, when almost half a month's rainfall is expected. 

The Environment Agency has 35 flood warnings and 80 less serious flood alerts in force today.

Met Office forecaster Mark Wilson told Mirror Online: "It will be a cold day later in the afternoon to evening, with a band of rain in Wales and south-west England which could bring snow, particularly in south Wales.

 

"At around 400 metres or above we could see up to five centimetres of snow, in places like the Brecon Beacons.

"There's less of a risk in the south-west, there could be sleet and wet snow over the moors.

"We also have rain warnings through Wednesday and Thursday. Locally some spots through Thursday could see 60mm of rain. That could lead to flooding."

Mr Wilson added that snowfall is also possible on Thursday and Friday on higher ground, particularly in the north.

Tonight there will be cloud and patchy rain across Northern Ireland, Wales, southern and south-west England, along with dry spells in Scotland, northern and eastern England.

 

Through the night it will become wet across southern areas but dry, clear and frosty in the north.

Up to 60mm of rain is expected in certain parts of the UK tomorrow, almost half the 121mm total average for November in the UK.

The Met Office has warned of 'danger to life flooding' and the prospect of communities being cut off by the water.

Tomorrow will be cold but dry with spells of sunshine in Scotland, Northern Ireland and northern England.

 

Wales, central and southern England will be mostly cloudy and there could be outbreaks of rain.

Wintry showers are expected in northern and eastern Scotland and the far north-east of England.

Friday is forecast to be grey and overcast with patchy rain and drizzle in England and Wales.

Scotland and Northern Ireland will be cold but dry with sunny spells.

Saturday will be cloudy with a light rain for many although it will be bright initially in the far north-west.

Met Office five-day weather forecast

Today:

Cold start for Scotland. Showers over some northern and western coasts but many places dry with some sunny spells. Southwestern parts seeing more widespread rain during the afternoon with some gusty winds, particularly near coasts. Staying cold.

Tonight:

Rain over south Wales, with hill snow possible here, and southern England. Showers feeding onto north and east coasts of England, Scotland and Northern Ireland. Less cold than Tuesday night.

Thursday:

Further rain for southern and southeast England, particularly southeast Wales and into Somerset, Bristol and Wiltshire. Showers further north, mainly for coasts exposed to the strengthening northeasterly wind. Remaining cold.

Outlook for Friday to Sunday:

Friday, bright in the northwest with a few showers, but cloudier with more frequent showers towards the southeast. Over the weekend showers becoming more isolated with many places dry. Cold.
