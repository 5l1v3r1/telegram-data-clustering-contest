What’s in store at Circuit night market

13 Nov 2019, 19:45 by Manila Standard Lifestyle

This year’s night market at Circuit Makati, happening on Nov. 15-17, will feature homegrown delights ideal for holiday gifts.  The Regional District features unique, locally produced products and delicacies from across Luzon, Visayas, and Mindanao. Shoppers can score classic and flavored coffee beans, an assortment of snacks and spreads, leather goods and proudly Marikina-made shoes, among other local specialties.

Meanwhile, the Wandergram-led Lifestyle District delivers a gifting option for everyone on the list: from sustainable, zero-waste presents for that environmentalist best friend to skincare and makeup finds for everyone’s favorite beauty junkie.  At Celebrity District, organized in partnership with children’s book author and celebrity mom Tin Tin Babao, guests can shop celebrity-made and -owned items from up to 30 merchants including Diana Zubiri, Miriam Quiambao, Tippy Dos Santos, Nene Tamayo, and Yana Asistio, among others.   Meanwhile, the Student District is a joint effort with the UP Organization of Business Economics Majors to support student entrepreneurs in their business ventures, such as baked goods, artisanal soaps, and other one-of-a-kind, gift-worthy products.

Over at the Cuisine District, up to 30 merchants will be serving up delectable dishes, mouthwatering snacks, and refreshing beverages. Organized by Mercato Centrale, guests can expect a curated selection of sumptuous Filipino and international food choices from home-based vendors.  Those who are gifted with a green thumb may consider dropping by the Green District. Organized in partnership with the Landscape Management Group and Ayala Property Management Corporation, it will offer more than just succulents and houseplants (although there will be plenty of those, too). 

Several performances await shoppers and those looking to hang out with friends and loved ones. Every evening, from 5:30 to 11:00 p.m., the Globe Circuit Events Grounds will set the stage for CineCircuit outdoor movie screenings, contemporary arts and dance performances, live chorale performances, and sets by some of OPM’s finest—indie band Munimuni on Nov. 15, Inadayo on Nov. 16, and Lola Amour on Nov. 17.  Shoppers and other guests can enjoy taking photos at the Interactive Wall Mural along Symphony Drive, which was painted by artist Nini Andrada Sacro with the help of artistic Makatizens.  Reg Yuson’s artwork located by the water along Circuit Lane will be unveiled soon.
