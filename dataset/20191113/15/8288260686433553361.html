Repairmen fixing burst water main forced to abort - after being attacked by rampaging pig

The raging hog, called Pickle, believed to be a pet, was startled to find engineers in his field earlier today (Weds), and charged them - before protecting the damaged valve

13 Nov 2019, 15:57 by James Rodger, https://www.facebook.com/jamesrodgerjournalist

Repairmen working on fixing a burst water main in London had to do a runner when they were attacked - by an angry PIG.

The raging hog, called Pickle, believed to be a pet, was startled to find engineers in his field earlier today (Weds), and charged them - before protecting the damaged valve.

Thames Water officials were able to coax the pig away from the valve with a bag of crisps, so both the farm animal and engineers would be safe.

The pipe burst in Surbiton, southwest London, damaged nearby railway equipment and caused train delays.

A Thames Water spokesperson said their efforts to reach a valve to cut the water were hindered by "a large pig" which was "acting aggressively".

They added: "We’re sorry to anyone who has had a longer journey than normal because of the road closure.

“This is due to a burst on a 48-inch diameter pipe.

"Our engineers were quickly on site and are working hard to fix the pipe.”

The spokesperson added there would be no water outages for customers in the area or flooding to properties.
