Morrisons issues urgent scam warning that will expose your bank details

The fake message offers customers £40 worth of vouchers

13 Nov 2019, 15:04 by Rachel Pugh, Jamie Hawkins

Supermarket Morrisons has issued an urgent warning to shoppers about a scam which asks for personal detials.

Scammers have sent emails, texts and social media posts posing as the company in a bid to shoppers' details.

The fake message offers customers £40 worth of vouchers, Manchester Evening News reports.

The warning shared by Morrisons  reads: "We are aware of a number of spam emails, text messages and social media posts from third-parties posing as Morrisons for the purpose of fraudulent activity.

"If you receive one of these suspicious messages, please do not click any links, open attachments or enter personal information.

"We would never ask for your personal details in order to redeem a voucher.

"If you believe you have been contacted please contact us on '0345 611 6111' or call Action Fraud on '0300 123 2040'.

"You can also visit 'www.actionfruad.police.uk' to report fraudulent activity to the authorities."
