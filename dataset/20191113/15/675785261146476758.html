Where is The Crown filmed? Netflix drama's UK filming locations you can visit

Netflix's The Crown has some brilliant UK filming locations from manor houses to picturesque estates you can visit - we take a look at the filming spots used in all three series

13 Nov 2019, 15:56 by Julie Delahaye, https://www.facebook.com/juliedjourno

Netflix's The Crown is back for a third season, with Olivia Colman taking the reins from Claire Foy to portray the Queen, and Tobias Menzies taking over from Matt Smith as Prince Philip.

As well as the gripping drama on screen, the show offers up seriously breathtaking filming locations from opulent palaces to picture-perfect gardens and awe-inspiring landmarks.

The best part is that the show is filmed on location in the UK, meaning you can go and see most of them for yourself.

It's not actually filmed in Buckingham Palace - the real Queen lives there after all - but the manor houses and British estates are just as spectacular.

New locations for season three include De Vere Horsley Estate, while previous locations feature including Hatfield House, Knebworth House and Belvoir Cathedral.

We take a look at The Crown's filming locations across the UK from all three series so far...

Oh, and for more royal-inspired days out, you can also visit the Queen's royal residences throughout the year.

1. Lancaster House

While a lot of the series takes place in Buckingham Palace, the cast and crew have previously used Lancaster House to double up as the iconic landmark.

With its lavish interiors and grandiose state rooms it's a fitting alternative (not to mention it's just further down London's Pall Mall).

Other spots included the 16th century Wilton House in Wiltshire, as well as Knebworth House in Stevenage.

2. Hatfield House, Hertfordshire

Hatfield House has served as a filming location across all three seasons.

The Jacobean stately home boasts opulent halls and rooms so it's the perfect setting for recreating lavish palace interiors (it also served as a filming location for The Favourite).

It boasts plenty of royal links - Elizabeth I was told her of her ascension to the throne.

Hatfield House is open to the public from April through to September. 2019 prices included adult tickets from £19 and child tickets from £9. You can find out more on the Hatfield House website.

3. De Vere Horsley Estate

The estate's mansion house, Horsley Towers, served as a filming location for the third season of The Crown, where it played the role of German Marburg Castle in Hesse.

The 19th century mansion underwent a multi-million pound renovation which includes updates to the bedrooms, suites and communal areas, while spaces such as the iconic Great Hall were restored.

Nowadays it's actually a hotel with prices from £139 per night on a bed and breakfast basis.

4. Ely Cathedral, Cambridgeshire

While the Queen and Prince Philip tied the knot at Westminster Abbey, the crew weren't able to film inside the iconic church for season one - so they recreated the wedding at Ely Cathedral in Cambridgeshire instead.

It's no surprise that the cathedral caught the production team's eye. Built as an abbey church, the present building dates back to 1803 and boasts seriously spectacular architecture, not to mention the jaw-dropping interiors.

5. Greenwich Naval College, London

Located in London's picturesque Greenwich district, the Naval College served as a stand in for Buckingham Palace's courtyard.

In fact, a specially-built portico was attached to the building to add to its resemblance to the palace.

It's free to go an explore the college and its historic grounds (open daily), but some tours can incur an extra fee. Find out more on its website here .

6. Belvoir Castle, Leicestershire

Belvoir Castle has doubled up as Windsor Castle in all three series of the Crown, with its Elizabeth Saloon and the Regent's Gallery serving as filming locations.

For the third season, the cast and crew spent over a month filming both interior scenes as well as exterior shots on the castle's picture-perfect grounds.

Want to check it out for yourself? The castle and gardens re-open from the 1st March 2020 - you can plan your visit on the Belvoir Castle website.

7. Woodchester Mansion, Gloucestershire

Woodchester Mansion was turned into the grounds of Scottish school Gordonstoun for the scenes of Prince Charles' time at the school, reports Gloucester Live .

The 19th century house makes for an impressive location with its pristine grounds, not to mention the building itself towers over the landscape.

8. Eltham Palace

Tucked away in South London, the stylish Eltham Palace boasts an Art Deco theme and was used for several scenes in Season 1, episode 8 of The Crown.

In fact, it provided the backdrop for travel-themed locations including Bermuda Government House, the HMSS Queen Mary, and the Queen's Quarters of the Royal Yacht.

Fans may also recognise the iconic Entrance Hall, where the Queen meets fashion designer Norman Hartnell in the series.

Adult tickets from £15.40, child tickets from £9.20. Find out more on the English Heritage website.

9. Waddesdon Manor, Buckinghamshire

This striking Grade I Neo-Renaissance château has heaps of filming credits including Downton Abbey and Bargain Hunt.

Built between 1874 and 1889 for Baron Ferdinand de Rothschild as a weekend residence in the style of a French château for entertaining, today it hosts plenty of visitors looking to explore the breathtaking grounds and gardens.

You can find out more about opening times and ticket prices on the Waddeson Manor website .

10. Ardverikie Estate, Scottish Highlands

This Scottish estate was used to represent the Queen's residence of Balmoral, thanks to its sweeping views of the countryside, not to mention features including the picturesque woodlands and meandering streams that can be spotted in the royal picnic scene with The Queen Mother.
