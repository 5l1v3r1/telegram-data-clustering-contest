Wolves tipped for busy January transfer window as Nuno urged to join Premier League rivals

The latest Wolverhampton Wanderers news and views from BirminghamLive

13 Nov 2019, 04:00 by Mark Wakefield

INJURY LATEST

Wolverhampton Wanderers have provided an injury update on trio Adam Traore, Morgan Gibbs-White and Willy Boly ahead of the November international break.

Traore received his first Spain call-up earlier this month for their Euro 2020 qualifiers against Malta and Romania, but withdraw from the squad due to a hamstring injury.

Gibbs-White has been suffering from a back problem in recent weeks, having not featured in the matchday squad since he was named on the bench for Wolves ' draw with Arsenal earlier this month.

Wolves’ head of medical, Phil Hayward, has confirmed that the pair will remain at Compton to receive treatment during the international break.

WEST HAM URGED TO LURE NUNO AWAY FROM WOLVES

Premier League side West Ham have been urged to "try" and lure manager Nuno Espirito Santo away from Wolverhampton Wanderers .

That is the view of talkSPORT host Adrian Durham, who claims that the Hammers should increase their ambition and bring a new manager in to replace Manuel Pellegrini.

West Ham have had a tough start to the season, amassing just 13 points from their opening 16 Premier League fixtures and are in 16th place in the table and five points above the relegation zone.

Wolves are having a better campaign than the Hammers so far, with Nuno’s side three points better off in the league and are also on course to reach the knockout stages of the Europa League.

Nuno is currently in his third season in Molineux hotseat, and Durham believes he would be a better fit for West Ham than current boss Pellegrini.

Durham also suggested that the Hammers “try” and bring in Jose Mourinho, who is currently out of work after being sacked by Manchester United in December last year.

WOLVES SEND SCOUT TO BELGIUM

Wolverhampton Wanderers reportedly had a scout watching a match in Belgium in recent days.

According to reports in Belgium , a scout from Wolves was in attendance to watch the First Division A clash between Anderlecht and Zulte Waregem.

The report also claims that scouts from other top clubs from across Europe were also watching the game, with Manchester United, Arsenal, Rangers, Ajax, Atalanta, Feyenoord, Genoa, Inter Milan, Marseille, Real Sociedad and Sevilla all represented.

It has been suggested that the players being watched were Anderlecht duo Yari Verschaeren and Albert Sambi Lokonga.

WOLVES TO BATTLE WITH MAN UTD FOR EXCITING FORWARD

 

Wolverhampton Wanderers are reportedly set to compete with a number of clubs for the signing of Atalanta youngster Dejan Kulusevski.

According to reports in Italy , Wolves will battle with Manchester United and Southampton for the 19-year-old’s signature during the January transfer window.

Italian giants Inter Milan are the club thought to be the most interested in Kulusevski’s signature, but do face competition from the aforementioned English clubs.

 

It is also believed that those clubs are ready to offer €17-€18m to sign Kulusevski, although that is a figure Atalanta would reject as they would prefer €25m for the player.

WOLVES COULD PLAY ROLE IN MAN UTD TRANSFER

Wolverhampton Wanderers are once again being linked with a move for AC Milan midfielder Franck Kessie.

The 22-year-old has been widely tipped to head for the exit door at the San Siro, with Wolves claimed to be a potential destination.

Fresh reports in Italy claim that Arsenal, Tottenham Hotspur and West Ham have all shown an interest in acquiring Kessie’s signature, but adds that Wolves are “above everyone” because of their relationship with Milan.

Wolves signed striker Patrick Cutrone from the Italian outfit in the summer, and it is believed the two clubs are on good terms following the deal.

It is also being claimed that Kessie’s exit could be accelerated to make way for Manchester United midfielder Nemanja Matic.

KEEPER IMPRESSES AFTER WOLVES HEROICS

Premier League duo Crystal Palace and Brighton are reportedly preparing to make a move for a goalkeeper who recently impressed against Wolverhampton Wanderers .

Dominik Greif , who recently starred for Slovan Bratislava in their Europa League group stage tie against Wolves, is being linked with a move to England.

Football Insider claim that Palace and Brighton sent scouts to watch Greif in action against Wolves in Slovakia earlier this month.

The 22-year-old stopper was called into action multiple times throughout the clash against Wolves, and even saved a penalty from Ruben Neves.
