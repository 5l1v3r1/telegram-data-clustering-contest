Credit union boss embezzled £180k by forging pals signatures on fake loan applications

Anne Costello was a credit union boss who embezzled £180,000.

13 Nov 2019, 04:30 by David Meikle

A credit union boss who forged friends’ signatures on bogus loan applications and embezzled £180,000 has been jailed for two years.

Anne Costello, 67, hatched a plot to pocket thousands from the Cumbernauld South Credit Union after falling into debt.

Costello, of Cumbernauld, used her senior position to make false applications for loans but failed to deposit money into accounts.

 

Her deception continued for 18 years but it unravelled after she took £77,000 from John Martin, 79, who she met at a mutual friend’s house.

His suspicious daughters confronted shamed Costello and she admitted taking the cash but begged them not to report it.

But police were called in and a probe discovered she also embezzled £39,095 from Lorraine Febers, 68, £19,298 from Anne Daly, £3588.77 from Anne Provan, 66, and £1232.83 from Marrion Cassidy, 77, who were all friends.

Airdrie Sheriff Court also heard she used forged details of Martin, Yvonne Murray and Carissa Bovill to apply for bogus loans totalling more than £40,000.

 

Costello admitted charges of embezzlement and forgery between October 1999 and June 2017 totalling £180,718.77.

Jailing Costello, sheriff Derek O’Carroll said: “You put yourself in a senior position to exploit the trust placed in you by those in your community.”
