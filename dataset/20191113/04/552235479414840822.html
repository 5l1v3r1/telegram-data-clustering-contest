Owner's shock after old Chinese tea pot sells for £1m at auction

The owner wasn’t aware it was a rare Chinese artefact from the 1700s.

13 Nov 2019, 04:30 by Louie Smith

A teapot sitting on a shelf as an ornament has sold for £1million.

The owner wasn’t aware it was a rare Chinese artefact from the 1700s.

He took it down to show expert Lee Young, who was invited to his property to look at some items.

 

Lee identified the stamp on the base of the 5in-tall china pot as being that of Chinese emperor Qianlong, who reigned between 1735 and 1796.

The green glaze pear-shaped teapot, which has a peach on the lid, was estimated to go for a couple of thousand but there was an international bidding war.

Guy Schwinge, from Duke’s auction house in Dorchester, Dorset, said the lucky seller, who lives in the county, “is understandably delighted”.

Lee, the firm’s Asian art specialist and an Antiques Roadshow expert, was doing a routine valuation.

Schwinge said: “The vendor brought it down for him and Lee sensed straight away it was a rather exciting discovery.”

There was stunned applause at the auction as the hammer went down at £800,000.

The phone bidder paid £1,040,000 after fees were added.
