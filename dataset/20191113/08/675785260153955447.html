Girl, 2, in pram dies after being hit by air-conditioner that fell from flat

The toddler was in the pram at the time of the tragedy in Toronto, Canada, where the air-conditioning unit plunged from the eighth floor of building run by Toronto Community Housing (TCH)

13 Nov 2019, 08:06 by Bradley Jolly, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/bradley-jolly/

A two-year-old girl died after she was hit by an air-conditioning unit that fell from an eighth floor apartment.

The toddler, who was being pushed in her pram at the time by her mum, was rushed to hospital but medics were unable to save her.

Witnesses in Toronto, Canada, described distressing scenes after the unit tumbled some distance from the building run by Toronto Community Housing (TCH).

The girl's siblings, aged five and seven, were present having just been picked up from school.

"I could see her [mum] trying to revive her daughter, and I didn’t even grab my jacket, I grabbed my keys and ran downstairs," Sherene Bromfield, who lives nearby, said.

 

Police said they investigated the death but have called it a "terrible tragedy".

Global News reports no criminal charges will be brought.

John Tory, the mayor of Toronto, said: "My thoughts are with the family involved and the people in that building.

"I'm sure it is traumatising for them."

TCH will do a "thorough investigation" into what caused the air conditioner unit to come loose and fall to the ground.

"Our thoughts are with the family at this difficult time and we offer them our sincere condolences," a statement said.

Its website warns tenants about the dangers posed by window AC units.

It says: “Improperly installed window air conditioners are a serious safety hazard to you and your family, and can fall onto other people."

It also says tenants must have permission to install the units and should by placed over balconies whenever possible.

The website adds: "You may be held liable for injuries or property damage from improperly installed window air conditioners.”
