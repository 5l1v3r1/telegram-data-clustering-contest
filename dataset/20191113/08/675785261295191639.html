Premier League referees 'stunned' by boss Mike Riley saying he's pleased with VAR performance

The comments left top-flight referees stunned, with the officials annoyed that they are being made to look amateur by the new technology this season

13 Nov 2019, 08:45 by Matt Maltby, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/matt-maltby/

The referees' chief Mike Riley has stunned Premier League match officials after controversially claiming: "I’m well pleased with the way VAR is operating."

VAR caused yet more controversy over the weekend, with Manchester City manager Pep Guardiola fuming after Liverpool's 3-1 defeat of his side at Anfield on Sunday, in which his side had two penalty appeals rejected.

Another controversial VAR incident occurred on Saturday at Tottenham , in which Sheffield United were denied a goal for the tightest of offside decisions.

And, following several controversial decisions and criticism from fans and pundits, Riley raised a few eyebrows with his bizarre take on the technology in the top-flight.

 

According to The Sun , he told Select Group One officials at a two-day meeting in Leicestershire he has been “well pleased with the way VAR is operating at the moment”.

The comments left top-flight referees stunned, with the officials annoyed that they are being made to look amateur by the new technology this season.

Riley isn't the only one to praise VAR this week, with Neil Swarbrick - the Premier League’s head of VAR - saying he has been impressed with the impact of the technology.

He said: "I’m really pleased, honestly, with how we have started out. We have more decisions correct with VAR than without it.

"I’d give us around about seven-ish. If the mark now is seven — early days — in two years’ time I’m hoping for maybe a eight and a half or nine. We are open to evolving with this.

"It’s not a case of we’re not budging. We will listen to feedback. I’m comfortable with where we are but there’s no doubt there’s room for improvement. It’s a work in progress."
