Girl, 12, raped by man, 27, in threesome is 'numb' and tormented by flashbacks

The traumatised girl was raped by Anthony Roscoe, who is 15 years her senior, after his girlfriend Chloe Bradley, then 17, arranged a threesome at his home in St Helens, Merseyside

13 Nov 2019, 08:10 by Luke Traynor, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/luke-traynor/

A girl who was just 12 when she was raped by a 27-year-old man in a threesome arranged by his 17-year-old girlfriend has told how she has been left extremely traumatised.

The girl told a court she now feels "numb" about what happened to her, struggles to sleep and suffers flashbacks.

Now 14, she said she doesn't let anyone touch her, has no interest in going to school and is getting through life "one day at a time" after being raped by Anthony Roscoe, who is 15 years her senior, at his home in St Helens, Merseyside.

Roscoe, now 29, has been jailed and his girlfriend Chloe Bradley, now 19, has avoided a prison sentence for the incident on August 20, 2017, the Liverpool Echo reports.

 

The girl told Bolton Crown Court in a victim impact statement on Tuesday: "I feel numb about what happened and I don't know how to feel about what's happened to me.

"It's changed me mentally, I don't sleep and I have flashbacks.

"It's made me more aware of men and I don't let anyone touch me.

"I feel there is no point going to school.

"I kind of hate Chloe Bradley and I feel different towards her.

"I'm just getting through one day at a time.

"I was relieved when they pleaded guilty as I don't know what I would have done."

Roscoe stunned detectives when he told them after his arrest: "When somebody hands you the Holy Grail, you’re going to grab it, aren't you?"

He admitted rape and was jailed for four years.

Bradley, who was said to have an emotional age of 10, pleaded guilty to arranging the commission of a child sex offence.

She was given a 22-month sentence suspended for a year and was ordered to wear an electronic tag for three months and sign the Sex Offender Register for 10 years.

The rape happened after Bradley, who lives with her parents in Wigan, messaged her on-off boyfriend Roscoe about a rendezvous and then escorted the young girl to his home on the bus.

He took turns having sex with Bradley and raping the 12-year-old victim.

Police became involved by chance when a passing patrol spotted the girls leaving Roscoe's home at 5.30am the following morning.

Bradley's phone was examined and found to contain conversations between herself and Roscoe.

Initially both girls told police Roscoe had forced himself on them but police charged Bradley after uncovering the messages on her phone and that of Roscoe.

The prosecutor said: "There was a significant amount of highly sexualised chat and discussion over a period of several days, which occurred mainly over WhatsApp and Facebook Messenger.

"...Bradley also sent a series of messages to Roscoe asking him if he would like a threesome with her and the complainant.

"She said both of them were 'up for it'. He was enthusiastic at that prospect.

"It seems that many of those messages between Bradley and Roscoe had been deliberately deleted from Bradley’s phone, including messages dealing with sexual talk.

"Also found on his phone was a message from Roscoe to Bradley saying he didn’t want her coming round with any kids.

"It would appear that Bradley deleted the messages to minimise the appearance of her offending and to minimise her culpability.

“Roscoe was arrested at his flat that morning at 10am. He was cautioned and he made many comments denying the allegations, saying they made him 'feel sick'.

"In interview he accepted he had sexual intercourse with both Bradley and the complainant - stating Bradley had suggested having a threesome.

"He said he had not met the complainant before, but he knew Bradley for about two months and had had sex with her before.

“He said Bradley told him she was 17.

Roscoe had not groomed his victim, his lawyer said.

He denied having a sexual attraction to children.

He lives with his mum who has significant mental health problems, the court was told.
