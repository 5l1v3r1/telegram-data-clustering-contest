Carnival: capturing all the fun of the fair across the US – in pictures

14 Nov 2019, 07:00

Between 1982 and 2001, the American photographer Mark Steinmetz travelled across the US recording life at country fairs, urban street fairs and small circuses.

‘These photos were made in Connecticut, Massachusetts, Illinois, Iowa and Georgia between 1982 and 2001 at country fairs, urban street fairs, and small circuses,’ said Mark Steinmetz, a photographer from Athens, Georgia. The new book Carnival, published by STANLEY/BARKER is out now

Photograph: Mark Steinmetz

‘People from all walks of life go to the fair seeking something to transport them from the everyday’

‘Amid the excitement and sounds of the rides and games, I could slip by largely unnoticed and capture gestures and faces’

‘I photographed families with young children, teenagers out on their own, and the carnies. There are quite a few photographs of young couples in Carnival’

‘Photographers, such as Diane Arbus, have photographed at circuses, but I’m not aware of a large sustained body of work made at fairs’

‘The very best photographs of fairs are probably those made in Paris by Atget and Brassai in the 20s and 30s

‘Murnau’s 1927 film Sunrise has a beautiful night sequence at a fair’

‘Carnivals look pretty much the same today as they have for the past 35 years. All that has really changed are prices and styles’

‘Many of the photographs were made with a 35mm Leica, but other medium format cameras were used as well’

‘The book begins in daylight, with people outside the fair, and ends with a night sequence’

‘My parents gave me my first camera when I was perhaps six years old. I remember coming home from a trip with my family, getting out of the car and deciding to photograph my house’

‘Some people can work inside but I have a need to be outside, to watch the weather and observe the light at different times of day. I love to describe this place and this time, my contemporaries and my civilisation’
