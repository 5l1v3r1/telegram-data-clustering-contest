Mercedes car delivery driver savagely beaten by car-jacker in harrowing attack

Gerald Wallbanks may now need facial surgery after he was left bloodied, battered and bruised

14 Nov 2019, 07:55 by James Rodger, Matt Jackson, https://www.facebook.com/jamesrodgerjournalist

A delivery driver has been savagely beaten in a harrowing attack by a car-jacker in the Midlands.

Gerald Wallbanks may now need facial surgery after he was left bloodied, battered and bruised.

Mr Wallbanks has told how he was viciously attacked by a violent car-jacker after dropping off a Mercedes at a customer's house, reports StokeonTrentLive.

The Blurton resident, aged 58, suffered a cracked cheekbone and broken eye socket during Monday's incident.

He was rushed to the Royal Stoke University Hospital, in Staffs, following the attack and may now need facial surgery.

Gerald, of Blurton, said: "I was delivering the Mercedes and sat outside the customer's house. I went to open the door and just started receiving punches.

"It was a housing estate and it was hailing so I was being slow with the paperwork hoping it would pass.

"I couldn't even see the person approaching me. The doctors told me he might have been using a knuckle-duster because the damage is that bad."

 

The attack happened in Melton Close, Congleton, at 4.26pm on Monday, November 11. It was witnessed by Gerald's colleague. The car-jacker fled empty-handed.

Gerald added: "My colleague was parked 20 metres away - because we work in twos - and just saw a figure throwing punches at me. He went to chase them but saw blood rushing down my face.

"It was a devastating attack and I was left shocked."

Gerald is currently signed off work and says he will be more vigilant of potential car-jackings when delivering vehicles.

He added: "I've never been in anything like this, I've had a few fights but have always fought back.

"It's always going to stay with me and I think I will look around before I open the door in the future - I will be a bit more vigilant now.

"I'm hoping that other people can learn from this. If I can stop one other old guy falling victim to this then these people will be losing."

 

Police have recorded 86 violent crimes and 11 vehicle crimes across Congleton in the past month. They have issued a description of the would-be car-jacker.

A Cheshire Police spokesman said: "The offender is described as being 5ft 8in tall. He was wearing a black bubble coat with the hood up and black trousers. His face was covered with a scarf or balaclava."

 

Witnesses should call the police on 101, quoting incident number 562638.
