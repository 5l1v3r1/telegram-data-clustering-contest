Driver reported by police over Gawler crash that killed Paralympic legend Kieran Modra

14 Nov 2019, 07:40

A 26-year-old man has been reported by police over the Kingsford crash that claimed the life of Kieran Modra, but officers have not yet decided on a final charge.

The low-vision five-time Paralympic gold medallist was cycling to Clare from his home at Hallett Cove when he was hit by a Kia sedan on the Sturt Highway about 7am on Wednesday.

Modra, 47, died at the scene.

The driver of the Kia – a 26-year-old Greenwith man – was not injured but taken to Lyell McEwin Hospital as a precaution.

Major Crash officers on Thursday reported the man for driving offences and are continuing their investigations to determine the final charge.

The man will be summonsed to appear in court at a later date.

Modra’s death took South Australia’s road toll to 97, compared with 63 at the same time last year.
