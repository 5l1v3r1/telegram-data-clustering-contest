Vivienne Westwood’s Punk-Inspired Corsets Make A Comeback For Limited Time Only

14 Nov 2019 by Yimin Huang

Vivienne Westwood is reissuing the corset, but not the traditional ones that dated back to the 16th century. Instead, Westwood is issuing the punk-inspired version that was included in the Harris Tweed collection back in 1987, according to Vogue.
Westwood first integrated corsets into her punk look back in 1970s. By modifying the design, she successfully changed it from an oppressive wear to an empowering one.
One of her legendary designs, the classic corset, is rolled out for a limited period in stores in London, New York, Paris, Milan and Los Angeles, as stated in Vogue. The corsets come in ivory, red and black, and cost a whopping £1,045 (US$1342).
Other designers such as Stella McCartney and Jean Paul Gaultier have also tried to incorporate variations of the corset in their designs. However, Westwood’s designs are the most iconic, as she was the first to include it in her fetish wear at Sex, the King’s Road boutique back in the 70s.
