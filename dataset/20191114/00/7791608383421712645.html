Facebook’s Content Moderation Quiz Lets You Decide Which Posts Violate Policy

14 Nov 2019 by Yimin Huang

Facebook has officially released a quiz that allows users to play the role of the third-party moderator, deciding which posts have violated the platform’s policy.
A Facebook spokesperson has told Mashable that the posts used for the quiz are actually “based on real posts” but have been modified to “protect user privacy.”
For the comfort of the users, the quiz does not include truly violent posts such as those dealing with child labor. However, there is a disturbing picture of the famous “napalm girl” running naked down the streets during the Vietnam War.
Some posts used in the quiz include one asking for fictional character King Joffrey to be “stabbed,” and another is a post selling prescription drugs. While the former is allowed as King Joffrey does not really exist, the latter should be banned as the sale is illegal.

Post indicating a potential suicide attempt is allowed on Facebook as well, as it is a way for victims to call for help. Meanwhile, threats of violence against actual people or groups should be banned.
While the quiz contains scenarios that are perhaps easy to decide for the moderator, real life posts can be much more messed up and confusing.
