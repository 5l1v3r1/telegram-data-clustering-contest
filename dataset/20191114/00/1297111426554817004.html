Local roundup: U of R Cougars great Cymone Bernauer enters Canada West shrine

Former University of Regina Cougars basketball star Cymone Bernauer entered the Canada West Hall of Fame on Wednesday.

14 Nov 2019, 00:10 by Regina Leader-Post

Cymone Bernauer (nee Bouchard), the leading scorer in the history of the University of Regina Cougars women’s basketball team, entered the Canada West Hall of Fame on Wednesday.

Bernauer, a member of the Cougars from 1999 to 2004, scored 1,439 points in 96 conference games.

In 2001, she helped the Cougars win their first national title.

The Regina Pats are to face the Swift Current Broncos on Tuesday in a rescheduled WHL game. Face-off is set for 7 p.m., at the Brandt Centre.

The game was to have been played last Friday, only to be postponed due to a malfunction with the ice plant.

On Wednesday, the Pats announced that they had acquired forward Carter Chorney, 18, from Swift Current for defenceman Sam McGinley, 17.

Chorney — a second-round pick of the Spokane Chiefs in the 2016 bantam draft — compiled 14 goals and 26 points in 62 games with Spokane and Swift Current last season. The 6-foot-1, 171-pounder has one goal and five points in 17 games this season.

“We are looking forward to Carter joining our team,” Pats GM John Paddock said in a media release. “He brings some good ability to our forward group.”

McGinley — a fifth-round pick in 2017 — has no points in eight games as a WHL rookie this season.
