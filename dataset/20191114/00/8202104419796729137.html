Saskatoon city hall seeks tenant for farmers' market building

14 Nov 2019, 00:52 by Phil Tank, Saskatoon StarPhoenix

City issues request for proposals for River Landing building home to Saskatoon Farmers' Market since 2007

As the Saskatoon Farmers’ Market co-operative prepares to vote on a new location near the airport, city hall is searching for a tenant for its current home.

On Friday, the City of Saskatoon issued a formal request for proposals for an entity to negotiate a five-year lease of the former power plant garage that has been home to the farmers’ market for the last 12 years.

The Saskatoon Farmers’ Market co-operative membership is set to vote on Saturday on whether or not to support a 10-year lease of part of the Cavendish Building in the Airport Business Area.

City hall believes the farmers’ market model is “evolving,” according to the 21-page document seeking proposals for the current building, and wants someone to operate an “animated” facility at least six days a week with a farmers’ market component.

“The city seeks development of the site as a vibrant centre for the community and a destination location that provides dedicated farmers’ market days with verifiable Saskatchewan produce and content,” the document says.

The co-operative and city council reached an impasse over the future of the group at the city-owned River Landing location. As the document suggests, city hall wants animation at the site six days a week.

The co-operative operates three days a week at its current spot, offering locally grown food. The group has expressed concern about mixing the farmers’ market operation with a so-called public market that sells food from a variety of sources.

Adi Ramachandran, president of the co-operative’s board of directors, declined to comment Wednesday on whether the co-operative would submit a proposal to the city, but said the board is not closing any doors.

“What I can tell you is that this (request for proposals), like the previous one, specifies business activities six days a week, while our last proposal focused on animating the site with a mix of community and business activities,” Ramachandran said in an email.

Regardless of the outcome of Saturday’s vote, the co-operative’s lease of the city building expires at the end of the year. Ramachandran said the last day for operations at the current site is set for Dec. 22.

If membership rejects the Cavendish lease, Ramachandran said the co-operative will revert to the temporary site model used prior to 2007. The co-operative says it boasts the largest farmers’ market in the province with about 100 members.

The city plans to begin repairs to the roof of the building sometime early in 2020 with the goal of completing the work by the spring.

Those who want to lease the River Landing facility have until Dec. 31 to submit a proposal. A lease agreement is expected by March, according to the document.

The successful proposal for the building must include a farmers’ market two days a week, year round.

The lease pertains only to the 13,561-square-foot farmers’ market building at River Landing, although the tenant would get right of first refusal for renting the outdoor market square space from the city.

The annual lease for the building, like the current deal with the co-operative, will cost $10. The tenant will also have to pay property taxes ($14,139 in 2019) and at least $27,000 a year into a building maintenance reserve fund.

Ramachandran has said the co-operative’s total costs for the site ran about $100,000 a year.

The space in the Cavendish Building the co-operative will vote on leasing is just slightly smaller than the River Landing building at 13,266 sq. ft.
