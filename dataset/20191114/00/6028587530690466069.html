Lego ‘Escape Room’ Debuts, Adds Kohl’s, Michaels, and Dollar General to Toy Distribution

14 Nov 2019 by Sanford Stein

Lego, the world’s number one toy maker, ahead of rivals Mattel and Hasbro is back on an upswing after a rough 18 months. The privately held toy maker reported $5.5 billion in fiscal 2018, an increase of 4% over the previous year. Like every other toy manufacturer they were impacted by the Toys R Us bankruptcy. But their fortune’s have taken a turn since adding 20,000 retailers to its US distribution network, including Kohl’s, Michaels, and Dollar General.Today’s Lego brand bears little resemblance to the simple 5-color block set that most of us boomers and Xers grew up with. Lego is selling over 70 billion pieces a year in over 130 countries and now includes over 3,400 different shapes and 60-plus colors. The breath of the company’s focus runs from STEM to Ninjas, cars to coding, real life heroes to robots for kids.

Lego’s product licensing agreements run the gamut from Jurassic world to Powderpuff Girls, Minecraft to Star Wars, Speed Champions to Friends (yes, the TV series).  In fact the companies renowned “The Art of the Brick” artist Nathan Sawaya, just completed a 700 square foot replica of Central Perk, the Friends iconic coffee shop to celebrate the series’ 25th anniversary. Warner Brothers commissioned the work which utilized just under one million bricks. It made its Las Vegas debut in October.

During this holiday season of giving Lego is encouraging kids to share their unused Lego bricks with kids who are less fortunate through a pilot program called Lego Replay. The program accepts any previously used Lego bricks and donates them to children’s nonprofits in the United States. They encourage kids that wish to donate to put them into a cardboard box and visit www.lego.com/replay to print out a free UPS shipping label.

This past weekend, my wife and I experienced, firsthand another Lego themed holiday promotion that captured both of our imaginations. Rosedale Shopping Center, a Twin Cities suburban shopping mall, was host to the "first-of-its-kind" escape room, called Lego Create Your Escape. The pop-up designed for children ages 6-12 had a short, weekend preview, before traveling to Los Angeles. The admission-free, approximately 500 square foot themed space challenges teams of four to cooperatively solve a series of clues in search of three Lego keys. Competitors are challenged on their knowledge of Lego Star Wars, Lego Marvel Avengers, Lego Speed Champions and Minecraft. Participants are given 15 minutes to solve clues and find the Lego keys.

New York based Set Creative, was the brainchild behind the interactive exhibit’s development. Set’s Lee Kociski was kind enough to allow my wife and I to partake during the “resetting” of the exhibit, even without age-appropriate accompaniment. We were both impressed by the interactive nature of the experience and the degree to which it was designed to be a collaborative experience for participants. The exhibit’s design includes a small “viewing room” giving parents the ability to view the activity. 

The relatively “soft marketing approach” taken by Lego in the overall theming was noteworthy. And, for a two-day pop-up experience, they spared no expense in creating a highly memorable discovery event that will create new memories around an already beloved brand. And it was all done without a single video touchscreen.
