J.M. Smucker Names Tucker Marshall To Succeed Mark Belgya As CFO

14 Nov 2019 by Howard Gensler

The J. M. Smucker Company (NYSE: SJM) announced yesterday that Vice President for Finance Tucker Marshall has been chosen to succeed CFO Mark Belgya, who plans to retire September 1, 2020. Marshall was promoted following an internal and external search, and will be named Senior Vice President and Deputy CFO as of Nov. 25, 2019. He will take over Belgya’s CFO role May 1, 2020, but Belgya will stay on the Smucker board through August.In a statement, President and CEO Mark Smucker thanked Belgya for his 35 years of service to the company, the last 15 as CFO. "His guidance and leadership have been instrumental to the Company's transformational sales growth from $2 billion to nearly $8 billion, and he has been at the center of the acquisitions and divestitures that have diversified and strengthened our portfolio of brands,” Smucker said. 

"Succession planning is a critical element of how we operate our business and develop our people,” Smucker added. "Over the last seven years, Tucker has become an integral part of the Smucker organization and brings significant financial management experience and a deep understanding of the Company to the role."

"I love Smucker and the people I've had the privilege to work with,” Belgya said in his own statement. "As I look back on my career, I am extremely proud of the work we have done to transform the Company and position the business to deliver long-term value to shareholders. I've worked closely with Tucker over the last several years and know the Company will benefit from his leadership."

J.M. Smucker was founded by James Monroe Smucker in 1897 in Orrville, Ohio, where it is still headquartered. The apples for its first run of cider more than 120 years ago can be traced back to Johnny Appleseed and the company, which now has more than 7,000 employees, remains family-run . . . and well-run. It is No. 39 on Forbes’ 2019 list of the World’s Best Employers. 

Known primarily as a jam and jelly giant, the Smucker company is a major player in all type of foods for humans and their pets. The company’s brands include Santa Cruz Organic and R.W. Knudsen fruit juices, Meow Mix, Milk-Bone and Nutrish for pets, Jif peanut butter, Crisco oil, Pillsbury baking products, Folger’s coffee, and many more. The company's market cap is a little over $12 billion.
