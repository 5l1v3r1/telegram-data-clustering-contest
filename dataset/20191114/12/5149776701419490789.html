Notts County's former title-winning manager interviewed for Hearts job

Talks were held in Edinburgh

14 Nov 2019, 12:05 by Leigh Curtis

Notts Countyâ€™s former title-winning manager Steve Cotterill could be handed a return to football after he was interviewed for the Hearts job.

According to the Daily Record, the 55-year-old held talks with owner Ann Budge as he looks for a return to work after being sacked by Birmingham City last year.

Cotterill is fondly remembered at Notts where he guided them to the League Two title in the latter half of the 2009/10 season.

He immediately made an impact at the club where he was named manager of the month for March in April 2010.

He then successfully guided Notts to the title and they were crowned champions with a 5-0 win over Darlington.

Cotterill also had huge success at Cheltenham where he won three promotions and the FA Trophy during five and a half seasons in charge.

He has also managed Stoke City, Burnley, Portsmouth, Nottingham Forest, Bristol City and Birmingham.
