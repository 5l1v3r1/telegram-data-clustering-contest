Why this Gateshead pub is still open after the council revoked its premises licence

A Gateshead pub remains open after its licence was revoked as pub bosses plan their next move

14 Nov 2019, 12:00 by Herbert Soden

A Gateshead pub remains open after its licence was revoked as pub bosses plan their next move.

The Bluebell was stripped of its premises licence last Monday, after Northumbria Police demanded a review following a string of allegations which raised concerns about its management.

Gateshead Council confirmed that after a premises loses its licence, its owners legally have 21 days to lodge an appeal, which will then be heard by a magistrates' court. In the meantime it can stay open.

This came some eight months after Trading Standards Officers seized 220 counterfeit Superkings and Richmond cigarettes and three pouches of tobacco at the Felling pub following a tip-off.

Manager Julie Simpson insisted they were for her own "personal use".

Now, bosses at Admiral Taverns, the firm which owns the Victoria Square venue say they are considering an appeal and the premises remains open.

"We’re disappointed with the decision which has been made concerning the Blue Bell and are considering an appeal against the revoking of the licence," said a spokesperson for the pub company.

"In the meantime, we’d advise our locals that it is very much business as usual at the pub and hope to see all the regulars there."

An alleged unprovoked assault at the premises late September last year caused a customer to lose five teeth, and police claimed that staff at the Bluebell didn’t call them, instead leaving it to an anonymous member of the public.

The part of the pub the alleged attack happened in wasn’t covered by CCTV and the victim, fearing reprisals, refused to give a statement.

There was also allegations that a woman was punched to the ground inside the pub, and that paramedics had to be called out to attend to another woman who had blood all over her face.

However, the alleged incidents couldn’t be substantiated or investigated “due to a lack of evidence such as CCTV” and “cultural fear of repercussions”.

A report heard by Gateshead Council licensing committee revealed that Northumbria Police raised fears the venue was failing to meet multiple licensing objectives.

Also blocked was a bid to transfer the designated premises supervisor to Miss Simpson's, daughter Tania Hope.
