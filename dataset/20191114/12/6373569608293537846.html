Plymouth Argyle team news for trip to League Two leaders Forest Green Rovers

Pilgrims' boss Ryan Lowe has given an update on the calf injury to midfielder Jose Baxter

14 Nov 2019, 12:34 by Chris Errington

Plymouth Argyle midfielder Jose Baxter is struggling to overcome a persistent calf injury, manager Ryan Lowe has admitted.

There is no timetable for the return of the talented 27-year-old, who has played only once since mid-September due to the problem.

Baxter is on a short-term contract with the Pilgrims until January so being out of action for so long is not doing him any favours at all.

Lowe said: “He’s struggling a little bit with the injury. We will just keep monitoring him.

“Soleus calf injuries are horrible. They are heart-breaking really. I done mine three times and you get a false sense of security.

“One minute you think you are alright and one minute you are not.”

Baxter is the only injured player in the Argyle squad ahead of the trip to League Two leaders Forest Green Rovers on Saturday.

Lowe added: “I think he has done a bit of work this week and we will see where he’s at with that.

“But it’s irrelevant to us at the moment. If he’s not available for us for the next week or two we just need to concentrate on what we have got.”

Baxter has been limited to five starts and seven substitute appearances for Argyle so far this season.

Right wing-back Joe Riley is available for selection against Forest Green after sitting out the 1-0 FA Cup first round win at Bolton Wanderers last Saturday.

Riley missed out against the club where he started his career after falling heavily on his left arm in training last Friday.

It was the arm which he broke during an away game against Blackpool at the end of March and he had an operation to insert a plate in it.

Lowe said: “Joe is fine. I think it’s just when he has an impact on it, it’s sore, but it has settled down now.

“He has trained Tuesday and he’s training today (Thursday) so he’s fine.

“I think it’s broke still but he doesn’t want to get the operation because he’s going to potentially miss games.”

Striker Dom Telford made his comeback from a hamstring injury as a late substitute against Bolton.

The 22-year-old then played for close to an hour for Argyle in their Premier League Under-23 Cup tie away to Everton on Sunday afternoon.

Two of Argyle’s other forwards, Billy Clarke and Ryan Taylor, also gained valuable game-time against Everton.

Clarke, who scored an equaliser from the penalty spot in the 1-1 draw, and Taylor were able to take part in the match as over-age players.
