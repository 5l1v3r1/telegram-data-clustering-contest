Will you be my bridesmaid? Can I meet Ant and Dec? Odd questions posed to Newcastle Travelodge staff

One guest asked if Ant and Dec could 'pop into the hotel' to visit their gran.

14 Nov 2019, 12:23 by Ian Johnson

A bride-to-be asked a member of staff at a Travelodge to be her bridesmaid - after falling out with her real friend ahead of the wedding.

The drama unfolded at a Newcastle branch of the budget hotel chain.

It has come to light after Travelodge revealed a list of the weirdest requests made to staff in the last 12 months.

One guest asked if Ant and Dec could "pop into the hotel" to visit their gran.

Another asked if their room at the Quayside branch could be made to look like a tropical island for a Love Island-themed proposal.

However, perhaps the most dramatic involved the wedding hiccup in which the bride-to-be asked: "I have fallen out with my bridesmaid, so can you take her place?"

It isn't known if the request was fulfilled - or if the friends reconciled before the ceremony.

However, Travelodge spokeswoman Shakila Ahmed said requests like these are becoming more and more common.

“Annually we welcome around 19 million customers at our 570 hotels which include four properties in Newcastle," she said.

"Our hotel teams receive thousands of interesting requests from business and leisure guests. 

"Where possible, our hotel teams will go above and beyond to help customers as they relish a good challenge.

"However there are some requests beyond their control such as getting a raft of ducks to go to sleep, stopping the traffic on the M5, getting the Northern Lights to make an appearance and getting a herd of sheep as a sleep aid.”

“Interestingly, over the last 12 months, our hotel teams have reported a rise in customer requests to help with wedding proposals. 

"Requests have included a customer asking for a unicorn pedal boat and for a rainbow to appear above the hotel at 16:16 for a wedding proposal at Thurrock Travelodge.

"The team at Rhyl Travelodge were asked to set up a romantic candlelit dinner on Rhyl beach with a path of red rose petals from the hotel to the table.

"At Leamington Spa Travelodge the hotel team were tasked to get a full moon outside the customer’s room window in order to create a moonlight setting.”

And on Tyneside , the requests were among the weirdest in the country.

Guests asked:

·         Can you turn my room into a tropical island, I want to propose to my boyfriend - he’s a massive fan of Love Island?

·         I have fallen out with my bridesmaid, so can you take her place?

·         Can you arrange for a pair of llamas to attend my wedding ceremony?

·         Can you arrange for Ant and Dec to pop into the hotel this afternoon as my nan is a massive fan?

·         Can I check my miniature Shetland Pony, Newkie Brown, into the hotel?

·         Can you teach me to change a car tyre?

·         Can you translate my presentation into Georgie as I want to impress my client? 

·         Can you help me create Cheryl Cole’s hairstyle?

·         Can take me on a Newcastle bridge tour? 

·         Can you arrange for us to see fog on the Tyne?

·         What time does the Angel of the North fly to heaven?
