Children's doctor who had sex with vulnerable 15-year-old has been struck off

Jonathan Bainbridge worked as a paediatric hospital consultant at the time of the incident

14 Nov 2019, 12:12 by Lydia Stephens

A children's doctor who had sex with an underage girl in care to whom he sent explicit images has been erased from the medical register.

Jonathan Bainbridge, 40, from Cardiff, met the 15-year-old in an online chat room where they traded indecent sexual images and later met up for sex, a court heard in November 2018.

At his sentencing last year, he was ordered to sign the Sex Offender Register for five years and complete 250 hours unpaid work and was obliged to refer himself for a disciplinary hearing which has resulted in him being struck off.

The Medical Practitioners Tribunal Service determined that: "Given the serious nature of Dr Bainbridge's convictions, it was both necessary and proportionate to impose and immediate order of suspension on his registration pending the erasure of his name from the Medical Register.

"This immediate order serves to protect the public, to uphold public confidence in the medical profession, and to maintain and uphold proper standards of conduct and behaviour."

The MPTS heard the teenager, who lives with her grandparents near Conway, North Wales, was from a "troubled background" and had carers to help her as she was at risk of sexual exploitation from older men.

Bainbridge, who earned £100,000 a year as a paediatric hospital consultant with Aneurin Bevan University Health Board, made contact with the girl over a seven-day period on Kik, using the pseudonym 'Cardiff Boy JD' which included a number linked to his work.

The girl told the doctor she was "lonely", that she was a virgin, was still in school and that she was under local authority care.

Mr Carlo Breen, lawyer for the General Medical Council, told the Manchester hearing: ''During the conversation that took place on the messenger app the child told Dr Bainbridge she was 16 and he replied he was 37 years of age.

"He sent a live picture of himself that identified his face and further messages were exchanged in which he stated he lived in Cardiff.

"They discussed meeting on March 26, during the initial conversation they discussed the fact that she was in school, that she was lonely and she was in local authority care.

"He asked her to use Kik Cam but she declined. They then discussed having sex and he asked her for a naked photograph of herself and she declined. On the March 23 she gives her number to text her, on March 24 he requests explicit images of the child but she refused, however indecent images of genital areas were exchanged before they met late that evening.

"CCTV footage noted a female matching the description of the child sitting at a bus stop the female approached by Dr Bainbridge and they walked out of view, it appears there was a sexual encounter between Dr Bainbridge and the child.''

Bainbridge left his pregnant wife in Cardiff and drove 180 miles to Conwy in March 2017 to meet the teenager, where he and the girl engaged in oral sex in his car.

He was arrested after the victim told social workers of the encounter, admitting he had sex with her but insisted the girl had told him she was 16.

He pleaded guilty to possessing a Category A moving image of a child under 16 and of possessing a Category C indecent images of a child.

Sentencing him at Merthyr Crown Court in 2018, the Recorder of Cardiff Judge Eleri Rees, told the defendant it was shocking that someone held in high respect in the medical profession has not shown great judgement.

He was suspended following his arrest and moved out of the family home. He said he was unable to attend the birth of his third child due to the nature of the charges.

In a statement to the MPTS, Bainbridge said his conduct was a "catastrophic error of judgement that has blighted the lives of everyone".

He added: "My behaviour has significantly strayed from what is expected from a doctor and I have severely damaged the public’s trust in the medical profession.

"I have failed to live up to the standards expected of a doctor and have abused the public’s trust in the profession."
