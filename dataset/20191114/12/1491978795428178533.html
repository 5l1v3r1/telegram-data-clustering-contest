Prince Charles's birthday and a dinosaur MOT: Thursday's best photos

14 Nov 2019, 12:14 by Guy Lane

The Guardian’s picture editors select photo highlights from around the world

Santiago, Chile

A young woman uses a catapult during Protests against Chile’s government in Santiago.

Photograph: Jorge Silva/Reuters

Asturias, Spain

People view an artwork by US film director David Lynch at the exhibition ‘Small Stories’ at the Old Institute Cultural Centre in Gijon.

Photograph: Alberto Morante/EPA

Bastrop, US

Brittani Smith protests against the execution of Rodney Reed in Texas. People rallied in support of Reed’s campaign to stop his scheduled execution for the 1996 killing of 19-year-old Stacey Stites. New evidence in the case has led a growing number of Texas legislators, religious leaders and celebrities to press the state governor, Greg Abbott, to intervene

Photograph: Nick Wagner/AP

La Paz, Bolivia

Riot police fire teargas to disperse supporters of the former president Evo Morales during a protest

Photograph: Ronaldo Schemidt/AFP/Getty Images

Hong Kong, China

A vandalised first-class train carriage after pro-democracy protesters battled with riot police

Photograph: Anthony Wallace/AFP/Getty Images

Sydney, Australia

Burnt cars after devastating fires tore through areas near Colo Heights. An estimated 1m hectares of land have been burned by bushfire. Fire crews remain on high alert

Photograph: Brett Hemmings/Getty Images

Brasilia, Brazil

Supporters of the Venezuelan president, Nicolás Maduro, and followers of his rival Juan Guaidó clash outside Venezuela’s embassy facilities

Photograph: Joédson Alves/EPA

Santiago, Chile

A police officer detains a demonstrator during an anti-government protest

Photograph: Goran Tomašević/Reuters

Castle Cary, UK

Boris Johnson leaves a train station in Somerset for a day of general election campaigning

Photograph: Stefan Rousseau/PA

Tokyo, Japan

Emperor Naruhito prepares to perform an elaborate and secretive ritual dating back 1,300 years, giving thanks to the sun goddess, Amaterasu, as part of his enthronement rites. The Daijosai, considered the most important ritual in Japan’s imperial tradition, comes three weeks after Naruhito proclaimed his ascension to the chrysanthemum throne and is performed once in an emperor’s reign

Photograph: Jiji Press/AFP/Getty Images

Venice, Italy

The Italian prime minister, Giuseppe Conte (left), visits Venice after severe flooding affected much of the city

Photograph: Filippo Attili/EPA

Paris, France

A worker installs a 17th-century copy of the Mona Lisa before its auction at Sotheby’s

Photograph: Johanna Geron/Reuters

Zigong, China

Workers service an animatronic dinosaur. There are hundreds of simulated dinosaur manufacturers in Zigong, whose products are exported to more than 100 countries

Photograph: Lintao Zhang/Getty Images

Keelung, Taiwan

Visitors tour the abandoned Agenna shipyard. Established in 1966 and used until the 1980s, the shipyard was designated a cultural relic by the Keelung city government after demolition plans were halted following a public outcry in 2016

Photograph: Sam Yeh/AFP/Getty Images

Brighton, UK

Richard Browning attempts to break his own Guinness world record for the fastest speed in a body-controlled jet engine-powered suit

Photograph: Gareth Fuller/PA

Prince Charles celebrates his 71st birthday with schoolchildren from the Kaivalya Education Foundation during his visit to Mumbai

Photograph: Divyakant Solanki/EPA
