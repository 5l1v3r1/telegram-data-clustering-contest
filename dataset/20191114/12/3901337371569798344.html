Kaizer Chiefs go in to the FIFA break comfortably with a 10 point lead at the top of the Absa Premiership league standing, this while preparing for the Telkom Knockout semi-final.

On the other hand, second-placed Mamelodi Sundowns will prepare for Telkom Knockout, the Absa Premiership and CAF Champions League action during the month of November, with the Brazilians trailing behind in the league as they are unable to catch up with Chiefs, despite having a game in hand. 

At the other end of the log, Chippa United continue to be at the foot of the standings, despite snatching their first win at the weekend, with AmaZulu FC, Stellenbosch FC and Cape Town City all floating at the bottom four.
