St Andrew's Hospice campaign aims to light up lives with memories of loved ones at Christmas

All donations go to supporting patients and their families across Lanarkshire

14 Nov 2019, 08:00 by Ross Thomson

St Andrew’s Hospice has launched its annual Light Up a Life Christmas initiative ahead of the festive season.

Light Up a Life is a special opportunity to sponsor a light on the Hospice Christmas tree or on the Christmas tree in your local area, in memory or in honour of a loved one.

You can have a handwritten, personalised card delivered to you or sent to a friend or relative to let them know they are in your thoughts.

All donations go to supporting patients and their families across Lanarkshire.

 

The campaign has once again been generously sponsored by the Mears Group. Barry Duncan, Mears managing director, said: “We are proud to be involved with St Andrew’s Hospice as it plays an important role in caring for the people of Lanarkshire.

“We are also delighted to continue supporting the Hospice by sponsoring the Light Up a Life campaign again.”

Lorna McCafferty, fundraising officer at St Andrew’s Hospice, said: “The Mears Group have been sponsoring our Light Up a Life Campaign since 2012.

 

“Their continued support is greatly appreciated. Mears’ support, coupled with the generosity of the people of Lanarkshire makes a great difference to the lives of our patients and their families throughout the year.”

You can sponsor a light by visiting www.st-andrews-hospice.com, by completing a form in any St Andrew’s Hospice shop or in the reception of the Hospice in Henderson Street, Airdrie.

This year, Light Up A Life pin badges are also available for £2. The pins can be purchased in the Hospice, from their online shop or by telephoning Lorna McCafferty on 01236 772087.

 

You can sponsor a light up until noon on Christmas Eve.

Any cards written that day will be posted but you will not receive the card until the Royal Mail resumes deliveries.

The St Andrew’s Hospice Light Up A Life ceremony will take place in the Hospice grounds on Sunday, December 1 from 7pm.
