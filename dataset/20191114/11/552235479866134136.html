Crosshill dog walker mowed down by driver demands action over accident which has left her bedbound

Jasmine Doherty, 48, was left with a broken arm and leg when a driver ploughed into her on Kirkmichael Road, Crosshill, near her home.

14 Nov 2019, 11:16 by Abi Smillie, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/abi-smillie/

A dog walker mowed down by a driver is questioning “what police are for” after no action was taken after the accident which has left her bedbound.

Jasmine Doherty, 48, was left with a broken arm and leg when a driver ploughed into her on Kirkmichael Road, Crosshill, near her home.

The air traffic controller was out with her Pointer-cross pooch Loki when a car came over a blind bend at dusk on October 11.

Police wrote off the accident as the driver had sun in his eyes due to it being low in the sky.

But Jasmine, who has been signed off sick for eight weeks and will need a full medical assessment before returning to work, is questioning why no-one has faced any action.

She told the Post: “I’d like the driver to know that it’s not open season on pedestrians- you can’t just mow people down and not face consequences.

“I’ve always been very independent and in the space of a second I’m basically helpless.

“I’ve got a wheelchair in my kitchen because I can’t prepare food.

“My friend’s looking after my dog because I can’t.

“I’d like the driver to face some consequences.”

 

Jasmine was out walking Loki on the rural road when the driver came round the corner at 40mph.

She said: “I don’t think he was speeding but he was driving too fast for the conditions.

“He’s saying the sun was in his eyes- and it was low in the sky- but even without that he was going so fast he couldn’t react.

“I just didn’t have time to step on to the verge.

Fortunately Loki was unharmed but Jasmine was “screaming in pain” and was taken to hospital in an ambulance.

Jasmine said: “I’ve had surgery on my arm and leg.

“They put a pin right the way down one of the bones in my leg.

“And they’ve put plates and screws on both bones in the arm.

“One of the breaks actually goes down to the wrist so I imagine I’ll get arthritis from that.

“And I’ll set off metal detectors everywhere I’ll go.”

Jasmine, who admits she now doesn’t feel safe on the rural roads near her home, is questioning the police decision.

She said: “I just wonder what the hell there for.

“They go on about road safety all the time but you can literally run somebody over and they don’t do anything.

 

“I don’t think they took a statement from me until four or five days later in hospital.

“And at that visit they told me they weren’t taking any action.

“They said it was because the sun was in the driver’s eyes.

“He could have killed me- would it still just be ‘oh well, never mind’?”

A Police Scotland spokesman said: “At approximately 5.30pm on Friday, October 11, a road traffic collision occurred on Kirkmichael Road near the town of Crosshill.

“Ayrshire Road Policing Officers attended and discovered that a female, who had been walking her dog at this location, had been struck by a Volkswagen Passat vehicle which was travelling towards Crosshill. The female was taken to Ayr Hospital by ambulance and found to have leg and arm fractures.

“As a result a driver was subject to roadside procedures and a report detailing the circumstances was submitted for record.

“The driver of the vehicle involved was not charged as a consequence of the incident.”
