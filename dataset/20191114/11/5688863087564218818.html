Under 17 Women’s Championship | Tigress beat Panthers 5-2 for second consecutive win

14 Nov 2019, 11:07 by SportsCafe Desk

Priya PV’s Tigresses stunned Panthers to win it by five goals to two in the under 17 Women's Championship at the Kalyani Municipal Stadium on Wednesday. Tigresses girl Anju scored a brace while Amisha Baxla contributed with a goal and two assists for Tigresses for their second consecutive win.

Priya PV's Tigress continued their winning form as they thrashed Panthers for 5-2 of the Under-17 Women’s Championship played at the Kalyani Municipal Stadium on Wednesday. Anju and Amisha Baxla stole the show as Anju scored a brace and Amisha contributed with a goal and two assists.

Tigress played a dominating game at the beginning with better possession in the midfield. Their midfield trio of Nitu, Aveka Singh and Martina Thokchom controlled the game in favour of them. In the 14th minute, a lovely through ball finds Anju who makes no confusion in netting it past the Panthers keeper Anjali Barke.

Three minutes later, Tigress midfielder Nitu fired a 30-yard shot which the Panthers custodian do not have any answers. In the 27th minute, Anju grabbed her second from a beautiful Amisha Baxla cross in which midfielder Nitu too had a role. However, Anju missed a sitter for her hattrick in the late minutes of the first half.

After the half time, Panthers fought hard to silence Tigress. However, in the 57th-minute midfielder Nitu once again orchestrated a move to Amisha who struck it easily to make it 4-0 for Tigeress.

Karen Estrocio scored the final goal for the Tigress as she received a ball from Amisha in the 66th minute.

However, the Panthers were not ready to go down without a fight as Lalnun Siami slashed a ball past the Tigresses defence to Sree Lakshmi A who scored the consolation goal for the Panthers. Three minutes later, Siami, who was a standout performer for Panthers, scored the final goal of the game to make it 5-2. Tigress will now face Lionesses for the final group match before both teams face in the title clash on Sunday.
