General election: Actor Eddie Marsan brands Lib Dems 'political enema' as he attacks Labour

The film star drew laughter with his comment about why he voted Lib Dem - but his message about anti-Semitism cases was deadly serious

14 Nov 2019, 11:53 by Nicola Bartlett, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/nicola-bartlett/

Film star Eddie Marsan today joined the Lib Dems on the campaign trail - claiming voting for them is like having an enema.

The Gangs of New York actor prompted laughter at an event in London as he described voting Liberal Democrat as a “political enema” telling the audience “it feels good”.

But there was a serious side to his intervention too - as he gave a moving speech about fighting racism.

He spoke about how he had voted Labour since Neil Kinnock made his iconic speech at the Labour Party conference in 1985 about kicking out militant.

Marsan, who grew up in a working class community in Tower Hamlets, said he was not able to “hold my nose” and vote for Labour with Jeremy Corbyn in charge because of his record on anti-Semitism.

 

He said of the Labour leader who criticised the removal of an anti-Semitic mural in 2012 - “you are either antisemitic or you are ignorant”.

He said: “Either way that should disqualify you from being the leader of the Labour Party and the Prime Minister.”

At the time the mural emerged, Jeremy Corbyn said he was "sincerely sorry" for the pain caused by "pockets" of anti-Semitism in Labour.

Mr Corbyn added his 2012 comment on Facebook was about free speech, he had not looked at the mural properly, and that on second glance it was clearly anti-Semitic and it was right to remove it.

 

Labour officials have since insisted the dealing of anti-Semitism cases - which make up less than 0.1% of Labour members - has been sped up and improved.

The actor, who left school at 15, described how he had often listened to far more educated people spouting anti-Semitism describing it as a “seductive racism for the left.”

Adopting a strong cockney accent he demonstrated how racism could sound intellectual or terrify depending on how was saying it.

But he said it did not seem so academic when it ended up with people being abused or living in fear.

He spoke about the abuse that Luciana Berger had suffered as he introduced his friend who is running to be Lib Dem MP for Finchley and Golders Green - a seat which has a large proportion of Jewish voters.

Berger described the difficult path she says she has been forced down because of the change in Labour over the last few years.

Speaking slowly and deliberately she told an audience in central London: “Never did I think I would be forced out of the Labour party because of anti Jewish racism but that is what has happened - and I’m not the only one.”

“Jeremy Corbyn has changed everything. I went from being a Labour MP to being seen as a Jewish MP”

She added: "I will tell you at the intersection of anti-Semitism and misogyny you will find a quantity and a quality of hatred which is worse than one plus the other.”

She called on the Equality and Human Rights Commission to publish their investigation into the party before the election so that voters know what they’re opting for.

Speaking directly to those considering backing her former party she asked: “How can you vote for this? What message does that send?”

The former Labour MP was speaking as her new party announced their plan to uphold equalities and human rights.

The Liberal Democrats have promised to protect places of worship from hate crimes and terror attacks if they win the general election . The party is promising more funding for security - and also says it would bring in tougher sentences for hate crime. Home Office figures released last month showed a 10% rise in hate crimes- offences motivated by hostility towards someone's race, religion, sexual orientation, disability or transgender identity - in England and Wales in 2018/19.

The Liberal Democrats say they will turn all such crimes into "aggravated offences", meaning maximum sentences would rise.

Just under half of religious hate crime offences were targeted against Muslims in 2018/19, the Home Office said, and 18% were targeted against Jewish people.

Liberal Democrat foreign affairs spokesman Chuka Umunna said: "This election like no other in recent times is about the kind of country we want to live in".

Umunna said the vote is a choice between an "open, liberal" society and "the forces of reaction".
