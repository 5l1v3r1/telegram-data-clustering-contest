RB Leizpig boss clears Timo Werner to leave amid Man Utd and Liverpool transfer interest

Timo Werner has been linked with a transfer move to Man Utd or Liverpool - and RB Leipzig boss Julian Nagelsmann says they will not stop him leaving for bigger things

14 Nov 2019, 11:54 by Rich Jones, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/rich-jones/

RB Leipzig boss Julian Nagelsmann has opened the door for Timo Werner to leave the club - by stating they will not stand in his way if a bigger club comes calling.

Werner has been attracting plenty of interest as a result of an impressive record of 76 goals in just 130 Bundesliga games.

The 23-year-old is considered one of the top talents in Europe with Manchester United , Liverpool and domestic rivals Bayern Munich all linked with a move.

And Nagelsmann has poured fuel on speculation he could be set for an exit by declaring they will not block an exit if he gets the right offer in January.

"I’ve already advised many players to take off their wedding ring with their current club to move somewhere else," said Nagelsmann.

"I’d do the same with Timo Werner if he were developing faster than the team at RB Leipzig."

Werner inked a new deal at Leipzig at the summer, but that includes a release clause.

Some reports in Germany have claimed that clause is as low as £27m, but others suggest it is over £50m and designed to reduce year upon year as he gets deeper into his four-year contract.

Sporting director Markus Krosche did address transfer speculation, however, and warned United and Liverpool they do not intend to sell.

Krosche told Bild : "Timo just extended his contract with us recently, and we're not thinking about letting him go at all."

United are known to be considering attacking options after struggling for goals so far this season with Erling Braut Haaland and Mario Mandzukic amongst those also linked with moves.

But Klopp could bring in Werner as an addition to their powerful front three to add an extra dimension plus more squad depth with a hectic schedule coming up.
