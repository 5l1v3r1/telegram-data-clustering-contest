Cafe closure blamed on parking enforcement causing downturn in trade

Customers refused to return after being fined £60

14 Nov 2019, 11:15 by Lynette Pinchess

A cafe at a picturesque marina is closing its doors from today (November 14) blaming a drop-off in trade after customers were stung with £60 parking fines.

The owners said it was "with a heavy heart" but they have been struggling for months since the new parking enforcement system was introduced at Sawley Marina near Long Eaton - the UK's biggest inland marina.

The Marina Cafe provided a welcome break for walkers, cyclists and families as well as boating people, serving hearty breakfasts, burgers, fish and chips and pizzas.

British Waterways Marinas Limited, which has its head office at the Sawley site, introduced a new system in April, with three hours free parking. Visitors, who failed to see signs and stayed for longer without paying in the office, were fined.

The cafe owners had already reduced its hours for the winter period, opening Thursday to Sunday, when making the decision to close.

Scores of customers said they were "gutted" by the news, which was announced in a Facebook post, saying: "It's time to say goodbye.

"It is with a heavy heart that we have taken the difficult decision to close our doors from Thursday 14th November 2019.

"In April 2019, our landlord BWML installed a Parking Enforcement System. Since this was introduced we have seen a massive downturn in trade, so much so that it is no longer viable to stay open."

One dissatisfied customer left a 'terrible' Facebook review in September after being stung with a £60 parking fine.

They complained: "DO NOT VISIT!!! Unless you want a £60 parking ticket.

"I have been to the cafe on numerous occasions for breakfast at the weekend. One Sunday we decided to have a coffee after breakfast and enjoy the sun outside. However, apparently if you stay too long, and spend more money at the marina, you are slammed with a parking fine of £60.

"Appalling way to treat paying customers, who are spending more money in the marina. Not worth the hassle, do not visit this place!!"

Another customer Tony Cella complained on Facebook: "I certainly will never set foot, sail or use any of the services of any BWML marina again.

"Ten of us travelled up for breakfast last month. I didn't see the new signage about the car parking. Lovely breakfast and walk round the marina... end result 3hrs 13min and £60 parking charge in the post. "

More than 140 fans mourned the loss of the cafe.

Shaz Zenith responded: "No way?! You are such an integral part of why it's so lovely at the Marina!! I hope the landlord sees a downturn in profits from people not going there and needing to use the parking. Pure selfishness and such a loss."

Jane Russell posted: "Oh no, this is bad, what a greedy landlord. We loved going there for Sunday lunch and a little walk with the grandchildren. So sorry for the staff."

Becky Henry said it was the best breakfast around. "Omg this is awful! I'm absolutely gutted. You will be so missed. Excellent service, every single time. I hope you find somewhere else to open as your customers will follow. Good luck."

Fay Rumley said she had never tipped anyone as highly as at the Marina Cafe, adding: "The staff are exceptionally hard working. I am so sorry this has happened to you all, it is not a reflection on your work ethic or dedication."

Nottinghamshire Live has contacted British Waterways Marinas Limited for comment.
