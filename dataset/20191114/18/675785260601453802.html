Hector Bellerin monitoring how Arsenal's season plays out amid transfer interest from Italy

The Spanish defender is a long-term target for Juventus and Bellerin's agent has admitted one club has already been in contact over a potential move

14 Nov 2019, 18:31 by Darren Wells, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/darren-wells/

Hector Bellerin's agent has stated his client is 'seeing how the season goes' at Arsenal after receiving transfer interest from Italy.

The Gunners full-back has only recently returned to regular action after suffering a serious knee injury back in January.

Arsenal have struggled so far this season, despite their current standing of sixth in the Premier League table.

And with potential options opening up in Serie A for Bellerin, it seems he may well be inclined to jump ship next summer.

Speaking to Sky Sports Italia, Bellerin's agent Alberto Botines said: "At the moment, he’s focused on the team's commitments.

 

“He comes from a bad injury and is returning to his usual level. He likes Italy, there’s already been interest from an Italian club but I can’t reveal it.

“He has a long contract and it won’t be easy to take him away from Arsenal, he’s their vice-captain.

“We’ll see how the season goes.”

Juventus are long-term admirers of the Spaniard, who joined Arsenal's youth system in 2011 from Barcelona.

Bellerin has developed into a key figure at the Emirates, hence boss Unai Emery's decision to make him part of the captain's group after Granit Xhaka's outburst cost him the armband.

Speaking at the time, Bellerin said: "It’s been a tough couple of weeks in terms of the results but I always say the thing with football and especially in a club like Arsenal is you have the chance to redeem yourself every two, three days."

Arsenal have opportunities to turn their fortunes around after the international break, with a string of fixtures that could prove pivotal to Emery's job.

They start with home clashes against relegation battlers Southampton, followed by Eintracht Frankfurt in the Europa League, before a trip to bottom club Norwich.

Brighton then travel to the Emirates before an away tie with West Ham, both of whom are currently in the bottom half.
