Sixth former's work on climate change has led to academy launching scheme to ditch plastic

Nathaniel McLaughlin, 16, has led a host of projects on the issue

14 Nov 2019, 18:58 by Matthew Bunn

At just 16 years of age, inspirational sixth former Nathaniel McLaughlin has already led a host of impressive projects to tackle climate change - not just at his school but within the local community.

Thanks to the Sneinton student’s tireless research and hard work, Nottingham Academy is introducing a new scheme to completely eliminate plastic across all its sites by spring term 2020

Nathaniel has also helped create a new greener waste disposal system at the academy, as well as working with local schools and businesses to increase awareness and share new eco ideas.

And to top it all, Nathaniel has recently been announced as one of 50 new ambassadors for the national campaign #iWill - a scheme run by the Prince of Wales to empower dynamic young people to take part in positive social action.

No wonder he’s also been nominated in the ‘keeping it green’ category of this year’s Nottinghamshire Live Education Awards.

Nathaniel, who is studying for A levels in geography, sociology and philosophy at Nottingham Academy, first became interested in the environment three years ago when he watched a series of videos on the internet showing the stark impact of climate change on the planet.

“I guess it was that which led to me to want to tackle the issue head on,” he explained.

Inspired by science teacher Sarjit Gidda - who won last year’s ‘inspirational teacher of the year’ category at the Education Awards - Nathaniel had already become a keen diversity and equality campaigner.

Deciding to use the skills he’d developed for green issues seemed the next logical step.

He said: “The first thing I really tackled was the use of plastic in the canteens at school.

“I wanted to reduce plastic use and I had to do my research and then present my ideas for a compostable alternative to members of the senior team at the academy and in front of the education trust.”

Thanks to Nathaniel’s determination, the academy is now committed to having zero plastic across all sites by spring term 2020.

Working alongside big organisations such as Ward Recycling and Severn Trent, he is also organising the introduction of commercial composting and improved waste disposal systems at school.

“We’re introducing a totally new waste disposal system, turning food waste into fertiliser," Nathaniel explained.

“We’re going to have compostable packaging in canteens and we’ll hopefully have recycling bins and composting bins across all sites by 2021.”

Since early 2019, the teenager has been working with a number of businesses to lessen their environmental impact - with The Avenues Cafe in Sneinton currently taking advantage of his tips on introducing greener ways of working.

At the same time, Nathaniel has teamed up with art education organisation PicMeUp Shuffle and Nottingham City Council to push for on-street recycling bins in Bakersfield.

And he’s even helped deliver lessons to primary school pupils about how their actions affect the environment.

Nathaniel says he gets a little nervous having to stand up in front of panels and organisations to present his ideas - but it’s worth it when he gets one of his schemes up and running.

“It’s nerve-racking but the teams here at Nottingham Academy are very open minded and they have a great outlook for the future,” he said.

As for his new ambassador role, Nathaniel is looking forward to making a real difference, with new opportunities to meet top decision makers and CEOs of large companies.

He said: “It’s going to be great to attend more events and raise the profile of environmental issues to more people.”

Fellow student Jasmine Karakoyunlu was amongst those nominating Nathaniel for the Nottinghamshire Live Education Awards 2019.

She said: “Nathaniel is a hard working, determined and ambitious individual and I admire him for all the work he has done. He has a passion for his projects and this is shown throughout. He is an inspiration and definitely deserves this award.”

Nathaniel said: “It’s overwhelming to be nominated and I’m very grateful. It’s not really the point of my work but also it’s always great to be recognised.”
