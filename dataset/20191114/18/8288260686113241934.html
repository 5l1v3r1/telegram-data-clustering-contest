These roads are at risk of flooding in Stourbridge

Pictures show how high the water levels had already risen earlier this afternoon

14 Nov 2019, 18:06 by Charlotte Regen, https://www.birminghammail.co.uk/authors/charlotte-regen/

A flood warning has been issued on the River Stour amid fears it could burst its banks.

Pictures obtained by Black Country Live show the worst may have already happened in Dog Kennel Lane, Halesowen.

The images show the water has turned a murky brown with the rising level covering a tunnel opening.

You can view the pictures in the gallery below:

 

A warning posted on the .Gov website, told residents to take 'immediate action'. The update said: "River levels are rising at the Stourbridge river gauge as a result of the persistent heavy rainfall today (November 14). Consequently, flooding of property/roads and farmland is possible this evening.

"Flooding is affecting properties around Prestwood, Bells Mill, Rushall Close, Morrow Way, Bradley Road, Canal Street and Mill Race Lane.

"Further light rainfall is forecast this evening, then. We expect river levels to begin falling over night. We are closely monitoring the situation. Our incident response staff are actively checking river levels and the weather forecast.

 

"Please avoid using low lying footpaths and bridges near local watercourses and plan driving routes to avoid low lying roads near rivers, which may be flooded. This message will be updated this evening, or as the situation changes."

A yellow rain warning is in place across the Black Country and is expected to last until 11.45pm tonight.

Tomorrow's weather is said to be windy with isolated shower. Brighter spells will develop through the afternoon as the cloud thins and breaks. Maximum temperature will be up to 7 °C - according to the Met Office.

What does a yellow weather warning for rain mean?

Damage to some buildings, fast flowing or deep floodwater is possible, causing a danger to life and some communities may be cut off by flooded roads.

What to expect

Spray and flooding could lead to difficult driving conditions and some road closures as well as possible power cuts and loss of other services to some homes and businesses.

Where flooding occurs, there is a slight chance of delays or cancellations to train and bus services. There is a small chance that homes and businesses could be flooded, causing damage to some buildings.

There is a small chance of fast flowing or deep floodwater causing danger to life.

There is a small chance that some communities become cut off by flooded roads, along with power cuts and loss of other services to some homes and businesses.
