The Newcastle University student who developed an app to support the West End Food Bank

Masitano Sichone, 36, has launched an app that lets community food banks send out notifications when essential items are low

14 Nov 2019, 18:00 by Rachael Nichol

As the cold winter months draw closer, food banks are facing an increased demand to help struggling North East families.

After volunteering at the West End Food Bank, Masitano Sichon saw first hand how it is helping people get by.

Now, the Newcastle University graduate has launched an app to help the food bank through the festive period.

Users who download the app get notifications when the food bank is low on essential items.

Masitano, who has a master's degree in in E-Business, said: "While volunteering I noticed that there were certain types of food that came in that they didn't have a lot of.

"They had no way to exactly tell people what they needed to be donated.

"They do have wish lists in supermarkets but sometimes they don't get updated.

"I thought that in this world of tech, why don’t we have a more up-to-date digital solution.

"I came up with the idea of an app to replace their traditional methods and to create an extra channel to get more food."

The app, called Spareable, allows food banks to notify people which essentials they are low on.

People can then purchase the items to make a donation which are then delivery directly from wholesalers to the food banks.

In 2017, Masitano pitched his idea at a boot camp event at Newcastle University as part of their START UP programme which offers support to students and recent graduates.

And after winning the competition, the budding entrepreneur was awarded a foundership from the university who provided him with support and funding to help them bring his ideas to life over the next six months.

After launching in August this year, Spareable has received an amazing response with more than 300 people using it and as a result the food donations to West End Food Bank have increased by 25%.

John McCorry, chief executive at The West End Food Bank, said: "We are really grateful for every donation we receive but find that we do have a lack of donations of essential items such as long life milk, tinned rice or jams.

"We are now running into the busy months from November to February with the demand increasing by a third.

"Part of the reason for this is the demand on family budgets over the holiday period, people will pay for gifts for their children before eating.

"Masitano’s app has been really helpful as the app is useful for people with busy lifestyles and easy to use, and it has helped us to reach out to a new market of people."

Following the success of the app, Mansitano is now determined to reach out to more food banks across the UK.
