1 Saie Mascara 101

saiehello.com $24.00 SHOP NOW

A mascara has to do a couple of things in order for me to become a repeat customer: It has to go on smooth (not clumpy), make my lashes look supple (not super stiff), hold a curl, and not flake off by 4 p.m. This new formula does just that and it’s truly clean.

Two notables I’d love to call out: The formula’s main ingredient is ethically sourced beeswax, which leaves your lashes super malleable, and the entire company is clean (it doesn’t put ANY nasties or fillers in its products). Saie is super transparent about its ingredients too—go on its site, roll over any ingredient with your mouse, and you’ll instantly be told what it does and why it’s in the formula. I’m so impressed with this brand—other beauty companies should definitely take note!

soldejaneiro.com $40.00 SHOP NOW

So I’m all about the cult-favorite Bum Bum cream from Sol de Janeiro, but as someone with crazy-sensitive skin, I’ve never really been able to tolerate the OG’s fragrance or the shimmer. 4 Prose Dry Shampoo

prose.com $25.00 SHOP NOW

I recently found out I’m allergic to a fun lil ingredient called limonene: It smells really good—which is probs why it’s in all my favorite hair products—but it gives me a super-cute eczema rash all over my face (yay!). 

So when Prose launched its first dry shampoo—a powder formula that’s tailored to your exact hair type and concerns—I was so damn relieved to learn that it’s completely free of limonene and instead loaded with gentle ingredients like oil-absorbing rice starch and ultra-soothing aloe vera leaf. Better yet, it actually works (even on my grimy, four-day-old hair. Trust).

✨ Ruby Buddemeyer, beauty editor
