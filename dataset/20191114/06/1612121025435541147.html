John Legere might be pulled away from T-Mobile to take over at WeWork

14 Nov 2019, 06:30 by Jared Peters

If you’ve kept up with the rest of the internet lately, you’ve probably heard about WeWork. The company has struggled to create a sustainable business model and failed to go public, with its most recent debacle being a loss of over $1 billion in the third quarter of the year.

Going public might not be in WeWork’s future, but SoftBank did make a heavy investment in the company and has a short list of new potential CEOs for the office-sharing company. You might recognize SoftBank as the current major owners of Sprint, and you might recognize John Legere on that shortlist as T-Mobile’s current CEO. What a small world.

Legere isn’t definitely going to be the next CEO, but he is on the list that SoftBank is looking at. They want a high-profile, high-energy CEO to turn the company around, especially after a rocky few months. After taking over T-Mobile, Legere has certainly done an excellent job of building T-Mobile up into an incredibly competitive wireless carrier after lagging behind for so many years. He’d fit the bill.

But he’s also in charge of T-Mobile, the company that’s currently in the middle of one of the biggest mergers ever in the wireless industry. With SoftBank looking to have a new CEO start in January, it would look terrible for Legere to dip out right after most of the approval process has finished for the Sprint/T-Mobile acquisition. That’s not a great look on a resume, and it might give both parties pause about actually wanting that merger.

Take it all with a grain of salt, but we might have a very interesting end to 2019.
