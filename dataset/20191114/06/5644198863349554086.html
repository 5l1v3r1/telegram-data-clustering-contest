US Senate to hold November 20 hearing on testing, deployment of self-driving cars

14 Nov 2019, 06:25

The U.S. Senate Commerce Committee will hold a November 20 hearing on the testing and deployment of self-driving vehicles that will include top U.S. safety officials as Congress has struggled to pass legislation on autonomous vehicles.

WASHINGTON: The U.S. Senate Commerce Committee will hold a November 20 hearing on the testing and deployment of self-driving vehicles that will include top U.S. safety officials as Congress has struggled to pass legislation on autonomous vehicles.

The hearing will come one day after the National Transportation Safety Board (NTSB) meets to determine the probable cause of a March 2018 Uber Technologies Inc self-driving vehicle crash that killed a pedestrian in Arizona.
