Former Plymouth Argyle manager Ian Holloway on chances of I'm A Celebrity appearance

Former football manager Harry Redknapp proved a huge hit with fans and went on to win in 2018

14 Nov 2019, 14:49 by Jak Ball

Former Plymouth Argyle manager Ian Holloway has revealed that he has not been asked to appear in I'm A Celebrity Get Me Out of Here .

Whilst the 56-year-old might not have had the call for the 2019 series he hasn't ruled out flying down under in the future.

Holloway has admitted to being a big fan of the show and would likely prove to be a big hit with viewers, as did eventual king of the jungle Harry Redknapp last year.

Former Arsenal, Crystal Palace and England striker Ian Wright is the ex-football man to get a place in the camp this year.

He will compete in Bushtucker Trials alongside the likes of Caitlyn Jenner, Nadine Coyle, James Haskell and Kate Garraway.

Holloway admitted it would be a tough task to enter the jungle in 2020, following on from Redknapp and Wright.

"As a person, I would have hated to have competed with him [Ian Wright] because he’s such a wonderful fella, he’s so lively, he’s so enthusiastic. He’s a great bloke.

"You wouldn’t want to be the third person to go on there from football when Harry Redknapp and Ian Wright both won and you then lose!

"I’m a big fan of the show. It’s good for anyone who wants to lose a bit of weight.

"I watched bits of it last year because everybody was talking about it and I thought Harry (Redknapp) was absolutely himself, his relationship with his wife is brilliant and he deserved to win it."

Holloway, who managed the Pilgrims between 2006/07, has said that the eating challenges wouldn't faze him as long as what he was eating was dead.

Jumping out of a plane also wouldn't be a problem for him, whereas trials that involved snakes would be his biggest struggle.

"They didn’t ask me did they? I think my son already put my name in it," Holloway told The Sack Race.

"I’m not being funny but I was with Colin Murray a few months ago and he said ‘You should go on that’ and the people at Talksport think I should go on that because they know how afraid I am of snakes, they will find it so funny.

"I’m absolutely terrified of snakes. I don’t really mind spiders but if I had to eat those bugs then I’d throw up. 

"Regarding snakes, I remember Shaun Ryder being bitten by one. That would have terrified me, I would have fainted.

"Crocodiles? Yeah I’m bound to be bloody scared of them because they can eat you can’t they.

"I wouldn’t worry about balls, they don’t bother me. It’s the things that are still alive that bother me or eyeballs, I would barf at that.

"I have this reaction to anything I don’t like and I used to be really fussy as a kid, so I think I would struggle for a lot of reasons."

And on heights, Holloway added: "I don’t know, because I’m so short I probably won’t be used to them.

"I wouldn’t worry about jumping out a plane. That would be a challenge I love that."
