EastEnders actress Tamzin Outhwaite 'emotional' as Mel Owen killed off in crash horror

EastEnders actress Tamzin Outhwaite has played Mel Owen since 1998, but her character was killed off this week

14 Nov 2019, 23:28 by Charlotte Tutton

EastEnders viewers were left reeling as Mel Owen was killed off in tragic scenes on Thursday, moments after a deadly crash.

Tamzin Outhwaite bowed out of the role, with the character introduced to the BBC soap in 1998.

Mel had only returned to Walford last year after initially departing in 2002, following the death of husband Steve Owen.

In her final moments, still grieving the loss of her son Hunter who was killed during the siege in September, Mel stepped out into the path of an incoming lorry.

She had just been saved from a burning car wreck by enemy Sharon Mitchell, only to be killed off in the same episode just as fans believed she was going to be okay.

After the episode aired on BBC, actress Tamzin thanked fans for their support as she spoke of the death for the first time.

Having filmed her final scenes weeks ago, keeping quiet until now, she took to Twitter to address her final episode.

She said: "Thank you all ... tonight thankfully I’m busy as I’m too emotional to address any @bbceastenders messages tonight... but..." with a praying emoji.

Viewers raced to comment under the message, praising the character for her time on the soap.

Mel has been at the centre of some huge storylines since her original stint and her more recent stay in Walford.

From her doomed wedding to Ian Beale back in 1999 and the loss of husband Steve Owen in 2002, to the more recent tragedy - she's faced a lot of heartache.

Earlier this year her latest husband Ray Kelly was killed by her son Hunter after he tried to murder her.

Then Hunter was killed after he took hostages in The Queen Vic, with him shot dead by police as he tried to kill pregnant Louise Mitchell - having already shot her brother Ben and fiancé Keanu Taylor.

In the past weeks she's battled with her grief for her only child, desperate for revenge on the Mitchells.

This revenge and the struggle to get over Hunter's death ultimately led to her own demise in heartbreaking scenes.

EastEnders airs Mondays and Fridays at 8pm, and Tuesdays and Thursdays at 7:30pm on BBC One.
