Man uses machete to hold up newsagency in Murray Bridge | Six arrested over Flinders Park brek-in

14 Nov 2019, 20:10

A man is on the run after using a machete to hold up a newsagency just after 5pm yesterday. Meanwhile, six people have been arrested over an armed break-in in Adelaide's west.

The man, wearing a grey hoodie and with his face covered, entered the business on Swanport Rd about 5.15pm.

Police say he threatened staff with a machete and stole an amount of cash before running away east on Robe St.

Patrols searched the area, but could not find the man. Crime scene investigators and detectives attended the scene.

He is described as being about 175cm tall with a slim build. He was wearing black track pants, blue sneakers, black gloves and dark sunglasses.

If you have any information that may assist the investigation please contact Crime Stoppers South Australia on 1800 333 000 or report online - you can remain anonymous

SIX ARRESTED OVER ARMED BREAK-IN

Police have arrested six men over an aggravated break-in in Adelaide's western suburbs on Thursday.

Just before 4.30pm, it is alleged three men, supposedly armed with guns, forced their way into a home on Van Dieman St in Flinders Park.

Police say they ransacked the home, stealing a laptop, mobile phones and personal items before driving off in a small black vehicle.

It was last seen heading east on Grange Rd.

Later in the day, Western District Detectives attended a house in Kilburn, where they arrested six men in connection with the robbery.

The men, aged between 18 and 21, have all been charged with aggravated serious criminal trespass and theft.
