'Disappointed' - The Chelsea and Arsenal legends' verdicts Aston Villa fans will agree with

The Aston Villa defender was left out of the starting XI by Gareth Southgate

14 Nov 2019, 20:35 by Elliott Jackson, https://www.birminghammail.co.uk/authors/elliott-jackson/

Former Chelsea and Arsenal defenders Ashley Cole and Lee Dixon have both admitted they're "disappointed" not to see Tyrone Mings start for England against Montenegro.

The Aston Villa defender impressed for the Three Lions having made his debut against Bulgaria during the last international break.

However, John Stones was selected to replace Mings after his return to fitness, despite his indifferent form for Manchester City.

The former Everton defender joined Harry Maguire at the heart of the defence, with Trent Alexander-Arnold and Ben Chilwell at full-back.

However, having dealt manfully with off-the-pitch racist abuse during the trip to Bulgaria and putting in a strong performance, both Cole and Dixon believe that Mings should've started once again alongside Maguire against Montenegro.

 

Speaking to ITV before the match, Dixon said: "He (Stones) is a good player, there is no doubt about that but he has lost his way a little bit. Does he seem like the character that can get himself back to how good he was?

"He plays on confidence and at the minute he's in and out at Man City. I'm disappointed, we've just been talking about Mings not playing again.

 

"A second game, a run out at Wembley, to build a partnership and just see if whether he going to be the one to step into the gap if there are injuries. I'm a bit disappointed he's not playing tonight."

Cole, who has 107 caps for England, added: "I think he played with quality, a little bit of arrogance, confidence.

"I think he fitted right in, he didn't look like a newbie to the international stage.

 

"We're probably a little bit disappointed he didn't play again today."
