'Do not travel unless absolutely necessary' warn transport cops amid flooding, landslides and city centre gridlock

The stark warning comes after almost 24-hours of non-stop torrential rain, leading to chaos on the road and rail network

14 Nov 2019, 20:36 by Charlotte Paxton, https://www.facebook.com/charlottepaxtonbm/

Police and train bosses have issued a stark warning to commuters after almost 24-hours of torrential rain sparked chaos on the road and rail network.

British Transport Police, together with Network Rail, urged people not to travel 'unless absolutely necessary' as Birmingham continues to be battered by heavy rain.

The adverse weather has had led to many trains being delayed or cancelled, after rail lines were left impassable due to floodwater and landslides.

British Transport Police Birmingham tweeted: "BTP and Network Rail strongly advise people not to travel today unless it's absolutely necessary.

"Due to adverse weather, a vast amount of trains are being cancelled or delayed due to flooding and landslides."

And workers attempting to head home for the night told of chaos in the city centre, with gridlocked traffic.

 

Pictures taken from the car park at Snow Hill show solid queues of traffic heading out of Birmingham.

One man told us: "My car's in Snow Hill, but the traffic hasn't moved for 90 minutes. It's chaos.

"The city centre is in total gridlock. I saw one woman storming out of Snow Hill, leaving her car. She said she'd not moved for an hour."

A number of roads across the city were shut altogether, including the appropriately-named Watery Lane in Solihulll.

 

West Midlands Police tweeted it was shut from Knowle to Chadwick End and a number of cars had been abandoned in the floodwater.

And a Mercedes was also engulfed in a rising ford in Hall Green.

The notorious flooding flashpoint swallowed up the pricey motor, when the driver tried - and failed - to cross it earlier today.

The car was left almost completely submerged at the site on Green Road, in Hall Green, despite red flashing signs saying 'caution - do not cross'.

 

Elsewhere, a string of Birmingham rail services have been impacted by today's torrential downpours.

The city has been pummelled by rampaging rainfall on Thursday, November 14, as floods sweep the area and the wider West Midlands.

The region is currently in the midst of a weather warning for rain with the Met Office warning of "danger to life" conditions in the city.

The alert, which affects swathes of the Midlands, is due to last until 11.45pm tonight.

The railway line between Hereford and Great Malvern is blocked.

West Midlands Railway confirmed the line has been battered by flooding and the dreadful conditions today and buses are being put on as a replacement.

At Snow Hill, passengers were being warned there are no trains between Shirley and Stratford.

And the line between Snow Hill and Kidderminster is flooded, causing lengthy delays.

There is expected to be a very brief respite in the rainfall tonight, before it starts again at 9pm.

Thankfully, the current Met Office forecasts suggests tomorrow the rain will clear to leave overcast skies.

There will be grey skies through the final working day of the week.
