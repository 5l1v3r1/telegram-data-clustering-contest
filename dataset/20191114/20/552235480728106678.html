Queen's Park end their 152-year amateur status in historic vote

The League Two side held a historic vote on Monday to define the status of the club after over 150 years as an amateur side.

14 Nov 2019, 20:05 by Robbie Copeland

Queen's Park have voted to end their status as an amateur club, marking the start of a new era for the historic Glasgow side.

It was a landslide as club members voted in favour of turning professional, meaning there are now no amateur clubs among the 42 in the SPFL.

The club's motto 'Ludere Causa Ludendi', translating to 'To play for the sake of playing' stems back to its foundation in 1867, when they played a pivotal role in the formation of the Scottish leagues.

As clubs around them adapted their status to move with the times, however, the League Two side remained amateur into the modern era, and were to date the only amateur club in the senior leagues.

There were concerns for the sustainability for Queen’s Park as an amateur entity, however. Alack of compensation for players such as Andy Robertson and Lawrence Shankland led club members to consider going professional, in a bid to protect the club from losing their top players free of charge.

 

Club president Gerry Crawley was among those to call for a vote on going professional back in July, and after the issue proved divisive among club members and fans alike, members were invited to decide the matter democratically on Thursday.

The result of the vote means that the club will be permitted to sign players to part-time and full-time contracts for the first time in their history.

The Glasgow club were once one of Scottish football's driving forces, winning ten Scottish cups - less than only Celtic and Rangers - but now find themselves in the basement tier of the SPFL.

Queen's Park find themselves amid a period of transition, with the vote coming ahead of a move out of Hampden Park from next season. They will play their games at Lesser Hampden from 2020 onwards.
