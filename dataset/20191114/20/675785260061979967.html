Vicky Pattison, Catherine Tyldesley, and Billi Mucklow lead the stars putting up Christmas trees early

Celebs are bringing out their best baubles in the battle of the tree bling as they flout tradition and deck the halls extremely early

14 Nov 2019, 20:06 by Tiffany Wallis

Christmas may be a whole 41 days away but that hasn't deterred celebrities from already assembling their trees.

It's beginning to look a lot like Christmas in the homes of Vicky Pattison , Catherine Tydesley, and Billi Mucklow.

The telly personalities are among the stars who have decked their homes out for the festive season extremely early following Jodie Marsh 's festive transformation back in October.

Former Geordie Shore star Vicky, 31, shared a snap of her towering Christmas tree dressed with shimmering silver baubles, pine cones and fairy lights to her 4.5 million Instagram followers yesterday.

Showing the magic of the festive season had reached every corner of her swanky abode, the TV icon told her fans that her "gorgeous loft apartment" has been transformed into a "winter wonderland".

The snap also teased a glimpse at Vicky's incredible skyline view through her huge glass windows which were decorated with a glitzy garland.

Also preparing to celebrate the festive season, recently axed Strictly contestant Catherine , 36, shared a series of snaps of her glittering tree to social media.

Showing Christmas spirit had already arrived at her family home, other photos showed her traditional door wreath and festive candle holders.

Her glowing tree featured dramatic star decorations as well as huge reindeer figurines sitting in between branches.

Unapologetic about putting up her tree almost seven weeks before December 25, the star wrote: "Accuse me of being premature...Christmas makes us happy!"

The soap star said the sparkling creation wasn't due to her hard work but thanked an amazing team of "elves", while adding she has a separate family tree elsewhere in the house.

Essex babe Billi Mucklow also debuted her home's festive transformation to social media this week.

The ex-TOWIE star, 31, wowed fans with a photo of her incredible Christmas tree, courtesy of a design company that came to her house to install it.

The flamboyant tree screamed Christmas excitement and featured candy canes, elves and delicious sweet treats.

Decorated to a "Willy Wonka and Elves" theme, Billi insisted the extravagant tree was something "fun for the kids."

With a traditional red, green and white colour scheme, the pretty tree was assembled with cheeky elf legs hanging from the branches, while a green leafy garland in the background also boasted a mini-elf character.

Taking the crown for the earliest Christmas decorations came courtesy of Jodie Marsh, 40, who put her tree up 11 weeks early.

The former glamour model left fans totally shocked as she transformed her Essex home to exude extravagant Christmas spirit and festive flair as early as October.

The CBB star showed off how she turned her home into a very convincing Santa's grotto with numerous Christmas trees, a gigantic elf in an armchair and an elaborate Christmas village with music, lights and moving ornaments.

Jodie gave viewers a glimpse of the 'village' with intricate Christmas train sets weaving their way around her lounge, artificial snow and a creepy Jigsaw character.

She is well known for her love of kickstarting Christmas early and last October she was also mocked for putting up her decorations early.

Christmas also appeared to arrive early in the house of mummy-to-be Lydia Bright .

The former The Only Way Is Essex star asked her 1.1million Instagram followers on if she was “too early” to be putting up her tree.

Lydia shared a video of herself sporting some very festive pyjamas as she was getting into the Xmas spirit before Bonfire Night.

The star must have questioned her family's eager festive haul as she admitted “So, we know we’re premature, we’re putting up the Christmas tree.”

The final star to jump on the early Christmas tree bandwagon is Formula 1 heiress Tamara Ecclestone .

The socialite, 35, put up her Christmas decorations this week alongside her husband Jay Rutland, 38, and their daughter Sophia, five.

Their London mansion was transformed into a winter wonderland as they decorated a giant Christmas tree with white, gold and silver ornaments.

Dressed in festive red pyjamas, little Sophia had to stand on a step ladder to reach the tree so she could hang up the decorations.

Traditionally it is socially acceptable to put your decorations up at the beginning of December but they must come down before January 5.

A lot of celebs seem to be flouting tradition this year as their homes continue to turn into winter wonderlands earlier and earlier.
