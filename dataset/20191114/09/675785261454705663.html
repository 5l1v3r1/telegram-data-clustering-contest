Prince Harry shares new Archie photo to mark Charles's birthday - and Kate Middleton posts Louis snap

Prince Harry shared the photo on the Instagram page he shares with Meghan Markle, alongside a cute caption for Prince Charles's birthday

14 Nov 2019, 09:34 by Zoe Forsey, https://www.facebook.com/zoeforseymirror/

Prince Harry has shared a new photo of his son Archie to celebrate Prince Charles's 71st birthday.

The Duke shared the black and white photo, taken at Archie's christening earlier this year, on Instagram .

The caption reads: "Happy birthday to His Royal Highness The Prince of Wales - Sir, Pa, Grandpa!"

The photo shows Harry holding his little boy whole proud grandad Charles looks down at him.

The Duke and Duchess of Cambridge also marked the occasion by sharing a photo, opting for one of the birthday boy with their youngest child Prince Louis.

The snap was one of the official ones taken to celebrate the heir to the throne's 70th birthday last year.

Their caption reads: "Wishing a very happy Birthday to The Prince of Wales!"

Harry and wife Meghan Markle broke with tradition at Archie's christening back in July by not inviting members of the press to photograph them arriving at the service.

However they released a number of official shots and have treated fans to even more over the last few months - including the new one today.

Prince Charles is celebrating his birthday India, where he is currently completing a Royal Tour.

Yesterday, he hitched a ride on an electric rickshaw and flipped chapatis in a Sikh temple as he celebrated Indian culture and its people.

The Prince of Wales - Charles, a prominent climate change campaigner - began a hectic two-day trip by learning about India’s efforts to combat the dense smog that covers New Delhi most autumns.

He was taken for a spin in a non-polluting battery powered three-wheeler rickshaw after visiting India’s equivalent of the Met Office in the capital New Delhi.

Later he was mobbed by visitors when he visited the Bangla Sahib Gurdwara Sikh Temple to celebrate the 550th anniversary of the birth of Guru Nanak, the founder of Sikhism.

Driving the rickshaw was Maria who has benefited from a programme, run by SMV Green, a sustainable transport organisation, which is training some of India’s first female rickshaw drivers.

Ahead of the two day visit Charles posted his first personal message on the royal family’s Instagram page.

In a message to Britain’s Sikh community, and others in the Commonwealth, he wrote: “The principles on which Guru Nanak founded the Sikh religion, and which guide your lives to this day, are ones which can inspire us all.”
