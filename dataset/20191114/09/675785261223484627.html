England v Montenegro prediction and odds as in-form striker Marcus Rashford's hot streak set to continue

Your best bets for Thursday night's international football action

14 Nov 2019, 09:53 by Dave Prentice

Montenegro's nickname is the Brave Falcons, but the Balkan birds of prey have had their wings clipped even before Thursday's trip to Wembley.

Devoured 5-1 by the Three Lions in March, they head to Wembley without the majority of their first choice defence.

Six of the seven defenders in the Montenegran squad boast no more than seven international caps - and that has to influence our punting on the match.

Of course England will also be missing Raheem Sterling, but that just means that Jadon Sancho and Marcus Rashford are likely to flank Harry Kane up front.

And England should score goals.

All five of England's group wins so far have seen them winning at half-time, so the half-time/full-time prediction is a slim 4/11 .

But more reliable is England to win and over 3.5 goals, which is is 4/5 , but even better is England -3 handicap at 13/10 .

So who will score the goals?

Marcus Rashford is bang in form, witness that filthy piece of skill which Match of the Day 2 highlighted against Brighton, and has scored six goals in seven games since opening the scoring against Bulgaria in October.

He's 23/10 to score first at Wembley.

Republic of Ireland are also in action the same night, in an international friendly against New Zealand.

Just days before a crunch tie with Denmark, Mick McCarthy will rotate his squad and blood some youngsters.

The Kiwis are on a solid run of six games unbeaten .... but New Caledonia, Papua New Guinea and Vanuatu, currently ranked 155, 165 and 163 in the world, were hardly likely to threaten.

And that run kicked off with a 13-0 demolition of Tonga.

The Republic are currently 36th in the world, to New Zealand's 121 ranking.

So 4/7 is a solid enough price for the home win, but don't expect a goalfest.

Twelve of the Republic’s last 16 home matches have seen fewer than three goals scored, and McCarthy’s side have averaged just 0.7 goals per game since the beginning of 2018.

That tallies well with New Zealand's record on the road (only six of their last 16 trips have yielded three or more) so under 2.5 goals at 3/4 is the value call.

But best call of all is Republic to win and Under 2.5 goals, which is a great 11/4 price .
