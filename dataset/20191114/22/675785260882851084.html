Kerry Katona shows off tiny waist after losing a stone with controversial jabs

Kerry has been showing off her weight loss on her social media profile which she credits to Skinnyjab injections

14 Nov 2019, 22:49 by Lily Waddell, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/lily-waddell/

Kerry Katona displayed her incredibly slender figure after taking Skinnyjab injections.

The singer, 39, has lost more than one stone in four weeks and she has credited her weight loss to the controversial hunger-curbing jabs on social media.

With incredible confidence, the songstress posted her envy-inducing snap on her Instagram account on Thursday.

Kerry showcased her toned figure in a sky blue crop top and matching bottoms while she posed in her gym gear.

The blonde bombshell had spent the whole day modelling up a storm for Skinnyjabs as she worked on collaborating with the weight loss product.

A lot of her fans were heaping tonnes of praise for the reality star in the comments.

"Omg you look fab," one fan wrote, leading the troops.

Another added: "You look amazing and happy."

Others filed: "Love this Lady.. Changed my whole mindset. love the Colour." (sic)

Previously, Kerry has documented her fitness journey after she begun taking the weight loss jabs.

She wrote on Instagram: "Soooooo I would like to reveal my amazing secret with you all, I have been doing SkinnyJab for 4 weeks now & I have lost over a stone doing the weekly programme! I feel amazing, fit & healthy.

"The dietary programme & aftercare is fantastic and super easy, I’m truly thrilled with Caroline & her amazing team." (sic)

The cost of SkinnyJab begins with an initial three-week plan costing £250 and top-up treatments are priced at £135 each.

According to the SkinnyJab website, the injection works by "removing hunger from the equation" while regulating blood sugar levels and helping the body to turn fat into energy.

Recently, Gemma Collins has also credited her weight loss to SkinnyJab injections.

Kerry returned to the red carpet and to her modelling duties after she was rushed to hospital with a mystery illness.

In the wake of her hospital visit, she has discussed her health and fears she has endometriosis.

It is a condition where tissue similar to the lining of the womb starts to grow in other places such as the ovaries and fallopian tubes.

In early November, she wrote: "You may have seen me out and about looking very uncomfortable - it's because I'm still in such awful pain with my back. I can barely stand up straight. I'm sick of crying about it, but it's really restricting me.

"I'm going to a clinic later this month to see a gynae specialist, but early indications suggest it's endometriosis, which can take years to diagnose."
