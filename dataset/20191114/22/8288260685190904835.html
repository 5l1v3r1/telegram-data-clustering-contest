'Freedom' - The Leeds United change for Helder Costa after Wolves transfer

Latest Wolves news | Costa delivered an improved performance for Marcelo Bielsa's side last weekend

14 Nov 2019, 22:11 by Shane Ireland, https://www.facebook.com/ShaneIrelandJournalist

Danny Mills thinks a small tactical change from Marcelo Bielsa has taken the shackles off Helder Costa at Leeds United.

The winger produced one of his best performances this season as Leeds beat Blackburn Rovers 2-1 last Saturday to keep their Championship promotion bid on track.

Costa has struggled to make a real impact for the Whites so far after joining from Wolverhampton Wanderers and has been criticised by pundits.

But he gave a far more encouraging display against Rovers at Elland Road and Mills reckons it’s all down to a slight tweak from Bielsa.

“It was a bit more of a 3-4-3 formation, tweaked a little bit,” the former Leeds and England full-back told Football Insider.

“More or less with wing backs and of course that just allows Costa to play a little bit higher up the pitch and to use that pace and ability go forward – not just having to defend as a wing-back.

“Sometimes it’s those little changes, those small, little, tactical changes that can often help a player out, release you of your defensive duties and allows you freedom going forward.”

 

Costa signed for Leeds on a season-long loan from Wolves in the summer and he’ll become their record buy when the move is made permanent for £15m next year.

When Bielsa was asked for his verdict on Costa after the game, he said: “In the second half, better than in the first one.

“He had the ability of unbalance that he showed. He has to achieve more than just chances than those ones that he has got in the match.”
