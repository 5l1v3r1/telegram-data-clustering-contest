How North East leads the way when it comes to producing England talent

When it comes to population, the region is punching well above its weight for England stars

14 Nov 2019, 13:55 by Jerry Chen

North East has been a bedrock for producing a steady line of talented footballers for the national team for almost half a century.

With England playing their 1000th game tonight at Wembley against Montenegro, the newly released statistics for the games serves as a good reminder of North East contributions more so than ever.

Out of 1,244 players that have turned out for the Three Lions,145 of them hails from the North East which is the fourth highest total of any region in England behind the North West (280), Greater London (195) and the West Midlands (164).

It may not sound all that impressive but when you take into account of the North East’s population the region is indeed punching well above its weight.

Each of the 145 North East born England players plays for 17,947 individual currently living in the region.

The North West has the next best record with one player for every 25,302 residents followed by the West Midlands (one for every 34,618 people), the East Midlands (one for every 41,151 people) and Yorkshire and Humber (one for every 42,535 people).

On a county level, county Durham has the best record for each of it’s 46 England internationals work out for every 11,180 people of the county.

Lancashire comes next with one for every 15,069 residents, followed by Shropshire (17,134), Tyne and Wear (17,308) and South Yorkshire (18,032).

In terms of the town level, Mexborough comes out on top for anywhere to have produced at least five internationals.

The town has produced six England players over the years at a rate of one for every 2,541 people who currently live there.

Blackburn has the next best record with its 26 internationals working out at one for every 5,687 residents followed by Middlesbrough (one per 7,707 residents), Bolton (10,734) and Nottingham (11,425).

Sunderland on the other hand have each player plays for 11,490 locals while every one of internationals for South Shields works out for 15,067 of the population.
