Millionaire who left girlfriend to die after 'rough sex' could be freed early

Natalie Connolly, 27, was found with 40 injuries - including serious internal trauma - at the bottom of the stairs in the Staffordshire home she shared with tycoon John Broadhurst

14 Nov 2019, 03:08 by Hayley Parker, Kelly Ashmore

A millionaire businessman who left his girlfriend to die after 'rough sex' is fighting against his prison sentence.

Property tycoon John Broadhurst was jailed for three years and eight months for the manslaughter of mum-of-one Natalie Connolly, 27.

Former deputy Labour leader Harriet Harman described the 41-year-old's prison term as "unduly lenient".

But now Broadhurst is the one launching an appeal against his sentence.

The case will be heard at the Court of Appeal today, Stoke on Trent Live reports.

The court heard Natalie was found lying in the hallway at her home she shared with Broadhurst in Kinver, Staffordshire, in December 2016.

He had gone to bed and left her there after a night of drinking.

 

She had more than 40 separate injuries - including serious internal trauma and a 'blow-out' fracture to her left eye – and a post mortem showed she had died from acute alcohol intoxication and blunt force injuries.

Natalie had told witnesses that she and the defendant had an interest in masochistic sex.

The defendant - said during his trial to be worth up to £15 million - had separated from a former partner, who was pregnant with their second child, around three months before Natalie's death.

 

Broadhurst was cleared of murdering Natalie and causing her grievous bodily harm on the directions of the judge following legal submissions part-way through his trial at Birmingham Crown Court.

As he jailed Broadhurst for 44 months, Mr Justice Julian Knowles told the father-of-three: "You were capable of taking decisions and making choices.

"This was grossly irresponsible behaviour by you.

"You left that vulnerable young woman to die in the saddest and most avoidable of circumstances. You showed blatant disregard for a very drunk and injured woman."

Detective Inspector Victoria Downing, of Staffordshire Police, said after the case: "Natalie was callously and cold-heartedly left fatally injured at the bottom of the stairs by Broadhurst.

"Yet he didn't call for help until 9.30am and then showed total disregard for what he had done when he spoke to the emergency services."
