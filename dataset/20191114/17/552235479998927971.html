Street valium addict set his Staffordshire bull terrier on cop in Burnbank

“He was shocked by what he did and shows genuine remorse" the court heard.

14 Nov 2019, 17:23 by Hamilton Advertiser, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/hamilton-advertiser/

A thug has been jailed for ten months after ordering his dog to attack three men, including a police officer.

The men were all bitten by Stephen McCafferty’s Staffordshire bull terrier in Hamilton’s Burnbank area on June 21.

McCafferty, 22, a prisoner, appeared at Hamilton Sheriff Court last week and admitted ordering the attacks.

He also admitted assaulting an employee and robbing her of alcohol and scratchcards at Costcutters in the Burnbank Centre on the same day.

McCafferty further admitted kicking a door that slammed into and injured a member of staff at nearby Optical Express and stealing a mobile phone from a car.

Defence agent Andy Iles said his client was addicted to street valium and had little memory of events.

Mr Iles added: “He was shocked by what he did and shows genuine remorse. He has a limited number of previous convictions and nothing as serious as this.”

Referring to a criminal justice social work report, Mr Iles said McCafferty had a difficult upbringing.

He told Sheriff Thomas Millar: “These incidents were a culmination of various factors and were two decades in the making.”

The sheriff told McCafferty: “This was a quite horrendous course of conduct which has to be marked by a period in custody.”
