'You ripped our heart out and stamped on it' - Keanu Bedward jailed for life for shooting Remal Hunt dead in 'gang-related' feud

Emotional statement from victim's family as killer who fled to Dubai after Erdington gun attack is told he will serve at least 30 years

14 Nov 2019, 17:23 by Ross McCarthy, https://www.birminghammail.co.uk/authors/ross-mccarthy/

A gunman who fled to Dubai after shooting a man dead in a gang-related feud has been jailed for life.

Keanu Bedward will serve at least 30 years after being found guilty of murdering Remal Hunt by a jury at Birmingham Crown Court.

The killer dumped the revolver in a reservoir after fatally shooting the 28-year-old victim in Church Road, Erdington, on April 18 this year.

Bedward, 24, of Norfolk Road, Erdington, had denied the charge.

After the sentencing hearing, the victim's family said the killer had "ripped their hearts out".

Passing sentence, Judge Simon Drew QC said: "This was an incident that took place in a public street in the middle of the day.

"After the incident you made a speedy get away and disposed of the weapon in circumstances where you no doubt hoped it would never be found.

"You then fled abroad at speed.

"It would appear this offence was in some way gang-related. Certainly you were known to each other.

"Whether this in fact involved gangs is not entirely clear.

"Undoubtedly there was bad feeling between the two of you.

"Precisely what caused that I am not in a position to make a determination.

"Clearly this was not pre-planned and it was, to that extent, spontaneous.

"You were armed and ready to deal with the situation if it arose."

In a statement the victim's sister, Shannon Dempsey, said: "The first born dead at the age of 28, cut short by the evil actions of an individual.

"He was a happy and smiling young man with three beautiful children.

"You ripped our heart out and stamped on it. That pain will never leave us."

The court heard both men had been in cars and that, after the incident, Bedward sped off in a Golf.

Mr Hunt, who was shot in the chest, was pronounced dead shortly afterwards.

Bedward boarded a flight to Dubai but was arrested when he returned ten days later.

West Midlands Fire Service crews found a black Nike bag filled with stones when they conducted a training exercise near Bartley Green Reservoir a few days before Bedward returned to the UK.

Inside it was the revolver used in the shooting which had four bullets still in the chamber.

Kevin Metzger, defending, said the defendant had come back to face justice.

He said the shooting came against the background of WhatsApp messages and a previous shooting when a shotgun was found, bearing the DNA of a number of people including Mr Hunt.

Mr Metzger said, on the day, the victim had been a passenger in a car which had stopped in the middle of Church Road, causing the vehicle behind it to come to a halt.
