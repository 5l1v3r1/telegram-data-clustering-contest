Pundit makes big Leeds United transfer claim - and it will interest Aston Villa and Man United

Latest Aston Villa transfer news includes claims over Kalvin Phillips who has recently been linked with Manchester United

14 Nov 2019, 17:03 by Shane Ireland, https://www.facebook.com/ShaneIrelandJournalist

Kalvin Phillips has a release clause in his contract allowing him to leave if Leeds United don’t win promotion, according to Noel Whelan.

Phillips, 23, has been linked with a move to the Premier League after becoming a mainstay of Marcelo Bielsa’s side at Elland Road.

Aston Villa were firmly interested during the summer transfer window when Phillips was a leading target after the play-off triumph in May.

But they couldn’t agree a fee with Leeds, who rejected bids, and the midfielder stayed put for another shot at promotion with his boyhood club.

Since then, Manchester United have also been credited with an interest amid reports they have been scouting Phillips this season.

He signed a new contract in August and Whelan has claimed that as part of that deal, suitors can activate a get-out clause in the summer if Leeds fail to return to the Premier League.

“Kalvin’s contract is based on promotion," the former Whites player told Football Insider. "He has a release clause allowing him to go for a set price – more than £20million – if we don’t go up.”

 

Leeds are believed to have turned down offers in the region of £20m for Phillips in the summer and Villa looked elsewhere to add to their options in the middle of the park.

Marvelous Nakamba and Douglas Luiz arrived into Dean Smith's squad as part of a big-money spending spree.
