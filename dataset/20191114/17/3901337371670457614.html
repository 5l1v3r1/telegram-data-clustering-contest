Yaya To Make EPL Return?

14 Nov 2019, 17:44 by SL International

Former Ivory Coast international Yaya Toure is widely regarded as one of the greatest African footballers to have played in the Premier League, but is the Manchester City legend set for a sensational return to England's top flight?

Read: Ronaldo Facing Two-Year Ban?

Toure spent eight highly successful seasons with City in the Premier League, and won three league titles between 2010 and 2018.

The 36-year-old most recently plied his trade in China with Qingdao Huanghai, but seemingly hinted at a return to the EPL during a recent interview with Sky Sports.

"I would love it (to return to the Premier League), but it depends on which kind of team," Toure said.

"We will see because, sometimes in Europe, people look at ages in a different way – it's not about that. It depends on the club and what they want to achieve.

"I don't want to go too far and propose myself, but I just want them to give me a chance, we will see."

The Ivorian is currently preparing to acquire his coaching badges, but believes he still has a few years left as a player.

"In my mind, I give myself one or maybe two years maximum playing football. After that, I want to enjoy myself with my family and try to work in other ways,” Toure added.

"I want to be proud of myself. As a footballer, sometimes when you stop it early, it is because people are trying to stop you, it's not coming from your heart, it's complicated.

"I remember when I was in Greece and people were speculating about my career – I was not happy. Something was missing. I want to be in control.

"I think I have to start to do my badges. Football has been all my life from when I was a kid until now. Football is part of me and I can stop watching football or being part of it."

Toure scored 62 league goals in 230 appearances for City according to the Premier League's official website, and ranks as the competition's fifth-highest African goalscorer of all time.
