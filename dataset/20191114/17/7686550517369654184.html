Alcohol, tobacco and cigarettes stolen in ramraid at Cardiff supermarket

The thieves used a vehicle to ram the shutters and force entry to the shop

14 Nov 2019, 17:09 by Ffion Lewis

Alcohol, tobacco and cigarettes were stolen in an early morning ramraid at a Cardiff supermarket this morning.

Extensive damage was caused to the Co-op store in Llanrumney after thieves used a vehicle to force entry into the shop.

Police said a vehicle was used to ram the shutters open of the shop on Countisbury Avenue, at around 2.25am.

Pictures from the scene show the external shutter was left in a crumpled mess, with the main doors also appearing to have been forced open.

There were also smashed bottles at the scene of the break-in.

The supermarket has been closed today as a result of the damage.

A spokesman for the Co-op said: "An incident occured at our Llanrumney store at around 2.30am in the early hours of this morning.

"Extensive damaged has been caused and we are appealing to anyone who may have seen or heard anything to come forward to police who are investigating.

"This week is respect for shopworkers week and as a community based organisation we see the impact of social issues in our stores - this incident further highlights the need for greater resources to tackle the root causes of crime.

"Our store is expected to reopen tomorrow morning, Friday November 15, to serve the community."

South Wales Police has launched an appeal for information.

A spokeswoman said: "Police in Cardiff are investigating following a burglary at the Co-Operative Store on Countisbury Avenue, Llanrumney, in the early hours of November 14.

"The store was targeted at approximately 2.25am, with thieves using a vehicle to ram the shutters and force entry to the premises. They then stole quantities of tobacco, cigarettes and alcohol before fleeing the scene.

"Enquiries are ongoing to identify the culprits. "

Anyone with any information which could assist the investigation is asked to contact 101, quoting occurrence 1900420484. Alternatively, Crimestoppers can be contacted 100% anonymously on 0800 555 111.
