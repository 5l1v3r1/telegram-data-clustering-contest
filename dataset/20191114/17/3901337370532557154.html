Arrest Made In Drive-By Shooting Murder Of Dutch Footballer

14 Nov 2019, 17:00 by SL International

Authorities confirmed on Thursday that a suspect has been arrested in connection with a drive-by shooting that resulted in the death of a Dutch footballer in September.

In September, former Volendam and Burton Albion defender Kelvin Maynard was killed after being shot in his car by two unidentified suspects on a motorcycle.

Authorities have now confirmed that a person has been arrested in connection with Maynard's murder.

"Kelvin Maynard died on 18 September 2019 after a shooting incident on the Langbroekdreef in Amsterdam-Zuidoost," an official statement read.

"He was fired (at) on that evening at around 8.30 p.m. Since then, the criminal investigation department has been conducting a very extensive investigation.

"Investigations are being carried out on forensic, technical and financial matters. Thanks to this investigation, a suspect was arrested on 14 November.

"The suspect was taken to a police station for questioning. More information about this suspect and the suspicion against him will follow later."

The motive for the shooting has not yet been made apparent.
