tours announced: Bikini Kill, Black Flag, Tool, KISS, Delta Spirit, Habibi, more

14 Nov 2019, 17:38 by BrooklynVegan Staff

The following new tours were announced. Check the Tour Dates category every day for more.

BIKINI KILL

The legendary Bikini Kill continue to sell out shows and add more dates to their reunion tour, and they’ve now added a second DC-area show for 5/10 at The Fillmore Silver Spring. All dates here.

DELTA SPIRIT

It’s been five years since Delta Spirit’s Into the Wide and while there hasn’t been an announcement of a follow-up, they will be heading out on the “Not Dead Yet” tour in 2020. Dates include San Francisco, Los Angeles, Solana Beach, Charleston (SC), Washington DC, NYC (Bowery Ballroom on 4/25), Cambridge, Toronto, Indianapolis, Chicago, and Nashville. Head here for all dates.

BLACK FLAG

Black Flag (with Greg Ginn and other people) have spent much of 2019 on their first tour in five years, and they’ve added several West Coast, Southern US, and South America dates for 2020. All upcoming dates and tickets here.

TOOL

Tool are in the midst of a US tour supporting their first album in 13 years, Fear Inoculum (which is getting an expanded book edition soon), and that tour hits Boston tonight (11/14), followed by stops in Newark, Philly, Brooklyn, Connecticut, and Atlantic City this week and next. They’ve also now added their first Australia/New Zealand dates in six years for February. Those go on sale Friday, November 22 (dates and tickets here).

KISS

KISS sadly had to cancel Australian dates this month and next due to illness, but in better news, they officially announced their 2020 North American tour.

MARYLIN MANSON (OPENING FOR OZZY)

Ozzy Osbourne‘s 2020 North American tour, which was rescheduled from 2019, was originally set to be opened by Megadeth, but now it has a new opener: Marilyn Manson.

CASPIAN

Massachusetts post-rockers Caspian have announced a new album and tour for 2020.

HABIBI

New York’s Habibi will release new album on February 14 via Mudguts which finds the band expanding beyond their garagey, girl group roots and incorporating Middle Eastern flourishes, exotic instrumentation, and other psychedelic touches. The first single is “Come My Habibi” and you can check that out below. They play NYC tonight at The Dance with Public Practice and Gustaf, and will be on tour in February and March, hitting Phoenix, San Diego, Costa Mesa, Los Angeles, Oakland, Sacramento, Portland, Seattle, Vancouver and more. All dates are here.
