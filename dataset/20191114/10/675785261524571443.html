I'm A Celebrity 2019 Focus Tower - where is it in jungle camp?

Focus Tower is an iconic attraction which featured on I'm A Celebrity... Get Me Out Of Here! in 2017 and is located in the heart of Australia on Surfers Paradise

14 Nov 2019, 10:54 by Brogan-Leigh Hurst

Focus Tower is a popular attraction dotted on Australia's Surfers Paradise and has previously featured on I'm A Celebrity... Get Me Out Of Here! .

Surfers Paradise is home to some of the country's most stunning beaches and has found popularity among tourists.

And right along the sand is Focus Tower, which found itself at the heart of controversy after appearing on I'm A Celeb in 2017, and stars will be climbing it again in this year's run of the series.

Here's all the important details you should know about the gorgeous attraction...

The tower itself is located near Brisbane in Surfers Paradise, Queensland and is a luxury apartment block boasting beautiful sea views.

It includes a heated pool, jacuzzi, gym, BBQ and picnic areas and landscaped gardens, all complete with a Chinese restaurant and free WiFi for residents.

 

Focus Tower is a 32 storey tower and 96m tall, made of reinforced unit tower.

It had a diameter of 30m and was known as the tallest building on the Gold Coast from 1967-77, designed by architects Clark & Gazzard Partners.

Back in the 2017 series of I'm A Celeb, the host of stars had to walk the plank before they were jetted into the jungle as part of a terrifying task.

 

One eagle-eyed viewer spotted something a bit off about the footage, as it seemed the stars weren't 334ft in the air - it looked like they were only a couple of storeys above ground.

Twitter user Tom Smith paused his TV to capture the dreaded task and posted it on social media.

The picture showed a woman walking along the road underneath the planks celebrities were walking over.

He wrote: "Bottom left, that woman's rather large considering she's 320 plus foot lower than the celebs!"

At the time, the picture had been shared over 38,000 times and had accumulated more than 40,000 comments on Facebook .

Others disagreed and shot down the theory, claiming the task was real and one fan claimed they watched the filming from their office window.
