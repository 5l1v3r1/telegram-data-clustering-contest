Drunk Kilmarnock man threatened to stab ScotRail staff and members of the public with potato peeler

The 25-year-old was jailed after telling the train driver: “You’re getting stabbed.”

14 Nov 2019, 10:04 by Tara Fitzpatrick

A Kilmarnock man who threatened ScotRail staff and members of the public with a potato peeler has been jailed.

Christopher Brown told a train driver he was “getting stabbed” before trying to strike a passenger with the weapon on a platform of Kilmarnock railway station on August 19.

Brown, an inmate at Kilmarnock prison, appeared at the town’s sheriff court on Monday and pleaded guilty to three charges of abusive behaviour, assault and possession of an offensive item.

 

The prosecutor told the court the 25-year-old had been waiting on a train to Glasgow, which arrived in Kilmarnock station around 9pm.

The driver pulled his window down and Brown walked up to him and said he and his friend had no money to get home.

The train conductor then approached to check if Brown had a ticket. The accused swore and said he had no money.

The prosecutor said: “At this point they got on the train and were asked to leave.

“He began to sprawl himself across the seats, ignoring the requests to leave.

“The driver had to leave the front and assist his colleague. The accused reacted abusively and the driver went back to the driver’s cab.”

At this point Brown walked up to the window of the driver’s compartment with an item in his hand which the driver thought looked like a pen knife.

 

The item was a potato peeler and Brown told the driver: “You’re getting stabbed.”

Brown then got off the train and turned his attention to a passenger standing on the platform.

The fiscal depute said: “The accused approached them with the potato peeler and was attempting to strike him.

“The passenger tried to strike him back with his rucksack.”

Brown then shouted back at the driver of the train: “You are getting it. You’re getting stabbed.”

The train then left the platform and Brown made his way into the station ticket office, where he began shouting and swearing at a woman working behind the counter.

 

He was told by the office worker that police would be called, at which point he stated: “I will be back in an hour to get you.”

The prosecutor told the court: “He held out the potato peeler and started to brandish it.

“This item was successfully removed from him by another member of staff and police were contacted.

“He was later traced under the influence of alcohol and cautioned and charged.”

Brown’s solicitor told the court: “What seems to have happened after the altercation with the passenger is another passenger has stopped him and punched him.

 

“It is quite clear that the person who punched him was perhaps justified in taking that action due to his behaviour towards the other passenger.”

The lawyer added: “He was intoxicated to the extent that he could hardly walk in a straight line.

“He recognises in the long term he has to do something about his alcohol consumption.”

Sheriff Elizabeth McFarlane told Brown: “If you want to do something about your drink problem then do something about it.”

Brown was sentenced to 16 months in prison.
