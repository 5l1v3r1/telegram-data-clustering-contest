Two arrested after 'men with weapons' raid flat in Plymouth

Armed police responded to the incident on Albert Road

14 Nov 2019, 16:48 by Rom Preston-Ellis

Two men have been arrested after armed police were called to Albert Road in Plymouth this afternoon.

Police responded to reports that "two men with weapons" attempted to forcibly enter a flat at around 2.20pm.

Devon and Cornwall Police said that numerous suspects then fled the property.

Two men have since been arrested - one on drugs offences and the other on suspicion of aggravated burglary.

Albert Road was closed by police but has since reopened.

A Devon and Cornwall Police spokesperson said: “Police were called at 2.20pm on November 14 to reports of a disturbance in the Albert Road area of Plymouth.

“It is believed two men carrying weapons have attempted to forcibly enter a property. Numerous people including the suspects have then fled the address.

“The police have attended with both local police units and armed officers. The road was closed but was reopened at 4pm.

“One man has been arrested on suspicion of possessing a class A drug and a second man has been arrested on suspicion of aggravated burglary.

“Police investigations and searches are ongoing.”
