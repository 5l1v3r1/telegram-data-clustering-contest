Cosmetic clinic put patients at risk with 'dirty' surgical tools and potentially lethal doses of anaesthetic

Bearwood Cosmetic Clinic offered a variety of procedures - including vaginoplasty and liposuction - which staff had not had sufficient training to perform

14 Nov 2019, 16:43 by Kelly Ashmore, https://www.facebook.com/dudleybclive/

A Smethwick cosmetic clinic was stripped of its licence for using dirty surgical equipment and administering potentially lethal doses of anaesthetic.

Bearwood Cosmetic Clinic offered a variety of procedures - including vaginoplasty and liposuction - which staff had not had sufficient training to perform.

The "unsafe" theatre was "not fit for purpose", with live wires exposed and plaster falling out of the walls.

Dust also covered cupboards and trolleys, while "brown reddish stains" which had a "foul smell" were found ingrained into the fabric and structure of a bed used for patients to lay on during procedures.

The unhygienic conditions were uncovered during an unannounced Care Quality Commission inspection in 2018. Findings were published by the health watchdog today (Wednesday, November 14).

Patients were given anaesthetic agents above the recommended dosage, which had put their lives at risk. The report also stated emergency equipment had not been readily available - a resuscitation trolley with defibrillator and emergency drugs were located on a different floor.

The findings prompted chief inspector of hospitals, Ted Baker, to write to providers across the country, raising particular concern regarding the use of anaesthetic during liposuction.

Within two days of the inspection, which took place on November 29 last year, the CQC cancelled the clinic's licence  due to the "serious and significant risks" it identified.

The clinic has since closed.
