Why Did U.S. Cellular Stock Fall 15% On Friday

05 Nov 2019 by Trefis Team

Regional wireless carrier U.S. Cellular (NYSE: USM), saw its stock decline by about 15% in Friday’s (1st November) trading after it posted weaker than expected Q3 earnings, although revenues beat expectations. The company lost 19k postpaid subscribers over the quarter, compared to 1k losses in the year-ago quarter, partly due to the defection of connected device users and feature phone users. Separately, the company also indicated that the uncertainty surrounding the proposed merger between T-Mobile and Sprint – the third and fourth-largest U.S. carriers, was resulting in some difficulties in its planning and securing new partnerships. How does United States Cellular’s Revenue Growth in 2018 compare with that in prior periods and what’s the forecast?

Total Revenues for United States Cellular marginally increased from $3.89 Bil in 2017 to $3.97 Bil in 2018; an increase of 1.98%.

This compares with Total Revenues growth of:

  • 3.54% in 2015
  • -1.02% in 2016
  • -2.51% in 2017

We expect Total Revenues growth to be 1.41% in 2019.

How does United States Cellular’s Total Expense in 2018 compare with that in prior periods and what’s the forecast?

Total Expense for United States Cellular decreased from $4.16 Bil in 2017 to $3.75 Bil in 2018; a decrease of -9.85%.

This compares with Total Expense growth of:

  • -8.22% in 2015
  • 7.75% in 2016
  • 6.50% in 2017

We expect Total Expense growth to be 1.8% in 2019.

How does United States Cellular’s EBT 2018 compare with that in prior periods and what’s the forecast?

EBT for United States Cellular increased from -$272 Mil in 2017 to $215 Mil in 2018. We expect EBT to decline by about 6% to $202 Mil in 2019.
