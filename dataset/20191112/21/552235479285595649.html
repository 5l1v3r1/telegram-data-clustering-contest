Ovie Ejaria showcases spinning technique Rangers fans will definitely recognise

The midfielder had his ups and downs at Ibrox but could pirouette with aplomb.

12 Nov 2019, 21:23 by Jonathan McFarlane

Ovie Ejaria may have left Ibrox under something of a cloud but the former Liverpool loanee left one indelible mark - as the best spinner around a football Ibrox has ever seen.

And it's safe to say it's not a talent that the midfielder, now at Reading after a £3m summer move, has lost since departing Scotland.

Playing during the Royals' 2-1 victory over Millwall, Ejaria found himself isolated near the corner flag, under pressure from Mahlon Romeo.

He spun around the defender in two full 360 degree rotations before laying off to a teammate in an astonishing display of balance and control.

Ejaria departed Ibrox in December last year amid claims the Premiership was too physical for his liking.

 

Steven Gerrard spoke at the time of his disappointment that the deal didn't work out and suggested the creative star had made an error in judgement.

He said: "Look he's a Liverpool player so I first and foremost have to respect the kid and the privacy.

"I'd like to thank Liverpool for the opportunity to have him here for half the season.

"Am I disappointed he's gone? Yes. Do I think it's the right decision? No, because I think he had an incredible opportunity here.

"But I'm never going to stand in a player's way who is not happy and who is not settled because I have people who are desperate to wear the shirt and who would run through brick walls to wear it.

"It is what it is. I have to accept the decision. I'm sad about it, I wish him all the best. He's a nice kid, very quiet. He told me it was 100 per cent football reasons and I have to respect that."
