Veteran battered by thugs in sickening Remembrance Day attack in East Kilbride

Staff at the Village Inn came to the aid of the man, understood to be in his 60s, after he was set upon in the street and repeatedly kicked on the face and stamped on the head.

12 Nov 2019, 16:29 by Nicola Findlay, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/nicola-findlay/

An appeal has been launched after a veteran was set upon by thugs in a "sickening" attack on Remembrance Day.

The senseless beating happened near to the Village Inn at approximately 11pm and staff raced to the aid of the injured man after he sought refuge in the pub.

 

Manager at the East Kilbride pub, Diane Grant, said she was "sick to the stomach" when she saw the footage captured on the premises' CCTV cameras.

"I have worked in pubs for a long time and I have never seen anything as vicious as this, I felt physically sick," she told the East Kilbride News.

"We desperately need to catch the thugs who did this.

"The victim was crossing the road at the lights outside when he was set upon by two men, who looked as if they were in their 30s.

"It looked as if it was completely unprovoked  - they just set about him, kicking him and punching him about the face and head, stamping on him even when he was lying on the ground.

"He had all his medals on and and a beret and was clearly a veteran. I don't know if something was said about the medals, I really don't know what set it off. Apparently he had a few drinks in Legends, there was nothing amiss and he left without any problems."

She went on: "The attackers are absolute animals and need to be caught and locked up. How anyone could do this I just don't know. It was absolutely horrendous.

"When the poor man came in to the pub for help he was in an awful state, his face was an absolute mess and the girls and I tried to do what we could to clean him up and see the extent of the cuts.

"He didn't want an ambulance or to go to the hospital  but eventually one of our customers phoned his wife who came and said she was taking the man to hospital, but I am not sure what happened after that."

The News contacted Police Scotland for comment but they failed to respond.
