Thug who stabbed friend in the next during heated argument learns fate at court

Rodney Brannan, 46, was sentenced today.

12 Nov 2019, 16:22 by Airdrie and Coatbridge Advertiser, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/airdrie-and-coatbridge-advertiser/

A violent thug, who stabbed a friend in the neck during a heated argument, was today jailed for six years.

Rodney Brannan, 45, of Plains' Bruce Street, admitted assaulting James McAvoy at his home on Aberfeldy Avenue in February, to the danger of his life.

Brannan was originally charged with attempting to murder Mr McAvoy, but his guilty plea to the reduced charge was accepted.

Judge Sean Murphy QC also ordered Brannan to be monitored in the community for three years after his release from prison.

Judge Murphy told him: “Your victim has suffered a loss of confidence, fears strangers and is more or less housebound.

“This was a form of revenge attack. You now have an opportunity to reform.”

Defence counsel Jennifer Bain said: “Mr Brannan accepts full responsibility for his actions in attacking a long-standing friend. He is horrified by his behaviour.

“The near death of a friend who was shot in front of him caused him to turn to alcohol, but this offence has been a wake up call for him.”

The High Court in Glasgow heard that Brannan, Mr McAvoy and two other friends were having a drink at Mr McAvoy's home when the incident took place.

Prosecutor Lindsey Dalziel said: “Another friend James Todd and the accused became involved in conversation which resulted in an argument.

“This escalated to a fight involving all parties during which Mr McAvoy grabbed a vodka bottle and struck the accused on the head.”

As a result of this Brannan went into the kitchen and grabbed a knife and stuck it in Mr McAvoy's neck, causing him to fall to the ground

The knife was taken from Brannan and thrown down on the hallway floor.

Brannan has previous convictions relating to violence and using knives.

Mr McAvoy managed to leave the house and called the police who arrived shortly after 10.30pm.

Officers entered the house and arrested Brannan who was still there as Mr McAvoy was taken to Monklands Hospital.

Mr McAvoy was found to be suffering from a stab wound to the left side of his neck and had bruising to his face.

He was transferred to the Vascular Unit at Hairmyres Hospital and kept under observation for 24 hours .

He did not require surgery because the wounds to his neck were sutured and he was discharged early the following month.

When Brannan was originally charged with assaulting Mr McAvoy he told them “Not guilty.”
