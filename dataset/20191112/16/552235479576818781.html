East Ayrshire councillor shares picture of hairloss following mum's sudden death

Elena Whitham said the photo showed the "real me".

12 Nov 2019, 16:44 by Kilmarnock Standard, Sarah Hilley, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/kilmarnock-standard/, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/sarah-hilley/

A councillor bravely shared a picture of the hair loss she has suffered since her mother died suddenly five years ago.

East Ayrshire Council depute leader Elena Whitham, 45, developed alopecia after her mother Irene McLeod died suddenly from lung cancer, aged only 58.

Recalling the grief, she said: “She got a lung cancer diagnosis and was dead within five weeks. It was such a shock.

 

“Within months I noticed what was happening with my hair.”

Councillor Whitham has been wearing hairpieces but wants the freedom to go without them occasionally as sometimes they cause an allergic reaction.

Having to wear a wig all the time has also been holding her back from going swimming and kayaking.

She decided to fling it off and post a smiling photo on social media, saying: “Having alopecia sucks but this is the real me bald spots and wispy locks.”

 

Councillor Whitham said: “I took the picture to send to my husband. I thought, ‘why not tell folk?’. I just did it on the spur of the moment.”

The mum said there is too much emphasis on how women look in society.

She said: “At 45 years old it feels there are so many things I could be worrying about. I feel I should not have to be worrying about this.”

The Irvine Valley politician said husband Les has told her he finds her attractive and urged her to go without hairpieces at home.

 

But the former Women’s Aid worker said it can be hard to “believe that until we get to the point were we accept it in ourselves”.

She added: “I lost my hair when my mum died suddenly five years ago and only now am I comfortable enough with myself to show the world after I accepted my husband really does still fancy me.”

Councillor Whitham has been inundated with good wishes and support online.
