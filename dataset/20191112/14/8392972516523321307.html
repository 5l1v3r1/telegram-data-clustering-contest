Aldi is launching a mac and cheese burger this week

It will be available on November 14

12 Nov 2019, 14:37 by Rebecca Carter

Aldi is going to delight shoppers as it's launching a mac and cheese burger this week.

The burger is made with a steak patty and is topped with a cheddar mac and cheese that melts over the top of the burger.

Aldi has described the burger as a "flavour fusion" and revealed customers can buy the treat on November 14.

Read more: Aldi launches halloumi burgers after shoppers went wild for its sell-out fries

The supermarket adds: "A fusion of classic American dishes, the Specially Selected Mac & Cheese Burger combines two delicious favourites to create a flavour sensation with every mouthful.

"The burger is made with a Scotch steak patty, topped with a scrumptious macaroni and mature cheddar cheese melt that oozes evenly over the patty during cooking. What’s more, this heavenly dish is completely gluten free, so everyone can enjoy it!"

The burgers will cost £2.49 for a 440g pack. However, once the product sells out, it won't be restocked.

The burgers won't be sold online, so you'll have to pop to your local Aldi to pick a pack up.

Earlier this month, Aldi launched a halloumi burger after shoppers raved about its sell-out cheese fries.

The supermarket is also bringing back its halloumi fries, which were a hit with shoppers and sold out last year.

The new halloumi burgers are priced at £2.29 each in a 190g pack.

Last year, the newly-launched halloumi fries sent shoppers wild and they took to Twitter to praise the delicious treats.

Aldi says about the fries: "Inspired by a similar offering from popular Portuguese chicken eatery, Nando’s; Aldi’s Specially Selected Haloumi Fries are made with scrumptiously soft - and salty - halloumi cheese from Cyprus and a lightly seasoned crispy coating making them the perfect side or snack for sharing."

One person said: "Halloumi fries from Aldi are the real deal."

Another wrote: "Late to the party but the halloumi fries from Aldi are 10/10."
