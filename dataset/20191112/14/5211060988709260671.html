Officials remove vandalism on cenotaph outside Toronto's Old City Hall

12 Nov 2019, 14:12 by Kayla Goodfield, Multi-Platform Writer, CTV News Toronto

City officials are removing graffiti found on a cenotaph just outside Old City Hall the morning after Remembrance Day.

On Tuesday morning, the words “ye broke faith” and "with us" were seen spray-painted on two sides of the monument.

The words appear to reference the line, "If ye break faith with us who die," from John McCrae's poem "In Flanders Fields."

Investigators confirmed they received a call regarding the vandalism at 7 a.m.

Around the noon hour, officials could be seen power washing the cenotaph to remove the paint.
