Livid business owner told to pay £1,000 by council for double-yellow lines outside office

Josh Hall is locked in a council row after being told double-yellow lines are being installed outside his office

12 Nov 2019, 07:52 by James Rodger, Bethan Shufflebotham, https://www.facebook.com/jamesrodgerjournalist

A businessman has been told to pay £1,000 for double-yellow lines outside his vehicle rental.

Josh Hall is locked in a council row after being told double-yellow lines are being installed outside his office.

Mr Hall, from the Midlands, opened NVP Rental Vehicles after buying a block of 'drug den' public toilets from Stoke-on-Trent City Council.

The 27-year-old transformed them as part of a £40,000 investment, reports StokeonTrentLive , our sister title.

The deal to buy the crumbling Keelings Road building, in Northwood, was completed in March, 2018.

Now Josh is furious that the council wants him to pay for parking restrictions - and he does not even want them.

Josh, from Milton, said: "I initially put in a planning application for a dropped kerb and it was refused.

"There are no double-yellow lines there so I have a right to park outside the business - the council can't tell me where I can and can't park!

"And why would I pay for them to put double-yellow lines in?"

He added: "We have taken the building from being a drug den to a tidy office employing six people. I got rid of more than 2,000 needles from the building.

"I feel like I've been going backwards and forwards with the council and now it wants double-yellow lines."

 

Council bosses say the double-yellow line demand is linked to the original planning application for the business.

A council spokesman said: "We have been absolutely clear that in order for planning permission to be given for the business, new parking restrictions would also be required due to its position next to a very busy junction and the lack of any off-street parking for the vehicle hire business.

"The need for yellow lines is a direct result of the business being there, and as such they need to pay for the costs associated with the installation."

The council says it last heard from Josh in July.

The spokesman added: "Despite being reminded a number of times by letters and site visits, nothing has been done and therefore the business does not have planning permission and remains unauthorised.

"As a council, we are passionate about supporting businesses in the city and we want to see companies thrive and do well. However, they must follow rules around planning permission.

"We want to get this situation sorted out as soon as possible but the ball remains in their court.

"If nothing changes, we will have no choice but to take formal enforcement action to stop the unauthorised use of the premises.

"We really don't want to go down this road but in the face of our requests and correspondence to the business being continually ignored, we will be left with no choice."
