Airdrie fans recognised in Westminster after superb charity trip to Mongolia

The group spent 10 nights in the Asian country where they are donating five homes – locally known as gers – to families in need.

12 Nov 2019, 12:11 by Mark Pirie, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/mark-pirie/

A kind-hearted group of football fans from the Airdrie Supporters Trust are to be recognised in Westminster following their charitable trip from Monklands to Mongolia.

The group spent 10 nights in the Asian country where they are donating five homes – locally known as gers – to families in need.

Hannah Eaton and her uncle Stephen travelled in memory of Hannah’s dad Tom, who passed away in June following a battle with cancer, and they donated a ger in his name.

The Monklands visitors also took part in a football tournament which raised funds for a cancer centre aiming to improve screening services.

As part of the trust’s KitAid project, they also took football shirts, shorts, socks and boots, as well as tracksuits and training equipment.

Trust members Dougy Allsop and Willie Marshall estimate they raised around £15,000 for various causes.

Dougy said: “There was a good support network and everyone helped everyone out; it wasn’t an easy trip.”

Willie added: “Everyone gelled. A lot of people didn’t know each other but they came together which was great to see.”

Now the team will visit the House of Commons next month as guests of the Honorary Consul for Mongolia in Scotland, David Scott.

Mr Scott said: “I commend the actions of the Airdrie Supporters Trust members. The donation of five gers to families in the Gobi desert, and in Ulaanbaatar is an act of life-changing generosity and care.

“Further, the team funded a year of school supplies for families across the city and Gobi region, as well as supporting a number of disabled children.

“The trust also took part in the Test for Life football tournament, raising several thousand pounds for the Mongolian cancer centre.

“Most impressively of all, the team were tremendous ambassadors for Airdrie, epitomising the very finest aspects of people to people diplomacy.

“The consulate remains indebted for their hard work and remains committed to supporting them in their admirable endeavours.”

The Trust team plan to embark on a similar trip every August and hope to visit Namibia next year. The 20-person trip will be open to everyone – not just Airdrie fans and Trust members.

Dougy added: “The plan now is to promote walking football and cost up school projects and donations for next year.”
