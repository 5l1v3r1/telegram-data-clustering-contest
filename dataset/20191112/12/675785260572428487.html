Richard Madeley tipped to be I'm A Celebrity late arrival as line-up confirmed

This Morning's Holly Willoughby and Phillip Schofield are hoping James Haskell will be joined by father-in-law Richard Madeley

12 Nov 2019, 12:44 by Charlotte Tutton

The I'm A Celebrity 2019 line-up could be about to gain another name ahead of the late arrivals being confirmed.

Richard Madeley recently appeared to deny he would enter the jungle, with it now confirmed his son-in-law rugby player James Haskell is a contestant.

The full original line-up has been announced, with James joined by Caitlyn Jenner, Ian Wright, Roman Kemp, Nadine Coyle and Jacqueline Jossa amongst others.

But there's still one or two names to be confirmed, as there's set to be a late arrival or two as usual.

After Coronation Street's Kirk Sutherland actor Andrew Whyment was missing from the line-up, fans are hoping he might be one of the late entries.

 

Now, This Morning host Phillip Schofield shared his own theory on who might be joining the show.

Phillip believes fellow ITV presenter Richard is now headed for the jungle to join daughter Chloe's husband James.

Speaking on This Morning ahead of the launch, Phillip pointed out: "There's always the surprise one."

Co-host Holly Willoughby added: "Yeah, and normally the surprise one comes in and shifts things around, stirs things up a bit because we had Noel Edmonds that went in last year."

It's then that Phil made his prediction over who would be this year's surprise campmate.

He said: "Do you know who I would like to go in.

\  

"It's never released you just don’t know, but my guess if I was to have my favourite... Richard Madeley."

Holly responded: "That would be incredible, and also because that's his son-in-law cos he's Chloe's husband isn't he?"

It comes after months of speculation over whether Richard would enter the jungle, with him recently speaking about the claims to Lorraine Kelly.

Richard refused to confirm or deny an appearance, so fans will have to wait and see.

This Morning continues weekdays and Sundays at 10:30am on ITV.
