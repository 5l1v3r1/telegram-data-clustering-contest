I'm A Celebrity's Myles Stephenson 'to quit Rak-Su after jungle experience'

Former X Factor winner Myles Stephenson has admitted that despite being single he is not ready to mingle among the creepy crawlies and find love with another campmate

12 Nov 2019, 01:59 by James Brinsford, https://www.facebook.com/james.brinsford

Myles Stephenson has admitted that he would quit R&B band Rak-Su if his I'm A Celebrity experience presented him with new opportunities.

The 28-year-old former X Factor winner has said that while he has enjoyed his time with the band, he doesn't want to be 'held back' if the ITV show opened new doors for him.

Speaking ahead of entering the jungle, Myles said: "They are really supportive and they totally understand.

"I would love to stay in the band but if other opportunities come along my way as well after this, you have to take it by the scruff of the neck.

"They would do the same. I would never hold them back.”

Despite this, Myles believes his Rak-Su band mates can’t wait to watch him and will look to have an influence on his time Down Under.

“I know they will vote to try and make me do the trials,” he adds.

Myles is one of this year’s Jungle bachelors he has ruled out finding romance in the Jungle.

He has insisted he wants to embrace single life and he definitely doesn’t want to find love under the stars.

Myles said: "I am single. I have just got out of a relationship but I think I want to be single for a bit and embrace single life!”

He admits that he is excited about meeting I'm A Celebrity hosts Ant McPartlin and Declan Donnelly, especially as he is a massive fan of the Geordie duo.

Myles said: “Going on I’m a Celebrity is going to be so much harder than doing The X Factor but I am so excited. I have watched it for years and it is something I have always wanted to do.

"I am a massive fan of the show and Ant and Dec. They were the reason I was a Newcastle FC fan initially growing up! Spending time with them and potentially people you have idolised over the years is so exciting.”

There could be plenty of time spent at the campfire for Myles as he describes himself as a ‘passionate’ cook, and promises to give it his best shot when trying to rustle up food from basic rations.

“I love cooking and I cook every night if I am not touring,” he reveals. “My favourite meal is Beef Wellington or West Indian food. You don’t get much to mess around with in the Jungle but I will try my best.”
