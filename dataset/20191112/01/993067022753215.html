Orange Order fails to respond directly to Mary Lou McDonald meeting request

12 Nov 2019, 01:00 by JOHN MANLEY

THE Orange Order did not respond directly to an invitation by Mary Lou McDonald to meet the institution and attend Twelfth celebrations.

In March 2018, just weeks after becoming leader of Sinn Féin, Ms McDonald told The Irish News that she would be writing to the Orange institution requesting a meeting.

She said she aimed to "speak to sections of society who wouldn't necessarily have had a dialogue or a relationship" with the party.

"We need to stretch out the hand of friendship, go to places you're not necessarily comfortable or familiar with," the Dublin TD said at the time.

When asked yesterday if the Orange Order had responded directly, Ms McDonald said it had not.

She described it as a "great pity".

The Sinn Féin leader said she was not trying to "persuade those that are British not to be British or those that are Orange not to be Orange".

"I just think it’s important that there is at least a civility and dialogue between the leaderships of the great traditions on our island," she said.

Last year Orange Order grand secretary Mervyn Gibson claimed there were "335 reasons" not to engage with the party – a reference to loyal order members killed by republicans during the Troubles.
