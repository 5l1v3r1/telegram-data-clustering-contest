Ex model, 85, in fashion shoot after Vogue photographer spots her in OAP home

Grandmother-of-four Lesley MacLennan, 85, shot to fame in the 50s while working in Paris with emerging designer Yves Saint Laurent - and has now been noticed for her "poise" by a top fashion photographer

12 Nov 2019, 20:18 by Louie Smith, https://www.facebook.com/dailymirror/

A former catwalk model has posed for a fashion shoot in a care home aged 85 after being spotted by a top Vogue photographer.

Lesley MacLennan, 85, shot to fame in the 50s while working in Paris with emerging designer Yves Saint Laurent.

She later teamed up with Yves by doing catwalk shows and photo shoots at Dior.

Grandmother-of-four Lesley now lives in care at Huntington House in Hindhead, Surrey.

But she has completed one final shoot after high-end fashion photographer James Muller noticed her "poise" while visiting another resident.

James, whose work has been featured in Vogue, said: "I met Lesley in passing and thought how amazing it would be to capture her beauty again.

 

"When I asked her to look my way or turn her face, she did it with such grace – an absolute professional. She was very easy to direct.

"Age to me is just a number. During our shoot, Lesley was 'that' model again. Age made no difference.

"I think there aren't enough older models out there in the media. In many ways, social media is telling us that it's wrong to age, but it's such a natural thing.

"There is so much beauty in ageing too - we've just been taught to look at it the wrong way."

Lesley began modelling in her early 20s after her mum spotted her potential and emigrated to France to further her career.

She met Yves around the time he enjoyed his big break - when the 21-year-old was named Dior's head designer.

 

Stunning black and white photos taken 60 years ago show a young Lesley posing in glamorous coats and large, furry hats.

And the mum-of-two has lost none of her passion for modelling despite being in a care home,

After spending a day posing for James Lesley said: "I thoroughly enjoyed the photo shoot.

"James made me feel perfectly at ease, he was the real McCoy. I would have said so if he wasn’t.

"The red and black outfit was so vibrant, it was stunning. I just thought it was a dream.''

James added: "I feel very honoured to have photographed Lesley, she has lived such a colourful life and to be a small part of it is magical.

"She’s an absolute delight."

Charlie Hoare, director of the Huntington and Langham Estate where Huntington House is based, added: "To see her back in front of the camera was just incredible - she’s never lost her sparkle.

Sarah Chapman, another Huntington and Langham Director, said: "Age shouldn't matter.

"With these experiences, they can really come out of themselves, and just enjoy the days, just like they were in their 20s and 30s."
