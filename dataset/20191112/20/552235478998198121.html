Tragedy as mum who went missing is found dead two days after being traced 'safe and well'

Emma Watson disappeared from the Gowkthrapple area of Wishaw at around 3.30pm on Friday, November 8.

12 Nov 2019, 20:03 by Dailyrecord.co.uk

A mum who went missing was found dead just two days after she was traced 'safe and well' by police.

Emma Watson disappeared from the Gowkthrapple area of Wishaw at around 3.30pm on Friday, November 8.

Later that night, police confirmed that the 32-year-old had been 'traced safe and well'.

A statement from Lanarkshire Police Division said: "We are pleased to report that missing person Emma Watson, who was missing from the Wishaw area, has been traced safe and well."

She was found dead at her home just two days later on Sunday, November 10.

There are no suspicious circumstances surrounding her death.

Heartbroken loved ones have paid tribute to the mum on social media.

Vicky Kennedy wrote: "So sorry to hear about Emma, totally shocked.

"My heart goes out to all her family, especially her kids.

"She will be sadly missed."

Linda Dougal said: "Thoughts are with her family and friends so tragic."

Pauline Lennox said: "Heartbreaking x."

 

A Police Scotland spokesperson told the Record: "Police Scotland can confirm that the body of a 32-year-old woman was found at her home in Gowkthrapple, Wishaw on Sunday, 10 November 2019. 

"There are no suspicious circumstances and the Procurator Fiscal has been informed."
