George Williams and Jake Trueman urged to make England boss Wayne Bennett's life difficult

Debut hopefuls must seize chance to stake claim for next year’s England series against Australia when Great Britain face Papua New Guinea in tour finale

12 Nov 2019, 23:41 by Julie Stott

George Williams and Jake Trueman have been urged to go out and make Wayne Bennett’s life difficult.

Gareth Widdop and Jackson Hastings have been the half backs in the first three Great Britain Tests Down Under.

Despite three defeats, coach Wayne Bennett is thought to favour them for next year’s England Series with Australia if he is still in charge.

But Bennett is expected to hand debuts to both Williams and Trueman in Saturday’s final Series game against Papua New Guinea.

It’s a chance assistant coach Ian Watson says the two youngsters must grab with both hands.

 

He said: “They need to have a real good input into the game and stake a claim going forward.

“They need to put their best performance out there at the weekend so that they are not coming on any tour again and not playing a part.

“They need to make sure they force Wayne Bennett’s hand so that they get selected.”

Zak Hardaker could make a shock return after being ruled out of last week’s second Test against New Zealand with a shoulder injury.

 

The Wigan full-back was expected to move from centre last week, where he’d played in the first two Tests, to the wing following injury to Ryan Hall.

His injury in training was initially expected to end his tour but he has been named in a 21-man squad along with Joe Philbin.

The Warrington forward was ruled out last week with a slight hamstring injury, after making an impressive debut in the first Test defeat by New Zealand.

If Philbin plays, it will complete a remarkable hat-trick of international appearances in Papua New Guinea by the 24-year-old.

 

He played there for Ireland in the 2017 World Cup and toured there last year with England Knights.

He said: “I am such a PNG veteran that I think I’m going to get citizenship. I’ve loved every visit here.

“It’s a life changing experience and it makes you far more appreciative of what you’ve got in your life.

“A lot of people live in poverty but they absolutely love their rugby league and are so happy when a team tours. I’m so glad the Great Britain lads are experiencing it.”

The Lions received a heroes' welcome when they arrived in Port Moresby yesterday, with hundreds of fans greeting them at the airport.

Centre Jake Connor said: “It was a bit crazy, a bit mind blowing. It was like something off a film - everyone wanted to touch you.”

The Lions always name a 21-man squad on Tuesday with the first 17 normally expected to play.

But this week’s team will not be decided until after training on Thursday. The 21-man squad is the same 17 as last week, plus Philbin, Williams, Trueman and Hardaker.
