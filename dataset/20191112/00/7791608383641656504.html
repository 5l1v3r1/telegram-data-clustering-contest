Greta Thunberg Mural Faces Mockery For Looking Too Much Like Vladimir Putin

12 Nov 2019 by Thanussha Priyah

A 60-foot-tall mural of climate activist Greta Thunberg has been painted at the heart of San Francisco by Argentinian Street artist Andres Iglesias, also known as Cobre. However, the looming Thunberg painting has gone viral on the internet for the wrong reasons. Many have pointed out their beliefs that the painting resembles Russian president Vladimir Putin.
According to Newsweek, social media users drew comparisons between the two due to their similar facial features. Putin and Thunberg both have “wide jawbones” and “the same intense blue eyes,” the publication describes.
Along with other internet users, notable British television host Piers Morgan shared the sentiment online. “Why are they turning her into Putin?” he wrote in a Twitter post.
The irony of likening Thunberg to Putin boils down to the relationship between the two. Previously, the Russian leader stated that 16-year-old’s UN speech was “poorly informed,” although he believed she was a “kind and sincere girl.”
Thunberg wittily responded to the statement by changing her Twitter bio to read, “A kind but poorly informed teenager,” for a short period of time.
The larger-than-life painting of Thunberg was put up by Iglesias, with backing by non-profit organization One Atmosphere, who provided the paint supplies. artnet News reports that the spray cans used in the project will be later repurposed into a sculpture.
Despite the internet’s mockery, the artist has shifted the focus back to his message, “climate change is real.” Iglesias told SF Gate that Thunberg “knows what she’s doing” and he hopes that this painting will spark the interest of many to begin taking care of the world.
