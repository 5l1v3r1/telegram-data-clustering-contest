Once upon a time

12 Nov 2019, 00:30 by Bangalore Mirror Bureau

Book a class with these facilitators to learn the magical art of puppetry

Treasure trove
Master puppeteer Anupama Hoskere owns the single largest puppet collection in India. She runs an organisation called Dhaatu in Bengaluru, which is single-handedly trying to revive puppet theatre in the state. This NGO aims to bridge the gap between what the young generation wants
to achieve and what traditional sights they tend to lose in the process. So if you are interested in learning puppet theatre from them, don’t hesitate in calling them up. At 3944/F, 17 D Cross, 4th Main Rd, Banashankari Stage II, for details, call 9886193999

A bundle of stories
Once upon a time on a rainy day in 1998, three teachers met under a banyan tree to discuss storytelling and its impact on learning in society and schools, and so was formed Kathalaya – The House of Stories. They believe, puppetry is a strong tool to tell stories, express ideas and illustrate points and that’s why they host workshops on puppetry from time to time. Make a note of them. At 88, BHBCS Layout, Stage 2, BTM Layout, for
queries, call 8277389840

Make and manipulate
Puppetry is a form of theatre or performance that involves the manipulation of inanimate human or animal-like objects. So when Mythili,

a teacher, puppeteer and founder of Cappella-School of Art, conducts sessions for children, she gives children not only the chance to make puppets, but also manipulate them in theatre shows. Because, you know, practice matters! In order to revive this dying tradition and put children in touch with their ancient roots, she conducts puppetry programmes often. At Capella-School of Art, 2nd Floor, 275/B 28th main, 37th B Cross Rd, 9th Block, Jayanagar, for details, call 98458 89840

Strings attached
Many forms of puppetry are practised in India, of which, string puppetry is popular across Karnataka. Some literary texts and musical compositions have made a mention of this folk art, tracing back to the 12th Century. These shows are usually based on mythological stories that the local

community is familiar with. Cilre Learning Spaces LLP keeps conducting workshops in the city to teach children how to make string puppets.
For instance, they do it every Dussehra. At Cilre Learning Spaces LLP, 154, Koramangala 3 block, for details, call 9986015483
