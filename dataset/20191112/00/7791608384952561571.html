Tesco Pulls Mel B’s Image From Ad After Using Her Face Without Permission

12 Nov 2019 by Izza Sofia

Image via Tinseltown / Shutterstock.com
Spice Girls’ Mel B demanded for the CEO of Tesco to call her immediately after learning that the company had used her photo for a bus stop ad without her permission.
The singer took to Instagram to share a photo of the supermarket’s Clubcard ad, which featured Mel B in a leopard print bodysuit and a sports bra underneath it. The original image was taken at the 1997 Brit Awards.
Next to her was a speech bubble that read, “Stop right now. You get 10 percent off two big shops a month for 7.99 pounds.” The tagline seemed to be a play on Spice Girls’ hit song Stop, which features the lyric, “Stop right now.”
The singer was stunned to see herself in the blown-up billboard and demanded the CEO of Tesco Dave Lewis to contact her urgently. Since then, the supermarket has removed the image.

A spokesperson from Tesco said that the company had “authorization to use the image,” and the team was excited to feature her in their ad. However, the image was taken down after Mel B expressed unhappiness towards the advert.
The photo was part of Tesco’s 100th anniversary campaign that draws cultural references from its last 100 years.
