Stella McCartney Called ‘Disrespectful’ For Promoting Meghan Markle’s Black Coat

12 Nov 2019 by Yimin Huang

British designer Stella McCartney attracted a lot of criticism over her Instagram post featuring Meghan Markle on Remembrance Day. Some people in the comment section were upset over her focus on promoting her brand.
Remembrance Day is the UK version of Veterans Day, intended to commemorate veterans and service members for their sacrifices, per Harper’s Bazaar.
The original post showed an image of the Duchess of Sussex wearing a black belted coat by the designer while attending the commemorative military service on Sunday.

The caption then made direct reference to the brand, with McCartney saying she felt “honored” to have the Duchess in her “Autumn 2019 coat” at the service, according to Page Six.
Though some fans left positive remarks on the outfit, many commented that McCartney did not pay tribute to the important occasion and instead tried to use it to market her brand. In response, McCartney deleted the post but did not speak further about the controversy.
