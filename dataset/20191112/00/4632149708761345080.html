Things to do today in Bengaluru

12 Nov 2019, 00:30 by Bangalore Mirror Bureau

Folk factor
India has a long and rich history of folk art. The good news is that you can learn some of them, thanks to this new, 16-day course that begins in the city today. On offer are Kalamkari painting (from Andhra Pradesh and Telangana), Madhubani painting (from Mithila region) and Gond art (from Madhya Pradesh). You can either specialise in one or
dip your fingers into all.

WHERE: Pencil And Chai Fine Arts Gurukul, Jayanagar 9th Block WHEN: 11am-1pm, November 12
FEE: `11,500 (for specialisation) or `15,000 (for combined course)
CALL: 7676006006

Listen to relax
Sound healing is the latest wellness trend sweeping the town. This practice uses vibrations (vocal or instrumental-like gongs, Tibetan singing bowls and tuning forks) in order to relax the mind and beat lifestyle diseases such as back ache and neck pain. It is ideal for all age-groups, irrespective of their nature of work and fitness level.

WHERE: MyBoTree,60 Feet Rd, KHB Colony, 5th Block, Koramangala
WHEN: 3pm-4pm, November 12 ENTRY: `700, eventshigh.com

All about oils
Who wouldn’t fancy a spa-like treatment at home? Stop wishing and make it a reality. The Aromatherapy Home Spa Workshop will teach you just that – the basics about head massage oil, hair pack, face massage oil, lip balm and feet massage. Also learn how to give a body spa, using body scrub, body oil, bath salts, body butter, and body soap. It’s a beginner’s workshop, so don’t fret.

WHERE: Zorains Studio, Amar Jyothi Layout, 100 Feet Intermediate Ring Road WHEN: 11am-5pm,
November 12-13 CALL: 9900032855

Get on the dance floor
Have you tried Kizomba, Bachata or Salsa yet? All are Latin dance forms and equally addictive, take it from the fans. Come, discover the joy of Latin dancing at this party where dancer DJ Anthony and DJ Jitu will go behind the deck. The fun bit is, it’s a social party, which means, you can ask anybody on the floor for a dance with you.

WHERE: Indigo XP, Elite Building, Industrial Layout, Jyoti Niwas College Road
WHEN: 8.30pm onwards, November 12
ENTRY: `100 + `200 (cover)

Watch a film
An intense high-school drama set in Bangalore of the 1990s, Gantumoote is the tale of a sixteen-year-old girl and her subtle journey from the life that she thinks is just like in the movies to the actual life that unravels in front of her. In this coming-of-age story, the movie showcases her struggles with understanding herself, the changing world, educational pressures, bullying, academic competition – all of it wrapped up in a transcending journey of first love.

WHERE: Bangalore International Centre, 7, 4th Main Road, Domlur II Stage WHEN: 7pm-8.30pm, November 12
ENTRY: Free CALL: 9886599675


Shop till you drop
Buy a range of art and crafts from the Rajasthan Grameen Mela. From traditional Pata Chitra paintings to cloth-based scroll paintings, a number of rare and extraordinary works of art are on display. You can add an ethnic touch to your home with colourful Rajasthani handicrafts, Kashmiri carpets, Andhra puppets, ceramic post, lampshades and more. Or, up your fashion game with Bagalpuri sarees, Jaipuri and Punjabi jootis, Kolkata Shantiniketan bags, clunky tribal jewelry or silver jewellery.

WHERE: Karnataka Chitrakala Parishath, Kumar Krupa Road
WHEN: 10am-7pm, till November 17
ENTRY: Free CALL: 9841278707

Body basics
Named after a contemporary mystic, Gurdjieff is a sacred dance, whose beautiful and charismatic movements are believed to create a magical feeling inside you. Explore this dance form in a four-day programme, which seeks to bring attention to neglected and subtle facets of our lives at the most fundamental levels of existence. The sessions will make use of movement, contemplation and conversation.

WHERE: Shoonya, 4th Floor, Rear Wing, Brahmananda Court, Lal Bagh Main Rd
WHEN: 5.30pm-9pm, November 12
ENTRY: `2,800, instamojo.com

Poetry corner
Do you have something to share? Have you been pouring your heart out on paper, or do you want to bounce your writing off of someone? Write
Out Loud is the perfect place to do that. Every Tuesday they bring to you a heart-plucking session of poetry and good company. In the first half, participants read out their works on a pre-decided theme, which is ‘Unsung Heroes Of Mythology‘ this time. In the second half, they will discuss the life and times of poet and dancer Tishani Doshi.

WHERE: Lahe Lahe, HAL 2nd Stage, Indiranagar WHEN: 7.30pm-9.30pm, November 12 ENTRY: `177
CALL: 9886294444

Blast from the past
SmaranniK will stage two distinctly-flavoured Bengali plays this week. One is Nati Binodini, which revolves around the life of the famous South Asian actress, Binodini Dasi (1862–1941), who born to prostitution, started her career as a courtesan and eventually joined Calcutta’s National Theatre. As the play moves forward introducing several acquaintances of Binodini, it slowly takes a steep turn and brings into light the sacrifice, pain, betrayal, and misfortune of the famous actress, and how the mentally-devastated artist left the stage at the age of 25.

While this will be staged on Thursday, Sesh Shringo will go up on Friday. It revolves around Chitrabhanu, a famous poet. Search of his long-lost painter friend, who went missing 40 years ago during the Naxalbari revolution, urges the poet to constantly leave his home. Mahua, the poet’s wife, being the silent witness of his behaviour in all these years, becomes worried because of the poet’s deteriorating health.

WHERE: Ranga Shankara, JP Nagar, 2nd Phase
WHEN: 7.30pm, November 14-15 ENTRY: `200, bookmyshow.com
