EU’s hand extended to Belarus as Lukashenko visits Austria

12 Nov 2019 by BNN

The President of Belarus, Alexander Lukashenko is on Tuesday, November 12, on a visit to Austria. It is his first foreign visit to an European Union member state in three years.

Belgian news portal EurActiv reports that the Belarusian leader has met with Austrian President Alexander Van der Bellen in his first official visit to the Alpine nation following a trip of then Austrian head of government Sebastian Kurz to Belarus in March.

Often described as «Europe’s last dictatorship», Belarus has been the target of Western sanctions over its human rights violations and lack of fair elections, and 65-year-old Lukashenko has rarely visited EU member states.

Karin Kneissl, Austria’s former Foreign Minister under Kurz’s previous administration, noted in January that her country wanted to develop closer ties with Belarus as a «buffer state» between Russia, the EU and Ukraine, EurActiv reports.
