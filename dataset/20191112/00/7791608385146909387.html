Instagram Is Removing Like Counts—Here’s How Your Posts Will Look To Others

12 Nov 2019 by Thanussha Priyah

Instagram chief Adam Mosseri announced that the photo-sharing app will start masking “likes” on posts in the US starting this week during the Wired25 event last Friday.
The move was previously experimented in various regions including Australia, Brazil, Ireland, Italy, Canada, Japan and New Zealand. Now, it will be rolled out in the US with certain users before a full implementation.
“We will make decisions that hurt the business if they help people’s well-being and health,” Mosseri explained.
However, it isn’t completely eradicated. Users can still view like counts for their own photos and videos but not for posts uploaded by other account owners.
