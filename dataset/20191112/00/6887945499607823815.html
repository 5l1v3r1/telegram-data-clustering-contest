Auction Preview: Post-War and Contemporary Art Morning Session at Christie's, New York, November 14

12 Nov 2019 by BLOUIN ARTINFO

What: Post-War and Contemporary Art Morning Session

Where: Christie's, 20 Rockefeller Center, New York

When: November 14, 10:00 am

Public Viewing: November 10, 2019 | 1pm - 5pm, November 11, 2019 | 10am - 5pm, November 12, 2019 | 10am - 5pm, November 13, 2019 | 10am - 12pm

Top Lots at the Sale:

— “Terrain Vague” — Joan Mitchell (1925-1992); oil on canvas, signed “J Mitchell” (lower right); signed again and titled “Terrain vague J Mitchell” (on the stretcher); signed again “J Mitchell” (on the reverse). Painted in 1965. Measurement: 115.6 x 88.3cm (45 ½ x 34 ¾in). Featured in lot 614. Estimate: 2,800,000 - 3,500,000 USD

— “Gray Scramble IX (Single)” — Frank Stella (b. 1936); acrylic on canvas, titled “Gray Scramble IX (single)” on the stretcher. Painted 1968-1969. Measurement: 175.6x175.6cm (69 ⅛ x 69 ⅛ n). Featured in lot 607. Estimate: 2,000,000 - 3,000,000 USD

— “Untitled” — Alexander Calder (1898-1976); hanging mobile—sheet metal, wire, and paint. Executed circa 1947. Measurement: 80 x 140 x 11.4cm (31 ½ x 55 ⅛ x 4 ½in). Featured in lot 668. Estimate: 1,800,000 - 2,500,000 USD

— “Knives” — Andy Warhol (1928-1987); acrylic and silkscreen ink on canvas, stamped twice with the “Estate of Andy Warhol” and the “Andy Warhol Foundation for the Visual Arts, Inc.” stamps and numbered twice “Andy Warhol VF PA95.052” (overlap). Painted in 1981-1982. Measurement: 228.6 x 177.8cm (90 x 70in). Featured in lot 631. Estimate: 1,600,000 - 2,000,000 USD

— “Avatar” — Isamu Noguchi (1904-1988); bronze, incised with the artist's signature and the print run number “Isamu Noguchi 2/8” (lower right). Conceived in 1947 and cast in 1964. This work is number two from an edition of eight plus two artist's proofs. Measurement: 198.1x83.8x71.1cm (78 x 33 x 28in). Featured in lot 619. Estimate: 1,500,000 - 2,000,000 USD

— “Le Signe du Doigt (The Sign of the Finger)” — Jean Dubuffet (1901-1985); oil on canvas, signed and dated “J. Dubuffet 54”(upper right); signed again, titled and dated again “Le signe du doigt J. Dubuffet aout 54” (on the reverse). Painted in 1954. Measurement: 100.3 x 80.6cm (39 ½ x 31 ¾in). Featured in lot 659. Estimate: 1,500,000 - 2,000,000 USD

— “Pac” — Sam Gilliam (b. 1933); acrylic on canvas, signed, titled and dated “PAC 1970 Sam Gilliam” (on the reverse); titled again and dated again “PAC 1970” (on the reverse). Executed in 1970. Measurement: 269.4 x 335.3 x 8.9cm (106 x 132 x 2½in). Featured in lot 604. Estimate: 1,200,000 USD - 1,800,000 USD

— “Flowers” — Andy Warhol (1928-1987); acrylic and silkscreen ink on canvas, signed, stamped with the “Andy Warhol Art Authentication Board, Inc.” stamp, numbered and dated “Andy Warhol 65 A111.011” (on the reverse). Painted in 1965. Measurement: 61 x 61cm (24 x 24in). Featured in lot 629. Estimate: 1,200,000 - 1,800,000 USD

— “Blonde” — Roy Lichtenstein (1923-1997); oil and Magna on canvas, signed and dated “rf Lichtenstein '86” (on the reverse). Executed in 1986. Measurement: 153 x 81.3cm (60 ¼ x 32 in). Featured in lot 630. Estimate: 1,200,000 - 1,800,000 USD
