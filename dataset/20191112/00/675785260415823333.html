I'm A Celebrity's Jacqueline Jossa 'is scared of everything and hates creatures'

Former EastEnders actress Jacqueline Jossa is not looking forward to coming face-to-face with I'm A Celebrity's infamous creepy-crawlies but will use the jungle as a detox

12 Nov 2019, 00:14 by James Brinsford, https://www.facebook.com/james.brinsford

Jacqueline Jossa has spoken out about her fears as she enters the Australian jungle for I'm A Celebrity .

The 27-year-old former EastEnders actress has revealed that she "doesn’t do creatures" and is not looking forward to coming face-to-face with the millions of creepy crawlies the ITV show has in store.

Speaking ahead of her time in the jungle, Jacqueline - who quit in 2018 as Lauren Branning in the BBC1 drama after eight years - has said she is "scared of everything."

She believes her friends from Albert Square will be shocked to see her in the line-up for this year's show, especially as she has such a nervous disposition when it comes to insects.

Despite her fear of anything with eight legs, Jacqueline has said that being herself and not a fictional character is also going to be a challenge.

She said: "It’s going to be so difficult going in as me and that’s definitely going to be the hardest part. I have never done a show where I have been myself.”

“And I am also scared of everything – especially spiders! They are the worst, there will be tears!”

“My old colleagues on EastEnders are going to be shocked. I don’t think they can imagine me in there. I don’t like creatures. It is not my thing.”

It's not just the creepy-crawlies that could prove problematic as Jacqueline revealed that she is not really a 'great outdoors' person.

She continued: "I have also never done rock climbing, jumped out of a plane or camped in my life. I am not that kind of girl.

"But having said all that, I am really excited. This is something completely different and it’s going to be a totally new experience."

The TV actress also hopes to dispel some misconceptions about her.

Jacqueline explained: “I think people think I am a pushover or a doormat.

“I also think some people pity me. I want to show I am fine, I am up for a laugh and that I will go for it and try my best.”

One of her toughest challenges, as a mum to two young children, Jacqueline readily admits she is going to find it very tough being separated from them.

She admitted: “This is definitely the longest I have ever been away from them and that is going to be really hard.

“I didn’t know how to face even telling them. In the end, I thought the best idea was to say ‘mummy is going away for work’ and I would see them soon. My family is so close-knit and I will miss them all terribly.”
