Ex-finance supervisory office’s head applies for Bank of Latvia governor’s post

12 Nov 2019 by LETA

Ex-chairman of Latvia’s Finance and Capital Market Commission (FKTK) Uldis Cērps has officially submitted his candidacy for the post of governor of the Bank of Latvia.

Cērps has been among unofficial candidates for the aforementioned post for several months. His entry made on social networks on Monday, 11 November, states: ‘Today I submitted my candidacy for the post of governor of the Bank of Latvia. I believe it is important for Saeima deputies have a range of choice.’

After leaving FKTK, Cērps has worked in Sweden’s bank supervisory sector. He is currently employed as advisor to the governor of the Central Bank of the United Arab Emirates.

Among the candidates for the governor of the Bank of Latvia are also Bank of Latvia council member Mārtiņš Kazāks, ex-Finance Ministry’s State Secretary Sanita Bajāre and Fiscal Discipline Council member Inna Šteinbuka. All of them have proven their readiness to take this post and have met with specific parties.

Previously the media had mentioned World Bank’s senior finance sector’s specialist Kārlis Bauze and Latvian Swedbank’s board chairman Reinis Rubenis among Bank of Latvia governor candidates. Rubenis has said he is not interested in the post.

Although politicians actively talk about potential contenders, Prime Minister Krišjānis Kariņš has said names of potential candidates will announced after the national holiday. ‘Currently the names mentioned publicly are the same that were mentioned during the Cooperation Council meeting,’ says the politician.

The number of candidates may be revealed during the state holiday week. This is when selection of the candidates is set to begin. «Our country has a tough but pleasant choice, because all candidates mentioned thus far are capable of taking this post,» said Kariņš.
