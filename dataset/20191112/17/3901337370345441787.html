Ronaldo: Winning Ballon d'Or Is Like Winning A Nobel Prize

12 Nov 2019, 17:02 by SL International

Cristiano Ronaldo has revealed how important winning the Ballon d'Or is to him, while also hinting at his plans for the immediate future.

Read: Ronaldo Facing Two-Year Ban?

The Juventus superstar has won five Ballon d'Or titles, a record he shares with rival Lionel Messi, and has been nominated for the 2019 edition of the prestigious prize.

As reported by the Evening Standard, Ronaldo has now revealed how much winning the title means to him, and compared the accolade to a Nobel Prize.

"I have learned from (Michael) Jordan like I have learned from all of those who have excelled," he said.

"I am not just talking about sport either. I like to read things on the Nobel Prize – who has invented, generated, created or developed something.

"The Nobel Prize for these guys is like the Ballon d'Or for me. I like to know why they invented something and how they did it – it is important, and it helps me to get even better."

Ronaldo also revealed that he has no plans to retire any time soon, and feels he still has a lot to offer.

"My aim is to keep myself young as I get older… to remain competitive," he added.

"Approximately 70% of my life is football. You have to be intelligent to last. I call it the education of a player. You have to look after your life, nutrition and sleep.

"I have been sacrificing myself for 15 years. After (I retire), I will admire what I have done. More than that, though, what I will do is enjoy my friends, my family, see my children grow up and study.

"I savour the present. I want to transmit my life experience. Not now, though, I still have a lot to give."
