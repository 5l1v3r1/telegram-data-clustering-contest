Police warn Sutton Coldfield public to be 'vigilant' after 12 burglaries in 12 days

Police say eight break-ins across Sutton nd four more attempted burglaries have been reported since November 1

12 Nov 2019, 17:35 by Nick Horner

A rise in burglaries across Sutton Coldfield has been reported this month, with police calling on the public to report any ‘suspicious behaviour’.

Eight break-ins across the town and four more attempted burglaries have been reported since November 1, with police warning the public to be vigilant.

Thieves have struck in Churchill Road, Falcon Lodge, Roylesden Crescent, New Oscott, Yeomans Way and Wheatmoor Road on the Defence Estates, Wheatcroft Close in Roughley, Garnett Drive in Whitehouse Common, Banners Gate Road in Vesey and Silver Birch Coppice in Four Oaks.

Burglars have also attempted break-ins in Windyridge Road and Penns Lane in Walmley and Clarendon Road and Blackroot Road in Four Oaks.

West Midlands Police PCSO Rachel French, from the New Hall Neighbourhood Team, said: “We have recently seen an increase in burglary in the Sutton Coldfield area, these offences have been happening day and night.

“We recently received some great intelligence from a vigilant member of the public which resulted in two prolific offenders being stopped and subsequently arrested.

 

“We are appealing for other members of the public to be vigilant and report any suspicious behaviour or activity.

“This can be done calling 999 if the crime is in progress, calling 101 or live chat on the West Midlands Police website: www.west-midlands.police.uk

In addition the West Midlands force has also urged householders to secure ladders – having seen an increase in burglars using them to gain access to homes via upstairs windows.

Sergeant Rachel Darby for the Birmingham East area said: “Across the West Midlands we have recently seen a rise in burglaries where offenders are using ladders belonging to the homeowner to access upstairs windows.

 

“Although this type of offence is not commonplace in Sutton Coldfield, I would recommend that your ladders are stored away where possible. If this isn't possible, you could put a bike lock, or similar, around the rungs of the ladder to prevent it from being extended.

“Please also look out for your neighbours, and pass this advice on if needed.”
