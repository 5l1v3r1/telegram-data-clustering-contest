Food bank usage still high in Sask.: HungerCount by the numbers

Saskatchewan food bank usage is still high — especially among children and young adults, according to HungerCount 2019 data.

12 Nov 2019, 22:48 by Ashley Martin

Food Banks Canada on Tuesday issued its 2019 HungerCount report, which shows Saskatchewan food bank usage is still high — especially among children and young adults.

The data comes from March 2019 surveys of Canada’s food banks. Here’s a look at the numbers.

37,216 — total number of food bank visits in Saskatchewan in March 2019. That included 15,778 visits by children.

42.4 — the percentage of food bank users in Saskatchewan who were children, age 17 or younger. Saskatchewan had the second-highest proportion of children users; Manitoba led the country, with children accounting for 43.4% of food bank users.

34.1 — the percentage of food bank users across Canada who were children in March 2019, though children only comprise 19.4 per cent of the Canadian population.

17.4 — the percentage of food bank users in Saskatchewan aged 18 to 30. Nova Scotia and the territories saw higher usage among people in that age group.

3.6 — the percentage decrease in total visits to food banks across Saskatchewan, as compared to 2018.

42.4 — the percentage of food banks in Saskatchewan that reported an increase in usage, as compared to 2018.

45.1 — the percentage of food bank users in Saskatchewan who were single people.

12.4 — the percentage of Saskatchewan food bank users who had employment income.

6.1 — the percentage of food bank users in Saskatchewan who owned their home.

6.3 — the percentage of food bank users in Saskatchewan who were homeless.

6.4 — the percentage of food bank users in Saskatchewan who lived in First Nations band-owned housing.

17.9 — the percentage of Saskatchewan food bank users who were from two-parent families.

7.7 — the percentage of Saskatchewan food bank users who were couples with no children.

29.82 — the percentage increase in food bank users across Canada age 65 and older, since 2016.

7.4 — the percentage of food bank users who had pension income in Saskatchewan.

52.6 — the percentage of food bank users who were on social assistance in Saskatchewan.

9.1 — the percentage of food bank users who had disability-related income support in Saskatchewan.

16.3 — the percentage of Saskatchewan food bank users who lived in social housing.

43.4 — the percentage of food bank users across Canada who used the food bank because their social assistance benefits were too low.

19.03 — the percentage decrease in the real value of social assistance benefits in Saskatchewan, comparing 1989 data to 2017.

22 — the percentage of income that’s spent on rent, in average-income households in Saskatchewan. Low-income people reported spending up to 60 per cent of their income on rent and utilities.

19.4 — the percentage of food bank users across Canada who cited low or delayed wages as their reason for food bank use.

9.2 — the percentage of food bank users across Canada who cited job loss or unemployment as their reason for usage.

The full HungerCount report is available at hungercount.foodbankscanada.ca.
