Dad starts family after "living autopsy" on BBC showed he needed to change life

Ryan O’Connor, 31, was too anxious to have a baby with wife Robyn because of uncertainty over his long-term health prospects.

12 Nov 2019, 11:43 by Andrew Bargh

Appearing on a national television show provided the catalyst for a Chapelhall man to start a family.

Ryan O’Connor, 31, was too anxious to have a baby with wife Robyn because of uncertainty over his long-term health prospects.

However, Ryan’s involvement on the BBC show Laid Bare – which aired last week and showed him receiving a “living autopsy” to highlight problems with his lazy lifestyle – inspired him to make important changes and ultimately help bring little Chloe into the world just a fortnight ago.

Ryan told the Advertiser: “One of the main reasons for going on the show was my worry about my health for starting a family.

“I wanted to make sure I was in good enough shape to look after a child.

“I really wasn’t sure if I could but the changes I made after filming the show gave me the strength to know I can raise kids.

“Chloe has been a delight since she was born and I can’t wait to see her grow up. Robyn and I are delighted.”

During the show, which was filmed last year, Ryan was warned he had a 60 per cent chance of developing diabetes.

This revelation prompted him to ditch his fizzy juice addiction, cut down on sugary foods and begin exercising.

He continued: “I’m an IT engineer who works from home, so my commute is literally walking down the stairs. It was normal for me to pour a glass of Irn-Bru or Coke first thing in the morning.

“I’d sit in front of a screen for eight hours a day for work and then play video games afterwards.

“It was a very sedentary lifestyle and I was consuming so much sugar, which is a silent killer.

“I get up and go for a walk every hour of my day now and I’ve completely cut fizzy juice from my diet, whereas before I was drinking at least two litres a day.

“I’ve never been morbidly obese but I’ve lost a good amount of weight and am down to a 32-inch waist. My main goal was to really reduce the risk of diabetes.

“My life is about my girl Chloe now. I want to keep these changes going for her.”

Meeting a diabetes sufferer while filming the show proved to be a wake-up call for Ryan.

He explained: “We were introduced to a man who had his left leg amputated because his lifestyle caused diabetes.

“The scary thing was, it wasn’t dissimilar to mine. He ate badly, didn’t do much exercise and consumed loads of sugar.

“It was a bit of an eye-opener for me and he advised me not to keep up that lifestyle.

“I’m really glad I experienced the show because I have a much more positive outlook on life now and I’m delighted to have started a family.”
