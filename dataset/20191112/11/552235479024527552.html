Funeral of dad Christopher Nicol murdered in front of kids can't be held until killer is caught

Christopher Nicol's body must be kept in the mortuary while police probe his murder but his distraught mum believes someone in the Greenock community is sheltering the killer.

12 Nov 2019, 11:40 by Paul Rodger, Lorraine Tinney

A mum whose son was brutally murdered in front of his children told of her heartache that a funeral cannot be held until the killer is caught.

Christopher Nicol, 27, was stabbed to death in his own home in front of his two kids during a botched robbery four weeks ago.

He was at home with his girlfriend Courtney Lowe and his two children, Amylynn, aged six, and Darren, aged five, when a masked man burst in and attacked him with a knife on September 26 - and is believed to have said 'sorry' as he fled.

In the moments before the brutal attack, Christopher had been helping Darren with his colouring book while Amylynn was in the kitchen helping to make dinner.

Cops launched a manhunt but Christopher's distraught mum, Karen Nicol, 52, believes someone in the community in Greenock , Inverclyde, is sheltering the killer .

Heartbroken mum Karen, from Dunoon, Argyll, said she cannot get closure or say goodbye to her son until she holds his funeral.

For legal reasons, Christopher's body has to remain in the mortuary while detectives continue to search for his killer.

 

Karen said :"It's so upsetting that no-one is saying anything.

"He knew so many people.

"Somebody has got to be hiding this person, someone's got to be feeding him.

"He is an absolute scumbag, a rat.

"If it was one of my kids who had done that I would hand him in, especially for doing it in front of kids. It's evil.

"I don't know why he didn't realise that kids were there, didn't go back out and run away.

"He took away a daddy, a son, a brother, then he's got the cheek to turn round and say 'sorry'.

"It's an insult.

"He could be out there, he could walk past me and know who I am but I don't have a clue who he is.

"Is he going to do it again?

"What if it's a child this time, is he going to stab them?

"He's got no morals."

Karen, who also has another son Craig, 33, and daughter Demilynn, 24, said Christopher's death has devastated the whole family.

She lived in Greenock until moving to Dunoon 18 years ago, but Christopher moved back to Inverclyde when he met ex-partner Kathleen, whose daughter Jasmine he was a stepdad to.

Karen believes her son may have been targeted in a botched robbery, for £1,000 he inherited from his grandfather.

She said: "When my dad passed away he left all the grandkids £1,000.

"Christopher said he wasn't going to spend it, he said he would save it to take the kids on holiday.

"The only thing I can think of is if he's said that to anyone."

She urged anyone sheltering the killer to turn them into police.

Karen added: "It just takes one person to get a conscience.

"It won't bring Christopher back but it would make me feel as if justice has been done.

"If this guy is never found, it will kill me.

"I hate the idea of him lying up there on his own.

"I want him home with me here.

"I visited in the mortuary last Friday and placed a St Christopher on his chest and I promised him that I would get the man who killed him.

"I've been living in limbo since it happened.

"My heart has been totally ripped out.

"I lost my dad last year and we weren't even over that, and now to get this a year later.

"We are a small family and losing Christopher has had a ripple effect.

"It's amazing how many people are suffering because of this one incident, it's really affected so many people.

"As soon as I close my eyes, the tears just start streaming down my face.

"I just miss him so much.

"The children doted on him and he doted on them.

"Him and Darren were more like buddies, he was a big kid at heart and Amylynn is a daddy's girl.

"He used to say he was lost between a Monday and Friday without the kids.

"He had them all during the school holidays and took them to safari parks, trips to the seaside, down to Largs.

"It wasn't just money he was spending on them, he enjoyed being with them.

"The worst thing is seeing the pain they're going through.

"Darren said: 'Nanna I miss my daddy, that was your son', and they ask when will he be coming back.

"When their grandad died they said he went to heaven and their gran's up in heaven but when they talk about Christopher, they say 'the bad man killed my daddy'.

"It breaks my heart when they keep asking if he'll be coming back.

"When you cuddle in you feel their wee tears on you."
