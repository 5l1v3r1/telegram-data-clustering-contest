I'm A Celebrity 2019 line-up confirmed as ten contestants head to the jungle

Caitlyn Jenner and Ian Wright are heading to Australia

12 Nov 2019, 09:47 by Jessica Sansome, Jamie Hawkins

The full line-up for this year's I'm A Celeb has been confirmed, which includes a former England footballer and reality TV stars.

For the 19th time, several celebrities will spend life down under, facing gruelling challenges to be crowned king or queen of the jungle.

Manchester Evening News reports Declan Donnelly will be reunited once again with his co-host Ant McPartlin after This Morning star Holly Willoughby stepped in for presenting duties last year - the first time the programme has never been presented by the popular duo.

The campmates have been arriving in Australia ahead of the series and will be roughing it for the next few weeks with only each other and a few rogue animals for company.

Here's all you need to know about the upcoming series ahead of its star-studded return.

When does I'm A Celebrity 2019 start?

The popular reality show returns at 9pm on Sunday November 17 on  ITV1  and will be on at 9pm every day following this.

Accompanying show I'm A Celebrity: Extra Camp will also air at 10:30pm on ITV2 each day and will be presented by the programmes former stars Emily Atack, Adam Thomas and Joel Dommett.

The contestants taking part this year are:

Caitlyn Jenner

American reality star Caitlyn Jenner will be swapping her glamorous Californian lifestyle for that of the Australian jungle as she becomes one of the first to appear in this year's series.

The 70-year-old Olympic-gold medal winning decathlete is probably now best known for being part of the Kardashian-Jenner clan and stole the headlines in 2015 when she appeared on the cover of Vanity Fair magazine to announce that she had transitioned from Bruce Jenner into Caitlyn.

She may have experience when it comes to jungle life having starred in the US version of the show in 2003.

Ian Wright

Taking a break from punditry duty is former professional footballer Ian Wright as he hopes to follow in the footsteps of last year's winner Harry Redknapp.

The 56-year-old BBC Sport commentator has already been teased by Match Of The Day co-host Gary Lineker for his appearance in the ITV show.

Jacqueline Jossa

Former EastEnders actress Jacqueline Jossa will be donning her best camp attire as she takes part in the latest series.

The mum-of-two is best known for playing Lauren Branning in the BBC One soap but left after eight years in 2018.

Andrew Maxwell

Irish-born Andrew Maxwell is amongst the stars heading for the outback this year.

If you don't recognise him, reality TV fans may have heard the comedian's voice as he is known for narrating MTV's Ex on the Beach.

Adele Roberts

Adele Roberts is also no stranger to a reality TV show having previously appeared on Big Brother in 2002 - the year that Alison Hammond and the late Jade Goody were also stars.

The 40-year-old is now a DJ on BBC Radio 1 presenting the station's Early Breakfast Show as well as regularly standing in for fellow radio stars Alice Levine and Clara Amfo.

James Haskell

Famous for his skills on the rugby pitch, how will James Haskell fare in the I'm A Celeb jungle?

The former rugby union player made his name during his time at Wasps and Northampton Saints.

He also has a couple of famous in-laws as he is engaged to Chloe Madeley, the daughter of presenters Richard Madeley and Judy Finnigan.

Maybe Richard will be able to offer him some advice following his surprising role in Channel 4's recent series of The Circle.

Nadine Coyle

Former Girls Aloud star Nadine Coyle has also signed up for a stint on I'm A Celebrity.

The Irish singer, 34, is best known for her days in the girlband that racked up a total of 21 UK top ten singles during their seven-year career.

The band officially split in 2013 after reuniting for a 10th anniversary tour and album.

Myles Stephenson

One of the youngest stars to head Down Under is singer Myles Stephenson.

The 28-year-old will be best recognised for appearing on The X Factor in 2017 which he won as part of boyband Rak-Su.

Kate Garraway

 

Swapping her early starts on the Good Morning Britain set for the outback is Kate Garraway.

The 52-year-old presenter has remained coy when quizzed by her co-hosts on her appearance in the jungle but it seems she has finally took the plunge and headed for I'm A Celeb.

Roman Kemp

 

Capital FM radio presenter Roman Kemp completes this year's line-up.

The 26-year-old is the son of Shirlie Holliman and Spandau Ballet and Eastenders star Martin Kemp but his famous connections do not stop there as singer George Michael was also Roman's godfather.
