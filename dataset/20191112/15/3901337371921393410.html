PSL & Zambia Legend Retires!   

12 Nov 2019, 15:59 by Soccer Laduma

After more than 20 years of playing professional football, a PSL and Zambia legend has retired from the game.

Having first arrived in South Africa in 2005 to further his career with Free State Stars, following a remarkable career with Zambia top-flight clubs Nkana and Zesco United respectively, Joseph Musonda went on to also play for Golden Arrows and Polokwane City, before returning home in 2015.

Having spent the last four years with Nkana back home, it has now been confirmed that Musonda has hung up his boots at the age of 42 to take up a position in the technical team at Nkana.

“Legendary Joseph Musonda has returned to the technical bench as a physical trainer. He has since signed a two-year contract as he continues to build his coaching career. All the best legend,” Zambian Premier League side, Nkana confirmed in a statement on the club’s social media platforms.

Musonda has been playing in professional ranks in Zambia since 1995. He was part of the memorable Chipolopolo side that won the Africa Cup of Nations in 2012 under renowned French coach Herve Renard.
