In This Week's Edition Of Soccer Laduma

12 Nov 2019, 13:52 by Soccer Laduma

In anticipation of this week’s 1149th edition of Soccer Laduma, we preview some of the features included. The latest edition will be available at your nearest outlet tomorrow, Wednesday 13th November.

Wayne Sandilands Interview

Former Mamelodi Sundowns shot-stopper, Wayne Sandilands, has come under some scrutiny, by the football fraternity, during his time at Orlando Pirates. Despite a string of impressive performances, at the back-end of last season, Sandilands has come up against stiff competition, in the form of French goalkeeper, Joris Delle. The 36-year-old spoke about the Buccaneers' difficult start to the campaign and ensuring the top two don’t open up a further points gap.

Mosa Lebusa Interview

Despite Ajax Cape Town’s relegation from the Absa Premiership in the 2017/18 season, central defender, Mosa Lebusa has grown from strength to strength after his move to Mamelodi Sundowns. Now an established regular and an important part of the Bafana Bafana squad, Soccer Laduma’s Tshepang Mailwane, found out how the 27-year-old has taken to life in Chloorkop.

Doctor Khumalo- My Story

Forget the fame and fortune that comes with being a professional footballer. To get to the very top isn’t as easy as some would make it out to be. Bafana Bafana legend, Doctor Khumalo shares his journey and shows us that it is possible to beat your circumstances, regardless of how dire they may be, to make your football dreams a reality.

Also…

Norman Mapeza Interview

Many eyebrows were raised when former FC Platinum coach, Norman Mapeza was named Chippa United head coach, following Clinton Larsen’s departure earlier this season. Tasked with keeping the Port Elizabeth side in the Absa Premiership, the former Galatasaray player believes he can drag the club out of relegation, by the end of this season.

GladAfrica Championship interview

TS Galaxy centre-back, Maluleke Mathonsi, caught up with Soccer Laduma’s Peter Rath, as he shared the journey into professional football and how he has had to deal with the difficulties of reaching a very high level within South African football and more.

Trent Alexander-Arnold feature

Liverpool young full-back, Trent Alexander-Arnold is probably one of the best in Europe, in his position and for good reason. Born and bred in Liverpool, the academy product is now a UEFA Champions League winner and a guaranteed starter. Soccer Laduma charts his journey from the youth ranks to one of Anfield’s household names.  

Revolving Column

In this week’s revolving column, Bafana Bafana and Cape Town City defender, Thato Mokeke, reflects on the mood in the camp, key injuries, and challenges the national team will face, in their next two AFCON qualifying fixtures, against Ghana away and Sudan at home in Orlando.

Siyagobhoza

In this week’s edition of our beloved Siyagobhoza, the crew reveals news concerning Kaizer Chiefs frontman, Leonardo Castro. The Colombian’s turnaround has been nothing short of remarkable. Before the season began, Amakhosi were ready to cut their losses and let Castro go, however, this is no longer the case. Scoring some really important goals for Chiefs this season, backed by stellar performances, could the Glamour Boys reconsider Castro’s future?
