An 18ct gold diamond ring, a Rolex watch and luxury holiday homes seized from criminals up for grabs

A Mercedes Benz A250, a plot of land in Canada and a two-storey holiday home in Croatia are among the items up for auction next week

17 Nov 2019, 13:41 by Charlotte Hadfield

An 18ct gold diamond ring, a Rolex watch and a plot of land in Canada with a guide price of £50,000 could all be yours next week.

These luxury goods are items which many can only dream of buying in their lifetime.

But now thanks to an Unreserved Government Auction, the items will be sold to the highest bidder with no reserve price, after they were seized by police from criminals living the high life.

Wilsons Auctions is responsible for selling off the ill-gotten gains seized by police forces around the country including Merseyside, with this month's auction taking place in Newport on November 20.

All of the high-end goods going under the hammer were  seized through the Proceeds of Crime Act (POCA)  - which gives the authorities power to seize items that may have been bought with dodgy money.

The ECHO took a sneak peek at the items going up for auction this month, which could make an appearance on your Christmas list.

This includes a two-storey holiday home in Croatia with a guide price of £90,000, a Mercedes Benz A250 and a pair of Gucci shoes.

There will also be designer shoes and clothing from Balenciaga, Versace, Giuseppe Zanotti, Moncler and Stone Island.

The plot which is subject to planning permission, offers an amazing chance to build your dream home with a guide price of £50,000 but no reserve.

Meanwhile, a two-storey property in Orebic, Croatia, with a guide price of £90,000, will also be on sale and offers bidders the chance to invest in a refurbishment project with the end goal of a fantastic holiday home on the Dalmation Coast.

Other property includes an 18th Century Grade II listed cottage in the historical village of Gayhurst, Buckinghamshire with an 100ft garden and stunning views of the idyllic countryside.

And closer to home, a two-bedroom detached bungalow, is up for auction, located within a cul-de-sac in Morley, Leeds with a guide price of £175,000.

Also up for auction is a 2017 Seat Leon Cupra, a Blue Topaz Brooch and a wide selection of diamond and gold jewellery including an 18ct yellow gold diamond and ruby ring with an estimated retail value of £1,800 and no reserve.

Wilsons Auctions’ Government Sales Coordinator, Michael Streight said, “With the festive season fast approaching you don’t need to look any further than our upcoming Unreserved Government Auction.

"Offering bidders the chance to purchase luxury and designer goods, with all items unreserved, it’s a great opportunity to be one-step ahead with your Christmas shopping!

“With the auction available to both physical and online bidders it is open to a worldwide audience and gives customers the chance to purchase quality goods for an affordable price.

"Wilsons Auctions plays an important role in realising any type of asset on behalf of our government clients which has seen us responsible in returning £100 million back into the public purse in recent years."
