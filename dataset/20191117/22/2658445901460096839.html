Funding, connecting SMEs to global players - The Nation Newspaper

17 Nov 2019, 22:06

GTBank Fashion Weekend has been at the forefront of promoting enterprise in Nigeria and Africa’s growing fashion industry. This year’s event, which held in Lagos, brought together fashion experts and personalities to interact with young Nigerians passionate about fashion and entrepreneurship. The event was in line with the Central Bank of Nigeria’s (CBN’s) directive to banks to fund Small and Medium Enterprises (SMEs) and creative industry for sustainable economic growth, writes COLLINS NWEZE.

 

ENTREPRENEURSHIP is seen as the backbone of great economies.  For many Nigerian youths, who graduate from universities without  good job opportunities, embracing entrepreneurship remains the best option.

Already, many of them, rather than roam the streets,  are unleashing their creative talents in creating jobs not only for themselves, but for others through the music, movie, fashion and Information Technology (IT) skills. But they also need the support of financial institutions to realise their dreams and play at the global stage.

The GTBank Fashion Weekend, a consumer-focused fair and free business platform, designed to showcase the best of Africa’s fashion to global audience and promote the  enterprise of the continents growing fashion industry, which just ended in Lagos, was one of such opportunities for the youths.

The event was an opportunity for Nigerian designers and youths in the creative industry to learn from global industry leaders and experienced brands on how to invest and move the local fashion industry forward.

The GTBank, according to organisers, believed that the little it could do was to create opportunities for the youths to access credit and bank loans to grow their businesses.

The event, which held between November 9 and 10 at the GTCentre, Water Corporation Drive, Victoria Island, featured over 30 distinguished fashion leaders, designers and industry experts. It also provided more than 130 indigenous Small and Medium Enterprises (SMEs) with free stalls to showcase and sell their apparels and fashion accessories to the world.

It was in line with the Central Bank of Nigeria’s’ (CBN’s) directive to banks to lend and support Small and Medium Enterprises (SMEs’) and support the creative industry.

According to the CBN Governor, Godwin Emefiele, Nigeria’s creative industry, which comprises the music, movie, fashion and Information Technology (IT) are beginning to emerge as key source of wealth and job creation in Nigeria.

He explained that across Africa and various parts of the globe, Nigeria’s creative industries, are spurring innovation, creating jobs, increasing export revenue and helping to shape perceptions on Nigeria, as a nation with a strong spirit of creativity and ingenuity.

The GTBank Fashion Weekend 2019 was one of the milestones by the bank to support SMEs and grow the creative industry.

Featuring dye presentations, popup shops, master classes and runway shows, the event connected Nigerian SMEs to a wider audience of consumers as well as their peers at home and abroad while providing learning opportunities for business owners and consumers.

The series covered topics addressing challenges and opportunities across the entire industry value chain, including entrepreneurship, growth, profitability, retailing, and several others.

Fashion enthusiasts were also treated to ultimate shopping experience as dozens of small businesses showcased range of carefully curated affordable and luxury apparel, footwear, accessories, beauty items, and much more. As part of the retail experience there will be a crafts market featuring indigenous fabric and accessories. The event closed daily with a series of epic runway shows featuring Africa’s finest designers and renowned international designers.

Commenting on the 2019 GTBank Fashion Weekend, the bank’s Chief Executive Officer and Managing Director, Segun Agbaje, noted that “Africa is home to some of the most creative fashion talents on the planet”, adding that the bank was delighted and proud to provide them with a global platform that connected them to the world.

“As we work towards the fourth edition of the biggest fashion showcase in Africa, our goal is to not only showcase the wealth of talent, innovation and enterprise that abound in Africa’s fashion space, but to grow the continent’s contribution to the global fashion industry by empowering budding entrepreneurs at home with everything they need to thrive on the world stage,” he said.

He further stated that at the heart of the GTBank Fashion Weekend was the vision of promoting enterprise in industries where it believed that small businesses need help to grow and dominate. “In fashion, as well as in food and the creative industry, we see not just the amazing talents and passion of our people, but how, by championing their entrepreneurial spirit, we can enrich millions of lives, uplift communities and grow our economy,” he said.

The event also featured a masterclass from several fashion industry experts, who educated the audience. They included the Ogidi Women, who  are one of the creative forces behind the evolution of Aso-Oke from linear designs to more vibrant and colourful styles.  The Ogidi Women made  live presentation on the Art and Craft of weaving Aso-Oke.

Others included the Law Roach, also known as the Image Architect, one of America’s most celebrated stylists was also at the event. His works have appeared in leading publications such as Vogue, Time Magazine, GQ, Billboard, New York Magazine, Notion, among others.

David Au is the Editor-in-Chief of Vogue, Hong Kong. Prior to joining Vogue HK, he was the Group Managing Director for leading Asian conglomerate, Fung Retailing Group, overseeing the overall marketing for the nine business units, including Toys “R” US, Macy’s China Limited, among others.

Unveiling the  runaway designers

The designers, who displayed their creative works on the runway were Lanre Da Silva, Huishan Zhang, Imane Ayissi, Ituen Basi, Sukeina, Fruche, Mafi Mafi, Studio 189 and Laquan Smith.

Others included Threeasfour, Odio Mimonet, Mantsho, Haus of Stone, Style Temple, Nkwo, Tzar Studios, Thula Sindi, Lezanne Viviers among others.

Haus of Stone,  a Zimbabwean ready-to-wear brand, driven by what the designer called a philosophy of “surrealism, harmony, and afro-minimalism”. Founded in 2014 by Dan Ayi Madondo, the brand’s fluid, hand-crafted apparel is both ethically and locally sourced and mixes elements of fantasy with subtleties of African influence.

Style Temple is a Nigerian brand founded by Og Okonkwo. The brand aesthetics is a combination of classic yet modern fashion and a play on balance between the feminine and edgy. StyleTemple diffusion ready-to-wear line is a mixture of structured wearable pieces with a couture edge, clean geometric cuts, and shapes with a standout detail. Easily fitted garments that flatter the woman’s body is our signature style.

Nkwo’s Collections, initially founded in 2007 in the United Kingdom by Nkwo Onwuka, the eponymous label relaunched in 2012 as an artisanal brand in Nigeria. Creating pieces that are free-spirited and nomadic, Nkwo’s collections are inspired by the lives of the wandering people of the African deserts and the great plains.

Thula Sindi is a Johannesburg-based designer and manufacturer of contemporary women’s wear that is sophisticated, elegant and timeless. The eponymous clothing label creates clothing inspired by the modern woman, which is timeless, dynamic, elegant, trendy and sophisticated.

Brazilian designer, Paula Cademartori, who spoke at the Masterclass session of the event,   said pricing and quality of designs are key elements that young designers should keep in mind.

“It is important to have the right price tag, but it is also important to know how the piece was done and the right quality. I think it is important to start with a low price and grow with that. People are always looking at the right quality at the right price,” she said.

On how people should start fashion business, Cademartori, said designers can start small, and grow from there, adding that how people feel in what they wear is also important,

“I think that shoes are important, and complement the dressing. I also think that the person wearing it should feel unbelievable wonderful,” she said.

On building global brand, Cademartori, said there was need for designers needed to have  have the quality on their side. “The world is full of handbags and shoes and you need to give a good quality that people will recognise you for. You have to manage how you want to enter. Any thing you put a price on, must worth that price. If you are going to chose a name, it is very important that the relationship with you and your style. It is important to have a team with the same vision and supporting the vision,” she said.

The Brazilian designer said she researched all markets her design would enter to have the best idea of what the customers want.

“You can believe not just on the brand, but the quality and beauty. It is also important to involve the client to get the best design. Being a designer is a job, you need methodology and very important to have like one hour a day to create a wonderful design that will impress your customers,” she said.

In a video interview on the bank’s website, Jennifer Obayuwana of Polo Avenue, described the GTBank Fashion Weekend as epic.

Understanding the GTBank Fashion Weekend

Created in 2016, the GTBank Fashion Weekend has become a platform for showcasing Africa’s finest and promoting real commerce for small businesses in the fashion industry.

In the last three editions, the show has hosted 500,000 Fashion Enthusiasts, 300 SMEs, 57 leaders in the fashion industry from 13 countries. The show has thus far presented 29 runway shows, offering spectators artistic insights into the modern world of Nigerian and global fashion design. It has also offered 27 master classes, which has provided fashion entrepreneurs the unique opportunity to learn from global industry leaders and experienced brands.

Experts believe that banks and other stakeholders needed to do more to encourage the innovative work of youths and other young talented Nigerians as it could significantly contribute to the growth and development of the country.
