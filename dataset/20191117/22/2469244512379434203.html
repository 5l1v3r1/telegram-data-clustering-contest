Two arrested after car mounts kerb and ploughs into Ottery St Mary church

St Anthony's Catholic church suffered damage during the incident

17 Nov 2019, 22:17 by Carl Eve

Two people have been arrested on suspicion of drink driving after a car careered off the road and into a church in Ottery St Mary.

St Anthony's Catholic Church was struck by the white BMW at around 12.30am today in the east Devon town.

Devon and Cornwall Police and Devon and Somerset Fire and Rescue Service firefighters arrived at the scene to find the car embedded in a corner wall. The car had knocked down a large number of bricks and damaged a stained glass window depicting the Holy Eucharist.

Police confirmed that two people had been arrested on suspicion of drink driving and taken into custody. It is understood they remain in custody at this time.

Police said there was no issue regarding the physical integrity of the church.

Locals posted a number of images of the crash and the aftermath on the Ottery Matters Facebook page.

Anyone who witnessed the incident or has information about it should call police on 101 or email 101@dc.police.uk quoting log number 23 of 17/11/2019.
