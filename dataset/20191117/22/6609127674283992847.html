PROUD MOMENT

Motherwell star Gallagher almost cried knowing family watched him play for Scotland

17 Nov 2019, 22:28 by David Friel

THE Tartan Army have been in tears watching Scotland recently.

Declan Gallagher felt like crying as he prepared to play for his country.

The Motherwell defender opened up on his long-awaited Scotland debut in Cyprus.

Gallagher, 28, was overcome with emotion as his proud family watched on at the GSP stadium in Nicosia.

The former Celtic kid is a late developer and admits pulling on the Scotland strip meant everything to him.

He said: “My mum and dad, my wife and my little girl, and my wife’s mum and dad were all over.

“They’d been over since Monday so it was like a holiday for them. It was good they were there to see me get my first cap.

“I said to my wife that it was special. I texted her saying I felt like crying knowing that her and my little girl were there to see me get my first cap.

“It was amazing. My mum and dad were over in Russia too when I was in the squad. They go everywhere, they never miss a game.

“They were happy going with the Tartan Army into Moscow and just basically drank a few bars dry.

“They were happy with that but it’s definitely a reward that they got to see me play in Cyprus.

“It’s a big achievement for me and my family because they are the rock behind me. It was great as much for them as it was for me.

“There was also a wee round of applause at the end of the game back in the dressing room.

“The boys gave a round of applause for Steven Naismith getting 50 caps and for me getting my first. It was a special moment for me.”

Gallagher has had a long road to the Scotland squad, coming through dark times in his personal life.

He was jailed for assault in 2015 and has previously thanked Livingston for standing by him.

Playing for Scotland would have seemed a dream back then but he has managed to rebuild his career.

Now he wants to cement his place in the national side, just like his early mentors at Celtic did.

He said: “I looked up to the likes of Gary Caldwell and Stephen McManus, they were absolutely excellent.

“I was in the youth team at Celtic when I was watching them. I saw them in training and it was great.

“I want to be like that, be in the Scotland set-up all the time and hopefully I’ve done enough to do that. I owe thanks to everybody that has worked with me.

“Livingston were a big part of it and Stephen Robinson has pushed me massively. He’s pushed me on and off the park and he is always pushing me in the press.

“I owe a massive thanks to him and the backroom staff at Motherwell because they push me non-stop.

“They probably deserve as much credit as I do for getting into the Scotland squad.”

Gallagher got the nod ahead of Mikey Devlin and Ryan Porteous to play beside Scott McKenna.

He thanked Steve Clarke for showing faith in him and hopes to keep his place for the clash with Kazakhstan.

He said: “Once I got my breath, I enjoyed it. I found out I was starting the night before the game.

“We had a team meeting and the gaffer likes to prepare the boys.

“It’s just to get any nervousness out of your head so you know you’re playing and you can focus on your game.

“I think nerves are a big part of the game. If you aren’t nervous it means you have no emotions.

“It’s a European qualifier and my first cap so it’s a big moment in my career.

“It’s good to be nervous at the start, but as soon as the whistle goes that all goes out the window and you focus on the game.

“All I can do is take my chance and hopefully I’ve done enough to get another cap.

"But there are a lot of good players out injured just now and a lot of good players in the squad so I just have to keep doing well.

“Cyprus played some good football but I felt we were comfortable. I was delighted with the win on my debut.

“It’s up to the manager what he does on Tuesday. He might want to freshen it up. I just need to keep working hard in training and see where it takes us.”

Gallagher will head back to Motherwell later this week and hopes boss Robinson will be there to greet him.

The Fir Park gaffer remains in the frame for the Hearts and Northern Ireland jobs but the Scotland man doesn’t want to lose him.

He said: “I’ve texted him a few things, saying ‘don’t think you’re going anywhere’ and stuff like that.

“To be fair, he deserves it. He’s an incredible manager and for me one of the best in Scotland.

“His attention to detail is magnificent and he deserves to go to the very top.

“Now I’ve been there and done it he is going to push me to stay there and keep going up and up. Hopefully that’s what I can do.”
