Barmaid thanked friends for 'cheering her up' hours before her body was found

17 Nov 2019, 22:58

A ‘beautiful’ barmaid thanked her friends for ‘cheering her up’ hours before her body was discovered near a fire station.

Sara Hopkins, 25, was found dead in Upton-upon-Severn at around 8am yesterday.

A man, 44, has been arrested on suspicion of murder.

Hours before her death, she had posted on Facebook, saying she was grateful to her friends for raising her spirits after ‘a s**t week’.

Checking herself into country pub The Drum and Monkey, she wrote: ‘Caz and Gordy cheering me up after a s**t week, I have the best friends.’

Tributes poured in from Sara’s friends after her death, with best friend Holly Barrett thanking her for ‘stopping by and bringing so much joy in life’.

She said: ‘Today it’s really sunk in that you’re not here anymore and I can’t get over the fact that you’re gone.

‘You were my best friend during high school and carried on making me laugh and smile into our twenties.

‘You were always so positive, kind and a great laugh to be around.

‘You had my back when not many others did and I’ll always be grateful for the time that we spent together.’

Sara’s ex-boyfriend Martin Haste, 54, said he had been left ‘absolutely devastated’ by her death.

He told The Sun Online: ‘It’s such a shock. We split up three months ago but stayed good friends.’

According to Sara’s Facebook page, she was working as a Planning Expeditor for local door supplier Permadoor.

Detective Inspector Jim Bayliss said: ‘Our enquiries are very much in their early stages and at this stage the woman’s death is being treated as suspicious.

‘A man has been arrested and is currently in police custody.

‘Extra officers will be in the area, both to carry out enquiries and to offer reassurance.’

Anyone with information can ring West Mercia Police on 101 quoting reference 150s 161119 or speak to Crimestoppers anonymously on 0800 555 111.
