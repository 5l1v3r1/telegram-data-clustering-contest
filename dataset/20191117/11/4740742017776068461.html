Bristol City midfielder Joe Morrell outshines Gareth Bale on Wales competitive debut

The Bristol City man was handed a chance to shine in the Euro 2020 qualifier

17 Nov 2019, 11:41 by John Evely

Bristol City midfielder Joe Morrell has earned rave reviews for his performance in his competitive debut for the Wales senior side.

The midfielder played a key role in Wales’ crucial 2-0 victory over Azerbaijan on Saturday thanks to goals from Kieffer Moore and Harry Wilson.

Morrell – who is on a season-long loan at League One side Lincoln City – was a surprise starter for Euro 2020 qualifier which the Welsh had to win as they chase automatic qualification to next summer’s tournament.

Morrell replaced the suspended Joe Allen in midfield while Juventus’s Aaron Ramsey had to settle for a place on the bench, having recently recovered from injury.

The 22-year-old repaid manager Ryan Giggs’ faith with an impressive display in Wales’ victory.

Morrell was given a rating of 8/10 and named Star Man by Wales Online’s Ian Mitchelmore, who wrote: “Fine effort from distance early on and looked slick in possession throughout. Performed very well in the absence of Joe Allen. Booked late on, but it was a top display from the central midfielder.”

The Press Association’s Jack Davies also gave Morrell 8/10.

He wrote: “Picked unexpectedly at the expense of Aaron Ramsey but won the corner that led to the opening goal and was also involved in the build-up to Wales' second. Hardly put a foot wrong.”

Teammate and Wales talisman Gareth Bale was given 7/10 by both.

With Slovakia losing 3-1 in Croatia, Wales know victory against Bristol City team mate Adam Nagy's Hungary in Cardiff on Tuesday will seal a top two place in Group E and automatic qualification to the European Championships, which will be held across 12 stadiums around the continent, including Wembley.
