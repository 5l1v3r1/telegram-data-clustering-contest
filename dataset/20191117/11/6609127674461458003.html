OWN GOAL

Shoppers spot embarrassing error as Sainsbury’s squash put on the shelves in Tesco

17 Nov 2019, 11:13 by Eleanor Langford

A BOTTLE of Sainsbury’s own-brand squash was spotted somewhere unusual by one shopper - on the shelves in Tesco.

The bottle and contents of the Sainsbury’s squash look almost identical to the Tesco own-brand version, with the only difference being the labels for two entirely different supermarkets.

This gaffe was spotted by Demi Boonham from Lichfield, who posted a snap in the Reduce Your Supermarket Spend Facebook group with the caption “spot the error”.
In the photo, a single summer fruits squash with a Sainsbury’s label can be seen among several others with Tesco labels.

Many users speculated that the mistake showed that both stores were stocking the exact same product, with the manufacturer simply changing the label.

One commenter said: “Just goes to show that some brands are actually the same.”

In fact, near identical bottles of this squash are available in Asda, Morrisons, Tesco, Sainsburys and Co Op.

As well as looking similar, the products also have near-identical ingredient lists and nutritional information.

It is not clear how the Sainsbury’s own brand product came to be on the shelves at Tesco.

Some commenters also shared their experiences of finding own-brand products in the wrong supermarket.

One user wrote: “I got broccoli with a Sainsbury’s label on in Tesco the other day and the cashier thought I'd planted it.”

Another said: “I bought Sainsbury's milk in Aldi once before.”

And, it seems supermarkets make the error with home deliveries also, one person said: “I got Morrison’s gluten free sausages in my Tesco delivery once.”

We have contacted both Tesco and Sainsbury’s for comment and will update this story when we get a response.
