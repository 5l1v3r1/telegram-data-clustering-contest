Kremlin says 'Normandy' summit to happen later this year

17 Nov 2019, 11:52 by Reuters

MOSCOW — Kremlin aide Yuri Ushakov said that the meeting of France, Germany, Russia and Ukraine leaders aimed at a peaceful resolution to the conflict in eastern Ukraine will take place at the end of this year, Interfax reported on Sunday.

The summit plans are not directly tied to the adoption of a special status for eastern Ukraine, Interfax cited Kremlin spokeman Dmitry Peskov as saying. (Andrey Kuzmin, editing by Louise Heavens)
