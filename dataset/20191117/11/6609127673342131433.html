JUSTICE FOR RHYS

James English invites Rhys Bonner’s heartbroken mum on podcast as she appeals for dashcam footage to solve mystery death

17 Nov 2019, 11:33 by Katy Pagan

THE heartbroken mum of a Celtic fan found dead in marshlands is set to appear on James English's podcast in her bid for answers about his death.

Stephanie Bonner, 36, has been going door-to-door searching for clues since tragic Rhys’ half-naked body was found in marshland.

The Hoops-daft lad, 19, disappeared as he headed to meet a pal near his home in Barlanark, Glasgow, on July 24.

He was found a fortnight later a mile away in a bog at Seven Lochs Wetland Park.

In her latest social media appeal for information, Stephanie urged drivers to check dashcam footage for any clues.

She said: "Anyone that was at Easterhouse on July 24, could you please check your dashcam in your cars.

"You never know, you could have come across something.

"He was with an older woman, could have been more people with them."

Rhys was last captured on CCTV at the shops on Conisborough Road, Easterhouse,  walking down Gardyne Street at 7.45pm.

When he vanished, he was wearing a long sleeved Celtic top, grey North Face hat, black Adidas trainers and dark trousers.

Glasgow podcast star James is now hoping to get his listeners on board with Stephanie's appeal.

Confirming his upcoming guest, he said: "I reached out to Rhys's mum Stephanie and spoke with her on the phone.

"She’s such a strong women and I told her I would have her on my show no problem.

"I want to help her get more exposure and hopefully get some answers."

We told earlier how Stephanie mounted her own probe after being told her son was last seen with a woman in her 40s.

Police scoured CCTV and found footage of the pair.

Mum-of-five Stephanie said: “I knew then something had happened to him. Rhys was half-naked when they found him.

“Why was he lying like that? This woman has never approached me to tell me why he was with her, where he went or what could have happened.”
