STREET DRAMA

Paisley teen arrested over ‘police assault’ as three other youngsters nicked after cops rushed to scene of ‘bonfire’

17 Nov 2019, 11:43 by Justin Bowie

A TEENAGER was arrested for allegedly assaulting a police officer in Paisley as three other youngsters were nicked as well.

It came as cops and fire crews rushed to the scene of a “bonfire” on Oakshaw Street West in the town at around 9.20pm last night.

Several police cars with flashing lights were seen as they dealt with the alleged incident.

And officers were seen frantically running to the scene.

Cops are also probing reports of a missile being thrown at firefighters during the alleged disturbance.

Today Police Scotland confirmed four teenage men had been arrested.

One male youth was nicked for a “police assault”.

And another two were cuffed by cops for alleged disorder offences.

A Police Scotland spokesperson said: “Police and fire service attended a call to reports of a bonfire being lit on waste ground on Oakshaw Street West, Paisley, around 9.20pm on Saturday November 16.

“Four male youths have been arrested in connection with the incident and enquiries are continuing.

“Enquiries are continuing into reports of a missile being thrown at the fire service during the disturbance.”
