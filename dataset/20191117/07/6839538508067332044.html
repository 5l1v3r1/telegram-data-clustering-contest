BLIND DATE: Laura and Aidan get the ball rolling at Holey Moley in Northbridge

17 Nov 2019, 07:33

University student and budding writer Laura and keen traveller Aidan went to Holey Moley in Northbridge to see if they could hit it off.

LAURA, 21, SAYS:

To say I was nervous was an understatement, I even got my friend to drive me around the block a couple of times because I just wanted to make sure he showed up.

My last relationship was when I was 17 for around two years. I got my heart smashed to smithereens, which kind of put a dampener on relationships in general for a while.

But Aidan was lovely from the get-go, he’s really handsome and definitely my type. I found out he was a few years older, which was nice because I do tend to go for older guys based on a lack of maturity in previous partners.

I really prefer doing activity-based dates, especially for a first date because it’s a way of breaking the ice and better than being stuck at a table for two hours just talking. Our conversation mostly flowed throughout the date and playing mini golf was great because if I ever got stuck for what to say, I just made a joke about the insane setups they have there.

I would be happy to see him again to figure out if that spark is there.

My only worry is it was clear Aidan has the travel bug, which is wonderful, don’t get me wrong, but I’m looking for someone that hopefully sticks around for a bit because I’ve got a year left of my marketing and PR degree to go.

After the mini golf we went to Northbridge Brewing Company across the road and had a drink there and walked through the city before we said goodbye. I didn’t know if he was interested in me romantically, or just as a mate as he was a little hard to read. I would be happy to see him again to figure out if that spark is there.

We added each other on Facebook and overall I had a great time.

It pushed me outside of my comfort zone and I feel like now I have the confidence to go on tonnes of dates.

VERDICT: 9/10

AIDAN, 26 SAYS:

Coming in to the date I didn’t have much time to think about it, I ended up moving house the morning after the date. I only recently moved back to Perth as I’ve been living in Norway for three years and spent a year travelling back to Australia.

I was pretty excited, a bit anxious too but in a good way. I think it would be weird if you weren’t.

On meeting Laura she was pretty bubbly and you can tell she was a bit nervous when we first got there. I have to admit she wasn’t really my type, she was a bit younger than I would usually date.

My last girlfriend was Norwegian and that didn’t end up working out but I stayed after that ended, so I have travelled a fair bit. Laura was really nice but still in university so we didn’t have too much in common.

I’m not really a gym junkie but I love hiking and the outdoors so looking for someone more similar, outdoorsy and adventurous in that sense.

It was still a lovely day, there was constant conversation and questions. It was the first time I had been on a blind date so it was a fun experience in that sense and it’s nice not knowing anything and you can ask any question and you can get a new answer you don’t know anything about.

I’d definitely do it again because it was a good experience.

Usually before a date you’ve checked them out on Instagram or something before so you know a little bit of back story about them. I’d definitely do it again because it was a good experience.

Also Holey Moley is great first date place, you can interact and have a good conversation but you’re not just looking directly at eachother. We played nine holes, then had a break and a drink and a chat and it was great that the ice was already broken.

Another date? It was a really nice day but unfortunately I don’t think I’ll be having another one.

VERDICT: 8/10
