Iran's Enemies Are to Blame for 'Sabotage' in Gasoline Price Protests, Khamenei Says

One person was killed in the southeastern city of Sirjan on Saturday during demonstrations, while social media reports referred to several other deaths as riot police and security forces clashed with protesters

17 Nov 2019, 07:43 by Reuters

Iran's Ayatollah Ali Khamenei on Sunday backed the sharp gasoline price rises that have sparked country-wide protests, which he blamed on the Islamic Republic's opponents and "sabotage" by foreign foes.

"Some people are no doubt worried by this decision ... but sabotage and arson is done by hooligans not our people," the Iranian Supreme Leader said in a live speech on state TV.

Riot police and security forces clashed with demonstrators in Tehran and dozens of other cities on Saturday, Iranian news agencies and social media said, a day after the government increased the price of gasoline.
