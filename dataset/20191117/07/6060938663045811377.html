NEW DELHI: The International Association of Athletics Federations (IAAF) will now be officially called as World Athletics. The sport's international governing body not only changed name of its Twitter handle but also the URL of its official website. The name change had been suggested in a bid to make the sport more accessible.

"We're now World Athletics," the sport's international governing body's website said.

"Welcome to the new World Athletics website, the place to come for all things about our wonderful sport. We'll be bringing you new features about athletes and the sport, as well as making it easier to find the things we know you love," it said.

"From information about our events and news about our amazing athletes, to the rules of the sport, historical stats and features to inspire you to be active, it's all here. Over the coming months, we'll be adding more content and continuing to improve the organisation of the content," the website said.

The IAAF was founded in 1912 as the International Amateur Athletic Federation, but changed its name to the International Association of Athletics Federations in 2001.
