Man, 23, charged after alleged stabbing in Plymouth

A local man was arrested on suspicion of wounding with intent to cause grievous bodily harm following the incident

17 Nov 2019, 07:46 by Shannon Hards

A man has been charged following an alleged stabbing at a block of flats in Plymouth.

Police confirmed a 23-year-old local man was arrested on suspicion of wounding with intent to cause grievous bodily harm, following the incident the block in Alexandra Road at around 10.15pm on Friday evening.

The alleged victim, described as being aged in his 20s, remains at Derriford Hospital where he is conscious and stable. He is understood to have suffered multiple wounds to his neck and back, although his injuries were described by police as "not life threatening".

Police revealed that the men are known to each other and no-one else was involved.

In an update on Sunday morning, the police force incident manager, who oversees all live incidents in the region, said: "A male has been charged in connection with this incident."

The scene was guarded yesterday as residents told Plymouth Live that the National Police Air Service helicopter was seen and heard over the city at around 10.30pm on Friday night.

Detectives carried out door to door inquiries, in an attempt to glean any information they can from residents in the block.

Anyone with information regarding the incident can contact police via phone on 101, or via email on 101@dcpolice.uk, quoting log 919 of November 15.

Alternatively, you can call Crimestoppers on 0800 555 111 and report information anonymously.
