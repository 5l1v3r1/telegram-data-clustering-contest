Shocking and callous 'killer children' who committed murder before they were 18

From Rhys Jones' killers to a lad who stabbed his love rival, these teenagers, all under the age of 18, threw away their futures when they chose to take away other people's lives

17 Nov 2019, 07:54 by Neil Docking, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/neil-docking/, Tiffany Lo

These teenage killers are responsible for some of the most shocking and callous murders in recent times.

The boys - driven by hatred, jealousy or greed - had not even reached their 18th birthday when they struck.

Parents have had their families ripped apart due to the rising wave of knife crime across the UK.

Some of the killers could soon be free to roam the streets, despite being sent to jail with life sentences, reports the Liverpool Echo.

Here are some of evil 'killer kids' who robbed victims of their futures:

Joe Ward

Joe Ward stabbed his young love rival  Eddie O'Rourke  to death with a 12-inch knife.

The murderer was handed the weapon by a 15-year-old pal, who was cleared of murder, but convicted of manslaughter.

Ward, then 17, struck at a young woman's 18th birthday party at the Royal British Legion in Runcorn, on the evening of September 7, 2018.

Jealous Ward and Eddie, 18, had regularly clashed over a girl, who cannot be named for legal reasons, over whom Ward had become "obsessed".

 

Ward was supposed to have a clean fight with Eddie in Halton Brook on August 12, 2017, but slashed his left arm with a blade.

Over the course of the next 12 months he made sinister threats to Eddie, and also texting the girl saying: "I'll cut his face, the fat goon."

His accomplice also messaged another girl, who he mistakenly thought was seeing Eddie, telling her: "Leg it u s*** – gna cut ur fella to pieces."

Then, on the night of the party Eddie and Ward came to blows again, with Eddie gesturing to Ward to take part in a supposed "no weapons" fight.

Ward, who had initially walked outside with Eddie, ran back inside the social club in Castle Road, where his friend passed him the knife.

 

 He plunged the "sword-like" blade, described by a witness as being  "like something in Pirates of the Caribbean"  , into the stomach of his victim.

However, he claimed he acted in self-defence and that he only "jabbed" out at Eddie because he was petrified of him.

Ward, 18, of Lockfield, Runcorn, was jailed for life with a minimum of 16 years behind bars.

His friend was locked up for just seven years.

Andrew Hewitt, Corey Hewitt, Joseph McGill, Keyfer Dykstra and their leader Reese O’Shaughnessey

 

The group of yobs were found guilty of murder for cornering and murdering Sean McHugh in the back of a launderette.

The 19-year-old, nicknamed Shorty, died days after suffering a single stab wound from a sword stick at the Priory Road launderette, on September 30, 2013.

Four young boys: cousins Andrew Hewitt, 15, and Corey Hewitt, 14 but 13 at the time; Joseph McGill, 14, also 13 at the time; and Keyfer Dykstra, 14, were found guilty of murder.

So was the gang's "leader" Reese O'Shaughnessey, 19, following a month-long trial at Liverpool Crown Court.

Sean and his friend Josh Williams had been doing their washing at the launderette when they encountered some of the boys on the road outside.

More boys arrived, including O'Shaughnessey, who was carrying a sword stick, part of a mini arsenal the gang kept on a nearby cinder path.

 

CCTV cameras captured him and Dykstra, who had a knife, started hammering on the door to the back room, where Sean was hiding, before kicking it open.

The ringleader stabbed the victim in the leg, before he managed to get away into the backyard, where he was later found bleeding to death by police.

O'Shaughnessey, who fetched the murder weapon, was jailed for life with a minimum of 18 years for what a judge said was a "truly wicked crime".

Dysktra - the "second in command" who led the charge after him was handed a minimum term of 12 years.

Andrew Hewitt, who was the first to physically confront their victim, was locked up for a minimum of nine years.

McGill, who gave Dykstra a knife and poured detergent on the floor to hamper Sean's escape received a minimum eight-year sentence.

 

Corey Hewitt, who did not take part in the attack physically but picked up the abandoned murder weapon as they left, got a minimum six-year sentence.

The lout, said to have an IQ of 69, putting him in the lowest two per cent of the population, later  had his minimum term cut to five years on appeal  .

Judge Clement Goldstone, QC, said that Sean and his friend Josh were from Walton, the home of a gang called the Walton Village Heads.

He said this gang were the sworn enemies of the Laneheads, of which the killers were "fully paid up, card carrying and enthusiastic members".

The judge said Sean was chased partly in revenge for the stabbing of Dykstra in a fight with a Walton boy some months before.

But that had nothing to do with him and there was no evidence he was part of any gang.

Cameron Cruddace

 

 

Twisted Cameron Cruddace  murdered an OAP with a claw hammer then  falsely claimed his victim was a paedophile  .

He struck  Richard Percival  , 71, at least 12 times in a frenzied attack, before rifling through his pockets and stealing £620.

The 17-year-old, who bragged he was “born to kill”, admitted smashing in his victim’s skull and blowing the cash on cocaine.

But he claimed he “flipped” after Mr Percival sexually assaulted him and was only guilty of manslaughter, due to loss of self control.

Judge Alan Conrad, QC, locked him up for life, with a minimum of 20 years behind bars, for the "brutal" killing on August 17, 2017.

He said: "You told numerous lies – both before and during your trial. Mr Percival was not here to refute your assertions against him.

"You never told anybody about it before the killing – neither your friends nor your sister, to whom you were particularly close.

 

"Furthermore, on your account, Mr Percival, having made a particularly serious advance to you in early 2017, you chose to go back to his house six months later.

"These allegations were invented by you after the murder to provide some justification for what you did."

Mr Percival, who lived in Newton-le-Willows, was a "club legend" at Vulcan FC, who he played for in his youth, and later helped run.

Both his family and pals said they were deeply upset by the false accusations "cowardly" Cruddace tried to rely on to excuse his guilt.

The teen bludgeoned the OAP at his Dixon Avenue home with the hammer, which he later ditched along with his clothes.

Judge Conrad said: "He was a decent, harmless and well-liked man, who kept to himself and loved sport.

"He let you into his home for conversation and to allow you to earn some money doing odd jobs.

"He was small in stature and slightly built and in no position to resist the vicious and sustained armed attack.”

Cruddace, of Oak Avenue, Newton-le-Willows, told his lawyer: "There’s no point in being sorry because it’s not going to affect things."
