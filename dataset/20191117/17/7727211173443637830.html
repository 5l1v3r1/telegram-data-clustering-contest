New Netflix Sherlock Holmes drama takes over Liverpool street

The Georgian Quarter was taken over by film crews and period actors for new Sherlock Holmes drama

17 Nov 2019, 17:41 by Max Clements

A Georgian Quarte r street has been transformed into the set for a brand new Sherlock Holmes series.

Filming is taking place on Sunday in Falkner Street as it was taken over by period actors in late Victorian era costume.

Horse and carriages were spotted taking part in the filming on the corner with Hope Street, which was transformed into the setting of a Sherlock Holmes mystery.

Actors and actresses could be scene dressed in old-fashioned costumes as the whole street was taken over for the production.

In October, camera crews were spotted filming the period drama outside St George's Hall and in Sefton Park .

Production teams had been spotted inside the Palm House back in September with film crews lining the streets outside the park.

Liverpool is one of the numerous locations being used to film the new Netflix series, The Irregulars, which is based on Arthur Conan Doyle's stories published between 1887 and 1893.

The Netflix show offers a twist on a popular classic by instead focusing on a crime solving gang of children.

The new series follows a group of street urchins, known as the Baker Street Irregulars, whose crime solving skills are used by Holmes and passed off as his own.

The Baker Street Irregulars are a fictional group of boys that did in fact appear in Sir Arthur Conan Doyle's novels and provided the famous detective with intelligence.

The new drama is written by Oscar-nominated writer Tom Bidwell who has described the show as his "dream project".

Speaking about the upcoming series to BBC Writersroom, Bidwell said: "Sherlock Holmes in Conan Doyle's books had a group of street kids he'd use to help him gather clues, so our series is what if Sherlock was a drug addict and a delinquent and the kids solved the whole case while he takes credit.

"It's my dream project and my oldest idea and I've been pitching it for ten years."

The show is expected to be filming until May 2020 across several different locations.

Liverpool has also been used as the setting for a Sherlock Holmes mystery with Robert Downey Jnr and Jude Law being filmed on Stanley Dock in 2011.
