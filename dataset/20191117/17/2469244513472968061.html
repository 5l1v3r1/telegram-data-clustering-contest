Woman taken to hospital after collapsing 'under a tree in the bushes' at Exeter park

The air ambulance was involved in the incident

17 Nov 2019, 17:18 by Jamie Hawkins

A woman was taken to hospital after collapsing under a tree at an Exeter playing fields.

The air ambulance landed at Exhibition Playing Fields at around 9.30am on Sunday, with paramedics seen surrounding a tree and bushes in the area.

A land ambulance also attended the incident, before the casualty was taken to the Royal Devon and Exeter Hospital.

A South Western Ambulance Service spokesperson told Devon Live: “We were called at 9.28am on Sunday, November 17 after a female collapsed. 

“We conveyed her to hospital for further treatment.”

An eyewitness described seeing the air ambulance land in the field from her house in the area.

The woman, who wished to remain anonymous, originally thought someone had spotted an injured animal in the bushes.

She said: “I opened the curtains at around 9.30am which overlooks the playing fields where they normally play football.

“But then I noticed two lads with their dad were looking down at the bushes under a tree.

“I thought they were looking at an animal at first.

“But it was definitely a person, possibly a woman.

“The air ambulance landed nearby and paramedics wheeled her off.

“It was very distressing to see this right outside my back door.

“It happened under a tree amongst the bushes.

“They stopped a jogger to help the woman.

“I would say the air ambulance was there for around an hour.

“I couldn’t see any police there.”
