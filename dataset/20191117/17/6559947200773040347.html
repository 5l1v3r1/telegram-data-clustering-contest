Disney+ to Fix 'The Simpsons' Aspect Ratio Issue Next Year

Earlier seasons of 'The Simpsons' will be offered in their original 4:3 aspect ratio in early 2020. Currently, Disney+ crops episodes and some viewers note that visual jokes are impossible to pick up on due to the 16:9 aspect ratio.

Soon you'll be able to stream episodes of 'The Simpsons' exactly how they were aired.

Disney+ holds streaming rights to the popular series, but earlier seasons aren't offered in the 4:3 aspect ratio. Instead, they're cropped to fit the 16:9 aspect ratio supported by new seasons and their episodes. Variety reports that, in early 2020, Disney+ will start offering all episodes in their original formats.

It wouldn't be an issue for some series, but 'The Simpsons' relies on visual jokes throughout its episodes. In one example, Tristan Cooper noted on Twitter a scene in which Duff beer flavors are all connected to the same tube. Disney+ cuts off the tube above each tank, though. If you watch the same episode in the 4:3 aspect ratio, you'll see the single source of Duff-branded beer and understand the joke.

Fortunately, an error like this won't occur after Disney+ rolls out the fix. Just be patient as there isn't an exact date for when 'The Simpsons' will no longer force the 16:9 ration on episodes prior to 2009.

"Over time, Disney+ will roll out new features and additional viewing options," The Walt Disney Company said in a statement. "As part of this, in early 2020, Disney+ will make the first 19 seasons (and some episodes from Season 20) of 'The Simpsons' available in their original 4:3 aspect ratio, giving subscribers choice of how they prefer to view the popular series."

Disney+ limited all episodes to the 16:9 aspect ratio in order to remain consistent across all seasons.

When FOX started airing 'The Simpsons' in 1989, it used the 4:3 aspect ratio to conform to 480i video resolution. The series switched over to the 16:9 aspect ratio a decade ago to match 1080p on HDTVs. Disney+ is capable of up to 4K HDR with some content, but fans of 'The Simpsons' simply want the glory days of 4:3 back. Eventually, they'll get just that through Disney+ alongside a plethora of other content to stream.
