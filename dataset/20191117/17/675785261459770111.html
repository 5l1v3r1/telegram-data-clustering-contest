Hero McDonald's worker hailed for being the 'Robin Hood of McNuggets'

Cody Bondarchuk revealed on Twitter that, over the two and a half years he worked for the fast-food chain, he put 11 nuggets in almost every 10-piece he made

17 Nov 2019, 17:16 by Chiara Fiorillo

A McDonald's worker has been hailed for being the "Robin Hood of McNuggets" after he made a surprising confession.

Cody Bondarchuk, who worked for the fast-food chain for two and a half years, said he put 11 nuggets in almost every 10-piece he made for his customers.

The man from Edmonton, Alberta, shared his good deed on his Twitter profile.

The post has since attracted the attention of social media users, who shared and commented it in big numbers.

One person wrote: "Not all heroes wear capes, just name tags."

Another Twitter user commented: "Are you working at a bank now and if so which branch?"

 

Some people asked Cody whether anyone ever thanked him for his kindness.

"Did any customer notice and actually thank you for it?" Kevin Jones ansked.

"The location was mostly drive-thru so not that I can remember, but I’d like to imagine they went home, saw the extra nug, and smiled a little," Cody replied.

One user said: "Guarantee you made someone's day at least once a day each day you worked over those years. Nicely done."

Since Cody published the tweet yesterday, it has been liked 776,000 times and it got 67,000 retweets.

Chicken McNuggets are made with 100 per cent chicken breast and are also served as part of a meal with French fries and a drink.
