Everton victorious over Liverpool but fans exceed all expectations on landmark day for women's football

A huge crowd turns out to watch the Merseyside derby as Everton win 1-0 thanks to Lucy Graham's goal at Anfield

17 Nov 2019, 17:02 by Sam Carroll

A crowd of 23,500 watched Everton record a 1-0 victory in Sunday's Merseyside Derby at Anfield.

Liverpool goalkeeper Anke Preuss fumbled a tame, 30-yard effort from visiting captain Lucy Graham on the stroke of half-time that spilled into the back of the net in front of the Kop and handed the Blues all three points. 

Home supporters stood with scarves outstretched to welcome their side onto the field while Everton fans – who valiantly filled most of the away end – played their own vociferous part.

Liverpool were the early aggressors and only a stunning save from Tinja Korpela – the goalkeeper clawing the ball off the line with her left-hand – kept the scores level.

Niamh Charles offered a constant threat for the Reds with her pace on the break while Korpela was once more called upon from a bouncing Rhiannon Roberts strike.

Everton midfielder Graham, however, had not read the script and her strike – the first on target for the visitors – caught Preuss off guard and handed Willie Kirk's side the lead.

Liverpool, try as they might in the second-half, could not find a response. 

Overall, though, this afternoon was not strictly about the result.

Both Vicky Jepson and Kirk would argue otherwise, of course, but Everton and Liverpool played out an enthralling spectacle with late home pressure ramping up the volume at Anfield. 

The surrounding area before the game was filled with supporters new and old but there was a palpable presence of young female supporters who could be heard shouting excitedly when Liverpool launched an attack.

While Virgil van Dijk, Lucas Digne, Mohamed Salah and Richarlison remain idols for followers the world over, this was the first real, publicised opportunity for women's football on Merseyside in a long time. 

The offering did not disappoint – and the men could well find themselves rivalled by the likes of Charles, Mel Lawley and Rinsola Babajide by some sections of the fanbase.

Preuss' error aside – the Liverpool stopper was otherwise excellent – every woman played her part in a true advert for ladies football.

Whether it be a new supporter or aspiring future talent, overcoming the initial target of a 20,000 attendance proves there is a real appetite locally for the game.

As Liverpool pushed for an equaliser, the volume crackled with the Kop desperate to see a goal against their bitter rivals.

It did not materialise, and Everton will be delighted with a hard-earned three points to continue their impressive start to the Women's Super League.

But as You'll Never Walk Alone rang out for a second-time pre-match, you would now like to think it will not be long before you hear it again, at Anfield, for a women's clash.

And with the reverse fixture set for February, after such a terrific turnout and atmosphere, perhaps the Goodison Park siren will continue the push to advertise women's football across Merseyside and inspire our local stars of the future.
