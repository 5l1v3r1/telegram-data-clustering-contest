Woman has baby at 90mph on M6 after loose dog and floods derail birth plan

17 Nov 2019, 14:01

When a woman goes into labour, she normally hopes for a calm atmosphere and an abundance of trained medical staff around her.

But you don’t always get what you wish for.

Hannah Rowlinson, 32, got floods, a dog on the loose and a 90mph drive down the M6 when she gave birth.

She and husband Joel had planned to welcome daughter Viola Florence into the world in a birthing pool at home.

Hannah was six days late and went into labour at around 4pm on October 24 but her contractions were slow, continuing over the next 48 hours.

The couple – who already had a son, two-year-old Sebastian – were told to expect a midwife from Leighton Hospital on October 26.

However, they received a phone call saying the hospital was too busy and telling the expectant mum to come in.

But the hospital called again soon after to say there were no beds available and that Hannah and Joel would have to go to Royal Stoke, which is a 45 minute drive from their house in Holmes Chapel.

Joel’s phone battery died after getting into the car and Hannah was forced to type the hospital address into her phone while she had contractions.

When they finally hit the road, the couple hit heavy flooding and traffic, with reports of a dog loose on the M6 as well.

After Hannah told her husband that she would be giving birth to the baby ‘imminently’, they diverted onto a bumpy back lane to escape motorway queues.

However, the back roads were equally as busy, so Hannah’s mum, who was in the back of the car, was forced to ring 999.

After the family pulled into a residential estate in Sandbach, the phone operator began to guide them through the process of preparing a birth.

Hannah’s mum ran down the road ‘frantically’ waving her arms and stopped a passing motorcycle unit police car.

Two PCs ‘secured the scene’ and began to knock on doors, asking if nearby residents had any spare towels they could use.

At one point, a woman came out of her house and mopped Hannah’s head through the car window with a flannel.

Thankfully, paramedics Dave Knibbs and Garry Thompson made it in the nick of time.

After just seven minutes of being in the ambulance, the baby’s head was crowning.

Hannah told Stoke-on-Trent Live: ‘We were going at 90mph down the motorway and I asked the paramedics to pull over because the baby was coming.

‘The roads were bedlam because of the floods – I didn’t even know where I was.

‘There was a coned off area and he pulled in. I did one more push and she was out.

She added: ‘Yes, it was not the birth I was expecting but everyone was so incredible.

‘There are things in your life that will stick with you forever and this is definitely one of them. I don’t have words to describe how grateful I am.

‘I will never forget those two paramedics. Some times things don’t go to plan, but who needs a plan when you have Garry and Dave?’
