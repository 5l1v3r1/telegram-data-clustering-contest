Declan Gallagher admits he was close to tears knowing his family were there for Scotland debut

The Motherwell centre-back put in an impressive performance in the 2-1 win over Cyprus.

17 Nov 2019, 14:03 by Chris Doyle, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/chris-doyle/

Declan Gallagher says he was close to tears knowing his family were there to witness his Scotland debut.

The Motherwell centre-back was included in Steve Clarke 's starting XI in the 2-1 win over Cyprus and put in a good performance for the national side.

Gallagher's wife Nikki and four-year-old daughter Shay were in attendance, along with both sets of their parents, and the 28-year-old was a proud man.

He said: "I was absolutely delighted to get my first cap and also get a win away from home. It's amazing and I am delighted for all the boys as well.

"I knew my family were up the back of the stand and a lot of people watching back home and to stand and to sing your national anthem and play for your country is a dream. It was absolutely amazing.

"My missus and my wee one were desperate to come over. I think they just wanted a holiday!

"But they are my biggest fans. My mum and dad were over in Russia and my wife and my wee girl are at every game.

"And obviously to have my missus' mum and dad there as well was amazing.

"I said to my missus after the game, I felt like crying knowing that her and my daughter were there to see me get my first cap."
