Stephen A. Smith Fires Back At NFL Player’s Colin Kaepernick Comment

17 Nov 2019, 14:45 by Andrew Holleran

ESPN’s Stephen A. Smith posted his thoughts on Twitter regarding Colin Kaepernick’s NFL workout drama on Saturday night. The former NFL quarterback ended up moving his workout from the Atlanta Falcons facility to a high school about 60 miles away against the league’s wishes.

Kaepernick, who was scheduled to work out in front of 24-plus teams at the Falcons facility, worked out for much fewer teams at the high school. He allowed all media and some fans to watch him throw, too.

Smith does not believe Kaepernick truly wants to play in the NFL.

“He don’t want to play,” Smith said. “He wants to be a martyr.”

Eric Reid, who knelt with Kaepernick, called out Smith on Twitter.

“Tap dancing for the NFL,” he wrote. “Damn straight Colin wants to control the narrative! He supposed to trust the organization that blackballed him and has done absolutely nothing in good faith??? Born on Tuesday, just not last Tuesday.”
