Highlights! Former UFC fighter Ross Pearson KO’d by stunning rolling thunder kick

17 Nov 2019, 00:52 by Dan Hiergesell

Former UFC fighter Ross Pearson can’t seem to catch a break these days, even in his debut for Probellum MMA.

Pearson, 35, entered the cage earlier tonight (Sat., Nov. 16, 2019) at Probellum 1 in London, England, in search of his first professional win since defeating Mizuto Hirota via decision nearly two years ago. Not only did “Real Deal” leave empty handed but he suffered the most brutal knockout defeat of his 15-year MMA career.

Pearson’s Probellum debut came against French veteran Davy Gallon, who took the former UFC fighter into the third round. While the two fighters circled one another on the feet Gallon launched a ridiculous rolling thunder kick. Fight fans are used to seeing this type of move used at the end of a round with very limited success, but the attack connected flush with Pearson and immediately cut the lights off.
