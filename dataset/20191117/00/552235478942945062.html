Borna Barisic reveals Rangers departure thoughts and mentality change that sparked stunning form turnaround

The Croatian international full-back has explained the change of outlook that sparked his dramatic Ibrox resurgence.

17 Nov 2019 by Scott McDermott, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/scott-mcdermott/

NK Osijek rarely fill their Gradski Stadium to its 17,000 capacity.

And in the Croatian top flight they still live in the shadow of giants Dinamo Zagreb and Hadjuk Split.

So it’s no surprise Borna Barisic had a small-club mentality when he pitched up in Glasgow.

At Osijek, a draw wasn’t a catastrophe. Finishing third in the league was a success.

And as a professional, he could probably get away with letting his standards slip.

But that all changed when he signed for Rangers – even if Barisic didn’t realise it.

The 26-year-old had a poor first season at Ibrox. After impressing in the Europa League qualifiers against Steven Gerrard’s side, he was snared in the summer of 2018.

But with injuries, a loss of form and – by his own admission – the wrong attitude, Barisic struggled to adapt to Scottish football.

The pressure and intensity of being an Old Firm player took its toll on the Croatia left-back.

At one point, he was ready to quit this country, fearing he’d made the wrong move.

But Barisic stuck at it. And in the close season, he took a hard look at how he was living his life.

On and off the pitch, he knew he had to improve or Gerrard would show him the door. And to his credit, he’s been like a new signing for Rangers this term.

After a tricky start to the campaign, he curled in a free-kick winner against St Mirren – and hasn’t looked back since.

 

Barisic has been outstanding in the Premiership and Europa League, with his assist for Alfredo Morelos ’ goal in Porto a standout moment.

The defender has revealed he had to turn his life around to be a success at Ibrox.

Watching consummate pros like Jermain Defoe and Steven Davis every day has given him a new outlook – but a pep talk from Gerrard was a pivotal moment.

Barisic said: “I came back from pre-season in good shape. I knew that when I was on holiday, that I needed to train – to make sure I was fit and ready to go.

“When I scored a great goal against St Mirren, it gave me a lift.

“But after that I had a chat with the manager and we were both honest with each other.

“I won’t say what we spoke about but I felt better after it – and I started to play a lot better.

“Last season I had a few injuries and couldn’t settle down here. My mindset was different.

“Now, I feel I have the mentality that is needed to be a Rangers player.

“To play here, your mentality has to be to win every single game.

“You have to be brave and try to take this team to the top of Scottish football.

“When you play for Rangers, it isn’t just about physical fitness. The mental side is important too.

“I came from the Croatian league with Osijek and it’s completely different there.

“On holiday last summer, I thought a lot about what I had to do better at Rangers.

“I’ve learned from the likes of Jermain, Steven and Scott Arfield , guys who have more experience than me.

“And of course, the gaffer and coaching staff have helped me as well.

“This squad is mentally stronger than last year.

“We’ve all lifted our levels. The pressure is massive at this club but I know now how to deal with it. Now I don’t feel the pressure.

“I just feel confident in myself and this team.

“I’m on a good path again and confident about my future now.

 

“Did I think about leaving here last year? Yes, I have to be honest, around February or March I thought Rangers and Scotland wasn’t for me.

“It was a hard time for me but that is normal when you get injuries. You start to think that maybe it’s too much.

“But I listened to the gaffer and kept working.

“I knew I could do better and I’m in a good place now.”

That place is joint top of the league alongside Celtic and sitting pretty in their Europa League group.

Barisic is relishing a fight for the title as Gers’ bid to stop nine-in-a-row. And given some of the Euro heroics at Ibrox and Parkhead this year – the Croat says people outside of Scotland will now show more respect to the league.

He said: “People will see the Scottish league as very strong this year because of what Rangers and Celtic have achieved in Europe.

“The title race has actually helped us in the European games.

“People who don’t know the league told me before I came that I could take it easy in Scotland.

“But you can’t! Every game is difficult and we have important games every three days.

“When we play some teams in Europe it’s like they don’t have a lot of respect for us.

“But we’ve shown how good a team we are and in the future, they’ll have to respect us. Teams may treat us differently now.”

 

Barisic admits the atmosphere at Ibrox on a European night is so electric that he can’t explain it to his family.

He said: “The Porto victory at Ibrox was a big win for us.

“If we keep playing like that, we have to believe that we can qualify for the next round.

“When I speak to friends and family about coming to watch me I tell them to come on a European night – because they are so special.

“I can’t explain it to them – they have to see it for themselves.”
