New plans to build M&S Foodhall on historic Derby site

A scheme was rejected over traffic issues last year

17 Nov 2019, 12:24 by Ben Lyons

New plans to build a Marks and Spencer Foodhall on a historic Derby site have been revealed.

A planning application has been submitted to Derby City Council to build a store on land off Wyvern Way, formerly occupied by the Derby Fireplace Company.

Last November, an application was rejected to demolish the Derby Fireplace Company building to make way for three retail outlets - a M&S Foodhall, a Nando’s restaurant and a Starbucks.

Councillors refused the plans after officers found “no solution” to any potential traffic problems.

But, under the new plans, the building would be demolished to enable the construction of a M&S Foodhall only.

The plans suggest the 1.4-acre site would be accessed by customers via Wyvernside and would share its access with Pizza Hut and KFC.

For some time now the developers have been hoping to build on the site. Back in 2014, another application was submitted to build three larger units on the land.

But at the start of 2015 the application was withdrawn because they “prejudiced” the city council’s plans to make road improvements between the Wyvern and the A52.

In planning documents submitted by RPS Group, on behalf of London Metric and Clowes Development, it said: "London Metric and Clowes Development have been in discussion with Derby City Council since November 2013 to explore the potential of retail development on this site.

"In early 2018 a planning application was submitted on the basis of proposing three units (including a drive-thru’ coffee shop) within a single built form but was ultimately refused in November 2018 on grounds of transport and highway congestion impacts on the surrounding road network, particularly at peak times.

"In late 2018 /early 2019 London Metric commenced reviewing the scheme composition and in part to alleviate traffic impact concerns, a design review instigated a fresh evolution of proposals (while in continuous liaison with Derby Highways) resulting in detailed proposals for a single occupier small food store on the site with ancillary car park and additional exit lane to alleviate peak congestion impacts."

The application was submitted to the council on Thursday, November 7, and is currently pending consideration.

Although the design and access statement does not specify what food store would be built under the plans, the proposed site totem details includes M&S Food branding.
