'We’ll see' - Sammy Ameobi talks about his Nottingham Forest future

The summer arrival from Bolton has proved an astute signing by Forest, and a hit on the terraces

17 Nov 2019, 12:30 by Barry Cooper

Sammy Ameobi has set his sights on a new contract at Nottingham Forest, having impressed since arriving in the summer.

The winger has proved a popular figure this season under Sabri Lamouchi, clocking up 17 appearances and popping up with a couple of vital goals.

After leaving Bolton Wanderers in June, Ameobi penned a one-year deal at the City Ground, though he hopes his performances thus far are doing him no harm and hopes to secure a longer-term deal on Trentside.

“Yes, absolutely,” said the 27-year-old when asked if he’d like to stay at the club.

“I’ve loved my time here so far, hopefully I’m working my way to a new contract," he told the club website.

“We’ll see."

Ameobi’s first goal in the Garibaldi red came at Stoke City, a towering header to put his side 2-1 up after being a goal down, and he revealed just how that goal made him feel.

“Relieved.

“To go 1-0 down is never good, to come back and get the goal we were relieved and we wanted to keep the result going.”

Having been rested for Forest’s 3-0 win over Derby County in August, Ameobi was pivotal in ensuring the Reds were 1-0 winners in the Championship last weekend, an experience he quite enjoyed, coming on the back of bagging the winner at Luton.

“It was wonderful,” beamed the former Newcastle United youngster.

“A wonderful experience.

“It’s the first one I’ve played in here so, it was great and an unbelievable atmosphere.

“I loved it.”
