Activists condemn alleged police torture

17 Nov 2019, 12:51 by Staff Reporter

MADURAI Dalit rights activists have condemned the recent alleged torture of a section of residents of the Scheduled Caste community from Ambedkar Colony of Oomachikulam.

A 15-year-old-boy, was allegedly picked up by Oomachikulam police on November 4 on the pretext of interrogation after some people stole four iron rods used in the construction of the elevated highway at Oomachikulam. “The last time I saw my son was on November 5. After that his whereabouts are unknown,” said K. Sumathi, his mother.

Advocate A. Rajini, while addressing a press conference here on Saturday, said that she would file a habeas corpus petition in the High Court seeking a direction to the police to produce the boy. The residents also alleged that a few others were picked up by the police for inquiry and tortured. They also said that they paid money and jewellery to the police to be released after interrogation.

Ms. Rajini said that appropriate action should be taken against the police. R. Adhiyaman, founder-president, Adi Tamizhar Peravai, was also present.
