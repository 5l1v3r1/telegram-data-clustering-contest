Liverpool captain Jordan Henderson reveals his first memories as a Sunderland fan

Henderson went on to graduate from the Black Cats' academy having grown up supporting Sunderland

17 Nov 2019, 12:00 by Sean McCormick

Liverpool captain Jordan Henderson has spoken to Jamie Carragher's 'Greatest Game' podcast about his earliest memories as a Sunderland fan.

Before Henderson was leading Liverpool to Champions League glory and captaining his country, the 29-year-old started his career at Sunderland.

Like most young boys growing up on Wearside, Henderson's dreams were formed while watching the Black Cats play at both Roker Park and the Stadium of Light.

The team Henderson grew up with was Peter Reid's side who twice finished seventh in the Premier League and beat Newcastle twice in successive years at St James' Park.

And in conversation with his former Liverpool team-mate, Henderson spoke about his first memories of Sunderland, as well as the players that shaped his love of the game.

Here is everything he said.

First memories of Sunderland

"Briefly, I remember going to Roker Park when I was really young with my dad and I can just briefly remember the stadium inside and the pitch and things like that.

"I was really small so I couldn't tell you [the game] but I remember the old-school ground.

"My proper memories would be at the Stadium of Light with Kevin Phillips, Julio Arca, Micky Gray, those sorts of players.

"The Peter Reid team was the team I grew up with."

The Peter Reid era

"Those two seasons finishing seventh were big achievements for Sunderland and it was just about trying to maintain that.

"They are really passionate about football the fans there and I know they would have wanted to get into Europe - that is just normal."

Favourite Sunderland player

"Phillips was a big one but I would say Arca was a big one for me, I loved him. The crowd loved him.

"They used to sing 'Julio, Julio', they loved him. He had a great relationship on the left with Micky Gray and he stood out."

Play-off final defeat in 1998

"I watched it on the telly. I think I watched it in my dad's local golf club actually. When he missed the pen in the gold strip, not a great day."
