Products being recalled from Waitrose, Tesco, Sainsbury's and Argos could damage your health

If you have bought these products, return them for a refund

17 Nov 2019, 09:55 by Joel Leaver, Geoffrey Bennett

Leading supermarkets are recalling products due to manufacturers' health and safety concerns.

Included in the recall are foods that may contain glass and a mattress which may not confirm to fire safety standards.

The Food Standards Agency is advising customers not to consume any of the mentioned items.

The products are sold at major stores in the UK including Tesco, Sainsbury's and Waitrose.

People are recommended to return affected items for a full refund, North Wales reports.

Here are the latest products being recalled:

Cadbury

A Cadbury product is this week being recalled by its parent company Mondelez UK over safety concerns.

The issue concerns some packs of Dairy Milk Little Robins (93g) which contain undeclared almonds and are therefore a possible health risk for anyone with an allergy to nuts.

The FSA have revealed the labelling error is due to some of the product having been incorrectly packed with Cadbury Dairy Milk Little Robins Daim Chocolate.

The affected items have the batch codes of CIS3092812 and CIS3092822, and have a best before date of March 31, 2020.

Little Robins products are currently sold at stores including Tesco, Sainsbury's and Asda.

Waitrose

Pots & Co. is recalling part of its range of products, which are currently stocked at Waitrose & Partners.

The manufacturer recently explained that four of its pot varieties may contain pieces of glass, and are therefore unsafe for consumption.

Colombian Chocolate Pot (65g) which has the identifying barcode of 5060253701920.

Mango & Passionfruit Pot (65g) which has the identifying barcode of 5060253701906.

Mango & Passionfruit Pot (6x65g) which has the identifying barcode of 5060253702224.

Summer Berry Pot (65g) which has the identifying barcode of 5060253701913

The affected products are those with dates codes up to and including November 23, 2019.

Argos

The final recall of the week concerns retailer Argos, which has told customers to return its Argos Memory Foam Mattress Topper.

A "small number" of this product do not conform to UK fire safety standards and therefore pose a risk to those using them.

The issue was identified during routine testing by the company, who are urging customers to stop using the product immediately.
