BOOTED OUT

Glasgow church minister who branded Jeremy Corbyn a ‘terrorist sympathiser’ axed from school he was linked to

17 Nov 2019, 09:11 by Oliver Norton

THE minister suspended after heckling Jeremy Corbyn has been blasted by parents at a primary he’s linked to.

Reverend Richard Cameron has been booted out of the Glasgow school and a Boys Brigade company after alleged racist and homophobic comments online.

Scotstoun Primary head Gill Mackay cut links with the Kirk preacher saying the posts could cause “potential damage to the education of our children”.

And dad Hugh Jarvis, 44, added: “Cameron does not belong in a pulpit.

"I certainly don’t want him providing moral guidance to my kids — and I’m glad he won’t be.

“The school and BB have handled it well by ditching him immediately.

“They weren’t to know he sends racist, homophobic tweets in his spare time.”

Church officials suspended Rev Cameron, of Scotstoun Parish Church, days after he branded Mr Corbyn a “terrorist sympathiser” in Glasgow.

We told how the Labour leader looked stunned as he was interrupted.

The minister shouted: “Do you think the man that’s going to be prime minister of this country should be a terrorist sympathiser?

“Who’s going to be the first terrorist to be invited to the House of Commons when you’re prime minister?”

As Mr Corbyn showed off a tartan scarf gifted to him by a charity, Rev Cameron then asked why he wasn't wearing an "Islamic jihad scarf".

The Labour chief did not respond and was ushered away by aides, prompting the minister to shout: “Aye you’re running away.”

A Church of Scotland spokesperson said: “In accordance with our procedures Rev Richard Cameron has been administratively suspended.

"This is to allow us to carry out an enquiry in relation to the incident which took place earlier this week and the subsequent complaints about his social media use."

The churchman was unavailable for comment.

We also told how Mr Corbyn was heckled by a "pro-independence supporter" at a separate event in Dundee during his visit north of the border.

The opposition leader struggled to speak over the vocal heckler, who told security at the event to “take your hands off me”.
