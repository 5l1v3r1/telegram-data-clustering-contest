NHS worker, 89, sacked over computer skills wins £200,000 in damages

17 Nov 2019, 16:43

A medical secretary who was fired from her job because she could not use a computer has been awarded £200,000 in damages after suing the NHS for age discrimination.

Eileen Jolly, 89, said she felt ‘humiliated’ when she was escorted out of the Royal Berkshire Hospital in Reading in January 2017.

Managers at the Royal Berkshire NHS Foundation Trust claimed she was ‘stuck’ in old secretarial ways and dismissed her over an apparent ‘catastrophic failure in performance’.

But an employment tribunal ruled that her case was ‘tainted by discrimination’ and she was offered a settlement worth £200,000 by the trust.

The sum is one of the largest ever awarded.

Mrs Jolly, who suffers from a heart condition and has arthritis said she had trouble sleeping and was prescribed anti-depressants after she was sacked.

The tribunal was told that she hid the news from her late husband and the rest of her family, telling them she had retired because she felt a ‘deep sense of shame’.

She was admitted to hospital with tachycardia after reading a report from her managers who made hurtful comments about her age and health, the tribunal heard.

Judge Andrew Gumbiti-Zimuto said training given to Mrs Jolly was not adequate and that no further training was offered.

His judgment read: ‘The role that the claimant understood she was performing, she was performing competently. There is a suspicion of the claimant being a scapegoat, the claimant was not offered training where it might be considered appropriate.’

Mrs Jolly’s complaints of unfair dismissal, age discrimination, disability discrimination and breach of contract were upheld.

The NHS accepted it unfairly dismissed her by not allowing her an appeal but insisted its decision to sack her was justified on the grounds of capability.

Papers showing the breakdown of Mrs Jolly’s pay out show she was given a basic award of £12,956.34 and £71,472.64 for unfair dismissal.

She was also awarded £20,000 for injury to feelings and £350 for loss of statutory right.

The trust also paid £1,727.52 for failing to provide written particulars and £22,868.16 for non-compliance with the ACAS code

In total, the document states the gross amount owed to Mrs Jolly is £200,000.
