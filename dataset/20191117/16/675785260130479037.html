Where and when will England play their Euro 2020 matches? Fixture dates and stadiums

The Three Lions have the bonus of a number of the tournaments' matches being held at Wembley, but they could face two different paths depending on their success

17 Nov 2019, 16:00 by Darren Wells, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/darren-wells/

England's path to Euro 2020 has been confirmed, and with it promises another summer of excitement across the nation.

Fans all over the country were gripped by the ventures of the Three Lions in Russia for the World Cup in 2018.

And with Wembley one of the host stadiums for the European Championships, 2020 could be possibly one of the best tournaments in recent memory.

Gareth Southgate's men have already secured their place, and only a miraculous 24-goal swing would see them miss out on top spot in qualifying Group A.

But when will England's Euro 2020 matches be taking place? And where will they be held?

Here are all the key dates for your diary...

Group stage

As England have sealed qualification, their group games will now all take place at Wembley, with England assigned to Group D.

It's been over two decades since they last played a tournament fixture on home soil, with memories of Euro '96 almost faded.

They'll kick-start they group campaign on June 14, before playing again on June 19 and June 23.

June 14: Wembley

June 19: Wembley

June 23: Wembley

If England qualify as group winners

This is of course never a certainty with England, as even at the World Cup they went through as runners-up to Belgium.

But should they progress from their group at the Euros as winners, they'll be handed a favourable run to the final.

There's the potential for a last-16 clash over the Irish Sea in Ireland's capital of Dublin, while the quarter-final would take place in Rome.

If they can repeat their World Cup form and make it to at least the last four, they'll return to Wembley for the semi's and - dare to believe - the final.

Last-16: Aviva Stadium, Dublin

Quarter-final: Stadio Olimpico, Rome

Semi-final: Wembley

Final: Wembley

If England finish second in the group

It's worth knowing these anyway, as... you know... England.

So it would be the same set-up for the group stages, with all three games at Wembley, as well as returning for the semi's and final.

But between would see England go on a slightly less than ideal journey to Copenhagen for the last-16, with a quarter-final scheduled for St. Petersburg in Russia.

Last 16: Parken Stadium, Copenhagen

Quarter-final: Krestovsky Stadium, St Petersburg

Semi-final: Wembley

Final: Wembley

Either way, you can see the pints of beer flying now...
