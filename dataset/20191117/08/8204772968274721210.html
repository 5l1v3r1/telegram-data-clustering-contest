Boy (16) dies after being hit by car in Co Limerick

Shock in Adare after death of teenager in early hours; gardaí appeal for witnesses

17 Nov 2019, 08:50

Gardaí believe a teenager who died Sunday after being struck by a vehicle in Co Limerick was walking home from a house party.

The incident involving the 16-year-old, named locally as Nathan Lawlor Hough from Rathkeale, happened at around 3.45am on the N21 Limerick to Tralee road.

It is understood that Nathan, a fifth year secondary school student, had earlier attended a birthday in a local pub before going to the house party.

“He would have been well known around the town, and there wouldn’t have been a bad word to say about him,”a local source said.

They said the boy’s friends “can’t get their heads around it and are in total shock”.

When gardaí arrived at the scene of the collision an ambulance crew was already working on the boy but he was pronounced dead at the scene.

A postmortem is due to be carried out on the teenager at University Hospital Limerick on Monday.

The male driver of the car involved was not injured and was helping gardaí with their enquiries, a garda source said.

A garda spokeswoman said: “Anyone with information is asked to contact Newcastle West Garda Station on 069 - 20650, the Garda Confidential Line on 1800 666 111 or any Garda Station.”
