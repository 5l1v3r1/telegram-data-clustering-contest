Harry Styles throws shade at One Direction band mate Zayn Malik in SNL monologue

17 Nov 2019, 08:49

Harry Styles hosted Saturday Night Live for the first time last night and wasted no time in dissing his former One Direction bandmate Zayn Malik.

In his opening monologue, the 25-year-old singer briefly discussed the group going their separate ways in 2015 before snubbing the Dusk Till Dawn artist as he hailed Niall Horan, Liam Payne and Louis Tomlinson as his ‘brothers’.

Showing off his comedic side, Harry began: ‘I don’t know if you heard or not, I’m not in a boyband anymore. I’m in a man band now.’

He continued: ‘I was in a band called One Direction, how crazy would it be if they were here tonight? Well, they’re not. Wouldn’t it be crazy if they were though? They’re not.

‘I love those guys, they’re my brothers.  Niall, Liam, Louis… and Ringo. Yeah, that’s it.’

Harry then quipped: ‘I’m just so grateful Simon Cowell grew us in those test tubes.’

Viewers really couldn’t handle the shade Harry threw at Zayne and rushed to Twitter in shock, with one writing: ‘HE REALLY JUST DID ZAYN LIKE THAT WOAH #SNL.’

Echoing a similar sentiment, another commented: ‘I was not expecting this episode to start out with some Zayn shade.’
