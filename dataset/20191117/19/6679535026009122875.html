‘Fake’ journalists warned

17 Nov 2019, 19:04 by Staff Reporter

The Nilgiris district administration has warned of action against “fake” journalists, following allegations that persons claiming to be reporters tried to extort money from business owners and merchants.

In a press release jointly issued by the Collector and the Nilgiris District Superintendent of Police, they noted that there had been reports of persons claiming to be journalists trying to extort money from business owners and merchants on multiple occasions.

People who had been targeted have been asked to contact the district police at 0423-2444111 and inform the police about such persons.

It has also been announced that persons who are not accredited journalists, but use “PRESS” stickers on their vehicles will face strict action from the police.

The district SP has announced that vehicles checks will be stepped up starting Monday, the press release added.
