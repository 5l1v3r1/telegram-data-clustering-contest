Max Verstappen takes victory in stunning Brazilian Grand Prix

17 Nov 2019, 19:43 by FSA Editor

Max Verstappen secured a stunning win in the Brazilian Grand Prix – on a shocking day for Ferrari and a tough one for Lewis Hamilton

Max Verstappen made it worth the wait as he triumphed in a remarkable Brazilian Grand Prix, a year after an Interlagos victory was snatched from his grasp.

A cruel collision with back-marker Esteban Ocon 12 months ago saw Verstappen hand the lead to Lewis Hamilton, who went on to take the checkered flag.

Verstappen was so upset on that occasion that he took out his frustration on Ocon when remonstrating with the Frenchman after the race, and was handed a two-day ‘public service’ punishment.

This time it was world champion Hamilton who was left frustrated, frequently chuntering complaints from the cockpit as he and Verstappen vied for supremacy over long stretches of the race.

Victory after a series of dramas went to Verstappen, and rather than Hamilton it was Toro Rosso’s Pierre Gasly who finished second. Hamilton crossed the line third for Mercedes, but a late collision with Alex Albon cost him a five-second penalty and dropped him to seventh place. Hamilton apologised after the race and a penalty was inevitable.

Hamilton was unhappy with the choice of tyres – soft rather than medium – at the first change, and seemed repeatedly irked.
