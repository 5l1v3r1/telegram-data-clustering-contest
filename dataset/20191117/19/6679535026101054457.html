Documenting a dancer’s journey

R.V. Ramani receives Bala Kailasam Memorial Award

17 Nov 2019, 19:43 by Staff Reporter

Well-known documentary film-maker R.V. Ramani received the Bala Kailasam Memorial Award 2019 for his film Oh that’s Bhanu, on 95-year-old dancer Bhanumathi Rao, at the Tagore Film Centre in Chennai on Sunday.

The film explores themes of memory and time via Bhanumathi, who is suffering from memory loss herself. Oh That’s Bhanu is a film that looks into the journey of Bhanumathi as a dancer and a theatre actor through the eyes of her daughters and Ms. Bhanumathi herself.

Speaking about the film, Mr. Ramani said it was heartwarming to receive the Bala Kailasam Award for a film that he started in the year Bala Kailasam passed away.

Nullifying the truth

“I started this film in 2014, when Kailasam passed away. It is strange that after completing this film, I am here,” said Mr. Ramani. He said he decided to make a film on Ms. Bhanumathi after he felt a connection with her, when he met her for the first time at her daughter Maya Rao’s house in New Delhi.

Responding to a question about whether her difficulty in remembering her past proved to be hurdle in getting to the truth, he said: “Most of the time, Bhanumathi is making up things — she had to make up things. I don’t believe in the idea of truth. I always nullify the truth. Here, the character itself nullifies it.”

Speaking about R.V. Ramani’s career, S. Panneerselvam, Readers’ Editor of The Hindu, said: “Whether it is a tragedy like tsunami or the recurring music season, Ramani has documented it in a particular manner — not in the academic sense, but as a practitioner. This has opened the space for the film. One of the reasons why we selected the film was that it retained the space for everybody’s idea of memory — it is not cast in stone and it is fluid.”
