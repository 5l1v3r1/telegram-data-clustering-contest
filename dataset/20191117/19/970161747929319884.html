What year is His Dark Materials set and where was it filmed?

17 Nov 2019, 19:00

BBC’s latest – and massively popular – fantasy series His Dark Materialshas gripped viewers across the country since it premiered earlier this month.

From nostalgia over the original Phillip Pullman books to the captivating storylines, stellar cast and impressive visuals, there’s a lot to drink in.

But one question which keeps niggling us is: where is it supposed to be set, and in what year?

What year is His Dark Materials set?

While the series looks completely other-worldly, we can look at the novels to get a sense of when the action is supposed to take place.

The books were written in the 1990s and were set in an alternate version of the present day.

Because the books were set contemporary to when they were written, the series has followed suit and is set contemporary to when it was filmed – AKA 2019.

Producer Jane Tranter spoke about their decision to bring the setting forward, introducing smartphones and computer hackers into the mix.

She told Entertainment Weekly: ‘We wanted to make it work for 2019 and Philip was fine on that.

‘We then were just mindful of how technology makes a difference… The rule of The Magisterium in Lyra’s world, the whole point of their rule is to discourage learning and information and exploration.’

Where is His Dark Materials filmed?

Given the fantastical and ethereal feel of the show, it’s little surprise that CGI plays a big role.

Many scenes were shot with green screens at Wolf Studios in Cardiff.

Cast member Lin-Manuel Miranda also tweeted that some filming had taken place in Brecon Beacons.

The National Park in South Wales is home to lush lakes and sprawling hills and greenery.

It seems Lin-Manuel is a fan too, as he tweeted: ‘Coming home from filming in the Brecon Beacons all day, no cell service and miles of mountains & sky. I’m a miss [Wales] so much y’all.’

Exterior shots for the fictional college of Jordan were filmed at Oxford University, as well as New College Lane and the city’s iconic Bridge of Sighs.

Other filming took place at the River Severn in Gloucester and Crickhowell, South Wales, where the crew built a tin-roofed village specifically for the show.

His Dark Materials continues on BBC One on Sundays at 8pm.
