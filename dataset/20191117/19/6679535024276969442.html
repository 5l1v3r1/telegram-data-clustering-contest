Hundreds take part in Navy marathon

Navy Week activities promote twin initiatives of ‘Fit India’ and ‘Go Green’

17 Nov 2019, 19:14 by Special Correspondent

The third edition of the Kochi Navy Marathon saw a large number of Kochiites participating in the event.

The marathon was organised as part of the Navy Week activities to promote the twin initiatives of ‘Fit India’ and ‘Go Green’.

While the 21-km ‘Venduruthy Run’ was flagged off by Vice Admiral A.K. Chawla, Flag Officer Commanding-in-Chief, Southern Naval Command, the 10-km ‘Dronacharya Run’ and 5-km ‘Garuda Run’ were flagged off by Rear Admiral Joy Chatterjee, Command Medical Officer, and Rear Admiral RJ Nadkarni, Chief of Staff, respectively.

Route

The marathon route took runners through Willingdon Island, before taking the National Highway and continuing across two of the longest bridges in the city till they reached Kundannoor Junction and returned.

Speaking on the occasion, Vice Admiral Chawla reiterated the importance of being health conscious and emphasised the importance of ‘Fit India’ and ‘Go Green’ initiatives supported by the marathon.
