JNU students to march to Parliament today

17 Nov 2019, 19:47 by Special Correspondent

JNU students on Sunday announced that as part of their protest against the hike in hostel fee, they will organise a “long march” from the university to Parliament on the first day of its winter session on Monday.

The JNU Students’ Union (JNUSU) said that the march was to “save public education” and they would like to ask Parliamentarians to ensure that “education remains a right and not a privilege”.

The JNUSU appealed to students from other universities to lend their support to the protest and join them on their march.

“JNU is on the brink of destruction and if the university administration has its way, fees will rise by over 1,100%..., As per JNU’s own data, over 40% of students will have to dropout and possibly leave their education,” said the students’ union.
