Son who battered dad with a hammer to protect mum 'would do it again'

17 Nov 2019, 18:10

A son who went to prison for attacking his own father with a hammer says he does not regret it.

Ben Wilson, 27, said his father had frequently abused his mum Tracey and that day had threatened to ‘chop everybody up’ with a six-inch knife.

He heard screaming from his parents’ room and rushed in with the hammer, where he hit his dad at least five times at the home in Billingham, Teesside.

His dad Craig was left with holes in his face and brain damage and was found with fractures to his jaw, cheekbone, eye sockets and nose.

Wilson said: ‘I’d do the same again.

‘I sacrificed myself to save my mam. I thought if I have to do prison for the rest of my life for my family, then I’m going to do it.’

In an interview with the Daily Mirror, he said he snapped after watching his dad beat his mum for almost a decade, including on New Year’s Eve in 2007 when he strangled her and was sentenced to two years in jail.

Ben Wilson said: ‘I picked up a hammer from my toolbag and went up. He said “what the f*** are you doing, you creepy ****, what’s behind your back?” I belted him on the temple.’

After hitting his dad he cycled to the police station and handed himself in, still covered in blood.

He was given six years in jail after he admitted grievous bodily harm in January 2015.

It was the lowest possible sentence, which the judge said took into account his previous good character and the years he had had to deal with his father’s abuse.

Wilson said he now believes the law should change to take into account if someone is defending a family member.

Tragically, he was rebuilding a relationship with his father while in Durham prison, but his father killed himself before he was released.

He was allowed to leave prison to go to the funeral.

After serving four years in jail, Wilson was released six months ago and is trying to rebuild his life.

Despite blaming himself for his father’s death, he said he can’t regret what he did because it might have saved his mother’s life.

Cleveland Police told the paper they had attended the house several times before the attack.

A spokesman said the force was ‘extremely sorry’ the family felt let down and officers now received improved training in domestic abuse cases.
