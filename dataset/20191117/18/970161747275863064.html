How long does I'm A Celebrity's new series last and when is it on tonight?

17 Nov 2019, 18:01

I’m A Celebrity is back for its 19th season on ITV, with Ian Wright the firm favourite to follow in the footsteps of fellow former footballer Harry Redknapp by succeeding him as the series winner.

As usual, the card is stacked with intrepid stars, although fans of the show will also be excited to see the return of Ant McPartlin as co-presenter, rejoining regular partner Declan Donnelly after Holly Willoughby stood in for him last year.

Promotion for the series has leaned heavily on Ant and Dec, showing them roaring at each other while adrift at sea in a gale, marooned in the Arctic, appealing to a cruise ship on a punctured dinghy and lazing on a deck in the sun.

Familiar cruelties potentially lie in wait for the glamorous cast, including isolation from the outside world and the prospect of being consigned to a Bushtucker Trial at the whim of the voting public.

So just how much I’m A Celeb do we have to look forward to as the new series kicks off? Here’s what you need to know…

How long is I’m A Celebrity on for and when did it first air?

Since 2013, I’m A Celebrity has run for 22 days – although not all of its contestants last that long, with the likes of Noel Edmonds, who was first out last year, staying for 13 days.

Illusionist Uri Geller was evicted even sooner in the first run in 2002, staying for nine days in a series eventually won by former BBC Radio 1 DJ Tony Blackburn.

This year’s lineup is typically intriguing and eclectic, with James Haskell, who is married to Richard Madeley and Judy Finnegan’s daughter, Chloe, in with a chance of becoming the fourth sportsman to win the series.

Haskell says his appearance as a beefy rugby player is no reflection of his resilience, insisting: ‘People think you’re big, they think you’re going to be tough. There is that pressure because people look at you and go, “you’re big, you’re going to be fine, mate.”‘

‘I’m absolutely not. All I want to do is make sure I don’t let my teammates down, cry or be sick on myself if some bugs crawl up my nose.’

As well as Wright, Coyle and Jossa, the contestants alongside Haskell include presenters Adele Roberts, Kate Garraway and Roman Kemp, comedian Andrew Maxwell, Caitlyn Jenner and Rak-Su singer Myles Stephenson.

What time does I’m A Celebrity start?

The latest series of I’m A Celebrity is returning in its usual 9pm slot on ITV, which is probably a wise decision given its audience figures of 11m viewers for the opening episode last year.

Running until 10.45pm, the series starter will introduce the uninitiated to the contestants as they get their first taste of jungle life – with Nadine Coyle already getting a bit emotional about it all.
