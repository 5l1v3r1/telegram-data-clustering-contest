NLCIL inks pact with Mineral Exploration Corporation

MoU envisages carrying out exploration in Pandiyur, R.S. Mangalam lignite block

17 Nov 2019, 18:25 by Special Correspondent

Neyveli Lignite Corporation of India Ltd (NLCIL) has signed a memorandum of understanding (MoU) with Mineral Exploration Corporation Ltd (MECL) for regional exploration of lignite blocks in Ramnad district.

According to a press note from NLCIL, this follows the Ministry of Coal’s approval for the NLCIL’s proposal to raise the outcomes of promotional exploration as per the annual targets set by Ministry of Coal in lignite areas by taking up regional/detailed exploration through outsourcing mode.

The MoU envisages carrying out regional exploration and detailed exploration in Pandiyur lignite block and R.S. Mangalam lignite block in Ramnad district.

The proposal approved by the Ministry was also included in the annual programme of lignite exploration (2019-2020). As per the project proposal, a total quantum of 48,000 m of drilling for Pandiyur and 34,5000 m for R.S. Mangalam blocks (Sub-Block A, B, C, D) have been planned.

The Ministry has given approval initially for lignite exploration on 5,000 m for Pandiyur and 30,000 m in R.S.Mangalm Block-A in the 2019-20 annual programme.

The exploration activity is expected to enhance the lignite reserves substantially.

The MoU was signed by Prabhakar Chowki, Director (Mines) and Ranjit Rath, CMD, MECL, in the presence of Rakesh Kumar, CMD, and Functional Directors R.Vikraman, Director (HR), N.N.M. Rao, Director (P&P), Shaji John, Director (Power) and T. Venkatasubramanian, CVO of NLCIL.

It is proposed to take up exploration initially in Pandiyur and R.S. Mangalam Block-A as per the approved annual programme (2019-20) of Ministry of Coal under the MoU with MECL.

Next phase

During the next phase, exploration activities will be continued under the same MoU based on the approved annual programme of the Ministry of Coal by adopting the applicable Ministry of Coal rates, the press note said.
