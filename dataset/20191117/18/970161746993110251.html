Harry Kane sets record as England beat Kosovo in final Euro 2020 qualifier

17 Nov 2019, 18:52

Harry Kane became the first England player to score in every game in a qualifying campaign as Gareth Southgate’s side beat Kosovo in their final Euro 2020 qualifier.

The Three Lions secured their place at next summer’s Euros with a 7-0 win over Montenegro and ensured they finished the qualifying campaign on a high with a 4-0 victory over Kosovo on Sunday evening.

Harry Winks’ first England goal put the visitors ahead at the break and Southgate’s charge pulled away from Kosovo in the second-half with Kane, Marcus Rashford and Mason Mount all getting on the scoresheet.

 

Kane doubled England’s lead with ten minutes remaining and in doing so became the first England player to score in every game of a qualifying campaign.

The Tottenham striker also became the first England player in 90 years to score 12 goals in a calendar year.
