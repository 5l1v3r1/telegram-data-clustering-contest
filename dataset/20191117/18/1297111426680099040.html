Campbell Tartans repeat as provincial volleyball champions

17 Nov 2019, 18:25 by Regina Leader-Post

The Campbell Tartans are celebrating back-to-back Saskatchewan High Schools Athletic Association 5A girls volleyball championships.

Campbell repeated as the provincial champion by defeating the Saskatoon Bishop Mahoney Saints 2-0 in Saturday’s gold-medal match at Weyburn Comprehensive High School.

En route to provincials, the Tartans captured their sixth consecutive Regina High Schools Athletic Association title.

Campbell’s roster consists of Eve Piché, Lena Giani, Chloe Bansley, Emma Heavisides, Danielle Wiens, Laila Philson, Victoria Aird, Jordy McEachern, Sara Bodiroga, Emily Berringer, Ally Taylor, Brooklyn Brodner, Brynn Endicott, Alyssa Burant and Willow Skagos.

Tania Harrison, Jordan Parisian, Tiffany Herman and Todd Carignan are the coaches.

In the 5A boys provincial final at Winston Knoll Collegiate, the Meadow Lake Carpenter Spartans defeated the Prince Albert St. Mary Marauders 2-0.

Four other provincial champions were declared Saturday — the Moose Jaw Vanier Spirits (4A girls), Oxbow Black Knights (3A girls), Macklin Sabres (2A girls) and Rose Valley Roadrunners (1A girls).

The 1A to 4A boys provincials are to be held next weekend.

The Saskatoon Hilltops are champions of Canadian junior football for the sixth consecutive season.

Saskatoon defeated the host Langley Rams 11-6 in Saturday’s Canadian Bowl.

Rylan Kleiter had three field goals to help Saskatoon win its 22nd national title.

The Hilltops also extended their overall winning streak to 31 games.

Saskatoon has not lost since Sept. 9, 2017, when the visiting Regina Thunder posted a 29-26 PFC victory.

On Friday, Thunder president Murad Al-Katib was named the Canadian Junior Football League’s executive of the year.

Erik DeRosier scored in overtime to give the Regina Silver Foxes a 4-3 victory over the Assiniboia Southern Rebels in Prairie Junior Hockey League action Saturday at Doug Wickenheiser Arena.

DeRosier and Maison Morin each had two goals for the Silver Foxes, who got 41 saves from Bradley Sentes. Riley Skarbon led Assiniboia with a goal and an assist.

Also on Saturday, Taylor Halbgewachs scored a hat trick to power the Triple Seven Chrysler Storm to a 4-2 victory over the Carrot River Outback Thunder at the Pilot Butte Recreation Centre.

Joseph Young made 34 saves for the shutout as the Weyburn Red Wings posted a 2-0 SJHL victory over the visiting Yorkton Terriers on Saturday.

Liam Rutten and Rhett Frey were the goal scorers.

Also on Saturday, Dalton Dosch had 19 saves for the Nipawin Hawks as they blanked the visiting Notre Dame Hounds 1-0. Mkyllan Couture was the goal scorer.
