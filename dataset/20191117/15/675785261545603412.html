When is the Euro 2020 draw? Date, location and how England's group is decided

England have qualified for the European Championships and already know that regardless of the draw, they will be playing in Group D

17 Nov 2019, 15:45 by Aaron Flanagan, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/aaron-flanagan/

Gareth Southgate's England side now turn their attention to the Euro 2020 draw.

England are desperate to rectify the wrongs of 2016, where they were dumped out in the last-16 by Iceland.

But this time, fans are much more optimistic of success having seen England reach the semi-finals of the World Cup two years ago.

England have breezed through their qualifying group, with the only hiccup coming in the defeat to the Czech Republic. Otherwise, the Three Lions have been at their prolific best.

Here’s everything you need to know ahead of the Euro 2020 group stage draw...

When is the Euro 2020 draw?

The draw will take place on Saturday 30 November.

It is set to start in Bucharest at 5pm GMT.

Which teams are involved?

The draw includes the teams that finished in the top two places from the ten qualifying groups plus the eventual four play-off winners.

How does the draw work?

Each team will be put into a designed pot. There are four pots altogether with six teams in each pot. The pots are ranked 1-4 with the highest seeded teams in pot 1, the next highest in pot 2 and so on...

However, as there are 12 host nations, each nation that qualifies (or could qualify via the play-offs) is placed in one of the following groups so that they are guaranteed to be at home in at least two group games:

  • Group A: Italy, Azerbaijan
  • Group B: Russia, Denmark
  • Group C: Netherlands, Romania
  • Group D: England, Scotland
  • Group E: Spain, Republic of Ireland
  • Group F: Germany, Hungary

If both teams from each of these groups qualify, another draw will be held to determine which team plays at home.

When will the group stage take place?

The group stage matches will take place between 12-24 June.

All of England’s group stage matches will be played at Wembley.

What channel is the draw on?

BBC and ITV hold the rights for Euro 2020 but no confirmation has been made on which channel the draw will be shown.

The Euro 2016 draw was shown on BBC Two and streamed on UEFA’s website.

Where will England's matches be played?

Here are the two paths England could take if they progress from the group stage.

All group games will be at Wembley.

Win group:

  • Last 16: Dublin
  • QF: Rome
  • SF: Wembley
  • Final: Wembley

Second in group:

  • Last 16: Copenhagen
  • QF: St Petersburg
  • SF: Wembley
  • Final: Wembley
