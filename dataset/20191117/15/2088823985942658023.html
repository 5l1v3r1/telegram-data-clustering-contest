Sabres game day: Tage Thompson could make season debut against Blackhawks

17 Nov 2019, 15:13

Matchup: Buffalo Sabres (10-6-3) vs. Chicago Blackhawks (8-7-4)
Where: United Center
When: 7 p.m.
TV: MSG
Radio: WGR 550

Thompson’s time: The Sabres recalled winger Tage Thompson from Rochester on Sunday morning, indicating they will likely be without Kyle Okposo against the Blackhawks.

Okposo suffered an undisclosed injury during the second period of the Sabres’ 4-2 win over the Ottawa Senators on Saturday night. Coach Ralph Krueger called Okposo’s status a “day-to-day situation” and told the media the team planned to evaluate the winger Sunday.

Thompson, who was acquired from St. Louis in the Ryan O’Reilly trade, had an outstanding preseason before being sent to the Amerks near the end of training camp. The 22-year-old had six goals among 12 points in 16 games with Rochester, showing improved play without the puck and an ability to use his size to make an impact around the net.

Thompson is the third Amerks player to join the Sabres this week, following Rasmus Asplund and Curtis Lazar. Buffalo has been without Johan Larsson (upper body), Marcus Johansson (upper body) and Vladimir Sobotka (lower body).
