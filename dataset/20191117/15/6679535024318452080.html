Officials told to ensure smooth travel to Medaram Jatara

Minister directs officials to complete road repairs before year-end

17 Nov 2019, 15:13 by Special Correspondent

Panchayat Raj Minister Errabelli Dayakar Rao has asked the Mulugu district officials to ensure proper transport for the historic biennial tribal fair at Medaram village.

He was reviewing the arrangements for the jatara which is scheduled to be held from February 5 to 8.

The Minister wanted the officials to ensure hassle-free darshan to devotees. Stating that continuous rains had damaged roads in the region, he directed the officials to repair the roads leading to Medaram before the end of December.

Referring to the ongoing road expansion work between Hyderabad and Warangal, the Minister admitted that devotees would have to face some inconvenience. They told the highways authorities to complete the Aler, Vangapally and other bypass roads so as to provide some relief to the motorists.

Mr. Dayakar Rao noted that the Warangal-Khammam road was also in poor shape and directed the officials to take up the repair work immediately. The internal roads in the Warangal city that connect highways should also be repaired. The work on proposed second road over bridge at Fathima Nagar should also be started as early as possible, the Minister said.

Mahabubabad MP Maloth Kavitha said that she would pursue with the national highways authority to start work on the pending the five roads in Warangal division. Wardhannapet MLA Aroori Ramesh sought the government to repair the Warangal-Khammam and Warangal-Karimnagar roads so that to avoid mishaps. Station Ghanpur MLA Thatikonda Rajaiah urged the authorities to speed up the work on road expansion between Hyderabad and Warangal which was moving at a snailâ€™s pace.

Parkal MLA Challa Dharma Reddy, Jangaon MLA M. Yadagiri Reddy, R&B Chief Engineer Ganapathi Reddy, Superintending Engineer Vasantha and Executive Engineer Venkateshwar Rao were among others present.

Medaram jatara is celebrated by the tribals the valour of tribal women Sammakka and Sarakka who went down fighting the imperial Kakatiya army eight centuries ago. Lakhs of people converge in the small village for three days and offer special prayers to the tribal deities.
