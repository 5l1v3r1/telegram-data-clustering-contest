WI-W vs IN-W Dream11 Team, Probable Playing 11, Toss And Pitch Report And Match Report: With the series in their kitty, India look to continue their stronghold over West Indies today as well.

Ever since winning the first ODI, things have only gone wayward for West Indies after a win which was assumed to be the first stone laid towards reviving their sojourn in the international cricketing fraternity. The side have failed to sin a single match against India in the ensuing five encounters contested between the two in white ball cricket, one which has seen them be pummelled in both the ODI and T20 series.

Losing all three of the T20 encounters fought between the two units till now, West Indies are now chasing an unassailable deficit when they clash heads against the Indian in the fourth of the five match T20 series come today’s tie. The side have not just gone onto lose those matches but being comprehensively defeated in them with the outfit left clutching at straws courtesy of their unsavoury and derogatory performances.

Making just 59 runs when the two sides had met most recently, the writing was on the board before West Indies even got to their run chase. They did manage to take India’s travails to 17 overs but the visitors were going to be miffed at peeling down 60 runs, a target they got to with 7 wickets to spare.

And it’s the same kind of display the Indians will be looking to emulate come today’s encounter between the two sides as they look to make it four wins in succession and further add to the home side’s harsh misery. This match looks to be a foregone conclusion before the first delivery is even sent down, a match where West Indies are set to be nothing more than pushovers. 

Pitch Conditions And Toss

The track over here the last time around made one thing blatantly clear. This is a track where the spinners will flourish and thrive with a mere 119 runs being scored in 37 overs in the last affair and today’s surface will be no different.

With the track set to fully crumble and open up n the second part, the team winning the toss is going to have no qualms in coming out and batting first.
