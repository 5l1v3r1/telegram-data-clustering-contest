Linzer tartlet (Austrian jam tart) recipe 

17 Nov 2019, 06:00 by Christin Geweke

These sweet, jam-filled tarts are an Austrian speciality. Delicately spiced with warming cinnamon, cardamom and clove, they're perfect for festive snacking. 

Prep time: 

MAKES 

six

INGREDIENTS 

  • 200g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 125g ground hazelnuts
  • 125g sugar
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 pinch ground cloves
  • 1 pinch ground cardamom
  • 1 pinch ground allspice
  • 1 tsp grated orange zest
  • 150g cold butter, plus extra for greasing
  • 1 egg
  • 250g raspberry jam
  • 1 egg yolk, lightly beaten, for an egg wash

METHOD 

  1. Combine the flour, ground hazelnuts, sugar, spices and orange zest. Dice the butter and rub into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse breadcrumbs. Add the egg and knead everything until well combined. Shape the dough into a ball, cover with plastic wrap and chill for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Preheat the oven to 180C/160C fan/Gas 4. Butter six 10cm shallow tartlet tins and dust with flour. Invert the tins and tap lightly to get rid of excess flour.
  3. Set aside about 200g of the dough. Roll out the rest of the dough on a floured surface until it is about 3mm thick. Cut out six discs to line the tins. Whisk the jam until smooth and spread it evenly over the bases.
  4. Roll out the remaining dough until 3mm thick. Cut into 5mm strips and arrange the strips on top of the jam in a lattice pattern. Brush the lattices and tartlet edges with the beaten egg yolk.
  5. Bake the tartlets for about 30 minutes. Remove from the oven and leave to cool completely.
