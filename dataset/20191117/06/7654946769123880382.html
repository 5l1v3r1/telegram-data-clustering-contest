Trump-backed candidate loses Louisiana governor’s race

17 Nov 2019, 06:34 by Selim Algar

Louisiana’s Democratic Gov. John Bel Edwards declared victory  against Republican rival Eddie Rispone late Saturday — a narrow win that came despite President Trumps’s aggressive support for the GOP challenger.

Trump had routed Hillary Clinton in Louisiana by 20 points in 2016.

“How sweet it is!” Edwards told a crowd of supporters, adding that he had spoken with Rispone by phone.

“And as for the president,” Edwards quipped. “God bless his heart.”

The Associated Press named Edwards the victor with 96 percent of precincts reporting and an advantage of just over 19,300 votes.

Trump had lobbied hard for the Republican businessman, making three separate trips to Louisiana to rally Republican voters.

But those efforts fell short, making Edwards Louisiana’s sole Democratic statewide elected official.

The result serves as a stinging setback for Trump and the GOP, especially after Kentuckians voted in a Democratic governor just 11 days ago, again despite the president’s campaigning.

While his repeated appearances boosted Republican turnout, observers said his injection into Louisiana’s campaign galvanized anti-Trump voters who helped push Edwards to victory.

An attorney and U.S. Army veteran, Edwards became the first Louisiana Democrat to secure consecutive terms since Edwin Edwards in 1976.

Trump had derided him as a liberal liability in league with Nancy Pelosi.
