James McCarthy's Ireland future uncertain after rejecting call-up

The Crystal Palace midfielder made himself unavailable for the New Zealand and Denmark games for ‘personal reasons’

17 Nov 2019, 06:00 by Paul O'Hehir

James McCarthy is facing an uncertain international future with the Republic of Ireland.

The Crystal Palace midfielder made himself unavailable for the New Zealand and Denmark games for ‘personal reasons’.

He was named in the provisional squad and boss Mick McCarthy indicated he would have featured in the Kiwi friendly.

McCarthy, 28, has stressed that he wants to be playing regularly for his new club before he focuses on international football.

It’s unclear what the personal reasons are, although the Ireland manager claims he has not fallen out with the player who last donned an Ireland shirt just over three years ago.

Ireland boss McCarthy said: “There is no problem with me, it’s his decision.

“You have access to him so ask him yourself. I’m not going to discuss what I discussed with him.

“The discussion I had with him was over the New Zealand game. And for personal reasons he’s not here so, please, speak to him and ask him.

“I’m cool with what he’s decided. I haven’t fallen out with anybody. Categorically not and I told him I wanted him to come in.

“He’d have been involved against New Zealand but he couldn’t come for his own reasons.”

Asked if McCarthy could feature next year at the Euros or in a playoff if its needed, McCarthy said: “I’m not ruling anybody out, we don’t have such a big squad to do that.”
