Jordan Pickford highlights importance of England starting with a win at Euro 2020

The Everton goalkeeper is ­already braced for the hype that will come with being one of next year’s co-host nations

17 Nov 2019, 06:03 by Simon Mullock, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/simon-mullock/

Jordan Pickford was “still in nappies” when England last staged the Euros.

But the Everton goalkeeper is ­already braced for the hype that will come with being one of next year’s co-host nations – and ­the country staging the tournament final, at Wembley.

Pickford has established himself as Gareth Southgate’s undisputed No.1 in the 18 months since he went to the World Cup in Russia.

That’s where he helped the Three Lions reach their first major ­semi-final since the original ­‘Football’s Coming Home’ summer of Euro 96.

And while time is on the side of Southgate’s young squad, the 25-year-old Pickford has already experienced enough in his career to know that England must do ­everything they can to seize the day next summer.

Pickford said: “It will be as close to a home tournament as you are going to get without being the sole hosts.

“So yeah, that will add a bit more to the pressure – but I also think it will add to the enjoyment as well.

“Being based at St George’s Park, in a home environment, we know what it will be like and it will give us all a great buzz.

“I was still in nappies in 1996, but I know it will be a great experience because we know how good the fans are. We want to repay them for that support.

“We know the fans will be right behind us – and they should know that as footballers we all want to be winners for them.

“We know how hard winning a tournament is. But the first thing we need to do is win the opening game and the fans will all start to believe.

“During the World Cup, it was quality when we saw how excited the fans were back home.

“We have made great progress as a team in the last couple of years and losing the semi-final in Russia can hopefully spur us on.”

It isn’t just the World Cup defeat at the hands of Croatia in 2018 that has made Pickford realise that time is of the essence in football.

Two weeks ago, he saw Everton team-mate Andre Gomes suffer a horrific injury, as a ­challenge by Tottenham ’s Son Heung-min left the Portuguese midfielder with a fracture and ­dislocation of his ankle.

It was another example of how the game can be cruel is so many ways.

Pickford said: “I didn’t really see the extent of the injury because I was at the opposite end of the pitch and I knew that if I went over to have a look I would risk going into shock.

“I spent most of the time just trying to get the lads to get their focus back on the game because we were losing 1-0. I also tried to help Sonny as well because I don’t think he meant to do it.

“It’s another example that you never know what football is going to bring. You have to take all your opportunities because you can’t ever take anything for granted.”

Pickford added: “That’s why I look back on the World Cup with mixed feelings.

“It was fantastic to be involved. But in the end, not getting to the final was a huge disappointment.

“I suppose that’s why we are all so hungry to achieve more.

“We want to improve as ­individuals and as a group – and we want to put smiles on people’s faces.”
