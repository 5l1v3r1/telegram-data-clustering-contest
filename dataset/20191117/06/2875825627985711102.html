Mick McCarthy hunting for Denmark scalp tomorrow - rather than another shirt

Ireland are a win away from securing automatic qualification to next summer’s showpiece

17 Nov 2019, 06:00 by Paul O'Hehir

Mick McCarthy was clearing out his garage last week when he found an old Danish jersey stuffed away in a cardboard box.

“I’ve got all the shirts and I’ve a few good ones in there,” said the manager who made his competitive international debut as a player against the Danes.

That was a 3-0 World Cup qualifying defeat in Copenhagen against a star-studded side boating Preben Elkjaer, Michael Laudrup, Soren Lerby and Mortel Olsen.

“When you’re out of work, you jet-wash the jet-washer. I’ve done that so many times.
But I was clearing the garage and I started looking.

“Hagi’s was still moving about in the box!

But tomorrow night at Aviva Stadium, McCarthy is hunting a Danish scalp rather than another shirt.

“I’ve not lost the passion to win. Not at all,” he said.

“Certain things I’ve mellowed at and have a different perspective towards, but not football, not competing.”

Ireland are a win away from securing automatic qualification to next summer’s showpiece.

Easier said than done when you haven’t looked in any way convincing in your last two competitive games.

But McCarthy claims the second-half performance against Switzerland in Geneva does offer signs of encouragement.

Thursday’s win over New Zealand has no bearing on tomorrow night as it was a second string team.

Denmark need only a point to advance and while they could adopt a more patient approach because of it, McCarthy insists it won’t change his mindset.

“We’ve got to beat them,” he said. “Will they come here and try and shut up shop? I can’t see that.

“If it’s 0-0 with 15 minutes to go, that’s not a bad scenario. Would I rather be two goals up, yes. Is that likely? Maybe not.

“We’ve only scored six goals so far but if we’re on top with 15 minutes to go, I’d fancy us to win

“We haven't scored that many but a clean sheet is going to be really bloody helpful for us to win a game.

“And we're not going to try and sit back. We haven't done that in any game.

“There’s a difference between defending, sitting back and letting them have the ball - a big difference.”

Acknowledging that Ireland will need to find an extra 5% from somewhere, he added:
“A bit of magic, a slip by somebody else or a free kick, a corner, who knows?

“We have to make sure we play as well as we possibly can. Because if we don’t we’ll get beat and we won’t win it.”
