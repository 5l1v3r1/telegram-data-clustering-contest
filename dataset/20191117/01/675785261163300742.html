Police seize drink-driver's car - where she has been living for six years

Amanda Allerton was living in her Vauxhall Corsa when she was found driving without insurance and driving under the influence of alcohol after a collision with a taxi

17 Nov 2019, 01:54 by Tiffany Lo

A woman arrested for drink-driving after a car accident has been living in her car for six years, a court heard.

Amanda Allerton, 27, was driving her Vauxhall Corsa when her car collided with a taxi near Abbey Hulton, Stoke-on-Trent.

Police received a report of a minor road traffic collision and spotted Allerton's car driving along Leek Road and caught her drink-driving, Stoke-on-Trent Live reports.

Prosecutor Christy Woods told North Staffordshire Justice Centre: "It was stopped and the officer spoke to the defendant, who was driving the vehicle.

"There were signs of intoxication and a roadside breath test was carried out."

 

Allerton, who is living at the Salvation Army hostel, in Vale Street, Stoke, later gave a reading of 77 micrograms of alcohol in 100 millilitres of breath – more than double of the legal limit 35mg/100ml blood.

She pleaded guilty to drink-driving and driving without insurance to the incident happened on October 20.

The court heard she has a previous drink-drive conviction from 2014.

Stephen Oldham, mitigating, said Allerton had been homeless for six years and living out of her car.

Mr Oldham said: “During a lot of that time she has held down a job. She lost her job in July and she is waiting for surgery on her knee.

"She has now lost her car as it was seized by police following this offence, but she has managed to get accommodation at the Salvation Army.

"She is dealing with her anxiety and depression and a drink problem."

Magistrates handed Allerton a £240 fine and ordered her to pay £135 court costs and a £32 victim surcharge.

She was disqualified from driving for 36 months.
