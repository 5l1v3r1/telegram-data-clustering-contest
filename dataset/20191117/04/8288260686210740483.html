DEFENDER KNOWS HE HAS TO IMPROVE

Wolverhampton Wanderers defender Max Kilman has opened up on the time it has taken for him to reach the level required to play for the club’s first team.

Kilman signed for Wolves from non-league side Maidenhead United in the summer of 2018, and has since been battling for a place in manager Nuno Espirito Santo’s starting line-up.

The 22-year-old made his debut for Wolves in May this year as a substitute in Wolves’ 1-0 Premier League win over Fulham last season.

Since then, the centre-back is yet to feature in the Premier League for Wolves, but has played a part in the cup competitions.

Kilman has played the full 90 minutes in Wolves’ back-to-back victories over Slovan Bratislava in the Europa League, and now the defender is eyeing a Premier League start.

The defender has admitted that he needed time to get to the level required by Nuno to feature in the first-team.

JIMENEZ MAKES WOLVES VOW

Wolverhampton Wanderers striker Raul Jimenez was on target twice as he helped Mexico secure qualification to next year’s CONCACAF Nations League semi-finals.

The Wolves frontman was in form for his country as he bagged two goals in a 3-0 win away at Panama on Friday night.

Jimenez opened the scoring in the 8th minute in a scrappy game in Estadio Rommel Fernandez, set up a Edson Alvarez's tap in in the 70th and sealed the win with a late penalty.

Mexico are next in action on Tuesday night against Bermuda, where Jimenez will be keen to add to his goal tally.

The Wolves striker is happy for his county to win against a country that has been a difficult opponent for Mexico down the years.

"I'm happy with the result, because historically we don't do well here [but] the goals went through for us," Jimenez told ESPN.

Wolves return to Premier League action next Saturday against Bournemouth, in what will be a quick turnaround for Jimenez given he will in action on the other side of the world in the early hours of Wednesday morning.

The 28-year-old says that he is “perfecting” his role at Wolves, but is keen to keep on improving for the Molineux outfit.
