Brexit Party candidate who labelled Islam a 'child rapist death cult' is slammed

David Ballantine, who is standing in Edinburgh South West, also made a complaint to the police about the Koran while citing hate crime legislation.

17 Nov 2019, 04:30 by John Ferguson, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/john-ferguson/

Shocking footage of a Scottish Brexit Party candidate calling Islam a “child rapist death cult” has been condemned by Muslim leaders.

David Ballantine, who is standing in Edinburgh South West, made a series of ­troubling comments in clips on his YouTube channel.

In one, he attempts to make a complaint to the police about the Koran while citing hate crime legislation.

An officer tells him over the phone the holy book was written in 609 and would not be covered by the 1995 Act.

In another, the former Scottish Conservative candidate, who is trying to oust the SNP’s Joanna Cherry, calls Islam a “theocratically fascist system of governance”.

And commenting on Labour’s London Mayor Sadiq Khan, he said: “Sadiq ‘my dad was a bus driver’ Khan doesn’t speak for the majority of people in the UK.

 

“Those that follow a child rapist death cult, which is Islam, don’t really have a point of reference for the rest of us.”

The videos also contain supportive comments about far-right convict Tommy Robinson.

In one, he said: “I’m not happy that Tommy Robinson is out of jail because he should never have been in there in the first place.”

Yesterday, the remarks were condemned by the Muslim Council Scotland.

A spokesman said: “By spreading false messages and stereotypes about Muslims and Islam, Mr Ballantine is sowing division and hatred.

“Political representatives have a responsibility to represent everyone in society and not to further hate speech and intolerance.”

 

Stuart Waiton, the Brexit Party candidate in Dundee West, has also been criticised for tweets.

He referred to claims from ex-Manchester United star Patrice Evra and ex-QPR player Anton Ferdinand, who said they’d been racially abused by rivals on the pitch.

A 2011 post read: “I oppose racist chanting. But it should not be illegal. Nor should players tell tales on each other about name calling.”

Another, posted after the footballers had made race abuse claims, said: “What happened to the idea footballers should ‘man up’? Aka Evra and Ferdinand telling tales.”

When the Sunday Mail asked Waiton about his comments, he said: “As an active anti-racist in the 80s and 90s, I have had to fight racists on the street.

 

“As part of a group called Workers Against Racism, we were always keen for working people to fight racism themselves rather than relying on the authorities.

“The best way, in my opinion, to fight racism is to have more free speech, not less, and to have active
anti-racist football crowds.”

An SNP spokesman said: “It won’t surprise many people that Brexit Party candidates have extreme views – but it is extraordinary the Tories are now effectively in a pact with a party which welcomes such candidates.”
