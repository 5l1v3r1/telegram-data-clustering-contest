Long-serving Tartan Army fans to get cut-price Scotland tickets thanks to Sunday Mail

Supporters of the national team over the age of 65 will now be able to see games at Hampden for less than the normal cost.

17 Nov 2019, 04:30 by Gordon Blackstock

Tartan Army veterans will be offered cut-price tickets to the national team’s games thanks to the Sunday Mail.

The Scottish Football ­Association has agreed to ­reintroduce concessions to some of Scotland’s biggest matches for 20 years for fans aged over-65 ­following our ­successful ­campaign.

The ­decision came after the SFA faced ­sustained criticism for having withdrawn the tickets in the first place – including from leading charity Age Scotland and former first minister Henry McLeish.

An SFA spokesman said: “The Sunday Mail has been passionate ­on this subject and this is a ­sensible outcome for all.” The governing body will offer ­concessions for Nations League play-off matches in March 2020.

The crunch fixtures give Steve Clarke’s squad the chance to qualify for a major tournament for the first time since 1998.

It is believed the new ­price structure will see pensioners given a third off tickets from March but details are still to be ironed out.

 

Sources say the SFA is ­considering a “bold ticket approach” to ensure a packed house at Hampden for the vital play-off ties.

The SFA spokesman added: ­“Concession prices will be ­available for over-65s as part of our ticketing strategy for the Euro 2020 play-offs and FIFA World Cup 2022 qualifiers.

“We are thankful for the input of Age Scotland in the ­process and look forward to ­welcoming fans of all ages and ­generations to Hampden Park to help the national team ­qualify for Euro 2020 next ­summer.”

Last September, we revealed the Tartan Army were angered by older fans not being able to get cut-price entry.

Under-16s can get tickets for as little as £8 and Age ­Scotland urged SFA leaders to overhaul their pricing.

Football authorities in other countries – including the English FA – already offer cheaper entry for ­pensioners.

Campaigners ­welcomed the announcement that the SFA has agreed to slash prices.

McLeish, who played ­professional football with East Fife, said: “Firstly, I’d like to ­congratulate the Sunday Mail on taking up this campaign and the SFA for listening.

“This is an important gesture from Scotland’s football body.

“The SFA needs to do all it can to get the fans back in the stands and getting behind our national team. Some of us can remember when Hampden boasted of ­attendances of 130,000. Those days won’t be repeated but the SFA needs to try all the ideas it has to get the attendance as close to full as possible.”

 

And former first minister Lord Jack McConnell said: “Our national team needs all the ­support it can get and this is a ­welcome move from the SFA that will be cheered on by pensioners and their ­families.”

Tartan Army stalwart Hamish Husband added: “This is welcome news. The Sunday Mail has ­campaigned long on this issue, backed by the fans, so this is an important victory.

“There has been a lot of ­negativity around the team and the stadium in recent years so it’s good to have something to cheer about.

“We need to do all we can to get young and old fans alike coming back to Scotland games.”

Paul Goodwin, of the ­Scottish Football Supporters ­Association, said: “I’m delighted that the Sunday Mail has secured this important victory.

“Our research has shown pricing concerns is one of the main reasons older fans stay away from ­Hampden.

“These are largely people without a wage but have contributed ­thousands of pounds towards the game over the years.

“It is only right that this audience is finally rewarded for their loyalty and patience for a lifetime of ­following the Scottish national team.”

Last month, we told how the under-performing national team faced playing in front of a ­near-deserted stadium when they took on the worst team in the world San Marino.

 

Just over 20,000 fans turned out for Scotland’s 6-0 thumping victory at Hampden Park – where the ­capacity is nearly 52,000 – on ­October 13.

The move was also welcomed from within the game.

Scotland’s former women’s team captain Margaret McAulay Rae, 68, said: “This is a great thing and well done to the Sunday Mail for ­campaigning on this issue. I’d get a discount on public transport to get to Hampden so why should I pay the full price to get into the ­stadium?

“A lot of fans have fallen out of love with the men’s team.

“Hopefully this will help get some back to the games.”

Finance experts told how the poor turnout was hitting Scottish ­football authorities in the pocket.

Sports finance expert Neil Patey, of Ernst & Young, said the cost of failing to sell more than half the tickets for Scotland’s five ­competitive home clashes against Belgium, Russia, Cyprus, Israel and Albania was more than ­£4million.

Just 127,969 fans had turned up for those matches out of a potential 259,330 – the equivalent of just 49 per cent capacity.

Commercial sales such as ­merchandise, pies and programmes were not included, so the bill was estimated to be far higher.

Brian Sloan, Age Scotland’s chief executive, said: “Many older people have been loyal Tartan Army fans all their lives but struggle to afford full-price tickets on their pensions.

 

“The SFA’s decision is a step in the right direction towards ­tackling loneliness among older people.

“Around 200,000 older people in Scotland regularly go a week ­without speaking to anyone – the equivalent of four full Hampden Park stadiums.

“A more inclusive pricing ­structure means it’s easier for older people to enjoy a night out at a game and encourage younger fans to bring an older friend or relative.

“We’d also love to see the SFA working with more community groups, such as walking sports clubs and veterans’ groups, to offer ­discounts.

“This is a win-win ­situation, boosting ­attendance at matches while rewarding
lifelong fans and ­reaching out to the wider ­community.”
