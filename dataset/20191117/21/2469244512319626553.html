Three people taken to hospital following serious A30 crash

The crash happened at Tedburn St Mary earlier this evening

17 Nov 2019, 21:25 by Philippa Jenkins

Three people have been taken to hospital following a serious multi-vehicle crash on the A30.

The incident happened near Tedburn St Mary at the Pathfinder Village junction near Fingle Glen on the eastbound carriageway just after 7pm this evening.

The crash itself involved four vehicles and a fifth vehicle was damaged by debris from the crash.

Devon and Cornwall Police have now confirmed the following details following the incident earlier this evening.

A spokesman said: “Four vehicles were involved in the crash and a fifth vehicle was affected by the debris.

“Three people have been taken to hospital but none have life-threatening injuries.

“They have all been t taken to Royal Devon and Exeter Hospital for treatment.

“Highways have been called to scene to deal with the oil spillage from the crash.”

The eastbound carriageway remains closed at this time while recovery work take places. It is best to avoid the area for the time being.
