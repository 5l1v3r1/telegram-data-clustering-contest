Trump bashed Fox News' Chris Wallace, saying he'd 'never be his father,' after Steve Scalise's contentious interview

17 Nov 2019, 21:58 by Kat Tenbarge
  • A “Fox News Sunday” segment between the host Chris Wallace and Rep. Steve Scalise was the target of President Donald Trump on Sunday, as he tweeted that Wallace was “nasty and obnoxious.”
  • Trump also said Scalise “blew” Wallace “away,” attacked the morning show’s ratings, and argued that the “dumb and unfair interview would never have happened in the @FoxNews past.”
  • Apart from congratulating Scalise, who stuck to the Trump administration’s talking points about the impeachment hearings, and suggesting that Wallace “will never be his father,” Mike Wallace, Trump also appeared critical of Wallace snapping back at Scalise.
  • Wallace pushed back at Scalise for seeking to discredit the officials who had testified so far in House Democrats’ impeachment inquiry.

President Donald Trump bashed the “Fox News Sunday” host Chris Wallace on Twitter after a segment with Rep. Steve Scalise of Louisiana about the week’s impeachment hearings.

“.@SteveScalise blew the nasty & obnoxious Chris Wallace (will never be his father, Mike!) away on Chris’s lowest rated (unless I’m on) morning show,” Trump wrote on Sunday. “This kind of dumb and unfair interview would never have happened in the @FoxNews past. Great job Steve!”

Wallace’s father, Mike Wallace, who died in 2012, was a longtime original correspondent for CBS’ “60 Minutes” news program.

Trump can readily expect support from Fox News’ opinion hosts, but he has been vocally critical of the network whenever any of its news segments paint him in a negative light. Recently, he has been tweeting that the channel is “much different” from what it used to be.

“We have to start looking for a new News Outlet,” Trump tweeted in August. “Fox isn’t working for us anymore!” He has also spoken favourably of One America News, which runs flattering stories about the president.
