'Infertile' couple get back together after 15 years and have a baby

17 Nov 2019, 21:55

A couple whose relationship broke down when they suffered a pregnancy loss have reunited almost 15 years later and are celebrating a miracle baby.

Karen, 36, and Sam Cranfield, 44, met when Karen was 17 and Sam was 25 and both worked at the same pub in Louisa Bay near Ramsgate, Kent.

Their relationship was broken beyond repair when two years after being together they lost a baby, a trauma which left Karen infertile.

The pregnancy was molar – where a non-viable egg implants and develops into a mass of cells instead of a baby.

Doctors removed the growth from Karen, apparently destroying her fertility in the process and leading to her pushing Sam away.

Karen said she knew she had made a mistake but never imagined they would get back together.

She explained: ‘I never for a second knew that we’d end up back together and with a baby I didn’t think I’d have.

‘I honestly believe we were supposed to be together.

‘I told him I didn’t love him anymore. It was a lie. The worst lie I’ve ever told in my life.’

After Sam failed to rekindle the relationship, the couple both remarried and he had daughter Perri, 14.

Karen married the year after splitting with Sam but the marriage ended in 2010.

She met a new partner and looked into IVF in 2013 – but doctors told her she had no chance of becoming a mum because of low ovarian reserves and severe scarring in her womb.

She broke up with her new partner shortly after that but fate was at last about to hand her some good news – a Facebook message from Sam

The pair met for dinner in June 2016 and, a month later, Sam proposed by hiding a bunch of flowers and a diamond ring in his wardrobe.

Sam quit his job in London and moved back to Kent in September 2016 and the couple learnt Sam was pregnant the following summer

Baby Louisa was born on March 10 last year at the Queen Elizabeth the Queen Mother Hospital – the same place the couple lost their baby in 2002.

The couple married at Ramsgate Register Office when Louisa was six weeks old.

Sam, 43, said he still can’t quite believe his story has had such a happy ending.

He explained: ‘I was heartbroken when Karen first dumped me. I got ridiculed at work because I was so miserable for so long.
