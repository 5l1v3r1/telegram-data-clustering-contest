Odell Beckham thinks he’s a target for NFL drug tests

17 Nov 2019, 20:20 by Jaclyn Hendricks

Odell Beckham Jr. isn’t pleased about being subjected to another PED test from the NFL.

Speaking with Cleveland.com after the Browns’ 21-7 win over the Steelers on Thursday, the wide receiver spoke out about the league’s random testing.

“[The NFL] made me come in Monday when we had an off day. Had a drug test,” Beckham explained. “Made me come in Thursday after the game. Had another drug test.”

Beckham has been on the league’s radar this season for his bold accessories, but it appears the continuous requests are testing the wideout’s patience.

“I know people who didn’t get tested for five months in the offseason and I’m getting tested every time,” Beckham said.

When asked about Beckham’s statements, NFL spokesperson Brian McCarthy addressed the league’s policy without mention of the three-time Pro Bowler.

“Neither the union or the league are involved in the random selection of players to be tested,” McCarthy said to the outlet. “By means of a computer program, the independent administrator determines which 10 players will be randomly selected each week.”

Beckham is hardly the first NFL player to complain about the league’s testing. Last fall, Panthers safety Eric Reid said he had been tested multiple times, too.

“This is like the fifth time since I’ve been here,” Reid said at the time, per Pro Football Talk. “They’re not going to catch me on anything.”
