LIGHT CHRISTMAS

Edinburgh welcomes Christmas with stunning firework show for Light Night – and Santa even made an appearance

17 Nov 2019, 20:23 by Ewan Mowat

THE festive season truly arrived in Edinburgh today as the capital's Christmas lights were switched on.

Santa even made a flying visit to the Royal Mile for the Light Night celebrations.

Thousands of Christmas-lovers descended on the city's Old Town this afternoon.

And they weren't disappointed with a stunning pyro show on the Royal Mile.

As well as the lights and the fireworks, locals were treated to performances from a number of local musical groups.

Edinburgh’s Little Voices Big Stars and Rock Choir Live both performed, as well as the Royal Lyceum Theatre Edinburgh’s Community Choir fresh from preparations for An Edinburgh Christmas Carol.

And Santa Claus truly came to town as he flew over the heads of the waiting crowds.

The event was hosted by Forth 1's Arlene Stuart.

It comes after Edinburgh's Christmas market opened yesterday.

The East Princes Street Gardens stalls and rides will be open from 10am to 10pm every day until Christmas Eve.

Glasgow also got into the festive spirit tonight with a spectacular fireworks show as the famous George Square lights were turned on.

The sky above the city chambers was lit up by the pyrotechnics, as the crowd watched on from the packed square.

Glasgow's first Christmas market opened last weekend at St Enoch Square - with food from around the world on offer.

And with George Square now adorned with its festive lights, a second market will open there next weekend.
