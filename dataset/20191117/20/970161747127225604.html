Who wrote The War of the Worlds?

The original novel, The War of the Worlds was written by H.G. Wells.

Written between 1895 and 1897, the story was one of the earliest to deal with a conflict between mankind and extra-terrestrial life.

H.G. – Herbert George to his parents – was a prolific British writer.

Some of his other popular sci-fi works include The Time Machine, The Island of Doctor Moreau and The Invisible Man.

He is often called ‘the father of science fiction’ and his work earned him an impressive four nominations for the Nobel Prize in Literature.

In addition to his awe-inspiring literary contributions, Wells was a diabetic and co-founded the charity The Diabetic Association in 1934.

That charity is still active and is known as Diabetes UK today.
