This Stat About The Redskins’ Offense Is Absolutely Embarrassing

17 Nov 2019, 20:51 by Tzvi Machlin

Week after week new NFL teams seem to be trying to assert themselves as the biggest dumpster fire in the NFL. If it isn’t the Dolphins, it’s the Jets. If it isn’t the Jets, it’s the Giants. If it isn’t the Giants, it’s the Browns.

But the Washington Redskins have put up an offensive “performance” this season that is putting all of those bad teams to shame.

With the end of the third quarter of their game against the New York Jets today, Washington went 16 straight quarters without a touchdown.

Per ESPN’s Rich Cimini, it’s a “feat” that hasn’t been matched since the 2000 Ravens went 21 quarters without a TD.
