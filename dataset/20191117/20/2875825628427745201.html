Ian Wright's emotional meeting with his old teacher that left him in tears

Wright credits his old school teacher Mr Pidgen as being his first role model who helped turn his troubled life around to become an inspiration to others

17 Nov 2019, 20:11 by Irishmirror.ie

Ian Wright has recalled an emotional meeting with an old teacher who helped him through his troubled childhood.

The former Arsenal striker cried after being reunited with Mr Pidgen as part of a documentary on Wright's career at the Gunners former Highbury stadium.

Mr Pidgen surprised his former pupil by appearing at the ground where Wright scored the majority of his 185 goals for the club, prompting him to burst into tears.

Wright was re-telling the story ahead of his involvement on the new series of ITV's 'I'm A Celebrity... Get Me Out of Here', with the first episode due to air on Sunday.

"I literally started to cry like a five-year-old," Wright recalled. "Uncontrollable crying because of how happy I was to see him.

"Looking back it was then that I realised how much of a positive impact that man had on my life.

"He's somebody that is very special to me. But I think what was more important to me than anything was how much he actually loved being a teacher.

"It's amazing what a teacher can do to somebody in their life."

Wright counts Mr Pidgen as the first real role model he had in his life due to a troubled upbringing, and looked up to him as a father figure.

He explained to his old teacher that he thought he had passed away previously.

Wright's father absconded and left mother Nesta to raise her family in a one-bedroom house in Brockley, South London.

"He was a weed-smoking, gambling, coming-home-late, gambling-his-wages, womanising kind of guy,” Wright told the Players' Tribune.

"He was rough with my mum and rough with all of us kids. And I don’t know why, but he didn’t like me in particular.

Wright, 56, is the favourite to win the ITV hit show, which will see him spend weeks in the Australian jungle, with former England rugby star-turned MMA competitor James Haskell also among the contestants.
