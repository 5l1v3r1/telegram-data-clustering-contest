Teenage P-plater with two 17-year-old passengers smashes into house at Grange

17 Nov 2019, 23:18

A 17-year-old P-plater driving a car with two other teenage passengers has smashed into a home in Grange overnight, prompting the SES to secure the property.

Police say the teenage boy was driving a Holden Commodore on Westmoreland Rd in Grange just before 9.30pm on Sunday when he lost control while turning onto Ian St.

He crashed into a house, as well as a car in the driveway of the property.

Also in the Commodore were two 17-year-old passengers, which violates P-plate conditions.

No one was injured in the crash.

A brick pillar was extensively damaged, prompting the State Emergency Service to sure up the property.

The driver was issued an on-the-spot fine for failing to maintain control of the car and breaching his P-plate conditions.

Police say further charges are likely at a later time.
