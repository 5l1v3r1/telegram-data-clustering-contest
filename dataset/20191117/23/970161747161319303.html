Holly Willoughby looks cool as ice as she joins Lily Allen at Frozen 2 premiere

17 Nov 2019, 23:39

Lily Allen, Idina Menzel, Holly Willoughby, Stacey Soloman and Jonathan Groff were among the stars looking cool as cucumbers on the red carpet of the Frozen 2 European premiere tonight.

Some stars even brought their adorable kids to London’s South Bank dressed up in Frozen-themed costumes and it’s too cute.

Voice actors present at the premiere include Idina Menzel – who plays the ice queen herself, Elsa – as well as Jonathan Groff (who plays Kristoff) and Josh Gad (who plays snowman, Olaf).

Also present were directors Chris Buck and Jennifer Lee, as well as Walt Disney film producer Peter Del Vecho.

Lily Allen’s girls Marnie Rose, 6, and Ethel, 7, stole the show with little Elsa and Anna costumes, while their mother stood tall in a long dress and boots.

This Morning presenter Holly looked chic yet warm in a simple all black outfit with a houndstooth coat thrown casually around her shoulders.

She definitely dressed for the chilly November evening adding long black leather gloves to her look.

And her look was finished off with a festive-feel thanks to her pretty snowflake shaped hair accessory.

It was a real family affair for Stacey Soloman, who brought along her two boys, Rex and Leighton, as well as her nieces, Darcy and Mila.

(Isn’t little Rex in the reindeer outfit the cutest?)

While YouTube star and current Strictly Come Dancing contestant Saffron Barker wore a matching dress with her little cousin, Ivy.

Guy Ritchie was joined by his wife Jacqui Ainsley, as well as their three children – Rafael, 8, Rivka, 6, and Levi, 5.

Although the plot details of Frozen 2 have been kept under lock and key, we’ve seen from the trailer that Elsa and Anna will venture beyond the kingdom of Arendelle.

And, perhaps ominously, a character in the trailer says: ‘We have always feared that Elsa’s powers were too much for this world. Now, we must hope they are enough.’

We can’t wait to see what happens.

Frozen 2 hits UK cinemas on 22 November.
