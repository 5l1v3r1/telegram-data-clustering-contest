Cab driver wanted for Bronx stabbing says he’s the victim

17 Nov 2019, 23:29 by Israel Salas-Rodriguez, Craig McCarthy and Jackie Salo

A cabbie accused of stabbing a Bronx man says police got the story backwards — and that he was only defending himself from a fare-beater who attacked him.

Sandy de Jesus Rodriguez — who says he plans on turning himself in to cops on Monday — is wanted for allegedly slashing a guy inside a Bronx apartment building on Oct. 13.

On Sunday, Rodriguez said he was defending himself after the man and his gal pal stole $190 from him and attacked him when he tried to get it back.

“I did have a small pocket knife with me, but I used it only to intimidate the man and try to get my money back, but I did not stab him,” de Jesus Rodriguez said.

The hack said the alleged fare-beaters ditched him four months earlier without paying when he dropped them off in White Plains, but he didn’t realize the woman he picked up Oct. 13 was a member of the dastardly duo until it was too late.

“Those people knew what they were doing, because they called the base through a private number. It was premeditated,” he said.

De Jesus Rodriguez said he brought the gal pal to her destination — where her beau was waiting inside the building — and the woman took off from the cab, claiming that she had to get money to cover the fare.

“I went to get something from the side of the seat and with a flash the woman was out of the car,” de Jesus Rodriguez said. “I looked back and she was gone, and I looked at my cup holder where I keep my money and that was gone too.”

He said he went inside the building to get his money back, only to be greeted by the man from the previous ride.

“When I confronted them and told them that they were the same couple from White Plains, the man immediately bum-rushed me, and the woman ran towards the elevators,” de Jesus Rodriguez said.

He said they engaged in a “brief scuffle,” and he pulled out his pocket knife to get the man to back off him.

“Not one time did I lay a hand on him,” the cab driver said. “I did not hit him or stab him unless he intentionally cut himself to make it look like I did but I did not cut him in any way.”

De Jesus Rodriguez said he wasn’t aware that cops were looking for him until they released a video of the scuffle last week.

“I’m going to turn myself in tomorrow at the precinct, because I already gave my word and I have to confront this problem,” he said. “I’m 100-percent certain that the police will find out that guy’s lying once we get the medical records.”

The NYPD did not immediately respond to a request for comment.
