CAUGHT LIVE

Jake Bugg tells Glasgow crowd it’s ‘a pleasure’ to be back in Scotland as he rocks Barrowlands gig

17 Nov 2019, 23:45 by Aidan Smith

JAKE BUGG hailed it was a “pleasure to be back in Scotland” as he took to the stage at the Barrowlands.

He was last on Scots turf at the Kilmarnock Dirty Weekend concert at this time last year.

But tonight he was the main man at a huge headline gig for his loyal Scottish fan base.

They roared him on as Be Someone - his popular collaboration with CamelPhat - blared in the background.

Trouble Town and There’s a Beast and We All Feed It were to follow and fans went wild as pints went flying.

Nottingham singer-songwriter Bugg released his new single Kiss Like The Sun this week.

The new track has a country rock jam feel and it was the first taste of what’s to come from the 25-year-old in the new year.

Despite only being released this week fans already knew the song word for word.

Classic Lightening Bolt was chanted as the iconic venue's roof prepared to be lifted.

Bugg added: “Just want to say thank you cause every time I come up here it’s absolutely amazing”.

Broken was up next and the way the rocker seamlessly moved between his electric and acoustic numbers was something to be admired.

Two Fingers rounded off the Barrowlands set and the youthful crowd absolutely adored it.

Jake Bugg fans are in for a treat if his latest album is anything like as good as his new tunes Kiss Like The Sun and Sing.

Here’s hoping a return to Scotland is on the cards in 2020.
