The best Home Bargains hacks you will need for your next visit in store

The retailer now stocks around 4,000 branded products and, despite its regular deals, there are some tips to save even more at the discount stores

17 Nov 2019, 23:34 by Chiara Fiorillo

Discount retailer Home Bargains has long been loved by shoppers for its low prices and wide range of products.

The Liverpool-based chain opened its first store in Old Swan in 1976.

The retailer now stocks around 4,000 branded products, according to the Liverpool Echo .

Despite its regular deals, there are some tips to save even more money at Home Bargains.

1. Look at the price tag to see how long an item is in stock for

An insider from the store has revealed the price tags, as well as revealing the cost of an item, also show whether the product is a one-off or a regularly stocked product.

The initials REG stand for regular, meaning the product will be in stock the majority of the time and restocked when they sell out.

ONE, however, stands for one off.

2. Head to bigger stores if you are looking for a particular item

Bigger stores receive daily stock replenishment, while smaller stores get less frequent deliveries.

 

3. Don't be in a rush to get inside

Some of the best deals are located near the front of the store.

4. Star buys

Usually located near the front of the store, they change weekly and include discounts on branded products.

5. Get there early

New stock is often delivered at around 7.30am in the morning so it will help to visit the store when it opens.

6. Try its food range

There is a mix of fresh fruit and veg alongside cupboard essentials and more.

7. Designer replicas

Home Bargains also stocks items which look like designer products - but for much less.

8. Take a break while shopping

Most larger stores now have cafes and garden centres too.

9. Wide range of products

Home Bargains stocks more than 4,000 brands.

10. If you like something, buy it

The best discounts always go fast and can often be limited. If you like something, buy it before it goes.

11. Park close to the store

It will be easier to carry your shopping to your car.

12. New openings

New openings often come with deals and offers on the first day so they're always worth visiting.

13. Check social media

This is where Home Bargains often reveals its new products first.

14. Christmas range

Christmas trees starts from £14.99 and lighting starts from £4.99.

On its website you can even order personalised Christmas Eve plates and decorations too.

15. Check the clothing section

Baby clothing, pyjamas and even limited footwear can often be found in its stores.

16. Ideas for pets

You can also Christmas jumpers for pets in store.

17. It has two websites

Home Bargains has its traditional website and one dedicated to flowers.

You can visit Home Bargains website here .

18. One website is dedicated to flowers

You can take a look at  www.homebargainsflowers.co.uk  where you'll find a whole range of flowers for birthdays, anniversary's and more as well as plants and gifts too.

19. Discount coupons

Discount coupons are not accepted in stores.

20. The payment card which won't be accepted in stores

Home Bargains doesn't accept American Express cards as payment.

21. If you lose your receipt you may still be able to return an item

If you do not have your receipt, you can use a bank statement instead.

It would be the store manager's discretion as to whether they would offer a replacement or goods to the value of with this.

22. You can no longer use its Click & Collect delivery service

According to Home Bargains website, it says: "Our Click+Collect service has been temporarily removed from our site while we update the service.

"So that we can completely rebuild the service to provide customers with the best possible new experience, we've unfortunately had to remove the option while this process takes place.

"Any items that are eligible to be sent home delivery can still be ordered for home delivery."
