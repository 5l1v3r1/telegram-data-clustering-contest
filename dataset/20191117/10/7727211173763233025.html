Hidden away house that's frozen in time

There aren't many properties like this in Merseyside

17 Nov 2019, 10:32 by Lottie Gibbons

A quirky bungalow has come on the market in Merseyside that looks straight out of the 1970s.

Located on The Drive in West Derby , this three-bedroom house is a throwback to years gone by - and is hidden behind beautiful mature climbing plants and bushes.

The home, up for sale with Entwistle Green, is priced at £600,000 - a £99,950 reduction on the original asking price of £699,950.

In a listing on property website Zoopla , the property is described as 'the jewel in the crown as far as bungalows go' - and there certainly aren't many homes like this in Merseyside.

The bungalow is just around the corner from Walker Playing Fields, West Derby Golf Club and Melwood Training Ground.

It has a secure gated parking entrance leading to a leafy landscaped garden with pond, optimised for privacy.

Inside, the bungalow boasts three bedrooms with the interior design scheme looking virtually unchanged for decades.

Following on from a wide and welcoming entrance hall, the art deco style of the property starts with the open plan kitchen and dining area.

Wooden beams and exposed brick play a central theme in the dining area which is decorated with zebra sofas and French windows.

In the lounge, interesting paintings and quirky lamps brighten up the room, along with light green velvet couches.

The chalet style kitchen has a comprehensive range of solid wood base and eye level units which complement the tongue and groove ceiling.

Adjacent to the dining room, one of the bedroom overlooks the rear view garden which is accessible via patio doors.

Storage draws that double up as seating wrap around the walls, meaning there's plenty of space for clothes.

The large family bathroom features fully tiled walls in keeping with the art deco style, but best of all, the bathroom has a generous low level circular bath.
