Scots teacher feared vicious Rottweiler would rip off her arm in savage attack

Kristina Aburrow was crossing a road in the Granite City when a Rottweiler left her with both physical and mental scaring in an unprovoked attack.

17 Nov 2019, 10:04 by Daniel Morrow, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/daniel-morrow/

A Scots woman feared that her arm would be ripped off in a terrifying Rottweiler attack.

Kristina Aburrow was crossing Great Southern Road, Aberdeen , when she was mauled by the dog.

The 34-year-old was rushed to hospital after suffering a four-inch gash after the animal clamped its teeth into her left forearm.

She was able to make a physical recovery inside Aberdeen Royal Infirmary , however the mental scars remained for months after the ordeal.

 

Kristina, who runs art classes for children, revealed that the kids she teaches are also terrified of the scar that has been left with.

She says that she was unable to cycle, hike, or even do simple tasks like dress herself or prepare food as she lived with PTSD following the attack.

“I can’t describe the panic and fear of seeing this giant animal tear at you”, Kristina said.

“The teeth. The snarling. It was terrifying. I genuinely thought it would rip my arm off.

“Now whenever I walk past big dogs I feel my heart pound.”

Kristina recalls seeing a woman walking two Rottweilers crossing her path before one of them lunged at her on September 18, 2018.

The art teacher reported the incident to Police Scotland but was shocked to hear that officers have taken no action against the owner.

She later secured justice in the civil courts and received damages, but called on the force and prosecutors to improve its handling of dog bite attacks.

“There was no reason for that dog to go for me and I don’t know why it did”, she added.

“I don’t believe in bad breeds, but I do believe there are bad owners and we rely on the police to help society and victims by keeping bad owners in check.

“For there to be no further action then is baffling - I wasn’t even pointed in the direction of other legal help or aftercare.

“Fiscals or Police Scotland, whoever is responsible, needs to address these failings because a victim shouldn’t have to campaign for their own justice – isn’t that what the justice system is for?”

 

Kristina's solicitor, Robert Kernaghan, Partner at Digby Brown’s Dundee office, said: “This was a truly traumatic incident for our client and despite suffering a terrible physical injury it’s often the scars we don’t see that have the biggest impact.

“That is why it is vital that victims know there are other ways to secure answers and justice in the absence of criminal prosecutions.

“Civil law is clear on dog bite cases - if an animal has bitten someone then the owner of that animal is strictly liable, unless of course the animal was acting in self-defence or was goaded into attacking.

“Digby Brown helps thousands of people every year after suffering from negligence such as road or workplace accidents, asbestos diseases or accidents abroad – and dog bite attacks are no different.”

A spokesperson for Police Scotland said: "Police were called to the Bridge of Dee roundabout, Aberdeen, around 4.40pm on September 18, 2018, following a report of a woman being bitten by a dog.

"The woman was taken to Aberdeen Royal Infirmary for treatment.

"Following enquiries, no crime was established and details were passed to the dog warden at Aberdeen City Council."
