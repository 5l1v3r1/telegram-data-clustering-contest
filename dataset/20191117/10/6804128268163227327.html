'Can't wait!' - Chelsea fans think they know what Gareth Southgate will do against Kosovo

The Chelsea winger could be set to make his second start for England

17 Nov 2019, 10:43 by Andy Ha

Callum Hudson-Odoi is among a handful of players who could come into England's line-up for the Euro 2020 qualifier against Kosovo on Sunday.

The Chelsea winger was an unused substitute during Thursday's 7-0 thrashing of Montenegro, a result which sealed England's place at the European Championship next summer.

Southgate is set to recall Raheem Sterling after dropping the Manchester City winger for his role in the training ground bust-up with Joe Gomez.

Nick Pope is also poised to start over Jordan Pickford, with Southgate wanting to hand the Burnley goalkeeper some crucial international experience.

Hudson-Odoi could also be set to come into the side ahead of Jadon Sancho, leaving the England boss with a tough selection headache.

But Southgate says he is delighted to be blessed with a group of young, hungry players - many of whom ply their trade for Chelsea.

“But I would imagine that Sven thought the same of Beckham, Scholes, Carrick, Gerrard, Lampard,” Southgate says. “If you’re the England manager, you’re normally in a reasonably privileged position. We have given young players their opportunity ahead of their time, which has given us a greater depth.

“There’s nothing better than seeing the smile on Tammy Abraham’s face when he gets his first goal for England [on Thursday]. That almost makes everything worthwhile.”

Chelsea fans took to social media before the game, with many believing Hudson-Odoi will indeed get his chance against Kosovo and win his third senior cap.
