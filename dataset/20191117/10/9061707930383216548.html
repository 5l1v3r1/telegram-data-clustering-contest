Neighbours raging as fireworks let off at 5am in Derbyshire town

The loud bangs woke a lot of people up

17 Nov 2019, 10:49 by Paul Whyatt

People living in a Derbyshire neighbourhood have vented their fury after being woken by fireworks in the early hours of this morning.

Residents in the Petersham area of Long Eaton say loud bangs went off between 4.30am and 6am.

Posting on the Spotted: Long Eaton Facebook page , one person wrote: "Anyone else hear the fireworks at 5am this morning around the College Street area?"

A number of people replied, including Lindsey Danielle who said: "Yes, I did. Quite a few went off too."

Several complained that the fireworks had woken them up.

Another person posted: "Some people have got no respect at all. I do not live in that area but I feel sorry for the other people, especially on a Sunday morning."

It is against the law to set off fireworks between 11pm and 7am, except on certain occasions:

  • On Bonfire Night, adults are allowed to set off fireworks until midnight
  • On New Year's Eve, Diwali and the Chinese New Year, fireworks are allowed up until 1am

Anyone caught breaking the rules can be fined up to £5,000.

If deemed serious, culprits could be imprisoned for up to six months for selling or using fireworks illegally.

An on the spot fine of £90 can also be issued.
