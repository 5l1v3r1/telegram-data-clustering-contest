BLUEUROMILLIONS

Ex-Rangers chief Craig Whyte was urged to try and tempt Euromillions winners to invest in the club

24 Nov 2019, 20:09 by Douglas Walker

CRAIG Whyte was told to ask Colin and Christine Weir for a day out at Rangers — 24 hours after they scooped their EuroMillions jackpot.

Finance expert David Grier urged the Light Blues supremo to try and tempt the £161million couple to invest in the stricken club.

But his cash plan was scuppered when it emerged the Weirs, from Largs, Ayrshire, were Partick Thistle fans.

A source said of the 2011 plot: “Whyte was desperate to attract more investment.

“He would have happily sold 20 per cent of his share to the ‘great and good’ of Glasgow.

“The Weirs would have been perfect with all the money they had come into.

“A charm offensive would have been launched to entice them. But sadly they were Thistle fans.”

Mr Grier, who later worked for Gers administrators Duff & Phelps, wrote in an email: “See a couple from Largs have won £160million.

"Let’s find out if Rangers fan and invite to the club.”

The Ibrox club went bust less than a year later over a £9million unpaid tax bill.

We can also reveal a mystery female was prepared to pump £5million into the ailing giants.

 

She pulled out at the 11th hour as it emerged Mr Whyte was previously banned from being a company director.

The source added: “She was offered a seat in the directors box for life.”

Colin, 71, and Christine, 62, split in April.

Colin bought control of his beloved Jags last week.
