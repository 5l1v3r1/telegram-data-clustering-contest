Greg Schiano isn’t coming back to save Rutgers football

24 Nov 2019, 20:22 by Post Sports Desk

The Greg Schiano pipe dream is officially over.

The former Rutgers football coach, who took the program to new heights during his 11-year tenure, will not be returning to the program as head coach, according to multiple reports. Schiano was in talks with school officials about a comeback, and there was optimism it could happen, but a deal never materialized.

According to Stadium’s Brett McMurphy, Rutgers offered Schiano an eight-year, $32 million contract. NJ.com reports Schiano “acquiesced on several key issues,” but he and the school still couldn’t agree to a deal.

Rutgers fired Chris Ash on Sept. 29, the day after an embarrassing 52-0 loss to Michigan. The Scarlet Knights fell to 2-9 (0-8 in the Big Ten) with a Saturday loss to Michigan State.
