Second arrest made over UFC star’s missing stepdaughter Aniah Blanchard

24 Nov 2019, 20:27 by Lee Brown

A second man has been charged with kidnapping Aniah Blanchard, the missing 19-year-old stepdaughter of UFC heavyweight Walt Harris, police have confirmed.

Antwain “Squirmy” Shamar Fisher, 35, was arrested in Alabama on Friday on a warrant charging him with first-degree kidnapping of the missing teen, authorities told the Montgomery Advertiser.

He was busted by Auburn police aided by members of the U.S. Marshal’s Task Force, the paper stated. He is now being held in Alabama’s Lee County Detention Center.

It’s unclear what role he may have played, and a gag order prevents attorneys and prosecutors from speaking about the case, the paper said.

He was the second arrest in the case, with Ibraheem Yazeed charged with first-degree kidnapping earlier this month.

Yazeed was allegedly captured on surveillance video at the same Auburn convenience store where Blanchard was last seen on Oct. 23, officials said, with them always predicting further arrests.

Her car was later found with blood, suggesting “a life-threatening injury,” authorities have previously revealed. Her whereabouts remain unknown.
