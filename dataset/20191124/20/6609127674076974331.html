BRAZEN HOODS

Glasgow kingpin Stephen ‘Jimmy’ Jamieson poses in Glasgow club wearing £10k watch while partying with £9m drug gang pals

24 Nov 2019, 20:07 by Douglas Walker

COCAINE kingpin Stephen Jamieson has been living the high life on nights out with freed cronies from his £9million drug gang.

The brazen hood, 36, splashed the cash partying with Steven Caddis, 40, at a bar in Amsterdam, Holland.

Another snap shows flashy Jamieson, known as “Jimmy”, with pals including Caddis’ brother Gary, 38, at Sanctuary nightclub in Glasgow’s West End.

A source said: “Jamieson and the Caddis brothers are living it up and are bold as brass.

“They don’t make any secret of being close pals.”

During the meet-ups Jamieson flaunted a £1,000 Moncler Down jacket and showed off a £10,000 Hublot watch.

The source added: “They swagger about wearing all their designer clothes and watches.

“But everybody knows what sort of people they are.”

The trio and Brian McCulloch were caged in 2009 for a total of 30 years after flooding Glasgow with coke.

Jamieson was freed in 2013 after serving half of his eight-year term.

It’s unclear whether the partying pals have breached terms of their release with the nights out.

The Parole Board for Scotland said it didn’t comment on individual cases.

But a spokesman added: “A breach of licence conditions can lead to a recall to prison.”

We told how Jamieson, of Paisley, is living in a £260,000 house owned by millionaire taxi tycoon Stevie Malcolm.
