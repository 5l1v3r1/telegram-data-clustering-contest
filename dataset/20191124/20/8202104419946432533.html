Freeland, Moe to meet Tuesday in Regina

24 Nov 2019, 20:49 by Alex MacPherson, Saskatoon StarPhoenix

The new deputy prime minister is headed west days after being appointed as Prime Minister Justin Trudeau's new deputy prime minister.

Prime Minister Justin Trudeau is sending his new top deputy west for an in-person meeting with Saskatchewan Premier Scott Moe, who has been among his government’s most vocal critics.

Chrystia Freeland is expected to sit down with the premier in Regina on Tuesday, less than a week after being appointed deputy prime minister and minister of intergovernmental affairs.

Regina Mayor Michael Fougere’s office also indicated that a meeting with Freeland has been scheduled.

Fougere and Saskatoon Mayor Charlie Clark both spoke with Trudeau last month, after the prime minister signalled his intention to hear concerns and potential solutions from different levels of government.

Freeland is one of three cabinet ministers with western ties tasked with soothing western anger following an election that resulted in a Liberal minority with no representation in Saskatchewan and Alberta.

“The Premier has had a positive working relationship with Minister Freeland, and he looks forward to discussing issues that are facing our province,” said Moe’s press secretary, Jim Billington.

Alex Lawrence, a spokesman for Freeland, confirmed the meeting with Moe on Tuesday.

Freeland’s first trip west as deputy prime minister comes days after Moe returned from a post-election meeting with Trudeau in Ottawa during which he pushed his “new deal for Canada agenda.”

Moe called that meeting “disappointing,” and said Trudeau made no commitments on his priorities: a pause on the federal carbon tax, equalization reform and better market access for the province’s commodities.

Ottawa subsequently offered a different account of the meeting. A federal official with knowledge of it told Postmedia that Trudeau came in with an open mind and Moe brought little to the table.

Moe has said he has a “fairly strong relationship” with Freeland, who previously served as foreign affairs minister, but offered a more tepid assessment of Trudeau’s new cabinet.

“It’s not about the people that are around the table; it’s about the decisions,” he said.
