Are, friends, families honour Olowude at 80

24 Nov 2019, 01:48 by Ademola Babalola

Ademola Babalola

The Chairman, Kakanfo Inn and Conference Centre, Dr Lalekan Are, was among other dignitaries that graced the 80 years thanksgiving service and luncheon in honour of a former Deputy Director of the Africa Project Management Division, International Fund for Agricultural Development, Dr Adebisi Olowude.

Olowude clocked 80 on November 11.

Are, who chaired the occasion, described the host as a “young old man, loyal friend, upright and blessing to humanity.”

“He is a very determined achiever, very generous to a fault. One of the best agriculturists in Africa,” he added.

Are said when Olowude was in Rome, Italy, working as a Project Controller in the Africa Division of IFAD, an agency of the United Nations, he displayed exemplary performance and rose through the ranks to become a deputy director.

“He brought a lot of reforms and initiatives to help countries and individuals be the best they could be in their fields,” he said.

The host thanked Are, family, friends and associates for celebrating him on his 80th birthday.

Dignitaries who graced the occasion included Are’s wife, Bisi; host’s wife, Adefunke; Rev Lowo Mamadelo; Rev Mayowa Ojuade; Rev Julius Okanlowo; and Mrs Yemisi Olateru-Olagbegi (née Olowude).
