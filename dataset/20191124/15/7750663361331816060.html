Arsenal team news: Injury, suspension list vs. Eintracht Frankfurt

Arsenal head to Germany for a meeting with Eintracht Frankfurt in the Europa League in desperate need of a victory.

24 Nov 2019, 15:14 by Leo Nieboer

Pressure on Unai Emery increased on Saturday following a 2-2 draw with Southampton that leaves the Gunners with a record of one win from their last seven games.

Victory on Thursday, however, would confirm Arsenal as winners of Group F, allowing for Emery to rest players for the final fixture against Standard Liege.

Here, Sports Mole rounds up the latest injury and suspension news for the Gunners ahead of this fixture.

Dani Ceballos

Status: Out
Type of injury: Thigh
Possible return date: December 21 (vs. Everton)

The Spaniard picked up a thigh injury whilst on international duty that was at first not thought to be serious, but now looks set to keep him out for another month.

Sead Kolasinac

Status: Minor doubt
Type of injury: Thigh
Possible return date: November 28 (vs. Eintracht Frankfurt)

Kolasinac was assessed ahead of the meeting with Southampton after returning from international duty with a tight hamstring, with Emery ultimately deciding against risking the defender. However, he looks set to return to action on Thursday.

Rob Holding

Status: Major doubt
Type of injury: Knee
Possible return date: November 28 (vs. Eintracht Frankfurt)

The defender has recovered from a long-term knee injury, making a comeback in September, but has since aggravated the area and is unlikely to be risked during the week.

Bukayo Saka

Status: Out
Type of injury: Knee
Possible return date: Unknown

Having impressed in this competition so far this term, Saka will be disappointed to miss out on Thursday due to a knee problem that has sidelined him since the start of this month.

ARSENAL'S SUSPENSION LIST

Arsenal have no players who are absent through suspension for the game this weekend.
