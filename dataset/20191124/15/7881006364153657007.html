'Alliance will go strong': Uddhav to NCP MLAs at Mumbai hotel, missing MLA returns

The NCP had on Saturday moved 44 MLAs to Renaissance Hotel in Mumbai.

24 Nov 2019, 15:01 by ANI

Mumbai: Shiv Sena chief Uddhav Thackeray and party leader Aaditya Thackeray held a meeting on Sunday in Renaissance Hotel where Nationalist Congress Party (NCP) MLAs are staying since yesterday night.

Party chief Uddhav Thackeray while addressing the NCP MLAs said, "Do not worry, this relationship will go long, our alliance will go a long way".

 

NCP chief Sharad Pawar and Shiv Sena chief Uddhav Thackeray, reportedly, also held a closed-door meet in the Renaissance hotel after the meeting with NCP MLAs concluded.

The NCP had on Saturday moved 44 MLAs to Renaissance Hotel in Mumbai.

NCP MLA Manikrao Kakate, who was missing since yesterday and who reportedly accompanied Deputy Chief Minister Ajit Pawar to the Governor's residence had also reached the Renaissance hotel on Sunday morning.

Earlier in the day, NCP chief Sharad Pawar also met MLAs of his party.
