ESPN Releases Prediction For Nebraska-Iowa

24 Nov 2019, 16:48 by Tzvi Machlin

Two storied Big Ten West programs collide in Rivalry Week when Iowa takes on Nebraska at Memorial Stadium in Lincoln. But while one team hopes to secure a New Year’s Six berth, the other just hopes to be playing come bowl season.

The Hawkeyes have somewhat quietly been putting together yet another stellar season with Kirk Ferentz at the helm. After beating then-unbeaten Minnesota last week, Iowa avoided a letdown this week with 19-10 win over a solid Illinois team. Considering that their only losses have been to ranked teams, the Hawkeyes have proven about as tough as they come.

The Huskers are coming off easily their strongest win of the season (which isn’t saying much unfortunately) following a 54-7 thrashing of hapless Maryland on the East Coast. They’re 5-6 on the season, already exceeding their win total of the last two seasons, but need this win to get bowl-eligible.

ESPN’s computer model has released its prediction for the Nebraska-Iowa game.

ESPN’s computer model believes that Iowa is the most likely winner. The computer model gives the Hawkeyes a 70.5 percent chance to win this Saturday’s game.

Iowa is currently on a four-game winning streak against Nebraska, and has three straight wins at Memorial Stadium.

With such talented opposition, Scott Frost will need to put all of his money where his mouth is to get that coveted sixth win.

The game will be played at 2:30 p.m. EST on BTN.
