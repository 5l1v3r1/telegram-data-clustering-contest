Kilmarnock cafe offering hope for those in recovery from addiction

The Foundations Cafe aims to give people on the road to recovery a chance to rebuild through teamwork and gaining useful employability skills.

24 Nov 2019, 16:00 by Tara Fitzpatrick

The Foundations Cafe will open its doors every Friday at The Howard Centre.

The project aims to provide support and community for those in recovery from drug and alcohol addiction.

The cafe has 16 volunteers and has received funding from Alliance Scotland, The National Lottery and Firstport.

The cafe follows an engagement event in August organised by Recovery Enterprises Scotland and Someone Else’s Addiction which included contribution from 110 stakeholders  including elected members, strategic leads, local and national service providers.

Last year saw a rise in drug related death in East Ayrshire with 29 people in 2018.

Additionally, alcohol related deaths increased from 21 people to 30 people; and suicides increased from 12 people to 26 people during the same period.

The Foundations Cafe aims to give people on the road to recovery a chance to rebuild through teamwork and gaining useful employability skills.

It is inclusive to families and aims to engage people across the community.

Organiser Mark Gallagher of Recovery Enterprises Scotland said: “This is purely the start and have some way to go in building and sustaining the model, hopeful there will be local buy-in where the approach is recognised as an asset to all community members.”

The Foundations Cafe is open each Friday from 5 to 8pm in the Howard Centre, Kilmarnock

Home cooked three course meals cost £2 and families cost £5.
