Boris Johnson says 'Twittersphere isn't my province' despite 1,200,000 followers

24 Nov 2019, 16:01

The prime minister has said Twitter isn’t really his thing, after being questioned about the Conservative Party renaming its official Twitter account to ‘factcheckUK’.

The party was criticised by official fact checking organisations after renaming its account during the live leaders’ debate on Tuesday this week, making it appear as though it was an impartial fact checking outlet.

It then went on to offer commentary on Jeremy Corbyn’s performance, in a move described as ‘inappropriate and misleading’.

As Mr Johnson unveiled his party’s manifesto at Telford earlier this afternoon, he was asked about the change of the Twitter account and whether it undermined trust in the Conservative Party.

Looking somewhat perplexed by the question, Mr Johnson replied: ‘The Twittersphere is not my province’.

He went on to add that ‘Labour have some sort of operation which is very similar to this’, before saying he ‘hadn’t really followed this Twitter stuff’ as much as others would like.
