4:07 pm November 25, 2019 Highlight

'No apology' for robodebt

Sam Clench

We have one last exchange to highlight from Question Time. This one was about the government's robodebt system.

"Given the government has now suspended robodebt after three years of operation, is it because the government, at the time of creating it, either didn't seek legal advice, had inaccurate legal advice, or received legal advice, but just didn't think that Australians would notice the government unjustly enriching itself at the expense of the most vulnerable in Australian society?" Bill Shorten asked.

"Let me say very categorically, this government does not apologise for its efforts to protect the integrity of the welfare system," the relevant minister, Stuart Robert, replied.

He said "refinements" had been made to the program, most recently to require more proof points before debt notices are issued.

"No one gets a leave pass. No one at all. We have an absolute legal responsibility to collect debts owed to the Commonwealth."

Not taking much of a backward step there.

Mr Robert also mistakenly referred to Mr Shorten as the leader of the opposition, before promptly correcting himself. Brutal.
