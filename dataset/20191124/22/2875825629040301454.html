Pep Guardiola explains why Man City are not failures if they don't beat Liverpool to title

City still trail the leaders by nine points, despite this gutsy win, with Jurgen Klopp’s side showing no signs of let-up in their pursuit of a first title for 30 years

24 Nov 2019, 22:30 by Irishmirror.ie

For Pep Guardiola, winning would seem to be everything.

Twenty-six trophies in a decade as a manager, including two Premier League titles and two Champions League triumphs, is testimony to that.

But with his Manchester City side facing the prospect of relinquishing their title to Liverpool , Guardiola claimed coming out on top is not all that matters.

City still trail the leaders by nine points, despite this gutsy win, with Jurgen Klopp’s side showing no signs of let-up in their pursuit of a first title for 30 years.

But even if City fail to make it three straight title wins this season, they should not be deemed failures for coming up short, according to Guardiola.

“I know here there are winners and losers - and that’s all,” said the Catalan.

“But I think for the sport, for a message for society, it’s not good for our kids, our teenagers, to show them that just the winner is perfect.

“We’re creating a depressed people, losing people and it’s not good. The most important thing is effort, commitment.

“In football, I know we want to win, but there’s just one winner and the other is a disaster. It’s not right.

“That’s why I said to the players, relax, do our job and we’ll see what happens in January, February and March. If we’re far away, OK, we’ll try again next season.

“After we won the last two Premier Leagues, it looks like we have to win seven in a row and get 200 points every season. It doesn’t work that way at the high, high level.

“Tennis players lose games, in golf Tommy Fleetwood doesn’t always win the tournament, even though I’d like that. If we lose, we lose.

“But I promise you, when I see my team don’t fight, with no spirit, a lack of intensity and desire, I’m going to say, ‘I don’t like my team, I don’t like the way they play’. In these three-and-a-half years, people can say we play good or bad, but never that we give up, no.

“Maybe we’re going to lose the title, but this team never gives up. That’s why we won today against an incredible, top side.”

City had to dig deep to beat Frank Lampard’s enterprising young side, who dominated possession, a rarity against Guardiola’s ball-loving teams.

Indeed, City’s overall possession of 46.74 per cent was the lowest recorded by a Guardiola side in any of his 381 top-flight matches at City, Bayern Munich and Barcelona .

Victory came at a cost for City, who lost record goal-scorer Sergio Aguero to a muscular injury which Guardiola claimed “looks bad”, while David Silva and Rodri were also forced off after feeling discomfort.

In a season blighted by injuries, with City still missing Leroy Sane, Aymeric Laporte and Oleksandr Zinchenko, they can ill-afford further enforced losses to a squad already stretched to the limit.

N’Golo Kante gave Chelsea the lead after 21 minutes, before Kevin De Bruyne levelled with a deflected shot.

Riyad Mahrez decided the game before half-time with a goal of individual brilliance, as Guardiola’s men – denied a late third from Raheem Sterling when VAR ruled him offside – held on to remind Liverpool they are not about to go away quietly.
