Steve Bruce explains why Villa job was harder than his Newcastle role, and that cabbage

Newcastle head coach Steve Bruce has been speaking about managing Monday's opponents Aston Villa

24 Nov 2019, 22:30 by Andrew Musgrove

Since arriving at St James' Park in August, Steve Bruce has faced some harsh criticism but he maintains managing tomorrow's opponents was tougher.

Over the last four months, Bruce has faced questions over his tactics and style of play with heavy defeats inflicted by Norwich City and Leicester City - yet Bruce reckons that the role he found himself in at Aston Villa tops United in terms of difficulty.

Having taken the job at Villa Park in October 2017 with Villa 20th in the Championship, and without a win in 11 games, Bruce guided them to mid-table before a play-off final the year later.

It was quite the achievement for Bruce, especially given that Aston Villa were struggling financially.

"It was arguably my most difficult job, even more difficult than this," Bruce said.

"I got criticised, yes, but for that 18 months only Man City scored more goals than us.

"It was always brought up as my style of play.

"They are a very difficult lot. Great club, great support with great history, but it was in a mess."

A play-off final against the backdrop of ever tightening purse strings wasn't enough, and with just two defeats in their opening 11 games to the 2018-19 season, Bruce was sacked.

The fan discontent had peaked with a cabbage being thrown at the feet of Bruce.

"It summed up that day against Preston, cruising 2-0 up at half-time, James Chester got sent off and then 3-2 down with two minutes to we equalised.. we then had a penalty, the ball fell to Glenn Whelan, he thought being the most experienced man on the pitch he should take it.

"He had never scored a penalty in his life, I said give it to Bolasie because he had scored twice. He wanted to take the responsibility.

“I thought it (cabbage) was a ball to begin with, it was a big old thing.

"How he got it into the stadium I don’t know. Stephen Clemence miscontrolled it when came in, my reaction was ‘what the f*** is that?’ It didn’t miss me by much, it fell at my feet. I thought it was a ball, the size of it.

"The fans can throw some things, but a cabbage?

“I went the next day so I didn’t have time to get angry. It was one of the most unsavoury times of my career.”

United head down to Villa in good form - and are looking to secure a third win on the bounce.

Magpie fans have sold out their allocation, and Bruce wants to secure all three points.

"Overall, I can look back on it and say I thoroughly enjoyed it.

"It's a great club with big tradition big history but I want to go back and beat them. It's simple as that!"
