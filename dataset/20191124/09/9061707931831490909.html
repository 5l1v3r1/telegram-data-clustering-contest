Woman thanks Derby hospice for looking after her dying dad so well

It gave him a last lease of life

24 Nov 2019, 09:46 by Noah Martin

A Derby woman has given an emotional thanks to the hospice who gave her dying dad a last lease of life.

Louise Mellor, 34, said the care her father received at the Treetops Hospice during his final months "helped him to live while he was dying".

She said her 70-year-old dad, Dennis Mellor, would always make an effort to go to the hospice even when he struggled to get out of bed.

When her father, who died in February, was diagnosed with cancer earlier this year, Miss Mellor said it came as a massive shock.

She said: "Dad had a really bad cough which initially was thought to be a chest infection but within a couple of months, he was diagnosed with Mesothelioma."

Mesothelioma is a rare form of incurable lung cancer and Mr Mellor was given between three to nine months to live.

"We decided as a family that weâ€™d assume it was three months, because then we would make every day count up to then," Miss Mellor said.

Once he had been diagnosed, she said her father accepted it quickly because he wanted to make the most of the time he had left.

"It was inspiring and his attitude helped me to accept his diagnosis, we had always been close, my mum used to say were were like peas in a pod," she said.

After researching places where her dad could go to improve his quality of life, Miss Mellor found the Treetops Hospice in Risley.

Mr Mellor started visiting Treetops every week and his daughter said it made a huge difference to his life.

"My dad looked forward to going, he would come back so excited to tell us all about what he'd done each week.

"It was somewhere he went where he could still be himself, not his illness," Miss Mellor said.

As well as talking "a lot about the food", Mr Mellor tried things that he "would never have" before like Reiki and therapeutic massage.

Miss Mellor said: "It was so nice to see him happy and that helped me to cope a little better."

It was only when the illness took hold that Miss Mellor said she realised just how important Treetops was to her father.

She said it kept him going, even on days when he wouldn't have been able to get out of bed, Treetops arranged transport for him.

"He kept setting himself little goals and one of his last goals was to have Christmas dinner at Treetops.

"It was just as important to him as having Christmas with me and mum," she said.

Mr Mellor made it to the Christmas dinner but that was the last time he visited the hospice.

"Treetops helped dad to live while he was dying, they were a massive part of his last year of life and whatever he needed, nothing was too much trouble.

"I genuinely can't thank them enough."

Miss Mellor urged people to support their local hospice so that the "amazing gift" could be there for other families.
