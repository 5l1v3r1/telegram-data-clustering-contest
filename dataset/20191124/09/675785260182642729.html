Digital arcade booths let players become part of classic games including Pacman

A new ‘hyper-immersive’ gaming experience has opened in London, where players become part of classic games, including Pacman and Brick Breaker

24 Nov 2019, 09:00 by Shivali Best, https://www.facebook.com/shivalibestjournalist/

From virtual reality to high-definition displays, gaming technology has come on leaps and bounds in recent years.

Now, a new ‘hyper-immersive’ gaming experience has opened in London, where players become part of classic games, including Pacman and Brick Breaker.

The experience, called Electronic Theatre’s LightBox, sees players don visors, each with a unique antenna on it, before entering a small square room.

While the room seems pretty bare, cleverly-placed projectors quickly transform the walls into motion-sensing touch screens.

Teams of up to six are then put through a series of interactive games and challenges, ranging from memory tests to agility training.

The unique antennas on the visors allow the room to track the position of each player, meaning games can be controlled by physically moving around the space.

For example, during a Brick Breaker game, each player becomes a paddle, and must run up and down the room to bounce the ball up towards the bricks.

Meanwhile, during Pacman, players take it turns to run around the room to collect coins, while avoiding the ghost.

Will Dean, founder and CEO of Electronic Theatre, said: “Electronic Theatre is an experience that any group - be they friends, family, or work-mates can complete and feel a bit closer to each other.

“Creating shared memories bonds groups like nothing else. By completing one of our Adventures, teams will create a memory they can look back on together”.

The first site is located on London’s Southbank, and is open to the public now.
