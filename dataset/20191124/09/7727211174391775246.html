Police raid cinema as 'group of more than 100 fight with machetes'

The Birmingham entertainment complex was evacuated when the mass brawl broke out

24 Nov 2019, 09:08 by Olivia Tobin

Police stormed a cinema after receiving reports of "more than 100 people" fighting with machetes as horrified children dressed in Frozen costumes watched.

Witnesses said the terrifying scenes at Star City, near Nechells, Birmingham, was "one of the scariest moments" of their lives after the huge riot broke out.

The youngsters started fighting in the entertainment complex which was evacuated on Saturday night.

Many young families were at the cinema, with lots of young children dressed up to see the new Frozen film.

One witness told Mirror Online: "That's probably one of the scariest moments of my life. Me and my daughter were in a queue to watch Frozen, loads of little kids there, all dressed up and everything, then these girls jumped on another girl and loads of these kids just started fighting.

"Armed police came with Tasers. All the people that were fighting run off into the cinema. There’s about 15 to 20 police cars. I’m shaking."
