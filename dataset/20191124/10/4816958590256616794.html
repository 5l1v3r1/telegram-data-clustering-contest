No further Brexit delay, says UK minister Gove

24 Nov 2019, 10:23 by Reuters

LONDON — British minister Michael Gove ruled out on Sunday asking the European Union for another Brexit delay to secure a new trading relationship with the bloc.

Asked on the BBC’s Andrew Marr show whether a Conservative government would ask for an extension, Gove replied: “No … We’ll be out of the EU actually by January, we will have formally left.”

“We’ve done a huge amount of work already, alongside the Withdrawal Agreement, in the political declaration which lays the groundwork for the deal we want … We will secure a deal.” (Reporting by Elizabeth Piper; Editing by Mark Potter)
