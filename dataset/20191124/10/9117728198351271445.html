Latifi won't return to F2 if F1 chance at Williams doesn't come his way

24 Nov 2019, 10:17 by Tom Mason

Nicholas Latifi has said he definitely won't return to race in F2 next year if he fails to secure a place on the 2019 grid. It's widely expected that the Canadian will replace Robert Kubica at Williams, but nothing has been confirmed.

“For me, Formula 2, this was always going to be my last year," Latifi told Race Fans.

“Coming into the season I knew it was gonna be my last year, good or bad. So I’m happy I had a good season. We just have to see for next year what will be the plan.”

The other alternative for Williams is to go for Renault driver Nico Hulkenberg who wasn't given a new deal and was replaced by Esteban Ocon for next season, although Latifi remains the front runner.

Williams has struggled once again this season, picking up just one point and are bottom of the constructors' championship.
