Police and fire investigation under way after Derby house fire

Emergency services are still at the scene

24 Nov 2019, 10:17 by Zena Hawley

Police remain at the scene of a fire in Derby today after spending all night investigating a blaze which broke out on Saturday night.

Fire crews from Kingsway, Nottingham Road and Ascot Drive were called to the scene in London Road, Alvaston at 9.21pm. Police also attended and the road was closed for a time while the fire was dealt with.

On Sunday morning, a police van was still parked outside the building, which appears to be a rooms-to-let property. Derbyshire Fire and Rescue Service has also confirmed it has officers at the scene involved in the investigation.

But both emergency services were remaining tight-lipped about what had happened and if there were any casualties.

A Derbyshire police spokesman said: "We are investigating the fire and we have had officers at the scene all night involved in the investigation. We cannot give any more details."

And a Derbyshire Fire and Rescue Service spokesman added: "I can confirm we attended an incident at a domestic premise and we arrived at 9.29pm having received the call at 9.21pm.

"I cannot comment further on the situation but it is still an open incident and we have officers there investigating."

A nearby resident, who did not want to be named, said she had seen the emergency services there last night. She said: "I don't know what has happened but there were police cars and fire engines here until quite late."

Another local person. who also did not wish to be named, added: "I live down Wisgreaves Road and heard a lot of sirens but I don't know what has happened. I just saw the police van there this morning that's all."

Derbyshire Live has asked the police for more details about what has happened and is awaiting further information.
