Deontay Wilder hits back at Tyson Fury's coach following weakness claims

24 Nov 2019, 10:00

Deontay Wilder dismissed Ben Davison’s claims he spotted weaknesses during his rematch with Luis Ortiz in Las Vegas.

Tyson Fury’s coach had flown into Vegas to be ringside for the fight in a bid to do some up close homework on his heavyweight’s next opponent.

Davison suggested Wilder showed the ill effects of fighting Fury against Ortiz, but the WBC champion laughed off the coach’s comments in his post-fight press conference.

‘Every time a person fights, we see different holes and different things,’ Wilder said in response to Davison.

‘You’re not going to be perfect in a fight, no one is. I hope he took notes and carries it back to his camp because I’m going to knock Fury out like I did the first time.

‘Point blank, period. I’m not worried about what anyone says. I’m proven.

‘If they were so sure about certain things and what they’d seen, he would have took the rematch immediately.

‘I’m not running round doing a 100 thousand things. I was the one who demanded the rematch after a controversial decision.

‘I’m the one fighting the best in the division. There’s no other fighters taking risks in any fights.’
