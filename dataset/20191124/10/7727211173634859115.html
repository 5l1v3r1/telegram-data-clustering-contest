Enchanting £1.3m home with magical garden hiding 'many secret areas'

With a tree house, zip wire and walled hideaways - it's something pretty special

24 Nov 2019, 10:28 by Emilia Bona

A historic home has come on the market in Wirral - and it's got a breathtaking garden unlike anything you'll have seen before.

The five-bedroom home is located on Column Road in West Kirby , a quiet and leafy street lined with some of Merseyside's most expensive homes.

Up for sale with Market Street Homes estate agents, only offers in the region of £1,299,500 are being considered for this property.

The detached sandstone mansion, named St Michaels, is a 'one off' and dates back to the early 1800s.

Photos uploaded to Rightmove capture the enchanting gardens of the property, with plenty of secret areas and little gems tucked away.

Boasting ponds, a wild garden, a tree house and a zipwire, the property is an absolute oasis.

A cobble stone walkway leading up to the property is lined with wildflowers and bordered by an immaculately manicured lawn area.

Stunning plants and flowers fill another patio area, with the main garden all centred around a quirky water feature.

A drawbridge style path leads to an impressive tree house that looks like it was plucked straight out of a child's fantasy playground.

With its own balcony, raised up on stilts in one of the garden's trees, the tree house also has a separate set of wooden steps leading down to the shrubbery below.

Elsewhere in the garden, walled areas offer secret little hideaways, one of which has a circular stone seating area, while another suntrap has a bench and old waterpump.

The property also has two driveways - one from Column Road and the other from Priory Road, which is accessed via a set of electric gates.

Inside, the property is equally as impressive as it is from the outside, with a games room, cinema, gym and library.

The games room has a snooker table, darts board, football table and pinball machine, with exposed beams above offering a reminder of the home's historic status. 

Just off the games room, the library is any bookworm's dream, with floor to ceiling fitted shelves and a ladder for and hard to reach titles.

The incredible entertainment suite also has a table tennis table and a bar area, with a projector rigged up to one of the beams for movies.

Listing details uploaded to Rightmove say: "The rooms have amazing views across to Wales, exposed sandstone walls, stripped wood flooring and mood lighting, this space is amazing something you do not see every day."

The impressive property also has its own gym, with mirrored walls and exercise equipment.

A downstairs sitting room comes with a welcoming inglenook fireplace, with a further two lounges and a number of cellars - described by the agents as being 'perfect for wine'.
