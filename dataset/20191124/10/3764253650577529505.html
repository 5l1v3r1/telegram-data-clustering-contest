Romelu Lukaku says Man United career was over after Solskjaer put him in right-wing

24 Nov 2019, 10:30

- Romelu Lukaku says he knew his time at Man United was over after Solskjaer starting playing him in the right-wing

- Belgium's all-time top scorer says he told Solskjaer he was leaving after Man United’s 1-1 draw against Chelsea in the Premier League on April 28

- Lukaku has become the first Inter player to score 10 goals in the first 13 matchdays of a debut Serie A season since Stefano Nyers in 1948-49

Belgium’s all-time highest goal scorer Romelu Lukaku has revealed that Ole Gunnar Solskjaer's decision to play him in the right-wing forced him to leave Manchester United.

According to a report on Daily Mail, Lukaku told Solskjaer his time at Man United was over after Man United’s 1-1 draw against Chelsea in the Premier League on April 28.

Lukaku said: "It was after the game against West Ham at home, Solskjaer decided to put me on the right wing, very wide.
