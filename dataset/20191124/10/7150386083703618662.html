Coach Shastri Spells 'Winning Mantra' After Historic Win Over Bangladesh in Pink-Ball Test

The trio of Ishant (9/78), Umesh (8/82) and Shami, who picked up two wickets in the first innings, spitfire with the pink ball to demolish Bangladesh in little more than two days.

24 Nov 2019, 10:53

Gone were the days when the cricket experts and fans used to talk about India’s batting heavyweights who dominated the game right from the inception. The Indian batting unit always boasted off some of the biggest names ranging from Sunil Gavaskar to Sachin Tendulkar, Virender Sehwag to Virat Kohli. But the tide has changed and it is the bowlers who have overshadowed the batters by some margin. With the likes of Ishant Sharma, Mohammed Shami, Umesh Yadav, Jasprit Bumrah in the ranks – the Indian bowling unit has developed into a lethal combination and arguably the most feared one in the sport.

After completing Bangladesh’s annihilation by an innings and 46 runs in the maiden Pink-Ball Test at the Eden Gardens on Sunday, head coach Ravi Shastri credited the hard work of bowlers and appreciated them for hunting in a pack. On the back of a high-quality pace attack, India crushed Bangladesh in the historic Day-Night match to record a 12th straight Test series win on home soil.

The trio of Ishant Sharma (9/78), Umesh Yadav (8/82) and Mohammed Shami, who picked up two wickets in the first innings, spitfire with the pink ball to demolish Bangladesh in little more than two days.

“The discipline and the hunger to win (is there). They understand how important it is to support one another and bowl as a unit. That’s when you create pressure and that’s when things happen. They know they are arguably the best side in the world,” Shastri said after India’s comprehensive victory at Eden Gardens.
