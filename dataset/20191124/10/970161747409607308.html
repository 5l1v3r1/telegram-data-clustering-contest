Eddie Hearn aims dig at Deontay Wilder after knockout vs Luis Ortiz

24 Nov 2019, 10:48

Eddie Hearn aimed a dig at Deontay Wilder for his tepid call-out of Anthony Joshua after his win over Luis Ortiz.

The WBC champion successfully defended his title in Las Vegas on Saturday night, knocking Ortiz out in the seventh round.

Wilder said after his victory he would fight Tyson Fury next before pursuing a unification fight with the winner of Joshua’s rematch with Andy Ruiz Jr.

The American has repeatedly said he wants Ruiz Jr to win the rematch and Hearn teased Wilder for avoiding saying Joshua’s name after his latest victory.

He told IFL TV: ‘He struggled to say it, didn’t he? “I want the winner of ….Ruiz and the other guy”.

‘As refreshing as it is in a long time, what’s next after Andy Ruiz is totally irrelevant right now.

‘It’s just about trying to beat Andy Ruiz. I think Joshua against Wilder is the most thrilling fight in world boxing.

‘Both guys have been hurt before, both guys punch hard and both guys are fast. It’s all irrelevant unless Joshua can beat Andy Ruiz.’

Wilder and Joshua blamed each other for their unification fight falling through in 2018 before the British heavyweight lost his titles to Ruiz Jr last June.

The failure to agree terms with Joshua pushed Wilder towards Fury, but Hearn insists the Bronze Bomber is vulnerable.

‘That’s why the division is so exciting. You can have any fight going any way you want,’ he said.

‘[Wilder is] very, very limited as a fighter skill wise, but he has this freakish power that can turn your lights off at any time.

‘That’s why he’s so exciting, Deontay Wilder. He’s vulnerable, he can get beaten by anybody, but if he hits you on the chin, you’re bang in trouble.’
