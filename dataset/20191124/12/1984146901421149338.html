Bungling Durham and Northumberland prison staff released NINE inmates by mistake

The inmates were all wrongly set free between January 2015 and March 2019 but were later returned to jail

24 Nov 2019, 12:00 by Sonia Sharma

Bungling officials released nine prisoners from North East jails by mistake, new figures have shown.

The offenders were serving sentences at HMP Durham and HMP Northumberland but were wrongly set free between January 2015 and March 2019.

The figures, obtained through a Freedom of Information request, show that eight people were released from the Durham prison - three in 2015, three more in 2016 and two in the following year.

The inmates had been serving time for crimes including sexual offences, theft, possession of weapons, and violence.

One prisoner was released in error from HMP Northumberland, in 2017.

However, the MoJ was unable to provide information about the type of offence the inmate was serving a sentence for.

The MoJ runs HMP Durham, while Sodexo runs the Northumberland prison.

The MoJ's Prison Service says it thoroughly investigates every incident to see what lessons can be learnt to help prevent further such errors.

A spokesperson added: "Releases in error are very rare but we take them extremely seriously and work closely with police to bring offenders back into custody quickly."

According to the figures, there were no prisoners released by mistake at HMP Deerbolt, HMP Frankland and HMP Low Newton between 2015 and 2019.

HMP Durham, based in Elvet, County Durham, is a reception prison serving the courts in the Newcastle, Durham, Teesside and Cumbria area.

As of February 2017, it has been able to hold up to 996 prisoners.

The facility has seven wings as well as segregation cells and healthcare areas.
