Thousands queue online to buy Aldi's Kevin the Carrot toys - but many are disappointed as they soon sell out

Website users were greeted with the message that they had been automatically placed in a queue - but the toys had all sold out online by 11am

24 Nov 2019, 12:18 by Brett Gibbons, https://www.birminghammail.co.uk/authors/brett-gibbons/

Aldi's website was besieged on Sunday morning as thousands of shoppers queued to snap up Kevin the Carrot toys.

The discount supermarket chain launched sales of its range of merchandise depicting the fun figure made famous by its TV advertisements and there was soon a long line of customers waiting to purchase the toy.

But many were left disappointed with the toys soon selling out.

Website users were greeted with the message that they had been automatically placed in a queue due to the fact 'there are currently lots of people trying to access the Aldi website'.

The queue was around five minutes when our colleagues at StokeonTrentLive visited the site they were assigned queue number 224582 - with 2,321 other users ahead of them.

Unfortunately for people keen to get their hands on them, these had sold out by 11am.

A message on the queueing page stated: "Oops! It looks like we're all sold out of Kevin and friend's plush toys. Give your local Aldi store a try from Thursday 28th November to see what's on offer."

Aldi has branches across Birmingham and the Black Country and customers queued for hours to secure a bargain when a new store opened last week in Carters Green, West Bromwich.
