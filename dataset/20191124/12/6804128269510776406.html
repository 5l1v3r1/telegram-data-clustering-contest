Gary Lineker slams VAR after Raheem Sterling's disallowed goal for Man City against Chelsea

The Manchester City winger saw a goal chalked off

24 Nov 2019, 12:38 by Andy Ha

Gary Lineker has criticised VAR after another weekend of high profile decisions made in the Premier League.

Manchester City beat Chelsea 2-1 at the Etihad Stadium, but Pep Guardiola's side had a third goal ruled out for offside.

N'Golo Kante scored to give Frank Lampard's Blues the lead in the first half, but Kevin De Bruyne and Riyad Mahrez both netted to turn the game on its head.

Raheem Sterling thought he made the game safe for City when he poked home in injury time, but the effort was disallowed after VAR ruled that Sterling was offside when the ball was headed forward to him.

The ruling was by the narrowest of margins, with TV replays showing Sterling may actually have been level.

Sterling took to social media after the game, writing: "Happy to improve my VAR overruled goals record today again. Damn this thing gonna kill me bro #cursed."

Lineker also tweeted during the game to question the decision.
