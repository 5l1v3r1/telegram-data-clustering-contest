Arsenal: If Unai Emery can do better…

After Saturday’s deplorable draw with Southampton, Unai Emery said he ‘can do better’ as Arsenal head coach. Well, if he can do better, why doesn’t he?

24 Nov 2019, 18:00 by Andrew Dowdeswell

Upon the return of the international break, Arsenal hosted 19th-placed Southampton looking to right their recent wrongs. While fans wanted head coach Unai Emery sacked for the troubles of the season thus far, the club saw this fixture among other kind fixtures as a chance for Emery to inspire some confidence into his squad.

But sadly, what occurred on Saturday afternoon could not have been further from inspiring. The Gunners snatched a 2-2 draw with a last-gasp Alexandre Lacazette strike after they conceded 21 shots in 90 minutes, including five or six gilt-edged chances that were all squandered, especially in the second half.

If you needed any more proof that Emery is not the man to lead the team forward, this was it. A match that was meant to inspire confidence and a positive run of form could not have backfired more.

Naturally, Emery was asked after the draw, which felt very much more like a defeat, what he can do to turn the fortunes of the team around. His answer was not the most convincing:

“Now my job is to analyse, to try to move on with confidence with the players. It’s difficult but we need to move on in the next days. After analysing that match are going to play at home again on Thursday, prepare as well as possible. We know, and I know, that we need to connect with our supporters. The club are supporting me every day and also I have a responsibility. I know I can do better, I can take more performances with the players and I’m going to try to do that.”
