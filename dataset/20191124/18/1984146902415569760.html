'Quite rough with small gangs': The Newcastle street some people are ashamed to live on

Residents on Stanhope Street in Arthur's Hill have revealed what it is really like to live on the Newcastle street

24 Nov 2019, 18:00 by Rachael Nichol

Once an area where everyone knew their neighbours, residents on this Newcastle street say it has now lost its community spirit.

Riddled by crime and with streets full of litter, Stanhope Street in Arthur's Hill is a shadow of its former self.

Right in the heart of the community lies Hutchinson's Fruit Shop, a family run business that has been open since the 1970s.

One employee at the shop said he has lived on the street most of his life and has seen a big change in the area.

The 32-year-old said: "It was amazing growing up as I knew people around here for years.

"It has changed over the years and now it has become quite rough with small gangs."

Jo Young, who also works at the shop, was born and grew up on the street and is disappointed in how things have changed.

The 42-year-old said: "It’s a bit rough now, it's changed quite a lot.

"There’s a lot of litter down the back lanes, I have to fight my way to get down them."

Home to many traditional and multi-cultural businesses, one resident had happy memories going up on the street.

Joan Ellis, 72, said she had seen huge changes in the area since she moved to the street 45 years ago.

And she admitted that she was heartbroken at how the area had changed.

She said: "I'm actually quite embarrassed about living here. Whenever someone asks where I live I'm ashamed to say.

"When I first moved here it was a lovely place to live. It’s not the same as it used to be, now nobody knows their neighbours.

"People always seem to miss bin day so they end up leaving bin bags out on the street and on the weekend there’s litter everywhere. It’s awful."

However, Stanhope Halal Food Store owner Hamid Muhamad said he is proud of his street.

And he insists that there is still a great sense of community spirit in the area.

Moving to Newcastle from Afghanistan, he used to be a taxi driver before opening his store which has been running for five years.

The 36-year-old said: "I love this area, it is very busy everyone's smiling and are really friendly."

Taxi driver Ansar Quaraishi, who has lived in Arthur's Hill for 30 years, agreed.

The 36-year-old said: "It is still very traditional here but there are a lot more shops now and there is such a great environment.

"I never want to live anywhere else."
