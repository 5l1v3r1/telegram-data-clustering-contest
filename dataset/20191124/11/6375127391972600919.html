NCRPO on full alert for 30th Sea Games

24 Nov 2019, 11:21 by Alfred P. Dalizon

THE 17,000-strong National Capital Region Police Office goes on full alert today to ensure the 100 percent availability of all its officers and men during the 30th SEA Games which will start on Saturday.

NCRPO director Brigadier General Debold M. Sinas said he has cancelled all passes and leaves issued to NCRPO personnel as part of the full alert status. Around 8,000 NCRPO members will be performing SEA Games security duties while the rest will continue performing their regular law enforcement functions including conduct of checkpoints, visibility patrols and anti-narcotics operations and other anti-criminality missions.

He also called on the public to take advantage of the 24-hour SEA Games hotline where all information regarding peace and order condition and other concerns may be relayed as part of the police effort to ensure a trouble-free SEA Games.

The NCRPO SEA Games SMS and Viber hotline numbers are: 0915-983-4636 for Globe subscribers and 0998-325-5626 for Smart subscribers, the official said. According to Sinas, only SMS and viber messages can be sent to the hotlines and strictly no calls at all.

The NCRPO official assured the public of prompt response to all queries, reports and complaints that will be sent to the 30th SEA Games hotlines. He explained that the hotlines will be monitored 24/7 by trained and competent policemen assigned with the NCRPO Operations Division at Camp Bagong Diwa in Taguig City.
