Protests drive record poll turnout in Hong Kong

The vote is the closest Hong Kongers – who have been protesting for true democracy at mass rallies and violent clashes – get to direct representation.

24 Nov 2019, 11:45 by Yan Zhao / Xinqi Su / AFP

A protester (C) is helped away by a secondary principle (2nd R) after a press conference where two protesters spoke to the media on the campus of the Hong Kong Polytechnic University where they remain barricaded inside, in the Hung Hom district of Hong Kong on November 24, 2019. - Meanwhile, Hong Kong's voters turned out in record numbers on November 24 for local council elections that the city's pro-democracy movement hopes will add pressure on the Beijing-backed government to heed their demands. Hong Kong has been shaken by nearly six months of increasingly violent demonstrations, with protesters calling for greater democratic freedoms and police accountability. (Photo by NICOLAS ASFOURI / AFP)

Hong Kong’s voters turned out in record numbers Sunday for local council elections that the city’s pro-democracy movement hopes will pile pressure on the Beijing-backed government to heed their demands after months of violent protest.

Lengthy queues snaked out of polling stations across the territory in the election for 18 district councils, where high turnout is expected to benefit democratic forces.
