Bristol Rovers midfielder explains emotional ending to the win over Shrewsbury Town

"It's one of the hardest things I've had to do."

24 Nov 2019, 11:22 by David Byrom

Abu Ogogo says not celebrating his last-minute winner in Bristol Rovers' dramatic 4-3 win over Shrewsbury Town was one of the hardest things he has had to do in football.

For a man who has become a cult hero at Rovers thanks to how wildly he celebrates every single goal, it is a measure of the respect he has to former club Shrewsbury that he managed to restrain himself when the ball hit the back of the net in the 87th minute.

Ogogo spent three years at New Meadow, captaining them to the League One play-off final two seasons ago, and the midfielder admits it was difficult to keep his emotions in check when he scored.

"It's one of the hardest things I've had to do," he laughed.

"Normally when we score I love to celebrate and go wild, especially a last-minute winner like that.

"Any other game and I would have stripped off and jumped in with the fans!

"Obviously I couldn't today but it was really hard to keep my emotions in check."

The Gas were leading 3-1 before two goals in six minutes saw them pegged back to 3-3 in a feisty encounter in Shropshire.

Ironically, Ogogo's first thought at 3-3 was to make sure they managed to secure at least a point on their travels, but then Liam Sercombe teed him up for a curled finish from 12 yards late on.

He said: "When it got to 3-3, I said to make sure we didn't lose the game.

"A point wasn't the worst result so I said to make sure we didn't lose, but luckily we managed to go up the other end and score so I was buzzing with that.

"The distance between me and Sercs was a little too close so I knew I didn't have time.

"He set it perfectly and I knew I didn't have time to take a touch so I just used the defender's body and bent it round him."

He added: "I wanted to celebrate and go wild but obviously I've been here for three years so I didn't out of respect.

"It was so hard not to.

The victory moves Rovers to ninth in the table, just one point off the play-off places, and Ogogo can see similarities between the Gas this season and the Shrewsbury team he reached Wembley with two years ago.

He said: "It's similar to the season under Paul Hurst when we got to the play-off final and the EFL Trophy final.

"There's similar things happening this season to what was happening then.

"So hopefully we can do as well this season as we did then."
