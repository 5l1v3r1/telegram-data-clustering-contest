Aldi Kevin the Carrot fans outraged as toys sell out after huge website queue

Aldi shoppers told how they woke up early to pre-order Kevin the Carrot toys which sold out quickly while they were in a queue of 20,000 customers at one point

24 Nov 2019, 11:16 by Chris Kitching, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/chris-kitching/

Aldi shoppers were left disappointed as Kevin the Carrot plush toys sold out quickly while thousands were stuck in a massive online queue.

Customers told how they woke up in the early hours of Sunday morning for a chance to pre-order the collection of characters, but they were all gone within just a few hours.

A woman told Mirror Online: "I was in the queue from midnight till 6am and a majority of the plush toys were sold out and only a few mugs were left."

By 11.30am, almost 220,000 users had visited Aldi's website for a chance to purchase Kevin and Katie the Carrot, the Carrot Kids - Chantenay, Jasper and Baby - and their friends Russell Sprout and Tiny Tom.

Frustrated customers told how 20,000 people were ahead of them in the queue at one point.

One shopper told the Mirror he was given a 10 minute slot to buy the characters, but he fell asleep and missed his turn to purchase them at 3.30am.

It was a repeat of last year when the Kevin the Carrot toys sold out quickly.

A Twitter user wrote after they went on sale on Sunday: "Just waited 20 mins to get on Aldi website for Kevin the carrot and they are completely sold out #scandalous."

Another wrote: "I didn't wake up early enough, so went on the Aldi website at 9:30. I had to queue 15 minutes to view the site & then unsurprisingly the Kevin the Carrot stuff I wanted was sold out.

"I wonder what time I should have tried? I'll have to take my chances in store on Thursday."

And one tweeted: "Trying to get onto the Aldi website to find I’m in a queue of 10,000 people all because THEY want a Kevin the carrot. Mental. What’s happening to the world."

The characters cost £3.99 each and will go on sale in stores on Thursday.

Only character bundles were available during Sunday's online pre-order.

Aldi wrote to customers on Twitter: "Due to popular demand, Kevin and the rest of the range do tend to sell out quickly.

"But please don't worry! These characters will also be available to purchase individually in store on Thursday 28th November."

Just before 11am, the supermarket wrote on the page where customers were queuing: "Oops! It looks like we’re all sold out of Kevin and friend’s plush toys.

"Give your local Aldi store a try from Thursday 28th November to see what’s on offer."

A spokesman told Mirror Online: “We’re sorry that some shoppers were unable to get their hands on these toys online this morning, however, demand has been extremely high.

“The range will be stocked in-store from 28 November but, as with all of our Specialbuy products, these will also only be available while stocks last.”

Aldi is also selling giant Kevin and Katie the Carrot plush toys - standing a metre tall for £19,99 each - and other merchandise including books and tree decorations in the run-up to Christmas.
