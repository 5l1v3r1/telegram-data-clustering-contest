The ultimate guide to Christmas pantomimes in and around Bristol in 2019

From the Hippodrome to the Regrave, these are our top pick of pantos

24 Nov 2019, 11:00 by Danny Shaw

Christmas would still be Christmas without pantomimes… (oh no it wouldn’t).

The festive season is nearly upon us, bringing with it a jam-packed selection of pantomimes for to entertain people.

Though primarily aimed at children, Bristol’s selection of pantomimes promises to entertain all the family with their silly humour and sparkly sets.

From Beauty and the Beast to Jack and the Beanstalk, there really is something for everyone.

So don’t miss out, here's our roundup of pantomimes this year.

Have we missed one? Get in touch via bristolpostnews@localworld.co.uk

Bristol Hippodrome – Dick Whitington

Dates and Times: Saturday, December 7 to Sunday, January 5. Show times vary between 11am and 7pm.

Tickets: From £13 via atgtickets.com/Bristol.

The famous tale of how Dick outwits King Rat, gets married to Alice and ends up Mayor of London will be brought to life this Christmas by Qdos entertainment, the largest pantomime company in the world.

The panto will star soap-legend Shane Ritchie , best known for playing Alfie Moon in Eastenders.

The production will also use the same set and staging as used in the West End version of the show, produced at The London Palladium.

This will be refreshed with a brand-new script written exclusively for Shane Ritchie.

After last year’s exceptional production of Cinderella starring Gok Wan, this year’s will surely raise the bar even further.

Redgrave Theatre – Beauty and the Beast

Dates and Times: Saturday, December 21 to Tuesday, December 31. Show times vary between 11am and 6pm.

Tickets, from £15, are available here .

The Redgrave Theatre’s first pantomime promises to be packed full of festive excitement.

Theatre manager Sue Ellicott said: “It is great to be able to stage a pantomime for the first time this year, which should be a great addition to our family programme and lots of fun for children and adults alike!”

Produced by the award-winning Polka Dot Productions, the show will star Andy Brennan as Dolly Doughnut and Daniel Chambers as French Franc.

Twenty four local children will also be taking part, who will undoubtedly win over the audience and make this a distinctly youthful production.

Expect colourful scenery, extravagant outfits and even some panto pooches.

Weston Playhouse – Cinderella

Dates and Times: Saturday December 7 to Tuesday, December 31 Show times vary between 11 am and 7pm.

Tickets: From £21.95 at www.theplayhouse.co.uk

A classic on the pantomime scene, Cinderella comes to Weston-super-Mare this Christmas, bringing with it, returning favourite Linda Lusardi as the Fairy Godmother.

Linda’s husband, Sam Kane, best known for his work on Coronation Street and Emmerdale, is both directing the production, and featuring himself, as one of the Ugly Sisters.

If that’s not enough, Amy Thompson of Channel 5’s Milkshake will also star as Cinderella.

This rags-to-riches tale promises a large production, awesome song and dance numbers as well as real Shetland ponies.

Cadbury House – Jack and the Beanstalk

Dates and Times: 2pm on Friday, December 29.

Tickets: From £8 via this link .

Touring pantomime company Chaplins will bring an evening of family fun and festivity to Congresbury, in North Somerset, for one afternoon only.

Children and parents alike will be delighted by characters such as Jack, Mayor Botchett and Barmy Bosworth among others.

Mehmet Kandemir general manager at Cadbury House said: “The great thing about our Pantomime is that it is a lot more intimate than many shows you can see in the larger theatres meaning the kids can really get up close and personal with their favourite characters.

“The lighting and music is fantastic and it also works out much more affordable for a whole family.

“The show will be full of magical scenes, modern songs and spectacular costumes. It has been specially written to entertain children and will be put on by a professional cast and crew. With loads of interaction we expect lots of shouting, cheering and booing – and that’s just the parents!”
