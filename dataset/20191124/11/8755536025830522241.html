Anna Geary opens up about wedding diets and body image

"Something that's bad is stealing a car, not eating a cake."

24 Nov 2019, 11:29 by Sarah Magliocco

Dancing With The Stars runner-up Anna Geary has opened up about her relationship with food and body image.

The Irish sports star tied the knot last month, and revealed that she was constantly asked if she was losing weight for the big day.

She revealed: “People kept asking me before the wedding, ‘Are you on a diet?'”

“I was thinking then, ‘Should I be on one?’ “I’ve never met a calorie I didn’t like, I don’t have a sweet tooth, I have sweet teeth,” she told the Irish Sunday Mirror newspaper.

“I feel we should eliminate the guilt we associate with food, it’s having a detrimental effect.”

Anna says that social media has warped out perceptions of what a female body “should” look like.

“Body image has been distorted from social media, this unrealistic image of perfection,” she said.

“There’s too much of a focus on numbers now, putting massive pressure on people, it really upsets me when I hear people talking about good food and bad food and cheat food and sins.”

“Something that’s bad is stealing a car, not eating a cake.”

“We have such a temperamental relationship with food, I’m guilty of it myself, sometimes I go, ‘I shouldn’t have eaten that’ but then I think relax for a second.”

Anna explained that food has always been a big part of her life, fueling both her sporty lifestyle and her love of socializing.

“I love food, one of my favourite pastimes is eating and talking, I love pizza,” she said.

“But I think it’s about being realistic, deprivation is never the answer to anything.”

“I worry about younger people, the only reason I knew what a carb was because I learnt about it in home economics. Carb-free diets were foreign to me because I always played sport, carbohydrates to me are fuel, like a car needs petrol, how can you expect your body to work for you if you don’t nourish it with fuel?”
