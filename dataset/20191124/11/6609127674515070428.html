DRUG SNUB

Scots plastic surgeons set to battle it out in court over alleged cocaine comment

24 Nov 2019, 11:07 by Aidan Smith

TWO of Scotland's top plastic surgeons are set to battle it out in court over an alleged cocaine slur, according to reports.

The Daily Record claim Dr Darren ­McKeown has ignited legal action against fellow surgeon Dr Simon Ravichandran over an alleged remark made last year at a cosmetic event in London.

Ravichandran, who specialises in ear, nose and throat surgery, is claimed to have made a comment about McKeown taking cocaine.

The case is set to begin in Edinburgh's Court of Session on January 29.

Paperwork in relation to the case will state that McKeown's cosmetic business suffered as a result of the accusation.

Ravichandran denies saying the drug slur and his legal representatives will argue McKeown has been unable to prove financial loss.

They will also argue that the case should not be heard in Scotland due to the incident allegedly taking place in London.

McKeown strongly denies any involvement with drugs.

He has regularly appeared in interviews discussing cosmetic work celebs have had done.

It has also been claimed in the past that he paid staff with Louboutin shoes instead of money.

Both McKeown and Ravichandran have been approached for comment by The Scottish Sun.
