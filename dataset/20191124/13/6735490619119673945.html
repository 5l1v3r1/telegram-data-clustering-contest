‘Frozen II’ Freezes Out The Competition With A Record-Breaking $127M Opening

24 Nov 2019, 13:07 by Brent Furdyk

The reception for “Frozen II” has been far from chilly, with the long-awaited sequel to Disney’s animated mega-hit debuting with a $127-million domestic opening weekend.

As Variety reports, “Frozen II” set a new November record for an animation, becoming the third-biggest opening for an animated film ever, behind only “Incredibles 2” ($182 million) and “Finding Dory” ($135 million). In addition, “Frozen II” also brought Disney its biggest-ever opening for an animated film (not including Pixar released).

“Frozen II” also proved to be a blockbuster overseas, generating $223 million in foreign markets for a worldwide total of $350 million, lauded by Variety as “the largest day-and-date global animated debut in history.”

Another new arrival in theatres this weekend, the Tom Hanks-starring “A Beautiful Day in the Neighborhood”, captured third place with $13.5 million in North America, followed by “21 Bridges” at fourth place with a $9.2-million domestic opening.

“Ford v. Ferrari” landed in second place, adding $16 million for a two-week total of $57.9 million.
