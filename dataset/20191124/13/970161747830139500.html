Michael van Gerwen threatens to 'do a Ronnie O'Sullivan' and turn down 9-darter

24 Nov 2019, 13:12

Michael van Gerwen says he could channel Ronnie O’Sullivan and turn down a 9-darter as there is no prize for hitting the perfect leg in the PDC.

The world number one hit the first televised 9-darter of the year on Saturday at the Players Championship Finals, on his way to a second round win over Adrian Lewis.

However, he claimed to be not fussed about the feat, and even suggested he could purposefully turn down a 9-darter in future if there is no prize money.

Five-time world snooker champion O’Sullivan famously turned down the chance for a 147 break at the 2016 Welsh Open because the £10,000 prize money for a maximum was ‘too cheap’ and MVG certainly thinks no prize at all is scandalous.

‘I don’t really care, to be fair, no prize money or nothing, I should go for the single eight, just like Ronnie O’Sullivan,’ said Van Gerwen on his 9-dart achievement.
