Off-duty NYPD sergeant slashed after fender-bender in Astoria

24 Nov 2019, 13:41 by Tina Moore and Craig McCarthy

An off-duty NYPD sergeant was slashed multiple times and robbed after a fender-bender in Astoria Sunday morning and his attacker is on the loose, cops said.

The collision happened just before 5 a.m. near Steinway Street and Northern Boulevard, according to police.

Following the crash, a man got out of his car and attacked the cop — identified by police sources as Terrance Howard, an anti-crime sergeant in the 69 — and stole his jewelry, police sources told The Post.

Howard suffered cuts to his face and back and was taken to Cornell Hospital in Manhattan for minor injuries, sources said.

The attacker and another unknown woman took off after the encounter, according to police.

Nassau County Police caught up the attacker’s car near Roosevelt, Long Island, where the man took off on foot, escaping arrest, according to the sources.
