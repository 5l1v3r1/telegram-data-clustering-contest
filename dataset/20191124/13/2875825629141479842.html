Deontay Wilder compares himself to Mike Tyson after Luis Ortiz KO

The heavyweight world champion knocked out the Cuban in the seventh round on Saturday night having lost each of the previous six

24 Nov 2019, 13:42 by Irishmirror.ie

Deontay Wilder believes he hits harder than Mike Tyson following his stunning one-punch knockout of Luis Ortiz.

The heavyweight world champion knocked out the Cuban in the seventh round having lost each of the previous six.

Ortiz was able to rise from the canvas but could not beat the referee's count and the fight was waved off.

Tyson is widely considered to be the biggest puncher in the history of the division, but Wilder claimed he has overtaken his predecessor.

"At this point in time you've got to give me my credit," he said.

"It's sad that it took me over 40 fights to get the recognition I truly deserve.

"When people see me they've never seen my style and I know it took a while for them to get used to what I display and the talents I possess.

"What I do is not textbook, you can't really reach it and that's what makes me unique and different to other fighters.

"At this point in time I've earned my due respect and credit. I am the hardest-hitting puncher in boxing history, period, and I've earned that.

"Consistently I do what I do time and time again, give people great fights and great knockouts."

Wilder will now turn his attention to Tyson Fury whom he will rematch in Las Vegas on February 22.

And he insists the quick turnaround will help rather than hinder his preparations.

"Every time a person fights we see different holes, you're not going to be perfect in a fight," he added.

"I hope he took notes and takes them back to camp because I'm going to knock Fury out like I did the first time.

"I'm proven; if these guys are so sure about certain things, he would have taken the rematch immediately. I demanded it as soon as possible, especially when it was a controversial decision.

"Come February I hope they're ready because I'm ready. this will be a quick turnaround for me and I haven't don't this since the beginning of my career.

"It will only make me better; I wish those guys good luck and I hope [trainer] Ben [Davison] took a lot of notes because I'm ready."
