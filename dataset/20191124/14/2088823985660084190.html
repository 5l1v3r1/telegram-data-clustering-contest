Sabres game day: Zach Bogosian could return against Panthers

24 Nov 2019, 14:31

Matchup: Buffalo Sabres (10-9-3) vs. Florida Panthers (12-6-5)

Where: BB&T Center, Sunrise, Fla.

When: 5 p.m.

TV: MSG.

Radio: WGR 550.

Crowded blue line: Sabres coach Ralph Krueger used practice Saturday to "play with some looks" on the blue line. Rasmus Dahlin was reunited with Zach Bogosian, who could make his season debut against the Panthers. Colin Miller was also playing his off-side on a pairing with Brandon Montour.

Jake McCabe remained with Rasmus Ristolainen and the same goes for Marco Scandella with Henri Jokiharju. It remains unclear whom will be scratched now that John Gilmour has joined the Rochester Americans.

Krueger told the media following practice Saturday in Coral Springs., Fla., that Bogosian is expected to play either Sunday against Florida or Monday in Tampa Bay. That decision won't be made until Sunday morning. Is Krueger willing to change the defense pairs after the Sabres played well during a loss Thursday night in Boston?

While the fan base views the crowded blue line as a problem, Krueger and General Manager Jason Botterill have maintained their opinion that this is a necessary asset. Neither said he is concerned about what sort of effect this will have on team chemistry, either.

"It’s part of being a team," Krueger said. "You’re not always going to like the role you have, the ice time you get or if you’re then scratched, but how do you react to that and how do you still support your teammates? So far we’ve seen nothing but support from players that haven’t been called up to play in a game and are watching from the outside. They’re the first guys in the room to greet the guys coming off the ice at the end of the game."

Potential lineup: Though the defense pairs could change, Krueger appears ready to use the same forward lines against the Panthers. Center Marcus Johansson remains away from the team as he recovers from an upper-body injury that has forced him to miss five games.

That has tested the Sabres' depth down the middle, however, Rasmus Asplund has performed well since being recalled from Rochester. Though Buffalo has lost nine of its last 10 games, Krueger is thrilled with how they performed Thursday in Boston.
