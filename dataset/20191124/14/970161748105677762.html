Man Utd and PSG planning huge January bids for Roma midfielder Nicolo Zaniolo

24 Nov 2019, 14:16

Manchester United are considering a January move for Roma and Italy midfielder Nicolo Zaniolo, but will face stiff competition from Paris Saint-Germain if they decide to make a move.

The 20-year-old has fast become one of the most sought-after talents in Europe after becoming a fixture in the Roma team and bursting onto the international scene as well.

The youngster made his Italy debut in March and scored his first two goals for his country in the 9-1 battering of Armenia last week.

Previously linked with Tottenham, Zaniolo has regularly been talked up as a target for Premier League clubs in recent months, with Manchester United now leading the chasing pack.

However, the Express report that PSG are also keen on the Italian, which means that he will become very expensive, very quickly.

If United or PSG are going to prise the midfielder away from the Italian capital, they will have to come up with at least £50m.

The club are under no pressure to sell as he only signed a new deal at Roma, keeping him there until 2024, however, they could still be tempted to sell with a big offer on the table.

The Red Devils may have an advantage over PSG in discussions, though, as they plan to involve Zaniolo in a deal to make Chris Smalling’s loan to Roma permanent.

The England international is currently on a short-term deal with Roma but has impressed and the club are thought to be interested in extending his stay.

Zaniolo has been made aware of the interest from England, but has only spoken of his desire to continue his career at Roma.

‘Interest from England? It would be easy to say ‘Yes, why not’, but it wouldn’t be me,’ said the Italy international.

‘I tell you the truth, I recently kissed the Roma shirt and I want to kiss it anytime I score a goal.

‘I’m very happy, Roma have given me so much confidence, now I’ll repay it on the pitch. I’m proud to represent these fans, I feel very good here.’
