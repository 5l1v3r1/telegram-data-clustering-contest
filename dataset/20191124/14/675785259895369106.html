BREAKING Boris Johnson makes humilating climbdown on nurses bursaries in Tory manifesto

The Tory leader announced the party's policy to scrap nurses bursaries would be torn up in a huge u-turn. The party's general election manifesto promises to recruit 50,000 new students

24 Nov 2019, 14:36 by Mikey Smith, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/mikey-smith/

Boris Johnson has reinstated the state bursary for student nurses in a humiliating climbdown on Tory policy.

The Conservative government scrapped the vital support for trainees just three years ago.

But already the policy has been branded a disaster, with nurse numbers applying to begin training dropping by 16,580 from March 2016 to September 2018.

The fall in mature student numbers was even more stark, with a total decline of 40% from June 2016.

Launching the Tory manifesto in Telford, Mr Johnson pledged to recruit 50,000 extra nurses - and to give them a £5,000 to £8,000 a year maintenance grant.

The manifesto promises: "And they won't have to pay it back.

"Everyone will receive at least £5,000 with further funding in regions or disciplines that are struggling to recruit - such as mental health - and help with their childcare costs."

In his 2015 spending review, George Osborne unveiled “disastrous” plans to scrap £6,000-a-year grants for student nurses and midwives.

It was supposedly done to allow more training places to open up.

But hours after the policy was announced, unions warned us it could prompt a recruitment crisis.

And figures later showed a 23% drop in applications, according to former Labour health chief Andy Burnham.

Tory peers killed off a bid to block the change in their final act before the 2017 general election .
