Woman dies in crash, man airlifted to Derriford Hospital with life-threatening injuries

Police have released a statement

24 Nov 2019, 14:54 by Chris Matthews, Rom Preston-Ellis

An elderly woman has died after a crash in Cornwall which has seen an elderly man airlifted to Derriford Hospital with serious injuries.

It was reported earlier that two elderly occupants were badly injured after the car crashed at the Heamoor Roundabout near Penzance and crashed into a post.

Devon and Cornwall Police has now confirmed that the elderly lady has died.

The man, who was airlifted to Derriford Hospital, continues to be treated for life-threatening injuries, reports Cornwall Live.

A Devon and Cornwall Police spokesman said earlier: “We were called to an RTC on the (Heamoor) Roundabout.

“It’s a serious crash involving two elderly occupants.

“A car has gone the wrong way around the roundabout and hit a post. There was only one car involved.”

An investigation into the circumstances surrounding the accident is underway and emergency services have remained at the scene throughout the afternoon.

Both the man and woman are believed to be local.

A Coastguard helicopter was seen landing at Derriford Hospital just before 2pm.

The incident took place near to the John Daniel Centre and there were around a dozen emergency service vehicles on the scene including police, fire and ambulance.

The Cornwall Air Ambulance and Coastguard helicopter landed nearby at Penwith College.

One witness described the crash as serious and said that the car ended up down an embankment.
