Mum charged with murder after daughters, aged 1 and 2, die in hot car on 31C day

Police are investigating whether Kerri-Ann Conley was under the influence of drugs while her daughters Darcey and Chloe-Ann were kept in her Mazda

24 Nov 2019, 14:25 by Anna Ye

A mum has been charged with murder after her two baby daughters died after being left in a hot car.

Two-year-old Darcey Conley and one-year-old sister Chloe-Ann died after spending hours in their mother Kerri-Ann Conley's car on a 31C day.

It is alleged Conley had fallen asleep inside the home and left her children trapped in the Mazda outside her home in Logan City, south of Brisbane, according to 9news.

When she realised her daughters were still in the car, the 27-year-old mum was said to have tried to cool them down with a cold bath, it is claimed.

Emergency services were called at about 1.30pm, but the babies died from heat stress.

Conley, 27, was charged just before 11pm on Saturday and is due to appear in Brisbane Magistrates Court on Monday.

Police are investigating whether she was under the influence of drugs when her daughters died.

Darcey's father, who wanted to remain anonymous, said Conley adored her girls and the girls were inseparable.

He told The Courier Mail: "They giggled all the time, the pair of them. Although they were 12 months apart, Chloe wasn't really that far behind Darcey. I think she learnt a lot off Darcey, mannerisms."

He said he thought of himself as a "father figure" for Chloe as well.

"Chloe, every time you looked at her, she just had a smile from ear to ear.Â 

"They were just two peas in a pod. They would have been inseparable when they got older. Unfortunately we don't get to see them grow into beautiful young people."

Experts say the temperature inside a parked car on a warm day can be more than 30C hotter than that outside the car.

A family friend who often babysat the sisters told 9News she is "heartbroken and devastated" and hopes "justice will be served for them little girls".

Neighbours have left flower bouquets and stuffed toys outside the family's home on Logan Reserve Road in Waterford West.

Queensland Police Detective Inspector Mark White said: "Paramedics made attempts to revive those children, unfortunately they were both declared deceased at the scene.

"The two children showed evidence of being exposed to extreme heat.

"It's tragic and we are putting a lot of emphasis on supporting our staff, because they do a very difficult job."
