Bellerin, Torreira - Latest Arsenal injury news and return dates ahead of Eintracht Frankfurt

The latest injury news as Arsenal take on Southampton after the international break

24 Nov 2019, 07:42 by Andy Ha

Arsenal drew 2-2 with struggling Southampton at the Emirates Stadium on Saturday to heap further misery on Unai Emery.

The result leaves the Gunners seventh in the table, 12 points adrift of the top four.

The poor form has caused unrest among supporters, with many calling for a managerial change to be made at the club, ahead of the trip to Norwich next weekend.

Take a look at the latest injury news from the Emirates below:

Dani Ceballos

The midfielder was substituted early in the second half of the Europa League clash against Vitoria before the international break with an injury problem.

Ceballos pulled up with what looked like a hamstring problem while closing down an opponent and was replaced by Matteo Guendouzi in the 54th minute, despite appearing to want to continue.

The Spanish international missed both the trip to Leicester and his country's games over the international break, and Arsenal have revealed that the midfielder could be out of action for a bit longer yet.

An update claimed that Ceballos will "return to training in mid-December", meaning that the midfielder could miss as many as the five next Arsenal matches.

Joe Willock

The young midfielder has already made 15 appearances for the Gunners this season.

And his form has earned England recognition in the form of the Under-21s as well.

Willock was in action during the international break, starting the 3-0 win over Albania last week.

He was substituted after 64 minutes though, with his Arsenal team-mate Reiss Nelson coming on and scoring the third in a 3-0 win.

There was no indication that the substitution was injury related, although the 20-year-old was not picked to start the follow-up clash with the Netherlands on Tuesday night.

His return to fitness was rubber stamped when he was named in the squad for the Southampton clash and came off the bench.

Kieran Tierney

Kieran Tierney was not called up for international duty with Scotland, for whom he would have got some playing time given Andy Robertson's injury.

Steve Clarke left him out of the squad for the Euro 2020 qualifiers against Cyprus and Kazakhstan, but that is not because Tierney has suffered a setback in his recovery from injury.

Instead, Arsenal requested that he did not feature as part of a pre-planned recovery programme to get him back up to speed quicker.

And Tierney was named in the starting line-up against Southampton.
