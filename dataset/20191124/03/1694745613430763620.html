21 Trader Joe's Products That Make Dinner Especially Easy

24 Nov 2019, 03:05 by Sara Cagle

Adaptable is the name of the game with these multiuse, sent-from-the-heavens foods, all available year-round at your nearest Trader Joe's. They could be cooked millions of ways, but here are a few dozen easy ideas to make the dinner-planning part of your life just a bit easier. However you decide to use these convenient Trader Joe's staples, you'll want to add them to your shopping cart for years to come.

Prices and products may vary by Trader Joe's location.

Trader Joe's Organic Broccoli Slaw ($2)

This crunchy and colorful veggie blend is easy to throw in a salad or on a sandwich, but it also makes a great base for a more substantial dish. Enjoy the mix cold in a healthy coleslaw or hot in a spicy chicken burger.

Trader Joe's Organic Brown Rice ($4)

These practical packs of cooked brown rice are ready after just three minutes in the microwave, which means that making rice bowls can, in fact, be even easier. Take a whack at whipping up this chicken broccoli stir fry — or perhaps this brown rice risotto with mushrooms and fresh oregano.

Trader Joe's Turkey Burgers ($3)

These flavorful, protein-packed patties give you the juicy taste of a day of grilling — minus the sweating over the grill. Lay them on a bun or lettuce wrap for a dinner fit for a queen. These tomato basil bruschetta, classic, and Aloha turkey burgers all sound like pretty great ideas.

Trader Joe's Healthy 8 Veggie Mix ($3)

This colorfully crunchy mix of carrots, cabbage, celery, broccoli, and more is basically a meal starter pack. Whatever fate you choose for this handy-dandy product — whether it be a crunchy chicken salad, spring rolls, or a veggie rice and beans — the hardest part will be opening the container.

Trader Joe's Corn and Chile Tomato-Less Salsa ($3)

This sweet salsa's destiny extends beyond tortilla chips, as it marries perfectly with seafood in these fiery fish tacos (just replace the salsa in this recipe with the TJ's stuff!) and with avocado and black beans in this corn salsa salad.

Trader Joe's Cauliflower Pizza Crust ($4)

While rejoicing in the fact that you're saving light years of time by not having to make your own cauliflower crust, rejoice more in your endless topping options for this lifesaving new product. Stick to basics with this margherita pizza recipe, get fancy with this BBQ chicken pizza, or change the game by cutting the crust into cheesy garlic breadsticks.

Trader Joe's Mandarin Orange Chicken ($5)

Jazz up this fan favorite Mandarin chicken by adding it to a veggie-packed stir-fry or serving it over TJ's brown rice or egg noodles.

Trader Joe's Carrot Spirals ($3)

Trader Joe's does all the spiralizing work for you with these cute and curly carrot spirals. Try them sautéed in olive oil as a quick side dish, or treat them as noodles with this recipe for carrot fettuccine.

Trader Joe's Organic Riced Cauliflower ($2)

Seasoning options are infinite with these tiny cauliflower morsels. Turn them into fried "rice," fritter 'em up, or transform them into a Tex-Mex casserole.

Trader Joe's Fully Cooked Pork Belly ($6)

Trader Joe's pork belly is the new bacon. It's not hard to see why, considering its ability to make pappardelle with wine sauce, tacos, and Banh Mi Sandwiches even tastier with its rich, salty deliciousness.

Trader Joe's Masala Simmer Sauce ($2)

This simmer sauce is ideal for when you are craving Indian takeout but just can't find the will to leave the house. Add chicken and rice for a quick and easy tikka masala or make these delightful three-ingredient mini masala meatballs.

Trader Joe's Shaved Brussels Sprouts ($3)

There's no need to shave your own brussels sprouts when Trader Joe's kindly takes care of it for you. Just tear open the bag and add the sprouts to four cheese ravioli or this warm slaw with Asian citrus.

Trader Joe's Gyro Slices ($4)

A brief Greek getaway is just a drive away with these delectable slices of Greek-style lamb. Go Greek with a gyros on homemade pita or make this tzatziki sauce just once, and you'll be obsessively buying these again and again.

Trader Joe's Fresh Goat Cheese Medallions ($4)

Lunch or dinner for one is made effortlessly efficient with these tangy, creamy, single-serving wheels of joy. Use these medallions in everything from your morning scrambled eggs to your midday blueberry and goat cheese crostini.

Trader Joe's Wild Rice and Mushroom Sausage-Less Sausage ($3)

Wild rice and mushrooms combine to make this vegetarian option hearty, wholesome, and perfect in place of meat sausages in plenty of recipes — like this kale and white bean soup or a creamy sausage and sweet potato pasta.

Trader Joe's Chicken Gyoza Potstickers ($4)

Serve these bad boys with rice and kimchi, or turn them into a potsticker soup, and no one will know you got them from the freezer aisle.

Trader Joe's Green Jackfruit in Brine ($2)

This nutrient-rich miracle fruit doubles as a convincing meat alternative for carnivores and vegetarians alike. Allow the fleshy fruit to soak up some savory flavors in these tinga tacos or BBQ jackfruit pizza.

Trader Joe's Penne Arrabbiata ($3)

This spicy Italian classic is conveniently packaged and ready for additions like spinach, kale, chicken, or sausage for a complete meal.

Trader Joe's Cut Butternut Squash ($2)

Ditch the cutting board and go straight for the pan with these precut pieces of butternut squash, ready for a tasty squash stuffing, pasta casserole, or brie pizza. For a satisfying side to any main course, break out the olive oil for a foolproof roasted butternut squash.

Trader Joe's Steamed Lentils ($3)

Treating your body as a temple is easy with these ready-to-eat bites of healthiness. Put together a quick lentil salad or satisfy your sweet tooth with lentil carrot bars.

Trader Joe's Organic Tahini ($3)

Pick up this bargain jar of ground sesame seeds, and prepare a money-saving batch of homemade hummus.
