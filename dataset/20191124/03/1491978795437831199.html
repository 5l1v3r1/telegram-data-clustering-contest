The brutal history of Bougainville – in pictures

24 Nov 2019, 03:55

A look back at the archipelago’s history, from German colony to civil war, and from reconciliation to independence referendum

From 1885 to 1918 Bougainville was part of the German colony New Guinea. Big chief Serais is pictured here with wife and children in main village Kieta, c1900.

Photograph: ullstein bild Dtl./ullstein bild via Getty Images

This 19th century photograph shows an initiation. Four or five youths were chosen and taken into a hut to do various forms of labour, learn the tribal rules and had to always wear these traditional hats. When their hair filled the hat the period of seclusion was over.

Photograph: Universal History Archive/Universal Images Group via Getty Images

Father John Momis, now president of the autonomous region of Bougainville, preaching during a mass in 1980.
