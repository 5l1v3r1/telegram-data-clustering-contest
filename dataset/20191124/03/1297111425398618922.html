Regina Pats erupt for blowout win over Swift Current Broncos

Robbie Holmes scored three goals and Max Paddock stopped 25 shots, leading the Regina Pats to a 6-0 win over the Swift Current Broncos.

24 Nov 2019, 03:22 by Regina Leader-Post

This time, the Regina Pats left no doubt.

The WHL club enjoyed a long-awaited breakthrough on Friday night, pummeling the Swift Current Broncos 6-0 before 2,036 fans at the Innovation Credit Union iPlex.

The victory came just three days after Swift Current escaped the Brandt Centre in Regina with a 2-1 win despite being outshot 49-28. Broncos goaltender Isaac Poulter was the first star in that contest, but he didn’t fare as well in Friday’s rematch.

With the win, the Pats (4-14-2-0) moved a step closer to climbing out of the WHL basement. They’re two points behind the Prince George Cougars (5-16-0-2) with three games in hand and three points back of the Broncos (5-13-1-2) with a game in hand.

Robbie Holmes led the Pats’ offence with his first career hat trick and netminder Max Paddock stopped 25 shots for his first shutout of the season.

The visitors finished with a season-high 55 shots on goal.

Riley Krane opened the scoring midway through the first period before Holmes scored back-to-back goals 1:54 apart in the last five minutes of the frame.

Nikita Sedov made it 4-0 just 2:17 into the second and Holmes completed his hat trick with 1:43 left on the clock. Zack Smith rounded out the scoring with 3:35 remaining in the third.

It was also a breakout night for Regina’s struggling power-play unit, which finished 3-for-6. Swift Current went 0-for-6.

Carson Denomie — acquired from Moose Jaw on Monday — picked up his first points as a member of the Pats with two assists. Tyson Feist, Drew Englot and Zach Wytinck also had two helpers apiece.

It was the first of seven straight road games for the Pats. They conclude a stretch of four games in five nights on Saturday versus the Brandon Wheat Kings.
