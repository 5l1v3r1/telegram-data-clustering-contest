Nigerian Newspapers: Top 10 Headlines Today Sunday, Nov. 24th, 2019

24 Nov 2019, 03:38 by Donatus Anichukwueze

Good day, and welcome to the Concise News roundup of the top 10 Nigerian newspapers headlines for today Sunday, November 24th, 2019.

1. What Jonathan Said About Lecturers Rigging Elections

Former President Goodluck Jonathan has advised university lecturers to protect the integrity of the nation’s democracy by ignoring the entreaties and pressure from politicians to rig elections in their favour. Jonathan said this in an address he delivered at the 10th-anniversary celebration of Godfrey Okoye University, Enugu on Saturday.

2. US Court Orders Arrest Of Air Peace’s Allen Onyema

A court in the United States has issued a warrant of arrest against the Chairman of Air Peace Airlines, Allen Onyema. A copy of the warrant published by Premium Times showed that the warrant was issued on November 20.

3. Nigerians React To Fraud Allegations Against Air Peace’s Onyema

Several reactions have trailed the allegations of bank and money laundering against the owner of Air Peace Airline, Allen Onyema, by the United States Justice Department. Concise News reports that the US government announced the allegations against Onyema on Friday, accusing him of “moving more than $20 million from Nigeria through United States bank accounts in a scheme involving false documents based on the purchase of airplanes.”

4. Biafra: War Bomb Found In Anambra (Photo/Video)

An undischarged Biafra civil war bomb has been found by some workers at a construction site in Adabebe village, in Amawbia, Awka South Local Government Area of Anambra State. The bomb was handed over to the Explosives Ordinance Disposal (EOD) unit of the State Police Command, who dismantled it.

5. Air Peace Owner Onyema Reacts To US $20m Fraud Allegations Against Him

Allen Onyema, the owner of Nigerian airline, Air Peace, has denied allegations of bank and money laundering leveled against him by the United State Justice Department. Concise News reports that the US government announced the allegations against Onyema on Friday, accusing him of “moving more than $20 million from Nigeria through United States bank accounts in a scheme involving false documents based on the purchase of airplanes.”

6. Ojukwu’s ADC Reveals What Ndigbo Must Do To Produce Next Nigeria President In 2023

Aide-de-camp to Dim Chukwuemeka Odumegwu Ojukwu during the civil war and head of Rock Family Church, Bishop Obi Udezue Onubogu has identified “dialogue” as the best possible means for Ndigbo to produce president in 2023. Concise News reports that in a recent interview with Sun News Online, Onubogu stressed that “Ndigbo cannot do it alone”.

7. UNN Cancels Venue For Witchcraft Conference

The University of Nigeria (UNN) Nsukka has withdrawn its approval on the venue for a witchcraft conference scheduled to hold in the university premises. Concise News reports that UNN earlier granted approval for the use of its Energy Centre for the event which has been generating mixed reactions.

8. Xenophobia: UNGA President Speaks On Discrimination In Africa

Prof. Tijjani Muhammad-Bande, the President of the UN General Assembly (UNGA) says no African country is silent on racism and xenophobia, as all nations on the continent condemned such actions. Concise News reports that he said this in a special interview with NAN in Abuja, during his first official visit to the country as President of the General Assembly.

9. Zamfara: Gov. Matawalle Threatens Arrest Yari

The Governor of Zamfara State Bello Matawalle has accused his predecessor, Abdulaziz Yari, of inciting violence in the state. Concise News understood the governor made the accusation at government house, Gusau, while receiving some firearms that some “repentant bandits” surrendered.

10. EPL: Man City Come From Behind To Beat Chelsea 2-1

Manchester City moved backed to third in the Premier League table on Saturday after coming from behind to claim a valuable 2-1 win at home to Chelsea. Although the victory was marred by injury concerns for Rodri, David Silva and Sergio Aguero.

That’s the roundup of the top 10 Nigerian newspapers headlines this morning. More Nigerian newspapers headlines are available on Concise News website.
