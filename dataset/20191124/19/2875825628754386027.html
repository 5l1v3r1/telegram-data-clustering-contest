Liverpool's Trent Alexander-Arnold fires warning to Premier League title rivals

The Reds are eight points clear at the top of the table and still unbeaten after 13 games following their late winner against Crystal Palace on Saturday

24 Nov 2019, 19:33 by Irishmirror.ie

Trent Alexander-Arnold has warned Liverpool's Premier League title rivals that nothing can stop the Reds this season.

Liverpool remain unbeaten and sit top of the table after 13 matches following Saturday's 2-1 win away at Crystal Palace .

Jurgen Klopp's side looked like they may drop points for only the second time this season when Wilfried Zaha fired in an equaliser with less than 10 minutes remaining.

But they immediately restored their lead just minutes later when Roberto Firmino stabbed home the winner from inside the box.

And Alexander-Arnold believes their constant knack of grinding out results has been the key to their impressive start.

Speaking to the club's official website , he said: "We have been showing that throughout the course of the season and even last season we showed how much of a good team spirit we have got and resilience.

“We had a setback with their goal and we bounced back pretty much straight away. We showed our quality and went up the other end and scored.

“That’s what good teams do, find a way to win, and that’s what we have done.”

Liverpool have scored a number of late goals this season to seal crucial wins in the title race.

Two goals in the last seven minutes helped them come from behind to beat Aston Villa three weeks ago, while a James Milner penalty in injury time secured all three points against Leicester .

Adam Lallana scored an 85th-minute equaliser against Man Utd, and Divock Origi's last gasp goal sent the Carabao Cup clash with Arsenal to penalties.

Speaking after the Palace win, boss Klopp said: "Today I have no problem that we were not brilliant because in a game like this you just have to make sure you're ready to fight for the result and we were that from the first minute.

"Could we have played better? Yes, but we had players on the pitch that were on Thursday morning still on a plane back from Abu Dhabi, where Brazil played. That's not too cool.

"For that, getting a result at Crystal Palace , you never take for granted. So, it feels good.

"I don't know what people will say but I know 37 points after 13 games is exceptional and you cannot have them with winning only brilliant games.

"We had a couple of really brilliant games but you can't only win them. We are not out there to send messages, we just want to get results."

Liverpool are next in Premier League action against Brighton on November 30, before hosting Everton in the season's first Merseyside derby a few days later.
