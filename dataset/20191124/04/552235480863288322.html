Delivery firm DPD orders contractors to stop fining drivers for calling in sick

Midlands-based DPD group insisted it was unaware DPD Local was fining workers.

24 Nov 2019, 04:30 by Gordon Blackstock

Parcel giant DPD has ordered contractors to stop fining drivers for ­calling in sick.

It comes after we highlighted the case of driver Mark Barnes, 34, who was given £150 penalties for missing deliveries when he rushed to his wife’s hospital bed after she had emergency surgery.

DPD had promised to end the practice after one of its drivers, Don Lane, died in 2018.

Don, 53, missed numerous hospital appointments for fear of being hit with the charge.

Ken Loach’s latest film, Sorry We Missed You, tells the stories of delivery drivers like Don to shine a light on the gig economy.

Last week, we told how dad-of-four Mark was issued with six £150 penalties by DPD’s sister courier ­organisation, DPD Local, in four years – the latest coming just before he quit in October.

DPD and DPD Local have similar logos, branding and have the same chief executive, Dwain McDonald, who earns about £1million a year.

Midlands-based DPD group insisted it was unaware DPD Local was fining workers.

A spokesman said: “DPD ­abolished fines completely for its self-employed drivers in February 2018. Mark Barnes was not employed or engaged by DPD.

"He was a self-employed driver who worked for a franchisee of DPD Local, a separate business to DPD.

"The DPD Local franchisees remain independent of DPD – they run their own businesses.

"As soon as it was brought to our attention, DPD wrote to all its suppliers to reinforce again that no driver should be penalised in this way.”
