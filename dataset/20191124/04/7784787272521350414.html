Whincup takes step towards Newcastle 500 redemption with dominant Shootout pole

24 Nov 2019, 04:05

Jamie Whincup has taken one step towards Newcastle redemption as he claimed pole position for the final race of the 2019 season.

The seven-time champion was at his imperious best in the Top 10 Shootout to put over two tenths over next-best Cameron Waters, who qualified fastest earlier in the day.

Whincup was the ninth man out in the dash for pole, and dropped in a stunning 1:10.5551s flyer to put distance between himself and his challengers.

It was the third pole of the season for Whincup, and the 83rd of his decorated career.

Launching from P1 will give Whincup confidence of sorts following his late-race meltdown on Saturday, which cost the Red Bull Holden Racing Team crucial points in the battle for the teams’ championship.

Whincup ran second behind teammate and eventual winner Shane van Gisbergen before his critical error with 20 laps remaining, with the 36-year-old plummeting to eighth by race’s end.

On Sunday, it was van Gisbergen’s time to strike drama as he missed the Shootout, before copping a grid penalty for impeding Will Davison. The 2016 champion will start 21st for the season finale, a stark contrast to his brilliant pole a day earlier.

Shell V-Power Racing holds the cards in the teams’ title fight, enjoying a 143-point lead over the factory Holden squad heading into Sunday’s action. Fabian Coulthard – who reached the Saturday podium and broke a four-month drought – managed to defeat champion-elect Scott McLaughlin in the Shootout to third, albeit by a scant margin.

Scott Pye (#2 Walkinshaw Andretti United ZB Commodore) continued his resurgence to drop his car into fifth – a sterling effort as he continues to battle a bout of gastro.

Penrite Racing teammates Anton De Pasquale and David Reynolds ended up sixth and seventh, ahead of Tim Slade (#14 Freightliner Racing), Lee Holdsworth (#5 The Bottle-O Racing Mustang) and James Courtney (#22 Walkinshaw Andretti United ZB Commodore).

After having Saturday night to reflect on his Race 31 error, Whincup was elated to claim another pole.

“That one meant a lot,” he said.

“Went all out, track was obviously a bit slower than yesterday or even qualifying, but got the most out of the car.

“It’s a bloody good feeling to get pole around here.

“We just gave it all we had, obviously the time we're very, very happy with, but you've got to keep a wrap on it because 95 laps is a long way this afternoon.

“We'll have a little celebration of this right now and then focus on the race.”
