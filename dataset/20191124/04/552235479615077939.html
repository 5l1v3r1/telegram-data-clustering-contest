Prince Andrew's favourite golf club could ditch Duke as new claims about paedo pal's parties emerge

Officials at Royal Perth Golfing Society have expressed "concerns" while new story about “big-breasted women” at Epstein party has emerged.

24 Nov 2019, 04:30 by Sophie Adams

Prince Andrew faces losing his role as patron of one of his favourite golf courses.

Officials at Royal Perth Golfing Society have said they are “concerned” about the Duke of York’s ­involvement in the Jeffrey Epstein scandal.

Golf-mad Andrew is the patron of Royal Perth, which is almost 200 years old.

The club was the first to be allowed to use “royal” in its title, having been given patronage by William IV.

It’s thought it could ask for another royal patron if it decides to cut its ties with the controversial prince after his car-crash ­interview on the BBC.

Club secretary Paul Lewis said yesterday: “Royal Perth Golfing S­ociety is concerned by recent events and is monitoring the situation. That is all we can say at this time.”

Andrew is also a member of the Royal Burgess Golfing ­Society of Edinburgh, honorary president of Inverness Golf Club and patron at Royal Montrose.

All three clubs declined to comment yesterday.

Andrew’s woes deepened yesterday after a former lapdancer claimed he blew a raspberry on her breasts at one of paedophile Epstein’s infamous parties.

She said the prince buried his face in her chest, then said she should be in a magazine for “big-breasted women”.

The woman told our sister paper the Sunday Mirror she was paid by Epstein to hire girls to attend, adding: “The girls had been specially selected to try to please Andrew. I believed the Duke liked curvy girls like his ex-wife, Fergie.”

The woman said there were rooms for sex and girls who looked too young stayed in the upstairs rooms at Epstein’s £77million New York mansion.

There is no suggestion Andrew was aware of any underage girls being at the parties in the late 90s.

The woman, now 47, also claimed cameras recorded guests so Epstein had leverage over them, if needed.

Epstein was found dead in a New York jail cell in August.

Andrew, 59, has vehemently denied any wrongdoing while associating with him – or witnessing his criminal activities.

Buckingham Palace declined to comment on the latest claims.
