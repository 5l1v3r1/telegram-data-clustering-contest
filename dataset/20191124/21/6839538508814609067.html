Harry Morton from Wollstonecraft said he felt a bit cheeky when he asked high school buddies to strip down to swimwear for Movember.

“We thought we’d add a bit of excitement and flair to the car wash to create some interest,” the 23-year-old said.

Mr Morton said the mates had raised close to $1500 for Movember in just three hours at North Sydney.

“It may have been a fun day with the boys, but it’s also about bringing awareness to men’s mental health,” he said.

“Many men are dying at younger ages these days due to things like suicide, depression and cancer. It scares me that I could lose one of my mates ­because of this.”

As a youth worker, Mr Morton has dedicated his life to helping others through youth organisation Reach Foundation and his online blog What’s Life About?

“A lot of young guys are too scared to come forward ­because there’s still that stigma around mental health issues. I really hope to change that,” he said.

His friend John Chapman was prompted to confide how tough he found his father’s­ ­battle with prostate cancer.

“His dad is doing really well now, but it still affects John,” Mr Morton said.

“I’m so glad I have mates who can be a little crazy sometimes, but can pause and reflect on what’s going on in their lives.”

Mr Morton said growing a Mo can help save a life. “A moustache is a great conversation starter because it forces people to ask questions. That’s a great first start,” he said.
