Order of nuns resist temptation to accept Britain's Got Talent invite

24 Nov 2019, 21:05 by Nick Fitzgerald

"Calm the Soul" sung by the Poor Clare sisters in Nuns' Island, Galway has racked up more than 50,000 views

An order of nuns from Nuns' Island in Galway are resisting temptation to accept invites to perform on Britain's Got Talent.

The Poor Clare sisters caught the eye of a top Britain's Got Talent scout after releasing a tune called "Calm the Soul".

Following the release last month, the song has racked up more than 50,000 views online.

It's so good that the sisters have received numerous invites to appear on the show, something they refuse every time, so as not to be counter-productive to their cause.

Abbess of the Poor Clare Monastery, Sr Colette told The Irish Times:

“A researcher from the show has been in touch a number of times asking us to perform Calm the Soul. We are thrilled by the invite of course but feel the message of the song might get lost somehow.

“We don’t watch a lot of television so we wouldn’t be following the X-Factor and other shows like that on a regular basis. We have seen a few highlights though including Fr Ray Kelly singing the REM song Everybody Hurts.

“Somebody also recommended that episode where Susan Boyle’s performance stunned the judges and that was lovely to watch.”

What prompted the sisters to record "Calm the Soul" was the reaction to their best-selling book of the same name.
