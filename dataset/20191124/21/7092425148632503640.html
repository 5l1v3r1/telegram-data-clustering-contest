Gardai appeal for witnesses following death of man in Coolock shooting

24 Nov 2019, 21:25 by Nick Fitzgerald
Due to critical injuries, the man was pronounced dead at Beaumont Hospital

Earlier, a man in his 20s was left in critical condition following a shooting in Coolock in north Dublin.

He has since passed away at Beaumont Hospital, due to "a number of apparent gunshot wounds."Â 

The shooting took place on Clonshaugh Avenue in Dublin 17 at approximately 5:15pm on Sunday.

Gardai are appealing for witnesses to come forward with any relevant information.

A garda spokesperson said:

"Gardai are appealing for witnesses to come forward, they are also appealing to anyone with camera footage from the vicinity of Clonshaugh Avenue between 4.30pm and 5.30pm today to make it available to investigators.

"Anyone with information is asked to contact Coolock Garda Station 01-6664200 or the Garda Confidential Line 1800 666 111."

Detectives investigating the case believe the incident is linked to an ongoing feud in the Coolock area.

It is believed the victim was shot a total of six times in the upper body and arms and that two gunmen were involved in the attack.
