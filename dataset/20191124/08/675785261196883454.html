General election: Boris Johnson vows not to raise income tax or VAT for 5 years

The Tory leader pledged to freeze income tax, National Insurance and VAT in a 'triple lock' unveiled the first details of the Conservative Party manifesto with general election pledges on childcare, hospital parking and pensions

24 Nov 2019, 08:36 by Nigel Nelson, Dan Bloom, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/nigel-nelson/, https://www.facebook.com/mirrordanbloom

Boris Johnson will promise to freeze taxes for the next five years as he finally unveils the Tories' general election manifesto today.

The PM is to announce a triple tax lock on income tax, national insurance and VAT at the West Midlands launch of the Tory election manifesto.

And in a pledge to Tory core voters, he'll vow to keep the triple lock on pensions, the winter fuel payment and concessionary bus passes if he stays in No10.

But he will not give automatic free TV licences to over-75s - saying that will remain the BBC’s responsibility despite the Tories breaking a previous pledge to protect the benefit.

The so-called 'tax lock' was an issue that dogged Theresa May in the 2017 election and is a bid by the Tories to take the fight to Labour.

Jeremy Corbyn has also vowed to freeze income tax, NI and VAT - for the 95% of taxpayers who earn less than £80,000 a year.

Mr Johnson will also introduce a ban on exporting plastic waste to poorer countries to stop it ending up in the oceans.

The PM will say: “It’s time to turn the page from the dither, delay and division of recent years.

“And to start a new chapter in the incredible history of this country.”

In addition to not increasing NI, Mr Johnson plans to raise the threshold to £9,500 next year, which he says will mean a £100 tax cut for 31 million workers. And there will be £1billion for extra childcare on school premises after lessons and during holidays. There will be a pledge to spend £6.3billion making social housing more energy efficient.

It was reported last night he will axe hospital car parking charges for some users, including blue badge disabled drivers, gravely ill patients and night staff who cannot use public transport.
