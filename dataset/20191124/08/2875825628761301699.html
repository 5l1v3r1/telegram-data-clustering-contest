Inside Gemma Collins' pink-heavy new pad - including a giant gorilla statue

The GC's always been one for pink and attitude, but her new house takes things to the next level by combining bold colour with traditional vibes

24 Nov 2019, 08:00 by Irishmirror.ie

The Diva Forever herself, Gemma Collins , doesn't shy away from a bold style statement or two - and her fancy new pad is no exception.

The 36 year old moved into the Essex barn conversion - or Gemmawood, as she named it - back in August to get a taste of the country life, far from busy Brentwood.

It's not got quite the traditional, old timey feel you might think.

Gemma recently took to her Instagram stories to share with fans that she was having a big old sort out of all her stuff.

Clothes strewn about the place, fans were given a glimpse of the classy wooden beams in the walls and the small, white windows.

What they might not have been expecting was the giant pink gorilla statue, sat cross-legged on a golden plinth - she's really showing off her animal-lover credentials.

We also catch a view of her radioactively pink carpet, as well as a blindingly golden pig statue being used as legs for a glass table.

"What's going on here today?" Gemma asked from behind the camera.

The star continued, focusing on the gorilla: "Ceasar is with us! From Planet of the Apes. I've painted him pink."

The GC added: "We are having another major sort out for my de-pop, but I am filming TOWIE today, the Christmas special! So I'm just deciding what to wear."

Gemma cuts to a pal - wrapped in a fluffy pink jacket - playing with a dog, who clearly loves pink as much as Gemma as he tries to claim it for his own.

There's more gold on a table behind, as well as yet another animal - this time a monochrome pink horse, paired with salmon pink curtains.

Unfortunately, we don't get a look at any palm trees decorated with lights that she fancied when she first moved in.

She's previously dished to MailOnline : "I've moved to the country, I love London, and obviously one day I'd like to move to LA, but for now it's the country and I love it."

She added: "I have been everywhere, but I love the country, with the trees and the nature and the rain. And autumn is a time for change. I am in my own little zone here."

Gemma continued that the country feels like a getaway because no one knows who she is.

"I can just go to the market, the farm shop," she said, "I am so happy here. I can finally say, 'There's no place like home.'

"I'm just settled here. It's been absolutely cosmic ordering."
