'Admire him' - Former Liverpool man can't get enough of Wolves star after latest showing

Wolverhampton Wanderers latest | Joao Moutinho's first Premier League goal of the season set them on the way to victory at Bournemouth - and his performance caught the eye in the Match of the Day studio

24 Nov 2019, 08:37 by Elliot Jackson, Josh Murray

Former Liverpool midfielder Danny Murphy has sung the praises of Joao Moutinho after his stunning goal set Wolverhampton Wanderers on their way to victory over Bournemouth.

The Portuguese star - who followed up the Vitality Stadium triumph by signing a new contract with Wolves - opened the scoring on Saturday by curling home a superb free-kick.

Raul Jimenez's 14th goal of the campaign doubled the lead for Nuno Espirito Santo's men, before Simon Francis was sent off for the hosts.

Steve Cook ensured it would be a tense finish when his deflected header reduced the arrears for the 10-man Cherries - but Wolves held on for their fourth League win of the campaign.

It owed much to Moutinho's brilliant first goal of the League campaign - and he has started all but one of their 13 matches.

That led to plenty of praise from Murphy on Match of the Day, who highlighted the experienced man's importance to Nuno's side.

"You have to admire him and I love watching him play," he said.

"He can create moments of magic like this. They have a few players with that extra bit of quality, hence the fact they’re sat in fifth but look at that for a strike.

"This is what you like to see, creative midfielder, switches off, Wilson, and Jimenez with his 14th goal of the season.

"He (Moutinho) has the ability to open you up anywhere on the pitch.

"I know Bournemouth went down to 10 men, but you can't give him this time on the ball because he’ll just keep spraying the play.

“Also, a player of his age it shows how well he looks after himself, he’s been involved in every game this season.

"He just gives them that extra bit of quality we were talking about. Wolves deserve a lot of credit because they started the season really poorly and they’ve really gone on a good run.”
