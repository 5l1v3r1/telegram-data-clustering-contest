Irish weather forecast: Unsettled weather to continue with dull rainy conditions

However, we will see temperatures rise slightly tomorrow

24 Nov 2019, 08:31 by Edel Hughes

Met Eireann have forecast further "unsettled" conditions next week as an area of low pressure continues to dominate.

The national forecaster warned we'll see dull and rainy weather with scattered showers throughout the week.

However, the good news is that temperatures will be around normal after the prolonged cold snap and Friday looks set to be a dry day.

Sunday morning will start dry but rather misty or foggy, slowly clearing this morning.

Most areas will be dry but dull during the day, but rain will slowly move into Munster.

A forecaster added: "Later in the evening, rain will move up into parts of Connacht and Leinster, along with freshening southeasterly breezes. Highest temperatures of 7C to 10C."

Tonight will see some scattered outbreaks of rain, with some heavy bursts across southern counties and lows of 5C to 8C.

Heading into Monday, it'll be "rather dull" again with scattered falls of rain and lingering fog in parts, especially across the west and northwest.

There will be some long dry spells but it will remain overcast.
