Deontay Wilder’s brother Marsellos brutally knocked out by 37-year-old opponent Dustin Long

24 Nov 2019, 08:40

- Deontay Wilder's brother Marsellos suffered an embarrassing knock out just hours before the WBC Champion defended his crown

- Marsellos was knocked out by 37-year-old opponent Dustin Long in the undercard of Wilder vs Ortiz II

- The 30-year-old Wilder has now seen his three-fight winning run (all by unanimous decision) come to an end following the defeat

Deontay Wilder did not only secure a second victory against Luis Ortiz to retain his WBC heavyweight belt, he also saved his brother’s blushes, after the 30-year-old was brutally knocked out just hours before the WBC Champion defended his crown.

Marsellos Wilder, the younger brother of Deontay, suffered his second knock out defeat of his career as he was caught by a big left hand blow from opponent Dustin Long.

Referee Jay Nady instantly waved an end to the fight with one minute and fifty-one seconds on the timer into the fourth round.
