Labour will let ministers campaign against Brexit despite Corbyn staying neutral

Shadow Chancellor John McDonnell said top Labour ministers are likely to pick a side and campaign on it in a second Brexit referendum after the 2019 general election - and those people include himself

24 Nov 2019, 08:52 by Dan Bloom, https://www.facebook.com/mirrordanbloom

Labour will let top ministers campaign against Brexit next year despite leader Jeremy Corbyn staying neutral, a top ally confirmed today.

Shadow Chancellor John McDonnell said while the leader will be an "honest broker" in a second referendum, other ministers will be able to campaign for or against Brexit "on the basis of their judgement".

It comes after several top allies to the Labour leader including Keir Starmer and Emily Thornberry pushed for Remain to be on the ballot paper.

Mr Corbyn confirmed on Friday night that he would stay neutral as Prime Minister in a second Brexit referendum so he could fairly carry out the result.

The Labour leader hopes to renegotiate a withdrawal deal within three months, then put it to voters within six months of becoming Prime Minister.

But he said he would refuse to take sides between backing his own pact or Remain in a campaign.

Speaking in a live BBC Question Time leaders’ special, he said: "My role and the role of our government will be to ensure that that referendum is held in a fair atmosphere and we will abide by the result of it.

"And I will adopt, as prime minister, if I am at the time, a neutral stance so that I can credibly carry out the results of that to bring communities and country together rather than continuing an endless debate about the EU and Brexit."

The Mirror learned on Friday that shadow ministers - who'll become Cabinet ministers if Labour wins - were expected to be allowed to campaign freely.

And today, Mr McDonnell confirmed to Sky News' Sophy Ridge on Sunday: "Members of the shadow cabinet, members of the government at that time, will be able to campaign.

"And I expect that as Harold Wilson did [in the 1975 referendum], individual members of the party and the shadow cabinet and the Cabinet then, will be able to campaign on the basis of their judgement."

Mr McDonnell indicated he personally will not stay neutral - but said he must wait to see the details of a Labour deal before picking a side.

He added: "I've said up until now I was in the negotiations with the Conservatives for six weeks and I couldn't see a deal even emerging then that could beat Remain.

"But let's see what we can negotiate.

"Whatever we do we've got to we've got to arrive at a situation where jobs and the economy will be protected. Jeremy's role will be an honest broker."
