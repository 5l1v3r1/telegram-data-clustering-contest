Pep Clotet press conference: Every word on Trueman, Clarke-Salter and Montero

Birmingham City news | Pep Clotet's full transcript after Birmingham City's 1-1 draw with Huddersfield Town

24 Nov 2019, 08:00 by Brian Dick, https://www.facebook.com/blueswriterbriandick/?fref=ts

Pep Clotet was pleased with the character of his team - and Connal Trueman as the young goalkeeper played an important part in Birmingham City’s 1-1 draw at Huddersfield Town.

They had to come from behind to claim it, though, after Frazier Campbell put the hosts ahead in the 55th minute.

Lukas Jutkiewicz was on hand to deflect home Marc Roberts’ header with 12 minutes to go, which gave Clotet a point he felt his team merited.

Match analysis

"It was a hard fought point, you knew it was going to be very difficult, they are in good form at home, we knew they would press us a lot.

"We knew today we would have to be a bit more patient with the ball, try to dominate possession when it was very difficult to get possession.

"We knew that if we managed to break this press the game would open up a little bit more in wide areas.

"That’s why we went a little bit wider today.

"I think the effort was there, despite the set-back of them scoring first because it looked like it was going to be a game of the first one who scores can take the points.

"The team kept believing and although we had a little spell where we were nervous, obviously going away from home and everything is a little bit more difficult, I think the team managed the game superbly.

"The players coming off the bench all made a big impact."

Connal Trueman

"I am very happy for him because it’s his debut this season - he played a few games last season - but he put his head down and worked for a year-and-a-half, we thought he deserved that chance.

"It’s up to him to take it, not only did he save important chances but at the same time he showed that he had the character as well. He helped us a lot from the back.

"I am very pleased for him because it really comes from his own work."

Jake Clarke-Salter

"There is no doubting the quality of Jake, he has already showed at international level and playing aboard but that position is never easy because a mistake in there is always a difficult one to overcome.

"But he is growing into his own game and the Championship is difficult, especially with today how physical Huddersfield would be. I think he managed.

"He is in a position where he needs to use his pace because that gave him a little bit of an edge with the strikers. It’s not easy for a young player to perform in the Championship."

Jefferson Montero's bright cameo....

"He was a handful when he came on because he has that ability to beat defenders one-v-one. He got us that foul for the goal and he was a constant threat on the wing.

"It’s still a bit early because now we have Bela and Josh [McEachran] featuring, sometimes I would like that we would be two months further down the road and everyone is used to everything.

"But that’s how we are, we have said we are a work in progress, we are growing and the fact we are getting results away from home always leaves you wanting more."

Set piece success

"The whole team works a lot on it. It was a massive strength for us last season, it got us 27 goals last season, all the set plays including throws and free kicks. It’s always been a massive part of Birmingham City.

"And this season as well, it’s very important for us because today if we didn’t find the perfect chance to score a goal a set-piece can always unlock it and today it was very, very welcome."
