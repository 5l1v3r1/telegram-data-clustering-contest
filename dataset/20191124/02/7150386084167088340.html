Chandrababu Naidu 'Thanks' Amit Shah After Amaravati Included as Andhra Capital in Map

An earlier version of the map showed Hyderabad as the common capital of both Andhra Pradesh and Telangana. The error was pointed out by TDP MPs in Parliament, leading to MHA rectifying the same and including Amaravati as Andhra capital.

24 Nov 2019, 02:17

New Delhi: Telugu Desam Party (TDP) president and former Andhra Pradesh Chief Minister N Chandrababu Naidu on Saturday thanked Union Home Minister Amit Shah for including state capital Amaravati in the new political map of India, after the error was pointed out by TDP MPs in Parliament.

Naidu, whose party suffered an acrimonious split with the BJP-led National Democratic Alliance (NDA) in 2018 over its demand for special status to Andhra Pradesh, tweeted, “Dear Sri Amit Shah Ji, truly appreciate your esteemed office’s quick redressal of the issue related to Amaravati not being mentioned on India’s map. You have endeared yourself to Telugu people by taking this step.”
