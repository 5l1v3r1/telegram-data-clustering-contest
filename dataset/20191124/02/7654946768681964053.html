Supreme Court Justice Ruth Bader Ginsburg admitted to hospital for chills and fever

24 Nov 2019, 02:26 by Rich Calder

Supreme Court Justice Ruth Bader Ginsburg was admitted to a hospital Friday night after experiencing chills and fever earlier in the day, a spokeswoman said Saturday.

Ginsburg, 86, was initially evaluated at Sibley Memorial Hospital in Washington, DC, before being moved to Johns Hopkins Hospital in Baltimore for further evaluation and treatment of any potential infection, said spokeswoman Kathleen Arberg.

Ginsburg has recently suffered a series of health scares.

She underwent radiation treatment in August for a malignant tumor on her pancreas.

In December, Ginsburg underwent lung cancer surgery.

She was hospitalized last November after fracturing three ribs in a fall in her office at the court.
