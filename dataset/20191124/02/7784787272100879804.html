Van Gisbergen wins Newcastle opener as Whincup throws away Red Bull one-two

24 Nov 2019, 02:46 by James Pavey

Shane van Gisbergen continued the Red Bull Holden Racing Team’s good form from the enduros to convert pole to victory in Race 31 at the Coates Hire Newcastle 500.

It loomed to be a sparkling Saturday for the factory Holden squad as Jamie Whincup held second behind van Gisbergen for much of the race, before he made a critical late error to hand P2 to champion-elect Scott McLaughlin.

Whincup’s error cost RBHRT valuable ground as the Holden outfit looks to steal the teams’ championship away from the Shell V-Power Racing Team.

Van Gisbergen escorted McLaughlin wide at the first turn after the start, helping Whincup slot into second to complete a RBHRT quinella.

As van Gisbergen held firm, Whincup kept the #97 honest as McLaughlin retained station in third. The champion was brought in on Lap 7 and was short-fuelled, and once Whincup (Lap 16) and van Gisbergen stopped (Lap 33), McLaughlin emerged in the lead.

Van Gisbergen, having taken a longer stop, dropped to third but quickly caught the rear of his teammate’s ZB Commodore, with McLaughlin unable to create a gap.

On Lap 43, Whincup moved aside to let van Gisbergen through into Turn 11, and the 2016 series champion set off after his countryman.

McLaughlin stopped on Lap 50, and Whincup followed him a lap later, the latter retaking effective second.

Trying to build a gap, having found clean air, van Gisbergen was pushing hard and screeched for blue flags at every chance.

On Lap 65, van Gisbergen stopped and emerged with a comfortable gap, and with the RBHRT teammates first and second, the deficit between the leading two teams had been slashed to 77 points.

However, an uncharacteristic error from Whincup at Turn 1 on Lap 75 sent the seven-time champion tumbling to seventh.

Van Gisbergen held a 13-second lead over McLaughlin with five laps remaining, and as the weather closed in on Newcastle, teams readied wet weather tyres in case of a late deluge.

However, despite the dark clouds forming over the city, the weather remained clear as van Gisbergen cruised to his fifth win of the season.

McLaughlin led home teammate Fabian Coulthard to help the Ford squad put one hand on the teams’ gong, with Whincup slumping to eighth.

David Reynolds (#9 Penrite Racing ZB Commodore) and Cameron Waters (#6 Monster Energy Mustang) completed the top five ahead of Scott Pye, who made a brilliant recovery from 24th to sixth following a crash in qualifying.

James Courtney trailed home his Walkinshaw Andretti United teammate to seventh ahead of Whincup, with Tim Slade (#14 Freightliner Racing ZB Commodore) and Mark Winterbottom (#18 IRWIN Racing ZB Commodore) completing the top 10.

With the win, van Gisbergen sealed second in the drivers’ standings.

“Awesome day for us, shame about Jamie making a mistake there, that would’ve been great team points,” he said.

“Thanks to the guys, the car was awesome. I had a cool start, good battle and then forced our way through, so pretty cool.

“Thanks to Shippy [engineer Grant McPherson] for tuning that up in qualifying, that set us up for the win, because you simply just can’t pass here.

“Caught up in the middle stint and then you just get stuck behind. Great strategy to get us the win, that was all a team effort.”

Considering the pace of the factory Holdens, McLaughlin admitted Whincup’s error gifted his team a two-three finish, but was pleased to keep the teams’ championship fight in the Shell team’s corner heading to Sunday.

“It’s tough around here, it’s quite hard work,” McLaughlin said.

“Great job for everyone at Shell V-Power Racing, two and three, obviously Jamie made a mistake there and gifted us a double podium, but I’m sure they’ll come back strong tomorrow.

“Overall, a great team result.”
