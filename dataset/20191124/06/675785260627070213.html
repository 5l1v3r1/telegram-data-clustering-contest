Marcus Rashford ready to step up and keep Ole Gunnar Solskjaer safe from Mauricio Pochettino

United boss Solskjaer is under pressure now that Pochettino is out of work - but Rashford is finally showing what a special talent he is and can help Ole keep his job

24 Nov 2019, 06:50 by Andy Dunn, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/andy-dunn/

There is something a little odd about someone who has never won a trophy in his decade-long managerial career casting a shadow over the job of someone who has won two league titles in a managerial career spanning a similar amount of time.

Yet Mauricio Pochettino’s presence will be felt by Ole Gunnar Solskjaer every time his Manchester United team take a misstep.

If Sheffield United turn over ­Solskjaer’s side at Bramall Lane this afternoon, ‘Angry from Manchester’ will be on the blower to the radio station, demanding Poch is in ­position first thing tomorrow.

Understandable. Those two league titles, after all, were of the ­Norwegian variety, and they don’t count.

Solskjaer made a hash of the Cardiff City job, Pochettino took ­Tottenham to the Champions League four times, and to the final less than six months ago.

Poch is considered one of the elite, Solskjaer is not. Simple as that. The United board – led by Ed Woodward – seem genuine in their long-term commitment to their manager, but if a poor run of results and ­Pochettino’s availability run side-by-side, ­Solskjaer will be under serious ­pressure.

If there is one man who can ­ensure Solskjaer can stay relaxed, it is Marcus Rashford.

It might sound ­ridiculous to say this about someone who has only just turned 22, but Rashford is ­finally turning into the player most of us thought he would be.

Finally? Finally? He is a rookie. Still learning, developing.

True.

But in February, it will be four years since he made his first-team debut. Now, we are getting ­confirmation of what a very special talent he is. Three moments stand out.

There was the free-kick in the League Cup win at Stamford Bridge.

There was the stunning piece of trickery and acceleration that took him past a nonplussed Davy Propper in the Old Trafford win against Brighton.

And then there was the first-time finish against Kosovo last Sunday.

We have been hearing wondrous tales of Rashford’s dead-ball striking for a long time and he could have provided no clearer evidence of their ­veracity than at Chelsea towards the end of October.

We know how easily he can go past a player, but his embarrassment of Propper was jaw-dropping.

Almost as jaw-dropping as his miss when given an open goal in the same match, a rick that revived the familiar debate about his predatory instincts inside the penalty area.

Well, we had his most emphatic response in that debate when a ­Raheem Sterling pass was despatched without breaking stride into the ­bottom corner against the Kosovans.

That is nine goals and one assist in his last 10 games for club and country.

Rashford has always dismissed suggestions he should be more ­prolific, even though the past three Premier League seasons – 30-plus ­appearances apiece – have brought him just five, seven and 10 goals. He already has six this time around.

With his country, it has seemed Rashford has been in direct ­competition with Jadon Sancho for the right to form a trio with Harry Kane and Raheem Sterling.

He has surely won that fight.

And with his club, Rashford has now become the key, go-to player every United fan has hoped he would become – the player who will keep Ole in his job.

Bale controversy flags up bigger issues at Madrid

Gareth Bale should not have gone anywhere near that flag last week and the reaction of the Madrid media was understandable.

But the flag covers up bigger issues at the Bernabeu – such as Eden Hazard (right) turning up for pre-season five kilos overweight. That meant a slow start to the campaign for the £150million signing, who – ahead of last night’s game against Real Sociedad – had scored just once and assisted four times.

Most wondered how Chelsea would do with a transfer ban and without Hazard. But the Blues might have got a decent deal. Bale might not suffer the lion’s share of Real scrutiny for much longer.

Sarri vs Ronaldo: There can only be one winner

Maurizio Sarri and Cristiano Ronaldo have been going to varying lengths to deny there is an issue between them at Juventus.

Sarri has hooked Ronaldo in successive games and, unsurprisingly, Ronaldo did not look best pleased.

And, despite playing for Portugal last week, Ronaldo missed Juve’s game with Atalanta yesterday.

It’s all pointing to someone making a premature exit.

And it’s more likely to be Sarri than Ronaldo.
