Leeds United star pays West Brom the ultimate compliment

West Bromwich Albion are top of the Championship with Leeds United in second place

24 Nov 2019, 06:00 by Alex Dicken, https://www.facebook.com/alexdickenjournalist/?fref=ts

Leeds United star Adam Forshaw has paid West Bromwich Albion the ultimate compliment.

The Baggies extended their lead at the top of the Championship table with a 2-1 win over Sheffield Wednesday at The Hawthorns, sealed by Charlie Austin’s late penalty.

Slaven Bilic’s team are two points ahead of second-placed Leeds and five in front of Preston in third.

And Leeds midfielder Forshaw, who hasn’t played since September due to a hip problem, reckons Bilic’s Baggies will take some beating.

“For me personally I think anyone who finishes above West Brom will get promoted,” Forshaw said during a punditry appearance on QUEST.

Albion’s latest win was their third in succession and came without key duo Romaine Sawyers and Jake Livermore, both of whom missed out due to suspension.

They were heading for a share of the spoils against Wednesday before Matheus Pereira was clattered by Owls’ goalkeeper Keiren Westwood to give the Baggies the chance to seal all three points.

Austin duly delivered from 12 yards and Forshaw insists Wednesday can have no complaints about the decision to award the spot-kick.

He said: “Pereira is a top player and he’s just nicked in front. Unfortunately for the goalkeeper, he’s made the referee’s mind up. He’s come out and with the momentum I’d say it’s a penalty.”
