Flight embarrassment motivates Derbyshire woman to lose 10 stone

She was determined to slim down

24 Nov 2019, 06:30 by Ben Lyons

An awkward incident on a plane prompted a determined Derbyshire woman to lose weight and transform her health and her life.

Katrina Brown, from Heanor, was verging on 24st when she found the motivation to start losing weight in October, last year.

With a dress size of 30, Katrina struggled to walk around and her weight caused her leg and back injuries.

Furthermore, on a flight to Dublin, her size meant that other passengers had to move seats so that she could fly.

Incredibly, just 13 months on, the 30-year-old has slimmed down to 13st 8lbs thanks to a big shift in her diet.

As a result of her weight loss, Katrina's energy levels have increased, her confidence has been boosted and she now swims and goes for long walks.

Katrina, a soft play manager from Heanor, said: "I was always big growing up and I just got bigger and bigger.

"I wasn't fit enough to do any exercise, didn't do much cooking at all and had takeaways or went out for food most nights.

"At my heaviest, I was struggling to even walk around because I kept hurting my leg and back.

"I was off work for a while with the injuries too, so I decided it was time to do something about it.

"One of my goals was to sit in a normal seat on a plane, without needing extra leg room.

"I went to Dublin and ordered an extra leg room seat, but I couldn't even sit on it because I didn't have a seat belt extender.

"That was really embarrassing because some of the people on the plane had to move for me.

"I didn't want to have to go through that again."

Katrina joined the Ilkeston Slimming World group in October, last year.

Supported by five of her friends and family, she lost 6.5lbs in her first week and soon made big changes to her daily diet.

Katrina said: "We go to Slimming World together. My mum, sister, sister-in-law, and two friends all go to the same group.

"I've always done Slimming World in the past, but I never stayed to a group for long enough.

"But, I stuck to it this time. I cook food from scratch most of the time, planning my daily diet by writing it all down.

"I used to drink a lot of Coca-Cola, but I've swapped it for Coke Zero and squash. I do still have hot chocolate quite often though.

"For snacks, I used to have chocolate, cake or whatever I could get hold of.

"But, now I have fruit, low-fat yogurts, and do portion control by weighing my food before I eat it."

Now a size 14, Katrina is delighted that her weight loss has allowed her to get into swimming and enjoy walking again.

She added: "The weight loss means I can now go swimming twice a week and dog walking most days. I would never have dreamed of going swimming before my weight loss.

"I never do less than 12,000 steps a day, and some days I do 20,000 when we take the dogs out on longer walks.

"I used to read weight loss stories in the paper and think 'how have they done that?', and I never thought it would be me who's lost the 10st.

"The weight loss has given me loads more energy at work and my confidence has grown so much.

"Now, I'd like to lose another stone and see how I feel then."
