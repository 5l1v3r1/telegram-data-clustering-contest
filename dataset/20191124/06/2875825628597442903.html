Deontay Wilder's message to Tyson Fury after KO win over Luis Ortiz

Wilder is scheduled to rematch Fury on February 22 in Las Vegas after his seventh-round win over Ortiz

24 Nov 2019, 06:11 by Irishmirror.ie

Deontay Wilder knocked out Luis Ortiz and then warned Tyson Fury: You're next.

Wilder was outboxed for six rounds by the tricky Cuban in Las Vegas on Saturday night but detonated his right hand in the dying seconds of the seventh session.

Ortiz rose to his feet too slowly and the referee waved the fight off as Wilder retained his heavyweight world title.

And the American insisted he will be ready for his rematch with Fury which is scheduled for February 22.

"Fury is next," said Wilder. "Then I want a unification fight with whoever wins, Anthony Joshua or Andy Ruiz Jr.

"We're ready for Fury and hopefully he's ready for me. I know his trainer is here and I hope he got some notes down.

"I'm ready to go at any given time and any given moment and February is looking like our time.

"I was very confident in my ability and my skill. I tell people all the time that my intellect in the ring is very high so I know exactly what I'm doing.

"I had to calculate Ortiz perfectly; he's a very dangerous fighter that's why no other heavyweight wanted to give him the opportunity.

"These guys have to be perfect for 12 rounds, I have to be perfect for two seconds and every time I prove that.

"It was a game plan, I had to calculate with him. I wasn't getting tired or fatigued, I was having a lot of fun. he was getting tired, once i had my range and distance, it was goodnight."

Ortiz looked to have tied Wilder up in knots and was seconds away from the end of the round when he was dumped on the canvas.

The Cuban said: "This is boxing, I told everybody that it was going to be him or me and that it wasn't going to go 12 rounds.
