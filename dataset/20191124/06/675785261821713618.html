Ealing stabbing: Man in 20s knifed to death in 'road rage attack'

Police and paramedics arrived at West Ealing station to find a man suffering from astab wound - sadly he was pronounced dead at the scene in north London, police say

24 Nov 2019, 06:24 by Tiffany Lo

A man in his 20s has died after being stabbed outside a Tube station station in north London.

A taxi officer manager reportedly said he heard a loud bang near the rank in Ealing and saw 'a man get out and stab another driver'.

He said: "Lots of police turned and paramedics.

"We have been told to stay in our office and not to leave."

Pictures circulating on social media show a black Mini Cooper and a white BMW in the middle of the road.

Met Police say they were called to the scene at 1.18am to reports of an injured man.

Officers and London Ambulance Service arrived to find a victim, in his 20s, suffering from a stab wound.

Despite the efforts of paramedics, the man was pronounced dead at the scene.

A police cordon is in place on Drayton Green Road.

A spokesperson for the Met Police said: "Enquiries are underway to trace and notify next of kin.

"Formal identification awaits. A post mortem will be scheduled in due course.

"There have been no arrests. Enquiries continue."

TFL tweeted in response to the incident: "Drayton Green Road is West Ealing is closed in both directions between The Avenue and Alexandria Road due to a police incident outside West Ealing station.

"Bus Route E1, E7 and E11 are diverted due to the closures on Drayton Green and Gordon Road."
