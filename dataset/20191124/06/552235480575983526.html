Jeremie Frimpong reveals the Celtic legend he's been getting tips from as youngster's rise continues

The exciting full-back put in a man of the match performance in Celtic's comprehensive win over Livngston.

24 Nov 2019, 06:00 by Scott McDermott, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/scott-mcdermott/

Skipper Scott Brown plays the role of a director in Celtic’s new Christmas advert.

But even he had to take a back seat as he was upstaged by young upstart Jeremie Frimpong’s virtuoso display.

The former Manchester City kid’s acting skills might be a bit ropey, judging by that club video.

But the 18-year-old was a star turn on the pitch in this comfortable win over Gary Holt’s side.

James Forrest helped himself to a double, with Odsonne Edouard also on the scoresheet, as Celtic avenged their defeat to Livi in October to move clear of Rangers at the top of the Premiership.

Brown wasn’t far behind Frimpong in the man of the match stakes as he orchestrated from the middle of the park and hit the Hoops’ crucial second goal.

But the little full-back, keeping Moritz Bauer out of the team, was relentless on the right.

He bombed up and down all afternoon, as TWO Livi players – Steve Lawson and Aaron Taylor-Sinclair – couldn’t live with him.

It’s early days in Frimpong’s Parkhead career but he already looks like a sound investment.

After the game, boss Neil Lennon heaped praise on him and said: “Frimpong is amazing.

“That performance is one of the best I’ve seen from a right-back at Celtic. It had everything, pace, power, assists, shots on goal, recovery runs, defending.

“For a kid at 18 to come into a daunting environment like this – he’s handled it brilliantly.

“His talent is unequivocal at the minute. He’s made us a better team, there’s no question about that.

“We thought he’d be a good emerging talent, a development player. But meteorically, he’s come through.

“It’s all down to him, no-one else. It was a team effort getting him, but his performances are down
to him.

“People think he struggles with height but he deals with it with great upper body strength.

“He’s amazing with the ball and is a real find for us.

“He’s contributing immensely right now and it’s his place to lose.”

Frimpong is a buzz-bomb off the pitch as well as on it and he revealed he’s even been getting tips off legendary Hoops and Scotland right-back Danny McGrain.

He said: “When I get on the pitch, I just do what I do best – attack and help the team out as much as I can.

“Hopefully the manager will keep putting me in. If you want to play at the top you can’t be shy, you just have to go and play.

“I’ve been ready for this all my life so it’s great. I enjoyed that a lot today.

“When I first came here I spoke to Danny and he helped me a lot.”

Frimpong was always going to start here for Celtic and there were no major surprises in either line-up. Lennon opted for Jonny Hayes ahead of Greg Taylor at left-back, while Tom Rogic got the nod over Olivier Ntcham in midfield.

Livingston simply don’t change for anyone. Their robust 4-5-1 was set up by Holt to frustrate their hosts and Celtic had to deal with their physicality.

Early on, there was a bit of needle and that livened up proceedings.

Christopher Jullien couldn’t handle Livi striker Lyndon Dykes in that 2-0 loss in West Lothian and they renewed hostilities here, leaving a bit on each other when the ball was gone.

Brown also had a nibble at the big Aussie seconds after Dykes had shoulder-charged him.

Livi were only playing to their strengths – literally – but Celtic stood up to it.

And on 20 minutes, they got their noses in front. Rogic picked out a pass for Edouard that Ricki Lamie will feel he should have cut out.

Suddenly the in-form French striker was clean through and his sublime dinked finish beat keeper Matija Sarkic all ends up.

It was a class finish from Edouard. Leigh Griffiths would later make his comeback from the bench for Lennon’s men – but he’s got a mammoth task trying to replace the former PSG kid.

Livi couldn’t get themselves far enough up the pitch to trouble Celtic in the first half and Sarkic was kept busy.

He denied Edouard before Rogic fired a shot over after terrific work from the effervescent Frimpong.

Lennon was forced into a change shortly after the break when Taylor came on for the injured Hayes, but it didn’t upset their rhythm, with Brown pulling the strings.

They needed a second goal to put Livi to bed and the skipper provided it.

Ryan Christie’s pass found him just outside the box and his shot deflected off Jon Guthrie to deceive Sarkic.

Brown’s rare goal prompted the biggest cheer of the day and Celtic’s players fed off it.

They got a quick-fire third when Edouard sent Frimpong clear on goal – and when Sarkic saved his effort Forrest followed up to bury a volley.

For Holt’s men it was now a case of keeping the score down, so the sight of Griffiths coming on as a sub wouldn’t have helped.

The Scotland striker hasn’t played since August but he immediately looked a threat.

He fizzed a shot over before his rustiness cost him a goal at the back post when his touch let him down.

Sarkic, who’s been an excellent signing for Livingston, produced a super stop to thwart Christie.

But he could do little to prevent Forrest making it 4-0 after great pressing play from his Scotland team-mate.

That was plenty for Lennon’s team, who edged ahead in the Premiership – and passed the baton on to rivals Rangers today in the title race.
