Timeline: 12 hours that rocked Maharashtra politics

24 Nov 2019, 00:30 by Bangalore Mirror Bureau

At 8.30 pm on Friday, Sharad Pawar announced that Uddhav Thackeray was the consensus candidate for chief minister. Less than 12 hours later, Devendra Fadnavis was sworn in for a second term

Friday, November 22

8.30 pm Congress, Shiv Sena and NCP meeting at Nehru Center ends. Sharad Pawar says there is a consensus on Uddhav Thackeray as CM. Three-party alliance says it will stake claim to form government on Saturday.

9.30 pm Fadanvis speaks to BJP high command, tells them he wants to stake claim to form the government before the Sena, Congress and NCP do so.

11.45 pm Ajit Pawar’s secret deal with BJP is finalised.

11:55 pm Devendra Fadnavis goes to Raj Bhavan and stakes claim to form the government. He tells the Governor he has the support of 173 MLAs, including 105 from BJP, 54 from NCP and 15 from smaller parties and independent candidates.

Saturday, November 23

12.30 am Governor Bhagat Singh Koshyari cancels his Delhi visit

2.10 am Secretary to the governor is told to submit the President’s rule revocation order at 5:47 am and arrange the swearing-in at 6:30 am.

2.30 am Secretary says he will submit the file in two hours and suggests that the swearing-in be held at 7:30 am.

5.30 am Ajit Pawar and Fadnavis arrive at Raj Bhavan.

5.47 am President’s rule is revoked, but this isn’t announced until 9 am.


7.50 am Governor BS Koshyari begins swearing-in ceremony.

8:01 am News breaks that Fadnavis has taken oath as chief minister and Ajit Pawar as deputy chief minister.


8:16 am Prime Minister Narendra Modi congratulates Fadnavis and Ajit Pawar.
