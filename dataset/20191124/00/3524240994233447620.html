Imo begins training for civil servants

24 Nov 2019, 00:39 by Chidiebube Okeoma

Chidiebube Okeoma

The Imo State Government has commenced the training of civil servants on public procurement processes.

The Senior Special Assistant to the Governor on Public Enlightenments, Mr Eze Ugochukwu, said the programme was to enhance the civil servants’ capacity on international best practices in public procurement processes.

He said Governor Emeka Ihedioha was committed to the training and retraining of civil servants.

Ugochukwu said, “The government of Imo State, through the Bureau of Public Procurement and Price Intelligence, has commenced a series of first level procurement training for public officers involved in the public procurement processes.

“The training will cut across all state Ministries, Departments and Agencies. The training programme was flagged off by the Secretary to the State Government of Imo State, Uche Onyeagocha, with the Head of Service in attendance.

“The first group of officers, on grade levels 10 to 15, were selected from the Ministries of Works, Housing, Public Utilities, Environment, and their parastatals/agencies.

“The next training is for officers in the Ministries of Transport, Justice, Commerce, and Industry, Gender and Vulnerable Groups Affairs and their agencies and parastatals.

“The training is in keeping with the assurances made by His Excellency, Emeka Ihedioha to re-tool and enhance the productive capacity of civil servants in the state to meet global standards,” he said.
