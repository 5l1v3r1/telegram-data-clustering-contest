Canisius wins state Catholic football title with dramatic two-point conversion

24 Nov 2019, 00:36

A remarkable fourth-quarter comeback had Canisius within one point of Cardinal Hayes with a big decision looming on the final play of regulation and the state Catholic football title on the line.

Senior leader CJ Ozolins made that difficult decision much easier for Canisius coach Rich Robbins.

After sophomore quarterback Tyler Baker led the team’s second touchdown drive in the final nine minutes – this one capped by a 13-yard catch in the end zone from classmate Nik McMillan with time running out – Ozolins came to Robbins to say that he believed he could find the end zone on a two-point play if given the chance.

Robbins’ faith in Ozolins paid off as the standout linebacker and fullback took the ball – which was already moved halfway to the goal line after a Cardinal Hayes penalty – and forced his way into the end zone to give the Crusaders a 25-24 win Saturday at Stransky Field in West Seneca.

It was the third New York State Catholic Athletic Association title for Canisius (7-5) and second over Cardinal Hayes since the state Catholic title game between the downstate New York Catholic High School Football League champion and Monsignor Martin Association began in 2014.

“When we got the half the distance to the goal line penalty, that certainly made the decision easier for me, and when your senior captain runs up to you, grabs you by your coat and says, ‘Give me the ball; I will get in,’ I had to give it to CJ,” Robbins said. “And he got in. What a great moment.”
