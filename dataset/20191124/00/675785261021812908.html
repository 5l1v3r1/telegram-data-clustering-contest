Dan Osborne takes son Teddy to Arsenal as he makes Jacqueline Jossa trial plea

The fed-up former EastEnders star will take on the Crevice of Cruelty with Ian Wright on Sunday but unhappy Dan has had enough of seeing his brave wife suffer

24 Nov 2019, 00:24 by Sam Elliott, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/sam-elliott/

It was probably a good way to distract them both from what's going on over on the other side of the world.

Dan Osborne took son Teddy, five, to see Arsenal - and it was a great chance to get their mind off the Jugle.

His wife Jacqueline Jossa is doing well in Australia but the relentless Bushtucker trials are adding strain.

Not that the Gunners-mad reality star was given too much to smile about at the football.

Arsenal were booed off against lowly Southampton after scraping a 2-2 Premier League draw six minutes into injury time.

Dan later appealed for the British public to stop voting for Jacqueline, who he shares daughters Ella, four, and Mia 17 months, with.

The ex-EastEnder will take on the Crevice of Cruelty with Ian Wright but ticked off Dan has had enough of seeing her suffer.

He said on Instagram : “I know I said to you all to vote for Jac because she isn’t in there to change lightbulbs, I didn’t mean every singe trial.

"Give her a break now, come on.

"She’s worried about it but she keeps smashing them all. She’ll keep proving everyone wrong.

"She’s doing it with Ian Wright too, which is good. He’ll look after her.

"Let her do this one and then just give her a break please because she’s getting upset now and it ain’t nice. But she’ll be good, she’ll smash it.

"She’ll get all the stars and head back to camp with meals.”

Meanwhile, Andrew Maxwell broke down in tears and threatened to walk out on Saturday's I'm A Celebrity.

The comedian confessed he had "hit a wall" as he became "overwhelmed" with jungle life.

Andrew said: "I honestly thought that I was gonna walk out, but I haven’t, when you’re tired, you just say ‘have I done the right thing’."
