Uber wins small court victory in California rape suit; victim vows to pursue case

24 Nov 2019, 00:47 by Sara Dorn

Uber won a court victory when a judge dismissed part of a federal lawsuit filed by a woman claiming the company was at fault for her alleged rape — because her attacker’s car was clad with an Uber decal, signalling to her it was a safe ride.

A federal judge decided Friday that the driver was not acting in the scope of his employment when he allegedly picked up the woman from a San Francisco-area shopping mall in 2018, drove her to a secluded location and raped her, because he was suspended from the company at the time for allegedly harassing another female passenger, The Recorder reported.

The woman allegedly mistook the ride for a legitimate Uber, because the company logo was plastered to the man’s car.

The man is facing criminal charges.

California Judge Jacqueline Scott Corley also dismissed the Jane Doe’s claim that Uber was responsible for meeting a higher standard than common carriers — defined by law as transportation services offered to the general public — are required to follow.

Corley said the passenger can revise and refile her complaint. Her lawyer, Matthew Davis, pledged they would.

“We are going to amend the complaint and proceed with the case,” he told Bloomberg News.
