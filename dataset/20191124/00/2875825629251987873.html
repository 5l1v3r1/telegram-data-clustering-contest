I'm A Celeb cast unveil bizarre luxury items including Christmas tree and a song

2019's I'm A Celeb bunch have some truly weird choices for their luxury items

24 Nov 2019, 00:10 by Irishmirror.ie

Tonight's I'm A Celebrity saw the famous faces finally earn their luxury items.

Each celeb is allowed one item each, with only Adele Roberts and Kate Garraway managing to win theirs in an earlier challenge.

While Adele bizarrely opted for a picture of her idol, Loose Women's Jane McDoanld, for luck, tonight's revelations show she might not be the only oddball in camp.

EastEnders legend Cliff Parisi opted for a dash of Christmas cheer with a quite strange - but no less sweet - choice of a camp Christmas tree.

Former Arsenal footballer Ian Wright meanwhile had his new pals holding their breaths after he announced his item wasn't "physical."

Music lilted through the jungle as he announced his item as a track of Kirk Franklin's Looking For You.

The newly reunited camp had a jig round the fire as they heard music for the first time in ten days.

Reality star Caitlyn Jenner went for face cream, explaining she doesn't like sleeping with a dry face.

Roman Kemp had a mug with a snap of his girlfriend on it, Nadine Coyle had a de-tangler for her hair, and Andy Whyment went for the simple pleasures with some lip balm.

Myles Stephenson got his disposable camera, Andrew Maxell his inflatable chair and newly elected camp leader James Haskell his neck support pillow.

Jacqueline Jossa had a pillow with photos of her family printed all over.

"All hail the leader," camp shouted as James walked into camp.

"James is perfect to be a leader," Ian reckoned, "When you look at the chores, they suit everybody. I know a leader when I see one."

After James picked Jacqueline to be his Deputy, Caitlyn joked: "Our romance is over. You picked someone else."

It was an emotional episode for a few campmates - Jacqueline and Andrew both had a cry.

Jacqueline couldn't keep back the tears as Ant & Dec told her she and Ian Wright would be taking on the latest Bushtucker Trial, The Crevice of Cruelty.

Andrew had some tears too as he confided to Jacqueline and James that she was considering packing it in and leaving.

"You'd never leave," Jacqueline insisted, "I can tell you that. You're just tired."
