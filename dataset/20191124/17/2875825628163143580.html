Thousands of banned drivers could still be on roads as just 13% disqualified in 2019 have surrendered licences

The rate is the lowest in five years

24 Nov 2019, 17:10 by Sean McCarthaigh

Thousands of banned drivers could be still on the road after failing to surrender their licences.

Just 1,257 motorists out of 9,656 banned by the courts or who have exceeded the maximum level of penalty points have handed them in.

The rate of 13%, which includes figures up to November 15, is the lowest in five years.

PARC road safety group chairperson Susan Gray said: “More and more people are taking the chance of not handing back their licence.

“You can be guaranteed a lot of them are not doing so because they want to be able to continue to drive.

“If there was a proper system in place there would not be a problem.

“The problem is getting worse and the Road Safety Authority and other authorities don’t seem to be doing anything about it.”

The statistics, provided by Transport Minister Shane Ross, show more than 4,000 of those disqualified – 42% of all motorists banned – have failed to respond to notices seeking the return of their licence.

Almost 4,400 other disqualified drivers had no valid Irish licence to surrender as they either did not possess one, it had expired or it was foreign.

PARC claimed there is a breakdown between the RSA, Department of Transport, Courts Service and Garda in ensuring details of all banned motorists are recorded and enforced.

Failure to surrender a licence carries a maximum fine of €1,000 on first conviction and up to €2,000 on a second or further offence.

The penalty for driving while disqualified is a fine of up to €5,000 and/or six months in prison.

RSA chief Moyagh Murdock said the figures were “entirely unacceptable”.
