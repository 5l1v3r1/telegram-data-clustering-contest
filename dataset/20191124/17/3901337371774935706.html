Makhanya Hat-Trick Crushes Cosmos, Birds In Top Eight

24 Nov 2019, 17:33 by Soccer Laduma

GladAfrica Championship leaders, Uthongathi FC, could only manage a goalless draw at home to lowly TS Sporting, however, JDR Stars took full advantage by walloping Jomo Cosmos 5-1, while Tshakhuma Tsha Madzivhandila grabbed third spot after overpowering Real Kings 2-1.

Furthermore, there were welcome wins for Swallows FC, Mbombela United and Cape Umoya United.

Ezenkosi were no match for the Hammer Boys at the Giant Stadium, where their star player, Moeketsi Makhanya, nabbed a neat hat-trick to bring his goal tally up to seven.

Other goal scorers were Sello "Tsiki" Letooane and Vusimuzi Mncube, which lifted Stars to within four points of the Cane Cutters, whereas Cosmos sunk back into the dreaded drop zone.

Thanks to Onyedikachi Ononogbu and Ndabayithethwa Ndlondlo, Tshakhuma secured their fifth victory as Kings remain mid-table.

Despite missing a penalty, the Birds stunned Free State Stars 1-0 at Goble Park courtesy of Lebohang Mokoena’s winner, a result that puts Brandon Truter’s outfit into the top eight.

Mbombela’s new head coach, Simon Ngomane, got off to a flying start after his charges crushed Richards Bay FC 4-0 in Mpumalanga with Felix Badenhorst netting a brace, while Senzo Gumede and Sabelo Ndzinisa were also on the score-sheet.

The Spirited Ones showed TS Galaxy, who was best in an exciting match at the Athlone Stadium, which ended 3-1 following their two late goals.

Earlier, Tauriq Dien’s free kick paved the way for a mix-up in Galaxy’s defence that led to an own goal before Bathusi Aubaas levelled matters via the penalty spot.

But clever finishes from Ddiviwe Mdabuka and Muzomuhle Khanyi left defeated coach, Dan Malesela, pondering his next move after five matches without a victory and a mere point from relegation.

Last-placed Royal Eagles sunk even further into danger when Steenberg United beat them 1-0 in the Mother City via Colin Ryan’s strike.
