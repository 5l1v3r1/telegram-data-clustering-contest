Plane crashes into homes killing at least 24 in DR Congo

24 Nov 2019, 17:51

At least 25 people have died after a plane crashed into a residential area.

The plane destroyed homes as it landed, killing several people on the ground as well as 17 passengers on board.

It crashed shortly after takeoff today in the Democratic Republic of Congo’s eastern city of Goma.

Two people are believed to have survived the crash, including one member of crew.

They are being treated in hospital, according to the National Border Health Program.

Provincial governor Nzanzu Kasivita Carly said the 19-seater aircraft hit homes in the Mapendo district near Goma’s airport in North Kivu province shortly after ‘missing’ its takeoff.

Black smoke rose from the plane in the morning, whose wreckage could be seen amid destroyed homes as dozens of men tried to help with the rescue efforts.

The smoke cleared as rescue workers carried bodies in stretchers and hundreds gathered at the scene.

Placide Kambale, a local pilot, said he took a taxi to the scene of the crash to help out. When he got there, the plane was on fire.

He said: ‘I called other young people from the neighborhood. They helped me to try to remove those who still moved.

‘We have managed to recover two that was quickly sent to the hospital.’

Joseph Makundi, coordinator of the North-Kivu civil protection, said that at least 25 people had been killed.

The Dornier 228-200 was owned by private carrier Busy Bee and was headed to Beni, about 220 miles north of Goma in the same province.

The UN mission in Congo said it sent an Emergency Crash and Rescue team with two fire engines to support Congolese authorities.

None of Congo’s commercial carriers, including Busy Bee, are allowed to fly into European Union airspace because of safety concerns.
