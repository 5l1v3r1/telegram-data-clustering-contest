Mum ‘left girls, aged one and two, to die in a hot car’

24 Nov 2019, 17:15

A mum has been charged with murder after her infant daughters were found dead inside her car on a 31C day.

Attempts to revive the girls failed and both were declared dead in Logan City, south of Brisbane, Australia.

The girls’ father said Darcey Conley, two, and Chloe-Ann, one, were ‘two peas in a pod’ and said Kerri-Ann Conley, 27, adored her daughters.

Police have said the toddlers died from exposure to extreme heat and are building a timeline to determine how long they spent in the car.

Officers are investigating whether Ms Conley was under the influence of drugs at the time. She will appear in court on Monday.

Ms Conley was already known to police, according to 9News, which said she rushed the girls into a cool bath inside her home after finding them in the car.

A neighbour claimed her grandson saw the toddlers in the car at 6.30am, seven hours before they were found, according to the Daily Mail.

Australian emergency services rescue more than 2,000 unattended children from cars each year, according to the National Roads and Motorists’ Association.

The organisation warns temperatures in a parked car can be more than 30 degrees hotter than outside the car, and three-quarters of this temperature increase happens within five minutes of the car being closed.

Members of the local community laid bouquets and stuffed animals outside the girls’ home in the suburb of Waterford West.

One person wrote: ‘Fly high little babies. So sad.’

Another commented: ‘Sad to hear… RIP little angels… thoughts with the service members that found the kids.’
