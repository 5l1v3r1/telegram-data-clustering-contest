'Nobody believes Boris Johnson and the hollow pledges in the Conservative manifesto'

The Prime Minister looks after Tory donors and billionaires while treating working folk with the contempt of a smooth-tongued Charles Ponzi, writes Kevin Maguire

24 Nov 2019, 17:56 by Kevin Maguire, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/kevinmaguire/

Nobody believes a word uttered by pathological liar Boris Johnson, a Tory cheat fibbing to all voters and not only lovers or the Queen.

This Conservative manifesto's promises are as hollow as that pledge in the last to keep the Labour free TV licences for over-75s he's busily stealing.

Because valueless, immoral Johnson says anything to be elected then treats the country like dumped Jennifer Arcuri.

His own position, status, urges, ego are what drive the ultimate Self-servative, a charlatan emphasising the Con in Conservative with his serial deception.

Johnson looks after Tory donors and billionaires while treating working folk with the contempt of a smooth-tongued Charles Ponzi.

His scorn is horrifying when the Tories stick a tissue paper over deep wounds inflicted by his own austerity mob over nine devasting years.

Jeremy Corbyn and Labour at least recognise the need for radical surgery to repair the damage, proposing real change to rebuild a better Britain based on shared prosperity and social justice.

Johnson declaring he'd fill a few of the hundreds of thousands of potholes proliferating after his lot slashed council spending is the spin of a fraud careering into a ditch.

No Tory deceit's greater than the moronic mantra “Get Brexit Done” which he failed to fulfil last year and knows is a poisonous lie when there'd be years of painful trade negotiations.

Secure a Parliamentary majority and impose his bad Brexit deal and bang goes the Tory sums, respected experts foreseeing a £70bn hole blown in the economy.

Dissmembling comes naturally to a Prime Minister who replied to a question about trust with three porkies.

Lying on Brexit or falsely claiming the Cons are putting record money into the NHS(they aren't) or pretending to construct 40 new hospitals(it's six) is an ideological strategy

Not just to hoodwink the electorate immediately but to destroy any public confidence in the honesty of all politicians so Corbyn's sincerity, and the ability of the Left to represent a coherent alternative to Johnson and the Tory Right, is doubted by the disillusioned who view all politicians the same.

The choice is huge in this election if you want free broadband, pay rises for low earners, improved schools and hospitals, council housing, social care and Universal Credit replaced.

Corbyn would be the fresh start, Johnson a continuity Conservative.

The difference between Labour vowing to keep taxes fundamentally unchanged for 95% and the Tories for 100% isn't the 5% on upwards of £80,000 a year contributing a bit more.

The contrast is decent public services with Labour and ongoing decline with the Conservatives.

Both sides aren't exaggerating for once in highlighting the chasm between the two main parties.

The Cons would win a decent majority if the election was this Thursday so mercifully it isn't, although crucial postal votes go out this week hence a Tory unconventional Sunday launch.

Much will happen in the two-and-a-half weeks until 12 December.

Hopefully Britain will wake up instead of sleepwalking into a Johnsonian nightmare.

Corbyn in 2019 is offering the new dawn that Blair spoke of in 1997 then never delivered.
