Kogi West: PDP rejects supplementary election, gives reason

24 Nov 2019, 17:03

- There are indications that the PDP may boycott the November 30 Kogi West supplementary election

- PDP’s spokesperson, Kola Ologbondiyan, said his party no longer has confidence in the ability of the INEC chairman, Prof. Mahmood Yakubu, to conduct free, fair and credible election

- Ologbondiyan also said his party rejected the scheduled supplementary election, saying it cannot reflect the wishes of the electorate

As the Independent National Electoral Commission fixed Saturday, November 30, for the Kogi West supplementary election, there are indications that Senator Dino Melaye and the Peoples Democratic Party (PDP) may boycott the poll.

According to Nigerian Tribune, PDP’s national publicity secretary, Kola Ologbondiyan, said his party no longer has confidence in the ability of the INEC chairman, Prof. Mahmood Yakubu, to conduct free, fair and credible elections.

Ologbondiyan reportedly added the PDP rejected the scheduled supplementary election, indicating it might not participate in the poll.

“We also reject the proposed Kogi West senatorial rerun election, which has already been desecrated by the manipulations in the main election,” the statement reads in part.

The opposition party said since the figures in the main election are allegedly flawed, the proposed rerun cannot reflect the wishes of the electorate.

The PDP added that its members are still grieving over the killings that greeted the November 16 elections.

Earlier, Legit.ng reported that Dino Melaye, on Wednesday, November 20, presented a petition to INEC in Abuja demanding the cancellation of the Kogi West senatorial district election.

The senator said he was received by the INEC secretary, Rose Orianran-Anthony and the national commissioner Festus Okoye.
