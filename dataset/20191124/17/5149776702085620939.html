Woman with cancer has 'best day of her life' as dying trike ride wish granted

'I still have not come down from the high'

24 Nov 2019, 17:47 by Ben Reid

A Nottinghamshire woman has had the "best day of her life" after she was granted her dying wish to ride a motorcycle trike.

Pat Whitehead, 76, of Ollerton, was diagnosed with terminal lung cancer earlier this month and told she had four to six months to live.

She always wanted to take a journey on a motorcycle trike, and to walk the Great Wall of China.

And on Sunday, November 24, alongside her close friend Annie Homer, 78, she was granted her bucket list wish.

As she's not able to go to China, Pat's close friend Alice Brazier, 63, of Ollerton, helped grant one of her wishes by helping to organise the trike ride with a bike convoy for free.

The three friends have known each other for years and play bingo together at the Ambleside Community Centre in Yew Tree Road, Ollerton.

Volunteers came forward to take Pat on the trike, with a motorcycle convoy leaving from Ollerton and taking her to Clumber Park near Worksop and back.

"I still have not come down from the high," Pat told Nottinghamshire Live.

"It was without a doubt the best day of my life. I only wish I did it 40 years ago. I felt no pain while I was on the trike, just joy.

"We went ever so quick it was so much fun.

"I'm extremely grateful to the people who helped organise this for me. I'll never forget it.

"Nick Farmer rode the trike. He was such a gentleman. He was checking we weren't too cold and if we were okay.

"My friend Annie loved it as well. I am so happy with it. It's what I wanted to do and now I have done it."
