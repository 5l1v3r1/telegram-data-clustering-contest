TOUCHING TRIBUTE

Hibs fans pay tribute in 38th minute to dad-of-six who died from brain cancer one month after son’s birth

24 Nov 2019, 17:51 by Chelsea Rocks

HIBS fans paid tribute to a dad-of-six of died of brain cancer less than a month after his son was born.

Jason Lynch, 38, fought his battle with brain cancer for two long years, passing away on Tuesday, just one month after the birth of his youngest son Jason-Junior.

The footie fanatic was surrounded by loved ones as at the Western General Hospital in Edinburgh as he slipped away.

Jason was diagnosed with a tumour while fiance Ewelina was pregnant.

He had previously been diagnosed with cancer before undergoing two brain operations and months of chemotherapy and radiotherapy.

The crowd rose to their feet in a standing ovation in the 38th minute on Saturday during their 3-1 win.

Fans also took to Facebook to honour the "warrior".

Jason's heartbroken fiance left a beautiful post in honour of him.

She posted:  "You will always be in my heart and I’ll always remember our good memories and laughs.

"You were the best thing that ever happened to me, my whole world and that will never change.

"Those special memories of you will always bring a smile. If only I could have you back for just a little while.

"We could sit and talk just like we used to do. You always meant so much and always will do.

"The fact that you’re no longer here will always cause me pain. But you’re forever in my heart until we meet again."

Jason leaves behind six children; Liam, Kyle, Niamh, Brooke, Jayden, Jason Junior and a young granddaughter, Lillie.

His son Liam posted a picture of his dad, he wrote alongside the picture "Rest easy Dad, love you loads".

Others commented on the posts, Susan Hall wrote: "So sorry to hear about Jason such a beautiful laddie and gentleman."
