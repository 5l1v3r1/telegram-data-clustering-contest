Baffled Derek McInnes queries Aberdeen handball claim as he disputes Kevin Clancy explanation

The Dons boss was left unsure after a chat with the referee at half-time during his side's draw with St Johnstone.

24 Nov 2019, 17:58 by Liam Bryce, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/liam-bryce/

Derek McInnes disputed Kevin Clancy's claim he could not see an Aberdeen penalty claim after his side surrendered two points in Perth.

Wallace Duffy appeared to deny Curtis Main an easy header on goal with a flick of the hand with the Dons leading St Johnstone 1-0 through Sam Cosgrove 's 17th goal of the season.

Matty Kennedy equalised for Saints after the break before Murray Davidson and Callum Hendry were shown straight red cards.

That sparked a frenzied finish at McDiarmid Park but McInnes' side could not fashion a winner in the dying minutes.

And the Aberdeen boss was baffled by Clancy's insistence his view of the potentially pivotal first-half incident was obstructed.

He told BBC Sportsound: "It's a penalty. I spoke to the referee at half-time and he said he didn't see it, didn't have a clear view.

"But the first thing I looked at was I thought he did have a clear view.

"Between the four officials here on duty... that is a penalty kick every day of the week.

"We've had few of these now this season where we've suffered for handball incidents - one that was outside the box and at Ross County where I'm not even sure it was a handball and Lewis Ferguson got done.

"That, for me, is so disappointing. It needs to be better than that to give the penalty kick."

That aside, McInnes admitted his exasperation at supporters heading back north without a victory to savour.

He added: "We've got to take responsibility.

"We had three points in our pocket and we allowed two of them to go. I'm really disappointed with that.

"From a winning position, we should see that game out. We should have more experience and confidence to see that out.

"So, I'm bitterly disappointed that a fantastic travelling support came down to see their team win and we couldn't give them that.
