Gunman opens fire at woman and four-year-old girl sitting in car in Liverpool

A woman was injured in the shooting, with police now appealing for information after witnesses reported hearing four loud bangs in Dovecot, Liverpool

24 Nov 2019, 23:45 by Matthew Dresch, Luke Traynor, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/matthew-dresch/

A woman and a four-year-old girl have been shot at in a parked car in Liverpool.

The woman suffered a minor injury, believed to be an arm wound, and was taken to hospital.

The child was unharmed in the shooting, which took place at around 7.30pm in Dovecot, Liverpool.

Residents said they heard four loud bangs which many presumed was fireworks, the Liverpool Echo reports.

One witness told the ECHO: "I just heard a loud bang like a firework when I was having a fag out the back and then police knocked on our door."

A large scene area was sealed off by police tonight.

Forensic officers in white suits were combing the area looking for clues.

There was a second police scene a 30 second drive from the cordoned off area.

A Merseyside Police spokeswoman said: “An investigation is underway following reports a female was injured in Dovecot this evening, Sunday, 24 November.

“We were called at around 7.30pm following reports that shots had been fired at a VW car which was parked in Aldwark Road.

“The woman and a 4-year-old girl were in the car at the time, the girl was not injured during the incident.

“The female has been taken to hospital where the injury is believed to be minor.

"There are road closures in place on Aldwark Road and Grovehurst Avenue.

“An investigation is underway and house-to-house and CCTV enquiries are being carried out in the local area.”

Detective Chief Inspector Mark Baker said: “This investigation is in its very early stages and enquiries are being carried out to establish exactly what has taken place.

“I would appeal to anyone who was in the vicinity of Aldwark Road this evening who saw anyone or anything suspicious or captured anything on dash-cam footage to contact us as they may be able to assist with the investigation.

“Firearms have no place on the streets of Merseyside and we are committed to finding the people responsible for using them and bringing them to justice.”
