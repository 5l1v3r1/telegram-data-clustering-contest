Dec snaps back at 'prickly' James Haskell after I'm A Celeb live trial sass

James flexed his sassy muscles after Ant & Dec asked him what had happened when he failed to win a star for camp against a giant and angry crab

24 Nov 2019, 23:35 by Irishmirror.ie

I'm A Celebrity's new camp leader apparently has a sassy side.

Rugby legend James Haskell snapped back at Geordie duo Ant & Dec after they asked him what had happened during tonight's live Bushtucker Trial.

Tonight saw most of the campmates take on the live trial, with the majority of them winning a roast dinner.

However, since James and Roman Kemp failed to win their stars, Ian Wright, Cliff Parisi and Andy Whyment would all have to make do with some rice.

The trial, based on another of ITV's reality smashes Love Island, saw the famous faces take on a number of challenges, which included downing blended vomit fruit and finding a star in a pot of mealworms using only the mouth.

James and Roman had to reach into boxes filled with all sorts of critters and unscrew their stars - but they didn't get them in time, meaning three of their pals wouldn't be getting their roast.

The pair made their way back to the others with heavy hearts and their heads hanging low.

"That was unlucky," said Dec, "What was it, James?"

A disgruntled James came back: "What was it?!

"The very fact that there was a giant crab in the way that you wanted me to carefully caress in a different direction while I was trying to unscrew a star in its face."

Dec mocked: "Alright, somebody's getting a bit prickly!"

"The competitive spirit!" James laughed.

Tonight's episode saw tensions reach a fever pitch as camp drama queen Andrew Maxwell clashed twice with different people.

One saw him slam the camp as "shambolic" after he didn't think they'd properly discussed the answer to a question that could win them a treat.

He hugged it out with James after they had a spat, saying: "I said my piece, everyone said their piece and big James gave me a hug, job done."

James headed to the Bush Telegraph to confide: "Everyone's tired, everyone's a bit chippy, everyone's had a long day, things happen.

"I'm just mortified if he thinks I just closed him down."

Another argument saw the comedian accuse Ian Wright of shouting at him, before they too hugged it out and Andrew threatened to let the waterworks flow again.
